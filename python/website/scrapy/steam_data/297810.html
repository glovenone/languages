About This Game

Welcome to the place where creativity and adventure thrive!

Landmark is a world shaped by the passion, talent, and ambition of its players. Explore and impact the world around you. Spark your creativity alongside a community where everyone has a chance to share something memorable.

What’s YOUR story?

Welcome to the place where creativity is power! As a powerful Luminary, you have the ability to mold landscapes and turn imagination into reality. Use your powers of inspiration to create your own story in this world, from a monster-plagued dungeon, to an intricate spaceship, or the house of your dreams. In Landmark if you can dream it, you can build it!
Along the way, you’ll meet other Luminaries eager to team up to build shared creations, battle monsters, or enjoy interactive experiences that YOU’VE designed. The possibilities are endless here, so establish your site, fire up your imagination, and get ready to create!

Key Features:
  • Create Masterpieces - Forge your own unique mark on the world.
  • Collect Treasures - Venture out into the world in search of materials, recipes, and props.
  • Share Your Vision - Inspire other players by creating interactive experiences.

This game is just bad. I'm hard pressed to even call it a game.

First off let me say I am not one of the people who bought into this as Early Access, I did not spend years waiting for the game and I am judging it here based purely on having purchased it today as a stand alone game for $10. My opinions are not based on any of the Everquest crowds expectations or any of Daybreaks previous statements. I went into this purchase with open mind and a hope that there might be something playable here.

The first 20 minutes:
It took 20 minutes to navigate the mess that is signing up for an account and linking it to steam (using the steam key). This is due to a combination of indecipherable web pages and frequent errors when loading those pages. A good portion of my sign up time was looking at error screens and apologies for their website not working from Daybreak. Then even after having the website tell me everything was complete there was a long delay before the launcher would proceed while it kept telling me to repurchase the game.

Deep breath, open mind, move forward.

Having already previously downloaded the game (about an hour prior to logging in) I would expect steam to keep it up to date (as it does with most other games....). Nope, after finally getting past the launcher: now it wants to patch... and patch the patch... and patch again... sigh.... I'll just go watch TV for 30 minutes while the game figures out what else to download after telling me it was already downloaded......

Deep breath, open mind, move forward...

The game finally finishes patching and I try to launch. The character creation system is as bare bones as I've seen for an MMO in many years. Minimal options for what to make your character look like. Some of the sliders have only 2 options on them and they don't even look that much different.

Thematically there is not even any cohesion to what the game wants to be. You get to choose between fantasy, western and sci-fi outfits. I guess that's what passes for a class system? There doesn't seem to be one, it's just what outfit you are wearing and is apparantly meaningless to the "game" (if you can call it a game... more on that later...)

So, navigating through the lack of customization that is character creation one glaring flaw really stands out. Everything clips. None of the bodies are well put together. Every type of long hair simply falls through the character instead of being sculpted to fall down the characters back. The armor and weapons are so overly large that they do not line up with the animations and simply clip through body parts. It's a very amateur look no matter what you end up going with. Maybe the male bodies were better? I was making a female and the hair issues alone were beyond stupid. Unless you think long hair is supposed to go down through your shoulders and come out in the arm pits. Or when you wipe your forhead the giant gun you are holding clips through your head. Basically no effort was put into making these characters look good or function well with animation.

Moving on.. hoping the "game" itself is better than the character creation.

And que loading.... I got up and made myself a sandwich here. I don't know why a game like this should take so long to load. The only thing I can equate this with is the loading times of some really old software tools. Programs like photoshop or 3D modeling programs that 10 to 15 years ago used to take upwards of 5 to 10 minutes to load up fully. (on modern computers with current gen versions of those programs they no longer even take that long ~ I'm talking about old school loading times)

By the time I was done eating my sandwich the game world was starting to show up on the screen after loading.

OK, time to play, right?

... ... what the heck is this?

First of all, the tutorial pages suck. They couldn't even be bothered to make the words fit correctly on the display pages with some of the words cut off by the border decorations. The tutorials also use a lot of jargon that they expect you to already know within the game: click on ABC to do XYZ.... great. where is ABC? And why do I want to do XYZ? Not explained.

Good luck guessing what buttons you were supposed to push to accomplish your non-goal because your screen is covered in buttons and the mouse overs are sometimes not present or filled with jibberish so you have to try and figure things out by trial and error.

Alright, maybe I can just walk around and see what's here. I mean, this is supposed to be an MMO, right? ...

I think they forgot to put a game in here. So, here I am standing on a big empty platform with nothing and a tutorial that wants me to build things. OK, I can do that, snap together some bits and pieces. Look I made a castle.

But... why? Now I have a castle on a big empty platform sitting in a big empty world with... nothing to do? I guess I can build another castle from premade pieces?

Ugh, this is work that doesn't lead anywhere. Why I am making buildings? No reason. What is there to explore? Nothing. Where is there to go? ... I'll look around... yea?

Lag... more lag... OMFGWTFBBQ how much lag can there be? Why does a big empty world with litterally nothing in it cause so much lag that the game plays like a slide show?

And that's what I asked myself for the next 30 minutes walking around. What is the point of this poorly optimized slide show while walking through a big empty world with nothing to do?

I don't get it. The game has no content, runs poorly and .. and.... is this even a game? Why would anyone subject themselves to this?

In conclusion: this isn't a game. It's a terrible set of world building tools to make ugly buildings in an empty MMO landscape where you don't do anything. And to add insult to injury, it doesn't even bother to run smoothly.

As for it even being labeled "MMO"... pff.... community is just a bunch of kids swearing at each other because they don't have anything better to do. And it's not like there's any point in having a community. There's no game here to play together.

The free mod tools that can be used for world building in Skyrim are a better "game" than this. And I'm talking about just the mod tools without Skyrim.

I hate myself for buying this. I hate myself for playing this. I am filled with remorse that anyone even had the balls to label this a game and put it out for sale. This is one of the worst purchase experiences I've had in my entire life.

I pray that steam refunds still work. I am now going to cry myself to sleep for even giving this game any of my time. Even if I get a refund (which means the hour or so I spent playing cost me no cash); I will regret every second of my time spent thinking or involving myself with this steaming pile of trash that some uneducated ♥♥♥♥♥ decided was somehow a "game" worthy of anyone playing, let alone paying for.

If you have any self respect at all. Do not buy this "game"..
