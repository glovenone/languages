About This Game

Ride your music.
Audiosurf is a music-adapting puzzle racer where you use your own music to create your own experience. The shape, the speed, and the mood of each ride is determined by the song you choose. You earn points for clustering together blocks of the same color on the highway, and compete with others on the internet for the high score on your favorite songs.
Audiosurf on Steam includes The Orange Box soundtrack, integrated with the game to enable "Still Alive" surfing and more. In addition, Audiosurf is one of the first titles to leverage Steamworks, offering full support for the Steam Achievements that appear on Steam Community profile pages.
  • Play with any music from your own collection--the game supports MP3s, iTunes M4As, WMAs, CDs and OGGs
  • Valve's The Orange Box Original Soundtrack is included.
  • Online scoreboards for each song you play.
  • Choose your strategy from 14 available characters.
  • Track your progress as Steam Achievements.

    Having scored 100% achievements in this game, I feel quite qualified to rate it 8/10 - would reccommend.

    As every game, it has pros and cons, and many users have stated them at great length. You should read other reviews, if you haven't yet.

    However, there are two key points that I haven't seen (so far) in the comments.

    As a downside, this game fails to sync the raceway speed to the audio track. Don't get me wrong, the blocks are perfectly synced to notes or beats. But the speed of your spaceship is constantly shifting up and down, for no reason.

    Therefore, you may compare this game to trying to jump into a train that is constantly and abruptly changing speed. In more upbeat trance songs, you barely notice this. But play a song like Arctic Monkeys - Do I wanna know? and it'll be hell. And not because it's a hard song or anything. It's just impossible to jump in and out of a lane if you don't know how fast you'll be going by the time you get to your color cube.

    Song selection can be quite tricky for this game.

    On the contrary, as an upside, it is remarkable that this game came out of a single person's work. Yes, it was initially developed by one person, although I'm not 100% sure at which point other people jumped in. Even Audiosurf 2 is a game made by few people.

    You might wonder why this matters. Well, it's not often you see nowadays games being developed by one person or super tiny teams. And when you see them, they are crap. However, even though the graphics of this game feel a bit outdated (you may play Audiosurf 2 for that case), you can feel the passion put into the game, since it was not developed by a 25.000 people team with a 25 billion dollar budget. You can see how things were prioritised, and I'm happy to report that gameplay was the priority in this game.

    Original idea, passion put into it, simple GUI, easy to learn, simple controls.

    Therefore: 8/10 - would recommend.
