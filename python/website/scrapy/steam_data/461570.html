About This Game

The Last Photon is a story guided platformer drama where the player is in control of the last photon ever to hit Joshua Cloud's brain. There are 50 levels in total, and 51 story fragments, each in one level and the final one after the game is finished.

Joshua is a young man who has suffered a terrible loss not long ago and is at the bottom of depression. He tried to find the answer in alcohol and that costed him his sight. Follow Joshua's stunning story and find out how his friend Donnie helped him feel a tiny bit of happiness and what it was caused by.

As the story progresses, levels get longer and more complicated. There are traps and enemies to avoid as well as the danger of falling to the precipice of the unconscious mind and never get to the brain sensors.

YOUR GOAL

Your goal in this game is to solve all 50 levels finding the photon's path to Joshua's brain and giving him his last tiny moment when he's able to see this world, even if it is just for a fragment of a moment.

FUTURE PLANS

The Last Photon is considered to be the first platformer drama in a series of few, each one with a completely different story to follow and take part in.

For the gamer who likes to brag they have completed difficult games on the hardest difficulty setting, this game might be fun, but for the rest of us, it quickly before infuriating.

The Last Photon tries to tell a story, in a way that reminds me a little ot Thomas Was Alone, but it doesn't work as well due to the difficulty of the gameplay. The gameplay is all about luck and perfect timing, sometimes with multiple sections requiring luck in a single level, leading to many deaths. When you die, you start at the beginning of the level again, but the narration continues, without pause.

The frustration is actually increased by the calm narration and soothing music, that might fit in a slow paced game of Mahjong, but is completely at odds with the frantic repetition of the gameplay, as you hope for more luck on the next run.

Nice art style though!

This game made me rage quit, but I loved it!

Really enjoyed The Last Photon and highly recommend it for the price it is!

I was expecting an extremely light, easy gameplay which emphasised the story and atmosphere. A lot of recent indie titles in this price range go for theme over mechanics, and because of the subject matter I anticipated much of the same. However, either I'm just terrible (a real possibility) or this game is a genuine challenge which is rare and enjoyable.

Flip side to this is I never actually finished the game, I was playing it for a Let's Play review so I couldn't sink hours into it - resulting in a rage quit. ( Here is a link to my gameplay of this title https://www.youtube.com/watch?time_continue=1&v=wVqgiEQoz4k ). I'm definitely going to finish the game in my personal time.

Only criticisms are a lack of game pad compatibility (which would work well in this case) and the range of music which is on loop.

But yeah, it's more than affordable so if you're looking for an addictive/challenging platformer I highly recommend you play this game!
