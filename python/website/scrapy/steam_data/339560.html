Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“With 13 fun levels so far we want to give our community already access to the game, after they voted for it. That is the least we can do to show our gratitude. Also we hope this will encourage more feedback for us to make refine the game together with the community and add more cool levels and gimmicks to it.
Remember that you have to thing different in our game and a bit out of the box, which we like.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Man Alive will most probably be in Early Access for most of 2015.
There a reasonable chance that it will be longer, or shorter. Members of our team and the community have differing ideas about how what exactly will be done before Early Access will be finished, and the scope of work to be done and hence time required.
This will be heavily influenced by your feedback during the Early Access process.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We plan to add 10 more levels and also some fun achievements for each level.
Also we plan to add full game pad support for the game pad lovers amongst you.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We have already 13 fun levels to share with our community supporters as a thank you for voting for us.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Man Alive's price might go up or stays the same during and after Early Access.
We don't expect it to change, but the possibility is there if we have to put in an massive amount of additional time and energy.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We love to hear from our community about the current status of the game and what else they would like to see in the future release.”

About This Game

Man Alive is a platform game with a twist (or two). We are working with the idea that death is not the end. There are no health packs, because we promote death in Man Alive! Actually, you HAVE to kill yourself to progress in our game.

That is why we believe it is something different and new, that we hope to share with you all. This campaign is very important to us, because we know we can create something fun!

We need your support to help us create Man Alive, to keep challenging the game industry and provide a steady flow of new ideas.

EDIT: PLEASE avoid this game at all costs.

I've done some more "research" and there are a few more concerning things I've found.

1. This game was released on Android in 2013.
2. This game was released as a full complete game on Desura in early 2014. The price? $3.99.
3. The last post on the Dev's website? Announcement of Greenlt status. Before that? 2013 was the last post.
4. The dev's contact is a twitter account. The last activity on the twitter account? 2014.
5. Zero contact, messaging, updates, responses to negative reviews/posts since the game released.

End EDIT.

Unfortunetly, this game is not very good.

Very simplistic platforming. At about level 5 when you get to the end of the level nothing happens. But considering how fast you can get there (2-4 minutes) plus the fact that there are only 13 levels (Supposedly) mean this game is probably not more then 30 mins of gameplay. No difficulty levels or anything. So thats $10.00 for about 30 mins of gameplay or to put it another way it's about $20 an hour. Considering how many games I've bought for $50 and played for more than 50 hours ($1.00 an hour), I don't think it's a very good deal.

Before my next observation, I would like to preface it with the fact that, overall, I am a big fan of the early access system. Prison Architect, The Forest and Stranded Deep are a few examples.

That being said, this game appears to be abandoned. Released on May 25th and not a word or sign of the dev. In my opinion, especially at the $10.00 price tag, this is just a money grab. Dev, please prove me wrong.

I ABSOLUTELY DO NOT recommend buying this game. At a quarter of the price it would still be overpriced.
