What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“With players providing feedback after they finish each early access section, we can use their invaluable opinions to shape the complete game. This includes information on what is running smoothly and what needs tweaking, what you would like to see changed or added, and even your moral support, all of which we will review to determine what can be changed for the better.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We plan to have the final version of the game completed and released by the second quarter in this year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“In the full version we are hoping to include:
  • New and previously unseen locations..
  • Finessed gameplay shaped using some of the views and ideas of early access players.
  • Added in-game events and more hidden secrets to discover.
  • Improved character and object models.
  • A wider variety of forest scenery and props.
  • An expanded upon and complete storyline.
  • A full set of achievements.
  • Linux support.
  • Possible translation into other languages such as Spanish, Japanese, German and French.
  • Possible Oculus Rift support.
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The completed game will be structured into four main segments that are comprised of many different interconnecting locations and events. At the moment the first and second section of the game is almost ready to be released. The other sections are structured and plotted out, with their content needing to be shaped and finessed.
As well as this the gameâs main systems and interface are complete and more than half of the gameâs sound is recorded.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes. The final version will cost more.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The original version of The Cursed Forest has a lot of fans; we hope that their support will carry over into helping us improve our new version of the game.
We want to improve the quality of the game by watching playerâs Letâs Plays, reading playerâs reviews, emails, messages on social networks and forums. We will analyse this feedback and compile a list of what needs to be done, and use this to determine what we can improve.”

About This Game



Over a year ago The Cursed Forest, a horror-adventure game, was released and became widely popular. Now the original gameâs creator KPy3O is working with team Noostyche to take you back into the forest again. Using the updated version of CRYENGINE, The Cursed Forest will be returning with enhanced graphics, a new storyline and many more improved features.

The old version is still available to download for free here:
http://www.indiedb.com/games/the-cursed-forest/downloads

Story:


When your character received the message that his mother was in the hospital he rushed to her aid, taking a shortcut by driving down an old, forgotten road through an ominous autumnal forest. When something appeared in front of his car and caused him to crash he was lost amongst the trees. The dark forest is hiding many secrets and creatures that wish to harm whatever crosses their path. Now it is up to him to uncover the mysteries of the past and save an innocent soul. But will he be able to survive long enough to do it?

Game Features


  • Travel through a vast world choosing your own path and explore the dark beauty of the mystical forest.
  • Survive sudden and dangerous situations, navigating though deadly traps set by an evil spirit.
  • Uncover a highly immersive story with an ominous and creepy atmosphere. Find in-game notes and discover the hidden tales of those who lived in the Cursed Forest, the history of the location and what happened to the lost expeditions who were unlucky enough to venture into the forest.

Can you stop the darkness that is rising?

Early Access Review
Posted: 15 September, 2015
Mysterious grunting from the treelines, scarecrows that move when you're not looking and monstrous rabbits lurking in cavernous burrows; The Cursed Forest is a pleasant break from half mutant serial killers stalking medical facilities and zombie hordes interrupting your attempts at deciphering another bewildering crafting system.
In its early stages, the game has all the atmosphere of a morbid children's story from the early 20th century, pointing you into the heart of the titular forest and inviting you to discover the eerie lore within. There's nothing so intense or vulgar as blood or guts on display here; the mood is held together by tales of disappearing villages, phantom deer and monsters that lurk in the lake and is far more of an exploratory experience than it is a QTE masher.
Very promising indeed.

Keep in mind there is a free version of this game which can be found here: http://www.indiedb.com/games/the-cursed-forest

I ended up playing TWO brand-new horror games tonight. The other one was your typical, generic, steaming-pile-of-dog-turd whipped up overnight by some enterprising (read: cynical and money-driven) fourteen year-old (its name is "BlackShadows", in case you have a few bucks you don't have the heart to simply flush straight down the toilet). The other one...was this. Suffice it to say, this one won. By a looooooooong margin...

To say that this (still very) Early Access game shows "potential" would be as self-evident as saying that Michael Bay needs to be drawn-and-quartered in a public square (before posting said footage all over YouTube-land as evidence of what we civilised folk do to people who can't even make GIANT â¥â¥â¥â¥ING ROBOT movies remotely exciting). Already, this game has more to offer than the vast majority of other "horror" games released onto Steam since, well...since basically all this Greenlight and Early Access nonsense started up, to be honest.

Rest assured, for once, this ISN'T just some "small development team" (read: one dude in his bedroom) who's whipped up a quick trailer and some semi-snazzy screenshots to dupe the idiot consumer (read: me) into giving them money for something which they put together in one afternoon (and maybe a tiny bit of the evening). Nosirree. The attention to detail in this game will make your JAW DROP. Why are there small rocks falling on me from above? Look up...holy â¥â¥â¥â¥, there's a skinny, creepy-â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ black figure with red eyes running over a stone bridge above me! Wonder what happens if I wade any further into this lake?
