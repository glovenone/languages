About This Game

The London Times started it all – calling the murders the work of an ancient Egyptian curse. I accused the tabloid of capitalizing on the superstitions of the populace. Yet, three men were dead, with the wrappings of a 4000-year old mummy binding their fates together. I find it hardly plausible and daresay believe the answers lie within the fabrics of a far more mortal realm. Once again, I have called upon the good doctor to accompany me.

Zojoi teamed up with famed board game company Sleuth Times to reproduce the award-winning Sherlock Holmes Consulting Detective video mysteries for Windows and Mac. This original titles was considered the pioneer products for emerging optical technologies and took advantage of the medium with full-motion video (FMV), intriguing storylines and fun game play. The restored retro game feature recaptured video, high-resolution graphics and strong storytelling and design.

Direct the famous Baker Street detectives through the fog-filled streets of London, and solve mysteries in a faithful adaptation of the board game that features:

  • 30 minutes or more of original video footage
  • Sherlock Holmes, his case companion, Dr. Watson, and over 20 additional characters (both trustworthy and unscrupulous!)
  • Full English subtitle support
  • Search the London Times for clues, peruse Holmes’ personal files, scour through the London Directory and receive hints from Watson himself
  • Present your findings to the Queen’s magistrate – and learn how you did against the master detective

A running theme through this series is that the game system is excellent and the cases fail to live up to the possibilities. It's just as true with The Mummy's Curse, though it's at least the best of a bad lot.

The biggest problem with The Case of the Mummy's Curse is it becomes fairly obvious who your culprit is early on and there is one absolutely threadbare path that allows you to haul ♥♥♥ to the conclusion with irresponsible speed. But if you reach that conclusion like most people would, by checking with witnesses, contacts, investigative resources, etc., you could have a thorough, airtight case that still won't satisfy the judge. The game requires that you've done investigation X while investigation Y is intended to make it more clear if you're hesitant to make an accusation.

It's a mystery that lets you approach the mystery from a bunch of different angles to build your case but only respects one of those angles as the true way to resolve it. It's reminiscent of getting stuck in a Phoenix Wright game where you've already reasoned through much of the mystery but struggle with understanding the tiny logical steps the game excepts Phoenix to make in order to catch up with you.

A straight FMV-heavy conversion of one of the cases from the 1981 boardgame "Sherlock Holmes Consulting Detective", first published in 1991. You solve a case by skimming newspaper reports and visiting any places or people who seem like they might be relevant - when you think you've worked out the solution, you take it to court and answer a Q&A to see if you were right, with your score being the number of investigative dead-ends that you avoided.

It's enjoyable enough. The video scripts do a clunky but passable job of being written to work no matter what order you're investigating the leads in, and the British accents are largely terrible ("English toffee? My favourite!"), but flipping through newspaper clippings and following up on anything that catches your eye does a fair job of capturing the amateur detective genre.

I don't think I'd bother to play any more games in the series, but I'd pick up the boardgame.

Edutainment out of the past!

You have to know that the "Sherlock Holmes Consulting Detective" series is a game series that was produced in the early 1990's. Back then they were, although they barely could be called games, quite a successful series. Professional actors tell you a story, you are actually deciding only which story is told - you can follow even red herrings and wrong leads (which are quite costly in points) aim of the game is it, to get as close as possible to the rating that sherlock holmes achieved in a specific case. So you test your mettle against the hero of Sir Arthur Conan Doyles stories himself.

This is just a rerelease of the old series, and it's really good that those made a reapearance. Though the videos are pixelated (clunky) the game still has it's old charm. It is actually fun to follow the leads and try to figure out who is the culprit in the who dunnit. Let's just hope the rest of the series also makes a reappearance.

The Case of the Mummy's Curse was the firs in the first set of games, the story is quite well told - won't give too much details though, because you should try out this interactive "movie" and find out for yourself who was the murderer, and what other information about the victims turn up, and what motive the murderer had for each of his killings.

Pros:
  • Nicely told narrative in form of an interactive short movie.
  • The rating system is very transparent, therefore you always can actually see where you are going.
  • The acting reminds a lot of the old black and white movies, which actually catches the atmosphere of the original Sherlock Holmes.
Cons:
  • Just to mention it, the clunkyness of the videos might be the biggest problem, but you have to keep in mind, that it's just a rerelease under a new company!
  • It is really, really short and has only a very limited replayability value.

Recommended

I can recommend this game on various reasons. First it's obviously a game which is thought to be a short sidestory, in between like some nice and tasty morsel. The acting and voiceacting of the artists is really good, given the time that passed between release and present, it's timeless to watch a nice interactive movie that was so smoothly added into a game. There are no complicated game mechanics that might disturb users, no hidden buttons, no traps and tricks; Just plain storytelling, interviewing and decision making.

Back in the nineties, the Consulting Detective Series was quite successful and founded the basis for a lot of other Sherlock Holmes games, most of them had to hold up against this series, which a lot of games, even adventure games with a lot more of variety couldn't reach the success of the Consulting Detective Stories.

It is a no brainer that I would recommend this game, alone for the nostalgic value of the series.

I will start off by saying that this game has very little to actually "do" in it, it is more of an interactive story almost, but not quite. The object of all three of the Sherlock Holmes Consulting Detective mysteries is to gather clues by deciding on what people and places to visit. Once you have gotten enough information to solve the crime/s you will go before a judge and he will ask a few questions that you must answer correctly. Getting to the judge and answering questions using the fewest amount of clues that you can will give you a score. The goal is to solve the mystery by using only as many clues as it takes Holmes to solve the mystery. There is not a lot of replay ability in the game except if your score doesn't match Holmes' once you have solve the mystery. I wouldn't expect it to take very many playthorughs to match Holmes scores.

If you like Sherlock Holmes , or some not too hard brain teasers, you will enjoy the game. It is only about 60-90 mins of total gameplay in most instances for each mystery. As for the price, I would definitely go the collection route as it's almost half price. I would probably also wait til it might be on sale as well.

Bottom line: If you like this sort of game then, splurge. An hour of my time was worth the 0.82 cents for the game.
