About This Game

You are a master of time. You can set it to past, present and future. Use this power to help a mechanical bird complete its extremely important mission of collecting the knowledge about a mysterious green world. This is not an easy task, as the bird moves on its own, and you cannot stop it. Control time to activate and deactivate various mechanisms, to make sure the bird reaches the cage without dying.

The game features fifty carefully designed levels. You will need to make your way through a hostile world - the blades of windmills will slice the bird, pistons shall squash it and spiked gates are going to crush it. Control time to deactivate traps and activate the steam generators that will change your movement direction. Find one of many ways to the end of each level. Plan your route and show your reflex skills to get through the most difficult paths and gather all the gears.

Game Features:

  • 50 levels full of dangers
  • controlling time and thus influencing the environment
  • many different paths to the destination
  • a unique green graphical style
  • soul jazz soundtrack
  • power-ups (immortality, slow flight, etc.).
  • 150 gears to collect
  • one-more-try-and-I-quit syndrome

Green game: TimeSwapper is a sequel to the Red Game which I have not played, but would like to try out in the future.

In the Green Game you control a mechanic world and time itself where you guide a bird from one cage to another.

As you control time itself the layout of the level changes. You can rot things to create ways for you to go through, turn on or off steam valves that blow you into the way you want to go through and go through portals.

The art style is outstanding, every time you shift time stuff in the background changes and it's just awesome to look at.

The gameplay is pretty simple, you guide the bird through the stages by turning on steam valves to blow him in the right direction, this can be achieved by using either the mouse controls or the keyboard, but I highly suggest using the keyboard as the mouse controls are kind of unresponsive.

The soundtrack at first is quite decent, but the it gets tiresome to listen to, so I suggest muting the game and adding your own music.

The game also has one major flaw and that's that a lot of the later levels are more trial and error than anything, being that most levels are massive it makes things tough to navigate and more or less a pain in the ♥♥♥ to reach the ending of the level.

The game consists of 50 levels and for the most part everything is pretty easy, there are a few really hard levels, but the challenge comes from collecting three blobs hidden in each level and sometimes it's hard to collect them.

The game lasted me 2 hours to finish, but if you will try to find all three blobs in each level might take some time and might get frustrating.

Overall, I suggest getting the game on a sale cause it's a pretty great puzzle game, not too challenging, but never too easy. If you like these types of games Green Game: Timeswapper is definitely for you.

Pros:
+ Amazing Art
+ Interesting Gameplay mechanics
+ Level Design

Cons:
- Too many Trial and Error levels
- Forgettable soundtrack
- Broken mouse controls

Final Rating:
7/10

Green Game: TimeSwapper is iFun4All’s second game on Steam following the December, 2015 release of Red Game Without a Great Name. This title has also been released onto PS Vita, Android, iOS and is one of those rare games that is suitable for all of the platforms that it is on. The game is pretty simplistic to learn the basics of but features some very complex and well designed puzzles that will be certain to be the bane of your existence if you allow yourself to be suckered in by its charm.

The charm is in the presentation. The music is fantastic and is among my favorite soundtracks in an indie game this year. The soul jazz soundtrack really does the job of establishing the mood and the atmosphere for the game but at the same time reminds me of television shows from when I was a child (1980’s) and prior. It’s sweet sound is melodic and a natural fit for a game that I think attempts to be the ideal chill out indie game that you can relax to and complete a few levels after work. The green graphics are suitable for the game as well and again add atmosphere to the game.

The gameplay mechanics are simple. I used my controller to play the game – I believe that is the best way to play the game – and you dial the use the time switches in order to set the smoke which pushes your mechanical bird in the direction it is blowing – if you decide to allow it to steam – otherwise the bird will keep flying in the direction that it is currently flying which may end with the bird flying into one of the deadly traps on each level. It sounds easy and the game begins easily enough but the challenges become increasingly difficult as the game goes on. As well as the smoke switches there are also teleportation markers on the field that will transport you from one area of the map to another. Although the game can be completed in one sitting I tend to be of the opinion that the mechanics would get a little boring.

There are fifty levels within the game with each level of varying lengths. One mistake takes you back to the start. There are also power ups within the game which all have their own special ability such as immortality. All of them have been thoughtfully considered and amount to relatively decent changes in the game play when you unlock them. The value proposition has been considered with the game hitting its mark nicely at the current US$4.99 mark.

There are fifteen total Steam achievements within the game and the game has a 5-card set of trading cards. Neither are listed on the Steam page which is something that I hope the developers rectify as it is undoubtedly a way that they can garner more purchases without it costing them a cent and they’ve already done the work.

I enjoyed my time with the game and I can certainly recommend it for arcade indie fans who are seeking a relaxing challenge.

I reviewed this game for Game-News.co.uk with a copy of Green Game: Timeswapper being given for the purpose of the review.

Time control seems to be a big theme in gaming at the moment, where players are given the control of time and are allowed to use it for the benefit of good. Or to play a series of twisted experiments with a cute critter that can fly. Green Game: Time Swapper takes players into a world where they can indirectly control the environment through the fragment of time that allows certain elements to change. This, in turn, allows a flying bird to escape a number of traps and it’s up to you to make sure it flies safely to the exit.

The flying bird cannot be controlled and moves in a single direction, unless made to do otherwise. You can change his trajectory by using various placements in the level and often these are controlled by the aspect of time. The player can control time by moving a dial from the left to the right of the screen; this changes certain aspects of the game world, from bringing machines to life that can push the bird in another direction, to huge obstacles that can, and probably will, kill that poor bird.

It’s a solid and rather dynamic concept for a puzzle game, where the environment reacts to your positioning of the dial. You must attune the dial to different positions at the right time, planning ahead of the bird’s movements and all within an extremely short amount of time. It’s clever as it tests your lateral thinking and reflexes.

Green Game: Time Swapper offers a good challenge with a simple design and mechanics that are easy to learn and remember. The game presents some nifty visuals with a mixture of silhouettes and surrealist colouring.

The overall challenge in the later parts of the game become a drag as the game fails to introduce enough new elements and even misuses some, such as power-ups that slow the bird down which are often placed in the right places while others just seem needless. They’re either too close together or are strangely absent when they could be needed most. The game never really delivers new challenges or ups the ante to make a bold spectacle.

The complexities of some levels become an annoyance when having to replay them over and over again due to its trial and error format of design. This isn’t helped when certain elements that alter the game feel a little buggy or too sensitive to the constant shifting of time. The trajectory of objects that push the bird in a different direction are difficult to judge and if you move them the slightest when the bird is close enough his flight pattern will reset and ruin your planned route.

The soundtrack is also rather irritating with little variation and just plain generic sounding. Not great to hear looping over when you have to repeat a level several times.

Green Game: Time Swapper is a stimulating and solid puzzle game with nice visuals and a dynamic mechanic. Yet it lacks any variation in structure for its puzzles/levels and becomes a short-lived affair when you start to see that the game becomes too repetitive without introducing other interesting elements. It can be fun and rather challenging, but sadly not fulfilling enough to ensure a healthy playtime.

+Simple yet enjoyable puzzle elements
+ Neat time control mechanic works well with the game's design
+ Nice visuals

- Becomes very repetitive around the half way point
- Certain puzzles drag on too long
- The soundtrack is rather annoying
