About This Game

Doodle God is an addicting puzzle & world building game where players use logic and ingenuity to create the entire universe.
Starting with the basic elements of earth, wind, fire and air players can build a world filled with locomotives, Dragons, skyscrapers, Aliens and more. In Planet mode players will discover a challenging new way to develop the universe of their dreams.
As you create elements watch the world come to life as new combinations create new elements, life-forms and objects that propagate the planet's surface. But beware with great power comes great responsibility. Players need to be aware of unintended consequences like a zombie plague and other catastrophes! Test your divine knowledge and challenge your friends online with the new Tournament mode. Each time players successfully create a new item they’ll be rewarded with wit and wisdom from some of the greatest philosophers and comedians of all time. Unleash your inner god with Doodle God™ for Steam!

Posted: 16 June
Let's list every good thing about this game:

1: Some item combinations are mildly amusing.

2: The art is decent.

Okay, done. Here's all the bad things:

1: It's a bad mobile port and the entire UI is clearly designed for touchscreen, and plays awfully on a computer

2: At least half the game is essentially advertisement for GameFAQs, because the item combinations are so arbitrary as to be impossible without a walkthrough or brute forcing. The only reason that the combinations are as nonsensical as they are was to sell more Hint IAPs, and it makes the game completely unenjoyable.

3: There's a few combinations I found which actually had a different result depending on when in the game I combined them - meaning that I actually had to enter the exact same input twice expecting different results to complete the game.

4: The game costs $6 despite merely being a port of an IAP-laden port of a sequel of a clone of a flash game from 2004. You could just play the first Doodle God on Kongregate for free and get an identical experience, if not a better one thanks to a UI that was actually designed for the platform they released the game on.

5: A significant amount of these are near-identical to other combinations of items so you can't even complete the game without repeating yourself constantly (e.g., there's ~8 types of alcohol, all of which count as different combinations with nearly any material that combines with any alcohol)

6: The game has 4 or 5 different gameplay modes, but I honestly can't tell the difference between any of them. The gameplay loop is exactly the same - pick a random thing, rub it against another random thing, hope the developers concocted some story as to how they fit together, and then rinse and repeat.

7: Every time you do luck out and find an arbitrary combination of elements that makes something new, you get thrown out of the combination screen and into a tedious animation of that new object appearing on a globe. This is mildly interesting the first 2 or 3 times. Less so after the 200th. In a game that's already frustratingly slow thanks to an awful UI and necessity of brute force, it just exacerbates the irritance.

Just save your money and play the thousands of free flash games that do thiis much better.
