About This Game

Il-2 1946 includes all of the original IL-2 series content: IL-2 Sturmovik™: Forgotten Battles, Ace Expansion Pack, Pacific Fighters™ and alternate histry campaigns of year 1946. What was years ago began as a one-aircraft study sim is now an astonishing anthology, with a whopping 229 flyable aircraft and over 300 aircraft total!

The game has been updated to the latest version 4.12.2. This community made update includes widescreen support with correct FOV, many new planes, maps and scenarios.

  • New Aircraft: 32 new flyable fighters and bombers, and four new AI aircraft!
  • New Maps: Large new historical maps of the Kiev region; a map of Manchuria focusing on a border region between USSR, China and Japanese-occupied Korea; a large bonus Burma map; and an online Khalkin Gol / Nomonhan map.
  • New Campaigns: Nine new campaigns with nearly 200 missions add incredible realism and attention to detail. There are detailed careers for fighter and bomber pilots, for the German, Soviet, and Japanese air forces, offering such varied objectives as flying jet bombers over occupied Europe, to intercepting American B-29 Super fortresses over Iwo Jima and Japanese home islands!
  • Alternate History: Three of the nine campaigns are a departure into alternate history, focusing on hypothetical battles between the Soviet Union and Germany in the year 1946, while WWII still rages on.
  • Nearly 1,000 new Paint schemes: The new planes, as well as many of the other ones in the series, come with a host of historical paint schemes, showing many of the individual aircraft or squadrons that served in WWII.

Title: IL-2 Sturmovik 1946
Developer: 1C: Madd0x Games
Publisher: iC-777
Genre: Flight Simulator

General Impression
Sturmovik 1946 is one of the best WW2 combat flight sims in recent years and I have been playing it in one version or another, almost since its launch. In its current 1946 edition, you have a whopping number (229) of planes to choose from as well as dynamic campaigns for almost every theatre of operations from that war. The amount of setting customization is also an integral part of this game. You can never claim that it might be too difficult since you can tweak the options until you play an almost arcade version. And yet, this title is best played on a flightstick. The mouse and keyboard do a decent job, but they are far from ideal and this game takes some time getting used to, especially with the taking off and landing parts (both skippable). Age had taken its toll on the general aspect, but Sturmovik 1946 is far from bland looking and the sound score fits the game perfectly.

Strong Points
+ Nice graphical details.
+ Excellent diversity in both missions/campaigns and in the airplanes themselves.

Weak Points
- No Steam Trading Cards or Achievements.

Rating 9/10

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 11 June
I'd been flying for the better part of an hour over endless, shimmering blue waves before they came into sight. At first I thought it might be just a trick of my brain, as I had been straining my eyes toward the horizon for a long time. But no, it's not my imagination, there are the faintest shadows against the sky in the hazy blue distance. First just a few, then more, and as I fly closer they elongate and begin to take shape. A squadron of heavy bombers, at least 20 of them by looks of it, and they are on their way to drop a hundred thousand pounds of high explosives on our airbase. Well, not if I have anything to say about it.

I pull back on the stick, climbing above the scatted, puffy-white stratus clouds and putting the incoming bombers a few thousand feet further below me. I let them come sliding underneath, and then I roll left and dive. The Gs push me back into my seat and I feel the blood rush from my head but I modulate my dive to avoid losing consciousness. In what feels like a matter of a few heartbeats I drop out of the clouds behind the squadron and get the tail-most bomber in my sights. The tailgunner has seen me and he opens fire, but its too late. A short burst from my cannons sets their right engine on fire. Another burst and the wing snaps like a twig in a gale, the bomber pitches sharply up and then topples over to the left. Thus begins their death spiral down into the sea below, a trail of black smoke highlighting the bomber's final path. I see a few white parachutes open along that trail of smoke but the helpless bomber crew no longer interest me. I'm getting near enough to the next bomber that their gunners start to open up, and white phosphorous tracer rounds are streaking past my cockpit glass. Cool, calm, collected... I feel the exhilaration of the kill but I ride the adrenaline smoothly. My hand rests lightly on the stick, my feet adjust the rudder ever-so-gently, and the swaying of the plane around me is like an extension of my arms and legs. The second bomber's left engine is in my sights and I squeeze the trigger....


10/10 Would stalk enemy bombers again.
