About This Game

Squarelands is sandbox adventure game where you explore a randomly generated world while fighting, building and becoming stronger as you play! Mainly inspired by Terraria, Squarelands aims to provide plenty of freedom for the player.

Features:


  • Day/Night Cycles
  • Mining, Crafting and Building
  • Procedural Map Generation
  • Fishing and Farming
  • Hundreds of Items and Objects
  • Death Penalties (and Optional Permadeath)
  • Key Rebinding

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 or Vista
    • Processor: 1.6 Ghz
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 512MB with shader model 3.0
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 500 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: Mouse support only. XNA 4 and .Net Framework 4.5.1 required.

2015 Oryon Entertainment

Played for a good amount of time. I don't have a lot of survival game exposure, but I do prefer 2D over 3D environments when available, so I looked for something like this when I wanted to check out survival games. I've yet to get Terraria, but this would probably be my follow-up after I get finished playing around with this game.

I've had to dump a lot of collected gear and I mostly dump them into treasure chests and leave them at my shelter, old shelter or at cave entrance "shrines" I'd create to make the cave entrances more visible on the map. The underworld is full of torches I place around as I investigate for ore, treasure and experience heavy monsters.

Spoiler: Collect bones from skeletons to make a powerful sword that moves the game forward a lot faster. I found skeletons on the first and second levels of the underworld and I assume the deeper I go, the more powerful the monsters may grow.

My problems with the game are the large mobs of wizards that control strongholds throughout the surface area. However, I've taken it as a challenge to grow powerful enough to take on and wipe out these strongholds. I hope these structures are defeatable with a computer as powerful as mine. As noted in a prior review, these wizard mobs tend to spam attack and lag the game for me, which is frustrating and prevents effective skirmish assaults to pick off these fools.

There does seem to be a lot more to discover in this game and I see myself putting at least another score of play hours into this game. I hope the developers return to this game and enhance it into popularity. Finally, Realm of the Mad God wasn't appealing to me as I wasn't looking for another MMO and as someone who has contributed some minor pixel art and effort into a game (The Mana World), I know I'd be honored to have many games use my art. I hope that more of this style of art is developed and used by Squarelands or a sequel to it and grow the play factor by leaps and bounds. So instead of backing away from these graphics, double down and be proud to use them.

If this were an early access game, I'd say that it was a fun little prototype with tons of potential.
However, this is considered to be the finished product and I just can't recommend it.
In short, it's very buggy, very unbalanced, and very light on content. You'll be ridiculously overpowered after 10 minutes, and you'll have seen everything that the game has to offer after about an hour or so.

It's such a shame because the whole top down mining game concept works really well, playing this game can be addictive, fun and relaxing but you're just constantly reminded of how much better it could be with more time and effort spent.

If I were the dev, I'd pull this and either go into early access or work on it for a year or two until it's ready.
