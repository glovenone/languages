About This Game

“Steel Ocean” is a free-to-play, MMO shooting game. As a navy commander, you may choose 6 types of warships used in the World War II to fight the 16vs16 battles. Start the voyage with your warships and fight for the honor of your fleet!


Game Features:
Essence of team play: Tactics first
  • 6 warship types and over 100 famous warships with distinctive features.
    Steel Ocean presents the 6 most representative warships of the modern and contemporary naval system, namely, destroyer, light cruiser, heavy cruiser, battleship, carrier, and particularly the “deep sea killer” – submarine, appearing for the first time.

  • Incorporating hundreds of classic tactics, and supporting the original tactic system.
    Steel Ocean also restores hundreds of classic sea battle tactics and sea battle maps. The players may replay the “Wolf pack tactics”, “Competing for T head” and other famous tactics at real time, and independently design the new tactics based on the maps, natural environment, weather conditions, warship combination and other objective factors.

  • Diversified competition modes, and team play varying from modes.
    The actual sea warfare is not merely the exchange of firepower. Steel Ocean provides tens of combat modes, from the MOBA random matching for combat, the battle to seize the isle for testing the team tactics, to the short distance contact battle without isle, combat of small teams in different sizes, and even the super large legion PVP battlefield in the boundless water…, available to meet the different requirements of game players. Each mode includes several sets of applicable tactics and derivative skills, bringing the endless and changeable battlefield experience.

  • Highly immersive interactive map to restore World War II scenarios.
    The development team of Steel Ocean accurately simulates the isles, reefs, buildings, metrological, weather conditions, etc. of many sea areas in the world, based on the results of consulting extensive historic records and many times of site surveys, and the actual geographic environment data.

  • Separated Damage Calculation system to ensure more accurate attack.
    In addition to the original damage calculation by health bars of the TPS vehicle war games, Steel Ocean is incorporated with the separated damage calculation system (“SDC”).

  • Commanded by hundreds of officers of World War II, providing warships with tactic soul.
    Steel Ocean includes the innovative warship commander system, incorporated with hundreds of famous navy generals during the 2nd World War, based on the actual history of the period, to impart the cold warships with the different tactic features.

  • Unique legion system to create super marine legions.
    The fleet guild system provides a big platform for players to communicate on tactics, and also combines the team play and the fleet growth mechanism into one.

  • Built-in radio voice to effectively restore maritime communication.
    One of the most critical factors deciding the success or failure of a military operation is intelligence. The information communication at any place and at any time is the basis for the battle with the team collaboration, and the key for realizing the strategic cooperation of multiple warship types in the modern sea battles.

Posted: 7 July
Game is really great fun, has moved along fairly well in its competitiveness compared with WoW.
Key Points:

1. A lot of people despise the game over the graphics compared with WoW (If your problem with this is the graphics then you can either get a better computer, go and use clipart instead or just play the game because it ain't about the pretty it's about the play), to be honest, the graphics aren't that bad, you have various graphic detail settings available for you to tailor to your computer's limits without it looking like something you could achieve from an iPad/tablet.
2. The tier system is pretty balanced overall (pro) with the occasional (con) OP ship in some tiers such as the CL Atlanta or DD/DC Laforey. On top of that, despite the insane scouting range on some BBs in tier 6+, all of these factors encourage you to play smarter on the open seas and rather think through your options without being spotted.
3. Heated topic around this game in comparison with WoW is the player base. This game really does deserve more players than it currently has and I understand why there is only US servers and the proxy servers. There really isn't much you can do about the sheep that follow the WoW franchise when there is a perfectly more enjoyable and entertaining game right here needing its global support - I sympathise with you.
4. Alike in Battlefield where there are "only in Battlefield moments", Steel Ocean (SO) has its own moments as well. Again, let down with its player base (low population), these "Steel Ocean moments" only occur every once in a while, however almost all the time in either a large battle or during Submarine warfare (dependent on your experience and play style in SS as well as any other ship class).
5. Only really frustrating thing I have with the game is the fact that, despite the in-game scaling of the environment, where in real life you could see an enemy ship off the horizon - obvisouly for game mechanics to work there is what is known as 'scouting range/visual', I really dislike the fact that an enemy ship can see you whilst you cannot see them if they're only a couple of meters ahead (say somewhat 300m). I have encourtered battles where I am a cruiser and cannot see an enemy destroyer in the open water that was only a few meters ahead of me and yet I am getting pounded by artillery with no hope - even with smokes they still see sometimes. (gotta fix this, the only real kickback)
6. Absolutely love scenario mode and I wish there were other game modes alike that within normal matchmaking - it would really enhance the whole WWII tier structure and encourage players to use their ship class as designed. This would also enphasise teamwork and, as seen within scenario mode, have all ships on one team be of the same country of origin (US vs JP).
7. Matchmaking is another heated topic as a lot of people in the reviews bag on about how it took them 30mins to find a game. This happens for a few reasons and the person at fault is usually themselves. After the first few minutes after you have initiated to find a game and haven't found one, you are given a message to advise you to perhaps change your ship or tier in order to get better results. Another factor is that you can see how many people are within your tier group that are also finding a match, if there are less than 4 per 15seconds, it's a pretty good indicator that you need to change tier. Finally, if you're finding a game and there are say 14 players within that team and 12 disappear and you and another person are the 2 left on the match making screen, it's a fairly good indicator that you chose a ship that was either too popular within that tier or wasn't popular enough in order to have a fair game. (What could really help is instead of showing all the ship classes that are searching for a game, only show the ship classes that are active within the tier you are searching for in order to adhere this issue)
8. The community is great and really enforces a more enjoyable experience for newcomers. Like every game, you can't just judge it by your first few hours of playing, especially a strategy shooter such as this one as it takes time to accumulate enough skill/experience in order to fully understand and appreciate the game and how the ships work individually, catering for your own play style.
9. Perhaps bringing in bots in higher tier games (where few players ever reach because of the grinding, alike in WoW, to get high tier ships) is an option and work them like how they operate in other games where if your player dies, they can choose to either leave or keep playing by taking command over a bot ship and the points earned from the bot ship will only be rewarded in silver instead of xp. This will increase and support the larger sea battles and help new players get their hands dirty while playing (bot difficulty increases within the tiers obviously).

It's a wonderful game and deserves more than what it get, it takes a lot to create a game such as this one. As much as I would love all the issues addressed by negative reviews, I've tried to keep these key points as open as possible in order for you to understand the game a little better without bias. I do hope the devs sort out those issues indicated within those reviews but alas, it is still a game worth giving a second chance and spending your time on.

First off this game is nothing like WoWS and is not pay to win as I can personally attest being one of the top players and having not spent a dime.

The aim mechanics are entirely different both for guns (aim in time vs point and shoot) and torpedoes (single shot capable vs autospread). Secondary guns, AA, and planes (yes even on non carrier ships) are all manually controled. The roles of ships are far more defined and have to work together to provide scouting (more discription on this at the end). All this makes the game play entirely differently than WoWS and means that if your team knows their role and knows how to support eachother you can beat far "better" players or ships that don't work together.

Some of the complaints about the game some are valid some aren't. The graphics aren't great but they aren't as bad as some say, there is only the NA server (and a European proxy), the population is pretty low though not as low as some reviews say since they are looking at the currently waiting for a game count, partly because of the former the matchmaker can give badly setup games, and the tutorials are lacking and many things go unexplained. People who complain about cheaters who can always see them don't understand the scouting system and how you use commanders to buff your scouting and that you can buy camoflauge for your ship (you can buy it with the non-premium curency which many don't know) because these things are not explained which is my biggest problem with the game itself unrelated to the size of the player base.

As to pay to win accusations people like to say that the commander system is pay to win but you would have to spend hundereds of dollars on commanders to get anything resembeling a gamebreaking advantage. Completing all the events of one week means you get one 36 dollar commander pack a though this requires playing in a fleet and tier 5 ships which can all be had within a week. And this dosen't include the monthy 20 days login event (worth 1/2 a pack) and the monthly fleet rankings. You can get gold items (excluding ammo which would be ludicrously expensive to use) from events and rankings and the 5v5 ranking even gives gold.

For ship types; destroyers function as scouts formost while killing subs and DD and as dropping torps to kill larger ships being secondary roles, light cruisers are larger less maneuvarable DDs with more/better guns, heavy cruisers have fast firing long range guns to kill lighter ships, battleships are your heavy long range hitters for taking punishment and dishing it out to other battleships and heavy cruisers, and carriers act as AA to protect against other CVs and using planes as scouts while only occasionally attacking ships unless the enemy has fewer CVs than yours, and subs can function as stealth scouts and assasins or they can draw the enemy destroyer back so he can't scout while harrassing the BBs so they have to turn and can't fire.

Posted: 6 December, 2015
If you've played World of Warships (or World of Tanks, for that matter), the game progression is similar -- play ships in PvP matches against other players to earn EXP to unlock better modules and/or ships.

Where this title departs from being just a mere World of Warships clone is as follows:

+ Has submarines, which are powerful if let loose among the capital ships in the back of the fleet, but are weak against destroyers.
+ Torpedoes are manually fired one-at-a-time, requiring manual input to nicely spread, but also allowing for fine-tuning of said spreads.
+ Ammunition isn't unlimited! You can't just fire all the time and expect to have ammunition (especially torpedoes) left by the end of a match.
+ Battleships are actually powerful with their main guns, and can lob rounds well out of their own view range (which is what scouts like destroyers are useful for!).
+ Aircraft carriers can pick and choose their own plane loadout, allowing for customization to your own playstyle.

- Because of battleships being really powerful, they're currently (as of this writing) somewhat over-used. Matches can often have more battleships than other ship types.
- Cruisers as a whole tend to be VERY sparsely populated (because of the glut of battleships, which easily shred cruisers).
- Physics is arcadey at times. Ships can accelerate or stop much faster than would be possible for a ship of their size (battleships getting up to speed within a few seconds, for example).

Gameplay is somewhat of a mix between Battlestations: Pacific and World of Warships.

Overall I would recommend this game to anyone who wants a good naval-themed game.
