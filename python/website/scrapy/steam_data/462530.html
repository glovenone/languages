About This Game

8i brings virtual experiences to life with the highest fidelity, most realistic volumetric video of humans. A truly immersive experience, volumetric VR gives you a sense of presence and freedom to move through space and time, opening up worlds of possibilities.

8i volumetric VR experience features:

True presence: Step into the story and feel like you’re not just watching someone, you’re with them.
Freedom of movement: Walk completely around the person you’re watching as if you’re in the same room, and choose your own perspective.
Experience it anywhere: View on virtual reality headset, mobile and on the web

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 8.1 or greater
    • Processor: Intel i5-4590 equivalent or greater
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia Geforce 970
    • DirectX: Version 11
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows 8.1 or greater
    • Processor: Intel i5-6400 equivalent or greater
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia Geforce 980 or equivalent
    • DirectX: Version 11

This is another one of those things where it's not so much a game as much as it is a tech demo for volumetric 3D filming, which is sort of like photogrammetry but for people by my limited understanding. It's not bad, but it's also not really that great, and the characters tend to have minor graphical glitches and white borders that make the scenes look more like you're living in one of those old FMV-based point-and-click adventures instead of a real world. It's free, so if you're curious you may as well try it, but it's a pretty big download so it's hard to say if it's worth the time to download it, depending on how interested you are in the technology and seeing a glimpse of something that might be more worthwhile in the future. I'm only rating it positive because there's no neutral option and I'd feel bad if I gave it a thumbs down.
