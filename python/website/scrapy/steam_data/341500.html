About This Game

Inspired by games like Fez, Braid, and Ghosts and Goblins, Camera Obscura takes you on a quest to the top of an ancient tower in a forgotten land.

You are a lone photographer on a desperate search. You’ll attempt to summit a ruined tower – a relic of a fanatical sect trying to reach the sun. Face off against dangerous animals, crumbling architecture, and perilous pitfalls.

Camera Obscura takes the beloved platforming gameplay from the 80’s and 90’s and adds an innovative "afterimage" mechanic that allows you to clone the world around you in order to span gaps, create staircases, and freeze moving platforms in place.

As you climb the tower’s 57 stories, you’ll also use this power to crush deadly monsters between real world objects and the ones you’ve cloned yourself.

Key Features


  • Custom level editor. Make your own levels with the included editor, play them, share them with friends! No programming required, just point and click to create platforms, enemies, switches, and doors. Share packs of levels on the Steam Workshop!

  • Original soundtrack. Listen to 11 beautifully-composed tracks

  • Multiple language support, achievements, multiple endings, and more! [/list]

Basic Information
Title: Camera Obscura
Developer: Anteater Games
Publisher: EQ-Games, Anteater Games
Genre: Classic style platformer

General Impression
Camera Obscura (“Dark Chamber” in Latin) is a 2D platformer that pays homage to old school 2D platformers but also tries to innovate with a pretty interesting gameplay mechanic. The game’s story revolves around the quest of photographer to climb the ruins of a tower. And the innovation comes in the manipulation of the levels themselves in a similar manner to Fez. But the afterimage effect is more than just a gimmick and it works very well with the storyline. It is not an easy game and you will be restarting levels often enough. The soundtrack is surprisingly good for a platformer and the introduction of Achievements and Trading Cards make Camera Obscura, a nice addition to any collection.

Strong Points
+ Clever gameplay mechanics.
+ Steam Trading Cards & Achievements.

Weak Points
- Quite difficult.
- Sparse save checkpoints.

Rating 7/10

I really don't understand why this game has been given so much praise. I personally cannot recommend this game ( besides if you are looking to try out new/ different types of platformer games or if you're wanting cheap steam trading cards to level up your steam account) for the following reasons:

- The 'unique' mechanic of creating temporary after-images of solid objects by flashing your camera. I find this to be interesting as i've never played a game with this sort of system before however as it's you're only skill and there's no way to boost yourself during levels or even the game game it gets dull and repetative fast. Despite the mechanic being unique and different the game doesn't really offer much else

- Controls are floaty. they don't work as they should and when they do they also start to vary which becomes frustrating.
Most of the difficulty comes from the fact that controls are imprecise. Making it more frustating than difficult anyway.
- Puzzles are dull. By this I mean they can all be beaten in basically the exact same way which gets very boring after about half an hour or so
- There is rarely any checkpoints in higher levels where the level is considerably longer, messing up on a long jump section, or killed by a slime at the end of it? Repeat from the beginning or if you misclick one of the controlls you fall through the level and usually have to start again too.
- the music is average but also gets annoying after a period of time
- the graphics aren't anything special, it doens't look amazing and more like a 2001 game, pretty simple and basic.

4/10
