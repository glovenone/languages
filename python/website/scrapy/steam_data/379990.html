Premise and artwork are fine, but the game lets itself down due to laziness/incompetence from the developers.

Firstly it's a console port so this should immediately cause most Steam users to quickly dismiss this game - it's a very bad port, too.

While you're given the option to change resolution, the game overrides your decision and sets a different one. Terrible. So while you can choose a modern resolution like 4K, the game won't go there.

Controls are terrible - the developers weren't even aware that PC gamers use WASD for direction keys - that's how woeful this port is. Movement therefore is equally clunky and hard to control because of the worst of breed console heritage.

Avoid this one. Developers deserve none of your money for taking a failed console game and porting it to PC for a quick and desperate money grab.

I hate to give a thumbs down but...

I was enjoying it in the beginning so much, but then it just went downhill from there... experiencing bugs, difficult maneuvering, confusion over controls... here's the gist of what I think:

Storyline: It had potential. I was very hooked on what was going on and why things were happening the way they were, but there no progression to the story. The little girl (whose name I still don't know) walks around fighting off barrel monsters, flying books, and suitcase things, but this portion of the game did not tell me what the heck was happening!
Also, the game was SO SHORT... If I evaded all monsters and progressed through the story, it honestly could be finished in half an hour, if not less. Worth $10?!
Rating: 6/10

August 6 update: The little girl's name can now be found by interacting with a few of the kids in the beginning.

Gameplay/controls: DIFFICULT. The developer is adding an update tomorrow (July 31) on an improvement for controls, but both keyboard and joystick (I used a ps3 controller) modes were not the greatest. I'd say keyboard mode would be easier overall, since you can only use one analog stick to move AND look around. However, you can't LOOK around when you're using the keyboard mode (at least, I didn't find a way to). There were a few objects in-game that you could simply walk right through, so there's a few bugs there as well.
---Fighting monsters: There is not enough area of effect for your attacks! You have to get RIGHT in the monster's face to deal damage, while their AOE is noticeably larger. The flying books were also a huge nuisance. Maybe it was my style of attacking, but I got hit at least once every time I attempted to knock down those monsters.
Rating: 3/10

Graphics: I LOVED THE ANIMATIONS, especially the cutscenes, it was beautiful! The gameplay graphics were okay, but there's nothing I would call out and say "that sucked". This is probably the best feature of this game.
Rating: 8/10

Overall: Needs quite a bit of work. I'm aware the game will continue later on (PLEASE DO NOT CHARGE MONEY FOR THE NEXT PART...), but I think the gameplay needs severe work and the story needs to develop more. Why is this happening, who am I controlling, WHERE AM I GOING?!
I would say try it out for yourself if you don't think this review is helpful, but I don't think it's worth $10 for a game that could realistically be finished in half an hour or less.

This is about 5.7/10 for me.

Was this game tested before release? Did the devs have fun when they played the game?

The art is brilliant and I like the mood/setting of the game. This has loads of potential, but the combat mechanics aren't good at all. Once you master a repetitive dodge+slash combo, you can see how it could be a decent challenge, but the poor combination of map layout and mob placement just makes the game totally unfair. I've had mobs trap me against objects, mobs hitting me while hiding in the dark... Also, the way the two mechanics (dark+combat) are presented just make the game really difficult before you saw enough of the game's plot to decide to try to go through it. The "afraid of the dark" mechanic was vey interesting and I thought I was gonna be shown some cool level design that made a challenge out of that, but instead I just got thrown pairs of mobs that make loads of damage while flailing their arms around.

I don't think this game was beta tested. and as a result the combat ain't refined at all. It's a shame, because the art was brilliant and it made me curious about the world I had just stepped in. Best of luck to the devs, and I hope you can still make the best out of this!
