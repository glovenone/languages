About This Game

Imagine waking up on a small dock with no recollection of who you are or how you got there. The first person you find is a grumpy, old man who enlists you to work on his ferry boat which seems to have materialized behind you. But not all is as it seems on this strange ship, and the people you meet may know more about your past than they let on.

Port of Call is a narrative exploration game that focuses on telling the player an immersive story. The game is an experiment in short-form storytelling in an immersive media. Interact with the ship's passengers and explore your surroundings in first person to try to uncover the secret of your past.

Good:

- The atmosphere was neat. It was effectively moody and there were a few things in the beginning that made me jump.

Bad:

- (Spoilers) I was able to figure out the story in the first five minutes. In the end, the story is just given to you through a monologue from a character that was just a bit too creatively pretentious. In the end I'm not entirely sure what the point of it all was. What did the character learn? His identity? Based on the choice in the end he was going to learn either way who he was.

On that note, it seemed like everything was being built up to reveal that the main character had in fact done something horrible, but in the end, it's just revealed that you didn't... I guess. As in, you remained passive your entire life. And in the end you get to finally make a choice. A choice that seemed mostly arbitrary and useless, because he either gets everything he wants, or... he doesn't. For no reason. Was it entirely necessary to make him forget who he was for thirty minutes just to make that choice?

tl;dr - Solid atmosphere but the story was a bit too pointless and pretentious for my taste.

This is a very short, but interesting little game. You wake up at a pier, not knowing who or where you are. The old man invites you to enter his boat, and then an original story unfolds. The dialogue is very well written, and the story drew me very easy into this game. Which proved necessary in order to get real enjoyment, since neither the graphics nor the audio is up to the level of the story. At the end the game left me in disarry for a moment, since it is rather grim and unsettling.

But since this is a free game, there's no reason not to recommend it. Furthermore, it comes with seven achievements which can be unlocked easily, so for anyone looking for 100% games this is a must. Do play it twice: the story made much more sense to me on my second playthrough, and the game is really short.

Recommend to everyone interested in something slightly different

A short but very enjoyable game. It has a cut down, almost simplistic, art style that works really well with the subject matter and gives the game a distinctive identity. The story in good enough to keep you engaged. Anyone familiar with greek mythology will pick up on what's going on almost immediately. But it's a fun story either way. While not getting too serious the themes and message that the game delivers(depending on how much you read into or think about it in the end) are well worth considering also.

You'll spend most of your time talking to the other passangers on a boat, trying to piece together the story of who you are and how you got to be on this mysterious river boat. Collecting tickets and trying your hand at barkeeping along the way.

There are a few easy achievements in it for achievement hunter. The game should last 30 to 60 mins, and it's free. So a very good way to waste a little time. Just don't expect fireworks or explosions.

I have to admit that the game feels almost like a proof of concept. If that is the case then it is a very successful one and I'd be interested to see what else the developer has to offer.

Let me start by saying that when I first saw this, I didn't want to play it due to the horror tag and the trailer. When I saw that it had achievements for a free game, I thought to give it a shot (since it was short). I was honestly ready to write a negative review right off the bat. I was wrong.

Though this game is short (and with a rather cliche story), it is still a well-told story that is to be enjoyed. To ease the fears of those who dread horror games, this game is very tame. Creepy...yet tame. No screamers. No gore. Only a feeling of discomfort in your own mind, yet not terrifyingly bad. Just creepy enough to catch your attention.

Pros:
Free
Achievements
A nice tale to be told
A nice cast of interesting characters

Cons:
Story can be a little overtold. (A euphemism of death rowing his boat on the river Styx)
A little too short.


It is also another "choose your own ending" game, but it's based on personal preference after everything you've experienced. Me? I'll admit that when it came to life or death, I chose death. (Though I'd normally choose life, circumstances led me to believe life would be more painful)

If this was longer and if more work went into it, I could see this thing becoming something great. I wish there was an epilogue or sequel for those taking the "Death Door".
