About This Game

Cartown racers, start your engines!

Putt-Putt® and Pep are ready to take your kids on the ride of their lives! Today's the big day as our curious little convertible prepares for the Cartown 500. Redline Rick is eager to start the race, but Putt-Putt® and Pep need a few items before they are ready to roll. Kids join in the adventure as they explore brand-new Cartown locations like Torvil Tractor's farm and Rover's Rocket Drive-in, looking for a safety helmet for Pep. Old friends like Smokey the Fire Engine and Outback Al help Putt-Putt® get to the track in time for the big race. As the grand finale, kids hop behind the wheel for the first time ever as they race their favorite purple car toward the checkered flag at the Cartown Speedway!

  • Kids actually drive Putt-Putt® as he races the speedway!
  • A fantastic original soundtrack will have your kids dancing in their seats!
  • There are hundreds and hundreds of the fun Click Points children love!
  • Out of time? You can save the game and finish it later.

Reesington's Review of Put-Put Enters the Race

Synopsis

A sentient automobile known as Put-Put prepares for a race with the help of his trusty canine companion in this point-and-click adventure.

The Good

Most importantly, the pointing and clicking controls are spot-on. I was truly impressed by the way the cursor changes to elucidate the direction in which Put-Put will go if one were to hover over a given path. Point-and-click franchise contenders such as Diablo have yet to implement a feature like this. Further, Put-Put's telekinetic power is a great complement to this control system wherein he can manipulate almost any part of any scene by summoning wild animals with seeming druidic magicks. Note, however, that there is another disturbing way in which Put-Put and other automobiles manipulate the environment as discussed later under The Absurd.

As for the graphics, the anti-aliasing and static shadows are on par with that of any Bethesda title. Also, the gritty, high-contrast delivery, while not cell-shaded, offers up Put-Put Enters the Race as a starkly obvious precursor to the Borderlands style of not trying, but making stuff look cool through the overuse of persistent Photoshop-esque filters. All in all, my best guess is that the graphic artists behind this game went on to work for and inspire many AAA game companies.

In a similar line of thought, the narrative is ambiguously revealing of Put-Put's inner quest. To the point, much of Put-Put's world is implied rather than directly told to the player, hence the lack of cutscenes. Thus, Put-Put Enters the Race is clearly the spiritual predecessor to Demon's Souls, which eventually led to Dark Souls, and later to Bloodborne. There is no doubt to me that From Software reverse engineered the storytelling acumen of this game to go on to create the greatest non-stories in video games of all time, minimalism at its best.

The Bad

Unfortunately, the lack of musicianship in the game detracts from the immersion big time, especially considering the use of the many royalty-free sound effects. As any professional gamer knows, the audio is what ties a game together at the end of the day. The developers could have taken a hint from Nintendo here, who, just through leveraging MIDI music, mind-controlled several generations of Americans. What young adult hasn't dreamed in the Mario or Pokemon theme? But, rather than getting too upset about this, I elected to replace the default music and sound effects with that of Tyler the Creator, whose style happened to somehow perfectly mesh with this game.

And, no DualShock support? What's with that?

The Absurd

For whatever reason, all of the automobiles have tentacles that emerge from their undercarriage. What's more, Redline Rick is ... how do I put this? He's "grabby" around Put-Put.... Take that for what you will. Piling up in the bizarre category, high-octane gasoline is free and available at any gas station. Put-Put seems compulsive and frantic about this stuff, uncontrollably shaking after consumption. When I noticed that Put-Put stores his canine companion in his glove compartment, along with batteries and a ladder, I realized what was really going on.

Put-Put is a hallucinatory addict, and most of what he experiences is probably not real due to his overconsumption of high-octane gasoline. This implication is strongly hinted when Put-Put begins finding empty bottles everywhere that he wishes to take a recycling center. The bottles represent his unspoken habit, and the recycling center represents his yearning to break free from his wrongdoings. Ultimately, upon proper reflection, the absurdity of the game is revealed as a masquerade for a deep life lesson otherwise kept away from children. Ultimately, the developers of Put-Put Enters the Race introduce the harsh realities of life to youth by subverting first-glance parental supervision in the same vein as SpongeBob SquarePants.

8/10
