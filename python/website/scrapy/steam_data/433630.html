Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“In this initial release of vrAMP, there is a lot to do! We would like to implement more features before our full launch. We would appreciate community feedback every step of the way.

We take our goal of creating the best VR music experience seriously, and it's going to be an awesome ride for everyone!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We are expecting to have the first full release by Christmas 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Currently, the Early Access version has the core features implemented. Through feedback we plan to refine the user interface, controls, beat algorithm, and other basic features.

Our plans for the future...

  • Ability to browse and play your local files in vrAMP

  • User customization of some visualizations
    We would like to add the ability for you to set the colors of particles or other effects that surround you, in addition to our current smoke and particle intensity customization.

  • Destinations/different scenes to experience your music in
    A more long-term goal is the addition of different rooms and scenes to walk (dance!) around in, or more relaxing environments for seated sessions.

  • Glowsticks!
    Dance like no one is watching!
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“It has a built-in library of hours of music (almost 1GB!).

You can pause the music and play your own in any program on your computer, including the Steam in-game web browser.

vrAMP is an awesome music visualizer with a beat algorithm that we continue to iterate and improve upon.

Toggles for the intensity of particle and smoke effects”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“After Early Access, the price will increase. Though the final price is as of yet undecided.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will work with the community of virtual reality enthusiasts to drive the process for this game beyond our initial plans. Feature requests, suggestions, and bug reports will be a large part of vrAMP as development progresses.

If you want something, say something! :)”

About This Game

vrAMP is the definitive music visualizer and player for virtual reality.


No need to move your music into yet another media player, vrAMP plays music from any program or web browser. You can even use the in-game Steam web-browser to play music. Simply pause the included catalog of music and begin playing your own.

Future Updates:

  • Musical Painting (currently available in beta)
  • Browse your own music collection from inside vrAMP
  • New destinations to feel and experience your music
  • Added visualization effects

Controls:

  • Center - Play/Stop
  • Left - Previous song
  • Right - Next song
  • Down - Toggles effect transitions
    By default vrAMP will transition itself with changes to the music. You can press Down to freeze the transitions and keep the effect, pressing it again will resume default behavior.

Notes:

Make sure you have your default playback device (in Windows audio settings) set to the HTC Vive / Oculus Rift.

Music provided by:

NoCopyrightSounds https://www.youtube.com/user/NoCopyrightSounds

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: Intel Core i5 or equivalent
    • Memory: 8000 MB RAM
    • Graphics: GTX 970 or equivalent
    • DirectX: Version 10
    • Storage: 1024 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: HTC Vive or Oculus Rift Required

Product received for free
I have discribed this to many as follows "Old school winamp visualizer in VR"
I did get this for free as a review and test so keep that in mind.
vrAMP Is not a game. Its a audio visualizer and designed to be used with any audio you chose. Its also a work in progress. Like many VR programs out currently there is much to be done. Visualizers to date are not used as a platform nor a program for other things to happen. I would suggest looking else if that's what you want.

currently vrAMP does its one thing very well. Visualizing your music so you can blissfully relax in a encapsulating environment while your music takes you away to places unknown. The developer has done a wonderful job of adding updates in a regular schedule. Features are being added regularly and that's something to pay attention to in these early days of VR. To many devs are making a cash grab in the new VR market offering "almost finished" products with no updates. vrAmp makes it clear upfront there is work to be done.

So lets see now on to the Pro/Con

Pro

- Visualizes all audio output from PC not just a single player.

- Updates are plentiful and regular.

- Supports Rift and Vive

- Supports Vive controllers

- does exactly what it claims.

Cons

- Its not free (Not really a con as we should pay for hard work and assets)

- its not a finished product.

- it wont make me waffles. I tried and its a no go.
