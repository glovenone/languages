About This Game

The internet-born and beloved countryballs comics finally have a game to properly represent them, Polandball: Can Into Space! Break through Earth's atmosphere to prove other countryballs once and for all they were wrong and conquer the final frontier!

Watch Polandball at Comic Con 2016:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1Byk6HA-XPg

Key Features:

  • Visually representative of the original comics, with an extra touch of style and care, keeping their core as authentic as possible.
  • 56 items to upgrade Polandball's rocket
  • 33 attributes to enhance your rocket
  • 25 countryballs with different behavior and AI
  • Learn about each country
  • Collect coins, fuel, burgers and magnets to last longer
  • Simple, yet effective controls
  • Amazing End Movie for fans who finish the game

Check our official website for more information and merchandise:

http://alienpixelstudios.com/polandball/

Polandball can finally of into own game.

Polandball: Can into Space! is a very simple yet fun game. It's gameplay primarily consists of launching Poland into space, avoiding other countries, (or sometimes purposefully running into them), and upgrading your rocket.

The game isn't really special. There's nothing too unique about it besides the fact that it's Polandball. And clearly, there are no other Polandball games on Steam.

And the one thing that makes this game unique is interesting because the fact that this game is about Polandball is a massive flaw.

There's one question I often ask myself while thinking of this game. "Is this supposed to be a normal game with Polandball features, or a game that commits itself to the entire Polandball series?"

Answer one is correct.

This game is good and stands fine on it's own, however, eh... it's just not worthy of the title "the only Polandball game on Steam."

Regardless, I find it fun and enjoyable to play this game and likely won't stop any time soon. The graphics and artwork are great (regardless of it breaking the whole circle tool rule of Polandball, however, I actually hate that rule) the story is practically useless to the actual game as a whole, but it's still fine, and overall i'd recommend this game to anyone who likes games like this and may be interested in Polandball or any Polandball fans.

Overall, i'd give Polandball: Can into Space! a 7/10.

The following reflects opinions prior to Polandball: Can Into Space's Patch 1.02.

In essence, Polandball: Can Into Space! is merely a case of getting from point A to point B with barely any deviation; in it’s own quirky way though, it manages to engage the casual gamer sufficiently for them to return for multiple attempts at beating their own best distance en route to the moon.

However it is when one looks beyond this layer of casual frolicking, as repeated failure and subsequent frustrations will cause one to do, does the game truly unveil a host of inconsistencies and flaws in its gameplay.

CONS

- Repetitive Countryballs behaviour and dialogues.
- Repetitive Music and Sound Design that despite the recent patch to diversify the game's solo tune, still forces players to turn their volumes off eventually.
- Friendly Countryballs do not function in providing one with a boost like they must, due to a bug.
- Attributes/Upgrades is a complicated business that doesn't justify why one is needed in the presence of another.
- Forces you to sit through all of 384,400Km despite upgrading boosters, drag and weight parameters making you wonder of their relevance.
- Stops randomly generating fuel, health and coins when the booster is used from the get-go or even in cases when one is running out of fuel or health; highly inconsistent.
- Abrupt Ending

PROS

- CONS 3, 4 and 6 are to be fixed in the game's next update as per the developers; The Attributes/Upgrades will be renamed to Perks/Parts with a more explanatory description system inserted to describe their functions.
- Clean yet imaginative visual design of rocket parts, weather and countryballs.
- Demands quick reflexes, prudence and focus in navigating through the focus especially during the latter stages of the game; simply gunning the rocket's thrusters from the launch pad gets one nowhere.
- Strange sense of accomplishment to be derived.
- Can be picked up and played by anyone regardless of their familiarity with the Countryballs universe.
- The developers also tease the onset of new missions, countryballs, dialogues, diverse behaviours and further proliferation of the PC version provided there's 'user interest'. Voice your suggestions, community!

This review has been a condensed version of the analysis provided by the Higher Eclectic Ground Network for independent Video Game talent, of which Polandball: Can Into Space! and its developers have been a part. View it in its entirety by reading 'Getting Polandball To The Moon' at - http://www.highereg.com/getting-polandball-to-the-moon/

Curator Pixel - Pursuing objectivity with a critical eye

Based on a popular series of user created web comics and within a market that sees a constant flow of games packed with memes and pop culture references Polandball: Can into Space! shows no hesitation in being a smaller independent title that caters to a specific audience.

Presentation
Visually the game offers a cleaner style compared to the internet created comics, this is a change that makes the game easier to look at while playing, as the mouse-drawn nature of the countryball comics could have resulted in an fuzzy experience when objects are set in motion. Sound effects are basic but very well done and the game is orchestrated with a single looping song, which can get repetitive after a while but it can easily be turned off in the game's settings.

Plot
The plot of the game is based on the running gag that "Polandball cannot into space" which originated from the Polandball series of user-generated web comics. Tired of all the bullying by other countryballs, Poland decided that it had enough and proceeds to attempt the difficult task of reaching humanity's final frontier, space.

The basic storyline is accompanied by the game's humor, which makes fun of a variety of stereotypes from countries worldwide. Countryballs display each country's respective cultural attire and technological stereotypes while delivering one-liners in deliberately broken English, this can also be found throughout the upgrades menu, where each item description makes fun of a particular country in the game.

Interface/Controls
The game's interface is very simple, big buttons flaunt all over over the game's main menu which quickly disappear as soon as we lift off, during gameplay the game presents us with a clean but effective HUD that displays the radar located on the top left side of the screen and the health, fuel, money and kilometrage on the top right.

The controls are easy to comprehend and feel very responsive even with the ship's starting gear, by default you can control your ship with either WASD, the arrow keys or the mouse, the player can also make use of a gamepad, controls can be rebinded in the launcher, but this has no option to revert to default.

Gameplay
The main goal of the game is to reach the Moon, in order to achieve this we need to avoid enemy countryballs that are constantly roaming around, increasing in variety and difficulty the closer we get to our final destination. At the same time the player needs to be on the lookout for fuel and money, Polandball will be forced to eject if our ship runs out of fuel, after that, we will be taken back to our launchpad for another try.

In order to facilitate achieving our goal, money can be spent on buying upgrades for our ship, and, while this offers some degree of flexibility, it quickly becomes apparent that the system is somewhat linear and gives an illusion of progression, perhaps it would have been more interesting to have branching paths in the upgrade system, as this would give more meaningful choices to the game as well as some replayability (which is virtually non-existent), there's no real mechanical reason for replaying this game, it is ultimately in the hands of the player if they wish to delete all their progress and start over from scratch.

Polandball: Can into Space! Is a game that can either get you hooked or completely bored within a few hours of playing, there is a fair amount of randomness involved in each run that can make it frustrating for some, while others will see this as an excuse to keep trying. Regardless, the game rewards determination, reaching new distances can be a very fulfilling experience, this is enough to keep that type of player interested long enough to complete the game.

Conclusion
Polandball: Can into Space! is a fun experience that can be enjoyed by a wide range of people, players who already possess knowledge of the countryball comics and are up to date with the related stereotypes, would find this game a pleasant experience all the way through. With that being said, the game has a few shortcomings in the audio department and the customization aspect, plus the random nature of the game could be perceived as frustrating to many; however, these do not detract a lot from what this game is supposed to be, a fun lighthearted trip that leaves a subtle message of determination to prove to the world that, against all odds, Polandball can reach space.
