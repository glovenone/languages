About This Game

Assassin’s Creed® Unity is an action/adventure game set in the city of Paris during one of its darkest hours, the French Revolution. Take ownership of the story by customising Arno's equipement to make the experience unique to you, both visually and mechanically. In addition to an epic single-player experience, Assassin’s Creed Unity delivers the excitement of playing with up to three friends through online cooperative gameplay in specific missions. Throughout the game, take part in one of the most pivotal moments of French history in a compelling storyline and a breath-taking playground that brought you the city of lights of today.

Basically I'm not sure how you would even be able to tell but, if you can run this game and you are already a fan of the AC series, I definitely recommend picking this one up.

The fans wanted realism, and Ubisoft definitely delivered. The graphics are superb with the GTX 970 I have, and it's very immersive for me, everything seems accurate, the architecture, the population density, even the fighting. The gameplay itself is one of the best i've seen in a long time. I don't recommend this game for reasons other than most other reviews i have seen, while i absolutely am a fan of the difficult fighting consisting of parries, rolls, and blocks it does sometimes seem to get in the way as you have to fight long and hard to battle the groups of enemies.

The thing that really turned me away was the story as well as the amount of work required to get 100% completion (which i am still working toward). While ubisoft went all out rendering a huge, accurate, and graphically demanding game, they failed to remember that there has to be a meaningful story. The workers at ubisoft gave us Arno in hopes of triggering the great times many of us had as we worked through Ezio's story before. It seems some crucial parts of Ezio's behavior were forgotten, in the story there is no development, Arno has some unrecognizable blood lust, set on destroying everyone with no planning, no strategizing, the sequences again contain only 2 or 3 memories and in each sequence we see arno go out and kill another templar with no understanding of who he killed or what they were doing.

After each murder the typical conversation between the assassin and templar is gone, it is replaced by Arno seeing bits of their memory and then going off to kill another person, for me it led to a very complicated story and i got lost towards the end of the game as i just kept killing more people not understanding what they were doing, why, or the relation it had to my goal. Another reason i like this series is the usual relation it has to the historical events, and yet the designers again just forgot about this aspect, the French revolution has been brushed back more as an extra feature that's like background music than it is a driving force for the story.

The collectibles are typically also a fun aspect as they add a little extra to the game, but in this game there are too many including; 21 social club missions, 50 paris stories, 14 murder mysteries, 18 "nostradamus enigmas" (riddles), 5 cafe theatre missions, 24 companion missions, 279 chests, 60 artifacts, 128 cockades (little medals around town), 40 syn points (earned in co-op), 23 viewpoints, 12 renovations to purchase, 7 heist (co-op) missions, and 11 normal co-op missions. Were i just playing the story i would be done within 5 hours, although all of these accessories on a map added in with stairs, shops, and such make it impossible to navigate without clearing out the map!

Despite the agonizing time spent on the collectibles, the story did have it's moments as well as a few twists and turns which had hooked me in to continue the story, but I just can't say i enjoyed the journey i was taken down as the story had really lost its main thrill ride of seeing the characters grow and learn.

The present day story is entirely absent (so far, i have 2 more main missions), you never leave the Animus sean and rebecca and their whole team are talking to you every now and then though to say the Abstergo (not even explained in this game) is trying to stop the assassin's from hacking the network, leading to a definitely creative rift in the game where you visit different time periods including WW2 paris.

ALL in all this game is very realistic, and a lot of fun, but the story has lost its thrill and feels pointless towards the end, especially after trying to finish all 692 side missions and colletibles. I wanted to like this game, it was just too straightforward without enough development on the characters and events taking place.

Ubisoft wants you to:
- buy Helix Points using real money to be able to get the best tier armor and weapons in game. If you don't buy them, you won't get enough money in your playthrough to get them (1 060 000 F and more).
- spend more money to unlock location of collectibles. Remember how you revealed location of collectibles by syncing with view point in previous games? Not anymore! You have to buy side missions/chests/enigmas packages to do this. Or go through the entire city and every street with your eagle vision on.
- spend even more money to buy some premium coop bonuses. Yay!

Pros
+ Graphics. It looks good when maxed out.
+ Animations. Climbing and fighting look so smooth and prettier now.
+ Climbing. Huge improvment. You have 2 different combinations for running (up and down). Your character won't jump somewhere down when you want it to jump up anymore!
+ They improved taking cover behind objects.
+ Added stealth mode. And it seems that enemies do not use tunnel vision anymore. They can spot you more easily when compared to previous installments.
+ I personaly like the talent system. You had to wait to unlock different techniques/items during your gameplay before. Now you can choose when to unlock them and which one you want. I don't see much difference and don't understand the complaints on this matter. I'd say it's an improvment.
+ A lot of collectibles and side missions (including new types of missions) for those who like doing them.
+ Coop missions. If you don't have anyone to play with, you can play them on your own. Open your progress tracker in menu, select coop mision you would like to play and choose a Private match. :)

Cons
- Glitches and minor performance issues. Even after several patches, the game still feels kind of broken. [more in Conclusion section]
- Removal of human shield. You can't use enemies as human shield anymore. You have to roll around (running away won't help) to avoid being shot.
- Fast walking was removed.
- Gentle push was removed.
- Whistle was removed.
- Eagle vision now has cooldown.
- Too much distance-visibility-lowering fog.


Conclusion
When it comes to story and gameplay, it's a good Assassin's Creed game. Ubisoft made some bad decisions, though.

They wanted to create a whole new engine to support the "next gen" graphics, add a lot of side activities and rework fighting and climbing animations. That's good, but they also wanted to keep the "1 new major Assassin's Creed game every year" tradition. And that's the problem. Even with studio this big, you can't finish and polish all this stuff in a year (not even a whole year, because they spent some time patching previous installment). I believe that if they took more time to polish it (fix tons of glitches, optimize it properly, ...), it would be a much better experience. Sadly, that did not happen.

What ruins this game even more are microtransactions (described at the beginning).

If you ask a question "Is it patched yet?", then answer is: "Nope." Yes, they released some patches that were supposed to fix bugs and performance issues. They made some major improvments, but it was not enough. During your playthrough, you will encounter with some minor FPS drops and some other problems.

When it comes to glitches, I've encountered 5 major ones, though they happen VERY RARELY.
- Camera got stuck in a weird position after performing leap of faith and killing enemy from a haystack.
- Arno forgot what he's supposed to do during a fight. He refused to attack. parry and roll. I fixed it by running away and leaving combat state.
- Double assassination sometimes doesn't work. Even if your enemies were hugging, Arno would refuse to perform double assassination. He would instead kill one of them and let the other one to draw attention of other +-20 enemies around. How great is that?
- Same goes for air assassination. Arno sometime refuses to perform it, even though he's close enough and there are no obstacles. You would not believe how many times this glitch ♥♥♥♥ed up my stealth.
- Some enemies will notice you, even though you're sneaking behind their back. I suspect them of having 6th sense or a 3rd eye at the back of their head.



Buy it if you really do not wish to miss the story line, have a strong enough computer and can live with the problems this game has. Or if you want to play with your mates.


I have not experienced any crashes during my playthrough.
6/10

There are some wonderful aspects to Unity, particularly in the environment and the detail that was put in the world. But there are countless annoyances throughout this game, and numerous problems and bugs that still exist. Optimization is still iffy, with framerate drops. There are countless graphical bugs throughout, from NPCs popping into existence 3 feet in front of you to thin white lines visible between some textures.

The biggest problem with the game for me, however, is that it's just not fun. The camera is clunky and often gets caught up on the environment. The combat itself can be fun and exciting in smaller groups, but is frustrating when you're facing against large groups. I've found myself mashing a button to drop a smoke grenade, which Arno would ignore, to attacks going through enemies without affecting them, to numerous gameplay mechanics which do not make an ounce of sense to me.

One of the problems throughout the AC series has been feature creep, but this game reverses that, to a negative effect. Enemies with guns are much more annoying as there is no effective way to dodge them. You can no longer grab another enemy and use them as a human shield (and in fact their bullets seem to go through their comrades and hit you anyway without a problem, which is particularly frustrating when you're surrounded by 10 guards and 4 of them are pulling out pistols), and your dodge move only works when you're near another enemy, not when you're trying to run towards a rifleman. The damage higher level snipers inflict is also riduculous.

Counter-attacks and parries that cause damage or instantly kill the enemy are out, as are kill streaks and combos. And under other circumstances, I would be alright with that, as previous games have often had straight-up combat being easier and much quicker than stealth. However, that combined with the other changes to the combat system just turns combat into a frustrating annoyance.

The changes to the stealth system is also similarly frustrating. In an attempt to make the game more difficult and challenging, the developers made it annoying. Hiding in crowds seems a lot more finicky, there's no tall grass to hide in, and you can't whistle to attract enemies. If you want to attract one enemy away from the sight of others, you have to stand in his line of sight and spin in place for a while until he notices you and decides to investigate.

Speaking of noticing and investigating, the co-op missions are also particularly awful. In order to increase the difficulty and encourage you to play them with multiple people (though you can play them solo), the enemies are horribly buffed, particularly in noticing you. You will be expected to sneak into a palace which has three guards at each entrance and overlapping snipers which have to be taken out at the same time or they will spot you. And then they will spot you on the other side of the street and immediately become suspicious and start shooting at you as well.

The storyline of the game is...mediocre. Nothing too special, though a bit more of a stronger personal story than previous AC games. However, I was disappointed with the treatment of the French Revolution. As with a lot of AC games, the actual historical events are often poorly explained or depicted, can be often played out of order, and they expect you to already know everything or just go with the flow. Not to mention the fact that the main character is locked in a prison during the lead-up and beginning of the revolution itself is horribly disappointing. You don't see a gradual transformation, you don't see people becoming more and more riled up and fighting back. It's just a cutscene and now it's the Republic, with the Reign of Terror, and that's it.

All in all, Unity is not horrible. It's a AAA game with a lot of resources put into it, but it's horribly disappointing compared to other AC games, and in an attempt to make the game more of a challenge, it was just made frustrating instead.
