About This Game

Meeting Semyon, the game's main character, you would've never paid attention to him. Just an ordinary young man with thousands, even hundreds of thousands of those like him in every ordinary city. But one day something completely unusual happens to him: he falls asleep in a bus in winter and wakes up... in the middle of a hot summer. In front of him is "Sovyonok" - a pioneer camp, behind him is his former life. To understand what happened to him, Semyon will have to get to know the local inhabitants (and maybe even find love), find his way in the complex labyrinth of human relationships and his own problems and solve the camp's mysteries. And answer the main question - how to come back? Should he come back?

Everlasting Summer is a VN that takes you (Semyon) to Camp Sovionok while you are sleeping. The soundtrack is well made. Fitting the music to the scenery and the situation. IMHO its very nice and calming.

If your a fan of VNs or looking for something to fill up your library this game is great! The fact that its free just gives it another positive point.

Given that it's free, the real question is if it's worth the time to try it out, and I'd have to say definitely! It appears that whoever wrote out the script isn't a native English speaker, and there are quite a few instances that you can see that, but it never felt so bad that you can't tell what the meaning is supposed to be. Also, It's a little slow to start as the story sets itself up, but you really get a feel for what the main character is thinking and feeling. The more you play and go on, and the more you go try to get different paths, the more clues you get as to what the heck is happening, and the questions that get raised eventually get answered. It starts off slow, but the further you go, the more you get sucked in.

This game was a lot more... versatile than I thought it would be. Some parts are comedic and playful, some parts are quite dark, and there's a wide variety of thoughts and emotions that you can think and feel as you try to see everything the game has to offer. I think I have about 15 hours on record, but that's going one playthough completely blind, and then using a guide to try to get the rest of the paths and achievements. Definitely would recommend giving it a try!
