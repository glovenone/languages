About This Game

Set in the 90's, Kathy Rain tells the story of a strong-willed journalism major who has to come to terms with her own troubled past as she investigates the mysterious death of her recently deceased grandfather. Armed with her motorcycle, a pack of cigs, and a notepad, Kathy begins to delve into a local mystery surrounding her hometown that will take her on a harrowing journey full of emotional and personal turmoil.

As she follows a trail of clues he left behind, questions emerge… What was Joseph Rain really looking for that night all those years ago? What turned him into a mere shell of a man, confined to a wheelchair? What secret did a suicidal young artist take with her to the grave, and why are so many people in Conwell Springs going mad? The truth is dark and sinister…

  • 320x240 (4:3) resolution with crisp pixel art graphics
  • 4,000+ lines of dialogue
  • Full English voice acting directed by Wadjet Eye Games' Dave Gilbert
  • Original soundtrack
  • 40+ hand-drawn environments
  • Compelling narrative with eerie plot set in the 90's

3 1/2 out of 5 stars.

For most of the way, I was leaning towards 3. But the sheer balls of the ending in which they went all out with the creepiness (for better or worse) earned them that extra bit.

I can understand why some people are ambivilant about that aforementioned ending. No, it's not a problem that the ending is "supernatural." (Kind of a silly complaint, as it was clear from pretty early on that this was a supernatural plot. Not a Scooby Doo type of thing.)

However, what some people might not be prepared for is how fully the game descends (literally) into all out surreal horror. (It's not completely out of left field. I mean, the game had been gradually getting creepier and weirder, especially when Kathy visits that guy in the mental institution and he starts babbling about conduits to Hell.)

I'm talking specifically about the extended sequence at the end that brings to mind "Hellraiser 2" or "In the Mouth of Madness." Gone are the familiar, comfortable touchstones of the plot, and we as the player are now stuck with our main character in a claustrophobic version of her own personal hell. In fact, it's so well done that it might hurt the enjoyability factor of the game as it veers into genuine unpleasantness. I mean, it gets dark, people. (If you were hoping for a Monkey Island knockoff, this is a bad thing. If you were hoping for a game that's unsettling, then I guess it's a good thing.)

The plot, thankfully, does resolve itself at the end. Sort of. Even if I wasn't entirely clear on what it all meant, I still felt some closure. And it made me curious--if this game is successful, how would they approach a sequel? I mean, Kathy is not a typical mystery-solver in this game. She's not a gumshoe who goes looking for crimes to solve. (Such a premise would easily lend itself to more adventures.) No. This story, rather, was entirely predicated on a very specific supernatural problem having to do with her hometown. So how could such a scenario happen again? Perhaps future Kathy adventures would have to be tied directly with her hometown and the "Crimson One" character introduced in this game.

A bit short, but a very nice "oldschool" adventure experience. The visuals and references to the world of 1995 (floppy disks! wav-files!) made me feel nostalgic, although the game's modern roots are evident in its good voice-acting throughout the story.

I highly enjoyed the protagonist and would love to see her and her quasi-sidekick return in a sequel!

Pros:
+ compelling detective story unfolding and going deeper as you play, almost X-Files style towards the end
+ great character design; the protagonist's cheeky/rebellious personality results in plenty of humor
+ good voice-acting supporting story developments and banter between the characters

Cons:
- a bit short for its price, compared to other games (but still worth it!)
- some very few puzzles are a bit unintuitive (had to resort to a walkthrough once)
