About This Game

Behold an anime-themed land of wonders, friendship and skill, shrouded in the mysteries of an ancient world yours to explore. Forge relationships and find new friends, take up a job, or soar through the majestic skies as you rediscover a social MMORPG beloved by many around the world!

It is a time of myth and legend, where great heroes rise up to fight side by side, united against the instruments of destruction . Journey to a world linked by fate to another, a mirrored reflection of the chaos that has erupted within mankind. Reach for your destiny and become the hero you were meant to be, in Dream of Mirror Online!

Features:

- Anime styled graphics
- Four playable races, including beastman and spirit
- Rich gathering and crafting system
- Many professions to choose from
- Learn as many professions as you'd like
- Swap skills between professions
- Pet system
- Marriage system
- Relationship system: Friends, teacher/student, rival.
- Flying Mounts
- Dedicated in-game chatroom system
- Treasure Hunt Events and more!

Further details:

1. In Domo relationships are a part of the experience, if you so choose. Take on a disciple to train, or find a rival to compete against. Find your love and get married, it's all possible.

2. Nurture and care for your pets if you wish to earn its trust. Don't take it for granted and it will surely be by your side.

3. Take to the skies on swords and magic carpets.

4. You are not limited to a single profession or set of skills, mix them up to your heart's content. Wanna be an assassin doctor? Go for it.

5. Collect resources to craft unique items that you can't buy in the shop. Farming, fishing, herding, meditation, mining and forestry are your choices. Which one suits you?

6. Treasure Hunts: Keep your wits about you and search for treasure in one of our daily events.

7. Character customization: Create a custom anime character with multiple accessory and item slots, to stand out and be unique!

8. Create private chatrooms for yourself and your friends. Talk one-on-one or invite multiple users into your conversations.

Thank you for your time, we hope to see you in-game soon!

Kind Regards,

Suba Games

Alright so I'm gonna list some pros and cons, some of them more of a personal opinion here.

Cons: The text is VERY small, however, this can be fixed by downloading a font called Mingliu, which can be downloaded safely and for free from FontPalace, as I did. Sometimes monsters walk through the walls, though this poses no problem in hitting them and dragging them back out. From my knowledge, you cannot buy perma-costumes from the item mall (but I'm fairly certain I've seen some perma-costumes in player shops, though I don't know how they got them). Lastly, the only way to know how an item is usually priced if you're wanting to sell it in your own shop is to either ask others or check other shops and hope they have it.
Overall I really love the game and its uniqueness, maybe just because of the nostalgia, but I really recommend it, because if you can get past the cons, the game is truly fun and lovable.

Hi, guys.
I decided to write a review because there seems to be a lot of negative comments on the game from people who tried the game for 5 minutes, got frustrated, and quit. Which, coincidentally, frustrates me.

TL;DR: If you can get past the clunky UI and a few bugs, the class sytem and community are amazing.

First things first, this game is one of my favorite games. I have played this game for multiple thousands of hours (before it came out on steam, before this server even existed), so understand that this is coming from someone who knows what they're talking about, but may also be a bit corrupted by my love for it. I will try to be objective, but it is one of my most-played and favorite games.

Pros:
  1. Class System is great, will go into further detail later in the review
  2. Community:The game is by nature very social, when hosted by Aeria was literally called a "Social MMO". Most people tend to be fairly helpful if asked.
  3. Pet System: Pets are cute, and help in combat.
  4. Graphics: Cel-shaded anime-styled graphics have aged well and look good in a stylized way, in my opinion.
  5. Crafting: The crafting/upgrading system is fairly simple after you learn the basics.
  6. Runs on most computers: This game came out in 2005, any computer from the past 5 years should run it easily

Cons
  1. Clunky UI: The UI isn't great. Not even good. Hotkey'd skills are used from the function keys, menus are opened with ctrl+key shortcuts
  2. Slightly Buggy: There are a few bugs in-game, some that should be fixed shortly, some that probably won't be.
  3. Poor Translations: The in-game instructions and NPC dialogue can sometimes be pretty strange, because it was originally Chinese
  4. RNG-loving Host: Suba, the company hosting the game seems to like to use chance boxes.
I would like to go in to a few points I mentioned earlier and expand on them, because bulleted points don't really cover the breadth of them.

Class System
This game comes with 14 classes, you do simple quests to receive the class "qualification", then you can talk to an NPC to change your class. You can have all classes on one character, and they all level individually. Classes have 3 sets of skills, two tend to be active skills the third is always passive skills. Along with these skill sets, classes come with 2 extra open active and passive slots, where you can drag and drop other classes' skills. Multiclass systems are fairly uncommon in general, and I've never found an MMO with a a class system quite this involved and unique.

Community
The easiest way to level for most of the game is in a 6-person team. Along with this, the highest level of content is only available to guild parties. By design, the game is very social, and there tends to be a very strong feeling of community. People will often help newbies, crafting them free armor/weapons or giving them pets.

Clunky UI/Buggy/Translation Issues
For all of these cons, there is a fairly simple explanation. This game was made in 2005, 10 years ago by a Chinese company. Look at the system requirements if you don't believe me. Does that excuse them? Not really, but it does give some context for them. In my experience, though, most people who put a little bit of time in can get past these issues.

If you managed to read this far, congratulations, you've finished my review.

TL;DR: If you can get past the clunky UI and a few bugs, the class sytem and community are amazing.

I hovered over all the menus, but there wasn't a hotbar for spells/abilities, so I have to assume either I hadn't unlocked them or there just aren't any. (I only reached 3, so it's likely I just didn't have any. I just find it odd that there isn't a hotbar at all.)

I don't expect World of Warcraft quality controls, but the above two are a tad bit of a problem for me.

Gameplay-wise, I have to say the game isn't bad. It seems like any other basic MMO and the graphics are simple with decent animations. I don't worry too much about graphics, though, so maybe most wouldn't agree that they're "nice". I think they are.

I ended up relying on clicking to go places because for some reason right-clicking DOES drag the camera, but doesn't hold the cursor in place like you would think.

At first I clicked on the wrong NPC and got a chat bubble above their head and nothing. I thought I was doing something wrong until I remembered I was supposed to talk to some "Grandpa" character. I don't know whose grandfather he is, but not mine.. I think.

After accepting the quest, it seemed to obstruct my view of the chat bubble with its confirmation "Quest Thingy Here Accepted" and I looked past that and moved on.

Then I killed a few piggies (Click and your character moves to them and attacks. Nothing special.) called "Pupu" and turned in their bones for a reward. Whatever reward that was, I am not sure.

My next quest led me to a tailor and I found him by his giant name on the map at the top-right, which is convenient, as there doesn't seem to be any sort of directional quest-tracker (WoW doesn't have this either, which can be dismaying for new players, but you can click the map to find things).

The tailor asked me to collect something from "flying Pupus" but I decided I wasn't going to invest more time in the game. The small window was a big factor, but the controls weren't helping.

End of story? Update your product before releasing it on Steam.

MMOs should constantly try to improve, both technologically and visually (the latter doesn't have to be 60fps Modded Skyrim graphics, just better models or lighting).

------------------------------------------------

Pros
- Nice Graphics
- Smooth Animations
- Functional Gameplay

Cons
- Limited Resolution Options
- No Hotbar
- Minor UI Complaints
- Limited and Unimaginative Race Options (Mage-like Thin Race, Brawny Fighter Race, Child Race, Humans)

Recommend?

I'm going to say no, but I want to say that if any of the complaints I've made seem minor to you, I would actually recommend giving it a shot. I've heard some interesting things about the game (pets that level with you, growing in power and eventually becoming mounts) and combined with the simple anime look, I think the game could be fun if you invest time in it or play with friends.

If you're not data-capped on your internet, there's no reason why you couldn't download the game, give it a whirl, and come up with your own conclusion. I'm just trying to give you a heads-up on what to expect.

Sorry if it seemed like a long read!

Hi All!

I first played DOMO back in 2008 with Aeria and I am really glad Suba gave it an other chance.

I like how the game gives you to choice to solo or party up and keeps both paths viable. The class system is nothing I have ever seen before or since. You can level up all 14 classes on the same character (classes open up at lvl 10), and you can mix and match their skills. You can sub 2 skill groups from other jobs to our current one.

The maps are decent sized but with flying available from lvl 15 and teleports to all cities getting around is easy. For the lazier or richer folks there are the item mall Teleport to Parties or Go Anywheres. If you are bored of murdering creeps hourly events can keep you entertained (quiz show/treasure hunt/pupu darts/flying race etc.) for quite some time.

Pets are an other highlight of the game. Other than they help you fight with each having their strength and weaknesses once you level and evolve them to lvl 61 they can become mounts (both riding and flying). Riding a huge metal bear or flying on a winged cat (my personal favourite) are both great. Or if you so prefer get one of the cute/handsome humanoid pets. ;)

It is true there are technical issues and things the game can improve on. However several of the negatives have a sollution ready if people were willing to look around.
- WASD is available from options
- Screen resolution can be fixed in the game files
- The font the game uses can be downloaded to fix the text issue lot of people has
- Start village is instanced, that is why you do not see anyone, the game is not a ghost town
- While only a few hair colours are available from the character creation you can buy a lot more later from the Item Mall or other players.

Item mall is just what you would expect from a free to play game. XP and loot boosters, luxury items and of course countless cosmetics.

As for end game there are several guild based raids with billboards to show off your times (while pocketing loot) and compete with other guilds. If you are the bloodthirsty kind you can also hop in the Arena for some PvP action (also with billboard). Or, if you are one of the clumsy you can try climbing the death billboard. :)

There are no perfect or bugless games. But if you really want to give DOMO a try there is a way to run it without most of the issues mentioned in reviews. Please keep in mind the game is old, and due to that it cannot race with modern games in a lot of aspects. Should you like it the main concern would be how long the developer (SoftStar) will support such an old game.

If you want some nostalgy, or want to have something to pass time with give it a try.

Name any other game where your cup size is a game mechanic. 10/10
