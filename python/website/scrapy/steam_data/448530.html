About This Game

Square’s Route is the addictive new game of path finding and weed whacking. Solve puzzles to move around each level and match colours to mash the mutant plant life into the dirt! Avoid the local wildlife, or splat a fire bug to trigger waves of destruction that can clear your path.

Run roughshod over four different environments and 100 fiendish levels. Will you run out of moves before the last weed has been mooshed, or can you trample your way to victory?

Key Features

● Over 100 levels of weed whacking, puzzle solving fun!
● Four different environments
● Lots of replayability - can you perfect every level?

Square's Route is a game in which you control a cube within a mostly isometric world.

The aim is to navigate throughout each level so that you squish the specified plants. To squish them, you must move over them with the plant's corresponding colour face down on the cube, however every time you move the cube, it will roll, causing the positions of the colours to change. While this may sound simple, it can get very difficult to work out.

Square's Route is very well designed, introducing one new feature and challenge at a time, with level design that ensures you understand what is being introduced before you proceed. I never felt too overwhelmed, but it mixed up the gameplay and kept it interesting.

Square's route is incredibly cute and full of charm. You should enjoy this game if you enjoy puzzles, planning ahead, and spatial reasoning.

"One more go".
This is what I thought about 25 times when trying Square's Route for the first time.
It's a game which seems "easy and harmless" when you first play it, with its cute graphics and simple control system, and the first few stages are extremely simple (some of them can be completed in a matter of seconds).
Then the game throws you more colours, more environments, more dangers... and I was hooked.
Square's Route is genuinely challenging: you not only need to think ahead to make sure you land on the target with the desired side of your cube and reach the end of each stage within a certain number of moves/rolls, but you also have to deal with less-than-friendly fauna and lots more dangers.
The secret of the title is that it gets everything right for a puzzle game: a simple control system, an excellently thought-out learning curve (basically, the first 10 levels are a fun tutorial which teaches you how to play and gives you the chance to learn the importance of "landing" on the right side of the cube), a very solid gameplay mechanic and A LOT of levels, which become a real challenge for your brain (but not before level 14) and a bait for your ego, "because I can get 3 stars in that damned level!"
Graphics are very clear and crisp, with a "solid" look, and are extremely functional, with no visual element constituting an obstacle to your view of the playing area. The soundtrack is nice, but it gets repetitive after a few minutes.
Square's Route is an excellent puzzle game. Recommended to anyone who likes to plan their next move ahead and is not scared by hard challenges.
You will spend with this game a lot more time than you'd originally planned to, and it's going to be fun times.
I have received a free code from a friend who asked me to give a "gamer's opinion" about the game.

A simple to control puzzle game in which you move a cube with coloured sides to crush matching coloured plants and collect gems.
The game is quite easy but does have a nice progression of difficulty and introduces new obstacles along the way.
You can get through the game fairly quickly but getting 3* in all levels will give you more playtime and a much better challenge.
It doesn't get frustrating when you fail to reach the 3 stars as it's fun to try figure out how you can do it better.

I've had no problems playing but there's a few things that could improve the game such as a quicker reset and a few other features to make it more user friendly which are on the developers to do list.

Overall I've enjoyed playing it and It's got a nice art style and the music is good too.
I believe achievements are to be added so I'll be looking forwards to completing all of them when they're available.

A decent game for anyone that likes puzzles and a more casual games.
The price is reasonable for the amount of content there is however due to the simplicity and not having anything new to do after getting 3 stars in all levels some people may want to wait for a discount if money is a bit tight or prefer games to last much longer.

I was asked to do a review if given a free copy of the game. I was not required to write a positive review and I told them not to expect one. Fortunately, it is positive.

There's not much else to say to be honest.
