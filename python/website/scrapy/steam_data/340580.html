Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Armed with Wings Rearmed is being developed by a single developer and he needs your feedback. Voice your ideas, opinions and concerns - together we can craft the best experience possible!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We are aiming to have the game complete in mid 2016. Rearmed will be in Early Access long enough for players to contribute and see their suggested changes or improvements realized in frequent updates.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of Rearmed will more deeply explore the games core mechanics, perfecting the gameplay balancing and progression. Expect new levels, weapons, perks, playable characters and more!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Rearmed currently features 2 playable characters, 30 levels, animated story sequences, 9 unlockable weapons, 8 perks and an endless Survival Mode to put your skills and equipment to the ultimate test!”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Depending on the value of content added during Early Access, the game may be priced higher when it launches.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We encourage players to join the Rearmed Forums to post ideas, suggestions and general support!

We keep a regular development blog over at: http://www.armedwithwings.com”

About This Game


Darkness continues to plague the world of Black Mist. The Rebel Army is crushed, the war for freedom is lost and the Tyrant King Vandheer continues to rule with his cold, iron fist. However, the cries of the perished have not been ignored by the divine ones who seek to save the world from darkness. So by their hands, the strongest of the fallen is resurrected, wielding celestial power and a sacred title, Armed with Wings.

About
Armed with Wings: Rearmed is a 2D action/puzzle platformer set in the dark and dilapidated world of Black Mist. Players control a capable hero and cunning eagle, traveling through various lands and fortresses that challenge the player’s ability to solve puzzles, engage enemies and traverse platforms.

The Sword
Your blade summons the power of Lightning and Thunder. Strike your foes and reduce them to mist.

The Eagle
Your Eagle companion will aid you in solving puzzles on your journey. Explore the road ahead, distract guards, move objects and collect unreachable items.

The World
The dark world of Black Mist is a blasted ruin, full of dangers and mysteries. Take on your journey through 30 levels of platforms, puzzles and battles.

The King
King Vandheer resides in his palace, waiting for your arrival. Challenge and defeat him.


  • Electrifying combat
  • Play as both the Warrior and the Eagle.
  • 25 mysterious levels
  • Brilliant Cinematic sequences.
  • Endless Survival Mode to test your skill'
  • Local VS Multiplayer, battle your friends
  • Outstanding visuals and animation
  • Unlockable weapons and player boosters
  • XBox Gamepad Support

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, Vista, 7, 8
    • Processor: 2.5 GHz
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 100 MB available space

Early Access Review
Posted: 30 May, 2015
Disclaimer: Review code was provided by developer.
Playing the role of an unnamed hero summoned into the world of Black Mist by power of the gods. You are given the title of Armed with Wings and extraordinary strength, sole job being to defend the world against the onset of darkness brought by Tyrant King Vandheer. Let there be light!
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xSShPKaJ_qs
Gameplay wise it's rather simple but aesthetically just beautiful, both artwork and soundtrack. Side-scrolling 2d platformer at it's core with some basic puzzle elements. Most of the puzzles involved use of your bird with simple timing and logic so it's certainly not a dealbreaker for people who detest them. Had strong flashbacks to Prince of Persia and similar games during my gametime.

Currently contains an endless survival mode and story with 26 levels, which can be finished in about 30minutes on normal difficulty after a few unsuccessful runs. Completing a run rewarded points for enemies killed, progression made and time taken. These points automatically unlock new upgrades for your character to further decrease the difficulty in future. Some of these included a range of weapons, increased combo counter and passive speed boosts. Did find the game a little on the easy side already, so once unlocked it really became a walk in the park which was unfortunate.

Most of want I want to see added is already planned. Including new levels, characters, weapons and higher difficulty modes. Personally I think increasing the difficulty should be a priority along with adding some new attack combinations for the other buttons available, since A, A, A became a little dull. With that said it's worth at least shortlisting. Overall I enjoyed my time spent playing, just needs a little more fleshing out and that's where the community feedback comes in. Cheers folks. :)

Early Access Review
Posted: 1 June, 2015
Having played the original Armed With Wings and its sequels as a kid, I was really excited when this was greenlit for Steam. I always wondered how a small 30min or so flash game would fare and what kind of final product the developers could create.

I gotta say, the final result exceeded my expectations.

SPOILERS BELOW (Though really, the original game has been out for so long that it really doesn't matter)

The lore behind the first game and its sequels really created a lot of creative potential in expanding the idea of this game. Now that I think about, the gameplay in the original Armed With Wings was a lot less dynamic then its successors. It got to the point where I was confused at how the Lone Warrior was able to defeat Vandheer when he was so powerful in the 2nd game.

The developers have really enhanced the gameplay to include a lot of abilities which couldn't be accessed in the original game and have made battles a lot more exciting in Rearmed. The use of an EXP type system also offers a form of reward system in the form of new weapons and abilities which definitely enticed me to play the story mode more than once consecutively. This game isn't overly difficult nor hard to adapt to but some of the puzzles may be a bit confusing and fighting multiple enemies at once early in the game may pose as a challenge.

The game's survival mode has also been a lot of fun. It was difficult to get into at first but after several rounds, I was desperately holding onto that last bit of hope that I would make it to the end of every round. Definitely something to keep me occupied when I'm looking for something simple and fun.

The art and visual effects of this game are done simply but definitely done right. Though they may not seem like much on the outside, the backgrounds definitely bring the game's world to life and set the atmosphere in whichever area the player is in.

Audio was very fitting for all of the levels and seemed to build the intensity of the battles quite well.

Overall, this was definitely a huge step up from the original flash game which I knew and loved as a kid. Props to you Sun Studios.

For those who have played the original, I really do recommend this game. For those who haven't, it would depend on you . I will warn you, it is a very short game. I've only played for just over 2 hours but I've already unlocked all of the content and beaten the story mode three times (Mind you, it got exponentially easier each time). However, the game's survival mode will definitely make up for the current small amount of content. Also, this game will be readying up for new content in its next major update with new playable characters, weapons, abilities and missions so this is not the full game at the current stage.
