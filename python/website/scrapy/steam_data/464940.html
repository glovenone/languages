About This Game

Teleport yourself to Rome, hold a beating heart, or walk among dinosaurs. Open Sketchfab VR to discover a showcase of explorations through remote places, fantastic creatures, game worlds, cultural heritage, science and other wonders.

Sketchfab is the platform to publish and explore a million things in 3D and VR. With a community of over half a million creators, it is the largest library of user generated VR content in the world. We selected some of the best experiences for you - discover them now! You can also browse the full gallery on http://sketchfab.com with our WebVR support, using Chrome beta (get it on http://webvr.info).

https://sketchfab.com/models/311d052a9f034ba8bce55a1a8296b6f9

    • DirectX: Version 11

This is reviewed after playing it on the HTC Vive.

Well, this is ok - being free I wasn't expecting much, but it was quite a fun 'experience' for a bit (there is no interactivity really so not a game). You are presented with some different things to explore in a menu, some game worlds, The Human Body, Objects etc and you select which one you want to view. Then you can move yourself around the said object (from pre-defined points) until you're done with it and move onto the next.

As I said, it's ok, but it does get a little old quite quickly. There are one or two things that are quite impressive, the dinosaur being one (although it just walks on the spot in front of you) and the Minecraft Cathedral, which is amazing!

If you've got a Vive and a few minutes to kill then go for it - there does seem to be more content being added quite regularly, but I'm not convinced it will be able to compete with other experiences that are sure to be released in the coming months.

5 / 10

What it is not:
This is not some sort of VR viewer for the Sketchfab website.

What it is:
The app gives you a "curated experience" (really have to remember this marketing slang) by letting you watch some dozen preselected models from their website (hard coded to the app and thereby downloaded to your computer).

While watching a model you cannot choose your point of view. There are very few, predefined points where you can teleport yourself to.
There is absolutely no way you can interact with the models, like scaling, rotating, changing the lightning.

There is no overview of the models available nor a search function. You are allowed to select a categorie, once you did you may choose the "next" or the "previous" model.

While watching you will be listening to some generic synth song.

Bottom line:
The app is free, that's the only reason why I recommend it.

The concept is good, but the current execution is rough.

- The 'look down' menu is quite intrusive and makes it difficult to enjoy anything that has a floor or content below you. This needs to go. I would much prefer a button press that brings up the menu instead.

- The music is very canned and hokey feeling. It takes away from the content you're experiencing. I would rather there was some seamlessly looping ambient sound (wind, nature, interior hum of electronics, distant muffled chatter), rather than the over the top stock film sound tracks.

- There needs to be a way to freely rotate the model in front of you in addition to the pre-placed viewpoints. Nobody is going to have enough room space to walk fully around anything.

- Having the ability to change the scale of a model on the fly so you could fully rotate and see models from every side would be essential.

- I realize this is probably a planned feature, but you need to be able to select exactly what you want to view instead of browsing through a linear set of models.
