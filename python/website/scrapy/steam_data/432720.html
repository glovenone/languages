Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The single-player part of the game is mostly finished, and we want to use Early Access to refine it and address any remaining issues. Additionally, we believe the game would benefit from certain community-driven features, which is why constant community feedback is essential.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“It is difficult to predict how long the new features will take to develop, but we estimate the game to stay in Early Access for up to 4 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“After balancing and refining the existing gameplay, we plan on adding features that focus more on the speedrunning elements, multiplayer and community interaction.

Currently planned features include:
  • Race against friends live
  • Viewing and sharing replays
  • Race against a "ghost" of your past runs or of the runs of others
  • Level editor and steam workshop support

Note that this is only a plan, and may change depending on how the community receives the game and which features players want the most.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game currently features 11 levels that are playable from start to finish. It constantly introduces new, diverse gameplay mechanics and combines them in a way that makes every level feel different. Also present is a speedrun mode where players try to finish levels as fast as they can to compete on leaderboards.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, the game might cost slightly more on full release to reflect the added features.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“While the single-player is for the most part complete, the gameplay requires balancing, which is best achieved with the help of the players. If the community feels strongly about changing certain elements of the game, we will do our best to incorporate the proposed changes. New multi-player and community driven features are planned, but are open to changes and will be developed with a constant dialogue with the community.”

About This Game



Soaring amid the clouds, Welkin Road blends free running with grappling-hook-powered aerobatics, challenging you to reach the end of each level using only your wits and the abilities available to you. The road ahead will test your resolve, your concentration and your reflexes, forcing you to master your swings and movement as you learn new techniques to overcome the obstacles in the worldâ¦ or plummet to your doom.

The gameplay is driven by two core components: grappling and parkour. Make use of the two grappling hooks to swing from point to point, creating momentum while using parkour-inspired movement to explore the world. Sprint, leap, balance, run on walls and hold on for dear life as you perform incredible, high-altitude, acrobatic stunts.

You have full control over the grappling hooks, as well as the direction, speed and timing of your swings. Master and blend all of these elements into fast, fluid and elegant runs. Challenge yourself further by competing against the clock and the time of other runners around the world.

Main features:


  • Grappling hooks: With full control over two grappling hooks you can chain swings together and use your momentum for fluid, elegant movement.
  • Parkour: Use wall running, wall jumping, crouching and wall grappling to overcome obstacles and find the best route to the pillar of light.
  • Different grappling mechanics: The game is full of different types of grappling mechanics which will impact the way you move and solve puzzles.
  • Challenges and puzzles: Levels require you to master swinging, movement, various abilities and to discover new ways to use and combine them.
  • Speedrun mode: Race against the clock or try to beat the times of other runners around the world, and compete for a spot on the leaderboards.
  • Intelligent checkpoints: Frequent checkpoints and manual resets mean you can get back into the action as fast as possible. This allows you to practice sections and master an entire level without having to constantly restart. Improve on sections, find the most efficient route and set the best time.

So I've been playing Welkin Road for a bit and I think it is a fantastic game. I feel like the game is challenging and pushes me to go farther while I swing from orb to orb like spiderman. That being said, I still think there can be (a good amount) of improvements to make the game the best it can be, but let's compare pros and cons first!

Pros:
-Pretty minimalistic design (Hey, I like minimammalissm)
-Challenging levels
-Replayability (honestly this might be the game I'd love to speedrun)

Cons:
-Poor player model (Looks like stock, poor shading during parts of gameplay)
-Confusing controls (What even are the keys to make a tether go up and down anyway??)
-Not much to look at (Although minimalism is great for the game, there's just a straight level with no pretty scenery or aesthetics, just blocks...)
-Lack of diversity (1 orb, 2 orb, Red orb, Blue orb)

(Room for) Improvements:
-Change Gameplay
Although the gameplay right now is enjoyable, it really is annoying to have to wait until I get to a certain point to be allowed to lock onto an orb and even MORE annoying to miss an orb and have no way to recover as I fall to my imminent death. I would absolutely love if the tether length was unlimited and you just have to aim it to hook onto the orb, similar to games such as "The Story of My Uncle" or "Windlands".

-Change Player Model
I'm going to be blatently honest, the player model looks like â¥â¥â¥â¥. The texture quality is pretty bad and the entire thing looks like the hands of a 60-year-old man. What I think would suit the game a lot better is a white, shiny, futuristic robot with a center palm and floating fingers, like something out of a sci-fi movie. It would fit the minimalistic style while also fitting the vibe of the game.

-Steam Workshop/Modding
I know it is already planned, but I want more than just a level editor, I would love to have access to mod the game with new mechanics, environments, etc. For it's minimalistic design, this would be a great improvement for others to add more to the game.

-More... depth
Right now the game is kind of bland without any scenery, atmosphere, or story. Adding little structures of civilization or ruined buildings or different things would make the game a lot more interesting and fun to play to see what I might come across next. "Windlands" does this BEAUTIFULLY with moving rock titans and interesting atmosphere.

In conclusion, I think the game is fun and challenging, but if there were more variety and more interesting... things, I guess... then I think Welkin Road could be a wonderful, and beautiful, game.

The problem is that the level were designed from the developer's point of view, who has so much more knowledge of the most finicky bits of the movements, and not from a player's POV that will take a considerable amount of time to perfect some of the hardest moves. Instead, you're faced with seemingly impossible obstacles as early as level 3...

- The targetting system of the orbs is completely unpredictable, and there are definitly problems with distance measuring where sometimes you can clearly see your grappling beam actually hit the orb but it will simly not grapple whatsoever.

- The movements, in particular jumping, are incredibly finicky and feel very unstable. You feel as if you could just fumble down anytime even when just running (it hobbles from left to right way too much). This can, however, be disabled, so it's advised you do so.

- Finally, there are clearly optimisation problems on the grappling walls who just feel incredibly akward and jumping off of them is a nightmare.

The truth is that this game has a ton of potential, but currently reaches no pinnacles anywhere. Here are some of the things it does very good:

+ The visuals are great. Simple, but great. Mostly cubic and one prominent color mixed with white. The phong shading on them gives more life to the overall picture and the clouds are among the best I've seen so far.

+ There is one thing that carries the game, and that's the checkpoint system. It's so good it actually makes me wonder whether the developer himself was clearly aware of just how mundanously difficult the game is.

+ The music of the menu is great. Now add music in-game while we're playing and it's perfect.

+ The physical movement of swinging around does feel realistic (but as mentioned, the controls are completey hampering this).


For now, I cannot recommend it because the negatives outweigh the positives by a longshot. What I think is most keenly felt is how there is just no story whatsoever. There are supposedly secrets in the levels, but they are nothing more than just 'trophies' to say you were able to get on spot X or Y. How amazing would it have been if this game had a story that elaborated on how this world got created, what it's purpose it, what the orbs are and where your powers come from. Combine this with ambient music that stages the atmosphere and this game would be a masterpiece.

If that could be implemented, along with some obvious bugfixes and optimisations, I would immediatly change my rating to a high thumbs up. But for now, I can't. Don't get me wrong however, I think that for the amount of playtime you would actually set in the game, this price is worth it. They're not charging too much, which is a step in the right direction. Just wait for some patches that make this game just a little more forgiving, and we're in for a great score.

(Will adjust my review and rating as this game evolves)

(Recieved early access code from developer with intention of reviewing. This did not affect my opinion about the game. You can also check out http://www.thegamerholics.com/ for more gaming news and content)

Welkin Road consist of a campaign mode with eleven levels, and an âSâ ranking system (like Resident Evil). Each of the eleven levels brings a new mechanic into the game, which adds variety to each level as the game progresses, since these new mechanics carry over into the later levels. The game is really simple in its premise. Players must sprint, walk, grapple, wall jump, and wall run from one checkpoint to the other: until they reach the pillar of light at the end that carries them to the next level. Since the setting of the game is on variously colored blocks that are raised high above clouds in the air, this might be what parkour heaven looks like?

Each new grappling mechanic is presented through varying colored orbs that hover throughout the level. Players must interact with these orbs through their grappling gloves (which look pretty slick to me), and manipulate their own momentum and movements to reach the next parts of the level. There is the most basic red orb (as an example), This orb is the first orb that players encounter since it is the first level. It simply sits in a predetermined position, making it easy to grapple to and swing around endlessly, or change the direction that you swing to land onto a platform on your side. Things get more complex quickly, with orbs like the blue orb, which run out of energy after about three seconds. This means grappling it for more than that time will result in you plummeting to your death. This introduction of varying mechanics is a great feature that keeps the levels progressively increasing in difficulty, and staying refreshing.

Welkin Road gives players access to all eleven levels from the start, so anyone feeling adventurous can hop right into the final level and punish themselves by trying to figure out all of the game'sâ mechanics on their own. When players do play the campaign mode, they will progress from one level to the next, with tips offered at various puzzles or obstacles. These tips are optional in the gameâs menu, but also only appear as a pop up message that players can activate, and read by clicking âF1â (by default). This was something I really enjoyed, since the game allowed me to first try to out various obstacles on my own. Only when I had died around ten times did I open the tips to get more insight into how to properly succeed.

There is speed run mode as well, where players can challenge themselves to race to the end of each level, once they have mastered the gameâs mechanics, or even just for fun. Welkin Road also features secrets hidden throughout each level, the number of the secrets per level is listed when players starts the game, and is shown in the campaign menu screen.

Welkin Roadâs mechanics function well, but the game does not feel perfect. The movements feel fluid when wall running, sprinting, and jumping. Wall jumping took a patch and some really good timing to nail though. There is a collision sound that plays when your player hits a wall, indicating when to press the spacebar to jump off of it. This threw off my timing more than anything, since it did not seem to be in perfect synchronization with when my character was actually touching the wall. Jumping upwards with wall jumps felt nearly impossible at some points of the levels as well, but the newest patch has made these motions much easier to complete. The physics of the grappling mechanic felt fluid as well, and stringing together jumps from one orb to another orb was manageable. My real issue was not the mechanics of the game but more so the level designs of certain obstacles. There were many jumps, or grappling sequences in the levels that took almost pixel perfect precision to complete. I loved the challenge of many of the obstacles, and figuring out solutions on my own. Yet many obstacles required literal perfect timing across three or four jumps, which at times became frustrating more than anything. The game is far from unbeatable, but players should simply be ready to die plenty of times, and players should make sure their mouse settings are just right.

The game features a minimalist style, in both its art and music, which I found relaxing and undistracting. As mentioned before, each level occurs on white blocks mixed with a single color, which corresponds to the color orb of that level. The game is well optimized and lets players adjust the usual settings like contrast, brightness, bloom, resolution, and more. There is only music in the gameâs menu but not while playing the actual levels. This was not an issue for me, I enjoy the atmosphere created by a game where you can hear the sound of the environment: especially atmospheric heavy games like Skyrim or Dishonored. Music does often enhance experiences however, so the same soft and soothing type of song in the menu would make the game more enjoyable for some.

Welkin Road lacks any narrative which for some may turn them off from the game. It was designed as a first person parkour, puzzle simulator and functions great at being just that. Adding these sort of mechanics into a full fledged game in the future would probably be a great asset, but for now, Welkin Road is entertaining, simple, yet a fun challenge.
