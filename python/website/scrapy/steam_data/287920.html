About This Game

Experience true open-world freedom in Mortal Online - a unique first-person, medieval fantasy, massively multiplayer online role-playing game.

Choose your path!

  • Choose from many different races, decide your own style of play and whether to focus on PvP or PvE, both sharing the same game world.
  • Choose a profession and devote your life to combat, crafting, magic, trading...
  • Discover countless skills, resources and tools, master a few or dabble in them all, or be the first to explore new areas to find hidden treasures and unique secrets.

Live or die!

This game is not for the faint of heart. With "full loot" mechanics, dying means that you risk losing everything you're carrying with you.
Danger is everywhere, safety must be earned. Learn to master real-time, aim-based combat where you decide every move your character makes, and where your skills really matter.

Not quite like any other MMORPG...

  • There are no character levels, only skill levels.
  • There are no PvP zones, only a single open and realistic world where you are free to choose your own path.
  • There are no separate server regions, every player inhabits the same huge world.

Strength in numbers!

  • Build your own house, or create a guild with your friends to build a keep.
  • Expand even more, build a whole city and surround it with defensive walls, or band together to siege and conquer your enemies' fortresses!
  • With enough power, violence or diplomacy, you and your allies will be able to rule an entire nation.

The possibilities are endless!

Unlimited free trial!

Mortal Online offers a free-to-play mode with a skill cap at skill level 60. In order to unlock the maximum skill levels and fully master your Mortal destiny, a paid monthly subscription is needed, but unlike many other games there are no microtransactions. Unsubscribing will re-apply the skill cap, but your skills will be maxed again when you re-subscribe. Every player gets one free character slot, and you can purchase additional slots up to a maximum of four.

I've been coming back to this game every few years here and there to see if there is ever going to be any actual improvements, and so far from what I've seen theres been only a few. As far as optimzation goes, this game has always had issues for anyone with a basic or low end PC. However before the few latest updates that where supposed to help, they seemed to have made things worse. On my basic PC that I somehow, at the lowest settings, manged to get it to run the game... until I got done making my character... again. I used to be able to play just fine on this PC without even latency issues but it seems as if with the latest updates they are more focused on giving the few players who can play something to do rather then optimize their game for the majority of players who cannot afford a high end machine to run this game.

Any time I do manage to play however I do enjoy the content to its fullest, its just a shame that they don't do more to optamize more. I'm not saying they don't try, because there is more settings to lower the quality of everything to a lower setting then there was some 3 years ago but it still doesnt do much to help the game.

Honestly I would recommend this game to anyone who has a real quality machine that can run it, if you manage to live through the grind and find a great guild to play the game with its wonderful fun. The landscapes on the higher settings are beautiful for their age and you can only wonder how a game like this can ever be so peaceful... then you get your head cut off by some guy who can run faster then your horse... its happened. a lot.

but I'd give this game a solid 7/10 overall, even if it has some issues. It has the potential to be a great game! it's just not quite there yet though, and I can only hope that the Dev team on this will make this game as great for everyone as it has been for me throughout the years.

Pss. this one is for the devs, please optamize the game farther, I know you guys are busy trying to make sure the servers continue to stay up but if you can optamize then I could only imagine the kind of positve feedback this game could get!
