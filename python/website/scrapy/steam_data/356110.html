About This Game

1 box, 6 transformations, 120 levels of increasing difficulty for skilled players who love challenges.

Help Boxy escape its factory and survive the outside world by mastering its 6 transformations: Rocket, Steel, Bubble, Spikes, Vapor and Fire.

More than a hundred levels of increasing difficulty will put your skills, reflexes and cunning to the test. Die, retry, perfect your run and dominate the competitive leaderboards.

- 6 transformations meaning 6 different types of gameplay
- 3 game modes (Classic, Time Attack and Survival)
- 10 worlds to explore, from a cold factory to a hot volcano or a watery jungle
- 120 levels, skill-based or puzzle-based

GREAT GAMEPLAY, EPIC CHALLENGES, AWESOME GAME !

No deep storyline here, Box Out! is gameplay focused. It is a real "easy to learn, hard to master" type of game. Kind of a mix between a platformer and a puzzle game but made for speedrunners.

The 6 transformations are very fun to use (I love the rocket and the spikes forms). Learning curve is well balanced so you can feel the difficulty slowly rising. Three optional challenges in each level: retrieve 3 cups, beat the level under a certain time, beat the level using a limited number of transformations. You can sometimes get 2 trophies at the same time, but generally I had to do seperate runs. But it's all good, half of the fun came from perfecting the run and the satisfaction of beating the clock by 0.01 second (YOU FREAKING PIECE OF SH**)!!!

Box Out! Isn't going to turn heads, but is a solid platformer that shows me a developer with real potential: As a lot of the design decisions regarding natural player progression, level design, and basic gameplay shows a lot of good design elements and smart decision making. While the game does seemingly lack content over the long haul, the two based action gameplay regarding switching between forms is the highlight here, and while the art and collision detection fails at points, it was an enjoyable experience in the end. I'd pick it up on sale when you have the chance.

+:
-Good Elements of Level Design here, leading to teaching the player the rules of its world naturally, rather then through a tutorial.
-Switching between elements is probably the most action packed it gets and can be rewarding
-Different objectives leads to different paths in the world to go through and try a level differently.
-Controls are what you would want for game like this and give you the freedom that you need
-Some designs of the actions are rather nice, despite the 8 bit nature of them.

-:
-Art sometimes ends up being misleading as a spike end for example is deadly, but the base of the spike is.
-Can lack oompf. Doesn't stand out at any point, hitting the solid point and nothing more.
-Music can wear on you after a while.
-Extra Modes seem derivative of the main mode.

For Gameplay and More First Impressions: http://youtu.be/CNFDsA21wSk
