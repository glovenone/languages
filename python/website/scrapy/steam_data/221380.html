TL:DR : This game is not for everyone and has many difficult facets to work around, but ends up being one of the most impressive accomplishments in gaming. The HD re-release does wonders to breathe new life into it, and the DLC is decent, but not enough to warrant a pre-sale purchase.

I have to confess something. When I originally got this game many years ago, I didn't like it. I thought it was boring, long, uninteresting, â¥â¥â¥â¥-poor graphics, you name it. To be fair, I was a pre-teen just starting to get into RTS and this was my first base-construction, turtle-mechanic RTS. But, I had this and Command and Conquer: Generals. The little kid me went for Generals because it's awesome in every way. I also had Age of Empires III coming out sooner on my end rather than later for some of the original purchasers of this game. That was the game that stole my heart.

But I always wanted to revisit this game eventually because it was something everyone I talked to about raved about. Granted, all these people were a few years older than me, but it confused me. I thought it was the video game equivalent of molasses.

And, actually, it is. It's sweet and full of amazing surprises, but it's all thick and slow as heck. If you're a younger gamer, looking for something fast-paced, I actually would recommend something like the Command and Conquer games. Those will get your insanely sugar-rushed mind to work. But if you truly want an immersive, impressive gaming experience, this is the game for you.

GAMEPLAY

Okay, so you've got x-amount of resources, a couple of workers, and a scout. GO! That's every skirmish. It gets old. Fast. Get some friends and be trolls on the diplomacy. Set it to "Island" and build a beautiful city on an island and strip-mine all the resources. Play the missions and spam all the cheats so you can just run around and destroy things.

Versatility is the name of the game, and this game has a lot of it. It is most pronounced in the aforementioned missions which, in my humble opinion, comprise the best the game has to offer. I have spent more of my life than I can admit to playing the El Cid misisons (sidenote: SO MUCH FUN!). The voice acting is strong, the maps are amazingly well made, and the stories are more or less accurate to real history. If you just want to do this for the singleplayer aspects, than this is definitely a purchase for you.

On the other hand, if multiplaying is your way of life in gaming, I'm not entirely convinced this is the best game to multiplay as an RTS. It is certainly ubiquitous enough to be well-recognized, but tends to be less on the impressive side at times.

One last note: The HD version increases the unit cap to 500. Just imagine 500 swordsmen spamming a small map.

Haven't tried it yet, but I came close! *sigh*....I'll wait until I've done it to brag....

GRAPHICS

Awesome. Just awesome. It's like buying a Blu-ray remaster of an old 1950s movie. It just really looks so good you almost forget how old it really is. I have no complaints or suggestions here. I will reiterate that the younger of you all might not be receptive to this as much. If this is the case, I would highly recommend Age of Empires III (I'll review that later, 'cause there is a lot to be said on that topic).

CONCLUSION

10/10 easily. Being older, smarter, and less judgemental of older, less-flashy things, I have found great joy in replaying some of these missions again. It delights me to see a piece of my childhood with so much care taken for it. The only thing that made me happier was the Age of Mythology re-release. Now that, I dropped a lot of money on very quick.

As far as should you buy it? Yes. Full price? Sure, if you feel compelled to. It's worth the time of $20 easy. Ultimately, I never buy stuff full price, because I see these as luxuries, not necessities. The DLC is NOT, I repeat, NOT worth the full price unless you are very keen on this game and expect to get good worth out of it.

This was one of my childhood games and it has aged really well. The gameplay is really addictive and it is so satisfing to watch your civilization grow and dominate everyone else. Playing with other people is a lot of fun and the game compares your skill level to everyone else's so you always know whether or not it will be a far match. That's not to say the game is perfect. As satisfying as the game is when your winning, it's really depressing when your losing; all that hard working building your civilization gone to waste. The learning curve is also really steep, especially if you're unfamiliar with this genre, and getting to the level of skill that most people have online is a bit of a chore.
