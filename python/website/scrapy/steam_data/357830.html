KILL MONSTERS. DESTROY EVIL. GET RICH.

The forces of evil have invaded the land. A labyrinth of evil has appeared in the kingdom and legions of monsters are pouring out of its vile depths. The king has called upon a small party of heroes, the Champions of Virtue, to vanquish the demon within and free the land from a grim fate!
An action arcade game for 1-4 players. With the help of ancient magics and piles of gold, you must find the hidden relic in each area to reach the heart! Who can vanquish the evil within the Labyrinth, and who can escape with the most riches?

GOT WHAT IT TAKES TO BE A CHAMPION?

- Arcade shoot-em up action
- Up to 4 players locally
- play as a Knight, Wizard, Archer or Dragon!
- dungeons are randomly generated
- battle through 10 levels of the labyrinth to reach the heart of evil!
- collect upgrades to help you mow down the forces of darkness!
- Find powerful scrolls to aid your quest

Overall, I've enjoyed this game. I don't exactly remember when/how I got it, but I wanted to play a dungeon crawler, popped this one in, and was surprised by it.

From what I've collected, this game seems to have had a rocky history/start, but I can't really find anything major to gripe about it. I'd imagine major issues have since been patched by the time I played this game.

Attack of the Labyrinth is pretty basic and it can be beaten in about 80 minutes if you're experienced with the genre, slightly longer if not. It's hard to get a game over in this game because if you're decent at it and like to explore you'll either find a ton of 1UP's or be able to buy lots of them from the store. There are two endings that I'm aware of and the requirements to get the "good" ending aren't that hard.

My only real gripe is that there's something weird going on with the achievements, namely that some people think one of them is outright broken, and that there are a couple whose unlock requirements are unclear. As I write this review there's no guide and no insight on the forum as to what's what. If you're looking for completion, I'd stay away from this one.

The game is a "solid 5" on a 1-10 scale, to me at least.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WbB4NGIG068

Attack of the Labyrinth is fun little arcade game. Sadly, it doesn't offer much depth, but it isn't an expensive title. This is first impressions of the game.

A peaceful kingdom is treathen by dark presence. A labyrinth of pure evil, with an army unlike any seen before. So, now it's up to four Champions to save the kingdom. Simple story.

This is 2D game, so the lacking of video options is understandable. So is the rebindable keys to ceratin degree. It would be nice to change them. The graphics are retro pixel art with a lot of colours

There is four classes to choose from. Those are the Champions. I only play with the Wizard. With him the gameplay is a twin-stick shooter. You have only one attack â a projectile and that's it. Well, there is more, but not much. In the levels you can find or buy upgrades. They change your projectile and your appearance. The wizard can be upgraded two times, I don't know about the others. The only other thing that can help you are scrolls. They are one time use magic spell. Health, Mace, Summon, Shield and Radiant. The last is extremely powerful clearing spell. You can carry one at a time. There is nothing more to the gameplay.

With the gold you collect from dead enemies, chests and everything that explodes, you can buy from a shop inside the level lives (yes, there are lives here), scrolls, health and upgrades and nothing more.

Dungeons or labyrinths are pretty diverce as layout. I saw ice cave, mines with tracks, grass, dirt or gold and so on. All levels are randomly generated, which add to the replayability. When you enter a labyrinth you have to find a key, then the exit. Also there is place with a map, that shows where those things are and the shop. There is no mini map and sometimes it's a little bit confusing. Before every boss you have to make it through series of levels. The first is small, but they get progressively larger. The labyrinth with the boss encounter are pretty big and without a mini map it can take you a while to find the key and the exit.

Enemies are okay. Different types of spiders, ghosts, archers, knights and so on. When they surround they are dangerous. The game is pretty intense and I like that. The bosses are creative and fun to fight.

Effects resemble 8-bit games from the past and they are nice. Music is okay, but not very memorable. I can't think of a single track.

The games is okay. It's very colorful with responsive controls and it's fun for a while. There is local co-op, but didn't try it. Probably it will be more fun with a friend, but I think it's a mistake not to have online multiplayer. Sadly, my biggest disappointment is the lack of gameplay depth. One projectile and scrolls. I played almost two hours and i'm already getting bored. It's just functional, fun little game. But when you consider the prise, I think the game is alright. Get the game on sale and find a friend to play with you.

Wow, that's pretty much the one word to describe Attack of the Labyrinth, this is of the most fun games I've had the pleasure of playing in a while. The gameplay is so simple, yet so addictive. This is a hard as nails arcade action dungeon crawling game. I'd also say it has a hint of rogue-like too.

There are plenty of classes to choose from as with most fantasy type games, my favorite here has to be the mage, his spells get so overpowered once you rank them up. I did also spend some time playing as an archer but I struggled to find my stride there.

As you roam around the dungeon killing the endless supply of enemies, you collect their coin and can upgrade your spells and weapons, along with buying additional lives and hearts. You have hearts per life, run out of lives and you have to start all over again. Thankfully there is some sort of gated progression here, each portal has three levels to it. As long as you beat the end boss in each portal, you'll only have to repeat one level, and not the whole game again.

I cannot wait to try out the local co-op here and can support up to four players! The controls are so responsive to your movements, it is almost perfect. This is an absolute gem of a bargain at its current price as there is tons of gameplay and replay ability here.

If you get this game in a bundle, or on sale for $1.50 or less, consider it worthwhile. You can easily get 3-4hrs of fun out of it, plus another 3 if you go for the secret and 100% achievement completion. There is also 2-4 player local co-op for some quick arcade style fun.

Consider this a casual arcade game that gets you into the action quickly but doesn't require a lot of investment. The sounds are enjoyable but I found myself disabling the music within the first hour of play, it loops fairly quickly but that is common and expected in indie games.

There are two ranged and two melee characters, each with different speed/attack/defense stats based on your style of play. You can upgrade each character twice with an upgrade powerup purchased from a shop or found during gameplay.

The maps are randomized each playthrough and there is a map stone on each level that can help you find the exit. I like the random maps because they are drawn like a classic maze, or a cornmaze, lots of winding corners and a dead end. If I pay attention it's not too hard but if I space out I find myself going in circles.

I didn't experience any bugs or stability issues and was glad to find that all features of the game and achievements worked fine.
