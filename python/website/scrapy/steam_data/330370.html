About This Game

Reload is a realistic weapons and tactics trainer for one to four players that also happens to be a super fun, super fast playing game.

Reload is one of the most accurate shooting training simulators ever created and is all about recreating professional shooter training. Watch your marksmanship improve as you learn to shoot the way the pros do. Go through 35+ training scenarios including Hostage Rescue, Sniping, VIP Protection, and Olympic Style Shooting competition. Youâll learn to use small arms, grenade launchers, Vehicle Mounted Heavy Weapons and more.

Players start on an indoor range, firing at stationary targets. They then move on to test their speed and accuracy with long-range outdoor drills, moving targets, and reflex drills. In the process, players unlock additional medals, gear and rewards.

Reload is an on rails shooter where you train to use firearms, but will you want to play it ?

Reload is simple, all you do is go through different objectives ranging from clay shooting, to terrorist takedown operations and target practice.

The game is simple and you use only four buttons. Right and left mouse buttons to shoot and reload, space to hold your breath and shift to use iron sights for sniper rifles. It's all really simple.

The soundtrack to this game is absolutely horrible. It makes me want to stab myself in the ear it's so bad. The gun sound effects are also horrible. It's some of the worst sound design for a game ever.

The game also is really easy. Apart from the missions where you can fail, you can do literally nothing and you will win. Out of curiosity , I decided to not shoot anyone and to my surprise I still finished the level.

The game took me 2 hours to finish every level, but there's still a lot to do, like challenges and Multiplayer ( not sure if that works though).

The game ran really smoothly from start to finish, but the only problem is the horrific resolutions and to my knowledge there's no way to fix it.

Overall Reload is a really mediocre on rails shooter. I highly suggest skipping this and trying out something else like Blue Estate. I give Reload a 4/10

OVERVIEW

Reload is just what it claims to be: a MIL/LE simulation-style rail/range FPS, only, instead of shooting live baddies, you're shooting target representations of them in varying circumstances. In addition, some less combat-oriented levels are included. It accomplishes this premise in a mostly enjoyable fashion, with some flaws.

GAMEPLAY

The levels are pretty well-designed and nicely explained, and it's not that difficult to get a handle on how things work, while also not being so easy that you're bored. The eyewear and camouflage unlockables are great touches that make obtaining platinum on levels feasible, while also adding a sense of accomplishment. There's enough variety in the types of levels to give the game good replay value. Enemies that shoot back in some levels are quite nice. Really dig the move-and-shoot factor on some stages. Controls are good.

On the negative, the tutorial thingies are quite excessive. You should be able to turn them off. The F key is definitely your friend once you get the hang of the game. Also, in the first skeet shooting level, at least, when it says you have less ammo this go around, it's...meaningless, because you've still got hundreds and hundreds of rounds you won't need even if you're a terrible shot. Quite odd. Also, it's irritating that the game zooms in every single time you get a pumpkin or a small target in the sniper levels. The first times, fine; every time, no thanks. The grenade levels are poorly designed, as it's unclear as to what you have left to shoot. The most irritating factor of all is how poorly the reflexive fire rifle level is. Its platinum (and even gold) rating is absurdly difficult, not to mention that the two pop-up targets in the very back are nigh impossible (if not impossible?) to shoot (the shotgun level with hostages had a similar issues). The dot can be right on them and firing does nothing. Really bad hitboxes. In fact, that entire level is broken - you don't get any bonuses from one of the rounds, no matter how you do, which is probably exactly why platinum is borderline impossible.

GRAPHICS

Quite decent, once you change them to the highest resolution - why the default is so low, I'm not sure. There are some slight glitches, but overall it's quite nice.

AUDIO

Sound is pretty good, from the instructor's voice to the gun effects. Music is OK and it fits well with the game.

STORY

You're some random dude who's learning some mad skills. That's about it. I did, however, use these skills to save the girl I love and the world (plus every other world out there), so I guess the idea is to just create your own story in real life, like I did. Thanks, Reload!

CONTENT

Nothing to say here. It's a clean, classy game in which you're preparing to toast bad guys 4 realz by toasting them in their simulated forms. Zero profanity, sexual content, worldview issues, or anything else.

INTERFACE

I'm assuming the word "Interface" works for this...Anyway, I'm talking about stuff like options and such. It's pretty basic but solid, with some problems, such as the aforementioned tutorial pop-ups, and the fact that, when you start a new career, it ditches all of the unlocked levels, too. That's...quite annoying. I assumed it would just delete campaign progress and that was it, letting you play all the scenarios straight through, but it doesn't. One other minor negative is that start-up is just a tad slow - staring at "Copyright 2014" (paraphrasing) gets old fast. Some other minor issues are, for instance, the lack of proper grammar in, "Exit to windows?", and it saying you unlocked two of...the same thing, or using "Unlocked" in reference to locked things (I can kinda understand the last one, but it's a bit weird. It would also be nice if it told you what some of the changes actually did (almost all are obvious). On El Presidente, it says to watch out for hostages...But there aren't any. Also, sometimes the game says, "Better luck next time", for...seemingly no reason. The "All Weapons Drills" don't work for me yet (I can't select anything), but it doesn't say why. In the mounted machine gun levels, reloading is still there even though it's irrelevant.

(Note, of course, that a lot of these are quite minor, "polish" issues - I'm just trying to list everything I can, positive and negative.)

CONCLUSION

The more I played this, the more I liked it, which one other reviewer alluded to (more skill, more fun). For five bucks, it's not bad. Its simplicity is rather attractive.

Overall, I think I'd give it about 6/10, maybe a 7. If you'd like a casual, at least remotely realistic FPS sim, then I'd recommend giving Reload a try.

I hope you found my review helpful. Thanks for reading, and Godspeed.

Think of it as an extended tutorial section taken from some kind of FPS game. You get to shoot different targets with variety of weapons, such as pistol, shotgun, sniper rifle or AK-47. You don't move your character - you only aim, shoot, and reload (sometimes you use scope as well). In most missions your time is very limited, so you have to work fast and try not to waste ammo. The game is not too hard though - if you just want to finish it there are only 2-3 missions that can be really challenging. Getting platinum medals in all missions is a different story and a real challenge. Game contains no violence - you only shoot objects such as wooden figures, pumpkins or cans, so kids are safe to play it.

Cons? Graphics are a bit dated (but acceptable in this price range) and the game itself is very short - you can easily play through all the levels in under two hours. There are achievements and medals, however, that will keep you occupied for a longer period of time. If you're looking for something casual or need a break from "serious" games - Reload should do the trick.
