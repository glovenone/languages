Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We've built a strong foundation for Dragonflight - great characters, the beginnings of a spectacular, fantasy world, and the fundamentals of ambitious, compelling gameplay grounded by story, with lots of room for growth. We've got a vision for this game that's coming along beautifully, though we've got a ways yet to go. We feel it's a perfect candidate for Early Access - players will immediately see the passion we have to make this game great - and we feel we and the project will really benefit by engaging now with fans as eager to pilot dragons as we are. We love this game, and we want to build on that with an audience who loves it too. We're entirely self-funded - we're doing this cuz we love it - and we're ready to start engaging with gamers who want to get airborne with us.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“8 months”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We want to build nine full levels for Dragonflight - we've got the outline for different story elements that unfold from chapter to chapter, with different kinds of gameplay emerging as the game progresses. Grow your dragon in strength, speed, and attack power. Future levels will challenge the player to navigate canyon mazes that differ everytime the game is played; or feature different modes of aerial combat; or aspects like tower defense. We also plan to build a rider for the already existing VR portion of the game - we've got some fantastic character designs already underway.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current state of the game is a robust "vertical slice" - it features flight and combat mechanics - the flight controller allows for finessed, acrobatic, aerial combat. The player has two kinds of attacks - short-range, instant-on, and long-range-slow-build. Each kind of attack has its place. The world is full of destructible castles, trees, and environments. We've currently built one breed of dragon, but we've got additional breeds under construction. We've written a rich mythology backing the storyline, with lush illustrations bookending gameplay from level to level. We've got preliminary AI plugged into enemy dragons and the player can fight up to twenty dragons at the same time while maintaining a completely fluid frame-rate. The first two chapters of the game are drafted - a training level, and our main level, which challenges the player to find and destroy a massive castle keep, while fighting off wave after wave of enemies, including dragons, archers firing flaming arrows, and a mysterious wizard tower - nearly impossible to destroy, but offering a spectacular reward for those tough enough to meet the challenge. We've also been working with a wonderfully talented composer and have some theme music integrated into the Chapter 2 boss battle.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, we expect to raise the price upon release.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“One of the great incentives of early access is participating with fans as we're building the project. We're making the game we want to play - but we want to respond to users wishes, engage them in the process, and build a project that's better because of the participation of our fans. We feel it's very necessary to fly around as a dragon in a fantasy world, and do the things dragons do - roast forests, demolish castle walls, parlay with sorcerers - - we want to connect with folks who feel the same imperative ;)”

About This Game

Engage in pitched combat piloting a spectacular dragon through worlds of medieval fantasy. Dragonflight features massive, destructible environments, fast-paced combat with up to twenty enemies swarming the skies around you, ancient castles teeming with archers, and lost wizard towers shrouded in mist. Dragonflight also features a VR mode - ride your dragon through showers of flaming arrows, or dodge trees as you fly low over a fantasy landscape, hiding from enemies arrayed against you. Dragonflight includes unique combinations of game dynamics - acrobatic flight compliments stealthly subterfuge, as you dodge from cloud to cloud, sneaking up on fortifications too large to approach out in the open.

[VR mode is available on systems running current Oculus drivers. We'll add support for major VR platforms as they emerge.]

Now, I'm really looking forward to seeing what's planned next about the storyline. There's really much potential here for a game combining a good background and nice gameplay mechanics!
The current version is very short and consists mostly in an introduction to the game, but again, one may expect increasing life duration with future releases.

This will be my first ever review on steam. Keep that in mind.

I love dragons. Also keep that in mind.

I just bought this game today, and have only played it for around 30 minutes. That is enough to get through the tutorial and play the first actual mission. Do not expect to be the unstoppable hell machine that a dragon should be. There are other enemy dragons, and there are many flaming arrows and exploding catapult charges. As it is right now, this game could do with two actual things. A difficulty modifier, and a free flight mode. Please put a free flight mode in.

Even more so though, this game has the incredible potential to be the most epic thing to come out of steam. As it is right now, I would call it a playable demo that you have to pay full price for. It takes thirty minutes to play the game through it's entirety right now. Therefore if you don't like it, you can get a refund.

Controls are iffy, but if you use a gamepad (don't use the mouse and keyboard), then it is most excellent. The difficulty comes in aiming your fire breath attacks. We could really do with ye-olde crosshair. The joystick, with the sensitivity turned up after you get the hang of the tutorial, is truely joyous to use. Before you know it you'll be doing swooping flame spewing straffing runs on citadels.

Graphics as is right now are ACCEPTABLE. We could also really use a fog reduction at higher altitude, so as to see the ground. However, there were times soaring over lakes that I thought, "Actually this game is rather beautiful. Sometimes."

The real problems comes in the difficulty. On the second mission, it is an almost necessity to have the sensitivity turned up for dragon-on-dragon combat. It also aids air-to-ground combat. You have to learn to use your mobility to evade the majority of attacks. For a first actual combat level, I feel it's a bit too intense and large scale to work. There are far too many enemies, all of which take several successful attacks to take down. The ranged attack might even need a homing buff to be able to use effectively in the air.

As a pre-alpha game, for me, this is so worth it. I'm a bit nervous about the eventual dirrection this game will take, as I think it would be far better as an open-world RPG a-la Skyrim, but with a much much bigger game world. I'd like to see factions and multiple cities, and the ability to take part in seiges with multiple dragons and armies of humans on either side and...

Yeah. I'd pay a million dollars for that game. I'd give my car, my job, just to have that game. I just don't think, with such a great premise, that just making chapters is a good direction. With that, all it is is combat combat combat. And after the 10th level or so, it gets a little old.

I'd recommend this game for anyone who loves dragons anywhere close to what I do. I'd also recommend it for those of you who love flying, or for those of you who love a great idea in the making. For those of you who want value for your money, perhaps wait till it comes out of early access.

All in all a very well polished little piece of epicness for 17$.

Early Access Review
Posted: 1 September, 2015
For an early access game that just came out Dragonflight isn't too shabby. There isn't much in the game right now, but with the whole early access thing and the release being yesterday (at the time of writing this) that's to be expected.

For those of you who prefer to see the game you can watch the first level, which doubles as the tutorial, and a bit of lore as well. Otherwise skip down a bit and I'll try and give a bit of a Pros and Cons comparison.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wTZNM8TON0Q

Pros:
-Looks: The game doesn't look half bad. Flying over one of the lakes in game looks especially great in my opinion. I've seen a number of games that have been out for much longer, early access and finished, that look worse than this. While there aren't many assests in the game, what has made it in looks quite nice in my opinion.
-Controls: They actually seem to be well done when they work right as well as being something you can adjust easily (That being the sensitivity of the controls. As of now you can't actually rebind any of the keys), though they can be a bit wonky. I mainly use the mouse and keyboard though, so you may have better results if you use a game controller instead.
- Story: Again, there isn't much here, but what is in gives you a decent understanding of the game world. Obviously this is something that will be worked on as the development continues, but it's nice that the devs gave us a bit to start out with instead of just some random levels.
-Options: The game actually lets you choose some settings. Not too much yet, but the ability to change sensitivity is honestly a godsend when the controls start to act up.
-Controller support: While I haven't used a game controller with this game yet, it does come fully supported. From some of what I've seen others do it's probably a more stable option as well. The mouse and keyboard, which do work fine, are probably going to need some fine tuning in the settings menu if you choose to play with them.
-VR support: I don't have any type of VR headset to use so I can't say how well it works, but the option is there if you have the means and urge to test it out. A bit of a warning though, with how diving around and the flight mechanics work, you may want to pass on this if you get motion sick easily.
-You're a dragon: I'm not really sure I have to say much here. You get to play around, fly, fight, and destroy things as a dragon. Even with the unfinished state this is a better dragon game then many others I've played before.

Cons:
-Not much in the game: For those of you who just want to play and don't care much about the development. Right now the game has two levels, one being a tutorial and the other being an actual castle assault. Neither of them take much time. Now this will change as the game is worked on, and you really shouldn't expect too much from an Early Access game on this front, but for those who just want a game to run around in then you may want to wait a bit.
-Sounds: As of now some of the sounds are much louder than any of the others. The opening menu, for example, has the dragon do a roar or two that's obnoxiously loud if you've got headphones on. Anyone planning on recording themselves play will need to equalize the sounds after recording if they want commentary as well.
-Collisions: Defintely one of the more frustrating things right now. If your controls freak out for a second, or you have the bad luck to run into something (Or you got close enough and the game thinks you ran into something) then it might just kill you. In the game you take damage whenever you run into something. This wouldn't be too bad, except that the game tends to start flipping out when you do. Sometimes you can get lucky and it'll work itself out, other times you're probably just gonna die. Not the worst issue I've seen in an early access game, but still definitely a frustrating one.
-Dragon combat: In the second level you have the option of fighting other dragons in the air. It may be easier with the joystick, but as of now it seems to need some fine tuning. It's still fairly enjoyable and not impossible by any stretch of the imagination, but some of the control kinks become a bit more obvious when you're trying to chase after another dragon or get them back in your sights.
-Aiming: As of now it can be difficult to actually see where you're aiming when you attack something. When using your sustained breath attack it's not as bad as you can move it around to get an idea. However using your ranged attack can take a bit of practice before you can start reliably hitting anything, but more than likely you'll still just be hitting the general area you were aiming for rather than any actual spot.

Overall I do recommend the game if you're okay with following the development process. The bits I've played I've enjoyed quite a bit and I'm definitely looking forward to any updates in the future. However, if you're hesitant to spend $15.99 USD on the game that's still got little in the way of things you can do I'd recommend following it or putting it on a wishlist to find easier. Wait for a few more updates or the game to go on sale and I'm sure you'll enjoy the game just as much as I do now.
