About This Game

Tenshu General Description


Tenshu General, is a simple, elegant, Real Time Strategy (RTS) game for those who favor short play sessions over long sessions. Quick casual game play combined with the challenge of continuously adapting enemies allow for a surprisingly challenging exercise in strategy. It is a cross breed between a board game and a classic RTS. If you like Risk or 8 Minute Empire, Tenshu General is your game.

Set in medieval Japan, you will take on the role of a Tenshu General tasked with defending your Tenshu castle against invaders while expanding your own territory. Can you beat the general of the enemy Tenshu at his game?

Tenshu General Features:

  • Easy to play, hard to master concept
  • Campaign mode, to help you teach the fundamentals
  • 10 different maps to allow you a wide variety of battlefields
  • Smart enemies make different choices in each new game
  • Terrain influences your movement and battle outcome
  • Map Editor (for advanced users): Create your own maps
  • Full screen and windowed mode
  • Steam achievements and Steam trading cards
  • Available for Windows, Mac and Linux

This is a summary of my review video, which I encourage you to watch if you are looking for a more in-depth review.


Tenshu General is a casual simple real time strategy game set in feudal Japan where you play as a General of an army defending your own territory and expanding and battling to conquer other territories. There is music that plays for the opening menu that is of that time in history and culture. I believe that it would have been beneficial to have that music continue during the actual gameplay, as it would have added to the atmosphere and immersion to the game, instead of the silence or marching of troops that is the games only sounds during gameplay. The graphics are simple, but it fits to the gameplay and mechanics that the game promotes. There have been a few crashes in my gameplay, but the progress I lost was minimal, and the devs are active in trying to fix any issues. It is clear from their activity on Steam and personally that they care about this game.

The game has a fair bit of content for the price they are asking, Each campaign is fast paced, and is centered around strategic, military and money management. You can upgrade tools, farming, and livestock to increase your resources. You have a max of four armies that you can upgrade, each of which need to be replenished after you engage in battle. It would have been nice to be able to replenish entire armies instead of individual troops as a lot of my time was spent micro-managing each army, as it pulled me out of the enjoyment of the game. It becomes a delicate balance between grabbing up available land in the beginning, then harassing enemies in a give and take scenario to keep them from expanding their territories into yours. You are fighting on different fronts, and you are at the mercy of the slow map movement before you can assess how you have progressed.

The game is simplistic, but overall it works out fairly well. I felt that it respected my time, and was enjoyable. The slow reaction time did pull me out of immersion a bit, but overall the game was fairly balanced. This is an easy recommend.

Tenshu General fills a very specific niche. It's a real time strategy game for those who aren't all the good at real time strategy games. This is a category I find myself firmly sitting in. A decent amount of the first PC games that I played (and were good enough to still remember) were real time strategies and my favourite game for a long time was Age of Mythology. With all that being said however I'm pretty rubbish at them. I'm honestly not sure how many of these games I only beat because I used cheats or how many I must have given up half way through the campaigns because it got difficult. The problem I think is that you need to manage so many different things in real time; troops, resources, upgrades. It can be very easy to forget about this or that.

Tenshu General is something of a devolution of the genre in much the same way as DiveKick is of the fighting genre. The amount of things you need to think about is very limited. You have one single base and can have up to four separate armies that all move as one. Resource Management is stripped down to a single resource, rice, and upgrading towns to collect more is a simple process. That isn't however to say that Tenshu General is simple. The troops you buy for each army, which upgrades you buy and when, where you place your ambassador etc etc. There's still a large amount of choice and complexity.

There are also elements taken from grand strategy, namely the provinces. Each section of land is a separate province, of your army is the only one on it you claim it gaining any resources collected there to you. But not only this you move slower through unclaimed territories and you cannot pass through them meaning that spending the time to capture a province with no rice production may be worth it. Each province also has a different terrain which can impart bonuses or negatives on a particular troop type meaning that army composition is important.

However Tenshu General has it's limitations. Whilst there aren't enough small factors to consider such that it overloads you they also perhaps don't have enough effect. The way to win almost any map feels exactly the same; capture as much as early as possible and crush your opponents main army before they are able to build it up then simply take out whatever small forces they can muster until you have control of the whole area and have four fully powered armies with which to storm their castle. It hardly matters about positioning and terrain advantage when you have a fully stocked army and almost every game plays out in the same way. The only real difference for this is if you include more players.

As I said right at the start Tenshu General fills a niche and it does it very well. It's simple on the surface but has just enough depth to it to allow for more interesting play. It's certainly not for a hardcore RTS fan but for those who maybe missed the bandwagon of the early to mid 2000's RTS it's a small and cheap game that would be a great introduction to the genre.

Tenshu Generals is a neat Indie Samurai Civ Game.

If you can't quite afford Civilization, are looking for a lighter game for your lower end pc or just want something that isn't so triple AAA this is the game for you.

Gameplay:

The game plays like a risk board game in which income is added over time with the addition of being able to upgrade certain terrain tiles that contain villages. These villages provide rice income which is used to buy everything within the game, you then must raise an army and compete against opposing empires for domination of the map.

Unlike Civ there is no diplomacy victory that I've encountered, it all about the combat conquest which is fun in itself. (And perhaps leaves room for expansion?)

Verdict:

Definitely not on the 2016 must buy top ign of the year list, but I enjoyed playing the game and will probably come back to it from time to time until completion as I did enjoy it. If you need a cheap fun compact civilization conquest game this is the game for you and at 5.99 can't really complain to much.

17/30 - Not a Top tier game
(But it's a great game for what it is and for it's price point it more than delivers.)
