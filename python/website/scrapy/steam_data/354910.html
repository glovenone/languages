Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The Imagination Vent team feels that a community tested, and balanced fighting game is a better result of interaction from the very beginning. Not all content is available and the game requires balancing feedback from the community.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We anticipate for Indie Assault to be in development for another 12 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of Indie Assault will include additional fighters, stages, items, and modes! Notwithstanding, overall engine revisions such as dynamic lighting and character balancing issues.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is in a fully playable Alpha build! Our goal is to release Indie Assault onto "Early Access", allowing us to grow our community and directly incorporate valuable feedback into balancing and expanding the game.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, the game will be priced differently during and after Early Access. For instance, early adopters will be able to purchase the game for 33% off its final cost.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Keeping the community closely in the loop about character moveset revisions, and content additions. Also working with them to polish the game's physics to appeal to an audience desiring to push the game engine to its limit.”

About This Game

Indie Assault is a love letter to your favorite fighting games, with iconic characters fighting in dynamic multiplayer battles.

! Notice that Indie Assault requires the use of a DualShock 3 controller or Xbox 360 gamepad. The game does have custom controls, but at launch the Keyboard inputs are not universally working.

Smash your friends to oblivion in Indie Assault on Steam! Our games features simple, intuitive controls, allowing us to provide fast-paced, accessible gameplay for competitive and casual players alike. Indie Assault will initially launch with a diverse roster of original and licensed indie characters. With entertaining game modes, the ability to play locally with up to 4-players, and an in-depth training mode. Indie Assault provides you with a great platform fighting game experience!

Key Features-


* A diverse initial roster of 4 characters, featuring stars from Freedom Planet, Megatroid, and the upcoming fighting game Ascension X!
* More original and licensed characters to be added as the community grows
* "Burst" - a unique new way to extend ledge play
- A strategic mechanic that is able to reset your double jump or enable you to break out of a combo
* "Blitz Assault" a comeback mechanic that enables the characters to utilize their ultimate ability
* Multiple game modes for competitive and casual play, with more to be added upon community request
* Detailed and diverse stages originating from each character's universe
* Up to 4 local players, in any configuration imaginable!
* Training Mode with extensive customization options
* Quick thinking AI to play in addition to or instead of others
* Steam Achievements!
^ Are acquired, when you unlock in-game Medals that have stat boosts

Do I recommend this game? No, not at all.

Why not?
Well, where to start... The game's physics is pretty messy. You don't feel like you are in that much control of your character, and your character will just abruptly stop when being launched off-stage for some reason, instead of continuing to fly off or letting you get back manually.

90% of the game's animations are pretty much a mess, as they don't transition well into each other at all, leaving characters to pop back into position during the time of 1 frame, which doesn't look very nice overall.
A lot of them hardly looks like an attack, with characters somehow doing damage by stretching out a leg like it was a dead fish.

Now, the balancing issues at the moment are also pretty huge. Spade can spam projectiles all the time as well as having a shield around him to damage enemies, making him almost too difficult to approach in any situation. Spade can also use the game's Burst ability around 200 times per second, which also nearly makes him able to fly.

Not to mention that Titus is like a moving tank with the speed of Sonic The Hedgehog. In other words a pretty bad mix of strength and speed; a character can't be too good on all sides, but Titus sure doesn't follow that rule too well right now.
And the other two available characters pretty much has similar issues too, but Titus and Spade are the worst offenders on the balance overall.

There's also quite a few ledges you can grab that makes you grab the air and fly several meters away from the actual ledge. Blast zones on stages were also inside the screen at times, making you die from an invisible killzone inside the camera's view.
Hitboxes on characters are also just a single big box, so it doesn't matter if you hit as character's leg or if you actually missed by hitting in-between limbs and such. All you have to do is hit the large box around the characters, and it'll count as a hit. Making it easy to just throw out attacks and hit even if you clearly missed the character.

Landing lag when jumping is also absurdly long, making you unable to move when landing for a whole second. Which really doesn't help on letting you feel like you are in control of your character.

The menu itself is also a mess. It works okay with a controller, but it's barely functional with a keyboard; with inputs taking up to a second to really react. This is not an issue with a controller fortunately, but it doesn't help that the menu itself is a bit messy and the fact that the game's text font is pretty hard to read at times.

The worst part about the gameplay itself with all other errors aside, though? The burst mechanic. Easily the burst mechanic.
It's clear that the burst mechanic is an extra feature the developers intended to make it different from Smash Bros, but it just doesn't work. The burst mechanic sallows you to release a shockwave and push other characters away, but it's extremely overpowered on it's own, enabling you to cancel and outright block much stronger attacks hitting you.
And after using the burst, you will regain your double jump as well as your recovery. This means that you can recover regardless of how far out you are. By using the burst mechanic I was able to burst, then regain my double jump and use it to gain height, and repeat. Allowing me to recover infinitely.
This mechanic is just outright broken and it just doesn't work. Even if you nerf it quite a bit I still don't like this mechanic at all, and like much of the rest of this game, I just don't think it can be saved at this point.

I will be honest, though. The game IS in Early Access. So I will cut the developers a tiny bit of slack on that regard.

But even with that, this game is probably not even ready for Early Access in the first place. It's so incomplete even on the small amount of content it currently has that I'm surprised it even runs. It's barely patched up enough to work properly.
The awkward physics and the downright stiff animations make it a pretty bad experience right now.

It's not the fighting game PC has been waiting for, and it's absolutely not worth the title of an indie alternative to Smash Bros. If you are looking for that, this game is just not the answer.

No matter how you put it, "Indie Assault" has a really, really long way to go before it even becomes an enjoyable game to play.

Indie Assault is by and large what you would expect.

Over the years there have been many attempts by indie developers to clone the Super Smash Brothers game mechanics. Apart from a few games such as Brawlhalla (which I dont personally believe to SSB clones), these have largely been flops.

So far the content in this game is significantly limited - but this is often the case in early access. However what it has done so far it has done right. The physics and controls are by and large pretty solid, they have also done the right thing by including a reasonable amount of combos. Basically any attacks or skills that SSB has, this game also has. The level design also seems fairly solid at this stage.

The graphics, as you can see, are not great. However it is crisp and clear, and there are a reasonable amount of frames involved in attack sequences - which has not been the case in similar games in the past. Ideally I would like to see some improvement in graphics, but I imagine this is unlikely. The graphics are suitable enough.

How this game turns out really depends on the direction it takes during development. New characters and levels are likely. I would also like to see a few tweaks in the mechanics - at this stage knocking people off the edge seems fairly easy. I would also like to see some more mechanics that differentiate this game from SSB, and am hoping for a few pleasant surprises during development.

Its a strong start at this stage, but it still has to prove itself in the following months.

In terms of current content, the price doesnt really seem worth it. This is often the case with early access games though. I would hazard a guess that future content will make the price tag more reasonable. Based on this I would suggest you purchase this game as an investment if you're into this type of game.

Not to mention that Titus is like a moving tank with the speed of Sonic The Hedgehog. In other words a pretty bad mix of strength and speed; a character can't be too good on all sides, but Titus sure doesn't follow that rule too well right now.
And the other two available characters pretty much has similar issues too, but Titus and Spade are the worst offenders on the balance overall.

There's also quite a few ledges you can grab that makes you grab the air and fly several meters away from the actual ledge. Blast zones on stages were also inside the screen at times, making you die from an invisible killzone inside the camera's view.
Hitboxes on characters are also just a single big box, so it doesn't matter if you hit as character's leg or if you actually missed by hitting in-between limbs and such. All you have to do is hit the large box around the characters, and it'll count as a hit. Making it easy to just throw out attacks and hit even if you clearly missed the character.

Landing lag when jumping is also absurdly long, making you unable to move when landing for a whole second. Which really doesn't help on letting you feel like you are in control of your character.

The menu itself is also a mess. It works okay with a controller, but it's barely functional with a keyboard; with inputs taking up to a second to really react. This is not an issue with a controller fortunately, but it doesn't help that the menu itself is a bit messy and the fact that the game's text font is pretty hard to read at times.

The worst part about the gameplay itself with all other errors aside, though? The burst mechanic. Easily the burst mechanic.
It's clear that the burst mechanic is an extra feature the developers intended to make it different from Smash Bros, but it just doesn't work. The burst mechanic sallows you to release a shockwave and push other characters away, but it's extremely overpowered on it's own, enabling you to cancel and outright block much stronger attacks hitting you.
And after using the burst, you will regain your double jump as well as your recovery. This means that you can recover regardless of how far out you are. By using the burst mechanic I was able to burst, then regain my double jump and use it to gain height, and repeat. Allowing me to recover infinitely.
This mechanic is just outright broken and it just doesn't work. Even if you nerf it quite a bit I still don't like this mechanic at all, and like much of the rest of this game, I just don't think it can be saved at this point.

I will be honest, though. The game IS in Early Access. So I will cut the developers a tiny bit of slack on that regard.

But even with that, this game is probably not even ready for Early Access in the first place. It's so incomplete even on the small amount of content it currently has that I'm surprised it even runs. It's barely patched up enough to work properly.
The awkward physics and the downright stiff animations make it a pretty bad experience right now.

It's not the fighting game PC has been waiting for, and it's absolutely not worth the title of an indie alternative to Smash Bros. If you are looking for that, this game is just not the answer.

No matter how you put it, "Indie Assault" has a really, really long way to go before it even becomes an enjoyable game to play.

Ideally I would like to see some improvement in graphics, but I imagine this is unlikely. The graphics are suitable enough.

How this game turns out really depends on the direction it takes during development. New characters and levels are likely. I would also like to see a few tweaks in the mechanics - at this stage knocking people off the edge seems fairly easy. I would also like to see some more mechanics that differentiate this game from SSB, and am hoping for a few pleasant surprises during development.

Its a strong start at this stage, but it still has to prove itself in the following months.

In terms of current content, the price doesnt really seem worth it. This is often the case with early access games though.
