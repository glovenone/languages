Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We have been developing Metaverse with a somewhat uncommon development model: to build it alongside our customers every step of the way, from the very beginning. We believe that the dedicated customers of a product often know what's best and what should be next for a product. A product can only be as great as -- and only as smart as -- the sum of its participants. Early Access affords us the unique ability to build Metaverse in this way, and we're really excited about that.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Since the most defining and desirable aspects of Early Access are the very ways we want to perpetually develop Metaverse, it may stay in Early Access much longer than other titles. We view that as a positive thing, and hope to create a new category of games. Most games continue past the stages of Alpha and Beta to a "gold master" state that is largely the final end product. But that's not what we want. Rather, we are excited to build Metaverse in a never-ending, always-improving "Delta" state.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“What many would colloquially refer to as the "full version" of Metaverse will represent many months of collaborative feature development and refinement from our Early Access participants.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“This version of Metaverse represents the product of more than two years of development by it's two creators. It has a playable intro story and a sandbox mode beyond that where players can explore and create. There is still so much we're excited to build, and we believe we're really only showing the beginning of what's possible with Metaverse. We believe that involving our customers in the development and design of the game from the earliest point possible leads us to immense potential. And we believe that this initial shipping version has strong core foundations in Lore, Mechanics, and extensibility for us all to build upon together.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We hope that with enough success and momentum the game will ultimately become free to access. We hesitate to use the term, "Free to Play," because our definition of ethical "free to play" is very different from the current standard definition. What we can say is that any amount of money invested into Metaverse during the Early Access period will yield the same amount (if not more) utility and opportunity than the equivalent amount invested differently after Early Access.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“One of the primary reasons we are building this game and releasing it via Early Access is to involve our customers in every aspect of Metaverse's development. We have a long laundry list of cool features and mechanics that we are excited to build and explore in the coming months. We desperately want customers to help prioritize and even redefine those goals with us. As it exists today, Metaverse is the beginning of the game that we've always wanted to make for ourselves. And we believe it is also the game that many others have been waiting for.”

About This Game


Dig, discover, design, deploy, and defend. Explore your very own planet, and eventually help conquer or help build up the planets of others.

What is Metaverse?

Metaverse is a voxel based action/adventure (Sci-Fi!) game about digging, discovering, designing, deploying, and defending. But we see it as more than a game. Metaverse is an ever-evolving platform, shaped by the actions of its players. Right now that involves players giving feedback about the direction of development. Before long, players will have a much more direct role in how Metaverse shapes up by traveling to the planets of other players and by trading and selling their fabrications. Fabrications are custom objects that can be created in-game by players, using the materials harvested from their planet. See below for more details.

What can I do in Metaverse right now?

Because of the technical challenges involved with setting up procedural terrains otherwise, most voxel based games offer only either completely cubed geometry or completely rounded geometry. Metaverse, being a simulation from the future, has cracked the code, and is able to beautifully combine the two in a way that makes logical sense! The procedurally generated planet that currently ships with Metaverse is 300 Km in diameter, which is indeed very big. For now, letâs just call it, âNew Texas.â If you were to run in a straight line without stopping, we estimate it would take you about 28 hours to completely circle the planet. Fabricator
In the Fabricator, you can process the raw resources youâve collected from your planet into refined materials. Using these refined materials in the Simulation mode of the Fabricator, you can create just about anything you want. And as long as youâve got enough refined resources for the elements youâve used, you can fabricate it into an actual object that can be taken outside to your planet and deployed. Elements in the fabricator are based on real periodic table elements, and have accurate densities. Early Access Review
Posted: 6 October, 2014
Cubic is a voxel sandbox game. From the trailer, it seems very much like Minecraft and, to some extent, it is. That's mostly at the moment because Cubic is very early alpha (0.0.2.9 in this review) and contains breaking, placing, jumping, crafting and storage. From what Cubic promises though, it has the potential to be much more than any voxel sandbox game.

In particular, there is a "building fabricator" which I originally thought was a remake of Minecraft's crafting recipes. In reality, the building fabricator promises to be a powerful crafting set-up to create a variety of interactive objects. At the moment, Cubic offers a button and a hinge which you can use to create your own doors in any shape or size. In the future (and I cannot express enough "in the future"), Cubic promises to add a variety of materials (which I assume will have different properties) and different components (like potentially levers, wheels or pistons). Don't get me wrong though - in 0.0.2.9, none of these have been added just yet.

The game is also clearly going to be more adventure-based. In the tutorial, you are assisted by a flying companion named Corban which gives you the tutorial via story-based lessons. Later, there's a story quest involving Corban. For starters, I liked the voice-acting of Corban but he could've talked less. Cubic looks to be planning more quests which opens opportunities for other NPCs, monsters, weapons, vehicles, worlds, modding opportunities, trading or side quests! Again though, Cubic has none of these just yet.

In brief though, the sandbox, features, quests, block types and others are very limited due to the early alpha build + there are a lot of bugs. Would I recommend it currently for $25 USD? No. Could it be worth it in the future? Yes, and it's worth following its development.

Posted: 30 September, 2014
At the time of this review this is a fresh game on steam.
Keep in mind this is an early access game. This is not AAA polished ready to go game. If you do not like the early access theme, supporting devolopment, contributing to the community. Then this probably isn't for you. If you do like supporting the develpoment and contributing to the community I urge you friend take a look at this game it's going to be a wild ride.

"Give me the place to stand, and I shall move the earth."

The building fabricator, I have a feeling many hours will be spent in here.
My first project was going to be a walker. You can have hinges, activated by buttons. I figured I'd hinge up 4 legs. Put 4 buttons spread out on a few blocks.( Call that a control panel son ). I had it in such a way it would've walked sideways..Fear the crab people...Little did I know how this story would soon end.
So I ran out into a clearing, I remember this all too well. The moon was out big and bright.. Like it had a front row seat to watch what was about to happen. I set the ClayWalker2 that I had carefully crafted on the ground in the bright moonlight. Walked to the back and jumped in the cockpit I had made. Smiled looking onto 4 red buttons my control panel is awesome. Pushing the first button that is all that was needed. I was sent into the air at super high speeeeeeeeed!

The devs are active on the forums with community and ready to answer any questions you have.
I am also working on a guide covering the basic fabricator functions.
