About This Game

Note:

To read the entire main story (5+ hours of story!) of the character of your choice, you'll need to purchase their DLC. The Prologue and Chapter 1 of each character is free-to-play. Only Shou's route has been completed so far.

Story:

This original English language visual novel tackles romance in an urban fantasy setting, with the aim of blending genres and deconstructing common tropes.


After a ritual forces immense magical powers onto you, you're thrown into a new world with new threats and challenges that you'd never dreamed of. Play as twenty year-old university student Tsubasa Fujimoto as she learns to balance her daily life with her new supernatural one - all while falling in love.

Will you survive the perilous journey and become a legendary sorceress?

Features:

  • Experience a different kind of love story with a balance between dark and light themes, and a strong emphasis on fantasy and action.
  • Learn about a world with deep lore and it's own unique universe.
  • Five guys, five completely different stories to experience. The story changes with every route!
  • Detailed sprites and backgrounds.
  • Several gorgeous painted CG images per route.
  • Enjoy atmospheric high quality music, hand-picked for every scene.
  • Three distinct canon endings per character - depending on your choices.
  • Five hours of content with each main story with additional content on the way.

So I grabbed Tatsuya's route after much deliberation over which to purchase. Overall I have to say that I was pleased with it. Let me do the customary pros and cons list:

Pros:
1. Art is gorgeous. The characters are so soft and colorful, and I love how their avatars actually change when they're speaking/upset/happy. The protagonist even changes her clothes to fit the situation!
2. The game is small and quick to download.
3. You're getting a lot for your money. I grabbed Tatsuya's route on sale for, what, 3 bucks? I got to play for about 3 hours, and that's because I'm a really fast reader. Honestly, I devour books like they're comics. You get 15 chapters to go through, and the epilogues are super cheap, too.
4. You get to pick and choose. I have no interest in some of the characters, so I don't have to shell out 30-35 bucks for them all if I'm only interested in playing a few.
5. There's a great variety of characters. I loved meeting Tatsuya's quirky family and seeing the beautiful avatars.
6. Main character gets an actual female friend she isn't competing with or jealous of. I love that.
7. Lots of great story building.
8. Beautiful music.
Cons:
1. There are not enough CGs in my opinion. My interest starts to wane when I'm going through 4 or 5 chapters without a special image.
2. There are a lot of grammatical errors that should be touched up. They just yank me out of the story when a word is spelled wrong or there's a typo that I have to go back and read carefully to understand.
3. The main character has some flaws that really bugged me. [Non-specific spoilers?] She couldn't remember ANYTHING! No joke, she'd forget someone we had just encountered the day before. She was so easily confused. She passed out 3 times in one route. She freaked out over Tatsuya (she'd known him for a very short period of time) talking to a girl he clearly had history with. Three men were pursuing her in one route and gave me icky Mary Sue vibes.
4. The blue/pink/neon red looks okay in anime, but it was so bizarre to see it on slightly realistic people. I laughed a few times; I couldn't help it.
5. Some big plot points were not resolved, and I suppose this could be an issue of you just have to buy all the routes to see how they intertwine.
6. The characters have a tendency to act like they're in high school rather than college. They avoid each other, have enormous emotional outbursts, overreact, and it was just a bit obnoxious.

A lot of the flaws are nit-picky, I know. I was irritated by the game at times, but I was also very interested in finishing it. I will likely buy more of the routes, and I'm looking forward to seeing what comes of this. I have a lot of respect for game developers who bring otomes to us in English! By all means, I recommend this game; it has it's quirks and flaws, but it's totally worth trying.

I recommend this game to those who enjoy visual novels, specifically otome visual novels, and an interesting supernatural storyline and cast. This is a "set in early university" and "discovering your supernatural abilities" kind of VN.

Pros:
- Interesting characters
- Generally well-written (I like smooth, well-edited writing and MDSA has it)
- You want to find out "what's going on"
- Main character is interesting and intelligent (a nice change)
- Artwork is very nice for the characters (a little basic for the backgrounds)
- Music is nice
- Because each storyline is distinct, you are only skipping text within a particular story arc to get to different endings (other than for the free prologue of course)
- The guys are cute and do "angsty but endearing" quite well; kind of important for this type of game!

Cons:
- No voice acting (a bit of a downside when you consider overall price of game); I'm a big fan of good voice acting, so this is a big one for me, but it's *not* a deal-breaker since this is still a nice game
- Cost: you will have to spend about $7 per route
- Little interaction between the various main characters while on a particular route (but there is some!)
- Maybe a slight feeling of blandness? Not as emotionally compelling as some other games? But to be fair, it's still a neat story with an interesting premise and interesting characters.

OVERALL RATING: 7/10 but a solid 7 (you won't get an Ozmafia/Hakuouki/Amnesia immersion/quality but in some ways you get a better MC and you do get a good story)

5. Some big plot points were not resolved, and I suppose this could be an issue of you just have to buy all the routes to see how they intertwine.
6. The characters have a tendency to act like they're in high school rather than college. They avoid each other, have enormous emotional outbursts, overreact, and it was just a bit obnoxious.

A lot of the flaws are nit-picky, I know. I was irritated by the game at times, but I was also very interested in finishing it. I will likely buy more of the routes, and I'm looking forward to seeing what comes of this. I have a lot of respect for game developers who bring otomes to us in English! By all means, I recommend this game; it has it's quirks and flaws, but it's totally worth trying.
