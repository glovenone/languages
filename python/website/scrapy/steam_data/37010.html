About This Game

It was July 7th, 2011.


While investigating reports of unlawful weapons trafficking, peacekeeping forces encountered an unclassified carnivorous species occupying a small village in Northern Africa. These rabid creatures were easily provoked and resistant to conventional attack. Eight specimens were encountered in the region now referred to as "Ground:ALPHA"... Only four were successfully contained for study. Victims of the attack were quarantined at a research facility nearby. These victims displayed signs of internal liquefaction and accelerated necrosis, similar to symptoms of other flesh-eating viruses native to that region.


But it wasn't a viral outbreak. It was a parasitic infestation. A previously unidentified species of hook worm was found beneath the skin of the Ground: ALPHA specimens. These parasites, we call them "Alpha-worms", laid dozens of eggs inside their hosts, which in turn produced numerous larvae. These larvae penetrated the bloodstream and triggered a variety of genetic failures. However, unlike most genetic failures, these symptoms were not fatal. Instead, infested victims were larger, strongerâ¦ and driven by hunger. Using the research gained from the Ground: ALPHA specimens, a team of some special individuals were assembled. Minor outbreaks occurred over the next few years, but elite squad quickly put them down. Which brings us to the current situation in Tokyo...

  • Exciting and challenging battles with 3 different weapons
  • QTE and cinematic action
  • Variety of powerful ninja abilities
  • Weapon and ninjutsu upgrade system
  • Crisscross the city of Tokyo with intense gunfights

I purchased this game in April 2016 and run it on Windows 10.
The game requirements are not high and you can play decent
at 60 FPS on older configurations (AMD 7750).

The game is a Ninja Gaiden clone infected with QTE all over the
place - you can not even finish a combo without being annoyed
by QTE.

The game is broken: you can not save your progress. At least
with the current version on steam. Also the game is sometimes
complaining that the right memory slot is not selected and
some texts are directly from the console version.

What kind of memory slots and cards shall I use on the PC?
This is rediculos. Also, the game says that your save game
is successfuly complted. When you try to load it, you spawn
directly after the INTRO sequence from the beginning of
the game at lavel 1.

It is not so bad. You can pick the game for casual play and
play it through. It is not hard. Executing basic commands
will not invoke QTE. But any kind of combo attempt will
fire the QTE imediately.

The enemies are very stupid and can easily be killed.
But - not everything is so easy. The enemies use
cheap tricks, when surrounded by 3, they wait for
you to attack one and then another one will jump
into you and damage you for sure.

Boss fights are nice but get repeditive. You just have
to repeat one and the same thing over and over
again. Killing a boss is epic. The feeling is nice.

The game mechanics are somewhat funny. Some
moves like wall run use an additional key for no
reason. In ninja gaiden if you run against wall
and jump on it, it becomes a wall run. Here you
have to hold the RT for the wall run.

You can play the game with the mouse. I find this
very nice. A lot of movie sequences use the game
engine with a far too strong blur filter.

Don't bother with the story. It has some flips but
everything feels so cheap.

If you could at least save and restore properly,
it would be a nice alternative weekend game.

For sure in 2016, this is not worth the $10.

Ever wondered what the b-side to Ninja Gaiden would be like? From Software knew the answer and gave us Ninja Blade. The mundane gameplay isn't exactly top-tier quality and in a way all of Ninja Blade reminds me a lot about Froms earlier works that were always kinda meh but had some distinct styles going for them (I am thinking King's Field, Murakumo, Otogi or Frame Gride right now). The actual storyline and monster designs are pretty cool, reminiscent of late 80ies and early 90ies anime and I would be lying if I wasn't into it - especially since the game takes itself serious, which is always a plus in my book. What starts as Resident Evil with swords (with the enemy parasite reminding me of "The Many" from System Shock 2) turns into something that has some resemblance to Fist of the Northstar and martial arts flicks.

Unfortunately, it's all downhill from here - for starters the game suffers from an overabundance of QTEs, turret sequences and AWFUL wallrunning/walljumping segments. Combat is imprecise and floaty, movement kinda jerky - reminding me about some Dreamcast-era hack'n'slay titles. I do not know wether or not it is the 60fps (that the console versions did not have) that turned Ninja Blade into the glitchfest that it is, but this game is seriously bugging out at times. In 7 hours playtime I haven't seen the animation for swinging and jumping from poles align once with the pole I am swinging and jumping from - the hero is always miles away from the pole. Then there are some dramatic changes of scenery - you go through a door and BAM! you are teleported to a different area, complete with different lighting and new camera position. And the wallrun-sequences are straight outta glitch city with the character repositioning himself constantly because the game has some serious hitdetection issues when wallrunning - all of it feels really poorly made, like the whole game barely holds together (in fact, it always locks up when I went to quit game). And honestly, it's just not that fun (should mention that at least 2 out of the 9 stages are filler and recycle bosses and even parts of levels) - not when you have alternatives like Devil May Cry 4 or Metal Gear Rising right here on Steam. Might as well just watch this one instead and don't bother with playing it.

"Quck Time Event - The Game"

This needs to be said right off the bat. The game is chock full of Action Commands and Quick Time Events. They are everywhere. 90% of the cutscenes will be Quick Time Events. Every boss fight will have at least 5, if not more. If you despise QTE's with every fiber of your being, this game is not for you. But there is good news! This game doesn't go all Resident Evil and make you watch 10 minutes of cutscenes beforehand or throw a game over at you if you screw up. Rather, the game just pulls a Prince of Persia: Sands of Time bit and simply rewinds the QTE back a few seconds. The prompts rarely change, so if you miss something, you can do it again very quickly instead of waiting forever to get it right.

In a nutshell, the gameplay is action/platformer. You will be wall running, jumping, and swinging through levels while slicing up zombies and other monstrosities through Japan as it's taken over by some kind of virus. Ken (The protaganist) is basically Ryu from Ninja Gaiden, slicing everything into 1/8ths. You get three weapons, a standard sword, a heavy sword for armored enemies, and daggers on strings (think sword-chucks) to clear swarms of easy enemies.

The story isn't anything special, but it thrives on being absurd. At one point you will land a broken airplace on a highway, and then pull it to a stop. By yourself. You will also smash a giant spider with a wrecking ball. It's all in good fun.

As was stated, QTE's make up a huge chunk of the game, so it tries to keep them non-punishing. Nothing bad happens when you fail, it just rewinds a bit. There's a score system in place which includes how many times you failed QTE's in a chapter, but you have a fail a lot (like 100's of times) for it to make a dent in the rating system.

If you like watching a super ninja slice, dice, smash, and destroy everything that Resident Evil came up with first, all the while running down skyscrapers, then this game is perfect for you.
