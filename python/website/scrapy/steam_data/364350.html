About This Game

Penelope Barker and Aaron S. Reid are agents of the extra-governmental Pandora Global Corp, which works in parallel with the CIA. Unbeknown to them, they are also guinea pigs in an ambitious and dangerous project...

The corporation is experimenting with a modification to DNA that is capable of altering the faculties of the guinea-pig subjects, thereby granting them powers beyond the ordinary.

But for what purpose? Find out as you venture into mysterious settings, unravel the adventure and come to understand The Apotheosis Project.

+ A budget point and click science fiction adventure by Midian Design
+ Full English voice acting starring Jesse Gunn (Warmer) & Donna Preston
+ Full original soundtrack by dagaz (also comes included in My Library > Music)
+ Control 2 characters, switch between agents to further your adventure!
+ In-game score system, can you reach the top?
+ Find over 30 extra hidden Steam achievements!
+ Soundtrack included in Library > Music
+ Suitable for younger players, lighthearted story & simple controls

There are maybe only two puzzles that require coordination (with the heroes just standing on two buttons) - and we never get to use their superpowers by our own (only automatically and in predefined places).

Sorry, but 'The Apotheosis Project' is not the game it could be if all its potentials were realized. Still, I would say it is a decent game that could be recommended to adventure fans.

Full Review:

INTRO:
Penelope Barker and Aaron S. Reid are agents of Pandora Global Corp., an organization that works in parallel with the CIA. What Penelope and Aaron do not know is that they are also guinea pigs in a secret experiment - the Apotheosis Project - where scientist has tampered with the human DNA. Subjects receive special powers, which are surreptitiously suppressed by antibodies, but can be activated by means of a code word.
Penelope and Aaron are sent on a mission by Pandora Parlow, the head of the organization, to get a relic. When they meet their contact person all goes wrong and they end up being captured. But, without the antibodies, their special powers beginning to awaken. Penelope discovers that she can raise hazardous energy with her hands and Aaron turns out to be able to trigger changes in time and space.
The two end up in a strange world, inhabited by aliens and robots. They discover the secrets behind the Apotheosis Project and its consequences for the entire world.

STORY & PLAYABLE CHARACTERS:
The story is told well dosed. Along with the main characters you'll discover more and more of the plot and the hidden agenda of Pandora. Aaron and Penelope remain well in their character role and handle the problems in their own way, whereby Aaron often tries to lighten up a difficult situation with some light humour. The two clearly like each other, despite their small humorous altercations.
They solve the problems they encounter during their adventure together or apart. It is important to let both Aaron and Penelope look at items and have them both to talk to other characters in the game. After all, they both have their own way of understanding, character and qualities. Moreover, you can be earning points along, up to a total of one thousand. It pays to reach that number and it also makes you more mindful of the environment and the story during the game, which benefits the fun of playing this adventure.

GAMEPLAY:
The puzzles are logically interwoven into the story. If you want you can obtain helpful information and sometimes even hints during the game. Useful items can be obtained in exchange for another object or by a service on return. While solving situations/puzzles you do need to use your ingenuity.
During your quest you will find many items you can pick up and place in your inventory and use in certain situations, but some of these items are not needed and only serve to confuse a.k.a. red herrings. While playing you're always looking for opportunities to use all of these items and that is a nice addition for the true adventurer.
You can, while playing, freely switch between Aaron and Penelope, through a portrait of both in the inventory bar. Sometimes it is even necessary, as Penelope for example encounters a problem that can only be solved by Aaron or vise versa.
Your inventory folds out if you hoover your mouse-pointer to the bottom of your screen, very pleasant and effective. Naturally, each character has its own inventory, but items are interchangeable between Penelope and Aaron. The object gets stored in the inventory of the character that receives the object.
With a mouse click, the two playable characters walk freely and smoothly through the game scene.

GRAPHICS:
The in-game environment looks nice. The backgrounds are very detailed and matches the atmosphere and story. A thrift shop is stuffed really messy with silly things and you see the flashing reflection of the neon advertisings while in a rainy street. An office is sleek equipped and on the platform of the subway you see rubbish and graffiti. When in Infraworld, the colours are sober and while in a modern residential tower, the colours are tight and bright. All together this creates a nice, convincing impression.
Many scenes contain one or two small background animations. The transitions between the game-chapters are shown as a close-up of a three-piece comic and match perfectly with the characters and surroundings.

SOUND:
The game scenes contain perfectly fitting background music and certain situations are supported with nice sound effects.

VOICE ACTING:
The voice acting is top-notch, done by professional voice actors/actresses.

STEAM ACHIEVEMENTS:
In total there are 35 steam achievements to collect, varying from easy to hard.
In total, the game offers 35 steam achievements, obtainable in a nice mix from easy to tough.

CONFIGURABILITY:
The game is configurable using winsetup.exe:
1. Right-mousebutton-click on the installed 'The Apotheosis Project' title in your game library,
2. Select ' Properties'
3. Select ' Browse local files'
4. Right-mousebutton-click on the file winsetup.exe and select 'Run as administrator' / Double-click the file 'winsetup.exe'
5. Set/select/change whatever needed
6. Click button 'Save'
7. Run game via Steam

Some available and possible useful settings:
* Set 'full screen' or 'windowed mode',
* Select Graphics Driver (Direct Draw or Direct3D 9)
* Select Grahics Filter,
* Select Scaling,
* Select Digital sound device & MIDI device,
* Enable/disable 'Black Borders' (Side, Top & Bottom)

SUMMARY:
The Apotheosis Project is a 2D retro indie adventure that comes with hi-res graphics, full voice acting, two playable characters, innovative and varied puzzles in a compelling science fiction story.

So, should I by this game:
If you are in to retro 2D adventure games with modern graphics and a good story/plot:
Yes, this game is for sure worth playing and worth the money. Definitely recommended.

I hope the developer will take the positive features of the game (good length/price ratio, pretty good dialogues, somewhat interesting story) and make a better game next time.

The story had potential, but it is developed in a very uninteresting and random way. If it would'n have been for my love for point and click and, at times, funny dialogue, I would have probably abandoned the game halfway.
Having two characters between which you can switch could be a very entertaining approach, The Unwritten Tales and Technobabylon come to mind, but in this game it becomes a dreary must of clicking on everything twice with very unsatisfying results.
Of course, those games are 5-10 times more expensive, so if you really love point&click, it could be worth buying, if only to support the trend for this type of games.

What I hated most about the game is the way it takes the least endearing features of P&C and make them nerve-grating. And I am refering here to:
- The pixel hunting. the backdrops are very dark which makes it hard, at times impossible, to spot the objects you have to pick up
- Big inventory - you will pick up a lot of junk objects you will never need to use, so you'll have a big, mostly useless inventory
- Artifical, random puzzles - That I consider the worst of all. 90% of the game you will go about not knowing what you are supposed to do and why you are doing it. Many puzzles can only be solved only with the walkthrough, because there is no logical connection you can make. The whole game is mainly a string of not story-related puzzles which are neither smart nor entertaining to solve. One, for example, is all about clicking at random with both characters. Figure that...

If a game is not entertaining, then it loses its purpose and that is why I cannot recommend it.

Posted: 24 July, 2015
The Apotheosis Project is a classic-style point and click adventure game about people who work for some kind of spy organization maybe and sort of have superpowers. The story is overall pretty weak and nonsensical. It's never really clear what the characters' actual job is and the superpower plot is kind of pointless, and the whole thing is drawn together into an incredibly bizarre and unsatisfying ending (full disclosure I got about 840 points so maybe the 1000 points ending is better). The dialogue tries really hard to be clever and funny but usually fails.

The art is serviceable, nothing special but fine for the price range. However the backgrounds can be pretty murky and with the amount of pixel hunting that you're going to have to do that might get irritating quickly. The voice acting is so bad that I'm pretty sure that the game would have been better off un-voiced. Every actor pronounces certain words (particularly "apotheosis" but also a few others) differently from one another, or sometimes the same actor will pronounce a word differently from scene to scene. The VA for Pandora speaks like she was being paid by the hour and is trying to draw the recording session out as long as possible.

In that "modern people making a classic point & click" way, there is a LOT of pixel hunting, and some really nonsensical puzzles (this is a wee spoiler but there is a scene where you convince a character to purchase something from you that they didn't want to purchase previously because it "looks low quality" by peeling a "high quality" sticker off a stripper pole and sticking it to the item). Most of the time there is no real need for the two characters to both be present, and I think the game could have expanded a lot on puzzles that required both characters to complete. As it is, there's this weird sort of artificial division of labor so that both characters are required, so one character is too weak to open a drawer, can't follow simple instructions to operate a machine and refuses to talk to like half of the characters because they're "too scary" and the other character is like "I am too busy to read what's written on this paper".

At this price point the game was okay (like it wasn't actively bad really) but I can't muster up the interest to replay it and try to get more points so I can't really recommend it.

They solve the problems they encounter during their adventure together or apart. It is important to let both Aaron and Penelope look at items and have them both to talk to other characters in the game. After all, they both have their own way of understanding, character and qualities. Moreover, you can be earning points along, up to a total of one thousand. It pays to reach that number and it also makes you more mindful of the environment and the story during the game, which benefits the fun of playing this adventure.

GAMEPLAY:
The puzzles are logically interwoven into the story. If you want you can obtain helpful information and sometimes even hints during the game. Useful items can be obtained in exchange for another object or by a service on return. While solving situations/puzzles you do need to use your ingenuity.
During your quest you will find many items you can pick up and place in your inventory and use in certain situations, but some of these items are not needed and only serve to confuse a.k.a. red herrings. While playing you're always looking for opportunities to use all of these items and that is a nice addition for the true adventurer.
You can, while playing, freely switch between Aaron and Penelope, through a portrait of both in the inventory bar. Sometimes it is even necessary, as Penelope for example encounters a problem that can only be solved by Aaron or vise versa.
Your inventory folds out if you hoover your mouse-pointer to the bottom of your screen, very pleasant and effective. Naturally, each character has its own inventory, but items are interchangeable between Penelope and Aaron. The object gets stored in the inventory of the character that receives the object.
With a mouse click, the two playable characters walk freely and smoothly through the game scene.

GRAPHICS:
The in-game environment looks nice. The backgrounds are very detailed and matches the atmosphere and story. A thrift shop is stuffed really messy with silly things and you see the flashing reflection of the neon advertisings while in a rainy street. An office is sleek equipped and on the platform of the subway you see rubbish and graffiti. When in Infraworld, the colours are sober and while in a modern residential tower, the colours are tight and bright. All together this creates a nice, convincing impression.
Many scenes contain one or two small background animations. The transitions between the game-chapters are shown as a close-up of a three-piece comic and match perfectly with the characters and surroundings.

SOUND:
The game scenes contain perfectly fitting background music and certain situations are supported with nice sound effects.

VOICE ACTING:
The voice acting is top-notch, done by professional voice actors/actresses.
