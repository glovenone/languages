About This Game

Void Raiders is sci-fi top down shooter focused on character progression and shooting both humans & aliens that stands in your way to become most feared raider on Neptune planet. Blast your way through many exotic locations, collect mysterious artifacts, powefull relics and shards of power to upgrade your characters, their equipment and even Lone Invader tavern whitch serves as a base of your marauding operations.

Product received for free
Void Raiders is a stunning quality rogue-like shooter, with tons of replayability

Gameplay:
So you start off the game by selecting one out of three characters and then pressing start to begin your dungeon run (no hold your hand tutorial :D). From here, you will be shooting up monsters, gaining xp, activating your character's special abilities, collecting credits, gems and coins, dodging sniper bullets, buying items that help your run, unlocking dungeons, racing the clock, fighting bosses, and you'll die...alot, but your death is not in vein! Cause as you level up by defeating monsters, you get skill points to spend to your characters tree! You'll also be rewarded with credits. Credits in the game buy you items in that run. Items like chainsaws give +3 damage! A rocket drone follower can shoot some enemies for you! Sythes can increase your critical hit chance, and all of these help you defeat bosses! Bosses and enemies in the game each of thier own unique attack, learn from thier timing and abilities and use it to your advantage! Choices you make along the way affect how far you'll be able to get, decisions can make or break your game so choose wisely.

As you progress through dungeons, you'll be finishing objectives. In a normal room, the objectives lead you to a point on the map where you'll find a power cell or cube, these items can be used to go through special dungeons that have enemies that drop gems , coins and other special currencies! These guys can be used to upgrade your characters gear! Your shield, armor and headgear all boost the stats of your weapons and abilities to let you get even further down the dungeon. On top of that, as you get better and better, and begin to defeat bosses, you'll unlock the artifact vault! This vault will hold up to 3 items for you to bring to each and every run, you unlock each of the three spaces by defeating the first three bosses. And when you thought it was over, there is a lone invader on the character selection page that changes a huge part of gameplay! You can select 1 of 5 options that do things like make health packs and ammo drops stronger, elevator store discounts, replacing supply drop with a teleporter strike and a couple more. TONS of stuff in the game to keep you busy :)

Character Progression:
While the game is a rogue-like, one of the things that shine through is character progression. The first site of this progression comes with leveling, as you destroy monsters you gain exp, and can build up to lvl 10 on each character,(which in my expierence takes around an hour or so to do with each character). For each level up you gain a point in your character's skill tree, these skills greately change how the character plays, some skills can give bouncing bullets, and others can give a drone companion, you can get up to 10 points, while there is 14 different skills, so this means you have to choose wisely when picking skill points and test others out to see what works best for your playing style, of course, you can reset these skills whenever you want and re-distribute your points to get a different feel to the character.
While you go through the floors that require item payment of neptune's dungeon, you will find item drops different from regular credits, like gems and coins. These items can be used to upgrade characters weapons in each of thier respective menus. There is alot of items to go through, and I would say it takes at least 10 hours to get all the characters to thier max, and thats only if you are super grinding.

Choices:
Choices in the game really can take you or break you, buy that mystery item for 25 credits or save it to pick from 3 you can see, in the elevator? Do I want to get the drink that gives me extra ammo or health, or change out my supply drop with an orbital strike? The game gives you decisions all of the time, to unlock the next level or not, character skill tree choices, buying items, picking a drink from the tavern. You have to experiment all the time to see what best fits your playstyle and what could take you past the next boss :) And thats one of the best aspects of the game!

Pros:
+ Randomly generated dungeons
+ Choices affect your run greatly
+ Lots of items
+ Characters that have different playstyles
+ Character skill trees + upgradable gear
+ Individualistic bosses and enemies
+ Great sci-fi atmosphere
+ Nice soundtrack
+ Steam achievements + trading cards

Cons:
- Only 3 different characters to play as
- No real story or background to characters

Overall:
Void Raiders is a stunning quality rogue-like shooter with multiple aspects of character progression, a time based diffiuclty, a wide array of unique items and enemies, and is great to look at. While the game can feel a little grindy at times, you are always trying to beat your last run and learn from previous decisions. The experimenting and learning is what the game is all about. There are tons of item drops in the game with different abilities, and tons of enemies with different attacks. The game sadly only has 3 different characters to play as, but they each have different skill trees and have a unique gameplay shift then the others. For the low price of the game, Void Raiders gives players tons of replayability and a unique expierence of a blend of genres. A gem for rogue-lite lovers and still an amazing game for the rest of us.

Strongly Recommended 8/10.

Early Access Review
Posted: 17 March
NOTE: This game was given to me by the developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.

==========================================================
---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Note: Void Raiders is currently in Early Access. The version reviewed was released 17-MAR-2016 (including hotfix).

Void Raiders is a hectic science-fiction twin-stick shooter with rogue-like elements and great pixel graphics.

==========================================================
----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Graphics - Void Raiders has mostly great pixel graphics with a technological theme. It doesn't quite look like cyberpunk, but there's a definite sci-fi feel to everything. From the "Lone Invader Tavern" that makes up the sparse main menu, through the handful of different level styles, character and enemy graphics are solid quality and well animated, while levels are made up of interesting objects, albeit laid out in only a limited number of ways.

Once the action starts everything gets a bit hectic. All of the guns fire bright pulses of colour, rather than bullets, adding to both the science-fiction feel of the game and the action on screen. At first it feels a bit cluttered and overwhelming, but you get used to it after a while, and begin to appreciate the chaos all around you. The screen shake effect adds to the atmosphere.

Graphics options are all but non-existent. You can change the severity of the screen shake, and there's a "not available yet" setting for particles, but you won't find resolution settings, levels of detail, or anything like that on the settings screen.

Sound - Original grungy techno music with a heavy beat infuses each level, encouraging the frantic action present in every level of the game. Sound effects are mostly good, with a few different gun sounds and suitably booming explosions. There is an odd beep, like the sound of a pinball bumper, that feels a bit out of place when you collect items, but overall sound is good.

Gameplay - Void Raiders is a frantic twin-stick shooter, with WASD for movement and mouse for aiming your character's gun and shooting, but unfortunately it doesn't yet support controllers (although that is a planned enhancement). You choose one of three characters, who all start out feeling pretty similar, and on each level you race around like a nutter blasting at anything that moves while you collect objectives and try to reach the elevator marking the end of the level.

Enemies respawn frequently enough that you'll typically run out of ammo if you just try to stay still, so the game keeps you moving constantly, dodging incoming fire, evading melee enemies, and raining death around you. Enemies sometimes drop health, ammo, or cash. The first two simply top up your ailing health bar or increase your ammo reserves, respectively, while the third adds to your cash stash, enabling you to buy upgrades that increase statistics, or add some other beneficial effect. I think I've seen about a dozen such upgrades, but I don't know how many there are in total.

You can choose from one of three characters when you begin, and when you die (which will happen frequently), you can either continue on with your previous character (minus upgrades) or you can return to the Lone Invader Tavern and start again with someone else. The tavern also lets you spend skill points, earned through levelling up each of the three characters, on various skills and bonuses from each character's skill tree.

There's no tutorial for the game and instructions are minimal, so you may have trouble getting into it. Once you get the hang of it, though, and you've upgraded a character a couple of times, the game really starts to shine.

==========================================================
-------------------------------------PROS & CONS------------------------------------
==========================================================
Pros:
+ Great atmosphere
+ Great pixel graphics
+ Good soundtrack: both music and sound effects
+ It's grindy, but it's fun grindy, due to the lasting character progression
+ Different characters evolve to be significantly different to play after spending a few skill points, increasing replayability
+ Achievements

Cons:
- Only three characters, although there may be more coming
- There's no story or introduction provided, so the characters' goals are completely unknown
- It gets a bit repetitive; would be great with some more added goals or even character development side quests
- Achievements seem to be a bit bugged, with some of the "Complete xxx level" achievements not always unlocking
- Terrible quality writing for the in-game text (e.g. "ADD A DROP OF LEVANDAR TO YOUR BATH", or "ON NEPTUNE NOONE CARES YOU SCREAM", or the level called "MOANSTERY")
- Still a few small interface issues, such as opening up the options menu when you have a character open in the tavern, or the tooltips being partially hidden by the mouse pointer (see screenshot in my profile)

==========================================================
--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------
==========================================================
Void Raiders is a quality rogue-like shooter that, to me, is what Early Access should be all about. The core game is completed, and there's obviously been some fine tuning already performed, and a lot of effort and love put into it. It still needs more content and a bit more polish to really shine, but even as it is now in Early Access, I see no reason to not strongly recommend it. It's a fantastic, fun game.

8/10

Early Access Review
Posted: 18 March
This is a first impression of the game and I will probably write more about it later.

Void Raiders is a top down mouse-aim shooter-RPG with random level layouts and item drops, persistent character progression and lots and lots of bullets.I played a pre-release alpha build and was immediately enamoured of it, and for five bucks, this early access build is an excellent foundation. Though it lacks plot or characterization of any kind at present, it has good enough music and spritework to convey a sense of style: a dark, neon-lit, occasionally rain-soaked metropolis on... Neptune?

It has three playable characters- Boris the Brawler, Tina the Gunslinger and Gudrun the Bladedancer, and of the three thus far I have had the most success with Boris, whose upgrades slowly turn him into a bullet-spewing tank. Tina puts out even more shots with her dual guns and has an entertaining special ability that causes her to spray bullets everywhere around her. Gudrun, with proper use of her blade-dashing and bullet-reflecting, is surprisingly survivable, and has a shotgun for when her sword isn't enough, but if you can't effectively use her sword to intercept incoming shots, she's by far the most fragile of the three.

Stages are split up into floors. On each floor you must obtain a certain number of key items, either these metal boxes with pink lights or these glowing green datacubes, before the elevator to the next floor becomes available. Enemies respawn endlessly, but you get xp for every kill. They drop cash, ammo and the occasional health pickup. Cash can be used to purchase stat-boosting (more damage, higher crit chance, health back on critical hits, etc) items from in-level vendors, one item per vendor, and you also get a choice of three items to buy between floors. However, you don't know what you're getting if you buy the in-level items, and in roguelike fashion, these items are lost when you die.

There's another review on here mentions the somewhat sluggish player movment, noting that you often have no choice but to "take the hits", and I'm inclined to agree, but if anything that's the game's only real shortcoming apart from not-yet-added features. However, I personally feel like the current level cap of 10 for each character is a tad low- admiteddly, leveling only gives you skill points, with the actual stat increases instead coming from upgrading your armor using components earned from defeating the bosses. I can only hope these upgrades have no cap, or at least a high one, as the game still becomes very difficult in later stages. Interestingly, I noticed that the difficulty increases not per stage but rather at certain time intervals in the length of your run- if you waste time, things will only get harder, but you might miss out on extra funding and items.

I've played a lot of roguelikes and am glad to see another one with character experience and skills that carry over between runs. I hope that, like Rogue Legacy, or Galak-Z's arcade mode, victory in this game is an eventuallity, or at least a reasonable possibilty as in Ziggurat.
