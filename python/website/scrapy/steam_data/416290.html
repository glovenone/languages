About This Game

Bleeding Border is a first person 3d survival horror game in which the player must escape through the top of a monster infested building using his own blood as the only defense mechanism.

The game starts with Zoey, losing blood from a fresh amputated arm, after a scavenge incursion went south and all her team members were killed by the apparently indestructible PaleOnes.
Zoey escaped thanks to a rare genetic mutation that makes her blood act like poison in contact with the PaleOnes bodies(it's a shame that to find that she first had to lose her arm :( )

Bleeding Border is inspired in old school games like ResidentEvil and Silent Hill, adding modern elements of gameplay and aesthetics.
In this game the player needs to hide, run and avoid , and only as the last resource use blood, because as soon as the player hurts himself he will start to bleed, and die if his wounds are not treated in time.

Needless to say is an adventure with a lot of gore and blood filled action.

Let's get the bad out of the way, first: The game's in the running for the absolute ugliest I've ever seen. It's blatantly unfinished, with two out of three game modes to be added later. There's a few nasty glitches, though no longer anything game breaking.

I still have to recommend this as one of the best horror games I've played. Beyond just having some legitimately creepy monster designs, it just has an incredibly effective central mechanic that ratchets up the tension. You're in an abandoned building, filled with monsters. The good news is that you have a 100% effective weapon against them. The bad news is that it's your blood -- every time you try to kill an enemy, you inflict a bleeding wound on yourself and need bandages or you'll eventually bleed out and die. The game becomes an exercise in avoiding combat where possible, and where forced to, it frequently becomes a tense race for the next medkit before you become too far gone and either collapse or get easily picked off by one of the monsters. I've rarely played a game that left me with as much dread and fear as this one did.

Bleeding Borders, developed by Curse Box Studios, is a survival horror game with a really cool premise. But… it’s ♥♥♥♥ing broken.

You’ll be playing as Zoey, a run-of-the-mill survivalist living in a post-apocalyptic world ravished by rubbery, fleshy monsters called the Pale Ones. After a scavenging trip goes awry, Zoey’s fellow survivors are slaughtered by Pale Ones. She just barely manages to escape before losing her arm to a monster. Although, in the brutal process, she learns that her blood is somehow capable of poisoning the beasts. (I’d tell you why that is… but I failed to beat this poor excuse for a game.)

Anyway, your goal is to reach the roof of the dilapidated office building and ride the cabal car back to safety.

Now, I’d first like to commend the concept this game has created. Using a rusty screw driver, the player must stab Zoey’s gaping wound and spray incoming monsters with blood to kill them. Really cool idea, but this game’s disgusting design, disgusting optimization, and disgusting character models ruin what might’ve been a decent horror experience.

There really isn’t anything else to this title. You’ll walk up floor by floor, slaying the odd monster, reading notes and picking up keys. Oh, and by the way, these notes are everywhere, and I mean EVERYWHERE. But trust me… it won’t be long before any sane person ignores them all together; trying to read while stomaching this game’s frame rate is as plausible as Donald Trump winning the 2016 election.

Oh yeah, did I mention the frame rate? It’s like something straight out of hell. My rig is nothing to brag about, in fact, it’s kinda ♥♥♥♥, but Jesus… COD: Ghosts had better optimization than this.

This game crashed on me three times, and one of those crashes managed to freeze up my entire computer. I couldn’t get halfway through this crap before succumbing to my migraine and quitting entirely.

Just do yourself a favor: Stay far, far away.

You'd have to say that the premise of Bleeding Border is pretty novel; you lose half your forearm before the game even begins and are forced to ascend a twelve storey office block to escape the monsters that dwell within it and a gradually fatal loss of blood. Your only weapon against the tentacled beasts that hunt you is your own vital fluids which you spray from your sketchily bandaged stump by jamming a screwdriver in the wound hole.
Then again, it's pretty novel for a reason. Why would a species so dramatically allergic to human blood EAT PEOPLE? How are there any of these imbeciles left in the first place?
I'd be willing to lay that to one side if the game itself were any good but, for a title that sees you trying to flee to a rooftop cable car before you wring yourself dry out of your mangled arm, it's a tediously placid experience. There's no urgency in your situation. You'll find more medipacks than you'll ever need. There are notes, NOTES for god's sake, like I'm going to stop to check them out mere minutes after my hand's been chewed off, and the monsters just kind of slowly amble towards you, giving you plenty of time to douse them in artery juice. Once you get over the amusing gruesomeness of this sole attack, it's a wildly annoying six second animation that will unavoidably cause you to take damage before you can kill your opponent.
At around 25 minutes long, the only positive you can take from this mess is that it's a surefire refund candidate.
