This game. Where to start?

Well I guess first off is to say, honestly this game isn't bad, it's just dead now. In a nutshell Nexuiz is a Unreal Tournament clone, and in and of itself it does the job well enough. At the time this was released, I do believe the gaming industry was in an arena shooter desert where they were far and few between, so we needed this game. However, it never picked up any real momentum due to not having enough marketing, and, well, Cryengine. Cryengine looks pretty, but back then it could alienate alot of your userbase just due to needing so much more power than other engines. Nowadays, I don't think that's as much of an issue unless you're running a 10 year old rig, you should at least be able to play on minimal settings.

Then THQ went under. Due to issues with that, the master server list for this game went down. This game will not start if it sees the master server list down. So for a couple of years it was completely dead in the water. I think you could start up stupid mode or the beta exe and still be able to play, but that would only be with bots, and you could only run stupid mode if you had a really high end rig, it was the same game, just in super graphics mode.

The master server list is back up, and if you are extremely lucky you might see a server, but more than likely it will be no humans, and all bots. This game has been dead for so long, and didn't have enough of a following to start with, that everyone who owned it has all but forgotten it, and any new owner will more than likely give up because all there is to do, is play against bots.

If you don't mind playing against just bots, and you are a fan of arena shooters, it really isn't a bad game. It's a UT clone, and in that regard it does it's job pretty well. It's just dead for players and I don't see that aspect changing anytime soon.

I'll be damned, this game is actually back from the grave! Now if only all the players would come back to populate the servers...

Whether you like the game comes down to only 1 thing: how much you like the crazy Nexuiz mutators. These change the game drastically, sometimes affecting all players globally. Of course, there are also game modes without these mutators, but this is what makes the game special.

Some examples:

- All players get jetpacks.
- Controls are inverted for everyone!
- 1 random player loses his weapons and becomes a living bomb. You can kill lots of players with a kamikaze (although most of the time it's rather annoying, because you lose your health and armor stack)
- Color blind mutator literally changes everything to a greyscale. You'll have trouble telling teams apart. Friendly fire is on.
- Ground suddenly feels slippery and everyone is sliding (careful around edges)
- Nuke. Everyone dies, then respawns.
- Invulnerability (only you)
- You get Strength (double damage)
- You get your favorite weapon.
- Shooting teammates now heals them instead!
...

There's many more (about 50?). The global mutators take turns in a stack. The stack can be cleared too. Mutators affecting only you take effect instantly when you pick em up, I believe...

Honestly, it's chaotic as hell and a lot of these mutators are rather unwelcome (kamikaze and nuke). But if you like chaos, you will like this game. If the devs would actually put some effort into balancing the weapons and creating a competitive game mode with less ridiculous mutators, it could be great.

As a remake from a 2005 MOD, NEXUIZ got revamped on CryEngine 3 and re-released in May 2012 with DX11 graphical upgrades.

I played the game a bit and from what I did played, the game didn't really impressed me much. Sure the graphics are obviously great but the "arena shooter" elements aren't that good compared to past games that did it right, namely Quake 3 Arena and Unreal Tournament 2004. Guess Nexuiz is a b@stard child of Quake 3 vs UT2004.

Currently, I can't find myself to like Nexuiz. The weapons are uninspired, the maps are not memorable and very tiny 4vs4 arena. Since the game doesn't have a map editor, I bet MAP PACKS will be the CoD standard.

OVERALL:
Nexuiz is a bland game with cool mutators that save's it from mediocrity. But you'd be better playing other alternatives instead which are far from lacking from F2P FPS games to older arena shooters like Quake 3, Quake Live and Unreal Tournament (any edition would do).

RECOMMENDATION:
The best recommendation I can give you is play QUAKE LIVE, which is FREE and the gameplay is much better than Nexuiz... and with MORE MAPS and Players!

This game is extremely underrated. If you're a fan of old-school Arena FPS such as Quake you will absolutely love this. This game is the Quake of modern times and needs a bigger userbase. If you like the genre, BUY IT!
The movement system of this game is possibily the smoothest and nicest one I've ever seen. It's even better than Quake's movement system. You have an extreme control of your trajectory in mid air, for example you can rotate by 90 degrees in mid air and change your trajectory with a sharp turn. You can accelerate slightly by jumping (similar to bunnyhopping).
The maps are AWESOME and there is custom maps support - users can create their own maps and share them with the community.
The graphics are next-gen but I don't really care, I prefer keeping them at minimum because all the nextgen effects and shading distract me.
For the first time ever, I like a modern game as much or even more than Quake, trust me.
