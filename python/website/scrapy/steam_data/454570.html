About This Game

Follow the real life events of the late Roman Emperor Caligula and his dear sweet sister Drusilla during his short reign. You will explore Rome and deal with the senate while living the life of an Emperor.

Story/Game
"I am Caligula" Will be the most in depth story driven game about Caligula ever made. What we have done is take the very best parts from our Caligula series which has been our most successful and put them into one game. The player will experience strong RPG elements with a beautiful visual novel style art.

Features:
-RPG level progression.

-Historical weapons and armor to equip your party with.

-Voice overs at key story events.

-Quest system.

-F5 to change screen size.

Posted: 9 April
Yes. This story is based on a true story. Yes. The events are historically accurate. Yes. The game has flaws but so does every blockbuster game now a days. Personally, I have never made a game before. I am sure it is a daunting undertaking. To take such an unusually and uncomfortable story and turn it into a game takes a bit of courage.
During the time I spent with I Am Caligula, I found many things to be surprisingly lighthearted. From the smug comments to soldiers or the vendor with 'crazzzzzzy' low prices, I found a bit of humor in this game. Whether that was intentional or I am just weird, I don't know. I also enjoyed the music and the voice acting is well done.
The game is obviously an RPGMaker game. The game is limited by that alone. I feel that there needs to be more in this game. More interactions in the streets and side quests would be cool. I am not too far in the game and I may find those things. I had a platforming issue where I was stuck on a narrow path behind a NPC that would not get out of the way. While I found this annoying, I also found my self laughing and wondering if this was a way to simulate the crowed city streets of Rome. I want to feel more of that Roman atmosphere. I don't know how to do that. I am a musician, not a game developer.
Caligula is a rather taboo creature in history and to make a game about him and his lesser known sister is refreshing. Re-imagining the familiar JRPGs of yesterday by throwing a ruthlessly insane emperor/dictator in the mix is almost post-modern. I Am Caligula is cheaper than the price of a candy bar. So why not say I am Caligula.
