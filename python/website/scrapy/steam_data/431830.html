“

”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The full release of the game is scheduled for the end of November 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“
  • Multiple save slots with tree diagrams to restart from any point of a game.
  • Full voice acting
  • Craft system
  • Research and upgrade system
  • More rooms!
  • Leaderboards
  • Other characters
  • Background and storytelling

Would like to learn more about our planning? It's right there”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is playable but only the core gameplay is implemented right now, therefore the game lack of depth.

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game price will be lower for the early access release. For the full release of the game, the price will be increased.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will continue to be very active on the steam forums - as we were during the greenlight process - in order to take in account the feedbacks of the community, to explain the design choices and to gather advices regarding what's missing.

About This Game

As the sole survivor of the crash of a long-haul spaceship, you find yourself trapped within its falling apart remainings, struggling to maintain the vital functions of the ship and coping with your own survival.

Explore, salvage, study and make a good use of the different modules of the ship, and try to survive as long as possible.

Inspired by our love for science-fiction classics and the survival genre, Drift Into Eternity presents you with a unique and difficult survival experience in first person view. You'll have to learn quick to take advantage of the slightest item if you want to overcome the many dangers threatening the ship and your life.

Key Alpha Features :



  • Enter a cold, stark, silent ship crashed on an unregistered world, slowly being eaten away by the corrosive atmosphere of the planet, while its vital functions are shutting down one by one.
  • Explore and chart the ship looking for food and water to prevent starvation, scavenge materials to repair the ship, and try to keep your morale up to avoid fatal depression.
  • Repair the many damages slowly tearing apart the ship: caulk the breaches, put out electrical fires, disperse the corrosive and lethal atmosphere slowly infiltrating the hull.
  • Study and keep running the essentials system maintaining the vital functions of the ship: air & water recyclers, food & heat generators and many more...
  • Take care of your diseases and wounds rapidly, if you don't want them to hinder your progression.
  • Launch researches and find unique items and upgrades to ease your fight for survival.

This game is meant to be difficult and challenging for the players. You will struggle and fail very often. But hold on tight, explore, observe and study the many modules of the ship... and maybe you'll find a way to survive each time a little longer!

And always remember...The answer may be at the very end of the corridor!

EARLY ACCESS review, 6/1/16

Short Version: A lonely, barren, poorly rendered spaceship crumbles around you as you futilely try to patch up your broken little world just enough to survive the inevitable decay, prolonging your lonely, barren, poorly rendered existence for one more day. First-Person-Survival that could work as an art piece for the meta 'feels'... but not so much as a full fledged game.

Drift into Eternity is a survival game that puts you in the shoes of the only wo/man alive on a wrecked spaceship that is falling apart a little more every day. You must scavenge supplies from all around the ship to fix up the hull breaches, fires, and broken life support systems that crop up in greater numbers every day as you try to survive in hope of a rescue that may never come.

The game's hook is interesting, and it certainly has potential to become a game worthy of playing, but in its current early access state it unfortunately lacks the content a $15+ game is usually expected to deliver.

The graphics aren't great, and even the current visuals caused numerous hitches on my machine. The audio is equally mediocre at this point, consisting of a lone voice telling you about objects when you press the tutorial button, the constant rumbling of your spaceship, and the ubiquitous wailing of sirens as things break down around you.

The gameplay itself just doesn't have enough substance at the moment to retain interest for very long. You open endless containers (which usually don't resemble containers or have any highlights or animations to mark them as such), 'take all' of the endless parts, then hunt down an endless number of broken/smoking/flaming things and right click to fix. Meanwhile you must eat/drink/self-medicate just enough to stay alive....endlessly. This repetition definitely adds to the atmosphere of desperate futility the game seems to be going for, but doesn't provide much in the way of entertainment value (which ironically, is one of the things you have to do to survive in the game - keep your character entertained).

All in all, Drift Into Eternity has an interesting concept that could be neat to try, but probably not at the current point in development for the current price. However, the developers do seem to be very active, patching the game frequently along with providing a clearly labeled timeline of what they hope to achieve in the next six months - and visibly active development is a definite positive for an Early Access title. So, if the concept of the game intrigues you, you could consider supporting the development of the game and hoping for the best... but if you're searching for a survival game to fully enjoy, you may want hold off until release is a bit closer before picking this one up.

Drift Into Eternity:
~~~~~~~~~~~~

What a ride, you might say! You are the sole survivor of a crash-landing on an unknown planet. The good news – well, you are alive! The question is, for how long? Your spaceship has sustained a lot of damage and as days goes by, more breakages occur!

Drift Into Eternity is a First Person survival game set in a fallen spaceship. At the start, you will wake up in your personal cabin and learn the basic functions of the game. Then you will be advised on how many breaks the vessel suffered on a daily basis via the logbook. You might have some specific equipment to fix or even to extinguish fires in several rooms. You will be able to identify where the breaks are located by the flashing red lights and the sound of the alarm in front of the room where the repairs are needed. Green smoke is also a sign of damage, so make sure you go through the area with a fine comb.

A small point I forgot to mention is that you start with absolutely nothing on you, and not only do you have to repair the ship but you have to make sure you find enough food and water to survive another day in this grim environment! Be aware of the rotten food which can cause you illness and make sure that your state of mind is at its optimum by reading comic books, magazines or listening to music. Try to stock as many useful items as possible in your inventory, so when you come across equipment to repair you hopefully have the right component to fix it.

The vessel has two decks that you can explore and you will have to find a teleporter to move from one deck to the other. Remember to read your log book carefully as damage can occur on both decks on a same day. One thing that I miss in this game is a mini-map; sure you have maps of the ship on the walls here and there, but I find it to be not so helpful.
The graphics are average but the game runs very well. I like the Keyboard rendition before starting the game but there is nothing else in regards to the sounds apart from the sound effects, which are footsteps and alarms.

Positives:
~~~~~~~~

- Great music intro
- Well-designed decks
- Plenty of items to find and use
- Achievements

Negatives:
~~~~~~~~

- No mini-map
- Graphics need to be polished (Early access)
- No trading cards

This game is currently in early access and shows good potential. I look forward to see more updates.

7/10

"A great and unpredictable survival adventure in space, with a Star Trek touch."

Introduction
At the beginning the game didn't call my attention, but when I played again the feeling changed. I understood the idea: survive at all costs being alone in a space ship full of cargo and look for those things you need for maintenance spread in the whole ship.

I loved the intro music, the piano, makes me feel great. There's no so much noise in a lonely spaceship except the sound of your stems and the alarms of a fire or a breach. The visual experience is quite good, maybe there are some things need to be polished, of course, I'm playing the game in early access mode. I guess I'll have more to discover soon.

Gameplay
Beware of poison food! The game is simple: survive, and you'll have to eat and drink and use vaccines if you get a cold, a bandage if you are wounded, etc. Also, you have to distract yourself a little, so there are books, yoyos, vodka and other things spread in the rooms. Use them. There is a log that tells you the everyday maintenance ship status, also your hunger and thirst status. You will be able to check the oxygen level and other important things like fires and breaches. At options you'll be able to see all commands, that's helpful.

Again, beware the poison food, some of the food and drinks are poisoned and you will have to look for the cure. You don't want to get poisoned if you have to survive for 15 days on that ship.
Sometimes I felt a kind of "lack of action". I'm not saying I have to run all the time but days can be a little longer without something to do.

I liked the "Star Trek" touch. In the cabins, you'll see the Enterprise in a poster. Also, if you look like the ship looks a little like the old constitution class.

Pros
+ Great environment for a ship
+ Lovely intro music
+ Difficult to pass, like that
+ The Star Trek touch

Cons
- The graphics effects should be polished
- The voice at the beginning should be a recording or nothing, sounds very off topic
- There are not so many things to do
- A lot of items you don't know what are used for

Final Thoughts

Drift Into Eternity:
~~~~~~~~~~~~

What a ride, you might say! You are the sole survivor of a crash-landing on an unknown planet. The good news – well, you are alive! The question is, for how long? Your spaceship has sustained a lot of damage and as days goes by, more breakages occur!

Drift Into Eternity is a First Person survival game set in a fallen spaceship. At the start, you will wake up in your personal cabin and learn the basic functions of the game. Then you will be advised on how many breaks the vessel suffered on a daily basis via the logbook. You might have some specific equipment to fix or even to extinguish fires in several rooms. You will be able to identify where the breaks are located by the flashing red lights and the sound of the alarm in front of the room where the repairs are needed. Green smoke is also a sign of damage, so make sure you go through the area with a fine comb.

A small point I forgot to mention is that you start with absolutely nothing on you, and not only do you have to repair the ship but you have to make sure you find enough food and water to survive another day in this grim environment! Be aware of the rotten food which can cause you illness and make sure that your state of mind is at its optimum by reading comic books, magazines or listening to music. Try to stock as many useful items as possible in your inventory, so when you come across equipment to repair you hopefully have the right component to fix it.

The vessel has two decks that you can explore and you will have to find a teleporter to move from one deck to the other. Remember to read your log book carefully as damage can occur on both decks on a same day. One thing that I miss in this game is a mini-map; sure you have maps of the ship on the walls here and there, but I find it to be not so helpful.
The graphics are average but the game runs very well. I like the Keyboard rendition before starting the game but there is nothing else in regards to the sounds apart from the sound effects, which are footsteps and alarms.

- Well-designed decks
- Plenty of items to find and use
- Achievements

Negatives:

EARLY ACCESS review, 6/1/16

Short Version: A lonely, barren, poorly rendered spaceship crumbles around you as you futilely try to patch up your broken little world just enough to survive the inevitable decay, prolonging your lonely, barren, poorly rendered existence for one more day. First-Person-Survival that could work as an art piece for the meta 'feels'... but not so much as a full fledged game.

Drift into Eternity is a survival game that puts you in the shoes of the only wo/man alive on a wrecked spaceship that is falling apart a little more every day. You must scavenge supplies from all around the ship to fix up the hull breaches, fires, and broken life support systems that crop up in greater numbers every day as you try to survive in hope of a rescue that may never come.

The game's hook is interesting, and it certainly has potential to become a game worthy of playing, but in its current early access state it unfortunately lacks the content a $15+ game is usually expected to deliver.

The graphics aren't great, and even the current visuals caused numerous hitches on my machine. The audio is equally mediocre at this point, consisting of a lone voice telling you about objects when you press the tutorial button, the constant rumbling of your spaceship, and the ubiquitous wailing of sirens as things break down around you.

The gameplay itself just doesn't have enough substance at the moment to retain interest for very long. You open endless containers (which usually don't resemble containers or have any highlights or animations to mark them as such), 'take all' of the endless parts, then hunt down an endless number of broken/smoking/flaming things and right click to fix. Meanwhile you must eat/drink/self-medicate just enough to stay alive....endlessly. This repetition definitely adds to the atmosphere of desperate futility the game seems to be going for, but doesn't provide much in the way of entertainment value (which ironically, is one of the things you have to do to survive in the game - keep your character entertained).

All in all, Drift Into Eternity has an interesting concept that could be neat to try, but probably not at the current point in development for the current price. However, the developers do seem to be very active, patching the game frequently along with providing a clearly labeled timeline of what they hope to achieve in the next six months - and visibly active development is a definite positive for an Early Access title. So, if the concept of the game intrigues you, you could consider supporting the development of the game and hoping for the best... but if you're searching for a survival game to fully enjoy, you may want hold off until release is a bit closer before picking this one up.
