About This Game

The Asskickers is a modern tribute to 2D arcade beat’em ups.
Join Alex, Diane and Marcus in their action-packed journey through the heart of the modern urban jungles.
Wrongly accused, our three heroes will stand up to a corrupt political and financial elite and make white-collar criminals discover the lost art of asskicking.

-A unique and striking art style hand-crafted by French comic book artists and animators

-An action-packed adventure through 18 levels and three game modes (story, time attack, survival)

-Kick some butt with a friend in local co-op

-Chain up combos, special attacks and exclusive two-player collaborative attacks in true beat’em up fashion

-An epic soundtrack by French composer Gery Montet (Rayman, In Memoriam, Raving Rabbids : TV Party)

-Choose your asskicking weapon of choice with three different playable characters

Posted: 18 June, 2015
The Asskickers is a beat em up. I love beat em ups. Um, this one has a few problems.

1.) You often clip through the environment, or your character and the enemy characters teleport

2.) Dreadfully short, lacks the replay value of other games like it

3.) One of the most important things in a beat em up is being able to quickly turn and deliver a blow to an enemy behind you. Instead of turning instantly, the character has a drawn out animation to turn, and if you press punch before he's turned fully, he performs a back attack. This might not be so bad, if the back attacks actually hit the enemies.

One and two may not sound so bad, depending on how starved you are for another beat em up to play. Number 3 is the big killer, but if you can work past that, there are some positives. Namely, the design. Like a lot of older side-scrolling beat em ups, Asskickers likes to introduce new enemies and gimmicks each stage. In one stage you're on top of a train dodging signs and bars, on another you're hiding from searchlights, and so on. The bosses are also very gimmick focused, probably because the actual combat is not robust enough to support much more.

The enemy design is decent. The most basic goons walk into your punches, but later enemies require precise timing and frequent use of your special attacks. There's really nothing particularly special here, and beat em ups made over a decade prior have done much better.

The humor, which was probably a big draw, is just generic "hurr durr corporations are evil", to the point where one of the enemies is a teleporting yuppie. The game has a few positives going for it, but no, please don't.

I can't, in good conscience, recommend this game. I know that the time on record reflects a very short period of time with the game play, but given the mechanics and what I did endure, I honestly couldn't tolerate any more. I was hoping it would be a fun side-scrolling beat 'em up game similar to Streets of Rage which, in all fairness, "looks" to be that way. The premise is the same, the style and design all seem to mirror similar titles, but the game mechanics are just off. The combat isn't fluid, combos are somewhat frustrating to pull off, and if you're not doing combos or jump attacks, your character's attack combos are non-existent. Basic attacks are limited to a single punch, there's no basic strike fluidity.

Enemies are significantly faster than you too, so it's always a matter of chasing them around on screen while they stay just out of range and then resolving to just wait for their attack animation before you can hope to get a strike in.

All-in-all, it seems like a very hastily put-together flash game. I'm quite disappointed with the purchase.

It's not great. You have three buttons to work with: attack, jump and use. Use is for picking up items and weapons. I played it with an Xbox360 pad and by default it maps the movement to the analog stick, which is not great when your supermove is tapping forward twice and then pressing attack.

The game seems to require you to use the mouse for all menus, including dialogue options. My hands should not have to leave the gamepad to navigate the game. Also in the menus the buttons are big but only the text inside the button counts as the active area for that button i.e. you have to click the text on the button i.e. clicking anywhere else does nothing. This is weird.

The combat is pretty basic. You have your one button combo, a jumping attack, a special attack that consumes non-regenerating stamina and a grab. Grabbing is usually bad, since it just makes you a sitting duck. Jumping attacks tend to be the answer to most problems. You can pick up weapons, but since you cannot jump while holding a weapon, they are pretty useless. I wish more thought had gone into the fighting system.

I don't like the art style, but YMMV.

I bought this game hoping for an old-school beat-em-up in the vein of Double Dragon, Battletoad, Streets of Rage and all the rest of their ilk. What I got instead was a SHODDY beat-em-up in the vein of... well, incompetently-made, poorly-thought-out games of any era you might care to name.

The controls are stiff and wooden, the fighting is booring - basically no combo variation - and the main 'special move' (forward, forward, attack) is simultaneously hard to pull off in the heat of battle, and easy to fire off accidentally... at least if you're using a controller, which would seem to be the obvious choice. The actual fighting boils down to 'stunlock them before they stunlock you', aided by enemies walking in random circles around you most of the time.

Said enemies are generally annoying and often use hit-and-run tactics which you are poorly-equipped to counter, since you can't move faster than a laid-back saunter - there's no run, dash or dodge-roll option. They also enjoy attacking from beyond the edges of the screen, where you can't see or reach them, or just positioning themselves INSIDE you to attack, taking advantage of the poorly-made hitboxes. Several of them also have far greater reach than their animations would suggest.

The bosses are, perhaps, the worst of the lot - they're invariably invulnerable, except for during a specific interval, for abso-bloody-lutely no apparent reason. Your punches just pass through them without doing anything, not because they're hiding behind a shield or using some kind of force-field, but because the game arbitrarily decided that they aren't vulnerable at that point.

Sure, it's funny - sort of, if you're into anarchic, rebellious humor - but the translation is as shoddy as the rest and the text is rife with poor spelling, messed-up grammar, and just plain old errors.

But hey - the GRAPHICS are very nice. Love the style. Looks great. Maybe they should've made a cartoon or a comic-book instead...
