Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to include the community in the development of the game. We're a small team, and having tons of players giving us feedback about changes helps us create something wonderful. We're not the only ones who benefit - you get to not only help shape and influence the game, you're ensured frequent updates and developer contact all throughout the game's development.

Check out the Development Starmap for a very in-depth look at our development plans!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“This is our best guess. We're probably looking at about 1.5 years of frequent updates and development to push the game through Alpha, Beta, and then to release. After release, we plan to support the game and continue updating it with new content.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We have loads of changes and improvements on the way! You can find a full, detailed list on our Development Starmap.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is currently in ALPHA stage. This means the game is still adding features and nothing is really 'locked in' yet. When we switch to BETA, this will mean we are 'feature complete' for release, but the game still needs polish and work. You can check out the currently available (main) features below on the "About the Game" section of the store page, or view the Development Starmap for a super in-depth look.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game will change price as it gets closer to release. We will likely increase the price during Beta, and once again on Release. After that, the game will follow the general pattern of steadily decreasing in price and frequently going on sale in Steam's many annual sales. The earlier you grab the game, the cheaper it will be - and you get to keep it forever, including all future updates!”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are very active on our Twitter (@UnfortSpacemen) as well as our community forums. If you want us to see something you've posted, these are you best places to suggest features, get help, or simply talk about the game.”

Dev Vlog 1 and 0.1.5 "Acid Reflux" are LIVE!

Hello Astronauts!
New patch is LIVE! You'll know you're on the new patch by the watermark at the top middle of your screen.

New Dev Vlog!
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XT0R8NKRuOI

v0.1.5 "Acid Reflux"

Summary: A new dev vlog! Tons of new monster perks, a couple new items, bug fixes, and more!

Change Notes:

General:
- Much of this patch's development time went into fixing join/late-join issues. This should help to (or entirely) sort out the largest issues that reuslted in players getting stuck or not properly getting spawned into the game when joining late. Please continue to report any issues encountered in our bug report forums. Thanks!

Spaceman Gameplay:
- NEW SPACEMAN PERK: Welder. Spawn with a Welder tool that can fully repair broken doors and cameras.- The Antibodies perk now reduces toxic and acidic damage by 30%.- Spacemen now react with physical hit reactions when impacted by bullets.- Spacemen heads can now be decapitated if the last damaging shot is a headshot

Monster Gameplay:
- NEW MONSTER PERK: Nocturnal. While the power is out, your Monster form gains 35% increased movement speed (does not stack with Posterior Claws) and 10% additional Claw and Burrow Trap damage. When the lights are shut off by a Monster with this perk, you will hear a distinct sound, and your HUD will alert you that the monster thrives under these dark conditions.
- When Nocturnal mode is active, the Monster's night vision is better, and a purple color, instead of orange.
- NEW MONSTER PERK: Toxic Ink Cloud. Upon transforming to monster form, the monster squirts a cloud of toxic ink that lingers for 10 seconds and deals damage to any Spaceman that enters it.
- NEW MONSTER PERK: Acid Monster. Your monster form bleeds acid on nearby Spacemen when taking damage, and instead of laying a regular egg, you lay an Acid Bomb that detonates when a Spaceman gets too close to it.
- NEW MONSTER PERK: Decoy: When shapeshifting, a decoy monster will be placed in front of you and lasts for a short duration.
- The Spaceman Antibodies Perk will reduce Toxic/Acid damage by 30%
- Monster Egg now displays a hitmarker when hit
- NEW OBJECT: Radio Jammer. Monster can trigger this device one time per round. Lasts 60 seconds. No text or VOIP communication can be used amongst Spacemen during this time. Monster Remote Arm works with Radio Jammer.

Items:
- NEW GEAR ITEM: GPS Locator. Spawns in a pair. One spawn per map. When active, the GPS Locator displays a waypoint on the HUD for anyone else with a GPS Locator equipped.
- Flamethrower now fires at a slightly slower rate of fire
- Updated Flare Gun item icon to look like a flare gun
- Flare Projectile has vastly improved visual FX, including a bright flickering light

Maps:
- Kema: Added a second colorblind virus sample to the Greenhouse
- Added 1 Radar Jammer location to all maps

HUD:
- Made some of the HUD button prompts about half as large.
- New image for Suit Lock
- Updated most of the skill icons with new artwork (all placeholder art still, just better)

Servers:
- Servers can now be named, instead of just using the Steam display name- Servers can now be passworded- Server browser will no longer show servers that are not the same game version. For example, 0.1.5 server will not show up to someone still on 0.1.4, and vice versa.

Misc Small Stuff:
- Fixed a bug where the map selection in the lobby could choose "blank" or none as the map under certain conditions.
- Fixed a bug where carried items would repeatedly increase in size if you switched to Monster Form while holding them
- Made customization buttons on main menu a tiny bit smaller
- Build watermark is now a bit larger
- Updated Unreal Engine 4 trademark in the credits to match format Epic prefers

Enjoy!

About This Game

Unfortunate Spacemen is a multiplayer game about space-based murder in space! Spacemen work diligently to be rescued from their failing outpost, but not everyone is who they claim to be...

Form alliances with other Spacemen and complete objectives, or, if you're not so human -- blend in, disguise yourself, and sabotage plans, betraying your friends and allies in the most gruesome ways imaginable. Death is part of life in space, and you're no exception to the rule. Paranoia runs rampant.

Are you the victim, or the cause?

Check out our Development Starmap to see our full plans!

Current Features:

  • Survive, hunt, or escape on desolate settlements, arctic outposts, derelict space stations, warp-speed hypertrains, or ancient digsites on alien worlds. 5 maps are available right away, with at least 4 more currently in development, and even more planned after that!
  • Supports up to 32 players in a single game!
  • Randomized objectives every time you play. No two rounds play the same!
  • Mimic and devour your 'fellow' Spacemen as the Monster from the blackest reaches of the galaxy!
  • Protect or betray your allies with at least a dozen unique tools, weapons, and items!
  • Rank up through The Company to unlock new customization and perks to customize your playstyle!
  • Bots with diverse personalities are available to help you learn the ropes and practice, or just add more chaos to a multiplayer game!
  • Customize your Spaceman (or Spacewoman) to stand out, or blend in!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 64-bit or later
    • Processor: Quad-core Intel or AMD processor, 2.5 GHz or faster
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce 470 GTX or AMD Radeon 6870 HD series card or higher
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Storage: 15 GB available space

Just over 5 hours in, and here are my two cents so far.

PROS:
Fun - ridiculously so.
The layout of the maps remains unchanged from game to game, so you don't feel completely lost around the station of your choosing. The locations of the supply boxes and samples do change, which helps keep this re-playable without being confusing.
They do have "achievements" of sorts, which help you get different types of helmet shields, abilities, etc. It also seems like these sorts of options/choices may be expanded in the future.
The developers do interact with people playing or interested in playing their games, and from my experience so far, are very friendly and helpful.

CONS:
The game is so new that not many people are playing this yet, which is a bummer because it is hard to find other people to play against. If you play against bots, you will be the monster every time - bots can't be the monster (confirmed by Zag himself - thanks, Zag!) and you're it. However, when there are other humans on the server, the random choice of who plays the monster does add quite a bit.
The tutorial is a lot of reading, and is not interactive at all. Moving was pretty straightforward, especially if you've played Minecraft with the default movement keys. However, if you want to learn how to use any of your abilities, you have to pay attention to the tutorial and the HUD, and use some trial and error to get the hang of it.

Once I have more playtime under my belt, I may add more to this. Also, as development progresses, some of the above may change. For now, these are my first impressions.

Early Access Review
Posted: 9 May
EDIT: Now that I can actually play the game since the crashing issue is fixed (thanks devs) I will post a full review.

It's hard for me to recommend this or not. There's such a low amount of players on simultaneously, yet the gameplay seems like it would be very fun if there were players frequently playing together. I haven't had a chance to test out the game with a full minimum lobby of players (6) without bots. If you're going to argue about my hours I am often offline on Steam and my hours do not record.

So here's my review about the game. Pretty much the spacemen must complete the objectives first before the monster kills the spacemen. It follows the general idea of many other objective-based horror games. But even though the developers do borrow this general idea from other games, they have put their own unique twist into the game, locating the gameplay in space and adding a variety of "environmental" factors into completing the objectives. For example, oxygen is needed for the spacemen, so their time to complete objectives outside is limited, allowing the monster to take advantage of their need for oxygen.

I do admit the visuals are quite good-looking for the alpha state of the game. The HUD looks great and the dark and lonely feeling of space are very present. I've known this developer team since their last unfinished game, The Dead Linger, which ended up being a trainwreck as far as visuals and gameplay goes (no offense, devs :P) and am quite impressed with the visual quality difference between this game and their last.

Combat mechanics are relatively simple. I'm not impressed with them but am not disappointed either. Grenades, guns, monster furiously swiping at the players, not really anything unique or new. The lasers from the guns look nice, although I do believe the rounds hit the target before the laser reaches it visually.

It all really comes down to how this game ends up in the future, whether or not the developers end up abandoning it like they did their last game, and if they continue with their development roadmap and implement the good-looking content listed in it. I'll be waiting for future updates for sure. Oh, and the lack of players makes things irritating, since the AI don't even shoot back if you're the monster and you're shooting them to death.

TL;DR Lack of players on at once make the game not worth the price, even though the core gameplay and visuals seem decent at the very least.
