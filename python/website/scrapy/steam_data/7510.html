About This Game

In X-Blades, the player takes on the role of the enchanting heroine Ayumi, who survives a breathtaking dance of blades through the Hordes of Darkness. The long-haired beautiful anime, with her pistol blades and mind-blowing leaps, whirls and swirls from one fantastic level to the next.

In brief: An anime-style, opulent hack n' slay spectacle for fans of entertaining action games. X-Blades creates a fantastic universe around the attractive heroine, who has to fend off innumerable monsters and imposing boss opponents with her power and tactics. A long list of all sorts of magic spells widens the range of potential forms of attack. Once Ayumi has collected enough experience points, she can activate new skills. The anime-style level design is what really gives X-Blades its special flair. Around 40 magical indoor and outdoor worlds dazzle in an enthralling rhapsody of color. Action and magical effects are impressively incorporated through the use of cinema stylistics such as bullet time or motion blurring.

Offense is the best form of defense!

Forget the competition - I'll give you all the action you'll ever need! OK... I am a treasure hunter, so my motives maybe aren't always angelic - but my "Slash Dance" through the hordes of the Dark is a thing of beauty! Innovative Gunblades and an arsenal of spectacular spells, power-ups and incredible leap combos - you have to catch the cinematic presentation of my adventures - awesome!

FEATURES:

  • An absorbing fantasy story with 40 spectacular landscapes
  • More than 25 types of magic in different classes
  • Learn to play easily with 4 difficulty modes AND alternative endings that depend on your actions!
  • Innovative Gunblades for long-range and close combat, plus new weapons and new outfits
  • RPG elements in the upgrading of skills and weapons, demonic special mode for the heroine (that's me!)
  • Hidden Power-ups and a Stats screen will hook you into exploring all the levels
  • Over 30 classes of opponents, plus powerful Boss opponents and an ingenious level design

I played the demo for Blades of Time, and I thought it was pretty good, so I bought it in a bundle and bought this with a 90% coupon I got for free. To be honest, my expectations were low--X-Blades has a pretty poor reputation from what I've read, so it couldn't really go anywhere but up. It took me nine hours to beat the game though I could've cut it down if I wasn't stuck in one portion.

X-Blades is the story of a young woman in a skimpy outfit destroying statues and vases while slaughtering scores of monsters and critters. I guess I'd classify it as a beat-em-up of sorts, the kind where the game will seal off an exit and you have to deplete a health bar on the bottom of the screen with enough monster death to continue. Or maybe you have to find these monster generators and destroy all of them AND kill all of the monsters in the area to continue. Combat is fairly simple, as you attack with your gunblades with slashes or shooting at distant enemies. There's a Rage meter that rises with damage dealt and taken, which falls with time or when casting spells, as it's kinda like an MP bar in a way. You gain Souls from enemies you butcher and the things you destroy, which you spend on learning new spells or getting in a quick anytime heal or Rage boost.

Battles are largely repetitive barring the boss battles. You usually have enemies spawn in over a series of waves and you destroy them, or do the same with the monster generators, and then you get a Level Complete and you're free to explore the stages for artifact pieces to power up your attacks or other goodies. All while staring at your character's ♥♥♥...I'm sorry, where was I? There really aren't any puzzles to solve again barring some of the boss fights, and you typically get a bestiary update that tells you pretty much how to beat the foe. This is the first game I've played where the bestiary updates BEFORE you kill something, but it's a handy hint system kinda thing.

For things the game did right...there's a pretty big variety of spells to spend your Souls on. Granted there aren't many effects, but they are at least somewhat different. Earthquake can't hit foes in the air, Ray of Light works best on darkness-aligned creatures, and Lightning Range is a kickass chain lightning spell. There are only four quickslots for spells but you can call up the menu and cast a spell there if you have enough Rage, to make use of a few combos or to use something you don't have bound. The music is a bit repetitive, being pretty heavy on the rock side during battles, but it kinda grew on me to be honest. Even the title theme sounded bad...at first. It's tolerable now, though I don't have to listen to it very much. And the manual is written entirely in-character, which I realize is corny as hell but I kinda liked it. There's also a results screen at the end of each stage so you can at least see if you missed any artifact pieces or are going for 100% completeness or something. And you can go right back to the previous stage if you want, but no stage-select feature here, unfortunately.

As for what I didn't like, some of the controls kinda bothered me. There was a section I was stuck on that required crossing a chasm or fall to my death, and more than a few times did I accidentally dodge-roll right off a ledge when I meant to jump. Dying in this game means you start the stage all over. Did you get a Level Complete and die? Too bad, start all over! And this happened to me a few times. Thankfully that section was optional and this game has no platforming sections like that anywhere else...and you get to keep all of the Souls you had already, so that's some consolation. Another thing was that the battles were rarely fun and felt like grinding more than anything. Even when you knew how to kill something, some things just took forever to die, and I hate attrition wars like that. I did kinda like the game, but at no point did I really tell myself, "boy, this game is fun!" I honestly bought it just to see how bad it was, and it was cheap, so no major loss to me.

So I'm not recommending this. I try to be patient with games, even ones that are said to be outright bad. This one...it's okay, I guess, but I wouldn't recommend this to anybody, or really most people. It's a rough game, it's trying quite a bit, and while I haven't played the games this gets compared to, it's largely mediocre and probably "a poor man's" version of those games at best. The game likely will be remembered more for the outfit the protagonist wears than the content of the game, but all in all, it's not a bad game. Just not one I'd see myself coming back to anytime soon.
