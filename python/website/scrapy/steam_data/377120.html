About This Game

Have you ever had this weird feeling, that the parallel world really exist? What if the door to that forbidden place hides in your bookshelf? Your mother used to be a covert chronicle keeper - a guardian that supports the stable connection between both worlds. After the sudden attack of evil force, you are the only one, who can save her. Do you have what it takes to keep up with family tradition? One book can change a life forever... Be brave enough to read the Chronicle Keepers: Dreaming Garden!

  • 2 parallel worlds - the gem makes you see, what's unseen
  • huge amount of well-thought-out side quests
  • thrilling storyline
  • breathtaking graphics and fabulous soundtrack
  • 17 Hidden Objects scenes and over 45 different, magical locations

Chronicle Keepers: The Dreaming Garden is another hidden object game set in a fantasy land. You play a woman on a search for her mother which turns into a more complicated journey finding magical artifacts and fighting evil forces. While not the best HOG out on the market, it is pretty and fun to play. The price tag isn’t too bad and even better when on sale and will give you a few hours of entertainment for certain.

In the real world lives a woman who was separated from her mother years ago. Following clues you’ve finally found a way to figure out what happened and end up traveling to a magical world. There you find out that your mother has been turned to stone and it’s up to you to help her and also save the land from an evil witch. If this all sounds generic it’s because it is. The story really doesn’t offer too much, if you’ve played HOGs before then you know what will happen. It’s not a bad story, on the contrary, it’s one that has worked in many games before but it’s also predictable so be warned.

What I liked most about the title was the gameplay. I really enjoy HOGs so it’s really hard to mess one up. Using your mouse you click to interact with people and objects, during a hidden object scene you are presented with a written list of items to find in what seems to be clutter. The unique feature is that some of the objects are hidden and can only be found by using your magical medallion. This medallion is also used throughout the game to find useful items and pieces to your magic book which isn’t really a magic book, but tells the background of the story. My only complaint would be that I wish there were more hidden object scenes. The game also has puzzles which are all not too difficult.

The graphics are rather dated even though it was only released two years ago. That being said they aren’t bad, the backgrounds are lively and colorful, the hidden object scenes are well done and the items aren’t too hard to find if you have bad eyes, I played this game often without my glasses. The character models, however, aren’t the best and the animation is so-so. The audio in the game is fine, nice background music and everything is fitting. The game also has voice acting, however the actors aren’t very good but there is an option to mute it.

I would say Chronicle Keepers: The Dreaming Garden is a decent game. It’s not winning any awards but it functions well as a HOG. There are some flaws, like some typos and your map not showing you activity spots to name a couple, but I got through in about three hours and it was fun. It does not have Steam achievements or trading cards which allowed me to play casually not worrying about meeting criteria for achievements. The game isn’t too expensive so I recommend giving it a try, especially if it’s on sale.

Usagi’s Rating

  • Must buy.
  • A good game to buy.
  • Only buy if on sale.
  • Mediocre.
  • Avoid at all costs.

I just recently discovered hidden object games and bought Chronicle Keepers: The Dreaming Garden on a steep sale. I've been playing these types of games on a HTPC connected to my LCD TV, which barely meets the recommended requirements for this game. Other HOGs have performed fine. Performance for Chronicle Keepers however was sluggish; transitions from one location to the next took a noticeable pause, as did zoomed in windows for hotspots. Kaspersky also quarantined the game executable complaining of trojan or something. I reinstalled the game on my main gaming computer which has far better specs. Performance was somewhat improved but it wasn't as smooth as other HOGs on the HTPC. Bitdefender didn't complain about the game executable so I think Kaspersky's identification is a false positive.

The game itself is fairly short (took me ~4 hrs total). The puzzles are fairly easy and the hidden object scenes for the most part were easy too. However there was at least one misnamed object (ladybird instead of ladybug) and a few spelling errors in the V.O. captions. Objects you had to locate outside of hidden objects scenes were sometimes very difficult to distinguish from the background. Hidden object scenes also suffered from occasionally having several objects of the same type with only one of them counting.

The story could not have been more generic. Descriptions of objective items were liberally sprinkled with words like "eternal" just to make the story sound more mystical. None of the characters, or even the setting, was given any depth. There's not much story depth to these games in general but Chronicle Keepers: The Dreaming Garden has the most cardboard characters and setting I've come across yet.

The art work was fine and the locations were usually interesting to look at. But the reasoning for you to do anything (acquire the eternal tree elixir, get the boatsman's lamp, etc.) came across as more contrived than is usual for this genre.

Not recommended. I paid $1.24. I got my money's worth but there are better games on Steam, and far better HOGs you could spend your dollar on.

While this game isn't the best of the best, it's definitely worth a play, especially with its reasonable price tag. The addition of the talisman-view to find even-more-hidden hidden objects is a nice touch that adds some uniqueness to the game.

Graphics seem a little outdated but they're colorful and nicely put together. More importantly, the hidden object scenes are very nicely done without any pixel-hunting or egregious naming errors. The scenes aren't immensely challenging but they aren't overly easy either. Minigames are also nicely put together and some are refreshingly unique from the standard fare in this genre.

Music is pretty but voice acting is just plain terrible in most cases. This is more common than otherwise in this genre of game, just be aware that if you're like me and really value good voicework, you won't be impressed here. However, if you are also like me and play a lot of these games, you're probably already resigned to the fact that voicing is rarely tolerable and it won't bother you any.

Overall, it's a recommendation due to smooth gameplay and some unique aspects although the story is a little formulaic. Just don't go into it expecting a masterpiece, rather take it for what it is: a fun, simple way to kill a couple hours for a decent price.
