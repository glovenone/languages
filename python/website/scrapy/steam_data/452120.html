About This Game

Monkey Island meets Over the Garden Wall, in a dark forest somewhere in Sweden.

In this cozy little point-and-click fairy-tale, you are a gnome on an abandoned farm. When you realize that humans have come to live there once more, you find yourself useful again - but also that you might have to work harder than ever to keep them safe from the dark creatures that lurk out in the forest. You might even have to call on some of the fair folks for help. Their aid, however, does not come without a price...

The game draws heavily from Swedish folk lore, with art inspired by artists such as John Bauer and Alvaro Tapia. It uses puzzles and riddles to tell its story, and there are several different ends to it. The player will also get to experience unique music and compelling voice over, as well as discover part of Swedenâs history, since the game takes place in the 19th century - a time where over a million people left the country to avoid starvation and build a new life for themselves.

This game was created by one person over 6 months, plus a composer who made the wonderful music. It fits anyone who would like to experience an atmospheric and cozy fairy-tale, that might remind you of the stories you heard as a child.

In brief: Midvinter is a short point & click adventure based on Scandinavian folk lore. It has a fun and original storyline as far as games go and about an hour of content.

Midvinter is a curious one. Folk lore of Scandinavian people with gnomes, trolls and other fay folk is a rarity not often explored in games. As a Finnish gamer (and our folk lore shares a lot with the Swedish one) I found the theme refreshing.

Taking the role of a house gnome, you are (as per the folk lore goes) a benevolent house spirit that helps out the people living there. New people have moved in, so once again you have a purpose. Unfortunately a troll has also arrived to cause trouble.

Praise...

Midvinter plays out like a traditional point & click game, with the difference that you have a time limit (time moves on when you switch scenes). Your actions during this time affect the events and the ending of the story. While a short game, there are four endings (three of which grant an achievement) depending on how you play it out.

Story and its motivation is simple but good. There are relatively lot of things to do on the farm considering the length of the game, and I enjoyed finding the different endings a lot. The game and its puzzles are also rather easy for anyone who's played point & click games before. It would work well for children.

Graphical style is good and does great service to the mythology the game is based on. Soundtrack also fits the theme quite nicely, nothing to complain there.

...and criticism

The English voice acting is the only place that could see some improving. I thought the human dialogue was quite okay but everyone else talked rather awkwardly: the conversations simply didn't sound natural. One could perhaps blame the supernatural origin of the speakers but I found the voice acting on those parts mediocre nevertheless.

This might only concern English: there's Swedish voice acting as well, but I didn't try that. Perhaps it's better, being the native language of the speakers. It's nice that options include setting subtitles and voice acting separately if you'd prefer.

Gameplay has a quirk that you can never simply move. Gnome moves only when interacting with stuff. And boy, does he move slowly! Fortunately you can skip both the movement and dialogues (after enabling this in the settings).

Technically Midvinter is pretty good, and I encountered only a couple minor bugs along the way, seeing a duplicate shovel and experiencing one animation freeze that was amended by a reload. By the way, the game saves automatically in each scene. However, the lack of manual saves matters little since the game is so short.

Aftermath

Nevertheless, the problems are rather small. Overall Midvinter is a very nice game, albeit also really short one. It could be played thoroughly in a mere hour, even though I spent a leisure 2 hours on it.

Its main issue is ultimately its short length: I don't think it's totally worth 5 â¬ for a one hour experience. Half the price and I think you have a nice purchase.
