About This Game

When Silvia is hunted by the person behind the attempted murder she was assigned to commit, she knows the only option remaining is to face the deadly conspirator openly. In the heart of the local underworld, Silvia will discover a game of power in which everyone is an expendable pawn. A forced alliance with her ex-target seems to be the only way to avoid the inevitable, but if they want to dispel the secretive mists of the Vatican, James and Silvia will have to come to terms with their limitations...

Pepper

Posted: 27 October, 2015
The second in the series and having enjoyed the first probably a year ago I'm glad to see act 2 arrive on steam and continue.
After a brief synopsis of act 1 the game picks up right where the first left off. Like the first its all fully voice acted and overall done well. Only on a couple occasion did I think it was slightly over the top but even then it somehow seemed to fit with the comic style and added to the charm.

Some areas and characters carry over from act 1 along with some new. The environments are nice clean quality HD rendered scenes though somewhat low polygon character models and sometimes little slow animations are not as nice but by no means an issue. Although the faces can sometimes appear creepy :).
Being a point & Click the controls are simple and effective. Left-click interact & right-click look/examine is basically all you need to get by and you can press space to highlight spots or objects to interact with. Works great and including the ability to double click to skip to other screens/areas saves the games from ever feeling sluggish.
My run-through came out around the 3 hours mark , roughly similar to my playtime with act 1, and I didn't really get stuck at any points.

I find it hard to give any kind of recommendation either way being only 1 part of a series. My opinions and experience is still only based only on a part of the whole (half if include act 1) but I've enjoyed this and the first act so far and I am eager to play the next instalments so they must be doing something right.
This was another good 'part' of what I hope will be a good complete whole and so far I find the story and characters intriguing and gripping. Bring on the next act.
