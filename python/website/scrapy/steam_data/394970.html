19 May

Map of the Week (May 19, 2016)

Hey guys! As hype of launch slows down, so does the Map of the Week's - but I'm still checkin' out your guys' and gals' custom maps!

This week's creator is no stranger to the Map of the Week - flatgub does it again with another reimagining of DEADBOLT's original campaign map. This week's Map of the Week is Critical Altitude.



Similar to flatgub's last map, this one takes the existing DEADBOLT Map (this time, "New High" and turns the difficulty up to a billion. The objective is the same - destroy the ash caches - but new vampires and zombies will make that alot more difficult than the original map. Thankfully, some new vents have been added as well!



Recommendation: you'll generally upset the ENTIRE map once you get a portion into the stage, so keep track of your ammo count, shoot out all the lights, and remember to save bullets by meleeing. Silent options are good to get as far into the stage as you can!

Also, with what seems to be a custom map tradition: there's backup.

To get the map, just hit the Subscribe button on the map page and restart Steam - it will automatically be downloaded and playable in the "Load Custom Maps" portion of the main menu.

Play it now!

Map of the Week (April 16, 2016)

This week's Map of the Week is sorter's "The Shadow of Death."

This map is a bit special to me because it is one of the few that use the trap system, which I spent like an entire week coding and building for essentially only one stage of use in the final game. It's nice to see it used, and it's a special love/hate relationship when I get tricked by my own game. Congrats!



It's also fun to play a trap stage that I haven't created myself - since I made Temur the Tinkerer, I knew all the tricks. It's fun for me personally to have that 1 second of "oh fuck" before the trap explodes in my face.

I also have to admit that, after the slew of backup-style stages that I've played, it was kinda nice that after a very difficult stage I didn't have to play a very difficult backup round. I guess I'm a casual.



Warning: don't step on the mine when you exit the level. Also, it's generally polite to let the undead go through the doors first. Learn from my experience.

To get the map, just hit the Subscribe button on the map page and restart Steam - it will automatically be downloaded and playable in the "Load Custom Maps" portion of the main menu.

Play it now!

About This Game

I bought this game because I kept seeing reviews and internet randoms talking about how Hotline Miami this game is. If you loved the fast-paced madness of running room from room, swinging and shooting like a madman, this game is 'not' for you unless you're an expert speedrunner. As someone who's completed Hotline Miami 1 100% on 3 systems and still plays it pretty often for fun, the gameplay is not similar. The only thing that is similar is the 'lone hitman taking on apartments of thugs' concept.


Deadbolt is far more like a puzzle than a crazy, think-on-your-feet style game like HM. Ammo is extremely scarce, and you need to score very accurate headshots to instant-kill. Melee weapons take many hits to kill enemies, and if you're in a room with even 2 enemies or a large dude (Bouncer), then you're just kinda screwed. Same thing if you used up all your ammo being a bad shot, or being careless. Screwed. Wanna rush them down with melee attacks from behind for quick kills? Screwed. Deliver some nasty punches of fury for kills? Screwed unless your enemy is alone and not facing you.

Is this bad? Not at all. Just something to consider if you've been seeing the "This game is like Hotline Miami!" reviews. Because in all honesty, it's not very much like HM.

I didn't have that great of a time getting into this game due to the fact that my expectations were placed elsewhere. That being said, I can't give it a negative review just because of that. It's a solid game and even though it's personally not my type of thing, I can respect it as a good, well-designed game. The developer did a fantastic job with the concept and I hope this is a sign of more to come in the future.

DEADBOLT is an amazing blend of stealth, mystery, and flat-out murder. You play as the Grim Reaper on his quest to slay hordes of undead in various gangs using a wide variety of weapons ranging from humble pistols to semi-automatic grenade launchers. Each level is unique and very challenging (sometimes a bit too challenging). The vent transport mechanic requires timing and is also super fun to use. The music adds a great atmosphere to the game, making it seem even more dark. DEADBOLT is obviously influenced by Gunpoint, but it's not a rip-off at all. There is an air of mystery throughout the whole game because you are only told to kill zombies, but the reasoning is never fully explained. All in all, Hopoo Games has made a terrific noir style game that is simultaneously mind-bendingly tricky and possibly more addictive than crack. Did I mention you get to kill zombies on the toilet? Big plus.
