About This Game

You are Dehon, the Great Disciple of the Caste of the 40 000 Moons. You have slain and devoured your brothers because they lacked courage in the face of the mysterious Evil that decimated your universe. Bravely fighting him in your fortress temple, you gradually lost your mind, and in a moment of lucidity you took your own life.
Your body and soul drifted through the spirals of time, going back to the origin of everything. When you woke up in the temple of Evil, you felt your hate towards this Evil hadn’t faded. You will have to follow an initiatory course that will lead you to confront this Evil, guided by your father and mentor Gommend. Will you really keep the promise you made to yourself?

Features:

- An anguishing atmosphere for a real gaming experience.
- Game-play: access to powers never seen before
- Full-3D universe with dynamic cameras
- Two game modes: Arena where you fight incessant waves of monsters or Adventure where you evolve in a storyline mixing action and adventure.
- Duration goes from 3 to 4 hours

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, 7, Vista
    • Processor: 2 GHz processor
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: DirectX 9c Compatible video card w/128mb vram
    • Storage: 200 MB available space

To make it simple, Monster Challenge Circus is probably the game that depicts hell in the most convincing way :

-clunky camera control
-confusing spell selection
-unintuitive mission objectives
-oversimplistic combat system
-glitchy gameplay/cutscene transitions

To make things even worse, the story revolves around a father-son relationship that is strangely reminiscent of Freud's oedipus complex. How awkward could it get?

To be honest, it is quite disappointing that Monster Challenge Circus turned out as an unfinished product since it had some potential. Imagine a game like Sanity : Aiken's Artefact but with a Thrill Kill/Silent Hill twist. This is what the game could have been.

I usually don't review games because there are usually other people to do it, however in this case the game is still pretty unknown for the most part and the reviews I feel don't do enough justice to this game.

To start off, I'm sure you can already tell this game is bad. But no, you won't know until you've played it. The UI is awful, ugly, clunky; pretty much every negative word you can think of. And that almost sums up every single other aspect of the game. The graphics would have been acceptable for an indie game, but the creator felt slapping a grainy filter on it would give it a more distinct look. It does have one though, it distinctly looks like ♥♥♥♥. The voice acting is cheesy, in a foreign/made up language, and it's obvious the developer was trying to create a sense of role-playing as the main character Dehon and to reinforce the fact that he's a... Great Disciple of the Caste of the 40,000 Moons? Story, there is no ♥♥♥♥ing story. It's copy-pasted "defeat the evil; save the world" ♥♥♥♥. The gameplay, this is easily the worst part of the game, yes even worse than the name "Monster Challenge Circus". There's so many pointless skills and features, and pairing that with the stiff point and click controls and terrible level design makes a minute in this game feel like five. Combat and movement is so slow, and there's no reward for killing an enemy. No expirience, no money, no notes, no anything. It's just ♥♥♥♥ing awful and tedious. You will hate the combat after your first encounter with an enemy, I am dead serious.

Finally, I feel the need to bring up I've played other despised indie games before like The Letter and Bad Rats. This game somehow beats them in being even worse. The Letter was awful but kept you hanging around until the end just to see how bad it could get, Bad Rats has a unique charm that although very ♥♥♥♥♥♥ makes it enjoyable for an hour or two before you get bored. But this game has no charm, no interesting story to keep your interested, not even memorable bad things. It's just very forgettable. Don't buy it as a joke, don't buy it to gift to your friend, don't buy it to play it just to see how bad it is. You will gain more spending the 50 cents on a ♥♥♥♥♥♥ toy from a gas station. It has more excitement and enjoyment than this excuse for a game, and how it got through Greenlight I will honestly never know.

Pros:
-You'll play it for 5 minutes and forget it the next week.

Cons:
-The single worst game you will ever play.
