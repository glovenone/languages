About This Game

East Africa, 2009. A 60-year conflict boils over as Ethiopia invades its smaller neighbor Eritrea, threatening the world's most vital shipping lanes in the Red Sea. An elite team of U.S. Army Green Berets, known as the Ghosts, moves in to safeguard the seas and free Eritrea. As the war rages on, the Ghosts are drawn from Eritrea's shores to the heart of Ethiopia in their deadliest battles yet.

  • New multiplayer modes - Team-based Siege and Domination modes allow teams to attack or defend territory.
  • Both new modes are fully compatible with your original Ghost Recon game - play for hours on the original maps.
  • 8 new single-player missions.
  • 4 new dedicated multiplayer spaces.
  • 9 new multiplayer weapons, including the Bizon submachine gun, the M98 sniper rifle, and the fearsome M-60 machine gun.

System Requirements

    Minimum:

    • Supported OS: Microsoft Windows® 2000/XP
    • Processor: Pentium® II 450 MHz, AMD K6 III 500, or equivalent
    • System Memory: 128 MB of RAM or above
    • Video Card: 16 MB DirectX® 8.0-compliant 3D card; Voodoo™ cards not supported
    • Sound Card: DirectX 8.0-compliant sound card
    • DirectX Version: DirectX® version 8.0 or higher
    • Hard Disk: 2 GB available hard disk space
    • Multiplay: Properly configured TCP/IP connection at 56 Kbps or faster
    • Note: For an up-to-date list of supported chipsets, video cards, and operating systems, please visit the FAQ for this game at: http://support.ubi.com.

Posted: 16 October, 2015
Ghost Recon Desert Siege is the first expansion pack for Ghost Recon, developed by same Red Storm and released in 2002, one year after original. Yep, still same tactical shooter. It added new single-player campaign, new weapons and expanded multiplayer.

Well, since multiplayer is kinda dead, you probably not going to care about those two new modes. However, you can play 5 multiplayer maps in custom mission if you choose Firefight or Recon mods.
Also I didn't notice new weapons personally. I mean, how different they can be from whatever realistical weapons Ghost Recon already had.

But it does add a new single-player campaign made up of 8 missions. And this time things are going south, from cold Russia itno whatever hot African country, where you will be annoying everybody off by the border. Yep, it's still same Ghost Recon gameplay in different enviroment. Still with frustating AI, with enemies now even more happy to throw grenades that teammates AI doesn't bother to avoid, feeling sometimes unfair. Still quickscamming.
It doesn't add new gameplay elements, the only new thing are enemy jeeps, which have driver and machine gunner. They can screw you up and are nice addition. But it's puzzling that Demolition's anti-armor weapon is useless against jeep. I mean, direct hit may not even harm everybody in a jeep. It should blow up!

But the levels themselves felt much better designed around Ghost Recon gameplay. They are now much more wide open, consisting of huge spaces. And I feel it makes it more pleasent to deal with teammate AI, as they are much better at providing support and cover over such distances. While you place them around to cover each other and move on slowly, taking baddies out from a far. Unless you once again have to enter building with only one path where you get one-shot instantly. Missions also didn't try to be gimmicky, the only armor escort mission has a very slow-moving convoy.

And well, this expansion is good. At least if you liked the original. Overall I think I had more positive experience with Desert Siege's 8 missions than with original's 15 levels.
