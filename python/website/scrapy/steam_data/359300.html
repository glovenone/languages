About This Game

Death Pirate is a new kind of shoot'em up mixing action and RPG elements. Unravel the mysteries of the Death Pirate and be prepared to face hundreds of enemies at once! Meet Hideo and his team in an exciting story. Level up and upgrade your weapons to survive in 26 unique stages and two difficulty levels.

Characteristics

- 60 fps action!

- Available Language : french and english

- HD Graphics

- A mix of action and RPG

- Play as a super hero who can fly and explore vast levels in various environments

- 15 stages and 14 special episodes

- 7 unique characters

- Explore the city of South Town and investigate on the mystery of the Death Pirate

- An original story and an edgy soundtrack

- Level up and upgrade your technique and abilities

- Two difficulty settings

- Trophies and trading cards are supported.

Posted: 21 December, 2015
I... don't really know what to say about Heavy Metal Death Pirate. Some parts of it, like the story and art, seem as though they had a lot of effort put into them, if perhaps not that much skill. Likewise, it seems as though the developer may have dealt with some downright shadiness when it came to how Desura/IndieRoyale treated him. (as seen on his blog: http://singestudio.blogspot.fr/2015/08/final-comments.html )

On the other hand, the result is just a pile of crap that feels in many places as though the author had never played a side-scrolling shooter before, with wonky and giant hitboxes, a forced position in the middle of the screen that doesn't give you much time to react to enemies once you see them, enemies that are obscured by the distracting backgrounds, background objects that position themselves in front of enemies, controls that are never really explained, and long missions that you have to restart from the beginning if you die. The first "side story" is supposed to be a tutorial, but there doesn't seem to be any way to complete it or even get beyond the first screen, as the tutorial guy never continues talking after his first line.

So yeah. I hope the author doesn't swear off game development for good, but this was a terrible first effort in a lot of ways, and I wouldn't recommend it to anyone except people who are more patient with bad games than I am (and that's saying a lot...) and really want to play a game about the adventures of power-suited superheroes.

(I did not complete this - I spent about ten minutes fighting with the controls and the apparently broken tutorial, and then after several tries completed the first real level. I tried the second level once, but decided there was absolutely no way I was going to enjoy this and that I would just delete it and be done with it.)

Terrible, F.

Just outright amateur. Everything might technically work, but the actual game in every aspect leaves your frustrated and confused as to how someone could create such a horrible product.

First of all, there's no fullscreen option, and there's no sound options, or basically anything useful, but it's not really a huge problem, since you can press ALT+ENTER and get fullscreen whenever you want. I tried to press on 'button config' but it kept just bringing me back to the main menu. Sometimes it will give you a screen which shows that it wants you to press a button and nothing seems to work, or in some cases if there is 20 lines of dialog it will all just instantly skip without any indication as to why or what you did, it just does things on it's own. I tried recording some video, and I couldn't help but notice that the game goes fullscreen on it's own sometimes, by simply pressing 'A' alone after like the 5th screen it would go fullscreen everytime I tapped 'A'. I'll give them credit for having controller support, but I give them no credit for making a playable game. It's awful. Played through the first level a few times now and it's sort of decently priced at launch ($2) but in my opinion, it's not really worth your time.

The introductary prologue part I went to, clicked on once, it gave a date and a time on a black screen and then went back to the main menu instantly. Upon choosing story mode it just shows that I successfully completed the prologue.

Virtually every time you press a button on your controller you are wasting your time with this stupid broken game. There is absolutely no hint of polish or video game development experience.
