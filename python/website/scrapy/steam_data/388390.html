OK It's a simplistic billiards type puzzle game: pot all the balls, avoid exploding traps etc.
It's kind of fun although very hard but at least you keep restarting on the same level you've reached when you mess up.

The trouble is that this is basically like a couple of days' worth of work in Unity and it's being sold as a "proper" game. It's not just that this fact offends and annoys me (although it does) but it leads to awkward controls and lack of control over tha ball.

You have to use wasd to aim the cueball then hold space for power. The trouble is that all the power settings are pretty feeble except the top one. You have no spin options, it's just direction plus power. Aiming with wasd is pretty hard because the increments it jumps are fairly large, so precise aiming is out of the question.

To top it all off the physics resemble no pool physics you ever saw. You are helped by holes which suck down balls like a greedy ball-sucking hole though, so you "got that going for you, which is nice".

A nice idea but a horrible execution.

This game should be fun -- use your cue ball to get the other balls into pits, avoiding obstacles etc.
Sadly, this game isn't fun. It looks and plays like an incomplete demo rather than a full game.

Controls are keyboard only. You cannot fine-tune shots at all, you just have to hope you somehow get the right angle by mashing wasd or the arrow cluster. Once you start powering up a shot and overshoot your desired power level, you're out of luck -- the only option is to release, shooting the ball, or to let it fill up the bar completely and fire.

Every proper shot earns you point, every shot you don't pocket a ball gives you a penalty. Go negative, and the level resets. Whenever you hit an obstacle with your cue ball or a shot ball, you get another penalty.

The ball physics aren't realistic at all and, worse, don't seem to be consistent. This makes puzzles incredibly difficult for all the wrong reasons, as you combine bad physics with trial and error.

Did I mention that levels fully reset whenever you make a mistake? There is no rewind, there is no undo -- you have to perfectly complete a full level or just start over.

Any one of these on its own is forgivable. Combined, they make the game bad and simply not fun.
Normally I wouldn't review a game with such a short play time, but this game offers me absolutely no incentive to keep playing. If the first four levels are already incredibly flawed and more frustrating than anything else, I'm not going to sink more time in it.

To the dev: add some fine-tuning to controls, if you can't add mouse aiming. Even Peggle has this! Also add a rewind/undo last shot option. When I have to pocket 8 balls and a simple error on the 7th forces a full level reset, it doesn't make me want to play any longer.
