Dragon Age: Origin [4/5]
This game helped me discover my love for RPG. Steep learning-curve, but worth it.

Story [4.5/ 5]
Gameplay [3.5/5]
Plot [4.5/5]

Similar to Mass Effect 2 this game provide a wide range of interesting characters that you get to associate with, in this case, you will have to bring the fight to darkspawn, ending the Blight. All the characters have traits, skills and personality that you would eventually have to get use to and stick with, and maybe fall in love with in the later time. The more time invested in the game, the more you understand the strength and weakness of each characters.

Character customization, this game allows you to choose your characters' race (human, elf, dwarf) and class(warrior, rogue, mage). All of those with different attributes and strength. Tinkering with the inventory is always fun. Deciding the types of weapons to equip depending on the class of the characters. Dialogues are cheesy, but still ok. The outcome and the consequences of the story depends on the decisions through all those cheesy dialogues that you make.

The story might seem straight forward, ending the Blight, reclaiming peace for Ferelden. True, that is the ultimate goal. The essence of the game is when you explore Ferelden through various quests while getting ready to face the Blight. Deep and in depth backstory of characters and the plot itself. The BGM was ok, but really gets you into the mood during combat.

The gameplay is limited, but strangely satisfying. The Combat System was well developed allowing you to pause during a battle, enabling you to strategise your attack. Strategy may vary based on players choice of character and companions.
The best part is that, no matter how high your characters' level is, one mistake would â¥â¥â¥â¥-up the outcome of the game.
