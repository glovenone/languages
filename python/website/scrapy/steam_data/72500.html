About This Game

Arcadia is a fun, casual little shooter. There are a variety of enemies that will hunt you down, and a few little tactics you can use to take them down. Try to get the best score and stay on top of the leaderboard. The game is played from a top-down perspective. All your enemies are in view 90% of the time so it's easy to see and, most importantly, blow them apart. Don't sit still for long, they're coming for you. Can you handle the pressure?

Key features:

  • Arcadia contains a casual mode for easy-going gamers, and a chaotic mode for those looking for a challenge.
  • Compete with thousands of other players to top the leaderboards.
  • An epic soundtrack
  • Custom Mode available via the Console.

System Requirements

    • OS: Windows® 7 / Vista / Vista64 / XP
    • Processor: Pentium 4 2.00GHz or better processor
    • Memory: 512MB RAM
    • Graphics: Dedicated 128MB graphics card
    • Hard Drive: ~50 MB
    • Sound: OpenAL supported card

5min of this game (a little bit of all 4 songs and all 4 modes)
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VQouJKjvJRA

-Note-If this game detects a controller it will not run right unplug it!

This is a bare bones shmup.
The main draw is the music and you will enjoy the game until the songs "get old"
There are 4 songs now (when i got this there was 1) so you can swap them out when your sick of one.
There are 4 modes of play though they are more or less the same thing and they are casual (gets steadily harder), chaotic (starts out hard), pivot (you can't move), & time attack (instead of a score you see how long you can survive).
As your score goes up you'll get 1-ups also your shots will get faster then split into 2's then 3's (not sure if there are 4's as i never lived that long) and your special will shoot out in a circle around you.
Your lives and specials are show in the upper left as dotes for lives and squares for specials and if you die your specials will be refilled back to 3.
There are only about 6 enemies in the game bubbles (chase you), big bubbles (when shot splits into 8 small bubbles), purple squares (when shot shoots a piece in 4 directions), green squares (will avoid shots), yellow squares (will also avoid shots), & red circles with shield (the shield will reflect shots).

Controller support is a lie! yes you can have one and it kinda works but you'll be playing with the keyboard and mouse.
With a xbox controller you can move with the analog stick but you'll need to enable auto fire cause there is none on the controller, also by default you'll face right but to face left you need to hit back on the other analog stick, then up, & down are your trigger buttons, then there is no diagonal option.
Even having a controller plugged in will destroy your controls so you'll need to unplug it to play.

Before Greenlight and Early Access, this was the first piece of true shovelware I remember making it onto Steam. I was baffled as to how it actually made the cut. In a way, it was inspirational at the time; if something like this can get onto Steam, a guy like me can still dream, right?

There's no way to describe this game other than "Crappy Geometry Wars". You mindlessly shoot hilariously unbalanced enemies that will fly at you from the sides of the screen and kill you before you can dodge. There's one broken achievement, the score doesn't even display properly (19,000 displays as "19,0"), and the game doesn't even remember your high score. The game even received a steady stream of updates for a few years, which somehow made it even worse.

The one positive thing I can say about this game is that it introduced me to "Surface" by Dimrain47, a great piece of music.

In beginning of a boom of Indie games back in 2007 and 2008 it saw a number of casual games featuring graphics much like these. I am going to try to be respectful in this review, but this game is very bad and easily the worst I've played in a while.

First of all, Arcardia fails at the basic requirement of being functional software. I was not able to get a working controls either by keyboard or mouse or my gamepad. There is no help menu or even a key binding screen I can tell what keys I need to press. If you want to rebind the gamepad keys you need to edit the configuration file manually. If you want to rebind the keybaord keys then you may be out of luck as I saw no configuration file for the keyboard.

Gameplay wise, this game really wants to be Geometry Wars. It's to the point where it follows Geometry Wars home and Geometry wars should consider getting a restraining order. If this game was commercially successful the developers of Geometry Wars should consider legal action. You might think I'm exaggerating, but I played them side-by-side and the difference is uncanny. The only difference is that Geometry Wars actually works and is a lot more polished.

Acardia mimics Geometry Wars in the following ways; grid background, distortion effects, weapons and weapon progression, controls (best I can tell anyway, seeing as they didn't really work for me). Even much of the enemy AI is a mirror of Geometry Wars. It's even to the point where the same AI had the same rough shape and color as it's counterpart in Geometry Wars.

Just buy Geometry Wars if you don't already own it. It's much more polished and, most importantly, it actually works.

Score: 0/10
