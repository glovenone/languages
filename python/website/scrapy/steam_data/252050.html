About This Game

Become a boss of a construction company. Demolish old houses, flatten the ground, fill foundations with concrete and build new buildings - this is what you do. Sit behind the wheel of different construction machines - from trucks and diggers to huge cranes. Win contracts and gather money to expand your company and buy or upgrade your machines.

Game Features:
  • advanced physics and interaction with environment
  • 8 different construction machine types
  • realistic handling
  • 20 contracts to fullfill
  • additional challenges and free play mode

I would like to recommend this game - equipment simulators are often sincere, dedicated to their simple premise, and deliver on a lowball promise to replicate the experience of using these machines. It's not flashy, or dramatic, but much like model railways, there's an enduring interest in simulation.

Certainly, all the stuff you expect is in this game - a variety of machines, even a first person view as you run around your machine, check it out, and jump in your vehicle, steer, drive, operate the bucket with simulated levers. Doing jobs earns you money, which you spend to buy better equipment, so it has a nice progression/career system.

In the case of this game, however - the technical settings let everything down. No support for 4K means the game is now effectively dead for all machines of the future. I'll be happy to review my review if they ever fix it, but until then, gaming moves on, and the developers have chosen, through inaction, to let Construction Machines 2014 stay behind.

Well I have played 4 hours of it upon writing this review. I am not quite sure where the complaints are coming from but here is my personal experience. Brace yourself it is not exactly short. This will more be a review for people who want a bit more detail of the game instead of just a "it sucks" type of review.

Camera:
Press C and get into outside view, use left mouse button and can move camera any angle you like.
I could post the suggestion to maybe in the future put more camera control in all the views.


Graphics:
I have not seen an issue there either, when launching game it gives you options to change settings and so far am getting basically what I see in the trailer. If you experience lag, just lower your settings. It has its bumps and jolts here and there but nothing to make it unplayable for me.

(Will also add I am not sure how peoples CPUs and GPUs are overheating, Mine are all within normal temperature while running this game)

Gameplay:
Challenging at times. But I enjoy a challenge. The game will require you to take your time and go slow at times, you are not a racecar driver. But if you do you can complete your challenges, I have been able to complete all them first try so far.

You can flip your vehicles over so be careful. To me this just gets you to be careful while playing and adds to the challenge.

Yes some things will bounce around, but I only seemed to have that problem if I went at it like a bat out of hell. It just convinced me to take my time doing it which honestly allowed me to enjoy the game more.

I find it to be a decent simulation, you are doing just as it describes. If the game made it too easy on you and were able to rush whole thing would not be any playablitity.

This game I think is suitable for people into this genre of games, you must be willing to accept this genre before enjoying it. It will not be for everyone.

Tips
As for anyone having crashes or not starting here is some things I personally found that helped if I ever get an error.
1) Do windows updates
2)Restart computer
3) Launch settings instead of game, click save and play. I have had this fix errors even if I didn't change the settings, is this gamebreaking? Not really as it is a 2 second solution. (If this is your problem. Not saying it will solve everyones but solved mine.)

Conclusion:
Is the game perfect? No, I am not saying it is, it has its flaws but what game doesn't?
The developer has also been quite active with the community to take in feedback which is a plus. Just give him your feedback if you see something, at least this game has an active one.

As for the price range, it is only $15, it is not like it is a $40-$60 AAA simulator game. I think the price range for this and how the game plays out is quite worth it for that range for people interested in the genre.

If I encouter any game breaking problems I will post it as well but so far my game is still running fine with the exception of the couple things I mentioned, all minor to me.
