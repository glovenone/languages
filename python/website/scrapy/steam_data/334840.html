About This Game

Drive to Hell is an action-packed shooter where you'll take a ride through an army of monsters to exact revenge on the Demon King for destroying your favorite dive bar. Enemies assault from all sides, each with its own special personality and attack style. To take them down, you'll have access to many weapons and items as well as unique vehicles, special abilities and more!



  • 5 levels with 5 waves apiece, each with its own unique look
  • 3 difficulty Settings, challenging beginners and experts alike
  • Up to 4-player local co-op
  • Endless survival mode
  • Mirror mode
  • Over a dozen power-ups & weapons
  • Hordes of deadly monsters including 5 bosses
  • Numerous vehicles each with their own stats & special abilities
  • Trophy system with a reward for completion
  • Original soundtrack
  • 3 control options (keyboard/keyboard & mouse/controller)
    • Please see Drive to Hell controller guide for more details
  • Customizable options for sound & display
  • Vertical display resolution options
  • Steam achievements & trading cards

Is Drive to Hell shoot'em up? Twin stick shooter? Bullet-hell? Actually, its more like a mix of all of them, plus a bit of driving game with a crazy storyline. Yeah, story is about aliens invading the Earth and you - some driver shooting the hell of them. Oh, and I'm gonna spoil it, you will me meet a big alien boss at the end of the game ;).

Drive to Hell has all elements what makes this genre so immersive - solid gameplay and good balance. Shooting is satisfying and the interesting part of gameplay is that it enforces player to pick and use different power-ups, because they are freaking powerful, while the main gun, in most cases, is weak and requires much more effort to succeed. Varied enemy types adds up to a great experience. As I mentioned, balance is suprisingly good, normal difficulty is suitable for anyone, while higher difficulties and survival mode really test your skills, but in a good way and is never impossible.

Gameplay feels good and features many cars to choose from, which can be fast, but without much of firepower or a complete opposite. Completing levels with good score earns money, which can be used only for different cars. Length is just right, there are 5 levels with 5 waves each and a boss at the end of level. Along with 4 difficulties, many achievements and survival mode it can easily immerse for at least 5 hours. For such a low price its amazing how meaty the game is.

What really separates Drive to Hell from others shoot'em ups is graphics. Its made with Unity engine and features very crisp colors, which makes it so easy to notice every enemy or bullet.

Well, there is some some problems too, coop mode works, but it can be hard to make your controller work, also music and sound effects lacks variety.

Drive to Hell really shows how indie shmups improved recently, with its slick gameplay and some inovations. True bang for your buck!

9/10

Typically, my recommendation is dependent upon whether or a videogame exceeds my expectations. Went I started Drive to Hell, I wasn't expecting much if any quality. Devils just destroyed this guy's favorite hangout, so he's gonna get revenge. I mean...really? That's just totally gag-worthy. Also when the title screen has "Like us on Facebook/Twitter/Other Networking Sites!" I begin to wonder why I even installed the game. After all, idlemaster works as long as a game is in your library.

But, I played through the game a few times, and it's legit.

Drive to Hell is a vehicular twin-stick shooter. You're on a highway (to hell) and devils approach from every direction. Some simply home in on you, others circle around the screen, and some prefer to harass you from the sides. Also since you're in a vehicle, your mobility tends to be limited. Most of the play-field either slows you down, or is blocked off entirely. Twin-stick shooters tend to get claustrophobic, so one where you have maybe 1/3 of the screen to actually work with gets pretty ridiculous.

While shooting everything that moves is fine, you also have to consider your vehicle. There are a handful to unlock, all with their own strengths, weaknesses, and special abilities. While you can drive a heavily armored tank, you'll find that it is slow, and its bulk makes weaving in-between bullets a bit too difficult. Alternatively something light and weak is liable to be much smaller and more maneuverable. The special abilities can be anything from a bullet-absorbing shield to proximity mines.

It's also important to look out for weapon and item power-ups. Weapons are just that, more powerful (but temporary) methods for bringing death to minions. Items are a bit different, since they can held onto for the right situation. This might be a good reason to choose a heavier vehicle, because they can carry more items. Being able to crack open a shield or a bomb when the situation gets out of control is really handy.

That's pretty much the extent of it I believe. Even though there are boss-battles, this isn't the type of game where you have to memorize patterns or develop strategies. Choosing the right vehicle for your play-style and your reflexes will get you very far. There are four difficulties though, and the later settings can get really intense. Thankfully, the difficulty doesn't do anything cheap, like force one-hit kills on you, or make it so you can't continue after dying. However, if you're the type that plays for score, you can't take damage at all, because it'll kill your multiplier. All in all there's a good difficulty balance for every kind of gamer.

Drive to Hell gets my recommendation because it exceeded my (admittedly low) expectations. I'd even go as far to say as I'm pretty impressed with it. Once I got over my initial "What is this hunk of.." reaction, I discovered that this is a solidly-designed game with satisfying controls, playability, and even a touch of depth. My only real complaint is that there are some annoying foreground objects that block a good portion of the screen. This hasn't led to any deaths (yet), but I've gotten hit once or twice. Otherwise this game is pretty decent, you might want to give it a try.
