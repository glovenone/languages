Although Half-Life suffers from its dated graphics, slippery movement, and annoying design choices (Teleporter puzzle). I still reccomend this game to anyone looking to play something old and different. It has an interesting story for its time, fast-paced action, and barely any glitches or random crashes. When I first heard of Half-Life, I was interested in the series and wanted to know why everyone was putting the Half-Life series in high regards. After playing this game however, I can see why the game has an overall review rating of Overwhelmingly Positive. However, this is the first Half-Life game I have ever played and beaten so, I still have to go through the expansions and Half-Life 2 to fully understand the love of the Half-Life series.

Pick this game up and I promise you that you'll love it in the end even after witnessing its noticeable flaws.

"Half-Life 3 is to be released immediately."

I was hesitant to get this or Black Mesa, but i wanted to play the two expansions as well so i went for the classic one. Most of the cool fun parts happen in the first two-thirds of the game, even before you get to Xen, which is just really annoying to play and get through. The last parts before Xen have you doing these mini mazes to turn on reactors and it wears down your patience.

The first half is really enjoyable though, watching everything go to sh*t as you unleash the alien hordes on the Black Mesa research facility, and then later having to also fight through the army/marines sent to kill all of you scientists for some reason. If you haven't played it before you may get stuck at some parts as its not made clear where you have to go or what you have to do, "oh i have to jump on this broken ledge and then jump to the other side and crawl into this tiny sewer/crevice, got ya"


Graphically it's dated pretty badly, some of the textures may be the worst i've seen in a game,
