Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“DRIFT 84 was originally built with the invention it would be played on mobile devices. However, the game was built on a PC and tested on a PC. We feel it is actually more fun in a lot of ways on the PC and have decided to work on making it a cross platform release.

The game engine has been completed and we are now spending time finishing off the 50+ tracks. We hope the community will help us offer the most challenging and rewarding version of DRIFT 84 possible through their ongoing opinion and critic.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We are aiming to have the final release of DRIFT 84 out 3-6 months after the early access release.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The PC release of DIRFT 84 is set to differ greatly from the mobile version. We want our customers to know that they are purchasing a very different game.
  • 6 car real-time multiplayer with collisions.
  • full feature track editor and most used track board
  • controller support
  • upgraded garage with 10x functionality
  • full stereo soundtrack pumped with 80's newave synth power
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We currently have a 99% bug-free game engine where you can compete in the first stage, England. You can continue to play the game after completing the stage and try to unlock any of the 6 available cars.

We will be adding new tracks on a daily basis so we are looking to the community for feedback on our designs.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“During Early Access we will be offering a launch discount and it is likely the game will increase in price once we launch with a fully integrated multiplayer system.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are finding the track building process difficult and will be looking to the community for feedback on the progression of the game and also track ideas once we have launched the in-game track builder.”

About This Game

DRIFT 84 puts you at the wheel of 6 realistic, ESC disabled drift racing cars as you make your way around over 50+ tracks across Europe. A hyper-addictive, easy to play racing game that can offer 30 seconds or 30 minutes of high octane drifting.



- Drift your way around over 50+ tracks spanning 5 countries across Europe.
- Upgrade your ride with real car upgrades from the Manifold and Turbo to Shocks and Tyres.
- The ultimate 80's nostalgia with Outrun/Retro music to neon text and graphics.
- Simple two touch steering with dynamic acceleration and braking.



Join us as we add new tracks to DRIFT 84 and push your lap times to the edge with new challenges and real-time leaderboards.



- Compete online in REAL-TIME against friends or foes.
- Race against your friends ghost laps, or challenge them to beat yours.

Real-Time Online Multiplayer Mode
- Log on and challenge friends in 6 player online mode.
- Go head-to-head in a real-time race or drift against players offline ghosts.
- Challenge the leaderboards and take on top ranking racers for the number 1 spot.

Full Feature Soundtrack
- 80's synth-wave sounds
- Downloadable soundtrack



DRIFT 84 was created by developer Thomas Webb as an experiment after playing famous top down racing games such as GTA as a kid. His father, Martin Webb single handedly developed the commercial console release of Outrun on the Commodore 64 in the 80's. Following in his fathers footsteps almost 30 years later, Thomas has taken his fathers past and used it as inspiration for his first release, DRIFT 84. Martin was on board half way through development, aiding with his extensive experience and adding the addictive 80's coin-op game mechanics.

I think DRIFT 84 has potential and is a good purchase if you're looking for a game to help you unwind. It's really easy to pick up, the controls are good (keyboard for now, controller support is planned for later), the audio is decent, and the difficulty curve, at least for the tracks available currently (at its Early Access launch), is well-tuned.

You'll need to play a bit to be able to unlock most cars and parts but it feels satisfying, especially after tuning them. Things should be easier though with future updates as I believe you'll get access to tracks that give you higher prizes. Once you get better cars you'll also find bonus opportunities (drift, burnout), you can get NOS, so there is incentive to play although there will be farming done, if you want something better. Again, this should be addressed once more tracks are added.

The game ran stable for me, no crashes, I didn't encounter any bugs and it was simply fun. If you get it one thing to definitely buy is the rainbow smoke, really worth the $1,000 (in-game cash of course).

Early Access Review
Posted: 14 July, 2015
This game appears to be in very early alpha. I feel it isn't worth even the low introductory price. I feel it simply isn't ready yet, even for Steam Early Access.

Prior to purchasing, I checked out the screenshots. It reminded me of classic games like Super Sprint or RC Pro-Am, games I was a huge fan of back in the day. The potential may be there, time will tell.

The game is tagged with Multiplayer and Cross-platform multiplayer, which would be great, however, the multiplayer aspects have not yet been integrated into the game. As of now, this is a major disappointment. "That's OK", I thought. "I can just play against the AI"

Well, there is just one problem with that: this game has no AI! In Single Player mode, all there is to do is race around the track solo, sort of a "time trial mode" but without the Time Trial. There is literally no incentive to do well.

At this point I feel the game isn't yet in a playable state, but I do see potential here. I'm skeptical of some of the positive user reviews left thusfar.

The options menu states that controllers are not yet supported. There is no support to change screen resolution. The only options available are choosing between Fullscreen and Windowed (a window that cannot be resized), and a Master Volume slider.

At this point I'm going to request a refund through Steam. If at any point this game improves on some of the lacking features that I believe should be there (even in early access), I might repurchase the game and update my review as I see fit.

The verdict: don't buy this game, at least not yet.

One other small thing -- What's up with the artwork on the store page? This is a game about racing on "50+ tracks across Europe". The little guy in the artwork is holding a Califormia license plate.
