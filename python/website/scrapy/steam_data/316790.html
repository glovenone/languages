About This Game

Something's rotten in the land of the dead, and you're being played for a sucker. Meet Manny Calavera, travel agent at the Department of Death. He sells luxury packages to souls on their four-year journey to eternal rest. But there's trouble in paradise. Help Manny untangle himself from a conspiracy that threatens his very salvation.

One of the most acclaimed adventure games of all time is now back, better than ever. Grim Fandango's epic story of four years in the life (or death) of Manny Calavera, travel agent to the dead, has been remastered to look, sound, and control even better than when it won GameSpot's Game of the Year award upon its original launch. Grim Fandango still stands as a classic of the genre, with unforgettable characters and unique combination of film noir and Mexican folklore.

Remastered version includes

  • Repainted, hi-res character textures
  • New, dynamic lighting
  • Classic score re-recorded with a full live orchestra
  • Over 2 hours of exclusive developer commentary
  • Concept art browser

An enjoyable point and click game with a fun story and lots of humorous lines from all characters.

Being a remastered version I did hope it would have been a bit better graphically but seems more to have just been updated to 1080p and touched up with the background looking better than the characters and they sort of stood out in a bit of a bad way. The lighting has been improved and now shows shadows on characters but the character models could have been done better and often looked out of place.

Some of the puzzles are quite tricky and, for me at least, caused me to end up running back and forth to try different items in my inventory on each character and anywhere else you could try them resulting in a lot of these quotes from our hero Manny:
"I don't think that would shed any light on things"
"I don't really want to do that"
"I don't want to mess up my blade" (But you'll drag it through Poo ?)
"That soul has already been reaped" (No, I don't want to reap their soul. I want to hit them with the scythe!)

I know this wasn't present in the original and this is only a re-release but having not played this before, I missed that you couldn't combine items in your inventory together like in Broken Sword or Kaptain Brawe (both really good adventure point and click games too). Obviously I understand this isn't something that they would add just for a remastered version but is something I like in adventure games when they merge two seemingly unrelated items together often resulting in comedic one liners from the character. Although you do end up doing this in the last year but it's not like you can select one item and try adding it to another in your pockets as that feature isn't available.

The Inventory did get quite tiresome to navigate. Rather than having a list of your items pictured, you instead have to open Manny's coat and then select right or left to see the next item which he removes from his inside pocket. Here you can then either select the item to use it in some way on someone/something or you can examine the item, in which Manny will talk about the item to give you clues. Because of the way this is set out its quite slow if you want to try a few items on something as you first have to select the item so he removes this from his inside pocket, and then before you can try another item you have to put it away. This was especially slow when taking out and putting away the scythe and got quite annoying as you cannot do anything while he is putting the item away and also cannot interact with anything else if you still have an item out that doesn't fit with what you are interacting with. E.g. select to walk through a door while your scythe is out and again "I don't want to mess up my blade"

Another annoyance was that he would have to walk to a certain point until he would interact with an item or talk to a person. Even if I just pressed the look option and was right next to the item he would walk half way across the screen, or several paces, stop and then comment.

Apart from the downsides of the game the story still keeps you playing and there are lots of funny comments from Manny and this makes up for the downsides in the game. Although the "remastered" version, for me, isn't much more than a 1080p resolution upgrade with shadows, the game is still enjoyable to play and at the end, I wanted more :(
Hope they make a sequel someday

70/100 - Story and characters make this worth it

Grim Fandango is a LucasArts classic adventure game, and hoo boy, is it a classic.
It's hard to go wrong when you've got this many skeletons in one game as it is, but Grim Fandango excels beyond expectations even then.
The characters are all memorable, the dialogue is hilarious and witty, the design of the world is gorgeous, the story is intriguing and complex, the visuals are striking (if you're a fan of 90's CGI you're in for a treat), and it ticks every box for me when it comes to what I want out of an adventure game.

For those curious between the differences between the original and the Remastering, the Remastering mostly offers a visual facelift (for the most part anyway), developer commentary, concept art, a point and click system, and not much else. The cutscenes and some backgrounds are noticably dated still, the cutscenes just being scaled up to widescreen and not actually having any visual upgrades. However, I'm willing to forgive it as the actual creation process behind the remaster was a nightmare, as none of the original source material had been preserved and it had to be hacked together through all sorts of crazy means.

Overall, the biggest benefit to the remastering is the fact that it is uploaded digitally, keeping this game, ironically enough, from death, easily accessible for the forseeable future. You can even switch to the original visuals any time you want if you want that classic 90's goodness.

The game's biggest weak point is probably the puzzles. Many adventure games feature puzzles less about logic and more about figuring out what kind of drugs the devs were doing, and Grim Fandango frequently is no different. I was not able to make it through the game without hints, and I imagine you won't be able to either. It's certainly a flaw, but it's one that people who like adventure games are used to dealing with.
If you like spooky skeletons, calacas, and stuff with heavy Spanish themes, you'll love this. If you're looking for a game for Halloween, this is right up your alley. If you like good games, you'll love this. Play this. I can't recommend it enough. THIS GAME. IS GOOD. BUY IT.

Posted: 9 June
There's a lot of nostalgia at work behind the ratings here. Grim Fandango starts brilliantly but loses sight of what makes an adventure fun. The story and characters are wonderful, the voice acting on the re-master is fun and expressive, and the theme is engrossing. It's a great start to an adventure game.

What the remaster didn't fix is an obnoxious inventory system and a set of puzzles that explodes outward until the game becomes unmanageably sprawling. Chapter 2 in particular is absolutely nuts in map size and puzzle density. You have some straightforward end goals but an absurd Rube Goldberg Device chain of causation to get there that can't possibly be understood. So instead of working to achieve your goals, you're working to do weird unrelated things just so your inventory will grow or something about the game state will change. It becomes less about intuition and more about "how many things do I have to fiddle with until the story finally advances?" Why am I stealing from a lawyer? Why am I borrowing a metal detector? Why am I helping an alcoholic relapse? None of it has a clear purpose when you do it. The purpose becomes clear when a hundred other things have been set in motion. It makes for intricate puzzles but really rips apart the idea that you're a character with goals and a story.

In total it feels unnecessarily padded out, which is really unusual for LucasArts. It feels more in line with the old Sierra games that were designed to get you buying the game guide. If you're looking for a kick of nostalgia, wait for a sale. Modern game design wouldn't tolerate such an unsatisfying slog. You've been taught to demand better than this by now.
