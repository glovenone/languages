About This Game

The Note - a game in which you have to find a missing friend.

Story


Andy works in the SCP Foundation, a bureau dedicated to the searching and retention of anomalous objects. Andy, an agent of this foundation, was sent to investigate the abnormality of a city called SCP-599. A few days later, no news was heard of Andy. Mike, as Andy's only partner who knew him well, went in search of Andy and the city. Although when he arrived to his realization the city has changed, meanwhile Andy had left notes of his research of SCP-599. Mike will have to learn of what happened in the city, and where Andy has gone.

Features


  • Realistic graphics
  • Perfect model of town, hospital, bunker
  • Perfect background music
  • Scary atmosphere
  • Gather history

Posted: 5 October, 2015
Im not entirely sure how I feel about this game.

There are major clipping issues. At the start, you can walk through the truck. Exploring the town, sometimes the camera "peaks" out through the walls. Most of the building assets are all the same, and the furnitue is non-existant. Even though it looks and feels half finished, what is there is decent.

Audio is something Im also on the fence about. It fades at random while outside. But when it is working as it should, its pretty good.

The objective of the game, collecting notes is a bit bland. Why not have more text in the notes? As it is, there are only a few words per note, barely enough to peak your interest. Some notes trigger a jump scare which seemed slightly out of place (because the monsters never attack or chase you), but did make me jump.

As a side note, as my friend was quick to point out, the bad guys are giant brown boob monsters in heels. And speaking of the boob monsters, becareful exploring the town. When the game goes fuzzy, a boob monster is near. If youre in a building, back away from the walls. I died at the start because I stood too close to a monster on the other side of the wall. And speaking of the boob monsters, they are on fixed pathes. They never chase, they never attack, they just follow their set path and ignore you.

Game play aside, the game lacks a UI. Hitting "escape" mid game doesnt seem to do anything. There also arent really any "options" other than graphics settings. No volume slider, no resolution sliders, nada. If you want to play in windowed, you need to hit alt+enter, but the window doesnt resize (or didnt for me at least). When I wanted to quit, I had to task manager kill it. Maybe it was just a freak bug I found, or so Im hoping.

Overall, the game appears and feels half-finished. What is there is decent, but you definitely notice that it is lacking in many ways, gameplay and graphically, which break immersion.

In the end I cant honestly give it a recommendation. The clipping bugs, lack of graphical assets (it bugged me beyond end that the buildings were all clean and empty), and buggy audio are what Id expect from an early access title, not something sold as a finished product. If you really want to play it, grab it on sale, but I wouldnt pay full price for it.

The Note's store description is a misleading piece describing how a man has gone missing in the town regarded as SCP-599 leaving only a few notes behind: you're tasked with gathering said research notes and finding the guy.
Too bad not only the actual game has nothing to do with the actual town described in the SCP wiki but is also just one of the countless poory made Slender clones that have been flooding game stores for years.

PROS:

-the soundtrack is somewhat decent

CONS:

-misleading store description, any reference to the SCP universe is completely squandered by the actual gameplay

-poor graphics, washed out colours, ugly textures

-lots of clipping everywhere, it's so bad you can even see through walls if you push against them

-below average sound effects

-awkward movement

-the notes you're supposed to gather will sometimes bug out

-uninspired, unthreatening enemy design

-lacks any kind of atmosphere

-cheap deaths

-broken English

-extremely linear and short

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=522732665

The original SCP-599 was an eerie town inhabited by people resembling those described in missing reports: the small town you'll explore in the game is made up of a few completely empty houses and 2/3 funny looking enemies loitering around. Said enemies have a sort of death aura and you'll die whenever one gets too close to you, even through walls thus leading to some frustrating deaths: their AI is super simple and, the rare times they'll try to slowly walk towards you, just ducking out of view will quickly make them lose interest.

You'll have to gather a certain amount of notes, said notes are supposed to be research left behind by the man you're looking for but between the broken English and simple, nonsense phrases ( what the heck is "it seems to me that what I saw" supposed to mean?) they just resemble something written by a 4yo. The notes also seem to always spawn in the same place so getting them will be easy once you know where they are: I had at least two notes bugging out though and I had to get near them and pick them up a second time before they finally disappeared.

If you can survive this dull exercise in frustration you'll have to do the same in an hospital: this part was even easier since there was only two floors with a monster each and both of them followed a very simple patrol route: once that's done you'll enter the final area and get the ending.

It took me 30 minutes to finish the game (including retries in the town area) and, after getting to the end, I can safely say there's really nothing going for it. The overall production values are very low, heck there's not even a death animation, the actual gameplay is awkward and lacks challenge once you learn how to avoid the cheap deaths and there's no sense of menace whatsoever as you travel through the bare environments. If you're looking for a Slender clone there's many better games out there, if you want SCP games there's some good ones for free: either way avoid this one.
