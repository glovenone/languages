About This Game

What is the longest possible length a bird can be? Obviously a question only all the fruit in the world can answer! But where could all this fruit be hiding? Follow Redbird, Greenbird and Bluebird on a quest for an amount of fruit beyond any birds wildest dreams.

Snakebird is a very simple but deceptively challenging puzzle game about assuming the right shapes for the task at hand, be it pushing, lifting, teleporting or just defying the laws of physics.

Key features:

  • Over fifty challenging levels
  • Non-linear world map
  • Several different zones with their own themed puzzles
  • Snake-like birds with narcolepsy
  • Dynamic fruit-powered length gain system

My problem with this game lies in the extreme narrowness of its solutions, despite the apparent breadth of ways to approach each puzzle. What I mean by this is that it's not enough to solve a part of a puzzle, you have to solve it by making the exact moves the developers envisioned, otherwise the next part of the puzzle will likely be unsolveable, however as you have no way of knowing this, hours of frustration await.

I consider myself a puzzle game enthusiast (I'm particularly fond of the masterful works put out by Zachtronics) and the difficulty in Snakebird is beyond anything I could have imagined. I note that most of the positive reviews here have less than 2 hours of play time and if I'd done a write up after the first dozen puzzles this review would likely be amongst them, however I don't review games unless I've finished them or have lost interest. Sadly, Snakebird falls into the latter category.

It's not a bad game, but when I'm railroaded into solving a puzzle from one particular angle, despite the illusion of a plurality of angles being available, I feel that the game is not being fair.

I'm actually on the fence about this one, cause the game is so hard. But it has such hilarious visuals that I just can't frown at it.

So, should a game be rated down because it's "too hard"? Well, generally I'd say no. But this game doesn't give you much help in learning the ropes. The developers must have assumed that the extremely simple base mechanic meant that nobody would need help mastering it, I guess.

After a couple of moderately challenging levels you come to puzzles that are mind-bendingly difficult, with only one solution, that seem impossible. Who knows, they could be impossible, I couldn't figure them out. It isn't so thrilling that I wanted to spend the time to figure them out, either.

But maybe that's what you need. Maybe you need to meet the mind-bendingly challenging puzzle, and prove that you're able to bend your mind that far.

So, I recommend this game to you, masochists and geniuses. And, anyone who just loves the idea of a birdsnake... those things are hilarious.

This game is basically like a cute puppy dog hitting you over the head with a chair.

The visuals make you expect a little game even a 5th grader could beat in a day. But in reality this game could probably make even Stephen Hawking feel dumb. The difficulty curve is less of a curve, but rather a difficulty cliff you desperately try to climb. And even though this description makes it sound like a torture device it is actually a really fun game.

It is a genuine challenge, It makes you think a lot harder than most games these days. And that's what makes this game so good. The mechanics are simple to understand, but take a lot of brains and patience to master.

Additonally to the gameplay and the simplistic, yet adorable, graphics the soundtrack is very calming and complements the game really well (which is perfectly fitting because otherwise I'd probably have punched a hole into my wall by now...)

Overall a neat and challenging game that I can definitely recommed you getting.

This is a very simple but punishingly difficult puzzle game. It starts off fairly straightforward, but progresses rapidly to seemingly impossible situations. I am sure there are subtleties to the movement that I have not yet learned, but as it stands, I've actually reached a wall where I can't solve any of the available puzzles. Still, the game is extremely well crafted. The graphics are colorful and crisp, the audio is unobtrusive, and the controls are as simple as it gets. All you have to do is have your little snakebirds eat all the fruit and make it safely to the rainbow portal. But good GOD it's hard. Luckily, it's the kind of hard that, when you figure it out, it's immensely satisfying. So, if you're in the mood for an extreme puzzle challenge (honestly, this is probably the hardest puzzle game I've played on Steam), this is your game.
