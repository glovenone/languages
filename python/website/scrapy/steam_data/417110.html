I'm sitting in the dark, it's 3.30am and I'm sobbing like my pet cat just died...

This game...is an experience.

The translation is poor, but it does improve as the game goes on - which is good since all the philosophical conversations are in the later part of the game and they are mostly intelligible. Despite this, I found certain passages to be oddly beautiful and well-written, even if the english translation isn't quite up to scratch.

The characters are excellent. I love the little complexities and flaws, particularly in Yu-Qing's character. The cast is varied and even the side-characters have a lot going on below the surface.

I didn't find the game particularly overbearing with its moral - it considers various viewpoints and its treatment of discrimination is complex and detailed. It's not a simple case of good v. bad.

That said, I am a little confused about the excessive gore. I looked up the may riots while playing the game, and I didn't see anything which suggested the kind of mass mutilation and carnage the game depicts. My information is coming from Wikipedia and a few other sites, so please correct me if I'm wrong, but most of the violence was directed against property rather than people, and the majority of deaths were those of rioters trapped in the buildings they torched?
I understand the need to dramatise the story, but the extreme violence and the demonisation of the rioters seems to work against it. I'm not squeamish, I just didn't get why such a level of violence was necessary, particularly since the writing is so competant. Again, I may be wrong, and if so I apologise.

The art is lovely, the facial expressions in particular. I also liked the stylised artworks which appeared later on (even if I thought the gore was over the top, the artwork was, for the most part, tasteful).
I really liked the second route and how we get a second perspective, if anything I preferred it. It felt like most of the 'meat' of the story was in this second section. Also I'm glad we see more of Melati. I didn't really feel much when she died in the first route because she didn't get much screen-time, but then seeing more of her personality and knowing how things end...The developers are monsters.

In short, trauma, excessive gore, philosophy and feels.

If you get this game for free, really cheap, or get gifted it by a friend then it's probably worth playing, but as it stands I would not reccomend paying full price for it.

-There are many spelling and grammatical errors with the translation, I often find myself having a hard time understanding what's being said.
-The game is very short, it only took me about 4 hours including a lot of time spent messing around and accidentally leaving the game idle.
-It comes off very preachy and pretentious about the message it's trying to convey. Of course I'm an ignorant American, so the culture is pretty much lost on me, but most of the characters seem overly racist just to shove the "don't be racist" message down your throat.
- The characters' relationships either aren't explored much or just don't make any sense, I honestly don't know why the main character is friends with Zi-Jin, that's not to say that they shouldn't be friends, but their friendship/history just isn't explained very well. Maybe it's just me
-Some scenes tend to drag on for quite a while unnecessarily, mainly the first cafeteria scene comes to mind.
-The sound effects are always either bland or unfitting, and the ambience tends to abruplty stop and then loop, of course this isn't a big complaint, just thought I would mention it.

The game has good artwork, the voice acting sounds great, and while the majority of the game was mainly boring, it wasn't too bad and I've definitely played a lot worse.

Bottom line, if you want it for the history/satire aspect of the game, go for it, I'm sure you know a lot more than me. But don't get suckered in by the cute lolis and somewhat misleading trailer.
