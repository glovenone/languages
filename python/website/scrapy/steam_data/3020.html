About This Game

Call of Juarez is an epic adventure western themed FPS game. The player alternately assumes the roles of two distinct, antagonistic characters: a sneaking fugitive Billy and his hunter the reverend Ray. Apart from the highly interesting, emotional and psychological aspect of the link between the two characters, the player will experience a variety of gameplay with many differences whilst playing a particular character.
The game features fast paced revolver duels using historically accurate firearms, horseback riding, mounted combat and stealth actions in memorable locations set after Western movies. Emergent gameplay based on accurate simulation of not only rigid bodies and rag-dolls but also liquids, gases, fire and smoke makes the gameplay innovative and non-linear.
  • Very serious approach to the Wild West theme and detailed reconstruction of the character and visuals of that times
  • The use of physics and innovative simulation of fire, liquids, dust, smokes and gases to construct emergent gameplay
  • State of the art graphics making use of the top technology graphics cards features on todays cards.
  • Playing 2 distinct characters with different abilities and different gameplay
  • Horseback riding and saddle shooting, gunfighter duels
  • Using the whip to fight, move objects and move the player's character
  • Legendary weapons form the Gold Fever era and full akimbo mode for the hands on approach.
  • Exhilarating storyline with full of twists and turns in this exciting Western Genre.

Call of Juarez, formerly known as The Lawman is an western first-person shooter developed by Techland and published by Ubisoft in 2006-2007.

In Call of Juarez you play two sides of the coin, on one side you play as Billy Candle and on the other as Reverend Ray. Billy Candle is an half-white, half-Mexican in search for the legendary lost gold of Juarez, a treasure hidden somewhere inside this city. When Billy is not capable of finding this treasure, he decides to go home to pay a visit to his mother and stepfather at the farm. On arrival, Billy is confronted with the death of his parents, murdered. The other character, Reverend Ray is Billy's step uncle and preacher. Ray is an reformed revolver hero who is dedicated to spreading the tales of The Lord, but when he hears about the commotion at the farm of his brother, he is going to pay a visit as well and sees Billy being panicked and Billy runs away from the dead bodies. Ray thinks Billy is responsible and promises Revenge and start hunting down Billy as long as he can.

During the storyline, the game switches from character now and then. The game knows several features such as akimbo (wearing double weapons), sneaking, climbing by rope, revolver duels and bullet-time where you have to time and mark your shots at your enemies in order to finish them off quickly in the end. You also have to deal with several types of enemies like bandits, Apache Indians, Mexicans and several landlords.

I really like this game because of the western atmosphere, the smooth switch between characters that initiate a story cliffhanger at the end of each chapter, the wild gunpowder action, voice acting, traveling by foot or horse and the vast landscapes from the rocky mountains, lurid towns and barren tundras filled with various wild animals.

I highly recommend this game to people who like western first-person shooters with a very good storyline and large areas, certainly when the game is on sale!

I'm crouched, hiding in a bush. I see my enemy, but he doesn't see me, yet. I draw my bow back, slow my breathing, aim center mass, & let the arrow fly. He goes down with a groan. The Ugly
Having to pick up boxes and drop them in order to climb up/over things is a pain which looks and feels clunky. When you quick save, everything freezes for like 10 seconds (gameplay, music, SFX), which kind of detracts from the immersive first person environment.

This review is a little lengthy, so if you just want a quick summary of why I recommend Call of Juarez skip the Results and read from there.

Techland starts the series with the epic western mystery action adventure. Call of Jaurez draws you in to a world of hardship and torment that plagues the old west. In particularly how racism and corruption of the older days impacted the life of Billy Candle, a young man.

Story:
Call of Juarez is a Western style action game. The story is incredibly interesting, but honestly takes a while to get into. Call of Juarez is a western mystery surrounding the story of the gold of Juarez. The story surrounds the protagonist Billy Candle, a boy without a father, from the little town of Hope (an ironic name). Billy ends up being caught up in a mystery of whether he may or may not have murdered his parents. The death of Billy’s parents and the prejudice of the Hope forces Billy to go on the lamb. Reverend Ray, brother to Billy’s mother’s lover, hunts Billy for this crime. In order to start a new life Billy is set for Juarez, but will Reverend Ray catch him?

Campaign:
In the campaign you play both Billy and Ray throughout. Billy as he is on the lamb and the Reverend as he hunts him down. There are fifteen chapters to the game, and they only become longer as you progress further. Each chapter begins with a dialogue read either by Billy or Ray. The story is rather gripping, but I didn't start really caring much about the characters until about Chapter VII. The game has some levels that are incredibly long, while others than are very short. I would recommend saving a lot during the game as for me the Auto-Save feature did not work. What is most interesting is how the levels are designed; some of them are essentially open world while others are pathways. For an older game, 2006, the levels can be quite expansive and rather innovative. Some of these levels will require you to solve puzzles, other will require you to complete tasks, but most the time you will just be running and gunning like a madman.

Gameplay:
Once you’ve mastered the basic mechanics of the game there is not much more too it. Billy Candle should always use the Bow and Arrow as it causes time to slow for him. While Reverend Ray should always quick draw as it causes time to slow for him. There are many different types of weapons, but you will notice that as the game progresses they are all essentially the same and pistols with quick reload are always the best bet. Rifles win over shotguns, because you will never be close to enemies that need a big dose of Lead (Pb). And on the incredibly rare occasion you find one, a scoped rifle is the best gun. The weapons break over time, so you will be constantly throwing away your guns and essentially selecting the same ones. The movement is rather clunky, but for a 2006 title it does a good job. If you are interested in getting into Call of Juarez series (although I have not played Bound in Blood) Gunslinger is a completely different game with no references to the original. So you could start wherever you want and just work your way back to this one.

Hints:
>Despite there being many interesting and cool guns, they all do the same basic damage and essentially there is no reason to use anything other than the Quick Reload weapons and the Bow.
>Just remember with Reverend Ray you can always quick draw, just put away your guns, wait a few moments, and then right click your mouse.

Difficulty Problems:
I beat this game on Medium Difficulty, but I would say I was really upset I couldn't play it on Hard Difficulty. I wanted to, but the first episode where you had to run away from the rain of bullets was essentially impossible, so because of that one annoying part I had to play the whole game on medium.

Results:
If you like Westerns, you can get past the mechanics/graphics of older generation games, and are interested in an epic adventure then this is the game for you. The game presents a lot of challenges, mystery, character development, open world environments, and combat. The game was incredibly easy and I never was really posed with a single challenge, although a lack of challenge just makes the game seem like a 80s action movie. Call of Jaurez is a fun game coupled with a fantastic campaign. I recommend Call of Jaurez, especially if you can get it for a good sale.

Pros:
+Interesting story, characters and level design.
+Nice variety of weapons (despite you only ever really using one type of them)
+No limit to “Quick Draw” Ability
+A long campaign that requires a variety of tactics and battle types.
+Being able to swing with your whip from tree to tree.

Cons:
-My game glitched making me unable to finish the final scene, but you can easily watch it online (despite it not being as gratifying as completing it yourself).
-The game can take a while to load even though its an old game.
-The dialogue at the beginning of each episode is read incredibly slow
-The guns lose duration and break, which is incredibly annoying as some weapons, like the scoped rifle, are near impossible to find.
-Some of the levels are so big you find yourself running between zones for a couple minutes with no enemies.

Call of Juarez is one of those games already fading from memory. Solid shooting mechanics mix with poor level design while potentially interesting protagonists are bludgeoned with ho-hum enemies. Sadly some of the game's signature features, such as the slow-motion focus shooting, are hugely overused and actually drag the game down.Call of Juarez's plot unfolds from each of two character perspectives. The first, Billy, is a worthless do-nothing that finds himself wandering back home after years of searching for the mythical treasure of Juarez.He's an entirely annoying character in spirit and nearly all of the levels designed for him involve some sort of sneaking or rock climbing expedition, which only compounds the issue.he second character, Reverend Ray, is a preacher with a dark past that has actively been seeking redemption through prayer and church. When his brother is killed, supposedly by Billy, his more violent side pushes to the front again as he begins the chase for righteous vengeance across the wild, slaughtering any wicked men in his way. The violent road eventually leads to unfortunate consequences as Revered Ray's convictions and faith are turned upside down. Both characters are given skills to help differentiate their styles of play, Ray being the run and gun and Billy being the sneak about, but the techniques aren't necessarily welcome.One of the game's more interesting features, called concentration mode, also ends up being one of the game's worst enemies. When activated, time will slow down for Ray after drawing his guns. The recharge time on Ray's ability is too fast, allowing you to simply wait and use it over and over. Having a special ability like this should actually be special rather than standard.While the special focus ability is optional, the levels, unfortunately, are not. The corridor shooter sections with Reverend Ray running point aren't necessarily all bad. Running and gunning through an Old West town is fun. But corridor shooting can only get you so far these days. Even worse are the levels with jumping puzzles and whip swinging. Visually Call of Juarez is decent enough though it won't win any awards. It looked pretty good when it was originally announced, but these days it's mid-range at best. Character models, faces particularly, are the best the game has to offer. Environments are blocky and offer up some strange texturing creating passable play areas. Multiplayer also has its moments, though most of them are more cheap thrills than deep gaming. Call of Juarez might appeal to you, but it's not the Wild West fun I was hoping for.

This game is nearly ten years old, I'm not going to lie to you, it's aged. Not in a good way like a wine either.

The Good:
The first person shooter mechanics work well, though they do feel like an older style. This game tends to be "run and gun", think Quake, but perhaps a little more tactical. The enemy AI don't exactly outwit you, although, do they ever in a first person shooter? The combat is surprisingly fun, and the bullets feel surprisingly real. You get shot in the head with a rifle in real life, chances are you're ♥♥♥♥ed. You get shot in the head with a rifle in Call of Juarez? Chances are... you're ♥♥♥♥ed. Don't get me wrong though, this is no Dark Souls. The combat can be challenging (at least I found it so on normal difficulty), but you're not going to be repeating fights over and over again.

The story on offer whilst not ground-breaking is what any Western fan would like. You've got fabelled treasure, Mexicans, a Native American who offers spiritual guidance, and of course vengance.

The Bad:
The voice acting in this game is average as hell. In particular the main character sounded so false I'm convinced that I myself could portray a ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ Mexican better. The Preacher's voice on the otherhand was pretty bad ♥♥♥, so not too many points lost here.

The Ugly:
The graphics like the rest of the game are close to 10 years old. They're pretty gross and the textures lack diversity. That being said, you probably can't find any other game of this age that has aged any better. The graphics are still at a level where gameplay isn't a blurred mess, all the main characters look distinct and that's good enough for me!

Conclusion:
Do you love Westerns? Do you like feeling love you're a cowboy? Can you tolerate a dated game?
If you're answer is yes to these, this games worth picking up.
Do you love high res textures? Do you love high quality voiceacting? Do you hate dated mechanics?
If you're answer is yes to these, don't bother. Get down to gamestop for the latest and greatest Assassins of Duty III - Escape to Africa :^)
