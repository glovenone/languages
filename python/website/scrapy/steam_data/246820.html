Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Jeklynn Heights is an entirely self-funded game made by a couple of friends who work remotely. When development first started in 2010, we chose to use UDK (Unreal Development Kit). Creating a multiplayer game in a very atmospheric world was quite the ambition for our small team, so naturally between school and work the game took many years to reach its current point.

By that time, Unreal Engine 4 was released. After several discussions, the best move was for us to stay on our current track with UDK. It would have been a sizable job to move all of our assets to UE4. We desperately want to take this game to UE4, and if our game does well on Early Access that is our #1 priority.

The Early access build is a glimpse of what our game could be with more funding, a newer engine, and a larger team. We’ve worked extremely hard to make sure the experience you have with our Early Access build is one that’s enjoyable, thought out, and re-playable.

Lastly, we deeply value community driven support. Great games are made when developers and players collaborate. Early Access allows us to get streamlined feedback on what we’re doing, and ways we can improve moving forward. We will be active and responsive both in-game and on our discussion forums when it comes to issues, feedback, and design ideas.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Approximately 1 year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Our first priority is moving to Unreal Engine 4. This will technically give us the capability to advance core systems including improved User Interface, Matchmaking, Statistics, and do a total upgrade of all of our art/level assets. Most of our artwork was made years ago when we were first starting out. Being able to re-do core artwork will enable us to optimize performance moving forward.

The Early Access version is a snapshot of what this game could be with more resources. A full version would include more characters, maps, events, and team/clan oriented systems. What we currently lack in content, we make up for in attention to detail, quality, and an eagerness to make meaningful improvements based on community feedback.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“- 5v5 matched based multiplayer fun!
- 3rd person combat system that features melee, blocking, dashing, ranged weapons, and abilities
- 3 playable characters with unique skills and play styles.
- 9 game events, randomized every match.
- Atmospheric fairy tale map called Conflicted Caverns.
- 12 original music tracks
- Dedicated Servers in US East, US Central, and Europe. (Will open more based on demand!)
- Front-end chat system, in-game chat system, voice work, custom key-binding.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“You will not need to purchase the game again beyond Early Access if you buy now. You guys are the pioneers that allow us the opportunity to develop this game further. As we expand this title, we will increase the base price of the game (for anyone who hasn’t bought the game yet), but we will not mandate paid expansions/updates in order to continue playing.

Our focus is making a beautiful, fun, and engaging multiplayer game – plain and simple!”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We want the community heavily involved in shaping the direction of this title. Our first steps will be ironing out any major bugs or issues right after our Early Access launch. As we make our transition into Unreal Engine 4, we’ll spend that time assessing what worked, what didn’t work, and map out a game plan with our community. This includes determining what objectives to keep, maps to add, and character ideas to implement.

As our competitive scene grows, we’ll work with players on establishing a functional and streamlined match making system, clan support, and tournaments. For the past year, we have held regular testing sessions with a closed group of supporters. We have operated in a very transparent fashion, and will continue to do so during Early Access.”

Patch 1.0.3 - June 3rd, 2016

Testing will be this weekend starting at 2 EST!

- Based on popular feedback, you now have the option to switch teams and characters during the game. In order to do so, you must go to the main structure in your respawn area and press 'H'. About This Game

Synopsis

A corrupt and bizarre town is the quirky setting to Jeklynn Heights, a fantastical and competitive online game. Run by a Mayor with a split personality and enigmatic past, a spell haunts the town he rules. Players must complete objectives, directed by the Mayor, to win and escape the town. Combat is 3rd person melee, ranged, and ability based.

Match Setup

There are two teams in Jeklynn Heights - the rich and the poor. The objective is simple; leave town! During the beginning of the match, the Mayor introduces himself and calls out the first event. Events are mini-objectives which last a few minutes each. Objectives range from capturing points, killing targets, stealing from the other team, collecting resources, etc. The first team to win 5 events wins the match, and is allowed to leave the town!

The game is designed to be fast paced, focusing on team-based play, high action, and very short respawn times.

Combat Mechanics

Melee/Ranged Combat
Every character has a 3 swing melee rotation and a blocking ability. You may hold your blocking stance for 2 seconds to deflect incoming melee damage. Landing a quick block (blocking a melee attack within 0.5 seconds of using your block), will stun the enemy player and grant you an empowered swing that does bonus damage.

All characters also have a dash mechanic. Dashing propels you a short distance forward and grants you an empowered swing that does bonus damage. Dashing is a great opportunity to travel faster, initiate combat, or narrowly escape danger.

Additionally, you also have a ranged weapon unique to your character to make picking off fleeing enemies easier!

Abilities/Spells
Characters have 4 abilities centered around their specific archetype (tank/support/agility based). Abilities range from AOE damage, skill shots, disables, movement boosts, and buffs.

Mayor Events

Events are randomized every match. Both teams are competing over the same event. The first team to win 5 events gets to leave Jeklynn Heights!


The Mines

The mayor needs resources to help increase the appeal of his town. Teams rush to the caves to collect ore. The first team to get 20 ore wins the event.

Sanity Post

A new structure has been built in the middle of the map, but which side should it belong to? Teams fight to control the area.

Sanity Orb Tower

Each team has an Orb Tower located in their base. Infiltrate the enemy base, destroy the Orb Tower, and return the Orb back to your base.

King Me!

One random player on each team is now the King! They grow in size, and are slightly more powerful. Kill the enemy king while protecting your own.

Murderer on the Loose!

A deadly crime has been committed! Work with your team to capture clues throughout the map. Once all clues have been uncovered, a murderer will be revealed on the opposite team. Eliminate them!

Escort the Zeppelin

The Mayor has spawned a Zeppelin filled with explosives in the middle of the map. One team must escort the Zeppelin to the enemy base while the other prevents this from occurring!

Loot

One team has stolen a bunch of paintings. If that's your team, defend the enemy team from looting and returning them to their base.

Rescue the Inventor

One team has captured the Inventor of Jeklynn Heights. They must protect the cage from being destroyed, while the other team attempts to free the Inventor.

Brawl

In the event of a tie, a Brawl will take place between the two teams. The Mayor transports all players to a new location to begin their fight. If you die, you don't respawn. Last player standing wins it for their team!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP
    • Processor: Core 2 Duo 2.4 GHz or Althon 64 X2 2.7 GHz
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: ATI Radeon HD 3870 or higher, Nvidia GeForce 8800 GT or higher
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Storage: 3 GB available space
    • Sound Card: Installed

Early Access Review
Posted: 28 May
Jeklynn Heights has a very "burtonesque" theme that is both charming and creepy at the same time, kind of like Alice in Wonderland. And it's not just reflected by the environment, but also by the characters you get to play as.

I like to play as Benny the barber most of the time, trying to find my next "client" who needs more than just their hair cut :p

Although the game currently only has one map, the gameplay is actually quite dynamic and varied as there are different goals to pursue that are given out by the mayor. There are multiple quests to complete per match, and each one plays differently.

For example, the mayor could ask you to steal paintings from the rich and bring them to the slum part of town. So the rich team has to defend their valuables, and the poor have to grab the loot and run home. There's also a "king of the hill" mode, where you and your team must hold the center of town for a period of time in order to score. Then there's capture the flag, payload, and other types of quests.

Now this may seem a bit odd to throw all these game modes into a single match, but it works surprisingly well because the narrative of the mayor holds everything together, so that all these little quests turn into a story about how the poor managed to overcome the rich, or vice versa.

Overall, I feel like Jeklynn Heights is a rough diamond that primarily needs some more work put into the technical aspects as well as more content (new characters and quests). But for now, it is a good start with a strong theme and direction, and I'm excited to see how everything unfolds.

Time to plunge down the rabbit hole and into Jeklynn Heights, as I'm sure YOU also need more than just a haircut!

I'm excited to see this title finally released, even if it's just early access! I've been following this game for a long long time and was lucky enough to beta test it the last month. I'm a huge fan of Savage 2, a now dead game, and I've been searching for a game with a similar combat system for far too long without success. Jeklynn Heights promises to deliver!

The developers are as friendly as can be, really dedicated and open to suggestions. The game is still in its early stages, but this also means great potential, the joy of seeing a game grow and the chance to affect the development by jumping on the early access train.

The combat is where it's at. While unpolished in its current state, the idea is what I love. A chain of different attacks with the possibility to block enemy attacks coming at you. Time your moves according to your enemy and you win the fight! With the different abilities and stats for each character, it makes for very interactive and direct fights and you feel you have control of what is happening. If you lose, it's on you!

The art style is also something I definitely like about this game. It has this bizarre vibe going on without exagerrating much. All the crazy things going on are just "normal". And the mad mayor's voice guiding you through the game is a great touch.

My main concern about the game right now is the playerbase. This is a multiplayer game and without players to fill a server, the experience is not quite enjoyable. There's an attempt to gather players at a specific time going on at the steam discussion page, 3 EST and 9 EST apparently, so I hope this helps.

I look forward to the future of this game!

Early Access Review
Posted: 25 May
Jeklynn Heights is a match-based multiplayer game. Joining either the Rich or the Poor, your goal is to win matches in an event-driven battle arena.

Already great are the mesmerizing style and gameplay core. The dark fairy tale style is mirrored well in a detailed map, bizarre characters, atmospheric sounds and lore-fitting events. Thanks to shorter matches, this game can be easily played even when life is busy. Cycling events keep up the interest as well as the active combat system. Melee-oriented with ranged as support and ability-based, the outcome of the game heavily depends on your own skills, priority settings and team play.

What you might miss at the current state are more contents, technical optimizations (this game is nothing for low-spec PCs and Windows only) and top-notch visuals covering overall graphics, better animations, UI and interfaces. Those issues are known and will be countered in the future. It’s planned to switch to a new engine, work on side-specific classes, more events and maps, better visuals, combat tweaks and fitting suggestions pushed forward by the community; not to forget matchmakings for competitive scenes.

The basics are promising, the lore allows lots of potential and creative processes and most importantly the communication between players and developers runs smooth. Bugs and errors were fixed fast and devs are open for logical and feedback-driven suggestions.

If you are unsure whether to buy or not, my recommendations are:
Wait with a buy if you expect a perfectly finished game with top-notch visuals. Keep in mind hitherto existing contents are functional and fun but limited.
Buy if you want to play for the sake of having chaotic fun, like to help developing or want to support this game growing. So far, I enjoy Jeklynn Heights very much. Got the game for free as a test player and staying for the overall good atmosphere and thrilled matches in-game. If you are up to that, I highly recommend joining.
