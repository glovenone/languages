About This Game

Play as Billy, fighting your way through the randomly generated halls of an old, abandoned mansion! Fight through the halls, find and collect loot in the side rooms, buy from shops, and battle exciting bosses at the end of each set of hallways. And when you’re ready, unlock the basements to completely change the game with whole new challenges, enemies, bosses, and loot!

  • More than 120 unique weapons to use, spanning over 8 weapon classes: Sword, dagger, axe, war axe, spear, magic, ranged, and gun.
  • Over 610,000 thousand costume combinations to discover and wear on your head, body, arms, and legs. Each set adds unique bonuses to your character.
  • Earn points to unlock weapons, difficulties, and completely new areas to explore.
  • Fight challenging bosses along your way through the randomly generated hallways.
  • Purchase new items, costumes, mana, and hearts at the shops along the way.

This key was obtained from the developer for the purposes of review.

Target Audience: Those looking for a real simple time waster.

Video Review: https://youtu.be/MbPF1HLARv4

Summary:
A cheap is great and all, but it comes down to an opportunity cost. When there's so many games on Steam, you need something unique to sell your game over others, and the Haunting of Billy has none of that. While the game's functional and can do the job in terms of a simple time waster, those looking for any sort of detailed or engaging gameplay are looking in the wrong place. The repetitive nature of combat got boring very quickly, as you're doing the same thing over and over again, even after 2 hours of gameplay. The game allows for higher resolution, but doesn't adjust certain elements, like text boxes and information, appropriately. Honestly, I'm struggling to finish this summary: the fact is, the game is a form of whack a monster: hit them with a melee weapon over and over again while trying to avoid damage, and exploiting the AI to do that. There's better games at a 5 dollar or less price point, even when you aren't looking for a sale title.


Lists:
Positives:
  • It's functional. Combat and controls work.
  • Premise the game has in the haunted house could have been interesting.
  • Can burn through time.

Negatives:
  • Boring. Combat gets repetitive quickly, and without a lot of variety to show for it.
  • Game doesn't teach you some things that you need to know.
  • Wasted story presence in the store page. Doesn't use it at all.
  • Doesn't take advantage of higher resolutions. Font is still the same size, making me squint, trying to figure out what's going on.
  • Aesthetic doesn't do well to tell the story the game has to tell. Creatures don't necessarily look like their counterparts (the mummy for example).
  • Font used can be confusing at times.

First impression: Game is a 2D side-scroller that looks like a simple platformer but plays more like a NES era beat-em-up with rogue-like elements. The weapon and costume combinations are a fun twist that gives the game replayability, it was neat exploring some of the options available and getting a new piece to wear was always exciting.

The game is certainly unique so if you're a fan of rogue-likes (Risk of Rain, Binding of Isaac, etc) or clasic side-scrollers (Ghosts & Goblins, Friday the 13th, etc) then this game may be worth checking out.

Pros:
  • Procedurally generated levels make the game feel fresh (new enemies, bosses, items, etc).
  • Art style has a nice retro feel to it.
  • Costume and weapon combinations provide a bit of strategy to the game.
  • Reasonable price for the amount of content, seems like there's quite a few items and game modes to unlock.

    Cons:
  • Game can be a bit cryptic at times. There may be a few things you weren't taught that can lead to some cheap deaths (but it might just be part of the learning curve).
  • Much like the classic NES era of games Haunting of Billy harkens back to there isn't much in terms of story.
  • Difficulty is on the higher end, like most rogue-likes.

    Overall, a fun little game from an indie developer that's worth giving a shot.



    NOTE: I was gifted a copy of the game to review by the developer.

Didn't think I'd end up spending as much time playing this game as I have... It's simple but challenging, I keep making it to the second last boss then dying. you really need to think out the custome pieces and weapons you bring to boss fights. Game allows for lots of player preferences, at first I liked using swords and axes but with more skill definitely prefer daggers now. Cons of the game are the fact that you lose your save if you die, but I guess that is part of the challenge. Also, since the game is randomly generated it makes each playthrough unique BUT you can get unlucky with the type/number of monsters that spawn and get wrecked... especially in the basements. Overall I would highly recommend this game.

tip: hoard mummy costume pieces (extra hp) for the first 2 bosses, but do NOT try to fight the 3rd without a bug costume piece (extra speed). Very frustrating if you do not, I died many many times before learning this.
