About This Game



Lightbender is a bright, colorful, and fun puzzle game based on the physics of light. Hit the targets by manipulating beams of different colors. Use prisms to split the lights, mirrors to bend the lights, lenses to combine the lights, and dark matter to invert the lights!

Play 240 puzzles with 5 difficulty levels!

You can even build your own puzzles! Challenge your friends by sharing your levels.


Place a mirror on the grid to bend the light. You can rotate mirrors to redirect the beams toward the goal.



Prisms split lights into their components. Hit multiple targets by breaking one beam of light into multiple colors.

White becomes Red, Green, and Blue.
Yellow becomes Red and Green.
Magenta becomes Red and Blue.
Cyan becomes Green and Blue.



Lenses combine lights to produce a new color. Place them on the grid to transform two or three beams of light into one new color. Think of them as the opposite of prisms.



Dark Matter turns a beam of light into the opposite color.
Red becomes Cyan and vice versa
Green becomes Magenta and vice versa
Blue becomes Yellow and vice versa

As the levels progress in difficulty, you'll have to use all of these tools in combination with one another to hit all of the targets!



Think you've got the skills to outwit your friends? Create your own levels using one or all of the tools listed above. Submit your levels to the Lightbender database and your work could end up featured in a future (FREE) DLC level pack! The top user-created levels will be featured as DLC levels for everyone to download and play.

In the meantime, share your levels instantly with friends and family. You can also save your levels on your device's Lightbender account for free play anytime.

Short version:
4/5. If you're a puzzle collector, get this. The simple interface and simple concept but challenging puzzles make for a nice, approachable game.

Long version:
Lightbender is a good way to spend 10 minutes or 2 hours, solving incredibly mind-bending puzzles. Such a simple concept, just mirror, reverse, break or combine light; and yet it is very challenging. Sometimes you'll solve the puzzles by accident when you're trying out something new, and that's not necessarily a bad thing. It'll teach you to think. There are also user-created maps, as well as a whole plethora of maps on the developers' part, so you won't be speedrunning this game.

The critical things that I would have to say would be that the music could be a bit better-ish. It's not bad but it can start to irritate you after repeating over a same puzzle for a long time. Fortunately, as always, you can turn it off in the options if you don't like it. Other than that, I can't really think of many bad things to say. If the game continues to be what it has been so far, it should be a nice, small little puzzle-game. To be honest, it might not be one of the most memorable ones because it doesn't seem to have a plot of any kind, but it's overall very tidy and nice game.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lu8N0XCAsrg

I had a lot of fun on Lightbender and there are a lot of puzzles ranging from very easy to very difficult. You have to get the light to change to the correct point whilst changing colours if necessary. There are also a lot of rules behind the game, as to what changes colours and what mirrors can move. In fact it’s probably wise to have a notepad with what happens to which colours and what makes it change to what colour. This can make the game a little scary. I just played the game blind and got pretty far and enjoyed what I played. I’m not the smartest of people though so I did get stuck quite often. Here are the positives and negatives:

Positives :
+ Great audio
+ lots of content
+ really good price
+ Level editor
+ Graphics are really good with the lighting effects
+ Gamepad Support

Negatives:
- No Audio sliders
- No Windowed Support
- No Mouse Support
- Lots of rules to colour changing which can be hard to memorise

Changes:
- No Audio sliders (Audio sliders have been added 20th December)

I personally would recommend this to people who enjoy puzzle games and those who enjoy light and colour changing. The music really adds to the game and I often find myself nodding and tapping my foot whilst trying to find out a solution. If you are slightly leaning towards purchasing, I would go and purchase it myself as for the price you really cannot lose (much.)

I would not recommend if you are not very good at mixing colours or if you dislike keeping lots of notes down about colours. I would also not recommend to people who want to use their mouse as the game has no mouse support but keyboard and gamepad.

Overall, I feel for the price it is worth a purchase.
