About This Game

Wind of Luck: Arena is a session-based team multiplayer online game dedicated to naval battles of the age of sail warships. It's a unique opportunity to feel like a true pirate captain in the midst of a furious seafight offensive!
The game features team vs team battles, which allows you to fight against random opponents, honing different battle skills, equip your ship with various types of armament, apply different improvements, etc.
Battles will take place in truly special locations from tropical seas to the Arctic offering you a good spectacle. Be a part of naval battles and become an infamous captain of seven seas!

Currently, I can not suggest this game.

There is virtually no community.

The game is still under development.
Examples: No crew visible on ships. Weapons rebalancing in process. Abilities rebalancing in process. Equipment rebalancing in process. Ship resizing in process.

Everything about this game is being worked on and changed. While that is a good thing, it is not a finished product yet, and there is no good reason to give them money for their premium time and ships.

The sound is.. off. I do not believe that they have sourced real cannons, for example.

Visually, the game is bad. Worse than the already quite aged Pirates of the Burning Sea. The seas are completely flat, even in the one map where it is storming. The boats look like they were made by somebody in Second Life. Despite this, it has some high graphics requirements. There's some reflection effect which simply borks constantly on my computer. There is NO REASON for the game to have these high requirements and look so bad.

The loading times are awful. It often takes a number of minutes for the game to transition from a match back to your shipyard, or for your battle report (and thus, your rewards) to get to you.

The ship selection is odd as well. There's a number of nice ships but they are forced to fight in their own weight class via tier limiting. Meaning no pirates are going to be sailing fast ships to out maneuver the heavy ships. Your fast pirate ships are always going to be engaging in nimble navy battles or quick, small merchant or fishing vessel. You're NEVER going to bring your light frigate up against a huge carrack, sink it, steal its loot (which you can do) and escape before the faster Man-O-War catches up to you and your friends.

Even if you could, a lot of stuff in the game is in the micro transaction area. There's a lot of secondary gear you can equip, but there's no differences between them besides their premium ammunition effect... Which the AI players always have access to. Want to drop some explosive barrels to mine up the water where that Man-O-War will be sailing as it tries to catch you? Better fork over some cash.

Which reminds me. You are often stuck in matches where it is only you and one other person.. or maybe just you, and everyone else is an AI.

I have more gripes, and the game is fun once you get in to it a decent bit.. so, since it's free, give it a whirl, but as it is, there is no way they can justify asking for money for ANYTHING in the game. Which they do. And they make sure you have to deal with that premium stuff on a regular basis. So, want to compete? Better pay up!

Wind of Luck: Arena lets you have your own flotilla. You take a ship and then you head out to battle other players in 9 vs 9 round or 1 vs 1. You have to sail it by deploying the stems (if I'm not mistaken with the words). You'll have cannons or catapults or any other trick to destroy your enemy. But pay attention! You can inflict damage to your team by raming into them or to your boat if you have a tendancy to run into rocks.

The game seems to be very interesting for anyone loving that kind of game. You can buy better ships but for that, you need to win battles and to level up. You can also upgrade your flotilla. On Steam, you have also achievements.

However, be aware that you need to set up an account to play, that you can't delete like that - as you need to ask the team to do it. I couldn't get in the game also as soon as I've created an account - servers were unavailable.

And unfortunately with an AZERTY keyboard, it's kinda hard to direct your boat (even if I can set it to QWERTY but then, it wouldn't solve my lack of coordination when S isn't used to get back). Also, I'm not into knots or speed winds or anything else, which means that the game is hard in itself for me.

But the graphics are very nice, with some good effects. The soundtrack is good too.

Wind of Luck is free with DLC, yeah. For anyone loving naval battles with many parameters to take into account, it will be an heaven. For the others, well, try it and make your own opinion.
