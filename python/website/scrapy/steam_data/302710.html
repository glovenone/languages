About This Game

BlazeRush is a dynamic arcade racing survival game with no health, no levelling and no brakes!
You can assemble a team of friends to play in local or online multiplayer, choose a car to your taste and chase, blow up and cut off!
Adapt your style and tactics to new enemies, obstacles and tracks.

Welcome to the World Of Racing and Frantic Fun!

key Features:

  • Simple multiplayer. Up to 4 players locally and up to 8 players over the Internet;
  • Couch vs. couch. Start races with local players;
  • Connect to the game at any time;
  • Dozens of fun tracks on three planets, 16 cars and a variety of cool guns;
  • Lots of medals for various tricks and antics.

Great party racer with simple controls and a diverse selection of fun vehicles, even if you will probably only end up using one of the last four you unlock ever after. The physics on the vehicle suspensions and responsive steering makes them fun and direct to control. The nuance of each vehicle is in how it drifts or steers and each style can be used to good effect in the right hands.

Good spread of bot AI, an entertaining mix of weapons and booster types, plenty of tracks of various difficulty, and three game modes, all of which can be toggled by the host to keep the game mixed up or to just play your favorites. The Death Race mode quickly became a house favorite here.

All in all, a fun updated remix of Mario Kart and R.C. Pro Am for NES. Great for screaming at friends, but quick and fun enough that it doesn't ever really turn to bitter frustration. Like most kart-racers there is rubberbanding to keep the racers from getting lapped, but it doesn't matter for this type of racing game which is about pulling off amazing upsets and glorious falures. It really is all about the screaming, and it's fantastic.

Apparently supports Oculus VR, this game is on GearVR as well, so if you have that it might clinch the deal, but it's not something that really needs VR in my opinion, and plays just fine on a standard monitor.

If you've got the people this is an amazing multiplayer experience. BlazeRush is a top-down racer with a few different game modes and plenty of pickups to fire at your opponents. There's a singleplayer campaign as well but you'll be finding most of your fun with the multiplayer options. All the racers share the same screen so you get pulled ahead (or destroyed) for falling behind. There's a standard lap race mode, a mode where the person in first the longest wins, and Death Race. The last mode is by far most fun I've had with this game. A giant machine chases all the racers around the track until everyone is eliminated. The person that survives longest nets the most points, and it starts right back up again until you reach a set number. This leads to plenty of hilarious moments as players struggle to stay far ahead with boosters or sabotage the competition with firepower. I'd say it's a definite buy. Online multiplayer's dead though so grab this with 2 or 3 friends.

BlazeRush is more or less a top down (angled view) kart racer. You select from a list of characters and racing along various tracks, collecting power-ups to knock the other opponents off the track. There are 16 characters to select (some are unlockable) along with a bunch of power-ups to choose from. BlazeRush really excells in it's smooth controls and addictive gameplay. I wouldn't have expected this game to be as much as fun but it is awesome. BlazeRush features a full single player campaign and multiplayer. The online multiplayer works flawlessly as well and playing it with friends is outstanding. You battle constantly and the game allows for some amazing moments to happen, due to the fast pace of the races. There are three main game modes: Race (a traditional lap race), King of the Hill (Whoever stays in first the longest wins), and the cream of the crop - Death Race. Death Race is when you are speeding down the track, fighting other racers and avoiding a giant death machine. Whoever is the last to die, wins. BlazeRush is one of the games you get in a bundle, activate the key, idle the cards and leave it in your library to collect dust, but trust me this game needs to played by everyone who like arcadey top down racing games.

BlazeRush is about racers who like racing (great story right ?) and there's a corporation that tries to stop them racing by taking over every planet they race in.

Of course you are not in it for the story and for the gameplay and let me tell you it's quite a treat.

There are main events and I'll go through each one. The regular races where you need to finish first and try not to get destroyed by the enemy drivers, king of the hill where you try to stay in the lead for 50 seconds and a deathmatch where there's a steam roller behind you and you try to be the final person alive, a time trial mode where you need to finish a lap in a certain amount of time and there's a boss battle in the final mission where you need to use the weapons you used in the game to destroy the "Boss".

Each of these events are very chaotic and they are incredibly difficult so don't expect to breeze through all the events. The enemy AI is violent and every chance they get they will take you out and trust me you need to play dirty to win.

The soundtrack is really great as well and is awesome to listen to when you are playing this game.

The graphics are alright for the most part, and I love the cell shaded look for the tracks.

The track designs range from mediocre to pretty good. Each track has certain points where you can jump out of the track if you use boosters, but those parts are marked on the map, but it's still sometimes frustrating.

Now for the bad things for the game:

The one thing I hated was the fact that when you die you are set back so far back that there is almost no point in competing and the only solution is to restart.

The controls if you are using a keyboard takes a lot of time getting used to and I highly HIGHLY recommend using a controller.

The difficulty comes off as really difficult even in the early stages and not to mention the final act in the volcano planet has a massive difficulty spike and the final boss is quite tough, so be prepared for a challenge.

The camera is atrocious, it only follows the leading car at all times so if you are too far behind and too far ahead you will have to use the map to navigate the entire map.

Overall BlazeRush is a really fun game and it's pretty difficult as well, while I don't think it's worth the full price, it's worth it if it's in a bundle or on a discount.

Final Rating:
7/10 - Worth checking out
