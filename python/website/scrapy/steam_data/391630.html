About This Game

Space Colony Alert Bulletin!

The Necromonads are at it again!! Colonists must in a orderly fashion proceed to the nearest shelter while the highly trained star pilot provides cover. Inspired by classic arcade shooters but with a twist. Captured colonists become infected and spread it to others. stop the Necromonads infection invasion now!

Features


  • Blistering Arcade Action
  • Keyboard or Joystick Control
  • Lasers, and yes that's right, Bombs!
  • Multiple Enemy types
  • Steam Leaderboards & Steam Achievements
  • Space Zombies

Gameplay


Each level of Necromonads consists of a number of Colonists and Infection shelters that the colonists try to run to. The Necromonads will attempt to capture and infect the fleeing colonists. To accomplish this they upduct them as the flee and bring them to the top of the screen. Once to the top of the screen the colonist becomes infected and dropped back down them.

Once infected they spit virus bile to nearby colonists causing the infection to spread. Your job as Star Pilot is to stop the Necros long enough for the colonists to get to the shelters, Moving to the next level occurs when all colonists are accounted for and more then 50% of the remaining colonists are safe in the shelter. The Necros will send various different forms after you to stop you as you progress.

Tips


Bombs can change Colonists Direction
Necros are not affected by Crystal Barriers
Crystal Barriers can be helpful

I beta tested Necromonads and have to say its mindless fun that captures the old Arcade feeling quite well together with some modern comfort.

The game almost runs on a toaster while looking and sounding gorgeous. I could play it on my tablet while on the bus or train.

The handling is smooth and you get a good idea of it from the video.

The game works! The game mechanics such as the whole abducting process are cool. The world changing its color theme is a nice way to never get old. There's some gravity applied to your ship so the movement has that "Asteroids" feel to it.

Its an easy to grasp concept with the potential to get you hooked if its your kinda thing. Question is, is it?

The replayablility comes from attempting to reach the highscore. You might have seen all it has to offer in a half hour but the challenge and with it the joy stays for many more hours to come.

Is it worth the 4$ its asking for? Absolutely.

Simple defender clone with a zombie mechanic. Your fellow spacefriends at the bottom walk to some bunkers, if they get snacked by the evil spaceships coming from above they turn into zombies and spread the infection to adjacent fellow space friends. It is your job to not let that happen by shooting the baddies out of the sky. Later levels have some walls that prevent the lil guys getting to the bunker, unless you bomb these wall things with your secondary weapon. Theres some more enemy types I havent seen yet since it also has defender style difficulty.
Delivers what it claims to, Defender style gameplay. Didn't encounter any bugs so far, had decent fun flying the lil spaceship around.

I'd recommend it tho I have only played half an hour. Picked it up at a 40% discount.
