About This Game

Become the best castle builder in this original and addictive game where you must at the same time create a castle accurately while protecting it from all the annoying baddies.

Features

  • Unique concept, engaging and addictive game-play
  • 18 levels in the normal mode, 15 for the King Mode (Extra Hard), and 4 rounds of "Protect the Castle" mode (Survival)
  • Upgrade your castle-building equipment and collect items to get rid of the villains
  • 30 trophies to unlock (achievements)
  • 20 atlas pages to unlock (character descriptions and tips)
  • Funny characters and events with cartoonish graphics

Story and Concept

Monarchs come to you with a specific castle request, and your job is to build their dream palace piece by piece before the deadline without making mistakes. In the meantime, enemies come in and try to break your castle. They will try to burn it, explode it or even steal the pieces but you must get rid of them with the right items to reclaim control!
In "Protect the Castle" mode, you must keep the pre-made castles intact as much as possible by dealing with swarms of enemies attempting to take over... thankfully you have unlimited items in this frantic mode since the enemies are unlimited, too!

Basic Information
Title: Castle
Genre: Action, Casual, Indie
Developer: Snails Animation
Publisher: Snails Animation
Release Date: 7 Jan, 2015

Basic Overview
Castle is a simple unique puzzle game that has a steep learning curve, low replay ability and some interesting mechanics that really bring the game to it's name. I enjoyed this game for a certain amount of time, then it just got boring because of the difficulty. I am however recommending this game because even though it has low replay value and does get boring at times, there was a nice session of when it was fun to play. I do suggest playing in short sessions though as you might get bored of this game if you keep failing.

Game Play
This game is very unique in the sense of the puzzle side of things. You have to create a castle with the tiles of the castle at the top of the screen as the scroll from right to left. All you have to do is drag them into the correct places and make the castle. Simple enough right? That's what I thought. The games concept is great, don't get me wrong. I haven't seen other puzzle games that are like this. There are a few problems with this though. First off, it's easy to misjudge what piece of the castle goes where. There where many times in the game where I would put a piece on the wrong slot just because I wasn't sure if it fitted there or not. This is not a puzzle factor of the game, it's simple misjudgement. If you combine that with the enemies you have to take care of, the time limit and the lives that you have then you are always on edge of this game. There are so many things to take into consideration that makes this game so difficult. This then makes you play a single level over and over again for like half an hour and that isn't fun. That also ties in with the repetitive nature of the game as each level is basically a harder version of the previous level. They add more enemies, and bonuses you have to buy every now and then. Despite all the bad things that have said there are some fun elements to the game that I want to talk about. They do produce a small and pretty basic graph of how many of each enemy will be on that level. This means that you can plan in advanced and buy the things that can stop them from you building, each enemy has a different type of item that you can use to get rid of them. A fireball for a bird, anvil for a knights, dynamite for a mole ect. Along with the variety of enemies there are also 3 different variations of castles to build, plain, desert and ice which kinda separates the art style.
Score (Out Of 10): 7 - Would like to see more variations of the castles and less of a difficulty curve.

Graphics/Art Style
The Art Style to this game isn't all that good or bad. It has nice, crisp and clean graphics for a cartoon like game. I personally don't really like the way this game looks, the Art Style looks too childish for me to really like it. I like some cartoon art styles but this just seems to go a bit overboard for me. I will score it fairly as I know some people do like this kind of Art Style
Score (Out Of 10): 7 - I can see why people would like the graphics, but for me personally I don't like them. They look too childish

Music & Sounds
The music to this game is great. Although it would be better if they could add some more variation to each different type of castle, as depending on the type of castle it is will change the music accordingly to that certain castle type. This means that there are only 3 different soundtracks that play throughout the entire game. Again gets very repetitive if your playing in a longer session. As for the sounds they are okay, the sound of a anvil hitting a knight on the head is pretty satisfying to say the least, and others like when you hit a fireball on the bird.
Score (Out Of 10): 8 - Would like to see more variation for soundtracks.

Conclusion
I would recommend this game to people who like puzzle games, I suggest playing in short periods to avoid not really having as much fun as you did the first time you played it. Also the soundtracks get repetitive so there is another reason why playing in shorter sessions could benefit your gaming experience better than mine.

Castle is a very unique puzzle/building game, where you basically build a castle while being attacked by various things that either prevent you from building your castle, or they remove pieces you have already placed.

You have a bunch of hooks at the top of the screen that will place the castle pieces for you, so you drag the right shaped pieces to the right hook. You need to kep an eye out for various things that prevent you from making the castle, such as a thunder cloud that makes one of the hooks rusty so it won't work, or a chicken could swoop in and take a piece from you.
You need to do all of this quickly and accuratly, or you will lose.
Between rounds you can use in game coins to buy upgrades like faster hook drops, and items to deal with the attackers.

It's very unique, but that doesn't make a good game.
I played about 30 mins and made it to level 7, but that was enough for me.
The game is quite easy to figure out and it explains what to do, but the actual gameplay is more a chore than entertainment.

I found the whole process to be rather annoying which may be your cup of tea, but it isn't mine.

It's easy to misjudge where pieces go, and if you send the wrong piece to the wrong spot too many times, you lose.
It's always rather stressful to constantly have to watch out for problems against your castle and there is little room for error, and I'm sure it only gets worse.

While this may sound like an inexperienced player complaining becuase I wasn't very good, the issue here is, this is not fun enough to BOTHER getting good.

This is the type of game that needs a Demo, because it will certainly be a hit and miss game for people.

For me, It's a miss.

This game is obviously made for mobile and hastely ported to PC. The controls are more intuitive for a small-sized touch-screen.

The options are severely lacking, ie no sound or music sliderbar and the monitor options reset as do the audio settings each time the game is launched. I'm greeted with blaring background-music and have to go through several screens just to get to sound options to mute them... The simplistic audio mute-buttons could've been in the main menu or in the main options menu rather than an additional level deeper.

As for the screen resetting; I used 1920x1080 which is a mistake for this game because the mouse needs to travel a larger distance to place the same amount of blocks, and you have to pay attention that a slightly rotated block isn't placed somewhere it shouldn't. For me to play this longer than 5 to 10 minutes at a time, I'd need to use a super small window so I don't need to move my head / neck. ----- Now, back to reset. I tried full-screen, then windowed and it resets to the smallest wide-screen size despite the window being the proper size. The rendering engine renders 1/5th the screen when you swap; after you change the resolution in the options between one other and the larger, it renders fine. There is an issue with the game not properly rendering when it loses focus, but not minimized, which is slightly annoying too.

The game is essentially: Build a 3 to x sized puzzle using only the edge pieces, more suited for children aged 3 to 4 or younger to gauge coordination and tracking; it's too easy. To counteract it being so easy, the developer added a very short time-limit ( but you can extend it if you need more time ). If you let the time run out the round resets, but wait.. there's more.. you get back ALL of the money you spent and then some... for failing the level... The developer also made the pieces move in conveyor-fashion.

To top it off, and this is where a few interesting things were added to the bland game: enemies / traps which rust your cranes, etc... which is a good touch... But a penguin running off with tons and tons of stone by pulling the bottom layer off and then the top coming down ( although, the way they'd build castles is by alternating stone so with the loss of stone underneath, the entire thing won't come crashing down ), but then again, to "add challenge" I suppose. It was actually more annoying than cute, although the first time seeing it I thought it looked odd.

Fix: Rendering issue with changing resolution, annoying critters that can't be stopped ( IE moles can be stopped with tnt but not a meteor which can provide a bit more energy ) with certain tools; let certain tools be more useful...
Add: If the game has lost focus but not minimized prevent sleep or provide an option..


Games are supposed to be enjoyable / fun, they should be challenging in some cases but not to the point of being annoying ( although you could create a clause such as certain holidays, depending on the map, that particular critter is overly annoying... Groundhog day, Christmas, Halloween, etc... ) which would add flavor without being annoying all the time..


I'll revisit this review at a later date; aside from the rendering glitch and the free-money "on-purpose??", you're a winner for trying, and some of the annoying characteristics I could see this game being quite enjoyable for the incrediibly young while making them pay attention to minute differences thereby stimulating the brain. However, with games such as Angry Birds, which is free, and which is fun aside from the noise, they'd likely turn to that after becoming annoyed with this after a very short time.
