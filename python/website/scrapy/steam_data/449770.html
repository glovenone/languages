23 June

Short update!

Hello everyone! I released a small update to the game that doesn't alter things too much, but I wanted to go over it.

-Sidequest tracking. Thanks to the Diary system, it isn't hard to pick up where you left off in the main storyline, but it was easy to forget (or in some cases, not even be aware of) sidequests you could do. A "Quests" tab has been added to the Item menu, and will update whenever you gain or complete a sidequest. You will also get a text box telling you when a quest is received.
-Enemy rebalancing. Legend of Moros has random encounters that are supposed to be challenging and give plenty of Experience Points, but fights can drag on at times. Enemy HP was decreased by an average of 12% (excluding bosses), and I may alter this further as more feedback comes in.

As always, please let me know if you have any questions or further issues. Thank you for playing, and I hope you continue to enjoy Legend of Moros!

21 March

Small Update #1!

Hello everyone! Now that I've gotten some feedback from players about some issues and oversights, I've released a small update to handle things. Some things included are:

-Equipment menu is found in the Main Menu, rather than the Items tab. This was causing issues selecting characters to change equipment for previously, and while it was possible, it was also annoying. This makes it much simpler to change your gear.
-Debuff duration in Hard Mode fixed. Some negative statuses weren't lasting as long as intended on Hard Mode, so now they all (should) have +1 turn duration compared to the other difficulties.
-A few NPC's added to the end game. The last few towns were pretty devoid of people, so a couple have been added in.

Please let me know if you have any questions or further issues. Thank you for playing, and I hope you continue to enjoy Legend of Moros!

About This Game

Legend of Moros is a retro-style RPG that focuses on the quest of Roland and his friends as they try to return to their homeland. But while they have a straightforward goal, adventures don't often end up that way...

Main Features:

Characters: There are over 12 characters that will join you on your journey. The main character does not have to be in the active party, which results in 1000+ potential party combinations!
Storyline: A carefully written storyline that is equal parts dramatic and humorous. NPC's and sidequests help to increase the player's understanding of the world outside of the main quest.
Original Assets: Custom animations, tilesets, music, and monsters help to set a unique tone and aesthetic for the world.
Gameplay: A tactical combat system that rewards proper execution of both individual character tactics and the overall team strategy. Select one of three difficulty settings to choose from for this 30-40 hour adventure.
Sidequest driven: Apart from the main story, NPC's or objects may start a potential sidequest. The player is free to choose whether or not to take these missions, but there are rewards for them, as well as helping to learn about the other character's motivations. There is also no time limit for completing a sidequest, but certain sidequest bosses may grow stronger if you choose to progress through certain points of the main story.

Equipment: Equipment has been simplified. As opposed to many separate pieces of armor and multiple hand slots, characters each have a few different and unique weapons that do most of the modifications to statistics. In many games, weapons get progressively better as you make progress. In Legend of Moros, there are always benefits and drawbacks. A weapon may have a high critical hit rate, but poor defenses, for example. You can also equip one weapon mod (to change your basic attack's element, or to give a chance for a status effect), one seal (which lets your character use a skill they otherwise couldn't), and two accessories (status resistance, extra attack power, health regeneration, and so on).

Unique Combat: This game uses an unusual Stamina system. As opposed to games where spellcasters use MP and everyone else is just attacking, Stamina is consumed by a character whenever they use a skill or spell.

But that's just using another name for a resource, and this is still a turn-based game. How is that different?

Each character can set a certain Tactic; this allows them to gain Stamina whenever certain criteria are met. Which do you prefer: having your main healer regain Stamina whenever they heal someone, or whenever a party member takes damage? While each option covers multiple criteria (so you could have both of these), there are many different ways each character can be used. While RPGs have tended toward more of an action and reflexive skillset as time has gone on, I have tried to make this the epitomy of a strategy RPG.

There's a large and interesting cast of characters, and for the vast majority of the game, you can choose to have any four characters that you've found in the active party. If you don't like the main character, you can swap them out very early on. Also, at the beginning of the game, you are able to pick the difficulty you'd like to play on. (You can also press F1 on the keyboard to check/edit controls, if need be.)

Okay, to start I should mention that I played this game in beta before it was on Steam, so I probably have about 10-15 hours logged total. I have not yet beaten the game. I'll try to give an honest mostly non-biased review. Since I have a preference and style of games I like (as I assume everyone does), I will try to push that aside for this review.

It should be noted that this game wasn't delvoped by a team or company, it was made by one man over the course of a few years. That alone should count for something. But I digress...

To start, lets talk about the obvious. Yes it looks like crap. The graphics are pretty barren and old. It really does leave much to be desired. But if you judge a game solely on it's looks then you'll miss out on a hell of a lot of treasures! The game shouldn't be played for looks alone.. or at all. The game should be played for the story, much like any RPG.

The story is good, it's not amazing but it's not trash either. It has a unique perspective and moves at a reasonable pace. There are a few times where I personally felt the story was dragging a bit, but I think the plot unfolds well enough and the over all arch of the story suits the game.

The characters are unique and indepth. They all have their own personal back stories that helps to understand their motivation and personalities. There is enough substance to the characters that you can either relate with or against them. I have a few favorites when I play, and the nice thing about the game is that I could play with all of my favorite characters in one party. (I don't because most of my favorites are support characters... but I could!)

The system is interesting... Its out of the norm and that adds to the game play, but I personally feel it was a bit hard to understand and I struggled to pick it up quickly. It took me a while to understand how to do a lot of things in and out of combat, but I also played the majority of the game in beta before there was a tutorial... It's probably much easier to grasp now that there is a tutorial to break it down for you.

The level of difficulty in the game is pretty rough, I tend to be more of a casual gamer, and I find myself struggling a lot to finish this game. I die constantly and get frustrated when things need to be repeated because I failed at them, or when I have to back track to grind levels out to defeat a boss or quest mob. But a lot of RPGs are grindy, and this one is no exception.

So to recap:

Cons:
It is not visually appealing
The unique system can be hard to pick up and learn quickly
It's really hard and requires a lot of grinding (that's what she said?)
Some of the story elements felt drawn out or forced

Pros:
The story draws you in
It has interesting and fun characters
It doesn't have a typical battle system
You get a sense of nostalga from playing it

Overall opinion, I'd recommend this game ONLY to people who really enjoy RPGs and don't mind being ported back to the NES era of gaming. This game isn't for everyone.

A lot beneath the surface

So first and foremost, I do know the dev personally, and I played this game previously from Itch.io. If you wanna call me biased that's fine, but just wanna be open with ya on it.

I see a lot of posts here about the graphics, and to be quite frank that is the worst part. Locations look NES era FF to me a lot of the time, characters fair better, and monsters can be hit or miss, but some of the designs are pretty original and remind me of things out of Earthbound. Everyone's first impressions are going to be from the screenshots here, but personally I found a lot of enjoyment to thisgame and wouldn't judge a book by its cover alone.

The stamina system in the gameplay really encouraged me to push out further and further while grinding as I built up meter to pull off my abilities, rather than in other RPGs where I'd stay close to home and return to town to refill my HP and MP constantly. There's definitely a risk reward to this system though as you might run into something that completely wrecks you if you go too far out unprepared. That combined with a system of titles that worked similar to FFX to customize how each party member gains meter kept me fine tuning my party. Which also you get a lot of characters as you go with different skill sets so you can really customize things a great deal.
I also really enjoyed other little touches to the world. I found a lot of npc dialogue to be pretty humorous, and loved that there was both a journal and party dialogue that could help you in the right direction when you're out exploring the massive world map, or haven't played ina while and lost track of what you were doing (this is a big one for me XD) Oh! There's also a crafting system which was an unexpected but welcome surprise. Also the music is pretty snazzy, i believe that was done by someone else for the title, but I can't recall.

Anyway I'd give it a 3.5 or 4 out of 5. Visually it's a mixed bag, but the gameplay and writing are both great IMO. It gives me the feel of playing an old school rpg (which it should) but with enough changes to the game to keep things interesting and from simply being some knock off Final Fantasy or Dragon Quest.

Also wasn't sure what it'd be sold at on steam but 6 dollars is a pretty darn good rate for the amount of content you find here. So seriously, give this one a shot.

EDIT: As mentioned above I played a non steam version of this game. While I have purchased it on here, I have just barely started it in case of any technical differences.
