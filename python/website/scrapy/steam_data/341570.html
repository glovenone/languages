What the developers have to say:

“During the early access phase of Throbax we want to gather as much feedback and input from players all over the world as possible.

“Our current time-schedule is to end early access phase in 2016 - a more detailed time-plan will be announced later.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Currently we plan the following key features for Throbax TD:

  • Battle your way through 50 levels, buy over 100 upgrades and get all of the 50+ achievements.
  • Captivating story that takes place in a universe created for this game only (sequel pending)
  • More than 10 different types of enemies
  • 25 level circle tower defense mode
  • 25 level standard tower defense mode
  • Over 100 upgrades for towers and your hero

  • English / German

  • Spells
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current status of Throbax is that the gameplay is fully implemented and the player can dive into thrilling levels with different mechanics and different types of towers.

Currently we have 25 fully functional levels, 6 different types of towers and 11 different types of enemies which await you!”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We plan to raise the price of Throbax as we ship the final version including all features and content.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We want to use all available steam communication channels in order to get as much feedback as possible. So please tell us your thoughts and contact us via Steam forums, chat or email.”

About This Game

THIS GAME IS CURRENTLY IN ALPHA STAGE

Throbax TD is a "Tower Defense" game with a twist. You have to survive wave after wave of fiendish enemies in a struggle for nothing less than your whole planet.

Key-Features

  • Battle your way through 50 levels, buy over 100 upgrades and get all of the 50+ achievements.
  • Captivating story that takes place in a universe created for this game only (sequel pending)
  • More than 10 different types of enemies
  • 25 level circle tower defense mode
  • 25 level standard tower defense mode
  • Over 100 upgrades for towers and your hero
  • Achievements
  • English / German
  • English / German voiceover in all cut-scenes
  • Towers with individual effects
  • Spells

About the game

The game was planned as an old-school tower defense game (like Blizzard's tower-defense map in Frozen Throne), but with a twist.

We wanted the game to have an immersive story and the first half of it to be a circle-tower-defense-game, meaning that the monsters will not vanish in a portal of some sort, but stay on the screen until you have destroyed them, or there are 100 of them on the screen (in which case you have lost).

But other than that, no bells and whistles, just basic action, but neatly balanced, good controls and not too easy.

The games controls are RTS-like (that means, that you control a hero, who will do most of the work in-game) and the second half of the levels will change the gameplay to a standard tower-defense game.

Early Access Review
Posted: 27 April
This is the most frustrating game I'll ever recommend. Gameplay is difficult, made more so by the amount of stuff that you need to know which the tutorials don't address. The single player campaign requires tremendous amounts of grinding, because your overall strength is limited by how much cash you make, and that's limited by what levels you can play on in what difficulties, so there's a fairly linear grind that has you replaying these same, circular little levels that do nothing and go nowhere (the singleplayer experience of this game), as you try desperately to keep the number of creeps below 100 (the unstated lose condition for the game, though you figure it out quick; there's also a nice visual effect that makes it clear you're boned as the creep number climbs up).

But it's a lot of fun. Seriously, give it a try. Funstrating. There are deep mechanics to this simple looking game. It's compelling to try to get further. Levels are unforgiving, even brutal, as a lot of the difficulty ends up being down to not allowing exponential increases in the enemies on screen... Balance seems to be 1. good, and 2. absent, in that 1. you'll find levels get progressively harder and you'll enjoy being challenged, but 2. there is no real effort made to ensure the player progresses at any given speed - you just slam into a brick wall of unsurpassable difficulty, or the level is easy and you can do it fine. Either you're over the hump and can do more damage than the enemies can take hit points, or you're not and you have to replay earlier levels more.

There are a number of improvements possible for this game, but it's already a quixotic, charming, maddening, enjoyable title. Hopefully the designers will continue to improve it. But I can recommend it as an enjoyable and even somewhat innovative Tower Defense title right now, as long as you're willing to lose quite a lot if you're trying to progress through the levels too quickly, and don't mind a total lack of hand-holding past a few basic tutorials.

Early Access Review
Posted: 27 January
Not every Early Access title has to have the most glamorous hopes and dreams or the desired budget to make a blockbuster game that otherwise wouldn’t be possible. Meet Unterrainer Informatik OG a small but experienced team of three software developers led by Psilo, the company’s lead programmer, software engineer. They have a well developed philosophy that in today’s age of sandbox games and user generated content that there is still time for old fashioned games. They believe in the fundamentals of gaming that are being thrown out of the window in favor of glitz and glamour. Those fundamentals being that a ‘game, above all other things, needs to be based on a good idea and needs an interesting story. I agree with this appraisal. This is of vital importance because if you are lacking in these things then it is impossible to create something that people want to play for an extended period of time. Even in today’s pick up and throw away world you still need these fundamental things.

So what exactly is Throbax TD? That’s an easy one to answer. It’s a tower defense game in which you, the player, have to survive waves of enemies otherwise your planet is destroyed and thus the game over. But it’s not your standard tower defense game. That would be too simple and although the production isn’t glamorous what it does offer is something fresh and challenging. As the Steam description aptly states there are two phases of gameplay involved. The first phase is an RTS where you use your strategic thinking in building your defense systems in order to avoid the incoming onslaught. The game then transitions into what would be considered a typical tower defense game. These two phases work wonderfully well together. You can clearly see where the developers draw their inspiration for the game from – which they unabashedly state is the tower-defense map in Frozen Throne, one of the most popular maps in gaming history. They have big shoes to fill if they intend on living up to their aspirations but thus far the development choices have been intelligent.

Arguably the most fascinating aspect of the project is that there is multiplayer. Unfortunately, in my time with the game before going to print I never got involved in that component of the game. However, people that I have discussed the game with me advise that the current status of the multiplayer is weak but there is more than sufficient grounds to believe that the multiplayer as it is developed will likely become the lifeblood of the game.

The graphics and sound are not going to blow you away. The team have concentrated on giving the best playing experience possible. The team have not offered any of the bells and whistles that you have come to expect from modern gaming. This would work against everything that the team stands for. If you’re looking for your smoke and mirrors then you’re best to look away.

Throbax TD is definitely a throw back to an older age in gaming but it’s a delightful visit and one that is highly recommended. At $2.99 it’s a cheap investment into a promising title.
