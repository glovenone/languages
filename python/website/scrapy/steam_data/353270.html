About This Game

Key Features
  • +1 to Combos! Customize attack combinations with up to five moves, opening up a whole new array of ways to defeat your enemies!
  • New Story! Play through new story and scenarios when someone else joins the Console War fray and refuses to play by the rules!
  • Bring a Dungeon Buddy! The realtime mini-game Stella’s Dungeon is back, and this time she can bring friends! Each is equipped with special skills that lend their support to Stella in her items quest, so choose wisely!
  • Remake in your Image! Modify the world of Gamindustri to your liking by creating “Plans” that alter dungeon treasures, enemy difficulty, and more!
  • Get Crafty! Customize powerful equipment that can unlock the true power of the CPUs, opening up a world of new skills for them to use!

Posted: 23 June
Beyond the horizons of the ever increasingly small place we live in lies the world of Gensokyo and Gamindustri, home to many an amazing sight most people here can never lay their eyes on. In this land, which has nearly been demolished in the Console Wars before threatening the balance between its rulers where an empty headed girl finds herself teleported to another dimension and now has to deal with the surprises coming from this situation while trying to find a way back to her own.

Even before the game itself begins, its picture on the store page and the opening animation after launching it already suggests that this game is going to be amazing, with the graphics coming into view next that shows the places waiting to be explored as the events of RB3 slowly begin to unfold. In that, the world map is visibly nicer to look at, with parts of the continent made separate instances so each would be much more detailed which makes it easy to navigate and even enjoyable to look at. Its dungeon mode and the combat taking place there are also pretty well made with the bigger moves being really flashy and the more basic ones being less so, but still interesting. The only gripe there is some of the underground caves being so dark, it makes treasure hunting very difficult without plans to help with.
There's also the story mode view which brings back many of the familiar places but with a new look, presenting various beautifully made landscape with Easter-style corridors, skyscrapers in the distance and many others, all of which make for an unusual but entertaining set. The character designs are also looking great with bringing back the old look of gals which were better in the first game and keeping the rest, while the new ones might be even better in appearance.

It also has a pretty great soundtrack with familiar songs returning that reall set the atmosphere, mostly the ones played during bossfights or in dungeons when fighting a harder monster. These pieces of musical arts make otherwise not too challenging battles awesome moments and one can't help, but seek them out to listen to these even a few more times. There's also the possibility to change them if one wishes to by completing plans, though since there are only very few of these and some of them don't fit the place where they can be heard, they aren't necessarily bringing the desired effect. Voice acting is also excellent with great performance from most actors, but even then, Plutia's is still really annoying which is not that nice since those playing will get to hear her a lot.

Still, even though these elements offer an incredible experience by themselves, so does the gameplay in this release. Our beloved dungeons make a comeback from the first installment with much the same layout which is helpful since it's easy directions this way, but this also brings some interesting changes. One being that finding hidden items is no longer achieved by spamming the radar once we begin exploring, but rather all spots are visible only the special ones being invisible while casting a shadow on the ground, and by jumping in the right place reveals them some coins and sometimes other stuff, but thankfully there aren't any items there which one has to scour the whole place for by visiting every blocks on the map. It is also possible now to find the location of materials needed for anything in the Remake interface, which is very helpful since you don't have to find everything all over the place anymore, so excellent moves here.
There have been significant changes to combat as well in the way that the developers cut the EXE Drive gauge out since everybody just spammed them to obliterate everything that happens to be in the area, so now they are tied to the SP meter depleting the vast majority of it after a single discharge. While this does make fights a little more tactical, it also sorta backfires sadly, because changing to HDD forms alone drains a portion of it already, and using their strongest attack takes away the rest, forcing the party members to spam rush attacks refill it or use less effective SP moves which often still require a lot. As such, this design was not quite the most agreeable way to make combat more exciting unfortunately.
This becomes even more pronounced with the introducing of challenges which are not unlike the ones one could see in Fairy Fencer F. Problem is, said successor of the EXE Drive is only unlocked through a plan for each character separately a milestone is reached in one, so the player is practically forced to play only a few girls, or they won't be able to reap the benefits they need. Not only that, this quickly becomes increasingly tedious as the next step in these require to repeat the same action impossible amount of times no sane person would bother to waste time on especially the last one, so while it sounds great at first its implementation is anything but satisfactiory. Therefore, only a few are actually worth to get like the fifth atrike slot in a combo or the flashiest skills you can get from there.
On the other hand Stella's Dungeon also returns but this times much more nicely with the adjustments to its design, mainly in the interface that is much easier to use now but also with other features like the option to pick a scout and also useful abilities that greatly increase her survivalibility as ashe ascends the floors of the tower in front of her. it is also the only way to acquire many plans for the Remake system with ingredients for stuff as a side benefit, so it's highly recommended to be used with a related DLC reducing risks and time sinking a lot as well.

Surprisingly enough the translation quality has also become a lot better with miswording so badly that I would fire the idiot who rewrote and not actually translated the original script being much rarer now, and while the destruction of cultural aspects like Nepgear-chan butchered into Nep Jr. is still difficult to accept, it finally reached a point where it's on a more tolerable level. It's kind of eyebrow raising when someone who never studied Japanese language in a language course could translate lines better than a guy who gets paid for that. Seriously.
And while it's not such an important issue one might find it amusing in the storyline that having different time speed is impossible since the characters wouldn't be able to communicate then. It'd create a phenomenon not unlike in a Stargate episode where time went so slowly it cancelled out any kind of way to contact others from the outside. It is most definitely a plothole, but on the other hand, it was never mean to be accurate in terms of physics.
On the other hand, Plutia's simply the worst since her aything but likeable and borderline antagonistic behavior ruins many of the otherwise enjoyable scenes which clearly shows how she should be an enemy, not ally. This is a very big demerit since anybody who isn't into that kind of messed up character will find her less than agreeable, yet we're forced to watch the stunts she pulls out of her rear end, especially as the events progress.

While this game still has its share of inconsistencies and flaws however both inherently and through localization, after the massive trainwreck of the second installment in this series liked only by the mindless sheeple who outright ignored every single flaw it had no matter how glaring, it was a pleasant turn seeing how this release was made so much more enjoyable. Following the best traits of the previous ones while fine tuning the gameplay now makes for an even more amazing experience which doesn't happen often in sequels. The Victory in its title is therefore an amusing piece of humor, since it's indeed a win.
