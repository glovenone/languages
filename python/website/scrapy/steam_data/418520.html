5 July

Update: Cylindrical Line Brush!

We've just released a new version of SculptrVR with a cylindrical line tool!



The line brush works by holding down the trigger, dragging out a straight line, and then releasing the trigger to place a voxelized line. You can adjust the detail of the cylindrical line with [Settings -> Brush Detail].

It's great for both adding straight lines and carving straight channels.


We've also updated to the rocket model to better match the theme :-)


There are a bunch of little back-end improvements that should increase sculpting performance as well as decrease multiplayer bandwidth.

We've also revamped the day/night lighting in preparation for some future features. You might want to check out the butterflies at night for a hint of what's to come ;-)

The next update will include another brush from popular demand as well as a few more visual upgrades. We'll be aiming for weekly updates starting now!

I hope you enjoy everything!

-Nathan

21 June

Multiplayer Release!

Multiplayer is now released on the main branch of SculptrVR!!



Join your friends on Steam, or join strangers in a public game in SculptrVR. Build worlds together!

Here's the launch trailer: https://youtu.be/8HgpL5-e1LM

During the beta, we've had the opportunity to jump into many of your worlds, explore them, play in them, and add to them. It's been incredibly fun, and I can't wait to share that experience with more people!

We've worked hard to bring down the net bandwidth per player as much as possible. This in turn allows up to 16 players (theoretically at least... we haven't yet been able to test with that many...) to join one world and create together! Currently in a 16 player game, each client only needs ~6kB/s of upload and ~50kB/s of download. The host needs 50kB/s symmetric upload/download. Not too bad!

Sculpting in SculptrVR is extremely computational. An i5 CPU is currently only capable of calculating ~200 actions per second. With 16 players connected, all sculpting at 90hz, even the fastest CPUs would be overwhelmed. To deal with this, we rate-limit your sculpting based on number of connected clients. Instead of placing a sphere every single frame, you'll only place a sphere every 8th frame. This also helps keep down the net-bandwidth!

I can't promise you that everything is perfect, but I can say it's very, very fun!

I hope to see you all in world soon!

-Nathan

Edit June 23, 2016: Hotfix update! There was an ambarassingly common situation where delete particles would cause multi-frame hiccups in the game. That has been fixed.

About This Game

SculptrVR is a multiplayer interactive world-builder for the HTC Vive. You can build at any scale. Make yourself as large as a mountain or as small as a flower. Build a detailed world, explore it from a different scale, and then blow it up with rockets.

You can even export your worlds to use outside of SculptrVR or 3D print them!

SculptrVR is a complete world building experience, but it's continuing to grow and improve.

Coming soon:
  • Upload and share your worlds
  • Download and explore worlds built by others
  • Game inside the sculpted worlds

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • Processor: intel i5 quad core
    • Memory: 8 GB RAM
    • Graphics: GTX 970 or equivalent
    • Storage: 500 MB available space

The ability to scale in and out to play with everything in a macro and micro level is awesome! Just like the videos show, you can create rooms, trees, mountains, and everything your mind can conceptualize and then zoom in and out to add unreal levels of detail. The demo videos showing a tree-within-a-tree-within-a-tree isn't exaggerating slightly: it really has about 6-7 levels of "trees" in their demo scene.

The most fun I had with this was sampling the multiplayer (still in beta, but coming soon). Fire up a team Skype call, launch multiplayer, and we collaborated on a work project, where everyone was "drawing" all over every surface like they were 3D whiteboards. You could watch your teammates as they drew Godzilla-sized in the air over your heads, while you marched around the front of your sculpture like an ant. We could see, in real-time, every stroke of everyone around us, and designing levels where you actually DRAW the levels together was a blast. (Don't forget to save often so you can pull up the work later on your own single-player world and review all of your collaboration from the comfort of your own room.)

Where a picture paints a thousand words, this tool explodes that by an order of several magnitudes. The concept was simple, but incredibly well-executed. If you're queasy around heights... you may want to keep your feet planted on the ground for this one.

SculptrVR is uniquely great. It is a much more powerful experience than Tilt Brush which seems comparable at first glance.

As far as I can tell, SculptrVR is the first of its kind in that people can use it to build at vastly differing scales. I've dreamed of an app like this for a while now, so it's not surprising that it kept me busy for several hours. However, in case the developer is open for suggestions, there are a couple of things that can be addressed:

There aren't very many options for tools; just sphere, cube, rocket, spraycan, and this one projectile-orb thing that I can't get to work (plus their eraser equivalents). I could think of a few tool ideas right off the bat that would certainly make building things faster (straight-line box fill, rectangular-prismic box fill, resolution of boxes in drawing spheres, cut/copy/paste, recolor, sunlight direction, bezier curve box fill, etc).

Also, there are only 16 possible colors for boxes, all of which appear to have the same color scheme as wool blocks in Minecraft. This is really one of my lesser complaints; I can understand if 16 colors is for memory-saving purposes, because I can tell the program was made with space-saving in mind. (I'm going to take a wild guess and say that every heterogeneous block is defined by eight smaller blocks, some of which might recursively consist of eight even-smaller blocks and so on.) Despite this, a color palette that allows users to decide which 16 colors are used would be nice.

Because this program is so new, I'll excuse these minor issues and say that I recommend this game, because let's be honest: creating worlds of vastly differing scales is an awesome and undervisited idea. I see so much potential as to what SculptrVR could become; in particular, I'm excited to see what you mean when you say "Game inside the scupted worlds" on the list of things that are coming soon.

Edit: I'm not very good at actually writing reviews, so my bad if this was a little unfair-sounding.
