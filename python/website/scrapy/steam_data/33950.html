About This Game

In this underwater puzzle game you control fish secret agents Max Flounder and Tina Guppy.
Seemingly a common case turns into a tough fight with a mysterious enemy and the strings appear to lead pretty high. Fortunately, you are not alone. Cool crab brothers, a retired agentess, lost baby seahorse and peculiar pair of snails - all of them will join you to help you to discover the evil one. You will need all your wits if you want to win!

Key features:

  • Unlimited gameplay
  • 100+ mind-boggling levels based on simple, yet interesting mechanics.
  • Funny story told by animated sequences.
  • 3,000+ professionally dubbed comments and dialogs.
  • Lots of bonus stuff to be found and unlocked by the player.
  • Three unique playable characters: agents Tina and Max + crab Virgil.
  • Dozens hours of gameplay for whole family!
  • Best solution challenge at www.fish-fillets.com.
  • Send your score to internet chart and compare to other players.
  • Includes Level Editor - allows fans to create new mission and campaigns that can be used in the game.

System Requirements

    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: nVidia® GeForce™4 - 256 MB
    • DirectX®: DirectX®: 9.0c
    • Hard Drive: 650 MB free space

Posted: 7 November, 2015
An excellent sequel to the legendary Fish Fillets. It offers a proper logical challenge on a reasonable curve. You quickly get obsessed with solving the rooms and collecting bonus stars that can raise the difficulty incredibly high.

Compared to the original Fish Fillets, the humour got somewhat tuned down, the rooms are more uneventful (no more talking heads and dancing crabs) and the game is generally less silly. However, it is still funny and lively, as there are more active characters and plenty of dialogue. The 3D videos are not of great quality and look like someone needed to check them off a list, but the in-game 3D graphics is surprisingly good-looking and not too distracting.

The rest is only praise. The game got incredibly improved with completely new and original mechanics. Apart from a big and small fish, you work with crawling crabs, sticky snails, items that float and rise up, deadly lasers and radiation. The authors also cleverly introduced bonus starfish that can be collected for an extra challenge. That way, the puzzles can be impossibly hard for those who want to, but the player does not get stuck in the story progress. Solving the puzzles is also made more efficient with the save and undo system, and you can unlock a square-grid feature that is really helpful. There is a great number of rooms to solve, to many of which you will return to solve them again, but this time collecting starfish on your way out.

The game does not aim to keep up with the current visual trends and may seem mediocre at first sight. However, it has a very catchy and cleverly designed gameplay. It is one the best logical games I have ever played, and you should definitely give it a try.
