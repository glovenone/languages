About This Software

Platfinity™, fast & easy game creation!

Create your own platform games, quick & easy! Move to play, click to edit. One click export to sell or share your games!

Multiple Tile layers, Platform, Moving and Rotating Hazard objects.

Create your own graphics with the built in painting tools or import graphics from the disk.

Use the Retrofilter to double or quadruple pixels to give your creations that vintage look.



Includes the Demo Games:
"Proving Grounds of the Mad Game Designer"
"Purgatory"
"8 Bit Love Affair"
"Ghost in the City" by George Dziov

System Requirements

    Minimum:

    • Processor: 1 GHz +
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: Any
    • Storage: 150 MB available space
    • Sound Card: Any

    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Any
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
    • Sound Card: Any

Posted: 26 May
As a game, it's terrible. Floaty controls making landing tricky jumps near impossible. You get a tiny handful of demo levels included to get a feel for how the game works.

As software to create your own platformer levels, it's easy to use and probably that's where 95% of the entertainment comes from for this game. However, going back to play created maps goes back to my initial point, the controls are not spectacular, and the overall experience as a game is lackluster at best, though it really depends on what you create.

The menus are also VERY ugly, which I won't penalize the software/game for since I'm judging it primarily on entertainment value and lasting appeal, but those menus look like they were built for a Web 1.0 webpage.

If you're hoping to pick this up and get some amazing platforming action, $10 is WAY too steep. If you want to let your creativity flow and see what you can create for a platformer level, well, $10 still feels a little too steep since turning around and playing your creation won't be a mindblowing affair. As a game, I'm not recommending, as software, I'm undecided. The game desperately needs Steam Workshop support to download other people's creations to justify the price in all honesty.

Posted: 25 May
Easy to use as the video suggests - and has plenty of basic retro looking stock assets, these might not be to everybody's taste but you can easily import your own graphics, sprites and artwork, so don't let that put you off at all.

The developer has put a lot of thought into making it user-friendly as well, this stands out.

If you are a sucker for these "easy game makers" and you want to make a 2-D platformer with a minimum of fuss or bother this gets you there without any scripting or programming. Just bring your ideas. If you want to sell them and avoid the wrath of the likes of Jim Sterling, bring some original artwork as well.

If you are gifted with any skills as an artist you really could showcase your talents very nicely in Platfinity. All the mechanics for a good 2-D platformer (without guns) are in there at the click of a mouse button.

It has useful features which are expected by users in your finished games, but which a lot of these game-makers leave out, or force you to code/script yourself: - screen zoom, user key mapping, controller support etc. So it hasn't just been written for the budding game developer, it has the player in mind as well. Your finished games should run on the most modest spec PC as well. (Windows, but not IOS or Android)

You can drag and drop static or moving platforms, ladders, traps background tiles and foreground tiles around, change their properties, movements etc, you really can make a multi level game in no time at all if you have the ideas.

There is a good manual, in plain english, but you won't need it for long because most of the functions are very intuitive and there are also comprehensive help files in game, and playing through the demo games provided with the software will give you a good taste of what you can be doing in no time at all.

Ultra low PC requirements, and it's a small download. Generous licensing terms, you can install on two computers for one user, and if you sell your games all the revenue is yours to keep, and the developers have released it at a very low price.

You will be able to have a lot of fun with this, and there is nothing to stop you making some quote polished entertaining games for friends and family, or something more ambitious if you have the the creativity and ideas. Well worth it just for prototyping a platformer concept, because it is so quick to work with. You don't even need to restart a level to play-test (almost a process of adding stuff as you play)

It has a built in paint app to create or edit tiles and sprites

It really is a joy to use, totally stable, reliable and solid. The store page details and video don't do it justice.

The manual available in the steam store page link is written in plain english, so it should be easy to understand even if english isn't your first language. Less is more seems to be the ethos with both the manual and the steam store page description.

Trading cards and steam achievements included.
