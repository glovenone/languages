What it is:
Another little app that let you view a few 360 degree videos.

What it is not:
An app from a content provider exercising a rigid QA on the video material, knowing that this is the only service he can offer making you stay with him instead with whatevertube in the long run.

What is bad:
Imho the visual or technical quality of most real world vids is quite underwhelming. Depending on the vid you have areal artefacts (probably where the camera hardware has been), blurry scenes, the scaling changes multiple times in a single video, you hover above the ground or the ground is up to your knees, you can see stitching artefacts, low frame rates or they throw classical cinematic cutting effects in your face.

What annoys me most is that you effectively not allowed to move while watching. When you move your head the video shifts with it. This is even true when you sit very straight and just turn your head (probably because you don't turn your head around the centre of the display but around the centre of your head).

What is good:
There are two computer generated videos. When your are curious, start by watching âEvolution of Verseâ. When the quality of this one is not up to your standards, you may safely exit this application at once. In case you want more I recommend you ask a friend to duct tape you to the armrests of your chair and start watching âCatatonicâ.

The application itself is basically a big menu, letting you choose the vid you want to watch. It also gives the you the option to stream or download stuff. You can queue multiple downloads.
The setting for this a lake (here it is safe to move your head).
The app itself just works.

Bottom line:
It's free and it has no ads. Giving thumbs down to free stuff just feels not right.
