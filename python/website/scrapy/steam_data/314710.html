Alright, so. I usually only make a funny or ironic review for games I really enjoy. However, with all the backlash this game has been getting, I wanted to make an honest review. Not an excuse to bash comcept or Inafune, but a real review of the game. While long, I plan to explain the mindset I had going into the game, the things I enjoy about the game/rebuttal to some criticisms of the game, what I didn't like about the game, then a closing set of final thoughts. So, let's begin.

MINDSET

I did not back this game. I found out about it about three days after the backing time closed, and as such, didn't get the chance to. I've been a fan of Mega Man for years, so to hear about a spiritual successor made me extremely excited. As such, I have been following it almost since the kickstarter and seen its ups and downs. I will admit, not backing meant that the delays and such didn't sting as much, but that didn't mean I wasn't still disappointed. I did, however, pre-order the game. I also subscribed to /r/MightyNo9. These two helped me keep informed of all the delays even moreso. This also led to me seeing some of the insane acidity this game dealt with, especially the last few months. /r/MightyNo9 became a place for people to circlejerk around their complaints about the delays, about their problems with how the game looks, and other general issues. This honestly confused me, as I didn't see any major problems with the 2.5D art style, though it was different from the one originally shown in the kickstarter. I also didn't mind the delays, as I hadn't expected it to be on time. They were releasing a game much larger than originally expected on 10 separate systems simeltaneously. Delays were inevitable. So when release day came around, after seeing all the negative reviews, I'll admit, I had more positive bias than negative. Take that for what you will.

PROS/REBUTTALS

-"Controls are floaty": Not really. In certain areas, such as Aviator's stage, they seems so, but that's due to the stage itself moreso than the controls. I found them to be rock-solid
-"Level design is â¥â¥â¥â¥": Again, not really. It is hard, for sure, and there are a couple platforms that I'd label almost unduly so, but overall, its pretty good actually.
-"It looks like crap": Personal opinion. I'm of the opinion that it actually looks okay. I've seen much worse and I'll admit it isn't the best, but I like it.
-"The voice acting is crap": For some people, I'd agree. One good example is Dr. Sanda. His voice actor is dumb. But others had some pretty solid performances, such as Dr. Blackwell and Dr. White. I thought both were believable and sincere.
Now for some actual Pro's:
-The dash mechanic. It makes for really interesting battles and makes the game very fast-paced, almost feeling like Mega Man X more than the classic series.
-Brandish's Weapon. The addition of this weapon allowing for an entirely different game feel essentially was amazing to me. The game goes from a Mega Man-esque game to a Mega Man Z or ZX or even a Strider-esque game. I ended up using it most of the game because it, when coupled with the dash, made the game very fast-paced and very fun to me.
-The Powerups. This was a strong point for me. These, while not making a ton of difference at first glance, could make the game quite a bit harder or easier, depending on whether you were fast enough to still have them. They also push for the player to not lollygag and go faster.
-The Music. Especially the main theme, all of the music was tracks that were not only reminicent of the classic themes, but also just good music. Unlike the uber-atmospheric music of Skyrim, these were themes I would actually like to hear outside of the game.

CONS

-The Insta-Death Pits. These are something that plagues many modern platformers today and, for me, simply break up the gameplay and make it more disjointed and cheap. They work in Super Meat Boy because everything is a one-hit death, but in games like Shovel Knight and Mighty No 9, they just feel unhelpful and cheap.
-The acidic community about this game. Undertale's community is a circlejerk around its success. Mighty No 9's is a circlejerk around its failure. If we don't get to see a followup, mark my words, it will be because of them.

FINAL THOUGHTS

It is a pretty good and fun game. Is it amazing? No. Is it horrible? No. Is it a true successor to Mega Man? Kinda. But I feel like you'll lose something by going in with that mindset. Would I reccommend it? Yes, actually. Its flawed, but still a fun game. I'd still reccommend Shovel Knight first, but that doesn't mean its bad by any stretch. If I had to give a number to it, I'd say about a 7/10. It isn't perfect, but its fun. And as far as I'm concerned, thats what matters. Not the release date or the cost of the kickstarter.
