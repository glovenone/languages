About This Game



"ROGUS - Kingdom of The Lost Souls" is the first Run'Em'Up: A minimalist, yet, epic, beat'em'up where your skills will be put to the test as you jump, aim, dodge, shield and cast spells, all at the same time.

Guide an insanely powerful wizard in his hardcore and infinite quest for love & redemption.


“Deep down under the mountain something has awakened and legions of monsters now pour out in the Kingdom.You are the chosen wizard, protector of the realm, sent to battle them.”
And somewhere, in his beer, an old man is crying.

FEATURES
  • Monster smashing pixel art action!
  • Fully playable introduction story
  • Awesome epic music
  • Six spells to choose from
  • 20 Steam Achievements
  • On-the-fly randomized runs
  • Controller Support
  • Best Score board
  • Kittens and Unicorns not included

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 30 June, 2015
Summary:

This key was obtained via the developer.

I do think that the concept while simple can work, but even with the low cost of admission and a basic but workable concept, the lack of variety shows up rather quickly and hurts the game too much to recommend at this time. Games can be simple and be effective, but the simple nature here is countered by the over reliance on reflex mechanics, and a lack of enemy variety and stage variety means that after 30 minutes, the game will start boring you a bit. Some rules in the game don't make a lot of sense, and you'll have a sense of frustration set in rather quickly. It's hard, but not in the good way in my opinion. Also, quick restart buttons.

Gameplay Footage and Video First Impressions: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ii2jEi9yu5M

Lists:

+:
  • Some good pixel art, in particular with the wood creature.
  • At the core, the simple playstyle can be interesting and more of a reflex based game.

-:
  • The lack of variety is core to the problem here. No variety in enemies and a lack of variety in background/spells to cast makes it so that the game starts to wear on you rather quickly.
  • The reliance on reflex mechanics is too high, as there's barely any room to fail in terms of the high speed of projectiles. While some will consider this a strength, with the randomization of enemy placement, this can lead to no win scenarios.
  • The powers variety in particular is a problem, there's other mechanics that could have been chosen that really could have been interesting, but there's repetition here.
  • Some unnecessary rules leads to a lack of user mechanics, which is a shame because it really was unnecessary.
  • While there is controller support, the necessity of the keyboard/mouse becomes apparent with having to go through menus with the keyboard, for example.
