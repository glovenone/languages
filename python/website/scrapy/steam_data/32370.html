About This Game

Note: Mac version only supports English language

Choose Your Path.
It is four thousand years before the Galactic Empire and hundreds of Jedi Knights have fallen in battle against the ruthless Sith. You are the last hope of the Jedi Order. Can you master the awesome power of the Force on your quest to save the Republic? Or will you fall to the lure of the dark side? Hero or villain, saviour or conqueror... you alone will determine the destiny of the entire galaxy!
  • A brand new Star Wars role-playing experience with unique characters, creatures, vehicles and planets.
  • Learn to use the Force with over 40 different powers and build your own lightsaber.
  • Adventure through some of the most popular Star Wars locations, including Tatooine and the Wookiee homeworld Kashyyyk.
  • Choose your party from nine customisable characters, including Twi'leks, droids and Wookiees.
  • Travel to eight enormous worlds in your own starship, the Ebon Hawk.

This is an amazing game but until Bioware gets off their lazy @sses and brings it up to date, I don't recommend buying it. The major problem with it is that ALL modern computers use widescreen aspect ratios but the settings for this game don't offer any ratios even close to 16:9, so you're forced to play in a more narrow ratio (which only utilizes about half your monitor's resolution). There is a 3rd party program called flawless widescreen that fixes this issue it but it is very buggy program and usually doesn't work well. The problem is that Bioware basically abandoned the game. If they simply went back in and added the 16:9 ratio resolutions (e.g. 1600x900, 1920x1080, etc.) it would look and run amazing on almost any modern PC, and because it's such a great game they would have a ton of happy customers recommending it.

The fact that a simple, free program like flawless widescreen (when it works) fixes the aspect ratio problem on this game and many others suggests that this is a VERY MINOR tweak that the developers could make that would bring a 10+ year old game up to date almost instantly. My point is that if the game developers would simply keep their games up to date for more than a couple of years that many great classics (KOTOR included) could have a significantly longer shelf life. I'm not looking for updated textures or a different game engine (both would take much longer to update) but simply adding in the most common modern resolutions, even 10 years after the game was released, should be a no brainer. The fact that someone (don't know if it was Bioware or not) ported it to Android with the native widescreen ratio also suggests that this shouldn't be a difficult problem to fix. Until it is, I definitely won't be playing it and wouldn't recommend it to anyone else either.

The Best Star Wars RPG out there. 10/10
This is one of the best RPGs I've ever played. This is probably the best Star Wars game ever made, and anyone who likes RPGs or Star Wars should play it. Even today, despite its age, this game is still amazing. The graphics are dated sadly, but that shouldn't put anyone off.

The overall storyline will mainly be the same, but everyone is likely to have their own experiences with this game. You get to choose your actions, and what your character says, and everything you do will guide you torwards the light or the dark side. There is so much satisfaction by the end of the game whether you choose one side or not, and the side that you do choose will affect the ending. The story is where the real experience is, there are so much likeable characters, there are side stories among the main stories that give yoiu more if you invest in them. The story also contains a lot of twists and shocking moments throughout.

There is so much content to this game, examples of this are the main story, side missions, Bounties, character backstories, swoop races, gambling and more. My playthrough of this game lasted about 30 hours, not completing every task.

I loved the characters in this story, you have a powerful enemy, and come across a variety of colourful side characters in the storyline. You recruit unique characters into your team as you progress, a Jedi, a Chewbacca, an Assasin Droid, a Utility Droid, a Bounty Hunter, Alien species and more. You will need to choose which ones you are bringing with you on each mission, and sometimes that isn't easy.

The combat was great, and enjoyable. I guess it can be a get easy when you're the correct level. You get to create your own Lightsaver which is fantastic, which can then be upgraded. I loved my Green Double Bladed Lightsaver. You equip weapons, and when enemies spot you, auto combat will begin, you can switch between controlling yourself and allies. As the combat occurs, you can choose a skill/attack for your characters turn. Its a mix of real time action with turned based combat.

You can unlock force abilities, upgrade stats of your team members, purchase droid teammates upgrades, purchase armor/weapons for you and team members. There are a lot of different weapons for you to find, and set to your team members. There are a lot of options to play how you want.
