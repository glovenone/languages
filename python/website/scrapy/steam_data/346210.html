About This Game

A serious menace has approached the gentle and snug garden, where various plants have been quietly living - masses of voracious insects rushed to it to fill their maws. The green defenders had to lay aside their everyday jobs to save their tiny motherland. But who will command the defense, who will lead them in action? Head this little army of garden defenders, rescue them from impending doom!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, Windows Vista, Windows 7, Windows 8
    • Processor: 1000 Mhz
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: 128
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 115 MB available space

In this game you will have to fight against hordes of insects that are trying to demolish your garden. In this battle you will stand shoulder to shoulder with the plants living in the garden. Earn coins, upgrade your garden, place more plants, it's all in your hands alone!

Garden Rescue is a cute and colorful tower defense game in which you use defensive plants to protect money-making strawberry plants from invading pests. Sound sort of familiar? Basically, it's Plants vs. Zombies without the zombies, and with more conventional tower defense level design (i.e. winding pathways).

There is nothing revolutionary here, but the game is really cheap and can be picked up for even less during sales or in bundles. It would be a good game for kids or casual players since it isn't difficult or gory, but if formulaic tower defense isn't your thing then you should probably just skip this one.

One bonus, the Steam achievements can be tracked via the in-game trophy room, which will tell you how far along you are towards hitting each of the requirements. Widescreen support is also a plus.

This is a decent but generic path-based grid-placement tower defense. There are very little PC options, but they aren't really needed to enjoy it. I 3-starred all 30 levels and unlocked all achievements in 9 hours of playtime, and probably a quarter of that was AFK. You start with like 2 towers, and have to unlock the rest by playing through the levels. There is no story at all. Just kill bugs with plants and collect gold that appears out of strawberries.

There is very little tower interaction with each other, although there is one buff tower (which doesn't tell you if it buffs speed, damage, or just makes a glowy animation). There is very little description of what a tower does before you place it, no description of how much benefit you get from the two upgrades each tower has, and no description of how much damage a tower does in raw numbers or DPS.

In my opinion, all of those are very important to make a good tower defense game. As is, its all guesswork. The levels with 3 paths, and it auto gives you towers are basically puzzles you have to start over several times to get right. You don't know the best placement since it doesn't tell you what else you will get or where the next wave comes from. If you don't want to trial-and-error it, then just watch a YouTube video of someone playing the level and match their placement. Useless levels really.

I found the cactus to be the best tower most of the time, since it hits ground and air targets, and each upgrade adds an additional target it can hit. The flame tower and the bomb tower do great damage and shouldn't be overlooked. Placing plenty of slowing towers before your kill zones is recommended.

I recommend it only if you like casual level tower defense games and get it in a bundle or for a cheap price. Don't come in expecting Bloons or Defense Grid quality, but its decent.
