About This Game

Welcome to Paperland!

Dive into the world of Paper Monsters Recut and watch a living world made out of paper, cardboard, and even a few cotton balls unfold right before your eyes.
Paper Monsters Recut mixes classic 2D platformer gameplay with gorgeous 3D graphics and brings it to life in a brand new title for PC.

--FEATURES--
**Xbox style controller highly reccomended - there's a lot of button mashing going on!

• Classic 2D platformer gameplay with gorgeous 3d environments.
• An adorable cast of fun, original characters.
• Tight responsive controls, with super-speed and double jump!
• Old school-style playable overworld, with plenty levels and secrets to unlock along the way.
• Awesome power ups including jetpacks, submarines, lasers and more!
• Collect special treasures to unlock bonus high score mini games.
• Awesome original soundtrack.
• Full controller support.
• Fun for gamers of any age.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 SP 1 or higher (works with latest 8.1)
    • Processor: Dual Core 2GHz CPU Intel or AMD
    • Memory: 4 MB RAM
    • Graphics: GeForce 400 Series or Radeon 6000 Series, 512MB graphics memory
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX Compatible

Posted: 18 July, 2015
“Do not reinvent the wheel” is something that the creators of Papers Monsters Recut has taken to heart. The game is essentially a combination of Super Mario platforming gimmicks with the art style and music of LittleBigPlanet with the storyline of Sonic.

Incidentally from Mario:
  • in each stage, there are three big collectables you can get
  • there is an overworld that links the stages
  • 100 buttons makes a life
  • grassland, water land, ice land, lava land, etc.
  • you stomp on enemies to remove them
  • … and MORE!


The near non-existant storyline is that Dr. Robotnik Bowser? is turning innocent wildlife into monsters and Sonic the nameless box protagonist is going to stop him.

Furthermore the game has a lot of bugs which makes it very frustrating to play. In the short time I’ve played this game, I’ve encountered numerous bugs which demonstrates a lack of testing. The most popular being the sudden absence of the health bar which renders your character immune to death. Several times, the protagonist will be able to pass through pipes (the warp area) and end up in the void. I’ve also encountered a bug where the stage wouldn’t end because the game wouldn’t recognize that I was in the end area.

So really the only cutting that the creators of Papers Monsters Recut have done were the corners of ingenuity, originality, and game design.
