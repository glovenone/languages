About This Game

Knight & Damsel is a two-player "uncooperative" multiplayer game set on a papercraft stage that subverts the age-old tale of knights saving damsels, with the knight and damsel competing to rescue the other!

In Knight & Damsel, players attempt to delay their opponent so their chosen hero can receive the most glory by getting the furthest through the stage. They do this by throwing blocks on to their opponents screen, warping the fourth wall between 2D screen and world space. Blocks have the ability to delay or knock back their opponent, with snow avalanches to pursuing ghosts at hand to ensure you can get the lead in the race.

Compete as Knight and Damsel across four stages in the campaign mode, or simply kick back and enjoy the full selection of blocks via the arcade mode. Knight & Damsel offers a competitive twist on the Damsel in Distress trope, fun for all ages.

Knight & Damsel takes a unique spin on couch co-op and makes it... less than friendly. In this very inspired game of "tug-of-war," you and a friend compete to clear as many rooms as possible in order to save the other person. While playing, your actions directly affect what is happening on the other player's screen. Knock a plant from your view? It drops down and overgrows on your opponent's screen. Boot a cannon out of your way? It will fly to the top of the screen and shoot a cannonball to knock your savior from their path.

The idea behind having both players trying to rescue each other is both endearing, and works really well with this game. The game screens are split up nicely, giving each player and adequate view of what is happening both on their own screen, and their opponent's screen. Coupled with the absolutely breathtaking visuals, and this game is worth getting the support of the developers to build more!

My absolute only gripe about this game is how short it is! My girlfriend and I completed the cooperative part of this game in just under 15 minutes, and due to the limited environments and props, the replay value is small. I'd love to see the developer revisit this game and expand on what could be a game that is picked up any time you have a get together. $10 might be a bit too steep for how short the game is, but if you catch it on sale, I would definitely recommend it.

As the developers like to put it, Knight & Damsel is an uncooperative game. Knight & Damsel is essentially a competitive puzzle game. Both of the players (two players required) are trying to be the player that ends up rescuing the other player. To do this players push, climb or destroy the obstacles in their way. Some of the hazards can also be moved onto the path of the other player in order to slow them down.

I had fun with Knight & Damsel. The gameplay is interesting if you have another person to play the game with. The game is easy to pick up so it could work for people that don't play a lot of games. The hazards do tend to create a runaway winner problem though since one hazard leads into another hazard and so on. While fun Knight & Damsel is the type of game that is best in short doses instead of long playthroughs. I think it would work best in 15-30 minute sessions.

Knight & Damsel is also really charming.

If you don't like these type of puzzle games or don't have anyone to play the game with, I don't think you will enjoy Kinght & Damsel. If you like the theme, have someone else to play with, and the gameplay looks interesting I think you will enjoy Knight & Damsel.
