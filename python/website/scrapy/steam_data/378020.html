Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We chose early access as it was the best way to release the game. We know this isn't a call of duty or any AAA first person shooter therefore we thought it would be unreasonable to ship the game at full release without improving it. Early access will enable us to fulfill our vision and concepts.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The game will be in early access for as long as possible to make the game to our standard. We will be delivering content updates and gameplay updates to insure that you have a fun experience.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We're planning at launch to have over 20 maps that are vastly different and we will have various other modes that are unique.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is lacking art assets but we have a few 3d artists on the case.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The plan is that we will keep the price in the same price region.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will be creating polls for the community to enable us to know what to add into the game.”

About This Game

Use your lightning skill and pace to outwit your enemies. Challenge other players in a deathmatch, team deathmatch or even capture the flag. The game is a cyberpunk themed first person shooter, it uses smooth and easy to grasp parkour to enable players to flank other players and much more. While playing, it's probable that time will change which effects every single aspect of the game. The game has three time periods: 1940s, 2015 and 2070. This forces players to adapt to new weapons and change tactics; the time periods each have different mechanics and layouts. Protocol brings together modern and WW2 shooters to create a fulfilling experience.

  • Pure first person fun.
  • Parkour.
  • Destructible buildings.
  • A large amount of maps available.
  • Dedicated servers.
  • Soundtrack by Matthew A.C. Cohen.
  • Game altering time periods.

Review Update 10.10.2015

After waiting 1 whole month, i decided to report the game as being broken for one reason:
It is online only, means without masterserver connection, you can't play the game, you'll only see the main menu and are able to click options, that's all.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=532733801

Please do not waste your money on this one, it doesn't allow you to play!

Here it comes, a short and basic review but you should treat it more like an overview

At first the features that are not included yet, despite being advertised:
- Destructible buildings. (Couldn't find anything that could have been destroyed, wasting several ammo rounds on different buildings)
- A large amount of maps available. (There is 1)
- Game altering time periods. (There is only 1 too and definitely not 2070!)

Pro's
- Parkour, basic animations and functions like climbing, jumping, sliding, there is crouching too
- Dedicated server
- Performance isn't bad (but the game not that demanding either) maybe because there are no textures anywhere

Con's
- No game modes
- There are no players, you spawn alone because nobody is playing the game, there aren't any bots, only you and your shadow
- There is only 1 (one) (!) map right now, the City
- Picked up ammo respawns every 15 seconds, so you can pick it up again, stand there all day long collecting ammo, until the round is over because nothing else can be done yet
- Every time you spawn you'll have 10% health remaining, the funny thing is, there is no health spawns anywhere on the map so you could heal and where are the other 90%?
- There is a main menu where you can set up basic stuff but once in the game, you can't even get out anymore because there is no Exit button anywhere! You have to close/force close the whole game and restart it to get into another match
- Graphic settings don't do anything, except the Resolution and Fullscreen toggle. Means AA, Dynamic Adaption, not even the FPS counter work.
- They removed 3rd person view for some unknown reason from the game, you can see it featured in the store video, right? The output_log.txt from the engine gets spammed with that info and can easily grow to 3 MB in size after a single game, which made the game crash for me upon exit
- Only 3 weapon types, a pistol, a automatic rifle and a shotgun, that's it
- You don't take any damage from falling, there the 10% health make sense, somehow
- Shadow only applies to the player, the equipped weapon is missing in the shadow?

Also, did i mention already? There are no other players anywhere, you spawn completely alone most of the time, i never met another player yet!

I don't know what i have done in this game, pretty much nothing.

But i have a answer to the question i asked myself after installing the game, the company behind it has the name Prototype Games for this very reason it seems, they make prototype games and that's all what you will see here right now, a prototype, nothing more.

What it really needs, ASAP
- Bots should be included, so you could play without real people joining into a map (and then it is not totally wasted to rent a dedicated server where nobody is playing on)
- More equipment would be nice (grenades ?, smoke bombs ?)
- Needs more maps
- Game needs textures
- Proper ingame menu required

It needs a lot of work but the most important thing may be a proper Exit button for the game, people will need and use it, i'd assume.

Anyway, i don't really see this game going anywhere anytime soon and it never should have been put up on the store for sale because there is nothing in the game right now that justifies others paying money for it and 9â¬ for nothing more than a empty map is TOO MUCH.
