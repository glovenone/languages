About This Game

Set 150 years before the events of BlazBlue: Calamity Trigger, XBlaze Code: Embryo is a visual novel that follows Touya Kagari, a high school student whose world is turned upside down when he is attacked by a seemingly otherworldly being and saved by a mysterious girl named Es. Delve deeper into the BlazBlue mythos and learn about events prior to Ragna the Bloodedge’s epic journey!

To those unfamiliar with Blazblue but have no interest in playing those games:
Do not let that disuade you from picking up this VN, as that familiarity is definitely not necessary to enjoy this VN. I would definitely recommend this one if you were ever curious about the BB franchise but just aren't into fighters, but it is a fine story that can stand on its own as well.

To those familiar with Blazblue but are unsure whether to pick this up:
To go even further, I completely played through the story of the first three core Blazblue games (CT, CS, and CP) and this story actually cleared up a lot of what I always thought was not clearly explained in those games. I love the BB series but its lore can be a little overwhelming to understand completely (if even possible) and this VN can double as a good primer for those games instead of just as a prequel. Also, one of the characters in XBlaze will be playable and a have a pretty significant role in Centralfiction's plot, so there's that.

Some other details for everyone:
This VN is not a linear story, and there are multiple endings. You arrive at these different endings by whether you read particular articles in Toi, an in-game phone app. The game does tell you from the very beginning that your choice of reading will change the story's outcome, but how is somewhat ambiguous until you play through the story at least once.

The game's text is available in both English and Japanese, but the speech is limited to Japanese. The translation to English for the most part is pretty good and certainly much better than a lot of translated anime and video games (including the main Blazblue games). There were a few discrepancies here and there, but I only recall one that I felt was significant (in terms of how it characterized that character).

I would recommend picking it up if the store's description of the game piques your interest, On a personal note, I am normally not very interested in visual novel games, but this one held my attention throughout and admittedly produced some major pudding-related feels at certain moments. Give it a try.

XBlaze Code: Embryo is a game set 150 years before the events of Blazblue Calamity Trigger, serving to explain some of the lore behind the game, Don't worry if you have not played the main series, this game does a good enough job of explaining most of the things. I think one of the great things about how they went about doing it is that lore is first introduced then explained (such as Izayoi and its curse). This gives some satisfaction to those who are fans of the series as well as not alienating the new comers.

I have been a great fan of the game, ever since I saw a preview of the first game, Blazblue: Calamity Trigger, in a magazine. This game was great in helping me answer some questions (the origins of Takamagahara) as well as providing me with more questions for this already confusing lore.

The art is not that amazing. It looks abit pixlated and stretched on my monitor. However, the music featured was nice, some of the tracks even paying hommage to the blazblue tracks. Moreover, the game play was interesting. As a visual novel, the game allows you to "make desicions" through reading articles. The type and amount of articles you read affect the decisions made by the characters, and by extension, the type of ending that you would get. Lastly, the story was actually quite cliche. If you are a long time anime viewer, the plot would not surprise you one bit. Before events happened, I had already kinda predicted what was about to occur.

The major turn off of this game for me was the main character. Did you watch the anime Gulity Crown? The MC is almost exactly like Shu , Season 1 Shu. He is seriously irritating. He is the kind of character which I know would die first if he was not MC.

All in all, I had a great time playing this game. I recommend this game to any Blazblue fan out there.

"XBlaze! Activate!" (Updated Review as of 7/6/16)

If you ever thought to find your normal everyday life wasn't normal, then you've come to the right place, we start out our story of the Azure/Blue/etc. with Touya Kagari, a normal High School student with his pals Hinata and Akira, but when Touya comes to a place of remembrance, he meets a mysterious girl by the name of Es, and the two start their journeys together.

As it is, XBlaze is the prequel story to the popular fighting game series "BlazBlue", the lore you'll seek from BlazBlue will also be mentioned here as well, including some characters within similar familiar names and other viable info. Originally available on the PS3/PSV, the Steam release is more or less the same as it is, with a few PC options to tweak to your style, along with the option to have the subtitles either in full English or Japanese. The game is pure Visual Novel, so the game relies on its own system called the "Toi" (which also has Character Bios), using it wisely will decide your fate, from getting the good, bad, or even the gag endings. Using "Tips" will let you know on info of the XBlaze world, though some of it will differ depending on which character route you're planning to clear.

Either way, the port is great, and it loads scenes and such faster than the console version (also, bonus extras if you happen to clear all routes in the game), but yes, as usual with majority PC players, there will be problems with people trying to run it, it happens to some, but not all, but it happens. That's what there's the forums/discussions are they for people to check out the issues and for users that can help out on it if at all possible.

As of 3/3/16, a 9.1MB Patch/Update/Etc. has fixed some of the game's issues as I've originally mentioned before, that being the A.O.S. crash, the half and half localization errors, and probably crashes when trying to continue certain chapters/routes. There still might be some bugs in there, but will be known whenever necessary.

As of 7/6/16, Aksys Games announced at Anime Expo that XBlaze Lost: Memories will now get its steam port, so yeah! \^^/
