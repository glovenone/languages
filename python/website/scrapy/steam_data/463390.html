20 April

Steam Release

Hello there everyone, and thank you for checking out One Thousand Lies.

For those who don't know, One Thousand Lies is an indie kinetic visual novel I've been working on for quite some time. It was released the 28th of March on Itch.io (https://keinart.itch.io/one-thousand-lies) and since then a lot of people told me to try to go for a Steam release, and here we are. The game is currently waiting approval from the gods above and if everything goes well it will be released this 25th of April. If it doesn't it may be delayed just a bit, a week or so.

So what can you expect from One Thousand Lies? Well if you haven't read it yet you are probably thinking this looks like your generic school japanese-wannabe visual novel some NEET otaku made in his free time. To be honest, you are probably not so mistaken about the last part, but OTL is more than that. It's not your average "romance", you won't be seeing any "I hope senpai will notice me" neither you need to know your anime tropes because everything follow an European setting.

What I'm hoping with this novel is to give some messages, this novel contains some psychology and philosophical themes that I've not seen anywhere else so I kinda wanted to tackle them, but at the same time I didn't want to be the usual pretentious author thinking he's above everyone else. At the end, even if you don't pay attention to all these "deep conversations" you will get a funny comedy full of crazy moments, some nice characters, and, why not saying it, some waifus too.

I tried to make sure to make original humor and not just follow the memes like the trend that seems to go these days, and although the novel contain a lot of references for all kind of things, they are put there in a way you don't really need to understand them to get the joke.

In any case, it's around 7 hours long, and for free. So why don't you check it out? Follow it now so you can download it once it's released.

Thank you for your time and I hope you all enjoy reading One Thousand Lies.

PROS: One Thousand Lies is probably the most well-written free Visual Novel on steam currently. The character art is beautiful, there are big laughs, and it is easy to grow fond of the characters. Everyone defies stereotype and Information is piped to you in a digestable fashion, making this easy to pick up and put down as needed without getting confused. Music is A+.

CONS: I would've liked to see more CGs, and more character frames. For how lengthy this one is, the same graphics get a little tiring. The backgrounds are also filtered photos, which I've never been fond of. I would've liked to see more characters in the world itself as well. The end doesn't knock the ball out of the park like I know the author is capable of, but it at least feels definitive and their idea feels fully realized.

WORTH: One Thousand Lies is pretty long! If you've got time to sink into a story, I'd say it's definitely worth your while for the sweet price of free.

OVERALL: I knew the moment I booted this game and saw the game's style, I stumbled onto something special. I am absolutely not a fan of kinetic VNs. I figure if I want a linear story, I can go read a book. This story though, this dialogue, and the amount of hours you get out of it: It transcends my preferences and delivers one hell of a cute, fun ride.

SCORE:9/10

One Thousand Lies is a free kinetic novel, so I thought I would pick it up for the (easy-to-get) achievement. However that eventually became secondary, as the story gave a very lasting impression on its own merits.

It starts off slow, and sometimes launches into large amounts of exposition when the characters were discussing their perspectives. The extensive vocabulary used was sometimes difficult to follow, and I was tempted to skip them and advance the plot, but fortunately did not as they provide insight into each characters' personalities and allow the reader to be emotionally invested in them, setting the context for the later parts of the story. Puns, hidden meanings and references also kept the dialogue entertaining and helped offset the pace somewhat. The music helped to set the tone for the scenes in the prologue but I turned them off afterwards and therefore cannot comment any further.

Actually reading the text will clue the reader in to hints throughout the novel that there is something else behind the narrated events. The overarching story is an adaptation of Greek mythology (Pygmalion), and the protagonist also did a bit of "foreshadowing" (quotes needed), which was explored towards the end.

The conclusion was not satisfying as it still left several loose ends unresolved and raised a few questions, so be sure to read the extra chapter that is unlocked afterwards (which is required for the achievement anyway). However, I had to head to the discussions for the final "truth" behind the story and to fill in certain gaps I had while reading (I did not complete the novel in one sitting).

One Thousand Lies is a well-written story that is almost certain to give some "feels" upon completion. Not requiring any monetary sacrifice makes the novel much more accessible, so you should read it if you like plot twists, humour and mind♥♥♥♥s, and have a few hours to properly give the attention it deserves.
