Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“While Automobilista is already at a very advanced stage of development, there are still some final pieces of content to be added to complete what is a very extensive package. The new simulation features and community tools will in turn benefit from testing with the broader user base we intend to gather during Early Access, not only from new comers but also everyone that purchased Stock Car Extreme before January 6th who will be getting Automobilista free of charge.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We estimate Automobilista will have a relatively short Early Access stage, in between 30-60 days before full release.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“While the content base is largely complete, a couple of remaining new cars and tracks are still in development will be added to the base package during Early Access stage, along with further fine tuning of our large roster of cars and tracks.

New core simulation features and gameplay options are also in development and will be added over the course of Early Access.

Community-based features such as online leaderboards and the Steam-based matchmaker, along with new tools to enhance both online and single player competitions will be introduced and developed along Early Access stage.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is currently an advanced beta stage and 90% complete.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes - we will begin early access 20% off the full release pricing, and gradually reduce that discount as we approach the full release.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Since Automobilista is free to everyone who purchased our previous title Stock Car Extreme before January 6th 2016, it should provide us a broader testing base for the development of our new Steam-based Matchmaker and Leaderboard systems, in order to make sure that upon release Automobilista is firing on all cylinders!”

1 July

Automobilista EA v0.9.67 RELEASED

V0.9.67 is now out - this is mainly a hotfix to address some issues introduced with the new track limit rules.

Our apologies to anyone who may have had Multiplayer races compromised this past week by issues with detection on pit entry. Thanks to your feedback we have managed to make quick progress fine-tuning the system and adding some new options, along with some other adjustments also included in this release.

Here is the changelog:

  • Added TRACK LIMIT RULES on/off UI switch to RULES menu (valid for single player only)
  • Adjusted track limit corridors in all tracks
  • Exceeding track limits in race conditions now awards a drive-thru (rather than Stop/Go)
  • Added driving aid weight penalties to all cars (to compensate for potential performance advantages when using aids other than the ones built-in to any given car)
  • Small overall tweak to AI draft stickiness to make them a bit less hesitant to move out of the draft for a pass;
  • Revised rear wing aero maps for Marcas, StockV8, Montana, (leading to less rear wing downforce in lower settings relative to previou versions); minor aero adjustments to Metalmoro, F3, Lancer Cup, Mini, Opala 86 cars
  • DynHUD: Fixed error causing Editor not to work
  • VIR: Tweaked trackside TV cameras
  • Granja: Added missing tire barriers
  • Marcas: Added onboard adjustable anti-roll bars
  • Montana: Adjusted damper rates

About This Game

AUTOMOBILISTA puts you in the driving seat of one of the most advanced racing simulators and delivers a uniquely diverse motorsports experience!

As the natural successor to Reiza Studios´ previous title - the highly regarded Stock Car Extreme, originally released in 2013 - AUTOMOBILISTA continues to simulate the premium Brazilian racing series such as Stock Car V8, Copa Petrobras de Marcas, Formula 3 Brasil and Mitsubishi Lancer Cup, now packing every car and every track from their respective 2015 championships.

Beyond the brazilian core, AUTOMOBILISTA is extensively complemented with one of the most diverse selections or racing vehicles you will find in a racing game: only in AUTOMOBILISTA will you be able to jump from a rental kart to a 800-HP single-seater; from drifting a Rallycross EvoX to jumping ramps in a Supertruck; from mastering the driving basics in a Formula Vee to managing a full 24h race in an endurance prototype; back and forth through historical and modern content featuring a sample from several decades of racing history - all of this and much more within a few mouse clicks. Thoroughly researched and cohesively developed for maximum simulation accuracy, AUTOMOBILISTA captures the fun only motorsports in its various forms can provide.

In addition to the content already present in Stock Car Extreme - all of which comprehensively upgraded - AUTOMOBILISTA turns it up to a new level by adding all the new cars & tracks proposed in the SimRacing Bonanza crowdfunding campaign, new simulation features such as dynamic track conditions, advanced transmission and tire modelling, substantial upgrades to physics, graphics & audio, new game modes and much more!


    These are some of the features that make AUTOMOBILISTA stand out as a purebred racing simulator:
  • Upscaled 720 Hz physics and 500 Hz input rates grants AMS super-accurate, high fidelity physics and Force Feedback
  • Vehicle physics based on models developed for professional racing teams with in-depth exclusive data
  • Minutely detailed and accurately modelled race tracks with 3D bumps, custom road properties and air density according to its location and altitude
  • Engine sounds sampled from high-quality recordings of real cars
  • Capturing the ever-changing demands of racing.
  • Cars with old-style gearbox mechanisms will demand proper gear engagement by using the clutch or synchronizing revs.
  • Modern cars will demand handling of advanced electronic systems.
  • High downforce cars will create an aerodynamic wake for those that follow.
  • 24h cycles will allow racing from dusk to dawn and at night.
  • Dynamic track conditions turn the racing line into a grippy rubbery trail and builds up marbles offline
  • Dynamic tire damage & dirt pickup add further extra dimensions to the driving experience

My experience... I have been driving Grand Prix Legends since it was released in 1998. I have tried iRacing, fFactor 2, and Assetto Corsa. In my opinion Automobilista is a more realistic driving experience than all of them. From my first session on track I was convinced. Now that I've spent some more time dialing things in, I'm hooked. When you first start out, be sure to take the time to peruse the "user guide". The physics and ffb had me convinced immediately but the graphics were washed out. After reading the "user guide" on configuring Nvidia/ATI graphics cards and applying the recommended settings, the graphics are very good. The ffb is the best I have experienced. I'm still using my old faithful Logitech Momo Red wheel and an even older, modded, set of Thrustmaster pedals with a Flexiforce load sensor on the brake. I was going to test drive Project Cars but there's no need. I am quite happy with Automobilista. I would recommend Automobilista to anyone who is looking for a sim with realistic handling and feedback. If you are looking for more of an arcade experience, there is nothing to see here.

Some thoughts on:

iRacing... I quite liked iRacing but for me the ffb was not very good. A bit like driving underwater. I tried different settings but never found anything I was satisfied with. That said; I would stay with iRacing just for the sheer number of online races that are available, except for the cost. Ongoing subscription and then track and car purchases to move up the ranks are too much for me.

rfactor 2... Great graphics but physics and ffb just seem unrealistic to me.

Assetto Corsa... I thought the graphics in rfactor 2 were better on my 32" monitor. AC was too sparkly and I was unable to adjust to eliminate the sparkles. And again the physics and ffb weren't realistic to me.

Final thought... of course all of this is subjective and may largely depend on your rig.

Early Access Review
Posted: 4 July
Automobilista is, in my opinion, the best (single player) racing sim on the market right now. With many many hours clocked on Assetto Corsa, rFactor 2, GT Legends, and a couple on Project Cars, I have to say that Automobilista tops them all. From it's physics to it's car/track lineup to it's modding, this game always keeps me coming back to it for more. While it does need some work in some areas, I feel that the whole package is the most fun and rewarding experience currently in simracing.

Physics - Making Sense of Madness
The physics in this game are some of the most believable out there. They feel just like rFactor 2, which is extremely high praise. The grip is there when it should be there and not there when it shouldn't be. It's definitely easy to put down the power too quickly or break too hard and spin, but it almost always feels catchable and it always makes sense. The road rubbers up nice and the racing line feels very dynamic. Tire and brake temps are extremely important and massively affect how the car handles. Brakes can be locked up, but don't feel like they are locking up too easily. Unlike Assetto Corsa, damage actually seems to affect how the car drives, rather than just how it looks, from losing downforce to wrecking the steering. The only thing the game physics are lacking are proper turbo modelling, like Assetto Corsa has, however the devs have confirmed that is coming.

Graphics - There to Check a Box
The graphics in this game are nothing special. Yes, the game runs on an extremely old engine. Yes, there are no fancy effects or shiny graphics. Yes, the stock skins from Reiza, while functional for telling cars apart, are very boring. However, the "meh" graphics have never hurt the experience or detracted from it in any way. It is still immersive to race around, especially in the faster cars where you don't have time to look around in. The game can even look nice with certain tracks and times of day. Plus, night racing is a blast, and the car lights work beautifully.

Sound - Music to My Ears
After the physics, the sound in Automobilista is it's strongest suit. They are some of the best sounds in any racing sim, heck, in any game out there. The cars sound raw and loud and actually sound like they are pushing their engines, transmissions, drivetrains, and tires to the limits. The scream of the Porsche Cup Car's drivetrain is visceral. The chunkiness of the V8 Supercars is some of the most amazing sound, and I can listen to it for hours and hours without it getting old. This is one game that I'm glad to turn the volume way up on, because it is just so worth it.

Content/Modding - Everything and the Kitchen Sink
Automobilista may say it speciallizes in the Brazillian Stock Car racing series, but it is so much more. It has huge variety in it's stock content, and every bit of it is wonderful, from classic F1 to ridiculously silly Super Trucks. The tracks may mostly be ones you've never heard of, but they are a blast to drive around on. Plus, with modding adding in things like late 90's champ cars and more known circuits like Spa, this game has a bit of everything. The mod community is already getting larger and could be as good as rFactor 1 or Assetto Corsa if the game keeps growing. Automobilista is a huge game and that is a wonderful thing.

Summary
Automobilista has a bright future ahead of it if it continues to grow from where it is now. Things can be improved, and more actual game can be built up, but it has one of the strongest foundations on the market right now. It keeps me coming back to it out of all the racing sims I own, and every race I have leaves me exhausted, stressed, and with a huge smile on my face. This is currently my favorite sim out there, and anyone who likes racing owes it to themselves to give this a try. Reiza Studios has one of the best track records out there and I trust them to make sure this game is properly finished and fleshed out, and can easily make this game last and become the dominant sim on the market.

What are you waiting for? If you like racing sims, get this game!

VIDEO AVAILABLE AT www.twitch.tv/ramalls.

WOW! All I can say is ............. WOW! iRacing, watch out. This game currently cost 30 bucks with no residual cost (as of this writing).

Let's talk racing simulation.
1. Car physics. Automobilista has nailed it! The different cars have different physics and handling characteristics that you can feel! Tire wear, suspension travel, brakes, camber.....you make adjustments and you feel the difference.

2. Dynamic tracks and tire wear. I swear while I was racing in the F3 car I could see my tires degrading over time............not just feel it but see it. Now, maybe it's just because you have real time telemetry data that tells you what your tire state is, but I kept looking at my right front tire and I could swear I started seeing tread wear. Anyway, they have their tire model spot on.........and it's only going to get better. The tires pick up gravel and grass if you run off the road and you can hear it on the pavement also. Run behind someone that has ran off the road and hear the dirt and gravel ping and pong off your car's underbelly. Great job.

3. Force Feedback. THIS RIGHT HERE PEOPLE IS WHAT FORCE FEEDBACK IS ALL ABOUT. They got it right..........on the first try none the less. There are a number of default setups for almost every racing wheel you can think of. G27, G29, MoMo, driving force..........if you have a wheel, it's probably supported here. (CAUTION: This is a TRUE racing sim, so please don't attempt to drive with a gamepad. I see some support is in there, but you really are not going to get the full experience).

4. Graphics. This is where some pundants say that Automobilista is failing. I say that they are a small team of developers with a small budget that have created a simulation that is DIRECTLY challenging Assetto Corsa, iRacing, RFactor, etc. If I was to put the graphics to a scale of 1-10, I'd give them a solid 7.............but remember, this is an Early Access of the game. How may of you remember Assetto Corsa's early access. Pretty damn strong graphics........horrible physics. Automobilista is headed in the right direction.

5. AI. I know, I know...........who wants AI in a simulation racer. Let's be honest, there isn't a racing sim out there that can compete with the online community of iRacing. You ALWAYS find human racers to compete with. That's not to say that Automobilista doesn't have a mulitplayer community. Indeed, you can log on and find some multiplayer races. However, the community has not expanded yet. Therefore, you NEED AI to get some traffice time it. The scaleable AI here is some of the best I've seen in a racing sim yet. They truly try to find the best path around you for a pass and seem to attempt to avoid all contact.

5. Complaints. As I said earlier, this is an early access of the game. However, I want to be sure to let everyone know if some of the downsides that I think exists. First, you will notice some clipping when you collide with certain artifacts in the game. It's nothing that ruins the overall realism and fun of the sim, however, it's there. Also, there seems to be some scaling issues with a few of the tracks. Montreal's straights seem to be pretty short. Speaking of tracks, there are curently 0 American Road Courses in the game. Hopefully this will change..........(Long Beach, St Petersburg......FORGET Laguna Seca). There are also a lot of tracks that are either new to me or not real world tracks. When setting up a single player practice session, you have to manually add the AI cars..............ONE CAR AT A TIME.....CLICK.....CLICK.....CLICK.... Hopefully this will get changed.

6. Speaking of cars............There are several in here that are not mainstream to your typcial racer. COPA is something complete new to me.............and I love it. COPA MONTANA are GT trucks that are pretty damn fun to drive. The other COPA (sorry cannot remember the spelling or name) is GT Hondas Civics and Cheverolets. Pretty damn fun to run these rice burners. There are also SUPER V8 TRUCKS and tracks (imaging dirt rally truck on a race track with buitl in jumps). Definitely a change of pace. What doesn't exist right now are true GT3/GT1 cars.

All in all, I'm really excited to see what Reiza is going to do with this simulation. They have definitely come out swinging and are directly challenging the mainstream racing simulations on the market today. This is how you do an early access game here........(hint hint hiht Star Citizen). Looking forward to more to come. I'll update this review as new content comes out.

See you on the track!!

Oh, if you want to see video, check out my twitch page at http://www.twitch.tv/ramalls Enjoy.

Early Access Review
Posted: 2 July
VIDEO AVAILABLE AT www.twitch.tv/ramalls.

WOW! All I can say is ............. WOW! iRacing, watch out. This game currently cost 30 bucks with no residual cost (as of this writing).

Let's talk racing simulation.
1. Car physics. Automobilista has nailed it! The different cars have different physics and handling characteristics that you can feel! Tire wear, suspension travel, brakes, camber.....you make adjustments and you feel the difference.

2. Dynamic tracks and tire wear. I swear while I was racing in the F3 car I could see my tires degrading over time............not just feel it but see it. Now, maybe it's just because you have real time telemetry data that tells you what your tire state is, but I kept looking at my right front tire and I could swear I started seeing tread wear. Anyway, they have their tire model spot on.........and it's only going to get better. The tires pick up gravel and grass if you run off the road and you can hear it on the pavement also. Run behind someone that has ran off the road and hear the dirt and gravel ping and pong off your car's underbelly. Great job.

3. Force Feedback. THIS RIGHT HERE PEOPLE IS WHAT FORCE FEEDBACK IS ALL ABOUT. They got it right..........on the first try none the less. There are a number of default setups for almost every racing wheel you can think of. G27, G29, MoMo, driving force..........if you have a wheel, it's probably supported here. (CAUTION: This is a TRUE racing sim, so please don't attempt to drive with a gamepad. I see some support is in there, but you really are not going to get the full experience).

4. Graphics. This is where some pundants say that Automobilista is failing. I say that they are a small team of developers with a small budget that have created a simulation that is DIRECTLY challenging Assetto Corsa, iRacing, RFactor, etc. If I was to put the graphics to a scale of 1-10, I'd give them a solid 7.............but remember, this is an Early Access of the game. How may of you remember Assetto Corsa's early access. Pretty damn strong graphics........horrible physics. Automobilista is headed in the right direction.

5. AI. I know, I know...........who wants AI in a simulation racer. Let's be honest, there isn't a racing sim out there that can compete with the online community of iRacing. You ALWAYS find human racers to compete with. That's not to say that Automobilista doesn't have a mulitplayer community. Indeed, you can log on and find some multiplayer races. However, the community has not expanded yet. Therefore, you NEED AI to get some traffice time it. The scaleable AI here is some of the best I've seen in a racing sim yet. They truly try to find the best path around you for a pass and seem to attempt to avoid all contact.

5. Complaints. As I said earlier, this is an early access of the game. However, I want to be sure to let everyone know if some of the downsides that I think exists. First, you will notice some clipping when you collide with certain artifacts in the game. It's nothing that ruins the overall realism and fun of the sim, however, it's there. Also, there seems to be some scaling issues with a few of the tracks. Montreal's straights seem to be pretty short. Speaking of tracks, there are curently 0 American Road Courses in the game. Hopefully this will change..........(Long Beach, St Petersburg......FORGET Laguna Seca). There are also a lot of tracks that are either new to me or not real world tracks. When setting up a single player practice session, you have to manually add the AI cars..............ONE CAR AT A TIME.....CLICK.....CLICK.....CLICK.... Hopefully this will get changed.

6. Speaking of cars............There are several in here that are not mainstream to your typcial racer. COPA is something complete new to me.............and I love it. COPA MONTANA are GT trucks that are pretty damn fun to drive. The other COPA (sorry cannot remember the spelling or name) is GT Hondas Civics and Cheverolets. Pretty damn fun to run these rice burners. There are also SUPER V8 TRUCKS and tracks (imaging dirt rally truck on a race track with buitl in jumps). Definitely a change of pace. What doesn't exist right now are true GT3/GT1 cars.

All in all, I'm really excited to see what Reiza is going to do with this simulation. They have definitely come out swinging and are directly challenging the mainstream racing simulations on the market today. This is how you do an early access game here........(hint hint hiht Star Citizen). Looking forward to more to come. I'll update this review as new content comes out.

See you on the track!!

Oh, if you want to see video, check out my twitch page at http://www.twitch.tv/ramalls Enjoy.

It's a little hard, but the driving physics seem very nice and immersive.
The graphics aren't great, even if you turn them up to max, the road looks too shiny and glittery, very distracting, So I had to turn the detail down to medium to stop that happening.

Definitely lest gen graphics being pushed as hard as possible.
If that doesn't bother you, then go for it.
The cars look and sound good anyway.

Updated review:
I disabled the in game anti aliasing by overriding it with Nvidia's options.
http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=638367451
It's MUCH better, looking VERY smooth - and the shimmering road has gone even with detail set to max.

Also after a little more game-play, I can say, the cars are fantastic, with a very realistic weight to them.
The sounds are also great.

Now I have no hesitation recommending this game.
It's a solid and immersive racing experience.
