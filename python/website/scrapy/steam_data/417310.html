About This Game

A chiptune rhythm-action game that also teaches you something!

Learn to read Japanese Hiragana and Katakana characters in a fun chiptune adventure! Not just for people who want to learn Japanese, this is a game anyone can enjoy.

Progress through 15 different worlds remembering patterns and tapping in time to an all-original catchy soundtrack! Learn how to recognize the shapes and sounds of each symbol in the Hiragana and Katakana syllabaries. Learn them all and be able to easily read and pronounce Japanese words!

Features:
  • 15 different worlds and themes.
  • 208 characters.
  • 192 missions for each syllabary.
  • 5 gameplay types that challenge you in different ways.
  • All-original score.

Hiragana Pixel Party does not teach words or phrases, but teaches how to recognize characters and read them out as words.

Update (Saturday 19 March 2016)
  • Rebalanced difficulty - Now easier songs are nearer the start of the game. Some timings have been adjusted to make the game a little less challenging.
  • Preview HUD when playing - You can now see the next button layout before it appears on screen, giving you more time to react.
  • Added a new song, bringing the total to 16.

This game is a great way to learn Hiragana and Katakana letters, if you don't mind the high level of difficulty. It is a somewhat fast paced educational rhythm game that uses Hira / Kata letters, and your knowledge of them, to go through levels. You would need skill in timing, memorization, and quick reactions in this game.

For someone who knows (or knew) Hiragana and Katakana before, it is still surprisingly challenging. The challenge lies in your reaction time, and memorization. Sometimes, some letters or characters would appear way too near the actual time you have to input them. I know this adds extra challenge, but I have a feeling this should be adjusted a bit.
**EDIT
They have fixed this problem by showing a preview of the characters that will be used before the actual time you have to input them.

For people who are brushing up on their Hira / Kata, this is a great way to remember the basics, and also to distinguish on the differences of how each character sounds. I usually had trouble with my ã's and ã's because they look so similar.

They say that this "is a game that anyone can enjoy", now I think that is kind of ballsy to say. I'd say that if you are even remotely interested in Japan or learning new languages, and also if you are really into fun challenging rhythm games, that's when this game can truly be enjoyed. (This is only my opinion though.)

I bought this immediately as soon as it got on the store, and I could safely say that it was worth it. If you are interested in learning Hira or Kata, and are fond of rhythm games like me, I think that this would be right up your alley.

Updated Reiew: I've changed my review from a thumbs down to a thumbs up. I still think that there are a lot of things that could improve the game itself and believe that the game isn't for everyone, as the game is more like flashcard memorisation with music than a game which will teach you the Japanese alphabets. There is nothing wrong with this if you want to use it to supplement learning the language in school or your own self studies but it's not really geared towards everyone.

As someone who already knows both Hiragana and Katakana here are my thoughts on the gameplay itself:

Pro:
  • Simplistic play style
  • Great for learning to memorise characters once you have learned what they are
  • Developer fixing issues/updating the game
  • Game runs perfectly with no crashing or errors

Con:
  • Gives Japanese characters but seems to forget to tell you what the English letter equivalent for it is. You can get the sounds if you click on the characters in the pre-mission screen but would be better to see the written letters. Also been reported an issue if you are using a controller that you can't get the sounds, you must have a mouse currently to do this.
  • Too much emphasis on memorising patterns of characters instead of teaching the characters themselves
  • Soundtrack is OK but the SFX are not great, feeling like they will get on your nerves after a couple of hours play. Thankfully you can turn the sound completely off if you feel this way.
  • Not enough notice given on which characters go to which keys (it randomly changes with each pattern) making the game unnecessarily difficult
  • Katakana tutorial is the exact same as the Hiragana one (didn't even bother to change characters to Katakana)

If you had more notice on which keys were for which characters, slower tempo for earlier levels to give people who are new to the language more time to adjust to the characters and less emphasis on what the pattern is after it's been flashed to you then the game might be ok but as it is now, it's not great.
