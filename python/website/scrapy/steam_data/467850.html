About This Game

METAGAL is an 2D platformer action game.

You will take the shoes of "Meta" ,a cyborg girl, in her quest to rescue her Creator "Dr Ray" from the hands of "General Creeper", the evil leader of a rogue army!!
Also Metagal's sisters, were kidnapped by Creeper: he turned them in battle cyborgs, in order to use their powers to rule the world!! Meta must face her sisters, free them, and copy their abilities. She must fight for 8 stages of intense action, and prepare herself for the final confrontation against Creeper himself!

- Classic jump and shoot style platformer.
- 8 levels with unique environment,detailed design and different gimmick.
- 30 Enemies types not including Boss
- Defeat Gal No.# Boss and use their weapon.
- Collect secret items to power up your character.
- Soft death penalty with "Gear" system allows player to resolve their mistakes with less frustration.
- Controller and Keyboard customize support.

*Note* This game support only "XInput controllers" Like Xbox 360 Controller for Example

Let's cut right to the chase; everything is decent up until fortress 3. The level design is a little uneven, the dialogue is clearly written by somebody whose first or even second language is not English, but the challenge of the game is on par with most normal Mega Man games as are the controls. Originally you'll think that the game is a fun, if short little romp into something we've all been begging for; more Mega Man (but with mostly female characters for flavor). However, once you hit fortress 3, everything goes out the window and it becomes apparent that the person or people who made this stage hates your guts.

The first section isn't that horrible, it's a bunch of moving platforms and if you stick to the top then everything will be fine... but if you do fall then you're in for a world of instant death pits, spikes and tricky jumps. The next section is delicious cake and perhaps the only small mercy you will ever see in this place, it's just simple timed dashing. Then the next part is where the stage first lost me; blind jumps onto spikes, having to abuse the fire dash special ability to pass over multiple spike floors, water that will send you head first into more spikes with enemies whose respawn is beyond anything Mega Man had seen, and then another section of more blind jumps with spikes with a gravity gimmick over instant death pits. Oh, and none of this has a checkpoint, so you need to do this all in one life.

When you do finally get a checkpoint it's just cruel sugar coating to the horror that the stage is half done; now comes instant death lava below you, sheets of timed screen filling instant death lava from above (which wasn't bad in the original stage you first saw them) and you have to use a property of a weapon never once explained to you to enhance your jumps to ensure that you can outrace a stage gimmick that has gone from fun to a waking nightmare. Then once the lava is done you get more spikes and fast instant death flamethrowers that never actually deactivate (see also Quick Man's instant death beams from Mega Man 2) that require more use of the weapon property nobody told you about and reflexes of a god which, even if you were a deity, would be tired by this point as you've died about 30 times from the horrible instant death nightmare.

Once that's done, however, you get an insulting easy boss, as if to pat you on the head for not throwing your PC out of a window in a fit of anger from the horrors you just experienced.

Fortress 3 is draining mentally and is out to crush your will to ever finish the game. It is there not to challenge you, but to torment you, to beat you down and try to break you. There is no fun in this, only mind numbing tedium, the kind of thing you find in I Wanna Be The Guy, only jacked up just a little more. Somebody wanted to outdo them and claim the title of worst possible stage design from Mega Man X6... and they did.

This game goes from scratching an itch you so desperately need to crushing your will to live within a few hours, and I am sadly beyond the point where I can get a refund. I cannot in good conscious condone this sort of thing, where the designer of this stage missed the point of Nintendo Hard so much that they went into territory that even the most horrid of ultra hard nonsense fan hacks (collectively called kaizo even though the meaning of the way has no inherent inclinations toward soul murdering difficulty spikes) would be jealous of. Do not, for the love of all that has ever existed, subject yourself to this unless you are masochistic enough to enjoy these sorts of things.

I don't usually write reviews for the videogames I've played, but I felt that this one deserved one. I have completed the game and now I try to improve my records on every stage, which means I have a good overview of the game. The game takes most of its elements from the Megaman videogame series but the point is that it improves a lot of these elements further. It provides a challenge without being devilishly difficult (+supports the player) and has a lot of great gameplay mechanics which vary among the stages (and a lot of clever level-design ideas too). Fortunately, you won't actually meet the cheap deathtraps that overwhelm the Megaman stages. I also liked the fact that the abilities you earn aren't just variations of your buster but completely different gadgets and "augumentations" that evolve your character's actions. Lastly, I loved the soundtrack of the game (it's just amazingly crafted) and the artwork during some of the cutscenes. The only minor negative I noticed were some small glitches here and there, but you may hardly find them during playing the game. Some may feel that there should be more stages, but I believe that the total duration of the game is fairly good given it's price. It's been quite a while since I had so much fun fighting epic bosses and shooting my way through a 2D action platformer (and I played "Megaman and Bass" last month). As a closing comment: those of you who can't stand the high difficulty of the Megaman videogames but would love to play something very close to the series' gameplay, this is just the game worth spending your money on.

The Good:
  • The Graphics are nice for a 16-bit Mega Man Clone

The Neutral:
(See Below for reasons)
  • Character Handling is pretty spot on.
  • Music is unmemorable and repetitive. 'nuf said.
  • The Controls are rigid, but decent.

The Bad:
(See Below for reasons)
  • Level Design is beyond abysmal
  • Enemy Placement is shoddy at best

Detailed Review:

(Sigh)...Where to begin...

I mainly played on the 360 controller, which wasn't too bad if a bit rigid. So overall the controls weren't too bad, as most of the movement was relegated to the D-pad which considering it is how a game like this is supposed to be played. There was a few issues with getting the controller to work, but that was due to a HID (Human Interface Devices) issue and is entirely on my PC setup.

Along with the decent controls, the handling of the player avatar is pretty spot on with what you'd expect from a game in this genre. The jumping and movement are decent approximations of the older platformers in this genre of games, even the inclusion of a dash is quite nice to extend jumps and push through obstacles. It definitely channels the old Mega Man games well, but that's where that comparison ends.

Now on to the really bad parts of the game and the stuff that made me start this review in the first place. The Level Design is possibly the worst offender in deaths and cheap moments in this game, but that's not to say that the theming and the atmosphere of the levels are bad, on the contrary they each have their distinct feel which is actually good. However, when it comes to the construction of the levels and overall enemy placement it is clearly so far flung from what a good game is that it creates it's own level of hell for all involved to stew in.

Let's back up a bit, the concpet of a decent level should have some Hazard Foresight/Telegraphing, where the player is put into a situation where they encounter dangers in a relatively solitary environment, then later on be thrust into a much more dangerous environment with multiple dangers coming at once. MetaGal does none of this, it basically throws caution and decent level design to the wind which results in a multitude of situations where the player is thrown into a situation where death is the only recourse for no misstep of their own.

This poor hazard telegraphing goes hand-in-hand with the amount of instant death pits and spike traps, the latter of which reminds me of a LEGO crazed six year-olds bedroom. It's nearly impossible to get around or through the levels when death is looming below numerous fall away and moving platforms. The worst part is all the levels are like this and make traversing them to the Boss room harder than the bosses themselves.

Along with these death traps, the enemies are a constant source of frustration and death which along with all the previously mentioned issues only ramp the problems up further. The basic enemies in MetaGal sponge up way too many shots and are so numerous that sometimes the screen scroll IS your worst enemy (Ex. Warp's Stage). Not to mention the placement of such enemies are often in front of instant death zones or in areas where a single hit can knock you into said death zone.

Overall, I don't recommend this game to anyone who is a fan of the genre or even looking for an alternative from other games (i.e. Mighty No. 9). This game falls so flat on what makes a platformer good and memorable that I regret adding this game to my library.

Full Disclosure: I received a review code for this game from the developer at my request.

Looking for your Megaman fix? Not thrilled with what happened on Mighty No. 9?

Then I've got a deal for you, my friends.

This is Metagal, from freshman developer RetroRevolution. They're an Indian developer that decided that rather than tackle an MMO, they were going to do a thing, do it well, and move on. As a result, they've delivered on what they claimed: A game that looks a bit like Megaman, plays a lot like Megaman, and still does its own thing in its own peculiar way.

Right down to the nasty case of Nintendo Hard it seems to have picked up from the original Megaman games. Sitting down to play this game made me feel like a teenager again-- all the way down to the Epic Fail getting much of anywhere.

For upsides, there's a fair list-- Hardcore Megaman/Metroidvania players will love the vertical difficulty curve (it's not playing around here) post-tutorial, solid look and gameplay, and a bad habit of killing you if you stop to think things through.

As for the cons, I only have two complaints: a very small native resolution and a bit of slop in the controls. The first is correctable with the maximize button (no fullscreen) and antialiasing, while the second might just be me. Your mileage may vary.

You already know what you're in for when looking at this, right? Like Legend of Dark Witch (another highly recommended Mega Man-like Run n' Gun for super cheap) this is a basically a gender swapped Mega Man. It's a retro-style run n' gun platformer pitting your mechanized heroine against a bunch of Android "Gals" vs. Mega Man's various dude Robot Masters (excluding MM9's Splash Woman). Unlike Dark Witch, which adds in it's own ideas (like a Gradius style upgrade bar) this one sticks to the MM formula pretty much exclusively. But it does pretty well!

Visually it looks fantastic; the pixel art here and the color palette used is as good as, or better than, genuine SNES/Genesis/Amiga quality. The chibi character art really evoke cute, Japanese sidescrollers from the era. The quality of the chiptunes is great too. Controls are absolutely on point. They're super responsive and tight; it feels great with a 360 controller. The levels are very well designed, with areas being themed around various classic MM stage hazards like timed drop platforms, flame throwers and conveyor belt sections. Once you defeat the four main Gals and move onto the levels beyond, they even start making great use of your powers! See a wall you seemingly can't get passed? Fire the black hole-thing at an enemy on the other side to switch places and warp over there! See a gap that's too large for a dash jump? Jump anyway, then use Magma Gal's Flame Dash to cross to the other side! A ladder you can't reach? Shoot out your Mines, then jump on them to use the explosion to give you a boost! It gets REALLY GOOD once you have all these powers; the levels know that, and are designed thusly.

Meanwhile, enemies are positioned in ways and combinations that almost resemble an "action puzzle"; they'll shoot bouncing projectiles to get you to move to certain safe spots or you'll be able to perfectly take them out one-at-a-time from certain vantage points. It's just like the good old days! Speaking of, the difficulty is tuned to be "NES Hard"; really difficult, but never impossible and rewarding when conquered (and able to be more speedily conquered after you get it down). Sit there and watch patterns, try different weapon & ability combinations, or die and learn from failures when you try again: you WILL make progress... most of the time.

There are *some* issues that affect the difficulty. The timing windows on some of this stuff is absolutely brutal (particularly the fullscreen flame throwers in the second half of Magma Gal's stage and the explosive, timed platforms on Buster Gal's stage), enemies respawn THE SECOND you leave their "screen" (there's definitely places you can stand still that will cause them to continuously fly in from the edge of the screen, one after the other. Kind of good for grinding, but awful for combat where you have to maneuver in the air while jumping).

EDIT: The developer is doing a good job currently of patching and otherwise updating this game constantly. Issues I had a week ago (I previously mentioned no way to change the controller config here) are no longer here! Meanwhile, the balance on things seem to be constantly tweaked for the better. I definitely have to give a shout-out to the devs for continually polishing this up!

In the end, I just have a tiny issue with everything. Namely, the music compositions are a little repetitive (they all seem to be variants of the same song to me). But this game is $4 so I know its petty to bring up. What's here sounds good, but it bares mentioning anyway.

That's it. Every other little issue I had is now gone :D
--
Overall, it's definitely recommended if you're looking for a cheap Mega Man style run-n'-gun retro action game. This is an extremely well-crafted little game and definitely provides the fun and challenge you're looking for. This title could absolutely go for $10 and it would put some other games in that price range to shame. RetroRevolution has a quality game here for the price of a sandwich at Burger King. I hope this isn't the last we see of Meta Gal :)
