Ultimately, the question that matters most is:

Is Crypt of the necrodancer enjoyable for me?
As with all games, not everyone will enjoy this. In fact, even a rouge-like lover might not like this due to it's vastly different playstyle.

The biggest turnoff that could ruin one's enjoyment is that the game is largely trial and error. There is almost zero chance of recovering from a mistake in a boss fight and this will end your run. While common in rougelikes, the bosses in this game are unforgiving. Any new enemy you encounter is and will (probably) kill you. It is nigh impossible to complete the game without knowing how to deal with them.

Fortunately, there is a practice dungeon that allows you to figure out how to deal with these obstacles. That said, (your experience) how frustrating this game gets depend whether or not you choose and have the patience to use this feature.

Spend an hour or two practising against all mini-boss/bosses should you get this game. There are other minor qualms about the game but overall, it was a great experience and the design of certain enemies are ingenious.

Note: There is a character (Bard) that would make the game much easier due to the removal of time pressure imposed by the rhythms and changes the game into a turn-based strategic one but the point above stands firm. Because this removes a core element of the game (the rhythimic pressure), it becomes just another rouge adventure and you will not be playing the same game that is advertised.

The game itself is fairly short and is broken into four (4x4) zones and there is a notable difficulty spike in the latter zones. The approach to beating each level is vastly different.
The upside? You are allowed to skip zones after beating them once.

Zone 1 and 2 allows you to take things slow. -Kiting-
Zone 3 forces you to manage and balance the different skill sets the enemies have and take the pre-emptive strike before you are swarmed. (Bad RNG can happen in this zone so be prepared for frustration.)
Zone 4 requires fast planning and twitchy reflexes.

-------------TL:DR-------------
Shuffle and boogie to da beat like your bootie in da heat dancing cha-cha
in the crypt. And here we go! Forward backward rock-step, side together side
rock-step~

Conclusion
Great game, not for everyone. If you have twitchy reflexes, patience practising, like strategy (more so chess as opposed to RTS) and rouge-like games, this is the game for
you!

Otherwise, grab the game on sale for a taste of this unique masochistic hybrid.
