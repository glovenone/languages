Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We're huge fans of bridge-builders and physics based simulation games, and our main intention during development is to create a gaming experience that will satisfy players we share this passion with.

At the core of Poly Bridge is the Sandbox, which is what we used to create the levels of the game, and what we hope will ultimately be the tool that you will use to tinker with the game's mechanics, explore new directions that we hadn't even thought of and share your creations with everyone.

To do so we believe the best course of action is to release a fully-featured game with the single player Campaign, while keeping the Sandbox and community features of the game open enough to allow integrating feedback, feature requests and suggestions from players who enjoy the game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The plan is to transition to full release in between late 2015 and early 2016.
With that said, plans do change and scopes get bigger, but we do promise to keep you informed all the way along.

If you would like to follow development more closely, a good place to start is our public Roadmap: https://trello.com/b/9IS3LKpk/poly-bridge-roadmap”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of Poly Bridge will differ from Early Access by having:

  • More content, more Campaign worlds and levels, more polish, more vehicles, fewer bugs!
  • Final Sandbox and Steam Workshop integration
  • Community suggested features and requests

We'll work hard to deliver the most polished and fun bridge-building game you've ever seen, while also staying flexible enough to bring on feature request and suggestions from players.

The largest piece of work that will change is the Sandbox mode, which is the main reason we chose to do Early Access.

With your help, and the help of the rest of the community, we hope to deliver a Sandbox mode with all the tools & toys you could dream of, allowing you and us to create endless amounts of puzzles and content for everyone to enjoy and be challenged by.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current Early Access version features the majority of the single-player campaign mode, as well as a beta of the Sandbox, including submitting and downloading extra levels in the Steam Workshop.
The social sharing features of animated GIF replays are there, as well as the ability to give us feedback in-game at any point.

More levels and worlds are also being added to the single-player Campaign mode, and we're aiming to deliver loads of different levels, worlds and vehicles for players to explore.

Please keep in mind the Sandbox is heavily under development, which means that any Sandbox layouts you save and/or submit to the Workshop might break as work progresses on the Sandbox and level sharing features.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Considering the advanced stage of development, and to be fair to past, present and future players of Poly Bridge, pricing before and after Early Access will not change.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“You and the community will be at the very heart of Poly Bridge during Early Access.

Our public Roadmap is here: https://trello.com/b/9IS3LKpk/poly-bridge-roadmap

At any point in-game, you can access the Feedback menu from the top-left, which allows you to send us a message, optionally attaching a screenshot.

We're hoping that this system will encourage consistent feedback from players, as it doesn't require ever leaving the game and provides us with as much information as possible.

We will also of course be active in the community groups, on various social media (Twitter, Reddit) and we can always be reached via email at any point.”

27 June

Build 0.77b-p2

New Road Changes
As you may be aware we made some fundamental changes last week to how the roads are simulated, which resulted in introducing a new mechanic: reinforced roads. We've spent the last week tweaking values and improving the reliability of road simulations.

Leaderboard Reset
As we iterate over new road changes we need to ensure leaderboard entries stay relevant; because of this we have reset the leaderboards. Please keep in mind we will likely be resetting leaderboards at least once more before Final Release, thanks for your patience!

Leaderboard Rankings
Many of you requested a way to get an overview of your Leaderboard rankings, we tried a few different things and eventually settled on a 'Ranking View' that displays the percentile you're in for each Campaign Level, giving you a clear indication as to how you're placed within each Leaderboard compared to the total number of players. Access via Leaderboard>My Ranks on the World Map.



Leaderboard Banlist
We're actively monitoring the leaderboards and banning players that hack or cheat their entries. If you see any values that look suspicious, please let us know. We want to ensure the leaderboards stay challenging and valuable for everyone to compete in.

In-Game Gallery
This patch also introduces a new in-game Gallery browser accessible from the Main Menu, and is also used to display the replays associated with a Workshop item, which you can open with the 'Community Replays' button in the Workshop browser.

You can now easily filter by level, show only your own replays, the replays of your Steam friends, and any replays associated with a specific user. If you want to share a replay that you find in the in-game Gallery a convenient 'Copy Link to Clipboard' button is available.

About This Game

Unleash your engineering creativity with an engaging and fresh bridge-building simulator with all the bells and whistles.
Enjoy hours of physics-based puzzle solving in the Campaign and then jump in the Sandbox to create your own bridge designs and puzzles.
Challenge friends and the rest of the Steam Workshop community to solve your puzzles and download more levels submitted by other players.

Check out the User Submissions Gallery at http://gallery.drycactus.com !

And here's our Roadmap: https://trello.com/b/9IS3LKpk/poly-bridge-roadmap





We put lots of work in the physics of the game and the result is an accurate and consistent simulation, which also gives you access to loads of different building blocks, including hydraulic pistons to create shape-changing and moving bridges, suspension bridges, cable, steel, iron, and more.



Enjoy hours of bridge-building fun with loads of levels to solve, ranging from simple light car bridges to multi-deck draw-bridges and jumps, just to name a few!
Levels get increasingly challenging from the engineering aspect and restrictions are also imposed on the resources you can use to build your bridge.



What would an awesome bridge-builder game be without an equally awesome Sandbox?
Go wild and create the most complex bridge the world has ever seen, or just an absurd creation that pushes the mechanics of the game in a new direction, and if you're feeling like it publish your design online as a puzzle level on the Workshop for everyone to try and solve!



Download hundreds of extra levels from the Workshop!
We'll also be featuring the most original and fun bridge designs that are submitted, making it easier for you to find the ones that are worth the challenge.



One of our favorite features of the game!
Save a super cool looking animated GIF of your bridge in all its glory (or failure!) and save it locally, upload it to the Poly Bridge Gallery and/or share it on Twitter!
Check out the User Submissions Gallery at http://gallery.drycactus.com !

Early Access Review
Posted: 8 July
Did you enjoy that school project where you build a bridge out of popsicle sticks?
Does being an engineer or architect sound appealing to you?
Like solving problems with logic and creativity?

You know the drill, if you answered yes to any of these, this game may very well be for you.

To briefly summarize the gameplay, in each level you have one or more automobiles that must be transported across a gap. You do this by building a bridge. Some of the materials at your disposal are wood, asphalt, steel beams, rope, and hydraulics. You can build draw bridges, suspension bridges, arch bridges, etc. As you progress, the challenges get more difficult. As an example, in one level a car will drive over the bridge, then a ship will travel underneath, and finally a heavier, longer car will travel over the bridge. Here, you must take into consideration the size of the ship when selecting a height for your bridge. Also to be considered is the length of the second car, which may hinder its ability to get up a steep incline. Some sort of draw bridge may be the best option here, which can be very fun and challenging to build.

In my experience so far, each new level brings a new challenge which keeps everything fresh and interesting. It's a game that makes you think in a very fun and relaxing way which is something I value. It's got great graphics and a really cool looking art style. The soundtrack is all wonderfully relaxing acoustic guitar from an artist named Adrien Talens, very Jack Johnson-esque, but purely instramental. All of this combined with a fairly polished presentation and a decent amount of content make this a recommendation for me.
