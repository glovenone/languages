About This Game

Couch Fragger Shooter Action for 2-4 Local Players

  • Frantic local multiplayer, twitch style arena shooting action!
  • Multiple playable characters. Play as the faithless Preacher, the desperate Demon, the Heavenless Angel, the Antichrist Deceiver, the peace keeping Nun or even Death himself.
  • Choose from multiple game modes, including free-for-all and team based modes.
  • Battle it out in 4 different interactive environments, with 10 arenas each, featuring unique environmental hazards and mechanics.
  • Pick up randomly dropped tarot cards that can either bring you good luck to you or your team in the form of a temporary power-up, or can curse your opponents, giving you a competitive edge.
  • Deflect bullets back at your opponent with your shield.
  • Dash across the arena to make a quick escape or a trail blazing kill.
  • Master shotgun jumping to reach high platforms and create shortcuts.
  • An original soundtrack by VHS Glitch, famed synthwave composer.
  • Featuring the epic voice of Jon Bailey from Honest Trailers.

Singleplayer & Local Co-op Enemy Wave Mode

  • Fend off the Devil's army and prevent the end of times in a 1-2 player enemy wave mode.
  • Face off against 6 different types of enemies.
  • Fight in 5 different unlockable arenas and fight your way to an epic boss battle against the Devil himself.
  • Collect the souls of your enemies and earn a 1UP.

Multiple Competitive Game Modes

  • Classic Deathmatch.
  • Classic Team Deathmatch.
  • Soul Reaping, where each player fights to increase their pool of souls by draining other players of theirs.
  • Team Soul Reaping, where each team shares the same pool of souls.
  • Capture the Soul, a variant on classic capture the flag where players fight for control of the single soul in order to capture it.
  • Team Capture the Soul.
  • Last Soul Standing, where players have only one life and fight in round based combat.
  • King of the Soul, where you fight to maintain control of the single soul, earning a point for every 5 straight seconds you have control.
  • Team King of the Soul.

Multiple Arenas & Environments

  • Fight at Deathâs Cemetery and uncover each labyrinth's hidden passages and secret hideaways while avoiding booby traps.
  • Square off in the abstract and bizarre caves of Hell, using portals to traverse levels.
  • Showdown on the rooftops and streets of a mega city, avoiding long drops and managing moving platforms.
  • Confess your sins, then commit a bunch more, before the church altar.

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 February
As far as local multiplayer arena games go this game is ok. It definitely works and has everything you'd expect from a game like this but beyond that...

The gameplay is solid. characters move how you'd expect them to, the shotgun's are satisfying to use and the powerups all do things. (to be honest we never fully grasped the powerups)

As far as my groups sessions went the deeper levels of play essentially revolved around gambling with shotgun distance. the close you are to the opponent when you fire the more of your pellets will pierce your opponents flesh. However the clsoer you get the more danger you are in as well. Making use of your shield and the recovery time between shots is the most important aspect to winning. You can also run out of ammo so playing it 'safe' means running out of bullets more often and having to expose yourself to pickup more.

Art and music are fine and befitting of the theme although I must admit that the background, characters, and stages blend a little too much at times which did lead to my death. Bullets especially can be hard to see.

Everything works and the game flow is certainly there but the game just seems to be missing its 'thing' nothing about the game stands out as being unique to only this game. All the characters are exactly the same meaning the only thing that changes the game are the stages themselves. there are quite a few modes but it still doesnt help this game from feeling like just another local arena game. I also think pretty much every hype moment will be experienced within a couple plays. Around our tenth game we weren't really surprised by anything that happened.

I'd say if you get it on sale or in a bundle its worth a shot but there are plenty of more innovative arena games out there.

Pro-Con thing:
+solid gameplay
+different theme
+nice art and music
+shotgun metagame
+mode variety
-lacks originality
-elements can blend together
-lack of hype
-unclear powerups

Furiously, Fast Paced, Frag Fest.

Aliteration aside, Demons with Shotguns is a gem of the local multiplayer arena shooter genre. A niche genre to be sure, but if you happen to have any real life friends, you owe it to yourselves to give this game a spin. The controls are super responsive and the shooting feels meaty and weighty. It's as twitch and skill based a shooter as you will find. If you're looking for something that's easy to pick up yet has a very high skill ceiling, this is one worth checking out.

While it may be in early access, the local multiplayer portion of the game is basically feature complete and highly polished. With a first class soundtrack and great presentation, Demons with Shotguns is a steal at its current asking price. With a single player and local coop mode in the works, the future of this little title is definitely looking bright.
