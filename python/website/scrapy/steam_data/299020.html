About This Game

A young mother's attending a train exhibition with her children, when something strange happens; the legendary Runaway Express starts moving while her children are still on board. Frightened, she jumps on the train and finds it full of unusual passengers--the ghosts of people who died on the Runaway Express over the 50 years it ran between Paris and Istanbul. Each ghost is a victim, his or her mysterious demise never fully investigated. These lost souls are cursed to ride the train until someone has the courage to go back in time and reveal the truth about their deaths.
Do you dare to break the curse? Will you uncover the mysteries and free the souls? Do you have what it takes to face and defeat the terrible masked Engineer and if you do, what price will you have to pay? Jump on the Runaway Express and find out.

Runaway Express Mystery is a decent HOG title and one of the earliest games coming from the relatively small Czech developer, Icarus Games. The game presents an interesting and well detailed supernatural storyline, ending up with a couple loose ends, convenient for developing future installation as series.

Amanda Waters is a mother of two young children, visiting the nearby antique train exhibition to see Runaway Express, a famous locomotive hailing from early 1900s. It's all fun and games until the appearance of a mysterious engineer and the train taking off with Amanda's children on board! Determined to find her children, Amanda catches the train in last minute. From thereon, she'll meet many strange passengers on the train - long dead and lost in time. If she is to find her children, she should solve their mysteries and shed light into their stories first. With each new clues she gathers, Amanda gets closer to saving her children. Can she solve the dangerous mystery of Runaway Express, revealing the dreadful affair of its last journey and return her children home safely?

As you can once again observe, a loved one in danger + a supernatural mystery dynamic never fails in HOGs. This game is no different, aside the remarkably irritable personality of our protagonist. Amanda seems to be immune to fear or surprise, and amazingly fixated on finding her children only. She is a mother first and foremost, okay. But she embarks on a train, meeting corpses and travelling back in time for God's sake! She keeps walking around, reprimanding living corpses (!) and demanding explanations about the whereabouts of her children from them! It's amazing to watch her reactions and listen to her comments. This whole eerie setting has literally no effect on her. Once she is declared that the only way to save her children would be solving the mystery, she gets into the investigation yet keeps challenging the rules, looking for shortcuts all the way. The mystery itself doesn't concern the mother of the year. Talk about one track mind. Imagine your mother reprimanding Frankenstein's monster - that is Amanda Waters for you. Now that would be hilarious.

Visuals are beautiful, and the atmosphere is appropriately rustic. Background artist knew what he was doing. Character designs are well done too. Talking animations of characters are a bit clumsy, but they have impressive facial mimics and body gestures. All characters are voice acted with clean cut accents. Listening to Evelyn's heavy Aussie accent was cute, for example.

The main attraction of the game is the investigation process for each new case about various passengers you meet. You walk around, gather clues about their pasts and try to solve their murders a century after their demise. You are expected to arrange your clues on a crime board, explaining the course of events. There are not many HOG scenes, but the ones that we have are short and easy to manage. Puzzles are mostly innovative and interesting. The train is huge - with a couple of visited stations along the way - and backtracking may become an issue if you are not playing in casual difficulty, but the map has a teleport function at least.

The ending was a bit anticlimactic, I won't lie. The mystery of the train is revealed alright - with a nicely arranged inter-relevant background story, but the nature of the engineer is not explained at all. It is hinted that this won't be the last time we'll be facing him as our enemy. Over all, Runaway Express Mystery is a good casual game for you to spare 3-4 hours and explore a nice story. Hit it when you'd feel like having a light reading on the mystery department. Enjoy!

Haywire Express...

A game about some mysterious murders, that happened along a trainride in the past, with each of the passengers telling their story.

Graphics: ++ The graphics are beautiful, some of them outshine others but all of them would also fit nice into a normal adventure game.

Sound: 0 Nothing too fancy, the sound is more standard fare, I would have expected a more atmospheric sound for this game.

Playability: 0 Normally I would have given one plus, sadly this game has a tendency of lagging, which actually makes the game not working perfectly. At least it didn't crash, but the lags were enervating nonetheless.

Genre-Type Scores:

Story: + The story is ok. It's kind of Orient-Express with some more murders, which you have to solve. And at last find the final conflict to get your kids back. The devs could have made more with the story, but it's ok. Not a full winner, but at least a nice story.

Technical Realisation: + Could have been better, but isn't all bad. Strange is the problem with frequent lags, not during the cutscenes, but in the actual gameplay. On the good side is how the HOS were presented, they are like Crimescene Investigation Pictures, you have to use Luminol and Powder, a Magnifier and a flashlight... But actually this was too simple, just spread dust and spray around and you wind up finding the clues...

Extras: 0 Sorry, no Extras present. This isn't a collectors edition and furthermore, the game is concluded after playthrough.

Overall Rating: 4 + The game isn't that bad at all, just let's say it's a bit of a disenchantment here. The characters you meet during the game are good, the graphics are sometimes very beautiful, the story is ok... it's more the trouble with the protagonist and... well... kidnapping. You've seen this so often now in different games that it too, becomes more and more a burden - especially if the protagonist is kind of... well, Mary Sue... it's so cheesy all the time, but other games also have this problem.

Value for Money: Low Basically a hidden object game with very limited replayability, the seven bucks do actually hurt. There are sales and bundles, if you want to play it try to get it cheap.

Conditionally Recommended

This game is more for hardcore fans of the genre, it also could be a good starting point for newbies - if they aren't familiar with HOG's. This mostly is the case because there are only a few Hidden Object Scenes around, and there is more sleuthing stuff, but this is better solved by any of the Nancy Drew stories - and Nancy Drew at least doesn't come along as a total Mary Sue. The game feels at times like an adventure light game, or casual adventure. And this is giving most of it's actual charm.

The story starts off as cheesy, you're on a train, searching for your kidnapped kids, you soon realize that the train is bizarre and something is off... I won't spoil ^^
The game is really well done, I really liked the graphics and the good music, the story is very well told too. The dialogues are fully voiced and it's rather well done. The characters are nice, the locations are beautiful and varied. Original thing: the evidence board, a special game mode where you piece up a story by collecting evidences, very cool. You have 3 cases to solve and you'll find blood stains, pictures, letters, etc. As far as HOG scenes, there's only the silhouette type where you have outlines of items to find, some helping you to find others etc. Pretty classic gameplay but it's very well done. You also have a forensic variant where forensic tools are also used to find some of the items (black powder for the fingerprints, flashlight to check dark places, etc). There's some mini games, really well done too.
It was short - 4 hours but I'm pretty fast with HOGs, so add an hour for a leisure playthrough. The short time length is also due to not having classic HOG scenes with lists of items.
Big up for this game, it's very good and should not be missed by any HOG fan!

Game breaking glitch. (sigh, so sad) In all good conscious I can not recommend this game, unless the developers fix this game stopper. (There is a game fix listed in the discussions for good game hackers, unlike me who messed up my game so badly I had to erase everything and start over anyway.) Also unsure if it is my computer or the game but there are severely lagging areas. When Amanda first jumps on the train, and the background graphics are moving, everything lags severely. This lagging occurs anytime Amanda is outside the train while the train is moving.

This glitch is avoidable so here it is where the glitch occurs for the intrepid or the just down right obstinate like me. The glitch occurs in Budapest as soon as the gamer opens the bank safe. Before you do anything, or even move, click on the vault and the gamer should get a photograph. If you do not get the photograph for criminal evidence you have encountered the glitch. Please understand this photograph occurs in the third segment of the game, and more then halfway through that segment. So play this game at your risk.

Other then this the gameplay is unique and interesting.

Story: Amanda Waters (is the name the game automatically gives the gamer but the name is changeable) has brought her children, Peggy and Jimmy, to a train exposition. The arrival of the train the Runaway Express is the highlight of the exposition. While her children are on board they are not allowed to leave by a person in an iron mask and the train starts to leave the station. Amanda jumps on board to rescue/save her children. On the way she encounters three different crimes and must solve each one.

Gameplay: A fair mix of Hidden Object finds and point and click gaming. The Hidden Object finds, however, are not the usual list word finds. The Hidden Object scenes are a combination of three different types of finds. The first find type is the criminal investigation. The gamer is given a magnifying glass, fingerprint power, a squirt bottle for finding blood stains and a flashlight for dark areas. When using the magnifying glass and flashlight the game will state whether they are needed or not. Finding blood stains is fairly easy as a find, since the game scene goes darker and the stains can be seen if looked for. Otherwise just spray all over the scene like one has to do with the fingerprint powder until all of them are found.

The second type of find is silhouettes. Find the object and click on it, easy enough. Which leds directly to the third type of find, interactive. If the silhouette picture stays this indicates that the item needs to be used somewhere in the scene. Overall the Hidden Objects are not overly challenging, but not especially easy either. They are a nice change of pace from the usual list finds.

Puzzles: The few actual puzzles (not counting the point and click obstacles) are brain twisters. Before my game glitched I encountered two quite worthy of the time and effort I put into solving them. I would have to call them master puzzles since they are between the segments of the game or between major points in the game.

I really would recommend this game if it were not for the game breaking glitch. (I would even recommend it despite the lagging, though I would warn everyone about that.) Too bad. I wish I knew how to contact the developers so I could tell them about the glitch so possibly they would fix it. That way I could change this "thumbs down" to a "thumbs up".
