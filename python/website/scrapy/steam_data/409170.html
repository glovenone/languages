About This Game

Contract is a side-scrolling shooter in which you must shoot, hack and massacre your way past armed guards, security systems and other challenges in order to reach your targets. You play as a man, blackmailed into the art of assassination by the kidnappers of his brother.

In order to get to your targets you must fight your way through mountains of private security and hungry dogs, find security pannels to hack locked doors and make your way to your unwitting targets. Upgrade your gear with the money you gain from your contracts to ensure you always stay on top of the increasingly difficult tasks as you progress through the game.

Main Features


  • Shoot your way through different types of enemies
  • Unravel the truth about the kidnapping of your brother through 20 missions
  • Gain the upper hand over your enemies by upgrading your weaponry as well as your clothing
  • Hack security pannels in order to open locked doors powered by security grids
  • Skip through the story by mashing ENTER

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP or higher
    • Processor: 2GHz
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 140 MB available space

Introduction
Contract is a simple 2D shooter that lacks replay ability and minimum level design and variation. For me this game is a bundled mess full of the same types of enemies and levels with a few changes every now and then.

Gameplay
Contract is a 2D shooter, I feel like it needs a lot of work and the way that the game is being played out needs to be worked on a bit. First off let's talk about the art style which I actually like the look of. It has a nice pixel type of art style which is continously ongoing throughout the game as you play through each of the levels. With the graphics being either very dark of midly bright it really looks fairly nice. When you shoot and kill a person their blood goes upon the wall and really stands out since the other colours are rather different from that colourful red blood colour that really stands out.

Something that the developers did well with the visuals of the game is the crosshair that you see when you obtain your gun and are able to shoot the enemies. When you are far away from a enemie then a circle appears, giving you a rough estimate of where the bullet could hit. This makes it rather obvious that the further away you are the less accurate your weapon is going to be.

Now let's get onto the actual gameplay of Contract. When I first saw the trailer of the game my expectations where for it to be fast paced with more level variation than the game actually offers. Unfortunatly, upon playing the game for about a hour and a half I really couldn't get into it. First off I felt like there was no point in continuing the game after the time that I played it. I felt like I had no real 'goal' to get to and that I felt the need to achieve and keep coming back to the game. The levels variation in design really didn't impress me, I thought that the game might of actaully change the type of graphics but it never did. It always seemed to stay in this office type location with many floors, which is a bit dissapointing as all the levels just felt like the same but with a bit differenty level design.

Pros
+ Nice graphics contrasting with the blood
+ Nice crosshair
+ Not bad for the price it's asking for

Cons
- Was expecting the game to be mroe fast paced than it was
- Same graphics throughout the game
- Felt like there was no real 'goal' to keep me playing
- Since every level had the same graphics it seemed like the game was just the same on repeat with different level design

Result / Final Thoughts
My thoughts on this game are fairly average. The game itself seemed to be decent when I was watching the trailer. Upon playing the game I felt like there was no real goal, levels where bascially the same thing over and over, the graphics never changed from this black and white sort of graphic. I felt like the first few minutes of the games graphics was rather nice, it just got boring and bland. For these reason I can't recommened this game.

I actively chose to finish it and it killed about an hour and a half. It sort of reminded me of a flash game you might play for free or something.

This game is like Gunpoint and Hotline Miami, but without the high octane action of Hotline Miami or the clever writing and gameplay of Gunpoint. It's also shorter and less challenging than either. I don't know how this game took two games I like a lot and managed to mash them together without including anything that made either good, but god damnit they did it. That's a talent in and of itself so good job.

Let's look at the pros and cons.

Pros
  • Playable. I know this sounds like a given, but after eating a pack of random steam codes I've found this is surprisingly rare
  • Art is good
  • Music is alright
  • Cheap, it's a decent game for the price
  • Combat is fun. This is the central mechanic and it works well enough to sustain itself for 20 levels
  • You can probably buy the game, beat it in under two hours and return it if you want
Cons
  • Writing, It's just not good and I honestly just skipped past the dialogue most of the time.
  • Length, The game is short and doesn't end satisfyingly.
  • Difficulty. This game is really easy. The hardest part is if you get killed really late in a big level you have to start from the beginning, but you can basically play this whole game without leaving cruise control
  • Variety. There's very little done to mix things up. A few enemy types are added later in the levels, bringing the grand total to 4. A few switches are also in later levels, but there's almost nothing to make one level feel different than the rest.
  • In the last 5 levels you can't upgrade your stuff, which was a bit irritating to me since I was saving up money for the more expensive upgrades, but it wasn't a big deal since you don't really need any upgrades to win.

The game doesn't really feel finished basically. There's still a few bigs floating around too, particularly regarding the dogs and the fat guys, which can result in your dying. Really though, this game has it's core mechanics and what it wants to be down, but then it just doesn't do enough with it. It feels like there could have been more in just about every category. More levels, more enemies, more difficulty, etc. The game just sort of ends as well. The plot is that you're out to rescue your brother and then at the end, there he is and there's the game over screen. The writing could have used work as well. A lot of games are able to get past lacklustre elements by putting some heart into the story or the line by line writing, but I don't think the developers really cared about the writing at all. It all seemed pretty generic.

To be honest, I feel like the people who made this game can probably make a pretty good game, which is sort of the frustration here. They covered the basics and proved they could put out a concept and this game ultimately works. My biggest problems are just that there wasn't enough of everything and it felt like they could have worked on it for longer. That's an easy problem to fix, and I'm hopeful about future titles from these guys.
