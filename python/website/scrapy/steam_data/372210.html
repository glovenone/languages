Posted: 27 June
PROS:

-Adorable:
Now something needs to be said, some people may find the designs to look like something from an 11 year old from deviantART, but I'm just assuming. I think the designs look really cute, now granted, the cuteness is nowhere near as say Show By Rock or Chi's Sweet Home, but it's good enough.

-Low difficulty:
This is a very easy game, the game is very easy that has a main character that can do a spin dash thing, double jump, and wall jump. The enemies are fairly easy to get through (Except for a few). And the levels are short. The only people that will have any sort of difficulty is kids that are bad at platforming games or those who are not even trying one bit.

-A great beginner's platformer game:
This is probably a good idea for those getting in to platforming games


CONS:

-A few cheap deaths:
There are a few cheap parts in the game, say like you are in a narrow area, then you are presented with a few spikes above you, and a trampoline below them, and if you think you can just jump then do a spin dash, there is a spike waiting there after you do that, leaving you either to take damage or be very cautious.

-Sound:
Now, the sounds in this game are a mixed bag, but leaning moreover to the negative. I mean, hitting the rats, and a witch's laugh sound great, but the meowing is not genuine at all. They sound like someone meowing on a microphone, I'm not sure why, but I think someone should have went to "freesound" or something to get some meows there. When the cute pink kitty gets hurt, it plays what I think is the "MLP Squee" but in a higher pitch, I'm not 100% sure of that.
The music in this game is odd. At first, it plays what is the scariest part of the game which sounds like some "paulstretch" song, second it plays some tribal drums (I'm not entirely sure if that's supposed to be scary). Then I saved the best for last, they play what I think is like some rock/disco theme as only two of the songs (only one was listed here) actually worked.

-(SPOILERS) Vauge Final Boss:
When I first got to this level, I was confused, was I supposed to hit the boss? Was I supposed to get the girl? But no, the game gives out ZERO explanation on what you're supposed to do. After a few minutes of killing a few skeleton cats, a key appears, you get that key, get the girl to her little ghost thing. Then the rest of the battle begins. Hit the witch twice and it's over!

(END OF SPOILERS)


While this game has it's flaws, but it's strengths outweigh it for sure.

7/10

When I picked this up, I honestly thought I was just going to waste a dollar. There aren't many non-sarcastic reviews on here, so I really had no idea what was in store.

This is a freaking adorable little puzzle-platformer. The puzzles are pretty easy; find the key, open the lock, find the little girl and reunite her with the ghost. The levels have slight branching as you get further in, but not to the point where you end up lost. The music is really solid; even though there's only three tracks, they're pretty enjoyable. One of them is a bit startling, being louder and faster than the other two, but still surprisingly fitting. The controls are dependable and glitch-free, which isn't always the case in an indie platformer. Hit detection is actually spot-on, a welcome change from some of the other atrocities I've picked up on coupon. Really, my only complaint thus far is that I can't spend all those pennies I've been picking up. o.0

This isn't a hardcore game, but it's definitely worth what I paid for it. It's short (you can probably beat it in under an hour), but still strangely addictive. I opened it up expecting to play a few minutes, realized half an hour had passed and came here to post. So if you have a couple spare bucks, spend them here.

Recommended for anyone who wants to play a short, easy-to-pick-up platformer, where you play the part of a cute little kitty collecting coins. There is no story unfortunately, but this makes it easier to just jump in and out of the action as you see fit. There are 19 levels in total which can be finished in about an hour, depending on your platforming skills. The movement and actions are very intuitive and easy to use/handle. Just dash, jump, and double-jump your way through the levels, while picking up coins, finding big keys to open locked doors, rescuing a few human souls, and avoiding or defeating enemies like zombie cats, mummy mice and flying vampire cats/bats. There are 2 ways to defeat the spooky/cutesy enemies: you can jump on them or dash into them. I haven't found a way to defeat ghost cats though, so I just tried to avoid touching them in order to sustain health points...

=THIS IS MY HONEST REVIEW, IM GOING TO BE POINTING OUT ALL THE GOOD AND BAD=

My friend bought this for me as a joke, and told me I had to play atleast one hour. That was actually kinda a problem, beacuse I was on the 2nd last level within 45 minutes.

Im not going to try to make this sound sweet, this game is short. Like, really short.
Its also not scary at all. I love how the devs in the boss fight (If you can even call it that) Consistanly says, "dis game is so scawwy!!" It drives me nuts. Anyone who says this game is scary is either a crazy 2 year old or a liar.

Any "Difficulty" that is in this game is from an insane amount of spikes. There is the one level where it is literally COVERED with spikes wall to wall. With the first one, you cant walk off without getting hit by the spikes. Later, they place 2 unkillable enemies in the places where you need to walkthrough. Meaning, you have to wat about 20 seconds to lure them out, then run through. Any illusion of diffuliculty is from the devs overplacing the unkillable enemies or over usage of spikes.

The introduction of trampolines, late in the game, is completly unesscary. Its used well on one level, but then completly forgotten. However, the level it was used well, was actually an ok one.

As for the level design, its simply a "Rinse and repeat" Cycle. Press a button, press another button, get a key, backtrack to the door, and leave. Or, sometimes you have to grab the spirit of some weird-looking doll thing, then bring it to a ghostly body of it, where it spins and explodes into stars. Not sure what was going through the devs mind at this point, but with a game like this I see they needed... Er... "Creativity"

Everytime you hit an enemy, it goes slo-mo and zooms in. I mean EVERY TIME. In parts where you jump on many enemies heads, it gets quite annoying.

Now, despite all these flaws, I still oddly found myself having some fun with this game. I kinda enjoyed it, in an odd, twisted way. Do I think it is worth the base price? Goodness no! Do I think it could be worth it for some weird fun at around 50% off? For sure. If you can snag it at a cheap enough price, go for it! You might have a fun in some sense like I did.

There is about the most honest review written on this game.

Have a good one!

-SpyroMan27
