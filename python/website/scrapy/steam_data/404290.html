I vaguely remember playing this game in the late eighties on my commodore 64 so I thought I'd give it a try. The graphics look OK, but enemies all look the same as do the obstacles. There isn't much of a story and the end boss is the same character over and over. There are a few different backgrounds but since they have no impact on gameplay you'll hardly notice that. The gameplay is also very barebones which mirrors the original game but even simple arcade games nowadays offer more. You can earn upgrades by winning coins and the end of each level. But the upgrades are unlocked automatically (you cannot choose how to spent your coins). Most upgrades are cosmetic, different skins that don't chance the gameplay in a meaningful way. It's not really a racing game but more of an on rails shooter since speed is controlled by the game itself and the canon automatically aims towards the nearest enemy. I blasted through the first eight (out of ten) levels on normal difficulty with no difficulty at all and then suddenly hit a wall as the difficulty suddenly spiked in level nine. In the first eight levels there are only few obstacles so it's not very important when you stay on the road and when you take off and fly over obstacles. In level nine you suddenly have to avoid large amounts of obstacles. Since the steering feels unresponsive (I was constantly either understeering or oversteering) the precission that is suddenly required is very annoying. Blasting enemies also isn't very demanding until you're suddenly overwhelmed in the last two levels. All in all it feels rushed, over simplistic and very rough around the edges.
It's not exactly a trainwreck of a game and some people might even find it enjoyable to try and get high scores on the leaderboard. But I would rather waste my time on another racing game.
