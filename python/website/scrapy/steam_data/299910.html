Forgotten Myths is on Kicstarter!

Hello everyone,
The time has come, and our Kickstarter campaign has started! For the past six weeks, we have been working hard to prepare a great campaign for you, with lots of nice rewards, stretch goals, backer achievements and many surprises along the way. For the next thirty days, it will be up to you to show your support to Forgotten Myths, and give us a chance to make this game better than ever!

You can check out the campaign here: https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/anchoragames/forgotten-myths-digital-card-game

During this Kickstarter, you can back the campaign and get awesome rewards for your support. You can take a look at the reward tiers from the campaign page. All you need to do is to click on the Backer Tier on the right hand side of the page, create a Kickstarter account, fill in your payment details, and you are done!

Do not worry, Kickstarter is one of the most secure crowdfunding platforms worldwide, and their payment systems are 100% safe. Plus, if the campaign is not successful, you get your money back! So you win lots of great rewards if the campaign is a success, and get your money back if it fails! You have nothing to lose.



What are we promising?
Forgotten Myths is still in development, and can be a much better game with your support. We have lots of ideas we want to incorporate into the game, making it one of the most comprehensive cross-platform TCGs ever. We are promising to implement and introduce almost all of these features, including tournaments, crafting, multiplayer co-op, and even spectate and playback if we hit the stretch goals. Here is a sneak peek into some of these features:



No trading card game is complete without a proper tournament mode. Knowing that, our aim is to bring you not just one, but four different tournament modes in Forgotten Myths. These are;

1- Draft Mode: When you enter Draft tournaments, you will create your deck by selecting cards from a random group you are shown. Every time you select one card, you will be given another choice with a different set of random cards. Once your deck is ready, you will then play a total of 10 games with other players, and get rewards based on your performance!

2- Sealed Mode: In the Sealed tournaments, you will be given a limited number of random Booster Packs, and you will construct your deck with whatever you get from these packs. Once your deck is constructed, you will then play a total of 10 games, and get rewards based on your performance!

3- Constructed: In the Constructed tournament mode, the Server will initiate different tournament modes, with various themes, and you will prepare your deck with your own cards before you enter. Once you join the tournament, you will play 10 games, and get rewards based on your performance!

The best thing about these three tournament types is that you can start the tournament, play whenever you want, and finish it at your own pace. There will always be others to match up against.

4- Monthly Grand-Tournament: This is a 64-seat tournament where only the best players will be invited at the end of every month, and battle each other to claim the top spot -and the bragging rights, as well as awesome rewards.



One of the features that we most talk about with our community, and definitely the one that we want to introduce the most is the Multiplayer Co-Op mode. This is a 4-people raid mode where you and your friends will take on fearsome bosses.
We are going to add six raids initially, and increase the number of raids with each card expansion. But don't worry, every raid will consist of 2-4 boss encounters, with different themes and abilities, and AMAZING REWARDS (of course) that will reset every week!



Be warned. This is not a dumbed down, simplified fight that you go through the motions. We are talking about legitimate boss encounters with FOUR people joining a party to raid dark and dangerous dungeons. Each raid will be different and unique, and test your deck-building skills, strategy, courage and nerves as you fight your way amongst dreadful enemies and powerful bosses!





Crafting is a fun and engaging feature that should be available in all trading card games. We are planning to add a system in which the game rewards you with crafting materials based on your activity in the game, whether this be on single player campaigns or PvP battles, and once you collect enough materials, you will be able to craft the card of your choosing.



Craft-able cards will be exclusive, meaning they won't be obtained through booster packs or decks. Working your way to collecting the materials for your desired card and finally crafting it will be a rewarding experience!



Wait, there is more?

Forgotten Myths is an ever-evolving game, and we strive to make the game better every day. This is why we keep introducing new card expansion, new features, and new abilities as often as possible.

We have great ideas we want to incorporate into the game; new card types, abilities, and more. A good example is the much anticipated TRAP cards. These cards will be played face-down and your opponent won't be able to see which card you played, and where the trap is. The trap will be activated when your opponent plays a card on top of it.



Another great addition to the game will be the new hero powers! You might be asking, 'but hey, there are no heroes in Forgotten Myths?!' and you would be right. But that doesn't keep us from introducing this feature to the game. So, how will it work?



Each avatar will have a unique ability that can be activated once every turn. Different abilities will have different energy costs, and the outcome will affect the game. With 100 Avatars to choose from, you will have all the options you can have to complement your deck with the appropriate hero power, and defeat your opponents!

On top of these awesome features, we are going to give the game a total makeover, with full UI rework, brand new dynamic 3D Battlefields, exciting visual and audio effects. However, we don't have the resources to complete all of these things on our own. With your help, we can create a much better and more polished game. This is why are asking for your support!



Forgotten Myths needs your support more than ever now. It is the ultimate test to keep this game alive, and give it the final push to put its name amongst the successful online TCGs. The future of Forgotten Myths is now in your hands. If you want to be part of a great trading card game that is easy to play, super-interactive, and has a fantastic community, back Forgotten Myths today!

I'm a huge fan of Magic: The Gathering, and own most of the duels games they have put out, but the last few entries have been pretty disapointing. Thus, when I see new CCG games come out I rush to give them a try. Forgotten Myths is one such game and I really wanted to like it, as it seems to have a pretty good combat system and during my play time was bug free, but I found one huge thorn in the otherwise level playing field that forced me to give this game a not-recommended rating.

Pros:
Solid CCG, has what seems to be quite a few cards, factions (colors), and game modes.
Good graphics, smooth animations for the most part, though watching my energy (mana) card animations got old fast.
No game breaker bugs found like crashes, dissconects, etc.

Cons:
Really only one, and that is the huge paywall that is rather transparent at first but once you start trying to earn coins (the grindable free currency) and diamonds (the much less grindable real money currency) it becomes apparent. Your energy cards come in 3 rarities and add 1, 2, or 3 to your pool when played. You start with all 1 energy cards, but the computer controlled starting quest enemies soon start throwing down 2 and 3 energy cards, forcing you to play against someone with 2-3 times the resources very quickly. This is especially apparent because you can be unlucky and not draw an energy card to use every turn. You computer controlled enemies do not seem have this drawback, as in roughly 50 games I never saw an AI opponent not have an energy card to play untill it had exausted its hand at turn 5-6. They also seemed to have a 2 energy card (if it was in their deck) about 1/3 of the time in their starting hand. The ONLY way to get 2 and 3 energy cards is to use diamonds (you dont have enough at the start to even afford one) to buy "energy card" booster packs. Yup, they shoot you in the foot before you even get started.

TL;DR: This particular free to play game is sadly not free to play.

What FM does right:
+ Offers plenty of rewards for active players.
+ Offers an incentive to play and do well in ranked (Gems are awarded at the end of the month based on rank.)
+ Offers strategic lane-based gameplay that is very interesting.
+ Allows both players play the game simultaneously with alternating phases and initiative changes every round.
+ Allows players to buy Common/Uncommon singles of all factions and to sell unwanted cards.
+ Functioning Auction House.

What FM does wrong:
- Offers exclusive promo cards behind a paywall (which you can't buy with Gems earned for free unless you make an initial purchase of Gems.)
- Tiers newer faction boosters (Green, Blue, Forgers) behind a level restriction (6, 8, and 10 respectively), unless you buy them for Gems, which is a bit much and can easily be misconstrued as P2W.

Overall, Forgotten Myths is still very much a diamond in the rough, but if you give it a chance, you will find it to be a very rewarding and enjoyable experience. =)

Hi,

Don't judge a book by it's cover!

I say this because originally I didnt like this game. Like some of the other reviewers here, I spent very little time in the game and formed an opinion after seeing just the surface.

I admit that the tutorial needs some work and there are some gameplay mechanics that aren't obvious. I've chatted and emailed with devs and have seen them make changes and fix issues pretty much immediately.


There are still things to fix however they know about them and are working on it.


So, to the game review!

This is somewhat similar to other CCG games where you create your deck with cards and then play against an opponent (2v2 and guild wars coming soon).

You start with one of three starter decks and quickly get a booster pack. I was very frustrated in my first hour because I was struggling in the single player missions (there are a LOT of them). That being said, as soon as I leveled up a few times I gained +2 energy cards which made a HUGE difference.

Coins (the free in game currency) come fast enough that I was buying extra booster packs and even individual cards to strenghten my deck.

One interesting aspect of this that is different from MtG and Hearthstone is Fortifications. They are back-row units that offer bonuses and can garison some other units for added firepower.

Community is very good, I haven't seen a single troll in game and only 2 spammers (which get shut down right away due to the anti-spam feature of the chat).


I suggest you give this game a try and have patience enough to get past the first few levels, it really opens up after that.

The game has a referral system which confused me at first. Basically when you start it asks for a referral code which is the case-sensitive username of another player. As soon as you put in a code, you are rewarded with 2000 coins and some free cards (no need to purchase, just make sure you put in a real referral code). If you end up buying emeralds then the person you entered as referral gets a bonus just on your first purchase.

Short and Sweet: "Forgotten Myths is Free2Play Mid-Level dTCG that will appeal to players looking for a card game which is much easier to access for normal players - while diehard players can get into exceptionally high skill ceilings, a new dTCG player will still feel comfortable playing this."

(Note: The "Pay 2 Win" accusation in one review is silliness - out of the dozens of dTCGs I've tried, this is one of the 3 least pay-2-win of the genre, it's by far the cheapest to get into, and one of the two easiest to get a full playset in without spending a dime)

Disclosure:
- I first got directed to look at Forgotten Myths in 2013, in its early alpha stages. Gave my critique then, and forgot about it for the most part (though I did cover the initial starter decks as well, as I am prone to do, being known for TCG coverage)

- A few weeks ago, several active members of the Forgotten Myths community started popping up on my old coverage saying the game was coming to steam and I should come look at all the changes (several of which were things that I mentioned and that they fixed :D)

I will post a video at the end of this review where you can watch the game in action, the first half covers the user interface, costs, etc. and the second half covers gameplay (you can click an annotation to skip straight to the gameplay) - if you don't feel like reading, that will be your best option.

Main Bullet Points:

Pros:
:: Affordable "F2P dTCG" - It is free to play, and it is possible to get a very respectable collection fairly easily wtihout spending real money (in fact, in terms of dTCGs, this is undercosted - it's cheaper than almost every other dTCG out there, and paying money is not required to advance - doing the Single Player campaign alone will net you enough for a few decks, without spending a dime). Community is also fairly active at helping people get the rarer cards they may have trouble getting. You don't need to break the bank or spend months grinding hardcore to get a full playset (unlike Hearthstone/Duelyst/MtG/Hex)

:: Good Entry or Mid-level dTCG - It is an accessible dTCG, simpler than some, but still enough depth of strategy to be enjoyable - diehard MtG enthusiasts probably won't be impressed by its depth, but fans of games like SolForge and the such, will find its got several layers of strategy and a number of nice features.

:: Good Community - the community has always been nice, sure with the surge of Steam players that may change, but there's a strong friendly community that's been there for 3 years, and will help you out. If you see a DoW member, you can usually ask them for help.

Cons:

:: User Interface still a little unpolished - being honest, it could use a little bit of work, it's still fairly rough looking, though it has improved - the gameplay is all there, but there's a few things they could do to make it 'feel' more satisfying (without changing the mechanics, the actual game is fun)

:: Prices are "weird numbers" - this is likely where the "Pay 2 Win" comment is coming from, the crystals are bought in weird amounts, and the costs are weird amounts, making the 'quick math' a little more annoying. They are planning to fix this though and adjust bought emeralds to match this. They were very open to the discussion on it, and took my experience with the genre into account during the discussion.

I can go on further, and if you have any questions about the game, I'm more than willing to answer them myself - as I've gotten nice crash courses in many of the things I missed as a new player.
