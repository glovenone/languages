Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“As it is now, Super Dungeon Run is completely playable from start to finish (as well as being hugely fun), but it is our intention to release initially through Steam’s Early Access. This gives us the opportunity to fully flesh out the game as we envision it and make it a truly satisfying experience.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We anticipate that it will take approximately 3-6 months to complete everything we hope to complete as part of the Early Access period. However, we're the sort of studio that takes quality very seriously, so will not come out of Early Access if we are not fully satisfied with the state of the game at that time.

It is our intention to keep you well informed by posting regular updates and announcements about the current state of the game!”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We hope to expand the game in the following ways:

  • Graphics - Adding dynamic lighting and shadows will not only make the game look fantastic, but will also open up new gameplay possibilities; traps may be hidden in the darkness and monsters will lurch at the party from the shadows.
  • Outfits - New costumes for your heroes to wear!
  • Magic - New magic! Imagine raining down a meteor storm on your enemies, or frying them with a lightning strike!
  • Enemies - Expect to see more rank and file creatures as well as unique boss monsters!
  • Environments - New monsters will need new places to call home, so adding more dungeon types is a no-brainer.
  • Quests - A greater variety of quests increases the lifespan of the game and keeps things fresh and interesting.
  • Abilities - New abilities and special moves for the heroes. Anyone familiar with skills in MOBAs or MMOs will immediately understand how these would work and how they can introduce endless tactics and strategies.
  • Deaths - Super Dungeon Run should be funny even when your heroes die. If you’ve seen the cartoon violence of Itchy and Scratchy, you’ll know what we mean!
  • Steamworks - Steam specific content like achievements and trading cards.

It's important to remember that, despite our best intentions, some or all of these features might not make it into the game. Again, we really want to make the very best game we can for you, but can only do that if the game is successful enough for us to continue development on it.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is fully playable and stable right now. Currently the game includes:

  • The village - a central hub that includes shops and upgrades
  • Main quests - special tasks the player can perform for rewards
  • Side quests - side tasks the player can perform for extra EXP
  • Party leveling system - levels that boost various stats
  • Hero outfits - adventuring classes that the villagers can become
  • Items - magic consumables
  • Crafting - stat upgrades to outfits and items
  • How to play - tutorials to educate the player
  • Credits - who made the game
  • User Interface - the interface that allows the player to interact with the game
  • Dungeons - environments in which the main game takes place
  • Enemies - adversaries the player will encounter
  • Traps - hazards the player will encounter
  • Chests - containers that the player can get things from
  • Doors - barriers for the player to open
  • Props - furniture and dressing for the environments
  • Audio - music and sound effects the player hears across the game
  • Loot - valuables collected by the player
  • Steamplay - Linux and Mac versions of the game
”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price during Early Access will be lower than when the game leaves Early Access and is officially released. The price may or may not gradually rise as we add more content and features during the Early Access period. The best thing to do is buy as early as possible to be certain of getting the cheapest price!”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are very keen to engage with the community that Super Dungeon Run is attracting. We want it to be a fun, inclusive place for players to hang out and talk about the game.

We have a strong vision for the development of Super Dungeon Run, but we really want to hear feedback and suggestions from the community on all aspects of the game. For example, if there is overwhelming support for a player requested feature or piece of content, we would be stupid not to listen to the fans!”

About This Game

Super Dungeon Run is a fast paced, addictive action game in which you guide a party of hapless heroes on a quest for loot in the depths of a dark and dangerous dungeon!




You’ll always start off with a small group of peasants. Here they are, the little scamps:



The peasants aren’t… how shall I put this… the smartest of individuals, but they’re a merry bunch and are always eager to please! They like nothing better than a good dungeon run, but sadly, they’re not very good at it.

That’s where you come in! Try and get them to the exit stairs safely. Everything else is a bonus as far as I’m concerned!


  • Super Dungeon Run is a massively single player roguelike RPG! It is designed to be accessible and addictive. It’s part Diablo, part Temple Run and part Pikmin.
  • Different every time! Semi-procedural levels mean no two dungeon runs will be the same.
  • Epic equipment! Unlocking new outfits and potions for your heroes will greatly improve their strength and capabilities, allowing them to get deeper into the dungeon for greater rewards.
  • Deadly traps and dangerous monsters! Encounter all manner of creatures and contraptions that are focused solely on your premature demise in a variety of violent (and usually messy) ways.
  • Multiple dungeon types! Discover multiple dungeons and their various inhabitants, such as the musty, mildewy cellar, or the eerie, enigmatic crypt.
  • Exciting content! Complete quests for colourful characters or see how far down into the dungeon you can make it. Will you discover what lurks in the deep dark depths?
  • Get loot! Make your fortune by pilfering gold from the dank denizens of the dungeon and discover valuable materials to craft upgrades for your heroes.
  • Level up your party! Gain experience and stop your heroes from spectacularly dying in the dungeon… for a while anyway!

Summary: Real time party based dungeon crawl
Multiplayer: No
Completion: 5 hrs
Cards: No
Cloud: Yes

Super Dungeon Run was a rather fascinating experience. It's rare for roguelikes to feature entire parties these days, and SDR takes that up to eleven by allowing you to gather 20 allies at once (or more if you find and free them) and wander around with your lynch mob to stomp over the opposition. It's a chaotic game, and easy to jump in and start whacking away at things.

Super Dungeon Run is a realtime isometric dungeon crawler. I'm not exactly sure if the game uses procedural generation. At the very least quest floors are all the same, but I haven't really noticed any other floors repeating. Combat is nice and simple. Merely move your men in the vicinity of an enemy and they will gang up to beat the poor sobs. Two units cannot occupy the same space, so it is recommended to bring some ranged classes along to maximize damage.

The party structure in the game takes some getting used to. Classes you hire are not automatically a part of your party. To actually make use of classes, you must find them in chests, which upgrades one of your peasants. I like that you can occasionally free a prisoner and add him to your party, which helps offset a bad room that nearly wiped the party.

The loot system is a bit odd. There's no equipment in chests. Instead you may find potions, crafting materials, class upgrades, or cash. Completing a floor quickly while grabbing all the coins and killing all the enemies will net you better rewards at the end of the floor. The crafting materials are used to upgrade your class equipment, which affects all units of that particular class. The drop rate isn't too bad and doesn't require an excessive amount of grinding.

At this time the game balance is a bit out of whack. Early on it is difficult as you have a small party with low levels. But a few upgrades turns that all around and makes floors significantly easier. By the end of the game enemies were nothing more than a nuisance. While there's a fair amount of enemy variety, they are all definitely susceptible to simply being mobbed. If there were some enemies immune to ranged or melee, that could help spice things up a bit. Huge bosses with aoe attacks could also be fun. Maybe some enemies who uses status effects such as poison or charm that can only be countered by a cleric would be nice to have around.

Traps are highly lethal in this game. As your party grows in size, it becomes very clumsy as your death blob moves around haphazardly trying to follow your mouse cursor. Otherwise easily avoided traps become meat grinders because your poor men do not know how to move intelligently or one at a time. It's not always easy to spot traps in time either, such as flaming traps firing from a south wall. If there's one class I would like to see in the game it's a rogue to disable traps because boy those traps kill.

This is the first game I've seen where a mere table can kill you by doing nothing. It manages this by blocking a path, so your troops may end up getting stuck and left behind. I've had a few rooms where I had to walk at snails pace trying to get everyone to avoid walking into a table. I would have liked the ability to change formations, such as single file, because rooms such as these, or traps you must walk around are among the most lethal in the game.

The graphics are pretty good. They are very clear, and show no slowdown, and the animations are fun to look at. For being early access there's a surprising amount of polish here. The music is is also good, though I would have liked a few more songs, as they get repeated often. I haven't noticed any bugs, so good job on that front.

The game doesn't exactly end as best I can tell. Once you've hit max level and finished the last quest, nothing new opens up. I can't help but want that last bit of closure. Thus I judged the end of the game to be max level and completing all quests, which all in all takes about 5 hours. Even though Super Dungeon Run is still considered in early access, in it's current state it's entirely enjoyable. Even the base price is quite reasonable for what you get. I give Super Dungeon Run a 8/10. It's unfinished, but you can definitely see the potential they've got going.

** Key was provided by developer for review purposes **

Super Dungeon Run

Super Dungeon Run is a fast paced, addicting game. In the game you control your greedy gold loving heroes fighting through dungeons, and dying to traps and enemies for a bit of loot. I will add a TL;DR at the end.

Gameplay/Controls

The controls are very simple as you use your mouse to guide you greedy heroes through the dungeon and use the 1, 2, and 3 keys to activate potions to help your heroes out.

The gameplay is very enjoyable. You start off with a set number of greedy peasants (you will know why I say "greedy" after) and fight through a dungeon to collect loot. In the game you can accept quest with the first one being to steal back a watermelon. The dungeons consist of floors which are anywhere from 10 seconds to 3 minutes long. As you continue to play this game you will unlock more heroes, more potions, and more quests in which you will get your butt kicked a bit. A key thing to note is that the heroes are very, VERY, greedy. More than a couple times a few coins would be lying around, and my heroes would run to the coins getting themselves, and most of my other heroes killed because of their greed. If you go too far away from one hero they will no longer be part of your party and you will need to go back to get them. In the game there are 7 different types of heroes (including peasants) and 4 different types of potion.

Graphics/Sound

The graphics are cartoony and seem a bit paper-like as of now. The area and village are rendered as a more 3D structure. The units are each unique in their design and very enjoyable to play. The music is done amazingly in this game. The music actually reminded me of an old game I used to play. Either way the music is very enjoyable.

Pros/Cons

Pros

+ Good music
+ Quick-paced
+ Simple
+ Quests are fun
+ Rewards you as you play and level up
+ Potions are fairly balanced
+ 7 different heroes and 4 different potions
+ Great art style

Cons

- Easy to lose a hero
- Slight grind
- Death is tasteless (they just flop over no matter what)

Suggestions

These are my personal suggestions for the game. My suggestions do not need to be taken seriously as they are my personal opinion for hopefully adding to the game to make it better.

- Controller Support
- Customization of heroes (different weapons and articles of clothing to choose from)

Final Thoughts

I have greatly enjoyed my time playing Super Dungeon Run. I have seen what the developer hopes to add and I am looking forward to it (especially funny deaths). The quests are nice and it was fun to play through this game. The only bad things that nudged at me was how easy it was to lose a hero, and how the heroes’ greed got me killed more than a few times. Overall I'm happy to have had the chance to play this early access game.

TL;DR

Super Dungeon Runner is fast-paced with floors in a dungeon ranging from 10 seconds to 3 minutes. The controls are simple as you only use your mouse and the 1, 2, and 3 keys. There are quests, potions, and of course your little dungeon running heroes in this game (7 heroes and 4 potions). The heroes are a bit greedy sometimes getting themselves killed for some coins. You fight through dungeon, gain money, get heroes and potions, upgrade said potions with crafting, and upgrade heroes as you level up. Truly fun and quick, Super Dungeon Runner is already a great game. Just in case you can check the features list to see if anything has been added since the time of this review.

Would you dare vicious traps, monsters, and inevitable death all for some shiny loot and gold? Well the peasants in Super Dungeon Run would! SDR is a strange mash up of roguelike, endless runner and RPG. There are quests, upgrades, and plenty of randomly generated death rooms, and of course delicious amounts of treasure! As your peasants run and collect gold you will begin collecting upgrades in the form of potions and costumes. These allow your peasants to become much more extraordinary and wield incredible abilities, such as the Sorceroress or the mighty Knight. I was pleasantly surprised by the amount of replay value and depth SDR has for it's adorable simple graphics and gameplay. I wasn't expecting things like crafting, class upgrades and skill trees. Where things need more polish is in the dungeon running itself. Let me be clear, everything works very well while running, you unlock potions, classes and move your poor adventurers about in a hilarious drunken wave. The problem is there is a bit of lack of interaction when the action kicks in. You kinda just sit there and watch what happens. Using a potion occasionally to help your adventurers out of a jam. However, things like class abilities and spells are already planned for final release and will more then likely take care of this aspect. In all Super Dungeon Run for it's price is an absolute steal and highly recommended to those looking for a funny and relaxing roguelike.

Early Access Review
Posted: 27 September, 2015
In it's current state, I can definitely recommend it.
I mean, c'mon...for the price of a Wendonaldby's fast-food meal, I've had almost 6 hours of entertaining and addictive gameplay. I've done all the available quests but still have a few more levels to unlock, so it does have a decent amount of replayability already.

Normally I'm very wary of early access games in general, but 2 things about this one:
1. Got it in a bundle for a few bucks.
2. Is more complete than many games that graduate from early access to full release.

As for the reviewer that mentioned something something grind blah blah... I very quickly (maybe <2 hours played) had more than enough gold/crafting materials than I could even use. If I had one complaint right now, it'd be that it's almost too easy to gain the resources needed for upgrades.

There's a ton of potential here. Could really be something great if the dev takes into consideration some of the suggestions on the forum.

So yeah, go ahead and get your gross burger and old stale french fries and regret it later when you need to run to the toilet.
OR, get this. Cause you won't regret this!

Early Access Review
Posted: 18 August, 2015
I bought this game after i saw a video of it online and i love it. Altough i think its great it still has some downsides i want to list.

- not enough upgrades (5 upgrades per character)
- upgrades not visible (would be cool to see some changes to the characters after upgrading)
- the resource balance is broken (the first 20 lvls you are always hurting for something after that you have tons of that stuff and cant even spent it)
- you are somewhat forced to do the same dungeon over and over again because the quest dungeons never change and you dont want to miss out on the extra XP because this will be your limiting factor after lvl 20

Everything else seems fine to me for an Early Access Game. I played it for about 4-5 hours and could finish the Game (all upgrades /lvl 32) in 1 more hour. So you get about 1hr of Gametime per buck which is a realy good deal. Plus you can play freemode without quest and random generated dungeons to see how far you can go.
