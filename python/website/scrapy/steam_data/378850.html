What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We initially created this game for Otronicon Game Jam in 36 hours.
It was the first project together for my son and I...we didnt want to quit...and then we just couldn't.
So once we had 25 levels you can't beat...we decided to keep growing this thing.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Maybe a year at most.
Everything that will be going into the game has already been decided.
We update frequently.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full game will consist of 75 levels, 15 bosses, 3 mini games and Galagan's Island ~ Metagalactic Monstrosities. Metagalactic Monstrosities is essentially Galagan's Island 1.5 and will be offered as FREE DLC. Co-Op mode will also be available for online play.
Not to mention an 85 track OST.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“-25 complete single-player swarm levels and 5 complete boss levels, each with their own unique musical tracks and swarm formations.
-Mac and PC compatibility.
-Support for both keyboard and gamepad control.
-Ship abilities scale with game progression. The further you get, the faster and stronger your ship.
-Damage & Repair Health System. As you take damage, your ship will decrease in ability to perform. After every Boss fight, a space station will appear, forcing you to choose what to repair before continuing on in the game.
-Co-Op Mode
-Gorgeous pixel art.
-Still not clear exactly where we are? Try out our Free Demo version. It's just like that but several times the content - all just as fun, polished, and wonderfully difficult.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Two price tiers, Early Access & Complete.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Of course we have a plan...but it means nothing...without you.
So let's do this together.
This is our very first game...we aren't going anywhere.
So...we want to connect with all of you now.
We are very active on social media...and now that we've published...we will start to make the convention circuit......These games don't sell themselves.”

Galagan's Island is an Arcade style Space Shooter, paying loving homage to classic arcade style and difficulty.
Currently featuring 25 LEVELS of handcrafted whimsical charm.
The formations are so pretty, you almost don't want to destroy them. LOL!
Galagan's Island utilizes dynamic scaling of ship abilities. As you progress through the game YOUR ability to perform improves as well.

Original Soundtrack featuring smooth jazz and hip-hop styles, personalized trip-hop TRACKS, 8 bit chip-tunes & 4th wall frenzies....not to mention a slew of rising Indie musicians ...brought to you courtesy of Forever Humble Productions.

Optimized For Both Keyboard and Gamepad Controls.


This is our first release under Skinny Jean Death Studios....a video game development company founded by teen programmer Jonathan Cunningham.

Early Access

Pros: Overall, the gameplay is smooth and feels very polished even for an early access game. Graphics remind me a bit of the past (Good thing! I am estatic when it comes to playing a game with retro designs) while still maintaining the feel of a newer game. The enemy fire is VERY tough to dodge and that definately adds to the difficulty. Don't expect the first levels to be a push over. While on the topic of the enemies, I love how I'm killing adorable aliens. Music for the first boss is shows that the developer(s) have an eye for ingenuity. The whole flow of this game just... works!

Cons: Right off the bat, the title screen feels like it takes too long to come up. If you had the option to hit fire to get into the game menu faster, this would be a non issue. I also feel that when you're not using hover, you fly back to the bottom of the screen far to fast. I'm almost always using hover because of this. Support for more controllers, if possible, would be awesome. There are a few gamers out there that still prefer Logitech pc gaming controllers and would most likely be disconcerted to have to purchase a controller to enjoy this game.

Other Comments: Game is designed with unity and configured for use with the PS3 and Xbox 360 Controller. These are the best two controllers to use. This said, the Steam Controller will work and most of the controls are the same; fire is a different button.

Overall, the pros definately out weigh the cons which honestly are more like me nit picking the little things. You can definately see the passion the creator(s) had while making this game as it shows in every aspect of the game. I can also see myself playing this game again in the near future and am very eager to try to beat this game. Support this developer and enjoy a very fun game.

Early Access Review
Posted: 22 November, 2015
Galagan's Island: Reprymian Rising is a classic arcade shmup shooter in a modern shell.
No continues, no lives, no checkpoints. You die, you go back to the beginning.

Gameplay:
25 Levels of chaotic shooting and trying not to die. And it's fun!
You have powerups like shield and triple fire that make it easier to blast aliens.
Everything is very fluid and well-optimised which makes the game very enjoyable.
Game features a local co-op so you can play with a friend, or a cat, or you can be two players at once if you don't have any friends (don't worry, I will always be your friend)

Graphics:
Cool retro style with modern effects. Lots of explosions and other similiar effects commonly seen in today's games. Enemies are pretty generic for a bullet-hell game, various blobs and space octopuses, but that's okay.

Sound:
Most sound effects don't stand out that much so there's not much to say about them. But the music is awesome! An interesting blend of many genres like electronica, jazz and even hip-hop. If you're planning on buying the game,I encourage you to also buy a soundtrack, If you enjoy at least 2 of these genres.

Controls:
Kinda weird. You must hold hover if you don't want to be at the very bottom of the screen.
When you hold hover your ship also steers slower, so it's really hard to dodge a barrage of projectiles when you're in the middle of the screen. The flip ship works only when you have the double ship powerup. If you don't have it on levels when enemies spawn behind your back, then you must wait till they break the formation and follow you, which can take a while.

Overall, nothing ground-breaking, but simply an enjoyable indie game that features an awesome soundtrack and doesn't cost a fortune. Give it a try, it can surprise you.

Early Access Review
Posted: 26 November, 2015
'Galagan's Island: Reprymian Rising' is a schmup/shooter that is still in Early Access, however while it is a complete game with 25 levels and 5 end bosses the developer is busy working on alot more levels and more end bosses. The game will be on sell until Dec. 1, 2015 for $1.99.

GAMEPLAY: Your typical schmup with gameplay that is already tight. You can play as a Single Player or Co-Op. While optimised for a control pad (I'm using my Logitech Xbox 360 controller in X Mode) it also works with a keyboard. There are power-ups, and you can link two ships, but you only get one life. You die. You dead. Each enemy target is worth 1 point, but enemy hits to your ship will deduct 10 points from your score. So shoot well. Dodge well. Live long (and prosper).

GRAPHICS: They are quite stunning, with several settings and resolutions to choose from. After I took several screenshots the developer contacted me to try a different setting and resolution. I have captured several screens at various settings and resolutions.

SOUND: Not as stunning as the graphics, but acceptable. As for the songs they are a matter of personal taste.

ACHIEVEMENTS & TRADING CARDS: 34 Achievements just went live, of which I have earned 2*. I only downloaded this game today so give me a break that I haven't earned more. The developer told me that more Achievements and some Trading Cards will be added as more levels and bosses are added. But so far I've only heard of 1 person beating the 25 levels and 5 bosses (one boss after each group of 5 levels) in 'Galagan's Island: Reprymian Rising' as it stands.

EXTRAS: There is a DLC game called 'Galagan's Island: Metagalactic Monstrosities' which requires the first game to be purchased and installed. In this DLC game you play as the 5 bosses. This DLC is FREE with the purchase of 'Galagan's Island: Reprymian Rising OST,' which replaced some of the song tracks of the original version. However the songs swapped out are included as bonus tracks. Currently you must own the original version to buy this version. The OST version is currently on sell for $1.49 until Dec. 1. 2015. So that's two versions of the same game, plus the bonus game 'Galagan's Island: Metagalactic Monstrosities' for a total of less than $3.50. A bargin.

RECENT UPDATES INCLUDE (Developer):

*In response to player feedback, we've turned down the "floatiness" of the player ship movement to tighten up the player's controls and emphasize the bullet-dodging slow motion mechanics of the game.

*Updated controls screen to reflect the hold-to-fire change and added a message to the screen that the game was designed with gamepad in mind and that it is recommended input method.

*Adjusted UI responsiveness.

*Adjusted position of the "Back" option on the Volume Control Slider.

*Added solid black backgrounds behind the Pause and Options UI menus.

*New soundtrack. The songs switched out will stay on the OST as Bonus Tracks.

I bought everything. So $3.50 for two great games and two music options.

Overall I would rate this Early Access version 5.0 out of 5.0. Or, put another way, 10 out of 10. As a member of highscore.com I have requested that this game be added there. I currently have approx. 2,072 High Score Trophies and one Patch. Writing this review you'd be surprised (or not) at how many times I typed 'Giligan's Island' or 'Gilligan's Island.' Ok, I'm a baby boomer in love with Dawn Wells (Mary Ann Sommers), so cut me some slack. :)

* I have now earned 6 achievements and beat the first boss. :)
