About This Game

Dragon Saga is a free to play arcade style side scrolling 3D Fantasy MMORPG set in a world of Dragons and magic. Choose one of six classes (Warrior, Mage, Thief, Archer, Shaman, and Twin Fighter) and explore a rich world filled with unique monsters. Power up to unlock devastating abilities, hidden combos and hilarious skill effects.

Explore a Wonderful Fantasy world filled with Hilarious Monsters and Characters.

  • Engage in Dragon Saga's unique Skill-Based Action Combat.
  • Adventure with your friends through over 30 Unique Maps.
  • Starting with 6 Unique Starting classes expand into 30 different classes and customize your character.
  • invite your friends to your own Private Home with decorated with furniture that give you in-game buffs.
  • Take care and love a large variety of pets that gives you buffs and sometimes a ride.

I've played Dragonica on gpotato back in time several years ago. I've been playing this game since the 2009 release until the EU server closed down, moving to this last server. This was the mmo that I played for the longest time; it's incredibly fun (although frustrating sometimes), but the community always kept me playing. I feel like I've made some life long friends in this game.
The game went through a lot of changes; this server is the least pay-to-win-like, so far I didn't spend a cent on the game and levelled/geared up perfectly fine, which is a huge plus compared to the old EU server.
The combat system is fun too; the dungeons and grinding can become repetitive and boring though, especially after around lvl 40-50, when the only way to level up fast is to grind tactic mode.
There are events in the game regulalry, so there is usually always something to do besides the usual gold farming when you're already higher level.

To sum up the game:
Pros:
+ fun combat system
+ relatively fast levelling
+ not cash-shop heavy/pay2win
+ regular ingame events
+ awesome community
+ mods/staff are active on forums

Cons:
- grinding/questing can become repetitive
- many bugs
- translation errors/typos
- duping is still a thing
- occasional lags or crash-to-desktop (although I didn't have this for a long time now)
- can't log in to an already existing account through the Steam version (this is probably because of the achievements)

Overall, it is worth to try. It's not the best mmorpg out there, but there is definitely something about the game that makes people play it for such a long time.

Dragon Saga. Where do I begin? This game brings back so much nostalgia that it means more to me than my education. I played this game back when it was called "Dragonica" and the basic feel is still there. Same simple, yet difficult to master gameplay, Same classes, with even more to add to the mix, and same community. The players in this game are awesome.

Gameplay: 9.5/10

You'll encounter a few glitches here and there, but the gameplay throughout is fun, consistent, and active. Early in the game you get a feel for fighting in the arcade-style scenarios, and you'll be using that knowledge you gain to expand your skills as you level. There's a ton of variety in the classes and skills that allows seasoned players to seamlessly switch between punishing monsters with powerful ground combos, and tossing the mobs like ragdolls to deal even more damage in the air! While some of the bosses can get a bit repetitive, you'll love wailing on them and watching your reward explode out of them!
I've never been a fan of party play, personally, but you'll want to team up with other players to tackle the bigger dungeons. If you don't like the group aspect of gaming, never fear, as a skilled player can handle most of the content on their own, if they have the funds to build a perfect character.

Story: 7.5/10 - SPOILERS in this section!

There are TONS of quests in Dragon Saga. Tons of little storylines for many npcs, as well as the main story. You're a decendant of one of the five legendary hero on an adventure to join the Dragon Fellowship and seal away the Dark Dragon Elga, who was released by the Black Witch Paris, a legendary hero who has gone rogue. Your adventure brings you through Paris' path of destruction on her way to the Tower of Drakos, Elga's prison. You battle her, and defeat her (if her damage reflect skill doesn't one hit you first!) with her final breath (is she dead? hmmm.) she is able to release the Dark dragon, who roosts at the top of your final destination. The Black Claw Nest. Seal him away before he can lay waste to the entire world. Not to mention there's loot in it for you.
Besides the main story, there are a lot of little sidequests to do. I personally don't find them all that interesting, but there is tons of funny dialogue.

Graphics: 9/10

The colorful, rich graphics make wailing on foes and other players a ton of fun! Some of the skill animations are hilarious, and make me smile every time I see them. One class actually summons an early boss in his underwear to freeze your opponents, while another spawns a huge wrestler spinning towards your foes! My personal favorite character designs are those of the Shaman class. Their summons/familiars are very well designed, and look intimidating. That huge Dark Knight strikes fear into the hearts of pvp players!
The world is beautiful, which each area of the continent of winds, and the continent of chaos having their own unique look. You travel from a nice meadow, that slowly transitions to a volcanic area in the early-mid game, which gives you a feeling of "growing up" in your fist playthrough, as you watch as your charater makes their way from such a welcoming place, to a treacherous area. While there are a lot of reskinned enemies in later areas, it's forgivable, as many games do it. The visuals are great.

Support: 10/10

The Support team for Dragon Saga is wonderful. They're always willing to help in any way they can. Their polite, knowledgeable staff show that they care for the players. I've had issues taken care of very quickly, and with the sheer number of tickets I've sent about different things ranging from bugs, to unruly players to suggestions are always handled with professionalism. Trust me, you may not see them, but the staff are lurking in-game, and they're watching out for rulebeakers.

Community: 7/10

This really can't be helped by the developers, but the community is a little small, and naturally, small interpersonal issues between players can escalate rather quickly. This is apparent with the pvpers of the game, with players occasionally shaming each other in the trade chat channel, using foul language, and personal insults. On the other hand, a friend in Dragon Saga is a great friend. Begging for items and gold is surprisingly uncommon, which is wonderful. I've met some awesome people here, and I've opened a facebook group for the community, where I've met some interesting people.

F2P vs P2W: 8.5/10

I've never been afraid to invest in a game that I enjoy. As an experienced gamer, I have to say that I'm impressed with Dragon Saga's cash shop. The items are reasonably priced, and dare I say, you can build a nice character without spending a cent. Be prepared to farm your butt off if you want to keep your wallet full, but the option is certainly there. The general agreement on the forum is that Dragon Saga is "pay to go faster" as opposed to "pay to win." Most of the premium items can be obtained without spending money. Insurance scrolls to prevent your items from breaking during the upgrade process can be bought for gold in the market, or exchanged for Silver coins which are an uncommon drop from monsters. There is no shortage of events in which you can earn premium items, as well!

Overall: 8.5/10, yes, I'm biased.

This game holds a very special place in my heart. It kept me sane throughout university, and continues to provide me with tons of entertainment and friends to hang out with. I highly recommend checking it out, despite it's many bugs and flaws. I have nearly 3000 hours of LOGGED IN time on my main character, and another 1000 or so spread throughout my alts, none of which I regret. except that one time where I overpayed for something in the market! :P
Dragon Saga is my favorite F2P MMO, and certainly worth checking out for it's unique gameplay and community.
