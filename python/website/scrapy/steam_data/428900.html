About This Game


The goal of the game is to survive for more than 60 seconds on each level, dodging platforms and objects, listening to bitpop soundtracks. Four levels, online-records, achievements and trading cards are waiting for you.

Features:

  • Challenging gameplay, aimed at reaction
  • Four unique levels, each with its difficulty, design and music
  • More than 20 achievements, 7 of them are hidden
  • Online leaderboards
  • Steam trading cards

Overall
---------

I felt like this game had a nice concept, but it was excecuted poorly.
This delevoper doesn't seem to have much experience, but I believe he/she can make it much better.

------------
The Good
------------

Completely original gameplay, which does come with its own patterns and mechanics.

Difficult enough to keep me going.

Online leaderboards.

-------------
The Neutral
-------------

Chiptune music is not very encouraging, but at least each level has its own song.

UI of the game is a little messy, but navigatable. The high score and achievement screens were my dislikes.

The bars under the levels to signify progress become completely useless later on. The scores should be displayed there instead.

The point system is just weird.

------------
The Bad
------------

The game has ONE background the entire game, so when you're not dodging there's just a white screen.

The hitbox of your character(?) should be the screen, but instead it's a box that's revealed to you in the tutorial, which is really confusing.

There's iterally an achievement for doing everything in the game, including opening it. It's just excessive.

There's an achievement that if you don't get right at the start, you have to wipe all your records and achievements (Steam too!) to get it. (Play a level before playing the tutorial. When asked about a wipe option, the dev said "Is it really necessary?"...)

The blocks are one full color, without any edges...

-------------
Conclusion
-------------

Gameplay: 8/10
Graphics: 3/10
Music: 5/10
Replay Value: 9/10


Final Score: 6.25/10

Linea is a minimalistic designed 3D dodge game like "The Collider" or "Aaaahhh for Awesome".
Mostly in a white enviroment you fly quickly through dark gates. You must dodge the procedural horizontally and vertically generated red bars. In the tutorial you see your little grey square, later in-game you don't. So you must handle your WASD controls gently because the deadly outer walls, too. Apropos tutorial: there's one missable achievement to play the first level before starting with the tutorial. If you didn't you must activate the reset button and all of your progress is lost.
I hate point-of-no-return achievements like this. Conclusion: Linea is a simple dodge game with just average graphics and a nervy gameboy soundtrack. Looks like cheap mobilware to me.
