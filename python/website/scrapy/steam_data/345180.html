About This Game

Victor Vran is an isometric Action-RPG with a massive selection of weapons, items, outfits, demon powers and destiny cards. Experience intense combat action with dozens of enemies attacking you from all sides and unleash powerful skills to finish them off!
Victor Vran lets you decide how to play the game. Forge your own personal version of Victor thanks to a vast array of powerful weapons, game-changing outfits, wicked demon powers and destiny cards. Use special moves, combine skills and weapons to wipe out hordes of hideous beasts and clever boss monsters.

One of the most fun action RPGs I've played.

Game mechanics:
There are no class limitations. This means you will be using a lot of different tools and combinations: each type of weapon has 2 secondary skills and you can fast switch between two weapons in the middle of combat, making management of skill cooldowns very interesting. You can also equip two different Demon spells which use an Overdrive bar. Overdrive can be gained in different ways depending on which type of outfit you are wearing. There are two potion/grenade slots with their own cooldowns. There's a whole system of passive buffs using "Destiny cards", these cards can be crafted together to be upgraded and you can equip many of them as long as they don't have a combined value higher than your Destiny points. There's also a "roll" evade mechanic in the game, which is very important as a lot of enemies have strong attacks with tells that allow you to avoid them. Bosses are really fun to play, each of them has some particular mechanic that makes them unique instead of just being a bigger mob.

Replayability:
In addition to the replayability that comes naturally with this kind of games, Victor Vran also offers many Challenges and secrets tied to each of the individual areas in the game. All areas can be replayed and restarted as many times as you want, and each area can be accessed via fast travel from the game's hub. Additionally there are "Hexes", which are basically handicaps you can activate or deactivate to make the game harder in exchange for extra XP and more juicy drops.

Controls:
Controls are fine but I recommend spending some time remapping them to something you feel comfortable with. There's a jump mechanic which I find a little annoying, but it doesn't really matter because the jump doesn't have any real effect in combat, it's mostly used to access secrets.

Finally I want to say Victor Vran is one of those games that gets more fun if you play on Hard. You really need to use all your skills in the right situations instead of just spamming your skills.

I saw Victor Vran when it arrived on steam. Added it to wishlist. However, I didn't buy it but got V. Vran from Humble Bundle. I couldn't find enough time to play but I see Victor Vran is shorter than Diablo 3, Van Helsing and Torchlight. I beat the game within 6.5 hours only.

Victor Vran is surprisingly good. I did expect this game to be good but this is a very well made game. Developers are the Tropico's dev team. Tropico and Victor Vran? Didn't match huh? Well don't feel awkward cuz they did the job. Victor Vran is an ARPG game. Isometric playstyle, Hack and Slash mechanics (also some shooting) and the most important this is an indie game. You simply kill, demolish/destroy or blow up your enemies. Then loot, then complete your quest, level up et cetera. Classic isometric rpg as you can understand. I'd like to say the story is quite interesting from the beginning.

STORY (7/10) :

You are an old prince who uses a power and made a deal with demons (like Ghost Rider but thanks god you still look stylish) and messed up. You have an unknown voice in your head. That voice make (always) joke on you. That voice also does some joke about movies/games (kind of easter egg). I won't tell you who is the voice (no spoiler !). However it's a quite good narrator and funny.
As i said, Victor Vran was a stylish guy. But he messed up. His land and people paid a price like famine. He had to move on and sort of escape. Also he is looking for a friend called Adrian. Adrian is last located in Zagoravia. Victor Vran travels to Zagoravia, becomes a hunter... Then he finds out that his friends gone crazy and turned (not like a zombie). Victor Vran decides to stay in Zagoravia, fight with evil force of demon realm, help the Queen. The rest is pretty cliche actually.
Victor finds out about Queen's deal and Zagoravia's fate. Then he meets with a old ghost. He travels into the demon's realm. Meets a guy which is the main villain and most likely douch-ebag (also it's not hard to guess. Main villain is not that superb), kills him and saves the Zagoravia for a while...

Also you do look really stylish as hunter Victor. Costumes are really good looking. Other characters also looks good and have some nice quotes.

GAMEPLAY (8/10) :

Well, you can jump. You can actually jump. I jumped when i am in a labyrinth. I jumped from a height. That's actually awesome for a Izometric ARPG. Rolling mechanic works good. There is some different weapons ; melee ones are Sword,Hammer,Scythe, Rapier. Hack and slash mechanis are enjoyable. Shooting mechanics are a bit 'meh' but it is good at overall. You can use a gun or Lighting Gun (that's my favourite). Lighting Gun is awfully enjoyable to use.
There is some unusual weapons aswell ; Lightsaber and Rainbow gun for example. I couldn't get them but i can say they looks pretty awesome (i watched the videos). The amount of weapon type is not that much but still fair enough.
There is various types of enemies. The champions are mostly same with the normal creatures and they just have better damage,health or some special effects. Boss fights are incredibly well but there is a two spider boss fight and the first and the final boss are a bit same. I did expect more creative boss fights when I saw the Adrian boss fight but it is okay. They are challenging and kind of unique.
You can select rewards when you got leveled up. It can be a random reward, destiny card, some unique rune (i'll call them rune, they just simply are. You can use them for your weapon/armor), armor/weapon, a special demon power etc.
Destiny cards gives you some special effects and advantages (like frost attack to enemies, health or extra armor). You can equip a demon power which is extremely strong. Weapons have alignment ; Common/Uncommon/Rare/Ultra Rare and Legendary. (It doesn't say that is ultra rare or not but you can understand from the colors. Green is always common, blue is rare, orange is better and in this one purple is the most good one the legendary one.)
Armors does have some advantages and all of them looks so good.
You can upgrade your weapon simply forging 3 same kind of weapon together. Also you can use your runes at them. It unlocks at level 16.
You probably won't get a problem with amount of potion. Potions are highly usable and they have recharge time. So you can not spam a health potion :)
Inventory is easy to use. There is no side quests really but high amount of challanges. Another thing is there is some extra boss fights and levels. I've finished the game in one day. Played 6 hours and around thirty min. I can say this is not a speedrun at all. The game is quite short and easy (i don't know it's just me, it says 12 hours on howlongtobeat). If you are not used to play games like this play the game on easy mode. It is really easy. On normal, it's fair enough. You can still beat within 6-8 hours. I've explored some ares, got 22 level and finished the game. Worth the money but it is short. I haven't complete all of the ares and challanges tho. Optional things can extend the playtime to around 12-16 hrs.

Level Design (9/10) :

Hell yeah. That's the level design i was looking for. City level, forest level, hell/demon realm level. It's all detailed and colors are matched up great. Well my eyes usually got rotten because the levels are too detailed and there is a lot of mobs (creatures) and they do some speels, i also do some spells... It's a total chaos. But level design and creature design is so decent. Just makes my eyes tried (my eyes are a bit bad, may be that's why).
Hah and you can control the camera. I nearly forgot to say that.

Graphics (8.5/10) :

Graphics are decent aswell as like Level Design. Damn good art style. The performance of game is pretty good. I've got 60 fps on high settings (gtx970, others are written in my profile page). Got 30fps on ultra settings. It's fair enough but i got some fps drops on high when there is a lot of creature and animations like spell casting, special powers etc.
Nothing more to talk. Graphics are fine by me. Since this is a indie ARPG, i am okay with these graphics. It looks like 2014-2015 graphics and that's a good thing. Matches up with current gen.

SCORE : 80/100

Totally worth it. This is kind of subjective review but I did try to do my best. At least get this from sale and there is a upcoming Summer Sale.

May I say Geralt? Geralt of Rivia's voice actor Doug in this game. The voice really remembered me Geralt. It actually similar as Geralt. I nearly can say Victor Vran is Geralt of Rivia, lol. VICTOR MADA FAKIN VRAN!

Is it worth your time and money?

This one definitely is worth to check. I played it for two hours and the game is fun to play, Unlike many arpgs, Victor Vran's jumping ability you may use for tactical advantage, so this makes for a full another gameplay dimension.
Graphics are neat, really nice. Geralt is Victor, I mean they have the same voice actor. Other than that, you have all the ingridients which are needed to make good arpg. This is more action with some rpg elements, so it maybe is not fair to call it arpg.

Map seems quite big, however I played the game for 2 hrs, so I could be wrong. The map size is important to me, since I believe it is not worth playing if game has less than 20 hours of gameplay.
Another thing what is important are enemies which require different tactics to beat. Weapons are diverse and there is a plenty to choose from.

So, is the VV the best game in genre? No. But above middle, looking to sit in the upper tier of the division. And if you take into account the low price of the game (browse internet!!), this game is definitely worth to play and buy.
It is not Torchlight 2 or Grim Dawn, but it is damn close.

A ton of fun. Very fast and fluid gameplay, with lots of features that allow you to customize the experience to your liking. You aren't locked into a class and skill tree; instead your "build" is based on the weapons, outfit, powers and destiny cards that you equip (which you can change at any time). You can also adjust the difficulty on the fly by enabling or disabling five different "hexes" which make the game more challenging. Each map has a variety of optional challenges and secrets for you to complete to add another layer of challenge. Lastly, the "voice" inside your head throughout the game is humorous and entertaining (think of the narrator from the Stanley Parable). If you enjoy games like Diablo, Torchlight, etc. this game is a must buy.
