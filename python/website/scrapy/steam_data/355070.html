About This Game

TDP5: Arena - the outstanding Free-To-Play 3D online shooter game with RPG elements, styled as 2D platformer.
The game will immerse you into the world of underground fightings of Darkness Project.
Different game modes and wide choose of weapons will not leave you indifferent, and rating system, unique talents plus
other very interesting features of this game certainly do not make you bored!


  • Flexible talent system - there are 211 unique talents! Design your fighter by choosing the most important ones!
  • The achievement system - 390 achievements that will surprise you!
  • Dynamic Rating system - Play better than the others and receive rewards for that!
  • Flexible leaderboard - Now each player has a chance to find out where they will be better than the rest!
  • The friendly shop - you can either rent or buy good weapons that you liked!
  • System implants
  • Various amplifiers
  • More than 50 types of weapons
  • Interesting game modes - DM, TDM, CTF, DOM, ELM

I wrote a rather scathing review of TDP4 a week ago. It was really bad. One of the worst games I've ever played in my life. Of course, then, when I noticed that TDP5 was a thing, I had to take a look. And it's...a huge improvement.

The gameplay is still the same: It's a 2D shooter where you hop around and try to kill other people. This time around, however, it didn't somehow murder my PC: The game ran smoothly, with no hiccups. Everything controlled fine. This is a vast improvement over TDP4, where my machine was literally dying, just because of how unoptimized the game was. There's also now a third person mode, where you can aim at certain parts easier, such as the head. I thought that was a neat touch.

Even better, low levels even stand a chance! Looking at the guns, nothing seems drastically overpowered or anything at first glance. The 'premium' guns, that you pay real cash for, don't seem drastically stronger than the lower level ones. Maybe they fire a bit faster, or give someone a small edge, but I couldn't notice anything outside of that. Still, I was able to hold my own against people wielding a variety of weapons with my starting ones. It feels a lot more fair than it was in TDP4. I'm doing really well with the basic shotgun, which you can buy as soon as you have the in game money for it...which is around 850 or so.

Character wise, artifacts and stat points are gone, thankfully. Instead, you have a huge talent tree that you can upgrade with skill points, that you get for leveling. They upgrade your skill in a variety of ways. I went with the knife upgrades because, for whatever reason, that's the weapon I'm doing best with right now. I need to stress, you can't buy skill points with real cash. This is a huge improvement over the artifact system, that literally allowed you to buy power.

A lot of the weapons and items are locked by level, and all the things that improve your performance can also be bought with in-game money, which seems to be plentiful. The one major complaint I have is that they also use a "rent weapon" system. It costs a good deal of in-game money to buy weapons permanently, but it is possible, at least. As a whole, it seems a WHOLE lot more fair than TDP4. Implants are unlocked at level 3, which enhance your skill with certain weapons, but again, those can only be purchased with in-game cash.

Really, I didn't have a bad time with it, and I would recommend it, except for one thing: There's only one server, and it's based in Europe. Naturally, this causes a good number of lag issues in game, and makes it awkward to kill things if they're teleporting around. If you're not in Europe, I can guarantee it will be a rather uncomfortable time. When other servers arise, I could actually recommend this. At least, as far as I have played.

I haven't seen the pay to win aspect as other people have. Maybe they didn't look at the shop to see they can just buy new guns, I dunno. Either way, I don't think it is. At least not yet.
