About This Game

"unbearable... this place stinks...
i need to get out of here.
the boat is waiting for me."

The Guilt and the Shadow is a "short story exploration game" about the past, death, illness and the thin line between life and insanity. A 2D puzzle platformer inspired by the old point and click games.

The Guilt and the Shadow is technically a puzzle platformer, but this gameplay primarily exists as a way for the player to emerse themselves in THGATS' creepy atmosphere. The puzzles never get complex, and the 'platforming' does not even include a jump button, but here it works; everything works together to bring across the oppressive and hostile headspace of the game's protagonist.

The game can be finished in a Saturday afternoon, and I would recommend doing so in one sitting. The game's story if focused on the protagonist attempting to work his way through his grief and depends on each level setting a mood that is capitalized on in the next.

This game was like a punch to the gut. The whimsical art style matches the slow, subtle psychological horror perfectly. I was worried at first that the strange graphics would make this less enjoyable, but after starting the game it definitely fits.

Overall the puzzles were simple and the game quite short (I finished it in three hours), but the experience is definitely a memorable one! You play as a man suffering from mental illness as he tries to deal with his fractured memories and thoughts. A majority of the plot is told through flashbacks via floating text and disconnected psychiatric evaluations. It gets progressively darker as the story progresses.

All in all this is a simple puzzle game definitely worth experiencing!

Posted: 25 August, 2015
Having just finished this game a mere five minutes ago, I must say this title was a breath of fresh air into the horror genre. It plays out like one long psychological nightmare, where the plot is told through flashbacks and floating text. I liked the art style, and while there wasn't much music I enjoyed the bits and pieces of it that were scattered about. The game doesn't rely on jump scares, rather it unnerves you through its story, and certain gameplay elements. The sounds the mechanical birds make got to me, as well as the whispers when leaving a fire. While the game is short, the experience is one I won't soon forget.

I also feel like I should add that during one of the levels, I encountered a glitch that would not allow me to go down any ladders. This in turn blocked a path I needed to take. The end result was simply having to restart the game. It doesn't detract from my enjoyment overall, just something I felt was worth mentioning.

The guilt and the shadow is a grim puzzle game narrative by Frederico Machuca.

The video is here: http://youtu.be/X_sVRGPNO_s

As it says on the store page it is a game about death, illness and the thin line between life and insanity. Sadly this game therefore invokes the feelings it should and having lost a friend quite recently, I wasn't really able to play the game that well prior to reviewing.
This is also one tip, I'd like to give to you: If you are momentarily having a hard time, I would not really recommend to play the game.

Mechanicswise the game works fine, but there isn't much to it.
What I'd like to point out though is that the art and music blend in really nicely into the theme of the game which rounds up this whole experience.

Again I want to say the same thing I've said with Gravity Ghost: This is a game with a narrative behind it, so take yourself a bit of time and delve into it.

Sadly due to its narrative the game is very linear, so that might be something to consider.

Lastly I'd like to say that performancewise I really have nothing to complain about.

Conclusion:
The game is 10€ which might drive away some people, apart from that the game does its job well and in a later sale I'd consider it a good buy.
