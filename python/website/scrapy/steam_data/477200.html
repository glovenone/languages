About This Game

Road to Legend is a companion app for the Descent: Journeys in the Dark 2nd edition board game. When you play with Road to Legend, the app assumes the role of the overlord player, controlling monsters and inviting you to explore the dungeon in entirely new and unexpected ways.

At its heart, Road to Legend introduces a fully-formed, cooperative way to play Descent, freeing you and your friends to all play as heroes and defeat the darkness in Terrinoth. With a series of fully-formed quests and the ability to incorporate your entire collection of physical Descent expansions, Road to Legend provides a thrilling new variant on the classic Descent experience!

Vetano
( 3.2 hrs on record )
Posted: 26 June
Road to Legend is simply one of the best "expansions" you can get for Descent: Journeys in the Dark 2nd edition. The fact that this thing is free with fully functional campaigns is absolutely mind-blowing considering the production value (there are actual voice overs for the story of the campaigns).

The app follows the core rule set of D2E, but has additional rules in place because it's fully co-op and thus no player will play the role of the Overlord.

Core differences:

  • Cooperative mode: No overlord player means that you all lose or win together. The simple fact, however, that failing a quest doesn't mean that the campaign ends is a big plus over the already existing COOP expansions (Nature's Ire, Forgotten Souls, etc.). Monsters will follow the "orders" of the app, i.e. by engaging, spotting (getting in line of sight) or retreating from/to heroes. This works very well, considered many people might say that an "AI" will never be as dangerous as a real experienced overlord.
  • Campaign-Only: I believe this is absolutely fine, considering the app tracks every relevant information (gold from search tokens doesn't do anything anymore, but you'll get gold for completing quests or through these very search tokens). Additionally you can finally play the game by yourself and the experience is actually enhanced through the app!
  • Setup is determined by the app: At the start of a quest the entirety of the map is NOT revealed. Players have to open doors and explore the dungeon to finish the quests by themselves. Monsters dynamically spawning makes for a great new mechanic that brings a big surprise element to the game.
  • Heroes and monsters activations alternate: This is also a great new rule introduced based on the popular Star Wars: Imperial Assault D2E "clone". It improves the game in a way so you don't have to wait a long time for the "Overlord" (in this case the app) to resolve his turn and the same goes for the heroes turn. All in all it feels a bit more dynamic as well.

There are many other great additions to the base game that improve the overall player experience, such as the examine feature (you can now examine objects - mostly different tokens on the map), a new traveling and campaign experience or side quests. In my opinion the addition of this app simply makes D2E feel more "alive" and it honestly just feels more enjoyable to play this way (at least for now).

Again, as this is free any new or veteran player of this "classic" board game should simply download it and check it out. The tutorial is a great way to get a feel for this new variant, while the PDF ruleset has all the nitty gritty details you might be interested in too.

The only downside is that this app was obviously optimized for touch devices. As I don't own a tablet and my TV is too far away from my table I've used a laptop so far. It works great, but misses a couple of things like mouse over texts for buttons and the likes. This is just a very small con though, so don't take this as a reason to not play the app.

TL;DR: 10/10 - it's amazing.

I have spent over $700 on Descent and my collection is short 5 out of print lieutenants. Ive been collecting long before the Road to Legend App became a thing and I do not regret a single penny. Ive also put in countless hours into the android app.

Descent is an asymmetrical dungeon crawl board game, much like a far more in depth, detailed and dynamic version of Hero Quest. One play plays as the Overlord and controls the games monsters, villains and traps, the other (up to) 4 players take on the roles of the Heroes.

The Road to Legend App replaces the Overlord player with a computer opponent so that all the players can work cooperatively. This means that you wind up in the situation where you have a physical game with a digital opponent and narrator which is an utterly unique experience.

Descent itself is a truly beautiful board game. The miniatures are pretty, the interlocking map tiles are pretty. Road to Legend follows a story with a laid out set of maps forming a campaign and the campaigns are offered for free. If you own physical copies of the expansions, Road to Legend will incorporate those new heroes, monsters, classes etc into the road to legend campaigns, keeping things fresh.

Descent is easily worth the $60 purchase price on Amazon. However do keep in mind that although the entry point may be low, if you start collecting things will get expensive quick. The base game and expansions are all great value for money. The Hero and Monster packs less so, but they do add a ton of variety. The Leuitenent packs (which act as playable characters for the overlord) are the worst value for money, but once you have all the rest you really dont want the games big bad guys being represented by a token. Its so more satisfying to slay the mighty dragon when its a gigantic figure on the board!

Road to Legend is a wonderful blend on Video game and physical board game. I cant recommend it highly enough!
