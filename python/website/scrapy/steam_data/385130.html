About This Game




DvDrum is Quality, Customizable Drum Simulator. It Allows to Play in Jam Session Mode a Virtual Drum with Keyboard, MIDI Controller, Joystick, Guitar Hero® and Rock Band® Drumkits and USB Pedals, in Realistic Mode for a Real Drummer Experience!
With External Scripts is Possible to Transform DvDrum into a Drum Machine!
DvDrum isn't a VST or Plugin, This is a Standalone Complete Game for Drumming.




Change the composition of your drumkit, remove/add components. Change component graphic and sound, change the carpet, the floor and add some decorations.
Hundreds of images and sounds, for endless possibilities for customization!




You can create endless combinations of sound to your drumkit. Choose a sound from the hundreds of sounds and change the pan and pitch, to create a completely new! Import a saved SoundSet file to change immediately the sound!




Exports the drumkit graphic settings, or audio settings or only keys settings. You can export and import only the settings those interest you, and make many combinations of settings customized. Coming soon support for Steam WorkShop




Record your performance, show your drummer skills with DvDrum!
Compared with other thousands of DvDrummer, learns and improve you from the users feedback. Share your video on Youtube or Play Live on Twitch!

Main DvDrum Features:

  • High Quality Sounds!

    Hundreds of High Quality Wav Sounds, Sampled from Real Drumkits.

  • Highly Customizable!

    Create your Drumkit! Change the composition of your drumkit, remove/add components.
    Change component graphic and sound, change the carpet, the floor and add some decorations.
    Hundreds of images and sounds, for endless possibilities for customization!

  • Mixer!

    You can create endless combinations of sound to your drumkit.
    Choose a sound from the hundreds of sounds and change the pan and pitch, to create a completely new!

  • Support for External Devices!

    DvDrum supports various types of External Devices such as MIDI Controller, Joystick, RockBand® and GuitarHero® Drumkit!
    Can you imagine playing the bass kicks with racing pedals?

  • Support for External Scripts!

    With "AutoHotkey" software is Possible to Transform DvDrum into a Drum Machine!

  • Export/Import!

    Exports the drumkit graphic settings, or audio settings or only keys settings.
    You can export and import only the settings those interest you, and make many combinations of settings customized.

  • Metronome!

    Train to keep time with the internal metronome!

  • High Reactivity!

    Optimized to minimize latency between input-audio for best experience.

  • Simple Interface

    The user friendly and intuitive interface makes each operation easier.

New features and improvements will be added with future updates

Early Access Review
Posted: 5 September, 2015
DvDrum is a drum simulator where you played it with keyboard (I haven't try it using other devices).
The improvement in this Steam version is really good from previous non-Steam version, especially at saving and loading your own keymap, soundset, and drum kit. (If you are non-Steam DvDrum user, you know that feel)

Personally, I think the main focus on this "game" is customization.
You can customize your desired keymap for your controller, customize your drum kit that will be displayed, and also customize the sound set of the drum that included in-game sound mixer (screenshot provided in Store Page).

This game is good for practicing drum, so some feature are:
-In-game metronome, you can set your metronome so you can practice at your tempo while you drumming.
-Embed music, while you playing drum, you can add a music in game and played it on background. Perhaps you want to practice covering some song? (Beware, if you playing with keyboard, some song is too hard to cover)

Other than that, the in-game drum sound is so great.
Also, MAY causing lag in the "game" window when you played and/or recorded it with video recorder software. Be sure to close unnecessary background process.

And here's my personal feedback/suggest:
  • More simplified the UI (Also, the fonts too, if possible. If not, then it's okay)
  • The main menu song is too loud!
  • Taking screenshot with Steam doesn't work, even there's Steam Overlay. (There's a taking image, in-game feature, but that's limited to taking drum kit screenshot only, not the whole screen)
  • You can record your "game"play (video/audio), but you must set it first in main menu Settings. Also, you must have your own video/audio recording software, seems that this feature is useless[?].
  • When you open this "game", then play it, the keymap doesn't load properly. Sometimes, the keymap back to default. The only thing to solve this, is to open the Keys >> Settings >> Return. Then it should set to your own saved keymap.
  • Maybe the option to change resolution?
  • Add shortcut/fix about placing/moving drum/decor part, I don't know, maybe by dragging middle-click?.
    Why? For example, when you "accidently" move your Toms beside the Toms panel like this: http://s6.postimg.org/s3qfh09dt/Screenshot_1.jpg , I can't move it again, probably because the button is conflicted?

Do not BUY.

Early Access Review
Posted: 14 October, 2015
Utterly useless.
To play along to "drumless songs" the program opens your web browser and navigates to their site where you can play audio files by clicking on them. It's completely not integrated into the actual program. What's more, the "drumless" audio tracks are not drumless at all but just clumsily filtered tracks to reduce the volume of the drums a bit. Also, given the fame of the bands they are using I can't imagine they actually have permission to use these tracks: Blur, Dylan, Sabbath, Beastie Boys etc. There's just no way they are doing this legally.

Similarly, the recording features are not in the program itself: instead you can set the program to launch an external exe to record video or sound.

Then we have the quality of the program itself, which is also, surprise, suprise, bad. You can select different samples for each drum but once you have selected one, that's it: you will get the exact same sample played at the exact same volume every time you trigger it.

You can play the drums with your computer keyboard or by clicking on them with your mouse. The mouse clicking is massively inaccurate: there are tons of places you can click on the drum and nothing happens. As far as I can tell you can't use a midi keyboard or other device like launchpad, maschine etc.

The mixer has terribly unintuitive controls: instead of clicking and dragging to set the on-screen pots you have to left click on the pot to select it and then use the scroll wheel to set it, then click left again to exit!

This program is utter garbage. It does next to nothing and what it does do, it doesn't do right.
