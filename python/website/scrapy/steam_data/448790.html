About This Game

Infinity Wings - Scout & Grunt



Infinity Wings - Scout & Grunt Take control of your chosen fighter (either Scout or Grunt) in this side scrolling shooter.

Badges :

Battle your way through hazardous procedurally generated galaxies to achieve the highest score and rank as the best fighter pilot.

Infinity Wings uses both 2D and 3D graphics to bring the excitement of these space battles to life. With vibrant weapon effects and challenging boss battles, this game will test your skill and amaze you with its visuals.

FEATURES :


*Play as Different Spaceships (Scout or Grunt)

*Compete on World Wide Leader boards

*Exclusive Weapons/Firepower for Each Ship

*Challenging Enemy Battleships and Boss Battles

*Progressive Difficulty - Each Wave of Hazards Increasingly Test Your Skills

*Each Level of the Game is Procedurally Generated, Every Time You Play the Game is Different

This Game Brings Back Old School Memories and Nostalgia of Classic 2D Shooters - But with a Modern Twist

Choose your fighter and relive the excitement, of classic shoot'em ups with Infinity Wings!



System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: WINDOWS XP / WINDOWS VISTA / WINDOWS 7 / WINDOWS 8 / WINDOWS 10
    • Processor: Any 64 or 32 bit processor
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Storage: 150 MB available space

Posted: 13 March
Infinity Wings - Scout & Grunt. I thought to scount and grunt would be my mission, but it's actually the names of the current playable ships, so that made me laugh. The horizontal shoot 'em up gameplay is quite standard and the graphics are pretty plain but somehow they're also pretty pretty, and I found myself clearing asteroids not just to avoid getting hit, but to see the pretty spacescapes. The in-game How to Play was kind of helpful, but it didn't mention that "Keypad #" referred to the controls on a gamepad. I had to look at the pre-launch setup option window you get with most Unity games to see what the control scheme was. After I knew what my keys are, I was enjoying a moderately difficult shoot 'em up with my large and slow moving Grunt ship. The weapon variety is nice and I like how you can use ALL FOUR WEAPONS AT ONCE instead of having to choose between one based on powerups you pick up. The bosses were pretty tough and the shots were fast. But I wonder if when the bosses keep firing missles during their death animations... was that intentional? 10/10, would buy for the kids again.

Posted: 11 March
I was never one of those guys in the arcades back in the day ,i was too rubbish at this type of game.
Though i did try :)
The game is made on the Unity engine so graphics have a nice look to them ,simple but ok for this game.
A warning ,sort out your key-config in the box that pops up when you first boot up the game.
I was stuck the first time i played wondering why the ship wasn't firing and banging various keys on my keyboard.
Gameplay starts of easy enough shooting asteroids that are rolling towards you ,it does seem to go on a bit long with the same "rolling rocks" .
Just when i was getting slightly bored with it i noticed a timer in the top right corner counting down and sure enough the rocks stopped rolling and a huge "mothership" appeared from the side of the screen .
This where my previous arcade experiences came back to haunt me :)
I was dead in about 5 seconds .
Now ,someone more skilled at this type of game would probably relish the challenge .
You will get hit with waves of incoming "laser balls" and trying to overcome them is no easy task.
You have 2 weapons to start with(shooter+screen blitzer) and gain new weapons the more you progress so if you're up for it you might do better than i did.

It's cheap to buy and plays well ,if your a fan of this genre then it's def. worth a look.
