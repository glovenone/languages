Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“That’s an interesting question. When we initially decided that time was right to move from the mobile platform to the desktop platform we already knew that this would have allowed us to push the game and the engine toward new horizons and features. So our work has been focused on enhancing the whole engine to support more powerful features.

Now, usually Early Access means releasing an unfinished game but, while this is true for Junk Jack, it’s at the same time totally different. Junk Jack is an ever evolving experience which started as a simple (and only at the time) sandbox game for mobile platforms and evolved in almost 5 years to a whole universe full of features. This journey won’t end, so in this sense Junk Jack won’t never be finished.

So getting back to the question, why Early Access? After more than an year of work we reached a phase in which the game is ready to be played on desktop platforms bringing the plethora of already available features enhanced to be played on desktop. But at the same time we still feel that some features are lacking to consider it a final release, in addition we feel compelled to hear some feedback from players to be oriented into the right direction.

So what you’ll have today is an already quite polished game, but still only the beginning!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Our Early Access phase won’t last much time we hope. In our schedule we hope to be able to tweak and add what we planned in some months of work, really depending upon how things go well. It’s hard to give a precise estimate because sometimes it happens that you get carried through new features and add them so more details we’ll surely be given after we’ll have received some feedback.

In any case, also after being fully released, the game will keep getting updates. So from a different point of view exiting from the Early Access phase will be just one of (we hope) many milestones.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The main features that will be enhanced during the Early Access phase are, for sure:
  • Working on multiplayer feature which is totally experimental with the Early Access launch
  • A complete overhaul of the stats system
  • A complete overhaul of the combat balance and progression through planets
  • A better Steam multiplayer integration
  • Miscellaneous Steam integrations (Trading Cards, Achievements and such)
  • Linux Release

In addition there are additional aspects which could be investigated like
  • Generated structures (dungeons, buildings)
  • A way to create / share adventure worlds (maybe through Steam Workshop)
  • Enhanced weapon effects and mechanics
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“As stated previously, the state of the Early Access is the state of a feature complete, polished game. We could have some bugs here and there, there could be some small issues but in general the gameplay experience is already complete and ready to be enjoyed.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We still haven’t discussed this in details but this is very unlikely.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“That’s an easy question to answer. As people that played our mobile game know, we’ve always read reviews, our forum, our email and all the media channels to listen for suggestions or improvements. In some years we’ve implemented tenths of features or enhancements suggested by people. That’s exactly what we’ll do, as long as people will enjoy playing our game.”

About This Game

Make the world truly yours in Junk Jack, a relaxing sandbox experience focused on building and improving your home. Tame and breed creatures, cook foods, collect animal companions, farm exotic plants, collect fish, grow flowers, play with your friends!



Travel in procedural planets full of content to discover, monsters to combat, crafts to learn and loot to equip or simply proudly display in your home.

All of this is possible in Junk Jack, and much more will be possible in the future. We have much more fun stuff to do planned forthcoming!


     
  • Use portals to travel between 11 planets with unique hand crafted environments
  • All worlds are procedurally generated and different every time you create a new one
  • Play online with your friends
  • Fully featured equip system with hundreds of different wearables with unique stats
  • Beautiful retro styled pixel art graphics
  • Deep Electronics system to create intricate contraptions, even complete CPUs!
  • Creative mode to unleash your creativity and build everything you can imagine
  • More than one thousand different crafts
  • Two styles of crafting: a crafting grid or simple mode which allows crafting items with a single click
  • Lots of loot to collect!
  • Hundreds of placeable objects, blocks and decorations
  • Fully dynamic day-night system, with weather effects
  • A vibrant coloured lighting engine that will make you enjoy every minute spent in Junk Jack worlds
  • Fish and display your captured animals in fish tanks!
  • Place your rare treasures on shelves, display your equip pieces on mannequins!
  • Collect tameable pets which will follow you around
  • Capture and breed cows, sheeps, pigs and many more animals!
  • Farm and grow unique plants and use them to cook delicious recipes
  • A thorough ingame tutorial will help you learn the basics of the game
  • Beautiful music tracks by Bright Primate
  • Craft hundreds of potions with che chemistry bench
  • Mix flower seeds to grow thousands of unique flower combinations
  • Decorate your home with many craftable and customizable furniture sets
  • Combat hordes of mobs with many different weapon types
  • Explosives, signs, paintable objects and many more features awaits you!

So, my opinion on this game is biased, but I'll raise a lot of points in my review. So first things first, the history of this game started off as an iOS mobile game, known as Junk Jack. 2 years later, in 2013, the released Junk Jack X which brought a lot more uniqueness to the game. The ability to go to many other planets and multiplayer brought the game to higher standards. Now, its been ported to the PC for Steam. I own all three, and have loved playing the game for 5 years now. A lot of people compare this game to Terraria or Minecraft, and to a degree, they are right. However, you also have to take into consideration that both of those games aren't original either. This game, although a clone, is very well polished and playable for what it is. So if you are a fan of Terraria or Minecraft, you'd very much enjoy this game too, even if it does look "just like another clone."

So let's go over pros and cons:

Pros:Cons:
Great and friendly communitybut the community is rather small.
Lots of re-playabilitySome of the controls feel wonky/things that ported over weren't implemented well.
Multiplayer is pretty fun with friends and items can be traded/bartered. Not exactly as economy but a market nonethelessHowever, the market can be easily abused throught cheaters. And if random multiplayer is ever implemented again, some people abuse the system. (rare, but happens) And also multiplayer isn't 100% functioning.
Forum to share maps/ideas/etc.Not always active, and mapping/modding is very limited.
Developers are very friendly and active. They actually take time to speak with people on the forums, take a look at bugs that have been posted. Etc. Overall they are great people (2 developers Jack and XsX) and I fully trust that they will invest lots of time into this game.While they are great people, there is only two of them. So don't expect things like updates every day such as Minecraft.
The graphics, although dated, look very nice, even on a 1920x1080 monitor. The game and its graphics hold up, and can easily be run on older systems at fullest details.

Overall, the game is very fun to play, however the main issue is the price. $15 is very pricy for what the game is, and it is still early access at that. I did buy the game at $15 because I have full faith in the developers, however, I'd suggest lowering the price until it becomes a full game.
I do, however recommend this game to fans of either Terraria, Minecraft, or previous Junk Jack players. This game (as you can tell by my hours) has given me a great deal of fun and I'm still playing. The game, although maybe not rushed updates, won't end up abandoned like other early access games on Steam are. The developers are very trusted to me, and you'll soon agree after buying the game.

Early Access Review
Posted: 5 May
INTRODUCTION:

At first glance, you may think that Junk Jack looks like a clone of a much more popular game, Terraria. While the similarities are there, I'd argue that this game's intentions are much different, and the art style is the similar aspect. If you're familiar with Terraria, Minecraft and Starbound, Jack Jack would be its child, introducing all the neat elements of each of those indie games.

PROS:
- More content than expected on Early Access.
- Fun graphic/art style.
- Music is well done (I have a con for this though.)
- Multiplayer (however, it's a con currently as well.)
- Wide variety of biomes and monsters.
- Many things to collect in the game.
- Simple AND advanced crafting for inexperienced or experienced sandbox-game players.
- Weather system.
- Good variety of settings for all areas of the game.
- When your character falls in water, you're not limited to try to jump out of water. You can control swimming around with WASD.
- Combat strafing with the CTRL key.

CONS:
- Combat is too simple, thus making fighting monsters challenging. Limited to a single strike, it's difficult to push back enemies and not be hit with a massive amount of damage.
- Music is great when it's there, but often the game's music will stop playing for some time.
- Multiplayer is currently experimental, and comes with bugs. Attempted doing a Steam game with a friend, didn't work. We eventually connected to eachother by IP through a router, but experienced some random disconnects every now and then.
- Sound effects sound out of place, the quality of the effects don't blend well with the atmosphere of the game.
- The price tag. Personally, for the current stage, I think $15.00 is asking for a little much. I'd price this game at $10.00.

CONCLUSION:

Steam has been plagued with many Early Access games, and many of them have turned out to be flunks. From what I can tell though, the Junk Jack developers are committed and have come to Steam with a fairly polished game. Updates seem to be frequent as well, and they listen to those who are interested in the game. Junk Jack, in my mind, is a good purchase if you enjoy indie sandbox adventure games.

RATING:

I'd give Junk Jack a score of 9/10 for now. In my mind, $1 = 1 hour of entertainment. I can see myself spending more than fifteen hours with this game. Happy building!
