About This Game

If you liked Commander Keen or Duke Nukem, this game is just what you're looking for. Lots of great situations to overcome and traps to work around. This game has great puzzles which make it much more than a simple run-jump-and-shoot contest. Superb colorful graphics and scrolling levels. James Bond never had it so tough!

Features


Sneak and Scroll: Full-screen scrolling as you sneak across huge maps.

Hide and Hunt: Enter various hideouts, each with traps to dodge, puzzles to collect and bad guys to blast.

Save Your Game: Save and load your game progress.

Controller Support: Partial Xbox 360 Controller support.

An old little gem from Apogee, this is a fun and challenging 2D platformer. When I was kid, I had the shareware version (back when shareware was still a thing), which included only the first chapter, and I had a lot of fun with it. Having the chance to finally complete it was great.

As far as story goes, like most games from its era, it doesn't have a lot. You play as a secret agent (hence the name) tasked to infiltrate a series of island controlled by an evil organization in order to recover the blueprints to a superweapon.

When it comes to gameplay, each chapter (there's three of them) consists of a large island filled with enemy bases you need to take down before gaining access to the island's fortress. Each level (or base) requires you to find an explosive to blow open the exit, although you might also have to find keys to unlock doors, or floppy disks to deactivate laser fields that may be in your way. There's also several enemies and different hazards on each level. As I mentioned above, it can be quite challenging, as some of the levels include sections that are basically trial and error, and you may end trapping yourself with no other option but to restart the level, which can be a bit frustrating. Even then, losing your three lives only forces you to restart the level, and all of them can be completed in a few minutes. Another good thing is that you can play the levels in any order you like, so if you find one too challenging, you can always go back to the island map and try another one, in case you're missing ammo, for example.

In conclusion, I recommend this game. Whether you lived through the 90s (or earlier), and have some nostalgia for the games of your childhood, or if you never experienced that era, and want to see some of the early days of gaming, you should give this game a shot.

I first got this game back in the late 90s on a 3.5inch floppy disk. If you were not born in the 90s, or know what a 3.5inch floppy disk is. Don't rate this review down. I've seen a few 12 year old not get old games. Don't be one of those. I clocked this game back then, and I'm on my way to doing so again in 2015.

This game was and still is pretty sweet for a 2d platformer. You have multiple gameplay elements to work with. Its not just fight to get the key and open the door. Sometimes you have to choose which key/door combination to unlock. You may screw up requiring a reset of the level! Add in the floppy disk/laser grid. Dynamite to open the exit, satelite disk to find and destroy, and sometimes the invisible platforms to change the level layouts! This gives a feeling of progress more than having to walk through a level again.

Oh and then there are the varied enemies you will fight. The game only allowing you to fire one shot at a time slows things down some, but means this isn't about button bashing. Its more of a chess game about when to put yourself into harms way and attack. Keep in mind you have a bonus if you survive the level with no damage. There are quite a few enemies that have various strengths and weaknesses. You can't just shoot and win. Some you have to attack only at certain times, and in certain places. Others will blow up and send shrapnel out towards you. The blue ninja guy is one of my favourites.

Don't also forget the challenge of spelling S.P.Y. It seems so simple but sometimes avoiding the letters you aren't ready to get can be fun/frustrating.

The graphics are nice and colourful, the sound is old school.

The only really bad thing is when you finish the levels and realise you are getting closer to the end.

10/10 would spell S.P.Y. again
