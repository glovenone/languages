About This Game

Presentation


Last Heroes is an RPG developed by Warfare Studios, the team already behind Vagrant Hearts, Ashes of Immortality, Valiant: Resurrection and Midnight's Blessing.

Plot


Amelia and Roland are headed to Memato continent, where they think Baroda Prison is located, the place where her mother is being held captive. Will they manage to free her? You must help them in this very dangerous and amusing journey!

System Requirements

I do not recommend this game at all. I have over 25 hours spent on rpg maker so while I only know the basics of everything and some advanced stuff, I can tell you that this game probably only took one week to make. There are countless problems with this game that I have noticed and I have only played thirty minutes!
Sound- I might be wrong but the music is probably stock. If it really is not stock is sounds way too much like the stock music therefore it needs improving.
Plot- The people who have reviewed this game say: Oh it's a very interesting plot! That's complete bull. It is no different than the thousands of other rpg maker games. It is not unique at all.
Art- It's just stock tiles, which I don't really have a huge problem with but it's still annoying to see.
There are many mistakes but I will just list a few:
-Music can be loud and just not fitting with certain scenes
-Windows in the back of buildings that are up against a wall (With people even looking out of the windows into the walls)
-My party was at near full health but both got paralyzed and instead of letting me escape of fix it. it just skipped my turn until both were dead.
TL;DR=You might find some enjoyment in the game if you have never had rpg maker, but it still has many problems with it. And you can forget any type of enjoyment if you do have rpg maker and know atleast the basics of it.

So now, in the 3rd game (of 4) of the Last Heroes series, we find Amelia (along with her friend Roland from the previous installments) continuimg the search for her mother who is being held in a mysterious prison called Baroda on the Memato continent. The problem is that no one seems to know the exact location of the prison so various clues must be gathered from various characters which leads to a long and interesting search that lends itself to more exploration, more treasure collecting, and more battling monsters.
It's all presented in the classic rpg style, complete with nostalgic game play, excellent art work, labyrinthine mazes to navigate, great music that adds to the atmosphere and charm, characters that are easy to like, and, most importantly, a solid story that will keep you interested and wanting to do "just one more thing" ---- very addictive.

As you progress through the game, you will pick up new weapons with the money you earn, and acqire new battle skills with a pleasing flow that suits the game ---- not a lot of grinding required. But you'll need to battle with a bit of thought ---- just adds to the fun.

If you choose, you can play this one without having played the first two ---- but I would recommend playing them in order just to get the complete story. And, at the reasonable prices, why not? But it can stand alone.
Either way, it's a great classic style game that fans will enjoy.
