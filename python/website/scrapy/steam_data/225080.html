About This Game

Guide two brothers on an epic fairy tale journey from visionary Swedish film director, Josef Fares and top-tier developer Starbreeze Studios.

Control both brothers at once as you experience co-op play in single player mode, like never before.

Solve puzzles, explore the varied locations and fight boss battles, controlling one brother with each thumbstick.

A man, clinging to life. His two sons, desperate to cure their ailing father, are left with but one option. They must set out upon a journey to find and bring back the "Water of Life" as they come to rely on one another to survive. One must be strong where the other is weak, brave where the other is fearful, they must be... Brothers.

This is one journey you will never forget.

Brothers - A Tale of Two Sons is often named as one of the best example of games that use the tools proper of the video game medium in a way that no other does.

The main "gimmick" of the game is the fact that you control the titular two brothers at once, using one analog control stick and one "action" button for each.
This mechanic, alien at first, quickly becomes second nature, letting you fully enjoy your travels through beautiful scenery following the brothers on a quest to find a cure for their dying father.

Talking about storytelling, that's another way Brothers sets itself apart from most other games, as the simple, fable-like story is told using gameplay and rare in-engine cutscenes.

You find out the personality and quirks of each brother by the way they interact with each other and with the people and creatures they encounter during the journey. You understand the power of their brotherly bond by the way they help each other out solving puzzles or getting out of difficult situations. All of this without ever having to read or hear a single word.

I would 100% recommend Brothers - A Tale of Two Sons if it wasn't already clear enough, as the way the game marries its storytelling to its mechanics is truly fascinating and definitely worth the 2 to 4 hours needed to complete it.

Brothers - A Tale of Two Sons

TL;DR One of those gorgeous games that feels like you're "playing a movie". Gorgeous visuals, music, sound and animations all combine with an engaging storyline to deliver an experience that won't be forgotten any time soon!

Things you should know before purchasing
  • Local co-op and single player only.

  • Game is actually completely playable with a keyboard. I controlled both brothers with my keyboard and didn't have any issues at all. Using a controller will make the game's controls feel much more intuitive however.

  • Single player is A LOT harder than co-op. Be prepared to get your left and right hands very confused :p Still very playable though, if you have no friends or your brother is a jerk (like mine) :'(

  • The puzzles are pretty basic. This game is very much storyline driven, so for the avid puzzle-solving gamers out there, don't get your hopes up on that front.

  • The game's settings are as minimal as it gets. You get an option to change the game's brightness (gamma), the game's music volume and sound volume. That's all.

  • There's no option to spoon your little brother (or curl up into your big brother's arms) beside the campfire at night... :')

  • Very little replayability value, but given how good the storyline is, the price of the game and most importantly the type of game that Brothers is; this can be easily forgiven.
What you can expect from the game

Completing the main storyline
~4 hours but I took a bit longer.

Completeing all Achievements
an extra ~4-6 hours if you start a new file and explore every nook and cranny.

Trying to force the two brothers to cuddle up to each other in front of a gypsy giant
~7 minutes.

Overall

This game is an absolute delight to play. I would however not recommend buying this game for the full price unless you're the type of person who cries non stop at the end of an emotional anime/TV series. This tale of two sons brothers will definitely stick with you for a long time, however it is hard to justify paying $15 US for a game that offers about 6 hours of gameplay.

Get this game on sale and you'll have a wonderful new gem added to your library.

Peace!
