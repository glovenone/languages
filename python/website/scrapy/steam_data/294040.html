About This Game


A legendary Dragon is haunting the lands.
Towns are set ablaze, and its magic power resurrects the evil creatures of the world.
A hero is needed to travel to the corners of
the lands and slay the beast to restore peace.

RPG grind-fest! Run, Battle ever harder enemies, Collect XP and Loot, Buy better equipment, Level Up, Do it all over again!

Features


  • A huge cast of different enemies and bosses
  • Tons of different equipment upgrades
  • Includes Idle elements where your miners collect gold while you are away from the game
  • Lovingly crafted Retro graphics
  • Achievements to unlock
  • Bestiary to fill out
  • Cards to collect
  • Controller support
  • Possibly infinite gameplay
  • Ridiculous amounts of Loot and XP!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP or higher
    • Storage: 200 MB available space

Poet

Posted: 30 June
Like other "incremental games", this seeks to allow you to pour hours of your time grinding away levels to upgrade your character in order to defeat endless waves of enemies and bosses with no end in sight. How it actually falls short of this goal in such a simple genre is incredible, really.

As some reviews here have said, the game is simply too easy. You hit the end goal far too quickly, and your goal then becomes a mission to collect all of the achievements. I was able to collect all of the achievements the game had to offer in a few hours time... making continuous play pointless, but that's not entirely why.

I don't think the game developers actually expect people to play this game for longer than an hour. You VERY QUICKLY hit a point in the game where your levels no longer go up visually, as though they didn't think a counter that works was necessary. Like saying "Nobody is going to max this skill, so who cares?", but you should care... because you can max the skill in under 30 minutes.

The levels don't have enough variety either. I mean you can't expect much from these incremental games as it is, but when you start reading "you've defeated the dragon 42 times", being the end-boss, it's really depressing.

There also comes a point where you gain so much coin from each level run, that you can honestly beat the final boss, load the next difficulty tier, and then jump straight to the end boss and kill it again... and again... The more you play, the easier it gets, which is typically the opposite of what these games are supposed to be about. You gain exponentially more coin which means you can upgrade exponentially faster. There is a COIN MAX of 99,999 and each upgrade costs 9,999 and will never cost more, no matter how much you've upgraded them. Again, they don't expect that you're going to play this game very long, so why increment higher than 9,999?? Yeah.... When you can gain 9,999 coins from hitting a mere few enemies, it's not hard to upgrade yourself beyond the capacity of the game.

While the game is too easy and runs out of runway too early, there are also certain things that annoy me after the 40th time too. Once I've beaten the dragon boss 40 times, you can stop SLOWLY giving me the message about the increase in difficulty and you can stop SLOWLY showing me that I've unlocked another miner, and you can stop showing me that same loading screen to take me to the levels because I'm just trying to get through it so I can jump to the final boss again and beat him in 5 seconds flat.

The miners... I reminded myself of these useless things. In the early game, once you beat it you unlock a coin miner which can, quite obviously, mine coins for you while you're away. The more you unlock, the more coins you unlock. Remember the coin issues I talked about earlier though... when you're making the max 99,999 coins within a few minutes, what exactly is the point of having several dozen miners also generating a max 99,999 coins for you?? Yay I can do about 20 upgrades to my defence and get through the end boss 5 more times for ABSOLUTELY NO REASON!

And this is my last point: With other incremental games, they're difficult enough that you feel like if you sit there grinding, ultimately you'll reach an end result that is awe inspiring. You can unlock heroes to help your DPS, you can "prestige" your character to reset all levels and grind from scratch, unlocking skills as rewards for hours of play, but with this game there's no point! You hit the end within the first half hour, and you can reach the point of no more level gain within the first hour, and you can collect every single achievement (the hardest being level 100) within a few hours of play leaving there absolutely zero reason to keep going. You're as strong as you ever need to be, and you can safely uninstall the game never to look back.

Of course it's a pretty cheap game on Steam, but this should seriously be a free app.... It's not worth the sale price and CERTAINLY not worth a full pricing.

This game is the perfect example of two concepts.

The first is difficulty vs. tedium. There is no difficulty in this game Anyone can beat it, given time, without the use of any skill whatsoever. I leveled up 20 times with my back to the screen while carrying on a conversation by idly moving the joystick back and forth.

The second concept is the awarding of arbitrary milestones as an incentive to continue "playing" something which contains no actual gameplay whatsoever. I admit, that's what made me spend three hours in this game, even though it was a joyless experience after the first runthrough. I had to grind up to level 100 because that's the game's only real measure of "completion." Upon reaching that stage, I felt relief that I could stop playing without feeling that I'd "given up."

To be fair, this is all disclosed in the game's description, and the price point is fair for what it is. Clearly many people enjoyed it, and there's nothing wrong with that. But I can't stand the thought of someone playing this while in the back of their mind remembering that I recommended it.

Loot Hero DX is yet another entry in the new onslaught of "clicker" games, although this particular title has a controller scheme. There are a total of nine "levels", which you can ascend an infinite (as far as I know) amount of times. You ascend to the next "difficulty" by slaying the dragon on level nine.

I'm currently on Difficulty 198, which means that I have slain the dragon 21 times. I found the first 37 Difficulties semi challenging, and after Difficulty 37 the developer got lazy. You have stats to upgrade; attack, crit, defense and speed. You start off with tiers for each stat (the little triangles above the stat on the upgrade screen), but once you hit the fourth tier of each stat, it no longer tiers but instead shows the stats in numbers on the right side of the upgrade screen only. It also appears that your stats are maxed out after purchasing 9 upgrades after the fourth tier, but really you can and MUST keep purchasing stats beyond this point. There's also a bestiary, but it's pretty redundant seeing as there aren't many enemies or bosses.

The stats aren't the only area that the developer got lazy in. There's actually a coin cap. The developers boast that you get "miners" to farm coins for you when you're not playing the game. This is true, I currently have a crapton of them because you get one each time you slay the dragon. But what good are they? Your gold caps at 99,999, and it's not one of those "soft caps" like in Tap Heroes where it just shows the maximum digits but still gives you the full amount of coins you've earned. Nope, it's 99,999 and if you earn anything over that it just disappears. I was gone to work for roughly 12 hours and when I got home, at the time I had 3 miners, my coins were capped.

Overall, it gets boring quick. If you want a game like this, just get Tap Heroes. It's way better in every way. I wouldn't even really recommend this title on sale unless you're in it for easy achievements, or if you have a huge fetish for clicker games.

Rating: 2.0/5.0 - It's not awful, but it's not great.
