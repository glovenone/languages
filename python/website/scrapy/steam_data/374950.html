About This Game

Something distinctly odd is happening in the Kingdom. In the town square, the plungers are feuding and the situation is quickly going down the drain. A huge, fire-breathing kitten has besieged the castle and threatens to bring catastrophe. And you just can’t brush off the fact that the King is actually an old wooden broom who wants to sweep all these mix-ups under the rug.

In this wacky parody adventure, the ridiculousness is unstoppable. Think “MadLibs” meets a classic “collect-and-combine” adventure game - but with more puns and time-traveling robots.

Features

ADventure Lib is a classic fantasy adventure game, except...
  • Randomness
  • Silliness
  • Awesome Puns
  • Chickens that aren't usually chickens (but probably still taste like chicken).
  • A terrible something that can only be described as “fish.”
  • The Coming Singularity
  • Epic Feuds
  • Impending death by kitten fire (or pineapple fire, or princess fire, or, well you get the idea)!

About ADventure Lib

Have you ever noticed that the puzzles in point & click adventures are bizarre or outright ridiculous? Like “combine the fishing line with the artichoke to get the platypus to open the grate so the rubber ducky floats down the tube...” when all you really wanted to do was open a door or something?

Adventure Lib lovingly pokes fun at this roundabout nonsense with puzzles that are more than ridiculous, they are completely random! All the characters and items you interact with are literally swapped with each other, so you could be Plunger the Brave, whose quest is to open the locked pants of legend and solve outlandish item combination puzzles like putting butter on robots (that would totally short its circuits).

ADventure Lib is not a long game, but it is designed for re-playability - much of the fun is found by playing multiple times and discovering new [often unintentional] jokes, and new object-specific puns and dialogue.

Should I Play ADventure Lib?

If you like randomness, puns, point & click adventures, and parodies, then definitely. This is a silly game, for silly people.

Object Manager

Want to add even more awesome wackiness? There is now an Object Manager that allows you to add your own objects or characters, to be immediately shuffled into the game! You can draw a banana, record yourself saying "banana" and before you know it you could be walking around as Banana the Brave!

Every achievement can be found in one single playthrough and can be gotten within 15 minutes with ease, though there is nothing to make you want to get them besides the fact you actually wasted money on this and feel like you NEED something in return.
The voice acting is poor, the graphics are basic and the story is terrible. There is no re-playability, and no fun to be had the first time you play either.

There MAY have been a good idea somewhere here but the lack of effort from the developers have left this game completely worthless.

ADventure Lib truly is a game that doesn't ever need to be played. I hope that no-one spends their money on this and the developers should feel ashamed charging $2 for this mess of a game. I know I'll be avoiding anything Fancy Fish Games makes in the future, even if it's from a cheap bundle.

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 4 August, 2015
Developed by: Fancy Fish Games
Achievements: Yes
Steam Workshop: Yes

Story:

ADventure Lib is set in a medieval world and you are a hero who embarks on an adventure to save the kingdom; however who/what you and every other thing in the kingdom is/are is totally shuffled when you start playing causing some hilarious moments.

Gameplay:

Start the game with a shuffle button; every character and item is randomized but still keeps their assigned roles. (IE. In my first play through the king is a butter knife and the hero is a potato)
Classic adventure gaming returns with looking at items, collecting items, combining items and using items. At least in this game the ridiculousness of that classic system is highlighted but the completely insane randomization
Because of this most of the game play is just trying to find the right tool for the job at hand, whether it’s fighting the PLUNGER or helping the CHICKEN. Results may vary.
There are secrets to be found, from the charming Easter Egg/Throwbacks in the backgrounds or the collectables that are a little tougher to find, sometimes.
One of the coolest features of ADventure Lib is the ability to create your own assets to put in the game! An easy to use interface lets you provide the image (I used a transparent PNG and it looked great) record the word yourself and it gets added to the game with everything else.
Steam Workshop is also integrated so you can share your creations with everyone. Whatever they may be. We all know what’s coming.
The game is admittedly on the shorter side but that actually works for me; the best part of this game is shuffling the entire game into a new experience and playing with new crazy stuff going on. That said, the ending I received initially was fairly abrupt and I am not 100% sure if there is a “good” ending that you can receive on subsequent plays.
All in all there isn’t much that you wont be able to figure out with a light application of intelligence
and patience. This isn’t Dark Souls; this is a lighthearted adventure and just a good bit of silly fun.

Graphics:

ADventure Lib, for being a simple 2D exploration game, manages to look really clean and well put together.
It looks they way you would expect, being light on the animation side with stuff bobbing around the screen. This helps when you add your own content it fits in perfectly.
Able to change character’s two main color schemes anytime

Sound:

Music and SFX: The sound effects and music are just fine. No problems with them; nothing special in that regard. It all blends into the game play well.
Voice Acting: This game is fully and independently voice acted. It is half of the charm, really! Its nice that they took the time to do this even if it was done by people who are new to the industry. Only a couple lines came off as recorded out of place but it was hard to notice amongst the crazy dialog. Also: If you add an asset to the game you can count yourself as a voice actor as well. 😀

Final Verdict:

ADventure Lib is a light-hearted and loving tribute to the “use X on Y” genre. While it is small in scale I had no technical issues playing it and had a lot of fun working out the puzzles (basic as they are). Adults and kids alike would have a fun time with all the goofy situations that arise in this game and at the price it’s damn near risk-free to give it a try. It is a little short but it’s fun to replay (especially after adding new characters/items to the game!).

Disclaimer: I was provided a copy of this game for review purposes. My opinions are my own.
