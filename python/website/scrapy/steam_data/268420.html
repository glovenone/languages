About This Game

Aura Kingdom is a new Anime MMORPG published by Aeria Games. Developed by the studio behind the highly popular Eden Eternal and Grand Fantasia, Aura Kingdom offers a grand and beautiful world for every player to explore with beautiful anime graphics.

Starting with one out of eleven different classes, Aura Kingdom offers the possibility for players to select a sub-class once they reach level 40, allowing for countless of original builds! Players will also walk along the side of their Eidolons in their journey; they are loyal interactive companions who grant the strength to unleash massive combos.

Thousands of players already walk the world of Azuria. It is time for you to join them!

Key Features:
  • Interactive companions – Eidolons are no ordinary pets. Not only do they participate in battle, but they will also remember your actions and tell you what's on their mind. They are part of Azuria!
  • Fast-paced, dynamic fights – Mobility is a key aspect in battle. Think fast, act faster! Dodge your foes' attacks and call upon your Eidolon to unleash spectacular combos!
  • Adaptability – Customize your character so he can overcome every challenge! Improve the skills that fit your play-style and switfly change them during battle to adapt to your opponent's strategy. Creativity can win you the battle!
  • A wonderful world – Travel the immense lands of Azuria, visit towns brimming with life and explore mysterious and hidden dungeons to stop the evil of this world. You can make the difference, hero!

So let me clear a few things up first. I have been playing AK for about a year now. I have 6 characters on the Chimera server(all of my characters are ranging from lvl50 to lvl 80). Aura Kingdom is not a new game but it has a very active and large community. The story line is good and the fighting mechanics are superb aswell.

CLASSES:
As in any MMO RPG when you first create a new character you will be able to pick between a couple classes. You can chose between Guardian, Duelist, Ravager, Wizard, Sorcerer, Bard, Grenadier, Gunslinger, Katar, Battle Bow, Tachi, Scythe or Holy Sword. Depending on your liking you will chose the class that suits your game style the most (Although I have seen a lot of new players chose Duelist as their first class I have to say that if you are just starting to play the game make a Holy Sword it is a easy to play class and it has a lot of dmg, while Duelist is one of the hardest to play classes(Hardest to play and be GOOD at it).

CHARACTER CUSTOMIZATION:
There is not a lot to talk about when it comes to the basic character customization that you get at the start of the game, however this isnt all you can do to customize your character. In game you get different costumes (body, head, weapon, back, face,..) With these costumes you can completely change your characters apperance to your liking (you can even change color of CUSTOM Costumes).

LEVELING:
The leveling process is as in a lot of MMO RPGs. Leveling from 1-40 is not that hard you can get 1-45 within a day if you get really into it. The next stage in leveling is at 45-60 you will notice that you need enourmos ammounts of xp for a lvl. And this just continues the ammount of xp you need to get to lvl 80 in insane but once u do its worth it. I also think its worth mentioning that the level cap is now 90!!! So there is a lot to do to reach endgame.

PVP:
The pvp aspect of the game is as in any other MMO RPG. You have arenas and Battlefields. While arenas will match you up with players around your level lvl 50-59, lvl 60-69 and lvl70+, Battlefields on the other hand match everyone togeather no matter the level. For PVP you will need pvp gear which isnt that hard to get (although pvp weapons are a bit trickier to get). Now I would not recommend pvp to new players since you will most likely get killed instantly. Since for pvp it is important to have your gear fortified and starred (This gives you bonus def and HP in armor and DMG in weapons).

PVE:
In the case of PVE there is not really one bad thing I have to say about it. You have a large veriety of dungeons (solo and party to chose from). Dungeons are locked to different levels so as you level up you will unlock more dungeons. Also PVE gear can be crafted but I would recommend crafting PVE gear after you reached lvl 60 since green gear for your normal solo dungeons until lvl 60 is fine.

All in all a very good Anime MMO RPG game with a great community and good mechanics.

I played a bit of Aura Kingdom during my WoW downtime. Thought I'd give a brief review of my experience.

Played I would say for the last two weeks very casually. The game has an auto pathing system that take a lot of the running around(exploring) out of the game. I actually liked the system because it let's you pickup and play easily without having to really invest. It also got me to actually pay attention to the story not that is was mind blowing.

The art is the games strongest point. It sort of reminds of the "Tales of" series. The costumes are also suuppperrr on point. Very cute, or very cool mounts, and Edilons(your fighting companion pets). The world isnt too bad and some of creature you fight are cool looking as well.

The class system I didn't get to fully explore. I rolled a Bard which is the games only pure healing class. It can still easily solo from what I saw and I had a few duels with other players winning with superior healing. At level 40 they let you choose from any of sub classes, three of which offer additonal spells with the right off hand weapon equipped. The system allows for some super creative ways to play but ultimately some are clearly better than others as MMORPG's go.

The community however is dead. Like there are people EVERYWHERE, but we can't talk. My entire time I spoke to no other players. You have to purchase an item from the store just to talk in /world chat and it has only so many uses. You may end up paying for an item just to have your message spammed out of sight. I was invited to a guild, no one was ever on. I never found a group for running dungeons either...

The cash shop wasn't too bad. I purchaes 2500 Aeria Points for about 32$ CAN and got a very cool costume for my character, and some large 20 slot bags. They also give you a bunch of items with EXP and Drop rate buffs, and a free mount. Later on I found out that I get even more free stuff which was x3 more 20 slot bags, and two more Edilon pets one of which is worth 50.00$ in the cash shop.

I found this game to be actually quite enjoyable. If I wasn't looking forward more so to WoW Legion I might consider giving it more time. I've never been a fan of "Pay to Win" but I am a fan of seeing how far you can take a free to play character. Even so the cash shop has some fair deals and most of the items in there are cosmetic in nature from what I saw.

Do you like MMORPG's? Do you like Anime? Then you might like Aura Kingdom
