Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to have the game available as soon as possible so we can benefit from your feedback to build the best possible online gameplay and level design experience.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We will be in early access atleast until early 2016 to polish the Level Editor and online gameplay. Ideally we want to keep going in early access for a year or more if there is sufficient interest in the Level Editor.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Our first priority will be polishing up the multiplayer experience.

We also have a lot of ideas to make the Level Editor and Workshop integration better if there is enough demand for it.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Gameplay and content wise, we are very closed to being done with 50+ polished levels.

The Level Editor is shaping up well, though still a bit bugged. Students for Digital ISART montrÃ©al have been testing and using the editor for over 1000 cumulative hours so far. 13 levels from their students have been uploaded to Workshop and integrated into the main game campaign.

Online play works, but can still be a bit buggy sometimes. It's still minimal at this point though.
We're also going to be rolling out updates to add a game browser instead of the current automatic matchmaking only.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We plan to gradually raise the price as we ship new content and features.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We hope to get as many involved as possible using the Level Editor.

Our first priority will be to make it easy for players to discover and access user created content. We will also be assisting top level designers to hook their best creations into our main campaign.

Feedback will also be helpful for bug hunting and shaping up the future multiplayer experience.”

25 April

Improved Graphics and Gallery Mode Patch

Hello Gamers,

We're very excited to share with you the content of our latest patch.

IMPROVED GRAPHICS

Some heroes have been redesigned and/or improved.

The big visual improvement is through the new particle system. Every hero now has his own particle colors and behavior. Those colors also apply to power ups and death making for improved visual personality of heroes.

Things like rocket trails and ice breaking look a lot better now.

We also added a feature that has been widely requested...

GALLERY MODE

In the Gallery, you will be able to see every goddess you've saved so far.

When you complete a level with a Workshop Image in the background, that Workshop Image will also get added to your Gallery.

This allows you to grow your Gallery as big as you wish by collecting every Workshop Image that interests you.

INTRODUCTION

Uncraft World is a retro style puzzle platformer made by Team Shuriken. This game is a piece of art. I'm not talking about the boobs gag, they are appreciated, but, the level design is incredible and the game keeps bringing in new obstacles and traps through the whole game. The game starts out really easy and gets progressively harder by each level you pass, thankfully you can choose to play multiple levels from the beginning. One thing I'm really not happy about is the ability to turn down the music volume (you can mute the music from the pause menu while you are playing level and you can't do this while you are in the main menu), the soundtrack is good, it changes itself throught the game and provides a great atmosphere but sometimes you just need a little bit less of it. You can also encounter some bugs like being unable to start a level or loot not appearing back after you die with it. The game also has buggy built in level creator (that shuts the game when you open it) which I tried experimenting with and can see a lot of potential in it and that Steam Workshop page of this game will be full of unique levels. Graphics are pretty retro and since this is a 2D game set in 3D world you will have problems jumping on the right block if there are other blocks behind it.

Early Access Review
Posted: 2 May
Don't confuse with minecraft or a dating sim. Totally different game.

This is a side platformer obstacle course in super meatboy style play, minecraft looking levels and characters, pulsing upbeat soundtrack, anime wallpapers, and a voiceover on death that yells profanities so you don't have to. These things make no sense to combine together in one game. So they did.

You'll use only three keys, or 2 and the left mouse click, to navigate your way through levels to hit checkpoints, which will gradually "un-craft" the world behind you, revealing scantily clad cartoon ladies.

Gameplay starts easy and gets very challenging. Play time per level ranges from a couple minutes to a half-hour.

Multiplayer is well integrated -- essentially it's just like solo-play but if either of you hits a checkpoint you can both use it. And you get to suffer the gauntlet together which is always nice.

The art is tasteful and well done. A lot of it is actually quite beautifully painted. If anime girls aren't your thing, there's an option to replace with any image you want.

There is a level builder but I haven't tried it yet.

In summary, I was gifted this and had zero expectation that it would be any good. Blown away by how fun and challenging it is. 10/10
