About This Game

Ever wanted to get caught out in the rain with a cute girl?

Well now you can! Except the rain is actually a giant blob of goo and you are the cute girl.

Raining Blobs is a fast-paced arcade puzzle game based around matching falling blocks. Matching blocks of the same color will link them together while matching blocks with stars in them will destroy the linked blocks. This game will put both your fingers and mind to a test of raw skill. Prove that you have what it takes to survive the endless blob rain!

Key Features:
  • Adore the cast of cute female protagonists inspired by anime style art.
  • Crush your competition with up to 8 player local coop or vs mode! Show your friends just how good you are! Up to 16 with CPUs!
  • Secure your position as the undisputed Raining Blobs master by rising to the top of the leaderboards in Arcade and Endless Battle Mode.
  • Advance your way through a selection of fierce battles in Tournament mode and unravel the story.
  • Solve all mind-bending puzzles in Puzzle mode.
  • Collect a wide selection of challenging and rewarding achievements.

Oh, and did we mention the cute girls?

Hello everybody, Shaun Meyers here from Universal Gaming Reviews to bring you a short summary/review for Raining Blobs!

If you'd like to read our full review for the game, you can find it here: https://www.universalgamingreviews.com/raining-blobs-overview-review/

Raining Blobs is a very entertaining and addictive puzzle game that harkens back to the puzzle games of old. It feels like a combination of Tetris and Dr. Mario but it adds it's own setup to give it its own feel.

The game is competitive in nature and features gameplay for up to 16 players (8 Humans and 8 AI players) so it's great for some intense couch co-op gameplay.

There's also a tournament mode where you battle your way through each of the game's 8 characters until you defeat the boss Enya. A new mode called Puzzle Mode was released the day we released our review as well.

The game is an excellent addition to the overcrowded puzzle game genre and if there's one game in the endless sea of puzzle games that you should try out, it's Raining Blobs.

We gave the game 4.5 Stars out of 5 and since we enjoyed the game quite a bit we're giving it our recommendation here on Steam as well.

People, please stop comparing this to Puyo Puyo or Dr. Robotnik's Mean Bean Machine. It's not that. It's more akin to Puzzle Fighter. Heck, it is basically Puzzle Fighter except with a few but important rule changes.

That said, this is a pretty good multiplayer block dropping game, and in an increasingly saturated marketplace there still aren't many of these. It's like puzzle fighter, except with a few rule changes that creates real good push/pull between players: You rack up block clears and combos to create garbage, but the other player will only receive that garbage if it clears four or more blocks. So your opponent can dump 20 garbage blocks on you, but you can recover given that you have a good amount of blocks or a combo to clear.

It is a little bare, the tournament mode is very simple and it's various endings for each character are only in the form of a few sentences at the end. There isn't a whole lot of visual content to look at, the faux-anime girl style only exists in the character select and backgrounds.

But what is in the game is very solid and polished. Stacking blobs results in a satisfying squish, effects stand out enough and in such a way that you can read your opponent's status with just your peripheral vision. The music is charming and the sound effects are perfect for this type of game. The rules are simple enough to learn quickly and rewards tactful thinking and working under pressure. Also, since indie games do less of this as time goes on: It works. Everything works. No weird glitches, no unpolished bullcrap to swallow, everything just works.

8/10

Easy to pick up and hard to put down, Raining Blobs is an exciting arcade puzzler like the old Dr. Robotnik's Mean Bean Machine (or Puyo Puyo), but better. This game offers six difficulty levels and three different speed levels, along with a solo mode, battle mode (supporting up to eight players) and tournament mode.

To me, this is the best game in the genre since Super Puzzle Fighter II (1996), and I like how friendly or brutal it can be. Also, it's worth mentioning that there's a colorblind option, making the game accessible to everyone.

The anime appeal is a double-edged sword. I like it (and I'm sure there's a lot of people who'll discover the game because of it), but some people can mistakenly pass the game because of the girls. Either way, it doesn't really matter, since the player will be much more focused on the playfield.

All in all, I can't recommend Raining Blobs enough if games like Puyo Puyo, Columns, Dr. Mario, and even Tetris, are your cup of tea. For a small asking price, you'll get an addictive and very well made game to play alone and/or with friends - and you can even team up with CPU bots to get the co-op achievements!

10/10
