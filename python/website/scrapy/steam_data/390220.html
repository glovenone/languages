About This Game

Toilet Tycoon is an unusual economic simulation in which you can prove your skills as a leader of the sanitarian installations of a whole town!

How would you like to be in a line of business that’s totally crisis proof? You can see for yourself that money doesn’t stink as a MANAGER of pay toilets!

When you start up as a toilet cleaner, you will need to make sure that your SEED CAPITAL of 500,000 euros is wisely invested. Therefore, you purchase your first allotment and equip it with only what is absolutely necessary: basic model toilets and paper...

But that’s just the beginning; with competitors that never sleep and the escalating tastes of your customers, you will need INNOVATIONS to increase profits. Next stop: Golden bowls, silk toilet tissue and seats with handy cup holders.

Your goal is to monopolize every john in town and rake in the DOUGH on your way to becoming a total “Toilet Tycoon”!

Features

  • Choose from a wide variety of components to create the most outrageous toilets imaginable!
  • Research new technologies that will make your customers want to buckle up before they flush!
  • Have saboteurs trash other people's toilets and send in the authorities to take them down!
  • Spy on the facilities of your rivals and spread vicious rumors to chase their customers away!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: XP,Vista,7,8,10
    • Processor: Pentium 100 Mhz
    • Memory: 16 MB RAM
    • Graphics: 800x600
    • DirectX: Version 7.0
    • Storage: 25 MB available space

Posted: 8 October, 2015
This game is nihilist art. The Germans, not satisfied with their extensive library of ho-hum simulators and scat porn, have combined the two in a toilet management tycoon game that almost invariably becomes a titanic, never-ending struggle for toilet primacy - albeit solely in an unnamed German city. As people will never stop pooping, so will the Toilet Wars rage for eternity.

I mean, that's probably because the game's already simple mechanics are broken six ways from Sunday, but still.


One thing this game has going for it, apart from the 1997-era graphics, Google Translated text, and general shovelware aesthetic, is the number of victory conditions to choose from. I say this because picking the default condition, to achieve the highest rank of Toilet Tycoon, will most likely end up in defeat or stalemate. (The AI difficulty is hidden in the options menu and defaults to 'normal', for what that's worth.)

If a stalemate happens, you're in for a long period of spamming chance-based sabotage attacks at the AI's toilets. The hope is that their toilets will become filthy enough for the sanitation inspector to revoke their permit, allowing you to swoop in and buy the toilet stalls. The AI will try do the same to you. The fact that this sabotage consists of hiring goons for several thousand Euros each to smash or puke on the toilets (mainly puke), complete with sound effects, is just icing on the urinal cake.

Despite the oodles of money you and the AI will have from your turbo-charged solid emerald thrones with attached cup holders - that people incidentally don't mind paying for by the way - sabotage attacks and inspector visits can only be carried out once a turn. And since the sabotage always has a chance of failing, and since the inspector's mood fluctuates daily, it could always be for nothing.


The dominant player (did I mention there's only 2 players to a game?) is continually given free toilet items every turn from a bingo, and while it's only really helpful when players are initially building up, good rolls count for more than skill there. Random events can also turn the tide, although in practice the AI always eventually uses them to screw you in a stalemate. Research is also luck-based, which means even more wasted money.

This game is therefore like World War 1 by way of Dungeons & Dragons, with toilets, where fittingly enough you can only play as Germany. The Great Toilet War module for 5e. And this isn't even mentioning:

* the stupidly opaque and quite possibly broken rating system which the Toilet Tycoon victory condition revolves around,
* the fact that there's really nothing else to do once players are established other than re-enacting the Great War and questioning why this is even on Steam,
* the criminally small un-updated 640x480 resolution buttons that make actually administrating your toilets a chore,
* the fact that there's an entire fourth of the game world with substantially less traffic which means starting there is a death knell,
* the eternal mystery that is the "Pig of the Round",
* or the inane looping music that will drive you up the wall.


You'll enjoy seeing your numbers go up, of course, and bad game aficionados will obviously like this one too. But while most of the other victory conditions let the player win, in true nihilist fashion the game won't actually end, and thus the only real victory is pressing Quit.

A combination of almost-broken controls and unfair game mechanics kills this game's fun.

I thought the game's premise was intriguing, and not at all crappy. I got this game (DRM free) in a Groupees Bundle earlier this year (2015). It's a turn-based game where you compete against the CPU to try to own and equip the most profitable toilets.

It has two major problems. One, your CPU opponent can send the sanitation board after you, and even if you cleaned your toilets 100% in your prior turn, they can cite you for having a terrible mess and divest you of that toilet and all its equipment. Not fun, and doesn't feel fair.

Two, the icons to control the various functions of the game are so, so tiny, it's an exercise in tedium just to click the buttons. And you have to click a lot of buttons to equip a new toilet, so the combination of these two flaws (losing your toilet and all its equipment, and the tedium of equipping a new one) kills this game. Either alone might be tolerable. Together they are infuriating, adding insult to injury.

I guess I played about 90 minutes before giving up on it. It's definitely a turd.

I loaded this up and let it sit (no "sit" or "load" jokes, please) in order to accumulate playtime, just so I could warn you all away from this. I see that the existing negative reviews aren't particularly informative. I hope this one is more helpful.

Finally, I just want to say that it is obnoxious for Valve to require playtime (and not just ownership) of a game before reviewing. It should be obvious by now that many people play games before they get released on Steam, and those people have legitimate opinions, too. It's also obnoxious that they have yet to fix their review-posting bug whose only workaround is to post a one-word review and then edit it.
