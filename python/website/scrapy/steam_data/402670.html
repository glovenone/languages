About This Game

Space Thinger is a thrilling arcade space shooter! Fight your way through multiple combat zones mining asteroids for crystals and collecting the debris of your fallen foes to upgrade your ship and engage in ever more epic space battles. Take on bonus missions for even greater rewards whilst keeping the emphasis firmly on close quarters space dog fighting.

Master a new mission in every zone
Every zone has it's own unique mission, be it collecting artifacts, defending a space station or destroying transmitter probes! Each will reward you with resources to upgrade your ship although failure will not stagger your progress.

First person and Third person perspectives
Switch between first person and third person perspectives at the press of a button. Choose whichever mode suits your play style.

Thrilling arcade combat loop
Fight a fantastic array of simultaneous combatants whilst making sure to collect enough upgrade materials to keep you alive and ensure success in your next epic battle.

Turn on random enemies for endless game variation
Select the Random Enemies option and on top of it's standard set of foes each level will spawn an additional horde of ever more dangerous threats to test your skill and provide endless mission variety.

Varied enemies with a plethora of different armaments
With lasers, missiles, lightning, fireballs and the ever classic devastating beam attacks your foes will pound you with everything they've got. But keep an eye on the resource stealing factory ships plundering your resources to make more foes and hazards for you to fight.

Reach the safety of hyperspace to upgrade your ship, weapons and countermeasures
Over thirty upgrades to keep your ship ready for even the toughest combat scenarios.

Fast and furious fun, Space Thinger takes you straight to the heart of adrenaline pumping space combat action!

Space Thinger (!?) is a first-person space sim where you need to shoot at bad guys until you fulfilled the goal of the level go pop up the warp gate. It can be thought of as something similar to "Elite" (the old one) with asteroids, bad guys, and so on. The game has no constant throttle though... Hold down the throttle if you want to move. Release to coast. This makes for some interesting maneuvers as it's physics based. You get bombs and seeking missiles, but very few of them. Most kills will be with your guns. There are secondary objectives like "shoot asteroids", which gives you extra "crystals" which you will need for upgrades along with "parts" (dropped by kills).

There really isn't much more to the game. It's more of a proof of concept.

Space Thinger is an exhilirating space dogfighter where you stay alive by destroying enemy ships and gathering raw material to mend your ship on the fly. It's fast paced, is slick to control and offers a focused arcade challenge. But it can make life a little difficult for the first time player.

While trying to stay alive, you attempt to complete the level's mission objective, which generally involves following the purple dots on your scanner and either blowing something up, or collecting it. Do this enough times and you'll be rewarded.

You heal your ship by collecting debris from destroyed ships and ore from asteroids - you need a bit of both to restore your ship's health, so you have to balance both "mining" (ie blasting) and actual combat. You can't rely too much on healing, though - the more collectibles you use up on healing, the less you have to spend on power-ups when you complete the level.

Gameplay video, showing footage from three different levels: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hPoFW_6Unoc

You'll spend a LOT of time staring at the local scanner rather than the actual ship you're flying - this often contains more useful information than the main screen! It can be hard to pick out the purple dots from the red dots in a busy area, but once you've learnt to "read" the scanner effectively (along with the long-range scanner that places the same colour dots on the main viewer for a very short time), you'll be unstoppable.

The in-game explanation of what you're doing is a little vague, so your first encounter can be confusing and needs a bit of trial and error to discover how to play. For instance, I was wrong-footed at how the mission objectives were actually optional, not the point of the game.

Really, you just need to know that you complete a level by killing most of the ships that appear as red dots on your scanner. Then a portal will open for 30 seconds, which you escape into. If you fail to get there in time, you are penalised (you lose some of the stuff you've collected) and the gate will open again shortly.

It'll probably take a few goes to get used to the (scarce) weapons you have, other than your vanilla infinite-ammo blaster. One's a very powerful missile, the other's a distraction to fend off homing missiles. But yeah, you hardly get any of those and you'll need to get used to using them in only the most demanding circumstances.

Finally, picking up the debris and asteroid ore can be annoying. You'll need to hit them precisely to pick them up, and often (even when there's no threats around) you'll keep sailing infuriatingly close to them without actually getting them. But again, a little practice should get on top of this.

So long as you're not put off by the rough edges and you manage to get a hold on the game, Space Thinger is great fun. A proper arcade dogfighter, with between-level powerups to earn for those who go the distance. It's not complicated - it does something simple very well.

(Note: This review is of the identical non-Steam build that Wytchlight gave me access to, so my hours in-game aren't reflected on Steam)
