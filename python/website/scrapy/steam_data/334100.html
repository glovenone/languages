About This Game

Extreme Exorcism is a paranormal platformer where every move you make comes back to haunt you.

Take on the role of Mae Barrons; an Extreme Exorcist with extreme measures. Her supernatural skills are called upon when everything and everyone else has failed to rid a haunted house of its ghostly presence.

Conventional methods won’t cut it with these ghosts. Instead, Mae comes armed with a deadly arsenal of ghost-busting weapons, from rocket launchers to razor sharp boomerangs.
But these aren’t your average poltergeists. At the end of each round, a ghost appears and mimics your every move from the round before. The longer you survive the more extreme the game becomes.

Engage in non-stop ghost annihilation in 10 eerie areas of the haunted house – each room presenting its own hellish hazard. Brave the winds on the balcony and the fire in the kitchen, surviving for as long as you can.

With a devilish local multiplayer – you can play co-op or deathmatch modes with up to 3 of your friends, and with 50 unique challenges even the most daring Extreme Exorcist will be put to the test.

If Co-op Worked, this Game Would be Worth the Time
This game is built upon a fun concept in which every enemy you slay comes back to haunt you, re-enacting your exact move set, including attacks, from your previous life. The more levels you go through, the more enemies you face. A fun and enjoyable concept this game has a few quirks that really prevent it from being a great game.

Pros
- Fun Concept
Who doesn't want to be a ghost kicking cool person
- Enjoyable sound (both music and SFX)
Although I never whipped this soundtrack out while driving I enjoyed it throughout the time of my playing
- Old School Graphics
The graphics fit well for this type of game. Personally I enjoy the throwback look for simple indy games
- Local Co-op for buddies
The whole reason I bought this game was to play with friend.

Cons
- Reptitive Design
Yes the levels change and you move from location to location but it all feels really similiar after a while.
- Lack of story/reasons to progress
I don't really know why I'm in the house in the first place, why I'm killing ghosts, or what compels me to keep moving forward other than a hate for chairs or things that copy me.
- Lack of controller support
When connecting an Xbox One controller through multiple means the game registers that i'm continually pressing left on the d-pad. It make it impossibe to play using it. I had to switch to my old Logitech controller to get it working.
- Lack of customer support
I contacted customer support twice, (about the above issue) one incident about a week ago and haven't heard back from them at all and still no reply, even to say we are looking into it. Sadly this game is wasted because without the ability to connect multiple controller the co-op awesomness is lost.
- Lack of co-op playability
This controller thing really breaks the game for me. Not only can I not re-map the buttons to different parts of my keyboard (no setting option), I can't even connect multiple controllers. This game would have a lot of potental if the co-op work but as a single player entry it lacks substance to keep me playing.

FULL VIDEO REVIEW:
https://youtu.be/dhnq3vnYXAg
PROS:
++Large amount of content, will keep you busy for quite a while between challenge mode deathmatch, and arcade mode.
+Leaderboards allow you to see the top scores for each level and encourage you to replay levels for a better score
+Ghost copy mechanic is interesting and requires rethinking of how to engage your foes
+Very solid platforming controls allow you to both dodge and aim extremely accurately, which is crucial when there are dozens of guns going off at once

CONS:
--Ghost copy mechanic is really the only unique feature, and doesn’t provide enough depth to keep the game interesting for a long period of time
-No online multiplayer, only local multiplayer.
-No rebinding of keys combined with lack of mouse support makes this game hard to play without a controller.

Extreme Exorcism is a survival platformer that relies on its unique ghost copy mechanic to create a new take on the never ending swarm of the undead. After each round a ghost will spawn and copy your exact movements and actions from the round previous. Over time more and more ghosts fill up the level, meaning you will have to think carefully about where you've been and more importantly where you've been shooting. While the game mostly succeeds at what it tries to accomplish, the mechanic just simply does not provide enough depth to put it ahead or differentiate it from the many other platformers on steam. The restriction of co-op and multiplayer to offline only is also disappointing, especially since the multiplayer is the best part of the game. That being said the game still has extremely solid platforming and I can almost guarantee you will enjoy yourself playing Extreme Exorcism, just don't expect the game to be anything beyond good.
