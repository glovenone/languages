About This Game



At ease, pilot!
Your goal is to destroy all buildings on the mission and defeat all Bosses. One combat operation includes 5 levels and 1 boss mission. The aircraft will lose altitude with each descent, so be cautious! The randomly placed bonuses on different building storeys might boost or hinder you during the bombardment. If you find such a Bonus crate, try to hit it before it’s gone. If a bomb hits the Crate, you get one of the 10 bonuses.

Features:

5 combat flight operations
5x5 levels
5 aircraft types
5 tricky bosses
5 good and 5 bad bonuses
5 languages
Hardcore mode
Unlimited fly
Weather affects the gameplay
Achievements and cheats

And, of course, a secret level with a seagull!

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP and above
    • Processor: Intel/AMD
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Any GeForce or Radeon
    • Storage: 200 MB available space
    • Sound Card: Any
    • Additional Notes: Best played at Full HD resolution (1920×1080)

Product received for free
Airstrike HD is a simple arcade style game that is competent, but ultimately shallow and lacks variety in its gameplay.

The game consists of thirty levels, consisting in five sets of five levels, and five boss stages. There is no plot or storyline other than that the player controls a plane and drop bombs on buildings. All of the levels must be completed in the order presented. In the 25 ordinary stages, the player is shown an animation of the plane taking off and flying high above a group of buildings. The plane flies from one side to the screen to the other, descends a little bit, and then flies in the opposite direction. The level ends in failure if the plane flies low enough to crash into a building.

The player's only action is clicking the left mouse button, which drops a bomb from the plane that falls straight downwards. Only one bomb can be on the screen at a time. A bomb that hits a building destroys one level of it. After dropping ten bombs, the player can't drop bombs for a few seconds as the plane restocks its supply. The level ends in success when all buildings are destroyed.

In addition, after hitting a building a powerup may appear, represented by a question mark. These powerups have a variety of effects, both good and bad. They can move the plane up or down a few levels, damage nearby buildings, repair nearby buildings or even restart the level. Powerups can make a level trivial to complete or make the level nearly impossible.

Each of the 25 levels keeps its own high score. In addition, each level gives the player a star rating. One star is given for simply completing the level, one is given for hitting ten targets in a row, and one is given for hitting the General, a character who can appear after destroying a building. A separate award is also given for destroying all buildings without missing a target once.

The five boss stages play slightly differently. The player has a limited stock of bombs, typically around five, and must hit all targets without missing once. Unlike other levels these boss targets move around a little and are not stationary.

After about six or seven levels it is clear that the gameplay simply doesn't change all that much. While the levels are aesthetically different, they all play almost identically. It becomes tedious and boring after a while, even for a player aiming for high scores or to get three stars in all levels. Fortunately the game is short; completing all levels with the highest rating can take roughly three hours or so. There are a few menu options that can make the game easier, but disable the high score on a level and prevent the player from earning stars.

Airstrike HD is a good example of a game that is best played in very short sessions of ten minutes or less, since it gets repetitive quickly. While it does feel a bit like a free Flash game it is decent enough for anyone who wants to play something simple and casual.

General Impression
Airstrike HD is an alright combination of the casual and indie genres. The graphics are 2D, so they are pretty simple. However, I like that this game is colorful, as well as the backgrounds of the areas make it more enjoyable. The textures are relatively decent, a better polishing would have been a plus. The atmosphere varies between 5 different environments, from the hot tropical climate to the cold polar climate, and further to the Moon. Every environment has its own buildings, background and especially population/vehicles, but sometimes… the weather could really affect the gameplay. Not only that it can be somewhat make the game looking more dynamic, but it can also cover your aircraft. The audio is pretty decent, even though sounds when clicking the menu/pause/resume icons should be added. The soundtracks are pretty nice, they suit every environment. The gameplay is relatively repetitive, but the surprise bonuses and the general being hold in an unknown building make the gameplay pretty decent, as well as the weather influences the ability to see your aircraft. The weather can cover it, so you cannot see where you are bombing. There isn’t any complicated story either, basically there is a guy who keeps coming to certain cities to bomb them, while he takes off from the ground (from hidden places, where usually a gate opens and the aircraft comes out to take off), and in the final, a cut scene appears with him and with his 5 aircrafts being happy, meanwhile a ton of fireworks are exploding on the sky in the background. The user-interface is very simple, without any additional designing. The controls are pretty alright, you can control the game with both the mouse and the keyboard. The engines are alright, I haven’t met any bugs, crashes, screen freezes or frame drops. The mechanics are pretty good everywhere, even though the bombs feel a little unnatural, especially when they fall. The damages of the buildings aren’t that realistic neither. But I like the constant burning of the buildings, they are relatively realistic. Overall, pretty decent mechanics, but some polish would improve them.

Positive Points
  • Decent and colorful 2D graphics
  • Alright SFX and pretty good soundtrack
  • Relaxing gameplay + weather makes it somewhat challenging
  • Many types of buildings and environments
  • 25 levels to explore + 5 boss levels
  • The ability to upgrade your 5 airplanes
  • Generals and tricky bonuses in the buildings
  • Steam trading cards and achievements

Neutral Points
  • The area of bombing should be bigger at higher levels
  • A level editor would be a great feature

Negative Points
  • The graphics and the mechanics need a little more polish
  • The droppings of the bombs are somewhat unnatural
  • It could get repetitive for certain gamers sometimes

This game turned up to be a mix bag for me, but after some thorough consideration, I decided not to recommend a purchase.

Graphically this is just about as good as a flash game, the art is neatly drawn and the animations are nice enough for what it tried to portray, though I wouldn’t agree with this as "high definition". Sure you can play this game in any screen resolution, but it’ll just stretch everything to accommodate your monitor, don’t fall for this stupid gimmick.

Presentation wise, I can see where the devs placed their efforts. You have 25 stages set in five different locales, 5 boss battles and a hidden seagull level. Almost every locale had some pop reference somewhere and every plane had their own unique take off sequence. But everything is ruined by repetition, time and again.

First of all, every plane performed the same way with the same upgrades earned by killing hidden generals. None of the bosses shoot at you ever and all of them were way too easy to beat. While you get to bomb buildings to the ground, they are always the same width of varying heights, with little variation on difficulty.

The biggest blunder must be the bonus crates, since they can punish the player by repairing buildings or "restart the stage completely". This mechanic has no design merits, and it can seriously annoy you as it did with other reviewers.

Lastly, this title can be completed within an hour, two if you go for every achievement. If you had to buy it, please do it during a sale.

Watch either video up there and you've seen the whole game. That's literally all there is: drop bombs on buildings to destroy them piece by piece. There are 5 stages that are essentially different themes, but nothing else changes. Each stage has a new plane to fly, but it's just looks -- nothing different. Each stage ends with a boss fight that is again just part of the theme. The only thing different about them is the number of bombs you have and the number of parts you have to hit (first boss has one thing to blow up, stage 2 has 2, stage 3 has 3, etc.)

I beat the game with 3 stars on all levels and 11/12 achievements in 90 minutes. I didn't even get all the card drops from the thing before it was over, so if my time goes up, it's because I let it idle just to get all the cards. I was bored after the first level, but I kept going wondering if it would get any harder or throw me any curveballs. Nope. The only comfort I can take away from this is knowing I can uninstall and cross this off my backlog as a game that I'll never have to think about again.

I don't think it's terrible, there's just not much to it. Recommended? No, not really. Maybe for kids, but I don't know why you want them to be bombing cities instead of making pie or something. The game functions just fine, but you can get the same experience I just had by watching one of the above videos 45 times and save yourself 2 bucks.
