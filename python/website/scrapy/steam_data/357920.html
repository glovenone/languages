Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Final Strike is a game that is designed and built to be played via a virtual reality headset and compatible vr devices. As this hardware is currently in the stages of development and iteration until the upcoming commercial release, we will continue to update and improve our software along with the continual development of VR hardware and interfaces.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Will will continue to develop the game in Early Access until top tier VR hardware, such as the Valve Vive, are completed with development and brought to a consumer market. We expect that this will happen between 8 to 14 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“There will be some improvements with the optimization and the speed at which the software can run in conjunction with VR hardware as we continue to balance the visual quality with the high framerate requirements of VR displays.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game currently has most of the playable stages and enemies included, as well a most of the final artwork and soundtrack. The final version will include some additional polish in these areas, but will not differ significantly from the Early Access product.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“No. The final retail price will be the same.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Yes, we love to get comments from players. It's one of the main reasons that we wanted to release in Early Access in the first place. With the slightly experimental nature of VR in it's development stages, additional testing and feedback from users is highly valuable and allows us to improve the experience.”

23 June

Seated Positional Hotfix Released

Thanks for all the feedback and support as we release Final Strike. We saw several comments on the forums and reviews that users were having issues with the correct centered seated position in thier VR setups.

We believe that we have isolated the problem in SteamVR and have just issued an update patch that should now override the tracking volume in SteamVR to seated, actively setting the HMD position to 0,0,0 in the game world. This should now place your view at the seated avatars' head position.

If you find that the revised view in on the ground, your SteamVR settings may have not been setup, or you may have SteamVR currently set 'standing' and will need to change that setting to 'seated'.

Keep posting any issues in our fourms as we continue to refine these settings so that we can ensure that all users are having a good experience. Thanks!

About This Game

What if fighter-pilot movies had a love child with alien invasion blockbusters from the ’90s? You’d no doubt get Final Strike, an edge-of-your-seat F-16 jet fighter simulator that puts you against an airborne invasion of alien invaders. Relive your favorite moments from your favorite end-of-the-world style blockbuster film as you pilot your very own military-grade death machine to eradicate the earth of the alien presence. A fully orchestrated custom soundtrack by legendary game composer Sean Beeson brings the cinematic story to life, making you truly feel like your in the scene of your favorite alien invasion movies.

Challenge yourself through massive aerial dogfights with the alien enemy. Fight either by yourself will partner team AI. For those brave enough to join the battle with the HTC Vive or Oculus Rift, a complete VR version of the game will put you directly in the action as you experience firsthand mission control where you receive your mission briefing and train to destroy the enemy. Complete VR immersion can be experienced with in-game VR menus, HUDS, and fully immersive 3D cockpits with working controls and gauges.

Early Access Watcher Public Service Announcement

Update Version: #1

Disclosure: This game copy was bought by the reviewer.

Generally I would write a full review, but with the amount of problems in regards to controls, I found it difficult to justify it. I tested the game using the HTC VIVE and Keyboard + Mouse to very negative results. The key issue involves the main window being unable to catch the mouse cursor before it leaves the border of the screen. If this happens, you cannot register the mouse clicks. I ended up, after several failed attempts, resting the Vive on my forehead and looking at the display window on the monitor to at least attempt a few more playthroughs. This allowed me to see where the cursor was, but it does not help the initial problem. Graphics are presentable, audio is extremely loud on default (and stuck on default), settings for volume and display options do not work, and motion sickness is a possibility. The content for this game is bare minimal with a couple of environments. The main objective revolves around destroying the alien mothership and that is pretty much the gist.

I cannot recommend this game purely on the circumstances I played it, which is a Vive owner using a mouse and keyboard. You might have better luck with a controller or the Rift, but if you fall under the other two categories I highly recommend skipping until these issues are fixed. I know this is Early Access, but if a game is barely playable with a listed component, it still needs work. I wish the developers all the best and hope to review this again six months down the line.

Early Access Review
Posted: 23 June
This is just barely reccomended for now as I don't think the game is good enough to warrant a purchase yet, but given a few more additions and fixes it might just be a fun little game.

Props to the devs for hot fixing the issue with the seated position on the Oculus Rift within a few hours of release and user feedback.

So for the mini review of this Early Access game:

Pros
- Great concept
- Lots of potential for fast and furious dog fights
- If you enjoyed the ending of Independence Day this probably is for you
- Very fluid and fast gameplay with good framerates
- Priced right for this type of game unlike so many other VR games out now

Cons:
- For some reason my guns and missiles auto fire - the dev stated they used the xbox 360 gamepads for development, and I'm using the xbone gamepad so that might be the issue - should be fixed as xbone pad comes standard with the Rift
- The scale of the cockpit and pilot model is off - I would say it is about 150 % of what it should be
- Extremely unrealistic flight model - yes, it is an action game but you go just as fast up down and to the sides there is no drag or gravity. It shouldn't be DCS World style, but a little bit more realism would make for more tactical gameplay and hopefully make it more interesting
- Some geometry glitch on the ground needs to be fixed, seems a solid is poking through the textures - nothing major it just is very noticeable
- The ground textures could possibly be made a little bit higher resolution on the highest gfx settings as they look very bland now
- A little more developed targeting system would be welcome - leading reticule for guns and a more solid lock on mechanism with tone for missiles maybe
- The enemy AI could be a bit more after the player - they have a tendency to leave me all alone at the start at least

At this price you can probably afford getting it even though it needs work - given some more development it could be an ok title for smaller play sessions.

I've played a few flight sims with my Vive and overall they are a very good experience. Final Strike is no exception, it has great graphics, fast gameplay and a lot of action. Compared to some of the other flight sims, like DCS, it is more arcade style, but no really a problem for me becuase the focus here is clearly on fighting, explosions, and taking down aliens.

Speaking of which, I think that the fact that you fight against an alien mothership a great addition, and I surprised that no other game has tried something like it before. You area really up to your neck in alien ships, and trying to take down the mothership while they are coming at you is pretty challenging. You have an assortment of weapons on your ship (even though unlimited ammo) but the balance feels good and even after an hour I haven't finished the first level yet.

A nice overall experience, and one that I expect to be coming back to regularly when I need a quick air combat fix in VR.
