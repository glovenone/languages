About This Game

1. What is âTactical Geniusâ?

Tactical Genius is a turn-based strategy game with the focus on multiplayer and a system of simultaneous orders with an emphasis on the tactical component. There is also an integrated with multiplayer ability to play against the computer opponent.

2. What is a âSystem of simultaneous ordersâ?

It allows you to give orders simultaneously with other people and watch the actions only when everyone has finished their moves.

3. So, all the units move simultaneously?

No, players simultaneously give orders to their units, capitals and forts. When all the players finish giving orders, units start to move by turns: by way of initiative, which depends on the unit type and its health. All players know at the stage of giving orders which unit will move first in this turn and take this into account in their plans. The greater initiative allows moving first.

4. How does it make the game special?

â¢ It doesnât matter who clicks the fastest, the movement begins only when everyone has finished giving orders. We donât spoil turn-based strategy by comparing the speed of mouse clicking!
â¢ Donât assume anything as an accomplished fact, but anticipate the moves of your enemies and mislead them. This interesting feature of the game will make you take a fresh look at the gameplay.
â¢ Here you can play a dynamic game, in which you don't have to wait for your turn alternately with your opponent (we all know those types of games and they can be tiresome).

5. What is the aim of the game?

Fight for the key points on a randomly generated map in mods 1x1 and 2x2.
More key points = more units.
Donât surrender your capital and try to capture the enemyâs capital.
Every unit has 3 simple skills and a fourth special skill, which is given only on level 5. You can get and improve skills as you gain experience in battles.
This gives you the opportunity to experiment with a variety of strategies, combinations of units, and their skills.

Choose what kind of units you will assemble and in which order. The current version of the game features six unit types: Warrior, Archer, Pyromancer, Vampire, Necromancer and Assassin. You will have to make a decision as to which unit will start the game, which will come next, and come up with your favorite combinations. For instance: a Warrior, three Vampire, and a Pyromancer. Or do you want to try to create the army consisting of invisible assassins? Or maybe you want to suppress enemies by using their own fallen warriors, focusing on necromancers hiding behind the archers?

You can play the game for free, earning gold to buy new units. Three basic units will be available at the beginning of the game, the other ones need to be bought for gold. Gold can be bought for real money, if desired.

Actually I am 50/50 on a recommendation here, because I think some people will like it. I have not played the game sufficiently to know how great the multiplayer pans out, and the finer points of the game overall. The theme is very generic, and there's no story or characters or anything that draws you in or makes you want to play it, other than the promise of a decent tactical experience. On that front, I think it is pretty straightforward, but offers enough depth to satiate a casual tactical appetite. My biggest criticism is the pace of the game itself, and the animations. It's very clunky and cumbersome. In some ways I felt like this was intentional, but I would have preferred a smooth experience. In my opinion, tactics of the game can be slow, even preferrably slow, but the mechanics should be fluid, and at least offset slower elements of the game with smoothness of the mechanics and animations; it's grating otherwise, and not enjoyable. I know these are completely different games (and it's a triple A big-budget title. not an indie title) but If you see the CIV games (CIV 4/5) - that is the sort of pace of mechanics, tile movement and combat I could envision being exceptional for this game. Over the couple hours that I played this, I found the game has merit, was even on the verge of interesting. And since it's free, you don't have much to lose seeing if you enjoy it. But if you're like me, I think you'll find the game's overall lackluster nature overshadows its finer potential.

[Original Review]

This game is excellent. I have just started playing recently but I find it very addictive already. Its simultanious turn based hex game. It kind of reminds me of mixture between chess and the battle phase of heroes of might and magic.

When i first played this i really wanted to like it but i could not understand the rules so I could not get into it. I was going to give up but instead i contacted the developer. I didnt expect any reply but he got in contact with me right away. I was really surprised by that and I told him what the problems I was having understanding the game. He then invited me to play against him 1 on 1 and showed me how the mechanics work in a game. After that the game made sense to me and it went from being a game i wanted to like to a game i am starting to love. I have only played a few hours so far but really feel this is a game i could play a lot.

I have seen some complaints about developer but from my experience he has been great and provided very friendly and in depty customer service. Also I have seen a bad review and I had the same problems when i didnt understand the rules but I have been told that the FAQ of the rules will be going up in the coming days so that should solve all those issues.

If you like turn based tactical games I would highly recommend this. I have not written any reviews on steam before but I feel this is a gem of a game that could be missed if people dont find out about it. The game at this moment is multiplayer only so it needs to grow the community.

My experience of matches in this game was great. Once you understand the mechanics you will find yourself engrossed in every turn and you have to think about every single move to try create any small advantage you can over your opponent and try stop him getting an advantage over you. It seems quite simple at first but as you get into a game it is actually very nuanced and complex. Sometimes and hour could pass of playing and it feels like only 5 minutes. Because the moves are made simultaneously you have to try second guess what your opponent will do and also how to use the terrain and your particular character classes to try out manouver your opponent.

Although this game is in early access it is at this moment in my opinion a complete game as it stands. I dont know where the developers plan to take this. Possibly add different units etc i have no idea but as it stands it plays great and is complete in and of itself. I would like this game to do well mainly because I really enjoy it and if it does well it would mean I would always have people to play against.

If you are still unure after reading this I would recommend at least try the demo and give it a few hours. Also look out for the FAQ which should be out soon from what I have been told because once you understand the rules the game is great.
