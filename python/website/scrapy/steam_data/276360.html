About This Game

Ride like a world champion! Breathtaking action, challenges in show jumping, dressage and cross country riding are waiting for your talent. You train rides and horses and lead them to success. The world championship venue, the world’s biggest riding Stadium in Aachen, invites you to take part in the finals in the large show jumping stadium and the Deutsche Bank stadium where the dressage championships take place.

The concept for dressage riding controls follows a completely new concept and provides more action while challenging the player’s concentration at the same time. In the atmospheric night level you will go for the ultimate feeling of excellent riding performances.

Features

  • Action-packed challenges in show jumping, dressage and cross country
  • Authentic reproduction of the Aix-la-Chapelle WM stadion (the world’s biggest riding stadium)
  • Beautiful graphic style
  • Editorial with Ludger Beerbaum

Posted: 30 November, 2015
The main reason I will recommend this game is because it's dirt cheap and it's quite fun to play for a bit, and even challenging on the dressage.

The game doesn't have any story line at all, just as a head's up. The game involves grooming horses, training for show jumping, cross country and dressage, hacking and competing.

This game was originally made for console (I had the game on PS2) and it doesn't seem they bothered to make any changes to put it on PC. As opposed to other equestrian games (most of which are not available on Steam) which use the arrow keys for direction, this game uses the mouse in a way that takes some getting used to. With a controller, one would use the right joystick/thumbstick to turn, pushing it to the right or left and releasing ; and so one must slide their mouse to the right or left to turn, but you must bring the mouse back to the center to stop turning. As we all have mouse accelaration, this can cause some frustration in the game play when you're not used to it.
I suggest turning the mouse accelaration off to make it easier, at least in the beggining, or just plugging your controller in if you have one. I have played both ways, neither is particularly easier.

This game is pure furstation, the controls are wonky and if your horse even a little bit off center for a jump it will refuse.

The grooming is annoying as you have to status bar to see how groomed your horse is, you just have to keep going until it says you're done.

The training is super repeative you just go around the same course over and over until your horse is trained enough for the competition.

The Cross Country and Show Jumping do not show how many faults you have on the screen, not to mention the Cross Country has really tight turns that would never be in a real Cross Country event.

The Dressage is good if you want to test how long your temper holds, it expects you to use the mouse and all four arrow keys at the same time to perform a 'figure'. You're supposed to press the right and left arrow keys to keep in 'harmony' with your horse, but this rarely ever works you must do this while keeping your mouse pointed in a certian direction and trying to steer your horse so it stays on course.

And it the higher competion levels you have to do the Dressage 3 times in a row, I've never seen a Dressage event that has 3 'rounds'.

Packed nearly 9 hours into this trying to figure out the controls, all I got in return was frustration.

The horse customzation sucks, you get to pick from three coats and a mane and tail colour. Can't even change the markings. You can edit the tack but half the tack is locked until you win the events.

You can free ride but the world is so ugly I can't stand using that feature.

If you're looking for a good horse game on Steam, try My Riding Stables 2: Life with Horses and pretend this game does not exist.

I just wanted to object to those very bad reviews of this wonderful game.
I know and have played all three RS Games. Especially RS3 has brought me years of wonderful gameplay. I don't even know how often I have restarted all three slots and how often I have finished the game compltely just to start anew.
The control, of which many here complain only suggest the usage of you mouse for the very best experience. It is not necessarily needed! It works perfectl using the arrowkeys and the W and S key for stirring the horse and commanding its gaits.
Also the rather long process of training your horse is very realistic. yes it gets harder and longer the closer you get to taking part in the grand show at Aachen. But it is also directly taken from reallife horseriding. It is indeed possible for a beginner to train for a week and then enter and maybe even win a beginners competition, but to achieve great results at a 4*/pro level or even attempt riding such a course it often takes years if not decades to achieve such horsemanship. Therefore far more realitic than many other horse simulation games.
And therefore also the free ride option is really an option if you get to stressed training your horses for the next competition Level. also it is a great help to memorize and just plan out the current crosscountry course (even though there are no jups in free ride mode). And that of course in the very calming athmosphere aof a magnificent sunset.
All in all this game is a game with hours of fun for everybody. Be they adults who ambitiously train their horses for the worldcup at Aachen or be they children just looking for some fun gameplay.

I got this game because I had the original Riding Star (actually Mary King's Riding Star) back in the day. Really enjoyed that, so I thought I'd get this for nostalgia reasons. It was a mistake. I don't remember the original having such frustrating controls. For this you have to use your mouse for just about everything, including steering and it just doesn’t seem to work. For the show jumping you have to approach the jump square, meaning if you over steer by just a little your horse will refuse. Dressage is even more complicated. The horse care factor seems to be the same as any other game of this type, nothing ground-breaking about it. The Riding Out option seems a bit pointless. The training you can do is probably the best part as you don't constantly have to stop every time you mess up!
If the controls were different this could potentially be a fun game, as it is it is just disappointing. I suggest sourcing an older PC and just playing the original.
