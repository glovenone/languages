About This Game

Based on the classic novel A Little Princess by Frances Hodgson Burnett. A life simulation game of romance and friendship between girls.



In British-controlled India, Sara Crewe was raised like a princess by her doting Papa, always given the best of everything. Now that she is old enough, she must be sent away to boarding school in a foreign land, relying on her love of books and fairytales to help her make friends.

But not all smiles can be trusted, and fortune can turn cold and hard at any moment. What would happen to a little princess if she lost everything?

Choose activities to build Sara's resources and change the direction of the story. Which girl will she form the strongest emotional bond with? Your decisions lead to six possible endings.

A Little Lily Princess is a life simulation game based on the novel A Little Princess. Those familiar with the novel will know what this game is about. The main character is Sara Crewe, a young girl (we don't really know what her age is so we can only guess) who arrives in England from India to attend an all girls boarding school. There, she meets a cast of characters and based on who you choose to have Sara interact with, she can form friendships or romance among six of them.

Although this is the second game published by Hanako Games based on a novel, I found this one to be more enjoyable than Sword Daughter. For one, as this is a life simulation game, there is an element where you choose to have Sara do certain activities (i.e., tutor, write in her diary, have a tea party) which will earn certain icons. These icons are needed to progress with various characters' paths. Despite this element, you don't need a walkthrough to successfully complete a path, as the game shows you what icons are needed to obtain certain scenes and such. Also, I found the writing to be more to my liking. Although I did find Sara to be naive at times (even after a certain event takes place), I liked taking on her character and seeing how she interacted with the others, even when she wasn't on certain characters' paths. Of course, the art (especially the CGs) and music were great and I really liked the ending song. (I suppose it's the genre that I like. XD) I enjoyed playing all of the paths as well although I did like some more than others. However, I'd say that I liked half of the paths I played and I was satisfied with the others. (For anyone who's curious, my favorite paths would be Becky's, Ermengarde's, and Jessie's.)

I would definitely recommend this game, especially for those who are fans of VNs, life simulations, etc. The story is very light-hearted although there is a bit of a serious element although it's more on the dramatic side than anything else. This is a game that young audiences can definitely handle. ^^

A fantastic novel, and one of the best from Hanako Games. You play as possibly one of the sweetest and sincerely kind characters I've ever had the pleasure of reading about and playing as.

The story itself is well-written, as is every character that is introduced. The story has a fantastic balance of comedy, romance, and tragedy. I found myself teary-eyed and even weeping at certain portions of the game, and I can't say many games have successfully done so.

The romance is all female/female (yuri), but it is kept to a very light and cutesy level, and sexuality plays no part in any of the interactions with the characters. For this reason, anyone can enjoy this game and be moved by all that it has to offer.

I cannot recommend this visual novel enough. If you need something bright and cheerful, or just simple enjoyment, this is money well-spent.

A Little Lily Princess is good, and cute, and occasionally touching, but it wasn't great. It felt like most of the relationships just ended when the time came for the story to end, often just when they were getting interesting, and the age gaps made the Lottie and Mariette routes feel not particularly yuri. The Lavinia route was good, though, and I'm glad I saved it for last. It broke many of the patterns the other routes followed, since Lavinia wasn't an antagonist. Well, not quite. You can tell the devs liked her route a lot, because she got the most unique art. Kabedon!

But then this piece is never used, and is only available from the "extras" menu for some reason. Lame. Also, only one route had a kiss, which is quite disappointing in this kind of game. Other routes had romantic holding, or cuddling, or a proposal, though, so that was nice.

The main complaint I have about the game is that they apparently felt the need to make it more "gamelike." You have to choose your tasks week-to-week to build up points to unlock events with, but the choices don't matter that much, the costs and deadlines are super forgiving, and the tasks after ♥♥♥♥ goes down in the second half are so dumb that I literally went with the same pattern of chores every week, a straight diagonal from upper left to lower right. It's a bunch of busy work, and I wish it just wasn't.

Due to this ad, I had the thought, "I wonder what happens if you don't view any events? Do things still work out well for our plucky heroine?" Not so much. The game normally ends somewhere around week 45, I think. After that, it just alternates between the above screen and the "you've maxed out your stats, skip this week's tasks" screen. So, it's not a bad end, per se, it's a bad eternity, a bad non-end. Horrific.

Overall, it's a good game I don't regret purchasing, and I'd recommend it if you're in the mood for a yuri VN.

A wonderful little Visual Novel based on the beloved turn-of-the-century novel in which you follow Sara, a quiet, imaginative, and kind young lady raised in India and sent away to England for schooling. The game features some light stat-raising elements and very light decision making, but it is primarily a linear story with optional events. In the second half of the game, the story divides into 6 distinct routes, each leading to a unique ending. The avaliblity of the routes depends on your actions in the first half of the story, so multiple playthroughs of the first act are required to see all the routes; fortunately, the game includes to option to skip through already-viewed scenes.

The writing is thoroughly enjoyable and adorable. Some of the routes feature light romantic elements, but not all of them do; you will find a range of different relationships from good friends, to nuturing parental figure, to sweetly romantic.

Overall, a highly recommended VN, particularly if you're ok with something lighter on gameplay and focusing on narrative. If you're looking for a Princess Maker-style stat raiser, on the other hand, this one might not be for you.

Note: This game was originally purchased directly from the developer and this review was posted immediately following the pre-Steam release beta period, so hours played might not line up with the actual length of this game.

EDIT: Oops, forgot to mention that my really short gameplay time is due to the fact that I played the beta quite a bit back before this game released on steam or even on their website.

The best and shortest way I can describe this game is that it is VERY precious. Might be the most precious game I've played.

Art & Music
You only need to look at the screenshots of the game to see that it has really beautiful art, with well-drawn backgrounds and character portraits that work together really well. The specific aspect that bumps this up though are CGs. They look amazing blown up on my widescreen monitor where all the little details can be easily pointed out. It must also be said that the same goes for the rest of the game. At least on my machine, it ran full widescreen with no indication of any stretched images, which for some reason is not as common as I would like in other works of the same genre. This game aced this part. I'm not really very good at describing music, but I have to say that this one tugged at my heartstrings way too often (to the point of abuse really). It fits the individual moments perfectly with every sense of dread, sadness, glee, or just plain heartwarming moments that make you go "d'awwww" while holding back tears. That won't work though, I tried, too many times.

Story & Gameplay
The story was paced really well and I didn't feel at any time in the game that it was moving too quickly or dragged out. Oh, and for people who haven't actually read the original novel this game was based on, do yourself the favor of not reading the story description as it contains a bit too much hints that I would've liked. Trust me, when THAT moment happens in the game, it'll be just a little bit more impactful, at least it was for me as someone who had no idea what was going to happen.(although I did sort of see it coming anyways, the way it was executed was still so good that I was genuinely suprised still). The writing is also excellent as it matches the setting of the game and gives enough flavor that no line would seem uninteresting or something to be skipped. I must also say that the writing is special in the sense that it could make the most simplest of lines sound so touching and heartwarming or heartbreaking. Again, paired with the music this REALLY leaves an impact. Gameplay wise, think Long Live the Queen except you won't be stressed out whatsoever. No need to stress your head on this one folks, just do what you think is the most optimal way and it probably is. Even if it's not, it'll still go well anyways. It's stat raising in order to meet requirements to progress on the different routes of the game. Although, I believe there's still one route that is unlocked even if you don't invest in it. So even if you don't bother with the gameplay and skip most of that, you'll still reach the end relatively unscathed.

Characters
I'll just put this here separately as an emphasis on the fact that I LOVED every character that was supposed to be loved and I HATED the characters that were supposed to be hated. That's all really -- every one was fleshed out, had depth, and no one of value seemed flat. Regarding Miss Michin and Cook, I don't think those even counted as human beings so they kind of don't count.

Addendum: Hmm...what else can I say...oh yes. This cute little girl protagonist is a braver person and a better ladykiller than I can ever hope to be. I cried more than she did while playing through just one route. So yeah, there's that.
