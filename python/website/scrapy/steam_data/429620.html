Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Dungeon Rushers is an ambitious game with a lot of different features, so we are looking for the Community’s participation to make some aspects of the game evolve in the best way for our players.

Players’ feedback is something precious that can make the difference if you care about it, that’s why we want them to test different game modes for our matches between friends feature.

We also want to test the online mode in real situation, because we know that players can be very inventive to exploit the unbalanced parts of a game (even the smallest), to use a gameplay mechanic in a way that we couldn’t anticipate or to combine them in a way we might have overlooked.

The dungeon building system is a really powerful tool which is now available in its second version. We have improved it a lot thanks to our beta community and we hope that the early access will give us plenty of ideas to make it become the perfect tool for our players.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We have planned to make it last 4 months, with a final release in September 2016 on Steam for PC, Mac and Linux.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We are working on new features to add like the never ending dungeon mode, which will be a potentially infinite succession of randomly generated floors, and will also make as much improvements as we can on different aspects of the game and its mechanics.

We are planning on adding new game modes for matches between friends as we gather Community’s feedback, to make sure that this is what or how they’d like to play with friends.

Finally, there will be an additional complete theme with new monsters, items, graphic environment, musics, and who knows… Maybe characters!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“For now, there are 3 different themes playable in campaign mode (crypts, mountains and jungle), all with their own monsters, items, graphic environment and musics, representing around 8 hours of play time through an original story full of humor.

We also find that dungeons’ replayability is something important, that’s why each one have bonus challenges, plus an heroic mode. In addition, you can already build your own dungeon to play between friends and in arena mode with rankings.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We're starting with a lower price for Early Access launch and it might be increased when releasing the final game, depending on the features added.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will follow and use Steam forum as much as we can to gather our Community’s ideas and suggestions, and sometimes ask them for some specific feedback when needed. There will also be an in-game feedback system to share informations or make reports more easily.

In addition, we will maybe put in place some skypes conversations sporadically with an “Elite” testers team who will represent the whole Community. The way they will be chosen is still to be determined if communication events like these can be done.”

About This Game

Dungeon Rushers is a 2D tactical RPG combining dungeon crawler’s gameplay and turn based fights. Manage your team, loot dusty dungeons, crush armies of monsters and craft mighty equipment. Be part of an epic adventure with a colorful cast of characters in an off-beat universe. Build your own dungeon and challenge other players!

The Game:


Over the course of an epic quest, explore many dungeons and loot their guarded treasures. Build your own team by choosing the best hero combination to overcome traps and monsters lurking in the shadows.

Upgrade the skills of your heroes to make them even more fearsome and place them strategically to achieve victory.

Craft your equipment on your own with components gathered during your journey thanks to an advanced system consisting of six different craftsmen.

Complete dungeons and their bonus challenges to unlock the Heroic difficulty, where only the best can make their way to the treasure!

Build your own dungeon in which you can spread traps and monsters to prevent other adventurers from looting your chest! Hire your « dream team » by exploring a world where dungeons and manors have been abandoned by men in favor of the worst creatures, then attack other players' dungeons to plunder their treasure!

Features:


  • Campaign mode full of humor with various sound and graphic environments
  • Interactive exploration system
  • Revised turn based fights with a hint of nostalgia
  • Advanced team management (equipment, skills upgrade, in-fight position, ...)
  • Wide range of skills to use (scout, heals, dodge, trap deactivation, monsters stalking)
  • Crafting system featuring many recipes to unlock
  • High replay value with a Heroic difficulty for each dungeon
  • Online arena system with matchmaking and ranking
  • Battles among friends, to die against people you love
  • Epic custom-made soundtrack

In Dungeon Rushers, you're quite literally just rushing through dungeons.

Though Dungeon Rushers does not sport a decent story, sense of progression, or intricate crafting system that we've become so accustomed to seeing, it's not advertised as including either of those.

What it does include, however, it does right.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s0j51WfbAvs

The quirkiness is not too much. There's a huge selection of members to choose from. The art-style is cute and charming, and each character has their own appeal. Thorgrim is your grumbling old dwarf who grows increasingly tired of your antics, that vampire chick is obviously high maintenance, and you can't have a proper dungeon travelling party without a musical bard at your side. The game allows you to create your own dungeons. In any game with any amount of drawbacks, a space for player-created content is always a plus.

Most importantly is the fact that the game is still in the very early stages of development. It's — in my opinion — one of the more well-developed games to hit Early Access, and it has room to grow considerably.

How simple the game is designed means developers can freely add content as time permits. While it does little to inspire and offer a completely memorable experience, the game definitely shows promise. That's a huge win as far as I'm concerned.


Bad
  • The combat system is so easy that even a chimpanzee can achieve success.
  • No progression, all characters look the same.
  • Can get repetitive.
  • Only three abilities, and there's no need to use the other two.

Good
  • Movement is simple.
  • No sad or typical story of heroism or vengence. Just a whimsical adventure of a boy turned treasure-hunter.
  • Random events keep you on your toes.
  • Dungeon designer is easy and intuitive, and player-made content is enough to force players to use their other skills.

It may feel like a mobile game sometimes, and a free-to-play game other times - but it somehow manages to just break through that barrier and give you enough control to make it feel like a good game that you've paid for.

It is slow though. Very slow. You have to grind through levels and do a lot of levelling, with features being given to you gradually over a very long period of time. 2 hours in now, and I still haven't gotten the arena or my own dungeon unlocked. I still haven't unlocked enough characters to even make use of the party swap in/swap out feature.

I can tell there's something great here though. A detailed RPG with a really cool art style that will challenge your decision-making skills and allow you to compose your own party of adventurers which will get slaughtered unless you think ahead and use some strategy with your composition.

BUT all that said - the game has certainly potential. And if they work on some of the problems I can see this as a fun game.
