Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“In order for the game to reach it's full potential, feedback from the community is an extremely valuable resource in order to improve player interaction and the core gameplay experience! Whilst confident in the game's direction, ideas and features may be improved even further, with the help from dedicated players.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“With the endless possibilities and features of the game, a full release date has not yet been set, however, this will certainly be before the end of the year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“New features are constantly being added and expanded upon, such as; combat improvements, new item recipes, better map generation. Full launch will add new variety to building, crafting and exploration, and ongoing balance changes.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“While the game is in Early Access, there is still much to do, including 10+ featured biomes, 400+ items to discover and craft and 30+ monster types to combat. However, as this is not the full release, the game may also contain bugs, item stats/monster imbalances, and rarely, corrupt or deleted saves.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“As more features are added, the price will be increased fairly to reflect the amount of game content available.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We wish the game to be as fun as possible, and is made for the players. New ideas and any issues from feedback will be constantly evaluated and changed, as players discovered new ways to play!”

23 June

Update 0.9.6

There was a lot of request for different keyboard layouts/more comfortable control schemes, so rebindable keys are now an option. In addition to this are a few GUI improvements that should make the game easier to play/understand.

Features/Changes

  • New keybind menu (also accessible in-game)
  • Camera will no longer offset while in a menu
  • Clicking a hotbar slot outside a menu will now select that item
  • Cursor prompt will now display whether an item/tile is usable (including it's function)
  • Shift + click to transfer items to and from a container or crafting slots
  • Placeable items will now display a 'ghost' tile before being placed

Bug fixes
  • Some item IDs were incorrectly assigned, causing a different item to load in it's place
  • Clicking 'Save' more than once will no longer corrupt the world
  • All resolutions for multiple monitor setups are now available as options
  • Can no longer place (and lose) items in the crafting output slots
  • Some floor types were not considered a legit tile for some placeable items

About This Game

Forsaken Isle is a wilderness survival game focused around crafting with RPG elements. You play as a pirate, marooned on a desert isle and must gather resources, craft items to survive the isle, of which its inhabitants are not all so friendly.

Key Features

  • A sandbox world
  • Randomly generated maps
  • Crafting
  • Rare items to find and discover
  • Free updates

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7, Windows 8
    • Processor: 2.0 Ghz
    • Memory: 1024 MB RAM
    • Graphics: 512 MB graphics memory, OpenGL 3.0
    • DirectX: Version 9.0

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 February
NOTE: THE SCREENSHOTS ON THE STORE PAGE ARE NOT AN ACCURATE DEPICTION OF THE GAME, AS OF V. 0.9.4, WHICH I HAVE BEEN PLAYING ON.

It's like a top-down Terraria, which is fantastic. This means there isn't platforming, but rather strafing (M+KB), but apart from that much of the gameplay is similar. I will add here that the sprite work is WAY better than Terraria's and though you get less customization overall (1 helm slot, 1 armor slot, 3 accessory slots), I expect this to change as the game develops.

The farming in this game is absolutely fantastic and mesmerizing, easy to pick up and satisfying to do (also super quick, though I expect this to be tweaked later; currently it's way too easy to just farm up a few hundred loaves of bread and solve your hunger forever).

This is a criminally overlooked game, probably because in-game there's no guidance whatsoever (might change but I hope it doesn't; discovery is great in it as it is currently).

Pros:

- Simple and easy controls, mechanics
- Visually beautiful - I expect there to be more variety as the game develops as well
- Familiar gameplay progression (build X to build Y, which lets you build Z, etc.)
- Farming is satisfying and rewarding (best foods through farming AFAIK)
- Exploration is satisfying and rewarding (required to get forging materials, early on you get some good stuff through just walking around)
- You can raise your own pigs/sheep/chicken, something that is often overlooked in many games like this. Unlike Minecraft where you had to gimmick rooms to spawn cows/sheep/whatever before, ranching is built into the game here.
- Auto-detect nearby crafting stations so you're not always switching back and forth for a single item. Bunch up your related crafting stations.
- A lot to do in-game despite early access

Cons (NOTE: The game is EA when I write this review, I expect most of these to be solved on launch because they are quite significant IMO)

- No multiplayer yet. I would be very sad if the game finished without multiplayer, this seems like a REALLY fun MP game.
- Hunger rate needs reworking IMO, currently it drops a bit too fast so you're forced to spend a lot of time foraging until you build a farm, after which your hunger issues go from "urgent" to "non-existant" really fast
- Saves lag my PC for unknown reasons, this is really annoying with auto-save as the game sill simply freeze for about 2-3 seconds every 12-hours in-game time.
- No way to make multiple items at once (shiftclick, ctrlclick, hold click, etc.); really annoying when you have 1000 logs and need to turn half of those into sticks.
- No way to split stacks of items, gotta move them wholesale.
- Item organization button would be extremely useful tbh, it's annoying to manually have to move items every time you craft something.
- Monster variety could probably be expanded a bit.
- Updates are slow. AFAIK the team is super small so don't expect this to fully release any time soon. The game is definitely playable but not finished.


Also under cons, but more bug-related:

- Items never disappear from the map (they just blink forever)
- You can activate doors and refill troughs from across the screen, not sure why this happens

Overall, for an EA game it's surprisingly well polished and I'm really looking forward to release. Honest 9/10 if you don't count the mildly annoying bugs that come with the game not being finished.

Early Access Review
Posted: 21 July, 2015
Forsaken Isle
Developer: Smoodlez
Publisher: Smoodlez

Note: A key for this game was provided by the developer for the purpose of writing an informative review. No other compensation was given.


Introduction
==========
"Ahoy matey! Me ship crashed on this island, leavin' me alone on this godforsaken island. It's up t' me t' find a way t' survive! This be a survival craftin' game, much like Minecraft, Terraria, and other games in t' genre. I have t' collect resources t' make what I need t' get by. Fightin' monsters, buildin' shelter, makin' food...all be skills that I will need t' survive on this mesterious island."


Gameplay
==========
Forsaken Isle literally dumps you onto a large island with next to nothing. There is no story, except for what is found on the store page. You play as a pirate whose ship crashed on this island. You don't start with anything. You'll have to forage for supplies and gather resources to build and craft tools to build and survive. The first night is very important, as you should be able to build some walls around yourself with a campfire to light your area, to ward off monsters.

There is a somewhat robust character creator (robust for this type of game with it's graphics), allowing different heads, hair, and other features. There is also a random option.

As you discover new materials and resources, new recipes are made available to you. But it's your job to discover them and put them to use! This is one of the fun aspects of any survival game: figuring out what does what, what makes what, and what's useless. You can also find/craft weapons and armor, which grant you different effects, such as increased defense or movement speed.

Graphics
==========
Forsaken Isle is entirely pixel based. Everything looks really crisp and detailed for being sprite-based. While this isn't a heavy 3D modelled game like something from a AAA title, it works. The retro pixel style they've used here is very appealing, and suprisingly pleasant.

Equipping different tools, weapons, and armor all change how your character looks. Put on a Wolf Helm, you'll see a wolf head on your character. Holding a sword? You'll see the wooden sword being brandished.


Sound and Music
==========
This is the game's lowest point: There is absolutely NO music in the game whatsoever. MOst everythign has a sound effect though: sword slashes, digging, animals, picking up items...but no music. Not even ambient sound effects like crickets or whatnot. Being an Early Access title, it is safe to assume that the music is just not ready to be implemented yet, and I'm sure it will be in the future. For now, this is a great game to play with your own playlist blasting in the background!


Performance
==========
In the dozen hours I played I ran into no issues with the game. No FPS drops, no lag, no stuttering. It's a very minimal game, no one should have any issues running Forsaken Isle. Heck, even a toaster could run it!


Final Thoughts
==========
A worthy entry into the survival genre that's not an obvious copy of another title, it's almost too good to be true! With some great systems and gameplay already set in place, Forsaken Isle should shape up nicely in the coming months as it runs through Early Access. If you don't want to drop the $8 on it now, just keep an eye out for the next few updates. Even if you do buy it now and are disappointed, $8 isn't as much as some of the AAA titles that have bombed recently and there's a chance it'll shape up into something you'd enjoy later.

==========

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 February
Forsaken Isle is a sandbox crafting game where you start with nothing and advance by crafting more powerful tools and weapons (wood to stone to copper, etc) while exploring the region and fighting monsters. Although it is Early Access, it is very stable and fun, but could use a bit more polish. I'm still having a lot of fun crafting items and designing my home base, however.

PROS
+ Simple, effective controls make gathering items and fighting monsters a breeze
+ Large overworld with multiple biomes and underground cave systems to explore
+ Building house/farm/base is easy and fun
+ Progressive crafting and hunger systems encourage exploration
+ Charming graphics

CONS
- Experimenting to find recipes is fun and rewarding at first, but quickly grows very frustrating
- No tutorial, quests, or instructions makes the initial hour of the game seem a bit daunting
- Dragging items from inventory to storage or crafting window sucks (needs a "Shift-Click to move" setting BADLY)
- No storyline or quests makes it seem a little lifeless
- Minimal sound effects and virtually no music
