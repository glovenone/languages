About This Game

Medusa’s Labyrinth is a mythological first person horror game set in ancient Greece. It takes myths and legends that have stayed with us for over 2000 years and mix them together in a game that blends horror with stealth into a unique experience.

The player will have to carefully pick their path through the abandoned hallways on Temple Island, through winding catacombs and dark corners to solve the mysteries that lurk within the labyrinth.

The game was originally planned to be a part of a much larger game, but we decided to launch this version as a short free adventure, so anyone with a Steam account can play it without paying a dime. We really hope you will enjoy the experience, as we made it public for just that reason: For you people to get a glimpse of what Medusa could have become with a longer production time and additional funding.

More of a demo than a finished product, this game takes heavy inspiration from Frictional Games' productions, but unfortunately the extremely short duration and the unpolished mechanics make the game feel a bit too amateurish and incomplete to really leave any lasting impressions. Nonetheless I still feel like recommending it, but only because it's free and will entertain you for a good 20 minutes perhaps, if you take your time.

The atmosphere is enjoyable and the notes you will find sparse through the level contribute to create an eerie ambient and make the exploration a captivating experience, albeit short. The visuals are not bad, but the developers failed to harness the power of the engine making the game unjustifiably heavy and demanding for the graphical quality it has. Many of the models have a too low polygonal count compared to the rest of the assets, same for the textures, some are decent, others are a bit on the low side, and lastly the lighting has some issues, proving distracting from the atmosphere. The sound is again decent, but the music lacks variety, and ends up being repetitive.

The little gameplay it has is mediocre, it doesn't have crippling problems but has some minor issues with dropping and picking up torches and slightly inaccurate hitboxes, and especially unlikable are the bow mechanics and how the main character suddenly interrupts the aiming when the enemy is getting close, making it look more like a glitch than a feature at first. Limiting the character to just exploring and hiding without the ability to use a weapon would have been a better choice, especially since the monster will die with a single arrow to the head and getting hit by the enemy will just respawn the protagonist shortly before the encounters take place, giving the player ample possibility to just keep trying until he succeeds, also eliminating any kind of tension that is crucial to this kind of games.

Also, if you use an average ADSL connection the download will last longer than the game.
