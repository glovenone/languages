Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Gunslingers was initially developed for social networks, but in spirit it is closer to the games we're used to seeing on Steam. Early access is required to ensure the Steam version's stability and to connect certain Steam mechanics such as Steam achievements.
Moreover, players of the Steam version will play with Facebook users. This interconnection of platforms cannot be fully tested before the game is launched.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We plan to spend no more than a month in early access.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Most of all, leaving early access means that we're confident in the game's stability. The interfaces will continue to be refined and balance may change somewhat after this.
We will also add Steam achievements”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current state of the game is just as good as the Facebook version. Players from Steam can play both with each other and with Facebook users.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game will remain free-to-play.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“You can leave reviews and suggestions in the group. Also, if you have any problems while playing, please use the 'report a error' function in the settings menu. This is the fastest and best way to help us improve the game.”

About This Game

A unique turn-based tactical game in a Space Western setting.


Direct your mercenary in team PvP battles. Pick one of 5 classes and choose from a large assortment of armor, weapons, and equipment.

The more battles you fight, the cooler your mercenaries become, the faster they run, the better they shoot, and (most importantly) the more abilities they learn â abilities that can dramatically change the course of the battle. Use attribute points and carefully choose your equipment to match your mercenary to your unique playing style.

Play on your own and join random battles. Or, team up with your friends in a clan and take part in tournaments.

key Game Features


  • Quick turnbased gameplay. Each team's players take their turns simultaneously, resulting in a fast-paced game with tactical interaction between team members.
  • 5 player classes. Command up to 5 mercenaries, each with their own skills and abilities â their own strengths and weaknesses.
  • 2 modes of combat. In Skirmish mode, simply kill your enemies and protect your allies to gain victory. In Capture mode, you also need to capture and defend control points.
  • Clan tournaments. Every few days, tournaments are held to determine the strongest clan!

More Info About A Mercs

5 mercenary classes are available in the game: stormtrooper, scout, sniper, medic, and robot. Each merc has his or her own skills, weapon, and playing style.



  • John the sniper, armed with a sniper rifle, deals immense damage to his target at a large distance. The sniper can also cover an ally who is under threat of being attacked.
  • Lira the scout, on the other hand, uses subterfuge and cunning. She's a scout and saboteur who can sneak up on the enemy's rear quickly and unnoticed, revealing the enemy's position to her allies and weakening and wounding him before nimbly hiding herself from his retribution. Her weapon is an SMG.
  • Next is the robot Bobby, a universal battle machine with a tough metal hull and armed with a machine gun. Bobby has two modes: defensive mode, where he can cover allies with his powerful shield, and attacking mode, where he turns into a weapon of mass destruction.
  • The medic is an essential unit on the battlefield. Her name is Leto, and she's not afraid to take aim at her allies â with a syringe gun full of healing nanobots or a stimulator that can refill action points. An efficient medic can make a good team unstoppable!
  • Last of all is the stormtrooper Greg. He is a tough guy armed with an assault rifle. His job is to break through the enemy's ranks, deal them damage, interfere with their activity, and all the while survive enemy attacks.

You can pick any merc to bring into battle and develop all of your mercs in parallel.

Posted: 19 December, 2015
Edit, 07.01.2016: In short: So the energy system is gone, since there were many negative reviews stating precisely that this kind of mechanic does not belong on steam. (Good work people. Speak out that we do not want steam to become a semi-Facebook.) Hence, I edited parts of my review. But in sum, I still find the game to be too repetetive, shallow and cannot overlook the fact that you can only craft a tiny amount of free stuff, while there is a p2w marketsystem in place aswell. Again: I'd rather pay for customization, knowing that we all have the same chances, than paying for superiority.

+ Fallout-esque artwork. The maps look alot like a polished Fallout 1 or 2. Or a apocalpse-themed Jagged Alliance 2.
+ Turn-based combat. Every team has 20 seconds for its actions. Thats more than enough, keeps the game at a great pace and opens various strategy options.
+ Lots of skills already. You can upgrade your merc with exp and ingame currency. So you do not have to spend any dime to advance. You can freely choose how to spent the exp. Also, when your merc levels up, you can spend 3 points per level on his abilities to upgrade him or her even more.
+ 5 different classes. Sniper, tank, scout, medic/tank and grunt.
+ Tactical gameplay on different maps. You will probably see some of the maps but currently their are not too many gamemodes.
+ Short loading times. There is barely no loading time. You can hop in and out without having to wait a longer period of time.
+ Nice soundtrack. Even if I do not understand a word, I can still feel that the soundtrack fits into the game and compliments it.

- Ingame cash for superior items. Cash items do have better stats and give great advantages over regular items. Like better evasion, higher damage output, more health, you name it. Sort of "pay 2 win" or "pay 4 advantages".
- Limited Energy mechanic. Not sure why this is in place. While you can buy the ingame currency, gold and premium time with real money, there is also a limited amount of energy. That means, you have like 500 energy units, a battle costs 5 energy units, traveling the map costs energy units. I did not make it to the limit, but it confuses me that its even there.
- Low playerbase. I know this game just launched, but with the current playerbase its hardly fun. Most of the times, each team has one real player and the rest is filled up with bots. Yes, bots. There is no way around them unless you want to wait 4 hours for the matchmaking. But the A.I. is really annoying since they will constantly run into their deaths and feed the enemy team. So, after all is does not make a huge difference if you play in any tactical way.
- Capture mode is a pain in the bottom and has nothing to do with strategy. Its basically spamming spawns, rushing to the capture point and standing next to each other spamming AOE damage. Thats not fun. Not at all.
- Grindy. Very grindy. That would not be a problem if the matches were engaging and exciting. But since you basically shoot bots all the time it quickly becomes a mind-numbing process.

In sum: While there is a great game that could be alot of fun, there are various mechanics that do not feel right at all. Instead of paying for customization, like in LoL or other games where the items are strictly only cosmetics, you can buy better items with real money. You could also get those items by playing the game and logging in daily, but it would probably take a year and you would always be the "worse equipped" merc in your rated game. Also, the energy mechanic makes no sense at all. Why make the player pay for items aswell as for playtime? So after you bought an item, you still have a limited period of time until you have to either close the game or pay more money. With that mechanic, I don't understand how the playerbase shall grow, since many players will have to shut down their game and will eventually be annoyed.
Like other players stated, features like this are known for facebook or such but do not make sense on steam. I'd rather pay to customize my merc in a non-p2w way than have to pay to keep up and to keep playing. So, I cannot really recommend this game, even if it looks so gorgeous and could be so much fun.
