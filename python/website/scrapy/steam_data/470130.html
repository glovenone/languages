Posted: 8 June
I don't leave reviews often, but I feel like I should for this particular title. I downloaded it because it had a positive user review. The user reviews are a large deciding factor on whether or not I purchase I game (I know, rather hypocritical because I don't leave reviews myself). I personally really enjoy VR and hope that it takes off which is why I am supporting the developers working on VR titles to show that there is a market. I do not want people to be put off by this demo because of the poor rating. Unfortunately every negative review I have seen has been posted by someone who does not own a Vive headset. They claim that the demo doesn't work or won't load even though it says on the store page that a VR headset is required. I personally can't see how anyone can so quickly go to negatively review something over technical issues that they obviously didn't attempt to troubleshoot. Any quick google search would let them know why this demo wasn't working for them.

Overall the demo is not perfect. The weapons are pretty uneven in power. The laser is extremely powerful with basically no negatives and it carried me rather effortlessly to the end of the demo. I barely passed the first wave when trying to use the mini-gun. Plus only one tracked controller is used meaning no dual wielding (not really to much of an issue because holding a mini-gun in each hand is a bit unrealistic). However there are a few different types of enemies, which most current Vive paid demos even lack.

The demo is short, it doesn't take very long to complete, and I probably won't be playing it again unless something new is added. But for a free alien shooter it is a fun experience the first time through. It's pretty cool shooting at giant scorpions and later seeing the roof open up and you end up having a boss battle with some type of ship. There is nothing here super amazing or incredible but it is still fun and would be a great game to demo to friends and family.

Admittedly I probably would not have downloaded this demo if it didn't have a positive overall rating and I would have missed out on a cool little shooter. For a free VR shooter game this shouldn't be passed up. I don't want people to overlook a fun free demo because of the negative/mixed rating. It definitely doesn't deserve that and only has that low of a rating because of people too lazy to do their research before leaving a negative review. Don't be fooled by the lower overall rating, because it should most certainly be positive.

It's not bad, but there's room for improvement.

1. The weapon choice desk doesn't spawn directly infront of me, but somewhat to the right.
2. With the amount of enemies from different directions, i'd say dual-wielding weapons would be a very welcome addition.
3. I like how the Minigun feels like an arm, maybe expand/change the gun models to have some sort of arm going in to increase the feel of immersion
4. Enemies don't dodge in the slightest, it'd be an added difficulty aspect if atleast the bigger enemies tried to somewhat dodge your fire.
5.Energy/electric weapon is too OP for clearing out the groups of small enemies, while the flamethrower is perfect for this, if a little too strong/fast in clearing them out.
6. Flamethrower is too powerful against big enemies.
7. I think you focussed a little too much on the difficulty stemming from the amount of enemies, i feel like they die like flies even the big ones) and there's no real accomplishement in killing them . Less enemies at once but with a lot more health per enemy would be my preference.
8. I like that you already have very different types of enemies and ways of spawning them in (Hangar doors,the slime nests,dragon-alien flying in) and that they have varying looks.
9.infinite ammo makes things too easy, maybe make ammo packs spawn around the map that we ned to shoot to gain more ammo/fuel/energy, doing this with health packs might also be useful.

All in all, i'd say it shows a lot of promise, and definitely not bad for a free-to-play game!
