Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“A massive thank you everyone for the overwhelming support and positive feedback we have received so far. We are Dark Amber Softworks, a small and independent development team from Finland, and we welcome you to to explore and experience the world of Avenging Angel.

We would like to have you, the players, involved in the development. Tell us how we can make the experience better, and tell us what you think is good, bad, and/or ugly.

We will update the game frequently during Early Access and we will bring new content into the game with each major update.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We want to make Avenging Angel good, really good. We are aiming for Winter 2016 full release.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Avenging Angel Early Access delivers the beginning of the story and the topside of the island to explore. Please note that we start to bring these new features and add-ons during Early Access in bigger updates based on feedback.

In Avenging Angel full version are planning to have:
  • A huge underground facility to explore and new above-ground areas
  • More quests
  • More enemies
  • New weapons, including the fabled GunBlade
  • More RPG elements
  • More neutral/friendly NPCs with full voice-over and subtitles
  • A second playable character with a slightly different story
  • Fast travel system
  • More crafting and modding recipes
  • Lots more ingredients and parts
  • Wildlife you can hunt for alchemy ingredients
  • A battle arena, which can be also accessed from main menu, when unlocked in game
  • A mini-game to unlock unique weapons and power ups
  • Achievements
  • Full VR compatibility
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Avenging Angel Early Access is alpha. The game, and the story so far, is fully playable and the autosave system is fully functional.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We do recognise that this is an Early Access product as of now, and the full release will be priced differently. Alpha access includes the full game when it releases and all future updates.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We see you all and we hear you all.

Every email, tweet, and note will be read by the development team. We handle our websites, social media sites, and forums ourselves, so whenever you give us a shout, it will be the development team answering to you.

We will also have bi-monthly shout-outs in Steam asking for feedback on the very issue we are tackling at the time. We will then post screenshots/videos about the progress at the end of every second week.

During Early Access we will have at least two contests where the person whose entry ends up winning gets his/her screen name (or real name) placed inside the "Memorial" building in-game and a limited edition t-shirt.

You can contact us in Facebook , Twitter or email us info (at) darkamber (dot) org”

About This Game

Overview:


Avenging Angel is a story-driven FPS/Adventure game set in a post-apocalyptic steam-powered world.

The story takes place in Northern City States, in a post-apocalyptic steampunk setting, roughly 10000 years from our current day and age, where 21st Century has not only passed from history to legend, but also from legend into myth. You are an Associate of the Inventors Guild, a freelancer/adventurer, dropped on an island in the Atlantic Ocean on a routine maintenance job. As you venture through the island, it becomes clear that there is nothing remotely routine about this mission as you are pulled into the web of personal, international, as well as metaphysical, conflict and conspiracy. Decipher forbidden history, learn about forgotten tragedy, and in the end, learn something about yourself.

Key Alpha Features:


  • A story-driven adventure FPS set in a steampunk world.
  • Interact with NPCs (with full voice over) to take on quests to progress in the story or discover new locations on your own.
  • Hours of gameplay.
  • Explore, interact, and fight your way through the island to discover its history and purpose.
  • Dual-wield powerful weapons, both melee and projectile. Gun down your enemies with pistols, wreak havoc with a grenade launcher, or do both, at the same time. Choose any combination of weapons you like.
  • A multitude of enemies: deranged automatons, malfunctioning cyborgs, and science projects gone wrong.
  • Equip and mod your "Goggle Glass" to discover hints and secrets invisible to the naked eye.
  • Download information and read journals from SoulFire terminals and "Orbnals". Discover and read notes, books, and new crafting and modding recipes.
  • Mod your weapons into more powerful versions and discover new "Goggle Glass" features.
  • Collect and utilise power-up potions, or discover ingredients and craft them yourself using alchemy.

What we wish to bring you during development:


  • A huge underground facility to explore and new above-ground areas
  • More quests
  • More enemies
  • New weapons, including the fabled Gun Blade
  • More neutral/friendly NPCs with full voice-over and subtitles
  • A second playable character with a slightly different storyline
  • Fast travel system
  • More crafting and modding recipes
  • Lots more ingredients and parts
  • Wildlife you can hunt for alchemy ingredients
  • A battle arena, which can be also accessed from main menu after unlocked in game
  • A mini-game to unlock unique weapons and power ups
  • Multiple endings depending on player character and choices made during the game
  • Achievements
  • Full Oculus Rift compatibility (Please note this is not available in Alpha)

Early Access Review
Posted: 30 August, 2015
I got this in the Steampunk Groupees Bundle, and I'm glad that I didn't spend money specifically on this game.

The graphics, environments, and setting are great, but those are the only positive things about this game.

From what I've played, both the gameplay and story are complete rubbish.
There are several issues with clipping, particularly when it comes to stairs for some reason. For example, when you get to the mines there's a spiral staircase which I couldn't get down without crouching (which only seems to exist for getting down stairs) and then walking along the rail. The map doesn't really show exactly where you're meant to be heading, so there's a good chance of wandering around for several minutes until you find it. The countrols aren't really explained, so you'd have to check the controls in the options. There's a option to holster your weapons by 'holding' down the Reload button (R), which seems to serve no purpose other than activating around a third of the times you press R to reload and forcing you to run away in a fight whilst you press the button for the weapon you were using.
As for the story, nothing is really explained. The protaganist starts in an airship, is given a secret mission which would be better accomplished by a group rather than a single person, and, up to the part where I just gave up on the game, meets a poorly voiced android who tells him to collect items for them for no given reason. There's no actual explanation as to who the character is or why he's the one given this mission. The only other human I encountered in the game talked as if the protaganist was basically equivalent to a Private in the Inventor's Guild (another concept which isn't explained).

TL;DR version:
Don't buy it. Good concept and graphics, terrible gameplay and story.

Posted: 17 August, 2015
Ahh man, at the time of writing, mixed is all right a grade for the first 10 minutes of playing Avenging Angel.

After considerable deliberation I've decided to give it a thumbs-up cause the genre (FPS exploration/puzzle/adventure) is somewhat unique, premise is intriguing, graphics are so far in a word - lovely, par for the course for a small-team indie Unity effort and all the problems aren't grand and earth-shattering and are solvable before going gold.
But there is so much missing one would consider standard productional polish in the year of our lord 2015. Currently, one can rebind keyboard keys for instance, but solely from the Unity menu outside of the game and tutorial prompts don't reflect the change in-game. Animation is awkward and can be buggy. Jumps propell you backward if you're too close to the wall, like you're a ball. One time I was propelled some 7m in the air while climbing stairs for no apparent reason. And when you crouch, you inexplicably and slowly change your point of view downward. No, thank you, game, I'm perfectly capable of bringing the mouse down if I want to. I. just. Wanna. Crouch. But those are forgiveable things.

The greatest problem is the flavour text found in dialogues and journal. It's badly translated. Yeah, one can determine "what the poet wanted to say", but pacing of the text, syntax, idioms, metaphores - they all seem off. I mean, I'm not a native eng speaker, and I can kinda glimpse original thoughts - they're very similar to something someone from my country would make, if he weren't great at translation and would translate word-for-word. And I'm aware I've most probably butchered english grammar right now, writing this review and it is probably choke-full of spell errors, bad syntax sentences and strange foreighn idioms, but then again I'm exhausted after too little sleap last night/day and I'm fully aware I'm writing a User Steam review, not a text I'll put into a product which costs money. The text in game is not "All your bases" bad, but it's not well translated text either and young aspiring indie devs should maybe set higher standards. Plenty enough time to correct it, I just hope devs won't forget it. As it stands now, I wanted to dive into an intriguing steam-punk world, but the text kicked me right back into reality where I grade foreign language texts for a living (german, if you must know, don't have a degree in eng).
C+ Could improve with lots of practice!
...
Aww, schucks, changing it into B- cause of all the potential! :-)

That's it for my first 10 minutes review. If I have more constructive thoughts after playing some more, I'll update the review.
