About This Game

Cinders is a mature take on a classic fairytale with a heavy emphasis on player choices and role playing.

Cinders is a witty young woman living with an overbearing stepmother and her two daughters, as if she was reenacting a certain well-known fairytale. But unlike its protagonist, Cinders is not afraid of taking fate into her own hands. Even if it means breaking the rules…

The game takes a look at four women and what made them who they are. It’s a story about balancing freedom and dreams with circumstance and harsh reality. About growing up and finding out the true meaning of independence. Distancing itself from the judgmental simplicity of the original, Cinders tries to explore the more complex nature of oppression, responsibility and innocence.

With 120 decision points, over 300 options to choose from, and multiple endings, Cinders gives you total control over the main character’s personality and how her story unfolds.

Darling Firiniel
( 11.8 hrs on record )
Posted: 5 July
Cinders is a visual novel that is based on the fairy tale we all know, Cinderella, and I thoroughly enjoyed every moment of playing it. It's certainly no fabricated Disney setup though, as it has a more mature take on the story, some of which involve politics, psychological wounds, social discrimination, etc., but still retains the light-hearted feeling of fantasy from the original.

Pros:
  • The lovely, rustic art style, one of the first things to catch my attention: a right mix of dark and heavy, as well as stunning, colorful fairy tale scenery, which creates the perfect atmosphere for the story's theme
  • The music is a masterpiece, from the sound effects to the background music that plays on each scene: lovely to listen to, yet not too overwhelming to distract you from your immersion
  • Beautiful dialogue you wouldn't want to skip, with just enough suspense that can't make you stop reading
  • The subtle animations, from the way the flowers would sway to the wind, etc
  • A handful of amazing characters, well-written so you could actually sympathize with them, even the antagonists
  • The variety of choices given to you, and the many, multiple endings you could choose to unfold with them: high replay value because of this, you could be a charming and innocent Cinders in one playthrough, and a deviously cunning one the next
  • As with most VNs, involves romance options, but is written so well that it makes said options neither a necessity nor the main driving force of the story, but only as a 'side quest', and focuses more on heavier themes
  • More than enough endings: you are left with four main endings, as well as many different sub-endings under each one
  • Provides you with a hefty amount of hours to complete, or read from start to end
  • Has a feature wherein you can skip through parts you have already read, to explore the different outcomes
  • Easy to obtain achievements, got to 100% in no time

Cons:
  • Some of the dialogues are... For lack of a better term, awkward, and a few moments are too cringe-worthy cliché
  • Lack of, or rushed character development: pacing feels off somehow, and it gives you a feeling that everything is happening too fast
  • Typos in some dialogues

This is the first visual novel I have played, finished to the very end and tried to get all possible endings I can. For people like me who are not that fond of anime VNs, and have a particular interest in similar themes, this game is definitely worth trying out. Can't wait to try out other VNs from MoaCube~

Overall Rating:
8.5/10

A visually stunning, feminist visual novel that sometimes falls victim to the same cliches it tries to subvert.

Pros:

- Jaw-dropping art with animated sprites and backgrounds
- Grand, sweeping soundtrack
- Complex characters with logical motivations
- Dozens of "sub-ending" variations depending on character relationships
- Unique "personality system" that subtly alters Cinders' baseline attitude


Cons:

- Dialogue can get overly transparent and "preachy" at times
- Romance options feel somewhat shallow and rushed

Overall, Cinders is a fantastic visual novel that's perfect for someone looking for something a little different.
