Steam Greenlight

Ryzom warmly thanks the Steam community, whose votes allowed it to be greenlighted so quickly.

Subscription

A few years ago WinchGate, owner of Ryzom, chose to open the game to the world for Free, releasing its code and to be the only open source MMORPG, playable on Windows, Linux and Mac.

However, maintaining Ryzom does involve costs, such as compensation for the small team of employees and payment for servers. That is why, even though Ryzom is playable as Free to Play, subscriptions are vital for it to continue.

To thank the players chosing to subscribe, certain features of the game are unlocked and certain bonuses are offered to them; an opportunity to level their characters to level 250 instead of 125, doubling all experience points gained, and access to several other means of storage.

To help Ryzom, make the choice to subscribe!

About This Game

Enter a unique science fantasy MMORPG and dive into a unique organic living world !

Opensource

Ryzom is one of only a few commercial-grade MMORPGs that are fully open source: client, server, tools, and media. Being open source means that Ryzom draws on a vibrant community providing fixes and functionality above and beyond that of what the team can provide. It offers players a unique opportunity to become involved in the development of the game.

Sandbox

Far from being a frozen world, Ryzom is constantly evolving in regard to your actions. Will you spark another war? Will you found a lucrative commercial guild or aim for political power? What if your hunting talents could be decisive in the struggle against Kitins?

Pick your actions well, for each one of them may impact the world and its inhabitants!

Classless

Ryzom characters are unusual in that they are not bound to a class. That means you don't have to choose between being a warrior, a mage, a harvester or a crafter. You can improve your skills in all these classes with only one character; you do not need to focus on one path. For example, you can, at your leisure, either train your pike handling, put on your magic amplifiers to heal or cast offensive spells, pick up your pickaxe and harvest materials, or go into crafting a new armor or weapon.

You have absolute freedom!

Roleplay

Ryzom offers official interactive events (with live NPC's), regular and varied, that evolve according to your actions. Nothing is preordained!
Would you like to create your own event? The Animation Team is always at your disposal and will gladly support and assist in logistics.
In addition, many guilds are prepared to open their doors to help your character evolve!

For lovers of imaginary languages, the players and Animation Team have created unique languages. Roleplay immersion guaranteed!

Invasions

Picture yourself, in front of a city gate, looking at a far hill and sighting the scouts of a Kitin swarm. Now picture hundreds of Kitins following them, moving like a united and swarming army. Huge battles call for the cooperation of a great part of the community to counter these threatening invasions. And what if, one day, the Kitin were to win again, threatening hominity for the third time?

Consensual PVP

You can fight everywhere, but only when you wish to do so. Duels, outposts battles, faction of region fights... You are free to engage in them... or abstain!

Advanced Forage & Harvest

Ryzom features an advanced havesting and crafting system.

Harvesting - allows you to dig materials from the ground depending on season and weather and your skill. It's not so simple once predators get involved!

Crafting - Careful combination of materials depending on their characteristics and colors, allows production of unique weapons and armors. Thousands of recipes are possible.

Living mobs

A powerful A.I. system allows for a realistic wildlife behavior: carnivores attack herbivorous herds, realistic and dynamic animal motions make them lively, sometimes endearing, other times worrisome. There are also mass migrations on season changes and different behavior for different species. Take your time to sit and observe the life around you, it's an unforgettable view. But be careful of predators!

Dynamic environment

Ryzom is a 3D persistent world consisting of five lush and varied ecosystems. Landscape, wildlife behavior and ground material availability all change according to season, weather and time.

Discover the beauty of the Forest in the spring, do battle in the Jungle in a rainstorm under a summer sky streaked with lightning, lose yourself in the dunes of the Desert in the fall, or relax enjoying the reflections of a winter sun on the pure water of the Lakes.

Full character customization

Entirely customizable character appearance upon creation: shape your avatar the way you like it!

For the TL:DR crowd this MMO is well over 12 years old where the original game company went out of business, sold the game and the current caretakers still want you to pay a $100 year subscription.

I remember the first time I encountered Ryzom.
I was 18 years old, working part time in a local retail chain in the electronics department.
I remember when the original discs came out. Being the only one at the time fluent in DOS, I remember because we just had a customer come in wanting to know how much disk space the new Lotus for DOS took. You just had to type DIR in the command line to see how much room it took. I tell you this to give you a time frame for how old the game really is.

The graphics are from 2000, 16 years old, they are still playable but not going to impress anyone today. The game was in development for over 5 years before it was released.
Sound and what little music there is are both okay.
There is no NPC chat all the interaction is with text only.
The population is very low, and is all French. I do not have a problem with people speaking French but it does me no good if I can not find someone else who speaks what I do English.
I have the English tab open and for the past 2 weeks, over 40 hours or so the only chat or players I have met have spoken French. So much for me joining a group or doing team stuff.

Now back to my story, I remember picking up a trial copy of the game when it first came out, the 21 day free trial period. This was because the game only had the full subscription payment model when it first came out. At that time I played a bit until I got to one of the earliest magic quests, go destroy some jugs with your magic spell on the training ground. Then I encountered the famous 1 foot high fence of shame. Almost everyone is familiar with it. You can not jump in the game and had to walk around the back of the pit to destroy the jugs and complete the quest. Even today you still can not jump or dive while in water. I remember thinking, well that is kind of stupid, maybe the game is not finished yet. You can not even jump in the game, this is something they should have caught early on in Beta. Needless to say I did not pay the $100 a year sub back then either.

I like the open world sand box aspect of it. As others have mentioned it is similar to Morrowind and the Elder Scrolls.
I have most of that series and found them all on Ebay full game for under $10 each.
I like the crafting aspect of the game, that was my favorite part in the old game Masters of Magic and the old RPG game Evil Islands. Still have the original floppy disks of those games and play them today with the help of DOSBOX. If you like open world sand boxes I recommend those.

I am currently at level 24 in all four of the main skill categories, Magic, Harvest, Fight and Craft.
I am off the training island and on the mainland. I have even taken the time to swim to all the 4 main cities for my race, hoping that they might be different then the starting capital city for my race. Spoiler alert, they are not. I have my tiny bag full, even the armor on your body and the weapon you are carrying counts as being in your bag. Right now I play for 15 minutes a day only, that is long enough to kill a few monsters or harvest a bit. Then you have to run back to town and sell everything. I am currently at my max items for sale 128 so I am not able to even sell any more items.
As a free to play player I can buy a single mount, if I can afford it to increase my bag size.
Then I log off and have to wait a full 24 hours to get enough of my goods to cycle through and sell in order to free up enough space to go "play" again the next day.
So yes, I have seen and played through the tutorial and seen enough of the game world to write an honest review of the game this time.

The main problems I have with the game is it is very old, probably close to 20 years old including beta and early development time..
The original game company went out of business, sold the company.
They made the entire game open source, the code, the artwork the graphics the sound.
Basically it is an abandoned game.
What that boils down to is there will never be any more new content added to the game.
The current people you are paying your yearly sub too did no work on the project, they are just basically a group of old players that picked the game up on auction in bankruptcy court for a song and a dance. You will never be able to jump or dive in Ryzom.

If they ever do decide to unlock the entire game and make it free to pay or $10 for a buy to own pay model like many other games have gone then I will recommend the game because it is fun to play.
As it stands now you get the 21 day free trial aspect of the game where they took off the 21 day time limit or you pay $100 a year subscription to unlock the game. You can only play up to level 125 the subscribers can play up to level 250 so twice as much.
If you ever do buy a subscription that account can never go free to play, if you later on decide to quit and just play for free. Also the free to play player can only buy a single mount to increase storage and bag size the subscribers can buy a mount, 3 packers, buy an apartment and do guild stuff. The subscribers earn twice as much experience as a free to play player and some other things I forgot all of them.

That is my two main points here, first the $100 a year sub for a 16 year old unfinished MMO.
Second the game is not truly "free to play" as the wording on the store page suggests, the bag size, apartment, mounts and half the levels are locked out.

A LITTLE OF WHAT RYZOM HAS TO OFFER
------------------------------

Ryzom does things differently to most mmorpgs, there is no *classes* you can learn all skills with multiple off branches, in total there is about 63 skill tree lines you can level each one to 250 if you sub, otherwise 125 without sub. that is alot of levels and months or years of gameplay even for a f2p player.

-----
COMBAT
-----

Ryzom has unique indepth style of actions / abilities you can actually edit and modify the way you want them with different effects, to combine into your own attacks or spells. its not your basic *press slash to slash a mob* you can input aimed shots to areas such as legs or head that add debuffs aswell as increase accuracy or damage of skills consuming more resources, further from this you have a few options on what your abilities cost to use them. for example, instead of stamina it could require some HP instead, or you can add special requirements like *skill is available after a parry or dodge. this all depends on how YOU build your skills. some mobs can also disarm you in combat and cause effects, aswell as certain mobs being more resistant to different types of damage.


---------
HARVESTING / CRAFTING
------

In my opinion Ryzom has the best harvesting and crafting system from any mmorpg similar to the attack abilities you can customise what type of resource you want to look for, the quality etc, and when you actually find resources to harvest then starts somewhat a mini game, where you have different abilities that determine the quality and amount of resource you gain using *fast, or strong or healing actions* there is chance for gas explosions that will damage you while harvesting aswell as surrounding mobs so it's not an easy mode walk in the park, you may end up with Q9 q 20 q11 mats if you arn't paying much attention. certain materials have different stat factors aswell, for example materials for ammo have different damage or weight to carry more, speed, etc all at once. Mobs also drop materials for crafting

same goes with crafting, they can sometimes *fail* somewhat so you end up with a quality lower than you wanted. you can customize your crafting to add stats like increased parry or increased HP etc and specalize in these, you need to learn each equipment seperatly you don't go *learn armorsmith... have access to all armor* you will need to learn to craft shoulder, chest, then quality, and ontop of this you can learn to craft other races armors and wear them.


--------
Other Bits
---------

Ryzom has seasons changes, weather and day / night. which can determine which mobs are available in some area's most other mmorpgs don't have this to the same extent.

Ryzom has different factions and reputation you can align to.


Despite what I keep reading, Ryzom does have missions / quests but its not linear story guided holding your hand like most mmorpgs and there is not 100,000 of them. if you don't want to do them you don't have to and they are mainly for fun / get some coin etc (beyond the starter zone which has tutorial quests to help you out)

there is death penalty for dying, which can stack up to 10x ( need to regain xp before you can xp towards skills) so dying does has its downfalls unlike most games.

As for graphics sure it is not the most up to date, but it is better then alot of eastern grind clones, and if you use gfx injectors / software like SweetFX to tweak it to hdr/enhance it yourself. personally i don't care about graphics on games, it is a silly thing to judge by, its all about game mechanics.

There is housing, guilds, achievements, occuptions seperate from crafting for moneys etc,

Ryzom is truly one of a kind, from the amazing community and open game play to the beautiful world and critters that run around on it.
I started playing when Ryzom was released on the Apple store about five years ago, it sucked me in in ways no other game has before.
You can really dive in and feel like you are a true part of the game, your ideas can shape and create new features, factions and lore. The possibilities are endless!

Ryzom does have a learning curve, it can be complicated for new players - but don't give up. Soon everything will start making sense and you will be a master in no time...if you do run into trouble use the "universe" chat and one of the amazing homins or CSRs will help you find your way.

Tips for new players:

1) Research the factions - especially before joining a guild.
Your faction (Kami, Karavan, Ranger, Marauder or neutral) will have a huge impact on your game play. It is very important to decide how you want to play and who you want to be on Atys. There are many players who will help you with weapons and gear without the requirement of you joining their guild. If you feel pressured or rushed, something is not right!

2)The forums are a huge source of information. You will find the lore, guild registries, reports from regional/factional meetings, ideas for Ryzom, occupation help and recipes. They can be a very crucial element for learning your way around (but they can be easily forgotten).

3) Don't be afraid to ask for help. As I mentioned before Ryzom has a truly unique community full of amazing helpful homins. We just want you to enjoy Atys and will offer help whenever we can.

Congratulations Ryzom for making it to Steam! I can't wait to meet some of you new players!

If you have enough from your standard MMORPGs with Elves and Dwarves and Dragons and whatnot then you might want to give Ryzom a chance!

Atys, the world the game is set in is quite unique - the planet is basically one huge, living plant, inhabited by a wide variety of creatures. The characters aren't bound to classes, you can learn whatever you want, as long as you spend the time to level it up. The crafting system is quite complex - the economy is 100% player based, whatever you use will be made by other players or yourself, except for some basic starter equippment.

The ability-system is also noteworthy. You get to build your own attacks and spells too in a way: They are made out of elements you can swap and adapt everything to your playing style. For example you can raise the time it takes to cast a spell and in exchange make it cause more damage.

The community is by far the best I've come across in any MMORPG. The playerbase is mostly mature, friendly and ready to help newcomers. The way the economy runs teamwork is a must if you want to get your hands on really good equippment. Some of the places you get your resources from are pretty dangerous and should not be travelled alone...

That being said - yes, there is PvP in certain areas and guilds can fight for outposts, but it's your choice to join or not.

What else... NO CASH SHOP!

The only thing you *can* buy is a monthly subscription. The game is free to play up to lvl 125 - more than enough to see whether you like it or not - and with the subscription the lvl cap is 250. Also subscribers can rent an apartment in one of the cities PLUS they can own pack-animals - the only way to get more storage ingame.

So - go ahead, give it a try!
