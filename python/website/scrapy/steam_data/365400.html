About This Game

Drop in to the world of two friendly beetles: relaxed Spoko and angry friend Poko. They need your help to transport a ball to their cottage. Are you ready to help them? Remember to collect all diamonds on the way!
In this arcade game, you can surf in search of diamonds


By manipulating the angry Poko beetle, bounce the ball using the cart
You will encounter many obstacles on the way; we make you familiar with selected ones:
Initial levels consist mainly of racing in search of diamonds and gold
Then you are moved into the world of the Galactic defenders in the good old arcade style
Levels above 15 consist mainly of pinball
Levels above 20 consist of cosmic black holes with eyes of aliens
Levels above 25 consist of arkanoid with our heroes in the lead
On the levels above 27, you will have to fight against angry birds, which will try to take the ball away from our beetles, avoid them! Pink birds will give you diamonds and only those should be smashed
On the levels above 30, you will find UFO flying with aliens
On the levels above 34, you will lead a battle against the alien force
On the levels above 38, you will find a mine, whose tracks lead to a golden temple
In the gold rush, everything is possible, so watch out for jumping carts
On the levels above 40, there are colored candies, which replicate upon touching the ball making the game more difficult
Collect all the candies to get a bonus
Help the beetles to find a road home, which is located at their farm
Moreover, we can hear funny talking beetles in the game.

Posted: 9 June, 2015
The game looks interesting from the screenshots, but once you start it falls apart. Usually I'm not a big fan of tutorials, but some games need one and this game surely is one of them.

There's no intro or anything else when you start the game. You are simply thrown in the first level without any explanation of the controls or the objective of the game. I could gather that it's a breakout 'mutant' in which you have to shoot coins and gems and then place the ball into a mining cart. You do that by controlling a handcar which has an adjustable 'bat'. The controls are quite unintuitive to say the least.

The visual presentation is nice, yet not very varied. The music doesn't fit and got on my nerves pretty quick.

It's not a horrible game, but it needs a howto or a tutorial. Also the controls could use some work. As it is right now I won't recommend the game.

Your supposed to collect diamonds for something I had no idea what for. There is a shopping cart icon for something I have no idea what for.

The game feels like a very lazy port of a mobile game. Unclear instructions. I still have no idea what the shopping cart icon does or what its even for. I even click on the cart and got me into another game with big boxes labeled on either side. One labeled flaps and the other with no cart. ? Not sure what that even is supposed to mean.

So no clear goals in game. Low resolution options and lack luster game play. The positives the colors are bright and the 2 insects look good. My score would be a 5/10 as the game stands now.

Posted: 20 May, 2015
It could have been decent if there weren't so many negative aspects.

Pro:
- Number of levels and variety
- Concept is kind of cute (if 2 beetles transporting a ball of s*** can be cute)

Cons:
- Clearly a mobile port (it's on Google Play) so controls are very limited (move with the mouse, click to boost, W/D to change the rebound angle) and impossible to rebind (and you'll have to find out for yourself what they are)
- No key to pause, restart, quit, you'll have to go click the pause button which means the cart will move and you'll lose so there's no point in pausing.
- Tutorial lacking in information: bumpers? hat tricks? greenlights? Are those just ways to score a bit more?
- Broken leader boards (my first score on lvl1 worked, but everything else never updated, even new high scores in the first lvl)
- Broken achievements? I'm pretty sure I did some of them (bullettime, firetime) since they appear around lvl10
- Limited amount of rebound: It's entirely part of the gameplay but in some levels and thanks to unresponsive controls/weird physics it's just frustrating and uselessly punishing.
- Muting the music doesn't mute other sounds

It's really hard to recommend at this point, it would need a more polished version to be worth playing. Wait for updates.

Just come out of an hour long session of this game and I feel like I'm ready give it a fair rating.

The art direction for this game is amazing! The characters are lovable and the music, sound and the settings for all of the levels are very fitting!

All this adds to the overall originality of the game. Where most games can get quite dull quite quickly, this games keeps you engaged by throwing in new mechanics and puzzle-like elements every now and then.

All-in-all, this is great game for its genre! 

If there’s anything I'd improve it would be throwing in more meaningful graphic choices and more resolution options!

It was broken on release, but it has been fixed by the developer (talking about admisitrative privileges). But beyond that, my biggest problem here is the controls. This game was designed for a mobile platform in terrms of the type controls that it's going for, you don't get the precision that you'd want (at least I'd want) regarding the mouse. Sadly, that leads to a bunch of problems, and while variety eventually develops, the gameplay doesn't excite me as much as I would hope.

+:
-It now runs
-Different premise, interesting to say the least.
-There's variety down the line.

-:
-Controls are too imprecise for my liking, and leads to some problems in terms of the technical precision needed to do some puzzles. Repeating the same parts over again can get irritating.
-Music will wear on you, and also got a DMCA on one part one when attempting to make a video (just fyi for YTers out there).
-Gameplay lacks oompf.
-Early on, combination of imprecise controls and gameplay leads to problems.
