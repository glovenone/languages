About This Game

Your world...Your rules! In Just Cause, you are a Latin field operative and specialist in regime change backed by top secret US government agency who will overthrow the corrupt government of San Esperito. The rogue South American state is suspected of stockpiling Weapons of Mass Destruction, and it's your mission to negate the threat this poses to world peace. It could be to your advantage that the tropical paradise is about to implode as various factions vie for power - it just needs a gentle nudge in the right direction.
Just Cause offers the freedom to tackle your assignments however you want: playing the island's factions against one another, inciting a rebellion among the masses and building alliances with rebel forces and drug cartels. The action takes place in an incredibly detailed game world which consists of over 250,000 acres of mountains, jungles, beaches, cities and villages. The island can be explored by land, sea and air, as you will have at your disposal one of the most varied and exciting array of vehicles ever seen in a video game.
  • Freeform game-play: approach missions in any way you like, or break off from the fight and enjoy exploring the islands.
  • Over 250K acres of land to explore: the largest environment ever seen in a game!
  • Over-the-top Stunts: jump from vehicle-to-vehicle, parasail, skydive and base-jump...
  • Massive selection of vehicles: over 100 of the most varied, exciting array of land, sea and air vehicles ever seen in a video game.
  • Avalanche Engine™: procedurally generated landscape delivers a huge, beautiful, detailed world while eliminating loading times.
  • Wealth of missions: story missions, side-missions, bonus missions and many more; ensuring plenty of action spread throughout the islands.
  • Support team: Sheldon and Kane, two US government secret agents, will provide you with reconnaissance info, extraction and vehicle drops.
  • Fly: The best way to explore San Esperito is by air!

Posted: 7 July
It's like Grand Theft Auto but on an Island, with a grappling gun, and can eject yourself from moving vehicles and skydive and use a parachute. There's also a lot of trees.

Enemies appear out of nowhere and disappear in front of your eyes on occasions.
You can steal a car and have the whole islands army on your ♥♥♥ with no stars to indicate when the assault on you will ever end. (It won't). They will gun you down to the end of the earth or until you reach your objective and then it's mission complete.
Cars stick to you when they are chasing and ramming you, can be a pain to shake off and driving uphill is a struggle.

But then, when you can climb out of your vehicle and jump in to another. Who cares about the bad things.
I drive my pickup and then climb out on to the roof and jump in to a sports car alongside me. Then I race to the edge of a cliff and surf on roof, leaping from it just as it goes over the edge. Free falling for a moment and then releasing my parachute, I float down to the sea, hop in a boat and speed off to another island, and on my way, spot a plane that takes my fancy.

Very short game if you only do the main missions, which is what I did. There are side missions which are like Assassins Creed or Far Cry where you take over the area by destroying blockades with your team of rebels and then eventually once enough damage has been made change the flag in the town centre. I didn't really care for these as they are very repetitive.

A nice feature which helped to navigate the map easier and a lot quicker was the extraction option that is in the PDA menu, which can be used at any time (even whilst on a mission) as long as you are not under attack. Where a helicopter will pick you up and take you where ever you want to go, be it to a new mission briefing or just to one of your safe houses. You can also have vehicles dropped off to you using heavy drop from the PDA.

Kind of hard to say if I should recommend this or not.
It has some very good positives and being an open world game has the ability to bring a lot of hours of game time just roaming the island and getting up to mischief. However there isn't a massive amount of vehicles and all of the terrain is pretty much the same (lots of dirt roads, few tarmac ones, trees. mountains) and not much going on the street in general unless the army are after you (which is kind of all the time)

The game is a bit of a bad port from (what I've read) the XBOX (not 360)/PS2 version of the game. When in the menu there is no mouse cursor to select options and navigating must be done from the directional buttons on keyboard and return and No controller support (although I didn't try).
The games display aspect ratio is 4:3 at 1024x768 with no support for widescreen monitors by default.

(To allow the use of 1920x1080 16:9 you need can get a universal resolution changer file from WideScreen Gaming Forum which is easy enough to add to the games install directory in order to change this - http://www.wsgf.org/dr/just-cause) Although this does stretch the HUD, PDA and map screens noticeably and the cut scenes more so.
I played at 5760x1080 res using this fix and in game the resolution was fine.

If you see this game on sale grab it. I got Just Cause and Just Cause 2 with DLC for £3.24 in the sales. For that price I would recommend this game all day. Even if it has got a few flaws, its still a fine game with around 21 missions, lots more side missions and being an open world game is more what you make of it when not doing these.

69/100

This game is a buggy, unpolished, and sometimes downright boring abomination. And I love it for that.

You'll get to experience a laughably primitive version of the Havok physics engine. Hell, it can't even handle a tank's turret turning without sending the tank spinning into the upper stratosphere, and other vehicles have a tendency to go flying for no apparent reason. (Looking at you, monster truck)

Enemies spawn in at complete random, more often than not have I been cut off during a car chase because an enemy car materialised right in front of me. I don't know why this is a thing, but hey, more mooks to kill, amirite?

Rocket launchers aren't unlocked early, I got the first one after almost 7 hours of playtime. The ammo count is pathetic and sometimes they refuse to lock onto enemies. This is a game where tanks are scary, beyond scary, actually. Because they deal a lot of damage, soak a lot of damage, and sometimes they enter hyperspace and chop your head of with a turbocharged swivel of their turret. Expect to engage in vehicular combat more than infantry vs tank combat, because your AT options will be limited at best. On the other hands, rocket-armed vehicles can decimate anything in sight in style.

The story is as deranged as it gets, nothing makes any sense and I've yet to figure out what the hell is going on. On the other hand, it provides some of the silliest and most enjoyable cutscenes I've ever seen in a videogame. Expect PS1-tier facial animations. (only in cutscenes, there are no facial animations in-game)

However, this game still succeeds with something, and that's providing those unscripted action-movie moments. Just as an example, here are two highlights from some of my boat shenanigans: http://plays.tv/s/Kr8ZOcCJLREW

Yes, it is primitive. Yes, it is buggy. Yes, it is unpolished. Yes, it is repetitive. However, if you're willing to try different things, this game allows you to create more fun for yourself. There are different ways to attack bases, you don't have to bring an attack helicopter every time, and it can be fun to get involved in a massive car chase with helis and explosions just for the hell of it.

Do you want to blow things up and can come up with many ways to do it? Can you deal with wonky physics and dated gameplay? If so, this game might be something for you. I'd not pick it up outside of a sale though.

What once was a very impressive little title has now turned into one of the biggest franchises for Square Enix and a Cult Favorite among the fans, so let's see how it all began shall we ?

In Just Cause we play as Rico Rodriguez. He is an agent sent in to the island San Esperito. Our mission is to kill a dictator Mendoza and free the island from his reign.

Back when this game came out I found it quite impressive. The Island is massive, but it doesn't matter cause there is nothing to see there. All of it is just a big jungle which all of it looks the same. There are small villages which act as outposts and you can clear them out. The city is uninspiring and there's nothing to do there.

The gameplay is by far the weakest third person shooter I have ever seen. The shooting feels clunky and sometimes my shots don't even connect with the enemies, not to mention most guns are weak as hell, and the better weapons have low ammo counts.

The graphics (apart from the CGI cut scenes) are still great. The lush island is awesome and getting a boat and going through the rivers is really fun, but as stated before there's nothing to see, but it's still fun.

The driving controls are some of the worst driving controls I have ever seen, they even top the driving controls in Watch Dogs. Since unlike in Just Cause 2 you can't travel with the grappling hook, so most of the time you will need to use vehicles.

The game also has side quests, but most of them are boring as hell and you can grind them since they never end. So if you want something more with the game after you're done you can do the mundane side quests.

The game took me 6 hours to finish, once the game is finished you can explore the island, but as stated before there's nothing to do there.

The game also has a major spike in difficulty in the final missions, so be prepared to be ♥♥♥♥♥♥ off.

Overall even though the game didn't age well, it's still fun to play for a bit, but it's the weakest in the series. In the end I will give this a positive review, but I still suggest playing Just Cause 2 instead.

Final Rating:
6/10 - Okay

Before you buy, consider..

- Limited resolution support

Game displays optional resolutions as low as 640x480 to 1600x1200, but only 1280x1024 is the max, selectable resolution for the game. And editing config files won't do crap for you.

To get your game to run in your monitor's native resolution, you're gonna need one of two versions of this program called res changer..

http://steamcommunity.com/app/6880/discussions/0/846959520923192823/

http://steamcommunity.com/app/6880/discussions/0/594821545181783362/

Version 1 or 2. Try 2 because it's newer. If it doesn't work, 1 might. 2 didn't work for me, so I had to use 1. Only drawback to having to use 1 is that it has no support for windowed mode like 2. And that alt-tabbing with 1 crashes your game. But, hey, it works. And I almost refunded my game. Looked awful downscaled. Game may be old, but still should have had more resolution support.

- Very few graphics settings.

You're gonna need to force AA, AF, etc on from your video driver control panel. Everything in menu only affects quality of textures, shadows, and post-processing FX.

Overall, the game is a good game. I have less than an hour on it over Steam, but I speak from experience from having played it on console years ago.
