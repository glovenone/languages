About This Game

An otome (Girl pursues Boy) dating simulation game, Lucky Rabbit Reflex! sees our seventeen-year old heroine beginning her first and final year at a new school. Will she find friendship - or even romance - amongst the motley crew at Northmain College? (Or a decent fashion sense, at the very least!)

Game Features:
  • 19 endings
  • 5+ romanceable gentlemen
  • 75 unique CGs
  • 150+ unique items of clothing and accessories
  • CG / endings gallery
  • 178,861 words

Recommended if you enjoy a fairly straightforward otome visual novel / stat raising game. Detailed enough to get to know the various characters and doesn't feel too short. Not a lot of surprises, but I thought it was good - I don't always need or want harrowing and dark, and these characters actually seemed to act more like normal people (of 17-19) than in many games.

Pros:

+ Enough unique features/plots in the different storylines to be interesting as you try to complete the different endings
+ Likeable characters
+ Generally well-written (and the dialogue was more realistic than usual)
+ UK setting (I really enjoyed the setting and the slang - so many games are set either in Tokyo or the US)
+ The endings (Best, Good and "Dual") were quite well done; I thought the "dual" endings were a neat touch
+ The intro to the game pokes fun at itself...

Cons:

- The artwork takes getting used to; many people have criticized the strange chins and it *is* a distraction (but I found many of the CGs were fine)
- Parts of the game are pretty repetitive
- Not sure why you can "fail" when attempting to improve a skill, since you can just save and reload

Overall: Glad I bought and played it, sorry I'm just about done.

I remember buying this game on the developer's site and as soon as it arrived on Steam, I was excited to play it again. Although there weren't any additions or changes made (other than a bug that was fixed from the direct only version), Lucky Rabbit Reflex! (LRR) is as great a game as I remember. ^^

LRR is basically a visual novel with dating sim elements. You play as the new female student starting her final year of college (in the US, it would be the final year of high school) in a new school. There are a total of 6 guys that you can date although only 4 of these guys you'll find in the school. The other two you'll have to search elsewhere. Also, there are a total of 5 girls that you can become BFFs with. The dating sim elements include, of course, asking each of the guys on a date on Saturdays or asking one of the girls to hang out with them as well as stat grinding. There are 4 different stats you can grind but you must make sure that the stress level doesn't get too high or else the MC will be more likely to fail at raising certain stats.

Although the art itself did put me off from buying this game for a while, the story and gameplay more than made up for it. I liked that you can achieve multiple endings with most of the guys. Four of the guys have three different endings-a best end, a good end, and a dual end. The dual end happens if you're trying to romance said character and another certain guy at the same time. I haven't seen this possibility in many VNs/dating sims. Also, one of the guys is a secret character so you have to figure out who it is and how to romance him although the developer's site does have a walkthrough of this game if you're trying to achieve a certain ending or pursue a possible character. So basically, what really hooked me to this game was the multiple playthroughs you can have although I liked the story that I read for each dateable male character. Each one of them had me hooked to his story although I'll admit that I liked certain ones more than others.

Overall, I'd highly recommend buying LRR if you're a fan of VNs, dating sims, etc. A lot of play time can be had from this game so it's well worth the money. ^^

This game has been around for a few years, but has made its Steam debut just recently. I picked it up somewhere else last year and got a Steam key when it crossed over. So I have a bit more time on this than is in my stats.

This is a pretty straightforward otome game with different routes, datable guys, friend-datable girls, day-to-day decisions and managing different attributes - it's also self-aware about the romatic-confession-at-the-end-of-school-term trope. In terms of sex, it's very tame. There's language to indicate these kids know what's up, but you're not getting much more than cleavage and abs. Sowwy. ;-)

Your protagonist (name and birthday of your choice) is a British girl going through her last year of UK high school at a new school, so she has to start from scratch making new friends. She does this in pretty short order with the prototype outgoing quasi-â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ girl and the prototype shy-ish high-achieving girl. There are also a couple other girls that come along in time for friendship possibilities with pretty distinct personalities and stories (some of which don't become fleshed out until you get through a couple routes). As for the datable boys, you've got the prototype flirt, the sensitive musician, the jerk brain/drama guy, the younger athlete from a nearby school and a young art teacher. While these guys fit common otome roles, they have enough twists to them to keep them somewhat interesting (said flirt is also a jock, the art teacher is also a model and tattoo artist). You go through the year day-by-day choosing different activities which get you points for different attributes which help your prospects with the guys. As you go on different routes, you can date on the weekends (which is also when you can buy clothes that certain people may like or not and also go places on your own), though if you've done well enough with a guy or girl, they may call you to go out while you're on school breaks. Along with all this, some routes have slight mini-games to test your memory (all routes have the one where you, that's right, are making pancakes).

There are a good number of routes, and a few of them can't be tried until almost halfway through, which makes them a bit challenging. The routes aren't as simple as racking up the points - certain decisions have to be made as well. While there are some repetitive aspects, I appreciate the variety of routes and what's needed to complete them.

As has already been discussed in some places here, the weakness on this game is visual. The character design is meant to be a riff on anime, though the shadows and body shapes will look really wonky to some. I think the look is pretty much the same as it has been through the years, with just a few tweeks here and there. Also the text is a straight and small Arial that people could actually have a hard time reading. But as you may have figured if you've gotten this far (thanks!), I don't care. I like these characters, I like the sense of humour they have and I like the variety of things they get to do. There are a couple other things (jobs! taking exams!) that I haven't covered through all this. But hopefully you get the idea now that if you're a fan of otome games, you can have a lot of fun with this one.
