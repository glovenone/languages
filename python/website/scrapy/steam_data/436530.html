Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The main reason is because early access allow us to adapt to players' tastes, this way we'll be able to listen and adapt Bullshot so that once of the full release arrives, it's as good as possible on the basis of all received opinions and feedback.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Around 2 or 3 months, first of all we’re going to wait between 1 and 2 weeks to receive all your opinions, we’d like the game to be as collaborative as possible so any kind of opinion to improve gameplay, ideas for locations, enemies, weapons o even for the soundtrack will be welcome. Once we have all the information we’ll go through with the full game.

Update: We expect release this game by late June / early July.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Currently this early access version features 6 levels and 3 sub-levels, the idea is that the full version of this game features between 8 and 10 levels with several additional sub-levels. Besides the locations, the full game would include a much bigger arsenal of weapons, and the possibility of including new game modes like cooperative, new characters, etc. all of them would be considered.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“You have 6 levels and 3 sub levels or bonus stages completed for the early access version, all those stages are 100% playable. The game it's localized to +25 different languages.

Some of those 6 stages are completely different scenarios, like "The Prison", "Sewers", "Desert" or the "Laboratory" so we consider that those are a perfect base to receive feedback about how to improve this game with new content.

Current levels have:

- 31 Steam Achievements
- 14 completely different types of enemies
- 18 secret collectibles
- 8 "Bulls of Anarchy" to rescue.
- 5 custom aims for Franky's weapon
- 5 screen presets: CRT, VHS, HD, Black and white and Broken TV.
- 4 weapons (more to come)
- 2 vehicles.
- 2 giant final bosses.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Once finished, the game will start to cost a substantially higher price, expect around 5-7$, however, those players who get the early access version will be able to enjoy the full game for free, without spending a single cent on it!”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The more opinions we receive, the better. In addition, we invite you to upload screen captures with your higher scores, every month Bullshot players who get the highest scores will be rewarded with Steam Keys for other Gato Salvaje Studio games.”

Hey! It’s been a while since the last update! Here we come with the 8th one; we are happy to introduce you a bunch of news, some of them very important!

About the update:

MULTIPLAYER BETA (versus mode) - ACTIVATED
From today an so on, you’ll have 4 scenarios to play Against any of your friends. This update allows you to play “VS mode”, but expect to see a cooperative mode very soon. In each scenario you’ll have different weapons and health packs.


The final version is expected to include up to 8 characters to choose with different “default weapons” and different skills set (speed, strength, special attack…)

About This Game

BULLSHOT, run & gun action shooting game for diehard fans and newcomers!

Twenty light years from AR-K space station is planet planet Valer, inhabitated by the peaceful valerians. After several years of war, the invaders, the evil cardesians rule with an iron fist, depriving the valerians from their freedom, apple pies and the valerian life style. Crooked cardesians have been trying to annihilate any spark of resistance.

However, Frank F. Franky, president of Bulls of Anarchy, it's hiding his secret identity in order to destroy them from the inside, setting his rebel arrested mates and the whole valerian civilization free. A fax has shown his next victim, they didn't give him another option... they've messed with the wrong bull.

Key Features:
  • 7 Levels
  • 3 Sub-levels
  • 1 Bad-ass main character!
  • Endless ammo!!
  • 200% FUN!!!

Early Access Review
Posted: 22 May
A very solid side scroller which reminds me greatly of Contra and the original DukeNukem. The graphical options are cute and gameplay is fairly typical of traidtional side scrollers--with mouse aiming. Play is fun, though there is a limitation in the raw number of levels-but then it is still an early access release.

My greatest "complaint" is the jump mechanic. I find the game is vertically unforgiving, with some areas being missed if one drops too low too quickly.

The collectable mechanic is typical of many side-scrollers, though I have found some "wall clipping" issues (the sewer level for one turtle for example) which have forced a restart of the level--though the collectable was still counted.

Mid-level save flags, reminicient of Mario, are helpful, however, all stockpiled weapons are typically deleted--which can make for some difficulties if the level is new to the player.

Weapon options are still thin, with 4 primary weapons available (Unlimited ammo base machine gun, flamer, rocket launcher, grenade launcher). Ammunition is very limited for the flamer and rocket launcher (the weapons I've also found have the least utility unless opening a wall etc.) The nade launcher was useful for blowing open some floors and vs. a particular boss but otherwise I've found the best utility from the starter machine gun. Some additional development to make the lesser used weapons more viable is hopefully on the horizon.

Levels are generally well designed and fun--if you enjoy the side scrolling format. The "hide in the shadows" element can be useful and the bull charge is fun.

Boss mechanics are generally well done, with identifiable patterns if one is paying attention.

Overall, this is an enjoyable "time-waster" with a limited backstory and a fair bit of humour with references to other games and shows/movies.

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 April
I just bought this one because it was so cheap. I have to admit I didn't expect too much, but it kept me playing for a few hours. My opinion, after playing it, is that Bullshot is a real bargain.

The pros:
  • A lot of references and tributes to old arcade games and movies (You break a car like in 'Street Fighter', ride a bike like in 'Sly Spy', and in the trailer I saw you'll have to kill a heart like in 'Contra'. This can't be just coincidence. ).
  • Franky charging at the enemies. (Best melee attack ever!! XD )
  • Good gameplay.
  • Trading cards are awesome!! XD
  • Music (I really liked this one!!).
  • Less than 1$ (The price raised recently, it's still a cheap one, anyway).
  • Great developer support and the updates keep a good pace.


The cons:
  • It looks like it's a little short at this moment. Maybe it's not a real con, as developers say it will have 8 or 10 levels (it has 3 plus 3 bonus right now). (The game is frequently updated and developers are adding levels almost every week. It has 6 plus 3 bonus now).
  • Took a little to find out how to play (there's a help image showing the controls but it's "hidden" in the options).
  • Difficulty is maybe too high at some points. I'm also not sure if it's really bad, as it makes Bullshot look more like the old arcade games.
  • The 'retro TV filter'. It looks funny for a while, but fortunately you can turn it off (and the game looks better without it).
