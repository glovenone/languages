Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We see early access as a good way to test the game on a broader audience before we release.
It's important for us to know that there aren't any particular levels in which a large amount of players ragequits.
We join early access to spot these harder parts, and either move them to later in the game, make them easier, add additional levels before the hard ones, or remove them.

If there is any visuals in the game that distrupts the players experience we would like to fix them before release aswell.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Hopefully no more than 2 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The final version will have a smoother difficulty ramp. and may have some minor visual changes. The game overall will be the same.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game could be considered finished, we just want the final polish to be based on user input.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We will price the game depending on it's reception, and it's likely that the price will rise after release.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Almost all our work after we release the early access version will be based on the communitys opinions.”

16 June

Gamepad and custom controls added!

Hi all, sorry it's been a while since the last update.

In this Update:

-Play the game with a gamepad, the game is designed for keyboard and mouse however, some of the later levels can be too difficult with a gamepad.

-Customize all the controls in the new "Controls" window in the game menu. The tutorial controls will also follow the settings you define.

-"Next Lock" locks the aim to the next lockable object in view, sorted by distance from the camera. Primarily for players without a mouse, since aiming with a trackpad is uncomfortable. But also fits well with a gamepad, define it to a button in the controls menu.

-"Previous Lock" locks the aim last locked object if it is in view, press one more time to unlock. Useful in the later levels for players without a mouse or with a gamepad. You can also press "Previous Lock" and "Next Lock" at the same time to unlock the aim.

-"Download progress from Steam" button is now only visable when you can download it.

There are currently no tutorials for "Next Lock" and "Previous Lock" in the game.
If you have any issues,discover any bugs or have some suggestions then please let us know.

For the next update we will work on the presentation to make the game feel more finished. Such as sound & music, graphics on some levels, menu graphics, and whatever else think we can improve. Have a nice day :)

About This Game

In Veer, you navigate the world through solving logical puzzles. The core mechanic used to do this is relative teleporting: Jumping between relative positions to pylons in the levels. This, in combination with the ability to change the direction of gravity, are the corner pieces of the game, where everything revolves around and is created for the core experience of puzzle solving.

Throughout the game, new ways of using the mechanics are introduced to create a more complex and challenging experience. Turn your world around, teleport yourself past obstacles, and find your way through the temple!

Early Access Review
Posted: 14 May
Product received for free
Veer is a promising game for sure. As a puzzle game, navigating rooms with a teleporting arrow/beam is pretty unique, relying on position and perspective to navigate small to medium sized environments. There's a gradual difficulty curve, which I personally appreciate; it gives you simpler puzzles to introduce new mechanics or obstacles, then ramps up the difficulty. There also seem to be multiple ways to solve the puzzles, which makes the time trials and leaderboards more meaningful.

The aesthetic is minimalistic, a lot of flat colors and an overall earthy tone, not strikingly beautiful like the Witness or Journey but fairly unique and pleasant to look at. The music is a bit sparse, it sounds okay the first 30 minutes you hear it but you'll most likely just mute it and listen to your own music or a podcast. That said, there's little to no story, and sound effects aren't important to puzzle solving so this might be right up your alley if you need something to keep your hands busy.

Gameplay is simple and the controls are good enough; move with WASD and aim with the mouse, click to fire the arrow into an orb which teleports you to the same position relative to the companion orb. So if you're standing 10 feet to the left of one orb, fire at it, you'll be ten feet to the left of the companion orb. The early levels are static, letting you grasp the mechanics of navigating the stage via relative positioning. Later levels introduce ramps that rotate the stage and moving platforms which add timing to the mix. There's also a simple but genius difficulty toggle in the game, a guide that shows you where your shot will place you. Seeing this makes the game significantly more straight-forward, but you can remove it for added challenge. You can change this option on the fly with a button press, which I think is a perfect balance of accessibility and challenge.

Overall I had a positive experience with the game: very easy to pick up and play, it became challenging over time without getting frustrating or punishing, and there's a decent amount of variety in gameplay mechanics. With a little more variety in music and set dressing, maybe an extra challenge mode or user generated content this could be a solid game with a lot of replayability.

Not a bad relativity teleportation physics based puzzle-platformer game about a magician girl armed with a light beam traversing abstract architectural structures (temples?) where you'll find some quantum globes instead of furniture. Those shiny globes go in paires - after you hit each of them with a beam it momentarily moves the character in an opposite direction (if one moves up, then another one shifts you down). There's also that gravity rotation mechanics like in 'The Bridge' and 'InBetween', so you can further experiment with the directions, trying to get to an exit. It's all nice and quality made but rather shallow and bland, I would like to see more stuff like story, characters, variations in style to get more interested.
