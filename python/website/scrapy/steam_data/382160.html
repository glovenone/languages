About This Game

Inspired by true events, Dark Years is set in 1953 Tehran, Iran.

Tehran is in the grip of fear following a series of grisly murders. Major Afshar has been tasked with finding those responsible. Meanwhile, some 3,300 miles away in London, an Iranian journalist is handed documents that uncover a conspiracy of seismic proportions. As both the Major and the journalist delve deeper, their paths will cross, plans for a coup will be uncovered and Western government involvement will be exposed.

Dark Years is an action-adventure game based on the real events surrounding âOperation Ajax â. In an attempt to secure the rights to Iranâs oil, the British and American governments secretly backed a coup that over-threw the democratically elected government of Mohammad Mosaddegh. The result was a period of great instability and violence.

Posted: 2 November, 2015
I am kind of shocked that this game only has mixed reviews. Honestly this is one of the worst games I have ever played. It is amazingly bad. Nothing works right and voice acting is so bad it's almost a crime.

Really, if you can think of a problem in a game this one has it.

The controls and camera are so bad it's almost unplayable. Just getting the hero to move around the world is very hard and good luck trying to tame the camera. It just zooms around doing it's own thing (at lest when it doesn't get stuck on something). Also the game's frame rate drops into 5-10 fps for a good chunk of the game even on the lowest settings. I don't know why this happens because early on in the game it runs just fine and then towards the end it gets better.

But even if all of that wasn't an issue. The graphics are awful and the game has about every single graphical bug and glitch you can think of. Windows shake, you can see through objects the world. Invisible walls, textures pop, you clip through everything. The models all have messed up proportions. Also for some odd reason at cretin points in the game the devs use filters on the screen that make the world look like it's in an awful light show.
And on top of that the story is a poorly translated mess that honestly makes no kind of sense and features the worst voice acting in English I have ever heard. The plot is nonsensical because the letters that the game uses to tell the plot are written in really broken English. And even when you do understand what it is trying to tell you it somehow manages to mess up it's own narrative by breaking it's own continuity.

It changes the dates of key events, first it switches between making the UK and USA the villain. Hell at some points the voice actor will say 'blah the Americans are doing something' while the thing he is reading from says: 'blah the English are doing something.'
I just beat the game and I have next to no idea what happened in it. Something about Oil.

And then we get to the puzzles. Basically to rub up against everything and hope a bottom prop pops up. Because that's how you find objects in this game. The is no thinking it's just rub on everything until something happens or until you are prompted to use something. It awful puzzle design and puzzles are just full of filler. Our hero is like hmm now that have this object I should go back to here. Oh now that I am here I should go back to where I was and look some more.

So much pointless back and forth to fill out the run time. Which the dev claims is around 8 hours but I beat it in 3. And with out the of filler they maybe 30 mins of content in this game.
