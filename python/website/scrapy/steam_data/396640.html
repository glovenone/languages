About This Game

TowerClimb is a difficult and rewarding procedurally generated platformer with roguelike elements.

TowerClimb is set in a fantasy world where enormous and mysterious towers of ancient unknown origin stand above humanity, extending to the heavens. Humans are driven by curiosity to climb them, usually dying in the process. Struggle forward as a weak human, driven by an iron will to reach the top.

The game borrows elements from the roguelike genre. A different adventure awaits each man; you will never play the same exact level twice. Run, Climb, Jump, Swim, and Fly your way up the tower's atmospheric and lethal worlds, using items and resources you find along your way. Quick thinking and strategy will keep you alive as you navigate the tower using unique platforming mechanics.

TowerClimb is challenging, and rewards reflexes, skill, knowledge and problem solving ability. Quite often, The tower throws your character into dangerous situations which require you to think very carefully about your actions. A unique, randomly generated level system ensures players must always come up with interesting ways to complete a level. Hundreds of men will die before you posses enough skill and knowledge to conquer the tower. A screenshot of every man's death is saved, along with how he died, to track each and every attempt.



  • TowerClimb at its core is an adventure platformer, with procedural levels and elements based on the rogue-like genre. It features local multiplayer support with an option for split-screen/single-screen. (a maximum of 25 simultaneous players is the limit. However, the game only supports 4 controllers (Xinput limitation). The remaining players must use the keyboard. A program like Xpadder can remap additional directinput controllers to keyboard keys)

  • The game features a TON of content. The Tower is tall and daunting to conquer, with plenty of areas and secrets throughout the game. Getting/seeing everything will take players a long time. There's bonus modes to unlock and play on top of the main game as well.

  • Each time you play the game, everything changes. The levels are different, random events can occur, rare items can be found, etc. The game will always keep you on your feet and give you refreshing challenges and experiences.

  • The platforming mechanics in the game are set up to feel very tight and responsive in combination with the climbing mechanics. You always need to be on your feet and aware of your surroundings, while continually planning how to climb higher. As you progress higher, different worlds introduce new mechanics that mean your play style must evolve.

5 years in the making, by Davioware and Quazi, a team of two brothers who have been developing and playing games since childhood.

This is a roguelite platformer that is criminally under played.

TowerClimb provides a very unique blend of atmosphere and challenge.
There are a plethora of opportunities to utilize the environment to your advantage: Need something to kill those monsters? Blow up those spikes and use them as improvised throwing weapons. Want equipment to climb even faster? Cook the rat meat in lava and give them to the crafter when you see him next etc.
Dangers you will face include terrain eating giant worms, rising lava, malevolent mages and spinning lazers; which you can often twist to your advantage to.

The soundtrack is amazing, and reaches a whole new level in the second chapter. The same can be said about the evolution of the graphics. The dev team have been extremely active in addressing player feedback across the last 5 years of the games development.

Not for everyone?
Be advised that it is also one of the most brutal platformers out there. Most players wonât see beyond the first 1/3rd of the content the game has to offer within the first 50 hours.
You can only carry one (replenishable) revive potion at a time (two on "easy" mode) and there are a few hazards like lava that will destroy you instantly regardless. BS deaths are extremely rare (<5% of deaths) but there are a few things early on that could be telegraphed better (like giant bats suddenly zipping across the screen). There is only one checkpoint for chapter 2 after the 20th floor. The game is very fair, however, and with the right combination of practice, brains and reflexes you will prevail...

Controls are a bit different, but become very intuitive after with an hour of practice or less (not sure why so many of those posting negative reviews played only a few minutes and forgot that they are fully rebindable).
My main nit-pick is that there is currently no proper way to pause the game if you're interrupted (though you can use <alt> in windowed mode, but this won't allow you to swap to other applications mid play). Thankfully though it usually only takes a few minutes to get to reach the safety of the transition screen.

Game Content/Length
In terms of content the game is huge: There are 14 main areas, each having some of their own unique mechanics across 46 levels. Such include bug sanctuaries, factories of death, disco club of twisted apparitions etc. There are some deadly secret areas to such as the world within the arcade machine as well as bonus unlockable game modes like Z-Ball.
The levels themselves are randomized each time you play so youâre forced to learn generalist strategies rather than rote learn layouts.
I've played a lot of roguelite platformers (also involved in making one called "Aura of Worlds"), and I found that TowerClimb has provided over 100 hours of engagement post main campaign. I still need to complete the bonus challenges such as the Colossus arena and 7 legendary fragment run...


TLDR:
Want a game with a focus on explorative wonder and strategic challenge? Buy this :)!
