About This Game

Age of Magic is a online Collectible Card Game with an MMO twist. Master four powerful classes, and build your deck using unit cards from five distinct races. Extend your collection and upgrade them! Defeat evil forces in an exciting PvE Campaign, take on powerful AI opponents with up to 2 other players in Dungeons. And then show off your deck building/playing skills and claim victory over your fellow players in the Arena!

Features

  • Unique turn-based gameplay. Easy to learn, deep strategy.
  • Fast-paced match, ends in 5 minutes for average.
  • Real-time PVP arena for 1v1 and 2v2.
  • PVP season and rankings.
  • Cooperative real-time dungeon supports up to three players.
  • Four classes with 18 skill cards and 5 weapons each.
  • Five races with 41 unit cards each.
  • Most cards can be upgraded up to 6 stars.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Microsoft® Windows XP, Windows 7
    • Processor: 2.33GHz or faster x86-compatible processor, or Intel Atom™ 1.6GHz or faster processor for netbook class devices
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Storage: 300 MB available space
    Recommended:
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection

Had a promising start but like most games of this nature, it has been (seemingly) abandoned by the developers. By this point, the Pay-To-Win (P2W) divide is so great that new players won't be able to catch up to those who have played from the start without investing a lot of $$ into the game.

One of the more frustrating points of P2W in this game is how the gold mine feature works. For 799 "gold" or more, you're able to invest in the gold mine and received daily amounts of gold. Unfortunately you're not allowed to use the gold you've earned in-game (via achievements, lotto, dungeon reward, etc), only gold you have purchased. This forces you to spend at least a fair bit of money each month in order to keep it active.

The stamina return is too low. There's no point in exchanging gold for stamina (or anything really) since the exchange rate is absurdly high for some reason when compared to other games of this nature. Guild tribute is meaningless in all honesty since the exchange rate for that is also horrendous. There's a bug that STILL hasn't been fixed since release that automatically ends your turn even if you have stamina to continue. Then there's also the commonly critiqued lack of a full screen option.

Overall there are better games of this nature out there that aren't as slow that you could enjoy.

This is part review and part beginner tips to have a more enjoyable experience in the game. I've played the R2game version for several years and have 3 high level accounts and our guild used to compete for top 1 spot in pvp until lack of updates killed the population.

The game will eventually let you be competitive (which cards are locked via paywall depends on publisher; there are several, but while free gold is less available here and costs are higher than R2, you can buy racial packs without being VIP so that's a plus - also R2game version is pretty much dead and unsupported). Just do your daily devotion, join an active guild, do the hot events and learn which quests/dungeons to farm and you'll be fine.

Now for new players, rushing is about the easiest way to go about it. Focus on getting Lupes (1-2 mana), Lyons (3-5 mana), Aves (backstabbers for defense, 2-4 mana) and whatever class cards you have. Add in extra 1-2 mana monsters to round off your deck. I suggest starting as a Ranger or Priest. Rangers can directly damage the enemy player and priests can heal themselves. These two classes are also fairly competent in PvP.

Later on you can branch out to other decks and builds but make sure you have your rush deck handy for minimal headaches while going through the early parts of the game.

I usually dont review games, but this is a game i have played alot through the ages and its many different versions. So im gonna give it a go and give you my thoughts. And also explain why i wont recommend this game to anyone at the moment.

The gameplay is really fun and addictive, nothing to complain about here. You construct a deck and fight others or NPCs. Together in dungeons or alone in the Campaign. Simple right?
But the problem arises the 1v1 arena. At a low level (arena ranking based) balance issues will occur due to high level differences and straight up Deck power. But at higher levels im beginning to see a very big difference in HOW the game is played.

Right now there are two major types of decks played:
Rush decks, and control decks. This does not bother me at all, because games like these always tend to have some strategies that are better thah others. But here comes the reason why this game is not any fun to play, brace yourselves!

THE DEVS DONT CARE.
Thats right, in other games cards are updated, classes are balanced and tweaked, new cards are added. But in this game? Nope! Nothing of the sort. You heard me right. Since i started playing this game, not ONE change has been made to the
-Classes (No nerfs, no buffs.)
-Cards (Like in general, nothing new, no balances, nothing.)
-Maps (Like sizewise lanewise etc.)
-The Campaign (No new Campaigns.)
-The Dungeons (No new dungeons.)

Right about now you must ask yourself, what are the devs doing then!?
Well right now the only \"new features\" i\'ve seen are some events. And mostly centered around the pay to win part of the game. And that is why i will not recommend this game at the time. I will of course change my review if the devs get of their asses and create some new content. But in my past experience with these games. Not going to happen.

TL;DR:
Devs are money hungry, dont make new content, 1v1 players (The only content which is fun at the moment is 1v1.) are one trick ponies, and no new cards are coming out to counter the one trick.

I rate the game
2/10
