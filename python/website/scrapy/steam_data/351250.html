About This Game

Climb the falling blocks as you race to avoid the rising lava in this challenging arcade platformer!

Play as an exceptionally bouncy marshmallow as you dodge enemies, defeat colorful bosses, and try to avoid getting crushed. Buy power-ups from falling shops, collect missions, bank your coins, and purchase powerful game-changing upgrades.

Avalanche 2 features a wildly addicting single player and a hilariously fun co-op mode. Avalanche 2 is a fun game with simple controls and a lot of depth. It's a great game for all players ranging from the casual to the hyper competitive.

Summary:
Avalanche 2 is a roguelike platformer where you try to get as high as you can by climbing falling (Tetris) blocks. There's only one big level splitted into segments and most of the fun comes from the smartphone game style of quests - you always get three random tasks with a small reward. Those tasks are about collecting coins, climbing, "surfing" (climbing on blocks that haven't fallen on ground yet) etc. Also, there's a in-game shop where you can buy hats, upgrades and level segment skips (you don't have to start always from bottom).

Positives:
- Entertaining gameplay, offering hours of addictive fun for fans of roguelike genre.
- Powerups will test your skill since some of those aren't just pure stat boosts, they may also change your controls (ex. bunny hop powerup).
- Co-op mode.
- Nice graphics style.

Neutrals:
- Some bosses are too easy.

Negatives:
- You might get bored fast if you don't like repeating the same level over and over.

Avalanche 2: Super Avalanche is such a treat. I got this in a Humble Monthly, and have no idea why it is not more popular. I had no expectations going in. If you love some great platforming action with a fairly simple rule set, yet tons of awesome meta-game upgrades: this game is a must-have. Controller support makes this sooo much fun to play from the couch.

Essentially, this is a tower-climb game. Blocks fall, and you make your way as high as you can go. There are powerups, and various enemies. There are banks where you can 'store' your money to spend on meta-game items (if you die, you lose it). There are shops along the way too. Oh! And there are quests that you can choose to do in between runs!

Pros:
+Very addicting. The core game is quite simple in premise, but definately expertly implemented.
+Great meta-game progression on this one (which I always like).
+Cute music, graphics, and sound that really fit.

Cons:
-You have to be into tower-climb games. Thats all I can think of. Comment more 'cons' in the comments, and I will rethink things.

Totally worth ten bucks. Really fun game.

I haven't played this game long, but i've gotten decently far and I can already say that this is an underrated gem.

I was actually reluctant to buy this considering how little attention it was getting as silly as it sounds, because the trailer was trying too hard to do the adult swim anti-humour bit and didn't show too much gameplay. I thought since that only a total of 28 people have reviewed it (upon time of buying) that it was just a small forgettable indie game that didn't have much lasting appeal or grabbing power. I finally bought it on sale, yet I would recommend it full price.

The music and graphics for this game are the first thing that stand out- catchy and gorgeous. The gameplay's grasp on you as the player probably won't be immediate though... I started out the game frustrated and bored. The tutorial only gives you the basics (which is enough, although it doesn't have any music for some reason, making me worry the rest of the game would be just as silent) and after that you're thrown into the game, which is painfully slow to start off. The game will also be shockingly hard when you first start playing as well, making every death a "god damn it, do I really have to start over from the slow bit again?".

The gameplay summed up shortly: It's that one level from yoshi's island where you have to vertically climb a tower of falling blocks, only it's the entire game with much more depth. When you first start out, you will get squished. Many times. The lava will probably never kill you, because it starts out slow as molasses unless you get the risk badge. You will never be able to actually land on those damn birds that fly at you, and sometimes you might be able to sneak in a snake kill, but more often than not you'll miscalculate your jump and land right beside them, killing you instantly. Manage to get to the first boss? It will be a tedious test of patience until you finally start to do some risky moves, which will in turn make you end up dead and sending you down to the bottom again.

Yes, this game isn't a forgiving teacher. But don't hit that refund button just yet- it gets better. I got a hang of the game surprisingly fast, and it's secret amount of depth blindsided me. You'll start learning how to surf, and how to get combos to squeeze out as many coins as possible. You'll actually start to remember that the screen wraps and learn how to use it to your advantage. You'll learn how to kill the bosses quickly. You might even make it to space, or the moon once or twice.

I do have some minor complaints with the game though, but nothing gamebreaking. Sometimes you can get an absolute terrible roll of RNG where blocks just do not stack at all how you want them to and it just never ends, making you die after a good run or make you restart at the beginning of one so you can actually progress. Walljumping feels responsive most of the times, but sometimes when you're trying to chain it, it's the most frustrating thing in the world. And for the love of god, I hate how shops work. I'm trying to run up away from the advancing wall of death and I accidentally jump on one of the shop chests, wasting my money. And if it's a deposit/withdrawl chest, I might hit my head on a ceiling and deposit multiple times. I would gladly rather press an action button to buy something than have to jump on it, even if it's some sort of badge that I have to equip to balance it out. Also, some challenges are lamely bugged, allowing you to cheat- the chest one in particular, you can just use a bank over and over to cheat it rather than actually get chests in one run.

I'm not sure how long this game is before looping, and I'm not sure if the flash game was just as good as this, but I'd say those things aside it's absolutely worth your money. If you're on the fence, maybe wait on sale, but otherwise you won't regret full price.

NOTE: Despite what Steam may say, I already have 35 hours of playtime with this game because I've been playing it in beta since before release on Steam.

Avalanche 2: Super Avalanche is a simple, yet highly addictive and extremely fun game that you will enjoy for hours. It's suited for kids and adults alike, and even has a local co-op mode to increase the fun! I'll name several features of the game that you might want to know about.

First off, your character is unique, you are a marshmallow called Marsh. As if that isn't cool enough, you can customize your character's outfit and starting items by unlocking items in the shop as you play. There's a load of achievements to get as well, and each achievement unlocks more items in the shop. Achievement-hunters will be pleased.

Gameplay is intuitive and highly addictive. It's got this 'just-one-more-time' feeling that many will enjoy. For those who like challenges, simply completing the game and doing the hardest missions will be difficult enough at first. Once you have that down, the next goal after that is to get as high up as possible to set a highscore!
Controls are intuitive and easy to grasp, but do not be fooled by the simple gameplay! There's loads of items to unlock and use, and the falling blocks and cute monsters will provide an exciting experience. There's few words to describe the feeling as you get higher than ever before, reaching just higher than last time!

The graphics are well-done and pretty cute, and they really enhance the feel of the game. The music also really fits the game very well and I enjoy listening to the soundtrack either on its own or when mixed in one of my chiptune playlists. I really can say that the soundtrack is top-notch and a good listen.

I would really recommend this game to anyone who has even the slightest interest after seeing the video. Look up some gameplay on youtube or something, but I can REALLY recommend this game. It's fun, simple, and has local co-op. The fact that I put in 35 hours on this game already goes to show that despite how simple it looks, it's addictive and exciting.
