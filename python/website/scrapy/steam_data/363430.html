About This Game



D Series is a New Off Road Racing Simulation. The game is intended for PC Computers featuring a force feedback steering wheel and pedal system but works with any style of controller. The title features 5 different vehicles and 6 large open world maps with more than 50 Miles of trails, two tracks and racing courses to explore. The main focus is capturing the intensity of off road driving and trying to give drivers the idea of what its like to harness the big power of these off road machines. So sit down, strap in and see if you have what it takes to tame these wild off road machines.


http://www.trophykart.com/

D Series Current Features
- Realistic off road physics and tire models
- DirectX Force Feedback calculated from proper vehicle geometry (with applicable FFB steering wheels)
- 5 different Vehicles (Trophy Trucks, Trophy Karts and Rally Cars)
- 6 different tracks were EVERYTHING on the map is collidable
- Day/Afternoon/Evening/Night Lighting
- Damage model with dirt/mud accumulation
- Global Leader boards for all tracks
- 50+ different vehicle tuning adjustments
- Adjustable vehicle setups with the ability to share
- Skinable paint liveries (Simply load your own car paint image)
- On Board GPS Nav System (We dont want you getting lost out there)
- 6 different Camera views with Multi-Monitor support and monitor angle correction.
- Tuneable Force Feedback, Degrees of Steering Rotation, FFB Damping, FFB Gain



6 Large Maps and Racing Venues

Would be good if this was on early access... But there really isn't anything here worth playing. You can't even race against other trucks so it's all time trials. The ability to paint your truck is neat but in addition to that it would have been nice to have more than 1 different truck in the game. If there was a career mode with upgradeable trucks and an actual path of progression it would be more interesting. Right now you have 5 or 6 tracks and you can drive through them. That's the game.

It's very buggy. The mountains just flicker randomly in many tracks, it is practically epilepsy-warning level of flickering. It looks funny though, I was laughing the whole time but I was thinking it was early access but apparently this is their finished product.

I will commend them for including both a pushbutton-handbrake and an analog handbrake but there is a problem with that. What I did was assign my handbrake to the right analog stick so I could try out (for the first time ever) the feeling of an analog handbrake in a game but due to the deadzone in the stick it causes the transmission to break. As in, if you center the joystick and it is only 0.05% off-center the transmission will cease to function and you can go in top gear and redline it and you won't be able to move. So that doesn't work at all, and to top it off you can't UN-assign a button so once you have assigned it you don't have the ability to change it.

In my opinion this game would be more fun without the invisible walls in the open areas of the track. I know you are supposed to just do the races but I see vast flickering mountains in the distance and it would have been fun to just go off-roading over there and explore.

At no point does it even feel like "off roading", it's more like driving on a gravel roads. I live out in the boonies with gravel roads so this just seems normal to me.

Overall I wouldn't recommend this to anyone. Literally the best part of the game is the fact that you can drive the truck in the menu/garage. This game has a retail price of $20 and for that amount of money you can get a copy of Spintires at a discount and play a REAL off roading game.

Dude, it's freaking trucks man. Why would you not like this.

D Series is a great off-roading game that was made by 1 guy, and the game isn't all that bad from 1 guy. You have a multitude of vehicles, from little TrophyKarts, to Pro2 and Pro4 trucks, and even a hatchback rally car. The tracks are all nice and varied with different designs and catering to different vehicles.

The only reason to get this game is if you have a steering wheel similar to the Logitec G27. This is a simulation game, and to get the most fun out of it, you need a wheel, and then preferably a racing seat. My setup is the G27 on the Playseat Nascar Kyle Bush seat. You can play it with a controller, but it's better if you get the wheel and seat.

All in all, not a bad Early Access game, even though not specified as an Early Access game. Some things need to be fixed, like more tracks, proper water physics, and other things, but, not a bad game if you own a G27. If you don't own a wheel, well, I'd say skip this one. 7/10
