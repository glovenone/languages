Hello Kitty and Sanrio Friends is a racing party game developed by Scarab Entertainment releasing on Steam in July 2015. Let me first point out that I have no interest in the Hello Kitty franchise (thankfully) so went into this game purely as an outsider wanting a casual kart racing experience.

Game play for Hello Kitty is extremely basic compared to other kart racing games. There are only three buttons you need to use, accelerate, turning and your ability button. I expected the gameplay to be simple given that Hello Kitty SHOULD be aimed at the younger audience so I canât fault the simplicity of the game at all. I played the game purely as a multiplayer experience as that is all I want out of a casual racing game like this. I think if I was to play this game solo I would not have had anywhere near as much fun as I did playing with my partner.

Graphically the game is again, very basic. No real amazing graphics on show. There is a little laziness when it comes to textures and general modelling. Some of the carts wheels will be sunk into the earth as they donât align with the ground properly, just little things that mount up to make the game not look as crisp as it should. There is very little in terms of animations, the characters are pretty much static the whole time and the only movement you get is when you activate abilities and even then it is very minimal. The soundtrackâ¦oh god the sound track. It is quite simply the most upbeat music you will hear. It had me and my partner having a rave with the subwoofer turned all the way up. The sound effects are nothing to rave about though, just very generic samey sounds.

Hello Kitty is not a hard game by any stretch of the imagination. I came first in every race we played in without having any difficulty. I mean, you get an achievement just from launching the gameâ¦ My partner on the other hand was not so lucky; I will touch on why in the technical section of the review.

There are 16 courses to play through, and 90% of them are all extremely short. You can complete your three laps in less than three minutes sometimes. Whilst there is a bit of variety to be seen between the courses, you do get the feeling that it is the same map just in reverse sometimes. The environments are varied to a good degree so it is not glaringly obvious.

As this is party game, you do get plenty of replay ability and you will probably not want to play this game on your own. The fun of this game certainly comes out when there are more than you. We have played for over an hour so far and feel like weâve had our hit of it, will we play it again? Possibly, it is certainly going to be a game I keep installed on my hard drive so we can play it again when we have friends over.

Technically we ran into a few problems, first of all as I mentioned above my partner had a number of issues which made playing the game more troublesome than I did. The main issue was that the controls are not responsive for anyone but player 1. You can move fine, but as soon as you want to use your ability, the game will just ignore the input. Secondly, the menus are EXTREAMLY hard to navigate and even harder to actually get into a game. If a child were to play this game, they would simply not have any idea how to get around the menus and play this game; we had plenty of problems even getting it to work.

To sum up, Hello Kitty and Sanrio Friends Racing is a poorly executed game that you will only get some enjoyment out of when playing with more than one person. The poor controller issues and bland graphics donât do the game any favours, but as with all party games, you tend to have more fun poking fun at your friends messing up in the game rather than the game itself. Sadly the game is priced extremely high for the production values on show, Â£14,99 is way way too high to even consider this a worthy purchase. If you can get it for less than Â£3, sure itâll be worth it just for the comical value, but other than that, stay away.

It's pretty obvious that there wasn't any testing done on this game and they just wanted to fire it out as quick and cheaply as possible. There are 16 tracks spread across 4 cup events as well as 20 skill events to complete a lap quickly or collect items with the entire game being completable in around an hour. The racing takes place in karts, boats and planes depending on the selected race. Although this game seems to be aimed at a slightly younger audience, you can get Sonic & Sega All-Stars Racing Transformed for the same price and it's a much higher quality product.

Positives:
+Bright and colorful tracks
+Many characters and vehicles to unlock
+Catch up mechanic helps keep the races close if the player falls behind

Negatives
-AI gets stuck or lost in every race
-Tracks and assets are reused frequently
-Massive invisible wall in the middle of one of the tracks
-Controller support is intermittent and doesn't work properly in the menus
-tons of clipping issues
-objects and vehicles can appear above or below the track instead of on it

First of all, yes, I know this game is made for little kids but after everything I experienced in the game (and out), I can not give this game a positive review.

The vehicles in the game, while varied, all feel awkward in the worst ways. Cars grip the road too tightly (and wheels clip through the floor), Boats are way too floaty making challenges involving them much harder than necessary, and Planes move fairly well with a controller but makes weapon powerups mostly useless.

Powerups in the game in general are a mix of too weak or overpowered. Each powerup is the usual Mario Kart-esque powerups you tend to see in kart racers, green shells, mushrooms, bullet bill, banana, and lightning bolt. The Lightening Bolt, Shells, and Banana powers are fairly weak and don't help give as much of an advantage as the speed boosts given by the other items and on a 3d plane with the... plane, dodging non-homing weapons is too easy making those powerups completely useless.

The game also offers controller compatibility, well, kind of. To use controller, you must turn on Gamepad controls in the settings, the issue with this is that the controller works in races but barely works on the menu screens, sometimes working, sometimes half working, sometimes not at all. The bigger issue with this is the fact that the game looks like it might have been ported almost directly from a console version as all the menu's show Wii-esque "Press B to go back" messages. Feels like a lazy port to me especially when my first completion of a tour was not actually saved in the game.

Stages are also a concern in my opinion. For a kid's kart racer there are a lot of twists and turns which is not a concern to me but the fact that they had the chance to use nice popping colors for a Hello Kity but instead make everything look bland, and in one case hard to tell which was the race path, an opportunity was missed there. Another missed opportunity is the fact that one of the first stages has a waterfall that pushes everyone towards a wall to show off the possibility of physics in the game, albeit it is not very clear that the waterfall is doing it and it just feels like the rocks are magnetized, they don't ever use the physics creatively again. Even the ice in winter levels doesn't feel like ice. Also there are again issues with clipping everywhere, the cars in general tends to clip their wheels through the ground but most starting lines and speed boost pads float above the ground.

The difficulty curves based on tours in the game, or rather that is what it is supposed to do. Each tour is like the next step in difficult AI drivers. Problem is that the final tour which should be the hardest ended up being the easiest due to glitches. On 1 stage, one of the AI continuously drove in circles near the finish line which also was a map with very hard to dodge iceberg obstacles but what was worse was that on another stage, the road had a very large invisible wall that stopped every racer, once you figure out where the invisible wall ends, the final race of the game ....is against no one. I finished the entire race before any of the AI finished 1 lap because they were trapped.

And my final complaint about this game...
I uploaded 7+ screenshots of my gameplay. Of these screenshots, the 3 screenshots of actual in game gameplay and not just Huds/Menus were flagged by Valve and caused me to receive a 3 day ban from posting to the community. Hello Kitty screenshots got me temprarily banned from uploading screenshots.


This game is not worth even $5.

I've always been a fan of Hello Kitty and I was excited to see this game up on Steam. I did read some negative reviews but decided to purchase the game anyway. As much as I love Kitty, I regret purchasing the game.

The biggest issue for me is that the game will not save. Whenever I shut down the game and load it back up later, nothing I "unlocked" is still unlocked. The cars are relocked and so are the characters. It's as if the game isn't saving anything. What's the point of a game if our "progress" is going to be reset every time we close down the game? It makes the game a waste of the money I spent on it. It upsets me honestly.

The game overall is decent. It's a fun little game, almost like Mario Kart but with Hello Kitty characters. The controls for the boats and planes can be a bit wonky but as you get used to them, it becomes a lot of better.

I would have given this game a recommendation but seeing as how my progress has been reset twice already, I can't rightfully recommend this game until that is fixed and updated. There is also no button to exit the game. It has to be done manually which isn't too big of a deal but is still a bit iffy.

It's pretty obvious that there wasn't any testing done on this game and they just wanted to fire it out as quick and cheaply as possible. There are 16 tracks spread across 4 cup events as well as 20 skill events to complete a lap quickly or collect items with the entire game being completable in around an hour. The racing takes place in karts, boats and planes depending on the selected race. Although this game seems to be aimed at a slightly younger audience, you can get Sonic & Sega All-Stars Racing Transformed for the same price and it's a much higher quality product.

Positives:
+Bright and colorful tracks
+Many characters and vehicles to unlock
+Catch up mechanic helps keep the races close if the player falls behind

Negatives
-AI gets stuck or lost in every race
-Tracks and assets are reused frequently
-Massive invisible wall in the middle of one of the tracks
-Controller support is intermittent and doesn't work properly in the menus
-tons of clipping issues
-objects and vehicles can appear above or below the track instead of on it
