About This Game

Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions is an action-packed adventure encompassing four dramatically different parallel universes, each with its own Spider-Man armed with unique strengths. Each stunning universe has its own detailed art design and thrilling gameplay with challenging skill sets to master in order to unlock powerful new moves. Explore dramatic, stunning environments, wield explosive new powers, and face epic boss battles against extraordinary foes. Experience the legendary Super Hero like never before: Four unique worlds. Four distinct Spider-Men. One unparalleled experience!

  • Experience Spider-Man x 4, with unique strengths for each world: Amazing Spider-Man, Spider-Man Noir, Spider-Man 2099 and the Ultimate Spider-Man
  • Go beyond the traditional New York City with a myriad of locations and styles
  • Immerse yourself in truly epic boss battles with boss-themed levels
  • Deliver damage up close and personal with a first-person perspective during key moments of battle
  • Discover new surprises around every corner with new abilities to uncover, plus multiple costumes and bonuses to unlock

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 / XP / Vista
    • Processor: Intel Core 2 Duo 2.6GHz / AMD Athlon 64 X2 3800+
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce 7800 256MB / ATI Radeon X1800 256MB
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 13 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX 9.0c - compliant sound card
    • Additional Notes: Internet connection required for installation and authentication

I absolutely loved this game on PS3. Add it to my Steam collection on the cheap? Why not!

It's a rather linear, story-driven experience. Not the open world of some Spidey games, but the premise of the game is unique enough that it's more of a "comic book that you can play", and it's really fun and very character-centric.

Each version of Spider-Man is a different incarnation of J. Jonah Jameson's nemesis, and the gameplay and powers between each version are different enough to keep things fresh and interesting as you switch between comic universes. Each level has a version of that universe's iconic Spider-Man villain (Vulture, Green Goblin, Rhino, etc.). Really enjoyed the Noir levels as they play like some of the more stealthy parts of the Batman Arkham series (YMMV).

HOWEVER...

*DO NOT BUY THIS FOR PC* It is most certainly *NOT* patched for Windows 10.

For whatever reason, your progress is not saved once you exit the game. So unless you like fighting Kraven the Hunter (that's the first level) over and over and over again, or if you have the stamina to play the game in a single session - you're kind of screwed.

I ended up uninstalling and getting a refund. The game is old enough that the developer isn't going to bother getting this fixed with newer versions of your operating system.

Such a shame, too.

Fun and unique entry in the Spidey franchise. Rather than continue the GTA-style formula from Spider-Man 2 onwards, this game goes with straight forward action similar to the old Spider-Man games on PS1. The twist being that you play four different Spider-Men who share some basic gameplay but have their own signature style. Amazing is the baseline, Noir focuses on stealth, 2099 incorporates a slow-mo ability, and Ultimate has you building up a "rage mode" to take on large groups of enemies. The levels too have some signature gameplay depending on the dimension: Noir has lengthy stealth sections (obviously influenced by Batman Arkham's predator stages) while 2099 features free-falling sections.

The plot is pretty thin and just an excuse to throw you into the different varieties of Spider-Man, but If you are a Spidey fan there's a lot to enjoy. Otherwise the Batman Arkham games largely do what this game does but better.

As to the complaints about Win10, well for what it's worth I completed the game over five different sittings and didn't experience any issues with the save game or any crashing.

When I saw Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions pop up on Steam I immediately had flashbacks to playing the excellent Spider-Man 2 for PS2. The open world nature was still quite new at the time and was the high water mark for superhero games. Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions released in 2010, one year after (arguably) the greatest superhero game to date, Batman: Arkham Asylum. Talk about a tough act to follow. Shattered Dimensions is a very different game, however. While Asylum was a modern day Metroidvania and Spider-Man 2 was open world, Shattered Dimensions opts for a much linear experience and is much better for it in the long run.


Shattered Dimensions plays out over the course of 14 levels, each centered around a boss and theme. While quite linear, the level design is quite strong and the locations are varied, from fair grounds to a futuristic laboratory. During these 14 levels, you get to play as four different versions of Spider-Man: Amazing, Ultimate, Noir and 2099. Perhaps the most brilliant thing that developer Beenox does with this game is that each version of Spider-Man has his own unique style of combat. While the button presses for the combos are the same, the animations are quite different and help keep the game fresh and varied. So while you may have just bought a nice combo upgrade for the Ultimate variant, it will look quite different when playing as 2099.


Along with different combo styles, each dimension has its own unique style and flair. I found the Noir setting to be the most compelling based on the fact that it’s built around stealth when the majority of the game plays out as a character action game, with my least favorite being 2099 (due to fairly mundane level design and subpar bosses). Levels are somewhat formulaic (you’re going to save a bunch of workers/scientists) but because each dimension has its own set of rules, I wasn’t bothered by it that much.


Like any great character action game, the combos are the meat of the gameplay and they feel quite rewarding for the most part. With each Spiderman having his own move set, the combat rarely feels stale and you’re always experiencing something new. The upgrade system was a real standout for me as well. You can earn points for upgrades by completing challenges in each level. These challenges can range from getting all the collectibles in a level to completing specific tasks (getting ten takedowns in a row without getting spotted, defeating enemies in a certain manner). I found myself quite engrossed by this system as it made me alter the way I played to complete the challenges and I tried things I otherwise wouldn’t have. To me, that is great game design when a game gives me a reason to constantly keep changing what I’m doing. Another mechanic that I appreciated (albeit slightly undercooked) was the first person combat. It usually only occurred during boss battles, but occasionally the view shifts from third to first person and allows you to fight from this perspective. Although not fully realized, it’s something I hadn’t seen in a game like this before and was yet another good change of pace mechanic.


Although I am by no means a huge Spiderman fan, I thoroughly enjoyed the story of Shattered Dimension and found the voice acting to be quite exquisite with numerous laugh out loud moments, and I’m not one to usually laugh at a videogame. The writing was certainly on point and the developers seemed to be really invested into the character of Spider-Man. I’m sure hardcore fans will get even more enjoyment out of it than I did.


While perhaps not the most complex character action game out there, Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions was quite the enjoyable experience and had me hooked for the better part of thirteen hours. By the time I had reached the credits I felt that it was certainly time well spent. If you are in the market for a fun beat ‘em up with quick, witty dialogue you could certainly do much worse than Spider-Man: Shattered Dimensions.

A great game that I remember playing on my Playstation 3 all the way back in 2010. It's a half-decent game that you will have some fun with. As for those on Windows 10 having issues, I have found a temporary workaround.

How To Properly Save Your Files

1. While in-game (make sure you are still using your current save file), alt tab out of the game.

2. Go to the save file location (Usually in Documents/Activision/Spider-Man SD/Saves) and copy your save file to a location where you will remember where it is.

3. When you exit the game, copy your backup save and replace the one in your current save folder.

NOTE: I recommend backing up your game save every time you beat a level, the game saves a checkpoint, etc.

EDIT: It appears that you cannot use the same save twice in a row. Make sure all of your save files are different in one way or another.

EDIT 2: It seems like the issue has something to do with the registry files.

EDIT 3: Windows 10 has been patched to support saving. This fix is no longer needed.
