About This Game

Are You Destined to Free your People from the Hands of Tyranny?

The year is 1337. During this, the darkest of ages, the bubonic plague was rampant. Kingdoms rose and fell with every swift blow of the sword. The Old Continent was in a state of great upheaval, as English kings hastily attempted time and again to uphold their claim to the French throne, shaking this fragile kingdom to its very foundation. The hundred years' war had been unleashed.

Relive the most intense battle scenarios of medieval Europe in this third-person 3D epic saga. Brandish your sword as you slay enemies amidst adrenaline-pumping action while you lead your troops to victory. Acquire new weaponry while you advance through eight massively gut-wrenching battle scenarios, each packed to the hilt with thousands of enemy troops to slay. Gain experience to obtain and unleash ever-more powerful combo attacks to devastate your foes. Slowly transform yourself from a single warrior into a forceful military leader as you develop and master the art of chivalry and tactical warfare. Become the greatest and most inspirational of French leaders by demonstrating your leadership abilities while commanding your legions of troops to victory. Tap into your arsenal of war machines as you relentlessly tear away at enemy forces.

Play as Joan of Arc
Live out the most exciting years in the life of Joan of Arc, the ultimate female warrior, in the ultimate epic battles! As you progress through her campaigns, other great military medieval leaders will join your cause and bring their own unique fighting abilities and characteristics into the fray.

Fight Epic Battles
Orchestrate your troops through eight massive maps featuring formidable strongholds, lavishly detailed cities and myriad landscapes, from rolling hills to thick forests and flowing rivers. Experience firsthand the sheer intensity and savage brutality of medieval castle sieges brought to life in amazing lifelike 3D.

Engage in Adrenaline-pumping Action
Slash and slay enemies from a thrilling third-person perspective view. Wield the sword to your advantage by executing unique combo attacks with perfect timing to devastate the enemy.

Acquire New Weapons
Train yourself to master new fighting techniques to unleash your full fighting capabilities, then acquire an increasingly devastating arsenal of weaponry to overwhelm your foes.

Master Long-Lost Skills
Demonstrate your chivalrous aptitude by mastering your timing and precision among dozens of unique attack combos. Train yourself to become the complete warrior.

Strategically Command Entire Armies
Valiantly marshal your troops through the thick of gut-wrenching battles and demonstrate your leadership abilities as generalissimo to your troops, country, and king.

Employ Fearsome War Machines
Use to your advantage the devastating war machines at hand, such as catapult, trebuchet and battering ram to tear down castle walls, vanquish enemy fortifications, and instill fear in the eyes of your enemies.

I'd like to point out that I'm recommending this game on a whim. It was overall a fun experience but only when you hit the last half of the game. This game is VERY buggy, has HORRIBLE AI and has overall bad balancing with enemies, specifically generals and archers/crossbowmen. I tend to write long reviews but, well, that's how it is. It's my personal experiences typed out for you to consider.

First off, the story. It's quaint. It's basically the whole Joan of Arc story, but it ends with a happy-note.Joan of Arc not dying and leaving to return to her home town and reunite with her friends and family instead of, y'know, being executed by the English. It's 8 chapters/missions, the first two being short and the rest being 1-2 hours, at least. The story is well done and the ending is very satisfying. Definitely gives Joan of Arc the ending she deserved and it definitely makes Joan of Arc awesome, gameplay-wise. Probably one of the strongest generals in the game, other than Metz. More on that later.

Secondly, the music. The music in this game amazing. It has atmospheric sounds (e.g. dirt crunching beneeath feet, sound of metal armour hitting ground when moving, bird chirping, shouting and cheering sounds when fighting, etc.)that go very well with the game. The actual music itself has two side. The first side being the atmospheric-y non-combat music like this one, my personal favourite https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=96AFp2Z7SUE&index=5&list=PL6FC7ED1C9B1507AE Then there's the combat music like this, which is my personal favourite : https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZZK2AUSdhlY&list=PL6FC7ED1C9B1507AE&index=2 If you listen to the combat music, at around 0:25 ish is when it really hits it hard. It just has a great feel to it for what the game's theme and atmosphere is. It's especially amazing when fighting the enemy generals. Overall, the music is satisfying if you like music that really goes for the home-run and fits into where it's placed.

Enough drooling over the music. Here we go. The graphics. The inevitable paragraph. The graphics are actually pretty amazing for its time (2004) and at the same time they aren't particularly anything to freak out over. The graphics fit the game and it doesn't hurt my eyes. Good enough for me.

Of course! An inventory system! You basically have 5 types of items : Weapons(main weapon for the hero's preference, e.g. maces for La Hire or polearms for Duke), armour(both aesthetic and pretty useful for damage reduction late-game), food(basically potions for health / energy), misc. items ( keys for chests and doors, letters for mostly story but also a few sub-missions), and ammo(for the crossbow/bow). Weapons consists of Base Damage + X% and bonuses for each attack such as +40% Down Attack, for killing downed enemies. The ranged weaponry is literally there for being the ranged weaponry and should only be used for taking out distance ranged enemies. Other than that, the inventory system is pretty simplistic.

Finally, the gameplay! This is where the game shines but also falls quite a bit. This is also where the wall of text is introduced. First off, I'm not exactly a "pro gamer" type of person but I'm also not horrible. I'm mediocre. The gameplay in this game is similar to dynasty warriors. It's a slash-a-sword-through-a-group-of-enemies-with-combos game. You can do both a normal attack and a power attack. The power attack uses up, well, energy and is significantly more powerful than your normal attacks, regardless of what bonuses you get. You have combos as well but for the most part they're there for the sake of being there. See, you can upgrade each individual attack/combo. The max level of an attack is level 10. Upgrading combos changes the damage by a minimal amount, so the best you can do is upgrade two attacks that are by far the most important ones. The first is the rush attack, which is THE quintessential forward dash. It also makes you invulnerable while perform it and costs very low energy (1 EP with a specific ring). The second attack is the "energy attack" which is basically an ultimate. It costs both HP and EP but is a ridiculously good crowd-control ability, mostly because it makes you invulnerable for the duration of the attack, knocks down everyone, and you move while performing it. Simple. Upgrade those and you'll do well... Against the normal enemies. Here's where the biggest issue of the combat comes into play : Ranged and Generals. Now, the ranged aren't *that* bad but the issue with them is that they constantly move around, shoot at a moderately fast rate (even the crossbowmen) and they generally don't die in one hit, being as durable as light infantry units. They can also make you stop in your tracks with one hit and any subsequent attacks knock you down. So, yeah, they're annoying. Cool. What about the generals? Simple! They're invulnerable when using their power attacks(which they can do an infinite number of times and in repetition one after the other), they deal a ton of damage(1-hit killed one of my generals with 108 HP SEVERAL TIMES, and the hit didn't even connect) and you can only really get hits in when you down them with a rush attack or sneaking hits in *immedietally* after they finish their power attacks. The good thing about all of this is that blocking can, well, block anything. You can defeat any of the generals 1-on-1 if you block-and-hit. That being said, it's horrible unbalanced and can make for a bad time if you don't know that. Yeah, that's it. Unbalanced generals and annoying ranged units.

A second finally, I'll talk about the siege and RTS aspects of the game. RTS is, well, RTS. I don't know how to explain this but somehow RTS makes the game easier. I don't know if it's because the AI goes ham on the combos and attacks and just bruteforces the combat but it makes it easier and it's one reason why the final mission is ridiculously easy, considering it's required that you use it to move some units. The siege mechanics in this game requires you to use RTS to move the units onto a siege weapon and then to move the actual siege weapon. Cannons can attack both units and buildings but the other siege weapons can only attack the buildings. You can either capture your own siege weapons from the English or you can build them at resource spots, which only the Duke can do. You're not even required to use the siege weaponry since you can buy explosive ammo which can take down gates, and generals are pretty good on their own. You can effectively solo any siege if you have the food and ammo for it.

Final finally, I promised to talk about the generals you can play as, so here we go. Joan of Arc is your all-around general at first-glance but she tends to have the most powerful attacks and her ultimate weapon has 5 socketable items that buff it up pretty nicely. She's definitely the spotlight. There's Jean Metz, who is basically Luke Skywalker with his attack speed, movement speed, and capability for high ranged damage(he starts off with decent dexterity). His power attacks are also VERY quick. There's the Duke, a halberd-wielding general that is required to make siege weapons and has a more AoE set of attacks. There's La Hire who takes the role of "lovable human boulder". His attacks are more AoE, especially his ultimate attack. His power attacks are in a circular formation. There's two unplayable characters : â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ of Orleans and Prince Charles. They are playable in RTS mode but that's not so much "playable" as it is moving them around. So, who do I suggest for the "most powerful"? Metz. He's ridiculously OP with his attack speed and movement speed.

Inevitably I missed a few things but they would be very small things and not worth mentioning. Overall, the game was pretty decent but there were a few issues with it, like bad AI and invincible generals. If you can get past the small issues that litter this game, it's a pretty good Joan-Of-Kick-â¥â¥â¥ game.

When to by this game? Think of it as medieval total war as a combined RPG/RTS that you can switch to on the fly. And if you've seen the well-rated movie 'the messenger' with Milla Jovovich (thats right, the resident evil chick) as Joan of Arc, you will definitely encounter many similarities and even in-game scenarios that happened in the movie too.

On normal mode, the game is very easy to play. It's a typical old-school hack-n-slash revolving around using your left and right mouse buttons for combo's, and Q for blocking. Jumping and pressing C can be used for other powerful attacks. What makes the game enjoyable is the historically correct story (to my knowledge). Downside is the not optimally working collisions at times or some NPC's getting stuck (or even yourself, because NPC blocks path).

The castles and encampments in this game look great and you can decide to destroy them in RTS mode. The walls, towers and gates can actually break and be destroyed. Or you can decide to take it up close and personal and breach our way in and kill all the soldiers inside.

There's a few classes of troops to choose from from light infantry and crossbowmen to polemen and macemen. you can assign all of those to your controllable commanders (the main characters, same as historic equivalents) or to the occasionally appearing less significant NPC commanders which look the same as regular units except they're donning goldplated armour. The configurations stays both in RTS as in RPG mode.

In the first few missions the RTS element isn't introduced yet which doesn't really matter as you will not have a lot of units under your command thus playing in RPG mode makes sense. Later on in the game, you'll be able to build more troops, buy more armor, weapons and food and even build siege weapons.

Despite all the positive goodness, here a list of things that may downgrade the gaming experience:
- the poor collision registrations when striking enemies who are down. Instead, just use a power attack, you'll hit anyone down and standing. problem solved!
- getting stuck. it's annoying when your fellow soldiers get stuck especially if thereby they block your path. the only place it happens a lot is at Tourelles. if you go up into that fortress to destro the arbalests, make sure you have not too many units following you. or it will be impossible to go back via the narrow stairs and you can't jump off the wall.
- food shortage. There is only so much food in the game. You have a special inventory for yourself and the main characters, and a separate inventory for troops following those characters. with 15 troops under your commmand, it's doable. If you've got like 50 of them and they're under fire from siege weapons and arrows, food disappears within a minute. The only ways of getting more food is slaying enemies, slashing crates or buying from the food store, and the food store's food is NOT infinite. ...
- The drag and drop interface. you can only move 1 item at a time. This becomes very problematic if youre in the heat of a battle and have to pause the game, to remove food items one by one to your troops inventory, to keep them alive -.-. Takes away from the gaming experience.


But yet, a game truly unique in its design with a nice story (you might learn something.) and oh yeah, checkout that movie joan of arc: the messenger with milla jovovich, you will recognize a LOT from that movie in this game.

- the poor collision registrations when striking enemies who are down. Instead, just use a power attack, you'll hit anyone down and standing. problem solved!
- getting stuck. it's annoying when your fellow soldiers get stuck especially if thereby they block your path. the only place it happens a lot is at Tourelles. if you go up into that fortress to destro the arbalests, make sure you have not too many units following you. or it will be impossible to go back via the narrow stairs and you can't jump off the wall.
- food shortage. There is only so much food in the game. You have a special inventory for yourself and the main characters, and a separate inventory for troops following those characters. with 15 troops under your commmand, it's doable. If you've got like 50 of them and they're under fire from siege weapons and arrows, food disappears within a minute. The only ways of getting more food is slaying enemies, slashing crates or buying from the food store, and the food store's food is NOT infinite. ...
- The drag and drop interface. you can only move 1 item at a time. This becomes very problematic if youre in the heat of a battle and have to pause the game, to remove food items one by one to your troops inventory, to keep them alive -.-. Takes away from the gaming experience.


But yet, a game truly unique in its design with a nice story (you might learn something.) and oh yeah, checkout that movie joan of arc: the messenger with milla jovovich, you will recognize a LOT from that movie in this game.

I'd like to point out that I'm recommending this game on a whim. It was overall a fun experience but only when you hit the last half of the game. This game is VERY buggy, has HORRIBLE AI and has overall bad balancing with enemies, specifically generals and archers/crossbowmen. I tend to write long reviews but, well, that's how it is. It's my personal experiences typed out for you to consider.

First off, the story. It's quaint. It's basically the whole Joan of Arc story, but it ends with a happy-note.Joan of Arc not dying and leaving to return to her home town and reunite with her friends and family instead of, y'know, being executed by the English. It's 8 chapters/missions, the first two being short and the rest being 1-2 hours, at least. The story is well done and the ending is very satisfying. Definitely gives Joan of Arc the ending she deserved and it definitely makes Joan of Arc awesome, gameplay-wise. Probably one of the strongest generals in the game, other than Metz. More on that later.

Secondly, the music. The music in this game amazing. It has atmospheric sounds (e.g. dirt crunching beneeath feet, sound of metal armour hitting ground when moving, bird chirping, shouting and cheering sounds when fighting, etc.)that go very well with the game. The actual music itself has two side. The first side being the atmospheric-y non-combat music like this one, my personal favourite https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=96AFp2Z7SUE&index=5&list=PL6FC7ED1C9B1507AE Then there's the combat music like this, which is my personal favourite : https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZZK2AUSdhlY&list=PL6FC7ED1C9B1507AE&index=2 If you listen to the combat music, at around 0:25 ish is when it really hits it hard. It just has a great feel to it for what the game's theme and atmosphere is. It's especially amazing when fighting the enemy generals. Overall, the music is satisfying if you like music that really goes for the home-run and fits into where it's placed.

Enough drooling over the music. Here we go. The graphics. The inevitable paragraph. The graphics are actually pretty amazing for its time (2004) and at the same time they aren't particularly anything to freak out over. The graphics fit the game and it doesn't hurt my eyes. Good enough for me.

Of course! An inventory system! You basically have 5 types of items : Weapons(main weapon for the hero's preference, e.g. maces for La Hire or polearms for Duke), armour(both aesthetic and pretty useful for damage reduction late-game), food(basically potions for health / energy), misc. items ( keys for chests and doors, letters for mostly story but also a few sub-missions), and ammo(for the crossbow/bow). Weapons consists of Base Damage + X% and bonuses for each attack such as +40% Down Attack, for killing downed enemies. The ranged weaponry is literally there for being the ranged weaponry and should only be used for taking out distance ranged enemies. Other than that, the inventory system is pretty simplistic.

Finally, the gameplay! This is where the game shines but also falls quite a bit. This is also where the wall of text is introduced. First off, I'm not exactly a "pro gamer" type of person but I'm also not horrible. I'm mediocre. The gameplay in this game is similar to dynasty warriors. It's a slash-a-sword-through-a-group-of-enemies-with-combos game. You can do both a normal attack and a power attack. The power attack uses up, well, energy and is significantly more powerful than your normal attacks, regardless of what bonuses you get. You have combos as well but for the most part they're there for the sake of being there. See, you can upgrade each individual attack/combo. The max level of an attack is level 10. Upgrading combos changes the damage by a minimal amount, so the best you can do is upgrade two attacks that are by far the most important ones. The first is the rush attack, which is THE quintessential forward dash. It also makes you invulnerable while perform it and costs very low energy (1 EP with a specific ring). The second attack is the "energy attack" which is basically an ultimate. It costs both HP and EP but is a ridiculously good crowd-control ability, mostly because it makes you invulnerable for the duration of the attack, knocks down everyone, and you move while performing it. Simple. Upgrade those and you'll do well... Against the normal enemies. Here's where the biggest issue of the combat comes into play : Ranged and Generals. Now, the ranged aren't *that* bad but the issue with them is that they constantly move around, shoot at a moderately fast rate (even the crossbowmen) and they generally don't die in one hit, being as durable as light infantry units. They can also make you stop in your tracks with one hit and any subsequent attacks knock you down. So, yeah, they're annoying. Cool. What about the generals? Simple! They're invulnerable when using their power attacks(which they can do an infinite number of times and in repetition one after the other), they deal a ton of damage(1-hit killed one of my generals with 108 HP SEVERAL TIMES, and the hit didn't even connect) and you can only really get hits in when you down them with a rush attack or sneaking hits in *immedietally* after they finish their power attacks. The good thing about all of this is that blocking can, well, block anything. You can defeat any of the generals 1-on-1 if you block-and-hit. That being said, it's horrible unbalanced and can make for a bad time if you don't know that. Yeah, that's it. Unbalanced generals and annoying ranged units.

A second finally, I'll talk about the siege and RTS aspects of the game. RTS is, well, RTS. I don't know how to explain this but somehow RTS makes the game easier. I don't know if it's because the AI goes ham on the combos and attacks and just bruteforces the combat but it makes it easier and it's one reason why the final mission is ridiculously easy, considering it's required that you use it to move some units. The siege mechanics in this game requires you to use RTS to move the units onto a siege weapon and then to move the actual siege weapon. Cannons can attack both units and buildings but the other siege weapons can only attack the buildings. You can either capture your own siege weapons from the English or you can build them at resource spots, which only the Duke can do. You're not even required to use the siege weaponry since you can buy explosive ammo which can take down gates, and generals are pretty good on their own. You can effectively solo any siege if you have the food and ammo for it.

Final finally, I promised to talk about the generals you can play as, so here we go. Joan of Arc is your all-around general at first-glance but she tends to have the most powerful attacks and her ultimate weapon has 5 socketable items that buff it up pretty nicely. She's definitely the spotlight. There's Jean Metz, who is basically Luke Skywalker with his attack speed, movement speed, and capability for high ranged damage(he starts off with decent dexterity). His power attacks are also VERY quick. There's the Duke, a halberd-wielding general that is required to make siege weapons and has a more AoE set of attacks. There's La Hire who takes the role of "lovable human boulder". His attacks are more AoE, especially his ultimate attack. His power attacks are in a circular formation.
