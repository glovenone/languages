About This Game

You wake up with an aching head and the world out of focus. In front of you is a man accusing you of being “fake”. He looks exactly like you, down to the last scar and pimple, has the same memories, the same voice and the same personality.

But who is lying and who is telling the truth? Who is fake and who is real?

With over nine different possibilities, you can either end up as the tragic hero, the sly villain, or an innocent victim. Will you keep your cool or pick a fight? Play through all the endings and take a peek at the truth of your demise.

Whatever action you take, ultimately, you must answer the question: “Who is Mike?”

Features

Screensize: 1280 x 800
Endings: 9 endings
Length: 10,500 words (approx. 2 hours gameplay)
CGs: 22 CGs
Genre: Mystery/Thriller, Supernatural, Comedy
Rating: PG-16+ for Strong Language and Violence

This visual novel was short among the ones I've played, but nonetheless it was an interesting experience.

Who Is Mike is nothing all too complex, though it can be fairly difficult to figure out the choices meant to lead you to a specific ending. You really can figure it out with a few tries, and it only takes about five minutes to get to the main ending, and around a minute for the shortest. Not a challenge for any seasoned visual novelist, I can assure you, but it can be fairly confusing at times.

Art was so-so as far as I can see. Slightly better than what you might see in any other plain game, but nothing special. Pretty pixelated for a game of this type, too, but I'm sure that was on purpose. Story was original by my standards, which, though my standards are not very high, I can at least pick it out among the other stories that attempted to center around clones.
Soundtrack was better than expected, building the atmosphere in a way that wouldn't be possible had there been none at all. However, I don't feel it stood out in any other ways, and thus faded into the background at most points. Still, it deserves a mention.

It really didn't take me too long to get through this. That's probably a good thing, as I tend not to stick with anything that doesn't offer achievements. That's kind of my selling point, I guess. But achievements or no, I'd give this a whirl. What's there to lose?

Who is Mike is a very short (<hour) horror/mystery Visual Novel that does many things right.

Pros:
*It features very nice art and interesting, suspenseful short story.
*There are multiple simple choices that branch the story in different ways, allowing you to reach different endings.
*The outcome (and even the premise) of the story depends on your choices.
*It's free, short, and doesn't overstay its welcome.

Cons:
*The choices and consequences are usually not very intuitive or clear, so the ending you get will probably be unexpected.
*Therefore, if you wish to see all endings, you will need to follow a guide.

If you've ever wondered what the hell is a visual novel, if you like short horror/mystery stories, or if you just have a free hour of your time - you should try it.

TL;DR: It's a bit repetitive, but still good.
My biggest problem with this one involves the endings, but I'll keep this review as spoiler-free as possible. Getting to them is a pain on its own: Which ending you get depends on how you answer a short series of questions. This would usually be alright, if the paths to each ending weren't so ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ random. Choosing between liking dogs or hyenas can completely change the entire plot of the game, and since there's no ramifications between questions, you don't get any indication of which ending you're headed towards, until it's already too late to turn back. If you refuse to use a walkthrough, be prepared for a ton of save-scumming, and just randomly checking every single possible combination of answers until you find the 6-7 endings missing from your list.
I don't really like the way that every single route completely contradicts eachother either, but that's really more of a matter of personal taste.

All that said, the game is alright. The art, despite having a few slips here and there, is pretty good, and the narrative can create some pretty good tension in important scenes. The music, as simple as it is, is pretty eerie, and really helps the atmosphere. And the story really compels you to keep reading, since there's no way of knowing what you're about to find. Definitely pretty good for a free to play game.

As frustrating as getting every single ending can be sometimes, I had fun with this one. Definitely recommended if you have an hour or two to kill.

This is a spoiler-free review.

Who Is Mike is a very enjoyable way to pass an hour or so.

Pros:

- Great art, especially for a free game.
- Good writing, moreso than in many of the indie VNs I've played. It can get repetitive when the protagonist is angsting, but in a believable way.
- Unusual plot that keeps you guessing until the last minute.
- Free.

Cons:

- Short. Which is fine, especially for a free game, but if you like your VNs long with many branches, this is not one of them.
- Lack of variety in player choices. For most of the game, you'll be faced with the same decision again and again in slightly different contexts.
- Non-intuitive branches. Maybe it's just me, but it took a lot of trial and error, followed by reluctant use of a guide, to figure out how to get all the endings that branch off from the main section of the game.
- Inconsistent storyline. This is the one truly bad thing about this game - instead of the endings gradually giving you more and more bits of the puzzle, like in a good mystery VN, different endings reveal that you were playing different scenarios all along. It's a bit like a murder mystery where the the murderer turns out to be a different person depending on the choices you make during your investigation, even though the clues are all the same each time.

In the end, I definitely recommend it. It is a distinct and memorable experience that costs no money and not too much of your time.
