About This Game

Developed by award winning studio Relic Entertainment, Dawn of War II ushers in a new chapter in the acclaimed RTS series – taking players to the brutal frontlines of war to lead an Elite Strike Force on a mission to save the galaxy.

It’s the 41st Millennium in the Sub-Sector Aurelia – a cluster of worlds on the edge of the galaxy – where a battle of epic proportions is about to commence. Ancient races will clash across the planets that dot this sector of space, battling for the greatest of stakes – not only for control of Sub-Sector Aurelia – but the fate of each race.

With a focus on fast-action RTS gameplay, Dawn of War II brings to life the science fiction universe of Warhammer 40,000 like never before. Experience the intimate brutality of battle as you play through your chosen race’s epic campaign. Clash with the enemies on battlefield ablaze with visceral melee and ranged combat. Lead and develop your squads from raw recruits into the most battle hardened veterans in the galaxy. Also included is The Last Stand, a co-operative game mode featuring user controlled heroes fighting waves of enemies.


  • Brutal Frontline Action & Tactics
    Get straight into the action to experience intense melee and devastating ranged combat. Use vicious melee sync-kills to obliterate your enemies. Outsmart your opponents using dynamic and destructible environments to suppress, flank and destroy your foes.

  • Non-Linear Single Player Campaign
    Command an elite strike force, developing the skills and abilities of your squads and commander as you progress through the game.

  • Co-Op Multiplayer
    Play through the entire single player campaign co-operatively with a friend, at any point in the game, anytime.

  • Next Generation RTS Engine
    Utilizing Relic's proprietary Essence Engine 2.0 Dawn of War II delivers cinematic visuals, detailed graphics and amazing special effects.

Posted: 11 June
The old DoW was a basebuilding RTS game ala Starcraft, and it was FANTASTIC... this is NOT the same game, however, it is very in it's own right. Almost all the negative reviews are due to the fact that the core game mechanics have changed, and in my opinion, these negative reviews would likely not have even cropped up had the title of the game been something other than Dawn of War. Being a sequal, people expected more of the same with a few enhancements, instead they got something totally different.

Pros:
- Decent Graphics for a RTS even today... the animations are a lot of fun.
- Great Micro gaming! If you are into Microing your units in RTS games, that is what the focus of this game is. You can pull off some really cool combos and tactics in this game, especially given all the different types of gear you can combine with various skills.
- The game is fast paced, bloody, and ultra-violent. It IS true to the name WARHAMMER.
- There RPG aspect of the game is pulled off very well, swapping up your team with various skills and loadouts really changes the gameplay. It's very fun to try new tactics and ideas.
- The star map and mission selection is cool. Sometimes you will have to pass up on a mission to persue a different one and there are reprecussions for your actions. Let a territory fall and you may lose some of your orbital support in the sector, but maybe it was worth it to get that blue legendary reward from the other mission. You can always comeback and reclaim the territory later with bigger better gear.
- The balancing seems pretty great. For every unit there is a counter, this includes bosses. Take heed to the tips before the missions as they will potentially let you know an enemy's weakness, or give you reports as to what the enemy is bringing to the fight so you can adjust your loadout appropriately.
- You feel like you are getting stronger throughout the game, and some of the end-game weapons and skills are great!
- Multiplayer is a lot of fun- reminds me of some of the greates Starcraft1 RPG mods.
- A ton of content. I haven't even touched anything other than the base campaign, and then I get to play Chaos... after that if I'm still binging on the game Ill check out the DLC, but you get a LOT of content with just the base game.

Cons
- Towards the end of the campaign the game begins to get a bit repetitive.
- Same maps are reused a bit too much. The maps are great, wish they had more.
- Sometimes the pathfinding will drive you insane. Multiple times Ive lost a team because I told them to fall back, and they do so by charging straight into the enemy to go around the long way. Very irritating sometimes.
- Level Cap at level 20 seems to come a bit soon... I want to keep progressing my heroes!
- The inventory screen could use some filters. Things tend to get a bit muddled and cluttered as you collect more gear. Also, while the character screen shows base stats, it would be nice if it displayed other bonuses given by your equipment (resistances mainly... many different pieces of equipment will boost resistences, but you can only see this when you hover over the items).
- Some of the bosses are kinda lame, while others are pretty awesome with their own unique tactics which can take you by surprise at first.
- Multiplayer is dying out. hopefully there will be a greater player base with the announcement of DoWIII

I think using the systems built here they have a great formula, and perhaps a different Warhammer franchise of games altogether. I'd love to see the same mechanics expanded upon in another game... BUUUT I also want to see another PROPER sequal to DoW. I enjoy both in their own ways.

So, looking at the game in a different lens of NOT a base building RTS, but a tactical squad command game, I would highly recommend it 8/10.

Most people know what this game is about. The store page is pretty accurate in describing what it is.

Is it good?
Depends. Some people enjoy making a massive army and steam rolling the enemy. Others enjoy having a smaller amount of units and managing them using their unique abilities. If you fall into the second of these two, then give this game a go. If you prefer large armies, I'd say pass on this.

The Warhammer 40k universe is incredibly well presented in this game. The gameplay is mostly focused on smaller squads and managing their abilities. The combat is great with it's array of animations (which you can catch repeating a little too often sometimes) and solid sounds to back it up.

The single player campaign is good, although I think most people will be attracted to the multiplayer more than the campaign.

What I would add to this game if I could would be to add more races (I miss Tau and Necrons) and have more maps to play on.

A solid buy if you enjoyed previous DoW games. Just be aware it does differ from the first and although the addition of Tyranids is awesome, they've left out other races.
