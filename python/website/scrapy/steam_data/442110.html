About This Game

PWI is a stunning free-to-play MMORPG where inhabitants of the pristine Perfect World must unite to battle against the Wraith - undead creatures who seek to destroy all of creation. Amidst a realm of stunningly rich environments, players can fly freely through the skies, explore dangerous dungeons, or join intense PvP battles for power and land.

Key Features:

  • Cross Server PvP
    Faction battles, featuring up to 80 vs 80 players, against rival factions to claim in-game territory and gain untold riches.

  • Free-form Flying
    The original MMO featuring flight. Fight in the air as soon as you create your character and explore the world vertically.

  • Constantly Evolving World
    Regularly updated with extensive content, including server-wide events to challenging end-game dungeons.

  • Create the Perfect Character
    Customize to your heartâs desire with a deep character creation system. Choose among 6 Unique Races with 12 Distinct Classes to best fit your playstyle.

It's strange... I played this game for countless hours back in it's prime, back when I was in high school. I made some friends, I made some enemies, I saw so much world drama, names like Wizzled came second nature to my knowledge. (This guy apparently bought thousands of dollars of stuff from the cash shop, then taunted GM's saying they could never ban such a huge supporter of the game. Long story short, he eventually got banned iirc. Never learned if he managed to get back on.)

I will say right now, I absolutely love Perfect World. It's probably the nostalgia talking, but I've never spent more time in a world than I did with this one. It's music, it's locales, just casually flying around and chatting with people... I've never felt the same way about an MMO as I have this one. (I currently play FF XIV, and as much as I love it, I can't seem to bring myself to converse with others on the level I did back on PW.) I've never clicked install so fast in my life... however... this is not the game I fell in love with...

Right off the bat, the character creation has been absolutely butchered. All of the old hair-styles have been removed from the character creation screen. Apparently you can still get them with ini file editing... but what the hell is the point in that?! I can't make a character without actually seeing how I'm editing them...
Not to mention, the slides have been absolutely destroyed. Even back in the day, you could make really strange looking characters, from twig apendages, to boobs twice the size of your head, but they were never in the incredibly rediculous range... now... oh my god now... these sliders... you can make the stuff of nightmares... Shoot, the default settings are horrific on their own.

The original starting zones have been completeley done away with, and replaced by a One for all beginer zone, that I never actually managed to get past due to a dungeon-esque instance near the end that I couldn't find a party for. (This game is old-school, no party finder. Gotta do it the old fashioned way.) Not to mention, the new starting zone just feels... wrong. This may be because I've made SO many alts and played through the original 3 starting zones hundreds of times, but this new one is just... ugh... I miss walking into my race's home village at lv 2-3, and just conversing with the large number of players that were always just hanging around those zones for no real reason.

I've never truly understood how Vanilla WoW players felt until now. Going back to a game I loved so dearly over four years later, and seeing it â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ized so badly... (I'm pretty sure the game is under new ownership, and that probably has a huge part to do with my feeling really uncomfortable with the game as it stands now.) It almost physically hurts.

I want to recommend this game so badly, but it is no longer the game I loved, and it's too aged to be of much interest to most newcomers these days. If it were to get some huge quality of life changes, such as a party finder, level synch, and a return to the old character creation system, I would recommend this game without a second thought.

In the meantime, I DO at least recommend looking up the OST for this game. I found myself booting up PW so many times just to listen to the background music while I did my homework in high school. It is so relaxing, and atmospheric... Going back to listen to it now gives me chills, and makes me yearn for a time where this game was great.

I'm going to try to give it some more time, maybe the changes will grow on me. But as a long time old school player of this game, my first impression is that the game is worse off than it was.

There are some pros and cons to this game, and unfortunately, the cons outweigh the pros in a massive way.
My gametime on Steam does not reflect the time sunk into this game prior.

Pros:
- Atmospheric
- Great soundtrack
- Entertaining lower level gaming (if you enjoy questing)

Cons:
- Grind to win, and no, not just a little.
- Pay to win
- Expensive cash shop
- No real customization of your character unless you use the expensive cash shop
- Shifts from PvE to PvP rather sharply, and PvP is extremely unbalanced
- Bad sound effect quality
- Terrible use of English. For how much money they ask players to spend they could hire some proper translators/writers.
- Terrible time sink. You're literally forced to spend many hours to progress anywhere. It's possible to avoid some of this, by spending, you guessed it, actual cash.

It's a smart setup by the game makers but players will inevitably end up spending a lot of time and money (or even more time) into this game only to find out that past the starter levels which are just an introduction to the game mechanics, it becomes an unbalanced PvP-heavy game with lots of people gouging the in-game market to get ahead of others by necessity. It's all the bad aspects of an unbalanced MMO with a pretty, Eastern presentation layer on top.

So, even if I'd want to give this good marks, I simply cannot. Their recent updates have only made it worse despite some of the redesigns they've done to improve characters. Don't waste your time on this, there are much, much better MMOs out there.
