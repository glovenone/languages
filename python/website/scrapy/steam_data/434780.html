The Renegades of Orion 2.0 is perhaps one of the most creative and yet terrible single screen shoot'em ups out there. Most shmups have you as a space vessel, but you are some piece of 80s rock metal album cover artwork sprang to life. I would have preferred a more traditional route, but if you're going to be weird, go so far over the top that you end up in space.

Your goal is to destroy bosses, but the real challenge to the game are terrible controls, poor design choices and giant hit boxes. The arrow keys control move you around in 8 directions, but you are so slow and sluggish. Enemies can move in a full 360 degrees and just aim at you. Under normal circumstances, this is never an issue, but you and the enemies are each so large that its tough to maneuver to avoid them.

The only real attack here is eye lasers that halt you so there's a fight or flight thing going on, but never both at once. You need to get to a corner of the screen, shoot then move as enemies pile on you. When you move there is a chance that your eye lasers will move with you like if you move north, the lasers will move north.

There are other attacks, but the seem cumbersome and complicated. They require two keys and your attack key to work and even then your eye lasers shoot at the nearest small enemy, so there's never a reason to use anything else. Think of that as the game's balance. When you're being rushed head on, your attack does the same thing. If you go to the top of the screen where your head is above the screen and then shoot, nothing happens. The eye lasers are made up of tiny weak projectiles that launch every frame.

The keybinds are bizare. H for save, G for load. F for fullscreen I can understand in a way. The rest is just spacebar and shift.... to pause! When I press F12 to take a screenshot in Steam, a chrome extension for developer tools pops up. That's an interesting feature. I was never expecting that.

The game starts with you one on one facing a boss who summons demons to hover at you. Even from the first moments of gameplay there are issues. At the end of the boss there is a glitch that seems to fire off the same sound effect each frame. Most developers would have the sound once, but whatever.I also took note that at the bottom of the screen you have a giant health meter at the bottom and the boss health meter is a small number in the right corner.

I would say this is someone's first game, but I can also say its trying to be terrible on purpose. Its amazing this game can be 2.0, because as it stands now its cumbersome, tough and over complicated. The Renegades of Orion 2.0 is a valuable way to learn game design at its smallest levels. You have a simple game here and yet everything goes wrong with it.

Perhaps the biggest sin of Renegades is that its just dull and uninspiring. You can laugh at how bad the game is, but beyond that, there's no reason to push on.

On top of the bare bones content of "just play" there is paid downloadable content. There's just not that much of a game here. Its more of an oddity than something that could be confused for entertainment or fun. I was done after a minute of playing, but for review purposes, I had to push on.

The game can be salvaged with some sort of score system to make it more of an arcade game. As a single screen shooter, there just needs to be something that's more engaging that lets me feel like I'm in control rather than fighting a tough game.

Thank you to my friend Blanka for giving me Renegades of Orion 2.0. Hey even if I dislike it, I still thank you for it.
