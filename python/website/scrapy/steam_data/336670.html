About This Game

WHAT IS HIT?
HIT is a free game offered at no cost to players. We do not have in-game purchases or DLC for sale. As a small group of developers with limited resources, we want to give this game to you for free to enjoy. We spent a lot of time balancing and enhancing the game throughout our development cycle, which often introduces bugs. We did our very best to test for quality, but undoubtedly many issues have slipped through the cracks and we are continuing to address them. We appreciate you understanding that HIT is not perfect, but we really want to share it with all of you and hope that you can see the love we have put into it.
~The HIT Team

GAMEPLAY:

HIT is a, multiplayer, deception-based shooter. HIT IS AN ONLINE-ONLY GAME, BEST PLAYED WITH AT LEAST 5-8 PLAYERS SIMULTANEOUSLY. Betray your friends, sniff out a Rogue Agent, and stop a mad scientistâs dastardly plot!

FEATURES:
-As an Operative: Work as a team to stop an evil geniusâs missile launch
-As the Rogue Agent: Secretly sabotage the Operatives and dodge the blame
-Play as 4 different characters, each with unique abilities
-Explore a luxurious volcano lair
-Enjoy short, fast-paced, online gameplay sessions

Developed in Unreal Engine 4, HIT is a multiplayer, third-person shooter for up to 8 players that revolves around deception, treachery, and espionage. Set in a sleek, 70s-style super-mansion and glazed with a decadent layer of treacherous whimsy, HIT is stylish, deceptive, and uproarious fun.

TOTALLY FREE MODEL:
HIT is available absolutely free with no advertisements or in-game purchases.

This review is subject to change if the game updates, but until then my recommendation is to avoid it sadly. Launched today and all of these issues remain, game is most likely abandoned (due to being a school project with no monitization, not like I blame them for abandoning it) so I'd just save yourself the download time and ignore it.


HIT is essentially a combination of many ideas, mainly ones from an old Garry's mod game mode called "Trouble in Terrorist Town" and a source game called "The Ship". The general idea is that a group of people are all seemingly trying to accomplish a goal, only one (or more) of them are traitors and you must find and kill them. It's a really old (and fun) concept, and HIT mostly succeeds except for some major issues.

Lack of people playing, and a incredibly gimped keyboard/mouse support.

Obviously the first issue is seemingly easy to fix, and it's a free game so with a bit of publicity people just might start playing in large enough numbers for a much more in-depth review to be possible, but as of writing I can only go off of what I was given.

The second is actually bizzare to me, unlike games like Dark Souls which were designed for a console with no intention of becoming a PC port, this game seems to ONLY have been made for the PC, which begs the question why a controller is almost required? The menu doesn't respond to PC input that well, and the keyboard seems to only be used for imputting names.

Now, the real reason I can't recommend the game comes from the fact it is terrifyingly unoptimized. Load times are long enough to convince you the game has crashed (which the game frequently does, I've spent barely any time with it and only got 1 stable play test) and the options basically give you a resolution setting and your choice of "High" "Medium" or "Low".

All in all with updates, bug fixes, optimizations and UI improvements I'd easily see myself playing a game like this, but as of right now it's just a mess.
