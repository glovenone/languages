About This Game

Evolution II: Fighting for Survival is a real-time action life simulation game. Conquer a world of randomly generated, evolving species. You and competing species eat plants and other organisms to grow and reproduce. Other species offspring randomly mutate. You can design your offspring with advancing parts. Each part can be moved and sized to achieve the best combination of acceleration, top speed, turning ability, reach and attack strength. After you and your family eliminate all the other species, the next level is reached where your family splits and mutates into rival families. As levels increase, the challenge grows as your family split size shrinks.

I rarely write online but I'm sad to see that this game gotten bad reviews as, despite its flaws, I love it. The criticism made me hesitant to get it despite the low price but I'm very happy I still tried it out; if you have an interest in virtual life simulation, evolution and/or nature, you may want to consider this game.

The interface is a bit clunky and it takes a while to "get the game", but for those with patience may see the depth of it.
I know many compare it to Spore and I see the likeness but I think it's rather unfair. It was awhile since I played Spore, but I think the mechanics of this game is far more interesting. Spore is an action & design game, while Evolution II simulates evolution, mutation and natural selection. If rightfully compared to anything, it's an action game that is splice between Gameware Development's Creatures-series and Spore's cell stage. But in the end, Evolution II is really it's own thing.
