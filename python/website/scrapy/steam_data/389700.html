About This Game

It is a short but very interesting horror,about a man who woke up in his own grave,next to his family.
In the past,he was a very bad man,he is confused and cannot make a choice between good and evil.
Our goal is to help the protagonist to make a choise between good and evil.
Only you will be able to choose good or evil,and only you can decide his fate.
The game have multiple endings,or rather two,good and bad.The ending in the game will depend on your actions.

My Bones is a short indie horror game brought to us by publisher/developer Infernal Dream, the same company that has provided such titles as VERGE:lost chapter, White Mirror, and Lost Souls. Sticking with its trademark, My Bones is made in Game Guru, and features overly-long loading screens when transitioning from one area to the next.

Sticking with the format of the other games from the developer's library, this title also features extremely poor optimization and horrid framerates. Even on my mid-high end rig, I saw no better rates than 44 FPS, and as little as 14 in some points. There is zero direction, and absolutely nothing that tells you about the control scheme of the game; with the exception of pressing E to pick up keys and open doors.

Yes, My Bones is a key finding simulator... a very short, horrendously optimized one. I spent most of the game wandering around in the dark because I had no idea that you apparently have a flashlight. There's supposedly an evil piggie in the second level, but I'd only heard it oink once, and it was no threat at all; therefore I don't believe that you can actually die. There are two endings, one good and one evil, but I'm uncertain as to why you would put yourself through the pain of this game more than once, just to see an alternate, mostly text-based ending.

I could also point out the poorly written aspect of this game, but I really hate doing that to developers who obviously do not have English as a first language. Yes, the grammar and punctuation is bad, but I will say at least the developer tried. I've seen many other developers stop trying to translate their games into English because of this type of criticism. A better proof reader, or translator, would help a lot in this scenario, but the plot points still come across just fine.

It confuses me as to how My Bones has a Very Positive and Mostly Positive rating on Steam, and how there are so few negative reviews on this game. Have people become so willfully accepting of badly optimized, poorly written, key finding simulators masquerading as horror games? This title is nothing short of horrible, and I would go as far to say as it's worse than VERGE:lost chapter, just with fewer loading screens... because it's significantly shorter. I got this title on sale for $0.65 CAD, and I honestly regret it. Stay away, even when it's on sale. Play Fingerbones instead... not only is it a hundred times better than this game, it's also free.

Rating: 0.5/5.0 - Abysmal, avoid it like the plague.
