About This Game

After a freak storm over the Atlantic, you find yourself stranded on the shores of a mysterious island. Do you have what it takes to escape the Lost Sea?

Lost Sea is an action-adventure game set inside the Bermuda Triangle. Recruit a crew of survivors who can help you explore the hazardous islands as you hunt for the artifacts needed to navigate the Lost Sea.

Features:

  • Explore millions of procedurally generated islands.
  • Encounter an array of deadly traps and dangerous critters.
  • Recruit from a large cast of stranded with their own traits.
  • Challenging gameplay and permadeath for you and your crew.
  • Unlock powerful new player abilities and special moves.
  • Discover useful items that will help you survive.
  • Buy valuable ship upgrades that will assist you in your journey.

NOTE: This game was given to me by the developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.

==========================================================
---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Lost Sea is a classic console-style action adventure game with rogue-like elements.

In Lost Sea, you find yourself on a tropical island in the Bermuda Triangle after your plane has crashed during a storm.

For reasons that I won't go into here, you need to travel to a series of islands of four different types (jungle, desert, swamp, and snow), collecting weird magical tablets on each, in order to open an interdimensional portal and escape the Triangle.

==========================================================
----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Graphics - Lost Sea uses cel shading to good effect to give its three-dimensional graphics a cartoon feel. Textures are mainly clean, bright colours, continuing the cartoon theme, and models seem to have relatively low poly counts, although are nicely animated.

There's a day-night cycle with nice lighting changes and a rain effect, which is also quite nice, but it seems to me that it never rains during the day part of the cycle and always rains at night, which is a bit disappointing.

The game includes some nice little graphical touches like animated plants blowing in the breeze and a shark swimming around the docks, which serve to give a bit more life to the world. Unfortunately there's no manual camera zoom, though, so if you want to see anything up close you're out of luck.

Sound - Sound effects are suitable. They're not bad, but there's nothing special about them either. There are no voices for the different characters in the game, which leaves it feeling a bit empty. Music is catchy, with a sort of pirate adventure movie feel to it.

Gameplay - Lost Sea plays like a classic console action adventure game. You can play with keyboard (no mouse), but a controller is fully supported and recommended. Controls are simple: left stick for movement, right stick for camera rotation, and buttons for interaction, attack, sprint, dodge, etc.

The game is made up of four zones, each with a different theme. Each zone contains a series of individual islands, culminating in a boss level, although in the case of Lost Sea you don't have to (and can't) visit every island; instead the runes you collect act like dice to determine how many islands you hop over on your next journey. Each island is then made up of a series of hexagonal outdoor areas, each enclosed on all sides except where it connects to an adjoining room. Each island, although outdoors, ends up looking like some sort of modular habitat when viewed on the map, with a few exceptions.

Where Lost Sea differs from classic games in the genre is in its use of rogue-like elements, namely permadeath and procedural generation of islands. Unfortunately, though, neither one really works that well here. The base components for the procedural generation are mostly large, and you'll often see whole hexagonal areas copied from one island to the next, which not only breaks the suspension of disbelief, but also makes for a repetitive game. Permadeath, also, doesn't really work that well in this game, as dying simply causes frustration in making you start again; there's no carry-over benefit outside of normal progression, and the levels are all similar enough that even though you don't end up playing exactly the same islands next time you play, you might as well be doing so for all the variation you'll see.

Lost Sea also includes the concept of crew members: seemingly random people who you find at various points on each island who will join you as your slave without even exchanging a single word with you. Each crew member has up to four skills that he or she can use to boost your abilities in the game, from unlocking chests or repairing wooden bridges, to resurrecting you (once only) if you die. It's a bit silly, but is a good addition to the game. Unfortunately, though, the crew members are also very limited in implementation, with only a handful of skills meaning that you quickly max out your limited quota of crew with the most useful skill combinations, and just ignore anyone else you meet. Poor pathfinding and an inability of the crew to move out of harm's way lead to increased frustration as well.

Finally, making the repetitiveness issue even worse is the amount of backtracking you'll find yourself needing to do on islands to deliver tablets back to the docks, or go back and pick up items or crew that you didn't have space for before. This is especially painful in the early game.

==========================================================
-------------------------------------PROS & CONS------------------------------------
==========================================================
Pros:
+ Rogue-like additions are a great idea, but need more work to fully realise their potential benefits to the genre
+ Crew are a good idea (but, see Cons ...)
+ Good cel-shaded graphics with nice animations
+ Steam Achievements
+ Trading Cards

Cons:
- Lots of backtracking required
- Procedural generation seems to be based on a small number of large sections, making many levels look the same, adding to the repetitiveness
- Limited number of enemy types
- Limited crew skill options make talking to most potential crew members unnecessary after the first couple of islands
- Crew are braindead and easily become stuck on terrain or won't try to avoid even the simplest trap or enemy
- No manual zoom
- No introduction or cut scenes, outside of small in-game scripted events; not everything is explained that well
- Needs a little bit more QA


==========================================================
--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------
==========================================================
I've listed a lot more cons than pros in this review, but don't let that give you the wrong impression. I like Lost Sea a fair bit -- enough to have played for over five hours straight in my first sitting, anyway.

It's good, but with a bit more work it could be excellent.

6/10

Textreview:
Videogames have 3 very important characteristics, A like atmosphere, B like banging music and C like compelling gameplay! I hope you were able to follow me because while some games are easily implementing A and B, some games just lost sea.

Lost Sea is a fun little game I would let my kids play, if I had any. A light and colorful game, where we’re stranded in the most famous Bermuda Triangle. The goal is to survive against lurking monsters, wildlife and challenges us to get all collectible treasures and tablets to continue our journey forward.

So, how do we play it? Mostly with a gamepad. Keyboard controls are there, but complex and the camera angle to your character doesn’t change, that’s why you need a gamepad. Because it has two sticks…

Basically the controls could be better for mouse and keyboard.

Most annoyingly, and I’m not sure if it’s the fault of my Xbox One Elite controller or not, is that once I plugged my gamepad into the PC, all options and travel screens automatically move to the left. So I can barely even move the cursor to the character that I want to start the game with or properly move the cursor over the next island that I want to visit. Again, not sure if it is the game’s fault or mine. Also it doesn’t really matter which character you pick, because none of them have any percs or advantages other another.

At least we can spam the attack button which is necessary because while some enemies are very easy to take care of, the later zones are giving you enemies which charge you so quickly, you need the reaction time of Flusha or JW or someone in that range. At least the first zone, with all its enemies, it is easy to learn how to approach each enemy type. Another good thing is that enemies are not respawning once you beat all of them.

To progress in the game, we need magic tablets which let us move around in the Bermuda Triangle. The islands have a random generated layout and are set together with hexagons. In those hexagon elements are different spawns for enemies, sometimes an NPC and the tablets that you need to travel the sea between islands.

On your way you can find helpers, NPCs, which have different percs like the ability to revive you, to build bridges to get to new areas and to unlock chests. Other features are the ability to dig, to make your attacks stronger and many more. There is a lot of RNG going on in this game and while it certainly varies the difficulty of this game, it’s mostly just the best way to find the one character with lockpicking, revive and maybe even the builder ability. I ran an entire zone just with one NPC from start to end, thanks to some lucky roll.

But watch out, some NPCs like to bring themself in danger and others can’t keep up with your amazing fast movement and get stuck behind a wall.

But wait, there is more! The RNG is also combined with a deep leveling system. You can extend your own abilities like finding the fastest path back to your ship, running… yeah running is and unlockable, although it has to be said that running only lasts for a few seconds and it takes forever for you to regain stamina to run again… oh and neat stuff like managing more than one crew member at a time. I boggles me, why can’t you just “collect” crew members and switch them out when you need someone with a skill? Ah well, you can switch them out, when you first find them. Just let the current crew member go to your ship and recruit the next one. Back at your ship the previous crew member waits and you can reswitch them again.

What’s also fun to look at is the voice acting for this game. There are basically only two voices ever used in this game as it seems. A male and a female voice. No matter which NPC you find on the islands, they either have the default male or the default female voice. But, that is not even the funny part, the really funny part is when you get to fight the pirate at the end of each zone. At least the first zone features are grumpy redbeard who seemingly has some anger issues and sounds like a twelve year old. Not sure what’s up with that but it does sound somewhat wrong when you see the character.

You know what I wished for? More healing! It’s mostly impossible to get heal items from chests or to find one of those trees that magically give you about half of your lifebar back. There is an ability that you can unlock to get a higher chance of health from beaten enemies, but man I wish it would have been there right from the start, because without it, you quickly die and have to play way more carefully and therefore play the game at a higher difficulty level.

So, while I opened the review with the outlook of giving my possible children this game, I still have to say, that some aspects of the game need some adjustment. The charging enemies should be that powerful, as they were in the desert zone, and I would wish for costumizable controls for the gamepad and the mouse and keyboard combo.

I did not finish this game as of writing this review, because while the game was surely in a proper state without game breaking bugs or other problems, I just could not motivate myself to play more than the about 2.5 hrs that I invested in this game. It’s not my favorite type of game and surely not really aimed at my age group. I hope you can take my review as some buyers guide and I also hope that the developers will continue to improve the game. There is certainly a fun little game to be found here.

Videogames have 3 very important characteristics, A like atmosphere, B like banging music and C like compelling gameplay! I hope you were able to follow me because while some games are easily implementing A and B, some games just lost sea.

Lost Sea is a fun little game I would let my kids play, if I had any. A light and colorful game, where we’re stranded in the most famous Bermuda Triangle. The goal is to survive against lurking monsters, wildlife and challenges us to get all collectible treasures and tablets to continue our journey forward.

So, how do we play it? Mostly with a gamepad. Keyboard controls are there, but complex and the camera angle to your character doesn’t change, that’s why you need a gamepad. Because it has two sticks…

Basically the controls could be better for mouse and keyboard.

Most annoyingly, and I’m not sure if it’s the fault of my Xbox One Elite controller or not, is that once I plugged my gamepad into the PC, all options and travel screens automatically move to the left. So I can barely even move the cursor to the character that I want to start the game with or properly move the cursor over the next island that I want to visit. Again, not sure if it is the game’s fault or mine. Also it doesn’t really matter which character you pick, because none of them have any percs or advantages other another.

At least we can spam the attack button which is necessary because while some enemies are very easy to take care of, the later zones are giving you enemies which charge you so quickly, you need the reaction time of Flusha or JW or someone in that range. At least the first zone, with all its enemies, it is easy to learn how to approach each enemy type. Another good thing is that enemies are not respawning once you beat all of them.

To progress in the game, we need magic tablets which let us move around in the Bermuda Triangle. The islands have a random generated layout and are set together with hexagons. In those hexagon elements are different spawns for enemies, sometimes an NPC and the tablets that you need to travel the sea between islands.

On your way you can find helpers, NPCs, which have different percs like the ability to revive you, to build bridges to get to new areas and to unlock chests. Other features are the ability to dig, to make your attacks stronger and many more. There is a lot of RNG going on in this game and while it certainly varies the difficulty of this game, it’s mostly just the best way to find the one character with lockpicking, revive and maybe even the builder ability. I ran an entire zone just with one NPC from start to end, thanks to some lucky roll.

But watch out, some NPCs like to bring themself in danger and others can’t keep up with your amazing fast movement and get stuck behind a wall.

But wait, there is more! The RNG is also combined with a deep leveling system. You can extend your own abilities like finding the fastest path back to your ship, running… yeah running is and unlockable, although it has to be said that running only lasts for a few seconds and it takes forever for you to regain stamina to run again… oh and neat stuff like managing more than one crew member at a time. I boggles me, why can’t you just “collect” crew members and switch them out when you need someone with a skill? Ah well, you can switch them out, when you first find them. Just let the current crew member go to your ship and recruit the next one. Back at your ship the previous crew member waits and you can reswitch them again.

What’s also fun to look at is the voice acting for this game. There are basically only two voices ever used in this game as it seems. A male and a female voice. No matter which NPC you find on the islands, they either have the default male or the default female voice. But, that is not even the funny part, the really funny part is when you get to fight the pirate at the end of each zone. At least the first zone features are grumpy redbeard who seemingly has some anger issues and sounds like a twelve year old. Not sure what’s up with that but it does sound somewhat wrong when you see the character.

You know what I wished for? More healing! It’s mostly impossible to get heal items from chests or to find one of those trees that magically give you about half of your lifebar back. There is an ability that you can unlock to get a higher chance of health from beaten enemies, but man I wish it would have been there right from the start, because without it, you quickly die and have to play way more carefully and therefore play the game at a higher difficulty level.

So, while I opened the review with the outlook of giving my possible children this game, I still have to say, that some aspects of the game need some adjustment. The charging enemies should be that powerful, as they were in the desert zone, and I would wish for costumizable controls for the gamepad and the mouse and keyboard combo.

I did not finish this game as of writing this review, because while the game was surely in a proper state without game breaking bugs or other problems, I just could not motivate myself to play more than the about 2.5 hrs that I invested in this game. It’s not my favorite type of game and surely not really aimed at my age group. I hope you can take my review as some buyers guide and I also hope that the developers will continue to improve the game. There is certainly a fun little game to be found here.
