About This Game

More than a game, Autumn is a relaxing place for spiritual learning and growth with a soothing atmosphere.

In each game you play as a Spirit which depends on the natural world for its progress in life. As you help the world around you to thrive, you receive vital energy(Prana) in return. During the Autumn season, while the trees are shedding their leaves, orbs of Prana are released into the air, which you can absorb. Your own Prana can be utilized to help and change the world around you and to obtain spiritual teachings.

More than levels, puzzles or in-game progressions, Autumn’s focus is on the growth of the player. Not the one in the game, but the one in the chair.

There are no time limits, no enemies to defeat, no goals to reach… except the ones you set for yourself. Relax, learn, grow… and enjoy Autumn.

Suffers from horrible instructions.

The in-game tutorial signposts do not help at all. None of them tell you what to expect; only how to advance towards the right and "open up new territory". No goals or motives explained. No purpose for doing anything. There are a few puzzles encountered as you move towards the right (including one inane slider one which I couldn't be bothered to finish). Then I found myself unable to advance anymore because, despite following instructions to "click on the orbs" or whatever, the new territory just wouldn't "open up".

Perplexed, I then took a look at the game description on the product page in Steam, and it contains instructions not found anywhere at all in the game. So, what is this? Players have to look at the product page description in order to know what they're in for, or supposed to do?! Unbelievable. (And even then, the product description doesn't tell you how to do it.)

I did like the art style and pastoral feel to the entire thing, but the instructions (or lack thereof) are so mind-blowingly frustrating they practically cancel out any positive utility this game is supposed to provide.
