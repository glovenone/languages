About This Game

A humorous adventure with a quirky cartoon graphic style
Immerse yourself in 30s America, between Prohibition and the Great Depression, and discover Charlie, a rich heir who divides his time between social obligations and his hobby as a private detective.

During yet another official museum opening, Charlie witnesses a robbery, the victim of which is none other than his own girlfriend. Firmly set on investigating the case despite the reluctance of his uncle, the detective in charge of the inquiry, he unknowingly embarks on what will become the greatest adventure of his life!

Thanks to his unique skill and unrivalled memory, Charlie explores and investigates all the way into his dreams!

Posted: 14 September, 2015
Charlie Chamber is rich, easily bored, absent-minded and a bit snobish, yet he somehow decided to become a private investigator and try his luck at solving a theft that occurred while he was attending a museum opening: despite not being the best person for the job Charlie can count on his special abilty, a photographic memory he can only access when he sleeps.
While the aforementioned premise is quite nice and original Dream Chamber sadly squanders it with easy gameplay, uninspired minigames, murky plot and short length.

PROS:

-the art style is quite nice and all scenes are quite detailed

-great jazzy soundtrack

-the setting (the USA in the 30s) is pretty interesting and has its charm

-fully voice acted, most actors do a good job

CONS:

-the dream mechanics are truly underused (being required about four times or close to it) and generally boil down to just revisiting an area while dreaming, being able to talk to your subconscious while asleep also has basically no use and there's little dialogue for it

-the dialogue minigame (where choosing the right answer gives you more powerful ammunition to bring down a metaphoric fortress with a few shots) feels out of place and doesn't really work well, other minigames are a dull hunt through empty drawers (where you only click over and over until you find the right one) and an unskippable, atrocious 2d stealth section with awkward controls and only one checkpoint

-there's no text description for things when you mouse over objects in an area, this makes it hard to clearly see what can be picked up/ interacted with and what's just part of the scenery: you'll have to try and click on everything to find out what's what

-the cliffhanger ending leaves most things unresolved as if the devs were sure of making a sequel, as things stand basically nothing is explained and this makes it unsatisfying at best

-the game crashed on me once, so you're advised to save often to avoid losing progress since the game has no autosave

-quite short at less than 3 hours

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=518114631

Dream Chamber is one fo those games that isn't bad per se, having bought it for 3$ it wasn't a total waste, but it just hasn't enough going for it to make it a good game. Its biggest sin is squandering the great premise of using the dream world and photographic memory to solve cases: it's just not used enough and, when it is, it basically just highlights objects and clues that other games leave to your reasoning. The various minigames are also dull when not downright frustrating and add nothing to its gameplay: most of the time they actually lower your fun factor with their bad design.

In the end I can't recommend it, it's just a very forgettable and somewhat awkward entry in a genre where many games have done things way better.
