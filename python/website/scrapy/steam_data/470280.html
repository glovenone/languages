Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Early Access is a great way to get valuable feedback and to bring this unique VR room scale experience to the community as soon as possible. Lots of features are coming in the following months, and with Early Access feedback and funding we'll be able to polish and improve the game drastically. We'll hear out all the ideas and suggestions players may have and will try to implement the best ones. The final version will be full of game mechanics and content.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The final version with tons of content should be done by the end of 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The main story will be implemented in the final version for users to experience, but the game will still be built on sandbox type driving survival thriller with random events and encounters. The game will grow with the ideas and feedback of community and in the final version everything will be nicely put in place with high replay value.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently what's implemented:
- Infinitely generated road
- Ability to exit the car, open door, trunk and hood
- Radio
- Collect doll parts and put them in the trunk to trigger event
- Move tree trunks that block the way
- Something lurks in the woods always hunting you, look around when you hear the heartbeat
- Coming soon: Gas Stations, Stores, Villages, Looting/Looters, Cults, Car breakdowns, Flat Tires, Day Night Cycle, etc.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Early Access price will be 70% off the final version of the game as a thanks for supporting the development.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are constantly working on the game and would love to hear feedback and suggestions from the community as we continue to add features and content to this unique VR title.”

About This Game

Lost Route is a driving thriller video game made for room scale VR.
Requires a chair to sit in which position is indicated in the game.
Features include procedurally generated road, random events, spooky atmosphere and encounters.
Wild fires forced community to evacuate; you take the closed road to try to make it out alive.
Something creepy lurks in the woods, looters try to steal things and cult members worship the fire...
Look around for paranormal and surreal. Be careful.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 8+
    • Processor: Intel Core i5-4590
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce GTX 970 / Radeon R9 290

Just played this and had to leave the game. I was getting so sick. That is rare for me. I was too high in the seat of the car. VR users need options for everything because we have so many different physical abilities or inabilities and ages as well as different PC Specs so every game runs differently for everyone. I am giving this a thumbs down because I could not even steer the car effectively before getting very VR sick. This game wil be good I guess but until I see more I spent my money on a dud. Watch this one though. Come back after a couple updates and see what it has going for it. I am playing on HTC VIVE/GTX 970/FX 8320/16 gigs of RAM.

Early Access Review
Posted: 14 June
I like the concept, but it's too buggy for me to recommend paying anything for this yet. It looks like the dev is still learning how to bind objects to controllers. Example, I had a doll head in one hand (makes sense once you play it), and a tree branch in the other. They both got latched to the controller, and you can't drop either of them. I've ran into this same issue in Unity, and it needs to be fixed. I really want to see where they go with this, but they need to get some basics worked out first - like learning how to program the controllers correctly. Also, please fix the velicoty programming on the controllers, so when you throw something it doesn't just drop. I'm seeing a lot of mistakes I made my first couple days working in Unity. If this was free, I'd say grab it - but charging for this? No....not when they are still learning the basics of how Vive controllers even work.

Aside from that, I had no motion sickness, so that is good. I couldn't find a way to re-align the car to my chair without starting the game from the beginning, but maybe I just missed how to reset the camera ( I ended up sitting outside next to the car while driving, lol). Very cool ideas in this, and I hope they iron this stuff out. But honestly, this should be a free demo as it's currently a buggy mess with the controls.

--edit-- The dev is making key points to fix controller issues, and like other reviewers said - buyer beware for this early access game. I changed my review to recommend, because the concept of this game does seem really good and different, and they are working on the issues people have brought up. Is it worth $5 right now? That's up to you - the game is a cool experience, if you can get around some controller problems (which they are working on).
