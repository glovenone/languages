Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“As the sole developer on the project, Early Access gives me the opportunity to share the game with a wide audience to gather support and feedback as I'm creating the game. The format of the game will make it well suited to Early Access, as once the base mechanics are in place, the focus will be on releasing more and more opponents over time.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The game could realistically be in Early Access from six months to a year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The plan is to move the game out of Early Access once it has more refined fighting mechanics, many more opponents, and extra visual polish. Depending on the success of the game, custom art assets and voice-overs may be commissioned. Other features will be considered during development, such as alternate game modes.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current version has a basic tutorial and a fully playable fight against a single opponent. Fight mechanics are still being tweaked, but greatly resemble their final state.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game will likely increase in price slightly as new features and content are added.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Community feedback will be taken into consideration every step of the way. While I plan to keep a strong vision for how the game will shape up, I want to be very responsive to whether or not people playing it are enjoying themselves and feeling satisfied with the game they're playing.”

4 July

Update #1

Today's update tightens up the fighting mechanics and changes the blocking system. The base mechanics will still be tweaked based on user feedback, but are now far enough along for focus to shift to adding new opponents.

  • The blocking system has been revamped and now works simply by stopping the opponent's punches with your gloves. You can not only cover up, but also deflect and parry. Due to the increased skill required to block, the fatigue drain for blocking has been lessened slightly. Tweaks will be made to blocking hitboxes and fatigue drain based on user feedback.
  • Tightened up punching physics and hitboxes, and made opponent's reactions more responsive to the punches you throw.
  • Added new turning speed and walking mechanics for opponents
  • Brought big punch damage and little punch damage closer together, and removed the unique big punch noise.
  • Addressed easily winning fights by repeatedly throwing big punches right as the enemy begins their punch, constantly interrupting them.
  • Ugly Joe is now 25% uglier.
  • Ugly Joe's battle damage now looks more like he's hurt, and less like he wants to eat your family.
  • The tutorial has been revisited based on user feedback.

Please leave feedback on the community forums or by emailing fyiandev@gmail.com

1 July

We're Live!

Thanks to everyone for your feedback and support! We're now live!

The game is still in an extremely early state, so expect lots of changes in the short term, and hold off on purchasing if you're looking for a fuller experience or a more stable feature set.

Short term focus will be on getting the boxing mechanics just right, and then more opponents will follow after that. Visual polish will be worked in along the way.

To those who played or watched the tech demo footage, here are changes you can expect in the current Steam version:

  • You can now block! Use it judiciously, though!
  • You can now hit the enemy outside of "sweet spot" windows. Those openings will be coming back in the form of bonus damage and stun states, but only for certain enemies and certain moves.
  • There is now a fatigue system for blocking and punching into your opponent's block.
  • New weigh-in screen for height calibration
  • Players head hitbox has been tweaked to allow for easier dodging
  • Opponents now track your head a bit as they swing, so leaning back a bit doesn't beat everything anymore.
  • The tutorial and the fight with Joe have been tweaked a bit

About This Game

NOTE: The Thrill of the Fight is still under active development in a very early state. Future improvements include tightened boxing mechanics, more visual polish, more opponents, more venues, cheering crowds, and more. Currently there is only one full opponent, but more will be added once the fighting mechanics are stable enough that fights won't require large reworking. Your early support is appreciated, but if you're looking for are fuller experience, please add the game to your wishlist and wait to purchase until later in development.

The Thrill of the Fight is arcade action boxing game designed specifically for VR. Enter a virtual gym and battle unique challengers with their own boxing styles and techniques. Use timing and skill to land the knockout blow. Evade punches, land jabs, and block blows to become the king of the ring.

This is an arcade style action game with many simplified or unrealistic mechanics for the sake of fun and straightforward gameplay and is not meant to be a fully accurate recreation or simulation of real boxing.

For those concerned with "yet another early access VR title"... you are right to be. In other words, as advertised, this game is presently low on options with only 1 full opponent (not counting the training partner and the practice dummy manager). There is a lot of work ahead for the dev.

... However, let's also look at it from the POV of what we have.

- A serious fun arcadish boxing game? check
- A way decent level of presence? Check (I swear when he gets right in my face, I HAVE to back off!)
- A workout and a half in trying to down the opponent? Check!
- A dev that has already shown an ability to listen and quickly apply some upgrades to key areas? Check!
- Ugly Joe ugly enough? With the update, checkmate!

Serious fun, superior to the initial demo in every way and well worth purchasing. Some tweaks still needed to the sound department (I want to hear the opponent grunt when I land some decent shots!) and the aformentioned need for other opponents but superior boxing game is attainable here when we look at the initial effort.

Highly recommended!
