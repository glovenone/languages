About This Game

Choose and hire your doctors and employees, build the departments of your choosing, and manage your finances! Are you thrifty or a big spender? Would you rather pamper your patients instead of your banker? Make your own decisions!

You'll have many missions to accomplish, littered with all kinds of epidemics, patients with disturbing pathologies such as the zombie disease, the Chewbacca syndrome, and the moonwalk virus. Be on the lookout: Many events will spice up your rise, such as exterminating extraterrestrial germs and stopping toad contamination!
Customize your hospital down to the last details, buy high-tech equipment, choose rooms that will make your hospital center the most popular one!

Key Features

  • Use comprehensive management of your hospital to your advantage: You're the boss!
  • Build your reputation with a large variety of factors
  • Accomplish diverse and surprising missions
  • Plunge into an explosive 3D cartoon style universe with wacky pathologies and situations!
  • Experience a nonlinear adventure: Every decision will lead to different consequences, depending on when the action happens.
  • A rich system of artificial intelligence that can adapt patient behavior to each of your actions and decisions!

Hospital Manager is an even more satirical Hospital management simulator than the highly sought after nostalgic Theme Hospital from Peter Molyneux and Bullfrog Studios.

This game sets you up in a pre built hospital in which you decide which pre built rooms will be what. From diagnosis, treatment and restroom facilities.

The reason the game is satirical is you are not treating any sort of typical 'real life' ailments. You sort out Zombies, possessions and some sort of affliction in which people moonwalk whilst wearing an unconvincing Michael Jackson get up. Among many other sorts of silly ailments.

The treatments of these ailments is equally silly. If someone walks in holding their amputated leg, send them to “surgery” which is actually just a huge sewing machine. Are you a zombie? Well hypnosis is the answer (that one actually kind of makes sense if you use the colloquialism definition of Zombie in where someone is predisposed with something relatively unimportant. However the sprite is most certainly a traditional zombie.)

All of that on its face its actually an interesting premise. I don't NEED a serious hospital simulator. I just want a good one. You'll need to manage patient moods, doctors training and maintenance workers as well as clowns. However the clown is more of a middle man between doctor and patient rather than a new age Patch Adams.

Here are the main reasons why this game is not good

- You cannot build the hospital. They are all pre built and because some issues arise in which multiple types of diseases show up you need multiple types of treatment rooms however there are not enough rooms to satisfy the demand. So you must now manage changing over rooms do deal with new things. Instead of making an actual functioning hospital.

- There is no sandbox. The only mode is mission challenges.

- It gets needlessly hectic. Constant pop ups telling you things you are already working on or things you can do nothing about until you have more money.

- Staff management, research, and doctor training happens too often. You have to research diseases in order to increase the percentage your doctors will be able to diagnosis the disease properly. I had to research the zombie disease upwards of seven times in one mission just to get it at a reasonable diagnosis level to the other diseases in the hospital at the time.

- The art repeats, there are only two doctor skins. This is a minor complaint. But as the game gets so hectic so quickly it makes it much more difficult to remember who is who and what everyones level is, so that in some vain hope of trying to keep the order I attempted keep all my doctors on the same skill level. So that I don't know.. I could make a successful hospital.

THE GIST
It isn't a bad game but its far from good. The satire nature of it I'm okay with. However in the actual gameplay of the game, the 'character' it tries to maintain with sewing machine surgery tables, hypnotizing zombies and attempting séances for the downtrodden and possessed it all falls flat.

Posted: 25 March, 2015
It's difficult to give the game a thumbs up or down because it all depends upon what you are looking for in this sort of game I think. If I could give it a neutral rating, I would. But because I expect more out of my Steam games (than mere mobile ports and because seemingly, there is little to no developer attention given to this game any more, I have to give it a negative review). Don't let that dissuade from the game if you just want a quick little casual game to pass the time with however.

The game intends to be a silly, casual, pc port of the not so well received mobile version (the mobile version is riddled with negative ratings due to stability, bug, and lack of developer attention on issues). https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.cccp.manager.java&hl=en

On with the nitty gritty....

PROS
  • Decent graphics
  • Quirky dialog and diseases
  • Fun to focus the attention where you like via your research room (Lab)
  • While you don't have room dimension control, you do have complete control over the hallways/lobby areas
  • No bugs or stability issues that ruined the mobile version, however there is at least 1 instance of incomplete text (text_placeholder) in a description of an item.
  • No Microtransactions (like there are in the mobile game)


CONS
  • It's a direct mobile port. While microtransactions were taken out, not much else was done to make it more of a pc game (but then, most mobile ports don't make that sort of effort anyway). One of the issues that turned me off was that you have to click "bubbles" to receive money (from machines) and fix the moods of some patients. It's obviously intended for finger tapping, but you use your mouse/pointer instead.
  • It's not optimzed for PC. In the later levels when you have A LOT of patients swarming your hospital, it can decrease your FPS quite a bit. I can play high end graphic games all day without issue yet with this little "app" my fps decreases to the point that trying to accurately hit those bubbles can be quite frustrating.
  • There is a pause feature, regular speed, and fast speed. Because so much can quickly happen on the map at once, it would be nice to have a "slow speed." Without it, it can be pretty hectic and I hate having to pause just to catch up.
  • You can decide what type of room goes into which room slot, but your room slots on each map are already decided for you. This means no customization in as far as room size or actual placement, you are limited to the map's layout itself.

It's important to understand that this is not a business sim. It isn't intended to be one. It would be like saying that the mobile app Airport Controller (https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=dk.logisoft.aircontrol&hl=en) is intended to be an air traffic controler simulator. No...Hospital Manager is an arcade game with the backdrop of a hospital. It's all "finger placement" and "finger tap" but with a mouse for the PC version.

This might be more fun on a tablet, but then you'd probably be buying the seemingly abandoned mobile version of the game.

CONCLUSION

If you want a casual, arcade game with a hospital as the backdrop, and don't mind the low fps at times when things get hectic, go for it. If you are expecting something more sophisticated like an actual simulator or even something with more options/features/customization such as Theme Hospital, then you probably will be disappointed and should stay away.
