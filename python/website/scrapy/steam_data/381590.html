About This Game

Shred! is created entirely by just one guy with a severe mountain bike and video game addiction...Just like the real thing, the action is fast paced and utterly relentless from start to finish! With over 30 courses to ride and two Bike Parks, you can rest assured that every inch of trail is carefully designed to keep you on the absolute ragged edge!

Features

  • AAA rendering effects and massive increase in visual fidelity over mobile versions!
  • NEW - First person camera angle takes intensity to a new level!
  • "Flowy" level design makes for an authentic, fun and very satisfying game-play experience!
  • Over 30 gnarly trails inspired by real world locations, events and famous video segments!
  • Compete in Downhill, Enduro and Freeride events!
  • Realistic bike and character physics!
  • Xbox Gamepad support!
  • Cinematic & dynamic camera angles!
  • Fun, bonus levels inspired by Mountain Bike culture!
  • Brutal crashes!
  • Made by a Mountain Biker for Mountain Bikers (and everyone else too)!

If there ever was a miniclip.com clone of trials extreme, this would be it. I remember playing these sort of games for free online so it's a bit of a wonder how it ended up in my library. Is it worth full price? In my opinion no, but it would be worth a second look on sale. This game is OK but do not expect anything beyond what you see. I will recommend it but if you don't find it appealing watching the videos, I doubt you'll find it fun while playing.

Shred! is a linear side to side 2D sort of platformer scroller in which the player is in control of a cyclist attempting to meet goal demands by either doing tricks or getting a lot of speed and air. There are two views to choose from : first person and 3rd person from an angled side view. First person is pretty awesome but some will be disappointed in it's function. In first person view, you may be tempted into turning to the sides to explore the track but reluctantly, you'll flipping your bicycle instead. This is sort of bad because the game isn't meant to be open world but good because your obeying the laws of the game (meaning this is how it's function is based). Most likely you'll be playing in third person view in order to properly land your flips and tricks. The physics in game seem to play out properly once you get the hang of them. Holding down "A" allows you to charge up your jump. In order to maximize it, let it go at a certain point approaching a ramp. The timing is a bit tricky but becomes sort of a fun challenge throughout the game. Using your momentum right and timing your jumps correctly brings the game up to a good speed. Once you hit this ideal speed, it's time to throw in some flips and some tricks. Tricks are done with the right joystick and are relatively easy to do and enjoyable to watch. Unfortunately when you master this, there is not a whole lot more to do than memorizing the workings of the courses. Then, it becomes easy. There are certain courses that will give you a challenge but doing the same thing over and over again may get to you. However, there are those that will find (as they unlock more courses) that they enjoy the challenge. Then again, there are those who will dislike the fact you have a button to make your player peddle up a hill that slows the game down.

The graphics are decent (at best) in the game. There are some nice backdrops (especially in first person) but nothing extremely out of the ordinary. Sounds are okay but aren't exactly diverse. Music is okay but suitable to the game.

In summary, it's an average game. In my own opinion, I believe games like this should be free simply due in part that I played games like this for free online when I was a kid. I find it increasingly odd what people can charge (but hey, if someone's buying it then it all makes sense).

Take the game for what it is, a budget title created by a single indie developer. No it's not going to win any awards for graphical fidelity, yes there are going to be some physics bugs. Given the low price of the game I think these factors can be overlooked especially when you consider how fun the game can actually be, as another reviewer has pointed out it has that "one more go" mentality, you can attempt a short amount of time with the game, dive in and do a few runs quickly before you realise an hour has gone past.
The tracks themself have a good "flow" to them, providing you hit the right landings etc it's as if your line is an extension of one motion, hit them wrong and it's pretty unforgiving, there's lots of possibilities and replayability in just getting that "line" right on each trail.
I think the game complements the Trials series pretty well and its apparent that it provided some inspiration for the title, it's not as polished nor is there as much content but for the price again that can be forgiven.
I have no hesitation in recommending the game providing you purchase it with the right attitude, it's definitely far from a AAA title but as a fun time distraction it serves up quite well.
Looking forward to see what the developer brings to the table in the future.
