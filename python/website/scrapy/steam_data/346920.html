About This Game

Undead Shadows is a fast paced action zombie shooter that keeps players on their toes and tests their survival instinct. You are a survivor in the harsh post apocalyptic world looking for any means of survival against never ending waves of zombies from sun up to sun down – and even worse in between.

You can scour the city and country side looking for weapons, food and other survival items to help you stay alive just 1 wave longer. You can play with up to 3 other people over multiplayer or over a local LAN for co-op.

Each level gives the players access to an array of weapons to help fend off the fast moving zombies and the deadly giant spiders that creep up on you when the sun goes down. Everything from a head-exploding shotgun, to high powered sniper rifles as well as machine guns and rocket launchers to mow down hordes at a time.

As you kill zombies they drop money for you to pickup and use to upgrade your firepower and build defenses to help slow down the waves. Each level features a hardware store with access to wood and barbed wire to allow players to create blockades and defenses to keep zombies out and players alive for just a little while longer.

If you’re lucky, a dead zombie will drop a money bag which gives you enough funds to purchase higher powered weapons; Or they may drop the rare but very powerful airstrike radio which gives the picking up player access to a one time strike from 2 supersonic F16’s that drop highly advanced weapons that kill all zombies that are alive at the time.

With leaderboards and statistics tracking, Undead Shadows allows you to compete with your friends for bragging rights on the highest number of waves you’ve lasted as well as how many kills and headshots you’ve posted on the Undead.

I don't understand why this game exists, and I don't even mean for that to sound harsh. I got this in a bundle and read reviews, and it sounded so awful that I wanted to be apart of it, knowing full well why I shouldn't. But it was a zombie wave game, there's not much originality left in it, but I suppose someone could come along and change the game. It's been done before, and it sure as hell can be done again.

So, right away, I have to make an account. Wait wait wait, pump the brakes. For what? The multiplayer is broken right now, what in the world do I need an account for? Well, leap of faith. Account made. Let's just configure this thing and... the dropdown box for "effects" has no values in it. That's... concerning. But, giving it a benefit of a doubt, right? Let's play a game then, 2 maps to choose from. Okay, maybe replayability makes it fun? Maybe it's randomized, that would be cool. I learned later that, no, it really wouldn't. Within the first seconds of the game, I realized what I had gotten myself into. The graphics, while very bad, could have been worse. After all, I'm usually not one to judge by looks alone, so I decided to try and make a good run.

Crowbar in hand, I use the old training method where you run in a line and small circles to gather up the zombies. Great, it works, so at least the AI is decent? Wrong. Turns out, you can just walk in a straight line, look back and m1 all of the zombies to death. Okay, but there's a lot of them, surely this method wouldn't last forever? Again, wrong. The only reason why I didn't put an engagement ring on that method is purely because it takes forever, seeing as though they add more zombies as you progress through the rounds. As I was trying to find a place to stay still, I realized I never tried jumping on an object, like a car. So I did. And I stayed there the entire game.

The reason why I chose this foolproof situation is because the AI is so bad, they'll clump up together in an effort to grab you even though they can't, and you can just crouch and whack them with whatever tool they started you out with. My actual gamewinning strategy was to run to the gunstore, buy a rocketlauncher, jump on the barricades via other platforms, and began the simple process of gather, shoot, collect, repeat. Every other item was rendered useless with this method. Flashlight or nightvision? Why, when you can just turn the gamma up. Health? You're not even being hit, why waste your time? ATV or other vehicle? That perch is now your life, no vehicle can change that.

I could have gone an infinite amount of rounds that way, but the glitches got the best of me. T-posing zombie models floating around, zombies getting caught on objects and moonwalking in random locations, only moving when I shot at them, and the gamebreaking spiders. There are big old hairy spiders in this game, and they're the most useless wastes of polygons. At one point, a spider froze in a parking lot and I had to kill it to advance to the next round. I apparently didn't get that it was an omen, so I thought nothing of it and resumed, only for a spider (I assume) later to freeze somewhere hidden that I was unable to find, making it impossible to progress anymore through the game.

Would I have kept on playing hadn't the big ♥♥♥ spider bugged (huehue) out? Maybe, but only because the (broken) leaderboard of the (broken) multiplayer aspect said that the highscore was 19, and I was already on 12 with 100% health. I wanted to like this game, mostly because I paid for it, and it just didn't happen. If you're actually reading this and on the fence about buying it, please don't. And if you're the developer of this game, why did you do this. You have plenty of time to fix this, I assume. I understand if you're not the best at making games, and this was just a fling, but this is kind of insulting. People spent money on this, and I feel like you don't care about that at all. If you did, you would make a patch trying to fix everything wrong with this game, but deep down we all know you're not gonna do that.

Undead Shadows is a wavebased zombie-and-other-monsters-from-minecraft-deflection simulator by Ignition Games.

The video review is here(warning! explicit langauge):
http://youtu.be/fHKVGo0-jPs

To make it short: I have found 18 reasons for you to not buy this game that are explained more elongated in the video. Here I will keep myself short and simply list those reasons:

1. The game has No Controllersupport even though it features partial controller support on the store page.

2. There are pretty much no steam features integrated into that game.

3. Because of reason number two, they have an own online service, that sends out no confirmation mail when registering... dogdy.

4. This game needs an internet connection for a singleplayer game with a multiplayer component. It's no always online system, but that doesn't make it any better.

5. The game has no FOV slider! Luckily for them the standard FOV is 90, so the problem is not that bad.

6. There is almost no variety in the game. You have two enemy types, two maps, 7 weapons and 4 building items... not impressive.

7. It is ressource intensive(~1.5-1.8 GB of RAM in singleplayer) for no good reason.

8. The game features an atrociousely dumb AI. Enemies can't even walk up ramps!

9. Due to reason number 8 it happens that zombies can get stuck anywhere and the duration of a round gets dragged out immensely.

10. The system of Airstrikes is a borked, random, overpowered thing that has no good reason to exist.

11. The game is highly illogical. Explosives don't hurt you and you need to buy everything in a zombie infested area... from whom exactly?

12. The game performs very badly. The framerate frequently drops below 30 on a system with superior hardware than the recommended specs!

13. The Texture Quality is abyssmal.

14. The gunplay is way below COD-level of simplicity.

15. The Building mode UI and handling is horrendous.

16. The game crashes often. Additionally it features no clean releasing of resources.

17. The game doesn't save stats when leaving game without dying.

18. The game is really easy. Either camping or backpaddling away from zombies is the way to go!

Do yourself a favor Buy Yet Another Zombie Defense or Killing Floor and you spend your money better than that.
