About This Game

Freshman Year is a vignette game about an ordinary night in the life a college freshman named Nina. Nina's best friend Jenna invites her to meet up at a local bar with some classmates. Jenna is sort of flaky though, so Nina has to navigate the party by herself. You play as Nina, texting with her friend Jenna as you mingle, dance, drink and wait.

Please be aware that this game depicts scenarios that may be distressing to people who have experienced abuse.

Freshman Year was developed by designer Nina Freeman, with art by Laura Knetzger and music by Stephen Lawrence Clark.

This game is autobiographical, based on the experience of designer Nina Freeman.

Please also check out the Steam page for "how do you Do It?", another autobiographical vignette game from a team including the designer of Freshman Year.

Freshman Year

"Plot"(?)

Freshman Year follows a female college student named Nina in her first year of college, as the title indicates. Her closest friend Jenna invites her out to a local bar in hopes of breaking Nina out of her introverted and sheltered shell she had built herself into. Jenna, being the party girl she is, is unresponsible to Nina's texts as she arrives onto the agreed meeting place and almost scares her into thinking she would flake out, forcing her to take action into her own hands. Before Nina could enter the club, she encounters the "attractive" (As Nina puts it) bouncer that stops her and asks her a few nosy questions before she is let through to enter. After Nina allievates her self destructive behaviour, she walks outside with her phone in hand, still expecting a text from Jenna. The bouncer approaches her again, but this time has other things in mind. He pins her against the corner of some alley and begins to feel Nina up, molesting her while holding her arms into a locked position. Jenna arrives onto the scene to break it up and takes Nina inside, Jenna begins to explain how the bouncer molested more of Jenna's friends and how it was irritating having to deal with him. As Nina finishes grasping her face in "trauma" she looks up into the mirror to discover Jenna trotting away back onto the dance floor.

Conclusion

In the end, this is only a short story, it's free and you shouldn't expect much from it. The whole purpose of this is to raise awareness for sexual harassment I believe, I also think this actually happened to the creator once. Ultimately I believe the message barely hits home, as it's trying to portray the fact (or the creator's opinion) that nobody cares about harassment when it's hardly true.
