Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Story-driven VR in a roomscale environment is a new form of content and technically it's a tough challenge, so we want to make it right for anybody who comes in... even grandma! "La Péri" is already a very enjoyable experience but we really hope to get valuable feedback from the community, in order to have a perfectly polished and a flawless release.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We expect it to be in Early Access for 2 months but this could change based on the feedback we get from our community.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Areas we're focusing on :
- Addingreplay value with small interactive features based on actions of the users in order to give a better feeling of impact on the story and environment
- Overall look will be improved (effects and environmental details will be embellished)
- Main mechanic will be refined (particularly through feedbacks based on the actions of the user)
- Overall sound design will be improved, and voice-over will be partially re-recorded for better understandability of the background story
- More animations might be added, and the current set will be polished
- Transitions will be shortened and visually improved”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Story, mood, structure, main interactions are settled, but some mechanics, pace of the experience, and visuals still need work. We estimate that we're 85% there !”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“At this time, we do not expect to change the price before full launch.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We really want to involve the community, and leverage all the feedback we can to help us shape a better and more meaningful experience. So, please feel free to reach us, and we will do our best to incorporate your suggestions into the product.

The Vive is a cool device you want to show around. So, don't hesitate to try the experience with your friends and family, we would love to know what they think of it! Help us to create an innovative yet very solid experience that will stay a reference, more than just a quick tech demo. At Innerspace, our goal is to pave the way to the future of storytelling in VR.

"La Péri" is also the first chapter of something bigger and your support may give us the resources to write the next pages, and give life to the Fantasia of VR!”

About This Game

“La Péri” is a short (10-15') narrative-driven experience based on classical music, ballet performance captured with mocap and exclusively made for roomscale VR. It relies a lot on interactions of the hand controllers, to make us able to play directly with the character and feel a deep sense of presence. The HTC VIVE headset allowed us to create an experience where anyone will be able to feel true presence on stage... The viewer is no longer a distant observer, but lives the choreography from the inside and can appreciate a dancer's performance as never before.

In this experience , choreography, visual processing, music and story-line will merge into one, producing an extraordinary audio-visual experience.

On the stage, you play the role of Iskender, a prince seeking the "flower of immortality". This precious artifact is guarded by la Péri, an ancient spirit. As you gradually fit into your role and try to take possession of the flower, the spirit appears. Alternately frightened and mischievous, it evolves into different living forms that increase in complexity until it sketches a graceful, feminine silhouette…

Early Access Review
Posted: 24 June
If you are into the more experience based titles, this may be for you. La Peri is very much in the same vein as The Blu, the apolo experience etc.

Pros:
- There wasn't any point where I felt confused with my task, although there is minimal interaction the game does make you feel involved, and succeeds in putting you not just front and center, but in the middle of a ballet experience.
- If you really indulge in the story it can be quite captivating. The narration is excellent.
- Frame rates and performance was great on my gtx970, game seems well optimized.
- There was a good balance between having your feet on the ground and parts where you'd experience virtigo.

Cons:
- The flashlight was annoying, I really just wanted it off for most of the experience and wish I could disable it. Making sure I had it behind my back took a bit away from the experience.
- Wish there were more human dancers, maybe another scene with two dancers or an extra scene... the animals were amazing, but the dancer... WOW... I want more of that!
- Scale seems slightly off with regards to the models, I felt the dancer was too small by 5-10 %.
- Too short. I'm not particularly frugal, but I feel like it could have been longer for my $10 spent. That being said, this caters to a smaller demographic in an already 'petite' VR community... so $10 isn't that absurd if it means I'm supporting these devs.
- End of the game had no prompts... just went black. I didn't know if there was more coming or if it had glitched... just black.
- All of the above is me being nit-picky, if this was twice as long of an experience, it would be perfect IMO.

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 April
I will admint, when I first got my Vive I was simply... whelmed. Not overwhelmed, not underwhelmed, but whelmed - lukewarm. I had concerns about resolution, clarity, and comfort. Were my lighthouses setup right? Is this going in the drawer in a week? I was really starting to doubt my purchase.

Then I loaded up La Peri. Like another reviewer, I didn't buy this game for me - I figured that ballet would be fun for my daugher. But being a responsible parent, I wanted to experience it first and make sure there wasn't anything that was going to jump scare her or be a problem.

My preconceived notions have never been quite this wrong. This "experience" is a totally new medium. This isn't a play, or a ballet, or even interactive theater. This is theater of the real. In every way, the neurons in my brain believed 110% that I was standing on the stage. Looking over the cliff. Watching the dancer whirl around me.

The environments are breathtaking. The flashlight seems out-of-place at first, but I quickly realized that it only further sold my brain on the reality of what I was seeing. I spent 5 minute examining a scultpure with my eyes, my flashlight, and my physical positioning. I held my breath as the dancer whooshed through me. Felt real empathy when she was in peril. Real thought behind the game's final choice.

The medium of VR storytelling was born this week. A completely new way to communicate and express art just came into being. And La Peri is an excellent first example of what that medium is capable of. After experiencing this performance, I had to sit down and be introspective for about 10 minutes just to regain my emotional grounding. Yes, it's that good.

Stop reading this review and buy it. Enjoy the first coming of the new VR art form. Transport yourself to a world far away from where you're physically standing.

Bravo, La Peri. Merci.
