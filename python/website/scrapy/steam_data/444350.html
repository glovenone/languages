About This Game

HACK_IT presents players with all sorts of hacking tasks, from sniffing passwords to taking down servers to even drawing money from accounts. Breach your way into user accounts and explore the unknown.

Key Features:

  • Narrative Hacking Experience
  • Interactive Terminal
  • Unique Hacking Tools
  • Characters
  • Attack Servers
  • Keylog Passwords
  • Interactive Desktop Missions

Progressive Hacking:
Missions will progress and get more complex as you learn new methods of execution.

Desktop Missions:
Get the full immersive experience with desktop view on some missions, open apps and use them with ease.

Hacking Executable:
HACK_IT is loaded with loads of hacking applications from money loggers to password fetchers, the hacking network has the tool for your needs.

Built For Steam:
The game has been designed with Steam Integration, packed with achievements and some trading cards.

My Impression:
I love hacking sims and am proficient in real life penetration testing. I own and loved uplink and hacknet (hacknet being the best hacking sim I have played). I went into this with an open mind. This is more of a visual novel than an actual game. There are minigames that are either too easy or make no sense in the context of the game.

Positive:
+ Achievements
+ Steam trading cards

Negatives:
- Price for what you are getting, worth $0.99
- Minigames that make no sense, dream sequences
- Unrealistic environment, most of your time is spent clicking next. The desktop environment is an area clickable region, meaning you can click away from a folder, but it still opens the folder.
- You just click buttons like: download, fix virus, etc. Not much thought or gameplay involved.
- Story? There really isn't one. It uses buzz words to sound hacker-ish like script kiddie, keylogging, etc
- Extremely tedious and boring with unnecessary screen effects
- Generic files, fix_it to fix a virus, let's hack a machine and send ourselves money, not realistic at all
- Overall, poor execution on every front of a video game: gameplay, sound, graphics, etc

Bad Game:
2/10

If you are expecting anything close to real hacking or a game like hacknet, you will be disappointed. Not worth the price, and not even worth the sale unless its $0.99 or less and you are a huge fan of the genre
