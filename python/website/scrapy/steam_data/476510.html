About This Game

It's time for Cally to do what she does best: rescuing her kidnapped parents!


Set before the events of Cally's Caves 3, Cally's Trials is a roguelike action-platformer with a weapon-levelling system that lets you evolve weapons as you use them. Cally must save her parents from the maniacal Herbert, who has laid waste to the Caves and built a massive lair deep inside.
Herbert's experiments have twisted the caves into a living nightmare, and our plucky six-year-old heroine must overcome staggering odds:

  • 110 levels packed with vile monsters to fight across 6 biomes
  • 6 insane bosses, including Mama Bear's first appearance, and Carl IV's Dad.
  • 12 weapon classes to level up, use your weapons and they grow stronger, every once in a while they evolve into a new form with new effects!
  • Item/Powerup Store to buy permanent upgrades for Cally
  • 38 song soundtrack
  • roguelike structure means earning money on every run and buying upgrades is crucial to success

Cally's Trials is a mix of roguelike and RPG, with a "Level Up Everything" sensibility. Witness the beginning of Cally's journey from average 6-year-old girl, to fearless warrior and Conqueror of the Caves!

Cally's Caves is a series about a girl who looks like Penny from Inspector Gadget, who has to rescue her parents. From now on, I'll just call Cally Penny. Hey Penny! Uh, nice games you got there! It's too bad that you can't swim since your parents never taught you swimming lessons. As you level up from collecting blue orbs, you'll become more of a damage sponge.

Seriously, you won't believe how easy this game is. Keep in mind that unlike Cally's Caves 3, when you die, you go back to the beginning without a lamp. Stage hazards will leave you with 1 HP left. You can buy warps that send you directly to the later chapters.

The enemies in this game are just amazing. Most of the baddies incorporate the same strategy of pacing back and forth. The slimes like to spawn at random positions, bouncing up and down in a weird way. Another enemy includes a generic stationary turret, skeletons and heavy bazooka guys that also like to pace back and forth and throw projectiles towards you. You can parry these enemy projectiles with your sword, which can also lock enemies in place.

Our first Boss, Mama Bear even uses the same movement as enemies like the bears, dire wolves and knife bandits. You can simply stand on one platform, and use the shotgun weapon (a la Contra), and she'll never touch you. One interesting note is that you can even escape during the boss fights. Are you low on health, and need to farm for health refills? Good news! You can back out of that boss fight, and go kill enemies and hope that they drop health items. In fact, I've beaten the first three bosses on my first try.

It might be just me, but the Super Arrow thingy would often steal my first jump, making it look like I'm already airborne. Sometimes it looks like I would touch the Supper Arrow, but the collision doesn't register. There have been occasions when apparently if I press jump and shoot at the same time, I would vertically stick between two solid objects until I press jump again.

All of the achievements in this game consist of killing certain amounts of enemies, and in less than 2 days I've already obtained all 32 of them. Mash the shotgun weapon, kill enemies, reload room, rinse and repeat. Pretty much every scene has you clearing the way of enemies with no moving platforms to worry about. The game is too repetitive to justify the $6.99 price tag.

Are you tired of complex platforming? Would you like to deal with incompetent enemies? Would you like a game that rewards you for mashing? Are you looking for a platform game with a female protagonist? If any of these fit your needs, then this game is for you.

Portable Description
Callyâs Trials is the updated, roguelite version of Callyâs Caves 2 that was released for free on the iOS Appstore, Google Play, and the Amazon Appstore. Permanent character and weapon upgrades, used alongside a leveling system, fuels this cute action-platformer through 110 levels of monster-packed mayhem.

Story
Callyâs Narrative
Set before the events of Cally's Caves 3, Herbert imprisons Callyâs parents in his massive lair deep inside the Caves. It is up to the young child to fight through Herbertâs nightmarish creations and rescue those she holds dear. Lloyd, a knowledgeable guitarist and occasionally helpful companion, aids Cally in her quest.

Gameplay
Roguelite Structure
Callyâs Trials utilizes a roguelite structure â entire game must be completed in one continuous run due to âperma-deathâ (shortcuts can be unlocked). Avoiding the roguelike vs. roguelite semantics, the core mechanics and features defining Trials include:

  • âPerma-deathâ (sometimes an area/zone reset instead of a full reset upon game over).
  • Character and weapon leveling system that does not reset upon death.
  • Currency that does not reset upon death.
  • Permanently unlocked character abilities/health: triple jump, damage boost, etc.
  • Unlockable/purchasable shortcuts to each subsequent zone upon defeating the previous boss.

Callyâs World
Trials is a relatively vast world, containing 110 levels across 6 different zones each with a unique biome (water, ice, fire themes etc.). Callyâs move-set includes 2-D movement, jump, double/triple jumps, weapon fire, and sword swings (melee). Typical platformer elements are present, including: ledges, cliffs, stationary spikes, ranged/melee enemies, platforms, and triggered events. Regrettably, each level is not procedurally generated; upon death and resetting, the player will be re-creating the exact same experience trying to get back to the previous point before dying. Enemy and coin placements are pre-determined. Loot chests, however, do award random items. Although repetitive at times, the levels are fortunately not too long and getting back to the death location should not be too difficult.

Roguelites/roguelikes traditionally employ full resets upon death. Although Callyâs Trials follows the same principles, it alleviates some of the mental anguish with such a system by using partial resets depending upon the manner of death. If Cally perishes by falling into water/spikes or succumbs to a boss, she will reset at the beginning of that particular level, instead of the beginning of the entire game, with a portion of her health missing.

After defeating a zoneâs boss (6 zones/biomes/bosses total), Cally has the option to purchase a shortcut bypassing that zone. To utilize the shortcut, Cally must have the triple jump ability unlocked. The coins required to purchase these shortcuts will require a little bit of grinding in addition to a normal play through by the time a boss is defeated.

Combat
Cally fends herself with ranged attacks in the form of firing one of her guns, or with short melee attacks in the form of a sword swing. Both actions may be performed simultaneously. Callyâs guns may be leveled up by continually using them (an experience bar indicating weapon usage is provided). Leveling up her guns have the possibility to increase: damage output, fire rate, accuracy or appearance. Specific gun levels act as milestones where a gunâs physical appearance and bullet animations get upgraded.

Guns
There are 12 weapon classes to level up (pistol, shotgun, assault rifle, etc.) each specializing dominance in certain situations. Some of the main differences between each weapon class include: bullet damage, bullet scatter or projectile trajectory. Changing between weapons are instant which is a very helpful technique when jumping into a group of enemies. Although the fire button can be held down to automate shooting, doing so will lower the gunâs fire rate. There is also infinite ammo.

Sword
Callyâs sword protects her when enemies get too close for comfort. Unlike her guns, Callyâs sword progresses differently. Sword upgrades do not follow a leveling system, but instead must be purchased. Her sword also has the ability to knock away most projectiles, handy for when sneaky shots are incoming.

Character Progression
Starting at level 1 with 3 hearts (each damage hit removes 1 heart), Cally progresses by leveling up. At each level up, Cally will fully heal herself, occasionally gaining another heart container. XP in order to level up is gained in the form of loot, dropped from defeated enemies. These loot drops may fall into the water or off of a cliff, so timing a kill is rather important. Lloydâs shop sells other useful items/abilities (permanent and single-use) to help Cally with her progression. Some of these include: coin multiplier, damage boost, level up, sword enhancements, etc.

Controls
Controller Support
Although Callyâs Trials has full controller support, the shoulder buttons generally will not be used for anything, and the D-pad is used by default for character movement. At the moment, it isnât possible to re-assign the button mapping for a controller, while using only a controller (DS3 used for this review). This makes it impossible to rebind movement to the analog sticks.

Conclusion
Possible Exploits
There are a couple, possibly unintended, âexploitsâ that may need to be addressed listed below.

  • It is possible to continuously re-enter a level in order to repeatedly open a chest, skewing the expected gold-gain trend. Possible fixes to this include allowing a chest to only be opened once per life, or removing the ability to travel to a previous level/zone.
  • After entering the shortcut for Zone 2, it is possible to go back and repeatedly kill Mama Bear. The issue is that it is too easy to defeat her this way because her back is turned. This also allows the player to easily level up the newly acquired assault rifle in Zone 3.

Advised Suggestions
There are a couple suggestions that would improve gameplay; these are listed below.

  • Allow camera control to a certain extent in order to see depth of a fall/ledge location or to time a long-double jump.
  • Change game over screens to differentiate between water death vs full death.
  • Make Lloyd's tips optional, or present it as a hovering dialogue box instead of an abrupt screen change.
  • Add achievements for defeating bosses: all current achievements are based upon the number of eliminated enemies.
  • Procedural level generation (if possible).

Verdict
With everything taken into consideration, Callyâs Trials is definitely recommended to fans of the action-platformer or roguelite genres. It is very important to note that Trials will periodically feel a bit repetitive at times, but progression is really fast and simple.
