About This Game

In this classic space-shooter, you take the role of a medical nanobot and embark on an exciting journey into the depths of a biological cell. Evade the defense tactics of the other cells and try to survive for as long as possible.

Features:
  • Eleven different versus modes (e.g. lasso, froghop, worms)
  • Play online against thousands of real opponents
  • Comprehensive tournament mode and demanding coop mode
  • Improve the ship´s capabilities with various bonuses
  • Adaption of the XBLA-hit

System Requirements

    • Processor: 1.5 GHz
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 128 MB RAM
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Sound Card: Windows DirectSound compatible sound card
    • Additional Notes: Mouse, Keyboard

Biology Battle; originally released for XBIG in 2008, makes it's debut on Steam.

As a huge fan of twin stick gaming, I hold high standards when it comes to this genre game, and Biology Battle didn't disappoint me. Ported nicely, the tight controls and varity of weapons, upgrades, and power ups make this twin stick shooter an enjoyable arcade like experience, where you'll be playing "just one more time" just to "one-up" your friends, or yourself.

Sure there are a lot of twin stick games that are "musts" over this one but if you are looking for something new, or an enthusiest of twin stick gaming, or just need some cheap arcade action, this is a great buy. It takes the best of other games from this genre, combines them into one nice neat little package, and adds it's own flavor at a budget price.

Game modes are limited but this does offer local co-op for up to 4 players and definately makes

Overview
  • Game Play: Acrade Twin Stick Shooter
  • Graphic: Decent - BB offers 2 different camera modes. Your typical top down or 3D which gives it a Mutant Storm type view.
  • Sound: The music and sound is really where this game lacks. When you look at other intense twin sticks like Ultratron, Geo Wars, Echoes+... they always have this driving Techno sound track that makes the game that much more intense. Sound effects are just okay but that mono-tone chicks voice (announcer) gets a little annoying, and quickly had to be adjusted to a volume level of "0".
  • Controls: Solid, tight, twin stick controls - when you die, you know its your fault.
  • Replayability: Yes. - Though, I find myself only able to play a game here and there, as it is not a game you are going to sit and play for hours on end.
  • Overall: For what its worth; 8/10.

Yes, this game is limited with play modes but that is compensated with it's price. Overall it's a fun shooter that you will pick up again and again.

You are a medical nanobot, stuck in a biological cell, destroying .... I don't know... bacteria ?
As you can see, I'm not great at biology and I'm not great at twin-stick shooters, either.

Even though I'm not a huge fan of the genre, I think Biology Battle is pretty enjoyable. It does not do anything out of the ordinary, though; Shoot enemies, get powerups, unlock skills and don't get yourself killed. The controls are a bit wonky sometimes and a controller is highly recommended.

Fans of twin-stick shooters, will probably have fun playing this game but there are a few reasons why you might not want to get Biology Battle.
For instance, I could imagine you want to compare your scores to others. Too bad, the leaderboards are only local. Even though, there is a global leaderboard, I do not think it works (unless I'm the only one on the planet, playing this game). There are also no achievements, which is not a big deal for everyone but it certainly does not help for replay value.

However, the worst part in this game would be the false advertisement "play online against thousands of real opponents". Perhaps, I just did not know how but whenever I tried to play online it simply did not connect me to anyone else (if this feature DOES work, I will correct this here). Besides, I do not think thousands of people are actually playing this game.

So yeah, Overall 'Biology Battle' is not a bad game but you probably will get bored with it soon unless you are a real hardcore twin-stick shooter.
