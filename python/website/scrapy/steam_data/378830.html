This is an open-world RPGMaker-based game where the protagonist Aerin Goldheart signed a contract with the demon of desire Izdul Kalag to make her husband Tommy Wocrome love her with all his heart. Things didn't turn out well as planned and Aerin has to travel through the world with her companions and defeat 5 different demons, each representing a different dark part of the princess to be able to break out of her curse and clear her husband's possession.

For an open-world RPG, the game is relatively easy to finish, with an estimated playtime of around 15 hours and doesn't require much grinding for experience levels or loot drops. The combat is simple and very consistent, with every character using the same weapon from start to end of game and only one optional jewellery slot to protect against a special negative effect or to enhance a certain combat ability. There are very few instances of failed or missed combat abilities or magic not working on allies or enemies that have no special resistance to that form of attack. The only con is the scaling up of prices for potions and elixirs with character level, which reduces the motivation to grind for loot and hoard 99 units of potions or elixirs. Among the side-quests available in the game, they are short and all can be completed without too much travelling or grinding for specific high-quality mob loot.

The graphics and soundtracks are great, it would have been nice if the game allows the take screenshot button on Steam to work. The deluxe edition is recommended when at discount as it includes the OST and the strategy guide which is needed to finish certain side-quests that require travelling to a specific area.

Very good, but rather short JRPG. The music in this little gem is really great. One of the best soundtracks I've listened to in a while. The graphics are rather standard for an RPG Maker game, but some effects and a distinct set of tiles for each location creates a pleasant to look at game. The story isn't very deep, but it's well written and gives enough background.

The gameplay isn't without flaws though. There's the odd auto leveling of prices for inns and items. I guess it's meant to balance out the higher rewards you get from stronger enemies, but it just feels wrong. The other thing that is worth mentioning is the 'streamlined' equipment. There's only one accessory slot to equip something and the selection of equipable items only covers protection against the various status effects, more HP or MP and a small boost to attack. There's only one special accessory that you can get for completing a side quest. At least two slots and a bit more variation could have helped here.

The enemy variety and the boss fights are again very good. You won't find any 'recolored' enemies in this game. Each area/dungeon has its unique set of enemies and you will need a bit of strategy to get past them. The overall difficulty isn't very high, but it's not a casual game either. Make sure you save up enough potions before you enter any boss fights.

The game is only 15 hours long (including all side quests) and there's not that much to discover outside of the main path. A good amount of that time is grinding for levels as the lack of equipment forces you to make up for it by leveling your characters. It doesn't get too boring though even if the dungeons don't hold any surprises. You hunt for a switch or two and figure out which paths are dead ends. That's pretty much it. Still the game doesn't feel boring because the dungeons don't drag on too much and as mentioned above each location feels unique.

Overall it's a very solid JRPG with a bit wonky gameplay mechanics, but a beautiful soundtrack, a good variety of locations and enemies and an entertaining story. Well worth the 5 bucks.

The Princess' Heart is the latest game by RosePortal Games to come on Steam and just like its predecessors, Sweet Lily Dreams and Whisper of a Rose, it's definitely worth buying and playing. I had a chance to beta test this game, too, so I can convey a pretty good idea of what it's like.

Unlike RosePortal's other games, The Princess' Heart doesn't take place in the same universe as those games. That's why the feeling from this game is quite different from those. For me, I found the gameplay to be somewhat more challenging but then that could be because it did take some adjusting on my part. There is only one difficulty level to play so you have to take that into account when battling monsters, bosses, and whatnot. There are aspects of this game that I did like. Unlike SLD and WoaR, TPH (The Princess' Heart-I'll use abbreviations to make references much easier to type) has more party members available to you as you progress through the game. In SLD and WoaR, you were pretty much stuck to four party members. However, in TPH, you can have a total of 8 party members. Of course, you can only have 4 members battle at a time so it's up to you to choose which ones will do the battling for you. I tend to have a preference for certain types of party members so I tend to stick to the same 4 (for the most part). However, like in other RosePortal Games, this one does have quests and once you complete quests, you earn experience points which benefits everyone, regardless of whether they're in your active party or not. Also, upon leveling up, instead of automatically learning a new spell, you earn journey points (JP). Using them, you can choose what spells for your members to learn so it's important to choose wisely.

Overall, I found the game to be quite fun and challenging at the same time. The storyline is pretty good and there is character growth for the main character, which I find to be a positive aspect when it comes to narrating a story. I'd definitely recommend giving The Princess' Heart a try and adding it to your Steam library. ^^

Sidequests...only a very few. Most of them are bring me x-items...and the rewards are not worth the trouble.
Of course your characters can learn skills. After you level up and solving one of the quests you get points you can spend
for new skills. But again there are only a very few skills. Halve way through the story you have learned all skills.
Of those skills you'll only use one or two for each character.
For winning fights you get exp. and some items you can sell. No money gained. Not a big problem, because the only
time you need it is for resting in an inn...oh, you could spend it for potions, but you'll find enough in chests.
The price for resting and potions goes up with every level your party gained.
Of course you gain access to an ship and balloon. Now don't get excited. There are no hidden locations to explore.
Also there are no hidden passages in dungeons.
The only problem you may encounter is finding one or two switches.
AND there is no map. Not a big problem. The dungeons are small. And the world only has a few locations.
If you rush you can finish the game in 6 - 8 hours.

Is it worth playing? If you are a fan of old JRPG's and the price is reduced...give it a try.
