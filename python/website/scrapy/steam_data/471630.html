20 June

Rexodus Update 6/20

Finally!

We've released an update that addresses a few technical issues:

- The "floaty" VR camera was an issue with the Unreal engine and they've had that problem resolved.
- The audio issue where a source's sound would cut out when looking directly at it has been fixed.

Note: While we officially support Steam VR and not any headset specifically, we are aware of and sensitive to the issues some of our users are having, particularly those using the Rift. This issue is a broader problem beyond our direct control but we are working with Steam to find solutions!

Thanks to all who left feedback!

Logan

Rexodus: A VR Story Experience is Live Now on Steam!

Its been a long road, folks. What started as a group of artists and animators experimenting with an exciting new medium has turned into a bold new direction for our team. And we can't think of a better way to introduce ourselves to our new Steam family than to offer up our first title for free!

Rexodus represents the core passion of our company: to create exciting characters and worlds and tell compelling stories. While the title is new to Steam and VR, its already had an introduction to the world via a graphic novel released by Dark Horse Comics. About This Game

Rexodus: A VR Story Experience is based on the Dark Horse Graphic Novel Rexodus. This is a VR narrative experience set in a universe where dinosaurs didn't go extinct, they left Earth. Those dinosaurs, utilizing their own advanced technology, are traversing the galaxy. Encounter new locations, characters, and dangerous enemies in Rexodus: A VR Story Experience!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 8
    • Processor: Intel i5-6600K
    • Memory: 8 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GTX 970 or AMD r9 390
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 4 GB available space

I don't believe I have ever reviewed anything on Steam before, shame the first review I will give is a negative one. I own a Vive and this review is for Vive users.

This demo was built for Rift users, no if ands or buts. I had problems playing this game right at launch; or failed launch in my case.

FIRST PROBLEM: Game would not start
I downloaded this game, turned on my VR and tried to open up the game in the headset menu only for nothing to happen. Odd, but I'm fairly versed in Steam, I know a few games require a first time setup that happens in the background so I tab to Desktop view... only to see nothing happening. Ok, fine I can still work with this, lets try launching this game by exiting Steam VR and just trying to open it via my monitor. Success.

SECOND PROBLEM: Tracked motion controllers 'support'
Hah.. Support. I finally get in game however I realize that I am at the end of my 4 meter square boundary as I was standing at my PC to launch the game. Tried teleporting... then realized I have no hands, panicked and started hitting buttons, nothing worked so I just closed out the game and was able to boot it from Steam VR. I was standing in the center of the room so everthing was cool. I later accidentally hit down on the touch pad and the center of the room reoriented to me. So sure... you can blindly find the center of your room, hit that button and you'll be good. Thats all the tracked motion controller 'support' this game has. Not to mention to interact with something you have to aim with your face and wait a good 3 seconds for it to select what you are looking at.

THIRD PROBLEM: Moving out of allowed bounds.
So I look at the 'Begin Mission' button and after 3 seconds it selects it and starts loading. While in the loading screen I got bored and like.. I dunno moved forward maybe two feet. So when it does load-- or moreover I hear a voice but have a completely black screen I'm all like, 'Odd, guess the character is blind.' Except this continued for a good minute as I was given instructions, confused I thought the display crapped out after hitting the menu button and getting the SteamVR menu I figured maybe it was only the game that failed. Cool... whatever, I'll just restart it... again. So I restart, get back in, and select begin mission. This time I was perfectly still and it came up. Cool, cool some little girl is comming toward me and looking through the glass separating us, I walk forward to get a better look and instantly get a black screen. Walk back and vision. The game instantly goes black when you clip through objects or their defined allowed bounds.

FOUTH PROBLEM: HTC Vive Head Tracking
She told me to look around and I was instantly aware of the fact that the floor was not at the right height, bending down to the ground there was quite a distance left to go. This actually brings up a question I've never thought about before. Rift users, do you not have a calibrate floor setting? Do you just float in whatever world at whatever predefined height? Its... disorenting for me to say the least. The head tracking was.. off? You really have to play it to experience. I mean it worked but something felt unreal or off or maybe not on scale? I apparently have an iron stomach so it doesn't bother me in the least but it was noticeable to the point where I was confused and tried testing my hardware so I walked to my bounds. Nope those are spot on, odd.

-EDIT
FIFTH PROBLEM: Standing Experience
You can move around in the world... but just don't. You're being talked to and addressed the entire time and if you move away they will still talk to and look at the spot that you were. You really are meant to just stand there the entire time.

So in all sure you can use your Vive to play this obviously Rift experience and it is kind of interesting for the short 5-10 minute demo but please devs do not just tack on 'HTC Vive Support' when it is quite obvious you don't own a Vive, didn't test it on the Vive and probably only looked up a youtube video to add in this 'support'. This is the main reason I have not bought a single game that did not have 'Room Scale' as I assumed it was obviously built for the Rift and would not make use of all of the Vives features. I was not wrong in this assumption.

As for the experience itself, it's incredibly short. I would have expected at least a "press A to warp" or something. As it stands, you're just there watching a fancy 3D movie, for all of 5 minutes.

I am a fan of the graphic novel myself, and I know the writers of Rexodus can do a whole lot better than this, especially considering that this is really aimed at someone to get them hooked. It just doesn't do that. Their Rexodus web portal does a better job, and that's just a real shame.

I recommend this demo, but only because it's free.
Rexodus is short, 10 minutes short. Which is not bad at all, but it feels more like a tech demo.

The whole cinematic experience is very nice, you get guided along nicely with the characters and the voice overs.
It's a static experience, stand still and watch. You can look around and it triggers some response but that's it. For this tech-demo it's enough, but they need to step it up.

Graphically this game is underwhelming at the least, the textures seemed ripped out from N64's Goldeneye and there is this weird sort of blur going around (maybe some bad post-processing?) The characters look nice, but the geometry of the objects are really really low detail. I like the art-style though.

I would love to see more of Rexodus, as the story seems interesting and the characters cool enough. But hire some quality 3D people. (Some interaction with the world would not hurt either)

Nobody hates to pay for games, it's better to pay 5 dollar for a nice experience, than 0 dollar for a bad one.
Step it up because the game/experience deserves it!

Totally worth checking, interesting characters and neat story but there is a few things that are pretty disappointing. For one the total amount of time you can really spend to enjoy looking at things is pretty short and the scenes will force you to move from location to location without really giving you a chance to soak in the area and forces you to rail along rather than be interactive, granted that I understand that the general idea is that it's a story based game.
(Scary part is, is that you're a silent protagonist who actually doesn't DO ANYTHING which makes me wonder if this is more like a VR movie rather than a game.)

The total time you'll spend in the Mission / demo is roughly less then 10 minutes which includes what you can / can't do in the introduction aka the starting screen.

The rendering isn't smooth and looks actually horrible on the HTC vive as I looked at the comic and it was totally unreadable even when i put my face right into it, which cut down the time i spent in the intro significantly being that the only other thing you can look at really is a hologram and statues.

In the HTC vive, my height positioning is off the ground when in the mission which I didn't notice until after it forces you into the second location. You'll understand what i mean when you try it.

The audio location is pretty bad as if you turn your head and let your left or right ear stay on the character that is talking, it'll cut out in half the headset to "simulate" sound location which isn't entirely good because it makes me feel deaf and breaks immersion rather then using 360 sound which I've enjoyed in other simulations. (If you really want to enjoy it, just simply look and listen to the characters, they're the most interesting things in the demo)

Besides that it's worth the 10 minutes being that it's free and it's a good introduction into a pretty interesting story line but for what it's worth, It'll need a lot more improvement and i mean alot of improvement to make it worth while to look into the series but I'm actually interested in seeing how this pans out since I actually liked the characters I've seen.

Also on a side note: It states that "input" Tracked motion controllers are supported but it doesn't, it uses a reticle stuck to your headset to do selections via looking at it, also this seems more like a seated game rather than a standing one so I'm not sure what that's all about.

I kind of wish i could of shaked the guys hand, just saying even if i had to simply look at his hand to change the outcome. A special moment.

(Now for what i wish for:

I wish I could of looked at things, and the character could of explained what those things were which would of made me feel like I was actually in a living/breathing world where the character is actually interacting with me rather then it feel scripted

I wish i could interact with the enviorment even if i can only look at things.

I wish i had more to go on since i can clearly see that this is fleshed out and interesting.)
