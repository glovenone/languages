About This Game

In This War Of Mine you do not play as an elite soldier, rather a group of civilians trying to survive in a besieged city; struggling with lack of food, medicine and constant danger from snipers and hostile scavengers. The game provides an experience of war seen from an entirely new angle.

The pace of This War of Mine is imposed by the day and night cycle. During the day snipers outside stop you from leaving your refuge, so you need to focus on maintaining your hideout: crafting, trading and taking care of your survivors. At night, take one of your civilians on a mission to scavenge through a set of unique locations for items that will help you stay alive.

Make life-and-death decisions driven by your conscience. Try to protect everybody from your shelter or sacrifice some of them for longer-term survival. During war, there are no good or bad decisions; there is only survival. The sooner you realize that, the better.

This War of Mine:

  • Inspired by real-life events
  • Control your survivors and manage your shelter
  • Craft weapons, alcohol, beds or stoves â anything that helps you survive
  • Make decisions - an often unforgiving and emotionally difficult experience
  • Randomized world and characters every time you start a new game
  • Charcoal-stylized aesthetics to complement the game's theme

This War of Mine is a story of civilian survival in a war torn city.

The core mechanics of the game essentially come down to resource management.

The basic resource categories you need to manage come down to these broad groups:

1. Food (ex. raw meat)
2. Building Materials (ex. wood)
3. Weapons/Utilities (ex. lock picks)
4. Happiness (ex. cigarettes)
5. Healing (ex. medication)
6. Bartering (ex. diamonds)

The game uses a cycle of two times: night and day.

During the day you will do things like:

1. Cook food
2. Distill alcohol
3. Sleep
4. Set mouse traps
5. Grow vegetables
etc.

During the NIGHT you will go out and SCAVENGE.

The night is the more interactive part of the game. During the night you will look at your map and determine where you would like to scavenge in the city. Some locations (such as the Garage) will be relatively safe and have people for you to barter with. Other locations (such as the Construction Site) may not be safe. You may need to bring a weapon or be prepared to not be seen. The entire goal of scavenging is to bring back food, materials, medications, etc. for your party back at base camp.

Criticisms:

Overall, I feel like the game becomes very grindy. The fact that you can only carry a limited amount of resources with you while scavenging is realistic yet tedious. You will have to spend multiple nights scavenging the same location in order to loot all of it. While this feels realistic and reasonable, in practice it just becomes monotonous and repetitive.

Day time phase is a bit of a hassle and simply becomes a routine. You will make water every day. You will put tired people in beds every day. You will cook every day/every few days. Basically day time is just a monotonous time sync as you go through the motions of keeping everyone healthy. And that's honestly what day time is - going through the motions. It's not fun, it's not interactive, and you do the same thing nearly every time. It's disappointing.

What I enjoy about the game:

I absolutely love the concept - it's clever and I have to give the studio props on originality. It's fresh and something I have never seen before. I like the resource management concept of the game - unfortunately I just didn't care for the way it was executed (too monotonous/going through the motions).


Final Word:

There is A LOT more depth to this game than I have presented here, but I have presented the basic gist of the title. While the game is unique and original, it lacks engagement. There isn't much of a story and the gameplay is simply too repetitive. I'm very torn on this title, as I want to recommend it but the game just isn't fun. The gamplay isn't strong enough and it becomes repetitive very fast.

5.0/10
