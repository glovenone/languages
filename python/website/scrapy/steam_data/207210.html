About This Game

Tony Hawk’s Pro Skater HD takes the best elements of the classic THPS games and gives them a fresh update to create an all new skateboarding experience for PC. The gameplay in Tony Hawk’s Pro Skater HD focuses on the satisfaction of linking sick tricks for maximum points. Combining sharp visuals with the best controls ever in a Tony Hawk game, the feel is cutting edge yet timeless.

Key Features

  • THPS and THPS2 gameplay comes to PC!
  • HD Upgrade: Game was rebuilt and polished by Robomodo using Neversoft’s original level models.
  • Classic levels: The style and feel of the best 7 levels from THPS and THPS2 (Warehouse, School 2, Mall, Hangar, Venice, Marseille and Phoenix Downhill Jam) refreshed for today. The levels are exactly the same specs as the originals - every gap, SKATE Letter, etc is exactly where you remember it but looks a hell of a lot better!
  • The Top Pros: Today’s biggest skate pros round out the cast including: Nyjah Huston, Chris Cole, Eric Koston, Andrew Reynolds, Rodney Mullen, Lyn-z Adams Hawkins Pastrana, Riley Hawk and, of course, Tony Hawk himself. Each has 8 boards, 3 special moves, and an alternate outfit.
  • Refined controls: The skaters’ animations, tricks and combos reflect the evolution of the THPS games. Animations are improved, the tricks have been redone (and some new special tricks added), even “skater celebrations” have been added for big combos.
  • Manuals and Reverts: You can do manuals as part of trick combos in THPS HD. Given the fact that this game combines the best gameplay from THPS1 and THPS2, Reverts will not work as part of trick combos in the initial launch of THPS HD as that mechanic was not introduced until THPS3. However, reverts will work in the post-release DLC for the THPS3 levels, as those levels were originally designed to accommodate this.
  • Endlessly fun objectives: Players pursue the same global and level-specific goals as the original games, including S-K-A-T-E, Secret DVD (used to be VHS tape!), and many more.
  • In-Game Soundtrack: It is about half classic THPS tracks and half new ones. Includes Goldfinger’s “Superman” and the Anthrax/Public Enemy collaboration “Bring the Noise”.
  • Downloadable content: Coming post-release, The “Tony Hawk’s Pro Skater 3 HD Revert Pack” DLC includes levels from THPS3 including Airport, Canada, and LA. New pros included are legends Steve Caballero and Geoff Rowley.
  • In Game Map View: Pause and see the location of objectives and gaps.
  • Quick Restart: Back button allows you to quickly restart their session. Perfect for the obsessive gamer who is looking to achieve the perfect combo.

System Requirements

    Minimum (or Recommended)
    • OS: Microsoft Windows(R) XP, Windows Vista(R) or Windows(R) 7
    • Processor: Intel(R) Core(TM)2 CPU 6600 @ 2.40GHz (2 CPUs) -or- AMD(R) Athlon(TM) II X2 250
    • Memory: 2 GB (Vista/Win 7)
    • Hard Disk Space: 1.8 GB of free hard drive space
    • Video Card: 256 MB NVIDIA(R) GeForce(TM) 8600 GTS -or- 256 MB ATI(R) Radeon(TM) HD 3650 -or- equivalent graphic card
    • DirectX®: DirectX(R) version: 9.0c (included)
    • Sound: DirectX 9.0c-compliant true 16-bit sound card

After hearing many negative things about this game, I was finally able to try it for myself, and I must say it is a mediocre game at best. It is very shallow, even compared to the source matieral. I want to go into detail and list why I do not recommend this game as a Tony Hawk veteran, and not based on blind hatred.

First, A few pros:
1) Game looks great. It gives what it sells itself on.

2) Other gamemodes are intresting enough to replay levels. They may be bland or uninspired, but it's something to do, about all you'd expect from side modes.

3) Stable(ish) PC port

4) Much of the old soundtrack was brought back, with some new school tunes thrown in as well.

5) Very cheap, especially on sale.

Now, why I gave this game a negative review.

My Personal Gripes:
1) Even though it includes levels from both THPS1 and THPS2, the fact that only a few from each were included was a huge letdown. The lack of New York, Philly, and even School 1, very good maps, were axed in favour of levels like Hangar, and yet ANOTHER DHJ.

2) Goal variety and creativity was very limited. In absensce of Interesting goals or ideas, almost every extra goal made for THPS1 levels were "Collect 5 ___". We already have Skate letters and the original goals, making them seem extremley redundant and halting any kind of flow you could get in the first place when collecting goals.

And let's talk about the "Projectives". On the first level, it shows some promise as a fun gamemode, more challenge and more goals for levels is something I can get down with, If not every level HAD THE EXACT SAME GOALS. Like seriously?! Combo and score I can understand, but who the ♥♥♥♥ wanted to boneless over a bum in EVERY level?

3) While I understand it is a remake and is supposed to follow the source matieral, they copied almost everything bad about previous games as well. Special manuals being on grabs, the old school special system, and Big Drops are all things we could have lived without.

4) Zero Create a Modes. No Parks, No Skaters, nothing.

5) On the topic of skaters, this game's cast is lackluster. This is more of a personal complaint than an objective view, but so many skaters from the original are missing. If they got Steve and Rodney's old asses back, Why not Jamie, Bob, or anyone else?

6) The complete lack of competitions is a little offputing. I could have lived without them, but giving goals to competition maps that weren't based around regular goals feels extremley awkward. But then again, with this game's 7 non DLC maps, doubt they could fit it anyways.

And that was just my personal list, here's why I think the game is Mechanically bad

Pure Mechanical Failures:

1) Manuals are far too sensitive. 50% of the time after a bail, you start in a manual because of how sensitive the controls are. They worked fine in previous games, what happened here?

2) Game is WAYYY too floaty. It's constantly enabled moon gravity, hell, With max air and hang time you go FARTHER than you would with moon gravity. It makes the game feel super awkward and inconsistant.

3) Combos drop super easy in this game. Whether it be manuals, reverts, or even grinds sometimes, you are at the games mercy of whether or not you keep your combo going. Extremley frustrating.

4) Hitboxes are insanley inconcistent. It's either a mile wide or a pixel small. This has always been a problem in the franchise, but it's a glaring issue here with the altered physics. I should not have to do a trick over a gap 5 times before it counts.

5) The engine in general. It handles almost nothing like older tony games, which is ok, change was expected, but this engine gives you little to no control of your movement. It feels loose and sloppy,


If you are a veteran Tony Hawk fan, only pick up this game on sale if you're curious enough. It is not worth the money or your time. I gave this game a legitimate chance and tried to turn the other cheek, but I couldn't. Avoid this game if you're looking for a genuine tony experience.
