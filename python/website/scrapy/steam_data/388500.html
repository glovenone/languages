About This Game

Super Space Meltdown - 2D dungeon-crawler. In space!


Learn, Die, Repeat as time rewinds. Or does it?

Super Space Meltdown is a 2D top down roguelike sci-fi shooter. It’s basically a sci-fi dungeon crawl. I suppose you could call it a space station crawler.

Super Space Meltdown makes you crawl through random rooms filled with alien scum that are begging for a blaster shot right to the face!

Humanities largest space station is under attack by mysterious alien invaders. You play as Sgt. Alex Cutter sent into deep space on a lone mission to save the day and annihilate the alien threat. But there's a catch. The presence of these alien invaders seems to have caused the stations reactor to go into meltdown. This means you have 60 seconds to clear each room before the reactor melts down and causes time to loop back on itself, taking you back to the beginning of the game.

Luckily you have your trusty AI companion to help you on your way and in dire times manipulate the reactor to your advantage. If you are defeated, your companion will cause time to loop back on itself to enable Cutter to try and save the day one more time.

That means that time will be reversed every time you die but you will get to keep every item and bullet you have managed to salvage. Most importantly, you will know how to do better next time. Learn, die and repeat baby!

You are equipped with your trusty space rifle, but as time is of the essence that is not always enough. Therefore Cutter has an impressive arsenal for disposing of hostiles and you will find more weapons and a fair share of bullets along your journey from room to room. For example, you can leave turrets to take out the trash for you or you can blow the nasties to smithereens with a space grenade. Yes, a space grenade. It’s like a normal grenade, but better. Because it has space in the name.

As each room is cleared Terminal Stations are activated which reveal messages left by previous inhabitants of the station. They unravel a tragic story and help reveal the mysteries behind the invasion and what is causing the meltdowns. Nothing is as it seems.

Who is Sgt. Cutter? Why is time reversing? Why is the station infested with hostile aliens? Who is behind the messages in the terminals? What causes the meltdown? That, and more can be learned by exploring the persistent world and delving deeper into the mysteries of Super Space Meltdown.

Key features:
  • Crawl through random, alien-infested floors. You have 60 seconds to reach the next floor!
  • Use strategy and trial and error to overcome the alien threat. Rush through and you’ll regret it.
  • When you die, time rewinds so you can try again. You’ll do better next time, right?
  • Unravel the story, piece by piece.
  • Special weapons, such as mines, grenades and turrets - for when your trusty rifle just isn’t cutting it.
  • Die. A lot.

Short little shooter game.

You basically have 60 seconds to kill all the enemies in each room for 20 rooms. That's it.
Assuming you do get to level 20, the game resets anyway.

I couldn't really find any instructions or guide anywhere, but basically there are 1-3 crates per room to give you extra weapons like mines, turrets, grenades, and ammo and super ammo clips.

You can also get credits, but they don't seem to do anything and I never saw any use for them, other than an achievement.

If you do die, and the game resets, you also get to keep any extra weapons you had when you died. There's also some random data terminals around, but none of them seemed to have any information on changing the ending.

The only real randomization to the game is the order of the levels (after the first level), which seems strange because this probably could have made a decent roguelike.

Unfortunately no controller support, and the controls can't be changed.

Probabably 30-60 minutes of entertainment for most people, so $4 seems a bit overpriced.
Fortunately I got it in a bundle (probably along with 99% of everyone else who has it).

Not too bad, but not great either.

There weren't really any bugs, so maybe that is a plus. But really, I don't know if there was anything fun or engaging in the game. It was a really forgettable experience everytime I tried to play, and it just felt really incomplete. All things considered, Super Space Meltdown is like you are wandering down a corridor when suddenly.

Super Space Meltdown is a top- down 2D rogue-like dungeon crawler set on an alien infested space station.

The game has a nice soundtrack which sets the tone of the environment very well. The sound effects have a retro feel which complement the pixel-art style very well.

The pixel art is well defined and colorful. All the sprites on screen are distinct in style and shape. However, there are no options to adjust graphical settings, such as windowed support, adjusting resolutions, adjusting refresh rate, or any more advanced settings.

The gameplay premise is simple. Complete each level within 60 seconds. If the timer runs out or you die, you restart from the beginning and play a random series of levels maintaining the equipment from the previous run. This is where the rouge-like elements come into play. However, it does become repetitive after a time. Perhaps farther in could yield more exciting level design or a boss fight, which would add some much needed variety.

The controls are difficult to get used to. Your character can play equipment, such as mines or turrets, and shoot with your rifle. Your character can only shoot in a straight line. However, nowhere in the game is there listed a tutorial for any of this information. The game needs a tutorial for the controls, or at least a list of key bindings, which you also cannot currently rebind. Also, this game would benefit hugely from controller support, or at least the ability to rebind keys.

Overall, I would not recommend this game yet, despite its price, which is $3.99 at the time of writing. It is a simple game that provides enjoyment, if repetitive, at the most basic level. If you enjoy rogue-like games, this is up your alley. However, this game fails in any kind of customization. Hopefully an update comes soon which could allow four things which would benefit it heavily; Controller support, an in-game tutorial or at least a list of controls, graphical options, and key bindings. After those changes, this game would be vastly superior to its current form and would be worth an extended play.

Super Space Meltdown is a game. Yep. It's a game, you can just spam the fire button for great justice, the special weapons all feel relatively useless and your gun auto locks on to anything that is in your "lane" North, South, East, or West of you, you can also only move in those directions, no diagonals here. You have 60 seconds to beat each floor, and each floor is also randomized. The enemies rarely attack, and when they do attack their shots are somewhat easy to dodge, also enemies take a ton of shots to kill, your hand will hurt. Some stages seem to feature laser traps that near instant kill you if you're caught in one. The game offers no instructions on how play the game, and feels clunky with keyboard controls and no way to remap them ingame. Though using Xpadder to map the buttons to my gamepad certainly helped a ton. The game is also locked into Fullscreen only mode, and for one reason or another kept crashing on me when I launched it. I was finally able to get a good launch and record some of the game play (I played poorly). All in all, you get what you pay for.

Some thoughts that could improve this game. First and Foremost, being able to attack diagonally. Many times I lost a few seconds of time because an enemy would just move to one of my diagonals making me have to fully reposition just to hit them, even though the enemy was still adjacent to me. Second, maybe progressive levels? What I mean is, I like the random aspect, but maybe make enemies on lower floors easier/take less shots to kill, but they get harder the more floors you go down, this could encourage the use of proper tactics and not just mash spacebar. I feel as though that you need more health/ammo pick ups (Unless I just somehow missed them), as well as maybe tweak the special weapons some, as I felt they were a bit on the useless side of things. The Turret was nice, but you can't retrieve it, or replace it once you've used it, and it doesn't last long before it explodes. The grenade is really clunky, its explosion range only seems to damage the imidiate square that it blew up in, same with the time bomb and proximity mine. The Proximity mine could definitely use a buff, make it to where it blows up the square its in, and the squares adjacent to it in a 3x3 grid. I didn't like the fact that the enemy needed to be directly on top of it to register "Hey I need to blow up now!"

All in all, for the pricepoint of the game, its relatively decent, but it didn't hold my attention all that long. I'd give it somewhere in the ballpark of 4/10, decent, but not great
