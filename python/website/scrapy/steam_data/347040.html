About This Game

Build weird and wonderful vehicles using whatever you can find. Master rockets, balloons, parachutes and more. Save as many Keebles as you can, and be quick - the Whale at the End of the World won't wait forever!

Keebles challenges you to build vehicles to transport the fragile Bobble across 30 challenging levels, saving Keebles along the way.

Use the Workshop to design your vehicle. Different levels provide different items to make your vehicle from. Make use of various wheels, beams and gadgets to roll, slide, float and fly. The best design isn't always the most complicated, nor the most simple. Experiment and see what works best!

There are five stars to be earned for each level, plus a bonus Time Challenge award. Getting all of them isn't going to be easy, but it will be fun!

The world of Keebles is as challenging as it is beautiful. Don't let the 'cute and casual' appearance turn you away. Spread across 30 levels you are tasked with building vehicles to collect Keebles in a fragile glass bubble.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jRoHiWngJe8
Starting each level you are placed into workshop phase where you experiment with building vehicles. Given basic construction pieces with simple design logic initially, progression rewards both new items and more difficult obstructions to pass. Can happily say controls were both intuitive and user-friendly. Quite similar to Spore or World of Goo which another reviewer mentioned.

Objectives for 100% runs included, collecting 3 Keebles, using less pieces then required and beating a timer. These rewarded up to 5 stars per level, so for completionists this would take some time. While this does provide a challenge the fun really does lay in vehicle experimentation with the unlockable items, quite amazing what can be pulled off with some twitch skills. :)

Personally I enjoyed the visual style, clean vector based art with an upbeat snes era soundtrack. While Keebles doesn't bring anything new to the table it's a solid physics based puzzler that's hard to fault. If you liked similar games in the past (such as those mentioned above) I can recommend checking it out or shortlisting. Cheers.

#45: Keebles

Keebles...oh, how do i describe thee? Pretty simple actually. World of Goo meets Spore meets lemmings meets (name of any physics based vehicle designer game). But it doesnt stop there. There's also timing elements, jump elements, and the mighty star based progress system. Pretty simple. It doesnt break any new ground, just adds together a bunch of the tried and true. I found myself laughing at more than one point in the game at some of the outrageous stuff you can build but after the 1st hour it became a hair tedious.

Pros:
nice designer system, with a decent hint system
Nicely colored graphics
decent sound (however, i have hypersensitivity to sound and after a few minutes i had to shut the sound off)
nice premise, that actually delivers
decent content for value (especially if purchased on sale)
works quite nicely with mouse and keyboard

Cons:
it became tedious after a while, but in short sessions no complaints
i found myself designing the same vehicle over and over, and it worked. But the journey to discover that was fun.

Otherwise nothing major to complain about. Nice mix of difficulty, design and variations. It is a good family game, but it may frustrate the littler gamers. Good value, good time.

7/10
