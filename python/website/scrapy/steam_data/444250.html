About This Game

Originally a jam game, Crown and Council is a casual strategy game of geopolitical drama. Conquer, expand, build and develop your kingdom, until all your foes are crushed!

Crown and Council relied on procedural generation for the 75 included maps. The generator is built into the game, and you can generate new maps on the fly.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: 7 ,8, 8.1, 10
    • Processor: 1.5 GHz or faster
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Integrated graphics card
    • Storage: 300 MB available space

I wish there was a better scoring option for reviews. This game gets like a 55 / 100 in my book, which is not to say it is bad, but it is not good either. I think it should have been a browser game, all things considered, not on steam, which is why I put the negative score.

PROS:
1) Highly accessible.
2) Neat concept.
3) Short play time.

CONS:
1) Too easy/simplistic.
2) Buggy.
3) Not very fun once you get the hang of it.

REVIEW:
The game consists of a series of maps where you play against one to 3 opponents. Every one of the first few maps introduces a new structure that you can construct, and there are 5 overall. After that, some random events are also introduced. The goal of the game is to destroy all of your foes. It is turn based.

So far, so good. The game does a great job of gradually teaching you how to play, and in the beginning, I felt some satisfaction when I won.

However, you quickly come to realize that you can win every map by doing the same thing:
0) If you are adjacent to an enemy faction, kill him immediately.
1) Grab as much land as you can, the enemy will prefer to grab unoccupied land before attacking you.
2) Populate your land with cities.
3) Sweep your enemies.

On some occasions, building forts in choke points may be advisable, but normally even that is not necessary. Universities are completely useless all things considered.

On top of the very simplistic and eventually unrewarding gameplay, the game is very buggy. Smaller resolutions are not supported, and there is something seriously wrong with clicking, as many of the mosue clicks are not registered. On top of that, the hit detection on regions doesnt always works. I would sometimes click on one region to build something, and that something would be built on a completely different section. And for certain regions that was very persistent, albiet there were few regions for which this happened.

TL; DR: Give it a try if you are looking for a decent time-waster and don't mind simplistic gameplay and myriad bugs. As it stands, there are better webgame alternatives.
