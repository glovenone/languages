Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to get community feedback on the mass amounts of variations of enemies and powerups in order to implement many more before full launch.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“This game will be in early access 30-60 days.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We plan to have over 150 waves/levels, with AT LEAST 60 different enemies and shooting patterns, 30 bosses, and 50 powerups.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Fully playable 30 waves/levels and an endless mode with a leaderboard.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Pricing will be slightly lower for early access.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will get feedback on which enemies are fun and entertaining, while also balancing those that are too difficult.”

About This Game

Chamber 19 is a bullet filled 3D VR environment. Dodge your way through complex layouts of Plasma Orbs, shot by enemies who attempt to destroy The Central Core. It's a game based out of a Human testing chamber located in space.You wake up in a testing facility run by Chrum, a member of a superior alien race, who has been commanded to test the processing power of the human brain. You will endure a series of tests that introduce new and more challenging enemies as you progress. This action packed adventure contains hours of bullet dodging fun and offers the chance to edge out the competition on the global leaderboards. Are you up for the challenges ahead?

Early Access Review
Posted: 21 June
Chamber 19 is an excellent bullet hell vr shooter.

You fight increasingly difficult waves of enemies with a ship you hold in your hand, exactly like Xortex. The game introduces new enemies at an acceptable pace, giving you time to get better throughout a single playthrough.
There are a few different power ups, some that spawn when enemies are killed and a choice of 4 powerful upgrades after defeating a boss.

I like the second drone upgrade, allowed me to score #4 on the global leaderboards.

Missile upgrade kind of sucks, I find the missiles loop too much and your stuck unable to fire at targets right in your face. I would prefer rapid fire micro-missiles over the current giant mess that they are. It's very distracting.

The bosses are good, however I feel the first and second bosses are far more difficult then the next 2. Not sure how to beat wave 30 though, that thing scared me.

I like the new options recently added, the 180 mode is a far superior experience.

Just a note that I got major slowdown around wave 27-29 with a GTX 1080, so definitely some optimization is needed.

Overall I recommend this game to anyone who liked Xortex.
