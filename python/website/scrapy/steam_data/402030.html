About This Game

Welcome to your Zenohell, soldier. Zenohell is a 90s styled shoot-em'up (in short, shmup) that is all about shooting, dodging, collecting medals and getting score, while blasting your speakers to the sound effects and music.

Warning: The game does contain profanities at some points so tread carefully.

- 6 stages of ever increasing scale and intensity - but there are more for the especially skilled pilots.
- A secret mode that you can unlock.
- 3 player ships offering completely different playstyles, with some more you can unlock by playing the game.
- A 28 track soundtrack made by Jorgen "dj_emphatic" Sjolander, that you get for free with the game - just look at the data files.
- 3 different gameplay modes.
- Music modding, which lets you swap out the music for your personal favorites, if desired.
- Options which let you customize your game view, TATE (rotating your screen), Filter, Transparent medals and more!
- Gameplay from times when it was more about reflexes and less about complex scoring.

We, at Grybanser Fox, are excited to finally show our game to the world and we hope that you will be as excited to play it as we were making it.

Zenohell is a fast-paced bullet hell space shooter in a 16-bit style.

Presentation
The graphics are spot-on, looking exactly like something that would have come out for the Sega Genesis/Mega Drive or the Turbo Grafix 16/PC Engine. The sound effects are great, except for the shooting sound effect of the player's ship, which is essentially just a click, but it's usually so buried under other sounds that it doesn't really matter.

The music sounds fantastic and really fits in with the fast-paced and chaotic gameplay, but it was kind of disappointing in that it didn't really fit with the whole 16-bit aesthetic, so instead of sounding like a midi from a Sound Blaster or anything that would have come out of a 16-bit system, it sounds like modern electronic music (which it is).

Controls
On a keyboard, the game controls quite nicely, using the standard emulator presets of Shift, Z, and X as the action buttons and the arrow keys for movement. The use of Shift as one of the action keys feels awkward at times, but it's nothing a quick rebind can't fix.

Using a controller, the joystick controls to move the ship feel quite jerky, with the ship only moving in eight directions, while the joystick, of course, moves in all 360 degrees. The action buttons don't work on first time startup, and must be manually bound using the options menu.

Plot
The plot in this game is negligible. It is the standard "aliens are attacking! Save us!" Of course, plot isn't the main draw for this game.

Gameplay
The gameplay is fast-paced and simple. The goal, of course, is to shoot all of the bad guys. To do this, the player has a choice between multiple ships, each of which plays differently. The main difference between the ships is their shot patterns, which, depending on the ship chosen, can drastically change the way the game is played.

The player is given three weapons: the basic shot, a bomb that clears the screen of enemies' shots and gives the player invincibility for a short time, and a "focus shot" which is the basic shot, but slightly more powerful.

The fast-paced nature of the game makes it quite difficult to dodge enemy projectiles, which seem to be everywhere. This challenge is what makes the game, really, since the enemies are really quite easy to defeat simply by holding down the shoot button.

There is a subtle strategy to using the bombs, since the player is only given three at the beginning of the level and there is no way to replenish them.

The game allows for infinite continues, which is wonderful, because it is ridiculously difficult at times.

Replayability
The game is essentially an arcade game, and always has the incentive of trying to get a higher score (or not use continues).

Conclusion
Zenohell is a bullet hell shooter, that, while it has its flaws, is thoroughly enjoying and does what it sets out to do.

Retro style Shoot em' up with simple mechanics

Zenohell reminded me of some of the games I used to play in the arcade when I was a kid, such as 1941. The game uses similiar mechanics; eliminate all enemies on screen, while at the same time avoiding the constant barrage of enemy projectiles filling the screen. The gameplay is similar to the classic shoot em' ups, where you have to avoid enemy projectiles while also trying to gain the most points possible. The game gives you three ships to choose from, each with different patterns of fire, and stats. This gives the game variety, and adds some extra challenge. Zenohell stays true the genre and is definitely worth playing if you enjoyed games of the 90s shoot em' up genre.

PROS
  • Fun, engaging gameplay
  • Different ships gives gameplay variety
  • Fun Bosh Rush mode
  • Stays true to genre
  • Music reminiscent of older shmups
  • Simple, easy to learn controls
  • Visuals resemble that of classic shoot em' ups

CONS
  • Too short; I completed the first gamemode within 30 minutes
  • Bosses lack variety
  • Enemy patterns are often too similar

Summary:
This game is perfect for those who miss the shoot em' ups of the 90s, or people new to the genre willing to give it a try. I had a lot of fun with this game, although I was disappointed when I finished it so quickly. However the Boss Rush mode is a fun addition to the game, for those who complete the campaign.

Rating:
I'd give Zenohell an 8/10.
