--- ABOUT THE GAME ---

I was supposed to be happy. I was supposed to get married to the man I loved.

Seduce Me 2: The Demon War is the sequel to the popular free-to-play otome game, Seduce Me the Otome. -----To Find Your Music!----

Steam will install the game soundtrack to your computer. The paths are as follows:

Windows: Please use your computer's File Explorer to navigate to the soundtrack
C:\Program Files (x86)\Steam\SteamApps\common\
Locate the game folder and the soundtrack will be within it.

Mac: Open the Finder Window and press Command+Shift+G (or select Go > Go to Folder...). Enter "~/Library/ Application Support/Steam/" (without quotes), then press Enter. Locate the Steam Apps folder, open it and locate the Common folder, open it. Locate the game folder and the soundtrack will be within it.

Linux: Using Terminal -
~/.local/share/Steam/SteamApps/common/
Locate the game folder and the soundtrack will be within it.

About This Game

"It seemed like the start of something new. Something that would change."
You are a senior in high school along with your two best friends, Naomi Patterson and Suzu Cappini. You are also the granddaughter of Harold Anderson, the CEO and founder of Anderson Toys, a toy company sworn to give children amazing products and give a large part of their profit to charity.
One day, you get called home to attend your grandfather's funeral. After the funeral, you learn that you have inherited his large estate. Your father suggests you move in immediately, so the next day, you move out of your parents home and into your estate.
When you enter your new home, however, you see five injured, yet very handsome men on the lobby floor. They eventually awaken and make it known that they were attacked by a group of dangerous 'misfits' and ran to find shelter, passing out as soon as they entered your home.
You find out that they are incubi, 'demons who consume and use sexual energy of humans to survive'. With nowhere to go and a target on their backs, they ponder what what to do. Out of sympathy, you offer your home as refuge for a while in exchange for minor servitude (it is a big house, after all). They happily agree.
What will happen as the incubi get comfy? Why exactly did they come the human world? Who are the 'misfits', and will they stop pursuing them so the incubi can leave?
...Will you WANT them to leave?

Embarrassed. That would be one word I could use if someone asked me how I felt right now. But hey, it's okay. I was bored and the game was free so technically I didn't lose more than my dignity. The game wasn't completely horrible and it was able to give me many good laughs... even though they were mostly on the game's expense.
However, I can still smile and give the maker of this game a big piece of that 'At least you tried' cake.

Over all, Seduce Me the Otome is a wonderful mix of all those possible reverse harem anime cliches you can think of with a nice spice of demons and cheesy, cringe worthy lines that make you feel sorry for the voice actors.
We have the classic bunch of male characters: Mr. Shy guy, Mr. Flirty guy, Mr. Wise guy, Mr. Tough guy and Mr. Childish guy. The two female friends of the heroine are Ms. Caring but humorless and Ms. Tough girl.

Here's what I liked about the game:
- The voice acting was pretty decent, occasionally really good.
- The music, when there was some, was really nice.
- Some of the characters were good.
- There was some suprising endings and more romances that I knew (I haven't played most of them).

Aaand here's some things I didn't like:

- Like many others who've reviewed this game, the lack of "real" choices annoyed me too.
If you want you can click trough the whole story in mere minutes. You just need remember where the important choices are (or you can just save before choosing anything). Oh, just a tip: remember to choose carefully your house cleaning pal.

- The art... oh god, the art. I'm so sorry, but when you make a visual novel or a dating sim it is VERY IMPORTANT that the players feel attracted to the characters. I don't know if this is supposed to be a message of some sort, but when you have to say "he/she has a very nice personality!" in some character's defence in a cheesy, romantic visual novel... you know there's something amiss there.

- Lack of music. It's not all that bad, but it could've made the game a bit livelier. At one point I actually forgot that I had the game running on the background and noticed it when I was shutting down my computer.

- The writing. Like I already mentioned, some of the lines were bad, and when I say bad, I mean really really bad.
The plot was also slightly childish and worthy of more than a few face palms. Many things were left without explanation or those explanations were so poorly done that they just made me sigh. I also hated how the father of the heroine was written. He was a "bad guy" just for the sake of it and was probably supposed to make us feel sorry for the daughter, and speaking about bad "explanations" the reason her father was such an asshat was explained very poorly.

- The endings. Some of them were so disappointing and half ♥♥♥♥♥ that they just left me staring at the screen. Couldn't even believe the game was over when I returned to the main menu.

So, that's what I thought about this game. You'll have more fun if you take a drink every time something here makes you roll your eyes or facepalm, although you'll probably be completely wasted after ten minutes into the game...

But hey... at least its free. :)
