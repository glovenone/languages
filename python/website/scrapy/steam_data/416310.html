About This Game


Tired of boring tower defense games? Goblin Defenders: Steel 'n' Wood breaks the mold with exciting and unique action! Why fight off enemies pouring in from one direction, like most other games, when you can battle hordes of monsters coming at you from all angles? That's what Goblin Defenders is all about!
Start with three guns capable of ripping through crowds of attackers and steadily upgrade your arsenal as the game progresses! But beware: the game gradually becomes more difficult as enemies approach from more and more directions! Your survival will depend on your ability to make the right choices when upgrading your weapons and towers. Ogres, spiders and evil spirits are coming for you, so answer their challenge in Goblin Defenders: Steel 'n' Wood!

Game Information

Goblin Defenders: Steel 'n' Wood, is a tower defense strategy game, developed and published by Alawar Entertainment.

First glance it looked like it was developed for mobile devices. My guess it was originally made for mobile devices before it was ported.

There's a second game for this series but unfortunately it's for mobile devices only.

Game Visuals

I want to mentioned that the visuals for this game is well done even it's simplistic.

There a couple of complaints about the user interface takes up plenty of space at the lower portion of the screen. The user interface vanishes when a defense tower is dragged onto the field. It reappears when the defense tower has been place.

There's a option available, dragging and zooming, to view the entire field. This feature is very handy and makes planning a bit easier.

Game Audio

I want to mentioned that the audio for this game is well done even it's simplistic and somewhat repetitive.

Gameplay

There are only two game difficulty modes to choose from which is Normal and Hard.

There are two game modes to choose from and they're Campaign and Survival. The campagin game mode features five different areas with two levels each. The survival game mode is where the player is being bombarded with waves after waves of enemies.

The defense towers can be upgraded to its' highest level but it does requires a bit of coins. The highest upgrade level must be purchased from the shop using gems. These upgrades doesn't come cheap after all.

There are various game achievements but they're only in game. Each of these achievements has certain requirements before they can be unlocked.

Miscellaneous

There are total of six different trading cards. You only get the three of those cards from the game. There are several ways to get the rest of the trading card: Purchased from Steam's Market; Trade with friends; Booster Packs and it's Booster Pack Creator.

My final thoughts, this tower defense game, fairly easy for those who haven't played these types of game before. But for those who had played tower defense games before won't find it challenging enough. I would recommend this game but not at the full price value.
