About This Game

Angels That Kill is a dark, first person adventure game. It tells the story of two men as they uncover the truth behind a murder that has shaken the city. Play through both sides of the story and discover the truth behind the murder.

Play as Micheal Bickel, a Vietnam War veteran that now works as a Noll City detective, and play as James Baley, a drunk and drug-ridden retired boxer who has lost everything and moved to Noll City to start again. Go through the different days playing as both characters and work around the case, interact with the population and make decisions that could affect a number of people. Hide, run, investigate, explore, and discover while becoming a part in Noll City in this tragic time.


Play both sides of the story.
Go through both the suspects and the investigators story, to discover the truth and interact with the people of Noll City. Play through two different perspectives and have unique interactions based off of who you play as.

Stay alive under a steel sky.
Keep a watch on and maintain your hunger level by scavenging through dumpsters to stay alive in this deserted time. Every scrap of food you take for one character is another lost for the other, so make sure you balance your portions carefully.

Explore all your options.
Just because there is a story doesn't mean that you cant help make it, choose between right and wrong, make decisions you didn't mean to, and discover your own ending.

Unlock and play through alternate game modes.
As you progress through the main campaign you will be able to unlock more game modes to play around in.

Explore the world.
It might not be the biggest game ever made, but it is packed full of secrets and goodies waiting to be discovered.

Immerse in a noire inspired world.
Every aspect of the game has been carefully detailed to appeal to any noire(or noir) lover.

Change the world to your choosing.
You have the ability to change the amount of rainfall, the amount of fog, the lighting, and more.

I played the demo of this game and after I've completed it, I simply couldnât resist buying it. If you canât decide whether you want to buy it or not, try the demo first.

tl;dr This game is one of the best I played in a long time. It's very organic and well balanced in almost every aspect: story, music, visuals and gameplay.

Story

Take role of one of the two protagonists striving for survival and/or better life (which is quite difficult, as you will find out). The tragedy of this city lies not only in the recent terrorist attacks, but also in the appearance of an unknown murderer wandering the streets. The game has 5 chapters (with more to come in updates) and multiple endings, depending on choices made by the player.

Music

The developers used music from freesound, masterfully picked and corresponding perfectly. It may be repetitive sometimes but never bothered me, especially radio tracks.
Dialogues are completely silent and show up in the form of text, which some players will most probably consider a flaw, even though it doesn't affect the overall feel of the game.

Visuals

The game has a minimalistic black & white (and sometimes red) graphic style, very atmospheric and pleasant to my taste. The city buildings and streets look very solid and appealing to the eye. If you don't expect graphics like in major studios titles and can get used to overall game style you won't be disappointed.

Gameplay

In each chapter you are free to go wherever you want, you can chat with people, visit different places and explore the city itself. The game has many little secrets which do not change the track of the main story, but can still amaze when you find them. Some choices you make are really important to the story and will lead to the different endings.

Navigation system is a bit confusing at the very beginning of the game, and you will sometimes find yourself lost in the city and not knowing where to go. The map and birds in the sky can help you orient though. After you play through the game to your very first ending and get familiar with the city, it ceases to be a problem. Your first playthrough can take up to 3 hours, the next ones will be shorter if you know exactly what to do, or may be even longer if you enjoy exploring as much as I do. Some chapters of the game are just literally jaw-dropping and had a high impact on me.


Overall: this game is very good, and is going to be even better, because the devs still improve it and add new content. If you like exploring adventures and walking simulators, you should grab this title without a doubt, or at least try the demo.

Angels That Kill is a first-person noir adventure game developed and published by Rising Sun Interactive. As some of you might know, I love the noir genre, but unfortunately this game was a big disappointment.

STORY
The story follows two different characters: one is an ex boxer who is down on his luck, and the other is a detective who is trying to discover the murderer that's been terrorising the city lately. In the 5 chapters short chapters of the game you will see different situations from the eyes of the protagonists. While this is a good idea, the overall story is barely interesting (mostly because of the absence of voice acting). You can talk to just a handful of characters, and they are all very bland. However, I do like that you have some choices during the story that will lead you to one of many possible (albeit disappointing) endings.

GRAPHICS
The game uses the Unity engine, and the performance is pretty solid all around. Unfortunately, this game is graphically very unpleasant. While it isn't the first noir game to use black, white and gray as its main colors, my eyes were feeling tired while looking at the screen.
The huge city doesn't feature that many civilians, the buildings all look the same and there is nothing interesting to see, which is a shame. I do believe that making this a more linear story might have improved the graphical department.

GAMEPLAY
The game is basically a glorified walking simulator: you usually just go from one point to the other, and on the way you might find a couple of NPCs to "chat" with or an item to examine. There are also a couple of instances in which you must avoid police detection, which means you should stay at least 10 feet away from a cop car. Other than that, you don't do much. And the worst aspect that makes this game feel like a chore is the city: it's just too damn huge and confusing to navigate. You do find a map, but it's way too stylized to understand exactly where to go, and especially where you are. There are some birds that fly over your objective, but they are white against a white sky, so good luck finding them.

CHALLENGE
Other than the stealth sections, the game doesn't feature much challenge at all, and most of the frustration will come from you trying to find where to go.

SOUND
Easily the worst aspect of the game: the game lacks basically any type of sound effects, with the exception being footsteps and the alarm of cop cars when you are caught. The music is mediocre at best, and it barely plays during gameplay. There is also no voice acting at all, meaning that all of the dialogue is written, and the cutscenes are completely devoid of sound, creating either awkward or boring situations.

REPLAYABILITY
There are 5 chapters, and in each one there are subtle choices that will change the outcome. There are also 8 endings to unlock and a some secrets that make replaying the game more rewarding.

Overall, if what you read sounds interesting to you, you will enjoy this game. However the lack of sound and boring city make this a game that will make you tired after a while. It sucks the fun out of most things.

Angels That Kill is a first-person noir adventure game developed and published by Rising Sun Interactive. As some of you might know, I love the noir genre, but unfortunately this game was a big disappointment.

STORY
The story follows two different characters: one is an ex boxer who is down on his luck, and the other is a detective who is trying to discover the murderer that's been terrorising the city lately. In the 5 chapters short chapters of the game you will see different situations from the eyes of the protagonists. While this is a good idea, the overall story is barely interesting (mostly because of the absence of voice acting). You can talk to just a handful of characters, and they are all very bland. However, I do like that you have some choices during the story that will lead you to one of many possible (albeit disappointing) endings.

GRAPHICS
The game uses the Unity engine, and the performance is pretty solid all around. Unfortunately, this game is graphically very unpleasant. While it isn't the first noir game to use black, white and gray as its main colors, my eyes were feeling tired while looking at the screen.
The huge city doesn't feature that many civilians, the buildings all look the same and there is nothing interesting to see, which is a shame. I do believe that making this a more linear story might have improved the graphical department.

GAMEPLAY
The game is basically a glorified walking simulator: you usually just go from one point to the other, and on the way you might find a couple of NPCs to "chat" with or an item to examine. There are also a couple of instances in which you must avoid police detection, which means you should stay at least 10 feet away from a cop car. Other than that, you don't do much. And the worst aspect that makes this game feel like a chore is the city: it's just too damn huge and confusing to navigate. You do find a map, but it's way too stylized to understand exactly where to go, and especially where you are. There are some birds that fly over your objective, but they are white against a white sky, so good luck finding them.

CHALLENGE
Other than the stealth sections, the game doesn't feature much challenge at all, and most of the frustration will come from you trying to find where to go.

SOUND
Easily the worst aspect of the game: the game lacks basically any type of sound effects, with the exception being footsteps and the alarm of cop cars when you are caught. The music is mediocre at best, and it barely plays during gameplay. There is also no voice acting at all, meaning that all of the dialogue is written, and the cutscenes are completely devoid of sound, creating either awkward or boring situations.

REPLAYABILITY
There are 5 chapters, and in each one there are subtle choices that will change the outcome. There are also 8 endings to unlock and a some secrets that make replaying the game more rewarding.

Overall, if what you read sounds interesting to you, you will enjoy this game. However the lack of sound and boring city make this a game that will make you tired after a while. It sucks the fun out of most things.
