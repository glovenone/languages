About This Game

STORY

Erwin Lemmor just got transferred to a prestigious military academy to study Armored Warfare. But when he arrives, Erwin realizes it's not Tank School, but "Tank School" for World War II Tanks. Who happen to look just like Japanese School Girls...!

Instead of studying military history and doctrine, he's in "Basic Artillery Computations for Fire Support 101", and "Radio Operations 214". Will he win the heart of the tank-girls, or end up as cannon fodder? Only you can decide in, "Panzermadels: Tank Dating Simulator".

DETAILS

Panzermadels is made by 'DEVGRU-P' a small team of American developers in the style of a traditional Japanese Visual Novel. Unabashedly goofy, the game takes every trope you know from visual novels, and puts a distinctly Tank-Related twist on them! With six different romance plot-lines and a multiple of endings based on your choices, Panzermadels will keep you entertained for a number of playthroughs!

FEATURES

  • Join the Army.
  • See the World Darkest Depths of Your Frail Existence.
  • Meet Fun and Interesting People Tanks.
  • Date Them.
  • Also There's a Beach Episode.

Target Audience: Those looking for sexual tank jokes.....on sale..

Summary:
Panzermadels tries to follow in the steps of Hatoful Boyfriend, but fails in the charm and substance portion of the dating simulator genre. While the game does a great job of using sexual innuendo regarding tank warfare, it fails to create characters that are truly interesting, and makes you want to woo them. The personal connection isn't there, as inconsistencies within the characters personalities can make for an uneven experience. In addition, things are too off the wall at times, making you truly wonder exactly what the reasoning was for adding satanic rituals into a characters backstory. And when different characters you are wooing end up feeling the same due to a lack of variation in the plot, you feel like you're just playing the same story that you've played before. Sadly, Panzermadels only reinforces how well Hatoful Boyfriend was put totgether, and just doesn't do the job in keeping me interested in the characters or the plot that was unfolding. For me, it's a pass.

Video Review: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WCXlGFG6GzQ

Lists:
Positives:
  • The innuendo using tank and warfare terms is on point. Ammo ranks and Armor penetration. Well done.
  • The use of tropes makes it feel familiar for those who played the genre, while still putting a tiny spin on it with the tank girl elements of it.
  • The T34 storyline did tie in the Russian/communism element rather well.
  • The artwork is reasonable. Nothing too out there, it feels like a typical dating sim.
  • There are some touching moments within the story, and it's what I wanted to see more of in the end. It can tug on heartstrings a bit.

Negatives:
  • The lack of variation between the storylines of the different girls is disappointing. While you want an overarching storyline, you want some uniqueness for each of the arcs.
  • The heritage angles don't work very well at times. The german tank for example, it's a Japanese Otaku in disguse. And while that's not a problem in itself, it's the problem of going back and forth between the German and Japanese angles.
  • The ending feels like the developers ran out of money, and just threw something together. It doesn't work, as it feels like you just spent the last hour and a half doing nothing to affect your own fate.
  • The characters just don't have the spark behind them to really get me interested. They all don't really stand out, they feel too relaxed in the overall picture.
  • Look, I like off the wall shenanigans at times, but even here things really felt like a stretch. The satanic ritual portion just whiffs at being used effectively.
  • Sound is lacking. The overuse of the AOL instant messenger exiting sound not only got annoying, it was distracting. And with no audio sliders, you can't adjust it compared to the music.
  • Lack of resolution options, hell, no fullscreen option either.

Do you like anime? Do you like visual novels? Do you like World War 2 trivia? Do you want to bang a tank?

If you answered yes to all of these questions then Panzermadels: Tank Dating Simulator is the game for you.

I got this game after watching it be played by a few YouTubers and since then I haven’t looked back. The idea of this game is fun, the characters are fun (yet oddly attractive), the story is fun and in my opinion the ending really suits the style of the game.

This is not a powerful and gritty visual novel, this isn’t a branching story with multiple endings and it most certainly isn’t a game that you want to tell your friends you bought. It is what it is, a tank dating simulator.

Pros
- Lots of interesting characters
- Lots of funny tank related puns
- All of the usual troupes that you would expect to find in a generic anime or manga
- Really rewarding dialog
- Secret British tank girl
- Strangely attractive tanks
- A really strong antagonist

Cons
- Story can really only be played through 5 times before it gets repetitive
- Some of the final scenes feel quite rushed
- The ending of the game doesn’t change depending on the tank chosen
- Can’t bang Bob Semple

Overall I’d give this a solid 9/10 with higher marks available when the StuG DLC is added or you can bang Bob Semple.

The Bad :
