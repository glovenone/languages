About This Game

Saints Row 2 brings true freedom to open-world gaming. Players can play as who they want, how they want, and with whomever they want in this sequel to the much acclaimed and tremendously successful Saints Row.
Set years after the original, the player finds himself in a Stilwater both familiar and strange and challenged with bringing the Saints back as the rightful kings of Stilwater and bringing vengeance to those who wronged him.
  • Limitless Customization - Play as fully customizable characters that are male, female or something in between.
    - Vehicles can be visually customized as well as performance tuned.
    - Cribs and even gangs all have an amazing degree of detailed customization options.
  • Multiplayer - Co-op full story campaign has seamless integration (for example one player drives while the other shoots).
    - Competitive MP pushes the boundaries of immersion in a living Stillwater environment fully populated with police, innocent bystanders and rival gangs.
  • Killer Combat and Awesome Vehicles - Planes, helicopters, motorcycles,boats and cars can be piloted and used as weapons. On the ground new combat options include melee, fine aim, and human shield.
  • Freedom to Explore - More missions, activities, diversions, races, weapons, vehicles, cribs, city districts, and interiors than ever before. Over 40 story missions with additional bonus missions take place in a transformed Stilwater that is over 50% larger than before.

I'm loving this game! It's best game release on PC, although it has some minor flaws.

+ True GTA clone;

+ Best map and very interesting places to explore;
+ Large variety of weapons;
+ Homies can help you to fight with police / gangs, as they fight pretty good;
+ Many Cribs that you can purchase;
+ Excellent customization and lots of clothes / jewelry / to choose from;
+ Many shops across map;
+ Ability to purchase songs for your own, as there are music shops - a feature missing in later games.

- Slightly poor PC port that lags with 6-18 fps on computers with older or dual core CPUs, You need to install Gentleman of Row mod made by IdolNinja that fixes many bugs in game and add a lot of new content;
- The game might run at faster speed - install Powertools plugin in this case;
- Homies have slightly stupid AI - they can gets stuck at various places and obstacles and might be impossible to make they following again - then there's no choice but to dismissing current homies and to call / recruit another ones. They also gets dismissed if you go too away from them. Unfortunately, if any of stuck homies was one from phone contacts, that homie cannot be called again for that game's session;
- In pause / crib / shop menus navigating controls are very weird - with Ctrl / Shift for navigating next / previous page and arrow keys for rest, as the mouse cannot be used there at all, only keyboard;
- On title and savegame select screens the mouse is also not usable.

I'm running the game game on...

Dell Precision 3400
Core 2 Quad 9450
8 GB RAM ECC
Nvidia GeForce 730 DDR5 64-bit 2 GB
HDD 2 x 80 GB + 1 x 160 GB
Logitech G110 gaming keyboard

.. and with Gentleman of Row mod installed it runs almost all time at nearly 60 fps with small dips, but FPS in my copy never goes below 35 - 40 fps. As average I'd recommend this game to anyone liking GTA style games, as some of the features present in this Saints Row game was stripped off from later Saints Row games. Saint Row 1 might be also good, but it's not available for PC, as it is only accessible for people owning a XBOX360 console.

Saints Row 2 is much more serious than later games of the saga (e.g. no dubstep guns, zombie islands and other hilarious things). However, it doesn't mean it's less fun to play. Furthermore, I enjoyed playing the main campagin much more. So, here are my favourite things about Sants Row 2:
1. The city is constantly living - every person on the streets feels unique, with his own reactions to your actions, you can find people talking about the latest events happened in the game, there are skateboarders, street musicians, homeless people and much more. The town itself is not just a bunch of buldings put up together - every district is unique, a lot of places are playable (you can even visit the office of Volition, the developer of SR :) ).
2. The story makes sense. If you played SR: The Third/ SR 4, you know what I'm talking about ^_^
3. Humor - unlike in the later games, SR 2 doesn't need a crazy situation to occur in order to be funny.
4. Customization. It just works, it's fun to play with, and it gives your character some sort of unique style and, well, character :)
As for the conclution I should say that the game is very atmospheric, yet doesn't have any strict and "correct" playtrough, and altogether feels free and unobtrusive.
