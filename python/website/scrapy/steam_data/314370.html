**QUICK TOUGHTS**

Boxlife is a short first person puzzle game in the same vein and stylistic approach as Fancy Skulls, the developer's previous creation. You're thrown into a large room 'Alice in Wonderland' style and are tasked with not only finding four glowing orbs to escape, but also some basic gaming features like jumping and croucing (which is replaced by shrinking), and even flying. The more you discover, the more orbs you find, as well as additional secrets (9 total at the time of writing this) that count towards the game's one and only achievement. It takes about 30 minutes to finish, maybe an hour without a guide (someone posted a completion video is posted in the guide to make it easier). For about a dollar, Boxlife is a decent time waster.

I'm not entirely sure what I just played. The things that are sure, are that it is a First person puzzle exploration game in an obviously constructed world that serves no purpose but to make you jump through hoops and explore the area you are confined to.

As you explore the area, you encounter and pick up new abilities; however, these are pretty much standard, except for a few original ideas. Design needs some work... there are few things to do with each of the individual abilities except use them to get to the next ability. Extremely linear game play, and not really that interesting.

Price: for $0.99 I suppose it's not bad, but I'd prefer it in a bundle... or gifted from a friend with a spare key.

Overall: 4/10 - If you find all the secrets, there really is no replay value.

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 14 January
I WAS GIVEN A FREE COPY OF THIS GAME FROM THE DEVELOPER

If you’d prefer a video review, have a look here: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lMFoEQxXGBY

This is an odd one for me. Nothing about the screenshots or trailers for Boxlife interested me, but the description: “Gain abilities, find secrets and escape from the box.” peaked my interest.

I’m going to be blunt; I don’t think this is a good game. I don’t take pleasure in giving negative reviews, and I even asked my followers on Twitter if it would be worth posting an almost entirely negative review. The conclusion I came to is that it would, because it’s my responsibility as a reviewer to lay the truth bare regardless of how I think I’m going to make the developer feel if they see it.So, here’s the truth laid bare.


Presentation
Boxlife is not a pretty game, especially since I’ve seen some really awesome stuff come from the Unity engine, that this game was made in. The colours don’t go well together, and there really isn’t any art. It’s all made up of shapes, and rectangles which there is nothing inherently wrong with, but it doesn’t really feel self-aware. I don’t get the impression that this was the planned look for the game, or that any real thought was put into the design. And the cheap atmosphere is made worse by the really awful sound effects in which you can hear the background noise for -- since there’s no music in the game to mask it.

Mechanics
The idea behind the mechanics is an old one, but not necessarily bad. You’re basically given free reign to explore wherever you want, and you're given no instructions other than the controls. What you find is that your character keeps finding new abilities that allow him/her to explore more and, in turn, acquire even more abilities. These range from jumping, to double jumping, to… being able to walk through certain blocks.. It’s not anything special, but I felt a twinge of satisfaction when I found a new ability that allowed me to progress further, but that’s no different to, say, any Zelda game.

Verdict
The real deal breaker here is that the game is only 15 minutes long. Or, at least, that’s how long it took me to complete it. There’s nothing necessarily wrong with that, because a 15 minute story, if told well, can be extremely compelling. For $3, a really good 15 minutes of story would totally be worth it my opinion. But you might have noticed that I didn’t include a story section in this review like I normally do. There is no story and, because of that, I think this game is nothing short of a rip off. There’s no one I could recommend this game to, unfortunately.

UPDATE: The following review is a review for the game BEFORE the first patch. I have played the game after the patch was released and it heavily improves how empty the game feels, which I state in my review. Please take this into consideration in regards to reading my review.

I saw this game on the 'Upcoming' section on the Steam Store and I was intrigued. Mainly by the description and the simplicity of it. I bought it when it came out (today) and when I finished it, I found that I enjoyed it, but it felt quite lacking.

We'll start with the flaws. You can tell that there is not a lot to the game from the trailer, but at times I found myself thinking that it was quite bland. I do wish that there was a story to go along with 'the box' or why you're in there, but there is no story apparent. I'm not sure exactly what the dev was trying to do with this game because if you add more sounds (which there aren't that many) and dampen the colours, it could look quite eerie, but that's not what came across.

But as you can tell, I do recommend this game, and here's why. I have already said that I really did enjoy this and that's because of how well it is put together. The game doesn't tell you what to do exactly, but it is almost always clear what you should do or where you should go and it all manages to just flow. It succeeds in creating a rewarding feeling when you manage to find a secret or your next ability. I didn't find all of the secrets, but I will probably come back to this game,

All in all, I found boxlife to be an enjoyable and rewarding experience, albeit a little too simple. I hope that the dev comes back to this game with a few more unique features to make it feel less empty.
