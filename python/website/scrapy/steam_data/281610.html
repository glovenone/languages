About This Game

HOMEWORLD: DESERTS OF KHARAK

A ground-based RTS prequel to the classic Homeworld games. Assemble your fleet and lead them to victory on the shifting sands of Kharak in this compelling strategy game. Crafted by Blackbird Interactive, a studio founded by veterans of Homeworld and Company of Heroes, Homeworld: Deserts of Kharak takes players to the deserts of Kharak where danger lurks over every dune.

EXPERIENCE AN ORIGIN STORY
In Deserts of Kharak’s campaign, an anomaly is discovered deep in the southern desert. An expedition is sent, but the story is not that simple. Discover the story of Rachel S'jet as she leads her people on a quest to find their destiny in an exciting campaign.

HEAVY DUTY HARDWARE
Take control of fleets of land and air vehicles – from nimble light attack vehicles to massive heavyweight cruisers – built to conquer the desert.

MASSIVE ENVIRONMENTS
Fight your way across massive dune seas, deep craters, and hostile canyons in sprawling maps – all in unprecedented fidelity and scale.

TACTICAL COMBAT
Position your fleet to take advantage of the high ground or create ambushes across dune lines in terrestrial combat. Attack your enemies’ resource operations, or assault their military head-on.

STRATEGIC DECISIONS
Meaningful choices in resource management, fleet composition, and technology research directly impact your chances of survival.

ONLINE MULTIPLAYER
Compete online solo or with friends in a variety of game modes, including ranked play, to rise through the ranks and become the most powerful Fleet Captain on Kharak.


Before you can find your Homeworld, you must first unlock the secrets of the desert.

Homeworld: Deserts of Kharak is a great game in concept. It delivers a really nice RTS experience with very beautiful graphics. Where it lacks however is the amount of content that the creators provided. Multiplayer maps are lacking, and although a few "new factions" were introduced, they aren't visually all that discerning. However, having said that, this title lacks allot of core or basic "old school" innovations. In some sense the game tactics should’ve advanced forward, not backwards in the Homeworld franchise.

If you are intending to buy this game, you might wait until it's on discount. As it stands, and despite the efforts of the developers, for the full price it's probably not a recommend purchase.

Deserts of Kharak is the planet-bound Homeworld game we've been waiting for. Take all of the tactical adjustments you made in space and map them on a three dimensional, ground battlefield and that's what you get here. Numerous times I found myself sweeping in a unit of AAV's to deploy smoke to delay an advance of enemy railguns; or cycling between fighters and bombers to suppress my opponent's ability to produce new units.

I reluctantly requested a refund on this one today, even though I very much liked the overall graphics, the gameplay, and pretty much everything about it. Unfortunately, the one thing that I didn't like was the proverbial "game killer" for me.

Here's the thing: You've got a good variety of units, all of them with very nice graphics, the combat effects are quite nice - great explosions and so on -- but you can't zoom out your view far enough to really see what's going on. To do that - to zoom out to a range where you can get a large enough picture to let you make solid tactical decisions, to see where the enemy are, and intelligently direct your forces to properly respond to the attacks -- you have to switch from the nice graphics that make this so nice to the eye into a high altitude radar-like view where all the units are little colored dots or hollow triangles moving around on a flat 2D map. Take a look at the FIVE video clips shown on the Steam store page. You can go through almost all of all five of them and what you see are almost exclusively shots of the good looking unit graphics, a few cut scenes, and more nice closeups of units showing how nice they look. BUT to see what you'll be looking at for at least 50% of the time, jump to the 21 second point of the fifth video and you'll see the dots and hollow triangles that are what you see every time you pan out to get a larger tactical of the full strategic view.

Yes, they make it easy to swich between close up and zoomed out - just touch the space bar - but they have to make it easy, 'cause you have to spend so much time in the zoomed out view, it has to be easy to access, right?

Why, oh WHY, make a game with such pretty graphics for their units, and their buildings, and then make you constantly switch to the view that robs the game of all those loveingly crafted components? *sighs heavily*

So - when someone posts a mod that lets you zoom waaay out and still see the decent graphics (they can even be tiny little units!) instead of dots and hollow triangles - someone email me and I'll re-purchase this one. I really, really wanted to like it (played both of the Homeworld games for years) but...honestly if I wanted to watch little dots moving around on the screen, I could get something made back in the 1980s.

Sorry, Devs - you made a very pretty game -- too bad you can't appreciate how nice the graphics are for over half the time you're playing.

Homeworld: Deserts of Kharak, the fourth game of the homeworld franchise and its first non-spacial RTS. In short words: Worth every single cent you put on it.

Warning: I never played any other homeworld game, I didn't know its story nor had read about it before playing this game. But I'm an RTS Veteran, I've been playing RTSes since WarCraft 2, going through all games from the C&C Franchise, AoE and AoM Franchises, StarCraft 1, BW and 2, Company of Heroes 2, Grey Goo and many others.

Singleplayer:

I started playing the campaign, hoping for a few hours of good story telling, that is not what I got. I got an outstanding, catchy story that made me complete forget about my schedule and keep playing, the famous "one more mission, just one, I want to know what happens". Solid storyline, characters development is there, and as the story goes, you get to know more of each one, including side characters.

Playthrough is not repetitive, levels have good variety and there is replayability for its campaign. It mays sound odd, playing the campaign again, despite being the same missions, the combination of reactive army + RNG makes the singleplayer extremely interesting to play over and over again. What do I mean with reactive army? the opponent's composition is based on yours, even on easy difficulty, imagine now in the hardest one.

The whole game is beautiful, and it has in-game scripted moments, that gives youa feel that what you play is what you watch (the player never feels dislocated like watching a movie instead of playing the game). Immersive, if you want a "gamer like" word.

Multiplayer:

I bought it when patch 1.0.1 was live. It had its flaws, won't go easy on this point, netcode was unstable, we had no replays, and just 2 factions, that was back in February 20th. Now we are in the version 1.2.1 (June 24th), after patches 1.0.2, 1.0.3, 1.1, 1.1.1 and 1.2! And A LOT HAS CHANGED, AND FOR BETTER!

Replay system was implemented, new maps were added, netcode was fixed, tweaks in-game made the user experience a lot better. Two more sub factions were released as paid DLCs (worth the cost, they are cheap and add a lot of diversity to the game), and a new future DLC was hinted (no official word about it but other players and I speculate a singleplayer DLC is coming).

Balance has been tweaked a lot to the point that DoK is one of the few RTSes in which you don't have to tech up to get imbalanced units, or do timings following a strict and 100% effective micro/macro management. No, the game is fluid, it allows you to set your pace and fight your opponent based on your rhythm.

It is most important "feature", the thing that you should master to be good in DoK's multiplayer, is Game knowledge combined Map awareness. Positional play is the key, to the point you can turn the tide of a Battle if you know how and where to place your army. But it is not restricted and mandatory like Company of Heroes 2, where you must secure territories in order to have fuel and ammunition.

There are strong units, but everything has a counter. There are "super weapons" that, despite the name I gave them, can't win the game, and it ends up falling into the player hands the responsibility to use such weapon as a tide turning tool combined with his army in order to win the game.

Development:

As I said, in 4 months 6 patches were released, 7 if you consider that 1.0.1 was released in early February. Blackbird Interactive is a small studio when compared with other companies, and yet they are able to consistently update the game, deliver content (paid and for free), hear feedback from the community and answer to it.

Here it is a little something that may sound as me using my "bragging rights": During a chat in one of the communities hub (the most active Discord server related to homeworld franchise) I mentioned that would be cool to race a "random" faction, so I could enjoy the game at its fullest by just having the faction selected by the game (I play as random in pretty much every other RTS). A few weeks later, this feature was implemented. By no means I'm saying it was because of me, but one thing is for sure: Devs listen, acknowledge, and if it is reasonable, they implement it.

And just recently they added support to OS X! You have an PC and a Mac? No problem, the game you bought back in Feb is now compatible with both platforms!

I have a lot of faith in these guys, and I hope that with them DoK only has one future: a brighter one.

Extras:

Expedition Guide: If you're like me, a complete newbie to the homeworld universe, the expedition guide is an excellent acquisition. I read it after finishing the SP by the first time and I was in a awe with the amount of backstory they developed for this game. I only felt like this with a game once: When I was a little boy and had bought StarCraft 1 that came with a huge manual telling the lore of each from beginning of time. If you're a Lore lover, expedition guide is a must.

Soundtrack: Love good songs? like a desert kind soundtrack with plus of mysticism, technology and apocalyptic aura? DoK gives you 111 tracks of it, in FLAC! Instabuy for me, it may be for you too.

Soban and Khaaneph Fleet packs: here is where things get tricky. In a DLC filled world, adding factions to a game as paid DLC could be called Pay2Win, and that could not be more far from true with DoK.

DoK Factions introduce, by a small fee, changes in the factions, adding new mechanics, new skins to existent units, and new ways to play it. As I said before, nothing is OP, and that is also valid to the expansion Fleets. Does it worth? yes, $7 is more than fair for a whole new way to play a game that I enjoyed.

Want an example? Khanneph Baserunner (scout unit) can create a blast drone, a fast little bugger that explodes dealing massive amount of damage. Sounds quite OP right? not really, while it is fast and has a lot of damage, when spotted, it can be taken out by the most basic unit such as sandskimmer and LAVs.

PROS:

  • Immersive Singlplayer with incredible in-game scripted scenes, good dialogues and excellent audio work (voice and soundtrack)
  • Replayability with an combination of RNG and player's choice (you can challenge yourself by putting your army in a bad spot);
  • Involving Multiplayer that does not make you feel overwhelmed with its micro/macro intensity like StarCraft/Company of Heroes, but it is not simplistic enough to make you bored after a few hours;
  • Hardware requirements are not extreme - despite what is said by other players I've been running it in a 2 yo computer with everything on ultra and at 60 FPS, if I disable AA, Vsync and anisotropic I can reach 150FPS easily.
  • Extremely hardworking development team, keeping the game fresh and updated;
  • All DLCs have a good price tag for what they add to your game experience.


CONS:

  • Due to its small playerbase you may feel "alone", but there is a community behind this game;
  • Despite everything it still lacks a few basic features that I would like to see added such as: A more complete replay system, an observer mode, a few tweaks here and there;
  • It is not exactly a con, but I would love to have more content.

Personal rating? I bought this game out of the sale, paid full price for it and all DLCs. And I would do it again. 10 out of 10, best RTS of 2016 imho.

I love his game, it has so much character, the sound design is awesome, from the soundtrack to the voice coms of your units, this is how a game should be done, it has a VERY Battlestar galactica (New Series) feel to it albiet sand based.

UI is clean and uncluttered, unit control is simple and the tactical map is genius.

The storyline is also really engaging although again feels like the writers were lending a little of the BSG vibe, it only actually lasts around 12 hours but it was a great play through.

I'm playing it on max difficulty and at that level presents a decent challenge, to prevent it from being the cake walk some have suggested it to be, at least for my skill level.

If the idea of a great looking, great sounding RTS game with a unique setting and superb story sounds good to you, and bonus if you're a BSG fan grab this game, especially when on sale.

One of my favourite RTS.
