About This Game

The last title released for the tenth anniversary of the "SAMURAI WARRIORS" series, "SAMURAI WARRIORS 4-II", is here at last!
Naomasa Ii appears as a playable character for the first time, and the various personalities of the age are explored in more depth in "Story Mode", which is now focused on individual characters.
Series favorite "Survival Mode" returns as well, powered up from its previous iterations.
The most well-received elements of "SAMURAI WARRIORS 4" are carried over, while the action balance, cut-scenes and character development systems have all been significantly upgraded. Explore the world of "SAMURAI WARRIORS 4" anew, in this whole new take on the title!

Let me get this out of the way now; I love the Warriors franchise and I think Koei and Omega Force make good games, but they really need get some of their act together.

This is not a good PC port. It has many problems. Oh so many problems so let me begin.

Terrible keyboard controls and no Xbox One controller support: If you do not have a controller, do not buy this game. Keyboard controls are awful and make it impossible to move the camera around. If you have an Xbox One controller, don't use that either. Your camera will forever spin the the right and none of the buttons will work properly in game. I had to download two programs so I could get a PS3 controller working on my PC before I was able to play the game.

There is no English voice acting: It should just be a rule of anything at this point in video games that if you intend to release a game in the West, you should dub that game in English. Koei decided not to here, and that's a big problem. Trying to fight the enemies in front of you while trying to read that tiny dialouge box at the bottom of the screen is unbelievably annoying. It's gotten me game overs at least 3 times now.

Last gen textures: They used PS3 textures for the ground and sky but they used the PS4's character models. I don't get that. Why wouldn't you just use all PS4 textures? Why is that so hard? You already did it with character models so why not go all in with it?

Things I liked so you know I'm not just salty: Character creator is nice.
When you do play with a controller the game feels good and responsive.
Hanbei Takenada is the best character.

There. Just don't buy this game. Get a Wii and play Samurai Warriors 3. Sure the graphics suck but at least you get English voice acting. And you'll get a much better story in that one than you will here.

I tried to get a refund on this game but I went an hour over the time limit so I'm stuck with it. Save your money. Buy something else.

Your menu system is clunky and graphics options are lacking... I forgive you.
Why? Because this game has something weird in it that makes me want to come for more, not sure if it is the feeling of power when I mow legions of enemies using an oversized sickle or... a scarf...while listening to amazing shamisen in the background or the interaction between various characters during the battle.

The Good:
+Some storylines are great, especially "Sparks of Rebelion"
+Game has a form of a survival mode where you climb up the floors in a giant castle.
+Every character has his own skill tree.
+The soundtrack is simply amazing.
+Has a form of weapon and mount upgrade system.

The Bad:
-Menu is one of the most clunkiest things, I played SWII before this one and that had a better menu than this one. For example in order to access the shop you have to go into storymode, select a storyline and get to the character selection menu... WHY?!
-The weapons you gain on the battlefield give no visible changes just stat differences.
-Keyboard controls are not exactly intuitive.
-The options menu lacks greatly in content.
-No 1440p or 4K

The neutral
=Some characters look like teenagers when in reality their sons are actually fighting on the battlefield with them... and look the same age.
=Most of the characters lack their own story, instead they are usually a tag along "Second Player/Character" for the main.
=Storyline can get really corny (I personally find this a postive :D)
=Getting all characters to the full upgrade tree would probably take couple months of grinding on various maps.

Conclusion:
Do you like Dynasty Warriors? You will probably like this game.
Do you like feeling like a god on the battlefield? You will probably like this game.
Do you like Japanese Shamisen while you are feeling like a god on a battlefield? You will probably like this game.
Do you need a good storyline and/or amazing graphics with nvidia hair works applied to every hair... Yeah you might want to reconsider.

I'm a fan of KOEI-Warrior games, I definitely recommend the Samurai Warriors series, I have the Samurai Warriors 4 for PS and it's great, in this entry we have Naomasa Li (who's one of my favorite characters), and some other changes from the previous game. I really like it in an overall point of view, but lack in some other categories.

PROS.
-Great Soundtrack.
-Nice character design.
-THANKS for the japanese voices and subs.
-Two character at the same time, this is nice because you can at least sense a little more of "strategy and control" over the battlefield.
-Nice and cool videos.
-Good story progression.
-Nice clan stories.
-Good abilities and unique powers for each character.
-Good challenging game in Hard Mode, good relaxing game in Easy or Normal Mode.

CONS.
-Expensive and really not useful DLC's
-BAD BAD BAD button mapping while using a controller, this games are intended to be played with a controller, playing this with a keyboard/mouse is TERRIBLE. I recommend using a gamepad but you need to learn the buttons yourself, and sometimes the counterattacks and special attacks are hard because you need to remember which button do each thing.
-Short story mode :(
-Weak fighting system, I know it is a hack & slash game but KOEI needs to make a change in this, not adding more powers or giving the enemies a harder punch, I would like to see a little more of strategy and control from the player over the field and one on one battles. I feel sorry to see the lame cavalry only charging toward nothing specific or archers units being killed without remorse without doing anything special, just standing there. The minor soldiers don't have a purpose, and the bannermen are useless, you only kill them as soon as possible and say goodbye to the challenge, another weak unit are the barrack captains, these guys don't feel to have any important role more than morale it would be good to see these guys having an impact in numbers and reinforcements...
-I don't like the weapon system on these Samurai Warrior, it's hard to understand and a little complicated, we only want to have the best weapons possible as soon as possible and destroy everything with amazing powers. In older Dynasty Warriors games KOEI used a Level Weapon system, where you started with an odd looking weapons in level 1, but while you complete missions and kill units your character level up an you weapon do it also, changing in stats and appearance I REALLY LIKED this kind of system...easier and the changing look in the weapons was like an achievements. (Obviously you needed to to something special in certain map to get the ultimate weapon)

Personally I like this game, it could be better but is fun to play.

7.5/10

It would be a 8.5, but it's really annoying the buttton mapping broken system. If you stop playing for a time you will forgot the buttons and you'll need to decipher each one AGAIN!
