About This Game

Sniper Tactical is a lemmings type puzzle game with sniper shooting mechanics mixed in. The gameplay revolves around bringing hostages to safe-zones which in turn is done by shooting enemies in correct order. Your job is to clear the path for your allies on the ground by taking out targets, bearing your own safety in mind. As enemies display various behaviour types, it is up to you to learn how they will react and find a solution to each level. Hostages will move after you fire a shot so delivering accurate hits on the bad guys is not optional. Act quickly or slowly - depending on the situation.

And if you really can't beat the level, check the armory for some heavier equipment that will do the job!

Early Access Review
Posted: 31 January
A pretty unusual game, as far as I can tell. Let's start with the basic aspects.

You are a sniper, shooting enemies from fixed positions. Most of the time, you have two vantage points to choose from during a mission, with one of them likely revealing enemy positions not visible from the other.

The shooting is relatively simple. You place the reticle over the target and press the left mouse buttons to shoot. Moving targets require leading, and the basic aim is not steady but it can be stabilized holding your breath or buying better equipment. So far, so good.

But here comes the unusual part. The primary objective is not to kill foes. It's to escort or protect NPCs. They move on a fixed path divided into stages, and your shot is the GO-word for each. You need to work out the best order in which to attack the opposing force and time your shots in order for patrols not to encounter and attack your rather defenseless ward while moving (or waiting).

And you are not invulnerable yourself as your rivals may have counter-snipers among them who attack you should they be able to pinpoint your location.

So. Protect the NPC, protect yourself, shoot targets in correct order and accurately. Accurately because depending on that you will earn more coins which you need to unlock better equipment like rifles, scopes or bullet-proof vests.

Mind you, the game's not flawless, performance could be much better IMO. The game uses the Unity engine, like many others. Amongst those is e.g. Interstellar Marines, an FPS which runs much better on my machine despite requiring much more calculations conneted to lighting, movement and whatnot. This should become considetably better in later versions of the game.

There are also some other bugs, like enemies getting stuck, weird ragdoll behaviour and a tutorial which does not forgive being hasty...

It's nothing game-breaking, though. And considering this is early-access with bug-fixes being released regularly, I am confident this will eventually present itself in a polished state. Well worth the price already, and it *should* just get better.

Early Access Review
Posted: 12 January
A quirky but strangely addictive little shooter. Not a traditional FPS as you snipe from a couple of fixed points per map (like different windows from a building for example). It claims to be like Lemmings with guns but that's not really accurate either. The hostage you're trying to protect only moves when you shoot so you really have to decide who to shoot next. It's further complicated by patrolling guards so timing is key as well. What results can be infuratingly difficult at times but extremely satisfying when you get it right. There's also equipment upgrades (sights, weapons, tripods and the like) to add a bit of grindability to it as well.

It's early access at the moment but what's there is easily worth $5 already. The Dev has been very reactive in squashing reported bugs and hopefully with more levels and more equipment as well as the challenge of trying to beat the best scores per level (online) the longevity should be more than adequate for the price being asked. A little gem.
