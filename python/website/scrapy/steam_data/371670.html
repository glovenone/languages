About This Game

Inspired by games such as Sonic The Hedgehog and Super Monkey Ball, Rolling Sun takes advantage of the latest version of CRYENGINE, with best in class physics, lighting and water effects. Discover ancient magic as you guide your Sun Stone through lost Incan and Mayan civilizations.

Take control of a defeated Sun God trapped on Earth. Use the last of your powers to guide a stone across and ancient civilization, gaining power as you get closer and closer to your rival, the Moon God.

- Skills to unlock :

As you gain power, you'll gain such skills as double jump, mid-air dash, and more.

- Physics-based gameplay.

Take advantage of a living environment, powered by CryEngine physics, to reach your goals.

System Requirements

    • OS: Windows Vista SP1, Windows 7, Windows 8.

    • Memory: 4 GB RAM

Posted: 13 June
First of all, this game seems to be extremely hardware demanding (built with cryengine and features lots of foliage, lighting, soft particles, etc). It was kind of unexpected from a game bundled with random pixel platformers. Anyway, if you have lags or input issues, lower the in-game resolution. I have a GT 640 and only managed to get stable 30 fps on 720p.

The gameplay itself is as frustrating as the game is demanding. I'm actually surprised I managed to beat the 6 levels it offers, having been ready to give up on several occasions.

The controls are... well, I can't call them completely 'unresponsive', but they sure feel stiff. Maybe they're supposed to be that way since you're controlling a giant globe after all, but there are lots of platforming sequences where you have to be quick and precise, and it's really hard to do because the controls complicate things. Especially until you get a "double jump" and "dash" abilities after beating levels 2 and 3.

The checkpoint placement is decidedly infuriating more often than not. At the end of the game I even had an impression that something glitched and the game simply stopped registering checkpoints mid-level.

Speaking of glitches, they happen occasionally and become more frequent the further you get. The aforementioned abilities (double jump, dash and float, although I didn't use the last one even once - it's literally not needed anywhere) interact with the environment in a weird way, sometimes making things cheat-like easy, sometimes being useless, sometimes working not as you expect them to, sometimes glitching out crazily.

The most outrageous thing in my opinion is the game's automated return system (a flying magical mask) that is supposed to return your ball to a checkpoint when it falls off the ledge. And it indeed does so, but not only when the ball falls, but apparently when it goes whenever it is 'not supposed' to go. Situations like "Oh, look, the road splits! Oh, I can even go there!... What, back to the checkpoint? Why?!" happen frequently, especially on laters levels. Sometimes you will be thrown back just for simply touching the edge of the way. Yeah, why bother making obstacles and invisible walls when we can just punish the player for being curious, right? And what's even funnier, sometimes the game suddenly decides to go non-linear on you, placing the collectible orbs off the beaten path or even offering two possible ways across the platforming area. So go figure when you're allowed to stray and when you're not.

To conclude, if you were attracted by pretty and relaxing panoramas on the store page's screenshots, don't be fooled - the game will keep you tense and irritated, the visuals being the only soothing thing about it.
But if you got it in a bundle, well... you might as well give it a go.

I would recommend this game. While it came be demanding on performance due to being built in CryEngine 3, it is absoulutely beautiful. I have a GTX 660 TI and was running it on 1080p, getting 50-60 fps constantly. The only issues I experienced were occasional stutters, which lasted around a second at the longest, and only happened on level three.

My only complaint was both the difficulty and length of the game. I was able to complete every level, including the four bonus levels, and collect every orb in around an hour and a half. The most difficult stage was absoulutely the last bonus stage, mostly due to its length and lack of checkpoints. The controls felt a bit floaty, but I adapted quickly and experienced little difficulty.

Overall Rolling Sun is a beautiful game that I wish I could play more of.
