About This Game

Are you ready to build the Greatest Midway on Earth?
Summer nights, flashing lights, stomach churning rides, girls and guys all combine to make the perfect carnival. Attract them, thrill them, feed them, frighten them, and leave them yearning for more!
  • Run your own traveling carnival. From ride construction to advertising to entertaining the masses, you run the show.
  • Choose from 25 different rides, 21 different locations, 12 food and drink stands, 10 games, 4 attractions and more!
  • Take on the challenge of Career Mode. Turn your earnings into better rides and bigger crowds, unlocking hidden locations as you become a Midway Tycoon.
  • Beat the bank and the clock in Mission Mode. Can you satisfy the throngs before time runs out?
  • It's more than fun and games. Spend your money wisely purchasing equipment, bathrooms and ticket booths as needed to minimize lines and angry patrons.
  • From Parking Lots to Big Shot - Start small building local fun fairs outside malls and grow your business one happy customer at a time on your way to running the State Fair.
  • Ride 'Em if you Got 'Em - Strap into any of the 25 different rides at your disposal and feel what your crowds feel as you plunge through the electrifying twist and turns.
  • Come One - Come All! Choose from 10 games of chance and attractions -excite your patrons with everything from human cannonballs and monster truck shows to rings toss and balloon pops.
  • Money Makes the Twirl Go Around! Maximize your money making potential by planning and constructing additional carnivals as the others are coming to a close.
  • Keep the carnival goers happy and full! Choose, set-up and design your food and drink stations - 12 delicious food and drink stations options available!

Posted: 24 March
It's easy to tell why this is bad - the publisher is ValuSoft - notorious for shovelware.

This is no exception! Obviously it wants to be Roller Coaster Tycoon/Theme Park but without the ability for the player to design custom roller coasters. Off to a great start! It gets worse. There's only about 30 objects you can place, and it doesn't really matter where you put them. You don't need to worry about paths and things, guests just sort of wander in and bounce around. At best, the game is whackamole as you click through the awful interface to try get your carnies to fix everything. The buildings are all managed the same way, so there's basically zero differentiation. Gameplay is overall terrible.

Graphics - ho hum, awful, even for the time.

Technical problems - it doesn't run in full screen even if you choose that option, it's always in a window. Unfortunately, this doesn't prevent you from playing the game.

Impossible to recommend - there's so many better games in the genre.

2.3/10 - A Low-End Tycoon Game

I hope my Sims had more fun at the carnival than I did! This game simulates running a carnival. You can choose state fair, parking lot or backyard... Woohoo! There is also a finite amount of items you can place and no development creativity of your carnival except for placement.

For this reason I can't recommend this game. It's really boring, and way overpriced. You can typically find this game in simulator bundles for cheap to nothing.

Worth $0.30 or so.
