About This Game

OOKIBLOKS turns the action puzzle genre on its head with bright visuals, laughs, challenging game play, wacky characters and a
super funky dance soundtrack.

Ooki the monkey must take cute, furry revenge on a bunch of banana thieving crabs, but to do so he must :

  • Force-feed a GIANT GHOST CAT until it FARTS ITSELF TO DEATH!
  • Punch CRABS, FISH, MUSHROOMS AND PINK ICE CREAMS - in the FACE!
  • Get help from a super cute flying ANGEL CAT!
  • LAUNCH MISSILES from Ooki's little pink BUTT!

All this and DAILY CHALLENGES that allow you to get more helping ITEMS from the OOKI BALLS vending machine!

A 17 track MP3 soundtrack is also bundled for free!

This game is obviously a "patched" game from tablets, made it obvious by how the game or trailer, can't remember where I saw that, states that it is DLC free and such. The lives and daily challenges are very likely to be relics of the game being available for free on tablets, but don't quote me on that, I may be very wrong.

Anyway, not claiming this is a problem, but it does make lives pretty much irrelevant and somewhat annoying since a game over screen is pretty much a loss of time. There's also the fact that there's no quick and easy way to reset a level when you try to go for a perfect combo and the background graphics seem to "break" if the resolution gets too big (On the world map with a resolution of 1600 X 900)

However, beside those nitpicks, the game is really nice. I saw that the music was given with the game and I was sceptical about it really worth being MP3s and oh boy was I wrong. The music is superb and really catchy, probably my favorite aspect of the game. I like the music so much that I went on the data files (\resource\shared\sfx) and got the actual music files myself and added .ogg to them so I get the real music files to listen to :P The MP3s have some very noticeable compression artifacts.

The gameplay is simple but challenging, controls are very responsive, however as I said the lack of a Retry button is annoying.

I also expected a custom level editor for that kind of game but it seems there is none of that. I didn't complete the game so maybe it unlocks once you finished it

RubyCosmos
( 5.7 hrs on record )
Posted: 12 December, 2015
This game is deceptively simple to pick up from the very beginning, with any new elements added gradually stage by stage in a way that is challenging but not daunting. Story mode is very cute, and I love the Japanese-inspired monsters and subtle anime and gaming references. It gets more and more challenging as you go, and there are still a couple stages I'm stuck on, but the ability to logic my way through them is rewarding. I've not hit the table-flip stage I do with most puzzle/action games.

As much as I love the art and the gameplay, the music (and the way the sfx feed into it) is easily the best element of the game. The sfx vary, remaining in tune with the music, and are offset just enough from the action that they also stay in time with the music. This results in the sfx actually adding a second layer to the music (which is already very fun in and of itself). While this is evident constantly in Story Mode, it comes out best in Banana Chase, where you're racing against yourself to keep combos rolling. Story Mode can require pausing to get just the right timing on certain moves, but as the Banana Chase levels are largely free of hazards, you're free to go winging around at top speed, and you can hear just how clever and well thought out the relationship between the music and sfx is.

It's cute and kid-friendly, but not so much that it's alienating to an adult. I can't think of anyone I wouldn't recommend this to.

I received a copy of ookibloks for review purposes. This is the first game I have decided to review.

So far, I am pretty impressed with ookibloks. I am not generally a huge fan of puzzle games and so I wasn't sure how much time I would spend with Ookibloks. Having said that, so far I am really impressed by Ookibloks. The graphics are bright and simple, but still cute and go well with the bright colors. The object of the game is to go through the levels as a cute little monkey and retrieve all of the bananas that were stollen by the crabs and other animals of the island. You do this by going through and touching or punching the blocks containing your sweet yellow fruit. touching doesn't seem to be as good a description as punching, as the way the game works you can only move perpendicular to your current location. so merely being next to a banana block isn't adequate, you have to launch into it from the block across the way. you are then rewarded points and combos that raise your rank as you pass through the levels. Crabs and the other animals come into play to make things more difficult for our little simian and there are also various tools and other obstacles to make your journey more difficult. Or at least require extra thought.

So far, I have played through the tutorial and through the first "boss fight" and like I said, I am not generally a huge fan of puzzle games. However, I am intrigued enough by ookibloks that I plan to return and spend more time with it. I like that you can challenge yourself to increase your rank on each level, and with the daily challenge levels there should continue to be plenty of challenge to come.

If you are a fan of puzzle games I would recommend you give Ookibloks a try!

Very charming and fun game. Ookibloks covers the basics of good game design and presentation perfectly:
1) It's very easy to learn, but hard to master.
2) The pleasant, well polished (and very cute) music, sound, graphics and play mechanics are very cohesive...nothing ever seems remotely out of place, confusing or imbalanced. Nothing is wasted, and all that is there adds to the depth and charm of the experience.
3) You want to not only finish the game, but also replay it to see how much you can improve on your speed, score etc.

The lives counter is also a great way to gauge your skill level. Can you make it through the whole game without the need to continue or even without loosing a single life?

Ookiblocks is a very fun and cute casual game which perfectly blends action and puzzle without causing stress or frustration. It's also a great game for a parent to share with their children due to its previously mentioned traits.

Sure, you can get a free to play version on mobile (which I will also do), but I much prefer a full screen, ad free version with d-pad support on the comfort of my own PC.

One final note: I was surprised and thrilled when I ran into the first boss. A great and well done added bonus when a game of this sort manages to have boss battles.

Solid time waster

Currently Ookibloks is also free on ios devices, Iphone or Ipad would have been my recommendation,had it not been for the nuisance of being bombarded with ads, common for free mobile games.
Atleast here you dont have to suffer through that.

As for the game:
there isnt much to talk about when it comes to visuals, backgrounds are not much to look at, even the animal design is very simplistic.At Least its colorful and cute looking, with thick outlines. It's pleasant to look at.
Which is fine because what matters here is the gameplay


You take control of a monkey whos bananas and been stolen by some sneaky crabs, which by the way, as far as i know, dont even like bananas.
So now its up to you to retrieve them from this awful creatures in this action/puzzle game
The idea is find a route where you can hit all the block items (bananas) while maintaining a combo string for as long as possible.
It wont be that easy though, you can only move in one direction at a time and the block needs to be in the way to be able to retrieve the item inside, just standing near a block wont do.
There will be new additions as you progress to keep it from getting repetitive, more things will be thrown at you, New enemies, different blocks, portals, breakable walls.
And just to keep things fresh theres are also Boss Battles and challenges and even another game modes

A couple things annoy me a bit
  • Challenges are easier than the mini game you have to play to get the rewards
  • The rewards are usually more lifes, which are almost unnecessary when the game as unlimited continues and lets you start from where you left on
  • There was a scarce amount of power ups in the mobile version for microtransaction purposes, that's fine but on PC what i see is a portion of the gameplay i can barely use

    EDIT: Seriously...i don't think i can be so unlucky as to only get lifes from these minigames.
    Developers, if you want this game on PC, that's fine, but power ups and everything that is related to the challenges were clearly only made for the mobile version and its microtransactions, so please remove them from the game.
    Overall, solid gameplay accompanied by some catchy tunes, the kind of game you play daily for around 30 min

    Sadly there is no android version right now, Which is a missed opportunity.
    There's always Humble mobile bundle and im sure many wouldn't mind paying just to play on mobile without having to suffer from ads.
