About This Game

OASE - Other Age Second Encounter is a quirky, bizarre game that incorporates several different types of gameplay into one - puzzle, adventure, dating sim, and visual novel. It's primarily a parody game, with over-the-top comedy and homage to many different anime, manga and RPG games of the SNES and PS era.

The newer version now comes with difficulty settings. If the game is too challenging, you can set it to easy mode and defeat the game with ease.

FEATURES

  • Puzzle Action Adventure
  • Available in Easy, Normal & Hard Mode
  • 200+ Illustrations & 60 Endings
  • Rated Everyone

PROS: Cute art, nice intro, some of the games are pretty funny
CONS: Really expensive, lack of overarching plot line, buggy games, too many passwords to remember
OVERALL SCORE: 2/10

Sorry, but this game isn't really worth the money. I randomly found this game in my library, and I remembered playing flash games by the developer a long time ago and decided to give it a shot. The premise was cute, you are a prince who is using a magical machine to find the perfect date. The game itself is something of a mix between a visual novel, dating simulator, and flash game. After each date, you get an ending and two passwords to unlock extras. I liked some of the dates, but some of the flash controls on the games didn't register correctly and were just difficult and annoying to play. The dates are also pretty brief, even if you manage to choose all the right options, and I didn't feel particularly connected to any of the characters by the time I was done playing.

One thing that also bothered me, at some point I managed to summon a character dubbed "Mr. Nobody." As you can probably guess, Mr. Nobody was the only male "date" out of the entire cast. He's called a lunatic and the mother of the main character is shocked and angry at the outcome, and no matter what your choices, you're made to beat him up and then kill him with a sword. Yeah, that was the point I stopped playing thing game...

Also, this game is really expensive. For a game with not a whole lot of content, that was developed over half a decade ago, I don't think it's worth the price. For $20 you can get a better, newer visual novel.

This game has A LOT of possibilities and outcomes and endings. Even though the game does not say it, it plays a lot like a visual novel, and also feels a lot like it, but it is very interactible and most of your choices modifies the outcomes of the game.

This game has some dungeons that are only accessible with a character and, since the beginning, your choices influence the rest of the game. The puzzles are really god, also, although some can be pretty challenging (I'm looking at you, Mist's Photo Minigame)

This game also has a strong Dating SIM aspect, since it is your job to find a girlfriend in the game. The graphics are actually fine and can be cute and cool looking, even though it may appear strange at first.

Talking about strange, this game is really bizarre. You have some things, like the Dudes, that make the game seem really quirky, but, as the synopsis of the game says, it serves well to mix with the parody aspect of the game, making it actually fun.

For those that like games with a lot of results of dialogue and actions, this game is really for you. It is really a game that can be pretty amazing, especially if you like these mangaesques aspects of the game. You'll probably gonna spend some time in the game, trying to achieve the endings.

I just think that this game should really have some steam achievements, it would be perfect for this game. I mean, if you are reading this, developer, really... your games would be way more worth it with achievements, and also, some players really are into it and consider achievements as a thing to consider when buying games.

Posted: 3 November, 2015
NOTE: Before you get this, try Other Age first: http://zeiva.deviantart.com/art/Other-Age-39931316

If you really liked Other Age, get this. It has:

* A LOT of girls
* Much better GUI and backgrounds.
* Improved Character design
* A walkthrough to help you out
* Places to explore.
* EASTER EGGS
* and finally, minigames.

The entire plot of this game goes like this:
* The queen gets a letter from some king out of nowhere.
* Said King wants to brag about his daughter going on a date.
* Said Queen takes revenge in her own personal style...by putting her son, the prince, on a date for brag rights.

This game is quite dated, but you can tell how good the quality of this work is, especially the art. It is quirky, it can be confusing. The "puzzle" in the gallery can only be achievable if you attempt to get all the endings. So far I only got some.

If this game is too hard for you, don't worry. the Author has provided a simple game walkthrough in "easy mode". It's the red stop sign at the top right. Use that, and everyone will know how "great" you are at puzzles. :/

Some of the minigames are hard to get through, which is why you'd rather take easy mode, but if you're more of a hardcore puzzle-solver, then take this up. Have I mentioned it has girls.

Would I recommend this? Yes and no. This game is good, and is pack full of content, but it later becomes more and more repetetive. It is however replayable, and the characters Interesting and fun.
