About This Game

Monster RPG 2 is a fantasy quest that spans continents and worlds and lets you take a simple villager and develop her into a hero with the power to save her world. The next instalment in the classic Monster RPG series, Monster RPG 2 is a turn-based role-playing game with enough variety, plot twists, secrets, and scenery to keep even the old school players coming back for more. If you loved the 16-bit classics on old console systems, you'll love this game. If you've never heard of those games, you're in for a real treat!

Role Playing Goodness:

  • Nineteen locations to discover and explore.
  • Nine adventurers to add to the party, develop and lead.
  • Turn-based combat against over ninety monster types.
  • Eighteen spells for Magic Users and Clerics to cast (not including spells cast by monsters!)
  • Enough swords, guns, potions, helmets, badges, keys, staves and armor to keep any adventurer stocked for life.

Monster RPG 2 is a 16-bit JRPG. The story focuses around the main character who is teleported to a fantastic world, because his friend finds a magical evil staff in the middle of the forest.

The main story is linear and quite simple and I haven't found any side quests in two hours that I've played

Items don't tell you what they do neither what is its purpose in the game. They don't show any stats and you must try it to know if they affect in some manner to the result of the battles.

The map is full of empty space and empty of life. There are some key npc if you need to talk to them, some red chest with potions inside and some decoration.

There are a lot of different kind of enemies but they appear too frequently, and injure too much to make the experiance grateful. For example: A bear killed one of my guys in a single turn and i had to hit it during three turns using the rest of characters to kill it. This point and that fact that there are no potions available between combats make the game too difficult, a difficulty near the limits of gameplay.

The inventory management is not very intuitive, i think developers must improve it.

The main character is easy to control using four arrows and spacebar or using a mouse

Graphics are japanese SNES style, music and sound are quite normal.

It has achivements and trading cards.

In my opinion, there a lot of games better than this but you could give it an opportunity if you grab it in a bundle or you like the difficult challenges.

Terrible game by all counts. I don't think the 2 dollar pricetag is justified even then.

- Very, very poor controller settings. Why do I have to click on an item twice just to use it? Why is there no button to toggle between characters in the menus?
- Equipment doesn't show ANY stats. I've tried toggling between different sets, and there is no visible change in your stat screen (Eg. atk/def) even though there is visible damage increase during battle.
- Boring and uninspired level designs. Each screen is overly large and expansive, but with nothing to show for each. Huge empty plots of land with a whole lot of nothing in between 'key' story NPCs
- Stat increase is broken, in a bad way. 1 point increase to your stats, really means one point, which also means your HP. There is no reason to increase your HP in this case
- Enemies appear way too frequently, and deal way too much damage to make grinding viable.

Overall, I do not reccomend buying this game, not even in a bundle. Idle it for cards if you must, don't waste your time playing it.

Posted: 16 October, 2015
I just cannot recommend this game and here's why:

- Controller controls were done sloppily, meaning that the controller's deadzone is not configured properly.
- The menu system is horrifying. If you choose to heal a party member, you can use the spell only once before being required again to rechoose it. This is just annoying. Also, there is no equipment menu screen - everything's done via the item menu, which is confusing.
- Also, the item system. There is no way to show item info. I have yet to find out what the juice does.
- The resolution. You CAN manually change it to whatever you like via the config in %AppData%, but why is there no option?
- The graphics are very pixelated and poor. Maybe because it's a cheap mobile port?
- There is no walkthrough available anywhere and the dev deleted the formerly available wiki on their website for no apparent reason. So if you're stuck, it's bad luck.
- The world feels extremely empty. There are just a few cities with not much to do in them.
- The story of the game is uninteresting and generic, just like the characters. Some even decide to follow you with no real motivation whatsoever.
- The achievements are rather uninteresting.
- There are way, WAY too many random encounters. It quickly gets to the point where you'll put the controller down because it's simply too much of a bother to keep playing.
- There are no spells to buy (You will acquire those automatically), only weaponry and gear. Both of which you can always change but just like the items, there is no stat info available.
- The level system is dull. You get 10 points each level-up but one level up-point is also 1 HP, for example.
- The overworld map is none like in Final Fantasy, but more like a level selection screen.
- There are virtually no side quests. The only thing you can do for an achievement is to collect 1000 gold. Yay.

So there you go. Not much of an interesting game. On the bright side, this game's only 2 bucks but I still cannot recommend it.
