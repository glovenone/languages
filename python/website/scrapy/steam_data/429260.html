About This Game

Picture a futuristic Earth plagued by aliens. Easy to imagine, right?

What you probably aren't aware of are the people who are rising up to the challenge. Meet the Super Intergalactic Gang, a team of the best alien-blasting professionals the Earth has to offer.

Super Intergalactic Gang is a classic arcade shoot-em-up with a new twist. Slow time to dodge waves of bullets, charge up shots into super attacks, and even punch your enemies to death! The game is a fresh new take on a genre that is an oldie but a goodie.

FEATURES
  • Unite former members of the Super Intergalactic Gang, with over 20+ characters to unlock
  • Wreck ten dangerous (and not to mention ugly) alien baddies intent on destroying Earth
  • Team up with one of your best friends to face the alien menace together in local two player co-op
  • Evolve as you upgrade your agent to face the ever growing dangers of space
  • Horde the vast selection of crazy weapons at the Super Intergalactic Gang's disposal

Don't miss out your chance to be a part of the Super Intergalactic Gang and kick some alien butt!

Shmupping again!

I got a key from the developer of the review. It doesn't change my opinion in any bit whatsoever.

SIG reminds me of Platypus, and carries both it's best traits, and it's worst.

Pros:
  • Fun power-ups with a variety of bullet sprays to play with.
  • The Time Distortion mechanic is really awesome! It doubles use for helping you dodge bullets and getting extra hits on a enemy.
  • Co-Op Mode is pretty great too, like Platypus!
  • A great variety of enemies!
Nitpicks:
  • The game is way too difficult. That isn't to say shmups like this aren't as difficult as this, but it's difficulty is sort of the back-bone of this game's content. It's gonna take awhile to beat it, despite the short number of levels. It's a double-edged sword.
  • Music? What Music? (not memorable)
  • No rebindable controls. Xbox 360 has to use the horrible d-pad to play
  • When some explosion happens, the screen starts shaking. Nice effect, if it didn't make me feel sick.
Cons:
  • The artstyle is a clashing mess. The boss sprites look pretty good but the rest look like a horrible 8-bit mess. It doesn't feel coherrent.
  • The Sound Effects are really, really grating
The Review

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=617019227

Seriously, doesn't this game remind you of Platypus? I like the variety of power-ups that drop, and most importantly, the balance of the power-ups. The overpowered ones that do a insane amount of damage but you can lose them in a single hit. It's not a perfect balance, but is that really important? The best weapons will make the game much more easier at the cost of it losing the minute you get hit. The power-ups drop a lot though, so don't be too worried on losing your weapon.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=617019206

Co-Op was a pretty neat highlight too. Hook it up and you've got a fun game to play with friends! Definitely reminds me of the days when I was playing [i[Platypus[/i] Co-Op with some good ol' buddies. Now despite how much I like Platypus, it's main flaws in it's cheap "one hit you're dead" mechanics were really grating. SIG doesn't have that thankfully, but it does have Platypus' main double-edged sword, and that's it's difficulty. Seriously, the best I could get was level 6, this game is way too hard!

Also, the screen shakes a lot and that's really annoying

The real highlight is the Time Distortion mechanic. While it doesn't work like it, it feels like the Bayonetta Witch Time. You can double it as a way to dodge narrow bullets, or get a ton of shots at a opening. I like that kind of dynamic mechanic, and it really makes what would otherwise be a simple shmup so much more interesting.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=617019171

My biggest gripe are the bosses. They are literal bullet sponges, go down easily with the OP weapons, and with a few upgrades only take a long time. They feel uninteresting to say the least, and are absolutely disappointing. And the sound design is absolutely grating.

Otherwise though, I can see this being a good time killer. It's hard to get down with completely due to a lot of problems, particularly it's incoherrent artstyle and poor bosses, but it's not a bad price for a great game to play. And I freaking miss Platypus so much that I've been longing for another shooter like it. Doesn't exactly fill the quota, but still fun!

TL;DR - It's not exactly high quality, but what it's quality shines at is being absolute fun.

FINAL VERDICT: Recommended with Caution. Also recommended to fans of Platypus.

These sprites just look dreadful and I found them a little distracting, why not stick with the blocky pixel art for all of the sprites?
