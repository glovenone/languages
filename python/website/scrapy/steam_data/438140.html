About This Game

Star Realms is a hugely popular space combat deckbuilding game. Download and play today to find out why Star Realms is the winner of SXSW 2014 Board Game of the Year, 7 Golden Geek Awards from BoardGameGeek, including Best Mobile Game, and 2 Dice Tower awards! (note: full version is required to play online)

Star Realms combines addictive deck building game play with exciting Trading Card Game (TCG) style combat!

Designed by Magic the Gathering Hall of Famers Darwin Kastle and Rob Dougherty (of the Ascension Deckbuilding Game), Star Realms provides tons of playability for free, and a one-time upgrade to be able play with your friends and more on any device.

•Playable on Mac OS/X and Windows through Steam, also available on iOS and Android.
•Addictive Deckbuilding Game with Player VS Player Combat.
•Tutorial teaches you to play in minutes.
•Stunning visuals.
•Play VS the AI.
•6 mission campaign mode.

Full Game Additional Features

•Play the AI on 3 different difficulty settings.
•9 additional campaign missions.
•Battle friends face to face with Pass and Play.
•Online play with global rankings.
•Challenge a friend online.
•Play against opponents regardless of their operating system

Played hours of this both in physical form and in the pre-Steam release, both on PC and on Android.

Star Realms is a quick strategic deck builder. Both players always start with the same base cards and then buy other cards from any of the factions from the trade row, the goal is then to use your cards to eliminate all of your opponent's health.

The game is quickly learned and sessions can either be done very quick, or over the course of a longer period by playing with 48 hour turns. There are online leaderboards where your progression is stored and as you go up in rank you earn more avatars -- everything else in the game remains the same so you are only held back by your skill.
The free game already comes with singleplayer campaign that functions like a tutorial, but the full game adds online multiplayer, as well as additional campaign levels and increases the AI battles to three difficulty settings.
On top of that there are currently three expansions that change the game in various ways. By signing up for a Star Realms username you keep access to any expansions (including the full game) on all platforms if you buy them here, likewise if you already have any of the expansions you can easily restore them to the Steam version and keep playing.

If you like strategic card games, Star Realms is a really great entry. Give it a try and maybe we'll meet in multiplayer :)

Star Realms is a self-contained deck-building game. This is great news for me, as I like card games but the business models of some other deck-builders that can involve hundreds of dollars of investment generally puts me off playing this genre.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YtdiNXhtDiY

The first thing I feel that needs to be addressed is the F2P status on Steam. Some people seem upset that the game is displayed as "Free to Play" but in fact this gives you access to no more than a demo: you'll get the do the tutorial, play the first campaign and challenge the AI on easy mode. If you want access to the other AI modes, the full campaign and most importantly the online play you'll need to buy the "Full Game" DLC. Which costs £4 in my country, what's that? $6?

So why has the developer decided to approach the game this way? Well, the motivation is actually a good one. Buying the game on any platform (mobile or steam) gives you access across all platforms. So you won't have to rebuy the game on mobile if you enjoy it on Steam. This is a refreshing attitude in an era where a lot of developers/publishers are cash-grabbing at every opportunity. A lot of the negative reviews for Star Realms are due to the fact the game states F2P and really it isn't, but once you understand that you are getting the game across all platforms for one price I don't see any reason to complain!

So the game... You have three stats that effect your strategy: damage, health and trade. As the game progresses the key to success is balancing these stats as well as of course managing your deck. Both players start with identical hands and deck management is achieved by buying new cards (ships and bases) through the trade you obtain and scrapping weaker cards via the special abilities on some ships or bases.

The ships and bases all belong to one of four factions (there is one exception). Many of the cards have an additional action or boost to its stats if it is in play at the same time as a card from the same faction. So collecting cards from the same faction will improve the overall power of your deck.

A little bit of luck certainly helps whilst playing too. Getting access to the right cards at the right times can make a big difference. Understanding the game and being able to adapt your strategy is more important though. So while a bit of luck can make a big difference, in the long run victory will come down to how good your strategy is.

The graphics are nice and clear and represent the game well. It looks identical on smart phone and also plays identical. One slight issue I had on my Z3 Compact phone that due to the display being fairly small it was tough to see the detail on some of the cards. I can imagine learning on a display that size would be really difficult. Of course this is no problem in the Steam version!

I love the sound track!! It may be slightly generic space music playing on a loop but it has a kind of epic feel to it. Suits the game perfectly.

The online community is great too. I've found I had to wait no longer than 30 seconds to find a match and most of the time I get matched instantly. Obviously the benefit of cross-platform and a benefit of the aforementioned business model discussed earlier. It would’ve been nice to have an in-game chat function, I guess this is one disadvantage to playing cross-platform to people on their smartphones without access to a keyboard.

Playing versus the AI's is really fun. I'm not one for playing against bots, but I find the AI challenging and the biggest advantage is not having to wait for a human player to make their turn. Although turns are generally quite fast in Star Realms, the length of time taken per turn generally increases as the game progresses. This is due to more complex cards being introduced to the decks via trade purchases and players using the special abilities of their ships and bases.

I was a little underwhelmed by the campaign mission I attempted. The boss fight has special rules that removed most the fun from the game. The core game is the 1v1 and that is where the game is at in my opinion, playing vs AI or a human opponent.

Best thing to do if you are on the fence about whether to purchase is download the free version, play the quick tutorial and then practise against the Easy AI for a bit. You'll soon know whether to invest a further £4 for the full game.

There is additional DLC available that introduces more ships/bases and also events. I'm having so much fun in the base game that after 17 hours of game I really don't want to upset the perfect balance the base game provides! Not that I honestly expect it to! If you do play against someone online that has paid for the DLC you will also have access to the exact same cards. So there is no P2W here. Good stuff!!

Massive thumbs up to Star Realms!! Strongly recommended.

This is one of my favorite card games.

I own 2 physical copies as well as all the expansions. It's a strategic deckbuilder game where being familiar with the cards is an advantage. Games are fast, and if both players know what they're doing, are very close.

I had this game on Android and would play it all the time against the AI and real opponents.

First few games should go like this:

1st game, figuring out the mechanics, lose to AI.
2nd game, try out some strategies, maybe spamming one faction, lose to AI.
3rd game, realize how good scrapping and deck optimization is, lose to AI, maybe win a few games.
4th game, lose to AI because it spammed bases, try out the strategy.
5th game and on, at this point you're probably familiar with most of the good cards, have a few games with huge draw cards combos and fleet hq and completely overkill the opponent.

It really boils down to internalizing the relative value of cards and possible events.

I own the physical copy of Star Realms, and have played the non-Steam digital version (same as this one). It really is an engaging and accessible deck-building game with clever strategies and a beautiful science-fiction theme.

The premise is simple:
You and your opponent start off with the same basic hand, and take turns whittling down the other's Authority (health) from 50 to 0. You compete to purchase cards from the Trade Row (5 cards in the middle) to assemble a stronger fleet (deck) and then crush your opponent with it. Your hand will become unique as the game plays, hence the "deck-building" aspect.

There are some really interesting card combinations and great strategies which allow experimentation with the game's 4 factions. Each offer unique cumulative bonuses if you have more than one of the same faction ship in play.

For free you can skirmish with an easy AI as much as you want. The core gameplay is deep enough if you just want to play against an opponent. The expansions aren't really necessary, just more of the same cards types or new ships. You could easily have fun as it is.

If you want a beefier experience however, you can buy the full version with online multiplayer and a single-player campaign.
-Campaign is essentially tweaked challenges for you to play. They alter the normal rules however so your enjoyment is based on whether you like fixed odds.
-Online multiplayer is quick, fast and efficient due to the availability of players cross-platform so it's never troublesome to find a opponent through matchmaking. The game uses an account system so that you can link your purchase across your Android/iOS or PC versions.

So go ahead and try before you buy.

Or buy the actual physical copy at a game store for two players for $12. it's self-contained and a great icebreaker at parties.
