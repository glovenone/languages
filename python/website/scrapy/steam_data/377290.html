About This Game

"Looks well made and polished."
-Kotaku

Somewhere in the far future, mankind succeeded in building the first artificial beings from biological and electronic components.
Not surprisingly, one of their first applications were as highly intelligent battle drones. These drones are called Bionics. The Bionics constantly enhance their artificial brains by learning rapidly from all previous perceptions. Their self-awareness develops day by day. Eventually, they chose to turn against their creators.
All training rooms in the laboratory are now occupied by aggressive battle machines. Their utmost desire is to survive in all scenarios and to preserve autonomy.

Your mission is to eliminate all hostile drones fighting to keep their recently gained autonomy.
A remote-controlled drone is the only way to defeat the rebels by using two methods that go hand in hand: shooting and morphing.
Unfortunately, your starter drone is too weak to compete with much stronger opponents. Therefore, morph into more powerful drones in order to benefit from their abilities for a limited time. Here's the drawback: you cannot morph without energy packs dropped by killed drones which leads back to method one - shooting.

Biodrone Battle brings classic top-down action rooted in the pioneer gaming era of the 1980s into your living room.
The game is a tribute to the memorable Paradroid.

Don't be fooled by the simplistic visage of Biodrone Battle. Underneath its surface there are a gauntlet of challenging levels and smart enemies to test your skill.

PROS:

+ The "morphing" mechanic-adds another element to the twich shooting mechanics of the typical top-down shooter, and makes into more of a tactical affair. Tactical in the sense that you get 3 slots to fill with drones that have different strengths and weaknesses, which can be employed in different situations. It also presents more than one way to advance through levels, as you are limited by the amount of energy and must mix fighting and morphing to survive. Gameplay with each drone takes on a different tone, as the special powers of each as well as their speed, range, and attack power make

+ The challenge-This game will test your reflexes and ability to play offense and defense at the same time. Many enemies are relentless (see below), and often a single mistake will prove fatal. This in turn makes every victory immensely gratifying, as it is not simply a matter of figuring out the exploits and taking advantage, but being faster and smarter than the machine, which falls right in line with the game's narrative.

+ The A.I.--Enemy drones react like human players. They don't sit still or wander into traps, and it takes some cleverness on the part of the player to dupe an enemy drone into a corner. Enemies also utilize their strengths--a long range Sniper will evade and create distance where it can pick you off from offscreen, a Maintenance drone will repair its stronger comerades and skate away before you can destroy it. Enemy drones know when they're losing--they'll retreat, recharge, regroup, and attack again with strength.

+ Classic gameplay--Underneath all the fancy bloom effects and explosions, it's still a classic shooter at its core. The non-linear level design allows you to go to higher levels and attempt to morph into high-level bots, but this of course comes with the risk of one-shot kills. The risk-reward factor is seen throughout the action and decision making.

CONS:

-As mentioned, the game is hard. Unless you're of legendary hand-eye-coordination, be prepared for multiple retries of the same level. Be prepared to destroy every enemy except one on a tough level, only to be destroyed by the last enemy because your hand slipped off the mouse, and having to do it all again. The difficulty isn't necessarily punishing, but may scare away gamers looking for a casual experience.

I have been playing this game for the last hour after purchasing it on sale at a silly cheap price.

I read the other reviews before purchasing, they are currently few and mixed. Somebody mentioned that it has too many keys, it doesn't have many at all, and you can view them at any time by clicking the question mark at the bottom.

The AI is different, but not in a bad way, the drones hide, pop out to attack, run away etc. I think it works well for the type of game.

The mentioned issue with the level not auto restarting, not sure if this has changed, but it sends you straight back to the level select screen, at which point you are one click from restarting the level, or choosing a different one if you desire, seems fine to me.

Difficulty may be an issue for some, I'm an old school gamer who misses the days when games actually provided a challenge, and this does. The game is well balanced though, stick with it and I'm sure you will get the hang of it, just requires learning a few simply techniques, after which the game takes on a whole new lease of life, fantastic!

I cannot recommend this game more, think Paradroid 90 with a dark atmosphere, and without the takeover minigame. Graphics and audio are perfect for the type of game.

This dev deserves a lot of credit for the game, I'm so glad I found it ! If it is your thing then buy it, enjoy it, and please give positive reviews if you do. It annoys me that four out of the eight reviews I can see are not recommending this game, half of them, WHAT !!!!
