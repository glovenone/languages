Wolfenstein 3D is clearly a rough predecessor to DOOM; there's a lives system that doesn't make sense considering the save function, there's only one ammo type, the levels are of a uniform height and are easy to get lost in, there are fewer of DOOM's level design tricks (that is to say, putting an enemy in a corner you normally wouldn't look in), both you and the enemies are far easier to kill, and there's just less content overall. In addition, it helped popularize first person shooters for an audience that probably hadn't seen one ever before, but then DOOM came along and...well, you know. For pure quality and importance to game history, DOOM is superior in pretty much every way.

However, on its own merits, it's a decent game that drags a little too much in the middle and was enough of a hit at the time to allow id Software to go onto bigger and better things. Also, it makes fun of the Nazis by portraying them as straight-up cartoon villains with mech suits and swastika-shaped bases and Hitler vampires, so that's good. I'd say buy it to experience a piece of gaming history, 'cause that's what I did.

EDIT: I did not play the Nocturnal Missions, because 1) they didn't come with the original release, and 2) once you've killed Hitler, you've won the game as far as I'm concerned.

I find that the right way to play this game is to play how it was intended. Get into a hard enough difficulty level, and play it without abusing the save function. You have a finite number of lives. That's how many attempts you get at an episode.
