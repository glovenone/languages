Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to use the time to incorporate suggestions and feedback from the community, as well as ensuring the gameplay balancing and stability is optimal. We’ll also be adding new content, platform support and language support during Early Access.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We are aiming to be in Early Access for 1 - 2 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Early access is our time to really fine tune the balance and mechanics of the game, there will be a big focus on making sure the game feels as polished and smooth as possible.
We will also be adding more content such as dungeons, weapons, and armor to the game through the time we are in Early Access.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“BloodGate in its current form is completely playable and very stable. We gave been through a lot of QA and stability testing already to make sure we can deliver a playable product.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We wanted the Early Access price to represent very good value, to entice people to try out a new style of gameplay. The final retail price will be assessed based on feedback during Early Access. It is likely to be approximately 50% more than the Early Access price.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Our goal is to have the community as involved as possible with giving us feedback on the areas of the game that need improvement. We will be relying on players during Early Access to help us prioritise our efforts to make a challenging and fun game.”

23 June

Balancing Update - 23 June 2016

We just released a balancing update to the game with the following changes:

Cheaper Crafting
Lowered the Gold cost for crafting equipment by 50%. Spell/skill crafting costs remain the same as before. This is to enable and encourage players to improve their items more often.

Ditched the Dodge
Reduced or eliminated the Dodge (and Spell Dodge) ability of all enemies. We found that especially on enemies with high dodge stats, it ruined the tactical elements of gameplay. PvP still has dodge enabled though.

Royal Rewards
Improved League Rewards (additional items and keys). Just because.

Kibosh the King-Hit
Reduced the "strong melee" skill multiplier of major bosses. This is to reduce the chance of being killed by 1 or 2 hits if your equipment is a little lacking.

Lots of Lootz
Improved uncommon item drop rates to help players to get better level-appropriate equipment.

Ground the Grind
Increased XP rewarded for lower level dungeons to help players level up faster if they find they are having to grind (or course we hope that the other changes will mitigate this somewhat).


We'll be evaluating these changes (with your help and feedback) over the next week to see if they are making enough of a difference. Cheers!

About This Game

A one-of-a-kind action puzzle RPG adventure. Players are unleashed into fast paced battles where they must balance intense real-time combat with precision rune matching in an attempt to defeat unspeakable demons.


The Empire of Toskyr is falling to a plague of horrific destruction. Behind the legendary Blood Gate, the plague’s creator, Black Dahlia, awaits the day she might leave her prison and inherit a blood-drenched empire.

The Toskyan Czar has put out a desperate call, offering any and all fortune hunters the freedom to plunder in return for wiping out the Plague Fiends and ridding the empire of Black Dahlia…

Build your party of heroes as you unlock new powerful spells and collect hordes of loot. Craft truly great weapons and armor to dominate your enemies.

Explore ever changing landscapes from the grassy lands of the Daraghul Highlands to the heart of the Iron Citadel as you delve deeper into the dark realms of the plague.


  • Unique blend of realtime rune matching and visceral combat.
  • Over 100 dungeons to discover, explore and loot.
  • Slay dozens of hideously mutated creatures and demonic bosses.
  • Hundreds of weapons and armor: Collect and upgrade to create your perfect loadout.
  • Three distinct classes: Level up with a wide range of skills and abilities.
  • Battle across several unique regions; from frozen wastes to deep infested Jungles.
  • 3 difficulty modes: Looking for more of a challenge? Activate hardcore or Hell and see what you’re made of.

Early Access Review
Posted: 20 June
For a game that has obviously been ported from a freemium phone app it is actually pretty fun.

I'll go through some of the issues through:

A lot of the in game app purchase mechanics are still in the game for some reason, although you can click the timers to reset them. The shop system is designed around in game purchases too, as rather than just being able to buy the items you want you buy "chests" which contain random loot. However, as a lot of the cash shop mechanics are bizarrely still in the game, you always seem to get the item you don't want to encourage you to spend more money.

The crafting system isn't actually crafting, you can break down items you don't want to give alchemy parts to upgrade other items. So you're entirely reliant on random drops or random chest unlocks to obtain gear for your characters. This issue was evident in that I'd killed the final boss well before I'd even fully equipped my 3rd character.

The game seems very, very grindy, even 3 hours into the first chapter. Once you've obtained your third character the difficulty ramps up enormously and I've spent the last hour redoing earlier missions over and over in the hope of becoming powerful enough to beat a random encounter, but still can't beat it.

That said the mechanics of the game are solid-ish, the combat is moderately interesting and keeps you on your toes. The drop system seems a bit broken at the moment, randomly dropped items are supposed to be on par with the level of monsters in any given map, but I usually get drops 10-15 levels lower than the monsters.

I suppose this is a reluctant recommendation. It might become a whole hearted recommendation if they scrap the free-to-play mechanics and rethink the loot system. All in all, if you like games like Puzzle Quest for instance, for the price it's worth picking up.

Edit: 20 hours in I'm still grinding for money to upgrade items, while trying not to level up, as when you level up subsequent maps become harder. Balance is a serious problem in this game currently, but apparently this is being worked on.

Very good game, already enjoyable in Early Access.

Pros:

- The most important feature: "Player Shadows" for PvP. If you are scared about empty games... DON'T WORRY! If you don't find players, you are matched against player clones who simulate that player fighting style.
- Much depth. There is endless longevity to improve your gear by farming, crafting, or by opening chests with retrieved keys or golds, not to say if you like PvP (and PvP rewards), which is the real endgame. As usual, item rarity is divided in trash (gray), common (green), rare (blue), legendary (orange) and epic (purple).
The paperdoll is well done, accurated under every aspect. You can level up each character, unlock and upgrade their abilities; however; the quickbars have limited slots in order to prevent an easy use of them all, so you have to choose. The appearance changes as well, based on equipped items.
- High quality. Decent graphics, beautiful UI, smooth animations, nice sound effects. Personally i didn't see a single bug; it's well polished.
- Skill based. The faster you are on connecting more runes as possible, the more damage you inflict, but it's not just simple spamming, as rune elements count: they charge powerful spells of the same element that hits harder against opponent vulnerabilities (fire, water, etc...), or adds effects (e.g. freeze); also, runes combined in circle increase your defense, and there are spells and potions to heal/defend yourself.

Cons:

- May be a little repetitive in PvE, especially because of monsters, but it's much more fun in PvP.

At the end what can i say? It's like "Puzzle Quest". Certainly worth more than the current price (8,99 bucks), so skip it just if you don't like RPGs and/or tetris genre.
