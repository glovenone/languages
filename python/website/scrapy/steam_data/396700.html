Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Hover Cubes: Arena has taken the first step. It's here and it's ready to be played. But now it's time to take it one step further, and that is with your feedback!

Our mission is to shape the game in a way that you, the gamer, has as much fun as ever possible. To a certain degree, we can only do this by learning what you like and what you don't like, and maybe you'll even join the process by helping us create new maps and game modes.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We currently plan to have Hover Cubes: Arena for approximately 9 months in the Early Access phase.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“As of now, we can assure you that we have a lot to add to the full version of Hover Cubes: Arena.

We're currently considering a lot of new features but we want to use Early Access to find out which of them you would like to have most, so we will focus on those! We're thinking of a map editor with rating and rewards systems, tournaments with special events, a complex achievement system, an item store where you can buy stuff with the points you collected in the game - and many more maps to be sure!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We implemented all core gameplay mechanics and a big variety of maps for you to enjoy.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“For now we are not thinking about changing the price after the Early Access.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We would like to invite you to "join" the developement and help us polish the game with your feedback. For this, we would also like to offer you to be part of a living process, having the chance to change anything that seems uncomfortable or could be done differently.

We are open for any new ideas, if anyone is struck by one!”

15 June

Patch 1.1.0 released

Greetings everyone!

It's time for an update, isn't it? And when could we do this better than during the E3? Games are being celebrated this week so let us take part in that party-time and have some fun. We've worked a lot on Hover Cubes: Arena in the last weeks and would love to present to you the current progress. Next to several improvements, fixes and other things we've also introduced a new Cup to the game. And that means for you: More maps to play and run! Find the detailed patch notes below.



Launcher

  • The Patch-Note-Categories "New, Updated, Fixed and Removed" now have matching Icons.

Balancing
  • Medal Conditions simplified for all cups.
  • Medal Times rearranged: Developer Time more difficult than before /
  • Bronce 3x easier than before.

Round Table in Lounge
  • Go to the Round Table and play our six brand new maps in Jungle Cup 2!
  • Scoreboard always visible.
  • Cups can only be started, when the needed maps have been unlocked. A
  • Cup can be entered during online matches without the needed unlock.

Better Map Handling
  • If you're playing alone, you can restart a map anytime pressing [Tab]. Attention: If you're playing an entire cup, it will be restarted completely! :-)
  • If you're playing with multiple players, a request (see below) will be started.
  • The old "Restart" function has been removed from the main menu.
  • You can switch to the next map as soon as you've reached the goal and unlocked it.
  • If you're playing a run with other competitors, but haven't unlocked the next map, another player, who has unlocked the map, can start it for you.
  • If you're playing a cup with other competitors, you don't need to unlock all maps.
  • If you're playing a run with other competitors, you'll start a request (see below).
  • If you're playing with other competitors and are still running, but can't continue, you can surrender when in doubt. Don't worry, this won't make you surrender an entire cup.
  • Return to the Lounge immediately brings you back to the Lounge. Attention: No confirmation request!

Voting
  • If you want to restart with multiple competitors or move on to the next map, a voting starts, which has to be accepted by more than 50% of the players.

Loading Screens Redesigned
  • The Layout has been reworked and the name of the map being loaded will be shown permanently.
  • Loading Screens now show more prominent tooltips and map-specific tooltips on the Training Course maps.

Match End
  • "Restart", "Next Run" and "Return to Lounge" now have an own navigation bar with more prominent buttons.
  • The functions "Restart", "Next Run", "Surrender" and "Return to Lounge" will now be performed without any confirmation request.
    The buttons for "Restart" and "Next Run" each show some info for the maps.
  • Other requirements will be shown, when the button can't be pressed.
  • The new scoreboard shows your own position in the categories: Global Best (Global Best), Personal Best (Personal Best), Current Place (Current Place) and Medals (Medals).
  • The Developer Medal (Awesome) is of course only visible, after you've acquired it. ;-)
  • "Empty Players", who could have played, are visible in the main section of the screen.
  • In a cup the total time will be shown to the left of the players.

Characters
  • Titles will be shown above the players' heads.
  • Title can be changed in the character editor.
  • The dash-effect has been reworked.
  • The character animations of the suit and skin sometimes didn't match.

F1 Info Prototype
  • Pressing [F1] opens an overview of the most important game-mechanics.

Performance
  • Overall better performance in the lounge and cup maps. Includes a reduction of lag spikes.
  • Connectivity/Accessibility of the the servers is more reliable.
  • Database- and network-dependencies have been optimized.

Others
  • Small adjustments, that are connected to the above named features logically or gameplay-wise.
  • Small things no-one has noticed before. ;-)

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 May
Product received for free
Hover Cubes has a genre that I would normally not consider my cup of tea, mainly because I'm not that much interested in action / sport related games and also happen to suffer from motion sickness.

The early access serves on first sight as very polished proof of concept, that lacks only a few things I could imagine on my wishlist. I found it a bit confusing in the beginning to learn the controls for example.

However, if you approach this game with a bunch of friends, Hover Cubes opens up a way more complex and tactical dimension beside scoring within a good time frame. As you probably saw, the cubes act as bridge elements that make the puzzles possible to begin with, however, other players can of course interfere with your strategies, which lead to a bunch of super fun cups, both from the players and spectators perspective.
The motion sickness element is something you should consider, however, I actually avoided it, by playing on very low graphics and doing breaks in between.

All in all, I feel very positive about the current outlook of this game. I voiced some concerns in this review (Mainly tutorial aspects and motion sickness), however, I doubt that the communication of gameplay elements can't be improved and given that this game is an early access title, it has established a solid foundation already.

I tried to keep this review as objective as possible, however, I enjoyed a free copy so you might want to consider that too.
