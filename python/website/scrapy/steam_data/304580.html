3 March

New Update Released: v. 1.0.214 (patch 7)

Dear Community,

We know you haven’t heard much from us for some time, but here we are, back with an update! We’ve fixed some issues you’ve mentioned and added some new options and features. This time the package contains:

New vehicle sounds
New is always better, some say. This time it’s true, we think. Check out the new sounds and let us know what you think!

Options: weather effects
Turn different weather types off in the Options menu so that they never appear in the game world. Turn them back on to keep the weather changing.

Hazard flashes
Many of you messaged us about this, so now all vehicles have hazard flashes for any situation you feel requires them.

Paper Truck warning light
Paper Truck was the only one where the warning beacon malfunctioned. Not any more, it works just as well as on any other truck.

Minimap fixes
There isn’t much to say about this change. We found a couple of issues with markers on the minimap and fixed them.

Achievement fixes
We’ve been receiving reports about some achievements not triggering when they should, etc. We’ve looked into this and they ought to work fine now and trigger if you should have gotten them already.

Bugfixes and optimization
We fixed some bugs, among which the most commonly appearing one was wheels very slowly turning after you stopped the vehicle. They no longer do that. Also, we looked into game logic for better optimization - the never ending story.

Thanks a lot for your patience and all the feedback we’ve been getting from you on the forums. We’d love to hear from you about this patch and any new ideas you have. You help a lot!

All the best,
Cityconomy Team

14 January

Patch v.1.0.200 Rleased (Patch #6)

Dear Community,

We’re coming back with another Cityconomy patch, which includes a feature a lot of you have been asking for from the very beginning and a set of some other changes and fixes. Thanks for keeping us posted about what your ideas are and for all the comments and reports.


Here’s more about this update:

Red light tickets
Before this patch you could get tickets for crashes, but not for driving through a red light. As the idea for this feature kept coming back in your comments, we decided to finally add it to the game. Now you will get fined for this kind of reckless driving, so be careful!

Gameplay options
Then we thought: ‘OK, but what if players got used to not being fined for running a red light and won’t be happy about the change?’ You can now check the Options menu and in the Gameplay section modify for which offences you want to be punished.

Unassigning keybindings
The lack of this option caused some confusion and issues during control configuration. Now it’s available and we hope this will help you in configuring your favourite controller to your liking and playing the way you want to.

Employee work time
Employees work too long and it is possible to exploit them to earn money by fast forwarding time? Not any more. As you suggested, now it takes an employee less time (only 15 minutes) to complete a task, but fast forwarding time (still available) will not affect it - they will still have to finish the job in real time.

Smaller improvements
Some fixes to how trucks and other task objects behave during work were made. This also included multiplayer and HUD improvements, and some bugs you recently reported.


Let us know what you think of the new patch! We wish it were possible to include all the updates you requested and all of your suggestions right away, but unfortunately it will require some time and us keeping on working. Stay tuned for more!

Best regards,
Cityconomy Team

About This Game


Time to clean up!


Assume the many jobs of a service company and take care of a vibrant metropolis in the open-world city economy simulation CITYCONOMY. For you, that means: From the classic trash collection to drain cleaning, from towing services to lawn mowing, there's a wide range of missions available across the entire city! A total of 15 vehicles await you, even in multiplayer!

Explore a huge, open-world city with its various districts, such as the old town, industrial park and residential neighborhood. You'll find new tasks for your constantly growing fleet all over the city. If the residents are sat-isfied with you, you'll gain access to new skills, jobs and machines.



Complete realistic jobs in the field of city economy, such as towing cars, cleaning drains, improving roads and trimming hedges, all together with your friends in multiplayer mode. Complete the tasks and ensure that the city's residents are happy and satisfied. Build a company that generates profits and is a pillar of the local economy.

Up to 5 players can take part in multiplayer matches. The player who starts the game acts as the host. Complete a diverse spectrum of jobs together with your friends, or simply have fun in the open world.

What are you waiting for? Get behind the wheel of faithfully recreated vehicles from MAN and start the engine!

Featues:


  • Authentic simulation of municipal services such as trash collection, car towing, sewage and gardening
  • Open-world city with an industrial park, commercial center, suburbs, old town and residential district
  • Realistic weather system
  • Day and night cycle
  • Cooperative multiplayer mode for up to five players, including voice chat
  • Faithfully recreated vehicle models from MAN's current TGL and TGM series
  • Varied, dynamic job system, side quests, experience tree and man-agement section

You would think that a game that builds on Construction Simulator 2015's engine would have been able to get over most of the hurdles and not make the same pitfalls again.
Well this game makes all the same pitfalls and ignores all of the lessons they got from Construction Simulator 2015.

For an example in this game the tasks become monotonous very quickly compared to CS 2015, and that isn't good if you need to repeat the same task with little variation or chance of creativity 20 times to unlock something.
Also the it feels very little like a simulator, most of the actions that in CS allowed you to manipulate each of the machine's components, are now reduced to a single push of a button.
The vehicle dynamics also feel very off in this game, the steering is so sluggish that backing up can be a hassle.

The list of this game's problems goes on, in multiplayer the list is long, especially with desync issues.
A classic is if you are riding along with someone in their vehicle, your map won't update as you travel along.
This means that the minimap that guides you to your job doesn't do anything until you get out of the vehicle.
There are also desync issues related to "tickets" for an example a light may be green for the host, but red for the client, and thus the client is ticketed.
And because of this, the cash between the client and host aren't exactly in sync either and gets unpredictable very quickly.

The ugliest problem of them all is the graphics, they are supposed to be DX11, but something about the shading and colors used, makes the graphics feel WORSE than CS 2015's DX9 graphics!
Also the use of DX11 makes this game a no no through Wine for Linux users, where CS 2015 actually works.

The good about this game is the improvement to the economy aspect of the game, something that was missed in CS 2015, the fuel parts could have made CS 2015 even better.
However, in this game it is more of a hassle than an improvement and is lost in the troubling UI.
And the final nail in the coffin for this game is that once you have started a game, you are LOCKED OUT of the options menu!
If you need a control reference, you can pretty much forget about that or need to tweak a setting for your controls.

All in all, you are better off buying CS 2015 for Coop simulator fun than this game, this game needs a couple of extra months of hard development to be a viable game, at the moment it is junk at best, and may be worth it on a 90% off sale.

OK, here are my thoughts at this point:

Let's get the "stupid review memes" out of the way:

- 11/10 - would push dumpsters again!

- Alternate Title: Cut Scene Watching Simulator 2016!

Other reviewers have already pointed out a few of the shortcomings of this game: It seems pretty un-optimized, Bins spawn in unreachable places, the steering radii of the vehicles makes them annoyingly unmaneuverable, the truck sounds are garbage (pun intended), the AI vehicles are unerringly stupid, and Why oh Why does it seem to be raining every 5 minutes?!?! But all of these are fixable in future patches. The developer has already released a major patch within a day or two of release, and seems committed to further updates to please the users. Great news, right!

Wrong. I'm afraid no level of updating is ever going to make this the great game you were hoping for. The freedom in this games' design is just too limiting within the confines of its skill tree and XP, and aside from unlocking the ability to move a different type of vehicle, over a different sewer, pothole, bin, car, hedge, or stain in a different part of the generic looking map to earn more XP leaves too little for the imagination beyond a few hours of grinding. Grinding can be fun but the mechanics and limitations in each skill tree are too similar. One issue I had was I was earning 3K and 250 XP per job for "towing" a car, then 6:00 AM the next morning it was 2K and 200 XP per job. Just when I thought I had found a way to earn quick cash for that next vehicle the rug was pulled under my feet! Also, hiring workers to do anything for you is useless when they earn 40% of what you would earn and take 4000% longer burning all of your fuel in the process.

The tools we are given look promising and realistic, but the handling is wonky and their uses are very much "on rails". I have a lifter on the front of my Paper Recycling truck, so that should give me the ability to drive up and lift the bins without doing the "dumpster drag" right? Wrong, buddy you are dragging dumpsters again. And why is there a small version of anything? I have to buy it and unlock a bigger version with a bigger tank of whatever to do 20 missions to get a bigger one so I can do it over again? I will say that I enjoyed the mowing and hedge trimming skill tree the most of all, as I felt it provided the best mix of challenge and reward.

And last and least, the setting. I'll give the developers an "E" for effort, as I can see touches in the map and city design that give it a little bit of flair. But on the whole the city you are doing these tasks in is just too bland and uninspiring to draw you back into doing any more grinding. In other games you can take a look out the window while you are doing whatever and just enjoy the setting in a relaxing way. Here there is just too little for the imagination to work with.

While this had the look of something great in this genre "on the tin", I'm afraid there is too little on the inside at this point to recommend it. On the positive, I’m totally ready for the “Dumpster Pushing Nationals” next year!

It's time to take out the trash!

At first appearance Cityconomy is exactly what you'd expect - an unyieldingly focused simulation where you have to empty large bins into your lorry in real time, and keep doing this over and over. It lets you turn this into a career, able to expand your business (and gain new types of job to do) as you make money.

But it mixes in some very unsimulation-like concepts taken directly from RPGs. You receive job quests from quest-givers, complete with cartoony exclamation marks over their heads. And completing quests levels up your XP and increases your customer satisfaction levels, with which you can unlock skills on your skill tree and new jobs such as street sweeper.

This is a blessing and a curse - it adds variety and gamifies a dry subject, but it also sets in stone the game as a grind where you have to really work at unlocking most of the game.

From the start of the game you can only play as a bin man - the gardener, plumber, tow truck driver and street sweeper are locked behind walls of experience. You will need to empty 20+ bins (up to 2 hours of playing) before you can unlock your choice of one new job / unlock a new district on the map.

Gameplay video for collecting trash:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OVZyRNVjOWg

You start your career with access to two vehicles - bin lorry and recycling lorry. Both operate the same. You can buy new vehicles, but you'll need to unlock the relevant job if you want to actually use them to earn money. You can instantly swap between vehicles using tab, which is weird at first but essential for time-saving if your vehicles are spread across the map!

Performing the garbage collection means knowing your vehicle - for each collection you drive as close to the bin as possible, open the "arms" of the vehicle, drag the bin over to the lorry, press the button to engage the emptying mechanism, take the bin back to where you got it from, close the arms again (if you're not driving far) and then tootle off again. Most quests involve emptying 4 bins in an area. It's fairly repetitive, but working out how to be super-efficient is oddly satisfying.

Other types of job each have their own things to learn, and you'll have to learn a bunch of new keys to press (press O to show recommended keys, otherwise it won't tell you!). The street sweeper, for instance, requires you to fill your tank with water, extend brushes, engage brushes, and run over the instructed patch of oil on the street.

All vehicles need maintenance (because you keep driving into a lamppost), emptying of trash etc and regular pitstops for fuel. Sometimes this is a bit mean - your bin lorry only has enough room for 10 bins of rubbish, so realistically you need to do this every 2 "quests".

The open world, if not the best looking city, works well enough - you can do whatever you want in the course of the day, even to the extent of skipping hours at a time if you, say, fancied working at night. It's best at sunrise/sunset, when the light catches the buildings in a surprisingly fetching way.

But the city's a bit plain and you cannot interact with it beyond doing your job - other vehicles and street furniture are unmovable objects that will stop you dead if you crash into them. There is a limited amount of traffic (which can be altered for performance), and very few pedestrians.

So Cityconomy is a strange mix of simulation and RPG. There's little scope for playing for laughs (although you could probably get some off-piste fun out of the multiplayer), but if you're going to drop the best part of £20 on a game about collecting bins, you're not going to be taking this lightly!

You would think that a game that builds on Construction Simulator 2015's engine would have been able to get over most of the hurdles and not make the same pitfalls again.
Well this game makes all the same pitfalls and ignores all of the lessons they got from Construction Simulator 2015.

For an example in this game the tasks become monotonous very quickly compared to CS 2015, and that isn't good if you need to repeat the same task with little variation or chance of creativity 20 times to unlock something.
Also the it feels very little like a simulator, most of the actions that in CS allowed you to manipulate each of the machine's components, are now reduced to a single push of a button.
The vehicle dynamics also feel very off in this game, the steering is so sluggish that backing up can be a hassle.

The list of this game's problems goes on, in multiplayer the list is long, especially with desync issues.
A classic is if you are riding along with someone in their vehicle, your map won't update as you travel along.
This means that the minimap that guides you to your job doesn't do anything until you get out of the vehicle.
There are also desync issues related to "tickets" for an example a light may be green for the host, but red for the client, and thus the client is ticketed.
And because of this, the cash between the client and host aren't exactly in sync either and gets unpredictable very quickly.

The ugliest problem of them all is the graphics, they are supposed to be DX11, but something about the shading and colors used, makes the graphics feel WORSE than CS 2015's DX9 graphics!
Also the use of DX11 makes this game a no no through Wine for Linux users, where CS 2015 actually works.

The good about this game is the improvement to the economy aspect of the game, something that was missed in CS 2015, the fuel parts could have made CS 2015 even better.
However, in this game it is more of a hassle than an improvement and is lost in the troubling UI.
And the final nail in the coffin for this game is that once you have started a game, you are LOCKED OUT of the options menu!
If you need a control reference, you can pretty much forget about that or need to tweak a setting for your controls.

All in all, you are better off buying CS 2015 for Coop simulator fun than this game, this game needs a couple of extra months of hard development to be a viable game, at the moment it is junk at best, and may be worth it on a 90% off sale.
