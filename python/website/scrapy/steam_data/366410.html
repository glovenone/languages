About This Game

LUDOKU is a unique, brain teasing puzzle game, where the goal is to unlock hidden treasures: defy the many traps and dangers to obtain the riches of an ancient Mayan temple.

This game is all about jumping, pulling and teleporting to reposition a marble. Align the many marbles throughout the game and unlock the ultimate treasure. Plan your moves, to make sure your marble ends up where you want it. While you’re at it avoid the many edges, walls, traps, lasers, spikes and opponents in your path.

Compel your mind to test its own limits and be prepared to redefine them with the many extremely challenging puzzles in Ludoku.

Surprisingly good game. expected nothing but got a nice brain teaser game.

Idea is to get the ludoku ball moved from start to finish in a very small grid with as few moves as possible. first two episodes are for learning ánd casual play but then slowly things get tricky. play areas are small but the amount of moves required to finish is now almost constantly over 20 as you pass ep. 3 and 4.

8.5/10

Good game all round but some cons exist:

- Lack of achievements which is quite big con as perfect solutions sometimes elude you even if you think you got the best solution. i see here great potential for some very interesting achievement hunting. No youtube videos exist so i am genuinely curious on how to perfect certain levels.

- Vertical pull buttons shallow bright blue color should be better highlighted. I play on living room tv and i dont always notice when a vertical button is lit.

- Gameplay is grid based so its strange that there is no gamepad support. small detail though. works fine as it is too.

First impression: good puzzle game.

The player moves a luchador-mask-like ball around the playing field by pulling it towards the edge of the playing field by a fixed number of steps (sometimes diagonally). Since you must not pull the ball into an obstacle, or end up on a spike trap, this usually leaves you with a limited number of possible moves, setting up the labyrinth structure that's often at the base of these kind of puzzle games. The challenge is to find the correct sequence of moves, and when there is more than one, to find the perfect sequence with the least number of moves.

While the gameplay is original (as far as I know), it's nothing earth-shattering; but then again, the game is fun, cheap (even cheaper on the Indiegala Monday bundle), looks ok at its 1024x768 resolution windowed or fullscreen, has an effective UI that doesn't get in the way, and provides more than 100 levels.

Recommended.
