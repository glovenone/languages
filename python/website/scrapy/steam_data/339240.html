Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“After having a closed Alpha Phase on our website we have found that Early Access gives us a wider range of PC users to help test and play the game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“That is a hard one ...It really depends on the feedback we get and what users want to see in the finished game and of course what they don't ..”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full Version of the game will have a fully voiced completed main story line plus missions and side quests other features are building and fortifying structures multiplayer and more of a fallout feel to the game....The idea is its up to you follow the story and find out what and why it went wrong or just simply survive and live day to day. A big request has been diseases and cold/wet effecting you so this is something we are looking into adding in at some point”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The Early Access build in its current state is a very playable game some quests and missions are already in the game which are fully voiced as is the main character... the survival aspect is also there and some of the basic crafting has been implemented and of course lots of infected! although there are some features missing and you will find bugs.

Day and night with dynamic weather is also in this build as is the deadly night time infected ...our advice is to run away when you hear them near you!”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price will remain the same during Early Access. After Early Access has finished, the final price will reflect the additional content added during development - but it won't be a big change”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We love our Community or as we like to call them "Afters" we value each and everyone of you and try to be as open and approachable as possible be that through steam or facebook or our awesome forum on our website, that is where we gained lots of information as to what you guys and girls are looking for in the game”

Posted: 2 May
Clearing Out the Steam Library
Review Opinion #1 By Mafoosta
Mafoosta's reviews are meant as first impression opinions only and should not be considered a scholarly or professional review of the game. My opinions often differ from other players. I also never pay full price for games, so keep this in mind when reading the following...

Like my reviews? Let me know, I'll keep doing more!


Price Paid For Game: Obtained through bundle.
Current Score: [Not Applicable]
Phrase: It's early access, only buy if you are very passionate about offering feedback for this dev team. :)

The "Story"
10 Days after is a product of the Unity Engine, that is very clear upon initial load and play. When I attempted to launch on Steam, an executable could not be found, so it simply wouldn't start. I navigated into the local folder, found an executable, and used that to start the game, but Steam didn't track my playtime, so... When I finally got into the game, I must say that I was very amused by the music played during the loading screen... Something about that tuba... However, when I loaded into the world, after taking just two steps, I got stuck and had to relaunch the game. This should allude to the state of the robustness of the game itself: very buggy from what I've experienced. And this is to be expected, because the game is in version 0.09 I believe from what the menu told me? Anyways, I talked to a few characters and killed a few zombies, ran really far to meet a player, ran all the way back to the central hub, did a few quests, then got stuck again and had to restart, lost all progress, and here I am writing this. This review is mainly to offer a few suggestions to the devs, who appear active by their recent update about a week ago.

The Suggestions To the Devs
The car wasn't so obvious, so I didn't know about it until after I ran all the way to Josh. I thought that was a huge waste of time, especially when nothing happened when I got there. You should have included a zombie ambush or something, that would have caught my attention :)

The car also ultimately broke the game from my perspective. When I got in it, I couldn't use the menu anymore. I also didn't know how to get out of it; I still don't, I just mashed buttons until I escaped it's greedy clutches. Also, when I was in the car, the "Press F to get in" wouldn't stop flashing in the upper left.

The zombies... I laughed. I'm sorry. :D They're animated so poorly, it's quite unrealistic and a bit game breaking, but I could live with it if the next point was addressed.

The world is so empty at this point. I was really interested when I came across the other towns, but getting there... It's so empty. Maybe this is because it would be difficult to render a bigger environment? There was a significant amount of lag, only from what I could imagine are too many assetts trying to render at a distance.

Finally, the dialogue is quite slow, although the writing isn't too bad. I would speed up the text as it appears on the screen and make the option to skip it more obvious, since it took me several conversations to figure out that you can press esc. to skip through.

Those are the current suggestions I have for the game. It's a good concept, but it's been done before very well by several games. If you can find a better way to stand out above the other competition, then I will continue to support the game. I'll hopefully be back to update this review when there's another update. Looking forward to continuing with this product!

Summary
Please, keep this in mind: It's early access, don't expect a complete game. Consider this scenerio: You're 9 year old daughter brings you a paper she's written for class. You read it and there are grammatical errors throughout, choppy sentences, and misspelled words everywhere. What is the proper response, to throw the paper back at her and tell her it's horrible and the worst paper you've ever read, then move on to reading scholarly articles on your brand new Kindle? Or are you going to kindly offer her suggestions and help her grow as a writer? This is, of course, a different situation since we are paying for this game and your daughter is not selling you her paper... But if you are looking for a good game, then don't buy in. If you obtain the game through the bundle, instead of refering to it as a heaping pile of waste, you could offer some suggestions and move on, or just ignore it in your library :)

Recommend?
No, I do not recommend this as a game or even as an early access product. If you happen upon it as a bundle, go ahead and play it and offer some suggestions to the developers. This is all I have to say for now.

This has been a review opinion by Mafoosta. If you liked what you read, let me know! If you have any suggestions, I am open to hearing them!

Early Access Review

17/09/15 - Looks abandoned. Still don't buy.

26/06/15 - So, the devs released an update at last, so, it's been over 3 months and a new update has arrived. I'll be revising this in a day or two. I have to say, though, marked improvement. I'm impressed.


07/03/15
So, I'll start out with - in it's current state, this game is not worth your money. This is a pre-alpha build, barebones, buggy, without a tutorial and overall uninteresting. Early access does not excuse an incomplete game - they're charging money, and I'm going to view it as a consumer product. Of course, there is potential in the title - as there was potential in Towns. Remember how that game went. So, lets start out with a few good things.

Pros:
+ Halfway competent voice-acting : You can tell this is from an indie studio. The voice acting is a little off at points, but it's there, and from what I've heard of it, it's not strictly bad.
+ Dialogue trees : I suppose I can put this as a plus, there's some decent dialogue trees in the game, a lot of it seems to be voice acted, though mind, I'm not far in the game, I cannot vouch for in-depth VO.
+ Communicative dev team : From what I'm seeing in the forums, the dev(s?) are very communicative and pro-active and open to criticism. It makes me hopeful that they'll use the EA model right, somewhat.

Cons, and oh lord there are a lot:
- No initial tutorial : When you begin the game, you're thrown into a room and are immediately set upon by zombies. If you're not quick enough, you're gonna get torn to scraps immediately. If you're unattentive, you're going to miss both the knife and the gun in the room. If you don't search every corner of the building, you're going to never escape this first level. From what I do hear, they're releasing a path that will "re-vamp" the first level. I'm unsure what this means, I'll come back to it when it's released and revise it. By the time they finally showed me where I could find the controls, I was out of the first level. Press F1 for controls. Yeah. Gimme that at the start.
- Poor optimisation : This game looks something like a game from 2006. Maybe Stalker at a stretch, though I wouldn't say it gives anywhere near the same atmosphere. Yet, I found myself repeatedly dropping to sub-30 fps. No, I'm not running on a potato, I should be getting consistent 60+
- Bugs, bugs, bugs : Repeating lines - I swear I listened to the same guy tell me he was going to rip my arms off and feed them to me about 50 times over the course of my "exploration" until I found the existence of the guide. Invisible walls, zombies being stuck in objects/walls, floating objects, AI ignores line of sight, corpses still existing on reload, zombies with 10 foot arms, the list goes on. Yes, I understand this is early access. That does not mean a consumer should not be informed.
- Poor graphics, no artistic consistency : I touched on this earlier, but it looks like something from 2006 if even that. There is no very little consistency between models, and a lot of them simply don't fit together. And there simply isn't enough variation in the amount of models, I've seen the same ones over and over. I'm assuming these are placeholders, and if they're not, I would advise reassessing.
- Features advertised in trailer unimplemented? : I give a question mark here, because maybe they have a trigger to be activated, but the hunger and thirst bars just aren't there for me.
- What IS the story? : Looking at other reviews, there's a backstory to this - on the website. Not the place to have it, really, because for me, currently, it's down. I don't know how long the site has been down, but it doesn't give me good feelings.
- Absolutely grating sound : Once you're out of the "tutorial" (I reluctantly name it so) level, you're greeting by grating, repetitive groaning... It sounds awful, it doesn't belong, I could barely deal with it long enough to get into the next section. Once in the hospital area, there was just noise that gave me a splitting headache. White noise? I'm not sure how to describe it.
- Buggy, unappealing menus : The option menu has a graphical glitch over the audio slider, and the font used makes it rather unclear and irritating to navigate the menus in general. They're a chore to use right now, and there's no exit to main menu as far as I can see.

All in all, I feel this game launched into early access way too early. Pre-alpha is not where you should be launching your game into the service - alpha, maybe. That means you've got your core concepts down and you're starting to implement features. Beta is ideal, as there's a fairly complete game riddled with bugs which the community can feedback and help you fix. From what I hear, they are taking feedback from the community which is good to hear. Hopefully, this will be worth the purchase in future. There's potential there. I just don't feel it's worth that purchase right now. Wait and see, it needs a LOT of work. The dev(s?) seems open and communicative, and it's my hopes that things do turn out well seeing this.
