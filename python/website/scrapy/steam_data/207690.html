About This Game

Botanicula is a humor-filled adventure game created by the makers of award-winning Machinarium, studio Amanita Design and Czech band DVA.
Five friends, little tree creatures, set out on a journey to save the last seed from their home tree which is infested by evil parasites.

Key Features:


  • Relaxed game perfect for hardcore gamers, their partners, families and seniors.
  • More than 150 detailed locations to explore.
  • Hundreds of funny animations.
  • Incredible amount of hidden bonuses.
  • Fantastic music and sound effects by fantastic band DVA
  • Not one but five main characters with various skills and personalities.
  • No text or lengthy dialogs to bore you.
  • Bonuses for vigilant players at the end of the game.

Botanicula is the second full-length game from Amanita Design, and it's absolutely amazing. Compared with Machinarium, their first full-length (and also amazing) game, Botanicula is a much more colourful approach, to both visuals and level design. In each level, there are obvious objectives you work towards to go onto the next section, however it's extremely rewarding and fun to just interact with the environment. There are so many uniquely designed creatures and beautifully drawn scenes, I can't imagine anyone wanting to rush through this game.

The sound design is also fantastic. All the sound effects for movement, creatures and various objects in the world make the game so wonderful to play through. I've played through the game twice now and I have a smile on my face the entire time. DVA's soundtrack is also very well done; I even bought it on vinyl!

I would give this a final score of 9.6/10. My only complaint is that there's a lot to keep track of during the fifth level, which can make it drag on and feel a little tedious. And occasionally, there are moments where I felt like I was just clicking for a solution; something that is too common in point-and-click games! As well, it would have been awesome to play this in higher resolution on my MacBook Pro w/ Retina display, however there does not appear to be an option. The game would look incredibly beautiful at a 2560x1600 resolution! Other then that, this is a near-flawless game. Don't get this on a sale, pay full price. I did when it launched, and Amanita is one of the few companies I would do it for again and again.
