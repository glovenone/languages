About This Game

"AudioShield -- a rhythm game -- is HTC Vive's killer app" (Jeff Grubb, VentureBeat)

"Fighting Music in VR. This is the coolest sh** ever." (Jericho)

"Let's punch the hell out of music in virtual reality with Audioshield" (Polygon)

"Audioshield found some neuroreceptor in the deepest cavern of my brain—one that can only be stimulated by an incredible combination of sight, sound, and motion—and flooded it with a sensation that has left me reeling for days." (Ars Technica)

Experience your entire music collection in VR. Audioshield puts you at the point of impact for every hit in your music. Beats glide in slowly for mellow parts of songs, then ramp up for intense parts of songs with cross-body, double-shield, and high-altitude hits.

Audioshield uses an advanced version of Audiosurf 2's music analysis system to sync with any song from your collection - and also with online streaming from Soundcloud.

Every song has its own online leaderboard. Audioshield also supports local leaderboards to easily compete with friends during look-at-my-awesome-new-VR-system parties.


"VR Missile Command inside a neon rave, set to your own music." (PC Gamer)

"The physicality of standing in place to fend off incoming orbs feels powerful" (IGN)

"Audioshield: Feels like music-melee Missile Command-and we love it" (Ars Technica)

"...felt like we were being pulled into a futuristic trance-like state with sparks of orange and blue splattering about" (Maximum PC)

"...your music library comes to life as a fully immersive gaming experience" (UploadVR)

This is a very physical game. If you're not really fit, you're not going to do very well. I've had some physical conditions that limits my movements a bit (probably by only 10% at most) so for me this game is quite difficult.

First off, basically you stand in the marked spot and hold the two vive controllers in the appropriate hand and those become shields. One blue and one "red" (looks more orange to me). When a blue blob flies toward you, you block it with the left hand, and when a red blob flies toward you, you block it with your right. Occasionally a big purple blob flies at you and for that you put both hands together to make a big purple shield.

Sounds relatively simple right? Well, it seems to me like the game likes to throw blue blobs on your right hand side and red blobs on your left hand side (requiring a big reach to hit it in time) a lot. These kinds of motions cause me enough pain that it's rough to do quickly. I try to compensate by turning my body more but it's still a fast motion.

So I'm getting ahead of myself. First off, there's several kinds of shots to block. The single ball (like a eighth note), the "Comet" type which is a ball with a streamer behind it (like a quarter note or more) and then the stream of balls that curves across your view (which would be either like an arpeggio or a slurred note depending on your point of view).

You're graded on your accuracy (how many of the shots did you block?) as well as an artistic measurement which goes by how you moved to block the shots... did you move a lot? Were you standing mostly still? Were you punching hard or just a little?I had enough on my plate trying to merely stand still and block so the most I did was try to punch each shot or slap it down as it came in my reach and after a while that starts to wear you out.

For music, this game can use your mp3s (though it prefers high quality music and some of your songs may be tagged as "low bitrate".) In a way though this game is like Audiosurf. you move to hit/catch certain things that are flowing to the beat. Most of the songs you'll find with a good leaderboard are the ones that are included from Soundcloud. Remixes and mashups galore!

I don't know if there's any check to see if a song is actually the song it purports to be, so it may be like audiosurf there as well (where you could have someone playing the 3 minute radio edit vs someone playing the 12 minute club remix on the same leaderboard).

Anyway, if you need a workout and don't mind working up a sweat (which is easy to do) then this could be a big help to your exercise program. For me personally I find it a bit TOO active. There should be a way to group songs by how much motion is required... I hoped there'd be a more mellow mode but so far I haven't found one.

This would be another one I'd give a "meh" rating to, but since I didn't have any trouble playing it (other than my old man bones creaking) I'll still give it a thumbs up.

Audioshield has to be the best bang for the buck VR currently available and a truly unique VR game too. I would describe it as a Guitar-hero-esque rhythm game in a catch the missle style.

The game has many many reasons to be bought:

-With a price of 19.99 Euros, it is one of the cheaper VR-titles
-It runs fine, even on mediocre Pc´s
-While playing, the risk of smashing something is rather small, as you do not have to move around a whole lot ( if you aren´t going batshit crazy when playing the game)
-The ability to use your own music is a godsent, but even without a huge music collection, you are provided every day with a playlist of popular songs to play
-The representation of your songs is very often on point, far better than I could ever have imagined! The game is not perfect, as only the most prominent notes are being picked up, even if it isn´t the melody or the bass-line, which is to be expected.
-Leaderboards provide a welcome challenge, and the possibility to be notified by mail, when you have been beaten in a song is a funny addition too

However, there are also a few ( nitpicky ) downsides which aren´t to be ignored:

-For me, the sound output which I activated in the VR menu isn´t being respected, the sound then suddenly coming from my speakers when the game starts, even tho´ I put the output to the HDMI output. I solved the problem by mirroring the sound output to both my speakers and the HDMI.
-The game is far too easy. Even when I pick the hardest difficulty, there have been no songs in which I had under 90% of correct notes
- The music-selection menu gets a bit cluttered, the songs not being separated on pages but one ongoing cluster with all your songs

Furthermore, I have to give the devs credit for fixing reoccuring problems very quickly.
For me, the game is a must buy for any VR- or Music-enthusiast.
