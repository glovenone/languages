About This Game

HassleHeart is an amazing arcade game about a robot running out of energy forced to rip out hearts to survive.


All along the levels in which the videogame is divided, Roboto will discover the existence of the legendary Golden Heart which gives immortality to whom will find it. To get it, he will face a lot of funny characters.

HassleHeart is a very addictive arcade game with simple mechanics.


The player will be able to control Roboto with WASD and SPACE keys, ripping out hearts from people on the street. But the task will not be as simple as it seems, because people will escape when Roboto approaches.

In order to chase them, Roboto will find bait inside mysterious boxes. Once he has set the bait on the floor he only has to move away and wait until someone gets trapped. That's the moment to rip out the victim's heart and get their energy.

Explore five different levels and discover the myth of the Golden Heart, which gives immortality to whom gets it from the "Final Boss".

Be careful, energy is scarce and people will fight for their lifes!

It's a fun little game that is reasonably priced. The graphics made me think of the old arcade game "Tapper" and the gameplay is satisfying for what it is. It's ridiculous, silly, crude, simple, but all in the best way possible. Essentially your battery power is draining, and to keep yourself alive you need to lure people using certain items that appeal to them, and while they are distracted you rip their hearts out and replace your battery with their heart to keep on going, and repeat this to get a high enough score to advance to the next level. The catch being however that different types of characters have better hearts than others, so a wealthy businessman might give you 30 seconds of life to work with to find a new heart, while some fat slob might only give you 10 seconds. There's also hazards and the likes to try and avoid that will shave off valuable time on your battery/heart. The plus side is your battery can be picked back up again at any time to replace a heart, and it keeps its energy with it so there is a bit of strategy involved for when to use your battery and when to use a heart. The gameplay is very reminiscent of oldschool arcades where the focus was a high score.

There isn't much content to be found here, but the content present is decent. Keep in mind that this game is only $3 and you should be pretty satisfied.

Pros: Fun sprite graphics, unique concept, plenty of levels.
Cons: It gets repetitive pretty quickly, the plot is pretty minimal, it takes a few minutes to figure out exactly what's going on (this isn't a huge deal, but a quick tutorial screen might have been nice for the unitiated).

The difficulty curve is pretty fair, and as the variety of targets increases and the environmental hazards show up, things get a lot more interesting. All in all, though, the gameplay is pretty straightforward, and it does eventually get stale.

All in all, this isn't a game you'll sit down with and play for hours on end. This is a nice little time waster. I might even go so far as to call it charming.

You'll spend a half-hour stealing hearts here, a half-hour stealing them there, and you'll probably finish it in a day or two. For $3 this is a worthwhile buy.
