What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“This is a preview episode of The FOO Show. We'll be publishing weekly starting this summer, but in the meantime, we wanted to give fans a sneak peek at what we have planned, as well as an opportunity to collect feedback on this new medium from a large base of engaged users.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Until we start regular programming in Summer 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version will feature regular episodes of The FOO Show, as well as some other surprises we have planned.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The early access version works great with the HTC Vive and is fully functional. We'll be adding and removing things from it as we progress.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We don't intend to change the price during early access.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We're going to collect feedback on new features and techniques that we try, as we explore the frontier of TV-like content in VR.”

Update 1

Hey gang, we just updated the first episode of the FOO Show with some stuff that we're pretty excited about. But first, thank you all for the overwhelming response to the first episode of our new show. We're one of the top-rated VR experiences on Steam, and that's thanks to you guys. We're reading all the reviews, comments you post on Twitter and Facebook, and anything else you want to send our way. We're really excited that you are into FOO.

  • We just rolled an update out that changes the way animations work and should improve the results dramatically while the avatars are seated. Let us know what you think on Twitter or our Medium post.
  • We've also added support for the Oculus Rift to the version of FOO that's on Steam. If you have a Rift connected, you can just download the app here and it should detect your Rift and work just like it does from Oculus Home. You'll need to enable third-party programs in Oculus Home, as we're using the Oculus 1.3 SDK for this build.
  • Other small stuff: We've done some behind the scenes work to shrink the space required on disk, fix a few annoying bugs, and generally get ready for regular weekly production later this summer.

That's it for now. We'll have some more small updates in coming weeks, and are on track to post a season of regular episodes later this summer.

About This Game

This is Virtual Reality! The FOO Show is a 3D rendered, interactive talk show, featuring Will Smith. Every week, starting this summer, Will will chat with interesting people from the world of games, virtual reality, film, television, and more, and you can come along. This week's episode features Firewatch. You can explore the fire watchtower in room-scale (or standing) VR!



The FOO Show is just our first program. In the next year, we'll introduce many new shows. If this sounds interesting to you, check out our website at http://foovr.com.

System Requirements

    • Processor: Intel i5-4590, AMD FX-8350 or equivalent
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GTX 970, AMD R9 290

Early Access Review
Posted: 27 June
I just experienced something short of amazing, and quite a revelation.

But just for a little background, right now it is 3 in the morning, and I am a person that does not have many, many close friends in my physical locale. I would even say I don’t have many friends. They tend to be acquaintances in my book. The number of people I consider to be close friends, or neighbors that I frequently visit, would probably fit on one hand (not more than 5).

I tend to not be THAT good at face-to-face conversations… well, that’s not absolutely true. I can talk up a storm, joke, and poke fun (in a good-natured way) with ones I feel very comfortable with, or if they are total strangers. Strange, right, in regards to the strangers? Possibly not. The reason being, I’ll never see them again in my life, probably; so I have no fear of losing “face”. With ones I don’t know well, that I hold in high esteem, probably above me for whatever reason, I tend to not have the best flowing conversations. There is that awkward silence that I dread.* I’m afraid of failing, or making a bad impression.

Okay, so what was this epiphany that I mentioned at the beginning? I was in The FOO Show, the zero “0” episode with the three gentlemen discussing about Firewatch, the game.** Now, maybe for some, 19 minutes would be a lot ask for those to sit through a recorded conversation. The experience in VR was absolutely different and amazing. If you are a fan of Firewatch, and the digital assets and creations of the game, it is quite the experience to be in that tower, grabbing a hold of the different things in the game (like a cup, an axe, the typewriter, the backpack, etc.). Not only was that really interesting to me, the best part, the epiphany, was that it felt like I was amongst these three, just enjoying their company, and listening to their conversation. At a certain point, I realized, this would be great for a person that is sort of the shy, possibly loner or lonely type, that doesn’t aren’t necessarily good at talking to people, but still would enjoy company. This virtual world, and interaction, and even participating with the things the three were looking at, was an amazing experience (okay, so I guess it wasn’t short of amazing. It was amazing). I think this could help many people out.

Early Access Review
Posted: 16 June
This was an interesting idea. Basically they did a talk show with 3D virtual reality avatars. Unfortunately all the avatars were really low poly and occasionally you saw their arms and hands bend in awkward ways, but it was kinda neat. You could walk up next to them on stage, view them from a side stage, OR you could choose to view them from any row and seat in the house.

The downside to viewing from the studio seats is that it looked like crap from a distance, plus it was harder to hear since the voices were farther away. The upside was that it felt more like you were sitting in the studio audience except you were the only one there.

Later in the "show" they talked more in depth about the game "Fire Watch" and teleported everyone to the inside of one of the fire watchtowers. There were several glowing circles you could teleport yourself to so that you could see better from a different angle. You could stand next to (or partially inside) the hosts of the show as they talked, and neatest of all, you could grab things in the environment with the controller and examine them. The hosts demonstrated this to a somewhat comedic effect at times.

I am not terribly interested in the game, but seeing it from the VR perspective was pretty neat.

Presumably this is going to be a regular show with this Will Smith (not the fresh prince) talking about things IN virtual realtiy. The only thing that would make it neater is if you could have a friend join you and you could both appear to each other as the hosts did. I have no idea how they were set up to be recorded in VR though. Maybe I'd need one of them "ping-pong ball suits" so the lighthouse base stations would Motion capture me?

Anyway, it's free, lasts about 20 minutes and if you have VR you might as well try it. It's pretty damn neat. I'm not sure I'd want to pay for these kinds of things though.
