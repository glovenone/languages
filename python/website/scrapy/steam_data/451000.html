Product received for free
Ember Kaboom strikes me as a beginner's attempt at their first game. The visuals are very simple, everything contained to a single screen height, linear design, very basic interface and controls. The other thing that comes to mind is the developer had a SNES growing up. The momentum Ember has while running is a sloppy attempt at Mario's running physics, and most of the enemy movement patterns are ripped straight from Mario (from the goomba-like mice to the Boss Bass narwal) with some Donkey Kong Country animal friends for good measure.

That said, is it any good? Not really; it's pretty bland, not laughably bad nor entertainingly good. It does show a decent amount of variety level features, different types of platforms and bouncy mushrooms, and the environments are always changing. That said, there isn't much pulling the stages together either. There are 12 levels with 3 stages each, each stage has nothing to do with the next one. There's at least one new gimmick or enemy type per level as far as I saw (I did 6 of the 12 stages), and stage names or early platforms will generally tell you what you need to know about a given stage's hazards. Aside from that, the level design is very basic and straightforward, occasionally hiding a 1 UP but nothing to explore otherwise.

The major compaints I had were the ice physics, general controls, and lack of features/polish. Normally you have momentum when you run, it's lost if you stop. On ice however, you go full speed in the last direction you hit, but if you jump without holding a direction you lose momentum almost immediately. The movement is not very good to begin with, but this just compounds the poor controls. The core gameplay simply isn't very good, it's all about moving forward, you can't attack aside from jumping on most enemies' heads, and you die in one hit. The levels are packed with some "not a coin but might as well be coins" swirly-thing that, when you collect enough, you get a 1 UP. The overall time is shown at the end of the level but it doesn't record it or have a leaderboard that I could see, so why bother? There are no graphics options, no key rebinding (stuck using arrows for movement and space to jump) everything is just bare bones. There are a lot of cosmetic improvements that could be made; such as adding a world map for the level select, more detailed and better animated sprites, the story has almost no impact on the game (it doesn't tie into the levels, the same animation is shown at the end of each stage, etc)

Not a terrible game, it is playable but not much fun.

Having won the game in a giveaway I was curious to see what it was like and if it could succeed where other indie platformers had failed to impress. Yes. The answer is yes, it did.

Ember Kaboom is a platformer where the best way to enjoy it is to try and keep your momentum going, making adjustments on the way to avoid hazards in your path. The option is always there if you want to try a slower pace though. Jumping challenges are fantastic as you'll navigate falling platforms, temporary bridges and using enemies themselves to bounce across gaps.

Variety is the spice of life and this is an area the game excels at. The game constantly changes the style as you move through the levels without straying too far off course, so in one stage you're using bouncy mushrooms to soar high, then you'll be riding a goat to outrun a giant snowball and then dropped in the ocean for a leisurely swim. The two narwhal levels do tend to hurt the pacing somewhat though.

The only major overall issue is that the game is pretty short, requiring only an hour to burn through all the stages it has to offer. That said, I can see this as a game people would like to revisit for some simple platforming fun.
