About This Game

Major\Minor will be given completely free content updates to continue the story.
This will be delivered via an automatic Steam update.

"The Midnight Deaths". A vicious rumor spreading through Tokyo, Japan.

A wave of murder and suicide has been hitting the country, but all of the cases share the same aspect; they happen exactly at the stroke of midnight. You play as one of the "Lucky Two", winners of a contest that give you the opportunity to travel the world with the international pop idol, Klace. But arriving in Japan just as this wave of incidents gets out of control, you quickly get caught up in a larger scheme. The launch of his worldwide tour promptly becoming the least of your concerns.

You must uncover the mystery behind The Midnight Deaths, and in doing so -- you will uncover shocking truths. Balance your social life, relationships and friendships during the day -- because at night you will be whisked away to an unfamiliar and hostile world. But with this second world being the key to saving Earth (and possibly many more worlds), you can't help but delve deeper into dangerous territory.

With an epic story, and the ability to socialize with a large cast of characters -- every choice you make has an impact. A simple choice could lead to the salvation or destruction of both worlds; and all of those around you. Choose wisely, and enjoy your journey.

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 8 December, 2015
This WILL be the last time I edit this review, seeing how the whole drama surrounding this game has seemed to subside and there has been some mutual understanding settled through both sides of the issue. Instead of feeding into the drama and instigating any more flame wars, I want to use this last edit to set things straight and try not to be biased.

I still don't recommend this game due to my own personal opinions. I think that the writing is bad, the artwork is cliche, and the plot was made to stroke the egos of the self-inserted "fursonas" that are part of the game. I see no real reason to enjoy this game other than the fact that it revolves around anthropromorphic characters, mainly those based around furry stereotypes.

The drama involving reviews [attempting] to be censored has subsided. Although the developer has admitted to trying to shut them down in the past and has apologized for doing so, I still believe that people have the right to know that it happened in the first place. I do believe that he has learned his lesson and will use his criticism to improve, but I can not guarantee that history can't repeat itself, no matter how improbable it seems.

People have sent me hate mail because of my review, but they were mere insults and trolling that could have been easily ignored, much like the troll reviews for this game. I do apologize for acting hostile toward the community for what has happened, as I believed that my anger was justified after feeling like a target for having an opinion. If I have not retaliated, there would have been less drama to deal with. Klace has posted a public apology about this issue and has attempted to convince the community to tone down on their hostility toward negative reviewers. Whether or not this is legitimate damage control, he is only trying to negate the flame wars, which is the right thing to do no matter which side you're on.

I do believe that this game has room for improvement, but it's going to be a long way to the top. The problems I have with the game itself are opinionated, so you could possibly enjoy this game if you are a "hardcore" furry. The developer and the people behind this game also need to improve on their behavior toward people outside of the furry fandom, or furries who aren't as involved in the fandom as much. They have already shown that they can improve and take an unbiased approach to the situation, so I thought it would only be fair if I did the same thing too.
