About This Game

With more than 5 million copies sold worldwide and more than 25 Sports Game of the Year awards won, NBA 2K12 was another monster release for the biggest NBA video game simulation franchise in the world. This year, 2K Sports has joined forces with the legendary JAY Z as Executive Producer to transcend sports video games and take virtual hardwood to the next level.

Featuring an enhanced MyCAREER mode and all-new gameplay features, NBA 2K13 will let gamers pit the best players in NBA history against the new dynasty of talent, including the much-debated matchup between the 2012 U.S. Men’s National Team and the 1992 “Dream Team.”

Key features:

  • A JAY Z PRODUCTION – Produced by multi-platinum recording artist JAY Z, who presided over the soundtrack, in-game presentation elements, and the overall style of the game.
  • IMPROVED GAMEPLAY – Featuring the Control Stick for enhanced ball control, Dynamic Shot Generator for more realistic shot variety, all-new Signature Skills and so much more.
  • MYCAREER MODE – Build your MyPLAYER by giving him Signature Skills, and acquire your very own animations. Includes new endorsement paths, all-new casual clothing & equipment, and much more.
  • USA BASKETBALL – Finally answer the question: Who would win a head-to-head duel between the 1992 Dream Team and today's 2012 squad?

System Requirements

    • Processor:Pentium 4 2.4 GHz Single Core processor or equivalent (2.8 GHZ for Vista/Win 7)
    • Memory:512 MB or More (1 GB for Vista/Win 7)
    • Graphics:DirectX 9.0c compatible
    • Hard Drive:8 GB or more free space
    • Sound:DirectX 9.0c compatible
    • Video Card:128 MB or more memory and one of the following chipsets is required: Radeon HD 2400 or greater; NVIDIA 6800 GT or greater; DirectX 9.0c compatible card with Shader Model 3.0 support.
    Recommended:

    • Processor:Intel Core 2 Duo processor or equivalent
    • Memory:2 GB

    • Hard Drive:9 GB or more free space
    • Sound:100% DirectX 9.0c compatible
    • Input:dual-analog gamepad

Posted: 21 May
I have played this game quite a while and have tried numerous settings and on my career mode with several player positions. I maxed the stats out on my first character, a point guard.

Pros
- character development can be engaging
- some people complain about the button controls but I find the flexibility to be interesting and compelling

Cons
- I know 2k tries to make the game realistic and I am sure they have experts but they are missing some fundamental dynamics that really hurt the game. For goodness sakes they dont even know the difference between a screen and a pick.
- While I dont mind the button controls, the actual player movement in the game is a disaster and I cant find a way to fix it. You have mangetic players that stick to one another. What I mean by that is even if you are trying to run in a certain direction the game forces you to face in directions you dont want to face and these crappy animations end up controlling more movement that you do with the controller. Response time is awful. There is a huge delay between when you press a button and when the action occurs. There have been times I tried to lunge sideways to block a pass and the ball is already at half court before my player moves, who was under the basket.
- player AI is aweful. Why on earth would a guard on defense stand at the corner of the key when the offensive player is at the three point line? The number of open 3's in the game is ridiculous and when the guard stands at the corner of the key he obstructs trying to guard the post player.
- 3 second defensive rule is rubbish in the NBA and it is rubbish in this game. If the offensive player is standing at the edge of the paint then you should still be able to stand between him and the basket without getting a penalty called on defense.
- The game is too stats driven. All that matters is what your stat is in a particular area. Success should have more focus on position both for defense and offense. A 7 foot offensive player should not miss a dunk with a 6 foot point guard on him. He misses because the guard has a defense rating of 95 - in that situation size is more important.
- Coaching management is aweful. Why on earth does my 6 foot point guard enter the game at center, power forward or even small forward? Why does my center enter the game at small forward? It makes no sense and there is no coach in the NBA that would be so stupid.

In addition to those issues, the game needs more dynamic interaction. Why cant I yell at that stupid scoring guard during the game who keeps jacking up 3's while guarded and without defenders back to try and rebound? Why cant I yell at that point guard who dances around the half court line and then mysteriously tries to pass across court to a guarded player on the base line? Why cant I hell at the point guard who goes fastbreak up the court when all the defenders are back and tries to drive into 3 defenders?

These comments are based on my career mode and not online play. Online play is a waste of time when people find ways to mod characters - like COD - I will never understand why people still put out money for that crap.

From the reviews I read, neither of the later NBA2k games are any better. They are producing the exact same product with a few minor features with updated rosters and player stats. Dont be a robot fanboy and pay attention to what you pay for.

Completely freaking rad game. I seem to be the only person in the universe who doesn't care about playing a sports game online, so I can't say anything about the online aspect. What I can say is that it is one of the best sports games ever made, up in the tier of Tiger Woods PGA Tour 2004 for the PS2, and Madden 09 for the PS2. The graphics are good enough, the gameplay is great and intuitive, there's a wide selection of ways to play the game, and the commentary is actually good. Seriously, barely anyone talks about the commentary but it's one of the best parts compared to other sports games. Also has complete controller support, which is a must, because playing with keyboard feels like piloting a submarine you know nothing about. The game also is very easy to mod, but don't expect anything crazy, you can only make the game look better. Overall great sports game, would recommend to any sports game or basketball fan.
