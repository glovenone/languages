About This Game

Ice Lakes is an ultimate ice fishing game with a sandbox approach to wintertime fishing. Rare subject, stunning graphics, realistic physics, fish behavior system, wide selection of fishing gear, single and multiplayer with different game modes in the various locations around the world, changing season, day time and weather conditions that also affect fish behavior makes Ice Lakes the ultimate ice fishing simulator.

Game Features

  • Single player
  • Multiplayer up to 32 players (Online and LAN)
  • Dedicated servers (alpha version)
  • 15 competition modes
  • 14 tournaments
  • Over 20 fish species
  • 12 open world maps
  • Extensive statistics about everything
  • Character customization
  • Rod movement. Lure the fish to take a bait by rod movement
  • Changing weather and time of day
  • Change of seasons (fall, winter, spring)
  • Fish behavior engine. A fish AI and swarm system controlling each species
  • Fishing equipment. Learn and master the use of dozens of jigs, rods, augers, baits and lures

Game Description


In this game players can roam freely in the beautiful open world maps and explore river, pond and lake locations in diverse environments. Dare to go deeper into the wild where no fisherman has gone before and find the biggest catches. All maps are playable both in single player and multiplayer modes.

Finding the best fishing spots by learning the depth maps and bottom topology of each location is fun, but requires patience and dedication. Nothing wins the expectation of finding the best location or catching the biggest fish from each lake.

The real excitement starts when it is time to throw fishing lines into the water and start fishing! The right equipment for right conditions are as essential as is mastering the location. The realistic fish behavior system, fish AI and fish swarms for over 20 fish species makes this game a lifelike fishing experience. Fishing gear can be chosen from a wide selection of different jigs, rods, augers and baits. A perfect combination of equipment for different species depends on the season, but especially weather and daytime conditions. Controlling a rod and a jig in the water in a right way and at the right depth in Ice Lakes is a skill of its own. Rod and jig physics guarantees that fish react authentically to player's actions.

Fishing independently without time limits or competitors makes Ice Lakes a relaxing game to play and tune your fishing skills in the comfort of your home. On the other hand, lengthy tournaments and very customizable single competitions will test the player's skills and knowledge, but will reward them with new equipment and limitless fame.

Future development

After the release the development of Ice Lakes will continue and is improved based on the wishes of the community. Moreover, the game will get free content updates including more maps, game modes, fish species, equipment and more. We will also continue improving the online multiplayer with near future plans on releasing a separate dedicated server and adding support for community arranged tournaments.

Ice Lakes Review
Ice Lakes is a great ice fishing simulator which tests the player's patience. I've never been a fan of sim games myself, but this game had its own niche and I felt like a real fisherman, albeit a terrible one.

Gameplay 8/10
Ice Lakes, like any other simulator, cannot be judged like any other game. The game itself makes players feel like a real fisherman, but moments after jumping into my first session, I realised that the game had no tutorial. Instead, the beginner's guide to fishing was featured in the tournament category. Why they decided new players would want to go straight into a tournament hoping to find a tutorial there is beyond my concern, but it was confusing nonetheless. I was left wandering in the dark, blindly drilling my holes around the lake hoping to catch more fish.

Ice Lakes is a game which requires patience, the player must attentively wait and watch the fishing pole for any sign of movement, then race to haul the fish up. This process was unexpectedly entertaining and I was ecstatic to catch my first ever flounder.

The game allows players to wander the map to drill holes in lakes for fish, then run back to the stall to sell the fish for profit. The issue with this system was that I was unsure of when I should be returning to the stall due to the absence of a tutorial, and the fish didn't net me as much money as I had hoped. The upgrades in this game seem to be ridiculously expensive and I would like to see the prices of bait or new fishing rods be lowered to give the palyer a sense of progression in the game, instead of the failure of a fisherman I see myself as.

Visuals/Style 7/10
Pretty standard, it's exactly what the store page shows so no false advertising. The only exception is that the game isn't actually third person, as one of the pictures would want the player to believe. The game is completely top down for the most part, and first-person when fishing. This is what you should be expecting going into this game.

Good game. As an ice fisherman myself, I can say that it is exactly what it says it is... Ice Fishing. I think the graphics are OK for what it is, the gameplay is good, and I'm enjoying my time with it so far.

One downside is that there is no tutorial (at least that I noticed). Meaning, if you don't know ice fishing already, you could be lost. Some simple instructions would go a long way.

A couple of tips for starters: You need to run out on the lake, pick a spot, drill a hole with your auger, BAIT your hook/jig (there are grubbs that go on the hook or you won't catch anything). You can vary your depth using your mouse wheel (where I live, slightly off the bottom is where you fish, but other depths can work). There is a strainer next to the hole to keep ice out of the hole (click on it, I think I caught more fish when the hole was clear). Watch the end of the pole, when you get a hit, hit the F button on your keyboard or use the reel icon on the screen to pull up the line. Lastly, there is a bait shop near where you started for bait, etc. and when the time is running down, get back to that area to have your score count! Not exactly "review" material, but I hope this is some help to you.

All in all, a good ice fishing game that I'd recommend to a friend. I haven't tried the multiplayer yet...

NOTE: This game was given to me by the developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.

==========================================================
---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Ice Lakes is a single- and multi-player ice fishing game.

It includes ten different maps and a number of different game types, but really they all boil down to catching the most fish of certain types within the time limit.

==========================================================
----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Graphics - Graphics are pretty good, with convincing snow-covered scenery and frozen lakes, and some nice additional touches like rabbits and foxes, cabins, rowboats moored on the frozen shores, etc, to maintain the atmosphere. Player character models aren't great when zoomed in up close (such as when selecting your character), but when playing they look okay, and are animated quite well.

There's a reasonable list of graphics options in case you're playing on an under-powered PC. I played in full HD with everything turned on on a four-year-old gaming laptop, and didn't encounter any performance problems, even after alt-tabbing a number of times.

Sound - Sounds are very simple, but appropriate enough. I found myself playing with some ambient electronic music of my own in the background to fill in the silence, but that's just me.

Gameplay - Ice Lakes is all about ice fishing, so the gameplay involves drilling holes in the ice, baiting your hook, lowering it through the hole into the water below, and wiggling it around until you catch a fish. There's no tutorial or guide included in the game, but after a couple of minutes of experimentation I had the basics down pat, and from there it was just trial and error to try to work out where and how to catch the most and largest fish.

The game includes 10 different maps, and you're able to select from a number of different goals or game types for each map, including a 'free play' option. I used the free play option to learn the basics, but really there's no reason you can't do that after jumping straight into one of the other game types instead.

The other game types involve you competing against a group of other ice fisherman for the specified time, with the winner being the one who catches the most of the specified types and returns to the starting area within the allowed time. I don't know enough about ice fishing in general, or the advanced tactics of Ice Lakes itself, to know if the other computer-controlled characters are acting intelligently, but they're fun to watch and the competition is a welcome addition over free mode. Online multiplayer is an option that I've not yet tried. I believe it plays exactly the same as the single player game, but with your competitors controlled by real people. Pre-set tournaments are also included and involve playing consecutive competitive levels one after another.

As well as the map assortment, the game keeps you busy with the included achievements, many different types of fish, and a whole host of fishing gear to buy with the cash earned from selling your fish at the market in the starting area.

==========================================================
-------------------------------------PROS & CONS------------------------------------
==========================================================
Pros:
+ Pretty good graphics overall, with interesting environments that look quite pretty
+ Attention to detail is good, with a number of little touches for good effect
+ Enthusiastic and responsive developers on the Steam forums for the game
+ Achievements, to give you something more to aim for
+ Trading cards

Cons:
- It can get a bit boring; some random events, such as cracks in the ice, have been suggested in the forums, and would be a welcome addition

==========================================================
--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------
==========================================================
I've never been ice fishing in my life, and the topic doesn't interest me that much. That said, however, I found Ice Lakes a fairly relaxing and moderately enjoyable game to play.

It's a game for a niche market, but I believe it fills that niche well, and leaves me with really nothing to complain about. I'd recommend it for anyone into ice fishing, or anyone interested in playing something a bit different.

7/10
