About This Game

Long ago, the Vile God and the Goddess waged war with each other. Equally matched, they were sealed away in another world…

Now a lazy young man named Fang somehow finds himself a key part of this war he’d really rather not deal with, but fate beckons in this uncommon RPG!

Key Features
  • More Flash & Flair! The world of Fencers and fairies gets updated with a 1080p graphic overhaul! Fairizing never looked so good.
  • Really Reluctant Hero With a hero more interested in his next meal and a fairy partner who will have none of it, this saving-the-world narrative is injected with oodles of madcap humor!
  • High Tension! Increase the Tension gauge in battle by dealing and receiving damage. The higher it is, the more damage you’ll dish. Once it’s high enough, you’ll be able to Fairize – combining with your fairy to create a super-powerful form!
  • Fairy’s Fate Find fairies to join you in your quest – only they can grant you special abilities, and by leveling them up you’ll also increase your effect radius in…
  • World-Shaping! Pull Furies from the Goddess and Vile God and place your fairies in them. You can then stab those Furies into the world map for new effects in nearby dungeons like EXP boosts or enemy changes - be careful though, because not all effects are good!


The following DLC packs are included with download of the main game:
  • Fairy Fencer F: Beginner's Pack, which includes the following packs: Beginner's Synthesis Kit, Emergency Pack, and Assortment of Rare Materials.
  • Fairy Fencer F: Secrets of Shukesoo's Tower Pack, which includes Secrets of Shukesoo's Tower 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8.*

    *Upon activation, this DLC cannot be disabled. Disabling may cause the program to be unstable and possibly result in a forced shutdown of the game. Before activating, please ensure that you wish to keep the content throughout the remainder of the game.

The game has a unique weapon and stat upgrade system; in most RPGs the player would obtain skills and spells as they level up, however Fairy Fencer F has a Weapon Point system where the player gains WP after each battle, and that WP is then used to purchase skills, magic, and other upgrades. I highly recommend this game for those who like Eastern RPGs and for those looking for an entry RPG game.

I purchased this game on a whim.. I wasnt expecting it to be anything good.
Ohh how wrong I was.

Short version : If you like JRPGs in general this is a good one. PC port is good, has keyboard cues & mouse support.Has English voice overs

Long Version :
But first I should mention that Im already a fan of Animes & JRPGs & other popular Japanese stuff in general even tho im not one. So I dont really find fantasy stories & themes like this awkward as some others do.
I dont usually play games for long stretches unless its a competitive multiplayer game or a really hard game which im tryin to beat but cant(example:dark souls). But occasionally there will be compelling games which you just cant put down because they give u just the right amount of depth & enjoyment.I guess thats what the RPG genre is known for but many RPGs fail in some way & end up being a boring chore. It can be uinteresting characters to unresponsive world.

Fairy Fencer F is not even an open world RPG. Its a typical JRPG,safe city zone where u manage ur party & dungeons for questing & grinding. But even then It kept me going... I just didn't get bored at all, the grind did not feel like boring grind. I cared for the characters & enjoyed the music & combat animations. Its not one thing which makes it good. Its all the little bits that combine & polish this to near perfection. Add to that english voice overs & a PC port which does not disappoint. It even shows keyboard button cues on screen, yes you can play with keyboard & mouse just fine. And just like most JRPGs it has very in-depth(sometimes confusing)Player development(I mean stats.. like buffing up your character in different ways).Although all confusion can be cleared by reading through the mini-teaching sections which pop up everytime you open a new menu or encounter something new.Im not even mentioning the absoulutely hilarious conversations the lazy protagonist has with his companions, which are a treat to listen to everytime.

At last.. this game is a typical JRPG & a good one at that & the fact that its a JRPG so it may not be for everyone, as the title mentions that its a turned based combat game. But if you're into that sort of thing like me, then look no further.
If you're still in doubt I suggest you buy the game & play it for 1 or 2 hours & then decide whether its your taste or not, you can refund it if you did not spend more than 2 hours & 15 days on it. The first 2 hours of the game will stand a good judge of the game trust me.. unlike some other games which start off good & fade into boredom.

Now if you'll excuse me.. I'll go back to gathering more Furies or food

PS: I've never played Hyperdimension Neptunia so I cannot compare, but others say its similar so if you like that... you may like this.
