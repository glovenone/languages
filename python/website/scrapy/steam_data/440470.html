About This Game

Absence is a horror\survival game that will make you question the people,
and things around you.

You've awakened in a bathroom, only knowing that someone has left to the main office to get you help.
Take a look around you, navigate through the area and meet your friend in the office.
There is a strange sense of something missing around you.

what were you doing there? where is there?
can you remember who you are? who's leaving you notes?
can you get to the main office? or out of the park?
will you find out what is missing? or will you go missing too?

& on top of everything else, what is that noise...?

I got Absence for literally 2$ in a bundle with other 7 games.
I never heard of it before.
Then, I read the reviews. The reviewers of this game seemed to really having great fun mocking it.
As a developer, this really angered me. One reviewer also received a free copy for evaluation and he even made a video for his own profit, literally spitting on it for half a hour. Ha-ha, so funny. Meh.

So I decided to play it.

Absence surely doesn't shine in storywriting, in gameplay or in the tech field but it's not THAT bad.
We're talking about an indie game, which often means the budget is REALLY tight (often 0 or less) so you can't complain the developer for buying third party assets.

It's not a game to pull out a full finished game.

That said, I played the game for a while and it definitely delivers what promised. I jumped on my chair quite a few times, and was very anxious all the time. The environment isn't that bad, it just needed some more detail.

So, yes, I enjoyed this game. A bit buggy, unpolished, with unfair difficulty in some points, but I liked it afterall. I would probably wait for a Steam sale to buy it, I have to be honest.

I like to support indie games so yes, please consider the fact that you may also like it.
It's easy to destroy other people's work, even for AAA titles, and there is nothing to be proud of when you do that for a smaller developer.

Add it to your wishlist and buy it while on sale. My personal score il 5.8/10.

I am a huge fan of horror games and honestly it doesn't take much to scare me at all. But this game only came off as mildly spooky because I couldn't get past the frustration.

It looks like there were a few who were able to complete the game without having the same problems as me which is cool. :)

I didn't have the same experience though so it's gonna be a no from me until some changes are made(if at all).

The Fragnostic

Posted: 13 February
REVIEW COPY PROVIDED BY DEVELOPER

As much as you want to encourage independent development in gaming, there are truly some creations that should never see the light of day. Take Absence. It's far from the worst horror title on here but it shares a lot of their traits: it's dull, sparsely plotted, ugly, buggy and cobbled together with pre-purchased assets to the point where there are copies of 'The Daily News' lying around proclaiming that 'UNITY MAGIC IS HERE TO STAY!!!'. Come on. Really? You want that in your horror game?

The stand-out mechanic here is that sprinting will eventually kill you. A red haze closes in around your field of vision as your heart begins to give out, doing more and more damage as you go. There's three enemies in the game: a bird, some sort of brain damaged starved gorilla that honks like a goose and the invisible water monster from Amnesia and all of them will kill you in a single hit if you've been abusing the run key... which you will have been on account of it taking too bloody long to walk between locations.

It's the random cobbled together nature of Absence, the sheer laziness of its design that kills it. First you're in a public park trying to grab a key from a crazed magpie, then you're running around a sewer flipping off power boxes, now you're in a refinery running around a lava pit...? What is this, Quantum Leap? You wander from a submerged flood tunnel to a warehouse in the space of one door. Good lord. Hope they're storing bath toys in there.

Like I said, I don't want to crap all over what is obviously somebody's very early design effort but just because you make something like this doesn't mean you should sell it. This is the kind of project you make and revel in the experience of simply having made it, take what you've learned and move on; not charge £6.99 for it on Steam.
