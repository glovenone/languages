About This Game

Operation Flashpoint: Dragon Rising is a game about a fictitious conflict on one of the Sakhalin islands, a conflict that is in danger of erupting onto a global scale. The player takes the role of a US soldier, part of the allied force fighting against outnumbering Chinese forces vying for control of the island.
The game centers on a campaign narrative that gives the player a variety of roles in the allied forces ranging from a Marine Infantry, tank commander, Special Forces and helicopter captain. The game has an incredible realistic depiction of war with careful attention to the detail of the weapons, equipment and tactics of the armed forces. The action is varied from intense fire fights through to slower pace tactical decision making on how to best tackle the mission objectives.
  • Most accessible, engaging and visceral sandbox shooter - Includes over 100 authentically modeled vehicles and weapons and players will be able to control multi-component vehicles including tanks, infantry fighting vehicles, APCs, attack and utility helicopters
  • Freedom of Play - Players will need to balance brute force with intelligent use of tactics with unscripted missions containing real objectives such as covering a friendly unit's retreat or conducting short-range recon patrols

First of all, let me be clear--IMO all positive and negative reviews on Steam stand CORRECT. Yes, this game is just as good as people claimed, and as aweful as some have criticized.

Skip to the end for TL;DR

This game is the bad brother of ARMA, but good enough to truly realize what CoD MW1 could have been on a next level of size, depth, and lethality. And I think a few things should be covered so that those who read on will know what to expect. note* I still have not dived myself into mission editor and not enough players for PvP, so no comments on these.

Missions--Campaign is similar to CoD, the missions do cover some of the most basic of types: ambush, night raid, charge and hold town, diversionary strike (support)...etc. Each mission would be a blend of these, so expect some time to be invested, especially if you do take things carefully. These missions are quite standard and relatable to what most contemporary armies would do, and yet both CoD and newer Battlefield doesn't quite capture the size and freedom that OFDR offers. The enemy do go on a patrol path, with another patrol that overlaps to provide constant oversight. It is a very reasonable design that challenges one to think "how can i approach this defense before 12 men start stacking grenades on me?" I truly appreciate this design work, but more on that on the AI behaviour.

Size/Gunplay--Since this game is loosely similar to ARMA, semi-open world travel is a key feature. All missions range from medium to large area of terrain. players must travel most of the time on foot to get to objectives. Luckily, the designers have put enough enemies along reasonable key points, hilltops with machine gun nests, and ammo crates to make things more interesting. Pick you routes, plan ahead, use your binoculars to SEE WHERE THINGS ARE, and proceed to walk your own path. No restirctions other than enemy fire, and you will be ambushed if you make a bad move.

See the issue is that camoflage in this game works. I am both impressed and traumatized at how enemies can sneak right into your face and shoot your at point blank range. And that is because there is no minimap, no Friend/Foe tags (need this more in shooters), and the bushes are such a well blend of colours especially if the graphics are set to low.
While this game is not as hardcore on the simulation side, the sway of the gun when moving, lack of jumping mechanics, and recoil system does give a very hard time to aim, if only I can see. While this may turn some of you away (door on the right, please help yourself), I certainly loved it because i actually felt like firing a gun for the 1st time. Granted, I still have yet tried ARMA 2, but at least OFDR gave me my first time. Taking the stinger from your shoulder also requires loading time, cuz no one ever puts an explosive near their head during bullet fights (do these things blow? if anyone knows plz inform), nd the same goes for putting it back on the shoulder.
And to fellow soldiers: please LOOK, then DO. if you don't know what to DO, stop and don't panic, LOOK MORE.

AI--their designs can go from baffilngly stupid to insane manhunt machines. They will duck if suppressed, alert if bullets fly by, flee if crushed, and will run a circle to catch you in the back 10min later. They will always spot novice players (like me) before you even see their muzzle flash. And yet, AI teammates will also stand in one spot and receive grenades while you, the leader, try to heal yourself. Im not too experienced in AI mechaincs, so I can't give a full analysis, but just bear in mind that they are very good shots over 300m+, your health doesn't count much across the various difficulties, and they will nail you when given the chance. Try to keep moving in unpredictable directions and keep far away from imminent danger; works alright for me.

Multiplayer--please, for the love of God, either hate it and don't play it; love it and Google how to fix it. There are pinned Steam forum posts on how easy it is to register and setup Tunngle. No knowledge on hosting servers, just install, launch and login, find the right OFDR server, and all your friends will be in the same public room. one of you host a LAN game, and everyone just jump in. My experience with coop is delightful--me and my bud had so much fun killing fellow PLA soldiers, getting bullet skinned by PLA soldiers, sneaking around in forests, and hiding behind bushes while the magnificent AI takes care of the rest. Think that says enough?

Guns/Characters/Vehicles--When entering coop and single playergames, you get pre-assigned by the game to a 4-men squad. The only thing you can choose is class: stand. rifleman, Spec Ops rifleman, grenadier, engineer (explosives), AT, sniper, medic, and squad automatic. It covers most classes like Battlefield, but no weapon choices like Battlefield. Quite sad, but I guess Defense budget got cut again. The first player/host will always have to be team leader, thus limiting player weapon choices even further. Luckily enemies do drop standardized weapons from the PLA side, and you can use that method to switch weapons if no ammo crates in the vaccinity.
Vehicle numbers and types are also very much lacking: jeeps, APCs, ENEMY tanks, choppers. This is a major disadvantage for OFDR, and I will not smooth this one because it is just that lackluster. Also, since Codemasters have quite deserted this game, it is ironic that the very 1st original OF (later ARMA CWC) has more available mods than this game. Shame...

Conclusion--I LOVED this game. My AMD HD6450 can actually run this pretty smooth at 900p (windowed), I had hours of blast on coop with my buddy, and completing the missions are actually a worthwhile experience. I am a diehard coop fan, and this FPS has provided me with enough assets to make a game challenging, walk a whole lot, plan ahead, fail my plan miserably, and try new ways at how to deal with crazy AIs.

As for others, please don't buy if the gun sway, heavy recoil is not your type; I understand, after all every boy wants to be laser-aimbot spec Ops. Also, this not not truly open world, lacks modding, very little ingame assets, and bascially no LAN players unless you have friend(s). Since Tunngle is basically hazzle free, please no further b*tching on that, or just be honets about how much you hate CodeMasters, which I won't mind.
