About This Game

Mega Man Legacy Collection is a celebration of the 8-bit history of Capcom’s iconic Blue Bomber. Featuring faithful reproductions of the series’ origins with the original six Mega Man games, the Legacy Collection will remind long-time fans and introduce newcomers to what made Mega Man such a popular and iconic character. Mega Man Legacy Collection will feature all six games and more!

In addition to the six Mega Man games, Mega Man Legacy Collection features new ways to experience the classic games with the Museum Mode and new Challenge Mode. Challenge Mode remixes gameplay segments from all six games, with plenty of scaling difficulty objectives for experienced players to conquer yet serving as a good starting point for new players, too. Museum Mode contains a comprehensive collection of history, high-res art and original concept pieces – a mega treat for any fan of Mega Man and video game history.

FEATURES
  • Six classic games in one – Play a piece of video game history with the original six Mega Man games and experience the origins of the classic series.
  • 8-bit style with HD flair – The games retain their retro 8-bit style with an added HD finish, giving a crisp, clean look to the charming, classic sprites.
  • Challenge remix mode for new and veteran players – The robust new Challenge Mode remixes gameplay from all six games for a new type of challenge. It provides both increasingly difficult trials for experienced players and also teaches newcomers to become familiar with the style of the games and tackle some of the trickier areas.
  • Museum of the era – The Museum Mode features a massive repository of history and art collected from the era of the games in this collection. Take a look back at the history of Mega Man with loads of information, high-res art, concept pieces, sketches and more in the Museum.
  • Developed with an all-new engine – Using the newly developed Eclipse Engine, Capcom and Digital Eclipse are able to archive these six pieces of video game history with faithful reproductions of the originals for a new generation of gamers.
  • Leaderboards and video replays – Fans will be able to watch video replays of the top players on the leaderboards, showcasing a whole new generation of Mega Man talent!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 Home 64-bit
    • Processor: Intel(R) Core(TM)2 CPU 6600 @ 2.40GHz (2 CPUs), ~2.4GHz
    • Memory: 1024 MB RAM
    • Graphics: ATI Radeon HD 4800 Series, Nvidia GeForce 8800GT or greater
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 379 MB available space

My views on the collection are variously mixed. I had the Mega Man Anniversary Collection on the Gamecube which it included MM 1-8 and even two MM fighting games that I never heard of, but were phenomenal. With the previous collection released 11 years ago you'd expect this one to be superior and include even more content in every way possible, it doesn't. Not all is bad, I'll have say the ability to save your progress anywhere now has really helped me considering my experience with the original MM games, for the first time ever with the help of save states I was able to beat the NES Mega-Man. The collection shows it's not only for experienced NES players, but also for "new" players with the help of the new save system.

This collection also includes the newly added challenge feature, which you complete a certain mission on a specific level being timed, also should be noted after you finish you're able to watch your replay. It even let's you listen to the hexalogy's music and look at concept art.

Overall, should you buy this for $15 or play the older collection on the emulator for free? My answer is simple, if you're not crazy about the titles outside the NES ones, then I'd recommend this all the way, due to the challenge mode. If you prefer to have 1-8 just download the older collection's GC ISO and run it on Dolphin.

PS this is a better option than Mighty No. 9. Case closed.

Anyone who grew up in the 90's should know Mega Man; it's a series that needs no introduction, as its lifespan far exceeded that of the NES and SNES. If you didn't have one of the games, it was almost guaranteed one of your friends did, and if one of you actually beat the game you became a playground legend. Yes, for a lot of people the Mega Man franchise holds a heavy quantity of nostalgia... and frustration. The action-platformer series has a notorious reputation for being exceedingly difficult, especially the first two entries.

The Mega Man Legacy Collection contains the first six installments of the franchise, so right off the bat the asking price of $18.99 CAD is negligible since you'll be paying roughly $3.17 per game; you sure can't get any of the cartridges for that price. It also includes various challenge levels that you can earn bronze, silver, and gold medals in. There's also a museum to take a gander at, which houses a lot of art and history of each individual game. All of this is wrapped up in a sleek interface that's easy to use. So right away, you know that you're going to get a lot of bang for your buck. Oh, and did I mention it's fully compatible in 1080p, 16:9? Long gone are the days of squinting at little pixels on a resolution long outdated; since Digital Eclipse used their engine, they've cleaned the games up so that they look extremely crisp on modern monitors.

You may have already read that there are "slow downs" and "low FPS" within the Mega Man Legacy Collection. I am here to tell you that this is both true and false. The game itself runs at a solid 60 FPS all of the time, absolutely no exception. However, the original slow downs are still in the game, so the titles may appear "laggy" when too much is happening on-screen, as they did back in the day. It states clearly on the store page that this collection contains "faithful reproductions of the originals for a new generation of gamers." With that being said, if you're expecting some super-fast Mega Man action, you're looking in the wrong spot; it's highly unlikely that the first six Mega Man titles will ever exist outside of their original state.

If you're not familiar with the series, these next paragraphs are for you. It's always the year 200x, and thanks to Dr. Light a plethora of robots have been engineered to help out humanity with various mundane and hazardous tasks. One day, they get fed up and begin a backlash against its human oppressors, The more advanced robots are the bosses within the game, such as Cut Man, Elec Man, Knight Man, Plant Man, etc. It doesn't take long for Dr. Light to realize that it was actually the evil Dr. Wily who caused the robots to go out of control, and so he turns his assistant robot Rock into an unstoppable fighting machine; and thus Mega Man is born. Of course, the plot changes from game to game, but the general gameplay and theme remains the same.

Each game has its own unique set of bosses, usually eight but sometimes six, which are split up into stages. It's important to know which one to begin with first, as whenever you beat a boss you obtain his power; each robot has a weakness to a specific power, which will make winning the fights much easier if you've gained which power they're weak against. Each stage is a challenging sidescrolling platformer, filled with enemies, hazards, and death traps. One of the biggest annoyances is that, in typical NES fashion, when you get hit by anything you jump backwards; most often to your demise into some pit. While you have a limited amount of lives, Mega Man Legacy Collection has a feature that allows you to save anywhere, so a lot of the frustration has been taken away from the old days.

If you're a fan of Mega Man, this is your chance to own the ultimate collection of the first six games, that look great in high resolution, for a very low price. Not only do you get to enjoy a piece of nostalgic history, but there are a lot of added bonuses that will please any fan of the series. As long as you're not going in expecting anything but the original games, with their original gameplay elements, you're going to undoubtedly enjoy yourself. There are many hours of fun to be had with the Mega Man Legacy Collection, clocking in at 20-25 depending on how good or bad you are at the series. It's unlikely that this title will go on sale any time soon, so just plunk down the funds and dive in.

Rating: 4.0/5.0 - Excellent, highly worth playing.
