19 May

Remaining Groupees Bundle Buyers

To the remaining groupees bundle buyers I'm sorry to say that I won't be sending out any more keys for the steam version of little walker, but please hear me out first on my reasoning.

When I made the agreement with Groupees it was for a version of the game I had put a certain amount of work into (a lot btw). The groupees bundle is long since over and I have already recieved my small percentage of the earnings (like very small, like less than 100 buckaroos). Meanwhile thousands of people got the game for a fraction of a dollar ( which is great! because I had agreed that that version be distributed by them. Also, a portion of that event went to charity, which is even greater ).

SINCE then I have put a lot more work into Little Walker. The steam version didn't happen in a day. Or even a week. Basically this isn't the version I had given Groupees permission to distribute, so I don't feel comfortable updating them with this version.

I'll be finishing sending keys to the people in my gmail inbox, but after that don't fret, Steam is known for having ridiculous sales that may or may not be devaluing games and making it hard for super small indie devs like me to make a reasonable profit from their many months of work >_>

I'm flattered that so many have specifically come forth asking for Little Walker. I thank you for your interest and hope that you find it worthy enough to purchase on the next steam sale or third party bundle.

I think one may be coming up soonish, so I'll be sure to post an announcement for it.

About This Game

A classic platformer from a neighboring universe. Journey your way high and low, far and wide, just for the heck of it. Meet weirdos along the way. Explode things. Explode them good. A game anyone can play with a 1 button mode for people with disabilities.

Features

  • Pretty pixels, or so I've been told. Unless your some kind of pixel-ist. In which case... awkwaard...
  • Whimsical, retro-y music-sounds just for your ear-holes alone. Well, and everyone else's.
  • 30ish level-type-areas. Some are long and some are short, It doesn't have flagpoles to mark the end of each area so I'm just sort of guesstimating here.
  • Snarky writing, cute n' quirky characters.
  • Easy to grasp, challenging to master game-play. ( I know that's cliche, \('-' )/ come at me bro )
  • An instruction booklet with drawings and descriptions just like the good old days.
  • 1 button 'accessible' so that most anyone can play.
  • Adventure.

Controls

  • Arrow Keys to change direction.
  • Z to jump and preform various situation based actions.
  • Customizable input of course.
  • Buttons for mode toggle, fullscreen, volume, etc.

    "...a delightful and smartly put together platform game." - Rock, Paper, Shotgun

    "...an amazing platforming adventure, full of large areas to explore..." - IndieGames.com

Now this was a nice surprise. This game just came out not too long ago and I honestly never would have found it if it wasn't bundled with some other games.

It is a wonderful little game with a lot of character. I don't see a lot of gameplay time in it because it seems like a small game but it has definitely gotten some laughs out of me. I think that's why I really play games, to enjoy myself and maybe get a few laughs. This definitely is delivering that and I can already see it giving me a few hours of fun. I'm not sure if the developers plan on expanding so don't expect a game with tons of hours of gameplay, go in and just see the world for yourself.

Now, for the actual game. The best way to describe this game is a hilarious, stupid Super Mario knockoff, and I mean that in the best way possible. Much of the gameplay resembles the original Mario games, with a few slight twists here and there. The art, the characters, the music, the secrets, the stupid jokes, THAT is why this is actually a good game. It's very ridiculous and maybe I have a stupid sense of humor but I find this game extremely funny.

All that being said, should you buy this game? I say yes, it is definitely worth $5 for a few hours of entertainment. I could see this probably going on sale eventually so if a couple bucks make the difference then wait. If not, buy this game, it might surprise you.
