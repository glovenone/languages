About This Game

The Badlands cluster. 100 years after the first Independence War. Your father was killed by the Corporations for a bad debt. And you've spent 15 years rotting in jail for trying to get justice. Now it's time for vengeance. Now it's time to turn back the Corporations before they corrupt the entire universe. Now it's time to take control of the universe's most advanced interstellar hardware and wage war in the greatest space-sim ever conceived. Command four futuristic battlecruisers and a band of revolutionary pirates into an all-consuming battle against impossible odds...because there just isn't enough Space for everyone!

  • Innovative user-friendly interface - 90% of functions can be programmed where you like - on the joystick or on the keyboard or both!
  • Online multiplayer support for up to 8 people. Play DeathMatch, Capture the Flag, and various other unique scenarios.
  • The revolutionary Flux™ Graphics Engine: Microsoft Direct3D® compatible - surface effects like rust, decay and wear on the ships, and stunning fire and flare effects. All ships in the game are modeled using the textures and details from the ships created for the CGI movies.
  • Physically realistic flight model. All ships use a Newtonian mechanics simulation and move according to the same rules as real spacecraft. This includes a simulation of both linear and angular momentum, inertia and rotational moment of inertia. Ships can use either a free-flight mode or use computer-assisted fly-by-wire flight controls.
  • Team-up on the enemy with detachable fighters. Keep them on-board as murderous gun turrets or order them to attack as wingmen.
  • Superior non-linear gameplay spanning 16 star systems, interacting with dynamically-created space traffic.
  • Command 4 flyable ships and over 30 weapons and ship accessories that can be used in near-infinite combinations.
  • Get to know the locals. Examples include taxicab ships, freighters, space patrols, and pirates.
  • Watch the space opera unfold through a rich storyline in four acts.

Important Note Regarding Multiplayer Gaming: Atari doesn't maintain active servers for this game anymore. However, multiplayer gaming on local networks is still possible.

Important Note Regarding Compatibility: Please note, although successful testing was done on modern machines (Windows 7 and 8) to ensure compatibility, this is an older game and certain configurations may not work. Our testing has found, however, that with most machines if you run this game under Windows XP SP2 compatibility mode it works without issue.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP or Windows Vista
    • Processor: 1.8 Ghz Processor
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: 3D graphics card compatible with DirectX 8
    • DirectX: Version 8.0
    • Storage: 2 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Optical (CD/DVD) drive required in computer for game to start

Played this game about ten years ago as a kid and I'm happy to say it has aged very well!

Sure, apart from the still teriffic pre-rendered cutscenes the graphics are a bit out-dated and most people these days don't own a joystick, but the gameplay, story, voice-acting and music all make up for it. Also you can play it just fine using a keyboard.

Playing it ten years later I can apretiate how ahead of its time it was. The physics, the open world, even the Mass Effect-like dialogue trees. And it's still the only game I've seen where the distances between planets are realistic. It's not just a cutscene and *zap* you're somewhere else, you actually see the planets and asteroids whizz by you as you travel in FTL speeds.

After a half-hour prologue you'll get your own crew of pirates and then it's off stealing cargo, selling it, upgrading your ships and getting revenge. This game is still good today and a I hiiighly recommend it for anyone who likes space-ship games.

Some missions will be vague and confusing. If you find yourself stuck, try this link.

This game is not your average space shooter where you just fly and blow up things in space. It uses Newtonian physics, so you will have to learn how to steer your ship in space and when to accelerate and decelerate. Just like in real space. :-)

This may not be for everyone and I can respect that - but I love the kind of realism this game gives me.

I also recommend playing this game with a controller or even better, a real joystick. That's what it was intended to be played with.

I-War 2 has its flaws. The missions are easy at the beginning, but after a while you will get lost without instructions. Also, the starmap is horrible and your pirate buddy Jafs is constantly nagging. All of these can be solved with mods or a bit of reading, though! :-)

The game may also have some bugs if you play it on later systems than XP, but these can also be solved.

If you want a realistic simulator with awesome music from Christopher Mann and a cool story I highly recommend this game! But read my beginners guide first:

Important: You need to manually set your controls to keyboard only if not using a joystick. You can do this in the options menu.

I am extremely glad to see this game on Steam as well. I have played this many times since its release and each year I hope to play it at least once. I own several copies, on disc as well as digital. It would be great to see an I-War 3 project revitalised, because the series is too good not to have sequels.

While you don't need to play the previous games at all to enjoy the story (mostly unrelated to the prequels), I recommend all the games. They are space simulators packed with action and adventure. The campaigns are all great, diverse, and challenging. The story itself is quite nice: you are trapped in an ever-escalating war between Marauders, Corporations, and independents of the cluster. While employing diplomacy and weapons to get through it and make yourself a living, you get to uncover some other surprising mysteries of the universe. Compared to other space simulators, I feel that the quality of any I-War game in the series is much higher. I was not able to enjoy any other space-related games since playing the series. While I-War 2 may be difficult to master at first, once you do the gameplay is very rewarding to say the least. The game was very well built as well: it natively supported widescreen, at least 5.1 audio, and runs flawlessly on modern hardware. In short, my favourite game of all time.
