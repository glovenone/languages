Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“At gaming events around the UK, Nature's Zombie Apocalypse has been incredibly popular and we are really keen to share a game that we enjoy playing with others. We hope that you'll love playing it with your friends too!
Early access is perfect for our game, because it really is tonnes of fun, with chaotic zombies and awesome animals - all we're working on now is some epic extras!
We plan on releasing regular updates with different game modes, even better weapons and more zombies. This game is ready to be the one you play with all your mates and it will continue to get even better!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Final release is planned for Fall 2015.

The core mechanics of the game are all in order and working. We want to add more features, gameplay modes and levels.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The main gameplay wont change much, but the overall content of the game will improve massively. We are planning on adding; more levels, more game modes, more weapons, and of course more zombies!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is running smoothly and all our feedback has been positive. Choose from over 15 weapons, 6 unique characters, hats, turrets, special weapons and grenades. You can play solo or local co-op with up to 8 friends. Choose from our game modes whether to destroy waves of zombies, guide penguins to safety or save kittens.

We are at the stage where we now need players feedback. For instance; what is your favourite character? What characters would you like to see and what specials could they have? Which weapons work? Is the game too easy? Too hard? What do you want more of?
We welcome all feedback!”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price will increase as we add more content.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We would love the community to help shape our continued development of the game. We have a lot of crazy ideas of our own, but we would love for you to share yours with us. We still plan on adding more weapons, game modes, turrets and characters, so it would be great to know what you want to see in the game and we'll do our best to consider every idea we hear!”

About This Game



Humans have succumbed to a Zombie plague. Desperate to prevent their extinction, animals have been forced to take what weapons remain and fight the undead. Great bears and enraged elephants wield everything from swords to freeze-rays in a chaotic crusade to ensure their survival. Play as an animal as you battle with friends against the apocalypse, whilst taking the time to protect penguins and rescue cute kitties.



In Nature's Zombie Apocalypse the animals are in charge and will stop at nothing to regain the world they once knew. To help them, each animal has a rage move that is uniquely adapted to obliterate the undead. When raging each animal intensifies their attack on the zombies, the duck sucks them into a tornado, the elephant charges through the hordes sending them flying. To help the main players, smaller animals can be strategically put down as defences, these are useful tools so place your suicidal snails and firing flamingos carefully!



Chaotic hoards of undead zombies will run anywhere and jump over anything in order to feast on your flesh. They have many tricks to stop you, from vomiting toxic liquid, to turning their heads into bombs and launching them at you. Get past a few waves of these minions and you will be confronted by far greater enemies, such as angry dinner ladies, crazy clowns and even more fearsome foes.



Simply surviving in this game will take great skill, but there are lots of other challenges to test yourself. Team up with other players and try to rescue kittens from the clutches of the undead, protect a group of penguins from peril or block zombies from crossing the bridge you are defending. Find more exciting activities as you progress through the game!



Features:
  • Online and Local Co-op: Team up with friends and complete challenges, level up and unlock new weapons.
  • Confetti mode: Option to turn all blood off to be more kid friendly.
  • Up to 8 players!
  • Tonnes of weapons.
  • Different game modes.
  • Iconic levels!
  • Support for XB360 / XB1 / PS3 / PS4 controllers and more!
  • Easy to pick up and play, even for complete beginners!

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 February
Everyone's Dead
EXCEPT THE ANIMALS!

I'm going to be grading each section of the game by number of Penguins because the Penguins from Penguin Protect are my favorite part of the game because their pathfinding is hilarious.

Disclaimer: I was given a free copy of this game (through email) to play it on my YouTube channel, which is really nice of Aniode considering I only have around 400 subs. Rob Saxton, one of the developers, responded to my emails and informed me about future updates. Can't wait! I was not told to write a review but I'm doing it anyways.

Disclaimer 2: This review was written during early access.

Story/Premise: 92 out of 100 Penguins.
With so many zombie games out there, it makes you wonder... What about the animals? Do they get infected, or is this virus only for humans? In Nature's Zombie Apacolypse, you play as the animals after the entire human population was infected by a zombie virus. You have to fend off the zombies with guns as lots of different animals.

Menu: 85 out of 100 Penguins.
You know you're in for a ride when you hear that awesome but edgy-sounding music with a ton of infected humans roaming around. You can quicky change the controls, options, or view the credits.

Characters: 77 out of 100 Penguins.
There are currently 9 playable characters. That's how many TF2 has. The characters are almost all the same with the exception of stats. Speed, strength, and agility. The stats are confusing because there is no clear explanation on what they change in the gameplay. It's also kind of unrealilistic. For instance, the turtle is one of the fastest animals. And the gorilla is one of the weakest in terms of strength. He actually flexes when you hover over him. Also, it's not very well balanced. The sloth has really low stats while the turtle has high stats. Otherwise it's completely fine. Each character also has a rage move which takes out the enemies quickly. I like the shrew character.

Controls: 71 out of 100 Penguins.
Keyboard controls are slightly better than garbage. Play with a controller if you have one. For some reason, when using WASD, the W and D keys work but not the A and S. Arrow keys are fine though. It does show you the controls everywhere (loading screen, pause screen, options) which is nice, but it's a lot to take in for beginners.

Graphics/Art: 96 out of 100 Penguins.
The art style is amazing. It's a great mix of futuristic, urban, and a pinch of cartoony. The animation is stunnig to look at, the environment is beautiful (for an apocalypse setting) and small things like splash screens and props make the game more visually appealing than a lot of other unity games. Too bloody? Turn off blood in the settings for a more family friendly visual.

Music: 83 out of 100 Penguins.
The menu music is so good. It fits the theme very well. Each map has a different tune, but none of them are as good as the menu one.

SFX: 70 out of 100 Penguins.
It's just sound. Not too repetitive, but not ear porn.

Unlocking/Progress: 91 out of 100 Penguins.
I'd like to see some steam achievements included to make me feel better about doing stupid things. Otherwise, the reason I find this game so fun is the leveling up system. Every time you level up you get a star. You need to have a certain number of stars to unlock weapons, hats, bombs, and little terrorist snails. You also get coins that can be used to buy those unlocked items. Unlocking maps and gamemodes is a bit different. You need to do something specific in order to get the next map/gamemode. To get Kitten Rescue, you have to save 45 penguins in Penguin Protect. Don't like unlocking things? Want to try out weapons, maps, and gamemodes that you don't have? Try the quick play feature, where you can play the game with everything unlocked.

Weapons: 88 out of 100 Penguins.
I was actually suprised by the amount of weapons in this version of Early Access. There are simple weapons like the pistol and the sword. But there are some pretty crazy weapons, too. From a freeze ray, to a flamethower, to a chainsaw, and even a gun that squirts out deadly bees. If you want a weapon that you don't have unlocked, there are ? crates that contain random weapons.

Hats: 89 out of 100 Penguins.
Hats have always made a game 10 times better for me. Team Fortress 2, Platform Racing 3, Duck Game, and now this. There's only 8 hats so far, but they're not just cosmetic. Each hat gives you a small modifier like more damage, defence, speed, exp, and coins. They also look badass, I mean look at that tiara!

Artificial Intelligence: 93 out of 100 Penguins.
The zombies come of from the ground and are pretty good at killing you. Like I said earlier in this review, the Penguin's pathfinding is my favorite part of the game. They trip over things and waddle around, it's really cute! The bosses are questionable in terms of difficulty. Some bosses I'm able to kill in a matter of seconds with certain weaons. Other bosses completely knock me out to let their minions nibble on my fur.

Price: 100 out of 100 Penguins.
This game is worth it for what it costs. A fair price.

Gameplay Elements: 90 out of 100 Penguins.

Pros
-Ammo is valuable and well balanced.
-Sensitivity is not changeable. (yes this is a good thing)
-Not too big of a difficulty spike.
-You can jump but you don't really ever need to.
-Local leaderboard and MVP for multiplayer.
-Revival system.

Cons
-Can't shoot through dead bodies or props.
-Bosses knock you out for a scary amount of time.
-Cannot be revived if fell in water, making you useless for the rest of the game.

Mutiplayer: Unknown number of Penguins.
I didn't get much time to play local because most of my friends live in a different state and they only visited for a weekend. But I had a lot of fun in that short amount of time. I'll be able to play with them soon because online multiplayer is a planned feature. It sounds great but I have no idea yet, we'll see.

Summary
Overall: 9.75
Art/Sound: 9
Gameplay: 9.25
Fun: 9.5
Price: 10
Future Outlook: 8.75

Posted: 23 July, 2015
Closest thing that comes to mind themewise is Tokyo Jungle. Not sure how someone didn't think of this sooner. Rather then playing humans with ak47s you now have a choice of animals, such as ducks, bears and turtles. While still provided with a standard array of weapons the real fun comes from adding some crazy items into the mix, including pelican turrets and rabbit bombs.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=O2AbMnUs7XI
Gameplay consists of clearing waves while huddled around the ammo pile. Stalks fly through at random intervals dropping boxes containg weapons, items and health packs. Every 5-10waves you are faced with a boss fight which didn't involve much more then outputting more damage, would like to see extra attack moves and such added in future. Currently the game contains 6 characters, 2 maps, 3 game modes and 3 difficulties. Alternate modes included penguin and kitten rescues, where you are tasked with taking groups of animals from A -> B.

Did feel it was a little on the easy side, even on higher difficulty settings but I do play quite a few twinstick shooters. Would love to see online co-op added in future as for the now the game supports local co-op only. Can't say I had a chance to test it but would like to think enemies numbers scaled depending on players connected, supports upto 8!. Both Gamepad and keyboard+mouse were responsive but unable to be remapped. Devs have plans to add a bunch of weapons, enemies, levels and further tweak damage output. Solid base to work from, keen to see where it goes.

If looking for a stylish twinstick shooter that's a little outside the box, Nature's Zombie Apocalypse just might fit the bill. Would make a wonderful couch co-op game but probably a little short lived if intending on just playing solo (with the current content). Cheers folks. :)

The stats are confusing because there is no clear explanation on what they change in the gameplay. It's also kind of unrealilistic. For instance, the turtle is one of the fastest animals. And the gorilla is one of the weakest in terms of strength. He actually flexes when you hover over him. Also, it's not very well balanced. The sloth has really low stats while the turtle has high stats. Otherwise it's completely fine. Each character also has a rage move which takes out the enemies quickly. I like the shrew character.

Controls: 71 out of 100 Penguins.
Keyboard controls are slightly better than garbage. Play with a controller if you have one. For some reason, when using WASD, the W and D keys work but not the A and S. Arrow keys are fine though. It does show you the controls everywhere (loading screen, pause screen, options) which is nice, but it's a lot to take in for beginners.

Graphics/Art: 96 out of 100 Penguins.
The art style is amazing. It's a great mix of futuristic, urban, and a pinch of cartoony. The animation is stunnig to look at, the environment is beautiful (for an apocalypse setting) and small things like splash screens and props make the game more visually appealing than a lot of other unity games. Too bloody? Turn off blood in the settings for a more family friendly visual.

Music: 83 out of 100 Penguins.
The menu music is so good. It fits the theme very well. Each map has a different tune, but none of them are as good as the menu one.

SFX: 70 out of 100 Penguins.
It's just sound. Not too repetitive, but not ear porn.

Unlocking/Progress: 91 out of 100 Penguins.
I'd like to see some steam achievements included to make me feel better about doing stupid things. Otherwise, the reason I find this game so fun is the leveling up system. Every time you level up you get a star. You need to have a certain number of stars to unlock weapons, hats, bombs, and little terrorist snails. You also get coins that can be used to buy those unlocked items. Unlocking maps and gamemodes is a bit different. You need to do something specific in order to get the next map/gamemode. To get Kitten Rescue, you have to save 45 penguins in Penguin Protect. Don't like unlocking things? Want to try out weapons, maps, and gamemodes that you don't have? Try the quick play feature, where you can play the game with everything unlocked.

Weapons: 88 out of 100 Penguins.
I was actually suprised by the amount of weapons in this version of Early Access. There are simple weapons like the pistol and the sword. But there are some pretty crazy weapons, too. From a freeze ray, to a flamethower, to a chainsaw, and even a gun that squirts out deadly bees. If you want a weapon that you don't have unlocked, there are ? crates that contain random weapons.

Hats: 89 out of 100 Penguins.
Hats have always made a game 10 times better for me. Team Fortress 2, Platform Racing 3, Duck Game, and now this. There's only 8 hats so far, but they're not just cosmetic. Each hat gives you a small modifier like more damage, defence, speed, exp, and coins. They also look badass, I mean look at that tiara!

Artificial Intelligence: 93 out of 100 Penguins.
The zombies come of from the ground and are pretty good at killing you. Like I said earlier in this review, the Penguin's pathfinding is my favorite part of the game. They trip over things and waddle around, it's really cute! The bosses are questionable in terms of difficulty. Some bosses I'm able to kill in a matter of seconds with certain weaons. Other bosses completely knock me out to let their minions nibble on my fur.

Price: 100 out of 100 Penguins.
This game is worth it for what it costs. A fair price.

Gameplay Elements: 90 out of 100 Penguins.

Pros
-Ammo is valuable and well balanced.
-Sensitivity is not changeable. (yes this is a good thing)
-Not too big of a difficulty spike.
-You can jump but you don't really ever need to.
-Local leaderboard and MVP for multiplayer.
-Revival system.

Cons
-Can't shoot through dead bodies or props.
-Bosses knock you out for a scary amount of time.
-Cannot be revived if fell in water, making you useless for the rest of the game.

Mutiplayer: Unknown number of Penguins.
I didn't get much time to play local because most of my friends live in a different state and they only visited for a weekend. But I had a lot of fun in that short amount of time. I'll be able to play with them soon because online multiplayer is a planned feature. It sounds great but I have no idea yet, we'll see.

Summary
Overall: 9.75
Art/Sound: 9
Gameplay: 9.25
Fun: 9.5
Price: 10
Future Outlook: 8.75
