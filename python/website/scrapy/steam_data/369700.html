5 May

May Update

Hey everybody! Thanks for all the great feedback this past few weeks. We've fixed a ton of bugs and made some major improvements.

Here's a quick Summary:

The Quest Journal: Before, you had little clues on what your current mission was, it was serviceable, but sometimes a little confusing. Now with The Quest Journal, you can see all your current quests and if you click on each one it'll show you what you need to do and where you have to go, it's super handy! Try if if you haven't already, it's the topmost menu button, the one with the feather icon.

Map Icons: Now the map is much easier to read, you have icons for the totems and for important things you need to find during your adventure, for example if you're into the crab quest and you go into The Quest Journal and select it, the map will show you where the crabs are, the same with most quests where you need to find something! You can summon the map pressing M or by selecting the map button on the menu, The Quest Journal will also show the map with quest related icons.

The Emuri Village: That area was closed to beta users for super long and now you can go there! Some people already visited it and died there a lot, some went and kicked ass! Our advice before you set foot there is: Gear up! Or you're going to have a rude awakening!

New lighting: We're still improving the visuals of the game a bit, and we just made some subtle lighting changes you might have noticed. It's not complete yet but you should see some flickering torches, character shadows and, if you have an artistic eye, much improved lighting all around to give Tanzia a much warmer and nicer look than before.

Bug fixes and tweaks: We're always working on improving the game, there's a ton of under the hood changes/fixes we made based on feedback and all the videos we're watching from Lets Players and Streamers. We fixed quests & combat balance issues and every day we add new stuff to the list!

About This Game


Become the greatest shaman to ever lob a fireball. Retrace the steps of your Akazi ancestors to prevent your island home of Tanzia from being overrun by an ancient evil. Your elders will guide you, but it is up to you to master your skills and live up to your familyâs legacy. An old-style RPG made with modern tech and set in a unique world, Tanzia combines elements of favorite classic action-adventure RPGs in an open 3D world full of magic and monsters.

Fight Fantastic Foes

As you venture deeper and deeper into the world of Tanzia, you'll face increasingly powerful creatures, from a lowly Akiri bird to the Skeleton King himself, with water spirits, giant spiders, zombies, corrupted beings and many more powerful creatures in between!

Discover the World of Tanzia

The island of Tanzia is completely open from the moment your adventure starts, you can get to discover and enjoy it at your own leisure, visit Turtle Beach, the Docks, the dangerous Emuri Village, the magical Lagoon, The Ruins of an ancient civilization and many more beautiful locations. Just make sure you're geared up & ready for the challenges you'll face along the way!

Learn Arcanity

Freeze your foes to make them slower, burn them to a crisp, root them in place! Learn different magic spells to deal with your enemies in creative ways, combine Arcanity with alchemy effects to become a one man army!

Master Alchemy

Heal, run faster, make your Arcanity spells more powerful than you can ever imagine! Mastering alchemy will be crucial in your quest to become the Hero of the Akazi stories.

Features

  • Unique backstory and island setting
  • Main questline and optional quests to unlock more spells, items, and zones
  • Open combat system where exploiting combinations of spells, enemy weaknesses, and environment is encouraged
  • MMORPG-like controls with responsive movement to allow kiting and other emergent techniques
  • Steam stats and achievements
  • Steam Cloud savegames
  • Steam Trading Cards, Badges, Emoticons & Profile Backgrounds

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: Intel Core 2 Duo 2.33 GHz or AMD Athlon X2 2.8 GHz
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Intel HD Graphics 2500, AMD Radeon 5000 series, or GeForce GTX 8 series
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
