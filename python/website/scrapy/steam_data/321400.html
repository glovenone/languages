Supraball
Supraball is a multiplayer game like football/soccer in first person view. Be a keeper, a defender, a playmaker or a striker. Use your imagination to pull off special moves and be the star player you always wanted to be. Invite your friends and develop your own tactics just like a real sport.

Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Supraball is a hobby project that we started in 2012. The gameplay itself is very polished. The player customizations, menus and matchmaking still need a lot of work. Your Early Access purchase will give us the financial means to work on the game in a more professional manner and finish it remarkably faster.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We honestly can't tell how long we will need to finish all our plans. It also depends on the financial success of the EA if we can hire more people or not.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“What is missing:
- automatic matchmaking
- customizations for your character
- better support for Clubs to challenge each other
- easy way to rent and manage servers
- many other things”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“It is very polished and tested for thousands of hours. There are some features missing that we consider necessary for a full release.
It is still unclear wether the servers can hold all players. Our Masterserver should be able to handle 100,000 players. As for match servers we rely on about 2% of the playerbase to host a server. Our experience is that even more than those 2% rent a server.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We want to cut the price in half by june 2016 to cost 10 €/$. If there will be more price changes afterwards is unknown right now.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The community can play the game day in and day out, give us feedback, tell us about bugs and suggest changes.”

29 June

Upcoming features of Supraball

The very next step is that we try to make ranked matches possible for all you new players.

We understand that playing normal matches all day long without a rank at stake is missing something.
We don’t want complete beginners to play ranked, because they would just be shouted at and they’d leave the game. But after 2-3 days it should be appropriate to start playing ranked. And at the same time we want to make sure that you are protected from playing with veterans.
We will make sure they are in separate lobbies.
I can’t give an exact number yet, but ranked played might get unlocked with more or less 2500XP.

The other major changes we have planned next are these:

  1. Customizations: you will be able to unlock skins, hats and ball launchers with your XP and customize the colors of some of them.
  2. Club support: you can already create a club but not much more than this. You will be able properly organize matches with your Club. Playing only with your 4 friends is supposed to be the main way of playing this game
  3. Real matchmaking including queuing with 1 or 2 of your friends
  4. Launcher translations in many languages

You can expect these things to happen until the end of 2016. We usually update the game like once a month or sometimes more often.

Everything about this game is new and exciting, this is newest inventive sport game that has grabbed my attention next to Rocket League. It takes time to learn and master the game, and having little to no player base right now leaves very few chances to actually grasp the concept and understand it, but all it takes is one steam sale to grow the player base and make this game super fun again. Although i have only played two hours on steam, I played it back before it was on greenlight with friends, and it felt so good to finally pay for it on relive it.

The community is simply just old Deathball (original game before supraball) players that have been playing since the games conception and instabought it when it became available on steam. Asking them for help in game is your best shot at learning to be honest, and most of them will be willing to help you. They love this game and want it to be as great and busy as it used to be, so crushing noobs that don't know any better won't help when they are on your team later.

The community is small right now, but will fill up. The developers know this issue and really want it to fill up. They are very transperant in all of their announcements and have all players (especially new ones) in mind. They have recently updated a bunch of tutorials for the true beginners, and then the community is always nice in game and will help you.

This is a great game, well worth the $20 once the community fills up and sales start happening. It pains me to see such bad reviews because few people have bought it yet. It is a good game, its unfortunate the player base is still small but will surely pick up. It is fun when there are people to play with, and I have yet to run into anyone that is rude or mean. Just alot of old wise Supraball players that have been playing before it was even named Supraball.

Basically, Yes the community is small and hard to find matches, but this is the beginning of something bigger. When the community picks up so will this game, but i still highly recommend it.
