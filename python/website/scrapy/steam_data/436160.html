If you are just looking to solve hidden object puzzles for 5 minutes each day, you'd find this game to be a pretty good choice. It is pretty well laid out and challenges could be interesting; most quests are BS though, very weak and fragmented, with bad translation. But this isn't an actual game for PC. It is microtransactions moneysqueezer.

This game is very manipulative in its attempt to squeeze as much real money out of you as possible. At start it gives you a decent amount of store items for free, kind of like 'first time it is free' drug, to get you get used to them. But in-game currency you buy such items with is _extremely_ stingy and prices are high. Copper tokens (in-game free currency) are even more useless, with absurdly high prices, and you can't buy anything decent with it anyway .

The game has idea of 'levels' â not only of the character, to unlock certain rooms, but also levels of mastery through each room. Each such level of mastery will give you less and less time to complete the particular room; some modes (there are multiple mods) are built in a such way that eventually you won't have enough time to go through the room on higher mastery unless you use store items.

Why can you play only for 5 minutes each day? Because the game uses 'health' system which regenerates extremely slowly (about 1 point of 'health' per 3 minutes, and below 'critical level' even slower). Your 'health' grow up with each level but stamina regeneration doesn't speed up, and with cost of 'health' for rooms also going up in the same rate as it grows with level, eventually you'd hit a point when 24 hours is not enough to regenerate all your 'health'.

Even full 'health' gives you just a couple of rooms per try, as 'health cost' climbs up quickly and some mods increase it even more; unless you want to go through first kitchen room again and again, you'd find the rising cost of each new room equal to no feel of progress at all.
The game continuously urges you to buy medkits, the only way to regenerate 'health' fast. You get some freebies, but they are drop in the ocean.

Like a slowly boiling frog, game almost entirely shifts its focus on microtransactions after about first 5 levels. It does it pretty slowly but at level 20 it almost entirely all about the store (and level 20 isn't even half way up). Just some examples:
â more and more quests require items you get from shop. I currently have 18 open and only 2 of them don't rely on something available from store
â more and more quests/collections require items you get from catching animals (with traps you get from shop or, extremely rarely, from rooms)
â amount of traps to catch animals _grows up_ with your level, but drop rate of such traps from game remains very small and random;
â collectable puzzle pieces can be doubles of what you already have, so it might take you extremely long time to get all 24 pieces for each puzzle even if you go just through the same room again and again.
â early rooms are all opened by your progress but eventually you'd hit areas where you'd have either to rely on stingy random drops (of multi level variety, i.e. get stingy random traps to catch animals, get a stingy random items from animals) or get the stuff from the shop.
â there are chests with keys. I have had more than 100 chests (it caps at 100) and I only even got 1 free key. I believe some keys are store-only. Drops from each chest are randomized.
â quests will make you either to find 1 uncertain item/person in uncertain room in certain modes (and even if the room/mode is known, item might not drop until many tries later) or, as you guessed it, get the stuff from the shop.
â into-your-face special events that rely on very random rare drops or on items from shop
â the further you go into 'levels of mastery' in each room, the more pixel-hunt it becomes. While it might look like a challenge (and until certain point it is) eventually items became so small, that combined with decreased time to go through level, it turns into pixel-hunt under pressure but quess what? Everything can be bought in the store.
â free drops of stuff you need to progress or finish quests are very rare, the needed number of such items grows with level but the drop rate is not; again, the game always mentions you can get stuff from store.
â the game relies on double random drops with small chances of useful stuff; say, you need an item you can only get from trapped animal (or a store) but traps drop very rarely and what you get from an animal is very random as well


If not for BS store mechanic and _numerous_ gameplay distortions it forces onto game it would be a solid hidden object game with quite good challenges and maybe a decent, if poorly translated, story. But the greed of developers fragments and mutilates the whole experience.

It's an ftp Hidden Object game with an interesting story.

Story is, you're lured into a house (hahaaa - I'm trying just that here too!) and can't get out. There are other people, one even acts as an npc friend. You need to find stuff to collect points of freedom, and there are additional quests like assembling collections or jigsaws.

There are also special events with extra quests where you have to accumulate something. I think the ability to enter depends on level, so don't expect to access them right away.

You can only play a bit at a time, as in the typical ftp game manner, you need to wait for something to regenerate, that you need to do HO scenes. (The choice here is something that fits well with the story too!)

There's also stuff you can do when Steam friends play it. Like, breaking or improving their decoration (doesn't harm you, gets you both something, and next day interaction may just only offer the opposite; fixing or degrading). You can even send each other free gifts (one per day per friend), as well as other things they may need, from your inventory. Need more of my friends playing this! :)

The english is somewhat amusing in parts (a bit eh, not native), and some objects are a bit very small, but well... they can be found anyway, and there are tools to help. And the story is really rather interesting.

Edit: With the 6/20/16 update, I, and many others, lost all of our progress in the game. If I could play through it straight away, or if I had a way to consistently replenish my health without having to give up gameplay time for it, I would enjoy it more. At present, you can get (maybe) about 5-10 minutes of playtime, depending on how much health you have, and whether or not you have any food/health kits/pills to replenish it with.

I really do like this game, but these flaws are enough to frustrate me into considering not playing it. The only reason I still have it is because it's free to play. (Though, not if you want gold tokens; you either have to level up, which takes a very long time, or pay for those...)

________________


I would rather not have to choose a thumbs-up or thumbs-down... But here we go:

Pros:
- Beautiful graphics
- Good story
- Opportunity for choice (karma, Firewoods)
- Puzzles and collections
- Always have something to do (multiple quests)
- Multiple search modes
- Many, many rooms to explore

Cons:
- Gold tokens run out too quickly; NO way to replenish them without using real money (which I will NEVER do)
- Health takes forever to regenerate
- Modes cost gold tokens to change (consider changing to copper tokens)
- Requires internet to play (this one is neither a pro, nor a con, but presents its own sets of troubles for game availability)
- Grammar can be confusing and detrimental to the aesthetic of the game (for instance, some rooms need to you to find a knight chess piece, but it is written as "pawn," which is incorrect; they need to hire someone whose first language is English to correct these sorts of errors)


The main concern is that gold tokens are too big a part of the game to not have them. Either they must MAKE a way to gain gold tokens for quests and such as well, or they must change the dynamic of purchasing food and room modes to use regular tokens instead. Otherwise, the game requires you to wait too long and you basically end up bored and not playing it (personal experience). You have to waste blood cycling through the room modes for the one you need, and you run out of health before you can complete the quest in the mode they actually need.

So my suggestion is that they fix the money and health mechanics: health needs to regenerate faster, and either you must be able to get gold tokens in-game, or you can use regular tokens for food and room modes.
