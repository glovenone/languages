About This Game

The ultimate Splatter-Strategy-Action-Game.



Fight side by side with your friends against hords of Aliens. Be prepared for epic battles with several hundreds of enemies.

  • 6 different Gamemodes (Standard, Survival, Deathmatch (with and without Aliens), Capture the flag (with and without Aliens))
  • 4 different weapons (Laser, Ion, Grenade, Rockets) with a total of 14 different fire modes
  • Netcode, which allows thousands of Aliens to fight agains together with other players around the world
  • Dedicated Game server to join, or host your own server for your friends

Free2Play, Really?

YES! There is no ingame currency, no paid DLC. Download, Play, Have Fun!

This is a really old game I played in my childhood and I am surprised to see it still being alive, so I gave it a try.

Game description:
This game is a 2D top down shooter reminding of the movie Starship Troopers. Humans versus buglike Aliens. Join up with other players and a lot of AI soldiers to combat the extremely fast breeding and aggressive alien menace.
You will need to lead the AI soldiers and use them to secure spots on the maps with turrets and further soldier spawners. Collect different weapons, earn experience by bug killing and choose from a few skills to get you alive through the map. Exterminate all bug spawners and fight till the last bug is dead.
The graphics are fairly simple, but when you look at other indy games nowadays that go with simple designs this one is not so different looking.
Gameplay is also simple, but can be quite challenging and thus fun.
While the normal gamemode is simply a fight against the bug army, there are other game modes present as well, like survival and a few competitive ones against other players ala deathmatch and capture the flag.

Now to the state of the game itself:
Sadly it still has all the old problems of keeping crashing and freezing waaay to often. :) But its still fun.
The UI could use a face lift, not necessarily in the art department but in usability. Audio settings are wonky, I ended up using OS level audio mixing to turn the volume down, especially because the game launch sound is...loud, I advise to tone the audio down before launching it the first time.

For all the new players a very useful tip, this game has a manual linked to it you can read, something you don't see that much with games having tutorials ingame or being very intuitive and I very much advise to read it, it explains controls and symbols.
http://cdn.akamai.steamstatic.com/steam/apps/348700/manuals/Walkover_Manual.pdf?t=1427137217 here is the direct link to it for all the lazy people.
Also something you should know, if you join a lobby, the game might be already in progress and you cannot join mid play, nor watch. If no one replies to you or only very slowly, that is because they are busy not being eaten by bugs :)
There is no indication of a lobby being ingame already so be patient if no one says anything or try a different server.

This game is pretty old, you will see that not just in its art but in its coding as well, the options menu as I said is totally unusual, the game can be a bit choppy with a lot of aliens, gunshots and whatever going on, even on modern pcs and it has its crashing problems. But I still think it can be an enjoyable game, I surely have a nostalgia bias since this is a game from my childhood but it can be pretty challenging and fun.
Rounds can take quite a bit of time depending on lobby settings, I'd say a good 20 to 30 minutes usually if its a very challenging game and you struggle to overcome the bug invasion. :) But you can have much shorter rounds if the settings are easy enough and the team plays well.


Seeing it still exist after all these years suprised me and gave me a smile.
Give it a try, it's not that big and have a look at a free game from many years ago.
