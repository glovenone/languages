About This Game

Enter the surreal, disturbing, and ridiculous world of the Knobbly Crook in the first chapter of this old-school point and click adventure. O'Sirus the Snip is a simple paper farmer driven to epic adventure by a spooky face with a spookier voice. He will ultimately seek to become a Knobbcrookian royal guardâa Guffaloon. Stories of their heroic battles against the fearsome Bogold empire echo throughout the Crook. Unfortunately, theyâre not currently recruiting simple paper farmers. Heâs going to have to use every dirty trick in his book to cheat his way to the top and that means YOU will need to understand the key elements of the Knobbly Crook - Rock, Paper, and Scissors. Creatures of stone crush the metallic Knobbcrookians. The Knobbcrookians herd and farm paper animals. Fearsome stone giants fear tiny paper animals.

In the first chapter, The Horse You Sailed In On, O'Sirus finds himself on trial for all the crimes you have not yet committed. Retell his nonsensical story as his journey begins, finding himself a stowaway on a ship that is half horse, half boat, and half whale. Every moment that passes takes you further from your goal--the Knobbly Crook. It's up to you to torment the Drafthorse's unsuspecting crew in an attempt to alter their course.

The Knobbly Crook will be released in several chapters, all of them completely free. No ads, no tricks.

This is a "must" for everyone who is into point-and-click quests, especially if you remember Sierra and Telltale. So start downloading while you read this.

Pros:
The art: love it. Everything is completely unique and author has done best to evade any overdone things. The details are clearly worked on, but do not give the "overly pulished round corner" feeling that the latest games of this genre do.

The Reality: nothing like it before. No fantasy, no sci-fi, nothing like that. It is all new and interesting. The best I could describe it is a world where metal and minerals substitute flesh, but can be harmed and is maintained by paper? Does that make sense? The whole game is on a big living, sailing mechanical horse. There is one particular character I am even looking forward to seeing in next chapters, even though he is not a main one.

Language: While all the text on the screen and all the jokes are in English, developer decided on using a complete gibberish language on audio. For you it will sound exactly like that one language you have heard once but can't really recall. That is the one that is used. This gives the game an even more alien feel and completely saves you from any bad voice acting (if there was one). I would suggest playing this game just for a few minutes to hear it, even if you are not interested in anything else.

The price: While I admit that the price is the reason I even played this, I really do have to put this as the lowest priority. I'd buy it.

Cons:

Lenght: It is kind of short. Of course, this is just chapter one, but the shortness is stressed by the story. You get a lot of various items, only to be used as "throwaway", so to say. It introduces about 7 new items, all to be used in a single place. This gives the feeling of a lot of things happening while actually it is already at the end.

Animation: Considering the amount of graphics done and the details on them, it is understandable that animating things is not an easy task. Sure, there are cutscenes, but those are done differently I guess. So whenever there will be a change in the scene, it will be offscreen.

Dialogues: It does bug me a little that dialogues sometimes do not advance. You do some things and those are not noticed by the people around. Even when they should be. It happens once or twice, but some places would be perfect to have another line about it. Maybe this is not a con, just an opportunity missed.


Overall:
8/10. Download it just to see the art and hear the language. Comeon, doesn't cost you anything and would probably have already downloaded while you were reading this.
