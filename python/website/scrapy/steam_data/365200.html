About This Game

LOUD on Planet X is an arcade-style indie music game featuring Tegan and Sara, CHVRCHES, Lights, Metric, METZ, F*cked Up, HEALTH, Purity Ring, Little Dragon, Austra, Shad, Cadence Weapon, July Talk, Monomyth and more!

Choose from a cross-section of top current indie music artists, then defend your stage from hordes of quirky aliens by tapping to the music and using a fun assortment of makeshift weapons like speakers, strobe lights, fog machines, amplifiers, bouncers and custom special attacks!

LOUD on Planet X is an exciting new indie alternative to Rock Band or Guitar Hero with a splash of Plants vs Zombies – a new hybrid of rhythm game and classic shooter with tower defence elements.

Featuring

  • 14 top current indie music artists
  • 28 different music tracks (2 tracks per artist)
  • 30 level single player campaign

It has been a while since I have been so critically negative on a game as I am with LOUD on Planet X. You cannot begin to imagine how disappointed this makes me feel. I am a big fan of music and generally of music games especially when they try and do something unique. This is basically a musical rhythm shooter. In a way it attempts to borrow from great games such as Crypt of the NecroDancer and the more action-y parts of Rock Band and Guitar Hero in order to forge something unique. But sadly it just does not work. At least not on PC and without a touch screen. I wonder if this game would work as intended on mobile devices.

The best critical praise that I can offer for the game is that the track selection is brilliant. I haven’t heard of most of these bands and I am quite thankful for being introduced to them through the game. Call me musically naive but unless you work hard on keeping up with the indie music scene in another country I imagine that the two scenes are very different. I’ve already decided that I am going to purchase some of these tracks and albums on iTunes and in a way given the game has had that effect on me – and I imagine others as well – then in a way the game is a success for those bands.

The keyboard controls are putrid. The key set up is 1, Q, A and Z. You almost have to play with the keyboard turned sideways for that set up to feel comfortable but then you have a keyboard jutted out towards you. Sadly, the Xbox One controller that I use isn’t much better as you spend far too long trying to groove around from lane to lane. This is the single greatest issue with the game and is the primary reason why the game in its current state is unplayable. If you can’t control a rhythm game in an appropriate fashion then you have a dud game.

I’d love to see the devs work on their game and release something more playable. Until then I cannot recommend this game unless you’re in it purely for the music.

TL;DR: Probably fun on a touchscreen device; unplayable on PC (as of the time of this writing).

This game has an AMAZING soundtrack. Lets just say that upfront. I love most of the bands and musicians on here, and was happy to back this on KS. How I was 11 Minutes into this game when I couldn't take it anymore. Eleven minutes into the game. Think about that for a second.

My PC will run anything I throw at it, except LOPX apparently. The game drops out repeatedly, and that leave me unable to keep the rhythm necessary to play this game. Default controls of mouse is pretty mediocre, and after having figured out the controls on my controller, well I still couldn't keep the beat, as the game itself would stutter the onscreen action out of sync with the music, leaving me completely unable to match the beat. I could attempt to follow the beats of the outer border light, but that too didn't matter as it appears I had lag upon reacting every time. As the game constatnly going out of sync kills the ability to actually play it on a PC, this was a real drag for me. I'd be willing to bet this game is great on a touchscreen, but I would avoid it on a PC, or anything else. If the controls and lag get fixed I'd love to come back to this one, as it has a lot of potential, but until then I can't recommend it to those on PC.

Posted: 27 April
Instead of sleeping, I decided to sit down and beat this whole game in one go. Here's my review:

(Disclaimer: I, along with many of these reviewers, it seems, backed it on Kickstarter)

PROS:
-I absolutely love the soundtrack. Purity Ring, Chvrches, Shad, and F*cked Up are some of my favorite artists, so it's wonderful to see them in a video game. I was pleasantly surprised by the bands/songs I had never heard of, like Little Dragon and Health.
-It's a cool concept?

CONS:
-Framerate drops. A lot. All the time, especially when the multiplier hits and the background goes crazy. For a music game, that's fairly unacceptable.
-Really hard to tell where the beat is in some songs to click to. There's a visual indicator on the edges of the screen, but it seems to go out of sync whenever there's a framerate drop.
-Gamepad support, but none of the buttons are labelled, so I can't tell which button does what.
-I got used to controlling it with the mouse, but this is clearly made for a touchscreen.
-There's just a complete lack of polish. I even went into the Partners screen on the Settings menu in-game and the game was flipping out just from scrolling down.

Conclusion: just look at who's in this game and go check them out. The soundtrack is superb, but the game is just badly put together. I appreciate what they're trying to do, but it's just rough all over.

Posted: 24 April
If you're not buying this to play on a touch screen, don't bother.

I wanted to like this game so much, the music is great, the concept is fun, presentation is clean.

BUT

Pet Peeve: 4:4 songs are *also* all divisible time signatures, thus, a hit on a half, quarter, or sixteenth should count. As this is a rhythm shooter primarily, it literally behooves the player to shoot on the 16th, but that's somehow "wrong" even while it's on beat (the majority of the songs in this game are a flat 4:4)

The keyboard keys are 1, q, a, z (try to imagine how your hand sits to hit those on beat,) and the studio has literally done absolutely nothing to make it accessible on a gamepad, which is criminal considering that without a touchscreen, this game is basically unplayable. *Maybe* a gamepad or a rhythm controller like a Rock Band guitar, could be mapped to make it better, but the control mapping functions are completely stock, have labels that are not related to game functions (so you don't know if you are mapping a button to "top lane" or to "Loud mode" or what,) and the stock gamepad mapping is basically unplayable as shipped. A simple gamepad control scheme would make this game great, but it apparently wasn't worth it to the team to map one, or even make it easy for us to do it ourselves.

Good music, neat idea, unplayable game. Seriously, take the couple of hours to map some worthwhile controls to the *primary* control method available on PC's before you just ship ♥♥♥♥ to Steam, where we'll be playing with gamepads or mouse n keys. OR at least relable the remapping menu, so that we can do it ourselves. For ♥♥♥♥s Sake.
