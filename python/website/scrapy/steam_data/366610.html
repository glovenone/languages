Short and sweet Summary:
The opening is badass and I'd kill to see a mini-cartoon series on it. This is ONLY a local co-op game, I think the game devs may have misunderstood the steam classification for Multi-player / co-op (online play).

The game has a lot of potential (excellent concept) and looks good, but falls short in game play; specifically around weapon strength and what's randomly given to you as you pick up donuts. As a single player game, I cannot recommend this. I wish I could, because the concept and a lot of the level design mechanics are excellent, but ultimately the ineffectivness of the majority of weapons vs. the toughness and number scaling of the enemy really kills the fun factor for me as a player.

The Full Review:

The opening intro was amazing, absolutely blew me away! Made me excited to play. Hell, I want to see it come out as a comic or a picture book that I can read to my kid. The art style is cute, but fits the world and the story perfectly and reminds me of the Worms World Party game or even Bubble Bobble...

...but then I got into the actual menu system and game play.

Game Functions / Menu Issues:
This game doesn't appear to have a true online multiplayer mode to it (which is typically what the steam indicator for Multi-player and co-op means, rather than local co-op). You have an option to pick one or two player, and when you select two player it asks for you to connect another controller to the computer.

Hitting escape at any point while the game is open will just shut down the game and put you back at your desktop (actually I kinda like this feature, except for the time I manged to bug myself on a level and couldn't die / move / end the level).

Sound controls are either on or off, there's no decreasing unless you simply lower the volume of your computer audio (just a heads up for youtubers, you'll probably have to edit the game audio to be a LOT softer in post production)

Game Play:
There's only 3 levels, which isn't necessarily a bad thing if there's a high replay value to each match, however even after half an hour of learning how to play, I couldn't get past the first level, or even survive past the first minute and a half.

There was one main reasons for this:
The game is steadily increasing the number of bugs that spawn and it increases quickly. By about 45 seconds in, you have an almost constant line of bugs (and frequently spawning rocket-launching trash cans) that's impossible to dodge, you have to shoot them with the weapons you get from picking up donuts. This wouldn't be a problem except every time you pick up a donut (which is needed to score) you are given a different weapon. Frequently this weapon will be a pistol or shotgun which requires multiple shots at that level to kill a single bug and the continual stream makes it impossible to do so, which means you end up getting murdered pretty quick and have to start the level over again.

The dev's tried to offset this by giving you a store where you can buy one-use items such as pipe bombs or shields and such, which costs donuts. So you use the donuts you collected from grinding through the level to help you try to advance to the next stage. While I've seen that work in other games, this one it doesn't really go over well because the items cost so much (cheapest item is 15-25 donuts) that it feels more like a grind to get the one use items and then if I don't use them effecitvely I'm back to square one.

You can also unlock new weapons through replaying the level and collecting more donuts and killing more bugs. By my 14th run through the first level I managed to unlock the rocket launcher which does massive damage and helps manage those hordes of insects that start spawning like mad, but again, the next time I picked up a donut, I was back to being stuck with a shotgun or pistol that sucked so bad that I just get killed. Two-player is a little more managable, but it still feels more like a grind than that of a really fun game.

I'm disappointed because I really want to like this game. The premise is awesome and I want to find out more about what happened, but I can't bring myself to play the same level over and over again where I don't feel like I'm really making any progress. I'm definitely going to keep an eye on this dev, because I think he has some great ideas and with a little more development/balancing of game mechanics he'll make some amazing things.
