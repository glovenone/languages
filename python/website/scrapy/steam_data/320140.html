About This Game

Drifting: the Art Of Sliding Sideways.




In Absolute Drift, you will master the art of drifting. Practice your skills in Free-Roam and compete in drift events such as Driftkhana and Mountain Drifting.

Key Features

  • Drive and customize up to 6 drift cars
  • 3 Game Modes: Driftkhana, Drifting, & Mountain Drifting with 24+ levels
  • 5 Free-Roaming areas, each with unique levels and themes, from airports and docks to a floating metropolis
  • Online Leaderboards
  • 3+ hours of clean Drum & Bass and Electronic music by C41 and Nyte
  • Full Controller Support

I genuinely wish I could give a neutral review for this one. The game is not bad. In fact, it is very good at what it does.

Absolute Drift is a minimalist drifting game, in which you're a white (can change) car, in a white world, with white objects everywhere. Except for a few things. Orange colored objects are destroyable and/or important. Each "world" also seems to have another flavor color splashed in periodically. (i.e. Yellow in world 2, Blue in world 3, etc.) And each "world" you unlock by completing various "missions" (such as doing donuts, spins, drifting between obstacles, etc.). There are no time limits as far as I can tell, and it's just a very open-world (on a table top), relaxed, easy going drifting game with no rules, no conflict, and no consequences.

All of that, it does EXTREMELY well.

However, it is not FUN. At least not to me. I'm playing on an XBox controller, and I also tried it out on my keyboard. There's five controls. Gas/Accelerate, Brake/Reverse, Steering, and E-Brake. You can also remove automatic transmission and have another shift up/down pair of buttons available. But I found the shifting on this tedious, and without shifting the game is repetitive. You can learn to do all you can do within the first ten seconds of playing. Spinning only works in reverse as far as I can tell, and "donuts" are just, basically, a slow lazy drift. When you are told to donut something, you can get 10%, crash, and get 10% more, crash, and repeat until complete. I've played plenty of other racing games with drifting components, and a few other drifting-centered games. This one, you have significantly less control over your vehicle. And the way the game calculates a "drift" you can literally drive straight up to your obstacle and slam on your brakes and succeed in just about any of the "missions". There is no incentive I can tell to do well (other than placing higher in the global rankings), and no punishments if you just crash into a wall over and over until you succeed.

To feel successful, one must suceed. To succeed, there must be a chance of failure. To avoid failure, one must learn. Learning is caused by either positive or negative reinforcement, or some combination. This game has none. Perhaps I just suck at this game, but I didn't find it fun.

TL;DR: If you want a laid-back drifting game with no clear rules, where you can just drive around a pristine environment to your hearts content, this is for you. Otherwise, look elsewhere.

This game features 3 level types: A-to-B mountain tracks, freestyle drift courses, and 3-lap race tracks. The free roam map has additional missions you can perform only once, such as donut around a light pole, drift between buildings, jump over a river, etc.

I was pretty terrible with the drift controls for most of the game, but finally seemed to start figuring it out near the end. (The controls work just fine; I am just not very good at drifting.) Despite repeatedly crashing into walls, I really enjoyed playing this game. I enjoyed the game even more once I finally figured out how to drive and drift. I particularly loved driving around the map in free roam and discovering new missions to complete. The minimalistic art style and varied environments provides for a beautiful game that keeps you coming back for more.

Pros
+ Beautiful minimalistic art style
+ Many missions to complete
+ Large soundtrack with ability to skip tracks whenever you want
+ Ramps! Launch your car into the air and spin out of control
+ Customizable car body and paint jobs
+ Controller support

Cons
- Not much replayability once you've completed all free-roam missions
- Difficult learning curve for controls
- Only three types of levels gives feeling of repeating the same thing over and over


The game trailer video perfectly shows a taste of what you'll experience playing this game. If the video makes you even remotely interested, then buy it!

This is a really fun game, reminiscent of Drift Legends, a game I used to play on iPhone.
It doesn't hold your hand; it gives you a few pointers, and then lets you get into it.
For most people who play car games on a regular basis, it's easy to get into, but still hard to master. For those who don't play car games, you're probably not going to enjoy it as much - read most of the negative reviews - they complain about not being able to handle. I say, GET BETTER!

Graphically, it's very minimalistic isometric style (even the menu) - much like Kill The Bad Guy - and it works most of the time, except your car can get hidden behind building at times: this is probably the only bit of frustration that could have been avoided had they designed it so buildings get translucent when the car is behind them.

Sounds work - again, very minimalistic, but they work - and the engine sounds are unique as well, not all boring same-as.

Physics are pretty good, and the cars all handle different - some more different than others - you will want to experiment to find which one fits you best (I really liked the Hoonivan).

The only real cons I have are the aforementioned inability to see cars behind buildings, and the listed retail price (I got it for $1, but it's probably worth $5, not the price listed on Steam). I've played about 7hrs, and seen all there is, the only thing to keep me coming back is to increase my scores on levels.

Conclusion: great little drift game, definitely worth playing. It's in a bundle right now, so snap it up.

EDIT: I thought I'd mention it IS playable with keyboard, but A LOT easier to play with a controller, because you really need throttle control (like most car games).
