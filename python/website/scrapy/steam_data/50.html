Posted: 3 July
I played this game a few times before buying it on Steam in a sale. It offers more of the basic HL1 gameplay but with more polish and a few welcome additions. The fights are more intense, and the new aliens keep the gameplay fresh. Human enemies feel the same though that's not necessarily bad thing. The game does a good job of showing how the situation at Black Mesa further deteriorated after HL1, as well as its climax.

Friendly NPCs are more useful since your fellow soldiers carry heavier weaponry than the Black Mesa guards. Their pathfinding still sucks but I suppose that's a problem inherent to the engine.

There's plenty of new weapons as well, though some feel redundant or gimicky/situational (like the barnacle gun). One weapon that was very interesting was the "portal gun." Primary fire teleports the target away to Xen and does massive area damage. The secondary fire teleports you to a random location in Xen, where you can restock from ammo caches there or simply fall to your death.

This game is also much "scarier" than HL1. Don't want to spoil much, but navigating dark caverns full of monsters or having an eight-foot-tall zombie charge you can be startling.

A worthy expansion to HL1 and I would highly recommend it to any fan.

A fine expansion. It builds upon the legendary HL gameplay (Run. Think. Shoot. Live.) and expands it with a myriad of new weapons, including many exotic ones, and many new enemies, mainly the menacing Race X. They will shoot you with lightning, poisonous darts and discharges of energy. Hazard Course was changed for a glorious Boot Camp where many drill sergeants (stereotyped after the film Full Metal Jacket) will degrade you to dirt bags, maggots and other derogatory existences. The game possesses a brilliant minimalistic soundtrack very different from the original Half-Life. AI is still quite buggy, doesn't like to follow you, gets stuck. There is no crowbar, all weapons were changed visually and functionally. There are many original features like the Barnacle Grapple that will help you latch onto organic objects and "suck" your way into them. M-40A1 Sniper Rifle, Shock Roach, Spore Launcher, XV11382, a laser sight Desert Eagle, all new toys. There are a few annoyances like operating the mortar and music stopping after loading. If you liked the original Half-Life you will love this.

The first of 2 expansions to Half-Life 1. I'd say this one is my favorite.
This one is about the length of Episode 2, but adds much more in terms of content value.

Opposing Force adds several new weapons and a few new enemies. The Shockroach, M249, and M40A1 are great additions that really gave [the game] a new sense of life, and the Desert Eagle was a fun replacement to the Colt Python seen in the other Half-Life games.

In terms of enemies, the Shock Trooper, Voltigore, and Pit Drones are very welcome additions to Xen's aliens, forming part of a new species simply named "Race X". They're more powerful than the Vortigaunts and Alien Grunts of the normal game, but are fascinating in their own right. However, Gearbox seems to be the main factor behind their creation, and I hope Valve could have them worked back into the main Half-Life franchise.

Overall: A very fun and very deserving expansion into the Half-Life universe. It desperately needs a sequel, and I hope for the return of it's mechanics.

9/10
