About This Game

This is a story about a man who finds himself in a difficult situation and urgently needs money, so he takes up dirty work being a mercenary. Being an inexperienced mercenary working alone, you'll need to find your own solution out of all the possibilities that the game will present you with.

Features:
- Trading cards
- Soundtrack by Grey Killer
- Lots of easter eggs
- Alternative solutions offering big replay value

If what I played was just a demoversion, then Jackal would be a somewhat alright game. Short and straightforward, but still somewhat alright. However, what I played is supposed to be a fully developed, functional product, and that's the problem.

First off, the game crashes at every single loading screen.
You have to relaunch it for it to load (worry not, though. The developer assures us that "the game should start with several attempts". Yup, that seems to be the official advice).

So when the game eventually does load, you get to play five extremely linear levels, including a very brief tutorial. I mentioned above that the game is short, and I mean really short. It can be finished well under an hour.
The graphic is average at best, the interface is represented by a single huge circle that is either red or white, depending on your casual/suspicious stance, and the options menu consists of "Load" and "Exit".
Music was actually kinda ok, but repetitive (a short loop).

Now I didn't look into it too much so I may be mistaken, but I'm pretty sure that Jackal was originally a game for mobile/tablet devices. If it was, playing it on a mobile phone or a tablet might be actually quite enjoyable.
On a computer, not so much.
