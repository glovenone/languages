About This Game

ZeGame is a beautiful puzzle game with complex but rewarding puzzles in three dimensions. Discover the new surroundings in every world, each having their own unique look and feel.

Slide your blocks in the right directions in order to progress to the next level and discover new block types in every world. Create your own levels and share them with your friends, or play levels made by the community.

Features

  • 8 worlds and 160 levels
  • Stunning backgrounds for each world
  • A level editor
  • Browse levels made by the community
  • Achievements and cards
  • Steam Cloud support
  • Leaderboards
  • 2 game modes
  • Controller support

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 28 May
Product received for free
(Game received for free solely because I was in the translation team -- I was never asked to, or hinted at writing a review for it)

ZeGame is one of the best, easy-to-learn-and-hard-to-master puzzle games I've played in a while. It starts easy, but quickly becomes challenging as you progress through the 160 main levels and unlock new cube types to enhance variability.

Basically, you have to move your cube(s) from point A to point B in a 3D environment. Thing is, the cube moves all the way in a given direction until it's forced to stop. If you send it to nothingness, you might lose the level.

There are many different types of cubes, like solid blocks, doors and switches to open and close doors, teleporters, death cubes, and duplicators. So, as you progress you'll be faced with new challenges.

160 levels is not enough? Don't worry! There's a lot of community-made levels, and they're really nice (and sometimes extremely challenging) to play. You can also create your own levels and share it with everyone else.

On the side, you also get a nice soundtrack to listen to while you're playing. Still want more? Ok, have some cards and achievements? What else can I say? It's really cheap, especially if you consider how much content you get.

Totally recommended!

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 27 May
I love a good puzzle game, and ZeGame is a very good puzzle game. The concept is simple-yet-innovative, the controls are intuitive, and the design is soothing. When I first downloaded ZeGame, I only meant to play long enough to get a feel for the environment. Several hours later, I was fully immersed in solving a particularly frustrating level.

Level progression is well thought out, introducing new concepts in each of the eight worlds, then gradually increasing difficulty as you progress through the world's twenty levels. If you make it to the end of it, you are left feeling both triumphant and shell-shocked, and you probably don't care that you didn't achieve three stars on every level (there's a time component in the scoring!), or that you weren't able to complete all the levels without 'dying'. Or you do care, and you're going to be in for quite a challenge.
