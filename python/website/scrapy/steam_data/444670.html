Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“SUPER IMPOSSIBLE ROAD is primarily a multiplayer racing game with local (split screen) and online play (coming soon). Early Access is especially important for games with a strong multiplayer component, to ensure a robust online experience, but also to balance and tweak gameplay. The only way to achieve this is by getting feedback from lots of players.

Single player aspects will also be tweaked and developed with feedback from the community. In particular, the career mode, which takes the player from beginner through to complete mastery will benefit hugely from player feedback.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We're aiming for a 2016 release for SUPER IMPOSSIBLE ROAD, but it won't be released until we're completely happy with it. The release date will be locked down as we go through Early Access and start to learn more about what needs to be done to complete the game.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“
  • Online multiplayer racing
  • Complete single player career mode
  • Full variety of track designs
  • Full variety of vehicle designs, each with different handling attributes
  • Tweaks and polish across the scope of the game
  • More music tracks
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“
  • Local multiplayer (split screen) races with AI opponents
  • Single player races against AI opponents
  • Survival mode (single player endless mode) with leaderboards
  • Time gate mode (single player endless mode) with leaderboards
  • Partially complete single player career mode
  • Near-complete graphical effects - physical based rendering, HDR, bloom, motion blur
  • Sound design and implementation 50% complete including voice actor for race announcements
”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Those who buy the game during Early Access will be buying at a lower discounted price point than the full release, to give our thanks to those who support the game early on and help us with their feedback and with spreading the word.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The backbone of community involvement will be the game's forum. Every post will be read by the developer and some, where appropriate, commented upon by the developer. Whilst this will be a good way to gauge community response to aspects of the game, we will also run polls whenever that makes sense to get consensus on specific features.

For the multiplayer, we will organise weekly coordinated play sessions, to make sure there are enough people online at the same time to properly test the multiplayer systems. The community will be free to play online whenever they want, but these coordinated sessions will hopefully be useful to make sure there are enough players online at the same time whilst the user base is still growing.

There will be a bi-weekly cycle for version releases. As much as possible, these releases will be done on the same day so that players know when to expect new features. The release will be accompanied by a project update showing what's new in this version, as well as what is coming soon.”

Coming Soon: The Customisation Update

Heads up! v0.4.0 is due to be another transformative update, with another big component for the game dropping neatly into place. This new update will introduce vessel customisation to the game.

Going forwards, you will be able to swap out the three main components of your vessel - the body, the wheel, and the core. You can further customise your vessel by choosing the key colour, as you have been able to up to now. This customisation will be more than visual. Each component that you choose will affect a number of stats affecting your vessel's handling, enabling you to create a custom build for your play style. However, there will be tradeoffs involved. Need more air time? Sure, but you'll probably have to trade off some cornering ability. Need more boost capacity? Okay, but you're probably going to reduce your air time. You get the idea.

So when is this splendid stuff coming to the game? This is a bigger update than usual, so it won't be tomorrow, which would be the usual two weeks since the last update. Instead I'm aiming for mid next week, with a couple more days to polish things up to the level you would expect.

23 June

Hey, why no Steam Sale?

So as you might have gathered if you were waiting for it, SUPER IMPOSSIBLE ROAD isn't in Steam's Summer Picnic Sale. I thought I'd better let you know what my thinking is on that.

First off, I do love a good Steam sale. I've bought plenty of games on them myself and saved lots of money doing so. I bought Deadly Premonition this morning for a couple of dollars, knowing full well I might either hate it or fall completely for its janky B movie sub-Twin Peaks charms. What I'm trying to make clear is, my reason for not putting SIR on sale certainly isn't a hatred of sales.


Fig. 1 - GODDAMMIT

Behind my reasoning is a belief in what Early Access is, or what it can be if it's done right. Developing a game alongside your players is an amazing thing. You can gauge opinion on specific features and change plans accordingly. You can fix bugs you might never have found before the game comes out. You can watch people play your game on YouTube and Twitch and hear their immediate unfiltered response. And you can shape your game into something you know people will love.

But most importantly, you can start to build a community around your game. The people who buy and play your games before it launches are savvy and knowledgeable, and are more likely to share their experiences with others. If we're going to get all businessy about it, this makes them your most valuable customers. Best treat them right.



One of the most important ways I can treat early adopters well is to be fair about pricing. SIR launched at $9.99, with a further 10% launch discount to reward those of you who have been waiting patiently for the game. I think it's only right that this is the lowest price the game can be bought at before the launch. If I put the game on a 33% reduction during the Steam sale, what message does that send to my customers? The message is that they made the wrong decision. Not a message I'm willing to send to my MVPs.

So what happens to SIR's price from here on? Well, there will be no sales during Early Access. When large development pieces like multiplayer and career mode are finished, the price will increase slightly to reflect the game you're getting. Eventually, the game will get a full release, and at this point, the price will increase to the full release price. In this way, the Early Access price *is* the sale, and SIR will have been on sale for months before release, for anyone who is interested enough to buy it. I think this is the best way to do it, and I hope you agree. Feel free to let me know in the comments if you don't.



Anyway, thanks for listening. Early Access doesn't just mean early access for players to the game, but also early access for developers to their audience. I feel privileged to get that access, so thank you all. Happy sale hunting!

About This Game

SUPER IMPOSSIBLE ROAD is a futuristic racing game about taking huge shortcuts.

Get ahead of the pack by taking huge leaps across the procedurally generated speedway. These risky overtaking manoeuvres can take you straight into the lead, or drop you mercilessly into the void.

  • Local races against human and AI opponents using 4 player splitscreen
  • Time Gate game mode with global leaderboards
  • Survival game mode with global leaderboards
  • Procedurally generated tracks
  • Physically modeled material system with real time reflections, object-based motion blur and HDR support
  • Full surround sound system support
  • Techno soundtrack

Coming soon during Early Access:
  • Online multiplayer
  • Single player career
  • Vehicle customization for boosting stats
  • More track types, music, improvements and polish!

Super Impossible Road is an awesome, fast-paced racing game.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4MNssqMYjIM
You control a very cool looking ball-vehicle-thing and race down a randomly generated track. Tracks are incredibly twisty, turny and bumpy. They remind me of audiosurf tracks, or rainbow road from Mario kart. Racing down these is a blast.

Even cooler though, is that you have the ability to roll off of these tracks and skip ahead. Since the tracks are constantly going downwards, you can simply fly off of the edge and land somewhere below. You have a set amount of time to do this, or else you'll respawn back at the last gate you past through. This adds a great risk, great reward mechanic in the game, where skipping ahead could gain you the lead, but not timing the jump properly could cause you to fall behind.

There are multiple gates throughout the levels, that once you go through serve as a checkpoint, and will fill your boost meter. You may use this boost while racing down the road, or falling through the air to make sure you land back on the road in time before you respawn.

There are a decent amount of game modes to keep you interested.

There's the basic racing mode where you can customize the amount of local players, AI difficulty, track length, track complexity and the environment look. There are currently only 2 environments as of the time I'm writing this review. It's not important that there aren't many, as they're completely cosmetic, but I like that the option is there. They give the game a fresh look every new race.

There's also a survival mode where you don't get any respawns, and simply try to go as far as possible without dying.

The time gate mode has a timer constantly ticking down to zero, and you are required to go through a special type of gate in order to gain some time back.

Currently both of those game modes are singleplayer only, so I would really love if more players would be able to play those together in the future.

There is a career mode and online mode currently under construction, but I can't comment on them as they aren't available in the current build I'm playing.

I'm very happy to see that the game gives you a lot of graphical options to tweak, which is very nice. Sadly, on the other hand the game doesn't control as well as it should with PC controls. Playing the game itself is fine with a controller or keyboard, but the menus are SLIGHTLY frustrating to maneuver. There's no mouse support in the main menu, which can be annoying. I'm hoping the simple stuff like that will be fixed, because I don't really have any problems with the game itself.

It's a great idea for a game, well executed, and I honestly can't wait to see the online mode available. The music is some pretty decent techno to get you into the moment, and compliments the look of the world quite well.

Overall, it's a really fun, and unique idea for a racing game!

You race down a winding path with no edges and attempt to get down the farthest or the fastest (depending on mode)... to do that youll be flinging yourself off the edges and aiming for the track below to gain a huge advantage. They balance this by allowing you only 5 seconds off the track, meaning you have to pick your shortcuts carefully.

And time atack mode, which has you going through gates every 10 check points to gain more time to go farther. In my opinion this mode goes against the spirit of the game but it does offer an interesting challenge as the check points get farther and farther away.

All in all its far superior to its iOS counterpart, the controller support alone accomplishes that. A game where you spot your landing like this just doesnt work great when youre tilting your screen around. At 8 bucks I feel like there is enough there right now to justify the purchase, but waiting for updates and a sale may be your best bet if you arent sure from the trailer and this review.
