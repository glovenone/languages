About This Software

The future is now with EVGA PrecisionX 16. This software allows you to fine tune your NVIDIA graphics card, including GPU Clock Offsets, Memory Clock Offsets, Fan Speed, voltage and much more. This latest version of PrecisionX has been revamped with a new GUI, new OSD (On-Screen Display) features, improved voltage and pixel clock control, integrated Steam Achievements and more. True enthusiasts know to go with the number one choice for GeForce overclocking; EVGA PrecisionX 16.

    Key Features
  • GPU, Memory and Voltage Control
  • Power Target Control
  • Pixel Clock Overclocking – OC your refresh rate!
  • Integrated Steam Achievements
  • Frame Rate Target Control
  • Custom Fan Control/Fan Curve
  • System Tray Reporting Support
  • Profiling system allowing up to 10 profiles with hotkey
  • Robust monitoring including ingame OSD with 64 and 32bit support
  • In game screenshot hotkey supports BMP and JPG formats
  • Support for wireless Bluetooth overclocking via custom Android app

Note: This application has many features and achievements that may void your graphics card's manufacturer warranty. Please consult with your video card manufacturer before using this application. If the card is an EVGA graphics card, overclocking is covered under the warranty term.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Microsoft Windows 10 / 8.1 / 8 / 7 / Vista
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce GTX TITAN, 900, 700, 600 series recommended
    • Storage: 50 MB available space

Oh, where to begin with this? I think I'll begin with what I believe the purpose of this software to be. EVGA marketed their proprietary PrecisionX 16 software as a means to overclock the GTX 1000 series of GPUs (something I never looked into until long after I installed it). That said, I have a 980 Ti in my personal system and the problems I've had with this software have been infuriating. Being an EVGA card, one would think that its very own software would be perfectly capable of taking the card further than any other third party OC utility, but to my dismay that was never the case. After starting up PrecisionX for the first time I tried setting the core at 1500 MHz, something that I was perfectly able to do on MSI Afterburner. Not so; In fact, after playing around with the sliders for nearly 30 minutes I gave up. I wasn't even able to overclock the card ONE MEGAHERTZ above the factory clock. So clearly my configuration isn't optimal for this software. That, or there's a bug in the code that makes it incompatible with Maxwell series cards. I certainly wasn't using the software incorrectly or in a manner inconsistent with its advertising.

In summary, my experience with PrecisionX 16 has been infuriating and dismal. The only two positive remarks I can possibly formulate are that, first, you can customize your fan profile to your heart's content. Secondly, since the software never technically shuts off you're able to boost your gameplay time. Yes, Steam regards this as a game. I think that terminology was used more loosely than "halftime performance" at a football game. Thanks to PrecisionX I now have 60 extra hours to my name.

I couldn't take myself seriously by saying that I recommend this software. Maybe I'll change my tune when (if) the GTX 1080 Ti finds itself in my poorly manicured hands. But until then, back to Afterburner I go.
