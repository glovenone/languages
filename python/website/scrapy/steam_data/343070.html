About This Game

The series "World of Subways" features some of the most interesting Subway Routes of the world for the PC.

Once again World of Subways 4 comes back to New York, perhaps the most exciting city in the world. The whole gaming world takes place in the 70ies/80ies and is created in its typical look.

Start the trip from the green Flushing in Queens and go ahead until you reach the Times Square – the centerpiece of New York. The 15km-long Line 7 runs above ground for most of the route on the well-known N.Y. tracks. This time it´s your chance to discover the Big Apple from a track point of view while you are sitting comfortably at home in front of your PC.

Enjoy exciting missions either as the conductor or the driver. Due to the brand new Aggro-Mode (optionally turn on & turn off) an evaluation of your handling is also possible during the drive. Always regard your speed, braking performance and stop position in the stations to keep your Aggro-display in the positive range. When the display is completely filled with red, your drive is cancelled.

WOS4 provides an interactive train depot with offices, a canteen and a garage. Talk to your colleagues at the interactive depot, listen to exciting adventures and try to manage them on your own. Expect tricky but most interesting missions, which will demand your driving skills.

Scheduled service with change of shifts, the original signal system and the interactive depot make the fourth part of the series as realistic as never before! Board the train now!

Features


  • 21 original subway stations
  • Eyeshot up to 4 km
  • playable drivers and conductors
  • interactive depot
  • 80% above ground and over the rooftops of the city
  • new Aggro - Mode for more fun
  • exciting missions based on stories
  • schedule rides with shift change
  • life-like signaling system
  • realistic KL-train traffic and dynamic passengers on platforms
  • interactive 3D- driver cabin with all relevant operations
  • with a screen for routes, stations and controlling
  • interactive 3D-conductor cabin for the optional conductor mode

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows VISTA/ Windows 7/ Windows 8
    • Processor: Intel i5 or comparable with min. 2.6 GHz
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia Geforce GTX 560 or comparable AMD Radeon (no OnBoard Graphics Card supported)
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 10 GB available space
    • Sound Card: Yes

Let's get this straight. This is an excellent game. I can understand why some don't like it but I personally do. I was a little skeptical before buying it, as I was a little worried about poor FPS that others were experiencing. But it turned out that I didn't have to worry at all, I get great FPS! Every aspect of the game is very authentic. It is most certainly one of the best games in the World of Subways series. It reminds me a bit of the first volume: The Path, as they was also set in New York, The outide graphics are excellent in detail. What more could you want from a subway simulator? The cab graphics and textures are also very good and I love the way that the controls in the cab have an 'old' look with the paint peeling off. A nice touch by the developers. Weather effects are also great. At least the game has a tutorial, unlike some of the other previous volumes. I love the way you can also see the Manhattan skyline in the distance, which I find a very nice touch to the game. Graphics on the track, inside the tunnels and stations are also extremely good, with dynamic passengers on the plaforms as well. Having the choice between local and express services is also a really cool feature, and really adds to the game. I don't understand why people don't like this game. What is there to complain about? I like the fact you can walk around the depot and talk to other colleagues and discuss shifts, etc. You can also be the conductor which I find a nice alternative to the normal driving mode. It's a shame there's not that many view mode choices. For example, I would have liked to have seen an interior (passenger view), as that would have been really cool. Another little bug is with the braking lever. The first three or four notches, seem to do hardly anything and the last three screech the train to a halt, which is a bit wierd, but it's not too much of an issue. One of my favourite aspects of this game is the incredible view distance. You can see for about a mile ahead of the train at once, and from station to station through the distant haze. And it doesn't make any impact on FPS! This is probably one of the best games I have ever played in terms of the view distance from where you are sitting. In most games, it significanly impacts FPS. World of Subways 1 and 2 had poor view distance, so bad that you could see the track loading ahead of you.
However, be aware that you will probably require a high end system to run this game, as the graphics are quite demanding on the CPU and graphics card. I had to alter a few settings to to get it to running absolutely perfect, and I'm sure that this will apply to most people as well.
I would say that this game is definitely overpriced in terms of the amount of content you get. For example, the line 7 is not that long, and only takes about 35 minutes to drive an all stopper service from end to end. So my advice is to wait for a sale, like I did.

And on top of it all the graphics are super choppy, and my system is not at all bad... TS2015 runs awesome on it, and even OMSI runs pretty good.

TML had mostly released really crap games in the past (all their laughable bus sims come to mind) but after WoS3 was good I actually started believing maybe this will be good as well. Boy was I wrong. It looks like they've wasted all their time coding a completely unnecessary headquarters with bosses, mechanics and a cafeteria, and making earthquake missions, instead of investing in the actual simulation. Can't believe they charge 35$ for this.

If Steam would allow refunds, I'd get one. And this is definately the LAST time I'm ever tempted to buy something from TML. The level of their credibility for me had dropped to zero. If you like rail simulators, and you value realism, avoid this. If you just want a fun game... there are much cheaper ones that are better.

UPDATE

After what may feel like a steep learning curve, driving the trains is actually fairly easy and there's not as much setting up as there was in WoS3. That said, WoS3 is still largely better than this in virtually every way. The depot with NPC characters providing missions and something of a more social edge to the game as you interact with colleagues and your bosses is a nice idea and makes it different from other simulators, but ultimately feels rather pointless once the missions have been completed. Conductor mode is also a nice feature but as it involves you switching from the driving cab to the conductor cab, performing the conductors duties then switching back to being a driver, again it feels like a wasted opportunity and it was easier in the end to simply leave it to the AI. Some of the sounds and passengers have been recycled from WoS3 (but with groovy 70s clothing!). The opening music and cut-scene is unnecessarily dramatic for what is simply a train driving simulator!

Ultimately, it's difficult really to know whether to give it a thumbs up or down - I enjoyed playing it, I liked the graphics and the feel of driving the 'Redbirds' through New York c. 1979, and I'll certainly go back to it. But in the end, I'll probably end up going back to WoS3 more. Perhaps because I'm a Londoner! But even if I were a New Yorker I'd feel that give the length of development, something so promising yet not quite as polished as this should've been put out there. If you can ignore the flaws, you'll get a lot of great enjoyment out of it if you're a subway fan. But to enjoy it at it's fullest, you really do need to try and overlook the flaws. I'm giving it a thumbs-up, but hoping future patches and updates will smooth out the faults and make it truly as brilliant as WoS3 was, as it's certainly a possibility.

But, if you feel you cannot ignore the flaws described very well by other reviewers too, you're advised to go with WoS3 instead.

Absolutely terrible! Not even closely comparable to WOS Vol. 3. Played for 20 minutes and hated every minute, and I love simulations.

- The graphics are terrible compared to previous volumes.
- The menu is sluggish and struggles to keep up with 4K, let alone any higher resolution (which states is supported in the setting menu).
- The game is very slow, and eats up FPS like nobody’s business (even with a very high-end PC).
- The physics are not even close to that of London Underground Volume, with the cab static (no sway)
- Re-used sounds from Vol. 3 except for a few brake pipe sounds.

But the character animations are terrible, jumping erratically, no speech syncing, and no sense of realism. In addition, the overall feel to the game seems to be that of a FPS, whereby the music is adventure-orientated, and the character interaction/AI is mediocre.

- In-game effects such as rain and snow are appalling.

WOS Vol. 3 has by far been their best simulator so far. WOS Vol. 4 is like a step back in game development. While other game engines and developers exceed the boundaries with their games, WOS seems to ignore all new technologies and game essentials. It is hard to believe that their previous games have been better than this one, and yet with all these negative aspects, they still charge £26.99 ($34.99 in the US).
It is very disappointing that such a long-awaited game on behalf of the community is such a let-down. For anyone who is thinking of buying this game; please don't. Keep your money for a better developed game which is actually worth this extraordinary amount.
I can see the reasons as to why they implemented such things as character interaction and storyline (as many from the community requested some sort of interaction/storyline), but it has greatly affected the quality of not only the graphics and user interface, but also the gameplay, of what this franchise has always been about; driving trains.

Personally I feel this is an improvement over WOS3, I can see where they were going with the missions, but if your a simulation fan like me, then avoid them. The whole depot thing is a nice touch, I like the fact you have to 'sign on' with the supervisor, and can take over trains from the AI, take your train to the sidings at the end of your shift. The fact you can do an immediate return trip after terminating by walking to the other end of the train and setting it up is really great. I can now do a return journey without having to come out and go through menus; like in WOS3 where if you wanted to do another trip straight away you had to go back to the menus and select the retun. Now it is part of your shift plan.

The game is far more indepth than TS 2015, don't come to WOS 4 thinking you can drive a train in TS2015 so you can here, you won't be able to. Good:
The depot interaction to put yourself on a route is great, much more realistic over previous WOS's
Much easier to walk in this than in WOS3
Weather and lighting engine is much better than WOS 3
It really does have the look and feel of the NYC Subway system and accuratly simulates this. You have to operate the doors from the conductors postion because thats how it was done in the 70's where this game is simulating.
Far more feedback at the end over WOS3
