About This Game

Endorlight is rogue-like game in which you have to find a way out to the next level.
Most importantly - you need to survive. There is no "Repeat". When you die, you have to start from the beginning.
Each level contains a new and tougher enemies. Every 5 levels there is a boss to so sed your axe, bow or whip to defeat them!
When you defeat a boss opens the door to new biomes. But remember that every boss is different, have more live and takes more lives per attack!


Collect gold, goes to higher levels and kill many enemies as possible and be the best on Steam Leaderboard

This is a very boring and uninspried "run through the cave, explore and kill stuff" kind of game.

Rogue-like? Whut? What exactly is rogue-like about this game except that death = game over? Isn't that a staple of nearly every game made before 2014?

The assets are uninspired (and apparenttyl stolen borrowed, but I have not been able to verify that myself). There is a fine line between using pixelated art because it looks cool/nostaligic and using pixelated art because you just can't do any better and this game not only crosses that line, it throws up on it and makes a little victory dance. Everything looks so ... boring. You have seen all of this somewhere else, multiple times.

The gameplay is extremely shallow and the controls are your worst enemy. Note that I did not finish the game so maybe there is an interesting world somewhere further down the line, but it was just too dull and mindnumbing to keep me interested any longer.

Seriously, there are hundreds of these type of games and you will find better - and free! - ones on the first random site with flashgames that you open.

Note, even if you - for some sick, sadistic reason that I probably do not want to know about - do want this gamer, I don't recommend spending any money on this. It can be found in cheapo game-bundles nearly 24/7, if it is not given away for free.

I got this game because I received an 80% coupon for Endorlight after completing a badge for Paper's Please. That being said, I want my 70 cents back.

Endorlight hardly qualifies as a game-it's more like an unfinished student project. The logo looks slick, as does the main menu screen, but the pixel art lacks spirit and uniqueness. The gameplay is laughable. The exit for the next level is nearly in the same location every time. The whip weapon doesn't work. There are jewels and chests to oepn, but what purpose do they serve other than scoring points? Am I supposed to score points? Can I use these points to buy upgrades for my crappy, non-functional weapons?

I can see this game being fun with more work- but the developers need to tie up loose mechanics and give the players incentive to progress through levels.

I know it's tiresome to hear complaints about the lack of quality control on Steam, especially since we're clearly at a point where there's no turning back, but I'll say I do miss the days when a game had to be an actual game in order for it to get on here. Endorlight is nothing more than an asset flip, a few cosmetic changes tacked onto a base engine that is itself a ripoff of Spelunky. This is a bald-faced attempt to make a few bucks while putting forth the absolute bare minimal effort. As such, Endorlight lacks any semblance of substance. It's just a bland, repetitive trip from the top of the screen to the bottom, collecting gems that do nothing and killing enemies with three weapons that are effective from varying distances but have no damage scaling to make any one of them more useful than the one with the most range. Occasionally, a bomb will blow out a portion of a wall and you'll find yourself unable to climb out of an area, forcing a restart. Even if the developer's bad practices don't bother you, the fact that it's a bad game should. Don't purchase Endorlight at any price.
