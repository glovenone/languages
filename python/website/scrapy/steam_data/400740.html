About This Game

Our hero has decided to get away from the urban bustle and out into nature to relax. On his way to his cabin, while anticipating the amazing sunsets, fresh air, the tranquility of the mountains, a heavy rain began to fall. His car breaks down in the middle of nowhere, the cold torrential rain dashing any hope of getting the car moving again. He hadn’t seen another person in hours, and as far as he could recall, there was nothing around…but for an abandoned building. He approaches it, seeking shelter, but is it really abandoned?


  • Features:
  • -Multiple endings
  • -High quality graphics
  • -Tense atmosphere of fear
  • -No cheap jump scares
  • -High quality audio support.
  • -Simple but enjoyable gameplay.

2/10

Being a fan of horror games and someone who likes to support small developers - and also keeping in mind this game was made with GameGuru -, I really wanted to come here and recommend this game.

Unfortunately, I can't.

The rain sound effect right at the beginning loops in such a way that you can tell when it loops, as there's a fraction of a second where the sound pauses. The loading screens take WAY too long to load and it completely breaks the game's immersion.

But the game's biggest flaw is that it just... Lacks content. It's incredibly short and it really has nothing to it. No compelling story, no challenge level, no puzzles, nothing. You really just find keys to doors, avoid some few and incredibly easy to avoid hazards and that's it. Also, your actions don't affect the ending, because you literally get to a part where it says "choose your end".

Here's the story on how I saw both endings the game has in less than an hour, and I'll keep it spoiler-free: I started the game, got to a part where you have to choose three paths (one of which leads to a locked door). I picked the left path, got stuck in a place that seemed to be a labyrinth of some sort. Couldn't find anything else or no exit so I started over and picked the path forward. After the door I went through on that path, the game gave me more story, so that seemed to be the "correct path". After that I was in place where I had to get a key, then get past a hazard, then get to a place where I had to get another key. After I opened the door with that key, for some reason I had to constantly jump towards the open door because the game wouldn't let me in. Then I got to the "choose your end" part and once more I had to constantly jump through the open door of the ending I chose. Saw the ending, played the game again, didn't get stuck in the doors this time, chose the other ending, got to another area with a hazard to avoid, saw the other ending and done.

And none of the endings explain anything about the story.

I'm not going to say the developer of this game doesn't deserve your money - everyone has to start somewhere and I believe people can learn from their mistakes. However, other than supporting a small developer, I really cannot see any other reason to buy this game at all. Even for the incredibly low price the game has.

This brand-spanking-new horror game was apparently made on some program called GameGuru ("the easy game maker"). It proudly tells me this on the menu screen. And I have to say, on the strength of this product, should I ever decide to join the glut of people currently making cheap-arsed, ♥♥♥♥♥♥ horror product in their bedrooms...I might make it a point to avoid GameGuru like the plague. I also might not opt to make and release a game, especially for money, until I confirm that I: a. Have some actual talent making games, and b. Have some remotely worthwhile ideas to fill said game with. Luckily for all of us, Mamedov Yuriy has no such hesitations. He/she/they have made a game, and while it starts promisingly enough in the first few minutes - in which you run down a dirt track through some admittedly lovely scenery - it's all pretty much downhill once you actually reach a large, spooky, asylum-like building and step inside.

In VERGE:Lost chapter - that's precisely how it's written, so for reasons of aesthetic purity, I shall keep it that way - you play a small, kangaroo-like creature (a wallaby, perhaps?) who hops along the corridors of said spooky building, goes through lots of doors, and triggers an excruciatingly long load screen approximately once every thirty-seven seconds. That's when you are not enduring excruciatingly long text screens which, in addition to scrolling by at the speed of darkness, CANNOT BE SKIPPED, at least as best as I can tell. Oh, and did I mention that you CANNOT SAVE at any point of the game, be it manually or by way of predetermined checkpoints...in spite of the fact that you'll basically be requiring a "primal scream break" every ten minutes or so, lest you put a FIST THROUGH YOUR COMPUTER SCREEN?!

Now, none of this is to say that there's absolutely NO redeeming features to this game. Some of the sound is actually rather effective - emphasis on the word SOME - and some - again, SOME - of the graphics aren't too bad (well, some of the outdoor graphics anyway; the interiors are pretty much uniformly ♥♥♥♥♥). Other than that, the painfully slow and vertigo-inducing movement basically led me to pack this one in before I'd even so much as laid eyes on a monster, though a quick glance at the trailer on the Store page assures me that I didn't miss out on much.

Y'know, when I was a young'un, I wasted an awful lot of my time and money watching almost every ♥♥♥♥♥♥, low-budget horror movie I could lay my hands on. Nowadays, I'm relieved to report that I only waste similar amounts of time and money playing every ♥♥♥♥♥♥, low-budget horror GAME I can lay my hands on. Hey...waitaminute...th-that's not any kind of PROGRESS, is it? God preserve me...WHAT AM I DOING WITH MY LIFE?!

Verdict: 2.5/10.

Your truck breaks down on a rural road, what seems to be in the middle of no where. Heavy rain starts to pour down and you find yourself seeking shelter. This is how VERGE:Lost chapter, the second title from indie developer M. Yuriy, opens. Let it be said now that if you intend on playing this game, prepare for some incredibly long loading screens that happen quite often. One complete playthrough for me had 8 load screens, which lasted between 30-60 seconds each.

The game starts off with a truly laughable rain effect that does anything but set the mood for terror. It's simply a couple of transparent JPG images looped to create the appearance of rain. The rest of the graphics are decent enough, though if you want to get the better experience remember to press ESC and go into the settings to set them to high; the game automatically runs on medium by default, which is much less impressive. There are only a couple of areas that have any real tension to them, but they last so briefly that it's all over before it actually began.

Whatever scare factor and terror potential that VERGE: Lost chapter houses is completely obliterated by the unreasonable load times. There's nothing quite like sitting around, picking your nails for upwards of a minute every 2-3 minutes. Just as a rough estimate of how often this occurs, I beat the game in 30 minutes; during that time I experienced 8 load screens. That means on average, every 3.75 minutes I came to a standstill at a load screen, and out of the time I spent playing the game, roughly 5-6 minutes was dedicated to load times. That, ladies and gentlemen, is worse than the Playstation 1 era.

Overall, VERGE: Lost chapter isn't anything special. You can choose from two different endings, but neither of them are anything to write home about. The "good" ending even has a massive plot hole in it. It's definitely not worth sitting through the loading screens again to go back for the other ending, either; just YouTube it. Without these awful sequences, the game might've stood a fair chance, but since they won't be getting patched out or fixed up, completely avoid this mess.

Rating: 0.5/5.0 - Abysmal, avoid it like the plague.
