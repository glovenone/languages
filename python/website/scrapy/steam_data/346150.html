Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“I want the players to be involved with the development of the game and I think Early Access is one of the best ways to do that. I know that a good game can be great when a community gets behind it.

Anyone that was involved in the Concept phase or Greenlight phase should already know that I believe that a game should be able to adapt to the player instead of the other way around. Players will have many options to tailor this game to their style and I want the community to help me come up with them.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“I would like to move this project along at a nice pace ,but I do not want to rush anything out. It is hard to estimate how long this will be in early access. Some major factors will be how the community responds to the game and how hard they are willing to push me to make things right. I would like to move to a full release by the end of 2015.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“I really want to get the core system going good before I start putting in the more complex features. Starting out things will be very basic. I will add in features to be tested and then let the community help me decide the ones that make the cut to the finished game. Here is a list of the planned features.

  • Building
  • Crafting
  • Bartering
  • Agriculture
  • Exploration
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Martial Law is currently in an Alpha state. Many of the features are still being added and the ones in the game are actively being tweaked. A list of the current features are below.

  • Match Mode
  • Level Editor
  • SinglePlayer
”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“My goal from concept through Greenlight was to produce a great game at a good price. It is possible that the price will go up marginally when it is finished but I promise to keep any increase minimal.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I will be doing discussion about the game on the Martial Law YouTube channel and in the Martial Law Alpha group about bugs updates and the general direction of the game. I really want people to get involved with the development and especially want to get input on the things everyone wants in the level editor.”

About This Game

What is Martial Law?


Right now, it's the framework of a single and multi-player survival game. I emphasize the word "framework". As of now, it has the basic functions and mechanics developed over months upon months by a one-man development team. It is *not*, I repeat *NOT* a complete game. If what you want is a complete game, DON'T buy it.

What you are doing by purchasing is supporting an indie developer who has built a mechanical framework and core that will be continually expanded upon in constant cooperation with the community. What you are doing by purchasing is guaranteeing that Prophet can continue to work full time (actually, significantly more than "full time") on the development. What you are doing is funding the continued expansion of the game, whether that means actual additional paid staff or new equipment to build and test the game, or just keeping Prophet from having to split his time between multiple jobs.

Who shouldn't purchase



  • Someone who is looking for a finished game

  • Someone who doesn't want to help grow and develop a true indie title

  • Someone who really can't afford the price for something that isn't a finished product

  • Someone with no patience for the unavoidable bugs and delays in WIP software

Who should purchase



  • Someone who wants to play a role in building a real, non-zombie based survival game

  • Someone who can afford the price without raiding the piggy bank

  • Someone who is patient, and understands the pitfalls and tribulations of a one-man dev team

  • Someone who really is passionate about indie games

  • Someone who just wants to try something completely new

I urge you to think twice, and think deeply before you purchase this game. I believe we are being completely up-front with what it is, and what it isn't. If you have *ANY* doubts, we would strongly encourage you not to buy it now, and instead wait until more features you want have been added. However, if you like what you have seen so far, I welcome you to help Prophet out with a purchase, so that the game can proceed at pace to its eventual full implementation. The more purchases are made, the more resources can be put into speeding up the development, hiring staff, paying artists for contributions, et cetera. So whatâs working? As of release, the following things (we will update the description as more features are implemented)

Current Features



  • Match Mode

    Arena based, jet-pack having, super jumping fun, or more realistic CTF, Team Deathmatch, or FFA matches.

  • Level Editor

    The level editor is meant to be a simple, yet powerful editor that will allow the community to build content and tell their own stories in the Martial Law universe.

  • SinglePlayer

    This will be the strength of Martial Law compared to most other survival games. Right now, the framework is a good portion of the way to completion, with (mostly) working inventory, a large open world (not yet really populated), functional AI (limited), and vehicles.

planned Features



  • Building

    Building in Martial Law will be a simple process of collecting the items you need to craft the object you want to build or place the random pieces of debris you have collected from around the world to build your own survival shelter, traps, or works of art.

  • Crafting

    Crafting at first will be a basic recipe system. You can find recipes by exploring the different areas in the world, meeting people that have already learned the skills that are willing to teach you or research the craft-able items that you find, steal, or trade for in the game.

  • Bartering

    Trading is important to secure your survival and so there will be a simple system giving players a secure place to trade, buy, or sell goods with other players. There will be no "Store" to exploit or pay to win, and all items will need to be found or created in-game

  • Agriculture

    Just like in the real world an area can be over hunted very easily and so learning how to grow and raise your own food is important.

  • Exploration

    Sometimes it is nice to be able to give your legs a rest. Players will be able to travel by multiple means on land, air, and sea.


    Thank you for reading this, and thanks for your interest in Martial Law.

First off, let me say that the trailer for this game is unreliable and borerlines false advertisement. PLEASE keep in mind what you see in that trailer is NOWHERE NEAR comparable to your in game experience. In fact, I will go so far as to say that until the game is built in the manner the trailer portrays it...the trailer should be removed.

This game has potential, but I cannot recommend it at it's current asking price. At least not yet. The single player is nothing more than a sandbox mode. I'm not sure if it's automatically online and I can't seem to find a multiplayer option in the menus anywhere. I've played for about 9 minutes, my experience in such a short time wasn't too great and was even enough to decide this game is just not what it portrays itself to be.

I know, 9 minutes is hardly enough time for some users to judge a game with....that is if you're new to gaming. (lol. Please, a gamer who knows his or her salt will know instantly what's junk and what's gold).

Upon starting I liked that I was given a knife, pistol and a car. I could have also kept the house I spawned in if I wanted to but decided to venture out.

I drove up the road in the car and started taking damage. I got out and took cover and suddenly a random NPC appeared for about 2 seconds then glitched and disappeared. I stopped taking damage up until I explored an empty house, something was shooting at me but I couldnt identify it. It was able to shoot through walls and any cover I tried to take. No matter how far I ran whatever it was damaged me until I finally died.

Wilflife NPC's look pretty poor. The wolves walk in a very fixed and stiff pattern like WoW when they should wander naturally. Also, wolves in the wild don't usually keep that close to civilization do they? Like literally in backyards?

The AI is terrible and glitches, you'll get shot at by nothing and seemingly no one at all. Don't bother taking cover, whatever it is can shoot through hills, cars, trees, houses. Pretty much just stand still and die when you begin taking damage. NPC's disappear after you kill them. No blood or bodies left.

Never got to see any building or crafting as of yet because I only got to explore one house before some unidentifiable hostile killed me by shooting me through a giant rock.

There is NO storyline whatsoever. You spawn in a random house, and that's it.

I would have liked to see more of this game, but every time I spawned I would die in this same fashion. This was every time I drove into town. It was annoying enough among everything else to quit. I hope I can get a refund for this until the game improves.

The only thing about this game so far that I can say is positive is the performance. It runs smoothly and the graphics are nice. But the game needs MUCH more than that to be worth 15$
At the moment, I cannot say it's worth it until the devs fix it.
