Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The Withering is currently in the early access phase as we continue to develop additional features and content. We want players to take part in this development process and become involved as the world of "The Withering" is coming to life. We want to hear from players on what features they would like to see in the game. Which aspects they would prefer the development to focus on in the current stage of development. We would also like to give players a chance to influence the game world and have a chance of becoming a part of the story, with original personalized content.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We estimate the game to be in the early access phase until forth quarter of 2016. However, we plan to release a major update in the first half of 2016 that will expand the gameplay features drastically.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The final version of The Withering will be heavily multiplayer orientated. It will include features such as Open World with Free Roam that will let players explore vast different environments of a post apocalyptic hostile world. PvE and PvP game modes will be implemented. Co-op game modes where players will be able to explore special locations in the world. Deep crafting and production system, world economy and diplomacy among different factions in the world. Along with a seamless randomly generated world events that will allow players to shape the future of The Withering universe.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently we have a stable alpha version with turn based management gameplay and real time action gameplay elements. The early access is currently supporting single player mode and we plan to expand it with PvP and Co-op modes in the first half of 2016.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The decision was made to have an early access discount until we will release a major update with multiplayer capabilities. Once the game will be updated with multiplayer mode, the pricing will be revised.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will have a dedicated team of developers that will be interacting with the community of players through forums, emails and many other means available to us. The main purpose of the early access is to have the community involved in the development process and work together with our developers to guide the team's focus towards the features players would like to have implemented as a priority in the game. We want to have the world of "The Withering" to be shaped by the community.”

About This Game

"The Withering" is a modern world post apocalyptic survival horror simulator. The story puts players in a world where a terrible viral infection wiped 90% of the population, which in return triggered a series of catastrophic events laying devastation on already dying world.

The gameplay consists of two elements, a turn based management and a real time action survival simulation. During the management gameplay players take control of a survivors camp and manage it's population by assigning different tasks, building up camp defenses, constructing building, managing upgrades / production and diplomacy. Players will also have to make decisions at random encounters and events, making each gameplay experience different. Defend your camp in a turn based tactical combat simulation from hordes of infected or bandit raids. Craft and equip your survivors with different kits to help them on their missions, or form raiding parties to take resources from other camps by force.

Player are also able to create and manage custom characters, and are able at any time leave their camp to explore the dying world. Taking control of their characters in a third person action / simulator to scavenge resources, materials and equipment that can be brought back to their camp and used in production / upgrades and fortification tasks. Random in world events and loot drops and different enemy types will make each play through different. Develop custom characters stats to increase melee and firearms skills as well as character physical performance by spending experience points earned during the real time action gameplay and running successful missions in the management gameplay.

First of all, after reading the negative reviews, I did NOT have any of those problems, even more strictly, they're probably outdated and need to redo their reviews.

Take This review from someone who actually DID put time and effort into the game, to write an honest, unbiased review.

Basic sum up Pro's and Con's

Pro : It mixed games like Dead State and The Division in the good sense.

- Frequent updates

- Developers listen and love feedback

- Its playable for its state, I can say I'm in the safezone with almost everything build 8/10 progress, and could finish it 10/10. 14 hours of game time is not bad at all.

- Games a lot of fun, scavenging isnt that much of a pain, and the Beta overworld at least is pretty good

- It takes strategy to plan in what recourses (Ammo for instance,) you're going to get, and what upgrades matter the most to you at present.

- Its very versatile, its XCOM camp management does it rather well.

- Play with up to 4 different characters in 1 team (excluding extra survivors you can get for the camp management part) which you can create at the main menu, and immediatly acces and deploy in your game, (You can start with 1 / 2 and gather resources till they knock out before you create more).

-Events at night like the flare (Marks the spot of loot locations, ranging up to 3 colors that all have their own kind of loot, and fight intensity

- 2 Special infected so far, that are not that easy to beat, and hopefully a lot more

- Co-op feature coming up, mixed with more actually deploy missions (Or raids better said)

Now, Cons!

- The combat system is clunky. VERY CLUNKY.
(Explained : Guns arent too bad at all, they're just inaccurate like real guns, and sometimes shooting at the same spot takes a couple spots to hit, for example a headshot when an enemy is hiding behind barrels); against the Melee, which has zombies flying very random ways from where you hit, and you can stun lock yourself in a combo that you can only exit by a well timed kick)
That overal takes some time getting used to it.

-- Sum up for devs, Gun accuracy isnt a problem that needs to be adressed, the rest is. ESPECIALLY THE MELEE WHICH IS STUPENDOUSLY LOW ON DAMAGE, AND WITH ZOMBIE GLITCHES PRETTY MUCH USELESS. UNLESS GUNS ATTRACT ZOMBIES / HORDES, ITS ALL YOU"LL EVER NEED; JUST LIKE SIDE-ARMS BEING UTTERLY USELESS COMPARED TO THE DAMAGE, AND NOT YET IMPLEMENTED REPAIR COSTS.

-Bandits spawn way too much ammo, you can literally farm the hard quest, or city runs for more ammo than the red flare spawns.

- Fuel is a pain to get as a resource, its very impractical and unbalanced compared to the others; as it is the most actively used resource in various ways; so you're pretty much always stuck at 0 unless you do manual deploy city runs.

- Stand on a car, king of the hill. everything is frozen and an easy shot (Didnt play like this personal because of obvious it being lame, didnt try if it worked on bandits; probably not.

- There's way to much unexplained, the tutorial does not do much of a great job explaining much of anything, and the description of some constructions can be a bit vague, and lead to (positive) surprises, so game can be confusing.

- The WHITE loot boxes near where bandits are, are VERY poorly indicated, and VERY EASILY!!!!!<<< DEVELOPERS READ confused for the random junk objects on the floor

-There's no indicator of where dropped loot is at, so its a pain to find.

- Guns are a bit unbalanced, most scoped rifles do far less damage than unscoped ones; makes no sense if you sport a game for realism like this one

All in all, for the 10 XDDD bucks I bought it for, I can say I didnt really regret playing this, and am happy to have finished it. Will wait for future updates to come back and play, but I hope the developers read this and understand their game is going the right direction, and they can expect (Me at least) and perhaps more following in the future if they continue building this game.

P.S To the developers, my friends laughed and made fun of your game for having written om the store page that basically this game is a pioneer at its field; It's really not. Its just a combination of in my best opinion (Scrambled into a nice omelette : DayZ, Xcom, State of Decay and the divison.) Your game does better than the latter 2; but still has a little to go before its a diamond. You do have serious potential of getting there though!
