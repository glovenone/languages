Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Ever since we had a running prototype of YamaYama, we've been getting playtesters to play the game and give us honest opinions and pitch ideas. This has been incredibly valuable to us in the development process, and we want to continue that work. With the Early Access, we hope and will actively encourage players to give us feedback. Tell us what you feel is bad, what is good and tell us what you‘d like to see in the game. We‘re hoping to add a bunch of new game modes, before our final release next fall – and we want you to have a say! Any ideas that end up in the game will be acknowledged and the person will be credited in the credits list of the game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The plan is to release the final version of YamaYama in the fall of 2016. So the Early Access process should last around half a year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“By the end of the early access process, next fall, we hope to offer:
• 2-4 player local AND online multiplayer
• A bunch of game modes
• 10+ unique characters
• The perfect game for the party!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is fully functional and has 20+ game modes to enjoy with friends and family. We however have some art left to do and we want to add more level and character variety. Currently we have:

• 2-4 player local multiplayer
• 21 game modes
• 5 characters
• An excellent new game for the party!”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We haven't decided fully on the final price of YamaYama, but the Early Access version will be priced at the lowest possible price. This is because we value all the feedback we get from the early accessers and want to repay them with the lowest price possible.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will run a forum for everyone who buys a copy of the game. There we will read every single post and take note off your feedback. We will run polls off of the feedback to ask what we should spend our powder on to add/detract/fix in the game next. As well as the forum, we will have a feedback sheet which can be accessed from the game itself, which players can choose to answer or not.”

Update 0.3.1

Update 0.3.1 is now up and running. We updated a bunch of graphics, changed game modes and tidied up the tutorials. All the new stuff:

If you put the afterdeath item on top of the button the bridge will open, as if the button was pushed - more mayhem!

* New graphics and animations for every power-up when picked up, instead of simple icons above the player.

* The game mode previously known as Rainbow Jello is know I Like Big Bears, And I Cannot Lie as players seek to catch the pretty bear:


* Defend the Jello is now known as Houston, We Have a Penguin. You must fight to free the penguin in your color, that is tied to a rocket headed for space:


* All tutorial graphics are up to date with new game modes.

* Minor fixes.

Let us know what you think :)!

3 June

Update 0.3 - New map & more

Update 0.3 is officially live and ready for the weekend! This is a big one, with a new map that drastically changes a few game modes. Here are all the features of the update:

* King of the Mouth is a brand new map that features interactive buttons that collapse bridges or roll out slow zones.

* The new map allows you to play three of the older game modes in it - Last Man Standing (now Last Man Skating), It's A Bad Day (It's A Sad Day) and Yes, it's a Platypus! (Yes, it's a Breidnefur!). The game modes have new names in the King of the Mouth map, as they feel very different in this new environment with the interactive buttons.

* The flamingo in Pretty in Pink has been improved with animations.

* Now the game supports all resolutions, but forces them 16:9 scale.

* Comeback Kid achievement has been removed but 6 new achievements have been added.

* Other minor fixes.

We're really excited for the new map and looking forward to hearing what you think!

About This Game

**DISCLAIMER**

This game is still in development, currently does not have single player nor online multiplayer and we strongly recommend using controllers. This is a 2-4 player local-party game.


YamaYama is a whacky party game where you complete all sorts of mini-games to collect points on your path to victory. Most of the game modes utilise the FatSuit mechanic, where characters blow out and push other players away. It is made with easy-to-learn controls, so you can play with both video game veterans and newbies. The game is functional, but we decided to release on Early Access for many reasons - please read "Why Early Access?" for an in-depth on the decision. Although we haven‘t decided on a final release price, we are certain that it will not be lower than the Early Access price. But what will the build right now bring you?


• 2-4 player local multiplayer
• 21 game modes
• 5 characters
• An excellent new game for the party!

Ever since we had a running prototype of YamaYama, we've been getting playtesters to play the game and give us honest opinions and pitch ideas. This has been incredibly valuable to us in the development process, and we want to continue that work. With the Early Access, we hope and will actively encourage players to give us feedback. Tell us what you feel is bad, what is good and tell us what you‘d like to see in the game. We‘re hoping to add a bunch of new game modes, before our final release next fall – and we want you to have a say! Any ideas that end up in the game will be acknowledged and the person will be credited in the credits list of the game.
To do this, we will run a forum for everyone who buys a copy of the game. There we will read every single post and take note off your feedback. We will run polls off of the feedback to ask what we should spend our powder on to add/detract/fix in the game next. As well as the forum, we will have a feedback sheet which can be accessed from the game itself, which players can choose to answer or not. By the end of the early access process, next fall, we hope to offer:

• 2-4 player local AND online multiplayer
• A bunch of game modes
• 10+ unique characters
• The perfect game for the party!

Apart from this we will work on improving all the art and add maps.

• Online multiplayer
• New characters
• Improving art in environment
• New „micro machines-esque“ gamemode

Early Access Review
Posted: 6 April
I usually play games on my Playstation, but after learning that a local developer was releasing on Steam I thought I'd give it a try. Fortunately for me I could plug the PS4 controllers to play instead of using the keyboard. Last I checked, I had clocked around 7 hours in YamaYama and I can say that I've had incredible amounts of fun and laughed until my abs were sore. The game is basically a collection of minigames that you play through in a random order - or creating your own playlist. I've found that I like some game modes better than others, so I usually play through my own playlists. Anyway, to wrap things up here are the pros and cons:

Pros:
- Hilarious gameplay moments.
- Art is ridicilous and only fuels the hilarity.
- Easy to pick-up and play a few rounds.
- Also fun to play for hours.

Cons:
- Even though the game is probably most fun with your friends next to you, it could use an online option.
- It's hard to stay quiet when playing!

If you're looking for a fun party game to play with your mates, this is probably one of the best values you can get. But as it has no online multiplayer or single player, it's strictly a couch game.
