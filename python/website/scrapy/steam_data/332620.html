About This Game

A robot, a poncho and a journey of discovery! Make your way through this wonderful world of pixelated parallax platforming to solve the ultimate puzzle: who is Poncho?

The world is ruined; humanity has disappeared and all that remains are robots, trying to find a new purpose in existence. But for Poncho, the adventure is just beginning! Explore an open world full of colourful characters, leaping between parallax layers to overcome obstacles and solve puzzles. Can you make it to the Red Tower, meet your Maker and ultimately save humanity?

  • A 2D world with 3D thinking! Use brains as well as platforming skill, moving back and forth through Poncho's world as well as left and right to progress.
  • Discover new challenges around every corner: platforms that move when you do, areas that switch from 3D to 2D and back again, shifting walls, seemingly impossible ascents and much more!
  • A philosophical and intriguing storyline filled with mystery and a dark twist...
  • An open world adventure, with a multitude of solutions to every area, hidden secrets and multiple endings!
  • Randomly generated NPC characters and critters that react to Poncho's presence make the world feel alive!
  • Seek out and find new abilities to unlock even more secrets hidden in the world!
  • Gorgeous pixel graphics mix with clever gameplay and a beautiful soundtrack to create a retro-style experience that's truly unique!

Please bear in mind whilst reading this review that I was given a review key for this game.

After getting an hour of playtime, I feel safe to say that I am able to provide a first impressions. PONCHO is a 2D platformer that focuses around exploration and a gameplay mechanic that allows the player to leap between 'layers', allowing them to jump into the foreground or background.

This game is inspired by FEZ and wears that fact on it's sleeve. The aestetic has been clearly been designed to look similar, but with it's own unique twist from the creator. The main mechanic itself is also very reminicent of FEZ, changing between 2D layers but in a 3D world. The way plot is communicated is slightly similar, in which you can talk to the robots around the levels and they give small snippets of information and character. Another similarity is the use of the 'glitchy' aesthetic and effects at the beginning, I'm not sure how I feel about it, unsure if it's well executed. Lastly, both titles decided all-caps were a good idea.

The game runs smoothly on average pc's however runs much slower on lower end pc's. This is evident of the huge frame drops on the main menu with the less powerful machines, however the drops arent as bad in game till you get to the larger and more detailed levels. The game is capped at 60FPS and there are no options within the main menu or the main game to change this. The game contains seperate music and sfx sliders although only one graphics option to change between "low" and "high". The game size itself is very small and is only around 300mb.

The "Lower end pc" I used for testing had an old 2.4Ghz processor, 4 GB of ram and a Nvidia Geforce 9800. I have also tried the game on a laptop with an 1.7Ghz i3, intel HD graphics and 4GB of ram, it ran at around 20FPS with occasional drops of 3-4 frames. The "average pc" (still quite low specs) has a duo core 3.3Ghz pentium, 8GB of ram and a superclocked Nvidia GTX 960, it ran the game at a solid 60 without any drops.

Gameplay wise, I could see where the game was going. The initial level attempted to teach you all of the basic mechanics within one level, there wasnt really much hand-holding aside from showing control prompts. The first level has been designed in a way that is similar to metroid, allowing the player to go at their own pace and then adding a timer for a small amount of pressure. Although I felt a bit cheated when I reached the end and the timer reduced itself to 10 seconds to make it look like I just got out by the skin of my teeth (but if you didnt notice the jump in time it would have been quite tense). The gameplay gives you a level, and tells you to explore it, you can travel to different levels once you reach the teleporter, but you can go back to previous levels to get secrets that you were unable to get with the powerups you currently had.

Overall, this game takes FEZ and mashes it with a bit of a bit of metroidvania. You can quickly run through the levels however there are plenty of secrets to get by heading back to earlier levels.
