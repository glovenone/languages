About This Game

Ruzh Delta Z is a frenzied shoot-em-up which draws inspiration from classics such as Gradius, DoDonPachi, and R-Type. Shoot your way through perilous stages and wreck thousands of rogue vessels in your quest to become the ultimate pilot.

  • Bullets, bullets, and more bullets
  • Nail-biting classic shmup gameplay
  • Tons of rogue vessels to destroy
  • Immersive chiptune soundtrack

Being this is a bullet-hell game, having fast-paced gameplay is expected. However, the games suffered from wrong pacing. In each stage, the player will go through a few minor enemies, and then fight a boss after only 30 seconds. After that boss is defeated, a few more minor enemies and another boss will appear again after 30 seconds. Having bosses so close to each other each, it lessens the pleasure of defeating each one. If the stages had the bosses spaced a lot further apart, it would have made the action much better.

One thing that could be considered a plus and a minus is the fact that your ship is auto firing the entire time. Logic says that this is good because you do not have to think about pushing any buttons in order to attack. However, when you are trying to avoid the enemy bullets, this will cause both shots to blend together making it harder to avoid. The way that the player controls the firing is by occasionally using one of its special shots. However, there are times when defense is more important then offense. Without being able to stop shooting when you want, being able to avoid damage is more difficult than it has to be.

Speaking of which, getting hit seems to have the wrong feeling to it. If a shot does hit your ship, there will be a slight pause in the action. Otherwise, things keep moving as if nothing happened. In other games like Gradius or Raiden, getting hit will have a clear impact on your ship if it does not destroy it. Seeing no damage does to your ship gives the false sense of security that it is still in good condition. When you consider that there can be dozens of enemies and bullets on screen at one time, knowing how you are doing through your ship is vital. The only time you know that is by turning up the difficulty to hard where only one shot is needed to destroy your ship. Considering the volume of enemies, that could be suicide.

While it is a decent amateur game, it is not one worth recommending. This game is available at http://blackshellgames.itch.io/ruzhdelta for free. If you are curious about the game, get it there. After playing it, if you still it is worth paying for, do it then.
