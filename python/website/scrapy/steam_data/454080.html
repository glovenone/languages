About This Game

Challenge your music! Destroy enemies. Avoid obstacles. Run for your life...and points. Go for a high score and always fight to the beat!

Burst is about free expression and hardcore reflexes.

Only .ogg files are supported.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP+
    • Processor: 2.0Ghz
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 256MB DX9 (shader model 2.0)
    • Storage: 200 MB available space
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: 2.0Ghz Dual Core
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 512MB DX10 (shader model 3.0)
    • Storage: 500 MB available space

( 0.8 hrs on record )
Posted: 21 May
An interesting idea, I'll give it that. It's a unique combat-centric game that works to your own music while providing appropriate bundled tracks. (The metalstep really works well, and I've found electro does the same.) The game overall is a bundle of nice ideas, but the game lacks polish. I understand the minimalist style you're aiming for, but...graphical details? Backgrounds? In-game sound effects? More than 1 kind of enemy behaviour? Particles? Hello?

Again, the gameplay is unique. The methods of fighting back each serve a purpose, but the fact that you have options only comes in handy in the harder difficulties, where the game presesnts a proper challenge in terms of how well you can manage the horde behind you, other than the constant challenge of dodging pitfalls. The fact that you can even accidentally veer off into a pitfall and have to restart is irritating, and the comments putting your low score to shame don't help either.

What I hate is that Burst is an arcade game with a score system, but earning points don't feel like they have any meaning; What's much more pressing are the enemies and pitfalls which have the potential to end the run entirely. If there was a better emphasis on unlocking things (through means other than getting better high scores) for something other than more enemy types, then I would be pleased to use what downtime I have for colouring the board for extra points. This would be a great time to include cosmetics or weapon upgrades or something. Most developers have the process backwards where they have a terrible idea ripped from a successful game and then expand on it too much, but you've got the opposite problem: a game that is unique and has potential but lacks in substance. Burst lacks in anything to give the game energy, and a proper lifespan to exist alongside the other use-your-own-music games.

One last thing is the requirement for .ogg files. I don't mind using them if it didn't mean having to pre-determine what songs you want to play before playing, and having to convert them all. It's a real hassle, and this is definetly what sets Burst back over other games where you can blow through your music library in one sitting if you like.
