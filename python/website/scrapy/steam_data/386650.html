Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Dungeons & Robots early access is intended to provide an opportunity to the community to play the game now, and provide critical thoughts and insights on ways to enhance the user experience. We hope to receive actionable feedback as we continue to actively develop the game. Because multiplayer is such a critical component of the user experience, it will be very helpful to us to monitor a broad user base and identify issues that need immediate attention. Dungeons & Robots is expected to have issues, so if you are not interested in participating in the feedback loop, or you feel you will be annoyed that the experience is not yet perfect, we recommend you purchase the game at a later date.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The game has been in development for almost 2 years. We are a dedicated group and intend to continue devoting our energy to make this an incredible user experience. We hope that we will be able to complete the development to our original vision, include feedback from the community and exit early access within one year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“For the full version we hope to add;

- Two additional dungeon levels,
- Additional characters, weapons, armours and enemies,
- PVP mode, 1 vs.1, 4 vs. 4, and free arena.
- Auction House.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current build is fairly stable with the first dungeon content complete, providing hours of exciting gameplay. We hope to quickly identify any balancing, matchmaking or networking issues and address them.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We intend to raise the price of the game as we add additional content and features.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We intend to be actively engaged on the message boards and forums, "listen' and address critical user feedback as well as explore implementing community suggestions and ideas.”

About This Game

Dungeons & Robots is an Online Action Shooter RPG, where players team up to battle various enemies, collect resources and dominate territories. Features include, fast-paced combat; elaborate crafting and inventory system; customizable characters, weapons, armour, powers and  skills; procedurally generated environments and items; solo missions, 1-4 player co-op, and guilds. Explore these futuristic dungeons to fight, conquer and dominate.

Current Features:
  • 1-4 Multiplayer - Find your posse, explore massive dungeons, complete missions and lay claim to your territory.
  • Procedurally Generated Levels - Force you to stay alert and hope your team has your back.
  • Randomly Generated Equipment - Allows you to select a variety of items that best matches your play style and efficiently upgrades your character.
  • Environment - Navigate monster infested dungeons and caverns to kill the enemy and collect valuable crystals.
  • Customization - Modular approach allows you to customize your Character, Armour and Weapons.
  • Guild - Join a Guild to upgrade your character quicker, conquer and control your territory, and compete agains other Guilds.
  • Crafting - Collect crystals and craft "Legendary" items to upgrade your character and create a powerful robot.
  • Weapons - Protect your comrades and obliterate your enemies with 7 awesome Weapon catagories.
  • Enemies - Battle a variety of aggressive enemies and encounter epic boss battles in fast paced action.
  • Single Player - Explore the environments, collect crystals, engage in fierce battles and upgrade your character.

Features in Development:
  • Levels and Maps- 2 massive additional dungeons.
  • Additional Content - Characters, Weapons, Armours and Enemies.
  • Modes - PVP mode, 1 vs.1, 4 vs. 4, and Free Arena.
  • Trading - Auction House.
  • Select Additional Features - Full Controller Support, enhanced player communication and paring, and optimized networking functionality.

Story - Background:
  • Planet Earth, the once beautiful blue globe and thriving civilization, is now nothing more than a wasteland. Unable to sustain any form of life, the population is destitute of a habitation, and struggling to survive. With no where to run, their only option is a distant space colony, Planet TSI-5, where darkness is tangible and the unknown permeates the air.

    Upon their arrival, the colonists survey this foreign territory and stumble upon mineral in crystal form. After further examination it becomes evident this is no ordinary crystal. Much time is spent studying and experimenting with this mineral, and eventually the composition of the crystals is decoded, revealing the incredible powers imbued within them. The researchers continue to explore the most effective methods of extracting various energies from these crystals and their many applications.

    They name it Pure Crystal, a rather simple name for something retaining powers never witnessed before by the human race; the power of opening portals to parallel universes.

    It becomes obvious that Pure Crystal is the key to survival, and a resource worthy waging war over. The colonist engineer a clan of powerful robots whose task is to hunt the nearby planets for large deposits of Pure Crystal. After an exhaustive search, a planet is discovered with an abundance of Pure Crystal deposits. However, the residents of that planet are well prepared to defend against invaders who want to plunder their valuable mineral. You are one of the robots, and are now tasked to secure and deliver this Pure Crystal back to TSI-5.

    Make a Guild, fight a war, and bring it home. it is the sole purpose of your creation.

Early Access Review
Posted: 3 May
I want to start this off by saying that the game is promising. It's a nice concept and the base for all the really good stuff is already there. When or if this game comes further along, I'll likely change my review. I intentionally played the game for under 2 hour so I would have the ability to refund it later if I did not think it was going to go anywhere.

PROS:
-Aesthetics:
It looks ♥♥♥♥ing great. Honestly, it does. The low poly style goes really nice with robots, although I can't say the same for some of the enemies.

-Customization:
Building your robot with different parts, although currently limited is actually really fun, and there are some pretty cool characters you can make. The game does let you have 5 different equipment set ups, that you're able to swap through in the menu and at a little pad near the start of each level.

-Ability to refund skill points
Skill points can be refunded for no cost any time. This is really great, as you can mess around and see which stats you think work the best for you.

CONS:
That's about it for what's good. For the rest of what I say, I wouldn't take it as why the game is bad, but rather what would make the game better. The only real reason I'm not playing more of this game right now is due to its clear lack of content and extremely repetitive "missions".

-Repetitive "Missions":
I'm going to chalk this one up to the game still being fairly new. Currently there are only two modes, PvP and Explore. I have not been able to play PvP yet, as basically no one plays this game (got to about rank 50 world after 1.5 hours). Explore is really misleading, because it's not exploration at all. You just walk through a very linear cave system filled with enemies, and you're offered two goals. The main goal is always the same, although they give them different names, you're basically just going up to objectives and pressing F. Once you've pressed F on enough different objectives, you head to the boss and leave. You can go to the boss before hand, but you won't get any loot if you haven't completed at least one objective. The sub objectives are really well done, they're basically just a small randomized goal. Collect X amount of X crafting materials, crit X number of times or don't change your loadout during the mission. These accomplish their goal pretty well, and offer an extra chance at loot when completed.

-Artificial difficulty:
The game never actually becomes more challenging. Just harder to do the same exact thing you were doing. More enemies with higher stats. It gets to the point where you'll just be swarmed and killed in the same spot over and over again unless you press the 'escape' button and select the suicide command, which teleports you to some spot back on the map. There's more to this swarming issue, but I'll bring that back up in a bit. I don't mind the enemies getting more stats, it makes sense because I do much more damage when I get better gear. But making boss fights and side rooms with loot just swarm with enemies is really annoying. They will block you in, stunlock you and just kill you over and over again. In harder levels, it's better to just ignore all the enemies and run past everything while completing the objectives. You don't get anything for killing them anyways, except 1 random crafting material sometimes. With the game being called an action rpg you would think fighting would be the main focus. If combat in a combat centered game become something you'd rather just avoid altogether, then there is very little reason to keep playing.

-Basic attacks cost power:
This really got me upset, and I put up with it because I wanted to get a better feel for the game before I wrote a review. There is a power system in the game, which is basically the equivalent of a mana bar in any rpg. Your skills use power, but their cooldowns are so long (and for a good reason, they're mostly pretty strong) that it's basically negligible. Because of this, you're left to just shoot your left and right guns most of the game, which is what is shown in all the trailers so I expected as much. The issue is that your basic attacks cost power. I can't tell if this was supposed to be something you were supposed to "strategically work around" or learn how to manage, but it basically just leaves you kiting enemies for half the time you're in the dungeon since you're literally unable to do anything but walk around. Usually in mmos, when you were out of mana or your skills are on cooldown, you'd use basic attacks for filler. In this game, since your skills are on a super long cooldown, your basic attacks are basically your spells and there's nothing you can even do for filler except run away until it's back up. I suppose this wouldn't be an issue if power regenerated quickly, but even with the regeneration stat almost maxed out and multiple pieces of regeneration gear you run into really long down times on certain weapons. It doesn't add to any sort of difficulty or level of skill, it's just frustrating.
I'm not a game developer myself, so I can't really say what they should do instead, but I have a pretty good idea. I'll include it at the end.

-Skills are bland:
I don't mean your active abilities, these skills are what you use your skill points on when you level up. They are the equivalent of your stats or attributes in other games. It was obvious at first look, there wasn't a ton of thought behind this. Like I said earlier, I'm not a game developer so I can't say what they should do instead. Usually when I play an rpg one of the most fun things is min maxing and creating cool builds. These skills offer very little in that aspect and its pretty obvious what you want to put points into, which leads to everyone ending up with basically the same stats. Tanks will probably differ from DPS, but within each category everyone will basically be the exact same.

BUGS:
The unintentional cons of early access games. I expected a few of these.

Can't jump, but can get on different Z-levels? What?:
So remember when I said that there is more to getting swarmed than just dying a lot? Well yeah, the enemies will just push you onto themselves or terrain and move you vertically up off the map. It's impossible to move some of the time and you can't shoot them at all since you're on a separate Z-level, but since the enemies can hit you and they will kill you in the same spot until your use the suicide option to reset your position. This also assists players in completely ignoring fights all together. If you get trapped only by a few, you can get yourself pushed on top of terrain and then just bypass large groups of enemies to grab objectives and leave the mission with very little risk. This needs to be fixed.

Honestly this is the only bug I really even noticed, but it was a pretty big deal. Otherwise, this game seems to be pretty polished in that aspect.

SUGGESTIONS:
I'll say it again: I'm not a game developer myself, so I can't really say what they should do instead. But I'm going to anyways.

Power? How about overheating:
The power mechanic is frustrating and sucks. It'd be easier to and a lot more fun to just change it to an overheat system. The support abilities' power costs are virtually nonexistent, and it's not like they should have any when they already have really long cool downs. As far as basic attacks consuming power, it just looks as though the developers wanted to make sure people couldn't just spray their guns forever. Which makes sense, that'd make the game kinda easy. An overheat mechanic would take out the massive amount of downtime where players have to kite for 20 - 30 seconds at a time while waiting for a decent amount of power to regenerate. Managing your weapons heat levels is also a lot more fun (in a challenging sense).

Option to disable robot voices:
This is just my opinion, but they're really annoying.

I can't believe I wrote all that.
Good game, has lots of potential.

Early Access Review
Posted: 1 March
This game is a really fun top-down ARPG shooter, it has an excellent concept and I highly recommend this game to those that want to join, play the game, and make it the best.

I would like to note that currently this game is very alpha. There will be bugs and glitches. The game does need some serious quick polish to get it to at least beta status. BUT the core concept of the game is great, it has some really huge potential. So I would only buy this game if you really want to help the game improve, otherwise I would follow and wait for some patches to roll out.

As I said above, the game is a top-down/isometric shooter. What makes this fun is that you don't click to move, you do so by using WASD and you shoot your guns with left click and right click, which makes combat very interactive. You have 4 skills that you can have and equip, an example is one that increases your attack speed and you have 1 armor ability that you get when you wear certain armors.

There are two game modes currently, defense mode where you defend a certain object against waves of enemies. The other one is explorer mode where you get a random objective and have to complete them, an example is to collect X amount, or escorting a person to the exit. For combat it all boils down to pretty much kiting and shooting aliens until they are dead, they have some variety in their attacks, but not that much.

For character customization there are a lot of things you can do. Each character has a skill list where you can put points, into a variety of different stats such as increase damage, increase health, increase armor, etc... Best thing is you can re-roll your points any time you want (when you're not in a game), this allows you to experiement with different builds. You earn these skill points by leveling up your character and to level up you complete mission objectives and gain experience at the end.

Another great thing is you can customize the looks of your robot. You can find different armor and weapon pieces when you play, they are random drops in the level, and they change your looks. Another great thing is that these pieces have random stats on them, so not every piece is the same, this will give you further character customization. Also you can craft gear by finding crystals in the dungeon and have a chance to make a rare.

Lastly with regarding modifications, you can upgrade your armor and weapon pieces, they increase defense and attack respectively. There can be other modifications too, but that I haven't discoverd yet.

Early Access Review
Posted: 3 May
I want to start this off by saying that the game is promising. It's a nice concept and the base for all the really good stuff is already there. When or if this game comes further along, I'll likely change my review. I intentionally played the game for under 2 hour so I would have the ability to refund it later if I did not think it was going to go anywhere.

PROS:
-Aesthetics:
It looks ♥♥♥♥ing great. Honestly, it does. The low poly style goes really nice with robots, although I can't say the same for some of the enemies.

-Customization:
Building your robot with different parts, although currently limited is actually really fun, and there are some pretty cool characters you can make. The game does let you have 5 different equipment set ups, that you're able to swap through in the menu and at a little pad near the start of each level.

-Ability to refund skill points
Skill points can be refunded for no cost any time. This is really great, as you can mess around and see which stats you think work the best for you.

CONS:
That's about it for what's good. For the rest of what I say, I wouldn't take it as why the game is bad, but rather what would make the game better. The only real reason I'm not playing more of this game right now is due to its clear lack of content and extremely repetitive "missions".

-Repetitive "Missions":
I'm going to chalk this one up to the game still being fairly new. Currently there are only two modes, PvP and Explore. I have not been able to play PvP yet, as basically no one plays this game (got to about rank 50 world after 1.5 hours). Explore is really misleading, because it's not exploration at all. You just walk through a very linear cave system filled with enemies, and you're offered two goals. The main goal is always the same, although they give them different names, you're basically just going up to objectives and pressing F. Once you've pressed F on enough different objectives, you head to the boss and leave. You can go to the boss before hand, but you won't get any loot if you haven't completed at least one objective. The sub objectives are really well done, they're basically just a small randomized goal. Collect X amount of X crafting materials, crit X number of times or don't change your loadout during the mission. These accomplish their goal pretty well, and offer an extra chance at loot when completed.

-Artificial difficulty:
The game never actually becomes more challenging. Just harder to do the same exact thing you were doing. More enemies with higher stats. It gets to the point where you'll just be swarmed and killed in the same spot over and over again unless you press the 'escape' button and select the suicide command, which teleports you to some spot back on the map. There's more to this swarming issue, but I'll bring that back up in a bit. I don't mind the enemies getting more stats, it makes sense because I do much more damage when I get better gear. But making boss fights and side rooms with loot just swarm with enemies is really annoying. They will block you in, stunlock you and just kill you over and over again. In harder levels, it's better to just ignore all the enemies and run past everything while completing the objectives. You don't get anything for killing them anyways, except 1 random crafting material sometimes. With the game being called an action rpg you would think fighting would be the main focus. If combat in a combat centered game become something you'd rather just avoid altogether, then there is very little reason to keep playing.

-Basic attacks cost power:
This really got me upset, and I put up with it because I wanted to get a better feel for the game before I wrote a review. There is a power system in the game, which is basically the equivalent of a mana bar in any rpg. Your skills use power, but their cooldowns are so long (and for a good reason, they're mostly pretty strong) that it's basically negligible. Because of this, you're left to just shoot your left and right guns most of the game, which is what is shown in all the trailers so I expected as much. The issue is that your basic attacks cost power. I can't tell if this was supposed to be something you were supposed to "strategically work around" or learn how to manage, but it basically just leaves you kiting enemies for half the time you're in the dungeon since you're literally unable to do anything but walk around. Usually in mmos, when you were out of mana or your skills are on cooldown, you'd use basic attacks for filler. In this game, since your skills are on a super long cooldown, your basic attacks are basically your spells and there's nothing you can even do for filler except run away until it's back up. I suppose this wouldn't be an issue if power regenerated quickly, but even with the regeneration stat almost maxed out and multiple pieces of regeneration gear you run into really long down times on certain weapons. It doesn't add to any sort of difficulty or level of skill, it's just frustrating.
I'm not a game developer myself, so I can't really say what they should do instead, but I have a pretty good idea. I'll include it at the end.

-Skills are bland:
I don't mean your active abilities, these skills are what you use your skill points on when you level up. They are the equivalent of your stats or attributes in other games. It was obvious at first look, there wasn't a ton of thought behind this. Like I said earlier, I'm not a game developer so I can't say what they should do instead. Usually when I play an rpg one of the most fun things is min maxing and creating cool builds. These skills offer very little in that aspect and its pretty obvious what you want to put points into, which leads to everyone ending up with basically the same stats. Tanks will probably differ from DPS, but within each category everyone will basically be the exact same.

BUGS:
The unintentional cons of early access games. I expected a few of these.

Can't jump, but can get on different Z-levels? What?:
So remember when I said that there is more to getting swarmed than just dying a lot? Well yeah, the enemies will just push you onto themselves or terrain and move you vertically up off the map. It's impossible to move some of the time and you can't shoot them at all since you're on a separate Z-level, but since the enemies can hit you and they will kill you in the same spot until your use the suicide option to reset your position. This also assists players in completely ignoring fights all together. If you get trapped only by a few, you can get yourself pushed on top of terrain and then just bypass large groups of enemies to grab objectives and leave the mission with very little risk. This needs to be fixed.

Honestly this is the only bug I really even noticed, but it was a pretty big deal. Otherwise, this game seems to be pretty polished in that aspect.

SUGGESTIONS:
I'll say it again: I'm not a game developer myself, so I can't really say what they should do instead. But I'm going to anyways.

Power? How about overheating:
The power mechanic is frustrating and sucks. It'd be easier to and a lot more fun to just change it to an overheat system. The support abilities' power costs are virtually nonexistent, and it's not like they should have any when they already have really long cool downs. As far as basic attacks consuming power, it just looks as though the developers wanted to make sure people couldn't just spray their guns forever. Which makes sense, that'd make the game kinda easy. An overheat mechanic would take out the massive amount of downtime where players have to kite for 20 - 30 seconds at a time while waiting for a decent amount of power to regenerate. Managing your weapons heat levels is also a lot more fun (in a challenging sense).

Option to disable robot voices:
This is just my opinion, but they're really annoying.

I can't believe I wrote all that.
Good game, has lots of potential.
