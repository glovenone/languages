Metal Slug Defense is a casual, laid-back, tower defense game that consists of a base on both ends of the map. Players can have a deck of up to 10 units maximum to send out to advance towards the enemy base and then watch them battle to the death. But there's a catch, after a certain time limit has passed the player may use their "METAL SLUG ATTACK" ability which sends out a flashing tank that charges across the map and mows through weak units and explodes on contact with heavy units. There are various factions in-game where if your deck consists of a single faction, you can get HP bonuses for the round. One may find the game to become extremely repetitive due to the lack of challenge, and this is coming from playing the iOS version of the game where you won't have to deal with any pay-2-win scams.

It's free-to-play and not necessarily pay-to-win on the original, better mobile version. You can also purchase units with medals in that version. No pressure, no harm done. You get medals over time (if you're willing to part with that) and so spending money is merely an express method to getting what you want faster.

It remains free-to-play in this version, but you cannot under any circumstances purchase units with medals. Instead, you have to fork out upwards of 30+ dollars (Canadian) for not individual units, but for entire packs of units. This monumentally insulting flaw paints this as an indisputable cash-grab, and nothing more. The original, mobile version was a decent way to kill time and didn't force you to spend money at all; this version is mostly unchanged but FORCES you to spend hard-earned money on units you might not even like, and in groups, too, rather than units that you actually want.

What if I don't want all of the Ptolemaic Army units but just want the Metal Rear? Well the joke is on me, then, because I'd have to fork out $16.99 regular price for that ONE pack (there are two for the Ptolemaic army, but that's just one group). The joke is not just on me, but on all of us.

So what of the way it plays? Well, unfortunately much of the interface remains unchanged. Sure, you can change the window resolution in the title screen, but that's the only thing that makes this resemble a PC title. On top of that, which buries the hatchet further, is the garbage control system. Somehow SNK Playmore missed the fact that you will be playing this version on the PC, which in most cases we can all assume will have a keyboard & mouse setup. You can use the mouse, but the controls are entirely designed with touch screens in mind. You know, the same screens adorning the mobile platform this game was originally made for. You have to click & hold your mouse and swipe to scroll horizontally (and as you go along, you'll do that a lot); you will have to do the same movement to scroll through the battlefield. All of this could have been changed with little work yet SNK didn't even bother.

So why should we bother, then? Why bother giving them dozens of dollars for apathy?

What is the excuse, that they were releasing it as a free-to-play so "f*** it"? Why not just stick with the mobile platforms if you're going to be this unintuitive, this lazy, this...condescending? It's fine on that platform because in contrast to this port, if you could call it that (minus the resolution options, which doesn't even affect video quality but rather the mere size of the window...), but this version is a complete money-grab. It won't appeal to everybody for sure because it's basically a collection of all the assets from the Metal Slug series retooled in a tug 'o war TD gameplay format, with little to truly set it apart from the series as a whole. But that's not the important part; the important part is that this version is meant to nab your money, and SNK Playmore wants to get as much money as they can get all things considered.

I'm not sure how far they think they'll go with the schemes on display with this half-♥♥♥♥♥ 'port', but minus the dolts who bother to pay out for the unit packs, no one is going to buy into it. If you really want to play this game then do it on your mobile phone, assuming your platform is supported anyway. If SNK Playmore wants to put this game on Steam then fine, but don't let them do it this way.

Gets a 1/10 for even bothering to do it anyway. Oh yeah and I have BlueStacks, so I could already play it with all of my progress from the game on my phone, on the PC with the aforementioned emulator, intact.

FIX THIS NONSENSE!
