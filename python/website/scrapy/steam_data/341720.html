About This Game

In Among Ripples you are presented to a soothing and meditative experience where you can experiment at your leisure. As the caretaker of a small pond you decide which creatures should be introduced into the ecosystem, and what species will thrive. Study the creatures you introduce and learn more about them in order to shape a pond that you find sustainable. This game is a short but sweet experience where you get the opportunity to relax and think about the balance in nature.


Features:
* Sandbox style gameplay where you are free to choose your own goals
* Intuitive communication of the game system
* Organic art style drawing inspiration from shadow puppet animations and watercolor paintings
* Serene soundtrack with ambience changing with the seasons
* Relaxed experience where you can take your time and see how things progress at your own pace


Among Ripples is a free product with no strings attached. It's a little experimental title we want to share freely with you all.

Interesting little experiment is this game. A literal fishbowl (or pond as it were) and a self contained ecosystem that you tweak by spawning various critters (some fish, some shellfish, an otter) and by adjusting the oxygen content, which adjusts the toxicity of the water and from what I can tell, the lifespans of the fishies.

It's a free title, and an interesting way to spend ten or twenty minutes. After that, the charm sort of wears out and you are left wondering why the otter never needs to come up for air, lol.

Interesting enough to be worth your time, but not enough here to hold your interest for long. I'd give it a 6 out of 10 (mostly because it was free, otherwise I would rate this more like 4 out of 10), and hope that the developer explores this genre a bit more and possibly creates something a bit more detailed. It has the potential to be a very cool simulation game with the proper time and dedication. As it is, you will find it relaxing but probably wont ever play it again.

I've only spent 10 minutes on this game and unfortunately that's as about as long as it lasts, the concept is very sound and I must admit when I saw it i was imagining something like Darwin Pond or Simlife so I was a little disappointed when I found out it was a simple what if flash game.

You'll start with the pond and can add different creatures depending on which part of the pond you choose to click on, naturally I found all the creatures spawn by themselves at some point and you really need to add all creatures to create a working eco-system, now if only the pond was much larger instead of cramming many hostile creatures into one small space (imagine if you put 5 phiranas and 5 goldfish in a small fishtank then just ignored them for a few weeks)

After this the only other option I found to have had was to control the oxygen levels of the water (I would have liked to have controlled the PH levels though without using just clams which makes it more of an arcade aspect than a simulation) this is turn allowed me to see low oxygen means no algae and fish swim lower and that was really it.

I'm going to recommend this game because I love the idea behind it and that it may be a brilliant concept for a much deeper gaming experience to come, it reminded me a hell of a lot of the Virtual Sea Monkeys game I played as a child so happy memories abound!

I'm giving this a 6 out of 10, purely on the basis that I think this is a concept demo for a much larger game due out in the near future!
