About This Game


GODS are not born, they are made by YOU! Join the action in this highly addictive multiplayer card strategy RPG “FORGE OF GODS”! Collect and train as many monsters as you can to assemble the greatest team and burst through hordes of enemies in thrilling, addictive and fast-paced game! Conjure powerful dragons, sturdy warriors and exotic beasts to fight for you in the land of Pangea! Are you ready for battle?

Kill enemies -> Capture souls -> Evolve them -> FORGE army of GODS -> Be no.1 !


  • Unique “rearrange and move” battle system is simple to grasp and very tactical to constantly test your strategic skills!
  • Huge tree of creatures evolutions! Mix different types to breed legendary creatures!
  • 1000+ creatures, each with unique skill and hundreds of upgrades. A broad field for your experiments!
  • 100+ locations (3 difficulty levels) with towers, runes, spawn points to fight and capture elite creatures for your army!
  • Great skill-based PVP duels against 1000’s of real players with rankings, streaks and tournaments!
  • Active guilds with chats, leaders, officiers, upgrades, raids and guild wars. Group up with your friends to be №1 on server!
  • Tons of quests and achievements to complete with good rewards.
  • Beautiful castle to improve with mines, barracks, sawmills, shrines and beast's camps.

Rush to FORGE your own GODS in this huge and dangerous fantasy world of Pangea! Enjoy spectacular new challenges every day you log on!


NOTICE: Forge of Gods is completely free to play. However, some in-game items can also be bought for real money.

TL;DR: If you are looking for a standard energy driven collection / battle game on the computer, this is an excellent choice!

FULL REVIEW:
At a glance it is your standard micro-transaction driven collection and battle game. There are countless similar titles available in the Google Play and Apple app stores. That may seem like a reason to look elsewhere, but that would be acting too hasty. Just because it has a lot of common features doesn't mean it's bad!

Micro-transactions:
YES, THIS GAME IS FREE TO PLAY.
Through regular play you earn both "gems" (purchasable currency) and "gold" (earned currency). Gold is used to level up units. Gems are used to buy items / save time.

Energy:
You have a set amount of energy for questing and attacking. This resets to full when you level up, and refills on a timer. Its a good thing there is energy, otherwise I would never stop playing. (highly addictive)

Controls:
Everything is either click or drag. Unfortunately the in game help references touch commands.

Collecting:
You can spend in game currency to buy eggs to hatch. Each egg has a random unit with rarity based on the type of egg you opened. You also can capture units while you quest. You can only hold so many monsters before they start to run away. Fortunately you can use gem to buy more space, sell monsters, or grind them into XP as part of crafting. I would like to have more space, but I have enough that I don't feel hindered by it.

Crafting:
The crafting system is a little buggy with respect to clicking. This is likely a side effect of porting from a touch app. You need to "fuse" monsters to max level and then "evolve" pairs of them. Sometimes these evolutions generally completely new monsters.

Questing:
You have more control over your questing than many other games. You have a 5x3 grid of boxes to place your units which impacts who they can attack. They advance one column at a time so you can adjust your strategy and targets each click of the “go” button. Alternatively you can simply fast forward.

There are multiple types of quests you can do.
Story Quests (which are available in multiple difficulties) advance the plot and can give healthy doses of XP.
Daily Quests which can be used for farming specific things like gold or evolution components. These daily quests do NOT give XP.
Guild Quests (raids). These help your guild as a whole earn prizes.

Leveling:
Each player gains experience as they play allowing them to level up. Each level grants a bonus ranging from increasing your squad size, to unlocking game features, to expanding your friends list, increasing your energy max, etc. One of the features you unlock is your castle. Your castle can be further leveled to make items work better, earn gold and resources, or achieve other bonuses. Each of your individual creatures level up as well, gaining a stat bonus.

Missions:
Every day you get missions you can complete for items / XP / etc. These bonuses are helpful, and there are enough of them you are unlikely to run out unless you spend money (in which case you really don't need their bonuses as much).

PVP Battles:
This is basically the same as questing, except you are fighting a squad created by another person. Depending on how many wins you achieve, you get rewards and harder opponents.

There are three types of PVP fights:
PVP Tab where you actively battle players for gold. You have a choice of 3 opponents.
Ambushes which randomly happen when you start a quest. You have a choice of 3 opponents.
Guild ranking where you fight for your guild’s rank. You can choose any undefeated member of the enemy guild.

Guilds:
The benefits of guilds are already discussed under Quests and PVP!

Summary:
I know this was fairly scattered but I hope it helps. It may seem like a standard micro-transaction collection game, but it does an excellent (and addictive) job of it. Give it a try, you literally have nothing to lose!

How to make a random cash-grab game with mindless mechanics and lowest cost: this game is the result.

You step a party through a predefined area by pressing space for each stept, and nothing else ever. If any enemy is adjacent, the party will automatically attack, you may however choose one target that should be attacked first. But in the end it doesn't really matter anyways. So basically you slam space till some game window tells you that you earned stuff. You can get the creatures you fight (by chance) and random loot that boosts your party. Creatures can be used to evolve them into stronger versions (kind of like a levelup+), or can be "eaten" to grant free XP.

The game starts easy, but already after a few levels, enemies sometimes just one-shot party members. And the only way to get through those levels is to use boost items. Some of them you get for free, the rest need to be payed with ingame cash-shop items. And just so you use a lot of them, the game doesn't even ask you if you actually want to use cash to activate the boost. One miss-click and 9 gems are gone. One boss that just cuts everyone's HP in half per turn, and all your cash is gone.

Thanks to the very primitive gameplay this game gets boring rather fast. All levels are the same, enemy abilities rarely matter, evolutions and new creatures are plain random, if you even manage to understand how it is done at all. Maybe it gets more tactical on the higher levels, but then you better already emptied your wallet into the cash-shop, or you have to endure a mindless grind of pure random luck.

The translations show the effort that was put into this game, namely none. I started with German, but everything was so wrong, even Google translate could have done better. English language is ok-ish, but the game only lets you enter the options menu after you played through the tutorial. Till then you have to read things like "This egg artifact must get. Beware!"

Unless you are a big fan of mindless money-grabber flash-games, stay away from this game. It is a pure waste of time.
