About This Game

A single player metroidvania 3D game with cooperative gameplay, set in space on a blocked water storage station.

Find out what's going on by taking control of distinct characters and change between them whenever you want.
Blast and slash the enemies, take care of your team health and avoid connection interference.

  • An original mix of 3rd person action shooter and challenging puzzles: a rare type of game on PC
  • One story mode + 3 Arena Modes in many areas, where you try to survive in 3 distinct game mechanics
  • Find and take control of distinct SynBs, alien clones made of flesh with cyborg implants
  • Explore and solve puzzles for finding and activating the purifiers for the water supply
  • Learn the different types of waters and their effects on the characters
  • Find and use different weapons to destroy all the enemies and take advantage of melee attacks on dazed opponents
  • Amazing colorful 3D graphic with cartoon shader in very distinct 3D art style
  • Original interact system with elements by aiming with weapons or the device called the Activator

It is so rare to have to use your brain in a video game that probably you forgot how...

Posted: 19 October, 2014
Let's start with a note: In the description the game claims to be a Metroidvania. This makes no sense, at all.
This game is not a metroidvania so don't be fooled by that, while there are rooms connected between each other that's not what makes a game a metroidvania in ANY WAY.
This game it's an action adventure full of puzzles and with a funny (even if not that innovative to be honest) mechanic, you can swap your body between human like manikins to resolve puzzles and go further in your adventure.
Your mission it's to depure a scientific plant.
And this is all about the story for now, let's talk more about the game, shall we?

Graphically the game does not offer anything special, the enemy models are color swaps of the same 4 so no variety under this aspect and the scenarios textures are almost nonexistent, of course this could be the developers' choice after all.
The musics tracks are few and you are probably going to easily forget there's any music at all, aside for the "battle theme" wich is a 10 seconds theme repeated until the battle ends, really.
Story wise the game does not offer anything special, sure it's still in alpha stage but i expected something more than "you are there to fix the mess, bye", after all this is adventure game and the story, at least in my opinion, is really important.
Controls are really messy sometimes and the aiming is a real problem, you get no crosshair aside for the "laser" but that does not help that much, maybe that's just a matter of practice but when you are doing the "light balls" puzzles not knowing where you are actually aiming makes these puzzles really frustrating sometimes.
The dodge is basically a barrel roll and after a patch you can do it pressing left or right two times, surely easier than before.
That and the realoading wich makes you really slow for like 3 seconds makes some battles really hard (and it's not a bad thing) and frustrating in some cases (and this is bad).
Puzzles are really easy, in fact the only way to waste time on it is to die because of some glitch (like the deaths sometimes happen in the "mirror rooms") or because enemies hit your uncontrolled character throwing it into the red water killing it without any way to save it, sure you need to be alert so maybe it's just a way to punish you if you get distracted, maybe.
You can't save your game whenever you want, the game saves automatically so if the save it's far away from the point of your death... well you got the idea right?
I guess this is another discutible choice, not everyone loves such a thing in fact.
The map it's so small and there's no way to enlarge it and this makes it. most of the time, completely useless.


This is my opinion (for now) about The Body Changer, the game is still in alpha but, to be honest, i haven't seen much changes from the old alpha.
The game has been released for less than a week on Steam so the developers have plenty of time to fix some things still, for now and for the price asked, this game it's a No for me.
If you still want to buy it you have to know about something, the game does not have an ending yet, infact when you get to the fifth depurator you can't go further (and that's half of the game).
So yes you are paying for half of game for now.
This is all I've got to say for now, i'm probably going to change my review if something big changes or maybe when the game comes out of Early Access just in case.
Until then.

Early Access Review
Posted: 19 December, 2014
If you want to pay $20 for an unfinished game that looks like it belongs on Dreamcast, here's your chance. Don't get me wrong, I'm not a big graphics guy. I can easily dig a good game with retro style graphics like Shovel Knight that pays good homage to its era. In comparison, TBC just looks dated (gaming circa 2002) and has probably the ugliest use of cel shading I've ever seen. The game's atmosphere feels sterile void of any charm or interest. And the audio? Completely forgettable, I don't remember a single tune from the game, granted most of the playtime is with the Desura build so this may have changed with the Steam release but from my brief testing, the game appears unchanged from its most recent build on Desura.

The gameplay isn't much better. The controls are ultra clunky and the combat reminds me slightly of the old PS2/XBox horror game Cold Fear but have action games come a long way since then and honestly Cold Fear already did it better. The puzzles are easy and occasionally well crafted but don't feel substantial enough to carry the game. It feels like the game was trying to be a mix of something cerebral like Portal and while having action similar to Resident Evil 4 but fell completely flat in both regards. If a developer is going to price their game at the level of some of the 2 year old AAA games, it should be able to hold its own. No, I'm not expecting AAA quality from an indie developer, but the pricing falls in the premium range as far as indie products go therfore I expect something good. This game is far from the indie cream of the crop. TBC fails miserably in many areas and it's simply just not fun to play.

I'm glad I won my copy in the winter auction for far less than the regular price. At regular price, the game is far too boring and unpolished to be worth the $$$. There's some honest hard effort evident in this game but good effort is not enough reason to give a recommendation. At least, the game seems to launch more stable in its Steam release, I had to jump through hoops to get the game to simply load through Desura. In comparison I tried loading the game 3 times in Steam and it loaded perfectly each time.

I could comment of the developers' behavior regarding bundle buyers but I won't delve too much into that area. I will say this: the gaming consumer base does not take kindly to censorship. The developers will have to learn how to better handle criticism of their product or for any of the choices they make. Deleting comments and censoring the issue simply doesn't help or resolve anything. I'm not one of the people who expect a Steam key from Desura games so this incident did not affect my choice in not recommending this game. I solely judged the game on its merits (or lack thereof) to give my rating.

Keep in mind this is an Early Access game, things could improve in the future and if they do improve I will be willing to re-visit the game and change my recommendation if the game warrants it. As it remains now, the game is a mess and not one I can recommend.

This game touts itself as being a metroidvania 3D game but honestly it has nothing of the genre. It's a 3D, character changer puzzler with shooter elements. Think 3rd person Gobliiins with guns.

I have a limited amount of playtime on Steam, but keep it mind I had the game on Desura from my previous purchase and had played it before.

The game is still in early alpha and it shows, very much. Controls and movements are sluggish, graphics are very bland and sterile (the washed out cell shading effect doesn't do it for me), and in my case the game wouldn't even start without completely deactivating audio. The default controls aren't well thought of either and you will find yourself frustratingly deactivating panels while trying to change character. You are thrown into the game with no real explanation but a few engrish lines of text as you go on, teaching you the bare minimum. The atmosphere felt dull, and I got no real entertainment factor. There's a reason no one is playing the game, including those who provided such good reviews.

I understand that the game is currently in Early Access, actually it has been in alpha state for nearly 3 years, and I'd be happy to revisit this review if I'm told that any significant improvement has been made regarding these issues.

There's also the fact that communications between the developers and the community has been less than stellar, their choice of providing only a subset of their previous customers with a Steam copy of the game they bought, and deleting negative comments on both Steam's community hub and the Desura game page. (Some of those comments were downright nasty but there was also legitimate criticism that got deleted.)

When I first saw the screenshots and trailers for the game, I'll be honest, It didn't look too great. Regardless, I gave it a try. And I will say I am impressed. The game was made by 2 people, and I was honestly shocked they could pull it off. This game is full is puzzling scenarios and fun adventures. The story is a bit clunky, but I think the gameplay really makes the game what it is. The controls are good with a controller, which for me personally is a huge plus. This game has been tailored to what the community suggested. I can even run this on my bad laptop. Not just that, but the graphics are great! And the graphics are a new-style unlike I've seen before. It's weird, cool, and ascetically pleasing in a way.I'll be honest to say it's not the best game ever, but it's above ok, FOR SURE! Last but not least, the game isn't the longest game ever, but I'm sure that'll change. If you are looking for a game that requires strategy, and a truly unique experience, pick this game up if YOU fell it looks good :) But if you don't have a controller, the keyboard controls are difficult, for $20 it's not a bad buy, but apparantly more levels and such are being added, so you may want to pick this up!
