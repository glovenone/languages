About This Game

Play with gravity to guide Naught through a mysterious underground world where darkness is a relentless enemy that takes many forms. Naught Reawakening is an adventure and platform game that challenges your skills giving you full control of Naught and his environment.

Naught Reawakening includes amazing features that reinvent platform game style. Naught is characterized by procedural animations that allow him to adapt his movements to the surroundings by holding on to walls, modifying his way of walking depending on gravity and the ground surface, and pushing objects in a unique and dynamic fashion. Naught can also take advantage of skills like jumping and diving to solve puzzles, avoid stalking creatures and fight dark monsters. Help Naught escape from darkness and recall his memories in hidden levels.

In Naught Reawakening you will find more than 30 levels that include 3 bonus levels, big enemies, diverse gameplay, time trial mode for every level and loads of secrets.

Nice and difficult indie platform game with a twist and unique visual style. You play as Naught, an undead cat-like creature that travels through dark levels filled with traps and monsters. You don't move your character directly - instead of that you fluently turn his whole world around to make him slide in the direction you want. If you wish to survive, you have to be extremely cautious and fast about it.

There are new enemies, deadly traps and rules introduced every few levels to keep it interesting and challenging - you'll learn how to swim, how to make use of the tree spirit following you, how to use bubbles of air to enhance your maneuvers and so on.There are three boss fights with giant creatures as well. There's no way you'll feel bored.

Naught Reawakening is quite difficult. Of course if you simply rush through all 30+ levels it might appear easy and very short - it takes less than two hours to get to the end. But its beauty lies in collecting diamonds. To overcome all dangers lurking in the dark and collect every well-hidden diamond you will need patience, wits, good reflexes and at least four hours. Beating time trials is even more challenging and requires a lot of practice, but awards the player with lots of achievements.

Naught's visual style is very similar to Limbo - the world is dark and atmospheric, almost entirely covered in shadows. Cratures inhabiting it are kind of creepy too. There are no graphics options available, but that's understandable, since it's a 2D game. Rest assured, though, that it looks good in FullHD resolution and runs well even on older rigs. It sounds nice as well since music makes the game even more psychodelic and strange.

The only major flaw of Naught Reawakening is its price. PC version costs more than twice as much as Android and iOS editions for no apparent reason, which is simply unfair, since it's exactly the same game. It might not be worth 9,99€, but be sure to give it a try as soon as it's on sale.

TL;DR
Pretty interesting game, good for those who know the Naught series as well. Rotate the world to move your character, collect diamonds, avoid obstacles and make it to the end of the level.

Full Review
The game I found intriguing as I have always enjoyed these styles of games. Think Puddle in a sense where you tilt the world to move except you can completely rotate the world in this one. It has the darker colour scheme with some of the enemies and objects adding the only colour into the world which works out great.

You can either rush the levels or try and complete them while collecting all the diamonds on the level, which isn't always as easy as it sounds. Collecting all diamonds unlocks a few bonus levels and time trials to do.

As I already mentioned you tilt the wold to move, you also can jump and mastering jumping and tilting at the same time is definitely something you want to do to reach some of the harder diamond locations. There is enemies that must be avoided and dangerous environments to be careful of. There is also swimming in some maps as well and some boss battles.

One thing I really like was the parts where you have to become the orb and make it through the winding passes clearing debris to allow Naught to continue on through the level.

The game play was smooth and did not experience any crashes (Windows 10 64 bit for those who wonder). Controls can be rebinded.

At the time of me writing this my game play has not included unlocking all bonus levels, completing a bunch of time trials nor collecting all diamonds, but I will get to finishing all that.

If I missed anything I'll be glad to update my review to reflect that.
