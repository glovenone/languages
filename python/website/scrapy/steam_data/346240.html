About This Game

Gameplay mechanics


The core gameplay mechanics of Ski Park Tycoon lie in ski lift and slope construction. Before building a park, the player needs to study the mountain topography and terrain. Therefore, it was imperative to allow the player complete freedom of camera movement. Building lifts and slopes is as easy as drawing lines on the ground. Skiers coming to the resort want to enjoy their stay so - apart from lifts and slopes - the player also needs to provide other infrastructure such as restaurants, ski patrols, snow cannons etc. This is where the economic simulation aspect of the game comes into play.
You can learn more about the game in the online tutorial (http://www.skiparktycoon.com/manual/Contents.htm).

Game features


  • classic tycoon gameplay
  • full 3D environment
  • explore your park in third person view
  • hundreds of skiers in one resort
  • 7 types of lifts
  • 12 types of buildings
  • campaign with 29 missions of various objectives
  • sandbox and unlimited game modes
  • real world ski resorts

Posted: 21 October, 2015
First, there seems to be a new problem with Win7 & Nvidia 535.62 driver. Black screen at startup, BUT the game works well in WinXP SP3 Compatibility Mode!
I can recommend this to anyone who likes building and managing ski resorts and can give an old-style tycoon game a chance, which takes maybe 1-2 hours to learn. Tutorial worked not so well or was I too dumb - so I started sandbox mode (you have achievements though) an read everything I needed in the well-detailled Help-menu,
Graphics are nice, Controls are a bit strange and not configurable - e.g. inverted mouse-wheel for zomming in/out of the map. The Sound is a boring elevator-style track - please add some kind of ski-resort-like music. On the other hand absolutely no severe bugs.
Gameplay is ok, but sometimes you are doing nothing but running 4x speed + watching the hourly changing statistics (which have the right measure). 8x or 16x Speed option would be nice for that reason.
The economy system is another matter. The right number of Parking lots is extremely important for the balance -especially at the beginning of a game- as they cost a very huge amount of money per hour and can so decide if you win or lose money. They also limit the max. amount of visitors per day. This is okay if you know it, but quite strange. They should definitely be bit cheaper in the easy mode. Then there is the bad reputation value, which seems to be a horrific multiplicator for any small problem in the resort. Furthermore the Grooming Garages are preparing too small spots on the slopes, so you need very much of them, there should at least be a bigger garage available with multiple vehicles.
But I have some fun with the game, really!
Buy a cheap key or here at discount, for € 5 you get great value!

1.9 hrs on record
Posted: 4 February, 2015
Generally I like games like this. I really been looking forward to seeing a tycoon in this area for some time and for a short time the game is a neat idea. But thats the theory. This game has quite a few problems that is unknown if they will get fixed.

Once a day every day (It's hard to know if its by day, basicially its whenever the daily quarter is over) you will have stacks of skiers go to the top of your staging area, only to go all the way back down to the bottom and exit the park through any terrain imaginable. Plus there is not very many things to build (Why no hotels? Bus or airports? Taxi service? Multiple kinds of vehicles for each building type?)

At first you are confused, and if you like me start off in sandbox, you will probably (maybe not all will) need to go to campaign and try a few missions to understand the basics. You won't learn much, the tutorials are rushed. Each map designed is poor, no random generating maps so you will have to pick the same maps over and over if you play the game for some time (low replayability), skiers look bugged (If you place a lift wrong the skiers will take the lift downhill and attempt to slide down the hill upwards which makes them stuck), and you make little income from the skiers. Charge too much, less will come.

In some games this might just be the challenge, but after some time you realize you can't afford what you need to run the park in the first place. So you constantly restart in the hopes you attract more customers. But then you realize all of you skiers are stuck in one spot so you let them stack up hoping they will die only for the next day they magically decide they can go through four mountain passes on skis and snowboards through forests and nothing but dry land and pulling out your hair wanting to grab your screen and throw it across the room while yelling "Stop SCAMMING ME". Yea, sorry, pro and cons.

Pro

Potential

Quite a few maps and some hidden bonus ones apparently

Cons

Small maps (Lower graphics requirements should mean bigger map capability in todays age)

Bugged Skiers

Bugged lifts

Not many building options

Must spam same buildings (What, I need fifty small 10 car garages in the same spot)

Need more ways to make income and attract tourists (HOTELS)

Slope creation mechanics look barbaric (Looks like something a child would draw.)

Not enough information sometimes to determine why you are not getting or losing customers.

Low graphics (This can be turned into a pro but at this time and stage the game is just so limited that it should have been put on better graphics. Now if the game is really expanded than I would accept the game in this condition but really, it does not deserve to be placed higher.)

Random bugs (I think the game has issues running on more powerful machines but so far no crashes for me, just random error messages every once in awhile.)

Map scrolling issues (sometimes in tutorial or when starting a new map the game prevents you from zooming out very far so you see a very small stretch of map that you have a hard time doing anything with. Trying a different map fixes the problem.

The game has a few issues that are difficult to reproduce so the bugs are not really a problem the developers will have a easy time fixing. But it should be run a little bit more before releasing. Also I played this game for a couple of hours. The game personally should be played in small doses. I would not recommond this game unless you love tycoon games and want to catch them all (oops wrong game). And for those who want to bash me for not playing more for the review, well, I'm sorry but the game is just too simple. It's not like dragon age, in this game you see all your options in the beginning of the game, not halfway through it. You don't research new things. You don't craft. You just build a few buildings and hope for success. Simple

Also, how come there is no town interaction? Or even town growth or anything that we can do in that line that would increase our customer growth?
