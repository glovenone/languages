Full Release!

Riff Racer (known as Drive Any Track during Early Access) is now in full release!
We'd like to thank all the community who have helped us so far during development of the game.
We'll continue to experiment with features as we go!

About This Game



Includes 25 fantastic bundled tracks!! Or, race your own music...

Inspired by some of our favourite games like Wipeout, Trackmania, Vib-Ribbon and Audiosurfâ¦



Riff Racer is a high-octane music based racing game where we create racetracks from the songs in your music library.

Not a rhythm game, but a fresh twist on music-powered game creation. Action on track is synced to the audio, you literally have to race the music to stay in time and score points.

You've seen other music powered games, Riff Racer takes the concept to the next level with our innovative MEGA engine. It takes any audio file, analyses it down to its core components and creates a unique racing experience from the structure of the song.

Verses, choruses and drops become checkpoints, and the environment and cars are styled to the genre of music you're playing.

Experiment with your music collection and find out which songs make the best racetracks!

  • Be the first to 'create' a race-track from a song and have your name forever enshrined on the trackâs leaderboard.

  • Multiplayer features - Challenge your friends' ghosts to your favourite tunes and beat their high-scores on the track!
    • **Please note Riff Racer does not support live multiplayer***

  • Score big for staying in sync, whilst performing stunts, jumps, drifts and rolls!

Riff Racer has unique environment styles based on different music genres, and a selection of genre based cars and liveries (and some secret cars you have to work out how to unlock!).

Riff Racer currently supports MP3, FLAC, OGG and AAC (m4a) file formats.

Riff Racer has full controller support in that you don't need a mouse and keyboard to play the game, but we only support newer xinput devices such as the X-Box 360 and X-Box One controllers. Older directinput controllers such as the Logitech Dual Action are not currently supported.

Riff Racer is a music based game with the feature to drive on any musical track you put into it. The core of the game is to rack up a large score by pulling off tricks on the tracks generated by your choice of music. Overall itâs fun to both listen and drive on the racetrack, with the game being a unique mix between driving and music games.

Any song can be used with Riff Racer to create a specific course to drive on. The gameplay is almost like Audiosurf with the looping, twisty design and the score based objectives. As you drive around the track, jumps, drifts, and boosts can be utilized to increase your score further. Along the track are white boxes to collect, which charges a boost that can be used at any time. At the end of the song, scores are uploaded to a leaderboard to promote competition between players on who can get the highest score for that particular song. The car based gameplay is quite unique, using some arcade driving controls similar to other racing titles. The music does sync up to the track in subtle ways. Fast and slow parts of the song are reflected by how frantic the part of the track is, and complex parts get more obstacles to avoid. Some key points in the current song get synced to obstacles on the track. Each of the certain track features from the song analysis is more loosely timed to the music, and results may vary depending on the songs used. Nevertheless, the driving gameplay offers a fun and relaxed experience to drive to your songs in a way that is timed fairly closely to the music. In addition, the competitive aspect of seeing who can get the highest score on a song is also there for those wanting to show off their skill in playing the game.

In an effort to showcase your music as the star of the game, the overall presentation is done in a minimal, yet bright and colorful style. The UI of the game goes for clean white text thatâs easy to read on the menus or on the track. The track design is quite flashy and retro in a way, with colors pulsing to the beat and changing based on the action in the song. Riff Racer does more than just analyze songs for their content. The game can also categorize what genre the song is and give the track an appropriate design. This genre detection is purely aesthetic, changing up the design of the track and scenery to better suit the mood. Sound effects are kept to a minimum to keep the music at the forefront of the action, but also to give feedback on the various events happening on the track. Overall itâs a clean and minimal design thatâs easy on the eyes, and gets visually more intense in time to the music.

As a game that takes cues from other similar music based games and racing games, Riff Racer puts a fun way to drive along to the music. The music synchronization to the track might be looser, but the driving gameplay is great and leaderboards are available to focus on getting high scores. For music and driving fans, Riff Racer brings an excellent experience combining both genres into one game.

I like this game. It took me a few hours to really decide whether I do, but I like it.

If you come in expecting the racing game equivalent of Beat Hazard.... really, this is not much of a racing game. You never race against other cars, it's all... kind of like a "time trial" but with points. By default, you don't even accelerate or brake!

If you accept it as "just imagine if Audiosurf was a car drifting game", then it's fun.

You just steer, boost, and drift (and drift and drift), on these insanely long rollercoaster tracks. Using ramps to jump and roll, avoiding obstacles. Drifting and jumping score points. It's addictive, almost hypnotic.

Most of all, it's a new way to enjoy your MP3 collection, and to me, that's worth a lot. :)

I do wish this game could connect to the streaming sites, e.g. Spotify, Google Music, Amazon Prime Music, or whatever. There are so many tracks I own but aren't actually downloaded.

BTW - I got this at 75% off in a sale and have no regrets. At the full price, however, I may have written this review grumbling about the game needing more features, diversity in track layouts, etc.

I absoloutely adore this game.
Its exactly what it says on the tin, Wipeout meets Audiosurf.
But its a satisfying marriage that sometimes has moments that even best its lovely parents.

PROS
- Tight controls and physics, even in midair. If you fall to your doom it feels like your fault (usually :p).
- Simple score system that has lots of aspects to it. When to boost? When to jump and when to go off those flips? When to start drifting for the longest drift? Little things can go a long way when going for highscores.
- Unlockables. Its a little thing, but I always love unlockables. Gives you more reason to play beyond mechanical reasons (which the game does wonderfully mind you)
- Nice, (generally) cohesive graphics that can run on any ol toaster like mine :)
- You can sort of snake with practice. Givin' me the GX goosebumps ooh.
- Ghosts are very well done and racing them gives a good competetive feel.

CONS
- Gui is servicable but text and buttons are too large. Text doesn't scroll when selecting new tracks so if a song has a long album/artist combination you can't read the track name :<
- Surprisingly even with unlockables, customization is minimal. Theres no graphics options, Audiosurf 1 alone had selectable "intensity" colours and custom skyboxes. I would prefer if I could choose the track type for a song as proper genre data isn't always available so you get stuck on "Dance" a lot.
- There are some rare issues with track generation. For instance, "The Hamster Alliance - Falling Through The Air" has a jump that you can rarely make without boosting. This is a reoccuring issues it seems from the bug report page. It makes tracks that would otherwise be low difficulty have a semi-frustrating aspect to them, cause you have to count jumps and remember which ones are death traps.
- There isn't proper leaderboards or statistics. You have to load a song to see the leaderboard. I know for a fact my highest score is about 210k but the game doesn't list that anywhere that I've seen. Also, doesn't have the cool aspect of Audiosurf where you had to try and keep your thrones. I loved that, was a good way to bring you back into the game.

TL;DR, super fun singleplayer race/music game, with a degree more customization and a gui overhaul it will really shine.
