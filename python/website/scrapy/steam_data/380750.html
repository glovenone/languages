About This Game

Anima: Gate of Memories is a third person action RPG that tells the story of two beings bound by an unwilling pact, an ancient monster and a girl who lost her past...
Forced to stay together, their lives will take a unexpected turn when both discover that something darker than they could imagine is about to start, a war in the shadows in which they will have a leading role.

Anima: Gate of Memories brings to videogames the world of Gaia from the Anima: Beyond Fantasy RPG table-top books. You will enjoy a deep and multifaceted story where your choices and actions directly impact the journey and decide the fate of the characters.

Dual characters

In Gate of Memories you control two different characters, Ergo and The Bearer of Calamities, and you can exchange from one to another thanks to the special synergy they have. What makes this system truly unique is that both are not "physically" present at once, but we can invoke the other even in the middle of any action, continuing the movement and creating spectacular combos. Each character also has at its disposal a large set of special skills that you will be able to develop and customize as in a traditional RPG.

Features

• A deep and multifaceted story. The player’s choices and actions will influence the journey and decide the destiny of the characters.
• Epic battles with formidable opponents. Challenge many legendary entities, each with their own special features and game play.
• A deep combat system. A combat system which combines RPG elements with fast paced action and spectacular attacks and spells.
• Unique locations. Travel through the tower of Arcane and the world of Gaia across stunning backgrounds and places.
• A huge world. You can move with great freedom, visiting different locations and giving you the option to always retrace your steps to revisit areas, discover new secrets and unlock access to previously inaccessible places.

So far, from what I've played from the game, I am not insanely impressed and I will probably be asking for a refund.

I got it because I had a craving for a third person action RPG and it looked similar to NiER which I greatly enjoyed. There is a ton of potential here - the art design is outstanding, the game has nice music and it has an interesting character dynamic.

But ultimately what determines how good a game is is the gameplay and it feels too unpolished. Even a B-level game like NiER had more fluid feeling combat. I really wanted to like it, but it is basically up to the level of a PS2 era hack and slash and it's hard to get past that when you KNOW you could be playing a better hack and slash RPG. It sucks because I really do want to see more 3D games from indie developers, 2D is overdone and boring. But then I am reminded of exactly why few indies make 3D games, there is a high bar to overcome and it takes a skilled game designer to make sure that the game doesn't feel amateurish, which this game feels like.

That leaves the story to make up for the failings of the battle system so it better be damn good. And well... the story is JRPG-esque, which is good, but the dialogue is a little too awkward. It's not bad, but it's not incredible either, and the lack of any animation during cutscenes certainly doesn't bolster the impact of the story. The personality of Ergo never really rises to that of, say, a Grimoire Weiss from NiER or Gig from Soul Nomad. And switching to him in battle is... literally the same as the other "Bearer" character. There is not much "light and dark" dynamic going on when both types of attacks are the same thing.

Ultimately I think with some more money invested in the game polishing the gameplay and the cutscenes it would be a good game, and I commend them for trying to make a third person action RPG with a JRPG bent, but in its current state it's too unpolished to make me want to keep playing, at least for the current price.

NieR meets DMC. Highly recommended.

Update.14.06.2016
Anima Gates of Memories is a beautiful action game with puzzle-platforming elements. There are two main protagonists with similar, yet different skill trees (the Bearer has better defenses, regeneration and dodge, while Ergo has faster and stronger physical attacks). Enemies and bosses are challenging and diverse (this is the only game, where I have been bounced by connected attacks. There's recovery in mid-air action, but the old habit to wait until I land to get some i-frames is hard to break), puzzles require some thought as well as platform sections require well-timed jumps (DMC4, Mission 7; DMC3, Mission 6 and NieR's Desert Temple style). The story is interesting, characters are relatable, combat system is quite good (a bit slow at the beginning, SSStylish by the end), soundtrack is amazing (I wish it'd be available as a DLC), VA suits the characters. There are side quests, a secret boss, several endings (which depend on in-game choices, not playthrough number). Perfectly playable and enjoyable with KB&M (controls are customizable).

Also the devs answer on the forums and patch the game quickly.

Cons.
  • only 5 save slots;
  • almost no KB on-screen prompts;
  • no 5-button mouse support.

Update.15.06.2016
Anima's sadistically hard at some points.
Completed the game. Going for NG+.

Update.20.06.2016
Some things worth notice:
  • Easter eggs (from Shakespeare to Zelda);
  • The more Messengers defeated, the stronger normal enemies become;
  • Tutorial;
  • When all characters' abilities are unlocked and it's possible to fully use the Link system, the combat shines.
