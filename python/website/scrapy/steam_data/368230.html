About This Game

Wise Rulers know their kingdom will fall,
Brave Rulers do not despair.
Great Rulers know their riches can rule,
And spend every coin with great care.

In Kingdom, each coin spent can tip the balance between prosperity and decay. Attend to your domain, border to border, or venture into the wild to discover its wonders and its threats. Master the land, build your defenses, and when the darkness comes, stand with your people, crown on your head, until the very end.

Gameplay
Kingdom is a 2D sidescrolling strategy/resource management hybrid with a minimalist feel wrapped in a beautiful, modern pixel art aesthetic. Play the role of a king or queen atop their horse and enter a procedurally generated realm primed to sustain a kingdom, then toss gold to peasants and turn them into your loyal subjects in order to make your kingdom flourish. Protect your domain at night from the greedy creatures looking to steal your coins and crown, and explore the nearby, mysterious forests to discover curious and cryptic artifacts to aid your kingdom.

First off, let me begin be stating that this game in its current state is a great indie game, I've had loads of fun with it so far. It's definitely a unique concept put forth. However, I would not recommend buying it, at the moment. It's late game is, fairly broken, this is attirbuted to a few things:

1) Lack of unit control. Now, I understand that the aim of the game is to have only 3 keys to play, a simplistic approach. And I applaud that approach because even with very few controls, there are many concepts that can be learned and played from within the game. But as the game progresses, and the enemies get much harder, you find that not being able to control where units guard or attack is an EXTREME frustation. One example are towers, a good concept, but without being able to control the units within them, they become a waste of effort and time, which leads me to my second point.

2) Building management. To put it shortly, there is none. You build them by using your gold, and that's is it. As far as destroying them for a refund, or controlling what units occupy them, be it farmers or archers, they go in and out as they please, which is TERRIBLE late game. The concept of towers in this game is broken because of that fact, I have dozens of archers sitting way behind the front lines doing zip for the entire game, precious archers which could be much needed in portal raids. In its current state, it is more benefical to completely ignore tower mounds and not build them, as it wastes archers AND leaves them vulnerable to flying enemies.

3) Difficulty. While I admire a good challenge, the difficulty spike in this game is just plain ludicrous. The waves get extremely difficulty around day 30, and if the game goes on for too long, it becomes a literal stalemate with victory being a certain impossibility.

4) Lack of force replenishment. The only way to recruit new subjects in this game is through forest camps. However, at a certain point in the game, forest camps completely disappear! Which basically means you need to win with your current force count, which is quite literally impossible. Most games there will be at least one camp left late game, but even then your forces are destroyed at a higher rate for you ever to go back to positive gain at that late in the game. There needs to be a mechanic for you to recruit forces within your walls.

As it currently stands, it feels as if the devs didn't put much time into testing each mechanic, because if they had they most certainly wouldn't have kept it the way it is. I don't mind the difficulty level of the game when it starts to progress, but certain things are just annoying and unfair to deal with. If most of my biggest grievances are handled with in the upcoming dlc, I will be a happy camper and happily take this review down. Until then, I think it is extremely important to rebalance the entire game.

tl;dr The state of the end-game is pretty terrible considering this is built as a pretty light one-time experience

I don't have an issue with a cute, cheap little game like this having a short and distinct lifespan. If this game maintained its playability throughout the 5-6 hour experience then I'd heartily recommend it to everyone I know.


Where everything breaks down is towards the final stages of the game, where there's no longer anything left to 'build' in your Kingdom, and you're left with simply trying to keep it maintained as you reach to destroy the last holds of the enemy. And this would be fine - if it was handled competently. Instead once you've built all the utilities, the game becomes a grind that hinges on the RNG of the AI. Yes, there are a number of things you can do to corral the AI's extremely simple decision making, but ultimately the game descends into a day-after-day repeat of the same runs, hoping that *this time* the builders will get their act together and repair a wall in time or that the archers will actually get into the towers instead of mindlessly swanning around under them for no clear reason. But then one citizen gets caught, and the whole systems rolls back to the starting point as you have to wait to retreive them, let them waddle back to base and reequip them - hoping that they actually return to somewhere useful



So no, I don't recommend this. It's cute, the opening of the game is lovely, but so much of the 'challenge' of the game is built on an unfulfulling grind and dice rolls.
