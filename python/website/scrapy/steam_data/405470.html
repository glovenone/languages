This has replaced my other audio tools, namely Winamp (audio player), Format Factory (format converter) and FreeStudio (ripping tool).

It's a pretty powerful software, allowing you to rip audio directly from your sound card and from some websites. You can search for music and videos and download them, converting them as you go and tagging them. Once you've learned how to use Audials, basic tagging and managing media libraries becomes fairly easy.

The program crashes sometimes and a recent patch introduced the below-mentioned activation bug - I believe the devs will fix that eventually, as they seem to work on the program. But there has been no official statement or hotfix to this in a while, which at this price tag is unacceptable to me. I currently have an earlier version running and that one's okay.

Please note this is not a simple audio player, it's a proper media management tool that allows you to do a lot of different stuff. You can convert formats, download music directly from youtube, listen to internet radio stations, manage libraries on different machines, clean out duplicates and so on. If you only need an audio player, this is too expensive and feature-rich for you.

Well, it's a mixed bag. If you're tight on money, look for something else. But if like me you're often ripping audio or video from websites and have big media libraries on different machines (some over network) that you don't have a dedicated tool for, look into getting Audials.

Audials One Suite is powerful, but can be inconvenient at times. Since it's replaced a bunch of separate tools I used to use, it gets a Recommendation from me.

Audials One Suite is powerful, but can be inconvenient at times. Since it's replaced a bunch of separate tools I used to use, it gets a Recommendation from me.
