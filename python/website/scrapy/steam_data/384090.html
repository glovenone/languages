Early Access Game

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Sketch Tales is developed by an experienced, yet very small team. Launching on Steam is a possibility to receive the players’ support, owing to which we’ll be able to expand the team and do what has been planned. Support us and we’ll make a game like you have never seen before.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aY5xuFmutvc
A new important stage of coming out to public access begins for Sketch Tales. We do it only for you and your feedback is very important to us.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We expect 9-12 months of further development and polishing of the game before it's ready for the release. There is still a ton of features to add, polish and tweak before the game meets our vision and your expectations.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“First, it's going to be a better quality and polished product. Secondly, we will add Multiplayer, possibility to modify landscape, work out the Sketch Tales story and... yet undisclosed features.
Last but not least, a Cloud server to host all players' creations that can be integrated into the game.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The story brings you to an island, inhabited by 3 types of living beings: monsters, sketchmen and animals. They live their own life, look for food, goodies & shelter, hunt & fight, and even build relationships with you. It’s basic AI Simulation which we plan to expand drastically in future updates. On the gameplay side, Sketch Tales in Early Access offers all necessary basics for both adventurers and creative minds:
  • Draw your character, objects, weapons, spells, items, construction parts and creatures (to enable this, the game features built-in doodling editor and guides)
  • Build structures and fortifications
  • Fight enemies, survive deadly traps and anomalies
  • Explore the world and get quests
”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The full release version will be priced higher than the Early Access one.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We're really counting on the community's feedback to move the game forward.
We plan multiple contests and activities to inspire the players' creativity.”

About This Game

What is Sketch Tales?

Sketch Tales is an immersive action-RPG sandbox where what you draw becomes what you play. You appear in a hand-drawn world, wherefrom all the colors had been stolen by a supervillain warlock. Get revenge on him, join him or ignore him – you have all the options for both gameplay and story are non-linear.

What can I do there?

The game has action RPG mechanics (with quests, dungeons, loot and weapons) that are based on drawing gameplay: your own artistic creations drive the world, which provides freedom of choice and stimulates creativity. With drawing you can create ingame items, weapons, spells, construction parts, living beings and much more. For instance, you could draw a monster within mere seconds and get him instantly live in the game.

Key Features:

In Early Access now:
  • Drawing magic: create anything and change the game world with doodling;
  • No sweat fun: zero artistic skills required to get instant fun;
  • Freedom of choice: create a fully unique character and chose any path for him;
  • Show your true colors: fight enemies, avoid deadly traps and magic anomalies.

    Will be added with upcoming updates:
  • Paint the town red: invite your friends into the world you created, explore together or fight each other, exchange your creations with other players.
  • Living universe: huge open world with deep atmosphere and random events. AI life simulation system gives each NPC unpredictable behavior patterns, desires, needs and emotional connections.
  • Law & disorder: Abide by the laws of physics or break them - destructible objects, forces of nature and degradable materials are affected by you and affect the world around you.
  • Cross-platforming: Join a game session with your friends while using different platforms.
  • Languages: Sketch Tales will speak to you in your mother tongue.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 8, Windows 7, Windows Vista
    • Processor: Intel Core 2 Quad CPU Q6600 @ 2.40GHz (4 CPUs) or AMD Phenom 9850 Quad-Core Processor (4 CPUs) @ 2.5GHz
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA 9800 GT 1GB or AMD HD 4870 1GB
    • DirectX: Version 10
    • Storage: 3 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX 10 compatible

Early Access Review
Posted: 28 September, 2015
Sketch Tales is based on a brilliant idea - a sandbox RPG where you can draw, animate and even voice things while in-game.

But you can completely ignore that, if you wish. There's fun to be had no matter how you approach the game - landscapes designed for exploration, quests to find and perform, structures to build, beasts to kill and loot to hunt.

I spent my first hour just wandering around in confusion with a goofy grin. I was chased by enemies who muttered daft things at me or played the harmonica. I laughed as I killed something whose body span comically down the hill. I picked up a quest from an NPC who then randomly wandered off to fight a ninja. The whole thing is a living, breathing cartoon that oozes charm.

My "blind let's play" video of my first game: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4eqT1-DvBbw

You'll need to consider a few things before buying the game close to the early access release date:

1. Early Access here means that - it's very early days yet. There's lots to things to do, but not much depth. Just exploring the one island won't take long, but you can spend as much time as you want building and drawing etc.

2. The drawing element is essentially an in-game paint programme, with the ability to add basic animation frames if you want. Right now you can edit/draw anything you're holding in your inventory (if you have picked up the Witch Brush), plus you get to draw your character when you first start playing.

3. There'll be bugs - unoptimised graphics, a couple of spelling mistakes, features that don't seem to work - but they'll get fixed. No full crashes, but did have to reload quite a bit to clear any built-up lag, though.

4. The building isn't as intuitive as Minecraft, with (simple) menus to select shapes and so on, but once you get the hang of it you can build blocky structures easily. You can alter the default cube to stuff like ramps, arches and flat bits too.

5. You don't get given all the building/colouring tools at the start - you need to go out and find them in the world on scrolls. Plus, everything you build requires energy, which uses up very fast and then slowly fills back up again. You can find items that increase your max energy, but at the start of the game you're very limited (This applies to story mode - I don't know yet if sandbox mode will have this limitation).

6. Fighting is very basic. You start with fists but I recommend finding an axe!

Sketch Tales is already a lot of fun and shows amazing potential but right now it's very rough around the edges. I recommend it, but I'm most interested in what's to come. Multiplayer, when it happens, should be a riot.
