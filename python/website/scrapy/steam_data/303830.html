  • From 125 to 2000cc, find all of the biggest makes, faithfully reproduced with 3 unique ways to play: Superbike, Roadster and Custom.
  • Hit the road in Tournament mode and take on over 40 challenges of skill on 20 different circuits.
  • Customize your riders and challenge rival clubs in solo mode or in wild online games with up to 4 players.

Posted: 3 December, 2014
Visually it looks great and it's good to see some officially licenced bikes modelled well. Although I'm not entirely sure how KTM would feel about their roaring twin sounding like a dodgy arcade version of a 4 cylinder 600cc screamer. Speaking of which, the noise from the bikes will drive you batshit crazy within 20 mins and a lack of ingame music doesn't help, recommend turning sound down and draining it out with your favourite tunes.
If you enjoy games that don't try to hold your hand then this is the game for you! not only will it not hold your hand, it won't even tell you what the hell you should be doing. 1.5h in and the only thing that seems to be unlocked are the first 3 races in the Beginners tournament that I have already done 3 times each.
The ability to change what bike you're riding during the race not only negates the need for the stupid rough area's that restrict the sports bikes to about 85kph and allows the cruisers to excel, but it removes what little immersion the game barely offered in the first place and just feels a little silly.
I was hoping to get a slightly more arcade handling version of Tourist trophy, what I got was a cheesy arcade racer from the 90's, even if it has been wrapped into a fairly pretty exterior.
The cost is inexplicably excessive, and I will definitely be pursuing a refund.
If you see this on a steam sale for $2.99 then sure, give it a go, why not, you won't get 2 hours of average entertainment for that much anywhere else.

3/10
