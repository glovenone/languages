Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Over the last 9 months developing Gyrodisc Super League, the feedback we’ve received from players at local events and from our community has been crucial in improving the game’s core gameplay. Entering Early Access gives us the opportunity to involve a wider community in this process, and we’re incredibly excited to find out what you think of the game and get your ideas on what you’d like to see in the finished game.

We strongly believe that working closely with the community and iterating on your feedback is the best way to make Gyrodisc the ultimate flying disc gaming experience!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We expect to be in early access for about 6 months while we tune the core gameplay, develop the full roster of characters and put in place a complete single player experience for the game.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The final version of the game will feature an expanded roster of six playable characters, three unique arena environments, and a wider range of different court layouts to choose from. It will also include a complete single player experience in the form of Arcade mode, multiple costumes for each character to unlock, and a selection of mini-game modes inspired by classic arcade games like breakout and space invaders.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The initial Early Access release includes two playable characters, five court variations in our first arena environment and a fully featured versus mode with up to four-player local multiplayer and AI opponents. It also features extensive controller support.

We are committed to keeping the game playable and stable throughout the Early Access period, but please bear in mind that you may encounter bugs, visual inconsistencies, frame rate or balance issues during development. We aim to address these as quickly as possible, but thank you in advance for your patience and your valuable feedback during this period.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We do not intend to raise the price during or after Early Access.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We value your involvement in the development process and will actively read and consider player’s suggestions.The Steam Community forums will act as our main source of feedback during Early Access, and will contain subforums for players to submit bug reports and leave more general feedback on various areas of the game. Gyrodisc is a competitive game, and we understand that the smallest tweak can make a big difference to the balance of the game.”

About This Game

Gyrodisc Super League is a bright, colourful arcade sports game for up to 4 players. Players compete in a lightning-fast futuristic sport that’s a blend of air hockey, tennis and volleyball. The rules may be simple, but victory is seized through quick reactions and devious mind games!

Features

  • Fast-paced local multiplayer: Compete with your friends in thrilling competitive versus matches for up to 4 players, or add AI opponents to get in some much needed solo training. We plan to add new game modes throughout early access, including an arcade style story mode, and mini games!
  • Singles and doubles gameplay: Take on your opponent solo in classic singles matches, or battle it out in teams of two in hectic doubles games, where a combination of individual skill and team tactics will win the day.
  • Choose your character: Choose between two unique player characters, each with their own strengths, weaknesses and a powerful signature throw. More player characters will be added throughout Early Access!
  • Choose your Arena: Select from 5 distinct court setups at launch, with their own arrangement of goals, score boosts and obstacles that change the layout of the Gyrodisc Super League Arena. New courts and arena environments will be added throughout Early Access, allowing you to mix and match the visual and gameplay style of your matches.

Gameplay


Using all the moves at your disposal is key to victory in Gyrodisc Super League!
  • Run, dive, catch and throw to keep the disc out of your goal and send it soaring towards your opponent’s.
  • Lob the disc over your adversary’s head to catch them off guard.
  • Throw the disc the instant you catch it for a high-speed perfect return. Chain perfect returns together and the disc gets faster and faster!
  • Advanced moves including looping curved throws and wild special throws.
Use these tools together to wrong-foot your opponents and keep them guessing!

In Short:

Gyrodisc Super League is a neat title to include in your collection of "party games" with an enjoyable local multiplayer/co-op mode, however there is no online multiplayer mode at all. Only purchase it at a discount or if you frequently have someone around to play with.
Recommended with reservations.


At Length:

My first match against an AI opponent ended rather fast as I was checking out the controls. The rematch, then, was already a close call! The AI didn't play particularly poorly or anything, no, the game's just that easy to get into. Even after a few more rounds, it was still challenging without ever feeling unfair.
Likewise, computer-controlled players also make for valuable teammates in a doubles match and generally know when to act and when not. On the highest difficulty level, they're true professionals!
Having the learned the ropes, I found the controls to be precise and responsive. I was initially put off a bit by the lack of a crosshair or similar visualization of where you're aiming, but you get used to it. In retrospect, it makes sense, too; you don't want to show your local opponent the direction of your next throw.

Now this is where it's at. Currently, there's just a normal versus mode with a couple of simple settings, for instance a time limit or little changes to the playing field. Although some singeplayer extensions are scheduled, I don't see myself returning to these for prolonged periods of time.
Dueling your friends right next to you, on the other hand, is immense fun and there's always room for a few short rounds of that. Gyrodisc Super League is very accessible and anybody can pick it up in an instant. Highly important for a game like this.
At the same time, you have two different characters with unique techniques (four more coming later) and the game does reward skill, so it's not exclusively casual in nature.
The biggest point of criticism directly relates to this: Despite a lot of new features to be released and multiplayer being the strength of the game, the developers have no intention to support online functionality. A missed opportunity, really. (If you want to play via internet, keep an eye out for Disc Jam.)

As for the presentation, the comic-style graphics aren't exactly awesome, yet look decent enough and suit the game well. You'll see and hear a cheering crowd and some arcade-y tunes in the background, which, again, fits nicely.
In the main menu, you have access to some basic audio and video options. Nothing sophisticated, but gets the job done.
You can also fully customize the controls for up to four players. Unfortunately, it is not possible to have multiple persons use a single keyboard for playing; you will need extra controllers.

Do note that the game is still in Early Access and, as such, unfinished.
For one, there might be technical issues. Personally, I didn't encounter any and it runs perfectly fine for me.
Additionally, it may be subject to both next to no as well as substantial changes. This encompasses the planned updates mentioned above, for better or worse. The developers seem to be active in the game's Community Hub, though, so it's not unreasonable to assume they're going to follow their roadmap.

My closing thoughts are pretty much what I've written in the first section:
Gyrodisc Super League offers quick and easy fun for everyone and is worth trying out, but I'd suggest you to consider the local-only limitations of the multiplayer/co-op mode before buying. In terms of singleplayer content, other games will give you more for your money - split the price between four people playing together and it's a great deal.

Posted: 16 March
Gyrodisc Super League is Valiant Interactive’s early access and Steam debut. It’s an exciting and vibrant arcade sports game in which players throw a disc from one side of the field to the other and attempt to make the opposition miss the disc to score. Games are played similarly to tennis with singles and doubles matches. The game has the potential to be one of the best couch multiplayer sports games on Steam with the fast, frantic action becoming very addictive and laugh a minute.

I did not particularly enjoy the experience of the single player game. The reason for this is simple. It’s far too shallow at this stage of development. You can play individual matches against an AI opponent and that is it. However, to the developers credit they are aware of this and are incorporating an ‘Arcade mode’ that will include multiple unlockable costumes for each character as well as a selection of mini-game modes that have been inspired by classic arcade games like breakout and space invaders. This sounds good in theory but I’d love for them to include a season mode. I find that these games are so much more exciting when you’re constantly battling to win matches in order to finish on top of the ladder and/or to reach the playoffs so that you can win a tournament. Examples of recent games that feature great arcade season or tournament modes include Rocket League (to be featured later on the Steamified Countdown) and Kopanito All-Stars Soccer which we featured back on January 7. It’s a very basic concept but it adds a lot of replayability to your game.

What is present in the game already is definitely enough for the local multiplayer component of the game. I spent a short time playing this mode and for what it was it was certainly enjoyable. I expect that with future updates there will be even more to enjoy on this mode. It’s definitely going to be the biggest selling point for the game and while its still leaning towards more of a concept than a fully fleshed out entity it is already a lot of fun.

A bit of a disappointment is the fact that the developers have no intention of incorporating an online multiplayer game. This will definitely have an impact on the size of the potential player base that the game will have. However, the action is so fast that any latency issues would make playing the game a nigh on impossibility – at least to an entertaining and enjoyable level so I can certainly understand why online multiplayer may be an impossibility for this title.

At this present stage of development if you have friends to play with then purchase a copy of the game because you will already get your monies worth. If not then I’d wait for arcade mode updates and see if what people are writing about it at that time is something that you would enjoy playing.

Early Access Review
Posted: 5 March
Ok, so let’s get one thing straight right away. Gyrodisc Super League is not an online multiplayer game (and according to the developer’s FAQ there’s no plan to turn it into one “at this time”). If you’re looking for a version of Windjammers you can play online, I recommend looking elsewhere.

If you’re willing to look past that omission though, you might see Gyrodisc for what it really is: a surprisingly addictive couch competitive local multiplayer game. Having spent several hours playing this game with a group of friends, it generates the same sort of good natured hollering and banter that you’ll get from a session of Samurai Gun or Gang Beasts, particularly in those chaotic four player doubles matches.

The game sticks pretty close to the Windjammers formula, with a very similar arrangement of abilities, although there are a few tweaks that set it apart. The throw, lob and spike and dive mechanics seem pretty familiar, although they’ve been moved onto separate face buttons on the game pad. Supers are now performed by holding down the throw or lob buttons respectively, so they can be initiated at any time rather than being automatically triggered by the game. You also have a little leeway to move around in this state (albeit slowly) which comes in useful for those cases where the disc looks like it’s going to just squeak past your charge. For all you purists out there there’s even an “Arcade Style” control mode in the options that puts the dive, throw and spike back onto a single button, Windjammers style (although personally I found that led to a lot of accidental diving).

My favourite addition though is a small one. Between rounds, and at the end of a match the players enter a sort of celebration state, where holding down the face buttons triggers different upper body poses like muscle flexes, waving your arms in the air or shaking your fist at a victorious opponent. It’s a small thing, but coupled with the fact that you can still move around in this state it leads to a surprising amount of hilarity, as you and your friends invent new and interesting ways to look like idiots on court.

Of course, this is an early access game, and there are plenty of issues to be addressed. There’s very little single player experience to speak of right now (just AI bots in the versus mode) although according to the developers there’s an Arcade mode in the works. The AI definitely has some odd moments (on a number of occasions I’ve watched my AI doubles partner dive out of the way of a disc that was coming straight for it) but ramp it up to the highest difficulty level and there’s real challenge to be found there. After about ten singles matches against the maximum AI difficulty I’ve yet to win more than a point or two.

There are only two characters to choose from at the moment (the final game will apparently feature six) and you’re going to be looking at that same stadium in every game for the time being, though the selection of different courts to choose from certainly helps with this. There are currently five, and most will seem very familiar in their layout to Windjammers players, although the hockey variant definitely breaks the mould, and was one of my highlights of the night.

Ultimately If you’re dead set on online play (as many posters on the forums at the moment seem to be) I’d recommend looking elsewhere. On the other hand, if you’re after a fun local multiplayer title to play when your mates are round of an evening, I’d highly recommend giving this game a shot.

Product received for free
In Short:

Gyrodisc Super League is a neat title to include in your collection of "party games" with an enjoyable local multiplayer/co-op mode, however there is no online multiplayer mode at all. Only purchase it at a discount or if you frequently have someone around to play with.
Recommended with reservations.


At Length:

My first match against an AI opponent ended rather fast as I was checking out the controls. The rematch, then, was already a close call! The AI didn't play particularly poorly or anything, no, the game's just that easy to get into. Even after a few more rounds, it was still challenging without ever feeling unfair.
Likewise, computer-controlled players also make for valuable teammates in a doubles match and generally know when to act and when not. On the highest difficulty level, they're true professionals!
Having the learned the ropes, I found the controls to be precise and responsive. I was initially put off a bit by the lack of a crosshair or similar visualization of where you're aiming, but you get used to it. In retrospect, it makes sense, too; you don't want to show your local opponent the direction of your next throw.

Now this is where it's at. Currently, there's just a normal versus mode with a couple of simple settings, for instance a time limit or little changes to the playing field. Although some singeplayer extensions are scheduled, I don't see myself returning to these for prolonged periods of time.
Dueling your friends right next to you, on the other hand, is immense fun and there's always room for a few short rounds of that. Gyrodisc Super League is very accessible and anybody can pick it up in an instant. Highly important for a game like this.
At the same time, you have two different characters with unique techniques (four more coming later) and the game does reward skill, so it's not exclusively casual in nature.
The biggest point of criticism directly relates to this: Despite a lot of new features to be released and multiplayer being the strength of the game, the developers have no intention to support online functionality. A missed opportunity, really. (If you want to play via internet, keep an eye out for Disc Jam.)

As for the presentation, the comic-style graphics aren't exactly awesome, yet look decent enough and suit the game well. You'll see and hear a cheering crowd and some arcade-y tunes in the background, which, again, fits nicely.
In the main menu, you have access to some basic audio and video options. Nothing sophisticated, but gets the job done.
You can also fully customize the controls for up to four players. Unfortunately, it is not possible to have multiple persons use a single keyboard for playing; you will need extra controllers.

Do note that the game is still in Early Access and, as such, unfinished.
For one, there might be technical issues. Personally, I didn't encounter any and it runs perfectly fine for me.
Additionally, it may be subject to both next to no as well as substantial changes. This encompasses the planned updates mentioned above, for better or worse. The developers seem to be active in the game's Community Hub, though, so it's not unreasonable to assume they're going to follow their roadmap.

My closing thoughts are pretty much what I've written in the first section:
Gyrodisc Super League offers quick and easy fun for everyone and is worth trying out, but I'd suggest you to consider the local-only limitations of the multiplayer/co-op mode before buying. In terms of singleplayer content, other games will give you more for your money - split the price between four people playing together and it's a great deal.
