About This Game

A young man, Wilson Cooper, is playing video games at home, something his Dad forbids him to do. His Dad unexpectedly returns home early to find Wilson in the midst of a game. Dad is so furious, he kicks Wilson out of the house and leaves him on the streets of a ghetto. It is at this exact point you take control of Wilson Cooper, and guide him to riches.

Posted: 8 July
This is an extremely low effort point and click game. It's not Retro styled, the developer was even lazier than that. At best, one could optimistically say the developer released a "satirical interactive artwork", but this is simply too kind.

Graphics/Art: Terrible. Paintshop screens. No real animations. Voice acting, but extremely low effort drunk people doing one-takes on the lines.

Gameplay: Terrible. The character movement is clunky. It's a point and click adventure but not in a cool way - the developer took no care and didn't bother polishing anything. You can skip dialogue but it skips it inaccurately and blows past most of the speech, but the speech bubbles aren't aligned properly anyway so you end up repeating the terrible dialogue. There's no depth or interesting puzzles, it's flat and lazy. The game makes a halfhearted effort at being slightly edgy, which at best comes off as some of the B section content of GTA without any of the surrounding gameplay.

Technology: Terrible. Looks and feels like a flash game. Resolution can't be changed. Clunky interface lacks polish and it shows, it's glitchy and janky.

This is an extremely poor effort and arguably should be removed. At best it's a very poor satire.

If this is supposed to be the remastered version of an older game, then why is it so insufferably tedious to play? Everything screams rush job here. Some things are intentionally badly done (graphics notably), which is fine by me, but the UI is horrendous, some of the voice acting is painfully delivered through a faulty microphone, there's a crash at the baker shop (I managed to circumvent it by selling for $5 and then immediately leaving the shop), there is no save feature (all right, you can finish the game in less than 10 minutes but when you have to re-do it several times to get past that crash, it gets annoying), the inventory is ridiculous, and worst of all, you have to move with the arrow keys and bring up the inventory with the 'i' key, while the rest is done with the mouse... Sorry? You couldn't spend five minutes adding arrow icons to the left and right borders of the screen, and an Inventory icon in a corner?

I liked Tokyo Hosto and even recommended it, and I had a few laugh at the silly conversations in here too. But at this point, it's just a ridiculous scam attempt, a project that was destined from the start to be bundled so the guy could make a few easy bucks and not have to worry about people asking for refunds. This would be enough to make you lose faith in the indie scene...

Highschool project adventure/visual novel.

The page says the game needs 200 MB HD space, but it downloads 18 MB, and a third of that is the XNA framework. So, actual game ~12 MB. The graphics resolution is 720p, quality ranges from bad Flash drawings to beaten-to-death-with-effects photographs. There are 18 backgrounds and 16 characters, but you'll only get to see about half of these because the game crashes as soon as you sell something to the baker. The voiceovers have amateur quality as well. I am calling this a visual novel because you can't really manipulate items in the environment; most of the gameplay is cutscenes and dialogue (but you do get a movable avatar in three scenes, animation is 4 frames).
One positive point: I can confirm that it will run (such as it can, seeing it crashes in the baker scene) on an old slow XP box.

It's a very incomplete amateur game. Don't pay money for this.

Abandoned by your dad cause you didn't code a "butterfly game" for him you're left to fend for yourself on the streets trying to make money and becoming a "baller".
Get Rich or Die Gaming wants to be an adventure game but what is playable, about five minutes of it before it crashes, just feels like a bad flash game.


PROS:

-none

CONS:

-simply doesn't work, crashes in the same spot over and over after a few minutes, this has never been patched

-amateurish graphics, I also found no way to make it fullscreen

-sound quality is horrible, every dialogue features echoing voices- as if recorded in a closet-annoying static and bumps on the mic: voice acting is also terrible

-bad controls, no mouse support on the main menu, use arrows to move (but it's useless, since moving has no effect on what you can interact with) and mouse to look around/ interact

-dialogues make no sense and feature so many profanity/ absurd lines that they quickly lose any comedy value they might have had

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=526207072

One'd be less harsh with amateurish projects, but being one doesn't excuse the horrible production values, barebones gameplay, nonsense plot and- even worse- having a nonfunctional game being sold for a price: while there are a few games on Steam that are "so bad it's good for a laugh" this is simply a bad one.

Is this worth a purchase? No, of course not, even if it worked 3$ would be too much from what I've seen.
