About This Game

Fairspace is an amateur developed open-ended space exploration, trading and combat game. A full and rich persistent universe awaits your discovery.

Key Features


  • Full 3D flight control, Go in any direction you want.
  • A scripted adventure is Included to help introduce the universe to you.
  • levelling system based on XP that can be gained by combat, exploration or mining.
  • Twitch based combat.
  • Trade, Loot, Piracy, Mining or Bounty hunter be whatever you want to be.
  • Modify your ships load out with equipment purchased / traded or looted from other players .
  • Hundreds of different ships with many different load-out combinations, Discover the best combination that suits you.
  • There are many races and zones with allegiances to build or destroy.
  • Take jobs to improve your reputation and of course get some credits.
  • Persistent universe featuring planets, space stations, suns and nebula’s all available to explore.
  • You can have Multiple Ships docked around the universe and teleport between them.
  • Once the game is loaded you will never see another loading please wait screen.

MMO

An experimental MMO server is available for players who want to engage with others, forming teams to take joint missions or just roaming the universe killing anything they see, There are presently no safe zones or rules on the MMO server. This is an experimental server and has not been tested to capacity. The MMO server is sparsley populated so If the Idea of playing the game single player doesn't appeal to you then this may not be the game for you.

Early Access Review
Posted: 13 August, 2015
As it stands this game has a lot of things working against it.

Small dev team, slow progress and set backs, empty mmo world with no playerbase, confusing interfase, confusing HUD, poor sound quality, super simplistic and unfun space combat, and no shred of anything ressembling what is in the "features" list.

For instance.

"Twitch combat"
No, there is no twitch combat.
The space combat is slow and plodding, it is literally just lining up your mouse with an enemy and shooting till it dies.
The enemies have slow and wide movement paths, and your own ships movement feels extremely unrealistic.
There is no sense of feel of thrust, or speed in anything you do.
You fly up to an enemy as fast as you can, and stand still as you shoot at it, hoping it doesnt fly out of range making you need to chase it down once again only to stop and keep shooting.


"Why early access?"
According to the post the dev claims this game needs to be tested with a REAL population.
When I bought this game on march, there is no one on it, and there really hasn't been many people on it since.
The dev will NEVER be able to test ANY real numbers on this game going as it is.


"How long till the game releaes?"
The dev claims this game was scheduled to release by christmas last year, but said because of setbacks it would take longer.
According to the post "I hope these take no more than 6 months"
Well assuming that psot was made in december it's still been 8 months since then and there has been no update on the release date.


"What is the current state of the game?"
According to the dev, all of the "core features" are done in this game, but no actual comprehensive list of what features the "core" ones are is given.
According to the features list on this page, this game has piracy, mining, bounty hunting, over 800 ships with millions of combinations of gear, and loads of quests to improve your reputation with the many planets and races.
I can't say for a fact I was in the game long enough to notice if there were really 800 ships and millions of different gear combinations.
But what I did see was about 5 available ships to purchase, and something like 20-30 different pieces of equipment.
Either way the description of what is actualy in the game is vague.


"How are you planning to involve the community in the development of this game?"
The quote given "“I hope future development will be community driven.”"
This is not an answer.
Like everything else it's vague or outdated.
A real answer would be "I'll be taking suggestions/polls from the community to determine possible future additions to the game".



The developer seems inexperienced, undermanner, and possibly underqualified to see the development of this game through properly.

If you have a serious interest in indie titles or space sim games, this game is unfinished and has no active player base.
I myself got a refund for this title and till it turns around I will not be purchasing it.
There are a lot of things to be said for not judging a book by it's cover and giving things more of a chance, so it'll be easy to write my review off because of my short time playing the game, but many of the top rated positive reviews of this game have less than an hour or two played in game as well.
I would suggest waiting till this game releases and see what comes of it.


My suggestion to the development team of this game would be to advertise a free weekend of some sort for this title, inviting a lot of new players into the game.
This would properly test the integrity of the servers with lots of players, and might possibly get more players to buy the game after testing it.
I do hope the games goals come to pass, but right now as it is, I cannot recommend this title.

Early Access Review
Posted: 25 October, 2014
I hate doing this kind of thing because I have been both a game developer and reviewer, so I know how hard it is to do an amazing game. The idea is sound enough, and the trailer is tempting, but the execution is lacking soul and substance.

So what works? You can dogfight both AI and human controlled fighters. The AI fighters are a lot easier, but still rewarding. You also have to discover the universe, it's not justg handed to you on a plate. This encourages exploration. The controls are ok.

What doesn't work? The sound is weak (which is easily fixed guys!). The waypoint system is a nightmare. Scratch that, the nav system is a nightmare. It needs simplifying. If a civilisation can create a spacefaring culture, then the navigation system would be paramount, and easy to use. You can fly a space station. Sounds cool right? Wrong. Space stations are for deploying and making money from while you are away doing someting else, not blasting level 1 pirates!

This game is not as terrible as some reviews would suggest, and even though there seem to be periodic updates, there is no info on the steam channel about what those updates are doing.

Overall this is playable, and a definite distraction, but the price is too high for what you get and there are stronger games out there for about the same cost that bring far more to the table.

Early Access Review
Posted: 17 October, 2014
SHORT VERSION:
Huge universe, twitch flight/combat, fly fighter ships, merchant ships and capital ships.....yes, maybe it's not as pretty as some of the new AAA games but awesome gameplay.

LONGER VERSION:
I've played Fairspace for awhile now (since way before it came on steam) and love it each time I log in. I've seen many improvements/updates made to it, and each time the dev has listened to the players, made changes they wanted or needed. The community is a big deal in this game, and luckily it's a good community.
The combat/flight side of the game is smooth and well executed and it's a breath of fresh air to play a space mmo that gives you direct control of your ship.
Trade and exploration is a big aspect of the game. Your map starts out fairly empty and planets, stars, nebula etc only appear as you find them. So if you want to make the money by finding the really good trade routes you will have to head out into the unknown.

The game could benefit from some updated sounds and graphics, but even as it stands, it is a solidly playable game.
