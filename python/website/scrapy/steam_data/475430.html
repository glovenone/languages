About This Game

Prepare yourself for a first person shooter with full motion video cutscenes ! In this game, you will play as a random French Canadian who need to infiltrate a North Korean base with his friend Jesus. Will this dude succeed his quest ?
Find it out in : Kimulator : Fight for your destiny

Warning 1 : This game contains some Dark humor , if you are easily offended , please take that in consideration

Warning 2 : Take in consideration that this game have lots of bugs , and by buying it , you accept to be a beta tester , Thanks :)

The game will offer :

-A free copy of DREAMBLASTER!

-A campaign mode where you will play as a French Canadian and his friend Jesus !

-A 3D platformer where you play as a rabbit !

-Full motion videos

-fast action first person gameplay !

-Full of bonus levels !

-Full of easter eggs !

-Real english actors and voice actors !

-And more !

Well... this is what I call a REAL Kim jong un simulator !

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: Intel core i3 , or similar
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Intel HD graphics , or similar
    • Storage: 5 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX Compatible Sound Card with latest drivers
    • Additional Notes: If you run with this kind of configuration expected some lag , and lots of crashes
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows 7,8,10
    • Processor: Intel Quad-Core (i5 2300) or AMD Octo-Core (FX 8150)
    • Memory: 8 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce 660Ti or greater, AMD Radeon HD 7950 or greater, 2GB Video Card (Minimum Shader Model 2.0)
    • Storage: 5 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX Compatible Sound Card with latest drivers
    • Additional Notes: If you run with a better configuration , the graphics will improve a lot's.

Early Access Review
Posted: 21 June
sigh

So, if you couldn't already tell, Kimulator (formerly Kim Jong Un Simulator) is pretty much a nooby college project game made in GameGuru, a game creation software, using stock assets. It's horrible. We all know that, it's not hard to figure out. Don't buy, 0/10, buy GameGuru instead and play the default map.

Okay, so...

I've constantly been criticized for making comments about the developer and ranting about the "how did this game get on Steam" effect instead of making a review of the actual game, however, there are some games that really need it. This is one of those games.

I used to not give any thought to the terribleness and cancer of Steam after campaigning against it for a while. I just came to accept that we would get the constant crap that comes with weak quality control. This is both good and evil; there are terrible games that are fun and good jokes (see also: Bad Rats, because we all know how amazing THAT phenomenon was), and there are the really bad ones, the cash grabs, that just slip by. This has spawned many memetic reviews on my part, and, to be honest, I just really enjoyed playing these games. It was a fun thing for me to sort my library from the lowest-rated to highest-rated and play the first item on the list.

Now, I've shifted from just a player of games to someone who actually works with a game (and is going to be developing one themself). My viewpoint has really changed and I stand by my anti-crap stance yet again. I see the love developers have for the games that they put effort into. I see the true art that comes out. And, y'know, right now, I feel this insults indie game development as an art.

It's totally unfair to developers that that put time and love and personal sacrifices into their games have to sit next to this 5-minute, 5-dollar, 5-ounce puddle of wank. I genuinely feel really sorry for them. I feel really sorry for the games on Steam that deserve the rewards for having to be a part of a platform where their exposure is taken up by memes and ludicrousness. Saturating Steam with these games wastes resources, time, and pushes the good games that actually matter and need the exposure out of the storefront.

Games like this need to go back to Greenlight and get rejected. Games like this should never be allowed Greenlight at all. I just hope that when I finish my own game, someday, that Valve will have thought some more about the process that goes into getting games on Steam, and, as a result, Steam will have evolved and raised the bar higher than this, giving the attention back to the good games and turning away the developers that produce things like this game.
