About This Game

Build flourishing cities and the largest monuments ever seen.

Enjoy an original card game with absorbing gameplay which whisks you back several centuries to the times when people built huge, unbelievable monuments. Work your way up from a small tribe to a flourishing Empire along the Nile. Build the large pyramids which tower above vast cities, win the Gods' favour and become the mightiest Pharaoh. The Gods are known to favor the mightiest one. Can you architect a new, unstoppable Empire of the Gods?

  • Enjoy original and simple card game.
  • Build 12 beautiful cities and monuments.
  • Improve your character.
  • Use dozens of bonus and extra cards.
  • Reach up to 15 achievements.

Boring! The concept is interesting, but doesn't have a lot of depth. Your only stategy is to beat the 'time' counter.
I loved the developer's two 'Hero of the Kingdom' games, so I bought this one for sale at a buck. Then I made most of that dollar back through luck with dropped cards. I'm still not sure the few cents invested were worth it.

- You're going to hover over the cards a lot (mostly for the bonus/extra cards). Well, the UI makes tooltips show up with a slow fade-in effect that makes you waste a lot of time. Easily a second per card. Grand!

- More time wasters: the wall transition (so slow and ugly!), and the upgrade screen that still shows up even after you've upgraded everything. (Okay, most people will never upgrade everything, so it's not really an issue but...)

- User interface in general is *bad*. Nothing is done to help the player. All you'll be doing in this game is read through numbers, and it doesn't want to make it easy or fun.

- Story is non-existent, and totally unrelated to the actual gameplay screens. It's clichÃ© and probably even insulting to Egyptians. And the story screens, while few in numbers, certainly don't lack in typos. There's only one character, and it's you. But you have nothing to say. You're just a fantastic, extraordinary god-like/snorefest of a person who's also so incredibly kind that they'll help others in exchange for a huge pyramid. Yeah, sure, that's totally someone I can recognize myself in.

So... Yeah, just play the other games by this developer, they're quite good, just not this one. If you want a good card/strategy game, I'm sure you can find it yourself, I'm not your mummy. (Ah. Ah. Egypt. Sorry.)

A Resource-Management Strategy / Puzzle Game
https://youtu.be/g9IWszElhzU
This game is somewhat odd, I'll explain how the game works.
You have 4 resources: Life, Wealth, Power, and Faith
You start out with a few or so of each resource.
The "Goal" is to get a lot of one or maybe 2 resources. (So let's say we need 10 Wealth to win)
You can trade any reosurce for 2 of another resource, but you can only do this a limited amount of times.
So say you trade 1 life for 2 wealth, 2 power for 4 wealth, and 2 faith for 4 wealth = 10 wealth you win
(watch the video above to get a better idea)

+ There is a small bit of strategy, as you need to keep track of how many "transfer cards" you have, once they run out it can be hard to complete the objective if you transferred too much to the wrong resource pool.

- Does a poor job at explaining what you are doing, and it's pretty odd to say the least. You are supposevely "building" statues and such, but all you are really doing is transferring resource pools to and from each other.
- The portraits in the character selection screen look pretty silly, like photoshopped faces
- The gameplay is far too simple
- The upgrade screen is a bit out of place for a game like this...
- Overall I just thought the game was a bit confusing, like a concept that just didn't work out in the end.
