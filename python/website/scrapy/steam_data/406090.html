Commander Trevor Allen is lost on a strange moon that formed in a mysterious way, surrounded by hostile creatures, trying to unveil the secrets of ancient technology. He fights to stay alive, and one day return home.

Early Access Review
Posted: 3 March
Lost Moon is a platformer, metroidvania-ish style game which is developed and publshed by Eplixe. After I played it for 10 minutes, I am very, very dissapointed on how is this game were made. Yes I know it's still an early access game. This is the reason why I wrote this review so they can find the downsides of the game and improve it. I don't really like to give bad comment but this is for the best to the developer. Anyway, lets get started.

Let me start with the pros. There are few pros of this game and one of them are the puzzles are quite challenging but it can be frustrating too. The first puzzle of the game took me awhile but hey, challenging puzzles are the fun part in every puzzle game. Other than that, the soundtracks are okay. Nothing special and nothing catchy. Plus, simple interface and simple graphics. There is a map in the gameplay (almost identical to Super Metroid) to show where you at. The theme 'metroidvania' is the reason why I'm attracted of this game since I'm a fan of Super Metroid for the SNES.

Now, the downsides of the game. There are many things that I would like comment. First thing first, in this modern era of gaming industries, a game MUST HAVE a tutorial. Probably before the event of the game took place. Idk, it's mainly up to them. Not everyone can understand to play a game that easily and there are probably newcomers played Lost Moon as their first game and they don't really know what to do. I know at the beginning of the game, there are instruction on which key to press to do specific action but it is not enough. Moreover, there is no background story. You start the game and finding yourself outside with a wrecked spaceship. That's it. Nothing else. As a gamer, I would really want to understand on how the event started and what is his mission really is. Other than that, the control is a total **** and the timing is crucial if you want to jump from one place to another and this is where the frustration began.

Speaking of gameplay, there is one feature of the game that I don't really agree the most and hopefully the developer remove it for better gaming experience. The oxygen bar. What is it? Let me explain. The oxygen bar is of course, a bar to show you on how much oxygen is left in the tank. When the oxygen is depleted from the bar, you will die and revive at last place you saved. Wow. I just don't understand why they implement it. It's like you're play the game in 'Time Attack' mode which you really need to solve the puzzle as fast as you can before the oxygen bar reach the end. This is not acceptable. I would recommend the developer to make seperate modes. The first mode would be the classic mode (without the oxygen bar) and the other one would be the hardcore mode (with oxygen bar).

Finally, the moment that you're waiting for? Do I recommend this game? For now, it's a no-no from me. With a price tag of RM19 (promotional price...uhhh what?), it is quite expensive to be honest even though I get this game for free but since it's an 'early access' game, there is always a second chance. Hopefully the developer make major changes to the game in the near future. I'll be back on May 2016 to re-review this game once more (since they have mentioned the final version would be on May 2016). Thank you for your time to read this review and have a nice day.

I would like to take this opportunity to give thanks to OrlyGift for this game which I can play, analyse and make a review of it :)

EDIT #1: I forgot to mention about Abode Air. I have no problem with the usage of Abode Air for the game as long the game is playable.

Early Access Review
Posted: 6 January
The game has potential, but aren't there so many EA games that start off with that very sentence?

While EA games may have bugs, I feel when you advertise yourself as a platform metroidvania, you have to at the very least before making it available to the public, make sure your controls are tight...and functioning well.

Currently for keyboard, the controls are the arrow keys for movement, I can't remember the last game I've actually used them as movement. But thats ok, cause they list full controller support? Nope. I have a 360 xbox controller, and they use the analog stick for controlling movement, but they seem to go unresponsive at times. Especially when you run and jump, at times you find yourself holding to the left/right then as you jump your character stops going left/right and instead simply jumps straight up in the air, and to regain the movement, you have to recenter your stick and then push in your direction again. Controls are basics in an platform/metroidvania game.

Also, the small part I did see, I'm not overly fond of being on a timer, which basically you are constantly on one, as your oxygen depletes, so you have to go back to your ship before you die. One of the things I like about platformers and metroidvania style games are taking my time and exploring...that seems like going forward won't be the case for this game.

Anyways, once the controls work properly I'll revist it and modify my review accordingly.

I was supplied with this key by the developer, this however did not affect the review. All my developer sponsored reviews are positive because I review games that I like!
═══════════════════════════════════
OVERVIEW

Lost Moon is one of those simple 2D puzzle platformers that you will want to do in one run because you can't save the game and is pretty short but still very challenging and addicting. You play as a lost astronaut who caught himself on a planet infested with monsters and needs to guide himself back to his spaceship while maintaining oxygen and avoiding monsters. When you enter the game you get no explanation on what to do and where to go, puzzles are challenging and require you to think outside the box. Another thing I enjoyed about the game is its graphics and animation while the soundtrack isn't that bad at all. One thing that I didn't like was downloading external software or almost non-existent options menu. Overall, even if there are more cons than pros the game is still in early access and almost every single con is about the product being unfinished.

http://store.steampowered.com/app/406090/

═══════════════════════════════════
PROS & CONS

Pros:
  • Hard Puzzles
  • Lovely Soundtrack
  • Fancy Art Style

Cons:
  • Requires you to install Abode Air
  • You can get stuck and since the game has no restart option you will need to wait for slow death
  • Minor Bugs
  • Puzzles are not explained at all
  • You can't exit to main menu from game
  • No mouse support in main menu
  • Doesn't remember options
  • You can't save
═══════════════════════════════════
SUMMARY & FINAL RATING

This game is for everyone that loves hard puzzles and challenging enemies while enjoying lovely soundtrack and amazing art style. I rate the game 7/10!

Was looking forward to this one, but sadly the starting experience just ruined any interest I had.

Firstly, the game requires extra software, something that should be included within the game files much like other games do that require certain versions of DirectX or Visual runtime. Instead I had to go to the forum to try and find people with similar issues, find the correct download link, and then download and install.

Secondly, the brief time I had with the game was frustrating as hell. Not in a good way either. Other "Metroidvania" games can be brutally challenging but fun at the same time. This just felt clumsy, unresponsive and frustrating. Not to mention being re-sent back to the start because you are on a timer (oxygen?) while you are trying to solve a puzzle.

It's early access and things may change, but in a vast ocean of indie games which are easily available, this one had nothing in it to warrant a second chance.
