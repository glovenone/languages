About This Game

The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind® Game of the Year Edition includes Morrowind plus all of the content from the Bloodmoon and Tribunal expansions. The original Mod Construction Set is not included in this package.
An epic, open-ended single-player RPG, Morrowind allows you to create and play any kind of character imaginable. You can choose to follow the main storyline and find the source of the evil blight that plagues the land, or set off on your own to explore strange locations and develop your character based on their actions throughout the game. Featuring stunning 3D graphics, open-ended gameplay, and an incredible level of detail and interactivity, Morrowind offers a gameplay experience like no other.
In Tribunal, you journey to the capital city of Morrowind, called Mournhold, to meet the other two god-kings of Morrowind, Almalexia and Sotha Sil. Your journey will lead you to the Clockwork City of Sotha Sil and massive, epic-sized dungeons, where strange and deadly creatures await you, including goblins, lich lords, and the mysterious Fabricants.
Bloodmoon takes you to the frozen Island of Solstheim where you'll experience snow, blizzards, and new creatures, including frost trolls, ice minions, and wolves... just to name a few. You'll have a choice of stories to follow and have the opportunity to defend the colony, take control over how the colony is built up, and eliminate the werewolves. Or, you can decide to join the werewolves and become one of them, opening up a whole new style of gameplay.
Key features:
  • Players can take their existing Morrowind characters and save games and continue their adventures in the Morrowind GotY edition
  • Adds up to 80 hours of new gameplay and quests for current Morrowind players
  • Explore the forests, caves, and snow-covered wastelands of the island of Solstheim
  • Delve into new, epic-sized dungeons and visit the Capital City of Mournhold and the Clockwork City of Sotha Sil
  • Fight new creatures including bears and wolves, lich lords and goblins, ice minions and spriggans
  • Direct the construction of a mining colony and face the threat of savage werewolves
  • Become a werewolf and indulge your thirst for the hunt
  • New armor and weapons including Nordic Mail and Ice blades

Pretty much the best Elder Scrolls game, objectively speaking atleast. While it isn't as "fun" or accessible as Oblivion and Skyrim, or as large and free form as Daggerfall, it easily makes up for it with storytelling potential and roleplay. Actual roleplay. This is the first time in a TES game where I properly roleplayed and felt like the game was allowing me to do so. If you can get invested into playing like your character would, and use a bit of imagination, it creates very entertaining stories that are completely player driven, with little interference from the game.

The roleplay is some of the best out there, but the deal breaker for most is that Morrowind is quite infamous for having "bad" gameplay. This is because everything in Morrowind is based off of dice rolls and skill checks, meaning that the skill of the player is irrelevant, its the skill of the character. While in Skyrim, for example, my Warrior class can just about sneak past a few enemies if the situation demands it, no such thing will happen in Morrowind (Not to say you should pick stealth as a specialty on your first playthrough, its brutally difficult if your new to the game). Classes are specialised, you really have to stick to your role or else the game will punish you. This could be seen as a negative, and many do see it as a negative, hence the infamousy, but for me it makes the game very replayable. I'll often see an oppurtunity that looks interesting, but I can't take it because my class isn't skilled enough at something, it makes me excited to try a new playthrough with a different class.

So before you pick a Theif class then try (and miserably fail) to kill your first bandit with a battleaxe, then think the game is ♥♥♥♥, just remember the above. The combat is fine, no matter how many tell you otherwise. Its not supposed to be based on player skill.

One thing thats interesting to note about Morrowind is its secrets, which I refuse to spoil. They are both a good and bad thing. Good in the sense that proper exploration is rewarded, bad in the sense that you can accidentaly get one of the most powerful weapons in the game at level one, just by going to a certain cave and then talking to a certain person. Also a certain scamp that just so happens to make you rich very early on, provided you can find him. Also the best cuirass in the game at the end of a fairly easy, but well hidden, dungeon. I found all of these things by complete accident on my first playthrough, and by level 9 nothing was a challenge. Turning the difficulty up only made things more tedious rather than difficult. So if your playing this for the first time, no matter how tempting it is, try not to use some of the more overpowered secrets (like Creeper or Dram Bero), you will enjoy the game more.

Other than that, I can't think of any major flaws with Morrowind, with the exception of the inaccessibility compared to other TES games, and Fargoth. If you go into it with the mindset of DnD, rather than Skyrim -2, you'll be fine. I can't guarantee that you will enjoy it due to a focus on roleplay rather than gameplay, but its worth a try if you have any interest in the series. I would also recommend that you try the other TES games first (feel free to skip Arena), because if you can get into Morrowind, you might find the other games boring by comparison.

With some graphic mods Morrowind has aged quite well.

Anyway. I treasure Morrowind as a dear memory of gaming (the fact aside it has been my first contact with TES) because of many reasons, but just to mention a few:
You can equip ...
- rings & an amulet
- shoes
- leg armor
- torso armor
- a robe
- right pauldron
- left pauldron
- right glove
- left glove
- belt
- shirt
- trousers
- helmet
... simultaneously as seperate items. (Kind of missed that in TESIV and V, especially the additional clothing for enchantments.)
Oh, and everything mentioned can be enchanted, too, in addition to weapons (daggers, longswords, katanas, shortswords, small axes, big axes, halberds, spears, bows, crossbows, maces, (small) throwing weapons and some others I just forget).
And nearly everything can be picked up and so many containers that actually contain things. Like pillows. Many pillows.
An environment ranging from lush woods and swamps to rocky paths alongside mountains, mushroom plains, ashen plains, collectives of islands and of course the snowy and icy Solstheim.
No quicktravel but a nice infrastructure of other transportations (boat, magic guild, silt strider, enchanted amulets/return spells).

For some starting equipment follow the road to the left (I think it leads to the north) from the starting settlement.
Alchemy can be used to brew some useless but high-grade potions with stolen equipment ... if you want.
