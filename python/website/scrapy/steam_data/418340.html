HOTFIX #1

Hey RWBY Hunters! Hope you're enjoying all the new content?

We've been watching the forums, trying to log all the bugs as they are reported. Thanks to everyone who posted bug reports. Some are known issues, others are mysteries we're looking into, and others may be related to specific hardware specs. We're adding them all to our task list and will get them resolved as soon as we can!

RANK:
We've seen several bug reports about advancing your RANK. Seems to work fine for some, but not for others. So we've been digging into the issue and think we have a hotfix ready for you to try.

There's a new "Opt-In" beta available for those who can help us test it out. Just follow these instructions and you'll get the new HOTFIX #1.

1) Exit and Restart Steam
2) Right click RWBY:GE, select Properties.
3) Under the Betas tab, select "hotfix" from the dropdown menu.
4) The update should start downloading. Give it a test and let us know if you can RANK up now!

FYI - You will see a watermark on the lower-center of your screen that says HOTFIX. This will remind you that you're playing the hotfix version. Once we verify all is working properly with Ranks, we'll push these changes into the main branch of the game.

There seems to be some confusion on how you advance from Rank 0 to Rank 1. You MUST level up all 4 characters to Level 10 before you can advance in Rank. There are more challenges for ranking to higher levels. We'll write up a Ranking Guide and post that in the discussion forums.

Again, we're still working on the other bug reports, but we wanted to get the Ranking issue fixed ASAP!

Hope it all goes well. Good luck on the battlefields of Remnant!

Cheers,

About This Game

RWBY: GRIMM ECLIPSE is a 4 player, online co-op, hack and slash game based upon Rooster Teeth’s international hit series RWBY.

Get ready for intense combat action as you battle Grimm across familiar locations of Remnant including new areas never before seen in the show. Play as Ruby, Weiss, Blake, and Yang in this character-driven adventure that explores new storylines, new Grimm types, and a new villain!

The fast paced, hack and slash gameplay takes inspiration from games such as Dynasty Warriors, combined with team play elements from Left 4 Dead, to create over-the-top, co-op combat along with engaging missions and storytelling.

FEATURES:
  • 4 Player Online Co-op (Multiplayer)
  • Play as Team RWBY - Ruby, Weiss, Blake, or Yang, each with their own unlockable abilities and upgrades
  • Experience an exclusive storyline with new locations, enemies, and a new villain never before seen in the show
  • Full voiceover from the cast of the show, plus new voice talent
  • Ranked challenges, unlocks, and achievements

I am a huge fan of the RWBY series and have kept up with the show for a while. So when I found out that it was getting it's own game,I flipped. And the result? I love this game!

Now I bought this game when it was still in early access for $15. That being said,$25 may be a bit much. I'd suggest getting it on sale.

Now let me list the cons to get them out of the way first.

#1. After you finish the campaign,there's basically nothing. Sure you could try a different character,maybe play online,but you're still getting the same story. A story that after the first few times,gets repetitive and makes you hope for DLC. And no one,hopes for DLC.

#2. Blake is a bit overpowered. Her moves are really strong and I'd suggest nerfing her a bit.

Other than those two,I don't have many problems with the game.

Now onto the pro's.

#1. The character designs look great! They are spot on with the show!

#2. The music is great,taking some classic tracks from the show and timing them right with key events in the game!

#3. The grimm varieties are amazing! I criticized this game before because all the grimm looked very similar to each other,but now that it's out and they've added more monsters,they look great! Especially the mutant grimm,they look awesome!

#4. The team attacks work very well! If an enemy has the RB symbol on them you can do a sort of special attack. But if you're playing online,multiple people can press it at the same time to make a team attack! It works super well since it's on the fly with the click of a button,which is great for the fast paced combat of this game!

#5. The scenery looks gorgeous,the particle effects and things like that make the game look great! And even if you don't have a super PC,the game still looks good.

#6. The attack animations look great! Especially with Ruby and Blake,the light effects when they do special moves look awesome!

And now,what I think they should add to make this game better.

As much as I know people hate DLC,if you do DLC right,you can really make your game great! Like in SSB4 with all the new characters like Ryu and returning characters like Mewtwo! So what do I think this game should do? I think they should sell sort of DLC packs! Like a new pack for team JNPR with a new story to complete only as team JNPR! And if you complete the story,you can play as them in the online mode! And maybe another pack with the enemy team with Cinder,Mercury,and that one girl that nobody cares about. It would be a completely different story with some very awesome things to cater to fans of the show! How much would these cost? I would think that a pack with a team and a new story mode should cost around $10-$15. But that's just me,if they do make this it might cost more or less.

Anyways,I love RWBY Grimm Eclipse. The game is fun and hopefully with some DLC they can pad out the game length.

Genre - Anime themed cartoon beat 'em up

Okay so I have watched all episodes of RWBY and I bought this whilst it was in Early Access at a discount price. However, I waited for the full release before playing it. This was because the content shown and promised sounded quite limited, so I wanted to enjoy it as a finished product. I think this was a wise decision.

So I have made one complete playthrough as Blake (clearly the sexiest character), a few random multiplayer levels with Weiss and a bit of Ruby experimentation. No Yang yet though, as she is a bit scary...

You will fight waves of enemies (The Grim) and they are quite varied to be fair, something I was worried about in Early Access. Later on there are a few robotic enemies to spice things up and of course a big bad, that doesn't dissapoint. I played through with one regular co-op partner and a few leavers for about 2 1/2 hours. This was in one sitting but you can break up the chapters, By the end I had hit the level 10 cap with one of the characters. So even if you limit yourself to one playthrough with each character that is 10 hours worth of fun to be had. You do also have challenges that will unlock seperate perks, such as killing so many enemies or reviving chracters, which you can work towards even after hitting the level cap. I did try solo mode once and it wasn't too bad as the difficulty scales down, but it is a lot more fun with others. Especially if you get a full team of unique characters.

If you want more you can trade in all four hard earned 10 levels for 1 rank. This is where I think the game dissapoints. As a rank gain is just cosmetic and doesn't incentivise you in anyway. As someone commented on the discussions it is clearly added as artifical replayability, which is a shame. If each rank rewarded you with perks, outfits or taunts then it would seem worthwile. Something very similar was done in the Dawn of War 2 last stand mode but at least you got a score booster as well as bragging rights.

The story isn't a masterpiece, but it fits well in the Rwby universe and you get to hear a few of the popular characters and a new villain via your phone along the way. This helps immerse you and you will be singing the them tune (badly) whilst pwning the Grim.

As a fan of the series I really enjoyed this game and will definately try and level all the characters up to 10. I am just hoping that more features will be added even if it is in the form of fairly priced DLC. New characters from the show and more missions. Also an arena mode against other teams would be a great addition, but that is probably a seperate game alltogether...

I don't normally write steam reviews, but as a massive fan of the show I feel like I owe it to the RWBY community to warn people like myself who blindly bought this because of its relation to RWBY.

The game itself has changed very little since early access, a new UI and a couple new levels is all that has actually changed between alpha and release. The main complaint being the extremely shallow combat system. The few combos that that game has are mostly uninteresting or plain useless, leaving you to just spam one or two buttons the entire game.

My second major complaint the lack of dialog from pretty much any of the characters. This game was produced by RT, they had the voice actors, so why didn't they use them!? The result of having nearly no character dialog besides the occasional out of place "Yeah!" from Ruby is that the game just ends up feeling rushed and unfinished as you walk long stretches with nothing happening.

The iceing on the cake though is how they tried to pad out the game. As boring and uninspired as the game is, maybe it wouldnt be so bad if it wasn't so heavily padded out. RWBY: Grimm Eclipse is fairly lackluster and doesn't offer much in gameplay other than a service to the brand.
The game is a multiplayer beat 'em up takes place in the webseries world. The four heronies, Ruby, Weiss, Blake and Yang are playable characters that share the same voices as the show's counter parts. You can team up with three friends or play alone and beat up monsters. I can say for sure, this game defintely had multiplayer in mind for two reasons: the maps are large and the waves of enemies are fustrating to fight alone. Those two reasons are some of the games flaws.

The game's developers did a pretty good job depicting the world of RWBY and fans of the show will feel like they are in Remnant, where the series takes place. However, it is fairly empty and lifeless at times. The maps are fairly nice to look at when you are traveling through them but there isn't much in the way of exploration. Other than looking for artifacts that rewards you with experience points, there isn't much incentive to go out and look for them. The game keeps you lock on a set path for the most of the game and just removes any sense of adventure because you are on a set path.

Enemies don't appear on the map as often as they should and only appears mostly at the start and end of the stage with a few random encouters in between. There isn't much variation in the types of enemies and gets pretty dull using the same strategy to beat them over and over again. You can almost always count on needing to clear out a wave of grimm before finishing the stage, its a cheap way of putting excitement in the game and gets boring very fast.

In the show, each of the heroines are unique in their own way and it did meet that expectation. Each of the characters fuctions fairly differently, for example, Yang is a close up one on one fighter while her sister, Ruby, is armed with a scythe is able to do large sweeping attacks that hit multiple enemies. However, when you are leveling up your characters and buying new skills, they all share the same power ups. They all get a damage buff and get an area of effect attack to prevent them being surrounded. I was hoping for some more variety with their power ups.

Another thing that was missing from the game is the show's amazing rock soundtrack. While there is a decent rock soundtrack playing while fighting the horde of grimm, it just doesn't match up with what they have in the show. I was hoping that I could count on the music to bring some life to the game...but sadly that isn't the case.

Overall, ifs a pretty underwhelming game and fans of RWBY like myself thinks the game could use a lot more polish and content. And with the final price set at $24.99, I suggest waiting buying the game during a sale if you are really a die hard RWBY fan. If you want a beat 'em up game based on a popular show, I suggest Legend of Korra. Its 10 dollars cheaper and played much better than RWBY.
