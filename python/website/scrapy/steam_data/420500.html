8 June

Version 1.45 - Fixes and other updates



Greetings friends of Rynn! In the course of going through the Lotcheck process for our Wii U release, we learned of a few rare but not not impossible scenarios which could cause the game to become unresponsive or crash. We're now feeding these fixes back into the Steam version so that you can have the best possible experience playing Rynn's Adventure.

Please note that if you are interested in getting Rynn's Adventure for your Wii U, it is now available on the North American eShop and you can take a look at the game page here: Rynn's Adventure for Wii U

The changes in this release are:

  • Fixed: Race condition with in-stage menu that caused the game play to freeze under certain circumstances.
  • Fixed: Rare game crash when player loses to a boss and the stage being played is destroyed before the boss.
  • Fixed: Out-of-bounds access in the player profile that resulted in a crash under certain conditions.
  • Fixed: Ensure Rynn is not affected through the ground by a certain water geyser in "Tranquil Falls Under Siege".
If you haven't been following us since launch, please note that there were a substantial number of gameplay improvements, fixes and the much requested "Checkpoint" UI added in the previous patch, version 1.38. Please see the Version 1.38 Patch Notes for more details.

8 April

Version 1.38 - Checkpoints and other updates

Greetings friends of Rynn! Due to popular request we've made it visually apparent when Rynn reaches a checkpoint while playing a stage. We've also taken the opportunity to address a few general bugs as well. Thanks to everyone who provided feedback and reported issues to us!

- Added: Display "Checkpoint" text when a checkpoint is reached during gameplay.
- Fixed: Ensure all checkpoints are safe for Rynn to respawn (or at least give player time to react). This includes the pre-boss checkpoints at the end of "Enchanted Forest Burns" and "Terror In The Heart of the Forest".
- Fixed: Ensure in-stage music is restored when a stage is started.
- Fixed: Ensure player cannot jump into the abyss at the end of "Terror In The Heart of the Forest".
- Fixed: Ensure movement impairments are cleared when Rynn is respawned.
- Fixed: Ensure physics forces can't impact Rynn's position during respawn.
- Fixed: Adjust some falling leaves in "Enchanted Forest Burns" so players can make the jump more consistently.
- Fixed: Ensure that the two big falling logs in "Enchanted Forest Burns" respawn in case the player dies before reaching the next checkpoint.
- Fixed: Fix some inconsistent lighting in "Terror In The Heart of the Forest" on some older GPUs.
- Fixed: Ensure player can't get stuck diving in between cloud wheel platforms in "Head In The Enshrouded Skyway".

About This Game

Rynn’s Adventure is a 2D platformer game in which the player guides Rynn through a variety of environments teeming with magic and danger. The player will help Rynn combat the many enemies she encounters and outwit each of their fearsome generals. Rynn will eventually face off against the evil wizard himself in order to free her parents and ultimately save the Enchanted Forest and its inhabitants.

Choose which stage to play in any order. Use Rynn’s impressive acrobatic maneuvers to overcome obstacles and enemies alike. Confront challenging bosses waiting at the end of each stage. Unlock fabulous power-ups for each boss defeated.

This is a side scrolling adventure for all ages and audiences.

Features

* Unlock many additional abilities and costumes for Rynn to use on her journey.

* Collect as many gems and treasures as possible to increase Rynn's power.
* Defeat challenging bosses that each have their own unique weakness to be discovered.
* Overcome stages and their unique deadly obstacles in any order you choose.
* Adventure in a wide variety of environments.
* Achieve personal best stage completion times and collection percentages
* Support for full controller or keyboard play.
* Play as a totally awesome fox! ^_-

Story Summary

The Enchanted Forest and its animal denizens are under siege. The invaders are a marauding army of fierce reptiles whose unopposed ruthlessness will destroy the Forest. Meanwhile, an evil wizard has kidnapped the king and queen and has them imprisoned in his dark castle. The only hope for the forest dwellers is Rynn, a spry young fox who also happens to be their princess. Rynn’s mental and physical agility, along with power gained from magical fruit, will allow her to halt the siege and defeat the evil wizard.

THIS IS A MIXED REVIEW!

Rynn's Adventure: Trouble in the Enchanted Forest does not appeal to me, but may appeal to others who are fans of challenging platformers.

Check out my Quick Game Review here: https://youtu.be/YU4Db1lo4es
Rynn’s Adventure: Trouble in the Enchanted Forest is an unforgiving platformer, and not for someone looking for a casual game. UPDATED: Originally this game was tagged as a casual adventure, but the tag for casual has been removed, and the platformer tag has been added to more accurately describe the game.

You start each level with 3 total lives, and a full health and mana bar, which is weird, because some of the screen shots on the Steam Store show 99 lives... and having 99 lives would have been really nice... You lose health if take any damage, and when you die, you lose a chunk of your health, but if your health bar can’t go lower then it refreshes both health and mana bars, and consumes 1 life. Killing creatures will sometimes cause health or mana bubbles to drop, but it seems random, and they don’t give a whole lot anyway. When you run out of lives and health, you lose the level and have to completely restart it.

You can kill most monsters, and avoid taking damage if you spin while jumping. If you hit something while spinning, you will keep the spin bounce going. You can also dive on top of monsters, and you will bounce higher than you are normally capable of.

The monsters in the game respawn VERY quickly, and there are some annoying mobs that you can’t kill at all. In the levels you collect gems and keys, and when you beat that level’s boss you unlock a special power up outfit that has it’s own unique ability, and your health and mana pool will also increase slightly based on what items you collected.

Each level has a boss fight at the end with different boss mechanics, but the bosses are a complete joke compared to the rest of the levels. You can use this spin jump to cheese many of the boss fights by just bouncing on their heads the entire time. I would have preferred less difficulty during the levels, but more on the boss fights, so it would be more balanced.

Pros:
  • full controller support. (I highly recommend playing with a controller)
  • music and sound effects were fine throughout the game, but they get a bit repetitive
  • 2D animation is looks decent, and I didn’t notice too many graphical issues
  • you can play the first 8 levels in any order you want to before unlocking the final 9th level
  • all the levels have unique design, their own mechanics, and different boss at the end
  • when you die, you don't lose a whole life, but only a chunk of health, until your health isn't high enough then the life gets used before respawning at the last checkpoint

Cons:
  • misleading screen shots on the Steam Store page UPDATED
  • keyboard controls felt a bit clunky, and are not customizable (use a controller instead!)
  • voice narration sounds like someone reading a story book to children
  • when Rynn hangs on vines, she twitches a lot, and when she’s on moving platforms, the platforms don’t always move smoothly either
  • when you jump off walls, you can't control your movement, and it will sometimes get you killed
  • checkpoints are not marked, and are not always in a safe location - UPDATED
  • some parts in the levels are extremely frustrating to try and get through, while the bosses are easy compared to some parts of the level leading up to it

There isn’t much to say about the story, since it is very straightforward, and you’re trying to save your parents and the Enchanted Forest from the wizard and his minions.

The whole game took me 9 hours, but it took a few hours to beat the first level. Once I finally did, and I got the first power up, the game got easier, until the final level at least. I wanted to rage quit many times, so I have absolutely no desire to replay the game, but there are achievements for collecting everything if you’re into that sort of thing.

What drew me to the game were the 'casual' and 'adventure' labels, which were very misleading. I had expected more of a story driven platformer with some challenges, but not anything too difficult. This game is NOT casual whatsoever. I know I’m not amazing at platformers by any means, but I’m not that horrible at them either. I found the game very stressful and frustrating. This is more of a game for people who really enjoy platformers, and are very good at that type of gameplay. This game is absolutely NOT for someone looking for a casual adventure.

* I was provided with a review copy*


The introduction to the game is much like a fairy tale which I also found enjoyable and it has some beautiful narration as well.

This game is very difficult and challenging. It borrows a few ideas from Megaman such as the level select screen and power ups that are collectable in each level.

The graphics are beautiful vibrent and colorful and the music is very enjoyable as well.

It's by no means a run carelessly platform to platform style game, This is more so a stop and think before you make your next move platformer, you want to avoid any instant deaths or drops into traps and sometimes they are unavoidable. This game requires a-lot of time and patience to make progress. Though at times the jumping and platforming is sticky leaving the character to be stuck on a platform as it drops to your doom. Some enemies are out right bullies and just beat the crap out of you the moment you get on screen and they block paths which require you to activate a special power if you happened to have found that power already otherwise that level will be incredibly rough on you.

Do I recommend it? Yes if you like really difficult platformers with beautiful visuals. It was a rage induced experiance but I did find it enjoyable as well.
