The year is 2121 as part of the crew on spaceship Endera your team is tasked with traveling the galaxy seeking out blackholes and closing them down. Coming across the last black hole in the galaxy that is close enough to be a risk to Earth, it explodes drawing the Endera and crew towards invititable death. Somehow by luck the ship lands on an unknown object resembling a planet, this is the start of your story. This is not your run of the mill platformer.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x3Kad9WqdnI
On board was an advanced AI named Auriel, the first to wake up finds herself contained within a PDA and able to have free thoughts, her regulation protocols among other things being destroyed in the crash. Second member of crew to wake up is the coffee boy who you take over as the main character. Your primary objective is salvage enough repair parts to fix the ship and hopefully escape the baron landscapes of the blackhole. Salvage is collected by completion of levels found throughout the larger game world, which also contains a bunch of 'sidequests' like finding lost black box recorders.

Levels do start easier but get quite brutal and quickly, akin to Super Meat Boy. Please don't let that stop you though, most can be passed by collection of 1 salvage piece if you just want to progress the story and thankfully the game had extremely responsive controls. Both local and global timed scoreboards are included for speedrunners and the competitive sorts.

During levels Auriel will give you her thoughts on the situation and while trying to steer you in the right direction, at least that's if you trust advanced AI (we all know how portal/RE went). Without dribbling on too much more that is where the game really shines. Story, characters and voicework really draw you while the humour stays both tongue in cheek and witty. Everything about this game screams AAA quality, yet it's indie and very affordable.

Highly recommended if you enjoy platformers/puzzlers or just a heck of a good story along the lines of a Hitchhikers Guide to the Galaxy. Cheers folks.

Pros
- The art direction is fantastic. The developer knew what they wanted and ran with it.
- The music is fantastic. Just like the art the developer knew what they wanted and executed it with perfection.
- While a bit fuzzy in parts of the game, the controls are pretty tight.
- The voice acting is really good for a indie title.

Neutral
- The game is hard. Really hard. Be prepared for a ton of restarts, retries, and frustration. This will either sell the game for you or make you pass on it.

Cons
- The storytelling is really weak. Very little of what is going on is explained properly.
- Finding hidden items is required to get backstory and a decent understanding of what is going on.
- This game is bogged down with needless dialogue. The voice acting is great, however the majority of the speaking lines are not needed. (I'm looking at you Ariel....)
- The humor tries too hard to be funny.


Since Steam doesn't allow a neutral recommendation, I have to lean this recomendations to "Buy It". The artwork and music are fantastic to look at and to listen to. The story is very weak and drowned out by massive amounts of dialogue that don't move the plot forward at all. Don't expect compelling reasons for character actions or an explanation on what is going on.

The difficulty curve of the game ramps up to 11 very early on in the game and never lets up. The game isn't hard because of bad controls or bad gameplay decisions, the puzzles are legitimitly challenging and precision is the name of the game with the levels. Odds of ragequitting out of frustration are high for later levels.

Your decision to buy this game rests solely on your tolerance for brutally hard games. If you don't mind tons of restarts and very tough puzzles then pick up this game. You will get your monies worth. However if your tolerance for that style of gameplay is low, I would highly suggest passing for another puzzle platformer.
