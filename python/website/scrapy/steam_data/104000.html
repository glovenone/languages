About This Game

iBomber Defense combines classic tower defense gameplay with intense action and strategy. Keep enemies at bay all over the world using a variety of weapons and tactics, from rattling Machine guns, explosive cannons and awesome anti-aircraft artillery!
Choose from Quick play and Campaign modes and battle across North Africa, Europe and Russia as both the Allied and Axis forces. Position your defenses and prepare for attack.
iBomber Defense is the complete entertainment package with hours of action packed and deep strategic game play.

Key features:

  • Twenty-Two challenging and unique stages across a vast campaign
  • Huge variety of weapons to repel the enemy; AA Guns, Artillery, Machine Gun Nests and more!
  • Use Counter Attacks, Rewinds and the High Ground to your advantage
  • Three tiers of difficulty
  • Slick, gorgeous, graphics

Cobra Mobile is a leading independent games developer and publisher.

A "rough gem".

The game is "rough" in the sense that is definitely unpolished and feels not yet finished in many places. For example, the only sounds while playing are the sounds of your guns and the many, many explosions. The enemy doesn't make a sound when they shoot. And the only music is in the menus.

But despite it's many flaws, I found the game entertaining enough that I kept on going through the Allies Campaign (5.2hrs), and then continued the MUCH harder Axis Campaign (11hrs). And after losing a mission (on the middle difficulty) that went on for what felt like an hour, I still found myself giving it another attempt because there's just enough about the game that I like that I really wanted to finish it.

The Good:
+ A bare-bones Tower Defense game.

+ Freedom of tower placement. For the most part you are pretty free to place a tower where you like. However to get a perfect mission rating and also on the harder levels, particularly on the Axis campaign, optimal tower placement becomes more and more critical.

+ Nice variety of maps. Each campaign is roughly 10 or so maps with a couple missions then being a "replay" of the previous level you just finished, but the enemy bombed part of the map, destroying any turrets you had in that part of the map and not allowing them rebuilt there, or a new enemy route is opened up.

+ Surprise Attacks. The "surprise attacks" during the middle of missions that change the map, allowing enemy to attack on the subsequent waves from a new path.

+ Multiple paths. The enemy can take a variety of paths to your base.

The OK:
~ Long Missions. Some missions have 20 waves, others 30, some even 40, and whoah, even 50! Get comfortable, because when a single wave can last a couple minutes, you're going to be there for awhile.

~ Graphics are OK at best. Playing a game on static wallpaper of the wrong resolution for the monitor (re: black bars on sides)..

~ Both campaigns feel the same. The Axis Campaign is very similar to the Allied and some of the maps are even the same. Disappointing.

The Bad:
- Waves are too long. Enemies are strung together in a seemingly endless, perfectly spaced line with no variety or change.

- Very little variety. All enemy from whatever path, all start at the same time and are all uniformly spaces...no groups of enemies, just long lines. Very long lines. If you can fend off the first 4, 5, 10 enemy in the line, why bother sending the remaining 30 in the line; it turns into just a long string of explosions.

- Feels very unfinished. No sound for the enemy or environment. No music while playing. No story.

- Some missions, you're just going to lose. Between the surprise attacks opening up new routes allowing a flood of enemy in RIGHT NOW, or the sudden burst of higher level enemies to open the next wave....until you learn to get ready for them in advance, they're just going to run right through you, and you lose.

- No replayability. There's no variety in enemy behavior, no change in turret selection, no random factor...

Overall: I recommend the game if you go in with low expectations because, in the end, I did enjoy my time playing -- enough to sink (yikes) 15+ hrs into the 2 campaigns. 7 out of 10.

I like TD games, so I picked up this one. Turns out it wasn't the best decision, as I only made it to the 4th level, then got bored. Why?

- Very few towers, with no upgradeability in missions, so you basically end up building the same towers over and over again each map.
- Upgrades between missions are just boring and laziliy done.
- Clunky controls - hitboxes of buttons weren't where they're supposed to be. I actually had to click multiple times press an OK button, increase or lower game speed etc.
- Boring waves. I have played other TD titles that appealed to me with the first mission. But here it just feels that you play the same tactics each level, fighting off almost identically looking waves of enemies.

Overall the game feels like a first try of an indie studio at tower defense where they lacked the focus to reiterate their game design and do sufficient testing. Or just another lazy mobile port. Whichever is the case, I recommend going for other titles, as there are enough good TDs out there.

iBomber Defense, yet another IOS game that has somehow made it's way onto the PC and onto Steam with little or no development in between.

iBomber Defense is about as simple and generic as a tower defense game can get, no effort has been made towards giving the game any features or gimmicks that might make it stand out from the crowd even a little.

You place down your turrets, Machine Gun, Cannon or Anti-Aircraft and fighting off waves of enemies that try to make it to your base, occasionally you might want to repair a turret that has taken too much damage or drop down one of the equally generically named support turrets that slow enemies or increase the range and firepower of turrets in range. There is no abilities to use or interest to earn and the maps are fairly small so the entire game is purely based around optimal placement.

The truth is that there is really nothing wrong with iBomber defense, but there isn't anything right about it either. Everything about it just so bland and uninspired, it is like so many tower defense games out if they had everything but the bare essentials stripped from their bones.

With the tower defense genre growing in both complexity and polish there really shouldn't be room for a game like this, even on mobile there is better alternatives and on the PC this barely even qualifies as a browser game. It just isn't worth the time or the effort.
