About This Game

“He who fights with monsters should look to it that he himself does not become a monster... When you gaze long into the abyss, the abyss also gazes into you...”

Kidnapped. Imprisoned. Tortured. The wizard Irenicus holds you captive in his stronghold, attempting to strip you of the powers that are your birthright.

Can you resist the evil within you, forge a legend of heroic proportions, and ultimately destroy the dark essence that haunts your dreams? Or will you embrace your monstrous nature, carve a swath of destruction across the realms, and ascend to godhood as the new Lord of Murder?

Features


  • Throne of Bhaal: Travel to Tethyr in this thrilling conclusion to the Bhaalspawn saga, and delve into the treasure-filled halls of Watcher’s Keep!
  • Fist of the Fallen: Help the Sun Soul Monk Rasaad find peace in his quest for vengeance, and determine the fate of a heretical sect.
  • A Price Paid in Blood: Join the Blackguard Dorn in carving a swath of destruction across the Realms, as he struggles to appease the increasingly bloody demands of his dark patron.
  • A Voice in the Dark: Recruit the mysterious Thief Hexxat and take up her mission of retrieving long-lost artifacts from tombs as far away as Zakhara..
  • In Defense of the Wild: Protect the innocent and stand with Neera the Wild Mage against a ruthless school of Red Wizards.
  • The Black Pits II - Gladiators of Thay: Explore the tactical depth of high-level Dungeons & Dragons combat. Turn your captors against each other while you dodge traps and battle for your life against Demi-Liches, Mind Flayers, and the legendary Githyanki.
  • Easy Multiplayer: Baldur's Gate II: Enhanced Edition makes it easy to find, list, and join multiplayer games, even while behind a firewall.
  • Widescreen and More: Baldur's Gate II: Enhanced Edition features full widescreen support, remastered area artwork from original renders, and a host of other interface and gameplay improvements.
  • The 1.3 Release: The latest update to Baldur's Gate II: Enhanced Edition fixes hundreds of gameplay issues and adds dozens of new enhancements such as adjustable font sizes, improved party pathfinding, and much, much more.

Note: All translations are text only. Voice-overs are played in English.

Put simply, this is by far the best western RPG I have ever played

Baldur's Gate 2 is the sequel to, surprisingly enough, Baldur's Gate. This game fixes many of the problems the first suffered from, eliminating those obnoxious huge wilderness areas that had to be explored completely if you wanted any sidequests at all. Some companions from 1 also return in 2, those being Minsc, Jaheria, Imoen, Viconia, and Edwin. The early game of 1 was fairly tedious, not only because of your weapons breaking if you so much as looked at them funny, but because of the incredibly painful low-level D&D combat that only gives mages 2 spells and takes 2 minutes to kill a rat. The combat is difficult in this game, but very fleshed out and you have an incredible number of options when it comes to gear and how you build your party. The central city of Athkatla is absolutely huge, being the best "central city" I have seen in a game so far. Exciting things happen in each and every area of Athkatla, giving you so many objective you may feel overloaded with quests. Baldur's Gate 2 has many mechanics that were great but never seemed to stick to RPG's that followed it. When a characters joins your party because they want help, if they do not get it fast enough, they will leave. Characters have many interactions between each other in this game, and many of them are unique and only happen once, unlike Baldur's Gate 1 where each companion had 1 interaction line and said constantly.

Perhaps the most important part of the game is the quests. Baldur's Gate 2 collectively has the best sidequests I have seen in a game. Each one feels meaningful and significant, no mindless fetching. None of the quests drag either, each being finishable in about 1-2 hours, which means you can start a playing session, feel you've accomplished something every time, and go do something else after you've stopped. The quests are not hard to find, with the people who wish to give you work basically forcing themselves down your throat everywhere you walk, which means that a pleasant stroll through the city will likely result in you having a few new jobs each time and sometimes even an entire new companion.

The story is good, it is unique and compelling, if not terrifically written. This game recaps the events of Baldur's Gate 1 terrifically, with out saying "This is what happened in Baldur's Gate" it seamlessly explains the backstory throughout the course of the game, and you feel as if you were just learning about it naturally.

Perhaps the only thing this game did wrong was introduce romances to the RPG world, which resulted in Bioware taking a turn down the path of questionable dating sims with mediocre gameplay that people praise as original.

Basically, this game is great, get it

Everything changed when the Update 2.0 Nation attacked.



As for the actual review:

I'm terribly nostalgia blind for this game. It's very good on it's own merit but as a 20-something-year old that's been watching/playing this for as long as he can remember it's smothered in rose colored glasses. out of 200+ games in my library Baldur's Gate II and Skyrim are eternally armwrestling for the #1 spot. with nearly 500 hours played on EE alone I obviously love this game......so why the negative review?


Like every other BG2 veteran I loathe, LOATHE the 2.0 update. Maybe for different reasons than the others.

When Baldur's Gate EE was announced I decided to replay Baldur's Gate and the sequel as a preemptive test of stability checking for bugs, crashes, glitches, etc.

Note: this also includes the expansions
Bugs in classic BG installed from disc: 1 (High Hedge, undying skeleton)
Bugs in classic BG2 installed from disc: 5 (dialogue bugs, a few invincible monsters)

Bugs in BG EE: 20+ (mostly dialogue bugs, gamebreaking but fixed with a reload)
Bugs in BG2 EE: 20+ (same as above)

I haven't played BG EE 2.0, so I don't know about it....but oh boy....BG2 EE....

Bugs in BG2 EE 2.0: ∞........after 2.0 game stability tanked for me, reinstalling, stability mods, monkeying around in regedit, nothing has fixed it thus far.

It's not just a rainbow of bugs, it's just 1 that keeps breaking my sanity, when entering an area random NPCs randomly spawn.

When 2.0 first came out I was kind of excited because of Legacy Of Bhaal difficulty, after 2,000+ hours on classic I thought this was going to be atleast a little refreshing, and it might have been. but when you're trying to fight Firkraag and a few dozen Neebers spawn, constantly breaking the fight with stupid conversation, breaks what immersion this game has. Some areas are more prone to this glitch, Windspear Hills dungeon, Planar Sphere, De'Arnise keep just to name a few.
playing this game solo is hard enough, on LoB it's mindbreaking, then with random avalanches of Neebers it's impossible.

classic BG2 rating: 10/10, perfect game for a nostalgia blind idiot.
BG2 EE rating: 9/10, tad bit unstable, made the game look sleeker, old dogs don't like new tricks.
BG2 2.0: 5/10 It's still playable if you want achievements and the like.
Neeber Apocalypse: 0/10, by the time I got through the first 3 chapters I had enough Bullets +2 to build a continent free of Neebers
