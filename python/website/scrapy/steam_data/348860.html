Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We've got the Online System and most of the fundamental aspects of MyDream in place and we're looking to you, the players, to give us feedback to help make the online sandbox creation game you always wanted!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“MyDream will be in Early Access until we're confident we have enough features and polish to call it a top quality Sandbox experience. We're planning for this to take approximately 6 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of the game will contain many more features to improve building and exploring in worlds. The social features of the game will also be expanded to include friends lists and more benefits to playing and interacting with others. Additionally, we will be planning on adding a leveling system that will give you the ability to improve your character and unlock special abilities.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“MyDream is currently in Alpha. Creating worlds, terraforming, building, and basic quest creation are currently in the game and functioning. You should be able to enter worlds whenever you please but from time to time we will have server issues as we work out the kinks in our online system. Terrain issues and performance hits can also occur on occasion as changes are made to our voxel terrain engine.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The final price of the game has not yet been determined.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We're planning to involve the community heavily in the development process of the game. Our game is dictated by our players and we plan to listen to what our players want and do our best to deliver features that enhance their game experience. We'll be extremely active both here on Steam responding to feedback and on our forums.”

MyDreamVR Tower Defense Demo - Now Available!

Our New MyDreamVR Tower Defense Mode is Now Available

Greetings MyDream Community!

We’re happy to announce that the MyDreamVR Tower Defense Demo that was on display at GDC 2016 is now available to all MyDream players! The demo starts with the player in a castle where the courtyard is being invaded by *gasp* the Rock Creatures! Using the glove tool’s weapon and assistance of defensive Dragon Turrets, the player must fend off three waves of creatures to protect their castle. The demo is currently set up to work with Oculus Rift CV1 and DK2 using the 1.3 Runtime.

The MyDreamVR Tower Defense demo is available in the Betas tab of the Properties window. To access the demo:
1) Within your Steam Library, right click on MyDream in your list of games
2) Right-click on the title “MyDream” in the library list and select Properties
3) Within the Properites pop-up, click on the BETAS tab
4) Within the drop-down list of “Select the beta you would like to opt into:” select the vr_towerdefense - beta. No access code is required.

10 February

MyDream Version 0.9.0.0 Update Notes

Greetings Dreamwalkers!

It sure has been a while since our last update, and we’re happy to finally deliver it to you all! This update has quite a laundry list of new features, and I encourage you to read the list in full to see what all we’ve put in!

Servers are back up and running, and the new build is up on Steam. Come join the fun and check out these new features in action!


-Engine-

Updated to Unity 5.2


-Inventory-

New Global inventory. Materials are now saves across worlds, deplete upon use, and are replenished by collection and crafting

Players are no longer granted 999 of all items upon each world visit. Plan ahead and be sparing with your resources, or plan to transmute some more!


-Bug Fixes-

Player avatar animation has been restored

NPCs now depart from their camps rather than being stuck inside them

Multiple functionality and performance fixes

All trees may now be removed from the world


-Controls-

Gamepad support added

New togglable Free-Aim Mode which unlocks the crosshair from the center of the screen

Flying - Doubletap jump to engage and disengage.

New Crouching functionality to fit through 1x1 tunnels or take cover from incoming attacks

Climbing changed to Jump and Crouch to ascend and descend

IK-driven climbing - hands and feet attempt to lock in on IK targets on climbable objects

New Power Jump Upward and Forward for increased mobility at the expense of energy

Slightly rebalanced controls, reduced non-sprinting jump height to 2 blocks


-Quests and Portals-

New Portal item added for additional teleport waypoints and A to B teleportation

Players desync from the server when questing, and all doors close, to guarantee that the quest is the
same consistent experience every time, and without the ability for another user to interfere

Item Stands now only distribute items during a quest, and only distribute once per quest attempt. This is used with items like keys to allow more effective replay, since chests do not auto-replenish between quests. Item stand gains are also removed when the quest is over

Keys, locked doors, quest start and end points, and item stands have been changed to be world owner only - this way visitors may not create unwelcome content if editing is enabled

Rewards have been added for completing quests


-New / Updated Menus and UI-

Updated art and gamepad support for Login, Pause Menu, Inventory, Transmutation, World Admin (check name), Settings, Keybinds, Sign and Container placement, and UI appearance

Energy has its own hotbar meter rather than being attached to the glove only

Buff/Debuff Indicators added to show what is active and how much longer it will go on

UI optimizations and fixes

A new notifications system and server announcements system


-New NPC and Functionality-

Added Petrified, Snow, and Lava rock creature variants

Added Fur, Fire, Ice, Nature and Galaxy horse variants

NPCs now randomly drop item pickups for players

NPCs no longer drop items on death

NPCs no longer linger after death - they are removed and a new NPC spawns from a camp

NPCs no longer trigger item stands or collect item pickups

All NPCs currently spawn from the same camp type


-New Art-

Replaced Crossbow with Tri-Barrel Laser

Trees Have Updated Art

Sand Shader Updated


-World Ownership and Permissions-

World Admin menu added to toggle guest editing (check name) on/off in an owned world

Worlds by default have all been turned to disallow guest editing, admin can change this setting in the
pause menu.


-New Known Bugs-

Weapon Aim Inaccuracy

Gamepad Keybinds not hooked up

Old homescreen doesn’t fully support gamepad

Player trajectory has a bit of a pop when transitioning from moving up a slope to a flat surface
NPCs don’t respond to terrain alteration in areas where they are moving

Additional Mac-specific issues

About This Game

MyDream is an online multiplayer sandbox game that combines grid-based building with organic and fully-deformable voxel terrain. Build, terraform, quest, and explore your way across various environments in beautiful and expansive worlds! Adventure with other players and meet the creatures and wildlife going about their everyday activities!

Join our online creative community to design and share your creations, or visit the ever-growing catalog of player-designed worlds. Players can even work together to make amazing creations! Give and receive world ratings and help the best creations rise to the top, where they will get featured in the game!

Key Features:

  • Combine powerful terrain tools, decorative blocks, and well-placed traps to shape and design content anywhere in your worlds.
  • Online system connects all of the MyDream worlds and makes it easy to visit worlds and play with others – even across multiple platforms.
  • Ability to easily make challenging quests and epic adventures using in-game items.
  • Place Geocaches for other players to discover or go exploring for Geocaches in other worlds.
  • Players can rate worlds they visit, and the top rated worlds are featured on the home screen.
  • Duel with your friends or take on creatures in the environment.
  • Ever-expanding database of items and content to enhance gameplay.
  • We host your worlds - no player server hosting is required.
  • No mandatory online subscriptions to access the game.

Can't remember how I got this, but who cares?

My playtime on this isn't accurate.

It happens sometimes.

Bizarro Lego

Going into the game I had no idea what to expect, but after playing offline for a few minutes I understood exactly what was going on. What you do in this is build. Primarily, that seems to be the goal of the game - to build, build, build your heart out and to set up your own little adventure games within the confines of the game mechanics. This sort of thing is my bread and butter and I LOOOOVE it. So these elements definitely elicited the, "Oh, Neat!" factor.

Move like mollasses!

My biggest complaint comes in the speed at which you actually move around the game world. Jumping is fine, mouse movement speed is fine, but the actual walking/sprinting speeds are so incredibly slow that it feels like you're swimming in a river of mollasses. There's simply no get up and go, as there is in other games of this type. If other games run like a Ferrarri, this one moves like an 84 Buick Regal. If the movement speed were turned up, it would vastly improve the playability of the game.

Graphics are the strongest part of this game.

Graphically this game is excellent, with a shiny-world sheen to everything that really sucks you in. The way the textures repeat and everything is simply divine - I didn't get eye fatigue from looking at the same stupid color palette the entire time I played it, and the cute-factor of the way simple things look is through the roof. Period, it's a really good looking game.

I have to confess something...

When I first started playing around with the game, and as I said my game playing time on Steam isn't accurate for some reason, I didn't see myself playing it for long. In the past couple of weeks, I've probably put about 6 hours total into the game. I'll probably multiply this by a factor of a dozen or so by the time I feel like moving on from it (That's not that many hours, I realize that, but consider that most games reach around 20 hours of play time for me and then get dropped off the planet because I don't feel like doing that much. There are only a few exceptions to this.)

Other things that the devs could improve...

Other than the pudding-swim movement speed, the devs would do well to try and improve the connection speed of the online options. Most of the time, I just give up because the load times are so incredibly long. Considering I have a connection speed of almost 200mbps, this isn't something that's on my end of the connection. They NEED to fix this sort of issue, or they'll be wasting a lot of potential on an amazing-looking game.

At the price, I'm going to recommend it without any hesitation. The problems it does have need to be fixed, but they seem relatively minor when you look at what the big picture. There really isn't much of a soundtrack to talk about, but if it had to be compared to something I would throw a Spore by Maxis vibe to it. This game is going to be great, and I can't wait to see where it goes.

Introduction
Developed and published by MyDream Interactive, Inc, MyDream is decribed as a fascinating open world, sandbox game that leads you into adventure and exploration. While exploring the world that you are in, you are able to build your own world with such a variety of tools and assets that are provided.

Gameplay
MyDream is a open world sandbox that was released on the 20th Febuary 2015. The game is based around generated levels and has a voxelized sort of world which you can create your own thing with anything that you can think of. Whether you wanna play online or offline is up to you, if your playing online you will need to create a account. There is a fair amount of assests and materials that are with the game, walls, windows, doors, plants, signs, stones and so many more. If I would have to describe this game, it would be a mix between Lego worlds and Minecraft because of the open world and creativity with what you can build throughout the game. Although I didn't spend that much time on the online part of the game, it does seem that you can visit other players worlds and see what they have created as well. With the game having pretty much endless possibilities to build and explore. The game is still in Early Access so you are going to expect some bugs here and there as you play through the game. I'm sure that they will be patched through the next few patches and updates. The game has a kind of unfinished tutorial, which doesn't really explain how each of the blocks work and how to use them.

The graphics of this game exceeds to be another minecarft clone. The graphics are way more than just blocked graphics and basic assests that you can use to create your worlds. There is a range of different types of graphics for each of the blocks, with all of them being nicely detailed. The players look alright, but I feel like the sprites could be crisped up and polished a bit more.

As far as the sound effects go for this game, they really aren't all that good. They seem to be a bit unclear and not really the high quality sound effects you would expect for a game like this. Although the games strong point isn't it's audio, I hope that they will sharpen up on the audio and make this game better in the upcoming updates and patches of this game.

Pros
+ Many assests and blocks to use to create whatever you want
+ Online and Offline mode
+ Exceeds from just blocked graphics

Cons
- Some bugs here and there
- Tutorial is unfinshed
- Player sprites could be a bit more polished up
- Sound effects could use some work

Result / Final thoughts
With this game being in early access, it does seem to have a decent and nice amount of content and blocks that you can use. There is a wide variety of sort of blocks and materials that you can use to create anything that you can think of. It's not another mincraft clone, I would say that this gam exceeds to being so much more than a minecarft clone when it comes to building. Although the game does need to work on some things, the tutorials, sound effects and some various bugs I still currently recommened this game.

Rating: 6.4/10

*DISCLAIMER; This copy of the game was provided by a group for the sole purpose of reviewing. This doesn't affect my opinion of the game in any way.*

(This Review was written about version 0.90, so some of the flaws mentioned may have been ironed out at a later date)

MyDream is definitely a game i can see having a lot of potential... if it stays afloat. It's an Open World Sandbox-Building game, not exactly dissimilar to Minecraft or it's many clones. In that regard, it does what it does well.

The building is fairly fluid, and fun. The interface can be a bit confusing to work with at first, but once you get adjusted to it, you can build some pretty impressive things. The Screenshots at the top of the page of those large, lavish structures? If you've got the time, it's not hard to do that sort of thing.

The controls are fairly intuitive. If you've ever played a similar game, then they'll make sense to you. If you've ever played Minecraft or anything similar, you'll adjust pretty quickly.

Graphically, it looks really nice. My only major complaint is that the blocks/voxels you use to build with don't exactly... "match" the enviornments, but that's easy enough to overlook. The playermodels have the same issue, but again, it's easy enough to overlook.

My only major problem is the multiplayer. At the moment, there's a decent amount of multiplayer going on, so it's easy enough to find a server. In a year or so? I don't know. The game, while fun, doesn't really hold up long by yourself; once you've built something fairly impressive, there isn't much else to do at this point. If the game holds up, perhaps this worry won't be founded. Here's hoping it does.

Overall, i quite like MyDream. It makes for a decent building experience, and it's honestly fun. I'd recommend it if you've got the spare cash, so long as you're creative and come into it knowing there isn't much else to it other than that.

MyDream is a 3D first-person god sim, where you start building from a scratch on a generated world and may create your own vision, with a mouse and keyboard, on how a fantasy world should look like, and what one would be able to do on it.

For the game you will need to create separate account on MyDream servers. Once you're done with that you will log into the main menu. From there I had a hard time finding tutorial- button, which I then found under the Question mark- menu. (Hint for the other new players!) I have to remind you that the game is still in Beta, so I didn't expect everything to work - just like the tutorial, which I wasn't able to finish since my way was blocked by rocky structure. Also I fell through the ground couple of times but teleporting back to the starting point fixed that.

Once you start creating your own world you get to decide your own world's name and layout. The given map is big enough for exploring and you are able to create your own quests for future world visitors. Think of this game as a Minecraft with graphical boost, plus the ability to create your own quests for others and yourself.

Building structures (I- button opens the inventory) was hard but rewarding - I built some mazes which I then accidentally destroyed. I realised that the more you build the longer you will have to wait for the world to get loaded.

MyDream has hours upon hours of structure building, terrain editing and quest creating - each world is an adventure of its own. However, I missed more sound effects and music inside the actual world, which would be awesome to be edited by the creator himself.

Judgment
If you get bored you can always play piano sounds from 9 different piano tones.

Worth the price for possible next-gen Minecraft. But not for the updates!

PLUSSES AND MINUSES:

- unfinishable tutorial
- bugs and lags
- no music inside worlds
- hardly any players online

+ many worlds
+ create your own buildings, terrains and quests
+ hours of planning and implementing
+ greater graphics than just cubes compared to Minecraft

After 3 days, not a single review has come in for this game, and I decided to be the first one to buy it, play it, and give all of you out there my 'honest' thoughts and opinion as to whether this game is worth it right now. The key words being "right now" as of February 23, 2015. Things can change over time, and this game could be completely different and much more evolved in two or three months then it is right now, just three days after it's Steam early access launch.

So... is MyDream worth it's asking price?

In my opinion, as much as I want to like this game... my answer is a firm NO. I think that answer would apply to most people who might be looking at this game with interest or curiosity. For most people, I think there are better options out there for your money.

Minecraft is cheaper right now and offers tons of player support, community add on, mods, texture packs and more available for free.

Creativerse right now is $10 cheaper, will be free-to-play sometime in the next 6-8 months likely and I believe is much further evolved and more playable then is this game.

If creating quests and placing treasure and loot and monsters is what you are looking for, then please take a strong look at 'Fight The Dragon' at half the price of this games current asking price. Fight The Dragon is much more complete and fun in my opinion then this game is currently.

All of the above options would be my recommended options for people looking at this game to consider first. There of course are always exceptions to the rule. I'm sure there are some people who love creating things that might easily get their moneys worth and be happy... but again.. I think that kinda person would be an exception rather then the typical onlooker curious about this game. Most people will probably feel a little scorched for their dollar paying $30 for this game right NOW in the state it is in currently.

Right now, there just isn't enough finished tools in the game, nor enough players actively playing or even on this game's own websites forums to support their asking price. Again, this is only my opinion... and I really WANT to like this game and give it my thumbs up, but I just can't honestly do that now. I will come back and re-review this game in four to six months and see how much has changed and if I feel differently at that time, but for now, I have to give this game a thumbs down. (If it were half it's asking price now of $15 USD, then I would likely give it a pass, but $30 is just far too much for what you get now I believe)

If you are looking for specific reasons as to why I say "NO BUY" on this game now, please watch my video above, at the top of this review. You should see many specific reasons in that video as to why I give this game a thumbs down review currently.

I hope that my honest review here has helped save someone some money and more importantly saved them the disappointment I felt with this game at it's current early access state of development.

Sorry devs! I DO see a ton of potential for this game... please keep up the hard work! I'd love to be able to play this game in a few months and change my opinion around 180 degrees and become a supporter and lover of this game!

MyDream is the answer to the question, "What if Minecraft and RPG Maker had a baby?" It's a game based around user-generated, story-driven voxelized worlds where just about anything goes, and this gives it a lot of potential.

The graphics are decent, although they're much better than that OTHER voxel-based sandbox game everyone enjoys. I'm playing on a laptop with an integrated graphics card, too, so I'm sure it's not running at the highest quality the Unity engine delivers. Audio quality is good, but I'm not much of a techno/electro fan.

I had issues connecting to other worlds, in that I attempted to join four/five of the most popular worlds for at least five minutes each time and could never establish a connection (server problems? location issues?). So that could use some work. But the offline mode was fun; I made a little maze, and my girlfriend had fun exploring it. We've played Minecraft and Terraria together, and we both agreed that this is a nice step up from those games in both GUI and accessibility. I look forward to being able to connect to other worlds and play the stories/puzzles others have created.

The game is in early access, so I imagine any bugs will be fixed before the final release. It's shaping up to be a great game, however. I can see this taking off.

Can't remember how I got this, but who cares?

My playtime on this isn't accurate.

It happens sometimes.

Bizarro Lego

Going into the game I had no idea what to expect, but after playing offline for a few minutes I understood exactly what was going on. What you do in this is build. Primarily, that seems to be the goal of the game - to build, build, build your heart out and to set up your own little adventure games within the confines of the game mechanics. This sort of thing is my bread and butter and I LOOOOVE it. So these elements definitely elicited the, "Oh, Neat!" factor.

Move like mollasses!

My biggest complaint comes in the speed at which you actually move around the game world. Jumping is fine, mouse movement speed is fine, but the actual walking/sprinting speeds are so incredibly slow that it feels like you're swimming in a river of mollasses. There's simply no get up and go, as there is in other games of this type. If other games run like a Ferrarri, this one moves like an 84 Buick Regal. If the movement speed were turned up, it would vastly improve the playability of the game.

Graphics are the strongest part of this game.

Graphically this game is excellent, with a shiny-world sheen to everything that really sucks you in. The way the textures repeat and everything is simply divine - I didn't get eye fatigue from looking at the same stupid color palette the entire time I played it, and the cute-factor of the way simple things look is through the roof. Period, it's a really good looking game.

I have to confess something...

When I first started playing around with the game, and as I said my game playing time on Steam isn't accurate for some reason, I didn't see myself playing it for long. In the past couple of weeks, I've probably put about 6 hours total into the game. I'll probably multiply this by a factor of a dozen or so by the time I feel like moving on from it (That's not that many hours, I realize that, but consider that most games reach around 20 hours of play time for me and then get dropped off the planet because I don't feel like doing that much. There are only a few exceptions to this.)

Other things that the devs could improve...

Other than the pudding-swim movement speed, the devs would do well to try and improve the connection speed of the online options. Most of the time, I just give up because the load times are so incredibly long. Considering I have a connection speed of almost 200mbps, this isn't something that's on my end of the connection. They NEED to fix this sort of issue, or they'll be wasting a lot of potential on an amazing-looking game.

At the price, I'm going to recommend it without any hesitation. The problems it does have need to be fixed, but they seem relatively minor when you look at what the big picture. There really isn't much of a soundtrack to talk about, but if it had to be compared to something I would throw a Spore by Maxis vibe to it. This game is going to be great, and I can't wait to see where it goes.
