About This Game

"Leviathan: The Last Day of the Decade" is a detective story told by a dweller of a cruel dark fantasy world affected by the pandemic. The world ruled by a 5 noble-born kins under the suzerainty of the undead Plague King.

A young man spends years to find a clue to bring justice for his mother's murderer. But what if the criminal is protected by the mighty family? And what if he is a man you really love?

FEATURES


— The world of aristocracy full of money and power.
— Romantic relationship.
— Daemons from the depths of outer worlds!
— Mysterious murder and secrets of the past!
— City streets, full of adventures.
— Stunning animated 2D graphics.

Episode I - The First Day of the Decade
Episode II - Clues from the past
Episode III - Being a killer
Episode IV - The Gift

So, Leviathan: The Last Day of the Decade is a unique point and click / visual novel adventure game in which you are Oliver Vetran and you are trying to avenge your mother's death. This game is certainly a hidden gem, if I have ever played a hidden gem. The game is constantly in bundles on websites and you can probably pick the game up for dirt cheap!
I WILL NOT BE GIVING A DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE GAME

To begin with, the story is a really great, emotion one however the ending is disappointing. The game starts off with a prologue and introduces you to the story. It also introduces you to a multitude of the characters and gives starting evidence for your mother's death. The story gets better from then on and I found my favourite episode to be episode 4 and the beginning of episode 5 as these, for me, were quite strong and emotional scenes. They really tested the relationships between the characters and some choices made me feel bad for certain characters, Kael . This game has not nearly been given the praise it deserves. Nearly the WHOLE game is perfect. Nearly. So without any spoilers I will say that the ending is disappointing. At first I was a bit confused by the ending because I had taken breaks between each episode and not paid enough attention to the dialogue. As I read more about it I understood but still was not completely happy. It definitely felt rushed. They could've made that final episode 2 times the length of a normal chapter (normal chapter is 1.5hrs) but instead they rushed it and that was really upsetting. However the ending isn't TERRIBLE, just a bit disappointing. So continuing on the story, the amount of detail and the dialogue between the characters was (mostly) very good and helped to add to the story's impact and the setting of the game. The story has a few hidden secrets here and there, and has multiple endings. I haven't ad time yet to fully playthrough another ending however there are a few things I need to say about replaying. Good news or Bad news first? The good news is that the game lets you start at any chapter and make your choices from the previous chapters. This means no replaying boring content, right? Well the bad news is that I so far have only found bugs when replaying the game. It started off small but now it is SO bad that I lost my previous saves and everything! I'm sure you could reinstall the game and start from a chapter there, however I can't guarantee that there will be no bugs with that method. Overall the detective story and the character's were really nice and made this game so enjoyable!

To go into a little more detail about the environment and the art of the game, the setting of Leviathan: The Last Day of the Decade is a fantasy world ruled by kings and affected by plague. I honestly don't really know how to describe the setting but for me that was the one thing that made me nervous before playing this game. I don't usually like most fantasy settings such as this. However, the setting was Great! It was absolutely brilliant and the art was what really made me enjoy this environment. The art of the game was SO great, so when I saw the new art style I was immdiately worried and a bit let down. It seems like they took a downgrade... :(
Still love this game's art though and the setting is great :)

Overall, I rate this hidden gem a 7.5/10!

Pros:
-Really cheap in bundles!
-A great, emotional story
-Excellent Characters
-Beautiful Art
-Wonderful and Completely Unique Setting
-FANTASTIC Soundtrack!!!
-Replayability
-Easy to get 100% on acheivements (if you don't have problems with bugs)
-Upcoming Sequel!

Cons:
-Disappointing ending that feels rushed
-Bugs when replaying!!!

Fantastic illustrations of a vibrant yet dark and cruel world. Mediocre English translation with zero effort put into localization, and this results in a (presumably) nuanced story becoming a collection of plot holes. What should be a three-act tale comes to an abrupt halt at the end of the second act, resulting in a finished game with an incomplete narrative. Finally, there are several technical issues with this game, including framerate crashes on animation, inconsistent text speeds, and the lack of a text log (a long-established staple for any VN.)

+Dark & mature world

Cons
-Awful localization
-Incomplete story
-Technical shortcomings

The world of Leviathan is very dark and unforgiving. Under the glitter aritocrats hide foul secrets and the longer we play, the more we uncover. Nothing is white or black, nothing is as simple, as it looks at first glance. Everyone hides something and seem to use main character for their own goals.

The game itself feels like a prologue for some bigger scheme. It sets up the world, introduces us to the foul ways it works and prepares the stage for something that can possibly change it, for better or for worse. What starts as teens revenge quest ends forcing many hidden players to make their moves. Power plays that go behind the scenes seem really interesting, but all we see through the eyes of our young protagonists are mostly the consequences. That's why sometimes it is hard to understand what was really going on. I love how the complexity of the world slowly unfurls, but we don't get all the answers. The scene is set for a bigger performance and I hope we will be able to play it in the next installment.


I loved the graphics and character designs, as well the characters. Kael has won my heart and I would love to see more of him. It is sad that I couldn't find any romantic content that included him, although the game didn't seem to be prude (there were some romantic options inlcuding girls and it mentiond incest relatonship, possibly some sexual abuse too). Although there appeared to be conversation that sets up MC sexual orientation early on but it didn't seem to matter later in the game, which is sad as Kael was the sweetest boy and best friend one can have (and he went through a lot of s**t because of our protagonist). I really feel for him and hope to meet him again in the next installment.

The game itself is mostly linear, would love to see more locations and sometimesi felt that the choices didn't matter too much. Although we can keep our MC pure or turn to evil (the world of Leviathan is foul and for every choice there is a pretty high price to pay), but other than that there is not too much when it comes to characte development.

English is not my native language but I saw some typos and sometimes the sentences were a bit weird, but it didn't bother me too much.

As for replayability value, there are more choices I would like to explore as they may provide more hints and new information about the world and its characters.

I bought Leviathan because I really liked the art, and it was on -75% sale or something.
So of course I decided that it was worth grabbing a copy of it, not expecting too much.
But once I actually played it, the story took me by surprise. I hadn't really read about the game,
so I went in completely blind. But the story really pulled me in, I couldn't stop playing it and
when I didn't play it, I was thinking about playing it. It was truly remarkable, the story and the characters.

The characters, oh the lovely characters. I grew to love Kael, our dear sweet friend from
our childhood. Our friend who would do anything for us, anything. And our sweet Darina,
the typical daddy's girl with fancy dresses and an awful personality. Edna, our dear darling
demon and of course, Oliver, the main character of this story. Each character was special
in their own way, they all had a special personality and you either liked them or disliked them.

The story, starting rather slow but then getting really interesting and keeps it up till the end.
It's a story about vengeance, wanting to avenge your dear mother's death. But also a story
of love and doing something life changing; Would you be able to kill the person you've grown
to love? But there's also a few flaws in the story, a lot of things that got left out and a lot of unanswered questions.



Would I recommend Leviathan?
Yes, I would. It's actually worth it at full price. I got it during a sale; but I would have paid
full price for this. Because the story is amazing, the characters are lovely and the game is
really fun. And it's not only a Visual Novel, it's also a point-and-click game.

(To be fair, the pictures aren't actually nudes but in the reality of the game they are supposed to be and that was weird enough for me) You have no say in this behavior, by the way, if you are on this quest, you cannot ask a girl who is not your sister and you cannot delete her photos from your phone. The weirdest part is how casual the game is about these things. They happen without explanation. I have 30+ playtime hours because I replayed it to see if you can avoid these sorts of occurrences. I was able to find an alternate to the above spoiler. However, I am fairly sure that the game makes your character masturbate to a picture of his dead mother before you an advance the plot. It is also impossible to avoid seeing your character express a desire to ♥♥♥♥ a woman .

Otherwise, the game has the expected violence and mild awkward sexuality that you can expect in the game. Qualities that I feel were well reflected in the content rating. Overall, it's a decent game. I just couldn't think of anyone that I could recommend it to without warning them about some of the content. It wasn't at all what I would call graphic, just extremely off-putting .

Leviathan is an extremely unique visual novel, with a detailed story and deep world building.

The visuals are very well done, with detailed scenery, charming pop outs and animated transitions, and animated story board elements. Even the menu design builds on the world in the story.

The music and audio feels bang on. Right at the opening credits, the music builds a dark but comic atmosphere.

One minor complaint I have is that wayfinding would be very hard without toggling on the hot point overlay. Fortunately you can toggle this one and off very easily without leaving the scene.

If you enjoy visual novels, I highly recommend checking this out!

This is one of the best adventure games I have had the privelage of playing in quite some time. Its spit into five chapters similar to any of the telltale games with three of the chapters currently released in english. Its worth noting that the first chapter is relativley short when compared to chapters two and three and acts as a long intro.

Its a story driven game heavy on text (so put on your reading glasses), the art style is fantastic (similar to Cinders or much of the Darkeye stuff) and the setting is wondefully eclectic. The cast of characters are all well crafted and by the end of chapter three all were very well fleshed out. I wont spoil the story but to say its an investigation based thriller with plenty of character interaction and dialogue.

The experience contains some light puzzle elements and some side quests which I find unusual for this style of game, seperating it from the linear visual novels also found in this genre. I also felt the decisions I made had consequences and was suprised to discover death or failure was in fact possible. The illusion of choice and "magicians choices" are often a problem for me in these types of games, but I genuinley felt that I was having an impact on the world and its characters. Incidentally the game does have a morality system which I wont go into but rest assured your actions do indeed have "consequences".

I highly recommend this game to anyone interested in adventure and or visual novels and considering the amount of garbage that comes out of greenlight its good to see something awesome for a change. The one criticism that I can make is the grammar and typos in the english translation of the game, certainly not enough to dissuade me from recommending it but they are obvious. I personally can overlook such problems and did not find them frequently enough to cause me frustration.

I truly hope this game is a commercial success for the devs and receives the attention it deserves I would love to see more from this studio in the future and eagerly await the release of the next chapter.
