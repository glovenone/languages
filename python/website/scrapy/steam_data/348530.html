About This Game

John is a middle-aged investment banker down on his luck. He has no family, no real friends, and his portfolio is in dire straits. Resolving to commit suicide, he jumps off the roof of his office building and at the last minute is saved by an immensely powerful alien entity named HEIDI. HEIDI is dying, and requires help in order to survive. Discovering that the protagonist has lost all purpose in his life, HEIDI offers him a new reason to live in exchange for saving her.

Incognito aims to mix elements from different gaming genres and bring them together into a game that’s vast in scale and requires different genre skills, but easy enough for the average gamer to jump into and play from beginning to end.

These genres change at each “zoom level” of the game:
  • When the player's on foot, Incognito is an FPS with RPG elements
  • When the player's in the orbit of a planet, Incognito takes the form of a simple RTS game
  • When the player's in their vehicle, the game turns into a Tank Combat game

In order to progress through each Episode, you’ll have to conquer challenges at each level, gathering information on foot, exploring in your ship, landing on uninhabited planets and mining elements, and on hostile planets, invading a planet’s surface defenses in the hover-tank.

Posted: 27 January
As others have said, the game is very unpolished and clunky, the graphics dated, and the UI not always intuitive. That said, the ambition of including so many different styles of gameplay into one game is impressive, and the game has a strange charm to it that kept me coming back for more.

The story is at times interesting, at times baffling, and it is very linear, with two paths to explore. But it has a slight System Shock 2 vibe to it with a dominant AI manipulating people.

I found it sometimes very obvious what was needed, and other times I wished there was a little more information. The voiced audio is mumbled and can be challenging to understand, especially for the parts without subtitles, but you can understand enough to get by.

The biggest let down for me was how money doesn't mean anything. The game refers to it multiple times, and even gives you 1,000 credits at one point, but you can never see how much you have, and the couple of merchants don't show prices because you can take their entire inventory for free.

There are lots of systems, with lots of planets to explore, and the mining aspect could have been more engaging, but you quickly realise there is no point at all in mining anything other than what is required for the current mission.

So, this all sounds like I didn't like the game, right? That's the confusing part... because I enjoyed playing the game and working around the quirks.

Also worth noting, the one time I was stuck and couldn't understand how to do what the game wanted me to do, based on the information given, I posted on the discussion group and the dev was quick to reply with a hint to get me going again.

While I do not think this game is worth full price, there is enough enjoyment to be had here for a sale price to be worthwhile. If you see it reduced, or find it in a bundle, give it a try.

Now, with that being said and with the reconstruction of this game, I must admit, I found it to be very understanding. What I mean with that statement is that I can skip (or accidentally skip) information and still be able to know what I am supposed to do and which keys to do it from. I believe this to be an excellent aspect of the game, considering the developers have put a huge effort and time into reconstructing it, specially taking into account notes given by the testers.

Regarding the storyline, having seen the game gone from the main game and it’s episodes into one solid game, I believe this to be another key point. I believe it has helped with understanding what’s going on and even for some players to really get into the role of being the protagonist. This is also really reflected in regards to the gameplay as it takes true to the previous aspect I mentioned. Keep that in mind, having played the game back in 2009, this newer version has really impressed me. Of course, all that glitter’s isn’t gold; the AI is a bit predictable and buggy, and I had a bit of trouble with the shooting (Crosshairs on the enemy, fired, but no death. I believe it was because I was on an inclination so probably there was a piece of the world blocking me or I just really bloody suck at it). There was also issues with the AI generally just rushing you and you having an excess of items such as ammo. I believe that the developers can fix this and even make it harder depending on the difficulty level.

Considering all this, from my subjective opinion, I would recommend this game. I consider not only the price to be a huge factor when it comes to getting your bang for your buck, but also just in general. In all honesty, with such a small development group and considering the game is from 2009 but remastered for 2015, I believe this group did a fine job with the resources and time they had. Even if it’s just to fart around and have some small fun, I still would recommend this game.

At the end of the day, the proof is in the pudding. You'll get bad reviews; you'll get good reviews. I think considering what such a small team put together + Price, it's a fun, humorous, action game.

I completed Incognito, on both branches if there was a choice. It works more like an interactive movie than a game, or rather a mashup of games that are so easy that they don't really provide a challenge.

There is an adventure part with voiced dialog and characters and some HHGTG references (if you know why a framed map of Norway would be one, you're a fan), but it is completely linear, and has all of two branches; the first branching unites seemlessly a short while later, the second branching has no effect other than the obvious one of playing the same section as either a wolf or a fox. There is a storyline, but all you get to do is fetch items.

There is a RTS part where you harvest minerals with 4 tanks and a probe and there maybe some enemies, but the enemy AI is dumb (all they know to do is to rush at you) and your own AI is no better (the weak probe always wants to repair the tanks and gets shot in the process). There are different building types, but no gameplay to use them.

There is an fps part where you have different guns (the railgun one-shots pretty much everyone, including closed doors), plenty of ammo, weak enemies (shoot them once, they die, they shoot you, you lose 5% health if that), and dumb AI: all they know is to rush at you, provided they notice you at all - simply shoot them down at range, done. You have lots of ammo, lots of food, it's as if the difficulty was set to "child's play", but the game has no other difficulty settings. There's a shop system in parts of this, but since there is no money, you can just use the shop as you would any crate.

There is a vehicle combat part where you drive a tank, but the enemies are weak and their AI is dumb: all they know is to rush at you, and they wont if you shoot them at range. Since the camera is above and behind the tank, you can see terrain where the tank can't see, and the reticule skips over that when you try to move it there, which is very annoying.

There's also space flight,and while you have been gathering material for shields and weapons, there is no opportunity to use them - it's stricly travel only, and the game is not quite aware that there's a difference between "star system" and "galaxy". Also, it's pretty much 2D, though you can do 3D if you tilt the camera, but then you have to accept that the ship isn't going to be pointing where you're thrusting.

Oh, and then there are 3 minigames that you can play from your inventory. Just don't. There's also an anagram game that you need to play to hack doors that has stats for "score" and "words found", but every door opened on the first word I found, so they weren't actually used.

This is a dedicated amateur effort alledgedly 6 years in the making that tries to do five different things, but does all of them badly. Sure, if you like amateur productions, feel free to have a go if it ends up in your Steam library (e.g. via bundlestars), but you (and I) would be better off spending your time on a game that's actually good: I recommed System Shock 2 if you want to play with/against an insane AI, or Starship Titanic (if you can still get it and get it to run) which was actually designed by Douglas Adams himself and has talking bots with 10000 lines of text.

But maybe it's not meant to be a game: maybe the "game" sequences are actually easy by intent, and the "game" is actually an ugly interactive movie. In which case, watch a real movie instead. Even Dark Star is much better than this.
