About This Game

Engineered specifically for the PC by Red Storm Entertainment, Rainbow Six returns to deliver the tensest close-quarters battles ever experienced online. In this episode, Rainbow faces independent terrorist threats, tied together by one common element - the Legion virus. Rainbow must determine the connection between the terrorist threats and the virus.

  • Change in story and mission composition - Rainbow faces independent terrorist threats tied together by one common element - the Legion virus. Rainbow must determine the connection between the terrorist threats and the virus.
  • Enhanced tactical gameplay allows you to immerse yourself in tense close-quarter combat, where split-second decisions mean the difference between life and death.
  • Superior multiplayer experience - Rainbow Six Lockdown™ has the total multiplayer experience, including a revolutionary class-based mode where players choose from four specialized operative classes, each with unique gameplay advantages.
  • Advanced weapons and equipment - Complete your mission with all of the tools of the trade including new items like lock fusers, laser trip mines, and the deadly virus grenades.
  • New "classic" maps - Rainbow Six Lockdown PC provides nine maps including three new "classic" Rainbow maps voted on by the fans.
  • Tension-building sound effects (creaking floorboards, whispers and hostages begging for their lives) hint at a threat around every corner.
  • Increased visuals - All levels contain increased visuals, accomplished by increasing resolution of textures and increasing polygon counts. In addition, lighting of spaces dramatically improves the visuals of the game.
  • All new user interface includes new radar element added to the in-game HUD - part of the motion sensor vision mode.

The fourth entry in the series, Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six: Lockdown is a tactical shooter that was released for PS2, Xbox, Gamecube in 2005. There was even a mobile phone version of the game released the same year. The game was slightly redesigned and then released for PC in 2006; the changes included the removal of sections that contained sniper gameplay and story-related cutscenes, along with the inclusion of redesigned levels and... better helmets.

Lockdown takes place in 2009 and pits the Rainbow force up against Global Liberation Front, a worldwide terrorist organization that have stolen a man-made nanotech virus that is code-named Legion. The virus itself is an aerosol that induces fatal hemorrhaging in those who are unlucky enough to come into contact with it. Through 16 missions, it's up to the Rainbow team to eliminate the Global Liberation Front and stop the spread of Legion before it claims too many casualties. The story, for a tactical shooter, is pretty solid and it's told through a series of debriefing videos at the start of each mission.

That being said, the plot of the game is pretty well the only thing going for it, everything else is completely horrid. The enemy AI is among the worst that I've ever seen in any title, often they will stand there and stare at you while you massacre them; if they do manage to finally pull the trigger, their chances of making contact with you are extremely slim... even at point-blank range. Not only that, but the three squad members that you command are as useful and spry as a bag of bricks; their AI is almost just as bad, they often get stuck on each other, walk in front of you as you're firing, ignore your "follow" commands, and more. Their aim is also terrible, but slightly better than the enemy. If you do not tell them to breach and clear, they're happy to stand there and let you do 100% of the dirty work.

For those that are unaware, older Rainbow Six games did not have regenerating health, but instead three blocks of health that must last you for the entire mission. Even though the enemies have a slim ratio to smack you with a bullet, it almost always seems like when they finally do land a hit, you're down for the count; this is more of an issue if you have two out of three health blocks left. The damage that you receive is very inconsistent. This can be tedious and frustrating, since instead of having checkpoints, Lockdown relies on a manual save system. If you haven't saved your game before dying, you must start from the very beginning of the level.

The badness doesn't just stop at shoddy AI with characters who will sometimes dance and flail around like they're on speed, but the level design is absolutely awful. The only thing that the cramped spaces do, is cause you and your team to fall all over each other and make it that much harder to get through doorways. There's also something that constantly blocks your field of vision, such as giant trees, that makes it impossible to see some enemies before it's too late; with their wonderful AI, they're happy to wait until you're right up on them before they do anything at all.

Another down note... the optimization of Lockdown is screwed up at best. My game consistently fluctuated from anywhere between 40-200 FPS at any given time, all the while still seeming to stutter as I walked. If VSync is enabled, it looks like you're hobbling with a broken ankle. The game also crashed on me if I experienced repeated deaths; I guess it finally gave up on me.

At the end of the day, the only thing good about Lockdown is its theme song, the rest is utter garbage. In fact, this game really has it all covered when it comes to being awful; inconsistent damage received, crappy enemy and squad AI, bad level design, shoddy optimization, crashing, enemies that are too easy to kill, and for some reason the menu of the game is the only part that won't render in 1080p... go figure. Only if you're a hardened Tom Clancy and/or Rainbow Six fan should you pick this title up. It shouldn't even be bought as a joke to see how many laughs you can get out of it, it'll just turn to frustration by your tenth one-shot.

Rating: 1.0/5.0 - It's Bad.

Rainbow Six Lockdown is the 4th entry in Rainbow Six franchise. It actually was made for consoles first, in 2005 by Red Storm. It would then be ported to PC in 2006, apparently reworked somewhat. But not enough, thus you still control only one squad and there is no planning stage. But hey, look at those helmets on rainbow six characters! Apparently, that was number one complaint from fans about console version. Quite a priority.
It also was released on Xbox, Gamecube and PS2. Yea, they are different enough from PC, at least levels aren't all that same.

Also, I think that this is v1.0 version, while there exist a v1.1 patch. But it only seems to fix multiplayer things.

Multiplayer community here is quite dead. Actually, online multiplayer servers are dead. Ubisoft shut them down. Some say that they could login with UPlay account, didn't work for me last time I tried. But oh well, still can use some programs, you know them.

And well, what can I say. The game got literally "consolized", as much as I hate this term. No planning. Only one squad. You can play only as main hero. Everybody has huge health now, unless headshot. Infinite pistol ammo, even though it makes sense gameplay-wise. Game became nothing more but a generic lame action realistical shooter that we had more than enough in 2000s. To be honest, Rainbow Six ports on consoles already had similar thing going for them, but it's the first time developers decided to bring this to PC where it's not really needed.

Which wouldn't be so bad had AI had not been so terrible. I am mostly talking about teammates AI. If you take a look at their AI separate from gameplay, then it's quite an improvement, as they learned to take cover and peek around corners, instead of just following you in straight line. But once you actually play it, then oh boy. They have messed up priority, ignoring terrorist with shotgun, they are often incompetent with their shooting and they can be way too happy to rush forward, often getting headshotted by you.

Hostages also are quite horrific to deal with. They seem to have same AI as teammates, happy to rush into next room and take cover. Heck, they are annoying like no other tactical game ever had, as policeman hostages would whine "police, here!" and "somebody help me!" while following you.

And you don't have as much tactics anymore. After all, you control only one squad, not even individual members. And with their horrible AI, you are pretty much a lone wolf with few lives walking behind you. The only times that they matter is that they can take orders on how to enter through the door. Shotgun, hammer, breach, grenades. They still screw up grenades, which also aren't as good as used to be either, as flashbang works like grenade that stuns, with small radius. And even then, teammates may die embarresingly during cleaning of room, easily.

Since there is little left of tactical part, they could at least make action really good. But nope. Developers decided that PC audience are some crazy hardcore people who hate any story or cutscenes fluff with all their heart and removed it pretty much in this version of a game. We don't even get intel to pick up anymore in these linear levels, so exploration feels empty, as tehre are no pickups at all. Which harms level design that is linear with some pathways.

Not to mention that you will easily blast through the game as it now allows quickscamming of save and loading. And motion detecter with regenerative battery, which I abused heavily.

Also, yea, it also includes modes like Terrorist Hunt, Terrorist Hunt in reverse map, Lone Wolf. Barely noticed difference. And multiplayer, which is dead, so no commers.

Overall, a game on PC that lacks on both tactical and action fun. Just a generic realisticalish lame shooter.

If you're looking for a serious tactical shooter, this isn't quite what you're looking for. While lacking the more linear style levels of the console counterpart, the PC version of Lockdown has larger levels, more open levels (still not as large and open-ended as Rainbow Six 3's, or its predecessors). The gunplay is very strange, it feels like a mix between Counter Strike and Rainbow Six 3, where your character is swift and nimble, but fire-fights are still extremely deadly and tactics and strategy are still required to overcome enemies. I'd say that the sound design exceeds Rainbow Six 3's, along with the level design, although the gameplay is much more fast paced.

No longer are you carefully planning your movements and decisions before striking your targets, as this is more about cover-based shooting. Commanding your squad takes a back seat to the action and its relegated more to a standard FPS with the Rainbow Six skin on it, but lacks the elements that truly made it Rainbow Six. If you're not a purist, you'll be able to find some joy in it, as it's not broken, plays very smoothly, and is made very competently, the only problem is the design behind most of it where it's trying to be a tactical shooter, but lacks the dedication to really commit to the genre.

Rainbow Six Lockdown is set up to be a squad based tactical shooter, where you and your team breach doors, rescue hostages, and take down the bad guys. You have up to four team members at a time, and you issue fairly straightforward commands to them. They are fairly mindless and sometimes stand stupidly while being fired on. It's not so bad, though - the enemy does the exact same thing. Both of you are dealing with not-so-bright comrades so it all evens out.

Really, the thing to remember in single player is that it is all a training mode for multi-player. You always get far better challenges playing against live humans than against computer-driven drones. So consider this your training grounds to get used to the commands. On one hand the game is a bit challenging - you can't survive infinite hits with a gun before you go down. It is much more realistic than many games in that sense. On the other hand, once you get even a basic grasp of how the tactics work, you should be able to rip through many of the missions without any need for cover or caution. The enemy is fairly clueless and you can usually kill them long before they start to take action.

Lockdown is worth a look and gives a solid decent amount play time, however people new to the series would be better to start on another Rainbow six game as this is certainly not a highlight of the series.
