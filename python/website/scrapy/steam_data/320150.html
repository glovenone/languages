About This Game



Death is lonely. He has zero friends on his FaceTome account and no one to hang out with. So, in order to feel better he begins “Project Deadlings”. Death buys a factory where he can build his laboratory and begin training a massive army of zombie minions.

As the army of Deadlings grows, the mazes of the laboratory become deadlier, loaded with puzzles and death-defying traps. Different Deadlings have their own unique abilities: Bonesack is agile - he can run and jump, Creep can climb on walls and ceilings, Lazybrain treads slowly but carefully and Stencher... well Stencher has gastric problems so he can use his powerful gas clouds to fly.

You will have to combine all of these abilities to find your way in Death's Maze.

Can you help Death to kill his boredom? Will you be able to navigate all 60+ levels available in Deadlings? Will you complete Project Deadlings, and successfully train all of your zombie minions?



  • Arcade side-scroller with strategy elements!
  • Four different types of zombie minions with unique abilities!
  • 70 levels of pure zombie gore fun!
  • Additional game mode - Nightmare - only for the hardcore gamers!

What's new in the Rotten Edition?

  • 10 brand new bonus levels
  • 15 new levels in Nightmare Mode
  • Steam achievements and leaderboards
  • Full controller support
  • Nightmare mode unlocked from the beginning
  • Steam Trading Cards and Badges
  • HD graphics

-- Six important points about Deadlings: Rotten Edition --

1) The title music is insanely cool!

2) A lot of reviews compare this to 'Super Meat Boy' and 'Plants VS Zombies'. Those a rubbish comparisons. Deadlings is a side-scrolling puzzle platformer: you evaluate the level, decide which zombies are best at each task, then you set them running into the level (inputting single commands like jumping, but never directional input). This game is immensely more like 'Lemmings' and a little bit like 'Braid' and other less-manic puzzle-platformers.

3) The fact this game isn't like those other famous titles is not an insult. This game is a great little gem! As you progress, the game expands on your options and the depth of the puzzles. I highly recommend not worrying about good scores initially and instead try to clear levels, otherwise you won't see much of the game's brilliant concepts for a long while.

4) This game is very much worth it's asking price, and it's an easy purchase when on sale! Few games are both this well made, and be within anyone's price range. Not perfect, but nothing that would really ruin your fun.

5) You can only unlock one level at a time, by completing the previous level. If you are stuck, this game doesn't offer you much else to do. This is the game's one major flaw.

6) There's also a weird interaction that 'Bonebag' (your fast-running zombie that jumps) will literally explode if he runs into a wall or clips into the side of a platform. It makes sense from a gameplay-design perspective, but is visually doesn't make sense and it's infuriating to behold! Just a minor flaw but when puzzle games can be taxing on your patience, you REALLY notice stuff like that! Especially since everything else in the 'Rotten Edition' is so crisp and well designed!

-- MY RATING: 8.7 --

Get it cheap, play it little-and-often, and enjoy!

I do not understand the hate that this game is getting: It isn't Super Meat Boy, but it is a pretty decent game, and quite cheap.

For those complaining that there is a lack of a tutorial, I'm rather dissapointed. A tutorial for this game is about as necessary as directions on a shampoo bottle.

At the start of the level you are able to look around the typicall small level, and decide upon a strategy. Usually these aren't too difficult, and it should be rather obvious what you need to do. You then select the zombie you wish to use for that attempt, and choose a direction for the zombie to run (left or right). From there, it is mostly just a timing based game, as you'll need to click at the proper times so your zombie doesn't get chopped to pieces by buzzsaws or whatever.

As I said before, this game isn't amazing, but it definitely shouldn't be considered a bad game. I got it in a bundle, and I intend on finishing it, as it should only take about 3-4 hours for a single playthrough (I'm currently 1/4 of the way through with .6 hours, but I've kind of been rushing through).

Would recommend if you want to get it in a bundle or on sale, or at full price if you've exhausted some of the better options for games of this genre.
