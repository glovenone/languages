So. This game. Where do I even begin?

I got The Magical Silence as part of a bundle, and when I read it was a short point-and-click game, I decided to keep it and give it a go. It looked like a good choice for a blindstream, so I went ahead with that. About forty minutes later, I had completed it. This would have been much faster if not for a part that I'll talk more about down below.

There is not much in the way of story or characterization. Only one character even gets a personality (besides what is drawn/shown) and it leads to a predictable yet blah ending. Saw it coming, still didn't care for it. While I could go into detail as to why I feel it is poorly done, it can't be talked about much without ruining the plot, so I'll leave my dissatisfaction for those elements at that.

In terms of graphics, they are fairly cute. The art is a nice asset. There are some creative design decisions that I do respect. You're going into a mental world, so the bizarre nature of it makes sense. Thinking you're on an acid trip is probably not far from the truth. Yet the world does appear to follow its own rules, and most of these are easy enough to pick up.

The music, on the other hand, gets repetitive quickly. There are not many different tracks. The ones that exist seem to fit well enough. I just had no reason to care much about them. I do give credit for how the sound works, though. When you scroll the screen, the music gets louder as you get closer to its source, and gets softer when moving away from said source. Since most of the game is on this single long screen, I found this to be pretty neat.

Since it is a point-and-click game and the mouse works fine, there's little that can go wrong there. Most of the puzzles are simple enough to figure out. However, there is one that I found a little bit unintuitive. Then there is another that I found utterly absurd. I still don't know exactly how I completed that one, and I swear it took over half my time actually playing the game. It doesn't help that if you fail said puzzle, you have to start all the way back at the beginning, which is an annoying time suck. Overall, the lack of direction may make sense for what little story there is, but it sure as heck doesn't help to make it an enjoyable experience.

There are trading cards, but be prepared to idle for them. Unless you are utterly stumped by one of the puzzles, there is no way the gameplay will last long enough to drop the cards. There is one achievement, and it is gotten by default for beating the game. If you're an achievement hunter and card collector, certainly it fulfills those roles. I have no other opinion on them.

Short games don't bother me, especially when they are at low enough prices. That being said, those short games have to do something to make them feel worth that price. I don't think this is worth a dollar. If it was a free game, I'd say that it might be worth giving a shot. If you get it in a bundle, maybe that's enough justification. I don't feel cheated by playing it out of the bundle I got. There are some good elements, and you could certainly do worse than playing this. My final verdict, though, is that its shortcomings outweigh its creativeness. The Magical Silence is an interesting experiment that just didn't pan out all too well.

I really wish there was an option to give a neutral review, because The Magical Silence is not without merit. The game is artfully made, and I really like its dreamlike, whimsical nature. If you pick up this game as a part of a bundle, I would say go ahead and give it a whirl, but I wouldn't buy it unless it's discounted.

As others have said, this is very short - the only reason it can take longer than 10 minutes is figuring out how the puzzles work. I'm ambialent about this; I'm going with thumbs down, because it's got a few technical issues, and not everyone loves point-click-puzzle games. If you don't love those, don't bother.

PROS:
  • Charming and surreal artwork
  • Pleasant music (for me, at least)
  • Unique, clever puzzles

CONS:
  • No save function (but it's short and simple enough that this is barely even annoying)
  • Froze after minimizing and restoring (again, not an actual problem)
  • Puzzles very non-intuitive, to the extent of "what did I do, so I can stop clicking on that thing trying to get it to do something else?"
  • Ending is depressing and unsatisfying.

    If you do love point and click puzzles, and you don't mind spending money for the game (yes, it's only a dollar, but if you're being careful about money, this is not the bargain game to pick up), it can be fun. There's nothing wrong with it (except needing to restart from the beginning which takes less time than many games take to load); it's just not as intriguing nor as enjoyable as I'd hoped.

    This isn't, "this game sucks;" this is "meh, you can probably find a better use for you dollar." But if you adore point and click puzzles and would like to try one with a different approach - the puzzles are nicely different from most - you're not likely to feel cheated.
