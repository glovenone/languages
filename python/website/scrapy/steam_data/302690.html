About This Game

Metal Dead is a hilarious comedy/horror point and click adventure filled with laughs, blood, insanity, and of course... hordes of the undead.

This twisted tale centers on two metal-heads in the midst of a zombie invasion. After crashing their car in front of a medical research facility, they become trapped and are forced to use their wits to puzzle their way to freedom. Along the way, they must deal with a cast of characters who range from mildly deranged to completely insane.

Nominated by Adventure Gamers for 'Best Writing - Comedy' and 'Best Independent Adventure Game'. Also named one of the best Independent Adventure Games of the year by IndieGames.com!

The first time I played this game, I said to myself, "What the hell is that music?". The main soundtrack of the game is a heavy metal low-fidelity music that sounded like crap. The game's primary saving grace were the characters in this game because they were so funny and witty. There are no sounds when they speak though, it is only text-based. There are tons of jokes here with a lot of pop culture and movie references.
Another wrong impression I made about the game was that, I thought it would be another generic zombie game, but this is definitely not. It gives us a unique twist on how the zombie plague occured. It was not viral but something else. Got you interested yet?
I'm very proud of indiegame developers who hit the mark when it comes to game development. Kudos to you Walk Thru Walls Studios.
The main character that you play is a true die hard heavy metal music fan. But for a metal head slacker, he is quite a smart aleck and knows a lot of scientific sheet and stuff.
The game is a typical point and click adventure game. You get your usual dose of of puzzles to solve with a "CLEVER" hint system when you get stuck and don't know what to do. Your ultimate objective is to escape the building you are trapped-in and to survive the zombie horde trying to enter the premises. The game has a great and surprising ending, you'll see.
In conclusion, the graphics are adequate and unique, the sound is terrible and low quality, the gameplay is a lot of fun and the story is unlike anything else. I recommend this game. You won't regret it.

TL;DR Traditional comedy/horror point & click, with a great sense of humour and decent puzzles. If you don't mind a bit of cartoonish gore and some crude jokes in your adventure games, it should provide you around 3 hours of solid fun.

Metal Dead first came out at 2011, as the very low budget brain child of the two aussie developers behind Walk Thru Walls Studios. Despite its very basic art style, the low-fi music, the lack of voice acting, the short playtime and some minor (non game-breaking) bugs here and there, it completely embraces its crazy humour and oddball set of characters and ends up managing to both charm and entertain.

Immediately after your unlikely duo of metalhead heroes manage to crash their car right in front of what appears to be Zombie Central, everything turns from weird to weirder; including your protagonist Malcolm ending up with one of the funniest hint delivery systems in gaming history stuffed right into his pocket. (Feel free to use it now and then even if you aren't stuck, it offers some very amusing moments). Any more story details will, I feel, take away from your enjoyment, but suffice to say that the writing is quite polished. The dialogues are successful in delivering both jokes and a sense of character, and the insane plot ends up holding your interest despite (or because of) its absurdity. There are some unexpected twists and turns to enjoy, plus an oddball cast of engaging folks to save - or escort to their inevitable comedic demise. And while much of the humour could qualify as crude or gross, it somehow still manages to hit the mark more often than not. As for the puzzles, they are all inventory based and mostly logical. I was happy to see that they tended to be backed up by dialogue and built neatly into the story instead of distracting from it. The difficulty isn't very high, but some of them are clever nonetheless.

There is something to be said about developers that succeed in making something this fun out of such a low budget, but you should still come to this game understanding that corners were (mostly gracefully) cut. The low-fi metal music has some nice moments, but I was more impressed with the clever use of sound effects that help emphasise the right points in the plot, either for shock value or for comedic effects. Similarly, the cartoonish art style may have character but is nonetheless very basic and doesn't scale well in larger monitors. However the animation saves it and deserves praise for its fluidity.

So Metal Dead is a fun little game, provided it finds the right audience that will appreciate its wacky sense of humor. I enjoyed replaying it, and I'm looking forward to Metal Dead: Encore, which is expected to stomp into our lives sometime in the next year or so.
