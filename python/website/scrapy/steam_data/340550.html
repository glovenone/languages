About This Game

Crunch Time! is a card game with a touch of humour, based on videogame development, in which you'll have to manage your own company, and complete the best and biggest projects before your opponent.

Cards will help you on your way: developers of all departments, positive cards to help you work faster; errors and problems to slow your opponent down; and much more which you'll discover as you play.

Current features:
  • More than 100 unique cards!
  • Complete the 28 available projects!
  • Break into the bestselling top 10!
  • Discover how a videogame is really developed!

Check our website to have your own physical version of Crunch Time!

I hate to say it, but this game is just not fun.

The core concept is that you're playing cards that count down over a few turns, then that card counts as a point towards your total score. You win by scoring the required number of points in each of the 5 categories.

You can interfere with your opponent by playing "bugs" that freeze some or all of your opponents cards for 1-3 turns, which delays their scoring.

The problem comes in that you get at least 5 cards in your hand, but there are certain cards you can't play or discard in most situations. This leads to situations where you have 4 dead cards in your hand each turn only to draw one card you can play. It leads to a very difficult situation where you're just playing every card you get and can't do anything strategic.

Like I said, the game ends up not being fun because of these fundamental game design flaws. It's not a terrible game, but not something that you'll likely find yourself coming back to again and again.

This game is so bad that I'm not going to bother organizing its flaws into coherent paragraphs.
As all the other reviews have said, there's no deck building or even choice of decks. That would be fine if this used a Spectromancer style system where all your cards are on the table, but instead you just have to hope you draw what you need out of the mystery deck. The only limit on cards is that you can normally play only one type of developer card per turn. There's no option to discard, so you need to play multiple cards a turn to maximize your card draw. But it's possible to end up with a hand full of defensive cards that can't be played until your opponent does something specific, leaving you stuck with a hand full of garbage while he destroys you. Very powerful cards have no resource cost to balance them against weaker cards. Some enemy attack cards can only be countered by a specific person, but all the people who work in that department have the same art. The cards' flavor text is in the same font as its explanation text. The music is awful, and you have to mute the game entirely to turn it off (the sound effects are OK). This game is so obviously broken that the only thing that got me to finish a full match was the programmer humor, which is its only redeeming quality.

I've put a lot of hours into this game in the DRM-Free version a while ago and I was happy to see it make its way onto Steam. The mechanics of the game are relatively simple and not a whole lot of explanation is required to understand what you must do which is a good thing, because card-battling games at times can get VERY complex.

The premise is simple, you're assigned a video game project to work on, and each department needs a certain number of completed tasks, and ultimately once each task has its required number you win. The game's cards represent these tasks, and how many turns are required to complete each one, and there are also counter-cards to make the AI opponent's life a little bit more difficult, by delaying his tasks, or throwing on more tasks for it to complete to ultimately win.

I'm a card-battling junkie when it comes to PC games and I've played my fair share of them, and this fits into my collection nicely. If I was to have any complaint at all towards this game it would be that it would greatly benefit from online multiplayer, but a minor setback when the price is considered, I'm definitely getting what I'm paying for and have already gotten my moneys worth outside of Steam.
