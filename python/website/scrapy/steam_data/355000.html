About This Software

GlassWire visualizes all your network activity so you can improve your Internet connection quality, save bandwidth, and help protect your computer from malware. If any unusual network-related activity is detected GlassWire warns you with a temporary polite notification that won't disturb your game-play.

Do you host any games on remote servers? GlassWire can monitor your game servers remotely so you can check their network connection quality, detailed bandwidth usage, and also look out for hack attacks. Do you not agree with how much bandwidth your hosting company claims you are using? If so you can double check your daily, weekly, or monthly bandwidth usage with GlassWire.

If you notice a strange connection on your computer or game server GlassWire allows you to block the connection with its simple firewall. Since GlassWire works with the built in Windows firewall it's unlikely to cause stability problems with your software or computer.

Protect your Internet resources, computers, and remote servers with GlassWire's free network security monitoring software.

    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Any
    • Storage: 100 MB available space

I want to love Glasswire. I really do. I am a network design engineer by trade and finding something consumer oriented that has a decent amount of information is tough. I thought glasswire was the ticket.

Used it for hours and loved it - found that it detected some obscure network traffic and showed some good source/destination information.

While it found some good traffic - it COMPLETELY missed over 6 Mbps of traffic from a few different video sites when I was going through a app detection testing round. I had taskmgr/resourcemon/Glasswire next to each other and Glasswire underreported network traffic by a huge margin. While going through Resourcemon, I found it also completely missed several other applications that were consistently using bandwidth when Glasswire reported zero throughput.

If they fix their app reporting then i'll give it another shot, but until then, stick with using Resource Monitor to look at your process tx/rx.

You cant recommend or rely on an application that misses on its core functionality.

EDIT: Developer responded and provided some information which unfortunately, didnt apply to me. They suggested reboot, but unfortunately that wasnt my issue as I had it installed over the course of several reboots.

Developer response:
GlassWire's normal desktop installer warns that to pick up all new connections you may need a reboot, but we don't force the reboot because some people find that annoying. We will try to add a notice about this on Steam also.

We use a well known and trusted Windows API to detect traffic and it's very accurate.
