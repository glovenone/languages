About This Game

When evil Puppeteer kidnaps souls of innocent children, you're assigned your first mission as Brothers Grimm's investigator - find a way to defeat the malefactor and bring rescue to all in need. Follow the trace of magical events to discover sorcerer's blind spot. Use it to defeat him and bring home all who have been lost!

Once upon a time, there was a man who went from town to town with songs and poetry, capturing the souls of children. From generation to generation, the tale was told. But every fairy tale has its true bitter beginning. Now a new evil has visited the town of Arbourshire, and children have been going missing. A mysterious puppet theatre seems to be at the centre of their disappearance. The town is in an uproar! A mob roams the streets and the Brothers Grimm have assigned to you the task of discovering the truth behind the Fairy Tale Mystery! Discover the truth behind the myth and save the children!

I've recently played through Crystals of Time, which was my first hidden object game experience. Overall, I liked it, but I did not enjoy the shallow story and its fairly minimalistic realization that much. Fairy Tale Mysteries: The Puppet Thief exceeds the aforementioned game in these aspects, making it an ideal choice for the lovers of hidden object games (even though by reading other reviews, it seems there are better ones out there).

According to the story we've been asked to investigate the mysterious disappearances of local children of the fictional town of Arbourshire, by none others than the Grimm brothers (who were German aouthor and collectors of folk tales, but nevermind). Apparently an evil sorcerer, called the Puppet Thief is kidnapping the children and uses them as puppets.

The premise seems exciting and spooky enough, but after having finished the game many unanswered quetions remain. Why did the sorcerer did this? Why did he need the souls of children? What happened to the other children (we get to release only one), especially to the daughter of the Silver Magician for example? It seems it's been put together in a hurry and feels rushed, even though it successfully manages to keep up the scary atmosphere all the way till the end.

I liked the relatively frequent cutscenes (usually after achieving something important) that added a lot to the interactiveness and also strengthened the adventure factor of the game. Speaking of interactiveness, I don't know how ordinary it is in hidden object games, but there were some unusual occurrences (the mob setting the wagon ablaze while we are in a shop) that created some new sources from which we were able to acquire items needed for progress. Unfortunately these events were scarce. Item and inventory management isn't overcomplicated, so be warned: it is still far from the complexity and depth of a classic adventure game.

The hidden object scenes are nicely done, it's not too hard to find any of the required items (with the exception of one towards the end). The problem here was also present in Crystals of Time: you'll have to backtrack and reuse the exact same hidden object scene a second or third time to find something. So if you play on expert difficulty and get stuck, make sure to check back on previously visited hidden object scenes. The minigames were enjoyable and often tricky to solve, wish there were more of these.

I realize this game was originally designed for tablets and smart phones, so the graphic design should be judged accordingly. The game has nice, stylish graphics with moderate effects (sparks and lighting), but the quality of the cutscenes isn't that great since they are basically still images with dynamic elements zooming in and out.
The music is conventionally good, with mysterious tunes along with sound effects that are perfectly good for these kinds of games, that creates the necessary mysterious atmosphere.

The game also contains a bonus chapter (that's why it's marked as collector's edition), the Bella Island Adventure which is a pretty good additional adventure and is the continuation of the main game, where the player has to discover a mysterious island and free a young witch who has also been cursed by the Puppet Thief. It prolongs the 4-5 hours long playthrough of the main story with another 1.

Fairy Tale Mysteries: The Puppet Thief is solid, well made hidden object game which I would recommend to anyone who likes or wishes to try this genre despite its plotholes and minor faults.

PROS
+ not so hard hidden object challenges with very good minigames
+ stylish graphics, relatively many cutscenes
+ good atmosphere

CONS
- flaws in the story, despite the possibilites
- too few minigames

RATING
6.5/10

The Magnificent Kickpuncher

Posted: 20 May
I've only played two hidden object games thus far, but if the general quality of those two is in any way indicative of the genre in general, I may be better off stopping at two. I received a roughly a hundred of these for about three fifty from one of those ever present online bundles, and it seemed like a good time to try the genre out as I have someone missed out on them up to this point. And while I might not know much about hidden object games, I can still tell that Fairy Tale Mysteries: The Puppet Thief isn't a very good one.

This is a hidden object game, so it makes sense that the core gameplay feature is hidden object scenes. Between the main game and extra quest, there are over thirty of these scenes in total. The game will present you with a cluttered collection of objects, which tells you both that it's time to find some stuff and that the game may have a pretty serious hoarding problem. Each scene gives you nine different things to find (with some objects appearing more than once in the scene) and you need to scour the scene to find them, or give up and use some of the copious hints the game gives you if you really like hidden object games but hate hidden objects for some reason.

The scenes themselves are fine. Thirty in total is a decent amount (especially in comparison to the paultry amount offered in the sequel to this game), but it feels like another ten to twenty were needed for the price they're charging. The scenes themselves are fairly bland and while the objects themselves are fairly well hidden, the overall scenes are simply uninteresting. Furthermore, the game doesn't really integrate these into the gameplay very well, and they just sort of pop up every once in a while when the game gets bored. They also reuse the same scene frequently, and after you clear a scene the first time, about half of the time you need to come back later in the game to do the very same scene with some new objects to find. Not only does this just seem lazy, it requires pointless backtracking and if you ever get stuck, the solution usually is to poke around all your old hidden object scenes and find out which on is open for business again.

Beyond the hidden objects, the rest of the game is flat out bad. There are a handful of logic puzzles, but there isn't a single one that is even mildly clever. The very best can only be described as "mildly annoying" requiring you to rotate things to get them in the right order, while the most are so simple it takes all of ten seconds to figure them out. "Match the colors" or "figure out the simple pattern" isn't a logic puzzle - that's something you do to make sure you're ready to pass the first grade.

And the story is just absurdly bad. It has an interesting and spooky premise, with some evil force kidnapping kids and cramming their souls into puppets. How terrifying does that sound? How does this game manage to make that concept so boring? They really deserve a reward for making the terrifying mundane, because this level of botching a story really needs to be recognized for how acceptional it is. It could be that the visuals are laughably bad, but it really is more than that. There is no development, no real world building or anything like that. Sure, this is just a silly little hidden object game, but why put in a story if you aren't going to try and make it entertaining then?

So the hidden object scenes are only "okay" and everything else is terrible. This may find some appeal if you are an absolute, die hard hidden object game fan, and have already played every other single hidden object game. Beyond that demographic though, this simply isn't worth the price. Maybe if you find it in a bundle. But, hey, I found it in a bundle and wish something else had been bundled with the rest of the games instead. Like a rock or a root canal.
