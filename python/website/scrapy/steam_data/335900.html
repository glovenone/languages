About This Game

What is Angels of Fasaria?:

Angels of Fasaria was originally released in 2005. With thousands of downloads and over 48 thousand active users in the browser based game, Angels of Fasaria maintained a loyal following and support base. After the release of "Fasaria World" and later the "Ancients of Fasaria" mmorpg, Angels of Fasaria RPG and the browser based game community was closed and discontinued.

After popular demand and by player's request, we submitted Angels of Fasaria on Greenlight and the community voted it in. It is now re-released on Steam, back and fully revised.

Enjoy the epic adventure that started it all and begin your adventure today!

Angels of Fasaria Game Features:

  • You get Angels of Fasaria 2D RPG.
  • You get one FREE access key to Fasaria World Online (browser Based, rpg, strategy and multiplayer online game).
  • Steam Cloud to back up saved games and user info.
  • Trading cards available for earning badges and backgrounds.

Posted: 23 August, 2015
This is for the offline single-player RPG.

DISCLAIMER: I did not beat this game. I couldn't take any more grinding to get past the probably final dungeon, after the random encounter enemies were wiping me out despite being at level 50+ and having the best armor.

It's stock art, enemies and sound. RPGmaker stock stuff isn't bad but it shows a relative lack of effort. Major enemies and allies share sprites as well - I thought Samson had betrayed me or something but it was actually just some guy who was identical. The plot is relatively standard stuff: You, the sole capable fighter in the kingdom, must fight the entire army of darkness alone. Naked and armed with a long knife.

The main method of unlocking new towns and dungeons is mana collection. You collect mana by playing tag with angels inside of trees and caves. I'm not exaggerating, that's exactly what you do. You need to catch about 10 angels per dungeon (more or less, as the mana gain is randomized) while dodging enemies and going back to respawn the angels at a prayer point. It's pretty tedious.

There's a lot of grinding, but levels are useless aside from skill and HP/MP gain. Equipment is nearly the sole factor in victory or defeat. You also get only a pittance of money for each win. 'Sidequests' involve collecting a rare drop from enemies and turning it in repeatedly for a slightly better XP and cash gain.

The party system has some issues. The main character and his healer friend are permanent, leaving you with two spaces. The first three allies you unlock have the same class, equips and skills with only slightly different stat growths. The next three, halfway into the game, are a clone of the main character, a clone of the healer and a slightly different clone of the main character. There's also party switching. It's broken! If you do it wrong you can permanently lose a character, and even if you do it right your old and new characters lose all levels and gear! Why is this? Many games have figured out dynamic parties, it's not a new thing.

Item crafting is lame. First you have to grind enemies or certain respawning material points (similar to angels, but the ore doesn't run from you). It takes a good deal per item, and you need something like 2700 for a full set. Additionally, you're capped at 99 like every other item so you need to go back from time to time and drop them off. God forbid you pick up a chest full of the stuff without having the space. The worst part of that is that after you collect the raw materials, you need to go 'refine' them. It takes five confirm presses to refine one item. There is no option for mass refinery. So if you want that end-game gear you'd better be ready to stand around and mash Z for a solid hour.

This may be a bug, but on-field enemies don't despawn. You can get caught in normal random battles, and flee or win as normal. But visible enemies pursue you and force you into a no-escape fight if they catch up. Even if you win, they don't go away - just take a step back (if they aren't blocked) and stop chasing you a while. God help you if you get caught in a hallway or have two enemies blocking you in a corner.

Pretty much the only thing this game has going for it is an active and interested creator. Overall I have no idea why you would pay $20 for this game! It might be good to kill ten hours if you got it for free. There are better RPGmaker games available.

I didn't play the mmo yet as I'm hooked on the RPG. Get it if you don't have it as I highly recommend it.

This is one of the best jrpgs I've played and I've been playing rpgs since the first zelda and final fantasy on nintendo.

Story: Very well told and simple story. Nothing is dragged out and made more complicated than need be. The story doesn't choke you with narrative desperately trying to make it's point. The dialogue is brief but impactful when made.

Game Play: The gameplay is straight forward. Anyone who has played video games of any kind should get the mechanics down just fine. The combat skills really turn out pretty nice and each class has a unique way of contributing to the party.

Graphics: Nice, colorful and fantasy. What else do you want?

Fun Factor: One of the best rpgs I've played. Angels of Fasaria has that "feel good" nostalgia of rpg's I've come to love from the past. If you like that old school "good wins", "save the world from evil" type of fluff, then I can say that angels of fasaria has that and more.

Re-play value: You start out with a static male character. You can not change that, he is the story but in spite of that the game is good enough to want to revisit years later. It has that classic feel.

If you played this game through to the end, I believe I speak for all of us when I say: WHEN WILL WE SEE THE SEQUEL? Why hasn't one been made yet?

I believe that Angels of Fasaria stands above a simple score so I want provide one. I'm very well pleased.
