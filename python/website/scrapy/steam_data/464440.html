Annie Amber - A life captured as a VR game

WHEN VIRTUAL REALITY LETS YOU FEEL A (VIRTUAL) REAL LIFE
Annie Amber is a VR game that leads you on an intense and dreamy journey through the life of a person condensed in a surreal and poetic experience. With innovative controls, AA makes you explore this strange and beautiful scenery that is life itself while using your wit to make your way through different memories.

LIFE IS POETRY, SO IS ANNIE AMBER
But the life of Annie Amber isn’t told you in a plain way. Memories are symbolic, fragmented and complex, so the journey will be full of subtle beauty and poetry. Through sad and powerful piano tracks, we will solve puzzles that will slowly unlock Annie Amber’s memories. The result is a game as minimalistic as intense: overwhelmed by huge celestial bodies, we will get to know and understand Annie Amber as if she were a close friend of us.

ANNIE AMBER MEETS VIVE
We have adapted Annie Amber to HTC VIVE so you can play it at its best. We have created a new locomotion system that uses the motion controllers. Annie Amber not only presents a new control system that goes better with VIVE’s specs, but we have also added a new playability layer: some levels presents a unique extra feature. These small features are a new way of conveying Annie Amber’s emotions in every moment of her life. We’re going even deeper into her mind, to the point we become one with her. VIVE has let us make the player become one with Annie Amber.
Be ready to experience a life captured as a game.


NO VIVE? NO PROBLEM. YOUR EYES ARE THE ULTIMATE IMMERSIVE CONTROLLER!
Annie Amber can be played with no controllers aside the VR device itself. The way of travelling through the memories is by means of your own eyes. This innovative system matches the minimalistic and subtle approach of the whole game, while enhancing the immersion of the player. With no external controllers at all, the player will be a silent explorer while also being able of experiencing VR at its fullest.


WELCOME TO VIRTUAL REALITY
The universality of its narrative and the accessibility of its innovative controlling system make Annie Amber not only a product for all kinds of audience, but also an excellent introduction to virtual reality and videogames. A whole new world of possibilities is about to emerge from this technology, and Annie Amber is a beautiful and intuitive way of embracing it for the first time.

About This Game

ANNIE AMBER – A LIFE CAPTURED AS A VR PUZZLE GAME



WHEN VIRTUAL REALITY LETS YOU FEEL A (VIRTUAL) REAL LIFE
Annie Amber is a VR puzzle game that leads you on an intense and dreamy journey through the life of a person condensed in a surreal and poetic experience. With innovative controls, AA makes you explore this strange and beautiful scenery that is life itself while using your wit to make your way through different memories.

LIFE IS POETRY, SO IS ANNIE AMBER
But the life of Annie Amber isn’t told you in a plain way. Memories are symbolic, fragmented and complex, so the journey will be full of subtle beauty and poetry. Through sad and powerful piano tracks, youwill solve puzzles that will slowly unlock Annie Amber’s memories. The result is a game as minimalistic as intense: overwhelmed by huge celestial bodies, we will get to know and understand Annie Amber (fictional character) as if she were a close friend of us.


USE YOUR MOTION CONTROLLERS!
Annie Amber is meant to be player with motion controllers. Use them to travel and interact with the environment and the puzzle elements. On the other hand, if you don't have motion controllers you can still play the game activating the mode without motion controllers. This innovative system matches the minimalistic and subtle approach of the whole game, while enhancing the immersion of the player. With no external controllers at all (aside from your hands or your head), the player will be a silent explorer while also being able of experiencing VR at its fullest.


WELCOME TO VIRTUAL REALITY
The universality of its narrative and the accessibility of its innovative controlling system make Annie Amber not only a product for all kinds of audience, but also an excellent introduction to virtual reality and videogames. A whole new world of possibilities is about to emerge from this technology, and Annie Amber is a beautiful and intuitive way of embracing it for the first time while also being good enough for VR pros.

And then the planets just keep coming, and you are orbiting or on some of the most beautiful representations of the planets I have ever seen.

So recommended for a beautiful, moody and awe inspiring experience. Not recommended for pure gameplay or puzzles. I personally loved the experience.

2.1 hrs on record
Posted: 17 June
So the game (or walking/floating simulator if you prefer) has you drifting between points (a bit too slowly for my liking) and occasionally maybe sort of solving a puzzle? There's one point near the end of the game that's definitely a puzzle, but other than that it's mostly looking for the next point to pull yourself along. Then there are a bunch of really boring and annoying trial and error "puzzles" which the game would greatly benefit from having removed. They seemed to be there just to pad a very short experience. (About 1 hour I think. I left the game on while I had dinner so the 2 hours is inaccurate). Also, the movement is likely to make you motion sick if you're prone to that in VR.
As you can see from the screenshots there is some interesting imagery, and also a very nice soundtrack. These are definitely the best things about the game, but anything more than a free experience needs more than that in my opinion. The point of the visuals of course are to tell the story of a woman's life. Problem is, unless I'm being thick and didn't pick up on some deeper meaning, the story is very uninteresting. I only picked up on one dramatic moment (aside from inevitable growing old and dying) that's about as basic and uninteresting a plot point as you could possibly imagine. I don't think the way it's told is particularly engaging either, just seems like a very limp attempt at environmental storytelling (failed to give me any connection to a character) that's just an excuse for some interesting visuals.
So yeah. Maybe pick it up if you want to float around some cool scenes with nice music when it's on sale. I think that this game would be much better re-purposed as a short and free VR music video or something. My favourite moment of the game was the very end just floating through on a gentle abstract rollercoaster listening to the nice piano music.

Edit: I've since been informed that the puzzles that I solved through trial and error were in fact not trial and error. If this has puzzled you too (look away if you don't want the solution spoiled) you must count the number of elements depicted in the images there in the scene. I've also been informed that the story is supposed to be a pretty standard life. The issue I had, if it is unclear in the review, is that the method of storytelling used in this game failed to connect with me personally. This may be different for you.

Two full playthroughs done. Enjoyed more the second one because with the experience of the first one I really watched details I missed the first time. Wow. Just wow. But let's start from the beginning.

Motion Sickness: No motion sickness.

I saw some bad reviews of the game complaining about motion sickness and I was worried about that. I haven't grown my VR legs yet (my Vive experience is quite limited). While the locomotion system is not a teleporting one, it is really easy to get used to it and it has an advantage. You can really feel the volume and depth of the enviroment while traveling from sphere to sphere and I thing you get more... positional sence, if that makes any sence at all.

Now about the game itself.

Annie Amber lets you experience floating memories of someone, in some key moments of her life: from childhood, to well... spoiler alert. People die at the end of their lifes. While the environments at the beginning are childish, often I found that while revisiting them I could observe much more connected meaning with the late environments. For example, there is certain picture that appears late in the game, wich is a key memory for Annie. In the second level you can actually see the place where the picture was taken if you pay enough atention to the footsteps.

What can I say. It really touched something inside of me.

In terms of gameplay it is not a walking simulator, there are some puzzles but they are easy and not too much challenging, and I think that is exactly what the developer wanted to do. Don't buy this if you want puzzles to get stuck or to challenge you like other games from the same developers like Mind: path to the thalamus. The soundtrack and the environments are what make Annie Amber a truly awesome experience. It is all about the little interactions and the little details.

Also, I've been thinking about it, and it is perfect to show VR to family and the little ones. Maybe it is not the most motion controller interactive product out there, but the vistas and the landscapes are impressive and for someone who hasn't tried VR I think they will be impressed when they find themselfs in VR floating for the first time in nearby, I don't know, the first planet (I think it is neptune) while listening to a relaxing soundtrack and watching some child's drawing floating nearby. Good job devs.
