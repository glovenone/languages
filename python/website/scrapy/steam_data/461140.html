About This Game

Welcome Kids and sweet-guzzlers! Let me introduce you Klabi, the sweet cat, who has a sweet tooth. This little rascal loves sweets and big, very big - no - HUGE SWEET CAKES!

This game is all about sweets as you might have realized and you can make Klabi's sweet dream(s) come true. In this simple platformer, you play Klabi in his dream through levels of raining sugar, dropping calorie bombs and sticky sticks of rocks called candy sticks, meow!

Attention Kids! Klabi's dream can turn into a sour nightmare before he reaches his goal to collect all the sweets to build his enormous pie. Because other little sweet-guzzlers appear and want to have a piece of the cake before Klabi even has all the ingredients.

Holy smokes. I was wondering how poor games can get on Steam, and bought this.

First impression? Obviously QUALITY CONTROL - M.I.A! Seriously, if I had the choice, I would not have passed this game. The fact that the original asking price is 0,99€ for this sounds like a joke. I'd say this game has, at most, the standard of a mediocre Gamedev 101 course exercise, not beyond that.

The game is riddled with badly tested, ill-designed and/or nonfunctional mechanics. The character can fall through moving platforms with no reason whatsoever. Jump and movement mechanics, while not exhibiting the vertical direction lock as in Action 52, are not as fluid as they could be. Objects are misleading - a candy bar can lead to death when rotated to one side, but not when to an another? Store mechanics seem to work, but the GUI is hastily done as texts do not indicate their user interface behavior, either by mouseover color change or any other means. The store seems to work somewhat, but due to the problematic mechanics, I have not been able to test them to their full potential. The store, and dying also induces major lag, which has crashed the game several times when I tested it.

Music is.. eh, practically nonexistent - the only thing I heard is the same short piano clip as in the trailer - but only in the main menu. I've been unable to turn music on for the main game, even though I've clicked the button for it. Sound effects do also exist, but are very repetitive. As of graphics, they seem to generally have some relation as to what they represent - however, the lack of graphics design is painfully obvious with cheesy, non-uniform environments and obviously stretched textures.

The idea does have potential, I give it that. But the frankly atrocious implementation renders the game unable to render any reasonable value to its player beyond being comic relief for its bad design.

The maker of this game needs major remedial education in programming, testing, game design.. perhaps music and arts classes could also do positive things for the possible future ventures of the author. If one is not interested in supporting the developer in their development with this kind of show for the work, or buying poor games for comic relief, they should ask for a refund.
