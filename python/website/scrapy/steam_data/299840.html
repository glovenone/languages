About This Game

Starships dropping thousands of combat machines on the planet's surface. Battles are conducted continuously. New warriors comes again and again to replace the fallens. Millions tons of metal has covered the land. War is never ends on this planet - Metal Planet.

The game is set on a distant planet called Metal Planet. Two corporations are fighting for control of a valuable resource capable of transforming matter. You take on the role of a soldier with equipped power armor and powerful weapon.

Features:

  • Fight against AI and other players in a multiplayer game.
  • 4 game modes.
  • 3-class fighters.
  • Modules to improve armor and weapons.
  • Huge bosses for co-op mode.
  • Lasers and smart ricochet bullets.
  • And BFG!

Hurricane shooting, tons of explosives, destructible environments, boss battles and more in Metal Planet.

Posted: 17 June, 2014
Metal Planet is a MP shooter in the vain of the classic Unreal Tournament games. Players are split into two teams and dropped into a map, with the goal of capturing all the spawn points and killing off the other team until they can no longer spawn back. For all it takes from the classic Unreal games however, it loses absolutely all the polish, balance, and addictive quality that made those such timeless shooters, leaving you with a buggy and confusing menagerie of incomplete mechanics and unpolished design.

The largest issue with Metal Planet is how absolutely unintuitive everything about it manages to be. The simple task of firing weapons and using abilities are never explained or even hinted at, leaving you to fumble about on the keyboard hitting buttons in the hope they do something (the outcome of which is even more confounding). Should you figure out what the various skills do, matches quickly divulge into a completely unbalanced back and forth of who can stun the other person first. It's frustrating and dull at the best of times, and completely destroys any hope of an even match.

I would like to say there's some hidden quality, some element of Metal Planet that keeps it a flawed but sometimes enjoyable gem, but there really isn't. Every aspect of the game is entirely ripped from other games, then melted down into an almost unrecognizable mess of broken and unenjoyable mechanics (not to mention the obnoxious visuals and single audio track). If I hadn't been playing with a group of friends I doubt I would have lasted more than a few minutes with this pile of rubbish (not that I could have, given the community is entirely dead), and would advise you to give it the widest of berths.

This game is a very clearly low-budget affair, with poor translations (I believe from Russian) and a less-than-polished UI. Graphics are nothing special, but they will probably run on just about anything when turned down all the way at least, and the aesthetic isn't offensively bad or anything. The game technically features AI if you want to play in single player, but even as someone who normally likes playing against AI I found it mind-numbingly boring. The mechanics are barely interesting. Kindly put they'd be described as functional, less kindly as soulless.

I could overlook all the little flaws at a low price if it weren't for the fact that no one is playing this. Without real people to play with I can't really recommend this at any price. According to SteamCharts the most people playing at once ever is a grand total of three, not even enough to fill a single game. The game is just not fun enough by yourself to be worth a purchase, and there's little chance that the player population will improve. It may be more fun if the player population picks up, so should that happen it may be worth your time and money to give it a try.
