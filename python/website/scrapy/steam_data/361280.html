About This Game

Turmoil offers players a visually charming, tongue-in-cheek take on the simulation genre inspired by the 19th century oil rush in North America. Get a taste of the rush and rivalry of the time as you earn your way to become a successful oil entrepreneur. As you make money digging up and selling oil, the town will grow along with you.

Lease land at the town auction and search for oil. Build a rig, create an efficient pipe network and bring up the oil to store it in silos. Sell the oil at the best times to maximize profits. Then buy essential upgrades in town to cope with rock, gas and ice. Acquire more town shares than your competitors in a bid to become the new mayor.

  • A campaign where you build your rags-to-riches oil baron career and beat your rivals.
  • Dozens of upgrades and new tools to improve your oil mining operations.
  • Meet the town’s characters in the saloon and make shady business deals.
  • Play single game mode to get one of literally millions of procedurally generated levels and earn as much as you can in one year.
  • Steam Achievements, Badges, Trading Cards and Cloud Saving.
  • Lease land at the town auction and search for oil using a dowser, mole or scanner.
  • Maximize your profits by using natural gas to boost the oil price.
  • Outbid your competitors at the stock auction.

Many smaller, simpler games often fail because they have difficulty managing to stay challenging with a relatively simple premise--but this isn't one of those. This is one of those games that have the great combination of 'easy to learn, hard to master' going and manages to keep on your toes.

The idea is simple, drill for oil and sell it. But with that simple premise you still have a lot of things that keep you always challenged:

Do you sell low and not worry about overflow or storage? If you choose to stockpile, you might run out of time and not get to sell the oil at all.

You never know where the oil is, so how much will you invest in exploring for oil? If you don't, you might lose thousands of dollars in oil, but you might also waste thousands of dollars fruitlessly searching for oil that isn't there.

How much money will you save for making important game winning purchases later in the game? (Won't get into that too much because, spoilers). If you save it all, you're going to miss out on important upgrades, but you could lose out on important things later on if you don't have any left.

Best $10 game I've played in a LONG time. Buy it, at the very least it's a great 5-10 hours of fun.

Turmoil Review
Disclaimer-I have received this game free of charge for reviewing purposes. Note that this will not impact the quality of this review, which will contain only my personal opinion on the game.

Turmoil is a lovely, stylish and surprisingly strategic little game, throwing players into a heated competition for oil where timing and decision-making is of paramount importance.

Gameplay 8.5/10, an enjoyable blend of resource management and good pacing, like a nice cup of coffee.
Turmoil revolves around the simple principle of extracting and selling oil for profit. The tutorial is concise and swiftly guides players' way around the game's mechanics. Players start a level by auctioning on a plot of land, then proceed to drill for oil with an initial budget of $2,000. The player is tasked with the task of drilling to the correct depth to find oil deposits underground and carefully managing their investments to maximise profits.

Oil can be sold to either the two aptly named businesses on the left and right side of the screen, these two stores buy oil at constantly fluctuating prices, prompting the player to keep an eye on the screen. I found myself constantly monitoring the two stores to make sure I could sell my oil for a fair price, simultaneously making sure that the oil reserves had not run dry or if I had bought enough horses to shuttle away the oil from the rig. Neglecting the oil accumulated in the rig will lead to a spillage, costing the player a hefty fine.

The player has until the end of an in-game year to gain as much profit as possible, which will be compared with the 3 other AI competitors. The cycle continues as players begin to earn enough money to afford upgrades to improve their efficiency, such as increasing the capacity of drilling rigs, storage silos and the oil carriages, or speeding up the dowsers. These upgrades are all situational and players will find that some unlocks, while being faster, can impact their economy. A great way to ensure the full and proper usage of all the tools provided to the player.

The game's mechanics are unique because the player's success can be determined by timing. Players who use their time wisely and effectively will be rewarded.

Story N/A
Although the NPC's are each given names and their own tone, the game's focus is clearly not on the story and a good story simply isn't needed for these types of games. In fact, the addition of a story would probably be far-fetched and wouldn't benefit the game.

Visuals and Style 10/10, playful, consistent and charming
Turmoil is set during the events of the oil rush, typically portrayed to be set in the "wild west". The game has a witty sense of humour and provides players with a comical, tongue-in-cheek approach to the oil rush. The visuals are a perfect fit for the game and all of this is backed by the game's soothing soundtrack. Without being over-ambitious, Turmoil brings a unique, light-hearted style to the table.

Conclusion

Pros:
+Exceptional style
+Enjoyable pacing
+Innovative game mechanics, properly executed
+Poses as a realistic challenge for the player
+Thorough tutorial and adequate player guidance
+A fitting price tag

Cons:
-No story (arguable)
-Can sometimes be extremely punishing (arguable)
-Can only be played in short bursts as gameplay can get repetitive after a while

Verdict: 18.5/20, a unique, humble game with a lovable style and a great price tag.
