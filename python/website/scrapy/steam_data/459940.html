About This Game

"Itâs a story about guilt, sacrifice and the love of wildlifeâ¦"

Deer Man is a short Interactive storytelling experience. In the winters of 2015, Jacob took a break from his busy and noisy life by visiting his parents in America. Sticking to his usual routine, he took his first morning run in the nearby woods. It was freezing and extremely quiet but Jacob wasn't alone.

You play as Jacob's memory, where you will have an incredible encounter with Deer Man & the dark side of his love for the wildlife.

So, are you ready for a run?


Key Features

  • An original story Inspired from the wildlife, The Virunga National Park in Congo, where rangers are risking their lives to protect morning gorillas and the rest of the wildlife from the poachers.
  • Find items, examine and dive in with the emotions as the story unfolds.
  • Take the experience to another level with Oculus DK2 + CV1 (Supported & Tested)
  • Beautiful Soundtrack by Mahesh Raghvan

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 8 (64 bit)
    • Processor: Intel Core i3
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Intel(R) HD Graphics 4000
    • Storage: 900 MB available space

Product received for free
From Andrew Jackson Jihad comes their next album: Deer Man. Featuring exciting new songs such as:
"The Heimo Korth of Drunk Driving" and "White Snow, Black Antlers".

Deer Man is one of those walkey talkey games that your average /v/ user gets really upset at for existing. As far as walkey talkey games go this one is alright.
You walk around a minimalist forest environment while your player character narrates things in past tense. You walk around these small spaces and click on things until you trigger the next sequence. Segmenting bits of the game into chunks assists the storytelling aspect, which is likely what you were looking forward to.
Its a simple yet engaging story with a cheesy message.

This game actually has replayability to it, involving collectables, an axe I never figured out what to do with (unless my character already used it in the past and the story is actually an endless cycle of people getting chased and becoming Deer Men) and in general its always interesting to play the game again once you have seen the ending.

The dev gave me this game for free and so perhaps my steam recomendation is biased. Who knows. Either way you made a solid little game and there aint much wrong with it. And even if there was, who am I to judge? Critics are nerds anyways.
