What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Prior the release of the game, we would like to ensure, that everything is functioning properly. We would also like to receive more feedback on our game mechanics from a wider audience. This game has been on preliminary testing for a while, and now we have decided to introduce it to a wider audience. Our team is very dynamic and we value opinions of every player.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We are planning to release this game in autumn of 2016. We do understand that we are creating a complex and a multi-faceted game with a huge open world, where social connections between players are elaborate. During the Early Access, we are hoping to finish up with all the game mechanics and we are hoping to finish it by the time fall arrives. (This sentence is repetitive and awkward, esp. the last bit)”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We offer a relatively complete version of the game for the Early Access. Needless to say that in the future we will be adding new territories, vehicles, and various types of settlements. Here is a list of thing that we definitely will not change:
  • We are not planning a wipe. There will be no wipe;
  • We are not planning to stop giving out gold in the game every hour, for this is an essential part of game economy;
  • We are not planning to change the gold price;
  • We are not planning to change methods of development of your character;
In general, we are going to carefully attend to our players and try to bring their wishes to reality, in order make the game even more enjoyable.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“This is a stable version of the game with properly functioning mechanics. We have spend a considerable amount of time in both open and close testing. We have rather frequent updates, and are trying our best to respond to playersâ inquiries as fast and attentive as possible.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“No, prices in the game will not change. Our game is an accurate illustration for the term free-2-play: we do not sell any premium content for the game, gold is the only item that we sell in this game, thought any other player can do that as well.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are always interested in feedback from our players. We are promptly and permanently present on the game forum, both during day and night: http://forum.luckcatchers.ru [RU/EN], we have VK page http://vk.com/luckcatchersmmo
Every fortnight we are giving away one of the unique maps of the game world and a dozen of coins.
The players who are to receive these prizes are selected randomly.”

About This Game

This is a social-economic sandbox RPG, based on fantasy/steam-punk novels by A.Pehov, yet actions in the game take place 300 years before those described in the novels.

This is a huge, savage and realistic world without any rules. The World is opened to any player without any limits from the very start. This is a World of steam, valor and magic, full of risky adventures and profitable calculations. This is a World of trade, economic wars and hard decisions. This is a World of a magnificent, endless and free sky.

Features

  • Really huge world without loading zones. Over 30 NPC-ruled cities where you can trade, take or offer jobs, repair your aircrafts and planes;
  • True player-based world trade system, over 100 goods for trade including currency, even Premium Gold!
  • Regular game events - lottery, migration of dragons, plane races;
  • Real labor market - players can officially hire other players;
  • Non-class or linear skill-based character development system - decide at any time whom you want to be: a trader, a pirate, a hunter, a farmer or a cargo carrier;
  • Flexible reputation system protecting you and your settlements in the safe city regions;
  • Construct your settlements (castles, mines, villages and other);
  • Extract and process minerals (8 types of basic minerals);
  • Produce weapons, ammunition and flight equipment;
  • Build your ships and planes based on your own drawings;
  • Improve your skills and study new ones to advance in military and industrial spheres, to pilot new types of ships and planes and to use heavier weapons;
  • Discover, conquer or "diplomatically annex" any territories;
  • Become a Governor and get rich by taxing other players on your land;
  • Get 5 Premium Gold coins for every hour in the game to build aircrafts or develop your settlements and characters.

Ok so a few hours invested in this game, it's about time I gave it a review.

Firstly, keep in mind when reading any review, positive or negative, that this is an early access game in it's alpha state. It is NOT a finished product and all info given in reviews is liable to be rendered inaccurate at some point.

The good:
Ok let's start with the description. Note this from the game devs -> "This is a social-economic sandbox RPG". So if you are looking for a fast paced PvP game or action packed adventure game, stop reading now and go away. This game requires patience, time, a little intelligence and certainly commitment.

This is a game where you work, trade, hunt or just blag your way to fortune. You start will a little airship hanging from a balloon and you set off into a world that is 500x500 miles in size to make that fortune. There are 2 playable races. The fantastic awesome Orcs, and the pink skinned lilly livered humans.
Game progression is simple. You take some jobs, deliveries mostly. You float across the sky in your balloon and complete your job. You get paid some copper and get a little reputation. Rinse and repeat until you can afford a bigger better airship or two (yes you can command a whole fleet of them with skills). Then you can take on bigger jobs or try your hand at buying and trading in consumables between cities.
A little further on, a little more money, and you can establish a settlement of your own. Produce your own resources, airships, weapons, ammo etc etc. All can be traded on the market or directly from your own store.
Another viable option is hunting dragons. The other denizens of the world. Trophies from dragons are used in crafting some of the more advanced items in the game and are in high demand.

Level advancement is done in the Eve Online fashion (one of many similarities). You train a skill over time, even when offline. XP earned from skill training increases your level. Simples. There are crafting skills, pilot skills, magic skills, resource gathering etc etc. There are not too many at the moment but there are enough for you to specialize.

The community is great. At least the English speaking one. There is a large Russian player base too, doing what Russians do, but I have had no real interaction with them. The Dev team is really good. They are constantly updating the game and helping players out. They have a passion for the game, and that is usually one of the most important traits a dev can have. They want you to tell them what's wrong, and then they fix it. Communication can be difficult, but not impossible.

Combat-wise there is not much. Apart from fighting/hunting dragons, there are also the pirates and rogue players. So far not a problem to me but they will be. Combat can be done with the auto attack feature or by manually aiming your weapons groups. There are also planes that you can launch from your ship like a carrier that will defend you, or you can station them in your settlement and have them fly combat air patrols over it. You can also launch as a pilot of one of these planes.

The economy is a work in progress. There is one, but it's a bit iffy with the small player base and the early players dominating the best settlement spots. Prices can be ridiculous too. That will change as more players join and there is more competition. There are 3 currencies in game. I shall simplifie them as copper, silver and gold. Gold is a premium currency you can buy. And it's actually dirt cheap really. You can trade all currency for one of the others but the prices are stupid in some areas. You need to look about for the best deals.

The Bad:
Well, there are certainly going to be no prizes for graphics. They are minimal at best. But, as you spend most of your time high in the air there is really no call for high level ground detail. So it actually works just fine. And will run quite happily on most machines.

The economy is a bit broken, as I said above. However there are many players trying to improve this by selling their wares at sensible prices instead of the greedy ones. It will take time but it will happen. The majority of the community is interested in promoting the game and making it accessible to new players. Including the prices they have to pay when starting out.

Server restarts are a pain as they reset the consumables on the market and you have to wait for them to restock. This is an unfortunate consequence of the Devs being very active in fixing and updating things. We just have to suck it up.

Scammers are present. Some of the jobs you will see are just unrealistic. Very high deposits that require you to take ridiculous loads over long distances. I would not expect to reach your destination, you will probably be jumped on the way so you fail the job and the job poster gets your gold. There are also players with names I can not pronounce who like to add newbies as enemies in the hope they will attack them in a safe zone etc. They get nothing financially if you do, just a good laugh.

The game has no useful tutorial really and can be very confusing when starting out. It shows some tips but they can be a little bewildering and misleading. The whole translation of the game to English is seriously bad. The names of some items are just strange or wrong. There are a lot of things that just won't make sense at all or give the wrong information. Devs do seem to be fixing this when its pointed out though.

Conclusion:
I recommend this game if you are the sort of player who likes a relaxing game and doesn't mind the slow pace and time required. Economists and traders will love it. If you are a more combative type, then you probably won't like it much unless you go down the pirate route. That's your call. Even then though, combat you have isn't going to be fast paced and intensive. You are going to have to chase down targets over distance in a slow moving airship and work for your kills. And probably be hunted by a lot of others in return.
The game is rough around the edges and needs a lot of improvement. However it has 3 things that make it stand out. An active and passionate dev team, a great community and a whole boat load of potential. With some major improvements in certain areas, this is going to be a classic for players who like this premise. It's free to play so just try it. Introduce yourself in game and you will get a warm welcome and a lot of help. (Please though, read the guides and watch the star vid, we do get sick of answering the "how do I play?" question every 20 minutes) My in game name is Crom Cruach, feel free to add me on steam or in game if you require any help. Not sure it will be of much use, but still :)

*Read to the bottom for an understanding of why I'm recommending*

The game has a long way to go to be playable. While the features currently in the game are on their way to being acceptable, there seems to be a lot missing. The user interface needs some help and can make the game physcially difficult to play. The concept is refreshing and fun though and I have hope for this game if the developers continue. I go into further detail below:

Features Issues:
- Flight is difficult to manage, as you have to click a move key, then click the mouse on the ground. However, that doesn't always register in certain "dead zones" on the landscape.
- Lacking tutorials. The tutorials at the beginning of the game are hard to read (more on that later) and the names don't always match the interface (such as "job" being "contract") .
- Overall lacking explanation. Why are certain engines called "demons"? At least I think they're engines because I can't tell what else they do because of a lack of information on the world as a whole. Additionally, the strekoleks are airplanes. It took me about half an hour to discover that on accident, having previously read the name several times with no idea what it was.

Interface Issues:
- The text is tiny
- The screen feels pushed towards the center and poorly optimized for larger screens
- Tutorial windows need to be close to the view of the user, though they are scaled with the objects in game. They should appear almost like a menu, overlaying everything else and much larger.

Pros:
- Great concept
- Airship idea is great
- RPG/Strategy mix is a nice touch
- Base-building is nice to see in a game (rare)
- The idea of commanding a fleet is cool

Bottom Line:
This game needs polish and refinement. It needs more features, a better map and better interface with widescreen support. Also, the controls need to be reconsidered. Otherwise, it stands a chance of being a wonderfully exciting game. I am recommending this because of its concept and potential. This concept is rare and we really need more "thinking" games like this that actually let us experience a world and econimic/strategic features without being confined to the traditional "Find resources, build soldiers, throw soldiers at enemy" scheme.

Please note that this review is solely based off of the first impressions I had in the first 2 and a half hours after played this title, and despite the "early access" label this game has been given, will give my thoughts on the current build, that being dated on July 3rd 2016.

The idea of piloting a zepplin in a majorly steampunk universe with the goal of providing commerce to the towns of this seemingly expansive world appealed greatly to me. As such, I decided to dive blindly into this game to see what it offered.

For the most part, the game looks relatively pretty, despite the moderately low texture resolution, even on the highest graphics settings offered. Ship models look very nice, and for the most part, control as expected, if not just a little clunky at times. The only real complaint I have with the graphics is anti-aliasing. Shadows for this game need serious improvement for a day-night cycle system this game has.

For an airship game, the controls, as I said before, operate as expected, but I found them to be rather spread out. To control the ship, for example, you use the arrow keys instead of the traditional WASD bindings. While this normally wouldn't be a complaint from me, you're forced to use your mouse in order to not only drag the screen, but to also select objects. And without, from what I've been able to see so far, an ability to change keybindings to something more comfortable, you can't do much. You can, however, use the mouse solely to not only control your ship, but other options as well. Problem is, with a game that totes the ability to explore, I'd much rather control the ship with button controls rather than click a location and go from there. To me, it's not very feasible at the current moment.

The gameplay itself is rather confusing as well. According to what I've seen on my HUD, there's supposed to be a leveling system. However, I've been unable to figure out how to successfully level up. There's no clear indication on how to level, nor is there a tutorial on what to do in order to do so. I figured the progression would be something like this: Get contracts from a port ---> navigate and deliver said contracts to the appropriate location ---> Obtain rewards and gain experience to raise your level. Basically typical MMORPG leveling. Because it isn't clear in this regard, I've been thus far unable to figure it out. Everyone in chat I've talked to has said to look at the guides on Steam to get an idea as to what to do. Problem is, and I can't really stress this enough, a player SHOULD NOT have to look to an outside source to learn about BASIC FUNDAMENTALS OF A GAME. This should be something in the game ITSELF that's explained, guides outside should explain more complicated stuff, not basic things.

With that said, I cannot recommend this game at the current build. Hopefully the devs refine the current content and systems than what they are now, because, to me, they're very frusterating at this current time.

When I started the game, I hated it for the first 2 hours. Things were difficult to figure out, but with the help of the community (awesome community for the most part), I was able to get on my feet. Here are some of the things that I like about the game:

  • Huge map.
  • Dragon Hunting.
  • Crate looting.
  • Settlement management.
  • Variety of ships.
  • Skills keep training even when you are logged off (much like Eve Online).
  • Good community.

Here are some of the things that I don't like or caused me problems:

  • Some of the interfaces are not intuitive. For example, the ship setup, weapon grouping (specifically setting the weapons to autofire). It wasn't immediately clear on how to do that.
  • When you have an item selected in the market DO NOT CHAT!!! Because when you press enter in chat with a market item selected, it will buy that item.
  • There is no skill learning queue like in Eve Online. You can only learn one skill at a time, which is cool, but I would like to be able to stack up multiple skills to learn one after another as long as I have the gold to do it. This way, you can get the 20 minute, 1 hour, and other short sklls out of the way when you are logged out. Eve does this and it works pretty well.
  • When you discontinue learning a skill you don't get to start where you left off. You have to buy it again and then it starts from the beginning. I would like to see a pause and resume on skill learning so if you need another skill in a hurry, you can pause a long running skill training to temporarily switch to another skill training. In real life, I don't learn one subject at a time and forget everything I knew about it when I go to another class.

That's it! The game is very nice looking and very complex, which I love! I am having a lot of fun and now am trying to figure out how to earn more gold in-game instead of spending real money purchasing it. Speaking of that, they made gold pretty cheap on their store if you need that to give you a boost.

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 July
*Read to the bottom for an understanding of why I'm recommending*

The game has a long way to go to be playable. While the features currently in the game are on their way to being acceptable, there seems to be a lot missing. The user interface needs some help and can make the game physcially difficult to play. The concept is refreshing and fun though and I have hope for this game if the developers continue. I go into further detail below:

Features Issues:
- Flight is difficult to manage, as you have to click a move key, then click the mouse on the ground. However, that doesn't always register in certain "dead zones" on the landscape.
- Lacking tutorials. The tutorials at the beginning of the game are hard to read (more on that later) and the names don't always match the interface (such as "job" being "contract") .
- Overall lacking explanation. Why are certain engines called "demons"? At least I think they're engines because I can't tell what else they do because of a lack of information on the world as a whole. Additionally, the strekoleks are airplanes. It took me about half an hour to discover that on accident, having previously read the name several times with no idea what it was.

Interface Issues:
- The text is tiny
- The screen feels pushed towards the center and poorly optimized for larger screens
- Tutorial windows need to be close to the view of the user, though they are scaled with the objects in game. They should appear almost like a menu, overlaying everything else and much larger.

Pros:
- Great concept
- Airship idea is great
- RPG/Strategy mix is a nice touch
- Base-building is nice to see in a game (rare)
- The idea of commanding a fleet is cool

Bottom Line:
This game needs polish and refinement. It needs more features, a better map and better interface with widescreen support. Also, the controls need to be reconsidered. Otherwise, it stands a chance of being a wonderfully exciting game. I am recommending this because of its concept and potential. This concept is rare and we really need more "thinking" games like this that actually let us experience a world and econimic/strategic features without being confined to the traditional "Find resources, build soldiers, throw soldiers at enemy" scheme.

Early Access Review
Posted: 8 July
Ok so a few hours invested in this game, it's about time I gave it a review.

Firstly, keep in mind when reading any review, positive or negative, that this is an early access game in it's alpha state. It is NOT a finished product and all info given in reviews is liable to be rendered inaccurate at some point.

The good:
Ok let's start with the description. Note this from the game devs -> "This is a social-economic sandbox RPG". So if you are looking for a fast paced PvP game or action packed adventure game, stop reading now and go away. This game requires patience, time, a little intelligence and certainly commitment.

This is a game where you work, trade, hunt or just blag your way to fortune. You start will a little airship hanging from a balloon and you set off into a world that is 500x500 miles in size to make that fortune. There are 2 playable races. The fantastic awesome Orcs, and the pink skinned lilly livered humans.
Game progression is simple. You take some jobs, deliveries mostly. You float across the sky in your balloon and complete your job. You get paid some copper and get a little reputation. Rinse and repeat until you can afford a bigger better airship or two (yes you can command a whole fleet of them with skills). Then you can take on bigger jobs or try your hand at buying and trading in consumables between cities.
A little further on, a little more money, and you can establish a settlement of your own. Produce your own resources, airships, weapons, ammo etc etc. All can be traded on the market or directly from your own store.
Another viable option is hunting dragons. The other denizens of the world. Trophies from dragons are used in crafting some of the more advanced items in the game and are in high demand.

Level advancement is done in the Eve Online fashion (one of many similarities). You train a skill over time, even when offline. XP earned from skill training increases your level. Simples. There are crafting skills, pilot skills, magic skills, resource gathering etc etc. There are not too many at the moment but there are enough for you to specialize.

The community is great. At least the English speaking one. There is a large Russian player base too, doing what Russians do, but I have had no real interaction with them. The Dev team is really good. They are constantly updating the game and helping players out. They have a passion for the game, and that is usually one of the most important traits a dev can have. They want you to tell them what's wrong, and then they fix it. Communication can be difficult, but not impossible.

Combat-wise there is not much. Apart from fighting/hunting dragons, there are also the pirates and rogue players. So far not a problem to me but they will be. Combat can be done with the auto attack feature or by manually aiming your weapons groups. There are also planes that you can launch from your ship like a carrier that will defend you, or you can station them in your settlement and have them fly combat air patrols over it. You can also launch as a pilot of one of these planes.

The economy is a work in progress. There is one, but it's a bit iffy with the small player base and the early players dominating the best settlement spots. Prices can be ridiculous too. That will change as more players join and there is more competition. There are 3 currencies in game. I shall simplifie them as copper, silver and gold. Gold is a premium currency you can buy. And it's actually dirt cheap really. You can trade all currency for one of the others but the prices are stupid in some areas. You need to look about for the best deals.

The Bad:
Well, there are certainly going to be no prizes for graphics. They are minimal at best. But, as you spend most of your time high in the air there is really no call for high level ground detail. So it actually works just fine. And will run quite happily on most machines.

The economy is a bit broken, as I said above. However there are many players trying to improve this by selling their wares at sensible prices instead of the greedy ones. It will take time but it will happen. The majority of the community is interested in promoting the game and making it accessible to new players. Including the prices they have to pay when starting out.

Server restarts are a pain as they reset the consumables on the market and you have to wait for them to restock. This is an unfortunate consequence of the Devs being very active in fixing and updating things. We just have to suck it up.

Scammers are present. Some of the jobs you will see are just unrealistic. Very high deposits that require you to take ridiculous loads over long distances. I would not expect to reach your destination, you will probably be jumped on the way so you fail the job and the job poster gets your gold. There are also players with names I can not pronounce who like to add newbies as enemies in the hope they will attack them in a safe zone etc. They get nothing financially if you do, just a good laugh.

The game has no useful tutorial really and can be very confusing when starting out. It shows some tips but they can be a little bewildering and misleading. The whole translation of the game to English is seriously bad. The names of some items are just strange or wrong. There are a lot of things that just won't make sense at all or give the wrong information. Devs do seem to be fixing this when its pointed out though.

Conclusion:
I recommend this game if you are the sort of player who likes a relaxing game and doesn't mind the slow pace and time required. Economists and traders will love it. If you are a more combative type, then you probably won't like it much unless you go down the pirate route. That's your call. Even then though, combat you have isn't going to be fast paced and intensive. You are going to have to chase down targets over distance in a slow moving airship and work for your kills. And probably be hunted by a lot of others in return.
The game is rough around the edges and needs a lot of improvement. However it has 3 things that make it stand out. An active and passionate dev team, a great community and a whole boat load of potential. With some major improvements in certain areas, this is going to be a classic for players who like this premise. It's free to play so just try it. Introduce yourself in game and you will get a warm welcome and a lot of help. (Please though, read the guides and watch the star vid, we do get sick of answering the "how do I play?" question every 20 minutes) My in game name is Crom Cruach, feel free to add me on steam or in game if you require any help. Not sure it will be of much use, but still :)

TL:DR
It is a visually pretty game, fun, and with the right people, exciting. Long waits in flights and researches for skills and upgrades. 7/10
==========

With 68 hours in, I would recommend this game if you have disposable income and/or huge amounts of time to donate to it. Not a game for a lot of FPS players as it's v e r y s l o w, and it's not explained very well especially for beginners which can leave them dazed and confused.

Steampunk-ish / Eve-type RTS.
Visually beautiful and can be very fun hunting dragons (or even pirates), or hauling stuff around AND shooting dragons (or pirates), or staring in awe as you fly your flotilla around the wonderous landscape and waterways shooting dragons (and sometimes pirates).

Great group of people online usually willing to help out.

Bought $10 worth of gold to get a first base going and some goods.
While it may give the impression of pay2win it most certain is not. Game even pays you 5 gold every hour just for being logged in.

Bit of a gold-trap though with skills (everything, pretty much) costing gold, and some ridiculous research times (imo). 130+ hours to build an single engine (in a 2nd stage of 3 building) AFTER you got the mats for it, AND the buildings needed to create it, AND the settlement the buildings are in.
But it can all be rushed with gold *wink wink*.

Beautiful job by the devs, visually and for the fact that they've got a gold-mine here, as people want results now and are too impatient to wait a month for something they can buy today for the price of a couple mochaccinos.

While I give this game a thumbs up and recommend it, I'd also caution people to be aware of the amounts of time (and gold, so much gold) that will be required for you to advance to any kind of "top end" character.

Happy sailing!
