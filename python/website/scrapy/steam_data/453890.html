About This Game

Black Rose is a first-person survival horror game in which you assume the role of Linda, an average girl who heard rumors about an old abandoned funeral home being haunted. No one knows what happened to many of the people who used to work there, but legend says they were murdered. By who? Linda wants to find this out for herself, so she goes to the secluded funeral home. But not long after entering, things go horribly wrong.

SCORE


Your score will be based on 'Close Calls'. Close Calls are achieved by getting close to the enemy without letting them catch you. When your score starts increasing, the enemy is dangerously close. Try to get as many Close Calls as possible to bring up your score, but be aware that you should not let your Close Calls with the first enemy last for too long. This enemy is particularly volatile, and will quickly catch you if you are close for too long.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7, Windows Vista
    • Processor: 2.0 GHz
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: GeForce GT 630M or equivalent
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 337 MB available space

WARNING: This game contains flashing lights, and may potentially trigger seizures in people with Epilepsy. If you have a history of seizures or unusual reactions to flashing lights or images, please do not play this game.

Black Rose is a free to play horror title by indie developer Sir Bedlam Productions. The story is simple enough, you take on the role of a girl name Linda who is exploring an old, abandoned, haunted funeral home. Your overall goal is to figure out what is going on, and what happened to the people who worked there.

The gameplay is equally as simple as the story, you will explore the funeral home and pick up notes along the way to unravel the plot. You will also find various items scattered about, although ultimately you need to find a couple of keys to open a door that is locked with two padlocks. Eventually, you will begin encountering enemies; there are only two within the game, Sullivan and Myrtle. After you find their initial resting place, they will randomly spawn and chase you around; if you get to close to them, it's game over. However, the game rewards you with score points for staying as close to the enemies as possible without dying. This is an unfortunately useless feature, as there are no rewards or leaderboards.

The graphics are alright, and the ambient atmosphere is suspenseful and inspires a sense of dread in the player. It's good to note that the game even has 5.1 surround sound capability. It also runs at a smooth 60 FPS and doesn't suffer from any random drops; it's a very well optimized title, which I commend the developer on. However, there are some bugs and glitches that need to be ironed out; such as getting stuck on doors, chairs, and the fact that enemies can spawn right on top of you. A save feature would also be a welcome addition, as not everyone would be willing to sit down and spend an hour to finish the game in one go.

Black Rose is one of the better, and more well optimized, free indie horror titles to come about, and for that reason I am going to recommend it. Yes, the game utilizes jumpscares a bit, but it more so relies on the tense, thick atmosphere that is set up by the use of ambient sound. Yes, the enemies do look a bit cheap and cheesy, but I have seen far worse models in far worse games. If you're a fan of horror titles, give this one a shot... you've really got nothing to lose, it's free after all.

Rating: 3.0/5.0 - Average, can take it or leave it.
