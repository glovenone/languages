About This Game

Requiem: Rise of the Reaver is a Free-to-Play, massively multiplayer online role playing game set in a dark distant world where an event known as the "Thanatos Incident" rendered the world almost unlivable. Create your own biological weapon known as a Temperion and experience the horrors that grip Ethergia. Fight alongside the Fenrir Canine and the Holy Xenon Empire to against the darkness that encroaches on the remnants of civilized society.

Key Features
  • Choose between PvP and PvE servers to experience the game your way.
  • Chose amongst 4 distinct different races with their own unique classes.
  • Experience the power of your Temperion by mutilating and utterly evaporating your opponents with gorey detail.
  • Explore Ethergia: walk the streets of Nova Lux, the desolate wastes of Numaren during the light of day or the blackness of the nightmare hours.

Alright, a little bit of a warning as I'll update as I go, but, I played this game when it was known as Requiem: Bloodymare, but the game hasn't changed much (if at all at it's core) since I last played, and I wish to let others know of this game and give a detailed review for it, as others simply say "it's crap" or "play it". I won't make it too long, as you guys should play it and find out for yourself, but I'll touch upon the basics.

Races and Classes

In Requiem, you can choose from four races, and 2 classes with each race, each class diverging into two separate branches at a certain point, for a total of 16 different end game classes. I'll just give details on the initial classes.

Turan (Humanoid, closest in similarity to Humans):

Defender - Your basic sword and board tank. Utilizing quick stabbing abilities and threat generating taunts, you make sure other people in your group are not being eaten alive.

Templar - Magician that utilizes electricity and healing abilities. Did not play this class much.

Bartuk (Really Bulky Humanoid):

Warrior - This game's berserker. You utilize a two handed weapon and slash your foes in two (sometimes quite literally. This game is gory, I'll touch up on that later.

Shaman - Utilizing flame abilities, you can give your own attacks flame power and add flame damage to others abilities. Didn't play this one much either.

Kruxena (Most similar to commonly recognized dark elves):

Rogue - Fast, brutal, and relentless. You shred your enemies to pieces with blindingly fast attacks(heh.). You use razor sharp claws or dual swords as this class.

Soul Hunter - I never personally played this class, as it's a debuffing class. You magically curse and weaken your enemies.

Xenoa (Smaller elvish looking creatures that wear high heels....yep. Even the males.):

Hunter - You utilize a comically oversized blaster and abuse the physics engine by BLOWING your enemies away. Uses ammo and a launcher, chaining abilities. This is the only class I've played where the monsters fly away when they die from how "hard" you're hitting them, as a consequence I had the most fun and am most experienced with this class.

Battlemagician - Good at combat assistance with elemental magic. Utilizing mainly short-range magic attacks and "throw" magic when at a distance. Only class I know of in a game that has an ability that lets your throw Hydrochloric ACID on your enemies.


Gameplay

Alright, down to the interesting stuff. Depends upon how you look at it. When you first login, if it's your first character you're asked if you would like to go to the tutorial level. Don't skip it, it introduces you to the fundamentals of both your class and the game....well, mostly the game and it's controls, and it's always useful information to have. As you level you gain skill points, which you can use to "buy" skills by the tier for your class, earn enough points in one tier you unlock the next tier and so on. If you've bought all skills in a tier simply start upgrading the skills your already bought, as a couple in the top tier unlock skills on the third tier if fully upgraded.

Combat is, for lack of a better word, slow. At least in the beginning. I was playing for about 15 minutes before I started to mow through enemies that were giving me trouble just two levels prior, as I was leveling and giving myself skills. You start with no skills as you level it becomes easier to chain combos and utilize the rotation for each class.

Quests are the same monotonous dribble you get with the standard issue MMO, this one leaning more on the grinding side. After you get past level 15 it starts to slow WAY down, to the point where I bought a XP booster back in the day just to make it seem like I was still making progress. In a game where the max level is 81...this is a problem.

This game has special monsters that come out at certain times in the game at certain points of the game map, called Nightmare monsters. These monsters are tougher, have more health, and actually utilize abilities against you. To give you an example of difficulty scale, in a level 6-7 area you're liable to run across a level 10 nightmare creature, at level 16-18 you're liable to run into a 20-22 Nightmare creature. These monsters give A LOT more experience, and a higher chance of dropping better gear and loot. You will need a full party of eight people most typically at the higher levels to take them down.

Because this is a free to play game, it has an in-game cash shop where you can buy costumes, mounts, XP boosters(you'll need them if you don't want leveling to feel like a snail race), etc. I have not looked at the shop in depth to determine whether this game is in the "pay to win" category of free to play games.

I never delved into the PvP aspect of the game so I cannot give a definitive review of it.

Graphics

This game was released in 2008, and the aging graphics engine is starting to make that apparent as more time goes on. This is not an ugly game, but it is very blasé. When I was in my WoW slump back in '08 I looked at this game and it looked amazing. Now, with better graphics every where, it's outclassed in almost every facet. Textures are very low resolution, characters have too much bloom for my liking, and characters just look too....glossy. The environments have this drab shade of brown that just doesn't go away, even in snowy areas, it's just depressing to look at for long periods of time.

One thing that drew me to this game was the gore. There's buckets of it. When you kill an enemy their limbs fly off, blood sprays every where, and their body flies away. It gets a chuckle out of me to see it because of how exaggerated it is.

Final Verdict

Alright, chances are you just scrolled down here, or you're getting tired of reading a wall of text. Fact of the matter is, this game couldn't "grab" me and keep my attention past a few weeks of intermittent playing. If your experience is similar to mine, you'll find it fun for a couple weeks and you'll most likely find it boring.

I never could push past the boredom to see what end-game was like, but I believe I'm equipped with enough experience to let you determine whether you want to give this game a shot. I did, I enjoyed it, it was fun, but there are just simply more fun games out there. Give this one a try, I can't speak for everyone.

FINAL VERDICT: 5.5/10

PROS:
  • Gore coalescing with the physics is amusing, if only for a while.
  • Skill and DNA system is fun
  • Nightmare monsters are scary to run into and amazingly rewarding to kill.

CONS:
  • Graphics are looking dated.
  • Questing is monotonous.
  • Combat in the lower levels is slow.
  • I got a item from the cash shop (free item spin wheel every day) and it transported me, a level 4, to an area with level 56 nightmare monsters. That's just bad design right there.

(WARNING) Long Review. TLDR at the bottom.

Oh man, where to start with this game. I used to be an avid player of Requiem. Myself and a few friends had great times and memories with this game, but for those who are looking to try this game out, you're in for a big surprise when you get to a point where you want to do high level PvE or PvP.

---===[Upgrading Armor]===---

Upgrading armor takes place in the form of Reinforcing, Compounding and Enchanting. Each peice of gear can be reinforced a total of 30 times. Each reinforcement increases the attributes on your armor. Each peice of armor also has a certain amount of enchanting slots depending on the grade (Ordinary, Supernous, Eximius, Carus, Maximus). You can even compound your own gear/enchants into stronger parts. Sounds cool, right? Here's the catch:

This is what we call "Pay to win". No, it has nothing to do with EXP boosts either. Once you get to a certain reinforcement level...say +20, you have to spend real life money to continue. Why? Because the success rate of the next reinforcement is so low, that you risk breaking the item, or going down reinforcement steps. I.e: Going from +20 to +13. So why is this a problem? Well, at low levels, it's not. But once you hit the Eximius grade, you risk breaking your items more often than reinforcing them, and it forces you to start over on that peice of gear. You don't get any of your gold or resources back when the item breaks. When you reach the Rare and Carus grade, it's almost impossible for a F2p player to go past +10 or +15. However, the person who spends a few hundred dollars can take each peice of gear to +30. When this happens, the person with all +10 gear gets steam rolled by the person with +30 gear because of the massive stat difference. This company has essentially allows people to PAY FOR ARMOR. The same applies to Enchanting as well. The players who pay the most get perfect enchants which further adds to the gap between F2P and Paying users.

---===[Player Satisfaction]===---

Gravity used to be under the management of a Korean based development team which is where the Pay to Win content came from. The players pleaded for years for Gravity to give us NEW content, balance out the classes, fix important bugs, ect. However, what we got was class nerfs almost every month that forced everyone to re-roll their toons, and RECYCLED content. That's a big one. You will see the exact same mobs in 20 different areas that were simply reskinned and increased/decreased in size. Hell, most of the dungeons have the same design (Premium Dungeon for example). Over the years our suggestions were COMPLETELY ignored and Gravity added more and more items to the cash shop because that's all they cared about. But lets fast forward to now. Gravity is now owned by U.S developers. They've had creative control over the game for close to a year, if not more.

What have they done? More harm than good just like the last development team. We're seeing more recyled content than ever, poor attempts at the new "Expansion" known as "Rise of the Reaver", and half-♥♥♥ class structuring that we have very little information about. Not only that, but they continue to cherish their precious item mall more than delivering decent content. Every event they host is broken in some way shape or form, the content they release is simply a reskin of something else, the game still runs like ♥♥♥♥ even on gaming pc's, and we have a lack of communication from the developers. Long story short, nothing has changed since the U.S developers got the game.

---===[TLDR]===---

- Questing is beyond Generic
- Classes have been nerfed to oblivion
- Outdated graphics (some people care, I personally do not)
- Bugs that have been around since the launch of the game
- Extremely Low Population
- Very poor performance even on Gaming Rigs
- Extreme gap between F2P and P2P
- Gravity can never host an event without it being broken for the first couple of days

And I'm just getting started, but I'm tired of typing now. My Verdict? Until Gravity cleans their mess up, 2/10. I give two points because I like Horror Themed stuff.
