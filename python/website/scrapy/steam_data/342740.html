This seems to have been written for consoles and hastily ported to PC, which is a bit weird.

Implementation of this game is generally good but it's let down by content - the format doesn't allow for much, 2D platformer/brawlers are really a console thing. Quality is fairly high for an indy game. Support for 4K is also a plus, gameplay is very smooth, although as a 2D game it's not really touching the capabilities of PC as a platform.

Also, the controls are obviously console orientated - keyboard/game controller only, no mouse (although again, kudos to the developers for including control customisability, many people have experienced some issues with that, unfortunately).

Gameplay is fairly high brow for a consoler, you have about to worry about movement as well as parrying/attacking etc etc.

Unfortunately at the end of the day this is what very much appears to be a console game on a platform it doesn't belong on. While the price is suitably low, so is the general interest. I would thoroughly encourage the developers to move into more serious game development, the quality ethos is there, and along with that can come success, if it's aimed in the right direction.

a 1-4 player fighting game with a nidhogg-like map scrolling progress component.

In samudai you fight each other in stages. A victory will earn you progress towards your teams final location - and a loss will push you back towards the opponents final location. This can either be achieved by pushing an opponent off the screan or by the opponent "drowning" twice. At the final stage your opponent will transform into a boss with HP that you must defeat.

The maps are dynamic environments. Water levels will rise, wind will blow, spirits will assail you.

Still feels somewhat incomplete but fun and original enough mechanics, not at all bad for the price.
