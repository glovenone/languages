What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Faeria is in early development and throughout our Kickstarter experience, we learned a lot from the community and we have still a lot to learn from you. Going on Steam Early Access will enable the community to get access to the game early and help shape its future!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We expect Faeria to last until September 2016 in Early Access.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The current version of Faeria already includes a lot of very interesting features. However, we need your help to shape the upcoming ones and in our roadmap, we have:

  • Emotes
  • Integrated Tournaments
  • Betting in-game currency
  • Spectator mode with broadcaster support
  • Twitch integration (linked to betting system)
  • Server-unique cards
  • Solo adventures as well as expansions
  • New cosmetics
  • Console and Mobile support
  • Bug fixing and performance
  • New cards
  • New illustrations
  • New languages

And much more! Join the conversation and help us shape Faeria on Facebook, Twitter, Reddit & our forums.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Even though the upcoming features’ list is quite consequent, Faeria currently offers all the main key features of quality card game:

  • Pandora Mode (Arena) [NEW]
  • 15 solo missions to learn the game
  • 256 Unique Cards
  • Practice vs AI
  • Battle mode (PVP)
  • Ranked mode
  • Friends list + challenge your friends
  • In-game shop for: Booster Packs, Full Core Set, Avatars and Orbs!
  • Awesome arts and illustrations
”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Faeria will be a Free-to-Play after its Early Access’ exit.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Every one of us is a part of our community. We are gamers first. We are open to all ideas, big or small through our various channels. Those ideas, will help us shape the future of Faeria in terms of features, polishing and platforms.

We will also:

  • Do Reddit AMA
  • Voting contests about the most wanted features
  • Run a Discord channel

And much more!”

8 July

Patch notes: July 8th, 2016

A small update will be made to Three Wishes today, as announced on our Discord channel earlier this week. Next week will be a much larger client patch.


Card changes
  • Three Wishes
    Three Wishes now has the following text appended to the end:
    "You cannot wish for more wishes."
    This means that if you should use Three Wishes and your opponent has a Three Wishes in the top three cards of their deck - it will be ignored, and the next card will be drawn (You will still get 3 cards back).
    In this way, we are targetting the way this card performs only in the mirror match, and it will not affect its balance in any other matchup.Monthly Cup deck preparations should not be affected by this change, and we are confident it is a positive adjustment that is needed sooner rather than later. Thank you!
Pandora artifacts
The following artifacts of Pandora will no longer appear in game:
  • Garudan's Scale
  • Tears of Ouroboros
These Pandora changes actually were made earlier this week without a need for a client patch, but we chose to document them here. Thank you for your feedback on Pandora artifacts in general, and you can expect more significant adjustments to come. We have much more fundamental changes coming in the future, but for now we'd like to remove these shards from the Pandora experience.

Note: We have a much larger client patch incoming for next week which, among other things, will improve the flow of the initial login process for a new player, and open the door for more user account control in the future. The changes are significant enough to warrant more testing, so we will not be pushing them forward before the weekend.
  • The Faeria Team

About This Game

Following a successful Kickstarter that gathered more than 3,500 backers in 2013, Faeria was revamped to make it closer to our vision of “easy to learn, hard to master”.

Faeria combines the core values of Strategy Card Games together with a Living Board, giving you the ability to shape the board as you play to create mind-blowing strategies. Each game of Faeria is not only unique, beautiful and fast-paced but also highly competitive with upcoming features such as an observer mode and integrated tournaments.

Faeria's Early Access Bundle helps you kickstart your experience by giving you access to:
  • The Game
  • 20 Booster Packs
  • 10 Arena Tickets
  • Early Access Exclusive Avatar and Orb

Product received for free
There's quite a few digital card games out there. A good portion of them have gorgeous art styles, evolving strategies, and solid cards.

Faeria, however, has a board. And that adds a whole heck of a lot.

Even if you think, "Well, I can't possibly wrap my head around another few hundred cards," the living board is what grounds it. It's easy to grasp the idea that good land placement can give your assaults an edge and prevent your opponent from placing creatures on your front door. The position of a creature and where it can move is just as important as how strong it is. A well-executed board strategy is arguably more important than just having a killer deck.

Faeria adds all that, and its matches still last about the same length as other DCGs. It's fast, strategic, and exciting to the end. The vast majority of my games end in close calls where one turn makes the difference between victory and defeat.

Progression-wise - between quests and crafting - the rate at which you gain new, useful cards feels about right.

All in all, can't recommend this enough.
