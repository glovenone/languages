Posted: 22 June
Broken Bots is a 2D fast-paced twin-stick action shooter developed and published by "Bunnycopter" in June 2016 serving as their debut title on Steam. The multiplayer component of the game allows players to connect from several regions (EU, NA, ASIA, JP, AU or just choose option "best" which determines the best solution for you). However, to team up with your friend,you guys have to set the same region and join the server after creating the party. Sadly I can't really tell how much of you get into one as we only played as 2 (Warwick (Steamified and I)). However the game is up to 12 players with 6 players on each side for every single game type game has to offer. These are : Capture the flag, King of the Hill, Crazy King (same as king of the hill but in that case point moves around the map) and Team Deathmatch with 6 different maps being featured. Therefore I am pretty sure that you would be able to invite more than one player into your party. besides multiplayer experience game offers extra modes such as Survival - where you have to defend yourself from enemy hordes coming wave by wave, Private Match - where you can play against AI with custom properties and an offline mode called Infiltration - which helds up 5levels,where you have to go through horde of enemies and traps to reach final destination and get closer and closer to the boss.

You play as a small tank-like style bot with a variety of power-ups and set-ups in regards of tank constitution. There's 6 unlockable bots including one you start with, 2 Assault types, Flamethrower, Grenade Launcher, Plasma Beam and a Rocket Launcher. As for skills you can choose only two at a time besides having one as a super power which is only one for everyone,called Malfunction which charges overtime after certain kind of actions,and serving is name causing either enemy bots malfunction in bad ways such as weapon or movement instability, or buffing your teammate and increasing their fire rate, damage, or the amount of bullets etc,once they get hit by your charge and extra buff for you and your teammates after completing small "hacking" action on the enemy Generators located near the enemy base. There are also personal skills you can choose. These include a Heal (Negating active malfunction and healing for small amount), Shockwave (generates wave which pushes enemies behind the circle), EMP (causes enemy bots near you to malfunction) , shield (generating temporary shield absorbing damage from the direction you are facing), Mine (places a mine which damages enemies once they touch it), Turret (drops down a turret attacking all enemies running through it's vision sight), Speed Boost (giving you a speed boost for a short time), Heal Wave (generates healing wave for you and friendlies), Teleport (teleports you to a random location), Cloak (makes you invisible for a short time) and 4 of the passives which you have a freedom to choose of, after you reach the certain level to unlock them.

In order to unlock new bots and abilities you have to reach certain levels. However, it feels really grindy since it's affected only by wins, kda or score you get doesn't really matter in that case. You only have to win so it doesn't matter how you perform which is really disappointing. The player base is not that high at this time and there's no mmr or some kind of balance system,you might get matched up with someone who already has end game unlockables and makes gameplay unfair. I personally didn't have that kind of problem so I found it really easy to adapt and was pretty much dominating every single round. However, someone who is new might have problems with that. I mentioned that game hasn't too much of a players,however it has a smart system replacing them with AI so even if you won't be lucky to get matched up with some other players for region,you won't get bored cause game will fill all the empty slots with AI-s and draw them out if another player tries to join.

The game is not really focused on graphics. It has decent looking retro graphics with the ability to zoom in and out in your preference. However, I would like to mention that it would have been good if hitboxes were more visible,playing as a flamethrower or sometimes even as an assault I couldn't define properly if i was hitting the target or not so I personally think that visualizing the damage or at least somehow pointing out would be nice,for example getting red after taking a hit or something like that. Also I would like to see improvement in HUD which shows the timer for buffs you get from generators,cause i don't really get how long I'm buffed for or if it even stacks if you get different or same type of buffs in a row.

PROS:
  • Variety of Playable Characters â (game offers you 6 type of bots to play with such as : 2 Assaults,Flamethrower,Grenade launcher,Plasma beam and Rocket launcher)

  • Variety of Skill-Setups â (even that you can have only two at the time you can choose from several different type of abilities such as: Heal, Shockwave, Emp, Shield, Mine, Turret, Speed boost, Heal wave, Teleport and Cloak but it's not all you can choose 4 different passives,having complete freedom of building it the way you want to match your style of playing)

  • Variety of Modes â (game offers couple of mods such as :Capture the flag,King of the Hill,Crazy King and Team deathmatch)

  • Variety of Power-ups â(besides having your own skill-set,you have superpower causing different types of buffs for friendlies and debuffs for enemies,along with the enemy generator located in the enemy base,hacking it gives you and your team several type of buffs)

  • Fast-paced â (maps are not that large and spawn timers aren't that long as well,so there's constant action all around,and actually all the bots are kind of fast so you will mostly have to outmaneuver your opponent to dodge bullets since all of the guns has infinite ammo)

  • Smart AI â (game has pretty smart system of replacing players with bots when slots are empty,and bots itself are pretty decent in terms of gameplay, it's hard to define whether you are facing a bot or player when you are drawn in action)

  • Extra Mods â (besides game being multiplayer,game offers some single player action as mods like Survival and Infiltration)

CONS:
  • Maps â (small amount of maps,only 6 so far)

Early Access Review
Posted: 4 August, 2015
What an interesting little game.

It's a Team Online-Multiplayer game with several game modes, all of which are classics. Team Death Match, Capture the Flag, and King of the Hill, with a few variations of these.

Everyone plays a bot, and there are a few to choose from. Each with their own little stats and weapons, although sadly customisation is very very minimal. You can select some active skills ( 2 ), and 4 passives. That's it.

I would have liked if you could actually customise your bot's look and weapons more as it seems rather funky that the smallest one with smallest looking weapons is actually the heaviest with the largest rocket launchers around.

The game is entirely meant to be played online, which could be very very problematic as so few people are online. Then again the game is only Just out, so it might pick up online.
It would have been better if there was even a small little single player campaign, even if only for the tutorial; which at the moment is just text and some animations of bots and a generator.
It's not bad, and it gets the point across but I feel it could do with more there.

In regards to single player, at the moment all it is, is the online matches played against bots. Similar to games of old, if there aren't enough people online the teams get filled with Bots. Their A.I can be lacking at times, and I've seen my team flag runner often getting stuck on another friendly bot.

Gameplay wise it's rather fun, and I do like the music. It's not hard to pick up at all and that's partially due to familiar game modes, controls and limited customisation. You simply quickly select your perks and get in there blasting.
At any time during the match you can simply press T, which brings up the original Bot menu. Here you can select a new bot or change your actives and passives.

That's it really. It's kinda fun, but nothing stellar. Although like most online focused games will be substantially improved when playing with and against friends over Voice Comms.

Early Access done right

Even though I do not agree with the early access platform, this game is a prime example of the correct way to execute it.

Let us start out with the negeitive aspects because that is the crusual information people need to know before the good stuff. There isn't much, but there is some.

Tournement Mode: It does not tell you how many games are in a tourny. I have been trying very hard to aquire the "Win a Tournement" achivement. I do not know if the mode is glitched or if there are more matches to be played, for there is no idicator of how many more matches are to come. On that note, if the tourny mode is not glithched and I have just not completed all the games, there are way to many matches (in my opinion) before the winner is selected.

Glitches: I have not noticed any visual glitches in the game so far, but there are a few audio glitches that are quite noticable. During sequences with tons of audio clips going at the same time, there could be an absense of noise all together. This is most noticable when a bot overloads and goes into "Fire Rate Broken" (i do not remember the exact name) and all audio goes silent for a second or two. There are also a few instences that i saw AI robots stuck in a corners being brutally shot and destroyed.

AI: the AI in this game is very easy to beat after you get a slight hang of the controls and mechanics. I have never come in lower then first places in a match with just AI robots. This makes it easy to achive very high scores on the leaderboards which will hopefully be reset after the full release of the title. If anything i would request that Bunnycopter create different levels of AI difficulty.

Turret Secondary Ability: When you place a turret, it can become stuck on it for a second or so after placing it. It becomes farily annoying sometimes but is a minor problem.

The negetives might sounds like a lot, but don't let any of them deter you from aquiring this title. I have simply listed, in detail, the areas Bunnycopter could implove on. These minor areas effect little of the game's amazing gameplay.

Broken Bots is a top down, team based online/offline shooter. You play as a broken bot, armed with cannons or flamethrowers (including different variations). There are a few different (but all fairly classic) gamemodes such as Deathmatch, Capture the Flag, King of the Hill, Crazy King (King of the Hill but with a moving "hill"), and a tournement mode. This is a limited amount of gamemodes, but is acceptable for an Early Access game.

The twist that this game pesents to this genre of video games is the "broken" aspect of the bots. These bots, after taking enough damage, will malfunction, inverting your aim, breaking the bot's movement speed or fire rate, ect.. These, as shown, can be positive or negitive effects (more likely to be negetive). This adds an exciting new aspect to the traditional kill everything idea. You could have grabbed the enemy flag and be halfway back to your base when an enemy, out of nowhere, gets that one hit to send you into 100% territory, inverting your driving, and sending your fingers scambling to conpensate for the change, all the while shooting back at your attacker and dodging his attacks. This mechanic puts a new spice into mid-combat, creating a sense of urgency.

Overall Broken Bots is an overload of fun and will be well worth your $6.99.
