About This Game

Passing Pineview Forest is a free mini-prequel set in the universe of Pineview Drive. Behind the abandoned house on Pineview Drive lies ominous Pineview Forest. The dark, foreboding forest trail leading directly to the Pineview property must be explored – but your every move and reaction is being watched. You must stay calm during your walk – or else IT will take you! To survive you will need steady nerves; for any trace of fear comes at a price — your life.

About Pineview Drive


http://store.steampowered.com/app/288880/

An old abandoned mansion lies at the dead end of Pineview Drive. A troubled man is standing at its gates, looking over the property. 20 years ago he had visited this estate with his wife Linda. During their stay his beloved vanished without a trace – her mysterious disappearance unresolved.


For 20 years no clues, for 20 years no peace, for 20 years this house on Pineview Drive has not relinquished its tormenting grip on him. Now, he returns in hopes of coming to terms with his nightmarish past.

The ominous legends haunting this seaside lodge have become renown. The stories abound that no one has been able to endure more than 30 days within the mansion since Linda’s disappearance. Facing a force greater than you ever imagined, you must cross the threshold and confront the evil that envelops this cryptic cottage. To solve the mystery of Linda’s disappearance and lift the veil on this troubling history, there is no time to spare.

Oculus Rift

This game additionally acts as a tech demo and supports the Oculus Rift (DK2). It should be noted that a DX11 system is mandatory. To start the Oculus version please select it via the integrated launcher.

Features

  • Free mini-prequel to Pineview Drive
  • Oculus Rift (DK2) support
  • Sound design and atmosphere on a par with Pineview Drive

What's the point? That's what i kept asking me while playing this game. First things i want to say: the atmosphere is good, the graphics do its work and the music and sounds are done well, they help build up the atmosphere.

Now the gameplay: wether you find the game scary or not (it's a personal matter) the problem is what you can do. There's no tutorial or anything, but you honestly don't need it, the commands are very basic. WASD to walk around, F to turn flashlight on/off, holdind SHIFT to run and SPACEBAR to make a very little jump (obviously put there to avoid being stuck in steps or doorways, wich happens constantly). You walk in a path that sometimes forks, with no purpose because choosing left or right doesn't change anything, the paths always meet back up with the road shortly after. Sometimes you see some barracks that are explorable. Again no purpose, there's nothing inside, you can't interact with anything, you can't close door to hide, there's nothing to stop the creature (whatever that is) from following you. I don't know, maybe if you hide for sometime in that buildings the "thing" will lose your tracks. Anyway, you need to be quiet to manage to reach the end, so your best option is trying not to run (wich is EXTREMELY boring) and keep your flashlight off as much as possible. Using the flashlight by the way is a little too difficult, it does very little light and your character will constantly point it in the grass, making it difficult to watch around with it, would have been nice if he pointed it a little bit forward than between his feet.

NEXT SENTENCES ARE A SPOILER OF THE GAME FINALE (nothing special i know, but i thought it was nice to let readers know)


Once you do that you will reach the gate to pineview drive (wich is the end of the game), in about 10 to 15 minutes. You touch the gate and you'll see: Congratulations, you reached the house, hope you enjoyed, etc. etc... (for a fraction of a second by the way, i don't know if i clicked something but i managed to read that only because i was recording the game session). If you mess up doing too much noise or keeping the flashlight on for too much time you'll see a pair of hands wich will suddendly cover your eyes from behind in a jumpscare and boom, game over. I know it's a mini-game, i know it's a prequel of Pineview drive and yes, i know it's free to play, but i honestly don't understand why there are so many positive reviews. In my opinion it's boring and exceptionally slow, and the lack of any kind of interactions doen't help. I didn't find it all that scary too, and not because it's lacking in atmosphere, wich is pretty good to tell the truth, but because the boredoom was so high i couldn't enjoy it too much. That's my opinion, maybe i missed something. To tell the truth i am not sure wether to reccomend it or not, maybe an "i dont' know" option would be nice in reviews. Let's say that as i said i found it boring, but that's my personal opinion, maybe others won't and will manage to enjoy the atmosphere. In conclusion, trying it won't hurt you, it's free and you'll spend very little time to explore the whole game, so even if you hate it in the end it won't be for too long :D
