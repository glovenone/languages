About This Game

Game Modes
  • Single-player adventure: help the witty Dr. Wallow get rid of all the critters and become the ruler of the galaxy!
  • Local battle for 1 to 5 players using game controllers.
  • Online battle: LAN or WAN, with up to 8 players per room.

Features
  • 5 picturesque worlds with 30 wicked mazes.
  • A large bestiary of critters to blast.
  • A multitude of items and power ups to collect.
  • 5 hellish boss levels whose punishing difficulty will only be matched by the satisfaction you’ll get once you beat them.
  • Over 10 minutes of caustic comments from Dr. Wallow, voiced by David Goldfarb (the voice behind multiple characters in Mario Kart 8).
  • 7 famous pieces of classical music electronically butchered by Samuel Safa.
  • Controllable with the keyboard + mouse or with a game controller.

All right. Lets get the big thing out of the way. This game blatantly copies the Bomberman series. Like REALLY blatantly. Even the icon designs look like they were taken directly from the titles. As such, you'll be seeing a lot of comparisons between this game and that series in this review.

The game is rather challenging and will be quick to ramp up the challenge from level to level, even early on. Unlike some games which will give you a small taste of a new trap or enemy before throwing them at you in full force, this game takes a trial by fire mentality. Those looking for a challenge will probably like what this game has to offer, just be prepared to adapt quick. Sadly, stages also tend to lack much variety from one another so the single player mode does get repetitive.

Aesthetically the game doesn't quite have the charm of the series that inspired it. The music is all taken from public domain and mostly seems rather understated. One problem that arises from the aesthetics is the playable character's design. Due to his bulkiness, it becomes difficult to tell if he is safely hidden from an explosion or still in the line of fire. Multiple self destructs have resulted from me trying to determine the character's hitbox.

Of course, given the inspiration, the game has a multiplayer battle mode. and if you liked it in Bomberman than you're in luck because it's literally an exact copy of everything from those games, from the level designs, to the power-ups to the stage gimmicks.

Overall it's a decent game. Not as good as the series it's clearly trying to be, but given the state of the Bomberman franchise ever since Konami bought it, this is probably the closest to a sequel we're getting for a long while.

The only Bomberman clone on Steam so far that gets it even close to being right. Unfortunately this isn't nearly ambitious or inspired enough, and they cut corners everywhere from recolored monsters, barely remixed public domain classical music, shared AI, and minimal Bomberman and Super Bomberman mechanics. However, it's still a fun and it's competent at what it does copy and the bosses are challenging if not cheaty at times. Online is also, naturally, dead if you don't have a friend/s ready to play with you.

It's just a shame they didn't put real effort into the game to make it truly great. Still, it'll do in the meantime if you want more of a classic NES bomberman that only shares a few things from Super Bomberman like spiked bombs and trigger bombs instead of interesting levels and rideable creatures.
