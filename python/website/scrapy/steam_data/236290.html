About This Game

Enjoy hours of bubble-popping madness with this absurdly fun and addictive color-matching puzzle game! Described as a 360-degree Bust-a-Move with a steampunk motif, Cranky Cat allows for hours of bubble-popping madness with numerous modes of gameplay.

  • 250 unique levels of bubbly fun and frolic!
  • Try your hand at Story, Puzzle and three types of Infinite modes!
  • Show off milestones and achievements aplenty to your chums!
  • Simple to learn, difficult to master and impossible to put down!
  • Also available on Android Market and Apple iTunes!

Featuring fun carnivalesque music, authentic old-timey lingo and painstaking artwork inspired by Victorian-era patent illustrations and snake oil posters, Cranky Cat adds a unique steampunk spin to a timeless game.

Cranky Cat is simple (if you know Puzzle Bobble/Bust-a-Move) - it's PB with the board rotating, rather than you aiming new bubbles. The big problem is that especially later on, a lot of the boards will feel identical, and if you're going for 3-stars, the RNG nature of each stage is going to become a pain because sometimes you get a good run, and sometimes it just won't give you that one bubble of the right color to clear a path to the last two.

There's a Puzzle mode but it's hardly worth mentioning, because it comes down to "find the opening spot, rotate board appropriately to make all the bouncing pieces clear things". In fact, a huge part of why I find Cranky Cat kind of unfun stems from either a) gimmick boards where you can't blow up the biggest mass, or else the entire board will shift off-kilter and go out of bounds until you blow up two layers of bubbles, or b) reused boards over and over. I've seen one show up at least three times.

It's not a classic game by any means, is all I'm saying. The formula has some kinks.

Cranky Cat offers a new twist on the marble-shooting, bust-a-move genre, and it does it well.

Instead of firing spheres upward, they float in from the sides and slowly gravitate toward the center of the playing area. The player can (and has to) rotate the "board" to match the marbles. Three or more of the same color will remove them from the board, which then causes now loosened marbles to bounce off and gravitating back, which allows for quite spectacular and fireworks-like combinations.

There are numerous special marbles, such as the traditional bombs, and power-ups, like those that remove all marbles of a specific color. The game includes hundreds of levels in the story mode, and it also features a variety of endless/random modes, with the "lightning" mode perhaps being the most enjoyable one.

There are also Steam achievements and local highscores. The only feature I've been missing are Steam leaderboards, but this aside, this is a fairly enjoyable, fast-paced (at least deeper in the story mode and in Lightning) and smoothly running casual game (which can be quite hardcore). The developers have been very responsive to feature requests.

Worth checking out, especially at the very inexpensive super-budget price.
