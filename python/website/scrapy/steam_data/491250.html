About This Game

Olarath, The Council of Entropy has commanded you and your brethren to wage war upon the universe. As one of the quarter million Yurmuth (corporal ranking) of the 58028th assault wing of the 12056th Dark Lord's Army, you have been assigned to a score of solar systems approximately 51 million light years from the core of a galaxy called the Milky Way. Your mission: Polarize all inhabitants by crushing their planets with shattering anti-matter shards, eat their souls remnants to ensure they do not return to this universe, then obliterate each solar systems star to increase entropy in the region.

A warriors welcome is prepared for your return, and you will be served as dessert to your wing if you fail.

This game is utter trash, just like the other games made by this "company"

Decimation of olarath gives you a background of what's going on on the store page because the devs were too lazy to put the background into the actual game itself.

"Olarath, The Council of Entropy has commanded you and your brethren to wage war upon the universe. As one of the quarter million Yurmuth (corporal ranking) of the 58028th assault wing of the 12056th Dark Lord's Army, you have been assigned to a score of solar systems approximately 51 million light years from the core of a galaxy called the Milky Way. Your mission: Polarize all inhabitants by crushing their planets with shattering anti-matter shards, eat their souls remnants to ensure they do not return to this universe, then obliterate each solar systems star to increase entropy in the region.

A warriors welcome is prepared for your return, and you will be served as dessert to your wing if you fail."

Well, they diefinately put alot of thought into it. Wonder if they used some sort of random plot generator to come up with that.

Anyways, in Decimation of Olarath, you "control" an alien squid thing with zombie sfx and eat planets, avoid stars and then go into the Sun which, somehow, leads you to another galaxy. I said "control" because the controls for this alien squid thing are terrible. You float around with a terrible camera, so half the time you have no ide where you're, where the planets are or what you're doing. The movement controls are also really floaty, I can sort of sea that DH might have been aiming for realism as this will occur in space but when this is combined with a terrible camera, this makes for a sickeningly bad experience. I doubt they were really aiming for realism as the enemies aren't affected by the same things you are. It isn't even on the limits that it's so challenging that it's fun, it's just bland and boring and nearing unplayability.

The planets look blurry as hell and are terrible to look at as well.

This is once again another terrible game by Digital Homicide and is another one fr you to skip, nothing good to see here.

Pros
+ It's not a krog wars clone

Cons
- Unplayable
- Blurry planets
- Physics don't apply to enemies
- Logic makes no sense in it
- Lazily put together

Final verdict: 1/10
