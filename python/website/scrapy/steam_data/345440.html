About This Game

WARNING! This game has an Age Restriction of 18.

Vapour is an experimental horror game, that fuses elements of both old school and modern horror titles with the frantic game play of classic First Person Shooter titles. Blending horror, shooter, action, puzzle and platformer in a dynamic yet cohesive combination.

In Vapour you take on the role of a psychotic warlock known only as "Charles", who has been held captive and brutally tortured his entire life by a sadistic cult. After being executed for his crimes, Charles used his last ounce of strength to avoid total damnation.

Now, trapped between the borders of Purgatory and Hell and with his powers diminished, Charles must battle through hordes of demons, face his haunting past and ultimately make it back to earth to exact his revenge on those who have wronged him.

FEATURES:

-Spell-based combat
-Interactive puzzles
-Non-linear level design
-Plenty of exploration
-Tricky platforming
-Terrifying demons
-Deadly scares
-Sneaky secrets
-One gory and twisted plot

This game looks really neat, but unfortunately for me (and a lot of other people, from the looks of it), it is completely unplayable because of the controls. I'm sure the devs know about this issue, since it's been brought to their attention in the discussion forums quite a bit, but I've had this game for a while now and have checked back every now and then to see if they fixed it, only to be dissappointed. You may have better luck than myself, as a lot of other people seem to be having no issues at all with the controls. But you're pretty much rolling the dice as to whether or not it will work for you. It's not something you should have to worry about when purchasing a game, especially one that looks like it's had actual effort put into it. Hopefully this will be corrected down the line sometime, but I'm not very hopeful about it anymore. Buy at your own risk.

Vapour isn't your normal horror game, it's a mix of old school elements such as Duke Nukem, Penumbra and a bit of DOOM as well.

Vapour starts you off as a vengeful fighter trying to eliminate those who have wronged him in his life, the ones who sent him to hell/purgatory and in this quest you discover new and interested features. The game itself when you first play it feels like DOOM slapped with a bit of Elder Scrolls with the magic abilities, although the game does feature a collection of horrifying and quite disturbing monsters, jump scares are also in this game and the devs make a good use of them as well.

Trying to understand this game might be difficult at first. There is no real helpful system in the beginning unless you ask a developer or someone who's played what to do. I had to ask the creator numerous times, " What do I do now? "
However later in the game it shows small clips of where your objectives are located. Though the game still does not easily map out the locations with a minimap or compass which would help with the navigation of the game which may be it's biggest fault.

PROS -

- Interesting atmosphere
- Unique concepts
- Challenging enemies
- Interactive story driven campaign
- Suspensful horror with a mix of amnesia type feeling.
- Dark and ominous music fit for this game.

CONS -

- No Minimap, system of navigation.
- No real backstory of why you are in purgatory.
- Difficult and sometimes glitchy campaign


I'd have to give this game a rating of a 7.4/10 It's not a perfect game but it's a good one you should pick up. Hopefully the developers can add a minimap or navigation system soon to make the game a lot more enjoyable to everyone who has high hopes for it's future development.

First impressions. Always a little cautious about horror games. 1) Because I'm not a huge fan and 2) Seem to be a dime a dozen trying to cash-in on the craze. Can happily say thus far Vapour has surprised me a great deal. While it's not without it's flaws and I will get slaughtered for daring to even mention it. Had strong flashbacks to games like Hexen and Heretic during my playtime. Mostly because of the level design and puzzle integration rather then monsters.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UFZWoS9WByo
Gameplay is straight forward enough. You play the role of a psychotic warlock known as 'Charles' who is more the a little peeved off at being kept captive and tortured for the better part of eternity. Broken free from purgatory he now must make his way through the borders of hell in some vain attempt to reach earth, reclaim his life and seek revenge on those who sent him there in the first place. Plays out rather well and more story then expected, cursing is a little overused just for effect but it's not entirely out of place.

Gamepad didn't work by default but it's unity so controls could probably be rebound through the launcher, keyboard+mouse controls were great. Problems were the lack of in-game options since unfortunately mouse sensitivity was through the roof and I had to tone it down via dpi settings instead. FOV sliders would be a welcome addition as well. As someone who is prone to motion sickness thankfully was able to play without vomiting though felt screen space could use expanding.

If you enjoy horror or dark adventure games it's worth checking out. Just keep in mind it contains more then a few jump scares and it's not just about 'running and gunning' as the store page might suggest . Few minor bugs that should be addressed in patches as well. Devs have plans to add future (free) content so we'll see how that goes, as this is first impressions cannot say how much gametime Vapour currently contains. Cheers folks. :)

Likes:
Non-linear level design
Bioshock-esque magic system.
Puzzles that require some thought
Creative monsters

Dislikes:
Physics bugs
Load times could be improved.
Repeated jump scares (uses 1-3 effects so far)
No Achievements

NOTES:
Gametime isn't logged correctly since I play offline.
Developer provided a steam code. Never changes my thoughts.
