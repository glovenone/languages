About This Game

Mystic Towers is an action-oriented and puzzle-filled adventure about exploration and magic. With over 30 different monsters able to crawl, slither, fly, roll, hop - and anything else you can imagine - there’s no shortage of game to play and enemies to slay.

Baron Baldric can jump, push, pull, flip switches, open doors, climb ladders and succumb to poison. He also has a map and various weapon spells, including fireballs and venom.

Features


Never Too Old: Navigate 540 rooms packed with monsters and puzzles using a mouse, keyboard or joystick.

Save Your Game: Save your game whenever and wherever you want!

Master Magic: Use spells like: Healing, Reveal (which finds secret areas), Levitate, and Teleport.

New Tricks: Look under objects, push buttons, flip switches and move almost any item.

Traverse the Tower: Climb ladders to new levels, teleport to new locations and cross invisible bridges and platforms.

This is one of the best games in the 3D Realms Anthology.

I've played this many times over the years, and while yes this recommendation has a fair bit of nostalgia behind it, I would not recommend many of the other Anthology games the same way I recommend Mystic Towers.

You're an old wizard and your goal is to destroy the monster generators in 12 towers, along with all the monsters. BUT you have a piddly amount of health. AND you have to eat and drink to survive. AND if you want to kill monsters within the next century, you have to conserve your consumable spells. This game is tough. It was tough for 10 year old me when I bought it and it's just as tough now.

You move in an isometric grid, the game is real time and has you moving through rooms of a tower. The levels are not randomzied which thankfully allows some strategy when approaching each one. Monsters roam throughout the rooms however, making it a bit more complex. There are a variety of spells, most of them are damage spells, there's a healing spell, a teleport spell (only between set teleport points), a levitation spell, a reveal spell, and a bomb spell (used to destroy monster generators). The meat of the game is finding the best ways to kill monsters from across the room without them eating you. Because monsters are moving constantly, the worst feeling in this game is using your last lightning bolt only to have the monster move to the side. Wizarding ain't easy.

You can control it with a mouse if you really want to lose quickly, or use a keyboard. Best option is a gravis gamepad but if you own one you have probably played this before.

What sets this game apart isn't the obtuse controls or (personally) insane difficulty. It's things like the sound and music. The noises enemies make as they attack you are ingrained in my mind forever. They're indescribable grinding screams. There is no tutorial level. As opposed to say, Commander Keen 4 where the first level is an upbeat village toon, the second you step foot in a tower an ominous tone plays and you realize you have no idea what's going on.

The monster design is another huge part. Sure you have your basic spider, you have a dragon, you have a floating goat head with spinning knives, you have your three legged earthworm, your classic steam powered single-wheeled lizard robot. But there's also fun monsters like ghosts and other things I can't even describe without sounding even weirder.

You can get drunk in this game and fart and spin around and make happy noises in the middle of a dungeon.

This game is great. Even if you turn it on for only ten minutes and die to a snake wearing its hood as a dress, you'll have a good time doing it.
