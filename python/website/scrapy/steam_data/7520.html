About This Game

A breathtaking journey into Antaloor's past.
After the downfall of Aziraal, God of Fire, the Dark Lord Gandohar has nearly achieved his objective to disrupt the balance between the elements. Dark magic surges into the land to fill the void. Recognizing the potential for complete control over the dark powers, Gandohar starts to use the power of Aziraal trap within Kyra, the descendant of the Orphans. Despite her ancestry, Kyra cannot endure the strain. With Antaloor already in his clutches, Gandohar concocts a new plan from his stronghold in Oswaroth. There are still those who struggle against the tyrant, hoping to shift the balance of forces in their favor. The fight for Antaloor continues...
Five years have passed since dramatic events brought the whole world to the edge of the abyss. The hero is a prisoner in the dungeons of Gandohar's castle. His hopes of saving his sister vanished with his freedom. Just as despair threatens to overcome him, hope comes from where he would have least expected. The Orcs, a race the hero had always hated, have put together a rescue squad that frees the hero from his bonds. He emerges into a land desecrated by evil. He embarks on a journey to shed light on Gandohar's dark past, hoping to discover a weak point in his enemy's defense. If he fails, he will lose his sister forever.

This game. THIS GAME...
After playing the first Two Worlds it would have gotten a rather, barely passable score on account of it's lovably screwed up nature. From borderline nonsensical dialogue from trying too hard to emulate old english to the mindless hack and slash style of gameplay as well as the primary character having such a screwy nonchalant voice which sounds liek it was ripped straight out of a car commercial at times, makes you chuckle to yourself at it's absurdity...

In Two Worlds 2? All of that is gone. All the charm that made the original one lovably awkward are stripped in favor of making the game more 'mainstream.' The quirky broken old english is gone, replaced with generic voice acting that tempts you to just skip dialogue altogether. The primary voice actor for the main dude you play is now an intense Batman-Christian Bale wannabe to the point it's eye-squinting, ear-fatiguingly obnoxious and 'tryhard' sounding in a word.

The combat is more of less what you could expect from Two Worlds, however it feels much more 'slippery' here on account of the console-friendly approach they took...As a result it feels -way- less appealing and equally less 'solid.' The charm, once again, is gone here.

And the story, uuuggh. It's dreadful. Suddenly, out of nowhere, the viscious orks you defended yourself against in the first game (and even sieged their homeland if you wanted to!) are the 'good guys' who are being..prosecuted by the evil king... I get the feeling they tried -way- too hard to go with the 'let's do something different than the norm!' strategy. To further back up the aforementioned point: dwarfs are nonexistant, as are elves. This is -not- an improvement. At all. All it does is make the game staggeringly -more- stale than it already is. And the ending? Without spoiling, I'll just say it was overwhelmingly unsatisying.

Oh yeah, and the game is short as hell too. I slogged through the to the end, hoping that it would get better and...it never does-- and then it abruptly ends before you can even explore the majority of the mainland continent (reserved for co-op only apparently...How insulting.)

Do NOT buy this game.

Just finished the game and... well, now this is a hard one to review...
Do i recommend it? yes, but...
and it's a pretty big BUT.

Overall the game is pretty decent, but it does have a lot of flaws.
The first few hours i absolutely hated the controls, but i got used to it.
The graphics are still quite decent for a 2011 game.
The voice acting varies from very mediocre to actually quite well.
Quests vary from poorly written from actually quite well.
Combat feels weird, but once you unlock skills it's really not too bad and can be quite fun.
I went melee, but i believe a mage could be a lot more fun if you look at the possibilities.
The story is better than i expected, but it still leaves much to be desired.
The ending also has some serious plot holes if you ask me.
The open world is kinda nice to explore, but still too bland. I much prefer the worlds of the Risen/gothic series (games which are comparable). At one point you get a boat, it works well... but it's actually completely useless.
I found the Swamp act quite fun and interesting, but the last act was a grinding bore.
I could go on a long time about this game, but this sums it up pretty well . I went from "meh, wtf is this" to "damn, this game is quite fun" all the time. Sometimes i couldn't stop playing, other times i didn't know if i was going to finish it. You can feel the game was too ambitious and they didn't pull everything off.
I had fun playing through it overall.

If you see it on sale and don't have too high expectations, it can be fun. Just don't expect something like The witcher series. If you can see past its flaws, it's pretty decent and fun.
But really, i have had rarely such mixed feelings about a game.

Lots of negative reviews for this title, and somewhat undeservedly I think. Fine, I had to register before I could play. That's hardly on the scale of the asshattery that is the UPlay client now is it?

Although I enjoyed Two Worlds, it had a few flaws, especially in regards to inventory management and crafting. Fortunately, Two Wolds II improves this and it is much better, although I wish some parts of it were streamlined - felt like I spent half my time melting items I had no use for, waiting for stupid â¥â¥â¥â¥ing animations to complete so I could upgrade items, and tedisouly selling necklaces and rings which otherwise take up space for no good reason.

It's not a bad RPG, the story line wasn't the best (OMG I must save my sister! After I go win a few fights, do some horse races, pick some flowers, etc etc), but there was lots of quests, places to go and things to see, with a wide variety of skills, weapons and armour. The magic system seems interesting too, although as always I played a warrior character, with ranged backup. One of these days I'll actually play an RPG as a squishy. Maybe.

While the open world areas had a bit of variety, I was bored sensless by the dungeons. Many were repetiive with the same sections of caves etc, and all were dull dull dull.

Graphically, it's nothing special, and while the different regions have their unique appearances, it's a bit bland and repetitive.

Audio is hit and miss. I really disliked how my character tended to chuckle like a homicdal maniac after killing a foe or made some other stupid comment. I especially found it irratating when I'd enter a place I'd been before, and the character would remark "now where does this go"... erm, you've been here before dumbass.

Combat is OK, ish. You don't have a huge amount of options really, although giving someone a fistfull of arrows is generally entertaining. Some mobs are resistant to certain things, but overall it didn't make much of a difference.

Balance was odd, the boss fights seem to ramp to a scale of difficulty that didn't match anything else and it was very noticable. However, that could also be becuase you are expected to use the alchemy system, and being the hoarder that I am, I didn't want to waste materials inventing pots. The only pot I made turned out to be poison resistance which eventually came in handy for one fight. Definitely should have tried that one more.

I also played the Pirates expansion. Although it started off fairly OK (and I really enjoyed being able to carry on my character with all his XP, items and skills), the last part of the game was an absolute pile of crap and I really hated it. Mind you, it didn't start off on the best foot when they used a different actor for the main character and the voices sound totally dissimilar.

While Gothic probably remains my all time favorite RPG, this one isn't too bad either and I look forward to the third game in the series.

Hit and miss in many respects, but overall more enjoyable than not, completed the base game and the first expansion.
