About This Game

Abandoned is the first part of big story about parallel worlds. Those worlds are very strange, because people left them forever. So, you should explore those worlds and find your brother who dedicate his life for finding out the way to Abandoned and disappeared there.
Russian version included.

Nice short (~1 hour) room-escape puzzle/adventure.

I have to explain the genre a bit: "room escape" is a genre of small games (typically free flash games) that originally had you puzzle your way out of a locked room by searching the room and using objects and contraptions found within to ultimately find the key. There's no storyline (or not much of one), often no hints or help (except for the obligatory Youtube video walkthrough), and the door you need to open to escape is usually readily visible but locked. The genre then expanded to allow for multi-room scenes, and one of the most famous representatives is Mateusz Skutnik's "Submachine" series of games. Abandoned is so well done that many people (including Mateusz himself) found the parallels striking. It's a very good room escape game.

You can find Abandoned on the usual sites on the net. You can play it there for free, but if you buy it on Steam, you get slightly better graphics (the images were scaled down for free online publication), and of course you're supporting the developer. I did play it on Kongregate (it was in my "to do" playlist, actually) and then bought it here for 50% off (release promo).

After downloading, instead of being ready to play, a game installer starts which actually installs a DRM-free copy of AbandonedHD on your computer. There's a bit of a glitch as the game is showing screens to the right and left (and on a bigger screen presumably also top and bottom), but you can probably ignore that. After that, it's all point&click and brains.

Hints that I would have needed:
1. If you are exploring extra well, you can find up to 4 secret symbols that will allow you access to as many bonus story pages at the end of the game.
2. The puzzle at the very top with the cube and the two switches can be solved right there, it doesn't need anything that is elsewhere.

The playthrough took me about an hour (of course the walkthrough is only 9 minutes, but that's ok). The production values are cheap: it's a flash game with hand-drawn graphics; but the soundtrack is effective, the puzzles are very well designed, and the game works well.

It's short, it's cheap, it's recommended - and if you have doubts, look for the free version (search for "Abandoned" and "Krutovig") and check it out before you buy. Or get a refund.

Short and sweet Point-and-Click adventure game.

Your brother went adventuring in the mountains of Tibet and found something mysterious and exciting. But he disappeared, leaving only journal entries giving you tantilizing hints. Follow in your brother's footsteps and discover the mystery!

The game consists of a sequence of screens that you can click left, right, up, and down to move (if possible depending on location) from screen to screen. There are items you can pick up and use. Unlike some other point-and-click games, this one does not hold your hand and show you possible moves. Click everywhere until you figure it out. Usable items will show up at the bottom of the screen in your inventory. There are also journal entries your brother left behind that you can gather and read for clues. Use the items by clicking on them in your inventory and dragging them where you want them used.

The puzzles are mostly logical and mechanical. Though once or twice there were times when it didn't make sense because I could have done something in real life but the game wouldn't let me do it. But overall they were good puzzles. Some of these point-and-click games and hidden object games have crazy uses for items. I'm happy to say that this is not one of them.

The graphics are a huge plus for me. I love the cartoon look. Or hand-drawn, or whatever it's called. It just looks so cool to me. But that's personal preference, and you can decide for yourself if you like the screenshots.

The music is just kind of spooky ambience background sounds. Nothing special. But it definitely fits the theme of the game.

Time to Finish: 1 hour or less. Depends on how quickly you figure out the puzzles. Also there are optional bonus items to find that reveal some more of the story. You can find them along the way or return to the game after you finish.

Oh, and I guess there's a free version online somewhere. Just so you know.

This was a short and yet interesting PnC game. It's actually quite nice looking. It wont win any graphics awards, but it looks perfect for what it is.

All told, it took me around 45 minutes to complete. One puzzle in particular was quite difficult, but the others mostly involve finding pieces that you then go back to a previous area to use and unlock another area or another piece. Standard PnC fare.

The story itself was sparse, and yet completely worked within the scope of this game. It did feel like it ended prematurely, or at least felt like a cliffhanger from where a sequel would commence. No word on whether that will or wont happen, but in my opinion, it should.

I feel like this game is a nice interlude between other games. I'd say it is worth the asking price, but you may want to wait for it to reach dollar status before buying if you arent really into the genre or arent willing to take a chance on a shorter game. That said, it's definitely worth your time. It wont blow your mind, but you also wont regret playing it.

I think all told, I'd give this around a solid 7 of 10.
