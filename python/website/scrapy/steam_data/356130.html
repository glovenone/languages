About This Game

Heroes of scene is a multiplayer strategy game with an indirect control. You are in charge of the Kingdom of live toys. Gameplay is based on the two general mechanisms. First one is the development of your castle. Second one is the attack and defense. You can summon toy warriors and spells from the magic cards. The summoning uses resources, which you can get from sawmills, textile mills and laboratories. Battle scenes in the Heroes of scene usually last less than 15 minutes.

• Your deck – your regulations!
Collect your own deck of heroes and spells. Plan your own strategy to win!

• Fast gaming session.
The battle rarely overruns more than 15 minutes allowing you to play on the way home or during work breaks.

• Build your castle!
You can build your own kingdom with a laboratory, a sawmill or even a forge.

• One account for all devices.
You have one account which is playable on all your devices giving you the ability to play on a tablet or PC using your favourite characters and spells.

What to say about Heroes of Scene? Simple. Don't play it!

No-one provides anything for free, and this is a perfect example. You play cards to summon units, but unsurprsingly, the best units are locked behind microtransactions. Every player online seems to have the best units, and you don't stand a chance against them. A microtransaction model in itself means that you have to be able to unlock the units through natural progression, though this aspect of the game is *broken*. You only are awarded cards when you win a battle against a human opponent, and you can only realistically win when you have *already* forked out for better cards!

I was thinking that because it advertises itself as a 'single-player' game, maybe I could just play against the AI, and rack up some wins there? Well, you sure can play against an AI! ...Only for the tutorial level though. To play any proper matches, you need to play with other players. You're not getting out of their payment scheme *that* easily!

You can buy new cards in the shop right? Well, I actually had enough tokens for a single pack, so I thought I'd check that out. You get three heroes or spells... or so you think. The screen after opening the pack shows that you got three cards... though if you check your library, you'll see you only actually got one of them! A heavily microtransaction-driven game that actually *scams* its purchasers!? Wow!

The gameplay itself is actually quite fun, and could make for a decent game if it was implemented properly. Sadly, it is not. It is incredibly glitchy. I tried to adjust the game to windowed mode, and found it odd that the toggle was set to windowed mode already, though the game had loaded fullscreen. I had to toggle it to fullscreen and back to get it to window properly. After doing this, it locked my mouse to the top two-thirds of the screen, so in order to fix that, I had to adjust the screen resolution twice as well.

The developers seem to have cut a lot of corners everywhere. I checked the official achievements to try and get an idea of what exactly the game has to offer, and got more confused than I was after trying to adjust the settings! The developers are clearly non-native English speakers, but they didn't even *attempt* to make the instructions in English. Seriously, a Google Translation would have done a far better job. That's a nitpick, but just goes to show how little the developers actually care about their game.

Ignore the fact that this game is "free", and avoid this game like the plague.

=== OUTDATED REVIEW ===
Please note, this review was posted 6 days after game launch. A lot has seemingly changed since then. This review focuses on what at the time, was a very big problem with the game and what many other players felt needed changing (see older forum threads).

The devs and the gaming community have since said that Koshey (a red card) is no longer imbalanced. As such, this review would no longer be relevant.

I'm leaving the review "as-is" until I play the game again, in which case I'll update the review accordingly. So until then, please take this review with a grain of salt.

========================

Perhaps the single most P2W game on Steam.

There is only 1 card you need to win this game: Koshey. Period. He's a red card (epic). There are only a couple dozen different cards (there isn't much variation at all), but this one, single card dominates them all.

If you do not have Koshey, yet your oppoent does, exit the match. You'll be wasting time otherwise. Koshey takes very little damage from any other hero card in game, takes very little spell damage, has a big chance to rez when (if) he dies, and 1-2 hits any other hero in the match.

Koshey can be defeated by playing A LOT of other cards against it...each taking a quick death, but doing at least a small amount of damage to him. The problem with that however, is that if you have Koshey, you get him out asap...before the opponent can get more than 2-3 heroes out.

Koshey, by himself, will march down the lane, take out 2-3 heroes very easily (which is at max, how much can be created in the same time period), then destroy the gate.

It's an absurd card that makes the game completely unfun. But, everyone recognizes this to be true, so most people will spend money on packs until they get him.

There is only 1 meta in this game: Koshey.

Interested concept...but one of the worst deliveries possible.

Understand that the review of this game is based on a patch released on 17th September 2015, meaning that some of the problems I have with the balance of this game might be changed if you are seeing this review on a later patch :)

Starting off, I don't consider this game pay to win like a lot of people are saying. The people saying this probably got matched into a couple of bad games and thought that the cards that other people had were unlocked through in game transactions. I got all of necessary cards by simply playing the game.

Gameplay & balance
In terms of gameplay, the game is extremely unbalanced and can be hard for new players who don't understand how the level scaling works. When starting off, only having 3 cards and not having a solid strategy, the game can seem boring and unfair (not saying it isn't, but maybe not as much as the first impression might suggest). It generally consists of throwing out your robot and reaper in the lvl 1-2 range, beating your opponent in 30 seconds if they put their resources into production and didn't plan to counter your attack. Until lvl 7ish the game will consist of these cheesy strats, from my experience it seems you get random cards from matchmaking and so you might have a bad experience between these lvls, but by the time you get to around lvl 7ish you should have enough of the cards to make a solid deck. The problem I have at this point is that the "Sabotage" card is way too strong and completely controls the game. Whoever uses this card effectively, stopping your opponents resources and research will win. There are not enough cards in the game to allow varied decks, and the Sabotage card has to be in your deck at the higher levels or you lose. This is the main problem I have with the game, there is no where near enough variety in cards making the game boring after you get to this point.

I will give the game some credit, it can be fun for those who hate the large amount of micro-management in modern rts games but like the idea behind it. Getting a strategy together with different cards and finding the best way to manage your resources can be fun, but it still doesn't help the fact that there isn't enough variation in the games mechanics for it to be fun for over 10hrs.

Bugs and "Bugs"
So what about how the game runs? Does it have noticable bugs? What about the community?
Well I can't say there is much positivity here. The game runs fine which is somewhat expected from what is essentially a mobile port. The big problem comes with the random crashes and some weird bugs that will force you to restart the game. Can't say much about the crashes except that they are random and somewhat frequent, never found a cause, never found a pattern as to when they happen. Adding to this, me and some friends who played the game encountered a bug in which the game would stick on a loading screen after a battle. The only way to get past this as far as I know is to restart the game. This bug seemed very frequent when I first started playing the game, though it seemed to happen less and less the more I played the game.

So onto the main bugs I encountered in the game. The bugs are mostly just annoying, but annoying is still taking away from the game experience and I think its important to point them out. First up is a wierd bug in which your heroes will just teleport back and forth as they are going along. This can be a problem when using spells on your heroes, but its not a huge problem outside of just being really annoying. Somewhat similar to the first bug is the strange way that rooms work. Resources seem to update very slowly, clicking to build a room can be slow with the building appearing a second after clicking to build the room. Probably doesn't seem like a big problem, but it just makes the game seem so out of sync and awkward. Final bug I've encountered a few times is a bug when searching for a match. So you want to play a game against another player, click play and wait... and wait.... and then you get impatient so you try to cancel the matchmaking and it does nothing. Turns out it was glitched and it was probably not searching for a match anyway. So you have to restart the game and click play again, takes 10-30 seconds and you are in a game. Again, its mostly just annoying but when annoying is so frequent in a game its hard to start liking it.

So I'll quickly mention the global chat and move on. Most of it is russian which is fine because you are not very likely to make friends on this game anyway. The problem comes with the random spam and racism that appears in the global chat. There is no community for this game so don't expect to find other people willing to make friends.

Conclusion
So if you want to pass the time with a fun little game I would say go play another indie title like binding of isaac or FTL. But if you don't have the money to get those titles and you happen to come across this game, give it a try. I enjoyed what I played then got bored really quickly. It has bugs to fix and needs to sort out its balancing, but its free to play and has a very small file size, so just try it out. If you can get past the bugs then you might just have fun playing this simple strategy game about pitting toys against eachother in a staged base race.
