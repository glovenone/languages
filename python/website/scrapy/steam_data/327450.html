About This Game

Reemus the insect exterminator and his companion/minstrel Liam the purple bear embark on a journey across exotic lands as they match wits with giant talking insects and bumble their way towards writing a ballad of their adventures that will finally impress the citizens of Fredricus once and for all! A fully voice-acted, funny, cartoon point and click adventure.

Journey to the Apnea Swamps to face the menacing but lethargic Bed Bugs. Escape the depths of the Termite Mound prisons and the jaws of the towering termite soldiers. Fight back against the powerful and scheming fire ants. Encounter bizarre creatures like the miles-long noserpillar and the hot-tempered honey bee. Marvel at the illustrious plant-life, like the ketchup and mustard plants, magnet flowers, mattress-shaped water lillies and illuminating light bulb vines. All this with tons of unique puzzles that tease your brain but have just the right amount of casual difficulty.

Ballads is based on the world and characters of the hit web game series The Several Journeys of Reemus and tells a completely new story. We brought in accomplished indie voice actor Josh Tomar to play the part of Reemus to give the universe a new level of immersion, along with a cast of 36 total voice characters.

Features:
  • Fully animated cutscenes tell the tale of just how far a small-town exterminator is willing to go to outshine his famous dragon-slaying brother
  • 36 voice acted characters to meet
  • Tons of hand-drawn environments to explore
  • Optional side quests to discover with in-game achievements to unlock

Developed by ClickShake Games, an independent game dev studio founded by Jay "Zeebarf" Ziebarth and Steve "EntropicOrder" Castro. Ballads of Reemus is the team's first commercial game, developed over a 2 year period and totally self-funded with the help of over 500 pre-orderers.

A nonsensical Point 'n' Click but a fun one that doesn't take itself too seriously!
Verdict - 7.3/10

Scroll to the bottom for an 'Overall' on the game.


Gameplay - 7/10:

When it comes to Point 'n' Click adventure games; there's not really much to them. You can pretty much boil them down to 3 sections: Gameplay, Story, and Graphics. Luckily for Point 'n' Click games though, these sections can be very diverse topics! So, Ballads of Reemus, the best way to put the gameplay - such as the puzzles and the overall progression of the game - is that it's nonsensical. By this I mean that it's some of the puzzles, and ways to obtain a certain item don't make sense and are... 'Convenient', however, it's not a bad thing, it adds humour the game. Overall, the gameplay is alright, just a little... Random.


Story - 9/10:

The story is as nonsensical as the puzzles! The characters are great with some good voice acting accompanying them, the progression through the world is great and it takes you to some weird places. The entire world is basically based on taking sayings literally, so for example, the 'Bed Bugs' are massive cockroaches that just sleep all day... And the horse flies... I think you can guess at this point. Overall, I really love the world this is based in, it's basically if a pre-schooler made up their own ideal world with Ice Cream Cacti and Breakfast Trees!


Graphics - 6/10:

There's not really much to say about the graphics except that it they really remind you of the old school Flash Style, the type you've seen on Newgrounds... In all honesty, I really like these graphics just because of how 'crisp' they look!


Overall - 7.3/10:

I like this game, maybe it's because it's one of the first Point 'n' Click games I've played for a while... Regardless, I enjoyed it, and if you're a fan of Point 'n' Click games then I think you will too! It's about 3 hours long by the way.

Pro:

- well-drawn and remarkable art design.
- good voice acting.
- some funny moments / dialogues, jokes never feel forced.
- puzzles are easy to solve and straight-forward. Good for people with little experience in adventures.
- reappearances of known characters (if you played Several Journeys of Reemus)
- Mini-"Sidequests" (clicking on pieces of something scattered around and then putting them together)

Con:

- no hot-spot feature, which could be useful since some items can be kinda difficult to see.
- It's short. Took me about 2,5 hours to beat it.
- while the voice cast itself is pretty good, the quality of their voices could've been better.


If you are interested in this game, I would recommend you to play the "The Several Journeys of Reemus" first. These are free flashgames which you can find on many websites, e.g. www.clickshakegames.com or www.kongregate.com. They give you a pretty good impression how Ballads of Reemus works. If you find yourself enjoy it or are already a fan of the series (like I am): Grab it, it's definitely worth the money.
I'm really looking forward to Ballads of Reemus 2 now. :)
