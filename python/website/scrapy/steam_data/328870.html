About This Game

If you've ever wanted to step into the front office of your favorite pro hoops team this is your chance! Draft Day Sports: Pro Basketball 4 puts you in charge of your favorite professional basketball franchise. Draft, trade, sign players in free agency, and build your team into the next championship dynasty.

You create the league you want. From multiple league sizes, draft format, limited or full financial models to allowing overseas and high school talent to enter your annual league draft. Hire your coaching staff, sign free agents, make trades, negotiate contracts terms, and draft college talent.

Take a more direct or indirect approach to running your team. If you don’t like how your head coach is running the team on the court, fire him and take over the team as General Manager/Head Coach. If a player’s attitude is disrupting the team, try to work with him or trade him away. You set your team’s player rotations, depth chart, and even directly manage in-game strategy on the court. Or just let your coaching staff handle those player and game day management duties.

Rookies and prospects get to compete in summer league games to showcase their talent. Send players to your affiliated D-League team for in-season development. There's even a fully playable European League option. Track and evaluate players with the latest statistical metrics - see where a player took every shot from and what his percentages are from each area.

A full historical league almanac will keep track of every single season played for every player and every team. The in-depth news system will keep you up-to-date on the latest big performances, trades, and rumors from around the league. It’s all here to create your ultimate “dream league” pro basketball experience.

Features
  • Control the vision, development and strategy of a professional basketball franchise over decades.
  • Draft college prospects, sign free agents, hire your coaching staff and put together blockbuster trades.
  • Choose from a variety of league setup options to create your own “dream league”.
  • Realistic, detailed financial models. Juggle the budget expectations of your team’s owner and the salary cap to build a winner.
  • Financial changes allowing the salary and luxury cap as well as player salaries to increase over time.
  • Build your basketball championship team according to your unique philosophies and strategies.
  • Manage the egos and personalities on your team. Do you support your coaches or trade the players?
  • Rebuilt UI with a brand new look and widescreen support.
  • Robust stat tracking from individual player shot charts and new advanced stats like PIE.
  • Combine stats added for rookies to use for scouting rookies to draft.
  • Fully playable European League and a full 32 team development league.

I would absolutely recommend this game.

It has its flaws. Bugs can sometimes instantly ruin your game (so be sure to save often. Particularly before every draft), draftees sometimes have really weird tendancies (had a guy go 1 of 328 on 3 pointers for example. Can be edited in game), and editing the game files isn't as simple as I'd like it to be (have to do it through Access. None of the free alternatives seem able to open the .dat file successfully. You can edit most of the attributes in game, but not all and it will be only for that league, whereas editing the dat will update all new files). The roster isn't up to date (and very few historical options are available, and require seperate purchases.) Comes with 2014-15, so some players are rated far below (or above) where they are now because it was made at the start of that year. Updates are very few and far between. I wouldn't expect any more past the current 1.3 update just released. The game takes up the entire screen, and doesn't seem to have a windowed (as in, scalable) feature. When you double click the top bar to make it windowed, the game screen does not adjust and results in being zoomed in. Need a decent resolution to play (for example, when I try a 1440x900 resolution, everything worked except for the draft, in which the buttons would be blocked with the draft board, thus not allowing you to continue past the draft.). Perhaps it's just that resolution, but I'd highly recommend downloading the demo from their site to make sure no graphical issues take place).

As for the good points, the simulating seems very realistic and is quite in depth. Each player is thoroughly different And allows for a complete team building experience. The difficulty is reasonable, and you won't usually find yourself building a perennial contender in a few years. Their is a coaching staff system implemented, although rather basic. They tried to remedy this in DDSPB 3, where they had offensive sets, but removed it in this version. The 2d play by play presentation is quite good. Trade logic is sound (and sometimes annoying. Haven't gotten close to the best thing from their side, supposedly. Argg) and the game is sustainable for very long associations. It is, in my opinion, by far, and another comma for good measure, the best basketball simulator available.

So once again, in it's entirety, I fully recommend the game, I have spent many, many hours playing the game outside of Steam, and got it on Steam since it was in a bundle and I didn't have to email the developer to deactivate my key when I had more than 2 licenses installed. Now, as I do recommend the game, I would moreso recommend waiting for the next game in the series, Draft Day Sports Basketball 2016. It was announced December 8th of 2015, although not much information is available. Why I recommend waiting? The game will come with a full historical replay feature all the way back to the post NBA/ABA merger. If you're as much of a fan as historical play as I am, this is a must have feature. If you want one now, then this is the best simulator. If you'd rather have a lesser simulator (although still solid) with full historical replay (even farther back. Not exactly sure how back, but before the days of Wilt Chamberlain definitely), then I would recommend Fast Break Pro Basketball. The Fast Break game also has a nifty historical shuffle feature, but this isn't a review for that game, so I'll leave it at that.

EDIT: Now that it's released, I fully recommend Draft Day Sports Pro Basketball 2016 over this or FBPB 3. It has a host of new additions, from new attributes to a new historical mode (start at 1976, play onward). It also is much more stable, in my experience.

As far as NBA or basketball sims go these days, this is probably at the top in the pure management / statistics market.

I've been playing the DDS:PB series for years, and it has evolved into a solid management simulation with good attention to detail and a fairly accurate simulation model.

To get it out of the way early, yes there are some bugs. The match engine is fine, it has more to do with the interface. One example is when you go to draft a player in the NBA draft, only to see no players available. Deselecting a position in the player filter and reselecting fixes this, but it can lead to some confusion.

More annoying is you will occasionally get an exception error while playing. My recommendation is to click okay, save, and then quit and restart the game. This has been an issue with 3 as well, but as the game is updated these bugs get narrowed out. Unfortunately I was unable to get the gamecast function to work as this error would occur every time. 2D play works fine, or you can quick sim. Hope they fix that. EDIT: The crossed out issues can be fixed if you run the game as an administrator.

You can expand the league to new cities if you wish (Seattle and Las Vegas are the default new cities), or you can customize the game settings to your liking. Real rosters are included, though team names and logos are different. But that's fine for me, since real players are all I wanted to begin with. You can also generate random players.

Playing games or quick-simming them is an option, and there is a full free agency period and coach hiring phase as well. There is some commentary during the draft, though it can be a bit buggy if trades pop up after a selection is made.

Coaching is well done, and you can play as both a coach and GM, or as either alone. Your owner will have expectations for you in GM mode, though you can disable GM firings if you wish.

Once you get the hang of the new interface (quite different from 3 and 2) it seems to navigate well.

The market is quite shallow for good basketball management sims (World Basketball Simulator is not worth recommending). This and the 2K series are your best bets.

I enjoy this franchise immensely, so give it a shot if a deep management sim is what you're looking for. But for those looking for NBA action on the court, stick to NBA 2K.

Before I got this game on steam I played it for many hours, probably around 50. I have also logged at least that many house into DDSPB 3 and I have played DDSPB 2 as well. The game has both pros and cons, but it consistently gets better and it is bar none the best baseketball simulation game on the market.

Yes the game has bugs and some aspects of it can be unrealistic, but if you truely love sports simulation games and basketball simulation games in general you really should consider supporting this game, because it is the best of its kind and will only get better with more support.

I am going to go into detail about this game and explain all of the flaws and strengths in the most accurate way I can. Before I start, let me state that I love basketball and especially the emerging statistics and team building theories that are present in the current NBA. I have been a lifelong Golden State Warriors and Oakland Athletics fan. I was very involved in the advanced statistics movement in baseball and Moneyball is one of my favorite books of all time. I noticed that basketball had never adopted advanced metrics in the same way baseball had. Watching the Warrior's lose year after year turned me into an armchair GM. There are great outlets for MLB simulation games like "Baseball Mogul" and the "OOTP" series, but none for basketball. I scoured the internet and played each and every attempt at making a basketball sim. I desperately wanted to play the role of the GM and see if I could do a better job than the professionals. I wanted to draft players and see them grow into super stars, I wanted to trade overrated assets for underrated ones. I will never be the GM of an NBA team, but I wanted something to simulate that experience. This is the ONLY basketball game that comes close to what I wanted. So now without any further babeling, let me list the pros and cons in as much detail as I can.

Pros
-Working Basketball simulation game(only one of its type.)
-Accurate Salary cap structure.
-The ability to scout college players and make intelligent drafting decisions.
-You get to choose coaches with have different styles, that can effect your team in certain ways(coaches are not too "overpowered" or "underpowered.")
-Good trade AI(You cannot trade a whole bunch of junk for a superstar, assets must be given up to get an asset back, just like in the real NBA.)
-Retired players sometimes become coaches.
-Realistic name generation for rookies.
-Lots of advanced stats to look at.
-You can customize players.
-There is an expansion draft, to allow two more teams into the NBA and it works realistically.
-There are lots of options, you can be a coach and GM, the owner's ability to fire you can be toggled on and off, you can coach/GM in the d-league, you can micromanage as much as you want, you can generate random players instead of real NBA players, you can coach each game individually.
-Free Agency is generally done well, marquee players sometimes will not sign for terrible teams.
-Again it is a basketball sim that works, the only one of its kind.
-The game is being updated and will improve so some of the cons may be fixed.
-There is an active community surrounding the game that is very helpful.
-"Commisioner Mode" allows you to correct a lot of the flaws manually.

Cons
-Some bugs, usually there are work arounds, but occasionally a save can become unplayable(This rarely happens, but can happen.) Occasionally error messages pop up, eventhough there is no real error, you just press "continue" and move on, this kind of scews with the excellent immersion of the game.
-I have heard some lower resolution computers have problems displaying all of the text on the screen,
-The most damning thing for me is that the computer generated players sometimes have really wonky stats, like a highly rated PF that shoots under 40% but is highly rated because he mindlessly takes a whole bunch of inefficient shots(this type of player would be out of the league in the real NBA.) You can trick the AI by trading these horrible but highly rated players for actual assets, therefore making the game somewhat easy. I remedy this by going into "commisioner mode" and adjusting some of these players to be more realistic. Also sometimes a good 3pt shooter will shoot 55% on FTs(This is rare.) Occasionally a player will be unrealistically efficient and be underrated because he doesn't shoot that much. The AI rates players who shoot a lot too highly basically and the shooting percentages can be wonky.
-The D-League Draft takes too long(not that big of a deal.)
-Sometimes the positioning assigned by the AI is strange. Like a PF may play SF eventhough he has no business doing so(this can be corrected in commisioner mode.)
-Coaches, coach well into their 70s a lot of times which is really rare in the actual NBA.
-Some awards are non existant (playoff awards, most improved player most notably.)
-Some of the player/coach/commentator graphics are amateur looking and might be better off simply not existing at all.

So in conclusion this is a far from perfect game, but nevertheless it is wildly enjoyable if you enjoy sports simulation games. Ultimately as flawed as it can be, it is one of my favorite games. I tried to be exhaustive in this review, because I have a lot of faith that someday this series will produce the definitive realistic basketball sim experience. It is currently very close and the creator deserves feedback and encouragement for working so hard on producing a realistic basketball sim. For the people who give this game negative reviews, they should look at it in the context that this is the ONLY game of its type and it deserves accolades for being ground breaking in its own genre. If your passionate baout basketball sims or sports sims in general this is a great game and it will only get better over time.
