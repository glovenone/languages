Greetings MechWarriors,

The Free Rasalhague Republic continues to be the shore upon which the bulk of the Clan Factions have broken; Clan Ghost Bear and Clan Smoke Jaguar are all but devastated, reduced to controlling only their regional capitals. As Clan Wolf fights to maintain their ground, Clan Jade Falcon has torn through Steiner territory with the strength of the falcons claw, ever-focused toward the shining jewel of the Inner Sphere.

The Inner Sphere is fractured; there is no doubt. The time has come for Operation Frontline.

To all Clan and Inner Sphere MechWarriors alike; do not allow inter-Factional conflicts and grudges to blind you to the true enemy. The focus of Operation Frontline is strictly upon Clan vs. Inner Sphere battles.

Battles which pit Clan vs. Clan or Inner Sphere vs. Inner Sphere will not contribute toward the Goal Conditions for this Event.

Warriors of the Inner Sphere, send your forces to augment the frontlines and break the tide of the Clan Invaders.

Warriors of the Clans, focus your combined strength against the Inner Sphere freebirths and achieve victory.

If your Faction does not have a direct Attack Lane against the opposing side, you must use the Call To Arms system to fill the ranks for critical Clan vs. Inner Sphere battles.

Whether you're a veteran of Faction Play or a green recruit, this event is a great opportunity to jump into the fray, help tip the balance of power, and earn some excellent rewards!

About This Game

MechWarrior Online™
MechWarrior Online™ is a tactical, 'Mech-based online shooter set in the rich BattleTech Universe. The battlefields of the 31st century are dominated by immense and powerful mechanized units known as BattleMechs. Piloting these war machines are the most elite soldiers that the galaxy has ever known. To most, they are known simply as MechWarriors.

You will assume the role of an elite MechWarrior, fighting for supremacy over the galaxy against other players.

Quick Play is where you'll find fast action, fielding a single 'Mech in team-based game modes and maps. Unlike Faction Play, Quick Play matches do not influence the layout of Inner Sphere territories; these battles are fought purely for rewards and glory.

In Faction Play you will field 4 ‘Mechs per match, lending your skills and allegiance to the Faction of your choosing in prolonged team-based engagements, conquering and defending planets in their name to earn rewards and expand your Faction's territory across the Inner Sphere.

Gameplay
Piloting a 'Mech to victory requires a mastery of tactical positioning, teamwork, and firing discipline. Strike out as a Lone Wolf or seek out one of the many player-controlled Units and fight under the banners of powerful galactic empires for prestige, wealth, or nothing more than the sheer thrill of battle.

Next Gen Mobility - By the 31st century, tanks have evolved to take on humanoid shape, their treads replaced with nimble legs. Like modern tanks, 'Mechs move their upper torso and weapons independently of their legs offering unparalleled mobility while exchanging fire. Depending on their size, BattleMechs travel at vastly different speeds and offer different systems like jump jets to aid in their locomotion.

Make Every Shot Count - Pilots must balance mobility and damage output against the amount of heat generated from their weapons fire and 'Mech operation, lest they risk the temporary loss of power that comes when a 'Mech overheats. Firing all weapons simultaneously (known as an Alpha Strike) can be the quickest way to devastate an opponent, but may leave a 'Mech helpless against its enemies if the heat sinks can't handle the stress.

Fight Smart - 'Mechs possess both external armor and strong internal structural elements to help minimize the chance of a 'quick kill'. As a result, pilots must treat each encounter with the utmost attention and awareness, prioritizing weaker or more critical components before delivering the killing blow. Communication among teammates and the effective use of targeting systems can help to identify vulnerable 'Mechs and components.

Teamwork is the Difference - Targeting a 'Mech will automatically allow your teammates to achieve their own lock, allowing them to ascertain vital location data, armor status, and loadout details for the targeted enemy. Information Warfare can be a vital component for victory, providing attack-focused allies with the necessary information to tear their opponents to pieces.

Information Warfare - While fast-moving scouts feed target and tactical information back to the main battle group and the team commander, long-range Support and heavy-hitting Brawler and Assault ‘Mechs will use this information to position themselves for the perfect strike. Pilots can also shield themselves and allies from enemy sensors and weapons fire using various counter measures. It is up to each pilot to customize their BattleMech loadout to fulfill their desired role.

Find the perfect 'Mech - Hundreds of unique 'Mechs are available, offering a wide range of strengths and weaknesses. Smaller 'Mechs should never stop moving, and can rely on their sheer speed to overwhelm heavier opponents. Larger 'Mechs move slow and deliberate but can stand toe-to-toe with other large opponents. Acquire and test different 'Mechs to find those that best suit your play style.

Choose Your Allegiance - Work for the powerful empires of the galaxy to earn their loyalty and spread their influence across the stars. Band together with other pilots using the Looking For Group feature, or use the Faction Chat feature to coordinate offensive and defensive strategies to capture or defend worlds and shift the balance of power.

Get Rewarded - Pilots earn ComStar Bills (C-Bills) from playing matches and completing faction contracts, which can be used to purchase new 'Mechs and equipment.

Certain items can only be purchased using premium 'Mech Credits (MC). MC can either be purchased directly via real world money or acquired in smaller increments in-game by earning reputation with a Faction in Faction Play, or as rewards from participating in special Events.

'Mech Experience (XP) gained during combat is applied to the 'Mech used during that match, and can be used to unlock improvements to various BattleMech systems.

MechWarrior Online™ also includes a host of achievements to unlock that will challenge even the most skilled players.

Grew up with BattleTech and the MechWarrior series, so my opinion might be a little biased, but I love this game. F2P isn't the easiest thing to balance apparently, but I think they did a good job here.

There will be grinding if you want diversity and mastered mechs (you need to obtain 3 of a chassis and grab tons of XP to get this) - not to mention the pilot skills which require an XP hit to unlock AND a C-Bill hit to purchase.

New mechs are coming out all the time and the devs are pretty good about releasing new maps and/or upgrading the older ones. You've gotta pay to get into the new mechs when they're released, but eventually they become available for either MC (premium currency) or C-Bills (grind money).

It's free, so you should try it out. Read up on some lore, too - BattleTech's is pretty awesome. :] And if you do end up playing, don't feel bad when you inevitably spend the $20 to get some new mechs + early adopter rewards. The devs gotta eat!

Et in Arcadia Ego

Posted: 30 June
The year is 3535. Humankind has made great progress, especially in terms of finding new and unique ways of killing one’s neighbor. Unfortunately for the denizens of the 36th century, theirs is a future that was conceived by bored DnD players in the mid-1980s. This is manifested in their weapons of choice: large legged robots the size of apartment buildings. Tank treads? Puncture-proof off-road wheels the size of a barn? Nah. These bots have size 357 feet, ferro-fibrous shins and thighbones made of endo-steel. As you might guess, this makes them clunky as all get out. They have 1987-esque optics to match. As you lumber along on your skyscraper legs, you might see enemies peeking out over yonder hills. You might want to flag them for your teammates. Not happening. Not until you can see the whites of their cockpits. Your futuristic laser pointer has a similar range and makes a visible line that lets the enemy trace it right back to your armor-encrusted jeep-sized fist. And when that enemy shoots at you, you do what any 36th century advanced attack platform does: flinch.

The above is what makes this game absolutely awesome (or should I say atlas?). Driving around big clunky robots makes for far more strategic combat than your typical 2016 FPS. The least clunky robots are light mechs. They move almost like they’re not the size of buildings. Mediums behave kind of like the heavy in TF2. Heavies behave like the TF2 heavy wearing concrete boots, and assaults play like the TF2 heavy with concrete boots and a concrete helmet. What does it all mean? It means that you need to lumber along with your teammates instead of just doing whatever you want. It means you really need to capitalize on terrain and cover to protect yourself or creep up undetected. Perhaps the best thing is that driving an armored skyscraper means that you have a lot of armor and will not crumple the first time you get hit. Don’t get me wrong, there ARE snipers, but these snipers do not sit around one-shotting everything that enters their crosshairs. I have been headshot once and scored a headshot once (totally by accident).

This brings us to the mechs themselves. You have two varieties. The more traditional ones are like tuner cars and hot rods (Inner Sphere). The latter are more like Italian sportscars (Clan). One requires a lot of time and tweaking to get up to speed. The other is pretty solid out of the box. The great thing is that there is a vast variety, and you see people using every make and model out there. There is, of course, a metagame, but builds beyond the meta are viable, and you have enough room to come up with something that personally works for you that may not work for the greater MWO population. I like that.

The greater MWO population is generally a friendly, well-behaved bunch. Yes, there are some droolers and screamers, but they are a decided minority. The community as a whole is older than you would find in most games, and the kids you encounter tend to be the ones who are grown up enough to sit at the adult table without being childish.

This is a result of the gameplay. Strategy trumps reflexes, and the game encourages you to linger after you die in order to get more assists and, while doing so, learning from watching more experienced people play out the rest of the game. This pace is not for everyone, but I enjoy it. In between matches, I do a combination of watching my team, researching builds, and at times surfing the net. With a max of 15 minutes a match (and the average probably around half of that), you play the game in bite-size chunks.

I really enjoyed my initial day or two with the game, but I was playing while traveling in Europe. Living somewhere off the beaten path, I feared that the servers would not be functional from home. I was wrong. Pings are not as solid, but the game is still very playable, and it plays incredibly well at pings that would make other games unplayable (150+).
The devs are engaged with the community. I’m still on the fence about how this relationship is going. After all, what online game doesn’t feature players criticizing the devs for anything and everything? At the same time, what business entity is angelic in its handling of online games? The good that I see is that we are now playing a game that has 3 years of development put into it. That means those who came before me sorted out the worst problems. The bad is that certain aspects reek of greed and price-gouging. Paint, for example, costs more than real life paint that you would put on miniatures. One discussion I saw about improving the HUD seemed framed by how the players would pay for such an improvement. In the realm of neutrality, there are a lot of game modes and whatnot that may or may not be coming out in the future. I’d love for them to be implemented, but I’m reviewing what’s there. Furthermore, the game has the licensing from the tabletop game, which means this IS the DnD of mech games. That is important to me because it attracts the diehard, long-term fans and thus makes me feel like this is a solid investment that will not suddenly go bust or implode. It’s not perfect, but it’s stable.

And investment brings us to money. This is THE best f2p model I’ve ever seen. Instead of paying to win, you pay for variety. Variety is more meaningful to me than bling, and the variety here is compelling. There are scores of mechs, but you get only 4 mech bays to start with. If you want more mechs, you have to pay for more bays. The bays are cheap. Beyond that, there is the temptation to shop for more. Yes, the mechs are expensive, but I get to pick and choose as opposed to being forced to pay a subscription fee or some such. And, if I only want to pay for mech bays, I can do that, too. The game will proceed far slower, but no one is being denied the best gear because they don’t want to pay cash for it. Is this a grind? Yes, but the grind is the game. I’m enjoying the ride.

Along similar lines, even newbs are vital parts of the team, and even newbs can get out there and hurt somebody. You aren’t out there getting the floor mopped with your corpse on your first days out, but it is obvious how little you know about builds and mechanics. There is sooooo much to learn, and yet so much fun to be had before you actually understand the half of it.

Perhaps most importantly, understand that this game was out for years before it came to Steam, and that means most of those playing it are not visible at the Steam community hub. The community is larger. Not huge, but again solid. And there ARE new people joining. You will not be the only new arrival, and it’s not too late to get in on MWO.

In short, I love this game. If I could compare it to anything, I’d say it reminds me of the days (in fact years) I spent playing Random Arenas in Guild Wars I. Those were good days, and it’s nice to be back in a similar place.
