About This Game

“We thought they were our friends and benefactors, but instead they are our masters. The Chronicles didn’t liberate us from our primitive ways – they conquered and destroyed us. The others don’t know, their heads are still full of the melodious voices of our alien masters. But that will change. I will change it!” – Sasaki

Survive in Space is a skill-based space shooter with a hardcore focus on gameplay mechanics. You take on the role of Sasaki, a young soldier who discovers the horrible truth of humanity’s enslavement at the hands of the alien Chronicles during a routine mission. His deep anguish leads him to spiral into depression, and in the darkest depths he finds The Shadow, a primal being full of hate that grants him the power to confront the Chronicles.

FEATURES

  • Over 50 different levels
  • 5 powerful bosses that you will encounter at the end of each sector – every nine levels. Each boss is a skill puzzle of his own, with shifting mechanics and environments that will keep you on your toes.
  • Over 20 types of enemies split into three different categories: defensive, offensive and supportive.
  • 4 different difficulty levels
  • Game modes: Additional enemies, Planet fortresses and Asteroids. Active them to make game more interesting.
  • 5 differenet player ships – each with its own strengths and weaknesses. Find the one that best suits your play style.
  • Dual Mode. Dual Mode allows Sasaki to toggle his ships into Offensive and Defensive configurations, sacrificing firepower for durability and vice versa.
  • No player level limit cap.
  • 9 different abilities upgrades
  • Full controller and mouse support, customizable keyboard keybindings
  • Music player with over 90 songs. You can add your own music too.

Fun, but a bit too simple. The later levels aren't much different from the earlier ones; they just take longer. The enemies later have more hit points than the earlier ones, but they behave the same way. The bosses were fairly interesting, but the regular waves got a bit dull.

In all, this is a good game, but it could use some polish.

If you've played Asteroid Bounty Hunter, then this game is going to feel very familiar. When I started playing Survive in Space, I didn't realize that both games were by the same developer, nor that they were released a few months apart from one another. I can definitely say one thing though, this game is a huge improvement over its predecessor.

The basic goal of the game is to survive several waves of alien attacks. A variety of ships, all with their own patterns and weapons, will assault the player from both sides of the screen. Your survival depends on balancing the ship's offensive and defensive capabilities. Playing defensively involves the use of rotating shields, which protects against enemy fire, and possibly collisions. However, you'll soon discover that the ship is underpowered against the constantly encroaching adversaries.

So that's when you have to trigger... the Edge. This is the aggressive mode, which causes the shields to expand outward, your HP to drop by about 40%, and your damage to substantially increase. In this mode, you definitely have to rely on your dodging skills to survive, but at least you'll have less trouble destroying foes. Alongside these modes, the player has access to a main cannon, as well as two secondary weapons. Both the laser and homing bullets operate on cool-down timers, so it helps to save them for truly necessary situations.

As with Asteroid Bounty Hunter you can level-up your ship. Aside from increasing its offensive and defensive capabilities, you can also unlock new abilities for the ship, and add new functionality to its weapons. Above all else, I recommend grabbing the upgrades that increase the range of your attacks. By the end of the game, you'll have a huge spread-shot, a laser that covers both sides of the screen, and a large mass of homing bullets.

The level of challenge is thoroughly customizable. Aside from difficulty settings that increase the enemy's attack and defense, there are also modifiers. The enemy waves can be bolstered with more troops, and assisted by Death Stars. Also, if you didn't get enough from the last game, you can also toggle the option to be assaulted by asteroids. Boosting the difficulty and adding modifiers will increase the experience rewards, so it's a fair trade-off. However, don't be surprised if you can't survive with everything turned on... at least before you upgrade your ship.

As far as complaints go, I have some nitpicks. A number of achievements are tied to the difficulty level, but let's say that you complete all of the stages on "medium". Normally this means that you'd also get the achievement for doing the same on "easy". Unfortunately, it doesn't work like that. Every stage has to be beaten on their respective difficulty in order to obtain all of the achievements. This can make large stretches of the game really dull. Naturally, this is all entirely irrelevant if you don't care about achievements. Still, this minor issue does tie into the larger problem with this game. Even with all of the modifiers and the difficulty maxed out, the game becomes a little too easy.

For the next game, I think that it's important for the controls to be refined even further. While the player-ship controls decently with the analog stick, it isn't nearly as accurate with a d-pad, which is really odd. Without improving the controls, I don't think the challenge of these games can be taken to the next level. It would be interesting to see this style of game-design, but with bullet-speeds and patterns typical of the arcade shootemup. You know, something like Radiant Silvergun.

In conclusion, if you were a fan of Asteroid Bounty Hunter, then you're guaranteed to love Survive in Space. By all accounts, this is a superior sequel. If you weren't a fan of the previous game, there still might be something for you here. Still, I'm a little surprised that the devs can hammer these games out so quickly.

DARKKi's Dark Star Reviews - Survive In Space

Survive in Space is an side-scrolling shoot em up (SHMUP) but not an bullethell SHMUP. Game only has one mode which is story mode and story is actually pretty good but in my opinion the story cutscenes has an amateur quality to them and voice over is an mixed bag as some are pretty decent and others are just the opposite.

Game mechanics are pretty straightforward. You fly to one direction and shoot everyone. You can change your facing which is nice bonus and you can also change your "stance" to defensive or offensive where both offers some bonuses. You also have four energy balls which circle your ship and in offensive stance they are spread further away from ship and you can damage enemies with them and in defensive stance they surround you ship and help block projectiles / enemies. Your arsenal consists of normal attack (machinegun) and two others which has cooldowns and they are laser and homing missiles.

This game has some RPG elements put into it as you can level up your ship, weapons and pilot (no level limit), which is nice if you cannot beat some stages you can grind some levels. Also before starting level you can adjust your difficulty, add more enemies, add asteroids and add starbases which all give you bonus experience. These are 50 levels and bosses every nine levels but there is no other mode than story so it affects replayability somewhat.

Graphics are okay for this game. They could be much better but it serves its purpose. While some effects are really cool looking (laser with lots of upgrades for example). I also noticed some slowdowns few times which is odd considering how this game looks and as my hardware should run this kind of game flawlessly, but it only happened few times so not a big deal. Sounds are pretty decent and serve their purpose and music is kickass! with 90 songs within and you can add your own music too! I did not encounter any bugs in my gameplay and game seems finished.

Rating and Should you buy this game

7.5/10

If you like sidescrolling shoot em ups you should try this game since its pretty cheap and it really hooks you up and those upgrade rpg/upgrade mechanics add much to gameplay as you essentially become an weapon of mass destruction but there is only story mode to play so that limits replayability to some extent. There are few things i did not like with this game and those are graphics which could be much better and more polished. It's not an original game but it has mashed up things from other games pretty well but still has it's own problems. I had pretty good time with this game so i would recommend it if you are looking for games like this.

Pros:
  • Great way to adjust difficulty by several different options (and to add how much exp you get)
  • Great soundtrack plus ability to add your own music
  • Upgrades are really nice
  • Nice variety of weapons and different stances
  • Price

Cons:
  • Graphics could be much better
  • Replaybility as there is only story mode (you can replay missions though)
  • Cutscenes look really amateurish and some voice over is not so good

Survive in Space is a 2D space shooter, based around Sasaki, who lives in a galaxy that has been taken over by an evil people known as "The Chronicles." Sasaki discovers the truth about The Chronicles and the history of his people, and must fight to earn redemption.

Presentation
In game, the graphics are great, and all the art compliments each other perfectly, making this game a feast for the eyes. Outside of the gameplay, however, the graphics seem to be lacking.

The main menu's art is quite nice, with an animated picture of Sasaki on a seemingly deserted planet. The graphical design of the actual menu part, however, leaves much to be desired. While when the player hovers over an option, the menu fits with the aesthetic of the game, when the player is not hovering over the menu options, they are just gray boxes with a font closely akin to Arial. This font is used throughout the game, and it really draws away from the rest of the game's aesthetic.

The art style in the cutscenes is extremely unpolished compared with the rest of the game, even to the point of looking unfinished at times. The voice acting in these cutscenes is very much a mixed bag. The narrator and the "servant of darkness" are both voiced extremely well, but the main character, Sasaki, is not. The voice actor sounds like he is reading a story to a kindergarten class, and manages to do it with no emotion. Any emotion given to Sasaki's character through the voice acting seems very forced.

The music, while it sounds great, does not really fit in with the atmosphere of the game at points. The player can also add their own music, as long as it is in .wav or .ogg format.

Interface/Controls
The user interface is generally very easy to use. The only major problem with it is that to access the upgrade and ship select screen, the player has to leave the level select screen to go to the main menu, where they must select the "Upgrades" option. A casual player who has never played this game before might totally miss it, as it is not mentioned in any tutorial, and try and get through some of the harder levels without any upgrades, or without realizing there were other ships in the game.

The controls are quite comfortable and make sense. On a keyboard, the game uses the MOBA system for attacks, using Q, W, E, and R for attacks, while on a controller it uses the regular buttons.

Plot
The game follows Sasaki as he fights The Chronicles. He has the truth about The Chronicles revealed to him by an ancient writing of some sort, and is then seduced by a "servant of darkness," who is also seeking vengeance against The Chronicles.

The script is poorly written, with Sasaki's emotions or thoughts only ever being revealed through him saying directly what they are. It does not flow well, with the characters often speaking in short or fragmented sentences, and going out of the way to make sure that the audience does not have to infer anything.

Gameplay
The game tries to be a sort of bullet hell space shooter, but fails in that it is too slow paced. This becomes apparent at the very beginning, when the first enemy to spawn takes eight hits with the base weapon to kill. This does not change as the player levels up - at level one, that enemy takes eight hits, at level five it takes the same.

Ther is a large variety of ships for the player to chose from, each having its own advantages and disadvantages. The only problem with this is that there is no indication of this capability from the level select screen or the main menu. To change ships, the player has to go to the main menu and select "Upgrades," then go to the "Shipyard" tab.

The player is given three weapons - the basic weapon, a laser, and homing missiles. The basic weapon is able to be fired every tenth of a second or so, and the other weapons take a few seconds to recharge, which can be upgraded to be less. The basic weapon is a machine gun which does about two damage to an enemy. The laser is a solid laser beam which does more, and the homing missiles are, well, homing missiles. They do a ridiculous amount of damage, but take a while to recharge.

The player is also given a "super mode" upon pressing R, that makes the players attacks more powerful, but moves the ship's shields farther away, rendering it more vulnerable to attack.

The player's ship can only shoot in two directions: left and right. This, coupled with the fact that the ship moves quite slowly, makes defeating enemies a chore sometimes. The player's ship also cant move to the very top or very bottom of the screen, rendering enemies in certain positions unreachable.

Replayability
After completing the 50 levels with the game, the player can continue leveling up indefinitely, playing through those 50 levels again and again, eventually becoming ridiculously overpowered.

Conclusion
Survive in Space, while promising, is plagued with a myriad of problems, chief among those being the slow pace and a lack of general polish.

Survive in Space is a 2D space shooter, based around Sasaki, who lives in a galaxy that has been taken over by an evil people known as "The Chronicles." Sasaki discovers the truth about The Chronicles and the history of his people, and must fight to earn redemption.

Presentation
In game, the graphics are great, and all the art compliments each other perfectly, making this game a feast for the eyes. Outside of the gameplay, however, the graphics seem to be lacking.

The main menu's art is quite nice, with an animated picture of Sasaki on a seemingly deserted planet. The graphical design of the actual menu part, however, leaves much to be desired. While when the player hovers over an option, the menu fits with the aesthetic of the game, when the player is not hovering over the menu options, they are just gray boxes with a font closely akin to Arial. This font is used throughout the game, and it really draws away from the rest of the game's aesthetic.

The art style in the cutscenes is extremely unpolished compared with the rest of the game, even to the point of looking unfinished at times. The voice acting in these cutscenes is very much a mixed bag. The narrator and the "servant of darkness" are both voiced extremely well, but the main character, Sasaki, is not. The voice actor sounds like he is reading a story to a kindergarten class, and manages to do it with no emotion. Any emotion given to Sasaki's character through the voice acting seems very forced.

The music, while it sounds great, does not really fit in with the atmosphere of the game at points. The player can also add their own music, as long as it is in .wav or .ogg format.

Interface/Controls
The user interface is generally very easy to use. The only major problem with it is that to access the upgrade and ship select screen, the player has to leave the level select screen to go to the main menu, where they must select the "Upgrades" option. A casual player who has never played this game before might totally miss it, as it is not mentioned in any tutorial, and try and get through some of the harder levels without any upgrades, or without realizing there were other ships in the game.

The controls are quite comfortable and make sense. On a keyboard, the game uses the MOBA system for attacks, using Q, W, E, and R for attacks, while on a controller it uses the regular buttons.

Plot
The game follows Sasaki as he fights The Chronicles. He has the truth about The Chronicles revealed to him by an ancient writing of some sort, and is then seduced by a "servant of darkness," who is also seeking vengeance against The Chronicles.

The script is poorly written, with Sasaki's emotions or thoughts only ever being revealed through him saying directly what they are. It does not flow well, with the characters often speaking in short or fragmented sentences, and going out of the way to make sure that the audience does not have to infer anything.

Gameplay
The game tries to be a sort of bullet hell space shooter, but fails in that it is too slow paced. This becomes apparent at the very beginning, when the first enemy to spawn takes eight hits with the base weapon to kill. This does not change as the player levels up - at level one, that enemy takes eight hits, at level five it takes the same.

Ther is a large variety of ships for the player to chose from, each having its own advantages and disadvantages. The only problem with this is that there is no indication of this capability from the level select screen or the main menu. To change ships, the player has to go to the main menu and select "Upgrades," then go to the "Shipyard" tab.

The player is given three weapons - the basic weapon, a laser, and homing missiles. The basic weapon is able to be fired every tenth of a second or so, and the other weapons take a few seconds to recharge, which can be upgraded to be less. The basic weapon is a machine gun which does about two damage to an enemy. The laser is a solid laser beam which does more, and the homing missiles are, well, homing missiles. They do a ridiculous amount of damage, but take a while to recharge.

The player is also given a "super mode" upon pressing R, that makes the players attacks more powerful, but moves the ship's shields farther away, rendering it more vulnerable to attack.

The player's ship can only shoot in two directions: left and right. This, coupled with the fact that the ship moves quite slowly, makes defeating enemies a chore sometimes. The player's ship also cant move to the very top or very bottom of the screen, rendering enemies in certain positions unreachable.

Replayability
After completing the 50 levels with the game, the player can continue leveling up indefinitely, playing through those 50 levels again and again, eventually becoming ridiculously overpowered.

Conclusion
Survive in Space, while promising, is plagued with a myriad of problems, chief among those being the slow pace and a lack of general polish.

Survive in Space

DISCLAIMER: I received this product for no cost for the purpose of reviewing.

The team at Just1337 must really love space and space themed games, as this is there 4th space game on Steam. Survive in Space is a 2D Space Shooter. The story is kind of interesting so I won't spoil it, but throughout the stages of of this game, you pilot a space fighter. You can choose between 5 initially, though I'm not sure if more unlock as you continue to play. Each ship has a set passive abilities that are different from the other ships, and you can choose a ship to cater to your playing style. You can also switch what ship you want to use before each stage. You have a basic attack and 2 other skills you can use in combat, and after each stage your character receives experience as well the ship that you are using. When your ship levels up you can assign more passive abilities to it, and the same applies to your character.

The combat itself puts you in a space battlefield for each stage. You are tasked with taking down all enemy ships as they appear on your screen with your various abilities to complete the stage. You can manage the difficulty of each battle from the classic presets of "Easy, Medium & Hard" as well as choose to add more asteroids (which pose as flying obstacles) as well increasing the amount of enemies you have to defeat. Of course the more difficult you make it for yourself, the more experience your will accumulate. The game can be a bit bullet hell-y at times, and there are boss battles at certain stages.

--------

Graphics: 7 / 10

The art style might be subjective. It's a 2D western animeish design for the characters, which isn't something I tend to enjoy but I could see how a lot of people would appreciate it. The back drop for the battles is decent, though I feel it does get a bit repetitive after awhile, although I haven't played all the stages to know if it's different later on. The animations are also decent, and the combat effects as well as the menus are designed pretty nicely. There shouldn't be any problems running the title on any graphics card or CPU as it is not a demanding title.

--------

Sound: 8 / 10

Everyone has different tastes in music, but I feel like anyone who can enjoy EDM music; this title has a nice soundtrack for you. I personally enjoy the music in this game, it reminds me a lot of their other title Neon Space, which I believe some of the music is also from. I noticed in the music interface that they have various types of EDM styles like: Drumbass, Dubstep, Electronic and House music, and as someone who enjoys this type of music, I would rate it pretty highly. There is also options to skip to the next track as well as shuffle and repeat features. The in-game and combat sound effects are also decent and surprisingly didn't feel repetitive. There is also some voice acting within the game which is also very subjective.

--------

Mechanics: 7 / 10

For a title like this, the mechanics are pretty solid. You have your basic attack, and your 2 other skills. You more around with either a controller, your arrow keys, or your mouse, and shoot down enemies on your screen while avoiding their fire. Unlike some shooters, you also have the option of flipping your ship and shooting in the opposite direction. I do find it a tad frustrating not being able to shoot up and down at times, though I feel the controls might be too messy if something like that was implemented. You can switch between two different modes while playing, attack mode and defense mode, which both offer benefits and disadvantages. The enemy variety is pretty good, there's quite a few different looking enemy ships with different abilities. You can upgrade your ship as well as your character with experience at the end of each battle as well as toggle the difficulty level.

--------

Final Personal Remarks

I think Survive in Space is a good title for beginners who want to get into 2D shooters. It's not as intense as some bullet hells but it can give you some rush and satisfaction when you're able to take down all the enemies. The art style isn't something I personally enjoy but I do like the soundtrack and how you can upgrade your ships as well as your character. The story seems interesting but also kind of cliché, but I see it hooking a lot of people. In my experience with playing the game, I preferred to stay in attack mode rather than defense mode. It's a bit riskier when you take a hit because you take more damage in addition to having your health points already lowered, but you deal a lot more damage, and it's easier to take out enemies.

Thanks to the Devs for giving me the opportunity to review the game, as well as anyone who took this review into consideration, I hope it helped.
