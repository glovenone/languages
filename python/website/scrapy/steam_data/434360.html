What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Kitten Rampage enters early access with endless crazy kittens! Currently in alpha stages, improvements are being made daily. Adding more content including levels, community integration, and player customization are just a few of the many things to come. Kitten Rampage was chosen for early access so that players can really enjoy while the title develops.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“8 to 12 months”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The scope of this project is semi-large. Early access version contains plenty of content to play, explore and grasp the full feel of Kitten Rampage, plus what it offers in fun and future gameplay.

The early access version will contain bugs and glitches. Regular updates will be pushed out adding more content and fixing any problems. We will be involved with the community, taking in suggestions and interactions! Full version will include a mass amount of content. Plenty of maps/levels, players ability to customize their character (fur color, eye color, effects, etc), level creator, and more!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The early access version of Kitten Rampage is very stable but may contain bugs and silly problems.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Prices may gradually raise as we ship new content and features.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We enjoy being involved with you, the player! Everything like more achievements, awesome trading cards, and more are always coming out! Of course everything takes time, but many plans are in place.”

About This Game

THIS IS YOUR KITTEN, THIS IS YOUR RAMPAGE

Kitten Rampage enters the arena with the latest in kitten technology. High end weapons, lasers, and explosives are all equip and ready for use on your adorable Kitten! Thoroughly cooked and coded by a team with marvelous dreams of becoming adorable kittens.


This silly, fun, broken all-in-one game includes gameplay with many explosions, many deaths, and many more kittens with hearts full of rage! Destruction is a must; destroy as much as possible! Conquer the world from humans and other hostile enemies as the deadliest kitten alive!

Review: 06/03/16 (Early Access Alpha)

Portable Description
Currently in an alpha state, Kitten Rampage is a kitten simulator reminiscent of a similar game of the goat variety that offers destruction at an epileptic level with an aside to scatological (toilet) humor.

Gameplay
Seizures Warning
It is important to understand that this game may induce epileptic seizures. The warning is hidden in the extended âAbout This Gameâ section in the Store page and the game fails to emphasize this upon launching. Kitten Rampage utilizes strobing lights upon shooting (which is almost always consistently activated), and the explosions present variations of intense particle effects.

Objectives
Kitten Rampage offers 4 main game modes: Time Trial, Free Roam, Expert, and Survival. Each mode contains a set of objectives that must be completed in order to clear the level. Some of these objectives may be achieving a certain score, killing a number of enemies or reaching a location. Throughout the game, there is also a hunger, thirst and health meter that âneedsâ to be maintained. The objectives are attained with the help of two shooting weapons that can fire simultaneously and indefinitely. Grenades can also be âpukedâ out for higher firepower. There are only 12 achievements in this game and most of them can be attained in half an hour.

Level Design
The maps and levels in Kitten Rampage are pretty generic, utilizing ordinary objects and locales. Often times, there is a lot of empty space making traversal a bit of a chore. Also, the sprint button cannot be toggled, a much needed quality of life improvement, making most of the gameplay uncomfortable from constant pinky usage.

Some of the objectives, such as killing X enemies, get extremely tedious due to the level design. Some rooms seem hidden, and many buildings blend into the background making these enemies annoying to track down. Having to redo levels due to the inability to discover the last couple enemies is extremely frustrating. A time bonus mechanic upon killing an enemy would help remedy this.

Presentation
Graphics
The graphics and aesthetics for Kitten Rampage are really poor. Stock assets were used, and the objects seem too two-dimensional. Upon closer observation, most of the repeated patterns that the assets utilize are either color gradients or basic low quality textures (no shading/light variation). There arenât any thematic colors or styles used to decorate the levels either. The kitten can, however, be personalized to an extent (fur/eye color).

Physics Engine
The physics engine is also very weak in this game. Clipping occurs everywhere, collision detection makes no sense and object disintegration are too similar which each other. Everything seems to be made up of flat panels or boxes, as observed upon destruction. Portions of the level can cause the kitten to clip so badly that it will get permanently stuck.

Conclusion
Current State of the Game
Although the game has not been released for long, the patches seemed to have waned from launch. For a developing game in Early Access (especially one that is in its alpha stages), it is crucial to be transparent with future content. The developer(s) do seem to take part in the Steam Discussions board; however, there hasn't been any updates for quite some time. Currently, there seems to be no one playing Kitten Rampage, according to Steam's community statistics. With such a strong similarity to another certain animal simulator, and having the same price, it will be very difficult to grow the player base without a lot more content/refinements added.

Verdict
It is imperative for any simulator to have a strong physics engine and intriguing maps along with an ample amount of varied goals/objectives/achievements to add longevity to the game and improve re-playability. This game has neither, making it very difficult to find an audience that can manage to overlook the frustrating bugs and tedious nature of the gameplay in order to enjoy Kitten Rampage.
