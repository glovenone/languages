“We’ve continuously released content updates every two/three weeks since our initial release on Steam. The amount of feedback and suggestions helped us in deciding which content to add next.”

“We plan to leave Early Access within the coming weeks. The game is already stable and feature complete at its current point.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“In terms of major features The Curious Expedition is already completed. We will be adding additional content over time.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

About This Game

Curious Expedition is a roguelike expedition simulation set in the late 19th century. Together with famous personalities you will venture on unprecedented expeditions to regions never explored before for fame, science and treasures. Put on your pith helmet and khakis and make your way through a lush, procedurally generated world full of wonder and mystery. Now go explore, adventure awaits!

Gameplay overview

  • Experience your very own adventure through a procedural story which makes every game unique.
  • Explore procedurally generated worlds, each with its own distinctive set of challenges for the ambitious explorer.
  • Plan and equip your trek. A good explorer is prepared for any eventuality.
  • Manage your resources to keep your trek alive and sanity high. Balance your needs with the desire to carry all that precious treasure back home.
  • Visit and interact with the land’s natives. Enter villages, trade and communicate with local tribes and civilizations that are unknown to mankind.
  • Loot mysterious temple ruins to gain fame and treasures, but watch out for deadly traps and curses that will compromise your trek and the world around you.
  • Equip and utilize miraculous treasures to gain advantages, but be wary of unforeseeable side effects.
  • Fight and defend your trek against a wide range of wild animals, mystical creatures and even dinosaurs.

You know those fun little european resource/exploration board games? Well imagine if one of those magically became a video game, and a good one to boot. That's Curious Expidition.

It's an exploration game where you go to visit a foreign land in search of riches and adventure, while at the same time competing with other explorers for fame. First place gets a golden statue made for them in their honor (and a golden border around that explorer in the character select screen). It's a blast to play, and they did a really good job at making almost every single decision important. Do you rest at a spring or push forward? should you attack that pack of velociraptors or sneak by? Do you go to the desert or the jungle? The game really gives you liberty on how you would act in each scenario, but it's not as oppresive as games like say Darkest Dungeon.

I have been playing this game since around beta 21. It has hands down the best developer involvement I have ever seen in a video game. That is not an exaggeration. They promised to ship an update every month, and sure enough every singe month they have either updated the game, or provided an explination for the delay. Every update they upload a video explaining the changes and hint at the content to come.

They really listen to the community too. In the earlier versions, you could talk with slave camps but not attack them. Many people that played the game asked for that to be a feature. Sure enough, as of now you can attack slave camps. It means so much to see such dedication to a project.

Next time you see this game on a sale, I highly reccomend picking it up.

Ok so first I'd like to tell you that my number of hours played are a bit lying, since I bought this game a few days ago, and I litteraly spent my whole weekend playing this game when it was free on Internet.

So first, I wanna give you what I think of positive in this game. I'll begin with the play life of this game, and you have to know that it is barely infinite : you can already replay this game as much as you want, and even if the expeditions are "limited" to 6 per save (which means you end the game after 6 trips, with leaderboards and everything) you can play again when you feel like it ! Everything changes and is set back to 0, so you are totally free to do what you want.
Then, the randomness of this game makes it very interesting. Indeed, since every land you visit is generated randomly at the beginning of each trip, there is no chance that you play twice on a similar land.

Also, the diversity of territories available makes the choice of the destination at the start difficult : even though you don't have the choice before your first trip, you will soon be able to judge by yourself which land you feel like exploring, weighting the pros and the cons of each : do I really feel like taking 60 waters because I'm going to the desert just to avoid the burried pyramid's land ? The choice is, in fact, crucial even before you have arrived at your exploration site. And I'm not talking about the huge amount of "grounds" there are, between the forests, the deserts, the mountains, the hills, the lakes, the swamps...

This leads me to my third point : the free and total gestion of your equipment, your expedition members, your standing (how well the natives consider you), your path, your time... You are litteraly able to influence your trip not only in the good way, but also in the bad one : you can decide randomly to dismiss all your followers and to drop everything you have. This makes the game interesting, especially if one of your follower dies or if you dismiss him. Your relations with the natives are an important point too in the gestion of your trip : do I really wanna steal this idol in the middle of the village, risking a fight if I get caught ? What happened if I steal those gifts to their gods ? The villages are so important in this game you better give everything you have just to make sure you will be able to sleep in every village you reach. The inventory is a crucial aspect of the game too : the choice of the equipment you leave at the ship, what you wanna buy before leaving, what you wanna trade with natives or traders, what you will need for sure and what you can drop. The path you wanna take is then not only given by the relief, but also by what you can use to reach certain areas, and by the animals that can attack you. Because yes, the combat aspect of this game is interesting too; knowing that your followers are really low on health will make you doubt about whether you can try to run in front of those tigers, gorillas, panthers, raptors (yes there are raptors in this game :D )... Personally I really like how the combat are set up : you have your dices, which give the ability you can use (shield, escape, attack, ...) and you can use them as you want, which makes it essential to understand the combat mode.

To get back to the randomness, I have consciously avoided the true enemy in this game : the sanity. Basically every move you do costs you some sanity, which represent the state of mind of your followers and yourself. It is EXTREMELY IMPORTANT to manage to use your sanity well : let it reach 0 and watch your followers getting paranoia, claustrophobia, cannibalism, disappear... How many times did I watch powerless my followers dying one after the other because a f*cking expedition member discovered the great taste of human flesh ? Of course, you can gain sanity, by different ways : resting in waterfalls or under a rock if you are lucky, sleeping in a village, or using alcohol or coke leaves at last chance, praying none of your followers will turned alcoholic or drug addict. This sanity bar will soon become your worst nightmare, forcing you to come back after 20 days to the ship to sleep freely and surely.

And this will be my last point : the feelings I have playing this game. I'm pretty sure I had never prayed to find something useful as much in any other games I played as in this one. Simply because everything relies on luck, randomness and the choices you're making all the way long from the beginning to the end, this game will surely makes you sweat more than a horror game, or a 20 kilometers run. This game is not only allowing you to play a story, you are litteraly writing your story, and this is the best aspect of it.

To sum it up (sorry I love to write a lot about things I love) :
PROS :
-great duration of life
-very interesting and unique
-total control of your expedition, inventory, followers, and so on
-22$ is a very good price
-makes you feel the story
-many ways to finish the game and the expeditions

CONS :
-luck is an important factor
-very "precise" type of game, not any similar ones (FTL, Minecraft, 80 days)
-Tim Timster always turns into a cannibal D:


CONCLUSION
Buy this game, it is 10 times worth his price, very interesting and fun game to play, with free control over your gameplay.


10/10 would steal this altar and eat Miss Donkey again

A game of exploration, equiping your party, hiring people, finding lost temples, trading with natives/traders/slavers. At the end of an adventure choosing to sell the goods or donate them for fame. Walking takes sanity points, which can be replenished by rest locations of consumables - but run out of sanity points and people in the party may act in odd ways.
When you get in combat, you have dice to roll and different characters offer different dice. You put dice together to make attack or defence combos.

I was initially a bit nervous about the graphics looking rather basic, but the interesting story text and strategy helps you forget about the graphics issue.

A key positive point about this game is the many ways you can choose to play - focusing on combat and hunting animals, focusing on trade skills, focus on science comes with later characters unlocked I guess (by some unusuable science items on sale) etc. It feels like the game gives you a fair amount of freedom.

A very enjoyable game - and the developer has said although it's approaching launch that they will add more content.

I like the way you can unlock new characters as you play the game, but I would have preferred a much greater focus on unlocks to keep my motivation.
The current game is a system of playing 6 maps to try for the best score. I would prefer if it was just 1 map per game, but that you can spend your fame/money points on permanent upgrades for characters or unlocking new characters - and playing harder maps allows you to shop for different permanent upgrades or unlocks. A bit like Lost Castle / Rogue Legacy systems. As your explorer gets more powerful you can penetrate further into the map and get more points for buying increasingly expensive unlocks. Then unlocking a new character you are back to being quite weak but with interesting new future options. For me this would be a lot more addictive and satisfying. I hope the developer can notice this comment and consider adding it as an additional separate game mode.
