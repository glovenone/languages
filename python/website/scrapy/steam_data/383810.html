About This Game

Doomed'n Damned is an action game based on retro/classic platform games from Arcades and 16 bit consoles, but revised to the HD era. The level design, characters, plot and difficulty have a lot of influence from the late 80s and early 90s games. The development and production is by arc-gen, an one man indie game studio.


Features:
- 16 Bit cheesy sense of humor
- Epic boss fighting
- Voice over dialogues
- Challenging difficulty
- Classic level design
- Classic weapons and skills
- Multiple path levels
- Secret items and areas

Doomed n Damned is an old school action platformer which takes a great deal of its inspiration from ghosts n goblins.

Control: Control in this game is simple, but very responsive. You have a jump/double jump, alongside an attack button. If you press up + attack you use your sub weapon(like castlevania or ninja gaiden) It is simple but it works very well and I never ran into a scenario where I was killed due to unresponsive controls.

Level design: When you load up the game you are greeted to a map, you start at the first level on the map and then the map branches off into 3 paths. Each path has 3 levels and then the map converges at the end. The first level has 3 different exits and each exit takes you to a different branching path. In order to beat the game you need to beat every level.

Level design in this game is very good. The levels are pretty long, and most levels have different paths you can take to get to the end, the paths typically converge at the end but the different paths leave the player with room to explore and give you options on how you want to tackle the levels.

There is also a nice variety of things to do in the levels, of course you have a few standard platformy levels, but you also get levels that have you riding a horse drawn carriage, riding an elevator while avoiding spikes, running from a huge unkillable monster and other things to keep it fresh.

The challenge in this game is just right. You have 3 lives to beat each level, if you die all your lives, its back to the beginning. The game is not easy but it is not rage inducing hard either. You can't expect to beat every live on your first run but with a bit of practice nothing is insurmountable.

Some levels end with a boss fight, the boss fights are probably the low point of the game for me. Some bosses have annoying unskippable, pre fight dialogue. Not bad the first time you fight but after you die you have to watch the dialogue every time which is incredibly frustrating. The bosses themselves range from fun to fight to incredibly stupid and boring, for the most part they are okay but a handful of bosses almost put me to sleep while fighting them.

Overall, this is a very fun game, it took me 3 hrs to finish and for it's current price I feel you will get your moneys worth. If you are into retro games give this game a shot, the level design, control, and challenge are all just right.

This is the best game I've ever found myself unable to recommend. It's a gorgeous update to the old formula, faithfully offering up gameplay from a bygone era, but in brand new clothes. I want to recommend it. Heck, I want to play it.

Thing is, the developers have put up a road block. And the kicker is that you don't design a game that's this pitch-perfect and do a thing like what they've done cluelessly.

Here it is: the game-breaker. "Full controller support" means, in this case, not that you can maneuver your character around the screen with your analog stick (as you may well have become accustomed to expecting to be able to do), but that you can woodenly limp around the screen with the d-pad. Yeah. I kid you not. Because: nostalgia.

You know they did it to evoke the NES era, but it's just not fun. Enable the d-pad, by all means, but there's no good sense in making my perfectly good analog stick flop there like a dead thing. Shame on you guys.

Fix it, and you get immediate "yes". And more detailed positive stuff. And me playing the game. And--I dunno--donuts. Maybe.

Solid action platformer just like the good old days.

You are the son of the hero who vanquished the bad guy the last time. Now it's your turn to beat up the baddies and save the world!

You start with a throwing dagger that can upgrade in size and power by collecting a power up. There are also throwing axes and a blue energy spell you can find or buy from a traveling merchant. Jump and double jump to platforms to defeat enemies and maybe find hidden things like food to increase you life or gems. Collect a key to open doors to continue to the next section.

Controls feel solid. I used the Xbox controller but you can also use keyboard. I had no trouble with landing on platforms, which sometimes happens with indie platformers. No slipping like the platforms are covered with ice (unless there really are platforms covered with ice in later levels, I don't know yet) and falling to your doom. Nope, in this game when you jump, you land solid. The only problem I ran into was enemies on platforms above you can hit you sometimes because your character jumps so high, even with single jump. But that's just a matter of getting used to how the game works.

So far it's been fairly easy, though I am no expert. From the title I assumed it would be hard as balls, but it's actually really fun. I died in the first section once, then restarted and made it into the second section before dying again. If you're a hardcore platformer you might be able to just whip right through this game in one sitting, I don't know. I assume it gets harder as the game goes on.

Graphics are nice, sort of a combination of pixels and cartoons. You can see from the screenshots if you like them or not.

Sound: The story is voiced by actors. There was a short intro and then another short scene before the first boss. I don't remember the music much, which just means it wasn't super good nor super bad.

Oh, I found one spot where you go could in one of two directions. You get 1 key but there are 2 doors. So that's pretty cool. It looks like a one-time choice, not like it loops around. That's nice for a bit of replayability.

Price seems right for the quality. Though, like I said, I don't know how long the game is yet.
