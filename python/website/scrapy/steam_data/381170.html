About This Game

Immerse yourself in a lost paradise, with a deep mystery hiding beyond its pristine shores.



As a crew member of a suddenly and mysteriously capsized ship journeying through the notorious Bermuda Triangle, you find yourself being the sole survivor on a seemingly deserted island. Deserted but not always uninhabited. Solve the mystery of the lost paradise and claim your destiny. Get back to nature, learn and hone your REAL world survival skills, solve puzzles and live the adventure.

Lost in Paradise is a third person point and click adventure reminiscent of the classics from 90s (similar to modern escape games or hidden object games, but deeper) with spellbinding ambient nature graphics and an art style all it's own.

  • Explore over 25 stunning natural locations with brilliantly alive animations, sounds and weather effects.
  • Enjoy six beautiful, relaxing and mysterious music tracks specially tailored to the environments and puzzles (with in game volume controls).
  • Challenge yourself with over a dozen logic puzzles and mini-games to tease your brain and test your skill.
  • Learn REAL survival skills that you can carry over into the real world, like building a campfire, knife, rope and much more in the wild.
  • Gather clues, notes and items to solve conundrums and unravel the mystery of the island.
  • In game map to easily travel between locations and in game diary to record and store clues.
  • In game tutorial system for newer players. Whether you are a casual gamer or a seasoned puzzler, the game is fit for everyone.
  • In game context-sensitive hint system - If you're stuck, you can get a single hint at a time to nudge you along, without having to leave the game to look at entire walkthroughs online and jump into spoilers.
  • Hot spot highlight system - no pixel hunting, all gameplay.
  • Multiple difficulty levels - Hard for veteran players and Easy for beginners,
  • Multi-language support - currently available in English, German, French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, Swedish, Korean, Japanese, Russian and Hindi.

3.6/10 - More frustration than satisfaction

This game had some cool/basic casual puzzles, but it wasn't enough to make this game a winner. You look at the price tag and see only $3; but that's for 40 minutes of painful gameplay. That's equivalent to spending $50 on a premium title with a long and captivating campaign. <-- No Brainer

The 25 locations remind me of static browser images mixed with some well-done water work from the developer, and a robotic character that awkwardly moves about the map.

The game really lacks the polish or excitement to charge this premium price. The worst part without a doubt is the navigation and item finding; without the help of the hint you can't determine what items are clickable or where to go. This is extremely frustrating and causes you to click the screen a ton without success.

Should have been free or like $0.27; but don't buy it.

A lovely relaxing experience.

When I picked this title up I really didn't know what to expect. It's not reviewed on any of the main adventure games websites and there aren't many reviews on Steam.

When I started it up I was asked whether I wanted to play it on Easy or Hard. I chose Easy as I didn't want to get stuck and it appears that the actual adventure gameplay is the same. The Hard difficulty level just affects the complexity of some sliders / picture puzzles.

On beginning the game itself, the introduction was very simple with just a few paragraphs in a diary to read about how my character got to the island. I thought at this point that it was a little too simple and lame. The story itself is quite bare bones, but it holds interest for the duration.

What I really liked about this game was the wonderful ambient, relaxing music. It's from Cyan Music and it really fitted the solitary exploration and puzzling of the game well. Other sounds in the game are the natural sounds of nature. There is no voice acting as there is nobody at all to speak to and this does not detract at all from the experience.

The graphics are simple but very effective, from snow scenes that give you a sense of the cold to plenty of water scenes and lush jungle / rainforest.

The interface is very user friendly. Combining objects couldn't be simpler and the whole game is very intuitive. Hot spots do need careful searching though, though again there is help in the form of an eye icon that lights up all hotspots.

I completed the game in 2 and a half hours and did not use any of the available hints. It could be completed faster with the help of the built in hint system, but where's the fun in that?

I am not interested in Steam achievements, but for those that seek out 100% success, this game should be very straightforward in 2 playthroughs at each difficulty setting.

I can recommend this game for a peaceful two to three hours experience.
