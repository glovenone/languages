I didn't play this game until I was 26 years old, so I like to think that my opinion on it is unbiased by nostalgia. That said, this game is absolutely fantastic. Easily one of my favorite stealth games of all time. The levels are large and incredibly organic, with several hidden passages and ways to traverse them. The difficulty is challenging but not tedious. The graphics are dated, but more than makes up for it in incredible sound design and ambient music, wonderfully composed by Eric Brosius. Not only that, but the game is filled with character. The atmosphere is dark and the fantasy world presented here is creative and interesting, but the game also has a very dry sense of humor that keeps things from getting too grimdark. Fantastic voice work from Stephen Russell, who voices the memoriable recurring guard Benny, the very eccentric, Truman Capote-esque villian Karras, and the delightful ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ of a protagonist Garrett.

Absolutely fantastic game. Demands to be played several times, to learn the levels, to perfect them, and bask in the atmosphere and world created here. Do not let it's age turn you away.

Thief 2, The Metal Age was a game that I very much enjoyed playing, and although it’s quite old, I still think it holds up very well.
In Thief 2, you play as Garrett, a master thief who must overcome recent trouble involving a corrupt Sheriff who has cracked down on crime, and who seems to have a personal grudge against Garrett. Throughout the game, you must sneak around in various missions and locations, all while uncovering a potentially much larger problem that goes beyond Garrett himself.
In terms of gameplay, it plays similar to the first Thief game. You sneak around, completing various objectives, all while trying to remain unnoticed. While combat is an option, Garrett is rather unequipped when it comes to fighting enemies, and stands little chance against more than one foe at a time. Stealth is by far the best option, and Thief 2 still holds up as one of the best stealth games i’ve played. Guards have long, unpredictable patrols they take, meaning they don’t simply walk from one end of the room to the other making them easy targets, which keeps the game tense and forces the player to always be on their toes in case a guard shows up unexpectedly. Guards are always alert, and will investigate a noise they hear. If they catch a glimpse of you, they may decide to look around, forcing the player to keep moving so that they aren’t found. Garrett’s main means of hiding is darkness. By hiding in the shadows, guards won’t be able to spot the player, allowing them to sneak around unnoticed. The mechanic of hiding in darkness and being exposed in the light works excellently in the game, and it was quite revolutionary for its time. Another interesting mechanic is how not all floors produce the same amount of noise when stepped on. Things like carpet and grass will produce little noise, while marble floors will produce more noise, potentially bringing guards over as well. As you progress through the game and begin stealing from richer people, more marble flooring will be found and more electric lights will be used, meaning it will get harder to keep quiet and stay in the dark.
The level design is some of the best i’ve seen, even an improvement on the excellent design of the first game. Levels are non-linear, large, and open, and can have many different sections, each with their own ways to sneak around undetected. The levels are also very varied. Some you may find yourself in a huge mansion, some in the streets of the city, and in many other locations as well. Garrett will have a large array of tools at his disposal to help him through the levels, including many different types of arrows such as water and vine arrows that will help in certain scenarios.
Thief 2 also wisely gets rid of the more paranormal settings and enemies that were common in the first game and replaces them with more stealth focused ones. In the first Thief, many of the levels revolved around ruins and caves filled with enemies such as zombies. These levels didn’t offer much in terms of stealth, and for the most part involved simply running right by the enemies while avoiding their attacks. Enemies such as zombies are rarely seen in Thief 2, and luckily don’t break the flow of the stealth gameplay. Thief 2 does use new enemies in the form of robots, but luckily they can be snuck around, making them similar to guards. They also offer a good challenge, because they cannot be knocked out, and throughout playing the game I never once destroyed one. I don’t even know if it was possible to destroy one in the game at all.
The game and the people in it also have a lot of charm. Garrett isn’t some lifeless protagonist; he’s a man who can take a moment seriously, but isn’t afraid to make a few cynical remarks about the people he’s stealing from. He isn’t some special hero who people shower with praise, he’s just a guy trying to make enough money from stealing to pay his rent. The guards in the game also have excellent, sometimes quite funny dialogue, and the drunken tone of their voice is a great way to get across their character.
The story itself is also very good, and is often told in a non-direct fashion, meaning the player will learn more about the story and world by reading notes left on people's desks, listening to guards talking to each other, as well as other ways. This also gives players more of a reason to look around and explore the environments. While the story itself may seem simple at first, it slowly builds up and becomes something more. It’s rarely a flashy story, but it’s subtle, as well as intriguing and interesting at the same time.
I can only really think of two complaints about the game, and they're both pretty minor. First, climbing in the game can be a bit difficult, and if you miss the ledge your trying to grab, the character may jump by accident and alert guards to your location. Second, it seems a bit unfair that taking one step on a marble floor brings every guard in the nearby area to your location, especially when guards are already walking over marble floors without anyone caring. Still, these are only small complaints and they didn't get in the way of my enjoyment of the game.
Overall, Thief 2 offers a great improvement over an already great game. It’s one of the best stealth games around, and it has gameplay that still holds up today. The graphics will seem quite dated by today's standards, but if you’re able to get around that, it shouldn’t hinder your enjoyment. One of my favorite games.

It's a game where you steal stuff in a medieval steampunk setting.
I recommend that you look up the plot summary of the first game before playing this game; there are a few plot points that you will miss if you don't. You can very well skip the first game and just play this one. You will miss out on the first game, but this game is the superior one, and I don't think that you'll lose too much if you play this game before the first one, save for the lockpicks not being in the first game until much later in the game.

Thief 2 is a pretty old game. You'll have to re-bind controls right away (F is crouch, for example), movement is less smooth than today's games, and combat kind of sucks. Luckily, the movement is easy to get used to, and you're never supposed to engage in combat anyway.
There is a bit at the end where the same map is used for 2 levels, and I'm not a fan of the final level, but the rest of the game is so excellent that it outweighs all the negatives in my eyes.

Seriously, this has to be the greatest stealth game in existence. Stealth is based on line-of-sight, whether you're in a shadow or not, how much noise you're making, and even if you have something reflective in your hands.

There are also a metric buttload of different entryways and routes that you can take. You could play this game over and over for years and never know about a certain ledge that you can jump on if you jump just right that gives you an entirely new perspective on a level. There are also a ton of secrets in the vein of Doom and Doom 2. The great thing about these secrets is that they're never hidden in a way that makes no sense. If there's a wall that slides away, then there's a button, or a movable torch-holder, or a bookshelf with a pullable book, or something else similar that's somewhere closeby.

This is all I have to say about the game

If you don't own it, start owning it.
If you've never played it, start playing it.

Unless, of course, you don't want to give money to Eidos & Squeenix
The orignal developers aren't going to see your money if you buy this, and the Thief reboot already crashed and burned, so buying this game doesn't really do anything other than give a big publisher money. That being the case, there are- other- ways to get this game.

Nostalgia really is a funny thing.

I've been a Looking Glass Studios fan since the 90's with System Shock 2, and a Thief fan for a long time. Thief II: The Metal Age (the sequel to the critically-praised Thief: The Dark Project) brings players back into the world of master thief Garrett, this time with a bunch of new missions and antagonist in the form of Karras, the leader of the Mechanists.

The one thing I loved most about this game was that the scale just felt bigger in (almost every way). While I still have an issue with the fact that roughly half the levels are strictly linear, the missions that open up to you (like "Stonemarket First Bank", "Life of the Party" and "Masks") offer a wide array of alternate routes and secret passages to accomplish your goals, as well as give a far grander sense of the City and the threat Karras poses to its inhabitants.

Likewise, the variety of weapons and items you can acquire is great and allows for lots of experimentation, although (since I'm a purist) I still stuck to water arrows for the majority of the game.

Admittedly, I found much more joy in the levels where I could pick off guards at will with hit-and-run tactics. I think it's fair to say that the AI in this game alternates between hypersensitive and woefully inept. When the guards aren't aggro-ing on you from far distances, they're complete morons in combat. It is exceptionally easy to take advantage of the combat system. When a sword-wielding guard runs at you, walk backwards and swing like mad, or circle-strafe and hit his back. Bow-wielders are an automatic win - just mash the attack button if you get one cornered. The only real challenge came from the Mechanist robots and groups of enemies, which sometimes stacked up in doors and got stuck or ran around the mansion in the "Masks" mission chasing me (all that was missing was the Benny Hill music).

Likewise, the voice acting is either really good (in the case of Garrett's voice actor and some of the minor antagonists) or near-laughable (the nobles, who sound like they're reading from a script). I couldn't take Karras seriously as a villain because of his nasally voice, which made me laugh more than get freaked out whenever I heard his voice.

I vaguely remembered a lot of buzz and praise for "Life of the Party", which sees Garrett take to the rooftops to rob every building in sight and infiltrate Karras' tower, Angelwatch - and that mission did not disappoint. Easily the best in the game, it had everything I consider to be so great about the genre - lots of expansive areas and alternate routes to take, lots of secrets and hidden goodies to find, great level design, the works. I wish more of the missions had those kinds of additional areas or features to make them worth replaying, because I didn't feel much compulsion to go back and do much besides replay the missions that were either "loot everything" or "steal and/or kill everything".

All in all, though, Thief II is still a standout in the stealth genre. I haven't played Deadly Shadows yet (as of this writing), but the improvement in scope in this game makes me excited to see what's next.

wow !

this is probably the best stealth game ever created, the atmospheare in this game is amazing.
if you want to expirence a well made game you should get this and the first thief game (thief gold) as well.

each level in this game is very well designed with huge space and many secret stuff,documents to read and alot of loot :)

comparing to the first game this game improves in the way that you don't have to deal with ghosts and monsters anymore instead you will deal with gaurds and automatic machines :)

also you get to use new equpiment such as gas mine,flash mine,invisibility and slowfall potion,you also get a camera like eye ball that you can throw and see behind corners,flares to see at night etc...

i wish game makers would learn from this game and invest more time to create a better gameplay and game machanics instead of fancy graphics.

anyway just click buy and enjoy one of the best games ever :)
