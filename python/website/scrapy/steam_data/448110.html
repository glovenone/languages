About This Game

So Long Earth is a light paced game that focus on puzzling and adventure, it tells the story of a grumpy protagonist and his new life outside of earth.
There is going to be hours of exploration all around the universe while unveiling the story of Dominique alongside his minion.
If you enjoy a simple game that you can just turn on, kick back with a controller and relax, then this is just the game for you.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 and above
    • Processor: 2.0 GHz or faster (x86 compatible)
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: 64MB of video memory
    • Storage: 150 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: The game is very light - it can run on very low-spec computers

9/10 – Travel space with your alien pal!

Not much of a reader? Watch my gameplay!

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jVEbiwKGBXk

So Long Earth is a beautiful adventure game about a guy named Dominique traveling across the universe alongside his alien pal who I've named Timmy. The first thing you'll see is his girlfriend dumping him and throwing him a bottle of wine because he'll surely "need" it. Turns out that you do need the wine because it'll numb the pain away - I mean, it's like your healing potion.

You'll get to ride an awesome spaceship in which you could also use to rub against (or mine) asteroids. In this game, you collect coins to buy more maps to progress. You could also buy wine and other things. It also includes some puzzles, which is always fun to figure out.

While you travel from map to map, you'll encounter some enemies so prepare to kick butt before they kick yours!

The game art is similar to Don't Starve and Don't Starve together, which is what the developer was going for. I've read (on his twitter) that the game art is inspired by those games.

Nonetheless, this game is fun to play and is definitely worth trying it out.

This game is a very fun, but sometimes challenging puzzle game. Although with an early access game there are going to bug, but this has little effect on the game play.

Firstly I am going to talk about the character it's self. The character is obviously French, and not to mention that you need to drink wine to heal, which is also a big giveaway as to where the character is from, and if it wasn't enough the first cut scene is set in a somewhat of a look a like to Paris.

Secondly I would like to talk about "Minion" the little character who follows you about as your travel to other planets/worlds. In my opinion in a game like this it is always good to have a character who follows you about. I'm going to use another game as an example to also describe this. So, for example take Portal 2 where you have the side character "Wheatley" following you about for most of the start of the game, until he takes control of GLaDOS body. Anyways getting back to this game, Minion is a really fun, but sometimes annoying side character. Although, and this is a big factor of the game. Minion is a loveable a character, but I need to get used to him as a character.

Thridly there is bad things about this game, but these aren't major problems, and will be fixed easily. The main one is the controls always reset everytime you go into to check them, and hopefully this will be fixed by the next update. Yes, the game is in early alpha, but apart from the one problem the game is perfect right now.

Now we're getting to the gameplay, and firstly the world revolve is amazing. By this I mean as you walk the world will spin round, and I really enjoy this aspect. I also enjoy the flight to each different planets, and the world designs are amazing themselves, but the ship is greatly detailed. Although the graphics are kinda similar to "Don't starve", but let's not get into that. The dev's have done amazing work to the game, and that shouldn't be taken away from them.

Finally as a whole I would recommend you go out, and buy this game. Before it goes off sale, and the game is also pretty easy to get achievements on, and the game also get's harder as you progress through the game, and it deserves a lot of support from the steam community.

Really fun indie platform/exploration game.
Love the art style and soundtrack.

So you are a french mafia member who has decided to go live in space.
He's definately french. Wine is health in this game.

It has a really interesting story.
You are trying to regain contact with your significant other.

The game is split up into two kinds of modes.
You spend some of your time on really small worlds.
This is where the platforming and combat take place.
There are many different types of worlds.
So far I have found Tree/Forest worlds and Ice planets.

The other side of the game is spent in the universe.
You explore this in your very steampunk styled ship.
You can mine asteroids and find loot as well as travel between different planets and ships.

Your main goal in this game seems to be collecting keys and maps.
When you find a map a new world or ship location will be revealed on the universe map.
Keys unlock chests which usually contain items and coins.
There are traders on ships who will sell you items and more maps.
The main story line seems to continue when you go to new ships and speak to people.

It's definately worth checking out.
The controls felt a little loose on the jumping side of things.
You get used to this pretty quickly.
It may just be more an issue with my ability at platforming than a problem with the game itself.
