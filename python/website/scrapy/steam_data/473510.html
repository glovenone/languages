About This Game

//[{Brief Overview}]\\

Gal-X-E aims to revive the old-school shoot em up genre, and adds modern elements to it! Fight your way through endless waves of randomly generated madness! Collect spaceships and show the enemy who's boss!

//[{GamePlay}]\\

Gal-X-E offers unique and interesting gameplay. Unlike traditional modern games where players can upgrade themselves until their power level reaches "Over 9000!", Gal-X-E relies on the actual skill of the player. Even though Gal-X-E offers new spaceships to be built, they only provide minimal improvement. All classes are completely unique in the way that they play, so try to find the one that suits you the most! Players are thrown into an arena where they fight of hordes of never-ending enemies. Kill enemies and collect score multipliers to gain incredible amounts of points!

//[{Spaceships}]\\

Gal-X-E presents the player with 24 different spaceships, divided into 6 unique classes as follows:

Light: High agility, as well as a rapid fire-rate with low accuracy.

Medium: Normal firing rate, higher damage, and slower speeds.

Heavy: Low fire rate, high damage, and good accuracy. It's almost a portable cannon.

Shotgun: Capable of dealing devastating damage, though you'll have to get uncomfortably close to maximize the effect.

Sniper: These bad boys are 100% accurate, and deal one shot one kill hits. You'll have no excuse for missing this time!

Chaingun: The most inaccurate, yet highest damage class! It's basically spray and pray for noobs.

//[{Enemies}]\\

Every enemy in Gal-X-E provides an unique challenge to overcome, but we won't go about spoiling your fun by telling you what to expect.

//{[Languages]}\\

Currently only English is supported, but the interface is self explanatory so that anyone from any language can enjoy it!

//{[Features]}\\

The main game consists of randomly generated waves of enemies, meaning that not one play through will be the same! The longer you survive, the more intense the waves become!

The Hangar is where you can open loot boxes in the hopes of finding a brand new, shiny spaceship!

The Badges screen is where you can view your current badges and your progress on those you still need to earn. Badges are basically achievements but with CRAZY challenges!

The Stats screen allow you to be disappointed in your life when you see how much of your life you've wasted on this game.

//{[Conclusion]}\\

If you're into old-school arcade fun, as well as games that rely mainly on the improving of the player's skill, Gal-X-E is for you!

Gal-X-E was released on the 14th of May, 2016, and was developed and published by NodziGames. It's genres are Sci-Fi, Shoot-em-Up, Indie, and action.

Gal-X-E is a Sci-Fi shoot em up, and resembles what you might have seen in an arcade back when those were the cultural norm.

Enemy space ships, asteroids, flying garbage and... well, an eyeball that shoots missiles at you willrandomly spawn and attempt to kill you. Some are indirect and shoot in random directions, while others will target you and attempt to shoot you down.

From what I could tell, there is no storyline to Gal-X-E, your sole goal is to survive for as
long as you can. I myself am not good at Gal-X-E, however, I could not find any boss fights, which would have been very beneficial to the overall enjoyment of the game.

The graphics for Gal-X-E are very cartoony, but games in this genre all seem to have very basic graphics, not that cartoonish graphics are a problem. The controls feel very smooth and don't stick, leading to a very nice experience during gameplay.

As you play Gal-X-E, you will collect scrap which you could use to unlock better ships, in a fashion similar to unboxing in CS:GO or TF2. You use your scrap and unbox new ships, however, you can still unbox a ship you already own, effectively wasting your hard earned scrap, just like in CS:GO or TF2, only the ship is a crappy item and the scrap is your real money. Anyway, off topic.

Some suggestions I'd make to the game, although they aren't downsides, are if I were the developer I'd include a pause screen, perhaps an online leaderboard (since the only game mode is survival), and steam achievements for completionists.

There is one obvious downside to the game which the other reviews on steam don't the audio. The sound effects are decent and very fitting with the sci-fi genre, however, the soundtrack is, unfortunately, unoriginal. Like Girlfriend Rescue, another game I reviewed, the music used is
creative commons. However, in Girlfriend Rescue, there was orchestral music that was
ill fitting, but in Gal-X-E, the music is very fitting with the theme and is actually
music that I like. Where does the music come from? NoCopyrightSounds, my favourite
record label. One song I could easily recognize which was used in Gal-X-E is
called Frag Out by DJ Assassin, and if you own Gal-X-E, listen to the soundtrack and
compare some of the songs to those on NoCopyrightSounds.

Rating:
Graphics: 6/10
Audio: 4/10
Gameplay: 6.2/10
Overall: 5.4/10

Introduction
Gal-X-E strives is a old school shoot em' up, developed and published by NodziGames. It seems that the game has a fair amount of content that is inside the game, however there are some issues with the game that doesn't really make it fun to play.

Gameplay
The gameplay itself focuses on only a single game mode, which is fine since this game is suppose to follow the kind of arcade style. Upon entering the game there is only one objective for you to accomplish, which is to basically shoot everything and get the highest possible score. That's it, now I feel like some people would argue that this isn't enough for a game but I feel like it is only fitting for the game to have one gamemode since it is following the arcade styled shoot em up.

I feel like this game had a lot going for it, I actually thought that the game was in Early Access when I first got to the main screen. Why? Well for a arcade shoot em up game like this your main focus would be to have a leaderboard to compete against your friends and others against the world. This game doesn't have this feature unfortunately, and therefore I feel like the game is lacking in that sense. I don't really feel the need to play more than I should because I only have my own high score to beat, in some games beating your own highscore is enough to make you play more of the game than you initially intended. But this game just feels like it really, really needs a leaderboard. I feel like without this essential leaderboard that the game itself hasn't got much replay value at all.

One of the main feature a game should have is the ability to have a pause menu. Well this game doesn't have that. Why I really don't know, but it just missing from the game. So if you are in the middle of a run doing really well but you need to take a break well your out of luck. Seriously please add a pause button for this game.

Something that they added to the game was the ability to level up and the large amount of ships that you can be. The ships have different types of shooting, so some of them have just a spray of bullets, while others are more accurate like the sniper class that has high attack but low reload speed. So the game is really nice in the way that there is a lot of different types of ships. This seems good, until you actually realise what you have to do in order to unlock all of these ships...

Now how does leveling up have to do with unlocking ships? Well the way that you obtain ships is by gather enough scrap, which you obtain by destroying enemies, and going to the Hanger and buying a ship. There are 4 different types that you can buy. Common, Uncommon, Rare and Legendary. You will only be able to buy from the Common pack to start with, in order to unlock the others well you will have to level up. Uncommon lv. 10, Rare lv. 25 and Legendary lv. 50. Now seeing as I have been playing this game for about 2.4 hours I am only just turning level 10. I'm not in the slightest bit motivated to unlock the other ship packs. I feel like this just drags the game on and forces you to play loads to unlock everything.

The graphics of this game are somewhat standard, the background when you play is just a still image. I personally would've really expected maybe twinkling of some of the stars so that it adds a bit of life to the game all together, The sprites of the ships are good, seem a bit plain at times but mostly all good.

The music of this game is a bit weird, sometimes the music would really suit the game that suits this type of gameplay sort of fast paced. While other times I feel like the music is just weird and really doesn't suit the gameplay at all. This is mostly for the music in-game. While on the other hand the music on the menu screen is a really nice opening to the game itself.

Pros
+ Nice retro styled shoot em up
+ Only one gamemode (a pro in my opinion following the traditional retro feel to the game)
+ Lots of different types of ships
+ Sprites of the ships are nice

Cons
- Needs a leaderboard
- Unlocking ships requires you to be a certain level
- Low replay ability
- No pause button...

Result / Final Thoughts
The final verdict of this game, it seemed before I played this game that it was going to be a very fun and addictive arcade shoot em up. I guess my expectations were too high, the game is fun if you play it for short intervals but it gets fairly repetitive over a long period of time. The game does, however, have a nice variety of ships and a nice retro feel to the game. Overall, I would recommened this game if you are a fan of arcade shoot em up.

Rating: 5.1/10

Product received for free
This review is created for Deduction's Review Club .
I received this game for free, however, my review is unbiased

There is also a video counterpart to this review, which I highly recommend you check out.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJpIGtmp_Oc
Gal-X-E was released on the 14th of May, 2016, and was developed and published by NodziGames. It's genres are Sci-Fi, Shoot-em-Up, Indie, and action.

Gal-X-E is a Sci-Fi shoot em up, and resembles what you might have seen in an arcade back when those were the cultural norm.

Enemy space ships, asteroids, flying garbage and... well, an eyeball that shoots missiles at you willrandomly spawn and attempt to kill you. Some are indirect and shoot in random directions, while others will target you and attempt to shoot you down.

From what I could tell, there is no storyline to Gal-X-E, your sole goal is to survive for as
long as you can. I myself am not good at Gal-X-E, however, I could not find any boss fights, which would have been very beneficial to the overall enjoyment of the game.

The graphics for Gal-X-E are very cartoony, but games in this genre all seem to have very basic graphics, not that cartoonish graphics are a problem. The controls feel very smooth and don't stick, leading to a very nice experience during gameplay.

As you play Gal-X-E, you will collect scrap which you could use to unlock better ships, in a fashion similar to unboxing in CS:GO or TF2. You use your scrap and unbox new ships, however, you can still unbox a ship you already own, effectively wasting your hard earned scrap, just like in CS:GO or TF2, only the ship is a crappy item and the scrap is your real money. Anyway, off topic.

Some suggestions I'd make to the game, although they aren't downsides, are if I were the developer I'd include a pause screen, perhaps an online leaderboard (since the only game mode is survival), and steam achievements for completionists.

There is one obvious downside to the game which the other reviews on steam don't the audio. The sound effects are decent and very fitting with the sci-fi genre, however, the soundtrack is, unfortunately, unoriginal. Like Girlfriend Rescue, another game I reviewed, the music used is
creative commons. However, in Girlfriend Rescue, there was orchestral music that was
ill fitting, but in Gal-X-E, the music is very fitting with the theme and is actually
music that I like. Where does the music come from? NoCopyrightSounds, my favourite
record label. One song I could easily recognize which was used in Gal-X-E is
called Frag Out by DJ Assassin, and if you own Gal-X-E, listen to the soundtrack and
compare some of the songs to those on NoCopyrightSounds.

Rating:
Graphics: 6/10
Audio: 4/10
Gameplay: 6.2/10
Overall: 5.4/10

Hello, I would like to start off this review with an apology to Gal-X-E's developer(s). When I wrote the original review, I was biased towards the fact that this was an indie game. Many of the original issues I listed have been addressed, which shows that NodziGames really cares about his players.

Gal-X-E is a space shooter that looks to bring back some arcade nostalgia. Does it do it well? Yeah! The game frequently reminds me of other space shooters from the 80s.

7/10 (Pick this game up if it's on sale!)
