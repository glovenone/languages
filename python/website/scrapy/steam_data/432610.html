About This Game

It's been months since we've heard back.
Their research was supposed open a new page in human understanding.
Where did they go? What happened to them?

Monumental is a narrative driven, puzzle based adventure game. Set on an alien planet, the player is given the goal of tracking down a research team on the brink of a great discovery. During this search the player encounters The Monument, a remnant of a highly advanced but long dead alien race and the key to uncovering the planet's dark secret.

Key Features
  • Utilize a variety of simple tools such as a camera, microphone, and flashlight in creative ways to overcome the challenges ahead
  • Immerse yourself in the logs left behind by the research team, relive their discoveries and trials
  • Learn the secret of The Monument that has been kept hidden for thousands of years
  • Solve a challenging selection of brainteasers and riddles in the name of science

Will you be able to find the truth long forgotten?

The game's not the best looking, and it's horrendously slow at loading savegames, so that it often seems crashed but isn't. Video capture software that hooks into the fullscreen have crashed the game for me too, but screen capture still works. Walking around feels a bit jittery at first, but that feeling faded away from me as I kept playing. The UI, although a really neat design idea, is sometimes tedious to use.

That said, it offers a large variety of puzzle concepts, some of which I've never seen before. My absolute favourite is probably the Pneumonics door lock you encounter early in the game (most likely because I love puns/word play). A few puzzles could be more obvious though, like the voice activated door that I found no hint for being that.

Despite its shortcomings, I think that this is a fantastic game, with some puzzles being harder or easier for you than for other people, but certainly not impossible in conjuction with the built-in hints system.
