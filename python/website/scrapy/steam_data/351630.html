Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We are adding to our roster of Towers, Creeps and Arena Maps throughout Early access based on feedback from the community. Help us develop our player vs player arena tower defense game. Even though Fire with Fire is more of an underdog compared to other big name tower defense games, I hope you find quality comparable to Clash Royale, Bloons, etc, with strategic depth far superior.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We expect to remain in Early Access for approximately two to four months depending on player feedback on the size and balance of the tower / unit roster. Full release is expected mid 2016”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We are regularly adding to the roster of towers and units during early access and tweaking balance.

New features such as activated abilities while attacking and defending will be added, along with numerous changes based on player feedback.

The final game will also include a story driven campaign where many items can be unlocked for use in online matches.

Full Feature List:
  • Online Matchmaking
  • New Towers, Creeps, maps, skins
  • Achievement based player leveling system
  • In match Creep leveling system
  • Tower set tech trees
  • New game modes
  • Using abilities while defending and attacking
  • Player ranking system
  • Custom Games vs friends
  • Equip fruit on your trees for added creep and defensive bonuses
  • Controller Support
  • In match emotes
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We currently have 25 towers and 12 creeps fully implemented, each with unique mechanics. Most of the features listed above are fully functional, fun and stable. Some add on features such as using abilities, ranking, emotes and other game modes will be finished soon.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game is free to play. All game play content is unlock-able within the game for free, arena skins and other items will have an option for purchase for lower prices than release while we improve and balance content during Early Access.

If you enjoy the game, please consider supporting further development by getting an arena skin!”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Feedback from the community is incredibly important to achieve the best online arena tower defense game possible. Together we can build a game the community will enjoy for a long time to come and as an e-sport in the future.

In addition to bug reports, game balance feedback and fire pun suggestions for unit names and description, we are always taking in tower, creep and ability theme and mechanic ideas. Though game mechanics will ultimately be decided by the team, we plan on allowing the community vote on which themed tower and creep sets will become available next.

Game updates will be frequent and ongoing throughout early access and beyond.
Detailed patch note and balance changes will be available every update.”

About This Game

Fire with Fire is an online player vs player tower defense strategy game about ascendant fireball creatures fighting over the last remaining fuel sources of a dying planet. Save the trees and rise up the leader boards. New unique towers, creeps and arenas added every 2-3 weeks.

Core Features

  • Our own system improving on traditional Tower Defense elements such as tech trees and tower upgrading.
  • Player vs player online matchmaking, opponents choose and deploy waves of creeps against each other in turn.
  • Achievement based leveling, players gain xp points by completing achievements, single player maps and defeating matchmaking opponents.
  • Unlock-able bonuses, grow fruit to gain stat bonuses and additional in game effects for your online matches.
  • Ongoing content updates, we add new towers, creeps, arenas, and balance updates regularly.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, Vista, 7, 8, 10
    • Processor: Dual-core CPU
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce 6800
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Storage: 100 MB available space

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 July
I'm a big fan of TD games and I found even the tutorial for this game extremely aggravating.

- The tutorial doesn't pause between waves. The next wave starts while you're still trying to understand your options, before you've placed your towers.

- The tutorial forces you to let some creeps through on the very first wave. Yes, it's just a tutorial, but this is very annoying.

- The controls are very clunky and require far too much clicking on locations spread out all over the screen. Let's say you want to upgrade a tower.
(1) Move your cursor to your tower.
(2) Click on the tower.
(3) Move your cursor right to the build icon for your tower.
(4) Click on the build icon.
(5) Move your cursor up to the upgrade tree that pops up.
(6) Click on the upgrade that you want.
(7) Move your cursor down to the upgrade button.
(8) Click on the upgrade button.

Most TD games just require you to click on the tower and then choose an upgrade from an array that appears just above that tower. That's (1) Move (2) Click (3) Click. Contrast that to the 8-step process we have here. Indefensibly annoying.

- The towers don't always upgrade in a regular manner (e.g., mortar tower upgrades to freeze tower). I'd imagine it wouldn't be a problem after a player gets very used to the tower trees, but for a new player, this coupled with the lack of time to think is quite the problem.

I uninstalled this after just 2 aborted attempts at the tutorial. That's how much I hate it.

Fire with Fire is a clever twist on a classic premise; You alternate between playing the attacking and defending forces in a tower defense game. The rules are simple and mechanics are easy to grasp. I'm almost worried they are too simple, because the game might be too easy to master. In order for the game to be competative, it needs to consistently provide challenges at every skill level. There's a good amount of interaction with the game, and it's fun to fine tune both your offense and defense depending on the enemies strategies. The game could use some more inirtia. As it stands, while using bad strategies, the game could go on forever. This could be fixed by slowly making the mobs stronger over time. As it stands, the games go on for too long. Additionally, if you're behind in the game, it is pretty difficult to catch up. If, while making the mobs stronger, the mobs also did more damage to the tree, then it would be easier for someone who is losing to make a come back.

All in all, really cool game and I'm excited to see how it evolves over time.

Whilse I like this game let me place the biggest cavet emptor I possibly can in the largest font size, bolded of course, that steam will allow.

THIS GAME IS NOT FOR EVERYONE!

With that out of the way, who is this game for then? I mean every game in existance is possibly not for everyone afterall. Well, this game scratches an itch no other tower defense game has ever managed to scratch. PVP. Infact PVP is all this game has to offer! If you were hoping for some VS AI or team play or anything else then this game doesn't have it, sorry.

The way the game works is as follows. You have 45 seconds to build up your base defenses AND select a creep wave to send at your opponent. You both get money for attacking and defending successfully.

If, however you think you can just sit back and let your opponent do all the work while you gain massive money then you are in for a shock my friend. Everytime you send your creep wave, they level up. You don't have to send your creep wave every 45 seconds if you don't want too, but it behooves you to do so otherwise your enemy will level up faster than you can keep up and leveling creeps up is free, they just need to be sent on a death march to gain a level.

After 3 level ups they can evolve into even more annoying fodder, usually ending the match outright.
You can however let the opponent level up while you farm their fodder and build the ultimate defensive wall. Its an interesting conundrum and shows surprisingly good design because their is no 'wrong' way to play the game, except by not placing any towers whatsoever but at that point why even play.

You seemingly gain nothing for losing and if your foe quits, you automatically win, likewise if they disconnect. â¥â¥â¥â¥ing hell, just take that in for a moment and then realize that Capcom couldn't even add that for Street Fighter 5.

The game isn't without its downsides however, first of all, their is only the tutorial whereby you can learn the ropes of the game. This is annoying but not game breaking. The tutorial will serve as a testing ground for your various build ideas over time so it is good that it at least exists.

The game also features a shop and some of the items in the shop, well, yeah. Its a free to play game so its what you'd expect, units that seemingly have an advantage over people who don't pay.
Also clicking feels off, can't put my finger on it but if i had to guess, I imagine this game is also on mobile.
Finally their is no single player campaign at all and wait times for matches are pretty long due to a low player count.

I can think of some quality of life fixes for example allow us to save a specific creep loadout rather than picking every wave, with the option to change it if we want, mid match. I often go with the same early creeps, just to level them up and picking them game after game is just repetitive.

With all that said, I had some fun with the game. It isn't deep like a CCG nor is it as good as multiplayer co-op tower defense games like Bloons TD5 but despite all the reviews saying this game is bad, I honestly enjoyed it, even if I won't be clocking in a lot of hours into it.
