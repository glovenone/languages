There are some cool, wacky, surreal graphics and good animations in this game. And it's quite long. But pretty much everything else is bad.

  • Nonsensical point-and-click adventuring. At some point you have two different hammers, but can't break glass: you need a rock for that. You have scissors, but no, you need a knife. What.
  • Awful story, script, and voice acting.
  • Buggy clicking: you click, but it doesn't register. This makes hidden-object scenes really frustrating. For one of them, I could not even complete the scene because it would not let me click the last item.
  • Instead of hidden-object scenes, you can play an incredibly stupid bubble game that is totally the opposite of the game's supposedly spooky mood. I think it's supposed to be ironic.
  • Bad hidden-object scenes: you need to pick a book, but there are three on the screen. One is the "correct" one.
  • Puzzles and other locations are bizarrely hidden in scenes: you click on them, and they open. But there's no graphic for them in the scenes. It's just an arbitrary set of pixels.

    The game features an innovative hidden-object scene tactic: objects morph. Not only is this annoying, but it's also inexplicable. Still, kudos for this attempt to innovate on this venerable game genre.

    What a mess!

Generally, I love hidden object/puzzle games and I hate having to give negative reviews, but this one just didn't have what it took to draw me in and make me really enjoy it.

First, there are severe compatibility issues with no indication that there will be a fix. The screen flickers terribly on normal settings and in every variety of compatibility modes I could try. The game does not flicker in windowed mode, but you'll find if you go windowed that the play area is tiny if you have a high-res monitor. In my case, in order to play the game I had to switch to windows and change my 4k res to half that in order to get the game big enough to see and play. It's irritating, but it did the trick to let me play without the screen flicker.

The game itself is standard HO game fare with a combination of HO scenes and minigame puzzles along with the usual inventory-based puzzles to complete. This is a formula that has worked over and over again with this genre of games, but for some reason this game didn't make it work. I found myself intensely bored after a short time, and only my personal obsession with never leaving a game unfinished made me complete it.

Whether it was just lack of a compelling story, nonintuitive puzzles (I was completely at a loss a lot of the time), less-than-helpful hint system, sloppy map system, or a combination of all of these things, I really did not enjoy anything about this game. Even the HO scenes were irritating (example: find a jar. No, not that jar. Not that jar either. There were some scenes that had a good five items in the scene that matched the one you were actually supposed to click.)

So in the end, it's not actually the worst HO game out there, and if you can deal with the compatibility issues you might find it more interesting than I did. However, if you do decide to try it, I would recommend getting it on sale. But I would recommend going through most of the other HO offerings on Steam first as there are many better.

You're sent to a town called Apothecarium, where famous scientists are residing, in order to discover what happened to a young noble and her daughter were sent to preserve them of the plague. They disappeared suddenly. But once a bright city, Apothecarium is now in ruins. What happened?

The plot is promising, as it's a scientist that began the curse and behind an evil plan to reach immortality.

The gameplay is the standard one: you search hidden objects scenes, you find items and use them and you solve puzzles. Unfortunately, where the concept is working for me usually, here, in this game, made me watch flies pass by. In other words, I got bored. Bored because I didn't always know what to do, frustrated because some stuff aren't making sense and besides, after a while, resolving the same puzzles in order to have surprises is boring. Even if I must admit that the idea to earn bucks or hints is a nice idea, it wasn't really well executed by having the same type of puzzles.

Also, it is also nice to have in hidden objects scenes items that are changing between two forms. However, having many of them is also disturbing, and two or three aren't even in the list. So you're kinda confused. Also, there is a problem with the translation: you're asking for a "gland" (I play in French) - for me, it's a fruit from the oak. I have one in the scene. However, it was referring to something else, that I know under another name.

There is also a nice shop where you can buy special stuff by collecting coins in the game or solving fast puzzles or hidden scenes.

The soundtrack is correct and the backgrounds are also quite good and creepy.

It's just a shame that I got lost in the way and that I was often left by not knowing what to do.

I'm quite mitigated about the game. I mean, the game is correct. But I didn't even finish the game. Steam is missing a neutral feature or a maybe button, because Apothecarium belongs to that category. Anyway, whoever is a novice in these kind of games should avoid, the rest, well, I let the decision up to you.

Oh well, it's not a complete failure, I will let the benefice of the doubt to this one and recommend it.

Picturesce Hidden Object Game

This game reminds me in some respects to stories of ETA Hoffman, and some Edgar Allan Poe's "Mask of the Red Death", also some reference to H. P. Lovecraft are existing.

This said the game actually is quite tame, it's more a romantic than a horror story. It's full of beautiful sceneries, some classic tracks for sound (Like Griegs "In the Hall of the Mountain King"), and the usual fare for hidden object games. Whats quite refreshing is the fact, that this game doesn't often use the same hidden object scenes twice.

Pros:
  • Graphics are nice, the hidden object scenes are fair and often are used once only.
  • A lot of Classical Music, which backs the romantic impression of the game.
  • Riddles are easy, sometimes too easy.

Cons:
  • The game had the uncanny peculiarity to crash at times.
  • Story isn't really original, thus you have to concentrate more on the things that are more on the good side.

Recommended for Fans of the Genre

Hidden Object Games are, as much as Visual Novells not for everyone a genre they like to play. It can get boring to watch a scene and not finding what you search, since this game is quite fair in the HOS you won't encounter boredome... Maybe just some frustration as you get spoiled quite often, and you also had to look into the bonus chapter to solve the game at last.

It's nice, without doubt. But it's not the rage, neither it's a game that earns over the way downvotes. The game is on a solid upper 70 % scale as a HOG, and as a Game overall it's a 69 to 72 % Game. A lot of stuff was just done right, and if you like romantic tales, with slight ghoulish background you might have found a winner.

This is a nice game. It's sad that it has a mixed rating. I realise a lot of people are having technical issues with the game (getting it to run, flickering while running, crashing) I prepared myself for a similar experience, but I had no problems with it at all. (I ran it in full screen, I have win 7 64 bit, geforce660Ti). It ran very smoothly. I had one crash when I thought an animation had ended and I clicked outwith the box the animation was playing in, but that was my only problem and just that one time.
The story is a little disjointed in places, where if you don't read the journal you'll miss bits, as it's not all displayed on screen or in the voice acting. The character draws conclusions in the journal about things that were never mentioned on screen. It was weird at the start but I got used to it. You should note that occasionally the answer to a puzzle is pretty much given by reading the journal, so following the story can have it's drawbacks if you weren't looking for clues. The spoilers weren't needed in there imo as there's a strategy guide contained within the journal as well.
The story can also be really illogical, so try not to pay too much attention as you play.

The voice acting is my least favorite part of this game. Mismatched and badly worded in places, by which I mean certain phrases don't match certain characters like when a guard was given lines more suited to a child I actually laughed. The child in the story has a voice actor who's way too old for the image portraying her, which I found a bit annoying....and the ghost girl....i think she was trying to sound dead and spooky, but she just sounded like someone reading her lines veeerrrrryyy sllllloooooowwwwlllly =P
Overall it was a good game, some of the animations were nice, the puzzles were good and none of them too difficult. Some of the achievement trophies you gather along the way were lovely looking so I actually looked forward to unlocking those.
You can find coins in the game and get them from completing puzzle chests as well. The coins can be spent in a store that you can access through your journal or from the intro screen, you can buy boosts for various things. I didn't use the store just had a quick look in it at the start. I also didn't play any of the match 3 games, which I think you can do instead of the hidden object game, I saw the button for it at the side.
Listen when you complete the puzzle for the dancing marionettes, it plays Dire Straits' Romeo and Juliet intro =) (I think it's actually in the video on the store page) It made me smile when I recognised what it was in the game =)
I enjoyed playing this, and was a little sad when it was over. I got this in a bundle, but I think it was worth the Â£4.99 price tag currently on it. Although, I guess unless you know it's definitely going to run for you, maybe it's not.
It would be nice to see this updated for those having problems, because in the HOG/puzzle genre it's a nice one to have in your collection, if you can forgive bits of the story and the voice acting.

Works fine with zero problems so far (Windows 7). A feature packed hidden-object game with a cheesy horror story.

Pros:
The game designers should be applauded for making this game so casual-friendly - this game bends over backwards to cater for casual gamers:
- stuck on a hidden object? Play a match 3 game to bypass it instead!
- stuck on a puzzle? Simply wait for a meter to fill up and skip the puzzle instead.
- built in strategy guide and hint system

Variety of logic puzzles, hidden object scenes and use object X on Y is enjoyable.

Atmospheric music and some nice orchestral scores

Cons:
Some minor glitches: 1 crash to desktop after I solved a puzzle and clicked too quickly before the animation had finished. A few problems with the detection of some hidden objects - clicking on the object didn't register as "found", i had to click hopefully on nearby pixels. The worst example I came across was "a ball of thread" that was only clickable after it had morphed into a pear!

Slight lack of polish - one of the NPCs is called "None name"!

Forgettable plot, contrived scenarios, mostly mediocre voice acting.

Sound volume was almost silent for ending cut scene. Bizarre "bonus" section unlocked after finishing the game which feels like an afterthought.

Summary:
Enjoyable hidden object/puzzle game that lacks polish considering this is the "Premium Edition". I picked this up in a bundle so have no complaints, the score would be lower if I paid full price.

7.5/10
