About This Game

Vampire®: The Masquerade-Bloodlines™ delivers a new type of RPG experience-one that blends all the core elements of a traditional RPG with the graphical richness, immediacy and brutal combat of a first-person action game. The game plunges players into the dark and gritty vampire underworld of modern-day L.A. as a creature of the night. Players will develop their character's powers, interact with other characters and embark on story-driven quests as they battle mortals and other vampires with an incredible array of vampire powers and weapons. Powered by Valve's Source Technology, the game is based on White Wolf's popular Vampire: The Masquerade pen-and-paper RPG series and its official clans.

This game has got a lot of flaws, but I forgive almost every single one of them because I am so enamored with its world, and its writing. Seriously, it takes a lot to get me to give a ♥♥♥♥ about ♥♥♥♥ing Vampires.

Set in the World of Darkness RPG universe, this game had writing and design that was just too ambitious. The developers clearly had a magnificent vision for this game -- a game where the experience is vastly different depending on which clan you picked. A game where playing it through once only shows you a fraction of what it has to offer. A world filled with choices, and so, so many ways to solve your problems. If this game had been allowed to finish, it could have been right up there alongside revered greats such as Deus Ex and Planescape: Torment. Unfortunately, its flaws, and the fact that the game is literally not finished, rob it of such a reward. It's almost tragic, really.

If you're gonna buy this game, be prepared to do some homework just to get the damn thing to run. The unofficial patch is basically mandatory. But this game is well worth the effort. If you're a fan of dynamic RPGs with loads of replay value, give this a shot. I put off trying it out for a long time and honestly wish I had played it sooner.
