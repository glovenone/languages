About This Game

Hunt - Explore - Survive

Welcome Pioneer!

Step into a huge Cretaceous world (24 km² / 9.3 mi²) where the very environment will test your survival skills to the max. This is the planet of Primal Eden. Help us reclaim, subdue and eradicate the savage wilderness so that we may plant the seed of civilization in its place. If you are successful, we will permanently erase your criminal records. If you fail, your dead body's nourishing of wild plants and animals will be seen as treason against the Expansive Galaxies Corporation and your extended family will be volunteered as Pioneers.

There should be some supplies in your drop pod to get you started. In order to survive, you will need to explore the world in search of more powerful weapons, clothing and ammo. Sounds easy? Well, not only is the island littered with poisonous plants, but deadly dinosaurs roam the island looking to feast on the weak.

Planet Primal Eden is inhabited by 5 different dinosaur species, including the mighty Tyrannosaurus Rex and the much-dreaded Quetzalcoatlus, a.k.a. Menace of the Skies. Careful planning, cooperation and backpacks filled with ammo and weapons are crucial to your survival.

theHunter: Primal is a new instalment in the highly popular and critically acclaimed theHunter franchise, created by Expansive Worlds and Avalanche Studios. Since its release, the original game has grown into a 5 million strong player community, and is still growing rapidly.

theHunter: Primal offers both single player and multiplayer modes, with the option of creating private gaming sessions. If you are looking for a serious challenge, turn on the PVP function, team up with your friends and let all hell break loose. If you prefer cooperative hunting, you can turn off the PVP function and join your friends and other players in an effort to bring down the beasts of planet Primal Eden.

The game features a persistent inventory system, which means that everything you’ve collected in one game session will remain in your inventory in the next one. But it comes with a price. If you die, all your equipment is lost. If you are fast enough you can pick up your lost backpack before another hunter loots your stuff.

Happy hunting!

Posted: 7 July
The setting of this game is simply stunning; the island and dinosaurs look amazing, and the day/night cycle plus the weather helps set the mood of being in uncharted and unfriendly land. Having the opportunity to simply walk around and appreciate the visuals and sound would be very enjoyable.
Unfortunately, the positives end there. For "the most realistic prehistoric survival game", the gameplay is pretty garbage. Dinosaur AI is buggy (You can just stand next to a Tric and hit it with a stick until it dies, it can't turn sharp enough to get you), and there are needless delays in the combat system that create very frustrating gameplay, such as drawn-out reload times and a very slow turning rate while aiming.
The biggest issue is the punishing nature of this game. You start with nothing but a machete, a very limited stamina bar, and a settlermate (a map/radar device), yet the first dinosaur that you could encounter could be a T-Rex or a pack of Utahraptors. After you die, you drop all of your gear and have to make your way past whatever killed you to get it back. That is assuming that you can make it before whomever is running the game shuts it down, at which point you will lose all of your gear and need to start over.
It sucks that this game has been abandoned, as with some gameplay adjustments I think that I could really come to enjoy the challenge that the game presents, but as it stands, I say don't buy it.

tl;dr: The graphics are great, the gameplay is buggy and punishing. Don't support developers unless you enjoy losing your gear time and again.

I got this game in a bundle, but it would still be well worth $10.

It has a top notch immersive environment with a full day/night cycle as well as weather effects. The sound design is absolutely amazing and you will feel yourself in a pristine island. The calls of dinosaurs in the distance, or the rustling of leaves in the foliage, or the ominous steady stomps from T-rexes are just a few of the environmental surround that you will experience.

There are weapon crates scattered all over the island, but these are still a bit scarce. This is the first game to actually give me a survival-feel that are absent in most open world survival/apocalypse games. The dinosaurs are scarce too, or will feel like it until you learn to notice footprints and the small darting movements from the corner of your eyes in thick jungles.

Two negatives I want to address. The island is freaking big but you travel really slow. That does add immersion but also adds tediousness. I recommend getting a trainer that gives unlimited sprint (but even that is slow).
The second is that the devs abandoned the game. So what you experience the first 10 hours will probably be just about everything the game has to offer in terms of novelty.

I would still recommend it, if you're into an immersive game.

If you like dinosaur games, this is one of the best. Although it is a hunting game, and has strong hunting elements such as tracking, score awarded for kills, and certain shots being more deadly than others depending on which vital organs are hit, I get much more of a survival-type game impression. The player must scavage around abandonded settlements to find better weapons, ammo, and clothing, while avoiding roaming dinosaurs and hostile other players. The dinosaur element, despite the variety being small, is very good. Dinosaurs, especially T-Rex, and its stronger Primal cousins, are very good at hunting players down, where the numbers of Utahraptors can be just as dangerous. This game reminds me a lot of a more modern Jurassic Tresspasser, and has the right element of fear when hiding from dangerous dinos and fighting for one's life.
