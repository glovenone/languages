Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“New kind of adventure is currently in alpha, I want to include the community of players on Steam in the development. For testing and feedback purposes, so that I can make New kind of adventure a fantastic game experience.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We plan to have NKOA in Early Access for around 6 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“NKOA will be updated reguraly while in Early Access, the finished product will have additional game-play features. New areas, items, quests. The finished game will be more optimized and polished.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“New kind of adventure is in mid alpha and will be updated regularly. There is very little content, in terms of interacting with the world. You can explore unfinished areas of the world, meet npcs, and check out the vibrant environment.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, the Early Access version will be at a reduced price.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I will ask the community what they like/dislike about NKOA and use feedback to improve the game and game-play experience.”

About This Game

New kind of adventure is a 3D adventure game with RPG elements.
Mary hides in a wardrobe after her parents had a fight. She falls asleep. When Mary awakes, she discovers that she is in a beautiful, magical world with giant strawberries and magical druids!
With her new friend Aquarius, Mary must find a way back to the real world!

Features:

  • Use Mary's new found powers and Aquarius' magical abilities to solve puzzles and complete quests.
  • Collect Artefacts to unlock new magical powers and abilities.
  • A huge vibrant world, with colourful wildlife and environments.
  • Gather items and resources to trade with Druids.
  • Original folk jam soundtrack.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP/Windows Vista/Windows 7/8
    • Processor: 2.5 GHz
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: DirectX 9.0 Compatible/ 1GB video memory
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX 9.0 Compatible Sound

Okay, I have to be honest. This game is horrible in nearly every way:

- Graphics are 2000's style, which are so bad it even puts shame on games like crash bandicoot.
- Graphics settings increase anti-aliasing, and nothing else.
- You can walk for about 30-40 seconds in one direction, and hit the edge of the alpha.
- Absolutely no plot, and the plot it gives sucks.
- Mary's "extreme abilities" include picking up rocks and jumping high.
- I really love that 30 second "folk jam" looping music track.
- Poorly optimized, runs 25-30 fps on my 3.5GHZ 6 core GTX 745 (i know that sucks) PC.
- Game physics are broken.
- The "puzzle elements" of the game involve playing the flute to open flowers which do nothing.
- There are 2-3 NPC's in the world all with exactly the same diolauge, and none of their quests are achivable now.
- the freaking task menu.
- No option menu, and you have to Alt-F4 to quit.
- Cursor clearly visible on screen.

But with all these bad things, I still love the game. Why? Because of how much FUN it is. The glitches are crazy fun.

- Pick up a rock as the girl, then mash space to Infi-Jump
- Use the infi-jump TO GET OUT OF THE ALPHA AREA but there is nothing around except void.

The only reason I put this as a negative review is because I don't want you to buy the game. It's like superhot. It's a good game, but fifteen dollars is a scam. This game is fun, but you shouldn't buy it, unless you want to make a profit off the trading cards, which I am currently doing for a profit. After you get the trading cards, it becomes your test for the uninstall button.

0/8 IGN

This is not a game - early access or otherwise. At best its the buggy, poorly crafted starting area for the first level of a game.

--

You can walk for under half a minute in either direction from the start point and hit the edge of the entire game map. That's how small it is.

The mouse cursor is constantly visible on the screen.

Character jumping is a glitchy mess. Tap the jump button half a dozen times and your character might jump once.

You can interact with a few things in the world but there's no real reason to do so.

The escape key doesn't do anything. There's no main menu and you've got to quit the game using ALT-F4.

You can switch between the characters, but you only learn this by hit and miss. There are no instructions.

The 'Features' listed for the game are a complete lie. There is no "A huge vibrant world, with colourful wildlife and environments.". At best that world exists in the mind of the developer. It certainly doesn't exist in this game.

Development seems to have been abandoned. There's been no significant activity by the developer in quite a while.

--

Don't buy this piece of rubbish.
