About This Game

Delver is a first person action roguelike dungeon crawl with crunchy pixels.

Take the role of an adventurer that tries their fate in the dark dungeons (and probably dies horribly). Fight monsters, grab loot, hoard potions, and level up!
Quest for the Yithidian orb at the darkest depths of the dungeon and attempt to bring it back to the surface - getting to it may be the easy part.
Levels are randomly generated in each playthrough so you'll never know quite what's around the corner.

Delver is in an unfinished beta state at the moment and has it's share of bugs and rough edges that need filed down, but it's very playable even in this unfinished state. Help us guide the direction of the game with your early access help and feedback. Our future plans are to revamp all of the art and sound, increase the depth of the RPG system, add special events and minibosses to the random dungeon generator, implement full gamepad support, and add a ton of new content. Help make Delver the best game it can be!

Key Features

  • Fast paced action rpg gameplay
  • Permadeath - once you're dead, you're dead
  • Randomized levels - never the same game twice
  • Loot hoarding
  • Chunky pixels!
  • Modding support - the desktop version lets you make and share your own monsters, items, and texture packs
  • Level editor included!

A solid rogue-like with an appropriate amount of content for a Beta. A small variety of weaponry and enemies allow for several hours of fun, afterwards it gets rather repetitive.

Immediately you're cast in the dungeon and it's up to you to learn, and until you do learn, it's trial and error which will lead to death. Once finished, the game doesn't offer any much more to do.

The combat is fun and I enjoy the artstyle and it's quite amusing to explore the randomly generated dungeons, however despite all that, even after a year, the game is still in Beta. Just another game dumped by the developers. Not worth the money, but it's a good purchase on sale.

5/10 - good game with potential but I don't tollerate abandoned games and false promises

Development isn't entirely abandoned, but a safe bet would be to say no new substantive update would come out this year at all. Go ahead and look for the company's twitter and the dates/frequency/content of posts if you wnat to get a better idea. Dont let the "Early Access" label get your hopes up. At the very least, it needs some optimization updates. This could vary machine to machine but just because your computer is othewise pretty good at what should be harder to process doesnt mean you wont get jarring stutters in this game. Reduction of particles does next-to-nothing to aleviate. There isn't even an update to change the pixel resolution at full screen, or any control customization/mouse sensitivity options whatsoever.

Beyond that, the game does have interesting aesthetics but with enemies that could best be described as an annoyance, with melee combat that feels more like a batting range with softballs. Maybe you wont regret buying it on a Steam sale, but if you're budget is 20-30 bucks for the whole sale, look elsewhere.
