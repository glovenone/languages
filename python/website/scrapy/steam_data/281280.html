About This Game

Mashed is a top down racing game that pits up to four players against each other.
Compete over 13 challenging and diverse environments using whatever means necessary
to batter and bruise your opponents into submission. Using a plethora of weapons or
just your driving skills unleash your fury, cause absolute carnage and send your
friends to a humiliating defeat!

Features:

13 tracks to race on, with a variety of cars waiting for you to master

Nine hi-tech weapons to pick up and unleash on your rivals

Use machine guns, mortars, missiles, shotguns, oil slicks, flamethrowers, mines, barrel bombs and blinding flares

Up to four players at one time, play “all-against-all” or collaborate with friends in the team mode

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 98/2000/ME/XP
    • Processor: Pentium PIII 800 MHz Processor or AMD Athlon
    • Memory: 256 MB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce™ or ATI Radeon™ 7000 or higher
    • Storage: 500 MB available space

Posted: 6 March
12 years later Mashed is still fun. A simple arcade racing game with a unique battle mode. Simply stay on the same screen as the leader or lose a point. It takes 8 points to win a match so it creates a great tug of war that can push both ways for an epic extended battle. Sometimes I won in a quick sweep fasion. Other times it swung back and forth for 15 min. The weapons, controls and camera make it feel like a tribute to the NES classic, RC Pro-am. The freeway battle level is amazing!

My only major complaint is the lack of vehicle upgrades or other forms of progression. You simply unlock new tracks, modes and cups. Regardless of the short comings, if you like oldschool racing with weapons, Mashing has enough unique ideas and solid execution to make it well worth the small price of admission.

"Mashed? MASHED POTATOES!"

When I was little I bought this title off the Xplosiv publisher and must admit that its by far the most amazing top-down game I've ever played and felt that I've spent my money right with this title. Played multiplayer with my cousin on the same screen and the insults were undeniably the most outrageous and funny ones. You'll get called a dumb cookie crumb and so forth, which adds even more icing to the cake.

Yet, the whole sad thing about this is the fact that this is Drive to Survive, not Fully Loaded so there's not any improvement to the gameplay and I've stated the bugs you'll encounter while playing.

Gameplay

It's all about survival, or more like mashing your opponents just like potatoes. MASHED POTATOES! Anyway- back to the main point I love mashed potatoes , you can mash your opponents with all kinds of hi-tech weapons like the lock-on missile that chases your target down (Only if you locked your target on the opponent). Furthermore, there's the machine gun that pokes the opponent's car with such violence that you can secure victory if used right. There are many tactics. But along with that... there comes the downs of it.

I've noticed that the AI is extremely aggressive even if playing at the easiest difficulty. I've witnessed the opponent cars crashing into me just to drive me off road to my doom, which is a total suicide for just a point. It starts to become annoying because you can lose the match really fast like this. Another thing worth mentioning is the fact that the AI doesn't know how to use the mortar powerup at all. I know, it's just one powerup they can't use, but it matters. Yet, not only that is annoying, but the terrain seems to be deformed or bugged out. I had my car jump out of nowhere while driving in certain parts of the map and while using ramps to jump, especially at parts with bridges (In Nukov and Sierra it's a really huge problem) it gets frustrating. The car either stops at the ramp and falls down the gap which is instant defeat if playing survival or the chopper attack event. I suppose there's no fix for this. But anyway, it's a really great game and worth playing. It's so worth playing that I managed to forget about all those bugs and continued enjoying it.

OST & SFX

There's not much about the soundtrack, but heck, it's surely fitting to the theme. About the sound effects - you're going to get shocked - This game, has INSULTS. Really rad, am I right? Being called a dumb cookie crumb was never more fun! Thanks Supersonic Software! The voice acting is satisfying, nothing to whine about.

Pros'n'Cons

Pros

+Huge amount of hours
+Replayability
+Multiplayer
+MASHING
+Nice powerups

Cons

-Track bugs

Should you buy it?

Absolutely! Sometimes it goes on sale at even one dollar, so it's worth it. But if you don't feel like it and you own a PS2, you can get the Fully Loaded version off the Xplosiv site or Amazon.
