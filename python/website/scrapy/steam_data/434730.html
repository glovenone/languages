Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We have been working on Half Dead for 1 and a half years and it’s ready to be played. We hope that early access will give us the feedback as well as the time and resources. They are necessary to create new features and game modes and make the game as good as possible. The final version of the game will have full DirectX 12 support.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We currently anticipate Half Dead to leave Early Access in the Fall of 2016. However, game development is always unpredictable and this timeframe is subject to change.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“In the full version, we are aiming for the support of the thousands of servers with up to 20 players. Also we are going to add new game modes, maps, challenges and other features.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current version of Half Dead is a very playable experience that shows many of the features of the final version. There are Single Player, 2 main game modes, over 100 servers with up to 4 players.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We plan to gradually raise the price as we ship new content and features.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“During the development of Half dead we hope to involve the community from the early stages so that we can produce a final product that is as close to what the players want as possible. Our team look forward to suggestions, ideas for new traps, scenario, challenge modes and bug fixing.”

About This Game

SUPREME COURT OF THE COLONY P-9174
NOTICE OF SENTENCING


Supreme Court of the Colony p-9174 informs you that your crimes classified as "violent crimes". The Court has nobly decided to give you an opportunity to choose a punishment from a list below:

1. Entertainment show "Half Dead" participation where you can win punishment exemption.
2. Any well-known capital punishment

We would like to remind you that the government respects the rights of its citizens. Therefore, choose the penalty due to your political convictions and religious beliefs. Your decision will be accepted without delay.

Extra information about the "Half Dead" show and exemption conditions:

The show takes place in the construction that is a network of rooms. Some of them are safe, but some can be dangerous for your life. Your goal is to find the exit from the building.

Win the game and you'll win your freedom!

Also it is an opportunity to serve your country. All the money earned by the show will be used to support democracy and fight rebels.

Early Access Review
Posted: 19 June
I had a couple hours fun playing "Cube: The Game" with friends. I also really liked the movie, and to see it as a game is pretty cool. Playing it as is, it seems functional, though being an early access game it is a little light on the content.

Speaking of as is, the variety of traps seems to be split up like this,
Trap room, Will it lock the player in (Yes/No)
If yes, it is defeated by throwing a boot inside, and having that room locked off from that point forward.
If no, can the player navigate the room with the trap deployed,(Yes/No)
If yes, In which direction and for how long?(Rotating/Side to side)
If no, how long do they have to realize the room is trapped and to exit accordingly?

The speed that the doors open is a little slow, though I suspect the reason for the current speed of the doors is balanced with the speed that the trap deploys. One thing I would like to see in addition to more trap variety, is different
clues for the player to pick up on to realize which trap they have or are going to spring (if any).
These clues can be visual or auditory, but should be if at all possible, as subtle as possible.
An example would be a room that slowly disintigrates them while they stand within it.
Since it is suble and works slowly at first, the player might think they found a safe room, but would lose while they are contemplating their next move.
Clues might include very small objects that could be emmiters on the ceiling and/or a slight difference in ambient
light. Additionaly, there could be visible cosmetic damage to the player model, or a gradual building health loss.
Which brings me to a different suggestion, a health system. Encounters with some traps need not be a win/lose situation. If in addition to a player needing to pay attention to the number of shoes they have left, they also have to be aware of their physical condition, then they might chose their next room more carefully.
Whereas, with the current method of "Throw a boot if you got one, if you don't, does the room have a guy in it, if not, flip a coin", I largely found myself blundering into rooms hoping not to die.
Once you run out of boots, it does become a coin toss whether or not the next room will lock you in with death.

Anyway, it seems like a really cool idea for a game, and I look forward to seeing what it becomes in the future.
As for how it is as an early access title, I think it is worh at least a couple hours of fun.

Give it a go.
