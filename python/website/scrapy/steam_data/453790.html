About This Game

Aight? I'm Candice innit (that's me up in the photo, the biggest one as I am well important), and this is an Action RPG all about the incredibly trick lifestyle of me and my m8s!

So basically, I was going 2 get myself a new fake tan one day, when an evil singing sensation named Krystal turns up & startz causing havoc innit?!? Naturally I wasn't having that, and tried 2 stop her. Sadly I failed, and she stole my mate's tanning business.

All was lost. But then I remembered I have magical powers! I can shoot out magical bulletz in every direction, like some sort of human twin stick space ship or something! I used this knowledge to go on an adventure around the world to learn new magical powers, and ended up saving both the world and the tanning business!

I had loads of fun along the way, did a bit of rapping (I am a well good rapper tbh), made new friends and had a few romances as well!!! Basically it was a right laugh, and u can experience it all in my new action rpg!!!!

Love,

Candice Xxxxx

Posted: 6 July
This is a game that would be describable as "lovably janky" if that jank wasn't "a lack of handy UI features and wild inconsistency". Some dungeons are massive (and I do mean MASSIVE) mazes, but there's no minimap there, because as far as I can tell, the minimap is actually just a top-down screenshot of the map itself, and the level is too large for it. Collision isn't so much "detected" as "occurs with the frequency humans blink", shoving you through objects, into the air, under the world, with regularity should you get poked at the right angle. Occasionally the game will just stop responding to button presses for a few moments, which, when occuring during a conversation, is irritating but harmless. When in the middle of battle, however, it's going to get you killed.

This is just gameplay issues alone. Graphically I can't tell if it's incompetent or going for next-level irony, and the music... I don't know if I can sum the music up without a sample, but "polarizing" is going to be the word of the day there. (In some small praise: the fact that the game seems to have a new track for every single dungeon and event is impressive given the fact that the majority have vocals of some sort and loop well. Until the dungeons become 5-level hell affairs.)

The trailer will tell you all you need to know about this game. Watch that and judge, because the narration, visuals, and music are basically the perfect storm of this title in minature, and you're gonna know if you want more or not.

Posted: 11 June
Candice Debebe's Incredibly Trick Lifestyle is a quirky game with a weird plot, even weirder characters, and a soundtrack that has to be heard to be believed. It's a game that at first glance looks like a poorly made rushed project that was hastily put on Steam to try to make a quick buck, but it actually had a lot of work put into it, despite it's shoddy appearance. It's a silly yet charming game, and if you're into silly joke games like I am, I say you should check it out. A few words of advice though on some improvements that could be made:

1. Add a run function so Candice can move a bit faster.
2. Add a map for Digital Hex or at least make it a bit easier to navigate. That was probably one of the most tedious parts that i came across and can be off putting since it's pretty early in the game.
3. Invest in a pop shield for your microphone.
4. There's a "bug" where you can use the boat to climb over the guard and get on the Lusty Mermaid before completing the necessary steps which should probably be fixed.
5. The price is a bit high to be honest, and I would definitely recommend the game 100% if it was at a lower price, but I'm not sure how much power the creator has over setting the price.

Overall, although it may look like Steam shovelware at first glance, the game is much more than that. It's not just an asset flip. It has original models, an original plot, and a soundtrack that although the singing may be not top notch, the music itself is actually well composed and the pianist definitely knows their way around a piano. Is it stupid? Yes. Should it be on Steam? Probably not. But for all its ridiculousness its amusing and you can't help but have at least a little bit of respect for the developer putting the amount of effort they did into this wacky RPG. I recommend this game if you are into silly joke games as this one has a lot of charm.

Candice DeBébé's Incredibly Trick Lifestyle is essentially a joke/parody action RPG that follows an extremely chavvy women on a quest to retrieve the fake tan van that was stolen from her own town (Think Ali G). The graphics are bad, the story is bad, gameplay is also pretty bad but still I enjoyed my time playing this.

As a brit I can relate to the jokes and songs found in the game that are all very tongue in cheek. I feel a lot of this humour would be totally lost on other nationalities so if you aren’t British or in some way very knowledgeable of British culture this may be a total no no especially when it comes to the games soundtrack.

Along the way you gain items, fight bosses and go through a number of different worlds and levels in an ultra-light action RPG game. The game is bad… in most ways but (and you are probably going to just have to take my word on this) it is oddly fun to play and I found myself continuing even though my usual common sense indicator told me the game wasn’t any good.

At £3.99 or your regional equivalent there are better games out there but for the price of a pint in London, a magazine or a coffee you will find a gag game that will make you chuckle and offer at least something.

This game really isn’t for everyone and most will probably not “get it” but Candice DeBébé's Incredibly Trick Lifestyle is a massive tongue in cheek game that for those of you who will get the references is a giggle worth undertaking.
