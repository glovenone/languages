About This Game

Do you love to wriggle through tricky situations?
Do you stay calm despite nerve-wracking situations?
Then Go Home is just right for you.

The goal of Go Home is to get home without dying.
Sounds simple, if there wasn't a variety of enemies and traps, which will make it difficult for you to reach your home before the time runs out.

    Many levels
  • Defeat the longing for the warm home and beat the 25 lovely designed levels.

    Mean enemies
  • A multitude of traps and enemies will stand in your way. However, this shouldn't be a problem, right?

    Collectables
  • Collect all the collectables and reach your home in time. Can you do that?

    Coop
  • Play alone or together to defeat the world of Go Home [Soon]

    Home Sweet Home
  • When the goal is near, reach your home before the time runs out, if possible without dying.

Go Home - Rage incoming is accurate in title but I don't believe in intent. It's supposed to be this very hard timing oriented game that I assume is supposed to irritate you into rage because you keep making mistakes. However, for me the real challenge was the aspects of the game design itself. I didn't try multiplayer, so that mode isn't being evaluated in this review.

The goal is to get your square to the home location, and on some levels it's required that you pick up tokens. Other levels require you to finish the level within a certain amount of time. Sometimes the timer kicks in once you get to a certain part of the level. Overall the idea is not very complicated.

Sound: No idea, I had it muted while I watched Netflix.

Graphics: This was one of the areas I believe that was unintentional rage. If you're slightly red-green colorblind like a good portion of men, playing the game is more difficult because of the issues with color choice. There is no color blind mode. Further, you NEED to play on the highest graphic setting for level 25. What is the only change that occurs between fastest and this setting? You can see wall shadows which is extremely useful. So, if yoiu want a harder challenge, play on the fastest graphic settings. Other than that, the game has extremely simple graphics which is all it needs. Don't expect a masterpiece.

Controls: This was one of the irritating aspects of the game. If you were pushing against a wall in a diagnol direction, you would push into it and then stop. So this could cause problems as you needed precise manuvering. Another issue was if you use the analog stick to control your character, clicking it will activate the level reset. This was my major rage issue. I'd be going along the level just fine, and then accidentally press too hard on the analog stick and it'd reset. Joy2Key would not override this setting either.

Rage incoming? Sure due to control issues and graphical issues. The actual gauntlets weren't that hard. They were quite fun, until the combination of all the issues together.

Overall: 4/10 unless you get it on a discount... and even then, it better be cheap. Forget that, wait for a bundle. If you get it that way, then it's worth the time and effort, but $5 as the base pricing is way too high. 25 levels, easy to complete, and honestly not really any replay value at least in single player. Still probably better to skip it: not really satisfying.

Posted: 10 September, 2015
Made me rage on level 25. 10/10

In all seriousness, the game is quite a simple concept, but at the same time, highly enjoyable. It will take around 2 hours to complete the game, based on your skill level. Unfortunately, both the spikes and the enemies are based on cycles - once you know how to avoid them, the same tactic will always work. Therefore the game has no replay-ability. The graphics are simplistic, making the game load very fast and play very fast. However, the harsh hitbox detection (and limited camera angles) make it very easy to get stuck around corners or bump into enemies.

Overall, Go Home - Rage Incoming is a very fun way to kill a few hours. However, I can't say the game warrants such a high price, as many other similarly-priced games contain hundreds of hours of gameplay, with much better graphics. If you can get the game on a good special or as part of a bundle, it's a fun one to pick up.
