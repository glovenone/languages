Before you look at my hours played, I should point out I played this a fair bit back in 2012, so what I say is based on my time with the game back then and from the changes I've seen so far.

Anyway to start it's definately WoW clone, but it's a WoW clone with enough of it's own identity to stand out. The setting is actually quite unique, with cyborg space zombies to a cold war Soviet city (Lulz Russia) all set on a 'Burning Crusade' floating island world. Combat is typical tab targetting and classes are pretty stock standard. We have dungeons, raids, and all the typical group activity dressing you find in most theme park MMOS.

End game is where the game shines in my opinion though. To describe it in a sentence.... Take the Gunship Battle from Icecrown Citadel, add a player pilot who controls said gunship and fly around landing on islands for loot while fighting off other player gunships to protect your bounty, all set on the backdrop of the 2001: A Space Odyssey LSD trip David takes at the end of the film.

And now the bad. As others have pointed out, playing for free is the epitome of p2w. It's a cash shop full of everything that makes this model bad. We have EXP boosters, gear and costumes with stats, mounts, pets, potions, end game currency, crafting supplies, anything you can think that's ever existed in a cash shop you'll find here.

Despite that, I'll be giving this a recommendation. The game itself is great. It's suprisingly polished considering it comes to us from Russia and if you're a fan of theme park MMOs it's definately one of the better ones. There's some interesting aesthetics here and while gameplay is a bit more old school, it's solid. The p2w cash shop will leave the worst taste in your mouth, but that's completely washed away if you subscribe.

TL:DR Good game if you subscribe. Good but pointless if you play for free.

Let me start by saying that I've played Allods since day one open beta, for around 4 years (over 12k hours of actual play time). I was for the most part an hardcore player, purely free to play, part of some of the best endgame guilds (Invalid, 3rdFaction, WarSong, MInc).

With that being said, my review is from a vet endgamer point of view, that went through multiple updates, server merges and even publisher changes (gpotato/webzen, Mycom).

Allods as a game, is a great mmo, it has plenty of content to keep your enternained, leveling was never so easy (those who were around during gipat update as well the lv47 24k gold cap, can relate). There's a very good amount of zones with different aspects to it, and the endgame can be fun if you play for fun and not to solely outperform the guy next to you in hope that your epeen will stretch a tiny bit more.

However the Allods curse is the "free-to-play" model it has. It's the worst piece of crap you can possibly find in a game. The company is utterly greedy and will make sure the cow is milked to its bones.
So unless you have very deep pockets or are willing to spend ridiculous amounts of time farming in circles, just to stay competitive, this game is not for you and you should avoid it at all cost.

The fact there is a currency exchanger where you can trade ingame currency (Gold) for Item Shop currency (Crystals) becomes meaningless when you take a look at the racio. All the best and must have items comes exclusively from gambling. Radiant Strongbox sales EVERY SINGLE WEEK, just to make sure you don't stack too much gold, and spend money to stay afloat. You can technically buy it from other players with gold, but the prices are off limits to most people.

And when I say "must have", you realy must have those items or you won't stand a chance in PvP and PvE. I'm talking about scrolls that give extra talent points, stat points, rubies, unlimited build reset and stats reset, combat mounts, trinkets and many more.
Even worse, there's different qualities on most those items (green, blue, purple, orange, fable) and they stack up, giving those that have all these Pay-To-Win items, a ridiculous edge over Free-To-Play, new comers and occasional buyers.

And don't even make me talk about runes. Basically, there's six of them, three offensive, three defensive, it goes from lv1 to 13. To make a lv2 runes you need two lv1, to make a lv3 you need two lv2, and so on... Bare minimum to stay competitive shoud be full lv9 by now.

Then there's patronage that increase your damage a lot and costs too much as well. It's just too many things you need to spend your money or huge amount of time on, and the worse part, they keep adding more with each new update.

The community in the game keeps getting smaller and smaller, after a month you'll already know who is who. I won't say it's a toxic community, but I have to say it's demanding and there's barely any room for mistakes.

The truth is, Allods always had a lot of potencial, but due to poor management, greediness above all, it never went past the hopes and dreams we, the players, had.

If you're still brave enought to waste your time in this mess, you better make a bank loan and keep in mind you're going up against many players that literally spent so much money by now, that they could have bought a new car or two.

Now you can avoid all of this if you just want you just have to spend $11 to play on Smuggler's Paradise which offers less of the P2W and more things like you would find in the more popullar game called World of Warcraft which this is pretty much a low budget copy.

I don't recommend you play this unless you have no other game to play or you have a small inheritance to spend. The game's on it's last leg, Smuggler's Paradise is a barren wasteland and New Frontier doesn't have enough Empire players to stand up to the Legue.
