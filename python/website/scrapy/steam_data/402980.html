I got this game from a bundle long ago and decided to give it a try.

The first thing you see when starting up the game is a little menu where you can select the levels, I couldn't find any options or settings. There are 40 levels, that's it. I completed 24 out of 40 levels in less than half an hour, which means it shouldn't take longer than two hours to complete the whole game. For some reason I couldn't select level 25-40 in the main menu, seems to be a glitch.

Once you start playing the levels, you'll notice that the gameplay is really repetitive. Level design is limited, you just collect all keys and move to the door, where you will be thrown to the next level, without transition or anything. The view can also be pretty glitchy in some levels, which is disorienting and annoying.

Two obstacles can be found in the levels; spikes and a little red enemy. Spikes, obviously, don't do anything. You just avoid them. Enemies, honestly, don't do a lot either. They move left and right, and that's it. You can kill enemies by jumping on top of them, but you won't get rewards or anything other than getting them out of the way. There's nothing more that will hurt you, and that's the main reason for this game being repetitive and not challenging.

In the first few levels, some text boxes occasionally appear, telling you what to do. These texts contain quite some grammatical errors, which make it pretty unprofessional. I know English isn't everyone's first language (it isn't mine either), but developers should at least take the time to make sure everything is written correctly, or ask someone who is fluent in English to write it for them.

The game also gives you the ability to teleport, which is sort of unique, but it feels pretty random and odd. Sometimes you get teleported into an enemy or into spikes and you have to restart the level. In some areas you cannot teleport, and it seems that the reason for this is to prevent levels from being too easy, which, in my opinion, is just bad level design. It is also a little unclear when you can and when you cannot teleport. The idea about the teleport mechanism was good, but the poor implementation ruined it. Overall I found this feature annoying.

Another repetitive thing about the game is the music. There are two music tracks, one that plays during the levels (which is incredibly repetitive and started to get annoying after a while), and one that plays in the main menu. After I finished level 24 I suddenly found myself back in the menu, where both tracks would play at once for some reason. As I mentioned earlier I couldn't select the next levels.

Pros:
  • Graphics are decent.
  • Player movement is mostly okay. (Played with an Xbox 360 controller.)

Cons:
  • The gameplay is repetitive and simply not challenging.
  • Level design is poor.
  • There is only one enemy.
  • Texts contain grammatical errors.
  • The view can be glitchy sometimes.
  • The music is repetitive and there are only two tracks.
  • I couldn't select all levels for some reason.
  • The game is WAY too short for its current price. (â¬4,99)

Osteya feels like it needs a lot of polishment and improvements and therefore I simply cannot recommend it, especially at this price.

Posted: 22 November, 2015
I was disappointed, the price is extremely high at what is $7.99 for a maximum of 2 hours. I paid a little over $5 for this and I still feel ripped off. The background never changes and there is only one "enemy", it crawls in a straight line back and forth and it dies if you jump on its head. No unkillable enemies, enemies that chase you, anything to make it a little less boring. The backdrop also never changes but I didn't mind that much at all.

The real issue I have with the game though is the camera. It is perfectly fine for levels which fit fine on your screen, but my god when the levels get taller the scrolling is horrible. It seems like every vertical movement you make the camera adjusts, there were a good few levels where I couldn't even see the only hud there is, which lets you know if you can teleport or not. Also when you die and respawn the camera starts in the middle and quickly pans down to your character, so the first third of a second is spent without your camera focused on you. It is disorienting and made my experience very bad. Also the funniest part, there's a few areas where you have to drop from the top of the level to grab a key and when you hit the ground the camera doesn't pan all the way so you can't even see where you are landing. Absolute joke.

I didn't like the teleport ability that much, it was very difficult to control. It seems you can only teleport up and to the side, might just be a personal gripe but I thought it felt awkward to use and preferred the normal platforming when I could.

The normal controls were solid though, with normal movement I felt very in control of my character which is probably the only reason I completed all 40 levels.

I can't see justifying this purchase if the price is anything above $2.50, there simply isn't enough content to justify spending that much money. If you are really trying to beat the game quick you can have it done anywhere between 35 - 50 minutes if you are decent at platformers.
