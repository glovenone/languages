About This Game

As the King of Iolcus, Jason had everythinga prosperous kingdom, the respect of his peers, and a beautiful fiancé. When she was killed on their wedding day, he vowed to do anything to restore her life. Now, in order to accomplish this heroic feat Jason must seek out the Golden Fleece -- and with the help of Greek mythology's greatest heroes -- set sail on the most epic voyage of all.
A grand scale Action/RPG, Rise of the Argonauts™ immerses players in a gladiatorial adventure set in the vibrant and powerful world of Ancient Greece brought to life. Taking the role of Jason, players will battle alongside Hercules, Achilles and other Argonauts as they engage in brutal combat against formidable beasts and enemies, in a vast world alive with wondrous inhabitants and stunning panoramas. The search for the Golden Fleece is not only one of exploration but of transformation -- from warrior king to exalted hero touched by the gods.
  • Immerse yourself in gameplay through a redefined RPG experience that streamlines character and resource management. Spend more time playing and less time micro-managing inventory and resources through a streamlined HUD and inventory system.
  • Unleash devastating attacks in real time with lethal combinations of weapons, powers granted by the gods and the battle tested loyalty of your Argonauts. Assemble the most effective team of Argonauts for each mission to put the best battle plan forward.
  • Explore the vibrant and powerful world of ancient mythological Greece brought to life. Experience a living, breathing and vivid ancient world taking you far beyond the broken statues and ruins that so often define the myth.

I really enjoyed Rise of the Argonauts. Yes, it's quite easy and not very long, the plot is kind of stupid in places, the loading times disguised as cutscenes (I'm talking to you, gates to the Acropolis of Mycenae) become tedious because there's much running back and forth, and it's not the best looking game out there. Why did I like it then?

Well, I'm a sucker for Greek mythology and I think this game gets the atmosphere right. Mostly. Don't get me wrong, the mythical characters, places and stories of old are misplaced, mixed and butchered without remorse, when not completely made up. If you want a more canonical depiction of Greek myths go play Apotheon (seriously, go play it, it's awesome!), this is more like a fanfiction or an alternate reality but still feels Greek and mythical enough.

I especially like the god alignment system. Dialog options don't influence the plot but they do influence gameplay because your dialog choices are aligned with one of four deities (Athena, Apollo, Ares and Hermes). Winning their favour by choosing their dialogs or dedicating your deeds (ingame achievements) to them lets you level up in their respective skill trees and unlock different powers and buffs. To me it feels very satisfying to devote yourself to a god (Athena rules and anyone who disagrees will eat my spear), it really gets me immersed.

The dialogs and characters are mostly well written. Each of your companions (you can choose two of them to join you in each level, out of four that you recruit along the story) have different personalities, and they interact between them and with the enviroment in different ways (Pan is snarky and will go on about old legends and trivia, Achilles is obnoxious and will brag about being the best fighter ever, and so on)

The combat, although not very tricky, is fluid, fun and bloody, and can get quite spectacular once you start throwing god powers all over the place.

Aesthetically, it's good enough. Some of the enviroments look pretty and colorful, like Iolcus palace (I really liked the guards openning the doors for you, I know it's probably there just to hide a loading time but it made me feel like a king and I've seen too many magical self-openning doors in games to appreciate the detail), while others are much more dull and uninspired. The bronze in your armour is shiny and many of the armours and wepons themselves are not too overly fantastical in design, which I like.

I haven't experienced crashes or major bugs that I remember, as many reviews say, and I played for several hours at a time. I guess I got lucky. I bough this game on a sale for dirt cheap (1.75 EUR) fearing that it would be awful or crash on me, but I was pleasantly surprised and, after completing it, I woudn't mind having paid more for it.

I really, really wanted to like this game. It shows so much promise, but for every step forward, it takes 3 steps backwards.

First, the game is a beautiful reimagining of the Greek myth of the Argonauts and the Golden Fleece. The art direction is absolutely gorgeous - the environments are great, your armor and weapons look unique and interesting, and all the characters manage to look "larger than life" while still fitting into the game world. The main characters, that is, because there are only 3 NPC models in this game. At one point, I was mediating a dispute between three merchants, and they were all the same person. It really breaks immersion to walk through a city full of clones.

The writing is really well done, and the conversation system (where you can pick responses that align with certain gods - like a witty response to appeal to Hermes or an aggressive response for Ares) works very well! But NPCs converse by stiffly holding the same pose and moving only their mouth, or repeating the same hand motion over and over, which is often distracting, sometimes unsettling.

The combat just kinda sucks, really. I like that you can switch between the Spear, Sword, and Mace, but they all play the same: just wail on your enemy until it dies. There's not strategy, it's just button mashing. It's also a real tragedy that there's a finite number of enemies in this game, and it's VERY POSSIBLE for your Argonaut allies to steal enough kills and prevent you from reaching max exp. So you get these really well crafted characters, but just end up resenting them for stealing your kills. Plus, the lack of a lock-on systems is BS, I can't count how many times I've whittled an enemy down to kill him with a thrown spear, only to have my character do a 180 and throw it at someone offscreen. Then one of my allies kills the weakened enemy, because of course.

The cherry on the top is that this game is an abysmal port. Apart from screen resolution, there's no visual settings at all, not even a LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH option. Plus, frequent crashes to desktop, which are inexcusable in my opinion.

It's a little embarrasing. This game could have been great - like, legitimately great, but they ♥♥♥♥ed it up so bad, I'm kind of amazed actually. I wish I could recommend this game, but I can't. Watch a youtube playthrough, though, because the story is pretty grand.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EDIT: Having played the game to completion, I'm now fully qualified to say this game is absolute trash.

Despite all the good things I said about it in my original review, my feelings about the game as the end credits rolled were overwhelmingly negative. The developer, Liquid Entertainment, should be ashamed of themselves that they took something with so much promise and potential and made it so irredemably terrible. Whatever good qualities this game has are so thoroughly drenched in filth, it's really not worth a playthrough to experience them.

Don't buy this.

Quick review of the game since I just completed it.

Overall a 7/10 for me.

I'll take a look at the gameplay/combat, graphics, story and miscellaneous things.

Gameplay/Combat: (8/10)

Since it's a story driven game you will sometimes find yourself in cities going around talking to various people instead of just randomly bashing in the skull of everything you see.
The dialogues are well done, whilst the actors look like blinking statues most of the time. One thing that bothered me the most about this is that in some cases cutscenes look like normal dialogue and then you end up skipping the whole cutscene instead of the one line you already read.

Combatwise it is a very fun game. You have 4 skilltrees to choose from, while you are able to fully spec about 2-3 of them depending on you enjoying the game or only doing the main missions.

The skilltrees are divided as follows:
Ares: Highly offensive berserker (focused on dealing damage and regenerating health)
Hermes: Offensive trickster (focused on dealing damage and dodging)
Apollo: Defensive melee tank (focused on blocking damage and regenerating health)
Athena: Offensive "godpower"-juggler (focused on dealing damage and using skills)

Each of the skilltrees provide quite a unique experience after starting the game, whilst you end up with 2-3 skilltrees filled at the end of the game.

The combat is intuitive, while the hitboxes of some enemies are constructed in a way that you mostly will push the enemy away rather than hitting them.

But the thing that bothered me the most about this were the invisible walls that are immediatly thrown at you as soon as you start a fight. Oh, you see a region more suited to fight these enemies? Nope, invisible wall.
Most of them aren't placed that bad and you won't even find them except you really try to run away from the fight.

Keep in mind: Best experience is with a gamepad/controller like with every hack and slash game out there.

Graphics: (6/10)

Yes, I know what you will say know. Oh my god, are you judging the graphics by todays standards while the game was released in 2008? No I don't.

Basically the games graphics for todays standards are poop. For the standards of 2008 they are ok and it is still enjoyable today.
Although a big minus is that the only actual graphics setting that it has is the ability to change its resolution.
In the game you will also find a lot of things that will kill immersion. Like guards opening gates by touching them rather than pulling a lever or at least pushing some kind of button.
Then the games regions are sometimes divided by gates that take a very long time to open. I don't know if it's an intentional "interactive" loading screen or just there to annoy the player.
Also some walls have very odd hitboxes, so you'll sometimes be caught on corners or can't get somewhere where you actually should be able to get to (nothing gamebreaking). Same thing applies to nearly every npc you'll encounter.

Story: (7/10)

I won't include any spoilers here, but some hints to what it is about. So don't read this part if you don't want to know anything about it.

The game is pretty straightforward when it comes to the story. As you probably can see from the screenshots it is inspired by the greek mythology, although not at all accurate.
There is one twist in it and it really is predictable.
It's your standard "perfect world - oh here's a villain - rescue your girl - be happy and marry her while you forget everyone that died for it".

Miscellaneous stuff: (No rating here)

Here just some stuff I noticed while playing.
You can add 2 followers to your group. You'll have 4 available in total.
There are no collectibles except for armor and weapons you'll eventually find while progressing in the story.
Your dialogue choices don't matter.
You will get overpowered at some point. The difficulty curve is just ridiculous.
Gameplay adds up to about 8-12 hours. Maybe rushing through it can be even quicker.

All in all I'd recommend this game to everyone that likes a pretty standard hack and slash game with a pretty standard story inspired by the greek mythology. I certainly had my fun and I'd buy it again.

If you found this review helpful, please give it a thumbs up so other people can see it too.

Rise of the Argonauts Review
General Score : 7/10
Worth playing ? : Yes
Replay value : Yes, but not for me.

Be warned that I played the whole game using a gamepad, and I do not consider any other way to play such games if not with an xbox controller or game pad. On the combat part of the game, because of this, your mileage may vary in contrast to mine.

Perfect example of one good game, that is a victim of it's own ambitions, but at least for me, this time, it is a positive thing.

Sometimes you can use Metacritic as a external support for your own conclusions:
Critic score:59
User score:7.2

You know why ??, because many of the players that reviewed the game on Metacritic and myself ,had the balls to hold up and finish the game, not like those "critics" that gave the game a 59.

Let aside the "twists" developers did on Greek mythology to build up the game, it excels on the story itself, voice acting and companions.

The message of humility, courage, wisdom, discipline and justice (Athena's virtues) spread all along the game's story, combined with other's Greek gods good and bad virtues, is just a great teaching for everyone.

Hell,close to the ending of the game, developers even let you go over Greek mythology on the creation of the world and all living things.

Through all the game, I was amazed how the selfish and particular desire of Jason (to revive his dead wife) was coped , mixed and contrasted with all this mythology, where serving the others and acting for the greater good above our own desires is the goal.

The first 8 hours of the game, I must admit that there is too much of dialog and too little on action.

The dialog system is smart, every dialog option you take is tied to a different god, so it's an alignment system. The way you think, the way you respond to dialogs, aligns you more with one of other Greek god(s).

All the deeds you achieve, via combat, quests, achievements or dialogs can be praised to a different god,thus, giving you access to different buffs and abilities trees.

First 8 hours of the game, this buffs and abilities might no seem much of a change, but later on, it is.

I specialized on Athena's (my favorite deity, of all there are) and the lighting - powers I got where an amazing spectacle on combat.

Combat pretty much is an arena combat, you get on a closed area, and fight your way out.

There are puzzles to be solved both in combat situations, and in normal quest situations.

3 different combat styles, really cool all of them. Sword, Mace and Spear.

Amazing usage of the shield ,and the shield charge against enemies to open up defenses.

The combat in general is really good, but at the second half of the game , it just rocks the boat.

Gods powers on combat are just the ice on the cake.

Companions are pretty useless on combat (unless you buff them al lot via skill trees), but all of them have great personality, characterizations and dialogs. You choose 2 of them to go with you , on different places.

So first half of the game, gets you into the plot with a lot of dialog, backtracking,some lackluster quests an little combat...... This is where I think most of the people quit playing the game.

Second part of the game, just gets better and better until there is no effort in continuing playing the game.

I must admit, that I managed to pass the first 6 hours because I like a lot Greek and Roman mythology, and I was thrilled with the game's plot, twists on the Jason story overall and with the general message on the game.

The scenarios are great and graphics are OK, besides some half ♥♥♥ job on some NPCs.

The fact there is no direct access to the map (you have to navigate menus to it) just makes me want to punch someone on the face.

GOOD: Interesting setting based on Greek mythology, and an original levelling system whereby you dedicate your accomplishments (completing quests, defeating enemies etc...) to the gods, which gets you points to spend on combat abilities. BAD: The game is short and fairly linear; although the dialogue options affect your character development, there is not much choice in how the quests play out, apart from which order you attempt them in. The combat is also pretty simple and gets a bit repetitive by the end. UGLY: This game has a lot of bugs, i had CTD's (though there are fixes on the discussion page that helped) and problems with the audio; weird distortions and sometimes it would cut out temporarily (make sure subtitles are ON!)

Overall i enjoyed it, i didn't mind the linearity or simple combat too much, and it has moments of real creativity. Its nothing groundbreaking, but if you can deal with the occasional CTD, for £5 I'd say its worth a shot

+ Battlesystem is fun, play it on highest difficulty otherwise it's really too simple.
+ You get attached to your companions
+ Easy to grasp Skill system
+ Playtime is just right. Not prolonged artificially
+ Fair autosave system

Cons:
- Graphics and animations are mediocre at best for todays standard
- Choices do not make much of a difference
- Buggy piece of software, crashed for me at least once per hour
- No keymapping
- Skills doesn't make much of a difference
- Could use some more enemy types (I recollect 5 or so)
- Manual save only creates a backup of the last autosave.

Verdict:
+ Good game. Definately something for inbetween blockbuster games.
