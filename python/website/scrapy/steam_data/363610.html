About This Game

A company is currently in development of robots for the new generation - Botology, conduct tests for a new system for the remote control of robots. The purpose of the company is to help future potential buyers explore the remote space systems. The consciousness of the robot's operation is connected with a mechanical prototype of sensory organs. However, risky tests can and will lead to difficult, unforseen consequences.

Basic Information
Title: Botology
Developer: Black Lime Studio
Publisher: Black Lime Studio
Genre: 3rd Person Shooter

General Impression
Botology felt more like a tedious shooting gallery than most 3rdPS’ I played over the years. The storyline is as generic as they get and it seemed more like an excuse to shoot endless scores of enemies until it dragged on for far too long. It didn’t help either that the level design involves a lot of walking and even backtracking (though at least we have a form of teleportation). Apart from the “Engrish” aspects, I was more annoyed by the performance stutters and screen tearing. It’s a game from 2015 and it had plenty of time to patch up at least the performance issues. That is unforgivable in my opinion. Indie titles have to work harder to gather up a playerbase since they lack the PR and Marketing machines behind Triple A titles. And when you see a game with potential and nice graphics, fail at a basic level design and performance, you begin to wonder how are the devs trying to cope with this ever shifting gaming industry? Trading Cards and Achievements add some degree of value to the game though. At least that.

Strong Points
+ Pleasant graphics and sounds.
+ Steam Trading Cards & Achievements.

Weak Points
- Translation problems.
- Liniar level design.
- Very repetitive.

Rating 5/10

As of right now, for the English players out here, this game is in broken English, and understanding it is far and few. I've tried for the longest time to get though the tutorial sector, "Sector 0", and their explanation of what to do is completely misunderstood and trial and error isn't seeming to work. Wait until there is an English guide posted with controls and gameplay basics before picking up the game, or, at least playing it. Because that way, you'd at least know what to do in the game.

EDIT: After 2 hours, I was able to finally make sense of it. It is possible to understand after a while if you're an English player, but it takes time and quite a bit of trial and error.

As for the gameplay, it's pretty repetitive. The game consists of: Shooting things, unlocking locks, shooting some more things, unlocking more locks, shooting more, crap ton of more locks to unlock. That's about it. :/

There is a lot of running from "point A" to "point B" to go back to "point A" to see if you can though yet, just to go to a "point C". A Bunch or large green orbs that teleport you to random locations on the map, but you feel obligated to go into them anyways because there is just so much damn walking.

The walking pace in the game is like a snails movement, and the sprint is more of a below average walking pace.

All of the max upgrades can be acquired within the first level, taking out all of the challenge of even fighting anything. Running into an enemy feels more like a nuisance at this rate, kind of like a gnat flying around you.

The "level", "sectors", "tasks", whatever you'd like to call them, are incredibly large for what the game is, making the game a walking/unlocking locks simulator, and going from "point A" to "point B" is always 100% boredom.

The game rips and tears non-stop while playing, and it's jumpy at all times.

During most cut scenes, you can still somewhat control your character, to it's a bit awkward. During the movement cut scenes, you can even press shift to move slightly faster, or as the game calls it, "sprint". If you have your weapon drawn before a cut scene starts, your entire HUD will overlay the cut scene. If you accidentally click more than 1 time to start an interactive cut scene, you will have a permanent text overlay on the bottom of your screen. For example, don't click "E" twice on the first lock in Sector 0, you will be forever annoyed with the rest of the game til you restart the game.

There are tons more bugs from here, and a good number of annoyances.

For the $4 the game costs, if you're going to achievement hunt and grab some steam trading cards along the way, go for it. The achievements are pretty simple, and pretty straight forward. But, for a few of them, the "task" achievements, you must complete the entire game, and that's several several hours of gameplay, walking, and unlocking.

If you're looking at the game for the gameplay, and a possible game for entertainment, your money is probably better spent elsewhere.

Summary:

Pros:
-Achievements easy to get

Cons:
-Hard to progress in the game
-Linear Gameplay (pretty much point and shoot, walk from A to B)
-Grammar pretty bad
-Instructions not explained well
-Enemies too tough first time around (1 life for 1 enemy)
-Enemies respawn too quickly
-No Manual Save Game!
-Graphics aren't top notch, but for $3 guess it's ok

Decided to try this out, conversations between NPCs were first to get my attention. They speak different languages with some english words inserted in the dialouge but there are english texts to accompany them, although the grammar's relatively poor. Gameplay's linear (go from A to B), you move pretty slow despite running and pretty much a point and shoot on one kind of an enemy early in the game. Graphics-wise it isn't top notch (depsite the max settings), although it's not going to bother you if you don't really care about those types of details.

Upgrading your gear isn't really well explained at the beginning of the game, just some form of text description on some of the recipes to craft upgrades but you are really only given a vague idea of how to go about it. You'll figure it out yourself - considering the fact that the enemies are very tough to deal with even at the beginning of the game, upgrading your gear is essential. It seems without upgrades at all it'll be one kill - one death, then you respawn at the furthest stage (e.g. You started stage A and got killed in stage D, you respawn back to A with respawned enemies which you have to kill again). With minimal upgrades you'll manage to get 2-1 KD, but otherwise don't bet on sweeping stages easily.

Lastly and also one of the most disappointing features of this game - No Saves! Or rather, it autosaves only on essential cutscenes with NPC, otherwise quitting afterwards will delete any progress you have made in the game - that means upgrades, inventory items, money, progress, etc. This no longer makes the game any more enticing to replay after one session.

Overall won't really recommend this game right now, as you can tell from the review already. There much better ones out there to get. If they can remedy most of the cons, I say for $3 it's ok to purchase but wait for an update.

Unlocking locks simulator 2015.

First things first: this game is very well made in the sense that everything is high quality (animations, world, special effects).

I was really impressed, and decided to continue to see how far into the game I could get. Unfortunately, it seems like the core mechanics of the game are not fun. You literally just open locks the whole time. It took me awhile to figure out how to open the locks, and I am still not 100% sure of which items I need to have in my inventory, as there are 3 different tiles of each color of lock unlocking elements. The descriptions hardly indicate what you would need, but it appears you need to get an empty jar, put the element in it, then you have a jar full of that element, then you need to click on it again and press 'use' and it creates another identical type of jar with the same description with a slightly different icon in the image and you need this to open a lock. After an hour I still had no idea how to do it but you can just click on everything and you'll get that lock open. [edit: it appears after watching someone else play that that identical jar type is a crafting ingredient! So you need to have the crafting jar, the empty jar, and the element, to create a full jar, It would have been much easier if we didn't need the crafting jar]

And then there are robots that want to kill you. It's pretty fun having to battle them, although they are pretty powerful and you may end up dying a lot. I found it entertaining until I saw that the robots just spawn randomly and constantly so you can't exactly "clear" them all out, you just have to deal with whatever is in front of you, just long enough to open the lock (while you are mashing the buttons in your inventory trying to create the right type of jar).

So from a quality standpoint, it's very playable, but as far as "fun" I would say that I do not recommend jar creator/lock opener simulator. PLEASE tell me that there is something beyond the locks. If so, I will do it. I will open all the locks if there is something of enjoyment on the other side besides more locks.

AND LET US RUN!!!

PLEASE!!

The game has a not-visible stamina bar, so you have to walk most of the time. You can run for like 10 seconds, then walking. Try to run again and you will have like 0.1 second of run, then back to walking. The gameplay is so frustrating and annoying that the least you could do is let us run around!!

Also, the english translation in the game is really extremely bad, but that's at least skippable.

Public Review on behalf of the Opium Pulses Steam group

Disclaimer: The copy of this game was provided by Orlygift. Thank you for making this review possible!

Few words about the game
Botology is a game where... I'm not even sure what's going on as it's explained with twisted and badly translated english. Intro tries to explain bits of backstory but it's hard to catch up even there. When you get into a proper game finally, you just go forward, without any clear instructions, just walk and hope you'll get somewhere. Once you get gun and some kind of objective... oh boy that's where fun begins. By fun, I mean grind at every corner. Literally. One of the most repetitive games I have seen in a while. Only thing that is good(ish) is music in main menu, that's all.

Advantages
  • Graphics here and there but only partially
  • As mentioned, music in main menu. Hey, it's something!

Disadvantages
  • Everything is translated into weird, broken English. It's hard to even partially understand what's going on and what you're supposed to do
  • Controls are weird and not really understandable thanks to translation that explains everything in default controls
  • There is no vocing, everything is "voiced" by some speech synthesizer. There's also no language used, just gibberish with random words used here and there
  • Gameplay is repetitive, all you have to do is kill enemies and unlock different types of locks, over and over again
  • Tutorial teaches us nothing and even if it does, it does it really unclearly
  • Animations and mechanics are compareable to game made in 2000s
  • Full of bugs everywhere (I fell through textures, died while touching the wall, walked on water e.t.c.)

Summary
Game is not worth time or money spent on it. Even achievements are easy to get but to 100% complete them, you need to pass whole game, good luck with that. Game is very below average, totally not recommended.

Personal score
2.5 / 10
