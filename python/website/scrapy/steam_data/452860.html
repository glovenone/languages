Mad Bullets

DISCLAIMER: I received this product for no cost for the purpose of reviewing.

Mad bullets is kind of a like a 2D first-person shooter but a little different. It's like one of those old machine games you used to play as a kid at Playdium, Chuckie Cheese, movie theaters or other random arcades. Those games put a physical gun in your hand and your objective was to shoot up the enemies on the screen, avoid hitting friendly targets, and stay alive. The only real difference between those game and Mad Bullets, is that you're using a mouse instead of a gun.

--------

Graphics: 6 / 10

The graphics are decent. It's very subjective, but I think the game pulls off the "Old Western" theme pretty well. The buildings, enemies and other objects within the game kind of look like moving cardboard cutouts, though I think that design choice was intentional. It also has a bit of a cartoon-y style with all the exaggerated facial expressions, body parts, and enemy design in general. The game shouldn't have any problems running on lower end graphic cards and CPUs as it's not that demanding.

--------

Sound: 6 / 10

The sound assets are fine, the generic gun shot sound and men grunting and yelling at you don't get annoying or repetitive, surprisingly. Although I feel that the soundtrack doesn't fit the motif of the game, the music is unexpectedly nice in some parts. I did have the music off and the sound effects lowered initially, as I tend to prefer playing this way when I feel the game is naturally too loud, but I turned it back up for reviewing purposes.

--------

Mechanics: 8 / 10

The game mechanics are actually quite solid. For a small title that plays like an old arcade shooter, it's competent. The game naturally progresses, and moves you from place to place, therefore you don't actually ever move your character. The only thing you need to do is click enemies to shoot them as the game moves you. You have unlimited ammo, but you must reload your revolver but clicking your bullet pouch when you see that it's low (or when the game tells you too). You have 3 "lives", and sometimes the enemies shoot back at you, and if you take three shots, you're done. You can however shoot the bullets down as you see them coming towards you.

It sounds simple, and really, it is, but sometimes the game moves quickly, and things can get a bit fast paced. Especially if there are many enemies at once on the screen. It can also be confusing as some people that appear on your screen are friendly, so if you shoot them, you lose one of your 3 "lives", these people are outlined in green so you CAN tell them apart from the enemies that are outlined in red. There are quite a bit of different enemies (and friendlies) within the game, and some of them have different projectiles that come your way. Some friendlies also are in traps, or locks, and if you shoot the trap/lock, you free them and get bonus points. You also get bonus points for head-shots.

At the end of your run, you get some gold that you can spend on power up for your next play through. The amount of gold you get depends on how well you did in the previous runs. Of course you can save your gold for better power ups later on, though I feel you don't get enough gold through each run.

--------

Final Personal Remarks

Although it's not something I see myself playing too much, as it's not really the type of game I enjoy, it did keep me coming back for runs every so often. I can see how people would enjoy this game. I thought maybe I could work on my head-shots for other games, by playing this game, though I don't know how much it helped, as I'm still terrible at CSGO and Overwatch. Maybe if I was better at FPS games I'd enjoy this a lot more. People who like precision clickers would enjoy this a lot.

Thanks to the devs for giving me the opportunity to review the game, as well as anyone who took this review into consideration, I hope it helped.

I want to share what I think of the game and I saw that there are no reviews for it yet. I have found it to be a great and intense rails shooter like games you would play at the arcade but with fun but easy to use mechanics. You aim and click to kill enemies, collect cash, save damsels, and collect power ups.

There is a great variety of both innocents and enemies to save/avoid and shoot respectively. There are even bats and chickens to shoot along with ninjas and crazy enemies out to kill you. You can save damsels by shooting the lock(s) off their bindings to free them which is tricky but rewarding. What's also really cool is that you can shoot enemy projectiles out of the air as they are coming at you granted you're quick and have bullets still in your magazine. One other fun thing they added is destructible objects like barrels, boxes, explosive barrels, and even things like guitars, pumpkins and boxes of fireworks.

I also find it awesome that it seems like each time you play, the route the game takes you on is different. I have played around 5-10 runs and so far it's different each time and I really like that. Sometimes you go up stairwells and fight your way through, sometimes you go through the main street of the time. It's a little different with each run which keeps the experience fresh, interesting, and unpredictable.

Another thing that's neat is that you can upgrade your gear and improve things with upgrades that you can purchase with the cash you get from your runs. These upgrades can increase your magazine of bullets, your score from a head shot, etc.

There's also missions to shoot for and tons of stuff to do including different modes of play such as Time Attack (best score in 3 minutes), Zen (just relax and enjoy shooting for 3 minutes without harm), Hardcore (harder and brutal enemies), Hyper (really fast and keeps getting faster), etc.

Also the music and sounds are great and makes the experience very enjoyable. It it catchy and gets better and better as it goes.

Overall, it's a really fun rails shooter game for only $4 with that 'just one more play' vibe. I highly recommend you pick it up if you like games like this or think it looks fun!

I'm not normally a fan of rail shooters as I prefer to roam freely, however that just wouldn't work with this particular game. That said at least the route is varied a bit & the targets are random when repeating previous routes so it's not easy to predict whether a friendly or enemy will appear. There are plenty of targets to shoot at other than the humans, some of these contain bonuses such as shields, money, infinite ammo (for a short while) & many others, whilst some contain a downside so be careful what you shoot at.
There is a shop where you can upgrade your abilities using money you gain from within the game, so it's not P2W. There are also several game modes some of which will certainly test your skills as you progress through the levels.

Pros:
Great bold & colourful graphics
Excellent background music & sound effects
Nicely paced progress through the game with difficulty increase to match
Wide variety of targets both good & bad
Plenty of achievements to play for
Lots of replayability

Cons:
Needs ingame music volume control rather than just off/on
Would benefit from controller support, I don't personally use one but I could imagine that many players would benefit

All in all, a great little game which is ridiculously cheap even if you paid full price, 9/10 will shoot innocent parties again. pewpew
