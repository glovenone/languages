About This Game

A Boy and His Blob makes its triumphant return! A reimagining of the NES classic, as the boy, you feed your blob jelly beans and watch him transform into cool and useful objects to solve puzzles and escape danger. With its robust and emotionally driven story, challenging puzzles, and visually impressive art style, A Boy and His Blob is sure to become a classic once again.

When Blobolonia is threatened by an evil Emperor, the blob comes to Earth looking for help. Instead, he finds a young boy. Help the blob dethrone the evil Emperor that's terrorizing Blobolonia and establish a friendship with the blob that will last a lifetime.

it's a really fun and creative game and I loved it. The gameplay is slow however, the boy's jump distance is tiny and the blob's AI can get a little annoying; you're more often than not, forced to spam the call button until the boy whistles to turn the blob into a balloon, then you have to call again to get the blob out of balloon mode which is very long and pointless (although it does seem the AI is designed that way as proof from some of the puzzles).

Still, despite the flaws, this game does have a lot going for it too: it's a very creative 2D platformer that has a fairly simple direct storyline but also has hard challenges along side it for experienced players. You're also made to use a certain set of the blob's abilities per level which let's you think differently rather than just using the same three abilities.

It's obviously meant to be a children's game but it's still fun none the less. If you can get used to a few of the minor and irritating flaws then you will really enjoy this game.

Exit Mundi
( 2.5 hrs on record )
Posted: 30 June
There are quite a bit of levels and content here and the game looks fantastic. However, it has a glacially slow pace of play and seemed really easy, so I didn't see the point in trudging through it. It seems aimed towards kids, but the slow gameplay seems like it'd put them to sleep. You'll spend most of the playtime waiting for a blob to slowly make it's way over to you until you want to choke it out through the monitor. It also doesn't have much in common with the original's open-world structure, which I think is what made this kind of gameplay distinct and made it work - without it you just have a level-by-level puzzle game that moves at the speed of molasses.

If the core gameplay of this game does appeal to you, and you are a more patient man than I, there are some things to like here. The game's charm and craft is undeniable and you do get more variety in the stages later on, with some cool blob powers and boss fights. I acknowledge the craft of it, but unfortunately I just find it all boring to actually play.

BlueTepig
( 20.6 hrs on record )
Posted: 20 June
NOTE: Received from Humble Bundle purchased for the other games.

The main mechanic of this game involves thowing jelly beans to activate a "blobs" special powers. You can only throw one jellybean at a time so these powers never have any interaction. Some levels seem like they're designed to make the challenge getting the blob through not just yourself, but the blob can clip through walls. Basicly this leads to a lot of waiting for the blob to poorly pathfind to the jellybean and then do an annoying animation. There is no reason the special powers shouldnt activate instantly.

This game also has a lot of handholding. If somehow you are having trouble with one of these simplistic, repetitive "puzzles" very often there is a billboard with the answer on it there to help you. This completely devoids the game of any challenge, and is actually insulting to the player. Another thing that has no place in a puzzle game is that in a few instances levels take advantage of hardcoded interactions in specific spots that cause things to happen that usually wouldnt. Additionally, the 3 collectable treasure chests in each level aren't always in order and if you miss one it was usually the other way at an intersection that you had no real way to tell which way it was til it was too late.

Ultimately this is a bland puzzle game with a lot of waiting annoying mechanics, and also bugs. There are a lot of small details i could complain about that are mostly bugs, but they wouldn't make sense to someone who hasn't played it and is reading a review. I was going to quit this game after world 2, but I beat it 100% out of boredom.

I got this in a Humble Bundle. I played the original game on NES back in the day and the game had a lot of cool ideas for that era. That said, it was difficult and a pain in my rear. It certainly didn't stop me from making many dozens of attempts at beating it, but the end area (bunch of candy/chocolate or something) kept wrecking me when the combat came up there, as I recall. When I saw there was a new A Boy and his Blob game, I thought to myself "Wow, that's brilliant. With today's technology and pedigree of gaming, this could be amazing!" I watched the intro video, was smiling by the end, started the game up, got my blob, pressed the "hug blob" button a bajillion times, and was "Daww"ing and grinning ear to ear.

Then I actually started playing the game. It only took a few minutes to see that the gameplay is uninspired with a main character who can't jump even half as high as a lardo like me can in real life. There's no evident story or narration that I noticed. If there was any, it was the most unforgettable stuff. Rather than innovate with the technology and cutesy fun story stuff we could easily get in this day and age, the game is just boring. I simply cannot be bothered to play this game a full hour. Does it get better later? Maybe! However, if a game can't keep my attention for that long, it has failed at its very core.

I would not recommend this game at all, whether you're getting it for nostalgia or because you've never heard of it but it looks cute, because nostalgia alone does not carry a game (for me, anyway), and the cute look and hug button are the only things going for this game. If you are really interested, I think you'll easily know within less than 2 hours if you're really feeling compelled to keep playing, and if not, the refund button can get you your money back, with your spent time as your only loss. That said, I want my 24 minutes back. =/

One of the first games I ever played on the Wii, enjoyed it on there and now loving it on the pc port!

Pros:

- Beautiful graphics
- Cute characters
- Fun story line
- Easy controls
- You can hug the blob

Cons:

- Sometimes what you have to do and where you have to go next can be a bit vague

A Boy and his blob is a game about friendship. You start off with a meteor falling from the sky so you go to investigate. Once you get to the crash site this white blob jumps out from the meteor and at first you are scared of it but then you become curious and become frends! Once you get back to your treehouse base a jar of beans on the table next to your bed falls over and a single black bean falls out. The blob eats it and turns into a ladder allowing you to climb up to a higher level of your base.

Throughout the game you will collect many different coloured beans that each allow the blob to change into a different form. You will need to use these to overcome obsticles and to safely get a boy and his blob to their next destination.

Also did I mention you can hug the blob?
