About This Software

The revolution for your gaming videos: MAGIX Movie Edit Pro 2016 Plus (German title: MAGIX Video deluxe 2016 Plus). Edit your walkthroughs on up to 99 tracks or capture exciting moments of triumph on Let's Play.Whether you're a beginner or an experienced gamer, you can work in studio quality even on low-performance computers thanks to native 64-bit support and proxy editing. Transform your experiences into videos that will impress your friends and the whole world.

The most important features:

  • Includes NewBlue Titler EX worth $199 (Manufacturer's SRP.)
  • Frame-precise video editing on up to 99 tracks
  • Proxy editing: Super smooth editing even on low performance computers
  • MultiCam editing: Edit footage from up to 4 cameras simultaneously
    ideal for Let's Plays - show us what you're playing and what you've got!
  • Enhanced dubbing in 5.1 Surround sound
  • HDR effects for unique contrast values in your videos
  • Secondary color correction: Detailed editing of individual hues
  • Includes high-quality special effects, cinematic transitions and templates
  • Export directly to DVD, Blu-ray Disc, YouTube, Facebook, Vimeo or as a file

The company MAGIX is allready very experienced in creating movie editing software. And this one doesn't dissapoint. Picture in picture is very easy to do, which was a prerequirement for me. Doing this in Camtasia was possible, but the software itself was unstable (I'm talking about Camtasia 7.1, other versions might be better but that's my experience with Camtasia).

After looking into multiple tutorials and reviews about MAGIX Movie Edit Pro 2016 Plus, I decided to give it a chance. If you are unsure whether or not to buy this software try out MAGIX's many trial versions of their software. So I gave the trial version a chance and did some time editing. The outputs on those trial versions can only be 3 minutes long, but it's enough to test out its functionality. Fading in and out, Zooming, Rotating, Blinking....you name it, all of those effects are easy to do.

Editing multiple clips and put them together is done well by allowing up to 99 tracks. The program also doesn't care what kind of tracks they are, be it music, video or picture track, it detects those by itself. It's also very versatile when it comes to movie formats. MP4-files are read and previewed within seconds. Audio can be split from the video file, so if you need to synchronize movies to sound (e.g. facecam) it is done very easily.

MAGIX also provides multiple video effects by itself, as well as multiple text fonts. I also believe creating a subtitle file for Youtube is also possible, since I came across a function in MAGIX which I haven't even tried yet. Additional effects and fonts can be downloaded from the MAGIX server (from within the software). The selection is wide!

The program also helps by telling you that files you added might not be suitable for the pre-settings you chose. Of course if you don't care you can simply skip those notes. The program also tells you if he finds any empty spaces between clips so the result won't have a few seconds of nothing in it.

The only complaint I have is that exporting videos takes some time. Of course it depends on the amount of effects you prepared, how large the files are and so on...a powerful CPU helps. Many effects can be rendered using the GPU, which lessens the computer's time effort to export your video.


Summary

MAGIX Movie Edit Pro 2016 Plus is a stable and reliable movie editing software which allows up to 99 movie / audio / picture tracks and is most suitable for mid-performance editors and Let's Players. This program has the functionality you need! Like most 'complex' programs it does take a few hours to get used to the functions and usability. If you have any trouble there's tutorials all over the place. If you are unsure if you should get it, simply go to the MAGIX website and try the 30 days trial version. But believe me, it's worth it!

Pros:

+ Stable
+ Good usability
+ wide range of functions
+ allows up to 99 tracks
+ synchronizing and editing multiple tracks at once is easy
+ overlapping of video layers is easy

Cons:

- takes some time to export the video
