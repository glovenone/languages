About a purchase button disabled

We here in Rai Studio want to say couple words about this situation and bring a little more explanation on what actually happened.

Firstly, we are not developers of our games, but the publisher.
As a responsible publisher, we have managed to bring some amount (yes, large amount) of keys for giveaways in different steam groups.

We haven't expected that one of this groups would be headed by an administrator of a steam-bot network. Nor we didn't expect him to use this keys and his bots to actually exploit the review system of our games.

Again, we haven't payed them any penny for this.

We got known of this situation too late, and yes, this is completely our fault.

We feel really sorry for the developers of our games, because they are totally innocent, and of course for the players, as this situation was cheating them. We will do our best to support players who bought the game, do refunds, try to get to consensus on this question.

Again, this is just a circumstance, but we agree with Valve's decision to disable a purchase button.

Speaking about this decision,

The games are NOT banned, only purchase button is disabled.
Everyone who bough our game can continue playing or do a refund. Every key will still work. However, we cant generate keys anymore.

We have only keys that has been generated before an incident, and they will still work.

So bundle buyers can still buy the game. People who want to buy it can still do it on Indie Gala. The only keys left are there, so if you want to try a forbidden game for purchace, go on ;)

And the last thing we'd like to share to you:

We have contacted Valve support. We have managed to delete all the bots reviews from all the games. We do agree that we are guilty and feel terribly sorry for developers and players.

This is a hard, but indeed important lesson. We will not let things like these happen again.

Thanks to everyone who have supported during this situation.

Regards,

Rai Studio Games Publisher.

About This Game

Rapid Squirrel is an arcade 3D runner with some crazy squirrel on board! Fly your character over very fun and entertaining levels in search of acorns! Make your way through obstacles and achieve a squirrel goal!

BE A HIGH FLYING SQUIRREL

A flying squirrel in search of some tasty acorns - that's what she's aiming for! And only your help can save a squirrel's soul. With great physics and animations gameplay becomes even more fun!

YOUR JOURNEY BEGINS NOOOOOOOOOOW!

Jump right into the action! One step forward and a squirrel becomes an airplane - with more than 20 levels and unique areas you have to complete different missions!

A SWEET HOME GRAPHICS

Arts & visuals are very stylish. You can feel the atmosphere and the style of the game in literally every small detail of the Rapid Squirrel's World

Key features:
* Fun and simple runner/arcade gameplay
* Great content & physics - for a real fly!
* Nice and stylish visuals
* More than 20 levels to unlock!
* Crazy Missions, Bonuses, Goals & achievements - all in one game!

Rapid Squirrel is a simulation game in which the player takes on the role of a flying squirrel who glides from one floating island to another in an endless search for acorns.

Rapid Squirrel feels like the kind of game that got published way, way before it should have.

I loaded up the first level and was immediately confronted with a number of perplexing instructions for how to play the game. The instructions were written in very imperfect English and kept referencing a touch pad, which I found somewhat confusing (since when does anyone build a game around a touch pad?) until I realized that this was probably meant as a mobile game and the touch "pad" was actually a touch "screen," which of course my computer does not have. With no idea how to play the game with a keyboard, I tried pressing the trusty "W" key, and voila, the squirrel started to walk forward. When it reached the edge of the cliff, it leapt off, and immediately started falling helplessly into the cloudy expanse below. After a while I managed to find some ways to guide the squirrel's movement, but there were still several things I couldn't quite grasp, particularly how to change the direction the squirrel was falling in. This was kind of a big issue, since most of the nuts that I was supposed to be trying to grab were not directly in front of me. To make matters more frustrating, birds would often fly by and eat the nuts right before my eyes. If there's a way around this, I couldn't find it.

The first try ended in failure, after a while. The end goal seems to be to land on a certain island, but it isn't remotely clear where it is. At one point in my second attempt, I did land on an island, but nothing happened; the squirrel just sat there. This is the kind of thing that makes me feel like the game came out too early; if you know what you're looking for, and how to move the squirrel around, then maybe it could be okay, but at the moment, it's entirely unclear to the player what is going on.

The other issue, though, is that even if you can figure out how to play the game, it isn't clear that there's much to do. It's satisfying when you finally manage to grab one of the floating nuts, but it doesn't last long. The action is too demanding to be a tab-in, tab-out at work deal, but too light to be engaging for very long, either.

    Pros
  • Fluid squirrel animations
  • Catching nuts can be satisfying, the first few times

    Cons

  • Instructions don't make sense
  • Controls are unintuitive
  • Gameplay is repetitive
  • Goals are unclear

Final Thoughts

Unless the developer makes some significant changes in the future, it's best to steer clear.

Final Rating (Out of 10): 3
