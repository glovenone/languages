About This Game

Get ready for the run of a lifetime. In this endless platformer you play an elemental spirit that has the ability to change form. Spirit Run: Fire vs Ice is inspired by games such as the platformer Canabalt and the classic shoot ‘em up Ikaruga. Jump, slide and transform to reach new heights. Use your spirit powers to change between ice and fire form. Match the elements to dash through fire and water hazards. The goal is to get as far as possible and to collect elemental orbs to multiply your score. Numerous obstacles need to be overcome if you want to compete with the best on the leaderboards. Spirit Run: Fire vs Ice features beautiful pixel art and 8-bit retro soundtrack that takes you back to the golden age of videogames.

  • Endless platforming fun
  • Beautiful retro-style graphics
  • Transform to escape traps and to collect multiplier pick-ups
  • Challenge your friends on the leaderboards
  • Includes Steam achievements

Posted: 18 June, 2015
Oh my god, that was awful.

I bought this on impulse during the Steam sale because I thought the graphical style looked pretty cool. It advertises itself as an endless runner with an Ikaruga color-switching mechanic. I thought, what could go wrong?

The controls. The physics. The visuals. It was a nightmare.

For starters, this game has absolutely no edge physics. None. For the unfamiliar, "edge physics" are a feature of just about every highly-rated sidescrolling platformer in the history of games, which gives you a few frames' window of opportunity to jump after walking off the edge of a cliff -- kind of like a tiny midair jump that feels like you're propelling yourself off the edge of the cliff. Mario does it, Sonic does it, every platformer character I can think of off the top of my head does it... but not this game. Here, upon stepping on the edge of a cliff, your character will immediately just slide off. This actually makes the game impossible at higher speeds, because if you get a bad combination of landscapes, it's possible to just trip down a bunch of stairs and into a pit with no way to save yourself.

There are only about 7 terrain shapes in the game which are just repeated in different orders ad nauseam. Certain combinations of terrain will make it impossible to grab pickups. Other combinations will make it impossible to grab a pickup without also jumping into a pit. As you speed up, certain combinations of terrain become outright impossible to survive. If you're going for a high score, you'd better just pray you don't get any unsolvable terrain.

The game makes you hold down the jump key to prolong your jump, yet it doesn't check to see if you've released the key and pressed again before making you jump again. This means you'll frequently bunny-hop to your doom if you're not careful.

The fire and ice switching is only useful for avoiding two kinds of death obstacles and picking up two kinds of powerups. That’s it. There are no other game mechanics.

The visual style, which is probably the best part of the game, is also a detriment. The ice pickups, as well as the ice character, have no contrast or outline, and are practically invisible on the game's bright-colored backgrounds. The scrolling terrain constantly stutters in a headache-inducing way.

And the music is one 60-second loop that plays during menus and gameplay. Over. And over. And over.

You will almost never hear me say this, but stay away from this game, even if you’re an achievement hunter. I know you can fully complete it in about three hours, but with the infuriating edge physics and the luck factor, it’s just not worth the emotional abuse. Please. Just trust me on this one. This game is torture, and I suffered through it so that you don’t have to.

And I want my three hours back.

Game claims to have "full controller support" and has ZERO controller support. Big turn-off. Acually, you can manually go and re-bind your controller buttons onto the inputs on the keyboard through the launcher, takes a few minutes but it is possible. However, this is not "full controller support", I would barely even call it "partial" support.

Then when you actually go to rebind your keys you realize there are only 3 buttons: jump, switch fire/ice, and slide. You hardly even need a controller since you can't even move your character freely. That's not a huge issue, though, the game was designed in a certain way that does not require movement. You just walk along with the world. Does the world ever speed up? It seems so slow it's boring. More like the "walk" of your life. Sure you need time to think about what to do, and slower = easier, and it probably gets faster and/or more difficult later on, but initially it comes across as pretty lame. Imagine Super Mario Bros except you have someone else holding down the button to walk and it is your job to jump. I would much rather have the ability to RUN, like actually FAST, but the screen moved at a minimum pace, and was rewarded for running faster than the game wanted to go. Having very little control over what is going on is a turn-off, definitely. It becomes a game of rock-paper-scissors very quickly.

Sometimes, though you can get a really really bad game and it is still enjoyable if the leaderboard is up to par. This way you can show off your achievements of torture. Does this game have a good leaderboard? NO! It barely has one. What I would have liked to see is this:

- see your rank globally
- see your rank among your friends
- browse through all the users' scores freely

Instead, we are given a list of the top 10 scores in the world. If you are not in the top 10, you don't appear ANYWHERE. Oh, and those scores are super high already, on the first day, so basically forget they even exist (and since this is a simple game, expect them to be hacked pretty quick).

So the leaderboards are basically non-existant, the gameplay sucks, the controller support is a lie, did I miss anything? I'm not even going to go over the empty steam repository issue yesterday, mistakes happen. Is the game worth $2? Hm. Sort of. If it were a bit more polished maybe. Give it a few months for the developer to fix all of these problems. You won't miss much!
