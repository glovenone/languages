About This Game

'BlazBlue: Chronophantasma Extend' is the sixth installment of the BlazBlue series, a combination of 2D fighting action game and visual novel. The game incorporates flashy performance and unique fighting moves with the Drive Button system to quickly take down the opponent. Now players can dish out super-powerful Guard Crush attacks when their opponent’s Barrier Gauge is low with the revamped Guard system. A player can quickly turn the tide of the battle by using BlazBlue’s unique mechanic - Overdrive - to enhance his skills and abilities. With two new characters, Celica and Λ-11-, joining the roster, the player will be able to choose from 28 different playable characters. Also, a story mode named “Remix Heart” is newly added, featuring two characters, Mai Natsume and Kajun Faycott.

My Room

A totally new system, ‘My Room’ has been added to the existing BlazBlue network system. In the room, you can talk with other players and decorate your room with wall items, furniture items and etc. You can even fight with other players as a team in Team Match.

Crush ‘Em!

With the all-new revamped Guard Gauge system, players can now determine when to attack/block using the Barrier Gauge meter. When your opponent’s Barrier Gauge meter is depleted, unleash a powerful barrier-shattering move, Guard Crush!

Ready to Fight with Style?

With Stylish Mode activated, even beginners can perform flashy attacks and combos! Four new features have been added to Stylish Mode: Auto Guard, Combo Attacks, Special Abilities, and Distortion Drive. These features allow players that are new to fighting games to pull off advanced combos!

New Stage, Characters and Additional Scenarios!

There will be a total of 28 playable characters with two new exciting characters, Celica and Λ-11-! BlazBlue: Chronophantasma Extend includes brand new scenarios and contains digest versions of BlazBlue: Calamity Trigger and BlazBlue: Continuum Shift, so both new and returning players can enjoy the Story Mode. Celica also comes with a brand new stage, Ishana, the land of memories!

Remix Heart and Gag Scenarios

Tired from all that fighting? Take a break and enjoy a heart-warming comedic story (mode) featuring two high school girls, Mai Natsume and Kajun Faycott, as well as the characters from the main scenario, Noel, Tsubaki, and Makoto! There are newly created 8 additional gag scenarios you can enjoy as well.

Note: This review is mainly on the single-player content. Not that I eschew multiplayer normally, but the single-player just kept me busy enough!

Blazblue is best seen as a branch of the Guilty Gear series, mainly with the anime and manga influences turned up to - if you'll forgive me - 11. And like anime and manga, this is a vehicle for fanservice. Now, I don't just mean having ridiculously sexualized female characters, though it certainly doesn't lack for those, but in general trying to cater to everybody's tastes in over-the-top characters that are designed so in theory everybody has a favorite to hold up. This is, of course, why the story mode is a endless torrent of visual novel word vomit, so that every one of the 26 characters gets a moment to shine.

I'm getting off track, though, so let's talk about the fighting. It's good. If there's a criticism I'd level with it, it's the usual tendency for fighting games to have feature cruft stack up, from Crush Triggers to Overdrives to Rapid Cancels and actually mastering all of it is literally akin to learning a language. Thankfully, at least at the casual to afficiando levels, mastering every system isn't necessary, but if you go cross-eyed when a game tries to get you to take terms like "Guard Libra", this may be overwhelming. Where the complexity shines is in the characters, though, that each get their own subsystem that really makes them feel unique, with very little cloning in effect. In addition, almost every one of these systems is interesting, and each character contains a lot of depth. Of course, that gets back to my original point about the appeal of Blazblue - shoving characters in your face until you click with at least one. Some require a level of motion mastery that gets downright farcical - Bullet requiring a 1080p motion for one of her super attacks (I'm sorry, Distortion Drives) comes to mind - but most you can manage basic mastery without overt effort if you've played a fighting game seriously in the past twenty years. The end bosses aren't even too much of a headache, which is a rarity. But they aren't fair. Let's not get too hopeful, right?

The story is a mess of anime garbage and word salad (Ars Magus, Hierarchal City, Continuum Shift, etc.), even including an extensive glossary, summaries of the story up until this point, and literal lessons on the game's lore. I do enjoy it as an ongoing anime soap opera, but you ain't getting more than relatively simple characters who will hit their character beats like a dead horse and a lot of melodrama. That being said, it's still fun if unsophisticated, though some of the humor in the gag reels can fall extremely flat, either due to the game's inability to ever let a joke die peacefully, or hinging things on bad sex comedy that feels intensely regressive (panty theft, "wacky" incest, false sexual assault accusations). The nadir is probably the Remix Heart story, which is an extension of the Blazblue manga of the same name which is 95% low-effort ecchi trash... and about 5% positive transgender bit, but it doesn't make up for the former.

That all being said, the game buries you in things to do and unlock, from a survival mode where you level up a character to murderous challenge modes to an extensive gallery mode. It'll take a long time to unlock everything legit, and most of it is cool... but I'm going to throw in a caveat. It's a small, almost inconsequential thing, but the gallery does contain some troublingly sexualized images of the underage characters. If you're in the anime and manga community, you know the drill, and it relates to what I was talking about at first. By trying to appeal to all sorts of people, they also appeal to some people that just aren't worth catering to. It's almost enough to make me give it a thumbs down on its own, and if you've read this far you can give it one yourself based on your willingness to tolerate this sort of baiting.

That being said, the fighting fundamentals are still amazing enough for me to give a recommend, with bright and colorful (if aging somewhat) sprites, and a lot of content for your money. While it relies on quantity over quality to an extent, it has enough unique gameplay notions and clever ideas to give it a recommend with the above caveats. While not as progressive as Guilty Gear Xrd, if you're looking for a solid 2D fighter with a fun cast that can essentially keep you busy for eternity, it's absolutely worth the asking price.

I think this game is the best choice for the one who may want to try fighting games for the first time. Very informative and nicely voice-acted in-game guides and tutorials here are combined with such a nice and noob-friendly addition like "Stylish" gameplay mode for those who want to see any results from insane buttonmash. Unfortunately, I am not competent enough in this game to tell how well-balanced the characters are, but at least it is definitely not netherrealms - I know Arc system Works as creators of Guilty Gear, one of my favourite fighting games, which is definitely pretty well-balanced if to talk about different matchups.

Well, it's a rapid 4-button 2D fighting game made by those asian guys who know how to make fighting games.
The graphics here is a perfect fusion of high resolution 2D character sprites and 3D-stages, with a bit of excess of different flashes and effects. Bright colors, awesome character animations, and very interesting backgrounds, each stage with it's own personality.
Sound and voice acting.. As in a generic anime game, music has it's typical features that you always expect from a game with such visuals. I'm not a fan of such soundtracks, but I will definitely say that they totally fit into the game and create a very pleasant vibe. I play with english voices and I really enjoy the voice acting - the somewhat similar looking female and male characters appear to be totally different, and the english voices give them a lot of personality.
The story mode is it's own sort of a masterpiece - it will take time for you to familiarize yourself with "how it works", and then it will give you a lot of fun. You will discover the depths of BlazBlue history and lore while listening how different characters interact with each other in a main canon scenario full of optional "hidden" hilarious "Gag" endings where somebody acts like it's not their but our world or like they have some very absurd issues to solve. Imagine the characters of your interest participating a cosplay contest, contest of manliness or teleport to another reality, where villians and heroes know each other as close friends, have a typical daily family life etc. ... I mean it, this fighting game has the awesome story mode.
In gameplay aspect this game is something between Injustice: Gods Among Us (4 attack buttons including one for very special abilities), Guilty Gear (a lot of gameplay mechanics and controls like aerial combos, combo escapes, burst, overdrive, improved instant kill analog, and stuff like dashes, aerial dashes, doublejumps, superjumps, faultless defence and instant block) and even Street Fighter (now talking mostly 360, 720, and even 1080 special moves input).

If you want to jump into fighting games genre from nothingness, this game will teach you most of the basic genre terms, give you all basic lessons about the whole game, advanced mechanics, and it will help you master each and every character personally with two character-individual training modes - with combo challenges and with very deep explaination how each character's abilities work - this game is for you.

Also, it's a must-have for visual novel genre lovers, I think.
