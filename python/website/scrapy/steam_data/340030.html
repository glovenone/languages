About This Game

Anciently the city was known for its artisans and cunning traders. The rumors about its undercroft wealth amazed even the men of great intellect, but wizards’ mastery dampens the ardor even of the most dashing adventures.
Recently, an unexpected thing has happened… the last caravan returned with an incredible news: an unknown force destroyed the city; some of the inhabitants scattered in all directions, some of them hid themselves in the dungeons. The rest of the City Council members promise a worthy tribute for those who will be able to find the stamp lost during the turmoil.
Many inhabitants wishing to get the tribute came back to the city. Only the time will show whether the main character will find the riches or he is fated to decorate one of the undercroft corners with his skeleton.

Early Access Review
Posted: 3 January, 2015
If you want to understand the history of this game and the strength of feeling against it then you should know that when it first came out it cost £10, it was not marked as early access and it had massive issues like escape key insta-quits the game and it would lock up for 30 seconds at a time and not respond to input then randomly walk you about when it "came back". You also could only play one of the three classes. (An issue, which like some others was working in the Desura version).

I have updated the review as the devs have massively lowered the price and fixed some of the bugs.

REVIEW STARTS:

This game is fairly cheap but it is also not very good. I really get the impression that the developer(s) are not fans of the roguelike genre. I don't imagine them enjoying their own game, I think they are just "pushing it out there" to make money.

One of the most fundamental problems with the game is that the controls are arbitrary and the way you do things is not consistent. In a normal roguelike, attempting to move into the space occupied by a thing (called "bumping") activates a default action: thus, bumping into a door attempts to open it, bumping a monster attacks it. In this game, bumping into a monster will attack it but doors you have to turn to face them and then press the action button (space by default). You can use the mouse to click on a monster to attack it (allowing you to attack diagonally) but you can't click on doors to open them. You can use the keyboard to move around but you can't use the mouse to click and move. You have to use the mouse to take things from chests.

The bottom line is that whether you prefer to play roguelike games with a mouse or with a keyboard you are forced to use both. If you just used keyboard you wouldn't be able to take anything out of chests and wouldn't be able to attack monsters diagonally (which is do-able but makes the game harder). If you just use mouse, well, you can't even move.

I mean come on guys, basic things like escape should close windows and enter should accept them. Do you guys not play PC games at all?

If you ignore the problems with the controls, or they get fixed, and all the various bugs get fixed you aren't left with a _terrible_ game, but it is shallow, boring, and possibly unbalanced. There are better roguelikes on steam that you could be playing, and lots of free ones not on steam.

***

List of bugs section, just dumping them here:

Bug: If playing as rogue (not sure about others) if you attack an enemy by moving backwards away from it and it dies, the corpse will become a barrier that you can't pass. It's very possible to get trapped this way.

Bug: Level gen takes far longer than it should. There is also sometimes a massive delay when searching a corpse for treasure: I'm talking like 10 seconds.

Bug: Selling a weapon you're not using un-equips the weapon you are using.

Bug: Sound disappears sometimes.

Bug: I got really, really far. I was on level 11 (hero level) and found some great weapon. I was even starting to enjoy it a little bit, or rather I wanted to see if I could get to the end since I had 700+ potions and nearly all skills on full. Then I picked a key up from a corpse and the game crashed. When I relaunched the game and continued my skills were lower than they used to be (even considering when I last "saved" by going to main menu and back, and the camera was not centred on my characted so i had no idea where I was. Quitting to menu and back again fixed it but now whenever I open my fitting screen (character equipment), the game crashes.

Bug: when buying potions, if you click on a potion more than once then you get an endless "do you really want to sell the potion health for $10" and every time you click ok you lose 10 gold until you go negative gold. http://i.imgur.com/llFpsc3.jpg

Bug: I bought a better weapon from a merchant and it showed both my old weapon and my new weapon as being used (green check (tick)) When I went to my character "fitting" screen it showed my attack as zero. All seemed ok once i equipped the new weapon.

Bug: Buying a weapon says "Are you sure you want to SELL this axe for ..." rather than "buy".

Bug: The currency in the game looks like gold coins but the merchant says "Are you sure you want to buy this axe for $75?" DOLLARS?! SERIOUSLYI? Did you just take the code from some other game that used modern day setting or something?

Not a bug but: Could you make it easier to buy more than one potion at once? It's tedious buying 30.

Bug: when you use a potion the graphic fullness of your hp orb fills up but the percentage number does not update until you get damaged again.

Bug: game sometimes locks up and won't process movement keys any more. Happened to me after using the mouse to attack a spider to my left when I was facing up (because pressing left turned me briefly towards the mob then back away again, see below). You can use the "pause" button (menu button really) to change settings etc- the game hasn't crashed, but you can't play it any more. Only way to fix it is to quit to main menu then continue option.

Bug: game sometimes locks up when just using movement keys. May be to do with anticipating the corner coming up and pressing the button early.

Bug: Depending on whether you bumped into something and what direction you were moving in, your character won't always stay facing the direction you tried to move in. (eg you move left, bump into a wall then press up to face the door above you so you can open it, but your character flicks back to face left so you can't open the door). Can happen so you can't attack a mob by bumping it too, as noted above.

Graphical bug: player standing facing door to the south appears to be standing on top of door rather than behind it (draw order / z order bug).

Bug: scrollbar handle (user interface element), which looks a bit like a jewel, shows up in the screen where you take things from chests, even when there's only one or even no items to take. It's very confusing because it looks like an object you can take.

Bug: The "won the level" screen is not centred, it's in top left of the screen.

Bug: Experience bar remains filled up after leveling so you can't tell how far into each level you are, xp-wise after the first.

Bug: If you go to next dungeon level when xp bar is filled and says "up" it will still say "up" on next dungeon level but the bar will be on half full. (And after you use the button to level up it will still say level 1)

Early Access Review
Posted: 10 January, 2015
Info for deaf and hard of hearing people:
Fully accessible. There doesn't seem to be spoken text as it is a very simple game. In fact, there's barely any text at all.

Cons:
  • The UI is not polished. Numbers overlap, are not aligned, and are even interrupted by a linebreak when they are too long. The words don't fare much better. (On widescreen monitors) the menus show pieces of themselves next to them.
  • The game takes a lot of CPU power, for what? Is it trying to calculate the prime numbers while you are playing the game?
  • The game is often 'Not Responding'. It seems to get progressively worse as time passes. Especially noticeable when you visit the store or check your inventory. It can get so bad that you have to wait over a minute before you finally leave the inventory. Trust me, I've typed this whole review while waiting for the game to respond again.
  • You can't see what you've looted until you check your inventory. And because of above issues... I suggest you go make a cup of tea in the meantime. Or coffee.
  • When using healing potions, you see your HP orb get fuller but its number doesn't update.
  • The store is flawed. You can switch between the Buy and Sell tabs repeatedly and keep selling your gear. Only when you've exited the store, your gear will have been marked as gone, but in the meantime you'll be rich! RICH!
  • Movement isn't fluid. Sometimes you'll even be 'stuck' as you are half on some other tile and need to move aside a bit. I'm not sure this should even be possible.
  • Combat is boring. You just bump into the monsters or click on them. I'm not entirely sure yet if the extra abilities do anything at all.
  • The menu button is distinguished as a pause button. Kind of weird when you realize this is a turn-based roguelike. But now you know!
  • Compared to other roguelikes of this style, this is way overpriced.
    I see the price has now changed from 13 to 3 euro, which is a big difference. It is still overpriced for its current state.

Pros:
  • Well, it is Early Access. And if the devs don't abandon this, it might have some potential. Though I wouldn't have put this game in EA at this stage yet. You're only going to get negative reviews for things that could've been fixed relatively easy enough.

Conclusion:
Definitely not worth any purchase at this stage. Many of the flaws in this game are so basic that I'm amazed they made this game public this soon. And because of the progressively worse lag, the game isn't even really playable at this point.

I'm not even sure the devs know what they're doing, seeing as they don't even have a sticky thread encouraging users to post bug reports. They're not exactly pro-active at a first glance.

Claustrophobia: The Downward Struggle is also in Early Access and is a lot more polished.
And if you want a roguelike that's fully fleshed out already, go for Dungeons of Dredmor instead.
Both will be more smooth and less disappointing. for a lower price. In fact, you can buy both and still be cheaper off.

Early Access Review
Posted: 9 February, 2015
I'm as big a sucker for roguelikes as you're likely to find, so any roguelike game starts out ahead of the game. Unfortunately, not even this advantage is enough to save Dungeon of Gain. Conceptually, it's a cookie cutter roguelike. There is nothing novel or different (in a positive way) about it. Given that the genre is getting a tad saturated, you'd expect new entries to try to bring something new to the picnic, but DoG just brings potato salad. Ok, fair enough - bein a fan of the genre, I'm satisfied with a well implemented "standard" roguelike, if novelty isn't on offer.

I am, for the most part, not a fan of Early Access. While there are a few shining exceptions, I find that for the most part, EA seems to be a way for developers to cash in on their game without finishing it, and with the increasing number of EA games either never leaving EA, or doing so by calling whatever they happen to currently have version 1.0, and then moving on to greener pastures, my willingness to cut games in EA slack is quickly diminishing. I am, however, willing to forgive a certain amount of sharp edges, placeholder graphic or just general unfinishedness.

I am not, however, willing to forgive a game in EA for being unplayable, or nearly so, due to broken implementation or design. And there is plenty of that in Dungeon of Gain. Worse still, even setting aside such brokeness, the underlying game mechanics exposed by the game leaves a lot to be desired - in its current state, the game just isn't very interesting. It's very barebones and simplistic, and while I supposed it might be possible that their development plan calls for them to first get the basics of moving around and fighting working before working on depth and more intricate mechanics, I am sceptical. Not just because it seems they're having such a hard time getting the basics to work, but also because there seems to be little in the way of underlaying structure on which to build such mechanics or achieve depth.

So, in conclusion, my recommendation is to stay away from Dungeon of Gain in its current Early Access incarnation completely, and should the game ever graduate from EA into a full release, to scrutinize the "completed" game carefully, to ensure it really has been completed, and not just had v1.0 slapped on the latest prototype and shoved out the door.

(Oh, and my limited playtime? I've tried playing the game multiple times before it got onto Steam, so my brief testing on Steam was mostly a matter of checking if anything had changed substantially between the last pre-Steam version and its Steam premiere incarnation.)
