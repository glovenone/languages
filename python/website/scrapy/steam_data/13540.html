About This Game

Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six makes its next-generation hardware debut in the most dramatic installment of the renowned first-person shooter franchise to date.

Rainbow operatives take to the chaotic streets of Las Vegas as an escalating terrorist siege in 'Sin City' threatens to take world terrorism to new, uncontrollable heights. The future of global security hangs in the balance as you battle to defend classic Vegas locations and environments like Fremont Street, the Strip, and casinos.

Experience Las Vegas like never before - through revolutionary next- generation technology as you work against the clock to keep one of the world's most recognizable cities from utter devastation.

  • New Leader, New Team - Team Rainbow ushers in a new breed of heroes. With a new team comes added responsibility. Tactics play a MAJOR role in your team's success, as each member comes equipped with his own skill set: Logan Keller, team leader and recon expert; Michael Walter, heavy weapons and demolition expert; and Jung Park, long range and electronics specialist.
  • Special Order Location - Experience a new level of squad-based realism as your teammates, Michael and Jung, offer you real-time tactical suggestions like busting through walls or hacking computers, all in a race against time to locate the heart of the terrorist cell.
  • Observe - Plan - Assault - Use tactical planning to maneuver through multiple-path Vegas environments, while utilizing high-tech equipment such as snake cams to strategically 'tag' terrorist targets to your teammates, prior to entering a room.
  • Real-Time Immersion - Prepare yourself for a new level of in-game immersion with real-time mission briefings, all in the midst of the most intense combat ever played.
  • Advanced Gameplay Animations - Motion-captured assault and recon techniques such as fast roping, rappelling, window entry, blind-cover fire, and more. All captured by an experienced Hollywood animation expert.
  • Next-Generation Artificial Intelligence - Next-generation technology brings a higher level of artificial intelligence to Rainbow Six. As you get past the grunts and advance closer to the center of the terrorist cell, encounter deadlier, more skilled enemies who work together as a group to counter every move you make.
  • Customizable Multiplayer Experience - Create your own multiplayer character that evolves as you play online, unlocking new equipment and achievements as you go.
  • Ubisoft Montreal Returns - Rainbow Six is back in the hands of Ubisoft's Montreal studio, creators of the award-winning and highly acclaimed Rainbow Six 3.

Rainbow Six Vegas is yet another part of Rainbow Six series, tactical-themed FPS. This time around there are no more planning and you control only two other squad member. Yet it's all good fun. It came out in 2006 and was made by Ubisoft. Also, it was made for X360 first, so it came into PC territory like a proud direct port, lacking slightly in options and not optimized very goodly. At least it got patched up since then.

It also came out for X360, PS3 and PSP. The first two should be similar, but PSP is different game. You play solo in that one. It doesn't interest me not even slightly, I am here to see squad members work along with me. You are supposedly saving those two squad members, being lone wolf.

Technically, well, yea. Port from Xbox 360. Used to be unoptimized, but I am sure that computers nowdays can easily just power through it. It still however rarely freezes for half-minute. Steam Overlay doesn't work either, have fun alt-tabbing.

Even worse, PC version did not support widescreen monitors at release! And while they patched widescreen in later, it still doesn't every wide resolution and even worse, the HUD is still designed for 4:3 monitor. So do some full-screen graphics. Like "to be continued" screen. Oh, yea, cliffhanger alert.
But I am pretty sure that there are fan patches to fix those.

Multiplayer servers were supposedly shutted down, but it actually still works, just requires UPlay account, at least now. Yep, new players will have to fancy around with email adress as nickname, from what I understood. Well, at least it works.

Community seems to be kinda there. One times I tried there were no servers, other times there were 5 of them, with players. Seems like people only play Cooperative modes only nowadays, you will not find any Competetive players vs players. Sadly, they all seems to be quite protective, as all the servers I encountered were password protected, so I didn't get to check out multiplayer in these few days.

And well, what can I say. It sure was made as a show off for Xbox 360, showing off itself with Unreal Engine 3, while trying to be accessiable to new players. Though, to be honest, I really dislike graphics in this part. That's because while they are cool on technical side, the art style is just awful. Or, rather, it feels like developers applied a really bad brown hue blur effect all over the screen. Music also isn't hot, being urgent but not very awesome. Shame that Rainbow Six main theme is gone.
Oh well, at least Vegas setting is kinda cool.

Also, yep, this Rainbow Six is now all about telling a good story and having fun. Not more planning before mission, head into action, main hero. Take on them with your regenerative health, until you get instakilled by sniper shot and realize that you can't just charge on even on Normal. Nope, it's still tactical, somewhat, sorry Doomguys.

It also got cover system implented, where you hold a button and go into third person mode, as Logan hides behind a wall. You are supposed to spend most of your time in this mode, but personally, I kept forgetting that I can do it and instead kept crouching behind corners, aiming and shooting from the first person mode. Ha. But hey, here is a cool feature that you can do a blind fire from behind cover. Enemies can do it too, also. Gotta snipe those hands.

And thankfully, you aren't alone either, for you can still command to your other teammates. Or more exactly single squad. Made of one explosive black guy and one haxor chinese guy, because yay Hollywood must-haves. No offence there, just stereotypical. Yep, just two people who you can't command separately, only to both at same time. To be honest, I am dissapointed that there are so little, I was expecting to get the second such squad and never recieved such. Rainbow Six Lite, gentlemen.

But thankfully, they still beat their Rainbow Six Lockdown rivals into jaws, as these two people actually have good enough AI to follow you fast and be reliable at clearing the rooms. And while levels are linear, you get few options on how to enter. For example, you pretty much always have two doors, you can tell them to bang and clear one set while going through another. Or you can also peek under door with snake-cam and call out two priority targets and just tell them to open door, they take out priority targets very fast and accurately while breaching. And I gotta say, I love hostage situations in this game, even if they are scripted.
Also, fallen teammates can be saved and made alive again, while you can't. Knowing this, you can guess how this team works. Answer: Like a horrible friends.

But yes, thanks to this small squad being reliable and easy to control, I like this game a lot. After all, the reason why I liked single-player tactical shooter is because of working together with friendly AI and making tactical choices and this one more or less provides it, while being accesable tojust launch and have fun. I think that I would take it over Rainbow Six Raven Six, for I don't care about 1-shot-kills so much and AI of teammates in that game acted too dumb. As well as I like ability to be able to order more or less good during gameplay.

But with promise that they bring back planning someday or let us play with more squads at the same time.

Still would rather take SWAT 4 though.
But oh well, this one is fun too.

Oh, and there is terrorist mode. In single-player you play solo, so I wasn't so interested. It's quite hard and enemies will try to storm your positions just fine.

Oh, yea, and there is multiplayer. I didn't get to try it. I know that it has unlockables. But most of stuff you got to play in single-player is there too, easy to access.

But yep, pretty fun shooter where you get to order around to two squadmates and do some job. Shoootiiiiing!

Recommendation(s): Play Splinter Cell or Ghost Recon, run this game as an admin
Major Theme: An old yet reasonable console port with meat
Bought at Price: $5 - bought during Steam sale

Throwing "Tom Clancy's" on a title usually means that the game is going to have some meat, or say solid content to it. The only downside is that this means Ubisoft is the sole publisher/developer of the title; boy do they love console ports.

Rainbow Six is an anti-terrorism organization who tries to stop threats before they become the front page news. With this, you lead a team with 2 other squad mates to tackle challenges that take place in close quarter building environments and city streets.

By commanding your squad mates, you progress through the challenges in the cohesive story. The single player story is about 10 hoursâ worth of content, which allows you to get to know characters and game mechanics well. As with many other Tom Clancy games, there is always a sci-fi twist that doesn't seem "out of this world".

With your squad mates, you can clear rooms, rappel down the sides of buildings, and coordinate to take enemies out. If one of the squad mates manages to go down, the other can be ordered to revive them. In the more likely event that both squad mates go down, you revive them with a "hypo". While there is an "infinite" health system in place, you will still find yourself dying quite a bit.

Biggest issues out of the game? Let's start with checkpoints, checkpoints, checkpoints... The worst thing known to humanity continues and was a "feature" back in the day. Rainbow Six doesn't escape this, and it will make you rage. People still arguge about checkpoints somehow defeating "save cheating" (the "art" or plain intelegence to save before big battles). This is simply untrue due to the fact that consoles simply couldn't save that much data back then. It was a technical reason (and poor one) that has infected modern games.

The "blur" or what seems to be some horrible pain effect isn't good at all. Because it's a console port, the memory and cpu usage had to be kept down and this horrible awful effect does its job. It is also strangely not optimized that well, so systems on the edge of requirements will suffer frame issues when effect kicks in.

Multiplayer, while it does exist, requires an Uplay account, and then a small chance of someone playing multiplayer as well. There are no dedicated servers it seems, so a peer-2-peer system is in place. Only good thing that seems to come from multiplayer is the very precise customization options, it even surprised me in fact.

Using an older version of the Unreal engine, AI has issues following and getting into positions sometimes. Once you play enough, you will get used to it. I came in knowing that, and automatically accounted for it. Your experiences will vary in this regard with high certainty.

Keys are places in odd spots on your keyboard, and there's quite a few to remember. Left alt for instance changes squad engagement rules and then pressing and holding r shows the gun equipment radial menu. V, B, and N are for room entering commands which change based on engagement rule set.


Beyond these aspects, I had fun playing the title. It was unique, yet required some chasing to get it to work on Windows 7.

Rainbow Six Vegas was an effective and divisive reboot of the Rainbow Six series. It took the previously slow and tactical shooter series into a much more modern cover shooter direction. I say divisive because Rainbow Six Vegas caused a split in the fanbase between people who wanted a faster cover shooter and people who wanted a slower game where you plan your movements before executing.

Rainbow Six Vegas largely takes place in the titular city of Las Vegas. You play as "Logan" an operative for the titular "Rainbow" special forces team who enters the city to combat a terrorist threat. The storytelling is a bit muddled and you generally don't pay attention to it that much. It mostly serves to get you from point A to Point B and shoot some baddies.

What Rainbow Six Vegas is most well known for it's cover shooter gameplay. While it is faster than all of the previous Rainbow Six games it's noticeably slower than say, Call of Duty or Gears of War. The game is built around using your squadmates to move to different positions so you can flank or take enemies out from a distance. It's much more forgiving as well since it has regenerating health and the enemy AI is fairly dumb. Since you die very quickly the game doesn't devolve into run and gun but it still feels much slower than it should.

Besides the campaign, Rainbow Six Vegas also has multiplayer and Terrorist Hunt. The latter is where the game excels in my opinion. In Terrorist Hunt, you and a few friends can team up to take out AI enemies in a series of multiplayer maps. It's very enjoyable and replayable and is where the bulk of the time you spend playing the game will end up. Deathmatch is also available but good luck finding a game for it.

It's hard to really describe the original Rainbow Six Vegas without describing how much better it's sequel was. While I do recommend the game if you're a fan of modern shooters and desire something that requires slightly more thought behind it, the sequel comes generally more recommended.
