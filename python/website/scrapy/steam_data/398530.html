About This Game

Ghost Cleaner is a combination of arcade game and puzzle game. At first glance it is very straightforward, as you use a gun to destroy pink bricks. But the game requires you to use both strategy and tactics to finish every level. Try it once, and you'll be coming back for more!

  • Physics Based
  • Lots of variety across all the levels.
  • 100 levels to complete!
  • Low price for fun game that's easy to play.

Are you looking for a game that gives you a little Peggle mixed with some World of Goo, and more? Well then do I have the game for you. The name of the game is Ghost Cleaner and you will be tasked with conquering 101 levels of varying difficulty as you try to figure out the mastermind behind an evil plan.

Graphically this game uses a lot of light blue and pink, but that's to be expected as this is going to be the color of the objects (bricks and balls) that you're going to try to be breaking. I have to say that I like the fact that the background image changes with the area that you're entering. Here's a couple of the areas that you'll be venturing through, a hospital, a school, and a graveyard. Each level is well laid out where you feel that even the very cluttered levels look good.

As you might imagine from my description of the story I'm not 100% sure what was all going on, but the sheer number of levels and the fact that there was definitely some challenge to them kept me hooked. The reason why I compared this game to Peggle is because it's almost exactly Peggle with the difference being that the shooter is going to be different places during the levels, which creates its own set of challenges. All of the challenges I either loved or screamed at the computer because I had to actually take a strategy into the level.

I also mentioned World of Goo and the reason for this is that there are some levels where the balls or bricks are connected via strings and as you break those connections gravity takes over and it just reminds me a lot of creating the towers in World of Goo and hoping they don't fall over.

Here's the one real weakness of the game, the object of each level is to get rid of the pink blocks, each one equals a ghost, collect enough ghosts and you can use them to buy power up items. The problem is that you have no idea what each of the power ups does. You get the icon picture to work with and that's it. There's also the issue of having to click on the different selections which sometimes if you are slightly off you waste that power up on a shot that will probably not hit a damn thing. It would have been nice to switch to the power up and then back to the regular shot.

Each level has an out of three stars rating, and the way it works is the following. If you use any power ups to beat the level that's one star, if you played without power ups and left some blue on the screen that's two stars, if you clear the board that's three. Now for most of the game I didn't seem to have an issue with the amount of stars that I was being awarded until the very end where I had to go back to a previous level and beat it for three stars.

Here's the deal, this game isn't perfect, but there is something that kept me trying to defeat the levels until I got to the end, or should I say a potential end with a cliffhanger. The difficulty balance over 101 levels is quite tricky and I think it also depends on the type of player you have playing the game, so I wouldn't say there's a steady ramp up but there are a couple of puzzles that definitely stood in my way from advancing, some almost to the point of making me want to punch something. This game gets an 8.7 out of 10.

A stripped down version of Peggle. There is NO high scores for levels. So its literally "get the stars" and move on. Instead of special characters- you get special items. And guess what? The special items make it so you cant get perfect stars on the level. To get these items- you literally have to grind through levels over and over. That would be fine- if the gameplay didnt get boring. Plus there is no indicator of what the special items do! So you have to waste your money at least once on each item to find out what it is.

Another big complaint- the game seems a bit glitchy at times. There is the moments when you clear every peg for a level- but the game tells you that you didn't. The level will end- but you wont have perfect stars even though you should.

Game maker hasnt been active at all on the forums, or when I emailed about a few things. That's kind of stupid- considering the game hasnt been out too long. So it honestly seems like shovelware/abandonware.

If the game maker updates the game or actually responds to some stuff, I'll update the review accordingly.

I wanted to like this game. I'm a fan of similar games, such as the Peggle series. However, there's absolutely nothing new here. It's almost like they took Peggle, reskinned it, and hung the blocks by strings. That's the gist of it. You shoot a ball, hoping to eliminate all of the pink blocks before you run out of shots. Same as Peggle, except that Peggle had nice twists to the gameplay, whereas 20 some levels in, there's nothing new to keep my interest. The game also has a few game mechanics that are rather irritating. First, some of the blocks will reset if you hit them twice. That wouldn't be a problem, except that you have no clue which ones those are. Also, appearently progressing in the game is dependant on a star system. So after truding through 80 levels, just to finish the game, you are rewarded with a message that says you have to go back, and redo levels to get more stars. Hard to do, when in order to get all 3 stars, you have to break every brick in a level WITHOUT your special weapons. In some stages this is damn near impossible.
