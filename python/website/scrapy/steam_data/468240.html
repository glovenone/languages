Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to build something that you will enjoy playing.

We think that the best way to achieve that is by getting community involved in the development process.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Till December 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Depends on the feedback from the community, but here are the things we have in mind:

* Multiplayer
* Make as many elements as possible on the boat interactive
* More locations to sail in
* Better AI
* Implementation of racing rules
* Changing weather”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Sailing is working. Very basic controllers support.

Very naive implementation of AI. No multiplayer. No (real) racing rules.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We plan to increase the price as the game becomes more polished.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will be looking for your input on forum, reddit and twitter.

Also feel free to reach to us over the e-mail.”

About This Game

Virtual reality sailing racing game for HTC Vive.

Learn basics of sailing, compete in races and relax on your own sailing dinghy.

    • Processor: Intel i5-4590 equivalent or greater
    • Memory: 8 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GTX 970

Early Access Review
Posted: 4 June
I like sailing, and I love the idea of a VR sailing game. I mean, it could be an issue with motion sickness for some people, but it's a cool idea.

This is pre-alpha though. Don't expect anything else. But I can see it could form into something pretty cool! It needs lots of work first though.

Observations:
-Boats have no momentum and will turn on the spot. Makes tacking weird, where you don't need to gather speed before making a tack, nor does carrying speed seem to help.
-No way of knowing the direction of the wind, no gusts (dark patches on water, arrows over water, etc)
-I rammed someone while on Port and didn't need to do a 360/720 (BONUS!), this is a feature.
-The guy I rammed proceeded to push my boat underwater with his epic downwind power, like an asshat.
-No mainsheet/jibsheet, but fair enough for simplicities sake.
-Switch of hands with tiller doesnt work.
-Downwind/running is like 5 times faster than upwind, should be 0.2 to 2 depending on how much of a bathtub you're sailing.
-Reaching is slower than running.
-Hmm, actually, change the last 2 points for no apparent wind mechanic.

Regardless, I appreciate the free content, and wish you well on your development!

Early Access Review
Posted: 31 May
Please note: At the point of writing this, this is day one of early access of this game (and it shows).

The potential here is fantastic but a lot of work needs to be done. The premise is simple. You sail a dingy. You can race or sail in a relaxing manner. The mechanics work well if not overly realistic (water is rarely that smooth). However the simplisic graphics (and whales floating above the water) let the game down. Much better visuals would do a lot for the game (especially in relaxing mode).

If the game remains "as is" then it's great proof of concept idea. VR sailing could work well with the right styles and enviroments. If however the devs keep working on the game then you've set up some intersting foundations for what could be a very interesting VR game .
