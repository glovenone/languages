About This Game

Project description


Wild Island Quest is a visual novel about the adventures of the teenagers on a dangerous island.
You can go to the Wild Island with the main characters and delve into a deadly adventure. Take one or a few friends with You, but keep in mind that all characters have their strengths and weaknesses, so the end of Your journey will depend on Your choice. Preparing for the trip, thoroughly plan your actions, take only what You really need. Any detail can be vital.
Enjoy the Wild Island's nature and beware its inhabitants!

Main features

  • Non-linear plot
  • Different endings
  • Possibility to travel alone or with friends
  • Every character has their strengths and weaknesses, and can give benefits or be a burden.
  • Unique picture to every location

Recommended IF YOU GET IT FREE. Don't pay money for this! There are plenty of giveaways!

In Wild Island Quest, you play as a boy who lives on a floating island with his friends, whom each have different skills. In the game, another island has collided with your island, and when your dog runs onto the new island, you chase after him and get lost in the jungle. Depending on the choices you make, you will get different endings.

Pros
+Multiple endings based on choices - This adds some extra replayability to the game.
+Soundtrack - I enjoyed the music in this game, even if there wasn't very much of it.
+Concept - The concept was good, though the execution was poor.

Cons
-Too short - It will take you a maximum of 15 minutes to beat this game for the first time. Any playthroughs afterwards will be a breeze since you'll already know the correct choices to progress.
-Graphically terrible - Just a glance at this game will show you that very little of the budget was spent on how the game would look. The humans are frankly terrifying and the rest is mediocre at best.
-Poor storytelling - Some parts of the plot seem either very forced or simply make no sense. For example, in the very beginning of the game, you see your dog in your yard, but by the time you get to the place where you will watch the island, he's somehow raced all the way over there without you noticing and randomly jumps onto Wild Island for seemingly no reason. The ening makes even less sense, but I'll let you see for yourself.
-Repetitive - Past a certain point, it becomes very repetitive trying to get all the achievements and trying out the different options. You have to click through the same dialogue over and over again just for some slight variation on what happened in your previous playthrough. Most of the time, there are a few "wrong" choices that lead to death and one "right" choice that lets you progress. Going with different friends or using different items had very little impact on the game in the long run.

If you got this game in a giveaway, then yeah, go ahead and play it. The game's short and only takes about 10 minutes to beat, and it's not horrible. But don't consider spending even a penny on this, it's just not worth the $2. Go buy a better game instead.

My full review can be found here: http://www.the-backlog.net/wild-island-quest-review/

Where do I even begin with this game... Wild Island Quest follows the story of a group of guys who are going from their home island (Mech-City Island) to Wild Island while it's on a rare pass-by. While you can go it alone, you can also gather up your friends to go with you. What choices you make determine how many of your friends you can recruit (I did the path with recruiting Conan only).

Interesting idea, right? That's what got me initially interested. Unfortunately, everything goes wrong in Wild Island Quest quite fast. If you're able to even follow the story, you'll find a a lot of English mistakes in terms of grammar and odd word choices. This pretty much tells you that this was translated with a Russian to English dictionary and in quite a few places it seemed they didn't quite know which word to use so they just picked a random one. Of course, this "random one" makes very little sense to a native English speaker, and in several cases I actually had to go search up words that were used to try and figure out what word/meaning they were going for.

The horrendous English coupled with the pretty poor graphics (the models just look awful) make Wild Island Quest fall very short of what it could have been. As it stands, unless some drastic improvements are made, this game gets a gigantic pass. There are better visual novels/choose your own adventures out there. Oh and no, there isn't really a "combat system". You just choose if you were lucky or unlucky in an outcome.

How far would you go to save your pup?

TL;DR:

A short yet charming indie visual novel with different endings depending on what options you choose. The achievements are exceptionally easy, which should just take under an hour to complete. The story itself isn't particularly memorable, but has an intresting concept. Despite this the price isn't worth it, not even for the achievements or card drops. Playing the game will give you 3/5 card drops.

Review:

"You're approaching the edge of the island as the "Wild Island" floats closer, now only a jump away. Your friends beckon you to come over, before the opportunity is stolen from you. Taking a deep breath, you jump -"
Suddenly you wake up, and here is where the novel starts. Today is finally the day you will be able to see explore the Wild Island, a dream you've had for years now. But first, you must prepare yourself. This is where the first options are given to you, who and what you take etc. After playing through the game three times, it doesn't really matter who you take with you, as the ending is usually the same: You either complete your objective or die in the process. The novel however does have checkpoints, so if you die you won't have to begin again from the start. The most important thing that matters is your Luck, which is rolled with a dice at the very begining off the game. Fortunately you can re-roll if you're not satisfied with the score. This determines if the choice you chose will succeed or fail, for example lighting a fire or combat.

Something you'll notice right off the bat is a lot of translation errors, as the story was orginally in russian. However this doesn't take away from the overall feeling of the game. Graphically it looks decent, although there was some scenes where a stock photo was used as the background, which does take away from the immersion of the game. The audio is quite pleasing, and can be relaxing at times when accompanied by naturistic sounds.

Overall this game could have been a great VN, if more effort was put in to the overall story. Apart from this everything is practically fine, but if you're looking for a VN with beautiful graphics/art and great audio, this VN isn't for you.
