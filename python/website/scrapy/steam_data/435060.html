"Ball And Roll" by GameLooper is on the App Store and Google Play

But don’t worry you will master.

27 May

Sneak Into by GameLooper is now on iOS and tvOS

Another addictive game created by using GameLooper is now available to download.

Sneak Into is a calm game but it needs good reflexes. Design is minimalistic and it can be played with one finger. Purpose of the game is to advance into square rooms, quickly decide which way to turn and correctly time it by a swipe of a finger and go into another room. There is only one chance to make a move in each room, so be precise. When you finish all 100 levels, you can play the endless mode.

Here is a short video:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xjufo0fwt8s

For the first time ever, we exported a game and published it to Apple TV. If things go well, you may be able to export your very own games to Apple TV as well as iOS and Android phones and tablets. Stay tuned.

Now, go to the App Store and download Sneak Into and let us know if you liked it.

PS: Android version is coming soon...

About This Software

***GameLooper is a complete platform for easy game development***
With GameLooper desktop app, you can create any kind of 2D mobile games by using drag & drop user interface. Then you can simulate your games with GameLooper mobile app.

***Share your game prototypes with friends***
You can share your GameLooper games with friends before they are ready to be published on the Apple App Store and Google Play Store. Anyone who has GameLooper installed on their device can play your games and give feedback when you share your GameLooper games. This is a great opportunity to show your game progress to your friends.

***We're looking for your GameLooper games to make them featured***
Just create your games with GameLooper desktop app and request publish through GameLooper mobile app. Our Q&A Team will be contacting you shortly. Then we will prepare your games for the Apple App Store and Google Play Store to publish.

Now, go to www.gamelooper.com and download GameLooper mobile app for your Android devices to start creating awesome games!

***Attention!***
GameLooper requires internet connection to work properly. Don't forget to turn on your Wi-Fi on your mobile device.

Not Recommended
0.4 hrs on record
Posted: 22 January
Keep in mind before reading my review, I've used code to make games previously and this tool might be better suited for beginners rather than more professional users.

First off, you are very limited to what file formats you can use. You need to use png for sprites, which I don't really mind, but all audio files are limited to using mp3. Most game engines use ogg for music due to its higher quality with higher compression and sound effects in wav to make it load faster. Just having mp3 isn't a good thing.

To clarify what I mean here is that music is very often in ogg due to it being able to compress below 128 kb/s without loosing much quality while mp3 haves issues going below 256 kb/s without loosing anything in quality. This can specially come in handy with mobile games, where you have limited storage and bandwidth and want to make your game as small as possible.

It's also really clear that this engine isn't made to use with larger projects. All sprites, music and other files you are working with needs to be dumped in the root of the project folder. You can't have one folder for sprites, one for music etc. If you start to get working with larger projects, I can imagine it getting really messy.

There's also no ui customization at all. I felt some UI elements were too small and I wanted to re-size it, so I can for example see the full names in the sidebar, but there's no such thing, which is annoying.

This last thing is prob just me and can be good or bad depending on the user, but I felt that the editor is way too basic. There's pretty much no buttons to press or customize, no compile options etc. Again, this might be just me, since it might be good for beginners to not have a lot of the more advanced options to make you confused.

So in conclusion, even though I couldn't even make a simple game in it and not having any way to code, this engine was really not for me, but might be good if you are just starting game development.

I graduated with a Degree in Game Design a few years back, but now program software. As a hobby, I am teaching kids how to make games, using GameMaker and hopefully advancing to UDK/Unity. This is awesome, very simple to use and understand. Easy to to teach kids since you can literally create a game in under an hour. Not to mention, they can play it on their phones. As a hobbyist, and even when I was getting my degree, this would have been a God send for quick prototyping. There was a review I read from someone who said this was not for an experienced developer. I don't know what experience they have, but it sure is nice to be able to hand someone your phone during a pitch and say "... and here is something we threw together to show how 'x' mechanic will work. Now will you front the money for this?" Or when you are brainstorming and want to try something out. Don't get me wrong, as we move away from the flappy-bird hype, this will not produce AAA games. But, one of the first lessons I learned in college was, sometimes the best creations come from having limited resources. For anyone interested in learning game design or any designers looking for a quick prototyper this definitely looks promising.
