19 April

ASCII Attack update improves Survival Mode play

The first major update for ASCII Attack adds some improvements to the Survival Mode.

In Survival Mode, you must survive 30 waves of invincible enemies for 30 seconds each wave. You earn points by retrieving launch codes, shooting enemy bullets, and, well, just surviving. It is a hard core mode and if you die once, you need to restart from the first wave.

The game now has a Restart menu selection if you die so you no longer need to return to the Main Menu to give Survival Mode another shot. The game also records your high score after each successful wave, so you no longer need to finish all 30 waves to record a high score. Of course, that is the true measure of surviving and the best way to rack up the highest score.

So get out there and collect those launch codes for the most points and outlast the Dot Matrix ASCII Army!

About This Game

A Cold War super computer has gone rogue and is printing launch codes for a first strike. There is only one way to prevent nuclear Armageddon; enter the dot matrix, collect the codes, and defeat the ASCII army.

ASCII Attack is a retro style indie action title inspired by arcade games of yesteryear. It is a skill based game with a unique set of controls and a new take on classic arcade action.

  • Take the fight to the enemy and battle on 25 levels of 80s era dot matrix printer paper.
  • Multiple modes and enemies provide extended gameplay challenges.
  • Peace out and live through waves of invincible enemies in Survival mode.
  • Embrace the fight and unleash the power of the ASCII @om bomb.
  • Face the ultimate challenge in the ASCII Kicker mode.
  • Full Steam Controller and 360 Gamepad support; perfect for Big Picture.

This game is a blatant low-budget rip-off of Namco\'s \"Assault\" arcade game. And I\'m totally okay with that. \"Assault\" was incredibly fun, and so is this.
This game\'s control scheme is only \"unconventional\" if you\'ve never played an arcade twin-stick tank game. So, anybody who spent any time in an arcade between 1980 and 1995 will probably find that their muscle memory understands the control scheme before their conscious mind does.
All that\'s really missing from this game is the \"aerial launch pad\" mechanic from Assault, which was both a large-scale attack and a limited-range map peek. Nudge nudge, Daisy Maze.
\"Detailed graphics\" are also missing, but that\'s a testament to this type of game - the fundamental gameplay is more than enjoyable on its own merits, regardless of what arbitrary visual skin is draped over it. I feel that that\'s the true essence of \"retro\" games - the game-in-itself should be fun because of its basic structure, whether it\'s dressed in chunky pixels, 16-bit Mode-7, or HD DX12 photorealism. Or simulated ASCII text, whatever. The visuals represent the underlying game, and that\'s all they really need to do, if the game itself is solid.

I've spent around an hour with ASCII Attack so far but wanted to get a review out for other potentially interested gamers.

This game has mechanics that are heavily influenced by an old school arcade game called Assault (see https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fxdh1hRqK2k). In that arcade game you had two sticks which controlled each side of the tank individually, requiring some amount of dexterity and learning on the part of the gamer to be successful at it.

Thats the kind of game ASCII Attack is. There isn't any hand holding, there are a few screens explaining how to move in the game, no tutorial, no ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥t, you just jump in and get owned because you aren't automatically amazing at a control mechanic you don't use every day (like WASD or something like that).

I think that's what I like about this game. My dexterity is challenged more than most games do, because I have to think about the way my tank moves and how I move it more than other games do. I find myself getting better as time goes on, so I think higher skill can be accomplished over time. I tried the keyboard layout too, which was really hard to get used to, then all of the sudden it clicked and my brain was processing it.

I like the whimsical ASCII art approach to the game. It's bare bones to be certain, but cute in its own way. I'm old enough to remember old printer paper like this and what a pain it was to tear the edges off without ruining my homework. ASCII art used to be one of the only ways to do simple art for BBS's back in the day (yeah I'm old) and its fun to see it animate in this game with the moving tank, turrets and explosions.

The music is pretty good, if a bit repetitious. That's a mark of an old school arcade game too, I guess. It's cool to see music DLC instead of hidden away levels though.

Overall I think ASCII Attack is a pretty fun little indie game, it can be a bit tough, but I like harder games and I like the lack of tutorial and hand holding, I'm so tired of that. Priced at under $3.00 is great too, my Starbucks that came and went this morning cost more than this game did.
