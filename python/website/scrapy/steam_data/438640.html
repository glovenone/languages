About This Game

The Force is strong with this one… The No. 1 LEGO® videogame franchise triumphantly returns with a fun-filled, humorous journey through the new Star Wars adventure. Play as all of the characters from the movie, including Rey, Finn, Poe Dameron, Han Solo, Chewbacca, C-3PO, and BB-8, as well as Kylo Ren, General Hux and Captain Phasma.

In LEGO® Star Wars™: The Force Awakens™, relive the epic action from the blockbuster film in way that only LEGO can offer, featuring brand new story content from the Star Wars Universe that explores the time between Star Wars: Return of the Jedi and Star Wars: The Force Awakens.

LEGO Star Wars: The Force Awakens introduces new gameplay mechanics, including the enhanced Multi-Builds system, where players can choose from multiple building options to advance the game, or engage in intense new Blaster Battles for the first time, utilizing surrounding environments to drive back the First Order."

9/10

Lego Star Wars: The Force Awakens is the best Lego game since Lego Marvel Superheroes.

PROS:

The missions are varied and feature several scenarios not in the film, fleshing out some of the story missing from the source material, including more Poe action.

The ship battles were reminiscent of Star Wars Rogue Squadron for the N64 to the point that I kept expecting the inevitable Hoth battle (not included, but the game does begin with a Battle of Endor prologue). The flying is very fluid and blasting enemy ships out of the air is, well, a blast.

The usual Lego humor was also scattered throughout gameplay and most of the Red Brick silly stuff this time around was fun enough not to be an annoying distraction and worth keeping in game, in particular the disco lightsaber effects.

The vocal talent was also excellent. Unlike other Lego movie adaptations, it didn’t sound like they just ported the soundtrack into the game but actually got most of the actors into the studio to record dialogue. It was very fluid and not at all jarring, unlike the hissing soundtrack of Lego Marvel Avengers.

CONS:

Like almost every Lego game that came before, LST:TFA is chock full of bugs. During the 32 hours of my personal playtime, I experienced four crashes (including one that locked my system so badly I had to reboot). Fortunately, most of the bugs and glitches are the typical ones found in Lego games, specifically the collectibles arrow (unlocked with a Red Brick) not showing up for several minikits, some collectible arrows remained even though the items they pointed to were already collected and the map waypoint wouldn't work properly all the time. Some world missions also glitched and wouldn’t complete properly (or when they did complete, would repeat the concluding character dialogue twice). Luckily, the broken missions seemed to correct themselves upon the next play session.

One of the more hyped newer mechanics is multiple builds from the same bricks. This may have given one the impression that he has choices on how to complete a level. However, instead of a choose-your-own-adventure style of play, most of the time both builds have to be completed in order to complete the puzzle. In other words, build the Lego object, perform the action, destroy the object, build the other object with the same bricks, complete the other action and thus solve the puzzle. In actual gameplay, this is more annoying than innovative because many times one has to build the objects in the proper order, otherwise he has to start over with the correct first build.

Navigating the map system is also cumbersome, requiring multiple button pushes to get to the next location. There are also interminable delays loading new zones, not likely caused by one’s system (in my case, an 8-core processor, 32 Gb RAM, GTX 970 video and all updated drivers). I’m surprised my Xbox controller doesn’t have bullet holes from mashing the A and Y buttons in an attempt to skip the loading screens.

Characters are now unlocked in two ways: the usual completing levels (and purchasing them later with studs) and the new carbonite method wherein one has to find 35 hidden frozen minifigs all over the galaxy before taking them back to the Resistance base to thaw them out for play. I would suggest thawing them as they are discovered rather than doing what I did, which was to wait until the end of the game to thaw all 35 in a row, because each thaw features animation that cannot be skipped. The real Han Solo spent less time in carbonite than I did thawing out his Lego counterparts.

FINAL SUMMARY:

This game is worth playing. It features all the old familiar Lego action while including fresh play game mechanics (fresh for a Lego game at any rate). The bugs are annoying but not game-breaking and the flying is a lot of fun.
