About This Game

Enter the futuristic year of 1984, an age where ducks run wild in a frantic battle for glory. Win over the crowd and gain a following by blasting your feathered friends with Shotguns, Net Guns, Mind Control Rays, Saxophones, Magnet Guns, and pretty much anything else a duck could use as a weapon. One hit and you're roasted. This is DUCK GAME. Don't blink.

  • 2 to 4 PLAYERS in either Local Multiplayer Couch Combat, or NEW Online Play!
  • Single Player challenge mode
  • Easy to pick up, emphasizing strategy over twitch reaction
  • 50+ Levels, 50+ Weapons
  • Level Editor

Alright, so, I've played this game for a little over a year now. And it has yet to get old for me.
I don't typically spend much time with games that are pretty much centered around online multiplayer and nothing else, but dammit if Duck Game doesn't suck you in with its quirky charms.
Honestly, a combination of it featuring on Steam Rolled and the knowledge that it has a dedicated quack button were really all I needed to by this: I'm a sucker for weird games (see: Goat Simulator), but this one kept me around with its party-game vibe, ridiculous weapons, and madcap energy. More often than not, I win or lose by sheer dumb luck. And it's perfect. While there are a lot of ways to exploit the mechanics of the game to give yourself a competitive edge (took me way too long to figure out that angled air shooting, to be honest), in the end, it's not necessary. This is a game that champions stupid and fun. You have nothing to gain and everything to lose by using it as another outlet for putting n00bs in their place. And there ain't a damn thing wrong with that. Just cut loose and quack with the rest of us, yeah?

10/10. Would duck myself over with a roman candle again.

First of all, before I say anything about the gameplay, I have some advice:
MAKE SURE YOUR FRIENDS EITHER HAVE THIS GAME OR WOULD BE WILLING TO BUY IT. There are barely any multiplayer servers online. Normally, the wait wouldn't be too long, but the game will attempt to connect to full servers about 3/4 times. While the multiplayer isn't too bad, don't expect to be able to just join a game right away. It would be better to have friends to play with.

With that out of the way, I'll talk about the gameplay. When you're using a keyboard, you might not expect it to be great, since the controls are mainly designed for a controller, but it's quick and easy to get used to. One thing that's slightly annoying is that some buttons which are used for the same thing are perfect for one thing but awkward for the other. However, since the game is designed for a controller, I shouldn't complain. I've barely played the game with a controller, as for a while it wouldn't work, and once it did I was too used to the keyboard to switch (I mainly play other games with a keyboard anyway). However, from what I did play with a controller, I can tell that it's definitely easier to pick up at first, although I doubt the effects are long-term.

Controls aside, what is there to the game itself? Well, imagine a 2D competitive shooter game. Now add hammers, swords, net guns, guns that shoot a very slow block that will never stop moving and will also burn you alive instantly if you touch it, grappling arms, jetpacks, boots for jumping on walls, boots for jumping on opponents, spikes, space, death rays, an hats.

Oh, and, of course, ducks. Everyone is a duck.

Pros:
- Has many weapons and tools, each with their own specific uses, range, penetration etc.
- Many unlockable hats, plus support for custom hats and maps
- Fast-paced, addictive gameplay makes it easy to start a "quick game" and hard to stop
- Rocking chiptune soundtrack
- Dedicated pitchable quack button

Cons:
- Netcode is weird; Lag seems to favor players with worse ping. I've often realized I've been shot just BEFORE an opponent stepped into range.
- There's no tutorial, so to learn the ropes you just have to get your ♥♥♥♥ kicked.
- No bots. Arcade mode has single-player challenges which admittedly helps, but only utilizes a handful of items in very specific scenarios.
