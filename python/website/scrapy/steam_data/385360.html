About This Game

Super Trench Attack 2 is a survival turn-based squad game set in a fictional world war setting.

You are Ada Frost, a lieutenant of the blue army. You find yourself deep behind enemy lines, with the stolen black army plans in your hands, you can end the war. That is, if you and your squad can make it home alive!

Of course the Black army won't let you escape that easily, They have dispatched their top general the evil Helmut, to stop you in your tracks. Fight through the desert sands, traverse the icy peaks, and battle through the muddy trenches, get those plans to H.Q before the evil general catches you!

Game Features
- Tense survival gameplay,
- Procedural generated levels, and missions
- Over 2000 random events, no play through is the same
- Play blackjack and hunt for food
- Four different C.O's to choose from
- Recruit four different character classes to your squad
- Upgrade your troops with the flexible 'weapon pack' system
- Goofball humor throughout
- Enemy commanders have different tactics and A.I
- Fight through snow, deserts, beaches, cities and forests

This title is the sequel to this steam game here.

Summary:
While Super Trench Attack 1 was just a regular top-down shooter with crazy graphics style, the second game is absolutely different in terms of gameplay. It's obvious that the developer got inspired by two great games - FTL: Faster than Light and Valkyria Chronicles.

Super Trench Attack 2 is a game where you proceed through randomly generated locations and battles, including special events like meeting mad men, bussinessmen, getting crew healed/damaged etc. The whole battle part is same like in mentioned Valkyria Chronicles - it's turn-based but all movement of specific characters are done directly by WASD controls.

As the previous game by this developer, graphic style is not serious but it's made in original silly way that you may like or hate. Check the screenshots and you'll see.

Positives:
- Unlockable content that motivates you to play again. More playable characters and upgrades.
- RPG level and item set system for every character.
- Unique silly graphics. Close to pixelart, not close to seriousness at all.
- Low system requirements.
- Low price in relation to the content.

Neutrals:
- Higher difficulty. But this is a standard for roguelike games.

Negatives:
- Repetitive battles. This guy goes there, shoots that guy. Another guy goes there, shoots any other guy...

Okay, I finally got around to making a somewhat decent look on the game after the early access period and man this review was way overdue. Basically, not much has changed from when it was in early access with the exception of additional content being put into the game, with the new Company Officers being as unlockables after making a couple of runs by earning points towards unlocking them with different setups to start your journey with. However, I will be mostly be talking about the differences between Super Trench Attack 2 and another game that is clearly based off from is the gameplay aspect of Valkyria Chronicles.

What traits does each game share?

-The turn-based strategy of selecting a unit and controlling them freely around the map with a limited amount of stamina based on unit type
-The setting is based around a world war setting
-The general difficulty is your small squad vs the world

Traits that's more prevelant in Valkyria Chronicles:

-Heavily story-driven with visual novel-like cutscenes (very good story if not a bit clichÃ©)
-Third-person mode makes it easier to spot enemies from a distance once your soldiers have line-of-sight
-Soldiers in your squad has personality in some description full with voice acting and unique character models
-Somewhat varying objectives throughout the campaign
-Multiple playthroughs are discouraging due to the imbalance in power between you and the enemy (mainly you can upgrade your squad and equipment to the point where you are straight up overpowered with orders breaking the difficulty even more)
-Again, the enemy is heavliy underpowered compared to you.You can go through the entire campaign without spending a single upgrade

Traits that's more prevelant in Super Trench Attack 2:

-Gameplay is clearly the focus of Super Trench Attack 2
-Very silly atmosphere compare to Valkyria Chronicles' serious atmosphere
-Rouge-like elements with randomized battlefields
-Difficulty is unforgiving so if you are not prepared for large scale fights, good luck
-Unit controls in a top-down view so it's more difficult to spot enemies across the level, but they do give you feedback on line-of-sight and when you've entered their firing range
-Little to no character personality
-Squad size is half the size of what you can deploy in Valkyria Chronicles
-no variable objecives with the only objective available is to eliminate the enemy team (Valkyria Chronicles mostly have you capture a specific control point, but they do mix it up from time to time)

Would I recommend Super Trench Attack 2? The answer is yes, especially if you love the gameplay of Valkyria Chronicles, but are also looking for a challange that Valkyria Chronicles fail to provide after your first playthrough.

Super Trench Attack 2 is a great game even at its recent launch into early access. It's different playstyle from the Original (Squad based-turn-based, rather then a top down Isometric Shooter) Gives a new playstyle which I've found to be quite enjoyable. The games small events are generally funny due to being out of proportion in a way, and the game still can prove a challenge if your not careful.

That being said, this game is a little repetitive due to it being in it's current state. Each Zone only has one map, and currently the only kind of mission is combat - Meaning it can begin to get repetitive, but im pretty sure new missions and probably maps will come out at some time sooner or later.

So yeah, from my experience, I'd give this game an 8/10 in it's current state.
Even if you don't really want this game, keep an eye on it - it has potential.
