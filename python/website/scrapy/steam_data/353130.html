About This Game

This is an MMO shooter where you ride dragons armed with missiles and lasers!

The plot of the game unfolds in an imaginary universe, where two highly advanced races, Neils and Atlans, fight against each other.

Their battle units are gigantic flying creatures controlled by adopiants, their riders.

The game offers the following:
- Multiple creatures with unique capabilities
- A wide selection of weapons
- Various combat modes
- Freedom and beauty of dragon flights
- Battles against players from all over the world!

Take your dragon into the sky and prove that you are the best!

As developers, we love our game with all our heart and will continuously develop it, ensuring updates every two weeks. Join our community to keep track!
It's gonna be fun!

So yeah, neat concept, nice that it's free to play but still a total waste of time. :/

As promises, I played the game again after this latest update. The developers did fix the bugs I experienced prior. However, it appears that the ship of opportunity has sailed for the developers.

To really kick this review into gear, let me start by saying this game that only offers PvP with no form of NPC or combative AI is a huge mistake. This game is international based, yet the entire 30+ minutes I flew in circles and I never ran across a single player on any map. This game is a barren wasteland of nothingness. Literally a total waste of time.

Next, this game is loaded with microtransactions. Now I get it, dev's have a right to earn funds from their product, I agree with the idea but they're doing it horribly wrong. For starters, this game offers NOTHING different, unique, or new in comparison to other PvP competitors. Sure, you can argue the basis of content that your fighting on dragon back but, there needs to actually "be" another player to fight. There is no one most of the time. So that argument is not only invalid but impractical. The prices for their products are incredibly high and there is no incentive to even contribute to this community of developers. Every single action has been a step on the wrong foot in the wrong direction by the dev's and this path may not be capable of recovering from.

Lastly, the final nail in the coffin of this review. You see, this is solely a PvP game. When a PvP game doesn't work, people will go to the thousands of other options out there. If a solely PvP game is gonna last, it needs to be good from the start or have some super stubborn Dev's who are willing to take a net loss early on. So, lets compare this game to another highly celebrated PvP game:

* Team Fortress 2 for the longest time was a pretty much just a PvP based game before the release of Man vs Machine. the game offered loads of different maps and combat strategies with a range of diversity FROM THE START. All characters and maps were unlocked from the very beginning.
By locking out all of the dragons and map types for a game and group of developers that have NO reputation what so ever the developers have completely locked out any possible redeming features. I'd try to get my profile to level 3 to try out other maps, but no one plays. If I can't play against anyone, I can't level. So definitely no redemption here.

As I said, neat concept and there are a load of opportunities with this idea, but at the end of the day this product was so buggy and incomplete with sooooo little content it's turned out to be a total waste of effort and time. I see there are a lot of people who wrote positive reviews, but when you take the time to read the reviews there is nothing sufficient stating why the game is good. People by nature like to positively rate something if has an idea they like associate with the material. In this case, dragons, flying, pvp, laser guns. All popular topics fused into one unique, though incomplete concept. Thing is though, pretty much the majority of all of these positive reviews have less than an hour of play and I doubt most people take time to spend initial gameplay to analyze and study the game before dedicating time to playing.

What this says is the idea has merit, people "want" something like this, but Dev's, you gotta fix your micro transations and a LOT more content and fast.

Best of luck, over and out.

Summary:
I've been playing this game for awhile and I personally enjoy it. Most of the bugs are UI/lobby related and the core game-play works well enough for a free game. Sure, the recent update added micro-transactions, but it's VERY far from a pay to win game. After having it be 100% free for awhile, I didn't mind pitching in $8 for a new dragon to support the devs.

My advice: Play this game with minimum expectations and just play it for awhile. Sure you'll die a lot at first, but it becomes enjoyable once you eventually learn how to move and shoot. Overall I give this a 6.5/10. It's a fun game, just that the bugs need to be worked out before I'd give it an 8-9/10.

Pros:
- Dragons with FRICKIN' LAZURRS strapped to their back!
- Decently balanced game-play. Each dragon class is the same for everyone, each weapon type damage the same as everyone. Doesn't matter what level you are. If you screw up, you die just as easily as everyone else. Although that's not to say there isn't room for improvement.
- "Paid dragons" are obtainable with normal game-play, and don't give an large, unfair, advantage. I bought one of the dragons and it's stats barely differ from it's default counterpart. Mostly cosmetic changes with a new ability, but still relies mainly on player skill. Advantage is marginal at best.
- Game-play is quick, fun, and straight-forward for the most part. This is the kind of game you play if you want a break and want to shoot down some dragons.

Neutral:
- Graphics are okay, but sub-par. Sound design is rather simple. There's definitely room for improvement.
- Learning curve ranges from moderate to hard. This is in-part due to being in the same room as experienced players. You will die a lot before you get a feel for how to move and shoot in this game. Then again, that's the same for most games.

Cons:
- Bugs. Bugs everywhere. Ranges from spawning outside the insta-death fence, to crashing when trying to load certain maps, to errors when unlocking dragons. The UI feels really clunky. Features like room/lobby info are missing, there's no chat history, and dragon selection in-game sometimes glitches. Just to name a few.
- No option to chose a room. Can't even know how many people are in a specific game mode without entering it to check. It's just "choose a game mode and hope for the best."
- No team auto-balance. It just places people in each team in order of when they first joined the room. This sometimes leads to all the high-level players being on one team, and low-levels on the other.
- Not a whole bunch of people play. Depending on what time you're on, all the game modes can be empty. Granted, people do still play and there are occasionally have full rooms.
