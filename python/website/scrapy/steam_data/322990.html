About This Game

Discover the secret behind this strange murder by searching the many detailed and varied backgrounds: visit the Octurni manor and its secret hideout, and escape from a high-security laboratory…

Your double agent role will help you get to the truth: you’ll carry out the investigation as a cop, by collecting clues about the victim's death and questioning the suspects, and you'll finally unveil the mystery surrounding this murder thanks to your talents as a thief.

Search over 35 unique locations to find the elements that will help you make progress in your investigation and the game. Solving the many available mini-games will also open up new paths of inquiry. Your determination will help you complete the numerous hidden object scenes.

Key Features:

  • One heroine, two identities!
  • A thrilling universe where sensuality and justice go hand in hand.
  • Stunning, highly detailed graphics.
  • More than 40 places to discover, 29 hidden object scenes and 26 mini-games to test your patience and reasoning.
  • Three levels of difficulty.
  • Captivating and engaging music.
  • Shed some light on your past!

This is an okay HOG, the graphics are pretty decent for the genre and this helps make the HOG scenes much more fun to play that some others, although there is a zoom function provided which probably gives the hint that some items are very small on the screen, although that isn't overused - and most items are hidden in reasonably logical positions, rather than scattered around randomly in impossible or unlikely spots.

The point and click segments and the puzzles are okay, so overall as a game it flows fairly well, and it is usually obvious what you are looking for and what you need to do to progress. One issue I did find was sometimes the hint system fails you and just has you shuffling from room to room without actually helping you work out the next step, but usually it does work correctly, but it did lead me to use a walkthrough in one section where I couldn't work out what to do and the hint system broke down in this way.

In terms of the story and characters, the story starts out okay but does get a bit silly - it seems very much aimed to be like a B-movie, but some of the developments at later stages get pretty weird and out of place compared to the tone for the first half of the game. While Sophia herself is actually a little interesting as a character, most of the rest of the game is populated by very one dimensional cliches - the businesslike police boss, the rich playboy, etc.

Overall then a decent HOG with a few flaws, but also a bit different from the standard for the genre, with a decent if somewhat strange storyline.

I hate when such things happen in a game!

First I have to note this game is a hidden object game title, it's about a police officer who is living a double existence as a ruthless costumed and masked thief. Well, that's in general most of the story that is to this game.

As player you can just be glad and happy it has no achievements, since this game is - well not exactly bad, but it also isn't a very good game of the genre - there are some riddles and puzzles that can give you quite the headache. I like hard puzzles, I like games where all isn't quite obvious - but I also like a barrier-free gaming experience. This isn't given all the time on that game.

The game is missing a lot of stuff, professional voice acting is non existent and the story is very rudimentary developed and highly illogical at times...

Pro:
  • Good to excelent graphics.
  • Nice and stylish soundtrack.
  • Some inovative riddles.
Con:
  • The story isn't logical and sometimes it's outright a mess.
  • The game is sometimes unclear about the directions and steps required to solve a given riddle, partially this is frustrating and you might give it to the overburdened scenes.
  • No Voiceacting.
  • Sometimes too restrictive in the placing of objects, which also might get on your nerves if you have to retry placing something half a dozen times...
  • Far too expensive.

Not Recommended

Given some of the graphics in this game are outright excelent, especially some of the scenes for the hidden objects, the game in itself is a lot of frustration and the story itself is cheesy - if you want to call it this way. The price is also a big minus and if it hasn't been in a bundle I probably wouldn't have bothered. You can call yourself lucky this game has no achievements, you would be forced to run through a game that's mostly time and money robbery - oh... That reminds me on the Title... Black Viper is a thief, isn't she? At least she wasn't able to steal my heart.

Short version: 65%
Black Viper is a below-average hidden object game with a cheesy, ludicrous story about an ultramodern female Diabolik knock-off.

Long version:
Black Viper: Sophia’s Fate was developed by a jack-of-all-trades Italian independent group. Who really started at the roots, because this game feels like something from 2006: no voice acting, cut-scenes consisting of character portraits talking, and archaic hidden object mechanics.
Also, the hint function is broken and tends to lead the player around in circles (it works in the HO scenes though).

Is the story good at least? No, it’s astonishingly bad, a mixture of low-budget spy movies and a female version of Danger: Diabolik. On the plus side, we have everything: a secret Egyptian sect plotting world domination, technical-looking gadgets everywhere (including roof-mounted laser cannons on the police station!), a predictable plot, clichéd characters, and even some horrible dialogues.
But it’s so incredibly terrible it’s hilarious; Black Viper is a prime example of the “so bad it is good” category. Thankfully the game is short, so if you ever need some cheesy spy story to laugh at, get it.
