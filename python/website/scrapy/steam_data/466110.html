Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Early access will be used to gain feedback help polish and refine the game to prepare for the official release. It will also be used to add additional content such as characters, stages and game modes in which the funds gained from early access will go the the production of the additional content.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“About 8 months”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Additional content such as characters, stages modes will be added as development of the game progresses. The game will also be more polished in the official release by removing any bugs, exploits and balancing the game. One of the big features I plan to add to the game is the Cinematic Story mode. In this mode you will see the retelling of the historic battles between the Shaolin and Wutang clans through in game cinematic cut scenes.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is in the "early beta" stage, and currently includes 14 fully playable characters, 12 stages, arcade mode, training mode and vs mode for cpu vs cpu, 1p vs 2p local and 1p vs cpu.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, I intend to raise the price of the game as I add additional content and features.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The fans and consumers will provide valuable feeback that will be used to help polish and refine the game. I also plan to have polls so that the fans will be able to vote on up coming characters and content.”

About This Game



Shaolin vs Wutang is a 1 on 1 indie fighting game featuring motion captured animations, hand to hand and weapon combat, great music and inspired graphics.

No flashy over the top effects, just combat influenced by classic Kung Fu films. The game features authentic fighting sounds from films and a licensed soundtrack with great music from classic kung fu movies.

Shaolin vs Wutang is a real love letter to classic 70's and 80's Kung Fu cinema. This is a indie passion project that was made by one person who has tremendous passion for, and knowledge of Kung Fu films. It is not my intention to provide the same experience as big budget fighting games. But rather I hope to provide players with a satisfying gaming experience blended with nostalgia and reminiscence of the great Kung Fu films.

It is being released in early access so i can get valuable support and feedback in order to help the game reach its full potential.

With the help of the community we can create the ultimate Kung fu game!!

Current Features Include

  • Hand to hand and weapon combat
  • Dynamic blocking animations
  • Licensed Kung fu soundtrack featuring music from classic films
  • 14 Inspired Characters
  • 14 Stages
  • Unique moves and combos for every fighter
  • Single Player & Local Co-op

Features I hope to add in the future

  • Enhanced multiplayer functionality
  • Additional Characters and Stages
  • Cinematic story mode (retelling of the history of the Shaolin and Wutang clans through in game cinematic cut scenes)

Early Access Review
Posted: 6 July
From the Golden Harvest fight intro, to the represented styles, even the music, Shaolin vs Wu Tang definitely has good flavour.

Gameplay feels a little stiff at first, similar to that of other indy 1v1 kungfuey games. It's not fluid like most of the top tier fighters like mk, sf or doa, but this game is still in development, so I will allow for some wiggle room.

Sound is top tier for those looking to play your fav ol' school kung fu flick. The only thing that would make it better is if they hit up the RZA to do a DLC soundtrack.

The fighter models look fairly simple, but any wuxia film fan will recognize the faces of each style-represented model. Among the few include Jackie, Gordon, Bruce and some older school faves I won't spoil.

The game is quite fun to play...I had a few things to pick up around the house, but insisted on running through the game with Lotus Palm after losing horribly as my boy reppin' the Shaolin long fist. I'd like to see a custom fighter option in the full release as the character roster is only a handfull at this time.

Some costume swaps would be cool, in addition to some more faves:

- Hung Gar Donnie Yen
- Tai Chi via Jet Li (Twin warriors alt Tai Chi master)
- More from the 5 Deadly Venoms
- Perhaps a represented Michelle Yeoh
- DAVID LO PAN (Big Trouble)

I will keep checking for updates...but any fan of kungfu flicks and fighters should get some fun out of this.
For a pre-release however, I'd like to see the price drop a little.

-Flu

Early Access Review
Posted: 10 May
If you are a fan of martial arts films, you are really going to enjoy Shaolin vs Wutang.

A few minutes into this game and I was hooked. I couldn't believe that for early access there is so much more accomplished here than in Kings of Kung Fu. The dev definitely improved his skill since then and it shows.

The visuals are absolutely beautiful. Some of the stages just blew me away with the detail that during the fights I found myself focusing more on the environment than the actual fight.
Adding to the awesome experience, I was really impressed with the music and sound fx. It just adds that much more to the experience making it feel like you are taking part in a 1970's Kung Fu film.

I'm in total shock that this is all done by one dev. I have to take my hat off to him. This is super impressive work.

Now being an early access game I did expect to find some issues in the game. I noticed graphical glitches very rarely appearing throughout the game (sometimes at random or sometimes when changing the graphics settings), I also noticed character clipping when they were too close to each other, and an imbalanced AI fighting system. There were a few other bugs but nothing serious.

Closing this up, all I can say is this game is definitely worth the $$$ and is an experience you should not miss. The dev put a lot of heart into it and as an early access game it's great to be a part of it to watch it get better with the patches and updates.

This is definitely a game I will be playing quite often. You should give it a try too. :)

Graphics and Gameplay are definitely a step up from Kings of Kung Fu, some of the backgrounds in the stages are beautiful (especially the one with the fire, reminds me of something out of a SNK figher, simply amazing) Gameplay feels much tighter and more responsive than the first game, while not Street Fighter/Tekken "fluid" this is only an alpha and one can expect it to be tweaked and improved with time

Overall, I'm satisified and I look forward to replaying the game in the future after a couple patches. If you're a fan of classic kung fu films, Bruce Lee, Jackie Chan... etc then this is pretty much a must buy.
