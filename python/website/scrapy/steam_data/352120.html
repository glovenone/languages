About This Game


Do you remember the time when helicopters flew not only in real but also in virtual skies? Those time when a decent combat flight simulators weren't as rare as hen's teeth? So whether you do or not remember that, now you have a perfect chance to try a piece of genre which was very well known in the early 2000s. So don't waste your time and jump into the cockpit of a modern attack helicopter and join a war against world terrorism.
Fair Strike is a game about brave pilots and their lethal celestial weapons fighting against terrorism and drug trafficking. Unlike many other combat flight simulations there will be no boring cloned missions over and over again in this classic game. No way! Fair Strike will vent all its fire fury upon the villains unlucky enough to disturb a peaceful citizens. Your goal is simple - Kill them all! Your choice - Attack helicopters!


  • Fly 6 playable helicopters: AH-64A Apache, RAH-66 Comanche, PAH-2 Tiger, Ka-50 Black Shark, Ka-52 Alligator, and the Ka-58 Black Ghost.
  • Battle heavily-armed narco-terrorists in the Caribbean, violent separatists in Eastern Europe, and Muslim fundamentalists in South East Asia and the Middle East.
  • Lay waste to enemy tanks, tankers, bunkers, ships, helicopters, troops. Everything on screen is destructible!

Fair Strike is an arcade helicopter action game originally released in 2004 which has now been re-released on Steam. In Fair Strike you pilot helicopters on missions to try and stop an international drug syndicate/group of narco-terrorists from doing their business. Obviously Fair Strike is an old game, and if I'm honest it does show, so this release is more aimed towards those who played it in the past for nostalgia.

+Six different helicopters to fly all with a few customisation options
+Quite a lot of missions
+Multiple camera views including a surprisingly well done cockpit view
+Missions are, for the most part, straightforward
+Nice variety of payloads for your helicopters
+Over the top but reasonably engaging campaign story mode
+Quite a lot of customisation for your HUD
+Nice variety of enemies
+Runs well on my modern PC which is good to see for such an old game
+Has Trading Cards

+/-The maximum resolution for the game is 1280x960 which is fine if you have a monitor with that resolution but it just makes the dated graphics look worse

-As already mentioned the graphics are very dated and there's been no improvement on them since the game's original release
-The controls are very difficult to get your head round and rebinding the keys only seems to work until you quit the game
-Sound effects aren't great
-The support for gamepad is very limited so if you want to play with a gamepad you'll need to use your keyboard too

Verdict:
6

Despite being very dated Fair Strike is a fun arcade helicopter game, with a nice selection of helicopters and payloads to use on the game's many missions.

Fair Strike is an arcade combat helicopter flight simulator.

Originally released way back in 2004 and supporting a maximum resolution of 1280 x 960 at 32 bit depth. The game does however work and plays smoothly on the core i7 and NVIDIA GT 770 under windows 7 64-bit.

Looking past the obviously dated graphics, the gameplay is fun. The default controls took a little time to get used to, but are functional and for the most part usable. Be prepared to master using a wireless X-box controller in tandem with the mouse and keyboard. There are 7 pages of default control keys / settings to memorise and the game is not playable without getting these under your thumb so to speak.

The missions are fairly straight forward with main missions and side missions to be completed and bonus objectives and achievements.

There are Steam trading cards and Steam achievements to be earned and the game is both fun to play and challenging. Be warned that the default game setting is Hard. There are two other settings available, Normal and Easy.

There are a variety of weapons and ordinance, and a few iconic Russian military helicopters which have been reasonably well modelled.

This game stands up fairly well with other similar games available at the time of it's original release such as the Apache Longbow series.

While not a state of the art flight simulator, this game is far from being a pure arcade game. It would fall somewhere in between the two. Best description would a combat flight simulator with arcade elements.

The missions are fun to play, but are a little over-the-top fantasy with a solid melodramatic touch. All the drug cartells in the world have united into this super criminal organisation which is stronger that the military organisations of the world's combined military super powers. This does however provide lots of cannon fodder and bombing targets so lots of stuff to shoot and blow up!

A competent game that will provide several hours of fun at a budget price.

5 / 10

Fair Strike is ancient, and it shows. Limited resolution, too many buttons, lack of full support for gamepad, it adds up to a somewhat frustrating flying experience when you just want to blow **** up.

One would have hoped that whoever made it run on modern platforms (it does run fine on Win7x64) would have bothered to create a decent mapping for gamepad, but no luck here. Game doesn't want to map a lot of commands, and have no way of figuring on which control is which. For example, let's say you want to map the two triggers to shoot... nope, can't do that. Those are ANALOG controls. ARGH! Okay, map to "rudder" (left right turns). Go into the game... It's REVERSED! I hit left and I turn RIGHT! had to go into options/controls and turn on "INVERTED". Do this for EVERY analog control. Oh, and it doesn't support the digital pad.

I guess I could have opened up XPADDER and tried to remap some more commands, but I'm too tired fighting with the game rather than playing it.

Being an ancient game even on older machine frame rate is silky smooth. And graphics are decent. Sound is okay.

The main problem is the game doesn't quite seem to make of itself. Being a "Strike" series member, Fair Strike is trying to invoke some old shooters like Desert Strike, Jungle Strike, and so on, but those are isometric and semi-3D shooters. This is a full 3D arcade-y flight sim. It'd be the difference between original River Raid and Lock-On. There's even a "simulation controls" (with full cyclic and collective controls) if you find the arcade controls (almost like hovercraft) too limiting.

If you want a fun helo shooter, go play Thunderwolves instead. This game is a bit too old and quirky to be enjoyable nowadays.
