About This Game

Pesadelo - Regressão (Pesadelo - The Regression) is a sequel of Pesadelo : O início (Pesadelo: The Beginning)

A cemetery filled with hostile creatures. A church that will not save you. Complex puzzles. The return to the school where everything began. And a fragile, intelligent girl with a thirst for revenge.

Relive Alex's dark memories as he tries to scape from an enemy that came back even stronger.

Is it another dream, or the naked truth?

Two weeks ago, Alex met Sara, a scientist at the Oblivion Laboratory that works in a memory recovery program.

Knowing that Alex didn't remember his own past, she encourages him to be a test subject for a software that rebuilds memories, recreating sceneries through dreams.

Everything seemed perfect during the first test. However, his brain tries to protect itself by creating a "defense" that hunts Alex in his dreams.

After he escapes from that creature, Alex end up at his old school, where he studied as a child.

A girl that was bullied by Alex and his friends shows up and tries to kill him in every possible way.

Once again Alex finds another way to escape. But to where?

I wanted to believe in this game, really I did. But I was let down so very bad by what the game offers out of the gate. "Partial Controller Support"? Nowhere to be found. Push a button on my wired 360 controller, character jumps. Push any other button, nothing happens. So there is clearly no controller support here (false advertising?). I don't see any button mappings for controllers, and no way to edit the key bindings. So either I play with mouse and keyboard, or I make do with one button.

Screen resolution is at a severe loss - game does not take into account that not everyone has a huge screen size. If I want to play at a resolution at anything close to my maximum resolution, I have to settle for black bars on the screen, which utterly kills the immersion one would expect from a game like this. Seems widescreens like mine don't get factored in. My top resolution is 1366x768, yet the only usable one is 1280x720 or 1024x768 - and those black bars. Oh, those black bars. The lack of widescreen support also kills the game for me.

The fact that I cannot launch the game before experiencing these problems leaves me wondering what else may be wrong with it - but I'm not willing to stick around and find out. I love horror games, even indie horror games, but this was a letdown out of the gate. Let's Play videos made this game look good, but unfortunately my first experiences (which have the biggest impact) were a huge letdown.

I'm sorry, but I just cannot recommend this game. Save your money for better horror games out there.

Pesadelo - Review
Full Review

Pesadelo is not Perfect-elo, it’s an ambitious indie horror that is desperately trying to avoid the common walking simulator horror tropes. It succeeds in a few areas, while comically stumbling in others. For a first time, low budget, indie offering that could use a fair bit of refinement --it's not devoid of thrilling moments. I was surprised how it turned into a stealth based cat and mouse maze through dark crypts and mausoleums. Good lighting and visuals construct a thrilling atmosphere to dodge monsters in the night. It's flaws will be ignored by fans of creative indie horrors, and this one fits that bill.

PROs
- Good lighting, texture, and level design ... pretty good use of Unreal 4's visual prowess.
- Tense cat and mouse gameplay, with some occassional jump scares.
- Pedro (see review)

CONs
- Confusing moments, with little or no direction given.
- Really bad animations
- Campy scenes and awkward moments, monsters thwarted by a door.
