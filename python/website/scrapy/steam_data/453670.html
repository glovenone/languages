About This Game


What does it feel like to find yourself in hell?
You don't feel the wind, you don't see the sun. No more hearing child's laughter.
No memory of those who once were your friends and family.

Main character dies and goes straight to hell during the ritual. Wandering through the dead lands in search of his soul, he finds an old cemetery. There seems to be a way to salvation..

Pika! Pika!
( 0.4 hrs on record )
Posted: 27 May
I was intrigued by the theme of this game and knew it had a significant number of bad reviews. I went ahead and got it anyway assuming other players were exaggerating. I found out that they were not exaggerating. It is a very short game which is generally empty in spite of the screenshots. The few monsters this game has are programmed in a way that they do not react to you, but merely follow a predefined path. The English is extremely bad and I would have expected the developer to let this game be reviewed by someone else more proficient in it. The story is only half a story. The puzzles are too trivial to even be in the game. You have to keep using the SHIFT button to move a little bit faster as without it, you would be bypassed by snails if they were in the game. The game has a replay value of 0. You can probably finish it in 20 - 50 minutes and will then never 'play' it again. As far as I am concerned most demos are more polished than this game. It really could have become something with a LOT of effort, but the developer apparently did not feel the need to do so and apparently chooses to keep selling empty games like this one in different formats. Unfortunate. The game does have a clever marketing strategy to draw players in, but that is all it has. In all fairness the game does have scary moments and they are not badly done, but they are very scarce and at some point you just know the full extent of what the game does and does not do and the surprises then end there. I just expected a lot more and a lot better.

In this case it was not a case of whether the game was good enough to play. No, it was a matter of whether the game was good enough to be in my library. I did not consider this game worthy to remain in my library and thus permanently removed it from my library.

I'll make this short and sweet.

Simply put, this is just another lazy cashgrab in a long line of lazy cashgrabs by this developer. The Lost Souls is a "sequel" to White Mirror - except it's practically the same game with a different color palette. Same setting, same "walk-up-to-statue-and-hit-keys-in-a-certain-order" puzzles, same underwater switch flipping, same monster, same, same, SAME.

Game is completable within 20 minutes, assuming it doesn't crash. (It's worth noting, the game didn't crash for me like it apparently did for others) The "puzzles" are literally just typing words backwards in order to open locked doors. Unfulfilling, dull, senseless tripe. I'm sure the same will be said about the next game this developer uploads about 3 months from now, per his schedule.

In summary, terrible.
