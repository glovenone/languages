About This Game

BS is the game where you make your own rules. Create the match of any size and complexity and get the reward for completing it ! Try to get all the costumes and weapons skins from the random store.

Features:
-Cool design
-The great amount of costumes and skins
-Unique gameplay
-The fast creation of your own match

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP and newer
    • Processor: 2.0+ GHz
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: Integrated Graphics
    • Storage: 120 MB available space
    • Sound Card: Integrated Audio

This isn't even a finished game. It's a proof of concept. If you buy this game outright on Steam, even if it's on sale, you are an idiot.

GOOD:
- The graphics are quite nice and run smooth
- The UI design is very good and visually pleasing.

BAD:
- Literally everything else. This "game" has a serious identity crisis.
- The game is 1 level, with 3 different backgrounds.
- The game has 3 enemies. 2 of them are redundant and don't give you even close to the amount of exp as the main one does.
- The in-game store is incredibly tedious. It is gambling, pure and simple. Complete waste of someones time.
- Music and sounds are repetitive and annoying.
- Very high asking price at $7.30 NZD. It literally wouldn't even be worth $0.50. Those rubbish, cheap trading cards games have more substance than this one.


OVERALL: 1/10 - Don't waste your time.

Do not ever buy it. There are literally items that are called "Untitled Item". This game isn't complete, and it is a joke that the developer is trying to sell it.

I'm quite puzzled by the number of positive reviews.
Positives:
The game looks nice. Graphics are simple but interesting, more so if you like cartoons as much as I do.
Negatives: The game's description says "BS is the game where you make your own rules.", but what it means in reality is that the game contains no story, almost no gameplay, and essentially no reason for you to play. Basically, this is a Kongregate game, unfortunately this is Steam.
The bar for Steam games has lowered considerably in recent years, and this is one of the better bad games on Steam. Also, have I mentioned that controls are horrible and don't work most of the time?
I got this game in a bundle, but the regular price of 5.99EUR is insane. Games like this one should be, and usually are, free.

Even in an era where free/absurdly cheap PC games are as plentiful as rain-drops, reviews can still be bought with keys. Gamers aren't exactly hurting for quality games to play, so there has to be more of an incentive to recommend junk aside from "Well, I got it for free." It's actually kind of depressing. Why not accept an worthwhile bribe? Like money. On the other hand, it would probably take a lot of cash to make up for playing a vile mess like Beater Spirit.

The absolute biggest problem with Beater Spirit is that there isn't any actual content. Each stage consists of knocking skeletons into pits, smooshing slimes, and/or whacking bats. This is the entire game. Just imagine repeating everything you learned in the five-second tutorial a hundred times. Why does BS take a couple hours to complete? Because you have to grind experience and cash to buy all of the stat-boosting equipment. If that wasn't enough, all purchases are random, and you have to be exceptionally wealthy or absurdly lucky to get the best stuff.

Perhaps calling this game a vile mess is giving it too much credit. At least a vile mess would be memorable. I figure that by the time I click the "post review" button, I'll have forgotten about Beater Spirit.
