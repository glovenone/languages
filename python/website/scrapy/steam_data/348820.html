FIM Speedway Grand Prix 15 Gets Massive Multiplayer Update

FIM Speedway Grand Prix 15 has received a major free update (numbered 1.2.0), which was created according to player opinions expressed on the game’s Steam community hub and official webpage. The update is focused on the multiplayer aspect of the game and introduces the following improvements:

  • New method of multiplayer match-making. Now it’s easier to find players in the Quick Race and Single Event modes.

  • New players can now join an on-going heat or event and watch the competition in spectator mode while they wait to participate in the next heat/event.

  • Thanks to revamped invitations, the multiplayer mode is now more open and easier to navigate. The time-frame for joining matches has also been lengthened.

  • A chat has been added, which can be activated by a player-defined key. Players can comment no matter if they are riders or spectators - it is now possible to comment races live, discuss which track to choose, and so on.

  • Connection stability for multiplayer has been improved.
FIM Speedway Grand Prix 15, is one of the most realistic speedway simulators to date. Thanks to the BSI Speedway Limited official license, the game features all riders that qualified for Grand Prix 2015 and faithfully recreates all events and stadiums of the current season. FIM Speedway Grand Prix 15 is available now on PC for 21,99 euros.

About This Game

Tremendous power and no brakes! Experience the thrill of speedway! FIM Speedway Grand Prix 15 is the ultimate speedway simulator of all time. Produced under license by BSI Speedway Limited it brings real sport into video game. Show your skill and tactics in the fight for the title of World Champion in all events and stadiums of the Grand Prix 2015 season!

Tai Woffinden, Nicki Pedersen, Jaroslaw Hampel, Chris Holder… In FIM Speedway Grand Prix 15 you will find all the speedway stars from the current season and real Grand Prix stadiums with different surface types. Powerful, loud engines, high speed, tight shoulder-to-shoulder racing, cheering crowds, clouds of shale and dirt will accompany your road to victory in Grand Prix events - from Melbourne, through Cardiff, to Målilla in Sweden.

Buy and repair motorcycle parts, change settings, on the track search for paths with better grip to gain an advantage over your opponents. Dynamic real time commentary by Nigel Pearson and online multiplayer for up to 4 players!

GAME FEATURES

- HIGHLY DETAILED REAL GRAND PRIX STADIUMS WITH DIFFERENT SURFACE TYPES
- DRIVING PHYSICS INCORPORATING CHANGES IN THE TRACK SURFACE
- MOTORCYCLE TUNING AND PARTS PURCHASE, WHICH AFFECTS PERFORMANCE ON THE TRACK
- PROFESSIONAL LIVE COMMENTARY BY NIGEL PEARSON
- TWO DRIVING MODES: ARCADE AND SIMULATION
- ONLINE MULTIPLAYER RACING FOR UP TO 4 PLAYERS SIMULTANEOUSLY!

I've been waiting for a Speedway game for a long time that actually did justice to the sport and finally it has arrived.
I purchased FIM 03 and 04 some time ago hoping they would capture the feeling somewhat but they were really lacking and very buggy so I purchased this one thinking the experience could be somewhat similar. Thankfully it wasn't and the devs have done a really good job of capturing some of the atmosphere.
The sound of the bikes as they get ready at the tapes, the way the camera gets in there for the line up really adds to the feeling of a real race. The bike models, riders and stadiums look superb although there is a little bit of blur on background objects that looks like motion blur is working even when the game is still, but once you get riding these things don't matter at all.
The only thing the devs didn't capture is the smell, it's such a unique smell at the speedway with the gravel and the fuel mixed with the sounds of the bikes but we're not getting the smells until we get smellavision. But saying that the atmosphere of the game does bring back those memories of being there.
I would love to have seen more content like the leagues and subsequently a lot more riders. I would love to have seen some classic riders put into the game like Ole Olsen, Ivan Mauger, Peter Collins, Phil Crump and a more recent classic like Tony Rickardsson etc etc and also some little touches that would have made it feel more complete like riding into and out of the pits, somewhere I spent a lot of time as a kid at Coventry (or Brandon) Bees.
I've been to most of the stadiums in the UK to watch the Bees but now I live in the south in order to get my Speedway fix I follow Poole Pirates but not as much as I'd like to. It's a very odd feeling when they face off against the Bees though :)

It's not an easy game to master, far from it, but the simulation mode is where you'll get the best feeling of actually riding the bikes and being out there on the track. Persevere on simulation and you'll get a surprising amount of satisfaction from even finishing a race due to the difficulties of holding a line and finding good grip around the bends. You'll find yourself crashing into the barriers more often than not for the first few hours but the hardest part is riding with three other riders who want the same thing as you do, to win. After a while you'll get used to what you need to do to avoid the other riders and to get good speeds out of the bends and compete.

Default settings for your bike may have it set a little to quick with lots of torque and high rpm so I would advise turning both these down quite a bit while you are learning the game. About half of what they are set on default will help you to finish the races and still compete with the other riders. I also found putting the AI onto Hard difficulty was a help when it came to staying on the track. They stay at a good speed and are subsequently easier to avoid yet are still beatable with good riding.

If the devs keep on improving this game then it will become a classic for fans of Speedway and hopefully over the years to come we'll see more content added. For me it was well worth the full price admission.
