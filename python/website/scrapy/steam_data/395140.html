Miracle Fly is a simple arcade game. You play one of three witches flying through the levels trying to reach the exit. While doing so you can collect coins, stars and shoot enemies. The different levels can be accessed through the world map. In total there are 8 worlds each with 9 normal levels, 3 bonus levels and one boss battle. So there is a lot of content.

I admit I was and am still a little bit on the fence for Miracle Fly . The "wow, interesting, fun" aspect which is the reason for most of my reviews is somehow different here. But I just can't find any reason why I shouldn't recommand the game. From a pure objective perspective the game is really good. While the overall gameplay between the witches is the same and the changes are rather small they are important and noticeable. For example is the range, style and strength of the broom attack different for each of them which can be make a change on how hard a level is to complete. Also while there are 8 worlds they all feel different enough to be justified. Every 4 level and new gimmick is introduced which keeps the game fresh and interesting (bouncing walls, giant enemies, different fluids to swim trough, reduced gravity, ....). The world and level design is really strong and show the creativity of the developer. Mixed to this are some puzzles (mostly needed for getting the three stars in each level) which do challenge your head. Additionally there are a lot of secrets and shortcuts. And the game has still a lot of content.

So what is that keeps bugging me? I think most of my concerns come from being an achievement hunter. In order to unlock all achievements you need to play through the game at least two times and play each level multiple times. You need to collect all Stars, need to win the time challenge (which also shows the creativity since the shortcuts are important now) and replay the whole game in New Game+ (IMPORTANT NOTE: You need to beat the game with each witch having completed the same amount of levels to unlock NG+) with three new characters. So all in all I probably replayed some levels far too often for them to be still enjoyable. Added with the high skill level needed to beat some of them there is a lot of frustration potential and I probably cursed more then recommanded for my health. Also most achievements are connected to the boss challenges where you have harder versions of the normal bosses like really hard.

On the other hand again the developer was during the time I played the game very active in the forum, helped with a lot of questions, gave away tips and secrets and even changed minor parts of the game to make it more accessible. This deserves some praise in my eyes and is one reason I recommand the game now. So if you are looking for a creative game which can give a lot of challenge if you are searching for it and can take away a lot of your time I don't think you can do much wrong with Miracle Fly.


PS: If you have any questions just comment on the review and I'll try to come back to them as soon as possible.

Miracle Fly is a fun little indie platformer that avoids many of the pitfalls that indie platformers tend to have.

To me, its biggest strength is you never feel like you're getting locked out of content. Most platformers are level based, and more levels are unlocked as you complete currently available ones. Though no matter how skilled I am, there's a good chance at some point there will be a level I simply will not be able to beat, and as a result, I miss out on experiencing anything from a few levels to an entire chunk of a game. Miracle Fly avoids that by having multiple paths on level selection screen, so odds are you will usually have more than one level to choose from in order to unlock more levels. Additionally, stars can act as a cushion for when you get into a difficult situation. These stars are collected by getting all the gems in a level, collecting a certain coin in a level, or defeating a world's boss. For a certain amount of stars, you can revive yourself at the location of your death in a level and receive a few seconds of invincibility. There will always be a limited amount of stars available and within a level the cost of revival doubles with each use, so it isn't feasible to abuse the feature to make the game a cakewalk.

The game is also flexible in its requirements to complete a level. Reaching the exit portal with time to spare is the only task required. Youâre welcome to collect all the gems, get every coin, or defeat every enemy, but ultimately such objectives are optional. However there is incentive complete them, given that the aforementioned revive mechanic is tied to gem collection and the occasional coin. This allows for an overall flexible approach that can be rewarding without feeling punishing.

The levels have a gradual increase in difficulty as the game goes on, but never feels insurmountable like Iâve encountered in other games. In the game's defense however, two of the three playable characters are able to regain health under circumstances, but depending on the level at hand, they might not have the abilities best suited for success.

Another strength of Miracle Fly is that it never gets stale. Many platformers are mechanically sparse, and end up becoming repetitive or poorly ramping up the difficultly to make up for the lack of mechanics. Miracle Fly is always introducing new blocks, buttons, and enemies. Additionally, the three characters you can choose from differ in their abilities, strengths, and weaknesses, so even if you prefer one, youâll likely use all of them at some point in the game. Some gameplay aspects introduced are more obtuse to figure out than others, but I never felt like Miracle Fly was getting old as I played it.

Besides the previously mentioned issues of limited health powerups and occasionally hard to understand mechanics, Iâd say another problem is itâs easy to become dependent on the revive feature to complete a level. Warning: The game is extremly difficult, the cute graphics are deceptive, if you are looking for an easy game this is not it.

Miracle fly is a puzzle platformer with the gimmick that you move your character by shooting behind you. At first the controls feel really weird, but after you get used to it they are great. If you are playing with a gamepad you have to rebind the keys because the default setup is not functional.

You can choose one of 3 witches to beat the levels, on the way through a level you want to collect gems that allow you to unlock bonus levels. Bonus levels are generally more difficult than normal levels and often require you to find a hidden secret or solve a tricky puzzle to collect all the gems.

There are a ton of levels at least 80 and the game also does a very good job of introducing cool new mechanics at a regular pace, the game never got boring for me. As i mentioned earlier, the game is extremly difficult, especially if you are trying to collect all the gems, but it is designed in a way that just completing a level is usually not a problem. If you ignore gems and a level is still too difficult for you, there is a limited option to revive.

The bossfights are difficult, all the bosses have multiple stages and the later bosses require some pattern memorization to beat them, but even if you know what to do the execution is still difficult.

Conclusion: If you like difficult platformers like super meat boy or dustforce and aren't afraid of the gimmicky movement you should give this game a try.
