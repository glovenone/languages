Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Disgraced is in Early Access because it isn't quite finished yet. Another reason is because we want our community to take part in deciding how the game is created and what features are added. Early Access allows us to take a period of time and gather community interest, ideas, and participation in order to create a better game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Disgraced is planned to be in Early Access for as short a time as possible. Our last game, Waste Walkers, was in Early Access for exactly 6 months (June 1st - December 1st) and we plan to do the same for Disgraced.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of Disgraced will obviously be a full game. This means that there will be more features and content.

There will also be a huge amount of community input by the time the game is fully completed so this is aiming to be a project run by gamers from all over.

Plans:
  • More Enemies
  • More Characters
  • More Classes
  • More Weapons & Equipment
  • Expand World Map
  • More Outpost Upgrades
  • More Quests
  • More Skills
  • More Open World Areas to Explore
  • And So Much More
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Disgraced is currently playable to the point where you can complete the tutorial/intro and explore a good portion of the world map. The majority of the features are already in the game, but must be further developed.

PLEASE NOTE: Please do not buy Disgraced yet if you're not willing to deal with the occasional bug, unfinished features, loss of save files, and errors. This is a work in progress.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We believe that you should get what you pay for. In Alpha Early Access, the price will be very low. Upon entering the Beta stage, it will slightly increase to match the amount of new content and features, and upon completion it will increase slightly more for being a complete game. Our first game was successful and it used this practice. The gradual raise over time will be very small but it will directly reflect the amount of content received at the time of purchase.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Like our last game, Waste Walkers, Disgraced is intended to be a community project. What we mean by this is that the community will have the final say over new features and content to be added in the game. If something is too easy, hard, or lacking, we encourage the community to tell us so that we can improve it. We ask for everyone to come together and help us make a truly fantastic game.

Join the Rebellion Now
    Join the rebellion now and help us create Disgraced! This is your chance to jump in early, and help us choose how the game is created! Together, we can create a truly fantastic game!
”

About This Game

Disgraced


Based in Feudal Japan, Disgraced allows you to assume the role of a soldier who was reluctantly conscripted into the newly proclaimed Shogun's army. Serving a corrupt and tyrannical government, you're expected to follow orders and commit terrible atrocities. Torn between duty and justice, you must choose your own path, and forge your own destiny.

Disgraced is a role playing game that follows a fictional storyline based on real events. Your character will participate in a resistance movement determined to bring freedom and justice to Japan.

Build Your Resistance
    Disgraced puts you in control of your own rebellion! Using whatever resources and funds that are available to you, build, manage, and upgrade your very own rebel faction!

Assemble Your Team
    Disgraced features battles that require planning, and strategy in order to claim victory. Choose from a wide selection of companions to accompany you on your quest for freedom and hone your skills as a team!

Fight Back
    Disgraced is a game about striking back against an unjust, and corrupt government. You'll need to meticulously plan out your raids in order to be successful, and use whatever you're lucky enough to get your hands on. You can follow the path of a ninja, a ronin, or anything in between. You play how you want to play.

Early Access Review
Posted: 20 June
Product received for free
I am friends with the developer and received this game for free

Disgraced is a game currently in Early Access. It was made by the same developer who created Waste Walkers. It follows along the same style of features as Waste Walkers, with the same dark, gritty tone. It's set in feudal Japan, at the start of a new Shogun's rise to power. You play as a character who was newly conscripted into the Shogun's army who deserts it faster than cherry pie. No, wait, that's dessert. My bad, I'm craving sweets.

Anyway, you leave the army and quickly find yourself part of the resistance against this new Shogun. You work with villagers, spies, and rebels to create a fortified town to fight and defend yourself against the Shogun's army and to probably kill the Shogun himself. At least that's what I'm hoping.

For gameplay, the core features that made Waste Walkers enjoyable are all here. You have to survive by eating food and keep an eye on your stamina so you're able to cheese it in case you're low on health and can't fight enemies. The game also focuses more on the building mechanics which had a small highlight in Waste Walkers. You need resources to build up your rebel town, and to get them you'll have to work hard and find them or earn them (or probably kill for them, you are a rebel after all).

New sounds and Japanese themed music keep this game's setting in tact and appear interesting. I can't say much else about sounds in general because that's not my expertise, but all I can say is that it definitely fits.

Currently, as the game is in Early Access, I would recommend to purchase it and wait, as the price will go up as the game continues to near completion. There isn't a whole lot to do currently, but the game is expected to retail for $10USD rather than what it costs right now. So if you liked Waste Walkers, or are interested in supporting RPG game developers, it's a rather solid buy. You can expect a finished game from the developer in under a year.
