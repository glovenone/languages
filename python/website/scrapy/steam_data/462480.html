Early Access Review
Posted: 5 July
Product received for free
Sweet Escape VR is an interesting concept. Climb the Candy covered obstacle course using the candy as hand holds. The world is bright and colorful, the concept at its core is a lot of fun and the more difficult climbs can be quite a challenge.

There are two main problems that I encountered with the game, the first (and more easily fixable) is that the game does not always recognize that you have grabbed one of the candy handholds forcing you to fall to your death for seemingly no reason. It doesnt happen often but it only has to happen once to ruin your climb.

The second problem is that instead of feeling a sense of a vertical climb, you instead feel that you are pulling the game world around. If you stop moving and are looking up or down you get that feeling of verticality but its dispelled the moment you reach for that next hand hold.

Finally the $20 price tag. I received the game as a gift from the PR Director for Monster VR for review and personal enjoyment. While I thank them for their generosity I would have been quite upset had I paid $20 for this game as the game is quite rough around the edges both in design and content and the $20 price tag is steep even for a VR title. $20 would get you Vanishing Realms, Audioshield, Tabletop Simulator, Solus Project, Pool Nation VR or Brookhaven. $15 could net you SPT, Holopoint or Battle Dome. Even if you have all of those games already, that $20 puts you halfway to the cost of VorpX which will let you play Skyrim, Bioshock, Borderlands, Saints Row on a movie theater sized 3d screen.

If you have children who will be less likely to smash their controllers into the ceiling and will be more likely to be wowed by the candyland'esk landscape then this game could be a good investment to encourage them to get up and moving around, For everyone else I would have to recommend waiting until the game is heavily discounted or you have already bought all of the other high quality $20 and under VR titles.

Early Access Review
Posted: 24 May
Product received for free
Sweet Escape VR

DISCLAIMER: I received this product for no cost for the purpose of reviewing.

Sweet Escape VR is the first VR game I've been able to play, so although I don't have much experience with this sort of game, I may revisit this review later on when the game is complete, and once I've experienced other VR titles. The game is an obstacle course. You climb and move through various candy themed hurdles until you reach the end of the stage. In single player mode, you're competing with yourself in getting the best/fastest time. In multiplayer mode, it's a race to see who can get to the end of the stage first.

--------

Graphics: 5 / 10

I did not expect the game's graphics to look good, however it is very colourful and the way the candy theme is incorporated is nice. Sometimes it felt as though there were too many objects all over the place which was kind of distracting to me, but I feel that might have been intentional. I did feel that there is a lack of texture throughout the game, as everything looked very shiny and rounded. That being said, the game ran smoothly on my system and I'm sure the game would run fine on any basic setup.

--------

Sound: 4 / 10

At first the sound assets were fine, and for anyone that enjoy rock covers of classic children's themes, then you might enjoy this. "Do your ear's hang low?" plays in the background of the stage and it does get repetitive after awhile. Although I did read somewhere that the devs were making an effort to incorporate other songs into the game eventually, and you do have the option to disable the music.

--------

Mechanics: 5 / 10

The mechanics with the VR system are pretty solid, I didn't run into any issues, and it was pretty responsive to the controls. It may take some getting used to at the beginning to figure out how far you have to stretch to latch onto an object in game, but it doesn't take that long to get a hold of it.

--------

Final Personal Remarks:

These types of racing/speed running games are not something I usually enjoy, so I wasn't drawn into this game as much as other titles. I can see how some people would enjoy this title however, and I do think that this game is good for beginners to test out VR capabilities as well as getting a feel for these types of games. As it is in early access, I will revisit this title and re-review it at a later date.

Thanks to the Devs for giving me the opportunity to review the game, as well as anyone who took this review into consideration, I hope it helped.

Early Access Review
Posted: 20 April
As an early access title, I can absolutely forgive Sweet Escape VR for being incomplete. I'm putting faith that an even better and more complete title is on its way in the future.

As it stands, the current game is a single race along rock walls made of HTC branded chocolate bars, gum drops, candy canes and gummy bears. There's multiplayer connectivity available if you have two Vives or connect via matchmaking features that likely won't turn up many other players until people both have VR headsets and buy the game. I demoed an earlier build last month at a convention and the competiveness of a two player race is excellent.

There's a bit of a challenge with multiple paths along the way to the finish line, with the green gumdrops being an easy path and the yellow gumdrops providing more of a challenge, but overall, there's currently not more than 10 minutes or so of single player content if you aren't racing friends or internet strangers.

What exists today is an extremely fun concept and it actually feels like I'm stretching to grab the next hold on a rock wall each time I extend my arm and controller. I think it is a good gamble to take at the highly reduced price for early access for a game very early in development. Now I just need more people to buy it so that I have an excuse to practice and race you!
