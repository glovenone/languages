About This Game

The TRANSFORMERS are back, and this time they’re in the hands of acclaimed developer PlatinumGames, who have combined their over-the-top brawler action with comic book inspired art to create a TRANSFORMERS game like none other.

  • Surprise the enemy: PlatinumGames signature fast action allows instant robot to vehicle attacks, slam downs, counterattacks, and infinite combos for to create a unique master combat system.
  • Five playable AUTOBOTS: Wreak havoc on the DECEPTICONS as OPTIMUS PRIME, BUMBLEBEE, SIDESWIPE, WHEELJACK and GRIMLOCK.
  • Actors from the original TV series: Reprising their roles are Peter Cullen (OPTIMUS PRIME), Dan Gilvezan (BUMBLEBEE), Frank Welker (MEGATRON and SOUNDWAVE), and more.
  • Play again and again: 5 difficulty levels with overhauls in enemy composition, combined with a deep customization system, means hours of intense gameplay.

Got this during the 2016 Steam Summer Sale. Did not regret it. This is by FAR the best Transformers game I've ever played.
It takes place in a setting not unlike a G1 episode, unlike WFC and FOC which looked like G1 but became Prime with Tron lines. Transformers Devastation is a welcome break from other TF games and, instead of being an action game that had WAY too much defending and puzzles for my taste, is a hack and slash, beat 'em up, collect the best weapons ever game riding a 32 year old nostalgia horse and MAKES IT WORK! If there's anything we G1 purists needed after 'Autobot Megatron' and 'Victorion the fan-made-female-combiner-who-is-now-the-defender-of-the-universe," it was this. The DLC takes the three main playable characters and gives them new skins of (mostly) different characters. Optimus becomes Nemesis Prime, the Unicronian herald who was created by the Uncreator himself to destroy all Cybertronian life (but for some reason wears a Decepticon badge instead of a Unicronian one). He gets a new weapon called the Dark Star Saber which is based off the TF: Prime weapon of the same name. Sideswipe becomes Red Alert, a paranoid Lamborghini Autobot that has trust issues. He's basically the Autobot version of Breakdown. Red Alert gets a toy and comic-accurate black helmet instead of his usual red one, like the Classics toy. His weapon is a Photon Disruptor which is also the only A class weapon of the lot. Bumblebee becomes Goldfire, (or Goldbug to yours and mine) an upgraded form of Bumblebee after he was grievously wounded by a hate-plague infected Superion in the last few episodes of Generation One. He still got infected, upgrade or not. His weapon are the Golden Hunters, which are somewhat weak double pistols, but enemies drop credits every time they're shot with 'em. Overall, this game is a 10/10 for me. Only thing is, I can't get Blitzwing's scimitar. Sad.

More than meets the eye


Transformers Devastation comes as the next entry in the Transformers game series after a less than than stellar end to the Cybertron series with 2014's so-so Transformers Rise Of The Dark Spark.

Devastation however does not have anything to do with the Cybertron games but is in fact a video game homage to the 80's Transformers cartoon with a similar look, tone and feel to the original series. Even actors such as Peter Cullen and Frank Welker return to reprise their roles as Optimus Prime and Megatron once again.

Developed by Platinum Games, known for their high quality hack and slashers such as Bayonetta and Metal Gear Rising, Devastation is yet another game from the company that can be classed as a hack and slash title, although Transformers has a vastly different feel to it than its contemporaries due to it's admittedly rather campy source material. I am aware neither Bayonetta or MGR took themselves too seriously but Transformers ups the ante as it knows it should be as cheesy and ridiculous as possible so the entire game has a burst of cel-shaded colour and imagination not seen often in games of this generation and this really helps it in standing out from the crowd.

The game lets you control five Transformers, Optimus Prime, Bumblebee, Sideswipe, Wheeljack and Grimlock. Each character plays slighly differently from each other and each of them attack in a variety of ways.

Gameplay involves, as always with Platinum's titles, fast-paced action where quick button inputs let you reign hell upon your enemies. A cool element of this game is that the transformer you choose to play as can transform into a car to make the action even swifter for smooth traversal through the game's environments. It helps break up the combat with an element not found in other Platinum titles.

Enemies include your standard grunts, insect-like robots, a giant hammer-wielding robot that can break you down easily if you aren't careful, especially when there are more than one, and there are a few Devastators to fight in arena-like boss battles, which are highly enjoyable.

The run-time of Devastation is about 4-5 hours so it's far from the longest game from Platinum, but it does have a lot of replay value as earning new weapons through the game's loot finding system grants you more power to help you take on harder difficulties as well as the fact the game rewards you for playing through it with each Transformer for even more replayability.

To sum things up, Transformers Devastation comes highly recommended for fans of Platinum's other hack and slash titles, for fans of the original 80's Transformers series as it does it's source material justice in both looks and tone, and really, just fans of over the top action games in general as this game is incredibly polished and just stupidly entertaining from a gameplay perspective.

Before we get into this review that nobody will ever see; Give this game a try if you want, seriously, I loved this game and played it nonstop for 7 hours.


Okay, TRANSFORMERS: Devastation is a title where you take control of the Autobots in an attempt to stop the Decepticons from cyberforming planet Earth.
Concept sounds great, right? Classic 80's transformers style game.
It is great! Bringing back the classic 'formers was a genius idea!

Okay enough cutting around the bush. No more praising T:D for what it is.
Let's start criticising the ♥♥♥♥ out of this game!

Well, Transformers Devastation is fairly short, is this a huge con? No, not really, but it could've had a lot more if given the time.
There's a specific lack of variety in enemies throughout the game. I guess this was to be expected, we're staying lore-friendly here, but the main enemy attack pattern consists of -Ground soldier A, Ground soldier B, Ground soldier C, Seeker Abc whatever we get it, variety isn't huge. While we're on the topic of lack of enemies, we find ourselves fighting the Constructicons in their combined form a total of 3-4 times throughout the 5 hours of gameplay, as if they're a main villain.

So we know how games have those horrible missions that nobody likes?

Everyone hated Half Life Blueshift because the entire game was a "lead NPC A to location without letting them get hurt."

Transformers Devastation has two missions in particular that grinded my gears.
Bluh bluah blah, protect Wheeljack from a horde of decepticons.

Not a big deal, ezpz.

But then we got the Heavy Ground Soldiers with their massive hammers that pose an insane threat to Wheeljack at the end of the first mission where you protect Wheeljack, and at the start of the second.

Now, if you're not playing as Prime with a hyper hammer that is boosted to level 10, you're going to have the worst time with these missions.

That aside, the rest of the game was worth the buy while on sale.
I personally would not buy Transformers devastation for $40 or whatever the hell it is, but that'll wrap up this review.

Overall, 8/10. Probably gonna continue playing doing challenge missions, but what then? DLC when?

Slick action game that wholeheartedly embraces the style and characterizations of the 80s toon. Combat is fluid, with a nice combo system that makes use of transforming. Equipment management feels a bit needless, since you're mostly just upgrading the same weapons forever, but there is some skill/stat twinkage you can do with it if that's your thing.

The top down missions are completely stupid though. That's my only real complaint.

Having finished it now, I wanted to add that the writing is surprisingly good. Transformers games are usually such a mess of fanservice quotes that they've just gotten old. Devastation maintains the spirit of the old Transformers while feeling like something new.
