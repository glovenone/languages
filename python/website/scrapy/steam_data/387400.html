About This Game

C.R.E.E.P.S is a FPS TOWER DEFENSE game that lets the player deploy squad members and equipment as he defends objectives and moves through the game story.

STORY
When a Canadian research team working at Trevain Industries opens an inter-dimensional portal known as the Hellgate, the city of Calgary and all of North America is thrown into complete darkness. C.R.E.E.P.S an elite military contractor led by Sgt. Jake Muldoon is deployed into the scene to restore power and close the gate.

ATMOSPHERE
Darkness and fog reign everywhere. Visibility is short and the atmosphere is eerily. Screams and growls are heard everywhere. You must reach locations where the lights can be turned on. When the lights are on watch out! Enemies hate the light. Wave of enemies will came after you, your squad and your equipment. You need to think on your feet to keep yourself and squad members a live or from being kidnapped.

HIGHLIGHTS
- Immersive 3D Graphics give players a detailed world and characters to play with.
- Player can choose from many different weapons, from Sniper Rifles to Shotguns.
- Player can deploy 4 different classes of squad members: Medic, Soldier, Engineer, Demolisher
- Player can setup special equipment to slowdown or kill enemies.

Early Access Review
Posted: 23 February
Keep in mind its still in early access at the time of this review. Version 1.8.
It was fun until the level after the gas station. Mountains. There for the first time those enemys with the rocket launchers appeared.
With the credits/money I had earned I didn't have enough to protect any generator and always died in the first wave, latest in the second. I wanted more cash to get past this so I thought I´d grind the previous levels to improve my score and earn some more cash.
But for some reason all the past levels suddenly also had these rocket launcher enemys where they didn't before. I gave up on trying to play them and started a new game, knowing all my progress would be lost and I´d have to start again from mission 1. I did this on purpose because I had planned to change my strategy on spending the money until I got to the mountains mission again. This did not work as I wanted it to. It looked right at first: I had only 2000 $ starting credits and all the missions were locked except mission 1. Then I started to play the first mission and... those ruthless rocket launcher enemys again. Also the weapons I had unlocked before were still there, which doesn't seem right when starting a new game. But now with a lot less money, the levels still insanely harder than the first time I played them, I didn't want to play anymore.

The other bad thing for me was the lag. Every few seconds the game would freeze only very shortly but when a lot was happening this occurred noticeably more.
I admit I don't have the best gaming pc but I can play comparable FPS games in medium to high quality without lag. Especially in singleplayer mode. In this game I had the graphics quality set to low the whole time. There weren't any other graphics options in the menus to get it to run better and I also disliked that I wasn't allowed to set up any keybindings.

I enjoyed C.R.E.E.P.S. until that one mission like I said and after that the story mode wasn't playable anymore. I also tried the "TODAY´S CHALLANGE" mode shortly but even in there were those rocket firing enemys, also always in the first wave. I know what I needed to turn the areas around the generators into defensive positions and keep them safe. To collect ammo and money after a wave and upgrade my weapons after a level. The first missions of the game worked good. It had potential.
Hordes of enemys, tower defense elements because you set up the soldiers, turrets, barriers and so on whilst shooting the zombies/monsters in first person perspective to protect the generators. I like these types of games and have played similar before. Sadly this game suddenly changed difficulty after playing half of it, so much infact that all previous missions became insane and I wasnt able to earn any more currency and have an actual chance of beating the rest of the levels so I gave up for now. I hope the problems get fixed so I can finish the game and maybe write a positive review. I like the artwork for the menus and loading screens also. Just the "gunplay" font was being a little overused in my opinion. Anyway I´d be happy to play it again if and when the higher priority issues are fixed.
