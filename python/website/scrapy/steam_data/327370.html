About This Game

HOW FAR WILL YOU GO TO SAVE YOUR ONLY DAUGHTER'S LIFE?

Is there a limit?

Help Owen Wright rescue his daughter from the clutches of the Lonely Hearts Killer, the mysterious figure who kidnaps and murders people who have suffered a love gone wrong.

Unravel the intrigue and put a stop to the whirlwind of crime that has paralyzed this late 19th century British city.

The killer must be stopped so justice can be done!

Features:

  • Discover the clues!
  • Amazing Hidden Object game-play, tricky puzzles and mini-games.
  • Explore a 19th century English city, amazing Victorian homes and meet an intriguing cast of characters!
  • Stop a demented mind and end the killing spree!
  • Save Olivia before it's too late!

The Collector's Edition includes;

  • A bonus chapter with a surprising plot
  • An integrated strategy guide
  • Downloadable soundtrack, concept art and wallpapers
  • An interview with the developers

And I can be very forgiving about things - too many HOS and not enough puzzles (or vice versa), puzzles which are so easy they're boring or so hard they inspire rage-quitting, lame live-action actors or bad voice acting, even graphics which are pedestrian. But this is far and away the most frustrating and irritating HOS adventure I've ever played. There are just WAY too many cut scenes, and those cut scenes go on FOREVER. At one point I got up when a cut scene had started, and spent a few minutes getting my nephew a cup of juice, only to find that when I saw back down the scene was still going. And it KEPT going, for at least another five minutes.

And the ending of both the main and the bonus chapters, is entirely cut scene, with no significant player involvement at all. I wouldn't be surprised if there genuinely more cut-scene time than playing time in this game (but I didn't do the math, and I damned sure am not replaying to find out. And nothing about the game play itself, the graphics, the characters, or the strange storyline, made up for insanely long over-abundance of cut scenes.

Like I said, I'm a forgiving reviewer most of the time, but this is easily the worst HOS adventure I've ever played, and nothing redeemed it's shortcomings.

One of the best hidden object games I've played so far, if you can forgive it's weaknesses that come from it's genre. Some story elements and puzzles makes absolutely no sense (Part of your clock ending up I think in a police officer's safe??), and the way characters move is a little rigid - though the developers took their time to animate whole body-scale moving of lots of (!) characters, this is quite a minor flaw.

Strong aspects of the game:
  • There is a story with lots of small details in it and lots ( and I do mean lots) of dialogue with actually quite deep characters.
  • Really nice, albeit short soundtracks which are pleasant to listen to even after half an hour.
  • Some puzzles are extraordinary. Lots of the puzzles' design is original, but there are some in the bonus chapter that actually challenging, and I'm really happy about it.
  • Nice graphics and quality hidden object scenes
  • Really intresting Interview with the Developers in Extras :) Check it out! (Sadly only with subtitles)
  • And lastly: a game that is a little darker than the usual. Sorry, but I'm a little fed up with HOGs where there is such a happy ending that even the murderous ghosts end up happy and content. (Khm, Nightmares from the Deep 1,3) I wanted a game similar to The Dorian Gray Syndrome, and this really brings the same atmosphere :)
Weak aspects of the game:
  • Relatively low number of Hidden Object Scenes, if you're liking them, you'll definitely feel this as a negative.
  • Strange logic of item placement, even compared to other hidden object games - it's especially easy to notice as the story heavily leans on logic.
  • A little short? I've ~ 10 hours in the game (at least 1 hour afk) and this is 2 full gameplay without skipping dialogues
Pricewise: I've got this game in a 3-game bundle for 2$ maybe, for that price it was really worth it. I would say it's a good purchase if you can get it for 1-5 $/â¬ - the lower, the better for you :)

"Oh boy, I love back tracking!" - Said no one ever.

This game is a bit of an odd ball to me. While I enjoyed it better than the first installment I also can't pin point what exactly I liked about it. It's a classic hidden item game, nothing about it breaks any molds. You play Owen who is looking for his kidnapped daughter Olivia. It's fully voiced, beautiful artwork, good mini games and hidden item scenes and the story is decent...so I don't know why I feel meh about it.

The story is what I'll touch on first. I liked it, I liked the mystery element and the hunt for a killer. What I didn't like was waiting until the very end to know who did it. There were no clues or build up to the identity, the cops didn't figure out jack and I wasn't given enough info to try to figure it out on my own. Instead, the whole game is spent wandering about and doing puzzles oddly placed in people's homes while Owen shouts and acts melodramatic.

This isn't a huge issue but it really annoyed me, however, the general story itself was alright so I continued playing until the story played out. The mini games and hidden item scenes offered nice challenges and I was able to skip any that I wasn't in the mood to play since there are no achievements attached to this title to worry about. Gameplay was fine, except for the map. The map doesn't do much, you can't even use it to move around quicker. Instead be ready to back track a lot constantly.

The game slightly connects to first game, it's set before Brink of Consciousness: Dorian Gray Syndrome and one of the characters in this game is related to Oscar, the main antagonist in the first installment. Not going to say who, though it seems a bit tacked on as an after thought. You don't need to play one to play the other. Story is alright, the gameplay is enjoyable and the artwork is nice on the eyes. While not a favorite hidden item game of mine, I do recommend giving it a shot.
