About This Game

“TWINKLE STAR SPRITES ”, a competitive 2D top-down view scrolling shooter released on NEOGEO MVS in 1996, departs for new adventures on the Steam gaming platform!
Master your shots, and aim for the magical Twinkle Star”!

MAIN FEATURES

・ARCADE:
Pick up your favorite character among the 9 cute characters available in “CHARACTER MODE”, and play until you clear the game. Choose “STORY MODE”, and accompany “Lord Ran” in her adventures in search of the lost “Twinkle Star”.
Play locally in “VERSUS MODE” with a friend for intense and epic shooting battles!

・MULTIPLAYER:
Connect to Steam, and use the “INVITE A FRIEND” and “LOBBY” features to play against a buddy or challenge the best players worldwide!

・LEADERBOARDS:
Check your current rank & score in “TWINKLE STAR SPRITES” at any time, as well as those of your friends and other top shooters!

・SETTINGS:
Configure your language, video, and sound volume settings to your preference for the most pleasant “TWINKLE STAR SPRITES” experience ever.

** WILL NOT RUN ON Windows XP **

This is one of the best versus shoot'em ups ever made.

However, what you get with this re-release is an easy to use ROM+emulator bundle with some additional features. The game ROM is not even openly accessible so you cannot use it with the emulator of your chose and are stuck with the given configuration options and the given launcher.
Besides, the Neo-geo original version is not the absolute best version of the game (the Saturn version had more content).

The additionnal features are a leaderboard, multiplayer and achievements, but every one of them is flawed:
- leaderboards are very buggy (with scores superior to 9,999,999 which should be the upper limit, and I am 99% certain at least some of them are due to glitches, not cheaters)
- multiplayer does not have proper matchmaking but only a friend invite system. Fightcade does better than this and is free (free as in "free beer")
- the achievements are very buggy. They unlock at the wrong time or do not unlock at all. I have done everything needed for "All endings" twice and it did not unlock. Waste of time. I hope you are not a completionist.

tl;dr: this port is asking for too much money for almost no value added.
Alternative software such as MAME and Fightcade respectively provide superior emulation and multiplayer, given you own a dump of the original game.

Twinkle Star Sprites is a unique take on the shoot'em up genre. Players compete via split-screen to defeat enemies and outlast their opponent. While unable to attack each other directly, you can summon fireballs, minions or boss-monsters to overwhelm your opponent. You send more for performing combos, where defeating an enemy causes them to explode and defeat nearby enemies. Because of this mechanic proving more effective than shooting every enemy down individually or via bombs, there is a action-puzzle game feeling to the whole experience, trying to line up enemies for the largest possible combo before shooting them down.

The cute and colorful graphics and characters really give the game its charm. Each character also feels very unique in their attack arsenal and movement speeds, with decent balance among them, though some may take longer than others to get used to using or fighting against (with only the ultra-final boss possibly challenging the game balance as a secret character). For better or for worse, there is also this game's share of Engrish to explore, including having main character's name labeled as "Load Ran" (presumedly originally intended to be Lord Ran? *shrug*, it's well established to be Load Ran now)

However, there are some flaws with this game. There is currently a bug (which was also in the original) relating to scores, which is bad considering this game uses leaderboards. There are also some instances of the music stuttering or doubling up. Lag relating to overpopulating the screen with bullets/enemies has been reduced, which I would consider a good thing, but some devoted fans of the original may not approve.

For offline play, this may not be the superior release of the game, as the Dreamcast had all the the above issues addressed, including an option to change emulation speed (to reproduce lag found in the original), and an option to change music to a newer arranged selection. There was also a Saturn version which included one additional character. But those are way more expensive to purchase, and lack online play . . .

So fans who have previous versions may have to weigh whether there is much to gain from this release. But it is definitely a fun and unique cute'em up for fans of the genre, or those looking for a unique versus experience.

EDIT 2 (6/3/16): As of a few hours ago, the game got patched. The slowdown seems to be fixed and the video options don't reset anymore. The sound seems good too. I haven't been able to test the online multiplayer again yet, but even so I can now give this port a solid recommendation. I'll leave my pre-patch review here marked though, for posterity.

Now please patch Raiden Legacy to run at 54fps and we'll be golden, DotEmu.

ORIGINAL REVIEW (Pre-patch)
This is one of my favorite NeoGeo titles, and also one of my favorite shmups ever made thanks to its unique 1v1 take on the genre. I'm very familiar with the original title as well as its Dreamcast port, and I have to say, this one on the PC is trash.

Sound emulation is very poor, when booting up the game's character select screen the audio tends to loop over itself immediately. I ran into a bug the first time I tried to play the game where my stick just stopped working for no reason (luckily only in character selection and in single player, but that's still unacceptable). Online has very noticeable input lag, feels like you're sliding around everywhere, which makes it that much more difficult to dodge incoming fire. Speaking of dodging, there seems to be very little effort put into emulating slowdown when there's too many bullets on screen, making dodging heavy attacks far FAR more difficult. This was also an issue on the Dreamcast port, but they had the sense to include a NeoGeo mode in the options which restored this slowdown.

Another mark of shame upon you, DotEmu. I'd rather play this on my hacked NeoGeo X.

EDIT: I'd like to point out that the emulation of the Humble Bundle version of this game, released just a few months ago also by DotEmu, is far superior. What happened!?
