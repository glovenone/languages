A fun side scroller beat em up

Title: The Ables: Freepoint High
Genre: Platformer, Sidescroller, Beat Em Up
Developer/publisher: Qiwi Games LCC


About

The Ables: Freepoint High is a side scroller, beat em up platformer that throws you back to the time where such games were the standard. The game has a great soundtrack and has a beautiful artstyle going for it. Not all of the levels have been finished yet, but so far I'm really enjoying what they gave us

Story

In this world based on the best-selling YA novel The Ables, by Jeremy Scott, you play as a powerful super hero known as an "Absorber'' You can collect and master many different super powers to save your friends from the mysterious "Believers" who have attacked your town!" You get to choose between 2 characters, Alex and Emily. You have to save your town with a wide variety of powers you get along the way.

Gameplay

The gameplay consists of platforming, boss battles and beating up enemies with the powers you collect and absorb. As I already said. The game is a sidescroller that has old school Metroid-like gameplay. For example, the first level consists of platforming action where you shoot your enemies with fireballs. The boss battle that follows is surprisingly challenging!
After you finish the first level, you get to choose between four levels, with each having a different student to rescue. Where the gameplay really shines, though, is the fact that you have so many powers at your disposal to annihilate your enemies with.

Graphics & Sound

The game has a beautiful artstyle, a great soundtrack and some neat 3D levels. Which means you move left and right, up and down like most sidescrollers do. Its nothing special for these days standard but the graphics are fine for a sidescroller like this. The sound effects are well made.

PROS
  • Great soundtrack
  • Variety in powers, I would not be surprised if more are coming!
  • Thrilling boss battles
  • Various enemy types
  • Old school platforming

CONS
  • Right now, you can only play three more missions after the first one. The other four are still in development
  • Some bugs and glitches
  • Some of the powers are a bit annoying to control

Verdict

This game had exactly what I wanted! It's challenging, fun, and has solid gameplay! Since it's still in early access there are some bugs that need fixing, but they do not impact the gameplay much. If you want the full game, you should wait until they finish the other levels. However, I recommend it for sidescrolling and Jeremy Scott fans. It is a fast-paced game with great potential. I had fun playing this game, and that is what games are for, right? $9.99 is a fair price for what it has to offer.
Final verdict: 8/10 The soundtrack: 10/10 \(^^)/

A fun side scroller beat em up

Title: The Ables: Freepoint High
Genre: Platformer, Sidescroller, Beat Em Up
Developer/publisher: Qiwi Games LCC


About

The Ables: Freepoint High is a side scroller, beat em up platformer that throws you back to the time where such games were the standard. The game has a great soundtrack and has a beautiful artstyle going for it. Not all of the levels have been finished yet, but so far I'm really enjoying what they gave us

Story

In this world based on the best-selling YA novel The Ables, by Jeremy Scott, you play as a powerful super hero known as an "Absorber'' You can collect and master many different super powers to save your friends from the mysterious "Believers" who have attacked your town!" You get to choose between 2 characters, Alex and Emily. You have to save your town with a wide variety of powers you get along the way.

Gameplay

The gameplay consists of platforming, boss battles and beating up enemies with the powers you collect and absorb. As I already said. The game is a sidescroller that has old school Metroid-like gameplay. For example, the first level consists of platforming action where you shoot your enemies with fireballs. The boss battle that follows is surprisingly challenging!
After you finish the first level, you get to choose between four levels, with each having a different student to rescue. Where the gameplay really shines, though, is the fact that you have so many powers at your disposal to annihilate your enemies with.

Graphics & Sound

The game has a beautiful artstyle, a great soundtrack and some neat 3D levels. Which means you move left and right, up and down like most sidescrollers do. Its nothing special for these days standard but the graphics are fine for a sidescroller like this. The sound effects are well made.

PROS
  • Great soundtrack
  • Variety in powers, I would not be surprised if more are coming!
  • Thrilling boss battles
  • Various enemy types
  • Old school platforming

CONS
  • Right now, you can only play three more missions after the first one. The other four are still in development
  • Some bugs and glitches
  • Some of the powers are a bit annoying to control

Verdict

This game had exactly what I wanted! It's challenging, fun, and has solid gameplay! Since it's still in early access there are some bugs that need fixing, but they do not impact the gameplay much. If you want the full game, you should wait until they finish the other levels. However, I recommend it for sidescrolling and Jeremy Scott fans. It is a fast-paced game with great potential. I had fun playing this game, and that is what games are for, right? $9.99 is a fair price for what it has to offer.
Final verdict: 8/10 The soundtrack: 10/10 \(^^)/
