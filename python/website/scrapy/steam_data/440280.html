Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We chose early Early Access to promote and grow a community around Overpower that will help shape the future of the game through feedback, participation, and suggestions. As a small team that is self publishing we have a great opportunity to quickly iterate and make changes based on the feedback players give. Reach out and talk to us and we'll happily respond to everyone as directly as possible.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Overpower will be in Early Access until it is feature complete. We are targeting a 4-6 month window for balancing, supporting, and adding additional content to the game.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of the game will have more balanced and content complete game modes. We will be adding features on a per class basis based on the feedback of the players. As well as creating new maps and modes based on what the community is enjoying and wanting to see more of. We also will be adding out of game RPG style account progression which will give more dimension to the gameplay. These systems are subject to change based on community feedback.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Overpower is currently in a Beta state with 9 levels, 4 classes, and 5 game modes. The gameplay is well rounded and offers players enough substance to experience the combat. Secondary systems such as UI, Vfx, sound design, and game modes require additional polish. Lower end systems could run into potential problems running the game.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Overpower will be priced lower during Early Access. We want to grow the community and expand upon the game with player feedback. When the game becomes more established and full featured we plan to raise the price to match.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Involving the community is our primary goal with an Early Access Release. We will be doing weekly scheduled playtests with the developers to give all players an opportunity to hang out with the devs and give us direct feedback. More information on the scheduled tests can be found here on our website.

We also look for direct playerfeedback from our Forum, Subreddit, Direct Contact, or Steam Forums. Through a combination of updates, public playtests, and Twitch development streams we will be interacting with our players on a regular basis.”

Update on the Big Patch #2

Hey there again! We just released another patch, a lot of the systems we've been discussing are falling into place (finally!)

From a combat perspective, every ability and VFX has been upgraded and balanced.

New Pyro Bow
New Magic Burst Staff
New Blood Seeker Staff
New Shadowbolt Assassin Gem

We've introduced a new Main Menu / Player Stats / Leaderboard Screens.



Player Stats, will give you a breakdown of all your stats by class, and combined.



The Leaderboard pulls your stats which are updated at the end of each match and uses them give you a score. The players are ranked by score and given a title accordingly.



The highest rank Champion and the rest of the titles are distributed according from General, Captain, Officer, Sergeant and Recruit. These titles are updated twice a day.

We're working out some bugs still with the system and will be tuning the formula based on player feedback and experience.

We're also dropping the price of the game to $8.99, we're just waiting on Steam approval. We'll be making all Red / Blue skins from Armory free, and as we iron out the bugs in the next few days we'll announce an official patch date, run a Steam Visibility round, and an additional discount on the game.

We'll have more information on that patch date next week. We'll also have a table at EVO Las Vegas on July 15-16th. If anyone is attending definitely stop by and say hi!

Thanks everyone,

About This Game

Overpower is a class-based fantasy combat game. Inspired by the pick-up and play nature of a shooter combined with epic RPG style class based PvP. Overpower puts a skill based medieval twist on traditional arena games, putting players immediately into the action! Huge damage combos, low cooldowns, and split second decision making allows players to overpower their enemies no matter what they're up against!

Key Features:
  • Skill Based Combat inspired by Counter - Strike, Quake, and the MOBA PvP genre!
  • A variety of class abilities and weapons to customize your unique playstyle.
  • Brand new fantasy twists on classic game modes create new gameplay experiences.
  • 9 Exciting Battle Arenas to destroy your enemies in!
  • 5 different Game Modes keep gameplay fast and refreshing.
  • 4 Classes to choose from with plans to add many more!
  • Both Male and Female Playable Genders included.

Steamified are proud to welcome Hydrant Games to the Steamified family with the feature of their debut game, Overpower. The developers are a two man team who made this game, completely unfunded, out of the sheer love and dedication to RPG games and PvP combat. They draw their inspirations from their favorite childhood games that include Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy III, Secret of Evermore, and Secret of Mana. Further inspiration has been born from the MMO scene including EverQuest, Final Fantasy XI, and the indomitable World of Warcraft. The final piÃ¨ce de rÃ©sistance comes from their affection of shooters such as Counter-Strike, Quake and Unreal Tournament.

Having gained an appreciation of the knowledge and passion of gaming that Adam, a career graphic artist, and John, an experienced programmer, have and having similar experiences myself I was excited to play their game. The ideology that they have for their game is something that a significant number of players in MMO communities have wanted to play for many years. Therefore I can argue that there is a sizable target player base for the game. It would be up to the execution and the marketing power of the small team to realize their achievement. Both are vitally important to the success of a game such as this given that there have been great multiplayer games die because of a lack of a community and it goes without saying that bad games die very quickly unless they become cult classic because of how poor they are.

Unfortunately, the servers were almost completely empty when I went to play the game. Not a good sign. On the very fortunate bonus side, however, the devs have clearly understood that garnering a player base especially early on was going to be a difficult achievement so they created bots so that any single player could log onto a server and immediately get into a game. Unlike other games that I have recently played that have implemented bots the bots in this game are relatively intelligent to the point of being tough to kill. There are times where their intellect leaves a little bit to be desired but this was rare in my experience. Also, unfortunately, the one time that I got into a game with another player I noticed him transport from one end of the arena to the other just as I was about to kill them. Given that we were the same class and that there is no ability that does this all I can put it down to is hacking. A disappointing experience.

In regards to the servers I am thankful that they are another developer who have decided that an Australian server is a wise investment. Having a healthy ping is of vital importance in this type of game so possessing the ability to play locally is important given that timing and aiming are vital. There appears to be local servers around the world for that matter. This inspires me to play the skill and talent based games much more than if I were forced to play on servers that have a constant 250+ ping because of the location.

There are a number of different game modes but the servers that I have played on â the official servers â have both been set to have team deathmatch where once you die you have to wait until the end of the battle to be able to play again. I didnât stick around long enough to see if these servers change from game type to game type or whether they are officially stuck at their default setting but I would be hoping that they are on some type of rotation.

As someone who played warrior Iâd love to see our whirling ability set to a hotkey rather than holding the button down to execute the move. Given that other classes have easier to trigger skills that are better than the warriorâs this puts the warrior class at a distinct disadvantage before play even begins. That said friends have also complained that other classes are either overpowered or underpowered so balance seems to be an issue that needs an overhaul. However, it is easy to forget that the game is in early access and balance issues are known to plague early access multiplayer titles so I am certain that the dev team will improve this before retail release.

The game has yet to be truly Steamified. Achievements and trading cards need to be added to encourage players to purchase the game and until they are I believe that the game will struggle to garner much of a player base.

There is an online marketplace that sells you cosmetics and similar but there is no need to purchase from it unless youâre a fan of the game and want to put extra money into the game. I do not believe that there is anything in the store that can be seen as giving a player a gameplay bonus aside from appearance.

Overpower is a great concept that needs the continued work from the dev team who are doing just that. It may be a little bit overpriced for what youâre getting at this stage but it has the potential of delivering a valued and enjoyable game by the time that it leaves early access.

Early Access Review
Posted: 21 April
First off let me say that the game has amazing potential and I really hope it takes that potential and runs with it to create the fun, rewarding game I know it can be.

Pro's:
1. Skill Based Combat : Aiming/Timing is key in this game especially when playing a ranged base character. There is plenty of room for players to outplay one another and I love that and im sure plenty of other pvp junkies do too. Nothing like going in a fight with low hp and out playing a full hp player.

2. Variety: There are 4 different classes to choose from, with plans to add more in the future. Each class has a choice of a few weapons each with a variety of effects from slowing people with auto attacks to burning them over time etc... On top of each class having different weapons they each have the ability to choose two skills out of a list of a couple of skills adding a huge variety of gameplay styles mixing and matching skills and weapons.

3. Gameplay Modes/Map Variety: There are several modes to choose from, ranging from a control point to last man standing and others. There also is a handfull of unique maps to play on with a nice unique medieval twist.

Con's: *Note this is a Early Access game so I understand bugs and whatnot are to be expected, just some thoughts on what i feel needs some love*

1. Bot AI can use a little bit of work, occasionally i've seen them get stuck or fall off maps. Also they can sometimes take a while to find each other when it comes down to only bots left. For the most part though they aren't too bad and do occasional suprise me with some nasty combos

2. Balancing: I know its early access and I would love to see a few tweaks to certain skills etc... Not to worried about this the devs seem very active and open to feedback and im sure in due time well get some balance changes. But for the most part I seem to be able to kill any of the other classes with any class.

So overall I think this game has HUGE potential and look forward to seeing how it develops over the course of its early access cycle. I can not wait to play ranked games against other players and climb the leaderboards or level my class to earn class based rewards, features i've heard that will eventually be coming our way. I'd give the game a solid 8/10 with potential to be a 10/10 as it progresses. This game truly does have the potential to be a pvp junkies paradise!

Steamified are proud to welcome Hydrant Games to the Steamified family with the feature of their debut game, Overpower. The developers are a two man team who made this game, completely unfunded, out of the sheer love and dedication to RPG games and PvP combat. They draw their inspirations from their favorite childhood games that include Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy III, Secret of Evermore, and Secret of Mana. Further inspiration has been born from the MMO scene including EverQuest, Final Fantasy XI, and the indomitable World of Warcraft. The final piÃ¨ce de rÃ©sistance comes from their affection of shooters such as Counter-Strike, Quake and Unreal Tournament.

Having gained an appreciation of the knowledge and passion of gaming that Adam, a career graphic artist, and John, an experienced programmer, have and having similar experiences myself I was excited to play their game. The ideology that they have for their game is something that a significant number of players in MMO communities have wanted to play for many years. Therefore I can argue that there is a sizable target player base for the game. It would be up to the execution and the marketing power of the small team to realize their achievement. Both are vitally important to the success of a game such as this given that there have been great multiplayer games die because of a lack of a community and it goes without saying that bad games die very quickly unless they become cult classic because of how poor they are.

Unfortunately, the servers were almost completely empty when I went to play the game. Not a good sign. On the very fortunate bonus side, however, the devs have clearly understood that garnering a player base especially early on was going to be a difficult achievement so they created bots so that any single player could log onto a server and immediately get into a game. Unlike other games that I have recently played that have implemented bots the bots in this game are relatively intelligent to the point of being tough to kill. There are times where their intellect leaves a little bit to be desired but this was rare in my experience. Also, unfortunately, the one time that I got into a game with another player I noticed him transport from one end of the arena to the other just as I was about to kill them. Given that we were the same class and that there is no ability that does this all I can put it down to is hacking. A disappointing experience.

In regards to the servers I am thankful that they are another developer who have decided that an Australian server is a wise investment. Having a healthy ping is of vital importance in this type of game so possessing the ability to play locally is important given that timing and aiming are vital. There appears to be local servers around the world for that matter. This inspires me to play the skill and talent based games much more than if I were forced to play on servers that have a constant 250+ ping because of the location.

There are a number of different game modes but the servers that I have played on â the official servers â have both been set to have team deathmatch where once you die you have to wait until the end of the battle to be able to play again. I didnât stick around long enough to see if these servers change from game type to game type or whether they are officially stuck at their default setting but I would be hoping that they are on some type of rotation.

Given that other classes have easier to trigger skills that are better than the warriorâs this puts the warrior class at a distinct disadvantage before play even begins. That said friends have also complained that other classes are either overpowered or underpowered so balance seems to be an issue that needs an overhaul. However, it is easy to forget that the game is in early access and balance issues are known to plague early access multiplayer titles so I am certain that the dev team will improve this before retail release.

The game has yet to be truly Steamified. Achievements and trading cards need to be added to encourage players to purchase the game and until they are I believe that the game will struggle to garner much of a player base.

SUMMARY & FINAL RATING

I tried the classes. I had the most fun with the archer. The quick shot skill is overpowered. Currently the game feels unbalanced - not the kind of asymmetrical balance you'd expect in a game like this (rock paper scissors is an example of asymmetrical balance where you have a strength and a weakness that is "class" based). Hopefully this balance issue is worked on as the game becomes more complete. Another problem I faced was a lack of other players. I tried this game at around 11am central time (GMT -6) on a Saturday and did not see another player in an hour of gamplay. The game will automatically populate the servers with bots allowing "solo" play.

For a finished game the price might be a good one. When trying to fund a project it might be necessary to evaluate if 3 people might buy it at $5 USD versus 0-1 at $15. Having active players on is key to the success of a PVP game
