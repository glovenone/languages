About This Game

People of Animalville got a carting fever and you must help one of them reach for the championship. Take the wheel, choose a kart, decide which character from 'Teddy Floppy Ear’ universe you want, and start the wild ride! Compete all day long on nightly and daily tracks that span around the beautiful and colorful countryside. Play in one of many modes including Time Attack and Racing Season. Use power ups to gain advantage or slow down your opponents. Improve your vehicle’s appearance by tuning.

Features:

✔ Split Screen multi-player competition
✔ Five daily and five nightly tracks set in the different parts of Animalville
✔ Ten kart models based on the popular cars from behind the "iron curtain"
✔ Five game modes for diverse experience
✔ Four levels of difficulty
✔ Thirty hats to change your appearance
✔ Collectable power-ups and attacks during the race
✔ Tuning to improve kart appearance
✔ Vehicles such as a unique Syrena Sport or… a Tractor
✔ Hours of entertainment included
✔ Over a dozen of achievements

Game modes
SINGLE RACE: You just want to have a quickie? Choose a track, kart and a character, to instantly start the racing joy.
TIME ATTACK: In mood for a bigger challenge? Race against time or yourself in this classic mode.
COLLECTOR'S SLALOM: It's the best place to learn the tracks. The stars are placed on the optimum path so you can learn the curves and shortcuts. Finish the race before the time ends and collect as many stars lying on your way as you can.
RACING SEASON: Take part in a rally season. Compete on yet unlocked tracks and win a place on the podium to advance to higher and more difficult league.
FLOPPY CUP: Fight for a cup in a playoff 1 – on – 1 races. Defeat all your opponents to advance.

tl;dr
A worth-while entry in the Kart Racer genre that is entirely over-looked because it's a Floppy Ear title! Fun for all ages and a Buy for Kart Racing enthusiasts.

full review.
Teddy Floppy Ear - The Race is a cute, little Kart Racer that has more going on than you can shake a stick at. It has several racing modes, several kart performance upgrades, plenty of unlockables from cars to characters, several difficulty levels to accomodate racers of all skill-levels and some beautiful and cartoony courses with enough variety to keep it interesting. Best of all, it has a surprisingly solid sense of speed, which is core to any good racing game.

It's unfortunate that this game doesn't get a fair chance inspite of it's lineage; from the perspective of a single-player Kart Racer, it's pretty darn good.

There are, however, some minor problems:

First: the wonky controls.
They are soft and slippery. They can almost feel broken when you first start playing. But... once you adapt to the steering mechanics and adjust your driving style, you will find yourself speeding around corners and passing your competitors with ease and glee. The learning curve for me was about 45-minutes of race-time.

Second: The weird music.
And as far as the music goes, it's weird and a total mismatch for what is happening on-screen. Turn it off in the Settings and play your own up-tempo music of choice. I played EDM and Trance for this game and it kicked up the atmosphere to eleven.

Once you disable the weirdly mismatched music and spend some time learning how to control your racer... it becomes really fun!

It's definitely no Sonic Racer, but, it's certainly better and more fun than, the albeit more polished, F1 Race All-stars. Of all the Kart Racers on Steam, it's a worth-while one.

At full price (4,99 at the time of this review), I would recommend this for Kart Racing enthusiasts. However, if you enjoy Kart Racing and are the type of gamer who is willing to put a bit of time into learning how to steer your kart, then this is an easy Buy, especially if you get it on sale.

pros.
+ Cute pseudo-paper-cutout graphics style
+ Well-designed tracks, including shortcuts
+ Beautifully cartoony tracks
+ Fun and responsive power-ups and attacks
+ Day and night tracks
+ Good AI competitors
+ Solid sense of speed
+ Split-screen, local multiplayer (I didn't test this)
+ Many unlockables (tracks, cars, characters, etc)
+ Kart performance upgrades (tires, exhaust, paint jobs, etc)
+ Full 1080P Support
+ Full Controller Support

cons.
- Music is just terrible (turn it off and play your own)
- Kart controls are sloppy, but fun once you adapt
- No online multiplayer support
- Limited graphics settings

This game is appropriate for all ages and skill-levels.

The music on the other hand.. i am indifferent to it, it isn't bad, yet it isn't great. I'd recommend playing your own stuff after a while.

I have only played about 15 minutes, but i must say i am pleasantly surprised by the gameplay itself. Kudos to the devs on a job well done.

Delightful racing game similar to Mario Kart series. Likable characters, easy interface, nice variety of race types, etc.

The game suffers from a major flaw. It is WAY too hard for its intended audience which is children. The NPC characters are too unforgiving of mistakes and children will be frustrated. At higher difficulties and tracks, some of the races are very difficult. I am a seasoned Mario Kart player and even I have difficulty winning some races or time trials. I’m guessing it would be near impossible for younger kids, especially if they have not been brought up on keyboard only controls (see below).

99% of the races are determined by fractions of second (which makes it even odder why the developers chose to use whole second rather than hundredths), often with racers crossing the finish line at the same time.

I found the controls simple unlike many other reviewers. You basically only use A W D and the space bar with an occasional S key thrown in when you screw up and have to back up…not sure what all the fuss is. I think many Steam users are console immigrants or are not old school keyboarders (read: spoiled). The controls are fine the way they are.

While the game has cute elements and is very enjoyable, overall I cannot recommend the game as it will not appeal to its target audience.

Cute small cart racer for beginners.

There are 6 well-designed tracks (some have a night mode, most have a shortcut), some racing modes (single race with 4 difficulties, time trial, cone collecting, and two different tournament modes, respectively), no performance tuning, street features such as speedup arrows, a slow sand patch and a wet patch that wets your tires so that your next turn is harder to control. There are also a bunch of power-ups that speed you up or the others down (no weapons more violent than a box of thumbtacks, though). There's no story. All in all it's fairly varied.

You get two keyboard setups and you can also use one Xbox controller, which unfortunately is also all you get for split-screen. I'm calling this a beginners game because the AI and the difficulty of the challenges are no match for more experienced players once you've gotten the hang of the game (do have a look at the help before you start). Physics are a bit strange in that you get rubber on the road long before you can hear a tire squeal, and I turned the music off after a while, but all in all it handles predictably enough for cart game physics.

I'm approximately halfway through the game, and to me it feels casual and I'm happy I didn't spend any money on it. But given the variety of race modes and tracks, it'd be a good game for a budding racing gamer - and that's precisely who it's aimed at. Recommended.
