About This Game

... 300 years after Aziraal has been banished, a brother and sister are drawn into the conflict which has flared up between the Orcs and the free world. Kyra, the hero's younger sister, suddenly disappears in mysterious circumstances.

«...If my family really belongs to the chosen ones, why then have we always been as poor as church mice? I have never given much credence to all the old stories about a relict in our family's possession - one which supposedly bannedAziraal, the god of the Orcs - they say that only those ofmy bloodwere chosen to safeguard the secret.
'Tis nothing but nonsense methinks – however, my sister Kyra always listened eagerly to the stories of the old ones... but she vanished several months ago... »

To all intents and purposes you're an unscrupulous bounty hunter and mercenary - but the search for your sister takes you back to your roots.

At the beginning of your epic adventure, a mercenary task takes you to the far north - but you're also following up a mysterious lead at the same time - the first clue you've been given since Kyra's disappearance. You get a shock during a meeting with the delegates of a dark Brotherhood - your sister's kidnappers are indeed after your family's relict. Whether there's any truth in your family being chosen ones or not, the others obviously believe it - and if you ever want to see Kyra alive again, you'll have to act swiftly...

  • Includes the original Two Worlds, plus the add-on Tainted Blood
  • OVER 90 NEW QUESTS: Journey even deeper into the world of Antaloor with over 90 new quests and eight new multiplayer maps
  • Soundtrack by Grammy Award Winner Harold Faltermeyer

FEATURES:

  • STUPENDOUS LANDSCAPES: in a fantasy world where every inhabitant leads a separate dynamic life with realistic daily routines, your deeds will determine the fate of the strife-torn land of Antaloor.
  • PLAYING FREEDOM: Two Worlds is not for those who don't like making decisions, because you'll often have to take one side or the other  an intelligent, networked reputation system for guilds and secret societies makes sure of that.
  • KNIGHT, THIEF, MAGE OR ALCHEMIST: spectacular, dynamically-choreographed fights on foot or at full gallop. An absorbing alchemy system for creating individual potions and power-ups provides diversification - and carrying out break-ins in Sneak mode will have your nerves jangling! If this still isn't enough, try the sophisticated magic system...
  • INTUITIVE INTERFACE AND INDIVIDUAL DEVELOPMENT OF ATTRIBUTES: you can experiment with all the character attributes and develop your skills as and when you like. An elaborate inventory system ensures that you always have a clear overview  no matter how hot the action gets.
  • MASSIVE MULTIPLAYER INCLUDED: bustling towns are the trading centers and meeting points for several hundred players. Self-created characters fight gripping PvsP duels in Arena mode  or solve a series of exciting quests as allies.
  • UPDATED GAME ENGINE: The brand new 1.6 engine update now included!

This is quite possibly my all time favorite game. I've now bought it for myself 3 different times (Once on xbox, once in disk form for the PC, and once on steam), and even gifted several copies on Reddit. It is astoundingly deep, and has some neat stacking mechanics that I thoroughly enjoyed. However, since I don't want someone reading this review, getting the game and going in blind, I don't want to sugarcoat it. The first 30 minutes are miserable. Maybe even the first couple hours. It has possibly the worst first impression of any game I've ever played. The animations aren't particularly smooth, giving it a pretty cheap feeling, the graphics aren't great, even for 2009 (though the scale is huge), the voice acting is atrocious, and there are parts of the game that they get incredibly wrong that seem like simple mechanics (Horse Riding and Archery) - and yet, if you give it a few hours, I think it's brilliance shines through, and would hook anyone that likes the genre.

There are tons of different environments - You've got your standard medieval knights, forests, swamps, caves and dungeons. There's a Samurai land with giant starship troopers like bugs. Creepy cults and necromancy. Desert nomad lands, and dune like worms. Cities are massive, and you can go in almost every house, with worthwhile loot in most of them. You can create permanent stat gain potions to a ridiculous degree allowing anyone that wants to become a demigod to do so. Finding extra copies of the same spell don't just become useless inventory items, each new copy you find stacks on previous ones making them stronger, in fact you can do the same with items in the game. You could go around killing the easiest enemies in the game, stacking hundreds of their clubs, and have a decent weapon for a while just from that. Speaking of items, the sheer amount of them is crazy. Where there might be 20 unique weapons in Skyrim there are hundreds in this game, and hundreds of armor items as well. You can look like a samurai warrior, a monk, an evil wizard or a menacing black knight.

I have never found a game like this (even the sequel in my opinion, while fun, pales in comparison), it is really incredible, you just have to struggle through a bit of muck before it will shine, but I can guarantee if you wade through it, it's a game you wont forget.

Okay, so I have to start my review by admitting I really enjoyed my time playing Two Worlds. As others have said, the game is fairly glitchy, but as a long-time gamer, glitches don't ruin things for me as long as they are consistent. What you need to know primarily is that tutorial is brief and very basic. This game needs your close attention to understand it. The first time I play I think there were major flaws and bad designs but with a second and third closer careful looks I understand that I was wrong. Once you understand how things work it is where you really get addicted. I am sure that most negative reviews comes from hasty gamers. I do not blame them, a modern game needs to have a proper tutorial, a complex game a large tutorial, this game most likely relies on the manual.
Let’s get on details(the bad stuff at the end):
The character: You only play a male human character, no females and no races. That’s it. Also your starting character is fixed, fixed attributes and fixed starting skills. You cannot start with point pool and boost what you like. However the starting skills are the basic ones and at level 1 of 10. You can level up and increase any you like. All starting skills are all you need to do all essential things and secondary actions. For example you have lock pick 1 and you can lock pick and also increase it for a better chance. You do not have alchemy skill but you can still use alchemy. Finally, playing the game and visit villages and towns will allow you to meet trainers that will teach you the level 1 of all skills eventually, so there is no skill point cost at all for level 1 skill. If for example you want to advance alchemy or water magic you do not start with, all you need is to find the appropriate trainers to get the 1st skill point by paying some money. While levelling you get 5 attribute points and one or two skill points, usually one. You may also get one skill point before you level up but I have not yet figure out when, the game has many good secrets. You can also permanently increase attributes using alchemy even without skill 1, if you find the proper ingredients. In other words the game is rewarding for the good and smart player offering more than the standard gain experience and fixed level up procedure. Two level 10 characters for example may be different, one may be even 40% stronger from extra attributes and skills alone.
The gear and items: Items are much like diablo, leveled items and many auxiliary items, consumables and especially ingredients. The only disadvantage here if it is one is that there are too many items. Not just leveled, too many items. Perhaps the greatest variety on gear I have ever seen. Ready consumables are just what is needed. Ingredients are plenty and not only herbs but also parts from animals and various minerals. There are also gems as consumables to add different types of damage to weapons, traps to set, spell cards and spell boosters and few standard items like the teleport stone and quest items.
One unique gameplay element here is that you can enhance any gear item, weapon or armor with another piece of item of the same type. Items have a special attribute called ‘class’ which is usually one. If you have a short sword for example and find another one you can drag and drop it one another and you will now have a class 2 short sword which of course is a little better, doing more damage in that case. This is a very good thing and very useful, as it allows you to use trivial equipment to avoid buying everything from the store at every couple of levels or so, or live and die by drop chance alone. For example I used a Grom Shield that happened to be a very common item groms carry and drop so I end up finding about 30 or so, effectively had a class 30 simple grom shield with a parry number high enough to keep me up to level 10 or so. Then there were better shields to buy and enemies no longer often carry that shield, so it was time to change. Spell cards also stack. Add a second and third and so on card of the same spell and the spell becomes stronger, requiring more mana however. Finally gems can be placed on a weapon for extra damage and you can put as many as you like as long as they are of the same type, e.g. rubies that add fire damage.
Money and trading: Surprisingly money are always needed here, and the game offers a fair amount of money. The trading is active to this game. You always earn cash and you always have reasons to spent, and not on consumables exclusively. I’d rather say you may spent for making your hero more powerful. Enemies either drop inventory with a fair amount of gold and random items or a single standard automatic pickup, like the wolf drops a wolf heart which is an ingredient. Anything may drop but specific items have better sources to be harvested and animals and beast always drop standard ingredient item instead of inventory. All except single pickups are level depended, chests, enemy inventories and shops. You are bored looking for ingredients that increase your stats permanently? No problem, pay some attention to mage shops and after a certain level you definitely come across to buy.
Enemies: Good variety of enemies, large size enemies are really deadly and not level depended, leveled enemies are logical like bandits; they got stronger but not as much as you.
Classes: The 3 archetypes work well, fighter, mage and archer/thief that is. Hybrids also work well. There is depth on each single archetype, fighting style and weapons for fighters, magic schools for mages and lots of choices to master for an archer/thief.
The story is interesting, the world is very good at least. The soundtrack is almost perfect.

Now the bad stuff:
The back-jump. Honestly I hated that. In my opinion it ruins melee. I do not even assigned it to a button. The game is much better without. Again, my opinion.
The dialogues. Not sure what English dialect that is but it is strange until you get used to it. Also some times are almost childish, some other times are very mature. Your hero speaks quite well though.
The NPC characters. Quite average.
The spells. They are lots and great and come in form of cards that stack and extra spell booster cards (more damage, less mana etch). However you may have 3 spells with booster cards (that also stack), the others need some special way to be placed on your custom hot bar, first on the spell amulet and then on the hot bar. No big deal but somewhat annoying.
