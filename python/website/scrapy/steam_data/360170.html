Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“When we look back at the original How to Survive we realize that we had followed a similar path. We released the game and then we were able to gather feedback from the community and work to deliver.
If we had had access to a community early in the development process most likely we could have identified what players felt was most important and implementing it in a faster and most effective manner. We also found out that some of the more ambitious features players highlighted required a whole new development to be implemented so… here we are working on How to Survive 2.
We think of Early Access as a tool to keep that relationship with the community going. Let’s do it.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Not forever! Which may not sound very precise but it’s a statement not every game on Early Access makes. Putting a date is risky because unexpected things might happen but we have pretty clear that we want to finish Early Access in 2016. But it will also depend upon your response and feedback.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“UPDATED TO APRIL 2016

We did launch Early Access with the basic gameplay structure laid out and functioning, you were able to create playable characters and specialize them, get together with friends, build a camp, defend it and go on quests and raids facing a variety of natural and unnatural threats.

So far WE HAVE ADDED TONS of stuff! Many customization options for male and female characters, new game modes such as Elite Quests and Zombie Horde Attacks, more Coop options such as Local Coop as requested by community, more skills to learn, more workshops, more parts, tools and weapons and crafting recipes, new NPCs, etc.
We also brought back a few things as requested by players: night zombies, finishing moves, Kovac tutorial videos (OH, YES!).
So the game, in a few months, is already looking pretty different. How different from the final game will it be? We don’t know because we are following the community lead, it’s ideas and suggestions. We just know it will be awesome.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“UPDATED TO APRIL 2016

The game is fully playable and fleshed out, currently you can find:
• Multiplayer! 4 players simultaneously.
o You can create your private camp and invite up to 16 friends or join their camp.
o You can also create a public camp, manage the admin rights of your community and welcome all the survivors on earth!
o Local Coop - 4 players on the same computer.
• Open world, sandbox play-over 43 different raids among city and bayou.
• Over 20+ hours of gore action gameplay and a lot more to come.
o Zombies with varied looks and behavior.
o Zombie fauna!
• Heavily customizable male and female characters (i.e., physical appearance and clothes).
• An impressive set of bloodbath finishing moves.
• Up to 29 skills to unlock, for the character, workshops or the camp.
• Leveling capped at 35.
• Avatar's physiological components to manage (thirst/hunger/stamina)
• Discover and gather up to 100 different resources that will allow you t craft up to 174 different recipes.
• Heavy physics-based objects to move, alone or with one or several partners.
• Unlock cars, doors, chests, etc.- careful what you open as a car alarm can trigger hordes of enemies!
• Over 46 unique weapons + up to 17 modules to mod these weapons (suppressors, scopes, reinforcements, bow stabilizers, magazine extensions, etc.)
• 3 full armor sets are also available to equip and protect your avatar
• 6 workshops to build and upgrade + various traps and defense structures (fences, watch towers)

We have a lot to add and are looking forward to hearing your suggestions.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We want to build the best game possible for you. We have realized that we may need to adjust the price while in development. That said, we will try to keep it fair and we will be part of Sales and Discounts during this period.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We have many profiles were you guys can contact us via http://www.facebook.com/HowToSurviveTheGame or @How2SurviveGame, but as we did in the past, we’ll keep an eye on the community hub and, eventually, we will run polls and surveys for players to participate. So if you want to help Kovac craft the new How to Survive adventure… come join us!”

About This Game

Several years have passed since the original How to Survive events on Los Riscos’ archipelago. Now the local infection has turned into a worldwide pandemic. Everyone around the world, groups of people or lone wolves, try to survive one more day among the infected, attempting to recover a semblance of normal life. You find yourself in Louisiana and your survival chance starts by building a safe camp and becoming a skilled survivalist.
Find water, food and shelter and talk to the unconventional locals to make your way around.

An eccentric masked stranger offers you help? Sure, sounds way better than zombies!

Well, in comparison to the first How to Survive, this one shows a huge development, it's still in early access and new contents are being made but the game so far is incredible fun to play with.

You can build things like a trunk to act as a settlement, to store things there to be used on upgrades or even to craft others items such as bandages or even pistols. Players can contribute with their spare EXP to level it up, a player can't level themselves any higher than their settlement, meaning that the game will request a cooperation, a maximum of 4 players can help each other on missions that can be changed to be harder or easier, the higher the difficulty, the more EXP they will give.

Players will need to eat and drink to survive, a kitchen allows them to cook better food, allowing for a better recovery of those needs, if a player is thirsty they will face longer times to aim at the enemy and if they are hungry they will have a hard time to hit an enemy strong enough to give a good damage.

The enemies are no different, zombies that wants to kill you, but the nice thing is, a normal zombie is only normal until you provoke them, a zombie can be either slow or fast, very fast, and you can find yourself in deep trouble if a fast one starts to run at you and you are still reloading your rifle or you crossbow. Oh, and don't forget that your weapons produces loud sounds when you shoot, so use your rifle/pistol wisely, in a time you won't bother too much with that, firepower is needed to kill those zombies, just make sure to know your enemy first.

Want bullets? Craft them with gunpowder and lead pellets. Want some good food? Cook them at the kitchen using foods you find on your quest, mixing those poor quality foods to make a high quality one!

How to Survive 2 is, so far, nice and comfortable to play, so far the game proved to be interesting as you can defend your base with barricades and even traps, because killing zombies with bullets and arrows isn't enough when you can put some zombies heads at some good spike traps. Remember, this review is being made at the early access so things can change, but so far so good as they say, or Kovac says...

Summary

I'm not entirely sure why people dislike this game. If it was before the SWAT update, maybe, but I've been having an absolute blast with this. I played the first game a bit and I had some fun with it but I never really got into it. Fortunately for me, this game is quite different from the first. This game is kind of reminescent of Hell Divers. You level up, you unlock (craft) new weapons/armor/food/meds/etc, you have a hub world, and the story progression consists of quests from a variety of sources. One of the coolest things, though, is the hub world. You're placed in what seems like a small area but the more you explore the larger and larger it is, it's actually pretty damn huge. In this world you are able to build what starts as a measly camp, but can be built up to a full blown zombie proof fortress. This game can get very intense when your friend accidentally runs into a horde, attracting 20+ zombies, not all of which are slow and incapable. Some zombies can keep up with your sprinting speed and there is no outrunning them. Night time is dark and atmospheric, leaving you partially dependant on your flashlight.

The combat mechanics aren't perfectly polished, especially melee, but it's not bad. I enjoy how the ranged combat is set up. You are required to aim/focus on an enemy in order to get a headshot, otherwise you don't do nearly as much damage. Melee is very simple but can be very satisfying at times. It consists of 2-3 press combos and different attacks modified by movement as well as the option to finish of stunned enemies with a variety of different fatalities.

Something pretty unique that this game does is how your characters and camps work. You can have a total of three characters. You can have a Local, Public, and Private character. Each character can only play in camps of corresponding permissions. Public camps are run on dedicated servers and are always open for anybody to join. The camp Admin can decide whether or not to have friendly fire and how much, change player default permissions and raise (or lower to ♥♥♥♥ with) friends permissions or ban whoever.

----------

In this game you will:

* Fight zombies and other creepy ♥♥♥♥.
* Hunt zombie animals for food and hides (soon to have non zombie animals as well)
* Loot and fight in towns, swamps, and forests.
* Explore a good variety of maps and a large hub world.
* Craft weapons, ammo, attatchments, armor, a large variety of medicines and tinctures, cook food, and create a variety of drinks.
* Turkeys.
* Build a base with many different resource and crafting structures, defenses, and traps.
* Upgrade structures, many of which change appearance as they progress.
* Test said base with survival horde challenges.
* Level up your camp as well as yourself and a quite large list of skills.
* Kick complete ♥♥♥ with your friends tag teaming zombies or get tag teamed by zombies and other creepy ♥♥♥♥.
* Have complete multiplayer freedom. Players in a camp are not confined to playing the same quests at the same time. Some people can be at camp, some people can be doing multiple quests all at the same time.
* Probably starve and suffer of dehydration.
* Most likely die because of said suffering.
* Grind.


----------

Notable Negatives

This game can be quite buggy. I have had quests break on occasion, I've had pretty significant connection issues both as lag and disconnections at times, and I've had times where everybody ends up needing to restart the game for it to work. These don't happen very often, but do happen more often than most other coop games I've played. I, however, have not lost any progress at all due to any of that so it's more of an invonvenience than anything.

Controller support is not good. A lot of UI isn't navigatable without using the mouse with the controller which is fairly annoying and really ineffecient.

If you want to have the best equipment as soon as you unlock it then you will have to do a bit of grinding. For example, my camp recently hit level 10 (out of 45 and I have 14 hours in) and we have unlocked an entire set of armor. However each set of armor takes roughly 20 animal hide (as well as 4-5 other not-so-hard-to-get ingredients) and we have 4 people. The best current way to get animal hide is a quest where you have to find and kill 10 coyotes (with zombies in between) who may or may not drop animal hide. You won't be grinding for hours on end, but you will have to grind missions for awhile every now and then.

----------

Conclusion

I definitely recommend this game. It's a ton of fun, especially with friends. Ransacking a town and looting it thoroughly while tag teaming zombies with melee and ranged combos and going home to the safety of your wooden fortress is incredibly satisfying. I don't reccomd this game if you don't want to grind now and then, can't deal with occasional connection issues or glitches, aren't a fan of twinsticks or survival games, or don't want to spend a hefty amount of time exploring and looting.

The first paragraph is a rant on the silly negative reviews that really put the game down even though it’s good so I want to get that out of the way so here it is:

I really don't understand why this game is 'Mostly positive' so I looked at negative reviews to see why, and they are just awful, one I saw was just disgusting and it basically got a negative review because the person though it was like Project Zomboid, so because it didn’t live up to his/her expectations they rated it bad/down. Disgusting.

EDIT: I just saw another negative review because someone thought it was supposed to be like diablo 3, really? Idiot. (As you can tell stupid review trigger me.)

Now that 'rant' is out of the way I’ll review the game, now please bare in mind am not a reviewer, I just want to say my opinion on the game and I’m rating it up to 'fend off' all the silly negative reviews because it is a good game and deserves a better rating.

Let’s start off, people saying it’s just a ‘Point A to B’ and ‘Collect this and bring it back’ type of game, well yeah that’s true, but if you think about it, just about all games revolve around that same ‘schematic’ I look at their profiles and they have 2k hours in DOTA 2 and say this game is repetitive, I swear some people, but really don’t take that as a negative point, because all games are like it in some way or another, in DOOM you just run from room to room killing sh*t but that’s still a good game right? Exactly so why can’t this be?

Pros –

The game has a really cool concept that I personally really like, you have a camp and a big map to build said camp anywhere you want, and you have loads of ‘crafting stations’ that all do separate things, like the Kitchen workshop for food, and Armoury for weapons etc. simple stuff, and you can build defences because every so often your camp will get raided by hordes of zombies for you to fend off which is also really fun, that in that aspect is a base building tower defence game.

Also just like the first one there are Food and Water gauges, but no sleep in this one, which Is fine, the sleep was rather pointless on the first one to be honest, I only used it to skip night.

Also the Skills you buy with Experience points is a lot better than the first one, a lot more skills that do many different things, more in depth if you will.

The graphics are good, I’m not one who really cares about graphics so to some it may be bad, so I suggest you judge for yourself by the screenshots.
Fairly big map to build your camp, but also every quest has a different map which is good for keeping the game fresh.

Cons –
The Aiming can be really fiddley at times, sometimes I will run up to a zombie to do a running charge attack and my character will just do a 180 degree turn away and hit the air rather than the zombie, and the zombie will just turn around and hit me, keep in mind this doesn’t happen all the time and I’ve only gotten killed once by it.
Also the Aiming for guns and bows will sometimes just make you have to restart the aim even though you didn’t take it off, this can be really annoying at time when in panic situations and zombies are closing in on you.

The UI for transferring items is really fiddley too, you can’t shift click the items and so therefore you have the click and hold the item drag it over and then select how much of said item you want to move, and you have to click the ‘okay’ button, pressing enter will not work, so this con could be removed if the Developers implemented a shift click.

Lastly the game has crashed on me twice but it’s in early access and they weren’t hard crashes, just alt+tab and close with no lag, no progress lost either.

End -

That’s about it I think, like I said I’m not a reviewer so this isn’t going to beat the better ones, but really I wrote this just to like a said before ‘fend off’ those silly ones that make no sense and should be removed because they’re just putting the game down on the ratings side of things.

Edit: Also want to say I have 7.6 hours as of writing this and I’m still really enjoying it and feel like I’m a long way off ‘completing’ the game, the reason completing has been highlighted is because you don’t just do all the quests to complete the game, you can make your camp bigger and better and perhaps play with friends amongst other things so its defiantly worth the price.
