About This Game

The Island of Von Shalitz has long been known to be a place of evil and despair. You are the unfortunate last living heir to Von Shalitz's fortune or what some might consider a debt. Your inheritance encompasses the entire island and the mansion on the central plateau. A variety of beasts and horrors are rumored to haunt the land. The Caretaker Mic will provide you with a book of lore in relation to the islands terrors and treasures. It's your duty to get to solve the problems from the past and reclaim the mansion of your ancestors.

Hey, Digital Homicide... I know you can't take negative reviews well... thanks for the childish invites through a private profile to lewd groups! You reek of homophobia.
http://imgur.com/0RwVrxE

Let's face it, Digital Homicide are not exactly a developer/publisher that are known for their overwhelmingly amazing games. In fact, they're infamous for quite the opposite; their games are laughably awful, and I'll just get right into this review by saying that Paranormal Psychosis is no exception. I managed to grab this 'gem' in a bundle with two other games for a whopping $0.45 CAD, and I wish I'd just stayed away.

First off, it took an update to even make the game function at a higher resolution than 1600x900. The first time I launched the game it didn't even have a 1920x1080 option, so I played in a pee-poor resolution reserved for hardware from the mid 2000's. When the game boots up, you have the option of getting "major" and "minor" spoilers... both of which simply tell you where you can find some in-game items.

When you actually start to play Paranormal Psychosis, you begin beside two ghostly looking horses drawing a carriage. Immediately, you will be blown away by the awful optimization that garners horrendous frame rates. The second thing that will happen, is you will be swept off your feet by the outrageously awful graphics. You will then laugh to yourself in a "going insane" sort of manner, because you will wonder how the hell developers still manage to screw up optimization so badly when working with textures that are barely above the level of a Playstation 2 game.

The 13 minutes that I spent with this game felt like an hour due to the walking speed being so slow. My first time around I made an actual effort to get some way through the game, and I ended up getting slaughtered by "zombies" that look like mummies, because I ran out of bullets; golly gee I'm sure glad there's a HUD to tell me how many bullets I have... oh wait, there isn't. The second time around I ventured down to the beach and picked up magic mushrooms, and I must've gone on some kind of crazy trip because the Jolly Green Giant with Tentacles came up out of the ocean and one-shot me. When I respawned, I noticed he was still there, so I went back and emptied my clip into him and got no reward for doing so. I then found the chainsaw and wandered aimlessly until I saw a fire burning in the woods, as I approached there were some gnarly looking hags dancing around a cauldron. They shocked me with spells, and my chainsaw went right through them... literally, not damaging them at all.

After that, I gave up because my god... between the headache inducing low framerates and the tediously slow walking speed, I didn't feel like making my way back up that giant hill to the castle only to be devoured by zombie-mummies that take half a clip each to kill. I could've saved my $0.45 and plunked it into the peanut machine at work instead, at least that would've given me something decent. Do yourself a favor and not only stay away from Paranormal Psychosis, but Digital Homicide in general. If you've ever played Game Developer Tycoon, or anything similar, Digital Homicide are a real version of the developer that you play in those types of games; they pump out one garbage game after another, spotting what's "hot on the market" currently, and hope they get a hit. It's really sickening.

Rating: 0.5/5.0 - Abysmal, avoid it like the plague.

Of all the games that Digital Homicide has put out lately, this is perhaps the most disappointing. Not in the sense that it's a bad game, but that it actually had some potential to be a little more than just another bottom barrel Digital Homicide release. Had the lighting been better, a little more work to the assets, a map, checkpoint system, aiming, and definitely a flashlight or lantern of some sort just to name a few things this title desperately needed, Paranormal Psychosis could have been something worth while.

I've played so much worse in the first person horror genre, which is the worst part about this title. Digital Homicide put in the bare essentials to make the game competant enough to exist, but far more painful to play than it absolutely needed to be. With the many recycled asset flips litering their greenlight page at the moment, and one terrible title after another hitting the store front (sometimes in droves), this is one that somehow manages to stand out. Maybe because it's a little different compared to the rest and returns to their horror-themed roots a bit, but Paranormal Psychosis shows that, even i using other assets from the Unity marketplace, they do have the chops to create something mildly engaging, not to menton one of the very few titles on here to legitimately startle me at least once (which you'll see in the video above).

This, however, just falls short from being mediocre. Thankfully the bundle options on Steam they have up allow for discounted prices if you have the other titles in the bundle, making the cost for this one a meer nine cents (US currency), and I'm satisfied with it at that price. However, for the full cost of about a dollar, even fifty-nine cents (as it costs alone on the store at the time of writing this), it's best worth passing up.
