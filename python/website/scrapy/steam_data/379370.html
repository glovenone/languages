About This Game

Mobile Light Force (better known as Gun Bird in Japan) is a classic action packed vertically scrolling shoot'em up. Five adventurers join together for the first time in this action packed title to uncover the mystery of the ancient Magic Mirror. Fight your way through the skies of early Europe to stop the evil organization who are out to steal the Magic Mirror. Blast off for fierce action with classic arcade shooter elements.

It's Gunbird--otherwise known as Mobile LIght Force. A great Psikyo shooter from the PS1 era. That cheap $10 game you bought at Best Buy or Gamestop is back on Steam, EXACLY the way you remember it. This is a direct PS1 port--including load times. It shouldn't be a big issue, but after playing shmups that have those taken out, it's a bit jarring. Waiting for the music to "load" for a boss fight is kind of weird in this day and age.

The graphics are dated, and the gameplay is quite simple, but it does change up a couple things from the standard shmup. For one, if you direct your character to the bottom of the screen, they will pull the action back with them. Which sounds cool, and benefitial, but it only causes your dodging abilities trouble when you have to quickly readjust to the moving bullet swarm coming right towards you. Be prepared for a bunch of cheap deaths until you finally get used to it. Also, you can't fly all the way to the bottom of the screen. There's an invisible barrier keeping you from it. This also limits your dodging ability.

In the end--you know the ol' saying (say it with me, kids)--if you like the genre, you'll like the game. Definitely pick it up on a discount if you're a fan, though. Full price might be asking a lot.

This is a direct emulated port of the EU version of Gunbird. To get the technical stuff out of the way first: Yes, this game is missing some of it's features that it had as a full release of Gunbird like Local Co-op. Because it's emulated, while they are considerably smaller than it's PSX counterpart, you will get loading screens. The game has no end sequence, although having never beat it in it's original incarnation I can't comment for if the original arcade machine had an end sequence. Fundamentally, it's a direct PSX CD-rip of the 'Mobile Light Force' cut down version of Gunbird, emulated on PC architecture.

If you're looking for a polished, built around your PC/Mac shmup with tons of options, loads of internet features like leaderboards, challenges etc, look elsewhere.

If you don't care about red tape and love gameplay pure and simple, get this in. Despite the bare-bones budget presentation and godawful low-res bootleg title screen, this is still Psikyo's Gunbird, which is an excellent late 90s early 00s style shooter. It's in the style of Raiden, Strikers etc in that there aren't really any fancy mechanics, just power-ups, smart bombs and skill. The graphics are cute, the game has a great sense of fun and the mechanics are really tight.

For me in the Psikyo catalogue, this game sits just behind Strikers 1945 and 1945 II as a solid 3rd best shooter they put out. It plays in a very similar way to Strikers 1945. It has a mildly frustrating mechanic where your power-ups only stay active for a timed period after which you need to collect more to power your guns back up, which would be fine if there was any display of this onscreen to tell you it was about to happen, but it just does it and it's up to you to keep an eye on your level of power via what you're shooting, which is incredibly hard when there's bullets flying all over the shop.

However, it counters this with a fairly low level of difficulty (relative to the genre of course, it's still quite hard lol!) so this is like I say, a frustration rather than a game-breaking issue. The game also sweetens the pot by having an excellent soundtrack (aside from a title screen tune that's rather nails-down-chalkboard) and a wide selection of characters to choose from, adding more to it's ability to cater to a wide range of playstyles.

Overall it comes down to this: This is currently the only legal way to play Gunbird on a PC. If you have a PSX, you could buy the original or this release on that, but it would probably cost far more than it's £4 pricetag on Steam as most shmups on PSX or PS2 are rare as hen's teeth as the print run was generally small for what was at the time a niche market. While the port is fairly direct with no concessions or alterations made (and an initially mindboggling but actually quite intuitive forced keyboard layout), it's still got multiple resolution options, multiple control support and very VERY solid emulation with no glitching or crashing. So you basically need to make a choice between tracking down a potentially very expensive original version of the game, or manning up a bit and lumping the slightly rushed port-job to play what is an excellent 2D shooter.

I firmly recommend it, but I also understand that it's an awkward port and that won't be for everyone's tastes.
