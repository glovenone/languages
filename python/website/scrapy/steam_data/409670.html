About This Game

This is a cute and fancy action game that lets you mess around and have fun to your heart's content


Playing as the pink-clad office lady appearing in Kero Blaster - the 2D scrolling action game - you must make your way through a cave to escape the enemy's clutches.
Find the missing documents and defeat your opponents by jumping over them.
Clear the game by returning safely back to the office with the reclaimed documents in hand.

Hard mode includes a new special ending that you won't want to miss!

Granted, it is a short game - but understandably so.
The game is a bit of an advertisement for a game called "Kero Blaster" developed by the same company.
This game itself along with its sequel "Pink Heaven" both do very well to do what it is they aim to do - give a good impression for what the game Kero Blaster is going to be.
In playing the two games, I was convinced that I wanted to buy Kero Blaster and see more for what the little series would be about and what they have to deliver as a cute but short little game.
While the two stand-alone games Pink Heart and Pink Heaven are very very short games, there is the offer for multiple endings in story mode depending on the choices you make in game (or mistakes) and the ability to unlock a hard mode - which will certainly fill up your time with trying to pass it perfectly with all obtainable items intact.

If you are looking for a very cute and easy game to play for very short periods of time without requiring you to get too serious or intense in playing, these games are certainly for you.
And, as a side note, get Kero Blaster. It is $9.99 (Though $5.99 with the current sale)!

Pink Hour is an amusing free platformer released as a "demo" of sorts for an upcoming game by the same developer, Kero Blaster. It provides a good half hour of fun, or ten minutes of fun followed by a half hour of ragequitting, depending on how good you are at platformers.

Honestly, it's a pretty well put together platform/shooting game, with the biggest "drawback" being that it's fairly short - something that would be resolved by buying the game it's promoting, obviously. The only real issue I had was that Hard showed that it might have some issues with precision jumping, as I found myself falling short or overshooting on those segments reliably, which was annoying in this short level and would be more annoying if I had to do it constantly in Kero Blaster.

That said, though, it was a good half hour of fun for me, and free, so I can't really help but give this a thumbs-up.
