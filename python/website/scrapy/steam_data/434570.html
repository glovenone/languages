NEW COOL STUFF


The latest Update to Blood and Bacon.

  • 3 New Camera Modes When You Die
  • More Easter Eggs in the Barn
  • Muliplayer Pistol Firing Rate Fixed
  • Secret F1 F4 F5 F6 Hotkeys Main Menu
  • Regional Foreign Language Bug Fixed

Okay, lots a little updates turn into one big announcement. When you die in CO-OP you can switch cameras with gamepad or mmb or F1 key to get a Free Roam, Enemy camera, Boss camera, or Friend Follow camera. Use the (aswd) keys to fly around in Free Roam and Mouse, the LMB and RMB will let you cycle through all enemies and bosses, and MMB is a shortcut to change camera modes. Only when you die will you have these cameras.
There are more easter eggs in the barn (red sun) (skulls).

In the main menu, if your game will not run or crashes when loading you can turn on a Debug mode with F1. You can also now reset your achievments, your preferences, and your saved game progress with F4, F5 F6.

First off Credit to @Shatterstorm for pointing out the Pistol Fire Rate drop in CO-OP mode. Shout-out to @Cheeki Breeki for having the idea for a Free Roam Camera.

About This Game

All You Need is Blood and Bacon


Blood And Bacon is a Multiplayer(4P) CO-OP "survival shooter fps" that pits heavily armed players against undead deadstock as you attempt to butcher your way through 100+ levels of insanity and twitching gristle. Day and night levels, players will take on 35 enemy types and numerous bosses, like the gargantuan monster affectionately named, ‘Princess Blubbergut’ and Minibosses every 10 days as you explore, shoot and run for your life against the onslaught of porcine monstrosities. The game creatively dispenses pickups through a meat grinder, from the very creatures you are killing. Ammo boxes, Adrenaline, Grenades, Rockets, and other exotic boosters. Boasting over 15+ hours of gameplay and the ability to battle a staggering (or shambling) 500+ undead creatures at once, "Blood and Bacon" looks to feature more barnyard excitement than you can shake Old MacDonald’s severed femur at !!

The price is low and reviews positive, but as a proponent of *quality* games, I absolutely cannot recommend this. B&B is basic, repetitive, and the graphics are a throwback to fifteen years ago (which many will argue is an "art style"). I don't mind blood and violence in video games if well utilized, but here it just seems mindless; hold the mouse down until there's lots of red and everything's dead. That's it. No story, no replayability, no... quality if you will. I wasn't able to get any sense of enjoyment out of it. A much better comedic shooter with heaps of blood is Postal 1 or 2.
