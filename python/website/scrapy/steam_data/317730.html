About This Game

An extraterrestrial spacecraft was discovered during the last manned mission to the moon in 1972. Although all information regarding this was classified, the USSR discovered it and in 1976 organized an expedition to explore the wreckage. 2 years later regular flights to the moon had become common and the scientific research center Orbit was built but this information remained classified.

It is now May 2023 and contact with Orbit was lost. The space agency has requested the Junior 2, which was orbiting the moon, to find out the cause.

The lunar module crashes while attempting to land and Gregory Klimov is the only survivor.

As Gregory Klimov, you will be in danger form the cold of outer space. Every breath of oxygen is precious. Every task is fraught with danger. The distraught crew of Orbit are in hiding under its surface. You must find any resources to build equipment. You must adapt, improvise and keep your wits as you try to survive this horror.

Although REVERSE SIDE looks superb and it has a great, spooky atmosphere, unfortunately it looks like there's only about 5 minutes worth of actual gameplay.

After spending a couple of minutes with flying around in an abandoned space station, the protagonist is tasked to refuel a spacecraft. Something goes wrong in the process and there's an explosion, and the protagonist can only escape by jumping into a lunar module spaceraft. After this the camera follows this spacecraft's (which looks like the Eagle of the Apollo 11 mission) flight -- or rather fall -- over a lunar landscape, but it doesn't seem to be controllable at all other than some tiny bursts of the RCS (maneuvering thrusters), but even these bursts don't affect the course. After a few seconds the spacecraft crashes into a rock, and the next moment you're back in the main menu, without any explanation or indication as to what happened, or if there's anything else you could try.

The game doesn't even have the tutorial which the demo had, so even though it looks very pretty, as there seems to be no content to it, I asked for a refund because sadly this is a vaporware. :(
