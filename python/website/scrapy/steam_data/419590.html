About This Game

Tales from the Void is an atmospheric real time tactics game inspired by early 20th century sci fi adventure stories. Prepare yourself for a novel gameplay experience, with squad-based tactical combat, resource management and an epic story.

Travel back to a time of adventure, heroism and mystery as Edwin Albert Taylor Bragg, the British captain of the HMS E18 - on a secret mission to obliterate the Germans and win the war for the allies. When a secret weapon misfires, you and your crew are warped into space. Struggling for survival, you venture out into the asteroid field to scavenge resources and fight off hostile aliens. Food is scarce, air supply is low and time is running out. Can you escape the void and find a way home?

Features

-Customize and control your marines to explore asteroids and fight the alien enemy.
-Third person-RTS hybrid with low-G physics movement and combat
-Play real-time or pause to plan your movements and make coordinated attacks.
-Complete missions and gather supplies before your run out of air.
-Play a extensive single-player campaign with an ongoing narrative.
-Rogue-inspired structure with semi-random mission selection and permadeath. -try, fail, unlock equipment, retry and improve, until you find a way home.


In June 1916, in the midst of WWI, the English submarine HMS E18 mysteriously disappeared in the Baltic Sea to be found again as wreckage in 2009. What really happened to the crew of HMS E18? Embark on an exciting journey, inspired by a true story.
"R.C. Halahan, commander of HMS E18 had his future told by a local woman prior to this last patrol. She told him he was in grave danger, this affected the superstitious Halahan" -Francis Goodhart, commander of HMS E8 (Wikipedia)

Tales from the Void is a very unique game with a nice Lovecraftian feel to it.

When I first started, I struggled a bit with the controls. I didn't guide my troops through narrow paths but clicked ahead and that sometimes sent people over the edge. But once I got used to the controls I really enjoyed this game.

As for picking missions, you have a set of resources; Air, Food and Crew. Apart from that you can also gather weapons for more damage and utility items for small boosts to your forces. You need to balance the first 3 while trying to gather up better weapons and items to be able to fight stronger enemies.

The game is forgiving as far as rouge-like games go, but you shouldn't relax just because of that. Don't throw your men away carelessly. It will come back to bite you in the end when you're out of gunners or scouts.

The story is quite funny and I found myself laughing a few times at the captains off-topic remarks.

To summarize, this game is fun, easy to get into and has a good story.

Tales from the Void tells the tale of the HMS E18, a Royal Navy submarine that went missing during a mission which objective was to cripple Germany during World War I. While trying to use an experimental weapon that would render the Germany war machine useless, the submarine disappears from the Baltic Sea and reappears in some sort of void.

The best thing about this game is the atmosphere, the sound effects are spot on, you can hear your marines breathing through their old diving suits and all other sorts of weird sounds. The music, sound and visuals all together create this sort of lovecraftian/cthulhu vibe that, for me, make this game stand out from others.

The game takes place in this âvoidâ where you and your crew are stranded and have to find resources in order to survive, while also persuing certain objectives. Before anything else, there is a very brief tutorial to fill you in on the gameplay basics. After completing it, you can proceed to the campaign mode, where you can choose to play one of three different scenarios, each one having three different difficulty options.

The story is told between each campaign mission, where youâll get to listen to your captain debriefing, either discussing the current resource situation on the submarine or issues with some of the crew. While these sections are fairly short, they do a pretty good job in creating some sort of background besides the whole weapon experiment gone wrong, adding a lot to the atmosphere.

Before each mission youâll get to choose one of several asteroids you want to visit. Each one of these has its own cost associated with it (air and food) and each one also has different objectives and rewards, as well as a recommended tactical approach. Some missions require you to take fast firing weapons so you can keep enemies at bay, while others require you to use explosives, for instance, to roll a block of ice into your submarine so you can melt it for water. You also get to choose your squad of marines from a set of three different starting classes, scouts, engineers and gunners. Each one of them is able to equip different pieces of gear, the gunners can carry heavy explosive weaponry and machine guns while scouts tend to stick with pistols and grenades. Youâll probably never have a favourite squad set for every mission because every mission environment is different and so, you must change your marines accordingly. The missions themselves are rather simple, you either have to gather resources and gear or go to a certain place, but the level design manages to keep things interesting with a mixture of narrow paths, steep âhillsâ and certain gaps which you must traverse carefully in order to avoid falling to your death.

Most of your time in this game will be spent in combat, which is tense, due to the fact that your marines move and fire rather slow due to the low gravity aspect of the environment. Combat and movement is, for the most part, real time but the game does allow you to pause and issue orders to your units. Issuing orders is as simple as selecting the desired unit and either right clicking to move, or pressing the designated hotkeys for them to attack. Still, even when you order your marines to follow the one youâre controlling you have to watch out, because they can just âfallâ into the void.

Overall Tales from the Void is an extremely pleasant game which I feel that has gone under the radar for a lot of people. The weird setting, the slow but yet intense combat, the captain dialogue sections, the resource management aspect and the marine customization, all these elements combined together make for quite the experience and make this a title I consider worthy of checking out.

This game is amazing. Lovecraftian story and setting. Constant fear of the enemies and environment.

Gameplay involves attempting to come to grasp with moving in a low gravity environment while shooting weapons which push one around from recoil. I wish there was a game to compare this to, but its so strange and awesome and fresh. Imagine a mix between Pikmin, The Call of Cthulhu, UFO Aftermath and FTL.

The dwindling supplies mechanic adds a bit of tension without being suffocating - this is the first time I've enjoyed it in a game, actually. Typically it is either so easy to acquire supplies in these sort of games that it's an afterthought, or you end up running out and losing because of rng -cough convoy cough cough-. Not so with tales from the void, the dwindling supplies mechanic serves to contextualize and shape your choice of mission next, rather than leaving you to simply hope you aren't going to lose to bad rolls, so to speak. For example, you may be low on food, and so there are three missions available today - one offers food, the other people and the third weapons, however all of their secondary objectives suggest there will be food at those other missions, but how much? Do you risk it?

So far I've been able to maintain adequate stock with just wise mission choices.

The combat is intense due to the low gravity setting where getting knocked off of the level is more than possible. Or crushed by meteors. Typically these environmental hazards can be evaded and mitigated by boosting out of the way, if you're fast enough. Which isn't hard to be.

The weapons are a mix of pistols, shotguns, submachine guns, machine guns and various explosive weapons from grenades to mortars. Friendly fire is dangerous, and certain weapons cannot fire within a minimum range. Positioning marines to cover each other and compensate for this is crucial.

You may bring up to 5 marines on a mission, but only one is dropped at the start, with the others being available for call in at any time should you need them. Its not always a good idea to have 5 people, however, as things can get clumsy with that many machine guns and rocket launchers.

So far I definitely recommend this. The permanent death is meaningful but not suffocating, the story is definitely original. The graphics have a nice style to them, even if theyâre not breathtaking, neither was FTL. So far this is an amazing hidden gem.
