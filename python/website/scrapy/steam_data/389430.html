About This Game

Knight Online is a PvP-centric Massively Multiplayer Online Role Playing Game that has been under continuous development for over a decade. Two nations: El Morad and Karus â or more colloquially, humans and orcs â are locked in an eternal struggle for dominance of the Adonis Continent. The PvP in Knight Online is fast-paced, action-packed, and oftentimes frantic. Dozens of players can be seen clashing in PvP zones during all hours of the day. With events such as Lunar War and Castle Seige War, the action becomes massive!

While PvP may be the beating heart of Knight Online, much of the game is spent embarking on quests, killing monsters, and â of course â collecting loot. The Upgrade System lets players tempt fate in an effort to improve their existing weapons, armor, or accessories (or lose them in the process!). With its unique ability to let players refine their skills and build their characters and equipment, Knight Online is able to entertain and excite its fans even after many years. Pick a side, build up your warrior, and join the fray!

Hello fellow gamers!
I'm a gamer myself, since 1997. I've started playing Knights Online back in 2004 and I've been through a lot of servers.

Knights Online always had, and still does, one of the best PvP systems, if not the best of all the MMORPGs I've played.
On the other hand, its graphs are really out-dated. Also, there's no real depth in the armors/weapons/accessories each class is able to use, and no depth at all on skills each class uses.

There are also lots of security flows. Don't let anti-hacks systems etc fool you. This game still has a lot of bugs that people are able to exploit on a daily basis.

Another downside of the game is that it might be free to play, but it's a 100% pay to win. Or even better, pay to enjoy, since you won't even be able to join the major servers without paying. And there is where it's PvP system lies.

Last, but not least, the staff of the game. They are laughable, at best. They won't ever be there if you have any problems, unless the problem you are facing is a problem on a payment method, then all of a sudden they will be there for you.
Servers are laggy, population is constantly decreasing, and they keep on making the same mistakes that almost any other server I've played in the past did.

I was foolish enough to come back in one of my childhood's games, hoping to find it a bit different, a bit better than back in the day. I even paid, hoping that this was gonna be something stable, as it's supported by Steam aswell. Oh boy, I was so wrong.

So, eventhough I LOVE, I truly do, it's PvP system, it's a 100% downvote from me. You'd better find another game to invest your time and/or your money.

I played this game from 2004 till 2011 on its own platform, and decided to stop playing for the amount of cheaters in game. Then when they announced their debut on Steam in January 2016, I wanted to give it a shot again. I am not gonna include anything from my past experiences on this game, and this review will be solely based on my experiences playing Knight Online on Steam.

So if you don't know anything about this game, it's an mmorpg game with heavy grinding required to level up and get strong items in game. Having said that, let me start by going with pros and cons on this game;

Pros

- Its PvP is probably the best, I've played a lot of mmorpg games including the World of Warcraft, and Knight Online's PvP system is by far the best one.

- Knight Online's upgrade system is pretty good and addicting, especially after a certain point, items start glowing and at +9, they glow like a light bulb and people always try to upgrade +9 items, and it's fun trying to upgrade items, of course if you fail, it destroys your items.

- Securitywise it is much better on Steam. Although it is usually too late, GMs usually ban cheaters.

Cons

- When GMs ban the cheaters, it is usually too late, because they sell the illegal items and in game coins to websites before the GMs ban them, so it doesn't really matter after they get their profit. They can open up a new character and quickly level it up by using the 3rd party software again. This affects the overall economy of the game, and cheaters create monopolies on items that are really hard to get.

- Selling in game items and coins for real money is illegal per their policy, however one of the websites of the NTTGame, publisher of Steam Knight Online, have a lists of resellers of their game codes which they clearly advertise, however most of these websites are selling gold bars(in game coins), and in game items as well, which are hard to get if you play legally. This is completely absurd because these websites are doing the exact opposite thing of what Knight Online's policy suggests, yet they are still being advertised on their websites. If this is a mistake, and they are not aware of this, it needs to be fixed ASAP.

- Your computer needs to be on for you to buy and sell items, they do not have an auction house system where you can set your items for sale and then you can turn off your computer. This is not the case in Knight Online, as my hrs on record suggests. And as I mentioned cheaters are getting banned but it affects the overall game economy, which ruins the lives of legal players. For example, while a legal player merchants day and night to make some in game profit, personally as my hrs on record proves, someone can go onto a website and buy a gold bar(100m in game coins) in like 5 minutes. This is not fair, and is the reason I uninstalled this game from my computer after playing for 1,385.2 hrs.

- Forums are a mess in my opinion, especially the Turkish side of the forum. Especially the Turkish GMs are in power trips in my opinion, and forums(mostly the Turkish side) are ran like a dictatorship.

- This is probably the worst game management I've ever seen. The population dropped from 20k+ players to less than 10k players in 6 months, but they are still doing the same mistakes.
