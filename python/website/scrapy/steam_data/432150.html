About This Game

What if alien life is closer than we think? Not in faraway galaxies or on planets still unknown to the human race... What if it was shining into our eyes every night for the past 4.5 billion years? They call themselves the Bantarans for Bantara is the native name of the Moon. Similar to humans, and yet so much different. Bantarans are known to have not one heart like the humans, not even two hearts like the Doctor, but even three hearts functioning perfectly within their bodies. But where are they? And why haven't we ever seen them? There's a catch... The Bantara mother carries the Bantara baby inside of her for about 4000 years... That's how much it takes for those three hearts to become perfectly synchronized. But their lifespan reaches more than 100 000 years. How come? There are absolutely no known bacteria nor viruses on the Moon leaving them with the perfect chance to evolve very fast... Until 1969 - human year. That's when us, humans, had made our big achievement and landed on the Moon's surface. We gloated for a while and then simply gone away leaving behind a neovirus that is not detectable by human technology. That was the end of the life as the Bantarans knew. Afraid, desperate and almost hopeless, the Bantarans decided to run for their lives... And their target was Earth... They didn't hate, they didn't want revenge, they simply wanted the humans to help them. But they were, as us humans would say, naive. As soon as they have landed and made contact, we started protesting against helping the aliens for they might be our enemies. Typically. Human scientists were forced to lock the Bantarans inside laboratories and conduct experiments, but there was one person who has restored the faith in humanity. Dr. Oliver Pendaka declined to commit genocide and released the Bantarans making it possible for them to flee further inside the laboratory. There still might be hope for the Bantarans from the Moon. Save the race of our night light!

The gameplay is similar to lemmings in a way which is fun. So if you're looking for a simple puzzle game to kill time and test your brain a bit ( or card idle ) then you might like this.

Summary
+ Puzzles are simple, challenging, and fun.
+ Makes you use your brain sometimes.
+ Cards ( who doesn't like cards ? )
- UI / Controls are poorly designed for mouse and keyboard, it feels like it was designed for touch screens. ( see suggestion 1 )
- The game feels incomplete and a tad buggy. ( see suggestions 2, 3 & 4 )


Suggestions
  1. The countdown gives a bit of challenge to the game which is good, but with the current controls it can be extremely difficult to place things fast enough. Make selecting the tiles go by keys such as 1, 2, 3, etc.. This would make it easier to select and place tiles, because sometimes I end up dragging the screen by mistake or I'm not fast enough. I think as is, this control scheme would be better suited for touch screens.

  2. When all the Bantarans die there's no game over screen, it's like I'm stuck in limbo. I have to click "Destory all Bantarans" to restart, even though they're already destroyed. Adding a prompt to restart would be good.

  3. Tutorials are good. The "help" button is a tad helpful, but you should consider adding a tutorial level so new players can learn how to play your game. Not everyone likes going blind into a game, so this could potentially turn some people away.

  4. The Level editor allows the player to easily bypass levels. This should be something that's unlocked AFTER completing all the puzzles. Otherwise if I'm stuck I can just move things around to instantly complete the level.

    There's also a bug in the level editor ; When I try to scroll with the mouse wheel in the level list it zooms in. When I scroll with the scrollbar it drags the level around. Might want to fix this.


Don't get me wrong, It's not terrible, but it has room for improvement. As is, I'd only recommend getting this game if you can get it on sale.

Lemmings was a fun game. It had many things going for it, and could be deceptively simple at times.
This game is similar to lemmings, but it is no lemmings.

The distinct lack of instructions, while not affecting my ability to play the game, was not the best. The menu feels incomplete, and the figures have some variability that does not help. In this sort of game, having a variable jump negatively affects the ability to solve the level.
Also, there is an excess of levels that require fast timing, turning a puzzle game into a test of reflexes.

In short, I would not buy this game, as much better lemming-esque games can be found on your local flash games hosting site for less.

If you can get the game for free, there is no reason not to play it, but again, it is not the best of it's type, nor, in my experience, did it do anything particularly new.
