About This Game

In Subterrain, you fill the shoes of Dr. West, the apparent lone survivor of MPO, an underground city on Mars. Do what you must to survive: battle enemies, fight hunger and thirst, mend your wounds, and gather resources in a constant struggle to survive the catastrophe that’s beset Mars!

You may end up being your own worst enemy if you’re careless!

Every decision you make is critical as the world does not wait for you.

SURVIVAL

  • Struggle against your own bodily urges, such as thirst, hunger, exhaustion, and more!
  • Beware of injuries, including bone fractures and blood loss!
  • Scavenge or perform research to obtain medical supplies!
  • Manage your oxygen and thermal levels while exploring!

COMBAT

  • Fight the horrors of Mars with melee and ranged weapons!
  • Customize your ranged weapons using modular parts!
  • Struggle against the persistence of time! Race against the spread of the infection!
  • Enemies will evolve over time from cocoons to monstrosities!
  • Defend your generator against invasions!

CRAFT

  • Collect and scavenge junk and creature parts to break down into components!
  • Produce armor, guns, grenades and upgrades using 3D printers!
  • Craft performance enhancers, survival gear, and more with the Bio Combiner!
  • Harvest plants and ores using the biosphere and mine. Refine them to create materials!
  • Research items to create more powerful versions! Create your own lightsaber or power armor!

EXPLORE

  • Explore randomly generated sub-levels and hand-crafted locations!
  • Experience non-linear storytelling through logs and story sequences!
  • Wander freely through Mars colony! Explore districts as you wish!
  • Restore the colony’s habitat by repairing temperature and oxygen generators!
  • Find clues to the infection! Escape the nightmare!

There is free demo version of Subterrain! If you would like to see how the game plays, please try it!

Setting:

By 2050, humanity has finally colonized Mars thanks to the efforts of one man, Jeff Murray. To avoid Mars’ high radiation levels and inhospitable temperature changes, he built a city underground. This city was named Mars MPO. Free of the constraints that often plagued businesses and researchers on Earth, scientists and entrepreneurs flocked to MPO.

Recently, Dr. Albert West has migrated to MPO at the personal invitation of Mr. Murray. West has been working on a cure for Alzheimer's disease in a race to save his wife, Jennifer, from dementia. His efforts are cut short; a lab accident leads to an accusation of murder and a prompt prison sentence.

We join Dr. West a little over a year into his prison sentence. It’s been a week since the guards mysteriously vanished. Dr. West is about to find out why.

I have been a semi-serious computer gamer for decades and I don't write reviews a lot unless a game is truly unique. Subterrain is a rare standout amongst many unremarkable games out there.

Sci-fi horror story with tension-wracked decision making as you struggle to balance exploration, resource management, time management, choosing item research paths, and even some good old tower defense thrown in! Who could ask for more than that?

I've had some very scary moments playing this game (especially on Hardcore+ difficulty with permadeath option). As the infection spreads you truly feel the urgency of being smothered with fewer and fewer options unless you make good decisions fast. It's a nail biter!

Much praise to Pixellore. You put together many different gaming genres in a smooth package.

As a footnote: Fans of Subterrain may also enjoy Dungeon of the Endless in their gaming library.

Preface: I have a little over a hundred games in my library, and I've only reviewed the few that really stand out (around 5 of them). I only say this because I may not be as well or long spoken as most reviewers and I feel like the previous statement helps show my feelings.
Review: I LOVE this game. It has everything I want out of a survial game(my favorite genre). The basic human needs are fairly realistic and at times very hard to fufill, and it has great resource management. At the beginning of the game you have a windfall of low level items which at first seems great Ex. "Oh cool A chest with 6 guns in it!" Within about an hour of gameplay you will have either burned through the weapons or cannabalized them for research. Eventually you can nearly automate certain aspects of production and research, but you'll probably die/lose waaaaay before that. Even when you are flush with food, medicine, and weaponry, your generator is forever dying so you have to scurry around the increasingly dangerous ruins of mars to scrounge up scrap and chips to research better power cores. And with two trees of tech (which doesn't sound like a lot, but it is) to keep up with you have to manage two different resource pools, one which you can farm or mine for, and another that requires mass slaughtering of infected beasts and men. After about a week you'll be fat and full of snacks and soda but you can die from a minor infection while on the short train ride back to your safehouse, or even worse, open a bathroom stall in a fairly safe area and be smashed to smithereens by a brute (Those are the first two ways I died, but have been followed by increasingly scarier deaths).
On top of all that, you have to manage your warmth and oxygen levels when you are in most areas which is a real struggle. After a certain point you have to make some serious decisions example: You see a room full of candy wrappers and medicine chests, but in the way is a spider and a weak mutant. Not hard to deal with. BUT you know that you are running low on power charges, and if your flashlight goes out this shopping mall will be your tomb. By the same token you have a broken leg and are bleeding out and you know that chest might have the cast or bandages you need if you need to turn tail and flee. What do you do? It doesn't matter because in the 10 seconds you were deciding you get torn open from behind. You try to dodge, but uh oh, your leg is broken so you can only walk! You whirl around and fire your shotgun 5 times into your foe. He splatters across the ground. Relief washes over you, but you are now crippled and gushing blood like a stuck pig. The blood and gun blasts alerts the spider and the mutant. As you get ready to face your death, guns blazing, you die from blood loss. Game over. *or you die from hypothermia, or suffocation, starvation, dehydration, or infection ect ect*
So now you're dead. You can load an earlier save! Whoopee! OR not. Which is another excellent feature. The game gives you the choice of difficulty(nothing new here) and permadeath mode! Now, I'm guessing 90% of people won't tinker with these but if, like me, you want to be afraid and high on adrenaline while you play because you put in 20 hours of gameplay and every door you open might be your last, then ramp up those settings, baby. Side note: I'm not particularly fast or good at these games, but I like them to be really hard because that's way more exciting and realistic.

As to negatives: None (at least none worth mentioning) I've seen people complaining(a lil bit) about graphics, music, and some things not being explained well enough. Personally I don't find any of these flaws to be real. The graphics aren't super HD, but that doesn't really matter with a top down game. Everything is very very clear and easy to distinguish and the lighting and view are very good as well. As far as music, I don't notice it at all. If you're activley listening to the music instead of playing the game, you aren't playing on a hard enough difficulty :D. And as far as things being explained, it is ALL explained at the beginning in bite sized pieces. You find great bits of stories around the game too. If you're paying even a little bit of attention to the story, you'll find really rewarding data logs all over the place. My favorite being a log from someone named "Martin" To his friend who is sent to scavenge for some equipment in the mine. He is warned up and down not to veer off the path to sate his lust for choco pie. You'll find more logs, detailing how this guy (of course) veered off his task, following his nose for pie. Eventually....well, let's just say you find him and his pie. No spoilers, but it's rewarding to follow stories like these.

All in all, I've played close to 40 hours in three days and I'm about as far as I was at hour 10 because I keep effing dying (:D). But that could have been easily avoided. So I give this game a 9(ish) out of 10. Which is how I would rate skyrim and Fallout New Vegas....maybe Terraria too. All excellent games. I'm not super far into the game, so I don't know if it will yield the hundreds of hours of gameplay like those games did for me, but I wouldn't be shocked if it came close. A piece of advice for when (not if) you get the game, be careful. And most of all, expect to be surprised. Most of the times I died I thought I was nigh invincible right up until my head was squashed. Anyways, GET TO IT!!!

This is a fantastic game. The gameplay loop works very well, and you always feel pressure to just. keep. playing.

The research and manufacturing are awesome, the design of the self-contained mars colony makes a lot of sense, and it's neat to go to all the doomed places and find logs about the goings-on. It may not be a new thing, but Im a sucker for audio/text logs in games. I also love how the main character will point out things and give his opinion. It really helps sell that the Mars colony was a working, successful place, and not just a huge investment of money, tech, and personel that was doomed to fail.

The base-building/crafting aspects are fun as well. The constant battle, sometimes literally, to keep the power on is stressful enough to give a creeping sense of dread and pushing you to make inprovements and use your time well, without becoming an irritating slog of a task. And I love that West is like...."why are there 3 open tunnels 'accidentaly' full of radioactive waste that leave the reactor open to attack.....i dont think this was an accident"

Add to that the constant creeping increase of infection; requiring every excursion to have more and more prep, wich takes more time, which causes a greater spread! The enemies types and behaiviors are pretty basic, but it gives a great sense of mastery over them once you get the pattern down. And those ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥s that can open doors never make it easy.

Requirements for food, drink, and rest are pretty balanced on the normal difficulty. It never feels unreasonable based on how much time is passing in game, and supplies are scarce but not unobtainable. I really appreiciate that items spawn semi-sensibly. It makes looting a lot easier than like: "Look at all these guns in the vending machine!"

One of my only negatives is that it's not well explained that the research and manufacturing stations share a chip type. I went about 15 hours without the 2nd level bio stuff and on a standard nano-repairer because it wasn't really explained that some things share software. I thought I had to find all the upgrades individually. Maybe that's just me though.

Overall a game with tons of content and an interesting idea that is well executed and overall very fun. I played my first 20 hours in 2 days!

I have been a semi-serious computer gamer for decades and I don't write reviews a lot unless a game is truly unique. Subterrain is a rare standout amongst many unremarkable games out there.

Sci-fi horror story with tension-wracked decision making as you struggle to balance exploration, resource management, time management, choosing item research paths, and even some good old tower defense thrown in! Who could ask for more than that?

I've had some very scary moments playing this game (especially on Hardcore+ difficulty with permadeath option). As the infection spreads you truly feel the urgency of being smothered with fewer and fewer options unless you make good decisions fast. It's a nail biter!

Much praise to Pixellore. You put together many different gaming genres in a smooth package.

As a footnote: Fans of Subterrain may also enjoy Dungeon of the Endless in their gaming library.
