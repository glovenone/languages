About This Game

The Way of Life makes the player relive the same life's experiences from the point of view of three characters of different ages: an adult, an old man and a child.
The experiences we want to tell about are very different, they go from a quarrel to a dream, and further to more serious topics like death and love. The main point about the game is that every experience will be told through different gameplay mechanicsâ.â
Also, every playable character has his own specific mechanics and a way to see the world.
The player throughout the game can explore and live different stories and feel a variety of emotions in an amusing and captivating experience.

Let me start by saying that this is NOT FREE TO PLAY. I don't know why they would advertise this as free 2 play. I am pretty sure Steam allows developers to publish a demo without actually needing to publish the store page of a full game. THIS IS A DEMO. Actually this looks more like an early version of a limited beta of the game.

It includes only some of the levels, the final game will be with its price (unknown to me) and with all the levels. Though it would have been nicer to advertise this as what it is, but from the store page title you can't tell it's a limited version (though it is written in the description). I don't know, it looks kind of shady to me, not very transparent feeling.

Anyway, on to the game: the style has a lot to offer. But as I said this really feels like an incomplete version of an incomplete game. Walk speed is not well balanced compared to the environments. The subtitles (no voice acting) come always very suddenly and you can't move nor look around when it happens....this breaks immersion a lot and is very annoying, also because the subtitle stays there much longer than the time you actually need to read it. Hub world as other people say is badly designed and not very effective (it is the first thing you see in the game....you have to make a better impression). The game looks promising and one of a kind.....I can recommend it but with lots of reservations.

It's an interesting story plot, the audio is soothing, and the visuals are aestetically decent, but the delivery and gamplay were really a trun off. A lot of the time spent in this game is walking and in some parts, walking very slowly to a destination. When you finally reach the destination, you're presented a linear puzzle and if you make a mistake, you're forced to start over again. In this game, starting over can mean a lot of different things. The game will sometimes spawn you at the begging of the battle (which it should do imo every time), and other times it spawns you at the begging of the game, forcing you to do a myriad of simple yet annoying tasks (i.e. walking for three minuets) just to reach your destination and start the process over again. In one level I played, I fell off the road, and then I had to re-enter the level from the "home spawn point" (not the level spawn point). Half of this game is just plain old boring walking. The bosses and puzzles were easy and linear and there is little skill that need to be used. Sure the story plot is great and there is more to the game than walking, but a huge part of the time spent in the game is mundane actions where I stare at the screen holding a button. This game spends so much time getting to the good parts, that the good parts don't seem worth the energy and time to get to.
