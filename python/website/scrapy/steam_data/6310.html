About This Game

The Longest Journey is an amazing graphical adventure, where the player controls the protagonist, April Ryan, on her journey between parallel universes. Embark on an exciting and original journey of discovery, where you will explore, solve puzzles, meet new people, face terrifying monsters, learn, grow, and live the adventure of a lifetime!
  • Over 150 locations spanning two distinct and detailed worlds
  • More than 70 speaking characters
  • 40+ hours of gameplay
  • 20+ minutes of high-resolution pre-rendered video footage
  • Cinematic musical score

System Requirements

    Minimum: Windows 2000/XP, Pentium 166 MMX , 32 MB RAM, Mouse and Keyboard, 640x480 SVGA high colour (16bit) video card with 2 MB RAM, Windows compatible sound device, 300 MB free hard drive space
    Recommended: Pentium II, 266 mhz, 64 MB RAM , 3d accelarator card ( Direct 3d compatible ) with 4 MB RAM, 1GB free hard drive space

TLJ, a pleasant odessey...

I landed on this game because I wanted to play what originated the story from Dreamfall Chapters, and while this is a 1999 game, I was very very pleased. Like they say, they don't make stuff to last like before nowadays!

Graphics
Well, this is a game from 1999, so forget about it, you cannot play this game expecting HD textures, expect Nintendo 64-like graphics at its best, with old Diablo-like cutscenes, nor there are any mods to that I could find to help with this, this is most unimportant anyway, since the true treasure of this game lies elsewhere. However, I do find appealing all the drawn scenarios, thinking of all the work that went through the creation of this game to make all the world, I absolutely love it. Also, just as me, you'll probably have find issues with the graphics, with 3D models looking incomplete, this can be easily fixed by following the instruction in THIS thread, easy peesy! The music and sound effects used always suits the areas you find yourself in, although only in some very specific ocasions I found the music to be of special notice, it was just pretty much suitable, makes you feel in the right ambient.

The Story
That is truly the point of this really, no? What is a point-and-click adventure game without its story. I must say, I had some expectations and they were fullfilled, I deeply enjoyed the story in this game. I won't go on the details, for what I love of the story is to be able to discover it as you progress, every little piece of it, and I won't take that away from you. Mind you, there is something you should know, this game is young adult-oriented, so be warned about the content you might expect to find in the dialogues. That said, if you have lots of crops on your sense of humor field, this game will certainly make you harvest them all, the characters are quite hilarious, sarcasm is the everyday bread, and witty verbal knives fly here and there. Even when it wants you to feel omnious, it keeps making you chuckle.This game makes you love its characters, so you are more inclined to care about the story has a whole. And the voice acting...is truly grandious. This is how I like my stories told to me. My favorite character without doubt was Crow, it is are amazing. And man, this had gotten me 20h to complete, I did not expect that either, the game is appropiately long.

Gameplay
Well, this is an old game so if you are used to have clear aids on games, be aware this has none of it. By "clear aids" I mean like "objetives menu" "map", etc. No, you do have "story follow up" materials that help you remember what just happened and might be expected to do, and lets you review the conversations you had with NPCs should you have forgotten something important that they said, however apart from that do not expect any kind of leads or explicit help.
The puzzles can go from simple to "OH MY GOD, REALLY?", sometimes it requires you to push and get going on those abstract thinking processes to be able to figure out what it wants you to do, makes your inner celeron dust its core quite a bit for sure. If you are truly lost, do not worry, it is acceptable that you might end up in a game guide at some point because some of them are quite tricky...very tricky...let's say. How to succed at completing this game though? My recomendations are the following:
*From the very start, ALWAYS check everything in your scenario, everytime your scenario changes (whereas you went to a whole new area or you simply decided to take a look at something specific, scan it thoroughly, and see if you can interact with it in some way, or interact with items in some way. If you can grab something it -WILL- have an use or various uses along your journey, so keep always an eye peeled. One item you might have overlooked, and it might prove problematic later on, so be very vigilant, and very curious.
*Pay special attention to what has been said by the NPCs.
*Keep track of the details, and track of what you have in your inventory so it is easier to connect the dots with the help of your logic skills! The way you have to interact in with your environment always appeals in great deal to your logic (*Stark-ing intensifies* lol).

Overall?
I would venture to say that this is a masterpiece. Despite the fact that I could send this game to be carbon-dated already, it surprised me as it surpassed my expectations. I was a little dubious when I purchased it despite having loved Dreamfall Chapters, but I absolutely do not regret it a single bit. I enjoyed every single chapter, and should you, reader, stumble over this title by chance, know that you are in for a treat, and one hell of worth it. And if you decide to give it a shot, I hope you enjoy it as much as I do.
This is the way storytelling should be.

It feels a little pointless to review a game that came out 16 years ago, but I had to throw my hat into the opinion ring - I absolutely adored this game so I felt duty bound to gush about it a little. Plus, having never played it when it first came out, it's brand new to me.

The Longest Journey is as long as the name suggests. The amount of content for a point and click game is phenomenal. And describing the story as anything less than epic would be like describing the Great Wall of China as a bit long.

Set in two worlds, one woven with magic and filled with dragons and fantastical creatures, and one in a slightly bleak but tech-filled future, this is a must for point and click adventure fans. The Longest Journey has a wonderfully told story, which equal parts intriguing, touching, engaging and funny. With only a handful of exceptions, the voice acting is equally stellar, and the puzzles pitched just right (I did need to use a walkthrough on a couple of occasions, but hey, what's new).

Although the graphics are a little dated due to the age of the game - the sprites and animation is dire in places - the backgrounds are beautiful.

My biggest - probably only - gripe with the game is that it takes an age to move your character from screen to screen (even if you're running). Top tip (which sadly I only found out towards the end) - if you enable it in the settings, the escape key will skip those bits for you.

All in all, this game was an unforgettable experience and even at full price is worth every penny.

I was born in 1993 and this game came out in '99, so I definitely wasn't playing it at the time. However, this game really interesting to me as it was both a throwback to my childhood and set in the future. Going on 20 years since it was made, it's definitely intriguing to see a version of how the future could be envisioned (plus a load of 90s fashion and cultural references). One of the things I definitely notice between then and now is how much more action focused our games have got - I was getting really frustrated with how slowly she travelled and walked to places.
I bought this game because I'd heard good things about Dreamfall Chapters and then wanted to play the prequels to have a better idea of the storyline. For story lovers like me, this game won't disappoint. It has a really solid storyline and had me hoooked. It was a really great intro to this series and keeps you thinking as a player of the game (which I really like).
It's also great to have a female protagonist and there's a lot of background legend to get involved in, if you enjoy that in your games. Overall, a great buy and I'd definitely recommend.
