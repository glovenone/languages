About This Game

The sequel fans have waited 16 years for is finally here - Sonic the Hedgehog 4 Episode I! Featuring enhanced gameplay elements, including the classic Sonic Spin Dash, and the versatile Homing Attack, Sonic the Hedgehog 4 Episode I picks up right where Sonic and Knuckles™ left off. Dr. Eggman’s back, and in an effort to finally rid himself of Sonic, he revisits – and improves – the very best of his creations. Get ready for the next chapter in an all new epic 2D saga built for old and new fans alike.

Why else are the hidden gem level backgrounds essentially the Windows Media Player Music Visualizations. Yes, this game is another mediocre entry in the Sonic franchise, however, when played right and especially when purchased on sale, you will find the enjoyment level to exceed your lightened wallet. This game is worth it, just play it right.

The sequel fans have waited 16 years for is finally here!

That's what SEGA proudly proclaims at the top of this page. And just from looking at screenshots, you would think that this would be the case. It looks like it's a return to form for the Sonic games, even if it's in episodic form (for whatever reason). This is not the case.

Instead of revisiting the fun, fast gameplay of the originals, this game tries to mix things up a bit - and falls into the exact same pitfalls that 3D games in the series have been trapped by for years. The developers sought to add the lock-on jump mechanic that was introduced in the Sonic Adventure series to this game, to no success. It seems like a feature that was added on to help players traverse the levels better - but only because the levels are hard to get through in the first place.

The difficulty in this game isn't the problem - it's the totally incoherent level design. Sonic the Hedgehog was a series known for its lightning-fast gameplay and ability to speed through levels as fast as you can. More recent games, however, focus on putting as many obstacles as possible in your way in order to slow the player down or even make them come to a full stop. One of these such obstacles in this game are vines - no matter how much momentum you have, you have a 50/50 chance of keeping that momentum when you go to make your jump, requiring you to stop what you're doing and build momentum on said vine in order to continue to the next one.

SEGA and Sonic Team have obviously lost their touch when it comes to the Sonic series. The Sonic Rush series on the Nintendo DS did a much better job of carrying on the 2D tradition. Let DIMPS develop all your 2D Sonics, SEGA. You'd do everyone a favor.

Instead of getting this game, maybe fill your 2D Sonic cravings with other games like Freedom Planet or the remastered version of Sonic CD on Steam. Don't buy this retreaded garbage.
