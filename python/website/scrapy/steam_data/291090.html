About This Game


The green light flashes; another caller is on the line. You can tell right away it's going to be one of those calls... he's got a simple request, but you're caught between the bureaucracy that requires a gauntlet of security questions and a person who can barely remember his last address.


It would be so easy to let things slide. Just ease him through the questions, give him a hint or a nudge... or maybe you're done putting up with this. Your tolerance is shot and you're just not going to hold back on the snark. But either approach could end disastrously. You need this job.


Features:
  • A branching narrative pulled from personal experience.
  • Over 250 lines of fully-voiced dialogue.
  • STEAM-EXCLUSIVE achievements and trading cards.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP maybe?
    • Processor: Doesn't need to be fast.
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Honestly this game should run on just about anything.
    • Storage: 25 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: Requires speakers or headphones.

I am a Technical Support Specialist. I usually do my work onsite, but I still feel some kinship to the fellow in this game. I come face to face with people every day who have insurmountable problems that have severely impacted their ability to run their business. I can usually resolve these issues with a simple Google search. Sure, sometimes I run into real problems where I have to call on some dedicated hardware support, or developers whose software has so many dependencies it could be considered a welfare state, but what I specialize in, and the calls I am given most often, are “user error” issues.

To be honest, this is low level support. I don't know the APIs, I haven't memorized the circuit diagrams. I just fix stupid. I'm ok with that. Sure, I wish I made as much money as those other guys, but I don't know the things they do. I do know stupid though. I also know how to fix stupid. Google.

Heck, I have Google open right now as I write this. Think I know how to spell dependencies? Please. This is 2016. Intelligence is dead, and my customers are living proof. Here's an example. I had a customer call in and say that their email scanning stopped working for everyone. Well, the first reason that would happen is a password change. So, as always, I ask. Did the password change? She said no. So I go onsite to see what may have happened, and when I get there the lady is standing in front of the copier CHANGING THE PASSWORD!

Stupidity and lies. That is what support deals with every single day. That is what this game is about. I approve of that.
