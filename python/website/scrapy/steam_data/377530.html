About This Game

Tale of Wuxia is a role-playing and single-player PC game, made by Phoenix Games and Heluo Studio. Added lots of new stories, gameplays and systems, it is a new version of Legends of the Wulin. The game engine used by Tale of Wuxia is U3D.

Tale of Wuxia has been dedicated to providing gameplayers with a player-defined platform, where they can customize their own Wuxia. Instead of being restricted to characters’ basic properties and martial arts, in this new version, gameplayers can also choose the personalities and talents of their own. The new talent system makes characters more distinctive. For example, the hard workers are good at monotonous missions, the slyboots prefer changeable missions, while the jade trees are more popular among girls. Different talents come with different gameplays, greatly increasing the fun in playing.

1.Stories on game maps are equipped with many new systems, picturing a more free and real Wuxia world. Furthermore, the ultimate fun lies in character progression.
2.There are more interaction between characters, even NPCs have their own emotions.
3.Multiple endings.
4.Lots of hidden stories needed unlock by special conditions.
5.Added lots of new stories.
6.The number of girls a character can have relationship with has been increased to more than 10.

The current version contains a free DLC - The Return of Devaraja

This game is ill!
I'm not usually into games with cute Asian cartoon graphics
but as a big fan of old-fashioned kung-fu flicks and ancient
Chinese folklore, I can appreciate most apects of this game.

+ turn-based tactical battles
+ vast amount of kung-fu styles and non-combat skills to learn
+ a variety of mini-games to simulate various non-combat chores
+ your dialogue/situational decisions seem to effect the story
+ interesting stories about various Chinese traditions and lore
+ mildly humorous portions of dialogue
+ despite looking cute, there are some mature aspects to the story
+ Simple controls

- Multiple presentation styles: Mostly non-animated 2D drawings
which switch to animated 3D portions for battles and exploration.
- The graphics do the the job, but are years past their expiry date.
- No real options for the asthetic customization of your character.
- Originally a Chinese game, the English translation leaves
something to be desired, and some of the dialogue is still Chinese.
- An enormous amount of reading, with no voice-overs/narration.
- Charming but repetitive flute music quickly gets annoying.
- Lackluster sound effects.


Bottom Line:
Buy this game if you like old-fashioned RPG's, Kung Fu,
turn-based battles, and/or ancient Chinese culture in general.

Don't buy this game if you require state-of-the-art visuals,
you want to play a fast-paced action game, or if you're illiterate.

'nuff said!

This game is great if you enjoy Wuxia. If you have no understanding of Wuxia or Chinese culture then a huge amount of the story might go over your head. It draws heavily from Jinyong Wuxia stories and the game itself is translated very poorly at certain parts. In fact some parts aren't even translated.

As for the gameplay, the battle system is similar to many other turn-based strategy games. It's like Disgaea. It's not very deep but it's hard at certain points. The main difficulty comes from character development. If you don't know what you're doing, you can actually completely ♥♥♥♥ up and make a gimped character that has no chance of clearing the final stage and therefore can't win the game. It's a fairly obtuse system that is hard to figure out without a guide, and the ♥♥♥♥ translation doesn't really help things.

But the bulk of your time won't be spent battling, it'll be in a 'simulation' mode that reminds me of the downtime in Persona games. It's essentially a dating sim where you choose to spend time with different characters in order to build a relationship. There are huge portions of the game that are entirely missable if you don't choose to spend time with a particular character at a particular place within a set time frame. In fact, I'd say that if you want to see more than 50% of the game on a single playthrough you MUST follow a guide. There are a couple on steam so that's not a big deal to me. Although people who enjoy figuring ♥♥♥♥ out on their own will struggle with it.

So to sum it up, if you enjoy Wuxia, I'd say this game is a must-buy. I haven't enjoyed playing a game this much in years. If you have no idea what Wuxia is, probably give it a pass unless you really like the idea of a Persona style game where instead of dungeon-crawling you have turn-based strategy battles ala FF: Tactics or Fire Emblem.

Where can I even begin, and I oh my God it's a great game. So Honest Review Time!

Tale Of Wuxia, is exactly as it title says...it's a Wuxia, and it has Bo Staffs...a pervert launching himself with a pelvic thrust...a thief girl (who is waifu) a medicine girl (waifu) hunter girl (waifu...) poision girl (eh...waifu-ish.) Are you seeing a pattern here, no? READ!

Anyway, let me just say this, if you're looking for a cheap game ($17) that is actually well developed, fun, and has a lot of asian themes to it. Then, you're looking in the right place. This game is set in China (After The Three Kingdoms.) The game has you as a nobody, and let me just say YES you're a nobody at the beginning. You will have even trouble fighting a F***ing chicken at the beginning on grandmaster (by spoiler there is a ending to the defeat from the chicken.)

Anyway, moving on.

The Gameplay is great, every style has it's advantages and disadvantages, and it's up to YOU to figure it out. However, it's not all that easy. Since literally the beginning of the game can change how you play, or stylize your character early on. It challenges in the way of either going purely brute, relying on you're internal styles to give you a tank-like combat style or you can focus on other basics to increase your base stats up to being a glass cannon.

Try to fnd balance.

Anyway, fists, sword, and um...heavier swords are your three starting styles you can try to learn. (Fists allow a higher combo chance, Sword allows an early extra boost in health regen, and heavy swords are a damage adder.) A gew things can be done to increase the early roster, from doing chores to get the leg (fast, high combo chance attacks) and the finger (ranged attacks that actually do good damage.) The Bo staff is a little tricky, but well worth the investment if you read the guides.

Difficulty
(Okay the first part of this rather simple, welcome to you basic dating-rouge-like screen (education mode) where you can increase stats, and every victory like that in the later game give you small stat boosts (THAT ARE IMPORTANT) for then and later on in the game.)

The latter half of the game, towards the end of it actually is where even on noob things get dicey. You see it's best to see what style fits you soon, because once your put in the effort you'll have very little time to change your choices, and add into the other aspects.

Also, you're victories are very important, because IF your lose at this stage in the game it's game over, and it adds the stakes to the level of gameplay. In fact, there are chances to befirend certain characters through out the game, and befriending them can add them to your party.

(For THE LOVE OF HOLY BUDDHA) do not go at it alone, it's like the trivia/tips say, it's a quick way to the worst ending. Get the medicine girl, she is literally your white mage in this, and she is your lifeline in the hariest battles. Try to maximize your relationships early on, for (at the moment) you can have the advantage of having a large party if you chose to do so.

This game isn't really causual as it's tagged to be either, since most of the time you're gonna be trying to figure out new paths.

The Endings are all well fleshed out, and some are funny self pointing jokes.

Anyway as you noticed, I been calling every girl Waifu material in this game...it's because for the first time EVER, I found a game where each love interest isn't a cookie cutter. There is an actual personality to them, from the poison girl's dejectful attitude, to Jen Wens princess/wild girl persona. Quite literally, investing time into the romance of this game is rewarding in of itself in story.

Sometimes they yield increases in base stats (mostly energy and health)

The music is the game is great, but it can get nerve wrecking sometimes after repeated loops.

EACH CHARACTER IS FLESHED OUT.

Also you're gonna find easter eggs, a lot of them.

Jackie Chan's Drunken Master/Rock Lee's Loopy fist is a style in game.
Xia paradise, book with a literal Naruto easter egg on it.
Kung Fu Hustle.
Iron Monkey.

quite literally, there are easter eggs, and they are very good to both Anime Fans, and Wuxia Fans alike.

final verdict.

9/10 would put the vase down again.

So some guy's wife tasked me with finding a tiger's sexual organ to feed him with. Met a girl who constantly questions why she was born so beautiful. Talked with a little girl whose name was Doggy.

And this all happened in the first area.

In this game, you start as a typical nobody in the Jianghu world (Ancient China filled with fantasy martial arts) who strives to be a master kungfu fighter as he adventures throughout the land.

Most of your time will be spent in a seclusive valley, together with your master and fellow disciples training in martial arts, practicing skills such as hunting, fishing, mining (aka Carpal Tunnel Syndrome Simulator), going outside, and whatnot in a turn-based text and graphics game. One thing to pay attention to is your mood during this phase, as happier moods reward more experience for activities.

The second part of this game is real time strolling through the various locations in the game world, doing quests and exploring. The biggest setback here is there is a time limit, not only for exploring the town/city but for certain quests as well. With the massive amount of things to do in the game (almost approaching MMORPG level), it is annoying to find your freedom arbitrarily limited. Furthermore, there's no map or detailed quest tracker, making managing your time even harder.

I've also seen critical reviews about bad or non-existent translation, so far I've only seen a few of those, and they don't detract from the experience that much, IMO. If anything, this game has some of the funniest dialogues in video games.

There is a HUGE number of set-encounters in the game, whether it be for flavoring/immersiveness or just pure combat. There are many secrets, hidden paths, and choices for players to explore, in true classic RPG spirit. The combat is turn-based but fast, requiring you to pay attention and plan out moves on higher difficulties. You can learn many different martial arts, unlike traditional class-bound RPGs.

For fans of Wuxia fiction like me, this game is a must buy in a market saturated with your typical dragons, guns, dungeons, mana potions. It's also cool to see some of the characters resemble those in Jin Yong's novels. For newcomers to Wuxia, prepare to enter an exotic world, filled with exotic people, and kill/defeat them in hand-to-hand combat :)
