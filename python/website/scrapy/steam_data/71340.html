I haven't played any recent Sonic titles but I'm well aware of the criticisms they have received as far as bugs, camera angles and unoriginality are concerned. I went into Sonic Generations with an open mind, but with awareness that I might be disappointed. Thankfully I can say that all my concerns were for naught. In short, I have had an absolute blast playing Generations, far more than I expected.

The 2D levels are a great trip into nostalgia as well as providing new experiences for me. I've only played one 3D sonic game and that was about 12 years ago. Some infrequent camera issues aside caused by intense speeds, I haven't had any struggles at all with the 3D levels.

The music is fantastic, I especially love the modern Chemical Plant Zone, Crisis City and Rooftop Run tracks (the latter of which is currently stuck in my head). There is also surprisingly high amounts of customization in the skill system, something I didn't even know existed before playing. I can pick between many skills to suit my gameplay needs such as recovery abilities or a single choice of a handful of powerful skills with gameplay changing impacts.

Add in the fact that there are multiple challenges and collectibles for both zones (evenly spread across 2D and 3D) as well as each level completion giving you ranks and the option to try to get the best possible rank for each zone and challenge and you'll find that for the completionist, there is a massive amount of content to be had.

I figured I would pick the game up, do a quick run through and then never come back. Instead this has been my game of choice for the last two weeks and I still haven't reached the end (partly due to time constraints). This is a great game and lives up to the standards set by the Genesis era.
