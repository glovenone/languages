Clash of clans in space. That pretty much sums up the core mechanic of VEGA Conflict.
You own a space base, and you manufacture fleets of ships to destroy other players space bases, and farm NPC ships for loot and building materials. Additionally, ships aren't lost when they are used, unlike clash of clans, and you can directly give orders to your ships, which actually makes for a rather strategic combat system.

Unfortunately, while the game's concept may be sound, the game is ultimately ruined by the corrupting evil known as KIXEYE, the game's developer. As you will notice after playing for about a day, build times in this game are staggeringly long. High level ships can take literally weeks to build, and can take days to repair once they are destroyed.
Of course, if you do not want to wait, you can spend real money to speed up the repair.

KIXEYE capitalizes on this shamelessly, as the repair time of your ships depends on their HP. The higher the HP, the higher the repair time, which is effectively a way to shackle any players unwilling to spend coins. (Coins are the premium currency.) Whales that spend mass amounts of cash can effectively use their ships as much as they want, even if they are destroyed, and can use them to freely destroy the ships of players that cannot afford to spend the money to get instant repairs.

Those who cannot afford to be whales must either settle for weaker ships with shorter repair times, or must accept the fact that their play session will be over the moment a whale sets their beady gaze upon their fleets.
We're talking 8 hour repair times at high level play, people.

What's worse, KIXEYE has a penchant for releasing powerful blueprints in events; these allow you to build ships with new and overpowered weapons. Of course, to win these events, you need to have powerful ships.
You have two choices when these events arrive; farm for literally 4 hours a day for 3 days....or spend cash.
See the pattern? But wait! If you're not a whale, and you can't get the special gear from the events, that means the whales get access to something you can't have. So the whales get stronger, beat your fleets/base to a pulp, and the whole cycle continues.

And if say, you don't mind spending a little bit of money in a F2P game, good luck!
Since most of the players that remain are new and naive, achievement hunters (such as myself), or whales that drop thousands of dollars a month on the game, KIXEYE has placed the highest possible prices in place that it knows the whales will be willing to pay. Skipping a 5 hour repair will cost you 500-1000 "coins"; that's 5 to 10 dollars! PER SHIP!
To insta-repair an entire end-game fleet will cost you 30-60 dollars! That's right, for the price of Fallout 4, you can skip the repair cost on your fleet of ships. SUCH WORTH.

To add insult to injury, KIXEYE feels they can do no wrong, and holds nothing but resentment and disgust for their player base. They refuse to change, and they won't add anything to the game, unless they think it will get people to spend coins. They won't tolerate criticism, even when warrented, and they take offense every time the playerbase DARES to tell KIXEYE what they want (and don't want) from the game. (Take a look at their recent idea for a "high counsel.")

Unless you yourself are prepared to spend thousands of dollars on this glorified, misplaced mobile game, or are as insane as I am, and simple must have the achievements, stay far away from this game, and the foul developers responsible for it.
