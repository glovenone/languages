About This Game

King Banana accidentally dropped his Ice Cream Wife, spreading her pieces across the universe! Only a Cool brat like Cool Conrad can help him to overcome this disaster! Cool Conrad will gather the Ice Cream Crystals and face bizarre dangers for a chance to marry Princess Cheesecake!

As Cool Conrad, the player must bounce from planet to planet, dodging any dangers in his way, like green cupcakes, dragon cows and many more!

The goal is to obtain the different Ice Cream Crystals in the correct order and bite the cheesecakes to unlock new worlds!


Features:
50+ Levels
8 Worlds to Explore
Intense and challenging gameplay
Up to 4 players co-op multiplayer!

Samurai Vader

Posted: 6 January
Summary:
Cheesecake Cool Conrad is a platformer based on planet jumping in the crazy world. I must admit I've expected mediocre game but this is actually a positive surprise for the price! Whole crazy hiphop/funky/arty style of visuals and sound just perfectly fits within the gameplay.

Important thing to say about Cheesecake Cool Conrad, besides characters being cool, is the difficulty. This game definitely isn't for impatient people. You aren't limited by any timers to beat the level, you just have to be patient to jump through the planets in right moments to find all ice cream crystals (and marry the princess in the end).

Pros:
- Great platformer gameplay. Gravity might feel weird (at the price of more arcade platforming) but the gameplay overall is really enjoyable.
- Unique visuals and sounds. There's many crazy ideas used in the game, you can make your impression from screenshots at least.
- Hip hop and funky soundtrack. I'm not listening to those music genres but it perfectly fits into the game. This connection feels even better there than in Sideway New York, which was completely styled into graffiti and hiphop style.
- Levels aren't completely linear. There are levels which feels more like mazes with planets and you'll have to find the right way.
- Full gamepad support. My Xbox 360 gamepad worked immediately without any requirements to config the game.
- Low price.
- Local co-op for up to 4 players.

Cons:
- Requires being patient. So if rage and impatience are your traits, you should rather not buy this game.
- No achievements.

I found this to be a lot of fun. It is the same kind of light repetitive difficult fun as Super Meat Boy, for example. But it does allow you to save your progress and give you infinite lives, which I really like. The story is simple, with nice characters, and the art (especially the planets and game menus!) and soundtrack are pretty awesome.
The direction controls are a little strange, particularly moving from a planet to another, because of the difference in their gravities. It takes a few tries to get used to them, but you eventually do. Also, I've been told the developers are working on an update to improve this. (I haven't tried using a controller, so I can't really say anything about controller support, which the other reviewer had problems with.)
If you're a fan of games like Super Meat Boy, you really should check this out.

This is a pretty fun game

The mechanics are very easy to learn, though moving around the planets can take a little time to get used to. T
he whole thing is pleasantly straightforward.

GET THE CRISTALS.
GET THE CAKES.
REACH THE EXIT.
DO NOT DIE.

As you progress, these basic objectives become actually very challenging. Sometimes you need quick reflexes to dodge obstacles. Sometimes you need to make a very precise sequece of jumps. Sometimes you need to really use your brain to find your way through.
The game makes a good job at mixing things up. Dull moments are a bit rare, tough sometimes, when you die, the moving planets might take a while to reset, and that waiting is a bit annoying, but certainly nothing gamebreaking or that migth spoil the experience.

Another highlight that deserves a big note are the graphic and sound designs.
The gaphics are neat, fun and clear, with interesting characters and funny enemies, things change a lot from a world to another, so its hard to get tired of the colorful and unique art style.
The music is fun too, it's a cool jazz improvisation that goes very well with the funny graphic design and the simple yet charming animations.

Overall, it makes for a pretty neat experience.
Certainly worth a look.

Extremely addicting game and fairly challenging too. I found myself obsessing over completing the levels, once you get two crystals in there is basically no going back because you don't want to quit mid level and have to do the whole thing over again. Some levels are much, much harder than others and the difficulty definitely goes up with each new world. I definitely utilized a lot of the save points, and liked that there were infinite deaths. On one hand, it is nice to not have to reset the level after a certain amount of deaths, on the other when you get over x20 deaths it really starts to hurt your self esteem lol.

Not very many bugs, however on levels where there were a lot of enemies shooting things I experienced quite a bit of loading lag that made the already difficult challenges even MORE challenging. Also, at 6-2 I had a problem where I had to keep resetting the level to complete it because after a certain amount of jumps the fish planets wouldn't reappear if I died trying to get a crystal. I don't know if that was intentional to add to the difficulty, but I didn't see it utilized in subsequent levels so I Assume that it was a bug.

Overall, great game, pretty smooth gameplay, appropriately challenging, addicting, and fantastic music.
