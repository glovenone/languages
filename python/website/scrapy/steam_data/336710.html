About This Game

Don your cape and mask and join the Supreme League of Patriots in this hilarious and irreverent superhero comedy adventure!

In Issue 2, Kyle's transformation into the Purple Patriot is progressing unabated, and he's discovering a lot of new prejudices he never had before. While Mel fears for the sanity of his friend, The Purple Patriot sets about creating his own superhero team. He soon discovers that a wall of bureaucracy stands between him and his dreams of becoming an official Manhattan superhero. Surrounded by temptations, he's forced to decide whether a campaign of justice can really be founded on some pretty nefarious practices.

Features:

  • Classic point and click gameplay with a hilarious modern sense of humor
  • Two playable characters of Kyle Keever aka The Purple Patriot and his sarcastic sidekick Mel
  • Battle the dastardly red tape of the New York bureaucracy!
  • Discover, befriend and declare your enemies from the cityâs zany and colorful cast of superheroes and archenemies
  • Fantastic original soundtrack composed by Jake "Virt" Kaufman

After getting hit in the head, Kyle's personality changed totally as the Purple Patriot. His quest to become a true super hero begins: between framing people, buying a car and finding a patriot cave, his day will be filled! Always with his sidekick Melvin, will our dear hero be the hero the town needs? Who is the Bleeding Heart? And what will happen to the Flamboyant Hero? And Nurse Julie? And the Cold War Warrior? And Consuela?

If you don't mind, I'll copy/paste parts of my review for the first episode, because some remarks for it is valid for Patriot Frames

The core mechanics is a point-and-click. But I wish that the inventory/utility belt was easier to use. I mean, why should I open the inventory for combining items and use the utility belt to... use them in the scene? Being original is good. But being practical is also a good option. I was really confused at that. I would have prefer the combine option in the utility belt. Why? Because I can easily the utility belt by placing my cursor at the bottom of the screen instead of opening the top of my screen and make a click on the box to open the inventory.

Another gameplay problem is that you can easily obtain items... but can't use them before speaking to a character. I don't have an exact example in the head - though I know that for episode 3, I had to ask help to Melvin (as he's also the one giving hints) and it was the only way to go forward.

The dialogues are still in the comedy mood: with the Purple Patriot being "mean" (Kyle is a sweet guy, when he's Kyle and you'll see it only once in episode 2). Thank god, Melvin is the wise guy and his job got harder with the personality change. But now, I understand that the new Purple Patriot is a parody of all the people that could be racist, sexist, homophobic but at an extreme. Besides, while I had a little hard time sometimes to solve the puzzles, I must say that I warmed up to the game.

Yet, achievements unlocking every five minutes is really boring after a while. And some are missable, as they're kinda all "hidden".

Anyway, the graphics or the soundtrack are still the same: in a comedy mood, cartoonish and with new locations.

Buy it when it's discounted or in a bundle but I suggest you strongly to play the first episode if you want to follow the story and understand all the references.

Review - Supreme League of Patriots

Supreme League of Patriots is a point and click game wiwth a dash of humor and comedy. The game let's you take the role of both Kyle and Mel as they venture through the world of superheroes and supervillains. The story is a bit slow on the build up but the actual plot is very unique and original. Each issue comprises about 3-4 hours of gameplay depending on how you solve the puzzles in the game. The puzzles are easy and you won't get off track much since Melvin is there to guide you when you don't know/forgot what you should be doing. The dialgoues and voices are great especially Melvin's british accent.

Pros:
+ A great funny story plot
+ Great voice acting
+ Great Music (especially each character's BGM)
+ Good Graphics
+ Narrative guide (Mel gives you a hint when you get lost)

Cons:
+ There's a bit of bugs whenever you make Kyle go to an obtacle-full area
+ There are times that the voices become out of sync with the balloons/text that appear

If you're looking for a point and click adventure game full of humor, comedy, patriotism, superheroes and supervillains, then Supreme League of Patriots is a must try for you.

I already finished all 3 issues and I just can't wait for season 2! :D
