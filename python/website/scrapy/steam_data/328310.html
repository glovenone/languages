About This Game



Have you always dreamed of operating large loading cranes and transporting heavy loads across a logistics hub site using high-powered logistics vehicles?



As a dock worker in "Logistics Company” it’s up to you to move thousands of tons of cargo and goods and make sure that they’re shipped on time! Build your tiny warehouse into a major logistics hub and expand it bit by bit with new cargo terminals.

Control the high-powered reach stacker and the amazingly tall cranes to load freight onto trains, trucks and ships. Manoeuvre the forklift through your warehouse. Only if you work hard and plan cleverly can you become a major logistics service provider!

Features:



  • Simulation with management aspects
  • Build up your own logistics company and earn enough money to expand your site and purchase new vehicles
  • Control all high-powered vehicles and cranes yourself to load goods: forklift, container stacker, straddle carrier, bridge and port crane
  • Expand your site with parking lots, storage space and a port to enable you to accept even bigger orders
  • Complete difficult but lucrative orders by handling fragile goods carefully or loading containers within a time limit
  • Act cleverly and strategically to load your containers smartly and in time
  • Learn from an animated and voiced 3D mentor (English/German), who will introduce you to the secrets of dock work

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows Vista/7/8
    • Processor: Athlon®/Pentium IV® or comparable 2 GHz processor
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 512 MB 3D graphics card with DirectX 9.0 and Shader 3.0 support or higher
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
    • Sound Card: Sound card
    • Additional Notes: keyboard, mouse with scroll wheel

Posted: 2 March, 2015
This game was released November 5th of 2014, making it four months old. Normally, for a game that is so new, you'd be okay with the bugs and patiently wait for the developers to fix the issues. The biggest issue with this game isn't even a fault of the game - the developers have given up on the title already. This is not confirmed, or something that the developers have straight up said, however, the last update to the game, as well as the last developer/staff interaction we had was in December, the exact day they released an update. To the developer's credit, they fixed issues that had the game crashing. However, we have gone three months without any sort of word from the developers. I was told that development stopped in November, meaning they had this update done in the middle, or toward the end of November, and just completely stopped development after that.

Lets move onto some of the bugs that have been encountered and reported. The first one I encountered was that a train was supposed to have two of the same company brand containers on it. However, after receiving one container, it'd leave! The other container would then be accepted onto the second train that was there for different cargo. While the train took the wrong brand container, it of course didn't end the job because it was on the wrong train. I reloaded the game several times, but it was a constant issue. This indicates that two of the contracts were conflicting with each other.

The second issue was that the truck you pick up and remove pallets from will often despawn when you're still on it. Most of the time, the truck waits until you're off of it, and then drives away. When it does despawn, you're left out front, possibly with a pallet that you're now supposed to drive all the way around (slowly, I may add, as forklifts aren't fast) the warehouse just to get back in and unload the pallet or park the forklift back inside.

Another issue was the physics. My truck would often get stuck on fences, light posts, and/or walls. Sometimes if I tried hard enough, I could wiggle it free, but most of the time the truck would have to be reset.

http://i.imgur.com/s2kZHlX.jpg

Looking on the Logistics Community Forum, there is no community anymore. Yes, you may get one post or two from myself or someone else, but those are either bug reports, asking for help, or asking if the developers are even around.

It's really sad and frustrating to see that the developers put money and time into this game and got it released, then got one update out, and then abandoned the game all together. The saddest part is that this is a fun game with really good graphics, and does what a good simulator game should - make you feel like you're actually a worker. When you take on a lot of jobs, and end up with multiple containers, you find yourself wanting to stack them together and keep them appropriately stored so you know where they are, you know how many there are, and you know if you're getting a little more containers than you can handle.

I got this game just a few days ago, and already put in 20 hours just because of how addicting and fun it can be. However, because of the lack of updates and lack of proper fixes, there's a good chance I'm not playing this game again.

I do not recommend this game, especially with it being $20. It is simply not worth that much, and unless the developers get their sh*t in order, it will never be worth more than $10 (but even $10 is being generous in this instance).
