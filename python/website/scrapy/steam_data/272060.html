About This Game

How long has it been? A man sits in a distant getaway cabin waiting for his wife Serena. Where is she? Things in the cabin evoke memories, and the husband comes to a disturbing realization...

This short point-and-click adventure is the result of a massive collaborative effort between dozens of fans and designers of adventure games. Rallying to support the venerable genre and its passionate community, these developers have brought together a grim and highly detailed horror story in what is the largest, possibly even the first, game project of its kind. Talents include crew from renowned indie companies Senscape, CBE Software, Infamous Quests, Digital Media Workshop, Guys from Andromeda, and many more.

Features

  • A (twisted) love letter to the adventure game community
  • Over 40 contributors provided their wit and talents
  • Extremely depressive mood, and a bit of fear here and there
  • A haunting story that will challenge your intellect (and temerity)
  • Amazing voice acting by legendary adventure game designer Josh Mandel
  • More awesome acting by popular video producer Pushing Up Roses
  • Yet another overwhelmingly astonishing surprise cameo by an ex-designer from Sierra!
  • Did we mention the gut-wrenching atmosphere?

Serena is a really excellent game. Though it is not terrifying and the gameplay consists of clicking on things a lot (it's a point-and-click game, but you have to click on the same thing repeatedly), the way that Serena's story is conveyed is spectacular. You start off in your little cabin with amnesia (not fugue, just amnesia). As the game goes on and you click on everything around the house, the narrator tells stories related to those objects. The more the game goes on, the more the stories piece together and the more you really figure out what's going on. It's perhaps the most mind-bending game I've ever played, and I can't recommend it more. Just don't expect extreme scares. There's a decent atmosphere and a few scary moments, but nothing too severe. The experience is what makes the game so great.

I will say that Serena is incredibly short, and will only take about 45 minutes to an hour to complete, but it's free, so that's really not a big deal. Also, after you realize what's actually going on, you're probably going to want to play it again to see what clues you didn't pick up the first time, doubling the play time.

This game is free. And it won't take up too much of your time. You have literally nothing to lose. Play this game.

Serena is a free point and click game.
You are a man, who's obsessed by a "Serena". You look around the cabin that you're in and try and get clues about what is going on. The story is interesting, if at some point kind of annoying, since the main character does whine in some cases too much. But that can't be thrown against the game, since that decision might've been deliberate and it might've been the point.
The voice acting is truly great. Especially for a free indie game.
The sound effects are proper and well made. It just adds to the atmosphere.
The visuals are fitting, but in some cases maybe slightly too low resolution. It's still ok, if you don't focus on the floor board textures too much.
But the story... it was weird. Truly weird. Having played through this game, I still don't think that I've figured out what happened. It's definitely well made and polished quite a bit.
Do I recommend this? Oh yeah! Approximately 25-60 minutes of free entertainment is never a bad idea. Give it a shot!
Truly, one of the better Point and Click games.
Overall
8/10
