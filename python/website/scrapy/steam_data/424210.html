SodaCity 1.2 - UX Update

Because all the feedback we received and the post release experience we had, we were able to see the game from another perspective.

That allowed us to make in this version the fixes, modifications and new implementations in order to make SodaCity the best it could be. We know SodaCity has some design flaws, there are some things we could have done better but generally speaking we think it has good and great things.

We upgraded the game engine from Unity 4.3 to Unity 5.3 gaining some performance improvements as well as new features like the in-game sound mixer.
As we were already fixing the game to fit the new game engine, we revisited the game to make improvements on different areas of the game like: weapon settings and behavior, enemy hitpoints and damage, general game progression, improved the feedback to the user, etc.

With all these changes we aimed to keep the original 'vibe' of SodaCity while improving the general game experience.

Major Changes:
  • Code base migrated from Unity 4.3 to Unity 5.3.
  • Improved gameplay UI.
  • Mixed in-game audio.
  • Added reverb SFX per level.
  • Linux version.
  • New drop animation.
  • Enemies won't fire if not completely on screen.
  • Improved controller input.
  • Reduced enemies armor points for the first 3 zones.
  • Reduced KanLee's Crossbow damage.
  • Increased SMGs damage.
  • Decreased SMG burst rate.
  • Increased Rifles damage.
  • Decreased Rifles burst rate.
  • Decreased Crossbow reload speed.
  • Decreased Weapon in-game prices.
  • Decreased Weapon upgrades in-game prices.
  • Increased arrow in-game price.
  • Decreased grenade in-game price.

Please help us spread the word.
Post your reviews on Steam, only with your feedback we will know what we did right and what we did wrong.

We hope you enjoy and like this new version. Please feel free to contact us for any comments or questions you may have.

Thank you to the people that has been playing SodaCity. and to the ones that shared their feedback with us.

About This Game

History

SodaCity is a fictional metropolis where inhabitants are soda cans, dominated by the organized crime, violence and warfare.

You play as Mr. Redd, who’s on a personal vendetta against SodaCity’s organized crime. Mr. Redd is a retired bounty hunter who seeks to avenge his brother’s death, carrying a one-can assault on every single criminal territory until he destroys every one of the city’s bosses.

Features

Experience a modern take on old school game genres:
SodaCity is heavily based on classic beat ‘em ups from the SNES era but the main gameplay mechanic is shooting, so we ended with a shoot ‘em up with the weird, tilted, 2.5D perspective of classic beat ‘em ups.

Try out 38 upgradeable weapons:
Weapons differentiate themselves by their different stats, which are: Damage, Range, Ammo per clip, reload speed, Rate of Fire. We focused on making every weapon type relevant for different situations.

Fight your way across 18 levels in 6 different zones:
As you progress through the game, you will dismantle parts of the main crime organization. The difficulty progresses as more “intelligent” and “emotionally” stable enemies appear with more powerful weapons.

Overcome 7 boss battles:
Each boss is the leader of an specific crime group. In order to clear a zone you need to beat the corresponding boss.
Boss fights operate as separate levels. Each boss have unique attacks that offer a different type of challenge.

Bullet Time!
Each kill has a small probability of activating the bullet time effect, in which time slows down but the player gets to move faster than the enemies, offering an advantage for a limited time. It’s possible to add time by increasing the combo meter.

Full controller support:
Because the amount of challenge and the comfort that a controller provides, we recommend to play SodaCity with controller.
On PC the game was developed and tested using a wired Xbox360 gamepad controller.
On Mac the game was developed and tested using a PS3 gamepad controller.

SodaCity is a small indie game that offers old-school gamers a familiar (yet distant) experience; one of the retro shoot-'em-ups, in classic 2D side-scrolling perspective with countless hours of fun distpaching enemies left and right and dying over and over again .

This game is pretty straightforward, very what-you-see-if-what-you-get.
The gameplay, as I described, is old-school cool that us adult gamers (at the time of my review anyway) will feel at home with, but it's also worth noting that once you've played a few hours, it's easily discernible that it's also deeper than it seems at first glance. Truly, each type of weapon offers very distinct possibilities, for example.
The graphics and visuals are retro with their peculiar aesthetic which I found charming and no doubt not everyone will necessarily like, but it's pretty neat and effective.
The audio is good too; there's clear cues for everything, distinct sounds and a simply superb soundtrack/score with music not only greatly done but also varied and fitting for each scenario and atmosphere.

Although the game might at first seem to rely on repetition of a number of goals or objectives, after a little replay each area and each evidently has its own thing going on, its own flavor, and it's easy to recall them and formulate strategies for them. That should occur naturally after being beaten many times over the course of a level. That leads me to the point that this game is challenging, but I never found it to be excessively so or otherwise unfair.
It's also worth noting that the game does offer a fair amount of replayability, especially for those who don't mind grinding/farming a little bit to enjoy the benefits of fully upgraded weapons. That also begs the distinction that grinding in this game doesn't become much of a chore, it hardly forces the player to do so and those who choose so will have more fun than dull repetition in seeking to gain more currency for upgrades or better weapons.
SodaCity, all in all, offers more than I initially thought and it will be a game to go back to every once in a while for sure. The fun is there, the appeal of a great game in a genre that seems long-forgotten in the industry is there, the replay value of the game itself without the nostalgia attached is there.


Last, but not least, I simply want to go over some of the details other reviews are criticizing which I found unfair, especially given how at the time of my review, those reviews have hardly two hours or less game time, and thus, in my opinion, extremely limited play to accurately judge the game as a whole. Here's what I think:

-Some reviws call SodaCity out on the graphics... to this I ask, what is one to expect from an indie game from a small team? Super Meat Boy, The Binding of Isaac, Terraria, Monaco and even better, Hotline Miami hardly offer much more in that department. None of them are AAA games, even if some did achieve similar sales, and that doesn't keep them from being awesome games.
-Quick deaths/dying a lot... Let us remember that this game, by its very genre if nothing else, can easily be seen as an ode to old-time games, from consoles such as the NES, SNES, Genesis, etc... I never beat many of those games. Videogames back then used to be hard and you used to die a lot trying. Some modern games have even appealed to that principle, such as the illustrious Souls series. I find this more of a taste-type-thing than a fault within the game.
-The aesthetic or lack of a deeper-developed story. As I mentioned previously, shooting ♥♥♥♥♥♥ off soda cans may not be for everybody, but dammit if it isn't fresh and indeed silly! It's good stuff for those can don't need every game to be serious and have an encyclopedia worth of lore.


Summary:
SodaCity isn't a game for everyone, the genre certainly isn't trending the way near-future FPS or medieval-fantasy RPGs are. But for adult-aged gamers, there is a ton of appeal to be found here, and to anyone willing to give a fair shot, tons of fun to be had as well. I found the first few levels to be among the slower parts, but if you can play a little bit beyond that, I'm betting it can easily draw most people in and have them hooked for days.
This is a solid and promising start for a young development studio. Imagine a sequel with full miltuplayer integration on console... Personally, I'd buy that with no second thoughts! Very much a recommendable game. Hopefully one of indie game's next big titles.

Posted: 10 December, 2015
This key was obtained from the developer for review purposes.

Summary:
A game that lacks punch, and the bigger issue is the game design complementing the genre in question. There are reasons that several basic game design rules are in place for side scrolling shooters, it's there to complement the action in question. Here, the game is somewhere between boring and frustrating. Very quick times to die from a barrage of bullets, in particular with bosses, will frustrate players as it overly punishes simple mistakes that can easily be made. Developers need to be careful of making comparisons or indicating influences of past games, as using TMNT: Turtles in Time and Battletoads & Double Dragon as strong influences for the game set up expectations of quality that the game's staff and resources may not be able to reach, even with the most skilled programmers in the world. SodaCity has core problems that makes me not able to recommend it, and the game lacks impact for the price point that it's being offered for.


Gameplay Footage and Video Review: https://youtu.be/5aEiLOixEms

Lists:
+:
  • Upgrade system is reasonable, gives reasons to use the currency and while it's rather basic, it works.
  • The music for being done by the developer is reasonable, there's a reasonable selection and complements the main action.
  • Dual wield shotguns are always fun.

-:
  • The game forgets basic side scrolling shooters basics. Time to die is too small, being overly punishing for a simple mistake.
  • In combination, the perspective of the combat makes it hard to see if your bullets are going to hit your opponents. In combination with the first point, that makes it easy to make mistakes.
  • The action doesn't keep up. The game is somewhat slow and can have some down periods that don't really help the action.
  • Guns lack impact.
  • Bosses in particular are pretty punishing, they'll one hit kill you and will lead to frustration.
  • Aesthetic doesn't really help the game. In particular, elements with the glow of the powerups don't really do a great job of drawing your attention in the right way. f
  • Story elements fall apart, as they seem to be introduced, and then are forgotten about.
