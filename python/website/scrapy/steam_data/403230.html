About This Game


Prepare yourself to get in a fantastic world of adventure, action and fun.
Prepare yourself for ZombieZoid - Zenith!

While the lovers were chatting on the Internet, the news reported that a terrible virus was infecting people in town and turning them into zombies hungry for brains. Now, this young boy will venture through the streets filled with undeads to reach the house of his girlfriend before the worst happens.
As Arthur, prove you are brave and confront the danger that you find ahead.
Your adventure begins at home that was invaded by kids zombies from the neighborhood. You will need to knock out everyone to protect the grandpa that is resting int the attic.
Those kids zombies are the most agile of all, therefore you need to be skillful to defeat them before they try to bite you from behind.
For twelve levels you will face strong enemies in unusual and comic situations.

The Tasmania Spin

An air vortex will be created around Arthur and will hit at every zombie that try to get close to you

Using energies from the future, Arthur receive a big power and conjure the Rush Hour, pushing all the zombies in front of him and leaving a large trail of fire.

All the prisioners were cursed and were transformed into skulls. Be careful with fire invoked from the depths of evil. Water attacks can help you.

The General is trying to organize all this mess but the zombies are very messy, it gets him very angry. Be careful not to be caught off guard when he digs.

The forest is a mess because of these zombies, let alone all the soldiers. Mack is very strong and can finish you in a few strokes. His leap is so strong that can even topple trees.

There is an accident on the road and Otto is trying to help the passengers zombies to get out. He said that nobody can pass over here. He's throwing tires to stop anyone that tries to pass.

The Arthur's mother in law is unhappy, because it seems that every zombie from the city decided to invade her house. Be carful because she's dominated and she can transform herself into multiple enemies.


Through the course of the game Arthur may collect many weapons like the powerful baseball bat that is his faithful companion; many type of water pistols, bottles of holy water and even popcorn to trick the most stupid zombies.

A fun little zombie romp. This is the type of game you play when you just want to relax and zone out for a couple of hours. Nothing challenging, just a colourful, mindless, zombie-bash-a-thon.

It clocks in at around 3 hours from start to finish so it doesn't outstay its welcome. At first I was appalled at the graphics and horrendous job of optimisation, but it didn't take long for the cartoonish style to grow on me. The zombies themselves are quiet varied, and the game throws plenty of easy bosses at you along the way. Unfortunately the majority of combat is just bash the attack button and then throw out your special attack once its ready (there are multiple special attacks, but when you get the best one right off the bat, that being the jump and stomp move, there really is no need to mix it up). There is only one type of powerup, beyond health items, which lets you speed up noticeably for a decent period and hit harder. There are stores where you can trade the coins you harvest from the zombies but you'll rarely bother as the zombies have healing items coming out their ears. In terms of weapons there isn't a huge variety. You'll end up hitting zombies with a baseball bat for 80% of the time, or your fists if it breaks on you. And the rest is ranged water pistols, holy water (turns zombies against each other), and popcorn (to distract zombies).

The basic story is your girlfriend is stranded in the middle of the zombie apocalypse and its your job to rescue her. This is the very height of it so don't be expecting a convoluted plot. This minimal presentation however has you visiting an impressive array of environments, all of them appearing and disappearing one after another in quick succession so it doesn't grow stale. They range from flooded church basements to ravaged towns, to mines, to army camps, to a haunted house. There isn't much to do but run across them and kill or outpace zombies as you go, clocking up one objective after another. It's very linear, and the most the game has you doing in terms of "puzzles" is pick up the key/explosive to open door/clear barrier. There are a range of other collectibles but as none of them appeared to do anything they quickly fell by the wayside for me, and it just became of soothing journey of zombie whack-a-mole. There is the very odd bit of imprecise platforming but thankfully this is kept to an absolute minimum.

People have complained about bugs/crashes in other reviews but the developers seem to have fixed the majority by now. I didn't have a single issue the whole time.

Not everyone will find this fun and it isn't worth full price but get it on sale if you want a lazy evening to turn your brain off.

Also be warned, this game does not have developers who speak native English, so be prepared for some ridiculous dialogue. Thankfully there isn't much, and it mainly consists of your character's hints whenever you come up to a door/barrier.
