About This Game

Welcome to TLE, you will be taking control of a spaceship as it's commander. Your goal will be to locate and destroy an enemy military platform.
You must manage a crew who have specific needs and make decisions quickly! What will you do if your OÂ² generator is damaged and it no longer produces enough oxygen for all of your crew? It is up to you to make the right decisions. All this in an open world with RPG elements. You are free to do what you want in a completely randomly generated universe, full of events that will benefit you or not ....

Main Characteristics :


  • Evolved in a completely random world : - The world is randomly generated for each game and every game. Nothing is planned in advance, any events are generated as you choose your journey. Once you have selected a destination there is no turning back.
  • RPG system : - Each ship will have different basic statistics with the ability to grow in power as you level.
  • A living crew : - You need to meet the needs of your crew Food, oxygen and not to mention their salary. Each crew member that can not be paid, will desert your ship. Every crewmember will have a special skill to improve your ship.
  • Total freedom : - Once in your new ship, you are free to advance in the game as you want. You will be hired or fired, but know that your ship will require improvements for you to survive, and therefore need money!
  • Random events : - Each new destination will bring new challenges. Will you meet an enemy ship? Or a merchant ship? Pay attention and react accordingly!
  • A slow and painful death : - Remember one thing. Once you are dead, it's over. No turning back, The slightest mistake or a bad decision and you will have to start over again!

Upcoming Features :


  • Planetary exploration : - You have the chance to get down on planets and explore randomly generated worlds.
    You must adjust depending on the gravity, atmosphere , monsters and NPC's that will be present.
  • Additional events : - The space is unpredictable, other random events to come out over time.
  • Dynamic events : - Once the game is "stable", you will have updates that will include dynamic events, the occurence of flagship, or an enemy commander, for example, that will save a lot of resources if you can defeat him. It is very hard to survive in the world of TLE, and through these events you are going to be a big help, which can eventually make you win the game! To you to keep you informed, announcements will be made in advance so that you can prepare.
  • Other surprises await you : - I will not reveal the rest, but know that other additions are planned for the longer term [/ list]

TLDR opinion; I would recommend The Light Empire as a nice little game if you are seeking a distraction. Worth a look if you are into sci-fi themed and / or rogue-like games but NOT if you are looking for an RPG.

Please note that this is a cautious thumbs up, the game is improving steadily but is still very rough and simplistic in some respects.


Now, what to say about The Light Empire?

The good:
This game is very open and honest about what it is; a rogue-like game based in a sci-fi setting in a randomly generated universe. It playes well to those classifications, offering some nice diversity in encounters and destinations. It also plays well on the rogue-like side of things, no two trips will play out exactly the same and so there is definite replayability here.

The RPG elements it claims in the description are definitely present but they are not that in-depth, aside from being able to customise the stats of your ship when you begin the game. There are some flavour texts in some of the events which is a nice touch and some unlockable ships but from what I have played there is no real overarching story or world development, being able to adjust the stats on your ship is the meat of it.

Some effort has also clearly gone into the presentation of this game, there are some good images and textures involved and a good amount of detail can be seen on the in-game assets. Sound-wise it is distinctive, having some strange and otherworldly sound effects and music choices that may catch you by surprise but help give this game a unique feel to it.


The bad:
Okay, yes, there are bad things I see in this game. First off is the UI which feels a bit clunky and unresponsive. It is also cluttered, presenting you with all of your stats and resources all of the time while not offering tool tips or explanations. There is, however, a small guide built into the hud that you can access at any time for a quick overview of things (READ IT!).

The game also feels a bit slow-paced overall and the interaction and feedback are not that good. There are many things you do that feel a bit drawn out and slow, listening to or watching things without needing any real action on your part as you wait for it to finish. This plays as part of the aesthetic design of the game but here I do feel like it gets in the way a bit. But I must admit, the sound when you set off a distress beacon because you have run out of fuel is the most unique sound for such a thing I have ever heard.

Lastly it feels just a bit repetative and slapped together. The former could be due to a lack of varieties in some of the sprites (like all planets of a certain type look the same) while how the galaxy exploration is handled is effectively random things popping up on a starscape. It feels a little like a flash game in that regard.


So. Conclusions?

Overall I would have to say yes, I recommend this game. Is it worth the asking price? Probably. If it continues to develop after release (which it looks like it might) then it can only get better and it does show a lot of potential. Will it provide a diversion and entertainment? Probably. If you are into sci-fi or space themed games and / or into rogue-likes trying to see how far you can get, then yes, this might be a game you would enjoy. If you are after an RPG game definitely look elsewhere.

This is the first game on steam from this developer and I think, honestly, that is its only real failing. It does feel like it is an early effort and could do with a lot more polishing or play-testing to help smooth things out, tighten it up a bit, and so on... but if you look past that you have the solid core of a rogue-like game in space. It may not have all the bells and whistles it could have but it is fully functional and does have its moments. So it is definitely worth a look and I look forward to seeing what this guy comes up with in the future.
