About This Game

When life robs Colton White of all that matters, the only thing left he can trust is his GUN. From award-winning developer, Neversoft, and accomplished screenwriter, Randall Jahnson (The Mask of Zorro, The Doors), GUN follows Colton on his quest for discovery as he seeks to exact vengeful justice on those who have wronged him. GUN is a realistic epic action/adventure that lets gamers experience the brutality of the lawless West.

  • New and savage ways to punish: Stealthily sneak up and attack your enemies, use them as cover, stab and shoot them. Blast away and watch the aftermath, shoot guns out of enemy hands, or destroy with dynamite.
  • Extensive variety of missions and game play: Wage war on horseback, commandeer trains, protect prostitutes, collect bounties, play poker, hunt buffalo, and more. Multiple game play styles include: precision shooting, stealth tactics, and use of explosives. Embark on numerous side missions that allow you to master gun slinging and horse riding. Unlock secret weapons and upgrade your skills and abilities to improve your weapon and equipment performance.
  • Brutal realism: Act as a gunslinger protecting righteousness, or seek retribution as you face corrupt lawmen, warring tribes, cold-blooded outlaws, and ruthless renegades. Encounter legendary gunfighters such as JJ Webb. Employ a multitude of authentic weapons including: Six shooters, shotguns, Gatling guns, flaming arrows, hatchets, dynamite, and more.
  • Expansive interactive world: Play an extensive variety of exciting missions as you journey west across the new frontier. Ride on horseback through the scenic, yet dangerous Western terrain of canyons, mountains, plains, forests, gold mines and towns.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pr7QqZ3Tra8

For me this is one of my favorite games of all time and the best game set in the wild west. I know there are other, like Call of Juarez, but Gun, which is older, have far more interesting characters, world, story and I can safely say it's higher quality product, as far as the first Juarez. While the consoles have Red Dead Redemption, which by the looks of it is superior, PC have Gun, which is rough around the edges. I read somewhere, before RDR, Gun was the best western game. It may be rough, but this doesn't mean it's not fun. Unfortunately, it's far from perfect. It does have some problems, that might put off some people. It all depends of your tolerance for something far from perfect. Let me continue with bad stuff, so I can end on a positive note.

This is a console port and it's obvious. It's not the worst, but this could be unacceptable for some. The game runs at 30 fps, which is not such big deal, I can close my eyes, but in some situation dips below that. It's an open world game, but it feels like it's divide by sectors and when you reach the border of some sector, the game need to load the surroundings and fps drops below 30. It's for e second, but it's annoying.

There are some weird bugs. Few examples: NPC is giving me task and you can see his neck,arms and legs are not attached to the torso. In one side quest you can see in the distance, the enemies are riding â¦ the air, or the legs of a horse are not moving. These bugs are more funny, than game breaking. The graphics didn't aged very well. For the time they were decent.

Maybe the biggest problem is the presence of slight auto aim. Why is this in a PC game? It would be nice if I could turn it off. The answer is simple â console port. The developer of the game is Neversoft, which were famous with the Tony Hawk franchise, but for the port is responsible Beenox (known for the Spider-man games) and they didn't do good job. Here is the contradiction. Maybe the auto aim it's not a bad thing, since aiming with the mouse is not perfect, it's not that precise. I always compare 3rd person action games with Max Payne in this regard, because the aiming there is perfect for me and Gun is almost there.

There are other 2 small problems. The first is the AI â sometimes it's just stupid and second one is the enemy models. You will see the same characters over and over.
After all I said, maybe you are asking yourself is there any good things in the game? Yes, there is. Absolutely love the game. Almost 10 years later, you can see the higher quality of the game, starting with the voice acting. The main characters are voiced by famous hollywood actors, like Thomas Jane, Kriss Kristoffersen, Lance Henriksen, Brad Dourif, Ron Perlman, Tom Skerritt. Even the other voice actors are doing a great job.

As a hole the sound design is great. The weapons sound like they came out from old western movie, which contribute to the atmosphere of the game. The music is phenomenal. When a track hits, you feel like you are in great adventure. And it is great.

The studio hire hollywood screen writer and the outcome is pretty good story. You take the role of Colton White, who with his father, makes a living with hunting and selling the meat to a steamboat. The steamboat is attacked, because the bad guys are looking for something, that your father is hiding even from you. He dies, the boat is destroyed and from here starts your adventure. The story is great, but short, so don't rush with her.

So is there anything else to do? You have a lot of side quests, most of them consists of killing everything that moves. But there are others, like get a cattle back to the their pens, making deliveries, protect people from wolves. And of course there are wanted posters. With them you often have a choice between dead or alive, which respectively brings you less or more money. When you are given a choice to get someone alive, the thing you have to do is to shoot the horse, cut him once or twice with the knife and apprehend him. You don't need to kill his buddies. And what is the wild west without a game of poker. In the two towns there are tables to play. The AI is not that good, but here is not that important.

While walking around you can find gold, which you can dig. So the first thing you have to do when you see shopkeeper is to buy pickaxe. Other things you can buy from him are standard upgrades for your weapons, horse, but not for the bow. For him you have to visit an indian outside the towns. There are two shopkeepers in the game and they sell different upgrades.

From everything I said until now, I almost didn't touch the gameplay. It's good. If you put aside the auto aim, everything else is great. You have pretty standard arsenal of weapons â pistols, rifles, shotguns, sharpshooter, whiskey bomb, dynamite and bow. My favorite is the shotgun. At close range is irreplaceable. After almost each story mission you will be given new weapon, each with different stats. In saddlebag you can choose which one to equip. The ammo for everything is not endless and you can't buy it. You have to find boxes with them in specific places spread around the world. Only for the pistols are endless and for the rifle are most easy to find.

There is one more thing and it is Quickdraw. This is basically bullet time. With a press of a button, you slow down time and arrows appear to show you where the bad guys are. Then you pres left or right and start shooting without reloading. The feeling of killing 6-7 guys this way is awesome. When you slow down time, you can shoot arrows and dynamite, while there are in the air. Of course, you can move the crosshair with the mouse. In some cases it's very useful for more accurate shots. You can also go in first person view, for better shots with the rifle and bow. As far as the bosses, all I can say they are a lot of fun, especially the final one.

Here is one interesting fact. Activision merged Neversoft with developer Infinity Ward in May 2014. In August 2014 it was discovered that inside the latest DLC for Infinity Ward's game Call of Duty: Ghost there was an easter egg of a fake poster advertising Gun 2.
The game run absolutely fine on Windows 7, 64 bit. There is widescreen fix, but it stretching the HUD. When you type in 1 in HUD_PATCH inside gun_res.ini, it will fix the stretching, but the mini map will be almost in the middle of the screen.

Gun is forgotten and underrated game. It's worth buying right now, if you don't mind the port. But it's definitely have to be played by everyone, who loves open world games and western movies. Gun is like human â it has some shortcomings, but if you look close enough, you will see beautiful soul underneath. From here you have to decide if you want to overlook the flaws, for everything else or not.
