Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Exanima is an important first step in our ambitious quest to create a new kind of RPG. We are a passionate independent developer who works closely with the community to deliver the best game we can without compromising our own vision. We are doing this thanks to crowd-funding and having reached the point where we have a playable and polished game, Early Access seems like a natural evolution and ideal platform.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Around six to nine months, give or take three months! Honestly, we can't provide an exact timeframe as there are many factors involved, some of which are beyond our control. These include feedback from a wider community and, ultimately, Exanima's success which will determine our budget going forward.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“While Exanima already feels like a complete game there are many defining features yet to be added and the current content represents an introductory stage to a larger, more open and varied setting. Exanima itself is a prelude to our even larger open world game Sui Generis, most features will make an appearance in both games bringing the scope of Exanima far beyond what it may appear to be now.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“After a successful Kickstarter campaign we have been developing the core game with the support and feedback of an amazing, passionate community. Exanima has already gone through alpha and beta stages with hundreds of testers and our focus has been on quality. It is absolutely playable, stable and highly optimised. While we have much more work ahead of us it already offers a complete and enjoyable experience.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The Early Access price is set at a point we think is fair for the game as it is now and respresents great value if you consider the features, content and polish that we intend to add. The release price may increase to reflect a more substantial and complete product. We will never charge for DLC or introduce any kind of RMT.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Exanima is part of a crowd funded project and as such community feedback has already played a significant role in its development. This is set to continue, both on our forums and here where we will actively engage the Steam Community. We will not only rapidly respond to bug reports but discuss ideas, take onboard constructive criticism, incorporate well thought out suggestions and prioritise our goals based on their input.”

27 February

Exanima 0.6.2.0

We're working on a lot of different things at the moment, the biggest focus being on getting the rest of the story content done. We're expanding significantly on what can be done in terms of level design, it's going to take us a little while to get it all working, but it will make things a lot more interesting and varied. Once we've got the basics working we can resume doing more frequent smaller updates, and we will release some new content quite soon.

In this update we've got a couple of new fairly important features, and some really nice gameplay improvements.

Changes in 0.6.2

  • Switch between two sets of weapons with a key press

  • unconsciousness mechanic in story mode

  • Greatly improved combat movement control and smoothness

    • More precise and predictable control over dashes

    • Removed sways and stumbles when ending dashes

    • No more falling over from huge lunging swings

  • More realistic and satisfying character reactions to hits

  • Improved physics and behaviours when losing balance and falling

  • Reduced graphical output lag from simulation

  • Improved supersampling motion smoothing

  • Improvements to piercing and crushing damage mechanics

  • Improved thrusting with 2 handed weapons

  • Numerous animation improvements

  • AI combat and pathfinding improvements

  • Removed speedup after the simulation fails to remain synched

  • Many bug fixes and tweaks


UNCONSCIOUSNESS

Before you start thinking that we're trying to make the game easier, know that this feature was planned long before release. Those who have followed the game long enough can confirm this. One feature of our two-tiered health system is to allow both death and knockouts. Our earliest release of the game was just the practice arena, with an already skilled playerbase and initially very little content, we kept the failure on knockout, and the intended knockout mechanics are now long overdue.

From this point on when you get knocked out you will wake up again. Much like in the arena mode. As it is you will suffer some permanent damage from knockout blows, but various things will eventually happen while you are unconscious. Bad and interesting things, that will spice up the gameplay nicely.

As for difficulty, it will inevitably fluctuate as features and content are added, but even as it is this is less significant than you might think. In practice the aim is currently to take as little damage as possible, in the long run you'll be very glad that not every KO is game over. Some things will inevitably need adjustment and we'll continue to do that with your feedback.

Note that unconsciousness is not a metagame "try again" feature, but rather something that just makes sense within the context of the game. You can still easily find yourself in situations where death is unavoidable.



WEAPON SWAPPING

This apparently simple feature gave us a lot of headaches, but besides some refinements it's mostly working as intended.

Managing that torch that we love and hate in equal measures should be somewhat less of a burden. You can equip the same weapon to both your sets, allowing you to switch between a torch and a shield while using the same weapon, for example. This feature will become even more useful as ranged combat is introduced, and supports some other planned changes to the inventory system.

About This Game

Explore, fight, survive and unravel mysteries in this unique and unforgiving 3D isometric RPG set in an original dark, low fantasy world. Exanima's exceptional attention to detail and realistic simulation of all things aim to provide a deeply immersive and dynamic gameplay experience.

Features include a deep skill based combat system, complex and smart AI, fully interactive environments, an advanced damage model with accurate collisions and locational protection, roguelike elements and an arena mode to hone your combat skills.

"Those who would enter the underworld shall not only forfeit their lives...
...but bring oblivion to all mankind."

WARNING: THIS GAME IS ACTUALLY DIFFERENT!

This game features a very deep, truly physics based combat system. This is not just some animation feature, it is a fundamental difference that is central to gameplay. Real momentum, forces and collisions are always at play here, every nuance of your inputs is crucial to the outcome. This will likely be unlike anything you've played before and may take some practice, but can be hugely rewarding and the skill cap is virtually infinite. It is not particularly fast paced or difficult but it does require you to pay attention, it is very tactical and just casually pressing buttons won't get you very far!

Our goal is to create a deep and immersive RPG world based on a more advanced simulation supporting an unprecedented level of dynamic and emergent gameplay. All of the technology we use including the game engine is our own, developed from the ground up to realise this vision.

Exanima is born as a prelude to Sui Generis (SG), our ambitious open world RPG that has been in development for over two years following a successful Kickstarter campaign. Exanima is a smaller yet complete game in its own right, a dungeon crawler that takes place in SG's Underworld some twenty years prior to its events. Both games are being developed in concert, many of SG's more ambitious features will make an appearance in Exanima as development continues.

Exanima provides a reasonably complete and meaningful game experience, but many more features are on their way! We have met our most challenging and significant development goals, now we are ready to bring new features to light and make an even more fun and compelling adventure. Here are some of the planned features, with many already being close to completion:

  • Per session and global character skill progression
  • Large non-linear main game following early content
  • Build and permanently save characters by completing early content
  • New encounters, items and environments
  • Ranged combat and new close quarter manoeuvres
  • Numerous moddable thaumaturgic powers
  • More complete arena game modes featuring outdoor arenas
  • Complex NPC interactions and dialogue
  • Cooperative and competitive LAN multiplayer

I personally think that this game is great in all aspects.
I recommend it?
Yes for all aspects with a caveat on gameplay.
Just to be clear: i think that even gameplay is unique, great and it's more than just satisfying, but of course not all people will like it. And there's nothing wrong on that, for example i hate coffee (and i think that there's a mass hysteria on that, but I digress).

To elaborate:

Tecnhically is a gem. It runs smooth, looks really good (and that new lightning is so cool), physics is great and collisions are on their own league. Forget paper armor and hits that go through bodies like a lightsaber. That brings some problems like weapons getting stuck (nothing game breaking, aside sometimes golems being stuck on doors but i bet a fix is on the road) but it's a fair trade for me and things are improving.
When i had an amd 5770 and an i5 750, i ran the game maxed on fullhd (with max aa too iirc) nearly flawlessly. Now the game has some more bells and whistles (still missing gongs like a proper mutilation system) and my pc a GTX770 and an i5 6500. Fps don't skyrocket but the game is always smooth and fps themselves don't vary too much (i don't have spikes at 140fps and lows below 30 for example... Mostly between 45-90 iirc, i didn't track them, rarely drops below 50, often on 55-70 range).
Oh, and it just looks so good compared to other isometric games. Ok, animations can still be weird but I can hardly think that techie side is worth of complaints.
They seriously should consider to sell that engine if it's not impossible to work with. Really, those collisions are so great in a melee based game.

I like the audio. Music is unobtrusive and very atmospheric. I think that soundtrack on level 4 is extremely cool and captures the pompous and decadent environment perfectly. Hit sounds feel right. Monster sounds are scary sometimes.
There's no spoken speech but at the moment there isn't even the point on that.

On core gameplay... Just read other reviews, much has been said, i just stress that it requires practice. I think it's great and feels true, maybe not so realistic, slower than real combat (though has been speed up and was a good thing) but every update doesn't mess up things at worst or actually improves something, and that’s a feat. It takes skill to land a really powerful blow but when you make it it’s so satisfying and if receiving one you nearly feel it.
Said that, without doubt someone will find it awful and the only way is to try it. Maybe releasing a demo containing only novice and expert practice arena or even the first dungeon level (just to understand game structure) could be good for those that aren't sure.

Arena mode is cool and elaborate, it can grant lots of fun and hours. I think that it takes too long to gain top tier equipment though, you can really waste hours on that and get bored before achieving it. Though it is an excellent way to train, a very welcome thing, and it's great to have. We'll see if this will become more elaborate on the future, varied and interesting, but it's already really, really cool and devs must be commended to have expanded on that and keep on doing that.

Dungeon mode is... Wonderful! I admit that some save states, maybe optional and called noob mode that wouldn’t grant some achievements when (if) they’ll be implemented (not that I care), would be good.
Anyway.
It’s a dungeon crawler. Like an old school one (except that is avantgarde too). Just not puzzle based (there are some puzzles on the first level, not the best nor the worst I’ve seen) but combat based and still, of course, exploring based. Maps are complex and big (maybe for it’s own sake, but who cares?). No handholding, no automap, you can find a compass though. And you’ll be lost, a lot.
No talking npcs, no quests, no amazing cinematics, no great and well explained story, no living world, no open world levels (for now at least).
No random enemies spawn but they will roam across the level (so there can actually be surprises). I believe there’s something random on loot but not too much or in a broken way…

It’s just a choice of game design and it’s bloody well done. If you don’t like that don’t buy the game (except arena could be still awesome) or be sad cause that the structure doesn’t meet your taste for something otherwise great, but don’t blame the game because this is a way to properly make a dungeon crawler.
It happens that I really like dungeon crawlers with those characteristics, though i wouldn't disdain an optional automap, hence I really like single player.
Atmosphere is that great… All feels so real and menacing. You feel and are lonely and lost.
GG the first time you’ll met Sir (you’ll understand…). I believe that it was some years that I wasn’t so scared by a videogame (and it’s fair to assume that I became less impressionable on the meantime). Though now he’s just a good sparring partner and seems just a cub compared to golems (that fortunately are bypassable) at beginning it’s plain terrifying.

This game is dark and i mean *a lot*. If there ins't light it's plain black and you can shut down your monitor to save energy. Fortunately you are provided with a torch at the beginning and without doubt is one of the most important items. Lots of cool shadows.

Equipment is one of the coolest ever seen. Or at least I think so.
Really, check various layers of armour, i can’t think about another game that makes that:

http://i.imgur.com/NEm93Lp.jpg
http://i.imgur.com/qLcGbh0.jpg

And no, it’s not just cosmetic. If you have an uncovered part of your body better cover it. And if you are against an heavy armored opponent better to put away your trusty sword and use a mighty pole axe or hammer. And speaking about that... Check also last image with a Pole Hammer (a Pollaxe). It’s so badass as it is. Tired about oversized and stupid weapons? For sure equipment design lean towards realism and looks excellent.
And there are more than a fair amount of armour pieces or weapons… From the small knife to the imposing zweihander. From the humble hand axe to the fearsome halberd. From the rough ball mace to various pollaxes (that are the most badass weapons). And spears too! From cloth tunic to full plate armour with loads of pieces. And there are shields. Lately even slotted items with consumable gems to enhance them.

This game has amazing features and is well refined on many aspects, though much is still missing (ranged combat, magic, many levels, feats, multiplayer) combat is one of a kind and if you don’t like it, well, there are other games to waste time on.
Personally i’m impressed, it’s nowhere near finished, I’ve already spent my fair time on (more than most of other full released games) and i've checked it every update never being bored. It looks glorious too.
Yeah, development it’s slow, but devs don’t mess up things (and if bugs are introduced they are also fixed) and actually improve the game. Maybe someone can consider Exanima like a placeholder for Sui Generis (that I’ve backed with no regrets till now) but this doesn’t detract from the game.

I can just say that i like dungeon crawlers and this one for me is great, arena is already solid and with every update all is getting better and better.

Early Access Review
Posted: 26 June
I've never been so immersed in an isometric RPG as I am with this one. There really isn't any way to do it justice in writing, but once you master the combat it really is an absolute joy to experience. Looks and sounds visceral, and somehow real -- despite fighting monsters and all that. The armor layer system is very cool, and the circular stat representations of weapons and armor is vague but also sort-of a relief. This game has enough going for it that stopping to do the math, seeing if a slight point differential is worth it really wouldn't fit this game; so the basic 1, 1 1/2, 2 circles and so on for each stat simplifies that a bit. There is minimal UI, which is both a good and a bad thing. The game doesn't give you any sense of what your objectives might be, it took me a bit to even realize I was traveling down floors. There is no map, which, to me, sucks. Some might like having to find their way all the time, and constantly getting lost, or searching the same room again and again, but I don't. I wish there was an option to toggle on a map. It's also a shame that keys are sometimes indistinguishable from each other, with their descriptions always (as far as I've played) being "a simple brass key" or something along those lines. It makes it so you have to essentially assemble a key ring, and try them all out on each locked door. They are also simple, and brown in the environment as well, making them very hard to find -- which is kind of cool, but also annoying, since, you often have to check the same rooms ad nauseum to find them.

I would definitely recommend this game; but I'd also like to warn any would-be buyers that it is difficult, unforgiving, and above all takes a very minimalist approach when it comes to interface and giving the player information.
