Take the previous Penumbra games. Substract monsters, inventory system and plot. What do you have? A puzzle game with extremely dark environment similar to the underground levels of Portal 2. Except there is no sense of progression, no thrill and resource management. And a needlessly creepy soundtrack accompanies you during tedious puzzle solving.

Actually, the game does have a plot. It starts out where the Black Plague left off but the whole game is set in protagonist's own mind. Each level takes place in a different facility complex and there is no connection whatsoever in between. It usually makes references to previous games but the connection is extremely weak and to be honest, is just there to remind the players that this is, in fact, a Penumbra game. I think you will only will be interested in the plot if you love the scenerio and recall what happened in Overture perfectly.

Puzzles reward observation more than logical and practical thinking. And for a game that features such large and dark environments, puzzle solving can be quite bothersome. Also, some new physics related elements were introduced here which make me think the reason this game exists is just to demonsrate Frictional's home-made sophisticated physics engine. Why? Just why is this beautiful classic horror series is trying to compete with Portal? Completely different genres.

To sum up, don't make the mistake i did and think that this is the final installment of the trilogy. There is no trilogy. If we don't count the recently launched fan made mod Penumbra: Necrologue, the series started with Overture and ended with Black Plague. And then there is this stupid spin-off, Penumbra: Requiem. Even the name doesn't make sense. Requiem to what? To the series? What a disappointment!

First of all, Penumbra: Requiem is a DLC to Black Plague, and I will review this as a DLC.

If you played Penumbra: Black Plague, you probably wondered what happened to poor unlucky Phillip. Well, you get an interesting answer in Requiem, which kind of poses more interesting questions than answers.

Requiem is all about the puzzles. There are 9 levels (puzzles) and as you solve each one, there are interesting books, artifacts, and audio recordings to discover that piece together the current reality you are experiencing. This game isn't scary like the first two in the series, but it does maintain that dark and foreboding atmosphere. Personally, I'm glad there were no monsters stalking me, since I died enough trying to solve the puzzles.

Now are the puzzles hard? Yes....and no.....sort of. The puzzles start off easy, then even though you know how to solve it, you have to execute it just right or start over. Thats where it got kind of annoying for me, since my timing can be less than great. I also like to overjump platforms a lot and jump to my death. So, if you have an excellent sense of timing and depth perception, you should have no problem.

Now there really isn't a story per se, since it just build off from the last two games. There are two endings plus an optional extra ending if you find each artifact hidden in each level. I was content to get the regular two endings and Youtube the special one, since I was in no mood to look for all the artifacts and solve the puzzles. This game gave me serious feelings of nausea since I had to repeatedly try to do puzzles correctly (jumping platforms, falling, moving puzzles).

I really enjoyed the dissent into madness and "meeting" the fellow insane people that have been dropped in the facility. The sound really keeps you on edge. The graphics would only let me play on the lowest settings, despite me trying to change the screen resolution repeatedly. If anyone ever finds a fix let me know. This was a minor annoyance to me, since graphics in a game like this is secondary criteria.

I feel a sense of accomplishment after playing this, and while it is not really a full "game" like the two previous Penumbra titles, it should be played to enrich the Penumbra experience. I am also looking forward to playing Penumbra: Necrologue!

If you just play Penumbra for the monsters Requiem is not for you. However, if you liked the story of Penumbra this is a great conclusion for the series.

Requiem takes a whole new approach to the Penumbra series: no monsters are encountered, so instead of being a horror game with puzzle elements this is now a puzzle game with horror elements. Keep in mind that Requiem is about 4 hours long (Black Plague is about 8 and Overture is about 12) and is not "Penumbra 3" but instead an expansion pack for Black Plague. Don't expect a full length story like the first two games, but that being said the story in Requiem is great for an expansion pack. It's actually quite scary at times, and it takes true horror craftmanship to scare the player while there is no physical threat to their character.

The puzzles are by far the best of the Penumbra series. They blow the Overture/Black Plague puzzles out of the water and are a lot of fun. They're not too hard and not too easy, which makes for a nice difficulty. Requiem is the only game in the Penumbra series where I felt the pacing was uninterrupted; don't get me wrong, Overture and Black Plague are great games but were often *plagued* by clunky action mechanics (Overture only) and occasionally bad puzzles.
