What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The Prelude is a limited first release of the Xsyon virtual world, intended for the developers to work closely with the player base in creating the world you want to live in!

An isolated basin surrounded by mountains in a future American wilderness serves as a launching point for the ever expanding world. This setting provides a pristine environment for players to lay the foundations of a new world.

During the Prelude, Xsyon is continuously evolving and expanding. This phase of the game was intended as a time for the game to adapt to its community. The development team has been working closely with the player base to determine the future of Xsyon and wishes to continue to do so with the Steam crowd.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Early Access will continue throughout 2015 until the upcoming feature checklist listed below is complete.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Although Xsyon is planned to evolve indefinitely, the first full release of the game, tentatively titled Xsyon Apocalypse will launch when several criteria are met:

- Pets and mounts are fully implemented. (In current development)
- Ranged combat is released.
- Enhanced building functions are implemented. (Complete and in final testing)
- New multi story construction system implemented. (Complete and released)
- New architectural components finished. (Complete and released)
- Systems are improved and balanced based on player feedback.
- Achievements are implemented.
- The expanded landscape (9 times the current world size) is ready for public release. (75% complete)”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Xsyon is nearly full featured but requires further system balancing and polish.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Xsyon - Prelude accounts will transfer seamlessly to Xsyon Apocalypse accounts.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The Xsyon development team works closely with players through regular feedback sessions on the Xsyon forums.

We are open to suggestions on improving features and regularly ask players what they most look forward to in the future.”

About This Game

Xsyon is a sandbox massively multi-player online role playing game (MMORPG), an online community, a virtual world and an adventure.

Set in the near future, Xsyon is an apocalyptic fable. The world draws its inspiration from history, mythology, and prophecies of the Apocalypse. Most aspects of modern life are now gone. Modern vehicles, weapons, appliances, computer and even the books that describe these things are practically nowhere to be found.

You are a survivor. Your youth was spent lost in times of war. Your memories of the past with its machines, computers, bright lights and buildings reaching for the skies are vague and ever fading, a distant and unreal dream. You grow accustomed to ancient ways that now once again rule the world.

Xsyon is an ever changing dynamic world. Players affect the environment by gathering resources, chopping down trees, hunting animals, building towns and developing trade. Players hold the power to create and destroy, to maintain a balance with nature or to cultivate a new civilization. The game is built around environmental, natural, economic, questing, and social systems. These systems are in constant flux and it's the players interacting with these systems that determine the current state of the virtual world.

Xsyon is a fantasy world, but is heavily based on reality. Set in an uncertain apocalyptic future, the virtual world draws upon real life geography, history, science and medicine, fauna and geology are slightly twisted versions of what is real. Whether you are determining what materials to use in a set of armor, trying out ingredient combinations in a recipe or gathering information on the local geography, there is always something new and real you can learn from the Xsyon world.

As a player, explorer and settler of this new virtual world you have many choices. You can play as a warrior, a trader, a craftsman or a cultivator of crops. You can specialize your skills or become a jack of all trades. You can play as a clan leader or town official, socially involved with many other players, or you can explore the world as a lone adventurer. Xsyon is a game for all kinds of players from casual to hard core. The path you take is up to you.

Whether you are a first day scavenger or a grizzled veteran, Xsyon should be fun. Players begin with a decent set of statistics rather than evolve from a weakling to a super human. You enter the world inexperienced and lacking skill, with strengths and weaknesses, but still as a hero. Rather than grind levels to reach predictable goals, Xsyon is based on change, interactions and consequences. As new quests, lands and features are constantly introduced, there is always something new for you to discover.

By combining elements of different game genres and social worlds and by blurring the line between fantasy and reality we have created a unique online world. We hope you will find the Xsyon experience fresh, enjoyable, rewarding and full of surprises.

Explore, learn, make friends and create the world you want to live in! Are you with us?

The short version:

Xsyon is a great game and if you're a fan of player made worlds, interesting crafting/building systems, or post-apocalyptic survival games, it's definitely worth checking out.

The long version:

I started playing Xsyon back in 2011, before the game was available on Steam. The game is early access, but don't let that scare you away - in it's current state, the game offers enough content to keep you busy indefinitely. Early access games have been known to contain game-breaking bugs and unfinished features, but that's not the case with Xsyon, as the features that are in the game work. I have encountered bugs from time to time (as in any game), but I have to say that once reported, they are fixed - fast. I've seen the game's dev come into the game and talk with me personally to fix one. The game's team of moderators (guides) are active in not only helping players out with any issues or even questions about the gameplay, but they also hold events and generally seem interested in what they're doing. There have been many major patches rolling out important new features since I've been playing.

There is a skill system in place leading to the gathering, crafting, food and building systems. The crafting system allows you to create a ton of different armors, weapons, and utility gear (carts, tools, etc) from materials gathered in the world. The building system allows you to take crafted and gathered objects and build structures in the game (other players can see and interact with the structures, and you and even let them work with you in building them). The building is extremely interesting and has a large array of pieces available, allowing players to create anything from small tents and huts to immense palaces and castles (I have to admit that I personally spend a LOT of time building with my friends in the game - by the time I finish 1 project I have 5 more ideas that I want to try out). The food system allows players to hunt, fish and grow and forage lots of different things, and cook them to make food - it's easy to do the minimum, but players that really invest themselves in this system can create very powerful food items that can help in every day things. The skill system as a whole works well - it's easy to throw yourself in one skill and master it, but harder to master 2 skills, and so on. Playing solo is possible, but the game is much easier playing as a group, allowing you to focus on your chosen skill(s) without worrying about the others.

The game is definitely worth checking out - I know I've spent a lot of time playing it (I only connected my Steam and Xsyon accounts a few months ago, even though I started playing in 2011, and am at over 600 hours on Steam) and will continue to play it for a long time.

Absolute blast. The community is very helpful and friendly. The crafting system is very, very, in depth. This is true sandbox. You looking for a theme park experience? This is not for you. Quests, carts, tools, buildings, roads, docks, bridges, everything is player built. If you can see it, use it, walk on it, someone took the time and effort to create it. Bad community? absolutly not. those bridges you are crossing, somone took the time to build them so you're journey is unobstucted. That fire lighting up the road... somone built it and cut firewood so you're way is lit. Sick of all the good sandboxes being PVP? no problem. It offeres both PVP and PVE servers. Like to group play? No problem. Someone is always looking for a tribe memmber to fatten the ranks. Like to play solo? No problem. Find a piece of land to call you're own and begin that settlement. So much gameplay I cant possibly mention it all here.
I would like to share a couple stories that I feel express the soul of the game. About two years ago I played this, I was lost. Decided to join a tribe, took a few minutes to find one. I did well we got along. Life took me from gaming for a bit. Logged back in and the tribe had vanished. Picking up my belongings, hungry, thirsty I decided to trail my own path. Eventually I found my piece of land to call home. Life was ruff. My shovel was worn and my back was sore from carrying so much timber. My old Tribe leader noticed me in chat and sure enugh the tribe was still kiking and had simply moved. I explained that my time and effort was imprtant to me and I would continue my work on my settlement. Several hours later my Tribe leader showed up with my old cart. She had dragged that cart all the way around the world to ensure my shelter would be up before winter. Inside the cart was a good supply of tools and food to keep me going. I never asked, she simply did it.
My first real multi-story structure was started on a Saturday morning. By Sunday evening I was about to ram Xsyon up somone's a**. that building caused me more grief than the IRS. I was flustered and agitated. I asked in global chat what was I doing wrong and being that no one was accually there to see my issue it would be difficult to determine. I simply had a melt down in chat LOL. Seriously, I was mad. I was about to log off. Closed my gate and began to log off. I turned around to go into my cabin and there stands a GM. I did not hit any help buttons. I didn't scream for GM help in chat. She simply showed up. She did'nt lecture to me about my attitude or threatened a ban, she simply helped me. After a few seconds of education my tower was complete. The issue was so simple. Wessendale is now populated by 14 people and thriving. Our land mass is vast and we house many multi-story buildings, and holdings that would impress even a king.
The game is buy to play. It's umm 30 bucks? No monthly fee. No in-game store or micro payments. What are you waiting for? just get it. Thats about the cost of a bad movie and some popcorn. You will get far more than two hours out of this. Don't show up thinking you will run the Xsyon world in twenty minutes, not going to happen. It's the hardships that make this game what it is. Show up with determination and perseverance. Be steadfast and study. In the end you earned what you created.

See you in-game!
