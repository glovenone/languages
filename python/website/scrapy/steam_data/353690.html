Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We've been working on D.U.S.T. full-time with a two man team for about six months. There were other ways of getting funded other than Early Access, but we chose Early Access given that it is imperative we stay as an independent studio. Maintaining zero external influence for developing unique games like D.U.S.T. was vital.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Early Access for D.U.S.T. will largely happen on a two-stage system. The first, we will devote our resources on enhancing core gameplay experience. D.U.S.T. has many systems that are unique. These systems require extensive research in order to accurately reflect mechanics as it would in real life. The second stage will be devoted to producing content such as maps and new equipments. It is also during this stage that D.U.S.T. will be made Directx12 compatible and go under a massive graphics overhaul.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Vehicles will be added into the game ranging from land vehicles and air transport such as helicopters. New weapons will be added into the game befit different factions, factions whom will also be expanded. Larger maps for and different game modes will included into the library of D.U.S.T.s play experience. Apart from the dessert setting, wetlands, forests and perhaps arctic maps will make an appearance.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We've set a foundation on which D.U.S.T. can continue to grow. Namely, a large player network game session (capable of 64 ~128 players) with physics driven ballistics, weapon mechanics and in-game voip. We are focusing on core rather than content at the moment.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“prices will definitely go up as development furthers.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We understand and acknowledge that our ways are not always the best and that we can be horribly wrong. So, we've started a forum where anyone with a valid point can pretty much post. Given that the post's request is substantiated and within D.U.S.T.'s scope, we prioritize and implement it. A good example can be found here:
http://www.terapoly.com/forums/showthread.php?tid=19”

Development Taking A Honest Break. (Update)

For those who are aware of us, we are a two man team. Since last year, we've been working on D.U.S.T. day and night and have come very far with it. We've had great success and have had many people yearning at the potential that the game possesses. We've also been working on another project, Tavern Tycoon, which is a first person fantasy tavern management game that we've been working on even before D.U.S.T. saw its first light.

With these two projects on my table, I've been literally cutting my personal, and mostly my sleeping hours to keep up with the productivity. As a result, D.U.S.T. 0.3 (newly dubbed as beta) is moving forward at a fast pace, and Tavern Tycoon, almost ready for release.

The only downside, is my personal health, and the lack of updates that D.U.S.T. has been getting for the last few months.

For these reasons, I am officially taking a break from D.U.S.T. development during the month of June. With Tavern Tycoon almost ready, I should be able to grab a good 1 to 2 weeks of recovery time after its release. That means no computers, no electronics...and more sunshine...and no late night snacks :D

I also have to admit that pressure on my health is slowing draining my passion of developing games, a negative influence for any creative work I am laying my hands on. In another words, I have 0 mana, and need to recharge.

With all this under the hood, I hope that everyone can see the benefit of taking a break at this time. I really hope this break will be enough to bounce back and carry on. I sincerely apologize for this.

I wish you guys the best and see you in a month's time.

Stay frosty.

Firstly, thank you so much for everyone for supporting me. It really helps when we get more support and encouragement than we deserve. About This Game

So, another multiplayer FPS that claims to be "real", what's the deal?
There are hundreds of them and we all know that realism isn't really there. There are few that comes close, but not quite there. Hell, I was so into realism that I eventually decided to make one instead of looking. D.U.S.T. was not made to be "better" or to be more "real", it was made to do it right. So here is a brief feature list.

Total Control Weapon
Pull the charging handle, slap the bolt catch. Turn the elevation dials and set the focus on scopes. Gone are the days of a weapon that is capable of reality bending physics. Our motto is, If you can do it real life, then you can also do it in DUST. If it is missing, even the smallest thing, let us know and we will add it.

An open level (4km x 4km) 128 player game.
Team driven fire fight at long distances for seasoned and to be more exact, veteran, hardcore FPS players. Please note that the initial release will be limited to 64 player games.

Total Weapon Modification
Having the ability to change scopes and suppresors is not considered enough. D.U.S.T. allows you to change the barrel, twist ratio, thickness, trigger, bolt carrier group, scopes, grenade launchers, handles, rails, magazines, round grain types and so on. We are actively adding and widening the modification level.

Physics driven ballistics.
That means gravity, air resistance, ricochet, fragmentation etc. Everything should be there is there

One shot, one kill gameplay.
Gone are the days of health regeneration or a number driven health system. A bullet will and definitely kill you.

Extended view distance.
Clear view of 4000m+, that means no more fogs, no more popping in enemies. There is just realistic atmospheric scatter.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 64-bit, Windows 8 64-bit
    • Processor: Intel Core i5-2300, AMD Phenom II X4 940 or better
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: nVidia GTX 560
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Storage: 4 GB available space
    • Sound Card: Windows Compatible Card
    • Additional Notes: Please note that requirements may change over time with further development.

Let me get to the pros and cons:

Cons:
-Background does not fit with the map
-'Interesting' AI behaviour.
-Lacking some basic attachments.
-No gear customization
NOTE: These cons will most likely be fixed in an update as these cons are created by the game being early access; thus meaning one cannot complain.

Pros:
-F**king decent graphics for a game in like Alpha 0.3
-Better than ArmA for gunplay and ballistics simulation
-Feels more realistic than ArmA
-More realistic than real life
-No out-of-combat/Global VON meaning the person you just shot cannot vent his rage unto you.
-Canteen/Hydration feauture
-Very solid game, minimal bugs - those discovered are quickly fixed.
-Legendary developer; probably the friendliest dev I've seen in my life.

Final Verdict:
Solid game with great, great potential but a worryingly small playerbase. I think this game could grow to be better than upcoming titles such as 'Escape From Tarkov' if done right; however more people need to play this game to ensure its potential is forfilled.

Would I recommend this game?

Early Access Review
Posted: 23 December, 2015
I'll start this by saying that I really do like the game and its concept. The mechanics, while frustrating at times, feel like they have a certain fun in their novelty, and I do enjoy all of the various adjusting and full control that they've tried to implemment in this game. Based on these factors alone, I would be tempted to give this game a positive review; all that being said, I do not give this game a strong recommendation however much I would like to. The positive attributes that D.U.S.T. holds are currently overshadowed by the lack of player-base, the oversimplistic map design, and the general disorganization of layout.

It becomes very apparent that the game lacks a large enough playerbase to produce exciting matches when the only server containing more than 0-1 players is comprised of 90% bots. This point alone would not generally turn me off to the game; I play many games regularly such as Insurgency, Star Wars Batllefront II, CS:GO, etc. which benefit greatly from the inclusion of bots and remain entertaining when playing matches even entirely comprised of NPCs/AI. But the nature of the bots in D.U.S.T. are simply frustrating. After spending so much time setting myself up (filling my canteen, adjusting sights, slowly travelling across the colossal empty plains, etc.), it is severely enraging to find that I am immediately acquired and killed by a bot from 75+ meters away with god-like accuracy. I understand that this game is intended for a hardcore, simulation audience, but when the average player, or even perhaps an operator in the real world, takes much more effort in the way of aim and could expend an entire magazine before being able accurately hit their target at medium-long ranges, it is severely enraging to find myself targetted and dropped by a bot after immediate, inhuman adjustmentsand only 2-3 rounds sent my way. This simple gripe would be forgivable in its own right, perhaps it would even be an endearing quality. D.U.S.T. is, after all, intended to be a very hardcore, unforgiving simulation game, however a problem arises when this effect is combined with my other two component gripes with this game: scope of map design and organization of its layout.

The design of the map in this game is ambitious, something which I generally admire about this project as a whole. Its 4km x 4km in size, a titantic playing area for a first person shooter, especially one without an established publisher's support. This is something I would generally have considered a positive trait, and something which actually drew me in primarily; but it is also fundamentally flawed considering the overall developmental state the game is currently in, a point which I will further explain after establishing my dependent complaint in the map's organization. The general layout of this map, disregarding its size, is quite good actually. There is a central city, positioned in a "bowl" of surrounding hills; American and Russian "control points" are tangent to the North and South of the city respectively and factional deployment areas are located within 50-100m of these points. The central city contains a sizable quantity of various buildings and cover elements which the player is allowed to interact with, enter and utilize in avoiding enemy fire. The control points are each composed of a tall building, a surrounding wall, small cover elements, and a water dispensary to fill canteens. The spawns are comprised of a small amount of cleverly placed sandboxes and trucks which allow you to take cover and find your bearings before heading into battle. I am quite fond of each of these elements to be clear, and I'd add that the relative location of each is ideal; however, an unwelcome situation is created when these elements are placed into such a substantially sized map. The size calls for each of these elements to be placed (I'll add that I am not quite sure of the exact distances as I'm unaware to if they've been recorded) seemingly 100s of meters apart from one another. What I would initially consider an adequate, possibly even accomplished, map design becomes exhausting and irritating considering the fundamental mechanics of this game (which I've noted before are a postitive attribute and even a drawing factor to me personally.)

You are first deployed in the spawn with an empty canteen and an unadjusted, unloaded rifle. To fill the canteen, you must walk to the water dispensary in the control point area, open your canteen [MMB], turn on the tap [`], close your canteen when its full [LMB], and turn the tap back off [`]; to bring your rifle to a usable state, you need to bring your rifle up [RMB], pull the charging handle [RShift], aim down sights [RMB], adjust your head position relative to your sights [LMB+Mouse movement], and change firing mode to semi or full [Up arrow, Right arrow]. These mechanics are essential to your survival and your abilty to take down hostile targets. They are also refreshing, immersive, involved, and above all else, entertaining. But when these mechanics must be repeated upon each deployment (respawn), they become time-consuming, especially considering the 100m or so journey to the water dispensary, and then the several hundred more meters you must trek to make it into the main town. This repetition is exhausting and takes much too much time away from possible engagements and firefights.

In conclusion, I cannot recommend this game, at this point, to anyone. The game simply does not give enough reward to your efforts when adjusting your gear and making it into battle comprise the majority of time spent playing. I would, however, like to specify that most of these points are intrinsic to the stage of development. This game has significant potential and I would love to see where its development leads. I can even forgive all the negative aspects, there is true thought and merit put into each one, and they only become negative due to the lack of synergy between them. I've taken this time to write such a lengthy and detailed review because I believe that it would be simple (although perhaps not without spending a deal of time) to assimilate these seperate parts into a smoother, better-operating machine. The main problem, the gist of my issue with this game lies in its current map size. A playable 4km x 4km landscape is a great goal to set for completion in the later stages of development, but at this point, with the current "density" so to speak of the map elements, it is simply to large. My main advice to the development team would be to remove all the elements of the map, save the foundation landscape into a seperate file for optimization at a later date, and place the elements into a more compact landscape, perhaps with more cover in the plains of sand between each distinct map location. Once the elements are more reasonably spaced and condensed, I have a feeling that this game will become much more playable, with the various small grievances becoming insignificant. An optional change which may be of use would be to save the player's rifle adjustments between lives. Although this specific change I'll admit may be detrimental to the games spirit, it could reduce time taken between death and reaching your squadmates, and could create a more inviting atmosphere for groups of players looking for a tactical, squad-reliant shooter. The best option regarding this specific idea may be to allow the player to decide whether they'd like their adjustments saved in the settings.

I really do hope that you see this, development team, because I deeply enjoy the fundamentals of this game, and I've only taken the effort to write such a wordy, exploratory review because I love this concept and I'd like to see where you can take it. My lack of recommendation is tied to the current state of the game, and I look forward to when I will be able to recommend this game to every single one of my friends and acquaintances in the near future.
