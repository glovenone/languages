About This Game

A mystical world on the edge of destruction. Unlikely saviors joined by fate and duty. A mission driven by music, magic...and survival.



Murasaki is a hybrid shmup and puzzle game that creates intense and high-speed gameplay against a serene landscape with gorgeous music. With the ability to only fire a single bullet at a time, you must use cunning, angles and timing to survive.
Random shapes will appear as the levels progress: these designs will trigger massive explosions when shot. If you can bounce corresponding shapes off of each other, they'll create a chain reaction that, with a little luck, will wipe the screen. Don't wait too long, because the enemies will overwhelm you in a second!
If you can't get a clear shot, each character has a special attack with limited uses. Use them wisely to get out of a tight situation or to put the finishing blow on one of several bosses!

Characters

Kairi
The older of the heroes, Kairi acts as a big brother, though he's not related to Satsuki. His movements are slow, but his long range shot can reach anywhere on the screen, perfect for ricochet combinations. This however, also means that you don't have rapid shots.
Kairi's special attack is a spiraling explosion that reaches most of the screen. This bomb can absorb enemy's bullets and destroy multiple targets. Use it wisely.



Satsuki
The younger hero, Satsuki is impetuous and headstrong, and it shows in her attack formation. Satsuki can move across the screen quickly, but the range of her shots is very short. Sometimes they won't reach the enemy.
However, using her speedy shots, you can bounce the explosive blocks farther than Kairi, which makes it easier to create a chain attack with other blocks. Just be careful not to bounce them too far, or the explosion won't reach the enemy!
Her special attack is a cross-formation energy wave. It hits harder than Kairi's, but could easily miss if the timing is off. Use it wisely.



Find your center and get into a flowing rhythm of evading and exploding. If you can master the mechanics, Murasaki is insanely satisfying, mixing the mayhem and the tranquil music in a beautiful dissonant clash. Failure means death, swift and painful. Don't look at where the bullets in front of you go: plan for where the explosions will happen afterwards.




There is a long and tragic story within Murasaki: each successful (and even unsuccessful) chapter unlocks new information, history, bios and even musical tracks. To truly understand the full game, you must become an expert with both Kairi AND Satsuki. It's daunting, it's hard, but the satisfaction of a full unlock is unmatched.

Config

You can start a config tool from "Launch Configuration Tool" when you start the game.

Here, you can configure the screen size, BGM/SE ratio, key bindings, etc.


The death of an empire. The rise of darkness. And two heroes alone combat a broken soul.

So this game is Murasaki, a puzzle/shmup hybrid by Katetama, most famous for the freeware puzzle/horror game Irisu Syndrome. If you played a bit of that, you should be somewhat familiar with the mechanics in this game, at least the puzzle parts anyway.

It's different from typical SHMUPs in that your bullet isn't really used to destroy the enemies. It has a low rate of fire, terrible range and won't really do much to anything but the most basic of enemies.

What your bullet CAN do however, is hit upon little geometrical shapes, "pucks" of sorts throughout the stage and when they are hit, they explode, taking along any bullets and enemies within its blast radius. Where it gets really interesting is when you make these shapes hit each other in a chain reaction and cause massive screen wiping explosions. Therein lies the real fun of Murasaki, the defining gimmick of this SHMUP. You hold out for as long as you can against cascading waves of bullets from all directions, slowly waiting for the shapes to pile up, and when the moment is right, you just fire one bullet to start off the chain reaction and watch everything blow away to void as you rack up a massive score.

It's a very satisfying and adrenaline pumping experience, one wholly unique to itself. Other shmups have their own unique methods, but this one is something really interesting.

That's not all this game has to offer though. There is a story in this game, but it's very cryptic and dependent on how well you do in a stage and whether you decide to let the boss characters say their piece instead of firing at them... And from what I can gleam of it from where I'm at so far in the game, it's a tragic story about good intentions leading to horrible circumstances, loss, horrific experiments, incredibly powerful beings destroying everything in their wake for reasons not entirely clear... But in the midst of all this, there's also a promise to be kept and the uniting power of music even after death... And it all happens in the background, never really at the fore, only told in snippets outside of gameplay. It's odd, but I think it's really good myself.

The music in this game is made with such passion for the themes present in the game that it basically defines moments in the games. The composer understood the the mood of the setting and pulled off a soundtrack that just completely hits all the right spots with aplomb. It's a soundtrack that many games want but often never get. I have nothing but the highest praise for the music and would recommend the game just for the music alone. Thankfully, the game is also really good too.

There are five stages, an extra stage, an unlockable hard mode (That is REALLY hard), and an endless mode, in addition to various achievements that unlock small portions of the story... This all sums to to saying there's a lot for you to enjoy here for the very low price of entry. You have two characters to play as, each has their own unique feel.

I am not sure if a lot of shmup fans who like traditional styled bullet hell games will like it, but I think all shmup fans should try it out anyway if they can. It's a captivating experience that left an impression on me that I won't soon forget, and I'd want others to play this and maybe feel a little bit of the excitement and wonder I got when I played this. I think it's more than worth the asking price.

Murasaki finally gets an English release on Steam. The original game was in Japanese. I originally played the Japanese version (without understanding a single word) and later on I saw it would get a release on Steam with English version. I'm a fan of Wtetsu's work, so I bought, even if I had played the Japanese version previously.

This is a bullethell game with some puzzle mechanics (borrowed from his other game, "Irisu Syndrome!, which is free-ware by the way"). You can shoot directly through the enemies (only one shot on screen, but they don't take long to vanish), or make chains with the red squares and blue circles. You can also use bombs in case you have too much stuff on screen. On bosses, sometimes a "kano" (red thingy) drops it and you can catch it. If you manage to beat it, you'll receive a brief part of the story on the Collection, along the time it took for you to defeat that part of the boss.

The idea of shooting on other objects that aren't necessarily the boss works pretty well. Dodging the boss bullet's and doing something else while doing so.

You have two characters in your disposal on the beginning (I don't know if you can unlock more later, I haven't beat it completely yet, but by seeing the store page, I don't think so). Each one has their own abilities. Kairi which is a more balanced character and Satsuki which has more power, but more difficult to use. Each character has different dialogues as well (on bosses), and you need to play as each to unlock certain information about the story (unlocked on the collection).

Speaking of collection, you can unlock various goodies (like music, character information, and such) while you progress in-game.

I just feel a little pricey for a simple game like this. Another problem is there is no way to "slow down" your character to dodge the bullets more easily, especially how the character moves kinda fast in this game. May be a good or bad thing, depending of your skill level.

Small bug: For some reason, the game seems to disable the Windows key (Running on Windows 7, 64-bits).

Pros:
- Good soundtrack;
- Interesting gameplay;
- Multiple routes (two characters to play as with different dialogue, and both of them unlocks specific features on the collection and your main story);
- Portrait artwork is pretty good.

Middle:
- Sprites and effects are ok.

Cons:
- No button for slow down the character (may not be a con, actually);
- Kinda pricey right now.

Murasaki does a good job of introducing higher difficulties for its original mechanics; you're not going to play bullet hell game the same way again(And luckily for me, I don't play other bullet hell games that much since Touhou 9).

Been playing since first day release in its original Japanese version, and I still haven't beat 6th stage. it is increasingly difficult like every stage is more than twice the difficulty of the previous, but it is learnable with low-moderate frustration when you're new. The tolerance between complete failure and perfection gets tighter, but this is where its 'puzzler' element comes in and rewards perfection and better play satisfyingly, something I don't really feel it could ever come up from a Bullet-Hell game(usually normal enemies would come out, spit you patterns to dodge while hitting them and enemy patterns would just be forgotten, but not this one).

The minimalistic(when compared to other Bullet-hell) concept is what it is and you have to overcome them as its featured mechanics(Otherwise it might not be your cup of tea)

Got the OST in CD and its always a treat for me to listen to T_Watson/MusMus(The soundtrack creator) works, A mix of genres here and there keeping the experience fresh(Yeah I still have to pay for the dlc to show that I own it don't I?)

Price point? I tend to support and ready to be a fan of what I'm paying for, otherwise I'm not buying; I'm not sure anyone need to buy then complain a perfectly working game that is fun to play.(Someone had already 100% Murasaki with hard mode on all stages, so its not 'untested' incompletable game). If I had it for free before and have to pay for localized version, given the chance to support the dev for making original concepts that works, why not? (Or maybe its because I've spent so much effort in the fanbase basically $5 means nothing)

Thanks for listening to my wish that this game has made its way into Steam! that was unexpected, but I've been using steamoverlay for Murasaki long before this game is even announced for localization, for screenshotting my way trying to read the Japanese version. :D
