About This Game

Dash, jump, fly and wave your way through epic levels with rhythm-based gameplay! Do you have what it takes? Follow the beat as you play levels or endless waves while staying in tune with the rhythm of each song. Join what could possibly be the impossible game to beat!

GAME FEATURES:
◉ 45 Amazing electronic/edm soundtracks from 23 great artists
◉ 130 unlockable characters
◉ 17 Levels to play from (5 endless modes)
◉ Customize all game textures, songs, trails, colors and much more
◉ Tons of power-ups for your Jetpack, Hoverboard and Spaceship
◉ Trophies and Secret Keys to collect every level
◉ Practice mode to enhance your skills

Dashy Square is a cute rhythm-based platformer with cute, challenging levels and a great soundtrack.

If you’ve ever played Geometry Dash you’ll feel right at home with Dashy Square as the gameplay is inspired by it and feels very similar. The idea is simply you must navigate your way to the end of each level past the various objects you’ll encounter. If you have little patience or hate cheap deaths then this game isn’t for you because without lightning fast reflexes you’ll be dying a lot; Dashy Square is HARD.
You constantly move through the level and must jump, slow yourself down, fly and dodge your way past the objects that’ll appear in front of you as you move. The level will often bounce, flip or disappear as you play so focus is a must! Each level is different and they continuously get harder even though the ‘Easy’ levels are pretty hard themselves but you can practice each level without the fear of dying if you’re really struggling.

Each level is nicely designed and very colourful and there are a total of 16 levels currently with more coming soon so there is a nice amount of content for the price.
Dashy Square is a fairly fast paced game with a soundtrack to match each and every level which I love. I had hoped to purchase the soundtrack via Steam after playing but this isn’t an option which is a shame. There is a link in game to the soundtrack in-game where I think some of the tracks are downloadable but it is still something i’d like to see on Steam so I could easily use the tracks in other platformers that allow audio import.

There is also a nice amount of visual customization in game from all the textures of powerups and the in level background, to how your avatar looks and even the colour of the trail your produce from items. You can also choose your practice, menu and victory songs. The combination of these two stops the visuals and sound becoming repetitive and increases the replayability. You also have a massive assortment of achievements to get as well meaning even once you’ve completed all the levels there is still stuff you can do.

The game has a few cons, some levels are uncompletable without dying at least once or playing practice mode first. I’m all for difficult levels and a nice challenge but some of the deaths are unavoidable without prior knowledge of what is to come. This makes replaying a level again that you would have otherwise completed a little annoying. The only other issue is the game can seem a bit confusing first because none of the menus really explain what they do but it’ll come to you very quickly.

Though I got Dashy Square in a bundle I still think $3 is still a great price for those looking for a challenging platformer with a great soundtrack to accompany it and plenty of customization. But be warned, this game is not for the impatient or those looking for an easy win.

General Impression
Dashy Square is a perfect combination of the action and indie genres, and in my opinion, a decent alternative to Geometry Dash. The graphics are pretty nice, they are 2D, with pretty decent textures. The atmosphere is basically a bunch of areas, where your square has to reach the final of the area, but he will have to repel many obstacles. The audio is nice, as well as the music is great, it’s really entertaining for an indie game. The gameplay is about repelling many obstacles, but in the same time, collecting different keys, which unlock many achievements. Once you reached the final of the area, the wave is finished. Each level has 5 waves, right now there are only 16 levels. So basically, a ton of waves. The user interface is well-optimized, with no bugs and well-synthetized information. It also contains funny elements, and an user-friendly design. The controls are well-optimized, where you are able to walk your square either with your arrows or with your mouse. Personally, I prefer the arrows, because they are more accurate. The engines are alright, I haven’t met any bugs, crashes, screen freezes or frame drops. The mechanics aren’t the strongest point in this game, I personally dislike the constant shacking of the map. Apart of that, the stopping and the jumping of the square are alright, as well as the moving of it. It’s pretty well-optimized even for very bad computers. Overall, it’s a really fun platformer game, with some pretty nice graphics, challenging gameplay and an amazing electronic soundtrack.

Positive Points
  • Pretty nice graphics
  • Good audio and soundtrack
  • Challenging gameplay
  • Funny characters
  • Levels/Endless mode
  • Upcoming level editor
  • Pretty well-designed mechanics
  • User-friendly interface
  • A tutorial is available
  • Steam trading cards & many achievements

Neutral Points
  • More polishing on some elements would be great
  • A CO-OP option would be an amazing feature to the game

Negative Points
  • A little too hard from the begging
  • The screen probably shakes a little too much (the developer has fixed it)

Dashy Squares is certainly an acquired taste in terms of video games. In theory it’s a simple game, your job is jump and dodge obstacles as you race towards the finish line. It offers different modes of gameplay and a wide variety of customizing options for your avatar as well the music. You have sixteen levels to play and plenty of in-game power ups and trophies to grab. It’s a game with a high replay value.

You can play this game with a controller, as I preferred, or your keyboard. Your avatar is always constantly moving in the levels, it’s your job to slow it down to avoid danger or time your moves right to collect bonuses. Despite being pretty simple to understand the game is very difficult. I found it frustratingly difficult. There is no story and no real goal, you play for the challenge of it and to collect things. One feature I did like was that you can play any level you like from the start with more to come, so it’s nice to have the option to bounce around try different things.

The music in the game is described as electronica/edm so I imagine club or rave type music. There are forty songs and you can pick and choose which you want to play, however, what I didn’t like is when you go to change up a song there is no sample preview of what the song will sound like so you’re left shooting in the dark. The music is also rather loud in the game and I quite frankly didn’t like the music at all. It wasn’t bad music it just wasn't my cup of tea. The graphics are alright, they’re pixels formed into shapes. They’re not horrible but they’re very simple, I feel more detail could have been added and maybe a nicer user interface.

This game really just went over my head, I didn’t like it at all. It feels like a game aimed at the meme/youtuber generation and that’s perfectly fine, just because I didn’t like the game does not make it a bad game. I feel it offers a great challenge with good controls that alone is commendable. This game also has Steam achievements and trading cards and it’s only three dollars. Look at the information, watch the trailer and make your own decision. I recommend Dashy Squares if anything in this review has caught your interest.
