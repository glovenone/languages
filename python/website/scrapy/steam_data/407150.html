17 March

Controls Update

Vehicle controls have been updated to be now perfectly consistent with the following rules:

1. Press a direction parallel to the surface (perpendicular to the yellow arrow) you are attached to, you will then start moving towards the adjacent surface in that direction.

2. If it is possible to move towards a direction you will do so by pressing this direction.

Rule 1. defines distinct movement along surfaces in any given situation.
You might find it handy that rule 1. can be applied in any corner case.

But the biggest change you will notice is the pointing of the yellow arrow. It is now indicating the attached surface/s more clearly.

I hope it helps!

Juri

About This Game

http://store.steampowered.com/app/375930/
Trap Them - Sniper Edition is a puzzle shooter alternative to Trap Them with new caves to explore. Equipped with a sniper rifle and a remote-controlled crystal collector you take the role of a sniper. Collect all the crystals without getting caught by the RoboTTech robots while exploring the depths of the mountain. Be smart about shooting down the environment to shape it to your needs and guide the crystal collector safely through the caves.

Features:
- smooth 60fps and clockwork perfect game mechanics
- deliberately designed for keyboard + mouse input

- innovative block physics
- wide variety of challenges (puzzle, action, mixed)
- multiple solutions rewarding player's creativity
- extremely challenging puzzles in the later stages of the game
- 90+ caves to explore

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: WindowsXP
    • Graphics: DirectX9 GPU
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 150 MB available space

I now have played the original Trap Them about 100 hours in total and I'm still not done with it. I couldn't wait to check out the Sniper Edition spin off.
Fortunately, the emphasis on mechanics is not noticeably reduced but is changed in a very clever and elegant way. The duality in operation, sniper on the one hand and the collector on the other, creates an interesting interplay and adds good balance to the puzzles once again. Now you don't have to move a bit but can shoot the rocks with a rifle from a secluded safe spot. This advantage is counter balanced by the limited mobility of the collector since it can only move along the surfaces. This makes some tasks easier but others more tricky. It turns out that this game, like its predecessor, has a good balance. The sophistication and diversity of the puzzles proves it once again.

Overall it seems more "lightweight" than the original Trap Them but it will still frequently make you feel like a total re-tard. As a puzzle fan you will have a lot of these “oh that is an elegant solution” moments.

Finally, I want to state that both Trap Them games are masterful works. It is mind-blowing how much diversity can be created with a small set of tools.
