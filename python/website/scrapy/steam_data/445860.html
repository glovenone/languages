Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We are in Early Access because VR is a totally new and immersive experience for the player. Therefore we want to learn with the help of the community how we can improve the game to be a perfect experience in VR.
We are also giving the player an early version that already makes a lot of fun and we are going to provide regularly content updates for the game.
We think in this way the player is going to have a lot of fun early in the game and we can learn and improve the game by the community feedback!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Around 6 month, depending on the feedback of the community.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We want to add more maps, planes, weapons and gamemodes.

But most importantly we want to learn with the help of the community feedback, in order to create a fun and inspiring VR Game.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“At the moment we have three maps, five planes, one helicopter and three weapons.

The three gamemodes, which already exists, are Team Deathmatch, Capture the Flag and Classic Race. We also have an interactive tutorial to teach you the basics of the game.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price will increase when we leave the Early Access. This is our way to thank the early buyers that put a lot of trust in the future of our game.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are going to look regularly for community feedback on steam, reddit and social networks...
We want to keep in touch with our community, so it is really important for us to have an active communication through these different channels.”

Added Highscores, Confetti Canons and a lot more!

Hi Pilots,

we've just released our next update!

We are now featuring Highscores for all of our gamemodes and maps! The top 10 users will be listed there, but your personal score will also be saved so that you can try to improve yourself. Especially for the Racing mode the new scoreboards are a great improvement. We are really excited to see what times you are going to accomplish on the different maps!

Major Changes:

  • Added Scoreboard for Highscores
  • Added Confetti Canons. (Reeeeeeally awesome feature!)
  • Respawn Timer is now 5 seconds, so it's less annoying if you get stuck.
  • Added 3D effect to the TV
  • Improved Tutorial
  • Improved handling of the Airliner
  • Added little "Toy Soldiers" to the maps
  • Improved HOTAS support. (Turn it on in the option menu)
  • Improved plane textures
  • New menu structure (Fly now was misinterpreted by some users)
  • Added plastic part tipp
  • Fix: Reset and open menu controls are now customizable

All in all this is a great update but we also had some troubles with our supermarket map. The lightmapping currently makes a lot of problems and we haven't managed to get a reasonable quality. We are working day and night on this but we already wanted to share the new features, so the supermarket map will be in the next update.

As always, have fun playing Toy Plane Heroes! :)

28 May

Update: Helicopter, Closed Cockpits, Capture the Flag and other cool stuff!

Hi Pilots!

We are very excited to announce that we've just released our next big update!

We've included a new level one standard plane called "Airliner". The plane looks a little bit like a Piper and has a closed cockpit. Therefor it should be a lot more comfortable for people who are sensitive to motion sickness. :)

One of the biggest changes in the update is that we've integrated our first helicopter called "Little Copter". We think we've managed to develop a flight system that is both challenging but not to hard for beginners. The helicopter is also a level one vehicle so that people can choose from three different types at the start. They should be comfortable in the regards of simulator sickness as you can't do rolling and loopings etc. It will still be very intense in regards of heights! ;) We made it a little bit slow so that people won't crash to fast. :P
We are really excited about your feedback! Helicopters are adding so much variety to the game, especially in the gameplay mechanics. We are really happy to have them in TPH.

The other cool feature that we've added is the new gamemode "Capture the Flag". The bots are able to play it so there won't be any problems in case there is nobody online. (There are not a lot of HMD's out there, so we made sure that it is fun against bots. ;) )

We've played it with three people and it was really intense. I was defending the flag with my helicopter and the other people tried to catch the flag. You really have to develop some strategies for this gamemode! :)

Overall we've also made a lot of smaller changes and bugfixes. For example: You now get a "cloud" message once you shot a plane so that you know who you shot.

Anyway we hope you like the changes and the direction Toy Plane Heroes is going! We still have a lot planned so stay tuned! :)

About This Game

Overview


NOTE: DK2, CV1 OR VIVE NECESSARY. (Seated VR: Xbox Controller/Keyboard/Custom Joystick support, please turn your controller on before starting, VR Comfort: Intense)

Launch Option: For the CV1 and DK2 please choose "Other VR" when starting the game and make sure that you've allowed Unknown Sources in Oculus Home. For HTC Vive please choose Steam VR.

Toy Plane Heroes is a unique multiplayer dogfighting game in which you are controlling a toy plane. The game is made for VR and you are going to experience an incredible feeling of scale, because you are a tiny toy figure. We currently support the Oculus CV1, DK2 and the HTC Vive. We are also planning to make TPH compatible with the most common HMD's.

Details


Features in the early access Version:
  • Intense up to 4 vs 4 multiplayer action
  • Made for virtual reality
  • Aim with your head
  • Including Helicopters, closed cockpit and open cockpit planes
  • Customizable Controls (Including Alpha HOTAS support, Note: turn on your joystick before you start TPH)
  • Collect cool Power Ups
  • Experience scale
  • Airplane Racing
  • Play with or against bots
  • Level up and get new planes

Planned Features:
  • Several new gamemodes including "Crazy Race", "Zeppelin Defence"
  • Continuous releases of new maps, planes and weapons
  • Customizable avatar
  • Support most common HMD's
  • A lot more, depending on the wishes of the community!

The target of each match is to try to defeat the other planes by shooting at them with toy styled weapons, like the „Bubblegum Cannon“. By hitting the enemy plane you get plastic parts which you can use to buy new weapons. After each round you are earning experience points. Once you level up, you will be getting new planes. The game style is inspired by movies like "Toy Story" or "Monster INC.". The environment is colorful and adventurous. We are also planning to have different gamemodes. Currently in our Early Access you can play “Team Deathmatch”, Classic Racing and Capture the Flage against other players or bots.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7, Windows 8, Windows 8.1, Windows 10
    • Processor: Intel i5-4590 or higher
    • Memory: 8 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GTX 970 or Radeon R290 or higher
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Storage: 8 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Requires Oculus Rift CV1, DK2 or HTC Vive

This is a cute game with a lot of potential, but for those who are prone to motion sickness it is a no go at the moment (I guess the "VR COMFORT: INTENSE" warning is a VERY honest assessment in that respect). I would love to see a control scheme more like Anshar Wars 2 (for the Gear VR), where you use the headset to control your craft's motion and the controller to aim your weapon - that makes games like this MUCH more tolerable in VR, as the motion of your head better matches what your eyes are seeing, thus decreasing feelings of disorientation and nausea (I feel none whatsoever in Ashar Wars 2 and none in general on most VIVE games). If at all possible, PLEASE incorporate that as an alternate control scheme to make this game more accessible to a wider range of people, including myself.

And as a side note, the game runs flawlessly on my system (GeForce 980 Ti x2 and core i7-5930 @ 4.0 GHz with a SSD on MSI Godlike board), so specs/settings have nothing to do with it. It is the disconnect between the visual stimulus and what the vestibular canals of the inner ear are sensing. Add the alternate control scheme I suggested, and it will be way more comfortable. ;)

Early Access Review
Posted: 17 April
First of all : I love this game! It's one of the best VR Games out there at the moment, and incredibly advanced for an early access game!
So now im gonna explain why exactly i love this game :

1. Gameplay :
The gameplay is pretty simple. You fly a plane, and shoot other planes.
But one point, wich a lot of plane games f*ck up is done incredibly well in Toy Plane Heroes : The controls.
They are very responsive, and very easy to understand and master. You use one stick, to rotate the plane around it's own axis, and the other one to turn your plane left or right.
To accelerate you just press RB (on an Xbox controller), and to brake you press LB.
Shooting is done by pressing the triggers, one for each weapon.
Where you shoot is depending on where you look. The two elements of steering your plane with the controller, and aiming your shots with the head makes the whole game very complex and deep, while it's still easy enough for people who know how to handle controllers! (If you're not a gamer though, you shouldn't start gaming with this game...)

2. The Graphics
I can't say too much about the graphics, because this game has a toy... -ish(?) style to it, so of course it isn't hyper realistic. But overall the graphics are really good! They do exactly what they're supposed to be, the textures have a very good resolution, and the whole toyish look comes across great!
Also, the style makes for relatively low graphics requirements, which is pretty good!

3. The motion sickness
So, for a plane game you would probably expect a lot of motion sickness, but, even though i get motion sick pretty quick, i can play this game for long periods of time without a lot of motion sickness! The only two times when i really get sick are 1. when i fly loopings (wich is understandable), and 2. when i get shot and my plane tumbles down. Because of the rapid movement, wich i can't control, it's really hard to look at. But after like 3 times, i just instinctively closed my eyes, which basically eliminates this problem.

So overall this game is just awsome! I just recommend playing it for yourself. Especially with friends, it's awsome. Though, the bots are also very fun to play against.

Overall im giving a rating of 8.5/10
