Elite Dangerous Arena is Completely Free This Weekend!



Greetings Commanders,

Elite Dangerous Arena will be completely free on Steam and the Frontier Store this weekend. If you purchase a copy of Arena between now and 6PM BST on Monday July 11 you won’t be charged a thing, and you’ll retain access to the game even after the deal ends.

This 100%-off deal will bring a whole new host of Commanders to the CQC Arena, so make sure you jump in now to show them the ropes and take advantage of the unique benefits that reaching prestige rank in Arena can bring to your main game account.

When you reach CQC Rank 50 you’ll be given the option to 'Prestige'. You will set your rank back to one and lose all your unlocks, but in return you’ll be able to earn more credits for use in the main game, and get a permit to the Attilius Orbital starport in CD-4311917 where you’ll get exclusive discounts on many modules and ships. You can prestige up to level 9 and at that point you’ll be receiving the maximum amount of credits you can take in to the Elite Dangerous galaxy.

With Arena being completely free until 6PM BST on Monday July 11, now’s the perfect time to recruit new wingmates to join you in the CQC Arena.

GLHF

---

Disclaimer: Arena is the PVP mode already in Elite Dangerous so all players who own Elite Dangerous or Elite Dangerous: Horizons will already have access to it. Simply select Arena from the in-game main menu to play.

About This Game

Notice: Elite Dangerous: Arena is free on PC for a limited time only – download now and play anytime! Offer ends 11th July 2016.

Fly agile fighters and go head-to-head against the galaxy’s greatest pilots in PVP dogfights - easy to pick up controls reward your developing skills with more to master for that ultimate combat edge. Choose your ship, customise your load out and prepare for instant Arena-based action to earn the right to become Elite.

Elite Dangerous: Arena brings Elite Dangerous’ Close Quarter Combat (CQC) PVP game to players as a standalone release, featuring full cross-compatibility with the large and growing Elite Dangerous player community.

First things first:

IF YOU OWN ELITE DANGEROUS, YOU ALREADY OWN THIS!

Secondly:

Ignore my play time. I play this through the main game instead of the standalone (had to install the standalone to write this review).

Now that's out of the way, on to the actual review:



Awesome PVP spinoff from Elite Dangerous where you can practice your combat skills against other players, without the risk of loosing the ships that you've worked so hard for in the main game... Good graphics, fast action, very well optimised... The modern equivalent of Xwing vs Tie Fighter.

It's a fun little FPS in space, with you piloting the very small agile fighters that you'd sometimes see defending capital ships.

It features rank progression with loadout upgrades and different ships, there are 3 different game modes (Deathmatch, Team Deathmatch, Capture the Flag), a variety of weapons, the ability to use multiple controllers (I find 2 joysticks the most fun way to play, but a regular hotas works, you can use a control pad, and even keyboard and mouse is viable (however if you use keyboard and mouse: CHANGE the controls, defaults are horrible from what I can remember).

As an added bonus, any credits you make in the Arena (aka CQC) will transfer over to the main game but to begin with the cash rewards are pretty low. You have to get a decent rank to start getting decent credits from it.

Been on the fence about buying Elite Dangerous? This will give you an idea how the combat works, though everyone will die a lot faster in these tiny little fighters compared to the bigger ships from the main game.

It's going free to play for the weekend I'm writing this, as sadly player count has been a bit limited recently (most people prefer the main game itself, but many like myself enjoy both - just for different reasons).

And you can ignore most of the negative reviews, most of them haven't even played it and instead are complaining about having to copy a cd key over to activate it which you only have to do once. There's a couple that have valid points, like low numbers of players, but hopefully this free weekend will help with that.

This is the best space combat arena game out there, hopefully going free will make it easier to get a match. The combat is fast, the flight model is great, it rewards high levels of skill and learning how the game works.

Top tips: If you put full power to shields it makes them 250% stronger, so long as you have power to fire your weapons you should do that the second you come under fire. Use flight assist off when you turn (its like turning off space traction control). Always try to pick up the weapon boost. Embrace the learning curve and you'll soon be kicking ♥♥♥.

If you wing up to play as a team in team deathmatch make sure you've put a fair bit of practice in first, it will match you against the first four players it finds and some of us have had a lot of practice ;-)

It does help to have a joystick (or two in my case) but there are good players who play keyboard and mouse.

Now that this is free I hope to see you all out there. Sometimes I think Frontier has no idea how well they hit this out of the park at the first attempt.

I bought Elite Dangerous from the Frontier store at launch. I played this Arena mode since it came out last summer. I downloaded this just to write this review. Its good.
