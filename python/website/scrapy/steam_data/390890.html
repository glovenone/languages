About This Game

Angry Arrows is a cooperative archery game about rescuing hostages from enemy strongholds using nothing but bows and arrows.

Angry Arrows will leave you excited, eager for more, and probably a little miffed. Remember, just one fatal mistake and it's back to square one. With a team of up to four players, communication is the key to victory. Use stealth to sneak around enemies and pick them off one at a time. Or rush in, arrows-first, and pray things go according to plan. With such open and dynamic gameplay, the possibilities are endless.

Key Features

  • Four intense game modes
  • Tough enemies, leaving you dead in your tracks
  • Experience the entire game with up to 4 players
  • In-Game Store featuring unlocks to spice up gameplay
  • Full Controller Support, with simple, yet responsive controls

Game Modes

Campaign Mode
Make your way through 25 demanding levels designed to test your skills and accuracy. Be warned, these levels are guaranteed to cause yelling and anger (no pun intended).

Gladiator Mode
Enter the Colosseum and work together to fight off increasingly difficult waves of enemies coming from all directions. When fighting in the Colosseum, players have the option to revive their fallen teammates, if clear from any crossfire. Or, the last remaining player can defeat the rest of the wave alone, if they are brave enough.

Rogue Mode
Rescue a hostage hidden deep in a randomly generated forest. A player can either enter their own level seed or let the game create one. Pausing the game while playing in Rogue Mode will allow players to see the seed. This is useful for any player who wants to write it down and play the same level again in the future. Rogue mode provides players with an infinite number of challenging missions sure to test even the best player's abilities.

Versus Mode (2 player minimum)
Tired of working together? Shoot your friends! Brawl with your friends on the Versus Island, featuring four various modes:
  • Extraction - Retrieve the hostage from the center and bring her back to your spawn while defending her from the other players.
  • King of the Key - Grab the key from the center and hold on to it for a total of 45 seconds.
  • Deathmatch - Good old-fashioned deathmatch. First to 15 kills wins.
  • Mine Game - Deathmatch with a twist. Players now hurdle landmines at each other instead of shooting arrows.

The Store

Whenever a player kills an enemy in the game, that enemy will drop gold coins for any player to pick up. Players compete to see who can collect the most coins during a level. Though, at the end of any level, win or lose, all coins from each player will pile together and add to the overall collective stash. Using this stash, players can unlock and purchase a variety of arrows, such as Medusa Arrows.

The full OST can be found at http://erikcld.bandcamp.com

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows Vista, 7, or later
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 1GB Video RAM
    • DirectX: Version 10
    • Additional Notes: Gamepad is strongly suggested

Angry Arrows is a small game made by independent developer Daniel Davis. You play as an archer who is sent to enemy camp where hostage is held, to kill all hostiles and save the princess captive.

Controls are made with focus on being precise, and the game reacts properly, without any delay. You can move, roll and shoot (to any selected direction). Controls reminds me of another indie game, Titan Souls (though there you could use only one arrow, Angry Arrows allows you to spam as many arrows as you want).

Difficulty of the game is set pretty high and requires a patient player. If you can get angry and tilted pretty fast, this isn't a worthwhile pick for you. There aren't any checkpoints, each level (though they are not long) requires to be beaten without dying. There aren't any lives - one shot, one kill, and that applies to both player and the enemy NPCs.

But the NPCs aren't everything to make your playthrough harder, there are also mines, explosive barrels and, mainly, fast annoying spider mines. They like to jump on your head, but trust me, you won't like it.

There are four modes in the game - campaign (25 levels), endless arena, rogue (procedurally generated levels) and multiplayer arena. Game as a whole is perfectly polished (for a cheap game made by yet unknown developer), everything works great - responsiveness, menu screens, gamepad compatibility.

Conclusion:
Angry Arrows is a great game for those who want to play something small but difficult, and is also great example many poor indie developers should check, as they could learn something about making the cheap game polished (say hello to Digital Homicide).

Singleplayer is okay, but can get frustrating because you have to start the level all over again if one of the enemies shoots you with an arrow. And they almost never miss! The game gets really fun in co-op mode (local only!). If one of the players dies and the other one is still alive, you respawn after five seconds. So you can strategize, like spreading out or one player heads out and the other cowardly hides behind a wall. So in campaign mode you try to reach a hostage and bring him/her to the exit, while not being killed by mines, enemies and robots. Some more enemy types or traps would be cool, though we haven't finished the campaign yet, so maybe there's more than these three types.
There's also a couple of other modes, Gladiator mode (fighting wave after increasing wave of enemies) and Rogue mode, which introduces random seed forests in which you only have a limited time to free a hostage. I did not think these two modes added a whole lot to the game.
Versus mode, however, was great, you can choose between four different types and it adds a lot of replayability.
We played this with two xbox360 controllers and after a bit of practice, the aiming feels precise and fast, so no complains about the controls.
All in all, great co-op game for a low price, recommended!
