About This Game

"Who Was I Meant To Be?"

A hand-sketched 3D adventure game. Control Sketch, unfinished and wishing to know who it was supposed to be. The only clue is to follow a pencil that continues to draw in the distance, followed by an artistâs voice as he struggles to complete his work. To reach new areas, the doodle will find left-over sketches to use for new abilities throughout the journey.

  • Collect abandoned sketches of other ideas as new swappable abilities, letting Sketch jump, run, fly, and more to progress through the environment.
  • Featuring traditional 2D animation in a 3D environment filled with crumpled paper and unfinished drawings from the developer and 25 other artists.
  • A short story of the art and his artist told in parallel, of themes of acceptance and self-discovery.

DISCLAIMER: this game is 5 levels long, and it is possible to finish this game in under 30 minutes.

Sadly this happens basically only once and it simply isn't enough to make up for the rest of the game.

What if your dreams, ideas, and creations all had a life of their own? One of man's most greatest abilities is to create, rather that be life, tools, or art. Sometimes though, creation isn't easy, sometimes things are left undone, inspiration is a valuable commodity that doesn't come easy. You will explore these concepts and more in this interesting and entertaining puzzle game. You play an unfinished doodle, who seeks identity in a world of art and chaos. The story is shared between Artist and Doodle as they both go through a journey of discovering themselves and purpose. While Unfinished - An Artist's Lament is a rather short game, what it does have to offer is extremely enjoyable aesthetically and gameplay follows suit. Finding unfinished drawings, you add abilities to your own drawing, such as spring boots, helicopter hat and other whimsical powers. The puzzles in Unfinsihed are rather simple, the game never draws focus away from what matters, the story. This leads the game to feeling like its full of good ideas but ends rather suddenly. While this was obviously intended, a part of me couldn't help but wonder what else could've been had the ideas and powers/levels been expanded upon. Nevertheless Unfinished - An Artist's Lament is a journey of self actualization that is fun, pleasant, and makes you ponder your own creations and goals that you never quite completed. This game is a fine example of Gamer Art and should be treated and enjoyed as such.

This is a difficult game for me to review.

Unfinished is an interesting short game. You play as an unfinished drawing who wants to be complete.
The game serves as an effective metaphor for the creative process in general: everyone who has tried to create something, be it art, writing, a game, or whatever, can recognise the story this game tries to tell.

The art style is great. All hand-drawn, and it stays this throughout. The art is accompanied by soothing music.
The narrative is told through voice over -- sadly no subtitles -- that is clear, if at a little low volume compared to the music.

Gameplay wise, you walk through white, empty environments to find body parts (different legs, different bodies, different heads), all of which give you enhanced abilities. As you progress the levels become more... drawn.

Unfortunately Unfinished is hard to get in to. I was stuck at the first real puzzle for far too long, since the game didn't make it clear at all that I could swap abilities in mid-air, and I only found this by chance.
It is also short. Too short perhaps for Steam refunds. If I hadn't gotten stuck at the beginning I could've finished it within 30 minutes.

While a bit rough the game is mechanically sound and the story resonates with me, as a fellow (want to be?) creator.

Unfinished: An Artist's Lament is a heavily story driven game. You play as a sketched out sort of stick figure who's chasing down the pencil that drew him, in an attempt to become "complete". It must be understood that this is not an action packed game and if you try to play it as such, you will not enjoy yourself. You're tasked with solving puzzles to make your way through each level while chasing down the aforementioned pencil by using tools that you find along you way. Each tool is applied to your stick figure's head, torso, or feet with some puzzles requiring you to use a combination of your different pieces of attire. The puzzle solving, while simple, is unique and entertaining, but not enough to keep someone captivated. However, the game makes up for its simplicity with its story. The narrator, and evidently the man who drew the stick figure in the first place, can be heard dictating different key moments in his life as an artist. The artist, struggling to look for work, faces the same struggles his stick figure creation does in the sense that neither of them are complete. As someone with experience in the field of art, the story struck a very emotional chord with me and was that much more meaningful. If you are looking for solid gameplay and replayability, this is not the game for you. But if you're looking for some quick and cheap fun, with what I think is a rather amazing story, then I would definitely suggest Unfinished for you. To enjoy this, you have to be able to appreciate the game for what it is: the story of the struggles of an artist, plagued by how he could never finish his work, only to feel lost and never truly complete himself. Unfinished is a unique and fun experince, perfect for the average puzzle solver, and relates to everyone in one way or another. On the other hand, if you think this is the kind of game you'd rather watch someone play, then you can check out my walkthrough on my channel (shameless self promotion): https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mwLyqMS5caU
