About This Game

Pixel Space is a 2D entertaining indie game where you are a ship in an infinite space and you can do quests, kill enemies in a waves system, customize your ship, buy weapons and lot of upgrades, each weapon has at least two paths of upgrades so you can play how you like. Your goals in this game are to arrive at the max level and difficulty, to upgrade your favourite weapons and to have fun as much as you can.

Pixel Space is a very simple yet fun indie game for all your spacey-shooter needs. It is games like this that stood out the most among all the others with its simplistic and clean style.

The game relies on standard WASD and mouse, alongside with 1-5 to control the spacecraft. Unfortunately, there isn’t an option to switch up the hotkeys provided within the game for those who aren’t comfortable with the initial setup.
Right off the bat, Pixel Space welcomes the player with a small pop up tutorial message that the player could choose to skip over. Once you’re officially in the game, a small list of missions is available on the left side for you to choose:
  • Recovery- Locate the cargo/item within the vast space by paying attention to the distance bar. The further you are away from the object, the more the distance bar would increase.
  • Rescue- Rescue the gigantic spacecraft and fend off the incoming enemies.
  • Waves- Defeat waves of monsters and survive until the end.
  • Boss- Eliminate the boss space ship alongside with its “underlings” to complete the mission.
  • Store- The one stop for you to improvise your ship with various upgrades to increase your chance of survival against the bigger baddies.

There is a small “warp” key under the selected mission, which enables the player to fast travel to the specified location in matter of seconds. Although after following the missions a couple of times, it could prove to be quite repetitive, the option to fast travel really makes it up for it by being conveniently accessible.

A game gets old if it doesn’t offer customizable weapons and whatnots, Pixel Space offers the player a chance to change it up via the store with several color options for your spacecraft and laser beams (it would be great, however, if there is an option to change the model of the ship as well). You can also find different weapons to help you shoot down your enemies (I suppose the enemies would be much better with more diversities and different formations as well to surprise the player).

In regards to combat, it is rather easy to play with the “shoot and dodge” method. With the asteroids floating around, my first thought was to avoid them, but when I realized my space ship is fine crashing into them, I wanted to use them as shields against the enemies. Unfortunately, the floating rocks didn’t seem to be able to block the enemies’ laser beams (I must add, it would be really cool if they could block the incoming fire and be able to explode into pieces), so I could only try to dodge the beams as if I was playing Touhou.

All in all, this game is quite fun if you’re looking for a casual game to play and I definitely recommend this for those who’re interested.
