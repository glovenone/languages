About This Game

The neglected and despised toys of the world are angry and have escaped their forgotten toy box prisons and are seeking revenge on the wanted and liked toys. You must stop them by finding and destroying their toybox lairs. At times, construction workers may come to help by building resources, so protect them as you battle the unwanted.

In first person, play singleplayer, 4 person co-op and 8 person Death Match and fight 21 enemy including deformed toys, wizards, giant cyborg bears and plastic soldiers.

The world is interactive and randomized and set to a real world scale. Play with an arsenal of 13 weapons, 2 armed vehicles, 3 racing cars ( a slot car racing track), model train to ride, a beach plus more ...

Enjoy an enhanced game only on Steam.

Intro - About
This game has a lot of potential. You play as a toy that is being hunted by unwanted and abandoned toys. You can select which toy you play as and, depending on your choice, you will start with unique weapons. Use these weapons to kill a bunch of old barbies, robots and what seem to be abominations of mashed together toys-- probably made by some kid out of boredom. While surviving these relentlessly angry and jealous toys, you must also find your way to their lair with the only help being an arrow showing which direction to go. Not all toys are evil, however, and you will encounter small construction worker toys who will assist you on your journey for as long as you can defend them.

Pros & Cons
++ Pros
+ Fun and appealing visuals
+ Easy to learn controls
+ Good sound quality
+ Co-op
+ Plenty of maps

-- Cons
- Expensive for the content
- Choppy animation
- No story
- Barebones in terms of content
- Repetitive enemy design

Personal Opinion
I enjoyed this game up to a certain point. The game is great fun and all, even having me on the edge of my seat from some scary moments, but there could be so much more! This game just seems empty, which is a shame given its huge potential. It just seems unpolished and unfinished. The UI is clunky and unintuitive. The controls are easy to learn but they often use weird keys. The graphics are nice, and the game has decent lighting and bloom, But it just doesn't feel immersive. Like I said, this game has no polish and still needs a lot of work, but there is no doubt in my mind that this game will be great if the devs put some more work into it!

Final recommendation:
This is a great game and it will probably get better considering there is a bit of love from the developers, but if I were you, I would wait until it was on sale. I've been keeping an eye on the changelogs, and once it gets a bit more polish, I don't doubt that it will be good fun to play with friends. I give this game a
6.5/10
due to the lack of polish and content, but it is still a solid title and delivers a good bit of fun! If you can get it for a good price, pick it up, or you can wait for more content and grab it full price.
