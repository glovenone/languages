About This Game

RUNNING WITH RIFLES is a top-down tactical shooter with open-world RPG elements.

In RWR, you join the ranks of an army as a common soldier, just like the thousands around you.

The open world approach lets you define your own path and story in the campaign. Push back the enemy with your comrades or go deep behind enemy lines to sabotage their efforts and loot valuable items.



As you gain experience, you are promoted with higher command over soldiers and equipment. Call in artillery fire missions or paratrooper reinforcements when the situation gets tight! Use your squad to man armed boats, tanks and APC's, or, become the expert lone wolf you always aspired to be - it's up to you!

KEY FEATURES

  • hundreds of locations to explore from trenches to towns, deserts to snowy valleys
  • emergent AI that will question if you're as smart as you think you are
  • realistic cover system
  • dozens of different weapons, support and cover items, radio calls, vehicles
  • side objectives to keep you busy: destroy radio towers and other assets, steal cargo trucks, rescue prisoners
  • 40+ multiplayer support, dedicated servers, coop, PvP, PvPvE
  • speech bubbles!
  • deaths and fails, a lot of them, including your own!
  • ...and of course, mods!

I know how to play this game and am not awful by any stretch of the imagination. My screenshots also include 30 "outstanding" events that I achieve on a regular basis. I have over 130k resource points as well to spend on equipment and abilities (while your basic life costs 7-10rp, body armor costs 40, and small artillery strikes cost 250).

Also know that I have published and republished this review a few times. Everytime I publish it, it gets several good ratings before getting slammed by a dozen or so negative ratings. Apparently, some people who are browsing reviews appreciate my warning before some people who like the game don't like how I'm giving their game a thumb-down. After that, the review gets a poor rating score, so others who are browsing never get the chance to read it since it's buried underneath other reviews.

In any case, over 15 years ago, a company called 3DO made a game called "Army Men" and there was a series that followed: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Army_Men

RWR is basically a remake of this series... except it's a lot more frustrating and doesn't really emulate the goofy gameplay that the videos suggest. Enemies will one shot you with instant-aim; you will get randomly ambushed by swarms of bad guys, tanks, and apcs coming from nonsense angles; and your AI squadmates can't be given detailed orders like when playing a real time tactics game. Fellow teammates are often either afraid to advance and just camp, go off to do their own covert op (which usually fails since stealth equipment is massively underpowered), or get your vehicles blown up in stupid ways.

The community also appears to be friendly at first glance, but it's really not. There are many trolls in it who just make fun of other players and lie about accusing you of being a troll when in reality, you're not. This includes cliquey behavior where a bunch of trolls will gang up on you, and claim that if you're accusing them of being trolls, then you're more likely the troll since you're probably simply accusing those who disagree with you of being trolls. The mods on the discussion board have also fallen for these cliques and cannot be trusted with moderating responsibly. They cater to the status quo's popularity contest without consideration to either the silent majority or future potential players.

By no surprise, the multiplayer servers are very underpopulated. There is usually just one up with 12-24 people online, and another one up with 5-10 people. People get sick and tired of the trolls, the cliques they form, and the moderation's refusal to address them.

Furthermore, most players don't carry medpacks either to revive each other when they're down, and there is lots of loot vulturing where players will grab dropped players' loot or leave each other hanging just to pick up what they dropped. Some players will also kill you on purpose with explosives 3-4 times just for fun, and they get away with it since it's under the 5x explosion TK cap where they would get banned for using explosives for 30 minutes. On the other hand, you have many idiots who will run into your line of fire when using vehicle weapons or artillery strikes even if you announce them in advance and provoke you into getting an explosive ban. Some idiots will ram you with a tank while you're driving a truck, and get you blamed for TKs. Other idiots will forget to look both ways when crossing a street and get out of the street ASAP such that you end up running them over while trying to rush to the front line.

Some people even troll by deliberately launching artillery strikes before you do even if you announce your artillery strike in advance. That way, other players believe the artillery strike is over and run in afterwards, and you get a bunch of team-kills you don't deserve.

I wanted to like this game. I really did, but there's nothing to be surprised by when playing it. It is not a Planetside/Battlefield top down shooter (which becomes especially evident when you want to run a chain-link fence over with a tank, but you can't). Your conquests on one map don't give you bonuses on others. PvP is also practically non-existant. It is simply, "What you see is what you get."

now dont let you deter you from this game it is very fun to play its just dont expect the final missions to be like everything else its not exactly fun but everything else is very fun.
