About This Game

Get ready to rock! Hordelicious is a semi-hardcore wave based arena shooter. Survive against endless hordes of enemies with over 45 randomly looted weapons and a plethora of items and upgrades to choose from!

Your original mission was to explore the new planet we found. We knew about the life forms of the planet, but what we didn't know was that these creatures actually worship the planet itself. Now we have disgraced their holy ground with our human feet and angered the creatures of this planet. You have to forget our original plans and fight your way out of there!

Features:


- Over 45 unique weapons
- A plethora of items of upgrades
- Epic boss battles
- Achievements to unlock
- Levels with multiple variations

This is like if you'd take Crimsonland, cut out about 70% of its content and stick whatever remained in a hip and modern, "blocky" graphical style. It's really fun once it's fresh and new to you, but this fun curve dips down shortly afterwards and doesn't recover. The game suffers massively from repetition and tedium, there's nothing hardcore or "semi-hardcore" about this. Where other twin-stick shooters are able to keep things interesting with more perks, enemies and level ideas (ie. Crimsonland) or graphics, or story (ex. Alien Breed, Hatred), Hordelicious falls flat on its face by having nothing particular to offer. By the end of it I was literally bored to tears. World 4 - what were they thinking?! Yes, there are lots of guns but at least a third of them are useless in practice and you want to get rid of them as fast as possible, shooting at the air if need be. Things like Toy Gun or Cupcake Gun or Guitar sound like the developer trying a bit too hard to be an EPIK TR0LL SO RANDUMB, in with the kids. Getting them is fun the first 2 or 3 times but after that it's just rolling ones eyes every time.

It's getting a thumbs up because I got it dirt cheap on sale and at that price it feels just right acceptable. Aaand I got the achievements. Can't bring myself to recommend it really, consider this more of a "neutral" / sideways thumb opinion.
