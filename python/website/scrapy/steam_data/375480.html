Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Chronicon is a large project for just one person to maintain, so I ask the Steam community to participate in the development. I hope to be able to build a great community which gets to help shape the game itself.

My goal is to collect as much feedback as possible through frequent updates with fresh content, tweaks, and other additions, so that I may create a solid and polished game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“I'm aiming for a full release some time after the summer of 2016. However, delays are expected as the game is constantly growing in scope based on the very excellent feedback I've been getting over the forums!”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version will feature many more items, spells, abilities, areas, worlds, enemies, bosses, crafting and other mechanics. The goal is to have five complete worlds for you to discover and explore, slaying your foes and collecting the loot as you advance further. I also intend to have a lot of replayability through scaling difficulties and the option to loop the game with increasing challenge (and reward) each loop. A Hardcore mode will also be available.

Additional gameplay beyond the "Story" (end-game content) is also on the drawing board, but nothing is determined here yet.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Most features and mechanics are present, however there is a lot of content to be added, as the initial release (0.20.0) only featured one of the five planned worlds. Worlds will be added in major content patches over time, and smaller patches will be added frequently with various tweaks and additions. See the news section for recent changes!”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, the final game will offer almost TEN times as much content as the initial release (v 0.20.0), and as such the price is lower while in Early Access. Major updates usually come with a small sale, too.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I will be actively participating in discussions on the Chronicon Steam Community Forums to answer any questions you might have, and reply to feedback. I would love to know what you like, dislike, and hear any suggestions or ideas you might have.

You can also reach me on twitter: @sqrbt.
And the official subreddit: r/Chronicon”

I had a dream, that one day I would make a kick-â¥â¥â¥ aRPG in the spirit of Diablo but with pixel graphics.
Then I found Chronicon, and cried many different tears. Tears of joy that I had found exactly what I was looking for, tears of frustration that I didn't get to make the game, and tears of ...more... joy... joyful tears lots of joy and tears... and...
Okay enough onto the review.

This game is spectacular!
In scope, character builds, and OMG moments, both in frustration because of the difficulty and pure awe of the carnage and destruction.

This is not just your average Pixel aRPG - the sound is great, the pixel art is amazing, the boss fights are fun, and it has so many great things going for it's gameplay I don't know where to begin.

The control scheme works very well, weather you want to use a controller or you want to use keyboard and mouse...

hold on... chicken patties are done...
Thank's for waiting.

The loot drops are fun, and seem right on par with a diablo 3 as far as quality items dropping requent enough to enjoy the tons of loot that does drop.

There are additional portals that can spawn sometimes that take you to challenge areas such as an arena with waves of monsters, or an area with magical monsters that all have a global mod added onto them.

The progression is non-nonsense, you will be killing monsters 95% of the time, and the rest of the time you are back in town selling loot.

The 'town' is an efficiently layed out space that has the NPCs close by and the teleportation pad close by as well.
Almost evey level in a given 'Act' has a waypoint. So there is really not a lot of needless running around.

I have not played with friends, but I imagine it would be just as fun as any other aRPG is with friends.

All highlight points aside, if you are looking for a pixel art aRPG similar to Diablo, then just buy this right now, it is completely worth the full price, and if you see it on sale there should be no question at all.
It is so satisfying on so many levels it is hard to put into words.

Early Access Review
Posted: 31 July, 2015
Chronicon
Developer: Subworld
Publisher: Subworld

Note: A key for this game was provided by the developer for the purpose of writing an informative review. No other compensation was given.

Introduction
==========
Chronicon is about a special magical device...a device that creates a link to the memories of the long-forgotten heroes of yore. A link that allows anyone who ventures forth to relive the past lives of those fallen heroes. You are chosen as one of the few this time...and being chosen is a great honor. What will you do now?

Chronicon is an action-adventure RPG, set in a (so far) small, Early Access world. Yes, Chronicon is in Early Access, but if you look past that tag, you might see the potential Chronicon has. Let's take a peek at what it has in store for you.

Gameplay
==========
So you've installed Chronicon and hit Play Game. You are greeted with a selection of 4 unique heroes: Templar, Berserker, Warden, and Warlock. Each one has a very distinct playstyle. The Templar and Berserker are melee characters, the difference being the Templar is defensive and the Berkserker is offensive. The Warden and Warlock are ranged, with their difference being physical offense (bow/arrows) and magical offense. Choose whichever playstyle suits you the best!

Each character has a small 'tree' of skills they can learn and assign to be used on the fly. You can access a new tier of skills every 5 levels. As you level up by killing monsters you gain skill points (1 per level), and you can invest these points into the skills of your choosing. The more points you invest into a particular skill, the more powerful or effective it becomes. Each character has unique skills and passives that can be learned, some offering great benefits, others offering new spells and actions.

At it's core, Chronicon is a dungeon crawler, similar to titles such as Diablo, Torchlight, or Titan's Quest. You explore a locale, kill monsters, collect loot, and level up. There are even some features in Chronicon that you may recall seeing in these other titles, such as the Portal Scroll which gives you a ticket back to the hub to sell your loot. There are also Waypoints, which you can use to traveI back and forth between predetermined points in each area. Speaking of areas, in the hub there are 5 Teleport Orbs, each one representing a dungeon/location. Only the Fallen Temple (first orb) is currently available, and during my 7-8 hours of play none of the other orbs were made available. The Store Page says these additional levels are coming in future updates. I played Chronicon with a controller, and the combat reminded me of a twin stick shooter. You move your character with the left stick, and aim your attacks with the right stick. It works, to be sure, but it was a little awkward for me at first to see this kind of control scheme in a game of this particular genre.

There are a lot of breakable objects, such as pots, urns, and baskets. These can be great for finding hidden loot and crystals (currency) but I felt like they served to padden the game a little bit. There are literally TONS of breakable objects in each stage, but only small groups of enemies scattered through each stage. I would much rather have mowed through hordes of enemies than break a room full of pots. This might be attributed to the fact that the game is still in Early Access, so I was able to look past it. When you did find monsters, it was fun to mow them down however you could, occasionally enjoying a slightly more prolonged encounter with a 'champion' monster. These are quite a bit more powerful than the normal monsters, and drop significantly more loot. Also spread throughout are small shrines, which take the form of statues. When activated, they may give you a buff or heal you, but they can also rarely spawn powerful creatures to attack you.

Certain loot can only be used by certain classes, and a lot of loot has its own special qualities. An item of one type can be very different than another item of the same type, depending on the quality. Try to stay outfitted with the best gear possible for the best chance of survival!

Graphics
==========
Chronicon has a very distinct sprite-base design. They aren't what I'd call 'basic', but they aren't too terribly detailed either. Don't get me wrong, they look great for sprites, and animations are crisp, clear, and flow smoothly. But don't expect anything special as you play.

The Fallen Temple looked really good...very dark and creepy, as something 'fallen' should be! The monsters fit the theme of the area...ghouls, skeletons, and ghosts running around everywhere. Even some bats thrown in for good measure. All of these have the same quality as the character sprites, and are animated pretty well.

Sound and Music
==========
This is easily the game's biggest downside. There is very little in the way of music or sound effects. The Fallen Temple has a low, somber tune that plays, but that's about it. Your attacks, breaking pots and urns, and hitting enemies have sound effects. But nothing else does. If it did, I hardly noticed. I also attribute this to the game being Early Access, so I was able to overlook it. Understanding that this is a very early Alpha build means that not all assets are implemented yet, and I'm sure they will be in the future!

Performance
==========
Chronicon ran without any hitches on my medium-tier rig. It should pose no issues to most players. It's not a very intense game, and I don't expect many people to run into any performance issues. Does your kitchen have a toaster? It can probably run Chronicon.

I played entirely with a controller, so I can confirm that it indeed has full controller support. The controls were very easy for me to use, even with the twin stick configuration combat has.

Final Thoughts
==========
At the end of the day, Chronicon is a decent entry into the action-adventure RPG genre. It's by no means the best, but it's not terrible. It's worth checking out and at least keeping an eye on as it develops further. With a handful of playable classes, lots of loot to collect, and small hordes of enemies to mow down, you should have a great time exploring the memories of past heroes. Future updates plan to include crafting, more quests, more items, a hardcore mode, and the 4 additional chapters currently missing from the hub. If you want to help this game out and see it suceed, please, by all means give it a purchase to support the devs! This is what Early Access is all about.
