Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to get the community involved to help shape Crowman & Wolfboy for desktop. Our team would like this to be the best version of the game that it can be.

This is also the platform that will help determine the fate of Crowman & Wolfboy! Currently the game ends on a bit of a cliffhanger, as Wither Studios was hoping to develop further expansions to this universe/story. If there is enough interest in C&W Early Access then that could allow us to produce more content beyond the original game. More details here: http://witherstudios.com/the-crow-and-the-wolf/”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“At the moment, it's too soon to give an estimated time frame. Let's just say, less than one year. It will really depend on the communities response to the game and if there's a demand for more content.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We have a lot of things we'd like to add and/or upgrade for the desktop version of Crowman & Wolfboy. However, what we do alter or add will heavily rely on user feedback and support. Here is a list of a few features we'd like to have in the final version:
• Quality Setting
• Achievements
• Leaderboards
• Additional Levels
• More Enemies
• More Collectibles
• Access to Bonus material”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The whole Story Mode's game content is there. So you can play it from start to finish. The game has full controller support, runs on both PC and Mac, and has an updated user interface. The bonus features such as unlockable Tome Pages, Alternate Menus and Infinite Space Level are currently non functional on desktop. Additionally the new interface is functional but still a work in progress. The tutorials are also not in place and there are multiple ways you can use the controls at the moment.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Early Access users are receiving the game at a discounted price. After Early Access, the price will increase based on the final product.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Our goal is to get as much feedback from the community as possible. We want to hear the opinions of what is and isn't working so we can then make changes and improve the game. Here are some examples of elements we are hoping to improve through user feedback:
• Overall Game Speed
• Game Difficulty
• Best Control Configurations
• User Interface”

About This Game

The critically acclaimed mobile game comes to Steam!

INCREDIBLE ADVENTURE
Crowman & Wolfboy are an unlikely pair of shadow creatures on a bizarre journey to escape their own nature and find the mysterious "humans". Help them flee the planet of shadows and the life-devouring Darkness following their every step. Will the path to “humans” lead Crowman & Wolfboy to happiness? Will they ever escape the persistent Darkness? Could a real life crow lift a real life wolf over pits of mutant garbage cans? ... What?!

GAMEPLAY
Crowman & Wolfboy is a story driven, level based, action-platformer/runner hybrid. The characters progress through a series of increasingly difficult levels as they attempt to flee their dark past. Throughout the levels, they'll collect orbs of light to push back the ever pursuing Darkness and learn abilities to better traverse the environments.

KEY FEATURES
• Over 30 levels of challenging action!
• 5 unique environments
• Revisit levels with new abilities to find hidden items
• Three bonus objectives per level
• Find hidden items, unlock ancient knowledge and save the babies!
• Captivating soundtrack
• Travel through space and time in unique play modes!

Early Access Review
Posted: 2 February
NOTE: This game was given to me by the developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.

==========================================================
---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Note: Crowman & Wolfboy is currently in Early Access. The version reviewed was the first release.

Help the good-hearted Crowman and Wolfboy flee the Darkness to reach the happiest place on Earth.

Crowman & Wolfboy is a two-dimensional platform game / runner hybrid, somewhat similar in basic gameplay to other games in this cross-genre combination, such as Vector, although with a completely different setting. It was originally released on iOS and Android back in 2014. Now in 2016 it has come to Steam in Early Access.

==========================================================
----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Graphics - Great! Although the characters and levels are mostly black and white and shades of gray, they fit the storyline to a T. The art style is fantastic and the few characters have great personality imbued in their two-tone forms and animations. Even the faceless Shadow or Darkness that pursues the titular Crowman and Wolfboy oozes evil, brooding personality.

Sound - The soundtrack is very good, with excellent ambient music that once again fits the story perfectly. Sound effects are mostly good, but nothing special. Although, particularly well done is the painful whimper that you hear when Crowman and Wolfboy are injured.

Gameplay - The 2D platformer and runner combination has been done before, but it is used to great effect in Crowman & Wolfboy. Each level starts with our heroes standing still and once you have begun to move them you can't stop them again until they hit a wall or barricade, finish the level, or are captured by the ever-pursuing Darkness. The Darkness moves faster than Crowman and Wolfboy, however, so you have to keep them on their toes. Thankfully each level is scattered with white jiggling balls of light of different sizes, which our heroes pick up and hurl behind them at the Darkness to push it back, allowing them more time to complete the level.

The game begins with Crowman and Wolfboy only able to run and jump/fly a short distance. Throughout the game they gain a few more useful abilities, allowing more flexibility and variation in the levels. The difficulty curve is thus quite gentle as you are introduced to each new ability.

The levels themselves are well designed, some with two or more different paths through them, but were it not for the time pressure and continuous movement, they would be fairly standard, but relatively easy, platforming fare. To enhance replayability each level also has three different goals that can be fulfilled: rescue a fellow critter, collect enough of the larger light balls, and finish the level without being hit. However, at this stage these are not tied to Steam achievements, which I imagine will reduce their attraction to many players.

The Steam page states that the game has full controller support, but I haven't yet tested this. I played with keyboard and was a bit confused at first because some instructions mentioned the keys to press and others were buttons (e.g. A, X), which actually mapped to different keys.

==========================================================
-------------------------------------PROS & CONS------------------------------------
==========================================================
Pros:
+ Great ambiance
+ Engaging story that's both entertaning and sad
+ Likeable characters
+ Very stable, with no bugs encountered

Cons:
- Short: a little over 30 levels, but based on where I'm up to after an hour, I'd say you should be able to complete them in significantly under two hours with a little bit of skill or luck
- Gets repetitive after a while; no achievements (yet) to award going back and replaying levels
- Nothing new (yet) if you've already played it on mobile platforms
- A little more work on the control displays would be helpful for those playing with keyboard.
- The level after Bob's (I don't want to give away any spoilers!) swapped controls from WASD to arrow keys without warning.

==========================================================
--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------
==========================================================
Crowman and Wolfboy is the most fun platform game I've played in quite a while. I really felt something for the main characters and wanted to ensure they succeeded in reaching their goal. It's a shame it's so short, but the developers have stated that there may be more coming in the future. Bring it on, I say!

8/10
