About This Game

In the battle to save your people, will you defy the gods? In an ancient world where myth is reality and fate is relentless, your destiny will lead you to secrets no mortal should ever know.

"Champion of the Gods" is a thrilling 217,000-word interactive novel by Jonathan Valuckas, where your choices control the story. It's entirely text-based--without graphics or sound effects--and fueled by the vast, unstoppable power of your imagination.

As you fight your way through a harsh landscape inspired by Greek myth, you’ll challenge monsters, soldiers, and even gods. Win allies with your humility, your haughtiness, or your powers of deception. Enjoy gifts from the gods, or refuse them all and face the consequences.

Will you earn favor from the goddess of love, or make the god of war your protector? Can you escape a watery death without divine intervention? And when you finally learn the truth of your destiny, will you overthrow the ones who made you, or will you become the Champion of the Gods?

  • Explore a rich world inspired by the ancient myths.
  • Use divine gifts to embolden your allies, and control your enemies.
  • Peer into the future, the far distance, or the hearts of your foes with second sight.
  • Receive a horoscope tailored to your character's unique personality.
  • Delve into romances, or play the game without them--the choice is yours.
  • Play as male or female; straight, gay, or bi.

CHampion of the Gods is a interactive novel heavily based in Greek mythology. It follow the tale of (you guessed it) the champion of the gods and the choices and dilemmas he faces in his destiny. The story is a decent length, when compared to other Choice Games interactive novels. The pacing is quite good, and there are a fair amount of action moments in the story. It took me only an hour to read, so interpret that how you will. The story is pretty good, and the author's creativity shows through clearly, blending their own writing style with the lore of the gods and fate. I did not notice any spelling or grammar errors in my reading of the story. Overall, an enjoyable read, especially at sale price. Supporting these kind of stories if you enjoy them also helps get more published on Steam. If you ever enjoyed stories about Greek mythology (Heracles, Perseus, etc.), this is a must read.

No save points yet and it's getting really tiring

Choice of Gamers is bringing back Choose-Your-Own-Adventure and they've had some good games, I've played FateHaven, Choice of Robots, MechaAce, and now Champion of the Gods (have others that I haven't gotten to yet, almost like my shelves of actual books!) Most of which I own on other devices out side of Steam.

Out of all those Choice of Robots would be my favorite with MechaAce close behind and I recommend both because while the plot is still somewhat linear the branches have more impact. Champion of the Gods, follows more along the lines of FateHaven and for those unfamiliar it comes down to making a choice, and still being shoehorned along one path.

I still yearn for a save function as well, most argue that it gives choices more impact but ultimately it really kills replayability, want to see what happens at the end if you pick choice B instead of A? You have to replay the whole thing. From the beginning.

It's frustrating. Also, I don't know offhand if there are keyboard shortcuts, I've tried some of the obvious choices but it seems the mouse is the only method to advance through the pages. Nothing even lines up either so you can't just place your mouse and click to skip through dialogue you've already read.

Considering the amount of games they have under their belt now this still hasn't changed, and it needs to.

The writing is alright, it's the main (and arguably only) appeal of the game so how does it hold up? It's good. It's still has the draw that will keep you up for 'just a bit more' to see what happens next. Recently it's come to my attention that some of the wording in their games is due to their political stances and requirements they place which just seems kind of ♥♥♥♥♥♥. The thread is in the community discussions, might be gone if you're seeing this later but it might not.

It's not the reason for the No vote though, the reason is I'm sick and tired of the crap save system. I want save points, I kept bookmarks for actual books like this why can't I have save points so I can go back to a point in the story without GOING THROUGH THE ENTIRE GAME?!
