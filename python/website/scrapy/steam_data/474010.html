About This Game

The NADI Project it is a graphic adventure, inspired by the already best-known successes of "The Vanishing of Ethan Carter" and of "Ether One", in which you will face various obstacles.

In this adventure, you will be catapulted into a reality set in a future not too far away.

You will play Jeremy Parker, a famous businessman in his field, but forced by misfortune to go where money has no value.

You will face the difficulties of a shipwreck, in an anonymous island, forgotten by every nautical map. You can't count on anyone, just on your intuition.

Bur nothing is like it seems, even the desert islands do not escape out of this law.

And so you'll find yourself on a journey through ancient ruins, traces left by one of the biggest electronic company in the world, the "Ceberus Electronics"

This giant business made this atoll one of his research bases and your destiny has made it your struggle for existence. You will be forced to explore thoroughly everything that surrounds you, without forgetting your main goal.

RUN AWAY !

I love exploration and story driven games but I hated this one. The environment was visually uninspired, the writing was very poor, and the pacing was far too slow. The main isssue with the pacing is the need to collect objects from the environment in order to progress through the story, for me this took 35 minutes of scouring the same monotonous (yet small) map looking for a sheet of paper. Even when additional options are available (IE gas tanks to fill a lantern) you must find the creator specified object to continue.

Eventually it grew so tedious I found a youtube walkthrough to see the ending which I found as uninspired and predictable as the rest of the game.

The adventure genre has enormous story telling potential, save yourself the time on this one and pick up far better crafted Firewatch or Dear Ester.

Tiny and short little exploring adventure made by a small group of independents and lovingly crafted into an atmospheric experience using limited tech.

The short and the quick of it is you'll spend anything from 20mins to an hour exploring and finding the trigger points and another 5-20mins figuring them all out. A full run through knowing all will be done in 15mins, but dont let that discourage you from giving the immersive little tale a spin.

Involving and atmospheric sound and lighting is cleverly applied considering we are experiencing visuals that are far from technically astounding, but applied very cleverly with sounds cues, music and voice overs on item activation that tells the whimsical tale of a father who has brought his young child to a research island to explore a mysterious path of idealistic thinking leading to a posible new form of communication.....with the spirit world.

I played this through in a constant state of mild terror to avid agitation waiting for some bloody thing to go BOO and far is it from my job to tell you whether or not it does, suffice to says there is a steam achievement linked to the tale called "Its Dark in Here" which is currently bugged for me, and is my only viable complaint for this title.

Take this 500mb download and go and enjoy an hour in here much like you would any good book, except this one you can play!
