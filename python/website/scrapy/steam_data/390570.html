About This Game

Three Digits is the sequel to our first game Two Digits.

The objective is simple. You are given 14 numbers less than 1000, and all you have to do is to choose two subsets with the same sum.

Just try it. See how difficult it is!

Features

  • 300 puzzles, 14 level sets
  • Random mode, generate your own levels
  • Beautiful, calm UI
  • Steam Achievements

3-PARTITION
The 3-partition problem is similar to the partition problem, which in turn is related to the subset sum problem. In the partition problem, the goal is to partition S into two subsets with equal sum. In 3-partition the goal is to partition S into m subsets (or n/3 subsets), not just two subsets, with equal sum.
source

So, basically the same game as Two Digits, just with 3 sums.
A nice game to play on the side.

Summary:
Three Digits is a logic game about finding two equal sums of numbers in sets with fourteen numbers (lower than 1000). Don't expect anything else, you won't get it. :)

Positives:
- Good idea for a logic game.
- Save profiles. Good for those who have shared computers and accounts.

Neutrals:
- Achievements are gained only for level solving (with exception of one), there aren't any especially hard ones.
- With bigger numbers and possible combinations of numbers, Three Digits is (obviously) harder than Two Digits.

Negatives:
- 300 levels might sound great but you might feel really bored after few tens of levels. It's just still the same without any innovation or perceptible gameplay change.
- Mouse control could be made more special for PC, not just quickly ported from smartphones and tablets. Why do we have to double-click if we can use left X right mouse button? Why can't we set controls?

3.9/10 - A Difficult Math Based Puzzler for Nerds

This isn't a bad game and I do enjoy some difficult puzzlers but this is a wee bit too much for me to enjoy! I like to be challenged but this Math based game proves to be too much! On a positive note there are over 500 of these puzzles, cards and achievements.

You are provided with 10 numbers ranging from 1-999; the range increases as the puzzles get more difficult. You have to highlight these numbers either red or green in order to make these 2 groups "sum" equal each other.

If this information doesn't get your blood pumping, don't worry that's normal. There is no tutorial so come into this one with the mindset that these puzzles won't be breezed though. There is a problem solver website that can help you once you have been mentally defeated.

It’s worth $1-3 based on the length. Only buy this if you like hard math based puzzles!
