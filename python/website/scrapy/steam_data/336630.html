About This Game

Crash and Burn Racing is a high impact racing game. You will be put behind the wheel of crazy vehicles that are fitted with over-the-top weaponry and power-ups such as a nitro chargers, bombs and homing missiles.
The sole purpose of the game is to create as much havoc as possible by taking out rivals and preferably causing a fair share of civilian casualties in the process. You get to drive in fast super cars, ambulances, fire trucks and even a heavily armoured school bus.
Each of the vehicles can be fully upgraded and customized to meet the driver's destructive needs. With each successful takedown money is earned, which can be used to upgrade cars.

High speed arcade racing with intuitive controls
Drive crazy vehicles and customize them at will
Wreck your opponent with over-the-top weapons
Stunning fast paced 3D visuals
Infinite replayability

Not even worth the $1 from the Bundle I got it from

The game goal is to get cash by destroying other cars so you can unlock other parts/cars. To do this, you get the help of random bonuses that spawn on the road.
While testing the game on Steam Big Picture Mode with a xbox 360 controller, the first 4 plays were quite nice, but then I started to note problems.
The cash wasn't updating correctly, the level is just a highway that keeps repeating itself, and the music...wait...just 1 music for the whole game ?? I mean, its the same one from the main menu...and the same one from last race...? Sure, I like dubstep (it's a good song for the game), but not over and over again...
Well, if the game was in early access, I would have given it a chance.
Discussion Hub is rather empty, noted some users with some other strange problems.
While trying to quit the game, I found out there is no quit button. I had to get off the couch to get my mouse and keyboard!!! (The horror!!!!)
Oh, and there is no reference what the controls are for the game. Figure it out youself.
It reminds me of a just lauched free mobile game without the ads...

So this is a game to avoid, not worth your time or money.

Graphics: 6/10
Gameplay: 4/10
Sound: 2/10

Final Result: Would "Crash and Burn" the game again.

I would like to give this game a postive rating but I just can't.
I got to this conclusion after very few minutes of playing - which is all the sadder

Which cars I may hit, what will kill me, what will give me points, how and when to use which power-up? None of this.

The only thing this game will do is blast the same Dubstep track* over and over. Did I mention that I have no means of turning that off? I don't need the music, you know, but it would be nice if I could switch it off seperately and not mute my headphones. Because the engine's sound is fine really.

I'm a die-hard Burnout fan and no, this is _not like_ Burnout. From the screenshots I gathered it would be about crashing into junctions, causing havok. A plain copy of Burnout's infamous game mode. It is not. Which does not make it a bad game yet. That's only because of the issues mentioned above.

Give me an options menue and a few lines of explanation and I will recommend this game. Oh, and maybe have a second song to choose from.

I don't really know where to start on this one. I was expecting a toned down version of the old school game Twisted Metal, but instead what I got was a mobile port of an endless runner, which uses a car instead of a character.

Your options are left and right to dodge, quick brake and attack; no accelerator or brakes, just keep moving.

The games main concept is to speed down a straight road dodging cars all while picking up coins, powerups and weapons; simple enough. As you get further down the road, you will continually pick up speed. The overall feel of the game was a port of an iOS or Android to PC which would have been better off as a Free To Play with micro transactions or a free web based flash game, not a full fledged PC release.

With all this being said, the game does have it's own simple quick game kinda charm, worth playing if you have some time kill. Without looking at the actual specs, I would imagine this game would run fine on most modern laptops with just integrated graphics and a modest amount of RAM, so might be a game for a long car ride or flight? I dunno.

If they added leaderboards and Steam achievements, this would be a really really great game. But for now, it's a really fun endless runner game where you are a car! The physics are just spot-on for a crazy-taxi-type arcade-ness, and the game itself functions well, there is stuff to unlock, etc.

However the downsides is in the amount of content. Sure you could unlock all those paint schemes for each car, but it feels grindy just thinking about it. I only played for like 30 minutes, but I felt like I saw it all. Literally there are a few different powerups to blast cars in front of you, and you basically see how long you can last. The pace didn't seem to get more intense either, but I could be wrong. I don't really see the 'hook' of why I should even keep playing.

So why the thumbs up?

Because this game is perfect for what it set out to achieve: an endless runner with a car. Now if the developers have a chance to add in some functionality to give it replay value and they could gave a gem on their hands.

Wishlist:
- Steam leaderboards
- Achievements
- Trading Cards
- Steam Cloud

Will check back on this title later, but for now look at that price tag! Day 1 release and it's like under $3 US. This price point makes the decision to recommend to someone infinitely easier, I would hope everyone have this title in their library, although seeing as how they set the price bar that low you will probably have spare copies from bundles in a few months time.
