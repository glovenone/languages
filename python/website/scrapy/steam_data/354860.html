About This Game

The Adventures of Tree takes you on an open ended adventure to save your fellow species from the evil invaders!
In this cute and charming hand drawn game, you will have to make choices that effect your ending.

Whether you're a fan of the hand drawn style or not, this game is a charming, cute and funny experience for everyone!
With over 30+ hours of gameplay, and huge replay-ability this is not a game you want to miss!
This game is open world, you can roam freely across the vast landscapes collecting epic treasures!
Keep yourself laughing as you read thru the quirky and comical dialogue.
Survive the harsh winter landscapes or relax in the sun on the beach!


Game Features:

-Customize your character with over 100 possibilities!
-Pick and choose from 40+ Quests to earn money!
-Explore 15 exotic locations all containing unique creatures and items!
-Choose your gear load out from over 50+ items!
-Battle over 50 different types of creatures!
-Defeat over 5 unique Bosses!
-Make choices that effect your game ending!

I'm not entirely sure what to make of this game. Maybe if it were actually as funny and clever as it seems to be trying to be, it would be good. Maybe I'm just not in the right mindset. Maybe I'm not far enough into it. Maybe I'll come back some day and say that ten hours into the game, I suddenly understood. But for now, I can't recommend this.

Anyway, yeah, this game. As others have mentioned, the load time is bewilderingly long. I didn't experience any gamebreaking bugs once it actually started, though, so that's a positive (?). The one notable bug was that every now and then the item I bought to light up caves would stop lighting up caves until I unequipped and reequipped it, which, ok, whatever. You play as a tree-looking thing named Tree (my chosen build was a Christmas tree with a top hat and cigar because there are definitely no problems with that) and wander around doing quests for townspeople and maybe doing something important eventually. You have both hunger and stamina along with health, meaning you have to walk around at a horribly slow rate most of the time and you have to find things to eat every now and then. When you level up, you can choose to make either of these things slightly less of a pain rather than upgrading your combat abilities. Eugh.

Anyway, you get a bunch of quests, and you can only have one visible onscreen at a time, and if you get a quest item for some other quest you have to manually look up whether it's actually something you need. To turn in a quest, you have to look in your quest list at the picture of the tree thing and town you got it from, and then wander around that town looking for a tree thing that looks like that. It's great.

If you want to attack enemies, you start out with a hatchet, which is great for swinging at the air in front of you and maybe if you're lucky hitting an enemy that's right at your feet, but if it's anywhere else, you're out of luck. You can buy weapons that are longer, which might fix that problem a bit, but I didn't stick around that long.

Overall, it feels like a game that jumped out of Early Access too soon and should still be in there until it figures itself out. It looks like it's still being updated, so maybe look back in a year or something?

Early Access Review
Posted: 1 May, 2015
This game is pretty good for an early access game and as it stands I would recommend it.

You play as a tree...called tree :). Who sets out on a journey across a beautiful hand drawn world to put an end to the Florps evil reign. Sounds pretty cool right?

The core of the game is simple. It is a side scrolling game with simple jump,fight and interaction mechanics.

It offers a limited customisation aspect with head,weapon and side arm gear. Although it does give you damage stats for different weapons (and there seems to be a few different weapons) so you don't have to stick with one weapon all the way trhough the game. Furthermore head gear also gives you extra stats such as long sprint or more regen from food etc.

It has a small aspect of survival with a food bar below your health bar. Keeping an eye on these two bars is essential and you need to make sure you have food on you at all times! Or you will suffer. You can even hire a fellow town member for 10 gold pieces to go out and scavenge food for you! So that is always a last resort!

You can pick up quests from fellow trees who will offer you rewards if you complete them. At the moment they seem pretty simple. Go out and grab a certain number of the item and bring them back for a reward. Im hoping eventually they may be qeusts to kill certain monsters etc that would be a nice addition if it isn't in the game already. You can even ally with different races to help you in the fight against the enemy!!

The game also does not tell you where to go, what to do or how to do it. So it is all up to you to figure it out. This could be annoying for some people but that satisfaction when you finally figure out how to accomplish something is a damn good feeling :). It took me ages to figure out at one point how to get to the next mountain area urghhh ha.

Hedghogs/spiky frogs are the hardest enemies on the game ha! Do not land on top of them or they will kill you within a few hits! A tree being killed by a hedgehog/frog haha amazing!!

As is stands it is playable and enjoyable but can be frustrating at times when you have no idea where to go! But hey thats the fun of the adventure right?? trying to figure out what to do! I would definitely recommend this if you like side scrolling games with a nice story, a beautiful hand drawn world, nice and simple fighting mechanics and trying to figure out where and what the hell to do!

I also want to add. The developers of this game are awesome! Very helpful, update the game when needed and all round are nice developers!!

Early Access Review
Posted: 2 January
First 1.5 Hours Impressions

So I played for an hour and a half and kept notes on what I was experiencing and things I liked and didn't like, I figured I'd share these notes with you (the developer) for some quick hints on how to patch up next, and how a new player like myself may experience your game (which hopefully gives you a good idea how to make future changes). This is mostly for the developer, but if you're thinking of purchasing this game go ahead and read through my notes. Warning: this will be length, but hopefully helpful.


Gameplay Notes
  • very professional-feeling intro
  • upgrading stats is cool, I like the design
  • i got three-shot by a porcupine 10 minutes into game, this better not be one of those games where the difficulty is ALL over the place. i was too tall to hit him
  • i really like how this looks like a much less serious and concrete game from the outside, but it does indeed have basically all the features most RPG's have
  • the Minecraft-styled Furnace is a neat touch
  • dialogue is pretty funny
  • my only complaint as of yet is the combat, it's very loose and sometimes (like the porcupine) downright unfair, how do i hit him? he's too damn short (edit: I think I figured it out, he's not killable, just jump over him...)
  • i love quests, don't get me wrong, i actually love the grindy ones where you just collect a few things and return, but i feel like i got bombarded with quests immediately, that may overwhelm some players
  • day-night cycle was unexpected and very cool
  • immedaitely noticed how the inventory is too small, especially with 10 different collect quests at a time
  • it'd be nice if the movement was a bit quicker, seeing as there's lots of backtracking to be done, or maybe if sprint regenerated faster; at this rate i'll be wasting Upgrade Points on speed (edit: upgraded map negates backtracking nicely)
  • some of the quests consist of just waiting and picking the twigs once they respawn, since I'm not strong enough to explore anywhere else
  • the fast travel options make the slow walking speed much more bearable
  • the stat page should pause the game, I'm fine with the map or inventory being open while the game is playing but the stat page should be safe
  • why don't the boars fight back?
  • should've been a sign pointing left from my house, I thought I could only go right (I may just be stupid, too). once you go left THEN this game gets like twice as good, now that there's a village and tons of merchants right next to my house
  • lots of content, tons of items, expected much less. still just wish the combat was better: maybe it would've worked better if it were turn-based instead
  • game progresses extremely slowly; might turn new players away
  • chests should have an "auto-deposit" type of feature, where you can just stack all the like items in your inventory onto the corresponding ones in the chest
  • NOTE TO NEW PLAYERS: the game starts extremely slow, once it gets going though it's a lot of fun and much more fleshed out than you may have originally thought!

Aesthetic Notes
  • awesome narrator
  • button clicks are annoying and too loud
  • axe sound is weird, especially considering i'm only hitting air. also too loud
  • so far, i actually really like the child-like art style
  • some interactable background objects are hard to distinguish from the rest of the background, I find myself spamming E as I walk around to make sure I didn't miss anything
  • the setting and mood of the game is heartwarming; it's lovely
  • while I adore the art-style, I feel like it causes the gameplay to be more clunky

Technical Nptes
  • no 1920x1080 support?
