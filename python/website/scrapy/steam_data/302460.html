A very decent little game. This is a plants vs. zombies like game (in the meaning that plants shoot at zombies, not like the PopCap great game).

You have three plants that shoot differently and need to reload after few shots and you need to survive waves of zombie attacks. Basically that's all that is to say about the philosophy of the game.

Pros:
- power ups - additional sacks of seed (read - extra lives), invulnerability (for some seconds) and air strike to wipe screen clean of zombies
- it's pretty pleasant to play
- variety of minigames - shoot at crates, collect coins, bomb zombies from above, etc.
- levels are greatly paced - this game is not boring
- colorful and funny enemies
- you can upgrade stuff by means of coins

Cons:
- plants look ugly - like I don't know, some grizzled good-for-nothings
- graphics are well, very basic as you can see on screenshots
- if you want to upgrade every plant that would mean a lot of coin-grinding
- reloading takes too long imo
- price - 7 Euros is a bit too many for such a basic game
- whole game can be finished in under three hours

Overall, I like this game. It's simple, it's funny, it's fun. If you like such games then go and grab it on some discount for about 2 Euros/USD.

UPDATE:
The game saves its state in the following path HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\AppMania\I Am Vegend (found it in the discussion section of the game), thus if you clean such folders regularly you may find your cash and upgrade state wiped clean. Surprisingly, you still can access levels that you have completed. Just have this in mind.

Updated Review:

"I am Vegend" is a poor attempt at merging the concepts of two widely viral games: Angry Birds + Plants vs. Zombies. Don't let the cartoon graphics fool you, this game was poorly designed and executed. It lacks even the most basic of functions such as the ability to adjust volume. You may either mute or unmute, that is all.

Gameplay remains unresponsive and laggy, especially as the game progresses and more units flood the screen. Ultimately, there is no strategy to gameplay. The launching of units does not require hard precision (as compared to Angry Birds) and the replenishment of units makes placing them simply a timing-response function. In other words, I found myself just clicking furiously rather than employing any real game tactics. I cannot recommend this game as it was not enjoyable to play.
