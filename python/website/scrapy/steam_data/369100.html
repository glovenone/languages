About This Game

DISCLAIMER

Have you had enough of pointless & boring "simulator" ripoffs? It's about time someone made a simulator that's actually a game you can enjoy. Prepare to restore you faith in the simulator games, GameIndica Entertainment brings you the next best simulator since the simulator genre was born.

INTRO

Predator Simulator is an action arcade RPG packed with endless gameplay and new missions for every game session. On a quest to become a true Lionborn, you get to save the planet by wiping out humanity from the face of the earth. Yeah.... didn't you always want to do that anyway? Now is your chance!

GAMEPLAY

Explore the wild countryside while enjoying fresh farm raised pigs, rooster & chicken wings. Reach the human populated city to start your manhunt and climb up the defcon levels. Eat anything that moves because that what a predator does to survive. Tasty and weak human prey will get you big rewards, but be careful, too much chaos will attract the hunters! You will be classified as a Predaterrorist and face heavy resistance. Be prepared for the worst. Unlock the hidden uncharted territory outside the game world by becoming a true Lionborn, where you can find secret journal entries from the game developer. We hope you will enjoy knowing more about the back story of this game and how it was made.

KEY FEATURES

+ Play as an apex predator AKA a lion.
+ Funniest part of the game is the Advanced AI system with real-time Predator-Prey simulation. Unarmed preys will try to run for their lives, armed hunters will try to hunt you down. Although getting them scared will make it easy to hunt them. Various scaring tactics can be used (ex. Attack from hidden corners or behind, jump scare them, roar at them, eat someone in front of them etc)
+ Kill various species depending on your own humanitarian or animal loving bias.
+ There are hipsters, rednecks, donut loving cops, cosplayers and even console peasants in the city.
+ Live tweets from your poor, whining victims
+ After hunting your prey, eat them and listen to the sweet bone crushing musical notes. It never gets old.
+ Defcon levels work like wanted levels in any open world game. The higher you get, the more bonuses you get but the game becomes more difficult.
+ Tons of characters to unlock as you play. Bigger rewards for hunting down higher level victims.
+ Powerful roar to scare everyone around you. Once you become a true Lion born by acquiring the glorious Fus-Roar-Duh skill, roars become lethal.
+ Xbox Controller support.

Predator Simulator is an 'action arcade RPG' game from GameIndica Entertainment. In PS you are a lion on a mission to become 'lionborn' and rid the Earth of humanity. As you kill more animals and people you'll get higher defcon levels which eventually leads soldiers to try and kill you.

+Decent graphics
+Simple controls (M&KB)
+Quite a few different 'enemies' to hunt
+Once you have got a few kills under your belt civilians will flee from you, sometimes even soldiers run away
+You can unlock upgrades with one of the currencies you collect

-It's made entirely from assets found in Unity Asset packs, take from that what you will
-Extreme lack of content, I'd seen everything that's in the game within 25 minutes
-The game has a twitter feed style interface which displays 'jokes', the jokes get repetitive almost instantly and I found a few of them somewhat offensive
-Despite what the dev says on the store page this is about as far from a simulator as you could possibly get, it's just another jokey simulator except it's not funny
-The screaming sound effect gets irritating very quickly
-Some things don't even soundeffects, such as gunshots
-Animations are awful and for some creatures, particularly chickens, the animations don't work
-It's riddled with graphic bugs and glitches
-It feels like a iOS game, especially with the two currency system
-One of the currencies is seemingly useless
-You lose health if you don't eat enough which is a good idea but the health bar goes down very quickly
-Not much in the way of environmental interaction
-Only one map which is not only incredibly small but as far as I'm aware it's an unedited map from a Unity asset pack
-Most importantly I did not have fun playing it

Verdict:
Don't buy it.

Predator Simulator is a clear attempt at making a game similar to Goat Simulator but sadly it falls flat in a ridiculous number of ways. Unless this gets some major content added and gets its issues fixed I simply cannot recommend this game.

Well, this is my first review, ever. Additionally, English is not my native language, so please bear with me ;)

Predator Simulator Review

You play a "Predator" and attack and eat animals and people with no real goal, except reaching a high score/"Defcon level".
To survive, you have to constantly kill and eat to fill your health. Later, you also have to avoid or fight hunters that want to put an end to your killings.

You can "roar" to stun (or later damage) anything around you, run, attack & eat. Very basic but enough though I'd love to be able to "jump" once in a while.
There are 2 "quests" active at any time which range from "Kill/Eat X of something" up to "Collect X stars" or "Survive X minutes". Those quests - when fulfilled - reward gems and gold. Gold can also be aquired by killing/eating pretty much everything. Gems are used to unlock upgrades and the gold is used to improve them, afterwards.
By killing and eating or collecting Stars, your "Defcon level" raises which means you are a higher threat. At some points, new people will walk around the town and stronger hunters will appear.

The game "ends" when your health bar is depleted though you can continue by investing some gems.

Pros:
  • Funny idea
  • Easy controls
  • Graphics are OK for a casual game

Cons:
  • The area you play in is ridiculously small - I mean: Come one! One forest, one small town... that's it?!
  • Graphics are glitchy like hell, I've been thrown 100m in the air, walked around with the prey in my mouth and more! This is actually very funny but I still have to list it as a "con"
  • At least one quest is seriously: Collecting X stars often doesn't count the stars.
  • Stars are extremely rare/hard to spot (some "map" would have been useful - call it "instinct" for "realism" and you're done ;))
  • The sounds are repetitive and extremely annyoing
  • The skills' costs are totally unbalanced, also, there is not much use for gems after you unlocked everything
  • Spawns are unbalanced, too. After some time, there are almost only hunters and "Kill 15 supporters" is almost impossible.


TL;DR:
I got this game for free and for that "price", it's OK.
Otherwise, I can only "recommend" to get this game if you like trashy games and if it's on sale and there's no way that I can recommend the current version of the game otherwise.


BTW: when maxing all abilities, you can read "AWESOM" - well, where's the "E"? ;)
