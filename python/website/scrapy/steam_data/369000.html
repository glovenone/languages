About This Game

Pongo is a 3D action platformer that combines pogo stick jumps with strategic FPS combat. Grab your Pongo, a tool usable as either a pogo stick or a gun, to obliterate enemies and traverse the vast islands of Flubber. Balance rapid movement and high jumps with challenging combat, hordes of enemies, puzzles and traps at every turn. Grab your Pongo and let the adventure begin!

Features

  • Leap through over 45 challenging levels
  • Obliterate monsters with your Pongo
  • Avoid deadly traps and slay huge bosses
  • Solve challenging puzzles and explore islands
  • Enjoy charming 3D environments
  • Jam to an exciting, quirky original soundtrack


System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: Core 2 Duo
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Onboard Graphics
    • Storage: 350 MB available space
    • Sound Card: Onboard Sound

Keep in mind that this is my opinion, everyone has the right to disagree. :)

Eh, Pongo is an okay game, but I wouldn't recommend it to others.

Pongo is basically a game that consists of 50 levels where simply put, "Get from point A to point B." Some of the levels can be challenging, others can be really easy, but most of all, they also have monsters you have to deal with. If you're planning to 100% this game, then completing the levels would take you a couple of hours at the most, depending on what pace you're taking.

Generally, most, if not all levels take place in the middle of an ocean, containing a lot of islands. You as a player has a pogostick and you use that to manuever through obstacles and get to the other islands in order to get to point B. You also can have another option to switch out your pogostick to a "gun" used to take out the monsters that get in your way.

But beware that you can die in this game. Since you're in the middle of the ocean, you can fall and drown in the water so you can expect to die like this when you failed a level. You can also die by the monsters, but you get multiple chances based on what difficulty setting you are in. If you die during a level, you go back to the beginning of the level and there are no checkpoints so you only have one shot.

Pongo also has 2 difficulties, easy and hard mode.
In easy mode, you get three lives which affects how many hits the monsters can hit you and you gain control in the air, which basically means you can still move around while in the air.
In hard mode, you only have one life so you can't let any of the monsters hit you and you no longer gain control in the air, which means that you can only move in one direction continuously while in the air. This is only for players who want a bit more of a challenge.
But it doesn't really matter what mode you take as you don't get any achievements regarding these modes anyway.

The level designs are really bland.
They don't seem very interesting and it feels the same every level you complete and they don't feel different whatsoever. The textures are plain and very boring to look at. Some of the levels get very repetitive and you will be bored playing this if you're not planning to get the achievements.

Although I like the music, most of them are overused.
I always hear this kind of music on Youtube, especially ones by Kevin Macleod. There should be more custom, creative music for this game to make it more unique, rather than putting in royalty-free music since to me they don't really make sense when putting it in a game like this.

Oh and I forgot to mention that there are badges and trading cards, so you can "play" this game for the trading cards and you don't have to play it again.

Should you buy this game?
If you're looking for a cheap game, wanting to craft badges from the trading cards, or just wanting to play the game for more easy achievements, then yes. Otherwise, don't get this game.

4/10

Placed on a pogo stick at the start of each level the only goal is to reach the end. Progress through the game and you will be confronted with new enemies, bosses and traps along with basic puzzle elements. Most were quite simple and more a matter of finding the item required to pass that area. Power-ups included rapid-fire for your weapon and boosts to pogo speed and jump heights.

Pongo is lighthearted and enjoyable enough but it's tough to recommend at full price. Enemies were colourful and quirky though levels seemed a little repetitive and didn't change colour schemes at all. Sometimes islands with power-ups were placed so far away from the objective the 10sec timer would expire before you could actually use them which seemed a bit pointless. Controls felt like they could be more responsive, often I found my self drinking ocean water instead of launching into the heavens but probably more issues with my throwaway keyboard to blame. Gamepad support unfortunately wasn't included at all.

Sounds all bad but I'll upvote the game as there is still fun to be had in the lovelyplanet-esque world. Just feels expensive for what's currently offered and personally didn't feel the urge to continue onward, some collectible items may have helped as a side objective. Would recommend waiting out a price drop or further tweaks to the game. Some ideas and a gameplay video are below.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QK3eMaVtn_4
@Devs
New colour schemes for later levels. Music should loop less and be reactive to what is on-screen. Tighten up the controls along with adding pad support. Leaderboards given the game is designed for speedrunning and it might be worth seeing if level editor + workshop support is viable. Achievements+trading cards never hurts either. Cheers :)

I really wanted to like this game. The trailer reminded me of Lovely Planet, which I really enjoyed. There are a few issues with this game that push it into the 'not recommended' category for me.

The pogo mechanic is interesting, but it's clunky to charge up the jumps and time it just right when you're right at the edge of a platform. This isn't a huge deal in most levels because you have enough space to make it, but in a few levels (like 1-22) there is very little room for error as far as having max charge and jumping at the very edge of the platform.

Moving platforms are horrible. You lose momentum when jumping and it's very hard to time it correctly. Also, there is no visible way to tell where a platform is going to change direction, so with faster moving platforms, you end up in the wrong place or it throws you off the edge.

Level design is boring with long sections of running straight with larger islands connected by the aforementioned moving platforms. Enemies are no threat whatsoever and you can run past all of them, but the few times you are forced to fight, you quickly find out that combat is also terrible because you can't strafe fast enough to outrun things.

This game has a lot of potential and I'd love to see some thoughtfully designed levels in the future. I had a lot of fun with the speed power-up and I'd love to see some maps designed around it. I would also love it if you removed the running button and just made that the default move speed. Having the sprint key toggle off on you because you slowed down too much makes platforming annoying and unpredictable and having to hold shift to avoid that makes my hand tired.

If some of the issues are fixed, I would be happy to change this to recommended, but as is, I can't suggest that anyone spend $8 for this.
