About This Game

The destiny of a galaxy hangs in the balance, and you are in command!

Lead the great armies of the Star Wars™ Episode II: Attack of the Clones saga in intense real-time strategy clashes. Enter the fray as the Galactic Empire, Rebel Alliance, Wookies, Trade Federation, Gungans or Royal Naboo to determine the course of the Galactic Civil War.

Expand the battlefield with the Clone Campaigns addon. Choose to play as the breakaway Confederacy of Independent Systems or the Galactic Republic in 14 missions based on characters, vehicles and locations from Star Wars Episode II: Attack of the Clones.

  • Execute campaigns over land, sea, and air in single-player campaigns, skirmishes, and multiplayer battles.
  • Combat arenas extend from interstellar asteroids to epic ground battles to aquatic realms.
  • Deploy vast armies up to 200 units per side - that can include Bounty Hunters, Jedi, Droids™, Wookiee Kat tanks and much more.
  • Manage resources and upgradable technology into your strategy, such as Wookie ingenuity, advanced Gungan biotechnology and Jedi stamina.
  • Create custom single or multiplayer campaigns featuring virtually any Star Wars units and settings with the Scenario Editor.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP/Vista/7/8
    • Processor: 1.8 GHz Processor
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 3D graphics card compatible with DirectX 7 (compatible with DirectX 9 recommended)
    • DirectX: Version 7.0
    • Storage: 886 MB available space

With a recent move of mine, I sadly lost one of my Cd copies of this game along with another copy that shattered in my hands after smashing it with some heavy books. When I heard this was on steam, my heart was rekindled with joy knowing this was finally back in my hands. Of course, I'm a fan of this game, its Star Wars for crying out loud. Yeah, its a copy of AoE with a few tweaks here and there, but its better than that, its Star Wars. Slap Star Wars on anything and I'm interested (figuratively of course). So, why should YOU buy this after all? Well let me give you a few reasons why I love this game and why you should play it.

The game play. OF course you could simply say, "Its a dumber version of SC2 which is the complicated version of Warcraft 1-3" but you can't. The game itself has its own uniqueness that is shown throughout the game. That unique aspect to it is of course, strategic plays over a large battleground. With a cap of 250 units per player, the ways you can annihilate each other is limitless. Which, who wouldn't want to annihilate his fellow enemy with 50 star destroyers (of course, he had to enable cheats before the game started).

Scenario Editor. Its confusing yes, but once you understand how the editor works, magical things happen. I've seen players create maps that aren't even possible in my eyes, yet the maps exist. A few classic maps such as Trooper fest, Tower defense, RPG maps, and even fortress defense (3v3 style) have been made in this editor. There is no limit of what you can create in this game, but of course its up to you to create these wonderful maps made in the Scenario Editor.

The Lore. Typically a game has alot of lore so, like any other game this game has lore. Of course, it can't contain the entire lore of Star Wars, but it does cover quite a few moments in the Star Wars timeline. Now if you haven't played this game ever, the timeline is between Star wars I-VI according to the campaigns that you can play. The lore is simple enough that even a nerf could understand it in spanish. And if you don't seem to enjoy the lore, you can hop into standard solo mode to battle computers or even player made computers if you get fancy with it.

Multiplayer. Sadly, the official multiplayer servers shut down, however Gameranger and many other sites have revived the multiplayer. Mutliplayer works beautifully with programs like gameranger that gives you simple ways to hop into a lobby and battle it out to the death. The official servers might be dead, but its up to you guys to revive it with other ways to play multiplayer.

I've covered everything you might think, but of course with all games there is atleast one issue. That is graphics. The game isn't the best in graphics wise, but its appealing. To today's graphics design the game is junk. But, like all classic games we must consider this as ancient as the original Metroid and not judge it by graphics design. So, with all that in mind, I do very recommend this game to anyone wanting to either player a classic strategic game or begin their journey in the Star Wars franchise.

AoE2's gameplay is fantastic, but I understand if some feel that Galactic Battlegrounds is not the most original RTS game.

The game has 8 single player campains, one for all but one faction, custom games and a map editor.

While it was a feature at the time of release (2001), the multiplayer servers no longer exist.

It also (after they patched it) runs well on Win10, although the resolution options are near non-existent and there is this weird thing where the open cinematics play in a seperate window before the main menu loads.
