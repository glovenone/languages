Shakes & Fidget is a fantasy RPG idle game. Welcome to the caricatural world of heroes and buffoons where your search for glory will have you fight monstrous bats, evil bunnies and moronic bandits while undertaking the most ridiculous of quests.

Pros: The game is a Steam port of the decade-old browser game that still runs today. Everything about this game, from character creation to weapon and armor design, parodies the tropes of fantasy RPGs, while each quests are randomly composed of the most stereotypical stories that mock the genre. In no way does the game takes itself seriously, and every line of dialog reeks of satire. As an idle game, it is perfect to run in the background while working on something else or playing another game, you just take a peek every once in a while to see if your ready to undertake another quest, fight another player, or explore another level of a dungeon. If you bother to involve yourself in the community, guild battles and competition can be fun to participate in. Should you play long enough, further content will unlock, such as fortress building and raising your companion animal. On top of that, microtransactions are completely optional, as it is possible to get plenty of mushrooms (secondary currency) during bonus weekends or from guild boosts.

Cons: As an idle game, there is absolutely no gameplay whatsoever. You simply you pick a quest, and wait for the timer to end to see if your stats allowed you to win battle against whatever opponent you were facing. All quests and fights are resolved the same way: your character strikes (random result, based on your stats), the opponent strikes back, auto-repeat until someone reaches 0 hit points. Clicking and waiting is pretty much all you do. Fight a player? Wait for the timer to tell you you can fight another one. Select a quest? Wait for the timer to tell you it's finished. Expand your fortress? Wait for the timer to tell you you can expand it again. Unless you're easily amused, you'll soon stop reading the humoristic text, at which point you might start finding the game boring.

Verdict: âââââ - It's up to you

Shakes & Fidget is nothing more than a compulsive behavior enabler. However, it's free, so give it a try and play until you're tired of playing. It might take 30 minutes, it might take a hundred hour, but seeing you're likely to minimize the game and do something else while waiting for your next action, even time isn't something you'd really be losing much of.
