About This Game

'Pay2Win: The Tricks Exposed' is a Pay2Win business model simulator(adventure) that exposes the tricks online game businessmen use to maximize monetization. This game also gives you a chance to think about the value of paying money to Pay2Win games.
In this game, you become a warrior in a fantasy world, experience the Pay2Win business model and uncover the hidden secrets behind the systems.

Before you play this game:

-The Pay2Win model is not necessarily evil. But you must know on what you spent your money. Now you can, with this game.


-I recommend this game to those who have read Thomas Baekdal's 'How In-app Purchases Have Destroyed The Industry' and were excited by it.

-In this game, you will have to purchase countless transactions just to play. But worry not. The cash in this game is free and virtual, and is provided limitlessly. (NO ADDITIONAL CHARGE!!)

-However, the accumulated cash amount you've spent will be shown in the game screen, and this will bother you a bit.

-This game cannot represent all the pay2win tricks that exist in the world. Even now, new tricks are being conjured.

-This game simulates an MMORPG, but only supports single play mode. NPCs who pose as other players appear in this game.

-The 'Avatar' you meet in the game is an extension of the game's creator. Talk to him. He is dead serious.

In fact, I think it's the opposite: the devs always make have the player the false impression that she/he is who controls the situation.
Until I left the game, I had some objections:

-Please, include an in-game Settings menu. The music was driving me crazy.
-There is just one button to add funds and gives only $0.10 per click. I suppose the dev was trying to remark that each time you click, you are giving the 'game' money, but 10 cents is not an amout enough to be concerned of. Even 10 clicks for 1 buck is still not enough to be lectured. The average cheapest item in a MMORPG costs about $3, maybe a $3 or $3 button would be more appropiate. In the end, it turns an Add Funds Simulator Clicker Edition.
-In real MMORPG's, there isn't almost any item you can't get as a drop or a regular/daily quest or events. The chance drop percentage is pretty low, but you can still get it without paying for it. Purchasing it from the store is simply quickier. In this game, there is no way of getting those items outside the Premium Shop, and even after purchasing them, the items can fail.

Remember a few years ago, back when World of Warcraft was at its player-base peak of about 12 million players? Back then there would be new MMORPGs that came out fairly regularly, nearly a new one every month. Each of them claimed to be the "WoW Killer", that they were going to force players off of WoW servers by enticing them to play something else. None of those games ever succeeded.

*This* game does. This $4.99 near joke of a game, designed purely to make a point about anti-fun... *this* is the WoW Killer. After playing this for less than two hours, I doubt I'll ever log onto WoW again. There are too many things in the world that generate true fun, so I'm not going to dump my time, energy, and MONEY into something that doesn't deserve any of those things. This is a lesson I had already learned, but this game served to remind me.

Also, for a game that was designed to make people hate it, this was surprisingly well put together. It managed to avoid all the terrible glitches that plague many of the low budget crap games on Steam. I get the feeling that if this dev wanted to make a *fun* game, then it would be pretty great.

Final verdict: I got this in a bundle, so it was totally worth the 58 cents, if for no other reason than to remind me not to play MMORPGs. If you have a friend or family member who is addicted to an MMORPG, then this might be able to break them of it... maybe.
