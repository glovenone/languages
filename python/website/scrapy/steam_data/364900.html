About This Game

Red Bit Ninja is an old school platformer with a rope climbing physic and difficulty of the good old times.
It features classic pixel art, 60 hair pulling levels and five mean bosses.

Features:
- Pixel art style
- 5 bosses
- Engaging ninja music
- XBOX 360 Controller Support (preferable play)
- Save Level Major issue fixed.
- Full screen issue fixed.
- Tutorial integrated
- Levels rebuilt from scratch, difficulty balanced better.
- BOSSES rebalance
- New enemies
- New Traps
- Level goals improvement, portal opens with 2/3 of coins.
- Ability to replay levels once game is finished to improve score.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: XP
    • Processor: 1Ghz
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 256 MB available space
    • Sound Card: Yes

TL:DR, it's going in my uninstalled forever category

first off, it is locked to about 640x480 full screen. you can go into the options menu and change it to windowed, but that requires a restart. so i did that. open it and i can barely see the game on my 2560x1440 monitor. click on how to play in the menu, you'd think it's a tutorial. all it says in this mini level is collect the coins. you have to press buttons until you figure out what does what. (space to jump, space again to shoot grappling hook, arrows for movement) then it opens a portal, again figure out how to go through it, because the game doesn't say.

oh and i forgot to mention, it went full screen again as soon as you play, so windowed mode only means for the menu i guess?

so i go to play the first level. fairly basic platforming style level. i died abut 6 times on level 1. hidden surprise traps, and the sprite for your character and the sprites for the traps are actually larger than the pixels of those things. so you have to have quite a buffer away from the dangers or you'll instantly die, restart level. after restarting level 1 6 times, i got through it. level 2. i died...i don't know how many times, until I gave up.

it's not that the levels are designed in such a way as to be entirely unfair, except sometimes it's clearly made to be particularly difficult, but the controls are so wonky. and combine that with the buffer zone i mentioned, makes it more difficult than it needs to be.

the artwork is mediocre. not horrible, i've seen worse. same can be said for the music. meh.

about the only thing I had fun with was getting a good circular spin going with the grappling hook. you can really get going fast!

if I were to rate all the games i've played (I have over 550 and played around 25% of them to some degree), this one is...at least better than GearCrank Arena. But I don't think ive played one worse than this otherwise.

EDIT: I played some more and realized that you can go up and down on the grappling hook, so glad i didn't learn that in the non-tutorial. ♥♥♥♥ you level 4. I ended up giving up and realizing my time was worth more than this game on level 6. Saw other reviews say that if you die on the first boss you go all the way back to 1-1, so again, ♥♥♥♥ the devs on that. stupid ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥t.

Red Bit Ninja: OK I have to say I like the look of this game I really do. And I'd like to be fair on my thoughts about the game.

The problem for me is I don't possess the naturally ability to play platformers. I lack the required dexterity, patience and sometimes interest to keep at it.
The game itself is fairly simple - walk, jump and a double jump activates a ninja 'hook' which you MUST use to pass obstacles, traps and obtain coins.
I think I scored this game in a bundle, as I don't think I would have willingly purchased it - not because it's bad, because I suck at platformers.

So the positives for me are cards and achievements (but I can't seem to get past the first level!), so the achievements are largely pointless - maybe one of my kids can have a go on my behalf :)

Look, if you like platformers buy it on special - otherwise stay away.

7/10 (if you ignore my sucking at platforming)

Alright, here's my comprehensive guide on how to beat this game:

Step 1: Select the "How to play" button.

Step 1a: This is the first trap, as you actually have to toggle Windowed Mode first. It won't actually change to windowed mode until you restart, but it'll make the game actually play. Otherwise, selecting the "How to play" button will freeze the game.

Step 1b: also, have both your keyboard and mouse prepared, as the menus use different conrols.

Step 2: Beat the "How to play" level. This requires a bit of guesswork, as this level does not actually tell you the controls.

Step 3: Start the game. Remember, your goal is to collect the coins.

Step 4: Die a pathethic death, because the controls are janky as all hell.

Step 5: Uninstall the game.

CONGRADULATIONS, you have now gotten the good ending: quitting the game before you waste any more time trying to play it properly.
