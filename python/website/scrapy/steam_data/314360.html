About This Game



Aritana is a platform game which the main aspect of its mechanics is the possibility of changing your stance. This stances define attributes such Speed, Jump Force, Attacks and Special Abilities. The player must choose between these stances to overcome obstacles and fight the Spirits of the Forest in a quick, intuitive and fun gameplay mechanic.

Aritana will learn new moves and abilities throughout the game and combo movements will help him fight the forest spirits. Meanwhile, Mapinguari will chase him constantly, forcing battles that require concentration and mastery of all skills learned.

With the help of the Shaman's Staff and the benefits of each stance, Aritana will be able to search for the Lost Artifacts and gain access to the Spirit World. This place is a labyrinth that holds pieces of medicinal leaves which, when collected in large quantity, can increase the player's maximum health.

The game follows the old platformer style and brings a high difficulty for those who are careless. Mastering different attacks and avoiding enviroment traps are key elements for the game.



A tribe of brave warriors lies deep inside the Forest. Tabata, the Chieftain, is terribly sick and Raoni, the Wiseman, is taking care of him. A powerful Forest Spirit took over Tabata’s body and only an ancient ritual can undo the spell. Unfortunately, the most important ingredient is missing: a feather of a rare harpy named Uiruuetê. Aritana, against Raoni's decision, embarks in this dangerous mission. The sacred feather can only be found at the top of the highest mountain, through the lands of the man-eater Mapinguari.



  • Uncover the mysteries of a story inspired by the Brazilian Indigenous culture.
  • Upgrade the Shaman's Staff with new Attacks and Special Skills.
  • Create Combos to help overcome the most difficult obstacles.
  • Face the Mapinguari in several epic encounters that will test all your skills.
  • Run through different scenarios such as tropical forests, undeground caves and the Uiruuetê's Mountain.
  • Look for Lost Artifacts to access the labyrinths in the Spirit's World and increase your maximum health.
  • Master a new style of fast paced gameplay inspired by classic games.
  • Play with the keyboard or with your Xbox 360 Controller.


***Only Keyboard or Xbox 360 Controller supported.***

Short Verdict: Aritana is a platformer with some puzzle elements about a Brazilian indian who's looking for the harpy's feather so the shaman can ban the spirit that possessed the tribe chief. The main aspect in its mechanics, apart from being a platformer that reminded me of the good ol' Donkey Kong games, is that you are always changing between Attack and Speed stances. The game is fresh, beautiful, and makes me really glad to know that there is at least one great indie game company in Brazil that can achieve this high quality. Kudos to DUAIK!

Pros:
  • Very unique, heart-warming theme
  • The two stances are really well implemented and makes the game really fresh
  • It's not a brainless platformer--you have to think about what you're doing and just rushing will probably get you killed more often
  • They've really hit the spot on difficulty. It's not an easy game and you'll die a lot, but it's not one of those "let's see how to make this even more cruel" games. You'll die quite a few times and you'll learn as you try. Soon enough, you'll overcome the challenge by yourself and feel a great sense of accomplishment.
  • Collectibles in each level (5 amulets called Muiraquitãs and 1 new combo) that add to the exploring experience
  • Total of 9 well thought-out levels, plus 3 smart boss fights, which is content enough for an indie game (but Tupã knows I longed for more)
  • Controls are customizable, although I think the idea of attacking with the right analog stick fits perfectly in the game
  • The cartoonish graphics are really beautiful
  • BGM and SFX are also very nice

Cons:
  • Combos seemed like a hidden feature to me. After you got them, there's no way to know which ones you've collected so far until after you beat the game. So if you forgot the sequence and moved on to the next level, there's no way of knowing. Also, the second and third combos I've unlocked (and I think there are other examples of this) could only be used after I got more basic attacks. Until then, I had already forgotten what the combo sequence was.
  • I would've really appreciate to be able to replay levels before I beat the game, in case I missed something. That level selection screen should've been available since the beginning, actually, and it would also have solved the not knowing the combo sequence problem.
  • To sum it all up, the start menu itself could have info on unlocked moves, combos, stage completion (if combo and amulets were found)

Bought on: Steam Auction for 1470 gems. Paying that much was a bargain! It's currently 33% OFF on Steam and I think this might be a good chance. Download the Demo if you're in doubt.

Aritana is a great little indie platformer. The visuals are fun and colourful, but do not let that deceive you cause the gameplay is pretty challenging (in a good way). I'd classify Aritana as a precision platformer with some action elements.

The visuals as you can see from the screenshots above are generally cheery (some levels are darker, like the ones in caves). The soundtrack is the same - cheery, happy-go-lucky music. The setting in the Amazon rainforest is a nice touch.

I wasn't too fond of the default controls, since movement is tied to WASD, but the good thing is that you can fully customize the controls. So I customized it to the way I like it - movement to the arrow keys, and use the left side for the action keys. As for the physics of the game, it feels good. The character is not floaty, and he doesn't feel heavy either.

The gameplay revolves around using your powers. You can switch the state you are in, and then activate a short burst of extra energy as well. The challenge comes from executing moves precisely to continue moving in a level. It gets pretty challenging, suffice to say, and for completionists, I am sure the challenge will be even greater.

It can get frustrating at times, but the checkpoint system here at least saves the day. You can lose 3 "leaves" (more earned later) and restart where you were, after which you go back to the last checkpoint. If you lose all your lives (1 live = 3 leaves, earn more as you play), you kinda get a game over screen. Shocked me the first time really, but actually you can go back and continue playing from the beginning of the current level.

Performance wise, it was decent, except for the parts where there was rain. Noticed a huge hit to my framerate there. I've played it for around 2.5 hours and am nearly halfway completed, so I assume there should be 5 hours of game time here (more if you want to 100% it of course).

Overall, I'd say Aritana is a pretty fun-♥♥♥-challenging platformer (and I never get enough of those!). It's not particularly revolutionary, but it's well-made. Recommended to hardcore platformer fans especially!

PS: I got a key from the dev to test the game, so yeah, just wanna put it out there.

PSS: Steam, you're really censoring that? Come on! (No, it's not the A-word)

Aritana is a great little indie platformer. The visuals are fun and colourful, but do not let that deceive you cause the gameplay is pretty challenging (in a good way). I'd classify Aritana as a precision platformer with some action elements.

The visuals as you can see from the screenshots above are generally cheery (some levels are darker, like the ones in caves). The soundtrack is the same - cheery, happy-go-lucky music. The setting in the Amazon rainforest is a nice touch.

I wasn't too fond of the default controls, since movement is tied to WASD, but the good thing is that you can fully customize the controls. So I customized it to the way I like it - movement to the arrow keys, and use the left side for the action keys. As for the physics of the game, it feels good. The character is not floaty, and he doesn't feel heavy either.

The gameplay revolves around using your powers. You can switch the state you are in, and then activate a short burst of extra energy as well. The challenge comes from executing moves precisely to continue moving in a level. It gets pretty challenging, suffice to say, and for completionists, I am sure the challenge will be even greater.

It can get frustrating at times, but the checkpoint system here at least saves the day. You can lose 3 "leaves" (more earned later) and restart where you were, after which you go back to the last checkpoint. If you lose all your lives (1 live = 3 leaves, earn more as you play), you kinda get a game over screen. Shocked me the first time really, but actually you can go back and continue playing from the beginning of the current level.

Performance wise, it was decent, except for the parts where there was rain. Noticed a huge hit to my framerate there. I've played it for around 2.5 hours and am nearly halfway completed, so I assume there should be 5 hours of game time here (more if you want to 100% it of course).

Overall, I'd say Aritana is a pretty fun-♥♥♥-challenging platformer (and I never get enough of those!). It's not particularly revolutionary, but it's well-made. Recommended to hardcore platformer fans especially!

PS: I got a key from the dev to test the game, so yeah, just wanna put it out there.
