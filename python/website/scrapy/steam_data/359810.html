About This Game

Fancy Point & Click cartoon adventure with puzzles and mini-games of varying difficulty. Game combines Hidden Objects and Room Escape mechanics among with traditional puzzlers like chess game and Hanoi tower.
Action in this chapter takes place in magician's chambers, filled with puzzles, riddles, clues and hidden objects where you have to find and a way to release the Hare from captivity.

Every night evil magician puts the Hare to the hat. It's a prison! Would you save the poor creature?

Hare in the Hat. Objective release the hare from his cage freeing him from the magician.

What a tough little puzzle game this is. I truly enjoyed the challenge. Everything occurs in a single room much like Please, Don't Touch Anything occurs on a single console. A little point and click and a lot of puzzles: picture puzzles, sliders, and logic. Excellent game.

Warning: Skipping a puzzle results in permenantly locked achievements and I will not get into the debate over the developers' personal opinion about the fact they consider skipping a puzzle cheating. As of April 29, 2016 with the purchase of DLC Hare in the Hat: The Abyss this permenantly locked condition is released. Reset game and try again for the achievements.

The slider picture puzzle is Hell. But I figured it out eventually (after several hours). I earned the SHUFFLE achievement. HINT: Use the center top or center bottom of the puzzle, not the sides or the middle to complete. I used the center top, and two 'diamonds'. It was the only place I could find to reverse those last two tiles.

On April 29 2016 the DLC Hare in the Hat: The Abyss was added. Objective: Free the hare from the confines of his underwater containment.

Not as hard as the first game and focuses solely on the mini-games and puzzles. Still a challenge though. Puzzles include sliders, matching, brain teasers and basic logic.

Puzzle from HELL this time is called Hungarian Rings. Basically put the colors in the correct place in two interlocking movable rings. I skipped it after an hour and the beginnings of migraine. Did not lock the achievements for the other puzzles but no achievement for that puzzle since I skipped. I can live with that.

Recommend this add-on. It is just as fun and challenging as the first game.

EDIT: now that the DLC is released, you can go ahead and skip any puzzle: only the achievement for it will need a second playthrough. In other words, you're no more banned. But my opinion about how it was handled stays.

Hare in the Hat is a short yet frustrating puzzle game where you have to free the hare owned by a magician. For that, you'll have to solve puzzles, find clues and pray that you'll not throw your computer out of the window.

Why am I insisting? In reality, the puzzles aren't hard to figure. You can go by trial and errors without problems. But there is one puzzle, with swapping pieces, where you'll end up with only two pieces to switch and not managing to do so. I suppose that I uninstalled at that point the first time I've played it. Don't try to find a walkthrough, the puzzle is random.

However, the developer added achievements and trading cards, prompting me to come back and beat finally the game. The famous puzzle was a pain in the ♥♥♥ but I was very very happy when I managed to push through it without skipping. Because skipping a puzzle here is not at all recommended unless you have unlocked all the achievements safe for the skipping one.

The developer is perhaps doing what he wants with his game. But punishing players that are skipping puzzles because he's under the impression that they're cheating, with a puzzle known to give white hair to everyone (the swap one), where you just want to skip after 30 minutes - 1 hour passed on it, after frustration and that you just want to come back after unlocking the rest... I don't agree with that. Why am I saying that? Skip a level and you will never be able to unlock the rest of achievements unless you buy the future DLC/the DLC is out, I still didn't understand that. I'm quite scared now to skip a level to reach 100% achievements.

Anyway, it's a dangerous point of view to consider that people skipping are cheaters. I've skipped puzzles before after spending time on them. I don't fall under the category of people skipping everything. It's probably the biggest negative point for the game.

Another problem is the flickering of the screen that happened a lot, especially when in Steam overlay.

Otherwise, the graphics are quite colorful and amusing, the soundtrack is good though the main sound is kinda annoying (I speak about the clicking) - I suppose that's why I disabled the audio the first time.

For the price, you can go ahead if you love puzzles. I will not put a thumb down because I disagree with the developer's point of view for an achievement as the rest of the game is good. But be warned anyway.

Hare in the Hat is a very short collection of standard puzzles with no instructions or story, and difficulty ranging from non-existent to nearly impossible. But at least it is really cheap.

Long version:
Hare in the Hat is your typical few-dollar tablet game. No story, no voices, no instructions, just one scene which gives you access to a bunch of puzzles.
The minigames themselves are standard everyday brain twisters. Some are childishly easy. Others can permanently get you stuck. And none of them can be skipped.

If you don’t mind spending a little money on some puzzles, get it, but you won’t miss anything by skipping this one.

Hare In The Hat is a short point and click adventure in which your objective is to free a bunny by solving a series of puzzles. The game consists in only one location that contains different clickable objects that trigger the mini-games. There is no story attached to it and no cut scenes. And even though the game has an ending, the fact that it is comprised of only one scene suggests that this was designed more like a demo or as a first chapter of a longer game.

The puzzles’ difficulty vary from the easy jigsaw puzzle to some really challenging ones, for example a chess variation or rotating 4 pieces puzzles. Despite the fact that some of them have a brief indication about what you're expected to do, there is no other hint option or skip button. On the other hand, having hints and skip buttons would considerably decrease the gameplay hours since 90% of the time is spent on trying to solve those difficult puzzles. I personally got stuck a couple of times and I gave up for the moment, but then I went back to the game and eventually completed it. There are no available walkthroughs at the moment online, so you can’t rely on them to save you.

I didn’t encounter any bugs while playing the game, although a lot of people are complaining about crashes.

If your idea of fun is solving challenging puzzles, then this game is definitely for you and you should buy it, especially while it is 40% discounted. But if you’d rather play it with a guide nearby, then this game might end up being quite consuming or frustrating for you.

NOT A GOOD GAME FOR ACHIEVEMENT HUNTERS (but still possible to get 100%).

It's a very short game with about 15 puzzles (finished in ~90min). There's no indication of where to start or what to do next but that don't really matter since everything happens in one room. Just click around and see what happens.
Some puzzles are very easy and some are hard but not impossible to do. Some of the clues (click on a question mark) don't really make sense and despite knowing what to do you can still get stuck. Again, no puzzle is impossible.

This is not a good game for achievement hunters. There are currently 16 normal achievement (you get most of them by finishing puzzles) and 1 hidden achievement. The hidden one is for "cheaters", you can get it by skipping a puzzle but (and here's the bad thing) it will lock out your ability to get 100% if you haven't already gotten the other 16. Your ability to gain achievements again will be unlocked by buying the DLC, whenever that comes out. Yeah, no. Bad. Skipping a puzzle because it's too hard or you're stuck is not cheating, it's skipping a hard puzzle. The "developer" added it just to punish achievement hunters. Cheating is using a program to unlock achievements without really playing the game. See the difference? The "developer" don't.
EDIT: Supposedly you won't get locked out of achievement hunting ATM if you get the "cheater" achievement before the DLC arrives but I'll wait to get it until all future achievements are active/collected.

Unfortunately, I'm an achievement hunter. I got this game on sale today because it was cheap, I was in the mood for puzzles and new achievements. I haven't done the "cheater" achievement yet (EDIT 2: I have now), luckily I read about it in the forum (after getting the game, though :P ). The game is cheap, I got it on sale and hopefully the DLC will be cheap too but without any future "punishments" from the "developer" because I like 100% achievements in my games. I won't refund though, because it's not actually a bad game (as in filled with bugs, stuttering, doesn't really work etc). If you like puzzles and don't really care about achievements (or a story) then this is a good game to spend an hour on.

I give this a thumb down mostly because of the "cheater" achievement but also because of the shortness of the game, the lack of story and lack of proper clues. The puzzles themselves were, for the most part, good but not enough to give this a thumb up.
