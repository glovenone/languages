Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We are deciding to go Early Access so we can improve the game and get ideas before releasing the final version. The game has come a long way, but we need the community for a final push of ideas and testing.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“4 months”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We plan to add new modes after fixing bugs and getting player feedback.

In addition to normal story, we are adding Endless and Survival gameplay modes.

In Endless mode, you play Mines of Mars as a fast paced endless platformer with weapon, fuel, and equipment pickups and monster barrages.

Survival mode pits you against the creatures of the night. Marshall your resources during the days as each time the sun sets prepare to hide, fight, or die.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“An early beta version - has most of the features and storyline working.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We have not decided on pricing of the final version.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are looking for player feedback on everything from bugs to new game ideas.
We will be using the community to look at daily feedback on the game while its in Early Access to address issues as well as listening to suggestions.”

About This Game

Some suspected that Mars once harbored life.
There was a reason that it wasn't ever found.

Mines of Mars is a procedural atmospheric mining game that is inspired by games like Metroid and Motherload.
It is also a story based adventure game with dark secrets, great weapons, tons of different items, and a gorgeous world to explore.
The world of MoM is procedurally created, so that every time you play it, it will be a little different.
There are tons of different enemies, dark corridors, and a large variety of fauna and ambient creatures.

-Huge Procedurally generated underground world
-Night and Day system
-Dark secrets
-Atmospheric vibes
-3 Mini Games
-Original Ambient Soundtrack by Evan Gipson
-Lots of flora and fauna
-Tons of Weapons and Items
-Great storyline
-Gravity Mining!
-Huge Bosses

Early Access Review
Posted: 22 February
A boring, tedious, poorly controlled copy of Spelunky. Almost the entire game consists of either waiting for a block to break (due to excessive number of hits required) or just running back and forth to the surface (there is no quick travel and your backpack fills up FAST).

PROS:
-Basics are all there (upgrades, mining, "caves" with loots, etc)
-Underground area seems to be pretty big (I went down to 1200 ft but nothing really happened)
-The voiceover sounds great.

CONS:
-NO MAP. Seriously?
-Extremely repetitive having to backtrack to the portal to get to the surface, especially considering how tiny your backpack is. You will be running back and forth *ALOT* (probably 1/3 to 1/2 of the gameplay is just this).
-Very slow mining. It takes 14+ swings to destroy an iron ore patch (lowest ore) with the 2nd level pickaxe. FOURTEEN swings per block !!
-Controls are awful (especially combat).
-Character will fly sometimes when you jump but not other times (not a major issue as fuel is prevalent).
-Plot makes no sense. Characters will mention things you've never heard of.
-Nothing actually happens? You just grind ores to upgrade your gear and go further into the mines...thats it? There didn't seem to be any new mechanics introduced, there was basically 1 enemy type (its size just changes), and the tiny bit of plot there was made no sense...

I think what im touching on is the pacing of the game is too slow and needs to be upped some.

While this might be a good game for some im getting kind of bored of it already. So i cant in good conscience recommend it at this time.
