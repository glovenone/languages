About This Game

Puzzle Box



Puzzle Box is painting with creative gameplay! Create fantastic pixel art images, by dropping your colour paint blocks in the correct place across the levels.

Badges :

Create colourful bold images in the classic colour-by-numbers mode. Whatever your age, Puzzle Box puts the joy and addictiveness of creativity in your hands, but with an added challenge.

Then you can use them as levels in other Modes with different action gameplay – it is a simple way to enjoy Pixel Art in different ways.

Modes for Creation:
- Classic Mode with scrolling
- Copycat Mode for still images

Modes to play in the arts:

- Boom Mode
Destroy your own made pixel art with a limited amount of bombs

- Run Mode
Run and Jump with Marshmallow Boy Roy on the top of your pixel art level and collect all the Combo Coins

- Kubi Mode
Pixel Hero Kubi has to collect all the colorful Pixel Flies and to feed his little dog within the Pixel Art, but be careful, evil shadow man are all around you

- Claw Mode
Control a flying Plattchen with a chain and a claw to kidnap all the Combo Coins in the polished Art environment. Avoid touching obstacles and watch the time.

FEATURES :


*Enjoy collecting all the Combo Coins and clear all 180 levels

*More Than 7 Different Game Modes, with Different Styles of Pixel Art Gameplay

*2 Levels of Difficulty in All Modes, for Relaxed or Challenging Gameplay

*Flexible Controls (Mouse+Wheel+Keyboard)

*Have Fun Creating Your Own Pixel Art

*Share Your Hand Crafted Levels With Players from Around The World

*Super Easy to Pickup Up and Play, but Very Addictive

*Relaxing Background Music

*Combo Coin Rewards for Special Pixel Precision and Creative Speed

*Amazing Painted and Animated Backgrounds, Great for all ages

Blending fun and creative gameplay, Puzzle Box has everything to keep your mind and reflexes sharp while enhancing your creative skills.

Paint yourself Happy.

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 2 April
Basic Information
  • Title: Puzzle Box
  • Genres: Action, Adventure, Casual, Indie & Strategy
  • Developer: BplusGames
  • Publisher: OtakuMaker.com
  • Release Date: The 1st of April, 2016

General Impression
Puzzle Box is a pretty nice combination of the action, adventure, casual and indie and strategy genres. The graphics are two-dimensional, in a platformer type, being simple and colorful, yet neat. The textures are clear and well-designed. The atmosphere varies between each map, where you basically have to fulfill with the required colors. There are 7 modes available, and each one of them will have a different way of displaying the atmosphere, and the size of it. From time to time, you will notice that some blocks have been already fulfilled. No worries, just keep fulfilling the required ones. In the foreground, there are some blocks which block you from continuing your trip. You must avoid them, in order to continue your trip. In the background, there is the sky. It won’t affect anything of the game. The audio is pretty nice, same for the soundtrack. Even though there could be more soundtracks for each mode. The gameplay is as simple as it following: reach the end of the map. In some modes, you will have to fulfill the squares while flying. In some modes, the map won’t be dynamic. In some modes, you have to jump throughout the map. The user interface is well-optimized, with no bugs and well-synthetized information. But, however, the main menu is a thing that is really great. You can splash bubbles just for fun, and you can also drive your bubble to the level and press “enter”. It’s a really well-designed menu, and dynamic as well. The controls are confusing, because you can use either the W, A, S and D buttons, the arrows or your mouse. So basically, you can choose what buttons you feel the most comfortable with, but I feel like 3 options is not needed at all, because some gamers might get confused of this fact. The engines are alright, I haven’t met any bugs, crashes, screen freezes or frame drops. The mechanics are pretty solid, I haven’t any glitches around them. Overall, it’s a pretty nice indie game, with two-dimensional graphics, simple and colorful, yet neat, tons of levels to explore, many modes available and even other nice features.

Positive Points
  • Well designed graphics for a puzzle game
  • Dynamic and challenging atmosphere
  • Enjoyable and relaxing sounds
  • Relaxing, but in the same time, challenging gameplay
  • Well optimized engines and controls
  • Beautifully designed main menu
  • Many environments and colorful arts
  • Many levels and 2 solid game modes
  • Very colorful and dynamic maps

Neutral Points
  • Same soundtrack at every map, there should be more and mixed soundtracks
  • A level editor would be an amazing feature

Negative Points
  • The maps probably move a little too fast
  • The controls are confusing sometimes

Early Access Review
Posted: 1 April
Oh boy, I've been excited for this game since it was in Greenlight - and I can say that it delivered!

Puzzle Box is a very creative and entertaining game for all ages - it's just great all around. The gameplay revolves around dropping block in the right places, but it's surprisingly not repetitive; the game is full of surprises and varieties (which I have to admit I was quite surprised by).

My only complains for this game are the menu (which is conceptually super cool but sometimes rather challenging to navigate through, especially due to it's size) and the drop light (the light that helps you drop your blocks more accurately) that is very hard to see - I don't know if this was intended as a part of the gameplay to add difficulty to the game, but on a lot of the stages it blends with the similarly bright background. It makes it rather challenging to drop boxes accurately, especially in moving stages.

But other than that, I'm loving this game. Hopefully there'll be Steam Achievements and Trading Cards in the future though!
