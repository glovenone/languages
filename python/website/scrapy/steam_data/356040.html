About This Game

Panting, scared, the shelter airlock smashes down behind you. You wish you did not have to go outside, but you do. It would be easier to give up. But you don’t. The hardship, the hunger, the thirst. The fear. You keep reminding yourself why you stay alive. Why you do this. Then you hear it. “Daddy you’re back.” You wife’s face lights up and you open a bag full of medical supplies. You need not tell her how you got them. You simply enjoy your small victory in this, the harshest of times. In the post-apocalyptic world, you must keep your family alive in your underground bunker in this deep strategy game from Unicube...

Sheltered is a post-apocalyptic disaster management game that gives a whole new meaning to the term “nuclear family”. Given a head-start over the billions lost in a nuclear holocaust, you must gather as many supplies as possible en route to the concrete underground shelter that will soon become your family home for the foreseeable dreary future.

With all hope lost, how will you cope in this bleak new world? How you handle the many moral choices you'll encounter on a daily basis could be the difference between your family surviving or dying. Combat claustrophobia, radiation and mental exhaustion as you cling desperately to life in the cramp, cold and scary underground shelter. You’ll have to leave the scant protection the shelter offers behind as you’re forced to venture outside into the dangerous desolate wasteland to scavenge for vital supplies which could ensure your families survival for just one more day.

Feature List



  • Family comes first: Keep your family alive and protect them from the ravages of the harsh post-apocalyptic world.
  • Make the shelter a home: Maintain your shelter - keep it secure, habitable and make sure that the family have enough resources to survive. Protect your shelter from hazards and infiltration!
  • Customisation: Your family is unique to you; you decide their gender, names and their appearance.
  • RPG mechanics: As your family develop, their stats will adapt with an evolving experience and trauma system. Attributes, strengths and weaknesses will dynamically change based on your choices.
  • Crafting system: Create comforts for your family’s shelter and weapons for combat.
  • Exploration: Send out your family to explore and gather resources for everyday living and crafting.
  • Recruitment system: Recruit outsiders with different personalities, integrate them into your shelter life and earn their loyalty.
  • Dynamic encounter system: Your family will encounter various threats including roaming factions and feral beasts.
  • Turn-based combat: For those that cannot be reasoned with, violence can be used as a last resort. Your family may depend on it.
  • Pets: Your pet is family too. Choose between the dog, cat, fish, snake or horse.
  • Vehicles: Make exploration quicker and safer.

Posted: 9 June
This is the first game I was inspired to write any type of review for. Normally I just keep my mouth shut, regardless of the game's quality, but something about this game inspired me to say something.

TLDR: While it's a horribly designed game no where near completion, the core of it is solid and enjoyable for the first 8-14 hours of play and shows a lot of potential. It's worth about $5 in it's current state as I've played many more free (not freemium) mobile games that were far more polished, thought out, developed, and appropriately implemented. This game is clearly not of finished release quality by any stretch of the imagination. It's almost a proof-of-concept prototype that their still hacking things into.

The good: The game's initial play over the first 8-14 hours is overall quite fun. Initially, it is a challenge to keep your family alive, upgrade, craft better items, etc. It is a fun resource management opener in that way. The random interactions, while still repeative are made at least somewhat enjoyable by the writing in the very early game. Oh yeah, and the graphics are--for the most part--pretty nice.

The bad: Sadly, the good ended there. Even before this initial period of play is over, I immediately noticed--and was frustrated--by the increadibly poor UI design (non-existitant keybindings for most things in the game, very inconsistent menus, overall horrible action/party management), badly implemented attribute system (only killing/subding blows count for STR/DEX, telling random people who want to join you to go away does nothing for your INT even though it clearly shows your actual intelligence, etc), and the... atually, I'll save that for the ugly (keep reading). Once past the first 40-60 in-game days (generally, actual game hours countable on two hands), the game turns insanely repetative. The game is backed by what seems to be a primative i.d.d. RNG (think dice rolls)... which--especially for this type of game--was a huge mistake. You can spend hundreds of hours looking for that one last item, only to get the same items you have over, and over, and over again. Insanely boring. There is no end game goal and there is no end game to speak of. There's no reason to even survive. It's just seeking items on a probabilty dice roll.

The ugly: As already strongly hinted at, this game is horribly shallow. After you get settled, the only thing that's a threat is a bug or simply a, "Really? Really? You have got to be kidding me!" moment that's sprinkled so poorly throughout. It's filled with bugs and "what-the-f?!?!?" moments that so near the surface of the game (easy to find without trying just playing for more than a handful of hours) as if they never tried playing their own game for any amount of time. Putting up with "the bad" is pretty easy, but there's really no point to continue when your playthrough is ruined because of some completely arbitrary dice-roll event or bug. The world is tiny, your ability to expore it is pointless, survival is meaningless, and every aspect of doing so results in increasing degrees of boredom and frustration.

Conclusion: This game is--even though the team17 logo comes with the tagline "25 years of gaming excellence" (or something like that)--clearly developed by amateurs. I write that as a 20ish year veteren of software development... not purely as a game enthusiest. However, it shows great promise. Again, the first part of the game is quite a bit of fun and has kept me creating new families over again just to get frustrated or bored enough to do it from scratch again. The very, very basic aspects of this game (it's a game of economic management) are genuinely engrosing. Everything else however, falls horriblely flat. Regardless of the fact that it's been officially released, it's an alpha (pre-beta/think pre-release or dev build) game for sure.

Like many people, I wish that I could somehow tell steam that I "conditionally" recommend this game, but because I can't I'm going to have to say that I don't for the general audience at the asking price ($15 at the time of post).
