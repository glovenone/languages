About This Game

About This Game

The Slaughtering Grounds delivers constant intense interaction within its environment. Zombie hordes plague the scenarios and random events are happening all the time. These random events include airplane cargo drops, frenzy zombie phases, boss spawns and more! Choose to play single player or in 64 player arena FFA's. Purchase new characters or upgrade your existing characters health, armor, regeneration and guns reload rate, damage, ammo pickup size(15 upgrades per character and gun!)

Ingredients:
1. 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
2. 1/2 cup white sugar
3. 1/2 cup brown sugar
4. 1/4 cup water
5. 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
6. 1 pinch salt
7. 5 apples - peeled, cored and sliced
8. 1 pastry for double-crust pie

Directions:
1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
2. Combine butter, white sugar, brown sugar, water, cinnamon, and salt in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, remove from heat and set aside.
3. Roll out half the pastry to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Place bottom crust in pie plate; pour in apple slices.
4. Roll out top crust into a 10-inch circle. Cut into 8 (1-inch) wide strips with a sharp paring knife or pastry wheel. Weave the pastry strips, one at a time, into a lattice pattern. Fold the ends of the lattice strips under the edge of the bottom crust and crimp to seal.
5. Pour butter-sugar mixture over top of pie, coating the lattice, and allowing any remaining sauce to drizzle through the crust.
6. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. Reduce heat to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C), and bake until the crust is golden brown, the caramel on the top crust is set, and the apple filling is bubbling, 35 to 40 more minutes. Allow to cool completely before slicing.

Talking about a game that was developed by a single person in Unity, you can expect there to be some minor issues in the game. However, I can sum it up with a couple of bulletpoints.

-The stock shotgun can one-shot zombies from across buildings, making it more akin to a sniper rifle
-Ammo packs will either become abundant or scarce, resulting in it being either way too easy or way too hard
-Ammo packs only give ammo for the weapon being held by the player
-The third and fourth weapons are completely pointless, as I cannot seem to figure out how to actually use them
-Death does not seem to end the game, as you simply respawn with the same amount of ammo that you had prior to death
-You can quickly equip another weapon while dead and shoot zombies before you respawn
-The game will start off with decent framerates, then slow to a crawl near the end of the level
-The boss zombie that spawns at the first level drops money that can be picked up infinitely, breaking the game
-There is only one multiplayer server, and it's completely empty

Overall, I wouldn't buy this game, even in a Steam Sale.

(For actual review including pros/cons/problems/bugs, scroll to the bottom of the review. The middle is just a little back story :)

|BREAKING NEWS| The game is actually playable on the factor of lag. You just must pass the first level. As if has a lot more spawn points closely grouped together as well as MANY more buildings than the 2nd and 3rd maps (Still got bored before I could finish the 3rd level though).

Another thing. If you are actually interested in playing and buying/upgrading people, the 2nd level is for you. About 5 or so minutes after the start of lvl 2, the is a message that appears that says "I will devour your soul" or something along those lines. When that happens, look around for a 15 foot tall (and as such, not hard to see despite the mountains in the area) demon looking thing. It is just about as fast as your walking speed, so just keep shooting him in the face while sprinting backwards (make sure to remember where you walked and how you got there so you don't get stuck on a wall or tree as i do not know how much damage he does). When he dies, he will drop 5 (or 5000 but in 5 stacks) of cash in a '<' shape. Keep walking over them (while, of course watching for flanking zombies) for about 1 1/2 - 2 minutes to grab all the ~$25-30k. Very good for buying and upgrading your weapons.

I Can't even ironically reccommend this game. It has been out for how long now? Coming on a year and a half? I think I bought it around July of 2015. It hadalmost a year to improve by then, and it was crap. I had bought it for $0.19 thinking, with its complete crap graphics, that my (at the time crap) Computer would be able to run it. I played it for 30 minutes then, then couldn't stand the lag any longer, but I thought 'Maybe once I get a better PC, it will run well, and by then it will likely have updated a few times to get rid of the many many bugs (Which were not fixed that I will go into more detail with later). So I uninstalled it.

A year passes. I've gotten a beefy pc. AMD FX 8350 CPU, GTX 960 graphics card, 8GB DDR3 RAM (which I plan to upgrade by putting in more/bigger Sticks). You know, the works. Granted, it isnt the complete top of the line, but it's up there. After downloading many of the games I could only run on the lowest settings or even not at all, I ran them and they worked beautifully. Then I remember this game. It's twangy theme song from the title screen... and also all of the rest of the gameplay. I say to myself "I bet it will be amazing looking and run very well now!" Boy was I wrong. It ran just barely better than it did on my old PC (Which couldn't even run the Stick War flash games at any sort of decent speed. That is the quality of "computer" we are talking about here). Let's go ahead and delve into its many problems now, followed by a Pro/Con list :)

Pro:

It is very inexpensive. I got it on sale for 19 cents. When i feel like letting my PC idle for 45 minutes, i will likely make at least half of my money back.

If you like about 7 different models of creepy enough looking zombies, this game can deliver. albeit very slowly. The character models range from a mime-ish slender man to somewhat of a charger from L4D2. The models, if textured well, would probably be quite scary and make for a better game.

Cons:

As was forementioned, The lag and lack of textures. I have already said enough, so will not make a full thing on them besides saying it plays at like 20 FPS max at PS1 levels of graphics on a pretty good PC.

There is a lack of hitmarkers. Not for you or your gun, but for zombies that sneak up behind you. You try backing away from zombies only to hit a wall. then die two seconds later. Wait, that wasn't a wall, it was a zombie and you couldn't tell because it doesnt say you're being hit or show it in any way besides the loss of life and some blood splatter on the upper corners of your screen.

Lack of sounds. Having played most of my PC gaming on a complete crap computer, I have learned to focus less on visuals (although I would like them to be pretty good) and more on audio. This game delivers on neither of those subjects. The only sounds you will get are your gunshots, reloading, picking up an item, or a zombie dying. They don't make a sound when they're agro'd, when they are moving, or even when they are hitting you (nor does your character). So basically, if you are at 23% health and haven't seen any zombies, look behind you. Odds are there will be a girl scout and an escaped prisoner/mime/senderman thing chasing you.

Lack of online community. There is, as far as I can tell, one online server up for this game at the time I am posting this video (as of like 11:30PM to 12:00 AM. Maybe most are daytime servers). I went on the server and there were two people. both were gone within 5 minutes of me joining. And there were two immediately noticed problems with online. 1. When you type in the chat box, you cannot move besides your cursor until you shoot. and 2. The only way to leave online is to leave the entire game.

All enemies are just retextured, but not altered to match. What i mean by this is basically that you can one-shot the little girl scout with the revolver to the head. That is acceptable. But then you face the L4D2 Chargers and such and they have just as much health. It would be nice for some to be easier and harder than others.

Overall, I would rate this game 3/10, or 5/10 after the 1st level.
