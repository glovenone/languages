About This Game

It's the year 2112. A hundred years following the apocalypse, humans quickly rebuilt a new world while developing science and technology. Earth, at this time, became a perfect Eden. Despite this, humans continued to be possessed of madness and desire. Wars, crime, and killings still continued.

In 2113, due to the demand for new weapons of war, a program surrounding cybernetic technology was initiated. A group of specially selected girls was sent to a secret research facility for body modifications.

In 2115, a mysterious meteorite from deep space was detected approaching the Solar System. Some scientists predicted it would hit the Earth in three years. Humans were so afraid that they tried everything they could to prevent this from happening.

In 2116, analysis of the incoming meteorite revealed something even more frightening: the presence of many unknown life forms and suspected viral substances on the meteorite. Even if humanity survived the meteorite hitting the Earth, the world would be destroyed due to these mysterious life forms and viruses.

Near the end of 2117, just before the meteorite is due to hit the Earth, three of the girls who had their bodies modified as weapons are sent, along with all the hopes of humanity, to destroy the meteorite and save mankind...

An Overlooked Danmaku Game on Steam
[Recommended Buy]

A Bullet Hell game reminiscent of the Touhou Project series.

Overview:
Gun Wings is a shmup/danmaku (bullet hell) game released in 2015 by DEZ Studio. For a cheap price, this game is quite a steal as it offers a challenging gameplay with a unique mechanic for both beginners and veterans alike. Sadly, it has a low replay value due to the low amount of content in this game. Despite being overshadowed by more well-known danmaku games, Gun Wings is still an enjoyable game.

Gameplay:
Gun Wings sets itself apart from other danmaku games by featuring a bullet-absorbing mechanic. In short, you are able to absorb bullets with the same color as your character to fill your Charge Gauge. While full, you're able to use a Charge Shot to convert bullets with different colors into points. This seems to be the only real way to score, as there are no advantages of grazing in this game.

Other than those unique gimmicks, everything is commonly found in danmaku games. You're able to choose to control one of the 3 girls available. There are also two types of weapons to choose. Generally, Weapon 1 is the Spread type, while Weapon 2 is the Forward type. Holding the shot button while using Weapon 1 charges your weapon gauge, and releasing it while the bar is full will make you perform a powerful shot. Choosing Weapon 2 will grant you extra homing/spread bullets instead, helping you to cover more enemies whilist dodging. As such, both weapon types are properly balanced to handle the enemies in the game. In the end though, there are not much variations as neither your Charge shot nor Bomb type will change.
Score: 8/10

Sound:
While not featuring fantastic compositions, Gun Wings still has several good BGM and SFX in its own right. It might be offputting at first, but it certainly fits the gameplay in many situations.
Score: 7/10

Visual:
Gun Wings has good enemies and characters designs. The bullets are easily differentiated from the backgrounds, too. However, the enemies' planes might be hard to see while facing an onslaught of bullets, potentially causing derp deaths. Main menu UI is fairly generic though. As a side note, it is quite common to control girls in Japanese danmaku games. Controlling girls as the ships rather than planes commonly found in conventional danmaku seems to be a hit-and-miss for some people. Though in the end, it's only a matter of personal preference.
Score: 8/10

Pros:
+ Cheap Price
+ Accompanied with Steam Trading Cards, beautiful Profile Backgrounds and Achievements
+ Unique bullet-absorbing mechanic
+ Steam Cloud support
+ As a niche little feature, you're able to shoot enemy planes on the main menu.
+ Generous in allowing you to use many continues
+ You're able to continue playing at the stage where you've quitted the game.

Cons:
- Not Beginner Friendly. Bullets travel quite fast in this game, especially during boss fights. While justified by the fact that the density of bullet is lessened, it is still hard to attempt to dodge the bullets.
- Inconsistent Difficulty Spike. Later levels feel like they belong in the difficulty above them. Even on Normal, beginners will certainly start having difficulty to keep up with the game starting from the second part of stage 4,
- Unlocking Training mode is pointless, as the player who actually need to use it is required to complete the game on Hard first.
- The enemies' planes might be hard to see while facing onslaught of bullets, which might cause several derp deaths.
- Lack of content
- High Score is not differentiated (e.g. by difficulty, girls and weapon types)

Score: 7.5/10

I rate the game Gun Wings 3/5. It's ok.

This is another one of those little vertical-scrolling arcade-style action games wherein hoards of enemies fly in from the top of the screen and shoot glowing globs of stuff which you need to keep your fast moving spacecraft (or in this case, your fast moving anime girl) from bumping into while you hose the entire screen down with your own faster-moving firepower. As is normal for this genre, this is not an easy game, even when played on the easiest difficulty.

+3 Different difficulty levels.
+3 Different characters to choose from each of which have 2 different modes.
+Good menu design.
+Nice stages and enemy design.
+This game is very inexpensive.

+/-Adequate sound and music.
+/-Extremely difficult (but then nearly all games of this kind are extremely difficult).

-Why do you not have little spaceships to control? Why are you controlling little anime girls? Despite the game's attempt at an explanation this really doesn't make much sense but then, this is by no means the first game in this genre to portray flying anime girls in lieu of nifty looking spacecraft.
-I cannot resize the game's resolution and there is no menu option to play in anything other than windowed mode. This is the game's biggest problem. This means that if you are using a monitor in the now standard 1920 by 1080p resolution this game's window will be pretty small and thus all the enemies and their projectiles will be that much harder to see. This is not an extra degree of challenge which this game needs and I'm not going to resize my monitor just to suit this silly little game.

At $4, this may be the best shooter deal on Steam.

I initially misjudged the music as mostly forgettable. However, after 7 hours of play, it has really grown on me. It's well done, complex, and apporopriate. On the whole surely above average. Sound effects are nicely done as well.

The graphics are technically adequate, and artistically very nice. Enemy bullets are colorful and easily distinguished from the backgrounds. The backgrounds are nicely done and enjoyable to look at. They're not the Plain-Jane, drab work you might expect from a budget game. However, the game seems to be running at 30fps, but that's just my guess. The in-game FPS counter I have enabled in the Steam options is curiously absent from this title alone. Graphics could use a resolution bump as well. The game looks quite good in windowed mode, but a little blocky in full screen. Not too bad though. Speaking of full screen, there is no immediately apparent way to activate full screen mode. There is no options menu to be found. Pressing F11 will toggle between full screen and windowed mode. It would be nice to see a setup or options screen to adjust sound/music levels and select full screen. Or at least have some text on the main screen that says, "Press F11 for full screen".

Gameplay is fluid and has a couple of interesting mechanics at work. The hit box is small and cleary defined. You choose from three charachters--red/yellow/blue. Each color has a different weapon pattern and can absorb like-colored enemy bullets (ie red bulltes do not harm the red player). Without getting into detail, absorbing enemy fire benefits you. Also the trigger buttons on the 360 controller slow down the movement of your player to give you greater control to weave in and out of the more intracite waves of bullets.

Difficulty ramps up nicely after the 2nd level and the 5th and 6th level were designed to submit you.

For $4 it's a no-brainer for the shooter fan. I HIGHLY reccomend it.
