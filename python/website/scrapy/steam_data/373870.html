About This Game

For as long as humanity existed, there has been an ancient order which prevented the dark forces from taking over the world. The order's name might have changed as the centuries wore on, but its essence remained the same. They were called "guardians," and our story is about the kids of one particular guardian. One day, fearing for their lives, he left his kids at the doorstep of an orphanage on the other side of the world. This is where our story begins...
The abandoned twin sisters were named Ariel and Belle. They didn't remember their parents, nor how they ended up in the orphanage. From the start they weren't like other kids. The girls really loved one another and were inseparable. They could predict the future, notice details that most people overlooked, and hear voices no one else could hear...
Visions haunted them throughout their childhood - strange apparitions, like echoes from a childhood they never knew, trying to make contact. With age Ariel distanced from her mysterious reveries, but her sister was still plagued by them… And then, one ominous moonlit night, Belle disappeared.
Resolved to find her missing sister, Ariel sets out to look for her. She begins her search at Belle's apartment. Soon it becomes apparent that something terrible has happened, and a closer look reveals a secret message. Belle has been kidnapped, and she begs her sister to help her.
Following the kidnappers' trail, she ends up in some backwater European town, where it seems as if time has stood still since the 18th century, and the air is filled with magic. Chatting with the locals, she learns that things have been restless lately: bloodless bodies have been turning up, eerie howling could be heard at night, and the skies are filled with strange silhouettes.
While exploring the mysterious land, Ariel meets people that miraculously turn out to have been friends of her parents. They reveal to Ariel the secret surrounding her family, and also explain the evil nature of recent events. The dark forces are preparing a wicked ritual, for which they need the blood of her sister - the last of the guardians. Ariel rushes into the lair of evil and disrupts the ritual, fulfilling her role as a guardian and rescuing her sister! However, evil has not been fully purged from the land...

Key Features:

  • Over 50 Location, 23 Hidden Object and 23 Mini games scenes
  • Over 8 hours of gameplay
  • Integrated Match3 Game
  • Global Hint System
  • Additional Game content (Music, Wallpapers, Screensavers etc.)
  • Special Chamber Orchestra Live Records, original soundtrack included
  • Built-in Strategy Guide

Short version: 76%
The detailed, memorable graphics of Sister’s Secrecy hide a game with flat story, bad voice acting, and incredibly good hidden object scenes.

Long version:
It seems that during the development of Sister’s Secrecy: Arcanum Bloodlines, most of the effort went into the hand-drawn graphics and the hidden object scene design. Both feature very detailed scenery with lots of elements, but without making them feel crowded.
The hidden object scenes especially deserve praise: there are many items, but their placement is loose, everything is in sight and can be found easily. In other words, it’s really fun and enjoyable to play these segments. And there are many of them.

The other parts of the game… well, they aren’t even nearly as good. The puzzles just feel old and tired. The story initially has a great hook– the protagonist’s twin sister gets kidnapped, and it almost feels like a good mystery–, but it just descends into a “stop evil countryside cult from resurrecting Dark Lord” plot right after the intro. Also, the voice acting is terrible.
Still, the game is highly worth for its hidden object scenes alone.

Fantasy and Steampunk... (Kind of)

This is a hidden object game, which actually does a lot of stuff quite right. You'll experiencing a good story which can be superficially described as a Steampunk/Fantasy crossover. You'll encountering Knights (some in ghostly form), Necromancers, Dragons and other Monsters of High Fantasy and on the same time steam engines, magic, gear driven machines and other stuff that is relevant to both settings.

The story actually creates some humorous aspects which don't feel artificial or set up, in the same moment the story is captivating like a fairy tale - with some twist and turns.

Technically the devs have taken care to create an inviting game environment, with some detailed and beautyful graphics, a good voiceacting (not overdone, not too shallow), and an atmospheric soundtrack to the game. The bonus Chapter is also quite well done - and it's really like in a fairy tale, where in the end the good ones always win. The hidden Object Games are also good, so good to the point that you don't need any hints - just patience to solve the HOS. The riddles are more on the easy side, just three riddles need more time to solve them - others are solvable in less than a minute.

Pros:
  • Technically well executed game, with some beautiful and atmospheric graphics.
  • Well told story.
  • HOG part and Riddles/Puzzles are mostly on the easy side.
Cons:
  • The merrit of being easy also is one of the downsides, some riddles are far too easy.

Recommended

This game is one that might sit well in a starters library. It's a perfect start for somebody who is unfamilliar with the Hidden Object Genre, or Adventure Light as someone called them more recently. The game has a lot of merrits, one of those is that it's very intuitive play, and very fair Hidden Object Scenes... As I already told the story is a mixture of High Fantasy, Steampunk and Fairy Tale - so that might get at times too much, the aimed audience for the game is young and adolescent women - but, who cares. It's a fun game with a sinister setting, which got so wide spread now.

If you have a chance to dig your teeth into this one, please do so - you won't be too disappointed. Though as with all HOG's - the replayability is very, very limited.

Ugh. I can forgive the low-rez graphics and clunky game engine, but there remain serious problems with this game.

First, forget about playing it in "expert mode": the game doesn't make enough sense to make that possible, unless you've played it before. Play it in "casual mode", and even then expect much bizarreness and frequent use of the "hint" button. "Well this definitely looks like a can of paint". And you need to moisten it using a goblet of angel tears. Of course, right?

Maybe it's my fault for playing in English? Maybe "viburnum fruit" means something more readily recognizable in other languages? Maybe. Well, my point is: expect the hidden-object scenes to be quite opaque (but still easy).

Other than the nonsensical gameplay, the other major flaw is the story and characters: they're there, but there's no chance you will care about any of that. The dialog is bad, and the voice acting (nice bonus!) is dialed in.

The graphics are fine, if inconsistent in style, despite the low resolution.

Fans of HOGs: unless you got this for free, spend your money and time on something better.
