Absolutely great game! This is not a mindless shooter per se, it's a dexterity based shooter where you need to use your intellect in order to pass some levels. It's not about just shooting every enemy in the stage, sometimes you just need to destry targets and proceed to the next level.

Oh, and the protagonist is not even a titular elk or a moose or you name it. Beat the game and see for yourself:)

Game is full of weird humor but it's fun actually. In what other game you need to shoot hamburgers summoning boars or giraffes in a ufo shaped vehicles?

One guy wrote that there is not enough ammo in this game. Sometimes yes, but in most cases you can end up with maximum of 99 bullets every other stage. Just wait a moment untill morse stall produces ammo. Have in mind that you don't need to destroy all enemies most of the times.

Useful spoilerfree hints:
- if you score enough points in arcade machine minigame the machine explodes and you can collect a rocket
- you can click ENTER at the big red button limited amount of times - untill it explodes actually. Just click both enters - standard return enter and num lock enter if you have it to speed up the process.
- when those things explode, arcade machine and big red button, they can hurt you. Just be sure to stand as far as to highlight the Enter or E button to use those, but don't stand too close or you can get hurt
- if you feel that the level is too hard and happen to have 100 ducks then you can build the last building for 100 ducks to skip that level. Just wait a moment and the usual elevator appears.
- at some levels you can be cursed with flying yellow thingies on blue balls all around character. This can happen if you go too close to the blue totem thing. Worry not, if there is a fireplace you can position yourself so the fire hurts those flyers and eventually destroys them.

Pros:
- humor
- graphics are nice
- toxic themed stages are really fun and well designed
- music is pleasant to listen and well composed for this game

Cons:
- some levels are really hard. That's not a problem, but if you lose you need to start whole level all over again.
- at every level you start zoomed. You can of course zoom out but what't the problem with the auto zoom at every level? Really, with the perspective zoomed in you can barely see anything happening.
- it's too short:) I have like 7 hours on the counter but I feel that you can beat this game in about 4-5 hours as there are only 10 areas/levels with like 5-6 maps each. No secret levels or anything like that sadly.

In summary - I really recommend this game. Such fine a piece of an indie game.

Early Access Review
Posted: 30 April
Review copy provided from developer. Note that my ability to enjoy a game is not influenced by how I recieved it. If you would like to see the TL;DR, go to the summary at the bottom of the review.

The Big Elk
is a wacky top-down shooter where, as the name suggests, you play as a rather large elk.

As a story isn't exactly applicable for a game like this, it wouldn't be worth describing a lack of story. What would be worth describing is the complete and utter lack of explaining anything. There's top hat wearing penguins and circling orb-ducks... everything is just complete and utter chaotic moose mayhem. Whilst this randomness does make the game unique in a way, a simple explanation of why an elk is interested in shooting up gun-weilding trees would have been sufficient.

Throughout your time playing, you will fight your way through a collection of levels consisting of a wide variety (and completely random) enemies using a small (slightly too little) variety of weapons. There will be more weapons added to the game throughout the Early Access period. A more major problem with the weapons is the minimal amount of ammunition given. This can prove to be quite a significant hindrance and cause a disruption to the flow of the gameplay.

Even with these weapons, you can decide to access the more unique side of the game by setting up a base and using different strategies to make your eradication of the giant hamburgers more efficient. This side to the gameplay adds some interesting tower-defence elements, keeping the gameplay refreshing. The only problem with this aspect is that you need money to set up your base, and like your ammo there isn't enough of it. Even with these problems, the gameplay still comes out as enjoyable.

The Big Elk has a simplistic art style as I think you would have managed to figure out from the screenshots. It definitely suits the game and adds a unique charm.

Whilst the in-game music is relatively enjoyable to listen to, there's nothing about it that warrants it a spot on the 'Pros' list down below.

The game also has a distinct lack of options, with the only two ones being the ability to change to fullscreen and adjust the volume. There is also a lack of controller support which may come as a disappointment to some.

The Big Elk is an enjoyable and unique little game. It is fairly priced, but I would only really recommend it for those who are fans of the genre.

Early Access Review
Posted: 3 April
The Big Elk is a casual, fun and rather unique top down shooter where you play as an Elk (it's actually a Moose I think) trying to protect his forest homeland from various invaders. Some of these invaders include gun totting Christmas trees, penguins, Jello molds and other interesting and humorous enemies. This isn't your mommy and daddies (as a 35 year old man I feel totally comfortable saying mommy and daddy...) Rocky and Bullwinkle, this is like Rambo and Bullwinkle had a child who slaughters Christmas themed enemies at any time of the year, with impunity. While I played the game I threw my voice to talk like Sylvester Stallone in some of his more memorable roles.. like "You can take my life, but you can never take my freedom!!", (I kid, I kid, everyone knows that line comes from Braveheart) My actual quotes while I played were more along the lines of "Stop or my mom will shoot" and Adrian... Anyway

Being that it's a top down shooter (with tower defense elements) you roll around one of three types of weapons, a Blunderbus shooting drumsticks, a rocket spewing rocket launcher and the penultimate carrot chain gun, the gun play and movements are fluid, the sounds are decent enough, and I'd say it would be a pretty safe bet to allow younger kids to play without much fear, as you aren't using bullets, real guns, nor are any of the enemies other humans. The total and utter lack of blood also doesn't hurt in the young kid playing department.

The game has 10 game zones, which are broken up into smaller little zones, where you have various task to complete, some are as mundane as going from left to right, others require you to go hog-wild and mow down hordes of enemies, whether they be slimes, meat pies, even some Jello Molds, while some others may see you dodging various enemies or blasting through piles of ice or rock.

Pro's

Silky smooth movements
good gun play
decent sounds which
challenging yet surprisingly casual game-play
casual pick up and play type game

Con's

Lack of controller support (this can be a positive to some, but I'm a controller fanatic)
I want some Moose/Elk Trading cards dang it..

Recommendation

This can be a fun game experience that can be enjoyed by just about everyone. While not the most overly complex game, The Big Elk has a charm about it, maybe it's the cute looking creatures, or the obvious Christmas themed enemies, after playing the game it will grow on you... (not like a fungus,) and you'll soon find yourself thinking about playing it when you aren't.. Eventually you'll stand to do moose calls in your basement and try to speak with animals like in the movie Brother Bear. I'd say pick it up if you are a fan of casual games or have a child you want to get into video games without fear of having them turn into a brainless zombie who goes on a murderous rampage later on in life. (ok in all honesty I don't think playing video games, especially nice family friendly games like this!!)

So if you have a couple of bucks just wasting away in your steam wallet, and like myself can't find anything to play, give The Big Elk a try, just remember to be gentle, the forest creatures are your friends. SIKE!!! KILL THEM ALL!!!

The Big Elk is the Steam debut by Anatoliy Loginovskikh. In the summer Anatoliy is a sheep herder and during the winter when the weather is too cold to do that job he becomes a game designer. That might sound a little funny to some of you but to me that sounds like a cool life. Although this is his first game on Steam he has created other games in the past. He claims to have a very old computer that doesnât allow him to make videos for his games and this would explain the simplicity of them.

Simplicity at its best can generate fascinating games that are open to a broad audience. Though itâs still in early access I believe that that is the case with The Big Elk. Itâs a top-down shooter with animal themes that suits Anatoliyâs background. Itâs a clever take on the genre that contains a number of elements, varied enemy types and a number of different tools that you can utilize to your benefit in taking down the enemy.

Each area has a number of different game zones that you need to successfully complete in order to progress through to the next one. Once you complete one part of the game more areas of the game open up to you to play. I believe that players also have options in which level they play next â some of the time. There is a smart tutorial at the beginning of the game that does a good job of teaching players the basics and once completed starts you on your path by opening up the first non-tutorial level of the game.

I do like the art style in the game. Although it is the telltale sign of how simple the game is at its core it is immaculately presented and is pleasing to the eyes. The music while not quite as good as the graphics is more than suitable for the game. The biggest problem with the game at the moment is that there may be too little ammunition offered in the game â I run through the ammo that I have very quickly and/or things take longer to kill than what you have ammo for, whichever way you choose to look at it). The other issue that I have with the game is that you cannot change any options (e.g. turn volume up or down, etc.) within the game. These are the two areas that I would work on aside from making the game more Steamified by adding trading cards and achievements (which according to the dev himself are on the way).

This is a game suitable for people of all ages because there is no gore and the action that is in the game is very basic. At its price its a good buy and one that fans of shooters should try.

Early Access Review
Posted: 23 April
The Big Elk is top-down shooter with RPG elements, this is a first game of the Anatoliy Loginovskikh, anyway this game surprisingly funny and challenging.

You are a Elk with weapon and no more can clear your lovely home land from invaders just you. Well your goal mostly at all levels is to kill all enemies and reach a target point. But you will find also some levels with different tasks, for example run between enemies without touching them etc. For all this you have 10 zones and all this zone have different levels with different enemies. Also you can use a enough various strategies to the kill enemies, for example build a base, use a air strike etc. The control in a game is enough good, also a tutorial well explain how to play it. The game graphic is simple, but well represented this world. The sounds and music are well fit to gameplay.

+ various and funny enemies
+ steam achievements and trading cards
+ nice retro style music
+ challenging gameplay
+ various weapons
+ some mini games

- no co-op
- ammo run out too fast

So if you just look at a pictures of this game , you can think this is a another silly game, anyway this is not true. This game can be enough deep thanks for a lot of different enemies, various weapons and strategical variation, If you like this sort of game i easily can recommend it for you, especially if this game get a coop mode.
