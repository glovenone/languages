Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Door To Door is structured similarly as an episodic "television" like series, meaning every "Case-File" would reveal another piece of the story. This means that these individual stories are not all yet available. By utilizing Early Access, the community's input becomes greatly flexible during development in comparison to a large block that has already been sealed before tested. The game does currently have testers to review the new additions, but Early Access will even more so support that when these testers are not available and at a much quicker pace.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The game currently as it stands is constantly being crafted. It will very likely be in development for the majority of 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“When the game does transcend into its full release-state, it will have all the "Case-Files" to play and gameplay polish that the Early Access version would have been lacking. The Ministry Of Order has a lot to offer, it is with best intentions to create a future of sufficient player experience.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“"Case-Files" are detective oriented gameplay, and during these gameplay sessions the player has the choice to stray from their current objective. While the environment will stay much the same for each individual case, the Lounge District is fully open to exploration and the player can complete it to their leisure. Updates to this Early Access will include new Case-Files to play and new environments to explore when the game is updated. As of now, very few gameplay bugs remain. The game also has continuing optimization methods being put into place with each coming update. Players will experience some hitching and sudden dips in performance.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Most likely. It's important for me to gauge your quality of time during your experience. Within Early Access, you are receiving the current state of the game, which will still have content being created for future updates. The game's price will only be raised when the transition to full-release is complete and when there is significantly more content.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Player feedback is incredibly important. Criticism when it is effective proves valuable to tackle issues assertively. I understand you are all here to help. I will be creating several discussion topics in the forums for specific problems that need to be addressed.”

About This Game

Door to Door drops the player into the Lounge District of the Ministry of Order, an over-glorified bureaucratic metropolis full of Jazz and light. They will assume the role of an Agent named "Bixby", a detective whom works within the ministry in a department known as "Correction Pending". Bixby will be offered several cases involving a leisurely stroll through the dark but colorful and satirical setting the Ministry of Order offers. All you have to do is step outside your office to take a chaotic spin through a retro-future city inspired by Terry Gilliam's 1985 film, "Brazil".

Key Features

  • Welcome Back, Bixby: Play as Detective Bixby, Ministry Of Order's "Correction Pending" agent. Select from a varied list of "Case-Files" and experience something different every time.

  • Welcome to Lounge: Take your very own stroll through the Ministry Of Order's vibrant and bountiful Lounge District. Complete the objectives at your own pace and explore the environment to your leisure.

  • Pipe of A Different Color: The story of the Ministry Of Order only goes as deep as you want it to go. With various methods of exposition, you can unravel the truths that this place holds most dear.

  • My Pal, Charizmo: "Charizmo" is an assistance bot and friend of Bixby, whom when commanded from his respected station, can lead the player to several locations within the map.

  • The Synthetic Freeway: If you've had enough of the scenic route and want a little more versatility when completing objectives, all you have to do is step inside any of the few Bus Stations located throughout the map for instantaneous "fast-travel".

    • Processor: Dual Core Processor 2.4GHz

Early Access Review
Posted: 5 January
The devs seem pretty up front about the gameplay being incomplete on this early access title. With that said I think its worth supporting this title for the level of quality it contains at the moment. Bioshock is one of my all time favourite game worlds and this game is heavily inspired by it with its art deco setting, adverts via the radio and poster and also its overall dark/dystopian vibe. This game also to my good fortune blends some of the "hitchikers guide to the galaxy" humour with a 70/80s view of the robotic future. The colors blast you with a fusion of neon and seemingly much larger areas than rapture had and the environmental sounds and noises also conjure up 70/80s sci-fi action movies. The game though centers around a noir detective setting of which you appear to be a robot dective. I stumbled across some of the story and very amusing incidents as i tried to just take in the game world. It's a great base and with some more love this game will hopefully turn out to be a gem even if it's gameplay doesn't get to advanced by the end. If you like any of the above i would recommend the game even at full price (it may go up in theory when released in a year?). Even if not I suggest adding it to your follow list to see what develops over this year--here's to hoping the "Ministry" delivers on this ambitious endeavour.
