About This Game


Yes, it's the game literally... SOME of you asked for! And so, in the spirit of "well, alright then, if you insist..." we present the third game in the Carmageddon series, in the knowledge that some of you Carma fans out there would like to have a complete Carmageddon collection in your Steam library. Nothing wrong with that.

Anyway, here's what the blurb on the back of the DVD case had to say about Carmageddon: TDR 2000:

The world's most controversial racing game returns with incredible graphics, fantastic game play and even more outrageous fun!

Max Damage, imprisoned for life, plots his escape. However, there's the small matter of an army of police, hordes of gangsters and thousands of enraged inhabitants - all with homicide on their minds. To succeed, it will take a four wheeling genius with no morals, a killer instinct and a burning passion to escape.

No such person existed... until now!

  • Drive 50 totally destructible vehicles.
  • Over 48 missions and races to complete.
  • 9 completely 3D single player environments to explore and destroy.
  • 7 multi-player game types in 9 custom maps.

Known Issue

Symptoms: When you start a mission, the game crashes to the desktop with an error about Directx, address 0x80070057.
Solutions: There's an easy fix, disable the MIP Filter. You can do this by running TDR 2000 Setup and opening the "Advanced Settings" dialog, and setting MIP Filter to "none". Another solution is to disable the Fog.

Multiplayer

How do I set up dedicated server games?
To set up a dedicated server, create a shortcut to Carmageddon: TDR2000, and add -cr -d -co -cs to your command line. This will use the options you have already in your Multiplayer settings.

I just played the trilogy recently. The 3rd one has problems.

-They changed the control scheme on it. ...Probably for the better, but still.
-The Eagle doesn't feel quite as powerful in it as it did in the previous ones. ...Which is again is probably for the better, since you basically never have to swap out the Eagle until late game in them.
-The time limit maxes out at 4 minutes. And not just "maxes out the display" and keeps rolling, but actually taps out at 4 minutes. THIS is my biggest complaint with it. You are basically forced to keep going in it, you never get a chance to really sit back and explore. I like exploring the levels. Pedestrians also give you almost no time.
-The missions. They were every other level here, not every 4th level like in the previous game. They also... I dunno, mostly were uninspired? They're not bad, but they seemed to be fairly bland, particularly early on. They are hit and miss overall.

Two other issues: This game includes the Nosebleed Pack. Now, it's nice in that "more cars, more maps, more content!" However, these vehicles are not well balanced, and are available from the beginning. And are available in the opponent's pool from the beginning. And the hovering car can be very, very annoying to deal with.

Another issue is that the game crashes on story missions. This is a known issue. There is a workaround. The workaround is to turn off the mip filter. HOWEVER, due to another glitch, that being badly coded menus where the content doesn't contain itself to the windows, you can't actually turn off the mip filter in game.

You CAN do it from the setup though. So open the setup, go to options, video, advance, and where you see "Mip filter", set it to none. You may also need to disable Pixel Fog (though I didn't need to). This should fix the problem.

There's a lot to like about the third game though. The graphics are a big step up. The final levels in this game really look fantastic. The story IS there, even if it's annoying at times.

My favorite of the bunch is the second. The third has problems. So, why am I giving this a "yes" recommendation, despite all the flaws, including crashes? Because, at the end of the day, it's still Carmageddon. And if you need to get your fix, there ya go.
