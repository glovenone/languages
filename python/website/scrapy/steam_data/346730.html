About This Game




Enter the studio, and join a make-believe war — the kind said to have been played by children almost a hundred years ago.
Up to 32 people can be in a lobby at once, and up to 8 players can join up to take on quests together.

  • Go to war in the guise of an adorable avatar
  • Addictive shooting gameplay with easy controls
  • You can enjoy short sessions of casual single player
  • Story spanning 21 quests, 3 difficultly settings
  • Includes online/offline modes (teams of up to 8)
  • Features over 200 characters, over 30 monster types
  • 1600 status upgrade badges
  • Over 350 garments can be synthesized with over 60 kinds of materials
  • 13 weapon types, each can be assigned an element and upgraded.
  • 44 achievements / 8 trading cards
  • Free chat worldwide (100 set phrase are also available)
  • 40 gestures for communication


This is "Gocco Studio Japan", an attraction popular with children 100 years in the future! Progress through the game by beating quests as you play imaginary war, sword fight and take on adventure with friends! Environments and enemy variations are tailored to each quest.

Want a change of clothes? Collect materials on a quest and combine them to make new costumes, upgrade your weapons and apply elemental properties for future quests.
If you are awarded with a badge, put it on for an extra status boost!
Impress everyone with your stylish duds and incredible strength!

Online lobbies can hold over 30 people, with communication via text chat and avatar gestures! Make new friends and fight through dangerous quests together.

It's time to have some real fun: become a kid again!

A friend and I bought this after seeing it's basically a miniature PSO and the rather positive reviews. Before you continue reading, I have to say that I understand that this probably wasn't made by a huge game studio and it's something I feel had a ton of potential to stand out.

Gotta say, it's not actually as fun as people in the reviews say. I don't mean it in a particularly bad way, but it was hyped up a lot in the reviews so I expected more.
I wouldn't say it's bad but just within the first 2 hours of playing with a friend it's pretty clear it's really repetitive. Really repetitive. I wanted to make sure I could get the refund (as the game has a decently notable price tag). While it had charm and appeal for the time I played, it wasn't enough to keep my friend and I coming back.

The car that's driveable is probably the most fun part (my favourite, anyways) and it isn't even included on a lot of levels. I felt that more direct-coop things like that would have made the game a lot more exciting. Vehicles, teamwork-based objectives, riveting and dynamic levels- stuff like that. The customization was nice but I felt held back because literally everything costs a certain amount of ingame money that actually takes a while to collect. There's no way to get loot, ever. You just grind money and spend it all, and not to mention those materials you gotta collect for stuff too. Unlike a game like Borderlands or even PSO (which this game seems the most similar to), there's no satisfaction of ever getting a rare item or just interesting equipment because all the loot comes in the form of money or materials and little else.

The gameplay as a whole works pretty fine but a part of me says that the 'air' energy/stamina system is being used for too many things, having to recharge a bit too frequently. I want to use the gun camera for the melee attacks as well but there's no way to change that, and it feels really clunky the way that it is. All of the combat feels really floaty and doesn't have that satisfying feeling when you defeat enemies, it's like hitting balloons. Really tough balloons.

There's very little enemy variety, most of them making up of recolours and remodels of enemies that basically do the same thing. The level design is also pretty poor in certain levels while other ones are more appealing in both visuals and gameplay, but overall it feels somewhat cheap, which was disappointing because there's a lot of potential. Since I didn't want to play over the two hours we had for the refund, I haven't seen the later levels so I did some research and it seems even later on you're doing basically the same thing.

The game's pretty hard to look at sometimes with all the bloom on, and drop shadows only appear when you limit the framerate to 30 from 60 (at least if I recall properly, anyways- it was a particular video setting I'd rather not turn down). Without the shadows, it really is hard to look at the game with their harshly stretched textures and ultra-vibrant colours.

The sound effects aren't really all that varied and you'll hear the same stuff a lot. You won't really hear any music in game like the trailer's, either. It felt really bland playing for extended periods of time, and I seriously don't want to hear any more of that rolling sound.

To summarize and some other stuff:
-Yes, it's cute. Yes, it has charm.
-The assortment of costumes is nice, but comes at a price of more grinding.
-It suffers terribly from repetitiveness and lack of variety, in almost all departments- from monsters, level design, game modes, sound effects, equipment.
-There's an overall cheap feel to it all and a lot of it feels same-y.
-Serious grinding of the same missions involved for money and materials.
-Small community.
-Visuals can be hard to bear with for long periods.
-Sound design is lacking.
-Game feels floaty, slow, almost.

You really can't go wrong with something titled "Global Online Chibi Combat Offensive".

GOCCO OF WAR is a pretty 'grindy' third-person shooter. Normally, something being described as 'grindy' wouldn't sound all that great, but here it works pretty well. In a similar fashion to a certain Japanese game where you hunt monsters, much of your time on GOCCO will be spent playing then replaying the numerous quests to get that ONE LAST MATERIAL or THOSE FEW MORE COINS you need to build/buy an outfit part, weapon or other material. There's enough action to keep it from getting stale though, even when you're replaying Quest #20 for the fifteenth time to try and find that frickin' Dragon Blood.

Quests can be played either by yourself on Offline mode, which typically grants you an AI teammate to follow and help you out, or Online with up to 4 players. Thankfully, material drops are 'instanced' for each player, so you don't have to worry about that guy with the max level weapon charging through everything and taking all the treasure.

An assortment of clothes and costumes are available to customise your character with. Military uniforms, t-shirts and shorts, alien outfits, pretty much anything. If you decide that you don't like the look of your character or just want a new hairstyle, you can also edit everything about your appearance at any time when in a lobby except for gender, but with 16 character slots that really shouldn't be an issue at all.

The weapons are fun to use, looking as bright as the rest of the game and having secondary uses such as charged shots or alternate attack functions. Each of the weapons can be upgraded to deal more damage, or evolved into a new form to add status effects (fire, lightning, poison, etc.) or just beef up the damage even more.

Overall, GOCCO OF WAR is a nice way to kill some time after enduring some of the other offerings Steam has available. Now if you'll excuse me, I'm trying to save up a few hundred thousand more for that shiny new gun...

GOCCO OF WAR:
~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Welcome to the Gocco Studio Japan, an incredible theme park where you can be part of an imaginary war and fight against enemies created by a hyper-holographic simulator. All you have to do is to pick up one of several ecological weapons available to you which are powered by air and jump into battle on your own or with friends via the online mode.

GOCCO OF WAR is an action/co-op type of game. First, you have to create your Chibi (Japanese slang to describe a person who is vertically challenged) which is really fun especially when you have a fair few options to choose from. Then you have to decide if you want to play online or offline. Either way, you will end up in a lobby. There you will have Chibis you can talk to and find a little bit more of what to do. Also, you will have 3 stands and a billboard.

To do a quest, just go to the quest stand. There you will be able to activate one. There are 21 quests available to you in the game but you have beat the quest to get access to the next one. During the quest, you will be able to pick up items and gold. At the craft stand, you can enhance or create other weapons. If you accumulate enough gold during your quest, head straight up to the shop stand where you can purchase more powerful weapons. The billboard is a tool for the online mode where you can join a party of 4 on a quest.

The combat system is very easy to master and the levels are well designed. This is a really cute, fun game to play by yourself or with your friends. For the control side of the equation, I personally prefer to play with the mouse and keyboard. Nothing wrong with the gamepad by the way but I just think using the mouse and keyboard is better.
Up to 30 different foes to fight against, some will throw projectiles and some will run towards you from left, right and centre.

Positives:
~~~~~~~~

- Cute graphics
- Fun and easy gameplay
- Great Chibi customization
- Good range of enemies
- Offline and Online mode
- Achievements and Trading cards

Negatives:
~~~~~~~~

- Could have been a little more challenging

A truly enjoyable game to play, it is cute, it is fun and it has a lot of chibis! I like it a lot!

8.5/10
