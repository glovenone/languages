About This Game

Forge your weapon and follow the path of the Jedi
Jedi Knight: Jedi Academy is the latest installment of the highly acclaimed Jedi Knight series. Take on the role of a new student eager to learn the ways of the Force from Jedi Master Luke Skywalker. Interact with famous Star Wars characters in many classic Star Wars locations as you face the ultimate choice: fight for good and freedom on the light side or follow the path of power and evil to the dark side.
  • Customize your character by defining both look and gender before entering the Academy to learn the power-and dangers- of the Force.
  • Construct your own Lightsaber from handle to blade. As you progress, discover the power of wiedling two Lightsabers or the ultimate double-bladed Lightsaber made famous by Darth Maul.
  • New vehicles, weapons, force powers and Star Wars locations.
  • Unique level selection system allows you to choose your own missions and adventures.
  • Six multiplayer modes including team based siege mode and two-on-one power duel. Fight in 23 multiplayer arenas!

Product received for free
I'm going to keep this short and simple (yeah right, who am I kidding?).

Pros
------
- The lightsaber combat is the best out of any Star Wars game I've ever played. You can use single at first, then choose between continuing with single saber or going with two sabers or a dualsaber.
- You get to choose between Human Male/Female, Twi'lek Female, Rodian Male, and the odd Kel Dor race. Presumably male. Nice variety.
- You can mod the game. Get some skins or a nice multiplayer mod (I'll get to that in a minute).
- Varied and enjoyable missions.

Cons
------
- Although the lightsaber combat is fantastic, I'm still not sure I know what I'm doing. I just sort vary up my directional attacks (move in one direction and attack a certain way, move in another and you'll attack in a different way). If all else fails, I use the dark side Rage power or Speed to get an advantage.
- Single voice actor. Per gender (at least I hope so, I haven't played females, yet). This is fine when you're human.. or maybe even Kel Dor... but it can be a little odd to hear a Rodian talk so... non-Rodian.
- Slightly confusing progression in missions. It's not too bad, but without a walkthrough, you'll get stumped a few times. Only advice I can give is to take things slowly if you get stuck. Like when you have a certain AI member with you who just so happens to be the person who opens a locked door if you wait long enough.

==========

All of my cons are relatively minor. I don't mind opening a walkthrough if I get stuck at most like... three times. It's a quick search to see where you need to go or what you need to do. It's just that the pacing gets slowed down if you haven't played the game at least once before. I really can't stress how small that complaint is for me, though. If you're easily swayed by criticism, like I tend to be (sometimes), then I can easily say that you should ignore my cons and focus entirely on the pros.

Speaking of the pros (and this is a biggie), there is an excellent mod that could really use more players. It's called "Movie Battles II" and it's a multiplayer mod that lets you play through scenario missions against another team of players. You choose your class, your skin, then set your abilities. It takes a good fifteen minutes to set your controls and get comfortable, but trust me... when you get used to it, you'll have a lot of fun.

Just don't get frustrated if you get your butt kicked. The mod is about 11 years old. Some of those players have had a lot of practice. It's that good.

Oh and just full disclosure (though it's unnecessary, I feel), I was gifted this game for free by a kindly redditor. I would have gladly paid for it, myself, if I had the cash. It's a terrific game.

Thank you, Voicy.

For those of you that hate reading, there's a condensed version of my rambling at the bottom of the paragraph.

Thought I'd write a review for this game but, the hours I have invested in it speak for me in all honesty. This game is the epitome of my STAR WARS wet dreams. Custom Jedi, Lightsabers, The Force, and Stormtrooper dismemberment; what more could you ask for? The story campaign is really good and provides a good time for everyone. Luke Skywalker is voiced by Mark Hamill and John Williams' original STAR WARS score plays throughout the entire game. The fanservice levels in this game are just literally off the charts! However, where this game really shines, in my opinion, is the mods. This game becomes ten times better once you start sinking mods into it. The moding for this game is super easy and definitely add to the experience once you've done all you can with the vanilla game. The mod that gave me over 400 hours by itself was the MOVIE BATTLES II multiplayer conversion mod. I personally believe the MOVIE BATTLES II mod is the best possible mod to get for this game as it is regularly updated and transforms the battle mechanics of the game completely to make it feel like a more realistic STAR WARS experience. And that is just one of the thousands of mods for this game floating around the internet.

So in short, game by itself is great. The Story is ok and the STAR WARS fanservice is seemingly endless. Game with mods is fantastic, tons of mods that add the experience in many different ways. Highly recommend this game for any person who has ever wanted to swing a lightsaber.
