About This Game

MEDIEVAL: Total War™ - Collection Edition is the compilation of the critically-acclaimed Medieval: Total War and its official expansion pack - Viking Invasion

About MEDIEVAL: Total War™

From the lush grasslands of Western Europe to the arid deserts of Northern Africa, and from the first Crusade to the fall of Constantinople, wage total war in order to expand your influence and secure your reign as you build a dynastic empire to stretch across four centuries.
Recruit and deploy armies, besiege settlements, fight naval battles and employ agents such as emissaries, spies and assassins to aid with diplomacy, offer alliances or bribes, or execute more clandestine actions.
A dramatic period of rebellion, civil war, and the birthing of nations provides the backdrop to your own scheme for ultimate power. Carve a niche for yourself through the crucible of battle and become the statesman and king the era demands.

• Fourteen playable factions from across Europe, the Middle East and Africa.
• Up to 10,000 soldiers on-screen deliver incredible, epic battle scenes.
• New siege systems make the most of an era where formidable castles dotted the landscape.
• Valour, Vice and Virtues colour your faction’s characters, effecting how other treat them and the men they lead.

About MEDIEVAL: Total War™ - Viking Invasion

Set upon an extended map of the British Isles and western Scandinavia and taking place from 793 to 1066, Viking Invasion allows the player to assume the role of leader of the Viking faction as they raid, loot and pillage their way to supremacy.
Alternatively the player may take control of early Anglo-Saxon factions, such as Wessex and Mercia, with the aim of repelling the Viking invasion and ultimately controlling the British Isles in its entirety.

• Adds new Anglo-Saxon and Viking factions with additional units alongside the detailed new Campaign map.
• Also includes added functionality for the main game, such as flaming ammunition and three new factions with which to play through the original Grand Campaign.

I played this game initially before Med2, a game which I was terribly addicted to. With the death of my desktop, I installed this beauty for a measly $2.50 (thanks, Steam!). To say the least, it has surpassed it's value ten fold. "Is it really as good as a $25 game?", you ask. The answer to that question varies from "THOSE PIGS BROKE OUR ALLIANCE!" to "Watch them flee. I love watching the enemies flee." and even "I... I lost!?". Losing never felt so good. It leaves you changing your view from "How could my idiots let me down?" to "How could I let down my brave soldiers?".. that's a lesson you can carry to victory in ANY game.
-
The gameplay. If you don't know this game, let's just say the obvious: It's responsible for funding a team who would create the most celebrated and brutal historical war simulation games, ever. Yes there are a few NOT made by the Total War team that are amazing, this is true. However, would those games have been as good if the Total War franchise didn't exist? I think not. The Corvette didn't become the 650 horsepower sternum-crushing monster it is becase there are no other cars in the world to compete with, right?
Play as several factions, each one like a historical counterpart. The Total War team skirts historical inaccuracies by saying "Before countries, there were only kingdoms", or something to the tune of that. It doesn't matter that it's not 100% historically delicious, this is a strategy game first, history channel lesson second.
Raise armies, make alliances, fight pitched battles. Switch between campaign map mode, which resembles what a grisly old emperor might look over to play war games with his neighbors; and battle mode. Battle mode is where the soldiers you chose to fight get to live up to their duties. This is where the middle ages greatest leaders made their names. Where they won their spurs. Thousands of soldiers fighting to the death, following your careful command. Battles lines are formed, archers are readied, cavalary are signalled and the fates of a kingdom, of and empire are decided!

-
One of the biggest problems with retro gaming is compatibility and dealing with the graphics. This game hurdles those problems (for me).
I have a laptop with no-name hardware, backed by some watered down Radeon card from years past. Retro PC gaming isn't as simple as consoles, however my hardware didn't hesitate to show it understood how to process MTW1, although I may have installed a wrapper. (If you don't know what a wrapper is, look it up. Glide wrapper, D3d, Direct Draw for example, make your graphics card able to read the outdated or defunct engines of years past. I installed one for D2 which may have made a difference)
The graphics are spot on, the menus crisp and animation as claymation as the day it came out. That sounds like a bad thing to some, but remember that the early 2000's is a hard pill to swallow when you're dealing with small scale 3D modelling. It's far better with the enhanced 2D style, especially for gamers who remember just how awesome MYTH 1 and 2 were, compared to 3. Imagine Diablo 2 with horrible nearly PS1 era 3D models. Wicked bad. The resolutions, I suspect, were updated. Running this game on higher resolution on a bigger monitor only makes this game better. All of the usual resolutions are available, max at 1920x1080
-
The iffy. The iffy is that I am running this game, like all my retro games, on WIN7. I run WIN7 because I can't run the new games due to the watered down GPU I have, and if I can't run new games.. or old games..than why PC? I have read that there are a lot of problems with WIN10 and retro games. In the beginning there were a lot of problems with retro software on every version of Windows, so this is nothing new.
-
I will be getting a WIN10 tablet in two weeks. Real WIN10, not "RT". "RT" is a mall cop to a Fox Moulder. Then, I will play this game on that tablet, no matter how much it fails to make sense. Mark my words, Sancho, that gold is mine.
