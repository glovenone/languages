About This Game

Recettear is the story of an item shop, the girl who lives in it, and the fairy who turned her life upside down. Recette Lemongrass finds herself in charge of an item shop built into her house, in order to pay back a loan her father took and then skipped out on - and Tear, her newfound fairy "companion", won't take no for an answer! As Recette, you have to decide how you'll get your stock - either through playing the markets in town or going out into the wild with an adventuring friend and thrashing beasts until they give up the goodies - how much to sell things for, what the shop should look like, and how to best go about getting the money Tear needs to pay off the loan. If you can't come up with the money... well, hope you like living in a cardboard box.

Key features:

  • Manage all aspects of an item shop – from stock to interior design!
  • Explore randomized dungeons – never quite the same twice!
  • Multiple adventurers to choose from when dungeon-diving – no two play the same!
  • Plenty to do after beating the game – challenge yourself with Survival Mode or play to your heart's delight in Endless Mode!
  • Engaging story, localized by Carpe Fulgur – get to know the people of Pensee!

At first glance this may appear to be an overly-cute game but don't be fooled... it's an overly-cute game with good gameplay. Basically you run an item shop so you spend half your time acquiring items and the rest trying to sell them to make a profit. The gameplay is far more deep than it appears at first glance item prices shift, certain things become more popular, you'll start learning which customers to hate because they only accept unreasonable prices (Little Girls and Dagger-wielding adventurers are the worst they never want to pay) And you will start figuring out certain customer traits (Housewives tend to haggle so if they reject a price you initially put, say the same price again and they'll give in) After a certain point customers will start bringing you stuff to sell YOU which is interesting as half the time its a nice item you can make a good profit on (but the other half the time it will be an Apple for 300 in a shop that typically moves 5,000+ value items ugh customer logic)

There is a dungeon element in which you can choose to enter a dungeon to acquire stuff that way. You have to pay an adventurer and it takes a whole day but you keep all the items you find so you make a large profit on them (assuming you don't get killed in said dungeon)

If you don't like crawling around dungeons or investing an entire day you can stock up on items at the town's market place or the Merchants guild (This is the primary way of getting stuff)

Tips going in [Light Spoilers in regards to gameplay]:
-When the price of an item "increases" (The item's name will be in red colored letters) you can EASILY sell it for 200% its base value.
-If you miss a payment you will get a gameover and the game will loop back to the 1st day however you will have EVERYTHING you had previously making the subsequent run noticably easier.
-Try to have at least 1 of each item type as customers will come in asking of a type but not specific item for you to suggest (If you have nothing they'll leave and that wastes a shopping time)
-Upgrade overall item value when you can as your loan repayment price increases rapidly each week. In other words don't keep selling 400g bows when you can buy and sell 1200g ones.
-When item prices "decrease" (the letters will become blue) you can only sell them at about 50-60% of their base price. BUT when this happens go to the Merchant guild and buy up a TON of these items as they will be cheaper there as well, then you hold those items until the price goes back up to make a good bit. (I got really lucky one day where I bought a bunch of weapons when the value decreased, then the value normalized, THEN the value INCREASED, much money was had on that day.
-And again Little Girls and Dagger-wielding Adventerers are the worst.
