About This Game

Royal Quest offers a fresh MMO experience from the creators of Space Rangers and King's Bounty series. Join Guild Wars, conquer Castles, and battle other players in unique PvPvE locations, or explore the vast world of Aura and enjoy the story on your own.

Innovative elemental battle system allows players to exploit enemy weaknesses by properly choosing what weapons and skills to use against the target, while game mechanics are easy to learn (including an automatic character upgrade system), and offer deep and challenging experience later in the game.

Download now to:
- Discover over 100 outdoor locations and multi-floor dungeons
- Complete nearly 1000 quests designed for both group and single players
- Fight more than 200 extraordinary monsters, each with its own rare card with special attributes
- Dive into extensive skills and talents system and combine them in a unique way for each of the 8 classes
- Summon pets that follow you around and collect loot for you
- Compete in PvP Arenas, Battlegrounds and Castle Sieges
- Earn the right to control a Castle and its surrounding areas with your Guild
- Gather powerful Shadow Gear by winning over other players in special PvPvE zones

Play Royal Quest now for free, or empower your hero with our special DLC packs!

Posted: 7 July
Royal Quest is just a grindfest with terrible controls disguised by cute and bright-colored asthetics. It's also pay-to-win. You can't do much without spending money in the game's store. You're limited greatly by the non-premium version of the game, too, not allowing as quick of movement between maps and getting less loot. The bag slots are in absurdly short supply, so you'll have to sell almost everything you loot from enemies just to make room for more loot.

The negative points aside, the character customization is somewhat detailed with face and hair styles, visible equipment, and suit items. You can also choose either gender for any of the four character classes, rogue, archer, warrior, and mage. I created one of each class and mostly stuck with the cute girls but gave the male mage a go and was surprised by how good the outfits and poses are. Everything just looks good.

The downside to the aesthetics is the camera doesn't tilt low enough for them to matter most of the time. You are always looking at everything from a bird's eye view. If you were thinking it would be like Torchlight, you're in for some disappointment. The movement controls also suck. Characters are slow and can't attack from a stationary position, making the long range attacks somewhat useless unless you find some kind of obstacle to lock the AI behind. That brings me back to my RuneScape days and reminds me how awesome that game was, compared to this anyway!

Posted: 6 June, 2015
Don't let the idea that you can purchase premium currency with in game gold fool you, this game is pay 2 win.

If you're high level (50+), you can earn enough gold to pay for a premium membership (or royal blessing, as it's called), but there's much more to be had with premium currency. More backspack in your tiny backpack? Pay up. Per item type (equipment, loot, potions, quest items). Would you like to revive instantly? Pay up. Fast travel for no gold? Pay up. Pet collars? Pay up. In fact, you're constantly nagged with functionality that requires you to either get a premium membership or pay a one time fee in premium currency. Constantly.

If you don't have a premium membership, you receive a lot less gold, experience and loot. Your resurrecting sickness lasts seven times longer, and much much more.

The game itself is alright. There are tons of different spells, and different classes really do feel like different classes. Some quests are unique and interesting, but most are fetch, deliver or kill quests. The PVP wasn't well thought through, and can be tedious at times. Most areas look similar, and enemies are not very unique in the way they act.

The chat is horrendous and unintuitive. This leads to very little chatter over the airways and a sort of isolated feeling, as if you're not really in an mmorpg.

Overall, the relentless pushing to get you to pay up spoils most of the fun, especially since your basic account is so limited it's hard not to feel pressured into spending money. I can't recommend it.
