Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We really believe in the Early Access process. It has consistently helped us get better at what we do.
It allows us the opportunity to more personally connect with our growing community.
We benefit greatly from the feedback from our players and use it to help grow or make fixes to our games.
Early Access allows us the opportunity to release a fully playable game to the public while still providing us the time to add more content and improve the game as well.
We also just really enjoy the chance to let players watch our games develop.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Much of that usually depends on the players feedback which oftentimes helps us to decide how much more to put into the game.
Having said that....we aim to be in Early Access no more than a year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Full version will include more enemies, more bosses, increased soundtrack, MAC and Linux builds, as well as localization for multiple languages.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently the game supports the English language with a dictionary of approx 10,000 words. It is fully playable in it's current state, with word challenges beginning at 4 letter words and ending with 7 letter words. There is a handful of bosses and multiple enemies to defeat.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We have no plans at this time to change the price during the game's transition from Early Access to full release. If we were going to say anything to just play it safe, the price once the game is fully complete may raise, but it would be an increase of no more than $1.00 US”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“That really depends on the players.
For the most part, it's about us continuing to build our community behind the games we make and allowing their feedback to help grow and shape the game into something they can always enjoy.
We are a young & growing company and the feedback & communication with our players let's us better understand what our community enjoys playing.”

Keyboard controls finalized

For keyboard users, the controls are as follows:

-Move up/down/left/right with arrow keys

About This Game

*EARLY ACCESS*
*Please See Our Discussions Board To Report Any Bugs And Read About Any Updates*
Find our High Score Contest Rules Here - http://www.foreverhumblepdx.com/no-mans-sky-contest.html

Spellblast is a genre mashup, between a Shoot 'em Up & a Word Game.

Imagine R-Type bought Boggle a few drinks on a Friday night.....some scented candles....little Netflix & Chill....
Bada Bing Bada Boom! 9 months later.....Spellblast was born.

A new take on an old genre, Spellblast mashes the best elements of Shoot 'em Ups & Word games.
Blast your way through infinite levels and hordes of enemies, to collect the necessary letters for each round.
Once collected, you must unscramble the word before time runs out....while still having to take on the enemy onslaught.
If you are successful in your task, you will face some of the baddest bosses space has to offer!
How long can you survive?

Spellblast will feature unlimited levels, more than 5,000 words in numerous languages, over 15 types of enemies, a never-ending slew of boss battles & an incredible soundtrack composed by Curt Victor Bryant of Celtic Frost fame.
Created in multiple languages and with random level & word generation, Spellblast is intended for worldwide enjoyment & excessive replayability.

We will be taking Spellblast to Early Access....for the purpose of continuously creating new bosses, adding more & more languages (we will first be addressing all languages that utilize the Latin Alphabet before moving into Cyrillic and others), optimization for all available platforms including console, adding achievements & trading cards....and well, we believe in Early Access.
We feel over the last couple years that Steam users have lost faith in Early Access games and with good reason, but we see it as a great opportunity to really connect with and grow our community.
There have been a few really great companies, like Vlambeer for example, that utilize Early Access greatly and we wish to be one of them.
Besides, we have a few extra bonuses in store for those that join us in Early Access and become part of our ever growing community of players.

So get your spacesuits on & break out the dictionary, because....
In Space....No One Can Hear You Spell!

Spellblast is a quirky combination of two genres; it's an educational word game and a weird mix of shmup and monsters (it reminded me of an old Alawar arcade game called Zak and Jack in Showdown at Monstertown. I found it difficult to even get all the letters dropped for my first few words before my lives ran out due to constantly hitting the letters, which in this game you are not allowed to do.

The premise of this game is to collect letters from the side scrolling onslaught of weird and wonderful monsters of which there are many varieties (including flying toasters, bloated boomers, velocicopter cats, mosquitomen and many other melded mosters of lore and imagination some of which I could't even start to describe). If you hit the ground, get hit by a monster, or one of its projectiles you lose a life, you also lose a life if you shoot one of your letters. Those you need to collect when they have scrolled to the far left of the playing field. Once you've filled the grid at the bottom you have to rearrange the letters into the correct word using a combination of the x key and the left and right arrow keys within a specific time. Once all of the letters have been collected you are on a timer to complete the correct word. While trying to rearrange the word, monsters continue their onslaught, so you also need to ensure you avoid incoming monsters and their weapons fire.

Trying to concentrate on rearranging the letters into the correct word in the time you have available before the timer bar is empty and at the same time avoiding the projectiles and monsters is quite difficult, especially for us males who can usually only concentrate on one thing at a time. ;) If you succeed in completing the word in the alloted time you will earn a power-up such as an extra life or a shield that once activated lasts for 20 seconds; create the wrong word or fail to make the word in time means no power-up which makes it much harder to survive the level, which is as expected the dreaded boss battle. If you fail to kill the boss you start all over again, though you can add your name to the score board. The only positive if you do fail is that levels appear to be randomly generated, so you won't see the same combination of monsters attacking you at each try. Level backgrounds change from level to level.

You only need to remember seven keys to play this game, enter, Z, all four arrow keys and the X key, all of which are explained in the brief tutorial, which on starting the game for the first time you are forced to endure.


Positives:

Excellent graphics and a fantastic variety of monsters. The ability to earn power-ups if you complete the word game before the timer bar runs out and the randomly generated levels. Finally, you really can't beat the release price which, with no discount was only Â£0.79/$0.99/E0.99/C$1.09 Of the four, Canada is significantly cheaper and is equivalet to Â£0.58 at present exchange rates. You can use either keyboard or controller.

Negatives:

Your ship can only move up or down, unfortunately there is no sideways movement, which in my opinion would have made this a better 'but slightly easier' game, there are only two music scores; a rock track when playing the main game and a hip hop track when fighting the boss, both of which really don't fit the game and became annoying after hearing the same two tracks constanly over the time I played the game. Finally I would make the tutorial voluntary rather than forcing the player to play through it before starting the game proper. Can't save progress.

How could the game be improved?

Include forward and backwards movement, add a greater variety of appropriate music and include the ability to add your own music. Enable players to save progress, or at least continue from the level they died on rather than have to start from the beginning again (that said, the randomness of the levels does counter this suggestion somewhat). Also add more power-ups and include different levels of game play, i.e. novice, average and expert players.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrv4jwaUQyo




Conclusion:

Despite the horrid soundtrack, (the rock music was okay, but hearing it over and over for a couple of hours really got on my nerves, the hip hop just didn't fit the game at all) and the lack of forwards and backwards movement, I quite enjoyed this game and would recommend it to anyone into side scrolling shooters. It was an odd combination; i.e. a side scolling shooter melded with a word game but it did work for me despite finding it really difficult to progress further than three or four levels at a time due to the difficulty level. Pity progress couldn't be saved, or when you lost all of your lives be able to restart from the level you finished on. That said, therir didn't seem to be a definite level progression in this game. High scores and beating previous scores are the main reward in this low cost game.

I got this in a bundle, so step off my Nikes.

Most frustrating 8 minutes of gameplay ever.

That doesn't make it bad, though. As a time killer, it's pretty good. The enemies look awesome and that's as far as really, really good visuals go. Otherwise, it's just not that impressive as a shooter. The overall idea of the game is great, but in execution it leaves much to be desired.

Don't get me wrong.

A good concept goes a very, very, very long way with me. Storyline goes a long way, as well. The integration of both will almost always get a game my recommended vote, but I'm on the fence on this one. It's like when the Howard Stern show was trying to get that one guy to win American Idol, but he ended up losing. The idea was funny, the execution was good, but something about it just made the whole thing anticlimactic.

Visuals

I feel the need to say something about the visuals in this game, in particular. The enemies are FREAKING AWESOME looking, and I hope that the developer reuses them in another game. They would be great in some sort of platformer spoof. This will only work if the developer really tones down the difficulty level of the game, though.

It's friggin' hard.

That whole thing about execution of an idea is what this particular part is about. Some devs seem to believe that if they can play their game, everyone should be able to play it. This developer is definitely very creative, and definitely very interesting, but they do not have a good idea of how other people will play their game. It's frustrating to play simply because it's next to impossible to get anywhere. You will die. You will die a lot. That's all fine, but this game's visuals don't justify keeping you playing for more than a few minutes at a time because you just get sick and friggin' tired of dying all the time.

How's about this, dev?

Maybe if, after collecting all the letters you need, you have until the timer runs out to solve the word puzzle. If you get it wrong, instead of losing a life, you just have to collect all the letters again instead of losing a life. The shooter element of the game is really friggin' difficult as it is. This would keep me playing longer and definitely push me to see more of what you've done here - and I sincerely want to see more of it. However, if I have to work this hard for it, I'm not going to. I play games to escape tedium and stress, not introduce it.

Still recommended!

Because it's good, and it costs less than a buck. It looks amazing - the soundtrack is alright and pretty varied. The sound effects could use some work, but they're not invasive enough to really give a turd about. This developer has a lot of potential, especially if they're this creative from the conceptual end of things. A few tweaks to the gameplay elements and this game would be playable for a few hours. As it stands, it's fun to tinker with until you feel you've gotten your dollar's worth, and then put down until the dev decides to make it playable.

Srsly, though, recommended.
