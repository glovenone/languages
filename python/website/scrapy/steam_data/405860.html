About This Game

Super Ubie Island REMIX is an adorable platformer with retro influences developed by Forbes 30 Under 30 developer Andrew Augustin.

Ubie, an adorable little alien from a distant galaxy, has crash landed on an unknown planet. There can only be one person behind this: his fiendish and decidedly less cuddly rival, Dr. Terrestrial! Now it's up to you to help Ubie get back home by fighting enemies, discovering secrets, and repairing Ubie's ship!

Inspired by Super Mario World and other classic platformers, Super Ubie Island REMIX is a game with old school roots, a full original soundtrack, adorable characters, beautiful graphics and an engaging adventure.

Features:
  • Save Ubie by overcoming difficult trials and bosses on his way back home
  • Chill out to a wide selection of remixed and revamped background tracks, as well as the original mixes
  • Uncover the secrets of Climate Island, with many hidden items, levels and characters to find
  • Explore four unique worlds with their own landscapes and inhabitants
  • Rescue lost worker bees and return them to their Queen for reward and fame

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Graphics: Any graphics card that can handle WebGL
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 300 MB available space

Super Ubie Island REMIX is a platformer made by Notion Games that was originally kickstarted in 2012 (as Super Ubi Island, but the name had to take the change because of Ubisoft).

You control a small cute alien that has crashed to a planet with classic biomes like forests, deserts, or tundras. Speaking of cute stuff, that's something the game deserves praise for - nice cutscenes (between zones) and overall sweet drawn graphics and good character animation (for a platformer). The successful presentation is also followed with funny sounds and fine soundtrack.

As for the gameplay talk, there isn't much innovation, developers followed the classic formula known since Super Mario World. Besides jumping and double-jumping, you can also glide which will be most frequently used, as level design is built a lot around it. Attacking NPCs is done by classic jumping on head. The controls are overall responsive without any lags, it was nice to play with Xbox 360 controller. (Though there are some bugs, as once I've seen webkit debug popup.)

There isn't an overwhelming amount levels and if you don't want to complete the game on 100%, don't expect many hours of playtime. Still, it'll depend on your skill, as even though the levels are short-ish, there aren't any checkpoints and since the first levels there are places that instantly kills you, making you starting the level from beginning (and losing collectable items from specific level).

Conclusion:
Super Ubie Island REMIX is not a bad platformer, but for relatively higher price it doesn't offer as much content as you could expect (in compare to Super Meat Boy, for example). It belongs to the mediocre category, but I believe the developers can do better and I'm looking forward to see their next game.

Super Ubie Island Remix is a cartoon-like platformer game that draws heavy inspiration from classic platformer games (think Mario, Sonic, etc). You control an alien who has crashed onto a planet after being attacked by an evil floating head. To fix your ship and get home you need to collect parts located at the end of each world. Each world has a short hub world that allows you to access all of the levels within.

The game handles very well with both keyboard and controller. I played through the first world with both and found the experience to be fairly comparable.

The strongest part of the game has to go to the soundtrack. This game is full of well produced music that fits it's aesthetic style very well. As you play the game you collect coins which can then later be exchanged for additional music tracks.

The game's art style is reminiscent of the bygone era of flash games. This would have been right at home on Newgrounds or Miniclip back in the day. Despite that this game certainly holds its own. It's quite a fun platformer and I've enjoyed my time with it so far.

This game definitely gives you feel of an old school platformer. The comparison between the Mario series stops at you pick up coins and it's a platformer. It is a platformer that is unique in the genre.

Super Ubie Island Remix is about an alien who is forced to land on Climate Island by Dr Terrestrial. He has to collect parts for his ship all while defeating Dr Terrestrial's demented creations. Ubie has two abilities, float with a balloon and jump up walls. Both of these abilities are extremely powerful. This game's level design will remind you of games on the Super Nintendo and Sega Genesis. Be preared to be challenged like no other platformer.

Challenge is inheriently built into the level design. What may seem like random object placement, is really hints to upcoming obstacles and skills you'll need. There is no such thing as lives or time. Take as long as you want and try as many times as you want. Exploration is built into the level design as well. Hints will be given but only cleaver thought will discover them. Take your time and explore the level. That is until.....

You beat the game. Then comes time trials!! It records how fast you went through the level. I find this element to be enjoyable. Each zone is unlocked as a player completes them. With the auto-save feature seemless built into the game, you don't need to worry if your progress will be lost. There are also other items that you need to collect to unlock different skins for Ubie. Coins are used to unlock songs on the boombox. There is a side quest from Queen Bee to collect her children bees scattered across Climate Island. There is a reward for collecting these bees, inwhich there are 3 in each level. There is also two additional different side quests in this game as well.

All in and all, this game definitely was enjoyable! I'd recommend it for anyone's library.
