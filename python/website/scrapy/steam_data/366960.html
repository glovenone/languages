Greatest game I ever done did play. Allow me to bring you point by point through my experience with this game.

I clicked play to start and the game booted up. I was met with the soothing sounds of a microwave engaging in passioante love with a hair dryer and from that moment I knew I was in for the greatest gaming experience of my life.

I looked at the menu and saw listed in the order I saw them, New Game, Loading, and Continue. You read that right dear reader, Loading. In my haste to begin my thrilling journey I clicked New Game without even CONSIDERING the possibility of what clicking Loading may do.

I was spawned in a room, dimly lit and possibly housed within the ♥♥♥♥ of Optimus Prime. After taking in my surroundings I utilized my W key to walk forward. To my delight I did in fact move in a very forwardly direction just as I had expected to do. By utilizing my mouse I was able to look around and properly observe the grey-silver room I had found myself in. The sensitivity for the mouse felt too high so I pressed the escape button to pause the game and change the options, but alas there was no options menu. That is when it dawned on me that who was I to tell the game how sensitive the mouse should be? T'was the game who dictated things and I was not to impede upon their vision!

From there I walked through several rooms, connected by short hallways, until I reached a room with three doors in it. Naturally I pressed the E button on one of the doors but I found that only caused me to lean to the right while standing in front of the door. I sytematically pressed every button on my keyboard but none of them seemed to do anything. I tried this in front of every door and none of them opened. I soon discovered that there was a door on the far side of the room which required a key. Excited to embark on my new quest to find a key, I departed back the way I came.

I searched and I searched to the best of my abilities but I was unable to locate the mysterious key. I walked around for literally minutes unable to find the key I needed to progress. I thought to myself "Is this what the game means when it says it is hard as hell? It must be." It was in that moment I realized. There is no key. This was the ultimate test. The key was truly my sanity and that was a threshold I was refused to cross willingly. Confident in my victory over the game, I closed it and came to write this review.

Writing (and reading probably) this review was more fun that SECTOR and you should never play it.

SECTOR is a FPS game created by Airys Software.
SECTOR is made with FPS creator.
"FPS Creator is an easy to use game maker. You can make a shooter in minutes and you can expand it with extra model packs."
So estimated development time: 1-3 weeks.

When I mentioned that they made SECTOR with FPS creator in steam discussions, they immediately deleted it. While threads that directly insult the developer or called his game ♥♥♥♥ remains intact (which is a good thing). Thankfully they can't delete reviews.

I promised the developer to make a walkthrough video of his game because there were no gameplay videos or let's plays of his game. And the official trailer was bad with huge ACTION! watermark on it (the trailer has been removed). So he provided me a steam key of his game.

SECTOR is a hard game. Enemies have pin-point accuracy, sometimes they get bugged and shoot rapidly which causes immediate death. SECTOR does not have auto-save, so you have to save manually. Sometimes there will be locked doors and you have to literally search every corner of the room in-order to find it, key searching will eat most of your time. SECTOR does not have any graphics options, so if you get FPS drops then tough luck. Sometimes when you load a save, you will immediately die on spawn for no reason, that means you have to use an older save. If you managed to reach as far as me (level 5) then this is your dead-end. "Crates and barrels puzzle" part cannot be completed because of the ♥♥♥♥♥♥ FPSC physics engine. In other FPSC games, they only stick to 3 crates stacking, even 3 crates stacking is buggy, but still manageable. I contacted the developer about this part hoping that he replaces it with a simple stair or elevator, here is his response:
#Discussions_QuoteBlock_Author
Hello,
First, thanks a lot !
Our team didn't find any problem with Crates and barrels puzzle, we solved all the puzzles, they are hard but not impossible, that is sure we finished the game a lot of times to test it.
Thanks."
"They are hard but not impossible"; the YouTube video proves otherwise.

I played a lot of FPS creator games and made some myself. SECTOR is entry-level FPSC game with a price tag slapped on it. SECTOR costed $5 at first, but they raised the price to $7.99 and then put it on "sale" with 10% off.

CONCLUSION: Bad buggy game made by lazy developer using easy game maker (level editor).
