About This Game

Entropy Rising

Entropy Rising is a first-person physics-based puzzle game set in a weird world filled with mysterious flying orbs and oversized measurement devices. Overcome any obstacle using the forces of gravity, electricity, fire, water and acid. If anything goes wrong, act like it didn't happen and simply travel backwards through time.

Features

  • 33 levels with increasing complexity and difficulty: Combine and manipulate gravity, electricity, fire, water and acid in order to advance.
  • Travel backwards through time anytime you want!
  • Optional Challenges: Beat level par times, find all collectibles and secret chambers, use minimal resources and get all 25 achievements.

Community: Share Your Own Levels

Entropy Rising comes with the level editor we used to make the game. Play levels created by other players or create your own levels and publish them to the Steam Workshop!

A review key was provided by the developers free of charge, but this has not, and will not, affect this review in any way.

Entropy Rising is a first person puzzle game. Playing through it you can tell it is heavily influenced by Portal and Braid, and possibly other puzzle games. I really enjoyed the puzzle aspect of this game, however I give it a cautious recommendation as it suffers from some presentation and pacing issues.

Thoughts, game play wise:
The good:
This is a first person puzzle game with several very interesting mechanics, which the game does tie in nicely together. If gravity fields and time rewinding sound fun to you, this game has them, and more! One of the main items in the games are orbs with different properties - water orbs extinguishes fire/ lava, dirt balls can be used on scales that generate power to open doors, etc. One unique thing I enjoyed was the momentum - you can grab orbs (they're heavy feeling in the game!) and roll them like bowling balls! Which means practice makes perfect and sometimes you can miss completely. Really reminded me of bowling but with huge, heavier orbs. The time travel mechanic kind of serves as a rewind/ save myself from death mechanic, and actually works as intended in this game - I haven't noticed any discrepancies so far.

There's also replay value in the form of: a 3-star rating system for time and the times you use rewind, as well as some secrets to find will be interesting to speedrunners and secret hunters.

The bad:
Here are some reasons I didn't enjoy the game as much as I'd hoped, even with the interesting mechanics and combinations of these mechanics. The pacing was somewhat strange to me, and it felt like there was no particular ordering or progression. The game does introduce mechanics one by one, but apart from the rooms/ levels getting larger, I didn't particularly feel like the puzzle difficulty was making any meaningful progression. But the fact that I have this expectation may be due to the Portal games.

Another problem I had was that for the gravity field gun, there was always a lag and it may or may not work even though I'm sure I shot the gun at a correct place theoretically. This led to me restarting some long parts of the levels from scratch, and felt like more of a chore. Also the gravity field made things so blurry sometimes. If this was improved I think I'd have a better experience.

Conclusion:
Recommended if you are a fan of pure puzzle games and enjoy playing around in large puzzle chambers, with a variety of mechanics. However the puzzles get sluggish, making some parts of the game feel like a chore. I give this game a cautious recommendation.

Entropy Rising is a first person indie puzzle game with a similar feel to Portal. It's set in a weird little world populated by flying spheres and oversized measurement devices. You follow a mysterious pink sphere through the levels and solve puzzles involving elements such as fire, water and rock (rock cools down fire, water extinguishes fire, etc) which have to be combined in the right ways and places.
One unique and pretty cool feature is the ability to "rewind time" at any moment which saves you some frustration over having fallen to death or having dropped the wrong element as you can always go back really fast.

Pros:
+ Graphics: Looks fabulous! Just check out the screenshots.
+ Difficulty: Creative and (moderately) challenging riddles
+ Controls: Well done jumping/platforming controls
+ Soundtrack: Nice and non-annoying background music

Cons:
- Story: Little of it
- Length: Rather short (as you would expect from this kind of game)

This is a quite nice physics puzzle game, if you want to get all achievements it contains jump'n'run parts, too. Nice thing is, you can always undo your actions, you don't need to reload the level or restart from a savepoint like in most first person puzzle games. Just don't expect a story to be told like in Portal, it's up to you to find out what all this might be.
The graphics is quite nice, way better than you would expect from a game set in a world of cubes and speres. Music and sounds fit perfectly and create an enjoyable athmosphere.

The puzzles are varying in type and complexity (as you would expect it, the later ones get more complex), and are neither too easy and boring nor too hard, and it seems they are created with love, even the details.

In addition, the Level(pack)editor gives you the chance to create your own levels. It is not quite the Portal2 style (not 4 points ov view in the editor and no fancy icons and pseudo 3D view), but you can "play" your level in a second window as yoou build it (it automatically reloads after every change as soon as there is a player spawn placed, without resetting the player position). And I did not find a way to crash it, in the worst case press undo and reset your player position :D. The developers already uploaded a small level pack showing posibilities on how to build puzzles in the games' style or even in a completely different style.
