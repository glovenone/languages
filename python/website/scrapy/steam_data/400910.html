In short, Rabi-Ribi is amongst the best games on Steam and it's certainly one of the best games released in the past few years.

That, of course, is assuming that you're into things like Super Metroid, Castlevania, Megaman or even Ikaruga/Touhou and the like. It's a beautiful mix of genres bundled in a thoroughly charming package. Don't be mistaken, though, this isn't really a waifu game at its core despite it having cute art and adorable character designs across the board. It's difficult and not for the faint of heart but if you can withstand a bit of a challenge you'll find yourself rewarded rather handsomely with what feels like a never-ending enjoyable experience.

Through increased difficulty levels and the free-form nature of the game (you can explore the world and fight bosses in whatever order you wish so long as you're capable of executing some more advanced movement techniques without the respective items) there's a ton of replayability. Some of the achievements require you to do what's seemingly nearly impossible which is fantastic for anyone seeking a challenge (true boss rush, no-damage boss runs, etc).

Notably, though, I have to mention that the combat isn't like any other platformers; it's actually very fluid and feels almost fighting game-esque in execution. Movement and pattern recognition is the key to success in Rabi-Ribi.

I could ramble on for hours about how I have absolutely no cons to point out about the game and how it's genuinely fantastic and well worth your time and money but I'll spare everyone from that.

Did I mention it has already gotten numerous free content updates/support from the devs? Yeah. It just can't be beaten. Support CreSpirit, GemaYue and Sekai Project and your need for a thoroughly enjoyable game in 2016 and pick Rabi-Ribi up. You won't regret it.

As much as I feel a bit mixxed with this game (and I'll explain why), it's a great, well done, creative game. Worth the price or no less than $10. Felt a hint of Paper Mario from the N64 with the badges.

The story is interesting, but I had trouble keeping track of it since most of the names aren't very easy to remember to a specific character. In fact some of the characters talk in first person and I forget who's talking at times or find myself not caring much about the story, sadly. (I would like to say I was bored of the story here and there, but everyone will assume I was in it for the breasts or whatever. Despite I have a thing for melons, that wasn't the case for this game.)

Here's the story from what I can recall:

Erina wakes up in a box in an area she doesn't recognize. She was a rabbit prior to waking up, but became human and for some unknown reason is dressed like a playboy rabbit (in terms of the clothing since the ears and tail are real). After exploring the unknown area, she wakes up back on her home island with her head on some girl's lap (who may or may not have a crush on Erina.) Erina eventually meets a frantic fairy named Ribbon, who believes she's a real bunny that eats fairies, and ends up attacking Erina in near exhaustion. After the battle, they become friends and head over to Rabi Rabi Town where they meet Erina's master (remember, Erina was an actual bunny before turning human, so think "pet owner" master). Her master, Rumi, is shocked to see her pet turned human and explains what lead up to the change, along with Bunner Lovers becoming very rampent and nearly causing an extinction of rabbits on the island, and Rumi's missing sister, Miru. From there, Erina and Ribbon set out on an adventure.

That's just the Prologue too (roughly the first hour of the game). As you progress, you'll notice some references/cameos, even from another Sekai Project game.

The gameplay just keeps me glued to the point I have to hit the panic button to summon a friend with a crowbar to pry me away from my laptop! It's that addicting and fun. Last game I played that was a metroidvania + bullet hell was Rogue Legacy (however the layouts change every death, but is a decent comparison) but that was more frustrating than this (yet both challenging).

Now this is where I start to have mix feelings. I'm fine with fan-service when it makes sense, is humorous, and/or subtle, but when it becomes abundent, even if subtle, I start to question the value of the game. Everytime I see fan-service in a game, my first thought is "Is it a poorly made game with eye candy to cover it or is the game very well made and the fan-service is just the icy to a very delicious cake?" This time, cake is no lie, but the icy does become too much that I can somewhat taste the cake and gardually become sick of the icy.

Majority of the fan-service I was comfortable with. Most of the characters, while may be sexualized, aren't TOO sexualized and feel more down-to-earth of how they appear. There's a handful that are beyond subtle, but it fits their character or the people they interact, so no real complaints. The game does recognize this that even Erina acknowledges she has no idea why she's dressed that way, with another character calling her outfit "Shameless", and another instance of one character showing boob envy of a close friend of hers. As for the fairy's clothing... well faries are faries. How they dress is really up to speculation and imagination (look at Tinker Bell) so there's really no use in throwing a fuss about that. Ribbon is about the size of an average palm anyway.

Sadly, no explanation was ever given for why Erina was dressed in such fashion, I say as I glare at the devs since Erina doesn't deserve to be dressed that way (she's WAY too innocent for that). Do expect innuendo jokes and context though. You be a fool to not expect them from this game.

Overall, don't let the fan-service deter you. It's 100% non-sexual/suggestive. It's just appealing and nothing more. Yes, I was skeptic at first, but I asked around to verify from other players to help ease my mind.
(However, if you get easily offended for seeing a girl in a bikini, some cleavage showing, and/or dressed in what's suppose to be sexual/suggestive but, in game context, isn't, well you're a kill-joy, paranoid person, or down right prude. Or maybe just a horny teen looking for content to get off on... you creep.)

There are at least two CG's that I just couldn't help but facepalm or groan in massive annoyance as it made two female characters look degrading than they should be. (You'll know once you see them and after you know the characters.)

The music I just LOVE. I WANT that soundtrack very badly!

Controls do feel odd, but will take a bit to get used to.

Now I need to mention some technical issues. My laptop surpasses the minimum system requirements, however there are isolated cases of frame dropping. I usually don't care about FPS beyond 30, but this game was programed to run smooth on 60, and if it drops to like 45 or 30 the effects clearly shows visually. Sadly there is no frame skip option, and even setting everything to the lowest settings there's still frame dropping. It's believed to be due to a certain number of sprites in one area, the background, or some unknown variable.

This is a known issue from others, not just me, and the devs are trying to find solutions for it. I'm unsure if this is recent or since release. Try not to let that deter you though as it can make boss battles easy since they're bullet hell/endurance.

Summary
Story is well made, however wonders off a bit.
Characters are each unique in their own way.
Fan-service is decent and subtle majority of the time.
Gameplay is VERY addicting and challenging.
You'll want to explore EVERYTHING and EVERYWHERE.
This is not porn.

Get it at full price or no less than $10 (any cheaper and you're hurting the devs and killing innocent rabbits.)

Front note: I wanted to fall in love with this game - I mean how can all those positive reviews tell me otherwise?

So here is the thing - Rabi-Ribi isn't bad. In fact, it is highly polished and has a lot of character and gameplay going for it. It definately does what it claims to do. Why didn't I like it? First, I didn't come for the bunny girls. Second, while everything was 'fine' there was nothing groundbreaking with either the platforming or MetroidVania elements in this game. Thirdly, it just didn't have me coming back for more. It was not addicting at all, unfortunately.

Essentially, you were a rabbit and are now a human. It is an open world setting, whereby you open new parts by gaining new abilities (ie. MetroidVania elements). There are lite RPG elements such as leveling up abilities the more you use them. I liked that part.

It is very dialogue heavy. While I understand it is a translation, the dialogue and script are really 'dumb'. We banter on timelessly about the rabbit girl meeting her 'master' and such things. It becomes tediuos. The fairy-tale story is fine, but the dialogue just goes on-and-on and interrupts the gameplay to a large extent.

Pros:
+Highly polished game with tons of content and replayability. No question.
+Great artwork, and really good music.
+Definately an accomplished MetroidVania.
+If you are really into bunny girls, this is your jam.

Cons:
+Dialogue is way too thick, and is largely meaningless. It's all pretty silly and tedious, even though I understand this is a translation.
+While the mechanics are solid, there is nothing new brought to the table - and for $18, I think there should be.

Once again, this is a very niche genre game - it very well may be for you. There is a demo on the devs website, I suggest you try that or watch some LetsPlays before taking the plunge!
