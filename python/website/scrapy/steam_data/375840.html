About This Software

Rytmik is a powerful music station allowing you not only to play with samples and musical instruments but also to mix them together, shape them and create music clips or whole songs. for your Friday night set-list.

Thanks to new features, Rytmik Ultimate takes music creation to a whole new level. Rytmik allows you to share your songs with other users through the Rytmik Cloud and can also serve as a music player for songs uploaded on Rytmik Cloud with it. On top of that, users can exchange their songs and cooperate on their composing.

Once your work is complete, you can save it in WAV format to local storage.

And thatâs not all!

Every instrument is now a wavetable synthesizer. With the advanced effect module, you have more power to shape sounds with the ADSR envelope, vibrato, portamento, noise shaper, or digital delay. In addition to that, the drawable waveform synth module allows users to draw and modify their own wave and use it as a sample oscillator â with that you can use Rytmik Ultimate as a sonically unique chiptune synthesizer.

Rytmik Ultimate comes with a sound library of more than 750 instruments containing the libraries of the previous Rytmik series (Rytmik, Retrobits, Hiphop King and World Music) and adding a brand new set of samples ranging from deep dubstep kicks and basses to cutting synth leads and special effects.

Rytmik Ultimate with Rytmik Cloud will of course include more features and additions to the popular series to enrich your experience with playing with sounds and creating music on the go.

Key Features
  • Share your songs via Rytmik Cloud
  • Save and export your music in the WAV format
  • More than 750 musical instruments
  • A drawable waveform synth module
  • Advanced effects like the ADSR Envelope, Digital Delay, Noise Shaper, Sample Offset and Loop Definition and more
  • Almost every effect parameter can be modulated via the step sequencer
  • Simple and intuitive yet powerful interface
  • Rytmik Ultimate is a powerful music station.

Rytmik Ultimate is a super easy way to make pretty good sounding music (although sometimes the quality seems a bit dull). I ported a song I made from a previous Rytmik title (Retrobits) fairly easy. I'm also excited about the ability to share music with others.

Overall, I think this is a great piece of software. Be sure to give it a try, and remember if something goes wrong/you don't really like you can always just refund it (Just also remember the refund limit is 2 hrs played / 14 days owned whichever comes first).

Rytmik Ultimate is an outstanding piece of music creation software that is powerful and flexible and at the same time extremely easy to use.
If this review is too long for you, I suggest you to only read the first paragraph about the game's content and the final verdict at the end.

What I created with Rytmik Ultimate:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yiR72fEwDEg
Please note that this was created purely for testing how Rytmik works, it's not meant to be a serious song.

What can you do with this software:
You get hundreds of pre-made samples, which you can full edit. You can also make your own samples using the instrument sounds that are all included in Rytmik Ultimate. You then put those samples together and create your own song. I am pretty certain that this is the most beginner-friendly software but at the same time it is extremely powerful. All you need is some time and a good idea.
In Rytmik Ultimate, you can create your own songs or just good-sounding loops for your game.

Pros:
  • Interface | The interface includes an enormous quantity of different functions in a really small available space. Although this might make you really confused at first, you will figure things out really fast and you're gonna love it. The colours are vivid and it really makes an impression. The funny thing is that after quite a few hours, I am still finding new features even though I could easily make a song with what I knew already.
  • Included Samples | You get hundreds of samples with your purchase and a lot of different virtual instruments, prepared to play whatever you want. What is really impressive is that everything is included without having to pay for the software, samples and everything separately. This makes buying Rytmik Ultimate a crazy good deal. Every single sample is modifiable.
  • Rytmik Cloud | There is also a dedicated in-build cloud support where you can share your songs and samples with everyone. Others can play, edit, remix and rate your creations. It's great if you're lacking inspiration or just want to see how people with more experience have done something.
  • Performance | When I showed Rytmik to my brother and told him that you can edit samples while they are being listened to, he said that this must use a lot of resources. The truth is, it doesn't. In fact, it uses a lot less resources than your everyday browser does.
  • Sample Editor | This just deserves its own paragraph. Apart from editing the existing samples, you can make your own samples with an extremely powerful editor. Every sample can include up to four tracks and while editing, you can of course mute/solo individual tracks. You can edit sample's pitch, speed and tons of other things I don't understand but I know some people will find useful.
  • Visualizer | While the newly created music is playing in the background, you can switch to the Visualizer mode and watch cool patterns. There are two visualizers included and both are pretty awesome.
  • Developer Interaction | I have to say that the developer is doing a really good job interacting with the community on Steam Discussions. Not may devs do it that way.

Cons:
  • Exporting | Don't get me wrong, you can export your songs, just the way of doing so is a bit weird. When you press Export, Rytmik asks you where to put your music file and then it starts recording. It records only what it hears, so you have to press play, wait until your song is finished and press the Export button again to stop recording. It's not a very big problem for me, but it could be for someone who did a 10+ minute song. My guess is that the Unity engine doesn't allow exporting the way we would want it, but that is purely a guess.
  • Resolution | You only have one resolution available, that is 1024x768. If you switch to full-screen mode, you will get a black border around the stretched and centered image. I did not find that problematic, because the stretching does not reduce the displayed quality.
  • OS Integration | You can create music on your 3DS and iPad, but you don't get to do that on MacOS and Linux. What a shame.

The final verdict:
Rytmik Ultimate is a really good and affordable music software that anyone doing something related to music should have. It has tons of features and includes hundreds of samples. Even if you're a complete beginner on the field of music creation, you will find using Rytmik Ultimate easy and you will have no problems realizing your musical ideas.
For what it offers, Rytmik's price is a really good deal and you should get it for yourself straight away. I can't recommend it enough.

Greetings,
BirdCute

Note that my copy of this game was obtained for free for reviewing purposes. However, this did not affect the quality and objectivity of this review.

I wanted to like this software... There are good and bad points about it.

It does remind me of Amiga software I used many years ago, but the bad unfortunately offsets the good. The EULA basically says that any songs put in cloud are owned by CINEMAX, this could legally mean that if you use the song that you make commercially, they could sue you as they own a copy. I am not saying that they "would" do this, just that the ramifications are there.

The other big negative for me is that you can't import your own audio. This limits you to the instruments provided in the software.

Also, the instruments (loops?) provided are set up on buttons for drag and drop, but they are labeled 1, 2, 3 etc which means until you click it, you are not sure what the instrument is going to sound like (is it a drum, a lead, bass line?).

I hope this review helps. the software looks pretty good and sounds good... Synthy (if that's a word)... but good.

The fact that the dev is actively upgrading the software is a good sign, they haven't just made the software and dumped it on Steam to sell whatever they can.

I hope that they keep up the good work and adding features/fixes, but unfortunately, this software isn't right for me.

I WOULD recomend this software to people who are new to writing songs as the software does all the hard work. I would not recomend it to a serious musician however.
