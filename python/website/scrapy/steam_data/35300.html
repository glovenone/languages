About This Game

Saudi Arabia, one of the world's leading oil suppliers and a key U.S. ally, has been taken over by a highly organized group of radical terrorists. The government is in a state of collapse and turmoil and you have been chosen to lead the United States' armed forces to restore control.

Search and destroy using the latest military hardware from the Abrams tank to the Apache helicopter in realistic settings ranging from the open desert to close-quarters infantry combat. Wear down enemy morale by capturing vehicles, weapons and buildings. You'll need elite forces to destroy the world's best-armed fanatics, bent on world-wide economic collapse. The soldiers who survive will gain experience and move with you from mission to mission.

Failure is not an option — This is Warfare.

Key features:

  • Fight with the latest U.S. & Russian Weapons Systems
  • Dominate real territories created from satellite images
  • Gain experience for your troops by keeping them alive
  • Capture Enemy vehicles and weaponry
  • Destroy everything in your path with realistic physics
  • Shock and Awe your opponents to wear down their morale
  • Hone your tactics in various environments from open desert to urban combat
  • 12 missions for the USA army expeditionary corps

It's great to encounter such an underrated gem!

Warfare is a slow paced RTS that tends to be realistic. The game throws you into a solo campaign (no skirmish) in Saudi Arabia in a fictional scenario where you play the US forces. Its graphics are good considered its age, no bugs so far (except that physX thing i've read).

What makes this game good is its small details, units responses are coherent (especially loving that "Sir?" when they got pinned and kindly asking WTH are you waiting for?) and immersive. The emails between mission reflects a great thinking for the "war" topic, seeing a patriotic american slowly loosing faith in a senseless war. There is also a nice soundtrack but sound effects could get a buff and voice acting is terrible.

Even if content sounds light with only a solo campaign, it should keep you busy for at least a dozen hours, a mission can take more than an hour.

Really consider Warfare if in need of some modern tactical RTS!

Underrated little gem of a RTS/ Tactics Game.

Like Military Real Time Tactical but sick of fighting WWII for the 50th time? Good modern warfare RTTs like World in Conflict/ Wargame only happen every few years so if you like those games check this out.

A good compromise between realism and fun. Unit promotion system and basic economy system to reward keeping units alive for multiple mission. Pre / Post mission unit management similar to a very simplified Theatre of War interface but without the frusturating soldier by soldier search through a platoon for a good gunner or driver.

Graphics on par with Command and Conquer Generals, which was good for the time. A lot more unit and ammo options for individual units but that is to be expected in a good Tatical Game. Unit salvage a good element that adds to the gameplay. Soundtrack good for the budget as well.

I have heard Nvidia graphics users have had problems but this game is old enough to run fine on the CPU graphics amd and intel have.

Real shame there is no demo for this game as most would be understandably reluctant to retail purchase a game from an unknown franchsise and developer that was under the radar from the day it was released. I might humbly suggest a certain bundle website.
