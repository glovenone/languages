About This Game

Race to the finish in Giana Sisters: Dream Runners, a fast-paced, head-to-head platformer for up to four players.

Run and jump, dash and smash, daze and teleport, morph and cannon… oh you get the idea: mess with your opponents!

The game pits up to four players (or AI opponents) against each other in local and online competition, speed-running for the finish in an effort to push each other off the screen with the help of boosters and power-ups.
Switch the level layout on the fly as you rush through dreamgates. Collect gems to fill your speed booster and use your power-ups to knock out the other players and claim victory!


Key features:
  • Competitive pick-up-and-play platforming game
  • Supports a party of up to four simultaneous local & online players
  • Be faster than your opponents, push them off the screen and win!
  • Use your arsenal of boosters and power-ups to blast your opponents off track
  • Nine levels to race, four characters to unlock
  • Dual soundtrack: Chris Hülsbeck along with Swedish heavy metal legend Machinae Supremacy

    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 3 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Keyboard
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows 7 / Windows 8
    • Processor: Intel Core i5 @ 2.66 GHz / AMD Phenom
    • Memory: 3 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GeForce GTX 280, ATI HD 4800 or higher
    • DirectX: Version 10
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Storage: 3 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Windows-compatible keyboard, mouse, optional controller (Xbox 360® Controller for Windows recommended)

Well, I guess i am late to write this review. Whatever, I've bought this game about 6 months ago. It was on the sale which is %50 off.

I thought the game is pretty. A platforming plus racing game and includes Giana Sisters. All the dynamics that I love in Giana Sisters Twisted Dreams. Now, I see this is an awful game for sure.

They tried to combine Giana Sister's mechanics with racing game. In fact it could be quite interesting (actually it is). I don't want to compare this game with Speedrunners that much I definetely can Speedrunners is better. It's fun and friendly. Community is huge also has steam workshop support. Dream Runners has few maps which is like 9. The game doesn't run very well. Items are not that much usable, You can host a custom game with 3 bots (and their level can be easy-medium-hard) and play all the maps but probably within 3 hours you will get bored. Since there is no players (probably sales are not good and reviews are really less) there is no multiplayer at all. The game is completely dead and the fact is, it haven't been 1 year yet.
There is a lot of con. The only pro's are musics/ost and level design as usually, no doubt.

Buy this game only if you want to play with a friend of yours. However I still do not recommend. I feel sorry for this game.

A while ago I played Giana Sisters Twisted Dreams as well as Twisted Dreams Rise of the Owlverlord and enjoyed both games quite a bit. Giana Sisters: Dream Runners shares a lot in common with those two games but is also quite a bit different. Dream Runners takes the previous Giana Sisters games and turns them into a speed running game.

I found Dream Runners to be a fun multiplayer game. The game does a good job keeping all of the races close with many races ending with players almost right on top of one another.

At times the game is a little too quick which makes it hard to see what is going on. This is especially true for the player in first place. I also encountered a couple issues with the game making some mistakes picking the winner for some of the rounds when two players were within steps of one another.

If you like speedrunners, like the Giana Sisters franchise, or like multiplayer platformers I think you will enjoy Giana Sisters: Dream Runners.
