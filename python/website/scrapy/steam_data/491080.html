About This Game

Calm farmer life is coming to an end. Fallen warriors of ancient times rose from the dead.

Help farmers to fight off raids of evil skeletons and protect their land. The only thing that will separate your territory from the skeletons - the wall. Don't let it fail. You will have bow and quiver of arrows in your starter arsenal. Be precise because one mistake could be your last.

Features:

• An exciting and intuitive gameplay.
• Great minimalistic design.
• Divided into waves structure of the game.
• Tactical Gameplay: The gameplay will test the strength of your planning skills.
• Skilled opponents: you will encounter dangerous enemies such as "bomber" that can transform your wall into a pile of trash.
• A variety of weapons: you will get different types of weapons in your hands, such as crossbow and huge cannon.
• Help in the fight: you can hire archers and gunners to confront the advancing waves of enemies.

tl;dr: All the spam-clicking of an idle game without the satisfaction.

BoneBone is a simple game. Waves of skeletons will approach your castle, gradually increasing in duration and enemy toughness. Your job is to click to fire arrows from your bow and prevent them from breaching your defenses. There's not much to the game besides spamming arrows. You can purchase two additional weapons but their cost and slow reload times make them inadequate for general use. You can hire archers and gunners for your tower and upgrade its health. That covers all the upgrades this game has to offer.

My main gripe with this game and the reason I have given it a negative review is that the developer has destroyed any replay value it might have by failing to include a wave select option. I made it to wave 20 (which was more tedious than it was fun) at which point a catapult-manning skeleton showed up with no warning whatsoever and blew my tower to pieces. I would like the option to choose to start from wave 5, 10, 15, or 20 with either the upgrades and money I had at those times or better yet, with no upgrades and the average amount of money players would have earned by that time, free to spend on different upgrades to see what works.

This is a feature I would expect from other games of this type. I'm not wasting half an hour every time I lose at wave 20 just to get back to where I was and possibly get wrecked again. Additionally, this game could be a lot less repetitive. I understand it's only $2 but Steam shouldn't be a place for newbie developers to dump MyFirstGame.exe. Add some new upgrades...a campaign...anything.

Clicker mix tower defense game. In this game, you defend your house from an endless horde of undead skeletons.

Game consists of waves of enemies, each wave getting harder. Each wave lasts exactly 60 secounds, with no difficulty option available. You start with a normal bow, clicking on enemies will shoot arrows at them, depending on their hp, may require several arrows before they die. Skeletons are your enemies, and they come in various sizes, armors and weapons, and run at different speeds at your base, so there is some timing involved to land the arrows. At later waves, there are "kamikaze" bombers and catapults (wave 20 onwards, which can launch bombs onto your base from a distance) thrown into the mix.

At the end of each wave, you get to spend the money you get from killing enemies, on either upgrading your weapon (only crossbow and gun available, and shots represent how many times you can click before waiting for reload) or base (few choices like upgrading your tower level and the hp of your wall, recruiting archers and gunners to help defend, and buying tower gun).

The whole gaming area is confined to one screen, your base on the right, and your enemies attacking from the left. Once your base fall, you lose. There are some minor changes like weather and daylight / night, but these changes are only cosmetic, and the general background setting remained the same between waves and games. Music is soothing but repetitive.

Overall, not recommended. Game gets boring and repetitive really fast, with limited replayability. There are more games on Steam, some even free, that offers more entertainment than this game.

Cons:
- same background setting
- each wave last 60 seconds, restart at wave 1 after death, which gets repetitive and boring
- limited choices of upgrades
- expensive / overpriced upgrades - gun
- long reload time on upgraded weapons - crossbow
- no explanation of the upgrades - unsure what upgrading tower level actually means

Pros:
- clicker mix tower defense concept
- trading cards

Review Excerpt:
"Bonebone is a clicker defense game where your tower is besieged by different types of skeletons and as is befitting a clicker game you try and destroy the skeletons before they are able to cross the screen. Now through this game you will encounter skeletons of varying types depicted by their running different speeds, different helmets for armor, and different sizes. So far I have encountered 5 different enemy types and I have been led to believe that’s all there is. Now as you kill skeletons you earn gold which will allow you to purchase upgrades in between levels. You are able to purchase upgrades like more ammo before you have to reload, more and different weapons if you are able to earn enough gold etcetera. In addition to that you can also purchase upgrades for your tower which add defenders and increase it’s health. All very Mobile device-esque. All that seems to be missing here are microtransactions.....

...The game just really doesn’t have much going for it. On the 4th wave the game goes through a fairly predictable major difficulty spike which one typically sees in mobile games where microtransactions or cooldown timers are involved. And I would fully expect these microtransactions to be fully in-place in the iOS App Store. However, beings they are missing from the PC port, all you are left with is a fairly expected progression system to a nearly impossible difficulty level to which you will have to play the level absolutely flawlessly and purchase exactly the right upgrades."

This game has nothing to offer anyone except casual players and is best left to the mobile platform where it belongs.
