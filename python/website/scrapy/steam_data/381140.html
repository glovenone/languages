About This Game

Cruel Arena is a classic arena survival zombie game.
Face endless hordes and survive as much as you can,
Through The Doodles and The Frames!!

Will you do the best record among your friends?





A few tips:
-Press S to buy something.
-Always, Always, Always keep yourself in movement! Learn how to move and they will never catch you!
-WASD to move, left mouse to shoot, and right one to throw a granade!
-P to PAUSE.
-That's all, get ready!

Posted: 12 June
When I decided to start this game, I was dead set on giving it a negative review, purely out of spite towards the player character's poop eating grin. However, after completing it (defined as getting all the achievements, which took only a little over an hour), I realized that would've been seriously unfair. Road Madness, the similarly priced offering I reviewed previously, provided me with some indispensable context here that made this turnaround possible.

Cruel Arena has few features (it barely has a main menu), but they are complete. There are no game-breaking bugs. The controls are tight and responsive. The combat actually works. The performance is pretty good (aside from a terrible issue when a lot of enemies die at the same time; something to do with the gore animation, I'd guess). The game is longer than a few minutes, and on top of that, has some replay value. It has decent balance and weapon design. The doodle graphics, while subpar, are legible and work well within the game's format. And above anything else, the game is surprisingly fun, even if it's unlikely anyone would play it for more than an hour.

None of the above positives can honestly be said about Road Madness. Writing this comparison (...this whole review) drove home for me how huge the variances in quality are in the rock bottom price tier - something that I occasionally need to be reminded of. I imagine the average consumer doesn't care much about these differences, same as they don't care about fifth league football results. And rightfully so, but... without them, I'd have little to write about. The bottom line is, you can do much worse than this for a euro.

After getting all achievements in this game in the short time I played it for, I cannot recommend it.

For a start, it is short, there's not much point to it but survive. The game progresses so fast that there is no point in buying any weapon but the strongest (which you get fairly quick). The graphics are nice, but quickly become bland and boring. There are 4 enemy types, each with different 'abilities', and different damage required to kill (Unless the saw gun, everything is 1 shot kill). The main issue I had with this game is how it lags, each enemy produces a blood splat when killed (up to the demon enemy, which is lots of blood and an explosion), the problem with this is, if you kill too many enemies in succession (which it rapidly spawns) in a small area, the game laggs up and becomes unplayable.

All in all it's not bad for a flash game, where it should be, but by far from being a good one. Save your money and play a flash game instead, it will probably keep you occupied for longer.
