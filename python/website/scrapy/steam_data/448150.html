Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Mystica is an MMO or ORPG that is very community and player centric. So we feel that we should be able to make changes that the community wants to see in the game, changes that will improve the player experience and makes the game a more comfortable and player driven experience. What better way to do just that then with an open beta on Steam where players can find the game, give it a shot, then start suggesting new features and updates! The more people giving their input the better!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Mystica will stay in Early Access until both the world is flushed out enough to stand on its own and when the player experience is up to par.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Mystica's core game-play is not planned to change that much. We plan to massively expand the world, number of NPCs, quests, items, etc. While we are growing the game quickly, it's still relatively small compared to other MMOs. We will also be adjusting the game according to the player feedback, it's hard for us to predict exactly what that will entail but most likely those changes will be mostly mechanical changes and not visible changes. As far as the various Steam systems that can be implemented such as Achievements and controller support are not in the game yet however as we assess what can feasibly support we plan to support as many of these systems as we can.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Mystica is currently in Beta, the client and server are currently very stable with minimal crashing and game breaking bugs. Most of the changes that need to be made are on the world and adjusting the mechanics so the players enjoy the game as much as possible.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We are launching the game at a low price point with the intention to slowly raise the price as the game get closer to a main release.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Not only will we be monitoring all of our feedback from our website, forum, and Steam discussion boards but we will put many more hours into the game just talking with players and getting their input and trying to correct the game for many of the issues that the players find. The more players that we get mentioning an issue or a similar issue the faster we will endeavor to fix that issue.”

About This Game

Mystica is a 2D MMORPG with minimal focus on a primary story. We wanted to provide an awesome MMO expirience but we wanted players to be able to explore the world at their own pace and to discover the games secrets and lore on their own. As the game is completed there will be large overarching quests built but they will be purely optional with nothing forced on the players. We want to encourage player interaction and socialization in order to build a solid community and world for both new and old players alike.

The game is far from finished but it is very much still a work in progress with near constant additions. We started work on Mystica with the hope of giving fans of 2D mmorpgs a solid game to enjoy, where they can hang out, go on adventures, and maybe make some new friends. Some of our best memories as a child are from all the hours we put into games like Maplestory and Endless Online. We feel that a simpler game allows for a better community of players. As we build up the game we hope to build story through lore and narrative. Less "get quests" and more of an open ended story that you can choose to take part in.

Features

  • Hundreds of items of collect
  • Hours of game-play just exploring the world
  • Familiar retro-style game-play
  • Complex and interesting lore
  • Awesome community of players

Basic Information
Title: Mystica: The Ninth Society
Genre: Indie, Massively Multiplayer, RPG, Early Access
Developer: Bread Vision Studios LLC.
Publisher: Bread Vision Studios LLC.
Release Date: 7 Mar, 2016

Overview
Mystica: The Ninth Society is a MMO with a small community at the moment. It seems that this game has a lot of potential and can be really good in the future. The game at the moment has no story, therefore you are just wondering around aimlessly in the overworld killing enemies.

Gameplay
Okay so let's start off with the gameplay of this game. There really isn't much to it, it's a MMO which (at the time of writing this review) has a small community that comes onto the game regulary. The game starts off by having to register, and then pick between 3 classes: Warrior, Wizard or a Villager. Now to me this isn't a very interesting set of classes, the thought of having a villager as a class doesn't sound very appealing against the Warrior and Wizard class. I personally chose the Wizard, because long ranged attacks that the Warrior shouldn't really have. The game starts you off in a small village, there isn't much there at the moment, one of the guys talks about peopel that might try to scam you for you're account (because you have register to play this game) and the other talks about skill points, how to attack and the what each of the stat points help with the characters progression. The tutorial however doesn't seem to tell you how to equip items onto your character. Although it's pretty simple (just double click) it seems that it should at least be mentioned to help the player along. The game to me brought back a nostalgia feel to Zelda, but I soon found that this game doesn't play at all like Zelda. Attacking enemies is very dull, you stand next to the enemie and hold down ctrl to attack the enemie. You take it in turns to attack (I think) and then either you die or the enemie dies. If you die you lose X amount of exp and then get teleported back to the start. If you kill a enemies you get set amount of exp and the possible chance of the enemies dropping a weapon or gold. This sort of fighting mechanic isn't very fun to play, right from the start it sounds like a grind for exp until you can proceed to the next area. Talking about enemies, there is really not much varation of enemies. There doesn't seem to be any mini-bosses or anything other than enemies that are constantly spawning in certain areas of the map. The game requires A LOT OF GRINDING, I wouldn't suggest choosing the wizard. You don't get a spell until you gather enough money (grinding) by killing more enemies (more grinding) and hoping that the enemie drops X amount of gold (Again more grinding). This game just feels like a grind fest, the enemies are unbalanced when it comes to the levels, and some of the areas are too high leveled to be able to kill them. The controls feel clunky, and you are restricted to only squares to walk on, so no walking in the upper left if you get what I mean. The only good thing about thid game that can think of is that there isn't any microtransactions... yet. I hope that they don't add any that makes you pay for coins or something like that because that will ruin the game completey.

Art Style
Mystica: The Ninth Society, the art style are 16 bit graphics and are kind of fitting for the type of game this is. The tiles seem to fit in with the 16 bit kind of art style. I seem to remember the sprite that they are using in other games... except recoloured. There is minimum frames to each of the characters and enemies, the battling doesn't even have a animation, just a red cross or whatever you are using as a weapon that is across the enemies you are attacking. I would like to see some walking animations from each of the characters, instead of it looking like it's sliding across the floor.

Conclusion
The game itself isn't worth buying at this moment in time. There is still a lot of work that needs to be done by the developers before I suggest buying this game for £1.59 at this current state. I really want to like this game, but it just feels too grindy, maybe that's just the Wizard set maybe the Warrior set is much easier. I really hope that the developers pull this game up and make it really good.

This game is brilliant.


Pros:

-The price point is extremely accessible
-There are no microtransactions
-The gameplay is very simplistic and somewhat laid back in a good way. There is a classic charm to just earning loot and new types of spells, leveling, choosing your stats, and exploring new areas with friends in typical classic rpg fashion with no bull.
-On that last note, this is an absolute must for co-op, collaborating and discovering the fun with others is half the game in my opinion, and this is entirely possible to do since the game is so accessible and cheap, plus this allows for way more strategies in a fight if you are using different classes.
-The community is friendly and its entirely possible to just launch the game to have a laid back conversation about anything in the chat.
-All classes can use all the spells. There are no restrictions, just different leveling bonuses. (I.e mages level faster if they use their spells, fighters level faster if they use their swords)
-There are hats and cosmetics, you can work up gold in game to buy entirely new sprites, ranging from typical class based costumes to being a giant ice cube. Once again, there are no microtransactions and the grind seems pretty fair with more difficult areas allowing you to earn progressively more gold. Enough to make the idea of getting new cosmetics realistic.
-The devs are extremelly friendly and dilligent, I had issues with the standalone version of the game and they immediately contacted me in the game itself and resolved the issue almost immediately. Plus they are really fun guys to hang out with. (I.e they don't have a PR stick rammed up their ♥♥♥.)


So long as you like the genre and don't mind the style of combat there is nothing bad to say about this game. The only serious improvement I would personally make is to allow players to hit Ctrl once and have your character auto attack as opposed to having to hold the button throughout a fight. That and it would be nice if gold was shared in parties.

Buy it. If you like the genre, and the idea of a laid back simplistic fun game that doesn't try to rob you with microtransactions, with a great community and co-op focus you have literally no reason not to. It's cheap charming and really deserves recognition and your financial support.

Early Access Review
Posted: 8 March
As I am to point out: Yes, I'm aware I've put 'no' for recommendations... but this game has promise, not a very strong promise. But nonetheless it does have promise. The reason I don't recommend it; Well, the reasons I don't recommend it are because the devs shouldn't be charging (even at it's very low and affordable price) for bog-standard alpha/beta testing.

The game currently consists of a few dozen maps (or tiles, each to their own) and a somewhat large variety of enemies. But nothing that stands out like a mini-boss or any type of boss character, with a few areas seemingly being pointless to explore, or to even grind in due to terrible enemy types, or locating.. or the plain fact that they're just giant dead ends of nothingness. To which no rhyme nor reason are there for them to be there. I do understand the devs are planning to add to the game. ...but even as an early access game, a lot of this stuff should be there.

There's currently 3 characters (Warrior/Wizard/Villager). And the Warrior seems spot on with it's weapons, stats, damage, health all being good in my eyes. And the game (for what little there is) works wonders. However the Wizard is another story. In it's current state it isn't even usable, with most players rolling melee stats and weapons in order to level it - with hopes it is balanced later on. The list of problems are endless with the class so I won't go in to detail. Just to note, what I did in 5hrs with the Wizard I managed to do in 20 minutes with the Warrior - I hope this does indeed clarify, even without details, how awkward this is for the games current standing and it's players. I have yet to try the Villager class, but as weapons and stats aren't character specific. Nor are skills... I've really tried and tested all skills and weapons that there is to offer - and the character choice at the beginning is to set you up with a free skill if you choice Wizard, and to pre-set your Sprite (which can be changed later with in-game gold).

I've spent the past 15hrs farming... literally just holding ctrl and moving my direction keys in order to whack the crap out of any and all that may lay in my path. Collecting gold, and items to sell in order to upgrade my gear... to see the vast lands of the game, and to go every place and kill everything has been my goal. But there's huge things missing. Like flavour text, to which there's none or not enough. That lack of NPCs that not only have something to offer (like maybe a quest, God forbid that should ever happen) but even to give passing comments when you go over and interact with them. Not enough lands, features that are visibly there but in-complete or quarantined off. It's a bit of a nightmare.

The game comprises of spawning in, harassing NPCs, grinding and upgrading and working out the grind route before you hit level 25-30 and what is the end-game area of the game currently with a few 'secrets' laid here and there. The drop rates are so-so which makes the grind real, in terms of hoping for loot. Experience, however, is a b*tch to grind for by conventional grinding. Especially when it took the best part of 10hrs to hit level 29... and all I did was hardcore farm with no more than 2 drink breaks. There's no real need for partying or co-op play unless you've taken the magic route and need to be carried or the higher level guys in the area are starving the farms for experience. PvP is limited to being within a single level (+/-) of another player. To which your demise leads to you losing an item from your inventory, and there are no areas or ways to have a consentual duel as of yet.

The controls are awkward - again, the mage route needing you to use 3 hands (mouse for aiming, ctrl/shift for movement and melee attack. WASD or direction keys for movement and F1-12 for pre-set abilities). Sadly there's no way to remap the keybindings, though I have reported this - and many other issues - to devs who say they're openly welcome to ideas and bug reports. I do enjoy that encouragement, but I do find a lot of issues with the game and the state it's been released in to be, frankly, unforgivable - although I am in an exceptionally nice mood at the moment, as the game - though leaving me unfulfilled - has left me on a high note. And I'm not making this review to tarnish their name or to deter people from buying the product, generally. I'm just trying to be honest, and calling the game and the devs out on the subsequent release of a product that still needs a lot of work, and using 'Early Access' as an excuse for it's current state. I understand expansion, and tweaking - but there's a lot of overhauling, adding and subtracting, etc. Too much to mention which I personally feel is unacceptable, even at the £0.95 it cost me.

I do really like the idea of a 16bit MMORPG. I've never known or had the chance to play one. And the base concept, the idea of taking one of the dime-a-dozen RPGMaker games and making it sociable and adding in, if only minor, elements of an MMO(RPG) is quite simply genius. I'm very fond of the idea: I like to be sociable. I like co-op games. I love RPGs of all varieties. This to me, on paper, is perfect BUT the game as it currently stands is a bit of a joke. And I really do hope they devs work their asses off and get it up to a more in-depth, diverse, and fully working level of it's development ASAP. My final score for this game is 3/10. I'm giving it's due for the potential it has, it's initial concept and - as it is - it's got a good, solid, start. But it needs a lot of work - so much so, I feel that even the beginning 25-30 levels of grind need to be reworked for any additional features (like quests, hunts, etc). Though these are ideas that AREN'T promised, planned or even mentioned by the devs, as far as I'm aware. They're more of my expectations. And, with a bit of luck, hopefully they may come to fruition.

Final note: I will stick with this game, checking back regularly and waiting upon updates and the like and will re-review at a later stage. I do hope that the devs will do this game some justice, and I'll finally be able to be fulfilled. So much so that I can up my score, and finally give it a positive recommendation.
