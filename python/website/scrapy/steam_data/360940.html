About This Game

Take part of history’s greatest battle of Green VS. Tan. Fight amongst and against others online. Jump, shoot and roll your way into victory with fast paced objective based gameplay. Sometimes the greatest battles are fought by the smallest of soldiers.

The Mean Greens is a 3rd-person shooter played from the perspective of toy army soldiers. Match up with others online for some friendly multiplayer competition. Enjoy a huge battle of up to 10 players in each environmentally unique map.

  • 10 Modes
  • 10 Maps
  • 6 Weapons
  • 5v5 Multiplayer Battles
  • Full Controller Support
  • Voice Chat
  • Text Chat
  • Party Matchmaking
  • Dedicated Servers

  • What does a bathtub, sunken boats, rubber ducks and a giant octopus have in common? The Mean Greens Bathtub Bash that’s what! Hop onto the floating rubber ducks and ride to the sunken ships in the middle of the map. Avoid crossfire, grab a flag off of the ship, and carry it to your base. The team with the most flags at the end of the round wins.


  • It’s game night! As The Mean Greens battle against each other in an all-out paper-craft war, more than dice will roll across this table. Go head-to-head as tan and green teams fight for control of the city. The objective is simple, be the first team to reach 30 kills.


  • Even a beautiful aquatic adventure has its dangers. Run over aquarium décor and capture flags in sunken ruins as you square off in an underwater playground. Fight your way across the duel bridges to steal the flag that sits in the sunken temples.
    This map puts a whole new twist on the traditional capture the flag mode with its “Low – gravity” action and captivating nautical theme.


  • It’s full on war to conquer the countertop in this decked out kitchen! Push the other team’s cup off the counter and destroy their only way to zipline into your territory.


  • The mood is a bit chilly as you fight your way through this food-filled freezer. Everyone melts the ice cube in the center to get to the lower level, and then it’s a mad rush as you try to free your frozen dinosaur before the opposing team releases theirs.


  • This train is leaving the station with or without you! Enjoy this Christmas themed level while you travel over holiday sweets to claim your capture points on this nostalgic toy train. It’s tug-of-war, as both tan and green teams battle back and forth to maintain control of all three capture points.


  • This farm style soccer match is a new take on Foosball that you’ve never experienced before. Shoot, blast, and push the ball into the enemies’ barn to score the most points. Don’t drop the ball and watch out for the moving 3-rod pigs and cows.


  • Got game? Explore a colorful world on top of chessboards, boxes, and racetracks. Hold the center of the map in this traditional King of the Hill game mode. Just don’t get hit by the other team or the racing cars.


  • It’s birthday time, but the other team is full of party-poopers that don’t want the festivities to begin. Fight your way up the cake and start the celebration by lighting all the candles on each layer. The attacking team must jump from the gelatin launch pad and onto the multi-layered cake to light each candle. The defending team must fend off the other team’s attempts to light each wick until time is up.


  • Turn the tables on your opponent in this death match. Be as crafty as you can to survive and mark your territory. Perfect the art of combat and chalk up those points to make it into the Champions Circle!

The Mean Greens - Plastic Warfare is an outstanding and fun game that brought back the nostalgic days of playing Army Men: Sarge's Heroes on the Nintendo 64 against friends/family. The gameplay is sometimes fast paced and the controls are outstanding and polished. The maps are graphically beautiful and are well designed for each objective. The game has not been abandoned and new content such as maps and vehicles are being added, according to the devs, for FREE! Why buy a game when all you can do is play by yourself or keep refreshing waiting for a regional server to become available. I know what you're asking, is it like the Army Men games?

Well, not 100% quite. It is a good game, however. It's like the Army Men series, but a bit faster paced. There is no single player, there is no bots, and there isn't weapon pick ups or drops or the likes as far as I currently can tell. You start out with all the weapons and they're poorly balanced.

The shotgun really out shines the main rifle, or all the weapons really. The flame thrower is a tad underwhelming. This balance can really ruin the game for some players. I also find the rocket launcher a bit too powerful as well, but it isn't like you have a whole lot of shots with it, so it isn't a strong complaint from me.

Though you will not get 5v5 games, I did just finish a 3v3 game. I do not understand how this game got so many negative reviews for being dead when there is plenty of games being hosted occupied by players.

Pros:
Nostalgia exploitation but satisfying
Good fast gameplay, easy to pick up and learn, good game to play every once in awhile with friends
Well made maps, all good looking
game modes creative and enjoyable, very refreshing, though some feel slightly similar to existing ones.
This game is still worked on frequent enough to not feel alarmed about the investment.

cons:
Dreadful balance system.
No auto balance, no option to join losing team in game, cannot switch teams when "game is about to start."
No single player, no campaign, no bots, nothing beyond PvP.
The crosshair is not perfectly reliable when used for aiming during situations where you might be behind cover; this is a common problem for other third person shooters as wel, consider this a nitpick.



Overall, I say 7.5/10 is deserved. Good game, buy it if you got a solid group of friends.

If the game were to ever develop bots and AI, this game has A LOT of potential to be a must have game for a group of friends. Whether it was to make a good campaign/coop mode, or perhaps a mode where all players fight a giant toy dinosaur or such, the potential is endless. The developers are currently looking into AI, but the results or implementation remains a mystery as of now.

This game definitely needs more people.

The Mean Greens - Plastic Warfare harkens back to a time when games had higher standards, a time that lived its' last days perhaps a decade ago or so.
When you get this game, you'll see that everything's in there already. Very few modifications or fixes need to be done. It has that rare combination of fun gameplay, fine graphics, clever map concepts, and epic soundtrack (the soundtrack is extremely catchy and was composed by three different people, which brings a harmonious variety of music genres into the fold). This is a stark contrast to the current blockbuster games which attract large numbers of buyers whilst [initially] containing very limited content.

The major curse that has befallen this game is precisely one of numbers. Namely, too few people are playing it. This makes most parties pretty much empty. And yet, the game is still enjoyable enough to be played in a 3v3 scuffle. I see that most buyers have resorted to handing out negative reviews because of that, and this is hardly fair in my book. The devs should consider introducing bots to keep the game on its' toes for what it's worth. Running With Rifles does that, so why shouldn't they?

What can I say? This game surely stands out in several ways. I wouldn't necessarily say that it is an underrated game, but it cruelly suffers from undeserved neglect instead.

If you enjoyed Toy Soldiers, then you'll definitely enjoy The Mean Greens.
