About This Game

A magic world, epic heroes, bright graphics, excellent humor and absorbing gameplay – all that in the adventure fighting game “Fairy Tales: Three Heroes”!
Everyone listened to fairy tales about brave epic heroes in his childhood. Everyone dreamt of joining battle with terrible ugly monsters. Everyone wished himself to be a magic hero…

And now you can bring back the memory of your childhood as there are three real heroes ready to cleanse Slavic lands from Monsters once and for all!

Enemies have surrounded Slavic lands, the Horde is pressing hard from the south, Teutons and Lithuanians are besieging Novgorod from the west and, they say, a sinister Monster is coming from the east! And again three epic friends have to unsheathe their sword, lance and bow!

Nevertheless, before dispersing foreign enemies, they ought to put their native lands in order, as footpads have thrown aside all restraint, starting to romp in broad daylight. But soon they will be defeated, right in the First Part of “Fairy Tales: Three Heroes”!


“Fairy Tales: Three Heroes” is a Fighting Adventure based on Slavic eposes and characters.

The key game features are bright graphics and easy-to-learn gameplay, ingame physics, directly affecting the gameplay, and numerous animation videos, vivid characters and, certainly, humor.

Main heroes are three Slavic epical characters. During the mission gamer controls one of them, while others follow him. At any time gamer can switch to another hero. Each one has unique skills which are to apply during the game depending on a certain task. Heroes improve their skills and learn new special holds. The heroes’ armory rises gradually as well.

How about instead of making a review, I just tell you my game-experience:

As you start you notice the broken english and horrid VO right away. It attempts to be funny, but gets horribly down due to that (and the gameplay, but we get to that later).
You start as a bowman, with a bow. You know... those weapons where you almost have to crawl into somebody to hit anything? Yeah, I didn't know bows required that either, but you do here. Gameplay pretty much consists of walking backwards and killing things point blank.
Then I got a few quests. I found someone who wanted 2 guards to ambush a bandit camp. After finding the guards I also found the camp and killed them all. No "camp is already slayed" dialogue, nope, all enemies just respawned instead. One of the enemies is a bowman, who also uses hit-and-run and has as horrid an accuracy as you do. So the best way to fight them without having to run the entire map is find an open spot (since pretty much everything can intercept your arrows) and then just wham away at each other for about a minute till the other is dead (since they can't hit you either, *and* your hp regenerates).
Killing it it dropped a +55 damage bow... sweet. Except it also reduces speed by 30. The default speed is... uhm... slugh, and there's no sprinting. NO, WHY? WHY? Why would you want us to go even slower? I want faster, not slower dammit... well, guess it's keeping to killing slowly too but atleast walking slow, just not uber-slow.
There also was another quest to gather 3 parts to get a better bow, but the questgiver just said he would "meet me later"... guess what... nothing at all.

Cue area 2.

Here you get a melee guy. Not much different since you pretty much meleed already but atleast he has AoE and a decent attack speed, making it all the less annoying to play. Weren't it that melee hits interrupt animations (including attacks and specials) so most of your hits get interrupted, and using your special is pretty much not done unless they're chasing down your AI ally who's fleeing again like a bowman all over the map... ugh.
This area starts with sheer horror as you need to kill 4 bowman in towers. Behind some kind of wooden shield. Yup, have fun going 2 minutes each to kill them just missing 90% of your shots.
So learning about the bandit camp I went in, bad move, instant gameover. Didn't save, had to re-do archers... NOOOO. Anyway if you want to play this game, save often, you never know when you just get insta-game-overs.
Finding the appropriate spots, running so sloooowly around, murdered another full camp, had to jump over water... missed. You guessed it, insta-game over. Second time it worked but upon returning it triggered a cutscene and my char was stuck in the ground halfway during it. No biggie I thought, nope. Insta-game-over.
Succeeding, you need to walk to point X, then ALL THE WAY around the camp (soooooo sloooooow) to point Y, then X again (WHYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYY?). Supposedly I was supposed to force my AI to stay there but I didn't so the cutscenes got really funny with a non-existant talker, and in the end my archer just was amongst the enemies chilling they did nothing when he shot all lumber bars ontop himself too, but he survived while all around him died. Did I mention this game is buggy?
The villagers were happy, they were saved, they wanted me to kill a stronghold. Wait, the one before? Yup, indeed. Again, everything just respawned once more, aside from 2 tower guards (thank god with how bow gameplay works).
After that area was over besides 2 sidequests which required me to jump water again and walk alot (failed once, insta-gameover) and one that forces you to hunt bunnies around the whole map (did I mention a lot of slllllllllllllllow walking).

The rewards? More weapons with speed penalties! AAAAAAAAAaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaargh.
Why? Why? WHY?
This game could be fun, the humour is there, but the horrid gameplay, the slowness (even without -slow weaponry, and ALL weaponry has that), bad translation and even worse VO completely wreck it's neck. Let's start by removing -slow from weapons devs, please?
