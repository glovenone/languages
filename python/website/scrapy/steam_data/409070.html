About This Game

Part man, part blade, all badass – he's Fist Slash, the ultimate action hero!

The evil Dr. X has a diabolical master plan: open the gates to the mysterious 'other side' using five shattered pieces of the all-powerful mirror! If he succeeds, who knows what might happen to Earth… and only Fist Slash can stop him! Explore six challenging worlds, each packed with vicious enemies and devious platforming obstacles, to grab the mirror shards before Dr. X. Collect awesome power-ups to take out his never-ending army and face huge bosses unlike anything you've ever seen. Do you have what it takes to defeat Dr. X and conquer the game on the dreaded Holy Gaiden difficulty?

  • Authentic retro platforming action harking back to the golden age of video games!
  • Six worlds to discover, each split into ten unique levels full of enemies and obstacles
  • Huge bosses that stand in your way of the five mystical mirror shards
  • Difficulty levels scaling from Easy to Holy Gaiden – the ultimate challenge for any would-be gaming hero!

A lot better than Jet Gunner. Jet Gunner was a good concept ruined by a horrible engine that was ill-suited for what the game was trying to do. Unfortunately Fist Slash is using the same engine and thusl suffers the same hit-detection issues (♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ spikes and platforming! huge hitboxes/hurtboxes) and overall glitchy-performance (had stuff despawn by hitting the ceiling, was able to squeeze and get stuck in spaces that where only half my height) - but the game is designed more in line with the engines weaknesses - so it's a lot less frustrating as Jet Gunner was on higher difficulties.

The game is more or less a rather playable Ninja Gaiden clone suffering from a few insane difficulty spikes and unfair situations. The majority of the stages are just trivial though - with no real challenge. The autoscroller-sections are terribly slow and most bosses are really simplistic - with the exception of the semi-final boss that has way too many random patterns that can be hard to react to or decide to screw you over right from the get-go. Frustrating. Doubly so if the bosses have flight and you have no means of hitting them until RNG decides they descend on their own and get into range or you bring the appropriate weapon.

Presentation is okayish - the game is a bit garish colorwise and has some really busy backgrounds, making it difficult to tell background from foreground at times. Music has a habit of becoming annoying if you get stuck on a stage replaying the same ♥♥♥♥ over and over again. Controls suffer from not being rebindable at the moment and you being stuck with analogue-stick if you are using a pad, which isn't making fighting the hit-detection any easier.

Even being a huge improvement over Jet Gunner, I just don't feel this game is good enough to recommend to any retro-enthusiast that has access to the games by Joymasher or Crazy Viking here on Steam or any proper 8bit to 16bit Action-Platformer on actual old hardware.
