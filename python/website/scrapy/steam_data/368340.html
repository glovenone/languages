Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“An extensive demo of CrossCode was released recently and we’re currently working full-time to complete the game. For players, Early Access is an opportunity to get access to new content as early as possible and to further support development of CrossCode. For us, it’s also a great source of feedback.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Early Access will continue until the game is completed. Until then, we plan to release updates on a regular basis (usually around every two month). We target a final release for beginning 2017.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full game will complete the story of CrossCode with a playtime of about 15 hours. The first updates of the Early Access version will continue the story of the former demo, while later updates will focus on gameplay components, introducing new areas, enemies, dungeons and puzzles. In order to deliver the story in the most effective way, we plan to release the final part of the plot - including the major twists and its conclusion - with the final version of the game.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current version comes with most core mechanics completed and polished, including movement, physics, combat, puzzles, menu systems, NPC etc. It also features the beginning of the story with a playtime of about half an hour, a short puzzle and an exploration mode where you can spend several hours searching treasures, bashing enemies and leveling your character! Future versions will mostly focus on extending the available content.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price for the early access version might be reduced once we get closer to the release.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The community can give us feedback to any available content of the game, such as enemies, attack skills, puzzles and plot developments. We will use this feedback to create a well balanced and fun experience for as many players as possible. We may also consider suggestions for new enemy types, skills, equipment among other things.”

CrossCode 0.7 Release!



Here we go! Today we release CrossCode version 0.7.0 that will finally extend the story, include the often requested Trader Book and comes with a couple new quest. And finally: the first wave of new character portraits!

New Portraits and Character Graphics
As previously announced we plan to rework all the portraits of the game for a new polished style. With 0.7 we introduce the first wave of new portraits for the majority of characters including Lea, Shizuka, Sergey, Carla, Emilie and Schneider. Here some examples:






In addition to that, we also improved many other large-scale sprites of Lea including the title screen and menu:





Extended Story

The story was extended quite a bit by about 1 1/2 chapters. The plot now includes the visit of the Temple Mine and end at your arrival at Ba'kii Kum. Along the way there will be several small excursions as you'll meet new players... and a guild! Here a couple of impressions:









Finally Emilie will also be more talkative around Bergen Trail and Village, commenting on enemies and also locations:



Trader Book

Many of you requested a feature to track all the traders in the game. We listened and with 0.7 we finally introduce: The Trader Book.



The Trader Book will list all the traders you meet on your adventure, sorted by location. You'll be able to see all the items they offer as well as all the items you need for the exchange.

More Quests

Version 0.7.0 comes with a couple new quests as well. One of them is connected to the story, others are optional. Make sure to visit all the faction buildings! Also these are not just your generic kill and fetch quests. This screenshot should give you an idea what to expect:



... or maybe you just wonder what the heck is going on there. Anyway, just try out the quest!

Other Stuff

As usual, the release comes with a couple of bug fixes. An important one: we switched to a new version of NW.js for Linux and Mac users. Hopefully this will remove the crashing problems of the 0.6.x versions!

In addition to that, you will find the first interior maps in Ba'kii Kum, new lore entries, an improved blue hedgehag, and an all new background theme for Faj'ro Temple (which will still be improved, but anyway)!

Next Steps

With the story continued we'll now focus on content again. Our goal for version 0.8 is the new Jungle area. Since this update will be about the size of 0.6 we might need a bit more time again. But that also means you can expect a buckload of content once it's done!

And that's all! We all hope you'll have a fun time with the new release! Also: if you find bugs, simply tell us. It's a major release and most of you know how it goes~

Until 0.7.1!

About This Game

This retro-inspired 2D Action RPG might outright surprise you. CrossCode combines 16-bit SNES-style graphics with butter-smooth physics, a fast-paced combat system, and engaging puzzle mechanics, served with a gripping sci-fi story.

CrossCode is all about how it plays! That's why there is a free Steam demo! Go give it a try!

Take the best out of two popular genres, find a good balance between them and make a great game. That’s what CrossCode does. And it works pretty well. You get the puzzles of Zelda-esque dungeons and are rewarded with the great variety of equipment you know and love from RPGs. During the fast-paced battles you will use the tools you find on your journey to reveal and exploit the enemies' weaknesses and at the same time will be able to choose equipment and skills for a more in-depth approach in fighting your enemies.

Check the Reviews to see who loves the game. We're sure you will, too!

TL;DR

PRO
Plot
Fast Paced Action Combat System, focused on dodging and good aim
Rewarding grinding and farming
Enemy AI will kill you if you spam balls, stand still or try to run away
Metagame (game within the game) atmosphere (take that, Shakespeare)
Good characters , drawings and dialogs
The pixel art is reeeeally good, lots of expression for each character, really well done, and the music still makes me think I'm playing a fantasy MMORPG
Parkour
Mad html5 programming SkillZ m8
Emilienator

CONS
Lack of personalization (everything is plot-bound)
The shop/craft system can be confusing at first (cause the big number of recipes and ingredients)
but you'll eventually understand everything and can keep track of every trader in the trader book
I hate rabbits now

Do you kwnow that feeling when you acquire some games in a bundle and you treat them as "collaterals" because you just want one or two specific games? I'm very glad that CrossCode came to me this way
At start I thought it was the usual rpgmaker 2k jrpg on steam (2 the moon, I'm not talking about you, stop fussing), but that's not the case... the game engine is developed in html5, there are no turn based fights, but evrything is done real time, it's a really fast paced action rpg, but if you're looking for the usual button masher, this is not the right stuff for you: every fight, even against the most harmless looking one, will have to be planned beforehand.

But let's start talking about the plot: I have yet to reach a far point, but the story gets me in a good way; you impersonate a game avatar, Lea, who has lost her memory and she's searcing for it a game universe set on an island in the real world.
The only thing that so far I didn't like is the class of your character, which is bound by the plot for some reason (I have yet to discover it). Lea is a spheromancer, who is able to rapidly cast projectiles while rolling away to safety, or comboing with a quite strong melee attack, a shield and a large number of skills that you acquire by spending cp in your skill tree.
The skill tree is not that difficult you have to choose between some basic passive feats (increased damage, defense, elemental resistance...) and active skill, like a stong projectile that can hit multiple enemies, some dagame reduction buffs, melee attacks... you can quite personalize your fighting style.

Unfortunately, this is the only way of personalization that I found in the game, you can't choose your class, you can form a party choosing between some online friends, but I still only have one, but you can't modify their skill tree or equipment, which is ok, because who can actually look into a friend skill tree or inventory in a MMORPG?

And that's what I thing is one of the major point of CrossCode: the setting.
It really feels like I just start playing a MMORPG again... the game world has everything, it's really well done: the Hub, hunting grounds for beginners, mob respawn, people running around doing things (it's a very populated world) that behave like real players, you can guess which NPC is acting like a real NPC or as a "real player", some players setting shops in the wild and stuff, even the dialogs between other people. I really liked the MMO atmosphere.

I still have to talk about the fitghint system: as said, CrossCode is a fast paced action RPG, you can basically play with a controller or with the mouse+keboard combo. I uses the second one, and I must say that it's really well done, the programming is awersome, I can say that because myself is in a love-hate relationship with html5...
Basically you can do 2 types of attack: a ranged one and a melee combo of 5 hits. As ranged, you can spam an unlimited number of projectiles in a spread that reduces over aiming, or charge a shot that will bounce against walls 3 times and that will set your spread to minimum, while you can hit everything in front you in a not so small radius with the melee attack, stopping your enemies blocking them from attacking you. Everything is done with the left click (click far and you'll cast energy balls, click near your character and you'll perform a melee attack)
With the right click you'll perform dodges while moving and use your shield while standing still. The game is really focused on dodging , because your character is quite frail, but incredibly fast, but you still have to guess the timing correctly, or you can see a large fraction of your hp bar flying away with a single hit.
while performing each of these action and pressing space, you'll perform a special attack (you have to buy and select them in the skill tree) Each skill consumes a sp point, which charges over time (you have a max of 4 sp points: 2 that are charged everythime, while 2 start to charge only in combat and will deplete over time after you finish), so no mp potions for you this time.
The combat system is really fun, enemies AI is really well done, there's a large number of enemies in different groups, and you'll have to think a different strategy to approach each different mob party, because they are very strong, hit very hard and will try to dodge and pursue you, some of them have shields on their body that will reduce the damage from the front. This makes grinding not so annoying, also because you'll get a "drop rank" that will deplete over time, which is important because it will increase the drop rate (which is very low, 12%-20% at D drop rank) and increase the rarity of drops. Everytime you stop fighting, you drop rank goes back to the lowest level, but this will permit you to regenerate your hp bar. Acquiring an A rank can be quite difficoult, but some drops are only acquired at that level.

Which reminds me of the shop system. You don't actually buy from shops, you instead craft your items by spending drops and money. This makes farming and drop rank essential in your explorations (you won't go past the noob area with the starting equipment). There's a lot of merchants around the map, each one will sell you a few number of items (which can be weapons, consumables or higher tier ingredients), a lot of items have a different recipe for each trader, but you can find everything in the trader book in the menu. So you can have fun going window shopping looking for the cheapest recipe for the same item.

What to say anymore about CrossCode... It's a really fun, rewarding game, quite different from your usual 2D RPG, a really good experience, It says early access, but it doesn't feel that way (I discovered that was early access 5 hours after playing the game, and I was still in the noob area)

If you don't wanna spend the full price, wait for the sales, I personally found this game in a bundle, and I'm glad I did c:
