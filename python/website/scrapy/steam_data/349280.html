About This Software

Ready for adventures?

Panda School Browser is a safe and simple browser which gives access strictly to a list of pre-approved websites and videos.
Clicks on banners or other external links are simply ignored.
The browser is developed especially for children, so it is very easy to use and encourages them to explore and study.

The collection of content is provided by «Panda School» web service which is free to use for everyone. You can take a look at the current collection here: http://pandaschool.org*
The number of the websites and videos is constantly growing, as they can be easily added to collection by any member of community, at the same time content is strictly moderated by our staff.

Second important feature of the browser is the ability to completely prevent access to any other application on your computer while running in the the «Kids Mode». Exit from the «Kids Mode» is protected by PIN.

Parents are free to choose which resources they want to add to their own «Classroom» but there is also a default set of hand-picked resources which provide high quality educational games for children of any age.

Panda School Browser is easy to start with - no additional configuration needed.
Let your kids explore the internet on their own!
Complete safety and lots of fun is guaranteed.

P.S. Even our great application can hardly substitute parental involvement. Enjoy learning new things together with your child from time to time and you'll be truly amazed by the results.

* Users from regions with censored internet access (e.g. Hong Kong) may use the website to check access to various resources

Early Access Review
Posted: 20 April
Panda School Browser is a browser for kids, still in beta. Children access authorized sites by the parents without possibility to use the computer or to go to other sites, like YouTube.

The Steam version allows you to have an account that can use the features of the browser, without having to register first. Then, parents can create their classroom, with links to sites or videos for their kids. They can choose them in the list of contributions approved by the developers or add their own discoveries. They can even share them to the rest of the world. The developers are quick to review it - I did it with a site from my country, Pepit.be. There is just a little weird thing in French, as it's translated in my language, sites are called classrooms.


The parent mode lets you do what you want on your computer, which is something the kid mode isn't allowing at all. Indeed, children can't do anything else apart surfing on the authorized sites, external links are blocked and you can't return under Windows. I couldn't make a screenshot of the kid mode because well, it was blocked too.

I also got locked in kids mode without even wanting to be in it: I didn't have the default code pin - the parental password so that you can go back to your computer -. I've typed it in my attempts to come back to the adult world as I knew that the developers had put probably an easy one for these cases but I must have missed or added a number. Anyway, don't think about killing the process in your task manager: you're still locked out of Windows. The only way to exit safely is to push CTRL-ALT-DELETE and to restart the computer. It shows how far the browser will protect your kids from any forbidden move.

While I think that it was a little too much for me, I found Panda School Browser very interesting, mainly for teachers that fear having students doing what they shouldn't do. It's a cheap software, well-polished.

But it doesn't mean that I will tell that you have to use it absolutely. There is a moment where you have to teach how to use the "real" Internet so that children stay "safe" and are responsible in their use. Panda School Browser doesn't provide this possibility - they're staying focused on young children.

Panda School Browser is a nice tool for parents for 4 euros, not too complex (it's easy to set the parameters contrary to many softwares that will monitor and filter Internet) and with a little interesting database for kids - mainly in English, but you can find some in French (Pepit.be is in French and was added in the public database).

Given the fact that it's still in beta, you may see more features in the future (like maybe, get out of kids mode if the process is killed completely...).

Early Access Review
Posted: 25 May, 2015
This is a great and constantly improving program, which has a decent selection of "safe websites" to visit.
As it stands, the software deserves a Thumbs Up with all the improvements over the poast few months. The developer is working hard to improve it (as seen in the comments section of the review).

Pros:
* Good selection of kid friendly websites, with the ability to submit new/unlisted ones.
* The "child mode" does not allow the use of "ALT+Tab" or "ALT+F4" or "CTRL+ESC" and so forth...and even the CTRL+ALT+DELETE can only call up Task Manager, but Panda still stays on top of Task Manager
* The Developer just added (on 1 July) the ability for users to add their own content without the need for community submission/approval! Wonderful!
* Does the basics of what it says it will do.


Critical Needs/Additions Required:
* The videos "added to the classroom" need their own "videos" tab and should not be dropped into the classroom. A bonus improvement would be to have the ability to organize the videos into sub-groups...like I might choose to name a group "Math Videos" and another "Cartoons" [NOTE: The Developer stated that he is working on this.]
* The option to allow a "Shut Down" button in the child mode. (This would be a very nice addition if you complete the auto-start functionality improvement.)
* A Close "X" Button in the area where the parents can enter a PIN to exit Child Mode (so you don't have to wait for the parents browser to load just to close it)

Other Ideas for Improvement:
* The ability to make this the default browser on a computer -- For kids that have their own computers.
* The ability to handle PDF files.
* A way to clear cookies/history (since some of the sites use web beacons)


I'm glad to see the developers listening to suggestions for improvement on Steam. This program has the potential to be excellent.


Note to the Review Readers: I am updating the review as the bugs are fixed and the developer works with the community. You can also see the progress that has been made in the forum, such as this thread: http://steamcommunity.com/app/349280/discussions/0/617336568086230021/
Also, one of the improvements I suggested, an autorun in Kids Mode, has been added now too: http://steamcommunity.com/games/349280/announcements/detail/137714623445934136
