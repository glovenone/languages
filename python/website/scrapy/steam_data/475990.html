About This Game

Welcome to the exciting world of MTB rides!
Prepare yourself for the madness of downhill!
Dangerous and difficult trails await you.
Take your bike and ride!

Now you can explore extreme trails and enjoy the thrill of a steep, rough terrain full of rocks and countless obstacles. Dodge or jump over them and reach the finish line as fast as possible! Collect money to improve and customise your own downhill and make it as unique and fast as you can.
Get ready for some awesome rides!

Full bike control including leaning or wheelies.

- Downhill rides including some elements of trial ride
- Difficult and challenging rides on diversified terrains
- Breathtaking maps with different types of weather conditions
- Possibility of customising your downhill and modifying its parts in a Bike Shop
- Bikes from NSBikes
- Clothes from Rocday

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP
    • Processor: Core i3
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GT 740
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 4 GB available space

Its main objective is to go from point A to B and as an extra there items for your character and bike (new clothes and bikes). His idealization is something very interesting, but the game is very problematic and ends up hiding their potential, and seen it from the start with a very simple menu which comes to be ugly and very limited options, "choose level" where it is if images to select the stage, something totally static, "store" where you choose your bike and clothing that uses and strangely is where is the change of language (this option is also available on the start screen) and finally, get out.
When starting the game you might feel the potential, however the graphics of years and years ago with end of this feeling, along with sound effects that are few and poorly produced, the potential almost vanishes. When you pause the game if you have the option to go to the main menu or move into a music player and nothing more. Soon it begins to phase you receive a text with the tutorial and what actions we can take? Accelerate, break, turn right and left, as well as change the position of the character if it is toppling. Not shown in the tutorial, but sometimes the bike seems to have the strength to climb certain areas and that should hold the "2" (in this case I refer to what is above the letters, the other is one of the movements of the character to get ready).

Fortunately every certain amount advanced in phase creates a checkpoint which is very useful after all you will be reset again and again to complete the journey.
Something very interesting in the game is that there are stages of two types, say, those that you have a clear path and you can even cut path (which does not recommend because you can lose one of the items) and the other which is almost all linear and you are surrounded by tracks to keep him, but due to bugs sometimes it did not apply of course to return it is almost impossible, because as I said in the beginning these tracks are one of the things that you have ease in fall. But it is better to fall rather than trying to get loose from the tree ... and these linear stages were really good, clear that almost freedom provided by the other is cool, but you can barely feel you really have a goal and a chance to spend anger comes to be higher.
However in spite of all the shortcomings, I particularly liked a lot and I hope that over time he is working in a real way and developer commitment. For despite his status does not reveal any beta this game is far from actually being done and do not recommend.

As both an avid rider and gamer I was stoked to see a mountainbike game appearing on steam.

I immediately bought it, hoping it was executed properly and felt like more than a mobile game ported badly into PC. Sadly, it didn't.

Upon opening the game, you find two options - "choose a level" and "shop."

Firstly, I went into "shop" where it appeared there was no currency, 6 items of clothing to choose from (which I couldn't see anyway while playing the game) and about 5 different frames, all of which were 1 brand, with numerous spelling and grammar errors in the descriptions. It was also unclear whether the different bikes, ranging from enduro through freeride to downhill, would actually handle any differently.

After trying desperately to find out whether it was actually possible to buy anything in the shop, I decided to actually play the game. After clicking "choose track" I was met with a screen of numbers. I clicked on "1." Then I clicked on it again. And again. And again. After about a minute, during which there was no load bar, beachball of death, or other means of telling me the map was loading, I found myself at the top of a track made of early 2000s graphics, prompted with a brief note on the controls, and set into action. There was no sound, the bike handled weirdly, the track looked like a 2 pixel pavement, and I was forced to quickly close the game and ask steam for a refund.

If you're a fellow rider looking for a game to play, I suggest MX vs ATV reflex, also available on steam, or real life.

TL;DR: I wouldn't play it if it was free, let alone pay for it.
