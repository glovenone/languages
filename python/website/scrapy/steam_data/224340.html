About This Game

BECOME A PART OF THE CREW! Strap in and get ready for an intense ride as you team up to take down a dangerous international gang that can only be stopped by you and the rest of the Fast & Furious crew. Get behind the wheel of some of the world’s most intense rides and take on high-stakes driving missions cooperatively with a friend or computer A.I. Put your skills to the test in Challenge Mode, an over-the-top game mode offering addictive objectives and high re-playability. Build your dream car and modify it with more than 50 awesome upgrades and performance perks. Heists, hijacks and mayhem combine across 8 epic global locations, from Rio to LA to Moscow, in an original storyline that spans the entire Fast & Furious film franchise.

Fast & Furious Showdown does exactly what the title says; it makes one quickly furious.

Showdown is a terrible game with PS2-like visual quality and the gameplay itself is even worse.
The game consists of 10 chapters; each chapter has at least 1 event, but the later ones might have 4 or 5.

F&F Showdown isn't all about racing, though. Well, technically, it is, but isn't just not about finishing laps. There's some variety in it (killing enemies and carjacking, for example), which fit the movies if you think about it but the execution is terrible and is exactly what you would expect from a mobile game just a few years ago. It's very unpolished.

The AI is either incredibly stupid or 'smart'. There's no inbetween. Checkpoints are very rare and if you do have to restart there's no way to skip the ridiculous intros.

The voice acting is pretty good and the character models do look like the characters from the movies (in a way) but it's not enough to make this a good game.

I was glad I finished it in just a couple of hours. Any longer and I would've thrown my controller into the screen. Avoid.

[Rating: 42/100]
