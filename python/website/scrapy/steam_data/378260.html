About This Game

Fortify is a real-time strategy game with a tower defense twist. Defend your castle from attacking monsters and siege weapons by using ranged and melee defenders, catapults, cannons, oil fields and more. You start at the iron age, advancing to the medieval age, and later, the imperial age. Each new age enables important upgrades and allows for new techniques to defend your castle.

The Steam Edition includes
- Two mission maps
- Multiplayer Player vs Player, tower defense style
- Arcade mode: How long can you survive the attacks?
- Real-time lighting and shadows
- Achievements
- Leaderboards
And more

Multiplayer mode plays just like normal, but instead of having enemies attack automatically, every monster that attacks you has been sent by your opponent. In order to gain an advantage you must learn to balance attack and defense.

This game is good. Just a simple Tower-Defense/RTS hybrid in the same vein as some zombie survival flash games you might've seen on Armor Games about a decade ago. Like those games, this one has a very addicting nature and you'll find yourself sinking a good number of hours into the Arcade mode, trying to get further and further every time. So far my best playthrough is 22 waves.

Fortify plays in a very simple manner. You have a fort that starts out very low tier with just a couple of slingshooters to hold back the tides of evil. As you gain more gold, you'll unlock new units. You'll upgrade your fort and build outposts which produce 1 of 3 resources that are used to further upgrade your fort. You'll progress through three tech levels; Iron Age, Medieval Age and Imperial Age. Starting from slingshots and ending with rifles and grenades. Aside from the 2 campaigns in the game (Northern, West) you also have an endless Arcade mode and a multiplayer mode where you can battle against 1 other player.

In summation, a very solid title that I've had an absolute blast with. And for 4.99, you can hardly beat it! A lot of indie titles price themselves out of the market, assuming they have plenty of content for the price. This title can actually carry that boast.

TL;DR: Really nice game to fair price. If you like RTS/Tower Defense and have someone to play it with in multiplayer buy it immediatly!

I followed the development of this game for a while and bought it on release and I wouldn't say I'm disappointed, no it's definitely a really nice game. I'm a huge fan of indie games and also of RTS games, so this game was an easy buy-choice for me. And I don't regret the purchase, but this game is still not perfect. There are many small things that bug me and some of them are easily fixable. And I hope this game gets more content in future updates.

The biggest thing everyone who buys this game will notice immediatly is that this game has no resolution option at all, which would be no problem if it was in HD anyway or would have pixelart style, but its more in a flash-game kind of style, i.e. it has sort of high resolution textures and tries to look "realistic". I like the design and it fits the game perfectly but because there is no resolution option the game will be noticable blurry (at least in the main menu) when played in fullscreen. But I can live with that.

Then there is the 30 fps lock, which really is no problem at all because of the gameplay. But I noticed that when you advance time at night the fps lock will switch to 60, 90 and 120 fps, which then speeds up the game. That's not really professional, but it works...I guess.....nice workaround...but this probably explains why it has a 30 fps lock instead of 60 fps. Since 4x60fps = 240fps which would probably cause some problems on less powerful systems.

Next thing that I noticed are some AI problems here and there. The AI really has no AI at all except maybe some kind of pathfinding. This kinda sucks because you'll always have to rotate your troops so that they attack the enemy. You can't set something like patrol paths or auto-rotation. And everytime you change the direction of your ranged units you'll have to change it back, because the position-saving only saves their position, and not their rotation, so when you say them to go back to their default position but you rotated them earliert they will then look in the wrong direction. Dev, pls fix this!

Then there is the problem that most of the times you won't find someone to play with, and I doubt this will change in the future, probably it will become even harder. But yea, that's because not enough people play this game, that's not the games fault :D
