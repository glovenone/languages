Feature List

- 4 univers w/ 4 vehicles
- 20 circuits and arenas
- 4 game modes
- up to 4 players in multiplayer games
- online scores

What this game is, and what it is not

This is a game for everyone, from 6-14 years old kids to adults who like arcade racing games.
We've mixed classical and original gameplay elements.

Micro Machines/Micro Machines 2 was a real influence to us while making this game, but our game is simpler.
There's no split screen.
There's no level editor.
There's no native gamepad support.
There's no collision between cars.

About This Game


Admit it — when you were a kid you used the floor, carpet, walls, bathtub and even the dirt in your backyard to race your mini cars; didn't you?
Well those days are back and this time Mom won't be yelling at you for making a mess.

Now become the natural behind the wheel you dreamed about when you were playing with your toy vehicles.
MiniOne Racing is a thrilling racing game which lets players drive colorful, humorous miniature vehicles around various locations created by a kid: beach, playground... and even other planet!

In-depth 3 minute Review
Below the short informative review video, you'll also find a written review, should you prefer reading to listening.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fhaWlZtVmfY
First, let me say that this is not going to be one of those hahasofunnyrandomthumbsup review.
REVIEW
From time to time, I am blessed to stumble upon gems like this game. A quick peek at the options menu already shows you what to expect. Atrocious pixelated and bug riddled options, often without a label or a clear effect, empty drop-down menus. Oh boy, you are all in for some delicious treats with this one. MiniOne Racing, a top-down racing game with power-ups and toy cars and all that kind of good stuff. Probably nothing could go wrong here, right? WRONG. Not only does the camera angle destroy all sense of momentum, which is so oh so important for racing games... NO, additionally, and maybe even worse, the game as a power-up which randomly assigns a so called “master” who obtains a permanent lightning shield until the “master” is switched again. This lightning slows all others toy cars nearby. Infuriating. Not only does the game fail to have good controls, a sense of speed, nice graphics, bombastic sound, it also fails in enjoyable cart racing by including nonsensical, random items, which are so powerful that random chance might as well roll first place for you. Possible playing random cart racing on random.org might be more enjoyable than this.
