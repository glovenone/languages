About This Game

In 'n Verlore Verstand you will be transported to a reality of dreams and nightmares. What will you discover about yourself in this journey through the subconscious?

In these realms of the unknown, you must travel through labyrinths of memories to eventually find your way to your own truth.

Features:

-Abstract adventure.
-Engaging dream-like environments.
-Mysterious puzzles.
-Platforming
-Exploration
-Oculus Rift integration.

An Abstract Exploration / Platformer Dream Simulator
https://youtu.be/WAXh72oJ8W4
In this game you basically explore a variety of dreams and get transported to a new one when you complete each level which typically involves finding a tree at the end.

+ Each "Dream" is very different from the last which encourages you to
+ Graphically this game looks pretty good, especially considering the great variety of stages
+ Some unexpected surprises One stage trucks are literally trying to run you over and end up suiciding into rocks and blowing themselves up lol
+ Some stages are pretty challenging, and does require a fair bit of platforming skills
+ Active Devs who responded to my feedback extremely quickly!


(This game just released in Early Access so don't take these negatives too seriously)
- Some stages are a bit too long, and lack any checkpoints causing you to reset the entire level if you fall or messup.

Dev Response: "this is something that we will be working in soon".

- The controls are bit strange on a controller, Y to Run, B to Jump, making it a bit hard to run and jump at the same time (which you will need to do a lot)

Dev Response: "we integrated the ability to change the buttons to whatever the player is most comfortable withm so feel free to change the keys to your heart's desire :)"

- I feel like movement in general could be improved, especially in the parkour stages.

[Bug] I had some audio issues with my virtual surround headset, footsteps and certain other sounds come entirely from the right side of my headset which is very disorientating, I truly hope they fix this at some point. Stereo users should be fine.

Dev Response: thanks for the heads up on the Sound issues you are having we will be sure to test this out a bit more and get it fine tuned.

I really enjoyed the concept of this game and feel like I'd like to see more types of dreams, maybe explore some more fears, and add some more good dreams as well. Lots of potential.

Dev Response: "we will be adding more level types and variations of existing types in the near future with the next level to be done and uploaded on Monday, so stay tuned :)"

Early Access Review
Posted: 13 February
It all started when I walked towards that damn tree… Then everything suddenly swirled and whirled before it faded to black. I woke up facing a door with wood panels keeping it closed. I hoped beyond hope that those wood panels were there to protect me but knowing that whatever was behind those panels could quickly tear those panels apart just as quickly as it would devour me. The vision behind those panels was blurry and it made me wonder where I was. What was I supposed to do? I turned around and explored….

There have been some mind trick games released over the past few years. Among my favorites are Antichamber not only because the developer, Alexander Bruce, is from my home town but because it is a genuinely fantastic game, the Portal series with their fabulous puzzles and more recently in a different vein, Pony Island. The really games in this subgenre (if you can call it that) have an effect on you long after you’ve put them down. They make you think outside of the box to the point where you may even question if there is a box at all or if the box is simply your self-constrained imagination. In other words, it makes you question reality. This game does that in a manner that is both similar to the aforementioned games – particularly Antichamber – but also in a manner that is unique in and of itself.

The first time that I spent time with ‘n Verlore Verstand I was feeling off color beforehand. That wasn’t such a clever idea. One of the game’s intentions is to disorientate you. It definitely had that effect on me to the point where I felt nauseous. That’s not the game’s fault but something that I felt that I should share given my experience with it. It wasn’t a good feeling. Thankfully when I was feeling better and I played it again I only got the sense of being disorientated – the game’s intention.

The objective of the game is to clearly solve each dream state which, in my experience, ends with a tree in every map. Each dream is different than the one previous to it and thus far I’ve not come across two similar, environmentally, dreams. Each dream encourages you to explore every nook and cranny. You will come across outdoor and indoor settings throughout your journey with each level increasing its level of disorientation.

While it’s not a game of the year contender it will be a memorable experience and for that very reason ‘n Verlore Verstand deserves to be recommended for fans of alternate realities and mind bending enthusiasts.

Early Access Review
Posted: 3 February
I played through this game in one sitting, and I loved every second of it. The game is visually astounding. The surroundings gave the game a certain artistic touch, that just resonated the time and effort put into this game. The puzzles were challenging in their own respect, but not impossible by any means. I loved the way you hopped from one dream/nightmare to the next once you completed a sequence. Without further ado, I will introduce some pros and cons!
(Note: I did not play this with virtual reality, so my review is without that aspect.)

Pros:
-Game is visually astounding
-The puzzles are fun and different in their own respects
-It has a lot of potential for a great storyline as time goes on
-Full Controller Support FTW (personal preference for games like this)

Cons:
-Game does not have any checkpoint features, which means one slip up and you are doing the WHOLE puzzle again (The devs responded to the review saying they are going to work on a checkpoint system.)
-There is not a lot done due to early access, so you can only play four levels as of right now.
-The pricing is a little hefty for only four levels, but obviously it is early access

That just about does it for me. I personally loved the game. There seems to be a lot of potential here for growth. I look forward to seeing where the developers take the game, and I hope that there is a great deal more to come. If you would like to check out some gameplay of it before making your purchase, then check out my Youtube video on it. I only have the first part up currently, but more to come. I hope you guys enjoy the beauty of the game as much as I did, and I highly recommend supporting this indie developer to see the project grow. That does it for me guys :)!
