About This Game



Only the Dead Survive.
A massive deep-space mining ship goes dark after unearthing a strange artifact on a distant planet. Engineer Isaac Clarke embarks on the repair mission, only to uncover a nightmarish blood bath  the ship's crew horribly slaughtered and infected by alien scourge. Now Isaac is cut off, trapped, and engaged in a desperate fight for survival.

  • Strategically dismember the Necromorph enemies limb by bloody limb.
  • Zero gravity combat means terror can strike from anywhere.
  • Uncover the horrific truth of this shocking thriller.

    • Hard Drive: 7.5 GB free space

Playing this game in 2016, I can honestly say it not only holds up but defies expectations.

Full Disclaimer, I am not a fan of horror games, I feel like they deliberately put the player in an evironment with jump scares, forgo good story and most importantly make no meaningful strides towards giving a player realisic power to combat the foe and difficulties they face. Dead Space does none of this. It gives players power, immerces you into the experience and, most importantly, lets skill impact the game in a meaningful way.

The game still has a profound effect on its player, creating tense moments through well designed use of sound and the power distribution of the enemies allows you to put yourself in Issac's shoes; A lone engineer in a hostile environment, barely surviving every encounter though, what the game carefully crafts unwittingly to its player, a mastery of your foe.

Combat feels solid and impactful, the animations especially convey a great deal of emotion and put you in the place of your character. Once again, I must praise the sound design; The thumping of necromorphs running around in vents have you looking around anticipating attack, playing with a surround system especially heightens the experience.
Zero Gravity fights are also quite tense, having you jump around, grab explosive canisters to throw at your enemies so that you can conserve ammo.

Puzzles are included in the game however require a little out of the box thinking but reward you with magnificent set pieces.

Exploration in this game is key to its mastery, you may find a power node able to upgrade weapons and your rig however you may find yourself ambushed and taking a load of damage making the next fight even more difficult but the strength of a single power compels you to push forward to make that actual risk worth it.

Speaking of the Rig(which is your space suit), it effortlessly intergrates your Heath and Statis power onto your character, along with that the game rarely breaks immersion, the entire HUD is intergrated with Issac and thus pushes your experience even further.

The game does offer a New Game + mode and I will no doubt be replaying it at some point however if there was at any point, somewhere down the line where you felt you forgot or never understood why this game recieved two sequels, play this game.

Crank the volume up, turn off the lights and give this game a go(or for some another one), it reinvograted my love for the franchise and has my approval.

Dead Space can be considered a modern classic for survival horror genre or just a bland sci-fi shooter with horror elements. What ever you might think, you can admit that this game does have a decent gameplay and good atmosphere at some points. Weapons and armor look cool and are indeed memorable. Killing necromorphs is fun and sci-fi weapons make it even more fun.

The game still does have a fair share of issues. PC version has poor performance for relatively old game and is buggy, but the amount bugs in play sessions changes. I had a invisible wall when I opened the door (at the first chapter in fact) and I couldn't get through, so I had to change some settings (I can't remember what I did, but you can find out yourself) to fix it. Another bug was in zero-gravity outside the ship where there was asteroids hitting the ship and you had to avoid those by getting into cover. You also had to jump to the walls because of cover and oxygen canisters, but my character somehow didn't land on it and stopped and died for no reason. I had to land much more carefully to avoid it. When I was a bit younger I stopped playing it because of this issue. I didn't understand, what I was suppose to do at the time.

WARNING: POSSIBLE SPOILERS!!!
This game does have a story which has a lot of clichees. Characters aren't that good and it doesn't help that your main character, Isaac Clarke, is mute. They added voice later on with the sequels gladly, but his personality wasn't that interesting, but still OK. I should still say that the foundation of Dead Space is great. Humanity is lacking resources and for that reason, they built planetcrackers that can go into lightspeed and then grab a huge part of the planet/moon/asteroid to mine it and gather all the resources. There's also a cult with large amounts of members who have their own devious plans. Good setup, but the story of characters is lacking and could've been better.

If you're new to horror genre, this game might be scary to you. Though it's possible that if you're an active gamer, you'll notice the patterns in Dead Space.

This review might sound too cynical to have a recommended status, but I should say that this game is good overall and you should try it if you're into shooters or horror.

Yes, this game has weird mouse controls. Now, with all the negatives out of the way, this is hands down one of the greatest survival horror games out there -- that is, if you play it on Hard or higher (a strange bug causing your game to suddenly switch difficulty from Hard to Normal has however yet not been resolved and never will be). Love atmospheric survival horror but feel like being unable to defend yourself is not fun? Dead Space has you covered -- in layers of blood. Yes, one or two well placed shots to limbs (great system, headshots make no sense here anyway) can disarm (har har) the enemy, but the same pretty much applies to you -- ignore an enemy and say goodbye to half of your medpacks.
Yes, this game utilizes jumpscares. A lot. But it also loves to fake them with long build-ups that result in a vent cover simply falling off. Together with DS2, this is THE survival horror series of our time. And yes, DS2 has superior gameplay, but it lacks the Alien-like atmosphere of this one. And DS3 lacks the ability to let your wallet intact (don't bother).
