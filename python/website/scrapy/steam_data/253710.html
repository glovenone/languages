7 July

Status Update 7th July 2016


Howdy hunters,

Hope you are well on this glorious summer’s day. Here’s what has been happening at the Expansive Worlds’ Headquarters.

Game Crashes – Workaround Test

Here is some important information for those of you who have been experiencing issues causing the game client to crash. We’re hopefully starting to see some progress on the matter, thanks to the dedicated efforts of our awesome community members who’ve discovered and thoroughly tested the workaround. While we are testing it internally, you can try to implement it yourself following the instructions in this forum thread. Please read both the original post from caledonianblues as well as the post from our producer Philipp carefully, ask your questions and report your findings in that very same thread. We will be closely monitoring the data and once all testing is verified, we will build the solution and roll it out automatically for everyone.

Thanks again to our community members Pretty_Boy, Fletchette, caledonianblues and others who helped to test and roll this out to the larger community. You guys officially rock and we are humbled and honoured to have you playing with us! <3

Geek out on your hunting data!



Staying on the matter of impressive community efforts, make sure to check this sophisticated stats web app, developed by the aforementioned caledonianblues: http://www.uhcapps.co.uk/stats_lifetime.php?username=caledonianblues (just exchange “caledonianblues” in the URL to your own theHunter username to see your own stats). Search among your weapons, species, collectables, see some nice graphs of your hunting achievements and much, much more. Excellent work!

Semi-automatic wonderfulness

Yesterday we released another member of our semi-auto weapon family. The ultimate varmint equalizer, a .223 Semi-Automatic Rifle. From what we’ve gathered so far, the majority of you seem to like it, so that’s sweet! One of the common requests has been to expand the permitted species list by adding Sitka Deer (which by the way are getting a score upgrade in our next patch, don’t miss it). Our game designer Daniel is investigating this possibility, stay tuned for more news on this. If you want to see the rifle in all its glory, check out this video by our community member ronMctube:


Also, there is a nice bundle in the store right now with the new rifle, matching scope and a scent hound – have a look here.

Jambofinal is (almost) here

Our Football Jamboree 2016 is – alas – coming to an end. This Sunday is the last day when you can buy the “winning” t-shirt to secure that delightful 7mm Magnum Bullpup with gold engraving (it won’t be available in the store, ever), so place your bets and keep your fingers crossed for your favourite team to win. Following the requests from many players, we’re also releasing all the 32 national caps from theHunter Football Jamboree 2014. You can get them until the end of this week in our store.

New textures for old weapons

Recently we’ve been trying to show some love to our old content, and as part of this attempt we’ve given facelift and slight remodeling to our oldest (and arguably the least prettiest) rifles, the .243, .270 (wood), and .300 (composite) Bolt-Action Rifles. Go and check them in game to see the difference!

Happy hunting!

About This Game

The most realistic online hunting game ever produced. Experience the thrill of the hunt in huge realistic environments using everything from crossbows to state of the art bolt action rifles. Realistic animal behavior and persistent online features provides a living world where you can roam casually or compete with other players for glory and fame.

Go hunting with your friends in multiplayer with up to 8 players - or explore the nine diverse and immersive hunting reserves in the ever evolving world of theHunter by yourself.

Key Features

  • Hunt 30 different species including Black Bear, Brown Bear, Polar Bear, Moose, Whitetail Deer, Turkey, Pheasant, Coyote, Red Fox, Roosevelt Elk, Wild Boar, Red Deer, Roe Deer, Mule Deer, Blacktail Deer, Feral Hog, Mallard, Canada Goose, Cottontail Rabbit, Reindeer, Alpine Ibex, European Rabbit, Red Kangaroo, Bison, Snowshoe Hare, Arctic Fox, Sitka Deer, Gadwall, American Black Duck & Northern Pintail.
  • Explore 10 different huge environments with state-of-the-art graphics, complete with day cycle and weather effects - from the Alps to the Australian Outback. from the sub-arctic Alaska to the swamps of Louisiana.
  • Co-op multiplayer with up to 8 friends
  • Extensive online features. Increase your skills, see statistics and trophy shots. Participate in competitions and leaderboards or take on challenging missions and achievements.
  • theHunter is continuously updated with new species, environments, equipment and features.

Progression & Acquiring Items In The Game

Five huntable species (Whitetail Deer, European Rabbit and Pheasant, plus two more animals rotating weekly), two weapons (.243 Bolt-Action Rifle & 12 GA Single Shot shotgun) and a bunch of additional equipment are available for free when first entering the game. Additional items such as weapons, species licenses and ammo can be acquired with gm$, the in-game currency that can be earned through play, or em$, the in-game currency that can be earned through winning competitions or purchase for real money.


There are several different types of skills in the game that increase when players use items & hunt various species. As these skills increase, more abilities get unlocked, such as the ability to tell weight & gender from animal tracks and steadier aim for weapons.

Achievements can be earned through diverse in-game activities. Each achievement adds to your HunterScore, which can be used as a progression comparison vs. other players. Additionally, increasing the HunterScore unlocks upgrade items such as pouches & holsters.

TL;DR

F2P is a Demo-Mode only. Solid hunting game with detailed hunting grounds, cheap MMO-like payment model, rare but large actual content updates but loads of optional microtransaction material.

F2P and Payment model

The F2P content contains the license to hunt Whitetail Deer, Pheasant and three kinds of Rabbit only. As of the latest major Patch you now get a shotgun for free as well, so the grindwall for hunting the rabbits and pheasant is gone. One ingame currency "GMS" can be obtained by doing missions like "Track a Mule Deer", "Harvest a Mule Deer", "Spot a Whitetail Deer". Its a pretty useless currency though.

The payment model lets you pay per month in order to get a license for all animals, so think of it as a "cheap MMO-type payment". However in contrast to MMOs, actual content updates to be played by you as a license holder appear only a few times a year. On the other hand these updates usually contain a new hunting reserve with new animals, so they offer quite some content.

There are a lot of weekly minor updates with new guns, clothes and other equipment not really needed for playing the game though.

The Game

In my opinion thehunter is a well-made hunting simulator with decent animal behavior and very well made highly detailed hunting reserves.

The negative points I want to mention are random fps-drops in certain areas of the map which many players seem to encounter, "old animals" seem a bit lacking in texture quality compared to the new ones as well as "old reserves" are not as diverse and different from each other as the more recent ones. Some reserves are considered lackluster by a majority of the playerbase due to "recycling" of animals, e.g. the Australia reserve only contains the kangaroo as a specifically australian animal.

Some content patches in the past caused problems for players to connect in multiplayer games.

How much does it cost ?

If there is no sale going I can not recommend buying a membership via the Ingame Store at all.
Instead, I highly recommend to get the DVD Version of 2013 for ~10 € which is a far better deal.

Basically this DVD Version comes with everything you need in contrast to the ingame-store sets. Even if you want to extend your membership, the DVDs offer much more.
For some animals, callers are quite handy, so you might want to plan another ~10€ for callers. For fox and coyote I think the "Jackrabbit caller" is essential, for the rest of the callers you dont own it is really dependant on your playstyle.
The same goes for any more "advanced" hunting clothing.
To summarize, I think buying a DVD-Version which offers a fair deal, if you can not get the 2013 Version you will need ~5€ for a decent big-game hunting rifle, depending on the ongoing sales.

Important:

Do not buy any of the starter packs unless the largest one gets a hefty discount on Steam, because the other ones will leave you with the lack of a big-game rifle as well and are lackluster in value proposition compared to buying any DVD Version.

One thing that should be mentioned here is goose and duck hunting. Though you can find those animals without any special equipment, you will need this equipment should you want to hunt birds in large scale.
Since geese and ducks require dummies and a special hunting setup you need ~15 €, depending on the current sales for a goose or a duck setup, so these act as "DLCs".

Alternatives

Most of the "Cabelas" Hunting games are just mass animals shooters, the "Cabelas Big Game Hunter Pro Hunts" is not bad though, just simplified mechanics.

If you enjoy the detailed landscape and the "slow" gameplay, you might want to look into "Fishing Planet". This one actual is true F2P, but comes with a high grindwall.

Let me just start off by stating that I’m giving a positive rating because I think TheHunter is a decent game, but despite this I’m going to get pretty negative in this review, mostly because it’s essentially one very long complaint of a problem that I think is crippling to the game. I’m also not going to summarize how the core game works at all, so if you’re looking for a review of that then this probably won’t help much.

As you can probably already see from the Steam reviews, there’s been a lot of controversy about Thehunter’s Free 2 Play model. I never had much experience with this, as I started out paying for membership, but the controversy eventually led Expansive Worlds to revise their model to a slightly less intrusive one, but asides from the confirmation that is was indeed a pretty bad model I think that maybe a lot of the negative reviews are undeserved.

In general I think that periodical charges are perfectly reasonable in a game which offers continuous updates and new content, and TheHunter is one of the better hunting games in a video game genre otherwise marred by some pretty embarrassing design standards. It should be pretty clear from my playtime here on Steam that I like it. Despite a visible need for polish in terms of the games UI design and how the game conveys important information, as well as a pretty ♥♥♥♥ decision to make everything including the launcher fully dependant on Expansive Worlds’ servers (which means that when there’s problems on their end you can’t play the game at all, not even singleplayer, and sometimes a host can lose connection to her or his own game). However, the main problem that I’ve chosen to write about in this review stems from how the ingame store operates.

For all means and purposes, TheHunter is a stealth game where the primary goal is to kill animals. In a stealth game about killing things, what are some of the general design elements that are the most crucial to providing variety and thereby prolonged engagement, i.e. fun, to the experience? Well, having different environments to play in and having different ways to kill things.

If you pay for membership, (or if you use in-game currency to buy permissions for hunting different animals) you’re provided with the rudimentary means to hunt all animals within all 10 different reserves, and the environments of the different reserves do impact the difficulty of the game and the way you have to play in order to hunt efficiently, so in that case the game fulfills the first of those requirements.

There’s also a large variety of different weapons to use and different equipment to use, and they all impact the way you can hunt significantly. Some weapons are silent, some weapons are light, some weapons are powerful and heavy, and you can get hunting blinds, bait, camouflage clothing, dogs, bows, and all other sorts of cool stuff to hunt animals in different ways,

-BUT ALL OF THAT COSTS MONEY.-

Like most Free 2 Play multiplayer games with microtransactions, there’s two kinds of currency, gm$, earned by completing missions ingame, and em$, earned by paying for it with real money. What is different and ironically worse than the microtransactions of most other Free 2 Play multiplayer games is that the stuff which can actually be bought with gm$ is currently limited to only the absolutely most pitifully uninteresting consumables that the game has to offer;

You cannot buy weapons with gm$. You cannot buy tents or hunting blinds with gm$. You cannot buy bait barrels, which are required to use bait, with gm$. You cannot buy dogs with gm$. You cannot buy clothing with gm$. You cannot buy scopes with gm$.

Essentially, any digital items which are permanently added to your character, or which actually do something significant to the gameplay requires payments in addition to your membership subscription, which to me is much more significant assery than the notorious former Free 2 Play structure. The concept of having to buy ingame equipment in order to have fun in a game that you’re already supporting through periodical charges is ludicrous.

TheHunters main equivalent of points in its semi-competitive community high-score system is HunterScore, which is rewarded for getting ingame milestone achievements, like “25 Mule Deer harvests” or “100 .243 bolt action rifle harvests”. There is a maximum limit to the HunterScore which can be achieved, but since getting there requires killing animals with all weapons in the game, including those which cost money, it effectively acts as a Pay 2 Win structure if you really want to spend the ridiculous amount of time it requires to actually compete with the top players.

The problems of the cost structure are slightly alleviated by the retail packages, which in some areas are actually way cheaper and offer more value than just buying membership online. With my first retail package I felt that I was getting significantly more value for my money than the 14.99€ “Wayfarer” starter package I bought on Steam, and this led me to later buy a couple of other packages from a german website in order to get some more new equipment and some added membership time. This time it was far less of a satisfying purchase, though, partly because it was less cheap and partly because only the membership and the single “special edition” weapon I got from the packages was actually useful to me. Most equipment offered in the retail packages is the same as every other retail package, save for usually one “exclusive” premium item, usually a weapon.

You can stack multiple pieces of the same equipment in TheHunter, but it seems like an oversight much more than anything else, because you can’t actually do anything with spare pieces of equipment. There’s no trading system, you can’t sell them for a bit of money, and there’s no point of having two of the same gun with you on a hunt since you’re limited to the same amount of ammunition as when you’re carrying one anyways. If anything they do make navigating your inventory more tedious, because you’ll eventually see, like, 6 .243 bolt action rifles and 6 12 gauge single shot shotguns because you’ve activated 5 different packages which all contained roughly the same guns and because there isn’t even any option to discard them. In fact, with one of the recent updates the 12 gauge single shot shotgun is now the only weapon in the store which costs 0 em$, so just for ♥♥♥♥♥ and giggles I bought 100 and now my ingame inventory is cluttered with them.

Anyways, now that I got the retail packages from Germany and activated them, I haven’t really ended up feeling like I made a good purchase. Instead, I really feel like a gullible idiot, and probably rightfully so. It’s mostly this feeling which is the reason as to why I’m writing this review in the first place. The weapons in TheHunter are only digital trinkets after all, and I don’t think it’s right to spend money on digital trinkets like this in the first place. I don’t understand the need for it either, if it was possible to at least also buy weapons for gm$ in addition to em$, it would provide a major incentive for players to keep playing the game, and since membership requires a subscription already, then what’s the problem?

Shouldn’t the membership subscriptions be paying for the maintenance and content updates of the game so that these seemingly kind of greedy and seemingly unnecessary micro transactions wouldn’t be needed? What’s the point of having two separate payment systems operating in the same game?

I like the game, but looking at the store really just makes me annoyed. After the tremendous amount of negative reviews following the former Free 2 Play model, though, they did revise it in the end. Maybe one of the next priorities could be revising the payment system?
