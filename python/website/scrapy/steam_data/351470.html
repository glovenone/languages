About This Game

Sometimes: Success Requires Sacrifice, the title says it all! You get thrown into a dark room, with no light to be found anywhere! Are you willing to end your life to light the way for the next generation to proceed further? Play it today and find out!

Sometimes features a classic mode, as well as several minigames to keep you entertained! Several in-game achievements and a statistics tracker will accompany you throughout your journey so that you can see how sacrificial you have been.

An almost cutsie little game, it's so simple that you can really overthink it when you first aquire it. The goal is to get into a big blue sharp looking swirly thing (I though it was killing me when I got into it the first couple of times. turns out it's the level end portal)

There are only five things that you can do.
1. Run around (or away from ghost / scythe / Death things)

3. Commit suicide to provide light
4. die on obsticles and provide light
5. get into the portal (swirlly blue thing)

I haven't completed the game, so there may be more ways to die than those mentioned here.

So, yes / no?
um it's a kind - of Zen type game, at least the early stages have been, with unlimited lives, and every death making the next run easier. Know what you're getting into, and you'll be fine.

You run around as a little guy in a dark room. The only source of light is you ... or rather your dead body. That's right, you have to kill yourself if you want to light your path. In each level (in "classic" mode) you'll have to find a blue portal to progress to the next level.
In the higher levels you will also encounter traps and the "reaper".
Optionally, you can try to find collectibles in the levels.
The game has its own achievement system, but the dev said (months ago, see the game's Steam discussion page) he'll consider to implement Steam achievements.

Beside the "classic" mode, the game also offers the following game modes:
o Endless: Find 20 medals in a dark forest
o Heart: Not really a game mode, this takes you to the dev's website to a free game called "Sometimes You Lose Heart".
o Trust Fall: An endless falling animation. You can't kill yourself, but on yourway to the bottom you'll encounter traps.
o Chips: You're Pac-Man! ... in a dark maze.

You can basically see screenshots of each game mode on the Steam store page (except for "Heart", for obvious reasons).

Now here are my personal pros and cons:
+ Nice background music
+ Good for casual game play
+ Nice idea and concept
+ Good character handling
+ Stats and achievement pages

- Can get boring fast
- No online leader board
- No options. You can't change the resolution or play in fullscreen.

I really liked the idea of Sometimes: Success Requires Sacrifice, but ultimately the game just isn't that interesting.

Each level of the main game mode has your character in a small, brightly lit area, only able to move a short distance before encountering darkness similar to the fog of war in RTS games. The difference is that the darkness in Sometimes can only be expelled by your character committing suicide and thereby brightening up a small area of the level. This goes on until you find the exit. Later levels include moving 'traps' and the Grim Reaper, who follows you around the level, but there just wasn't enough in it to hold my interest.

Other game modes offer a bit more variation, but ultimately not enough to make the game worthy of play for more than an hour or so. I've played each of the different game types a few times, got annoyed at the game not always saving in-game achievements (there are no Steam achievements), and discovered that all in all it's just a bit boring.

It's certainly not a bad game. It's just not really anything special.

Sometimes is good little game. The gameplay is pretty basic; you move with WASD and suicide with spacebar.
Why suicide, you ask? The map is always dark, you being the only light source. So, when you suicide, your corpse/soul becomes another light source, making you able to see more of it.
There are 5 game modes;
Classic: Constantly suicide to comprehend obstacles and reach the checkpoint.
Endless: Suicide to see/gather medals.
Trust fall: Free fall while evading obstacles.
Chips: "Dark pacman mode". Collect chips while evading ghosts.
...And heart mode, which I can't acess because it opens a Web Browser page that won't load.

So, would you recommend Sometimes? Usually I would say no, but considering the price tag and the nice little classical BGM, yes.
The suicide sound effect is irritating though.
