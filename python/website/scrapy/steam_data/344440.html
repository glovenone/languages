About This Game

The demons of the Seven Deadly Sins have broken free of their fiery prison and taken up residence in Sacra Terra! Use your cunning to defeat each demon and rescue humanity from destruction! As you explore a decaying hospital, unlock the secrets of a medieval abbey, and search eerie catacombs, you'll receive help from a pure spirit known only as Angel. Who she is and what happened to her is just one of the shocking truths you will uncover as you find well-hidden clues, gather useful items, and solve cleverly designed puzzles! The Collector's Edition of Sacra Terra: Angelic Night contains a bonus chapter, a step-by-step walkthrough, desktop wallpapers, and concept art.

Seven Deadly Sins, Seven Ways To Win...

Sacra Terra is a story about the seven deadly sins, and the protagonists quest (the player) to defeat them before they can bring doom to earth.

Graphics: + The graphics are well developed and at least they catch the atmosphere of the game.

Sound: + Atmospheric sound, although you hear that there isn't much of variance between the sounds and the music. Some of the atmosphere is produced by crying children and desperatly screaming and yelling people... It works on a subliminal level.

Playability: ++ The accessibility of the game and the areas is good, the riddles (minigames) and the Scenes are well done and there wasn't much need of using the hint system during the game.

Genre Type Values:

Story: ++ The story is plain and direct, you are in a strange dilapidated hospital, you are freed by a strange entity going by the name "Angel" and being one too, so far so good, but then you have to solve riddles to destroy each one of the seven sins, by using their own indulgence. I won't tell too much, it's just spoiling you the fun. It sounds harder than it is, and you actually will find the answers spread through the game. Especially nice is the descriptions in the journal, and the cut-scenes of the game.

Technical Realisation: ++ The whole game is a smooth experience, even when you quit the game and play later, you are easy in and out the game. The story was nicely realized, the graphics are atmospheric and fitting, the sound lends to the sinister and dreadful atmosphere. Not one bug sprang up during gameplay, and there were no lags, no crashes encountered. It worked like a charm, even the cutscenes were fluently executed.

Extras: ++ The game has a bonus chapter, wallpapers, a walkthrough (not needed, but nice to have) and art prototypes of the game. In all alone the bonus chapter is a nicely done upgrade to the story (plays before the events of the main game), the wallpapers might not be up to modern standarts but they are just what one might look for.

Overall: 10 + The game is a good example how a game is well executed and can, though it's quite short be entertaining and satisfying. Given it's a HOG, but this Hog really is one of the good ones.

Value for Money: Very Good. 2,99 Bucks At this price you can't do anything wrong. It's a good Hidden Object Game, and it's entertaining for some hours. It offers some suspense and satisfaction, although the replayabilityt is next to zero.

Recommended

If you are looking for some fast and relaxing fun in a game, or if you are looking for a good example to try out Hidden Object games, this one might be the right one for you. It's inexpensive, it's actually having a story that doesn't come all too cheesy, and it's working perfectly like a charm. The length of the game might dissappoint you and like in any other HOG there are no multiple endings to try out. But you can't be too choosy with a small budget, and for the given price it's really good... even better on sale or with a voucher, bundle...

This is one of the best hidden-object games on Steam and should be played by any fan of the genre.

The game is gorgeously painted and animated, and though the story isn't especially deep, it's well told through the visual mood and gameplay. It's nice and long, with plenty of perfectly balanced and pretty hidden-object scenes, and a decent amount of non-trivial point-and-click adventuring. The game's only deficiency are its mini-game puzzles: there are very few of them, and they are all far, far too easy.

All in all, it's a satisfying way to spend a few hours for those who enjoy relaxing, atmospheric, visually appealing games that demand only an attention to detail.

Game Series

"Angelic Nights" is the first installment of "Sacra Terra" game series.

Game Visual/Audio

There isn't much can be said about the game's visuals asides from that they're nicely done. Pretty much the same thing can be said about the game's audio. The visual and audio can be adjusted in the game's option menu.

Gameplay

There are two different game difficulty modes to choose from: Casual and Expert.

The notebook has two sections: Map and Journal.

The journal keep tracks of all story events and clues in the game.

There are interactive items in the game, they're marked with a symbol, but sometimes these interactive items do require extra items before they can be interact with.

There's a map in the game but it can be only accessed through the notebook. There's no fast travel in this game at all. But it does shows the individual's current position and tasks that can be done at certain location.

Most of the hidden object scenes consists of traditional word list and shadow items. Some of them do require little or no interaction.

Most of the game's puzzles are rather easy to solve without much difficulty. For most of these puzzles there's a reset button. However, there's definitely a skip button, for all of the puzzles. These two features are only present in "Casual" game difficulty mode.

There are collectibles, they are called beyond objects, but otherwise known as morphing objects. There are forty-two beyond objects scattered throughout the game.

Since this is a collector's edition game, there's a bonus chapter, but it can be unlocked only after the main story of the game's completed. The bonus chapter is the pre-sequel to the main story.

Miscellaneous

In the extra section there's some bonus contents: thirteen concept art, a built-in walkthrough, and wallpapers.

There are total of six different trading cards. You only get the three of those cards from the game. There are several ways to get the rest of the trading card: Purchased from Steamâs Market; Trade with friends; Booster Packs and itâs Booster Pack Creator. Badge can be crafted and upgraded with these trading cards. This feature is only available for Steamâs version of this game.

Final thoughts, I definitely enjoying playing this game all the way through to the end. However, there's one thing that would make traveling less tedious is having an map that fast travel ability. As for the replay value I would say it's low. I would recommend this game but not at the full price.
