Welcome! And some FAQs...

Hey all, and welcome. K Station is an adventure game of disappearing lives, that puts its characters through the prism of alternate universes, to see what makes them unhappy -- or happy -- when it's gone.

I've gotten a few common questions during playtesting and soon after release that I wanted to weigh in on...

  • Do I really type in my commands? Oh my, yes.

  • Mac and Linux? I'll be taking a hard look at bugs and sales on Windows before deciding whether to put out Mac and Linux versions. If I do move forward, I'd estimate release in mid-to-late July -- but to be honest, it all depends on how Windows does. I'll be doing my best to get the game out there!

  • Soundtrack? No plans for releasing it separately, though it's not hard to get the soundtrack out of the game if you look through its files...dot dot dot...

Hope you enjoy the game, and please weigh in with bugs and feedbacks in the Steam forums.

Thanks,
Yash (K Station developer)

About This Game

> WHAT IS GOING ON?!


It's New Year's Day, 2319, and the happy denizens of K Station are disappearing one at a time. That's all you know.

> WHO AM I?


You are Maya, a lab assistant and government employee working on K Station. You're hung over from the New Year's Eve party, cranky, street-smart, and a little tired.

> WHAT SHOULD I DO??


Find a way out. Get help. Get to know your colleagues as their lives transform, one missing coworker at a time.

K Station is an adventure game where the world and story are collapsing, room by room and life by life. Discover new lives, new hopes, new dreams and new disappointments -- all from the friends and coworkers you thought you knew.

> HOW DOES IT END?


Logically.

K Station does everything that a classic point-and-click game would but 10 times better and without the pointing or the clicking. The type-and-look style of the game, where you have to type a command in order to make your character do things, is a brilliant and unique idea that I personally have never seen used before and would not be dissapointed if I started seeing it more. It makes the puzzles a little more difficult in the best way. Finally solving a puzzle I was stuck on was incredibly satisfying.

The writing of the game is by far where it shines the most, the dialouge is at time hilarious and very well-written but also gets serious towards the end. The story is incredible, the characters are great, and they all make for a very memorable game.

If you love the point-and-click pixel art genre then I guarantee you'll love K Station. And don't give up when you get stuck, there's a solution, I promise.

Cons: Wanting more content

I recommend it, especially if you're a fan of old style adventure games.

Overview
K Station is a fun and nifty little adventure game that will give you a good few hours of entertainment, all depending on how good you are at solving puzzles. The game isn't for everybody, I think it's important to state that right from the get-go. It'll mostly please gamers that enjoy old-school adventure games, and even more so if you like games where you type commands to interact with everyone and everything in the game world. But I don't fall into any of those groups and still enjoyed it, so there's that.

Pros
Maya (the character you play) is hands down the best thing about the game, her personality and snarky comments gives life to the game in a way that nothing else can. I found myself wanting more of Maya after I had finished the game.
Then there's the art style, the simplistic pixel art fits well with the game and allows it to look good and simplistic at the same time. Another pro is the puzzles, a few of them really had me running around for a good while before I managed to solve them, and the feeling you get when finally solving the hard ones is satisfying. Then there's the ones where you've missed the obvious and feel like a complete idiot afterwards, they'll give you a good laugh afterwards. â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ library probably had me running around for a whole days play session; missing the obvious will do that to you :P

Cons
The art style, yeah, I know, I've already covered this in the pros, but bear with me... I'm talking about the colours, I would have wanted to see more saturated colours being utilised, the levels feel a bit dull with the washed-out colours. It's supposed to be a space station in the future, the colours don't do it justice ;(
Then there's the soundtrack, it has some good bits, don't get me wrong, but the majority of it fits too well with the washed-out colours, giving the world a dull feel. I guess playing your own music in the background isn't too hard (Spotify, iTunes, etc...), but still.
I guess it's only fair to cover the games length as well. I would have wanted it to last a bit longer, maybe 2-3 more levels at least; maybe it's just my longing for more Maya speaks for this :/

Conclusion
Taking both the pros and cons into account, you'd probably have a good time playing K Station. The pros outweigh the cons in my opinion. Everything that has to do with the actual gameplay is solid. It's more or less the aesthetics and sound that falls a bit short.

Final Thoughts
Seeing as this is the developer's first game, and that it was only developed by a single person, all in just over a year, I'd consider it a great game. Kudos to him.
