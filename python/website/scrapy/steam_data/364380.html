About This Game



CINEVEO - Virtual Reality Cinema is a popular VR Cinema app for the HTC Vive, Oculus Rift and Android Cardboard. It lets users simulate their own movie theater in virtual reality, with an unprecedented level of detail, realism and sense of immersion. Grab some popcorn, it's movie time!

With CINEVEO you can watch your 2D & 3D video content (AVI, MP4, MOV, WMV & MKV), as well as streaming video content (YouTube, Vimeo, etc.), on a huge silver screen in six different ultra-detailed and beautifully designed virtual reality cinema environments, such as a 1960s Drive-In Theater, a 4D Movie Theater, a Space Station, The Ocean, a Haunted Valley and The Void.

CINEVEO takes VR Cinema to the next level with powerful features such as multiplayer audience and integrated chat room, watching streaming video content together with other users, integrated web browser, movie subtitle support, dynamic lighting effects, customizable cinema screen, using an external webcam as window to the outside world and much more!

"CINEVEO is not just a game, it's a mind-blowing voyage into a new realm of movie entertainment and forever changes the way you will want to watch movies."

CINEVEO FEATURES



  • "6" VR Cinemas - 4D Movie Theater, 1960s Drive-In Theater, Space Station, Ocean, Haunted Valley & The Void.

  • Plays 2D & 3D Video Files - Plays all video formats smoothly (AVI, MP4, MOV, WMV & MKV).
  • Plays Online Streaming Video - Watch streaming videos on YouTube, Vimeo and other sites.
  • Watch 3D Movies - Plays 3D videos in SBS and Over / Under with a superb 3D effect.
  • Movie Subtitle Support - Watch movies with subtitles in any language with SRT files.

  • Advanced Dynamic Lighting - Dynamic casting of light, color, reflections, shapes and shadows.
  • CINEVEO's ULTRA 3D Modeâ¢ - Makes you feel as if you are literally inside the movie.
  • CINEVEO's Stereo PFOV Lightingâ¢ - Extends the movie beyond the edges of the cinema screen.

  • Integrated Web Browser - Watch videos, surf the web and play browser games in a VR Cinema.
  • Multiplayer Audience - Sit alone in the cinema or with a live audience made up of real people.
  • Chat Room - Chat with other CINEVEO users and watch videos together.
  • Customizable Cinema Screen - You can change the size and the shape (curved or flat screen).
  • Hands-Free Interface Controls - Control the app without keyboard or mouse.
  • Webcam Video - Use your webcam in VR to see what is going on around you in the real world.

This is an interesting tech demo -- sort of. There are a few things that need to be done before this app is really ready for prime time.

This is delivered as a folder. In the folder, there are two separate Unity executables for each of six environments. And then there's an additional batch file to support DirectX 11. It requires installation of a specific CODEC pack from GitHub in order to operate, and overall is at a quality similar to the Multiplayer Theater demo that is available for free.

At the very least, take half an hour, write a launcher. Put a button to download the required CODEC pack, have Steam launch that by default.

Use Environment.GetSpecialFolder and ask for videos -- look for the movies there. If you're not going to do that, then at least use a single movies folder for all the environments. But using the folder under Steam versus the user's Video folder (or any user specified folder) is something that is going to annoy users.

Think about if you really need 12 separate executables here -- that cannot be pleasant to maintain in Unity. Those scenes can all be in a single executable, and you can use LoadLevel to load the level you want. So instead of separate executables, make one executable.

At that point you need a menu -- there are a lot of good Oculus-enabled menus in the Unity asset store, and the default Unity UI is workable as well. Basically, if you're going to ask for money you need to be a cut above the tech demos in the forums, for free, at the Oculus forums.

Keep in mind, Oculus is currently a developer only club -- developers are going to hold you to a higher standard than traditional users may, usually, and are going to have seen the various demos on the Oculus site most likely. I know it's the first thing that I did after I unboxed my Oculus -- install the SDK, look at what people are uploading, see "hey, I should do that."

This is not currently ready for prime time, and needs some work before it will be. As a demo on GitHub, I'd recommend it. As a commercial paid product, it needs some basic professionalism brought to the table.
