About This Game

MagNets: an intense arcade-style robot-recycling collect-em-up! Players must fight to restore order in Polarity City, one arena at a time. Do this by capturing malfunctioning Bloxbots in your Electromagnetic Nets (or 'MagNets'), and recycling them into tools to complete the level and power-ups to survive their attacks!

Featuring:


  • Compulsive, frantic and challenging gameplay over three difficulties: Medium, Hard and Impossible
  • Single-player campaign with 20 levels over 4 zones
  • Two-player local PvP!
  • Child-friendly casual mode (more health, no highscores)
  • Character unlocks
  • Optimised for controller play

Coming Soon:


  • Network multiplayer
  • Co-op multiplayer
  • 4-player support
  • More free characters and content!

MagNets is made by Total Monkery, an independent family-run games studio in Devon, England. Collaborators on MagNets include Phil Corbett (Concept Art), award-winning musician Jamie Hamshere (Music and SFX) and Harry Holmwood (Music).

Hidden gem is a term that you will see me use quite a bit. It is a term that describes Total Monkery’s MagNets better than any other that I could possibly use. From time to time I look through my own personal wishlist – which is routinely made up of all of the games with trading cards that I do not own – and I’ll gather a list of games that I believe have been under appreciated. These are generally games that have been positively reviewed by the Steam user base but are not owned by many of my over three hundred Steam friends (many of whom have game lists in the thousands) and it’s not on many wish lists (odd for trading card games). In the case of MagNets only two of my friends owned the game and only four friends wanted the game yet it currently enjoys a 100% positive feedback score and it’s now over fifteen months old. It fit the profile to be a hidden gem.

MagNets is a very enjoyable casual action game that is almost puzzle-like and definitely fits the arcade genre. The aim of each level is to capture malfunctioning bloxbots and recycling them to create the tools that you require to complete each level and their power-ups. One of the early highlights are the tutorial levels. They teach you the fundamental skills and abilities required to succeed throughout the game but are also challenging enough that the player doesn’t feel as though they are going through the motions. I like this because the game isn’t supposed to be easy. For example, the levels of the game are titled ‘medium, hard and impossible’ for a reason yet if you find the gameplay too difficult or strenuous for your liking then you have a child accessible (easier) level to play at which is the game’s casual mode.

The presentation value in the game is solid enough to the point where it gets the job done without wowing anybody. It’s not something that you’ve never seen before graphics wise but the soundtrack is fantastic. It definitely fits the atmosphere and vibe that the developers were trying to achieve with the game and although it gets a little repetitive – do note that I did have the game open for five hours – but I enjoyed it. The controls and the game play are tight.

It’s truly one of the better hidden gems to have been released over the past couple of years and its a real shame that more players haven’t played or bought this game yet. It’s time that you do!

MagNets is an arena based game with simple problem solving. You play as bot, let's call him Mag. Since Mag has a round-head I assume he dislikes square-heads.
Gameplay Video:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ki-m9Ck9g1k
Starting at a relaxing pace the game quickly ramps up the amount of enemies thrown at you and speed they get deployed. Gradually being introduced to new mechanics the basics always stay the same, cast your electromagnetic net (MagNet) around the square-heads and forge the parts collected into tools, preferably ones with rounded edges of course. Using these tools you then solve 'puzzles' contained within each arena and clearing them rewards progress to the next. After completing X amount of levels you will face boss fights and get introduced to characters you really don't want to destroy. :)

Some of the later levels became almost tower-defense like in nature requiring a balancing act of defending structures and taking down all square-heads, which was much higher paced but controls stayed responsive. Great soundtrack, interesting side-story, loveable characters and very family friendly. Cheers.
