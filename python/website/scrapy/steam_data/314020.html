About This Game

Lose yourself in the mesmerizing, fantastical world of Morphopolis, a game about transformation and discovery.

Inspired by Machinarium and The Tiny Bang Story, Morphopolis takes you through a lucid insect world in a puzzle adventure that will stick with you for years.

You take on the role of an aphid grub and guide him through several stages of metamorphosis, as he journeys for his spawning ground deep within the insect microcosm. Discover hidden secrets as you journey deeper and deeper into the game world.

Winner of the Association of Illustrators Award, Morphopolis features 100% hand-drawn art and animation. Its soundtrack was composed by well-known British composer Thom Robson with the aim of captivating and immersing the player as he or she takes their time to solve puzzles and enjoy the organic world of Morphopolis.

We are very grateful for all the encouragement we have and during the production of this game and we hope you enjoy playing Morphopolis as much as we did making it.

Over-View
Morphopolis, a simple yet intriguing game. A pathway made to learn the morphology of the small creatures living in unseen ground. Playing a tiny creature gathering and evolving to the next stage. It is an abstract except realistic concept of evolution.

Presentation
You are first invited by the artistic style of visual artwork as you soon immerse into the world of insects. The visual layout is painted into 2-Dimensional layers which creates the 3-Dimensional atmosphere and depth. The colours are all earthy and saturated which emphasises the textural tone of the visual design. The creator really has brought out the artistic feel and paintings to life.

My sound was turned off so I cannot comment about the sounds. But I suggest that if it had peaceful and natural sounds that it would enhance the experience of being one with the insects of nature.

Plot
At first, especially during the tutorial, I had a vague understanding of story. Eventually as the levels progress you begin to understand a bit about the story. You begin as one small creature and each stage you are finding elements such as the insects prey (food) or items to assist the travel (to gain access by âgiving/findingâ items) for the next creature. You crawl inside the head of the next creature and begin their journey. Resembling of life, there is not alot of text to guide but just the act of doing and completing the life-style of the insect.

Gameplay
You are set in these surreal 2-Dimensional layered artworks which is nature. Simple game of âeye-spyâ to progress adds to the simplicity of these game. The end level could be thought as the boss, but is basically a âsimpleâ puzzle to solve. Both getting progressively harder in each stage by either having more items to find, difficulty, and multiple items sets to find. It somewhat feels like a child-like game, but itâs creativity and puzzles, can be fun towards elderly people looking to burn some time.

Controls
The controls are fairly simply and all movement is controlled by a simple mouse clicks. The character moves by holding down the mouse as the character moves towards it. This development of movement is a really nice touch towards the essence of the lower level world. However I do have to complain that there is no quicker movement. Travelling back screens if you missed pieces is very tedious as you are trapped in the sluggish movement of your creature, but at least it does give some reason on how these bugs move. That they travel at their own pace and shouldnât be rushed. Controlling movement around objects and towards areas can be fiddly as the direction is not always accurate when you want to turn above and right except it just goes straight up. ( -.- )... At least it has a unique style of movement and is a patient game as you find the items.

To complete it section you need to collect items, these items can be collected by the power of your mouse. By clicking on the items required they are instantly added to your âinventoryâ of sorts. Simple mechanic but effective. It plays really well to the calm aspect of the game.

Replayability
It is unlikely to replay the game after it is finished. The story will not change and levels just become easier. You can replay in the sense of finding the items again or redoing the puzzles but there is no real reason to replay the story again.

Achievements are progressive achievements (get it as you play) as they are all story-based. After each level completed you should be awarded an achievement and 100% completing this game can be done with ease.

Overall
Simple, yet fun game. Curiosity to the insect world brings interest to this game. I really enjoyed the artwork and solving the puzzles (totally did not google them, Iâm smart I swear). It really engages the players down to the small level and this game should be played. I would have really liked more elements to the game such as story content.
