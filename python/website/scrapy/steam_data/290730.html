About This Game

My Lands is a free online RTS where dark and light forces fight for Black Gem and dominion in a vast fantasy world.
Cooperation between players, joint economy development, creation of clans and alliances and elaboration of a team strategy serve as the very foundation of the game. Player chooses one of four races to lead it to the victory through ruthless wars over dominion in the universe of My Lands.

Main Features

No to "grind": a unique system of skills allows players to develop cities and heroes even while being offline.
Vast game world: infinite expanses of land to be explored and conquered by thousands of players in one universe.
Advanced quest system: even the most experienced gamers will be struck by the great number and variety of quests which encourage faster development and help in making out your own game strategy.
Diplomacy, intelligence and trade: a well thought-out team strategy leads to victories in the world of My Lands.

Granted, I'm not really giving this game much of a chance (only several hours). But do I need to? I've spent 99% of my time waiting for things to happen. It says, "Here's a quest: click that thing and build it." I do so. I wait several minutes to hours. Then it says, "Here's another quest: click that other thing and build it." I do so. I wait several minutes to hours. Rinse. Repeat. Realize that progress in this game is P2W, and incredibly tedious. Quit and write negative review.

I don't know how this game currently has 88% positive reviews, because it definitely isn't that good. Somebody may have rigged the reviews. My guess is that with time, that'll get down below 50%.

The mentorship system seems to be a broken one. Mentors gain rewards when their apprentices accomplish quests, but there is no motivation for the mentor to actually do anything to help the apprentice. You can request resources from the mentor, but they don't have to give them to you. I'm not sure, but it seems like you can't fire your mentor, either. Once you've accepted them, they own you, and leech off of you.

You should also be aware that no matter how much progress you've made in the game (even if you've been playing for years), they'll delete your kingdom if it's inactive for between 7-30 days. So... basically, what's the point of playing this?

How many more terrible design decisions are there that I'm not aware of, yet?

It's just too bad, because there's something very tempting about the slow-growth style of this game. But why waste any time on it if you'll just lose it all when real life happens?

Typical browsergame style city building game. This means: Everything takes ages. And then more ages. And then a lot of ages. And then you decide to quit before everything takes even longer. DLC is far too expensive. But for that price you get some bonuses that make the game a bit less unplayable... timed bonuses, of course! Yes, you can invest roughly 500 Euro (without the initial 75p discount) to get bonuses that last for up to a year. For that price you get over 40 times the Skyrim Legendary Edition on sale, or still 16 times without a sale of which each one lasts for up to a year also if you play for several hours every day. With the difference that Skyrim actually is good.

An example: You build your building in a minute. Then you upgrade it in an hour. Then you upgrade it in I think three or six hours. Then in 24 hours. And that is only if you have enough people.
Wait, what? Yes, you need free people to upgrade buildings. Which you get - surprise - by building and upgrading certain buildings. If you have partial people, the upgrade takes longer.

Then there is science, which works pretty well so far, except everything takes ages. The first science thing is done in an hour. Then if you upgrade your buildings science goes faster, but you can't upgrade enough in a reasonable time to make it worthwhile.

The "innovative" system that makes you not grinding is - you guess it - resources trickle at a ridiculous small rate (since you'd need to upgrade buildings to get more resources) while you're offline. You can use them then to upgrade buildings and build armies. At which point you probably rather want to play through Ride to Hell: Retribution, Secret of the Magic Crystals, Rambo, Kane & Lynch 2 or even E.T. 2600 because you have more fun there.
