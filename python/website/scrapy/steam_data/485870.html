About This Game



Have you ever wanted to poop on party goers but were too socially awkward to just go for it? Well, Super Duper Party Pooper is about to change all that. In this follow up to the smash hit There’s Poop In My Soup by Rudder Games the player will find themselves in the middle of a raw human story of existential panic.



Visit exotic locations, travel from beijing to paris, from the polar ice caps to desert islands Sample the local delicacies, let them pile up inside until you couldn’t possibly take anymore, then, turn around, take aim, and drop a deuce or two on the party crowd.



Super Duper Party Pooper is a rhythm based party pooping simulator that has the player chomping down as much food as possible to the beat of the music. The more food you eat, the more poops you create, much like real life. The player then unleashes those poops onto the party crowd trying to rack up as many points as possible, allowing them to unlock new locations and bonus features.



But the fun doesn’t stop there, Players can add their own music to the game and poop to the beat of their favorite tunes, you can pinch a loaf to the beat of beyonce’s new hit single, drop a deuce to michael jackson's thriller or drop the kids off at the pool while listening to Adele’s relationship problems, the poosibilities are endless. The game also features Steam Workshop integration and the characters are completely customizable allowing the community to edit or create new characters, animations, pooping styles and more.

There are parties out there just begging to get pooped on, what are you waiting for?

Super Duper Party Pooper is a rhythm game with a silly sense of humor.

I wish I could give this a neutral review instead of a negative one, but Steam doesn't give me the option, and I can't bring myself to give it a positive recommendation.

I want to start by saying I actually loved the previous game made by the same developer. "There's poop in my soup" was also equally as silly, but the gameplay gripped me more.

Super Duper Party Pooper is meant to be a rhythm game, yet it doesn't seem to reward me properly for going with the beat. I've played the drums for years, and have played tonnes of rhythm games throughout my life, so I'm not new to the genre, and have a very good sense of rhythm, but the games notes just don't seem to match the music's beat. I even tried playing on another monitor to make sure it wasn't mind causing the problem, but it was still slightly off. That's the biggest problem. It's like if you play a shooter and the bullets don't go straight to where you're aiming. Who knows, maybe it's because I have a very good sense of rhythm that I might notice it more than most people, or my computer is messing it up somehow, or perhaps I'm just terrible at this particular game.

I still unlocked 5 out of 6 levels despite that because the game gives you visual queues for when you're meant to press the button.
Each level is set in a new location, which looks relatively different to the previous one. The music more or less fits the theme of the environment. In my opinion, the music is the best part about the game. Unfortunately, I don't even think it was made for the game.

The game does a good job at keeping levels fresh by adding new obstacles constantly. You'll need to focus on multiple places at once, or avoid bombs. When you successfully press a button at the right time, your character will eat and fill up their stomach. Depending on how well you do throughout the level, you will have a certain amount of poo to fling at the backup dancers behind you, for a few extra points at the end.

The game also allows you to import your own music, but it's way too frustrating to use. You'll need to copy paste the exact file path to tell it which song to use, and it only allows for mp3s. The game would benefit from a file browser. Not only that, but you then need to enter the Beats per minute for the song on your own so the game knows at what speed you should be matching the notes at. It's not like in games like Audiosurf where the game detects all that on its own. The game recommends using a certain site to find the BPM, but there's only a set amount of songs there from what I can tell. The chances of finding your song are very slim.


Eventually I figured out a song that I wanted to use, inputted the BPM, but I encountered a problem. Because it was quite a slow song, some of the objects I needed to press were going off the screen before their time came.
In other words, there are some food items that have lights on them. They slowly pass by your screen in the rhythm of the music, and when their lights turn green you are allowed to eat them. But because my music was slow, the lights only became green when they were off screen. It made playing that song very hard.

Overall, I can't recommend the game. Yes, it's very cheap so you can give it a go without losing much, and it isn't awful. It's just that I can't enjoy a rhythm-based game that doesn't work properly. Again, I think the dev is capable since I 100%ed their previous game, but with broken mechanics in this, it just isn't fun. And I don't think I would enjoy it too much even if the rhythm-timing was fixed.
