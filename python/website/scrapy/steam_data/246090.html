About This Game

In Spacebase DF-9, you'll build a home among the stars for a motley population of humans and aliens as they go about their daily lives. Mine asteroids, discover derelicts, and deal with the tribulations of galactic resettlement in Earth's distant future. Meteor impacts! Explosive decompression! Unbearable loneliness!

Key Features:

Base Construction
  • Build your base using available matter and expand by mining more from nearby asteroids.
  • Gradually expand from a small oxygen-filled room to a thriving, well-decorated hub.
  • Build Gardens, Pubs, Fitness and Research facilities, and more.
Citizen Management
  • Assign citizens to duties to ensure survival-critical duties are performed efficiently.
  • Monitor citizen needs to ensure overall morale, balancing duty demands with citizen happiness.
Base Defense
  • Send security forces to investigate mysterious derelicts that appear near the base.
  • Fend off hostile invaders as well as parasites that
  • Construct turrets to lend firepower to hostile
Research and Progression
  • Research new equipment, armor, weapons, and base structures.
  • Discover blueprints for new technologies aboard derelict ships.
  • Research cures for deadly space diseases.

I quite loved the potential for this game, but in practise it is horribly disapponting!

My first playthrough went fine, pretty basic management fare but then a raider showed up and I discovered that the 'security' crew assignments I'd made meant people couldn't last two seconds against the lone raider..
Alright then, it was the first go and in tutorial so I can accept an hours play lost and decided to start a new game.

First realisation, there was no "this will erase your data" or "do you want to save" warning. Surprising and not great, but as said, I hadn't planned to anyway so I went ahead with a new real start.

My hopes for the game returned, I enjoyed starting anew and building up the base and this time I had a plan for raiders!
My plan... reassign every crewmen to security and overwhelm in numbers! XD
Not great I know.. but it worked, though highly tiresome to have to remember and reassign everyone back to their old jobs as you can't assign saved positions to default back to.
Then of course, things went bad again...

I find a derelict ship, so badly laid out it could've been a rushed post-battle vessel from Galaxy Truckers.
So of course, I decide to tear everything out as salvage materials.
Unknown to me, one crew decided to remove the ships oxegen generator and then the door of another room from the inside.. leaving him stuck and likely gormless, as there was no notification of him being stuck.

I'll admit a possibly mistake on my part: Not painstakingly watching and assigning each item removed one at a time, but when I tried that previously the crew had a habit of just heading off to bed or returning to my base for a minute to build some shelves I'd requested another builder to build, before coming back.. which is why this time I just left them to it.

Anyway, I found Mr.Gormless and got him to let himself out of the room but before he got any further the oxegen in the ship got too low and it turns out people panic when they realise the oxegen is low (fair enough I'm sure).
Unfortunately, this panic was apparently too much to allow the builder to obey my orders to rebuild an oxegen generator.
Close to frustration I see my second builder heading over in a space suit to save the day..

Unfortunately, Gormlessness is clearly contagious as our superhero stepped out of the airlock, went into a panic himself, rushed back into the airlock and flushed himself into space...

So yea, there you have it. My adventures in Spacebase DF-9. I advise you against having any of your own.

Posted: 13 June
Dissapointing.... Back when the game was in early development I fell victim to its "appeal". I saw a game with potential and I was all and all hopeful. In those ealier days the game was riddled with bugs and glitches, and when I found out they were dropping the game and rushing development, I was hopeful they would eliminate those bugs and glitches in the game. So as a review of the official release of version 1.06 here is the breakdown. I'll have a simple review and more in depth... or in this game... anger driven review...

----- Simple ----

The game is crap... It had so much potential but since it got the axe and a speedy release... it has so many issues. I honestly tried to play the game again... but yet I wasn't able to play longer than 10 minues. I would not recommend this game to anybody unless it's less than a dollar or given to you free. It's buggy... rough to control, and just honestly crap.

----- Angered Version -----

Graphics:
As this is not a "Highly graphical" game I wouls say they are acceptable. The sprites perform actions that is befiting, and nothing to standoutish. However in aspect of gameplay the "isometric" angle makes the game difficult to know exactly what is going on. In the typical fashion the walls disappear in a way to allow you to see in the rooms, however being able to roatate the camera would be nice.

Gameplay:
This is where the game loses me. Although the controls aren't "hard" they are difficult in there own way. I've played many builder games, and most games play similarily with minor adjustments to the controls. In more recent terms since the era of shooter games almost every game depends upon the W-A-S-D model. This game doesn't stray too far fram that idea and it feels comfortable... at least until you start building. At that point the game takes a turn for the worst. Edge scrolling doesn't feel like it's in existence, so your dependant upon the WASD model, but once you open the construction menu, foul language fills the mouth as when you click with your mouse to select the item and press "D" to move your contruction item changes. As soon as that construction menu comes up the game shifts from a comfortable WASD model to a point and click model. This wouldn't be a problem if they didn't establish you could use the WASD in the first place.... So that is annoying. Then it gets worse. As you play you decide to speed up the game, and most games using some button near the WASD (tab, 123, something) changes the game speed, but in comes the mouse model, and you have to click the speed options. Now I wouldn't have an issue with this, if it wasn't for the fact that when any ship arrived or any notification appears the game switches back to speed 1.... and DOES NOT RETURN.....I find it quite annoying because I'll be in the middle of waiting for my characters to build a room... then all the sudden a message... then the question, "Why are my guys moving so slow"... then oh gotta click that speed... then the game goes NOPE... and another message. It just continually pesteres you until you get so angry that you give up.

AI:
I don't normally judge AI as it's always an issue with all games. It is always about finding that right balance between what the computer needs to process and what the character prioritizes.... unfortunately it appears that this game said, "F*$k it... have the guys do whatever." And like loyal programs they do whatever. You have absolutely no control over the individual character, so when your trying to finish that bed... your have to wait until the game decides... "know what I think I should build that bed." But at that point your characters are now falling over because they are so tired... that they have no energy... then get depressed and not want to work... so now that bed will never get done.... it annoying the bleeping crap out of me as I was just trying to enjoy the game.

Glitches and Bugs:
Yes I'm bringing it up... as a "fully" released game you would expect most of the bugs and glitches to be taken care of... in fact thats what most of the follow up forums established... but know what.... turns out that your characters get stuck (dumb AI) and because you can't do that they sufficate.... yeah... so just started finished the basic build... airlock... air... and such... but oh no... two of my guys just sat there... doing... NOTHING... could it be AI? probably... but to me that's a bug. But it's not the worst oh no... in my honest to goodness no opinion try I got "invaded" another ship attached itself to me, and I was assulted. After killing the AI I noticed, Hey there is a gun outside... Then I slowly watched one of my security guards go out to take care of it.... two shots later dead.... ok..... so what does the AI do? "Well he's dead... probably should take care of that... but naa...." Guess where my miners had to go to get resources? Right in the line of fire.... so there goes two of my miners... Well maybe I can control a security... oh wait can't control the guys so whoops... maybe set a security point for them.... nope... dead... dead and dead..... I'm happy they incorperated the guns for defences... but when a gun is established as enemy and even if you took over everything it appears that that guns still acts hostile.... My frustrations knows no bounds.

Overall the game is fine.... just fine.... not great... not good... just fine.... if you want something new to distract you for 10 minutes... here is the perfect game for you... but don't buy unless it's on uber sale.... and by uber sale like... I dunno $0.99 per copy... or heck maybe if the company pays you to play... becuase honestly this game isn't worth anything more.
