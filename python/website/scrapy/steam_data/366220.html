Patch Notes 22 June 2016 Cave Dwellings are live!

General Changes

  • Removed the check preventing gift items from being created (these now require a GM account)
  • Fixed an issue that could sometimes allow for corpses to be looted on PvE
  • Added generic templates for rift items and creatures.
  • Note: These were added so that Wurm Unlimited users can have fun with the rift feature assets without needing to mod them in.
  • Fixed issues with graphics.jar containing older .dae models and missing the rift assets.
  • Source Crystals can now be mined from any tile type.
  • Silver Mirrors can no longer be traded in containers.


  • Cave dwellings!
    • Due to their effect with PvP servers they default to off but can be turned on via features.
    • They require reinforced flat floors.
    • Can be built up to 8 stories.
    • Plain stone building types only.
    • Can be built against walls on deed only.
    • Take fast decay when off deed.
    • Merchants may be placed underground, traders may not.

  • Bugfix - Fixed a few grammatical and text errors.
  • Bugfix - Destroying altars now checks the 'destroy items' permission for allies and non-citizens
  • Bugfix - Scythes no longer take damage when harvesting non grain fields.
  • Bugfix - Movement speed on bridges corrected.
  • Bugfix - Attaching a lock to a group of items now only locks the first unlocked item.
  • Bugfix - Fences in arched walls have improved permission checks for destroying.
  • Bugfix - Unlocked and unmoored boats on PvP servers can now be dragged by anyone with the appropriate strength.
  • Bugfix - Keyholders can now take items from locked containers even if they lack permissions.
  • Bugfix - Corrected an issue which allowed some priests to pray at any altar.
  • Bugfix - Life transfer now correctly heals based off the resulting damage dealt to opponents. This may result in reduced healing affect against opponents with higher armor protection.

20/JUNE/2016 - BETA - 1.0.0.7 Update

Patch Information
We are pushing an update out to the beta branch to address issues regarding that branch before it moves to live. We apologize for the short notice, but as some of these changes are fixes for the much anticipated Cave Dwellings feature, we felt they needed to be included.

Due to the pack issues mentioned before, this will be another large download.

There are no breaking changes between client and server, so there is no version bump for this beta update. The version number will remain 1.0.0.7 as to not prevent clients from connecting to servers already running at 1.0.0.7. See the "Going forward" section below for an important note on future versioning.

While there are no breaking changes, it is strongly recommended that all servers update to 1.0.0.7 due to the bugs fixed by this update.

Edit
Beta has been updated to address the issue with the settings window not opening. It will also now be an always on top window to assist users when it would open behind the main game window.

Cave Dwelling fixes
  • Layer check for tile border actions.
  • Fix so merchants will appear in caves when used there.
  • Restricted traders to surface only.
General Changes
  • Removed the check preventing gift items from being created (these now require a GM account)
  • Fixed an issue that could sometimes allow for corpses to be looted on PvE
  • Added generic templates for rift items and creatures.
    Note: These were added so that Wurm Unlimited users can have fun with the rift feature assets without needing to mod them in.
  • Fixed issues with graphics.jar containing older .dae models and missing the rift assets.
Going forward
We realize that adding code changes without much lead time may make things difficult for server administrators. We will be moving to a new versioning system that will only force same version when there are breaking changes or if the administrator has strict versioning enabled. More information on this system will be made available after the next update to live.

About This Game

What is Wurm Unlimited?


Wurm Unlimited is your full standalone version of the fantasy sandbox world Wurm Online, the MMORPG where the players are in charge!

A pioneer in the ideas of player influence, crafting and adventure, it is now one of the most deep and feature packed sandbox experiences available.
Whether you enjoy building your home, conquering kingdoms or hunting dragons, Wurm will let you.

Explore the world and make your mark!

In Wurm Unlimited, players can explore an elaborate fantasy sandbox world in the predefined Creative or Adventure modes as well as set up their own game server and invite friends to live and experience the Wurm universe.

What can I do in Wurm Unlimited?

  • Host your own Virtual World! The Server Management GUI hels you change settings such as skillrate gains, action timers, creature count ...
  • Explore huge, diverse landmasses with creatures and mysteries!
  • Modify the terrain; dig, raise, flatten and sculpt the land around you!
  • Craft and use thousands of unique items.
  • Wage war on other kingdoms, and lead yours to victory.
  • Discover and fight over religious artifacts on a PvP server.
  • Capture and breed animals from the environment.
  • Train 133 Skills, 10 Player Characteristics, and 3 Religion Characteristics.
  • Follow one of four unique deities and religions. Or attempt to become a Deity yourself and join the existing ones on the moon of Valrei!
  • Hunt creatures such as the unique red dragon, forest giant, kyklops, troll king and others!
  • Become a priest or champion of your religion and learn powerful spells and enchantments.
  • Choose one of five meditation paths and take advantage of special meditation abilities.
  • Earn as many of our 200+ skill and achievement titles as you have time for!
  • Mount various creatures, from horses and carts to unicorns, bears and even dragons!
  • Construct, crew or even captain six different ship types with other players, from small rowing boats to impressive caravels.
  • Build a variety of structures, from guard towers to stone houses to fences, bridges and statues.
  • Found your own settlement wherever you desire; own land, build a farm, a castle, or perhaps an entire village!
  • Pave roads to connect settlements and plant signs to improve local infrastructure.
  • Live off the land by creating fields to farm a variety of crops including potatoes, garlic, cotton, wheat, strawberries, pumpkins and more!
  • Cook nutrious food using a huge range of ingredients.

Wurm Unlimited is one of those gems that's not for everybody. I had mixed feelings about giving this game a thumbs up or down, but only because of it's price tag. I'll get to that a bit later though.

First thing's first. Going into this game I knew to expect a few things. This game is from the early 2000's, which means the graphics were going to be severly dated by todays standards. I also knew this game wasn't for the faint of heart. It's not your typical survival game, and it doesn't pull it's punches. This was quite evident when I first entered the world, spawning almost direcly on top of a spider which proceeded to slaughter me without remorse and then camp my corpse.

Ok, so I mentioned a bit about graphics. This game is pretty dated as far as the graphics go. If you can't look past that then you really won't enjoy what this gem of a game truly has to offer. Being an all-around gamer I found this to be not so bad. In fact, there are many aspects of the graphics that I actually really like. Little details, like fish swimming in the water, are very nicely done.

The interface of this game will frustrate the hell out of a lot of the newer generation of gamers. Most of your actions are done via right-click menus and require you to have certain items or tools "activated" before the right-click menu will give you the options your looking for. For example, having a hand saw activated and right-clicking a log in your inventory will give you completely different crafting options than if you had a knife activated. That said, once you get the hang of the interface you can actually see it's really well designed for the level of detail and openness this game offers.

It's hard to judge the world of this game, having not seen all of it yet myself and also knowing that you can use custom worlds with a little tweaking. It seemed very "real", as if the map was created naturally rather than by a person at a computer. The jist of it is that there are 3 kingoms on opposing sides of the map and they expand their territories as much as they can, eventually meeting each other and then... Well, that's open to whatever the players want do at that point. They can be diplomatic and trade lands for other lands, they can war and attempt to take land from the other kingdoms, or they can just live peacefully with their current borders. There are goals to the game, with "winning" being an option, but depending on what style of server you play on those goals can either be important or not.

Now we get to crafting. This is where this game REALLY gets some major points. The crafting system is incredibly indepth, as mentioned earlier. It's so indepth that you can't actually craft a lump of metal, which you have already smelted, unless it's reheated to a "glowing" state. In addition, the more indepth a crafted object is the more resources and crafting stages it requires. Example, to build a guard tower you have to first build the base of the tower, which is easy. Then you have to add 500 stone blocks (a crafted item), 500 clay, and 100 planks (a crafted item) before the tower is finished. This is a HUGE task! Another example would be a knife. You can't simply place a lump of ore on a stick and call it a knife (lookin at you Minecraft). You have to craft the knife blade out of metal, and then a handle from wood, and put the two together to finally make the knife. Crafting materials and requirements seemed very logical, almost "real". That seems to be the theme of this game, bring as much reality to it as you can, while not loosing the fact that it's a fantasy game.

In addition to crafting, the materials of the world didn't come in numbered items. For example, if you picked up a rock you didn't have 1 rock. You had so many kg of rock. Each time you crafted something it took a portion of that rock to craft it. You could also have many rocks, of various weights. Some might be too small to craft an item with, some might be big enough to have leftover. They could also be combined to merge their individual portions into one larger portion. So you could have one rock that is 10kg and another that is 20kg, merge them together and get a 30kg rock. The weight of items was very important to monitor, as your movement speed didn't just go from 100% to encumbered. There were actually multiple stages to walking speed. So carrying 40% of your maximum weight would actually have an impact on the speed you walked, again very realistic.

So getting down to the nitty gritty. What is Wurm Unlimited? Well, it's a very good MMO, in a fantasy setting, including a high detail of realism, that's been converted to home use. If it's just you playing it you'll find the world very empty and a lot of things will be a very difficult challenge. If it's you and a few friends there is quite a bit of enjoyment to be found, although the 3 kingdom aspect of the game will probably never be used. If it's you and a dedicated server of 30+ players you might be able to experience all this game has to offer.

So do I recommend it? This is where the difficulty is. For a player such as myself, who loves a challenge and doesn't mind tedious grunt work to get a task done, this game is absolutely awesome. For the newer generation of (spoiled) players who like to press one key and click one button to get stuff done this game will come across as boring and very hard to play. Personally though I absolutely love this game for it's mix of realistic crafting, true sandbox environent, and RPG aspects.

So again, do I recommend it? Oy... It's extremely hard to decide. If I was to look at this game 10 years ago, at it's current price, I'd think it was a hell of a deal. Today, not so sure. Personally I think $32 is fairly overpriced. That said, I got it on the Steam Sale for $16 which is a much more reasonable cost. So?

Yes... Yes, I recommend it. ---- BUT ---- Only if you can get it on sale at 50% off it's $32 price tag.

Per Wikipedia, Wurm Online started beta back in 2003 and went gold in 2006. In 2015, the developer completed a standalone version of Wurm Online and packaged it as Wurm Unlimited.

Pros
- I like the soundtrack and find it soothing. There's something soothing about mindlessly mining ore and listening to the audio that keeps you in a trance.
- Lots of parameters can be altered to generate the experience.

Pro and Con - depending on your perception:
- In depth crafting system. A large amount of resource gathering is required to build end products. Building ships, buildings, etc. involve quite a bit of resource gathering and give a sense of accomplishment when you finally build the finished product.
- Age of game. You get a product that has been around for quite a while. This means that people have worked on it for quite a while to develop the systems (Pro). It also means that you're not likely to get a "fresh interface" or the latest graphics (con)

Cons:
- Declining population:
Steam charts - http://steamcharts.com/app/402370
Steam charts - http://steamcharts.com/app/366220
Per the above, you can see that there's never been more than 2000 players online playing this game at one time. The population and number of servers continue to decline.
- Cost:
As of June 2016, the regular price for this game is $30 ($29.99 for those worried about a penny).

As far as would I recommend it to others... Overall, I enjoyed playing this game, but I would have to give it a no based on the current price tag and current decline of the population. Truthfully, this game is not for everyone. If you can find the game on sale at a significant discount, then it's worth picking up, but only if you like games that involve an extensive resource collecting experience where you don't mind finding yourself spending a considerable amount of time playing solo.

Community: Very friendly, people construct towns together with complete strangers and form bonds just like in real life.
Gameplay: Dated, but very solid and compelling, deep. Turn-based combat mixed with realtime movement
Visuals: While not as highly detailed as newer games on the market, Wurm's forests and mountains feel very real and get many things right that other games never will.

Excellent game for those who desire a greater sense of depth, adventure, and progression from their survival sandbox type experience. Wurm Online (Unlimited is basically a player-hosted version of WO) is a very old game, and you will not find a game that plays quite like wurm to date... a very specific niche in gamefeel that makes this a hard game to recommend without one first trying out Wurm Online for free. Creation in wurm arises from necessity, players construct the very crucial highways and sideroads that span across the world, everything seems to have a purpose. This is because everything that isn't natural landscape is player-made, giving every server and every world on the server a very real, breathing sense of life and wonder. For those who admire an abundant amount of roles to fill and an elegant (if hard to get used to at first) style of crafting, where things can be constructed based on what makes physical sense and not just what the developers want you to put together, Wurm is possibly the perfect game.

Bottom Line: It's great for the people that find joy in living peacefully instead of constant looting and pillaging (although there are places for that in wurm), it's great for people who enjoy turn-based games like classic roguelikes who want a compelling multiplayer experience that is a bit more up their alley. Wurm is a great game for many people but it is certainly a very unique experience, which is not very easy to break into without a bit of effort.

For example, having a hand saw activated and right-clicking a log in your inventory will give you completely different crafting options than if you had a knife activated. That said, once you get the hang of the interface you can actually see it's really well designed for the level of detail and openness this game offers.

It's hard to judge the world of this game, having not seen all of it yet myself and also knowing that you can use custom worlds with a little tweaking. It seemed very "real", as if the map was created naturally rather than by a person at a computer. The jist of it is that there are 3 kingoms on opposing sides of the map and they expand their territories as much as they can, eventually meeting each other and then... Well, that's open to whatever the players want do at that point. They can be diplomatic and trade lands for other lands, they can war and attempt to take land from the other kingdoms, or they can just live peacefully with their current borders. There are goals to the game, with "winning" being an option, but depending on what style of server you play on those goals can either be important or not.

Now we get to crafting. This is where this game REALLY gets some major points. The crafting system is incredibly indepth, as mentioned earlier. It's so indepth that you can't actually craft a lump of metal, which you have already smelted, unless it's reheated to a "glowing" state. In addition, the more indepth a crafted object is the more resources and crafting stages it requires. Example, to build a guard tower you have to first build the base of the tower, which is easy. Then you have to add 500 stone blocks (a crafted item), 500 clay, and 100 planks (a crafted item) before the tower is finished. This is a HUGE task! Another example would be a knife. You can't simply place a lump of ore on a stick and call it a knife (lookin at you Minecraft). You have to craft the knife blade out of metal, and then a handle from wood, and put the two together to finally make the knife. Crafting materials and requirements seemed very logical, almost "real". That seems to be the theme of this game, bring as much reality to it as you can, while not loosing the fact that it's a fantasy game.

In addition to crafting, the materials of the world didn't come in numbered items. For example, if you picked up a rock you didn't have 1 rock. You had so many kg of rock. Each time you crafted something it took a portion of that rock to craft it. You could also have many rocks, of various weights. Some might be too small to craft an item with, some might be big enough to have leftover. They could also be combined to merge their individual portions into one larger portion. So you could have one rock that is 10kg and another that is 20kg, merge them together and get a 30kg rock. The weight of items was very important to monitor, as your movement speed didn't just go from 100% to encumbered. There were actually multiple stages to walking speed. So carrying 40% of your maximum weight would actually have an impact on the speed you walked, again very realistic.

So getting down to the nitty gritty. What is Wurm Unlimited? Well, it's a very good MMO, in a fantasy setting, including a high detail of realism, that's been converted to home use. If it's just you playing it you'll find the world very empty and a lot of things will be a very difficult challenge. If it's you and a few friends there is quite a bit of enjoyment to be found, although the 3 kingdom aspect of the game will probably never be used. If it's you and a dedicated server of 30+ players you might be able to experience all this game has to offer.

So do I recommend it? This is where the difficulty is. For a player such as myself, who loves a challenge and doesn't mind tedious grunt work to get a task done, this game is absolutely awesome. For the newer generation of (spoiled) players who like to press one key and click one button to get stuff done this game will come across as boring and very hard to play. Personally though I absolutely love this game for it's mix of realistic crafting, true sandbox environent, and RPG aspects.

So again, do I recommend it? Oy... It's extremely hard to decide. If I was to look at this game 10 years ago, at it's current price, I'd think it was a hell of a deal. Today, not so sure. Personally I think $32 is fairly overpriced. That said, I got it on the Steam Sale for $16 which is a much more reasonable cost. So?

Yes... Yes, I recommend it. ---- BUT ---- Only if you can get it on sale at 50% off it's $32 price tag.
