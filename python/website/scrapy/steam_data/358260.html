About This Game

The game begins with Mylo rocketing to the Altairian star system on a simple treasure hunt. But what happens is far more dangerous than even Mylo bargained for. He enters an underground world of chambers full of alien technology and traps. Strange alien creatures and robots inhabit the caves, protecting the priceless crystals from money-greedy explorers.

There are three unique episodes to the Crystal Caves saga: "The Trouble with Twibbles", "Slugging it Out", and "Mylo Verses the Supernova". Each episode has a unique story twist and graphics. Can you help Mylo strike it rich and quit his day job?

Features


Alien Acrobat: Mylo can walk up-side-down - some levels have such low gravity that the recoil from Mylo's laser pistol can knock him into other dangers!

Brave New World: Lightless levels, falling hammers, egg-dropping alien bats, free-roaming slither eyes, web-shooting alien spiders, and much, much more!

Controller Support: Partial Xbox 360 Controller support.

I got this game in the 3D Realms Anthology bundle on Steam and, surprisingly, this is one of the best platformers I have ever played!

Although, the premise is simple, collect all crystals to advance to the next level, but the levels are designed in such ways to make you think before performing your actions because you die very easily in this game and some of the platforming is a bit tricky in some levels, like pixel perfect jumps, inevitiable hazards, and some traps that will require a level restart. Thankfully, unlike a lot of older platformers, you do not have lives. You can die as many times as you want and it will immediatly restart the level you are on. I wish a lot of older games were like this as it makes the game more relaxing and not pain-stompingly difficult to the degree you rage quit.

To the people who complain about the lack of music, just use Steam's Music player and put on some tunes. I used Retro City Rampage's soundtrack to this game and it fit perfectly! ;)

Since this game is a platformer, I could not recommend enough to use a controller when playing this game. If your controller does not work in the game automatically, use JoyToKey ( http://joytokey.net ) to map your buttons to a keyboard key. If you don't use a controller, your fingers might cramp up ;)

A very fun game that I wish I played back in the day! This would have easily been one of my favorites!

( 3.2 hrs on record )
Posted: 30 January
Crystal Caves is a simple, old-fashioned 2D puzzle platformer, bearing some similarity to the very first Duke Nukem.
Each level, you need to figure out what order to do things in so that you can take out the baddies, grab all the goodies, and make it to the exit alive; it's easy to die or sometimes to get trapped so you have to quit and re-enter the level, but if you're fast and clever enough you may be able to succeed without taking a single hit for a massive score bonus. You can take as many tries as you need to beat each level (no lives limit), which makes the challenging puzzles much more forgiving, and if you get frustrated, you can go back to the main map and platform to a different level for a change of pace.
The story is silly and the action simplistic, but with Steam automatically setting up and configuring a dosbox for it, this is an easy way to relive the old days.
From a modern perspective? It's not all that exciting, but if you like retro simplicity, or are craving nostalgia, it's not bad either.

A fantastic puzzle-platformer from the bright and gaudy EGA days. Runs perfectly in DOSBox, the controls are tight with a 360 controller, and it sounds great with those old fashioned buzzes and blorps I wish every video game had, not just the ones made before 1993. There are three episodes with 16 levels each, so that's a lot of puzzle-platforming greatness for a buck or two. The creator, Frank Maddin, would go on to work on notable games such as the 1997 Shadow Warrior and its 2013 sequel, Donkey Kong Country Returns, and Metroid Prime 3, among others.
