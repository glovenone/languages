About This Game

CortexGear:AngryDroids:



IN CORTEXGEAR: ANGRYDROIDS, YOU PLAY AS:
an alien(main character) hacker who take control of a CortexGear(A robot with an implanted brain) to destroy the other AngryDroids. The alien is one of the last survivors from a Cortex war(A war based on hacking the opponent) against the AngryDroids. The alien is also from a new generation of CortexSoldiers(Soldier with great hacking abilities), the generation after the ultimate CortexWar against humans. Find his(main character) true origin through the game story,in a world infested by angrydroids.

To get and know more about the story, you should visit the game's website page. The full story of the game is placed there.

BRAVE THE GAME WITH YOUR SKILLS AND REFLEXES
CortexGear:AngryDroids is a challenging action-adventure game where you have to use your head to find your enemy's weaknesses, to trick your enemies with traps and make puzzles. You will have to hack computers, get use to the tricks and combos, because the places are vast and hostile, the way forward is not always clear, and death will meet you as soon as you let your guard down, but be brave, fight well and explore carefully, because droids are angry, and have no pity.

  • Multiplayer (Cross platform).
  • Multiplayer stage customization.
  • Survival mode.
  • 20 extra challenging levels.
  • More than 6 hours of campaign.
  • Random enemy.
  • 35 different enemies.
  • More than 100 sets of weapons (24).
  • Tricks list (specials and combos moves).

The hardest sci-fi shooting sidescroller
is a clasic 3d Sci-Fi shooting sidescroller where quick tactics and skills need to be in harmony the beat the game. A run-and-gun shooter with both mouse and keyboard controls at the same time. An adventure game with a emotional story and epic game-play. Potentially therapeutic and emotionally educational, it's a game for PC, Mac and Linux.

"Cortex Gear: Angry Droids" is an adventure game that will challenge your skills in many forms. Robots with a conscious and they won't budge. With a be with me or you're against my mentality, the Angry Droids are on mission: 'I will destroy you'

Where to begin? Very few games give me a headache when playing them. This is one of those games.

This game really needs someone with incredible patience to create a video walkthru just to explain how to play this crazy game. So far no one has done this. Not sure anyone will. I'm sure as hell not going to try. I didn't even get through what I think was the tutorial before giving up in frustration.

I got to a wall where it said hold W to climb wall. Uh no. That doesn't work. Not at all. You have to use a combination of hitting W and the space bar in the right way to get up that wall. And that is provided you didn't waste energy sprinting around and stuff.

The game doesn't bother to tell you that sprinting or using your "jet pack" consumes energy. And if you consume too much energy, then you cannot get past the next obstacle. Too bad. What to do? Hit X and autodestruct and start over. My first attempt I did just that in trying to get over that stupid wall, where I didn't have enough jet pack whatever to get me high enough. Except I didn't know to hit X, until I accidentally hit in randomly hitting keys on the keyboard. There is a way to get a list of the controls, including the magic use of X, but it's not obvious why you would want to autodestruct, so it's easy to overlook.

After the wall, I got to a switch of some kind. I tried everything, but I could not figure out for the life of me how to flip that freaking switch. It's probably some use of the E button with the cursor positioned just so, maybe with CTRL mode on, maybe not. After futzing with it for a few minutes, I decided I no longer cared to play this game. It was giving me a headache. And not the fun kind of a headache either. If such a thing exists. No. This is get an aspirin and not ever play the game again kind of headache.

This game might be playable, or possibly in some alternate universe, enjoyable even, if you had any idea of what you were doing half the time.

The game boasts that it is the hardest sci-fi side scroller. Ever. Yeah right. Having a game where not enough time was taken on basic stuff, like spelling or creating a descent tutorial, doesn't make a game "hard", it just makes it "unplayable", or more precisely, "frustrating to the point where you don't want to play it anymore".

As a side note, it's kind of amazing really how bad the spelling is at times. Instead of the word "destroy", you have "destrov". Instead of the world "following" you have "follawing" and so on. But I digrees and I'm getting yet another headache even thinking about all this.

This game is very bad. I believe that I got it for free, but even at that price I would strongly advise against getting it. The developer's claim that this game is the "hardest sci-fi shooting side scroller ever" is certainly true. In fact, it is so hard that it is unplayable.

Graphics: Terrible. The game is in windowed mode and takes up about 1/3 of the screen which is useless. The resolutions are bizarre and nonstandard. The background is patchy and looks like a game being run on windows 95. It's so badly optimized that it lags dreadfully even though it's a side scroller with essentially one moving part.

Gameplay: I think I got stuck somewhere during the tutorial section of the game. Certainly there were parts which seemed like tutorial, but it was still incredibly difficult to glean any instructions from what was on screen. At one point I had to hack a computer. It told me to press "pause, storage, info" to know how to hack a computer. When I did this it simply said "melee attack can turn on the rage mode". This didn't seem very useful so I tried to work it out myself for a minute or two before giving up and coming to write this review.

Story: N/A. The only fragment of story I got was a strange voice during the load up screen saying "I will destroy you". One of many background voices that seemed to bear no relevance to anything within the game. I had a mission at one point "Destroy every droids in the ruin". Just one of the many grammatical/spelling errors within the game.

Sound: As mentioned before there were weird voices/sounds popping up in the background with no discernible purpose.

Overall, a dreadful, dreadful game. Never buy this.
