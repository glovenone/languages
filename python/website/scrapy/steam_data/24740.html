About This Game



Download Burnout™ Paradise: The Ultimate Box today. Paradise City is the largest and most dangerous setting yet for the best-selling Burnout series. The massive setting gives players an open-ended world to explore, as they race their vehicles through hundreds of miles of roads and underground passages with more than 70 different cars. Speed through the streets from event to event, racking up points that are saved to your Paradise City driver’s license. Earn the vaunted “Burnout” license by smashing through billboards, jumping ramps, and sustaining crashes with the improved damage system.

The Burnout™ Paradise: Ultimate Box features all of the great gameplay and modes of the original game, as well as added enhancements especially for the PC. Now you can cruise the streets of Paradise City at night with the new Day/Night time cycles. There’s nothing like racing at night. The Ultimate Box also includes motorcycles for an all-new thrill ride. Take on 70 new motorcycle-specific challenges. Two bikes are available at the start, including the FV1100 and Nakamura Firehawk V4, with additional rides that can be unlocked or downloaded. We’ve also added dynamic weather that features a variety of new conditions. Even if you’ve played Burnout™ Paradise before, you’ve never experienced a challenge like The Burnout™ Paradise: The Ultimate Box. Download it today for the PC.

As someone who loves racing games, this one just never clicked for me. I sunk a good 50 hours into it back in the day on console and it was just okay back then. Any way you slice it, it is easily the worst of all the Burnout games (it's a shame the better games never made their way onto Steam). While a bit disappointing, Paradise was still a solid game at it's time, but it simply hasn't aged well. The city is bland, the cars handle very poorly and the takedown system gets really annoying over time. It seems we've had a deluge of open world racing games since this one launched, and with the exception of NFS Rivals, I'd say all of them are superior to Paradise in one way or another. Even The Crew, despite suffering dreadful gameplay, has a much more vivid and engaging world than Paradise. Not that this is a bad game, it's just the weakest in the series, below par when compared to more recent open-world racers, and for me, the “ultimate” kicker that turns this from a “buy during a steam sale” into a “no way”, is the fact that the game is blatantly falsely advertised. What EA meant by ultimate is a mystery seeing as it does not contain the DLC. Big Surf island, the most important piece of DLC released was never even ported to PC, and the rest of the DLC you have to purchase through Origin. How exactly is that “The Ultimate” edition?

**REVIEW CHANGED FROM NEGATIVE TO POSITIVE** - See Edit for details

I'm torn. Giving this a negative review is something that hurts me a lot, because I really love this game and I have done for years. I get annoyed when people start their reviews like this, but hear me out.

This game is fantastic. I first played it back when it first came out on the old 360, and it was a blast to pick up and play with my friends. The game is great and I had unlocked every car in the game. The driving is more than solid, and holds up well today. Got a great soundtrack, a lot of cars and an interesting world to play around in.

So why the negative review for the PC version? In fact, the negative review isn't for the game itself, it's because the DLC is not available on PC any more, at least on the Steam version. This is a huge shame because the DLC has some of my favourite parts of the game, especially the toy cars pack and, in this case, the time savers pack.

Of course, if this doesn't bother you, please disregard the negativity. It's a very good game, especially so with friends. If you don't want the DLC then consider this review a shining endorsement! I LOVE this game! But if you want my advice? Just don't get the Steam version. Get it on Origin instead as you can at least get the bonus vehicle DLC pack.

Edit: This review wasn't really clear when I first wrote it, so I'm making some quick corrections. The game is good, don't get me wrong. I guess the biggest contributor to the issue is that the game just doesn't have any support on Steam any more, hence why I suggested getting the game on Origin instead as some DLC is still available. I am aware Criterion have moved on from this and aren't supporing it any more at all, but I don't expect them to continue supporting a game this old anyway. I've changed the review to a positive one after realising my mistakes (thanks for bringing me up on it, Illimitus). The game is great, I really want to sing it's praises, but if you want the DLC Steam isn't the place to do it. If you want a good game without DLC, this is absolutely worth a purchase. Apologies for a misleading negative review, any future reviews will not be made just before going to bed on a late night :)
