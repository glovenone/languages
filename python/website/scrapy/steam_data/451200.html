About This Game

A world cast into darkness… but there is hope. After a mysterious entity extinguishes all the candles in the land, a lone surviving candle sets out on a most important adventure to bring light back to the land.



Get ready for an adventure that takes the lone candle through many unique and dangerous lands. Visit lost tropical islands, desolate deserts, lava filled volcanoes, vast oceans, dark forests, puzzling castles, and more. Jump, shoot, push, and burn your way through the quest of a lifetime.

Features

  • 15 huge levels in a world filled with action, exploration, and puzzles
  • Explore 45 hidden temples based on water, earth, and fire
  • Blast TNT with the candle's fireballs to reveal secret areas
  • Battle 3 bosses based on smoke, water and ice
  • Take the skies in a hot air balloon
  • Cross oceans in a pirate ship armed with cannons
  • Realtime lighting system that casts beautiful dynamic shadows
  • Find and collect idols throughout the world
  • Speedrun times supported out of the box

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Win 7, Win 8, Win 10
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Storage: 800 MB available space

Don't play this game.

Rather, don't play Candlelight if you expect a game meant for anyone above the age of 10. Overall it's a cute romp, but it's a fairly introductory platformer with no depth or skill. The graphics are cute and stylized, perfect for someone on the younger side of the gamer spectrum, but still fairly unpolished.

The price of the game is fairly irrelevant. As an experienced gamer just the time spent being utterly bored by the game was bad enough, but if you want to pick it up for someone new to the platforming genre, what the devs ask would be perfect.

I'm currently 10 levels in and the game is solid. It's undeniably indie, but I'm into that. You're a candle and your primary ability is to double jump. Lightning clouds are putting out your candle buddies and you have to reignite them. I don't know what the deal is with the lightning clouds yet. You have to collect wax to stay alive, but if you die you get full wax and there isn't much consequence except for not getting full stars on the level rating. The pace and gameplay variation is a little slow, but the style is great and there are no major issues to speak of. If the rest of the levels are similar, I'd give Candlelight a high rating of 7/10. If the next levels evolve nicely, that could get up to 8/10, if that means anything... It means it's a solid game. Review full circle.
