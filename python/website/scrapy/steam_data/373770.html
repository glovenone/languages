About This Game



Efina is a very special young girl. She's curious. She's optimistic. She has the power to make lies manifest as physical beings, and then she eats them for sustenance.

She's also a dragon.



LiEat is the classic story of a vagabond and his dragon protege traveling a curious world, buying and selling secrets, all the while trying to uncover the truth about themselves. Along the way, they'll discover a web of hidden mysteries, meet a variety of colorful characters (including other dragons) and learn about the past events that brought them to where they are today.

LiEat is broken down into three fantastic, bite-sized chapters:




In the first chapter, meet Efi and her caretaker, the multi-titled Leo, as they happen upon a small town with a big secret. Efi begins to understand the world around her in a different perspective, and the player finds that the legends that haunt the night aren't necessarily just children's tales...



Chapter two finds Efi and Hal (formerly Leo) investigating rumors of something magical and possibly dangerous inside a resort casino. Posing as a new guard, Hal gets room and board for himself and Efi, but they quickly realize that a roof over their heads doesn't mean it's a safe place to stay.



The final installment of LiEat bring Efi and Sid to a lavish mansion to foil the plans of a master thief. However, after the great robbery occurs, Sid begins to realize there's more at work than a simple heist, and Efi watches as his past - and his lies - finally catch up to them both.


This amazing trilogy has been beloved by JRPG enthusiasts and, for the first time ever, can be enjoyed in its entirety on Steam, in one singular package! With support from the original developer and the fan translator, PLAYISM is proud to help bring LiEat to the world at large!


  • A fantastic JRPG focused on storytelling.
  • Atmospheric music to entrance the player within LiEat's world.
    (OST included in the build! Make sure to check your local folder.)
  • A series of hidden items AND conversations throughout.
  • Multiple achievements for each chapter of the game.
  • All three chapters in one package - no DLC, just the whole story.

I havent finished the game yet but from what I've finished; I've really enjoyed the game so far.

While the game itself is short, each part of the story is split into 3 chapters / games.
For everyone who likes story-driven RPG games, I'd suggest this to you!
However, the game is very weak on the fighting system. You don't need to grind as much as you might expect from the usual JRPGs. Which is fine, but it still needs a bit of extra work in my opinion. There is no variety of monsters aside of the bosses, I wish there were more disgungished differences between the lie monsters but theres barely any in-battle. (Outside of battle they have different looks yes, but in-battle, there isn't much difference. They all have the same attack moves so there's nothing special to it?)

Aside of that: visuals. The visuals in this game are good! As a fan of anime art-style, I felt the art style used here really fit the characters. The pixel art as well is very lovely and I do fancy it quite a bunch. At start the weird filter of the game's first chapter bothered me, but as I proceeded the story; I began to understand it was part of the atmosphere each chapter gives you.

The music is fantastic, very soothing and very nice to listen to. It's very fitting to the atmosphere the visuals & story is trying to give you.

The characters are fantastic as well, there is literally no other complaint other than I wish I knew more about them! Theo does his job great playing the "mysterious" character, and Elfie is way too adorable! Honestly in every JRPG I tend to hate one specific character because of traits that I don't like, but I don't really think theres any character to hate here. All of the characters you get introduced to are reasonable and have well thought-out backgrounds. Some remain more mysterious than the other, which is fine as it leaves things better to interpretation to how they may be like outside of our protagonist's views! Each story is left with an "open-ending" to what happens next to these characters aside of the protagonists and I like that.

Honestly, my only complaint about this game is: I wish it was longer! I wish the plots were longer as well, or that one chapter had more to it than the main plot and the tiny errands you do! Because the plots are so short, the maps are short as well. There is barely anything to explore. However if you're bored, inbetween you might come to find some interesting things. (I stole money from the snake lady just by checking out one of her bookshelves haha!)
