30 June

Beta 0.5.5 Now Live !

New Hero enters BattleSouls!


The top requested feature for BattleSouls has been more heroes and we are finally ready to give you Tanak - The Shaman !

Tanak - The Shaman
skills:
  • Primary attack: When Tanak hits an enemy with his hammer it damages that enemy and sends out a healing aoe that heals all allies in range.
  • Secondary attack: Tanak smashes his hammer in to the ground and causes a small earthquake. Enemies hit will be slowed, and those close to the center will also be stunned.
  • Change effect: When you change in to Tanak he summons the power of the winds, and after a short time he pulls in all enemies in range towards him.
We are having a lot of fun playing with Tanak on the dev team, and we hope you will like him as well. You can set him up for some pretty epic agressive plays and still also use him to protect and support your team.

Other changes:
  • Resolved some more of the mouse sensitivity issues and they should now be complety gone.
  • Fixed the color of player names in chat so they now represent the color of the team the player is on
  • Corrected the color of the victory and defeat text on the win screen to correctly represent your team color

About This Game

BattleSouls is currently in Beta. We’re listening to you. Please check out our forums and let us know how we can make the game better for you as we continue to add more in future updates.

BattleSouls is an online multiplayer action game about destroying your opponent’s soul crystals before they destroy yours.

Master balancing a loadout of three different classes out of five at launch, which can be switched to freely at any time while you are alive. Each class has his or her own unique set of abilities — including two active skills and one passive skill that triggers when you change character — to adapt to whatever the situation calls for. Strike down the opposition with swords, throw punishing fireballs, or use devious bottles of magic to make your opponent cower to your might.

  • 5 unique classes ranging from Knights focused on melee attacks to Wizards focused on ranged attacks, each has their own gameplay style, active and passive abilities
  • Colorful environments
  • Fast multiplayer combat in 10-15 minutes sessions
  • 4 different maps, each with their strategy of attacking and defending, and more to come

In BattleSouls, Class Change is King!

The action awaits!

The game is quite fun, and while it seems simple, there's actually a lot of strategy that can go into creating good loadouts to synergize with your team.

The basic premise is this; you capture an "Inhibitor" in the center of the map to put up an invulnerability shield on your crystals, while DISABLING the shields on theirs. The first team to destroy all 3 enemy crystals wins.

What sets this game apart, is each "hero" only has two skills (left click, right click), but you switch between 3 different heroes by pressing 1/2/3. Switching to a hero happens instantenously with no delay, and will activate a "special ability" which is usually quite powerful. The cooldown is only about 2 seconds or so, and there is a lot of room to outplay someone.

The game is definitely more casual with each game lasting maybe 5-20 minutes, but has lots of fun tricks to figure out. Have fun!

Total snorefest. Gameplay is bland, everything you do is low impact -- low damage skills, low healing, limited mobility. You feel like you're chipping away at each other with foam darts until one of you dies of boredom. There's no satisfaction of landing a good skill in other MOBAs or making a clutch shot in an FPS.

If teams aren't completely mismatched in skill, the game is a perpetual stalemate because the respawn timer is about the time needed to cross half the map. This means that after winning a fight at the middle objective (required to make the end objective "vulnerable"), the entire enemy team will respawn before you can get to their base.

Graphics are generically cartoony, like TF2 but with low-quality wizard hats. Animation and sound are simple and repetitive, which only highlights how low impact your skills are.

The only interesting feature is the on-the-fly class switching. You choose 3 classes to play (out of 5 classes: 2 ranged DPS, 1 melee tank, 1 melee DPS, and 1 support) and can switch between them on a short timer. Each class has a passive skill that triggers when you switch to that class (e.g. the tank has a short duration of invulnerability, support does a small AoE burst heal). I'd love to see this mechanic in a proper game.
