About This Game

With their kingdom in ruins the elves were forced to set off overseas to search for a new home. By fate's will, a storm crushed their ships on the shores of a lost island. When attempting to leave, the elves learn that the island is surrounded by an impenetrable magic storm. The survivors recall a legend about the last of the ancient elves, who shall awake from a thousand year's sleep to help them overcome hardship.

Lost Lands: A Hidden Object Adventure has received quite a lot of negative reviews during the first days after it was released, and this because of 2 main reasons: on one hand, the game had a lot of bugs of various severity (from typos or bad translations to crashes, achievements unlocking and the impossibility to start the game on some systems); on the other hand, the game featured two achievements that were obtainable only by investing a huge amount of real money (around $200) that attracted the anger of a lot of people, including myself.

However, the feedback that the community received from the developers is quite impressive â not only they changed the paid achievements but they also started to fix the major bugs and they are currently working on fixing the minor issues too, and all this in a very short period of time.

The game itself is not entirely bad, itâs quite repetitive but at the same time it was designed to hook you up, just like the standard smartphone/Facebook games. The fact that you have a limit to the actions you can do in one play session might or might not make you revisit the game next day, to progress some more.

The gameplay consists mainly in solving hidden object scenes that award some items used to complete the quests or collections or unlock additional bonuses. The fact that you have a most 15 locations (depending on your level, 1 - 15) that you visit over and over again adds to the monotony of the game â but on the bright side, after you solve 3-4 times the same hidden object scene, you learn exactly where all the objects are which makes it much easier afterwards.

Additionally, you can receive some chests from killing mobs, but unlocking them proves to be a real challenge â the puzzles are timed and most often of high difficulty.

For those that want to 1-click solve a chest puzzle or a hidden object scene, there is an in-game shop from which they can buy items in exchange for real money. The shop also provides all the objects needed for unlocking new locations or completing the collections. However, in comparison with most of the other pay-to-win games, all these items can be acquired also by playing the game, without spending a cent of real money, but of course these drop in small amounts so one is expected to invest a lot of patience and time to finish this game.

The objects in the hidden object scenes and the whole artwork is designed in the style of Artifex Mundi games, meaning that overall the artwork is nice and with vibrant colors; the objects are quite visible but at the same time hidden strategically so that they blend perfectly into the background, making them harder to be spotted, which is by definition one of the characteristics of this genre.

At the moment of writing this review, I think that the biggest complaint from the community remains the long time to achieve 100% - in order to do that one needs to revisit every location a few hundred times and all of this require energy; the energy pool is quite small and limited, allowing you to play only 4-5 hidden object scenes in a single play session - this will probably make people loose interest in the game quite quickly since they will not be able to enjoy the game for more than 10 minutes at once. Hopefully, the game parameters will be adjusted in the future.

I decided to give this game a positive review, not only because it is an enjoyable experience (despite the long grind for 100% achievements) but also because of the constructive attitude from the developers, that are actively working on improving our gaming experience.
