About This Game

This game takes place in the same universe as our upcoming tittle Secluded; you take control of a lone astronaut that lands on the surface of a planet, miles away from the other members. And it’s your job to meet up with the other astronauts.

This game is based on constantly moving, as your suit leaks oxygen at random times. You must also avoid hazards that accrue on the planets, such as falling debris and asteroid strikes. This game is Free to play and experimental in some ways. We want the player survive in their own way and not have the game hold their hand through the experience, the games objectives are randomized so the locations change each time you play, and once you die that's it.

FEATURES
. Gravity Effects
. Randomized Endings
. Asteroid Strikes & Hazards
. First Person Experience and Future VR Plugins

This game was the second game I've reviewed for my "Steam Redlight" series, where I review games when they're 'in the red' in reviews to see if it's deserved or not.

You can see the view review / reaction .

In summary, I can't recommend this game unless you feel like practicing handstands over a bed of nails shortly afterwards. In this game, you play as an astronaut who's been separated from his colleagues, somehow, because I guess you were drunk-driving the Voskhod on your way to the landing zone. Your Communications Officer (or whatever she wants to call herself) tells you that your HUD is screwy and that you'll have to go to one of the signals to rejoin your team, but not all of them are real. Hell yeah, let's just brave the mystery of the moon, alone, on the off chance that there's Mooninites around every crater. The CO says that you only have enough time to go to one signal, and that's the whole premise of the game.

The antagonist of the game, besides your half-efficient CO are the asteroids trying to plow into you harder than half the town when they come to your mother's house for dinner. However, these asteroids are really fickle -- when the asteroids meet your face, they do nothing. They honestly don't even damage you. You can see this hot asteroid-on-face action in my video if you don't believe me. You randomly lose oxygen (which is your life-blood) as you walk around. You can move these asteroids with your face at the cost of oxygen. Just run into them, because you're a strong man you dirty Machamp. The asteroids have a tendency to also disappear out of the ♥♥♥♥ing sky (or on the moon, if they land) for no reason other than 'just cause'.

So, the game overall is pretty short. I 'beat the game' in 0.5 hours. I went to one of the beacons, nothing was there. However, when I tried to go to points 2, 3, 4, and 5 I ran into an invisible ♥♥♥♥ing barrier. This barrier never ceases to exist, no matter what you do. This is everything I ever expected to experience in a free to play game. My favorite part is when you go against the final boss and by final boss I mean you kill yourself by moving asteroids around trying to get through this invisible force-field. Once you hit 0% oxygen, it just says "%DEAD" and doesn't inhibit you at all. It's a real tear-jerker of an ending.

Anyways, I might buy the DLC just because I'm curious if it's better than the initial campaign. I can probably imagine I'll be the only person on this great green Earth who owns it. I'm hoping it will be a purchase of prestiege and purpose.

All-in-all, it's a free game; buy it if you want to waste half an hour. Graphics are pretty neat-o, besides the random spontaneous cell shading that runs rampart if you look at your feet. Otherwise, the asteroids and the environment look pretty A-OK.

Score & Breakdown

Score: 1/10
  • +5 for existing.
  • -1 for disappearing asteroids.
  • -1 for zombification of astronauts.
  • -1 for length of game.
  • -1 for not carrying a laser gun.
