About This Game

Vinyl is a dynamic indie music game created by graduate students at the University of Utah EAE program, that experiments with the aesthetic distance between gameplay and music.

Every Song is Unique:
- Load your own music.
- Your music procedurally generates the world...
- The way you play changes the music!
- Pick a music genre and let Vinyl remix your song how you choose.

Arcade Styled Runner:
- Dodge all of the obstacles to see your music in vibrant neon synesthesia.
- Make mistakes and your music will be taken back in time.
- Rewind to undo your mistakes, and your score.
- Collect audio filters to manipulate your music even more, and score more points!
- Too easy to dodge obstacles? Boost to increase your multiplier and gain the ultimate score!

Score Attack:
- Use all of your abilities to rack up points.
- Challenge the online leaderboards.
- Enter your initials to claim the number one spot!

Vinyl: Play Your Music

Vinyl is an okay Unity game (There's that word again...) that focuses on being a literal Audiosurf.

The gameplay is okay, though it feels like there is quite a bit to be desired. The models are...what you'd expect from most Unity-based games, and the replayability is low (unless you like to just play the game to custom music). This project was also a STUDENT PROJECT from people over at the University of Utah, so keep that in mind as you play (especially if you played similar games like Maui before). It's also painful to listen to the filter walls distorting your music for the most part...that's probably the largest drawback.

The drawing feature is the ability to play tracks that are generated by the music (or audio files) you upload, which is a neat feature, but doesn't really provide what Audiosurf has not already provided. I'm also grateful that this is not as bugged as Maui is as the result of a student project.

If you're looking for a free game with achievements or if you want a poor-man's Audiosurf, check it out.


5/10 It's okay, but has room for improvement.

I can't recommend this, it's just got so many flaws. If you want a rhythm game where you play to your own music get Audiosurf. The only redeeming features of Vinyl are that it is free and it has really easy achievements if you can find the right songs.

There are three big problems. First, it looks terrible and only gets worse as you play. I can forgive it not looking great since you can have decent gameplay and terrible graphics, but as you play the game adds this horrendous blurring effect that obscures the track and makes it a headache to play. I don't know if it is supposed to add difficulty by making it harder to see obstacles coming or if someone thought it looked cool. Either way it's a bad design choice.

Second, it frequently distorts the music. Hit an obstacle and it interrupts the track and slows it down, use the boost and it speeds up. I can see why they did this since the game is called Vinyl and they must have wanted to simulate speeding up and slowing down a record. But if I'm playing a game to my own music it's because I want to listen to the music. Messing with the song is annoying and I'd rather just play something else and listen normally.

Thirdly, the track you play on has sharp corners with high walls that make it impossible to see very far ahead of you and anticipate how to avoid the obstacles. Again I'm not sure if this is to add difficulty or someone thought it was a good idea. You can be on a decent streak then have a blind corner with obstacles you had no way of avoiding jump in front of you. There is a 'rewind' feature but you really shouldn't need it, it distorts the music and interrupts the flow of the game when you need to rewind a few seconds over and over until you can get past the ninja corner obstacles.

A smaller problem is that sometimes the game will freak out when you try to play a long track. I wanted to play something that was about 11 minutes long and it was less like a game and more like a slideshow. Shorter tracks work fine.

Just get Audiosurf instead.
