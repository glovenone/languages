First of all, this game is vastly harder than the graphical style would suggest.

Negatives: Powerups are poorly documented and require trial and error to figure out, and not all of them are particularly useful. It's too easy for one mistake to result in multiple points of damage.

If you can figure out what's going on and survive a couple levels, it becomes clear that there is a legimate game under the pixelated affectations, made with love and care. Runs are short enough that trial and error isn't a major timesink.

At some point between when I originally wrote this review and now, the game went free-to-play; if it seems even vaguely appealing there's no reason not to try it.
