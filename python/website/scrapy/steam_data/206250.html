About This Game

Cladun X2 is a hack n' slash dungeon crawler with a retro RPG finish and a nearly infinite level of character customization. From stats to equipment to character classes to creating your own hero, Cladun X2 promises an addicting experience that gamers new and old will fully enjoy.

Key features:

  • Randomly generated dungeon maps
  • Fully customizable characters using an in-game paint app
  • Classic platformer-style action RPG

This game plays VERY well for a hack and slash, theres a bazillion costumization and character advancement systems going on that give it a very familiar NIS feel, but isnt overbearing.

For 20 bucks id say it heavily depends on how much you like hack and slash RPGs, as it is definitely pricey, but if you do see it go on sale do make sure to grab this gem. If it is not on sale, I would find it a bit hard to recommend, luckily for you though, there's a free demo.

The music is decent, the combat feels smooth, and there are plenty of dungeon types and achievements to go for, from timed stages to more roguelike dungeons. And the idea is as classic and simple as it gets: You and dungeons with lots of monsters. Great to play in small breaks, still great to play for longer periods in one sitting. If you love retro RPGs, make sure you put this on your wishlist.

Don't let the playtime fool you- the mojority of time I spent playing this game was in offline mode, so about 100~ hours are missing there.

Cladun X2 is a game made for those who love customization. Want to smack enemies with a giant wooden spoon? Go for it. Want to play as one of your favorite RPG characters? If you can sprite art it, sure. Add along the fact that levels and dungeons go to an obscenely high number (same guys who made Disgaea, of course this is going to happen,) this is not a game you will get bored of quickly.

it does have one major flaw though- this is a PSP port, and the wonky controls show that immediately. You would think that a game that prides itself on customization would allow you to change the controls.

Another flaw is this does NOT allow you to transfer any of your art or data from the PSP game itself, so if you're buying this a second time you will be starting over from scratch.

All in all, if you can get past the odd controls and you're a sucker for replay/customization, this is the game for you.

Before thinking I completely recommend this game, hold on for a second and read ahead.

TLDR: Please play the game... But not on Steam

ClaDun x2 IS a fun game. It's exciting to play, has good music and the customization options are through the roof.
Under any normal circumstance, I would recommend this game, but not in this scenario.

20 US dollars is not what this game is worth to any normal player. I would recommend this game on a sale, but there's one problem with that; The publisher has abandoned Steam. There is most likely never going to be a sale for this game ever again and that's the sad part. I want to tell my friends to buy it. I WANT to let others have this on their library, but 20 dollars is WAY too much, especially considering that it's not very well ported. There's flickering with the Steam Overlay and is generally a pain, but I had loads of fun.

I cannot recommend this game for 20 dollars.
I recommend getting the PSP version, whether it's emulated or not is not my choice.

I own this for the PSP Vita and PC, i have well over 1k+ hours dumped into the game over the years, It's an extremely replayable top-down rpg with the combat system being very akin to the 2d Zelda games, You compose your party from characters you create in the tavern, you can make them look however you want with the in game editor that is easy to use but very detailed, this editor is also available to make your own gear skins to apply to items gathered while slaying monsters or looting chest. There are Many jobs to choose from, base jobs as well as a few unlockable ones.
You're allowed to Reclass after you reach a certain point in the game, when you choose another job
you retain all abilities/magic, ''Magic circles'' and a fraction of your stats, There are 3 dungeon modes.
Story mode, Ran-Geon ''Random dungeon'' and Tri-Geon ''Triple Dungeons'' (Ran/Tri are both randomised Multi Floored dungeons) Really basic summation of the game, It's worth its ''Steep'' price in my opinion IF you have the option to get it on the PSP/VITA over PC do so, But if PC is your only platform then it's no big deal, it's just as responsive as the handhelds and emulated great as well. Thank you for taking your time to read the review, and good luck!

If you don't mind the graphics it'll be fun until it gets repetitve. Your mileage will vary on that; the regular dungeons mix things up pretty well, but if you do the randomly generated dungeons they seem to be quite similar most of the time and get old. Nothing like Disgaea's item world at all.
