What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want the community to help us test and improve the gameplay experience as we continue development. We have been developing, and thus play-testing, for a while now and it always helps to have a fresh perspective on the ease/difficulty of gameplay. We will use any feedback we receive to help direct and fix development as we move towards the final release.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We're now targeting a 2015 final release, however a specific date hasn't been decided on.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full game will include a story-rich campaign, animatic cutscenes, fully-voiced passenger dialog, a faction reputation system, a complete city of 4 unique major districts, and a wide variety of upgrades and weapons for customizing your taxi.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current version of the game includes 2 of the city districts, 10 mission types, and an assortment of upgrades and weapons. The campaign has not been implemented yet, however a sample of a basic story mission chain has been included.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price will increase on release of the final game.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We greatly appreciate community feedback and being informed about bugs whenever they are found. If players find a bug, or feel like a game feature isn't working the way it should, then we encourage them to tell us what's wrong. We also like hearing suggestions, though we can't make any promises to implement all of them.”

24 June

Dev Blog 24/6/16 - Smart Design

Hey everyone!

We’re back with some more vehicular works in progress. Instead of telling you about this vehicle in particular, I’m going to take you through some of the thoughts behind its design.


Octopi’s faction vehicle designs went through a number of iterations, from paying homage to the Jetsons to an art-deco approach with a retro-futuristic vibe. However the initial designs felt too separate from the vehicles already created in game, so instead design cues were taken from the Cursi NPC vehicle. Its softer edges and shape give it a more luxurious and advanced look compared to standard factionless vehicles, suiting Octopi’s interest in advanced technology.


The final aesthetic for Octopi’s vehicles draws upon the faction’s primary business ideal, genetic manipulation. Their vehicles somewhat resemble single cell organisms, utilising hexagonal patterns and shapes as seen in skeletal formulas of molecules and a double helix DNA-like structure for the rear propulsion.


We’ll be back with the finished textured shots next time. For now, thanks for reading! -Mark

About This Game

Step into the driving seat of a highly customisable and heavily armed flying taxi in the amoral cyberpunk future of 'Collateral'. You play as Zack Edgewater, an average Joe hover cab driver, caught between warring factions, bloodthirsty customers, and the corrupt corporations that run the city of New Bedlam. The city is under lockdown, and your goal is to earn enough favour with one of the city's factions to gain access to passcodes that allow you to leave the city. Win favour with your chosen faction by undertaking a variety of missions that test your speed, driving skills, and accuracy, then spend your hard earned cash on a huge assortment of weapons and upgrades for your cab. Every mission you complete brings you one step closer to escaping this depraved, maniacal metropolis.

Visually loaded, futuristic metropolis

Navigate your way through the sprawling, cyberpunk dystopia of 'New Bedlam', a bustling neon city populated by hoodlums, riffraff and genetically modified mutants. This chaotic, futuristic urban landscape makes Collateral a visually loaded, eye-gasmic experience.

Open world environment featuring multiple themed districts

New Bedlam is a large, open world environment consisting of several sectors, each with a distinct aesthetic and unique landmarks. Explore areas such as the Greenzone, the Industrial Zone and the Redlight district.

Highly customisable, heavily armed flying taxi

In-depth customisation allows you to tailor your taxi to suit the way you want to play. Performance enhancing vehicle upgrades give you an edge over your foes, and your cab's multiple weapon sockets allow you to deck yourself out with the ordnance of your choice. Customisable weapon groups give you even more control over your lethal arsenal.

Weapons

Unlock and take aim with a huge variety of deadly weapons, including but not limited to, the ion cannon, mine layer, plasma cannon, gatling gun, cryo gun, buzz saw, burrito cannon, cluster bomb, drunken missiles, laser cannon, turtle mine, napalm, scuds and more! Experiment with combinations, turn your cab into a flying death machine and blast away your foes to your heart's content.

3D Movement

Unlike other car-based driving games, Collateral features a flying cab that can travel through the three perpendicular axes. We have implemented an arcade-style system that gives you control of your direction and altitude, whilst keeping the controls uncomplicated. This allows you to navigate through New Bedlam in a way that feels intuitive.

Choose your allegiances

Collateral features a reputation system, allowing you to form allegiances and rivalries with the factions and corporations of New Bedlam. Each faction has inherent advantages, such as access to special weapons unique to that faction, as well as cumulative rewards, like unlocking new city districts when enough favour has been gained. Complete missions to earn the respect of your favourite factions, and blow enemy factions out of the sky.

Tangible results for in-game choices

The allegiances you make with New Bedlam's factions have real effects on the future of New Bedlam. Choosing to undertake missions for particular factions can result in parts of the city being altered, permanently changing the landscape of New Bedlam.

Enemies

Face off against enemies such as 'The Juggernaut', an aggressive semi-trailer equipped with machine guns, missiles, and a cannon, or 'The Fuzz', New Bedlam's quick to the trigger police force.

Challenging Missions

Undertake a variety of challenging jobs in order to earn cash for upgrades and win favour with the city's factions and corporations. Test your speed, accuracy and driving skills with assassinations, police chases, package deliveries, bank heists, and more.

Original Soundtrack

We are working with several composers, including Jay Taylor and Kevin Greenlee, to produce an original soundtrack inspired by the chaotic, cyberpunk environment of Collateral. Featuring a selection of cyberpunk electronica tracks, this soundtrack will be available as one of our Kickstarter rewards.

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 August, 2015
Update: After giving this title more than 8 months and not seeing a single promised update I've changed my recommendation to No and updated my review throughout, including better comments relating to Early-Access.


Discovering Collaterals' incredible style and visual design, the punky and dystopian world and writing, and the fairly unique Crazy Taxi-esque gameplay with weapons and customization to boot are all the wishlist points of any good video game nerd. While Collateral does offer a stunning city scape which is, for a short time, very pleasing to explore and look at, it's ultimately limited by shallow gameplay, no story, and more technical problems than Microsoft ME.

Highlights

The main appeal and the thing that this title does best is the gorgeous visual design and immersive styling. It looks and feels incredible and driving around what little of Bedlam City exists is already genuinely interesting and exciting. The rich depth of satirical advertisements, varied vehicle styles and hidden graffiti make for a real pleasure in just casually exploring the cities vertical architecture. In my first 10 minutes I found myself avoiding missions because I just wanted to drive to some landmark I could see behind a carpark, or drop down to read some graffiti scrawled on the side of a hotel, only to catch a vehicle duel reminiscent of block wars from Judge Dredd blasting away above a lane of traffic overhead. It honestly has some very brief moments of visceral immersion that are cool to witness.

* The factions and their presence in the forms of vehicles and the buildings that make up their small sections of the city, be it Big Larry's blocky in-your-face industrial complex or the Strippers' smoggy narrow streets in the Red Light district help add character to the map and make good landmarks/reference points.

* The witty writing and little hidden extras like sexy billboards, blunt satirical advertising and things like a UFO hidden in a shipping container in the cargo hold all show a sense of humour and slight detail that helps add a little more.

Mistakes & Annoyances

Bedlam City, despite being beautiful, interesting and showing promise of increasing in size, is currently extremely limited. I completed a circular lap of the entire map in less than 3 minutes, and with an engine upgrade I'm sure you could drive from one side of the world to the other in a little over 2 minutes. Granted, there's vertical depth to take into account, and being the type of game that involves relay means you're going to be retreading the same areas a lot, but it's still just a ridiculously small world compared to other city driving titles. The developer is currently working on a commercial district called the New Bedlam District or NBD, which promises canals and boulevards, and may be larger than the current regions, but there's only another one region planned after that for a total of 4 small regions of Bedlam, and it's hard to see this game offering enough space and exploration to justify casual exploration for more than the initial few minutes of each area.

So the focus then is on the gameplay, which is where Collateral falls down. There are 10 mission types, including Group Races, Taxi Fares, Package Deliveries, Search & Destroy, Bank Jobs, Prison Breaks and Story Missions, but they all fall into just 3 categories: Fetch & Return missions, Point A to B missions and Destroy Target missions. Only Races really stand on their own as different, and even those are as bland and boring as 3D racing gets. Cash and items do provide reasons to keep playing and completing missions, but they're all far too repetitive and similar and until the story/campaign additions, gameplay is overall flat.

* There are some really big problems with FPS stuttering, rendering times and lag in general, though a developer has alluded this to the Unreal Engine 3 and suggested a redevelopment involving AI and other functionality is planned towards Unreal Engine 4, which should help reduce the graphical and framerate problems.

* The price deserves special mention for being, frankly, ridiculous and is further put into perspective below.

Early Access Specific

The developers have promised a lot, “a story-rich campaign, animatic cutscenes, fully-voiced passenger dialog,.. a complete city of 4 unique major districts, and [more] upgrades and weapons for customizing your taxi.” but with a planned release within the 2015 that only leaves another 2 weeks of development at the time of review, and it's hard to see anything other than an incredible story campaign or miraculous increase in world size significantly affecting gameplay for the better.

The development blog (available from the Steam Community Hub) is very frequent and regularly posted with fun art design, but after 3 quarters of a year of owning this title, I haven't seen a single update and the Steam reviews touting developer abandonment are starting to look a lot more like accurate prediction.

* The price is set to increase after launch, which does at least provide some small incentive to buy now, but it's already very overpriced and given the other issues and lack of updates it doesn't make sense for most of us to take a risk on this title.

Recommendations

Collateral is priced at £18.99/22.99€/$24.99 at the time of review and to put that in perspective I can pick up 3 and a half copies of Crazy Taxi, or the entire Grand Theft Auto III trilogy including 3 different complete cities offering several powers of magnitude more gameplay content than this title delivers. This game currently matches Saints Row IV, Sleeping Dogs Definitive Edition and other Triple A titles in price, and if that isn't enough to turn you away, they intend to increase the pricetag on release.

In short, Collateral is a rip-off for what's currently available. If it wasn't for price, I could honestly forgive the lack of content and some of the problems I have with this title and the gorgeous city would be enough, but for now it should be avoided for anyone but those with plenty of faith or plenty of money.

* The games Quarantine and Quarantine II - Road Warrior are strong inspirations for this title, and amazing games with thick history (and with huge flaws) in their own right. They're available as Abandonware on various sites including Abandonia.

Collateral is fun, and it could be a lot more fun. But as it is, there isn't a lot to do other than three basic types of missions and some upgrading. I really feel like I'm missing something, because the game is fun, but it's lacking to such an extent that I really think I might not be seeing the whole game.

Pros:
+Flat learning curve: It's very easy to pick up the game and understand the mechanics of it.
+Simple fun: With the learning curve, it's such a simple, fun way to pass the time.
+Visuals: I really like their take on the visuals. It's really arcade-like and keeps things fresh.

Cons:
-Minimal soundtrack: It sounds like there are two songs in the entire soundtrack and it can get very repetitive.
-Limited missions: Missions fall under three basic categories. Race, deliver, and destroy. The actual number of different missions ends up coming out to around 12 at best, and these are all that you have to choose from.
-Upgradability: Not a lot to choose from. You can't even upgrade to a different taxi altogether.
-Maps: You have two. That's it. Both are cool, both are unique, but two can only keep someone entertained for so long.
-Awkward UI: I'm mostly refering to the map, which when you bring it up, it covers the entire center of your screen and makes it difficult to navigate.

I understand the game is in Early Access, so I'm hoping the devs take the potential this game has and push out an amazing product. Until then, however, I really don't think it's worth the pricetag of $25.

Early Access Review
Posted: 25 November, 2014
The game feels rather dated, with graphics that although pretty to look at, dazzle you with bright lights and neon colors; also much of the scenery feels haphazard, and a little blurry at times. The vehicles look like they were created a decade ago. You can play Collateral using either a keyboard or game controller. If you are familiar with the former, I strongly advise you use it because the keyboard controls are not very intuitive; for example, to decend or ascend you need to press a combination of two keys for each action. Being able to do these two actions is important because to gain missions you have to move through rings that are often at a different altitude and parallel to your vehicle. As a left handed player whose never liked controllers I found it quite difficult to accomplish this task using the keyboard controls, often overshooting the rings or missing them completely. Once you fire your weapon you'll attract several police vehicles, who will attempt to shoot you down. Their fire is pretty accurate and they are likely to shoot your taxi down quickly unless you can escape them. This is quite easy, but if you are on a mission, you'll most likely fail due to the time limits. I found it was better to avoid using your weapons, at least initially so that you could complete some of the easier missions and use the money you earn to upgrade your vehicle and get better weapons. The games HUD is quite cluttered and in combination with the busy scenery, and, if you are firing your weapons, can lead missing your targets.

I played Collateral for around 5 hours over three sessions. Each time I had to give up because the movement in game gave me eye strain and made me feel a little nauseous after a while. I tried adjusting my mouse sensitivity and lighting in the room, but that didn't cure the problem. Playing it in shorter sessions will probably sort the problem out for me.

As mentioned above to earn cash and thence to upgrade requires you ro complete missions; trouble is, you get very little time to complete missions, at least in the early stages of the game. They count down either in dollars or time, the longer you take to complete the mission the less you earn and therefore the longer it takes to upgrade your vehicle or weapons. The coundowns go fast, so, unless you are very adept at the game, it's going to be hard going progressing very far without getting bored. I failed loads of missions whicch was offputting. I found at this stage of development the game wasn't holding my interest for very long.



What the game needs is different environments and possibly a new graphics engine as well as a selection of different taxis to drive, each with their own advantages. I'd also consider changing the rewards system, so that you can take much longer to complete missions, or at least give the player the option to toggle an easy mode that gives you longer. At least then no one need fail. What's the point if you're going to fail more times than completing a mission.

If this was a budget indie game I'd say okay, go and buy it as it would be worth around £8/$12 to £10/$15, unfortunately, the release price of £18.99 (approx $28) was way too expensive for what you get (IMO). Granted it's an early access game, so we may get updates that vastly improve the game in the future, but from my experience with early access games; not many have delivered as yet. I hope the developer proves me wrong as I love the basic idea of the game; it just needs a lot of improvements before it's released as a completed title.

If you are undecided about buying the game, i'd recommend you wait till it inevitably goes on sale. Judging by other recently released games that have then appeared in Indie Bundles (including Early Access) or gone into the weekly sales within several weeks post release, you probbably won't have too long to wait.

Update: After giving this title more than 8 months and not seeing a single promised update I've changed my recommendation to No and updated my review throughout, including better comments relating to Early-Access.


Discovering Collaterals' incredible style and visual design, the punky and dystopian world and writing, and the fairly unique Crazy Taxi-esque gameplay with weapons and customization to boot are all the wishlist points of any good video game nerd. While Collateral does offer a stunning city scape which is, for a short time, very pleasing to explore and look at, it's ultimately limited by shallow gameplay, no story, and more technical problems than Microsoft ME.

Highlights

The main appeal and the thing that this title does best is the gorgeous visual design and immersive styling. It looks and feels incredible and driving around what little of Bedlam City exists is already genuinely interesting and exciting. The rich depth of satirical advertisements, varied vehicle styles and hidden graffiti make for a real pleasure in just casually exploring the cities vertical architecture. In my first 10 minutes I found myself avoiding missions because I just wanted to drive to some landmark I could see behind a carpark, or drop down to read some graffiti scrawled on the side of a hotel, only to catch a vehicle duel reminiscent of block wars from Judge Dredd blasting away above a lane of traffic overhead. It honestly has some very brief moments of visceral immersion that are cool to witness.

* The factions and their presence in the forms of vehicles and the buildings that make up their small sections of the city, be it Big Larry's blocky in-your-face industrial complex or the Strippers' smoggy narrow streets in the Red Light district help add character to the map and make good landmarks/reference points.

* The witty writing and little hidden extras like sexy billboards, blunt satirical advertising and things like a UFO hidden in a shipping container in the cargo hold all show a sense of humour and slight detail that helps add a little more.

Mistakes & Annoyances

Bedlam City, despite being beautiful, interesting and showing promise of increasing in size, is currently extremely limited. I completed a circular lap of the entire map in less than 3 minutes, and with an engine upgrade I'm sure you could drive from one side of the world to the other in a little over 2 minutes. Granted, there's vertical depth to take into account, and being the type of game that involves relay means you're going to be retreading the same areas a lot, but it's still just a ridiculously small world compared to other city driving titles. The developer is currently working on a commercial district called the New Bedlam District or NBD, which promises canals and boulevards, and may be larger than the current regions, but there's only another one region planned after that for a total of 4 small regions of Bedlam, and it's hard to see this game offering enough space and exploration to justify casual exploration for more than the initial few minutes of each area.

So the focus then is on the gameplay, which is where Collateral falls down. There are 10 mission types, including Group Races, Taxi Fares, Package Deliveries, Search & Destroy, Bank Jobs, Prison Breaks and Story Missions, but they all fall into just 3 categories: Fetch & Return missions, Point A to B missions and Destroy Target missions. Only Races really stand on their own as different, and even those are as bland and boring as 3D racing gets. Cash and items do provide reasons to keep playing and completing missions, but they're all far too repetitive and similar and until the story/campaign additions, gameplay is overall flat.

* There are some really big problems with FPS stuttering, rendering times and lag in general, though a developer has alluded this to the Unreal Engine 3 and suggested a redevelopment involving AI and other functionality is planned towards Unreal Engine 4, which should help reduce the graphical and framerate problems.

* The price deserves special mention for being, frankly, ridiculous and is further put into perspective below.

Early Access Specific

The developers have promised a lot, “a story-rich campaign, animatic cutscenes, fully-voiced passenger dialog,.. a complete city of 4 unique major districts, and [more] upgrades and weapons for customizing your taxi.” but with a planned release within the 2015 that only leaves another 2 weeks of development at the time of review, and it's hard to see anything other than an incredible story campaign or miraculous increase in world size significantly affecting gameplay for the better.

The development blog (available from the Steam Community Hub) is very frequent and regularly posted with fun art design, but after 3 quarters of a year of owning this title, I haven't seen a single update and the Steam reviews touting developer abandonment are starting to look a lot more like accurate prediction.

* The price is set to increase after launch, which does at least provide some small incentive to buy now, but it's already very overpriced and given the other issues and lack of updates it doesn't make sense for most of us to take a risk on this title.

Recommendations

Collateral is priced at £18.99/22.99€/$24.99 at the time of review and to put that in perspective I can pick up 3 and a half copies of Crazy Taxi, or the entire Grand Theft Auto III trilogy including 3 different complete cities offering several powers of magnitude more gameplay content than this title delivers. This game currently matches Saints Row IV, Sleeping Dogs Definitive Edition and other Triple A titles in price, and if that isn't enough to turn you away, they intend to increase the pricetag on release.

In short, Collateral is a rip-off for what's currently available. If it wasn't for price, I could honestly forgive the lack of content and some of the problems I have with this title and the gorgeous city would be enough, but for now it should be avoided for anyone but those with plenty of faith or plenty of money.

* The games Quarantine and Quarantine II - Road Warrior are strong inspirations for this title, and amazing games with thick history (and with huge flaws) in their own right. They're available as Abandonware on various sites including Abandonia.

Early Access Review
Posted: 2 April, 2015
Collateral is fun, and it could be a lot more fun. But as it is, there isn't a lot to do other than three basic types of missions and some upgrading. I really feel like I'm missing something, because the game is fun, but it's lacking to such an extent that I really think I might not be seeing the whole game.

Pros:
+Flat learning curve: It's very easy to pick up the game and understand the mechanics of it.
+Simple fun: With the learning curve, it's such a simple, fun way to pass the time.
+Visuals: I really like their take on the visuals. It's really arcade-like and keeps things fresh.

Cons:
-Minimal soundtrack: It sounds like there are two songs in the entire soundtrack and it can get very repetitive.
-Limited missions: Missions fall under three basic categories. Race, deliver, and destroy. The actual number of different missions ends up coming out to around 12 at best, and these are all that you have to choose from.
-Upgradability: Not a lot to choose from. You can't even upgrade to a different taxi altogether.
-Maps: You have two. That's it. Both are cool, both are unique, but two can only keep someone entertained for so long.
-Awkward UI: I'm mostly refering to the map, which when you bring it up, it covers the entire center of your screen and makes it difficult to navigate.

I understand the game is in Early Access, so I'm hoping the devs take the potential this game has and push out an amazing product. Until then, however, I really don't think it's worth the pricetag of $25.
