About This Game

You are a God! You are master and ruler of a loyal nation. You have unimaginable powers at your disposal. You have claimed this world as yours. But there are others who stand in your way. You must defeat and destroy these pretenders. Only then can you ascend to godhood and become the new Pantokrator.

In Dominions you take control of a powerful being that rules a nation and aspires to godhood. The type of Pretender Gods can vary from magically powerful arch mages to huge titans or large monuments. The pretender gods have different strengths dependent on what kind of god you choose and what nation you play.

When you start the game you decide what kind of god you are and how your Dominion affects your lands and followers. It is an expression of your divine might and the faith of your followers. If your dominion dies, so do you. Your dominion also inspires your sacred warriors and gives them powers derived from your dominion.

In order to win and become the one true god you have to defeat your enemies one of three different ways: conquer their lands, extinguish their dominion or claim the Thrones of Ascension.

In Dominions 4 you can also play team games where one of the players take the role of supreme god, and the other members of the team are his servants and loyal subjects. The lesser gods rule their nations, but you all share the dominion of the supreme god.

Dominions is set in a fantasy world that draws inspiration from historical nations, cultures and myths. You will not encounter the elves of conventional fantasy in this game. Instead you might lead a nation of vanir from old norse myth. Aztecs, romans, israelites, greeks, shona and kievian rus are just a few of the inspirational sources of the game.

The first Dominions game was released in 2002 and was well received by strategy gamers. Since then Dominions has been much refined and Dominions series is still actively played making it one of the longest running 4x turn based strategy games.

Dominions 4 is also a highly moddable game. You can create your own monsters, spells and entire nations. Since Dominions 3 there has been a vivid modding community and Dominions 4 will enhance this capability even further.

If you really want to experience a "god-game," then the Dominions series is pretty much the only option. Literally hundreds of spells and units, incredibly customizable deities, and a nice modding community that offers even more content. The developer still updates the game despite several years after release, with some of these updates adding more playable empires and more god-creation options.

The core game design is the reason for Dominion 4's compelling nature. One can min-max the hell out of this game, but the interesting and fleshed out empires also allow emergent narratives and roleplaying, should you choose to pursue this style of play.

So whether you want a deep strategy game that has solid mechanics, or you simply want bang for your buck, Dominions is definitely worth checking out.

Dominions 4 is an extremely deep 4x, with more "under the hood" than its competitors. The combat, unit choice, list of factions, spells to research and ways to command your units is all far and above what other games offer.

But it doesn't have much interest in teaching you how to play it, and the interface is functional at best. Someone sitting down with Civ 5 or Endless Legend could quickly enjoy intuitive and simple mechanics, while Dominions 4 is a struggle from the start.

The setting is very rich, with mythological and history-based factions ruled over by a God of your design competing to bcome the omnipotent divinity. Dominions 4 requires a lot of investment to understand what's going on, why it's happening, and what to do, but it's very rewarding once you do so. 70 hours in I am still learning new things- Axe-equipped infantry are more likely to lop off an enemy's arm in combat, giving him hideous penalties and generally ruining his day. How awesome is that? But the game is in no hurry to tell you about its many wonderful features and possibilities.

Finally, the multiplayer is much smoother than other 4xes i've played, moving along comparatively fast. It's possible to play out thirty turns in one sitting with multiple friends, and a great way to spend an evening. Especially recommended to those who love history and mythology.

This game is pretty amazing. Even now, years after its release, Illwinter continues to update it. Just a few days ago, one of these updates added and entirely new faction to the game. That kind of dedication to their game impresses me, and I appreciate it a lot as a player.

Dominions 4 is a pretty complicated and difficult-to-learn strategy game. That complication comes mostly from the sheer depth of the game's mechanics, and a lack of an in-game tutorial. You'll probably need to read the manual (which is linked here on the store page) to learn the proper way to play. I'd recommend skimming over it even if you haven't bought the game yet, as it can probably give you a pretty good idea of what you're buying.

The gameplay focuses heavily on the use of magic and the managing of your dominion - dominion measures the population's belief in you, and their willingness to worship you as their god. One method of victory involves spreading your dominion across the entire map, eliminating all belief in other gods through fanatical preaching and construction of temples.

The community still seems pretty active, and you can probably find people to play with on the forums - along with user-made content like maps and mods(including player-made factions).

As far as fantasy-themed grand strategy games go, I haven't played very many... but I'd hazard a guess that Dominions is by far the most deep and varied one, with 83 factions, hundreds of spells, almost 400 craftable and equipable artifacts, and like a zillion spells (I actually have no idea how many there are, since almost every faction has faction-specific spells). It's definitely worth your time, and I'd say it's worth your money as well.

It should be said early that this game is not for everyone, but if you like this type of genre, then Dominions 4 is definitely worth it in my book.
The mechanics are mostly about overall strategic movements around the world map, and army compositions, and axcept for a pre-battle plan, there is no way to control your units in combat.

To start, you design your god, who will then command your chosen nation in the fight for the thrones of succesion, and ultimately the title of Pentakrator, the sole remaining and most powerful god in the world.
The goal is to expand your "empire", which means conquering land and spreading your dominion, and claiming the victory points around the map to crown yourself victor. Dominion is basically your chosen gods influence, and will give provinces modifiers based on how you designed your god.
Conquering land is vital for securing enough resources and gold to build an ever bigger army. Enemies will usually not be far away, and on multiple fronts. Smart decision making will often decide the outcome of your games.
As you gain land, send skilled mages into them to search for magic sites. Magical sites will provide gems, which are mostly used for spellcasting (combat) and rituals (world map spells). Having a high gem income can a lot of times be the edge needed to subdue enemy nations.

Personally, i prefer less micro intensive strategy games, which is why i like this game so much. The god aspect is very well thought through, and there is tons of variety in gods and magic, which is the ultimate charm of the game.
