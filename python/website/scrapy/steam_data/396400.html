About This Game

Welcome, new ghoul!
Come roam the halls of Monster High and get to know your new classmates. Join clubs, make friends and dress up in fierce fashions. But be aware, there’s something strange going on at Monster High! Cleo’s new amulet has been cursed by the spirit of Spookenhamen, and the student body is falling under his spell. Use your skills and savvy to help save the school and all your new classmates from the mummy’s curse! Be Yourself. Be Unique. Be a Monster.

• Play through an all new story-driven adventure featuring over 40 of your favorite Monster High characters!
• Create your own unique monster self – choose from dozens of skin tones, hair colors and styles, and tons of fierce outfits
• Shop and fill up your closet with outfits and accessories
• Build relationships with your new classmates and join clubs, like Fashion Club, Cooking Club and the Fearleading Squad

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, Windows Vista, Windows 7
    • Processor: 2 GHz Dual Core
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: GeForce 6800 or ATI Radeon x1950 or better, with 256 VRAM
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 4 GB available space

Yes, I'm an adult male, and yes, I know what I'm playing here.
No I am not a brony, and No, I have not completely lost my ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ mind.

So why do I reccommend this game?

Because not every game has to be for the hardened gamer. I actually enjoy the Monster High cartoons for their terrible, terrible, monster-related puns, and the creative designs of the characters. This game is much the same, only this time, you get to be part of it, as the eponymous "New Ghoul."

Pros:
Simple gameplay for younger gamers.
Everything is pretty child-friendly, but adults will (mostly unironically) enjoy it as well
Voice acting of some of the main characters in the show
Draculaura's Voice isn't as pitch shifted as usual, which is a blessing
Familiar mechanics (I'll talk about this soon)

Cons:
Game-crashing glitch can be encountered with the iCoffin's 'Feed' function.
There are only a dozen or so characters voice acted, and even then, they don't sound right.
A very small soundtrack, while the songs aren't terrible, they do loop, seemingly endlessly.
None of the newer characters appear in the game, nor are they likely to ever be. (Luna Mothews being the one I missed seeing)
The game tries to be modern and trendy for the kids, but introduces some of the worst trends, like their 'Feed' AKA Twitter.
Can only play as a girl, Mattel is kind of behind while at the same time trying to be current...
Maybe too liberally copying a certain game that also has a female protagonist. (I'll talk about this soon.)

OK, so you've bought this game, now you get to join the characters of the show and fashion doll line, Monster High, where who you are is more important than what you are. Sounds good, good message for the kids.

Enjoyment of this game quickly comes once I started just exploring, moving boxes, climbing ledges, and that's when it dawns on me that I've played this game before. It's Tomb Raider. Not the new Tomb Raider nee Uncharted, more like Tomb Raider: Anniversary. I swear, I want to find out what physics engine this game is using, because the characters move exactly the same. It's like playing TR:A only they took out the health and combat functions. As a gamer this is interesting, but maybe one day, Monster High will be my hypothetical daughter's gateway into playing Tomb Raider games.

As far as features go, this game comes fully loaded with Controller Support, achievements, and trading cards, though its menues leave something to be desired. It's not the absolute worst console port that I own by a long shot, and that has to count for something.

It's not 'so bad it's good' or mediocre, it's actually surprisingly good among a tier of games that you don't actually expect to be any good whatsoever. Sure, it can't compete with AAA games, or their biggest business rival, Lego, but this is a gem, you know, by Mattel standards.

Findal Verdict: Buy it, if only for your kids.
6.5/10 - While a long ways from perfect, and showing of Mattel's status as second banana to Lego in terms of quality, this still provides some entertainment, if only for the terrible, terrible monster puns, and the familiar Tomb Raider-esque gameplay.

I don't know anything about Monster High. Just from the look and feel, I'm calling it MLP meets Harry Potter, but with the horror genre. Personally, I love almost any game where I can customize a character for my avatar. It's what made me like THUG, and Tiger Woods PGA. So, this game starts out with that plus. You can choose from a very large variety of monsters to be your main character. Then you get to pick clothes (and you can buy more outfits in game) and hair-style so you can be as unique as you want to be.

The game is controller-supported, which is great. It reminds me a lot of Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone on the PlayStation. I haven't played too much, but you walk around school and can talk to people, and there are collectibles scattered all around for you to find, encouraging you to explore. In addition, you can find Locker Codes scribbled on the wall to open old lockers in the hallways. You don't even have to worry about writing them down. They are saved in your phone, and exclamation points are placed above the lockers you found the codes for!

The graphics are smooth, and aside from a couple times when I jumped down and glitched into a box, forcing me to reset my game, everything flows well. The atmosphere is nice, and I really dig the spooky dance-beat (probably based on the show's title music) playing in the background.

Some characters (possibly main characters? Again, I have never seen the show to know anyone yet.) are voice-acted, and the game-makers picked good people, I think. If you like the horror genre, and exploration/adventure games where you go on fetch-quests, and you don't mind the setting of a school and being a female, talking about monster fashion, this is a great game. I don't think the point is story so much as just being a part of the Monster High world as a character of your own. As a male, I am not ashamed of playing this game.

This Game is a nice Console Port brought to PC. It runs smooth and without any fps drops. The Graphics itself are very colourful, bright and cartoony. The Actual Story will take you 10 hours to go through and the story itself is rather rich and amazing.

The Game itself takes place in the Monster High World which is a Webispode Show and popular mostly among the younger audience. The Controls are also very easy, especially with a controller.

You will be able to costumize not only your clothes but your hair and with it your hair colour as well. Each haircut is provided with 2 differen colours. Via your iCoffin you will be able to buy even more clothes which lets you in the end create your own specific look.

All in all I can recommend this game especially for kids, if you are older than the age of 14 and you are not into Barbie or Disney or anything similiar I do not recommend you to get this game as you might think it is boring.

I also recommend this game for every achievement hunter as it is rather easy to get this a perfect game, also another + in my opinion is, this is the first game with actual achievements and trading cards.

Due to the fact that this Game is 10 hours long (or longer if you want to get all the extras) I can recommend it for its original price of 20€ but of course it is never a mistake to get it for cheaper as well.

This Game is by far one of the best Kid-games on Steam and I hope in the future there will be more such great games!
