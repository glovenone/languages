Update #6: Wealth and Weather



For our 6th update, we've overhauled the weather system making it more realistic and adding different intensities of rain up to thunder and lightning. We've also adjusted the scoring calculations adding a new category, Wealth, and doubling the effect of Merit so expect a new personal best in your next playthrough. Exposed modifiers in the travel panels mean you can judge which items you can and cannot do without next time you're planning a journey to some far flung desert stronghold and the addition of bullet effects on guns will help you work out who is shooting at you when you get there! Read the full list below and let us know what you think in the forum!

Update #5: Thugs and Lighting



Update 5 brings a new danger to the quieter corners of colonial India. Borrowed from the pages of history (and repurposed in modern parlance), Thugs are members of a secret society sworn to rob and kill defenseless travellers in service of their goddess Kali. Thankfully, the suffocating embrace of your colonial overlords comes with some perks. Notices about Thug activity will be at letter writers around India so you will hopefully be able to avoid them...unless you have other ideas. We’ve added a snazzy new map feature, which displays all the buildings in each town so you can better plan your journeys. We’ve also overhauled the lighting system and polished every light in the game so nighttime has never looked so pretty. Check out the changelist for all the details and let us know what you think in the forum!

About This Game

Kim is an open world RPG set in Rudyard Kipling's colonial India. Play as his ragamuffin hero Kim and roam hand-painted towns and procedurally generated countryside. Learn to survive as a penniless urchin, complete objectives, collect items and garner favour with the spies of the secret service. Your aim is to live Kim's youth as best you can for it is fleeting but if at first you don't succeed, you may retell this classic adventure as often as you wish!

Features

  • History: 1880s India brought to life in an enormous 2D open world
  • Literature: Rudyard Kipling’s dialogue in ‘choose your own adventure’ conversations
  • Survival: Manage Kim's health and happiness by collecting food and items
  • Action: Simple but satisfying stealth and combat in pause-able real time
  • Music: Original soundtrack from acclaimed composer Murugan Thiruchelvam

I really like how Kim captures the atmosphere of colonial India, and the spirit of Kipling's stories. Having backed the Kickstarter I moved this up my backlog, and I finally managed to play just under an hour.

I personally would love a story-mode without the survival mechanics (food and rest), but they are already balanced enough to be acceptable, even on a 'slow gaming' day. I am looking forward to travelling through India on a beggar's budget again.

I could happily spend hours just soaking in the atmosphere of this world. Kipling's prose draw you right in to the end of the Raj and help set the tone & mood for one of the most evocative RPG experiences I've played. The warm palatte and surpisingly sophisticated sountrack compliment each other beautifully and the gameplay, whilst easy to get to grips with seems well layered. Each mechanic is simple in itself but great thought has clearly gone into how they relate to each other creating a pleasing level of depth that players can approach at their own pace.

Playing an RPG with such a reality based lore is a novelty (at least for me) and Kiplings India is so rich, relevant, fascinating & wonderfully realised here that a genuine drive to explore and discover is itching away at you even before the (less than 5 min) Tutorial is over. The dialogue evokes it's period beautifully but is brief enough to ensure a brisk pace that let's you explore & experiment many times over many play throughs

Knowledge of the book is not necessary but there's a good chance you'll have ordered a copy before you're done.

Just finished the first of many imminent sessions playing this charming new indie game. Hadn't read Kim in years and was pleasantly surprised by how quickly the book came back to me and how faithful this new medium is to the original, with great graphics and an interface that really gives you a feel for the period.

Keeping both the tone and character of the book could have been heavy handed or confusing but delightfully neither is the case and this game of exploration, spiritual questing and intrigue recreates Kipling's story in an accurate, fun and accessible format.

Looking forward to seeing how Kim progresses, both as a character in my charge and as an engaging new game in my arsenal.

Having got excited by the prospect of this game when Kotaku listed it as one of its most anticipated adventure games of 2016 and then doubly so when it got the approval of Indie Game Mag, I took the plunge and backed its Kickstarter campaign. I'm happy to say that it was money well spent, as this game is a little beauty...although "little" is a bit misleading as there's loads to explore and do in the atmospheric world of Kipling's most impish creation.

Colonial India, with all its charm and craziness, is wonderfully brought to life by the hand-painted illustrations, the awesome soundtrack, and the use of Kipling's original text. The gameplay is solid, with the choices you make having a constant effect on Kim's character, both in terms of his happiness and his skillset. I seem to find myself in moral quandaries way too often, but maybe that's me!

Have only had a couple of sessions so far, but am looking forward to many more. Would definitely recommend to anyone, particularly if, like me, you're a fan of the original book.
