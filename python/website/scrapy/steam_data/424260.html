About This Game

Feelings Adrift is an amusement park filled with attractions. Each attraction has different environments and goals. Some are more scary than others, but they all have the same intention, to make a good, frightening experience.
Each attraction has a rating composed of five stars. One star attractions are slightly scary, while 5 star attractions will push you to the limits of your fear.
Starting out, Feelings Adrift will let you play two attractions: Black Hole and Hospital. Dark Room will release 3/03/2016 and Slender Woman, Mansion, and much more will come in future updates.
When you complete each attraction you'll win one gold key. When you obtain all the keys you'll have access to a 5 star attraction which is incredibly terrifying and different from any other experience in the game.
Accept the challenges that we propose and fight your fear.
More content will be coming in future updates.

KillerG Is Magic

Posted: 25 March
Disclaimer: I've played one of the three currently available "attractions."

I really like the idea behind this game. You're at an amusement park and each "attraction" is a mini-game of sorts. The developer has done a decent job of keeping the game updated, so I can only imagine how big this game could end up getting. I do feel like it would've benefited as being an Early Access title rather than getting a full release with only a few of these mini games to play, but at least it's still relatively cheap.

So far what I've seen is a lot of cheap jump scares. It's nice to see that in a game that's also pretty and has great atmosphere, no one seems to be doing those cheap jump scares anymore unless it's a 2D platformer or something. And the fact that the developer can create multiple different atmospheres you can jump between is pretty damn cool. But don't expect this game to be a psychological horror game, because it's not, it's more of a thriller. There are a few puzzles too, which weren't necessarily easy, but they weren't necessarily intensly difficult either.

Also I should point out again that this game is GORGEOUS. The dev built this on Unreal 4 and it really makes this game pop. The engine really helped with making the atmospheres feel believable if at times disjointed, at least in Black Hole. The audio was great, although I had trouble with music stopping sometimes if I left the area and then there would be no sound. Some of the effects for the scary portions are so strange that they're scary because you don't know what the hell they are...and for me that's good, but it may not work for you.

Would I recommend you buy this now? Probably not. Would I recommend you buy this when more attractions are available? Probably. And maybe even then buy it on sale. It currently costs $8 and it's a little steep for maybe 3-4 hours of playtime, but of course there will be more later. Give it a try though, you might be surprised.

TL;DR

Feelings Adrift is a great game if you're looking for cheap jump scares, but that's honestly all it is.



The visuals are awesome, at least where the level designs are concerned, but everything is uninspired. The gameplay of each attraction is just a watered down version of other horror games and the sound/music used is taken right out of other games and films. The AI is random and unintelligent, seeming to have no line of sight, and only catch you when you're right next to them. The worst thing to me is not the excessive use of jump scares (which is ALL the time), but the ugly enemy designs. They look so cheap and fake that it's hard to be afraid of them. The game has some cool elements and I like the concept, but I really can't recommend it unless you really like bad jump scares.
