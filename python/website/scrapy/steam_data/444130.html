About This Game

Auralux: Constellations is the awaited sequel to Auralux, the critically acclaimed minimalistic Real Time Strategy game. Start with a single planet. Then, upgrade, expand, and attack until you’ve conquered the entire system. Now with Auralux: Constellations, for the first time you can play against your friends in local or online multiplayer games.

Featuring gameplay constellations with one new feature at a time, Auralux: Constellations is a real-­time strategy game that distills the RTS genre to its essential elements. Explore new gameplay possibilities: what happens if you take these basic rules and add fog-­of-­war? What if the planets moved? What if you had wormholes connecting distant parts of the map? You can replay the game to near ­infinity.

All this with a slow, graceful feel and vibrant minimalistic graphics, all pulsing to the rhythm of ambient music. This game is designed to provide a relaxing, cerebral experience. Every choice matters, and the only path to victory is through clever strategy. It’s Real-­Time Strategy, deconstructed.

Main Features:
  • Minimalist but challenging RTS gameplay
  • Multiplayer, both local and online
  • Each constellation has its own specific gameplay variation
  • Gameplay­ driven music pulses and beats to the battle

TL;DR: The game is pretty pricey for $10 dollars, but will provide numerous hours of fun, would highly recommend picking it up if it goes on sale.

I won a copy of this game in a giveaway from /r/pcmasterrace, and honestly I didnt know what to expect at first, while I havent been able to log very many hours in the game, I would say that I have got enough to get a general feel for the game. Its a pretty simple RTS concept, build up units at your base, move them to another base, try to capture a couple bases and get a bunch of units and take over enemy bases. The graphics and style of the game are minimalistic which is actually pretty nice because you can run this game pretty stable on older computers, I tried it on an old HP laptop with only about 256MB of VRAM and it was a playable experience. Headphones are highly reccomended because the music in the game is great and in game you will need to listen for when enemies are making moves, the tempo of the soundtrack adds a kind of fast paced vibe, but make no mistake, you will need to go slow and take your time. As far as content goes, there is a lot of content to the game, there are about 4 seperate singleplayer chapters that I have seen, but I have not been able to progress pass the first chapter in my half hour of playing. The difficulty curve is very steep, the levels start off extremely easy but by about the fifth level you will notice that it will take serious thought and care to progress. The multiplayer aspect of the game seems a little bit bare, I was not able to find anymore that one public game when I logged on, but it appears that the developers are active in the game and even host events to play with them, so I give them a point for that. The ammount of content you get in reference to the price is toeing the line between acceptable and just a little too much, I would say that while there is enough content for $10, I could see people who are not die hard RTS fans simply not willing to spend so much money for the ammount of effort to put into it. But RTS fans who are looking for a real challenge should have no qualms with spending $10 on a game that well deserves their money.

Now onto some more pressing matters, how are the controls? Well the game claims to feature full controller support but I was having issues getting steam to detect my XBOX 360 contoller at the time so I cannot verify claims that it works with a 360 controller, and I do not own a steam controller so I cannot verify those claims as well, however I think that a keyboard and mouse will be much more suitable for this game to be played on.

Overall I am very impressed with the game and I wish the devs the best of luck with any future indeavors of theirs and thank them tremendously for such an awesome game.

EDIT: So one of the Devs commented telling me that there actually is the controls I complained about implemented and that I missed them in the tutorial, so I recant my earlier statements about a lack of units macros. Also this should be proof that the Devs are an incredible team and actually look at the reviews for the game and care about their audience.

AURALUX: CONSTELLATIONS, developed and published by War Drum Studios, is the sequel to the indie classic Auralux. This is the Steam debut for the franchise which has gotten a cult following from both the PC and mobile audience.

In each round the player begins with a single planet. The task is to then upgrade, expand and take over the enemies planets for sole power of the solar system. That may sound simple and it is at the easier levels. However, once the game reaches normal and harder modes the challenge becomes increasingly difficult. It’s a case of minimalist RTS elements being executed perfectly. The more you play the more elements are added.

The biggest upgrade from the original game to Auralux: Constellations is the addition of multiplayer which can take place in a local and online format. This addition gives the game significant playability if you have a friend or family member that can play at home or online. It’s been a feature that the game’s community has been begging for a long time.

The graphical presentation does not deviate away from the minimalism that is the main drawcard of the game. In fact, I would suggest that it takes it a level further. The graphics are simple enough that it allows the player to focus on the core focus of the gameplay. The music does the same only it helps raise the intensity to the next level. Although the developers claim to have designed it to provide a ‘relaxing, cerebral experience’ I would argue that the intensity and the intelligent gameplay forces the player to think and act quickly.

I cannot really fault it. The game achieves what it sets out to do at a high level and although I am yet to experience the multiplayer – holler at me if you’d like to play against me – I can only imagine that it is a wonderful addition.
