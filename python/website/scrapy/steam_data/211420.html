About This Game

New Content for PC
Dark Souls™: Prepare to Die™ Edition will include an untold chapter in the world of Lordran. Sent back to the past, player will discover the story when Knight Artorias still lives.
  • New Bosses – Including Black Dragon, Sanctuary Guardian, Artorias of the Abyss.
  • PVP Arena & Online Matchmaking System – Quick matching for players to play 1vs1, 2vs2 and 4 player battle royal.
  • New Areas – Including Oolacile Township, Oolacile Sanctuary, Royal Wood, Battle of Stoicism which is the training ground for online PVP battles.
  • New Enemies – Including Wooden scarecrows, Chained Prisoner, Stone Knight.
  • New NPCs – Including Hawkeye Gough, Elizabeth (keeper of the sanctuary).
  • New Weapons and Armor – Equip some from the new bosses, enemies, and NPCs

The Story

Dark Souls is the new action role-playing game from the developers who brought you Demon’s Souls, FromSoftware. Dark Souls will have many familiar features: A dark fantasy universe, tense dungeon crawling, fearsome enemy encounters and unique online interactions. Dark Souls is a spiritual successor to Demon’s, not a sequel. Prepare for a new, despair-inducing world, with a vast, fully-explorable horizon and vertically-oriented landforms. Prepare for a new, mysterious story, centered around the the world of Lodran, but most of all, prepare to die. You will face countless murderous traps, countless darkly grotesque mobs and several gargantuan, supremely powerful demons and dragons bosses. You must learn from death to persist through this unforgiving world. And you aren’t alone. Dark Souls allows the spirits of other players to show up in your world, so you can learn from their deaths and they can learn from yours. You can also summon players into your world to co-op adventure, or invade other's worlds to PVP battle. New to Dark Souls are Bonfires, which serve as check points as you fight your way through this epic adventure. While rested at Bonfires, your health and magic replenish but at a cost, all mobs respawn. Beware: There is no place in Dark Souls that is truly safe. With days of game play and an even more punishing difficulty level, Dark Souls will be the most deeply challenging game you play this year. Can you live through a million deaths and earn your legacy?

Key Features:

  • Extremely Deep, Dark & Difficult – Unforgiving in its punishment, yet rewarding for the determined – learn to strategize freely and conquer seemingly impossible challenges.
  • Fully Seamless World – Explore a completely integrated world of dark fantasy where dungeons and areas are seamlessly intertwined, with great height.
  • Mastery Earns Progression – Contains 60 hours of gameplay, with nearly 100 uniquely despair-inducing monsters & an incredibly nuanced RPG systems including: weaponry, armor, miracles, faith, and more. Player success depends on their eventual mastery of how and when to use the magic spells, choice of armor, the number of weapons, the types of weapons, and the moves attached to the weapons.
  • Network Play – Players may cross paths with one another, invading each other for PVP battles, or to play co-op and take on giant boss encounters as a team.
  • Flexible Character Development & Role Play – As the player progresses, they must carefully choose which of their character’s abilities to enhance as this will determine their progression style.
  • Community – See other real players and empathize with their journey, learn from seeing how others died, find and leave messages for your fellow players; helping them or leading them into death.
  • Symbolic of Life & Hope – The Bonfire is an important feature in Dark Souls for many reasons. Though in gameplay it serves as a health and magic recovery and a progression check point, it also happens to be the one place in the dark world where players can find a fleeting moment of warmth and calm.

System Requirements

    • OS: Windows XP , Windows Vista, Windows 7, or newer
    • Processor: Intel Core 2 Duo E6850 GHz+ or AMD Phenom II X2 545 3.0 GHz+
    • Memory: 2 GB
    • Hard Disk Space: 4GB
    • Video Card: GeForce 9800 GTX+ or ATI Radeon HD 4870+
    • DirectX®: 9.0c
    • Sound: Direct Sound Compatible
    • Additional: Microsoft Xbox 360® Controller for Windows® (or equivalent) is strongly recommended.

Dark souls...whew, I just finished, and I'm ready for a review now.

I was one of those people who knew that Dark Souls was supposed to be a pretty hard game and that was it, until the DS3 hype came in. I tried a friends' DS3 and I loved it, however I felt that something was missing, I felt I was missing much of the rich "Dark Souls" culture. So I said thanks a lot for letting me play DS3, and bought the DS1. Best, idea, ever.

Graphically
For some reason I did not expect much for all being "old game" & stuff of the like, however I was a quite surprised at how beautiful the environments looked, if you like looking around at the panoramas like I do, oh you're in for a treat. I loved the style. But then I wondered if it could get better anyway, well it can! With DSFix (mod) + HD textures mod it will make your retina soothe a lil bit while playing.

Game mechanics
If this game is thought to be pretty hard, it is because you have to actually "git gud" as they say. Getting used to the hitboxes, judge where should you roll to, understand how to choose your setup, analize the boss and see what is the best approach, etc. That takes time, in fact, that is pretty much the hard part. As you progress, you will find (or at least I did) that the TRUE empowering comes from all those tricks and tips in your Dark Souls journey, not so much from leveling up your character or your weapons. So what really makes you stronger is not the +15 weapon you'll be holding at some point, but knowledge (but the +15 weapon helps a bit anyway).

Curiosity wins
I thought this was quite interesting. Sometimes perhaps one is way too linear, making it so that you might be focusing on being able to handle gracefully your inevitable thousands of deaths, and overcome the impression of some scenarios, monsters, bosses, clinging to the last second as to safeguard your hard earned souls. However, this game WILL punish you for being linear, apart from being vigilant for your health, you need to be able to have a keen eye for secrets and items lying around. You have to be curious and say "Oh, look, a freaking dark hallway! Probably there will be a thousand monsters but perhaps I find something!" and so you may...
The item descriptions, the tricky environment taking advantage of your camera angles, illusion magic, and what not. You need to make Dora the Explorer be freaking proud of you here, else you might lose tons and I mean TONS of items, upgrades, CONTENT! Not all the content is there for you to gobble at plain sight.
Also, if you are getting your gluteus maximus handed over to you, you're probably not where you are supposed to be yet (according to your strength) however, you can technically access almost all the main areas from the start, if you discover how...

This game comes from a console, is it good for PC?
To be honest, the thought of using a mouse and a keyboard for this game was discarded after the first 60 seconds. If you plan on getting this game, I STRONGLY recommend to use a controller. There are some bosses/monsters there that I seriously doubt that you will have a nice time with M&K instead of a controller. So if you don't have one, you might want to have that in mind.

Stress factor
I believe this is a game far more enjoyable for those able to keep a serene focus on everything that needs attending. If your ragemeter fills up fairly quickly, this is not for you. If you are expecting greenlands and rainbows, this is not for you. If you tend to be rapidly stressed by demanding situations, this is not for you. You should not suffer over a game either for a hype or because your want the achievements, or who knows why, only enjoy. Those with a serene focus will be able to adapt better their responses to the always-changing situations that the game will place you in.

Lore
As of today, you are probably used to quest markers everywhere, a minimap, perhaps a little dot trail for you to follow. Well, this game has none of that, you are thrown out of the blue, with a few character control indications and it is expected of you to figure everything else. Everything. This not only includes how the game works, but what the hell is actually going on. The background story is not spoon-fed to you as you go here and there, you have to fusion your archaeology and anthropology skills. Collecting here and there some items, reading descriptions, very very carefully listening to what the few NPCs might have to say about anything, oh yes, that is of outmost importance, if anyone (or anything) opens their mouth to say anything to you (which they won't except for one because they use like telepathy or something) pay special attention to every syllable.

Multiplayer
Is this game dead? No, to my surprise. I actually bought it with the hopes of having buddies from around the world to come and join me in my treks. Well, actually this was my first and perhaps my biggest negative experience, you will find that some people just simply want to see the world burn and will on purpose try to wreck your experience, so you have to be smart. Not just with a message saying "Jump here" where you'll fall to your death, no, that is actually part of the fun of the game, I don't enjoy tricking people, but sometimes it make me chuckle when I saw the blood spots near one of those or saying "Oh hell no I'm not dumb man!" after reading it, etc. No what I mean, is people using cheats or the "namebug" just to get your game to crash, I was so excited when I saw the first summon sign ever, and when I activated it, my game crashed, and I was terrified it might have corrupted my save or something. Which is why I recommend to setup the watchdogs and the DSCM mods, which will boost up positively your multiplayer experience.
Be wary of those invaders *chuckle*! I dislike PvP, so I can't really say I had much success at invading as to provide you with accurate experience on how balanced the matches were, etc. (must git moar gud). However along your way you'll find not a few phantoms ready to help you (humans or NPCs alike).

My experience
This game was absolutely overwhelming for me. I fell in love. The level design (expect for Blighttown, god I hated that, and you will too, you will too...), the music, the bosses, the armor designs, the not-so-obvious storyline, the not-so-obvious mechanics, everything. This game has taught me what distrust is though, but I sort of liked that, made it fun, I would never open anything before hitting it (you will find out why), randomly hitting walls (you will find out why), watch my back from times to times, etc. And while this world is certainly an unforgiving, blackned, eerie, devastated, hell, there are times when it will make you smile and laugh. When I finally got to the last boss and managed to beat him in my, like, 7th try, I was shaking. It has been long since I deeply enjoyed such a masterpiece.

Now I have to wait until DS3 is way more affordable, sometime in the future...Time to hop on to DS2 while that happens.


If you plan on buying it, just remember: patience, curiosity, and serenity.

Praise the sun!
