About This Game

Cubicolor is a minimalist puzzle game with soothing piano music.

The rules are simple: Match the color patches on the board and reach the exit, all on one single path. Every tile can only be touched once before it falls into nothingness. The game features 50 different levels and gets harder as you progress, always accompanying you with relaxing piano tunes.

Bloxorz with Colors
Bloxorz was an old flash game I used to play and that is what drew me to this game.
It has 48 levels & 3 achievements which you can get by beating all levels so for us Achievement Hunters that’s a win!
The objective is to move to a color and match it to the same colored block to unlock parts of the exit and ultimately get to the end. You can only move on a block once so you need to think about the best path to take before stepping out. You can always just go for it and it does work out sometimes but in the later levels planning out your path will help you beat it faster without guessing.

For $2 this is a great game for puzzle lovers, only took me 2.2 hours to complete all levels so it's not crazy difficult but some levels were harder for me than others.

The difficulty scaling is a little odd, I found myself stuck on a few late levels for a good 5 minutes trying to figure out all the different paths and just couldn't see the answer but that could be a fault of mine as the longer you look at something your brain tends to go over the same areas over and again and it makes it harder to see anything else but then the next level I do in 1 shot just by going the most logical path through each color.

8/10
- Quick, Challenging, Relaxing, $2, 100% Achievements, What’s not to like

There are games that resonate with you more than others. Sometimes they’re from genres that you don’t regularly play. Cubicolor reminded me of the things that I loved about my school education. I can appreciate that to some of you that may sound bizarre but I was at my best as a student when I was given patterns, arithmetic and comprehension tasks to complete. I’ve always had an avid love of both words and numbers. Although I didn’t do as well in maths in high school as what I should have I can put that down to teacher-student conflicts more than a disliking for the curriculum that was being taught. I’ve always been fascinated by sporting statistics as well and working out the various patterns involved in comprehending the how and the why certain things perform the way that they do.

Cubicolor is yet another minimalist game that we have featured. This time you need to find your way throughout each level by changing color by moving over the plus marker and hitting the painted square of said color before moving on to the next color until you’ve completed all colors and get to the exit. It sounds easy in theory but once you move over a box it disappears and you are unable to move over that square again. Therefore the game is a pattern maze. Some of the levels are easier than they look yet the majority are the other way around – much more of a challenge than you would initially think.

One addition that would add a lot of value to the game is for Moltenplay to add a level editor. I’d love to see what the Steam community would be able come up with. Some of the workshop items added in other games are some of the best contributions to the game and are often pieces of brilliance that the developers of the game couldn’t have possibly thought of. This is often the case in puzzle games as it is a genre that often allows for creativity.

Thus far I’ve only completed the normal levels but I very much look forward to completing the even more challenging levels.

Simple puzzle game with intuitive gameplay and nice aesthetic, with a very limited amount of levels, no real replayability and a lack of difficulty even in the later levels. You are introduced to the concept of the game in the first few levels, and nothing gets added - just a slowly increasing amount of waypoints you have to pass through to get to the goal - the only real step up in difficulty is about half way through the second set of 16 levels when you start to get two pairs of waypoints of the same colour, although in most cases it tends to be fairly clear which ones pair together, but this mechanic does allow a few more interesting levels late in the game.

In terms of the interface it is mostly fairly good - the menu is simple to use, and in the game I used arrow keys to move around and enter for the next level (saving having to switch to mouse until the set of 16 had ended). The main frustration I had was the animations for colouring up/down and the goal descending, which jar the flow of the game every time you get to a checkpoint on your route.

So overall if you want a quick relatively simple puzzle game that can be finished in an hour or two you should be okay here, but don't expect too much.
