About This Game

Yury is a deliberately retro platform/arcade action game that combines old-school graphics, classic mechanics and challenging gameplay with a sense of sci-fi survival horror atmosphere.



Accompanied by original EDM/chiptune soundtrack, player will control a cosmonaut on a distant and dangerous planet, helping him to avoid intricate traps and get rid of sinister monsters. In-air dodging, fast paced puzzles and intense shooting will keep on the edge of one's seat.

Who said spikes and lava should bring immediate death? Yury is a fresh look on classics! Yury's space-suit lets you quickly jump through the hot flow and gives you some time to crawl to the checkpoint after being hit by sharp edges. But keep an eye on the temperature and oxygen level!

Features:
  • Nostalgic look
  • Amazing atmospheric soundtrack
  • Challenging platform experience combined with intense shooting
  • A special suite with oxygen & temperature indicators

There is absolutely nothing wrong with platformers that make you want to smash something into your monitor or induce an incredible wish for self-torture.
As long as you're the only one to blame this is all fine.

But if you keep on dying because the controls are bad, then the game is not difficult or challenging or demanding, no sir, in that case the game is just simply bad.

Give me Super Meat Boy or Electronic Super Joy and you will see me rage, scream, cry, swear ... but you will never hear me saying that this or that was the game's fault.
In case of Yury, most of my deaths were the game's fault simply because the controls are so bad (and keys aren't rebindable) that the required precision is barely achieveable.

You like performing precision jumps just to get drowned in lava because you slide off the edge?
You like having to use the "z" key to jump?
You like finally managing one passage just to be forced to jump down without knowing what's below you?
Then Yury is the game for you.
I know it's not the game for me.

P.S. You might be able to change keys in Unity's launcher, but that's like saying "you can rebind it in the ini file".

I play a lot of hard as nail platformer games, electronic super joy, super meat boy, you name it, this is just another game trying to be like that but it fails in several aspects:

1. Any hard as nails platformer trys to keep you going by respawning you instantly or providing a method to instantly restart from the last saved checkpoint, this game does neither
2. No good soundtrack, what is this music? I guess it is trying to replicate the 80s 8 bit music, but it is almost over trying in an attempt to sound stereotypically 8 bit. This music makes me want to stop playing, not keep trying
3. Wired messed up physics, I get it, its a hard as nails platformer, thus the physics are usually messed up. But why in the world does the guy randomly slide when I am not telling him to go either left or right. There is a particular part when the save point is such that as soon as you respawn, you slide off the platform to your death, unless you jump the other side and by luck land on another platform, but not slide off that platform
4. No ability to change the controls. Really? Can I use anything else? How about the giant space button you are ignoring for some odd reason?
5. No graphics options, ok maybe you wanted to make a really retro looking 8-bit game, that doesn't mean it should lack features that define games of this century, other wise go and charge the price we would pay for such games, the 25 cents for such a game at a garage sale

Just because a game is supposed to be retro does not mean that you can be lazy and not feel obligated to list the keybindings anywhere

I would like to say firstly that I voted for this game on greenlight. I rather liked the art style, and I enjoy games like Electronic Super Joy, Stealth B-stard Deluxe and Dustforce. I honestly wanted to like this game, but after 30 mins of frustration I just cannot continue to play.

The biggest problem that makes this game unplayable for me is buggy/unresponsive controls. I get that it's meant to be one of those impossibly hard platformers, but you'll find yourself sliding off platforms when you're not moving (at one checkpoint it happened every time I respawned) and falling down when your character fails to jump. I didn't even get to face enemies because it was just too painful to get past the second checkpoint. If the control issues were to be fixed, I would probably give it a go again.

To those saying you can't change graphics settings or controls, you can use the "Yury Configuration" window that pops up before you launch the game, the only issue is it doesn't seem to save your options so you have to configure it every time you launch the game. I had set it to play in windowed mode and changed the jump button to spacebar, which makes it more bearable.

The music could be better, too. One thing common to the games I mentioned above is that they have excellent soundtracks that make you want to keep playing for that alone. This game is cheap though, so I can't complain too much.

It does seem that the dev is trying to fix the issues with the game, so I may change my review when and if these issues get sorted out.
