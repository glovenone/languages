About This Game

About
Runeous is a 2 part story. In this episode we introduce the characters and the main plot which will be concluded in part 2.
Each installment is free to play and serve as a beginning for our studio's future products.

This Episode contains roughly 20 minutes of game time.

Story
The story follows a small group of heroes in a world ravished by the wars of its past.
Runestones possessing magical qualities are what fuel the industry, their secrets long ago lost.
Humans research them to unlock their hidden potential.
Overpopulation in cities built for far fewer inhabitants. Homes built atop homes with giant walkways connecting the various levels together.

The world of Ceir'a faces a new threat. It is here that Leif, a young runic apprentice meets a Jacawi tribeswoman Eme. Together they must overcome the differences of their people and unite the factions to thwart the rising evil empire. Though not all is as it seems when the very fabric of nature begins to fall apart!

Characters
The story follows 4 main characters and various 'NPCs' who help the story along. Being a story of 2 parts, we will be introducing more plot essential characters in the second episode.

If you like indie visual novels, then this one is worth trying out. It's a bit short and stops at a rather lazy cliffhanger that has little to do with the story thus far, but the lore given during the story and the characters could make for an interesting story if the writer knows where he'll be going with this. There were some choices too, but they don't really matter yet.

The biggest plus from this game is the art, both quality and quantity wise. The main cast has a wide range of poses which is quite impressive considering the detail of each sprite, and even the less important characters who weren't even named got a sprite while they would be corporeal voices in other VN. Even the archer who only got one line in this story got an appearance. And the background art is equally impressive. There is quite a lot of it for the length of the story, and it's in a picturesk art style which goes well with the character art and the feeling of the story.

The only minus of this story is the coding. It's definitely not bad, but if you're spoiled by Ren'py like me, you'll miss things like the auto skip function or the ability to scroll back. I'm not sure what engine these guys used, it did the trick so it's fine, but I'm (not so) secretly hoping they'll switch to Ren'py in the later chapters.

(I hate to be the odd man out. But this is the first legitimately negative review for this title.)

This free short story is one-part of a two-part plan and is about 30-40 minutes long from my experience (slow reader). Both parts 1 & 2 are intended to be presented in 2 formats each, Visual Novel (or VN) and Animation. Logically, this review is for the VN version of Runeous Part 1.

Personally, it took me about an hour to see all paths. To be frank and with all due respect to the team who developed this particular title, I'm not really impressed. Although the story started in a decent manner, the bickering of the two narrators left an awful taste in my mouth. (Why does there need to be more than one narrator? If they don't cooperate, it just falls apart.) However, I may just be spoiled by other VN projects. I personally believe this one missed its mark.

PROS:
+ Great scenery backgrounds
+ Skip works when you're not in-window
+ Character art is...decent

CONS:
- Story feels generic
- Narrators constantly bickering
- Occassional grammatical errors
- Some character portraits are not properly alligned
- Lene's portrait when skipping the story had the wrong image (terrified Lene)
- No BACK button
- Music loops will get irritating
- "Skip" is too slow
- Character art may not be bad, but still bothers me


I respect this small team for developing this title (along with their ambitions to complete the story), however, it's been a negative experience on my part. I can't say whether part 2 would be good or not, but aside from achievements and a free and easy 100%, I cannot in good faith recommend this title.

3/10 A visual novel that just didn't hit its mark.

This is an OK game/visual novel about a group of individuals with some goal (it isn\'t too clear) after a war that happened 600 or so years prior. It involves runes and stuff of the sort. Pros:
+Free

+Credits actually look kinda cool

Neutral:
=Some choice- however it is obvious which one is the better because it either has not effect, a +1, or a -1 right next to the option.

Cons:
-No way to change settings- so you are locked in a window with music that you can\'t really adjust. However, the window can be changed size by dragging the corners, but if not done correctly, the images will be stretched awkwardly.

-Dialog is iffy- it isn\'t the most believable but I guess it sort of fits the \"time frame\" or \"style\" that the developers were going for.
-Displays the actions for the characters as -(action)-. For example, it would appear as -Clears his throat-. It may get meshed together with what they are saying and it may become confusing.
-If you missed some text, it is impossible to go back to it if you did not save beforehand.

For the record, this is my first time playing/sitting through a story game like this. I do enjoy a wide variety of game types, and have far more hours of game time in general than I'll ever admit to my wife (most of it pre-dating Steam). So, since I cannot review this game against its genre, I'll review it for the experience overall.

The long and the short, I'd give it a 3/5, considering that it is a first release and a trial run for the developer.

I won't lay out any spoilers about the story itself, but I will talk a bit about the game content. If you want to be fully surprised by what's to come in this story, stop reading now, go play first, then come back.


The Story - 3/5
The story itself was interesting. There was nothing very original in the concepts laid out, but it was still a fun read. In this Part One, as the developer already told us in the release video, we only get a taste for what is to come. It could have used an editor with strong written English skills to fix the punctuation and grammar in several places, but there is nothing so broken as to be unreadable or not understood.

Art Work - 5/5
The character and background artwork are both wonderfully done. I'm not sure what more I can say about that, I really enjoyed them!

Soundtrack - 4/5
I think there were three tracks throughout the whole: a common background, a battle track, and a suspenseful track for the cliffhanger. For the length of the overall story I thought this played out nicely. The only thing I didn't care for was the transition between. It was fairly jarring to me moving from the common track to either of the others. The suspenseful track could have been a bit longer. I read at the pace of the text cursor for the most part, and the track had just turned its first loop as I was finishing up reading the last bit of text. If it lasted five to ten seconds longer it would have lined up with the text. A small thing, but it did jump out at me.

Mechanics - 2/5
The mechanics are very limited. Having only played through once I honestly don't know what function they served. I will play through at least once more choosing different options to see how it changes the story, if at all. Since the developer said this is only a taste of what's to come I'm currently chalking this up to "future use", where the choices you make now will impact the story later down the road (thinking Chrono Trigger trial). Also, having the effect of the choice listed right there on the choice seems odd. Again though I'm new to this genre, maybe that's a norm? The only thing I can compare to is the same mechanic employed in some D&D RPGs, where the choice affects your alignment and NPC response, and part of the game is figuring out which choices to make when. Anyway, starting to ramble... In summary, I found the single limited mechanic to be unnecessary.

Configuration - 2/5
Very little in the way of configuration. All forms and dialogs had default icons, and dialogs felt "out of the box", not skinned/incorporated. Very rough/alpha feel, but again I think that's what this was intended to be in the developer's grand scheme. First release, feeling out their future a bit. Would still be nice to see some cleanup in a patch. For a story game I would expect immersion is key from start to finish, so incorporating all of the small elements is mundane, but would make a significant difference. Also, should go for a widescreen aspect ratio, not square. I say this again for the same of immersion. If I can't fill up my screen I'm being distracted by what else is going on.


I hope that was useful to you. I think this was my first review on Steam as well, so if you've time/inclination please give me feedback on the review.
