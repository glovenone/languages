Introduction
Royal Bounty HD is a fantasy turn based strategy game that harkens back to classics such as King's Bounty and Heroes of Might and Magic.

Gameplay
Don't get me wrong, Royal Bounty HD is a fun-ish game, although can be extremely fustrating at times and can really annoy you. Although the game does have a strange amount of addictivness to the game, it is sometimes beyond a challenge, at times the game be so fustrating and annoying. The game doesn't do very well to balance the game with fun and challneging gameplay. I did find the game fun here and there, but the game is more of a challenge than many gamers really want. I understand that this game is suppose to be difficult, but in my opinion the game is too difficult. The gameplay itself is a real challenge, so I would only really suggest this game is you are into really difficult games. The game doesn't really focus much upon the story of the game, making this game almost all about the difficult gameplay. Another small feature that seems to be missing in this type of game is a mini map or overworld, this would save a lot of backtracking that is wasted in the game that you are required to do.

The graphics of the game are based upon the the original King's Bounty & Heroes 1 and 2, from what I have seen from the other gameplay the graphics really do well to represent those other 2 games in the series. In my opion, I love these type of retro graphics, brings back a nostalgia feeling to the game that you don't really get with other retro games.

Pros
+ Cheap price
+ A nice retro art style
+ Steam Achievements
+ Addictive gameplay

Cons
- Hasn't got many features and gameplay improvements
- Interface is clunky
- No mini-map or overworld map

Result / Final thoughts
This is a difficulty game, that lacks a few basic features that the previous games used to have. Although the gameplay is fun and somewhat addictive, the difficulty will overcome that and take most of the fun out of the game altogether which is what the real downfall of this game is unfortunatly. On the other hand, the game does a terrific job on handling the retro art style of the game which I will definatly give them credit for. But unfortunatly all in all I cannot recommend this game.

Review Copy
Royal Bounty is probably a game that falls in between two reasonably well know game sagas gameplay-wise, one beign Heroes of Might and Magic, and the second one being King's Bounty. Why am I saying this? Well there are elements taken from here and there, such as the time (days, weeks, months) system, the recruitment system, and stuff like that.

Anyway, without further ado, here's a point by point review on the game:

Art 7/10
The art style of the game is something that you will either love or might not really be fond of. A little trick I personally applied was downscaling my screen options to something like 1280x720, and everything became much neater, bigger, and overall better.

Gameplay 6/10
The gameplay mimics those of the previously mentioned game titles, but falls short in some aspects, such as not allowing the player to move a unit and with the remaining action points, attack. This is a bit of a let down, since in King's Bounty, kitting is such and important ability to use.

Sound 5/10
Here's where the game really feels a bit unpolished. The sound seems generic, the music is unimpressive... It's definitely a department which needs a bit of work.

Conclusion
Is it worth it? For it's cheap price tag, I would say it's worth a shot, especially if you're into this game genre. The developers keep updating the game every couple of weeks so these issues might get a deserved improvement. Something worth pointing out is that I feel the game is best suited for a smartphone device rather than a PC (As far as I'm aware, the game has support for most mobile devices).

Overall a nice game, with room for improvement.

It's a fun, and at times frustrating (I don't mean challenging), fantasy turn based strategy game that harkens back to classics such as King's Bounty and Heroes of Might and Magic. However, I can only recommend it to people who have already played those games, really love the art style of Heroes of Might and Magic 2, and enjoy the gameplay of the original King's Bounty and Heroes 1 and 2. This game is like the original King's Bounty ported into the Heroes 2 engine. If that sounds good to you, then this game is worth it. But keep in mind that it doesn't have any of the improved features and gameplay of the later Heroes games or the new King's Bounty games.

For people who haven't played the games that inspired this one or are new to turn based strategy games, play the Heroes of Might and Magic and King's Bounty games first. They're a bit more expensive but they go on sale often and even at the normal price, they're worth their value.

Pros:
-Cheap
-Lovely art style very reminiscent of Heroes 2.
-Wide variety of spells, creatures, and artifacts.
-Easy to learn.
-Addictive gameplay.

Cons:
-Bugs and occasional crashes.
-Not a lot of resolution settings.
-No manual of any kind.
-Interface is somewhat clunky.
-No mini-map or world map. This becomes quite frustrating after you've explored a good chunk of the world.
-While there are a lot of creatures, many of them are very similar and only differ in small statistical ways.
-The difficulty can skyrocket if you move too slowly, get lost, or backtrack too much.
-Lacks a lot of the features and gameplay improvements of Heroes games after 2 and the new King's Bounty games. And I'm talking about simple things like a wait option in combat.

Don't believe the bad reviews. A lot of the negative opinions about this game are from people who have played many of the games that this one is supposed to be based upon (Kings Bounty, Heroes of M&M). I've never really played either of those games, so I was able to come to it with a fresh perspective, and I happen to enjoy it. "It's supposed to be based off of "x", but it's not exactly the same. Thumbs down!" Who cares! Enjoy and respect this game for what it is.

The advent of the "easy" mode is a huge plus, as it allows you to get a few playthroughs in to explore the world, and figure out which quests you need to tackle and in what order. This seems like a game that would be very difficult to beat on the first play through, due to the increase in enemy armies over the passage of time. With that said, it only adds to the value of the game, as you'll get in more play time to hone your strategy and approach to each play through until you know exactly where to go, and the best ways to get there and what units (and treasures) to pick up along the way.

A lot of the strategy required in this game falls outside of battle. Do you spend the few extra turns to go get the those treasure chests guarded by those tough monsters? Or skip them, and complete that quest that's been waiting for you to complete it? Or take the time to do both, but in which order?

I'm enjoying the game right now, and feel like I'll get many more hours of enjoyment as I delve deeper into the world, and work on better paths to complete the quests. At first, I didn't like the idea of feeling "rushed" to complete certain quests, but I'm beginning to like the idea of doing several play throughs to "perfect" my path.

I find myself wanting to restart a lot of the RPGs I play, because after getting a feel for them, I see how I could do the beginning levels more efficiently. In quite a few of those RPGs though, I experience what I like to call "restart remorse", in that I spend so much time on my first play through, that to restart to get "a more perfect party", and it tends to bother me a bit when I do finally restart.

In Royal Bounty (though not a traditional RPG per say), you are rewarded when you restart, as you have more knowledge that ends up benefitting you tremendously when you do begin a new game.

This is game is well worth it's $5 price tag, and lends value for the price. Give it a try, and enjoy it for what it is.

Review Copy
Royal Bounty is probably a game that falls in between two reasonably well know game sagas gameplay-wise, one beign Heroes of Might and Magic, and the second one being King's Bounty. Why am I saying this? Well there are elements taken from here and there, such as the time (days, weeks, months) system, the recruitment system, and stuff like that.

Anyway, without further ado, here's a point by point review on the game:

Art 7/10
The art style of the game is something that you will either love or might not really be fond of. A little trick I personally applied was downscaling my screen options to something like 1280x720, and everything became much neater, bigger, and overall better.

Gameplay 6/10
The gameplay mimics those of the previously mentioned game titles, but falls short in some aspects, such as not allowing the player to move a unit and with the remaining action points, attack. This is a bit of a let down, since in King's Bounty, kitting is such and important ability to use.

Sound 5/10
Here's where the game really feels a bit unpolished. The sound seems generic, the music is unimpressive... It's definitely a department which needs a bit of work.

Conclusion
Is it worth it? For it's cheap price tag, I would say it's worth a shot, especially if you're into this game genre. The developers keep updating the game every couple of weeks so these issues might get a deserved improvement. Something worth pointing out is that I feel the game is best suited for a smartphone device rather than a PC (As far as I'm aware, the game has support for most mobile devices).

Overall a nice game, with room for improvement.

Introduction
Royal Bounty HD is a fantasy turn based strategy game that harkens back to classics such as King's Bounty and Heroes of Might and Magic.

Gameplay
Don't get me wrong, Royal Bounty HD is a fun-ish game, although can be extremely fustrating at times and can really annoy you. Although the game does have a strange amount of addictivness to the game, it is sometimes beyond a challenge, at times the game be so fustrating and annoying. The game doesn't do very well to balance the game with fun and challneging gameplay. I did find the game fun here and there, but the game is more of a challenge than many gamers really want. I understand that this game is suppose to be difficult, but in my opinion the game is too difficult. The gameplay itself is a real challenge, so I would only really suggest this game is you are into really difficult games. The game doesn't really focus much upon the story of the game, making this game almost all about the difficult gameplay. Another small feature that seems to be missing in this type of game is a mini map or overworld, this would save a lot of backtracking that is wasted in the game that you are required to do.

The graphics of the game are based upon the the original King's Bounty & Heroes 1 and 2, from what I have seen from the other gameplay the graphics really do well to represent those other 2 games in the series. In my opion, I love these type of retro graphics, brings back a nostalgia feeling to the game that you don't really get with other retro games.

Pros
+ Cheap price
+ A nice retro art style
+ Steam Achievements
+ Addictive gameplay

Cons
- Hasn't got many features and gameplay improvements
- Interface is clunky
- No mini-map or overworld map

Result / Final thoughts
This is a difficulty game, that lacks a few basic features that the previous games used to have. Although the gameplay is fun and somewhat addictive, the difficulty will overcome that and take most of the fun out of the game altogether which is what the real downfall of this game is unfortunatly. On the other hand, the game does a terrific job on handling the retro art style of the game which I will definatly give them credit for. But unfortunatly all in all I cannot recommend this game.
