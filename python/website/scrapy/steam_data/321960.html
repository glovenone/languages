18 February

Patch 1.7 notes

Hello Heroes,

Today we're finally excited to be releasing not only the long awaited patch 1.7, but part one of our Lost tales of Axeoth campaign.

As always, downloading this new update is automatic on both Uplay and Steam. Should this for whatever reason not happen, please then make use of the Verify files feature on either platform, which should then start the download.

Full patch notes are available below but here are some highlights;

- As previously announced, the level cap has been removed on Skirmish maps.
- The main menu has been reworked to provide an easier user experience.
- Several fixes to Artifacts.
- A couple of adjustments have been made to the in-game camera, automatic height adjustment has been smoothed and some clipping issues have been fixed.
- New key-bindings:
• Browse towns with left and right key (works in town screens and all town pop up’s)
• Toggle combat map grid with "G"
• Escape can now be (re-)assigned as key-bind
• Warfare Units now wait or skip turn, when pressing the wait or defend keys
- Rendering on Wide-screen: Aspect ratio management has been improved in council hub, adventure and combat modes.
- Mini-map Settings are now saved and UPlay names are now only displayed in online Multi-player matches
- Several gameplay bugs have been fixed
- Sylsai’s second starting skill has been improved to ‘Warcries’
- Several balancing improvements
• Diminishing returns - When the "Incapacitated" de-buff wears off, it applies the "Immune to Incapacitation" buff for two combat rounds.
• A cap to the number of raised creatures for the Necromancy skill and the "Ghost Ride" ability.
- Multiplayer:
• Improved connectivity of online game
• Nat indicator now available in multiplayer lobby.
• Out of sync when attacking an empty AI hero in sim turns.
• Out of sync when surrendering in combat against AI hero.
• General out of sync stability improvements.
• The ability to connect to each other has been improved and a couple of implements to the Uplay-Overlay.
- A new CM_Academy_Garden combat map has been added.
- Editor:
• Couple of fixes on the Combat Map
• Duplicate and imprinted spells from all spell lists have been removed.
• Fixed FPS drop when switching to underground surface and create combat map feature. (+several other fixes.)
• New script Editor Condition "Has Resource Percentage"
• +15% Saturation LUT to the Combat Map templates
• BattleSite properties accessible in the Map Editor.

About This Game

Our story takes place during a war of succession. The Empress’ murder has left a realm in flames with many hungry rivals ready to seize the throne by force. The Duke Ivan then calls together a prestigious council of six trusted advisors to restore order and end the conflict that set ablaze Ashan’s lands.

Might & Magic® Heroes® VII makes a triumphant return bringing you the essence of turn based strategy gaming. Set in a fantasy universe with RPG progression with a strong story narrative, you will embark on a journey that you will never forget!

Lets get down to business...Might and Magic Heroes VII

As a foreword, I have been playing the Heroes of Might and Magic games since part 2, so I do believe I can safely say Ive been playing them for a while. One thing Ive noticed by every Heroes game since part 3...is that its completely compared to 3, just let it die folks. Heres some food for thought, if you want a part 3 so badly, just go play part 3? Regardless, onto the review. I will, as with every Heroes game, be viewing it as a whole, and completely ignore the fact that its 'supposed to be part 3' like so many others.

I have recommended this game seeing as I personally obtained it when it was for 15 euros, yet I would NOT recommend it for its normal full price and this is why, on to the review.

I wont be giving numerical scores, seeing as I believe everyone enjoys different things to different extents, so Ill just say what I personally enjoyed...and then what I didnt enjoy.

The armies and factions are nice to look at in my opinion, Ive always very much enjoyed trying out all the different armies, fiddling and tinkering around with set ups etc. Heroes of Might and Magic passes in that regard for me, They are all interesting to play and coordinate I also very much enjoy the fact that you can 'pick' your champion creatures to match the current playstyle you are going for. This in itself gives the game a bit more diversity and things to keep an eye out for when playing against the ai or other players.

The collecting of reagents is also fun again, they thankfully went back to having numerous special reageants so you have to search high and low to maximize your army as soon as possible, or you just buy them on the market, which will of course be far more expensive and upkeep crippling (atleast at the start).

The graphics are easy on the eyes, they arent over the top realistic looking, but also dont look completely outdated. All in all very pretty.

The sound is also nice, though a few of the tracklists are a bit too much for me personally speaking, in regard to this I mean for instance the Haven and Sylvan themes.

The battles have somewhat been dumbed down in comparison to part 6, but this in my opinion is a good thing. One of the most important and enjoyable parts for me from the HoMM games was always getting my army to a degree where Id feel like a god waltzing through. It does feel like it connects when your mobs hit, and you do get that 'uber' feeling once you have a stack of 500 or so mobs and just..obliterate stuff with it, all in all that is of course a good thing haha :)

Now sadly, to the negative aspects...

The game is still riddled with bugs even after such a long time after release, random CTD's. my screen going black and it freezing my PC (my rig is completely capable of running games such as Fallout 4..Witcher 3...and Doom with the graphics all the way up..it should not be freezing by something like this) the game stuttering sometimes, etc etc, just to name a few.

The random map generator is sadly also quite a bit buggy, I had a bot stuck in the underworld behind a really wide gap seeing as the game did not make a portal or exit of any kind.

The AI is also a bit..awkward at times, sometimes waiting for you to come and gank it..and sometimes going complete Civi 5 Ghandi mode, Im quite sure that the game was not designed to have such large gaps of AI happening.

Uplay (I dont think I really have to add anything to that haha)

There are a few factions not ingame currently. Im going to go under the assumption they are going to be behind a paywall in form of dlc some day..which I find a real shame. To be precise I mean the Fortress and Inferno. The heroes designed have been on the main site, and you can even still have heroes from either of the two as portraits on the site itself....

Various objects you should be able to interact with just.dont sometimes, this can get very infuriating after a while.



All in all, to be fair, I am enjoying myself with the game, so I am glad I bought it. The negative aspects and relative slow time they need to fix ingame things though sadly completely brings it down.

At the current time for 15 euros, its quite ok and worth it in my opinion but it would not be worth the original price of 30 euros, atleast not until all the bugs are ironed out.

This is really one of those games where I wish there would be a neutral rating because I do believe many would choose that over the positive or negative. More importantly, do give the game a chance instead of seeing all the negative reviews, as I wrote above, Im quite sure that a far bit of those are along the lines of 'This isnt HoMM 3 therefore it must be crap'



Final Verdict: Worth the buy if you can get it cheaper and can view the game as a seperate game from HoMM 3.


Sidenote: If youve bought the game, and Uplay asks you for your cd key, (which you actually never recieved) just exit uplay/the game and steam, then start it all up again and the game should run fine.

I've played every HOMM since HOMM2 and love the series. Sadly the last 2 releases by Ubisoft have me thinking I may be done with the series. I definitely won't be playing any Ubisoft games until I see good reviews first.

The game clearly needed more time and/or a better QA team.

There are a lot of bugs which would have been obvious with ANY level of QA. Just a few examples:
- Sometimes buildings which should be clickable just don't respond to your hero. For example a weekly "tax collector" says not visited but cannot be clicked. A fort you just captured sometimes cannot be rebuilt for you. In both cases, restarting the game from an autosave makes the buliding clickable on the same day.
- Sometimes your towns will flash that you have a caravan to unload for the rest of the game long after the caravan area was emptied.
- The game froze on me several times. Most were just at the end of a level. Thank God for autosave.
- After freezing, the game continually told me it couldn't authenticate my UPlay account and wouldn't start. The screen that said that had a support link which points to an dead webpage. Double-DRM is no fun, especially when it's not rock-solid. Why do you need to check DRM on a Steam version of a game anyway, especially when I'm going to play in 1-player mode?
- A few times I updgraded a stack where I only had enough money to upgrade some of the stack. Rather than just refuse to upgrade or just upgrade what I could afford, it upgraded what I could afford and the rest of the stack disappeared. I tried to upgrade 20 Cavalier and it turned into 2 Cuirassier and 18 poofed!

There are also a lot of issues which aren't bugs, but just show they didn't spend enough time with the game:
- The council room has "Cutscenes" which are really just camera pans of frozen 3d characters with voiceovers. That might have been expected in the early 90s, but doesn't make sense today. Hand-drawn stills with audio would be better than creepy frozen talking 3d models.
- Somehow the UI was better in HOMM 3 and 4 than 7. The UI should improve over previous games, not make things worse. The buttons to move all armies disappears when it can't move every stack, rather than just being smart enough to combine as many stacks as it can and leave the rest behind. It takes more clicks than it did 20 years ago to go from town to town and build and purchase units. The necromancy screen doesn't even have combine stack buttons. You have to drag your skeletons manually every time.
- The game needed a lot more performance tuning. My laptop can play Overwatch at moderate graphics settings without much problem, but it chugs on this game even after swapping to a low resolution and changing all graphics settings to lowest. The problem gets worse the longer the game is open, suggesting leaks. Even at high settings the graphics aren't good enough to explain why this game would push a computer so hard. Ironically HOMM has always been more of an "icon" based game and I would have been happier with worse graphics or psuedo-3d view like the older games than poor 3d graphics if it meant the game ran better.
- Some of the levels needed balancing. In past HOMM's, sometimes you would get slaughtered if you took too long to expand on a level or you pushed into the more difficult areas of a map too early. On several maps in this game I would get slaughtered even just a few weeks in the game, still in the general starting area of the map. Sometimes very high level enemy heroes with huge armies would come out of the fog and kill my low level hero fairly close to my first town. Because you could not have seen them coming, it seemed just luck that you and they didn't move close enough to each other than the other hero would "aggro" from the fog.
- Maybe it's just a limitation of using Ubisoft's DRM in conjunction with Steam's system, but the game runs a "first time installer" every time I run the game.

Personally I'd like to see Ubisoft give someone else a shot at this series. They've really tarnished it with the last 2 games. If they're going to keep it, I hope they don't make another one unless they're going to spend the time to make a game that runs well, doesn't have many bugs, and doesn't feel like I'm fighting the UI.

I've played the HOMM series (HOMM 4 lyfe, screw this Might & Magic Heroes BS) since the very beginning and while this isn't the worst game in the franchise (HOMM I sets a pretty low bar but that game at least has the excuse of being really old and dated), it's certainly one of the worst. This is mostly due to the fact that it is so buggy and a lot of those bugs are yet to be fixed.

The cutscene graphics are horribly conceptualized and the result is this dated look that does not offer any of the flavor they obviously were trying to achieve. The faction leaders don't even move in the meeting room, and yes I know recent great games have used still images with voiceovers but that is intended to portray an authentic storybook perspective. This is just lazy.

The story itself is derivative as all hell and Ubisoft ripped off both the Warcraft universe and George R.R. Martin's universe from "A Song of Ice and Fire". An orc named Thrall, carbon copies of Tywin Lannister and a sylvan character that is so blatantly a redux of Daenerys. These characters are in the MAIN STORY of the game, not customized maps. That is inexcusable and in very poor taste. The game play itself is nothing special either, basically everything previous games have offered but with nothing new. This was Ubisoft's sorry attempt at a cash cow. Sure we get some new creatures but this game is a pile of garbage.
