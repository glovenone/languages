I might have liked Gridberd more if I had done like other reviewers and never played it.

With sharp graphics and sound, Gridberd builds a thick, dark atmosphere. The story is ok, and the developers obviously cared and worked hard, but bugs and design choices blow it. It's like someone set me a beautiful dinner table - with candles and music - and then served me poop.

Whether you're accidentally walking on water, or crouching to overcome an invisible wall, Gridberd throws so many irritating goofs at you that I can't list them all, so I'll just illustrate with *JOURNAL PAGES*. If you see a journal page in the environment, a visible key-prompt appears ( [F] ) which lets you pick it up and read it. But often, standing in front of the page and looking at it doesn't elicit a prompt. I repeatedly had to scurry about the floor randomly, waggling my head, until a visible key prompt appeared - often at the corner of the screen. But occasionally, it was wasted time because the page turned out to be non-interactive. And once (in the dining room) I was able to read a page by pressing the interact key after completely failing to find any visible prompt.

Gridberd is a few months and some tough feedback away from being a solid, very scary game. But once you serve me poop, I'll be eating out, thank you :)
