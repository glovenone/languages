About This Game

It starts on the 4th day. Ever since you arrived on this planet, you've felt a dark influence. You've lost all memories of your previous self. Who you've become is an even bigger mystery. The darkness hides the truth, and maybe it is in the darkness the truth should remain hidden. Something sinister has happened, but you don't know exactly what took place. These dark silent ones, why do they want to kill you? When death arrives, why does it start all over again... You know you have to escape this planet, and this endless time loop. Yet there is something itching your mind, something tells you you'll have to do more than just escape...

Features

-Very subtle dark story told by collecting memory fragments

-Enemies that increase in numbers as time goes by (spend too long and it will become very challenging)

-Gather enough meteorite and fuel to power your spaceship to escape

-Robots allies that will help in collecting resources, or resources you've tagged

-This planet was named after the blind idiot god Azathoth. When traversing outside the base the darkness can be blinding, it is essential to use your flashlight and radar to help find memory fragments and meteorite. This planet was also named after Azathoth for many other reasons...

-You can get sustenance in your base, but getting food requires going out and scavenging buildings

-To escape requires balancing your body's needs with keeping the base running along with collecting fuel and meteorite for your spaceship. How you balance these task's will determine how quickly you can escape the planet. Not paying enough attention to your sustenance level, and being too far from base will lead to death

-Saving is an important resource. It requires converted matter, in which you can enter an altered reality with the same time, and resources accumulated along with respawning everything. You can also use it to restore your health, but be wary it will reset bullet counts and weapons. When you choose to save will become strategic

-The silent ones blend in with complete darkness, you'll have to rely on the reflection of their skin to shoot them correctly. Be wary take too long to aim, and they'll teleport

--Simple Multiplayer

-Invade other worlds with the ability of summoning a huge silent one(Time it right and give other players a great jump scare)

-Play from the perspective of the enemy, and see how they really perceive you

Nightmare on Azathoth is an H.P. Lovecraft inspired survival horror game where you play as a lone astronaut who is stranded on a foreign planet. The game was released a day before Halloween in 2015 by publisher Divergent Game Studio, who are also responsible for releasing the poorly received survival title, Downpour. Has this game managed to redeem the studio, or is it another embarrassment to add to their release list?

This game was made in Unity... Personal Edition, que the "dun dun DUN" music, so right from the start it's understandable to be wary. Once you actually start playing, after you get past some bad voice acting, you will quickly realize that this title gives you zero sense of direction; you have to figure out all of the controls, and what you're supposed to do, completely on your own. Now the absence of hand holding isn't always a bad thing, but this title is one that requires some sort of explanation prior to adventuring off into the dark unknown. Especially with the amount of factors that you have to take in, such as "matter", "memory", and "sustenance".

From what I gathered during my whopping 10 minutes of play time with Nightmare on Azathoth, you run around collecting memory fragments and matter, and you exchange the matter for sustenance. While you're running around gathering matter, you'll need to look out for fuel depots to keep your base functioning. As you're running around collecting these resources, you will be assaulted by zombie-like men and black, oozy alien creatures. You start off with a rifle and 8 bullets in total, with no idea of how to get more ammunition; and trust me, the ammo provided does not go far.

It'd probably go farther if the hit detection in this title wasn't the worst that I've ever seen in any game... ever. You can be standing right in front of an enemy and shoot them in the face, and the bullet will miss. Everything that I encountered was a one-hit kill, so this shouldn'tve happened. The oozy alien enemies are as fast as you are, and they will chase you until your unavoidable death. They even go out of their way to teleport so they appear in front of you from time to time if you run away long enough. This makes it absolutely impossible to attempt to find more ammunition, or heal properly (however one might accomplish that task), if you get overwhelmed by enemies at the beginning of the game.

The atmosphere is bleak, desolate, and creepy, but it doesn't make up for everything that is wrong with this game. Why anyone would play Nightmare on Azathoth beyond 10-20 minutes is beyond me, and why anyone would pay $6.49 CAD for it is even more of a mind-blower. I was... fortunate (?) enough to get this game in an Indie Gala bundle, so my loss is a very cheap one. On top of everything else, the optimization is also garbage. Even though the developer, Modesto Rabena, promised "continuous support" for this game, there have been no updates since February 9th, 2016. Beware.

Rating: 0.5/5.0 - Abysmal, avoid it like the plague.

Cool Sci-Fi/Horror Indie Project

An interesting and intriguing concept. It definitely has potential as full Horror/Survival game with more content and polish if the publisher and devs ever wanted to expand this project. (This is a kind of short, bitesized alpha-ish game)

I haven't completed this game yet but got to the very end and then died what seemed to be a random physics glitch or invisible insta-death area in my base before I could escape!

I died when walking next the maintenance robot that moves back and forth between the volatile, burning dark fuel (that will kill you if you move into it) and the maintenace-pad-type-thingy that is normally safe to traverse.

But apparently, it is safe only for most of the game it is NO LONGER a safe area at the end, to my dismay... Perhaps I did not notice, but I suppose that area has dangerous, buring dark fuel too once you're at the end of the game.

All I know is I died instanly from mostly full health when I walked across it, and that only happens when you walk into the blazing dark fuel that is in the corner of the base. I was kind of upset after I made it all the way to the end of the game and almost escaped > : (

I do not remember seeing anything dangerous on the pad. Perhaps my maintenace robot crushed me when I moved across it and the big robot stepped onto it. Or it dumped burning dark fuel on the pad as a walked on it. I have tried to recreate it at the begining of a new game to understand why I died. But, I can stand on the pad with the robot moving around me and it does not kill me...??

Other than this fairly off-putting moment, this game has a dark, spooky sci-fi atmosphere and creepy, lovecraftian monster vibe which I think is a pretty cool blend of genres.

It is not exactly unique, as there is a similar Sci-Fi/Lovecraft Horror style game on Steam called Noct. But that is a top-down shooter while this is a third-person shooter.

The TPS and survival mechanics of this game are pretty run of the mill, but the games winning assets are the atmosphere and mysterious story. Again, considering this is sort of an alpha and looks to be a mostly independent concept project, it would be unfair to knock it for gameplay mechanics/polish.

Still, I can't exactly recommend this purchase, currently at $8, for someone who is not a fan of these genre's considering said amount of content and polish.

But, if you like to support independent projects like this and do like Horror and/or Sci-Fi, you will get a few hours of intriguing gameplay from Nightmare on Azathoth, once you get past the learning curve as this game has no real tutorial.

You will have to learn from exploration and moslty figure things out on your own with a handful of help messages in the menu and the base.

I'm really enjoying this game. Haven't escaped yet, but enjoying trying/failing/re-trying.

Pros
- Atmosphere: very dark, dark, and twilight -- love the combination of Cthulhu and Plato (made me actually go read up a bit on some of Plato's works)
- Monster design and avoidance/teleport mechanic
- Sound effects: creepy, appropriate for the Lovecraft/Cthulhu mythos that it's partly based on
- Cool graphics, not for being high end but for being appropriate to the story and creepy. Reminiscent of some of the original black and white Twilight Zone episodes (the ones on alien planets or in space)

Non-spoiler tips
- Read the hints at the beginning of the game on the giant white boards (look all the way around before selecting New/Load)
- Remember that you can save (don't have to just let things get harder with each fail)
- The game is meant to be vague and not have detailed instructions. That's part of the fun
- If you're having problems hitting the silent ones, try running backwards and waiting for the silent ones to move towards the center of your view, then quickly move to the side and shoot

I like Nightmare on Azathoth. It's an interesting survival game on a planet of darkness. Even daylight doesn't reach the ground, as the creatures you face push away all light on the surface. The enemies are troublesome without being ridiculous (except for the flying head) and are original in their simplicity (except for the flying head). I like the resource management, I like the giant floating monster that summons enemies to attack you when it gets close enough, I like the power system.

The only thing I don't like is the flying head monsters. It's not because they are annoying, it's not because they steadily glitch out worse and worse, it's entirely because of the fact that the flying head monster's model comes from the updated version of the game "Eyes - The Horror Game". This was a theft, as Eyes was made by a completely different person.

But, let's try and focus on being optimistic. Maybe it wasn't theft after all. Maybe Modesto Rabena got permission and used the model. No idea. What I do know is that it means that they decided not to make something original for the game. Even a different looking model would've sufficed.

In the end, I do recommand getting Nightmare on Azathoth. It's difficult in the beginning because of the lack of a tutorial but, you learn quickly by doing things. Once you learn the game becomes significantly easier but, it will still take you time to complete, and the longer it takes the harder it will get as The Silent Ones will become more numerous.
