About This Game

Moto Racer Collection features the original Moto Racer, Moto Racer 2, Moto Racer 3 Gold Edition + Moto Racer 15h Anniversary.

MOTO RACER / MOTO RACER 2: Retrace the series roots and tackle legendary races such as the famous Great Wall of China. Put your skills and reflexes to the test in Arcade and Simulation modes.

MOTO RACER 3: Feel the thrill of speeds 300 km/h and over on some of the world's greatest racetracks including Suzuka and the Sachsenring. Hop on a trial bike and negotiate the trickiest of obstacles. And take a nerveshredding high-speed trip through the streets of Paris.

MOTO RACER 15th ANNIVERSARY: Race against fierce competitors in GP Mode. Challenge the best bikers in spectacular Supercross races Perform the best aerial tricks in Freestyle Mode. Break the records in Time Trial Mode.

Key Features:
EVERY MOTORCYCLE CATEGORY UNDER THE SUN, BROUGHT TOGETHER IN ONE GAME

MOTO GP:
  • Hit speeds of over 300 km/h as you race flat out against the toughest of rivals.
SUPERCROSS:
  • Go head-to-head with your rivals in all-terrain races and tease them with your fanciest tricks.
FREESTYLE:
  • Pull off amazing Superman seat grabs, Indy backflips and can-cans as you fly through the air.
GAME PACKAGE INCLUDES:
  • Moto Racer, Moto Racer 2, Moto Racer 3 Gold Edition and Moto Racer 15th Anniversary

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows® XP/Vista/7/8
    • Processor: Intel 1.6 GHz (or equivalent)
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Geforce 3 128 MB
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 2 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX-compatible sound card
    • Additional Notes: Minimum screen resolution: 1024 x 768; software installations required (included with the game), DirectX 9, Microsoft .NET 3.5 Framework, Visual C++ Redistributable 2005, Visual C++ Redistributable 2008, Visual C++ Redistributable 2010

I'm not too sure what people were expecting with this collection, but from the reviews I hvae read, you people have been far too harsh for what it is and when it was made.

Moto Racer 1 is a PS1 game which, I believe, was ported to the PC (or released at the same time as the PS1 version). The graphics are old and it's going to have the odd glitch and incompatibility now and then. Anyone without common sense should know that your 'Octa core processor with SLI'd GTX 770s' would have compatibility issues with a game from the 90s.

Additionally, you cannot expect a game that was made for the PS1 to be fancy. If a HD remake was produced, the original old-school gamers who played this game probably wouldn't have liked it. Why expect a game or a collection that's released onto Steam to be new and sparkly? Developers ARE allowed to release old games onto Steam.

So overall, I recommend this merely because I congratulate the developers for releasing this great PS1 game onto Steam. I've played all of the games in this collection enough in my childhood and away from Steam to know this deserves a recommendation even if Steam says I've only played for less than an hour.

Hey fellow steam users!
I bought this on 11/13/2014 and I have tested both Moto Racer 1, 2, 3, and 4 are all playable. Here's my review for each one:

1. Moto racer, the bonafide classic. not many tracks, but the game is wonderful, old-school, and arcadey. A definate must-have! 4:3 only, use the "black bars" mode.
2. Just like its predecessor, this game is wonderful and fast and arcadey. Much more tracks this time around, but the game is a tad more realistic and not quite as arcadey as MR1. If you add custom widescreen resolutions lower than 1024x768, usually they appear in-game.
3. Moto Racer 3 is a departure from the second one as there is a heavier focus on "arcade realism". The COD of the Moto Racer series, but this game is very enjoyable still and as always, has a wonderful sense of speed. 4:3 only.
4. The classic from ios, this game was created with auto-acceleration in mind. Play this with a controller and don't be as heavy on the throttle! The shoulder buttons are best for acceleration as button A is hard-coded to be Boost. 4x3 only, just like the older versions. This game is fairly faithful to its predecessors and is a good time waster, but not quite as good as the originals.

Overall, i'd say if you are a fan of the series, by all means get this title. It's cheaper than buying each one on GOG, and it's great fun for all ages!
The game had extreme bugs on release, but on my windows 8.1, amd fx 8350, and nvidia gtx 660, these games run fine and have few problems.

This is a collection, so be aware that the original games have not been improved upon.
The store page is not ambiguous about that, but I'd rather reiterate because you might have different expectations in this age of HD remakes.
Having loved the original Moto Racer back in the day, a straight port was fine by me, except...

Moto Racer 1 won't launch on my machine -- launcher fails to set colour depth. There might be a way around it, like setting my display to 16-bit manually, but I can't be arsed right now. I might put effort into finding a workaround later though, because I recall this one being my favourite.

Moto Racer 2 does launch, but there's no sound during races, no music or SFX. Plays well, but feels weird because of that.

Moto Racer 3 works (huzzah!). Don't think I played this one back in the day (or not much); it seems less arcadey and fun than the previous two instalments, but that's just my impression after a single race.

Moto Racer 15th anniversary is the most recent entry (you can tell it's modern because you accelerate with the right trigger, not the A button!) and it's utter garbage. Not only does it manage to be uglier than the other, way older entries, but more shamefully it plays poorly. Not the game I cared about anyway, but dang, morbid curiosity is the only reason to bother trying it at all -- and even then I couldn't finish a single lap in a cross race. The way it handles just feels entirely wrong, like someone's first Unity game on XBLIG.

So all in all, if I find a way to make sound work in 2 and for 1 to launch at all, maybe I'll spend some time with this, reminiscing. But I cannot recommend it in good faith.

(Oh, and I spent more than 5 minutes with this -- but Steam only counts time spent in the launcher apparently)
