About This Game

The Prism is a 2D puzzle platformer about two pixels, 02 and 03 lost inside a vast prison. Unlike a normal prison, this one is alive. It is called the Prism. Its eyes stare from a distance and watch its prisoners carefully, analyzing their actions and breathing down their necks. You will face many challenges but you don't have to do it alone. You can play by yourself or locally with a friend.

The game holds many challenges and puzzles. Different elements and environments are added to create more unique situations using the abilities you have been given. As well as that, you will need to use each other as a means of getting past puzzles, opening doors and continuing your journey. The puzzles are challenging, the prism is dangerous and the world is cruel. It is possible to escape, but at what risk?

This puzzle platformer puts the player in control of a pair of runaway pixels. Technically they look like at least 256 pixels cubes. Each controlled with a different position on the keyboard to allow both to move independently.

https://youtu.be/tiLZqJN1C-w

The visual style of this game is simple but the high contrast makes it enjoyable. The lack of visual cues for dangers will cause some frustration.

However, the greatest frustration factor is the controls and surface detection. This finicky combination can result in missed input resulting in pixel death.

Occasionally the game will ask the player to hold keys down to manipulate the pixels to progress through levels. This is an attempt of physicality to add weight to the actions of the pixels, that they can't be ignored. Regrettably, the end result is more tedium than empathy.

THE PRISM REVIEW

Looking at the current reviews for this game leaves me more puzzled as when I played the game. Why does this game have mixed reviews? (at the time I wrote this)

This review is written with currently only 1.5 hours put into the game. I have quite liked so far of what I have played and figured I would give it a quick review.
So far what I have played makes me realize how under-rated this game really is. This is a one of a kind puzzle game that I have not seen anything like before.
I'm usually a total newbie when it comes to puzzle games but The Prism was simple enough at the start for me to pick up as well as constantly introducing new level elements as the game progressed.

Gameplay

The main gameplay of The Prism is you controlling two cubes. As long as one of those two cubes reaches the exit then you progress.
The creator claims its similar to the game 'Thomas was Alone' and I have to agree with them. It is quite similar to that game.
There are some levels that feature new random mechanics that come into play for only that level. This keeps the game interesting and I honestly like that a whole lot, even though you may never see those mechanics again.

There are some issues with the collision detection however as sometimes, '02' and '03' can get stuck in each other if you make them jump into each other.
This is only a minor issue however, it doesn't take away much from the game.
Also the resolution seems to be all over the place between levels, The game auto stretches to your native resolution but its obvious when you change levels.

The puzzles in the game are very well crafted. None of the puzzles seem rushed and they all feel very different to each other.
The level design feels like it always features one new "thing" that you get to play with or over come.
Be it making a bridge out of lying cubes or flying through valleys in a 'Helicopter'-esque mini-game. 'The Prism' always feels fresh.

Story

There is very little story to The Prism, you are just spawned into the game and left to figure it out.
There are some hints at some story but nothing is given to you at the start to give you a hint to the story.
You just play as cube '02' and '03' and help them progress.

Even if there is little to no story, I cant help but feel for the little guys.
I WANT them to get through the levels and progress forward. I cant even explain why because they are just little cubes.
Again, this is similar to 'Thomas was Alone'.

Audio

The soundtrack to the prism is absolutely stunning. If anyone knows me well, they will know that I ♥♥♥♥ing love music and a games soundtrack is something I almost certainly hunt down.
The Prism's soundtrack was done by 'MISTER STRANGE' and it is very atmospheric. It leaves me simply enchanted because of just how calm it is.
The music fits in almost perfectly with the game, it matches the visuals perfectly as the locations you take place in, you could imagine them actually sounding like the soundtrack.
This is a damn good sign of a damn good soundtrack.

The soundtrack can be found on Soundcloud here! , You should totally check it out.

Where I do think the soundtrack is perfect. What is there is very limited. There is only 6 songs and they repeat maybe too often.
If there was a couple more songs that would change every now and again, to remove the repetition, then it would be a 10/10 perfect.

Visuals

Okay this game looks STUNNING. The game feels like the developer crafted each and every frame with extreme precision as every little detail looks gorgeous.
Each and every environment in this game bursts with vibrant colors, if it was a steak, it would be extremely juicy.
The colors in the game just look absolutely stunning, Every time you transition into a new environment, I always take a step back to just look at how lovely the game looks.
Because Jesus, this game provides many 'Wallpaper' Moments where i just want to stop playing and take photo's.

The only thing on the Visuals that I could criticize would be that some of the backdrops look a tad bit blurry, they look really odd compared to the rest of the game.

Final Overview

So in overall, The Prism is a puzzle platformer that I feel is incredibly underrated on steam. Its another case of a game being misrepresented by its reviews.
Where I cant say that the other reviews are wrong, because that is their own opinion, but my opinion on the game is that it is pretty god damn good.
The visuals and the audio fit together perfectly, like a match made in heaven. The gameplay and the level design always feel fresh and new.
Every chapter brings something new into the mix and the game never feels like its stale at all. Minus a couple of bugs and graphical errors.
The games gets the following scores from me.

Gameplay - 7.5
Story - 5
Audio - 9.5
Visual - 9.5

OVERALL SCORE - 8.5
