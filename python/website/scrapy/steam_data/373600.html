About This Game

The world is being taken over by gene-combining beings from beyond the stars!
Hideous new creatures are popping up everywhere, and it's up to the Solus Operatives to grab their unique weapons and split some disgusting alien skulls!

High-resolution and carefully hand-drawn art style breathes life and originality into this bizarre game world.
Dynamically branching levels are tied together by an intriguing story with multiple endings, giving the player tons of replay-ability and chances to master each operative’s Weapon of Choice.

Features:
  • CHOICE is your Weapon! Play through non-linear levels and make dynamic in-level branch decisions!
  • Each playable character has their own special weapon of choice with primary and alternative fire modes! Jet-engine guns, plasma-saws, limpet grenades and more!
  • Rescue unique playable characters and add to total number of playable lives!
  • Experience Death Brushing! Unlike traditional one-hit kill games, Weapon of Choice gives you the opportunity to get out of a tight spot. Death Brushing automatically kicks in when danger is near.
  • Reap revenge for your downed Operatives with the Vengeance Missile!
  • Scale walls and attack from anywhere with the automated Spiderpack!
  • Replay to experience different storylines with completely distinct endings!
  • Rock out to original music with a heavy metal guitar soundtrack!
  • New for the Steam version, widescreen 16:9 format!
  • All new Steam Achievements--try finding the MBG pies in each level for a new adventure!

Enough gameplay to rot your teeth!

I'm gonna keep my review short, sweet, and simple.

Weapon of Choice falls on the HIGH end of mediocre. I'd go as far as calling it 'good enough'.

The gameplay is kind of generic. Very simple.

The story is.... Kinda blah. Nothing special.

The art style is vibrant and very alien/mutant. Interesting monsters and bosses. Big plus there.

It's got interesting gameplay mechanics, like the 'rescue system' where you find an ally and carry them to safety. And, the spider-pack was kinda fun. No more obscure firing angles!

The near miss 'death brushing' system was definitely interesting. I don't recall anything else like it.

Unusual characters with vastly differening weapon and play styles. Kinda nice. Just needs MORE characters!

The game is short. Crazy short. Replayability and branching paths only go so far.

But my favorite feature of the game?

The BRUTAL FREAKING METAL SOUNDTRACK!!!!!!!

A lot of love went into the soundtrack and I was more than happy to slap down the 2 extra bucks to get it. It is good, classic, hard and fast metal. The way metal should be. Especially action-game music. Really driving and invigorating.

The music saved this game, by me.

7/10~ Will play again if more content/sequel comes out.

Seriously. The music is awesome. Buy the soundtrack too.

Posted: 9 November, 2015
Weapon of choice is a very original and beautiful 2D shooter. Though I'm not a fan of most shooters where you can die in one hit, as it can oftentimes feel 'unfair', this game has a system I quite like called death-touching. Basically, when you're about to be killed by something time will slow down to allow you to focus on survival until you're escaped the threat. Despite this though, the game can be quite hard, especially on the higher difficulty settings.

The lives system is quite original as well. You have several operatives to choose from when you start a mission, each with their own unique primary weapon and double-jump. When an operative is shot or otherwise maimed, another is deployed, and you can have the new operative rescue the old one by carrying them on their back. You can only carry one though, so if you die multiple times in a level you'll have to choose which one you want to save in order to use them again later.

Although short, this game offers branching paths that lead to 4 endings (one secret), and each level is packed with some of the most original and gross enemy designs I've ever seen. If you're a fan of old-school shooters, I recommend this game heartily.

Aliens from deep space are splicing their genes with humans and creating mutants beyond imagination. Surprise! Surprise! Humanity is doomed unless the Solus Operatives can do something about it. With your help, Earth could be saved for a day or two. You know, at least until the next alien invasion. Combining striking visuals, interesting weapons, and run & gun shootemup action, Weapon of Choice is a pretty memorable experience.

There is a lot that's unique about this game. There are 7 stages, but your average play-through takes place over 2 or 3 of them. Admittedly, this decision is pretty strange. Usually action games - even if they have branching paths - consist of 5 or more stages. I suppose it works due to the variety of environments and enemies. Each branch changes things up, and the operatives you control can have different effects on the mission. The replay-value is further improved by a selection of difficulties. Since the good guys die in a single hit, the harder settings are a bit more creative in their implementation. You won't see boring changes like "good guys take more damage". The harder settings have enemies exhibit different behaviors, fire more projectiles, drop menacing saw-blades, and so on.

Choosing the Operative that fits your play-style is something to consider. Each of them have their own weapons and special maneuvers. Everyone also carries a back-up gun. Unlike the main weapon, this machine-gun has an attachment that allows the player to move it around. So if you need to shoot an enemy that's around a corner, behind a post, or in some other hard-to-reach area, you can count on this gun. Experimentation is just one of the many keys to success. Xerxes carries a flame-thrower. When using the ALT-FIRE (hold the right-trigger) and jumping, Xerxes will soar through the air, like he just turned on his afterburner.

Overall though, I'll say that I didn't have too much difficulty with this game. I started with the easiest setting, played through all of the stages, then immediately jumped to the hardest. I've lost a few operatives on each path, but I never got a game over. Even without "death-brushing", you'll survive as long as you take it slow and destroy everything. I think going for "no-miss" campaigns could be a worthy challenge though. Speedrunning the game would be interesting as well, especially with the unique movement abilities everyone has.

Weapon of Choice is a fascinating shootemup. Along with the vivid graphics, there are some cool weapons to play around with, and the bosses are impressively designed. It is very short though, and is oddly more expensive than Mommy's Best Games other recent releases. If you haven't already purchased one or two of the other games, I advise that you should just grab the bundle. For a few dollars more you get four good games. It's a reasonable value.

Disclosure: I helped with pre-release testing for this game, and received a key from the developer for that purpose.

If you've ever played Mommy's Best Games' best-known title, Serious Sam: Double D, you might be interested in this, their very first release back on XBox Live Indie Games, which turned out to be something of a prototype for Double D. Like its descendent Weapon of Choice is a Contra-esque side-scrolling "run-n-gun", but best played (assuming you're using a controller) with a "twin stick" scheme, with the left analog used for movement, the right for aiming and shooting, and the shoulder/trigger buttons for other functions. Moreover, WoC helps to showcase what would become the developer's instantly-recognizable brand of creatively gaudy visual flair and dark but good-natured humor which would prove a perfect match for Sam.

As the title suggests, the main draw here is the playable selection of hapless commandos and their ludicrous individualized weaponry, from jet engine flamethrowers to lightsaber chainsaws - while some armaments are more useful in certain situations than others, everybody can switch to a more-or-less "normal" machine gun if they need something more general-use. You only have a couple of soldiers to choose from at first, but MIAs are waiting to be rescued in several of the game's levels, and your handy standard-issue "spiderpack" allows you to climb almost any wall and ceiling in search of them (and the occasional delicious pie). Each member of the crew has a different double-jump ability as well, so you'll want to find and try them all.

That being said, doing so isn't likely to take you too long, since the game encompasses fewer than ten levels total, and due to its branching path structure you'll only see three of them per playthrough. On the bright side, there are a wide selection of difficulty levels to unlock if you want to (re)test your mettle; when you're first getting started things are relatively breezy thanks to the game's "death brush" mechanic (if something's about to hit you time slows down, usually allowing you to weasel your way out of danger), but go up high enough and not only do enemies and obstacles become more difficult but "death brushing" is out the window, so you'll be burning through your one-hit-and-they're-dead ranks quickly even with the ability to pick up and rescue a downed character once the next one shows up (in an exploding rocket, of course).

While we're on the topic, I'd be remiss not to devote a few words to the game's unmistakable sense of style. Sure, you've basically got another "shoot all the meanie-face aliens" situation on your hands, but when said aliens (particularly the bosses) are as freakish, silly, and invariably bursting with rainbow goo as they are here you'll still enjoy filling them with hot lead/plasma/spinny blades/etc. Oh, and did I mention you're taking orders from a commander who bears a striking resemblance to a noted actor, disease mispronouncer and oatmeal spokesperson? Couple that with entertainingly droll (and skippable) bits of text, a cheese-rock soundtrack, bold colors and smooth animations and you've got a game that's fun to watch as well as play, though a handful of improvements (faster walking speed chief among them) also make for a better behind-the-controller experience than before.

As such, it's not a big name like Serious Sam, and isn't as expansive or ambitious as Double D turned out to be, but one can't really blame the game nor its circumstances for that; if you enjoyed that game, or were at least somehow curious about it, I recommend giving this one a try as well. It's a bit more expensive then the other XBLIG-to-Steam ports Mommy's Best Games has engineered, so if WoC is on your wishlist you might want to go ahead and grab the full package, which gets you three additional games for a measly buck apiece. If you do, any time you ever need a few minutes to let off some stress and cause some nutty mayhem without being led away in a straitjacket, you're all set to go.
