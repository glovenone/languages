About This Game

An artistic narrative designed to evoke a surreal feeling in the player on a psychological level. The work is not structured like a typical game, one could even describe it as an anti-game in that there are no "Game Overs." Don't let that fool you though, it's a platformer-puzzle-advanture with a handful of different endings!

Remnants of a Beautiful Day

DISCLAIMER: I received this product for no cost for the purpose of reviewing.

The first thing you notice about the game is the art-style, then after that, it's the fact that the game seems like a "walking simulator" or something similar. The title actually is not a "walking simulator", and instead is a very interesting exploration title, that does have core mechanics, as well as alternate endings.

Like mentioned above, Remnants of a Beautiful day is a game about exploration and moving your character through obstacles via jumping and crouching. You could call it a mix between parkour and puzzle-platforming.

--------

Graphics: 7 / 10

Although art style is very subjective, I did enjoy this title's visuals, and thought the style was very nice. Combined with the music, the game was very atmospheric wherever you were. The bright and vibrant colors were very pleasing to my eyes. I felt that the character movement wasn't as smooth as the animation of the plants and trees, but it wasn't a problem. The game should have no problem running on any graphics card or PC. I'm running it on a mid-tier build with no problems.

--------

Sound: 7 / 10

The peaceful and calming music mixed with the soothing narrative of Dr. Wolf also adds the atmospheric pleasure of the game. The sound effects are decent, and none of the sound assets ever feel repetitive or forced. There are different melodies playing at certain parts of the game though they all fit their respective area of the game.

--------

Mechanics: 6 / 10

The mechanics are very simple and solid. the player can crouch and jump. These two abilities are what make the title a game. As you progress through the game, there will be different platforming challenges as well as progressively difficult puzzle elements.

--------

Final Personal Remarks:

I really liked the visuals and soundtrack of this title, and although it's not a genre I enjoy too much, I can appreciate a title like this and understand how people can enjoy it. I know this review was very short and straightforward, though it does reflect how solid and simple the game is. Which isn't a bad thing. You can play a game like this to be taken in by the atmosphere of it, and use it to relax.

Thanks to the Devs for giving me the opportunity to review the game, as well as anyone who took this review into consideration, I hope it helped.

This game reminds me of the half-baked freeware games that I had way more patience for growing up. Except unfortunately I paid money for this one. Imagine Myst, but change the colors randomly, repeat the same scenery over and over, dumb down the controls and graphics, then remove any aspects of gameplay or excitement. This game is tedious, confusing, jittery, simplistic, nausiating, and just not well made. I stopped playing when I got hopelessly lost on a 'level' with giant grabby disgusting hands floating everywhere. I'm fan of surealism and dreamy games, but the game was not thought through. 'Anti-game' is a good description. I'm actually pro game, so maybe that's why I didn't enjoy this.

What I didn't like:
- Jumping and moving is rudamentary, sticky and disorienting. It takes a lot of patience and effort to get anywhere.
- Zero help in game to know what to do. I wasn't even sure there was any way to progress for a while. Then when I did advance, I thought it crashed the game for a second, because you get a blue screen.
- The aesthetic choices are novel, but stomach-turning when you have to look at it for as long as you do while you try to figure out what you're supposed to be doing. The patterns and colors look nice at a distance, but when you have to get super close to objects to jump on them, they look shoddy.

The gfx and music quickly builds an atmosphere for this title and fills you with curiousity of whats ahead. Narrative seems to be told through the scenery and events, and at points even looks like it takes place in a dream. Visuals range from environemental eye-candy to dark imagery. The OST is spot on for every mood the developer(s) intended to convey.

There's a bit of a difficulty spike in platforming and memory puzzles early on, and you'll find yourself lost or wondering if you can even clear a section. It becomes easier once you figure out how to approach, and making near-impossible leaps can be fun. I will say that there are some areas that are better to use the arrow keys and others the mouse (haven't played on pad).

A challenging, yet decent, first-person platformer with the undertones of a creepy-pasta, boosted N64 styled gfx, and gorgeous, mood-setting music. Beginning to end your first time is about an hour and half, but multiple endings and in-game achievements (unlocks?) gives it replayability.
