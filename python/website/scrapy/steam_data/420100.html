About This Game

Delve further into the world of CLANNAD and experience the stories beyond the original visual novel adventure. Known as CLANNAD Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de in Japan, this collection of linear side stories allows you to explore and experience new sides and twists to your favorite characters from the little town of Hikarizaka. These are the stories which are not explored in either the visual novel or in the anime adaptation.

After the death of his mother, Tomoya Okazakiâa guarded delinquent student at Hikarizaka High School struggles to find meaning in his life. While on his way to class one morning, he meets a mystifying girl named Nagisa. From their brief encounter, Nagisa decides they should be friends, and enlists Tomoya's help to revive the school drama club. Along the way, the two make friends with many other students and try to find both solace and belonging in their endeavors.

Originally released on the PSP handheld platform, this all new Steam release collects all the side story chapters and features high resolution assets at 1280x960 and in English for the very first time.

If you have played Clannad VN or watched the animation, it's needless to say that each of these side stories are worth the bucks you spent on it.

The story itself is basically manifestation of life: growing up together with your precious families, friends, teachers, etc. These side stories give us deeper insight to each characters in Clannad and each of them are so heartwarming until 30 minutes passed so quickly. Personally, it feels nostalgic to see Akio-san running with Sanae-san's bread, bullied Sunohara, and so on. Botan's side story is so puhi puhi~

For the background music, there is no new soundtrack introduced in this one. Nevertheless, it's Clannad-ish so you can expect the power of feels from listening to it.

The only drawback is you cannot control the game flow. Once the start button is pressed, you can only pause the game (by using right-click). It's kind of annoying that the subtitle is sometimes stuck because of long texts and you need to click on the screen to continue the subtitle while the narrative keeps playing in the background.

In short, "Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de" is one of the best collection of side stories out there. If you haven't checked the main Clannad VN, it's obviously suggested to read it before buying this one. Some of the side stories are branched from a certain route in its VN, so it may feel a bit strange if you have only watched the animation without reading those routes in the visual novel.

So I sign into steam and much to my surprise I see Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de (Clannad Side Stories) on the store page, and after a brief moment of incomparable excitement and a small amount of time to recompose myself I bought the game, now if you have seen my original review of Clannad you will most likely know I have a great deal of passion for this subject matter.....

so I guess I'll preface this review the same way I did with the original Clannad VN, and this is by saying I maybe a tad bit bias in this as I absolutely love Clannad, I have the Manga, the Blurays, the 10th anniversary art book, so yeah just keep that in mind while reading this review, and try to forgive me ;)

Well to start out I must say that Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de is very different from Clannad as unlike Clannad this is not an arc-choice based Visual Novel rather it is a collection of 16 short stories based around the characters both main and side from Clannad.

The game is not so much a side story serving more as a series of prequel / after stories; for Nagisa-chan, Fu-chan, Kyou-chan, Ryou-chan, Tomoyo-chan, Mei-chan, Botan and Akio-san just to name a few.

Anyway letâs talk about the game system: itâs very distant from what Im used to when it comes to visual novels. It has a forced auto reader with no real way that Ive found to configure the speed which is rather bothersome.

I have also not found a way to advance the story faster, or to go back to parts I might have missed; you can however jump between chapters which is very useful for going back to parts that you like and love rereading over and over *cough* Kyou-chan *cough*

(>_>)
(<_<)

â¦.....anyway there is also an option to check out all the CG's, which I believe is a must have in any visual novel, however the only downside I must say is that Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de does not have as many CG's as I would have like but this is mearly me nit picking as it is a easily overlooked thing.

Length wise each story isnt very long clocking in at aproximatly 25 - 30 min give or take so yes they are not terribly long however you can not hold this against it because-

A. they don't need to be any longer; they tell their story well in the period of time needed, any longer and the feel would be completely off, as if to make it worse by drawing it out.

B. Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de was originally a PSP release so they could only do so much.

And finally C. there are 16 beautifully written stories so the fact that each story is only around 25 - 30 min is quickly forgotten.

Now onto the stories themselves, as I have said previously in my honest opinion I belive that each and ever story is beautifully written, unlike other VN short story collections, Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de is not just a collection of entertaining but ultimatly irrelevant stories.

The stories in Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de are definite additions to the character backgrounds and the history of Clannad itself, this helps build on the already well established origin stories of the characters that I had come to love.

The stories are at times fun, but also there are times that are touching, moving and sad, they shed light on intruiging aspects of the past and future of the various characters: how Kyou-chan and Ryou-chan fell for Tomoya-kun, how Akio-san and Sanae-san met, etc...etc...

Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de doesnt have to much CG as I said before but that is made up for with it being fully narrated and I mean every character, even Tomoya-kun....however much to my annoyance Tomoya-kun doesnt seem to be voiced by the same voice actor as the anime (while the original Clannad VN was fully narrated Tomoya-kun was not voiced)

Music wise Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de is the same as the original Clannad, so itâs pretty good; actually now I think about it Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de reused a lot of assests from the original VN.

Im not saying there isnt any original content but a lot of the scenes and music are from the original but that is to be exspected really if you take into consideration that they where making this for the PSP and they had the assests avalible to them so why not use them.

Overall Id have to say that Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de is truly a worthwhile addition to the Clannad world; and my recommendation is to check it out, as someone who has completed the Clannad NV more times than Id care to say this was something that gave me a more indepth look at the characterrs I love so much.

I hope that this review has helped you in your decision in getting this VN and I hope you will enjoy the whole experience as much as I have, Clannad is my favorite subject and I could go on for hours about it; the Clannad anime is my favorite anime of all time, if you are so inclined I'd recommend you go check that out too.

Happy reading :3

(Like all my reviews I do not put a score as it is my belief that games like all forms of art are subjective and therefore it is impossible to afix a number to them)

(Disclaimer: I did QA for Sekai Project on CLANNAD Side Stories... without ever having read or even watched CLANNAD, so this is possibly a very unusual opinion which you should feel free to ignore if it doesn't seem to apply to you!)

CLANNAD Side Stories, as the name might indicate, is very much targeted at people who have already read CLANNAD, and presents sixteen stories, each about 20 minutes long, which further expand on things not seen in the original novel, including getting to see things from the viewpoint of some of the main characters' parents. And, uh, a tiny pig.

However, despite it being targeted at people who aren't me, I still found it was well-written, and there's a few stories that particularly jump out at me even now, several months later:

05 (Tomoyo Sakagami - Me in those days): This involves a lot more heroic combat than I thought CLANNAD was about.
07 (Botan - Various smells): This story, told from the perspective of an adopted pet pig, is incredibly adorable. The fact that I didn't know the characters did not detract from this at all, because small adorable animals are universal.
08 (Akio Furukawa - A special night): One of the two stories that I really enjoyed, despite not knowing the characters going into this. It's about Nagisa's dad going out of his way to take care of a lost child on Christmas Eve.
12 (Misae Sagara - The memory between the two of us): The other story that I really enjoyed. It's about Misae and Yusuke in high school. Yusuke kind of reminded me of Nekki Basara from Macross 7, which may have been why I enjoyed this one so much.

The only real problem I had with it is that the controls are terrible. Normally this would be an odd thing to bring up with a visual novel, but CLANNAD Side Stories is an adaptation of two drama CDs, so there's no ability to speed up or slow down the playback, only pause and skipping to one of five "chapters" for each of the sixteen stories. This is an understandable choice in that they were, as I understand it, designed to fit audio that was recorded three years before the original version of the game was created, including both background music and sounds, but it still makes it awkward if you read particularly quickly (as I do) or slowly, especially as opening the pause menu also removes the text from the screen.

So yeah, while I can't really speak to the experiences of a CLANNAD fan, seeing as I know almost nothing about the series, I still found parts of this an enjoyable read and think it would be significantly more enjoyable to someone who was already into the series. If you aren't a CLANNAD fan, I would not recommend this as your first entry into the series, however. I definitely had to look the characters up on Wikipedia a few times to understand some of the stories.

I have also not found a way to advance the story faster, or to go back to parts I might have missed; you can however jump between chapters which is very useful for going back to parts that you like and love rereading over and over *cough* Kyou-chan *cough*

â¦.....anyway there is also an option to check out all the CG's, which I believe is a must have in any visual novel, however the only downside I must say is that Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de does not have as many CG's as I would have like but this is mearly me nit picking as it is a easily overlooked thing.

Length wise each story isnt very long clocking in at aproximatly 25 - 30 min give or take so yes they are not terribly long however you can not hold this against it because-

A. they don't need to be any longer; they tell their story well in the period of time needed, any longer and the feel would be completely off, as if to make it worse by drawing it out.

B. Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de was originally a PSP release so they could only do so much.

And finally C. there are 16 beautifully written stories so the fact that each story is only around 25 - 30 min is quickly forgotten.

Now onto the stories themselves, as I have said previously in my honest opinion I belive that each and ever story is beautifully written, unlike other VN short story collections, Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de is not just a collection of entertaining but ultimatly irrelevant stories.

The stories in Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de are definite additions to the character backgrounds and the history of Clannad itself, this helps build on the already well established origin stories of the characters that I had come to love.

The stories are at times fun, but also there are times that are touching, moving and sad, they shed light on intruiging aspects of the past and future of the various characters: how Kyou-chan and Ryou-chan fell for Tomoya-kun, how Akio-san and Sanae-san met, etc...etc...

Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de doesnt have to much CG as I said before but that is made up for with it being fully narrated and I mean every character, even Tomoya-kun....however much to my annoyance Tomoya-kun doesnt seem to be voiced by the same voice actor as the anime (while the original Clannad VN was fully narrated Tomoya-kun was not voiced)

Music wise Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de is the same as the original Clannad, so itâs pretty good; actually now I think about it Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de reused a lot of assests from the original VN.

Im not saying there isnt any original content but a lot of the scenes and music are from the original but that is to be exspected really if you take into consideration that they where making this for the PSP and they had the assests avalible to them so why not use them.

Overall Id have to say that Clannad Hikari Mimamoru Sakamichi de is truly a worthwhile addition to the Clannad world; and my recommendation is to check it out, as someone who has completed the Clannad NV more times than Id care to say this was something that gave me a more indepth look at the characterrs I love so much.

I hope that this review has helped you in your decision in getting this VN and I hope you will enjoy the whole experience as much as I have, Clannad is my favorite subject and I could go on for hours about it; the Clannad anime is my favorite anime of all time, if you are so inclined I'd recommend you go check that out too.
