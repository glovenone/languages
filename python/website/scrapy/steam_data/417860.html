About This Game

Sign In again.
Remember a time before Facebook and Skype? When Windows XP was the next big thing and AIM was king. Relive that era with Emily is Away. Create a screenname and browse buddy infos in this chat-bot meets adventure game. Explore your relationship with Emily, a fellow high school student, in a branching narrative where you choose the outcome. And most importantly, change your text color to lime green so people know you're the coolest kid in school.

Features
  • Buddy icons, profiles and away messages.
  • A dialogue decision-based branching narrative.
  • Over an hour of nostalgia-inducing gameplay.
  • Five chapters spanning five years of the main characters life.
  • Computer sounds you hoped to never hear again!

Recommended - with some reservations for those expecting a game, and not a novel.

Rarely does a game come along where I feel the need to actually take the time to write a review - but even rarer still does something as simple as a game have the power to instantly transport me almost 20 years - short of heading to GOG, of course.

If you spent any significant portion of your life in front of a clicking Windows XP box, chatting over AIM, _everything_ about this title will rocket you back to a distant memory in a way that only Q could rival. From the perfectly executed windowsXP login screen, to the dings and doors of AIM, to the buddy icons and song lyrics in profiles and the ability to set your type to an unreadable blue-on-red, to the whirring of fans...

Superb. Simply put - the 'feel' of this is masterfully executed.

The tone and syntax of the writing is so spot on that I found myself actually remembering conversations with my then-girlfriend, now-wife as if it were just yesterday, and not two decades ago. If you didn't 'grow up' with AIM in this context, you won't get it, and I'm truly sorry for your loss. If you did, however, prepare to be astonished at how well Emily Is Away captures what is likely a forgotten, but very important, part of your life.

For the first playthrough, at least.

And here's the reservations: There's not much replayabilty, as much of it is significantly scripted, but honestly, that's to be expected with such an expertly crafted tone to the words. HOWEVER, as it's more of an interactive novel with a story to tell, emotions to evoke, and commentary to make, I cannot fault it for this - it's lasting impact would be vastly damaged if you could alter the outcome in any way.

THAT SAID: This game is free, and it is totally, unequivocably, worth the price of admission, and more. If you can afford it, donations to the author are completely justified.
