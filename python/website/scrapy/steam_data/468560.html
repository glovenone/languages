UPDATE: Many of you have already discovered this, we were a little late to the party, but Hamachi, Evolve, and many other services, are great, free tools to "fake" a local network and connect with a friend no matter where they are.

________________________________________________________

To find a game, you must have someone create a game on the same network connection that your computer is on. If you and another player are not on the same network connection and you try to "Find a Game," the screen will tell you there are no servers available.

Again, download Hamachi for free to play the game at https://www.vpn.net/

Known Bugs

Consistent: If you play a game of Blind Trust and try to immediately start a new game from the menu, the game will crash. Please always close the game entirely before playing a new game.

Inconsistent: Intermittently, one or both players will get stuck on a tutorial step despite having completed that step. The tutorial text will not advance. Please close the game entirely and restart the game to try to play again.

About This Game

Blind Trust is a two-player, cooperative game where the deaf leads the blind on a fantasy island. Navigate using only 3D sound as the blind Oracle, or lead the Oracle with magical sound beacons as the deaf Soldier. Work together to fight monsters, solve puzzles, and reach your ultimate goal.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP or later
    • Processor: 1.8 GHz
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Not recommended for Intel onboard graphics
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Storage: 550 MB available space
    • Sound Card: Required

Well... Um... This is a game... I guess...

I'm gonna be fully honest, I really, REALLY want to upvote this game. The concept is really cool and the open-endedness of the bizzare scenario the two players are placed in gives you lots of opportunity to create a story of your own. This game is amazing for it's co-op idea and total concept is, once again, super cool, but the game has a ton of errors within itself that make it almost unplayable.

I first played this game with my younger sister who was new to the whole "Steam" business and was browsing for Free-to-Play games to add to her library. She talked me into getting this and playing with her on our local internet connection. While we managed to play with the time we had, we both could tell how flawed the game was (and that's coming a newcomer to PC gaming).

The game frequently crashes when you're holding a LAN game, forcing you to playthrough the whole adventure once again, since there isn't a checkpoint or save system in the game. This makes it very annoying for either players to be fully invested in the game's environment. Seriously, for one moment (before we were disconnected), I jsut asked my sister to share her PC screen so I can navigate through a segment we had already done as the Oracle with ease.

I also wish this game had more diversity to it's gameplay. Sure this concept is really neat as previously stated, but it got old, even for the few moments I played. If you're a fan of unique co-op games that are pretty rusty, I'd give this a shot, but I can't recommend you hold onto this game for a decent period of time, until the developers fix the crashing bugs or make playing the game more through adding an online feature.

"The oracle is blind while the soldier is leading"
Innovative gameplay design to lead someone through an invisible world. The game is a F2P 3D walking similator for 2 playroles - The Oracle and the Soldier. The Oracle is blind and follows pulsing beacons the Soldier sets. If the oracle hears a monster it marks it with her wand and the soldier take it down with his crossbow.

There are basically three levels
1. A tutorial area
2. A middle part with a puzzle area (laser columns)
3. A final boss (huge monster) until you arrive to the exit.

Unfortunately the game is quite buggy for the puzzle part. Sometimes the first column has no glow area, other times there doesn't appear a gate key in the end. Also it seems to be a natural law that on the first attempt to connect to your friend's game it crashes every single time. Sometimes it also gets stuck on a worldview map. So it's quite annoying to restart the game just for those quality issues and not for hardness of bossfights or abyss falldowns. Idk if 3rd party tools like hamach can prevent from those issues.

Otherwise the game is quite an unique funmaking experience for a short while.
