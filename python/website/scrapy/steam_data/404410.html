24 June

Stable release 0.9.2 [beta]

This is a "stable" release, focusing on bug fixes, polish, and usability rather than on bigger changes to the game engine and content. After a week of testing in the "beta" branch, 0.9.2 will be pushed out to all players. Changes include:

  • Tooltips for all ship and outfit attributes.
  • Confirmation dialog if taking off will force you to sell something.
  • Subtle blue background haze to make the star field less boring.
  • You can now recharge shields while on an uninhabited planet.
  • Rebalanced the new Hai outfits.
  • Gave all missiles mass so you can't store infinite numbers in cargo.
  • Three "previous" saved games are now automatically stored as backups.
Bug fixes include:
  • Fixed the crash when holding the jump key while jumping.
  • If excess outfit cargo is sold when you take off, you are now properly paid.
  • Fixed the escort icon merging code that was broken on some OSes.
There are many other changes, as well. For a complete list, see the changelog.

Special thanks to everyone who contributed to this release: @Alkallid, @egony, @lheckemann, @LocalGod79, and @MarcelineVQ.

26 May

Unstable release 0.9.1 [beta]

Version 0.9.1 is now available in the "beta" branch. This is an unstable release with lots of new features, some of which may not be completely polished or bug-free. Major changes include:

  • New Wanderer missions giving access to new ships and technology.
  • The Hai now have their own unique set of outfits. (@Wrzlprnft, @LocalGod79, @Amazinite)
  • New human side missions giving back story about alien species.
  • New missile tracking modes based on target size or temperature.
  • Missile jammers that work against certain missiles.
  • Better AI for escorts matching your speed and heading.
  • Added a pop-up mini-map when you are jumping.
  • Ships now have big "final explosion" sounds.
  • The shipyard now shows full stats for the player's ship.
Bug fixes include:
  • Capturing an NPC now counts as evading and destroying it.
  • The "southern carriers" appear more gradually and in fewer numbers.
  • Weapons with zero shield damage no longer cause crashes.
There are many other changes, too; for more details, see the changelog.

Special thanks to the folks who contributed new content, bug fixes, etc. to this release: @Alkallid, @Amazinite, @kilobyte, @LocalGod79, @pootthedestroyer, @toilethinges, and @Wrzlprnft.

Note: because the 0.9 series adds lots of new graphics, some old computers with only 256 MB of video RAM may experience intermittent slow-downs. If so, you can install the "low dpi plugin" to conserve VRAM by eliminating some of the largest images.

About This Game

Endless Sky is a 2D space trading and combat game inspired by the classic Escape Velocity series. Work your way up from a relatively wimpy shuttle, cargo ship, or fighter, to a highly upgraded and customized flagship or to a massive fleet of warships or freighters. Earn money by carrying passengers or cargo, escorting convoys, bounty hunting, or plundering and capturing enemy ships. Take sides in a civil war, or ignore the story line and just enjoy exploring the galaxy and blasting pirates.

The main story line takes about 8-16 hours to play through. The galaxy contains hundreds of star systems and planets, and several alien species with their own unique technologies. You can choose between more than fifty ships and several hundred ship upgrades. A plugin system allows anyone with a text editor and a paint program to create new ships, weapons, or missions, and the galaxy editor can be used to add new star systems and planets.

Endless Sky is free, open source, and cross platform (Windows, Mac, and Linux). Over time, the game universe will expand as contributors add new missions, story lines, alien species, places to explore, sound effects, and artwork.

For more information, please visit the website or read the player's manual.

This is a fairly simple game, but surprizingly enjoyable!

You start with one of three basic ships. A passenger shuttle, a barge and a fighter.
Your choice here will affect a good first four to six hours of the game.
Shuttle will be your simplest jobs, but it makes money the fastest. You ferry tourists, workers and cargo around the universe and make a quick buck all while paying off your mortgage. The object of this playstyle is to get all the people you can from point A to point B while turning the best profit, and dodging pirates.
Choose a barge and you'll be carrying bulk and rush deliveries of vital supplies throughout. If not taking up jobs, your time is best spent experimenting with trade routes and playing around with prices, selling what to where.
Should you choose arguably the most challenging, the fighter, you'll be pit against pirates. Really annoying space pirates. You start out as a fighter, only taking on other fighters. Once you finally pay off your ship loan and get a better ship, you'll be ready to aid the Navy again pirates and they who call themselves "the free worlds", basically space ISIS.

Allaround, it gets a tad repeditive later on, but funny enough, I never got bored. I always found something to do. I even recorded a lot of my adventures and more dangerous routes and am using it as a base for a novel. Legit.
Eventually you save for bigger and better ships and, one day, may decide to raise a fleet. You can board enemy craft and capture them, should you have the weapons and crew, and everything is just real dandy. Prices fluctuate, you basically run an actual business, and it can all make for a rivetting tale. It's free, so I'd very much suggest picking this up if you have some extra time on your hands.
