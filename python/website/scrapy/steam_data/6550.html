About This Game

Dante's past is now revealed as Devil May Cry returns to its roots. Master Dante's multiple fighting styles while battling never before seen demons and new characters as you fight your way towards a brutal confrontation with Dante's mysterious twin brother, Vergil.
  • Choose Dante's fighting style - maximize the impact of close-up blade attacks, or blow enemies away from a distance.
  • Huge arsenal of weapons - handguns, shotguns, swords, and more.
  • A horde of demonic enemies - more ferocious than ever - with powerful weapons and abilities to transform and fly.

System Requirements

    Minimum: Windows XP/2000*, Intel Pentium III Processor, 1.0 Ghz and above, 256 MB RAM, DirectX 9.0n and above video card, 128MB VRAM, Shader 2.0, DirectX 9.0 Soundcard, Mouse/keyboard, 2.0 GB free disk space
    Recommended: Intel Premium 4 Processor 2.0 Ghz or better, 512 MB RAM, VRAM 256 MB and above, game pad with 4 wheel-12 button analogue stick, 4.7 GB Free disk space

    Operation not assured if VRAM is shared with Main Memory. Video card of NVIDIA GeForce 7500 and above is recommended. Please end other applications when playing with this product. Defects may occur if other applications are running.
    * Not designed to run on Windows Vista

NOTICE : THIS REVIEW CONTAINS A CONDITIONAL RECOMMENDATION, THOUGH IS OVERALL NOT RECOMMENDED.

Devil May Cry 3 is a legendary title that defines it's genre. However, the PC port is completely broken, and in it's vanilla state is unplayable. It's a real shame that Capcom has neglected to fix this port or supply some sort of alternative for those who don't have a console or wish to play the game on PC. Devil May Cry 4 has one of the best console ports around, but Devil May Cry 3 might just be one of the worst. For those who have never played DMC3 and wish to, I highly recommend finding another way to play the game, as this version just isn't the way to do it, no matter what you do to it. Now, for those of you who have played DMC3 countless times, and perhaps are fond of DMC4 Dante's style switcher, this version does yield a bit of potential for you. This game can be modded and cleaned up a bit to work a lot better, though still no where near an ideal performance, and also include Dante's style switching mechanic from DMC4 in it. I'd like to stress though, that this is not the way the game was intended to be played, and using the mod does break the challenge of the game somewhat, so like I said earlier, for those of you who have yet to experience DMC3, I highly recommend you avoid this version and play a console port. For those of you who are interested in playing with the possibilites of this port, I'll include a link to the guide showing you how to fix this port up. To sum everything up though, for those of you yet to play this game, do not buy this, play a better version. For those of you like me who have played DMC3 to death and want to play around with it in new ways, grab this when it's cheap, and apply mods to fix it. This is in no way a replacement or the definitive version of Devil May Cry 3, and Capcom really should give us a better port on PC.
