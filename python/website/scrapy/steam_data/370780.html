About This Game

Description


Formerly known as Dark Souls, Fated Souls is Warfare Studios' first commercial release, and one of their most popular along with Vagrant Hearts, Ashes of Immortality and Valiant.
Note: to toggle between screen modes, press ALT + ENTER.

Storyline


You are haunted by your mercenary past and seeking peace. You are talked into taking what you believe is a simple job as a bodyguard for an introverted girl with divine power. Soon however, you find yourself thrust into events beyond your control on a global scale, and must use your wits and skills to survive.

Disclaimer: I won this game in a contest, and while grateful to the publisher and developer for this prize, I'm very glad I didn't pay for it...

As a fan of RPG maker games, I've played my fair share.
I've played some excellent titles such as Sydney's World, and Vagrant Hearts 1 & 2.
They had interesting stories, unique and memorable characters, excellent battle systems, and very good interfaces.
This game doesn't make me feel the same way.
It should be noted that the Vagrant Hearts series came out from this same developer after Fated Souls.

Fated Souls is a very generic turn-based RPG.
It features a side view battle system, a handful of different characters, and random battles.
Typical problems with the RPG Maker engine are here such as no Steam Overlay support.
I'm surprised to see that this game costs full price $10.99 CDN. This game is NOT worth that much by any means. There is simply not enough good qualities here to make this game worth that price.
I've also discovered that some Steam Achievements do NOT work in this game for those who care.

Pros:
-Good battle system
-Player characters are animated (I don't see this in RPG Maker games very often)
-Decent music and sound effects

There is simply not enough here to make me want to keep spending my time playing. Everything about this game is just so generic, and there are too many spelling and grammarical errors to take the attempt at good writing here seriously. I found the story very unusual, and the characters were very strange. The main character was a very moody childish person. I found it unappealing.
Travelling from town to town through maze-like forests got very boring eventually, especially once I realized that this was the main path of progression in this game. It just felt so boring and generic.
The interface is very bland. The artwork is terrible. The portraits of the characters don't match the in game sprites and one of the characters portrait is zoomed in way more that the rest of them and it just looks bad.
For some reason, your main character walks around at lighting speed during the first hour or so of the game. No, there is no Walk/Run button or game setting. The walking speed than changes later in the game automatically to something more tolerable.
I found this made no sense and was a very memorable negative about the game design.
No attempt was made here to make this game stand out as exciting or impressive.
I think the issue here is that this is the first game this developer made, and Vagrant Hearts is proof he got better at this, but this game has no buisness being priced the way it is.
I guess I just expect a lot more out of a game released within the last couple years, since everyone else is doing much better things with the engine.
There is no excuse for the bad spelling and grammer, and broken achievements. It's 2016, let's act like it.

MAYBE, get this game if you have nothing else better to play, and you can get it for a low price, otherwise don't waste your time.There are plenty of better RPGs on Steam with lower or equal prices tags than this.

Skip this game, you're not missing anything.

In Fated Souls the main character (Gauly) is thrown into an innocent enough sounding situation wherein he is called on to escort a maiden (and her mentor) of the "Great Church" to a faraway temple. Simple enough, but, of course, obstacles of all sorts present themselves in this quick moving and absorbing story of a man (Gauly) who is tormented by his inner conflicts and past history as a mercenary.
As the trio move across the world, there are many characters, situations, monsters, and "anomalies" ---- boss fights ---- that have to be dealt with along with a lot of exploration and things to discover that will keep you alert and interested in this nicely told story.

Character development gets high marks in this game ---- each of the main protagonists slowly reveal their true selves and their motivations as you get further into the tale. Gauly especially is believable and interesting.

High marks go to the music in the game, which sometimes adds to the suspenseful atmosphere, and sometimes offers relief in the form of light and catchy tunes ---- but is never overpowering.

Artwork is very good, particularly in the battle scenes. And the world itself is bright and colorful while caves, gloomy forests, etc. are presented in good detail.

The game offers a bit higher challenge in the beginning than most, but once you get the feel of how to battle and use magic the balance is just fine for moving along easily.

It's a classic 16-bit style rpg that is exciting and smoothly paced, and I'm glad it has arrived on Steam. I hope the sequel comes along soon.
