About This Game

New York, 1955. It's become dangerous in the city. The mafia is trying to seize power. But recently, a new force appeared. A far more terrible force. Over the last few days, five mafia bosses have disappeared in mysterious circumstances. A strange liquid and a butterfly were found at the scenes of the disappearances. But this isn't what scared the citizens... Children started to disappear in the city. All of them drew exactly the same butterflies before they disappeared. This forced detectives and journalists alike to start their investigations.

• A bonus chapter in the underground city.
• An integrated strategy guide.
• Collections, morphing objects, trophies, and bonus puzzles.
• Wallpapers, concept art, soundtracks and much more.

This is a pretty solid HOG imo. I experienced no glitches or crashes while playing it. Most of the achievement will be earned in your first playthrough as long as you don't use skip ever or hints in the HO scenes.

The story follows a journalist hired by a museum director to look into the mysterious disappearences of mafia bosses and children. You travel through many places in New York City through a secret underground train network.
The bonus chapter was imo more interesting story-wise than the main story though it sadly does not get too indepth into the worldbuilding that looks interesting.

A few nuisances about this game; the protaganist almost never speaks. She never has half a dialogue or explain or wonder about things in the game through voice. I was getting used to her being mute (which would be impractical as an investigating journalist) until she randomly speaks when finding an item. I guess they paid some famous voice actress by the word or something,
Another thing is the few achievements that can only be earned when doing a specific puzzle well, As the game autosaves you have no option to go back and try again if you should fail on your first try; you have to play the game again, and as the game is locked to your ingame profile, you have to create a new one if you want to start over.

Extra bonus for the game for having the areas with the collectable objects open for the entire game. The game also has a Srtategy Guide which if you should so wish can look up and story progress, puzzle solution or location of collectable, without any punishments to your achievements.

The puzzles were decent and the collecable items were decently hidden. The HO scenes uses silouettes instead of words for better and worse.

My playtime is once throught the main story and one and a half times through the bonus chapter; the extra half for picking up an achievement, so the game has a pretty good length and content.

New York Mysteries: Secrets of the Mafia is a really great Hidden Object Game. The story is compelling: you're a detctive in 1950s NYC trying to solve a string of disappearances of Mafia bosses under mysterious circumstances. In addition, each of these disappearances seem related to another dissapearance, this time of a small child. Somehow butterflies are also related. It's a nice little mystery.

The puzzles are challenging, and there are several difficulty options you can choose from, affecting the amount of help the game gives in the form of visual cues or hints. The music's really cool and fits the theme nicely. I love the retro style and the "period feel" of the 50s and the artwork really showcases that. Overall, New York Mysteries is a high-quality HOG.

There are loads of collectible items (and completing each collection earns you Steam achievements) and there are a lot of different areas to visit in the game. The map is huge, but fortunately, there is also a convenient fast-travel system allowing you to jump around from place to place quickly.

One thing you won't find in this HOG are word-lists and cluttered scenes where you have to cross all the words off the list by finding the items. Hidden object scenes are more organic here, you're given a group of silouettes and you have to work out the objects that fill them. Each of the objects is usually used in the scene to uncover one or more of the additional objects, so the scenes work out more like a puzzle than a straight-up search.

This seems like it is one of the longer HOGs I think I've played, though it's still a game you can complete pretty quickly. I don't really see that as a down side at all though, since the polished and well-presented gameplay and story are really enjoyable.

AT A GLANCE
(Full review follows below)

  • Game Name: New York Mysteries: Secrets of the Mafia
  • Original Release: 2014
  • Genre Tags: Point & Click; Adventure; Hidden Object; Casual
  • My Overall Grade: B
  • Estimated Playtime (Campaign): 5-7 hours
  • Multiplayer Aspect: None
  • Recommended To: Established fans of the genre; Casual gamers

REVIEW
I play a lot of hidden-object/point-and-click adventure games because sometimes I just want a relaxing gaming experience where I have to think, but I don’t have to react. Nevertheless, I will be the first to admit that: even when these games are well developed, I can understand why a large percentage of gamers would find them unexciting. New York Mysteries: Secrets of the Mafia (Collector’s Edition) is one of the better hidden-object/point-and-click adventure games I have played, simply because there are so many things done “right.” But if you don’t really like these types of games, you probably shouldn’t bother with it, because there isn’t anything special about it that will change your mind. However, any gamer who already enjoys this particular genre should definitely check this out.

All of the things you would want a game like this to have, it does have. Various difficulties, a fast travel map, challenging-yet-intuitive puzzle solutions, collectibles, and achievements. It also has a decent story and the cut-scenes aren’t horridly cringe-worthy, which many games in this genre can’t truly claim. Another rare and awesome feature is the fact you can unlock most of the missable achievements from the “extras” menu to replay specific puzzles— rather than having to replay the entire game for one achievement.

One of the best features of the game, though, is the integrated strategy guide. I think it is a great dynamic and it is certainly easier to use than having to alt-tab to find a guide online. It is organized so that you can choose to use it: only to help find collectibles, only if you are stuck, the whole way through, or not at all.

New York Mysteries: Secrets of the Mafia (Collector’s Edition) certainly won’t be blowing anyone’s socks off, and it probably won’t appeal to gamers who aren’t big fans of the hidden-object/point-and-click adventure games. But if you are a fan of this type of game, then I can wholeheartedly recommend this game to you, because it is definitely one of the better games in the genre.

As many of you may be aware, I'm a HOG addict at this point. I won't lie; I love this genre. I love puzzles, I love revealing stories step by step, and I love spending some mindless time on them. Yet, the HOG genre consists of one of the most repetitive gameplays ever. Thus, it is remarkably hard to make anything either new, or even refreshing as a HOG. New York Mysteries: Secrets of the Mafia is one great HOG, with hours of gameplay, imaginative puzzles that I haven't seen before which comes with comparatively logical item combination. Plus, the story is interesting!

It's New York, 1955. The fearsome Mafioso of their time disappear one by one, leaving only an odd green residue and a butterfly behind them. It is also reported that children all over the city disappear alongside these dangerous criminals, drawing the exact same butterfly as the last thing they do. Our protagonist, investigator and reporter Laura James is contacted by the curator of the New York Museum, where the last disappearance has occurred. Laura is asked to investigate this odd phenomenon and find the missing children if possible. She suddenly finds herself in a picture with a secret organization, old underground passages that trace all over the city and an Indian ghost story. How come all these odd happenings are connected?

Coming up with a strong and interesting story, this is a great game for its genre. Art and environment design are high quality - depicting the hidden beauty of New York City during its shadier years - , with beautiful character design and marvelous cinematics. There is real labor put into this game, I tell you. Voice acting isn't excellent, but it's obviously beyond par. I'm a bit disappointed that we don't hear Laura's voice commenting on something, but it's not a priority.

HOG scenes are not your usual. They are compact; weaved with puzzles and item combinations similar as an adventure game within themselves, designed to reach a singular item at the end. Puzzles are numerous and mostly brain scratching, designed elaborately. I went for not skipping any puzzle for the achievement, so it was a tough and fun experience for me. If you are into puzzle solving, this is a neat addition.

Maybe the greatest bonus of the game is its game time. Added up with the time you'll be spending for many imaginative puzzles and the bonus chapter, there is an approximate game time of 6-7 hours! The gameplay has an usual amount of backtracking, but with the usage of the interactive map, nothing's a chore. You are also given the option of collecting some phantom objects and collection items for achievements, if you are into that sort of thing.

If you are fresh out of HOGs to play, do not think twice. New York Mysteries: Secrets of the Mafia is an A+ HOG for you to enjoy. The game comes from FIVE-BN games, - nope, it's not Artifex Mundi, but definitely AM quality - a relatively small producer that I suggest keeping an eye on in the future. Enjoy!

Short version: 89%
New York Mysteries is a great casual adventure game with a wide variety of puzzles and a surprisingly good plot. Don’t expect any traditional list-based hidden object scenes in it though.

Long version:
New York Mysteries: Secrets of the Mafia is an ambitious game that tries to push the boundaries of the hidden object genre, but it doesn’t try to change too much.

Like many good HOGs, New York Mysteries knows the importance of a good story: we are following an investigative journalist on the trail of some mysterious child disappearances that always occur at the same time when a prominent member of the Mafia goes missing. While the plot involves a secret society and some supernatural elements, it retains the more realistic thriller-like atmosphere to the end.

Although it is classified as a HOG, the game has no traditional hidden object scenes, only procedural ones, where you have to use uncovered objects to get even more items. The developers focused on the numerous and diverse puzzles instead, including several types that are rarely seen in these kinds of games, elevating New York Mysteries among the best HOG types on Steam.

I'm a fan of the noir genre, and I've played many HOGs over the years - so I was very surprised to find this one that combines both features; it was a very fun and charming experience!

The main story features a journalist named Laura, who takes it upon herself to try and solve the mystery behind the kidnapped children and some mafia bosses who have been disappearing from all over New York City.

The main story features elements that are borrowed from the typical noir settings, as well as some paranormal traits, and it also borrows from some Native American folklore, as well.
It's very unique and enjoyable because it has pieces of the HOG and noir comfort zone, but the developers weren't too afraid to step beyond the borders to keep things fresh and unpredictable.

After completing the main storyline, a 'mini-episode' will be unlocked and players can enjoy going just a bit further into New York Mysteries' universe; via an underground city that has been left untouched for years.
But, when one of Laura's previous aquaintances has been poisoned by a mysterious toxin from an unknown plant, it's up to her to explore the abandoned city to try and find the antidote to save them.

As for gameplay, it's a mixture of HOG features as well as some from classic Point & Click adventure games.

It was nice to find and pick objects from a 'clean' scene instead of looking for them in a jumbled mess, like most typical HOGs have players do to progress. And, on the flipside, I enjoyed interacting with the enviroment, turning keys to open doors, mixing chemicals, rejoining torn papers as well as clicking on puzzle pieces.

On top of that, there's some nice cutscenes, along with some voice-acting (Though, sadly, Laura doesn't have much of a speaking part) to really paint a picture for new players.

It's a good balance, there's something for everyone to enjoy.

Now, with all that in mind, I will definitely say that the game was most definitely enjoyable from my end and was worth every penny - six hours for a comfortable playthrough for six bucks sounds fair to me; and with the replay value of revisiting the puzzles and the massive open-world levels with lush artwork, it's almost a steal!

However, that doesn't necessarily mean that it doesn't have some issues, however minor...

-You only have ONE save file, you can't have multiples.
Also, even worse, if you're replaying some of the main game again but want to also replay the bonus chapter, the save data for the bonus chapter WILL overwrite your save data for the main game - and vice versa.

-Spilling text on certain documents.
On some of the books and journal entries, the bottom of the text runs off of the page.
This can prove to be frustrating, I'd like to finish what I'm reading instead of it running off into oblivion or cutting abruptly.

-Bonus chapter's too short.
I can understand that it's a 'mini-episode' and all, but it just felt like the very end was a bit sudden; Laura explores the underground city and rescues her companion, but, there was no real explanation for what happened to the people there.
Maybe it's supposed to be left up for interpretation, but there could've been a little more to it.

Other than that, I loved playing through the game; it was nice to sit down and relax with.
