About This Game

Unpossible will test your skills, your reflexes, and even your memory. It will make you smile. It will probably make you scream. You will die often and it will always be your fault. Fight to learn the obstacles…they already know how to fight you. Relentless. Thrilling. Unpossible!

Gameplay levels:
Simplicity: Super approachable introduction to the Unpossible world. Hone your skills here. Last 60 seconds in Simplicity to unlock Futile and Daily Futile.

Futile: Where most people will spend their time. Faster than Simplicity, Futile will find your weaknesses and make you conquer them. Survive for 60 seconds in Futile to unlock Ultra and Daily Ultra.

Ultra: It's Ultra Unpossible. This is the pinnacle of Unpossible. The obstacles spin, the sequences you learned before will need to be unlearned. You will be challenged all the way to your soul. Ultra is hardcore. Masocore even. Only the strongest will make it through Ultra.

Daily gameplay levels:
Daily Simplicity, Daily Futile and Daily Ultra. If you like to practice the same level, these levels are for you. These levels are the same difficulty as their namesakes except the levels stay the same for a full day. There are also special achievements for the daily levels - beat them every day to find out!

Special mode:
Cruise: This is the zen level. You can’t die. Practice your turns if you’d like or just watch it like a live-action art piece. It’s chill.

Having played this game on my cellphone for quite some time I was happy to purchase it for pc as well. It is a good game and excels for wasting a few minutes of time should your mind find boredom waiting for other tasks to complete. Unpossible is simple to pick up yet difficult to master, requiring strong, constant concentration to avoid collisions. For its tron-esque appearance and addictive gameplay style I give it an 8.5/10.

I have compiled a brief list of my thoughts/ideas for improving either your play or perhaps the game itself. They are suggestions so take them with a grain of salt. It’s Unpossible that 100% of my ideas are good.

1. Have a 'training' mode where the user can select or deselect which types of segments will appear. (If there is a particular set of obstacles like the rotating triangles that keep killing you, then isolate them and practice running their gauntlet until mastery has occurred.)

2. Add variable speed scaler in either the (+) or (-) direction

3. Add color option to modify the luminosity/hue of Unpossible's color theme.

4. For PC add controller support for gamepads, analog sticks, etc....

5. Increase Max sensitivity range for controls.

6. For those who might be having difficulty add a "ghost' mode that allows the player to follow a translucent line through the obstacles. Users can follow the path and become familiar with certain 'difficult' areas of the game.

7. Simultaneous multiplayer mode (VS.)

Personal improvement area (at least it helped me)

8. When playing, stare downrange as far as possible to see what is coming and use your peripheral vision to navigate.

9. Practice when you first wake up after you've had a good rest and some healthy food.

10. Try to position yourself on the upslope of the curve to see what is coming next.

Finally, in update 3 you asked what the super hard mode past 'ultra' should be. I would vote the simple route. Call it 'Unpossible.'

A pretty fun and nice-looking reflex game. It has 3 modes of difficulty: Simplicity, Futile, and Ultra, and every time you play, the stage is more or less random with obstacles placed in a different pattern. You do get some types of obstacles more common in one difficulty than another, but their placement and pattern often varies. Your goal is to last as long as you can.

For each difficulty level you also get a special daily stage. This stage only changes after every 24 hours, so if you're not a fan of the randomness aspect, you can always play the daily stage and try to memorize your way through it.

There isn't much else that this game offers, so my only critique so far is that it needs more content, perhaps in a form of more game modes (not necessarily difficulty, but just different goals), but what it does have is pretty fun if you fancy to test your reflexes, precision, and decisiveness, and it costs hardly anything.
