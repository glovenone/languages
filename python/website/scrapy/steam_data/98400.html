About This Game

In the only remaining human city of Bezoar, Major Fletcher, an Army Combat Veteran and soldier of the CLN, is dragged into a conflict between two of mankind's greatest enemies. He is to discover that nothing is what it seems to be.
Hard Reset is an action-packed, single-player shooter for the PC. With over-the-top destruction, loads of enemies, great weapon variety and a beautifully realized cyberpunk setting, Hard Reset is sure to shake up the shooter scene.

Key features:

  • A haunting cyberpunk / dark sci-fi setting
  • Fast-paced, old-school shooter gameplay
  • Hordes of enemies to destroy
  • Epic boss fights
  • A deep, experience-based weapon upgrade system
  • High-fidelity graphics with full dynamic lighting
  • Extensive use of physics and dynamic environments

This was one cool game in my book. Made by the devs of Painkiller, Bulletstorm and Shadow Warrior.
It's an old school type of FPS - think of Serious Sam, but in a Cyberpunk future.
The story is presented between levels, simplisticly drawn and in a comicbook style. The story lacks... but this is a "Robot masacre simulator", so the story is there just to have something to follow and move forward.
Now the great part of this game are the graphics. They nailed all design elements:
* a world taken from DeusEx combined with Terminator future
* also the DLC has a more junkyard vibe... think of the "Enslaved: Journey to the west" game
* lots of details in all elements, from world design to enemies
* great weapons
The sound effects are also great, the music is there and is not bad, but the voice acting lacks... luckly there is not much of it. Gameplay ways... had lots of fun. These are the things I want to point out:
*the weapons are originaly implemented, but not perfectly
*enemies are really cool and engaging, but only 3 types with 3 variations on each
*bosses are lots of fun, but again, too few
*linear levels, but the secrets are a cool addition, giving you a reason to explore
*environment traps are amazing and one of the most fun part of the game
* ~8-10 hours of gameplay for the main game+DLC
The DLC has a few more enemies, new setting and is equaly as fun as the main campain - worth it!

The Redux version brings 1 more enemy type (zombie like), 1 more weapon (sword) and a dash move, this along the improved graphics, performance and more balance to the game - if you own the original, I would say that this is not worth it, unless you want to support the devs or have extra cash at hand.

It was very interesting to come to this game AFTER "Shadow Warrior." Flying Wild Hog are seriously the kings of building nifty-looking environments, as well as giving strong nods to classic PC game mechanics. You can also see their lineage of "lock you in an arena and spawn enemies" tricks, but what's rather strange is that they did it MORE blatantly in "Shadow Warrior" than in "Reset."

"Hard Reset" has its interesting moments, especially with it feeling like a playable homage to "Blade Runner," but it only has a few tricks to pull. The enemies are actually interesting to fight (which is an excellent bit of craft), but you'll encounter lots of copies of every baddie. In that way, the experience can get quite "samey" after about the halfway mark. Plus, there are only so many corridors and corridor-esque situations that can be strung together before the magic starts to wear thin.

The upgrade system is decent, but the overwrought interface sometimes gets in its own way. (It looks really cool, though.) The weapon balance is sensible, if seemingly a bit tilted towards the CLN weapon. The "physical ammo" gun feels like it hits much harder than the NRG weapon. A rocket launcher with laser guidance will really clean up the battlefield in a hurry, and the plain-vanilla assault rifle is actually very accurate for a rapid-fire weapon. Even with that sensation of CLN favoritism, the NRG weapon can become almost a touch ridiculous. Yes, it feels generally underpowered and slow, with a parallel tendency to run you dry of ammo at a critical juncture, but the sticky EMPs are very useful...and just wait until you get homing projectiles that you can fire through level geometry!

This original iteration of the title has no manual save, so there are times when the checkpoint spacing can be inconvenient at times. Even so, the devs were nice enough to hand out a pretty decent number of checkpoints overall, and those checkpoints do tend to occur in helpful spots. The only real complaint here is that, come on, quicksave is pretty much a basic feature anymore.

The story elements are something of a rougher ride, because of how "lurching" they are. Tantalizing pieces of world-bulding are dangled in front of you, but then very little actually gets done with them. I felt like a real opportunity was missed there, especially since it wouldn't have taken much more to tighten up the narrative side of the presentation.

"Hard Reset" is a good, shortish diversion of a shooter. It's not a jaw-dropper, but it does a decent job without overstaying its welcome. I hope Flying Wild Hog keeps honing their craft, because they've come out of the gate doing quite well.

Quick Summary: A first person shooter with a variety of weapons, no reloading, and swarms of bullet resistant enemies. The bad guys all have enough life that you need to focus your fire to bring down even the little dudes, but they usually come in packs so survival is all about crowd control. The game was developed by the same people who made the Shadow Warrior remake, so if you enjoyed that game, you should like Hard Reset.

You play Fletcher, a kind of cyborg cop on the streets of Bezoar city, investigating a breach in the city walls that allowed killer robots to enter and run amok killing civilians. Thereâs no cover system to protect you, so if you stay in one place youâll get squashed, especially since a lot of the things you might think to hide behind can be destroyed by charging enemies. Nano can be spent at upgrade stations to buy attachments for your guns, turning them into different weapons.
