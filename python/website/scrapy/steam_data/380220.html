About This Game

About Hover Junkers

Hover Junkers is a VR with tracked controllers ONLY multiplayer shooter designed from the ground up for VR. Immerse yourself in a virtual world of drought stricken future where junk hunters scavenge for scrap left behind. Choose from 17 different ships that range from tiny to huge to fit any size VR play area. Engage in multiplayer combat to claim scrap/loot and defend against enemy players. Use scrap to fortify your Junker from incoming fire. Physically dodge, hide, and shoot from behind the cover of your Junkers defenses.

Hover Junkers is unlike anything you've played.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: Intel i5-4590 3.3 GHz
    • Memory: 8 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GTX 970
    • Storage: 20 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Run on low settings only :(
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows 10
    • Processor: Intel i7-4770 3.4 GHz
    • Memory: 16 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GTX 980
    • Storage: 20 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Don't cheap out on your VR PC :)

Apoc

Posted: 5 July
This is a very very impressive game. It is among the most polished and complete titles for the Vive with a large amount of replayability. Note that the words "complete" and "polished" are hard to come by for VR games currently.

The art style and general atmosphere are very reminiscent of a Borderlands style game, with similar humour mixed with post-apocalyptic junk. It works well and the developers did an excellent job immersing you in the worls, as you will hardly find a menu or start screen. Everything exists in the world for you to manipulate.

The general idea is that the players float around on these craft called "Junkers", that are various piles of junk welded together atop some sort of hovering mechanism. You pilot this craft around and look for more junk to weld on to your Junker to provide you with cover.

You are free to move about the craft by moving around in your room and the controls are quite intuitive once you get used to them. The game has a bit of a learning curve but this is not a bad thing as it is about some time that we had some multiplayer pvp games with some depth on this VR platform.
The gunplay is satisfying and the reload mechanics take manual dexterity, meaning that gunfights are fast and furious as you are ducking and diving and shooting and trying to reload two weapons at once.

The developers are very active over on the official subreddit r/hoverjunkers, and are constantly releasing new updates. I am looking forward to how HJ will evolve over the future

A must have for the vive!

I cannot belive how much fun I just had shooting robots on a stationary platform, with a complete stranger.
jumping over rockets, ducking beneath lasers - THIS GAME HAS IT ALL.

It's clear that i love this game, it's the most fun i've had in VR, but let me just break down the pros and cons for you.

CONS
  1. You're gonna move alot.
    This is probably gonna be a problem if you're overweight or don't like to have fun. No but seriously, be prepared to literally throw yourself on the floor to avoid enemy fire.

  2. There are almost no EU servers.
    This is quite a shame since i live in the EU and my internet connection is less than optimal. It hasn't been that bad yet. I've seen people disappear, teleport and so forth. It's in no way unplayable but it is an annoyance. This will be a non issue as soon as the playerbase increases *hint *hint

  3. The game is badly optimized for processors in the i5-k range.
    This is probably the worst offender and something to consider as i have had to fiddle with quite alot of options to avoid missing frames. The developers are aware of this issue and are hopefully working to fix it (it affects job simulator as well). This means that i have had to play the game on the lowest possible setting. This is a shame, but it hardly ruins the game and hopefully it will be fixed in the future.

PROS
  1. Real ACTUAL gunplay.
    How do you reload a revolver in hover junkers? By pressing a button? HAVE YOU LOST YOUR MIND!? NO. This is VR, we're living in the future. The act of reloading your weapon in hover junkers is an active process which involves you opening the chamber of the revolver and inserting bullets by moving your thumb around the touchpad. GENIUS, absolute genius. This is incredibly intuative and adds an extra dimension to combat. There's no UI so you'll find yourself thinking things like "did i fire five or six bullets?" or "how fast can i reload this gun?". As time passes you'll get better at micromanaging your weapons and let me tell you. Shooting at an enemy with one hand and reloading with your other is about the most satisfying thing I have ever done in a videogame.

  2. The most immersive and exciting multiplayer you will ever play.
    Sure there are better, and probably more balanced online multiplayer games, but let me ask you this. Do you have to physically duck for cover in Team Fortress? Can you literally wave your arms at a teammate in Counter Strike? Or are you required to literally aim and reload a weapon in Call of Duty? I think not. Don't watch gameplay videos of hover junkers, they don't do it justice. You have to experience this to fully appreciate the extend of how fun this game is.

  3. The best locomotion in Virtual Reality to date.
    The way you move around in hover junkers is as simplistic as it is utter â¥â¥â¥â¥ing brilliant. You're on a platform that represents your playspace. You move your playspace around by pointing in the direction you want to go and pressing the trigger. This has caused me no nausea or simulation sickness. It's a fun and intuative system! NEXT!

  4. The interaction between players.
    It is a ton of fun to play cooperatively with people and coordinate attacks with your teammates (who have been very nice thus far), especially if they're on your ship. But that's not even the best of it. What really takes the cake, what makes killing people in this game so damn enjoyable, is that I know for a fact that during gunfights there's a sweating, scared, fellow man, laying flat on his livingroom floor somewhere on the planet, prayning that I don't get a good angle to ram 3 good revolver shots up his â¥â¥â¥ and send his virtual coil to scorescreen heaven... No other game will give you that feeling.

It may be one of the best games I have played, period. It's one of the most polished, well made and downright fun experiences I have ever had in a video game. I applaud the developers of this game and i'm more than excited for future content.

BUY IT, you will not regret it. And if you do, it can be refunded... you weirdo.

Posted: 26 June
As of right now, this game is just too repetitive. There are few lobbies (though I blame that more on the low number of VR users more than anything else), and though ducking behind cover and exchanging gunfire with someone is an awesome thing to experience, it's only fun for so long.

Every exchange is basically the same. You fly up and fumble around for a bit because vehicles just tend to slide around when they get close, then you take a couple seconds to look for the person or get killed in the process. Ducking behind cover seems to do nothing since most cover is full of gaps that can be exploited by other players. While fighting with someone else, it's easy to get interrupted by someone driving up and killing you from behind, and because you always have a thick cable running from behind your head, it's difficult to turn and move around quickly without risking ripping the cables from your computer.

There's a few other things that really, really bug me about this game. There's no sort of feedback when you shoot people except for a small spray of blood that is easily missed, so you're basically just left spraying enemies blindly until they die. Maps are also HUGE. It takes at least 20 seconds of flying around each time you spawn before you can even find someone, even in a full lobby. The large maps allow chances for you to collect "loot" (basically pieces of cover lying in crates around the map) without being destroyed immediately, but it's almost never worth it to go after this loot since, as I said, the cover is easily exploited by enemies and looting really slows down the gameplay. The recoil on the guns is also insane. Rapid fire simply doesn't work in this game, even at close range. Perhaps most aggrevating of all is how every lobby has one or two guys who are completely dominating the other players, and I mean really dominating. You could argue that all you have to do to counter this is get good at the game or find that rare, precious lobby where everyone is a new player, but the gameplay is just isn't fun enough to invest the time.

Like I said, the first time experience for this game is truly awesome, but I just can't see this game being fun long-term. I have a lot of respect for the developers since they took a small indie project and turned it into a flagship title for VR, and I can tell from their YouTube channel that they worked very hard to make this game great. Even so, $35 for this game is robbery, and even at $25 on sale this game seems grossly overpriced. The developers just added a new PvE mode and are likely to add more content in the future, but right now, at its current price, the game just doesn't seem worth it. In the world of VR where almost everything is more expensive than it should be, there are still better VR games out there for your money, even multiplayer VR games.
