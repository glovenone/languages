Update 2.1.0 â Now available!

Update 2.1.0 is now available for download! Please see the changes below.

General
  • Starting with this patch, only 64-bit operation systems will take advantage of future improvements
  • Various performance and stability improvements. Thanks to all users sending us crash reports and informing our support via https://www.world-of-emergency.com/support about problems found.
Graphics
  • Windows: Replaced the DirectX 9 renderer with a DirectX 11 renderer
  • The available display resolutions are no longer limited to the operation system display resolution in order to support GPU driver supersampling techniques
  • Fixed a rarely triggering bug causing fire lighting staying active after the fire went out
  • Visual polishing
    • Fixed overlay bars and icons being updated one frame too late resulting in a swimming overlays effect
    • The animation for falling trees is now smoother
    • SWAT team members leaving the helicopter via row is now smoother
    • The helicopter door of the rescue helicopter was closing too early resulting in a visible clipping with the paramedic team
    • No longer active contamination clouds now fade out smoothly
Gameplay
  • Fix for hostage taking event being stuck in case the gangsters give up
  • Added support for second speaker voice (command and selection feedback for player units)
  • Fixed a bug that local highscores got lost
  • Fixed traffic light issues inside the Hamburg and Berlin map

Editor
  • Multi-monitor support: With the DirectX 11 renderer, the main window can now be moved to other monitors
  • Added a dialog for local asset datasources which can be found inside the customize menu bar entry
  • Inside the map properties dialog, the create mini-map button now opens a dialog with additional options

Modding / SDK
  • Similar to the building clipping effect, the unit shine through effect can now also be disabled by editing the game configuration file
  • Added more EM5_API_EXPORTs for better accessibility for plugin modders
  • Preview and loading screen support for custom maps (using file naming "em5/gui/asset/map_preview/<mapname>_preview.dds" and "em5/gui/loading/loadingscreen_<mapname>.dds")
  • Custom maps can now also be used for multiplayer modes

Additional Hints
  • Users of 32-bit and 64-bit operation systems will play on separate servers without the need to change any settings
  • Please note that the used graphics adapter need DirectX 11 support and as a result EMERGENCY 5/2016 will no longer run on systems below the minimum requirements of 1 GB VRAM, NVIDIA GeForce 400 series or AMD Radeon HD 6000 series

About This Game

Infernos, spring floods, terrorism: There's no shortage of action in EMERGENCY 2016.

If you always dreamed of commanding firefighters, the police, technical relief forces and paramedics as head of operations, you're the one we've been looking for! The new medieval map takes you to Cologne, where the plague ravages the city in the shadow of the new cathedral. As a plague doctor, you attempt to stem the epidemic with remedies available at the time. Centuries later, the plague strikes again and you must fight it in the metropolises of our time in a new campaign. In the course of the extensive campaign, minor accidents alternate with challenging major events, ensuring hours of engrossing gameplay. Before starting, you can complete the integrated tutorial, which teaches the game's basic mechanics and functions.

  • Face numerous unanticipated situations in tense major operations underway in the metropolises Berlin, Cologne, Munich and Hamburg.

  • Use the powerful editor to create your very own Emergency and work in parallel on a shared project together with your friends online.

  • Command over 20 unique vehicles from the police, fire brigade, rescue service and technical relief forces.

  • Includes all content from Emergency 5 Deluxe in the latest Reloaded version + New Emergency Vehicle + Medieval Operation + New Campaign

As someone who works in the emergency medical field, I really appreciate and enjoy this game. In fact, all this week after each of my long shifts on the ambulance, I've been coming home to play this; actually looking forward to it. The game isn't without faults, but I'll list each fault for you, as well as each positive point.

The Good:
+The graphics are nice and for the most part, I can get 60 fps (unless there are six emergencies going simultaneously).
+The animations are pretty sweet. For example, while checking patients, the doctor has several animations depending on the injuries...he will take out his stethescope for a sick person, start a fluid bolus on a trauma patient, and take out his pen light to asses the pupils of a patient with a head injury. Sometimes I just stop what I'm doing to watch the doctor.
+Additional content compared to the other games.
+Some pretty wicked calls to go to, including: hostage situations, bomb threats, robberies, medical emergencies, mva's, gas explosions, forest fires, building collapses, protests, missing persons...you get the point.
+Day/night cycle. The weather also changes..so far I've only seen light rain and rarely a thunderstorm.
+A.I. seem to be improved from previous games.
+Level editor, although it's not too user friendly..unless you're familiar with SDKs, which I'm not.

The Bad:
-As mentioned by others, the tracking issues are still present, but for me they haven't been too bad. The worst issue I had was with the search and rescue K9 vehicle making a loop around the block to get to an area literally 20ft away from the truck. And after I gave up on the missing persons mission, I sent it back to base, and it drove off the road and got stuck. That was frustrating, but in the end, it didn't break the game for me.
-So far I've had two unexplained crashes while playing free play (I've submitted bug reports).
-Minor glitches here and there.
-Pedestrian A.I. seem to be pretty stupid. For example, if there is a fire in a building next to a sidewalk, the pedestrians will walk right next to it and sometimes can injure themselves.
-I never purchased EM5 (the negative reviews scared me away), but from what I've seen and heard, this is basically a DLC and should not be marketed as a new stand-alone game. It's pretty much a re-skin.
-Pricey for what you get.

Conclusion:
When all is said and done, I can personally look past the glitches. I enjoy playing the game and find it to be relaxing. I also understand why some people are â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ off at the marketing of this game. Hopefully this game doesn't go the route of Train Simulator "2016" where every year the devs release a "new" game with <insert year here> in the title, when it's essentially the same game. But for someone like me who owns "911:First Responders" but nothing after that, this game might be worth it for you. I was blown away at the improvements between the original game and the "new" one. Anyway, I would suggest doing your research before purchasing, but I'd also suggest not letting the negative reviews scare you away from purchasing it all together (maybe wait for a sale if you don't think it's worth the money). If you're interested in the 911 system, it's pretty much the only game I've been able to find that does it any justice (and even then, it isn't as realistic as it could be). Give it a shot!
