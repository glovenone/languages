About This Game

Experience incredibly atmospheric and intense medieval combat action!
Go ahead and enter a new world, the award-winning world of ENCLAVE!

The people of Light and Darkness are divided by a bottomless rift that split the earth many millennia ago. The lands of light are an Enclave of truth and order surrounded by the twisted and barren lands of the dark, known as the Outlands. Over the centuries the rift has started to close…

And now it is only a matter of time before the forces of Light and Darkness will clash in an unprecedented, merciless struggle for survival!
Remember... When a storm comes up, for some it’s better to keep out of its way… while for others it’s time to block its path!

FEATURES:

  • Breathtaking combat action
  • Amazing 3D graphics with latest Shader technolgy support
  • 2 separate story driven single player campaigns: Light and Dark Campaign
  • 25+ highly detailed fantasy themed missions
  • Secret bonus games
  • 12 different playable character classes
  • 5 mighty end bosses
  • Epic musical score and impressive sound FX

OK, first of all, this game is not really an RPG. I know it reads up there on the genre description here on Steam, but this game is more of an Action RPG then anything else.

So, basically you enter this world where humans are fighting against demons and vice versa. In your first playthrough you start playing on the Light campaign, as one of the humans. The controls function like any other RPG, except here the combat is more fast paced. You don't go around and do quests and there is very little dialog. What you do is basically kill everything you see, and collect as much gold as you can. With the gold you collect, you can equip better weapons, armor, and bring more potions with you each time. There are quite a lot of races/classes that you gain as you play (some by completing levels, and some are special classes that you get after collecting all of the gold in the game).

The game itself is fun. It's obviously not a modern game. The graphics are dated, but they're not ugly or anything. The soundtrack is lacking, with only a few songs that you keep hearing over and over, but hey, at least they're good. The gameplay is simple and intuitive, but I have to warn you that the hitboxes are very small, and the game can be a bit difficult sometimes. I finished the game on medium but even then I had to replay most levels quite a few times. After you finish the game for the first time, you can play the game now on the Dark campaign and get a different perspective.

Even though the game is from 2003 its It puts a lot of new games to shame. It's challenging, fun, and has a lot of content.
I recommend.
