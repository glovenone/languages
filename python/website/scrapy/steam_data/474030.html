About This Game

A free game inspired by Umberto Eco's novel and Jean-Jaques Annaud's film "The Name of the Rose", and based on the game "La abadía del crimen" by Paco Menéndez and Juan Delcán.



Don’t follow several and odd sophistical doctrines that spread pestiferous seeds – wrote Clement VI himself in the year 1346 to the masters and alumni from Paris University.

The Pontifex addressed in his letters to the doctrine of an illustrious Franciscan monk of English origin named William of Occam, who ran away several years earlier from the prison of Avignon, accused of heresy. He had been imprisoned for four years, until he could escape, and shought asylum in the court of Emperor Ludwig of Bavaria, who was at odds with Pope John XXII.

When the Emperor died, William wanted to return to the mission with his order and the church, so he traveled to Italy with his young novice to meet Bernardo Gui, who was the head of the Inquisition at that time in that region, and, ultimately, the representative of the Pope.




However we cannot know for certain whether William achieved his goal. Only the testimony left by his disciple years after the events happened, in a collection of scrolls; where those amazing and terrible events that happened during the week William and him spent in the abbey the meeting was scheduled were described.

The testimony of the story you are about to revive…





In this game you play as a Franciscan, Fray William of Occam and his young novice Adso of Melk on a quest to discover the perpetrator of a series of murders in a Benedictine abbey. The entire game is played in an isometric 3D environment, with a series of objects that has to be collected in order to successfully complete the game.

That said, if your experience is anything like mine prepare for a lot of confusion in the first hour or so as you try to find your way around the massive monastery (easier said than done with the crazy camera switching), the strange rites you need to attend (you'll see what i mean - tip: just follow the other monks and read everything CAREFULLY) and the strange control scheme.

Also, save often. ;)

The perfect remake for the old spanish classic. It takes the good things of the classic game from the 87 and improves them, and fixes the bad things (such as the horrible difficulty). it's also free, so I'd say it is a must have.
It still suffers from being an adventure game, and as such it features a lot of walking, but the clues are common enough that you don't need a guide that much, and the story and atmosphere are good enough to keep you hooked up until the end (it is much better than in the original game too, which didn't really go too deep into the story of the book and film because of the technical limitations of the spectrum computers).
Also props for the protagonist to be Sean Connery this time around. Even if it's less faithful to the book, he certainly looks less generic, and stands out a lot more in the crowd of holly (and not so holly) men that roam around the Abbey.

Abbey of Crime Extensum is an atmospheric remake of an old game I had never heard of before. It's pretty nice game over all, yet not without it's problems. There's a lot of walking. The main gameplay consists of walking to the right place at the right time to speak with the right character or get an item. And the character walks fairly slowly. On top of that, the monastery the game takes place in is rather non-euclidean as whenever you move from one screen to another the angel changes in a confusing way. This takes a lot of getting used to. Fortunately you can find a map to help you a bit.

Despite these flaws, I recommend Abbey of Crime Extensum, as it is worth playing if you can get over these things. The story and the atmosphere are quite good. The graphics are excellent too. Plus, it's free. It is a slow-moving old school game.
