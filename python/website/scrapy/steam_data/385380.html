Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We wish to improve the game by collaborating with the community, and for this we need everyone be able to access the game. Even if the game is currently stable and includes lots of features allowing players to have hours of fun, it won't compare with what Planet Centauri will be during its final release.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“A little more than a year. This may seem a bit long, but we promise the huge amount of upcoming features will be worth the wait!”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Here is the list of the features that we still need to implement:

  • Themed dungeons | Biomes: post-hell | Biomes: sky | Technology | Vehicles & Mechs
  • Grapple hook / ninja rope | Modding compatibility | God Saucer (minigames area)
  • Tower of 100 challenges | Themed parallel dimensions | Fluid dynamics
  • Biomes: oceans | Fishing | Programmable robots | Wiring | Pipes | Thunder
  • Fire (buildings can catch on fire etc.) | Achievements / Game encyclopedia / Compendium
  • Temperature | Gas dynamics | Dynamic buildings (build your ship)
  • Space exploration & combat | Exoplanets
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We currently are in version v0.5.5, which includes the following features:
  • Monster/Weapon/Armor/Rune rarity levels
  • Custom spell creation
  • Player to monster transformation
  • Capturing and summoning monsters
  • Quests
  • Agriculture and farming
  • Create your village and give the villagers jobs
”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Absolutely not! But only people buying the game during Early Access will get the Phoenix pet, which can transform into a mythical phoenix blade.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are always listening to the community and player requests concerning upcoming features are the highest priorities! We also answer every question on the forums or social media and read all the comments. Feel free to write us a message if you have any questions!”

About This Game

Embark on your greatest adventure to Planet Centauri! Discover new biomes, explore hidden dungeons, capture and tame monsters, build and protect a NPC community, craft your own weapons and create your own magic spells! Save and protect the native Chlorinian species from the evil Night Walkers! Develop and raise the Planet Centauri to the heavens as the cradle of a new civilization!

Catch them all! Capture and tame monsters!


Your new companions will support you and will evolve to become stronger! Each captured creature keeps its unique features, evolving as it gains experience fighting by your side.

Suffixes, characteristics and runes


  • Use runes to enchant your equipment with various stats and effects!
  • Weapons and armor share 7 levels of rarity! Higher rarity always coming with stronger, and more unique effects and characteristics!
  • Monsters also happen to have rarity levels, rarer monsters being much more powerful and dangerous than their common counterparts, with unique abilities and reinforced stats.

Craft your own magic spells!


From controlling the arc and pattern of your fireballs to simply enhancing your jumping abilities, craft a plethora of magical effects to fit your desires.

Agriculture and Cooking


Eat well to get stronger has never been a more truthful quote than in Planet Centauri. Eating various types of food will cause your character to grow stronger and develop his abilities.

Build a village and assign roles and jobs to its inhabitants


Save the native Chlorinians, build and fortify their villages. Befriend them and grant them jobs as hunters, tailors, guards, wizards, researchers and much more!

Programmable robots


Thanks to the LUA script, you can easily program custom AIs and behaviors for your robots and villagers! Imagination is your only limit! Mining, hunting, defense, exploration, whatever you want!

Create without limits with our unique network system!


Planet Centauri's network system allows you to design various electronic systems, from humble automated house lighting to frantic death lasers! This is achieved through a large number of electric and electronic components, including : - generators, power wires, resistors, capacitors - noise, serrated, square and sinusoidal signal generators - up to 16 bit information signal, with a large variety of mathematical and binary operators!

Haven't played the game too long yet but I can tell I'm going to love it, as a fan of Terraria and Starbound. Almost everything is VERY similar to Terraria. The artstyle, even the menus and character/world creation when starting up are almost identical.

Unless something drastically changes in a negative way, I highly recommend this game. Seems well worth the 15 dollars they're asking even in it's early access state. Will update review with any new things I find significant.

Posted: 8 June
I went into this game as a big fan of Starbound and Terraria. Games much like this one. I expected it to be pretty copy/paste, but not in a bad way. And while I was right there, it does plenty to differentiate itself from those two aforementioned games. In those games, as much as I love them, they're relatively shallow compared to Planet Centauri. A lot to do, but not too many ways to really optimize how you go about it. A few potions and an arena in those games and you're all set.

This game is quite the opposite. Not too much to do, but so many ways to go about it! The customizable magic is intimidating at first, but it is really worthwhile, the monster capturing and animal morphing work well, taking the best from games like Pokemon and such to make it feel like something that can only benefit you.

Don't let the trailer fool you about it's current state. I'm 99% sure the trailers show off a future version of the game. Possibly an unstable build. Most of the things you see there are not present in the actual game at this time.

I said there wasn't much to do, and I meant it. Very few skills, the worlds are small, the "customizable" magic has several options that are visible to the player but not implemented yet... or there's something about it I don't understand. And weapon types are few and far between. The skills you can aquire are few in number as well, and not too effective. the Transformations I spoke of are, while useful, very limited in number. In terms of the magic, that's been shown to be fixed very soon from some of the trailers.

All those issues are likely to vanish with time. I made this review less than a week after the game's release, so I have no doubt that the issues involving lack of contect will be fixed in time.

A few issues I have that might not be adressed with time are that a) it doesn't seem to stray too far from typical fantasy material. Giant talking crystal, check. Zombies, check. Worms, check. Skeletons, Check. Slimes, check blah blah blah. Not easy to do, but it needs to find it's own style late into the game.

Incredibly basic progression. Copper/Tin to Bronze to Iron to Steel to silver to gold. No variation on that. That's... all there is to say.

The inventory system and hotbar are VERY unintuitive going into it. I couldn't figure out how it worked for two hours before I started being able to use the left mouse button.

Building, even worse. I won't even start. There's room to make something amazing with it, but it's so time comsuming to even make something acceptable to the human eye.

Last and biggest problem for me is that the difficulty it reaches doesn't line up to the power you can attain. Or maybe I just suck, but it really does feel unfair with equipment I currently have. Especially that purple dungeon.

I will sing it's praises though. It's incredibly well polished in it's animations and it has a great visual style. It is only sprites, but they're FANTASTIC sprites. Enemy variations keep things fresh and challenging, everything has a nice feel to it but the double jump.

In short, it's nothing special now, but good GOD there is SO much potential here. Click follow on this game to keep up to date because I'm willing to bet money that these'll be patch notes you NEED to read. This game is going to be fantastic, but for now, I feel like it was released a bit early. Maybe two patches into the future it'll be in a state where release would have been better saved for. Don't buy it now, it's nothing special for now, but keep your eye on it, wait a few months or a year, and it's gonna be one hell of a good time.

Early Access Review
Posted: 22 June
Here's a quick review of Planet Centauri:

Pros:

- Expanding on the crafting/exploring/sidescrolling/building category in a good way.
- Nice visuals all around
- Smooth functionality

Needs work with:

- Not a large amount of content at the moment. You'll probably get about 15-20 hours of playability when gameplay slows down.

- Multiplayer is not yet implemented. (On the way)

- Inventory can be difficult to understand.


Overall, the game is fun and there's lots of opportunity to grow. Developers seem active with the community. Just needs more content. I would warn against purchasing right now solely for this reason. But if you are interested in getting in on a promising game from the get go, this could be a good purchase.
