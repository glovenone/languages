Bumpy launch (Solutions)

Greetings Users.

We're in the middle of trying to sort out a crash bug that many are encountering. While we try to find a permanent solution, if you go to the instalation folder through Windows Explorer and run the Technolust.exe file (or create a shortcut on your desktop) everything should run without a problem.

The issue appears to only occur when launching the game through Steam.

On a side note, there is an option to disable snap turning directly behind your forward direction on both the title screen and M.U.R.C. menus.

You can turn "smooth turning" by selecting that option.

Thank you for your understanding during this early launch phase.

Blair "Anticleric" Renaud
IRIS VR Inc.

Posted: 5 June
If you enjoyed Blade Runner, Total Recall or Johnny Mnemonic, you'll probably like Technolust. It has incredible music and great environments that really make you feel like you're trapped in a 90s vision of a dystopian future.

Before playing this game, I've got to warn you: It's not action-packed (except in the arcade minigames), and it won't hold your hands. It's an exploration game, and while it's possible to sprint to the ending in relatively little time, you're going to have a lot more fun if you slow down and try everything in the environment. There's a reason the world is full of collectables, arcade games, TVs and advertising - Technolust is about getting you immersed, and it does an exceptional job at that.

It's the first first-person VR game I've played that hasn't given me sudden motion sickness, and there is even a "cloudwalk" mode that further reduced camera movement for when I felt a bit out of place.

Iam a huge fan of cyber steampunk 80s stuff and I bought that game because it promised me to dive in such a world.

And it delivers. I can fully recommend this game if you you are in search for cyber steampunk feeling.



but there are some points you need to know:


1. you still cant start the game from steam because it with crash - but the workaround works (start the .exe file directly).

2. the VR experience is incomplete.
You can move ingame by moving in real life (oculus roomscale works fine) - if you have a large enough room, long enough cables and no problem with breaking your nose when running against a wall or strangle yourself with the cables. after a while i gave up and sat down and moved around with the xbox controller (no sickness issues for me).

Also you interact with the gameworld by looking at something and press a button on your x-box controller. that feels more like a point&click game, not like a complete VR experience. i think you need the touch controller and a simulation of your hand position for this type of game.

3. the difficulty level... well there is no difficulty. you just advance through the game. it doesnt have to be a bad thing. I have played all 3 Shadowrun games and they too had no difficulty level at all and they are among my favorite games.

4. It is a Indie game and with it come some limitations. nothing that reduce the overall experience, but dont expect a multi million dollar aaa title when you pay 20â¬/$. the game is very strong in delivering a cyber steampunk feeling.

5. all VR games seem to be pretty "flat" when it comes to game mechanics and story telling. they all feel like someone had a cool idea and made a techdemo and then tried to build a game around that techdemo. all VR games i have played so far are very polished and with high amount of nice little details everywhere, just with no depth. Technolust is no exception.
I like it and i would probably buy a sequal, but if i compare it with a 20â¬ shadowrun game, there is so much more gameplay in flat games of the same price class. its almost like all the budget is spent for researching VR possibilitys and building the enviroment and nothing is left for creating content.
