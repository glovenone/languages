If win '95 puzzle games had Worms style Ninja ropes, It wouldn't be far off from Tinboy's style. The music and atmosphere may be minimalist, but the logic is grand. Great game or kiddos. Positive learning and progressive challenges without any dialogue means that kids who are preliterate can enjoy this game as much as adults. The music sounds like it was sampled off of a long lost Gameboy cartridge, reminding me of Koichi Sugiyama's work in Dragon Warrior III, if more simplistic, still wonderful and humming in my head. This game has potential.

I haven't seen much 2D puzzle platformers to be honest. Basing it on the screenshots it just seems like another childish flash platformer but the quirky mechanics and level design open possibilities for advanced and difficult levels. There are some minor annoying things though. Adding a couple of simple tunes to the game won't hurt :) It will make the game a tiny bit enjoyable.

Customization for the controls won't be bad. I don't think changing that can be a big problem.

I suggest the developer the make an update of the game based on feedback. The game can be made extremely fun to play.
