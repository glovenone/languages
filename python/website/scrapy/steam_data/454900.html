About This Game

Amaranthine - is a dynamic horizontal scrolling shooter with elements of procedural generation. Travel through warp to the distant frozen planet that sent a distress signal.

Features:

  • Random level generation
  • Loot system
  • Precise mouse controls
  • Big variety of enemies
  • Legend mode with permanent death
  • Endless gameplay

Good idea, but falls down in a couple of key places.

1) The loot variation is not nearly interesting enough, and the weapons are not different enough to matter. It becomes just getting the biggest number, no thought to playstyle.

2) The actual game is very frustrating. The enemies that use fast/hitscan laser weapons will do a warmup cycle that shows their thing warming up, and most of the ships will give some kind of indication that they're about to do their (very rapid) attack. So, you want to keep your eyes predominantly on the playfield watching for incoming threats. The *problem* is that the bullets and attacks tend to be very unpredictable, so you need to watch what's going on around the ship. The experience of having to need to look in two places at once to avoid damage results in the experience of watching the playfield, only to have unpredictable bullet sprays and other ships run into yours, and then moving your eye over there and being hit by rapid attacks that you couldn't easily anticipate.

Good shooters are built on memorization of patterns. Even randomized shooters like Steredenn (which I'd recommend instead of this game) give you enemies that are predictable, so you can take advantage of muscle memory. This game is just flailing around with no pattern, so you never have anything to build off of.

Audio and graphics is ok for an indie game. Good music, and sound effects are as they should be. One of the tunes doesn't loop very well. But that's a minor issue. I feel there could be a bit more varied graphics in different backgrounds and such. As it is now, each level feels the same. Just harder mobs.

I was surprised that it was mouse controlled. I haven't played a mouse-shooter since the Amiga days. And this one utilizes the mouse 100%. No lag, and you feel the ship flies where you want it.

As for content, the game tends to be a bit repetetive. But the developer doesn't drag it on forever for us that want to complete it once. I beat the boss on easiest difficulty after 1,5 hour. I had to farm a little bit to get my shields up to 1000. But then I had kinda learned how to avoid the different attacks. Still good to have a buffer. Good thing you didn't have to start all over when you died, as the boss was one of the most difficult encounters I've battled in a long while.

I was a bit dissapointed of the gear though. All the guns fire forward, and there is no difference in how they fire. I boils down to just get the guns that has the highest dps you can fit with your power. It's like the loot of borderlands, but with less detail. And it's kinda awkward that even the bullets follow your ship vertically instead of going straight forward. But that's just nitpicking.

Will this last, then? I doubt I will play it much more after this. The final Achiements are winning on legend and getting to level 20 on legend. I guess that is doable with a lot of grinding and careful play, but I feel this isn't worth my time. But others may try getting further and further. If only the weapons and levels were a bit more diverse... The high-score attack in the "Elite" game is fun a couple of times, but nothing more.

Finally, was it worth my money? I got it 50% off, and it was enganging for 1,5 hour. I would even say it's worth picking up at full price.

Product received for free
Amaranthine is very nice little SHMUP.
It has pretty graphics, great soundtrack and simple, but fun gameplay.

Since it's SHMUP, it is played by shooting enemy ships and avoiding their bullets.
Controls are very simple, hold left button to shoot and move mouse around to dodge bullets.

There are a lot of different enemies in Amaranthine and they are thrown at player randomly.
To make it less monotonous - if you find dodging hundreds of projectiles and turning enemies into explosions monotonous - there's few well made random events.

There's also neat spaceship upgrading system.
Spaceship can be equipped with three weapons, to be equipped weapons require energy, which can be increased by spending alloy - currency of the game.
Alloy is obtained by destroying enemies and selling weapons.

Weapons are received from drops.
If I remember right, there's six weapon types and they come in three tiers - common, rare and legendary.
Higher tier weapons deal more damage.
Every weapon is unique, but it doesn't matter that much.
Still, it's fun to collect weapons.

Shield, that is spaceship's health, can also be upgraded.
Since it is possible to replay levels and keep upgrading the shield, that makes game rather easy.
But that doesn't ruin the experience.

Story-wise, there's short skip-able cutscenes in beginning and end of the game.
I find ending cutscene satisfying.

I'm glad I played Amaranthine.
I recommend buying it, if you want few hours of good time.

Bonus fact: Amaranthine means purplish-red or everlasting.

PS.: Sorry for grammar!

Review copy provided from developer. Note that my ability to enjoy a game is not influenced by how I recieved it. If you would like to see the TL;DR, go to the summary at the bottom of the review.

Amaranthine
is a side-scrolling shoot 'em up consisting of you, a spaceship commander, blasting the metal brains out of enemy space vessels.

The premise is something along the lines of a distant frozen planet that has sent out a distress signal, and you having to investigate. There is a fairly brief explanation of this at the start which plays each time you start a new game. (I'll just add here as a tip: to skip the cutscene and get straight to the action, simply press the escape button.) This brief explanation is perfect because it makes the story simple and unobtrusive, whilst at the same time giving the game a purpose.

Once you are ready to obliterate some enemy ships you will be plunged straight into the action. Each level is randomly generated to add more replayability to the the gameplay. This gameplay is a great lot of fun, consisting of you traveling through an endless amount of (surprisingly difficult) levels with a variety of enemies, weapons and environmental hazards. For many shmups, this would be the end of the list of gameplay features. However, Amaranthine has an interesting weapon upgrade/loot system. During each level you can collect alloys from the destroyed ships which you then use to upgrade your ship in between levels. You are also given the chance to collect weapons from other destroyed ships which you can then use to place one of the three weapon spots your ship has. I've seen some people complaining about the sensitivity of the mouse, however this sensitivity is required (especially in later levels) to perform precise manoeuvres.

The pixelated graphics suit the style of game and are pleasing to look at as well as the backgrounds, though they do start to get a bit monotonous. I would suggest that some different styled backgrounds be introduced.

Regarding music and sounds, one word: majestic. These sounds, both soothing and heart-pumping are the reason this game is tagged with Great Soundtrack. Take a listen here.

The game is a great deal for the low price of $2.99. The cost is definitely not overpriced and respects the content of the game. This would be something that I couldn't excuse not buying in a sale.

Any other points regarding the game would be the lack of options. An ability to change the mouse sensitivity may stop the complaints coming through.

Summary:

Pros:
✔ An unobtrusive story
✔ Fun gameplay
✔ Great upgrade system
✔ Nice visuals
✔ Majestic music
✔ Low price

Cons:
✘ Backgrounds can get monotonous
✘ Lack of options

Conclusion:
Amaranthine is a great, simple game and definitely one of the better SHMUPs you can get your hands on. For its low price, I'd heavily recommend picking it up especially if you are a fan of the genre.

Rating: 8/10
