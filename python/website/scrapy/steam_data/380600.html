What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The universe of sportfishing is practically limitless. And as our team is aiming to deliver the most realistic and fun sportfishing experience, we want to use Early Access to get feedback from as many gamers and sportfishing enthusiasts as possible on the earliest stages of our project. We need to know whether you like how the rod bends and if the walleye realistically strikes the lure as it falls, does the twitching retrieve work the way you're used to in real life and tons of other details that are the core of the perfect fishing simulator.
After all, we are making a game that would allow you to use your real-life fishing skills and experience, and for that, want you to be a part of our creation and help us shape this brave new fishing world! All your feedback and opinions count and can make a real difference. Together we will turn Fishing Planet into a Universe of angling excitement and bring this wonderful hobby (perhaps even way of life) to the masses!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Our main priority is to ensure uncompromised realism of the fishing experience combined with a fun and exciting gameplay, so we intend to use Early Access to polish everything out by fixing any bugs and introducing new features with the help of your feedback. That should take us approximately four months, with a full release planned for November 2015.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“While in Early Access, we plan to introduce a number of features to the full version of the game, including:

  • Multiplayer action â online tournaments with personal and team scores, clubs and series.

  • Quests

  • Kayaks and Boats

  • Huge real-life lakes

  • 10+ fish species
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently the game boasts following features:

  • 32 game fish species, each with its unique behaviors and AI;

  • 7 scenic North American waterways with multiple locations, various climatic conditions, bottom terrains and vegetation;

  • Thousands of tackle combinations available in the game shop. Each with unique physical and hydrodynamic properties; About 300 various tackle and equipment items in our game tackle stores.

  • Superb dynamic water graphics with ripples and surface that changes depending on wind, current and depth;

  • Weather â day/night cycle, change of seasons, different weather conditions (rain, fog, bright sunshine);

  • Achievements, leader-boards and player top lists.
”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game will stay Free to Play but the starter-packs and in-game purchases will cost less in Early Access, in comparison to the final full-version retail prices.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We will have an intense and fruitful Forum interaction between the development team and the Community as well as weekly Twitch Q&A sessions that help us gather ideas, feedback, and any possible bug issues.
We perceive the Fishing Planets Early Access community as one big family that is helping us shape the gameâs evolution and prioritize the development process by giving their votes in support of certain features and ideas. Together we're making this game to let everyone enjoy the excitement of fishing anywhere and anytime!
Join us in our Fishing Planet universe!

Tight Lines!”

About This Game

Fishing Planet is a unique and highly realistic online first-person multiplayer fishing simulator developed by avid fishing enthusiasts for anglers to bring you the full thrill of actual angling! Choose your lures, make your trophy catches, discover new possibilities and sharpen your real angling skills anywhere, anytime with your friends!

The game is FREE to play and just a download away!

Aside from a rare few, there were no decent fishing games created over the whole 30-year history of game development. And not a single one in recent years! Donât we anglers deserve better than that?

Fishing Planet decided to set this historic injustice right and finally make a game that fishing enthusiasts were waiting for all these years! And according to our Beta Testers, we hit the mark!

Stunning eye-candy graphics, superb hydro and aerodynamics, realistic tackle behavior and damage based on actual simulation physics and, most importantly, fish with artificial intelligence for completely lifelike behavior make this a true-to-life fishing simulator that can help you improve your actual angling skills!

I would not recondmend this game. The game has some redeeming qualities but since I am not endorsing this game I won't mention those few items.

1) Yes it is free to play, at certain levels though it is pay to win because if you want in a tourny you have to be lvl 17+ in order to have access to tackle that will catch the target fish or a fish keeper big enough that will let you be competitive, So until your high enough level to get those items it is pay to win IF your interested in tourny's.

2) Fish mechanics are horrible. Everything from how a fish strikes to the mechanics of the catch are pretty bad. When the game started there was a huge cheat inbedded in the mechanic that allowed for almost instant catches, ever since then they have been trying to "fix" it but it has never really gotten there, they should have scrapped the whole thing and started over.

3) Super grindy (understandable to a point) but the game squeezes you into certain lakes ao it basically spend all your time for a big group of levels at one lake then when old enough move on to another....The worse thing is they DO NOT tell you any info but you not being old enough for a lake equipment wise. I lost over 6 hrs of play (making money) only to pay the extra to "move" to a new lake and find out that I couldn't catch anything worth while because I was not old enough to buy the "magical" bait that only the fish in this lake will strike lol..

4) Going back to the mechanics...the fish are all caught in "spots" Yep, spots, no wandering fish no moving targets and although fish in RL are often caught in "spots" only when those spots are active ( ie that special cove you are lucky at alot is because the wind blows often into the cove, increasing the food chain and the fish will move into the cove following the increased food chain, when wind doesn't blow into cove, no fish in cove) So it would have been far more realistic to have either multiple spots that change because of weather except there is no real changing of the weather( you have 2 weathers rain and no rain) OR just have fish wander all over and not have any stupid spots since they couldn't realistically simulate the other aspects of fishing like weather and water conditions.

5) Now to squeeze out more money they have an equipment damage, destroy and repair system. Which is horribly stupid. I have been fishing with the same 3 reels and 3 rods for 25+ years ( I am in my 40's) I understand they need to get paid this is thier job BUT... Picture this... you pay 250 in game currency to do a few days of fishing somewhere. You forgot to repair your pole or lets say you want to goto new york and fish pike which will eat your pole alive. It is possible to end up destroying your pole before your trip is done, wasting all kinds of money. Now yea it would be the stupid players fault for not paying attention but moments like that should never happen in a game....NEVER...and no it did not happen to me but was the first thing I thought of when I played with the damage system for the first time.

6) The game is full of "non-explanatory" moments. No in game pop ups or stuff to help keep a player from making mistakes like going to a fishing hole too early in level or joining a tourny that your too young to have the "tools" to compete. You WILL eventually waste money on something and have a "wtf, nice of you to tell me" moment. How uch time/money wasted will be different but sooner or later you WILL make a mistake somewhere, even if it is buying a lure that won't fit a pole...(yes only certain lures on certain poles)

7) Which then brings me tooo...certain poles/reels ect.... This is a major say whaaaaat moment. For starters you can only bobber fish from a "special" pole which makes me giggle becuase NO ONE uses these poles in RL. They are alot like the old style bamboo poles as kids we would use in that they are really really long. But these things have reels. Basically the whole idea that I cannot take 2 poles (one 6 1/2 foot spinning set up and one 7 1/2 foot casting set up and fish with that everything and use every type of lure/bobber ect is just re-tarded. Other than special things like here in Oregon fishing pike or salmon you need steel leaders, salmon you need a very stout pole (think deep sea size pole). But other than a few specialty fish you can fish for over 30 "game" fish in this country using the same two poles I mentioned above.

I still play every once in awhile to scratch an itch but casting in the same spot 150 times in a two hour period is just not fun. Not using my brain to figure out where the fish might be, is not that much fun. So when I do play its usually about 15-30 mintes because after that I want to hang myself with some spider wire.

What they could have done to make it better:
Real fish movements based on weather,time,moon phase.
Real fish actions after hooking.( not enough variety)
Total openess in fishing tackle I can buy and what set-ups I can use.
NO DAMAGE SYSTEM
NO magical fish spots.
Find another way to make money other than breakage ect.
Tourny's for ALL players broken down by level brackets with fish that CAN be caught by lures ANY player can buy not just DLC items like tourny's are now unless your old enough.

Sorry but I really hate the whole pay to play feeling hiding behind a mask of well "if you level long enough you can play in those tourny's for free without the DLC".....You shouldn't let a player join if they can't fish it and be competitive against others.....
