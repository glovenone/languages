To keep it short; it's Quake except not good.

If you want a longer explanation, allow me to explain:

Monument is an FPS that is trying really hard to be Quake. It has a hard-rock/metal soundtrack, it's got a really browny-grey colour palette, and it's got GUNS AND BLOOD.

What it lacks though is speed and fluidity (amoung other things).

The speed of the game is incredibly slow, like, REALLY SLOW. You move like molasses on molasses. Enemies are sluggish and floaty as well, probably to compensate for your own lack of speed. Now I wouldn't call this slow for the sake of strategy, stealth, or tension; because if that was the case the enemies wouldn't be so easy to avoid, or hell, to kill.

The difficulty of this game is non-existant. I never died from an enemy once; from falling into an instant-death pit yes, but never from the enemies. Their AI is nearly brain-dead, they'll get caught on walls and never catch up to you. This coupled with their aforementioned slowness makes them a piece of cake to deal with. Even if they do land an attack, it rarely does that much damage, and it doesn't even matter since you can easily heal up from the generous amount of health laying around the maps.

The game is made even easier by two weapons you get in the game; the rifle and the plasma gun. These two weapons hold so much ammo, and do so much damage, that they make every enemy feel like a mosquito.

The game of course has other problems, like awkward platforming, getting permanently stuck in walls (just by walking into them), ugly enemy and weapon models, unsatisfying combat, wonky physics, and the over-all lack of content and length (the game's only 5 levels, you only get 5 weapons, and there's only 5 enemy types).

The game at least feels like the developer tried, some heart was put into it and an attempt was truly made; so I wouldn't say its completely terrible. The game is just really mediocre in its current state. There's way worse games you could get, but there's also a plethora of much better games as well.

I got Monument on sale for little over 80 cents, which is a fair price for what you'll be getting. Otherwise, do not buy Monument; its not worth its full price.

For $4, I cannot recommend this game. However, I got it on sale for $.79 (that's how I heard about this game), and I think it's worth it. Fix the lag and either lower the price to $1, or add on some more content, and this'll sell much better.

As many people pointed out, this game totally looks like Quake. However, what people fail to mention is that the game plays more like id software's other game: Wolfenstein 3D. Wolf3D, the pioneer of FPS, was always more about strategy and stealth, rather than being a fast-paced, in your face shoot 'em up game. Monument, in my opinion, is meant to bring back elements of all different styles of old-school FPS games. And it does a pretty good job, if you liked Wolf, Doom, Quake, Heretic, etc., then you'll have fun with Monument. You won't spend as many hours into the game as you've spent in Castle Wolfenstein or Hell and Mars, but you should still enjoy reliving the olden days with new levels and gameplay.

+Old-school FPS action.
+Cool looking monsters to kill.
+Money goes to independent developers.
+Controls are simple (standard WASD keys, space to jump, e to interact, and mouse to aim and shoot).
+Heavy metaaaaaal! It may not be Slayer or Ozzy or any other big name in metal, but it's better than nothing.

N) Enemies take inconsistent amounts of damage to die. You can kill one monster in five hits, and another of the same type might only take three.
N) Weapons are very unique. The starter gun is so strange, I can't even tell what kind of gun it is.

-Very very laggy, especially when near an enemy. Having the settings higher actually slightly helps with the lag, but it's still TERRIBLE.

What I say:
While the game is mostly fast paced by movement, it does get a bit slow-paced due to the monsters coming at you and animating slowly. I honestly never experienced any lag and I have it on high settings. Terrible controls? The controls were simple enough for me. Maybe because I'm used to Half-Life.

Anyways, Monument is a game that brings you back to the old-school shooter age such as DOOM and Quake.

Graphics:
The graphics do not meet the expectations of today's high end gaming, but looking from going from the old-school shooter to going to these 3d graphics, it gives a good nostalgia feel.

Gameplay:
The movement is fast-paced. However, when it comes to monsters, things become a bit slow. Unlike DOOM, you can't just crash in and nuke the monster with bullets. It requires thinking and moving around the monster in this game.

The puzzles presented here reminds me of the usual puzzles I see in the DOOM games. Also, what is great is it comes with a save feature, so before you screw up your moment, at least you don't have to worry to run through the whole level again.

Level design is a combination of "straight-forward" and "hidden path" type.

Music:
If I was listening track by track, I would enjoy the music. Depending on how long you spend on the levels, it can get annoying. It's mostly a metal/hard rock type of music here. Really suits the atmosphere.

Conclusion:
Monument may not catch up to today's look-a-likes of DOOM and Quake. However, it reaches just enough to give an enjoyable and nostalgic experience for old-school shooter players.

Level design is basic. Easy moments and hard moments.
Save feature.

Atmosphere that brings back DOOM and Quake memories.

Cons:

2/5 for passable graphics and level design/simple puzzles. -3 for brainless AI, poor performance, and no audio controls (I may have continued playing this a bit farther if it wasn't for the in-ability to mute the music).

Monument makes a noble attempt at imitating the style of the classics Quake and Quake 2, and at least in that much it is somewhat successful. While nothing visually in it is anywhere near as good, it definitely captures an unmistakable feel of those games. Unfortunately it fails on almost every other level. Awful levels, boring shooting, bland weapons, abysmal weapon designs, poor testing and polish. The soundtrack is alright, the visuals - while they could definitely use some refinement - are alright, but the gameplay has too many problems to make this a worthwhile experience.

The enemy models look ridiculous and the way that they spawn in your field of view rather than just existing in the levels is annoying and pulls you out of the experience, as well as killing any potential strategy in approaching a fight. The guns aren't fun to use, they don't have punch, the gunplay is unsatisfying, even the aiming feels a little bit wonky. With a lot of fixes and a dedicated level designer, this could be turned into something good, but what has been released isn't there. Not even close. You can grab dozens of Quake mods for free which will give you a more enjoyable experience than this.

They reduced the price on this game so I'm not really sure if I can recommend it or not, but I will go over what I saw.

While others did not like it, I saw some potential in this game. But I also saw a wide variety of graphical errors, poor and clunky controls, and a weak enemy AI. In addition, the game was short and beatable. Yes, I actually did go through it in 2.3 hours.

The graphics were merely okay in parts. In other parts there was flickering, misaligned textures and so forth. Even in Doom I didn't see any graphical errors that bad. The guns themselves were okay graphically, but obtuse and awkward. Some of them didn't make any sense either. Some were totally overpowered and others were completely underpowered.

The level design was very linear and predictable. There were no jump out of your seat moments in this game. One level after another of predictability. That is what made it so easy to complete in such little time. The final boss was a bit of a joke... and was way too easy. Even Doom and Quake were more difficult than this game.

The music... now this was one of the weakest points of this game. The constant 80s guitar metal shredding was annoying! And it was just looping over and over again. There was no reality variety in the music. This is something Doom and Quake excelled at.

If you paid $10 for this game, I pity you. I think I bought it for $2 (but don't recall). It's $3.99. Do I recommend it for that price? No. Wait until it goes on a bigger sale. Perhaps it'll be a $.99 bargain bin game soon enough. So this game will get a thumbs down from me.

Perhaps Steam's rating system is too simplistic. If I could rate this game from 0-10 stars, I would give it a 3.5 out of 10. It has some redeeming factors, but this game is to first person shooters what SyFy movies are to science fiction. Don't expect much.
