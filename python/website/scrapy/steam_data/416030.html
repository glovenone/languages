About This Game


You awaken to the dawn of a new world.
It's streams are clean, the forests lush and the local wildlife is hungry.
You're cold and in need of shelter as you hear other voices on the wind. You are not alone.

Your quest: Establish civilization and secure their safety in this new world.



Your Quest is a casual 2D Strategy/RPG with progressive gameplay where you gather resources to construct a village, craft new weapons and armor, and try to survive the depths of a procedurally generated dungeon under the surface.



Features
  • Turn-based Combat
  • Village Building
  • Craftable Armor and Weapons
  • Village Raids
  • Procedurally generated dungeon



Controls
Keyboard
W/A/S/D: Movement
Enter: Interact and Menu Selection
Esc: Main Menu and Cancel
Ctrl + W/A/S/D: Turn in that direction
Ctrl + A/D when managing jobs: Increases/Decreases jobs by 10
I: Inventory

Xbox 360 Controller
Left Stick/D-Pad: Movement
A: Interact and Menu Selection
Start: Main Menu
B: Cancel
Left Shoulder + Left Stick/D-Pad: Turn in that direction
Left Shoulder + Left/Right on the Left Stick/D-Pad when managing jobs:
Increases/Decreases jobs by 10
Y: Inventory

Product received for free
Version: 2.1.2.1
My playtime: 11.8 h (last boss killed, normal difficulty, map size 70, dungeon level 10) (based on steam)

Intro
Your Quest is a semi idle-open world rpg themed game where you can build a village and slay monsters. The game will end after you slain the last boss.

Graphics
The graphics in this game looks like RPG maker graphics, and it looks worse than that because it's zoomed out. The UI is also quite simple and only shows what's important.

Music
The music is decent. It sounds like music from old RPG games where everything is on bass.

The game
When you are starting a new game, you'll be provided with several options based on what world that you want to create. Will it be a big or a small map? Will it have lots of few caves? Will it have easy or difficult enemies? You can also modify your stats and character looks.

At first, there will be a tutorial to help you understand the game. The game might start slow because of the delay between actions (chopping a tree and pickaxing a stone might take like one minute) but you can make it faster after you upgrade the tools. Although there will still be some delay between actions after upgrading it to the "ultimate gear", it's still bearable compared to the initial gear. Note that the character clothes will change based on the armor you are using. In the first few hours, you'll mostly looking for resources in order to be able to build anything you need to slay the monsters.

You are supposed to build a village in this game, which means, you will build houses. Houses will grant you housing for people which will come to your village whenever there is a food. The villagers can be assigned to a job which will be unlocked after you build a certain buildings. The villagers can look for food themselves or even help you to look for materials. This is what makes the game to be semi-idle. You can just wait for the villagers to collect the material for you and leave the game running while you are doing something else.

Besides the collecting resources, crafting, and building, there is a minimum of one cave in each game which can be explored. There are several floors in the cave (a minimum of 10 floors), and the last floor will contain a monster which is supposed to be the last boss. You'll need to be prepared to beat it because it's very strong.

The game will end after you slain the boss, and you'll have to start a new game when it happens.

Mods
The game itself is heavily moddable. You can add new equipments, buildings, everything! You can also even edit the save file in order to change whatever you want. In my case, i change the movement speed to 32 to make my character (and monsters) run faster.


Pros:
- Heavily moddable game content
- You can start the game in your preferable setting

Cons:
- Starts very slow

Summary
This is a game which will require you to be patient. You'll need to wait a lot and i advise you to play it idle. Although this game mostly will be played idle, you actually can make it non idle by changing the save file. The game's fun can be ruined by changing the save file though.

Should you buy this game?
Yes.
That's if you love idle game with RPG theme. Also, if you also like a moddable open world game you'll like this game even more.

Pleasantly surprised by how much fun I'm having.

Forewarning:
Note the casual tag and the price...this game is fun, but don't expect any hardcore depth. It's more something i've just been playing while watching some YT vids. Also, I will say I used a 360 controller, the controls both keyboard or controller, I think could use some touch ups. But at this point I've gotten use to the 360 controller.

Btw, once you get to the best tools, you can chop tree's, or harvest rocks in 1 second.

This game has 2 parts to it. On one side you're building your village, and crafting new items & buildings, to give you access to more items/buildings. Take note, I didn't realize this, but some buildings require you to be at a certain level (discussed later). Basically you have your tools, ranging from axe to sword. Each level increases dmg down, along with length of time it takes to harvest. The first time you harvest a tree, you just click once, but it will take a long time. You have to realize that you don't really want to do much harvesting, let your villagers do that...which they retrieve items from the jobs you've assigned them to on an hourly basis in game (in game hour = 1 min real time).

The other side is a simple dungeon diving exercise. You'll see a dungeon entrance on your map, you can go down and kill things. Killing things earns your XP, which earns you levels. Start out with killing the bunnies and squirrels above ground first, and watch out when you're level 1, those squirrels hurt. Then aim for armor/sword...to help kill faster. As you go down further into the dungeon, the levels get larger, monsters get harder. Save often (not something I really need to say for an rpg, but I said it anyways). However, as you gain levels, from killing things, check to see if there are new buildings or new tools/weapons to be built. Some buildings/items can't be built until you're a specific level.

Overall I've really enjoyed playing, and I'm still not done.

*Updated Review*

I ran into a problem that was easily fixed with a reinstall of the game. So I changed my review to a positive review

Your Quest is a pixelated 2D stratergy/survival game. You start out with nothing but wooden tools, and you work to progress your way to better tools and weapons, as well as building and maintaining a successful village of your own creation! It really is "Your Quest" because you can build your village like you want it.

As a fan of retro 2D pixelated games like this, I find this game to be extremely satisfying in both visuals and mechanics. The movement is a bit slow, but other than that I think this game is very well made in those aspects.

There is a simple yet effective crafting system and another simple building system. I'm a huge fan of the way the developer set these up, because they're not confusing in the slightest but they also get the job done well

One thing outside of the game that really impresses me is the developer's interactivity with the community. He listens to suggestions and actually recently fixed a bug pointed out in a steam review.

All and all, I'd recommend picking this game up. I'm curious to see what's in store in the future.
