About This Game

All hands on deck! Pirate armada dead ahead!
Take part in an uncompromising struggle repelling freebooters' assaults. Watch out, the enemy may catch the tide for ruining your fortress. Build towers, collect crystals, upgrade spells and believe in your victory!

Welcome to the unique pirate style Tower Defence. Now the battle rages on by land and by sea!
Two worlds, more than 40 levels, unique ships, menacing bosses and powerful spells - all this will deepen you into the game.

1. To arms! Pirate armada dead ahead!
2. Engage in incredible sea battles!
3. Master the tactics of magic and arms!
4. Defeat powerful and scary bosses!
5. Download! Load! Sink'em all!

- 40 different progressively challenging levels
- 2 unique game settings
- 12 kinds of creatures
- 12 kinds of towers and structures
- Extensive magic system
- In-game store, for various upgrades.
- Fascinating gameplay

Pro:
  • Decent graphics for a Tower Defense game.
  • Three difficulty levels.

Con:
  • Lazy, boring upgrade system that requires grinding.
  • Enemy paths sometimes unclear.
  • Poor targetting priorities.
  • Little variety in towers and only half of them are viable.
  • Crappy UI, Escape doesn't even open the menu.
  • No screen resolution options, just Windowed or Full Screen.
  • No keybinding option.
  • Terrible 10-second music loop.
  • Uninspired spells. Meteor Rain, really?
  • Very little variety in maps, the same map is used for 2-3 levels.
  • The publisher is so lazy the description still refers to collecting crystals like in Royal Quest.
  • Tutorial text (new tower/spell/enemy) plays every time you play a level, on every difficulty.
  • Adds nothing to the genre.

Conclusion

This game makes Royal Quest look inspired by comparison, and that is not a compliment. The whole thing is just lazy and uninspired, there are tons of better Tower Defense games out there, even free ones (like Cursed Treasure 2).

A pretty straight-forward tower defense game. I really enjoy the pirate/nautical theme; I actually think it is a much more appropriate theme for a tower defense game as a waterway could require attackers to follow a set path at times and defensive towers could easily be constructed to hinder them. In most tower defense games I always wonder why the attackers would only follow the roads, and why the defenders would rely on ranged stations rather than, say, an entire hoard of military units advancing on the invaders.

This game does have the same unit types as its predecessor, Royal Defense, but with different maps. So if you enjoyed the first game you will enjoy this one, as it is another just like it.

The graphics have a vibrant, painted look to them and the art style is very pleasing with a degree more polish than some of the other tower defense games such as Kingdom Rush or Garden Rescue.

The music is pretty standard battle fair. Decent enough background music for any military skirmish game, but I do wish they had included more of a jaunty pirate shanty as the mission music with a few more nautical sound effects for a much richer pirate-themed immersion to set this game apart from the other games of its genre.

This is a great game however it is lacking two very important things that a lot of tower defense games have. If you can get past those two things than this game is really great.

The first problem with this game is that it's a tower defense with no endless mode, and - to make things even worse - very short levels. Everytime I feel like I'm really dominating the attacking enemies the level is over and I go to the next one and start building again. Not ever have I felt like the enemies were really requiring to use or upgrade even half of the map, no matter what difficulty I play on. The levels are simply too short to ever be really challenging after a couple of waves (The first few waves are often challenging though.).

My second problem is that a new wave cannot be started untill the last one is completely defeated. I've seen a lot of tower defense games and this is not something that every tower defense game has but since the game gets really easy after a couple of waves it would give players an option to make things more difficult at times.

The game is simply not challenging enough after a couple of waves in a level and there aren't enough waves in one level to actually require you to use and upgrade the entire map, meaning that you'll dominate the enemy since you'll have enough gold to actually place and upgrade enough turrets to fill the map after a few waves.

Apart from those two points the game is really good, but it just isn't a real tower defense without some kind of endless mode.
