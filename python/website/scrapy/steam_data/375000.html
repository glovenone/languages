About This Game

Blast your way through waves of enemies and bullets and defeat massive boss ships in this fast-paced vertical Shoot'em Up with a traditional Arcade Mode and an experimental Campaign Mode that borrows from the mechanics of ARPGs such as Diablo and Borderlands.

CAMPAIGN MODE


Choose from 3 ship classes, gain experience up to level 30, loot randomly generated weapons, shields and mods from your enemies and carefully spend points in non-resetable skill trees.

Make your own challenge: overwhelm lower level enemies with devastating firepower, min-max your ship's stats to farm a specific chapter for rare loot, or bite all that you can chew and risk losing it all in hardcore mode.

  • Randomly generated enemy waves
  • 3 sub-modes: Casual (no death penalty), Normal and Hardcore (perma-death)
  • 3 ship classes: Fighter, Cruiser and Destroyer
  • 30 levels of ship experience
  • 15+ stats to min-max, such as Critical Damage, Armor, Shield Regeneration, Hitbox Size and more
  • 3 skill trees (Attack, Defense and Technology)
  • Evolutive special powers
  • Billions of randomly generated items with 4 quality tiers and according rarity
  • 4 tiers of difficulty
  • World ranking

ARCADE MODE


  • 1 bullet death: no life bars or shield shenanigans
  • Chain scoring: combos increase your score multiplier
  • 1CC scoring: you can continue when out of lives but your score and chain will reset
  • In-game power-ups

COMMON FEATURES


  • Gamepad support
  • Vertical screen support
  • Replays
  • Leaderboards
  • Achievements
  • Original soundtrack

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 8/7/Vista/XP SP3
    • Processor: 1.7 GHz Processor or better
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: OpenGL 2.1 compatible dedicated graphic card with 512MB memory
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 300 MB available space

Posted: 13 October, 2015
I'm terrible at shmups but I like this game. Developer "Quadsphere" has made a casual mode for scrubs like me to practice on before I graduate to the three higher difficulties.

What sets this game apart from your average schmup is the ability to collect tech as you play. Unlike your typical game which gives you a short boost, these upgrades are permanent and may be better than your existing shields, weapons, and missiles. If so, you can switch them out at the end of each level. If you're playing casual difficulty, you can also switch gear if you die before the level is finished. Any of your items can be sold for cash if desired and there is a cash shop available to purchase additional items.

You accumulate experience points to level up and build the type of ship you want -- Attack/crit, defense/armor/resists, or tech/special weapons. For example, you can increase the damage of your weapons, increase your crit chance, add armor or increase the amount of tech you receive.

My only complaint with the game is the items in the cash shop are usually low quality weapons aside from maybe one of two items that may not have the weapons or stats you need. In addition, the items in the shop do not change until you complete a level. This can be a problem if you're stuck on a tough level with weak equipment both on your ship and in the shop.

My tips?
Decide on your preferred playstyle (Attack, Defense, or Tech) in advance.
Pick the ship that matches your playstyle best.
Invest at least 21 skill points for your playstyle to get the maximum buff.
Always max Bullet Resistance with skill points regardless of playstyle
Try to buff up Tech Find with your equipment to keep a steady stream of gear flowing.
Rotating lasers suck.

Pretty fun little scrolling bullet hell.

Has some interesting additions to customizing your ship and gameplay. You have RPG aspects to upgrade your skills, you get boosts to stats with skill points, and it has a complete weapon and accessory system like an RPG with the colored levels and whatnot. Special skills, secondary weapons, XP and tech pickups, and quite a few non-upgradable stats that are boosted by the equipment you find and buy. (shield regen, crit. %, pickup distance, etc.).

You pick up weapon parts, shields, and modules to give yourself different stats and it is kind of randomized like a Diablo/Borderlands as it says on the store page. It's defintiely NOT an adventure RPG game, its a SHMUP, but it has that style of item value so there isn't much other way to describe it. White, green, blue, purple...yadda yadda. xD

Music and the backgrounds aren't great. You can dim the backgrounds though so it makes it a lot easier to see enemies and stuff through bullets and the background graphics. Sounds, music, and backgrounds make it feel Indie and it does make the presentation feel a little less than amazing. Options to force V-sync, change resolution, and a few other video options like changing to windowed and landscape. Audio is standard volume so you can shut the music off if you want. I think that the gameplay and mechanics are solid enough to recommend this, however. Watch the store video if this is a major issue for you.

It also has the option to turn on auto-fire so your main weapon fires constantly. This is actually pretty much what I use because you wouldn't stop firing in this game regardless. No reason to bother turning it off imo. You also have a speed up button to skirt out of tight spots much faster.

It's not a AAA made SHMUP by any stretch, but defintiely has a lot of great qualities that aren't common in this genre. Getting it from a bundle I am pleasantly surprised, and the low price isn't something I'd cry about if I had bought it either.

The gamepad controls are OK, but not great until you rebind them. Initially, before you are able to rebind them they feel very awkward trying to move around the menus (they actually do work in the menus, but may not seem like it the way they are bound). They do become easier to use once you figure out how to bind them and that the cursor is being moved by the left stick, or D-Pad. Trigger buttons, are not available to bind, at least on my PS3 gamepad using 360 emulation, but the shoulder buttons work fine. There aren't enough controls to worry about this, however. Unless you turn off auto-fire the only other in-game buttons you need are "Special" and "Speed Up/Boost" So you don't need the triggers anyways. Once you bind them everything works fine and you don't need to worry too much. You are dodging a TON bullets (bullet hell obviously) and picking up loot so not worrying about firing is nice. :P

Fun scrolling arcade bullet hell that has Campaign, and arcade modes. Leaderboards, achievements, and trading cards. Presentation isn't amazing, but the mechanics and gameplay are fun and worthwhile. Has some difficulty spikes that might need some balancing, but I didn't have too many issues.

Awesome emtoes too. :icarussmile:

Icarus-X has seen it fit to introduce that most traditional of gaming mechanics - The Grind. Even in scrolling shooters, a branching skills tree could perhaps be labelled uncommon if hardly new, but I've not seen many looting system within the genre. Games like Borderlands and Diablo are liberally name-dropped as comparison models, and not completely unfairly. Either by defeating enemies and picking up drops or purchasing from the in-game store, you can deck out up to four equipment slots including primary and secondary fire, as well as a core chip and a shield.

It all works pretty well; though physically there’s not a huge amount of variation. For example, the primary fire is purely distinguished by the rate of spread, the more focused the collection of bullets, the more powerful they usually are. Secondary fire has a little more choice to it, offering homing missiles or a collection of lasers with a small variety of tricks ranging from vanilla constant beams to a circling weapon that rotates constantly around your craft. Things like armour rating, shield power, tech drop rates, critical hit percentage and power consumption are also tracked through your equipment making any alteration you go for more of a balance trade off rather than a straight swap. That new laser might be more powerful, but it does chip away and your shield regeneration rate and make your hitbox slightly bigger.

Alright, this is rather interesting. Although the description references Diablo and Borderlands it is actually a very standard (length wise) 5 levels top-down shooter with Rpg mechanics mixed in. You level up, get some loot and allocate points in skill trees with the usual critical strike, armor, shield and such stats you would find in a typical Diablo-like. So while it's not the big adventure you might expect, there is some interesting theorycrafting to be made.

Pros

Super smooth framerate
Good variety of loot and stats
Weapons feel great
You get a massive regenerating shield which is a relief when tons of bullets are fired your way
Controls fine with a gamepad
Pretty cool music

Cons

The backgrounds are really minimalist and repetitive
Some bosses are op, some are jokes, could use some balancing
Gamepad in menus controls the mouse cursor, might as well use the mouse?

All in all I'd recommend it, just don't expect a full blown RPG but rather a small top-down shooter with Rpg mechanics.
