24 June

Announcing the pre-season beta

Hey sports fan!

Things have been quiet while Tipping Goat has been working on new features, updated art, tweaks and fixes for your favorite all-sports mashup. In addition to all this, a lot of older code has been reworked and will need testing and feedback as we get closer to the official Season Mode update.

To this end, a pre-season beta version of SSDT will be released on Steam that you can opt into. The pre-season beta will include:

* Updated arenas from Season mode: Play in the classic American football stadium, or change it up with the all new basket ball court, all new practice lot or head over to Canada for the new hockey arena.
* New spotlight celebration for the player who scores
* Live, in-game, per-player scoring stats right on the field
* The controller drives the mouse for easier controller configuration
* Many smaller bug fixes, art updates and more

The pre-season beta will be updated as more features get added, leading up to the Season Mode release. So stay tuned! Another announcement will be posted when the pre-season beta is ready in the next week or so.

22 February

SSDT v1.3.3 is here

Hey there, Sports Fan!

Rick here, with the latest update to Super Slam Dunk Touchdown. It's live right now! Let's get right to what's in the build:

Features

  • Partial Spanish language support: Spanish on all UI buttons, player stats and some other UI elements as well.
  • Lock icon added for locked players in Challenge Mode
  • CPU levels 2 and 3 are more challenging in Free-For-All mode
  • American Football player has a greater penalty for missing a tackle
  • More city and sports team names added to name generator
  • New "Free-For-All" achievement
  • If sudden death is on, there is no longer a normal "OVERTIME" round before sudden death kicks in (previous behavior was intentional, but ended up confusing people)


Bug Fixes
  • Can no longer select characters on a team that is full (player icons restricted to the open team's half of the screen)
  • Title "post-wiggle" fix
  • CPU players much better at dunking!
  • Season Mode colors locked (oops!) -- stay tuned for Season Mode later this year!
  • End game/round "moon walking" and other odd behavior fixed
  • CPU player icons revert to black & white after removing them from your team
  • "Pause at game over screen" bug fixed
  • Scoring glitch fixed in Free-For-All mode

That's it! Enjoy the update, and, while you still can: hop on over to the forums and let me know what features you think are essential to Season Mode!

About This Game

Super Slam Dunk Touchdown is a retro styled, local multiplayer, couch-competitive, all-sports mashup driven by a 3D physics engine. Assemble a crack squad of sports athletes and use their unique shot styles and abilities to score goal units and demolish the opposing team. American football players tackle, soccer players steal, roller derby players skate and everybody dunks! Battle for control of baseballs, basketballs, bowling balls, tennis balls, and more. Configure your matches your way, and play with up to six players locally, 3-on-3. Watch out for banana peels, streaking fans and ice resurfacing trucks, not to mention the opposing team!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: Pentium® IV or AMD® equivalent at 2.0 GHz
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Graphics card with DX9 (shader model 2.0) capabilities
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 120 MB available space
    • Sound Card: Soundcard required for sound
    • Additional Notes: Local multiplayer only. Analog game controllers are HIGHLY recommended.

At first I thought this game was going to be a just a silly party game, with just chaos and luck reigning above skill. But what I found was that this games mechanics are FAR deeper than just the surface.

Shooting, for example, has 3 levels of charge, and each character does different things based on the charge level. The Football player will just throw the ball on the first 2 levels, but he'll punt it on the third. Soccer will shoot on the ground on the first charge, but the second, he'll kick it above his oppents heads, and on the third, he'll aim for the field goals.

Most mechanics are like this, where at first it seems simple, but in reality, the devs were thoughtful and creative to create a game that's simple to understand, but hard to master

Early Access Review
Posted: 12 August, 2015
Super Slam Dunk Touchdown harkens back to the roots of arcade sports fun. I would describe it as a combination between Ice Hockey for the NES and NBA Jam. For anyone who has played either or both of those, you can probably stop reading now and just buy it. If you haven't played those, imagine a 2 vs 2 or 3 vs 3 arcade sports mashup (of just about every sport) where you can pick different types of athletes with various strengths (e.g., speed, power, and shot types). There are multiple ways to score, and you can attack opponents and use a short burst of turbo to break for the basket/goal/goal posts. The matches are quick and frantic and there are plenty of wild distractions like banana peals and even streakers.

The sprites are well done and the game is funny in the way that Ice Hockey was. There's just something about watching goofy sprites pummel one another while dribbling, kicking, and running with an ice hockey puck, and then bouncing the puck off the rim in a botched dunk. The game doesn't resort to references memes or anything like that - it's just fun and humorous, particularly with a group of friends.

I suggest picking the game up and enjoying it with friends (or even alone - the AI is pretty competent) and hop on the forums to encourage the developers to continue to improve the game. That's what early access is all about, and this game has great potential to be one of the better games to come out of the program.
