About This Game

Grab your friends, hone your blades, and set off for adventure in Trove, the ultimate action MMO! Battle the forces of Shadow in realms filled with incredible dungeons and items created by your fellow players. Whether hunting treasure in far-off lands or building realms of your own, it’s never been this good to be square!

Cubular Classes

Play as a Knight, Gunslinger, Ice Sage, Dracolyte, Pirate-with-a-parrot, or any (or all!) of the other Classes in Trove while mastering abilities from deadly ninja techniques to deliciously deadly ice cream crushes. Upgrade your gear to tackle epic challenges and strut your stuff with killer looks.

Infinite Realms

Explore through fully destructible realms on the backs of fire-breathing dragons and purrfectly whiskered Meownts. Jump into action tailored to any level as you conquer untamed wilds from the Treasure Isles to Candoria, home of the sweet-toothed Candy Barbarians.

Delve into Dungeons

Assemble a group of hardy cube-kind and crawl your way through huge dungeons unique to each realm. Brave deadly minions, bosses, and traps to “liberate” powerful armor and weapons, or dip into smaller Lairs for single servings of danger perfect for solo fun.

Get Rich with Loot

Rake in a shimmering hoard of treasure and collectible items from the deepest and darkest places in Trove. Load up on special gear, costumes, decorations, recipes, crafting ingredients, flying carpets, sea-faring ships, and dragons of mighty renown.

Build Your Home…

Cornerstones are personal homes you can build block by block, but they’re also mobile bases! Drop your Cornerstone at specially-marked plots in any world you’re visiting, and voilà: your hand-crafted home-away-from-home will appear, giving you a place to kick back, relax, and craft to your heart’s content.

Build a World…

What’s better than crafting alone? Building entire new Club Worlds with friends. Feel like making enormous geodesic domes in the desert? Magnificent castles in the sky? Do it – and land yourself in our list of the cubic masterpieces in Trove!

Build Anything in the Game!

Dragons? Check. Bacon swords? Yup. Rainbow-grade 3D glasses of insufferable doom? Just the tip of the iceberg! Collect a HUGE range gear made by your fellow players and make and submit your own items – including Dungeons, Lairs, Class Costumes, and more.

Plus

Music Blocks, Meownts, Fishing, Farming, Pets, Sailing (with Boats!), Flying (with Wings!), Mag Trains, Dance-Pads, Heroes, Villains, Allies (who might be Villains), Flasks, Bombs, Piñata Parties, and the Crafting of any and all Things.


Did we mention it’s free to play?!

The game looks cute, appealing,a bit childish though, many classes to choose from and the possibility to change the skin of the weapon, hat and face that you wear. The game is enjoyable when you are low mastery/level because you level up quite fast. Abit of killing, a bit of mining and you're fine.

A few dozens of hours later, you need to do much more to level up. Quite normal, since you need more xp to reach the next level. Except it's in way bigger proportions. At that point, it's called grinding. You have to spend hours and hours just to progress a little. Doing dungeons gets repetitive real quick.

A few updates later, you can't get new classes for free (you could unlock them through in-game money that you earned everyday) and you need to spend real money to get them. New features were introduced at the cost of making the game even more grindy. Don't worry, the devs also released some items that you can use to make progression faster, by buying those items (with real money again) to upgrade gems. Though, those upgrades may break the gem (unless you have the items preventing this). BUT, that still doesn't guarantee that the upgrade will succeed. So the tedious work and the time you invested to gather the materials simply go to waste and you need to do that again to MAYBE be able to upgrade a gem. You need to upgrade them several times and there are several gems to obtain, making you stronger. What happen when you get stronger? You are allowed to go in harder worlds, allowing you to grind better items that you need to upgrade too. And the cycle continues.

I don't like this game, and it doesn't like me either. I only (rarely) play it because a good friend plays it.

Do not play this game. It used to be fun, a nice little MMORPG with some unique ideas in it, cute character and enemy designs, maybe a bit grindy for my taste but the grind was the gameplay to an extent, like in most dungeon crawlers. But the thing is, the developers have gotten more and more greedy over time; I am not exaggerating, they *actually* looked over how their game stood and how well things were going, and decided to A. reduce the rate at which players gain in-game currency, B. make the things that were in the store for in-game currency more expensive, and C. not allow players to buy classes using in-game currency anymore. All for the explicitly stated goal of encouraging more store purchases and less ability to get things without paying real money - they weren't even trying to hide it or couch it in good PR terms, it's very blatantly "people weren't buying enough stuff, so we made things worse for non-payers". Don't believe it's that bad? Read for yourself.

I get that with any free to play game, it's a fine balance. On the developer's end, they have to incentivize real-money transactions, while offering enough for free that people continue to join and play. Otherwise, they can't keep the game running or support their families. On the player's end, they have to be constantly aware that the developer directly benefits from making playing for free a frustrating and difficult experience - just not too frustrating. In both directions, each side is in a delicate balance indeed, and the most important thing to cultivate is trust. Developers should trust players to buy things if they're actually worth it, and players should trust developers to make things that are worth it, while offering enough reliable, quality free content to keep playing the rest of the time.

Trust is what enables this whole model to function, which is why one of the absolute worst things a F2P developer can do is to take things away over time. Once offering something, especially for a long period of time, then taking it away is a betrayal of trust, and that's exactly what Trion has done with Trove. They lured people in with the promise of what the game used to be, and then took it away to make some quick cash. For the sake of the entire F2P gaming community, for the sake of better developers who respect their playerbases, don't reward this kind of cash-grubbing, short-sighted behavior.

Edit: Oh, and I'm not even going to get into things like the quality of recent updates, or the promised bounties for finding glitches that were never delivered. Those subjects are too nebulous for me to weigh in on - what I'm talking about here are factual matters that are well documented in public record.
