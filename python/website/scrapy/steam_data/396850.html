About This Game

Bad news! The lawyer Kathleen Wilson has discovered that her beloved Danny Reed has been arrested under strange circumstances. He’s being accused of murder. In order to free Danny, she’s going to have to carry out her own investigation.

Kathleen has to find out the truth of what really happened. Was he set up, or was it just some kind of misunderstanding? Is there something bigger behind all this? Right now, nothing is clear...

Collect photographs, notes, slides – anything that might be useful in your investigation. Record all the facts and clues in your notebook - they could come in handy later. It isn’t hard to hit a dead end in an investigation, but don’t panic - you can use a hint.

A long hidden-object adventure that won't win any awards, but is fun to play and would satisfy fans of the genre.

The story and characters are forgettable, but the point-and-click adventuring is well designed and will reward pragmatic thinking. Graphics are quite good and bring the world to life in ways in which the writing does not. Hidden-object scenes are OK, but easy. There are very few puzzles and they are all quite trivial.

This game is very poorly named: there are neither witches nor warlocks here, just some limited time travel (magic!) having to do with "totems" of some sort.

Also note that the achievements are a bit buggy: this counts for many of the negative reviews here, and the developer deserves them for as long as they aren't fixed. However, the game itself is fine and can be played to completion.

A pleasant, if unremarkable, hidden object game. It's a relaxing way to spend two hours. All the usual trimmings are there - hint/skip buttons so you're never stuck, a teleporting map that indicates the areas in which there are things to do, and a journal to keep track of the (minimal) story.

The graphics are nicely done. The music is inoffensive. There is no voice acting, and no extras of the sort you'd see in Collector's Edition hidden object games.

The hidden object scenes are list-based junk piles with the occasional interactive object (e.g. pick up the sponge and wipe the mirror with it to reveal an image that is an item on your list). Each hidden object scene is revisited, but not really repeated, since the objects you found the first time remain found.

There aren't that many puzzles, and those that are present are mostly of the tired old slider variety.

Most of your time in-game will be spent point-and-click style, finding an item to help unlock a box that has another item you can use to reach another locked box and so on. These inventory... interactions? They're not really puzzles.... are satisfying in the same way that popping bubble wrap is.

Harmless fun at a reasonable price. Recommended for fans of the genre, as long as they know going in that this is just more of the same.

Your lover is being accused of a murder he didn't commit. While you're a lawyer, you want to find the artifact he was hunting in order to make the culprit come out of his hidden place. However, you would have never imagined to travel through time...

The plot is kinda going everywhere, though you visit places from an hotel room that are very different (an airport, the village in the winter, a old mansion, a cabaret) to find pieces of totems.

What? Oh the title? Yeah, I'm still searching the connection with witches, warlocks and the main plot.

Anyway, the gameplay is the standard one: hidden objects scenes, mini-games, everything is well balanced. However, there is a little problem at one moment: the item you need is an axle jack but is not in the list - in reality, it seems that the lists were inverted for that scene.

The graphics are quite good and I loved the unique track.

However, I'm quite mixed of this game because of two things. The first one is a game breaking bug out of nowhere that made me do a second profile. Indeed, I couldn't access anymore a scene where a vital piece was stuck. When I start the game again, suddenly, I lost my diary, my totem case and my map. And the scene was still unavailable.

The second one is the problem met for the achievement with no skip and no hint for scenes. In reality, you have to avoid any click on the hint button, even outside of the hidden object scene. You have to wait until the skip button is charged for the mini-game because you may risk the hint button to resplenish itself and counting as a skip/use of hint. Some people got a problem when exiting by accident an HO scene. And you need to do it in one go: you can't leave the game if you want these achievements.

So, yeah, I'm still letting a thumb up but I would wait for a discount or another bundle to get a key, even if 6 euros isn't expensive.
