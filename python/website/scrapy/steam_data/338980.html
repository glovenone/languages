About This Game


Discover the wonders of an uncharted island and help the natives restore their ravaged land to its former glory in Moai: Build Your Dream! Overcome the fury of an active volcano, hordes of attacking ghosts and the threat of dwindling resources as you guide the hero and a native princess on their quest. Coax the resources you need to survive and rebuild villages out of the earth. Calculate the quickest way to accomplish your tasks in order to unlock dozens of brag-worthy achievements. And learn to use powerful Moai statues to defend your workers as they perform the tasks you assign. There will never be a dull moment as you enjoy the colorful graphics, life-life character animations, and dazzling comic book style story interludes in Moai: Build Your Dream!

  • Comic book style cutscenes
  • Outstanding visual design
  • Addictive gameplay

Game Storyline

There's actual story plus with banter between characters in-between game levels. The story itself is okay even though it isn't one of those stories that tends stick with you.

Game Visual/Audio

There isn't much can be said about the game's visuals asides from that they're well done. Pretty much the same thing can be said about the game's audio. The visual and audio can be adjusted in the game's option menu.

Gameplay

There are no actual difficulty choice selection but each of the levels has their own difficulty.

This is a time/resource management game. So there's a timer presence throughout the entire game. The time on game's timer is generous for the most parts. The levels can be finished before the time has completely run out on the timer. It's advisable to plan ahead before starting the level.

The character's rank is updated after earning certain amount of points from playing the game.

There are five statues in the valley. Four statues are connected to four tribes and the center statue is connected to the four statues. All levels must be completed in gold in order to full upgrade the four statues. Completing each of the four statues will upgrade the statue in the center. The process of upgrading the statues is a long one.

The minor setback is that all levels must be completed within gold time. This is a long process to fully complete. Multiple replays for some of the levels maybe required.

There are sixteen different locations with sixty-four levels in total. Each of these levels has certain objective that's needed to be carried out.

The resources that building structures generates has to be manually collected by the players themselves.

There a few different temporarily power ups to help producing more resources and completing task much quicker. These power ups are available later in the game for most of the levels.

There are fifty-three different achievement to be earned from the game. Each of these achievements has certain requirements before they can be obtained.

Miscellaneous

There are total of six different trading cards. You only get the three of those cards from the game. There are several ways to get the rest of the trading card: Purchased from Steam's Market; Trade with friends; Booster Packs and it's Booster Pack Creator.

Final thoughts, I felt that this game is good, even though the story would've been better. As for the game's replay value it's low. If you're into time management game then I would recommend this game but not at the full price.

MOAI is another Time Management game from the guys over at Alawar. This time your character is stranded on an uncharted island and must work with a tribe of natives to restore their island and make your way back home. It's a fun premise which is enhanced by storytelling sequences between each level and a simple narrative that adds some meat to an already enjoyable game.

As with other titles in the genre, the gameplay is pretty standard and consists of multi-tasking activites as efficiently as possible while managing resources to complete specific tasks (build, gather, etc.). Coming up with the best strategy to complete the objectives in record time can be quite challenging, but not impossible once you determine your strategy and execute it well. If Gold (3 coins) is a little too stressful for you and you're wanting to go more casual, Silver is easily doable on most levels, and snagging Bronze a cinch. I don't think you can fail a level without actively trying to fail it, so there's not a ton of pressure if you just want to play through for the story and artwork.

And the artwork is indeed a reason to play through the game. MOAI's presentation is REALLY well done. The art has a nice hand painted look to it with high quality story CG's that are both colorful and creative. The story narration is also full English voiced and the narrator nicely acted. While the story itself seems a bit random at times, often being just enough to link each stage together, the writing is polished and even quite humorous. Music-wise, I found the Disney-esque orchestral soundtrack a highlight, enough so that I wish there were a DLC or music tracks included as an extras, but I'll just have to live with listening to it while I play.

With full Steam integration including 53 Steam achievements, Trading Cards and Cloud Support, if you're a fan of the genre you really can't go wrong here. These things add a ton of value to any game on the platform, but for casual titles they're almost a must and are a very welcome addition. MOAI is definitely one of the better Time Management games I've played and has handily exceeded my expectations. I will be sure to 100% this gem and enjoy every moment of it.

Bottom Line: Recommended for those who enjoy strategic, time-based gameplay, but will also appeal to more casual players with its storytelling and high level of polish.

MOAI: Build Your Dream:
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

In your attempt to cross the ocean in a hot air balloon (what a brilliant idea, you debonair you), and after a small issue with your magnificent vehicle (just lost of altitude due to small hole) you end up in small Island after being rescued by the local tribe.

You will find out very quickly that you stand in a middle of a ravaged paradise and your only way out is to help the native chief to restore the beauty of the island. And of course, he will help you to get back in the air in your terrific mode of transport in view to come back where you come from.

You will have to rebuild farms, quarries (stone), houses, temples, statues, markets and more by collecting all resources (food, stone, wood and mana) on the island. Also, you will be able to open chests with well needed goodies in them.
But, beware of bandits and ghosts. Give resources to the bandits to send them off and zap all ghosts with your magic wand (fun, fun, fun).

You will be assign tasks in each level to achieve as fast as possible. Upon your success you will be given up to 3 native coins which help you carve the 5 MOAI statues that represent the spirits of the elements. If you do not get those three coins, well you will have to try again!

A really nice and well structure strategy and management game that offer good and colourful graphics with plenty of fast, addictive and challenging levels. You will be entertained for hours with MOAI: Build Your Dream.

Positives:
~~~~~~~

- Good graphics
- Challenging levels
- Well design game
- Plenty of achievements
- Trading cards

Negatives:
~~~~~~~

- A bit frustrating at times (getting the 3 coins)

Give it a go! It is really fun and good value for your money!

8.5/10

Nice time management / casual building and strategy game. It follows a formula along the lines of other such game but does a bit better job at mixing things up and constantly adding new game play elements than some other similar games.

There are 64 levels that have three different time goals to meet each giving progressively lower score, which in turn will effect how quickly you can meet several of the achievements.

Speaking of which, there 53 achievements in this game fully integrated into Steam which is more than most casual games tend to have. Achievement hunts will definitely appreciate that. I found that the achievements motivated me to keep going and to replay levels with different play styles that I might not have otherwise done.

Though it's not required, meeting the quickest time goal and getting all three coins for a level can be quite challenging and required a bit of planning and strategy. You'll probably have to replay a level multiple times to do that as you really need to run through each level at least once to get a feel for what works and what doesn't, particularly once you've dived in past some of the earlier levels.

Visuals are all really well done and look great in 1080p on a monitor. There's a story that's told between levels with narrated still cut scenes and also in text boxes by various island companions who are helping you on your journey. It's a nice touch be really the main thrust of the game is resource management and building and then optionally managing your time should you choose to challenge yourself or want to go for the achievements.

Overall I was pleased with MOAI and found good entry in the casual time management and building genre. If that's your cup of tea or you're looking for a lighter, low stress game to take a break MOAI would be a good choice.

A charming time management game with a simple storyline. You play as a westerner who is stuck on a tropical island, and have to guide them through building huts, gathering resources etc..
The game is narrated by a relaxed male voice who guides you through the simple story. As you proceed the challenge becomes higher, starting with simple tasks like 'gather 200 food' but later on introducing obstacles like bandits and ghosts. The story makes it all fit in the world though.

MOAI has a nice art style with vibrant colours that make each level nice to look at, without hiding the various clickable spots from view. The soundtrack is relaxing and never gets in the way.

Alawar has a lot of time management games out and it's obvious they've mastered the genre by now. MOAI is another example, design-wise it is perfectly balanced.

It has Steam achievements, trading cards, and Steam cloud support for saves.

The only real negative remark I have, and it's a tiny one, is that I can't get it to run maximized on a single 1920x1080 window, instead I either have to play it full-screen with other monitors blanked; or windowed at what seems to be 1440x900.

Definitely earns a positive recommendation.
