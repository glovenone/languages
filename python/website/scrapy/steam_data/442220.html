Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to have the community help us shape the game, and help focus our efforts on the right area of development.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“At least six months but the longer we work on it the better it will get, the more content we can add, the better we can make the existing content.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The game will be alot more robust with more: features, more maps, more modes, more cars, more music tracks and one liners”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“It is very playable right now, we have 6 modes. 4 maps, 3 cars a single player campaign and 4-player split screen mode and a multiplayer mode with private rooms.

The modes currently in the game are: Glass Cannon (one hit kills), Death Match, Elimination, Flagger, Red Vs Blue (Team death match), Ringu (Chase the golden ring).”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, during early access the price will be lower.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The community will help us to focus on what type of content they would prefer we focus on, we have ideas on what type of cars, maps, and modes we'd like to add but we like to get some input from the community which we believe will help us make a game more in line with what they want.”

About This Game

In the future there's only death and destruction, so we took all the criminals and put them in cars, now they battle each other for your entertainment, so sit back relax and just hit start to play Iron Madness.

Iron Madness is a fast-paced multiplayer/split-screen car combat game with up to four players, blow your friends away with a wide variety of weapons including rockets, saw-blades, mines, and machine guns and more.

  • Fast-paced multiplayer action!
  • Single player campaign against AI opponents
  • 4 Player split screen mode
  • Partial controller support
  • 6 Game Modes
  • 3 Cars
  • 4 Maps
  • With lots more content coming.

Basic Information
Title: Iron Madness
Developer: Inane Games
Publisher: Inane Games
Genre: Car combat

General Impression
Iron Madness is still in Early Access and yet the dev team has taken the wise decision to make the game Free to Play until they can iron out the still present bugs and glitches. It is their first project on Steam and it looks polished enough. It can be quite fun to play in co-op or split screen and it is a homage to already classics of the car combat subgenre. I shall give this game the benefit of doubt since it can still be greatly improved and receive Trading Cards and Achievements in a future update. It deserves them.

Strong Points
+ Nice 3D graphics.
+ Free to Play.
+ Potentially fun MP experience.

Weak Points
- Random bugs and glitches.
- No Steam Trading Cards or Achievements.
- Boring SP experience.

Rating 6/10

Early Access Review
Posted: 27 April
I have to say, Iron Madness is a pretty interesting game.

However:

Although I do understand this is an Early Access Game, I think it does need plenty of shaping before I starting playing this game purely for the fun of it.

"We want to have the community help us shape the game"
- Iron Madness Developers

I want to start by saying that this is a very cool concept and I think this can become something really cool, but I think there are a few corners that need to be smoothed out in the overall shape of this game.

Pros:

  • Cool Car Models
  • Multiple gamemodes/maps
  • Multiple powerups
  • Multiplayer
  • Decent Car Physics
  • Split Screen Mode

Cons:

  • Music seems repetitive
  • AI Can become very difficult to beat
  • Basic car controls
  • Absolutly NO multiplayer games were available (I looked 4/27/2016)
  • The environments you play in seem dark and not visually appealing
  • Many bugs
  • No storyline
  • After the first 10 min. of gameplay the game becomes boring.
  • The price of this game!!!

I bought this game during a 90% discount for $0.49, but without the discount the game costs $4.99 and for the kind of content and gameplay I currently see, $4.99 is extremely overpriced.

As harsh as it might seem, I think this game deserves a 1/10 rating - because it is overpriced - but if the price tag would shrink down to ~$1 (until much more content is added) I would give this game a healthy 3/10 because it is obvious to me, and probably anybody else that has played the game, that this game is not worth $5.

*I crossed out the things which were not an issue anymore.

I will check back later and re-evaluate my review if there are changes because this concept has much, much potential and I would love to see it grow!

3/10, has lots of potential

Thanks for reading folks :)

The game thus far is a good start: Motor City Mayhem. I reckon this has the potential to be every murderous driving game since GW's original and completely mental 1984 release "Battlecars" (you may have noticed the alternative name in store). The sountrack is bonkers, the controls are classic, the maps are simple and effective-all in a very Early Access Game. For those of you with a good old-fashioned simple-minded attitude towards direct violence, I point out the low cost and potential of this game. For those of you without a sense of humour who just want a cheap game with Steam rewards and suchlike-go away. For anyone witha shred of hope-I suggest that you to look at "Orion: Prelude", a giant failure that ended up as a massively popular online game once the moaning clowns were driven from the discussion board by the designers' final release and the fans' support. Get stuck in to this for the price of a third-rate burger and chips or a substandard sandwich that will leave you hungry ten minutes later. It's your choice-a game that may eventually turn into a hilarious killfest for you and your pals when you wish it was 2001 and everything was metal AF, or another night in wondering what happened to the last fifteen years.
