About This Game

Militia is a bite-sized turn-based strategy game that brings the thrill of victory to single-player. It's simple to pick up, and from the moment you start, you'll be learning and improving -- building your skills, learning new strategies, and ranking up on the single player ladder. And as you do, the game evolves with you, providing a worthy challenge every time. Militia also features procedurally generated levels so the challenge stays fresh.



In Militia, you are the leader of a small troop of warriors, mages and cavalry and attempt to rout the local marauding banditry by dispatching their captains. Gameplay is turn based; on your turn you can issue one command to each of your units, and then the enemies each move on their turn according to strict and transparent movement rules.

  • NOTE: Includes Dark World expansion content for free (The Dark World is a separate and advanced game mode with its own heroes, enemies and ladder).

Kafro Hudrep
( 5.1 hrs on record )
Posted: 5 May
Militia is a simple, tile-based strategy game. Each time you start a new game, you go through three floors of randomly-generated battles, with three units out of five classes on your side and a bunch of enemies with an equal amount of diversity on their side. Everything moves and attacks according to specific, easily-understood rules; for example, the Warriors can move two spaces and cleave all three spaces in front of them, and the Imps can only move two spaces diagonally.

The goal is to kill all enemy captains marked with a star, but going straight for them is often a bad idea since all units die in a single hit. Keeping track of areas the enemy can attack is as crucial, if not moreso, as it is in more mathematically-complex tactical RPGs like Fire Emblem or Advance Wars.

If you manage to get through all three rounds, you gain EXP, and if you gain enough EXP, your rank goes up and so does the difficulty of future games. Losing, on the other hand, makes you lose EXP, which can be a bit upsetting since it feels like an arbitrary time-wasting punishment.

Last but not least, there's also "Dark World" mode which has entirely different units from the normal game, as well as Endless and Timed modes.

Overall, this game is great if you want something quick to pick up, puzzle through a few rounds, then put down for another time.

Militia is a unique turn-based strategy game with minimalist graphic design featuring many pictograms that might make this game look like a weird complex Chess variation. But that isn't true.

The gameplay follows the "Easy to learn, hard to master" scheme, as you'll learn almost everything about the gameplay mechanics in 10 minutes, then you are thrown into randomly generated levels. The challenge there is getting to the highest rank possible - every won game gets you closer to rank promotion, every lost game gets you farther.

Rank determines the difficulty of generated levels and provides a big challenge for everybody, as you'll have to keep a winrate better than 50%. While you'll be trying, you'll realize strategies and combos with specific units that you haven't thought before and... well, you'll get better.

There are two modes, light mode that is best for new players (but also provides a challenge) and dark mode, which was introduced later in Militia (but on Steam it's a expansion that is already included with the basic game). Fighting both orcs and undead is really tough job, you'll often find yourself thinking your plan for some time, as there is no way back after you take your turn. There isn't an undo button, every mistake comes with a regret (and lose, and getting closer to losing your rank).

The whole presentation, from graphics to sounds, is simply awesome. Graphics are great for the game's intention, you'll just learn few pictograms, falling in love in it meanwhile. Militia is a big surprise for me, as it's simply great game. It features balanced difficulty and it's a pleasure to play for everybody who wants a casual strategy game, or for those who are alone and would like to play a board game.

Militia was developed and published by BrainGoodGames. It was released on December 2nd, 2015 for £4.79. A review copy was provided for The Hidden Levels.

Militia is a “bite sized” turn based strategy game which heavily plays like the classic board game chess. The game is simply played with just a mouse and has no controller support. The game has nice contrast between the two competing sides and the ‘board’ on where it is played. No issues arose for me during my time playing Militia. The replay value is very high if you can learn and adapt to a more simple styled game. There are no difficulty options but the enemies get difficult with each level you complete.


What I enjoyed:
Easy to Learn – Within a few moments of playing I fully grasped the game controlling my troops with ease. There was a help screen in the game which I did not need
Ever changing challenge – The enemies adapt to you, the levels are randomized and the difficulty ever more increases keeping the challenge fresh.
Music – The music in the game is really outstanding and a real strong suit to the game. It really adds to the atmosphere. You could easily just leave the game running and enjoy the music.


What I disliked:
Price – The price at £4.79 seems steep for the type of game you’re getting.
No control of your units at the start – All the units are placed down randomly posing a greater challenge. I would like some control over where the troops would be placed though. I would also love to have control over what type of troop is gained.
Limited in turns – You only have a certain amount of turns to kill all troops before losing. You also are heavily outnumbered.

If you enjoy games like chess and have a great amount of patience for simple games, you could easily find yourself playing this game for long periods of time. The challenges keep rising and the randomization of the game keeps you on your toes. The game may not have a lot of depth but I fully enjoyed the simplicity and the easiness to grasp the game and rules. If you do love chess and any other styled game, this will be enjoyable.

Score: Wait for a sale

A charming, deceptively simple game. Easy to learn, difficult to master.

You pit three random player-controlled units against a much larger number of weaker enemies. You have trickier and more powerful options, but a single mistake means losing 1/3 of your firepower.

The game has an elegant tutorial and is very quick to learn.

Its ranking system accomplishes two difficult tasks at once: motivation to get better at the game, and an effective difficulty curve. You'll probably blast through the first few ranks easily, but soon you'll find yourself in challenging environments that push you to master the mechanics. I can confidently say this regardless of your skill level or aptitude for this sort of game.

Overall, a great buy for the price. You can play one of two distinct rulesets, either Light or Dark. Light offers much simpler mechanics, with units that jump and attack in easy-to-understand ways. Dark offers complicated individual pieces that do things like push other units, generate explosives, and warp across the edges of the board.

I am excited to see how many hours of play this game offers me.

Militia keeps being compared to chess and I feel that's a rather short sited comparison. At the beginning of each round you are placed on a procedurally generated board with the objective of using your given pieces to clear the board of the 'Captain' pieces. The only real way that it's like chess is that any given piece has a specified move set.

This game is the almost the definition of "Easy to Learn, Hard to Master" the rules are always the same and in theory if you just think hard enough there's no way you can lose! However you always miss that one archer in the corner or that imp. It's strategy in its purest (and arguably best) form.

Whilst the game is local only it scales in difficulty with your ability. The more matches you win the higher your ranking the more difficult the scenarios. However if you fail a match you lose ranking and thus the difficulty decreases. Ideally this means that you slowly hone your skills and hit rank 10! (I'm still only at rank 5... it gets hard fast).

If that's not enough for you the game also includes the 'Dark World' Expansion which adds an entirely new mode. It features completely new pieces for you and the computer which allow for abilities that wrap around the board, create or detonate explosives and lots of other new abilities that make for an even tougher experience. This again comes with its own laddering system. It pretty much doubles the amount of content in the game.

Militia is a great game and one I'm likely to lose a fair amount of time to. It's simple yet compelling and likely appeals to a very wide audience including those who do not traditionally play games. The game is great on Steam but also maybe better as a portable game which is available on tablets. The developer also looks to be adding even more content in the future such as endless mode and time attack so it seems that I'll lose even more time to it.
