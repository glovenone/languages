About This Game

CASHTRONAUTS is an arcade space combat & trading game that takes place in an alternate universe where capitalist dinosaurs rule the galaxy. Fighting viciously to climb to the top of the economic food chain; how your make your millions is entirely up to you. Will you be a bounty hunter, a pirate, a smuggler or a miner?

As you progress, you unlock Risks: gameplay modifiers that are toggled on/off at your whim. Each one will introduce new elements and dangers to your next run, but also grants you a bonus to your income. If you rise to the challenge your profit margins will soar!

The stealthy Raptor pirates are always looking for their loot, especially if that loot is your burnt-out remains. And then, there's the soft-skinned mammalian threat looming on the horizon... but you'll burn that bridge when you reach it. Space is a dangerous place even at the best of times. Will you have what it takes to beat out the competition and dominate the market?

Key Features

  • Unique gameplay: Space combat & trading sim meets fast-paced arcade action
  • Varied ship types: Everything from the nimble Vermin fighter to the lumbering Stego mining barge
  • Splitscreen multiplayer: 1-4 players all contributing to the same bottom line. Will you cooperate or fight for the company's number one spot? (Gamepad required)
  • Constant challenge: The game evolves as you do: unlock new Risks as you progress to raise the stakes of your next run
  • Dynamic factions & AI: Tricera-cops are trying to stop a Raptor attack a Stego mining vessel. Do you jump in and help, or wait until it's all over and scavenge the remains?
  • Tight controls: Enjoy fine-tuned controls for keyboard & mouse and gamepad with "juicy" weapon recoil, kickback and effects
  • Beauty: Stylized, colourful flat-shaded 3D world paired with striking 2D character portraits
  • Steamworks: Steam cloud, achievements, trading cards and leaderboard
  • Kickin' tunes: New soundtrack from Patrice Bourgeault (Mercenary Kings, Curses & Chaos)

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: 2.9 GHz
    • Memory: 6 GB RAM
    • Graphics: AMD Radeon HD 6670
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 210 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: Note: For smooth framerates during splitscreen multiplayer better hardware is recommended.

Disclaimer - I have been gifted a free key for this game for review purposes, however my review will be kept as truthful as possible, since I am meant to help developers and buyers/gamers.

Cashtronauts Review:

Overview
Cashtronauts is a game where you get rich and unlock risks, which then give boosts to your income depending on how hard they are. You can collect money by selling ores and Teeth dropped by Raptors, which are enemy ships who are on the hunt for you once you go outside your city barrier. You have limited fuel and ammunition, which can be refilled/restocked, so is your health, however it's not replenishable, so fight wisely! You can carry only so much items at a time, whether it to be teeth or ore, so you have to make many trips from outer space back to your settlement to be as efficient as possible ,you're going to need lucky and hopefully you won't bump into any cops or Raptors trying to kill you. Speaking of cops and murder, don't carry red ores! You will be murdered, it is illegal to carry around. This is a sandbox game, where the only objective is to get as much money as you can in a run, which unlocks more risks once certain money milestones are achieved to get even more money. Some risks give invisibility cloaks to unseen Raptors, other introduce new gameplay mechanics, such as civilian transport ships (murderable, carry lots of money, will get you into police trouble) and generally the more riks the more fun the game is.

Gameplay
It's a sandbox playing area which at early game is a bit difficult to navigate due to not having any minimap of any sort, but easy to pick up the general game. The speed rapidly builds up as you unlock the harder risks, such as sometimes while on low fuel you accidentally shoot a passerby insetad of an enemy. This builds up adrenalin and tense times that will be remembered. This technique leads to many addictions being made along with an actual relationship to the ship you're playing as. It's simple to understand. You get items to sell, you sell them, get rich, restock, repeat. Over, and over, and over. It's fun, knowing your ship can blow up at any time when your about to beat an all time high score.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
Fun
Simple
Addictive
Cheap
More game coming
Clean graphical look

Cons:
Not that much game once you get later into it

Finale
The game is good right now, will only get better as time goes along. Gets an 8/10 from me.

Early Access Review
Posted: 16 August, 2015
A compulsive twin stick asteroid-mining rogue-like set in procedurally generated space with a brilliant "risk" system where at the start of the game, you can choose to enable any or all of 10 different scenarios to make the game more difficult in return for a higher percentage of profit.
One risk makes your armour weak so only one hit can be taken. Another adds gold to some asteroids but also adds competition in the form of big mining ships.
The game eases you in gently, the 10 risks are unlocked as you play, letting you add more challenge as you get more confident.

You have a starting area which includes an ammo and fuel shop, a shop to sell minerals to, a place for upgrading your ship parts and a police station which rewards cash-monies for any teeth you bring them as proof of a baddy bashed.
From here, you go out exploring space, blowing up asteroids to find juicy minerals to sell on for profit, searching for cash crates, ship upgrades to beef up your engines and weapons and bad lads who drop teeth and cash on death.
Blasting benign civilian ships into space dust and collecting their cash and hopefully ship upgrade parts will set off a GTA style wanted state where police will chase you around the starting area until it wears off.

The game keeps you on your toes, every time I've gotten cocky or I've started to relax a bit, I've been chinned.
The starting screen shows your own personal high score and the highest score from around the world for that day which spurs you on to have "just one more shot".
This is early access but feels like a complete and polished game already. There are no reasons not to jump into this right now.
60fps, plays great with both pad and keyboard and mouse.
Highly recommended.

The music is also worth a special mention, can't stop bopping in my chair while playing this :D

Early Access Review
Posted: 13 August, 2015
Cashtronauts is a twin-stick shooter where your only goal is to become as rich as possible. No matter how many innocents (or pirates) need to die along the way you must become the wealthiest spaceship pilot in the galaxy.

We've all played twin-sticker shooters before so we know that aspect of gameplay. It works well here, one stick to move and one to shoot it's pretty simple. What makes this game special then? It's the mechanics built around the shooter.

You are playing in space where you have to monitor your ammo, fuel, and cargo space. The ammo and fuel is purchasable from two floating space stations. You cargo can be ejected from your ship if you need to or you can sell it to the mining space station. You earn money by collecting floating cash in space, destroying asteroids to pick up contraband to sell to the miner, or kill all the pirates you want (or kill the police and stupid innocent people).

Cashtronauts introduces a Risk mechanic, where as you play and collect money you will unlock one of several different risks. Each risk increases the percentage of money you earn, whether that be collecting it from crates, shooting pirates or cops, or turning in mined drugs (red pill or blue pill, I'm not sure if they are drugs but they look like space drugs to me. In addition to increasing the money earned percentage, each risk also changes the game in someway. The first I unlocked made the Raptor enemies gain cloak, it also made them drop Raptor Teeth when they died, which I could turn in at the police station for a nice bounty. The second added Reddium, which was an illegal red mined ore (or drug) that I could sell to the miner, however, being illegal it would make me wanted by the police until I sold the cargo or dumped it. You can choose which, if any, of the risks you want to use in between each run.

Overall I think Cashtronauts takes the twin-stick shooter in a challenging and fun direction and the risk mechanic adds for a nice depth too the gameplay. I'm looking forward to new risks and enemies as the game makes it's way through early access.
