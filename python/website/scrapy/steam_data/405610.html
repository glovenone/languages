About This Game

Flight of the Paladin is a fast-paced top-down shooter where you play as a knight of valor and light vanquishing the nightmarish creatures of darkness. Inspired by the old school arcade-styled shooters, shoot your way to victory as a lone champion of justice.

In Campaign Mode, though your quest begins easily enough do not be lax in your crusade, Paladin, or you may soon be surrounded and overwhelmed. Every second the path grows more treacherous and the minions of evil increase in number. But take heart, you have the protection of goodness on your side! Subdue your enemies quickly by unleashing that power on your foes. Each stage ends with a boss that needs to be vanquished to move on to the next area. Navigate through the deep Woods, take on the Hollow Path, and you might find yourself at the Castle Ruins of Dracula!

In Survival Mode face waves of challenging monsters and random bosses to try to survive as long as possible. Can you stand shoulder to shoulder with the best Paladins on the Leaderboards? Play now to put your skills to the test!

I thought I'd give this a try, seeing how it's only $2. Unfortunately you get what you pay for and this game was sadly not particularly good.

The gameplay is very simple, as demonstrated in the trailer on Steam. But that's also all there's to it: a very light shoot 'em up.

The game is incredibly repetitive. Monsters come at you from the exact same angles every few seconds, making the game more of a pattern recognition exercise than anything. You have a basic attack that you can pretty much spam continuously and a whirlwind attack that needs to be charged but only kills certain enemies. The lack of a health bar, score meter or progress meter to the next boss is very annoying.

Graphically there's really nothing to it. Playing it full screen is not recommended, as it makes all the ugly stand out.

There's also not much sound. Aside from the looping organ music, the sound assets seem limited to a whopping 3 sound bites.
