About This Game

The once-prosperous aerial kingdom of Lunnain lies in tatters, assaulted from above by the Fallen - ruthless monsters of nightmare who seek only complete destruction.

But the people will not take their fate lying down, and their strike back at the heavens starts now!

Challenge the world and ascend the rankings until you rule over all!

KEY FEATURES:
  • Battle against players across the world in simple yet deep tactical battles!
  • Collect and train over 700 fully-animated units
  • Embark on an epic quest across Lunnain to save the legendary Seven Sisters
  • Climb the Sacred League to earn epic rewards
  • Take part in regular events and weekend Arena competitions
  • Features characters designed by Ryoma Ito (FINAL FANTASY TACTICS ADVANCE) and music by Ryo Yamazaki (FINAL FANTASY CRYSTAL CHRONICLES THE CRYSTAL BEARERS)

GAME SYSTEM:
PVP (LEAGUE)
Battle players across the globe in real-time multiplayer!
Feel the pounding tension that you only get from real-time competition.

SQUADS
Collect, strengthen and evolve over 200 units into your own unique squad!
The interplay of your various units creates endless tactical possibilities!

RANKING
Compete in regular league battles and special arena tournaments!
Fight your way to the top of the world rankings.

Fun game that you can accumulate a lot in with little effort over time. Once the regular story missions are complete, a level opens up with a 20% chance for 1 of the premium currency and it levels you up fast. I stay logged in and do this mission on auto repeatedly and sometimes get a lot of crystals. Note that while new events have the guaranteed legendary unit pool, returning events do not, so don't spend 150 crystals expecting a Juliet during the steam event like me yesterday. Not only did I get a bad legendary, I could have just used that final pull on a different event with a guaranteed legendary. Check out guides for restarting until you get good units, and you will never need to spend money. I did once, only to then realize how powerful the Cain I had already gotten for free was. I would only spend money now if the Cain event came back and I had no free crystals saved up. They just made PVP unlimited so, like Hearthstone, you can always find a game. Here are units to save your crystals for: Dracula, Juliet, Cain. Also, units gain a skill up when they are promoted or combined with a like unit, and rank 5 is highest. You can only have 2 of each unit in a squad, so combine 4 of the same unit each because their skill be maxed when they reach level 15 and are promoted. One last trick is for extra units. It seems to be most effective to make pairs of extra 1/2 star characters to save for experience fodder for other units. If you have extra promotion materials, it may be worth leveling them to 15 and promoting them before sacrificing them, but pairing them up is quick and easy and will help when characters only have a sliver left to level up and you don't want to waste a whole XP boost item. Learn from my mistakes!

Heavenstrike doesn't suffer too much from being a mobile port in terms of control. The music is fine, I don't particularly like the art style, but I can respect it enough to get over it. My suggestion is that if you're interested enough to be reading reviews, you might as well just give it a go. It's free to download, and it's a fairly short one at that. It'll be pretty quick to see for yourself why it's so bad, but here's my experience anyway.

As far as game play, you drag and drop troops in lanes, and they automatically act at the end of your turn. There is some strategy, but it's not particularly deep. There are unit types, but you'll want to stick with your best units regardless of composition or tactics. It says "Tactical RPG" and the tactics are limited, but the RPG part even more so. It's a moot point, but I don't consider it "Role playing" when you're just following a set story that you have no input or control over.

There isn't much for character customization, you pick from a handful of avatars, and obviously a name, and that's about it. There are a few skills where you can select one from, and swap it out at any time.

The game itself is unoriginal, I've played thousands like it. It's a little better produced, but it's on par with the old flash games of the newgrounds era.

The game is plagued by pay walls. There is an energy system, so you can only play a few battles every few hours, or pay to regain your energy. It's the worst kind of pay wall. They also sell units in the item shop, and just about anything you could want.

Normally I'd let it go, and not bother writing a review. A game so typical isn't worth it. Being free, you should know what you're getting into. Being a short download, what have you really lost? I don't expect younger folks to understand, but Square/Enix put their name on this. Square and Enix both had such an impact on my childhood, especially in the SNES era. To see the two merge, and be reduced to putting out bland, unoriginal mobile games is disheartening.
