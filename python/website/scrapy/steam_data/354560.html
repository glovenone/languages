About This Game

Ancient China, 220 AD. China is in disarray and within years the population plummets from 60 million to less than 20 million. Massive war involving millions of men devastates the land. When General Huangfu Song 義真 was asked how he got around the unit caps to build such massive armies, he replied "小馬是世界上最好的開發商" which in the barbarian tongue of English means roughly, "What the %$#^ are unit caps, this is war!"

Forget about micro. Forget about build orders. This war is about one thing. Ridiculous, uncompromising, seething masses of blood hungry warriors massacring each other by the hundreds every second.

There is no unit cap. Control armies of hundreds of thousands, even millions. Send orders to a single unit, or send orders to a million units. Frantically maintain control of your resources across epic sized maps, while constantly building out your fleet of barracks to churn out millions of more units.

I don't know if fast paced, retro arcade style strategy is a thing, but if it is, this is it. 我會看到你在戰場上。

The only reason this review is negative is because the developer seems to have abandoned this promising title.

TL;DR: The game is fun, but it's limited to multiplayer. Wait till the devs start updating it again before you buy it.

Some friends and I bought this game after talking to the devs and seeing gameplay at RTX 2015, and it seemed absolutely worth it. Aside from some initial technical difficulties (which were quickly fixed by the devs within a day of us bringing it to their attention) they game was really fun. It's fun to play, has decent music, and functioning multiplayer. Unfortunately, that’s essentially as far as it goes. There isn't really any single player aside from the training maps, but those are essentially just you trying to build up your army fast enough to not be overwhelmed by the preplaced enemy buildings. There is no AI that tries to out build you or gather more resources or anything of that nature, so the only real strategical matches you'll play in this game will be in multiplayer. Nonetheless, if you have someone to play with, then you'll have a good time.
Also, after launch the devs added a "Map Editor" feature that allows you to design your own scenarios and maps (much like the scenario editor in Rise of Nations). This is a great addition; however, you cannot use the custom maps in multiplayer matches. Thus, the feature is somewhat worthless considering the lack of a single player AI.
Honestly, this game feels like it should have been an early access release considering the sheer lack of features. And like many early access releases, the developers stopped pumping out patches and additions in September.
Overall, I wouldn’t buy this game unless it is heavily discounted or the changelogs reveal some more recent updates.

Posted: 18 August, 2015
I absolutely love this game.

At first, I thought it was just another 2D RTS game that had really bad graphics. After playing a session for like an hour or so though, I love the concept. They made an RTS game that is as minimal as possible. Literally zero options for units, buildings, or anything of that sort. You just have a barracks that automatically spews out enemies and you place gold mines on gold mines to generate money. The Jade system is basically a special currency of special weapons. You need it to cast nukes and such. Harvested exactly the same as gold, you place little jade harvesters on jade mines.

You have the ability to send 1000's of units into battle at once. The game is all about volume and strategy. Your unit creating barracks will automatically send them to a desired waypoint, so you can literally create 100's of barracks all over the map and send them to your enemies bases or you could try to surround your enemy with barracks so they are just getting infinitely bombarded. And you will have armies to face as well. The sheer numbers of units you will be using is so entertaining. It would be too complicated to have direct control on any of them so it's great how you can just focus on setting the waypoints to your battles so the fresh units will be at the right place. You could also build up an army of a zillion units to send in all at once and overwhelm them.

I only played one match so far but I plan on playing the game a lot more often, I just wanted to post my thoughts here. It's not a game I could see growing old anytime soon since it's so shallow there isn't much to get bored of right off the bat, but no other game I have played uses these very simple rules with insane numbers. It instantly became one of my favorite RTS's of all time in a very short period of time.

Something you notice early on is that the cost of each barrack or gold mine increases with each one added. Also, the amount of jade required for a nuke (or any other ability) increases with each use. This helps to bring balance to the game over periods of time!

There are some aspects I would like improved, but the main drawback is that the pathfinding is not very bright. Sometimes the units get confused and just stop. Or one prime example is I had a barracks creating units and sending them across the map to the north-east but 20% of the units created were being sent into a corner and pooling up for no apparent reason, even if I changed the waypoint they kept just leaking out into the corner (in the complete opposite direction). That being said though, for something this "indie", they have created something very unique and worth checking out.

If you love board games, such as Risk, or are even a fan of old-school RTS games like Rise of Nations, you should definitely pick up this game.

WAL is a game of war in which the overall battle can change in seconds. You start out with a Dragon Lord (General), and possibly a handful of troops, and start out to build Gold Factories and Jade Factories where the resources can be found. You use Gold to build more factories as well as barracks. Jade is gathered so the Dragon Lord can cast spells to harm your opponnent/strengthen your position. The sole purpose of Barracks is to continuously and indefinitely pump out soldiers at no further cost to you. The more barracks you have, the quicker you can build an unstoppable legion. And from there, use your wits and strategize against your opponents.

Overall, WAL is a great RTS game to play either solo or with friends. So what are you waiting for? Play it!

The most basic RTS elements would work wonders on this game:

- more than three units types(ranged shooting units)
