Raywin
Raywin is a 90's style 2d RPG. The story focuses around the main protagonist Raywin who, because of prophecy, has to go off to fight in the war of Salemsun and save the kingdom. Along the way he is accompanied by his friend Helena who he at first hates, but along the way they grow closer than ever.

Saw all the negative reviews and thought, "Oh, it can't be that bad. People are probably just being harsh because it's an RPGMaker game."
Got the game in a bundle.
Started it up.
First room, immediately climbed up the wall.
-_-

Short version: it is that bad. Like, if other bad RPGMaker games are Twilight bad, Raywin is legolas by laura bad. It's so bad I now feel guilty for every other negative Steam review I have written.

There's no intro or tutorial or anything, the game just starts with you standing in a room. I know what the default RPGMaker controls are, so I'm not going to complain about that. I will complain about not being able to interact with anything in the room. Trying to talk to your father does nothing. You have to try to leave the house for his scripted dialogue to pop up. (And of course all the dialogue in the game is full of typos and poor comprehension of how English works.)

Oh, and you can walk up the walls. This isn't a one-off thing; it happens in every room and dungeon. Consider it a feature, I guess. The prophecy foretold of the one with magic wall-walking powers. It makes as much sense as your random ability to turn into a rock later on.

Second town map, "The door is locked," you're told upon trying to enter a house that has no door. And you pick vegetables by walking over them and pressing Z, and why did I even bother because it's just some random filler side quest?

Then I realized the main quest and story has about as much depth. Like, you're the chosen one of prophecy (the game helpfully informs you in a brief text scroll) because you're a fighter traveling with a mage, which has somehow never happened before in RPG Cliche Land. And... that's it.

The maps are all poorly designed, particularly the stores and houses, which are big, awkward empty spaces apparently designed by someone who's never seen the inside of a building. The best/worst is one store that's literally just a full screen of empty wooden floor and a counter, with a bed in the top corner. When the storekeeper goes to give your Healing Macguffin to the girl in bed, he's so far up the screen that his head is cut off.

Oh, and the bed with the girl just disappears. "Oh, who cares," I thought at this point. "At least I can just continue with the game." Except the quest reward was only one half of what you need to continue -- apparently you can't open the plot door with your locksmith tools unless you have a pouch to put them in. (And you can't walk around the door, either, even though it's just a regular house-sized door lying in the middle of an open gate that's three spaces wide.)

So I wandered around trying to find a locksmith tools pouch and got attacked by eye beasts. One at a time, because I guess programming more than one enemy sprite on the battle screen was too difficult?

And why eye beasts? If you're going to face hordes of enemies that have no special abilities, do negligible damage, and go down in like three hits, why something that looks so alien and dangerous? (Why am I trying to apply logic to Raywin?)

If you get past those, you have to guess your way past a riddle, go into a labyrinth (which is actually just a room) and rescue a "Preist," who gives you the locksmith tools pouch, and blah blah blah, you get on a ship, you have to catch stowaways from the Clan of Stowaways, who show up and join your party before you actually receive the quest to find them. I was going to say at least the game doesn't bug out completely, but then I was told to take off my stowaway disguise that I had to put on to infiltrate the secret stowaway meeting, and the game just froze, and this is the point where I just give up and idle the game for cards.

This is the kind of game you label "Unfinished Test Project" and release for free to get feedback, only to delete later out of embarrassment. The fact that someone looked at Raywin and thought, "Yes, I deserve money for my efforts," is ludicrous. Either they're straight up delusional, or they're just taking the p*ss.

I'm probably one of the first to finish the game. It took me a while longer than it should have because I had to contact the dev to figure out a bug. A regular play through would probably take 45 minutes at the most. First things first, the dev is very helpful and great. Unfortunately the game has a ways to go before it's worth buying.

There are some glaring problems, and I'll try to make this review as constructive as possible so hopefully Raywin 2 addresses these problems.

1. There is no tutorial to learn the controls. They are easy enough but you first need to try all your keys to see if they do anything.

2. Items don't tell you what they do. You have a healing potion? Great! But it doesn't tell you how much it will heal you. Vegatables? Fun! What are they even used for? I didn't use an item throughout the entire game.

3. You can't see the HP of the enemy. How am I supposed to know when to heal/attack? So far I just attack and eventually win, and have some NPC heal me. Not much strategy.

4. Wall/noclip glitches everywhere. Doesn't feel like a complete game. Not to mention, 95% of every shop screen is empty space. There is no decoration, and on the off-chance there is decoration, half the time you can walk right through it.

5. Can't go back to places already visited. Found that out too late, so I think I missed some content.

6. The "quests" are as simple as "walk 5 spaces and click 'space' 8 times then come back". Another quest you literally turn into a rock, for no good reason, to sneak by some guards. That's the only time you turn into a rock in the whole game. I would understand if it the game was introducing a new mechanic, but it's not. I just don't get it.

One nice part about the game is the leveling system. It seems really cool, but since you only defeat maybe 5 enemies (you can avoid the rest without problem) you don't really get to try it out. At the end, each of my characters levelled one.. to level 2. I feel like this would have been a fun part of the game, I just wish I was able to take advantage of it!

Overall, not a very gripping game. Needs a lot of work fixing some bugs. I don't recommend it in its current state.

**To get into the museum, you need to press space to enter. This is a confirmed bug so may not be relevant if the dev fixes the bug before you read this review.

The key for this game was indirectly given to me by the developer for review purposes but it will not change the tone of my review

Raywin is a 2D JRPG made on RPG Maker. Just as the name of the game suggests, the story is focused on the hero Raywin who according to a prophecy must defeat Salemsun. The game is just bad, even if it is the first game that the developers made.

Heck, one of the game's selling points is having "★ Over 40 items" which isn't even that much compared to other RPG Maker games or RPGs in general. Let me start of by saying the game is incredibly short and overall not a fun experience.

Where do I start with the flaws of this game.... First of all the game has tons of noclip/wall glitches, the first one being on the first room, yes you read right the first room. I know it’s the first game they made and they used floor tiles to give the walls effect but still, they should have tested it before release. One bug that I encountered is that you need to press space to enter the museum.

Uh.... the quests which maybe the worst part about the game. The quests just involve you going to some place maybe two to four screens away to rescue a person or get an item for someone and maybe fighting a monster along the way.

Graphics wise the game is mediocre at best, the sprites are basic and what you would expect from an RPG Maker game, nothing special but dear god.... the dialogue character faces and overworld monster sprites just look horrendous. If a game has its entire overworld pixelated then keep the graphics the same at least with no need for 3D CGI images, though this is entirely my opinion.

This game like any other standard RPG Maker game has item drops after battles but the item descriptions and the mundane items themselves are god awful.

Here is a scenario : Oh great! a monster dropped an orange maybe I could use it to heal one of my characters. But wait! It doesn't tell you how much it will heal, same goes with the monsters whose health you cannot see so you don't know when to heal or attack. However, you won't ever need to strategize because every encounter is a cakewalk.


This game like any other standard RPG Maker game has item drops after battles but the item descriptions and the mundane items themselves are god awful. To point out in a game based in a fantasy world our main healing items are not potions, but fruits. Always remember to eat healthy kids.

Moreover, I had to fight like what? Only 8 monsters including the bosses throughout the game! (Monsters not encounters) On a sidenote the game is barren, huge houses with one or two NPCs with bland surroundings.

Before I end the review I would like to mention that the story is generic even for RPG Maker games, just the same old chosen one story which might I add has been outdated since the dawn of the fourth generation of gaming consoles!

This game though being the developers first try to make a good game is just an unfinished, sloppy RPG Maker test project. The only good thing about the game is that you can get an extra perfect game because all the achievements in this game can be earned throughout the game which is about 1 hour long.

Without any innovative mechanics or a fresh story, Raywin fails to separate itself from the generic RPG Maker games. It is not at all worth the buy.
