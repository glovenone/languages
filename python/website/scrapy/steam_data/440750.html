About This Game

Take control of the Cube and blast your way through an abstract world full of danger.

Story of a Cube is juicy and fast-paced shooter starring a cube who sets out to take revenge on the evil circles who kidnapped his family.

  • Shoot.
    Armed with a mysterious weapon left behind by the circles, you must fight your way through six challenging chapters. Shoot everything that moves and make sure to counter bullets with bullets!

  • Dodge.
    Watch out for crushing spikes and other terrifying things as you navigate the maze-like levels and try to stay alive. Slow down time just enough to dodge the bullets coming at you from all directions.

  • Fight the Bosses.
    Each chapter contains an epic, genre-defying battle that will be sure to test your limits. Get hunted by a swarm of missiles, avoid being crushed by the wall of doom and try not to explode.

  • Solve the Mystery.
    Why did the circles abduct your family? What will they do with them? Will you make it out alive? Find out as the plot slowly unravels and you are faced with the horrifying truth.

Story of a cube just isn't a well executed bullet hell/shmup game. The bullets are hard to process all at once, and it feels almost certain you are going to get hit, and attacking feels off as well. This is normally the point at which I would say to myself, "That's not the game's fault, I'm just not very good at it yet." The problem is, it has a TERRIBLE respawn system. You have 10 lives, and it uses one to respawn you at the last checkpoint every time you die. This doesn't reset any of the enemies, it is literally just walking slowly through an empty box back to where you were. In boss battles, the checkpoint is not right outside the boss room, but instead inside, so you are thrown right back in every time you die. And when your 10 lives run out, you have to restart the level. This may not sound so bad, but combined with the unorigional plot of "We were happy until the circles came, circles are evil, you can save people from the circles, etc." and the inability to skip dialogue, I hope you have some good reading material on hand if you plan on dying a lot.
