About This Game

A single player exploration driven adventure with survival elements, set on a mysterious and seemingly uninhabited alien planet. Survive through exploration, and unlock the mysteries of Gliese-6143-C. From the creators of "Unmechanical" and successor to "The Ball".

Earth has been destroyed and mankind lingers on in a small fleet of ships near Pluto. As humanity's last hope, you are sent to scout a distant planet as a potential location for a colony. After years of space travel disaster strikes however, and your ship is destroyed when approaching the planet...

With your team members dead and your equipment gone you have no way of communicating or receiving help from anyone... you are completely and utterly alone...

Desperately you look for a way of phoning home, but as you explore the seemingly desolate planet, you uncover the dark secrets buried within the depths. In this otherworldly odyssey you uncover mystery upon mystery as you try to survive the harsh climate.

FEATURES

  • Explore - Unlock the depths of the uncharted planet, and discover the secrets buried deep below. Delve into huge cave systems and tombs and uncover over 200 secrets.
  • Handcrafted - 10 large entirely handcrafted levels to explore. Explore and puzzle your way through 5 islands and 5 large tombs/caves.
  • Atmosphere - Atmosphere rich environment that is fully dynamic. An alien day and night cycle and a dynamic weather system that calculates everything from temperature, wind, humidity, cloud coverage impact on the climate and so on. It handles ocean tides. Plants respond to the climate. Storms form. Earthquakes happen.
  • Survive - Survive the extreme climate, tornadoes, storms, meteors, lightning, and not least the dark secrets buried beneath the surface.
  • VR - Supports Vive and Oculus, including room space and motion controllers. Explore the huge world start to end in VR! (*Some VR features are still in development - see notes at the bottom for info)



FEATURES CONTINUED
  • Depth - Plants grow and rot. Ocean tide based on the moon. Your body responds to everything from getting wet to lack of sleep to walking uphill to the humidity. Hundreds of upgrades to be found. Secret areas within secret areas within secret areas.
  • Savior - Your death means the end of mankind. Save us and rebuild a communication device to phone home, by scavenging useful items.
  • Adventure - Be a pioneer, an archaeologist, and an astronaut to explore a mysterious alien planet. The adventure has a beginning and an end, but it is a harsh world. A mistake will cost you your life.



GAMEPLAY
  • Linear - The game has a start and end. It is a singleplayer adventure with 10 large levels.
  • Your Time- Scale the game as you wish. Go from a super hardcore experience to a relaxing stroll on an alien planet. In fact you can cheat old school console style even. It is your time, your experience.
  • Just You - Little hand holding. No obvious enemies. It is just you. All alone by yourself.
  • Old school - Fond memories of games from 10, 20 years ago? So do we. Complex level layouts. Dozens of secret areas. Keys to unlock doors. Underground environments. A ~ key to open the game console.
  • Exploration - There are hundreds and hundreds of things to find hidden away in the world. Secret areas the size of modern "next gen levels". Just how deep does the rabbit hole go?
  • Puzzles - Puzzle your way through environmental puzzles left by an alien civilization, like our titles Unmechanical and The Ball.

THE PLANET
  • World - Gliese-6143-C is a harsh and barren planet, covered by a vast ocean. Days are over 45C/113F, and nights under -30C/F.
  • Archipelago - 5 islands, with a shallow sea between them and connected through a system of caves. To travel from one island to the next you must traverse these dark caverns.
  • Wreckage - The spaceship in which you arrived crashed on arrival, and its debris is scattered across the world. Find and locate debris sites to find additional resources.
  • Tombs - Megalithic tombs are all over. Clearly you were not the first intelligent being to have set foot on this planet. Who was here before? Where did they go? What happened?



VR
  • Vive/Oculus - Works on both. Supports Xbox controller on Oculus, and either the Xbox controller or motion controllers on Vive. Supports room scale for Vive
  • Movement - Supports blink movement, regular locomotion walking, and teleporting. For rotating you can pick between room scale rotation, snapped rotation (45 degree steps), smooth rotation, or enable strafing. Movement speed can also be scaled freely.
  • High end and huge - This is not a small VR demo or game. It is a massive 16 hour experiences with miles and miles worth of world to explore, and provided you have a powerful machine (980 and up) you can experience it with some of the most ambitious high end graphics tried in VR to date.

TSP is not a pure VR game. It was designed for non-VR gameplay and thus a VR headset is in no way required to play the game. If you do have one, it is an awesome (but different) experience!

VR is work in progress - Currently not implemented: VR Loading screens, VR Inventory menu, VR in-game tutorial system. VR additional movement options are being developed. Everything else works solidly.



ABOUT US

The Solus Project is the brainchild of Sjoerd "Hourences" De Jong. As a one-man army he crafted the vision for the game and a large part of the game's looks. Sjoerd has over 12 years of experience in the industry and has worked for a range of large and small studios such as Starbreeze, Epic Games, Guerrilla Games, Digital Extremes, and many more.

The game has been in production since July 2013, and has a team of 5 to 10 people working on it.

Teotl is a small indie studio that was founded in 2010 and is located in Sweden. It released The Ball on PC in 2010 and followed up with Unmechanical Unmechanical on PC and iOS/Android in 2012.
The studio focuses on creating high quality and immersive single player experiences. The Ball won the PC Gamer USA Action / Adventure Game of the Year 2010 award.

Grip Digital is a privately owned developer and publisher of games for consoles and high-end portable devices, based in Prague, Czech Republic. The company was started by industry veterans who worked on games across all major platforms. Grip Games is working with the top independent developers to bring their games to console platforms.

I played this in VR and even though VR-mode is currently only in Beta, I found the experience amazing and enthraling. Would totally recommend it.

The sense of presence in some of the areas (particularly the caves) is huge. And when various monsters appear (which happens only after a few hours of peaceful exploration) then they have a real sense of danger. Particularly when you are waiting in the dark listening to a monster passing, and afraid to equip your torch for fear it sees it. And yet there aren't really any jump-scares, just occassional areas which give a sense of dread. This is an exploration game, not a horror game.

VR-mode has had some negative reviews lately so I'll say that the main problem right now is the inventory. Instead of selecting an item from a list (in Monitor mode) you have to press a button up to 15 or so times to cycle round every object you own. And while doing this you naturally put away your only light source and so are left in a dark cave with spooky noises while you fumble for 20 seconds with your backpack. You get used to dropping a torch before swapping items to avoid this, but it is still a pain. I am confident the devs will implement a better method soon. The second issue is over locomotion. You can move with the pad but this makes you feel sick. Luckily you can also teleport about, so naturally you do that and forget about the pad. But there are a few points in the game where you must either walk into something to push it, or walk directly over something to trigger it. If you are teleporting, you never think to do this (as you naturally teleport beside things and don't touch the pad), and so the puzzles are insolvable. I had to watch a Let's Play to work out the "pushing walls" puzzle.

But these things aside, this is easily the most in-depth game you can buy for the Vive right now, which I can easily spend a couple of hours playing in VR in a single session without fatigue, and I look forward to playing it for many hours to come.

Review is based on the non VR version of the game.

I am fascinated by the end of the world topic / exploring a distant alien planet and this kind of ScFi adventure games as such. This game delivers it all in very good quality! I’ve been following its development since 2013 and now I have seen the end!

The main objective is to reestablish the radio transmission / call out for help. But this isn’t as easy as it seems at first glance. You have to survive the extreme climate (tornadoes, storms, meteors, etc.) and the deadly/dark secrets buried beneath the surface of this hostile planet. This journey can be done with a female or male character.

Gameplay: First person, main focus is clearly exploration, but with survival elements. You can save your progress almost always, when you're sleeping.

Graphics (Unreal Engine): Amazing day and night cycles. The very decent graphics, with great attention to detail, reflected in diversified level design fills the planet and its hazards with life. There are a lot of settings to adjust the details of graphics quality. (Presets, VSync, Field of view, etc.)

Sound: Realistic, well done quality of waves, thunderstorms, the howling of the wind, etc.
There are very atmospheric, selectively placed elements of music.

Score: The story and dense atmosphere kept me hooked from the very beginning till the bitter end…! Clear recommendation! 9/10

This is a VR review of the game.



Are there flaws with the VR? Yes. Could it have been done better? Yes. That said, this is still the single best experience so far for the HTC Vive I've played, approximately 40 or so games/experiences (Haven't played starseed yet).

All in all, the pros vastly outweigh the cons and this is a game any vive owner should have. Please support the developers and write them a good review as this game is exceptional and the few people who cant read that VR is a work in progress, should not tarnish its reputation.



5/5 Stars, absolutely would recommend!

The Solus Project is too simplistic to be a survival game, too easy to be an old-school adventure game, and too linear and dull to be an exploration game.

Survival elements in the game are so dumbed down, they're pointless. Your character doesn't starve or dehydrate as quickly as actual survival games and you have easy access to unlimited food and water at frequent resting spots throughout the game, and other food/water items are easy enough to find anyways. Crafting is extremely limited, with too few items and combinations. Surviving the planet's climate extremes is marginally better, but you spend the majority of the game underground in low temperatures, and you get access to a heating ball fairly early on that renders most of the surviving parts irrelevant.

The game's attempt at 'puzzles' is laughably poor. They are either (1) find key to stick in lock/device, (2) put rocks on pressure plates to open door, (3) step on/operate mechanism to select clearly-marked symbol, and if we're being really generous, (4) press button to open door. None of these would ever count as real puzzles in any other game, but this is the best The Solus Project can do. Clearly, this was meant to be the spiritual successor to developer's previous game The Ball only because it involves giant metal spheres as plot devices.

And finally, the exploration is limited. You start on a very small island with terrain that, while not completely impassable, locks you into only a handful of paths, most of which just lead you to the single cave/ladder entrance to the island's underground. From there it's a single underground path to travel through, hitting the occasional large space that, again, has a single entrance and single exit, any number of the previously-mentioned non-puzzles, and then you hit the loading screen that takes you to the next island, where you do it all over again. In theory, you can try and wander everywhere to find the secrets the game keeps count of. You could just pull out the teleporter and squeeze yourself into every nook and cranny, or start jumping around the rocks to get places, but what's the point? The secrets you find are items that boost your survival stats, but the survival gameplay as described earlier is completely pointless.

And it's not like the setting and story are interesting enough to explore, either. You're basically just walking through the same-old tunnels to find just different varieties of underground temples with different collections of skeletons inside. You figure there would be an interesting story to them, but The Solus Project also falls apart here. The game's only way of communicating is to make you read exposition from these giant stone tablets that your tricorder-like Wilson device translates for you. It's clunky and uninteresting, and you quickly get bored of stopping to read everything.

Overall, The Solus Project is a terrible game. If you want a survival game, go buy a real survival game. If you want an adventure game, get one with actual puzzles. If you want something with an interesting world to explore, there are a ton of other options that aren't even open-world. Whatever it is you want, The Solus Project isn't it. Save your money and your time.

TLDR at the bottom - The Solus Project is an adventure exploration game that borrows some aspects from survival games. That said, while it is not by any means a survival game like the ones that’ve flooded Steam over the past few years, I think this is one of the few that delivers a true survival experience, even if it lacks in many aspects.

Earth is doomed, and in an effort to save Humankind, the UN creates the Solus Project, a series of ships destined to search for a habitable planet for the top tier scientists to colonize. You are sent to Gliese, one of the most promissing planets, before the rest of the fleet in order to scout the surface and determine if it can sustain life. Unfortunately, after years of space travel you crash land upon arrival and soon find yourself stranded, all by yourself and in a constant fight for survival in an unknown and alien world. Welcome to the Solus Project.

Right from the start the game introduces you to the light survival elements that it has, which consist mostly on barebones crafting and collecting items. You’ll have to keep an eye on your PDA for your calories and thirst meter as well as body temperature, wind velocity, condensation, ambient temperature, health and sleep deprivation meter. In order to keep these things in check, which for the most part of the game is an easy task, you have to eat, drink, sleep, avoid getting too cold or too hot in order to avoid hypothermia and heat strokes, which is most efficiently done by seeking shelter and sleeping there for a while. However, these are not your only threats, there are multiple natural phenomena that will come at random times and can kill you in an instant. These are particularly pretty well done and the first time I saw them I was blow away by the scale of these events.

Despite the fact that this is at its core a very linear exploration based adventure game, there is plenty of optional and side stuff to do. Most of the narrative and background story is actually delivered via texts that you find and read through your PDA, but there is the ocasional voice dialogue where you record a message or you catch a glimpse of a transmission from the fleet that is out there waiting for you to report. For me these were some of the best parts, initially you can see that the operator that is transmiting is worried about you, but by the time you’re in the later stages of the game you can feel the terror in her voice, the idea that the Humanity could potentially become extinct if you don’t make it, you are the last hope of Humankind.

This is what sells this game for me, being trapped in a beautiful alien and unknown world, struggling to survive, trying to keep on searching for a way out, witnessing marvels of nature of enormous magnitude, while very slowly starting to unravel some pretty grim and dark secrets beneath the planet surface. While the visuals do a pretty decent job, the sound design is way much better in fully immersing you into this world and the sountrack is pretty good as well. Some areas have this very dark and spooky atmosphere and there were a lot of times I was very tense on my chair, looking for small signs of light in the middle of dark caves.

Final verdict

Do I think that this is a perfect game? Absolutely not! But I strongly believe that this is a must play for anyone who likes dark and tense adventure games with an emphasis on exploration, or games with a great atmosphere filled with mystery, intrigue and a whole lot of sci-fi and doomsday scenarios. That said, I was kind of sad about how the game ended, it felt very anticlimatic, and explaining how and why that was would be spoiling you what was to me the joy of playing this wonderful little gem.

Also, I mentioned that most of the background story and lore is delivered via texts that you find, this is entirely optional and I’m pretty sure I didn’t find a lot of them.
