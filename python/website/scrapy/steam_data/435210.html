Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“After two months of development and a successful greenlight in december we wanted to get the game in your hands as soon as possible to be able to keep developing on the game while receiving valuable feedback from you, the gamer.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We feel like we can make this a solid release worthy title if we're able to work on it for 3 more months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We love to create way more content mainly! Think of more race tracks on different planets, more interesting track mechanics like draw bridges, loopings destroyable environments but also bigger features like a single player story, online multiplayer and car customisation in combination with a reward system.

You can look at the full planned features list in the 'About this game' section below to get a sense of what we currently have and what we planned for the game!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“You're able to race across 2 tracks on planet BAK-ZAND, alone, versus bots or against up to 3 friends.

For more detailed information check the 'About this game' section below.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We'd like the price to reflect the value of the content, so it most likely will be higher once it's released.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Our development process is shared weekly on our YouTube channel, we regularly tweet and will be very much active and engaging on the community forums. We hope to get a lot of feedback and input from you, the gamer to make this the best game possible!”

About This Game

Intergalactic Road Warriors is a side scrolling combat racing game set in outer space. A violent grand prix which takes place on a wide range of planets. You have been invited to join this intergalactic tournament, a battle royale to the finish line. Eviscerate opponents in glorious explosions, pass them through black holes or copter your way overhead.

If you win this tournament, you'll be set for life. Maybe that's why it attracts so many racers from all over the galaxy. You'll start with your shitty car and unlock better parts, guns, car paints, decorations by winning races and eviscerating opponents on the battlefield race track.



In terms of full disclosure as stated in MEGACOORP's Intergalactic Trade and Digital Distribution Treaty we hereby provide what's included in early access at this stage.

(Current) Early Access Features

  • Intergalactic racing chaos: Fully featured racing including boosts, steering, jumping and firing deadly powerups.
  • Crazy powerups: Teleport to a random racer by using the portable black hole, jam everyones screen with the scrambler, fling your card forward with the superboost, refill your boost bar with the quickflip, fire a seeker missile at the unsuspecting car in front of you and show off your coolness by driving in first and enabling S.W.A.G.
  • Local multiplayer: Compete against your friends with up to 4 player split screen multiplayer, show them who's boss.
  • Time Trials: Put down the best time on the intergalactic (steam) leaderboards.
  • Singleplayer VS bots: Practice against bots to make sure you get hold of that precious S.W.A.G. once you play against your friends.
  • Over 4 racetracks! (5 to be exact): Five race tracks which take place on the deserted sand planet BAK-ZAND, ice planet DUOK-7 and lava planet ORDEAL.
  • Characters: 8 characters to choose from, one more visually appealing than the other!

Planned Features

  • Singleplayer story mode: Experience what it means to be competing in the grandest tournament of all tournaments, upgrade your car, unlock new parts along the way and discover that this was never really just about racing...
  • Online multiplayer: Competitive online multiplayer, put your skills to the test versus other extra terrestrial life forms. Climb the ranks and become the reigning champion.
  • Even MOAR crazy powerups: Cluster bombs, tesla coils, grappling hooks, instant ramps and flamethrowers. Remember... no rules.
  • Advanced car customisation: Pimp your ride with unlocked parts, put on that new exhaust and adjust your suspension to deal with that sticky track on HOTGUEL ZX-7.
  • Loads of tracks on different planets: Every tournament new tracks arise, dealing with sandworms, loopings or reversed gravity? Why the hell not.
  • Track editor: Who are we to think that we discovered every single planet out there?, MEGACOORP is always looking for new dangerous planets to organise their tournaments on.

Yoka the Changeling

Early Access Review
Posted: 23 April
I'll be updating this review in the future.

At it's core, it's a great, fun game. A concoction of 2D side scroller, high speed racing, physics and power-ups, feels fresh and, dare I say, original. Combination of jumps and boosts, and constant tilting of your car to be at the right angle plays fast and loose. It can be a great multiplayer party game, since it's very easy to pick up and it has that "Mario Kart feel" to it, but because of it's physics, this game allows for a surprising amount of finesse and rewards jumping or boosting at exactly the right moment or being at exactly the right angle on landing. And because of that even the single player feels fun and rewarding if you want to learn the game and do some cool tricks.

::Pros::
-Great core mechanics.
-Good mix of 2D physics and racing.
-Amazing visuals. Awesome music.
-Great sense of speed.
-Great camera work. In a 2D game? Yeah. The camera zooms out further the faster you go. So it never feels like you don't know what's coming even on the craziest of speeds.
-You can do both back AND front flips! Just think of the possibilities. (and they give you speed boosts :3)

::Cons::
-Screen shake causes framedrops for some reason. But you can turn it off, so it's not a big problem.
-AI is pretty tough if you're just starting.
-It's pretty low on content right now, unsurprisingly. It has a pretty good amount of power-ups and racers to choose from, but currently it's only 5 tracks. To be fair, 3 of them were added after the release, so, I'd say, it's updating pretty fast comparatively.

I love this game even in it's unfinished state and can only see it getting better in the future.

Rahul

Early Access Review
Posted: 12 March
It's unbelievable what you guys have achieved in two months of development. Now starts the long process of fleshing this thing out! What I like so far is that there's a definite sense of potential for core mechanics. You've nailed the sense of speed, worldbuilding, art direction and music, but now it's time to evolve the basic gameplay.

I like that there are several things to focus on at once while playing: the tilt of your car (since the surface of the course isn't flat, and you're constantly being thrown off balance), your acceleration (since you'll be overtaken unless you focus on this) and the boost meter (which requires you to keep looking for opportunities to jump or launch yourself in order to flip). These all play off each other pretty well, leading to a pretty deep baseline racing experience. I'm especially glad to see this develop since I was concerned that making a 2d racer would remove a lot of the dynamics that 3d racers/over the shoulder racers present, including collision with other racers and the ability to move horizontally across a track.

I'm not sure I'm feeling the power ups right now. I feel like they're pretty straightforward and don't really enhance or change the gameplay in a meaningful way, and that there's an opportunity to come up with some that do. I get what you're going for, and they do feel very Mario Kart. I wonder if there's more to achieve here.

The idea of a singleplayer campaign sounds intriguing and different and I'd love to see what you have up your sleeves here. But first I think you should keep tweaking the basic game to make it incredibly replayable; I need to close my eyes and see and hear IRW and feel that addiction. Hope you guys can unlock that over the next few weeks!

Promising so far, but very little content.

I'll be keeping an eye on it and update my review as it progresses.

-----------------------------------

A new track was added, bringing the total to 3. The screen-scrambling item honestly makes me not want to play this very much. Hopefully it will be removed or made far more rare in the future.

-----------------------------------

Screen scrambler has been brought under control, and the bugs are being fixed reliably. There's still some weirdness with powerups (what do all these things do?), and I'd like to see some more tracks and possibly more customization.

The AI could use a little bit of variance as well. It seems like they all race near-optimally, so racing against them isn't very exciting because it turns into a combination of luck and not screwing up.

My main concern at this point, though, is the low number of tracks, and the lack of a smooth flow between them, a-la F-Zero or similar.

Progress overall is good.
