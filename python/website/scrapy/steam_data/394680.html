Hello, hello! We are together for a review of another HOG again! How marvelously different and interesting, is it? No, of course it isn't. This is our millionth HOG, and after a while they all end up resembling one another. Still, Mountain Crime: Requital is one of the better ones out there, with an interesting enough scenario to establish a sense of curiosity.

We here have Dr. Philip White, who receives an urgent phone call in the middle of the night, which asks him to attend a patient at the White Wolf Mountain Resort, in a really bad storm. As any self-confident wise guy with no common sense spared, he travels to middle of nowhere to attend said patient, only to find a murder-mystery. All communication and transportation is cut off, hotel guests are dropping like flies and there is a sick â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ on the loose who treats all this chaos as an elaborate game. It seems that everything is connected to a bigger crime buried in the past. "But what crime?" asks Dr. Philip White, and starts his investigation like any Scooby Doo wannabe.

Cute, huh? Don't be deceived by my mockery. Our protagonist is a naive sod, but the story itself and the connection between hotel guests are a mystery worthy of uncovering. As for the game itself, graphics are pretty good. Background design is interesting and there are many, many lockboxes, automatons and hidden doors around. If I'd visit this hotel in real life - without a murder-mystery present, of course - I know I'd love to spare a week or two there. Character animations look pretty until they start talking. Then with the tongue-tied voice acting and clumsy lip synch, the atmosphere crumbles. It's like a really fine looking lady with no smarts. You'd love to look her all day, as long as she doesn't talk.

Puzzles, I hear? Oh, they are good. There is a balanced combination of ordinary ones, and 'just annoying enough to stall you off until you were about to give up' ones. HOG scenes are very neat, with open and distinguishable items, with more than only one item of the kind lying around. Each HOG is designed to gather pieces for a machine of sort that you're trying to fix, so you can reach another item of use. This way, items laid around kinda make sense, which is rare in this genre.

All in all, this is a fine HOG, but I'll attach a warning. This game is included in a Hidden Object Bundle 5 in 1 in Steam, so if you have the bundle already, there is no actual need for you to buy this game. You already have the game within the bundle. If you don't have it and are even mildly curious, go ahead. Gonna end up with 3-4 hours of decent HOG fun. With this said, my work here is done! Good gaming!

One of your patients is having an heavy health problem and you have to go to the White Wolf Hotel in order to save him. However, he's not here but the clients, all invited for the inauguration, are attacked one by one and killed. Everyone seems to be tied to an old fire that killed two children when the building was still a house for kids.... So why are you there?

I must admit that I played the game in the Hidden Objects Bundle 5 in 1, that lazy as I am, I've not reviewed. Activating this game was part of making a better review of it as an stand-alone than a quick one for the bundle, because of the character limit. The trading cards are a bonus added later and so I replayed the game for them.

Now, with memory refreshed, I remembered why I liked it, though the core gameplay isn't my favorite. Indeed, at first sight, you think that it's about two different stories: the death of Bill Wilton, a benefactor for the hospice, and the death of two children years later. In reality, everything is tied and everyone reunited, save for you as you had no influence in that fateful day, played a part directly or indirectly. The ending however is a huge twist.

The core gameplay is a scene with an object to rebuild and with the parts to find in it. It's not my favorite though, as some objects are hidden by the hud itself and that others are sometimes not in the same position as shown. You have also to solve puzzles to advance. While the map is useful to see where to go, it can't be used to travel, which is sad.

The backgrounds are good too, though you may ask yourself why there is a ski resort behind an old child hospice. It's making sense in the story. You'll also see that the hotel is renovated in only the parts used by the guest, the rest is left like that after the failure of the resort.

The soundtrack is good too though it's not too much present.

Anyway, Mountain Crime: Requital is a nice game, I wish that I could see more of the story but hey, it has enough suspens for a sequel too. Maybe it's existing out there.

A murder mystery written like an early Agatha Christie novel, presented like an Alfred Hitchcock movie, with the solvability (and continuity) of an Ellery Queen mystery. Engaging, intriguing, heart pounding. Keeps the gamer enthralled and guessing until the end. I felt like I had read a thrilling murder mystery without actually any reading involved. Excellent pacing with just enough time to almost catch your breath.

Storyline: Dr. Philip White (one dark and dangerously stormy night) is called to the newly renovated White Wolf Hotel to examine and attend to a Mr. Grey. When he arrives, the bridge washes out leaving him stranded. He discovers there is no Mr. Grey and a hotel filled with other guests also invited to this grand opening by Mr. Grey. (And Then There Were None ~ Agatha Christie, anyone? And no that is not a spoiler.) The mystery begins when the body count begins. Who is Mr Grey? Does he even exist? Why are these people here, unknown to each other yet have a connection? What is that connection? And why this requital? As the body count rises, the answers are revealed.

Gameplay: Hidden Object puzzler with point and click elements. The hidden object scenes instead of being find lists, are picture finds (somewhat like a silhouette). The HO scene opens and small pictures form a circle of the things or pieces needed to be found. Not as easy as it sounds since the objects needed to be found blend into the surrounding objects (which are appropriate for where the scene is). For example looking for desk items, on a desk covered with items typically found on a desk. Also there are generally several of the same item and like a matching game the gamer needs to pick the correct one.

Puzzles: Sliders and logic, or maybe better stated: logic sliders. For example the typical moving of X number of one color object to the opposite side of a board/grid while the x number on that side are moved to where the other color is. Typically found on locks. Also there is the finding of keys, which are usually found behind some type of logic slider puzzle.

The Point and Click element is just as important to the story and in true point and click fashion there are plenty of puzzling obstacles to overcome. However this element is so intermixed with the Hidden Object aspect it is hard to tell the two apart.

Note: No map (not needed), no penalties for game hints or puzzle skips. No achievements but trading cards for those who care about those things.

Conclusion: Enthralling and believable murder mystery, and like all excellent murder mysteries when the pieces start to fall into place, it is an avalanche. Definitely worth full price.

An old abandoned hotel, a monstrous storm, a mysterious invite and lots of murder...it's like the movie Clue only not as funny.

You play Dr. Phillip White and you've been summoned to a hotel to help a person in need. Then a storm hits and your car crashes leaving you stranded at the hotel. You'll meet lots of characters but honestly none of them matter since they all fall over dead like flies. The amount of murder in this game is just ridiculous. It honestly got to the point where I'd expect to find a dead body in every room. However, the story itself isn't bad but it quickly takes a predictable turn and the ending is down right hilarious. I laughed out loud at the end just shaking my head.

Of course, the game doesn't intend to be funny. It takes itself seriously. The voice acting isn't perfect but it's passable. The graphics are dated but not hard on the eyes. The game play is where the game shines. I enjoy the hidden item scenes because you go into each one with a list of items to find that go together to form something actually useful. So no finding random gum wrappers or etched hearts. The mini games are very standard fare; sliding puzzles, memory puzzles, pipe puzzles, etc. They're fun but some are a bit vague when it comes to instructions.

The game lacks achievements which is a shame but it also makes playing the game more relaxing. It's an old game at a cheap price and a good one for fans of the genre. Though the story isn't amazing it's funny and I enjoy laughing while playing a good game. So I say you should buy it and give it a shot.

Mountain Crime: Requital is a game where you play the worst doctor in the world as people all around him fall prey to the stupidest, most easily avoidable deathtraps imaginable. (Seriously, imagine Saw, if the traps were designed by an idiot, and managed to ensnare even bigger idiots). There's a big mystery about an old resort where a bunch of kids burned alive that the hotel you're in was built on, but all in all the plot ranges from boring to hilarious, without any detours to interesting.

The hidden object scenes are okay. The main mechanic is you're searching for objects by a given silhouette (although sometimes the silhouette doesn't match with the item, so thanks, game). A few scenes are repeated, but at least they all have the gimmick of ostensibly using all the items you find to acquire the needed object to advance.

The regular puzzles are okay too. No real stand-outs; just typical HOG fare like "Slide the blocks around until the one block can escape", "Turn the pipes so the liquid can pass through", "There is a combination lock, but you saw the solution so it shows up in your journal", stuff like that.

There were a lot of cute little animals, including a prominent squirrel guy in one puzzle that had big ol' cutie ears. There was also a moment when the character you play as was standing next to an exploding gas stove and seemed completely unaffeted. The ending is hilarious.

All in all, it's a fun game, and definitely worth a shot.
