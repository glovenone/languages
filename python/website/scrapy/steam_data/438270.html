About This Game

Learn Japanese To Survive! Hiragana Battle is an educational game that is the ideal introductory course to the Japanese language!

You’re reading this page now because you have more than a passing interest in learning Japanese. Maybe you’ve tried learning it before, but never had any luck? That’s completely normal, so don’t worry! Learning a language can be daunting. And with a language like Japanese, full of strange and wonderful written characters, you might not even know where to start.

You need a learning tool that’s going to keep you interested, challenge you at the right pace, and most importantly, keep you coming back for more.

That’s why Learn Japanese To Survive! Hiragana Battle was made! This isn’t just educational software – this is a full-blown RPG specifically designed to keep you engaged. By playing along, you’re going to be introduced to the Japanese language like never before.

Learn how to read and write Japanese characters (Hiragana) at a steady pace. There’s a new set of characters to learn each chapter, and your revision takes place on the battlefield! If your knowledge is up to scratch, you’ll claim victory in exciting strategic battles. If you’re falling behind, you have the opportunity to revisit specific chapters and brush up on your knowledge.

As a bonus, you’ll also learn common Japanese words and phrases and important grammar points!

What is Hiragana?

Hiragana is the basic writing system used in Japanese. Japanese actually combines 3 different sets of characters to form written works – Hiragana, Katakana and Kanji. This game focuses solely on Hiragana, the foundation. By learning Hiragana, you can write and pronounce nearly any Japanese word.

Features:

  • An exciting story based on the concept of learning Japanese. As the characters in the story learn Hiragana, you learn along with them!
  • Learn every single Hiragana character, with detailed instructions on how to read and write each character.
  • Visual animations displaying how to write each character.
  • Audio speech examples demonstrate how to pronounce each character.
  • Exciting gameplay with familiar RPG elements – collect items, complete quests, engage in battles!
  • Earn experience points and level up your characters.
  • Revise completed lessons to brush up on your knowledge.
  • In-game quizzes to test what you’re learning.

The game teaches hiragana and a few words and phrases. You will learn romaji (English letters), hiragana, and stroke order. It chunks information into sets that you can easily look up in tutorials later. If you understand the kana, you can easily escape battles and progress the quest quickly. As the story continues, the game incorporates more japanese into npc dialogue. You can always revisit an area later for more practice. It's not timed, so you can go at your own pace. If you use a notebook, you can practice writing hiragana.

For critics: Did you learn English with just one book? Probably not. The game operates a cheap and useful supplement to whatever learning tools you are already using.

Con: The pronounciations are robotic, but if you are truly looking to learn Japanese, you should be listening to multiple sound sources. They same clusters of hiragana occur, but it could be helpful to people who don't know the hiragana as well. It could use more randomization of the characters.

I recommend this game to anyone who wants to learn Japanese. I look forward to the sequel!

#hiragana mathblaster

Spent about 9 hours in this game and i'm feeling pretty confident with my hiragana and enjoyed the game for what it is. The pacing of the game is excellent. The rate of which new hiragana is introduced was perfect for me And if you want to grind, it's also benifical for you to practice hiragana if you're struggling, but isn't required.

Story: Well, it's unique. I'm not going to say this is the greatest rpg story in history, but still a better story and makes more sense than FFI. Laughed a few times, got hopefull around the middle, and then the story went flat again. But it was enough to keep me to the end.

Characters: your party stays with the same 4 and gains no new party members. Considering the mechanics, more characters aren't really needed as your main four aren't really special.

Mechanics: sadly, theres a lot to be desired for an rpg. I went through the whole game using only one other move in combat other than the "attack" button, and it was the heal for grinding. hopefully in the next game, they can have some kind of magic system that does some kind of speed read or something. But the point of the game is to teach Hiragana and the game accomplishes that in a more entertaining way than writing out the characters repeatedly.

Quests/sidequests: main quest is stop the evil plan. Sidequest usually offer a better reward for going back to a place to grind for 5-10 minutes and can sometime work on your vocab which the main story doesn't focus on till the very very end.

Overall, I feel good about my hiragana and was entertained enough by the game that I would buy the sequel for Katakana when it comes out. Would recommend if you're looking to learn hiragana and want to have a reward and evidence of your improvement along the way.

*note* I found zero bugs during the game.
