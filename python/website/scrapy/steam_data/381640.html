About This Game

Start your Astral journey and fight at the arena along with more than 10 million players from all over the world! Gather your best friends or join one of the top guilds to meet faithful companions, who will stand by you through thick and thin! Whether you are an experienced player or a beginner adventurer, in the world of Sarnaut you will find yourself a great company!
Game features:
  • Fascinating atmosphere of the world of two rivaling factions: industrial Empire and fantasy League
  • Ship battles and unique Astral adventures
  • Personal achievements and guild ratings
  • Exciting raids and challenging bosses
  • Various PvP activities, including from 2 to 48 participants
  • Automatic group assembling in PvP and PvE activities
  • Random quest generation system

System Requirements

Here we have it: Allods Online (Theres a TL;DR at the end in case you are lazy)

Back in 2009/2010, among the huge pile of crappy MMOs, this wonderful game emerged. With a big budget, nice graphics and an interesting storyline it promised to be a great and riveting adventure. And so it was, but only during the 1.0 version of the game (as I like to call it "vanilla" allods) because at first there was no cash shop. It got introduced later into the beta, but it didn't upset the balance that much, yet.
Then comes the first big expansion which forced quite a lot of cash shop stuff into the game making it borderling unplayable for non paying players. Even if you decided to be supportive, and buy the cash shop currency from time to time, you usually found yourself wondering if its worth it. Cash shop aside, at least the content was fun during this phase! But that was quick to change.
Soon further expansions arrived, making the game more and more into a unfun tedious grind which required even more money invested than before to play properly, or nerves of steel if you were a free player (farming is not fun I tell you).

In my opinion the game is now a shell of its former self and not worth getting into. Yes it is true, many people spent thousands - some even 10 thousands of euros/dollars/whatever currency on this game.

If you are a new player looking for a fun temporary MMO then I can recommend this game to you. Download it and play it (but don't bother paying). Levels 1-40 will probably be the most fun, and then you'll feel the fun gradually drop off as you climb towards the max level, and then you quit. That's the only thing this MMO got to offer.

I guess the original devs leaving early on did make an impact after all...

TL;DR - Pay2win concept taken to the extreme, probably one of - if not the worst example of pay2win (or should i say pay2lottery and pray4win?) ruining a good game which had a lot of potential
