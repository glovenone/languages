About This Game

Pajama Sam is off on a nutritiously edible adventure!

Pajama Sam chases down some snickering cookies, only to find himself in the midst of a food squabble! The fats and the sweets are taking over Mop Top Island and an emergency peace conference has been called, but four of the delegates are missing! Now, it's your turn to spring into action to help Pajama Sam find the missing delegates, put a stop to the sticky quarrels, bring peace to Mop Top Island, and still make it home in time for dinner.

  • Meet friends along the way - friendly food walks, talks and even tell jokes.
  • Explore and Learn: Investigate every location. There is something new to learn with every click!
  • Interact with the characters and listen closely for clues.
  • It's a different game each time you play.

Posted: 8 May
That was pretty good. I'm glad Humongous got back on track for Pajama Sam.

I don't think companies should stick to a solid formula if they create a franchise. Nintendo has managed to evolve the Mario franchise over the years, but with very few distinct changes. That's honestly one of the biggest problems with series. How far do you go to make a sequel distinct and different, while also keeping the same general formula of your previous installment(s)?

I think Pajama Sam 3 did alright. I liked what it did. It was different, but it was still distinctly a Pajama Sam.

The story is similar to the other installments, where Sam has a problem and some supernatural imaginative event carries Sam to a separate world where he learns the error of his ways. Although, this is the first time in the series where Sam doesn't deal with a fear: it's more or less a personal flaw of overeating.

The world, much like the Land of Darkness from the first game, is incredibly imaginative. It's a massive rendition of Sam's body, as he goes around and "restores balance" to the food groups. There's no B.S, random conflict that comes out of nowhere. No random World Wide Weather business complex. It's simple imagination- like the first Pajama Sam.

But, despite what good things there are, there are still plenty of bad things. The mini-games are absolutely awful. I hate the bean-sorting game and the ski slopes. And some puzzles were a little vague, especially if the game was made for kids. Like specifically finding the sand castle for the bag to fill for the Cheese Delegate guy. And, the voice actress for Florette the Broccoli sounds like she's just reading a paper in front of her face.

However, the game follows the original Pajama Sam formula, without using the first game as a crutch, like Pajama Sam 2 did. It tells a new story with new characters, while keeping the charm, subtle humor, small symbolism, and story of personal growth that the original game created. And I enjoyed the time I spent playing it. I'm giving this one a 7/10.

When I found out recently that Pajama Sam 3 is on steam, I immediately bought it as I can finally complete the game as I was not able to when I was a kid. It gave me a chance to finally have a closure to one of my favourite childhood game.

Throughout the game, it gave me a very nostalgic feeling as we've been constantly playing games of high graphical quality, contrasting a lot with the graphics of games used in the 2000, making me enjoy every moment of it.

Even though this game is recommended to kids of ages 3-8, it doesn't seem that way as I'm sure that there are many adults here that would like to ride the nostalgia train and enjoy the game like how we used to when we were kids. Playing it as an adult sometimes surprises me at times as I remembered completing a particular stage of the game as a kid, but was stuck at it for a while when replaying it as an adult. I'm proud that I've not succumbed to the temptation of reading guides when I'm stucked since guides of any games are so readily available everywhere on the internet now. I mean, there ain't many guides when I was a kid and I almost completed the game. Even if there is, I doubt I know how to surf the internet at that age.

To those that have played the game before as a kid, but have never completed it before, or simply just want to play the game for nostalgic purposes, it is worth every penny to buy the game.
