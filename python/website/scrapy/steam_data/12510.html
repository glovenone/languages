About This Game

Crack that Whip! Take reigns of a privately run prison. You are responsible for the care, custody, and control of individuals who have been arrested. Begin with a low security prison and build it up to a SuperMax. From construction to daily operations, you must protect society while turning a profit. It's time for lockdown.
  • Country Club or Billy Club? - Manage security levels, food rations, recreation opportunities, and much more. How tough will you be?
  • En Guard - Hire trustworthy prison guards and arm them with weapons, riot shields and guard dogs to maintain peace and control, but keep your eye on your budget. Underpay and organized crime will run rampant in your prison.
  • Watch with an eagle eye! - Monitor all activities 24/7. Keep a look out for escapees, contraband or illegal activity.
  • The Devil's in the details - From the guards you hire to the food you serve, every choice you make will ripple throughout your prison.
  • Back on the chain gang- Assign prisoners work detail from cleaning and maintenance work to back-breaking manual labor.
  • Throw away the key? - You determine whether to release your prisoners on parole or keep them locked down tight to protect society.
  • Danger Zone- Manage gangs and prisoner morale to avoid riots.
  • Interrogate military prisoners for vital intel- Earn extra bonuses by getting prisoners to provide crucial information.

If you imagine that this game will bring you pleasure...you are mistaken.

I owned and played this game often when I was younger and was stuck at my grandmother\'s house for days. When I purchased this game, it was from pure nostalgia. I remembered when I would play this game for hours on end, and I recalled how much enjoyment I got from it...

...After I bought this game, I remembered that the only reason I played this game was because the choices at my grandmothers place was 1) play this game 2) go to Church or 3) watch re-runs of the Andy Griffith show, and there is only so much of Deputy Don i can take before I\'m willing to do almost anything...and I\'m not a Christian, so I chose Prison Tycoon.

Don\'t waste your money. The only reason this game should be bought is as a prank gift to a good buddy when you have a few extra bucks to spend.

The graphics are sub-par (even when it was first released), UI is almost worthless, no tutorial (let me say that im not a huge advocate for tutorials...i don\'t usually need EVERY small detail explaned over the course of a half hour. But this game REALLY needs it), and worse of all...your prisioners WILL escape. Put up a tall af fence with rolls of barb-wire? They climb over it like a jungle gym. Got 100+ guards on duty & 20 Guard Towers to help catch them? Still climb fence, and still escape. Nothing you do will prevent this, and no matter what they WILL get away.

Not to mention the economy. Seems simple...build a factory or two, fill it with shops, assign prisoners to work schedules, and make money...right? WRONG! Why? Because even though you have the proper facilities and mechanisms in place to get them to work...they just dont feel like working. So, you will go broke pretty quick as you watch your lazy idiot guards pimp walk around while your prisoners are running off into the sunset.

TL;DR - Game sucks. Move along.

This game is terrible.

For starters, there is no tutorial. The game thrusts you right in with the options of Mens Prison, Womens Prison or Military Prison. It does not explain what the differences are between them (if there are any at all) or what each prison will actually involve, you're just expected to guess.
I went with the first option. Mens Prison.
Once in the game there is no guide on what to do, no objectives, nothing helpful at all. It doesn't even clearly show you which plot of land you should be building on which is annoying as first impressions.
Once you start to figure things out on your own, such as how the menus work, what you should be building and such, you expect the game to get better but it doesn't.
It's drab attempts at being a 'prison tycoon' just makes you frustrated and bored. The learning curve is huge and i'm sure there's many things i'm missing because the game just doesn't explain to you what to do or how to do it (apart from the 'manual' which i've given a read and it isn't very helpful).

The graphics are average for the release date but when the gameplay is boring the drab graphics don't do anything to help ignite your enthusiasm and the same goes with the menus and other interface options. They are downright ugly, and 90% of the times the options are not labelled and do not explain to you what they do, there is just a random picture which you yourself must decipher.

I'm sure if I put another 10 hours into the game I may learn how to play it fully, but I doubt that even if I did learn the more complicated parts of it I would still not find the game enjoyable.
There are many better tycoon games out there for your money, I just can't recommend this at all.
