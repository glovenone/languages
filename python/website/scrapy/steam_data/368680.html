What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Super Mutant Alien Assault is entering Early Access to help with balancing and feedback during the final push of the development. We want to get real player feedback and data to help us apply the final polish as well as giving us feedback and ideas for the last slice of content before launch.

The game is fully funded so this is purely about polish and feedback and the full game will launch regardless of what revenue we make during Early Access.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We expect to be in early access for around six months. Although the game is nearly done we want to have enough time to get meaningful feedback and ideas from the players before we finish development and launch the full version.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We expect the full version to have more content - more level maps, enemies, bosses, some new items and weapons and more character skins. The full version will also benefit from tweaks and balancing adjustments based on the feedback gathered during Early Access.

If unexpected ideas come from the community during Early Access we may be able to implement them before launch.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is about 85% complete and has all the core features as well as being pretty polished and stable. At this stage itâs mostly just additional content to be added such as more level maps, some more enemies, some more boss enemies, weapons, items and character skins.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“At this point we intend to price the game the same during Early Access and Full Release. Please note - this may change as the game will be in Early Access for around six months and the market is likely to change in that time.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We would love to receive feedback on the game in our Steam forum and the Surprise Attack Games subreddit. Weâre particularly looking for feedback on balancing and also ideas for cool features, items, weapons, objectives or enemies that the community would like to see in the game.”

About This Game

Three fleets of spaceships flee a doomed Earth carrying the last humans in cryostasis. Pursued by aliens, itâs up to the lowly security droids to save humanity. Taking up arms solo or with a friend, you must dance with death in a brutal and addictive arcade platformer shooter inspired by Super Crate Box and stuffed to the brim with unlockables.

Fight through rapid fire single screen levels each with a random combination of platform layout, objective, vending machines and enemies. Dodge and weave amongst the aliens to stay alive but donât leave them standing too long or the shipâs radiation will mutate them into bigger, nastier versions.

Only by unlocking and mastering the wild variety of weapons, explosives, special abilities, defense moves and perks can you hope to survive across all three galaxies, rid your ship of the mutant menace and save the oblivious humans.

Features


- rapid fire levels give you no rest as hordes of enemies arrive, hell-bent on your destruction. Clear the objective quickly and be sure to kill each enemy before it has the chance to mutate into a bigger, nastier version of itself. Take a tiny breather and then itâs on to the next room.

- in the tight confines of the space freighter, standing still is not an option. Your ability to dodge around enemies is as important as your aim. Survival requires quick thinking and rapid reflexes and a true master of the dance is a beautiful thing to behold.

- each single screen level is randomised, drawing on a pool of platform layouts, objective types and enemies as well as which vending machines will be present. Youâll never know which weapons or grenades will spawn in the vending machines whilst crate drops containing special abilities, sidearms, perks and defense moves are also random, adding further to the mind-boggling possible combinations.

- a wealth of weapons, sidearms, special abilities, perks, explosives, defense moves and character skins awaits you. Each new unlockable joins the melting pot of random ingredients, increasing your options but also the need to master each individual element and the way they combine with each other.

- take on the mutant hordes by yourself or team up in local co-op. Two player adds further complications to master as you must be careful to avoid friendly fire from explosives and will have to share/fight over item and health pack drops.

DISCLAIMER: Game provided by publisher for testing purposes. However I am not an employee, and I chose to test this game because I initially disliked it and thought I could provide a more balanced opinion. Freakin' game won me over regardless.

It's difficult to get your head around it during the first couple of hours of gameplay, so I recommend playing with friends as much as you can early on. However once you've figured out the game there is incredible fun to be had in short bursts here!

Super Mutant Alien Assault is a 2D single screen shooter where you're a robot tasked to save the last ships of humanity from an alien invasion. You're tasked to wipe out all the aliens in each stage, while completing objectives like carryiing fuel tanks to the generator or releasing pressure on steam valves as you fight for survival.

Enemies mutate if you leave them alone too long so you have to be active in hunting them down before numbers get overwhelming. There's a great selection of special abilities and weapons to help you out with this task and they are all varied and fun to use.

Its also definitely a game that gets better over time. I've put close to ten hours into it, and due to the awesome unlock system there are still recently unlocked mechanics like hacking that I haven't finished experimenting with!

Going to re-state that normal mode can seem too hard while you're learning the game, but stick with it.
Its an action packed shooter that only gets better, and once you've found a play style that suits you, even heroic difficulty will be crushed by your mighty skills!

Tips for new players: Focus on surviving until you get the weapon you want.
Check your loadout and take advantage of your abilities.
Explosives can release pressure so take advantage of it.
You can revive your team mates.
Know your objective!
And don't be afraid to experiment! Weapons and abilites that seemed too hard to use at first can turn out to be very useful later on once you know how to use them. Maximise the potential of your arsenal ;)

Best of luck out there! Those aliens are tough!

tl;dr
Super Mutant Alien Assault is an unashamed Super Crate Box clone, and with good reason: it's every bit as addicting as it's paragon, but adds it's own twists.

words
Super Mutant Alien Assault is an arcade platformer shooter, where you play a tiny robot combating an onslaught of mutant aliens. The controls are solid (the only gripe I have is not being able to remap the d-pad to make the pad controls mimic the keyboard controls), the graphics are excellent some of the best art I've seen in a long while. The music is okay and the sounds are passable.

The levels have a nice amount of variety without having "bad levels" that you wish you won't get. There are a variety of "modes" for the levels ranging from just surviving a set amount of time to transferring fragile gas canisters to a receptacle elsewhere on the level. As you play, you unlock more game modes, enemies, weapons and power-ups.

You get weapons, explosives and health from vending machines, that take a while to recharge, and sometimes levels spawn with crates containing pick-ups that range from granting you defensive moves, to just plain health pick-ups.
The game has a bunch of interesting mechanics, like the towers that power up enemies when they activate, making the enemies larger and more dangerous, but large enemies also drop ammunition for your super attack, which you can receive from crates.

Bottom line, I almost passed on Super Mutant Alien Assault, but I'm so happy I decided to take a chance on it because of the appealing visual style, it's a very good game. If you're wary about Early Access, the devs plans seem to be to basically just add more levels, enemies and items and to tweak balance, ie. the game is basically feature complete. If the trailer or screenshots look at all interesting to you, or if you enjoyed Super Crate Box, I definitely recommend checking Super Mutant Alien Assault out.

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 December, 2015
Cannot recommend the game as it lacks a survival mode, sure it's a bit of fun but as it is now the game is far too short. When it possesses a survival mode I will change this to recommended.

EDIT: The dev's goal isn't to add a meaningful survival mode but to make the current story/level based mode stronger, this was contrary to my expectations of a similar experience to Super Crate Box.

The game itself looks amazing, its mechanics are solid and the variation upon Super Crate Box in terms of objectives and weapons were really, really nice.

However going in to Super Mutant Alien Assault you shouldn't expect to get the same arcadey feeling you got from Super Crate Box, in the current state you won't be trying to beat your high score and frantically pace through a level trying to get more crates but rather aim for a larger objective and beat the story mode.

In its current state this game isn't as much of a Super Crate Box clone as I'd like, if you're looking at the comments then you're probably having second thoughts about the game so you should know exactly what type of experience you're getting.

I refunded the game but will repurchase when I feel the feature set aligns more with my interests, with that said I believe that the artistic vision of the developer shouldn't be knocked and that the best game will result from what the developer finds fun, not pandering to my needs.

TL:DR The game is good, but if you're expecting a frantic high score beating experience like Super Crate Box then you are going to be dissappointed.

The developer is currently planning an objective based survival mode so I'll probably check the game out again when it comes out.

Super Mutant Alien Assault is a lot like Super Crate Box, but it brings a lot of new stuff to the table.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CjPKupwuqdQ
The game is filled with all sorts of randomised features. In each level, there are some machines set to help you out. For example, a weapon dispenser that will give you a random weapon when you interact with it. Once you unlock a new weapon, it is then added into the possible weapons for the dispenser to choose from. There are some really cool weapons, like an explosive pogo stick.

There are multiple modes that can be selected for the levels you play. Sometimes you might just need to kill all enemies in the stage, and sometimes you might be required to transport fuel tanks from one side to the other. The different modes do a good job at keeping the game fresh.

The game really picks up once you've decked out your character. You can grab all sorts of equipment from random crates. A secondary weapon which is weaker, but a good back up plan for when your primary weapon runs out, and the weapon dispenser isn't recharged. Sometimes you might get defensive gadgets like a dash ability, or a perk which allows you to double jump.

The music is really cool. It does a great job at pumping your adrenaline as you try to survive as long as possible through different enemy waves and bosses.

If you enjoyed Super Crate Box, I'd definitely recommend this.
If you never tried Super Crate Box, I'd say try this game anyway.

Overall, a lot of fun!
