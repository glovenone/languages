About This Game



The Beggar’s Ride is a unique, story driven puzzle-platformer. While exploring a queer land as an old beggar, you can wear ancient masks to see the world through the eyes of a god! Use the powers of the masks to solve puzzles by altering the very fabric of world!

Follow the beggar’s ride to a distant land, a world hidden on the other side of the rain, and prepare to explore, jump and run for your life. But to overcome the dangers of forgotten realm, you will have to wear the masks of a forgotten god and use its powers to command the forces of nature.

Are you ready to start a dangerous journey and to unveil a secret as old as time itself?


One night, an old man found an eerie mask. It was a key to a world hidden on the other side of the rain. It was destined for a hero, a champion, a saviour…
But the old man was just a beggar…

Explore a strange and colorful land while delving into a poetic story. Read a moving tale of loneliness and mysteries from texts integrated in the game world and hear the whispers of the ancient memories of a fallen god.
Journey through a fading realm and discover the secret locked behind the door of all doors.


  • A huge world full of intriguing and fiendish puzzles to solve
  • Explore as a Beggar, then wear the ancient mask to see the world through the eyes of a god.
  • Four masks to discover, each granting a unique god like power
  • A poetic and meaningful storyline
  • Collectables, secret areas and much more!

The Beggar’s Ride is a casual puzzle platforming adventure with a well-told story.

Check out my Quick Review here: https://youtu.be/s251Jggt6dk
The game starts off fairly easy, and new mechanics are introduced as you discover mask abilities. You use this mask to enter God State to manipulate the area. By moving the earth, controlling the sun and moon, and making it rain, you solve puzzles to progress. I almost always enjoy puzzles in most games, so this game was no different.

There were a couple areas that I died repeatedly, so I did find that the platforming got a bit harder towards the end, but I admit that I am not the greatest at platformers to begin with. Fortunately, the game is very forgiving. All the crystals you earned before you died still remain collected, so you don’t have to find them again. Also, with a fair amount of checkpoints throughout the game, and infinite lives, the game is friendly to people looking for a more casual experience.

Pros:
  • colourful, cute, and visually appealing animation style - everything looks great
  • a nice mix of puzzles and platforming with good gameplay mechanics
  • map showing all the checkpoints, and where you haven’t been to yet
  • map also allows for teleportation to certain checkpoints that you've already activated
  • awesome narrative - it was done extremely well, and it did an amazing job telling the story
  • good story: it conveyed a strong, positive message that some people might have forgotten...

Cons:
  • puzzles were too easy throughout the game

The Beggar’s Ride took me about 3-3.5 hours to complete. Normally, I don’t pay much attention to mobile games, so I had never heard of this game until its Steam release, but I am glad that the developers ported this over to PC. I enjoyed the game, and am completely satisfied with the purchase. If you're a fan of casual puzzle platformers with story, it’s a solid buy.

At the point of writing this review I have finished ~60% of the game

Minor spoilers in the review:
This game is about a beggar that finds a mask(s) that gives him godlike powers, sounds cheesy right? Well it isn't, it has a pretty intresting bigger backstory to it that I won't spoil.
The music is pretty great and provides a nice calm atmosphere to the game, while the gameplay itself is AWSD to move and a right click to jump kind of control.
The gameplay mostly involves solving puzzles that may sometimes be completely easy and sometimes might take a couple of tries, it may not be the easiest puzzle game but it kinda is easy. The graphics are cool and whenever you switch between your beggar self and god-like self the world around you changes in a cool way.

However, I don't know if it's just me but the manuvering in the game and going through levels to unlock different powers is kinda hard, like rn I'm kinda stuck cuz I don't know where to unlock my last power. The level manuvering is kinda messed up because you don't know if you're sometimes going ahead of where you should be or if you're on track. That's probably the only negative thing I've noticed...

I would recommend you get it but it's not rly a must-buy, it's still a pretty great game and I'd give it a 7/10.
If the game is on sale (like it is the time I'm writing the review) then it would be pretty great to grab it.
Hope I helped anyone with this review...
