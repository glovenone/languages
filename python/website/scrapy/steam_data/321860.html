INTRO
There's a lot of mixed reviews but still a lot of unanswered questions. I am going to try to review the game and explain at least well enough to help you decide if you should buy this or not. Keep in mind this is MY opinion on the game, it might not necessarily be the same as everyone else!

Differences between "Free" Version and Steam Version
I'll start by informing you that the pack Steam is selling, is also available on the Disney Infinity website. You can also download the game for free on the same website.
Although the game is supposedly Free to Play, the basic version of the game doesn't offer you that much, presenting itself more as a demo. You have access to 3 of the many characters that rotate each 5 days, you can play demos of the Play Sets (these are like open world story modes) and you have limited access to toy box (it's kinda like a creative mode). I recommend you download and play the game for free through the Disney Infinity website before you buy any pack.

By buying one of the starter packs (in this case, the Marvel Super Heroes one) you unlock:
- The Avengers play set (basically it's an open world story mode)
- 3 permanent characters (Thor, Black Widow, Iron Man)
- Full access to toy box 2.0 (creative mode where you can create your own maps/levels, share them with friends, download other people's maps, race your friends, create/customize your own house, etc. In my opinion, this is where the game really shines)
- Two toy box games (these are kinda like game modes. Eg: tower defense game)

PROS & CONS
I'm going to balance the pros and cons based on the version Steam is selling. Some of these are very subjective and they are based on my own experience of the game.

PROS:
+ Huge possibilities - Through the Toy Box mode, the game can truly be "infinite"! Create your own maps and levels, or download maps from the community! (Remember how Little Big Planet did it? Close enough!)
+ Characters - Great cast, both from "classic" Disney and Marvel.
+ VERY fun! - Personal opinion, of course. But I had a really good time playing this game! (Try the free version to see if you have fun too! If not, then it's probably not for you.)

CONS:
x Too expensive - and I don't mean just the Marvel Super Heroes pack. Even with this pack you only get to play with 3 of the Avengers. This means no Hulk, no Captain America, no Hawkeye, no Falcon and of course none of the other characters associated with them, like Loki for example. You can buy these characters on the website, they're cheaper than their physical versions (that can also be bought, but all you really will use is the code that comes with it. It's nice if you like to collect figures though). So basically, you have a bunch of characters you want to play with, but you have to pay for them. And that's not all! You want new story modes? You can play the demo, but for the full version you have to buy the corresponding Play Set pack (for example, Guardians of the Galaxy). You want new Toy Box games? (Not the ones you can download for free, the "official" ones) Well, that's also gonna cost you. Pretty much everything "extra" in this game is not only paid, but it's also kinda expensive.

CONCLUSION
Disney Infinity 2.0 is a good game overall, in my opinion (7/10 for the story modes, 8/10 for creation modes). It will definitely give you that "greedy â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥s" vibe, but even with the content of one pack, you can already have lots of fun with it!
Once again, i recommend you try the free version of the game first because it really can show you the game's potential before you buy it.

Quick overview for those wondering what exactly this version of Disney Infinity 2.0 is:
1. This is the full version of the game, no "toybox only" version or the likes we saw before.
2. What you get with this starter set is three figures (Thor, Black Widow and Iron-man) and the Avengers play-set (essentially an open-world 5-hours long story mode) plus a collection of free-to-try characters that rotate at certain dates. You also get two toy box minigames:
one is a tower-defense styled game and the other is a roguelike styled game (and also includes randomly generated floors).
3. There are no direct uses for physical figures. To compensate, digital figures are cheaper and physical figures come with download-codes for unlocking them.
4. There is pretty much zero graphics customization available beyond different resolutions,
luckily, the game is fairly lightweight for those with mid-range rigs.
5. There are some bugs to look out for at this time.
You have to wait a couple of seconds after getting to the main menu to choose your play-set.
If you don't, the game won't recognize that you own the play-sets and instead ask if you want to play the demo. And lastly, some actions during the menus, like choosing your support characters, are currently impossible to do without the aid of a mouse

â¢ If you understand the value of money, then this game is not for you.
â¢ If you think this product is about the Marvel Super Hero story gameplay, then this game is not for you.
â¢ If you think this is an open sandbox with every tool already at your disposal to build whatever you want, then this game is not for you.

So, then... What is Disney Infinity? This product is a pseudo game builder that is intended for older children or young adults.
Yes, there is a pre-built Avengers (or Spider-man, or Guardians) game, but it's really just there as a means to an end... you are playing or paying to unlock new ways to theme the game you will build, or to fast-track access to a wide variety of models and features to add to what you build.

THE CONS:
The price is hard to justify, but once you turn that portion of your brain off (which I did when TF2 introduced micro transactions) you learn to let it slide.
The custom Exteriors & INteriors you build have a strong tendency to become corrupt... items disappearing (including floors!), INterior rooms becoming misaligned and closed off, props sinking into the floor... This is the biggest complaint I have. It can become terribly frustrating to work on something for a long time to have it ruined on load... in some cases irreversibly

THE PROS:
I have had a great time building things with my kids. I love seeing them learn about logic connections between characters & props and watching them develop their own gameplay. I love the stylization & combination of the Disney properties... it's lighthearted, funny, and makes for a good time.

I'm sure there are other game builders out there that are cheaper... but this is the one I bought... My kids and I like it; and we'll continue to play.
