About This Game

Discover a new breed of Action-RPG game powered by an enhanced version of the Source™ Engine by Valve. Set in the Might & Magic® universe, players will experience ferocious combat in a dark and immersive fantasy environment. Swords, Stealth, Sorcery. Choose your way to kill.
  • Cutting-edge technology: Experience an enhanced version of the famous Source™ Engine created by Valve. Discover the fresh perspective of a view with complete body awareness, realistic movements, physics rendering, and a complete first-person melee combat system in a fantasy setting.
  • Never-ending action: Challenge the forces of evil in 12 huge levels and learn to master over 30 weapons and an arsenal of devastating spells.
  • Evolve your character: Extend your gameplay experience without being limited to a single discipline. Learn powerful new spells and attacks using Dark Messiah's unique Skill Evolution System as you progress through the game.
  • Revolutionary multiplayer mode: Get ready to battle with up to 32 players in the revolutionary Crusade mode, which will enable players to gain experience and new skills across dynamic online campaigns. Enlist with the humans or the undead and choose among five complementary character classes.

If you can play this game, it is FAN-TASTIC. i almost immediately got addicted for a number of reasons. first and foremost, the M&M franchise to me is one that has spanned across my whole life.

as a little child my first and fondest memories of the franchise was in M&M mandate of heaven where being the little cheating bugger i was, i used a cheat to get the apocalypse spell and constantly ruin everyone in the starting towns day. after that it was the first 3 Heroes of M&M for me, the turn based strategy games that kept it alive.
Im 21 now, the days of the old Heroes of M&M games are behind me as its difficult to get them to work properly. i played Mandate of heaven again and actually made headway ( albiet difficultly) and then stopped. until i found this game. . .

Now I will start off by saying this, I NEVER heard of this little gem until recently. and i honest to god wish i had. The reasoni said " if you can play this game" the big word being "IF" is this, the game suffers from a plethora of bugs a majority of which consist of ones preventing you from playing or starting the game. it seems to be a bug that has hundreds of symptoms and no 3 people have the same lol. ive encountered bugs like this before, but for OS not video games. and i honestly dont know what made my game start working after 2 weeks of trying but it did and oh, my god...

You play as a young man named Saref. (sareth? sarif?) and are tasked by your master with getting an item known as the Shantiri crystal. he then sends you on your way to a city to enlist the help of a powerful mage. but not before giving you a sexy lady friend companion to live in your head.

The combat is refreshingly different and you wont find anything like it in another M&M game. since it runs off source you KNOW physics are involved and used in the best ways. there are many different ways to kill an orc lol. for example, there may be spike boards laid against a wall, just position that orc between you and the board and KICK THE ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ BACK TO HELL! or if thats not good enough for you, you can kick them off a cliff and have them fall a thousand feet down. really give them time to think about where they ♥♥♥♥ed up before they die! there are also numerous traps, such as unstable platforms with various heavy breakable objects that you can knock down onto your enemies! seriously the choice is yours! kill away!

now, dont be thinking that you'll get rich in this game and buy the greatest gear ever before your final boss encounter.
there is no money, no shop system at all. you gotta survive on the potions you either find, or grab up that healing spell quick because you will need it. as for the weapons, ive only just made it to the giant worm and only now do i have an enchanter weapon im in love with. ( souldrinker ftw) but if youre looking for powerful weapons, you better be ready to go above and beyond level exploration. i mean it, its crazy where some of these secret areas are! and while im on the subject easily in my list of top 3 greatest things in this game is the rope bow. which allows you to shoot an arrow at a wooden object, (often in the ceiling or jutting out of the wall) and it will make a rope that you can climb with. got a tough fight ahead and not enough potions to heal with? running is a good option, but creating a matrix of ropes along the walls and ceiling like some medieval spiderman? EVEN BETTER! just climb the ropes and jump the unrealistically long distance between them to escape your would be killers!

i could go on and on about how great this little known gem of a game is. but the bottom line will always be this, if you can play it GET IT.

Dark Messiah of Might & Magic is an awesome First Person RPG filled with tons of action, a decent storyline, great graphics using the Source engine and loaded with fun-filled combat. The campaign is pretty lengthy at ~10 hours long with fully voiced NPCs and cutscenes. As you complete the primary and optional objectives you are given skill points to allocate in 3 different areas and it also allows the player to play a certain playstyle/class: fighter, mage or rogue. The combat is pretty awesome with some in depth actions (for example the sword has multiple ways to attack) and you have the ability to kick enemies off of ledges, into each other or into traps. There are also loads of unique items in the game, such as the all mighty Sword of Dragonclaw and the Lightning Shield and also the Earthfire Sword & Shield combo; most of these items are also hidden in the various secrets areas in every level. For fans of first person games or RPGs this is a must play, it's a polished and truly well made game.

Really REALLY good, like seriously good. Probably one of the best games I've played in quite a while.

Do you like swords and high action combat?
Did you enjoy Dishonored but didn't like how the game chastised you for killing people?
Do you like paper thin plots that allow you to get to the action quicker?
Do you like Ignoring weapons and using physics objects to kill people?
Do you like kicking people off of cliffs?

Then I've got the game for you, right here, this one.

The swordplay is by far the best in any game I've ever seen, its very similar to Dishonored's swordplay (same devs) but it feels a lot more satisfying, and you can also throw almost every object in the world to completely ignore that great swordplay, also kicking, lots and lots of kicking. If there would be any comparison to make, Imagine thief, but with dark souls levels of combat, in first person. That is this game, this game right here, so good, so very very good.

On a scale of 1 to 10 I'd give it an 8.5, while the gameplay is really, really good, it doesn't give you quite enough downtime to make the mechanics not feel stale after a while. Also enemy variety is sorely lacking.
(enemy with sword, enemy with sword and shield, archer, zombie, mage, faster zombie and spiders.) There's a few neat boss monsters, and by a few I mean like 3, despite there being 6ish boss fights in the game, meaning that they get re-used often (I swear i've fought 4 cyclopses by now, once you figure out that only their head takes damage, the fight gets a little less epic. Also the plot is almost complete garbage, the only meaningful choice you get is one where you decide on whether or not you want to use a demon form (useless outside of very narrow circumstances) or a SLIGHTLY better sword than the one you already have.

There's a perk based leveling system in the game that gets in the way of having fun more than it helps, you have to UNLOCK the ability to do a jumping attack, which is literally you just holding your sword upwards and slashing down. You may as well just give yourself all the upgrades at the beggining with cheats. Besides those two things, the games is pretty much perfect. All the maps are neat and unique (although the "orc tunnels go on a little longer than I would have liked) and up until the very end you're going through a very nice dungeon crawl.

So yeah, this is a good game, pick it up when you can, even if you have to wait for it to be on sale, it's worth it.
