About This Game

Near future. You are a prisoner in a deep underground prison. Your only option is escape, but can you make it and get out? Overcome your fears and solve puzzles all alone.

Features:
- Solitary dungeon atmosphere that will challenge you
- Puzzles and objectives to solve and get through
- Sci-fi environment to explore
- Cinematic feel as you are engulfed in this sci-fi thriller
- Trading cards

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP / Vista / 7 - Attention: Windows 8 MAY cause problems
    • Processor: AMD Phenom 9550
    • Memory: 2048 MB RAM
    • Graphics: Radeon 4800 or better (of course also NIVIDA)
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 2 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX 9 compatible
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows XP / Vista / 7 - Attention: Windows 8 MAY cause problems
    • Processor: intel core i5 or better @3GHZ
    • Memory: 4096 MB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GeForce 8600 или ATI Radeon HD series

Honestly the game is kind of interesting, but not really worth the price.

The graphics were nice, and the atmosphere was great, but the world still felt blocky, so adding more static objects or something to make it feel real and not square would be awesome. The walking felt really slow, but considering the first part of the game is set in a small area, it makes some sense if they want the game to play a tad longer, but even the running was barely faster than that and felt way too slow for a game. The puzzles I played were pretty bland, lack-luckster, and had been done countless times since the 90's, nothing really special there, although I didn't get the full experience as the game keeps crashing when loading the 2nd area. Sound effects were great, but the lack of music made it awkward. No, not tense, or suspensful. Awkward, and kind of distracting from the whole atmostphere.

The writing seems bland, un-motivated, and very simplistic. It's kind of obvious they were going off of nothing here, which isn't inherently bad, but it really didn't have anything special going for it. Bland "puzzles", bland writing, no music, and decent at best graphics didn't really give this game much to stand on.

Honestly, not a bad game at all, the devs are off to a good start, but it doesn't seem polished yet. I can't say it doesn't feel complete, as I didn't get a chance to finish the game, but the part I HAVE played, the beginning, wasn't very polished, and the beginning is where you want the majority of your polish, since it's the first thing the player gets to experience for your game, and will form A LOT of assumtions and expectations for the rest of your game, so it's important to set that tone and atmosphere correctly, but who knows? Maybe it has better puzzles once you beat the beginning in 10 minutes!

Overall has the potential to be a cool game, but right no it's not worth the money unless it's you have some extra steam-cash laying around. I think the developers have potential, FOR SURE! They just need to work on polishing their skills a little more and maybe communicating with others in their field to gather opinions and playtesters to REALLY polish the game and make it a solid $20 experience rather than a half-baked $4 one. Maybe do some more research as to the deep, intricate analysis of video games. Games aren't as simple as just making them, there is A LOT of thought that must go into all aspects of games, and that's something I personally find enjoyable to educate myself about, and is a necessity for any budding game-developer. Good luck to the devs and their future endeavours, and if you decide to buy the game, I hope you can actually load all parts of it!

EDIT: As a side note, there are no options in the game either. No brightness, no volume, no resolution, nothing. Just "New Game", "Load Game", and "Exit". Don't expect anything else.

You are prisoner #8, and have allegedly killed (7) other people including your family. Something at the underground prison has gone ghastly wrong and an opportunity to escape has presented itself. Worth $0.99-$1.99 assuming you know up front about the issues.

Case #8 is yet another cookie-cutter first person horror game. I'd say it's a technically poor one, but despite the frequent crashes and bugs, it does get an acceptable framerate, so overall it's average for its genre in that area.

Walk around a bunch of poorly lit rooms, pick up items and activate arbitrary event flags so that you can open doors and walk around more poorly lit rooms. If that sounds boring as hell, don't worry. The game also attempts a few jump scares, which lack any followup and thus end up being about as scary as a typical YouTube Poop.

The story is a different kind of bad in each chapter. Chapter 1 sets up the bland "escape the place" plot common among amateur games. In chapter 2 you have to glitch yourself through a door to get some dynamyte, which you then stand too close to as it goes off in order to set up the next chapter. Chapter 3 is a clone of P.T. I haven't played or even watched P.T., but I can still tell it's a clone because the level's Steam Trading Card is labeled "P.T." I guess when you admit your game is a rip-off it instantly becomes an homage. Finally, chapter 4 reveals that the player character committed the crime that the game said he committed at the beginning, and then ends with a "To be continued." So in summary, the game goes from cliché storm to idiot plot to borderline plagiarism and concludes on an anticlimax. What a great way to spend an hour and a half.

It's okay for the game to suck, though, because it's only four dollars, and everyone knows that MSRP is the only metric on which we should base our expectations. Nevermind that good games like Ballpoint Universe: Infinite are in the same price point; if it's under five bucks, all standards go out the window!

Seriously, though, ♥♥♥♥ this game with a red herring laser mechanism.

You're trying to sell to people who actually enjoy video games; people who are more savvy than you're apparently used to. This kind of behavior reflects very badly on you, so cut it out.
