About This Game

"Julai" - is an arcade flight shooter.

The action takes place on Technogenic Planet. You can improve your ship and flying through the Thorns to earn a lot of points.
The game is designed for reaction and ardor.

Key features of the game:

  • Horizontal-endless game world;
  • You can fly wherever you want (down, horizontally, upwards);
  • Each ship item affects its functionality;
  • Any item of the ship can be attached or dropped via construction yard;
  • Every item of the ship can be taken as a bonus;
  • If you catch the 'autopilot' bonus, you can fly with "autopilot";
  • There are various types of opponents (fighters, drills which clinging to the ship, turret...);
  • Ship death occurs in case of loss or destruction of the pilot cabin.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows Vista
    • Processor: Intel Core i3
    • Memory: 1000 MB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GT640
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 1200 MB available space

Introduction
Developed and published by Gennady Guryanov, Julai attempts to distance itself from your average arcade flight shooter by introducing a horizontal and randomly generated level design into the mix. For being the first and so far only project of a lone enthusiast, the game is highly polished.

Gameplay
Julai features customizable ships, fitting the player’s preferences to some degree and also avoiding the gameplay from becoming stale at any point. While the lack of a multiplayer mode can be understandable given the genre, I cannot overlook the fact that we have absolutely no Steam Achievements or Trading Cards, to form a badge from. This game surely deserved to appear in more than just our total games list. Apart from the total playtime, we have no other indication of the effort put into passing through the levels of this title. Julai is not an easy game and playing it with the keyboard or mouse, is not the best approach in my opinion. I was fortunate enough to get it up and running on my joystick/flightstick without much of a hassle and I found that control scheme, to be the best experience you can get from this flight shooter.

As far as the graphical details are concerned, Julai is gorgeous. The levels feature enough detail and diversity in both terrain and enemies, to make it a pleasant playthrough even after several hours. You can almost forgive the game’s lack for a competitive mode, since it looks the part and stands out amongst more washed-out flight arcades out there.

The game features a rather generic selection of sounds and beats, which manage to keep up with the Sci-Fi atmosphere. Nothing special, but it gets the job done. The sounds made by the laser weaponry could have used more variation. I did not apreciate the total lack of any voice acting.

Pros
+ Beautiful graphics
+ Fun gameplay with adjustable difficulty
+ Customization options

Cons
- No multiplayer
- No Steam Trading Cards or Achievements
- Generic sounds

Result / Final thoughts
Julai proved to an interesting title in which I really managed to use my flighstick efficiently. There aren’t very many games on Steam that can take use of this gaming peripheral so if you have one, use it by all means. It will make the game far more enjoyable. I couldn’t rate the game less since it proved to be entertaining even with just the singleplayer mode to contend with. I’m certain that both Trading Cards and Achievements can be added in a future update and this game certainly deserves those features. Please make it happen, Gennady!

Rating 7/10

This game is crazy, the atmosphere, graphic style and especially the music are very strange. Got it for 2 bucks shortly after launch, but well worth 5-10 if you are into contraption building and roguelike shooting.

So this is like a post apocalyptic cybertron planet thing, and you are flying in a space truck cabin, that you can upgrade by picking up modules (green wrench), and pressing space to enter the builder menu (keys are not rebindable atm, which is a shame, probably only playable with KB&M). You start with a basic set of cabin, gun, side armor and engines, and go from there. You only lose when the cabin is destroyed, but once without engines, you move very slowly, so unless you got replacement engines handy you are as good as dead, especially at the core of the planet.

The level. Yes there is only one level, but it's never ending procedurally generated. You have the surface, with the run of the mill enemies, then when you enter the tunnels to go undeground, you start finding the tougher stuff, and when you get into the core, unless you are heavily upgraded, you are going to die within seconds.

+ Very cool upgrade/building system
+ Smooth performance
+ Can reach very fast speeds with upgrades
+ Ridiculous weapons
+ Big and difficult world

- It's Unity with cheap assets
- Needs an extra pan camera view on the builder
- Very expensive for what it is, unless on sale.

It's hard to give a thumbs up or thumbs down for a game that's clearly a first attempt at designing games of any kind. There's little to be said about money-grabbing, and for this cost I feel like that's what this is. For the most part, its borrowed assets thrown together in Unity's free version to create a contextless, visually noisy arcade flight shooter.

There is no opening scenes, no voice-acted introduction - you hit start and go. It's an understated approach to design that we a game can have no story or context. For the purposes of this game, its just fine. Hit play, blow ♥♥♥♥ up. Works for me. I wish Just Cause 3 was like that. The problem arises from the design of the map and enemy placement.

There's no order to it. It's - presumably - a randomly generated landscape of infinite length and width, covered with entirely random enemy placement. For the short time I played, there was only 2 enemies - small flying buggers that were really great at remaining behind you, and stationary turrets. Combat is a combination of on-rails shooter and an actual flight game, and its nice to not have to rely on keeping my nose pointed directly at enemies to be able to hit them.

While it is possible to mod your ship, understand that along with no context, no tutorials are present. The player will spend some time figuring out exactly how to build a ship and what the various parts do. These parts can be collected by flying into some of the many hovering icons throughout the map.

Sound design is a puzzling combination of music I couldn't hear, and the obnoxious laser fire that was drowning it out.

Overall, there's something playable here, more or less. It isn't broken, per se. It's a beginner's first time out, as far as I can tell. I don't believe this is a scam, but the astronomical price would suggest it. Some people are going to see it that way.

From me to the dev: people expect certain things from a game that costs 20 bucks. Even other, more original, more complete games have been harpooned just for costing more than they're worth. If you're going to continue designing games, please take that into consideration. Thanks for taking the time to make it though, and please keep going. Hopefully next time you put a game out, I'll be delighted enough to hit the Yes button rather than the no.

Edit: Ah! That reminds me. The reticle is a touch hard to see, since the game's predominate color pallette is warm colors and a lot of yellows. Also, maybe switch the roll buttons to Q and E rather than R and F? That might make it easier to fly without thinking about it.
