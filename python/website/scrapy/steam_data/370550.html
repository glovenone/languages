About This Game

Challenge the darkness with your swift orb matching skills, mighty magical enchantments and earthshaking powerups. Match the orbs before they fall into the abyss and find your way through mysterious lands of startling beauty!

A long time ago five enchanted keys were created. The keys were scattered around the lands, and they still remain undiscovered. Many have come to find them, and so far all have failed. Will you find the keys and unlock the secrets, or will you join the endless ranks of souls forever trapped within these lands?

Sparkle 2 is the long-awaited sequel to the award-winning hit game Sparkle enjoyed by millions of players since 2007. Sparkle 2 is an independent sequel and a spiritual successor to the first installment suitable for both new and existing fans of the series.

Features


  • Experience premium action puzzle gaming
  • Admire state of the art special effects
  • Explore three game modes: Story, Survival, and Challenge
  • Discover over 90 unique levels throughout the story
  • Supercharge your Slinger with over 200 combinations of 16 different Enchantments
  • Find exciting locations and delve deep into the mystery
  • Immerse yourself in a story narrated with professional voice overs
  • Enjoy the soundtrack by the award-winning composer Jonathan Geer

Sparkle 2 is a game very similar to Zuma, but enjoyable in its own right.

It's quite a casual action based casual game, where you need to shoot balls at others in order to create matches of 3 or more. The queue you are shooting at is constantly moving, and if too many balls go past the line at the end of the path, you lose. It's a simple objective, but very fun as the game progresses and comes up with more intricate map paths, and powerups.

The powerups offer a decent amount of customization and strategy before you go into a level, but I found myself not changing through many because it felt like they didn't do enough to bother with it.

The powerups in-game are very fun and satisfying. Some do things like move the queue of balls back, where as others will give you awesome weapons that allow you to blast through rows of balls to buy yourself some time.

Overall, it's a very easy game to pickup and learn, but I had so much fun playing it through that I have to recommend it.

Sparkle 2
is a match 3 puzzle game heavily inspired by Zuma where you "challenge the darkness with your swift orb matching skills, mighty magical enchantments and earthshaking powerups. Match the orbs before they fall into the abyss and find your way through mysterious lands of startling beauty!

Story
It has something to do with you being tasked to collect 5 ancient keys that have been scattered across the lands. The story was the weakest part of the game for me and it felt unnecessary. I think that the only reason it was put into the game was to give you an extra incentive to complete all the levels and to differ it even more from Zuma.

Gameplay-It consists of you trying to match up specific coloured orbs and make them disappear before they reach the end of the track. This track winds around and changes each level.

There are also powerups which can help you take down more orbs quicker aswell as enchantments that makes it more efficient.

In all the 90+ levels the only things that really change in between each one are the track layout and the order of orbs. This ends up leading to repetitiveness making this the sort of game that you would drop in to play for only a short amount of time. Luckily, there are 3 gamemodes -quest, survival and challenge- which partially saves it from becoming overly monotonous.

Graphics-The visuals are really good. They look nice and colourful, they even went to the extent of putting in a feature to assist colourblind people!

Summary

Pros
✔ Gameplay is fun
✔ Powerups and enchantments
✔ 90+ levels
✔ 3 gamemodes
✔ Graphics are nice
✔ Trading Cards

Cons
✘ Achievement is bugged
✘ Mediocre story
✘ Repetitive

Conclusion
Sparkle 2 is a fun game which is great to pop in and play every now and then. It does copy Zuma, but atleast it does it well.

7.7/10

Sparkle 2 is an enjoyable addition to the Luxor/Zuma type of match 3 game.

A line of different colored balls slowly creeps forward and you have to shoot balls to match 3 or more to make them disappear. If they reach the end of the line before you get rid of them all, it's game over (though I'm assuming you can keep trying after failing). It's in the Puzzle category, but it's acutally an action puzzle rather than turn-based or one that just sits there while you decide what move to make. In this game you have to think fast and have good reflexes sometimes.

Occasionally you get power-ups that will help you. Fireballs that destroy balls. Color Match that makes several balls all the same color. There are ones that slow the train or make it go backwards for a short time.

There are also equipable power-ups that you can choose once you've unlocked them. Such as unlimited swap of whatever colored ball is currently next in line to shoot. Or every 10th ball you get is a fireball. You can try out the equipables and customize the way you want to play the game.

There is a main story with a path that you follow, but there are also other game modes that you unlock along the way.

Graphics are nice. I especially like the details on the balls (no jokes please). They aren't just a solid color, there is a pattern to them that is nice.

Music is very nice. To me it has a sort of fairytale classical style. Kind of hard for me to describe. It sets the mood perfectly for a jaunt through the creepy forest.

I haven't finished the main story yet. I might be halfway through, going on the equipable power-ups that I have yet to unlock, but I'm not sure, and I've played almost 3 hours according to Steam. And I haven't played the other modes yet, either.

Sparkle 2 is a wonderful puzzle game, very similiar to Zumas Revenge.

The main story talks about five keys and light and dark forces. The keys were scattered around the lands, and they still remain undiscovered. You need to find the keys and unlock their secrets, or fail and join to the dark forever.

As you have noticed it is not a great story but it is the excuse to complete all the stages.

The objective is to throw orbs to a queue of them making groups of three or more orbs to blast them and avoid that they reach a black hole called the abyss.

When you reach some levels, you can add special powers to the "orb thrower" and collect many power-ups that are going to help you to destroy all orbs.

After some levels, you are going to unblock the survival mode and some special challenges too.

Graphics are good and well defined, and their art style remember me forests and druids.

Music is very nice and helps to reach a pleasent enviroment that changes when orbs are near of the abyss.

The game is not very difficult and its very addictive. You can play a couple of hours and lose track of time.

It has achivements and trading cards.

Nice game to play if you want to take a break and stop to kill monsters.
