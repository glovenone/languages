About This Game



** This version of VR Karts is designed specifically and only compatible for HTC Vive **

The checkered flag is about to drop on a new Virtual Reality racing experience!
Get in the drivers’ seat of your own customized kart and race against other players from around the world.
Race through stunning environments, collect power ups and use weapons to gain the upper hand over other racers.
VR Karts is a fun and family-friendly Virtual Reality racing game designed specifically for the HTC Vive.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: Intel i5-4590 (or equivalent)
    • Memory: 8 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia Geforce 970 (or equivalent)
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Sound Card: Direct X compatible
    • Additional Notes: SteamVR compatible headset required

Posted: 8 July
I just picked up this game, and I feel bad for both the developer as well as the community, as the game is a really fun little racing game with a lot of (unjust) bad reviews. Now... don't get me wrong... I feel like they should add some real motion control steering, but I have been playing using my xbox 360 controller, and it's a blast. The graphics are cartoony and fun. The driving on the controller is smooth and very responsive. The view itself is great, with nearly zero interference from the cart itself. You can get a great view of everything happening around you. There are a wide variety of different weapons to use in the game... from tire spike strips to homing missiles. The game also runs really well. I have a GTX980 (not a ti), and it runs perfectly smoothly through the whole race. The graphics are also easy on the eyes, being that games with a lot going on often times end up looking rough and pixelated in VR. There's plenty to look at in the levels, but it's all pretty crisp (as crisp as VR can be). I certainly feel like they could improve some of the control features. Having boost and drift the same thing is kida silly, and I think it's way too easy to spin out when you're bumped. Still... for 15 bucks, you get a fun, great looking Mario Kart feel with great VR driving action. Adding true motion controls should clearly be at the top of their list, but for those who enjoy using a controller... you should be plenty happy with your purchase.

I'd love to actually play this game, but the current control system is broken. I tried racing and my driver kept steering off in different directions even when not touching the Vive touchpad. Conversely, it would sometimes stop steering even when using the touchpad. There also doesn't appear to be any support for driving wheels, whereas the Rift version advertises that support.

There is also no in-game representation of the Vive controllers even in the menu. This makes it difficult to know exactly where on the track pad you are pressing, since you have no visual feedback, but they still divide the trackpad into multiple buttons.

It's disappointing how something as basic as getting the controls wrong can break an otherwise promising looking game.

Updated: I tried it with an Xbox 360 controller and that worked fine. It was actually controllable. However, the game did not specifically indicate it could use an Xbox controller; the custom controller setup also only seems to work with the Xbox controller but not the Vive controllers.
