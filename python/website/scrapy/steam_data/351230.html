About This Game

"Counter Spell" combines elements of classic role playing games with modern stealth-based shooters. The player can choose from six different classes, each with their own unique spells, abilities, and perks.

Single player, multiplayer coop, and competitive multiplayer are all supported with different game modes for each. A wide array of spells and items make for fun and intense matches. Throw flasks of wildfire at your opponents, lay cleverly placed traps, or just rain fireballs down upon them.

An open, streaming world makes for an exciting sandbox to adventure in!

Features:

  • Six Classes (Mage, Priest, Shaman, Warlock, Warrior, Rogue)
  • Eleven Game Modes (Quest, Adventure, Deathmatch, Rescue, Overlord, Survival, Defense, Kingslayer, Tag, Capture, Reaper)
  • 60+ Spells
  • 500+ Items
  • 13 Unique Zones plus a Random Dungeon Option
  • 15 Enemy Types
  • Talent System
  • Achievements & Stats
  • Multiplayer Level Editor

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP
    • Processor: 2.0 GHz Single Core
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Shader Model 3.0 or better (DX10 GPU)
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 300 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: XNA HiDef uses DX9 but requires a DX10 or better GPU
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows 7 or Later
    • Processor: 2.0 GHz Dual Core
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 512MB Dedicated Graphics Memory
    • DirectX: Version 10
    • Network: Broadband Internet connection
    • Storage: 300 MB available space

If you enjoyed old RPGs like Hexen, Daggerfall, Enclave, Gauntlet or Thief, buy Counter Spell! This game may be Early Access, but you're getting a quality game with tons of content for under $10.

▼ THE GOOD ▼
● Singleplayer took me 5-6 hours with the Warlock Class the first time. About 2 hours as the Shaman the next. It takes long enough that you'll feel satisfied but short enough you won't get bored. Also you can play Singleplayer with friends :)
● The game world is a LOT bigger than you'd think! It's not Skyrim big, but big for an indie game and filled with hidden passages, loot and monsters.
● Each class has their own unique spells but most of the funnest ones are at max level so it gives you something to work towards (like the "vine-ladder" spell in the trailer). The game also let's you blatantly see what spells you'll unlock when picking a class.
● Very well balanced! You should reach level 20 (max) by the time you reach the final boss without any grinding outside of killing everything you see once. The game does a good job being tough, but not impossible and has harder modes for those who find it too easy.
● Treasure and enemies respawn so you don't need to worry if you miss anything, chances are you can find it again.
● Aside from the passive quests (kill enough enemies in zone-1, collect 3 unique items from zone-1, kill a unique boss / enemy in zone-1) there are player activated repeatable quests. These are great for leveling up as most of them spawn lots of enemies in an area that's just been cleared.
● TONS of other game modes for Co-op and PVP!
● In-game Map Editor is very in-depth and allows some super complex builds.
● Constant game updates. We're talking a couple a week (depending on what's added), not once every 5 months.

▼ THE BAD ▼
● Finding other players online is a bit tough since the player base is so small. This isn't really a problem with the game, just it's popularity. If you're buying Counter Spell specifically for multiplayer, make sure you have some friends to play with you.
● Online play can be very laggy depending on the host and result in game crashes.
● The Map Editor doesn't have a tutorial and is very complicated to learn.
● Ranged enemies are all over the place making the fighter classes very difficult as a starting character.
● Combat is shooting with spells / arrows or slashing with a sword. If you've played one mage, your basic attacks won't change, only your spells. There isn't any way to "fix" this, I just found the game gets a bit grindy on your second playthrough with a similar class.
● XP drops from enemies in the form of purple crystals and forces the player to collect them. This isn't so bad at first, but try doing it for the entire game. Quests give XP instantly and even playing online shares XP as soon as an enemy is killed, so I'm not sure why the crystals are there.
● Enemy AI either "sees you" or "doesn't see you yet". When activated, they'll run towards the player no matter the distance until you or they are dead. NOTE: Minions use the same AI so they will aggro everything they see. I'm not really complaining about this feature because the AI works fine for what the game is, but I didn't consider it a "good" selling feature :P

If you want to see some gameplay, here's my First Look video where I explain the basic game mechanics and show a few spoilers about the end-game https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D5zmVlBg5Ss

Thanks for reading! I hope you found this review helpful ^_^ v.

Ah. Counter Spell.

How I love the.

This game is insane once you get into it. And no we're not talking rubber room insanity here. This is full on 25+ enemies rushing you and your party that total only 3 or 4 people. Sure you can have more people if they join you or if you drag them into it. But you will know when you find that other person that knows what they're doing with this game. Because then things just explode forward.

Survival is the best mode in my opinion. Endless waves of enemies that grow in level. Your level caps at 20, but not the enemies. If you can survive. As of right now, me and some other people have hit wave 200. And because I had to go, didn't get a chance to see how much longer we've could've gotten. The enemies were level 50 or 45 I believe, while again we stayed at level 20.

As for the other modes. I've not tried them much. But it mostly depends on what you like and who you play with. You can build maps then upload it to the workshop. Which the building system is a little tricky, but you can do quite a bit with it. Plus it's fun to make a map then survive on it.

Let's talk about the dev a moment. The dev is great with updates and he/she listens to suggestions and updates roll out regularly. Which is a huge plus for this game, and I love how this game is shaping up to be.

15/10 Gameplay
15/10 For the dev being active.
10/10 for the graphics
20/10 for the price

999999999999999999999999999999999999% Fun.
