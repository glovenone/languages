What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Dark Forester already has a big content (17 Character level, 100+ Monsters, Primary and Secondary skills, PvP, forest decoration, Traps, Units; Garrosons, Events, Sandbox Elements and more) and i want to give a chance to play the game now! This in turn gives me a chance to improve the game based upon player feedback! By getting Early Access not only are you supporting me, you'll get to follow the development and help me tune and improve the game to be the best it can be! My goal is to make Dark Forester one of the greatest PvP adventure. I would be thrilled to have you join Dark Forester.

WARNING: This is an Early Access, Alpha game is undergoing continuous optimisation.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“I'm estimating that the game will be complete, it takes as long as it takes. Alpha goes in three phases. Next alpha phase (2) will get additional content and max. avatars level 17.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The final release will contain a campaign, extra PvP and adventure maps, generated forests, developer Mode for Unity3d fans. i'm planning to add more avatar classes, story, additional monsters, weapon shop, traps, primary skills, forest soul skills, forest brush, battle grounds, arenas and more. I'll also make changes based upon player feedback!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current Early Access has 17 Avatars level, 100+ monsters, primary and secondary skills, PvP content, forest decoration, traps, units, protectors, 2 camera views, garrisons, heroes, 10+ Fantasy objects, adventure map, 17 spells, weather and day/night effect influences the game, 28 art of trees and more. ”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Alpha: 0,99$, i need more Feedback, and also stronger PvP Forests
Final release price: 27,99$”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Heavily, The community is encouraged to mail me feedback@darkforester.com , i post weekly updates/bug fixes on community hub and darkforester.com website. You can chat me or start discussions, i'm always there for you. I need you to make good game.”

About This Game

Dark Forester is a Hardcore Forest Management Game that employs your best aspects of the RPG, RTS, Crossplatforming MMO and god game genres. Create your own forest, customize it like your heart's wishes, set magic and physical traps. Protect your land from enemy waves! I would be thrilled to have you join Dark Forester! I would like to improve game based upon your feedback!

Dark Forester is still in early alpha development phase!

What awaits you?
Part-physics based Objects, like Great Ground Cata, Corpses, Falling Trees, Exploding Objects.
New Art of Gameplay: Units stuck in corpses or fallen trees.
Units have 'detection range' and 'lose sight' characteristics. They go in fight only if enemy is in 'detection range' radius and will follow him only if he is in 'lose sight' radius.
Enemies attack everything what you own. Also trees, but only if they are on they way.
Only special enemies attack fixed units, like your avatar or livestock. Normal unit does not distinguish between your avatar, your buildings, your fences or anything else what you character own.

Early Access Review
Posted: 11 October, 2015
Bought it because I liked the concept and it was below $1. It might have potential, but right now, you are just stuffed in the middle of a forest without much explanation. There's a short tutorial that basically tells you "trees are good, press this button to summon waves of enemies", but not much beyond that. When you start, your forest is very large, and your actual buildings take up only a small part of the area, and the enemies can attack trees in the forest directly, and calling waves of enemies often results in them just attacking the trees, taking your stuff and you don't even see them or know where they are. You do have the ability to move the trees, but given the clunky interface and the size of the forest, I really didn't feel like spending literally hours positioning all 300 and some trees closer to the base so I can actually protect them. Also, the main character's spells (at least the starter ones) will destroy trees on contact, and trees cost resource to build or replace, and they have a tendency to sail right by enemies.

TL;DR - This could be good with a polished and more intuitive interface and a smaller or at least better defined area where the combat takes place, but as it is now, lack of these things cripples it.

Edit - quick dev response is always a good thing though, hopefully there will be improvements and I can change to a positive review.

I think this feature is the one that needs the most revamping of all of them. It should not strive to be a tower defense. The least pressing but preferable if enhanced issues are the graphics and display. I can give a pass for the battlefield graphics, but it's so pixelized even at optimal levels that when you need to read something it's hard to decipher (yes I have 20/20 vision.) The display is more of a problem however in that you can't set it to full screen and your cursor disappears when you play the game, so in the middle of something important you often find your mouse pop out of the screen and that's kind of annoying. There should be an in-game cursor so you know where the mouse is and it should be put at full screen.

Once again I really like the idea of this game and I hope it improves and even becomes more popular, but until those much much needed enhancments come in I feel this was money poorly-spent.

Posted: 12 February, 2015
The Game
First of all, I understand that this game is Early Access, even this type of access is not beta, but on alpha stage, which, in my opinion, is too early for release, even for Early Access games. This issue will be covered later. The game's interesting premise is a mix and match of several genres, and the premise is simple : build and defend your own forest. One of the interesting mechanic is the enemies will be looking to destroy trees, not your base or something like that.

On the gameplay though, the game acts like a tower defense game, where you place units, and their facing, towards the enemies. You could purchase buildings, trees, and other things from the shop inside the game. Like any other TD game, you will gain gold after finishing a wave. You can also move your hero around to do some damage, like TD games in old Warcraft. Your hero could cast spells, which are weak, but get stronger as you progress throughout the game. You could purchase also buildings, which can unlock units, or to gain extra income. You could also purchase traps to stop the enemy.

The game mechanics is good enough, but unfortunately, the game play is not, which will be covered below.

Observations
Missing tower defense mechanics
The most blatant failure in this game is the game don't mention what enemy will approach in the next wave, but the most important failure is the game don't tell you where the enemies will be. As your units can't be moved during the wave, placing your units become paramount (hence, this game is not RTS, because the unit can't be moved) Not to mention that the enemies are attacking trees which can be far from your starting position, where you can't do anything because you can't move your units. This is the most serious flaw on this game.

If the intention is to create a strategy/RTS game, the units should be allowed to move/ordered. If the intention is to create a TD game, there should be a notifications where the enemies will spawn, and limit the areas the enemy could attack. The enemies could spawn anywhere, where you could only defend a point on the map. This is a serious failure, as in this way the game could not be played. Either allow your units to be free-roaming, or constrain the attacking enemy units to a specific and known position.

Bad UI
The UI is bad. The option of "Main Map" takes us to "Main Menu", there are mistakes in writing, which are OK (for example, price is written as prais). If you try to save the game, there are no notifications that your game is already saved, and also this game only have one slot. Of course, the load function is confusing too, and only load 1 saved games. Even when you are killed, there are no notifications, the game only restarts the game, and continue.

In the game, the UI is also bad. To rotate a unit, you must use Right + Left click, which are very unusual, while a lot of others have more user-friendly ways to rotate unit, such as "hold to rotate" that are very typical in C & C series. Or, a shortcut in keyboard to rotate, which are less user-friendly than "hold to rotate" but still do. Some games also use the right click function to rotate (Deus Ex inventory, Prison Architect). Also, when you move units, the mouse is very clunky, that is takes 3-4 times to move an Ogre to a destination that is still within your screen. If you are moving units across the map, this is very frustating. When moving a unit, the game, instead of using the selected unit as a cursor, use the Windows cursor as a cursor, thus creating disrepancies of movement.

The game also did not show the stats of unit in the shop, instead the game show the stats on your current unit. This is bad decision, since one must take a look at the stats before purchasing a unit. The building role is also not clear, for example, the Ogre Hut is for defense. What is the meaning of "for defense"? It should be made clear, if it will help defense (acting like a defense tower) or unlock new units, or anything else. "For defense" is a very vague term.

Adequate tutorial
This game has adequate tutorial, which is a great step, as usually EA games forgo this for a better gameplay.

Look and Feel
The weather system, as it seems not bring anything in gameplay, I categorize it as look n' feel, along with other ambience features. The music in the opening screen is good, but the other things in the game, including the weather system, is nothing special.

Verdict
At the current game state and price, I suggest not purchasing this game. The game price is a little bit steep, yet the condition of the game is not very good. The developers are working on this, but like I said before, the state of this game is not ready enough even for Early Access. I really like the notion of building and defending your own forest, but the game execution of the great grand design still fails. I hope that the game will improve on the future, but for the current time of writing, this game is not worth it (yet).

Early Access Review
Posted: 11 February, 2015
This is Early Access Game, so this review is also based on the game's current state. (11/2/2015)

1. Game performance: horrible. Game's engine is unity aaaaaaaaand the performance is indeed horribad. My machine can run Skyrim without lag but I'm getting only like 15 fps here in this game. It needs so much optimization since I honestly think it's unacceptable for game like this that doesn't even require advanced graphic capabilities.

About the settings, in-game menu doesn't give any noticeable options. You won't see any exotic options for graphic settings here, no fancy AA, Shadow or particle options. The setup is mostly done in menu pre-game (has launcher) where the game will present you with preconfigured setups - Fastest, Fast, Simple, Good, Beautiful, Fantastico.. oh I meant Fantastic. The launcher also allows you to preconfigure your keyboard mappings, though my experience when setting up these controls was not pleasant one. Very difficult if not impossible to reset back to default state, so I finally just left them alone.

2. UI/UX: awful. You're left on your own to learn the mechanics of the game. No tutorial, no hints whatsoever. If you first timer loading the game you'll be puzzled as to why the random buttons are placed on random places. So, main menu, what's this connect button for? Ok let's tap it.... Oh my it loads my game...

Hmm... ok, so next!

Game's loaded and I'm ready to go! Let's check around!

To the left there are UI to dropping... supposedly... your units. Cow, ok for what? Prise: 1 gold. Prise? What is this madness? Did the developer want to write 'price'? Alright typo, let's check another. Hm... Ogr... Attacking unit? What's with this power level Power, Strength and Potential indicators? What do they mean? Can my Ogr have over 9000 powahhhh???

Then to the top right of the screen supposedly there are counters to tell you how much resources you currently possess. Seemingly it's gold... lumber... crystals? What am I gonna do with these stuffs? Again, this is where the game's failing, it doesn't even give tooltip for these items to help players how to utilize them.

I'm appalled.

3. Sound: Put the game on silent, fire up Winamp or MP3 player of your choice, and play your own music. That's all you need.

4. Story: Mostly revolves like Clash of Clans. You write your own story... or so from my observation. Despite it has RPG label slapped on it, story is pretty much non-existent. If you're looking for games that has touching, well written plot, then this one is definitely not for you.

Thankfully, the Developer seems to be fairly active on Steam forum. Well, this is just the beginning, so I can't say much about how he will do this project. Whether he'll make a good product out of this game or not, it remains to be seen.

For 30 USD (US region) it's not worth it. Slash it by 90% still not worth it. Game has so much to be desired. I bought this game when it glitched (like 0.002 cents), so I don't feel being ripped off, but certainly I will if I had paid 30 USD.

Buy at your own risk - you've been warned.
