About This Game

War Birds: WW2 Air strike 1942



War Birds is a 2.5D World War 2 air combat game, with impressive 3D warplanes and beautiful 2D environments.

Badges :

Fly as a legendary heroic pilot on the side of Allies against hostile enemy. In the solo campaign you will be fighting across the whole of Europe. Each mission is unique and you will have to use skill, tactics and strategy to complete the levels. Missions span historic events like, The Battle of Britain, The Battle of Stalingrad and The Battle of Berlin along with many more significant events.

You will encounter many different types of ground and naval enemies, such as tanks, artillery, infantry, warships and battle cruisers.

Missions feature scenarios such as, having to ambush an enemy base and terminate specific targets using air strikes. Also taking out enemy battle cruisers and then destroying the enemy bases with limited bombs.

You earn points, gold and experience from every enemy destroyed in battle. Use your gained skills across the 22 battle zones and custom upgrade your aircraft to enhance your firepower and movement.

FEATURES :


*Intense, hardcore and thrilling combat

*Three types of difficulties

*Different types of warplanes: Avia B-534, Morane Saulnier 406, Supermarine Spitfire, Avro Lancaster

*Follows historic events

*Stunning animations and realistic units & buildings

*22 levels and over 130 missions

Earn your medals and achieve the highest rank in this airborne shooter which takes the intensity of a dog fight to new heights.

A fairly interesting and exciting game for those who like war-themed games. It's great and somewhat entertaining, however it's not without it's flaws. The menu and the instructions really unclear. I ended up accidentally buying two sets of magazines in the beginning because I wasn't sure if I had already bought it. There was no notification whatsoever upon purchase. I'm pretty sure navigating through the main menu was more of a challenge than the first few games itself.

The amount of spelling and grammatical mistakes in the game is also incredibly high for a game that's being sold on Steam. Made me question whether or not this game was proof checked before it was released. I saw that they just added two additional languages (French and Dutch) into the game a few weeks ago. Perhaps they should start focusing on the base language of the game first instead of adding new ones.

Flaws aside however, the gameplay is actually pretty nice. The graphics are acceptable (could've been better, but I'm not going to complain; each game has their own style), the sound effects are quite on point and the game itself is neither too easy nor it is too hard. Overall I would recommend this game - even more if the minor messes I mentioned above get fixed. But you get roughly what you paid for, if not more.

General Impression
War Birds: WW2 Air strike 1942 is a indie, strategy and simulation game. The graphics are very good for the price tag, being 2.5D, and the textures are fine as well. The air fighters, bombers, tanks, vehicles, etc. are well designed. The atmosphere takes action across Europe, where you have to fight the Germans in every country. There is much grass, waters, trees, as well as villages, airports, alleys, etc. The audio is very enjoyable, they make you feel like a true patriotic soldier. The airplane crashes are alright, also the explosions are well made. The gameplay is pretty much just to destroy air fighters, vehicles, boats, tanks, etc. You can go either close to the land (small altitude) or in the skies (high altitude). The small altitude is for collecting coins and for killing the terrestrial enemies. The high altitude is for destroying the aerial enemies. The user interface is somewhat messy, but if you pay attention to it, you will figure it out, but not very easy though. The controls are pretty decent, even though they could be explained before the game. Something like a tutorial. The engines are pretty nice, I haven't met any bugs/crashes/lagging. The mechanics are pretty nice, I like how the airplane moves, and overall, the mechanics are pretty nice. The air fighter can maneuver, turn forward/back/left/right can go very fast (with turbo), also you can set the speed of the air fighter, and mostly it can shoot air strikes, bombs, machine gun bullets, as well as cooling, repairing, ammo resupplying, gas filling, etc. Overall, it's an enjoyable and relaxing game, if you are into strategy and indie games, but I can recommend it to any gamer as well.

Positive Points
  • Pretty nice graphics
  • Good soundtrack and explosions
  • Challenging gameplay
  • Many areas and environments
  • Three difficulties available
  • Many types of enemies
  • Big areas to fight in
  • Itâs a pretty long game

Negative Points
  • The instructions could be a little more clear
  • Convoys are hard to fight for the first air fighter which you receive
  • The campaign menu is pretty messy
