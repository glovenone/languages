Early Access Review
Posted: 11 October, 2014
Adventurezator is an adventure creator for point and click adventures. Still in its early stages at the time of this review, It shows a lot of promise, and the style and ease of use are pretty good, allowing a complete amateur to create professional looking levels in a 3d world with little knowledge and experience of adventure games.

The way the npc's and the puzzles are 'Emergent' is the main goal to this. You just add various elements, set a few simple parameters, and let the npc's act accordingly. Set them a few objects to interact with, set up a few friends and enemies, and things to say and just let them go. You don't have to worry too much about pathing or complex dialogue trees (at the moment, though I imagine this may change later)

The cutscene creator is of necessity a bit more complex than the other editing tools, but it looks like it won't be too hard to make a nice looking cutscence to add an interesting story for the campaign. All it requires apart from some experimenting is a bit of artistic skills or use of others work, which makes the ability to use the workshop to use others content a boon to this type of game.

The problem at the moment, apart from the inevitable bugs during development is the simplicity of the puzzles available and lack of ability to customise objects. Only had the game for a short while, but looking at the current state of the game it seems the developer has concentrated on getting the look and the basic mechanics in place first, so while it looks good, and is easy to use, the content and ways to interact are a little limited if you want to dive in and create devious puzzles, and truly create your own world. The current playable levels are also rather limited, as another reviewer put it, they are more to give you examples on which to to base your own work.

Time will tell of course. The developer has to balance ease of use with the complexity and flexibility of the tools available. As more content gets added we will be able to see just how powerful this game creator will be. I can say that even at this early stage, its fun to mess around with and create a little world, and I look forward to seeing what else I can do in the future.

The humour and style of the game is great, and I think it can be a very interesting and involving way to tell your own story. I would recommend this to anyone who would like to express their creativity and make an adventure game, but do not want to have to mess around with the more complex tools available elsewhere.

Update 29/10/14 Published first level. Despite a few difficulties that will be patched out, the process is pretty easy, and the developer is easy to work with when I told him of my problems. The forums have examples of how the developer wishes to move the game forward, and they seem encouraging.
