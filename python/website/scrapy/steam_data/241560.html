Complete Edition


The Crew® Complete Edition includes The Crew® original game with all missions and modes released since launch, its expansion The Crew® Wild Run plus Season Pass content.
The Crew is a revolutionary driving game that leverages new hardware capabilities to connect players online like never before.

  • Enjoy the best driving experience for open-world multiplayer
  • Experience new thrills with motorbikes, monster trucks and roaring dragsters!
  • Customise your cars, for mayhem on- and off-road!
  • Join FreeDrive Challenges and FreeDrive Stunts for unlimited fun

Key features:

  • Over 100 Licensed Vehicles
  • 200+ Tuning Kits
  • A 30 hours Story Campaign
  • Over 210 missions
  • Unlimited Freedrive activities
  • 100+ New Events in The Summit tournament

Wild Run Edition


Team up, compete and get wild across a living open-world USA in the ultimate expansion of the revolutionary MMO driving game The Crew®!

  • Experience thrills with new vehicles including motorbikes
  • Fill your garage with powerful cars, agile motorbikes, unstoppable monster trucks, evasive drift cars and roaring dragsters!
  • Enjoy the best driving experience for open-world multiplayer
  • Immerse yourself in the original The Crew® open world action driving game
  • Show off your skills to earn your ticket to The Summit, an unofficial gathering of thousands of drivers and mechanics from around the world which takes place in iconic locations all around the USA.

The gathering has begun. The Summit is here. The promising thrills of new challenges await you and your friends.

The Crew® Wild Run Edition is your go-to edition of the revolutionary action-driving game, including The Crew® original game plus the all-new The Crew® Wild Run expansion.

About This Game

Your car is your avatar - fine tune your ride as you level up and progress through 5 unique and richly detailed regions of a massive open-world US. Maneuver through the bustling streets of New York City and Los Angeles, cruise down sunny Miami Beach or trek through the breathtaking plateaus of Monument Valley. Each locale comes with its own set of surprises and driving challenges to master. On your journey you will encounter other players on the road – all potentially worthy companions to crew up with, or future rivals to compete against. This is driving at its most exciting, varied and open.

• NEVER DRIVE ALONE
Jump in and out seamlessly and build your crew of four through bonding or intense rivalry. Whether your objective is to take down a convoy or to escape the police, achieving it with friends ensures a fresh experience each time you join in.

• A PLAYGROUND OF UNPRECEDENTED SCOPE AND VARIETY
The entire United States is your driving playground – all roads and everything in between. Every type of terrain imaginable is yours to master – from downtown city streets to suburbs, hillsides, cornfields, canyons, desert dunes or even race tracks.

• PREPARE YOUR CAR FOR THE NEXT CHALLENGE
Collect body and performance parts as you progress to customize and upgrade your licensed cars. Equip your ride with 5 different tuning specs (street, perf, dirt, raid, circuit) to adapt to different driving terrains. Choose from a wide range of perks to enhance your vehicle according to your driving style.

• THE EXPERIENCE GOES ON, WHEREVER YOU ARE
Continue the experience on iOS, Android tablets, and on the web. Track of your friends’ performance and fine tune your ride before taking it live to set new records, or send your crew on jobs while on-the-go to earn more XP and parts.

This game will annoy you and hit every single nerve. You have been warned.

For easily driving around with all freedom in the world, this is it. For a realistic game get NFS.

What I just said I did not just say lightly. I like this game, I do. But in the 5 hours I have played this game so far (In one sitting) I have been so insanely upset and happy at the same time, I still don't know what to feel.
You might ask now: What? How's that possible? Should I buy this now or na?
Well, let me get to that for you now:

If the game is on sale, YES. Get it. Own it.
If not, then don't waste your money.

(I paid 30€ for the Complete Edition and I do not regret it.)

The first thing I can tell you is the handling is something you will HAVE to get used to. I played Need For Speed 2016 before this, so I was used to something more accurate. But the Handling in this game feels so heavy. Too heavy.
But taking curves is fairiyl easy and makes fun.
Offroad it is really easy to slide around corner and get overtaken by AI in every single race, but on the street it's much more difficult to steer. Irony, right?

The handling in this game isn't great, that's obvious. But it's not the worst, either. After 5h I have gotten used to it and no longer get upset because of it. I'm sure if you get into the game even more you won't even notice it. It sure as hell doesn't make the whole game bad though.
Depending on what vehicle you use, the handling should change, but it doesn't really. Even lighter cars still feel heavy. Bikes on the other hand feel much more comfortable and I actually prefer riding them.
The drifting is great though, offroad and on the street. No arguments.

Like I said, I played NFS2016 before this, and I can tell you something now.
If you want a realistic racing game, that challenges you in every turn, is accurate with every car and requires skill and thought, get NFS. It's not just the realistic graphics and models, but the insanely good handling and difference with every car. It requires you to sit down and study it. It's Need for Speed. There's not much more to say, I loved it.
The Crew on the other hand is different.
It's not as realistic, the graphics are lower and the handling is a lot different.

So, why should I even consider The Crew over NFS? Both MP and SP and both racing games.
Well, no.

NFS is good, really good. But like I said, it needs time and liking and skill. It's not something for everyone.
While with The Crew you don't need to worry about your Computer Specs or low framerate. No need to study the game and learn how to drive every corner. Also; you can specialize on off-Road or Dragrace or whatever.
In NFS, you can't.

I like the Crew because it's a game for everyone. You can costumize your cars to whatever your prefered style is:
Dragrace, Drifting, Performance, Street, Offroad, Monster Trucks, etc. In NFS, it's just street racing. But it's perfect.
You see?

Need for Speed is the ultimate Racing game because all you do is Race.

In The Crew you do much more. You can specialize your vehicles and try different kinds of races, different spcialized enemies and upgrade them accordingly.
The little challenges on your way help you improve your Parts and even get your amazing stuff for finishing it. Whenever you ride a road with a challenge twice, you always do the challenge. No matter what.

NFS's tuning is detailed and thought through. You gotta see what parts fit and earn them.

In The Crew you literally get them thrown in behind you. They fit your vehicle.
And this is great. You can level easily and get good parts without having to win any races.

Now, Pro's and Con's:

Pro:
- Huge World
- Specialized Vehicle Costumization
- A lot of Vehicles
- Bikes
- Easy to get into
- A lot of small & big things to do
- The sounds are actually good and don't cause earcancer

Con's:
- Not too good graphics
- The god damn handling
- AI
- AI
- AI
- Did I mention AI?

Now, AI. Yes, yes, yes. I HATE IT. Why? let me explain to you, my friend.
So, in the beginning I chose wisely what veicle I chose and what parts, coming from NFS and all. I was always high in points than my opponents. My car was much more advanced. I did so to win easily, of course.
But the AI didn't care. My car: 210 Street level. Theirs: 150. AND YET I ATE THEIR DUST. Seriously, at level 20 you can have the best gear, the AI can be low, you will have to play multiple times a single god damn race because the AI just takes off, and you start with no Nitro and they do.
Plus, the traffic AI seems to be out for you, always stopping for the enemy AI, but never for you.

There is this one Offroad race that I had to play 7 times, because the Mini copper had infinite Nitro and just blasted away. Had to hit him into a tree to win. #Tactics.
And that shouldn't happen. The game can be so frustrating, it's annoying. It doesn't happen often, but when it does, it really gets on your nerve.

Oh, you need to hunt down a car? Here, let me change lanes from the left to the right without indicators, so I drive right into you. Aw, that car is almost down with 20sec to go? Let me give it infinite nitro so you won't be able to catch up.

Yes, the "ram this vehicle to kill it" missions are unfair as ♥♥♥♥, because they are always faster than you A lot.
I had to learn their route to get them. #TacticsNr.2


But despite all of that, I worked through it and I have to say: This game is good.
Not the "YOU GOTTA GET THEM ALL"-kinda good, but it's good. You can play it.

Getting the entire game, plus Season Pass + ALL DLC' for 30€ was a really good deal. But I wouldn't pay more for this game.

Ending:
This is a good racing game with specialized cars and certain terrainracing for everyone. It's simple to mod and easy to get parts. The graphics are alright, but at least it works smooth on medium PC's. MultiPlayer is great, but no worries, Singleplayer is a lot of fun, too.
For a -once-in-a-while-online-racing-game where you don't have to put a lot of time in, it is amazing.
For a NFS-like racing game, it's not.
If you see something challenging and realistic, not annoying, buy NFS 2016.

If you just wanna drive around, offroad, drag, drifting, get this. The Crew.

8.7/10


And watch out for that grey van. It's always standing around corners or on streets. It doesn't move. It will find you and kill you.
