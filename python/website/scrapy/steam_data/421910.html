Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The game already has a lot of content that we want to share with our community. We’re looking forward to your comments and help to build and polish the game. We’re going to introduce a plenty of extra features shortly. Early Access is the best choice for testing them.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The full release of the first episode depends on your feedback but we assume it will happen in the second half of 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of Panoptes will have serious improvements of the interface, new features in the battle system, and deeper narrative. There will be new enemies and cards, plot missions, and rules. Also, expect to see cutscenes, hear voices of characters, and enjoy additional soundtracks. We are planning to improve some visual parts, balance and general impression of the project. Currently we are developing new characters, enemies and story progression. The game will be available in several languages, but the Early Access version will only be in English.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We consider the current version to be a Pre-Alpha. It is packed with challenging fights, hard bosses, big variety of cards, and lots of lore. UI, audio and visual effects, and some of the game mechanics need serious rework. The tutorial includes only the basic concepts.
Be aware that you won’t be keeping your progress. Users who will beat the main campaign before the wipe will receive additional card’s pack.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The final price has not been determined yet, but presumably it will be higher than in Early Access.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We released the game in Early Access to include everyone in the development. We want to start active discussions and gather feedback from you. We're always open to feedback and appreciate your opinions, comments and ideas.”

About This Game

PANOPTES

A STORY DRIVEN TCG

The game’s plot follows operations of the autonomous unit of clandestine, off-the-books organization — The Club.
You face a duty of shaping up a squad of unique characters. Your employers will provide you with cutting-edge weapons and magic.
Once equipped and ready, they will take on legions of mythical creatures, aliens, and walking nightmares.

WARNING:
THIS GAME IS EARLY ACCESS PRE-ALPHA. PLEASE DO NOT PURCHASE IT UNLESS YOU WANT TO ACTIVELY SUPPORT DEVELOPMENT OF THE GAME AND ARE PREPARED TO HANDLE PERIODIC WIPES, SERIOUS ISSUES AND INCOMPLETE CONTENT.

Games Features


Awesome new TCG experience
Game mechanics focused on PVE, where the sequence of cards and synergy is important as ever.
Deck construction begins with selecting character. Each character has a set of unique abilities, class, and his basic parameters. Each card has a type and unique effect. That allows you to create many different combinations during the battle.

Loaded by Challenge
Every mission is filled with dangerous enemies. Winning is like solving a difficult puzzle.
Player's characters are the ones that fight monsters. Battles are divided in phases: first, the player's actions, then the monsters and the player once again. The player's goal — to destroy all monsters before they kill the character.

Story Driven
It’s an immersive story in a large, multi-faceted world full of mysteries, magic and unknown technologies.
Supernatural exists. People try to convince themselves with reason and often become distracted from unnatural activity. The truth is there if one is willing to see. We know what are those dreadful creatures, that lurk in the shadows. We’ve encountered them every day for many years. We fight them and we push them back. One day they will crawl back to their holes and never return. Until then - join us and watch them gone.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP / Vista / 7 / 8 / 10
    • Processor: Dual Core at 2Ghz and above
    • Memory: 1024 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Intel HD 4000
    • DirectX: Version 9.0

PANOPTES is a surprisingly deep strategic card game with a single-player campaign. It is a bit overwhelming at first, but it quickly becomes easy to understand and play, and is a lot of fun.

This is a game with a lot of room for improvement, but is still a lot of fun. Despite my gripes about the game, I've been hooked since I started playing. With a bit more polish, this game could be really special.

This is not meant to be a technical review (as I'm a beginner with Trading Card Games) but only an effort to help pushing this game out of Early Access by giving Developers a thumbs up and recommending PANOPTES to new potential gamers.

I'm having some difficulties with "Hard" quests, not only because they are meant to be difficult but also because of luck; yes, when drawing cards from the deck, luck plays a very important role, like you'll die several times when, for example, you're not getting Medikits in time to heal yourself (deck has 30 cards and you can have max 2 cards of the same type, so there are only 2 Medikits max - there are some other healing cards but they are not sufficient on their own).
Solution for success is going to the Arsenal and build a new deck (or modify it) until you get the proper cards for the current quest you're playing. There are several different aspects to take into account while fighting, starting from the type of cards you're going to use, effects and conditions that are applied to them, type of damage they inflict and monsters you're dealing with, because each and every one of them is pretty much unique.

So, I'm having quite a lot of fun playing PANOPTES and feel like recommending it with flying colors.

Early Access Review
Posted: 16 March
"A brand-new addition to the Collectible Card Game genre that showcases a wave-focused game play instead of the AI-focused standard"


Game Overview

This game features a wave-focused, strategic PvE aspect that is rare in Collectible/Trading Card Games nowadays.

You have three (3) classes (Hunter, Occultist, and Scientist) to pick on which makes up the variation for deck building. Each of these classes has their own specialty and traits but you are limited to have only thirty (30) cards per deck. These variations range from "the amount of health, energy, and maximum cards in hand" at the start of each stage up to "the cards available for their use".

There are "class-specific" and also "common cards" which gives you the freedom to make a more flexible and versatile adjustment that matches your play style.
The cards also have other sub-categories that they belong to, so you better be aware of them to avoid confusion. The game proceeds with a quest-style campaign which gives of points after you clear the stage, which you can use to buy booster packs.


The Pros

  • Well-Updated and Balanced - With monthly expansive and balancing updates to the game, you can feel that the developers are really committed and passionate in working towards their game to become better and better. There was a very hard stage (and by this, I mean you will have difficulty clearing the stage even if you have epic-legendary cards) at a time before "Update Foxtrot" came. It's not that making a stage easier is good since higher difficulty gives more replay value, but there has to be a certain limit to it and the update was a good adjustment for it.

  • Beautiful Interface and Graphics - A user-friendly interface and an artistic display of collectible cards are one of the factors that contributes to the appeal of the game as a whole.

  • High Replay Value - The right balance of difficulty in the game, the point reward system, and its feature to collect cards of various rarity (common, uncommon, rare, epic, and legendary) are the factors that contribute to this game's replay value.

  • Low Random Factor - Though the RNG (Random Number Generator) system in card games is sometimes what makes them interesting and fun to play, I find it appealing that this game and the developers decided to have a low priority on this aspect to make it more strategically immersive. It would be more satisfying that you clear a stage using a strategy rather than just relying with luck, wouldn't it?

  • Steam Cloud - A game with a Steam cloud feature is almost always a plus. Especially in this case, you won't have to worry on carrying over save files in case you are using a different computer to transfer over your progress.


The Cons

  • Deck Variations - Currently, the game has three classes which mainly differentiates each other depending on the type of damage they produce but isn't limited to them. While it is more than sufficient in the game's current status,it might feel somewhat limited in terms of deck versatility in the long run.

  • Repetitive Audio - Good soundtracks are always a big plus for any game, but unfortunately this isn't the focused attribute for this one. Yes, there is a good soundtrack in the game, but hearing it repetitively will make you fed-up at some point (or even make you annoyed enough to disable it).

  • Single Player - Not that this is a true downside of the game, but more of a recommendation in the future. A multi-player game play is always welcome when it comes to card games, however it might be hard to grasp on how this will play out since this game is focused with PvE.

The Verdict

Frankly, this game is a diamond in the rough. While it has it's own flaws, the immersiveness of the game play and its beautiful interface covers for it and gives more.

If your question is whether it is worth to buy the game now even if it is still on early access, my answer is "Yes, it is worth it".

Early Access Review
Posted: 15 March
Panoptes is a truly engaging game. It's classified as a horror Trading Card Game (TCG) and takes a Player vs Environment (PvE) approach as opposed to the more common Player vs Player (PvP) approach usually seen in this type of game.

Graphically, the game is unexpectedly beautiful. The artwork is unique and has a dark urban fantasy vibe to it. The soundtrack actually enhances this mood and creates a suspenseful experience.

The story itself is also engaging, and that's very unique to this style of game where storyline is generally minimal. The controls are very easy to master, but keep in mind that this is a game that doesn't offer controller support. It's strictly mouse and keyboard functionality.

Gameplay is also fully functional and easy to learn, even if you've never played a game of this type before. The premise for this game is that you're an agent of an organization called "The Club," and your purpose is to vanquish mythological and supernatural creatures.

Game objectives are met through the drawing and playing of cards, and deckbuilding is central to the game, as is typical with this genre. There's no mana, but instead "energy" comes into play, and it is spent and regained through the course of gameplay.

The game has more than 50 different missions and three character classes: Scientist, Occultist, and Hunter. Each class will have cards that are specific to the class while other cards are universal. Points are awarded for completing missions successfully, and those points are then used to obtain additional cards.

The ability to customize your deck and playthrough multiple times with different character classes means that this game has a great deal of replayability.

There are just two Steam achievements at this point and no Steam cards, but this game is in the Early Access stage, so it's entirely possible that we will see expansions to game content and extras in the near future.

I didn't experience any game crashes or game-breaking bugs, and overall the game feels and functions like a fully finished product. I'm actually very amazed at the overall package and excited to see what the developers will consider to be the finished version.

I recommend this game very highly to any fan of TCG and CCG games, as well as those who appreciate a darker gaming atmosphere.

Early Access Review
Posted: 20 January
PANOPTES:
~~~~~~~~~

You are part of an organisation which is known as “The Club”; an organisation which operates totally off-the-books. Better that way, as you will fight against Mythical Monsters, Aliens and more.

PANOPTES is a TCG driven story where you have to engage in combat against enemies using a deck of cards. In this early access, you will be playing 3 types of characters: Hunters, Scientists and Occultists. Each of these types have they own abilities and basic deck of cards. The basic deck of cards (30 cards per deck) for the Hunters will have more common weapons, cards and tools. The Occultists, in comparison, will have more magic cards and the scientists will have a balance of the two.

Combat-wise, it is simple to understand how it works. You have a deck of 30 cards at your disposal. You can draw or see a maximum of 5 cards at any given time. You might fight 1 wave of enemies (it could be 1 enemy in one wave) or several with a single deck of cards. Can be tricky at times. Each card will use a specific number of energy points to use against your foe. Remember that each time you draw a card from the deck, it will cost you 1 energy point and at the start of each turn you will have 5 energy points at your disposition. Cards like the Adrenaline Injection will give you an extra energy point but only for a minimum of turns. If you win the battle, you will be given a couple of new cards and reward points which you can use to purchase a booster pack at the store to help you to unlock new cards (you might not gain any new cards). Booster packs are not cheap; you will need 600 points to purchase a booster with 5 extra cards in it, and 7900 points for a booster which includes 75 cards in this current version.

The graphics component for this genre of game is very good and the audio creates very suspenseful atmosphere. I do like the information tab, you will get information about your team, missions, factions and more.

This is a solid early access as far as I am concerned and I look forward to seeing updates.

For those of you who haven't played this style of game before the aim is to build your own deck from three classes (Scientist, Occultist and Hunter) each with their own class specific cards and play-styles, to defeat waves of enemies per mission. Panoptes is a truly engaging game. It's classified as a horror Trading Card Game (TCG) and takes a Player vs Environment (PvE) approach as opposed to the more common Player vs Player (PvP) approach usually seen in this type of game.

Graphically, the game is unexpectedly beautiful. The artwork is unique and has a dark urban fantasy vibe to it. The soundtrack actually enhances this mood and creates a suspenseful experience.

The story itself is also engaging, and that's very unique to this style of game where storyline is generally minimal. The controls are very easy to master, but keep in mind that this is a game that doesn't offer controller support. It's strictly mouse and keyboard functionality.

Gameplay is also fully functional and easy to learn, even if you've never played a game of this type before. The premise for this game is that you're an agent of an organization called "The Club," and your purpose is to vanquish mythological and supernatural creatures.

Game objectives are met through the drawing and playing of cards, and deckbuilding is central to the game, as is typical with this genre. There's no mana, but instead "energy" comes into play, and it is spent and regained through the course of gameplay.

The game has more than 50 different missions and three character classes: Scientist, Occultist, and Hunter. Each class will have cards that are specific to the class while other cards are universal. Points are awarded for completing missions successfully, and those points are then used to obtain additional cards.

The ability to customize your deck and playthrough multiple times with different character classes means that this game has a great deal of replayability.

There are just two Steam achievements at this point and no Steam cards, but this game is in the Early Access stage, so it's entirely possible that we will see expansions to game content and extras in the near future.

I didn't experience any game crashes or game-breaking bugs, and overall the game feels and functions like a fully finished product. I'm actually very amazed at the overall package and excited to see what the developers will consider to be the finished version.

I recommend this game very highly to any fan of TCG and CCG games, as well as those who appreciate a darker gaming atmosphere.

Good game with plenty of content already. It's got some pretty challenging fights in it, so not for casual players.

PROS:

Good, consistant, artwork throughout. Cards are nicely illustrated, with the techno/magic world being well represented.

Decent ambient music and incidental sounds.

Three characters with three different basic playstyles. Each can be heavily tailored to deal with different threats and synergize decks for rushing, DOTs etc. Plenty of scope for customisation.

A pretty impressive amount of cards already. You start with a basic set for each character, and unlock new cards by completing missions or buying boosters with in-game currency. There are NO MICROTRANSACTIONS :D

Genuinely tough fights in places. They need a customised deck and a proper strategy to beat.


CONS:

The UI is clearly a work in progress. It's fully functional but bare bones at the moment. It's also a little "dry", without much feedback when card interactions happen.

Lack of mouse-over text. All the rules and card effects are explained in the game, via the help button. However, if you want to find out what, for example, the "indomitable" effect does on a monster card, you'll have to go through quite a few clicks to get there.

Wall of text cards. Some of the boss cards have a ton of effects and conditions on them, which can be a little hard to parse. I'd like to see these eventually condensed down to mouse-over hyperlinks, which bring up what each effect does as you hover over it.

Lack of damage/effect predictions. Some cards do things like "Damage = 3 +number of spell cards in the card graveyard", for example. At the moment, you have to manually go into the graveyard and count the spell cards. Things like this should be automated, and hopefully will be. I'd also like to see what the potential effects of a selected card will be on enemies as I hover over them.

That's it so far. I'm on mission 37 at the time of writing, and I definitely feel like I've got my money's worth so far. Looking forward to any improvements and extra content.
