About This Game

Purchasing this game on Steam comes with $5 worth of Town Points (in-game currency)

Town of Salem is a fresh innovation on the classic party games Mafia and Werewolf. It is a game of murder, accusations, deceit and mob hysteria.

How To Play

The game ranges from 7 to 15 players. These players are randomly divided into alignments - Town, Mafia, Serial Killers, Arsonists and Neutrals. If you are a Town member (the good guys) you must track down the Mafia and other villains before they kill you. The catch? You don't know who is a Town member and who is a villain.

If you are an evil role, such as a Serial Killer, you secretly murder town members in the veil of night and try to avoid getting caught.

Roles

Town of Salem has 31 unique roles ensuring a different experience each time you play.

Before a game starts, players are put into a lobby where the host can select what roles will be in the game. Players are then assigned roles at random from the list of chosen roles. Players have an in-game role card that explains their role's abilities and alignments. For an in-depth look at the abilities of each role please visit: www.blankmediagames.com/roles

Game Phases

Night

The night phase is when most roles use their abilities. For example, Serial Killers stealthily murder people, Doctors heal people who are attacked, and Sheriffs interrogate people for suspicious activity.

Day

The day phase allows the Town members to discuss who they suspect of being an evil role. Once the voting phase starts a majority vote from the town will put someone on trial.

Defense

The defense phase is when you plead your innocence to the town. Have a convincing story or find yourself facing the gallows!

Judgement

During this phase the town will vote on the fate of the defendant. Players can vote guilty, innocent or abstain. If there are more guilty votes than innocent votes the defendant is sentenced to death by hanging!

Customization

Players are able to choose their own map (town setting), character, pet, lobby icon, death animation, house and a custom name. The other players in the game will see your selections. There are currently 10 maps, 29 characters, 20 pets, 5 death animations and 20 houses to choose from.

Achievements

There are currently over 220 unique achievements in the game. Earning achievements will grant various in-game items (however the items for donation rewards are exclusive to Kickstarter).

It's an excellent game in development, but there's a but or two.

First of all, this is a free game playable on a web browser. Sadly, I realised that AFTER purchasing it. That's very unfair, that to easily play with your friends, you'd have to pay for the game.

Second of all, a role or two *cough*mayor*cough* is incredibly broken and unfair. It immediately confirms one person as a townie and any following leads and so on go through the mayor only. This reduces the game to only Investigative roles figuring who's who, to Jailor and Bodyguard protecting mayor, to Mayor guessing who's lying and to everyone else? Just standing there, doing nothing. If you're not an investigative role, never mind that, if you're not a town role, you have almost no say as to what happens and who gets lynched, you have no clue why anyone gets lynched, and all you're doing is hoping the Mayor is smart enough not to screw up, while you wait for them to say "Guilty or Innocent".

Meanwhile, Mafia and evil roles are reduced to guessing who is who randomly, rather than going by the leads the townies give out. This makes the game an absolute guesswork, and is all about finding the Jailor, Bodyguard and Investigative roles through pure luck. Town of Salem, the whole design and concept of it, is not that sort of game.


Now, so that this isn't just a mayor rant, the good things.

It's an incredibly addictive brain stimulator. If you're a manipulative person, if you're a lie detector, if you can apply logic and put 2 and 2 together easily, this game is for you. It's all about achieving your goals while deceiving your opponents, while convincing your team mates that you are, in fact, a team mate, it's about revealing leads as to who's who without revealing who you are or how you know that. I mean, assuming there isn't a mayor, of course *grumblegrumble* #NoMayorGames *grumble grumble*

The amount of fun you can have playing different roles, figuring out what's going on, and so forth, it's crazy. The dynamic between the different and numerous roles is amazing, and if you play Ranked Practice and/or Ranked games, you will really be in for a treat, as there is only one confirmed role, and everything else is just a random selection of categories.

While I'm on the topic, you should not judge the game by the "Classic" games. It's a great start point, to figure out how roles work with each other, how to trick people and so forth, but it's not all it is. I suggest keeping the wiki open until you learn what each role does, how they are detected by others, and what categories can be what roles. This will help loads.

Overall, I suggest you try this game (Remember, it's a free game on web browser), but be prepared to lose track of time, each game lasts around 30 minutes (on ranked/ranked practice) and it's very easy to go "Just one more game". There have been rumours that certain roles will be rebalanced as well, so that should help with the Mayor problem.

Story

In Town of Salem, each game is its own story.


Graphics

Very simple, since this game is a browser game. They are nothing to complain about.


Gameplay

Each game has 15 people, and each of them have roles. There are the Town and Mafia. Mafia kills people, and Town has to figure out who the Mafia is. Each role is unique, and takes time to master. No one knows who is what role, but you have to find out. There are some other unique roles that speed up the game. They are under then Neutral class. There is a day and night. During the day, everyone can talk to each other and discuss what happened or any leads. During night, players do their "jobs." No one can talk to each other at night, except for the Mafia and Vampires. In the day time, people can vote other players to get lynched. When a player dies, their role is reavealed to everyone. Mafia wins if everyone is dead, and Town wins when the Mafia is dead.


Music

Simple background music. Nothing to much.


Score

10/10 - One of the best games I have played.
