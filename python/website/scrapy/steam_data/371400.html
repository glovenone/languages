About This Game

You are Battle Class Bot X-17 and you have crash landed on Planet Downpour. Another surviving builder Class Bot B-16 seems to have built a base and activated you. An empty X-16 case seems to be the reason why. You are the last chance of escape...

Battle the never ending hordes of enemies set on overwhelming your base. Protect the antenna B-16 is building to contact home planet. The journey is unforgiving, and will require mastery of the hover shield battle system. Will be you able to manage your resources to keep yourself and your base running? Will you stay at base, and endure the slow process of building an antenna or will you risk venturing out to find antenna boxes.

More:

X-17's allocated memory for how the base runs has been corrupt. You must rediscover how it all works.

Negative levels. Reach below negative 10 and failure is inevitable.

Relentless enemies. Flying screamers that will chase you into the sky, and Defilers that will deactivate your shield.

Protect the Antenna B-16 is building. Damage will only prolong your stay on the wretched planet.

Armor that takes damage. Once armor is depleted, it will leave X-17 susceptible to 1 hit kills.

Become stronger the more enemies you destroy.

Full Day/Night Cycle.

Avoid the danger of the running giants...

Master the hover shield system to fight the enemies. Take the fight to the skies and make quick decisions with your shield. Be wary of the screamers.

A hardcore experience. How will you prioritize you resources to fight the endless enemies.
Base requires energy from generator to operate. Generator requires enemy matter harvested from enemies. The synergy of resources will allow for some tough decisions to made made. Sacrifice repairing Armor for more bullets? Or hold out a little longer in your base, the decision is yours.

tl;dr -> Open door to base, open door at opposite end, get swarmed by enemies, die. Repeat over and over, killing one more enemy each time. 11/10 hardcore survival indeed.

A friend gave this game to me and I was excited to play it because I enjoy base management and survival gameplay. I had a pretty mediocre experience, which is why I am not recommending this game.

Pros:

-very challenging gameplay; emphasis on bullet dodging
-encourages advanced techniques (ex: bunnyhopping on your shield to propel you to or away from enemies)
-interesting HUD concept (zoom in to player character to see all relevant details like health bar, resources, etc)
-no hand-holding - brief tutorial, most interactions in the game you must find out for yourself
-good amount of base management elements - every action requires some type of resource
-decent sounds and music
-not demanding on the hardware at all
-has Steam trading cards

Cons:

-brutal difficulty means you will die often while you get your bearings, potentially turning people off
-understanding what to do inside the base can sometimes be unintuitive, prompting use of more trial-and-error
-a bit lacking in aesthetics; simple designs
-better base management survival games available as alternatives
-current price - $5.49 CAD - is a bit too steep in my opinion, better to wait for it to be in a bundle deal or discounted more

Do not recommend.

Cheers,
DJSF @DJSF's Rogue Reviews

Posted: 18 May, 2015
I actually really like this game. I realize this game is for a specific type of gamer. I've only just put a bit of time in but the game is solid. Definitely an unusual artistic style, simplistic, but I think it fits the game. Play this game on fantastic, and it looks pretty great on a 1080p screen. I like how the screen kind of rotates to give a more 3d I think the art style emphasizes solid clear lines, and a lot of white, because fighting outside can be pretty hectic. Having a white/grey background really helps enemies stand out.
Game play wise is where I think this game shines. The combat is responsive, and I haven't played a game where you can hover with a shield and use it to block bullets. It is definitely unique, and the developer has nailed a good feeling with it.

The whole resource system is pretty neat, with you starting with few resources at the beginning. How well you fight the initial monsters sets the tone for how well you'll do. It can be pretty easy for the generators to lose power quickly at the beginning, and then the doors are forced open, allowing enemies to attach antenna. Combat is hard, but can be learned quickly and after a few try's I was lasting much longer.

Only gripes I have:

If you reach negative level 10, you fail and get sent to the main screen. Starting a new game is fast, but having to go through tutorial is a small drag. Thankfully you can skip pretty quikcly, but having the option to start in a new game right away would've been nice.

Some advice:
I think the game is vague on purpose on some mechanics like saving, and being able to teleport to the top of the base. But to save basically generator needs to have enough power, and when you do save it takes a lot of power so be strategic when saving. To teleport I will let you guys figure that out on your own.

Playing I reliazed how long it would take to escape just staying at base. It would take hours! Then I realized I could venture out to collect antenna boxes. Collecting one adds 5 percent to total antenna progress. Not sure what happens when reaching 100% but I can't wait!

Overall I'd highly recommend this game. It is really challenging game, and innovative how and indie should be. Don't think this is a roguelike or any other genres you've played, it's been an awesome mixture of many it seems. The price was worth it to me.

ps. Being the second reviewer or third, I really wanted to write an in depth review.
