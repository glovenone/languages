About This Game

A retro-inspired masterpiece, Mystery Castle is the one puzzle game that will rule them all!

This game is for everyone who loves adventure, treasure hunting, laughing out loud, transforming into creatures, drinking potions, possessing monsters, explosions, inept wizards, witless golems, huge floating eyeballs, magic portals, magic lanterns, grumpy dwarfs, cheerful eskimos, irate hotel owners, giant monsters, throwing bombs at giant monstersâ¦ and then laughing some more!

How about a half-witted ogre? Along the way, you'll meet a host of colorful characters, from Stumpi the treasure-hungry dwarf in the Fortress Underground to the bossy Mr Chow in the Mystical Pagoda. In one moment you'll be dodging between boiling lava and Fire Golems, and in the next sliding perilously over sheets of ice â just be sure not to fall to your doom into the bottomless abyss. You will need to transform into a troll to move heavy objects, a bat to fly across deep chasms, and a frog to squeeze through tiny holes. But how will you avoid the deadly gaze of the Eye-Spy that can turn you to stone in a blink? With all the recent big blockbusters both coming out soon and having been released in the past few years, sometimes it's nice to just sit back and relax with a simple puzzle game. Despite lacking any particular unique qualities, Mystery Castle is just plain pleasant to play, and if you're into that then perhaps this is the game for you.

The Breakdown
Mystery Castle is pretty bland and vague story-wise, but coming from a game that looks so cheery and cute I'd expect nothing more. You play as Monty, a fat wizard guy (or at least, a lot of the jokes other characters make seem to generally be about his weight for some reason) who's placed in a mysterious cave area. There's a dwarf you can talk to and a puzzle to solve. The dwarf tells you that some evil guy appeared and did some stuff and now there's bad monsters and a bunch of lava. Cool, that's super vague and unimaginative. But hey, at least the dwarf guy is fairly funny! And story-wise I guess that's all I can give this game credit for. Most of the characters you meet are somewhat humorous (though your mileage may vary) so it helps both explain the simple narrative and adds some jokes to the mix.

Gameplay-wise, every level plays out in a different sort of puzzling manner. Each level (besides the bosses) basically involves obtaining five collectible items that open the exit door when all of them are collected. It sounds deceptively simple, yet in reality the game creates various challenges and obstacles that block your progress in some pretty creative ways. For example, one level consists of a "race" of sorts between you and a golem. You have to navigate your way around lava as fast as you can before he gets to the exit or else he'll kill you if he touches you. Many of the levels seem to use the same old "push some boxes into holes" formula of solving puzzles, but even those levels are difficult and pretty creative in a fair and enjoyably challenging way. As you progress, more and more stuff is added that helps to keep things fresh. Oh, and the bosses! I can't forget those. They add some nice variety and a decent challenge to the game since you have to find out how to kill/defeat them using your puzzle-solving knowledge.

The game is set across 5 different worlds with 36 levels each, so there's a total of 180 levels in the entire game. Luckily, you only need to beat 25 levels to unlock each new world, plus you can skip any levels you get stuck on and come back later if need be. The only mechanic that I wish would have been implemented is an "undo" feature, since I constantly made mistakes yet had to go to the menu and restart the entire level, redoing things I had already done in the process. It was just a minor annoyance, but still a noticeable one.

Mystery Castle has a pretty charming aesthetic to it. Is it anything mind blowing? Well, no, honestly. However, everything looks all cute and cartoony and nothing looks particularly unpolished, so that's good at least. The only other technical aspect that I feel is worth mentioning is the inclusion of controller support, which is nice. Despite that though, most of my time playing was spent just using a keyboard since the controls were so simple (literally just the arrow keys and spacebar), so both playstyles are equally feasible and comfortable.

Pros
  • Charming art style
  • The characters are pretty humorous usually
  • Clever, challenging puzzles with varied mechanics yet simple controls
  • Definitely no lack of content - there's 180 puzzles set across 5 distinct worlds/areas
  • Steam achievements, trading cards, controller support, and cloud saves

Cons
  • Story? What story? (hint: there pretty much is none)
  • An undo feature would be nice to have
  • The game doesn't feel particularly unique, even if it is decent

Mystery Castle may not be the "masterpiece" that it claims to be, but it's still a great game for fans of the puzzle game genre. The puzzles are varied and challenging and overall the game is simply a charming experience. It may not be fantastic or especially unique, but Mystery Castle is certainly an easy recommendation, especially for anyone who craves more puzzle games.

Final Verdict: 7/10

An excellent puzzle game in the vein of Sokoban and Adventures of Lolo. The graphics are charming and the music doesn't get repetitive even after listening to it for a long while. The sound effects the main character makes upon dying do feel a bit out of place, though.

Most of the time you have everything you need in order to solve the puzzle staring right at you, and it's up to you to figure it out, which is exactly the kind of puzzle game I like. There are a few cases where you need to check what switches do, but that's still acceptable. Enemies are of the passive kind, they'll never actively chase you. Couple of times you do need some timing skills to get past them, but overall it's very much of a slow-placed, thinking man's game. It's the kind of game where you can stare at the screen for 30 minutes trying to solve it in your head before executing your plan. And then feel an equal amount of pride for doing it and shame for taking 30 minutes to figuring out just where the boxes should be pushed to.

The puzzles can get quite inventive at times, cleverly misdirecting the player and placing red herrings to sow further confusion. It took me 15 hours to play through the game, solving every puzzle without any help. There are still some castles in the map that can't be entered, so I'm hoping they'll be added in a future patch. Or even DLC, I wouldn't mind paying a few bucks for more of this.

If there's anything to criticize, it's that the puzzles' difficulty is uneven. In every castle the first 20 or so levels (out of 36) are pretty easy, then the difficulty starts ramping up. But there were levels in the third and four castles that I found much harder than anything in the fifth castle. Still, that's a minor complaint.

Definitely recommended for box-pushing aficionados.

Introduction
Mystery Castle is a relaxing puzzle game that stretches your mind and to think outside the box in order to succeed throughout each of the levels that you are put up against. There is some really cool and uniquely styled mechanics that you will find as you play through more of the game.

Gameplay
This is a relaxing puzzle game that has some rather interesting mechanics that are implemented throughout the course of the game. You play as a wizard, of all people, you are put in a tower and you have to escape, along with a friend that will help you understand the mechanics that are being put into place through the first few levels. Obviously, like most puzzle games, the first few levels are simplistic and easy to follow, just so that you can grasp the concept of the mechanics that they are throwing at you. After the first few levels you are then left on your own until a new mechanic is put into place. The game has steady difficulty curve (as long as you are playing the levels in order) and uses quite a lot of mechanics, so getting your head round what each of them do is essential to playing this game.

But what do you actually have to do in these levels to complete them? Well it is rather simple. There are 5 items that are needed in order to open the escape door to exit the level. Your job is to collect these items and get to the exit. There is no time limit, nor are you being timed to gain 'extra stars,' this just makes the game more relaxing and not rushing you into completing the levels.

There are a enormous amount of levels that you can play throughout the game. You start off in the castle, and as you complete more levels you will unlock new places with a different setting and a few new mechanics to make the puzzles that bit more difficult. Something that the game does really well is that the game won't force you to stay on a single level and try to figure it out. You can do the levels in any order that you wish to achieve them in and therefore won't be stuck on one specific level and lose interest because of that.

The game doesn't seem to be repetitive or just remakes of older levels. Each level seems to have had a fresh and new thought process instead of just remakes of previous levels. This really does make the levels that you play that more interesting and gives the game more play ability as you get into more and more levels of the game. Although something that I did find a bit annoying was there is not hotkey for a instant restart of the level, which is a bit of a shame for a puzzle game like this that has a lot of depth and gameplay in it.

A interesting new concept that I hasn't seen done before was boss battles in puzzle games. Now normally puzzle games don't venture out this far, and even if they do the bosses are only small bosses that aren't really necessary to their game just seems like they took them from nowhere and added them to the game for no reason. The bosses in this game however are put here for a reason. In order to unlock each of the bosses on each of the areas you need to complete a certain amount of levels in order to unlock it. Defeating the boss is a lot harder than the plain simple levels before that boss room. I won't spoil any more of what the boss room contains just know that the boss room is awesome and well worth unlocking.

The graphics of this game are simplistic which adds more to the innocent looking side of this game, even though this game can get pretty hard in the later stages. Some of the characters that you meet don't have any animations, they are just still and you can talk to them. I would of liked to see maybe them looking at you at a certain distance just to add a bit of life to the characters. As for the player and enemies, their animations are smooth and presented well. Although sometimes I feel like your character tht you play as hasn't got as much of a walking animation as I would've liked to see. Then again this is a puzzle game, where the main focus of the game is to create challenging and interesting puzzles

Pros
+ Nice relaxing puzzle game
+ Steady difficulty curve
+ Enormous amount of levels
+ Doesn't force you to complete each level
+ A variety of different areas to unlock
+ Interesting bosses
+ Steam achievements and Trading cards

Cons
- Could do with a hotkey to restart the level
- Would've liked to see some more 'life' in the characters you meet with their animations
- Sometimes I feel like the character hasn't enough animations in his walking

Result / Final Thoughts
The final verdict of this game is that Mystery Castle is a pretty solid and well structured puzzle game that has a lot of mechanics and requires precious timing in order to complete some of its levels. It combines boss battles into the mix of every area that you can unlock by completing more levels. It is a very relaxing and easy to understand puzzle game.

Rating: 8/10

An excellent puzzle game in the vein of Sokoban and Adventures of Lolo. The graphics are charming and the music doesn't get repetitive even after listening to it for a long while. The sound effects the main character makes upon dying do feel a bit out of place, though.

Most of the time you have everything you need in order to solve the puzzle staring right at you, and it's up to you to figure it out, which is exactly the kind of puzzle game I like. There are a few cases where you need to check what switches do, but that's still acceptable. Enemies are of the passive kind, they'll never actively chase you. Couple of times you do need some timing skills to get past them, but overall it's very much of a slow-placed, thinking man's game. It's the kind of game where you can stare at the screen for 30 minutes trying to solve it in your head before executing your plan. And then feel an equal amount of pride for doing it and shame for taking 30 minutes to figuring out just where the boxes should be pushed to.

The puzzles can get quite inventive at times, cleverly misdirecting the player and placing red herrings to sow further confusion. It took me 15 hours to play through the game, solving every puzzle without any help. There are still some castles in the map that can't be entered, so I'm hoping they'll be added in a future patch. Or even DLC, I wouldn't mind paying a few bucks for more of this.

If there's anything to criticize, it's that the puzzles' difficulty is uneven. In every castle the first 20 or so levels (out of 36) are pretty easy, then the difficulty starts ramping up. But there were levels in the third and four castles that I found much harder than anything in the fifth castle. Still, that's a minor complaint.

Definitely recommended for box-pushing aficionados.
