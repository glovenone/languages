14 June

Why we’re launching Sniper Elite 4 in Feb 2017



Dear Sniper Elite fans past, present and future!

We hope that you’re all enjoying the sights, sounds and megatonnes of E3! Of course we’re going to be in Los Angeles too with Sniper Elite 4 and Battlezone, so expect to see lots of live footage, gameplay clips and more over the next week, but we also have some more news too that we want to talk about...

Today we’re announcing that Sniper Elite 4 is launching February 14th, 2017.

When we originally revealed the game back in March, our plan was to release this year, and this was only reinforced when our pre-Alpha build was received incredibly well by those who got to see it at GDC in San Francisco.
Was it nerve-wracking showing the game for the first time, and quite a lot earlier in production than we normally do? Absolutely, but in the end we felt confident the game was where we wanted it to be. I’d go as far as saying the demo had some of the best preview feedback we’ve ever had.

So when we sat down recently to decide a launch date, we had a decision to make. Do we stick to 2016 as planned many moons ago, or do we give the team just a little more time to help them deliver beyond the promise that the game has already shown, to hone the game’s best features, or even add new ones that previously might have been left on the cutting-room floor?

It wasn’t an easy decision but for us it seems like a win-win for both Rebellion and Sniper Elite fans.

This is why we’re proud to be completely independent. Being independent means you can make changes when things are going really well, rather than just reacting when things don’t go to plan.

We know a lot of you will be disappointed that you won’t get to play Sniper Elite 4 this year, but we hope you understand our decision and enjoy all the new footage and details from our beautiful “Viaduct” level this week.

We’re also heading to some public shows in the UK and Europe in the near future, so look out for us there and make sure to say hello!

Thank you as ever, for supporting independent UK games development.

Chris and Jason Kingsley, Rebellion Co-Founders

About This Game

Observe. Plan. Execute. ADAPT.

The latest chapter in the award-winning series, SNIPER ELITE 3 takes players to the unforgiving yet exotic terrain of North Africa in a savage conflict against Germany’s infamous Afrika Korps.

Stalk your targets through the twisting canyons, lush oases and ancient cities of the Western Desert in the deadly rush to sabotage a Nazi super-weapons programme that could end Allied resistance for good.

Use stealth, planning and execution to hunt your targets – whether human or machine. From signature long distance kills, to melee takedowns, distractions and explosive traps, you are as deadly up close as you are from afar.

It must end here. You are the turning point. Because one bullet can change history...

Key Features


  • Award-winning gunplay – Experience celebrated rifle ballistics honed to perfection. Take account of distance, gravity, wind, even your heart rate for intensely satisfying third person combat.
  • Expansive new environments – Stalk huge multi-route levels with multiple primary and secondary objectives than can be tackled in any order. Never play the same way twice.
  • Real tactical choice – Adapt to any situation. Use stealth, distraction, traps and sound masking . If things go hot, use the new Relocate mechanic to slip into the shadows and start the hunt again on your own terms.
  • Revamped human X-Ray Kill cam – The acclaimed X-Ray kill-cam is back and bolder than ever, including a detailed muscle layers, 3D mesh particles and the complete human circulatory system.
  • New X-Ray vehicle takedowns – See vehicles disintegrate in intricate detail with X-Ray vehicle takedowns. Multi-stage destruction allows you to take out armoured cars, trucks and Tiger tanks piece-by-piece.
  • Tense adversarial multiplayer – Five unique modes of online competitive action. Earn Medals and Ribbons as you play. Gain XP across all game modes, customise your character, weapons and loadout. Become a true Sniper Elite!
  • Explosive co-op play – Play the entire campaign in two player online co-op, or put your teamwork to the ultimate test in two dedicated co-op modes, Overwatch and Survival.
  • Customise your experience – Veteran or Rookie, play your way. Turn off all assistance and turn up the AI, or customise the experience to your preferred playstyle. Tweak the regularity of X-Ray kill cams, or turn them off all together.

Extra features

  • Supports Steam Big Picture Mode
  • Supports Stereoscopic 3D + Ultra Widescreen + Eyefinity screens

Mmm.... okay I guess.

Sniper Elite 3 (SE3) is, like it's predecessor, a third person game where you play as a US sniper who goes around and tries to sneakily whack people who are up to no good. Specifically Germans involved in creating "wunderwaffen", or "wonder weapons" such as the V1, V2, Me-262 and other such projects that, while coming too late to make much difference in the war (Germany was ♥♥♥♥ed by then), were nevertheless cutting edge technology for the time and were the first forms of some of the technologies that we still use today (Cruise Missiles, ICBM's/Atmospheric Rockets, and Jet Aircraft respectively). But since these are war efforts being made by the enemy, your job naturally is to mess them up.

Story-wise this game really isn't worth much. The "wonder weapon" showcased in this game is so impractical and absurdly large that it sucked me out of the story and made me think "yeah, this is a crock." And when a player's reaction to the core part of your game's story is that it is nonsense, then you have a real problem. In other words, you don't play this game for the story.

No, what you play it for is shooting people, and this game's rather uncomfortable need to show me in graphic slow-motion detail of how the bullets I shoot tunnel their way into another person's head/chest/etc and messes up their insides. This existed in SE2, but it seems to happen more often this time. And while you can skip it, I suppose the first few times it happens is kind of novel, if only to know where your bullets are headed. Otherwise, the game itself functions just fine. The stealth is better done, there are more toys to play with, and all the other sorts of little improvements you should expect from a game sequel.

But... here I come to a problem. I don't hate this game. I don't really dislike it. But I don't think it's very good either. It's fairly short, lacks in story, and the "killcam" thing messes up the game's pacing a fair bit (unless you skip it). In other words, this would be a "Maybe" on steam if that was an option, but it's not. If I had to give it an arbitrary "score" I'd say it's a 7/10, or "average".

But since we live in a world where so many things are "below average", particularly in the endless tide of nonsense that finds itself on Steam, an average competent semi-stealthy shooter game kinda does deserve a "yes", if only to distinguish it from all the garbage far worse than this game that deserves to be tossed into a bin and set on fire. SE3, for all it's faults, is not a bad game. Just... don't expect me to replay it unless I get a sudden urge to watch bullets tunnel their way into people's heads. :o

Bwee...

This review is limited to the single-player campaign. There isn't much of a multiplayer community left.

I enjoyed Sniper Elite 3. The game looks great and it's fun sneaking around and sniping people. There are only 8 missions in the single-player campaign, but each map is very large and will take a while to complete. I wouldn't pay the full $50 sticker price for just 8 missions, but if you buy the game on sale it's fine. The level design is a bit uneven. Maps 1-5 are ok, 6-7 are very good, and the final map is awesome. The game also gets more fun as you go along. At first you're just dealing with rifleman, but by the final mission there are enemies wileding rocket launchers, enemies riding tanks, and enemy snipers in ghilli suits. SE3 is more limited than other war-themed shooters like Call of Duty and Battlefield. There are no vehicles to ride and every stage is set in North Africa, but with only 8 missions it doesn't overstay it's welcome.

In terms of DLC, definitely buy Saving Churchill. The Hunt for the Gray Wolf is not nearly as fun and can be skipped. Weapons packs aren't really necessary (in fact they kind of detract from the fun of earning the non-DLC weapons during the campaign, as whatever DLC weapons you buy become available immediately). But if you must, the best packs are the Axis weapons (which gives the Type 99 sniper rifle) and the Camo weapons pack (which gives a semi-automatic suppressed pistol).

8/10

Sniper Elite is a fantastic WW2 stealth shooter, where you play as OSS officer Karl Fairburne (known from the previous SE games) set on a special mission to hunt down the German general Vahlen. The game takes place on the North African front. You fight both Italian and German armed forces. As you progress trough eight missions, you gradually reveal Vahlen's plans to build a superweapon and, of course, face himself at the end of the game.

Sniper Elite is also a tactical shooter, which means that, first, you explore your surroundings, and then you plan your attack. You can play stealthy or just rush through the maps. Stealthy gameplay is recommended. Not only it's safer, but it's where this game shines the most.

You can sneak behind enemies and take down them with a knife, you can set traps, you can distract and isolate enemies to kill them in a quiet spot, you can blow a vehicle or explosives near the enemies, you can set up a chain reaction, you can snipe them both with and without sound masking, you can shoot them with a silenced pistol - the options are, indeed, many and all of them are fun.

As to the weapons, it's almost all WW2 arsenal (American, Brittish, Soviet, Polish, Japanese. German, Italian), including Panzerfaust, a special rocket launcher in the DLC mission "Belly of the Beast", dynamites, trip-mines, s-mines, hand grenades and land-mines. Not all weapons are available in the base version of the game, though. And, you'll need to unlock some weapons and weapon parts, as you progress through the campaign and ranks. The equipement consists of a sniper rifle, an assault rifle/shotgun/panzerfaust, a pistol and 5 items (explosives/anti-armor ammo or medkits).

Each mission is unique, each level is unique, it's really fun to explore the surrounding, not knowing what's behind the corner, when you play for the first time. The level design is one of the greatest that I have seen. I don't know who is the senior level designer, but, apparently, Rebellion has the right team for the job, because the level design is always excellent in the SE games.

The graphics are beautiful, as well. I did find the colours a bit too saturated, but, nevertheless, graphic quality is high and you do need a pretty solid system to run this game on highest settings. Also, the fact that North Africa is not that common setting in videogames makes SE3 to stand out. It adds a bit of extra fun.

Finally, this game has a CoOp mode and it's a "real" CoOp, you can play all campaign missions with your friends.

The other modes are survival challenges (solo or MP) and PvP. I did not try PvP, but I tried 10 waves in the Survival mode. I found it boring. Luckily, with the campaign and DLCs missions, there is enough content, so that you don't feel like you've wasted money on nothing.

The DLC packs Target Hitler: Hunt the Gray Wolf and Save Churchill are highly recommended. As to the weapon packs, see for yourself, how much money you want to spend. The default M1 Garand + Welrod are pretty much the only weapons you need. I also used: Lee Enfield, T99 (Axis Weapon Pack) and Springfield rifles (Patriot Weapon Pack). But, if you play stealthy and aim for the head, there is not much difference, which sniper rifle you use. I got all the DLC packs with the Season Pass. From secondary weapons, I also recommend: Panzerfaust and Semi-Auto HDM. MP40 comes handy, if you don't choose to play stealthy, as MP40 magazines are always available behind the enemy lines.

My overall rating: 9/10

This game is similar to the Hitman series, which is a good thing. I enjoyed stealthily killing enemies, making sure to get everyone on each mission. I didn't realize until halfway through the game that unlike Hitman, it doesn't really matter whether bodies are found, and thus stopped spending hours per mission sneakily killing people in a manner that allowed me to dispose of their corpses. On the later missions I began to experience significant bugs, such as multiple copies of the same enemy spawning in the same location. A few times this made for easy double kills, but it seemed my bullets wouldn't kill more than two stacked enemies, and I sometimes saw three. All in all, it was worth buying and playing, so I give it a 'buy-it-on-sale' thumbs up.
