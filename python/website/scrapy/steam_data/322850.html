About This Game

TAKE YOUR TEAM RIGHT TO THE TOP!

Become the sporting director of one of the pro cycling circuit's 90 teams, and compete in a thrilling 2015 season. Participate in over 200 competitions (550 stages) and the ultimate challenge: the prestigious Tour de France 2015 and its official route. Recruitment, personnel management, finances, contracts with riders and sponsors, training programs and the race calendar: leave nothing to chance. Your role will be equally important during the race, where every decision will be crucial in your bid for victory!

Embark on a season in Solo mode or organize online competitions against virtual managers around the world!

Pro Cycling Manager 2015 is packed with new features:
  • Pro Cyclist: a new game mode where you create and control a cyclist from the start of his career and turn him into an international star!
  • Career: a revised rider progress system, specific training programs, a more extensive scout management system and the opportunity to manage a reserve team!
  • More realistic and strategic sprint sequences with the option to choose your sprinter’s preferred trajectory
  • Even more meticulous graphics with richly detailed environments and a high-performance AI to make the action more challenging
  • New interface and design of the menus and in the race

* I have played this game a lot more, but not on steam.

This review only covers the new Pro Cyclist Mode.

Pro Cyclist Mode is a new game mode in Pro Cycling Manager 2015. You create your own rider, where you can choose your riders nationality, age, height and weight. You can also choose skin colour and a profile picture for your rider.
All in all you don't get a lot of customization options.

The big decision is in choosing your focus in your career; are you going to be a climber competing with Vroome and Contador or are you competing for the green jersey with Sagan.

Your starting stats are decided from what you chose as your focus, and the age of your rider.

As you start the game, you get the choice of 3 teams, usually from the country you chose. You can choose the worst team, and become the captain, or choose a higher ranking team, get access to bigger races, but having to ride for your captain.

When entering your main hub after choosing your team, you get into the familiar career menu. Only here you have less options. The only thing you can really adjust is your training schedule.

If you toughen up your training schedule, you will level up at a faster race, but of course you will suffer from more fatigue.

Leveling up increases your stats in what ever area you are trying to improve your rider in. If you are trying to win the Tour de France, you will quickly get improved Mountain, Time Trial and Restitution.

To level up, you can gain XP by training, finishing races in top positions and getting in the early breakaway.

In the races you can control your rider with 4 and 8 on the numpad to move him right and left. Or using 5 and 8 to increase or decrease his effort.

Unlike career mode, in Pro Cyclist mode you can't look at other groups other than the group, your rider is currently in, which means if you are in a breakaway, you can't see who is leading the chase in the peloton, and instead have to use other means to figure out how much of a chance you have of winning.

All in all this game mode is a great improvement to a game franchise which desperately needed a new game mode.

I have played almsot every pro cycling game for the last 8 years. I really like the 1st player career mode, only a few weeks into the season but it is a great addition to a game that over the last few years hasn't added much. Which is why typically I don't suggest buying the new game until it has been out a while. As always with a new game it will crash occastionally and I'm sure they will put out patches soon as the game as only been out 2 days now.

1st person career mode:
You determine what type of cyclist you want to be (sprinter, climber, stage races, et cetera).
Then pick your team, typically a contentional or contentional pro team.
You have a race director or tell you want he would like you do during the race (just like in old career modes you used to give to your riders). So he declares you the leader, or free element, or teammate, and gives specific instructions like don't attack, ride in the breakaway, follow counter attacks, et cetera.
You have a small number of points that you are allowed to use to adjust your race schedule during the year, which is nice when you want to get more training in.
You select how much workload for your training, which in effect hurts your freshness for when races come up, so you need to decide how many rest days you want.
Overall just a great addition, and as always if you like the career mode as a manager that has changed a little, they do R&D now on equipment, but quickly other than that I didn't see any major changes from previous version of the game.

So far I highly recommend this game if you like cycling. I will update if any major issues arise.
