About This Game

What kind of hero will you be?


In Spandex Force: Champion Rising you take control of an everyday citizen struggling to become a true superhero, with the dubious aid of the pompous Blizzard Wizard. Create your own hero, train your superpowers and battle crooks and villains in this tongue-in-cheek hex based match-3 RPG.

In you quest you will encounter supervillains, evil robots, corrupt government officials and heroes in need of anger management training - and much more. And who is the shadowy figure that Blizzard Wizard has been seen conspiring with? Solve the mystery by finding all the secrets in Vigilance Valley!

Features


  • Five acts of heroic action
  • Seven exciting minigames
  • Four different endings
  • Level up your superpowers
  • 30 villains and evildoers to defeat

One-Sentence Summary
An excellent premise and some nice ideas, artwork, and writing for the match-3/rpg-adventure mashup genre, but unfortunately the lack of balance in gameplay makes the journey extremeley tedious rather than fun.

Pros
+ Nice art direction and dialogue writing
+ Narrative arc has some intrigue
+ Interesting premise and gameplay ideas

Cons
- Poorly balanced, unfun match-3 gameplay
- Grindy and feels laborious to progress
- Gameplay is not well-explained
- Map is hard to navigate, frequent misclicks

Final Thoughts
I really like the premise here of the match-3 plus rpg/adventure/management genres all set in a satirical superhero comic book world. I also like the artwork and feel the dialogue writing and characterizations are pretty good, and the story carries with it some mystery and intrigue. It's rather a shame then that the gameplay is so uninvolving as to make the game elements feel like an annoying chore to progress the story as opposed to something fun. I don't mind grindy games if I find the grind elements somewhat stimulating -- unfortunately, the match-3 gameplay here is just kind of tedious and requires little skill or thought-process to advance.

For some examples of puzzle hybrid games that get the gameplay balance right and make it so fiendishly addictive, try these:

* 10,000,000
* HuniePop
* Legend of Fae
* Puzzle Quest
* Super Puzzle Platformer Deluxe
* Triple Town
* You Must Build A Boat

Posted: 2 September, 2015
This is not a strong "don't buy this", but the game really is a bit of a technical foul-up. It's a hex-grid focused Puzzle Quest knockoff, but it's got a lot of problems:

- The entire game runs on a timer of "one month". I'm pretty sure this is a loose enough limit, but the reason I bring this up is that its incredibly imprecise map screen will lead to you running through more time than you mean to on almost every action. Clicking a location makes it guide you there, but the pathfinding takes weird circuitous routes, and every second you're "in motion" runs the clock. Similar bug: if a quest/level dialogue is popping up over the map screen, it will refuse to let you into a targeted area... but it'll keep running the clock.
- It's really, really sluggish. A lot of the match-3 genre runs on "make move, watch board quickly refill" - you're going to be waiting as things slowly work their way down hex by hex on your larger combos, and in a few modes, this is made worse because guess what: the match timer keeps running, punishing you for better play.
- The AI cheats. Not in a "oh, it knows what's coming and makes combos you can't" kind of way like some people claimed with Puzzle Quest, I mean in a literal "it creates connections you cannot make on the board playing by the rules" fashion. I don't know if this is a bug or just designed to keep you from curb-stomping it, because it is dumb, dumb AI.
- There are ten stats in the game and they are, as far as I can tell, explained nowhere. A few are easy to puzzle out (three of them equate to the three "elements" of battle, and levelling those will give you skill points), others eventually become clear (reputation), but the rest I can only take a flying guess at their purpose because they give absolutely no visible effect on gameplay. One might play into an ending (ethics? can I go evil?), but the rest? No idea. It also doesn't help that random tasks out in the world (some of the jobs) will lower certain stats, and you can't find this out until after completing them.
- Hope you like grinding, because this game likes to toss a lot of "go do some crap for a while, I'm busy, kid" quests at you. At least once I managed to fulfill the "goal" of a quest during these grinds, but the game refused to allow me to go on with it because I had not finished the "pay $500" portion that came before the quest's actual goal. (It was "raise your reputation", before someone claims this is impossible. Go ahead. Try it sometime.)
- Lazy touchscreen port. Some of the minigames require you to click and hold your token on the screen to move it around. Not a dealbreaker, but also not a plus.

I can't gripe too hard, I'm still playing it and it's functional, it's just a very low-effort, underwhelming title. I wish this genre didn't basically drop off a damn cliff after Puzzle Quest.

edit after beating the game: Less fond of it because it's one of those games that gives you a perfectly beatable 'invincible' boss and counts your win as a fail state. Time Master's plenty killable if you have a lot of freeze powers.

TLDR; Spandex Force is a fun casual match 3 game at the right price, but be warned its main story does have a time limit.

Spandex Force is a match 3 game which you play a superhero in training, and you gain power and attack through a match 3 game type system. Its core gameplay is pretty interesting at times, but overall it doesn't require alot to play a few matches, and you can go off and do your own thing. The story is generic, but it has a few side minigames that provide some variety. While progression is slow (painfully sometimes if you are just trying to rush through) it has enough bells and whistles to sidetrack you from feeling like you are just grinding one scenario. Overall a fun game, but the main mission does have a 30 day in game time limit.

Pro's:
Match 3 with an upgrade system to vary how you play
Fun gameplay (if you like Match 3's)
Never really over/underpowered in the main storyline
Varied minigames/sidegames that break the monotany from time to time
Casual match 3 fun

Con's:
Upgrade system could use some tweaking
Can become really grindy in some aspects
The rock paper scissors aspect of your attacks never really come that far into play
Attacking enemies is slow, takes like 5 seconds each attack and it takes on average 6 attacks to kill a guy

Buy it if you are looking for a under $5 game to pass a few hours, it will give you that at a minimum.

*Final note: Dev seems to be pretty responsive on the forums, which is always really appreciated.
