Ricochet is perhaps the Game i've ever played. The first time I started this game, I was blown right away by the Main Menu art and the background score and I knew it that it would keep me hooked until the end, and it did.

This Game is not your regular game as it's quite challenging, perfect for me but some casual players may find it difficult to play because this game severely punishes you for your mistakes. Initially it took me a while to get used to the controls because you cannot re-map them, it's not a big deal but it would be great if we could.

The Visuals are breathtaking as you will just stop by and gape at the gorgeous art for minutes. The Audio department did a great job in making the high quality sounds, even minute sounds can be heard clearly. You may get frustrated in certain parts but it's the beautiful sountracks that encourages and keeps you going, and thats an important aspect of a game. They are so beautiful that you just wanna die in peace listening to them. The Dialogues are mystically unique, they don't actually make any sense but they sound so cool. The letdown is that they reused most of the same dialogues in some scenes.

Coming to the story, its very emotional and may make you cry easy peasy. Its not complex as The Walking Dead or something but its a Heart touching story. I hate to admit this but tears came to my eyes at the ending, and I'm sure that I'm not the only one.

So yeah, every aspect of the game is damn-near perfect. Do yourself a favor and get this Masterpiece, it's worth every penny.


TL;DR:

Pros:
Fluid Gameplay
Heart touching Story
Breathtaking Visuals
Cool Dialogues
Beautiful level design
Animation variety
Amazing Soundtracks by Gareth Coker
Cons:
Reused Dialogues
Cannot remap controls
Maybe too difficult for some?
Can't return to some areas
Glitchy Leaderboard
Bash Skill control using Keyboard

Simply put, it's a Masterpiece

10/10

I actually enjoyed Ricochet. Don't listen to all of the hate it gets. Now, I wouldn't recommend spending Â£4 on the game alone, but if you buy the CS Anthology or Valve Complete Pack, then give it a try. Sure, you'll find very few people. Sure, the gameplay is hard to get used to. But damn, does it feel good to soar through the air, decapitate a man and carry on. Now, so far I've only played one map, and only against bots, but I'm sure I'm going to play some more. Here are some things I liked and disliked -


PROs -
  • Variety of powerups, each spawning randomly every time.
  • Two ways to kill: hitting off the edge, or decapitating.
  • An interesting way of managing how many shots you shoot at once.
  • No fall damage! Use those jump pads as much as you like!

CONs -
  • You cannot shoot up or down. This leaves out any potential of getting an awesome kill after falling.
  • Very few people who actually play. Yeah, this isn't the game's fault, or Volvo's fault. It's the same with most multiplayer games that aren't relatively new, such as HL:DM or DoD.
  • Few official maps to play.
  • NO official Valve servers.
  • When bodyslamming a player in midair, not much happens, and it tends to be kinda glitchy.


    I could probably list more PROs and CONs for Ricochet if there was a need/if I played more, however I don't feel a need at this time. I'd give it a 7.3/10. Don't bother buying it individually, but if you get it in a pack, give it a try. It's a piece of Valve's growing history, even if not one of their best moments.
