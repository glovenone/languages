Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The game is in Early Access because we need more time to finish with more game-play content and, our followers are really wanting to play themselves for about a year ago.
So for this reason we are releasing in Early Access and, we don't want people buying an incomplete game thinking it's complete.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We were planning for a 60 days Early Access, but we are having a lot of work (we are listening to a lot of suggestions and feedbacks) and it will be delayed a little.
Won't give a exact date but we hope to have it ready in October.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We added more maps and new objectives, but we are planning a new class and alternative maps for the objectives we already made.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current state if fully playable, there are five functional maps and they works with bots.
There are no official servers, but it's very easy to create your own session and play with friends.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price will raise a bit, we felt the pain of taxations and we can't really afford the actual price.
After all we had much more work after the early access release than before.

We will also make a sales when early access ends so people can buy it at the price it was.

And thanks a lot for all those who are supporting us in our early access phase.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We hope the community will help us with bug reports and with some ideas to make the game better.”

About This Game

When a kingdom and an empire collide, the best warriors are called to battle. Join the mighty Kingdom or the glorious Empire and conquer your enemies.
A team-based, 3rd person action shooter with cartoon styled, fantasy themed backgrounds. 3 classes to choose from, each with its unique set of skills. Take over fortresses, take over kingdoms, take over everything.
Work with the team and win; work alone and perish.

Play against players or against a vicious A.I.!!

Play with:

  • Archer : Shoots arrows, set traps, shoot even more arrows at your enemies!
  • Warrior : Charge at enemies, block attacks and slice everyone with great sword skills!
  • Mage : Help your team mates with healing, magic barriers and shoot exploding magic missile!
Capture the Castle : Each Team have to invade and capture the enemie's stronghold!
Domination : Capture the most objective points to damage and defeat the enemy team!
King of the Hill : Capture and hold the flag pole in the middle of the map!

Early Access Review
Posted: 2 July, 2015
I really want people to play this game! It's a cute idea that seems like a blast to play with friends. However, the game needs more people playing it. If you're on the fence about buying it, here is what you should know:

Pros:
-Cheap! Game is a steal honestly. Its not at its peak value yet because of the small playerbase, but it's a tiny investment.
-Simple concept. Gets the job done with streamlined gameplay that really gets to the core of class based "shooter." Doesn't really fit that title much, but its more akin to TF2 than other games.
-Quick rounds! Doesn't take long to play honestly which is great! Haven't played with people yet but should be a blast with friends!

Cons:
-Game simply doesn't look all that nice. This helps gameplay by simplifying interactions with very little clutter and helps keep the game running smoothly on any machine, but be warned, it doesn't look any better playing than the screenshots do.
-Poorly optimized. Even though it plays well on any machine, the game is more sluggish than a well optimized AAA game or even most indie games. Early access does mean some of this is expected.
-No players. This might bug potential buyers more than it should because the AI isn't exactly great, but buying this game helps in this regard!

Add me if you want to play, this game is cute and cheap and frankly I think its a good example of what an amateur early access title should look like.

Early Access Review
Posted: 28 July, 2015
This review is VERY VERY early. But Warriors and Castles shows promise. The developers are dedicated to it and are actively working on it. The price is very cheap, and this makes it a great candidate for a party game with friends. The system requirements are modest (though it does need some optimization.) The idea is somewhat like Chivalry Medieval Warfare, though it is much simpler. There are currently three classes, Warrior, Archer, and Wizard. Their functions are relatively self explanatory, and the type of game I've played is a battle in which there are two castles, you must breach the gates of your enemy castle. The dev's are working on setting up dedicated servers, and the game does contain a decent enough AI which does allow the AI to be able to complete any objectives. The Wizard seems a bit underpowered as his abilities are defensive. He can fire a magic missile, which I think does more damage than an arrow. He can heal himself and I am pretty sure heal allies, but Warriors have a spinning attack which can only be countered but a warrior holding his shield up. This spinning attack as a large radious and can wipe out a cluster of enemies pretty quickly. The game is in very early stages of development, it is no where near it's final product. But since the developers are very active and EXCEPTIONALLY responsive to suggestions and criticism, this game is definitely worth supporting.
