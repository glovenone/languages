About This Game

Astro Lords: Oort Cloud is a free-to-play, science-fiction MMORTS. The game seamlessly combines construction management with click-and-drag Artillery style combat and strategy elements such as exploration, raiding and espionage. The game takes full advantage of stunning Unity 3D graphics to provide a visually rich experience.

You will take on the role of an Astro Lord, leader of your own asteroids in the Oort Cloud on the fringes of the solar system. As well as vying against other players for dominance in PVP combat, you must also face off against a mysterious alien race which has invaded the Cloud.

Build!

Asteroids are at the heart of your Empire. You begin the game with a home asteroid which you must develop through the construction of facilities such as mines, power plants, factories and laboratories. Resources must be gathered, or stolen, to construct ammunition and cyborgs to fuel your expansion. Secondary asteroids can be added through colonising empty ones, or capturing developed asteroids from your enemies.

Astro Lords: Oort Cloud is the first MMORTS to feature moving bases on a spherical map, putting you in the driving seat. Will you manoeuvre your asteroid in pursuit of your enemies, flee from hostile foes, or explore vast nebulae and alien ruins?

Shoot!

Leading the charge against your enemies are the Captains. You can recruit up to 6 Captains from 7 different classes. Will you recruit a Warrior and focus your efforts on combat? A Scientist, to boost your research output? A Spy, to keep tabs on other Astro Lords and alien bases? With over 50 different skills to develop, you can tailor your Captains to suit your gameplay style. Each Captain can be fully controlled in battle against other players and AI alien enemies, and will fight to defend your base while you're offline too.

Captains and starbases can be improved through skills, runes, artifacts and officers, giving you complete control over your combat style and abilities. The click-and-drag Artillery style combat in Astro Lords: Oort Cloud provides fast-paced real-time combat – think Worms, but without having to wait your turn! Players can choose between a number of different battle modes including Team Deathmatches, Duels, Boss Fights and Alien Challenges.

Raid!

To maintain your power in the Oort Cloud, you'll need to have strong friends and rich enemies. Forge alliances with other players and help defend each other’s asteroids, and send your spies to infiltrate and sabotage enemy bases. Lead daring raids against other players to steal their resources for yourself. Deploy Explorer cyborgs into the Cloud to discover ancient technology and mysterious artifacts. In the Oort Cloud, it's up to you whether you want to be the hero or the villain...

About the Game

Astro Lords: Oort Cloud was inspired by classic games such as Master of Orion, as well as modern MMOs like OGame and Dark Orbit. As an MMO in constant development, Astro Lords: Oort Cloud receives free regular updates containing new game features and improvements.

Astro Lords: Oort Cloud is an interesting spin on the F2P base building genre, don't let the playtime fool you as i'm a few days into the game at the time of review and i'm enjoying it.

First off i'll start with some of the bad things about this game:

- Starting off the game can be quite slow at least if you research some of the advanced technologies too early.

- The game doesn't seem to want to register that i'm playing it through Steam, when i launch the game it's all good but when the game actually opens i go back to being online. The devs have fixed this by forcing the game to launch with a generic Unity launcher, the in-game Steam UI doesn't work though.

- Research relies too much on multi-leveled technologies like Stronghold Kingdoms but these are mostly for upgrading structures to or past a certain level aswell as a few other things.

- Hovering your mouse over buttons doesn't show their name which can make interface a little confusing.


Now for the good things:

+ Progression is slow but rewarding.

+ The base mechanics are interesting.

+ The arena mode is surprisingly deep and fun.

+ Most of the time it's fairly clear where you need to go and what you need to do.

+ In-game quests give you goals if you haven't set a few yourself.



Starting off in Astro Lord is quite slow but there is a tutorial to get the hang of things, it teaches you how to build structures, research new technologies and the basics of the arena mode but the rest you're left to figure out for yourself and there are quests which help give you suggestions of what you should do next.

The game is split into two parts, the base building and a top-down arena. The base building isn't much different to most other games, order structures to be built/upgraded, wait untill they're done, rince and repeat. Some structures produce resources like the crystal extractors and cyborg factories while others produce special consumables for use in the arena like the laser factory or the rocket factory aswell as a few structures that increases the amount of storage you have or speed of certain materials. The arena on the other hand is quite interesting, you choose a captain you want to use in an arena, these can range from lasers to missiles and even drones in addition to their basic abilities, these include a move, shield regeneration and attack. When you complete an arena you gain experience based on how much damage you did to the enemy side, this mode can be played against the AI or other players, if there are less than 3 players on a team the empty slots are filled with bots. The arena mode is also how the attack missions that involve your captain play out, this can include AI or "alien" asteriods or other player or "astro lord" asteroids.

In addition to building your base once you unlock a certain technology you can fly your base around in an open world on a hex-based grid, you do this by either setting a speed and choosing a direction and you're shown where you'll go or by autopilot and putting in the cooridinates and the game will do it for you. Be warned, moving around costs energy and crystals.

There are a few resource currencies in Astro Lords, the three you'll be using the most are crystals, microchips and credits aswell as two special currencies, the premium Deuterium and the in-game earned Tritium. Tritium can be converted to Deuterium on a 1-to-1 ratio but be warned, it can't be converted back. You also have a power resource which is used by pretty much everything, from maintaining, building and upgrading structures to producing resources like Microchips and arena consumables.
