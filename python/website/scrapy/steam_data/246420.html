About This Game

Get ready for an epic journey to defend your kingdom against hordes of orcs, trolls, evil wizards and other nasty fiends using a vast arsenal of towers and spells at your command!

Fight on forests, mountains and wastelands, customizing your defensive strategy with different tower upgrades and specializations! Rain fire upon your enemies, summon reinforcements, command your troops, recruit elven warriors and face legendary monsters on a quest to save the Kingdom from the forces of darkness!

Key Features


  • Epic defense battles that will hook you for hours!
  • Command your soldiers and see them engage in hand to hand cartoon battles!
  • Full screen High definition awesome illustrated cartoon art!
  • 8 specialized tower upgrades to customize your strategy! Mighty Barbarians, Arcane Wizards, Forest Rangers to name a few.
  • Over 18 tower abilities! Have your Sorcerers summon earth elementals and polymorph your enemies!
  • Over 45 different enemies, from Goblins to Demons each with their own skills! (Beware of the mountain Trolls!)
  • 7 Epic Boss Fights! Face the unstoppable Juggernaut, the unbeatable Troll Warlord and the humongous JT!
  • 12 Legendary Heroes! (All unlocked with no additional cost!) Bring them to battle to face the hordes of evil!
  • The classic 12 stage core campaign: Defend the Kingdom from the forces of Vez'nan!
  • The Winter Storm 2 stage mini-campaign.
  • The Rise of the Bandits 2 stage mini-campaign.
  • The Curse of Castle Blackburn 4 stage campaign.
  • 4 Bonus stages: The Ruins of Acaroth, The Rotten Forest, The Lair of Sarelgaz and The Fungal Forest!
  • Hours of gameplay with over 50 achievements
  • Extra game modes that will challenge your tactical skills to the limit.
  • In-game encyclopedia with information from your towers and enemies.

I praise the developers, it's THE Tower Defence game.

With over 78 levels to complete and master, Kingdom Rush remains a solid tower defense strategy game that is offered at a more than fair price. Challenge your ability to think and plan ahead whilst also maintaining a degree of entertainment and fun.

---------------------------------------------------------

I started off playing Kingdom Rush as a casual joke: as you can probably tell, it quickly evolved beyond that. With a little over 43 hours under my belt, you can be certain that you're receiving the most bang for your buck.

Gameplay
Strategise and defend you way through a series of waves; protect your kingdom from armies of enemies whom are hellbent on dismanting all your work. Test your skills by overcoming the casual, iron, and heroic challenges: show your skills by becoming the best Kingdom Defender that you can be.

---------------------------------------------------------

Pros
+ Numerous amounts of heroes that you can utilise to defend your kingdom
+ Vast customizability of your defense system: ranges from different towers to useful skill trees and boosts
+ Achievements are a fun challenge to get and really enhance gameplay as a result; they challenge you to plan in ways you never though possible
+ Offers various levels of challenges: casual, iron, and heroic challenges each differ in their level of skill requirements, obviously randing from very easy to difficult. This does not, however, impede on a gamer's overall enjoyment of the game
+ As with most tower defense games, the graphics are cartoony and cute. What makes the sprites from Kingdom Rush stand out though? I can't quite pinpoint it, but there's something about the animated trolls, orcs, demons, and werewolves that just seem unique

Neutral
± Replayability value is high, however it is repetitive. It is good for those who enjoy a familiar gameplay at the end of the night, but not so great for those whom prefer variation

Cons
- In my opinion, it would be nice to have an online endless mode where you could compete with other Kingdom Defenders
- Honestly, I have no real complaint besides the above and teh fact that there are a few bugs and glitches; they only happen very rarely so it almost seems insignificant to mention. Let's just say... don't overload the arena with many "moves"

---------------------------------------------------------

If you're looking for a decent tower defense game that has withstood some platform upgrades successfully (phone to computer), then this game is a great contender and worthy of a spot in your Steam library. Give it a try and you will quickly find out why this game has an overwhelming fan base and an "overwhelmingly postive" rating. It truly deserves it.

Overall
9.5/10

Most Tower Defenses (and a lot of other genres, really) can be easily slotted into one of two categories: Sci-Fi and Fantasy. Defense Grid was probably the definitive example of the former, while Kingdom Rush is one for the latter. The evil overlord Vez'nan has summoned a horde of evil to conquer the land, ranging from simple goblins and bandits to the legions of hell. You're a nameless commander that has to take charge of the kingdom's forces to fight them all off.

It's a bit simplified compared to Defense Grid, especially at first. Enemies are trying to get to the end of the map, and travel on paths you can't alter. You also only have four main towers: Archers which shoot quickly for low damage and are good against enemies resistant to magic, Mages which shot slower for larger damage and can pierce armor, Barracks that release units who block and attack ground enemies, and Bombarders which deal area damage. As you go through the game though, you unlock two unique upgrades to each you can select after their third level. Archers for example, can become Rangers, which shoot even quicker and can be upgraded to poison and paralyze enemies, or Musketeers, which trade speed for better damage, minor area damage, and a chance to kill any enemy in one shot. A lot of combinations of them are quite useful.

The game gives you plenty of chances to see that, too, as in addition to the usual three stars for doing them perfect each level has a Heroic and Iron mode, which reward one each. Heroic restricts you to a certain level of the general upgrades (which you get by paying stars) and typically disallows the Hero units, while giving you only one life for the whole stage. Iron takes it a step further by restricting your towers, and having enemies come in one continuous wave. These range from simple, to tricky, to quite difficult.

I should note that this is one game you can technically get for free on Kongregate, or on mobile. The Steam port just contains everything that's Premium Content in those versions, and apparently at a lower cost; this includes a number of stages unlocked after the main campaign, and a few hero units you wouldn't get ordinarily. I got it for two-fifty a few Steam Sales back, which seems more than fair to me (plus there's also a current bundle where you can get it for barely more), but you could use those free versions to see if it's the kind of game you'd throw money at to own. It doesn't have quite as much content as Defense Grid, but there's still a lot of fun to be had.
