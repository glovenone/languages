Product received for free
I was gifted this as a joke, and can't believe I'm doing this, but I'm recommending it on the basis of it being, well...kinda fun.

It's a casual, on-rails real time strategy (RTS) game. You assign workers to tasks by clicking their destination, but they can only move along set paths and have to clear obstacles to get to additional resources, building sites, and end-goals in each level's self-contained one-screen map.

The Greek-mythology-lite story is pretty dumb, and clearing the levels is hilariously and mock-ably easy. But like many casual games, trying to earn 3 stars on each level raises the stakes to a fun level. Getting the 3rd star requires beating the level before the timer reaches the "Gold Time" mark, which turns each level into a trail-and-error efficiency puzzle.

For example, on one level I attempted 4-5 times to get the 3rd star, there were 5 branching paths from a central path to the end goal with 2 farms (which produce food) and 2 worksites (which produce wood) near the starting point. First attempts focused on resource gathering and left obstacle-removal until later, and failed. Focusing on clearing obstacles ran up against resource shortages. Finally I figured out a perfect balance of selective resource gathering with one worker while clearing just the right paths with the other to clear all the end goals just before the timer dinged. Pretty satisfying!

If you go into this with appropriate expectations - a pretty goofy casual game with some light but entertaining strategic challenges - you could have a good time. If you enjoy playing RTS games solo but can't handle the constant pressure of scrolling all over the place, this might also be a good fit.

For a harder efficiency-focused strategy game, definitely try the harder but less clicky and goofy "Spacechem," or the turn-based "Desktop Dungeons."

Short version: 83%
The sequel of 12 Labours of Hercules brings more levels, increased challenge, and additional game mechanics to this enjoyable time management game. Sadly, it still couldnât afford to have some form of story.

Long version:
12 Labours of Hercules II: The Cretan Bull is the second instalment in a fairly successful line of time management games. Just like its predecessor, this one has no actual story or any real connection to the mythical jobs of Heracles, the title is just an excuse for the pleasant visual theme.

The core game mechanics are the same as before: you have a set of servants who are used to gather resources in order to remove road blocks to the next level. There are more instances where you need to call upon the help of Hercules himself or one of his mythological friends, but the bulk of the work still falls on the never-tiring servants.

Since a sequel needs some new gameplay elements, there are also a number of changes. Storage space is unlimited, but farms have to be harvested manually, before thieves steal the resources. You now start with at least two servants and most levels offer some form of achievable bonus to boost their efficiency; but you need to be a lot more careful and faster to clear the increased amount of obstacles to reach the bonus time limits.

The result is a sequel that is a little easier for casual players but a lot harder for those who want to get every award, which is probably the best outcome both sides could hope for.
