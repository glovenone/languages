Grey Acumen
( 1.0 hrs on record )
Posted: 29 March
I saw the negative rating on this VR software, but decided to try it anyway, since most of those negative reviews were about how much they didn't like one video or another, or how the general video lineup was weak. Ignore them, not because they're wrong, but because that's all subjective. What videos will interest people varies based on the individual.
No, the reason to avoid this program is twofold; because the experience itself is flawed, and the program doesn't run well.

Flawed experience; NO HEADTRACKING - 360 videos are a great concept, and live action stereoscopic 360 videos really give a great sensation that you're standing right in the middle of the action, at least until you move your head. When you're watching a 3d movie on a screen,(3ds, 3d glasses, etc) it provides an effect almost like there's a hollow box behind your screen that houses a stage for action to take place on. There's no sense of personal immersion, so there's no sim-sickness. When you're in a game, the entire experience is being crafted live around you, and the camera can be controlled by you and its motions can completley match up to both turning your head, as well as shifting the position of your head.
Live action stereoscopic 360 videos are all filmed beforehand and the entire experience is set in a fixed viewing angle. There's simply no way around that. Because of this, TURNING your head is perfectly fine, but every single tiny little motion your head makes horizontally or vertically will result in the video following you, even though your inner ears are expecting your viewing angle in the video to change accordingly. It is one of the worst instigators of sim-sickness. Unless you keep your head perfectly fixed, then you are in for a world of nausea, even within just a small 5 minute timespan.

Program Issues; Laggy headtracking and Annoying video buffering - If the head tracking issues inherent to stereoscopic 360 videos weren't bad enough, the program itself has a tendency towards juttery movement even when you're just turning your head to look around. When the entire purpose, and pretty much only feature of your program is to let you turn your head around to see all of a video, then you should make sure that feature works flawlessly. This was only a minor nuisance compared to the video buffering, which consistently let me see the first 15-30 seconds of a video, then needed to take 3-5 minutes of buffering to finish loading the rest of the video, even when there was only 1-2 minutes worth of video to load after that. If your background streaming can't keep up with the regular video playing speed, make sure it preloads BEFORE the video starts, not after.

Also yes, the current video lineup ranges from tepid, to horrible. Right now it's all free, but a comprehensive rating/review system and video previews will need to be implemented if this is to have any hopes of warranting any form of in-app purchasing, which is what it seems to be pushing towards.
