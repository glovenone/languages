Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“I'm well in to the development of Dimensional, but I need feedback to ensure the later levels are fun and well balanced in terms of difficulty. I want to engage with the community to explain what I'm trying to achieve with this highly varied and experimental game and get your feedback and ideas for further development.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“About 4 months”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version will continue to expand on the number of levels and the range of experiences. It's already a very varied game, but I'm keen to continue experimenting to see what works best in VR, so the variety will continue to grow.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Still expect some bugs, but the game should already offer a fairly solid and enjoyable experience. The first 5 levels are included + a bonus level in the credits. I hope you will want to play through multiple times just for the experience.

The narrative is already finished. It's up to you if you want to seek out the hidden messages and figure out the full story.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“I'm intending to increase the price as the amount of content grows, but I will also be looking for advice from the community.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I will be asking the community for both feedback and ideas as I progress. Dimensional is an experimental game where I am trying to tackle challenges specific to VR and make players feel truly immersed. I'm keen to hear from people who've played the game and want to make suggestions, e.g. "Wouldn't it be cool if you were trapped in a shrinking room and had to smash your way out before you were crushed"”

6 July

Patch 0.2.0.7

Some important fixes in this patch include:

Positional teleporting - missing GO icon
Sometimes when you were asked to move over the GO icon during a positional teleport, the icon would be missing. I couldn't reproduce this but believe it was due to an incorrect startup sequence. It shouldn't happen now, but if it does: 1) please tell me. 2) the reset button on your controller will allow you to return to the last check point.

Teleporting into walls
If quickly teleporting around a room it was possible to end up in limbo due to being in a wall when you completed the teleport. You cannot teleport within 5cm or so of a wall now, which should fix the issue.

FPS-mode stuttering
When in FPS mode it would stutter as you moved along a sloped wall. It should now move smoothly.

Other minor fixes are included too.

Thanks for reporting the issues.

Also, I wrote some notes about the difficulty of the game and my plans. Feel free to comment.

5 July

Changes to jumping

Thanks for the useful feedback on jumping.

Some changes are now live that I hope will make it more enjoyable for you.

1) If you are off the floor when the laser passes, you should clear it. Previously I used a collision detection where your feet where calculated (poorly) to be. Cheating will still fail, such as standing on a chair, because it constantly checks your height. You will probably be able to jump over them while jumping forwards now too. I've left the advice to jump straight up because that's the safest way to complete the task.

2) If you just don't want to be noisily jumping around or have a low ceiling, or a bad leg, you can now disable jumping in the new "Gameplay" menu option.

There's not going to be lots of jumping in the game, but I felt it was an interesting mechanism to try and hopefully with the two options everyone should be able to finish and enjoy it.

I would be interested to hear from some of the players who struggled to see if it feels OK now.

I'd like to keep experimenting with new ideas, situations and gameplay mechanisms during Early Access. I hope that's what you want as well. Your feedback is important to help me get it right, or throw it out.

About This Game

A first-person, narrative-driven puzzle game


Dimensional throws you into dangerous chambers to save an alien race. This is not a passive experience. To succeed, you'll have to feel and act as though you're really there. You'll be standing, moving around, manipulating objects with realistic physics and reacting to dangers.

  • Nausea-free - a very comfortable experience that won't make you feel sick
  • Real-life movement - walk, duck, dodge, lean, jump to avoid obstacles and search your environment. Only 1.2m x 50cm required to play. Full room-scale supported.
  • Puzzles - you'll need brains, reflexes and precision to beat the chambers
  • Explore - find the hidden messages and learn the truth
  • Mic support - expect to be heard as well as seen
  • Choice - teleporting for comfort + optional FPS-style controls

More than just puzzles and action, I'm trying to put you in a variety of unique situations that can only be appreciated in VR. There's lots to experience and I have plenty more ideas coming. Here are just a few examples of what you can experience today:
  • Slide along a narrow ledge as a giant pendulum swings inches from your face
  • Test your courage by jumping off a high walkway. You're told a trapdoor will instantly rise to save you from the spikes far below
  • Carefully move your head through a narrow gap filled with spikes
  • Knock blocks out of a wall without bringing the roof crashing down on your head

There's a story for you to find. It's up to you if you want to find the hidden messages and work out what's going on, or just play through the levels and complete the puzzles.

Area required


1.2m x 50cm required to play
Dimensional uses its own chaperone system which allows you to move around even if you only have a small playing area.
If you don't think you have enough room, try the free demo first. You may be surprised how little room you need.

The story


A trans-dimensional race is being hunted to extinction. Many years ago humans helped them build hidden chambers on Earth to safely hide their offspring. Now the hunters have built machines to smash their way into the chambers.
Can you rescue their offspring before it's too late?
The chambers are filled with traps and challenges that can only be completed by a human working together with an alien droid.

Your companion


You're accompanied by Digby. He's not the brightest or happiest droid, but give him a target and he'll hit it. Use him to manipulate objects or for more destructive purposes. Think of Digby as your hands and fists.

FPS-style controls

FPS mode allows you to move using analogue sticks like traditional firt person shooters. Only use FPS mode if it doesn't cause nausea. You can toggle between teleporting and FPS mode as required. You need to exit FPS mode to teleport.

To activate:
* On the HTC Vive, click the grip button to toggle FPS-mode on and off. Use the right track pad to rotate and the left track pad to move.
* On the Oculus DK2 / Rift, hold down your left shoulder button. Use the right analogue stick to rotate and the left to move.

When in FPS mode you drive your floor grid around. For this to make sense you should face forwards (as indicated by directional arrows) before starting to move.

An experimental game


I'm a solo-developer and spend all my time experimenting in VR to see what works best. Dimensional is a result of my continuing experiments to see how to make the most of this new technology and create highly immersive experiences.

Standing and using real-life movements is a key component. I wanted to allow as many people to play as possible so I use a custom chaperone system where you only need enough room to take a couple of steps forward.

Teleporting provides a nausea-free way to move around, but FPS-style controls can be used if you prefer (teleporting is still required to jump over gaps and reach heights)

Dimensional insists you move. You'll have to walk, crouch and lean as you explore and duck and even jump to avoid dangers. The ability and requirement to react as you would in real life increases immersion.

Interacting with the environment and seeing it react as expected (including dynamic lighting and shadows) makes the chambers feel more believable.

The microphone is used so that you can be seen and heard. Shout at droids (some will shoot at you), or activate sensors with your voice.

To complete the chambers you'll need to solve problems as though you are really there rather than thinking about it like a typical game.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 SP1 64 bit or newer
    • Processor: Intel i5-4590
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GTX 970 / AMD R9 290 equivalent or greater
    • Storage: 2 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: HTC Vive, Oculus Rift (or DK2) required + 1m x 50cm of space to move around

This game looks promising, but so far only five level are available. Dimensional is a puzzle game with room scale support, and at least a little space is required (120x50 cm) as you have to move to pass through the many parts of the levels. The five levels is in the game currently aren't that much of a challenge, but they are engaging and to me a good very VR experience. All of them being fairly short they are mainly give you a taste of what's to come, and i expect that levels will become longer and a lot harder as you progress. Having to physically react to dangers and and move to get through to levels really increases immersion, as you are doing things that come naturally to you. I have the Oculus Rift, and no tracked controller yet, so this is the only game i have that enables me to feel the extra precense, that using your body in VR gives.

The game lore seems fairly interesting all though i do not find that the strong point of the game, that would be the immersion, and the game design. Finding the secret messages that reveal the lore of the world, they weren't very interesting to me at first, but after finding them all, i am a bit more intriqued by it. All in all this was a great experience so far, and i look forward to more levels, and more of a challenge. No part was physically demanding, but i would personally enjoy some parts that could raise your pulse a little bit.

This is early acces and i will expand/edit my review as more content is released.
