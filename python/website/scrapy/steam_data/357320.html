Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“I chose to bring Break_In to Early Access because I believe it's a great way to gather feedback, ideas, and iron out bugs and other inconsistencies before the game goes fully live. As you can imagine, it's important with a game like this! It's also nice to give those willing to help out a better deal on purchasing the game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“9-12 months, approximately.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The Early Access version of Break_In will feature the core gameplay mechanics (base building and breaking in) without featuring some of the more advanced features. These mechanics will be expanded upon throughout development, until Break_In reaches a state where I feel it's sufficiently polished, tested, and ready to go.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is functional and extremely playable, but it is missing more advanced features which are still in development.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, the game will cost more at full release.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Every step of the way. It's a great opportunity to gather feedback, criticism, ideas and suggestions and it'd be foolish not to take advantage of that. I'll be doing my best to listen to absolutely everything.”

About This Game

What is Break_In?


Break_In is a base-building/FPS game where the main aim is to amass the largest amount of money you can, and invest it in defenses, traps, and other security measures to protect the rest of your stash. To do this, you will raid other players' bases and steal directly from their account.

  • Build up the defenses on your own safe house to deter other players.
  • Attack other players' safe houses and try to make it past their defenses.
  • Progress through different types of safe house as you make more and more money.
  • Upgrade your own abilities, skills and weapons as you make money.
  • No subscription fees, no pay to win, no microtransactions.

Key Features

Although it is very early days, I have a lot of ideas for where I want to go with Break_In. You can expect regular updates on this!

Dynamic



The main strength of Break_In comes from the customization. With a range of bases to take down, and the variety of defenses available for each base, you will very rarely have the same experience twice. Player A may have a handful of badly equipped thugs, while Player B could have highly trained guards, lasers, cameras, and even traps. Every experience will be different and keep you on your toes.

Progression



With Break_In you will start out with next to nothing, just a tiny shack of a safe house and your pistol. You will need to break into other players' bases to take their cash. You will then be able to use this cash to upgrade your own safe house, buy a new safe house, install security measures, hire security, buy new weapons, and much more. And when someone does attempt to break into your safe house, and falls victim to your well positioned camera and backup squad, you'll be notified and receive a percentage of their cash.

No pay2win, no microtransactions, no subscriptions or additional costs.



Goes without saying, really. I have zero intention for Break_In to go this way. Obviously I won't rule out future expansions, but I can promise right now that if the day comes when this project is ready for expansion, it will be sensibly priced and provide a lot of content.

Usually, I find no reason to review a game this early on. However after a single day with the game, I need to write the review I wish I could have read BEFORE deciding to purchase.

1. You do not decide what upgrade goes where.
Why does this matter? When attacking a structure that has upgraded, I already know where the cameras are. I know where the guards are. I know how the landmines are places. Upgrades slow me down, but I can rob every target I hit 100% of the time after only a few trial and error runs. There is no cheeky battle of the wits between intruder and owner. This is without a doubt 'the' game killer right here, as it's the biggest feature that appeals to people (and myself).

2. High value targets pay the most, and can be robbed over and over.
Why does this matter? Well you'll find where the vault is, spam it until their bank is dry. You then become a high value target for someone else to do the exact same thing to. It is inherently broken and I do not see a way it can be fixed. There is no exception here.

These two problems alone loop endlessly back on eachother, as you will discover for yourself if you choose to play. I see this going one of two ways. Either enough people purchase to allow for a nearly full rebuild...or the game becomes an ironic vessel for heist-ing 10 dollars from my safe that I'll never see again (which I'm cool with, I love irony).

Oh ps dev, please remove the underscore in the name :P
