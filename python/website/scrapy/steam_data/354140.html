About This Game

Thirty years after Freddy Fazbear's Pizza closed it's doors, the events that took place there have become nothing more than a rumor and a childhood memory, but the owners of "Fazbear's Fright: The Horror Attraction" are determined to revive the legend and make the experience as authentic as possible for patrons, going to great lengths to find anything that might have survived decades of neglect and ruin.

At first there were only empty shells, a hand, a hook, an old paper-plate doll, but then a remarkable discovery was made...

The attraction now has one animatronic.

Weakest game in the Five Nights at Freddy's series in my opinion, however, it is still a fun game to play, and, fanbase aside, a pretty good game. One complaint I have is that it has the lowest number of actual nights out of the entire series. Night 1 is simply an introduction, and there is no activity in it whatsoever, night 2 is where things start to pick up. There is a night 6, which leaves only 5 nights of legitimate gameplay, instead of the usual 7 that its predecessors had. In FNaF 1, 3, and 4, night 7 is a "custom night" in which difficulty for each animatronic character could be adjusted, and that is understandably absent in this game, since there is only one real animatronic. I do not mind the smaller amount of nights in this game, because, like the other installments in the series, I believe it has quite a bit of replay value, and, in this specific game, two achievable endings. All in all Five Nights at Freddy's 3 is a good game with some minor flaws. I would recommend it.

I believe this is my least favorite game in the FNaF series...because I can't figure it out... The mechanics seem simple enough: Springtrap is on the move! Use Child's Voice to keep him in rooms far away, close off vents to keep him from taking shortcuts to you. If one of your systems goes out, reset the computer.
The first night, I was scared. Absolutely nothing happens, and I was scared the whole time for what COULD happen. The next night...Springtrap is there. So far, I have not once seen him in the cameras until it's too late and I die. I hear you can just spam the Reboot button on your console and never be jumpscared to survive the game. I'm willing to try that, but this is also one of the deepest games of the series, from what I heard. After the nights, there is a minigame where you walk around as an animatronic and get murdered by the Purple Guy. If you take a certain hall, the wall tells you what to do the next night for a secret minigame.
Someone mod this to give Springtrap an orange safety vest so I can see him coming! I still recommend this game, and I'm thankful that even with the random hallucinations, the Springtrap attacks are...mildly shocking at best. I mean, the guy looks like he's coming in to give us a friendly hug.
