3D Realms tries to capture lightning in a bottle twice.

But it didn't quite happen. Shadow Warrior is an FPS by 3D Realms, created fresh after the success of Duke Nukem 3D, and on the surface level it's basically the same game with a new coat of paint. Unfortunately I think it has some problems that Duke Nukem 3D didn't have.

Shadow Warrior is split into two chapters. The first chapter is four levels and the second chapter includes 18 levels. They follow the traditional progression of hunting for keys, killing monsters and getting to the end of a level to go on to the next. This package also includes two level expansion sets. One with 12 more levels and another with 13 more levels. All together you get about 50 levels.

Level design on 3D Realms' part is mostly good. Good levels of detail and good conveyance for the player. All the levels have a sense of place and play well. Levels are varied from temples to subway stations. The levels by Sunstorm (Wanton Destruction) are a mixed bag. Some of them are designed as well as the 3D Realms levels, whereas others feel like they need some polish. The levels by Level Infinity however feel incredibly amateur and poorly designed. Under detailed, short, and rarely doing anything to stand out from the two other developer's level sets.

Graphics wise, the game looks fine. Nothing about it really stands out as great looking or poor looking. Using the Build engine once again just like their previous game Duke Nukem 3D, they made a few advancements with the engine like vehicles you can drive, but overall it's nothing special. The enemies look uninspired and boring in their design, but the weapons are all drawn nicely and animated well.

The balance of Shadow Warrior is the area I take most issue with. You will get a variety of weapons including your trusty sword, your fists, shurikens, uzis, a riot shotgun, grenade launcher, rocket launcher with heat seeking and nuke firing abilities, a railgun, sticky bombs, a demon heart that creates a clone of yourself that fights alongside you, and a demon head with three modes of fire.

The problem with the weapons is that, quiet simply they feel unbalanced. The sword is extremely powerful, and can kill most enemies easily. The fists then, are useless. The shurkins only purpose is to preserve your ammo should you run low, the uzis feel under-powered and sound wimpy, the riot shotgun is only useful in its spray fire mode, the sticky bombs are a â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ to use effectively, and all bosses including the last can be beaten easily with enough demon hearts. I didn't even have to shoot at the final boss, my clone from my demon heart did all the work for me and kicked his â¥â¥â¥. The nuke is also too much of a risk to use thus I never did.

The items in this game are also mostly useless. Gas grenades do tons of damage to everyone including you, I didn't need to use night vision, the caltrops are only effective in MP and do NOTHING in single player and you'll probably just hurt yourself with them, the only useful item is the smoke bomb which grants you partial invincibility for a short amount of time. The lack of balance doesn't stop at the weapons either. The enemy cast of Shadow Warrior is not only rather uninspired, but they are also either a. cheap or b. weak. You will mostly be fighting various colored demon "ninjas", the amount of damage these lowly goons can do to you should be enough proof of the lack of balance. To make matters worse, various colored ones will randomly shoot missiles & grenades out of their chest with no warning whatsoever. It's cheap â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥t to take a missile to the face and die because you can't read your enemy's attacks at all.

The biggest offender of all the different ninjas however, is the shadow ninja. These fast moving and cloaked ninjas have a variety of attacks that can insta-kill you should you get caught in them. I just can't fathom why 3D Realms thought it would be a good idea to give the lowly grunts the most OP attacks in the entire game. The same enemy that, when hardly touched, will sometimes blow their own head off with their uzi. It's very hard to predict which attack pattern the shadow ninja will use, so your only option is to blow it up as fast as possible and run away to keep maximum distance.

You'll also face suicidal bombers that will charge you, and upon death, might raise again as a ghost that floats in and out of reality and shoot fireballs at you. There are also various ape like creatures that are total push overs, green orc demons that shoot fire out of their eyes, female assassins which are not threatening at all, small hard to hit and annoying hornets (Which bring back memories of Daikatana and enrage me even more), Serpent minibosses that are easy to circle strafe and kill, and demonic sumo wrestlers that fart at you and cause mini-earthquakes. None of the enemies feel like they are working together, pretty much all of them are rather easy to kill and boring to fight, and the strongest enemy you will fight, are the first ones and most common ones you see the entire game.

So, my word of advice? Grenade spam. The grenade launcher feels like a god damn mortar and is extremely powerful. Shoot it around corners, into rooms, bounce it off walls, it destroys everything. It will be your most used weapon in the entire game. 3D Realms simply did not balance this game and it feels like a mess to play.

Sound design is mostly good. Explosions are beefy and loud, blood and gore sounds squishy and gross, glass and objects breaking sound loud and satisfying when mixed in with all the explosions going off. I think most of the weapons sound good as well. The rocket launcher and grenade launcher sound good, and the shotgun has a beefy sounding report, but the uzi is just laughable and sounds like someone firing a gun far off in the distance rather than right in your face. the monster sounds are mostly mediocre and some of them are actually annoying like the grey ape creatures which cry out with every, single, hit.. Music is mostly passable, if not a tad bit generic sounding. Typical oriental sounds mixed in with somewhat generic rock. I find your character Lo Wang to be annoying and lacking any of the charm that a character like Duke Nukem had. He does say a few funny lines and references here and there but overall I found him irritating.

I also take a lot of issue with how this game feels to play, I played with a KB+M, though you can play with a gamepad if you so wish. I felt like I was really fighting with the controls. Movement doesn't seem to flow as smoothly and looking around just feels odd. I also had to scroll my mousewheel a lot just to switch weapons which became a problem fast. This may be a result of the port they used, which gets rid of the DOS-isms and makes the game run natively on Windows, though as nice a gesture this is, this port they've created needs some work. I've encountered numerous graphical glitches from voids in the walls to broken skies, there is also a lack of official Workshop support or Multiplayer. Rather unfortunate.

The story in Shadow Warrior is very simplistic. You work for Zilla Enterprises, they own every major industry in Japan, Zilla gets power hungry, brings demons in, you decide you're not a fan of this plan. Very unimportant in this game.

At the end of the day, I'd recommend other games that have this same design like Duke Nukem 3D, Quake or even DOOM before I recommend Shadow Warrior. I feel like the balance issues this game has are severe, and while some might just chalk that up as "old school hard difficulty", I'd be willing to argue it's just bad game design. There are many 90's FPS that are hard but fair (Like Duke Nukem 3D) instead of hard for the sake of it, or from lack of balancing your game, and that is the key distinct difference I think. One requires skill, and the other requires luck and/or patience of steel.
