[Workshop Servers]

Hello Blocksters!

We launched our new official Workshop servers.
Workshop servers are ranked like any other official server.
This means that if you join them, your score and your achievements
will be updated during the match.

Workshop servers periodically download the most voted and the most trending maps (according to Steam) in our community workshop and make them available to play online in dedicated servers.
Most appreciated maps (that is, maps that are mostly played till the end of the match by users) have a higher priority to be selected in the following matches. On the other end, maps that are not enjoyed by users (by quitting the match or not voting them) are automatically discarded.

We hope you'll be glad to see your custom maps in ranked servers too.
Soo.. have a nice fight and may the best win!

About This Game

Blockstorm is a FPS game set in a world made of destructible blocks.

The voxel nature of characters and maps and the in-game editor allows you to fully customize your game and quickly share your experience with other players.
You are free to create your own maps and characters from scratch or to customize existing ones. You can edit weapons too.


All maps and characters included in the game are made with the same tools that are available to the public.


-------------------------------------------------------




Weapons range from precise rifles to heavy machine guns or even explosives capable of destroying hiding places and creating tactical advantages.
You can pick out your own load out, choosing between a set of weapons configured by the creator of the map.





You can build everything in Blockstorm, and you can destroy it.



Currently supported modes:
  • Deathmatch
  • Team Deathmatch
  • Capture The Flag
  • Assault
  • Castle Battle
  • Hunt

Ok, letâs get this review started. For those of you that want a brief overview (or those of you that can't be stuffed reading the massive review below), there is a pros/cons list down underneath the actual review.

PROS

First good thing is that the game has a weapon, player model, and map maker, which is rather nice, as it brings a personal touch to the game.

Second good thing is It has workshop support which is also quite nice.

Third, is the pretty decent game-play, although the lag is a mild problem, its mainly due to the horrible internet where I live.

Another upside is the balanced weapons.

And the fifth and likely most important upside, is the completely destructible environments that don't take a beast of a computer to run.

Next, there is the sixth good thing...the MECHS...Completely awesome and deadly, those things tear through almost anyone in their path. They take a fair few missiles to bring down too.

On a final point, you can create your own servers quickly and easily.

CONS

First off, the editors are rather limited. The gun and player model editor are ridiculously frustrating with the editor being unable to remove most blocks from both gun and player model is infuriating. I could understand if it was a single block in the middle of the gun or at the back, however unfortunately you cant remove half the gun and/or the middle layer...for unexplained reasons. On top of that, the size of editing space is incredibly limited, only allowing you to place one or two blocks in any direction outside of the original gun, which makes it extremely frustrating if you are trying to make your machine gun look like the heavy lancer from Ratchet and Clank 2...Not sure how many people remember that game...ANYWAY, back to the review. On to the map editor. The editor is relatively less limited, however the inability of placing very many NPC's in a small area is annoying to say the least when trying to create a zombie survival. Another downside is that the map editor can at times be borderline confusing with what is considered the actual map, and what isn't.

The second downside is that-despite the weapons being balanced, there simply aren't enough to make the game feel
different from most other shooters.

The third downside is that other than an A.I for turrets and zombies, there are no bots.

The fourth downside is that you have to be online to play, even to just test an edited map or piece of equipment.

LIST OF PROS AND CONS

PROS
-Mostly balanced weapons
-Decent game-play
-Bi-pedal mechanical walkers (Giant walking things with a minigun, rocket launcher and what seems to be a power-fist :P)
-Editors-for the personal touch that you need while mass-murdering everyone in your path with a bright orange machine-gun while looking like a storm trooper..,
-Workshop support so you can upload your creations and make use of what other's have uploaded. Useful for doing what is mentioned above :P
-You can make your own server and watch as it fills up with random players from around the globe :P


CONS
-Editors are extremely limited-but have good potential.
-Not enough weapons...(THERE CAN NEVER BE TOO MANY WEAPONS...well..most of the time)
-No A.I/Bots (except zombies/turrets)
-Online play only-no editing maps and playing them offline.

And finally, dear reader, you stumble in relief over what is known as a summary :P
So, decent shooter at its core, but it needs a lot of modification with the editors, as well as requires actual A.I to fight against offline. I'd personally recommend buying it on sale.
Thats all for this review. Dragon out.
