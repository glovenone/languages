About This Game

Description


Girlfriend Rescue is a thrilling adventure by Aldorlea Games, where the gameplay of "Dragon Quest" meets the atmosphere of "Streets of Rage".
Innovative, mixing rogue-style RPG and Beat 'em Up atmosphere in a unique way, this game features a great deal of strategy through a mix of character selection and creative skills.

Plot


You have very little time to find where your girlfriend has been taken, and to defeat whoever did it.
It seems a tough and big task. But you want her.. death or success, what will it be?
Maybe YOU can decide on the outcome!

Features


  • Enthralling Adventure Set in a Modern World
  • 50+ Achievements to Unlock
  • Stunning Artworks from the Team Behind Moonchild, The Book of Legends and Undefeated
  • Rename all your characters to yourself, your friends, your family - whoever you want, really
  • 14 Bonus Items to Obtain Upon Completing Each World
  • Innovative and Strategic Battles
  • Original and Inventive Spells Unique to Each Character
  • Incredible Set of Items, Weapons and Equipment
  • Tons of Status Ailments to Enhance the Strategic Aspect
  • 6 Modes of Difficulty (including Rogue, Maniac & Legendary, but also Easy)
  • Visible Encounters, Auto-Save & Optional Mouse Control
  • Great Replay Value with Tons of Possible Parties
  • Cast of 9 Characters to Choose from, Not Including 4 Unlockable "Secret" Ones



  • Martin the Cool Kid - for sure the fastest of the bunch, and has some wicked moves up in his sleeves, too
  • Lester the Cop - that dude will love to apply the law and command you guys, but he can pack a punch, too



  • Joy the Bombshell - a girl who loves to make sure others are alright, especially if they are young handsome males



  • Katia the Nerd - you wouldn't believe how many crazy, flat-out weird skills that girl has
  • Cookie the Bullfrog - that small(ish) dude has some many venoms and poisons you have to wonder if they didn't get it from Australia or the deep woods of the Amazon


And more!...

Hints and Recommendations


  • Pick Easy for a first play-through - you can attempt more difficult modes later on
  • Search everywhere - this is a game that rewards exploration
  • Try to kill all the enemies - your girlfriend (and the exp. points ^^) are worth it
  • Keep your party members healthy - don't let them starve or with a bad cold
  • Use stat-increasing items immediately - no need to store them for later
  • Plan out your strategies - check what kind of skills each member has

Girlfriend Rescue. Well this game was something to experience, that’s for sure. I need to start by saying that this review is, unfortunately, personally biased. No, not because I received a copy of the game for free (although I did that didn’t affect my review). No, I found myself personally biased because quite frankly I love RPG maker games. As a man very close to his 40s at the time of writing this, I grew up playing Final Fantasy on my NES, and consuming every RPG I could get my hand on. RPG maker games often evoke a feeling of nostalgia in me that takes me back to the late 80s and early 90s. Boy, let me tell you… Girlfriend Rescue triggers that sentimentality, and triggers it hard, in all the right ways.

So, the store page claims that “Girlfriend Rescue is a thrilling adventure where the gameplay of "Dragon Quest" meets the atmosphere of "Streets of Rage".” One word, yup. I get it, I totally get it. Playing Girlfriend Rescue absolutely reminded me of the game play of the old Dragon Quest style JRPGs and had the pointless, often campy, story reminiscent of games like Streets of Rage or Bad Dudes. First of all, hilariously, this game isn’t about rescuing your ‘girlfriend’ it’s about trying to rescue some chick the main character has a crush on. A girl that likely has no idea the hero even exists. You’re less a boyfriend and more a stalker to be honest. The inclusion of choice of gender of both the protagonist and the ‘crush’ was a nice touch. It felt unnecessary to me, but it was one of those choices that was simple to include in the game and some players will probably appreciate. So you set off on a quest with three of your ‘gang’ to try to rescue your crush.

This is one of the game’s interesting points, you choose three members of your gang with very little information about what they are going to bring to the party. There were several times that I wanted to restart just to see what the other gang members would have done because I ended up with a melee heavy party with no healing. Still I found the game not too difficult, even on normal. Though I was searching every square inch possible for every scrap of food, healing medicine, or coin to make sure I could keep my party healthy and satiated. Oh, yeah, because hunger is a thing. A thing that actually adds a little depth to the game in fact, you might not want to use all your healing items if they are also the only food you have, because someone might get hungry soon.

Actually there’s a lot of different status effects in this game. Almost too many, and I couldn’t tell you after playing for three hours if I’d seen them all, and I certainly couldn’t tell you what most of them did. But in the end that doesn’t really matter too much, as you get the idea that good status effects are good, and bad ones are bad. It’s not required to know that one lowers strength by 10% and defense by 30% while another one lowers strength by 30% and defense by 10%. Just keep smashing in the faces of bad guys.

The game isn’t without it’s faults of course. The music is a bit repetitive and I found myself turning it off after not too long. Likewise the graphics are quite underwhelming. I’m sure that the idea was to go for a retro feel. But somehow it doesn’t work out quite as well as other RPG maker games I’ve played. The in game sprites feel very flat, and the levels all just feel like cookie cutter layouts.

But in the end, I find that I don’t mind this too much and they are more minor points when it comes to the story and the game. I’ve found myself quite pleased with the time I’ve put into this one. Comparing it against other RPG maker games, this one comes out very well. But you have to get into the campy feel that they are throwing down. Throw yourself back to 1990 and you’ll get it, but if you look at this and compare it to modern RPGs you’ll be disappointed. So caveat emptor I suppose, but for me, I’ve enjoyed it quite a bit and recommend it to anyone, who like myself, is old enough to recognize what the developers were going for with this campy beat em up RPGMaker game.


NOTE - I was given a free copy of this game in exchange for an honest and unbiased review.

Girlfriend Rescue is a turn/round-based RPG through and through which starts out with a conversation among friends about approaching your crush, but you find out that some recently escaped prisoner has taken her! You have to form a party to find a way to save her. You start out with choices you can make about your gender, name, crush’s name, and once you can move around, you have a selection of up to two other members you are friendly with that can help you out as you start your quest to get your girl back. I would have liked to have been able to try them all to find out what each possible party member could do, but I would not really know future abilities as they leveled up anyway, so it is probably not a very important issue.

As you begin, you get a lot of nice tips from the local police NPCs on various aspects of play. This was helpful for me as I began journeying around. There are a lot of various original things about this RPG game that I like, such as the various debuffs and what can change when things happen. They are atypical to what I am used to seeing, which is pretty interesting and refreshing to me. I mean, a lot of it involves various stats, so it’s not too different, but the way it is done is not the typical type of stuff. I like what I’ve encountered so far in this game. The combat system is a standard turn-based style where you have attack and defend commands along with special moves you can learn as you level up, which you pick for the next round, and watch the round unfold.

The actual gameplay outside of combat is roaming around a map, trying to find X amount of enemies on the area you explore, and taking out the last guy on the map. I have not tried finishing an area with less than the target number, but I am guessing your bonus reward at the end would change if you got less than the number it shows, which finalizes after the last “boss” character on the map, as far as I can tell, ever since the first area I explored fully.


Pros

The plot is different, and I find the initial gameplay and overall setting nicely done.

The look of the areas so far work with what’s going on at the paticular time. Fits in nicely.

Since you can only pick limited characters for each run through the game, there is some replay value with this to find out what other characters are about, and which combinations might work the best on a harder difficulty, for instance. I haven’t finished to see if there is a new game plus, and what that entails exactly, but there is definitely some replay value here in some way shape or form, which I always like!

I am always happy when I can be rewarded with items for exploring around. Most stuff seems to be added to inventory automatically when you are on the proper square or area, so it makes it a bit easier not having to mindlessly press buttons to click each spot. Some boxes do require a click, but they are usually obvious.

The various combat mechanics in the special moves are very different than the standard RPG type of moves. I really like the originality that is put into many of my party’s special techniques.

The status effects are not standard to RPGs either, and the originality is nice to see. While most just lower stats in a few areas or stop you from moving, etc. they are not your typical “paralyze” or whatever. Some will have a chance to go away after a turn or so, while others can last a bit longer. They have interesting debuff names and the debuffs are generally not the typical types of effects. Usually seems to be a series of stats that are lowered for most I have encountered so far. Some will persist until you cure it, like hunger for instance. There are a good many battle stat debuffs, and they have various different things they change or lower on your characters.

By clearing areas and dispatching every enemy there, you gain a special bonus, which carries over to the next areas. You also get several items, which I haven’t tried comparing yet to unfinished area bonuses.

Autosaves after five battles have gone by.

While it doesn’t affect things too terribly much, the hunger aspect lowers stats a bit in certain areas. I like the addition of needing to eat as you travel.


Cons

Very linear gameplay. While I don’t expect anything like a total open world experience, I would like more choice, and the possibility of returning enemies to grind if I wanted to do so. One aspect I enjoy on RPGs that I miss here.

If I level up with items, I am unable to see what improves. I can only see this directly when I win battles and level up.

Some of the items are not very clear as to what they do, or are buggy. Some don’t seem to work the way they should. The Red Toro is supposed to counter Tired status, but it remains on the alien I try to use it on, but works on other characters. Another character was Infected, and Cortisol never works for removal of this. It might only work when actually in battle, but I haven’t really tried and compared this to being out of battle, where it doesn’t change the status effect while on characters.

Near the beginning, when I am first able to choose my party, it would be nice to be able to see what each one is capable of. Maybe being able to switch out at various times if I wanted to just try others instead of needing to start over and try them a while. Of course, with no grinding, this would probably not be advantageous unless they took the same level as the character they were switched out for, since you wouldn’t get as many experience points for the three you would mainly use, and they would be spread out too much, which would cause an imbalance in the parity that exists in a limited battle grind-free style environment..

I love a good map, even on something I consider a bit linear. Good to know where I am.


Conclusion
Is the game worth the $4.99 USD price tag to pick up? Probably. I think with the replay value, and the decent length of the game so far among other things, that alone is worth it. The combat has some interesting abilities and the buffs and debuffs are not your ordinary stuff, for the most part. I’m the type that likes to get games on sale, so I’d pick it up on a deal, but saying that, this game is definitely worth the price it is at, and I am surprised it does not carry a higher base price, honestly. It’s a good one for the RPGMaker style engine. I can highly recommend this game after my fairly limited gameplay thus far.

At first glance, and from it's straightforward name "Girlfriend Rescue" looks to be a fairly easy or casual sort of game ---- but not too long into the very first world you will find that it is full of intricacies, assorted choices, and complex decisions that reveal a great story that is very difficult to take a break from.
The story told is solid and good fun, the characters are very well developed and likable, and the action/exploration is virtually non-stop.


As the title reveals, your girlfriend has been kidnapped for unknown nefarious reasons in worn down Detroit, and you and your friends immediately set out to rescue her. A big part of the game is the creation of your party from the available characters since each character has different attributes which will change the way you approach the game ---- so the replay value is very high. (especially if you are wanting to unlock all the achievements.)


The artwork is beautifully done, the music adds a lot to the atmosphere of the game, and the gameplay itself is flawless.


There are a great many items to find and spells to use and learn so you need to explore the game very carefully to find everything. Each world (presented in a maze like style) will tell you how many enemies need to be defeated, and once defeated, they do not reappear. Defeat them all for big bonuses.


So there is great fun to be had here with lots of replay that will keep you busy for many hours. Give this one a try, and you will be drawn into a game that is a bit of a variation on the old style rpg experience that is familiar, and very appealing.

Girlfriend Rescue is perhaps one of the dumbest and most simple RPGs ever. It has nothing particularly special about it – it's made with RPG Maker, the story is laughably silly, and the gameplay is pretty much what you'd expect from a typical turn-based RPG. So, why am I recommending it? Well, read on and find out!

The Breakdown
The game “combines the atmosphere of Streets of Rage with the gameplay of Dragon Quest”, according to the store page at least. The gameplay might be a little different than Dragon Quest, but the bit about Streets of Rage is pretty much spot on – this is clearly a silly game with a silly premise. I mean, the name says it all!

Perhaps one of the funniest parts about the story though is that the girl you're trying to rescue from the bad guys (ninjas, of course!) isn't actually the main character's girlfriend. Instead, she's just some random girl that the guy has a crush on. He decides to team up with 3 of 13 (4 of those are unlockables) characters/friends to stop the bad guys. Personally, I found the character designs for the party members to be pretty amusing. My party consisted of the main dude, a shady-looking “dealer” character, a little boy, and a frog. Yep, a frog. Each of the characters have amusing dialogue occasionally, yet what truly sets them apart from each other is their strategies during battles.

See, the gameplay of Girlfriend Rescue basically consists of a series of battles in a set area. The game throws you into a series of areas linked together by an overall location (like say, a line of train cars in a train). In these, you fight a variety of enemies, collect items by walking into objects (yeah, it's weird), and manage your party through equipping gear or using consumables like pizza, drugs, or “red toro” (Red Bull). The battles themselves are pretty much what you'd expect: turn-based attacks, skills, defending, and whatnot. Each character has their own special skills (sometimes with silly names) that sometimes apply status effects to enemies; certain weapons can also do this.

Now, a somewhat minor complaint I had is that it was a bit difficult to remember what most of these status effects actually do. There's a LOT of them – quite a bit more than most RPGs – and it became tough to try and remember what would happen if an enemy “infected” one of my party members, made them “tired”, “doomed”, etc. There's police officers NPCs that can be found every once in a while and explain about a few status effects, but it would be nice to have a help section or even just an item that gives little text blurbs about each status effect as a quick refesher.

Another part that bugged me about Girlfriend Rescue was how repetitive it got after a while. Each “world” was basically the exact same – slap on a fresh new coat of paint for the scenery and enemies, fight a bunch of enemies in a mostly linear path, then rinse and repeat. There wasn't a lot of variety besides the pixel art, different songs for different worlds, enemy designs, and bosses. Speaking of bosses, they were pretty much incredibly easy, or at least all the ones that I fought were. I would have gotten farther, but.....well......

….the difficulty. This game has really strange difficulty spikes. At the beginning of the game, it recommends you play on Easy for a first playthrough, which I found a bit odd, so I decided to be rebellious and pick Normal. Now, for most of the game I had a good challenge, but it was nothing too tough. For the first few worlds, my characters rarely died at all. Yet, when I reached the 4th world, I kept dying to the enemies of that area, zombies. It wasn't too much, but it was definitely a noticeable difference in difficulty from the previous areas. The weird part though is that the 5th area was one of the easiest for me – I beat the boss (or rather 2 bosses) quite quickly and the enemies were similarly a breeze. However, the 6th location......oh jeez. It began simple enough: a town with snakes. So what? Well, it turned out to be extremely difficult, to the point that my characters were all either dying or nearly dying after every battle. Those damn snakes were crazy hard, and the weird part is that the difficulty spike sort of came out of nowhere – I had great equipment and a decent combo of powerful fighters and spell casters at that point. So, I don't really understand why that area was so incredibly challenging for pretty much no reason. Eh, oh well.

Anyway, why might I be recommending this game then? To be honest, I think a lot of it was how charming I found the game to be. The story made me laugh at its silliness, and pretty much everything about the game was dumb. Yet, the game embraced its stupidity and just sort of went with it. I love it for that. Yeah, it's clearly not amazing – the repetitive nature of the gameplay limits it quite a bit. For a silly little RPG Maker game though? Not bad, not bad at all!

Pros
  • Charming, silly story about teaming up with some goofy characters to rescue your "girlfriend" from a secret conspiracy involving ninjas, zombies, and much more!
  • Battles can involve a decent amount of strategy (skills, status effects, etc.)
  • Lots of options in terms of equipment, items, and party members (you can choose 3 of 9, along with 4 unlockable ones)
  • About 6-10 hours long (from what I can tell), though the incentive to try different character combinations and unlock new characters adds the option for extra replayability
  • Sprite art and character designs are quite nice
  • Music is a little generic and cheesy, but it's interesting and not too repetitive usually
  • Has Steam achievements, trading cards, and cloud support

Cons
  • Gameplay can get a bit repetitive after a while - definitely play in short bursts!
  • The story isn't really serious, so if you're looking for one, this may not be the game for you
  • The game has random difficulty spikes about halfway through (the 4th and especially the 6th world)

Girlfriend Rescue is a strange mix of a charming and silly atmosphere mixed with slightly repetitive "beat up all the bad guys" turn-based gameplay. Sure, it has its fair share of problems, but overall this is a great addition to anyone's library. If you're not a fan of RPGs or RPG Maker games, it may not appeal to you, but for those who enjoy a silly story and a good ol' fun RPG, I think you may quite like this one.

Final Verdict: 7.5/10
