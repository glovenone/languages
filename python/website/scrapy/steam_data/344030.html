About This Game

Mysterious Things are Happening on Vooju Island...

Legendary video game writer and developer Bill Tiller (The Curse of Monkey Island, A Vampyre Story) tells a gripping tale of pirates, ghosts, and mysterious Vooju magic. Vooju priest Papa Doc, bumbling cook Blue Belly, and impetuous pirate Jane Starling have fallen victim to the nefarious plans of the Papa Docâs wife, the devious Queen Zimbi. The nefarious Zimbi has used her mysterious Vooju magic to curse and transform our three intrepid characters into ghosts. Can you help them rejoin their bodies before they join the ranks of the undead forever?

Set amidst a mythical Caribbean world of Vooju magic and unmistakable pirate humor, Ghost Pirates of Vooju Island challenges you to help the heroic ghostly trio regain their human forms and thwart the dark machinations of the Vooju priestess and her pirate allies. The fate of the entire world lies in the hands of these three unusual heroes!

Key Features

  • Tickle your funny bone in this lovingly hand-painted game world, with a story penned by legendary video game creator Bill Tiller
  • Combine the abilities of the three playable main characters to outsmart the pirates
  • Play as a ghost and human, and use Vooju magic to solve mysterious puzzles
  • Meet a huge cast of creepy and hilarious characters

Previously I couldn't play the game, but with the Dec. 5 update the game starts up for me (Windows 10).

I will start off by saying that I enjoyed the game. I enjoyed playing the Monkey Island games and this game does scratch the itch a bit for playing a point-and-click adventure game in a pirate setting with comedy. No doubt the similarity is there because the designer originally worked on the visuals of Monkey Island. What I liked the most were the characters. Each one, Papa Doc, Blue Belly, and Jane Sterling, are strikingly different from each other yet mesh well together as a trio out to dismantle the villain's plans.

However, the game does have its faults but mainly due to bugs. Despite being able to play the game now, most of the cutscenes between acts have failed to play for me. Fortunately the game didn't crash out and was able to skip to the next act, but it is a hassle as information about the plot is mentioned during those times. Furthermore, from what I have seen, the transitions can be fairly abrupt in terms of continuity between scenes. Another issue is that the gameplay becomes buggier later in the game. For example, after moving a laundry basket from one location to another, hovering the mouse cursor over its former location would still allow an action on it. Clicking it again would then trigger the same action again. It's not game breaking but it is there.

Overall, I would recommend the game. It's a fairly good point-and-click adventure game and the bugs weren't serious enough for me to stop playing it. I hope that the developers will make a sequel one day after they finish making their spin-off of this game.

Posted: 26 January
4/10 - Not good enough to buy, but all right to play if you get it in a bundle or on sale.

The problem with "Ghost Pirates..." is that it, sadly, does not quite realise the potential it had in any area. The premise is very good - three different characters linked by a voodoo curse can talk telepathically and become ghosts. Unfortunately, the ghost part is not for the player to use at all - the characters will just become ghosts when the game needs them to, so there are no nifty puzzles to figure out through, say, combined use of physical body and ghost-hood. And the telepathic link starts off interesting - you ask others about things you don't know about - but, given the counter-intuitiveness of most puzzles, this soon devolves into one more inventory item that you combine randomly with anything in the hope of advancing.

The authors of this game really, really should have played "Discworld Noir" first. They would have picked up a lot of very useful guidelines, such as how to do switching between different forms of the PC to great puzzle advantage. And exactly how much good voice acting matters, especially when you have bad 3D graphics.

Because the two main problems of "Ghost Pirates..." are the horrible 3D models, and voice acting. The 3D elements stick out like a sore thumb from the breathtakingly beautiful backgrounds, making you wish you could play this in FPP and just watch the amazing pictures without the "3D models" punching holes in them.
And since you have to watch the clunky, graceless things, they could have at least spoken well, because while the actual lines are often pretty funny, the voice actors speak as if doing extremely thick accents took up 100% of their powers and none was left for actual delivery. So the swashbuckling dashing spy talks as if she didn't understand her own words, and the bokor is permanently in "me big black angry guy" mode, even when waxing poetic about his lost love. The voices are so insufferable I mostly played on mute, and then a cutscene happened and I had to reload, because they have no subtitles for some inscrutable reason.

All in all, I still give a positive opinion because I'm glad someone made a game like this, but it would need some serious overhaul to be a success.

I love this game, and since its rating was so negative due to spending so many months being unplayable for the majority (game worked fine for me), I felt I needed to help balance reviews now that a patch was finally issued.

All I need to say is that Monkey Island was 'my' video game, back in my teen years. I finished the second episode over the phone with a friend I was trying to solve puzzles with. I love all of the episodes, even the last two. I try to buy every pirate-themed game I can find, especially those with a comedic side. Vooju Island was one such 'find', back in the day.
So, yes, it's Bill Tiller's shameless attempt at making his own Monkey Island follow-up after he left LucasArts. I never found him to be crucial to the Monkey Island mythos, however Vooju managed to make me laugh and, most importantly, I got attached to the characters. Yes, the game has many flaws. But it also got what mattered right, at least what mattered to me: story, atmosphere and characters.

tl;dr: if you want to play a Monkey Island-like game, buy a Monkey Island game. If you've finished them all, buy Vooju Pirates and Jolly Rover, the two best MI copycats around.

I was able to play this on my Windows 7 64-bit system. I have dual monitors, and for some reason it insisted on running on the secondary monitor. I had to disable the second monitor to make it run on the primary. It also failed to run at all sometimes when the second monitor was active. But turning off the second monitor made it work fine.

I liked the art work. The game play was a bit strange. You could get "ideas" and store them for later use, but you couldn't actually pick up items. The story felt disjointed, jumping between scenes without a smooth transition. There were long cut scenes that couldn't be paused or rewound, so try to play this in an environment without potential for interruptions.

Most disconcerting was that there was a lot of interplay between the characters, love interests, and other back story, and in the end none of that was brought to any conclusion. It was just solve the last puzzle, game over. It felt like they left out the last chapter of a book.

So it looked good, characters were interesting, it was reasonably fun to play, but I'm kind of neutral about my feelings. If you like this sort of thing, it might be worth a few dollars to you. It took me about 18 hours to play, with copious help from a walkthrough.
