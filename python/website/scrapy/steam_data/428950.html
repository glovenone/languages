Kivi, Toilet and Shotgun
Kivi, Toilet and Shotgun is a single player top down action game where your goal is to reach the end of the desert by avoiding all kinds of obstacles and fighting the desert raiders. Go up against foes in your vehicle and on foot. Take on powerful bosses, but make sure you're quipped for the fight!

I love this game, probably too much. It's hard, short, and occasionally unforgivingly buggy, but it's a complete game and almost elegant in it's minimalism. For me it really scratches that retro, post-apocalyptic late 80's arcade itch.

The concept is super simple, you play a guy driving down a broken road in some post apocalypse wasteland heading for some unstated destination. Along the way there are towns where you can pay for repairs and buy ammo, fuel, guns etc for you and your car. In between the towns are bad guys you have to avoid or kill for loot. Entering each town triggers a fight either with some waves of bad guys or one of 5 scripted bosses that you have to beat before you can do your shopping. The difficulty is in preserving your armor, conserving your ammo and picking up enough cash along the way. Oh and avoiding canyons. Those things can be brutal.

Interesting little game with surprisingly non-repetitive gameplay.
For a game with this price you would expect very few features, but this is not the case.
PROS:
- different types of upgrades and items that aren't there just for the sake of numbers (some are really useful)
- base defense / car combat mix
- reminds of the good old flash games with similar purchase upgrades style
- simple rules to follow
- easy to play and requires very little of your game time
CONS:
- sometimes the art feels to simplistic
- small sound variety. Would be nice if the dev would have an update to give the game more sounds.
- fuel consumption can prove a pain sometimes, so casual players might get frustrated and quit

Overall I recommend this game. Considering the price tag, the game is basically free. Much cheaper than any so called 'free games'. So since it's very cheap, the only concern is that if this game is a time waster. In my opinion is not.

When I first picked up this game, I was not sure what to expect. When all you know about a game is that it has to do with toilets and guns, there is much to be skeptical about. However, shortly after getting into the game I was pleasantly surprised by a simple, fun concept that provided what I would say was a good value, for a 99 cent game.

Soon after reaching the first checkpoint, I experienced my first death. Restarting was no big deal, since I had made progress and I was okay with having to go back a few steps once a lesson was learned. What I did not expect (or enjoy) was the many, many deaths it took me to reach the next few checkpoints. I rethought my strategy several times, buying many different upgrades and never forgetting to refill on health and supplies. But checkpoints were few and far between, and dying became tedious and very unpleasant.

Once, after barely cruising into a checkpoint, I dismounted my vehicle, weary and ready for another fight. However, instead of aiming for me, a resudial enemy from the road came in and destroyed my car, leaving me without a vehicle. Not built to handle this phenomenon, the game then had me walk for miles with no vehicle enemy spawns. Once I had reached the end of the road and arrived at the next town, I braced myself for the foot soldier attacks that would come before I could claim my checkpoint. However, they never did come. Since I had never dismounted my vehicle, the game had never been signaled to spawn the enemies for my battle!

Unable to save and stuck with no action, I left to the main menu to try Test Drive mode, which had worked bug-free for me prior to my little dilemma. Upon entering of the Test Drive section, the game promptly crashed (which I suspect had something to do with my previous bug).

That said, Kivi, Toilet and Shotgun is a great game for the price it's at. It's pretty fun, with a moderate replay value. However, the difficulty slant in this game along with the many bugs I have experienced make this review a downvote.
