The Next Door is an easy game to review because it has no substance. It is an aberration that is desperately striving to be abstraction. The only tangible thought one can come away with after experiencing it is just how poorly a video game can be made. Listing the flaws of The Next Door could indeed be a game in and of itself; incredibly uncomplicated puzzles, nauseating music that resounds in cursory loops, terribly designed and piercingly bright areas that burn out your retinae, awful player movement and interaction, the worst optimization imaginable and it takes thirty minutes to complete, due to the harsh content however, this is a blessing.

The Next Door will be forgotten quicker than it ends as you laugh and move onto the next game.

Basic Information
Title: The Next Door
Developer: Labory
Publisher: Labory
Genre: Pure abstract

General Impression
An abstract game that deals with topics such as secret societies and religions without offending anyone and providing a quite interesting albeit short experience. Built like an adventure and puzzle game, The Next Door features decent graphical details but it is dragged down by the glitchy and outright obnoxious selection of sounds. I do agree that it is currently overpriced at 18€. This is the dev team’s first game on Steam and surely they can correct the pricing issues eventually. The puzzle sections need to be slightly better organized as the difficulty scale is off-balance.

Strong Points
+ Interesting 3D graphics.
+ Puzzle diversity.
+ Steam Trading Cards.

Weak Points
- Controls are not very responsive.
- No Steam Achievements.
- Awful sound selection.

Rating 5/10

Remember SCP games? Like the endless staircase and the first actual slender game?

This here is similar to those games in a number of ways.

First one - it's made with Unity. It's also as poorly calibrated and as untested as containment project fangames are.
Second off - there's no presentation, no menu. All you get is a start menu, pause menu and a weird inconsistent way of exiting clue-screens. Also no save functionality - though it's not needed, as the game is very short.
Last point being - it's basically free. Keys are floating around seemingly everywhere. Here it's less like SCP game and more like herpes - it's kinda free but no one really wants to get it. And if you did, it's gonna stay in your library forever.

Despite this, The Next Door stands on its own. In its corner. With a dunce hat. And I can't help but feel sorry for it.

The same way I feel sorry for Kanye West. They're both as short and as shallow as they get.

Opening sequence blew me away, though. You see this dialogue happening on one black line on the screen, and immideately assume a role of a... litle sperm. In a "flock" of sperm; a whole current, if you will.
Then, after going in towards what seems like an ovary there's a sudden change.

Your spermmates are now stars. Your background music is now an Apollo radio comm. Your ovary is now the Moon.

That was so bizarre and out of context, like a dream! Sadly, it did not keep up the standard.

Every single puzzle from the get go is bland. It's as easy as: here's a clue, look at it, now go do the thing so it's the same. Floor flip, where the other part or the whole puzzle is located on the ceiling, is an interesting mechanic. But it's not elaborated on - it's present in only two rooms. Which, to be fair, is a solid third of the whole experience.

What tries to spice the blandness up is the color scheme. It tries, but it does not do a good job at it.
Half the game is set in the lighthouse from Matrix: Reloaded. I mean, everything in the background is white. Not ivory-pleasant kind of white. Sharp, eye-shanking rgb255, #ffffff kind of white.
The other half has a mix of night and twilight skies and some artificial light, which does a disservice to the spicing-up goal. It only brings attention to how dull basic tile textures are.

The stew all of this brews into is highly unappetizing. It feels like a dream of someone who had just taken occult studies course and tried to make sense of it all. An after class stomach flu fever dream that can only turn into one thing upon awakening.

Though saying so is a bit too rough. This game is more like a 30 Second to Mars album. It has bright colors and intersting symbols on the cover and it's deep for the sake of being deep. The only difference is - there's no music. There's no game.

But hey, "it's almost free and they put greenlight comments in the final room!", right?
