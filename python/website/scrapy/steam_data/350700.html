About This Game

Red Stone is an online 2.5D Transformation MMORPG which commenced in May 2003
and the most important title of L&K Logic Korea that received great attention
with its remarkable success in both Korea and Japan.

Official Facebook : https://www.facebook.com/LnKREDSTONE

The story of Red Stone begins in the fantasy world of Prandel as players go on
an adventurous quest to search for a mystical red stone that fell from the Celestial World.
During the search, players are able to experience the magical transformation of their characterâs class and role.
For example, a wizard, who specializes in long-range magic attacks,
transforms into a fearless werewolf who specializes in short-range claw attacks.
Red Stoneâs game system focuses on the dynamic and liveliness of the battle as it enhances excitement and fun, allowing players to emerge themselves into the game.
The system also incorporated a unique CP (Charge Point) feature
which players may strategically utilize to increase the fun in their game play.

This is one of those old RPG's that you will either love or hate. It's kinda like trying to jump into something like Ragnarok Online in 2015. I played this way back in beta on game n game or something. Eventually played on OG Planet for a bit. The game is different now. Way more classes and characters. Hello i have played a few mmo's so far, so i know a few things. This game is not for the casuals let's state that right now.
PROS

There's over 600 levels with a rebirthing system. You can use it to reset yourself back to level 1, this in turn gets you more stats and skill points.

Classes they have a some pretty cool classes, there aren't many games that offer some of the classes this game does.

Tamers/summoner's, this class is my favorite so far they can have two summons out as well as two pets. This game has the best pet system that i have found. As you level up, you can put skill points into your stats which upgrades your pets stats. You also get a skill, where you can ride one of your pets to increase your movement speed.

Stat points that's right this game has them. Most games are going complete casual, where you can't build your class how you want. As you level up you get stat points which you can put point's into to increase your characters proformance. You can pick a dps class and build tank, or you can sacrfice stats to increase your luck so you can find better gear, or increase your wisdom to increase exp ammounts.

Drop rates seem good, tons of stuff drops left and right.

CONS

graphic's are a bit out dated, mind you this game came out in 2003 from what i have found. If the gameplay is good the graphic's don't mean much to me.

Genderlock classes, this game did come out 2003. When this was acceptable, but it doesn't mean they can't change it now...

pay to win cash shop, yes you can pay for buff's the costumes you can buy increase stats a bit. Sadly this i can't give them a pass on this. It's an out dated system that shouldn't exist, league of legend/dota showed, you can survive without a pay to win cash shop. So why are they doing this? If anything there elimanting more people, because of the unfair advantages.

Clunky movement, and no v-sync option. This game has some pretty bad movement, at times you will seem to get stuck on nothing. Also the graphic option's are very limited, i can't even put on v-sync so i get screen tearing. I had to use nvidia control panel to do it, i still get screen tearing tho.

CONCLUSION.

Should you play redstone? It depends on if you like old school games, where they gave you freedom of choice. If you grew up in this time of gaming, where bad movement/graphics/pay to win cash shops was a thing then you found a pretty nice game.

However if you never played very many old games, nor grew up in the time i doubt you will like it. There's a lot of turn offs to the modern gamer, but it still doesn't mean you can't try. I say try the game, who knows you may like it. After all it is a free game, what do you have to lose?

Pretty much if you can sit down right now and play the elder scrolls 3 morrowind/diablo 2 and like it, this game should feel right at home with you. If you sit down and you don't like morrowind/diablo 2 there's no hope for you liking this game.
