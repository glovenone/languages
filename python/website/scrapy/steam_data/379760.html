About This Game

Are you skilled enough to guide Force in his quest seeking emotional stableness?

Gravity Error is an indie 2D puzzle/platformer about changing gravity. Guide Force in his quest seeking emotional stableness in a set of levels and avoid being crushed. Solve the puzzles in multiple creative ways.

Features

  • Help Force in his quest seeking emotional stableness.
  • 2D platforming using 4 gravitational directions.
  • Creatively solve levels the way you want.
  • Beautifully composed soundtrack by Agent Whiskers.
  • Challenging levels to make you suffer.
  • Supports 9 languages including: English, Arabic, French, German, Italian, Russian, and more.

What is Gravity Error?

Gravity Error is a short platform puzzle mix. You are playing in rectangular rooms that only fill a screen each, using placeable arrows to influence gravity.

Gameplay
The gameplay consists of the strategy and the action phase, as I will call them. In the strategy phase, you place the arrows. There are four arrows: Up, down, left, right. Each arrow makes gravity pull the protagonist in the direction it shows. You can place them on the whole playfield, with some exceptions. You can't place them into other objects (orbs are exempt from this). You want to collect all orbs and then reach the exit, so you must plan a bit ahead, but only in one level was it actually interesting to figure out how to do it. Many times you have more arrows than you will need.
The game consists of five sets of ten levels each.

Achievements
There are ten achievements that unlock as you progress while collecting all orbs. As the developer currently is moving, he will fix the achievements when he is able to, he says on the forums!

Music
The music is very nice, it's very catchy. The sound effects are sparse and fitting, but of a bit a low quality.

Controls
The controls are working fine, but have two big issues to me. First the protagonist does not steer well. He oversteers easily, running further than he should. This can become tedious later on.
Second the protagonist goes on the 2D plane in the direction of the key you press. Now this might be a matter of taste, I would actually like him to always jump when you press up and accordingly go for the other keys, so for example pressing left is going right when the gravity points up.

Graphics
The graphics are very basic, there are few different graphics. The game feels just like a series of chambers you go through, which it is, and does not build any graphical atmosphere.

Overall you get a small nice game, one I can actually recommend if you got it from a bundle or cheaply. Since you get only around an hour of entertainment until you 100 percented everything (except of course the broken achievement), you should keep that in mind when buying.

Recommended: If you get it cheap
