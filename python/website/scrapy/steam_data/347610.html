About This Game

This 4-in-1 bundle includes 4 Hidden Object games:

Stray Souls: A Dollhouse Story:

Welcome to a town where something that should not exist runs free; where a desperate wife will risk her beating heart to find her husband; and where a terrible secret lies buried behind an orphanage. Search for clues, solve puzzles, and unlock new areas as you visit spine-chilling locations, play stimulating mini-games and locate hidden objects. Stray Souls: Dollhouse Story is more than a game; it's a heart-stopping journey of discovery!

House of 1,000 Doors: The Palm of Zoroaster:

A cursed gem that has left a trail of smoldering bodies in its wake is threatening the residents of the House of 1,000 Doors, prompting the head of the mystical dwelling, Gabriel, to once again summon Kate Reed to its aid. Guide Kate as she searches Tibet, Jerusalem, Madagascar and more for the artifacts that can break the power of the gem, solve a massive array of cleverly designed puzzles and find hundreds of ingeniously hidden objects. Production values that rival the quality of theatrically released animated 3D films and superbly performed voiceovers will draw you into this story of supernatural intrigue, unbounded adventure and sweeping scope. The House needs you. Will you answer its call?

Dark Strokes: Sins of the Fathers:

When Evil comes for the people you love, what will you do? That moment of reckoning has arrived for Ethan Blake, a young man whose bride-to-be was snatched up in the blink of an eye by a terrifying menace known as the Faceless Ones.
Guide Ethan as he explores a decimated city, interacts with its remaining inhabitants, searches for clues that will lead him to his beloved Clair, and peels back the layers of a chilling supernatural mystery that began with the stroke of a paint brush and will end in a dramatic confrontation near a fiery abyss.

Eternal Journey: New Atlantis:

Travel from the deepest trenches of the Atlantic Ocean to the awe-inspiring wonders of Mars as you solve the greatest archeological puzzle known to man: the origin of Atlantis. Through stunning production values and cunningly designed puzzles, Eternal Journey: New Atlantis will draw you into an experience unlike any other hidden object adventure.

Posted: 14 May
I'm going to group the games together for their mechanics until the end of the review because they're very similar/the same. You have all the classic types of puzzles in here: memory games, spin-and-match, etc. For the most part, they don't seem to be very challenging but are time consuming. They're still fun (besides a few puzzle types I don't really care for in any game), but you may hit "Skip" on a few just to keep moving.

The graphics are pretty decent -- mostly standard fare for HOGs. Don't expect excellent quality, however, as most HOG companies focus more on puzzles and story than graphics. Some computer animations will look like circa 2000s technology, but that never really bothered me. The voice acting was pretty decent for the most part, but there were a few characters who made me cringe a little.

House of 1000 Doors:
~I enjoyed the different settings you go through and the different themes. The story was okay but the ending was obviously cut off. You're promised an explanation and then the credits roll. The end. I've read that there's a collector's edition of this game that has the real ending and a few bonus levels. I was shocked that Alawar would do such a thing, but I guess they can do whatever they want with their games.

Eternal Journey:
~I played this one last because I didn't think I'd like it and I was totally wrong. This particular game was my favorite of the four and is easily in my top five favorite HOGs. I've never considered myself big on sci-fi so when I found myself really getting into the story, I was surprised. I wouldn't call it realistic sci-fi but I wouldn't call most HOGs realistic either. Even if you're not big into this particular genre, give it a shot.

Dark Strokes:
~Yes, I giggled at the title for a few minutes. Yes, I cringed at one particular voice actor who just couldn't act anyway but bored. Yes, I spotted the ending halfway through. I didn't hate the game but it was my least favorite. I don't normally harp on about a poor plot, but this one was legitimately bad. Every time something happened, I spotted a plot hole that could've been fixed with a little tweaking. I don't want to spell out spoilers, so I'll say don't bother unless you don't really care about that stuff.

Stray Souls:
~This one was weird. I really liked the dark atmosphere and design, but the story was so strange. The heroine, too, was very gullible to...everything. She liked to just accept things at face value with little coercing or argument, even when a desperate person would go, "Wait, what? No way." The "twist" about the bad guy was so obvious I wouldn't even call it a twist. I enjoyed enough things in this one that it's not the worst of the bundle, so it sits above Dark Strokes (teehee) and below House of 1000 Doors.

A bundle of 4 HOGs containing:

House of 1000 Doors: The Palm of Zoroaster
Nice graphics, the HOGs scenes are beautiful but the difficulty is very low. 46 morphing objects to find. The story is fantasy style, with magic and fire spells and a very classic construction. Also it's a sequel and I didn't play the previous game(s?) so it felt a bit awkward. The characters and the cutscenes are nice. The ending was a total let down though: the cutscene lasted 3 seconds! It's the game I liked the least in the bundle but it's still a good game and I do recommend it.

Eternal Journey: New Atlantis
You're an archeologist exploring an underwater city, then end up exploring the future. This one is more original than the usual run of the mill: you'll dive underwater, repair solar panels, use space suits etc. There's also 18 collectibles to find and the map is very handy. The graphics and the music are nice and the future is clichéd but well done. The mini games are very good. I liked it a lot. The best game in the bundle IMO, the most innovative and the Sci-Fi setting is pretty rare within HOGs.

Stray Souls: A Dollhouse Story
Creepy and so well done that I've already replayed it a second time. There's a lot of good ideas in this one, like a live doll clone of Number Nine, a creepy doll house, with creepy dolls, a creepy little boy and 36 morph objects to collect! If you like horror theme then you'll surely like it. The only con is the res that seems lower than the other games, so the image is a bit blurry. The second best game in the bundle IMHO.

Dark Strokes: Sins of the Fathers
I had already played that one previously but I enjoyed replaying it. Graphics and characters are gorgeous, music is okay. Didn't like the mini games much, tedious and not original at all. The HOG scenes offer a bit of challenge, being dark and cluttered. Again there's a boat model and you find little dolls to fill it but it only unlocks videos, no small featurettes with the dolls like in Stray Souls. The videos you unlock are rather bland. And no special collectibles to find. A dark setting but all in all it's rather classic. But I liked how the story unfolded and how the player is guided toward the next scenes. Third best game in the bundle.

Conclusion: all the games are good and you won't be deceived! Each game lasted 4 hours (5 for a leisure playthrough) so it makes this bundle really worthy.

After the 5 in 1 bundle, Alawar released anoth batch of hidden objects, though this time, they released only three new games on Steam. Indeed, House of 1000 Doors: The Palm of Zoroaster was already released as a Collector's Edition. It means that for anyone already owning it, it's not a complete surprise. However, for the rest, it's a nice addition.

House of 1000 Doors: The Palm of Zoroaster

Kate Reed is called back to the House of 1000 Doors: a gem spread a plague in it and every resident, particulary Gabriel, is in a grave danger...

You understand, this game is the second in a series of four (the first is also on Steam) but you don't need to play the previous one to understand the universe.

While the game is pretty standard in terms of gameplay, what's great in that game is the universe itself: you're travelling between different worlds, through the house. And this time, you'll learn more about Gabriel, the guardian of the house. The graphics are very good too, which is pleasant.

For more information, read this review: http://steamcommunity.com/id/vicrabb/recommended/302120

Eternal Journey: New Atlantis

After an accident, you're waking up into the future, thanks to new technologies and because your knowledge was important. You don't know what happened to your fiancé, making you sad about your new life. Sent to a mission in space, you're crashing into a planet... How will you manage to save yourself? And will you discovered what happened to a previous crew of the base?

When the game is showing clearly a sci-fi adventure in space, you don't understand why you're beginning in the present, with an underwater experience. However, given the twist at the beginning of the story, I feel like this part could have been avoided. However, the rest of the game is very faithful to the sci-fi theme. You're arriving into an abandoned base and your journey will be full of dangers, though at the end, a familiar face will appear. The gameplay isn't innovative as it's the same as any other hidden games.

In other words, you search for items, you use them, you clear hidden objects scenes and you solve puzzles. Everything is pretty easy. You can search also memory cards, explaining about the future. And with all the video cards around, you can also know more about life on New Atlantis and the secrets hidden there.

However, what I loved with this game is the graphics, very sci-fi, with some sounds making me think about Doom.

Anyway, it's my favorite among the three (I don't count House of 1000 Doors, as it's already a favorite of mine) new hidden objects in this bundle. If anything, I wouldn't mind to see this one as a collector's edition.

Dark Strokes: Sins of the Fathers

Ethan and Clair are visiting the young man's father but she's kidnapped by The Faceless One. Ethan, decided to save her from them, is making his way into a decimated city. However, all the kidnapped persons are tied to a tragedy and the death of a boy on a boat...

The game is also a good one: the plot is really well-written and I didn't realize the truth before nearing the end. While it's shocking to discover who the Faceless Ones are, the title make a reference to Ethan's father and to the boy's one. The graphics are also good, as you're navigating through a city that is diving into darkness progressively. The gameplay is still the standard one: hidden objects to find, puzzles to solve, etc..

Stray Souls: A Dollhouse Story

Your husband is in danger and you need to save him. However, the town seems to be the home of a terrible spirit and the orphanage may be the key to stop it...

Stray Souls has an interesting plot: your husband being the "damsel" in distress, a young sad boy, a sad clown and a mysterious woman, everything related to your man's past. Besides, what's good is that you have a little companion: a doll that can talk and can help you to discover the truth behind everything. Besides, the concept of cleansing some rooms of the evil is very nice as you can discover new items in them.

However, the gameplay is still the same as any other hidden object game: find items, use them, clear hidden objects scenes and solve very easy puzzles. The hidden objects scenes are kinda reminiscent of the early era: with objects being in the same tone of color as the background or with a very small size.

The graphics are what I like generally: a town plunged in darkness, with everything around making you understand that evil is watching you. However, I wished that perhaps, the locations were a little more original than the mandatory cemetery or police station.

The soundtrack was also good.

I enjoyed the game, that's a fact but I feel like something is missing. I don't know, maybe more interactions for the doll?

Anyway, this is also a good pick for the bundle.

Conclusion

This bundle is way more worthy than the 5 in 1. I can't judge for the 6 in 1 as it's a new release and that I'm in the first game. Anyway, for the price, it's a very enjoyable experience, as I wasn't disappointed in any of the three games that I discovered. And as I was already convinced by House of 1000 Doors, I can easily recommend this bundle, more than the previous one. Every game has good graphics, good sountrack, a gameplay, while being standard, polished without too much brain frying.

Any lover of HOG can buy this bundle and any person wanting to discover the genre can too, as there are no "weak" games like in the 5 in 1. Go ahead!

This is a decent bundle of four Hidden object games. Fun and challenging if you like those types of games. Consisting of a storyline with side mini-games and several avenues of searches to complete puzzles and goals. As is standard with hidden object games, it consists of a lot of rooms or areas with several objects to collect, as well as puzzles and gadgets that you need to find parts for to activate or build so you can move to another level, room or location. Search areas may be inside structures, outside, in walls and through alleys. The artwork looks nice with great background sound effects, some background annimations and music. If you stop playing, it auto saves to the point that you left the game, so you will start back where you left off. At this time, they offer no achievements or badge other than the cards through steam.
