About This Game

Victor Frankenstein, an old friend and a brilliant scientist has managed to resurrect the dead! However the border between life and death is fragile and Victor has learned the hard way that this balance should not be tempered with...

Face terrible monsters, overcome obstacles and solve clues. Save unhappy creatures from being a victim of an extremely inhuman experiment and stop the baron Igor.

Help restore the course of nature and save Victor and his wife, Elizabeth!
A new look into a classic story
Solve fantastic puzzles
Stunning 2k HD graphics

Frankenstein: Master of Death is the newest title from JetDogs to make it to Steam and is a nice addition to the hidden object catalog here. An alternate take on the Frankenstein story, the premise is interesting albeit a bit hokey at the same time. Granted, HOGs are not known for their great storylines as they're often just a base to build a game upon, but that's usually enough for me. The time period offers us an amazing setting for gorgeously drawn backgrounds and artwork, my favorite aspect of these games.

Keys, keys and more keys. A good portion of the game involves finding different keys for lock boxes, doors, and just about everything else. Some open puzzles while others give access to new rooms and areas of the map. While it gets rather tiresome opening door after box after door, once I realized that's basically the shtick of the gameplay I was able to sit back and enjoy it for what it is. Fortunately, there are plenty of puzzles to play through, but nothing too difficult or mind bending. The hidden object scenes are interesting in that many include mini-puzzles within them as well, a neat addition that you don't see very often. As for the scenes themselves, they're nicely drawn with no deceptively hidden items, just challenging enough to keep your eyes sharp.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=407653953
If I had to describe Frankenstein: Master of Death in one word it would be "pleasant." There are no leaps in logic needed to progress, no puzzles that completely boggle the mind, and nothing about the presentation that I feel was grating or annoying. That said, If you're interested in something a little more challenging, you might want to look elsewhere. But if a casual experience with some light puzzle solving is what you're after, this may be the HOG for you. Although on the pricier side for its length (it only took me 3 hours to 100% the game), full Steam integration adds a bit of value back into the package. If nothing else, it's worth keeping on your radar for a nice sale.

Bottom Line - Recommended for casual gaming fans looking for a relaxing and enjoyable experience.

I'm sorry to say that I'm not recommending "Frankenstein: Master of Death." Looking at the individual elements of the game as stand-alone things, most of the pieces are OK. But, when looking at the game as a whole, then the thing edges into the Not Recommended arena.

On the plus side, the graphics are good.

In the middle, the puzzles and HOGs are very easy (even for the genre) and the voice acting is tolerable.

For the slightly negatives, the story is very weak (again, even for the genre), the game is short (3.9 hours for me to finish it), a couple of items in the HOGs were mis-identified (cow <> antelope, forceps <> toenail cutters). There also seems to be some kind of problem with some of the Achievements. In my case, the game didn't give me the "Expert" Achievement (which we're supposed to get by playing in Advanced mode on Hard -- which I did).

But, the things that hurt the game the most are:

- An awful lot of the blocking items throughout the game are variations on keys. Key after key after key after key. Yes, you have to find the crowbar or something. But, a very heavy trend is that those things you need are in a case or behind a door that needs its own special key.

- The chronological anachronisms strewn throughout it. The story is a variation of Frankenstein and takes place around the year 1800 (give or take). Yet, here and there in both the scenery and the HOGs are modern objects. The big ones in the scenery are what look like a WWII airplane and a VW Beetle car. There's no reason for this. In addition to those, here are some of the other anachronisms I noted: modern fire extinguisher, 1950's era toy robot, dynamite (not invented by Alfred Nobel until 1863), aluminum step ladder with plastic parts, movies, gramaphone (1887), digital numbers on a neon sign, reel-to-reel tape recorder, 1960's era camera, rubber dishwashing gloves, incandescent light bulb, and a foil tube of glue (complete with a recycling symbol and volume in milliliters).

So, I'm sorry to say I'm not recommending the game. Definitely not at the list price of almost $9. But, I'm still not recommending it at the dirt cheap price I got it at (less than $2).
