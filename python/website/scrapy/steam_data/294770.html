About This Game

The year is 2104. Mars has been colonized and has decided it’s time for independence from Earth. Tensions rise until finally war is declared between the two factions. After weeks of fierce battles and heavy losses – just as humanity is in its weakest and most divided state - a cunning alien force emerges from the depths of space and threatens to take over the solar system.

The two sides must now band together, forming the Legions of Iron to fight this new enemy force. From the makers of the award-winning Imperium Galactica 1 and 2 games, discover a powerful space-opera like RTS game.  

Can you stand up to the challenge?

  • Epic real-time space battles waged with waves of fighters and massive battleships
  • Stories and random events keep you busy as you climb the ranks
  • Research and development of technology will either help you be successful or be your demise. Do you specialize or diversify… choose wisely.
  • Prepare to test your cunning, strategic skills… collect resources, harvest, manage your inventory and your budgets carefully because your enemies will come at you with a godlike force.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, Vista, 7
    • Processor: 1 GHz
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: 3D graphics card compatible with DirectX
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
    • Sound Card: Windows DirectSound compatible sound card
    • Additional Notes: Windows compatible mouse and keyboard. Local multiplayer only. There are no servers.

I love this game. I have loved it ever since it came out in 2002. Yes it's an older game. Yes it has bugs. But these can be worked around with a simple amount of research (like the sound error that some people get). And no it does not have widescreen support. You'll have to enjoy the game with either the black borders or a stretched image, depending on your computer's settings. Is it inconvenient? Yes. Is it game breaking? not in the slightest.

For a 2002 game, the game play is great, and the graphics are great (for a game of its era) and the explosions are great even for modern day games.

It's easy to get frustrated when you fail a mission, many people haven't quite gotten an understanding for the game yet and blame their mission failure on bugs. I've beaten the GOG version several times and I will surely play through the steam version just as many times. The replayability of this game is endless to me.

This is not a short game to play through, many missions will take hours to complete. You start out in the Earth-Mars war, you even get to choose who's side you want to fight for. These missions are introductory missions, teaching you how to play the game, planetary management, and research, and the levels get progressively harder as you go. In some missions, money management is absolutely CRUCIAL.

Battles can be quite intense at times and definitely fun to watch.

If you enjoy Space RTS games, this is definitely the best. Yes it's old, but its fun, very fun and a must have. Just make sure if you get errors, you look for the fix before rageposting about how "bad" of a game it is.

Oh and, in the first missions, make sure to press the F1 key (it will inform you of this) as all the information for controls you need will be listed there
