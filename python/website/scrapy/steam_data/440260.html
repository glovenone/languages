About This Game

Temporal Temple is a fast paced arcade-platformer-shooter where you survive in a temple for as long as you can.

Key features:
  • Six playable characters
  • Eleven weapons
  • Eighteen unique powerups
  • A level editor

How long can you last?

Product received for free
Full disclosure: I worked on this game as a lead tester and did some design work on levels, characters, the game's title and general balancing. I am also friends with Dlastons, the creator.

Having seen this project grow all the way from a simple exercise in learning Gamemaker (back when this game went by the simpler name 'Arena') over the course of about a year and a half into Temporal Temple has been nothing short of an amazing experience, which, in my opinion, resulted in an equally amazing and enjoyable result. Needless to say, this review is pretty much going to be positive regardless, but I'll do my best to give a rundown on gameplay and a bit of a reflection on the development process.

Temporal Temple plays similarly to a game like Super Crate Box, with rogue-lite features inspired by various roguelikes the developers enjoyed i.e. The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth. The goal is simply to survive for as long as you can, bringing the game's core objective back to a simpler, points based experience i.e. Space Invaders or Pacman to encourage competition amongst friends and give them stories to share amongst one another of their best runs (multiplayer was unfortunately out of the question. However, I believe this was a good compromise).

So obviously the purpose of the game is to get the highest score you can, with the basic mechanics being a sort of single-room action platformer; you can run, jump and shoot within confined levels. What keeps this gameplay from becoming stale is the presence of three factors: unlockable characters, items and enemy scaling/intensity. Enemy scaling/intensity is a no-brainer, the further you get, the more powerful your enemies become, the higher the reward for defeating them. Unlockable characters spice things up by presenting different stats, special abilities and different starting loadouts. The big drawcard of Temporal Temple for me, however, is the item system.

Over the course of a run, enemies can drop items amongst their regular drop of coins that can alter your movement, damage output, HP and several other factors. For example, there's an item that halves gravity amongst some of the more interesting ones. The coins in the game function as both your score and your access to HP as well as new items and weapons during a run; There is an 'item shop' with 3 randomly chosen items that you can choose to purchase, a health shrine that will heal a certain amount of your HP for a price, or a weapons chest, similar to the boxes from CoD: Zombies that will provide you with a random weapon for a certain amount of points.

So that's the general design of the game out of the way, the 'basics' if you will. If you're wondering what really makes it all click for me, it's two major factors: Firstly, you're never out of the action to access item stores, health shrines or weapons chests. Due to the single room nature of the game, you're always in the action, and in some levels or long-lasting runs, simply deciding whether you want to go from one end of the room to the other for items presents a huge risk/reward factor in and of itself. As a result, the game maintains a wonderful pace that gradually intensifies to keep runs satisfying and interesting.
The second factor I praise lies simply in how this game is presented regarding movement; I feel this is something that goes without saying in a lot of reviews, but to me movement and environment traversal is key to how a game feels and controls, and this game both looks and controls excellently. Movement feels fluid, instant and fast, both due to the response of your inputs and the wonderful character animation by Cake. Obviously this sort of thing is the groundwork of this game, but I adore the fact that Dlastons and Cake got it so right.

Honestly, I can't recommend this game enough. It's cheaper than a cup of coffee and it was an absolute pleasure to be involved in the development process. I can't wait to see what Dlastons will produce in the future, because this is a great start.

P.S. Try my level "They're Coming Outta The GD Walls!" on Hard Mode (as any character, but if you're ballsy try Bone Boy) if you're looking for a good challenge ;)
