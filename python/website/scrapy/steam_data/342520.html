About This Game

BoxesWithGuns is a 2D neon vertex endless shooter game. Try to survive as long as possible by avoiding collisions with the enemies that spawn. One game equals the length of the background music track playing. Collect dollars and customize your spaceship by adding new boxes and weapons. Upgrade existing weapons to increase your chances of survival. For temporary boosts, power ups spawn randomly.

Based on the current music playing in the background, the attached weapon systems get enabled or disabled. The blur and bloom effects and pixel destruction are influenced by the music as well.

Very basic twin stick shooter with somewhat intriguing concept yet horrible execution to say at least.

Gameplay
The game has one and only play mode. You shoot waves of squares that simply fly around you in order to gain points which you then spend on guns and gun upgrades inbetween the waves. There's an aspect of building the ship. You need to expand the size of your ship for every new gun you want to attach making you bigger target and sacrifice your movement speed in exchange for better firepower. If enemy hits your base section, it's game over. If it hits expanded parts, you lose only the guns attached to that part. This is interesting on paper yet you'll find out right after few games that it's more efficient to just keep your ship small with one gun you continually upgrade.
Concept of buying upgrades and new guns is also very much undermined by the fact all guns are very weak against the enemies so you're basically waiting for power-up so you can kill something.

Graphics
Visual presentation of this game is at least atrocious. Every graphical part of this game seems what you would expect from very first InDev version of a game. Your ship is square, your enemies are square. Background seems to be squares. These graphics would be low for a flash or a mobile game. Everything feels lazy and undone. This theme is acompanied by visual effects that are beyond annoying. Half of the time you won't even see what's happening on screen. And this is game focused on precision. Imagine playing Counter-Strike with screen going bright white every few sec... oh wait.

Overall
The game is sold for a very low price of 2€ yet you won't get any content worth that much. If this was free flash game on a site like Newgrounds or Kongregate, it would still have a low score at best. The one positive thing is soundtrack which is very catchy but artists provided the music are not even credited? Shame. Thumb down.
