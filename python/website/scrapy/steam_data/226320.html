About This Game

Marvel Heroes 2016 is the Marvel MMO ARPG that you have been waiting for! Play as Iron Man, Star-Lord, Thor, Wolverine, Hulk, Spider-Man, Captain America and many more as they team up to try and stop Doctor Doom from devastating the world with the power of the Cosmic Cube. Marvel Heroes 2016 features a deep leveling system unique to each Super Hero, diverse locations set across the expansive Marvel Universe, a robust crafting system, action-packed player-versus-player (PvP) and much more. Marvel Heroes is a free-to-play Massively Multiplayer Action RPG for PC created by David Brevik, the visionary behind Diablo and Diablo 2 with an extensive original story crafted by Marvel super-scribe Brian Michael Bendis.


About the Author: The story of Marvel Heroes 2016 is crafted by comic book super scribe Brian Michael Bendis (Ultimate Spider-Man, New Avengers, House of M, Secret Invasion, Avengers vs X-Men and many more).


About the Story: The Cosmic Cube is a device of untold power capable of changing the very fabric of reality at the whim of its possessor. The brilliant but malevolent Dr. Doom has the Cube, and with it he aims to reshape the world in his own image. Determined to succeed where the Cube's previous wielders have failed, Doom has lined up villainous allies to prevent the super-heroes from interfering. Can you unravel Doctor Doom's sinister plan and stop him before it's too late?
Marvel Heroes 2016 combines core gameplay elements of Diablo with the expansive library of characters from the Marvel Universe.

Key Features


  • Engage in Action-RPG-style gameplay, but in an MMO world.
  • Team up online with your friends, or play solo.
  • Randomly generated environments are different every time you play.
  • Collect and play as your favorite Marvel Super Heroes including Iron Man, Thor, Black Widow, Captain America, Star-Lord, Wolverine, Hulk, Storm, Dr. Strange, and many others.
  • Choose from a list of fan favorites from the Avengers, X-Men, Guardians of the Galaxy, Fantastic Four, and every corner of the Marvel Universe.
  • Build your character using a library of spectacular powers appropriate to your hero.
  • Play through hours of story content, or try out many of our other numerous game modes such as X-Defense, Holo-Sim, and more!
  • Marvel Heroes 2016 features the first ever raid in an Action-RPG, gather friends and take on Surtur!
  • Customize your character with a library of costumes from Marvel’s rich history.
  • Summon other heroes to fight along side you with Team-Ups, such as Falcon, Firestar, Drax, Gamora, and Groot.
  • Discover an endless variety of gear to increase your hero's power.
  • Explore the Marvel Universe in a story crafted by comic book super-scribe Brian Michael Bendis.
  • Visit famous locations from the Marvel Universe, from Avengers Tower to Mutant Town to the Savage Land.
  • Smash through hordes of iconic Marvel enemies, such as the dreaded Purifiers and the legions of HYDRA.
  • Face off against dozens of super-villains.
And It's All free-to-play With Marvel Heroes!

This is a Diablo like APRG with superheroes. If you like those two things, you will probably like this game.

How free is it?

You get all content (story mode, instances and raids) for free. Gazillion (the developer) also hands out a lot of stuff for free at irregular intervalls.

What do you get when you start?

You get one free hero unlock the first day. The second day you play you get 400 splinters (the in-game currency), which is enough for one or two more heroes. After that you get a splinter every 8 minutes on the average, when you play actively (not sitting in a hub). You use these to unlock more heroes and team-ups.

You also get a free team-up in ch 3 of the story, and you can unlock another one easily (in under an hour) once you reach lvl 60 on one hero. Go to the MH site, the Achievements forum has intructions, search for the "Pirate Deadpool" thread.

How do they make money?

Selling skins (hero costumes), more storage and lotteries with skins as the top prizes.

You can also buy heroes and team-ups for real money if you are impatient.

Is there Pay-to-Win?

No. And there is no way you can tell in-game if someone has spent money on the game or not. Many of the people you will see wearing non-starter costumes actually got them for free as well, through prestiging (starting over from level one) where you get a new standard costume. 3 of these can be combined to make a new randomly selected costume.

This game might or might not be your cup of tea, but it's free, so if you like this type of game, give it a try!

If you are looking for a great Free 2 Play MMOARPG, Marvel Heroes 2016 is an amazing choice. It's like Marvel's Ultimate Alliance meets Diablo 2. Not only is it a great game, it also has an amazing community and dev team to match. A portion of the dev team are very active on the forums and communicate with the members of the community as well as play the game themselves.

There's currently a nice mix of 57 playable characters such as Spiderman, Dr. Strange,Moon Knight, members of the Xmen, Avengers, Guardians of the Galaxy, Fantastic Four, Thunderbolts(Venom, Green Goblin, Elektra, etc.), and more! At the end of this month the 58th playable, Ultron, will be entering the game. You can test out all the characters to level 10 to see which one you want to unlock first. As you play the game you will earn in game currency which will allow you to unlock playable characters as well as other things like team ups and pets. You can level your playable characters all the way to Level 60..but just because you reach 60 doesn't stop progression! The Omega System is there to keep it going. Here you can continue to customize and develop your characters even further! Want a different look for your playable characters? There's currently 423 costumes, which is a whole hell of a lot of costumes! Costumes you mainly get from purchasing for real cash, HOWEVER each character you can unlock (just by playing), comes with a default costume. Once you reach level 60 you can choose to start back at level 1 and take them back to level 60(prestige). You can prestige them each numerous times, and each time you do it gives you a default costume. You can take 3 costumes to the crafter and blend them together for any random costume that in the game. Not only that but there is a small chance to get a costume as a loot drop as well. As you might notice some of the older costumes might not look as good as some of the newer ones, but don't worry as they have already started giving visual updates to them. Emma Frost is about to get one this month and it looks amazing.


Not only are there currently 57 playables, there's also 49 team ups that you can get to fight along side your playable heroes, most of which can be unlocked just by playing the game. There's also a slew of pets, a total of 45 pets so far, which include Bamf!, Doop, Spider Ham and a mini Blackbird! There's also tons of achievements where you can get special costumes, team ups, and other kool stuff.

There is one thing I do suggest you buying if you love collecting lots of loot.. stash tabs. This is one item that you can't normally get from just playing, you need to purchase them with real cash(you get a couple to start and you can get one this month though for free just buy playing). If you love the game it's definitely worth purchasing and showing the support and help fund the continued development of the game.

As far as content is concerned...This game has 3 different Patrol zones(Midtown Manhattan, Industrail City, and Secret Invasion) with 2 difficulties that you can play solo, group with up to 4 others, or just join people to complete missions and battle lots of villians in the Marvel Universe. There's an Age Of Ultron zone where you can team up with 4 other people to clear missions and battle Ultron. This mode will soon be getting an even greater difficulty. There are 13 boss terminals with 3 difficulty levels in which you can run solo or group up with 4 other people. They just recently released the Secret Invasion terminal which features Super Skrull and they are about to release the 14th, which is an Ultron terminal around the end of this month. There's a 10 chapter story mode that takes you to places like the Savage Lands, Asgard, and Latveria. They are about to introduce Dynamic Level Scaling to the game where you will never outlevel the zone you are in and you can play with your friends no matter what level they are. They also have 3 side story mission zones known as One Shots where you can do solo or up to 4 other players to take on Map Ape, Mr Hyde, Lizard, Kraven, and the Red Skull! Not to long ago, they also released the Danger Room which randomly generates secarios with different difficulty levels. You can solo them or group with up to 4 others(Cosmic difficulty is heavily aimed at group play). Marvel Heroes is also the first MMOARPG to introduce Raids! There are currently 2 different 10 man raids, one that takes place in Muspelheim where you battle the might Surtur(2 difficulties with different mechanics), and the other takes you Marvel Heroes version of the Axis story line where you battle a jacked up Red Skull/Onslaught(currently one difficulty). They will also be introducing a 5 man version of the Muspelheim raid soon. There's also 2 older modes that will be getting an overhaul down the road, the SHIELD Holo Sim and X Defense modes. Currently Holo Sim is a mode where you can take on waves of enemies that escalating difficulty that can be done solo or with one other person. Currently X Defense is aimed at group play for up to 5 players. Here you battle waves of enemies in the X Mansion to see how far you can go.

They have a good variety of weeky alternating events which includes one event that takes you into Limbo to fight N'astirh. Besides these events there's also Tournament with various visual prizes. Every few months they put on special events. For example, May was a month long Civil War event that that ran with the Civil War movie, and this month we are celebrating 3 years of Marvel Heroes!! Each week has something planned and you can earn cake slices to get get a free stash tab, hero ticket, 2 team ups, a potted Groot pet, chests that contain the best items in the game, and other stuff as well!!

As you might read from some reviews there are bugs that come with each patch. Obviously if you play online games you know this already, it's not going to be any different anywhere else. Also the game has had it's share of performance issues, mainly in busy Patrol zones but the devs are working constantly to improve the overall performance of the game. The launch was really rough and unplayable for some and MH has come extremely far since then. You may see people posting they can't launch the game or there's an error can be fixed simply by verifying they integrity of the game cache or running steam as an admin.. or if you run the regular client repairing the files or running the MH client as an admin. Another thing I wanted to mention character balancing. Balancing over 50 characters can't be an easy thing to do. Some devs can't do it with 6 characters, imagine having to do it with over 50.. so yes, some characters are stronger than others. However, the devs put forth effort each week to do the best they can at trying to balance the huge roster and taking in lots of players feedback. You also may see people complain here or on the MH forums that there's not enough content. As you can see above there's actually a good amount of zones and things to do in game. Often times when people are complaining about not enough content, it's not because there's not new or enough content but it's not content they wanted or preferred.. there's a big difference. I'm not joking when I say this, but new content when added to the game you will still see people saying we don't get new content. Like I mentioned, it's not that there's not new content, it's just not content they wanted or preferred.

So as you can see the game isn't perfect, no game is. So expect things not to go perfect each week. The pros of this game outweigh the cons by far.Do I recommend this game? Hell yeah I do! I think this game is awesome and we have an absolutely amazing community which includes the dev team. We do have some trolls.. but what game doesn't. Some people just want to watch the world burn.

What are you waiting for? Download the game and try it out for yourself.

From the original boring wasteland that was this game three years ago to this gem of an ARPG, Marvel Heroes hits so many of the right notes when it comes to fun, fast, engaging, and ultimately satisfying gameplay.

My first experience with Marvel Heroes was their introduction at PAX East 2013. Like most, I was awe-struck by the promises of an mmo, isometric, loot hunt set in the Marvel Universe. Down foes with Cyclops' laser eyes? Bounce Captain America's shield between baddies? Yes please; count me in.

And in I was with my Starter Pack pre-order, that is until the game's release. Maybe it was the bright lights, raised staged, or Ms. Marvel's cosplayer, but my ten minutes with the beta was enough to convince me to buy-in. Then June 2013 rolls around, and instead of seeing my childhood heroes brought to life in adorable, isometric, diablo-esque fashion, I'm greeted with painfully slow progress, a static, lifeless environment, with not a lot to do. I could swing around as Spider-Man and not much else, until now.

Perhaps I have the 3-year Anniversary Event to thank, but all that time has made a huge difference for Marvel Heroes. It feels like a game transformed. There is so much that is not only right with this game, but innovative. Everything you do contributes to your overall power in some way: leveling a new hero, playing an old one, bringing along a team-up hero, feeding a pet unwanted loot, donating rather than selling to vendors, exploring, passing cosmic trials. My personal favorite? Synergies.

Synergies are account-wide stat and affix bonuses earned by playing with different heroes. Rather than feeling beholden to one "Main," you're encouraged to experiment with new heroes. Best of all, synergy buffs make thematic sense in the Marvel Universe. Level up Spidey and you'll be harder to hit thanks to a +4% dodge rating. Level up the Hulk and you've got 6% more health. That may not seem like a lot, but consider activating 10 synergies at the same time and the benefits start to add up.

Likewise, each new hero you bring to level cap increases all experience gains in the future (up to +200%), making subsequent heroes that much easier to level. Even if you don't reach the level 60 cap, bonuses are awarded at levels 30, 50 and 60. While your level 60 Silver Surfer nets you +5% experience, even your middling Black Cat is helping with +3%. And that's the general theme of the game: all of your actions are rewarded.

Working in tandem with your heroes' powers and synergies is the "Omega Program". Regardless of whether you are playing a new hero or the same hero over and over, all experience earned also counts towards unlocking Omega points. The "Omega" system allows you to spend unlocked points on 14 sub-sections in exchange for additional account-wide boosts (think Skyrim's node system), allowing you to tailor your strengths the way you like to play. Like your power specializations, your Omega points can be reset and respent at any time.

But what if bouncing around a roster of ~56 super heroes doesn't interest you. You can always just level the same hero multiple times through the Prestige system. Granting more bragging rights than tangible rewards (besides an extra default costume), the Prestige system actually changes the color of your in-game name for everyone to see your dedication to that specific hero. A white or green name may indicate someone's fleeting interest, an orange or red name may mean they are currently playing a favored hero, and a yellow name exemplifies undying devotion. Earning a yellow name, also known as Cosmic prestige, takes 25 times longer than usual to level, affording the player unparalleled expertise. Green, Blue, Purple, Orange, and Red, on the other hand, all take as long as the standard 1-60 route, which is to say not long at all. A savy player will stack bonuses, buffs, and knowledge of the story-mode in their favor, cutting their 1-60 time to as little as 30 minutes (mind you, that is only for the most dedicated players. I managed to get my time down to 90).

Along with heroes and their relevant account-wide synergies, you can also have a Team-Up hero. Also known as TU's, these heroes act as AI-controlled escorts that can attack, taunt or heal depending on their available powers. And much like your own character, they have their own gear and abilities for you to spec. Not in the mood for a companion? Then change your TU's specialization to an "Away" spec which provides you and your party with extra damage, healing over time, or any other number of advantages. All of this and I have yet to mention to loot, and there is lots of it.

In direct opposition to some of the more horrible design trends/flaws in gaming of the past few years, Marvel Heroes throws loot at you, from everywhere and all the time. My past week with the game has made me all but forget Diablo 3 at release, or all of The Division (that dumpster fire of a game). You're not slogging through uninspired incursions for one chance at one piece of loot that has three layers of RNG stacked against. No. Instead, everything you find is either hero-specific gear or all-accessible gear.

And no matter what you find, you're always upgrading. Always trying new builds and new pieces of gear, because respeccing is free, just like the multiple specs you can have on each hero. But even gear you don't want is useful, serving as a donation to grow the in-game crafter or enchanter, allowing those vendors to expand their selection.

And if all of that doesn't get you, then how about a pet system with pets that are actually useful. More than just a cosmetic tag-along a la Path of Exile, these pets will happily suck up those unwanted drops (literally, it's called a cybernetics vacuum). Vacuumed items are tallied according to their rarity until you hit that rarities threshold. Give your pet a few hundred common items, and they'll give you an additional 8% health. Give them 180 cosmic items and they'll increase all your powers by one. And if you don't like the bonuses your pet is providing, your crafter can help reroll any number of them (at the price of having to vacuum up loot of that rarity again). Best of all, if you happen to get lucky and find a new pet that you love, the crafter will swap the boosts between them.

I'm not sure who to thank, whether it be Gazillion Entertainment or Secret Identity Studios, but the developers have been making all the right decisions by taking an active interest in raising everyone's in-game quality of life. So how do these guys make money?! While Marvel Heroes is free-to-play, the one bottleneck is Stash space. Though a couple of extra tabs can be unlocked for free, and hero-specific Stash tabs come in hero bundles (like the Civil War bundle that was on sale for $2.50), you will eventually have to make the choice between 'travelling light' or giving in to the gamer hoarder in you. Some might argue that unlocking heroes can be seen as another pay wall, but even the average player will have no problem unlocking random heroes at a reasonable pace.

Regardless, and this is coming from a life-long gamer that detests F2P/P2W/IAP monetization garbage, there is enough enjoyable gameplay here that whatever money you do choose to spend on Marvel Heroes will be money well spent. Now if you don't mind, I have a newly unlocked Scarlet Witch that has been calling my name.

With all of the P2W games out there it is a relief to find something that is truly Free To Play. I've only paid 2.49 into this game so far and I've already unlocked 12 heroes and haven't had time to max all of my characters to 60 as of yet. The community is extremely helpful and the devs interact quite often.

Before you jump into the game I'd suggest doing some research on the free characters that you have to choose from (which I didn't do). This would make your start a lot more effective to learn about the characters before you choose your first to level past level 10. You can play all of the characters to level 10 but that doesn't really tell you what the characters are capable of and how they operate at higher levels and how easy it is to get the optimal gear.

The only reason that you would need optimal gear at 60 is to get into raids. Raids are the only downsidey-ish (this is my review and I can make up any wordy-things that I want) thing to this game. Unless you have friends playing, or you join a supergroup/guild, it is kinda hard to get into raids. That being said I haven't noticed this as yet because I am busy levelling my characters.

As a game I give this an 8/10 and as a free to play game I give it a 10/10.
