Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We love active gaming and sword play in VR, where players are able to use their real physical and hand-eye coordination skills. We started with something we thought most people would understand and enjoy, slicing fruits, a simple but deep sword simulation challenge. Slicing stuff in VR is fun but only if done right, so that you actually feel like your wielding a sword and interacting with the virtual world.

With the game running smooth on min spec VR systems and the blade physics pretty solid we are now ready to mold the game's evolution with your suggestions and support! We have plans to build this game out beyond just chopin' fruit.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We estimate to be in early access for 8-10 months. We'll be adding more fun challenges and game modes, more environments, more swords, and features with community suggestions and support. We plan to be out of early access with 3-4 more fun challenges/modes and improvements to game play, art and sound.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Our vision for ZenBlade is to create a game that challenges you both physically and mentally in dynamic martial arts environments, while focusing on sword play. We're aiming for several modes of play that require the player to build up their real world physical skills and also have options for more thought based challenges. We want to create quality over quantity and experiment with new ideas and suggestions through community participation.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently there is the options Zen and Challenge:


Challenge contains:

1. Time Trial - Get the most points in 90 seconds. Easy (great place to start), Medium, and Hard difficulties available.

2. Survival - Don't miss 3 fruit or hit any bombs! There are two modes available: Classic, which has a tempo similar to Time Trial, and a mode called "Taiko" which has no bombs, a consistent tempo, and gradually decreases fruit size.

3. Typhoon - Get the most points in 90 seconds, but with a chaotic tempo and spawn patterns.


Zen contains:

1. Free Play -Players can choose to create custom wave patterns, wield two katanas, with no pressure for scoring or time limits.

2. Puzzle -124 wave pattern puzzles to solve. This is good place to start.


The game plays well on minimum spec VR machines, utilizes the power of higher spec machines and the blade dynamics are fairly polished. We'll be adding new modes and environments over time.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Price likely will not change when leaving early access.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“It's early access! We definitely want to involve the community with ZenBlade's evolution! We'll be engaging the community though regular updates and engaging through discussions via the community hub. Let us know your suggestions and comments, we'll be listening.”

About This Game

"ZenBlade (formerly known as Ninja Trainer) is a sword simulation action game designed specifically for VR utilizing tracked motion controls. It's a highly accessible game that is easy to get into and strangely addicting once you start. This game takes real physical sword skill and zen like focus to get high scores. If you like motion gaming and swordplay, this is a game for you.

We are happy to present our early access release and have you join us as we add more fun and unique challenges only possible through virtual reality! With your support and suggestions, we'll be adding more content, swords, game modes, challenges, achievements, with opportunities to compete and socially interact in ZenBlade.

The game was initially inspired by the famous mobile fruit slicing game, but we wanted to make a game that you can actually feel like you are wielding a sword and correctly physically interacting in the virtual world. Slice with the sharp edge of your sword, try for combos, and avoid hitting bombs to achieve the high score! With practice and the right technique, you might be surprised how high a score you can achieve. Who will be the next ZenBlade Master?

WARNING: PLEASE PLAY SAFELY!

We care about your safety and the safety of others. This is an active, skill based game that simulates real world sword wielding behavior. Due to the nature of fully immersive virtual reality, you may not be aware of your real surroundings. Please make sure your play area is clear from walls, people, animals and any other obstructions that you may collide with once in this VR game. Don't forget to use your wrist strap. If you feel tired or any discomfort, discontinue playing. Take breaks often. By playing this game you accept all risk and liability. You know, basically use common sense when playing this game and have fun!"

  • Live out the dream to be a fruit hating ninja
  • You can use two swords in some modes

Ultimately I didn't find the game to be as fun as I had hoped. It's not a "bad" game really, but it's not that great either.

Early Access Review
Posted: 2 May
ZenBlade is an addicting casual VR game that resembles Fruit Ninja! It is currently in early access.

Check out my Quick Preview here: https://youtu.be/SDIjbFyeogk
I played the ZenBlade using the HTC Vive, and I felt like fruit and bombs are really flying at me, and I don’t know why, but I kept trying to jump away from the bombs even though they don’t hurt me.

There are two modes available. In timed mode, you play on a timer, and as the clock starts ticking down, you aim to get the highest score by slicing as much fruit as you can. In survival mode, you can’t let the fruit drop, when three fruit hit the floor, you lose. Slicing more fruit in one swing awards bonus points, but if you hit the bombs, you lose points!

I really love the sword mechanics! You have to swing hard enough to slice the fruit, and swing from the right angle. You can also knock bombs away with the hilt of the sword, and juggle fruit off the sides of the blade to set yourself up for better combos.

Pros:
  • even in early access, the game is pretty polished
  • the room scaling is done very well - I had zero nausea!
  • moving around, and swinging the sword feels very natural - the sword play is very responsive, and extremely fun to use
  • the haptic feedback makes it feel like you’re actually slicing through the fruit
  • addictive gameplay, that is easy to learn, and fun for the whole family
  • taking turns and competing with friends locally is fun

Cons:
  • repetitive gameplay that can get boring, especially if you're playing alone
  • lacks content, there are only two modes available, but I expect more to be added before full release
  • no way to keep track of players top scores while playing locally with friends (maybe an option to save scores with initials could be added)

Playing Zenblade is addictive as you compete to get better scores. It is still in early access though, so I’d still like to see more modes added, maybe some where the fruit comes in from other directions, or fruit comes endlessly until you hit a certain number of bombs or a mode where you have to dodge the bombs before they kill you. Or maybe even something where armored fruit enemies come at you from all directions and you have to slice and stab them where they are vulnerable. Now that would be awesome!

So far, I’ve been playing with others for over 4 hours, and even though I’m absolutely terrible at the game, I’m still enjoying it. ZenBlade has a lot of potential, and I look forward to seeing what they do with it before the full release. Since I have been playing with others, I am satisfied with the purchase. But, if I had intended to play alone, I would have waited for more content to be added or a slight sale price.
