The future's a difficult place and you must earn credits/ pick up materials as you progress, using them to upgrade/ change your ship to match your playing style.

Patch Release Notes May 30th 2016

Our thanks to the great Project G community for point out the couple of glitches in the 1.0 release of the game. About This Game

The year is 2110 and humanity depends on secret marine farms for survival. Created by a philanthropic organisation through a centuryâs tireless work, the aquatic farms are under threat from predators emboldened by an ecosystem in chaos. Cybernetic fish-bots were created to keep this precious food source safe. Then it happened: a glitch? A hack? All you know is that the fish-bots turned from guardians to enemies. Now you stand between the human race and extinction.

In this retro-styled side-scrolling shooter, you pilot your sleek submarine through levels by using keyboard (CTRL to shoot and SPACE to deploy one of your special weapons) or gamepad. The future is a difficult place to find resources and you must earn credits and pick up materials as you conquer levels, then use them to upgrade your ship to match your playing style, or take control of a different model. Select one of three kinds of special weapons to take with you into each battle: depth charges, torpedoes, or nukes. Learn how the world came to this crisis as you collect log entries from those who lived through the decline of the planet, and by compiling them unlock the location of Earthâs final hope, the main research lab of Giga Corp.

[Update] as of today 17th June Multiplayer is now a feature. Using dual control pads you can share a screen and battle your way through all the levels.

Retro style side scrolling robofish action.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iOTsCy8nRN4
This game heavily reminds me of the classic side scrolling games that I used to play in my childhood back on the Amiga (And no I wasn't very good at them back then either)

What makes it unusual is rather than deing set in space like most games of this genre it is set underwater and draws its enemy stylings from real live fish and other sea creatures.

There are multiple ships to unlock each with their own upgrades, along with the replayabilty of trying to obtain three stars on each level (you will probably need upgraded ships to achieve three stars)

Overall a very fun casual shooter with cartoon style graphics thats an ideal game to pick up and put down when you have a few minutes to spare

+ Great visual style.
+ Unusual side scrolling shooter setting.
+ Plenty of unlocks and upgrades.
+ Replayability due to multiple awards available per level

- Even with the unusual setting it is very similar to lots of side scrolling shooters.
