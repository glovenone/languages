Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Shift Happens is our first full-scale PC game. The funding is secured, the game is almost feature complete and we are adding content as you are reading this. As a newcomer to the game-biz, we are in need of feedback and sanity checks for the product: Is it fun? How can we make it even more enjoyable?

We decided not to go through a publisher, as this project is our baby and we wanted to keep making our own decisions. Although the game is not an open-world sandbox game, we think Early Access is a good fit to help us make a better game than with just us making decisions. We are a small studio and listening to player-feedback directly instead of market analysis by an anonymous publisher makes much more sense to us.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We are aiming to launch the game in early 2016, depending on the feedback and resulting changes this could be extended by a few months. So hopefully Early Access will not take more than six months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The Early Access version will have increasing content from patch to patch. The content will be added as we develop the game, with EA players being able to play some of the “forgotten” and “rejected” levels during development. Those levels may make it into the final version, but Early Access players in any case will get access during development.
The final version will include one additional stage consisting of the final story levels, which won’t be part of Early Access so you guys have a little surprise at the end. Also the final release will be performance optimized and hopefully bug-free.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“At this point (July 2015), the Early Access includes the first complete “stage” and some extra levels of different stages. The online multiplayer mode is also included in an early version, as this is one of the most test-intensive feature we are working on. At the current time, online multiplayer is not stable and you should expect asynchronicity from time to time.
The game will be available for Windows at first, other platforms to follow.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Early Access is priced below our full-price, to reward you for your trust, investment and feedback.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are very much relying on the feedback of experienced players, as this is our first full-blown PC title. Although the core game mechanics are complete, we still need input when it comes to details and the feel of the game. We have been working on the game for almost two years and are looking forward to having a few pairs of external extra eyes reviewing Shift Happens.
The steam forums will be our main feedback focal point, but we will be live-streaming some development and are looking forward to feedback on these channels as well.”

About This Game

Shift Happens is a game about two charming jello creatures called Bismo and Plom that were bound together by accident and thus can exchange their size and abilities at will. The world and its challenges revolve around this "shifting" mechanic, forcing two players to work together (and occasionally stab each other in the back).

As a team of two, you explore the four different themed stages:
  • The laboratory, where Bismo and Plom were created,
  • the overgrown and green forest
  • the windy and dry canyon,
  • and the icy, cold Grotto!

Together you solve puzzles and fight over precious coins. Coordination and communication are key to a successful journey. Shift Happens is best enjoyed on a couch with a good friend and a pizza. We recommend using gamepads for maximum enjoyability™.

The game also features singleplayer mode with different level content. Although the game is primarily meant to be played by two people, you can play the completely different singleplayer levels without another person.

The game features all the good things you should expect from a co-op platformer:
  • over 40 levels of handcrafted fun
  • local and online co-op gameplay
  • extra challenges/rewards in the levels for completionists and explorers
  • more than 30 different levels designed for the singleplayer mode
  • 8 unlockable bonuslevels (4 single- & 4 multiplayer)
  • achievements
  • and timechallenges for each level

Early Access Review
Posted: 9 September, 2015
Shift Happens is a cooperative puzzle platformer where you play as one of two creatures that end up sharing the same mass. Since they share the same mass, when one creature is large the other has to be small and vice versa. The two creatures can change shape at any time. Many of the puzzles in the game are designed around this premise. This mechanic is interesting and the developers have created some puzzles that do a good job utilizing it.

While the single player levels are better than I expected, the multiplayer is the best part of the game. I played the multiplayer local co-op so I can't comment on how well the online co-op works. I thought the game did a good job of keeping both players active in the puzzle solving making it that no one player can take control over everything.

At this early stage of early access there are a couple issues that will be hopefully resolved through further development. In around 2 hours I completed all but the most recently added levels. I also found the puzzles to be kind of easy. I play a lot of puzzle platformers though so that could be a contributing factor. As the developers add more levels I think both issues could be resolved.

Note: I received a free review copy of Shift Happens. Other than receiving a free review copy I received no other compensation for this review and it had no impact of the content of this review.
