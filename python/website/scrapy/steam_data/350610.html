About This Game

Combat Cats is a tile-matching game with unique combat aspects that add RPG and adventure elements. Kitty Island is in shambles. This once purrfect paradise is now without an ounce of catnip. You, a member of the Kitty Island Homeland Securityâs veteran Ace Fighter Pilots, are the last hope to return peace and harmony. Go against impawsible odds and battle waves of enemies in the world of Combat Cats.

The game features a variety of offensive and defensive actions that can be used by matching various combinations of icons. These icons will unlock things such as rockets, bullets, repairs and much more. Longer combinations will unlock various sized nukes which will destroy rows, columns, and chunks of blocks when detonated.

Key Features:
  • Match bricks in any pattern you want.
  • Earn extra points and do more damage for bigger combos.
  • Fire off nuclear weapons!
  • Pop pesky water bubbles that block your game board.
  • Upgrade stats, weapons, aircraft!
  • Unlock achievements!

Posted: 26 November, 2015
Good puzzle game. When I only have a few minutes to spare, I still go back and play it every once and a while despite finishing it.

You can probably cruise through it in 3-4 hours, if you just push for completion and ignore collection and achievements.

I liked the idea of earning earning catnip (The leaves?) and money to buy things and upgrade ability. Keeps it interesting.

Money was weighted quite well relative to the length of game. This means you don't grind too much, but it takes a decent time to get the cash.

Catnip was poorly weighted. You end up topping out too quickly to where it does not matter.

The level progression was weighted quite well. You did not have to grind too much to get the items you need to stand a chance on the next level. In other words the abilities of the next level up were well matched to the powers and ships you have by the time you get there. This makes it a fair fight, without too much boredom and frustration.

Two of the achievements to do with survival mode are extremely hard to do. They are unrealistic compared to other achievements. It does not have an enemies killed tracker so you can see how close you got, instead it has a useless points tracker.

Survival mode is kind of lame since there are not any upgrades you can do. Once you complete the quest likely you have everything. So you go to survival mode and basically keep getting to the same point of progress. Since you have everything it makes catnip and money meaningless. It would have been cool to add a new set of weapons and abilities for survival mode after the quest is complete.

As it is survival mode is kind of pointless.

i should also note there is a currency system which is used to upgrade your health, repair and the various attacks.
there is 3 storefronts 1 which takes coins to upgrade your weapons, a 2nd which takes catnip to boost your weapons and the 3rd is the unlockable cats/ships , which takes a bit of both.

in total there is 15 stages all replayable, and a score system based on time with 1 to 3 star ratings.
included in these 15 stages are 3 boss stages which act as normal stages leading to the bosses.
once every stage has been completed survival mode unlocks, and i will admit it is a challange.

last thing i should add is that each ship includes its own attack visuals, grumpy cats is a cardboard ship, so he fires tollietpaper, the aliencat fires a laser beam, oh and lets not froget the witchs cat who fires a magic blast.

overall the game is short and just about everything can be unlocked within 5 hours that includes majority achivements, the only achivements that will take you time is the 500 surfer defeats and survival mode kills.

so if you like puzzle games with rpg twists and alotta cuteness check this great game out

to avoid any confusion, catnip and coins both are gainable via battles, all currencys are available ingame without having to pay more then just for the game.

Personally, as a casual gamer, I'm enjoying this game a lot.
Let's just jump right into what you want to know:

Gameplay: Simple! Swap the tile you're given with any on the board to make groupings of 4 or more to damage your foes before they damage you! Get money and catnip to spend on upgrades and new ships and weapon power-ups to help you hit harder and faster to get more stars at the end of each level in order to progress.

Sound: Retro but a little repetitive. What you hear in the trailer is what you get. Nothing obnoxious, but nothing spectacular either. You can turn on or off sounds or music but that's it.

Controls: It's obviously meant for mobile as some things mention tapping. But it's all mouse from here, baby! Nothing simpler than that.

Graphics: Pixels! Pixels everywhere! Except for the strangely large space behind the gameplay area which is just filled with cute cat and fish drawings. You can window the game (alt+enter) or full screen it, but it seems to always only have this tiny gameplay screen inside a giant window with the doodled background. Obviously remnants from its mobile origin.

Story: Yes, there is actually a story, again, shown in the trailer. Funny and cute; steal back the cat island's catnip from the Aquarium Alliance, beating one fish into submission at a time!

Overall: I'm having fun; there's a nice amount of achievements for how short the game is so it ups the replay value a little with that, and there are plenty of different ships to unlock, too. My only concern is the lack of resolution choices but it's not a must for such a little game as this, in my opinion, so I can stick it out. On launch there were a few bugs including no sound, but the Developer handled it almost immediately. I recommend this game; the price is nice for what you're getting.
