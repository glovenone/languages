March of War: FaceOff
The Battlefield Card Game.

Played out on a beautiful game-board battlefield, FaceOff offers many of the familiar mechanics of the typical card game; however, the game-board play adds a unique âterrainâ dimension missing from other card games.

FaceOff not only plays different than other card games â it is the only Steampunk card game we know of on Steam.

Come join the FaceOff alt-history Steampunk wargame community - choose a faction, build a devious deck, and then put on your poker face as you Face Off across the game-board battlefield. Unleash devastating barrages, advance your army, and pulverize the enemy with a dazzling combination of cunning strategy and totally unexpected card combinations.

With hundreds of cards across three unique factions, no game is ever the same. Craft your perfect deck and play for free against the AI or human opponents!

Posted: 12 April, 2015
This game is an interesting hybrid between a trading card game and a classic board game like chess with a Diesel Punk-WWII theme. The combination makes for an extensive depth to the gameplay. While the game is pleasantly easy to learn, mastery is a different story. The large range of card types, different factions and the unique nature of the gameplay ensures a varied an interesting experience with no two player made decks being the same and with no single deck having universal acclaim to the leader-board.

All the content is accessible for free (with the exception of aesthetics), and there is a tonne of content too, three full factions and a neutral support faction. Also, paying players get no advantage over free players with all the content that they purchase having the same drop rate as that of free to play players. As to the grind of the game, its not too bad, especially in getting you into the mid-ranks. Plenty of initial gold drops and boosts to help you get started, after that it balances off a little, but the crafting system takes over at that stage, helping you make powerful custom decks.

The 3D graphics are decent, not great but clean and well textured. The animations are many and mostly well done. The card art however is amazing and considering the hundreds of cards in-game, all of it is surprisingly unique and enjoyable to look at.

PvP is where this game excels, while the first few matches can be bumpy once you're accurately ranked the system gives you quite evenly matched opponents. If you don't have great cards yet, or want to play with some that you don't have the recently released Arena mode is great. Arena mode levels the playing field and beyond the initial luck and skill needed to craft the deck its all skill based, so you can prove your mettle against far more veteran players with ease.

The development team are very active with the game, in the last six months they've added a constant stream of new content and had two major releases. One was the addition of Arena mode and the other was the Shogun Empire, the first of four planned additional factions. Any changes that negatively effect a card you own when addressing balance is also treated fairly, with you being offered the opportunity to exchange the "nerfed" card for any other of equal rarity.

Overall a very well implemented game with a great twist on the classic real-world gaming for the digital age.

This card game is based off another free to play card game of a similar name, March of War. This game is very well made and well balanced, with the developers actively working to enhance the game, and spending plenty of tiem in the chat talking to the players and taking suggestions. Unlike some other card games, the only luck is in the unavoidable draw of the cards and cards obtained from packs, the rest is all skill. An easy PvE mode allows you to get a simple understanding of the game before moving onto PvP. Currently there are 0 items which require you to spend money. Everything can be obtained for free.
The concept of March of War is 6 super factions composed of numerous countries are in an eternal alternate WW2 battle to rule the world, using different technologies and weapons unique to each faction. In Face Off, 2 factions are already incorporated with a third coming up, hopefully for August 2015. Your choice of factionoverns which cards can be incorporated into your deck. Additionally decks can be made with completely different strategies, such as focusing on units that buff your other soldiers, or on creating a defense using shield abilities aswell as advancing across the field. Most card games are fairly simple, but this game allows for in depth strategy with a variety of different unit abilities, and over 200 cartds already in the game.
Sadly the game is not perfect, but what game is? Purchasing packs of cards can require lots of grinding and may yield frusturatingly poor results, and the game does not yet have a large player base, so PvP can get a bit slow at times. Fortunately daily objectives and login bonuses are in place to make grinding easier and the game will expand for more players to join PvP, and further updates will improve the game even more.
Overall this is an excellent card game, the first one I've played that has been addictive and fun. Besides, the game is free, nothing bad can happen if you download it, so give it a try.
