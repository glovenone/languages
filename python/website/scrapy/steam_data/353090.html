About This Game

Chicken Invaders puts you at the forefront of a battle against invading intergalactic chickens, bent on revenge against the human race for our oppression of Earth chickens. In this episode, the chickens have hatched a devious plan to block out sunlight and freeze Earth to death. It's up to you to travel around the galaxy in order to piece together an ancient artifact and (quite literally) save the day.

Hero's log, Stardate 8008.5

As the last intergalactic chicken invaders flee our Solar System
and lingering feathers still smolder in interplanetary space,
I ease up on the trigger and wipe the sweat from my brow.
My work here is done.

Saving the world sure works up an appetite.
I'm plotting a course for the nearest Space Burger.
I think I'll have the spicy chicken wings tonight.
Or perhaps their galaxy-renowned chicken pie?
Ah, the choices of a hero are never easy.

Suddenly, I am yanked back to reality by the whine
of my trusty spaceship's automated warning system.
Why are all my dashboard lights flashing red?
Why has the Earth darkened so?
And why does it all have to happen just as I'm about to eat?

Looks like tonight's dinner will have to wait...

Difficulty
The game has 3 difficulty settings. One of the few differences between the 3 is that the enemies become more bullet spongy than the previous difficulty. If it takes you one shot to kill an enemy on rookie, it will take you 3-4 to kill it in Superstar Hero.
The second thing that changes is that on higher difficulties the enemies drop more keys (used for unlocking stuff), but that's only because the enemies stay longer on the screen. You can get the same number of keys on lower difficulties if you don't kill the enemies quickly.
The third difference is that on higher difficulties you get more points. Points which are completely useless on Steam because you cannot use the official site's leaderboards.

Game length
The length depends on the difficulty you play at. I timed the last 3 times I beat it:
â¢ Rookie: 01:01:34h

This does not include the cutscenes. With a rough estimate I guess the cutscenes are about 35 minutes long. Is that worth the 4,99â¬ you will pay for it ? Yes. Is it worth the â¬11.25 I payed for it ? Yes. I've completed it 15 times and I am still not bored of it.

Steam / non-Steam versions
The Steam version is very crippled and I see no point in owning the game here. If you buy it from the official site you will get so much more content, the most important is:
â¢ Leaderboards. You can compete against many people in the world.
â¢ You will get all future special editions for free (judging from Chicken Invaders 4 there will be 4 versions). On Steam you have to buy every one separately. Let's do the math:

Improvements from previous titles

â¢ CI1 had 10 levels repeating until you lose all your lives (you can play forever)
â¢ CI2 had more than one weapon and the ability to move vertically
â¢ CI3 had proper working mouse support, overheating weapons and a good storyline
â¢ CI4 revolutionized the whole genre. So many more weapons. Tilting/zooming camera and so so so many other improvements.
â¢ CI5 has nothing new. Sure you have 3 unique levels, but the game is the same.

Weapons
We have 3 new weapons in this one. 3 completely useless weapons I should say. They are terrible and I avoid them at all cost. If I play the previous CI games I will not miss these 3. The other problem I have is that you can complete the whole game using 1 weapon: the green one. It is great for everything. There is no need to switch to other ones. This problem is apparent in all CI games, the green weapon is always the best. There should be a reason for me to use the other ones, but there isn't.

Upgrades
While playing, randomly an enemy will stat glowing yellow, if you kill it while glowing, it will drop a key. If you collect enough keys you can unlock certain abilities.
