About This Game

Your uncle is Murdered, the only way to unravel the mystery is to follow his footsteps, will you reach the end? will you pass every guard? will find the way into the castle? will you steal the Alchemist's Artefact?
Over the span of three exciting episodes Castle Heist will take it's players past countless of armed guards, artefacts will be stolen and Castle's will be infiltrated.
This is a game of Pure Stealth, being caught is not an option! You must follow the clues left behind and pass unseen, no mercy is left for those trespassing!

Chapter 1 is the small first episode on a grand adventure, your journey starts here!

NOTE: This game was given to me by the developer for review purposes. This did not influence my review in any way.

==========================================================
---------------------------------------OVERVIEW-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Castle Heist: Chapter 1 is an indie first-person 'pure stealth' game. You play the nephew of a member of the Cartographer's Guild who has spent his life planning a heist on the castle town of Redfort in order to steal a powerful artifact from the evil Alchemist.

==========================================================
----------------------------------------QUALITY-------------------------------------
==========================================================
Graphics - Graphics generally look nice and the game performs well. The trees in particular look very nice. There are a few weird clipping issues when you approach some objects and a fair bit of obvious popping in and out of objects in the distance, and although guard models are reasonably nice and animated well, they don't quite move across the ground properly, with feet sometimes floating or moving under the level of the ground. And for some reason your character always carries around a map in his hands, making it basically impossible for you to see anything useful when you look down. In the second area I fell off a cliff while trying to see what was down there because the map was in the way!

The game world is very sparsely populated with identical guards, and there are invisible walls preventing you from departing the action areas, but the models that are used are quite sharp and the game does generally look quite nice, if a little lifeless. When ascending stairs or navigating some of the more crowded areas (e.g. the market or kitchen) I found it was very easy to get stuck on game objects, requiring me to back away.

Sound - Sound effects are lacklustre, with loud footstep sounds that don't sound that good making any movement sound like you're scuffing your steel-cap boots through piles of gravel. There is also pretty terrible voice acting at the beginning and when you reach certain points in the game, with no way to skip it. I'd have preferred simple text instead.

The music is mostly okay, though, with a suitably medieval feel to it most of the time. I did wonder about the out-of-place jazz number in Redfort at night, though.

Gameplay - Castle Heist plays like a simple version of Thief without the combat. There are no sound or vision indicators telling you when guards might catch you, and it's not always obvious when you're hidden or not, but the basic stealth mechanic usually works well enough to make the game fun.

Controls are simple: WASD to move, shift to crouch (no option for toggle, unfortunately), and space to jump. Mouse looks around, and since there's no combat and no way to use anything, the mouse buttons don't do anything. Since crouching is the most useful action in the game and there's no toggle setting, I mapped it to a mouse button instead, making it easier to move while crouched for extended periods.

==========================================================
-------------------------------------PROS & CONS------------------------------------
==========================================================
Pros:
+ Nice looking graphics with mostly very good models and textures (available from the Unity asset store)
+ Level design makes sense; the developer has made good use of the available assets
+ Stealth mostly works well
+ Mostly good atmospheric music (from Incompetech and Sleep Facing West), except for the weird jazz piece that is completely out of place
+ Two achievements (both hidden)

Cons:
- Poor sound effects and pretty bad voice acting; the footsteps in particular are annoying
- Movement and clipping issues, and objects not interacting with the game world properly (e.g. guards hovering or walking in the ground)
- There's no way to tell when someone can see or hear you until it's too late, making it more frustrating than it should be
- 'Always-on' map when you look down is a terrible design decision; it just gets in the way
- Lacks polish


==========================================================
--------------------------------------CONCLUSION-----------------------------------
==========================================================
I'm a big fan of stealth games, but they also frustrate me a lot when the stealth aspects are done poorly. Thankfully, that's not the case here.

Castle Heist: Chapter 1 is very short: probably a bit too short for the asking price, as it can be completed in less than an hour. It plays more like a tech. demo than a game, but it is just Chapter 1 at this stage.

Caste Heist has its glitches, but the developer has done a pretty good job of fashioning an interesting enough stealth mission from some generally very nice assets. It's not the best stealth game out there by a long shot, but it's a good start; I do want to see what happens next.

Recommended for anyone wanting a quick fix of stealth gameplay, who doesn't mind a few glitches.

6/10

Castle Heist: Chapter 1 is a short stealthy walking simulator game that introduces the plot of the Castle Heist series.

Check out my Quick Review here: https://youtu.be/lAfeXTyxEw0
Once you start playing the game, you are immediately introduced to the story through a narrative text. At first, I felt like the voice acting of the narrative was being a bit over-acted, but after multiple playthroughs, it grew on me. The game plays as mostly a walking simulator, but with stealth aspects.

Pros:
  • the graphics look great overall
  • music is decent throughout the game, and each area has its own (I especially liked the music that reminded me of the Pink Panther theme song)
  • the stealth in Castle Heist is nice because you have to be careful, or guards will one-shot you on site in some areas

Cons:
  • very short to playthrough
  • too easy (guards have very predictable paths, and they aren’t very attentive unless you're in their line of site or standing right next to them)
  • some very minor bugs with textures/draw distances
  • guards sometimes appear above or in the ground if you're paying attention to them

Each area has a map that you are given with notes, and marks on it of where you need to go next. If you rush through the objectives, you can finish the game in less than 15 minutes, but I suggest playing the game more like a walking simulator, and just taking your time, and exploring all the areas. Right now, there are a couple achievements you can unlock by exploring, but I would like to see more achievements added to encourage more exploration.

Right now, there isn’t any reason to replay the game, unless you missed the achievements, but I did wind up playing through it 4 times since it is such a short game. Chapter 1 is a good, but brief introduction into the Castle Heist plot, but as expected from an episodic series, you won’t get any closure, so I'm looking forward to chapter 2. If you’re looking for a game to simply play and finish, you won’t enjoy this game to its full potential. However, if you’re in the mood to take your time to explore a short, but sweet, stealthy walking simulator game for a reasonable price, it’s a decent purchase.
