Polychromatic is a physics-based arcade shooter with a pop of color. With dynamic gameplay, a unique flat geometric art style, and exhilarating soundtrack, Polychromatic is the arcade shooter experience players have been waiting for.

For the best experience, a gamepad is recommended.

If you'd preffer to see the game in action and my ideas as they form, check out the following video (Disclosure: I asked for and received a review copy of the game for the purpose of the video)

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gH91UXO1zt4

Polychromatic is, in essence, a spiritual successor to Geometry Wars. The basic concept of the game is the same, a endless SHMUP shooter consisting of several waves of various types of enemies with the clear all bombs and simplified graphics. The change from vectric wireframe to minimalism is a nice distinction factor and the change in how the enemies behave gives it enough distinction from its inspiration.

But with that said is there really a reason to buy the game? I'm of a mixed opinion.

On the positive side is the core gameplay. It takes from a highly successful SHMUP formula and makes its own unique game with great visuals and unique sound effects. Controls are fluid and smooth, and the variety of enemy units makes the endless waves pretty enjoyable.

On the negative side though, there is almost no variety in game type. You have three game types. First is endless waves, which gives you a stock of lives and bombs (and dashes that give you momentary invulnerability), there's timed which gives you 2 minutes and adds more time on each wave completion, and there's "one life to live" in which if you duy you're done. There's no other game option beyond that. Given the formula it's replay value is rather in the core game, but some structured gameplay variation would be nice.

Next is the music. It feels... jarring. I wouldn't say it's bad really, but it's definitley a very distinctly different from the sound effects and visuals of the game. Feels like (I think I described it as) weird electronic lounge music that gets hyper all of the sudden.

Finally is the price, where I'm not sure given my first issue how I feel. Ten dollars is alot to ask for, especially when its inspiration is $4 on steam, and gameplay wise the only thing differentiating it is the enemy behaviors and graphics.

So do I recommend the game? With hesitation, but yes. If Steam had something akin to a five star rating I'd give it a 2.5. It's a solid game on its own, but the pricepoint and lack of gameplay options hurt it. Still I can't say I don't recommend it. If you can pick it up on sale I'd say it's worth some time spent as it's a great formula. Just a little disappointed that there wasn't more to it.

Introduction

Polychromatic is a colourful, twin-stick arena shooter from Brushfire Games. You, a small blue shape, must fight off waves of other shapes in order to get as many points as you possibly can. Polychromatic is very much about trying to get progressively better scores to not only beat your previous personal best but also in order to get higher on the leaderboard which creates a real sense of competitiveness even though you’re playing by yourself.

Presentation

Polychromatic is very simple in its appearance, relying on the vibrant colours of the different shapes to make it interesting. While this does work relatively well for the game as it makes all the shapes really stand out, I was very much left with a sense of blandness due to the grey background of the game arena. One thing I did really like about the presentation was the fact that information about your current game is displayed in the middle of the arena, rather than on any UI elements around the screen.

Game Modes

Polychromatic is split into three different game modes; Endless mode, Timed mode and One-Life mode. The major things that stay the same across the game modes is that you always have access to three blast power-ups and three dash power-ups (more can be earned by beating waves) and each consecutive wave is progressively longer and harder to beat.

Endless mode consists of you beating as many waves of shapes as you can before you run out of lives. In Endless mode you start with three spare lives though more can be earned by beating waves of shapes. As far as I’m aware there is no ‘wave cap’ so in theory you could play forever but since the game’s difficulty does ramp up a fair bit I don’t see that being at all possible.

Timed mode gives you infinite lives but only two minutes on the clock to beat as many waves as you can. Each time you beat a wave ten seconds is added on to the clock. It’s worth remembering that respawning takes around two seconds so while you may have infinite lives it’s in your best interests to keep yourself going as long as possible.

One-life mode is identical to Endless mode but instead of starting with three lives and getting more every few waves you only have one life so you better hope you’re good at dodging!

Gameplay

There’s not much to say about gameplay other than what I’ve covered in my description of game modes. As you whizz around what appears to be a petri-dish of shapes you fire tiny circles at said shapes in order to eradicate them! As well as these tiny circles you also have the two power-ups I mentioned earlier at your disposal. The blast power-up sends out a ring of the little circles to eradicate shapes all around you, of course these circles spread out the further away from you they get so it’s best to use it when there’s a high concentration of enemies very close to you. The dash power-up sends you shooting around the little arena at a faster than usual speed while also making you a lean, mean shape-squishing machine, i.e. any shapes you touch while the dash power-up is active will be destroyed.

Audio

The sound effects are very good in Polychromatic and fit with the game nicely. The soundtrack is also good but I felt like it didn’t really fit with the game. With a game like this I’d expect quite lively music, something along the lines of electro or dubstep to really get you into the game for the more frantic, later waves. However, the music we’re presented with, while very nice, has a more relaxing feel to it and just felt out of place to me.

Miscellaneous
  • For the amount of content included with the game I feel like the £6.99 price tag may be a little too high, I think it comes down to how much you like games similar to this as to whether or not you should be willing to pay that price.
  • The only graphical option is a fullscreen toggle.
  • While the game is playable with a keyboard & mouse it’s a twin-stick shooter so it’s definitely geared more towards players with controllers, I for one enjoyed playing with a controller much more than with KB&M.

For a brief look at the three different game modes, check out my gameplay video:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qun-eFfBeQU

Verdict

Worth Purchasing on Sale

Polychromatic is a fun little twin-stick shooter, with a simple aesthetic, good gameplay and a real sense of competitiveness due to the leaderboards. The only thing holding this game is its price tag but chances are you’ll think it’s worth it if you like this kind of game, if not then hold off until a sale.

You have 3 game modes in Polychromatic:

Endless: Survive for as long as possible for an endless amout of waves with a limited amout of lives and powers, you regenerate your lives and powers over long periods of time.

One Life: Same as Endless mode but you can only get hit once.

Timed: You have a limited amout of time to stay in the game, every time you finish a wave, you gain a few extra seconds to your timer. You also have infinte lives and limited but regenateable powers.

In the game, waves don't really start and end. More and more enemies spawn but you have to reach X number of kills to finished wave Y.

Artstyle:
The artstyle is very simple and clean, colourful shapes and a grey arena, not much to be said but it's still nice.

Soundtrack:
Techno upbeat soundtrack, really nice and it is fis in to the game very nicely.

Performance:
Ran at a smooth 60 FPS all the time, expected but still I note it.

Additional Notes:
- Controller recommended, but keyboard controls are still playable.
- No tutorial.
- Not a whole lot of content, sure it IS an arcade game but only 3 game modes with not much variety make the game seem a bit basic.


Summary:

Polychromatic is a small, colourful, twin-stick arcade shooter with a basic and clean artstyle. Fun for a few hours but the price is a bit high for the somewhat lack of content.

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 2 October, 2015
If you’d prefer a video version of this review, I made one that outlines similar points:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vKSo0J1OBFw

Polychromatic instilled an old feeling in me that I used to get, but it was a very specific set of events that led me to that feeling.

I’ll explain what I mean in a bit, but first; Polychromatic is a top down endless twin-stick shooter, that is designed to be played casually.. You have to survive for as long as possible while also destroying as many of the other shapes as you can.

The game does a good job of making each different enemy shape behave in different ways, and I noticed, in particular, things like group AI and AI in general were done very well; I know that stuff like that can be difficult to get right, but the enemies move in a way that is challenging to avoid but still completely predictable and that should be commended.

It’s also just a generally well polished game, and that might be because the mechanics are simple; and maybe they’re too simple. Initially, when I played the endless mode for the first time, I felt like, while the gameplay was good, there was little incentive for me to keep playing. It felt kind of bare-bones.

That was before I saw the leaderboard, and this is what I mean by instilling an old feeling in me; since I have a promotional copy of this game (that is, one that comes out before the official release date) the global leaderboards were quite empty. I instantly got tunnel vision; I had to get the highest score in the world before this game released. So I tried again… and again.. and again.. and suddenly was having so much fun. In the end I failed; I gave up once I reached fourth place, but the main point is that this is where the game really shines. The gameplay is designed perfectly for leaderboard competition.

The problem is, what are the chances that you, the viewer, are going to experience this? The way I see it, there will either need be so few people playing the game that you have a realistic chance of reaching the number one spot globally, or there will need to be so many people playing the game that there is enough competition in your friends list to make it worthwhile.

Because, I’m gonna go ahead and say that it’s unlikely you’ll get much from this game from a purely isolated experience. Sure the gameplay is solid, but after a few attempts it becomes the same thing over and over. Yeah you can try and get better every time, but to what end? The game is infinite, even the timed mode is still infinite it’s just how far you can get within a certain time.

So I can only recommend this game if you are of the small subset of people who not only enjoy competition on leaderboards, but are in a position that that’s going to be fun for you. And then, you need decide if that’s worth $10. For me, I think the experience I had, and still am having since I’m gonna keep trying, is worth that price. But I have a feeling that’s only going to last until release day.

It’s a difficult one, but I feel I’ve given you the information you need to make an informed decision about this. Officially I will be recommending it, but be aware of what my feelings are behind that recommendation.

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 4 October, 2015
While not highly original, Polychromatic does a good job at what it is, a twin stick shooter.

Polychromatic works like most twin stick shooters where one analog stick controls movement and the other controls the aim of your weapon. You can also collect two different power ups which includes releasing a burst that kills all of the nearby enemies and a speed burst that makes you temporarily invulnerable.

Polychromatic has three different modes. One mode has you try to score as many points as you can with a given set of lives (earning more based on points scored). Another mode gives you a certain amount of time where you try to score as many points as possible. Finally there is a mode where you get only one life and try to score as many points as you can before you die.

While the artwork looks simplistic I actually liked it quite a bit. With a simple graphics style the game runs really well even when there are a bunch of enemies and bullets on the screen at the same time. I also liked that all of the enemies have distinctive shapes and colors which makes it easy to determine what special abilities each enemy has.

The only real problem with Polychromatic is that it is not that original. If you don't like twin stick shooters I don't think Polychromatic will change your mind. If you like twin stick shooters though I think you can get quite a bit of play time out of Polychromatic.
