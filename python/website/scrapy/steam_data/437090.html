About This Game

Wobbly Jungle looks incredibly sweet, but it will drive you to the brink of insanity! That's because in Wobbly Jungle the wolf's in sheep's clothing – erm, sorry... in Sina's enemies!
You have to survive 60 challenging levels that will demand everything from you. The sweet look is actually crystal clear camouflage, as a variety of enemies and obstacles lie in wait. Are you ready to face this challenge? Only those who demonstrate perfect timing and fast fingers stand any chance at all – true to the motto "SURVIVAL OF THE FITTEST!"
In addition to the 60 increasingly difficult and varied levels, lots of challenging enemy types await you, as well as a rewarding "surprise" at the end. Some enemies can be defeated; others you're probably better off avoiding – preferably without stumbling right into the next trap.

  • Varied and exciting: 60 challenging levels await you
  • Gleeful: See where other players died and leave them behind you
  • Challenging: Whether or not you finish the level is entirely in your hands
  • Smart and forward-looking: Many enemies lie in wait – it's probably best to avoid them.
  • Confrontational: Compare your skills with those of your friends with the level summary

Forget the cute graphics and presentation. Sourena Game Studio’s second game on Steam, Wobbly Jungle, is one of the most difficult precision platformers that I have ever played. And that’s a wonderful thing. The studio based in Iran previously developed Legends of Persia (not to be mistaken with Ubisoft’s Prince of Persia franchise). I have to admit that I was almost going to quit the game without having completed the second level as it wasn’t immediately clear where I had to go after reaching a certain point in the level. Thankfully the skill that was being taught in that level, the double jump, held the answer to my prayer and I was able to continue on and complete the level.

I unfortunately do not have very much to write about the game because of just how difficult the game is. This is a game that I want to dedicate some time to so that I can write a better review. I find the graphical presentation to be more than suitable except when you’re left guessing at what is off screen which resulted in most of my deaths. I would argue that this is unfair but that’s my personal opinion. The music sounds good but unfortunately becomes quite repetitive. This is a little bit of a disappointment because had the soundtrack been more varied it would have been a very good soundtrack. The controls are a little on the floaty side but not to the point where it is a detriment to the game. If you spend enough time with the game they will become comfortable.

Although I haven’t gotten very far into the game as of yet I am definitely leaning on the positive side. There’s just the right amount of charm to keep you engaged with the game thanks to good, not great, production values and factoring the sub-$5 price tag.

Wobbly Jungle - Review

Gameplay:
When I first looked at this game I didn't expect much from it. Looked like a very basic, simple platformer. And whilst it isn't revolutionizing the genre, it certainly did a very good job at offering challenging, long lasting and varying gameplay.
The controls are very basic - move and jump - with the addition of double jumps and wall jumps.
Whilst the game seemed to be quite simple at the first few levels, I soon was faced with a repertory of crazy and challenging
twists - forcing me to act both fast, and cleverly.
The game offers 60 levels, which come with lots of hours of diverse adventure.
The only real problem I encountered whilst ingame, was the camera management - often you don't see where you need to jump and you're just forced to leap and hope for the best.
Thus, the gameplay overall is solid, what is missing are some other things:
The menus are very confusing and lack some features (for instance the ability to manage the volume!), and the game comes
with only one track, which repeats every 3 minutes.
Furthermore, great potential was given up, by leaving out the ability to create own levels and share those.

Pros:
+High variety in gameplay
+A lot of content
+Challenging
+Simple controls

Cons:
-Poor menu management
-Soundtrack consists of one track

Conclusion:
Alltogether I can praise this game for both its length and challenge and can only recommend it.
8.5/10

When I first looked at this side-scrolling platformer, I've known at once it will be like Mario (Mario with edited levels, to be precise). And I doubted I'll manage to make a quick run with it.
Boy, I was right. The game is quite challenging just from the start, and I really don't know how many hours of pain and rage-quitting it will bring to people, but I'm sure there will be plenty.

I'm still not that deep into to name Wobbly Jungle a rival for the Super Meat Boy... But surely, it is much, so much more killing than any of the Dark Souls, self-proclaimed "hard games".

So. The game is tough and challenging, yet gratifying. I strongly recommend to use a joystick, though, as your keyboard arrows and space button aren't that precise and durable, as the game demanding.

The only noticeable downside of the game (unless you're a sissy which afraid of the real challenge) - is music. Sure, the looped track sounds nice... for a first hour or so. Yet, there are absolutely no means to tune the volume in the game. It really, really lacking such option. Jeez, even the most most basic flash games have a "mute" button at least! I really hope the devs will do something about it, as the frustration is growing with every hour I'm spending on the same level, with the same track - without the possibility to do something about it!

So far I can recommend the game for the little sessions (about 10-30 minutes long) for the players who are fit to break hard platformer games. This little piggy holds much we're all so fond of, from the times of the first Mario game.

My mark 7.1/10.
PS: I got this game for the free, for that I'd like to thank my friend Petar!
