About This Software

The Source Filmmaker (SFM) is the movie-making tool built and used by us here at Valve to make movies inside the Source game engine. Because the SFM uses the same assets as the game, anything that exists in the game can be used in the movie, and vice versa. By utilizing the hardware rendering power of a modern gaming PC, the SFM allows storytellers to work in a what-you-see-is-what-you-get environment so they can iterate in the context of what it will feel like for the final audience.

To get started, just click the button above to install. The SFM includes all the base game assets from Team Fortress 2 along with assets from some of the first “Meet the Team” short films.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 / Vista (Windows 7 64 bit suggested)
    • Processor: 3.0 GHz P4, Dual Core 2.0 (or higher) or AMD64X2 (or higher)
    • Memory: 2GB (4 GB suggested)
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce 200 series card or better, or AMD Radeon 3000 series or better (NVIDIA GeForce 400 series or AMD Radeon 5000 series preferred)
    • Monitor: 1366 x 768 (1920 x 1080 suggested)
    • Hard Drive: At least 15 GB of Space
    • Sound: DirectX 9.0c compatible USB headset with mic (suggested)

Source Filmmaker

This piece of FREE software by valve is a brilliant feat for Game Development and Marketing. People are now after years of dealing with glitchy TF2 Garry's Mod models can finally animate them properly and with the correct rigging. This software for many has sparked a community of Poster artists, Animators, Careers and finally, NSFW Art.

Yes, the elephant in the room. I have no reason to be mad at the NSFW community of Source Filmmaker, they are actually decent people who I know and love. Instead I part blame the community on the behalf of its "Eugh" nature. Yes, us Poster makers/Animators can no longer use Tumblr as a place to post our SFW art as we will be bombarded with requests to do Porn. However I firmly believe that without that Tumblr Barrier NSFW artworks and animations would flood the community hub, which it does very rarely now. So I'm part glad that NSFW side of things stay within the confines of Tumblr and such areas.

This is a great place to start animation, great tutorials, a big community and a whole lot of learning. Many people who started out animating on this went on to create bigger, bolder projects that ended the collaborates to a TRIP TO VALVE SOFTWARE. If that's not the best part about this kit I don't know what is.

Sadly, over the past year SFM has been neglected. It's last update (as of this review) was 12th Jan 2015, well over a year ago. This means if you needed new content from TF2 or surrounding games, you'd have to upadte it manually. This is off putting, as you would expect Valve to actually do this themselves and not leave it to the community, however many generous users are quick to grab the new content and put it up on the workshop for you to download instantly. Again, thank you to those to do that.

I myself have only 576 hours as of this Review, and my previous reviews where not as serious as this one, I just felt that SFM deserved a decent and moderately lengthy review as I use it pretty much daily.

This is a fantastic animation program.
Like most new Source Filmmaker users, I had no idea what I was doing when I first started this up. Then, after watching a few tutorials on Youtube, I made a few animations; they were pretty bad though.

However, as time progressed and I got more experience, I created animations that I would never have been able to achieve when I first started. They're not MechaFourth or The Winglet level, but they are decent. I've been able to form my own techniques which help my animation look better.

For those who are just getting into animation in general, this program has a steep learning curve. It's recommended that you take the time to watch tutorials, read guides, and experiment with this.

It's a great program to use for animators out there, with it's almost limitless possibilities due to it having a Workshop and other sites dedicated to bringing users content to use in SFM.
