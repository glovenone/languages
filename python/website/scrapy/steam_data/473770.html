Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“BallisticNG is still in active development and having it on Steam in Early Access allows people to come in and suggest new ideas and mention issues that we ourselves might not have noticed. This is important for ensuring that the initial 1.0 release is as stable and feature complete as possible.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The estimated time for BallisticNG being in Early Access is around half a year to quarter of a year. This could change depending on how the game grows, but as of right now that's where it roughly is.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Throughout Early Access the game will be receiving bug fixes and polish. New features and content will undoubtedly be added but the main concern over the course of the game's Early Access lifespan is stability and presentation.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The Early Access version is already pretty representative of what the game wants to be. As of entering Early Access the game was on version 0.4, its second beta release.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game is free and will always be free, including future track packs for adding additional official content.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Hopefully the community can help bring new ideas to the game that haven't been thought of before. Making a game that people want to play is important, and allowing people to voice their ideas is very important in supporting that.”

About This Game

BallisticNG is a free anti-gravity racing game. You can play as a variety of teams competing in the 2159 Anti-Gravity League, each with their own unique vehicles. Visually the game emulates the limitations of the Playstation One hardware and a lot of care has been made into making sure it looks authentic while including more modern graphic techniques. The game has 5 different speed classes for different skill sets, starting off slow and eventually becoming brutally fast.

Features

  • 14 tracks in different locations
  • 13 teams setup for different play styles
  • 5 gamemodes - Arcade, Time Trial, Free Play, Tournament and Survival
  • A variety of weapons for damaging and slowing other racers
  • Modding Tools - Track Editor, Ship Editor and Custom Sounds
  • Cockpit view - each ship has a unique cockpit!
  • A soundtrack full of songs inspired by electronic music from the 90s

Early Access

As an early access notice, the dev team are very happy to help out in the game's community discussions if you have any issues with the game at all. We check it often so you should get a response fairly quickly and if the problem is big enough, you can expect a fix in the next update. As part of Early Access, BallisticNG is getting very frequent updates, sometimes small and sometimes big.

Welcome to BallisticNG. Buckle up, pilots.

Overall, the game is very well put together for an indie project. With old 32-bit style graphics and an adrenaline-pumping soundtrack, old WipEout fans will be taken on huge nolstagia trips and newcomers serenaded into Anti-Grav Racing games. And as a free game, how could you say no.

my hands hurt.

i mean, i'm not even particularly good - sure, i have some records with the barracuda but i'm not a zen-level enough pilot to pop it open normally. nevertheless i've spent hours chasing hundredths of a second on the leaderboards. i powered through each season of the campaign, learning just how i needed to dart around each corner to keep up. i've restarted a million times at the first imperfection, frustrated by my own inconsistency. and the process has not been kind to my hands.

and it's all totally worth it.

if you're at all a wipeout fan in any capacity - whether you're a middling player like me, a venom class pilot that likes to casually race, or a literal zen-level deity with a neGcon - install this game. it's still perhaps rough around the edges in some places, but don't let that or the early access tag keep you away. there's a ton of AG goodness in this package.

the visuals (and the physics) hearken back to the days of the PS1 - the game looks like 2097/XL, feels like 3, and has a wealth of tools for for the intrepid modder. custom ships are already supported (with custom tracks forthcoming) if you know your way around a 3D model, and steam workshop support is, i believe, in the works.

online play isn't in yet, but don't let that stop you either. the leaderboard competition is stiff as it is, so you can still show off your skill and sate your competitive appetite. and besides, it'll be great practice for when the online mode does release.

best of all, the devs are wipeout-heads, so you can guarantee that the game will stay true to the spirit and even the aesthetic of the wipeout series.

so grab a controller and come put your hands through the wringer. you'll be grateful you did.

The ships you will fly all differ in stats,and all behave differently,just like in Wipeout, there are ships for beginners with good handling and low speed, fast heavy ships with poor handling and even some fast and maneuvrable ships with low shields, try to find your own!
You will fly them on various tracks that have this old-school feeling doing much with little
5 different speed classes are present, they will suit everyone, from sunday drivers to insane record hunters.
Keep in mind that this is an hommage to older games,mistakes are heavily punished, walls slow you down immensly,you can die pretty often(mostly from walls),and there is no hand telling you permanently what to do.

Controls are not "clunky","strange" or "unusual", no no, you're just not good enough yet.
It takes time to master the game, try again, and again until you start making perfect laps, then increase the speedclass each time.
If you really like challenges or if you are a masochist, you can try flying the Nexus ship in the highest speedclass(Zen).


Can't wait for multiplayer!
