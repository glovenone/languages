Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Game is playable, but it is still not finished and there are incomplete features to our final imagination. We want to get players on board, listen to their feedback and make a better card game with their help. We strongly believe that involving as many players as possible in the development process is a great way to achieve fast results which satisfy both of us, the developer and the players.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Our wish is to finish WolfWars during this year (2015). However we'll be ready to develop/improve it further as long as there is a need.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“It is planned - at the end - to have many features which aren't there yet, such as:

- Powerups
- Summoning creatures
- Different locations to play
- Weather magic events - storm, fire, flood, sand storm, earthquake.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current version of the game is playable and includes the core features. Currently there are 4 heroes and 16 different playing cards.

The game has 2 singleplayer modes which can be played against bots and 2 multiplayer modes supporting up to 4 players.

WolfWars is not a game about trading or collecting cards. Each player has the same amount of cards from the beginning to the end. There is no f2p/pay2win model, you all have same chances at the start.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Possibly, yes. Since it has many features which are not there yet, we will re-evaluate the price when we complete the Early Access stage but we know that it wont be a dramatic price change.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are open to any good suggestion and opinion. We will be monitoring the forums on Steam and are looking forward to receiving your feedback.”

About This Game

WolfWars is a fantasy tactical strategy card game.

It may sound a bit weird, but we took & simplified rules from many other card games and created a new concept so anyone on the planet can enjoy it.

WolfWars is not a game about trading or collecting cards, but you will be able to build or edit your decks with available cards and heroes. Each player has the same amount of cards from the beginning to the end. There is no f2p/pay2win model, you all have same chances at the start.

Before you start the game, you will need to create your deck. Deck needs to have 1 hero and 5 playing cards. You can have as many decks as you wish to have. These 5 cards are all what you have during one game. You do not sacrifice any of them.

Key Features:

  • Singleplayer (PVE)
  • Cross platform multiplayer (2-4players)
  • Simultaneous turns
  • Realtime turns
  • Co-op (1vs2, 1vs3 2vs2) (players vs players & players vs AI-bot)

Early Access Review
Posted: 6 January, 2015
I've been playing MTG for 5 years and nothing will ever compare to that so never get your hopes up. This game is trying to stand out on it's own and it does just that. If you've ever played a TCG then you already know how to play this game. So far so good although I just finished the tutorial and checked out deck building.
Give it a shot, help this game grow into what you want it to be with forum feedback. You will pay less in Alpha now than you will in full later. Already my first trading card is worth $1.00 on the market. Makin' money! I will update this when I play more.

Update-ish: Well I played a bit more. So far there are two match variants: deathmatch and horde. Deathmatch is always 4 player unless online. You can attack anyone.
Horde mode I do not understand. I'm pitted against one other and I can seemingly only use each card once during the fight then nothing happens until I quit the match... It happened twice. I just played Horde again and beat the first guy, then two more A.I. showed up and our health was reset and they killed me eventually. So this appears to be some kind of survival gametype.

The lack of cards available and card variants so far makes this game kinda stale out of the box but that will change in due time. The cards you can choose from are Creature (oppressive), Witch (defensive), aaand that's it. Now, they do call a card type the "Hero", he adds a permanent boost to your playstyle depending on which one you choose like auto-healing every turn, taking one less damage from each attacker, and so on. The cards have a bit of synergy to them but are basically the same across elements (fire, water, air, earth). At one point myself and two A.I. kept healing and attacking and running out of stamina (mana) and this kept on forever until I had to quit because no winner was surfacing. There needs to be more card types similar to enchantments or something so the game can feel more suspenseful rather than predictable.

In the end I think this game will evolve into a whole system rather than alpha-chunks but for now try it out, give them feedback, and help make a great digital card game.

Monday, June 29th, 2015: Update: No work done on this and it's still stale, not worth it any more, it had promise.

Not much here....The artwork is great on the cards but the tutorial is the only thing close to complete. I love TCG and am happy to support any developer brave enough to attempt to make their own. However I can not recomend this game when I can say I am honestly this bored of playing it after twenty minutes. Needs improvement on pretty much every level. I think unfortunately they may have oversimplified the basic mechancs a bit too much in an attempt to target card players.

The artwork is great and I give them points for trying something a bit different than the MTG, Infinity Wars, Might & Magic, Solforge, or Hex all of which I would recommend for a first time player over this. Even with the learning curve involved players will be rewarded with a deeper appreciation for the strategy and tactical awareness each has to offer. That's something I see missing from this game. I will be checking in hoping for some updates but for now I've got to give it the thumbs down. Points for trying to do something different and putting together a decent UI (not to be understated in how enjoyable a digital tcg experience can be) but overall this is just not quite worth your time or dollars unless your a tcg fanatic such as myself.

2/10 (Early Access Review here's hoping it goes up)
