A short point-and-click adventure game with a few puzzles. This is not a hidden object game, though it does have some similarities to them.

Olivia is visiting her uncle, who is an archeologist, and ends up going along with him and his young assisstant to a site at the ruins of an old church. Once there they discover that the workers have disappeared! And they discover something never seen before. There is some funny dialogue. The overall tone is light-hearted.

The game is played by clicking with the mouse to find objects on the screen, and clicking on the sides or bottom of the screen to move to the next area. There is a hint button if you are stuck and don't know what to do. There are also a few mini games that appear, but you can skip them if you want. Certain areas of the screen sometimes pop up with more detail, like a part of a shelf, and usually there's a special item there you have to interact with.

The items you find are stored in a backpack which can be opened and closed by the zipper (very nice idea there!), though sometimes you have to close the backpack before you can exit a room because you can't click at the bottom of the screen while the item tray is up. I had no logical problems with the items and their uses, unlike most hidden object games I've played (in one game you have to find scissors to cut something even though you already have a knife that could have suited that purpose). It's refreshing to play a game that doesn't make me go "Oh, seriously?" and facepalm.

The graphics are nice. Very colorful. Objects are nicely defined, sort of like cartoon or comic book. I prefer this style of art to the more realistic style because in this type of game I want to know what I can click on. I've played some games where I didn't know what something was or that I was supposed to click there until the hint button told me.

No voices. And I really don't remember the music, if there was any. If there is music it was very bland and did not stand out. Or else I had my volume down.

Total time playing according to Steam is 1.8 hours. I did have a little problem figuring something out so some of that is wasted time going back and forth trying things. So it might be an hour or little over for fast players.

It's a good price for the length and content. Actually I've seen similar games priced higher, so this one is a bargain.
