About This Game

Aliens Versus Predator Classic 2000 features the Colonial Marine, Alien and Predator Campaigns and the frenetic single-player Skirmish mode from the original title: Alien Versus Predator Gold Edition, which was released to massive acclaim in 2000.
Alien Versus Predator Classic 2000 has been updated to run on modern PCs and laptops using DirectX 9.0c and includes support for Xbox 360 Controller for Windows.
The three most ferocious species in the universe in a bloodthirsty battle for the ultimate prize: survival. Take your pick: be a Colonial Marine, a Predator or an Alien. Fight through suspense-filled environments: from starships and space stations to colonial bases and eerie planetscapes. One wrong move turns you from hunter to prey.
  • Three campaigns: Colonial Marine, Alien and Predator
  • Includes all the levels from the original AvP Gold Edition plus the Millennium Expansion Pack
  • Skirmish mode against infinite Alien hordes
  • Bonus Episodes
  • Unique abilities, weapons and equipment for each species
  • Scale walls and elevator shafts as an ALIEN with frightening speed as you attack and devour your victims
  • Stalk your prey as the deadly PREDATOR using wrist blades, shoulder cannons and stealth mode
  • Lock and load as a COLONIAL MARINE using motion trackers, grenade launchers and flame-throwers to exterminate your foes
  • Supports Xbox 360 Controller for Windows
  • Music from the original soundtrack CD

Multiplayer Features

  • Integrated with Steam Overlay
  • Uses Steam name
  • Host/Join games via bare-bones in-game lobby browser
  • Can invite and join friends and others via the Steam overlay
  • Game attempts host migration
  • Dedicated servers are not supported or planned

You ever come across something once in your life where you feel you need to play, watch, listen to, or read but find something along those same lines which is absolutely better than what you found in the first place? This has happened to me several times. Meshuggah with their Obzen album and then finding their Destroy Erase Improve album, deep and engrossing anime with Neon Genesis Evangelion and then Serial Experiments Lain, the Rainbow Six series with Lockdown & Critical Hour and then Rogue Spear and Raven Shield, things like that.

The biggest example would be the Alien Vs. Predator movie, and then exploring both series' previous movies because I loved the creature design so much. As a kid in 5th grade, I watched the original Predator movie (GET TO THE CHOPPAH!!!!) and it absolutely blew me away. I had never seen anything like it and it really blew AVP out of the water. Then I watched the original Alien movie on VHS (It was at a yard sale for god's sake!) and again I was blown away. It was a good thrill and I found out why people hated AVP and AVPR so much.

But alas, AVP would soon get a second chance. I found out that there was a video game coming out called Aliens Vs. Predator and that it would be coming out on a bunch of platforms. Xbox 360, PS3, and this new thing called OnLive. I tried it out for the first time at my sister's house, my nephews owned the game, and I was blown away again (Okay I'm easy to please when it comes to Sci-Fi. :P)! I finally got to play as these deadly creatures, and a human soldier, and I would be playing through a story which is played out through three separate campaigns. I had to own this game immediately!

Then...time passed. I never got to own the game. Hell, I still don't own it. What I DID stumble upon though was that there were already quite a few AVP games that were released throughout the years. Knowing I had already stumbled upon a bunch of awesome classic shooters before, I decided to try this AVP game out. Holy...f***ing...sh*t. This game right here makes the 2010 AVP look like Call Of Duty. This game is a freaking blast. I'm not even joking when I say that this game needs to be in everyone's collection. It's THAT good. Now I know what you're asking, why do you think this game is so good when the other AVP game you checked out before was more recent. Well, let me tell you why.

Alright so story...uh...well...I'm not really sure what the story is. It does have three campaigns though, so I will tell you all I really know. As the Colonial Marine, I guess there's a Xenomorph embryo inside you, so I guess you're looking for some way to get it out of you I never really beat the game, which I will get to in a moment, but guessing by the whole embryo thing, Weyland-Yutani is probably going to use it for research. With the Alien, I guess your location has been violated by the Marines, not really surprising, and you gotta get them out. Predator...I'm not sure what he's doing there. Okay sure I'll be honest. The story is garbage, but whatever. Who plays these kinds of games for the story, anyway?

Okay so as I've said, the game contains three separate campaigns, one for each creature, so how do they play? Exactly how they do in the movies. With the Colonial Marine, you pretty much go around shooting the crap out of the Xenomorphs, which literally come out of the god damn walls, and also shooting up Predators, which have no time to bleed. With the Xenos, it's all about causing as much mayhem as possible. You not only have his claws, but also the signature tail. Not only that, but you can use the infamous bite when sneaking up on foes. With the Predator, it's just like in the movies. You HAVE to use stealth. Luckily, you have all of the Predator's weapons at your disposal. From the shoulder cannons to the wrist blades, you are sure to have a good time.

There are a few problems I have with the game though. One of my biggest complaints is...it's a little too dark. Like yeah, I can understand going for suspense, but because the game is supposed to be a classic shooter like Quake or Doom, this really doesn't fit. I know there are vision modes for both the Xenos and the Marines, but, at least in the Marine's case, there are moments where in the dark the lights are pretty dim, but they are blinding in night-vision mode. Another problem I have is the difficulty. There are some REALLY cheap deaths on the higher difficulties. I can barely get through a level without swearing at least once. Luckily, however, there is an unlimited save feature exclusive to Classic 2000, albiet via command, that makes this game a bit easier. If you are a fan of a challenge like this, buy it.

My other problem with this is the multiplayer. Well it's not really the game's fault, but it's really...empty. There's no excuse for it being this empty either. Rebellion made sure that in this re-release that this game would be compatible with not only Steam players, but GOG players! Why is nobody playing? Come on! This game is amazing! I know it's a nearly 20-year-old game, but it's no excuse. Quake III and Unreal Tournament still have people playing, so there's no reason as to why this game doesn't!

But overall, the reason you should get this game, even with its small annoyances, is how well this game really emulates the feeling the movies have with the three species you play as. With the marine, you really do need to kill or you will be killed. With the Xenomorph, you need to stay in the shadows while at the same time making humans your prey. With the Predator, you need to stay invisible and kill the humans stealthily or you will be shot. This game is amazing. If you are a fan of either the Alien franchise, the Predator franchise, or even both, you need to add this game to your collection.

And please, play some multiplayer. This game needs some love.

Alien versus Predator Classic 2000 is a first-person "shooter" made by Rebellion. As being both an Alien fan and a Predator fan, I bought this game having little to no knowledge about the game. My results... well let's take a look shall we:

Pros:
+Amazing Gameplay - Each character (Alien / Xenomorph, Colonial Marine, and Predator) has a different play style. Example: Xeno. = Stealth & Speed, Predator = Michael Bay everything, and Marine = Survival. This variety of gameplay makes the game more diverse gameplay-wise.
+The Campaigns - The Campaigns are done very well. None of the Campaigns feel the same. It's almost like playing three games in one. Alien campaign = Alien: Isolation Xenomorph's point of view, 2. Marine campaign = What Aliens: Colonial Marines should've been, 3. Predator campaign = The perfect Predator game (Come on Terminator wheres your game)
+Respect for the source material - The entire game respects the source material from both the Alien and Predator franchises. Locations like Fury 161 and LV-426 and weapons like the smart gun and the shoulder canon are faithfully recreated.
+Facehuggers - Thats all I'm going to say...
+Not made by Gearbox or Paul W.S. Anderson

Cons:
- While playing as the Alien, the Alien's fast speed does cause the camera to go all over the place. This may cause motion sickness.
-Level design may cause some confusion since there are no maps and some parts of some levels may look the same as other parts.
-Objectives can be tedious as sometimes you may or may not know what to do / where to go. Objectives are given by messages that pop up on the top of the screen, but they can easily be missed.
-Graphics have not aged that well, but their decent for the time. The graphics kind of look like Half-Life 1's graphics.

Overall, (8.5/10) AVP Classic 2000 is a really good game. However, this game does have some flaws and is a bit challenging at times (but it can be completed if you have a lot of paitence). If your fine with the cons and are an Alien / Predator fan, then this game is for you.

Aliens versus Predator Classic 2000 is an example of how to do First-Person-Shooters right. The fast gameplay requires more skill and focus than most modern First-Person-Shooters. Instead of trying to level up your character to get OP weapons, most weapons have a legitimate purpose, and most of them don't feel too overpowered (except Disc, Pred Pistol, Skeeter, and Sadar. Not to forget Mines). Instead of grinding to get perks to power yourself up, Aliens versus Predator requires skill from the start.

Aliens versus Predator Classic 2000 allows the player to take control of three species, Aliens, Predators, or the human Marines. Each have their advantages and disadvantages.

The Alien is a melee only species, a hurtful disadvantage, but is patched up by the speed and wallcrawling ability that the Aliens have. Aliens also can smell the scents of other beings, allowing it to see its victims in the dark. The Alien regains health by headbiting Predators and Humans, you can do this on both the living and the dead. Obviously headbiting live beings is harder than dead ones, but gains back more health.

The Predator is a mix of Melee and ranged weapons. The majority of his weapons, Medicomp, and cloak are powered by 'Field Charge'. This will slowly recharge if you are not using anything that uses charge, or you can find field-charge items around the map which will automatically give you full energy. The only weapons that don't use energy are the Wristblades, and Disc (Unless you use the recall ability, this will use a small amount of energy), as well as the Speargun. But this uses ammo, which only replenishes upon death. The Predator can heal using his Medicomp, this will use a large amount of energy, but puts you back to full health everytime (TIP: The Medicomp's secondary fire can extinguish you if you're on fire). In some of the bonus levels the Predator gains a new gadget, the grappling hook, which can pull him to higher heights. The Predator also has three vision modes, which can help him spot enemies. The first vision mode can help spot humans, the second aliens, and the third other Predators and field-charges (Of course you also have the default normal vision).

The Marine is a ranged only species. The marine uses weapons like the pulse rifle, smartgun, minigun, etc. The Marine replenishes health with medkits, found about through-out levels. The marine can use armor, which can protect against Alien acid and absorbs small amounts of damage. In some of the bonus levels the Marines get a jetpack! In multiplayer Marines can select a specialist sub-class, this will allow them to pick from any of the weapons in the game (unless game host restricted it), and you will always spawn with the selected weapon, but you can only use that weapon (and the pistol).

Each species can unlock bonus missions after you complete the main game, (you will unlock more if you beat it on a higher difficulty) these levels are the levels from other species singleplayer, for example: You can unlock a backwards version of the Predator's first level as Marine if you beat the Marine SP on Director's cut [Hard].

Not to forget, you can unlock cheat options in the game by completing certain objectives through-out the levels. These can be things like slow-mo whenever you are near enemies. Or unlimited speargun ammo and more spears per shot as Predator.

The Multiplayer is where this game shines most. Fast gameplay and the different play-styles of each species make for very fun and memorable experience. This is one of the fastest games ever, you will die, fast, and a lot. It is extremely well balanced, and I almost never feel like a player is winning because of the species they are playing as. But, unfortunately, this game has a small player base, so at times you may not be able to find a game. Because of this the community doesn't play competitively [most of the time], anymore. Servers will also usually have a small amount of players. 8-player games are kinda rare, but when they happen, it's a lot of fun!

Aliens versus Predator Classic 2000 is easily one of the best games ever made.
