About This Game

Bloop is back and it’s bigger and better than ever!

Your favourite chemistry potions puzzler is back in Bloop Reloaded and ready to test the brain cells like never before. Make sure you save as much of the potions as possible. How you do it is up to you!

Features
  • 27 Levels
  • Build Paths and guide your potions to safety.
  • Use environmental physics to your advantage.
  • There is no one correct solution, just the limits of your creative mind!
  • See what happens when you mix potions.
  • Create your own levels

═══════════════════════════════════
OVERVIEW

Bloop Reloaded

This game is similar to classics like Lemmings. You guide an oject to an objective. The main difference between this game and Lemmings is that while Lemmings deals mostly with a programming aspect to guide the objects to the objective, Bloop Reloaded uses physics to guide liquids. You must create platforms to guide the liquids to the bottle, avoiding hazards and sometimes mixing to liquids to get an end product. Aside from the pre-level setup of platforms, the player can use the right mouse button to pick up and move quantities of the liquid.

http://store.steampowered.com/app/315060/

═══════════════════════════════════
PROS & CONS

Pros

  • The ambient music is great

Cons

  • The mechanics are poorly explained
  • The right mouse button mechanic defeats the puzzle aspect of the game
  • Instead of a cooldown for the right mouse button mechanic, you must hunt down and left click on floating ojects to gain more time that this ability can be active

═══════════════════════════════════
SUMMARY & FINAL RATING

This game suffers a few design flaws that make it less fun to play. Being able to pick up the liquids and move them around defeats the need to solve a puzzle. If Lemmings allowed such a function, the game would not have been as successful. The fact that this ability can be active for a certain amount of time and then be recharged hurts the gameplay. Having the skill slowly recharge would make the skill more interesting than having to switch tasks to find the skill recharge items. It plays like two distantly related minigames that are expected to be perceived as a whole game. I really like puzzle games for their need to plan out how to complete a level, but the right mouse button skill negates the need to really plan for anything.

3/10

Posted: 23 February, 2015
This game is a lot of fun and reminds me a lot of those oil and water dripper games that I used to really enjoy as a kid. The presentation is nice and the fluid physics seem to be pretty spot on. They react how you expect them to react and do so reliably each time which is important for physics based gameplay like this. It seems like it would be a simple concept that would be difficult to find challenge in, but the levels do up the ante as you progress. Very early in I was presented with a few that really had me scratching my head a bit. But they weren't so difficult that I left the experience frustrated.

Most importantly, the dev is very responsive to the community and you can tell they are very passionate about the game. I had a small scoring issue which I posted about and got a reply within hours. The bug was not only fixed within the week but followed up on in the comment thread by the dev to let me know. It's a pleasure to support devs who are so active with their community and willing to fix things even when they can't replicate the bugs we're seeing. It's a refreshing change from so many of these unfinished or bug-ridden games that release then wallow for months without updates. Well worth a purchase if even just to support those qualities that we should be looking for in game devs. It doesn't hurt that it's pretty fun to boot.
