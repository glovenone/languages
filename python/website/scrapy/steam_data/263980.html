About This Game

From the artists behind the smash hit Towerfall comes the critically-acclaimed Out There Somewhere.

Inspired by games such as Cave Story and Super Metroid, you play as an astronaut stranded on a strange and dangerous alien planet. Low on fuel, your only hope for escape is to make your way to the planet’s core.

It will take brains and quick reflexes to survive in this incredibly clever action platformer.

“Out There Somewhere isn't a normal game, though: it's a platformer with a devious twist and a truly shocking difficulty curve. It's a platformer with a very late level, for example, that contains absolutely no platforms at all - just empty space and a doorway right near the ceiling. This is challenging stuff, but it's wonderfully creative with it, and I'm not sure I can recommend it enough.”

-Eurogamer 9/10

Basic Information
Title: Out There Somewhere
Developer: MiniBoss
Publisher: EQ-Games, MiniBoss
Genre: Classic style platformer

General Impression
Out There Somewhere is a 2D platformer that emulates the classic side-scrolling action of older titles while offering a breath of fresh air in the almost entire absence of platforming sections. The story is all about a stranded astronaut trying to survive. Quite odd for a platformer is the lack of many enemies as well. You will mostly teleport here and there with ocassional text based dialogue pop-ups. This is definitely more an experience about exploration than combat and avoiding obstacles. Even if the game is rather short it felt a bit boring at times. It features simple graphics and sounds but for its price point and considering the inclusion of Cards and Achievements, the game is ultimately worth trying out.

Strong Points
+ Plenty of collectibles.
+ Steam Trading Cards & Achievements.

Weak Points
- Unbalanced difficulty.
- Too short.

Rating 6/10

Posted: 25 June
Great little game. It'll take you about 2 hours to beat the first time but it was so good I played it until I got all the achievements.

I feel like everything this game does is good. The teleporting mechanics are on point, the graphics are very good if a bit too dark for my liking, the level design is very competent, the game provides a decent challenge and it's very rewarding to try and speedrun it.

If I had to complain about something, the side-scrolling bits at the beginning and the end, while justified story-wise, feel a bit out of place. Although they are competent and don't feel rushed.

I've seen some people say it's too short but I didn't find that a problem. I often get quite disappointed with games that overstay their welcome by adding more and more mechanics that end up not feeling coherent with the main mechanics of the game. That's the problem I had with Portal 2 over the original Portal and I would say Out There Somewhere falls on the side of Portal: it has few mechanics that work very well and that are exploited as much as possible through the course of the game. All the mechanics that get added through the course of the game complement the main teleporting mechanic and get used from that point onwards.

Overall, for its price, it's a must to have in your library. Also, it seems like it's always discounted so more reason to own it.
