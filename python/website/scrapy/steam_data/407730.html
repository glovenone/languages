About This Game



Dungeons Of Kragmor is a new unique mixture of the survival, FPS, and dungeon crawler genres. In the lands of Kragmor, wizards who commit crimes are sentenced to a special magical realm known as the Dungeons Of Kragmor. Slay epic creatures - Upgrade your wizard tower - Craft - Gather - PvP - Dungeon explore and more.



The Council of Kragmor once used this realm to rule it's lands from afar. When the need arose for a new realm to contain those who broke their laws, they left this once majestic land to house the evil wizards of Kragmor. Explore the overworld for supplies and journey into the depths of the dungeon to discover hidden relics to boost your strength.



The Council's towers still inhabit the overworld areas. Claim one of the twelve wizard towers as your base of operations. Upgrade your tower to obtain powerful crafting stations. Defend your home from other players seeking your treasures.



As the years have gone by the Dungeons of Kragmor have become populated with all manner of vile creatures, bringing with them magical components that can be used to create artifacts. Summon minions to gather stone and wood, while you rain your fury down on creatures to obtain powerful crafting components. Select from a variety of equipment tiers from common to epic as you progress through the crafting stations in your tower.



Immerse yourself in a vibrant colorful world. Journey into the depths and discover treasures. Stake your claim in a volatile land and defend it from others seeking to free you of your obligations! Welcome to the Dungeon.

Yes, I originally put up a positive review for this game but, a couple of things have happened since I put up that review. It was recently brought to my attention that this game is a rip off of a game that's currently on Steam called "Counter Spell." Another reason that has made me change this review is the fact that I've found more and more issues crop up throughout the game, which is stuff that should have been fixed before the release. I understand that you guys have lawsuits that you want to pay attention to, but you're not the ones being forced to follow the lawsuits plans, you can control them because you're the ones who's placing them. That means you could have easily mde more time to fix some of the issues that have cropped up in this game, within my extra hour of playtime. I know that Digital Homicide know about these issues because they were in the same server I was in, when I was complaining about these issues. It actually hurts to put this up because I really wanted to like this game. Digital Homicide has made turd after turd and this one was actually half decent and I originally rated this a 6/10 but the rating has dropped down because of the rip off and problems which I will call out now.

The inventory screen, doesn't work half the time. The reason that I didn't bring this up in my original review was because I thought it was an issue that I was encountering because most people in the servers I was in were fine and weren't encountering any issues. But, as I looked at the reviews, I found out that I wasn't the only one encountering this issue. DH knew that this issue was apparent, because I told them I couldn't open my inventory and they've barely done anything, even when more people have complained about it.

The main problem it seems to suffer from which I discovered in the extra hour of playtime I put in was that this game doesn't seem to know if it wants to be an MMO or a single player game. It's basically a single player game and you see other players running around every once in a while. You can kill each other, I'll give them that, that's already a step up from Forsaken Uprising but the aspects that they put into the game don't really scream MMO material. The enemies that you see in world are only enemies you can see. The other players can't see these enemies that you can see, they see their own version of it. They won't see the enemies attack you or anything.

The gameplay also gets really tedious and boring the more you play so it doesn't have much lasting appeal.

Some of the servers sometimes randomly dissappear off of the server list aswell isn't that right "Arcatraz" still can't believe they spealt that wrong but hey, I always make errors in my review so I would be hypocritical if I were to criticize them for that, if I didn't admit that I do that as well.

Is the game just ll bad? No. I can see some form of effort went into this and I applaud Digital Homcide for that as I've criticised their previous releases for putting little to no effort put into them. I know for a fact that an MMO is hard to do and I praised Forsaken Uprising for being an OK first attempt but as a game, it was bad. With Kragmor, you've taken some of the criticism from Forsaken and you've fixed SOME of the issues that plagued it.

Graphics are bright and colourful and really appealing to look at. Enemies are even bright and colourful and fit in with this world. Like I said in the previous review of this that I put up, I do know that these are assets but these assets fit in well together, look at all these ssets and see wht they have in common:

Evil mushroom: https://www.assetstore.unity3d.com/en/#!/content/24559
Goblin: https://www.assetstore.unity3d.com/en/#!/content/58800

I still appreciate that this game tried to bring something new to the table, blending all sorts of different game genres into one and working relatively well (except for the MMO part.)

I really wanted to like this game as it's better than other DH games but after this stuff has been brought forward o me, as well as getting an extra hour of play time, I can't recommend it and I'm also going to remove my curator recommendation. I'm sorry DH, you came really close to making something half decent, which is why I've gone a bit easier on them this time

Pros
+ Colourful graphics
+ Assets fit well
+ Blends different genres together

Cons
- Bugs
- Controls sometimes unrepsonsive
- Little to no lasting appeal
- Can't decide if it wants to be multiplayer or single player

Final verdict: 4/10
