What is Space Survival?

Space Survival is the next great SIMPLE 2D casual space action shooter where you have to defend against wave of enemies, have fast reflexes and strong will to win. It is great way to relax, but also a great challenge. How long can you stay focused?

I received this game from the publisher for review purposes.

Before I go into the review, I feel I should point out that I think Space Survival should be an alpha.

Space Survival is a simple shmups ala asteroid, except not as good.

The game play is simple to grasp, the player needs to avoid the enemy onslaught and shoot them down. Unfortunately due to a number of factors; the game does not allow for a reasonable way to do that. The controls are all on the mouse, the players’ ship will follow to where they right click, and shoot where the players left click. Meaning it's impossible to escape in one direction while firing in another, and since mobs will end up behind the player as they escape, the only way to shoot the mobs down will be to stop escaping and face them down. Each phase is 300 seconds long, during which wave after wave of enemies will attack the player – but only if, the player kills a wave. So if a player simply escapes without shooting for 300 seconds, they will advance to the next level. This is what I did for 20 minutes before worrying about carpal tunnel and committing suicide.

In its current form Space Survival is not nearly complete. There are hardly any options to speak of, just some resolution options. The controls cannot be remapped, and as I've explained in the previous paragraph the current controls are poorly chosen. There is only one game mode, which is survival. And there are a number of other issues with the game that I would consider subjective so they may not affect others, but definitely detracted from my experience.

There is one thing I do like about the game, and that is the fluidity and responsiveness of my ship in game.

Pros:
  • Fluid and responsive motion
Cons
  • An unfinished product
  • Only one game mode
  • Poorly thought out controls
  • Lacking options menu, with no control or sound options
  • Excessive explosions and flashing lights that are painful to look at
  • I can simply break the game; going from start to finish without ever shooting an enemy
  • There is no variation in the game, it's just a number of levels that are exactly the same with a different background

Finally, there really isn't enough of a game for me to give a score or a final verdict; I think the game will require a lot more content before I can say anything substantial about it as a shmups. For now, as the product stands, I cannot recommend it, there is very little there.

I'm going to cut this review short and jump right into the good and the bad about Space Survival after a short summary.

Space Survival is a game where you are a spaceship that flies around shooting orange enemies. The game is broken up into phases, with each one requiring you to survive for 5 minutes. If any of the orange enemies touch you, its game over, and you have to start from the beginning. You move with the right mouse button and shoot with the left, and where you point the cursor determines where you move and shoot. You can also hit Q to switch your weapon to one that takes up more energy but fires faster.

Good:
- Interesting concept
- Has a lot of potential
- Buying it supports indie game developers

Bad, or what I would improve:
- While the game features no checkpoints and no saving as a highlight, its honestly a negative aspect. The lack of a story mode and checkpoints leaves the game feeling a bit pointless at times.
- There is only survival mode; add more variety.
- The system requirements don't seem to match up to the gameplay. I have more than the recommended requirements and find myself lagging at times.
- The enemy tails of light last too long and probably help cause some of that lag.
- Make it an option where you can turn down the size of the explosion created by dead enemies. When shooting into a group of enemies, it is hard to tell where any of them are after the long, large explosion of the first one.
- Check the hit boxes. I'm not positive, but the hit boxes seem a bit off.
- Allow the player to modify the controls. I personally find the most complicated part of this game the controls. If you add in WASD support for moving and just leave the mouse for shooting, I feel like this game would be much more simple. The reason I say this is because I find myself moving instead of shooting and vice-versa.
- Phase timers. 5 minutes leaves players feeling like they accomplished nothing. If you were to make the timers 1 minute, players would feel more accomplished and like the game is actually going somewhere.
- Score. The score is completely messed up at the moment. Every time you die, it resets, so you don't have any idea of how well you are actually playing the game. I would add in an ability to make a name in the main lobby or a profile and then have the score with your name on it flash up after you die. Something like "Alex recieved 12900 points".
- Add a leaderboard to complement this new score system, whether it be personal or worldwide.
- A personal suggestion you could play with is an exclamation point or something indicating where a new enemy is going to spawn.

Overall, the lack of "unnecessary stuff" that this game gets rid of is more harmful to the gameplay than helpful. The game doesn't feel like you are getting anywhere without all of it. That being said, the game is still being developed and there is still hope for it. If this was the final iteration of the game and it wasn't being developed, I wouldn't recommend it to anyone, but for 66 or 99 cents, its not a horrible buy.

Right now I would rate the game a 4/10, but if the game developers implement more features, I feel like that rating could quickly rise. I'm only recommending this game so more people are attracted to the game through higher reviews, causing more people to purchase it and hopefully allow the creators to make changes for the better.

A pretty tiny space shootem-up game.
It's at first stages in development because of that it lacks at content of course.
But not bad for fresh start & still developing game.
Developer says that they'll develop the game further & they'll pay attention to feedbacks.
Under these infos & this price & if you like to support new developers, it's acceptable.

Content that need to be added as far as I observed for now:
* There should be more settings like button config, 1920x1080 res., sounds etc..
* Tutorial isn't essential but that would be good if there's a little text or something for button config at first start.
* The exploding effects are very very shiny. You can't see other incoming enemies when you shoot 2-3 or more.
* It's kinda hard. Developers want this game to be challenging as they say in the game introducion, still there could be an ingame difficulty curve, starting easy & getting harder every stage.
