About This Game

Curse of Mermos features Abdu, an adventurer searching for fame and fortune in the ruins of Ancient Egypt. One of the greatest mysteries still standing is the location of Pharaoh Ebo, once a great ruler of the land. Abdu is on the brink of a great discovery but he will find out that Ebo’s tomb hides more mysteries than he thought.

Curse of Mermos is a free, lighthearted hack and slash game with fast paced action and challenging combat. Players wield the Hammer of Horus or shoot the Crossbow of Neith to defeat hordes of monsters in the tomb of Ebo. The game features unlockable abilities and skill upgrades for personalized combat. Special game modifiers allow players to customize each playthrough.

The game is fun, free, but most importantly, very difficult.

As you progress through the game, you will encounter more and more difficult enemies. For example, an enemy you can't hit, until it slams it's shield onto the ground. However, once those same shield enemies clump together, it becomes very difficult to kill them. There are also enemies that heal their allies, two different ranged attackers, an enemy that rolls near you, in droves, and blows up, an enemy that throws flame AoE at you, and small critters.

The game became very fustrating towards the end, which was at wave 29-30. You are expected to retrieve 3,000 souls, which is a lot. You will be engulfed in flames, shield slammers, enemy healers, and lots of explosive enemies.

I was terribly, terribly destroyed and I ended up quitting on my 5th try on completing the wave, where I must retrieve the 3,000 souls. I have yet to complete the game.

I do not recommend this game to casuals, but those who are willing to experience a game that will challenge their gamer minds.

Quick Review of the game I've played so far.

Pros:
It was challenging at first.
It is controller compatible
Nice scenery for a free game.

Cons:
It was repetetive.
It is not optimized for keyboards.
Grinding souls is tedious.
You can clip through walls like a knife through warm butter.

Analysis:
The game starts you off in the tomb. That's it. There's dialogue, but nobody reads anymore. (amirite)
Apparently you're supposed to help this Mummy guy, Ebo, lift the Curse of Mermos (GASP the game title!)
You fight ♥♥♥♥. Cool. But that's about it. Sure there's a shop and all, but there's only those 5 items. Two of the items aren't even items.
The glyphs are alright IMO. They add a little bit of risk to the game while still adding worthwhile benefits such as life regen or more health at the cost of agility.
Cool ♥♥♥♥. So, lets get down to the gameplay. If you're using a mouse and keyboard... So sorry. Controllers are fine! They work great with this game and almost every review besides mine has the controller influence it.

I'm gonna get down to the other stuff. Clipping.
I'm not talking about "Oh I can go through one of the pillars in the main room" clipping. I'm talking game breaking clips.
You can go through the wall behind Ebo at anytime. It's literally not there. You can go through any gate you want (closed or not) with these clips, but the cost is you can't continue your game any further. It will literally keep you in the room you are supposed to be in forever (whether or not all the enemies are killed).
The game is solid until you screw up with one roll and end up through a gate you aren't supposed to be behind.

Next thing. Saving.
There is none. I quit (after i broke my own game testing the clipping and such) and restarted, expecting to resume from where I had left off.
No. That is not a thing. You WILL have to restart if you quit.

Conclusion:
The game, if you don't break it, is solid. Great for controllers, but not for keyboard users such as myself (luckily I had a spare ps3 controller just lying somewhere). The story is straight forward with little interruptions, but the gameplay is repetetive. I give this game a 4.5/10
