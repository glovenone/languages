About This Game

Welcome to Thugtown circa 1920, the height of Prohibition.
Prepare yourself for a non-stop, action-packed, completely over-the-top, fast-paced, platform game. Set against the gangster heyday of the roaring twenties. Lose yourself in an exciting, elaborate tale of the underground and underworld. Experience friendship, betrayal, vengeance, and witness first hand the rise and fall of a criminal mastermind.
This is survival horror, wiseguy style... Capiche?

"Leave the gun, take the cannoli... actually, on second thought, take the gun and eat the cannoli, you’re gonna need it."

Story
The game tells the story of Vinnie Cannoli and his mob family, set in the fictional Thugtown circa 1925, the height of prohibition. Beneath the city overrun by zombies, there's a dark story of conspiracy and betrayal. Unravel the sneaky mafia ways and government conspiracy while you try to look for your lost pal and for answers about the zombie-outbreak.

Key Features
  • Action-Packed Gameplay
    Prepare yourself for a non-stop, action-packed, completely over-the-top, platform game where you have to fight, shoot, blast and kick your way through a deeply engrossing story. Not only will you have to take on the undead, but also rivalling gangsters and the army trying to cover things up.

  • Beautifully hand-drawn 2d graphics and animations
    Step into the atmospherical, shady world built by HD hand-drawn 2D graphics. Experience fluid animations and cinematic style cut-scenes.

  • Immersive World and Period
    Despite Prohibition and the imminent Depression, people are ready to drink themselves to (un)dead. Every inch of the game is like a picture straight from the Prohibition era. The cars, the furniture, the Speakeasy clubs, the music and the weaponry reflect the turbulent days of the roaring twenties.

  • Local co-op mode
    Partner up to 4 friends and unravel the mystery together.

  • About Crazy Monkey Studios & Claeysbrothers
    We're a passionate team on a mission to create amazing games!

ParanoidAndroid

Posted: 27 June
Crazy Monkey Studios knocks it out of the park with its freshman effort, Guns, Gore & Cannoli. GG&C is a sidescrolling action game in the vein of the Shank games, but much better executed in my eyes. GG&C wonderfully mixes accessibility with fun and excited gameplay that looks great to boot.


Like Shank, the first thing most people will notice with GG&C is the fantastic art direction. I really liked the ripped-from-a-comic-book aesthetic that the game presents and it fits perfectly with its 20’s era gangster vibe. The animations pair quite nicely with the art and are satisfyingly fluid and responsive. The visual layout that Crazy Monkey went with here was very smart, as the action is never muddled and it’s always clear what is background and what is foreground, which not every game does well. These characteristics carry over to the enemies as well as there is good enemy variety and each one has a distinct silhouette and is instantly recognizable. The enemy animation is quite nice as well, as I found shooting the chef and watching him pop like a bloated blood balloon particularly satisfying.


The gameplay itself is quite tight with just the right amount of float (which I’m usually not a fan of, but it works well here). You are progressively given more guns to use and each feels like an incremental upgrade over the prior one without making the previous guns obsolete. One of my biggest complaints with Shank was that when you were hit or knocked down, your character was locked into an animation of them getting up which, if you were swarmed, would lead to an infinite loop causing you to be unable to escape and eventually dying. Klei usually makes very good design choices but that was certainly not one of them. Thankfully, Crazy Monkey does not make this cardinal sin. When you are hit you slow down a bit, but it is easy enough to recover and gives you a chance to escape.


The story is also better than it has any right to be, honestly. While not the deepest of plots, I was engaged enough to care about character’s motivations and it’s clear the team over at Crazy Monkey loves themselves some gangster movies. It’s a simple narrative, but charming all the same.


While I played through the four hour campaign solo, (and loved it) GG&C can be played with up to four players coop, and I imagine it’s even more of a blast there. For me, the game was just the right difficulty when played solo but I’m not sure how enemy scaling works when played with more people. In addition, there is also a versus mode where players can face off against each other. Again, I did not try this mode.


I had my eye on Guns, Gore & Cannoli for quite some time and I’m glad that when I came around to picking it up, it surpassed my expectations. This is definitely in the upper echelon of sidescrollers that I have played and would love to see Crazy Monkey Studios do another. Whether playing it solo or couch coop there is plenty of fun to be had here and it’s definitely a game that deserves your time and attention.

Guns, Gore & Cannoli

"Join the Mafia, meet interesting people, then whack em."

Guns, Gore Cannoli is a mobster style 2D shooter and platformer. The graphics are polished and the gameplay is enjoyable.

Weapons

Through this game you will find 8 weapons, each showing something different. You will start off with a basic pistol that has infinite reload. But soon you will find a shotgun, a flamethrower, a tommy, an electricity gun, and 3 other weapons


Gameplay

The gameplay was highly enjoyable and supports controllers. You start off as Vinnie Cannoli, an enforcer of the mob who has been hired by Mr. Bellucio to find Frankie the Fly. You head to the destination of your job "Thug Town". Soon you will be fighting fellow mobsters, zombies, mutant rats and of course the army. This game is set in the 1920's. Throughout the whole game it really gave that classic mobster feel. The game has everything you would find in a normal mob movie. The combat is nice and fluent and you will find a number of enemies and a few bosses.

Consider buying this/ not buying this if

Consider buying this if:
- you love platformers
- you like a nice little story
- you like mobster movies
- you love shooting zombies/mutants/army guy/mobsters
- you love cannoli
- you just want a nicely overall designed game

Consider not buying this if:
- you hate 2D platformers
- you hate zombies
- you hate the mob
- you hate cannoli
- you hate fun

Final Thoughts

This game was highly enjoyable through the time I have played it. It had everything I expected with a mobster and the mafia. It had a satisfying ending. For some reason I really liked the name Vinnie Cannoli and now I'll want to try Cannoli.
I would recommend this game to my friends because of its art, gameplay, story, and mobsters. I also hear skins are coming, so YAY. I really don't have anything negative to say on this game's behalf because I enjoyed the game so much.
