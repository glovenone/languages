About This Game

Hyper Bounce Blast is a stomach-churning retro shooter blended with classic platforming action. Bounce, shoot and dodge to survive the ever-increasing enemy attack. Bring it on!

Use the unique bounce multiplier system to maximise power-ups, build your hyper and survive the onslaught! Featuring a fantastic soundtrack produced by electronic musician Quantum Firefly.

Features

  • Bouncing keeps you invulnerable to bullets, so stay away from that floor!

  • Totally unique gameplay mixing twin-stick shooting with bouncy platforming action.

  • Dodge a ton of bullets, lasers, Tesla coils and buzzsaws to survive.

  • Online Leaderboards

  • 6 Modes of play, including Arcade, Time Rush and Tournament mode.

  • A very classic bonus round.

  • Full Steam Controller integration, plus Xbox 360 controller, keyboard and mouse support.

Review copy provided from developer. Note that my ability to enjoy a game is not influenced by how I recieved it.

Hyper Bounce Blast
is a hectic arcade shoot 'em up, with a bit of platforming blended in to add an extra bit of flavour.

Gameplay-wise, you take on the role of an energetic shooting machine and plough your way through chaotic waves with the ability to not only blast an infinite amount of bullets in your enemies' path, but to bounce your way whilst doing so. But does this platforming mechanic affect the gameplay enough for it to be an effective use of innovation, and warrant a whole word in the title? Definitely. You'll see why as I go on to explain. Throughout Hyper Bounce Blast you'll be constantly trying to stay off the floor. Why? Whenever you are touching this floor, you are vulnerable to bullets. However, when you bounce your way through the level (via jumping on to anything that's blue) you are not only invulnerable to these bullets, but also absorbing them, turning your bullet hell into a bullet heaven. There are also other features of the bounciness, such as multipliers and the screen-clearing hyper that annihilates everything with some serious visual displays.

You have a couple of gamemodes to choose from which sounds like it would be a huge portion of the replayability, but unfortunately each mode doesn't seem that worthy of its own mode and rather changes some minor settings such as number of lives, etc. Then there's the Tournament Mode. The speed of the game is changed to an incredibly fast pace (this can be done yourself in the settings for the all gamemodes, so I don't see why it has been granted its own mode) which ultimately conflicts with your platforming abilities. There is no skillful way to speedily bounce through a level, you will almost always fail or be granted success by pure chance. This being said, the gameplay as a whole is very entertaining and will provide both an enjoyable and satisfying experience through both classic shoot 'em up action and innovation.

On an aesthetic side of things, Hyper Bounce Blast does provide a vibrant experience, but one that is merely passable. There is nothing special about these graphics and whilst having and overwhelming amount of colourful visuals, they unfortunately show to be slightly underwhelming. This being said, they are by no means 'bad'.

Hyper Bounce Blast is filled with electronic tunes from the musical mastermind, Quantum Firefly. I will say that whilst not being outstandingly complex they will soothe a simple mind, thus I enjoyed them.

USD $5 is a fair enough asking price. Not the cheapest, but I assume on sale it will be very much worth it.

Want to know if you can configure and rebind everything to your hipster needs? Sure. There is an extensive list of settings that you can knock yourself out with.

Target Audience: SHMUP fans wanting an alternative experience.

Summary:
If you're looking for the same old same old from the SHMUP experience, you can look away from Hyper Bounce Blast. Just like Horizon Shift, it makes a small twist in the genre by adding a platforming mechanic, and that mechanic is well implemented in terms of gameplay. There's something satisfying to bouncing from enemy to enemy while shooting everything in site. And this core gameplay feels fresh in the genre, even if it may not have a lot of staying power over the long haul. Look, this is a 5 dollar game, and there are things that could have raised the game to the next level in terms of replayability, such as some random enemy formats or different options. But the core experience keeps thing simple and yet fun, allowing you to focus on small simple bite-sized elements in the game that combine to a good couple hour experience. It's a developer that doesn't want to make the same old game: he's experimental: and not in the âartsyâ type of way. It's the gameplay mechanics he plays with: and I will openly admit, I'm always fascinated by those who try something different in a genre that usually keeps things safe. And good on Flump studios for producing games that change things up: and deliver an enjoyable experience.

Video Review: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jUGUbQabLmI

Lists:
Positives:
  • The addition of the platforming mechanic adds to the chaos, and it does work. Gives good incentive to use with not only the point multiplier, but the ability to absorb bullets. Complements gameplay, without being distracting.
  • Boss battles do a good job of testing skill, without being overly complicated. Introduces small new mechanics that don't go too far off what has been played up to that point.
  • Simple aesthetic compliments the gameplay here. Simple to see action, and even through the chaos, I felt like I could easily see everything at all times.
  • Bank system is cool. Gives good incentive while not being forced to use it. A good point chasing mechanic.
  • Color mechanic. Every enemy is easily identifiable in terms of weaknesses because of this color system. Good information that's easily processed by the player.
  • Controls works for the game on both the controller and keyboard/mouse (I prefer the controller here).
  • Music selection once again complements the action here (yes, I have used the word complement a lot in this review, but when a word works, it works.)

Negatives:
  • Some modes don't work, like the Tournament mode. The new platforming mechanic + the speed element makes movement too fast, not letting you capitalize on the gameplay in question.
  • A mode that had some randomization of enemy placement would have really taken the replayability of the game to the next level.
  • A lot of my complaints really comes down to mechanics regarding the length/variety of the game over time. Again, at the 5 dollar price tag, I shouldn't expect a lot, but I do wonder if the next game in the series can really push the envelope with enemy variety and mechanics, and make a indie hit. I end up wanting more.
  • Would have liked to see an option to change the color on the aiming reticle. The gray on the black background, while not distracting, doesn't really help.

Introduction
Hyper Bounce Blast was developed by Flump Studios and published by KISS ltd. It is described as a Shoot Em' Up combined with the classic platformer style which is kind of unique for this style of gameplay. It also seems to be a sort of bullet hell game but without the free flying and sort of wall jumping along.

Gameplay
This game seems to be a rather different kind of Shoot Em' Up/Bullet hell game with some platforming elements combined into the game, which make it much harder than your average Bullet Hell / Shoot Em' Up. The main mechanic is being able to wall jump from the left and right wall, which means there is also gravity involved in the game. This really separates it from other similar games of this genre, and makes a different sort of gameplay style from other games like this. I rather liked the platforming elements that are implemented into the game, it gives it that more difficult sort of feel when you are trying to avoid the bullets but are trapped in the corner.

Although it seems to be that bit more different from the other sort of games of this genre, it does have a big flaw that I personally don't like. Some of the bosses in the game have a rapid amount of bullets that are being shot at you, and compared to the size of your character makes it so difficult to avoid the bullets. Especially if you wall jump and try to swerve your way through the bullets you are almost certain to get hit. I really don't like this, even though this game is really fun, but just a bit too difficult that takes some of that fun out of the game.

With the game being practically the same over and over again, you would expect there to be several different types of game modes. Some of which are locked and can be unlocked by progressing far enough into other game modes. You have the Arcade Classic, start with 2 lives and try to fight through all the waves, you get a checkpoint before and after each boss battle. Arcade Arrange is the same as Arcade Classic except you have no extra lives but you get a checkpoint after each level. Time rush, see how far you can get with in a fully powered up ship when the game speeds up each wave. Lastly there is Tournament Mode, see how far you can get in a fully powered up ship with each wave increased speed.

There is a nice range of different game modes to keep you entertained for longer than you normally would. There are also 2 locked gamemodes which I don't know what they are as I haven't progressed enough to unlock them. I for one don't personally like the locked gamemodes for the game, forces you to get far into the game to unlock other modes which you may or may not like. There is online leaderboards, if you are the more competitive sort of players and want to try to beat other people at their score, I for one am not very competitive so it doesn't compel me to play more and try to beat other peoples score.

Pros
+ Has some platforming elements, makes it unique from other games of the similar genre
+ Lots of Game Modes
+ Online Leaderboards
+ Full Controller Support
+ Steam Trading Cards & Achievements

Cons
- Lots of bullet, makes the game too hard and takes some fun from the game
- 2 Locked gamemodes, unsure why they are locked

Result / Final Thoughts
Hyper Bounce Blast is a unique Shoot Em' Up / Bullet Hell game that has some platforming elements to it, the game itself is fairly difficult a bit too difficult that it takes the fun out of it at times. There is plenty of game modes to play through, although two of them are locked. There is a competitive leaderboard, full controller support, steam trading cards and Achievements. Would recommend to those of you who love Bullet Hell games.

Rating: 6.8/10
