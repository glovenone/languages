About This Game

Audition is an online music and dancing game that allows players to compete against and cooperate with players from around all over the world. Dance your way to virtual stardom by syncing your avatar's moves with the beat of today's hottest pop music. Players get to engage in a variety of single and multiplayer modes, while dressed in your choice of threads. No two avatars are alike, so customize your avatar to your heartâs content.

Key Features

  • Fresh Content: Audition is updated regularly with new content. Enjoy diverse music selections, pick your preferred new clothes and try new dance moves.
  • Background Music (BGM) selector: Want to listen to your own playlist when youâre not on the dance floor? No problem! Our BGM selectorâs just perfect for you.
  • My Album: Create your own set list of favorite songs for your DJ pleasure whenever you man a game room.
  • Over 20 different game modes to choose from.
  • Couple with another player and unlock new modes and swag with the marriage feature.
  • Thousands of outfits, hairstyles, shoes and fashion accessories to choose from. Dress your character in new threads with every update.
  • Stay connected with old friends, make new friends and meet fellow hipsters from all over the world with the built-in instant messenger, friend list, chat, and more.

Being a Audi player ever since Nexon had this and it was popular back then. In fact, my older sister loved the game back then. I didn't noticed that Redbana even got the game until like a few years later and this was actually when I moved from American Audition Online to AuditionSEA which in my opinion was better and how I got into the well known Korean Pop that now spans throughout YouTube.

But let's talk about Audition Online. The one that's now owned by Redbana and I'm sorry to say that this one didn't hit my interest at all...

Why? For starters, I didn't even like the song choices they have for this Audition Online.... In fact, Nexon had a way better song choices you can play in their version of Audition Online. Some of the more famous songs you heard in the old updates for Redbana's Audition is gone! Literally gone. In place of these songs that I never even heard about it or even want to hear.
Oh and Redbana just [b[HAD[/b] to include songs from X-BEAT (Audition Online for Japan) and KPOP to bring in more people to play the game. But only a few.
everyone ready, and not locked behind a password. What do you do?
For standard players, there's the usual 4 Key and 8 Key mode that all Audition Online players knows. 4 key with the arrow keys, 8 keys with the number pad.
And from my experience, it's almost impossible to get a single perfect in the game. I'm not joking either. Even songs that are practically impossible to even hold a series of Greats and Cools because 190 bpm.

But if you aren't really satisfied with the normal mode, there's always a buttload of modes to go through. Including the famous battle parties where you literally take on an AI and it scraps a lot of points. Requiring players to work together to beat the AI. Your reward? A load of in-game currency.
There's even Club Dance 2 and Couple Dance for those who like to get their romance on and yes, you can have a couple and get married in this game. A couple garden as well.

Then there's the story mode, which is by far... Not my favorite in my opinion.... The story mode does require you to complete these objectives in order to progress. You know, as of this year and probably next year, we would have cinematic cutscenes and all that. This game stuck to it's roots and provides a text load. Probably not really making you feel so important in the story. However, by completing these missions, you can earn more in-game money or EXP and if you complete a series of mission in a episode. You get a badge for it. I guess you can say it's your alternative way to farm EXP while you solo.

And I have to talk about this... EXP.
You only earn EXP if you either play with other players or complete missions in story mode. This means that if you're hosting a private room for your own self and thinking that you can earn exp that way, you probably have the wrong thoughts. When you're competing against other players, the ranking you're on determines how much EXP you get. Someone in 1st place can get more EXP than someone in last place. Do I think it's fair? Eh... I guess?

Now let's dig deep into leveling.
In this game, when you touch level 5, in order to earn the next level, you have to complete a song with a specific amount of perfects and score to clear the license mode and earn the next level. As you progress through the levels, the bpm will increase. Along with the requirements. And yes, perfect chains is a requirement as well. So if you can't chain perfects well, you're pretty much stuck on whatever lvl you're on until you can master chaining.

Of course, it's not a game without hacks. Yep, there's already perfect hacks being sent out as it goes. It's no surprise that it's even on Nexon's Audition Online. Many hackers would use it for Story Mode or Battle Parties or just use it in normal mode and crush everyone in the room. Badly.

The shop is no doubt a place where you can really deck out your character with some cute outfits. That's true. However, you do have to consider that most clothes, accessories, and all that requires you to pay money. And they only last for like 7 days. Unless you wanna cash in some more money to get the clothes or whatever permanently.

Now... Let's talk about the downfall of this game.
As I'm aware, I heard it came on Steam and released into Steam. Only to be heard that it's forcing players to do a good review on it in order to get a 40 dollar in game cash. Not only that, GMs are corrupted and there's too much drama in the game. Updates are rare in this game. And the game life is just slowly dying. Some of the songs you all hear and love on the radio isn't in the game anymore. Some songs don't even peek an interest. It's clearly proof that this version of Audition Online is slowly dying and Redbana is just milking the game even more to prevent it's death. Even the tourney isn't even going well as there's bound to be hackers wanting to take the top.

So in my opinion, this game is pretty much dead. With all the shenanigans the game goes through, from bribed reviews to corrupted GMs who bans you for no reason. It's literally no doubt that the game is fun yeah. But it's gonna need a lot more work to really get this game to where it was. With the community slowing dying, I don't think this game is gonna last much longer than what it was intended to be. Graphics are no doubt outdated. I can't recommend this game because of what Redbana is doing to make more money.

I would suggest another free music game like osu! or LoveBeat.

Audition has come a long way and it has changed a lot over time. There are several modes, different maps, new outfits, a new marriage system, etc. However, since changing from Nexon to RedBana, the community has become pretty dead. Most of the time, game rooms are locked because of people playing with others that they personally know or are close to in game. There are barely any males, making the marriage system difficult, as well as ballroom dance and couple dance modes a challenge to even start. High levels tend to dominate over this game leaving new comers feeling at a huge disadvantage. (It's not fun when you continue to keep losing right?). The songs don't update often so you're stuck with having to pick from the same song list. It would be nice to have more up to date, popular songs. Despite all this, I find that I like to play Audition when I'm extremely bored because there is still pleasure in being able to punch in all the arrow keys before your time limit. Audition seems more fun when you're married because you can spend your time trying to upgrade your ring and work on your garden. Otherwise, the game itself and community has a lot of work that needs to be done.

Bribed reviews

I started playing this game in 2009 and have hundreds of (non-Steam) hours in this game.

You could not ask for a better example of the hypocritical, low tactics that the management of this game uses. They are incenting players to leave positive reviews to the value of $40 USD of in-game currency. Now that they've closed the event and stated that any participants who review the game before or on June 8 will receive the $40 in-game cash. Count how many of the positive reviewers only have that 1 review and put their IGN in the review (required to receive the in-game cash.)

Do you really want to trust a game that is essentially stating it cannot naturally get positive reviews? They also heavily censor their forums from bad publicity so don't be surprised if this thread disappears. There is a very "you're either with us, or banned" kind of mentality here. Very little transparency. In fact, the opposite. They want you to only know what they tell you.

The game itself, while outdated, sometimes buggy, and pretty lackluster by today's standards, is fun - and with the relative lack of good rhythm games out there, that is a point in Audition's favor. If you like social rhythm games and dress-up, you might enjoy yourself for a while. This server does not really have the best hits because by their own word they don't have enough money. However it does have songs with uncensored explicit language and racial epithets, suggestive clothing /themes and a good chunk of their playerbase under their required age of 13.

However, I cannot recommend this game because of how horribly this game is managed. Redbana is doing anything they can to keep the cash flowing in this dying game and it's showing. They will run events where the cost (in real money) to enter is the same as the value of the prize (IF you win!) They will pretend to care about you right up until the moment you are no longer giving them money or helping them. I wrote the X-Patcher software that provides a better launcher interface than the existing one. I gave technical support to hundreds of users to get more people to play this game. I got fed up with their apathy towards players and their inability to actually do anything that mattered, and quit.

The GMs have previously shown preferential treatment. They are inconsistent in punishment and will do anything they want with very little transparency. They are incompetent children that have banned hard-working players because they levelled up 'too fast'. Even the game's "player council" has very little influence, and does not get much more information, having been on it myself. Or the sad reality they do leak out to the council members is hush-hush, don't tell our players because they might leave. There is a lot they keep secret, and for good reason: They want you to play, and give them money, and hope you don't have any issues or concerns or suggestions. Everything is fine as long as you don't create waves. Because they can't / won't do anything to help you. There are bugs in the game that have never been fixed.

I am disappointed, again and again and again.

- tgfcoder.
