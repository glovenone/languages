What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We have a large and enthusiastic community that love to give us suggestions, tell us how they feel about the game, and give us feedback. By releasing on Early Access we are allowing our community to come along with us on a development journey of weirdness and discovery.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Six months to a year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version will have more player customization (something the community has requested a lot). Creatures will have real memories and naturally evolved behaviors; essentially a more advanced empathic intelligence AI. The game will go from one geographic location to an infinitely generated world, where players can explore to find new resources, creatures and landscapes. Many, many more creatures and structure types will be added. Down the line we will also add a persistent online environment.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Early Access includes creative and adventure mode. Our empathic intelligence AI 1.0 is completed with creatures able to learn their environment, develop relationships with fellow creatures, with the player as well as with objects in the game. Creatures are able to express a range of dynamic emotions. The geographical terrain is limited to one full biome; a tropical island. There is structure building, creature riding and commanding as well. Currently we have 142 life-blocks and counting!

Multiplayer mode will be included, although a little big rough around the edges and limited to 4-8 people.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, the game will be priced differently after Early Access because we intend to add enough features and polish to warrant premium indie pricing.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We have involved the community from day one, asking for feedback, opinions, fan-made creations and art, and criticism. We are making the game for you guys and we love to hear from you. We have a forum currently and the community hub on steam will also serve as a great way to connect to our community during Early Access. We look forward to your help with squashing bugs and helping us make CHKN the best it can be.”

"Mutated chickens everywhere (Mttd CHKNs vrywhr)"
CHKN is a nice little Voxel Survival (Co-op?) Sandbox game with crafting menu and a builder mode. It is Early Access on Steam and also on a very early state of development (V0.1.1). That means the game engine works quite fine, but some basic mechanics, the unit balancing and data safety still have to be improved.

The game simply makes fun and it is amazing how creative the ideas are implemented by the devs. You start on an island in the ocean. A small beach with chicken and eggs. So far so good. But then you breakup one of the eggs and it drops you all pieces of a new chicken scattered on the floor. You'll see a bumping heart in the air and a laserdome. That's the lifeform creator! Now you can puzzle together your own animals out of blocky pieces. And after exploring the island I can say there are many different kinds of animals. They're all cutely chickonized, like Chickenphants, chickengiraffs, chickenbulls, chickenpigs, chickensheeps aso. Lateron they even get mutated by themselves with all different kind of block pieces.

The game also offers features like farming, daylight-night circle, taming and riding animals and at last new alien invaders. Not all keybindings are explained entirely. So you must testout many things. Gathering metal, stone and wood is a pain in the CHKN since every tool breaks within 10 times of use. Another freaking thing is the poor pointing abilities of the crosshair. Even if you point at a chest it snaps back to the last thing you pointed at (tutorial mode). I wish it was more precisely.

Btw if you zoomout with key C your character looks like an assassins creed chicken. In conclusion I'm amazed by the potential that is in this game but they must do work on the stability and polishment. Otherwise the ppl get fastly frustrated if hard gathered resources and buildup bases suddenly disappear. Just because of saving issues.

CHKN is sort of like a blend of Ark and Spore, but the Spore part is the only aspect of the game that I thought was very fun. There's not a whole lot to craft, and the hardest part of the game is figuring out which tree or different colored rock will give you the item you're looking for. There isn't much of a goal, and for that reason I think the Creative mode is a lot more fun. The game's wilderness is full of unholy abominations made from a bunch of animal parts stuck together, and the game gives you the option to do it yourself, too. It's kind of fun just building and interacting with your band of freakish mutants.

Is it worth $15? I don't really think so. It's Early Access, so I'm trusting the developer to add more things to do on the adventuring side, but at the moment it's essentially the Spore Creature Creator except attached to a game that's still more fun than Spore. But I won't deny that the animal building is fun, and it might be worth getting on a sale. If you're looking into this game for the animal building side then you'll probably enjoy it, but if you're looking for a more Minecrafty or Ark-like game then you should probably wait.

So yeah. If you like open-world sandbox survival, and also admire Dr. Frankenstein, you'll probably like CHKN, too.
