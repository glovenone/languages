About This Game

Remedy, a recent graduate from the Saturnian healing school, must descend to Hurtland and use her powers to heal everyone from stray ducks to dark lords, and finally cure the ailing Prince Hingst. It's a short, whimsical adventure with plenty of heart(s).

This game was one of Ludosity's entries in the Games Against Ebola charity game jam in 2014. This version was made after the jam to include options, multiple savefiles, more difficulty levels, and gamepad support.

When using a gamepad, no setup is necessary - every button is used as the "action button".

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP
    • Memory: 50 MB RAM
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 10 MB available space

Oh, you mean you want me to write more than this?! Well the graphics look a lot like late '80s EGA-stile, but better done than what was available at the time, and with good reason, since this is the kind of game that would have tanked a computer of that era.

- Yes it's a 1 button game, that looks like it was made for an '80s joystick in mind,
- Yes it looks dated,

But the hardware of that time was a lot less than what is available today. Probably a microwave oven of this time is more powerful than the computers of the '80s.

Yet it still recaptures the kind of gameplay that was available at that time. It has a fun story, hidden bosses, hidden monsters, an '80s "we need to be good" end of a cartoon feel which is both charming and amusing.

Also it's free.

Give it a try, it could be an afternoon well spent.

This is an awesome retro game short (about 1 hour) though it may be. Feels and sounds like this game came from the NES era which is something I can appreciate. The controls are also very simple. Basically move with directional buttons and you have an action button to talk to people or use vials (bombs) in battle or bring up your stat screen or even to run while holding a direction. You can also use a controller which is what I did. Remmy (or remedy as reffered to in game) is sent down to hurtland to heal everyone including Prince Hingst. The main objective is to find him and cure him of his own ailments. You must go around the various maps and find people and other creratures to cure. To heal someone in this game you go into a "heal mode" battle where you face off against various virus creatures. Whichever direction you're looking is where you will shoot your healing powers. To clear the battle you have to destroy all of the viruses on screen. You do have vials to throw which can help with crowd control. It's a lot like a mini pixel bullet hell game with a bit of rpg elements thrown in. After my first playthrough I was addicted. I wanted to get a full completion file and played it again. This time I got 101% Now working toward doing that on the hardest difficulty.

It's a free game just try it already! Especially if you like a little shmup action. This game is a real gem and I think I've found my next speedrun game.
