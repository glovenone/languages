Early Access Game

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“It needs to attract community of gamers, to identify possible issues in the balance of the game, to fix possible bugs, to hear constructive criticism, to test the game with a lot of players.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“No exact answer. I have many plans for game development. Likely I will change the game version from pre-alpha to beta when I get a more stable version.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“In full version game will be:
- extended tech tree
- extended player development
- improved graphics
- improved the game balance
- Steam achievements
- extended gameplay in different directions
The game will continue to develop even after it has become "full".”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current state of the game allows players to participate fully in the gameplay.
Players can mine resources; can make different items of interior, the manufacturing machines for firearms. There are able to engage in farming. Building development and combat mode are completed fully.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game will remain free.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We have website of game with description and guides to the English and Russian languages, which available to edit by players. We always listen to new proposals for improvement gameplay; most of them have already been successfully implemented.
Active players that participate in game content creation may receive different game bonuses such as unique goods and premium coins.”

About This Game

  • Online Multiplayer Sandbox meets Tactical RPG!
  • One single huge world for all players.
  • Full freedom of choice, interaction between players, hostilities and trading.
  • Everyone is absolutely equal: you are what you have achieved in the world of the game.
  • In-game items can only be crafted from resources of the game world.
  • Single shared environment where everyone lives, builds, produces and battles.
  • Every action affects the world story, and you can become the part of it!


Main features of Game:
  • Fully generated world to explore.
  • Different biomes such as forest, desert, mountains and mush more.
  • Two game modes. First is real-time mode for mining, crafting, building and other peaceful activities. Second is turn-based combat mode.
  • Component system of crafting.
  • All objects in game world can be destroyed.
  • Opportunity to buy up to 5 game units and development of their various skills and parameters.
  • Game units can be exposed to the effects such as fracture, poisoning, bleeding etc.
  • Different kinds of game bots for PVE: animals, zombies, bosses.
  • Full interaction between players in one world.
  • Private area with limited access to equipment and warehouses for other players.
  • Building development.
  • Farming.
  • Trading.
  • Clans and clan areas.

Early Access Review
Posted: 10 June
I mean, mechanically, it's not terrible. It just has such a horrible means of explaining the more intricate details of the game that are essential to progressing. The lack of explanation is so bad, that the help page rips you from the game to some sort of webdoc; seems even the game doesn't want me in the game. This is without mentioning the, apparently, terrible community aspect, to which I didn't find at all. Probably because I couldn't find anyone at all. Not even my friend, to which we were so bewildered by what was going on, it took us an hour to even get close to each other; only for him to die to what was apparently a mob of zombies.

So, what was the result of this venture? I think a few dead trees, a random â¥â¥â¥â¥-off bench, and 1-1/2 hours exploring abandoned, empty towns that probably belonged to all the players that got banned. I deleted this game as soon as I could.

Perhaps it's important I elaborate, given the divisive feedback. I will fully admit, I came into this game with a stigma; I was honestly hoping to run into one of the abusive admins mentioned, and have a grand ol' time of watching them crap-talk me or Hayner or something. I wasn't expecting the gameplay itself to be enjoyable, and I'll be honest; it wasn't to me. There are, in fact, several reasons why:

1. With the map as large yet as barren as it is, it gives everything the Bethesda Syndrome; massive open world, but with basically nothing in it (The only difference I'd say is that at least Bethesda's games have mods..) Everywhere I went, it was desert, forest, and more desert, maybe a bit of a craggy area inbetween, but I found essentially nothing but ruined buildings and a couple dead mobs of enemies.

2. As was mentioned before, travelling to meet with your friends is rather annoying. Not because it's unintuitive, even though it somewhat is, as we figured out the coordinate system rather quickly. The problem was distance; map direction is rather obtuse, even with the compass, and it felt rather easy to end up getting sidetracked or lost on your way to anywhere.

3. The research system is slow and lackluster. I wasn't given any clear direction on what exactly to start with, and asking chat yielded negative results. So after researching into weaponry to defend myself, I realized I needed key resources from another research tree to even make it. Which leads me into the crafting...it's boring and unrewarding. I mentioned it took me an hour and a half to make a single bench? Yeah, I actually wasn't joking, even if I was being hyperbolic; it was at least an hour, but mostly because I was having to double that between finding Hayner.

4. The combat just isn't fun. I want to like it, I really do, but it just wasn't any fun for me. For one, there's only the vaguest mention of how many of an enemy you're engaging with, which you can easily miss if you're not paying explicit attention; it's the main reason Hayner got murdered by a horde of zombies when he didn't realize it was a horde of them. Aside from all that, the turn-based nature of the game seems innocent enough at first, and I'd wholy admit, if I were more into it, it could be a lot of fun. But as it stands, it just didn't engage me at all.

5. The community, go figure, is kind of dead. I couldn't get any questions answered at all, though I'll admit I mostly asked them to see if some jerkwad admin would get uppidy because I didn't magically know that stuff already. Still, I asked, and I asked, and nothing. I got no answers, I got no directions. The webdoc the help page sent me to cleared things up far more than the actual game or community did, so I'm more inclined to give the webdoc a positive rating than this game.

I hope this clears some things up. Can people stop shouting at me now? or is Xas now going to ban me, or something?
