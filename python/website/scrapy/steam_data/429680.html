About This Game



Spellweaver is a fast-paced and strategically deep digital card game, featuring several mechanics unique to the genre. Created by an indie team of seasoned game developers, and designed by a Magic: the Gathering National Champion, it is easy to learn, yet challenging for veteran card game players.

One of Spellweaver's core gameplay features is hero development. During a game, your hero levels up, gains resources, and can develop additional hero skills, letting you craft the perfect hero to complement your deck's strategy.

Spellweaver's combat system features an innovative creature speed mechanic. Fast creatures allow you to dictate how combat goes, but they have generally weaker stats. Slower creatures are bigger and stronger, but they are also easy to outmaneuver.

Spellweaver's design allows for quick games, yet offers a huge number of combinations and paths to victory to explore. Join in and have endless hours of fun with this strategic card game created with a lot of passion!

+decent degree of complexity
+balanced card design (legendaries are niche cards, powerful only under the right circumstances instead of autoincludes)
+nicely drawn card art
+generous f2p model

however, this is where the dream ends, and you wake up to the incessant beeping of an alarm clock, painfully aware that it's monday morning.

-tutorial is rudimentary and does not explain anything other than the most basic of interactions
-the game board is unnecesarily crowded and colorful with flips and shuffles between phases. this makes the game dizzying at times and conveys the feel of a cheap asian knock-off. this is a somewhat subjective point, so feel free to ignore it
-questing system is nothing to write home about, every 8 hours or so you get a selection of quests (number depends on starter decks owned). you can only chose one quest per faction, the rest are lost. again, it conveys the feel of a cheap browser game and not a ccg.
-leveling up feels more like a grind than a natural progression, with quests oftenly requiring from 5 to 20 wins in a given play mode. the rewards are worthwile, but the system itself is unoriginal and feels like a chore.
-board/card interactions and controls are weird and unnatural.
examples: cards played appear on the right side and are being tracked all the way across the board on the left side, card chains are unintuitive and happen in a fast slide show manner that will leave you scratching your head about what happened, triggered effects are crammed in the left side of the screen often cliping into the side, attack/pull back command must be given by drag and drop only, clicking will only shove a hi-res version of the card in your face, right click does nothing, multiple activation abilities must be individually clicked the number of times you want the ability to trigger, complete with you waiting for the animation of said ability, rushing it before it's done will only zoom in on the card. sure, these are all quality of life issues, but being in such a high number, they have a noticeable negative impact on the whole game.

and finally, what i consider the real deal breakers
-game crashes mid fight if you dare play more than a pathetic number of creatures it can safely keep track of.
-pvp matches are a lagfest, to the point where you can't understand the order of play or card chains without the combat tracker.


all in all, a game with solid and creative concepts, tied down by questionable design choices and overall clunkiness. it feels like trying to sew buttons while wearing gauntlets. gauntles that are nicely adorned and made of the finest steel, but that's of not much help now, is it?
