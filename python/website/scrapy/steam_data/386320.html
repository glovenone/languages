About This Game

Naninights is a point-and-click horror adventure.

You are working in a test lab, whose aim is to investigate and learn to control gifted children.

Someone accidentally left a gate open and a test subject escaped. Track him using an A.L.I.F.A. mask and limit his ability to hunt you down.

Use gates, cubes or mirrors corresponding to the attached rooms. Remember, there are few steps for Naninight to make in each room before he gets you.

You are going to face 3 rounds consisting of 3 phases each, and 3 rounds on Hardcore to get to the truth.

Alone in a lab you must survive the night as a young test subject roams the place hunting for you: sounds interesting? Well too bad Naninights is a terrible FNAF clone with no redeeming qualities whatsoever.

PROS:

-none

CONS:

-horrible graphics, it looks very low res and blurry

-you're stuck in windowed mode with a terrible Windows-style menu tab on top

-barebones soundtrack

-laughable sound effect

-the enemy is not menacing at all, in fact it looks and sounds silly and its jumpscare is cringeworthy

-broken gameplay punishes you if you play as you're supposed to, the third level also seems to be totally random in how and when you die

-little to no story

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=551030044

Naninights is a mess, there's no way around it. The graphics and sound are horribly bad and being forced to play in a small borderless window is something I honestly have no words for, just look at the image that goes with this review.

The gameplay is also terrible, you're supposed to be looking for the kid through cameras and use the according objects to make it go away, sadly it doesn't work. This is because the enemy will sometimes switch rooms in a matter of seconds leaving you with no time to react: this is made worse by the fact that certain things, like the mirror, are bugged and show the enemy even when it has already left.

I finally managed to beat the first two levels by simply doing nothing, waiting for the indicator that tells you Naninights is near to show up and quickly activating two defences while spamming the mirror up and down (cause keeping it up doesn't work, another bug). This dull exercise worked until I reached the third level where the mirror stopped working: it basically either didn't stop the kid or just showed its face making me lose tons of mind power: is this purposefully broken to make the game "hard" or did they just not bother to test it? You decide.

Naninights is an embarassing "thing" and I can't help but think it's been cobbled together in a few minutes by people with no shame just to ride the FNAF hype train: it's not worth your time, not even for a laugh, just watch a video on Youtube.
