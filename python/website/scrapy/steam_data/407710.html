Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We are big believers in the power of Virtual Reality to transport users to emotionally engaging, immersive experiences. That's why we need your help to push the boundaries and design a unique title that provides an unparalleled sense of presence, while remaining comfortable for the user.
Help us create an experience that blurs the line between the real world and the virtual.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We are targeting a full release, Q3 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The following features are planned for the final release:
  • Realistic paddling system with free roaming
  • Two additional unique zones to explore : underground caverns and whitewater rapids
  • Fully dynamic weather system
  • More interactivity with the world
  • More Artificial Intelligence driven creatures populating the world
  • 2-player multiplayer support
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently the experience is in beta phase.
It features:
  • A vivid, living world for you to visit and explore!
  • Fully dynamic cloud generation
  • Highly realistic Artificial Intelligence driven animals, can you discover them all?
  • 3D positional sound design
  • Control the speed of your kayak with the keyboard or game pad controller
  • Use the motion controllers to paddle , the faster you paddle the faster you'll go!
”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes. The Grand Canyon Experience will be priced at a significant discount during Early Access. You’ll be enjoying it at a fraction of the final price.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The goal of this Early Access phase is collect feedback, suggestions and ideas from VR Enthusiasts like you so we can make this one of the most spectacular experiences possible, when the full release debuts later this year.”

8 July

Patch 1.62 is now live!


Hi everyone! We just released a new patch with plenty of bug fixes and some new features!

Note about the water shader

We finally were able to identify the bug that is causing the water to flicker under certain angles. It turns out it is an issue deep inside the VR rendering pipeline of Unreal Engine. Epic is working on a fix for us so as soon as they release it we'll issue a hot fix. In the meantime you can cycle between transparent / opaque water with “pagedown”.

Some of the changes include:
  • NEW: Our first of many physics props is enabled => you can now throw, drag, push, move & hurl some small rocks around in the world.
  • NEW: HRTF fly, you can't see it, but boy you sure can hear it!
  • NEW: The pier : ideal for hopping on and off the paddle board!
  • NEW:Self-driving kayak tours is now also available for Vive users (sit down on the floor in front of a fan for maximum immersion!).
  • NEW: Oculus users can now also teleport to various destinations in the world.
  • various environment tweaks.
  • you should now find a "chill area" at most hotspots that line up with a typical desk chair height -> move your real life chair into position so it lines up with a virtual rock, chair, pier. Take a seat and let the chilling commence...
  • lots of new sounds!
  • We tweaked the paddling physics based on your feedback.
  • F key displays frame-rate .
  • various small bug fixes.
As always please use our bug report forum to help us identify & fix issue as well as leave us suggestions for future improvements and additions!

Happy exploring!

The Immersive Entertainment team.

16 June

Patch 1.61 is now live

Hi everyone!

We just released patch 1.61, adding gamepad/keyboard support & various bugfixes:

Patchnotes
  • Fixed an issue where some users couldn't load the application
  • Added gamepad & keyboard support for the self driving kayak tour (see below)
  • Some minor performance improvements for low & medium setting
  • Some minor bugfixes

Gamepad & keyboard controls :
  • Enter Kayak : P or A button
  • Enter Campsite: O or B button
  • Recenter: ENTER or Back Button
  • Flashlight: Left Ctrl or Y
  • Speed up: Triggers or W/S or Up arrow/Down arrow

We also started working on the next big content update, so stay tuned for more cool stuff in the coming months!

The Immersive Entertainment team.

About This Game

Note : This VR Experience requires an HTC VIVE /Oculus Rift & the latest STEAM VR / Oculus runtime

VISIT ONE OF THE GREATEST NATURAL WONDERS OF THE WORLD


  • A VR-FIRST Experience designed for maximum immersion
  • Explore the beauty and splendor of the Grand Canyon via kayak (Oculus) or stand-up paddle-board (Vive)
  • Vivid, living world populated with procedural generation of animal life driven by artificial intelligence
  • Enjoy a fully dynamic day & night cycle
  • Help us on our quest to create the world's first highly immersive living virtual Grand Canyon!

MECHANICS


  • Use the motion controllers to paddle the Colorado River at various speeds
  • Discover new area's and interact with new animals
  • Watch the sun go down and explore the Canyon under a starry sky
  • Use a flashlight when exploring the Canyon at night
  • Many more mechanics in the works!

EARLY ACCESS ADVANTAGE


  • Get access to this living VR world right now
  • Regular updates with new features and bug fixes
  • Influence future development and features

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 or higher
    • Processor: Intel Core i5 (or equivalent)
    • Graphics: GeForce GTX 970 GPU (or equivalent)
    • Storage: 2000 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: Requires an HTC VIVE /Oculus Rift, Xbox 360 and Xbox One controllers supported.
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows 7 or higher
    • Processor: Intel Core i5 (or equivalent)
    • Graphics: GeForce GTX 980 GPU (or equivalent)
    • Storage: 2000 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: Requires an HTC VIVE /Oculus Rift, Xbox 360 and Xbox One controllers supported.

Early Access Review
Posted: 9 October, 2015
There's one thing that comes to my mind: Detail.

The sheer amount of detail put into the environment is stunning. The textures look incredible, geometry is great, all coupled with procedural AI animals that react to the environment naturally, AND in VR? That's too much for one person to ask for a small purchase.

But the game delivers big time on it's promise: Being a nice relaxing, while still engaging, experience.

And the kayak freaking tips when you lean over the side!

But still, this is a great experience, if only it ran better on my PC. Currently running an I7 4790K and 2 GTX 770s, and that was enough to get me a judder-free experience on medium. High has issues, and Epic is out of the question. Note, this is all with the Oculus Rift on, and to get the best experience possible you need at least 75FPS.

Overall, it's a great experience and is worth a try, but could do with some performance improvements.
