About This Game

In Game Dev Tycoon you replay the history of the gaming industry by starting your own video game development company in the 80s. Create best selling games. Research new technologies and invent new game types. Become the leader of the market and gain worldwide fans.

A journey through gaming history


Start in the 80s
Start your adventure in a small garage office in the 80s. Enjoy the hand-crafted level design while you develop your first simple games. Gain experience, unlock new options and create your first game engine.

Create games your way
In Game Dev Tycoon the decisions you make during development really matter. Decide which areas you want to focus on. Does your game need more gameplay or should you focus more on quests? These decisions will have a major impact on the success of your game.

Grow your company
Once you have successfully released a few games you can move into your own office and forge a world-class development team. Hire staff, train them and unlock new options.

Make larger more complex games
With experience and a good team, you can release larger, more complex games. Larger games bring new challenges and you will have to manage your team well to deliver hit games.

Features


  • Start a game development company in the 80s
  • Design and create games
  • Gain new insights through game reports
  • Research new technologies
  • Create custom game engines
  • Move into bigger offices
  • Forge a world-class development team
  • Unlock secret labs
  • Conduct industry changing products
  • Unlock achievements
  • Modding support
  • Workshop support
  • Community translations for German, Spanish, French, Portoguese (Brazil), Russian, Czech, Swedish, Dutch and Italian are available through the in-game menu.

The full game has many more features which are not listed here to prevent spoilers.

Overall this game is pretty fun, but it really does need some help;
Things that need fixing;
-bugs do too much to ruin your games, if you make a great game with only one bug you won't even make back a fraction of your money. This adds a frustrating lack of realism for the pursuit of gameplay, that really just makes it worse. Look at Skyrim and Fallout 4, they're buggy as hell, but are also two of the best-selling games of all time.
-the reviewers are unrealisticly harsh. You can be setting records for your games in both Technology and Design, but you'll get reviews saying that the technology aspect "Wasn't good enough." They'll also say things like; "the Genre and Topic were a great combination." and then give you a rating of 4.
-It needs a difficulty setting, that way it can be enjoyed by those that like to breeze through their games easily, and those that want to have to re-start their games 50 times before success comes their way.
-It will make you game worse to have an aspect over-done. Since when is doing something well a bad thing? If a game type has Gameplay as "not-important" but take your time and refine it anyway, it will somehow make your game worse. I simply cannot fathom how doing something better than it was can make a game worse.
-More research points; research points are worth more than gold in this game, you simply cannot have enough of them. Also having making a game engine be the most lucrative source for them seems rather odd, seeing as how the main use for research points is new technologies for you game engines.
Don't take the rant above as a bad sign it is sill a fun game, but it needs a little TLC.
