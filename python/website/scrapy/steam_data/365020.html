Posted: 6 June, 2015
Gynophobia tries really hard to symbolize a deep, dark meaning, but unfortunately does a very poor job at explaining or hinting to it. There's little to no continuity in the little bit of story that peeks through, levels don't match and feel stitched together, and the game ends as abruptly as it starts. The time I have logged in is almost enough to beat the game twice (it crashed towards the end my first time).

Spoilers Inbound:
Without extrapolating more information than I need to, you play as a young man with an acute fear of spiders and women, living with his mother in a rather small apartment. The game starts with you staring out the window in your mother's room. Quickly growing tired of this, you decide to go play PC games, but on the way there you are "ambushed" by a tarantula-sized spider walking across your hallway. After it passes, you are free to enter your room where you begin the first portion of the game, a crappy Left 4 Dead knock-off consisting of two short zombie-shootin' levels. After this is complete (no more than 10 minutes later), the doorbell rings and you are terrified to learn that it is a young woman with a very low-cut top. Spooky! You make a dash to the kitchen to drink your stress medicine out of a very ornate silver bottle. This makes you tired, so you need to return to the bedroom and rest, starting the second (and final) portion of the game: a dungeon-crawling shooter that plays exactly like the first portion, except now you have spiders to worry about in addition to the all-female zombie horde. Oh, and weird floaty head things. After a quick jog through two generic dungeon/cave levels and a simple boss battle, you awaken to find your courage restored. You charge out of the room, squish that pesky spider, and throw open the door to greet the young woman who had apparently been standing outside during your entire nap, and the game ends.

In Short:
Gynophobia lacks so much that I'm not sure I could even qualify it as a game. There's no variety, no continuity, no explanations, and not much of a game to play. As I mentioned above, I'm sure the developer had every intention to make this game as symbolic and deep as he could, but failed to realize what his game was short on: everything.

Hm, what's to say? Well... it's okay at best. I got this on sale for $2 and I can't say it's worth more than that. The game is all of 30 minutes long maybe, assuming you play the zombie mini-game. Basically, Gynophobia is about a young male of indeterminate age with a paralyzing phobia of spiders and females. His mother slips him some strange potion and he goes on a mental bender within a dream, fighting his fears to overcome them. That's basically Gynophobia in a nutshell. While I feel like there is an idea of a better story to be had here, the work simply hasn't been put into it.

To start, Gynophobia has some buggy walking detection. The difference between a street and a curb can cause your character to bounce up and down while sometimes loading in from a continue screen leaves your movement (and controls frozen). Additionally, you can surf atop of enemies! Land squarely on an enemy's head and you'll be taken for a ride wherever they go. Dead foes also present walking obstacles until they fade into the ground.

Concerning the art, outside of the last boss, it's generally very low. The female zombie-like enemies resemble something from a cruder version of the first Timesplitters with poorly stretched out textures and barely recognizable humanoid shapes. The spiders are somewhat better off, but I'm not certain as to why. According to the developer, these are stock assets. Understood, but they really don't make things look all that great.

This is the kind of title that's bad enough to be amusing and I suspect it capitalizes on that concept so people like Jim Quisition (who gets referenced in this game) will give it free advertising and cash in value. Gynophobia DOES have the potential to be an interesting game, but it's so short and cheaply done that all the tongue in cheek references can't make it genuinely good. I honestly wish someone more competent could have helmed this titled because the concept is there, but the execution is barely worth the penny. The game is so-so at best.
