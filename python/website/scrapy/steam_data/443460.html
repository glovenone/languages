About This Game

Explore the world around you, the magic that surrounds it. Learn the ways of the nature, the mountains and caves, snow-filled tundras and serene lakes. Uncover the different courses life can take.

Star Sky 2 is a personal experience, and a deeply atmospheric and meditative experience. Walk through the landscapes, interact with the environment and take notice as you go along. Explore the different roads presented and enjoy the sound and sights of the world.

In Star Sky 2 MÃ¥rten Jonsson continues the story set by the previous one, with a similar style of gameplay. There are more choices and most importantly, more consequences. Learn from the choices you make, and see the different paths a life can take, and the scenarios that unfold. The game is a slow experience meant as a way to get lost in your thoughts, to feel the game as you are playing, and most importantly, to simply relax.

Goodnight, and may you find what you're seeking beneath the stars.

If you enjoyed Marten Jonsson's Star Sky, you will enjoy Star Sky 2 even more. As with all his games each one improves on the walking simulator/puzzle concept. This game is no exception.

In Star Sky 2 the player interacts with two different characters the woman (the gardener) and the man (the dreamwalker) separately. The woman walks to the left, the man to the right, each with their own encounters, adventures, and mysteries (puzzles) to solve. However, both can "interact" or change the circumstances/environments of the other. This is where the layered mystery is and were the puzzles become more complex.

An added feature to this game (taken from a previous game called Meadowland) is the hint/clue boxes which occur just before an event. The hint/clue boxes not only tell the player what to do, and how many events there are, but also what circumstances/conditions need to be present just by looking at the clue picture. Match the picture and watch the event unfold. Now just exactly how does one match the picture is the puzzle part and quite often requires the other walker's involvement. (There are four circumstances/condition variables the walkers effect but I am not saying what since that would spoil the discovery.)

The main "painting" of both the gardener (woman) and the dreamwalker (man) that occurs at the end of each "adventure" is also a huge clue to where events are located. Also if the player looks closely at the main painting, three different types of pictures occur on the far left under the window showing the type of ending the player recieved: sadness (gravestone), loneliness (torch in hand), or happiness (child in hand)

Another feature of the game is the ability to "circle" through areas more then once which can be quite helpful when trying to trigger certain events.

Same light relaxing type of soft music as Star Sky with the same no-stress gameplay. Relatively short gameplay, I finished in 2 1/2 hours, which is fine with me. Something light, soothing and positive at the end of a long day is something I do appreciate. Star Sky 2 like Jonsson's other games is a love story.

I very much recommend this game, even at full price.

P.S. Quick hint for my achievement hunting friends DO NOT skip the ending.

The soothing night-time walking simulator is back, with more places to stroll through, more events to wait around and observe, and now you can play a left-walking lady in addition to Right-walking Reggy. Visual cues and hints have been added (and can be turned off for the hardcore night-walkers who don't need hints of where to stand), it's possible to return to the same place during the same session, and there is even a map which will highlight which paths you've already taken and what's still waiting for you. Some may think it's odd that I was giddy to play Star Sky 2 after the first one, but others will appreciate a very serene walking simulator like this, which is great for relaxing or a change of scenery.
