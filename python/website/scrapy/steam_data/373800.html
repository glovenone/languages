This is an OK pinball game. I recommend it only for those who are not pinball snobs though and only on sale.

Non-proportional stretching of the item models on my widescreen appear to make things look slightly squished vertically, ball physics are definitely off, the music is very repetitive after a bit, and the ball is fairly dark for some reason. The 6 tables included seem to include minimal modes and mini-games so, no, it's not comparable to Real Arcade or Pinball FX 2, so just be aware of that up front.

That said, the controls work well for keyboard and 360 gamepad, and it even has one feature that the big boys don't: you can enter your highscore name with the keyboard. Maybe they could learn something from this after all. :)

MarkMSH
( 2.3 hrs on record )
Posted: 14 October, 2015
This game, in a word, is Horriable. About the only good thing that you can say about it is that it always runs; It Never crashes. Depending on how you look at it though that could be a bad thing, because then you'd be more tempted to play it.

There are six tables, all of them bad. There are no goals and the ball physics are sickeningly inferior to any other pinball game that I have played, and that is truely saying something. I play every pinball game for very every platform I own that I have been able to find. I have imported pinball games for various systems from Austria, Australia, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Japan, Norway, Sweden, and the U.K. My Android Tablet, for example, currently has 8 GB of pinball games. Fantastic Pinball Thrills is tied for worst place along with it's sister games Hot Pinball Thrills and Soccer Pinball Thrills amoung all that I have played. None of them are fantasitic, hot, or thrilling. Just bad.

Apart from the mind numbingly bad ball physics are the table designs. As generic as is humanly possible to achieve with no goals what so ever! Zip! Nadda! It does award you points and thus a score on each game, but whatever score you achieve is pointless, because it takes little-to-no skill to achieve. You just flail away at the flippers and hope for the best. There are no difficulty settings but you can change the angle of the tables. On some angles you can't even see the multiplier drop chutes, not that it matters because the physics are so bad.

As my friend Manatlow has pointed out, the ball won't even enter/go up some of the ramps. Very frustrating (like every other aspect of this 'game.'

Now for the sound. The music is truely irritating and the voice acting, what little there is off it, is so bad that I turn the sound way down whenever I suffer from memory loss and have forgotten how bad it truely is and start playing it again.

I post all of my high scores at Highscore.com, and as of this moment I have earned over 1,500 high score trophies. Most (but not all) of my scores are for pinball games and tables. I have many friends on Steam that are my friends there. I jokingly dared one of the other pinballers there to try this game. I immediately felt guilty doing so and warned him how bad it is. He bought it anyway, then regretted it big time and asked for and got a refund. Good for him. I wish that I had done likewise, but I bought it too long ago.

I told this same friend that I'd rather go the the dentist then ever play this game again. I was serious - and I need a root canal.


*Note: I recently suffered from memory loss and started playing this game again. By the third table I had given up on it. It was making me feel ill. I did manage to beat some high scores, but it wasn't worth it. At least the game was fresh in my mind when I wrote this review.

Posted: 27 June, 2015
I love pinball games, whether they are the real mechanical tables, or the digital types. When I saw this in the Steam store I immediatly tohught, another series of pinball tables, yeahhhh!. Sadly my euphoria was short lived because this set and the other two sets of tables (i.e. Hot Pinball and Soccer Pinball Thrills were one of the biggest let downs I've ever had with pinball games since I made my first pinball game using a freeware program.

The only positve apart from the price was the ball physics, but even that was marred at times with a sluggish feel to the ball movement. There was little to keep me interested in continuing with the game, but I was determined to try out each table from all three games so as to be as unbiased as possible, once I'd accomplished that I had to shut the game down as it was excruciatingly mind numbingly boring.

Basically all six tables are similar to each other, with little to keep even the most ardent of pinball fams interested. Right from the outset I could feel I was going to be disappointed; the graphics were lack-lustre, the inability to control the initial ball strength, with its all or all response from the ball plunger immediately put me off the game. I love to try skill shots, which are available with all good digital pinball games. There's no way you could get a skill shot with these tables; that's if they were available, which they are not.

So, no tweaking of the ball plunger to accomplish that hard to get shot that you've been trying for for ages, or attempting to get all of the gates lit at the top of the table. Basically, It's all down to luck, with no skill required. There appears to be no additional features or hidden puzzles; at least I didn't unlock, or find any over the couple of hours I spent playing all 12 tables from the three sets. Everything looks so dull, even tohugh the tables themselves are quite colorful. The ramps and rails are similar in all tables, few of the tables have enough targets to hit and most targets appear static with no lighting effects, there are few animations, and what animations are used seem to be repeated in most tables e.g. the ball wells. The graphics are so so and lack the vibrancy that we have become accustomed to with the two recent additions to the Steam library of Pinball FX and Pinball Arcade, both of which are fantastic series and exciting to play, and which have loads of hidden extras, brilliant animations and loads of features, and more importantly are worth every penny. This series of tables are sub standard so I doubt I'll be playing them again.

The only thing going for it are the number of tables you get for the price. It is cheap, working out at Â£0.67p/$0.83cents per table, buying the triple pack doesn't save you money in terms of tables gained as the two other sets i.e. Hot Pinball and Soccer Pinball Thrills only have three tables each. The price for the triple pack is basically double the price of the Fantastic Pinball Thrills, though the other two packs on their own cost more per table, even though they are the same style of table. If you are determined to buy these games, i'd seriously wait till they are on at least a 50% to 75% off deal, which I'm sure they will be once the negative reviews stack up, and sales dry up. I honestly cannot recommend this game, even to the most dedicated pinball player.


Edit:

After some though, I reckon these tables are ideal for those new to pinball and maybe even to the younger aged 5 to 9 year old player.


I will be writing separate reviews for the other two sets (Hot Pinball and Soccer Pinball Thrills), but they will not be very different from this one as they use the same basic targets, the same ramps, the same bumpers etc. The main difference is the art work and slight changes to the look of the tables.
