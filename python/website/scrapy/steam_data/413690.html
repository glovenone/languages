Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Pirates: Treasure Hunters is in a very good spot at the moment for a game in the last steps of development. It has online matchmaking, various modes and maps, characters, items, everything essential works just fine. However it isn't finished yet since it is lacking some elements to round it up, and many things are still half way of been finished or need to be polished. Like any other online multiplayer game based on competition and skill, our game needs from the feedback and experience of real players to help tune the game in the right direction. Though Pirates: Treasure Hunters already has a bunch of modes, we are planning on adding some more that we consider important to have in the final version. By having players to access early to the game we hope to get a better perspective on how to improve the game before it is released.

We have been bug testing Pirates: Treasure Hunters for some time now and we are confident the game works to a fair extent. However in a game as big as this one bugs may hide in unknown, unexpected places only able to be discovered by having a good amount of players actually playing the game.

Getting into Early Access will help us to solve all those issues and keep moving towards final release.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Our plan is to be in Early Access the time needed to receive enough feedback and see the feeling the game gives to the players. With that information we can adjust it while we add the content that is pending to be added. In this manner we can invest our effort where the community might need it to help make a better game. A specific date isn't set yet but we are confident the game won't stay too long in Early Access thanks to its current development state (close to finished).”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Our vision for Pirates: Treasure Hunters is quite ambitious. We would like to expand on the competitive side, tune the fights to make them feel fair, diverse and exciting, focus on its strengths and fight any issue that might rise from real player experience. We are excited to get more content into the game and see how the players react to the growth of Pirates: Treasure Hunters.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently the game can be played online without major issues. Your account will level up and progress, you will unlock Hunters, get items from the Store, play online with matchmaking or custom matches, practice with bots or go through the tutorials.

There might (certainly) be some bugs in the game since it isn't finished yet, and some content may vary from its current state. There will also be some content still in development and requiring some polishing. However all the core elements will remain as they are if not improved thanks to the players feedback.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game is a Free to Play experience, and will always be! There is the possibility of getting content through the in-game Store with the virtual currency. We would like to make emphasis on what kind of content is purchasable in such a manner.

In Pirates: Treasure Hunters all the core content can be unlocked with the currency obtained by just playing matches. The players can make their accounts progress by leveling up and unlocking characters, and also obtain amulets to enhance some specific characteristics. Just the Outfits (cosmetic alternatives for the Hunters’ looks) and temporary XP and currency boosts are obtained only by making online purchases of the hard coin currency. Every bit of content that has a direct effect in the battles is completely accessible to everyone and therefore the game can be fully enjoyed by just playing it.

All the content that is unlocked while the game is still in Early Access will be kept when the game is released! There are no plans of reseting the accounts once the game comes out of Early Access and gets released. Any content unlocked by you no matter how you unlocked it (by using the established methods) won't be removed. In other words, what you get now will be yours forever!”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Our hope is to build a strong and diverse community to receive all kinds of feedback so we can improve the game with your help. As we have previously stated, a game of this size needs from the users to experience and express what the game feels like and what might need to improve. Every competitive and multiplayer game gets enriched by that dialogue, and we are eager to have it with you!”

About This Game

PIRATES: TREASURE HUNTERS


Introduction

Pirates: Treasure Hunters is a Free-to-Play MOBA where the action dominates the course of battles through a dynamic and direct character control. Choose from a broad range of Hunters, each one equipped with unique weapons and skills. Take them to battle and face the opposing team in exciting online matches for up to 6vs6 players. Use cooperative vehicles to destroy your opponents and claim victory. Show that you are up to the challenge.

GAMEPLAY


Competitive Online Battles


The action in Pirates: Treasure Hunters takes place in the competitive online multiplayer matches. Two teams of up to 6 players each fight to achieve a series of objectives, like destroying the Totems and the Bastion of the enemy, or conquering Domination Points to surpass the rival. All of this with action packed fights making use of the Hunters’ weapons and skills (the Hunters are the characters the game offers).

Hunters


Choose your Hunter from a broad selection of unique characters. From buccaneers and soldiers to mages and priests, through tribal warlords, inventors, swordsmen and more mysterious characters each one with a set of personal weapons and skills. Discover which Hunter fits your style and together conquer the enemy.

Vehicles and mobility


To claim the victory you can use the cooperative vehicles with one player driving and the other shooting from the cannons or machine guns causing mayhem among the enemies. There are vehicles for land and sea, although they aren’t the only way to traverse the map. Other resources come in the form of grappling hooks or retractable bridges that open a wide variety of possibilities to create genuine situations where the skill and wit of the players can shine.

Theme


Set in a fantastic universe with strong roots in the pirate flourish period, Pirates: Treasure Hunters assemble a wide variety of characters of very different origins. The depiction of this universe is made through a style that reinterprets the stories and adventures set in the age of galleons and buccaneers, and broadens them with magic, machinery and characters with a unique background, in order to create a rich and diverse world. In this way the game, without losing its focus on action, offers the players eager for lore and stories a reason to approach the game.

Progression



In Pirates: Treasure Hunters all the core content can be unlocked with the currency obtained by just playing matches. The players can make their accounts progress by leveling up and unlocking characters, and also obtain amulets to enhance some specific characteristics. Just items like the Outfits (cosmetic alternatives for the Hunters’ looks) and temporary XP and currency boosts are obtained only by making online purchases. Everything that has a direct effect in the battles is completely accessible to everyone.

Early Access Review
Posted: 6 July
Pirates: Treasure Hunters is a very well polished MOBA style game with a WASD control scheme along with a full repertoire of skill-shot style skills, combining for an extremely fun skill based experience.

The character models are highly detailed yet well optimized, the maps, modes and characters are interesting and filled with unique features I've not seen in a MOBA before such as vehicles (land AND sea) which feel well balanced, an ammo system for ranged attacks and some grenade/mine style skills for certain characters, and a grappling hook that allows quick travel between islands around the center of the map but only to specific areas that the grappling hook is usable on.

The game has a very similar system to League of Legends wherein you can equip items (very akin to runes from LoL) to increase the stats of your characters. You however cannot purchase these items with real money (just like runes in League) and can only purchase them with in-game currency earned by playing or sometimes you obtain them after a game. These items feel far less significant than runes however as the ones I've gotten so far only give .5-1% bonuses to certain stats and you cannot stack them as much as you can runes in League.

The control scheme is very smooth for mouse and keyboard, input is instant and the default controls are perfectly usable aside from the quick level up buttons which use your F# keys. I'd like to see the default system changed to something similar to Smite's where holding control and pressing the button to use an ability will quick level it, but you can also hold Alt and click on the weapon/skill on your HUD and level it that way, which I personally prefer over the F# keys.

The game also includes a well done tutorial which explains the game modes and controls flawlessly to help new players.

All in all Pirates: Treasure Hunters is a wonderful gem of a game with a sadly dwindling playerbase. If you are a fan of MOBA style games, I highly suggest you try it! It's an exceptionally fun breath of fresh air when compared to other MOBAs. If you enjoy it, please tell your friends about it too! This game truly deserves a thriving playerbase!
