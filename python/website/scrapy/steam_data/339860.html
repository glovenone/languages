What is AoF World Online:

AoF World Online is a single/multiplayer fantasy online experience where you unravel the mystery behind an illusive force threatening Fasaria and her 3 moons.

Bad slow grindful online Scifi/fantasy RPG.

The premise is cool an a bit like Star Wars: you are a Jedi Guardian whose job is to defend this star system from an approaching death star huge evil space station by upgrading your own space station and fighting on the planets below; oh, and you're also allowed to play as Sith evil Dominion. Unfortunately, the actual game is bad. The writing is bad. The interface is bad. The level design is bad. The grinding is bad. You need to walk everywhere, even to the next planet (even if you have wings), and while you can "run", if you did it any slower it'd look like the moonwalk. The glitches are bad (had to quit the game 4 times). The NPCs are bad (there's an attribute NPC you have to seek out when you level to upgrade your stats; it's not a skill trainer, just gives you a choice of upgrading Att/Def/HP/Mana). The quests are triple bad ("bring me 10 rats" means you have to kill the 2 actual rats in the level 5 times each -- and you have to walk there - and there's no point to kill other mobs on the way there because you can only have one quest open at a time). There's no main quest line to advance, only mission points, and you grind them; you can actually advance by running around your base repeatedly opening the same boxes (they even have a visible cooldown) and then crafting with the stuff you found and turning the result in. Did I mention the crafting is bad (twice so because of the interface)? You can't craft any equipment, and none drops.

The developer is friendly and posted a nice reply to my original short review, which is why I put in the hours to create another character, played to complete the first zone and then some more to peek into zone 3, wrestle with the end boss of zone 2, and and get my first set of wings. I feel I have a pretty good grasp of the game by now. However, the execution is such that it suffers from bad design decisions everywhere. I haven't found a single aspect of this game that didn't have something wrong with it, except maybe the soundtrack and the way potions scale as you level (via your pharmacy skill). In all of my hours playing, I saw one other player (the game's on a bundle that sold well over 1000 copies), so I can't comment on the party system or the chat.

I have to admit this game has all of the fascination of a pileup on the motorway. As with a clicker game, you can get into it and go through the motions thinking "wtf" all the way, knowing you're playing a bad game and still continuing precisely because it is so predictable and requires little thought or skill. But why would you want to when you could play other RPGs that outclass this one in just about every respect?

Don't.

Thus far we have giant â¥â¥â¥ empty maps (that require pointless running), unengaging combat, and combat that requires continuous clicking and takes a lot longer than it should. I have played games that were free to play that are many times better than this, and from my first hour in game, I can't reccommend this at all. I will however, continue playing, and perhaps things will get better and I didn't just waste 30$

Edit: All right, after.. approximately 30-40 more minutes after first impressions, I decided I couldn't take it anymore (partially because the camera is awful, but also because I seemed to have fallen through the floor...)

Anyway, the game is not good, and especially for the asking price, I would say don't buy this game unless you literally have nothing better to do. Even if the devs are the greatest people in the world, and they could be, this game is pretty awful.

Too much running time between encounters, pretty much empty maps, junk crafting mecahnics (in that it's incredibly time consuming and there's no way to craft multiples at a time.. just have to click one by one) make this game.. Well, pretty awful in my opinion.

The entire thing would have worked better as a jrpg with pixels, and the game certainly feels like an (old) jrpg with pxiels and awful camera... in 3d.

So yea, I would highly reccommend against buying this, unless you'd like to be generous and give the devs a donation. Haven't tried the other package (battle tactics, or chess) and that could be better, but I would definitely pan the primary game itself.

Edit 2: Since people are clearly not finding this review helpful either via bias or because they think I need more information, let's update this review again.

Why Combat is Bad: Combat is basically sitting next to a monster and using auto attacks on it. Each auto attack is 3 mana. In between auto attacks, you have the option to use one of the skills you have unlocked which consume more mana and do extra damage as compared to an auto attack. (Or use one of the hp/mana consumables) While this is acceptable for something like a jrpg, in a format such at this game, it leads for very dull, uninteresting combat where you sit around and wait for cooldowns to pop up before clicking on a button.

I suppose one could compare this to many themeparks on the market, but for this game it's especially bad because the graphics are lack luster. On the flipside, the simplicity means that anyone can understand combat after a few minutes of it, so the game has that going for it.

Another gripe I have (minor, really) is that hp/mana do not regenerate out of combat. You must use consumables to restores these stats, or walk into a glowing barrier like thing, where upon you get teleported to the entrance of the zone with full health, but the same amount of mana.

The crafting of consumables is easy, but because the crafting menus don't allow for producing more than one 'set' at a time, you end up sitting at the npc's who craft (Yes, your character does not actually craft, as far as I can tell), for a long time, reptitively clicking "craft->main menu_>craft->main menu" until you can finally craft either mana packs or health packs, which each require at minimum 6 clicks, as far as I can tell.

Now, all of this is bad, but what compounds all of it and makes it worse is the maps.

The maps are HUGE, giant!... And very, very empty. Now, I could be completely wrong about this because I didn't spend much time exploring any of the maps, but the maps seem to be huge just to be huge, with 2-3 monster spawns of each enemy 10-15 seconds away from each other. This is annoying at best, especially given that either running or walking is relatively slow, there's no eye candy to explore, and the camera is jerky and takes some time to get used to.

So then, we have bad combat, irritating crafting, giant, empty maps, and bad cameras.

Anything else I'm forgetting..?

Ah quests. Quests are generic kill monsters here, or find the npc. Nothing intuitive about them, and they use all the worst aspects of the game to complete (combat and moving from place to place).

Gathering is acceptable, albeit very lackluster in that you walk up to a box and click gather. You wait a few seconds and then the resources are in your inventory. Wait about 15 seconds before gathering the same type of node again, because otherwise the boxes will be marked as 'empty' (No, they don't despawn).

I think that should just about cover why I thought this game was bad, and I hope others find this more helpful now that I've gone over the game more in depth.

As a side note, I will say the game would have made a perfectly good jrpg, but the game attempts to be a jrpg in 3d format and... does so very badly.
