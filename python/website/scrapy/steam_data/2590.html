About This Game

Somewhere in deep space, at an apparently abandoned mining base on the asteriod Alpha Prime, something has gone terribly wrong. Miners were warned that the hubbardium they were mining was too dangerous, but the lure of unimaginable wealth was too strong. Alpha Prime is a terrible game, and I kind of like it that way.

It's chock full of awful encounters, you fight the same three enemies the entire game, half the guns are useless and the other half only slightly less so, and levels seem to stretch on endlessly as they blur together. "Balance? To hell with that" you can hear Black Element yell as a barely discernible enemy on the other side of the room head shots you with a shotgun. Elements from other games are unglamorously fitted into parts of the game, like a patchwork quilt of design that's been out of style for years and manages to be significantly worse still than what they were lifted from.

There do not currently exist words in the English language strong enough to properly describe the true horror that is Alpha Prime's writing, such an elongated mess of horrendous characters, dialogue unfit for a B movie, plot lines thrown out and then just left there, and a bit of casual misogyny thrown in just for flavor. It's agonizing to listen to, stilted voice acting keeping it from even being somewhat amusing, and there is so, so much of it.

Alpha Prime is bad; a masterwork of mediocrity at the best of times. But again...I kind of liked it, maybe because for as miserable as it most certainly is, it's strangely polished in ways that made it kind of endearing if only ever so slightly. It feels like a game that might have been an obscure hit a decade ago, and I guess part of me still enjoys the sort of game that is so unmistakably rubbish, but its developer still clearly put a ton of effort into making it so I'm willing to go along for the ride even as I ask myself what is wrong with me. It's not broken, well, mostly. It's just bland, tedious, uninspired and a posterchild for dreadful videogame writing (though I will definitely be using "total lamers" as an insult).

Can I recommend it? Probably not. Would I play it again? Not on your life! Do I regret playing it? I wouldn't say so.

I have nearly 500 games in my Steam account, and the vast majority of them are still unplayed. So now I'm dedicating an hour to each of these games and writing a series of One Hour Reviews.

One Hour Reviews - Game 6
Alpha Prime
Purchase Date: 1/16/2014

Alpha Prime is another game I picked up thanks to a Humble Bundle. 18 months later I am finally getting to check it out. Turns out I could have waited.

Alpha Prime starts out much like one of those B-movies you expect to see on the Sci-Fi channel, with a bit of a cheesy story and some bad acting. After listing to a laughably bad impression of a drunk guy, I meet my ex-girlfriend and get an exposition dump to explain the start of the game. So about the first 10 minutes of my hour long review is spent in the opening cutscenes.

Eventually though I end up on a planet and find myself in trouble in no time. I quickly discover this game has all of the hallmarks of a standard shooter. It starts me with a crappy melee weapon and slowly but surely I find better weapons to upgrade to. And it's all the weapons you'd expect to see from pistols, to shotguns, to assault rifles and grenades.

Along with some rather bad acting and dialogue, everytime I would pick up a weapon, the game would tell me "Picked Pistol". Not picked up. I started to feel like I was playing Pokemon. Pistol, I choose you! Shotgun, I choose you!

The enemy AI seemed pretty dumb, although they were all good shots, so it still added some difficulty to the combat. You definitely want to quick save the game often, as you will be reloading a lot. But not necessarily because you died in combat. Once I had to reload because the entire game crashed. Another time I was crawling through an airduct (another hallmark of a first person shooter), and I somehow managed to crawl through a fan without the game registering a collision. So I kept crawling through the airduct but then I ran out of level, and fell out of the world. Some games when you fall out of the world it will 'reset' you to a nearby save spot. This game just kills you dead.

The game lets you interact with items in the game, although most of the time it serves no purpose. There was one time I had to drop a sheet of aluminum over a hole in the floor to run across it. A few times I opened up air ducts or lockers. But a lot of objects were just randomly lying around like books or boxes.

My favorite though was the random basketball I ran across. Not only that but I found it in a room with a basketball hoop. I wasted several minutes trying in vain to make a shot. It's hard to actually throw items in the game. All you can do is drop things, but if you happen to be moving your mouse as you drop it, you can kind of 'fling' things around. In the end, all I succeeded in doing was get the basketball stuck in mid air. I even tried shooting it down with my assault rifle, but it wasn't going anywhere.

Between the crash, the glitches, and the bad dialog, you're probably thinking this isn't a very good game, and you'd be right. The only charm it has is in that B-movie vibe you get from the story, but even then it's pretty cheesy and there are some gaps in logic that make it hard to ignore.

Like Pablo. Pablo is a character you meet, who is kind of charming with his broken English. He gets shot during a cutscene and goes on about his shot leg and how he can't go with you. Then the cut scene ends, and there is Pablo standing up on both legs, watching as I leave. Meanwhile, 5 feet away is a med station. I guess the med stations don't work on bulletholes in legs or something.

It's not so much that I dislike Alpha Prime, but there's really just nothing particularly redeeming about it. There are simply much better games out there to spend your time on.

Overall Recommendation: Pass
