About This Game

Test your ability in this casual survival game!
Hit the right color robots to get maximum score, how much time will you be able to stay in space?

-Classic 8 bit style arcade game .
-Hall of Fame with the best 15 scores.
-15 Steam achievements.
-Play solo or with your friend in cooperative mode.
-Pixel-Art design and original soundtrack.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, Vista, 7, 8.
    • Processor: Intel Core 2 Duo
    • Memory: 256 MB RAM
    • Graphics: Intel HD 3000
    • DirectX: Version 8.0
    • Storage: 15 MB available space

Product received for free
PSA: I received a free copy of this game on a public giveaway run by the developer.

Super Blue Fighter is an arcade, retro-style, fast-paced, 2D arena style. I believe that is the ebst description I can make of this game. Let's review the main points of this game:

+ The soundtrack is amazingly good.
+ The gameplay is smooth and the mechanics are fairly simple to understand and execute.
+ The difficult curve ramps up really well, each level being faster and more populated with threats.

And here are some takeaways:

- It gets boring after a while, needs more gamemodes or challenges in order to keep it interesting.
- The game menus are surprisingly bad in contrast to the really good gameplay:
- Can't select resolution, windowed mode... No graphical options at all.
- Can't rebind controls, which in my particular case, is really annoying, since I usually tend to use WASD for movement and not the arrow keys.

My final judgement is that this game has huge potential and room for many upgrades. However, I also must admit that it plays really nice and it's really entertaining for what it is currently offering.

Super Blue Fighter is a fun to play casual indie game with arcade characteristics. This game is an excellent entertainer in terms that it removes boredom and is for all ages. It can also serve its purpose to the more intune gamers, as a fun retro fps game.

In this game your task as a player is to touch all the system selected units, without crossing over to the wrong colored units. You do this on a square grid, be aware the purpose of this game is only to entertain. So theres not much of a storyline, but there is however a leader board where your scores get recorded. You can just touch your system specific colored unit and gain points. You can even do this by planting bombs.

But be careful of this addicting game, it can take hours of your play time with its simple design, presentability and concept.Some times you get shot by other units and die, or get trapped by your own bombs and in that way it is fun gameplay. The music is good, but the graphics is early 80's.

There are many pros and no cons to this game, but to mention a few:-

PROS

1. Its simple yet addicting
2. It has got neat music
3. It cuts out boredom
4. Its pocket friendly

CONS

1.The only draw back is it has got no story line

All in all its a great time passer, a solid 5/10 for repalyability

Quite a fun an relaxing simple casual arcade style game. The music is well made and reminds a bit at times strangely of something you'd hear in Phantasy Star or old Megadrive/Genesis games with the old Yahama synth sound. It seems to run on a endless board loop which get's more and more challenging as you progress levels with a pretty balanced diffculty curve. Scoring higher points can be assisted with the aid of using bombs to create chain attacks for higher bonus points. The controls and hitbox detection are pretty well done, although just catching the tail end of a bomb blast that you think you can avoid can be annoying at times. Certainly worth picking up for such a cheap price if you like older arcade style games. Well recommended.
