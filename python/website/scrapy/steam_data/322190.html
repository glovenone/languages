Introduction


Steamworld Heist is a side-scrolling, turn-based strategy game that is set on a space sci-fi/steampunk environment with its gameplay being inspired by the combination of both the X-COM and Worms series.


Gameplay


As you start the game, you will be introduced to five (5) levels of difficulty that suits your preference. Those are "Casual", "Regular", "Experienced", "Veteran", and "Elite", and as it increases in difficulty, it also yields more bonus experience the harder it is (and you can change it before you start any mission).

At your disposal is a space pirate crew composed of robots referred to as "steambots" and is led by Captain Piper Faraday. Along the way, you will find recruitable characters that can be enlisted by either proving your worth through the amount of "reputation" you have, paying them with "swag", or in some cases will join you by their own accord.

The gameplay focuses on mission-based levels which yields reputation in the form of golden stars that indicates how well you performed on that certain level once you clear it.There are objectives that you need to follow and once they are cleared, an escape route will be opened for your members to finish the mission. You can also find loot called "swag bags" and "epic swag" in these stages that appears in a form of a sack and a box respectively, and its contents are unknown until you have revealed them at the stage's end.

The way the battle system works in the game is turn-based and each unit can do two types of action per turn. Moving through the area and re-positioning yourself while in the orange highlighted area will allow to still be able do an action after moving, but if you "sprint" (represented by blue areas), your character will end its turn once you move on its position.
While you are able to do action, you can either fire your weapon, use your secondary equipment, or use your "active abilities".

Each weapon has its own merits, damage area, and aiming type which are all specified on their descriptions. In accordance to this, your characters can only equip certain types of weapon available for them (for example, Piper can only use Sharpshooter and Handgun weapons). Another type of equipment are the hats and secondary equipment, and unlike weapons, any character will be able to equip them (though hats are purely for fashion, while the secondary equipment has their respective uses).

Upon finishing the mission, you are will receive the reputation according to what objectives you have cleared and an additional star per epic swag you get minus the number of fallen members. You will also be able to unlock your loots and reveal what item they might be and receive awarded experience points that is distributed evenly to all alive crew members after the mission. When a crew member levels-up, they will get either a passive or active skill that will help through your future endeavors.



The Pros

  • Fun and Challenging Battle System - I really love the mechanics of their battle system as well as its weapon physics (like the ricocheting of the bullets and the splash damage from explosives), and even enacts the unsteadiness of the aim which requires both calculation and perfect timing of the player (which further heightens the challenge). It also has that added satisfaction when you land a calculated no-scope MLG pro shot, and that surprise factor when you accidentally hit an enemy that wasn't planned. Overall, it brings a very wholesome gameplay that makes you wanting more and more.

  • Tons of Content - From the numerous items, weapons and equipment (and swag) for you to collect, to the various characters available to recruit and play as, and the dozens of challenging missions that you can engage on, you can easily have a day’s worth of hours in gameplay time that awaits you. Oh, and the hats. Have I mentioned that there are various hats (like cat-on-top hat or some anime-related hat) available for you to suit-up your steambots?

  • Brilliant Soundtracks - Not only that the sound effects perfectly fits the sci-fi/steampunk nature of the game's theme, it also showcases some really nice songs/soundtracks that catches the ambiance of what a classic midnight bar should be. My favorite track is "Honeybee" by Steam Powered Giraffe.

  • Clean Game Graphics - Similar to the developer's earlier game (SteamWorld Dig), this new entry to the series presents a very clean art design throughout the whole game which is something that I always expect a game would have since not only it looks visually appealing, but also prevents possible confusion that players might encounter in terms of either the game mechanics or menus.


The Cons

  • Lack of Ability Choices - To be clear, I am pertaining to the lack of "skill trees" that are normally available in role-playing games. While the game is great at its current set-up, I do think that it would be more enjoyable for players to be able to customize their preferred characters rather than blindly waiting for the abilities/power-ups unlock as they level-up. For example, you kept on playing a certain character that you got early in the game because you already invested the experience points to that one just to know that another character is more suited to your preferences/play-style.

  • No Co-op/Multi-player Mode - Unlike the X-COM and Worms games, both of which inspired the battle system of this game, the game only has single-player story mode available (at least during this review has been posted), in which is pretty important in terms of replayability and gives its players the option to enjoy the game with their family, friends or even duke it out with their rivals.

  • Repetitive Gameplay - Same old nemesis by the games from this genre. While the game is very fun and challenging that will occupy you for hours and hours of gameplay, it doesn't deny the fact that the same gameplay is presented during the course of the game. I was actually excited when I reached one the point of the game where a certain NPC tells you about a "brawl club" that you can join in since it might be a mini-game/mode added that is different from the regular battles. And while that mission did introduce a new part of the game, where it introduced a three-way battle between you and two other factions, the battle premise is no different which was disappointing in a certain way.


Final Thoughts

For those who are fond of turn-based games, this game is easily a MUST-BUY. And for the people who even have the slightest of interest of playing, it is worth the try and am pretty sure you will love it.

Rating - 9.4/10

SteamWorld Heist had actually been on my radar for quite a while. Mentioned by Austin Walker on The Giant Beastcast podcast earlier this year, the phrase “2D X-Com” set my hopes high. Not having a 3DS, I had to anxiously wait for it to come to PC. Every month or so I’d looked to see if there was even a release date, until finally, recently, it popped up on Steam.

Made by Image & Form, it’s been my turn-based tactical game of choice. These are the same developers who did SteamWorld Dig, which has the same aesthetic and charm, but is an adventure game in real-time. In Heist you play as a captain of a mercenary group of steambots (robots powered by steam). You’re a bit of a rogue with a heart of gold. The Han Solo of steambot piracy. It’s all presented charmingly in 2D, except for a couple of cutscenes. Each mission has turn-based combat also from a 2D perspective, and it takes some of the baseline mechanics from the recent X-Com series and repurposes them. The two mainstays are a cover system, and an action point system tied to movement.

Here’s the catch, where things break from X-Com or other turn-based cover shooters: you’re the one lining up your shots. No percentage based shooting, no hidden dice rolls. It’s all based on your ability to line up shots, in a very Worms-like manner, and have them connect. There’s a strong focus on being able to ricochet shots, in addition to using cover, which has a really enjoyable kinetic feel to it. I found myself turning my head to the side to try to line up shots. Pulling off a long shot, or a shot with a ricochet, is a blast of endorphins for me. It’s a “problem solved” moment, where a tactical challenge was faced and defeated.

Your character’s aim of each shot does ‘float’, for lack of a better term, up and down just enough to add to the challenge but not unfairly. There’s a level of anticipation while you’re trying to line it up. And while some guns have a laser sight, most do not. Not really a problem with close range guns like shotguns, but bouncing explosives and pistols each present a challenge and heighten the stakes.

Additionally, you also have a roster of characters that have different activatable abilities and strengths. And that last bit might actually be more crucial than I’m giving it credit, as it really added to my enjoyment in switching and exploring the different classes (for the most part — sorry Bea!). They offered enough variety and provided some different solutions to the missions you run into.

All of this has me actually considering a 2nd run. Most of that has to do with the tactical layer. The engagements were fun to solve and making those shots never got old. The missions themselves had enough variety, and the overall length was solid. I finished the game on the default difficulty level (Experienced) in 14 hours. Playing a couple missions here and there was easy and enjoyable, and while I was ready for things to end, I wouldn’t mind restarting on a harder difficulty.
I didn’t find the story particularly engaging, but the bits of humor scattered throughout were amusing enough. The music was a mixed bag as the general background and battle music was a little too monotonous, but some of the songs you heard in the bars or at the end were great and brought a light-hearted tone. The visuals speak for themselves and the animations were well done and brought their own charms as well.

In closing, X-Com or Final Fantasy Tactics, this is not, and thank goodness. Not that I don’t want more of those games (see Arcadian Atlas for a modern hopeful at recreating the latter), but I definitely want turn-based games going in more directions. Using 2D physics is a fun way to mix the genre up. I‘d love to see this concept revisited, and possibly expanded upon.

Steamworld Heist is an amazing strategy game where XCOM meets Worms in Metroidvania style gameplay to create one of the best games I’ve played this year.

Story
The story of SteamWorld Heist follows Piper, a cowbot whose ‘job’ it is to visit planets making deliveries and gathering ‘moisture’ in a time where the Royalists run everything. But Scrappers, rogue cowbots who destroy others for parts, are on the rise and terrorizing every corner of the galaxy so you decide to lend a helping hand with your crew. The story actually gets a lot more detailed within two or three missions and is really well written. So far (I haven’t completed the game) I’m loving following it and I feel it is one of the strong suits of the game if you follow it and read all the dialogue (if you don’t it’s really easy to skip it and miss what is going on). The characters and crew you meet along the way are really loveable and feel a lot more than just random soldiers or cannon fodder which comes from just how well the dialouge and small bios are written.

General Gameplay
The gameplay of SteamWorld Heist is so freaking fun. If you’ve ever played XCOM and Worms you’ll find it melds the gameplay style of those together perfectly making for an amazing turn-based strategy that requires a lot of skill and tactics rather than luck.
You start with two crew members which you can control but you’ll be able to recruit more crew members as the missions go on. Each crew member has a class and their own stats and abilities which you’ll be able to level up as you complete missions and earn experience. Though the abilities are predefined for each class and you cannot choose how to upgrade each crew member you can choose which crew goes onto which mission allowing you to level some members first if you prefer their skills. When you begin a mission you’ll be allowed to choose your difficulty and you’ll gain experience and rewards based on whichever you select, harder reaping better benefits but also having larger penalties for failure. Once you’ve chosen your level and difficulty you’ll be able to change your loadout choosing weapons and items for use in combat that you can equip your crew with then you’re ready to battle. Weapons can be class specific or used by any class, it depends and each weapon can have different perks like added crit chance or guaranteed cripple and some weapons are rarer than others with a few not being able to be bought only found. Items can be bought at any shop and range from grenades and melee weapons to healing kits, all of which can be using during missions.
The gameplay is turn based and as I said before is reminiscent of XCOM. You can move a certain amount of tiles per turn and also have the option to attack (depending on the distance you moved). Your attacks can be controlled much like you can when playing worms, you just choose your direction and fire either directly at your target or using a little ricochet to help you along. Different weapons will attack differently, have different effects and do different damage so you’ll be able to customize your loadout to your playstyle.
Enemies you kill drop hats sometimes (hats are just superficial but for some reason I find myself wanting to own every single one) and you can find ‘swag’ throughout the levels which will reveal its content at the end of the level. This can range from new weapons for your crew, items to sell or moisture. You don’t have to collect all the items on the level but I strongly advise it if you want your game to not get too hard as you go along because everything you find can be used or sold. Stuff you don’t want can be sold for moisture meaning exploring is always the better option and you feel rewarded for not just rushing.
Each mission you start will have one or multiple goals which you’ll need to complete before advancing to the next level but you don’t always have to complete them all, some are entirely optional. Some of the missions are optional also and you can ignore them if you don’t feel like it’s worth your time or if it’s too hard meaning you’re able to play the game at your own pace rather than being forced through the motions.

Pros
--- Great easy to learn gameplay.
--- Loveable crew members.
--- Fantastic story with well-written dialogue
--- Multiple genres melded well.
--- Intuitive gameplay and interfaces.
---Procedurally generated maps for replayability.
--- HATS OKAY. HATS..
---That song in Gat’s bar. Seriously the best thing i’ve heard this year.

Cons
--- The robots ...breathe…? Meaning some shots i’ve missed because the aim changes ever so slightly without warning.
--- Can’t block, defend or set up any way to protect yourself between rounds like you can in similar games.
--- Can’t compare item stats when buying them.

Summary
SteamWorld Heist is one of those fantastic games that keeps you coming back for more and more every time. The unique way it combines multiple game styles to create such a perfect game is unbelievable and it’s yet another game I can’t recommend highly enough. The gameplay works perfectly, the story is well written and the characters are engaging. This is one of the few games out there i’d say is fully deserving of it’s price tag and worth every single cent.You’ll find SteamWorld Heist satisfying cravings you didn’t even know you had.

Portable Description
SteamWorld Heist is a fantastic turn-based strategy game sporting vibrant and polished aesthetics. This steampunk space adventure focuses on skill rather than chance, and is jam-packed with bullet-bouncing, turret weaving, robot commanding fun.

Story
SteamWorld’s Narrative
Following the events of a shattered planet, Cowbots farm leftover land for moisture and water. These resources are a precious commodity used by all bots, excluding the diesel-driven Royalists. With a new danger lurking in the depths of space, gentlebots have so much to worry about. Not wanting undue attention from the Royal Space Force, Piper Faraday, notorious smuggler and occasional pirate, leads a crew of seemingly misfit mercenaries on a quest to drive the bad-for-business Scrappers gang away from her domain. Little does she know what the universe has in mind for her and her crew.

Gameplay
Starting the Adventure
Piper’s journey begins upon selecting one of three available game slots, then choosing a preferred difficulty level. These difficulties are listed as: Casual < Regular < Experienced < Veteran < Elite. Each tier provides various changes to: enemy damage, enemy quantity, mission failure punishment and bonus XP earned. SteamWorld Heist’s gaming structure is comprised of missions (primary and side/challenge missions) that must be unlocked with a reputation requirement (stars). Losing or aborting a mission will result in a loss of water (currency) at any difficulty level above Casual.

Missions & Loot
Every mission rewards the player with reputation which is used to proceed further into the game and unlock additional characters/weapons. Most missions are scored on a 3-star rating system; however, there will be missions that score differently. On each mission, various opportunities are presented to obtain loot in the form of loot bags, epic loot (chests), or hats. Perfecting a mission requires completing the objectives (clear the ship, defeat X, destroy X, retrieve all loot etc.) without having any casualties and retrieving the epic loot(s). Before beginning any mission, a loadout screen is presented where the player will choose the participating characters and their equipment. Due to a crew limitation on missions, it is advised to change up which characters go on each mission regularly to keep levels relatively even; however, this is definitely not required.

Each mission environment is procedurally generated, providing a unique experience for every player. Heist never feels repetitive because of this, along with the fact that random missions will have a theme (turret factory, explosive carrier etc.). Boss fights also introduce eccentric characters with unique abilities. If that’s not enough, alarms that trigger infinitely spawning enemies/turrets or countdown timers with difficult consequences may be included.

Combat
Heist’s combat utilizes a turn-based strategy system with a unique mechanic where the weapons are manually aimed and shot by the player. This removes a chance or hit % mechanic that many other similar turn-based strategy games use, and promotes skill-shots or ricocheting around objects to reach the target(s). The entire crew will individually perform their turns before the enemy performs theirs. Each turn is comprised of a movement phase, and a sprint/action phase.

Movement & Sprint Phase
Positioning is crucial in successfully attaining reputation. Each mission is littered with destructible barrels and walls for partial cover; however utilizing complete cover will be necessary at times. Typically averaging about 6 move spaces per turn, characters can move further using a sprint mechanic. Although it is not split into discrete phases, transitioning from the movement phase into the sprint phase is marked with a differently colored marker or floor space. If the player moves a character into a space beyond the movement range and requires sprinting, no further action can be made by that character. Sprinting is useful for running to partial cover, hiding from turrets, or eluding spawning enemies.

Action Phase
If the player chooses to keep a character within their movement range, that character can perform an action. An action would either constitute firing that character’s weapon after aiming it, using a special utility item’s free action, or performing a melee attack if an enemy is adjacent to the prioritized character. There are many different weapon types in Heist, and some weapons have specific requirements for use, such as a particular sniper rifle’s “move or shoot – pick one” specification. A rarity and tiered weapon system also promotes loot-based progression. Rare weapons will generally have higher base damage/critical damage stats, or attachments equipped (laser sight).

Aiming provides the primary game mechanic that sets Heist apart from other turn-based strategy games. If the player chooses to fire a weapon, it must be manually aimed to deal efficient damage. Depending on the weapon attachment or gun type, the projectiles may travel in an arc, bounce multiple times with or without subsequent damage reduction per bounce, travel in a straight line, or scatter. The characters have the ability to shoot directly straight above or below, 90 degrees from the horizontal; however, each character breathes, adding another level of skill required to hit the target(s).

Character Progression
Each character utilized in a mission gains XP after successful completion of that mission. At each level up, a passive ability will be gained. Some characters have unique abilities, while others gain more stats. A couple unique abilities that are very crucial in deciding team composition include:

  • Kill Shot: upon delivering a fatal shot, the character gets one additional shot
  • Inspire: Increase adjacent allies’ damage by +1, along with your own if Inspired

Presentation
Art
Even though Heist is a steampunk world, it is very colorful and glossy. The artwork is extremely well polished, providing a truly immersive role-play. As opposed to using the traditional rustic, dull or textured color palettes seen in many steampunk artwork, bright blues, pinks and greens are employed. The settings used for the ships can also vary from oil-crusted rigs to royal and elegant cruisers. The pit stops/bars/social hubs in Heist are very active, filled with neon signs and questionable characters.

Conclusion
Current State of the Game
SteamWorld Heist was just recently released on Steam (Dec. 10th 2015 for the PS4, 3DS, Vita, WiiU and Xbox One versions). The developers have shown active and continued interest in Heist, as can be seen in the Steam Discussions board. With a desire to receive input on support and feature requests, SteamWorld’s popularity is still growing.

Advised Suggestions
Although Heist is in a near perfected iteration of its development cycle, there are a couple suggestions that would improve gameplay; these are listed below.

  • Allow loadout changes from the “Start” button menu, or upon item acquisition instead of only at mission startup
  • Mark what difficulty a mission was beaten in to aid with achievement tracking

Verdict
With everything taken into consideration, SteamWorld Heist is definitely recommended to any fan of strategy games, especially turn-based ones. Kind to newcomers or veterans alike, there isn’t much left to be desired from Heist. It truly deserves to be a contender for 2016's indie game of the year.

Introduction


Steamworld Heist is a side-scrolling, turn-based strategy game that is set on a space sci-fi/steampunk environment with its gameplay being inspired by the combination of both the X-COM and Worms series.


Gameplay


As you start the game, you will be introduced to five (5) levels of difficulty that suits your preference. Those are "Casual", "Regular", "Experienced", "Veteran", and "Elite", and as it increases in difficulty, it also yields more bonus experience the harder it is (and you can change it before you start any mission).

At your disposal is a space pirate crew composed of robots referred to as "steambots" and is led by Captain Piper Faraday. Along the way, you will find recruitable characters that can be enlisted by either proving your worth through the amount of "reputation" you have, paying them with "swag", or in some cases will join you by their own accord.

The gameplay focuses on mission-based levels which yields reputation in the form of golden stars that indicates how well you performed on that certain level once you clear it.There are objectives that you need to follow and once they are cleared, an escape route will be opened for your members to finish the mission. You can also find loot called "swag bags" and "epic swag" in these stages that appears in a form of a sack and a box respectively, and its contents are unknown until you have revealed them at the stage's end.

The way the battle system works in the game is turn-based and each unit can do two types of action per turn. Moving through the area and re-positioning yourself while in the orange highlighted area will allow to still be able do an action after moving, but if you "sprint" (represented by blue areas), your character will end its turn once you move on its position.
While you are able to do action, you can either fire your weapon, use your secondary equipment, or use your "active abilities".

Each weapon has its own merits, damage area, and aiming type which are all specified on their descriptions. In accordance to this, your characters can only equip certain types of weapon available for them (for example, Piper can only use Sharpshooter and Handgun weapons). Another type of equipment are the hats and secondary equipment, and unlike weapons, any character will be able to equip them (though hats are purely for fashion, while the secondary equipment has their respective uses).

Upon finishing the mission, you are will receive the reputation according to what objectives you have cleared and an additional star per epic swag you get minus the number of fallen members. You will also be able to unlock your loots and reveal what item they might be and receive awarded experience points that is distributed evenly to all alive crew members after the mission. When a crew member levels-up, they will get either a passive or active skill that will help through your future endeavors.



The Pros

  • Fun and Challenging Battle System - I really love the mechanics of their battle system as well as its weapon physics (like the ricocheting of the bullets and the splash damage from explosives), and even enacts the unsteadiness of the aim which requires both calculation and perfect timing of the player (which further heightens the challenge). It also has that added satisfaction when you land a calculated no-scope MLG pro shot, and that surprise factor when you accidentally hit an enemy that wasn't planned. Overall, it brings a very wholesome gameplay that makes you wanting more and more.

  • Tons of Content - From the numerous items, weapons and equipment (and swag) for you to collect, to the various characters available to recruit and play as, and the dozens of challenging missions that you can engage on, you can easily have a day’s worth of hours in gameplay time that awaits you. Oh, and the hats. Have I mentioned that there are various hats (like cat-on-top hat or some anime-related hat) available for you to suit-up your steambots?

  • Brilliant Soundtracks - Not only that the sound effects perfectly fits the sci-fi/steampunk nature of the game's theme, it also showcases some really nice songs/soundtracks that catches the ambiance of what a classic midnight bar should be. My favorite track is "Honeybee" by Steam Powered Giraffe.

  • Clean Game Graphics - Similar to the developer's earlier game (SteamWorld Dig), this new entry to the series presents a very clean art design throughout the whole game which is something that I always expect a game would have since not only it looks visually appealing, but also prevents possible confusion that players might encounter in terms of either the game mechanics or menus.


The Cons

  • Lack of Ability Choices - To be clear, I am pertaining to the lack of "skill trees" that are normally available in role-playing games. While the game is great at its current set-up, I do think that it would be more enjoyable for players to be able to customize their preferred characters rather than blindly waiting for the abilities/power-ups unlock as they level-up. For example, you kept on playing a certain character that you got early in the game because you already invested the experience points to that one just to know that another character is more suited to your preferences/play-style.

  • No Co-op/Multi-player Mode - Unlike the X-COM and Worms games, both of which inspired the battle system of this game, the game only has single-player story mode available (at least during this review has been posted), in which is pretty important in terms of replayability and gives its players the option to enjoy the game with their family, friends or even duke it out with their rivals.

  • Repetitive Gameplay - Same old nemesis by the games from this genre. While the game is very fun and challenging that will occupy you for hours and hours of gameplay, it doesn't deny the fact that the same gameplay is presented during the course of the game. I was actually excited when I reached one the point of the game where a certain NPC tells you about a "brawl club" that you can join in since it might be a mini-game/mode added that is different from the regular battles. And while that mission did introduce a new part of the game, where it introduced a three-way battle between you and two other factions, the battle premise is no different which was disappointing in a certain way.


Final Thoughts

For those who are fond of turn-based games, this game is easily a MUST-BUY. And for the people who even have the slightest of interest of playing, it is worth the try and am pretty sure you will love it.

Rating - 9.4/10

Curator Pixel - Pursuing objectivity with a critical eye

Originally a Nintendo 3DS title, Steamworld Heist manages to succesfully transition to the Steam platform with increased fidelity. The developer decided to take their next title in a different direction, drawing inspiration from games like XCOM and Worms. The end result is a game with a unique combination of mechanics which make for addictive and rewarding gameplay.

Presentation
Despite being set in a time of struggle, Steamworld Heist manages to maintain a beautiful artstyle, with every asset showing high levels of fidelity. Characters are very well animated throughout the experience, and on top of that, the game features fully voiced cutscenes which help further set the tone of the game. Nothing strikes you as lazy or out of place in this game.

The audio is also impressive and packs a real punch. Great sound effects give the player a sense of satisfaction when downing an enemy, or a sense of urgency once they hear an alarm go off. The game features a great soundtrack as well, from the main menu’s theme, to the dramatic combat music and catchy tavern songs. All music is performed in-studio by the band Steam Powered Giraffe.

Plot
Earth has blown up, making water a very scarce resource. The game’s main character, Piper Faraday, and her crew of smugglers will have to discover why the Scrappers, a criminal organization, are causing so much trouble with farmers and other citizens. While handling this situation, you must also keep a healthy stock of water supplies, and focus on avoiding the attention of the anti-smuggler Royal Space Force.

Interface/Controls
The interface feels very natural. It is easy to understand where your character can move, and which objects can be used for cover. Each character's path has a color which indicates if they can still shoot after moving. Damage is also presented in a form that is easy to comprehend, even without the need for massive numbers which clutter the screen. The game also features a very simple, but effective, inventory system, giving you the option to equip items to your crew members before each mission.

The controls are very well done. It is easy to cycle through your crew members and issue them orders. The game recommends the use of a gamepad, although less optimal mouse and keyboard controls are available in the options menu.

Gameplay
Steamworld Heist is primarily a turn-based strategy game. What sets this game apart from similar games is the 2D nature of it, and the ability to manually aim your weapons. This introduces a level of skill that is very unique for this genre, as you won’t be depending on hit chances and critical strike percentages. If a shot misses, then it’s solely your fault. On top of that, some of the game’s weapons feature a ricochet mechanic, meaning you can get really creative with how to shoot enemies that are taking cover.

The game has five levels of difficulty: Casual, Regular, Experienced, Veteran and Elite. So if you’re the type of player who only wants to see the story, you can do just that. Or if you’re up for a challenge that is more intense, you can go with the higher difficulties. Each mission is randomly generated, giving the game replayability. However, due to its randomized nature, luck can work for or against us. If a player finds themself struggling to complete a particular mission, they can opt to die or abandon and restart, hoping that the next generated map will be “easier”. Despite what could be considered a flaw in any other game, Steamworld Heist deters this in a very smart way by introducing a currency penalty every time we fail.

We collect loot in every mission, and are rewarded with reputation and experience at the end of each mission. Reputation is granted based on how successful a run was, and can be used to unlock new paths. It can also be used to recruit new crew members who want to join our cause. The game punishes sloppy performance, not only by diminishing the reputation gain, but also by preventing dead crew members from earning any experience at all.

The backstory of your crew members can be explored by initiating a conversation with them in between missions. An exclamation mark over their head will indicate when they have something new to say, which is reminiscent of other games in the genre. By not shoving a bunch of backstory into the player's face, the game provides a nice balance between gameplay and narrative, allowing the player to engage in the game’s lore if they so desire.

Conclusion
Steamworld Heist oozes with quality in almost every aspect of the game. It would be nice to have a multiplayer mode and a map editor, but this is no excuse to skip the game. Fun characters, amazing game design, and great music make this title one that every fan of the genre should play. At the same time, thanks to its flexible difficulty options, it serves as an entry point for players who want to get into the genre.
