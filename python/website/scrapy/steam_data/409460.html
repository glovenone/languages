What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“As we've moved through development over the last few years, we've created a game that we are super proud of! We released an alpha demo of Blackfaun some time ago, and it provided invaluable feedback. Since then we've made many additions and revisions to the game - both to mechanics and content - in large part due to player feedback.

Blackfaun is now nearing the end of alpha, and as such we feel that the game is in an decent state to show to a larger audience. We hope that through Early Access we can interact with players on a regular basis, gathering feedback and insights from a wider perspective - with the goal of modifying and refining Blackfaun so that it can be as fun and enjoyable for as many players as possible.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We anticipate that Blackfaun will have about a 6 month Early Access life cycle. (This is our best guess based on previous development time and is subject to change!)”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“In addition to shipping the remaining content and features that we have planned, we hope to incorporate as many ideas generated by the community as we can. We have a good handle on the scope of the game, but we aim to collaborate with players to evolve the mechanics as much as possible.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Blackfaun is currently in a mature alpha state.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We'd like to give our Early Access players a lower price point as a way of thanking them for providing invaluable feedback and to acknowledge their much-appreciated support of our game! With that in mind, we are considering increasing the price when we leave Early Access, but have no firm plans.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We'll be active in the forums, talking with players as much as possible to hear what the community has to say about what they like, and don't like! We are planning to be in the discussion threads hanging out and responding to playersâ thoughts, and taking copious notes on how we can make Blackfaun even more fun for players.”

Blackfaun is a roguelike game that was released in early December 2015 being developed by a lesser known team, Wild Guess Software. Roguelike is probably my favourite genre just behind RPG, so I was really looking forward to getting stuck into this game.

The game play is very similar to most top down dungeon crawler games, you have a top down view of your character and you have to navigate each level and reach the defeat the final boss on each stage. You have a regular attack (left click) and a special ability (right click); each of these will change throughout your run due to the best part of this game, the loot pickups. Your main attack will change in style, from single target damage, to AOE damage, to a more trap effect. Whilst your special ability can be all sorts of different attacks too, you really get to pick and choose how you play your character with the items on show in the game thus far. As you kill enemies they will drop a sort of potion, whether it is health or mana. Whilst exploring you will also find experience orbs, these orbs also offer the chance to give you a random increase to one of your stats. I thought this was a nice little addition that really makes exploring one of top aims whilst playing.

The game starts out without a tutorial, I would have liked to have seen one explain what everything does, but it is fairly simple to work out, and of course, since the game is still in Early Access, it could still be added. As you explore further into the dungeons, you will come across areas which have a shop keeper, allowing you to purchase access to loot chests using the gold you have looted. At the moment, there are only 5 or 6 item slots; I would really like to see this increased to include many other item slots, such as maybe an additional ring slot, capes, boots, and gloves. I think having tons of loot is what makes dungeon crawlers, and whilst Blackfaun has that, it would be just amazing to have even more!

The art style in Blackfaun reminded me very much of League of Legends, the bright colours and stunning looking environments just look awesome. The animations of all characters and sprites are spot on as well.

There is an underlying plot to Blackfaun, I was paying attention, but to be honest, I wanted to just continue the addictive gameplay. I couldnât get enough of it! I really see this game blowing up big time if the developers keep going as they are. I would love to see some sort of daily challenge mode introduced, or maybe an infinite gauntlet based mode too on top of the story mode.

At the moment, the game has 2 stages, which have about four levels in each of them. This took me just under an hour to complete, and it really left me with a sense of wanting more, immediately. There is definitely a lot of room for replay ability just going off the fact that the game play is so addictive. Another cool feature that has been included is that collecting achievements in game actually rewards you with more items in game too! This is the only game I can think of right now that actually does that with achievements, and itâs something more developers should be doing!

I didnât have any technical issues; I was playing at 1440p with a smooth 60fps at all times on full graphics. I was a little shocked to see the graphics options was just a slide bar, but maybe thatâs just there because of early access, since it is a PC game, we really need to see proper graphics options though.

To sum up, even though Blackfaun is in early access, I can easily highly recommend this game. The last update for the game was on the 30th December 2015, so the developers are active. The retail price at the moment is Â£10,99 â if you are a little hesitant about picking up an early access title, you may want to wait for it to go on sale, but if you are a roguelike/dungeon crawler fan like me, I cannot recommend this game enough. I can really see this game being top-notch as the developers continue to update this game.

Early Access Review
Posted: 23 December, 2015
This game was reviewed using a key provided by the developer and/or publisher for the purposes of an unbiased review for Your Game Source.

Over the past few years, many roguelikes (or roguelites) have been released to great success: Binding of Isaac, Don't Starve, and FTL, to name a few. Blackfaun is the newest addition to this list, just recently entering Steam Early Access. In Blackfaun, you play as a faun as she explores different dungeons on a quest to prove herself to her parents. Along the way, you will find treasure, fight enemies, and deal with an unexpected troublemaker. Can Blackfaun stand on its own, or is it another to add to the masses?

The gameplay can be best compared to that of Diablo meets Binding of Isaac. Using WASD to run around and the mouse to aim and shoot both primary and secondary spells, the goal of the game is to navigate the maze of rooms, reach the boss of the floor, and vanquish it so that the next floor can be unlocked. There are two acts (with a few floors each) that must be explored, with plans to add two more acts as development continues. While running around the maze, there is treasure to be found in the form of different weapons and armor. More often than not, these weapons and armor change the way the game will be played: some may cause the primary attack to become a wall of flames, while others might cause your armor to slow nearby enemies upon being damaged. Each item's special change feels meaningful and has to be considered when exploring.

The stats, however, not so much. The stat system feels confusing and ultimately ineffective. After killing enemies and picking up purple wisps, levels are earned and points can be put into three main stats: VIT, INT, and AGI. These main stats also branch out into three sub stats which are affected by both the total of the main stat and the items wielded by the character. The issue I have with this is that I never noticed any big change as stats were earned. One run I might pump just VIT, and the next I might pump just AGI, but in the end I felt like I was both doing and taking the same amount of damage. Even after equipping a few trinkets (which are unlocked by completing unclear objectives during runs), I still felt just as powerful as I did before I received the massive +110 VIT. After a few runs, the floors started feeling identical and the enemies felt predictable with only a few different variations for each floor. While the different spells provided different ways to go about the encounters, the encounters all felt the exact same. Much like the regular enemies, the low number of bosses per act (four) added to this feeling of sameness, despite each boss behaving completely differently from each other. That isn't to say that it feels bad to play; Blackfaun controls well and the gameplay is great if you're wanting some action without paying too much attention. If you're wanting an experience that is radically different each time you play through it, perhaps Blackfaun isn't for you.

In regards to aesthetics, the game looks quite nice. It isn't the best looking game on the market, but it definitely holds its own. The models of the characters and the enemies are easily distinguishable and nice to look at. The scenary of the mazes is also a pleasure to see, with both acts being distinct from one another. As different weapons and armor are earned, the character that you control can be seen equipped with them, contributing to that level of detail that isn't required but is nice to see.

The music and the sounds of Blackfaun aren't anything too spectacular. They're just alright. The music isn't bad at all and is quite nice for the first few runs, but after listening to the same track for multiple floors, I grew tired of it and wanted something different. The sounds felt the exact same way, especially after hearing a wolf whine for the hundredth time after you kill it. While not bad, they're not great, and in a genre that is supposed to pride itself on its replayability, I expected a bit more variety.

Overall, Blackfaun is far from being a bad game. Despite the same feeling from multiple playthroughs and the mediocre audio, Blackfaun is still enjoyable to play and there's just something that is incredibly satisfying about slaughtering massive groups of enemies. While it may not be quite there yet, I believe that Blackfaun will be able to stand out after some more content is added (which the developers have been very open about in their Development Roadmap). If you're looking for a roguelike, keep your eyes on Blackfaun. I'm rather faun-d of the future I see for it.

Blackfaun is a roguelike game that was released in early December 2015 being developed by a lesser known team, Wild Guess Software. Roguelike is probably my favourite genre just behind RPG, so I was really looking forward to getting stuck into this game.

The game play is very similar to most top down dungeon crawler games, you have a top down view of your character and you have to navigate each level and reach the defeat the final boss on each stage. You have a regular attack (left click) and a special ability (right click); each of these will change throughout your run due to the best part of this game, the loot pickups. Your main attack will change in style, from single target damage, to AOE damage, to a more trap effect. Whilst your special ability can be all sorts of different attacks too, you really get to pick and choose how you play your character with the items on show in the game thus far. As you kill enemies they will drop a sort of potion, whether it is health or mana. Whilst exploring you will also find experience orbs, these orbs also offer the chance to give you a random increase to one of your stats. I thought this was a nice little addition that really makes exploring one of top aims whilst playing.

The game starts out without a tutorial, I would have liked to have seen one explain what everything does, but it is fairly simple to work out, and of course, since the game is still in Early Access, it could still be added. As you explore further into the dungeons, you will come across areas which have a shop keeper, allowing you to purchase access to loot chests using the gold you have looted. At the moment, there are only 5 or 6 item slots; I would really like to see this increased to include many other item slots, such as maybe an additional ring slot, capes, boots, and gloves. I think having tons of loot is what makes dungeon crawlers, and whilst Blackfaun has that, it would be just amazing to have even more!

The art style in Blackfaun reminded me very much of League of Legends, the bright colours and stunning looking environments just look awesome. The animations of all characters and sprites are spot on as well.

I really see this game blowing up big time if the developers keep going as they are. I would love to see some sort of daily challenge mode introduced, or maybe an infinite gauntlet based mode too on top of the story mode.

At the moment, the game has 2 stages, which have about four levels in each of them. This took me just under an hour to complete, and it really left me with a sense of wanting more, immediately. There is definitely a lot of room for replay ability just going off the fact that the game play is so addictive. Another cool feature that has been included is that collecting achievements in game actually rewards you with more items in game too! Early Access Review
Posted: 23 December, 2015
This game was reviewed using a key provided by the developer and/or publisher for the purposes of an unbiased review for Your Game Source.

Over the past few years, many roguelikes (or roguelites) have been released to great success: Binding of Isaac, Don't Starve, and FTL, to name a few. Blackfaun is the newest addition to this list, just recently entering Steam Early Access. In Blackfaun, you play as a faun as she explores different dungeons on a quest to prove herself to her parents. Along the way, you will find treasure, fight enemies, and deal with an unexpected troublemaker. Can Blackfaun stand on its own, or is it another to add to the masses?

The gameplay can be best compared to that of Diablo meets Binding of Isaac. Using WASD to run around and the mouse to aim and shoot both primary and secondary spells, the goal of the game is to navigate the maze of rooms, reach the boss of the floor, and vanquish it so that the next floor can be unlocked. There are two acts (with a few floors each) that must be explored, with plans to add two more acts as development continues. While running around the maze, there is treasure to be found in the form of different weapons and armor. More often than not, these weapons and armor change the way the game will be played: some may cause the primary attack to become a wall of flames, while others might cause your armor to slow nearby enemies upon being damaged. Each item's special change feels meaningful and has to be considered when exploring.

The stats, however, not so much. The stat system feels confusing and ultimately ineffective. After killing enemies and picking up purple wisps, levels are earned and points can be put into three main stats: VIT, INT, and AGI. These main stats also branch out into three sub stats which are affected by both the total of the main stat and the items wielded by the character. The issue I have with this is that I never noticed any big change as stats were earned. One run I might pump just VIT, and the next I might pump just AGI, but in the end I felt like I was both doing and taking the same amount of damage. Even after equipping a few trinkets (which are unlocked by completing unclear objectives during runs), I still felt just as powerful as I did before I received the massive +110 VIT. After a few runs, the floors started feeling identical and the enemies felt predictable with only a few different variations for each floor. While the different spells provided different ways to go about the encounters, the encounters all felt the exact same. Much like the regular enemies, the low number of bosses per act (four) added to this feeling of sameness, despite each boss behaving completely differently from each other. That isn't to say that it feels bad to play; Blackfaun controls well and the gameplay is great if you're wanting some action without paying too much attention. If you're wanting an experience that is radically different each time you play through it, perhaps Blackfaun isn't for you.

In regards to aesthetics, the game looks quite nice. It isn't the best looking game on the market, but it definitely holds its own. The models of the characters and the enemies are easily distinguishable and nice to look at. The scenary of the mazes is also a pleasure to see, with both acts being distinct from one another. As different weapons and armor are earned, the character that you control can be seen equipped with them, contributing to that level of detail that isn't required but is nice to see.

The music and the sounds of Blackfaun aren't anything too spectacular. They're just alright. The music isn't bad at all and is quite nice for the first few runs, but after listening to the same track for multiple floors, I grew tired of it and wanted something different. The sounds felt the exact same way, especially after hearing a wolf whine for the hundredth time after you kill it. While not bad, they're not great, and in a genre that is supposed to pride itself on its replayability, I expected a bit more variety.

Overall, Blackfaun is far from being a bad game. Despite the same feeling from multiple playthroughs and the mediocre audio, Blackfaun is still enjoyable to play and there's just something that is incredibly satisfying about slaughtering massive groups of enemies. While it may not be quite there yet, I believe that Blackfaun will be able to stand out after some more content is added (which the developers have been very open about in their Development Roadmap). If you're looking for a roguelike, keep your eyes on Blackfaun. I'm rather faun-d of the future I see for it.
