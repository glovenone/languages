About This Game

Presentation


Thorne: Tales of The Crow is a character-driven, episodic RPG developed by a Brazilian Indie company. Each episode tells a different tale of Thorne, an infamous mercenary known as The Crow.

Story


Thorne finds himself in chains for a murder he did not commit, which is unusual. The explanation to the facts that led him to his arrest unveils the sinister truth of Hollow Lake, a nearby village crippled by corruption.
Play as an infamous mercenary known as "The Crow" in a decayed and corrupt world, find your way out of chains and get paid for your contract.

Features


- Explore a corrupt and dark world, meet ambiguous and interesting characters, judge them as friends or foes.
- Fight against magical creatures, wild beasts, cursed monsters, witches, sorcerers and professional hunters who are after the prize for your head.
- Make your character progression as you wish. Choose from a vast variety of accessories that best serve you in combat.
- Be "The Crow".

Thorne is an interesting idea for an RPG Maker title. It's set in a dark fantasy setting as opposed to your standard anime looking world. In fact, the world feels more like something ripped from a George R.R. Martin book! So, the setting is incredible! Unfortunately, that's where my enjoyment ended!

The English version of the game is full of spelling errors! Nearly every paragraph has a few misspelled words. In fact, sometimes it is difficult to understand what exactly the characters are saying. This to me, is a game breaker in an RPG! If a game rips you out of your immersion, so you can try to decode what the characters are saying, then it's NOT a good RPG. A role playing game, should allow you to lose yourself into the fantasy world. This however does not.

The controls seem awkward, and even moreso when using a gamepad! The second battle in the game, pits you against 4 enemies. The enemies are easy enough to defeat, but they are on you quickly. They can and do push you all the way to the back entrance of the map. Forcing you to fight them while locked in the corner since the only way out of the entrance passage is up....and up is where they are standing.

Overall, the game had potential, and could have been good. It just infuriated me more than entertained me.

If you enjoy RPGs -especially those made with RPG Maker- but want something a bit grittier, then Thorne - Death Merchants may be the title to satisfy your cravings. It tells the story of the aforementioned Thorne, also known as the Crow, a mercenary that leaves a trail of dead bodies behind him. Travel through a corrupt world as you meet new people and collect bounties. A fair warning, this game contains quite a bit of vulgar language so some may find it offensive.

Thorne has been taken prisoner for a murder he did not commit and most of the game is spent in a flashback as he explains his story to the captain of the guard. The story, and therefore the game, is rather short. Most of it takes place in one village where the murder youâve been accused of takes place.When Thorne first arrived he only wanted to drink at the tavern but soon found himself knee deep in the villageâs problems as he accepts mercenary jobs from the people. The story that unfolds is rather interesting and becomes more complicated than Thorne anticipated. To go further would spoil things but do expect witches, werewolves, corrupt leaders, beautiful women and evil specters.

The controls of the game pretty simple, you can use your mouse and keyboard to move and attack enemies or you can use a game controller. I found it easier to use a controller but certain things didnât quite work right. The game gives you no indication of which buttons match which actions, unlike if you use a mouse and keyboard. I never figured out how to use the rage attack with the controller. If the cursor of your mouse is hovering over something it will keep that thing highlighted no matter what you try to select with the controller. That being said, I still appreciated being able to use a controller as I prefer them for RPGs. The user interface isnât very fancy, a common occurrence in many RPG Maker titles, but it works.

Thorne has a basic sword attack, a ranged attack, and a desperation rage move he can use when his health is low. He can also heal himself. Unlike with many games of the genre, battles are not turn based, but real time. Enemies walk around the screen and will engage you if they see you. This will either turn some people off or please some people, either way it is nice to see something different even if it wasnât my cup of tea. You have the ability to change your gear to fit your play style, however there arenât many options due to the length of the game and you can get by pretty ok with just using the optimized gear choices. I also found money in the game to be a bit useless since you find plenty of items during your quest, most of which I rarely used, but if you find yourself in a pinch then stock up. You have no other party members which is a shame but makes sense in the story, however you can acquire a wolf companion to aid you in finding treasure. I never used this feature at all but it is needed to acquire certain achievements.

The artwork in the game is dark and gloomy, fitting the tone of the story. Everything is done in a retro pixel style with well drawn portraits for important characters. There are no cutscenes and the only piece of full artwork is the main title screen which shows Thorne in chains. I did have a bit of an issue with lighting in the game when night fell. I know itâs supposed to be dark at night time but it make it hard for me to see what I was doing and where I was going. The music is in the game is fitting, nothing too amazing but also nothing too annoying either. Everything is in a good balance and nothing seems out of place.

As mentioned earlier, this game is very short and can be completed in two or three hours, maybe four if youâre on the hunt for all of the achievements. My biggest issue with the game was lack of directions. You donât get a map until near the end of the game and unless you look in every nook and cranny and talk to everyone you possibly can then youâll miss side quests, and even then some things are time sensitive, which was not indicated in-game. The game has Steam achievements and trading cards and the developers seem to working on new updates and listening to the community. It would see that more episodes of the story are coming in the future which will hopefully flesh the story out more, as of now the price isnât too bad for the length of the game and itâs a nice change from a lot of the RPG Maker titles currently on the market.

Albeit short, this game was fun. The plot is intriguing and the characters are believable. The dialogue is both mature and entertaining. I could definitely see a sequel to expand on the current plot.

The game is a little flawed though:

1) The grammar and spelling could use some polishing.

2) Manipulating the wolf is a rather wonky process, as he tends to get stuck behind obstacles and keep a rather long distance from you; I guess he likes his personal space? It would also be nice if you could point to where you specifically want the wolf to search within a reasonable distance of the main character.

3) You really don't have a need for much of the game's currency (Rowens), as you can often find things to survive by just roaming and defeating enemies, or having the wolf search for things. Might want to improve on that for the sequel.

Some optional interaction with the ladies who take payment for a good time (can't write the W word here lol) could be fun too but that feature would likely offend many players.

On a side note, the rhythm of the Hornhold theme makes me think of "Exes and Ohs" by Elle.

My favorite genre of game, the rpg, I remember fondly my first rpg, the amazing Final Fantasy 7, Cloud and his merry band of terrorist (yes they were terrorist, but back when the game first released terrorist meant something totally different than it does today) I still recall being amazed, the graphics, the sounds, the mini games, I couldn't play the game enough, I put well over 100 hours into FF7, and that was my first save.. other amazing games the suikoden series, who could forget Breath of Fire 3, even Shadow Madness (called one of the first American RPG's by Game informer.... whatever that meant) So what do Final Fantasy 7 and these other amazing games have to do with Throne - Death Merchants.. not a single thing, except they are both members of my favorite genre and they are all amazing games..

Well then, shall we move on with my review of Thorne - Death Merchants? Aside from the main character being named Crow, and the name of the fort, nothing else was related to Game of Thrones, so I removed this section

The Story

The story starts you off as Crow, a man shackled in a keeps dungeon. He's bloody and retelling the tale of a murder!!! Did Crow commit the murder, was he framed? Did the butcher, the baker or the candlestick maker frame him??? That is what we'll get to see first hand as you relive the story that Crow tells to his jailer. I'll expand upon the story as I move through the game...

The Gameplay

The game play is different from many of the other rpgmaker games out there.. it's made using real-time combat as opposed to turn based combat. I certainly don't mind the real-time combat, It felt out of place for a game using this type of engine, although I did quickly adapt to it, and quickly grew accustomed to it. After nearly an hour of game play I have to say real-time combat had me more immersed in the game, it felt more interactive, rather than just snoozing through battles pressing the attack button, I was more apt to pay attention during combat, and the combat had an air of difficulty other games made with the same engine don't have.

All the normal rpg options are there, items, equipment, quest, status, the game had an added selection called encyclopedia, this section gives you a brief description of the enemies and NPC's' you're met throughout the game. (handy addition and helps with immersion). Another difference between Thorne and other games, is armor, you don't equip armor in the game, instead you equip accessories, which increase your stats, you can equip 4 different accessories. Another really useful option is the auto loot, no more toiling away checking every barrel/crate/pot/bookshelf. The items are automatically looted by using sprint, can make finding all the hidden items fairly simplistic.

A few tips to keep in mind... if the game starts in a tiny window, use alt + enter to bring the game into full screen mode.

Some useful default button's if you're a controller user (xbox 360 wired) You can remap the buttons but these are the default selections
Y is the attack button,
A is the run button
B brings up your main menu
X is used for interacting with people, and options in your menu.
LB is used for range attacks
RB is used for switching which ranged attack to use
Backspace/select is used to heal

Pro's
Fun and interactive combat,
Start to a great story
contoller support (yeah i'm that guy, who loves playing his rpg's with a controller)
Auto loot ++++
Music is pleasant
The game is fairly difficult, and requires more than just looking away and choosing the attack option

Con's
Lots of grammar and spelling mistakes (I suck at teh (intentional) spelling and grammer, but I'm not selling a product)

Unresponsive controls are the bane of this game existence. Action rpg's are fine, but the combat needs to be adjusted to allow far more fluid attacks, and magic.. no reason exist why I should be full MP, press the button and have no magic come, no reason exist why I should press the sword attack button 5 or 6 times before my character eventually attacks..


The Verdict
So the meat and potatoes of any review is the verdict, was the game worth playing, and if it was worth playing would I recommend it for other gamers... The short answer is absolutely....

The long answer is.... ABSOLUTELY!!!! if you aren't into THESE types of rpg's, then odds are the charm and quaintness will be totally lost on you. If you have a natural bias against RPG maker or old school games you'll most likely not enough this game at all... (but hey it's cheap and has cards so maybe even you can find a use for it.) For those who appreciate a well told story (albeit with some spelling and grammar issues) you'll do well to pick this up. It certainly won't have 50 hours of gameplay, but for 3 dollars what do you expect. You'll play it, you'll love it, and you'll be wanting the developer to finish up the story (This seems to be an episodic game where you'll get a part of the story now, and then after development is finished have the option of buying??? (not sure) additional episodes to finish out the story)

The game took me a little over 2 hours to complete, the developer is planning on making this an 8 part game, thus far the game has a pretty good foundation on which to build.

I loved to play RPG Maker games, especially those that has good storylines and that have some unique features that is different from the run-of-the-mill type games.

Well, I had a wonderful time with this game, Thorne.
The game mechanics is very unique, as we do not have he regular leveling, or armors, etc.
What it has is a single weapons, and a number of different accessories slots.
So, it is quite refreshing.
The fighting itself is also different as well.
Instead of turn-based, 1-action-per-turn combat, you actually move your character around to try to avoid being hit, and press the attack button (direct combat or range combat) to try to hit the enemy. So the combat mechanics is slightly different.

Another really interesting thing is the 22 Steam achievements. Although you can finished the main quest in 2-3 hours, I actually spend 10 hours, playing the game 3 times, and trying to figure out all the different achievements. This is because the activation of certain events are not too obvious and trying to figure out how to trigger them is part of the fun of the game. There are also different type of exploration puzzles, hidden items, partial keys, etc, that make the game interesting as well. I loved this game.

The only shortcoming that I can think of for this game is : IT IS TOO SHORT :).
However, as I understand it, this is Part 1 of an 8 part episodic game and that we would be able to carry forward our save game to the next episode.
This is really excellent. Furthermore the price of slightly more than $1 for such a game is defintely worth it!!
I love this game, and would look forward to buying and playing all 8 episodes.
Keep up the good work, developers... and I definitely recommend this game.
