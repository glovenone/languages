About This Game

Color Guardians is an exciting fast action side scrolling game that combines quick thinking with frantic gameplay into a hard to master challenge! Switching colors and collecting items, all while avoiding obstacles will surely test your reflexes!

The colorful world of Terra Color has been turned to a dark and sad place by the dark powers of the evil color thief Krogma. The only ones capable of restoring the world to its original state are a colorful bunch of characters made of paint called the Color Guardians.

Help the Color Guardians bring color back to their world. Guide Rod, Lia and Grock through 70+ levels of fast-paced action and defeat the evil color thief Krogma. The core mechanics are easy to understand, but build up quickly -- and start challenging the limits of your skill.

Game scope
Color Guardians includes:
  • Loads of content! Play more than 70 levels of fast paced and cleverly designed hard to master challenges.
  • 5 Different worlds to explore! Each world introduces new challenges and exciting new mechanics.
  • Play as Rod, Lia and Grock and discover the world of Terracolour!
  • Hidden unlockables including music, concept art, renders and 3D models.
  • Compare your score with your friends through leaderboards.
  • Unlock a crazy bunch of achievements!

    Game features
  • Clever, challenging design that will keep you busy for hours.
  • Each level is uniquely designed to keep up the challenge!
  • Fun and fast color-matching gameplay while avoiding obstacles and collecting items. This game will surely test your skills!
  • Vivid, eye-catching visual design across several different worlds.
  • Leaderboards to compete with friends, and plenty of unlockables to fight for!
  • 60 challenges to unlock extras: concept art, music, and more.
  • Localized on 7 different languages: English, spanish, french, italian, german, portuguese and russian.

Posted: 13 May, 2015
Plays like Bit.Trip Runner, Rayman Legends, and Guitar Hero had a baby on top of a Jackson Pollock painting; and I promise that's a good thing.

Unlike most endless runners you actually have a lot of control as to where your character goes. Depending on the exact path you take you'll end up doubling back for more points or missing bonus orbs (points), it takes an almost 'strategy' to correctly play this game. In that regards, Color Guardians elevates itself from the rest of the endless runner pack.

Helping it ascend further is it's multi-lanes mechanic. Instead of playing exactly like a rythym game where you HAVE to hit Button 'X' at this specific time, the game lets you sidestep certain obstacles if you are out of sync or focused on a different path. It even rewards finding your own way through a level, by letting you double back.

Color Guardians has the precision of Guitar Hero, the goofy aesthetics of Rayman, and the gameplay of Bit.Trip Runner, but goes a step further than all of them by granting the player a level of control I've not seen in an Endless Runner. If you are looking for a break from narrative heavy and AAA titles and just want to hit a flurry of buttons for a hectic, fun time, get this game.

Posted: 12 May, 2015
edit: As far as what the developer has said the glitches I stated in this review should now be fixed and won't be a problem anymore.

Color guardians is a great addition to the classic runner genre with a new mechanic that makes the game have very fast paced and hectic gameplay. However it does have inherrant problems that need to be fixed.

The game is a classic runner game that is heavily inspired by experts in the genre such as temple run and subway surfer. Although that has a very interesting mechanic that makes it stand out. Your character you play as can change colours between yello, blue and red. These colours are used to progress through certain parts of the level and to collect different coloured orbs. The mechanic creates fast paced gameplay requiring you to very quickly change colours in order to successfully progress through the level.

You also have other obstacles to avoid. These can include walls, moving enemies or spikes. There is a very wide variety in these obstacles. The game never leaves you with repetitive gameplay. All the levels are very different and keep you amused throughout. They are very challenging and aren't easy to complete first time.

There is plenty in the game for collectors and achievement hunters. The levels contain optional orbs to pick up. These require you to be the colour of them. Depending on how many orbs you pick up, you get a different ranking for the completion of the level. This can keep people entertained trying to 100% every level for a long time.

The game has a very stand out graphics style. The cartoony look is represented throughout the game and is consistent. What I found really unique is the way in which the world reacts to how you are doing in the game. If you aren't doing very well then the levels turn very grey because you aren't colllecting many colours. As you collect more colours the world becomes more and more colourful. This is a beatiful addition that makes the gameplay more fun for people.

The game has multiple control methods. You can use classic mouse and keyboard. However I wouldn't recommend it. A controller is key to being able to play this game properly. The colours of your character is optimized to the colours of the buttons on your controller. Therefore it is very hard to remember the keys on the keyboard wheras it is very easy with a controller.

The game features 2 controller setups for this. They have one for a playstation controller, and another for an xbox controller. This convenience does make it much easier for people with different controllers. However this was very annoying. I was using an xbox controller to play the game. I found that every time I rebooted up the game it defaulted to playstation controls. So I had to go in the options menu each time to reset this back again. This was an uneccessary hassle and was an annoyance.

Color guardians options menu is also very lacking in the needed options. There are no graphics options besides the default fastest, good and fantastic. For a 3D game this is unnacceptable. Other seperate options should be present. You then only have 3 different resolution options to choose from. This is unnaceptable range of resolutions. Finally there is a full screen option. There are also seperate audio sliders for different settings. However this is all that is in the options menu. The menu is also extremely difficult to navigate with both mouse and controller and can be glitchy.

While I was writing this review I had a very big issue with the navigating of the menu. For some reason when I clicked the back button to go out of the options menu the game automatically by itself went onto the new game button. This means it completely deleted all of the progress I have made and I now have to start from the beginning again. They definitely need to fix this issue to make sure this doesn't happen to anyone else.

I would cautiously recommend this game to people. There are a lot of problems that could lead to bad gameplay for people. I would recommend waiting for a fix before buying it. I also think the asking price is a little high for the content at the moment. You should wait a bit and wait for a sale before buying in my opinion.

  • Maybe consider it on a sale.
  • An average game.
  • A good game that should definitely be considered.
  • A must play!
