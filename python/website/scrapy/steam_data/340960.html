Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“I really want player feedback. I'd love to hear from everyone even if they don't like something I've added or changed. Getting your feedback will help us improve the game and fix any remaining bugs :).”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Approximately 8 -10 months, with a planned full release in first half of 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The foundation of the game is in place already at Early Access launch. There are many currently planned features and improvements that are being worked on that we will be adding over the course of Early Access.

We're working to add re-adjusted charts, replays and friend replays, updated noteskins, a much-improved UI, and in-game instructions for new players on the main menu. Brand new OS X and Linux builds as well as DirectX 9 capabilities (over the old DX8.1) are currently being worked on.

Additional features being explored that may or may not be in game: Challenge, Story, and Fitness modes as well as Steam Workshop integration for custom content, and a level editor.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Everything described on the Key Features list is available at Early Access launch in the Pulsen version 1.0 build â bug-free (as far we know), just not all 100% polished. *Please note that your progress may be reset when new builds are released throughout the Early Access period; although, the plan is to keep everything going-forward.

There is also a Beta branch available through Steam where you can playtest new features, modes of play, and chart additions. As beta builds become more stable, they will be moved into the main branch for all users.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We currently have no plans to change the price for release just yet. We might change this idea depending on the finalization of features and other game modes.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“You will be able to test the latest features and gameplay, providing valuable ideas, feedback, and issues to make Pulsen the best it can be. Use the Steam discussion boards, or contact me directly via my Twitter or Contact Me.”

Pulsen Adds 13 New Free Songs, Ludicrous Timing, and Improvements

Pulsen Build 1.2 is here bringing with it a ton of new content, a new note timing window, as well as various polish and fixes. Pulsen is a music and rhythm game currently in Steam Early Access with core gameplay involving hitting four directional arrows to the beat of the music as they pass by the screen (similar to games such as Dance Dance Revolution or In The Groove).

13 new songs have been added to Pulsen, for a total of 80 songs in game. Songs include a remix of the viral Nyan Cat, music by the popular artist Renard, a track by dance game music veteran Oscillator X, and more. In addition to the new songs, some previous songs have seen updates. Several bonus charts have been added, and include some of the most difficult charts in Pulsen. There have also been revisions to over a dozen existing charts with updated effects, timings, rhythms, and fixing minor issues.

A new note timing called âLudicrousâ has been added to Pulsen, inspired by the Ridiculous timing seen in DDR and StepMania. The timing window for Ludicrous is only 11.25 milliseconds, making it the narrowest timing window in Pulsen and twice as difficult to get as the Fantastic timing. Ludicrous timing has been implemented to provide all players with an extra challenge they can optionally strive for, and to make Pulsen more competitive for players that want it.

Pulsen has also seen many minor improvements. These include revised option menus, noteskin previews during noteskin selection, some updated images and more consistent colors, and of course bug fixes.

Pulsen will feature over 80 songs, with at least 5 difficulty levels for every song. It can be played on a dance pad, computer keyboard, or controller as solo or local two player. Steam integration allows comparing scores in game, and will include achievements and trading cards.

About This Game

Pulsen is a music and rhythm game featuring 80 songs. Core gameplay is easy to learn but hard to master, and involves hitting four directional arrows to the beat of the music as they pass by the screen. Every song has five or more difficulties and its own set of challenging arrow patterns and play style. Score is determined by how well arrows are hit in time with the music. Song experiences can be enhanced and customized with numerous song modifiers, settings, and arrow noteskins. Compete against a friend locally and compare scores with friends in-game on Steam.

Key Features

  • EXPANSIVE SONG LIST: 80 songs from independent artists!
  • DESIGNED FOR ANY SKILL LEVEL: 5-6 difficulties for every song totaling over 420 charts!
  • MULTIPLE WAYS TO PLAY: Play with a dance pad, controller, keyboard, or Rock Band drums.
  • CUSTOMIZE YOUR SONG EXPERIENCE: Tons of song modifiers, settings, and arrow noteskins.
  • TEST YOUR LIMITS: Unique song Special Effects keep you on your toes.
  • STEAM INTEGRATION: Compare scores in-game with friends, unlock achievements, and collect trading cards.
  • BRING A BUDDY: Play solo or with a friend in local two player.
  • AND MORE!: Remappable controls, dynamic resolutions and aspect ratio, and mouse functionality.

In short: This game is Stepmania with Steam integration.

I would advice most people wanting to try this game to play Stepmania first since it's the same thing, free, and has way more songs to play.

Still, I'm torn to whether I should give this game a "Yes" or "No". Given this game is in Early Access I want to see what the developers can come up with to make this game different. Sure, it has all this crazy stuff going on with the scroll velocity and notes on most (if not all) of the songs but I myself am not a fan of that at all. But I could see how this game is easier to get into for casual players than something like Stepmania. From what I've seen it doesn't seem to have any kind of accuracy other than hit/miss. For most VSRG (vertical scrolling rhythm game) players, this is just a straight "NO" but for casual players it could be the opposite.

To conclude: If you're new to VSRG's or want to get into them more, maybe you could give the game a chance, but you're coming from other VSRGs like Stepmania, osu!mania, o2jam, lr2 or any other, stick to those, you won't find anything new here except for some easy charts with lots of SV changes.

Voting yes simply because I think this game has potential, but if it stays like this for the release, it will probably change to a no. Custom charts and an editor will probably be essential for the survival of this game.

Also, to any developer that may be reading this: Please allow us to have scroll speeds above "600 BPM". It's a way low maximum. I myself would probably play with 900-1000.

From the viewpoint of someone who is hardcore into Rhythm gaming I just can't recommend Pulsen for its cost.

If you're looking for a packaged standalone experience that requires no additional setup and is super casual friendly, then it might be the game for you. But for an advanced user, who is already accustomed to modifying a game like StepMania to fit my needs, I find the experience lacking. There is the promise of a song editor coming eventually and steam workshop integration, but nothing you can't already get in SM for free. No custom themes, noteskins appear to be limited to paid DLC, limited mod-ability.

The chart gimmicks are cute, but I don't really feel the same depth in them as other packs. Look up WinDEU or charts made for D.O.W.N.S. for some examples. And as far as overall difficulty, even the most challenging of charts was relatively tame compared to the majority of what I commonly play on SM/FFR.

If you're a pad player, you can expect a similar experience to picking up an old console copy of DDR or ITG in terms of content and customization options. And if you're OK with that, and you rather not deal with setting up everything yourself (if you haven't already) then you should probably get this game.

Nothing but respect to the developers, they are trying, they do listen to feedback. Its simply not something I'm looking for as a veteran rhythm gamer.

TL;DR:
>Wide variety of difficulty and styles
>Varied songs, some may even be familiar
>Based on Stepmania 5, polished to a shine

Details:
It has calm charts for easing in new players, up to mind-bending charts to challenge the hardcore. It supports keyboard, dance pad, and controllers, with separated scores as well. Early Access Review
Posted: 2 August, 2015
I had found this game about a year ago, before it had come to Steam. There were more songs available at the time, but the game was essentially the same (I hear that there's going to be a bunch more songs added later anyway). If you like DDR, ITG, FFR, Sequence, or really any rhythm game, get Pulsen. It doesn't really break the mold of rhythm games (four directional arrows move towards markers that you need to hit pertaining to the song's beat), but it definitely does it well. The game runs really smoothly on my crappy laptop, and I haven't had any issues with calibration.

With the limited song library, you may end up exhausting new content fairly quickly, but if you're a perfectionist or a high-score shooter, you can always try to beat your previous scores on higher difficulties. The songs themselves are generally catchy and entertaining, with the occassional stepfile that really throws you for a loop with interesting and challenging mods. There's a two-player option as well, if you feel like playing with a buddy.

As this game is still technically in early access, I expect the developers will be adding many more songs and extra content in upcoming updates, so I expect to be playing this game off and on for quite some time.

TL;DR:
Pros:
- Runs well on most hardware
- Stepfile mods galore
- Well-done DDR clone

Cons:
- Limited song choice
- Only one game mode
