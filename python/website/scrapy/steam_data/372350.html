Tumbleweed Express: v1.1.2cb5430 Patch Notes

NEW FEATURES:
+Train Painting: Players can now customize the color scheme of their train via the Paint Job panel in the Warehouse Depot
+Hold Fire Mode: Automatic Turrets can now be instructed to hold their fire via the Battle Menu target priority interface. This will help players complete contracts that their turrets may have been interfering with

BUG FIXES:
+Fixed looter enemies occasionally skipping over their looting animations
+Fixed case where Drillcar Jim would pass through the player’s train during the Cactus Plains town fight
+Fixed Wagons occasionally not registering TNT hits for contract credit
+Fixing map alignment offset in levels The Foothills (1.2) and West Canyon City (3.2)

MISCELLANEOUS :
+Normalized distance culling (disappearing for performance) of Cacti for all graphics quality settings
+Normalized shadow draw distance for all graphics quality settings
+Added new Paint Job tip to the Warehouse loading screen
+Minor performance overhead tweaks
+Drillcar intro-cutscene now specifically refers to the enemy as a ‘Drillcar Looter’ to reduce contract confusion
+Improved slider functionality in all menus
+Improved Fireball trajectory to always pass through the player’s crosshair.
+Some environmental polishing in The Heights (0.2)
+Tweaking credits read outs and background art
+Updated default graphics quality on launcher to "Good" (from "Fastest")

About This Game

The year is 1885, the height of the Age of Steam in the United States of America. But something is not quite right in the land of destiny and the free market: Dirigibaus, a ruthless entrepreneur, has monopolized trade in the West with a fleet of airships! Dirigibles and zeppelins dot the skies as agents of an oppressive business regime.

Someone still fights for the little people, though. The venerable Tumbleweed Express Steam Engine, long-thought to be obsolete and scrapped, is set to saddle up and ride again. The Tumbleweed is about to spearhead a logistics campaign meant to compete with the Dirigibaus, bringing support to those who have been downtrodden and forgotten. Dirigibaus doesn't take kindly to competition, however, so the Tumbleweed has armed herself for war...

Do you have what it takes to beat the Baus?

Features:
- Race against the clock to collect stars in 12 levels.
- Engage in combat directly by manning the caboose and controlling the action.
- Upgrade your train defense systems and crew in your company Warehouse.
- Complete shipping contracts that increase level difficulty.
- Do battle with a variety of enemies and bosses.
- Pick up mail to learn more about the Tumbleweed Express.

Tumbleweed Express is a rail shooter with tower defense elements. A rail shooter where you protect a train on rails....yup. Anyways, you control a 360 degree rotating turret on top of a caboose in an old west steampunk world where blimps and dirigibles have overtaken railroads as the main method of cargo transportation. You inherit the tumbleweed express from a dead family member and as the new owner it's your job to ride the rails to glory.

The graphics fit the theme of the game prefectly and so does the music. It also sounds like the narrator is using a fake southern accent and that's a bit off-putting. Game progression is stage based with the option to replay each stage as many times as you want. I'm glad you're able to do this because the difficulty curve is sharp and soon enough you'll become overwhelmed by all the crap coming at ya from all around ya. Upgrades include cool stuff like being able to buy more cars for your train so you can add automated turrets. You can also hire engineers that'll repair your traincars and different types of gunners that will also shoot stuff on their own. There's also turret upgrades and different types of turrets you can purchase such as cannons. I'm not too far in it but it looks like there's a lot to this game. Each stage has multiple side-objectives you can enable to get a bigger reward if you complete them however it also increases the difficulty of the stage. And since you can replay each stage you can experiment with those to see if the risk is worth the reward. There are secrets hidden in each stage too that will uncover more of the story but they aren't necessary.

All in all it's a fun game and a fresh rail shooter that does a good job at being different. In fact probably one of the only rail shooters I'd recommend because I haven't played that many. The developer is really nice, productive and vocal with the commuity so that's a plus in my book. It's cheap so pick it up if all that sounds good to you.

This game is probably the best game I've bought for a long time. It's completely worth the $5 and is tons of fun. The gameplay is pretty great - it's essentially a tower defense game but with your towers on a train, and you operate one of the guns. It's fairly fast-paced, but slow enough that a person like me without CS level reflexes can learn. The levels are pretty replayable, with slight variations on your goals. Upgrading the train is cool, and outfitting with different weapons to reign destruction down upon bandits and airships is very satisfying.

Overall, few issues. The only bugs are visual problems, like the tracks having little gaps when you look back on them, or mountains in the distance appearing or dissapearing as you travel. Another problem is that the train isn't EXACTLY on the tracks at all times. But these issues don't really affect gameplay, and it's pretty good as-is.

Awesome game, great visual style and artwork, good worldbuilding, one or two minor problems. Definitely reccomend.

Really enjoying this game, but on release there were a few niggly (small, easily fixed) bugs which are now pretty much sorted. I'd consider this to be in the "travel defence" genre, a bit like Space Run.

Pro:

-Nice borderlands style graphics.
-Good music which really ties in well with the atmosphere of the game.
-Literally, a rail shooter. But in a good way.
-Each level can be re-played for more cash and better upgrades.
-Without giving anything away, some of the upgrades feel really great.
-Difficulty seems spot on, with a mild curve, but the game is understandable and easy to learn quickly.
-Developers are friendly and actively encourage feedback.

Con:
-Voice acting is a little shaky and unbelievable. Should have got some real Southerners! The main voice (in the intro and trailer) is really quiet due to the "droney" Southern style accent and is hard to hear.
-Can get stuck on levels if you do too well - killed a boss within seconds and it made the level uncompletable. (Devs looking into this)
-Although the graphics are nice, some things don't appear until you've already gone past them. This means you're costantly looking backwards, which seems odd. It's not that big of a deal, but some missions ask you to destroy scenery which may or may not spawn by the time you've gone past it. (Found out that this is due to the graphics settings - just turn it up if you can.)
-Other niggly bugs, like the launcher has an "empty" button which doesn't mean anything. (Fixed)

Hi, I just played this game and I can't believe it.

People are noting that it's "ONLY 7-10 Hours of Gameplay".

Only? The game is $5. The cheapest McDonalds meal you can buy is $5.69. Have you ever eaten a Mc Donald's meal for 7-10 hours? More like 10 minutes. Have you ever played a pinball game for 10 hours with $5? Have you ever done ANYTHING for 10 hours of entertainment for $5? They are basically charging you .50/hr to play this game.

The art style is very, VERY good. You are treated to authentic train maps and rotating items in the game during loading screens (like in Skyrim). The voice acting is really cute and amusing, and MAN the music. The music is absolutely phenomenal, it's BETTER than the music in most professionally released games.

The only cons I would say is that there isn't more of it, more levels, multiplayer, VR, etc, but if your biggest Con is that there isn't more of the game (and what I said about $5) then how can you complain. These guys are indie developers and built this passion project from the ground up. Mad respect.

This game has extraordinarily fun and engaging gameplay, an original concept (MOVING tower defense) and an intuitive interface so that you don't need instructions on how to do anything in the game, it all is designed to be played without a complicated tutorial or manual.

LET THE TUMBLEWEED ROLL OUT!
