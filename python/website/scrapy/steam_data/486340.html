About This Game

Your kin have long been the victims of brutal experiments performed by the lich lords of the land. One fateful night a witch assistant took pity and empowered you with magical strength to free yourself. Learning of the assistants treachery she was brutally slain and now it's time for Gnarltoof's Revenge. Battle through randomly decorated castles on your quest to defeat the 10 lich lords of the realm and free your people from a life of agony!

Some Features Include:

Randomly decorated castles
Randomized chest pickups
Variety of awesome power ups
Fast paced action
Obstacles to avoid
Monsters to slay
10 castles with lich lords to defeat
And More!

Posted: 17 June
The best thing that I can say about Gnarltoof's Revenge is that it is better than what Digital Homicide have come out with as of late.

You play as Gnarltoof (which may or may not be this asset https://www.assetstore.unity3d.com/en/#!/content/33310 ) is killing all of the lichs in the area ( https://www.assetstore.unity3d.com/en/#!/content/30711 ) and jumping over saw blades. Each time you kill a lich, you stack up on revenge points and you can use said points to pull off either more powerful attacks or to heal Gnarltoof. Now the gameplay for maybe the first 5 minutes is actually pretty fun but it quickly becomes broing really fast.

Like Kragmor, the game looks decent, because the assets are done by the same developer (the wererat and the lichs) While the wererat, sorry, Gnarltoof and the lichs look decent enough being bright and colourful, the backgrounds look bland and DH decide to just copy and paste the backgrounds they were given for the whole game with only some slight lighting changes. I can't exactly applaud them for doing this because it still seems lazy and get this, they actually try to use this as a selling point for the game "Battle through RANDOMLY DECORATED CASTLES on your quest to defeat the 10 lich lords of the realm and free your people from a life of agony!" actually speaking of which, is there a whole species of wererats that Gnarltoof needs to free. I'm not sure about you but if it were up to me, letting the wererats be free or letting some lichs keep them, I would go for the latter, who knows what those wererats will do.

This game is also surprisingly full of bugs and glitches. I flew up in the air once I ran backwards and forwards, jumping up and down for too many times. I clipped through some of the backgrounds as they seemed to have put the same backgrounds ontop of each other twice on the boss stage. On top of that, the hitboxes fr both Gnarltoof and the lichs are awful, The enemies can hit Gnarltoof from a mile away and Gnarltoof has to literraly be up close to these guys to hit them. I also experienced 4 crashes in just 12 minutes of gameplay.

The only reason that this game is slightly better than some previous Digital Homicide games is because I did have fun for the first 5 minutes of the game but it quickly got boring quite fast, and when that is something that I'm considering a point to be that this is one of their better games, you know they've been doing something wrong their whole career.

Pros
+ Fun for maybe 5 mins
+ It isn't a krog wars clone

Cons
- Using the same assets from the same developer for 2 of your games now
- Boring backgrounds
- Repetitive
- Clipping
- Crashing
- Glitches

Final verdict: 2/10
