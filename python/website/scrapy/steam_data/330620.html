It's A Patch! (Version 1.9.9)

Happy Memorial Day!

Been playing It's A Wipe! again recently and found a couple minor things that could use fixing :)

* Get Back In Line! *

- Added a small change that after a fight ends, melee DPS will move back to the front row and Healers will move back to the front row.

It's A Wipe! 1.9.8 1/2 - Mini-Patch!

Hello All,

A few bug fixes have been applied, so I thought that I would roll it up into a nice set of patch notes for anyone that doesn't read the forums :) Also, one updated button graphic! Here is what is new in 1.9.8 1/2:

----------------

* Unintended Iron Man *
- Fixed (hopefully!) an issue where the new save file index, added in the last patch, was not building correctly for new installs causing saved games to only appear in the session that they were saved in. If you have this issue, in the %appdata%\Roaming\Independent\IAWGame\game\data folder there should be a saves.txt that you can delete. The next time you load it will rebuild this index file from the existing saved games.

- Fixed an issue with Too Much Free Time achievement not triggering. If you have completed this achievement it will trigger the next time that game is played and the day progresses.

----------------

Please let us know if you do find anything else that you feel may be a bug so we can look into it! We hope that you enjoy the game!

About This Game

A game of fantasy guild management! In It's A Wipe! you assume the role of a guild leader within a fantasy game world. You must recruit other players, lead them to victory against increasingly difficult odds, and make sure that your group remains happy enough to stick with you!

The game is done in a style reminiscent of old school role playing games. While the 'game' within It's A Wipe! is supposed to represent an on-line game world, this is really a single player game. The game play is a mix of strategy, in that you need to figure out an effective plan to beat the encounters, and management, in that you need to balance the various needs and desires of your team of players.

While the game does revolve around trying to win battles to progress, it's not your typical RPG combat. As the leader, you do not directly control your group. You set-up an initial plan, but your group members may decide to go off and do their own thing! You can shout a command at them each turn to try to keep them in line, and do your best to keep them happy with items and by paying attention to their needs.

Look at my time played. I have enjoyed every hour I've spent on this game.

The developer is great about listening to the community, fixing bugs as they're found in a timely manner.

I STRONGLY RECOMMEND not reading a guide before playing, since a major part of the gameplay is figuring out each boss' mechanics on your own.

Essentially, IAW is a puzzle with multiple solutions. Which raiders you recruit, your group's class spread, balancing loot distribution, everything plays a part in your success, and there isn't a single 'right' answer. Do you take two rogues for higher damage, but knowing morale might suffer from loot disputes? Do you take two wizards, knowing they'll deal high damage, but have trouble swapping targets? Do you take a bard for the buffs or replace them with a different class?

I've played through the game with a lot of different spreads. Once with three warriors, once with only one, sometimes I take a bard, sometimes I don't. Once I took two druids, even. It's all about figuring out how the group will interact and doing what you can to keep things balanced.

Plus the humor is great. It's amusing even if you don't get the references, but is pretty great if you do.

So microwave a burrito, turn on voice chat and grab the rum: It's time to go raiding.

I saw this game on sale, and thought "What the heck, I'll go for it." I had an hour before I had to leave for work, so I thought I'd give it a go. Over an hour later, I casually looked at the clock and grabbed my coat and RAN out the door, racing in traffic to get to work on time before I was late. Barely made it with a few seconds to spare. I'm normally a guy who shows up early just because better safe than sorry.

So I would say this game is pretty spot on. Think of a group of raiders, in any game you could play. World of Warcraft, Ever Quest, Final Fantasy Online - just to name a few. Then, take that group, and think of them as real people (we all know that you picture some guy playing a chick character just to stare at pixel booty all day) - Not to say they aren't, but the perception of most people is to look at who they are, and assume everyone else is a robot or just some random nobody.

This game is full of comedy - Everything from "Killalot talks about his political views" to a person "Afking in the middle of combat to check his email" - And will remind you of what really happens. I love when I tell a tank to take over agro, and the tank (who is better geared and more able to do so) cannot pull agro off the first tank. I love when a mage just happens for wander into creature range and gets attacked wildly. I love when the healers forget to heal because they think a tank is good, and start to dps, letting the poor tank die because the fight required a focused heal at specific times. Just like in real MMOs.

This game is easily one of the more addictive games, and as my first day playing, it has already caused me to come close to being late to work AND caused me to rush home just to try to see how far I can make it after the fact.

It's A Wipe is worth the money for the nostalgia factor, but most of the game feels relatively spartan in features or gameplay. Generally, the game consists of managing a sitcom-version of a guild raiding party in an indirect fashion with breaks in between; additional characters or other amusing features that one think to find in a game about managing a guild are absent.

Further, the game is quickly over -- there seems to be only 7-8 raids -- and some of the mechanics really need some work, particularly infighting over loot. This can make the game frustrating and the game itself seems to have relatively little replayability; which is odd given that the overall concept of the game, if developed, could provide for a fascinating guild management game.

The problem with that is that the game not developed sufficiently; it is, as stated, feature light.

With that said, it's five dollars and I had trouble not liking it. For that price you get what you might expect. I would tepidly recommend the game and hope the developer fleshes out the features of it.

Pros:
-Interesting concept.
-Nostalgia.
-Humor is good.
-Good framework for a game; seems easily developed.

Cons:
-Relatively thin in gameplay depth; feature light.
-Sometimes frustrating.
-Sometimes boring in some fights; cannot manage one's own character.
-The UI is really very ugly.

Fun game for the price. Battles require thought after the early dungeons are out of the way, and keeping everyone happy about loot is a bit of a challenge. Your raid's strength grows noticeably over time, and some of the character actions can be amusingly nostalgic if you've ever raided in an MMO (like someone going link dead, or a mage deciding to tank). MMO experience can help you get a handle on strategy quickly, but I don't think it's a requirement to enjoy the game.

The character quirks don't impact the gameplay too much, so luck isn't a huge factor. Fights boil down to managing the raid strategy (like DPS, healing, and tanking priorities) and figuring out boss patterns. I'm impressed by how well the game simulates the raid management experience ("WHY DID YOU DRINK THAT POTION INSTEAD OF HEALING THE TANK?!?") without getting bogged down in micromanagement. Fights can drag on at times, but hey, they're raids. They do that.

It would be great if characters had more personality, or if raid groups could be bigger, or if character abilities were more varied. For the price, though, those aren't complaints - just wishes for a DLC/sequel someday.
