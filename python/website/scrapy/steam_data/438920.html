Patch Notes v1.0.1.9 - Card Changes and Quality of Life Adjustments

Patch Notes v1.0.1.9

  • General
    • Added a Win Condition popup when the game starts.
    • Added a Surrender popup message when a player surrenders mid-game.
    • Updated UI with Unit Stat display to show up to 4-digits.
    • Fixed Unit Training UI to commit changes when switching to another panel.
    • Allow Organize action on garrisons without a hero so you can disband those units.
    • Disable win by VP has been made the default scenario option for all game modes.
    • Fixed an issue that didn't allow for No Turn Limit on Skirmish mode.


  • Cards
    • We've removed the restrictions for some cards so you only need to have an awakened specific hero class (Warrior, Rogue, Mercenary, Wizard) to use said cards instead of only being able to play them on territories where those specific hero classes are currently on. Below is a list of affected cards.
    • Warrior
      • Rallying Cry, Flaming Missiles, Charge, and Trample can now be played globally.
    • Rogue
      • Run Away, Forced March, and Hidden Treasure can now be played globally.

    • Wizard
      • Raise the Dead can now be played globally.

  • Other Adjustments
    • Card Descriptions and Error Messages updated to better reflect what they do.
    • Added Faction Icons as Emblems.
    • Miscellaneous bug fixes and network optimizations.

About This Game

The Great Callasian War rages across the lands. Choose a faction and awaken the legendary heroes to lead your armies and destroy enemy forces! Build cities, conquer kingdoms, and form or break alliances on tabletop-style maps with bonus cards that can turn the tide of battle. Follow the story of the factions in single-player campaign missions, or test friendships in online multiplayer with an original simultaneous turn-based system that adds deep strategic elements in predicting other players’ moves. Fight your way to the top of the leaderboard, and dominate the fantasy world of Callasia!

Note: This is a free demo which includes the tutorial, starter campaigns and four heroes for each faction, and three maps for skirmish and multiplayer. The full version can be unlocked through a one-time purchase from within the game or via DLC.

Full Version Features:


  • Three Factions to choose from: Hundred Kingdoms, Faeborne, and Revenant
  • Over 25 Heroes with different classes, traits, and bonus cards
  • 30 Single-Player Campaign Levels worth almost 40 hours of gameplay
  • More than 35 tabletop-style Skirmish/Multiplayer maps playable up to 8 players
  • Pay once and play on all platforms: soon on iPad and Android tablets

NOTE: Key provided by Boomzap for this review.

TL:DR:
Recommend: YES. A good turn-based strategy game with a nice art style and fun mechanics. Suffers a bit from a small community. Still worth a purchase for single player.

Video Review/Look At
Click here to watch a relaxed look at one of the game's single player campaign missions.

PROS
  • Lovely Art Style
  • Good Amount of Depth
  • A Lot of Content
  • Variety in Single Player
  • Demo Provides Hours of Gameplay
  • Progression (Sorta)

CONS
  • No Combat Interaction
  • Small Community for Multiplayer
  • Tutorial Leaves Out Some Small Details

In-depth

Lovely Art Style
Really like the art style. Part of it has a brush-like quality, like the mountains and forests were Bob-Rossed onto the map. Colors are clear and it's really easy to differentiate between kingdoms and territories. The character and unit portraits are bright and have a lot of detail to them, unfortunately they're often shown too small to make out much detail.

Good Amount of Depth
Army units have simple attack and defense values but also unique bonuses. It's easy to apply them in the right situation, but tough to master them for perfect use all at all times. Characters apply bonuses to any army they command, adding another layer of depth. The Card system provides some interesting randomness (which cards you draw) and many can only be used with certain Characters, furthering the depth of understanding needed to master the game. Choosing which building to build on territories adds another layer. All of these things are easy to understand by themselves, and feel really good when combined.

A Lot of Content and Variety in Single Player
3 factions to play, each with 8 unique heroes and 6 army units. Each faction has 10 campaign missions to set up the story of the conflict. Skirmish offers 37 maps (that's a lot!) to play on, and are varied in their layout. Some of the skrimish maps do come from the campaign. Ranked and unranked multiplayer are available as well.

Demo Provides Hours of Gameplay
Game is listed as Free to Play with an in-game purchase to unlock the full game. Confirmed with the developers that the purchase goes through Steam, so Valve still gets their percentage. Demo offers 3 campaign missions plus some skirmish maps (though they have turn limits set). You get multiple hours of gameplay to determine if you want to purchase the game, which is great. Some have critisized that the in-game purchase is to get around the Steam refund policy, but that arguement isn't logical, as the demo gives you a lot more than 2 hours of gameplay to figure out if you like a game or not.

Progression(Sorta)
Some Characters and maps are locked for play in Skirmish and Multiplayer until they are played in the campaign. Could be seen as both a good thing and a bad thing, depending on the player. Now-a-days, many players need motivation (IE: progression) to keep playing a game, and LoC provides that.

No Combat Interaction
Combat is automatic in this game. The challenge comes from unit composition. Would prefer being able to control combat speed or choose targets for archers and melee units. As it is now, all you do is sit and wait and hope.

Small Community for Multiplayer
Player count has been quite low since release. Not necessarily a fault of the game, but if you're looking for an online, competative experience, LoC may fall short. There is an in-game chat system which can be used to coordinate a game, as well as a Discord channel and a Subreddit. A dedicated community of players should have no problem putting games together.

Tutorial Leaves Out Some Small Details
Not a huge gripe, but I missed one or two details that would be helpful. Recommend playing the first mission of each story campaign before diving too deep into one, as the first missions teach you some additional features (like requesting your AI allies attack or move to a specific territory). Or that attacking another territory means you attack first in combat, which seems to make a difference in determining the winner.

TL:DR:
Recommend: YES. A good turn-based strategy game with a nice art style and fun mechanics. Suffers a bit from a small community. Still worth a purchase for single player.
