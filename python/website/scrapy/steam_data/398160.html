Shapes of Gray is a top-down arcade-style game and is what would happen if Binding of Isaac and WarioWare had a baby. Youâre confined to a single room per level with the objective of defeating the surrounding enemies within a set time. The characters and environments are all different shades of gray (hence the title) and while the environment is the same each level with minor change for the boss levels, the characters are all uniquely designed in both looks and attacks.

There are 49 levels initially plus two more gameplay styles to unlock upon first completion. These both add more character types to encounter and while one is a âSecond Questâ style game the other brings a coin-collection mode for added difficulty.

I was given a key-code for the download of the Steam version for review but I had previously purchased the Wii U version upon its initial release. The Steam version fixes the frame rate issue that a couple levels (Level 3 in particular) faced and also adds in a trail effect for all characters and weapons. This comes in handy for catching bullet trajectories and is just a cool affect in general.

Each boss acts as a checkpoint which is very welcome but seeing as there are only two checkpoints then, I do wish there were one additional checkpoint after Boss 2 or a way to recover one heart at least. This is due to both the time mechanic in each level and the random enemy layout. The quick levels make the game a fun and easy way to kill some time and random enemy layouts keep you on your toes so you are not memorizing their patterns. However, with both the time mechanic and some achievements being for fastest completion, it becomes difficult to complete some later levels without losing a much-needed heart. The split-second it takes to relocate all enemy locations means their attacks have begun and you canât proceed with the shortest route anymore for their defeat.

This does become frustrating but once the final boss is defeated, the feeling is very rewarding and I dug right into the next sections. Definitely grab this game and see if you can unlock everything.

Considering that your character and most enemies die in a single hit, learning enemies' weaknesses and how to exploit them without hurting yourself provides an interesting challenge. What sets this challenge apart from the microgames of WarioWare is what happens if you do die. Once you lose your allotted three hearts, you have to continue either from the beginning of the game or from the stage immediately following a boss fight. In either case, the orientation of the stages sometimes changes, which keeps the game feeling fresh even though you are redoing stages you have already completed.

Even though I have not completed it at the time of this review, the inclusion of a "second quest" is my favorite part of the game. Rather than simply being a more difficult version of the first quest, it includes entirely new enemies, which means new behaviors and weaknesses to learn and exploit. It's almost like having two games in one, which is great without even taking into consideration the game's affordability. Shapes of Gray isn't the most intense game on the market, but it is one of the most enjoyable I have come across in recent memory, and while I can't guarantee you'll feel the same, I can guarantee that the time spent playing won't feel like a waste.
