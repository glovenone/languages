About This Game

Welcome to your new summer job at Freddy Fazbear's Pizza, where kids and parents alike come for entertainment and food as far as the eye can see! The main attraction is Freddy Fazbear, of course; and his two friends. They are animatronic robots, programmed to please the crowds! The robots' behavior has become somewhat unpredictable at night however, and it was much cheaper to hire you as a security guard than to find a repairman.

From your small office you must watch the security cameras carefully. You have a very limited amount of electricity that you're allowed to use per night (corporate budget cuts, you know). That means when you run out of power for the night- no more security doors and no more lights! If something isn't right- namely if Freddybear or his friends aren't in their proper places, you must find them on the monitors and protect yourself if needed!

Can you survive five nights at Freddy's?

"For all the simplicity of the game’s controls and premise, Five Nights at Freddy‘s is frightening. It’s a fantastic example of how cleverness in design and subtlety can be used to make an experience terrifying. Simple still images and proper character design steal the show in this game, and show that Scott Cawthon knows quite a lot about the secret fears people feel when looking at creepy dolls and toys. It’s elegant in how it sows fear, and is a must-own for anyone who likes scary games." -Joel Couture IndieGameMag.com

Before reading: this review is more of a neutral review than a not recommended review (which they should add) because I can't recommend it but it isn't garbage.

I decided to buy it because almost everyone I know told me that it's the best horror game ever. I'll admit it's scary the first time you play it because you don't know when the jumpscares are coming and if they're scary, but then after the first couple of them you realize it's just the noise that makes you jump and the actual jumpscares aren't scary, it turns into a strategy game. You can see if a jumpscare is coming if "it's me" flashes on the security tablet thingy or if the power is off and if you see eyes flashing and hear music. After the first two or three levels it starts to become boring (play in bursts?). There was something I liked about the vague ending though.

In conclusion, it's not bad, but not good either. It isn't really that scary to me, more adrenaline-rushing when I need to close the door fast (which needs power to close?!), so if you like strategy games with horror elements or something, then this is the game for you.

and for the love of god... DO NOT search "fnaf" into deviantart unless you want to cringe or vomit.
