About This Game



Recruit. Explore. Control.

Two years after Commander Shepard repelled invading Reapers bent on the destruction of organic life, a mysterious new enemy has emerged. On the fringes of known space, something is silently abducting entire human colonies. Now Shepard must work with Cerberus, a ruthless organization devoted to human survival at any cost, to stop the most terrifying threat mankind has ever faced.

To even attempt this perilous mission, Shepard must assemble the galaxy’s most elite team and command the most powerful ship ever built. Even then, they say it would be suicide. Commander Shepard intends to prove them wrong.

Just like in the previous game, what you say to the other characters matters a great deal. Fortunately, the conversation system has been improved. The camera now pans around characters as they talk to one another to evoke a dramatic, cinematic feel. Also, Shepard can interrupt the conversation when given the appropriate prompt, taking situations into new directions that could help or hinder the mission.

Shepard is more customizable than ever, letting you create your ideal lead character — gender, facial features, class, and of course, attitude. Once you embark on missions, you’ll be treated to an improved battle system that lets you target weak points and blast off your enemy’s limbs with a variety of weapons.

Set out to lead your team to victory… or die at the hands of The Collectors. Either path is possible in BioWare’s smash hit, Mass Effect 2. Which destiny will you choose?

Features:
  • The second act of BioWare’s epic sci-fi trilogy continues the story of Commander Shepard and humanity’s first steps onto the galactic stage
  • Success – or failure – depends heavily on the crew you assemble and their loyalty to you and the mission
  • Stunning graphics and a refined dialog system will draw you into the story like never before
  • Numerous improvements to the intense, third-person shooter combat
  • Deep character customization options let you create your own vision of Commander Shepard

If you're here reading this, I'm assuming you're one of two people.

1) You heard about how amazing this game is and you're wondering if you should buy it.
2) You played the first ME and are wondering if this one is worth buying.

For the first group of people, I have a very simple response. GO PLAY ME1! Trust me when I say you will be grateful that you did. First, it's an incredible game and it won't be a waste of your time. But most importantly, it will make this one feel so much more satisfying. Yes, it isn't "necessary" to play the first game. However, since this game follows the storyline of the exact same character from ME1, I believe it is more than worthwhile to experience the beginning adventures of Commander Shepard. The actions you take and the people you meet in the first game WILL import into this one. For that reason alone it is worth playing ME1 before ME2.

If you are the second group, let me highlight some of the key differences between the two. This game focuses a lot less on aimless exploring and more on progressing the story. Some of the quests even have timers on them and force you to progress at certain points. Your party members are an integral part of your adventure and in my opinion, have a lot more to share with you and are able to build better relationships with Shepard. The Mako has been replaced with a planet scanner, yes this means there is a lot less world exploration, but there is still plenty of time consuming extra missions to partake in. You can have pets, whom I killed multiple times. Has anyone figured out the key to keeping your space goldfish alive? And, for my personal favorite...there is no inventory. None. Nada. But, I made my armor purple so that's pretty cool I guess. You win some you lose some.

Overall this game is a big update on the first. To me, they felt like two very different games that meet at a central focal point. That central point is Shepard, who stays true to who they were in the first game. And isn't that what ME is all about?
