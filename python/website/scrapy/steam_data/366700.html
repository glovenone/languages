About This Game

"Breakout Invaders" is a fun fusion between two classic game genres: "Breakout" and "Space Invaders". Play through 100 variety levels, with a lot of original game styles. Defeat the space invasion destroying enemies and blocks structures like castles, temples, towers, cities, cemeteries, cathedrals,... You can customize and improve your ships, collect the enhancers items, fight again final bosses and much more.

~ABOUT THIS GAME~
Game type- breakout / arkanoid style game play with a little space invaders mixed in
* You control a ship that can only move back and forth to deflect a ball to destroy things.
* There are 6 ships in all with 5 different gun and 5 different missiles.
* As you break stuff sometimes power-ups will drops like: shields, summon comets, ball splits, bumper grow/shrink, speed, and items that give you use your missiles & bullets.
* The levels are a mix of destroy all blocks, keep X ball on the field, to protecting blocks, or taking out the invaders.
* Some of the break block levels are interesting like the castles, temples, cities, & there's one with 4 a pad on all 4 sides.
* The space invaders levels normally give you inf. bullets and have you fight a bunch of them or a larger boss type.
* Some will even place you in the middle protecting blocks from them and yes, you can die from taking hits from them.

~MY OPINION~
The 3d is a little simple and awkward at times but the overall experience is enjoyable. Some of the more memorable boards are the the space invader ones, some of the cities, & they should have had more with the 4 bumpers though it was one of the harder levels to get used to.

I was a little, disappointed with this breakout, but for £1.99, what was i expecting. After collecting hundreds of them over the years, I was expecting something with at least some innovation, and much better graphics. Hmmm! well....... maybe the space invader like levels are different, but overall Breakout Invaders is a poor second cousin removed to many of the breakouts I've played over the years. Some freeware versions have been more challenging, and look better graphically than this game. It's not seriously bad, but compared to a lot of the more recent breakout games I've played, it looks dated , even though some of the level designs are quite interesting.

The game felt sluggish with my mouse (a Razer Naga), the graphics are a retrograde step when compared to the likes of games like Magic Ball 2, 3 and 4, Strikeball 2 and 3 (to name just a few of the more well known ones - for some reason Alawar changed the name of the Magic Ball series to Smash Frenzy). The original, released over 10 years ago, is the only one in the Magic Ball series that looks and plays worse than today's game. The second in the Magic Ball series looked better than today's game (released way back in 2005), had more power ups and played well. The third in the series had a major graphics update (see video link below). Compare Breakout Invaders to the likes of the Magic Ball games, Ricochet Infinity and Shatter, the latter two of which are available via Steam. They are worlds apart in quality and game play. Breakouts were my first love w.r.t. arcade games (next to shmups and TD games). All that said, Breakout Invaders is only £1.99, soooo.... despite my disappointment, it's not a great loss; I've still got thousands of free levels to play via Ricochet infinity. :)

You can compare videos of the breakouts I mentioned above by clicking on their names. Magic Ball 3, Magic Ball 2, Ricochet Infinity, Shatter, Breakout Invaders, and the freeware game Chak's Temple.

Posted: 1 July, 2015
Breakout Invaders is a mash up of both Space Invaders and Breakout. If you are not familiar with those titles one is a vertical shoot em up and the other is pretty much reverse Pong in where you try to break all the blocks and not let the ball pass your goal. There are 100 levels and each level usually changes how the game plays. One mission you will be tasked with breaking all the blocks and in the next you may be tasked with defending blocks from aliens using your ball and lasers as a weapon. Lasers are used by either right clicking or using the alt key and can be used to destroy blocks or damage enemies though whether you have unlimited ammo depends level from level. You also have a missile which causes far more damage and has splash damage but you can only carry up to 10. In later levels they introduce metal gates that won't allow you to shoot blocks and must use a ball to reach them.

That is easier said than done. The physics in Breakout Invaders are completely insane and have no logic behind them at times. Your ball is the bounciest thing in the universe and hitting it with the edge of your paddle does not bounce it to the side like you would expect. It's a hard thing to explain but imagine playing Pong and the ball always going straight up instead of being able to bounce it off the sides of the screen when you learn to hit it properly. Unlike Breakout or Space Invaders this has a pinball like perspective to the maps instead of a simple 2D plane, so that adds some more complexity to it. Destroying castles and cites is great fun even though most of the times you will break blocks by chance instead of skill. Unfortunately later on they introduce small pipes that you must shoot your ball into to reach an area but the controls and physics are just not up to par to require the precision they so those end up being a pain.

You can control the game with either your mouse or your arrow keys but the mouse is way too sensitive for my liking. It works perfectly fine with the arrows keys but in some levels you are required to use the mouse which is pretty annoying. Which brings me to my biggest gripe with the game. Every level seems to have some kind of cheap gimmick like having 10 balls on screen for 2 minutes or having to destroy some blocks but having one paddle and goal on each side of the screen, making it feel like a game of Warlords that makes you have to control all players at once . The game is at it's best when it has you just breaking blocks yet every level has some kind of nuisance to break the flow once you start to actually have fun. It really makes it feel like a collection of mini games instead of modes built around the basic mechanics of Breakout and would have benefited from having them as extra features instead of being forced into the base game.

Another good portion of the game will have you fighting the aliens from Space Invaders and these are in my opinion the worst part of the game. Your paddle is way too large and makes avoiding projectiles a real pain plus you still have to worry about your ball. It's like being the fat guy in a game of paintball and having to text your boss in the middle of a gunfight. You do usually have infinite ammo for your laser but when you are down to the last few aliens they become extremely bullet spongy. It is insane how many hits they take and I actually thought my game was glitched until one of the three finally exploded. That is due to the tacked on upgrade system this game has. You will need to collect money to buy new ships or to upgrade your firepower. The problem is the game is incredibly stingy with money and the only way to get more is completing a level so you have two options, either grind some prior levels or deal with it. With that and having a gimmick every level it is like the devs think we have the attention span of a newt and need some kind of motive to play the game instead of it simply playing for the fun of it like say the two games they so proudly mention.

Both Space Invaders and Breakout were about skill. They were both based around improving the player, not the ship with extra firepower or the paddle with more speed. They gave you all the tools you needed from the get go and designed the game around that. Breakout Invader on the other hand simply beefs up the enemies and hopes you spent your limited cash correctly if you don't want to spend the next couple of minutes slowly chipping off the enemies health. It honestly would have been a better game if you had a single ship, no upgrades and optional game modes like the games it was inspired by. Moving on from that rant, there are other power-ups besides rockets and lasers. There are meteors that will destroy parts of the map, items that either shrink or increase your paddle and one that makes balls move faster. One small gripe is that items come down too slowly and would benefit from a bit more speed going down. Can be a bit bothersome waiting for it to makes its way down the screen while keeping track of the ball.

Environments are not pretty bland and takes quite some time to see any new ones. Music is a mixed bag, it ranges from good to terrible. Some sound effects sound right out of the late 70's which is nice. It was a fun game when it played like Breakout but with power ups, though every other gimmick was sub-par. If you are a fan of Space Invaders I wouldn't bother with this game. If you are a fan of Breakout there is a bit of fun to be had here but the good will ultimately be outweighed due to having to put up with gimmick after gimmick just to get to the next level that plays like Breakout. It is such an annoying design decision, it is like playing one level of one game, switching to a few other games and then going back to the one you actually like. Having them as different game modes instead of lumping them together to have “100 levels” would have been preferred. It's a shame, it could have been a decent little throwback but the devs spread themselves too thin and choose quantity over the quality of the gameplay.
