Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“The game is fun as it is. But to be able to make a great party game I need your help and imagination. I want to know what you want to see in the game and make the community a big part of the development. And together with me make 8Bit Fiesta as fun as possible. So join the discussions and tell me what you would want to see in the game and I will listen :)”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Hopefully the full release will be sometime early 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of the game will have a nice variation of different mini games and game modes. And with a fun singleplayer/co op story mode totally different from the multiplayer part of the game which isn't that common in other games in the local multiplayer genre.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“A lot of features will be added during the development. But the game is still enjoyable as it is in its current state and have proven to be a blast for many.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price will probably go up a bit after the release of the full game and by entering the early access you are guaranteed a better price.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I am all ears when it comes to your thoughts about the game. And I will listen and evaluate all of your ideas and suggestions and implement as much of it as we can.”

About This Game

Need to get a party started? All righty then!
8Bit Fiesta brings everything you need to the table with a challenging singleplayer story mode and its action packed local multiplayer mayhem including a built in drinking game. Choose between a ton of characters and challenge your friends and prove yourself worthy the title "King of the Fiesta".


System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7+
    • Processor: 2.0 GHZ
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Pixel Shader 2.0
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 100 MB available space

Early Access Review
Posted: 1 October, 2015
Genre: 2D Platformer/Indie

Note: Copy provided by the developer/publisher for the purpose of beta testing.

This game has a singleplayer and right now only local multiplayer(it's still not known if that will change). It may not be clear enough on the store page, but the game is more multiplayer oriented. Gameplay in this game is pretty simple and pretty smooth. In singleplayer you can only jump and double jump. Landing on the small places can be a bit tricky at first, but it's not that hard to get used to it. Only addition in multiplayer is that you can shoot in some games.

Singleplayer

Singleplayer right now consists of only two worlds and it's called "No Friends"(just saying so people don't get confused). Story is about Santa trying to save the world from swaggy Hitler, so it has funny moments. Every world has 12 levels, where levels 12 and 24 are bossfights. Levels in the first world are pretty short, usually not that hard to beat and same goes for the first bossfight. As for world 2, you can really see an improvement there, since levels are now better designed, much harder, and longer. There's one level in the world 2, that looks like Flappy Bird game, except you're driving an airplane, so it's a bit different than real Flappy Bird and not that hard. Singleplayer also has "No Death Run" and "Speedrun" modes. I am not sure how many more worlds will be added since it's still an early access, but from what I've heard singleplayer is just a bonus and right now it's pretty short. Takes between 2-2.5 hours to beat I would say since the second world is pretty hard, so you can get stuck somewhere for a while.


Multiplayer

Multiplayer is like I already said only local for now, and it has pvp with 2-4 players. Right now it consists of 12 games. This is the list of games: http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=527371719.
You can deselect the games that you don't want to play in a tournament. Before starting the tournament you need to select one of many characters that you want to play with: Santa, Joker, etc. http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=527396989
and many of those characters can be unlocked by playing the story mode. When tournament starts, games are selected random. In Battle Arena for example first who kills opponent 5 times wins, in Trap Run first who reach the finish wins, Blast Cast is like Bomberman, plus there's Duck Hunt- remember the game on Sega where you needed to shoot ducks, well this is it, except it's pvp. All in all multiplayer is pretty fun, and playing with more people certanly makes it more fun and probably more confusing since the levels are not that big for now at least.

Verdict:
Decent game, that is most fun when you play it with friends. Has singleplayer too right now, however it's pretty short and consists of 2 worlds.

Personal rating: 7.5/10

Early Access Review
Posted: 2 October, 2015
Played this as an actual drinking game last night - and it was a riot! I have a group that plays TowerFall and SpeedRunners like twice a week. We will try anything that supports major local co-op action. This game was a no brainer.

What is cool about this title is that the game modes are randomized for each round. One round may be a race, one may be a deathmatch, and yet another may be an obstacle course. The variety is great. Everything stays lite and accessible for newish players yet there is still some cool difficulty for old-school gamers. Here is a great example of how they accomplish this: there is a room with lots of spike hazards, and it is a race to the end. If you can get to the end without touching a single one - you can win it in one go, BUT if you touch a spike, it is removed, essentially making the run easier. You keep respawning infinitely after death.

Totally accessible game that is really fun for local co-op. I highly recommend.
