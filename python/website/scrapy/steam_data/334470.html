About This Game

Inspired by Daniel Defoe’s classic masterpiece, Robinson Crusoe is a story of a man who survives a terrible shipwreck and finds himself cast away on an island in the Caribbean. In this incredible hidden object adventure that spans over 28 years, help Robinson explore the island, fight with cannibals, visit a lost village and construct a boat to sail back home!

- Two game play modes – Relaxed and Survivor
- 31 unique levels
- 36 unique locations to explore, from a wrecked ship to desolated well.
- Fun adventure spirit!
- Based on the classic novel by Daniel Defoe

This is a “pure” hidden-object game -- no point-and-cilck adventuring -- that would make fans of the genre happy. The hidden-object scenes are quite challenging, and get progressively difficult. The few puzzle mini-games, often hidden-object oriented, are also satisfying.

The only problem is the low resolution: it makes the hidden-object scenes even more difficult than they need to be.

Also slightly annoying is some bad logic: some of the objects you need to find should not be found on Robinson Crusoe's island! There are several such inconsistencies that detract from the atmosphere, otherwise well-established by the addition of a pleasing musical score.

Nowadays it just cannot cut it unless you are an old and nostalgic HOG player.
And even then, this game is nothing spectacular. Sure, it has some plot (very loosely based on the title material), the scenery looks nice, the hidden object scenes are fun enough for their age, and there is variation in them… but that is not enough to warrant a purchase.

Play as Robinson Crusoe during his time on an island, after a shipwreck: find items to build a boat, meet Friday... and escape cannibals!

Adventures of Robinson Crusoe is what I would call an "old" game. Indeed, you don't have any story in it apart being Robinson Crusoe during his 20 years of stay on a lost island. You'll begin on the ship after the accident, then explore the island, then build your house, etc..

The game is a pure hidden objects game: nearly each location begin by finding X times the same item, then you get a list of objects. Some are hard to find, mainly because of a little problem with graphics and because it was like that back then: items were often hidden behind another or being the same color as the background, etc.. Some locations are puzzles: differences, play the right tune, jigsaws, etc.. You even have a guessing game, which is not easy if you're not skilled in English. However, the game isn't punishing you for that, you can give up and you'll go to the next word.

As I've said, I've a problem with the graphics. Indeed, for recent monitors, the game isn't adapting to it. The game was reduced at 1/3 of it and the result was that I was under the impression that it got badly reduced. It can also have played in my impression of not seeing the objects. Apart that, they were pretty acceptable, even if you can say that they're outdated. You have some bright landscapes before getting in a much somber atmosphere with the cannibals part.

The soundtrack was also acceptable, though I didn't have really time to listen to it as I had to play it with the music low for not disturbing my mother, that was watching TV (I could have taken my headphones but I was too lazy to get them).

If you're in for some good old hidden objects games, Adventures of Robinson Crusoe is a nice game, though I would prefer largerly Mystery P.I., Escape the Museum, Mystery Case Files or Hidden Expedition. If you prefer to look for a casual game, you'll better check Artifex Mundi's or Alawar's.
