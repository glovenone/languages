Introduction

The chronicles of Emerland. Solitaire. is exactly what the title suggests, a solitaire game. The most basic of games and one of the few that has come already installed on PCs for years is now given an updated look with updated features. There are many games like this on the market, taking a simple card game and adding bells and whistles to keep the interest of the player and attract new ones. However, in my opinion this game got it right and created something thatâs more than just a game of solitaire.

Story

One aspect that The Chronicles of Emerland. Solitaire. has going for it is that it has a story. Your character is an apprentice under an old gray bearded wizard and things are going fine until an evil sorcerer attacks and makes threats against the world. Itâs up to you to travel to the four kingdoms to save the elemental stones and gain the power you will need to defeat the forces of darkness. The kingdoms are basic, you have Humans, Elves, Dwarves and Mermaids, but you also get a companion from each kingdom creating a sort of party like in a role playing game. Together you and your team travel the land and try to save the world. Itâs a pretty simple fantasy story but enough to keep a player entertained.

Gameplay

If youâve played regular solitaire then you should have all you need to get into this game. The board has cards laid out randomly and you have a draw pile, your goal is to stack cards in order from your drawn card to create combos and score points. If youâve played modern day solitaire games then you can expect to see some added features to spice things up. Roadblocks, a common one, come in the form of ice, vines, bricks, etc. Each one requires you to unlock the roadblock by finding an item like a sword hidden under another pile of cards. This adds more of a challenge, especially in later levels, as you have to plan your moves precisely to continue on and sometimes may not get the draws you need to even get past the roadblocks. Each level has ten stages to it and in between each level are small hidden object scenes, which I enjoyed.

I mentioned earlier that the game gives you four companions, each character helps in a different way, the archer for example can shoot down one card off the field which is useful if you have a lingering card and want to get a perfect rating. You can also power up your companions by buying certain amulets! There is one amulet for each party member and then other amulets to add bonuses to the game such as getting more gold or experience or even seeing every possible move on the board. The last aspect is leveling up, when you do this new perks are added that can pop up randomly on cards that are on the board and each perk has two levels to it so instead of being able to get two extra cards you can then get three.


Graphics / Audio

To top off this fun game are some great visuals. The cards are all beautiful though I would have liked an option to change the design of the deck. The scenery is wonderful and fits the mystical theme of the game. Bright colors along with lovely character designs help round out the visual aspects of the game. The music is also great, if you play the game long enough youâll notice that the tracks tend to repeat quite often, I think there are maybe three or four unique ones. I didnât mind this but the music was so lovely and soothing I wouldnât have minded more. The game is also fully voice acted so every time a character speaks you will also hear them. The acting is not the best but itâs passable.


Pros

+ The game is cheap.
+ Offers a good amount of gameplay for the price.
+ Overall presentation looks good.
+ I love fantasy so this was a personal plus for me.
+ The game has two different difficulties.
+ It has trading cards and achievements.

Cons

- Levels can get repetitive, so itâs best if played in short bursts.
- There is a bug in the game, if youâre playing a level and want to exit the game, the game counts that stage as a failure for some reason. This has been addressed on the Steam forums but the developer sadly cannot fix the issue.

Final Thoughts

I personally loved this game and would find it perfect except for the bug. Iâve played quite a few solitaire games but this one is definitely a favorite for me. I loved the fantasy theme and I enjoyed having party members with different helpful skills. The game can be a little challenging even for solitaire veterans but the game gives you plenty of perks and ways to power up to help level the playing field. Plus, all of the achievements are relatively easy to gain if you simply just play through the game. If you want to play alone with yourself I recommend this title, as a great card game of course.

Rating: 8/10

So do you like solitaire games? Then go get Regency Solitaire or Faerie Solitaire. Those are much better games than this one is. The main reason why I'm giving this a not recommended is the fact that there is a pretty hideous bug that when you quit the game it records the hand you were on as a failure and moves to the next one, even if you didn't start that hand. That's pretty bad for a game that is meant to be more on the casual side and played off and on to relax, not to be played in one sitting. Though thankfully the game's achievements are easy enough that unless you quit during like every hand, you'll be able to get them all even with this bug.

The other issue I had with the game is that even on the easier of the two modes, it was very luck based and not skill based for a large number of hands. After about two of the chapters all of a sudden you get layouts with a lot of blocked cards and very little ability to get a combo. To be quite honest if you don't get the achievement for a 20 combo before restoring the fire amulet in the human realm, you aren't very likely to get it because getting any decent combo without saving up a ton of wildcards is not happening with those layouts. You'll get very sick of Seth's laughter for leaving more than 5 cards quite quickly because you'll be hearing it a lot. Of note, I played on casual, though I am a perfectionist. Between this and the bug I had to deal with not being able to perfect every level.

Another issue that is present is that at times the music just stops for no good reason.

Put simply, this game is a promise of a solid game that really needs some work and I'll gladly edit this review if some of these issues get fixed, though truly these sorts of issues should have been worked out before release, not after. The artwork is very lovely and the music when it actually plays right is relaxing. The game can be fun when I'm not getting frustrated at retrying a level a bunch of times just to get less than 4 cards remaining. Edit to add: The dev has responded that the bug causing fails in hands cannot be fixed.

As it stands now, I can't recommend the game outside of a much better sale or in a bundle because it's more frustating than relaxing and fun and a game should get harder as you progress, not easier It also mostly gets easier because you get amulets and power ups to be able to deal with the frustrating factors of luck playing such a role.

Nice solitaire game similar to Faerie Solitaire.

If you're played Faerie Solitaire or Three Towers (as it's sometimes called) then you are familiar with the idea of this game. You have to clear all the cards in the layout by clicking on a card that is numbered one higher or one lower than the card in the deck. For instance, if your deck card is a 5 you can click on either a 4 or a 6. It's easy to get combos by going up and down the numbers, like 4-3-2-3-4-5-6-7-8-9-8-7-6, etc. depending on the layout and luck.

As the story goes on you gain 4 helpful allies with special abilites. There are also amulets you can purchase in the store with the gold you gain in the levels.

The story is kind of silly, but it's just a card game so who cares. Bad guy in black robe and holding a snake staff wants to conquer the world. You and your allies have to stop him. You travel the land of men, elves, dwarves, and mermaids (though you only get to see a merMAN, no maids unfortunately). In each place you have to restore the temple that has been desecrated by the bad guy, and restore the elemental crystal.

In addition to the card playing levels there are also occasional hidden object levels. That makes for a nice break from just cards, cards, cards all the time.

The gameplay is solid. It's fairly easy to get good combos even without using bonuses. Though, I was playing on casual difficulty, so i don't know if the gameplay is harder on the harder difficulty or if they just give you more things to accomplish to win.

Graphics are nice, with soft colors fitting a fantasy world.

Music is nice background music. It's sort of like classical music, just nice background music that does not intrude on your thinking.

There is also voice acting in this game. Each character has a voice. They also print the dialogue in boxes.

Length of playtime for me was 12 hours. However, I tend to keep replaying levels if I don't get a perfect or near perfect. So it could be less for someone who wants to just rush through the game.

tl;dr version:

Similar to Faerie Solitaire (Three Tower, etc)
Hidden Object Levels
Easy to get combos
2 difficulty selections
Full voiced audio
10+ hours of gameplay for first time through
It's Cheap!!!
