About This Game

New York City, sometime in the 1950s. A wave of mysterious murders has swept through the city. At each crime scene, only a pile of ash remains. Witnesses claim to have seen a ball of lightning. Could a human being be capable of such a thing? Who is behind the cryptic killings? Are they connected with a strange incident which took place during an execution at the Sing Sing penitentiary? The police are unable to answer any of these questions... and so the White Stone Order takes the reins. Fearless journalist Laura is again brought in to investigate murders nobody can explain! Immerse yourself in an exciting crime story and visit uncharted locations from 1950s New York!

Supernatural terror has once again struck the big apple and it’s up to our heroine Laura to save the day. New York Mysteries; High Voltage is a suspenseful hidden item game set in 1950′s New York City. It has a strong plot, great aesthetics and is full of tons of fun. Can you help Laura solve the mystery before it’s too late?

People are dropping like flies and the police don’t have much evidence to go on, however witnesses claim to see a ball of light at the scenes of the crimes. The White Stone Order, a secret underground organization specializing in the paranormal, attempts to solve the mystery from the shadows and invite Laura to help with her keen mind and investigative journalism skills. The story leads Laura on an adventure to stop a serial killer from exacting his revenge on those that tried to execute him. Travel with Laura around New York City as you search for clues and find a way to bring this murderer down once and for all.

While the story itself is filled with thrills and sharp plot turns, the game also has lovely visuals to help keep you enthralled. All of the backgrounds are rich with color, the hidden object scenes are beautiful done and easy to see while still offering a challenge and the characters are very lively. The game also has well-animated cut scenes and excellent voice acting. The game also has a beautiful score to listen to as you play.

Great scenes and puzzles offer a challenge which makes gaining all the achievements that much more rewarding. There is also a bonus chapter that gives even more depth to the drama. The bonus content gives you access to wallpapers, videos from the game, concept art, re-playable mini games, puzzles and a brand new mini game! There a lot of in game items to collect as well during a play through.

New York Mysteries: High Voltage is a solid game that offers hours of enjoyment. The only issue I had while playing it was a saving glitch which forced me to replay the ending again, however, the issue seems to have been fixed with a recent patch at the time of this review. The developers were quick to fix the issue which shows, along with such an overall superb game, that they’re dedicated to their fans. I highly recommend grabbing this title, you won’t regret it.

If you have played Secrets Of The Mafia, then you'll enjoy High Voltage, the eerie sequel!

I really appreciate the developers for preserving the art style and atmosphere in this entry of the franchise, I was worried that there would be too many drastic changes.

In this entry, Laura returns, now taken under the wing of The Order, an underground group of investigators that resolve paranormal crimes, uncover hidden mysteries, and preserve the secrets within New York City.
(And if you find all the hidden projector slides, you'll get to play more bonus puzzles, and you'll get to watch a bonus cutscene that delves a tiny bit more into The Order's origins!)
For her new case, Laura must uncover the truth behind a series of mysterious deaths - people who are zapped and turned to ash!

Also, just like in the previous game, I was thrilled to unlock and play a bonus chapter to the game.
Just like in the last game, the previous chapter acts as a prequel leading up to the events of the main game. I appreciate the length in gameplay and how nicely the chapter wrapped up. (In the last bonus chapter, there were a few plot holes. So this was a nice change!)

While I enjoyed this game, I sensed a change in theme...

Secrets Of The Mafia had a creepier vibe to it, and it also borrowed quite a number of noir themes to add to the mystery (Wandering the city at night, unsure of who to trust, conspiracy plots, etc.).

This game takes place almost entirely during the daytime (which was refreshing, don't get me wrong), and it was devoid of the noir feel. However, to make up for that, the new lore borrows a lot from Nicola Tesla's work and the mystery surrounding physics. So, instead of a noir crime mystery, you'll get to experience more of a sci-fi mystery.

~ . ~ .

+ Pros +

*6+ hours of gameplay

*Same lush, curious art style

*NEW areas of New York to explore

*MORE characters to interact with

*Additional game lore and character origins



- Cons -

*Still only have one save file
(You can create multiple profiles and start the game over that way, but if you play bits of the bonus chapter AND want to replay some of the main game, your save file will be overwritten. I kind of wish there was a chapter select and multiple save slots.)

*Voice acting
(Laura's voice was a bit unpolished, but it wasn't too unpleasant; just needs practice!)

*Abrupt ending
(The ending for the main game was pretty good, but it was a bit rushed, in my opinion. Also, I wish that there had been a bit of a 'wind down'; a time for Laura and Dr. Bishop to reflect on the case.)

~ . ~ .

Overall, the game was a very relaxing and enjoyable experience.
It's a little pale compared to the previous game, but just as quirky as ever.

I've come to adore the franchise and I'm very hopeful for a sequel!

Laura is back! After solving the mafia case, she's sent to investigate a series of strange murders. She discovers that it's a paranormal being that is behind these. Why? How can she stop that?

This time, the story is revolving around a man whose execution has gone so bad that the authorities never spoke about it. That's how the game is beginning. So you have to find how he disappeared and how come it's related to the murders. As usual, the game is mixing reality and paranormal and underground train as it's how you reach each location, like in the first game. You're working for Bishop, who was the one that asked for your help previously. The bonus chapter is about the father of the convict and how he tried to save his son to a death sentence.

Anyway, the gameplay is items to find, to combine, shapes scenes (let's say that maximum 5 of them may be harder to solve), puzzles (with some that are rare in the genre or combining several in one) and people to meet and discuss. It's not that I like the shape scene, however, I would have preferred some variety, like alternating with a list of hidden objects.

The developers are also the same as Lost Lands, so, yeah, given the fact that I've played three of their games nearly in a row, the first NY mysteries being weeks ago, I can't forget about the bonus contained in the opus: minigames to replay (new or familiar ones - practical to get timed or click counted achievements involving some of them), concept arts, music tracks, etc.. Besides, you have also objects to collect to unlock stuff: beyond objects (changing appearance) and four specific objects (Liberty Statue, cats, towers or balls) that are all in each scene. Besides, collecting diapositives is also explaining the background of the Order you're working for - though I have to confirm that because I didn't look about them.

Anyway, the graphics are quite good and diversified: from a prison to a lab that would have pleased Tesla (as the game is also based on what he discovered) without forgetting New York under the snow in the bonus chapter. And that soundtrack! I mean, it was giving me chills.

So, yeah, I can say that it's among these HO games that you need to play, as it's not boring and very well done. Perhaps the voice acting of Laura could be better..... but hey, I didn't regret my purchase. Go for it, seriously!
