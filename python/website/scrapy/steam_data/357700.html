Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We have reached a stage of development where we have most of the core ingredients for the game but we want to involve a community of players in finalising the way those ingredients come together as well as the final feature set for the game.

We have been testing the core gameplay at a lot of events and will continue to do so but with a roguelike game, we need data from real players over a period of time to get the meta-game and balancing right. Early Access is the best way for us to do this.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We expect to be in early access for around six to seven months with a full launch around March or April 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We are planning for the full version to include online multiplayer, more environments, enemies, items, weapons, special events and bosses and at least one extra playable character. Many of these features will be added during the course of Aarly Access.

Weâre planning a level 15 cap on the Early Access initial build, which will be expanded to 30 on the full game launch.

If unexpected ideas come from the community during Early Access we may be able to implement them before launch.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is about 65% complete. Almost all the core features are in the game and the main four playable characters and enemy races. About half the environments are in the initial Early Access version.

Online multiplayer is not in this version but this is one of the first things we will be adding during Early Access.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“At this point we intend to price the game the same during Early Access and Full Release. Please note - this may change as the game will be in Early Access for around six to seven months and the market is likely to change in that time.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We would love to receive feedback on the game in our Steam forum and the Surprise Attack Games subreddit. Weâre particularly looking for feedback on balancing and also ideas for cool items, weapons, enemies, bosses or player skills that the community would like to see in the game.”

28 January

Online Multiplayer

Hey everyone,

Some people have been asking us where the online multiplayer update for Rogue Continuum is. Well, we wanted to take the time to explain the delay.

A few months ago, Unity (the game engine we're developing Rogue Continuum on) released a brand new multiplayer framework for developers. After doing some research and testing it out, we decided to switch our multiplayer engine from our current method (we were using an older multiplayer framework), to the new native Unity multiplayer implementation. As a result, we had to re-code all of our network code to make it work, but this would mean a smoother experience for everyone, as well as allow us to keep the netcode updated longer after release.

The good news is that we have the basic online multiplayer functionality in our developer build, and we're making great progress towards the public release. Once we are confident that the netcode is stable, we'll launch the online multiplayer update on a new framework that offers more stability and support post launch.

Thanks for your patience! We're look forward to getting the online multiplayer into your hands as soon as possible. In the meantime, please keep providing us feedback for the current build on the Steam forums :)

regards,
William Tan

Posted: 15 September, 2015
It's exactly what it looks like; a sci-fi roguelite shooter with some RPG elements, upgrades, etc... - Does this game do anything revolutionary? No - does it manage to be some awesome fun? Absolutely! The game is early access and from what I can tell is lacking content, but that's to be expected from an early access game. It's actually very, very fun though and as long as development goes smoothly from here on out, should shape up to be the great game it looks like it's going to be. If you are ok with being an early bird in a game like this, I would highly recommend it.

I've tried every character, but I spent very little time with the melee guy. Every character felt genuinely unique. Each character has a "limiter" of sorts to stop them from putting out endless DPS. The generic shooter guy has to reload, and thus plays much like you'd expect a generic shooter guy to: small engagements, reloading inbetween, etc... He was very fun though, don't get me wrong. He is clearly meant to be the "basic" class of the game, but manages to do so with finesse.

The sniper girl also plays like you'd expect: She wants to engage as few enemies as possible at once and does extremely high single (or few) target damage. Some of the later levels have large enemy swarms and I haven't made it that far with her yet, but I imagine she is VERY difficult as the game goes on. She was very fun though. Her gimmick is that she needs to charge her shots up, and can "overcharge" to do extra damage. She â¥â¥â¥â¥ing DESTROYS bosses.

The melee guy, as I said, I didn't mess with much. It was difficult to deal damage with him without taking damage in return. His gimmick is that his weapon can overheat and be rendered unusable if spammed for too long.

The robot is by far my favorite character. It has amazing stats all around and a shield to absorb damage before losing health, but to deal damage drains an energy bar that slowly fills up. You can switch between 3 modes that charge your shield bar faster, your weapon bar faster, or split charge power equally. You HAVE to switch to do well, trust me. It's a very fun character though.

The game has a fun unlock system for content, but as I said, there doesn't appear to be a ton of content as of yet. But early access is early access. If you like roguelites and sci-fi, this is a fantastic game.
