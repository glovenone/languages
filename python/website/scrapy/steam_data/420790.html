StarBreak Community Contest #1: Design a Fire Forest Monster



Welcome to the first StarBreak community contest!

THE GOAL:

Create your very own enemy character for the Fire Forest mission and see it come to life inside StarBreak!

THE RULES:
  1. We are accepting all forms of visual media. You may send us drawings, paintings, videos, sculptures...anything! Feel free to include a brief write up as well, but your entry should be primarily visual in nature.
  2. The enemy character should be designed to fit into the Fire Forest mission. Give the mission a few runs and get a feel for the environment and enemies!
  3. What’s important is getting your idea across clearly! When judging entries, priority will be placed on the concept of your enemy character, not necessarily the quality of artwork.
  4. Nothing too vulgar; try to keep content in line with what you’ve seen in StarBreak so far.
  5. All entries should be sent to contest@crunchy.com
PRIZES:

1st Place: Your enemy concept will be refined and placed into StarBreak’s Fire Forest stage and you will receive 150 UC<br>
2nd Place: 150 UC<br>
3rd Place: 100 UC<br>
4th Place: 50 UC<br>

DEADLINE:

Entries must be received by 11:59pm on July 4th, 2016

That’s it! We’re looking forward to all of your ideas.

If you’re excited about the contest and would like to discuss it or even share your finished or in-progress entries, check out our Community Contest #1 forum thread.

Good luck, Breakers!

-Crunchy Games Team

These are all subjective so they may or may not apply to you. Read at your own discretion

Pros:
  1. Skill-based gameplay: Barely any if none cheap hits that you cannot dodge even if you see them coming. Som enemis can and do seemingly spawn projectiles on top of you but that's rare.
  2. High replayability.
  3. Good sound design.
  4. Fair pay model: You can buy more storage to save items between deaths, storage for your characters so you don't have to kill yourself and lose all your levels if you want to play someone else and hats. Nothing that gives you an advantage over other non-paying players.

Cons:
  1. Resolution is tied to the visual scaling: This is a big one for me. If you're playing on an older laptop or using a smaller monitor, you'll be at a SEVERE disadvantage compared to those that aren't. While people with larger monitors will be able to see what is below them and shots coming towards them from far away, you'll costantly be guessing yourself if by dropping down you're dropping to an endless pit (I'm looking at you, Strands) or an enemy or a safe platform and constantly getting hit by bullets that by the moment they get on your screen you have less than a second to dodge. No idea why devs decided that would ever be a good idea to do but there you go.
  2. Visual noise: If you're playing a map with a decently large amount of players(15-20+), better keep your eyes open. With the amount of visual effects flying from every player, enemy projectiles become nigh invisible in the mess that ensues. That applies to your character as well which leads to the next con
  3. Barely any customization: There's hats. And that's it. And you lose them if you die so that's a waste of money if I ever saw one. But I digress. No customization means everyone looks the same. Which as I mentioned above, makes really really dificult to tell who you are in a big group. Game tries using a visual effect around your character which woul be great if it wasnt the same color as some of the effects of other players whih onl adds to the visual noise I mentioned earlier.
  4. RNG: Getting items for your character depends purely on RNG. Many times I've ran through a map and didn't get a single item my class can use. That can be quite frustrating as your starting weapon is as powerful as throwing ♥♥♥♥-filled baloons.

And if you can't be bothered to read all that, I rate the game:
7/10

Overall I had a positive experience playing this game.
Now the first thing you need to know about it is that it's a platformer with gameplay much like contra.
And, like Contra it is brutal. Which is a nice change of pace, it's an actual game that takes skill to play, many games cannot say this.
So brutal that 90% of the community do not have what it takes to progress to a 'hard' level.
There is a perma-death in this game, so that nice equipment that you obtained is just gone once you lose your character. And then your back to square one, using whatever equipment is in your stash.

That's not to say that it's impossible to get a high-tier mech. There might be half a dozen players in the community who have achieved it. They take their MK5 shells with the best equipment and solo levels that are designed to be taken on by 20 people. Then post their achievements on youtube for the sake of ego stroking.

The rest of us decently skilled players run around with exotic equipment from Tier 2 bosses. I've lured 8 people into the fungus cave boss and been one of the only 2 people to survive. The entire community fears boss.
Only to lose it all in underground base because the biggest group we could get together was 5 people in a level designed for a dozen.

If you do want to progress then I will give you the following tips.
1. Don't be a hero. You are not beholden to anyone.
2. If you are missing more than 25 hp, be more cautious. If you are missing half HP, hang back and let other people do the work. If you are near death go home immediatly, if you are not locked inside some boss fight. Always ping HP.
3. Learn about what the situations each shell is best at. Do you go in? or would it best to let another go.
4. Learn what the enemies do. Know how they are going to attack, so you can avoid the damage.
Watch youtube videos on the bosses before you even attempt them.
