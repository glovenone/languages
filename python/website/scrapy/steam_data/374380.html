About This Game

THE GAME

From the Baltic to the shores of the Pacific and through the arid steppes of Central Asia, relive on PC the biggest civil war ever!

October 1917. The Bolshevik Revolution starts a tidal wave that will sweep the whole of the XXth century. But the birth of the Communist regime was not an easy walk, and it faced dozens of tough but often unknown oppositions which did their utmost to destroy it in its infancy, in an amazing conflict where a central side is attacked from all corners and directions.

Make the Reds side triumph to save the Soviet Proletarian revolution, or, on the other side, lead the Whites side to victory in order to place the old regime back in power and protect the countries of Europe, weakened by the Great War, from the perils of the revolution.

Revolution Under Siege GOLD is the first complete historical simulation on PC of the Russian Civil War between 1917 and 1923. A wealth of historical events and geopolitical options, real or possible according to the player's choices, will bring a realistic tempo to the maneuver of your forces and those of your opponents.

Revolution Under Siege GOLD has been designed by a French team, with the aid of volunteers from all over the world, all dedicated fans and contributors of the historical strategy games from AGEod.

FEATURES

  • A map of unrivalled precision covering the whole of Russia and its neighbours, from Poland to the Pacific Ocean.
  • Be immersed in historical realism: hundreds of officers, from Trotsky to Semenov, and many more units, from Red Guards to Cossacks are faithfully represented in the game.
  • Politics, requisitions, revolts, repression: Gain support of the population or pressure them with your political police.
  • Cleverly use the brand new weapons of the time: tanks, armoured trains and aircraft.
  • Recruitment, organization: Create massive armies and divisions from groups of volunteers, train and use them in ambitious plans for victory.
  • Use diplomacy to trigger foreign nations entry in the war or attack neighbouring neutrals. New allies on all sides will endlessly change the course of your games!
  • Enjoy unique campaigns covering each one of the years of the war in Russia, as well as the Finnish Civil War (1918) or the Russian-Polish war (1920). Or rewrite history with a what if scenario set in an alternative post-WW1 world in 1921.

WHAT IS NEW IN REVOLUTION UNDER SIEGE GOLD

  • Dozens of new historical events and semi-historical ones to give a permanent flavor to your games
  • A myriad of new options and game mechanisms to immerse you even more in the Russian Civil War
  • A wealth of new geopolitical options, historical or alternate historical according to the player's choices, which impact resources, popularity and diplomacy to involve various playable factions.
  • A Chronology of all the Events and Options of the game, so that players can prepare to the strategic choices they will have to do.
  • New updated manuals in English, French, Russian with details rules and design analysis
  • A new updated Strategic map useful for your AAR and PBEM.
  • Dozens of big fixes and game balance additions, as well as hundreds of minor improvements based on the last 4 years of players experience and feedback,

I am old time fan of AGEOD and their games are not too easy to learn, but when you do, then you are rewarded for it greatly.
This game ( I will call it RUS Gold) is a standard type of AGEOD games that is using the a simultaneous turn-based engine (WEGO system). This means that both sides are giving orders and they are executed together when you hit next turn.
You get a lot of playtime in this one as it has Grand Russian Civil War scenario (1918) the Russian-Polish war (1920) and a what if scenario set in an alternative post-WW1 world in 1921 called Drang Nach Osten where the victorious Central Powers are attacking Soviet Russia. Along with these, you also get some smaller scenarios that are great for beginners or a fast game when you are short on time. Such are a 1917 early Revolution scenario and Finnish civil war one. The later is really recommended for beginners as it is pretty easy to play as the Whites and it is not too complex. But playing tutorial scenarios and reading the manual is a MUST for absolute newbies to any AGEOD game!
Grand scenarios are really HUGE and you are in total control there. You create new armies, you lead them to glory or defeat and you have tactical and strategic choices. Here you can win a lot of smaller battles and still lose the war if you are not careful. Every action has its re-action and not two games will play the same. Your nights will be short and mornings will come too early :)

All and all I simply love this game as a lot of attention to details and revisions and changes/fixes of the old version was put into it.
There are not many (if all) games with this topic and if you want something different, are not afraid to sink a lot of hours learning things and love history ... then you will also like this game like I do.

A personal note:
Great work Mr. Chopin and the crew!

If your eyes glazed over in high school when they were describing these particular events, this is your chance to make sense out of it.

You'll see the Russian Civil War unfold two weeks at a time in all of its confusing glory. Dr. Zhivago, Quiet Flows the Don, and Riley Ace of Spies will finally have some historical context.

There are a few (maybe all of two) games out there that touch on this subject with single scenarios, but this is the only game that concentrates on giving you the whole Civil War. If you weren't paying attention in high school you might remember something about American troops landing in Russia, but you probably won't remember all those Germans running around in the Ukraine. It throws an interesting side light on what happened in WW II when you realize that they had done it before, and not in some remote period of history, but in many cases earlier in their careers.

The music in the game is probably the best music that has ever been included in any game ever published. I should also mention that I used to be the lead domra player in a balalaika orchestra: I am extremely partial to Russian music but I also know what it's supposed to sound like. The music in this game is the real thing. It's worth buying the game just to get your hands on the mp3 files that come with it.

This is an Ageod game, and a very mature one. It doesn't follow the gaming logic that most people are used to, and learning it is a bit tricky at first because the manual is written with the implicit assumption that you have some understanding of how Ageod games work. It's probably the easiest to wrap your mind around, but really learning the game is a three step process. You have to play the three tutorials, you need to read through manual very quickly so you know where to go back to to look things up that you won't understand the first time you encounter them in the game, and you need to play a simple scenario like the Ice March a few times and keep making a mess of it until you finally figure out how to handle troops.

An important concept is that units are collections of men that need to be well-rested and organized. Keep an eye on your units' cohesion and strength states, and if a unit isn't up to snuff, make sure that it sits around doing nothing inside a town for a few weeks or it will melt away when you try to march it across an increasingly hostile steppe.

If you are interested in 20th century history this game is a must-buy. It also just happens to be a lot of fun to play, and you'll catch yourself not starting battles because you're so entranced listening to the music.

After spending hours with the game so far I actually learned how to play, and what I want to say is this game is amazing. There is a lot of depth in crafting an army, and while I thought it wouldn't be much fun, turns out I was wrong. It's very rewarding to see armies you nurtured duke it out with enemy armies and try to figure out the best configuration to lead your faction to victory.

Alright so to keep this review clean, organized, and productive I am going to start off first with a list of pros and cons:

Pros:
Competent AI The AI will often go for undefended regions and try to cut off your supplies. If you have the upper hand the AI will avoid your troops and won't suicide which is a good thing. Therefore, winning a scenario is very rewarding, since the AI puts up a very good fight to the end.

Historical Accuracy Revolution Under Siege Gold goes through detailed historical events and decisions that adds immersion and satisfaction to the game. Rarely is such period covered, let alone covered with historical accuracy

Chain of Command feature like in the other AGEOD games Create divisions, army corps, and front GHQ in order to effectively manage your army. Also there is naval units, but they are bogged down to gunship and such so the impact of their involvement in the war is dependent on the scenario. Combine elements to effectively avoid combat penalties due to unorganization, and heavy effects of casualties and movement. Not to mention elite police units for both sides which prevent desertion.

Brilliant use of the unit cards and overall break down of the army, aswell as excellent simulation of the depth of logistics (ammunition and food are both accounted for the well being of the units and how well they perform in battle).

Music There is a variety of music from the era of Imperial/soviet Russia, definitely helps immerse in the game, and may give the urge to learn Russian just to understand what they are saying. The music is just beautiful.

Regional Decisions bring the masses to your cause in friendly or enemy regions. Sentiment to your faction will give you production boasts if in friendly territory and can also mean stopping the enemies ability to produce troops in their territory if the support for your faction is greater.

Historical decisions Like events, sometimes you can choose between two historical decisions or options which will give you different benifits depending on the one you choose.

Con:
Game can crash

Massive map I might recommend if you are new to AGEOD games you play Rise of Prussia or Alea Jacta Est because RUS has a lot of regions. It will be daunting to players who have never played any games of such magnitude as this. However, if you live in Russia, the game might be rather easier to manage since you know the geographic areas well.

FPS capped this is to avoid crashes

Slight learning curve you are going to at least need to be able to read, and have a little common sense. The game holds your hand for a few turns but after that you need to be planning your moves and carefully assemble your army. Your army needs to meet the needs of the objectives indicated at the beginning of the scenario. Carelessly glossing over important events and clicking the end turn button without making sure everything is right can result in series of defeats in battle. You will also need to read the manual to understand how combat works and make notes of important details like the effects of bad weather and why your troops may be taking attrition.
