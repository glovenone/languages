About This Game

As a member of Delta Force — The Army's Elite, top secret special forces unit — the word 'impossible' is not in your vocabulary. In Delta Force, you will conduct missions swiftly and silently, in every possible terrain, under every imaginable condition and through unconventional means.

Features

  • Immersive environment consists of real-world effects, such as bridges that creak, insects that buzz, flocks of birds that form and re-form, and fish that bite.
  • Watch your enemy's every move, listen for their approach then strike with brutal precision over the most heartbreaking pieces of terrain the world has ever seen.
  • Play in either 1st or 3rd person across miles and miles of vast outdoor environments. Engage the enemy over unprecedented distances.
  • Play over 40 gut-wrenching missions across five continents ranging from airfield takedowns in Central Asia to clearing out S. American cartels to stopping Nuclear terrorism in Northern Russia.

Posted: 3 January
Delta Force is better than whatever dumb shooter you like from this time period.

In a world where Call of Duty was never tactical, Rainbow Six had tiny maps, and SOCOM had broken AI, Delta Force is the premiere tactical shooter of the late 90s. Imagine Rainbow Six, worldwide, on giant outdoor maps. The size of the maps, quality of teammate command, and loadout choices combine to provide encounters that are never the same twice. Each mission can be approached from any direction. You can run into bases and twitch shoot, or you can paint it with air strikes, or you can snipe everyone, or you can send your buddies to get seen so you can ambush troops in valleys. The choice is always yours with Delta Force.

Delta Force 1 and 2 use a unique raycasting engine that directly draws voxels to screen every frame based on what you look at. In laymen, this means the game has infinite draw distance and maintains a consistent framerate on machines with absolutely no graphics hardware. This game will run on anything, provided its windows.

The only bad thing I can say about this game is that resolution is hard-coded to the executable, and the highest choice is 1024x768. To my knowledge, there are no tools to fix this or enable widescreen. It's a shame, because the voxel art style can get in the way when 1 game pixel is 4 on your actual monitor, and you have to force your monitor into 4:3 to stop stretching.

Still a great game, highly influential, was doing things with giant maps that games like Tribes would later get praise for. Every shooter fan should get this if they see it on sale.
