About This Game

This is a story of a man who has been closed in his own subconscious. In medicine we call that state "locked-in syndrome". The main character is going to solve all the riddles of his own conscious and find the way out or he'll stay there for good.
We're gonna live the life of a man who has been left alone in a really weird world where everything is happening according to its own rules. Who are we, what are we doing here, where are we going and what is this place we'll find out during the game.




FEATURES

•First person exploration
• Eerie high suspense atmosphere
• Shocking and immersive moments throughout the game
• Solve intuitive puzzles and clues

Basic Information
Title: Locked-in syndrome
Developer: Studio 48
Publisher: Studio 48
Genre: Horror

General Impression
I wish I could have rated this game higher, but I simply can’t. It is a rather decent walking simulator with some horror elements glued to it, but it controls so badly and the ghastly sounds themselves make Locked-in, a tedious affair. I apreciate its ambition and the fact that at least they added Trading Cards. This makes this game seem almost worthwhile, if bought on a cheap bundle. The puzzles aren’t half bad and neither are the graphical details. I will not dismiss its few redeeming points but they hardly could convince me to keep playing it any further.

Strong Points
+ Decent 3D graphics.
+ General atmosphere.
+ Steam Trading Cards.

Weak Points
- Random glitches.
- No Steam Achievements.
- Awful sounds.

Rating 5/10

Watch my "First Play on Launch Day!" Impressions Video Right Here:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8gFrqVnAcgk

After this game had an initial problem of not being able to actually download the game for most of it's first day after it launched, the developer finally got that issue fixed... Thanks Dev! :-)

The game offers a pretty typical horror theme affair, there is nothing in this game particularly special or that makes it stand out. In fact, it's fairly bland and is certainly offers nothing memorable, but it does what it offers fairly well and looks to offer an hour to an hour and a half of content. (I was able to complete 4 of the 7 sections of the game in about 40 minutes)

While not a great value with so little content included and nothing that makes it stand out, it's hard to recommend this game to anything but diehard fans of this sort of game. Maybe a sale price of 50% of more off the price would make it more appealing.

Don't expect anything here to scare you, it's just not scary in the least bit.. and I consider myself a "wimp" to scary games if that helps you understand.

While I played a good sport in the video, honestly I feel no desire to go back to finish the game as it just feels much more like a 'chore' then a 'fun' game to me. I think when I recorded the video, I was actually so surprised that the game worked as well as it did, I was just happy about that.

It's too short of content, it's controls are janky, the puzzles are more lame then challenging, and there is nothing here that will scare anybody in my opinion. In short, I wouldn't ask a friend to play this game even if it was free because I would consider their time alone wasted on this when there are so many better games out there with loads more content.

Add in the fact there is nothing procedural or random in this game means ZERO REPLAY VALUE.

Naw, I cannot recommend this game, sorry devs. There's many much better free games that do this better and offer more content and scare fun.

To keep this short and sweet. Locked-In Syndrome is defiantly NOT "a story of a man who has been closed in his own subconscious." There is no story. You basically just walk/jump around, so now I will contradict the "Features" of the game.
• ''First person exploration''- Yes it is.
• ''Eerie high suspense atmosphere''- Kinda, but not really. There's also some really annoying music at times.
• ''Shocking and immersive moments throughout the game''- Nope not even one.
• ''Solve intuitive puzzles and clues''- If you consider finding a key to open a door an "intuitive puzzle" then I guess so.
This game has many physics-based bugs where I got stuck countless times. Not to mention countless crashes, in the fact that the game doesn't even disable your mouse, so you're constantly clicking out of the game. Overall the game falls between bad and ok, but I don't recommend it.
