Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Whilst the game, content wise, is currently unfinished, we believe that what exists currently is a blast to play, alone or with friends. Arcade Mode alone is enough to hopefully whet player appetites and give them an idea of the total chaos and panic that will ensue after the game launches in full, later this year! Having a fan community before the full game launches would be fantastic, and is something we're hoping for!

Development on SBR! can be expedited through early player engagement, and as a bonus to us, we'll get to hear what everyone has to stay about the game and potentially what they'd like to see added.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“SBR! is due for release this year (2016), so how long it remains in Early Access is purely dependent upon how many new features players would like to see included after we complete the main core modes that we have planned.

Any additional features that the Steam community come up with, that we like the sound of, can be applied or integrated before final release.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Initially, we'll release modes to Early Access on a 'modular' basis. This means Arcade Mode will be available from the get-go, with Versus, Co-op, Beat Rush e.t.c either being made available soon after, or in partial form today.

Versus and Co-op modes are Local Machine based only at the moment, but Online functionality would be something we'd love to see implemented ideally in the months after SBR! releases in full.

Also, everybody loves loot, so maybe if enough players suggest things they'd love to see obtained and played for (maybe even tied to their Steam accounts via Trading Cards and Inventories), this paves the way for another dimension of value to the game's core.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“All of Arcade Mode is available now, with 50 bombs beckoning to be defused. An early iteration of the Rush Coin reward system is also in place, and any rewards/coins earnt, will remain unlocked and stashed throughout the game's later versions. Co-op Mode is now available.

Access to Versus Mode is currently limited.

Beat Rush Mode is currently inaccessible.

The ability to claim some of the game's Steam Achievements is also limited.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes.

During Early Access development, the game will be priced significantly lower.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We'd love to build a fan community for SBR! as early as possible, and enable those players to suggest anything to add to the game in terms of future modes or potential unlockables.

These suggestions can be made right here, on the SBR! Early Access forum.

We also encourage players to record Let's Plays and share their sessions with friends and family.
We think the game is best played with lots and lots people, all crowded around a keyboard and hyping each other up!”

About This Game

[SBR! is a constant work in progress. Some features are not yet complete] [Currently only supports 1920x1080 resolution]

SUPER addictive. SUPER challenging. SUPER BOMB RUSH!

A new era of hardcore Defuse 'Em Up has begun!

Keep calm as you defuse a barrage of bombs, each one more complex than the last, all within five seconds. Can you clear all 50 and proclaim yourself a Master of defusal? Just remember to REMAIN CALM!

Final Features:

  • Addictive, challenging, yet rewarding bomb defusal action!
  • Arcade Mode beckons with 50 intense bombs to conquer!
  • Versus Mode allows you to challenge a friend to a duel to the boom!
  • Co-op Mode allows you and a friend (or friends!) to work together to overcome the barrage as a team!
  • Beat Rush Mode is a unique, stage-based quest mode where you defuse to the beat!
  • Heaps of hidden Modes and secrets to unlock as reward for your dedication!
  • Delve deeper into SBR! by completing special Challenges that unlock super-secret content!
  • Music produced by ArgoFox. A soundtrack so full of pure energy and filthy bass, it should be made illegal!

Current Features:

  • Arcade Mode (complete) v0.5.4a
  • Versus Mode (currently not available) v0.5.4a
  • Co-op Mode (complete) v0.5.4a
  • Beat Rush Mode (currently not available) v0.5.4a
  • Various SBR! Challenges (some not yet available/Elite Challenges not yet available) v0.5.4a

All unlocks and game progress will carry over to new versions of SBR!

Some Steam Achievements are not yet unlockable.

Windows Xbox 360 controller support is enabled (on Windows).

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows Vista, 7, 8, 10
    • Processor: Intel Core i5-4460 (3.40 GHz) or better; Quad-core or better
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA GeForce GTX 650 (2GB) or better (DirectX 11 card Required)

    • Storage: 3 GB available space
    • Sound Card: DirectX 9.0c compatible sound card

Early Access Review
Posted: 27 February
I wasn't sure quite what to expect with this game, so I came into it with an open mind ready to play a casual game. Right away, I felt lost. There's nothing to indicate from the on-game screen or GUI exactly what the objective of the game is or how to play it.

A basic Google search for, "How to play Super Bomb Rush" led me to a post in the Steam discussion forum asking exactly the same questions that I had. "What do I do?" "How do I play" What's the objective?" I saw a response from the developer to, "adhere to the help menu," and of course, that was not at all helpful. Still at a loss, I went back to the game and looked at all the menus that were available to me. I came across the instructions stating to, "attack the directional arrows," but that just confused me even further. How can I attack the arrows on the left side of the screen when the bomb vibrates but doesn't move? Is it a racing game? A combat game? Tower defense? What the heck is this crazy little game?

I then came across a post from another player explaining that we were to, "copy the arrows." From there, I figured out exactly what this game is. It's like Simon, a game where you need very quick reactions to mimic button presses that are being randomly prompted by the game. Mimic the directional arrows with your joystick quickly enough, and the bomb is "diffused." This process then becomes more difficult with more complex combinations of arrows. Having gotten at least that much out of the way, I went back to the game prepared to diffuse some bombs.

It was at this point that my confusion turned into frustration and flat-out being underwhelmed by the entire experience. There were countless times that I knew I was completing the arrow combination correctly and quickly enough using the joystick on my XBox360 controller, and yet, the stupid little bomb blew up anyway. There just didn't seem to be any rhyme or reason to the game itself and no consistency to the controls.

Graphically, it was mediocre. The colors were bright and even distracting, but didn't appear to require much thought. However, when you're staring at the left side of the screen at the arrows, I guess it really doesn't much matter what's happening on the rest of the screen.

Audibly, there's also much to be desired. The sound effects give the impression of a classic standup arcade game from the 80s, and if you were gaming at that time like I was, then this bit of nostalgia might be appealing, but overall, there's just not much to it.

The user interface, sound design, and graphics all left a lot to be desired, and the lack of direction is noticeable to more players than just myself. If that weren't enough of a deterrent, the gameplay itself was inconsistent and underwhelming. I simply cannot recommend the game, but I do applaud the concept. It's got potential, but there's work to be done here.
