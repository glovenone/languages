Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Being a one man studio my most valuable asset is time, And I need a lot of it! With early access you can help me keep working full time on Megatect and get the features you want quicker, as well as request new features or changes while everything's still fluid. This is a game about letting you build what you want after all!

I also chose early access over a crowdfunding campaign since it lets me sell you something playable, rather than promises you can't trust me to keep.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“My plan is to hopefully be able to work on Megatect for at least a year. While the main core of what I want is already working, there's a lot that can still be added. For this game the early access banner is less about reaching version 1.0 and more about letting you know that more is still to come and requests are very welcome!”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“While the first few builds will still introduce some main core features, after that it will mainly be adding more to work with. I will still try to do something big for version 1.0 though!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently you can build grids alone or with friends and test out how setups will work for game mode. The next couple of builds will hopefully add in a grid sharing system as well as the ability to publicly host games. After that the sky's the limit!”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Possibly, depending on the popularity. Don't worry though, as the price will still remain very cheap. Since I have very little development costs I cant in good conscience charge as if I have a full team to pay.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“As much as possible! Feel free to request features or changes, Weather it be as complex as changing the way a wired block reacts or requesting a different style of chair! I'm not the best programmer though so some features may still be above my skill level!

And please share any pictures or videos with me of the things you create! I can't what to see what you can do!”

About This Game

Essentially, Megatect is a multiplayer map builder without limitation!

Use a plethora of blocks, objects, and wires to create your own challenge maps, arenas, new sports, whatever you can dream up! Play solo or with friends and share grids online for others to play!

Megatect is currently in early access. Being a one man development team means updates may be slow, but much more content is definitely on the way!

As of Version 0.13:

  • You can Build grids alone or with friends via direct connect IP (currently requires port forwarding, I'm looking for a solution)
  • Build your map with a large selection of blocks to work with, and many more coming!
  • Place special blocks such as spawn points, jump pads and teleporters
  • Place Guns, Pickups, and Ammo spawners
  • Use wires to connect doors to buttons, activate platforms or lasers, and generally create puzzles!
  • Launch the grid you're working on into Game Mode and play a match with your friends!
  • Customize your character with different colors and armor pieces
  • Change the time of day, world color and game properties from the world node
  • More stuff I've forgotten to mention!

Big future plans:

  • Public hosting: Host a map you made in a public lobby and let everyone join you in your own map!
  • Grid Sharing: Upload your gird for others to play and rate
  • vehicles!
  • Waypoints for races, navigation or as an end for your challenge maps
  • team based power cores and receivers for CTF style games
  • Even more stuff I've forgotten to mention!

Be sure to check http://timedropstudios.com/megatect for recent information, And always feel free to get in touch with me and share your ideas or to tell me how broken and awful it is!

I hope you enjoy Megatect, happy building!

Early Access Review
Posted: 21 May
With like 25h played, I think it's time for a little review.

First of all, I want to remind people this game is still in Early Access, and yes, it is still a bit "quiet" so to speak.

But, this game is pretty friggin' awesome!
You play a robot that can jump, run and shoot. That's it :)
See this game as a Quake vs. Minecraft
You get the Quake (team) deathmatches and the building blocks from Minecraft, neatly packed into a futuristic look.

There are some small islands available to choose from before starting to build, or you can build completely from scratch out at sea. Place and rotate blocks, give them different colours (like a red base for the red team and blue for the blue team) and several other colours in between.
Hopefully added is a simple "colour pad" where you can choose your desired colour as for now it is a bit limited.
There's foliage that you can plant, also on the metal-looking blocks, you can adjust the lighting for different themes and change the day/night cycle.

You can make traps with fire, hidden doors, teleports, jump-pads, lava basins, dripping acid, you can add timers to normally invisible "light paths", or have them constantly on, or with pauses, and it all works pretty straight-forward!
You can do the tutorial, but you don't have to be a genious to figure out basic building, not even with (timed) traps/constructions!

I'm impressed with how well things work so far, the looks (Unity) a pretty good and the lighting effects are nice.
The textures are really quite well (not so pixely as Minecraft) and the movements, also in build-mode very well working.
For those who love that everything connects, this game, too, is awesome as round objects such as arches merge prefectly with square blocks placed on top and on the sides!

Here's hoping that more options will be added to the game during the course of development (I'm not sure but I think it's just 1 guy doing this! That's impressive!!) and that the game will catch on so we can all look forward to old-school Quake-like Death Matches!

Before I forget: It says "Create your own sports", but as there are no really movable objects most ideas don't work out.
You can, for example, make a sort of "dodgeball" with guns instead of balls, but I think it'd be cool if there are movable objects, such as a ball, so (obviously with guns) you have to try and shoot the ball (literally) into a goal.

Conclusion: If you still have a soft-spot for old-school shooters such as Quake, and you love designing your own levels that you can share (either editable or non-editable) with the community, it's worth buying the game! It's really not that expensive and you will have a lot of fun designing, running through your levels, expand, change, adjust, run around again, place guns, shoot at your placed targets to open secret doors to rooms with better guns, and when you're satisfied that it all works: share :)

Now, I've played this game for nearly 2 hours when writing this review and it was the first time I have played, so let's get started with my first impressions and then follow through to a pros and cons section.

First Impressions:
So I first launched the game and found myself a launcher for it. I was a bit confused as I thought that the game would throw me right in. I hit the play button and nothing happened. "What?" I thought to myself as I quickly googled the problem and found no solution. I continued to attempt to "repair" and "redownload" the game from the launcher to no success. I then decided to check to see if the game was downloaded correctly from steam though the "Verify Game Cache" function in the games properties. I did this and found out that 7 files had not been downloaded. Withing 2-3 seconds I had those files and went back into the launcher of the game. It worked! I could download the game with no issues and finally got in.

Once the menu loaded I was blown away. The game, although in an early access stage, still had a very nice and sleek looking menu. The game looked and navigated quite professionally and is substantually better than some other early access games I have tried. After a bit of fiddling I managed to get the graphics to what my computer could run them at (max basically) and then decided to load up a world!

I was feeling a tab bit lazy so I decided to load up somebody else's creation. Very easy and simple to use navigation allowed me to load up somebody else's map that they had created. I could edit it, play on it, and even interact with it. From what I can see, nothing was missing from the user created map and everything was working just fine. From here on I got very excited. I wanted to make my own map. A map I could call home.

I loaded up a new map and got to work. After about an hour of learning the controls I had finally gotten a hang of it! I flew around adding blocks, destroying blocks, placing enemies, placing guns, and of course, placing jump pads. I finished a bit of my map with many jumping parkour areas and some more difficult jump pad areas to reach. Overall I got a fun map that I played on by myself for some time. I then decided that this wasn't enough. I needed a bit more of something "cool". I went into the menu to see what I could grab for myself and found hidden doors. Well, I used them as floors.

Once my map was finished I decided to see what else I could do with the game. The maps options were quite in depth with details such as: Gravity, Time of Day, Theme, and also Day/Night rotation being possible within the options. With my map done I decided to hop off the game and write a review, because overall the game amazed me. The graphics (although not spoken about much in this review, or even at all) are quite amazing and look very sleek. The character customization is fun and all of the weapons (although only a few so far) are perfect for an arena style shooter.

Now for the pros and cons:

Pros:
Great graphics
Fun gameplay
Good progress in the games development
Easy sharing of your games
Has mutliplayer functionality (although I haven't explored it yet)
Has good plans for the future of it's development
Developer shows good knowledge of creating games
Can be played alone/Co-Op/Multiplayer/ in PvE or PvP
Many fun user created maps for the game
Relatively cheap to buy and well worth it!

Cons:
It is only a 1 man developer team which worries me if he loses interest in developing the game.
The use of an independant launcher is cool, however steam updates would be a lot easier to keep track of from the users perspective.
No publically hosted games on official servers.

At first I wasn't able to play the game due to some NVIDIA issues, but those were promptly resolved by the developer. He is very dedicated to making this game both in his vision and in the collective vision of the players.

Now onto the game!

At first glance, this game seems very polished already. The theme of a futuristic, "build your dream" platform works very well with the mechanics that have been established. Do you want to make a puzzle grid and challenge other players? Megatect can do that. Do you like sports or racing and want to invite your friends to play in your custom-built stadium? Megatect endorses that. Do you want to make an unforgiving deathmatch arena to stomp other players? MEGATECT ENCOURAGES IT!

The pros and cons of this game are really in favor of pros.

+ Graphics
+ Aesthetics
+ Environment
+ Music
+ Creativity
+ Infinite possibilities

- Underdeveloped (but it's Alpha)
- No mode selection preset for grids yet (i.e. "Deathmatch", "Race", etc.)

Other than that, I highly recommend dropping the money for this game as its potential is as limitless as your own creativity. Workshop grids will also be available in the near future, and the fact that the Dev plans on allowing you to host your own server with your friends and build your worlds together is amazing in of itself. I wholeheartedly endorse this game!
