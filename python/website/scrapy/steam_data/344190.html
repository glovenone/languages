About This Game

Immortal Empire is a multiplayer tactics role playing game. Control a party of up to 5 immortals as you travel through a unique fantasy world, using spells and strategy to defeat your enemies.

Features
  • Play co-op with your friends, or destroy them in versus mode.
  • Unravel a mysterious storyline complete with fully voiced characters in the campaign.
  • Use automated matchmaking to compete in the PvP ladder.
  • Participate in official tournaments and win rewards.
  • Choose between 13 unique immortals, level up, collect loot, enchant your gear to fully customise your party.
  • Your dialog choices matter, the way you behave may form relationships or grant powerful character attibutes.
  • Try the challenging co-op Gauntlet mode where you must work together with other players to defeat enemy waves of increasing difficulty.
  • Use strategy and synergize spells to defeat challenging bosses.

    • Processor: Intel Core 2 Duo or equivalent

While it says I don't have much time shown on steam, I played this game years ago and have thoroughly put many, many hours into this game. Back then I just took whatever I wanted into each game. Now I can look back and say holy hell, this is such a great game.

The combat is a turn-based tactics-style strategy with some rpg elements thrown in. While there is a 'cash shop' (by what I remember) as you progress you can get more 'radiance' (the secondary currency for buying more characters) by simply completing levels and by daily quests. With enough time you can get pretty far.

The web-based version didn't have voice acting, and while the steam version does, I think it was still well done though not the greatest in the world. The story was great back then and I hope it added more over the years (I'll update this post once I get a few hours in :P)

The animation is something I really liked about this game, at least during the combat. They are clear and readable skills and spells, even the passive buffs are easily read with distinct colors. Each summon has their own sets of skills, and while two or more can heal there is specialists-- the priest being the main healer, the tree-ent the best tank as far as I remember it, and the druid doing the best sustained field-control with her summons and rocks that stun things for at least a turn. I didn't get a mage type summon until later on, but the nukers were there.

All in all one of my favorite games of all time that ever came from the internet-- well done Tactics Studio. :)

:Updated now that my account has been transfered and I've played it again:
This game is a VERY FUN party turnbased RPG. Especially for it being free. Dont let the tutorial fool you. You dont just right click enemies 5 times per turn. Those are level 1 characters with 1 ability and no mana/mana regen.

Gauntlet mode is insanely fun. 4 player Co-op survival against waves of enemies.

This is a REALLY REALLY good turn based strategy game. Its kind of simple at first, but the levels later on will have you trying multiple times to beat them because they can get hard. And not artificial difficulty. I mean you actually have out play the enemy. Despite alot of the classes being unlocked, you CAN earn the ingame currency to unlock them through challenges in missions and achievements. Or you can be like me and sell items from other games to not actually pay money out of pocket to unlock stuff anyways. On my Kongregate account I'd already bought 2 extra character slots and had about 250 Radiance left over. I dont think there were achievements before, and it reset the campaign progress for me, so I can just earn that Radiance over again and keep my old stuff B)

The story seems to have been updated and the UI may have seen some work, but the UI is still a little bit weak and all of the menus are a bit much to take in all at once, but it's still being updated after all these years so that's a major plus. I always really like games where you can build your character in different ways. I have a Valkyrie that is built for tanking even though it's 'supposed' to be a dps class. I'm currently not constantly playing this because I'm waiting for my account to be transfered to my steam account. That and Warhammer + KF2 just updated and all my friends are sucking me into that. But believe me, I probably have ~100 hours in the game from like 2011. This is a fun game if you like any of the tags: Turn based / RPG / Party based / Strategy, you'll enjoy this game if you play past the tutorial.

Decent co-op TBS with a good ammount of single player content. Perfectly playable without spending money but like all FTP it's a question of how long you can resist the temptation to splurge.
There are a couple of opening tips that the game doesn't make clear and you will want to take note of:
Each time YOU level up you can only level a single skill/attribute/feature of a single character. This means you will be most useful if you only ever have one character. Your first character's name will also be YOUR name as far as your community presence goes. The first thing you should do when you start this game is hit the "?" and read the help notes. Or you will want to start over from scratch very quickly. Many players prefer to play with a single character as this provides the fastest way to create a powerful presence for gauntlet games. I have revised my opinion on this because making a party for your first account is more fun, familiarises you with hero types, and you lose nothing by it because you can still create "solo" accounts later and use your party account for farming. "radience" the in game currency is effected across all accounts linked to your steam acct and items can be passed down through other players. Given that it's "free" it's certainly well worth a look. But be prepared for some grind.

Immortal Empire is a game I have found myself enjoying, it is not often that we get strategy turn based RPG's that are free to paly, and even rarer that we get one that is not complete garbage. In Immortal Empire you play as an immortal or party of immortals and fight your way through the campaign alone or with co-op. So far the story seems to be primarily about riding the world of this corruption that its found itself in, as of me writing this review I have not progressed very far in the story. The immortals themselves are your playable heroes who respawn after death, there is a pretty decent variety of immortals to chose from, the majority falling under standard RPG class system, dps assassins, melee tanks, healers, supports and even a few immortals who have hybrid skill sets.

The combat is turnbased and functions around a unique resource called time, not only does movement and attack run off of this resource, so do most ability cool downs, so when running a large party its always good to have an idea what order you should use your skills in. When it comes to game modes, aside from the campaign, there is gauntlet mode, from what I can tell you try to survive with a group of co-op players against waves of enemies for increasingly valuable rewards. I have not played much gauntlet as of this review. Immortal Empire also has PvP or some sort, but I have not seen or tried PvP at this time.


Overall I feel like Immortal Empire balances RPG gameplay and strategy in a way that makes it a fun co-op game, and would recommend it to people looking for a combination of co-op, strategy and RPG in some way, shape or form.

This game is really good - old style tactical RPG. Provides lots of fun! It has great potential. At the moment of the review there were not much of game content yet for a MMO. I really hope that this game gets futher development!
Pros:
+ Many characters: some you can play from the beginning, some you will unlock by playing the game
+ The "VIP" in-game currency is easily earned by playing the game, so you can farm it to unlock a new character, like, each day!
+ Arena "gauntlet" system is really fun - facing waves of monsters with friends, and at higher levels a simple mistake may get the whole team killed!
+ Nice plot
+ No restrictions on player inteaction: free trade, free partying etc - play with friends or random people as much as you want!
+ Your character is as powerful as you design him - how good you are at the game depends on your build, skills and choices - and not on random.

Cons:
- Game is quite short, and mostly you end up farming gauntlet arenas, which become repetetive, as they are not randomized.
- Again, lack of content: all items look the same with some little exceptions, monsters and player characters have all the same spells and skills. Hopefully it gets repaired with futher development.
- It might be tricky to learn to play for new players: most likely you will have to start over after first attempt of playing, considering your past mistakes. Not sure if this is a con though.
- Lack of players: there are probably not more than 100 players online at peak moments. This game deserves more attention, however, it should be filled with more content to get it.

For anyone who like RPGs and tactical games - this one will surely grab your attention for at least a week. It is worth time playing and getting more support and development!
