About This Game

Deceived by the forces of evil into prematurely bringing about the end of the world, War – the first Horseman of the Apocalypse – stands accused of breaking the sacred law by inciting a war between Heaven and Hell. In the slaughter that ensued, the demonic forces defeated the heavenly hosts and laid claim to the Earth.

Brought before the sacred Charred Council, War is indicted for his crimes and stripped of his powers. Dishonored and facing his own death, War is given the opportunity to return to Earth to search for the truth and punish those responsible.

Hunted by a vengeful group of Angels, War must take on the forces of Hell, forge uneasy alliances with the very demons he hunts, and journey across the ravaged remains of the Earth on his quest for vengeance and vindication.

  • Apocalyptic Power – Unleash the wrath of War, combining brutal attacks and
    supernatural abilities to decimate all who stand in your way
  • Extreme Arsenal – Wield a devastating arsenal of angelic, demonic and Earthly
    weapons; and blaze a trail of destruction atop Ruin, War’s fiery phantom steed
  • Epic Quest – Battle across the wastelands and demon-infested dungeons of the
    decimated Earth in your quest for vengeance and redemption
  • Character Progression – Uncover powerful ancient relics, upgrade your weapons,
    unlock new abilities, and customize your gameplay style
  • Battle Heaven and Hell – Battle against all who stand in your way - from war-weary
    angelic forces to Hell’s hideous demon hordes

I got around to playing this game after grabbing it in the 2016 Summer Sale. I gotta admit - for a game six years old, I was pleasantly surprised! I watched a trailer and purchased the game assuming that I was going to get a mindless hack and slash experience. I DID get that experience - and got so much more.

Darksiders is a great entertaining distraction with an interesting character and plot. The game wonderfully integrates a great blend of genres - the hack and slash of Devil May Cry, the puzzles and exploration of Zelda, Progression of Metroidvania, and even some shooter elements. As soon as I was tired of hacking things to bits in 50+ hit combos, I was rewarded with a couple hours of puzzle solving and dungeon exploration. That back and forth continued throughout the game.

That said, the game is far from perfect. The end game collect-a-thon grew very old very fast. I can't help but think this was a dropped ball by Vigil Games narratively and gamplay-wise. It felt as if they took the "easy way out." Go collect these four hearts...and then collect the seven pieces of the sword...and then go fight the boss, which requires you to fight the four mini bosses before hand. Darksiders is set in a cosmic world with compelling ingredients - interesting characters, unique plot, and a blend of mechanics that somehow falls short in the end.

I also went for 100% completion to get all the achievements, and the achievements were a drag to get. I'm a completionist at heart and prefer gaming that way, but I felt like an idiot at the end killing spiders over and over again to farm money and experience for the sake of the achievement and not the sake of the game. I felt even dumber riding the horse for 100 miles -- two hours of rotating the joystick while I watched TV.

All in all, though, for $4 -- Darksiders does not disappoint. A great distraction that holds up wonderfully, even six years later. I'll pick up the second one eventually, but not for a while. The 100% achievement collecting kinda burned me out.

I have completed Darksiders three times now. It doesn't really amaze me as a game in any way. The graphics are cartoonish and just about worthy of the Xbox 360 (not a compliment), the gameplay is formulaic and platformy reminiscent of a Playstation 2 game (that's sort of a compliment) and the story-line and script is about as impressive as Michael Bay at his best (not really a compliment either), although having said that its "twist" ending keeps things at least a little fresh.

Despite all this, to this day I find something in Darksiders simplicity that I like. Maybe it is simply the pleasure in experiencing the humble origins of its overly-ambitious sequel, perhaps it's that typical millenial love of wandering a post-apocalyptic Earth, or maybe it's the playing of a story-line (which will likely never be finished) based on the idea of ageless god-like creatures on a self-serving mission to resurrect the annihilated human race.

There is nothing new or groundbreaking about Darksiders, and although it was one of THQ's larger releases it is not at all a game that was made to be a money spinner. Somebody loved Darksiders, and in 2016 I still do. The level design is varied in a predictable sort of way (forest level, desert level, city level, scary level), the bosses which you hunt are quite interesting but again nothing new (giant spider, giant bat) and a lot of the game's completion relies on that obsession with collectibles and point gathering that 2006-2010 games are so infamous for.

This game however IS charming. And it did give us the bug-riddled but excellent Darksiders II. If you are a fan of the latter or you think that the Playstation 2 was the best games console to grace God's green Earth, pick up this game.

Man.. What do I say about Darksiders.. Well, it is by no means a bad game. The gameplay and combat is clean; the puzzles are intriguing enough; the voice acting is fairly well done (especially work done by Mark Hamill); and most enemy designs are intuitive and fun to fight. Speaking of enemies, instant killing enemies is such a joy!

However...

Darksiders is woefully uninspired. There is absolutely NOTHING original about this game. Everything about the combat, the puzzles, the level/game design, the level up schematic, and even the way you ride your horse (which you won't do very often) is borrowed from other game designs. If you have every played God of War and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time you will quickly pick up on the tropes and cliches that this game uses. Also, the bosses are simple and gimmicky. Not to mention that the game is fairly easy, even on hard. Lastly, the lore is mildy interesting but the plot and story execution are weak and it just feels like set up for the sequel.

Final Word:
Don't take my criticism as me not liking the game. I did enjoy it but it is far from being a great game. If you like linear hack and slash action games like God of War and Devil May Cry then you will enjoy this game. However, if you are looking for something fresh, new, and innovative, then Darksiders simply won't fit the bill.

P.S I recommend playing with a controller; better layout and feel.

Happy Gaming!
