12 March

Version 1.03

Dear oil barons
The last update on Oil Enterprise was some time ago as we have been completely focusing on finishing the game. We are now being supported by the publisher astragon Entertainment and will very soon be able to reveal the release date. Thanks to this collaboration, our game will also be available as a "real" boxed version from all well-known retailers.

In version 1.03 we have:
  • new scenario "Siberian Blues"
  • fixed minor bugs in the German language version;
  • reworked the English language version;
  • added French, Spanish and Russian translations;
  • removed the incomplete Polish community translation.
Additionally, and rather unusually for a business simulation, we have made Oil Enterprise fully playable using a controller. This currently still appears as "partial support" as a few graphics are still missing for "full support" according to Steam, but we will check this.
And two other minor bug fixes:
  • points for quick mission completion are only awarded if the scenario has been completed;
  • building rotation was saved incorrectly on some oilfields. This has been corrected.

About This Game

In Oil Enterprise, you enter the multi-million dollar business of black gold as a shrewd businessman. Accept the challenge and grow from a small-scale en trepreneur, investing in your first oilfield, to a global oil magnate.

Acquire oil production rights in up to 15 regions around the world and use the potential of your oil reserves as efficiently as possible on the dynamic global market. Fulfill lucrative long-term contracts to improve your reputation and make your cash registers ring. But watch out! Make a wrong investment or fail to meet con-tractual obligations and your company could be in danger of going bankrupt.

Features:

  • Invest in new oilfields, pumps, refineries, storage facilities, maintenance buildings, logistics and much more
  • Constantly expand your headquarters
  • There's no such thing as a free lunch! Stay in the black or take out a loan if needed! Always keep an eye on your numbers
  • Various events, such as earthquakes, exchange rate fluctuations and ac-cidents, provide new challenges
  • 15 global regions, 90 oilfields and 20 building types
  • Two mentors will let you in on the secrets of the oil industry
  • Improve your reputation to gain access to even more lucrative long-term contracts
  • Dynamic global market
  • 20 different scenarios, free play and multiplayer mode for up to six players

A Modern Oil Management Simulater
https://youtu.be/ylhTZJrBOXY
+ A pretty nice and relaxing soundtrack
+ A great tutorial, easy to learn, and the user interface and clean and simple
+ The gameplay mainly consists of building a pumpjack, refinery, pumping station, and storage unit. Once you have these you will start gaining barrels of oil each day which you can sell on the market or take out contracts. Contracts are far more profitable, but if you fail to meet the daily quota you will be hit with some very heavy fines. The goal of the game is to reach a certain amount (example: $10million), and you fail if you go negative $1million or stay negative for more than 12 months. (Which is surprisingly easy to do if you fail contracts. Overall it's pretty relaxing and quite engaging as you try to get the maximum amount of contracts possible without running out of barrels of oil.
+ Some neat mechanics later on where you can buy licenses to drill in other countries with different biomes and real-world perks like cheaper labor / construction costs. You can also build up a headquarters which are large research buildings that improve your structures.

- At some points it becomes a waiting game where you just wait a long while for your contracts to finish, and to raise enough money.
- There are some bugs in the game. At one point I had over 7,000 barrels, and it randomly said that I failed to supply enough for a 300 barrels / day contract, which fined me $1million and I ended up failing the scenerio and had to restart.
- Feels a bit too basic, I would have liked to have been able to build pipes to transfer the oil, or at least see people working or transporting oil below. Even when a station breaks down nothing really happens you just have to wait. I would have liked to have seen more animations in the building process, and perhaps more unexpected disasters or events. Although these are just minor things.

2) Music is the techno equivalent of elevator music

3) No vehicles or construction or roads or anything you just plop down facilities wherever you want.

4) Extremely shallow financial/production information.

5) No staff management at all. You can see little oil workers drawn in parking lots and outside of buildings but you don't hire them and they don't move. I guess it is just implied that you have staff.

It looks good but it's a tablet game at best and the type of game you'd play while taking a long dump.
