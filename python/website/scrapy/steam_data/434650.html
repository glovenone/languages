Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Early Access will help us to get player feedback and continuously improve the game until final release. It allows us to connect with the players and the community, so we can reach out to the community for ideas and suggestions to make the game even better.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“2-4 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We plan to focus on the following during the Early Access period.
  • New chapter for the continuation of the story
  • New enemies and challenging bosses
  • Better combat experience.
  • New cool items
  • New weapons and armor.
  • Better music and sound.
  • New abilities in Sacrifice system
  • Online coop (depending on player's opinions)
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current version is an stable beta. All mechanics fully functional and most the features and gameplay are present. You can enjoy the singleplayer campaign or co-op for two players locally.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Once the game leave the Early Access stage the price will be raised.
For the reasons below:
1. Make sure the people who supported us early are rewarded for doing so.
2. Considering the funds we raise during Early Access will be largely used for further development.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The Steam Community forums will be the main source of feedback during Early Access.
We will actively read and consider players' suggestions. Help us make our game better!”

About This Game

Lost Castle is an action RPG beat'em up with roguelike elements and randomized dungeons.

Prologue:
Castle Harwood used to be a castle governed by nobles. Unfortunately, evils had come upon the land and ended the calm days. Peoples were tortured, enslaved, murdered by demons. Gradually, demons wanted a bit more and began to invade surrounding areas. For the humanity, honor and treasures, the braves from far and near come continuously enter Harwood to find the root of all evils...
In the game, you play as a treasure hunter who is also plan to enter Harwood. You want to uphold justice with the sword in your hand, and, by the way, take some treasures. However, Harwood has been eroded by evil. Will everything be as you wish?

Key Features:
  • Rogue-like and massive random contents.
  • 70+ items and 50+ potions with various effects.
  • 100+ weapons and 50+ armor. Various attack actions with different types of weapons.
  • Randomly generated dungeons, items, enemies and bosses.
  • Sacrifice system: Level up your hero and gain new abilities.
  • Singleplayer campaign or co-op for two players.

So far this is one of my favorite games of this genre.
game is currently on v0.22 beta and doing great so far.

The Game

The game has the fighting style of Castle Crashers, being a 2d beat'em up, except in lost castle you'll need to be more careful since you cannot jump and do an endless combo like Castle Crashers.

The progression style is really similar to Rogue Legacy, since this game is a rogue-lite, you collect souls from monsters and when you die, in your next run you get to spend them to upgrade your characters or your "base", you can upgrade the blacksmith to give more/better weapons, or the pharmacist or the thief who provides random treasures, and when you start a new run all your souls are lost so you have to spend them on upgrades "like rogue legacy"
Every time you get a random character with a random weapon and stats.

There's a various selection of weapon types, -> bows, Magic wands, heavy type, sword+shield, knives, shotguns, spears and each one of them can be different, such there's a fire, poison or ice wand, same goes for all the other weapons.
Every weapon gives you a random active ability to use.

There are a lot of items you can find, hp items, throwing daggers, boomerangs, random effect potions which I found to be pretty cool since one of them transformed me into a werewolf and all the effects and animations were really outstanding.

You can find pickups that gives passive effects "on the current run only" they have a lot of effects that can raise stats or gives other boosts, or you can find a dino egg that gives you a cute small dinosaur that fights beside you and can grow bigger if you feed it drumsticks,

Level progression requires you to kill all the monsters in the room to unlock the path to the next place, it also can give you other doors to enter and get a different path.

Pros
+ Good level designs, music, effects.
+ replay-value with it being randomized, giving huge selection of weapons and abilities.
+ Progression system similar to rogue legacy which I personally love.
+ Thrilling boss fights with great intro's.
+ Challenging difficulty.
+ A lot of nice pickups that gives various abilities and positive/negative effects.
+ Buttload of achievements, devs even added 25+ today (7/8/2016)
+ Local Coop is fun to play with a friend Developers have stated that they will be adding online coop eventually


Cons
- Poor translations, text aligning..etc, several typos, but this is no biggie.
- I am not a huge fan of character designs, since you'd usually get a character with small "Asian" stereotypical eyes, or a weird hair color that doesn't match the outfit.
- Character animations are good but they sort of strike me out as a "Mobile game" look, which isn't bad I guess but I do hope they enhance it a bit.
- a few animation bugs in certain characters.
- a bug when the blacksmith had only 1 weapon, sometimes when I pick it up causes the game to enter an endless pickup animation+sound that I can only escape by alt+f4, I don't know if they have fixed this or was it automatically fixed for me after upgrading blacksmith into having 2 weapons per run.

Overall
The game is really great, I am having so much fun playing it, its such an outstanding rogue-lite so far into their early access journey, updates are coming frequently and I cannot WAIT for online coop.

So far ? The game is definitely worth its full price, you get what you paid for and more, I do believe they should have made the price 9.99$ because it gives a lot content and replay-ability that it deserves that price.

And if you read the pros and cons, you'll realize the con's in the game are not really that big, however I do have to point out that some players did say they felt the game being "repetitive" after a while, which I didn't find that at all considering it is a rogue-lite and there is progression and a final level, so it should give hours of fun and waiting for more updates is a plus.

Early Access Review
Posted: 28 June
Lost Castle is a side scrolling beat-em-up and RPG hybrid with roguelike features. It’s inspired by Castle crashers. It has roguelike elements and every time you play a different dungeon is presented to you. The game is in Early access, so there’s a few glitches etc. but not a big deal. The game is fully playable. The developers are pretty active which means the glitches should be fixed very soon.

Graphics:

Lost Castle feature cartoon-like graphics and art-style. The graphics are very sharp and pleasing to the eyes. The characters are cute and kept reminding me of Scribblenauts characters for some reason. There are not many graphics settings in the game, the only one available is the resolution adjustment option and full screen option. The game ran smooth for me throughout my gameplay with it with over 60 FPS.

Sound:

The music in this game is very neat. I really liked the creepy type of music this game features. There could’ve been more tracks though. Sound options have separate music and sound adjustment options.

Controls:

The game has fully re-bindable keys for both keyboard and controller. The mouse works in-game menus and the upgrades page. The controls felt really responsive. A controller is highly recommended for this game though I had no problem with keyboard as well.

Gameplay:

The story is about demons and other evil creatures have come to the Castle Harwood and your character is a treasure hunter trying to get rid of the evil in the Castle and in the way loot some treasure. Your character gets kidnapped by these evil elves who throw you into the dungeon. That’s where you start every time you die. You’re given a random looking character with a random weapon. Depending on the weapon you’re given you get different normal, heavy and super attacks. The dungeon is also randomly created. There’s 5 levels from what I can tell (didn’t progress through the later levels) and every time you die you start at the first dungeon cell. Each time you clear a screen a door opens and you’re given a chest with some random items in it. The chests also upgrade from wood to silver to gold and so on as you progress through level. Along the way you can also find a dodgy salesman every once in a while who will sell you a random thing for some coins. This can be an apple or a very nice weapon.

There’s a lot of different types of weapons, bows, dual welded swords, long heavy swords, shotgun, fireballs, sword and shield combo etc. Every weapon has their own stats and abilities, for example the bow and arrow has a super attack which shoots an electrocuted arrow which passes through enemies. The fireball weapon lets you teleport if you use the heavy attack or a crystal spawns and shoots fireballs at all nearby enemies with the super attack button. Some weapons have dash or evade or teleportation abilities instead of heavy attacks. You can also find companions who follow you and shoot at enemies and other upgrades like a percentage chance to electrocute enemy etc. Armor pickups can not only alter the stats they can change the look of your treasure hunter entirely with different themes and cosmetics.

You gain souls by killing enemies, these souls can be used to get upgrades and unlocks after you die. You start off pretty weak but as you gain levels and get additional bonuses using these souls you get better and better. Though as you get upgrades the other upgrades become expensive and if you cannot afford to buy one your souls will be sacrificed. One of the upgrades does let you keep a certain percentage of your souls though.

There’s a lot of enemy variety. Skeletons, goblins, spiders, slime creatures. The game has some hilarious mechanics too like unknown potions which can either be good or bad. One of the potions has you pooping everywhere which reduces your move speed for a short time. Other funny things like you slipping off your recently eaten banana’s peel, making your vulnerable to enemies, or chest falling on your head and other such things. Plenty of bosses in the game as well. Consumable items include apples, bananas, finger lickin’ good chicken (yes, there’s a reference to KFC in the game as well) all of which restore some of your HP. Throwable weapons includes daggers, bombs etc. Potions are there as well and usually random which can either poison you, make you fart uncontrollably, restore health and many other things. So it’s always a gamble using these potions.

A short gameplay video can be found below:

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=713112176

Graphics: 8/10
Sound: 8/10
Controls: 9/10
Gameplay: 9/10
Overall: 9/10

Conclusion:

This game is an extremely fun dungeon crawler/brawler/ /RPG/Roguelike combo. This game is very addictive and you’ll have that “just one more game and I’ll quit, yet you keep playing for hours on end” syndrome. It also has 2 player local co op, so you can play with friends as well. It’s not that expensive and for the amount of content it has I think it’s very much a must buy for brawler/RPG genre fans.
