Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“I'm putting Corona MotorSport in Early Access because I want to build an audience before the game is released and have them help to make it better experience for future gamers.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Corona MotorSport will remain in Early Access for about 4-5 months depending on the amount of help we get from hiring people to having people in the community help out”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The final version will be a lot different from early access. I plan for the game to get more realistic vehicle physics and even better graphics as well as seamless multiplayer and storyline integration. I want the game to have a professional look and feel, to make the gamer think this is truly a unique experience.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Corona MotorSport is in its early stages of development, even though it began being developed about a year and a half ago. it will take about 4 months to fully finish the storyline and multiplayer modes of the game.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, the final release price will most likely increase as we continue to add new features every update”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I plan on greatly involving the Steam Community to help me finish the game. Im looking for people to provide feedback, point out bugs, make suggestions, and even help with designing the game such as making textures or modeling tracks/vehicles. I would also need people to help translate the game into their own native languages, so far we have people volunteered for Russian and Spanish.”

About This Game


Introducing Corona MotorSport, the newest independently developed and published racing game on the market. Corona MotorSport offers a great combination of beautiful graphics and simulation-grade physics. Don't miss your opportunity to be part of the development process; purchase a copy, support the devs and enjoy the ride!


Tri-Dimensional Tires
Unlike other Unity games, which use Wheel Colliders that only detect a single point on the wheel that create an unrealistic look and feel, this algorithm recognizes the entire wheel model for beautiful suspension simulation and realistic handling.

Next-Gen Graphics
Corona MotorSport features top-of-the-line graphics with constant tweaks and improvements each update.
  • Screen Space Raytraced Reflections (SSRR) - Adds onto existing reflections for more realism
  • Dynamic Time and Weather system - Drive in rain, sun, night or day!
  • Image Effects - Bloom, Color Grading, Tonemapping and more come together to create a beautiful racing experience
  • Physically based shading
  • DirectX 11

Real-Time Damage Physics
Using precise mesh calculations and hinge physics, realistic damage is now possible in-game with no lag as well as detachable parts. (Tire deflation, wear and powertrain damage coming soon)

Tracks
  • Monza, Italy - Long track with challenging curves and a short circuit layout
  • Daytona, Florida - Oval circuit with road course
  • More coming soon

Vehicles
  • Serato Conquistador - powerful Italian sports car
  • Factory Five Mark IV Roadster - Officially licensed Factory Five Cobra replica
  • Factory Five Type 65 Coupe - Officially licensed Factory Five Daytona replica
  • Tonnere Serpent - French supercar
  • Chevette G1 - American Classic car
  • Nyzan GTX - Japanese sportscar
  • Doge Hellfire - American 4 door Muscle car
  • Trophy Truck - powerful offroad vehicle
  • Serato Stretto - Formula one car
  • Obey V10 - AWD German sportscar

    • OS: Windows XP

This game was first on sale in 2013 (on Amazon). It is still in alpha. Judge for yourself.

Update May 28, 2015: The dev team shrinks from [one or] two teens to [definitely] one [see comment section]. (source)

Update May 20, 2015: CMS v2.0 alpha (fresh out)
Currently, the game has a single mode, which is freeroam, with no AI and no way to run timed laps. The available tracks are Monza and Daytona, and Daytona looked glitchy to me; someone put advetising billboards on the tracks in Monza, creating traffic hazards. There are 7 camera positions per car, which includes an interior cam, but there isn't a bumper cam for all of the models (well, technically, there is, but a "back bumper cam" is quite useless for racing), and many of the positions are unsuitable for driving. The deformable tire physics seem gone, and the only bit I was able to deform on the Lamborghini clone was the front. Note that right now, you'll be driving in the dark some of the time, without any street lights. Judging from its current state, it's still going to be years until the game lives up to the promises made on the store page.

Sadly, I am now the reviewer with the most hours clocked on the game (and that's not even counting the beta version I own).

The next generation of indie racing games is here
Get behind the wheel of 5 (more added every week) exotic cars and 2 licensed Factory Five Racing cars and wreak havoc! There are currently 3 modes planned for CMS. Freeroam, where you can go crazy on the dozens of maps with every car, truck, helicopter, and plane unlocked. Multiplayer (coming soon) where you can compete against your friends in races or freeroam around the massive city with up to 16 players. and Story Mode: An action packed, tragic, and emotional story of a young aspiring pro racer that ended up in the wrong place and the wrong time(coming soon).
  • I'm skipping over the issues I mentioned above.
  • 7 cars now, but they're not being added by the week
  • Currently, there's one game mode: free roam.
  • Currently, there are two maps.
  • No trucks, helicopters or planes.
  • No multiplayer.
  • No races.
  • No city, massive or otherwise.
  • No story.

Autodrome Di Monza - A nice track with plenty of straights and challenging curves
Nurburgring Nordschleife - A dangerous, 20km track in Germany with plenty of opportunities to wreck - Coming Soon

Dunsfold Airport - Airfield in Dunsfold, England. Now used as a test track for cars - Coming Very soon
Bathurst Circuit - Coming soon
  • Tracks are Monza and Daytona.

Features

Tri-Dimensional Tires
Unlike other Unity games, which use Wheel Colliders that only detect a single point on the wheel that create an unrealistic look and feel, this algorithm recognizes the entire wheel model for beautiful suspension simulation and realistic handling.

Next-Gen Graphics
The Unity 5 update (Alpha 2.0) brings great new features such as Global Illumination, Real-Time vehicle/environment reflections, Dynamic Skies, improved Anti-Aliasing, Bloom, DirectX11 (coming very soon), and much more!

Real-Time Damage Physics
Using precise mesh calculations and hinge physics, realistic damage is now possible in-game with no lag as well as detachable parts to make you feel guilty wrecking your car!

Unrivaled Aircraft Physics
