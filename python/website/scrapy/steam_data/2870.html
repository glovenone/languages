About This Game

In the distant future, the X universe faces a period of profound and irrevocable change. While the universe stumbles towards an uncertain future, countless adventures await as new enemies rise in search of power. Enter a young adventurer and his unlikely female ally traveling in an old, battered ship with a glorious past - two people alone against the galaxy, playing a key part in the events to come. The fate of the universe rests in the hands of the player...

X Rebirth represents a spectacular new beginning for the X series, offering loyal fans and newcomers alike a vast, vivid and extremely detailed universe to explore throughout the game. Varied gameplay elements such as action-packed space combat, detailed trading mechanics and high-speed space exploration with fast travel allow players to experience a truly unique space saga that will keep them hooked for dozens of hours. X Rebirth features a whole new user experience - it is easier than ever to start with, and remains as deep as fans expect it.

The learning curve for this game is **staggering.** It is not for the feint of heart. It has very many complex systems available for you to play with, but nothing in-engine gives you a really good grasp of how or why you would want to use these systems. You will restart the campaign, probably more than once. The character models are atrocious for a game of this era; for example, women are either bald, or have a hairstyle lifted straight off Bob from ReBoot, and they all look old (as in aged, not dated). There is a first-person navigation system when docked at stations where you walk around and talk to merchants and other NPCs, and it's really f-ing awful. There is a mechanic to allow you to conduct the vast majority of business from your ship, saving you from this, but it comes with a significant resource penalty. The story is also a mess with a protagonist who quickly becomes unlikable because he's making decisions with his ♥♥♥♥♥ instead of his brain, getting you involved in a lame war that you're not really equipped to fight unless you knew this outcome ahead of time. The HUD also lacks appropriate customization options, such as color or font sizes. There is an object targeting system which is not at all intuitive.

However, if you can gain proficiency in using and interacting with all the various systems and world-building available, there is a really interesting and deep game here; there's a lot of callbacks to great space shooters from the past like Freespace and Freelancer and the game's world is indeed vast with lots and lots (and lots!) of beautiful space scenery. The different systems in the game are convoluted, but they do all more or less work once you've grasped the logic. The game does have tutorials, but they're bleak and lacking in explanation as to what value the tasks it teaches you have in gameplay. Trial-and-error is going to be your best means to learn systems in this game unfortunately. The one thing I wish this game had but doesn't is planetary bases and missions. There's nothing in here like that, but there are still quite a large number of things to do and places to see, so it's not that bad in that regard.

I'd recommend this game at a sale price, but I have a hard time recommending it at full retail due to the steep learning curve that will probably turn off most casual players. If you want a space shooter but not something so involved, Strike Suit Zero is a better choice. But if you want a little Skyrim or Fallout with your space shooter and are willing to put in some real time figuring out this game's systems, it's worth a look.

(v4.0 w/ HoL and TO expansions)

X3:TC and X3:AP set a high bar. X:R doesn't reach it, but is still a decent game. On its surface, it seems like a straightforward upgrade to the X3 games. The same core mechanics are there: you fly around in a universe with rich lore, trading, fighting, or exploring, whatever suits your fancy.

Lots of people have gripes about mechanical issues of the game, like the highway system. That doesn't bother me. The universe is less dynamic than X3 (pirates don't really affect the economy, for example, they just constantly send small squadrons to die). In some ways, the mechanics improved: the addition of surface elements to capital ships is a really great addition to combat that makes small ships pack a huge punch if used effectively. But these minor issues aside, the real problem with X:R versus previous X games is lack of content.

By content, I don't mean amount of space to explore: there's plenty of that. But there are relatively few types of ships and stations to buy, and you can only fly one ship, which is bad, although you can mostly get used to it. The amount of lore and numbers of factions is cut down significantly from previous games.

By far the biggest problem is the relative lack of subquests or subplots. Once you've beat the campaign, the game is completely open-ended. Not open-ended like Skyrim, where the only problem is choosing which of the thousands of quests to do. Open-ended as in, there essentially ARE no other quests (except repetitive, randomly generated ones), and sure you can build stations or blow up stuff, but it all feels quite pointless. The pinnacle of X3 was quests like the Hub Plot and Player Station, which gave awesome rewards in return for huge amounts of financial engineering. In X:R, there are a few sub-quests that give you cool rewards, but they are all of the form "find these items randomly hidden throughout the universe". It would be enormously tedious if not impossible without internet guides, and with them it becomes trivial.

So, in future expansions, it would be nice to add ships or factions. But what the game really needs to step things up a notch is things to DO in the X Universe. It's still worth $30 but not yet worthy of the X Series IMO.

First off, Get it, I think you will like it a lot epecially as its not nearly as expensive or load intensive as Eve Online or Star Trek...

If you're new to the modability of the X universe you probably don't know this but, the X3 universe had great components to it. It still had profound limitations that the raw Vanilla or un-moded program had that confined what a mod script could actually do. Like you couldn't make the text size bigger very easily...

Why? To many conflictual errors that bleed into other scripts. I imagine that XRebirth will still have parts that a mod can not change and will have to come down the pipe later from the dev's. For this reason, I will add all my comments as I have played with about 80-90 mods installed right now.

Pro's

GRAPHICS: See system info at bottom of review.
- This is a very immersive space sim, much more so than X3, and most other space sims ever could be.
- It's visually astounding in many ways w/(ULTRA SETTINGS) and I love that you can not only pilot ships but that you can move around them.
- I can't say anthing has seemed "glitchy", produced atrifacts, or caused the texture panels to split when you are close to them at just the right angle like a lot of open world games have issues with. It's refreshing in that arena for sure.

SHIP/STATION DYNAMICS:
- Stations are vastly more complex, beautiful, and formitable in combat.
- The change from weapon "energy usage p/sec" to "over heating" is and interesting componnent that makes combat more user skill dependent and less "cookie cutter" when piloting fully out-fitted ships.
- In the "skunk", M6 Corvette class, your 5 main assult weapons have their own unique applications and it makes for some more realism. Sometimes I'd switch to the wrong weapon and well, if I were a space pirate, in the heat of battle, I might just do that so.... again... more immersiveness.
- Sheilds, Engines, and Weapons can also be modified to fit your play style, tanky high sustainability, high damage dealing but overheats quickly, agile and fast shotting or a mix of all.
- Combat, especially with regard to capital ships is much better. You can target individual weapons turrets, engines, and other ship components like radar to help ensure boarding opperations.
- Boarding opperations are also much more complex allowing you to disable engines and weapons before capturing.
- The addition of diverse and specialized crew holds a lot of promise as well.
- Drone piloting is much more fluid and clear. In X3 I had very little idea when or what they were doing.
- Scanner functions and Hacking give a modicum of chance regarding profiterring,treasure hunting, and sabotage.

GAME PLAY:
- The story line seems decent and the missions that are available have greater realism.
- I have only logged about 35+ hrs but, have found it to be very fun and awe-inspiring.
- There are many worth while mods now that give the game greater depth, character, ease of interface, diversity, and fluidity.
- Most of the Vanilla music while flying is great at setting more immersive ambiance.

Neutal's
- Having to scan all the parts of a station is tedious for sure. But, they look so cool! My favorite is the one with the mono-rail train transporting on top of it! They have unique sounds and characteristics that make the whole floating in space to investigate much more cool and worth the time invested.
- The addition of so many craftable items is interesting but, how does all that stuff not take up cargo space??
- Saved game and startup load times are substantially longer... maybe its time I got a SSD...lol

Con's
GAME PLAY:
To a large Extent the videos add depth to the main plot missions but they are overall lacking in orginality. I think more will come through mods... we will see.

SHIP/STATION DYNAMICS:
- The highways and station scanning are interesting components. Unfotuanatly, they necessitate "no collissions" being instituted. Infinitely bouncing off stuff with speed and size of objects being completely irrelevent, is gimmicky to say the least. It does distill the immersive experiance substantially.
- Where are the Boron?? They had some pretty cool ships. Especially with the "living hulls" mod...
- There do not seem to be very many ships in this version of the X universe yet. There is some diversity among races still but a lot of bleed over. As in, multiple factions if not all use the same sets of ships... maybe more comming?? Its been 2 years or more already...
- - - What happend to having "Wings" with different color codes?

- THE CREW INTERFACE:
- Needs a lot of work.
- I don't see how you can "lockout" the ability to command a MORE experienced but not full 5 stared engineer on your M6 from commensing 100% hull repairs and need to dock somewhere to use another, LESS experienced engineer...do they have access to full schmatics or something??
- The inability to command your docked ships crew at all when on a station is just stupid. That needs to change. Com Badge??
- The "Property Menu" was much better organized and complete in X3. Now, it doesn't show your personel at all, even though you have so many more types of them... why?
- - What would it take to be able to move piolts around?? Now, your pilot gets sold with the ship?? That is annoying...
- - Setting up a squad and quickly initiating commands has always been a problem in the Vanilla versions but this takes it to a whole new level. I have spent way too much time trying to figure out why I couldn't give commands to a secondary squad or crew memeber and only through the "details" menu under that persons info... Too many levels deep. What about a similar interface to drone selection?
- Why are your bargans with particular traders only good for that visit?? Too much to load and remember? I don't like having to do the more complex conversation stuff through the psudo-dice-rolling-manipulation-interface-thing everytime I wan't a deal. Especially with the 1-2 minute load times when mistakes are made..

HACKING:
- If you are going to hack something, you should have choices. I DO think it should be bouned to what a console can do but NOT in such that its just a general "Hack" that gets the pre-destined result...I'd like something like, I'm in, now what can I do, how much time can I stay in?

Having written this little book, a good number of the cons will probably be changed through mods. Those that can't be will hopefully grab the attention of the Devs to focus on for updates. They have already come a long way since its launch and I'm very happy to pay for this version of the game so far.
