About This Game

Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six® Vegas 2 - the sequel to the award-winning next-generation first-person shooter - returns to Sin City. Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six Vegas 2 is your last chance to rescue America's sexiest city from an escalating terrorist siege that will force you into heart-pounding action from beginning to end. The best are back and this time winner takes all.
Recipient of numerous awards, including 2006 Best First Person Shooter and Best Online Game, Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six Vegas 2 pushes the top-selling franchise to new heights with new gameplay features and groundbreaking co-op and multiplayer modes.
  • Ubisoft Montreal Returns - Rainbow Six Vegas 2 is back in the hands of Ubisoft's Montreal studio, creators of the award-winning and highly acclaimed Rainbow Six Vegas, to ensure that ending to the Vegas story is one you'll never forget.
  • More Vegas - Sin City is back and bigger than before. Explore all new Vegas hot spots as the environment changes from dusk to bright daylight to dawn, creating a visual experience that is as challenging as it is breathtaking.
  • More Multiplayer - The highly acclaimed multiplayer mode in Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six Vegas has been upgraded to include 11 new, smaller maps that force more intense face-offs, 2 new adversarial modes, more rewards, and easier access to matches using the improved and intuitive matchmaking system.
  • Unprecedented Customizable Experience - Create your own identity that stays the same in both single player and multiplayer modes and progresses as you do. Reap the benefits of your single player rewards in multiplayer and vice versa.
  • Vastly Improved Co-op mode - Turn single player mode into co-op mode at any time, just by jumping into the game. Now your friend can jump into your single player game and help you without any change to the story. Single player maps and storyline were created with co-op in mind, which means every map has multiple entry points and the story is the same.
  • Improved AI - AI is more challenging than ever. Now, the terrorists are equipped with thermal vision, night vision, and shields - not to mention a multitude of varying reactions - all of which are guaranteed to give you a new playing experience every time you go into battle. As you progress within the game, you'll only be able to outsmart your enemies by taking advantage of intel opportunities, such as thermal scanning, and tactical strategies, such as leapfrogging.
  • New Weapons and Armor - Take down the terrorists more effectively using 11 new weapons. New camouflage, clothing and gear allow you to take customization to the next level.

Rainbow Six Vegas 2 is a much needed improvement over the original game, and it improved aspects of the original game's design without changing very much of the overall gameplay.

The biggest addition Vegas 2 added was the ability to customize your character. Instead of playing as a gruff southerner named "Logan" you instead play as a customizeable character named "Bishop". Bishop can be male or female and his/her apperance can be heavily customized. You're given the option to pick between what clothing Bishop wears and what armor you wear. Which will impact how much damage reduction you get and how fast you move.

In addition Rainbow Six Vegas 2 helped start the trend towards unlock based multiplayer. Now you played as a character who was universal across Terrorist hunt and PVP. And your character would gain stats in both singleplayer and multiplayer, which would unlock new guns and cosmetics. This was really popular at the time and reminded me a lot of multiplayer games like Syphon Filter the Omega Strain. These were all great improvements and made the game feel very fresh and new. It made it feel more akin to an RPG despite leveling being automatic and the game fundamentally playing the same. The multiplayer was expanded to have many more gamemodes. And Terrorist hunt was expanded to be much more customizeable.

There's actually very little I can say that's negative about Rainbow Six Vegas 2 apart from how they didn't really change very much about the actual gameplay, so it still has the same flaws like the general stiffness to the combat. The storyline is also a bit weaker. And if you're a fan of older style Rainbow Six games, you're going to remain disappointed.

All that aside though, it's a very effective sequel. And there's little wonder why it's more fondly remembered than the first game.

Rainbow Six Vegas 2 is a direct sequel to the R6: Vegas. Kinda funny at that, as main hero is different but you control same squad. Still a tactical FPS, still without planning stage that older games had. It was released in 2008 and made by Ubisoft Montreal.
It was also released for Xbox 360 and PS3. I heard that the only difference is that ability to put your face over your avatar with facecam is much improved in PC version. Oh, yes, and consoles also supported split-screen mode. No such thing on PC here.

Technically, I encountered very few problem. First, quitting from gameplay into main menu would give me black screen. It was easy to fix just by alt-tabbing though. And second, I am still confused why bombs keep loudly tick even after your teammate disarms them. It's either very strange design or a bug too. It sure made some scenes quite awkward.
It also has in-game achievements, but not linked to Steam. Hey, Steam overlay works now at least.

As for multiplayer, it's alive. Ubisoft said something about not providing servers anymore long time ago, but the only thing it means is that you can't register new account in this game. BUT you can use your UPlay account instead, putting your email as login. And password as password.
People mostly play Terrorist Hunt mode, which is incredibly fun. I also seen coop servers around, but this game was on sale not long time ago so no promises that they will stay. It supports coop only for 2 players after all, so usually people just invite their friend. There also were few versus servers, but people only play Deathmatch and Team Deathmatch now. Not every server was password protected here, good thing.

And well, what can I say, it's a direct sequel and a much better PC port as well. Though this time it has global leveling system, so you aren't likely to use shield on your first playthrough. Which I am not sure that I approve. Talking of it, there is also character customization that also applies to single-player and coop, as you play as custom account called Bishop instead of Logan. And you can slap your face over the character head too. Most of the times it's going to apply eyes to forehead. Or you can apply drawing, if you are creative enough and willing to.

And hey, at least this time around the game actually looks good. Much better than Vegas 1. Well, one of the reasons for that is because PC port was made with widesceen in mind this time around, supporting all the resolution. But most importantly, Ubisoft finally realized that applying brown filter doesn't make it next gen. What you mean that X360 isn't next gen anymore?
Music and setting are much more varied around as well, it's not just Vegas now. Sadly, I noticed that sound seems to not always be mixed good. At least I had trouble hearing everything I am being told.
Oh, and plot sucks. Alright, it's fine, but nothing amazing.

There are very little new gameplay mechanic. I was dissapointed there, I surely would have loved it if they let you command two squads at same time. But nope, just one squad with same two rainbow bros. Oh well. I can easily list new features. First, you can sprint, with very limited stamina, always forgot that it exists. And more importantly, you can finally order AI teammates to throw grenades at spot you want. That pretty much means that you get 10 types of grenades for almost every type, if you can use AI effectively. Awesome. Also, look, finally have supressed sniper rifle.

AI of your teammates were supposedly improved. They cover each other while advancing, one overlooking while another moves in. What I felt however is how often I would bump into their fat collision boxes while backpedalling away from corner, with them refusing to move away without order. And how slow they moved toward cover which I told them to, due to covering each other. Because on practise when you order them to get behind certain cover you usually want them to get there ASAP.

Campaign itself is awesome though. Plot isn't as good, but those missions are much better. Still punishing, it feels more challenging. More good spots to use smoke grenades, sometimes more good entrances. Enemies are much more awesome, looking professional and with bad intentions, having laser sights to show off. And yep, they still show off personality, with all the behind-wall talks.

Oh, and they brought enemies with shield this time around, so you know that you want to flank them while AI does frontal assault. Or abuse sniper rifle, it still works. And it still mixes open fight with room clearing, so you don't get tired of either sniping or breaching.
Too bad that pre-last chapter makes you go solo. It's very hard, yes, but without teammates it's just boring sniping with smoking.

Now, other modes. You can play campaign in coop with one more person if you want, seamlessly. You can't revive each other, nope. AI teammates are still around, only host can command them. It's easier with friend, yes. Especially when it ends only when both are dead in same time, you can just hide and wait for respawn.

Then there is Terrorist Hunt. You can now play it with AI teammates too when in offline mode, but it still feels extremely scripted in how terrorists spawn. It's predictable and frustrating, artificial. However, once you get online, it becomes super fun for some reason. Terrorist spawn feels more natural with 4 players going around and it gets more intense, with difficulty being just right. Fear the terrorist with SPAS-12 shotgun, he is going to be your main demise. It sure gets tense when you are the last person online and there is only one terrorist left, with shotgun. You don't know where he spawned and they are really good at travelling around map.
It does sometimes feel that baiting tactic work much better than actually clearing from all the sides. But oh well. Maybe it could use Hostage Rescue mode.

Oh yea, there is Versus mode too. Maybe it was great in other Versus modes, but people play only DM these days. I tried. It was awful. People sprinting around, stopping only to shoot right into faces. Like other bad realistical shooters. Try to get more tactical in Team Deathmatch by overwatching territory and you are mostly likely will be called out as camper.

But overall, the plot doesn't feel as interesting or urgent in this part and it doesn't have much of new, but it's still an improvement in everything else and is really good Rainbow Six title.
