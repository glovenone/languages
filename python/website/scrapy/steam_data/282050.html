About This Game


Rip, jump and scrub your way to victory with over 35 tracks in this vastly extended version!

Only the Encore edition features the brand new Rhythm Racing mode, extra outdoor Nationals tracks, and exclusive Waypoint races in open world maps.

The improved game engine brings remastered HD graphics & sound, smoother online gameplay, and updated riding physics for full control.

Features:

  • More than twice as many tracks as the original MX vs. ATV Supercross
  • Rhythm Racing mode: Drag race your friends on straight dirt tracks filled with obstacles and jumps
  • 12 player online mode
  • Free Ride mode for racing without boundaries
  • All new gear and graphics kits
  • Over 60 professional riders to race against
  • More than 80 licensed motocross companies for maximum authenticity
  • Remastered audio which includes for the first time 3D positional sounds and new environmental effects

I think this game has gotten more hate than it deserves.
It is too often compared to Reflex, which was a fantastic game. In terms of realism, I would agree that Reflex is the best game to this day. However, this game targets a slightly different audience. The objective here wasn't to have an ultra-realistic game that offers no forgiveness for messing up in a rhythm section. The goal was to have a supercross game that gave players the sensation of realistic racing, while still allowing newer players to make small mistakes without throwing their entire race away. There are more supercross tracks than Reflex, and the poor excuse for an upgrading system has been replaced. The "physics" that everyone complains about are actually pretty good too.

Bottom line: If you're looking for a new supercross game that takes a certain amount of skill and knowledge about sx, but doesn't have simulator-level difficulty, I think you'll enjoy this game.

Also, for anyone who says it's impossible to do a whip - it's possible; you just have to learn how.

Posted: 25 June
I'm glad I took a chance and bought this (on sale), despite all of the negative reviews. Basically, it feels like mx vs atv supercross on the 360/ps3, except they cleaned up the handling a bit (feels like I have some traction now) and threw in the motocross tracks from alive. I love reflex, but the 90mph tracks and doing a forward or back flip off of a tuff block at 10-20mph really got old. This game has some quirks for sure, but I'm enjoying it. For example, as with the console version, there are rare phantom inputs that will steer me off course, or throw my rider way forward off a jump. Also, I can really feel the loss of forward speed (like crazy wind resistance) in the air. On the other hand, though, I like the tighter tracks (especially the outdoor tracks from alive), the fact that I actually need the brakes, and the handling in the turns. The clutch is crazy op as others say, but I like that I can goof a turn and make up for it with that.

As for the graphics, maybe some people are reviewing the game without launching the graphics settings program first (a seperate thing for some reason, just like reflex)? I played at 4k and other than the inconsistent framerate and some lod popping, it looked just as good as reflex to me (even better in some places where texture resolution is higher and meshes have more detail). I think rainbow/nordic REALLY need to force the settings program to launch before the first play, because the default settings look terrible, and leave a bad first impression.

As a fan of the series since the first motocross madness, I would have preferred that rainbow just tighten up the tracks and ease up on the crash-if-someone-nudges-me physics in reflex to make a follow-up game (instead of completely changing the engine for alive/supercross), but I'm definitely not in agreement with the chorus of bad reviews this game is getting. Get it on sale if you have some doubts, but don't even launch the game without changing the graphics settings first (unless you want to get a laugh or something).
