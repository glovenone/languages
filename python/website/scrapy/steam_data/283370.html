About This Game

In this follow-up to the hit Marine Sharpshooter, you'll be a lone operative fighting to restore democracy in an African country. The President of Burundi has been taken hostage by rebels and mercenaries. The brutal civil war that would ensue from this will threaten American interests - so you'll use your elite military training and advanced weapons to take the rebels down!

Marine Sharpshooter II: Jungle Warfare is a game which I have nothing good to say, but since I know that the developers have poured hours into this (I hope not), to put it gently, the game needs some work, and by some, I mean a lot.

Graphics - Haven't aged well, it did come out more than a decade ago, which to some extend covers why it looks like this.
There isn't much to be said here, some games age very well in terms of graphics, this isn't one of them. 4/10

Music - Repetitive, it doesn't matter if you are scouting the perimeter or actually in a gunfight with someone, the music stays the same. A forever going loop of mixed sound files with no variation whatsoever. 3/10

Cinematics - Unskippable, if I am watching a game cinematic and I want to skip it, it means that there is nothing of interest happening or the dialogue that you have implemented isn't progressing the overall story. Well this game does just that, a frame where subtitles are moving and they are telling you the same thing for the 4-5'th time. 4/10

Story - ClichÃ©, you are the *chosen* one, who has to go and save the president, you have to go through a bunch of rainforests and deserts, battle fearsome africans, who for some reason all wear camouflage clothes, but never blend in with the terrain. A story used way too many times, in mediums more than one, just this particular use is badly implemented. 3/10

Content - Could have used some more, some rainforest as well as deserts, guns galore (3), the fiercest africans you have ever laid your eyes upon and let's not forget the best new thing in games ever, bushes which provide cover. For a sniper, who should always be in the tall grass and killing enemies from a safe distance, the addition of cover bushes, wasn't a smart one (sarcasm can be hard to understand through text). 4/10

Controls - Stiff, I don't need to go in depth here, just one little detail that amazingly covers it all, they just had to make the movement a little less robot like. 2/10 *Bonus detail: Never make the screenshot button the same as jump*

Gameplay - The pace of the whole game, was a problem, either it is going way too slowly or way too fast, never inbetween. Also that you start a mission from the middle of nowhere and when you get to the end, you end in the middle of nowhere, are you teleported out of there ? 3/10

Overall a game that had premise, but failed to deliver. It isn't anything revolutionary, that a game promised more and delivered little, but to this day, there are still games that do that, this had to end a long time ago. Futhermore, who in the right mind would give 9 euros/dollars for a 12 year old game ?

Final Score: 3.3/10
