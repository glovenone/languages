About This Game

Hotline Miami 2: Wrong Number is the brutal conclusion to the Hotline Miami saga, set against a backdrop of escalating violence and retribution over spilled blood in the original game. Follow the paths of several distinct factions – each with their own questionable methods and uncertain motivations – as unforeseen consequences intersect and reality once again slips back into a brilliant haze of neon and bloodshed.

Blistering combat against punishing opposition will require intense focus as new variables, weapons, and methods of execution are introduced throughout the struggle. Let the striking colors of an unmistakable visual style wash over as you meticulously cut down those that would stand between you and the ultimate meaning behind the massacre. This is the finale, this is the unquestionable end.

Relentless Hostilities: Adjust combat techniques as the narrative shifts between factions and brings unique variations to a familiar approach along with new weapons, movements, and melee attacks.

Enthralling Soundtrack: Over forty pounding tracks from dozens of artists punctuate the savage conflict and bring the larger scale of your actions into focus. New artists join returning favorites like M|O|O|N, Perturbator, Jasper Byrne, Scattle, and El Huervo for an incredible audio massacre.

Custom Level Editor: Design custom levels using characters, enemies, weapons and music tracks from the game and share your creations with others online via Steam Workshop. [COMING SOON]

After the thrill of completing Hotline Miami, I couldn't resist playing its sequel. I was prepared for a semi-strategic murderfest where I dispatched my enemies through a variety of interesting and brutal methods. I was prepared for a vaguely incoherent plot lined with mystery and doubt. I was not prepared for Hotline Miami 2.

Enter a world filled with characters you will see for 5-10 minutes each and watch them die while thinking "Wait... which one was that again?" You will unravel an elaborate controversy in the last 15 seconds in the game, which may or may not be a dream within a dream during a bad trip in the underworld while on a deserted island on the cast of a film. Do not be afraid if the plot confuses you. All great novelists know that the best stories force ambiguity and nonsense down the audience's throat. You just get the honor of imagining your own plot for Hotline Miami 2, a blessing from the developers themselves.

To be honest, I'm glad they sacrificed the storyline of Hotline Miami 2, that means they can focus more on the mechanics... right?

Halfway through the game, any melee item you pick up at that point will either be ironic or for a cheese strategy where you shoot your gun, wait for the enemies to turn the corner, and brain them with a baton. Now you may be thinking "Pfffft, please! I beat the Dark Souls Trilogy, you think I can't take out a couple gun-wielding AI monkeys with a pipe?!" In the end, some levels have nearly 50 enemies you have to kill in order to proceed, and half of them have guns. You won't be wanting to challenge with yourself by sticking to melee weapons. You'll be begging for the sweet release of completion after dozens of tries. Forget the use of your targetting reticle, just close your eyes and hold down the left mouse button as you mow through hordes of enemies. 128 rounds of machine gun ammo won't even be enough to stem the horde of tanks, wolves, and gunmen that are crammed in every available space in the maps. You will then lure this army of meat around corners by peeking out from behind corners or shooting your gun, then quickly switching to a melee weapon as the bodies pile up around you. Just be careful not to pick up one of the several shotguns scattered around your feet. If you peek one centimeter out from the corner, you will not be seen. If you peek 2 centimeters out from a corner, every enemy in the map will charge at you while shooting their guns frantically.

In Hotline Miami 1, almost every death felt well deserved. Perhaps you forgot to throw your weapon or you foolishly charged a gunner with your fists. It felt very fair compared to the horrible oreals you will have to suffer through in Hotline Miami 2.
During your journey, you might find yourself muttering the following:
"Where did that dog even come from?"
"Am I in a greenhouse, when did they replace the walls with windows?"
"Is this a rage game or a shoot 'em up?"
"Did he just shoot me across the map through 6 rows of windows?"
"WHO THOUGHT THIS BOSS WAS A GOOD IDEA???"
"DO I USE THE BASKETBALL TO KILL HIM???"
"WHAT DID I DO TO DESERVE THIS???"
"Is that a person or a computer?"
"What happened to the arrows, where do I go next?"
"What is this sound blaring from my headphones and why is blood leaking from my ears?"

One thing you will not be muttering to yourself is:
"Oh boy, I can't wait for Hotline Miami 3!"

I could only reccomend this game to people who loved Hotline Miami 1 for its fast-paced combat that required a mixture of quick thinking and strategy, which this game barely manages to uphold. Do not expect an interesting story, memorable soundtrack, or a great sense of satisfaction. Even then, wait for it to go on sale.

Posted: 30 June
It's tough with steam reviews since the only options they give you is either "recommended" or "not recommended" which doesn't allow for nuance.

As someone who loved the original game, Hotline Miami 2 is not a bad game by any means, but it's also not a great game either. It's a "recommended only for certain people" verdict from me.

Pros

- Smooth and (mostly) responsive gameplay.
- Excellent soundtrack, probably the strongest part of the game.
- Has many references to the original game's story which is nice for fans of the series.
- Decent diversity in number of locations you visit.

Cons

- The storyline tries very hard to feel deep and controversial, but it's paper thin and generally incoherent. This is especially a shame considering the first game, while not a literary masterpiece, had a generally coherent and interesting storyline. It tries to tell too many narratives, but ultimately just feels hollow. You will likely not care about any of the protagonists or antagonists as they all blend in together in their violence.
- The "explicit" sexual scenes really add nothing to the storyline. It feels like the developer added it just to be controversial. It didn't bother me, and you can skip it, but it felt completely unncessary.
- The game feels "buggy" or at least inconsistent at many points, eg: the range for melee weapons seems random at times. Also sometimes enemies become invulnerable when they enter doorways, and god help you if a bunch of enemies run into a swinging door, all hell breaks loose. The game is also very RNG based, which can be good or bad depending on your view.
- The sequel switches to very heavily gun focused gameplay. There are lots of hallways that make melee almost always suicidal. The only way around this is to "bait" enemies into corners and then mash them with your weapon. This gets old quick, and I wish there was more variety in the strategies you could employ.
- The windows, oh my god, the windows. Apparently in the late 80's and early 90's, Miami city adopted ordinances which led to the construction of 5 billion windows inside every building. Be prepared to be shot through countless windows.
- The game's difficulty just feels cheap generally. I beat it in 8 hours, but content wise, there's probably 1/2 of that, which is artificially inflated by amount of times you'll have to die. Most of it is just trial, error, and frustration as you constantly get killed by gun wielding enemies at the far edge of the map, or attack dogs that spawn behind you.
- Because of the above points, you will generally spend most of the game holding the shift button to peek around the edge of your screen and hope that a shotgun enemy doesn't blast you from a hidden location.
- The difficulty is also very inconsistent. Some levels feel fun and challenging, while others just become a chore as you mow down dozens and dozens of gun wielding enemies.
- A lot of the masks or weapons you unlock are really underwhelming, and this is a missed opportunity for gameplay variety.

Verdict: Ultimately, I beat the game and felt like I got my money's worth. However, certain levels and stretches of the game felt like an absolute chore to get through, and I found myself just hoping it would end already. The storyline wasn't satisfying to me either (despite a cool last level).

Pick this game up if it's on sale and you're a big fan of the original. Otherwise, you can probably safely skip this, you won't miss out on much.

TLDR: Semi-recommended. Decent game but not great, and not as good as the first.
