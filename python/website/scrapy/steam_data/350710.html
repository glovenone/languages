About This Game

Wizards' Clash is fantasy turn-based card game. Gameplay is easier than classic card games, yet it has some particular features.

The game shows the epic battle between young wizard and evil forces that took over his homeland. By means of giant mechanical castle the wizard is overtaking dark mages at their strongholds and ancient monsters: fire ifrites, golems, dragons and others. The wizards uses magic spells to crush his enemies and some abilities of his unique castle. During his crusade he will be able to learn lots of new spells, new skills and upgrade for his castle.

Game features:

- More than 120 different spells
- 9 unique skills for mechanical castle
- Upgrades for the castle
- 50 levels of different enemies to crush
- Giant monsters each of them requiring special tactics to win

Not good...not good at all.

The concept is interesting: you build and protect a tower while defeating the enemy's tower or hero, and use a variety of cards from your deck as spells or minions to do so.

The problem is, it's far too overly simplistic, which creates an incredibly boring experience.

No Deck Editing

You GAIN cards either from winning a match or from buying it in store with in-game currency that you win. Your deck continues to grow, and grow, and grow. Which, as any CCG/TCG player knows, is a HORRIBLE thing because it saturates the deck with low end cards, prevents synergy, prevents specific tactics.

Eventually, your deck will have 100+ different cards, and you'll rarely see the good ones as a result.

No strategy whatsoever

Given that, there really isn't any strategy here at all. There are no real special cards that do something unique. The more powerful cards just do more of the same (higher damage, give more resources, etc.).

This summarizes the available options:

* increase your wall (which acts as a shield)
* increase your tower height (which wins the game)
* do dmg to the enemy (wall or height)
* increase level of resource building (generates more resources per turn)
* increase x amt of resources

There is NOTHING else in the game play. Cards either do more or less of the above, or they may combine one or more of the above. That's the extent of the creativity and variation here.

There is no synergy between cards, there is no way to edit the deck to be "more tower dmg" focused or more "wall defensive" focused. You just get random cards put into your hands, and go with it. You'll be doing a little bit of everything, which means there is no actual strategy.

Devs are non-existent

Devs are non-existent, as is the community. At the time of this review, there are 3 threads, 2 posters (myself included), and zero dev responses. You'd think the devs would care enough about their game's launch to at least introduce themselves, see how things are going, answer questions of prospective or new customers. But...they don't.

If the devs don't even care about their own game...why should consumers?

Stay away. Even though it's $5, it's a waste of time...and your time is probably more valuable than the $5. There are plenty of F2P card games with substantially more depth and creativity, which will provide a fulfilling, enjoyable experience. Unfortunately, this low cost game...just can't compete.
