About This Game

A Video Game About a Woman Talking to the Police

Her Story is the new video game from Sam Barlow, creator of Silent Hill: Shattered Memories and Aisle. A crime fiction game with non-linear storytelling, Her Story revolves around a police database full of live action video footage. It stars Viva Seifert, actress and one half of the band Joe Gideon and the Shark.

How does it work?


Her Story sits you in front of a mothballed desktop computer thatâs logged into a police database of video footage. The footage covers seven interviews from 1994 in which a British woman is interviewed about her missing husband. Explore the database by typing search terms, watch the clips where she speaks those words and piece together her story.

Unlike anything you've played before, Her Story is an involving and moving experience. A game that asks you to listen.

This is not so much a game as an interactive drama, all told through the words of a series of police interviews. The game mechanics are basically built around a search engine - you watch some short clips and follow up on any interesting bits of information by typing the key words into the database search. The thing that sets it apart form a traditional narrative is that you decide how the story unfolds. Another person playing the game may arrive at the ending following different clues to you. There are plenty of intriguing plot twists, which make it incredibly difficult to write a review without giving much away!

TL:DR- next to zero interaction, an interesting story that might or might not live up to the hype (depending on you), still a brilliant idea.

Her Story goes along with a certain "kind" of games born in the latest 4 or 5 years (i guess?). It basically is an FMV sort of investigative game that's splitted in about 270 short videos you have to get through in order to "guess-estimate" the truth. Mind you, there is no such thing as an end-game revelation, you will (would) just happen to carefully look for all of the keywords from the interviews and you will be able to get an idea of what happened.
It ends up being a bit too criptic and fragmented, that's perhaps what makes this "game" less enjoyable in a way, but i can see why the whole FMV is fragmented like this and thats how it actually works. BUT, but...this is a sort of halfway through, or perhaps more like, 75% through? :D of what it could have been.


I still recommend this game, because it is a good take and somewhat a brilliant one away from the stereotypes and classics of the genre. It also makes it suitable for mobile devices, and that is quite an achievement.

Pro's

- interesting story
- good acting
- brilliant concept

Con's

- very fragmentated experience (about 270 videos!)
- next to zero interaction
- brilliant in concept, but not in its shape.
- really aims a fair bit at nostalgic people, and with that being part of the "style" without it being actually purposeful to the game experience itself.
