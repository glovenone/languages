Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want this game to be a blast for everyone. Engagement with the community and constant feedback are the most important part of development. Everything in this game has been heavily tested by players and the direction and focus of work is based off player responses.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“It's hard to say but maybe a month, perhaps 2 or 3. It depends dramatically on player feedback and how quickly we can build compelling content.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The biggest difference will be content.
We'd like to expand the story line, add more play modes, weapons, and of course zombies!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is playable with the first level of story mode (over 25 minutes), a dozen enemies, and a large variety of weaponry.
The core gameplay mechanics are implemented and it's optimized to run at 90fps on a 970.
There is also a survival mode to see how long you can last.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We're unsure now, but the price may rise in the future as new content and features are added.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Constant communication is very important.
We will be speaking with players via our subreddit as well as surveying for player opinions on all aspects of the game.”

About This Game

Grab your guns and journey your way out of the city to freedom.
In Armed Against the Undead, you have access to a large variety of weaponry, ranging from Chainsaws to Flame Throwers.
No-longer are you limited to pistols and machine guns!
Grab the big guns and start destroying the zombie hordes!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: Intel i5
    • Memory: 8 GB RAM
    • Graphics: nVidia 970
    • Storage: 600 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: HTC Vive Headset & Controllers

Early Access Review
Posted: 18 June
I wanted to like this game. I loved HordeZ and Brookhaven.

Gameplay wise; you can complete the only level of this game in about 20 minutes. There is very little.

Graphically: This game is horrifically ugly; primarily due to the greyed out and extremely short view distance against a garishly dark and oppressively low skybox. The models themselves are quite hideous (in a good way) but since they move so slowly and start so far away, you literally have to stand with your arms at your side for minutes before they get close enough for you to appreciate them.

Also the animations all look like they have messed their underwears, which made me giggle for a few rounds.

Lastly, the game simply is not scary, the poor graphics and jarring screenbox steal what eery mystique the darkness may have given the game and there is really no sense of pressure! The tone of the game lurches about frequenty which further spoils any mood, with the ambient music veering wildly between silence, creepy music and action music and (rather bizarrely) random disco-style pink /gren lighting and carnival music.

In fact the overwhelming impression was merely a malais of boredom which made the "wacky" 'Frenzy-modes' come across as merely desperate.

I feel strongly that this game is NOT ready for any kind of release, not even alpha, and especially not at wht amounts to a "premium quality" price-tag for VR games. Stack this game up against similarly prized gems such as Space Pirate Trainer, or HordeZ or even the Brookhaven (free) demo and the low-level of quality of this game is quite appalling.

Much work remains to be done, including for the developers to choose a tone and theme and GO with it instead of lurching horrible between failing attempts at horror or action or.. 'carnival'.

I hope in 6 months time to be playing this game again, in a more completed and polished format but until then, save your time and money because right now, this game simply is not worth it.
