About This Game

Bibou



The evil Cocus have invaded Bibou's island. The island was a peaceful home for him and his family.

Badges :

The Cocus and their leaders want the magical Yellow Stones, which are scattered around the land. Bibou must explore each area of the island and collect the stone before they fall into the hands of the Cocus.

Select and explore the different areas of the island, in this non-linear platform adventure.
Also you can play mini games.

New contents updates coming soon.

Simple pixel art and platformer. Controls needs some getting used to as it is fixed but it will be best if it can be customizable.

You press the arrow keys to move around and jump, and the down button to shoot. I would prefer the spacebar to shoot though. Press P to pause the game.

It is a pretty tough game despite the simplicity of the game as you have limited lives and the traps are pretty well thought out and placed. (You have to learn the map in order to achieve high scores)

Some minor issues; platforms and obstacles need some improvement in the art to show the player which are the platforms you can actually stand on.

For the price, it is a good purchase for some retro good ol' fun.

A very cute and simple game, but there are a lot of things that needs to be improved on.

For starters, it's practically a gamble to guess which blocks you could actually stand on - and that could REALLY risk you your game. Apparently you can step on flowers but not trees. Okay.

Secondly, the controls are a bit odd, making the game unnecessarily tough. It's not uncommon for you to lose because you fail to jump on a cloud (because you REALLY have to aim for it's top) and end up landing on spikes. Also ended up getting stuck on flowers a few times.

Third, lives are practically useless - you restart from the beginning if you die anyway. they serve no purpose whatsoever other than to save you time from going back to the menu and selecting the level again.

Fourth, There are very, very little help for new players in the beginning. Yes, the game shows you which key to press when you open the game - but that screen barely lasts for like a second. I had to restart the game like three times to read it to figure out which key I have to press to shoot (it's the down arrow key by the way, which is highly unusual). In fact, I didn't even know I could shoot until about ten minutes into the game, which was when I realized the monster placements are far too ridiculous for there not to be a way for players to kill them.

Fifth, the game lacks a 'tutorial' level for new players to get used to the game. The very first level, it ruthlessly throws you into a full-on game where you have to collect literally 70 lemon blocks or whatever it's called (barely read what the guy in the beginning said since it's one big, clumped wall of text with no punctuation whatsoever).

Overall, the game is cute and it definitely has some potential, and I would recommend it to people if what I wrote above got fixed - but otherwise I wouldn't really bother with it. I really dig the music though. Super fun and bright.

Tough little platformer game with a cute character .
I spent the first 5 mins watching him keel over with his legs in the air :P
As with other games from Otakumaker there is very little help on what to do at the start .
Maybe this is ok and my brain is just slowing melting with age.

It took me a while to figure out the keys ,ESC did nothing ,F1 did nothing .
Up arrow to jump and down to shoot + P to pause.

I like the graphics ,it seems to be a theme with other games from this dev/publisher .
Fresh original art style ,it has some "Benny Hill" type music in the background .

You'll die a lot i think as it is quite difficult but obviously rewarding if you can overcome the obstacles.
It would be better if it had remappable controls ,if they really bother you you can use a program called Glovepie V4.5
which has an easy to use gui to remap the keys to your liking.

Overall a good small game ,well made but needs a key config.
