What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“I want to create this game with community, with your ideas. We should have games, that we all want to play!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Until it's done of course! More specifically, right after the Story Campaing Mode is finished, the game will be released.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version is going to have multiplayer, co-op(now it's only local), co-op with missions, story mode, more ship upgrades and customisable player models, weapons etc.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Currently it's in alpha 0.30. Even in this state it's really fun to play. You have side campaign at your disposal.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“It will be probably a litle bit higher, but not that high. I don't want to sell this game for a big amount of money. So, in conclusion, it might raise gradually as I add new content and feautres.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I'll be reading their ideas, thoughts on a community forum. That's how people have a chance to add their ideas to the game.”

About This Game

The Extinction is an indie 2.5D Space Shooter. In the current stage, the game focuses on the arcade type of gameplay, which can offer a lot of fun. Player has an opportunity to play four Game-Modes including the most expanded one - Side Campaign. In this gamemode player has to invade planets which provide money and resources like iron or fuel, then protect them with defensive buildings and finally - get to the boss of the solar system and destroy him. While playing this gamemode, you can always complete side missions, to get more money and XP and explore, freely the void between the planets to search for some more resources.

Profile, ranks, experience

Player earns experience by destroying enemies, invading planets, completing side missions, buying and upgrading weapons or even trading. Along with experience, player keeps getting promoted and earns new ranks.

Game Economy - iron, fuel and others (related to Side Campaign)

In game, player has three major resources - Iron, fuel and Hi-Tech parts. These resources can be used to either trade them for money or crafting your own custom weapons. Each resource has it's own value, so obviously fuel is more valuable then iron, because iron is easier to obtain. You can get this resource by invading planets, digging in the space mine and while exploring the space. For an example, hi-tech parts are a hidden resource and you can get it only if you find it.

Customization

Since, it's pretty neat to have your own models or images in games we have prepared te feature called "Custom Weapon Crafting". This ability, uses your custom image to display how the fired bullet looks like. In the future, as it is in early access we're planning to add more customizable thingies, like ships.

Side Missions, side activities

For now, the player can complete a few side missions like "Have 500 units of iron", "Destroy 3 bombs on the different planets", "Have 3 profit buildings" etc. As a reward you can get money and XP.

Difficulty - Is it hard or casual?

The game is not hard, but sometimes it makes player to stick around the map a while longer due to the better, faster, and stronger enemies, but with propertly armed and upgraded weapons - it's not a struggle at all!

Story

Since the story mode is not completed yet and we have only semi-story, side campaign mode.. It's pretty unknown right now. I surely want to expand the game, make it even more "sandboxy" and procedural in the Story Mode.

For now, We have come up with a little storyline for a side campaign.. but it's told in the trailer. If you haven't watched it, do it right now! :)

Early Access Review
Posted: 8 March
Anotehr Update: The boss is incredibly easy since he only moves across the top of the screen and shoots down. And after this there is nothing left to do as far as I can tell. I don't know how to craft your own weapon at this time, but I own everything in the store. I learned after playing a little more that the faster shooting does not do only .2% increase in speed like it says in the store but makes it shoot much fasetr and allows you to actually compete with enemy ship outputs. Decent game but will be better with a few updates hopefully...

Update:
The control scheme has been changed so that now up goes towards the mouse and down goes away. This is still not as good as it would be to have controls independent of the mouse location. For some reason holding the shoot button keeps the camera frozen in the place that you began shooting, so that if you are moving backwards you can move out of view and potentially kill yourself on objects off screen. It is still hard to tell whether an object is on screen or in the background. Your bullets can collide with enemy bullets and destroy each other, they can shoot many more bullets much faster than you and usually get bunched up as the level goes on so that it becomes like trying to snipe thorugh a wall of bullets with a control scheme that makes it that much harder to try to get a couple shots in. The permanent defence button seems bugged, as I buy it and play a level but I see no indication of it being bought and I believe the money that I supposedly spent on it is always back in my bank. There are not many missions and I am not sure what to do after conquering the 3 planets. I am also not sure how to unlock other weapons or anything, I have the money but cannot buy them. I am still holding out hope but would like to see these problems taken care of if possible.

Original Review:
This game is very promising so far and I am excited for its future. I like the idea of capturing planets and building an empire, and also being given the option to have permanent defense, something that I wish was available in another game I played recently (I'm looking at you Fallout 4). It looks good, seems like it will have plenty of content, and the ship upgrades look promising, as long as they are balanced and implemented well.

While it is fun, I have only captured one planet due some problems I have encountered with the control scheme. Instead of the directional keys moving the ship in 4 cardinal directions, the left, right, and down keys move the ship in those directions while the up key moves the ships towards the pointer (the direction in which the ship is oriented to fire). This leads to problems with moving around the map. For example, attempting to move in a left-up diagonal direction while shooting right will cause the ship to remain stationary, as it is attempting to move left but also in the direction of the cursor, so no movement occurs. I thought that a controller would remedy the control problem, but it was even worse. Movement is made easier, but my cursor remained on screen and the ship would only point in the direction of the cursor, no matter where I tried to point the ship with the controller. Therefore my killing abilities were greatly hampered. If the control schemes are fixed then the game will be vastly improved.
A few other issues:
It is hard to tell when objects are part of the background and when they are actual impediments that can cause damage, so making obstacles more obvious would be helpful. The edges of the map are also hard to see on some maps. Spawning was good for the most part but on the second planetâs invasion 2 ships spawned on top of me and damaged my ship, resulting in my death and a failed planetary takeover. I stopped playing at that point.

While I have not played long, I still enjoyed taking over that first planet and hope that many more conquests will come. I did not want to continue playing and ruin the experience for myself, I am hoping that these issues will be fixed in the future and I will get to do a proper review of the content soon. But thumbs up for a job well done so far and high hopes going into the future!
