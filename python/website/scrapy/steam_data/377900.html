Empire TV Enhanced Edition (v1.5)

Hello everybody! After some delays, we present you Empire TV Tycoon Enhanced Edition. This patch was going to be the version 1.3 of the game, but weâve added so much new features, upgrades and implemented lots of your suggestions -during those last months- that we decided to skip 2 game versions, so this patch will become Empire TV Tycoon v1.5, also known as Empire TV Tycoon Enhanced Edition.

Due to the massive changes, improvements and new features that have been added to the game with this patch, the 1.2.3 save files (latest stable build) aren't compatible with the 1.5 version. But as usual, the old version is available under the âbetaâ option of Steam Library for Empire TV Tycoon, so if you still want to finish your saved games, you can revert your game to the 1.2.3 version and keep playing.

The new features of Empire TV Enhanced Edition are:

Mods and Workshop
Empire TV Tycoon v1.5 include mod support and steam workshop. Right now with mods you will be able to modify or add the following:
- Add new movies into the game and modify any value(name,genre,score...) of the existing movies of the game. Also add a poster for your movies, visible in the movie tag, and Add or Modify the lower left image that appears in the tv for each movie when it's on air.
- Add new ads and Modify any value(name, description, rewards...) of the existing ads of the game. Also add a poster for your ads, visible in the ad tag, and Add or Modify the lower left image that appears in the tv for each ad when it's on air.
- Add new actors with their photos and Modify any value(name, prefered genres...) of the existing actors of the game, and add a custom photo for them.
- Add new news and Modify any value(description, genre...) of the existing news of the game, and add a custom tv image for them.
- More options will be added over time (feel free to make your suggestions in this subject).

Antennas Map
The antennas map is our new progression system for the game. We weren't 100% happy with the progression system that we had in which you had to buy 2 upgrades for increasing your max audience, it was a little simple and the audience numbers increments were too drastic, so we wanted to change it at some point. Also, the community was nostalgic of how Mad TV handled this issue and suggested us to do something similar, we were a little hesitant at first because we thought it wasn't needed and we didn't want to make the exact same system that Mad TV had.

Finally, we decided to implement it but in our own way and with a twist, buying antennas will not only increase your channel max audience, it will also attract a specific audience groups that you choose and make them watch more your channel.

With this feature you will be able to customize your channel even further and thus if you decide to specialize only in one genre or audience group and only buy antennas that attract that group you will start noticing with each antenna than more and more hours of you daily schedule is filled with your prefered audience groups. This opens even more the possibility to make a themed channel because buying the right antennas will fill all your scheduled hours with your selected groups.

Reworked Ads
We have changed how the ads works in the game, thanks to the new progression system with the antennas. Now, to complete an ad you must achieve a minimum of viewers, based on a % of your channel average potency (which you upgrade buying new antennas).
The required viewers and % to complete an ad varies depending of the ad and his difficulty, also keep in mind that harder ads gives better rewards. When you are playing a game you will see new and harder ads appearing while the easier ones disappear during the game progress.

We have also added over 50 new ads in the game to make things more varied and interesting.

Special Content
With this patch we introduced 6 new tags for default content and also for player made content, those tags add another layer of options and possibilities on top of the movie genre types. The special content gives a bonus to normal movies making them even more liked by specific audience groups.
- <cult> movies have a bonus to all audience groups.
- <+16> movies have a bonus to all audience groups, but children shouldn't watch them.
- <xxx> movies have a huge bonus to elders and a big bonus to other male audience groups, but female audience groups don't like it. Those movies shouldn't be scheduled before late night.
- <glamour> movies have a big bonus to all female audience groups, but male audience groups donât like it.
- <cartoon> movies have a big bonus to children and a bonus to men, geeks and women, but elders donât like it.
- <anime> movies have a big bonus to geeks and a bonus to children, but elders donât like it.

The players will be able to produce special content once they buy the corresponding upgrade at 300 fame, but added a custom game option to have that upgrade on day 1. Also now, the player made content have a chance to become cult content when it gets high audiences during the emission of the content.

Movie Archive
The new movie archive keeps records of all your produced movies during the same game. In there you will be able to see how many points you assigned in each category in the production process and the score you got in each of them. Also you can use the previous movies as a template, and assign the same point distribution into your new production with just one button.
And finally the movie archive also keeps a record of the awards that won each movie that you produced.

Second Seasons
Now you can produce sequels, prequels, spin offs and new seasons of your produced content!
To do so, the scriptwriters can produce special scripts based on the previous movies that you produced. Those scripts have special bonus and tend to have higher probability to become a master script. Then you can keep the actors that performed at your first production and they will get a bonus in performance, and finally in the production window you can choose what type(sequel,prequel, new season,,,) of content you want to do.

Workers
- New worker: Content Specialist.
This worker increases the freshness recovery of your content and greatly reduces the reroll cost for ads and movies .
- The Public Relations now also reduces the cost of hiring actors.
- The Hacker now also reduces the monetary penalty of any police mail.
- Fixed the tooltip of the Community Manager ultimate.
- Reworked the way in which workers appear in the hire worker window, and now one of each type are guaranteed.


Custom channel logos
This is the first iteration to customize the tv stations logotypes in Empire TV. Once you launch the game with the v1.5, the game will create a folder âlogosâ under your local folder. This folder have 3 transparent images (80x60) named logoBlue, logoRed and logoGreen. Feel free to modify those images with the logotypes that you want for each channel. This images will be shown in the TV Picture (bottom left) and in the main floor of each player (floors 1, 5 and 9) near the elevator.

New languages and custom translation
The game have been translated to French, Russian and Portuguese (movie names are still in english, but fans will be able to modify, add and translate them through mods and the steam workshop).

With this version you are able to create your own translations and/or modify existing ones. Once you launch the game with the v1.5, the game will create a folder âtranslationsâ under your local folder. This folder will contain 7 files (6 for each language available in the game and 1 for your custom translation).

The 6 language files are here for reference, that means that if you modify any of this 6 files the changes will not be shown ingame. On the other hand, the âTranslation_custom.xmlâ is a file with a custom translation that you can modify and share with your own translation (or a improved one) for the game. To use this translation, while you are ingame, select Options/Language/Custom.



Other fixes
- Added the channel names in the floor selection window in the elevator.
- Removed the <by player> movie tag, now the content made by players is listed with the name in blue.
- Added the current episode number in the tv show tag.
- Raised the max number of movies you can have to 35.
- Added a new smiley (dark red) for the really hated content.
- Added over 200 new movies and tv shows
- Added a button to reset your game global stats.
- Modified the color of some texts in the game to make them more clear.
- TONS of quality of life fixes

We are really happy with this patch. Of course we will be adding more features and improvements over time and more options to do more varied mods with the workshop. We will continue fixing any new bug that appears with this patch while we plan new features, and also we are finishing a version of the game for tablets, we hope to have it ready for this summer.

As always, thank you all for your support!!

About This Game

Empire TV Tycoon is a game in which you manage a TV channel and fight for audiences taking decisions that will project your channel to fame and fortune.You will decide the content of your channel, select advertisers, hire workers, make your own productions, hire actors, and engage in much more activities! It's inspired by games such as Mad TV, Mud TV, and other movie management games, with a modern approach on how to produce custom content such as movies or tv-shows.

Empire TV is a big corporation having serious losses, and It can't afford to maintain three channels, but only one, any longer. You have been hired to lead one of the channels to achieve fame and be the one that remains.

Features

  • Manage your channel and determine the best content for the current audience. Also, increase your fame and win the war between the three Empire TV channels.
  • Each audience group has its likes and dislikes. Make them happy and your fame will increase; make them sad and your channel will sink into the TV deeps.
  • Buy and sell movies or TV shows that suit your audience best. Also, you can select your favorite movies from about 1000 movies.
  • Don't be tempted to use illegal content. While pirated movies are cheaper, the risk is high and police is always watching.
  • If you can help the advertisers to reach your audience, you will win some money. But be careful: Sometimes, their requests and deadlines are hard to fulfill.
  • Hire workers who will help you to execute different tasks, and each one with their own level and skill.
  • Make your own productions, movies or tv-shows. Distribute your resources effectively for each available movie genre.
  • Enjoy a personalized and randomized movie script for each production. Lots of combinations for each movie genre.
  • Hire actors, knowing their own strengths and weakness, to act in your production. Do your best to keep them happy and utilize their skills efficiently.
  • As the director, you will have to take prudent decisions during the production, and these decisions will affect the final quality of the production.Submit your productions to the industry awards in different categories. Try to be the best and win glory and fame.
  • And discover many secrets and other surprises related to the film industry in the last 50 years.

I'd like to preface my review with a disclaimer; my reviews are typically written and intended for older gamers like myself. If you are looking for a competitive management sim with defined objectives, it is a game you will want to try. If you are looking for more freeform play as with previous tycoon titles, you may want to wait until a more suitable mode is included. Having tried and failed as a programmer myself, I respect anyone who can complete a project and follow through.

I've added suggestions as an update to provide a more constructive form of criticism as I had previously stated: There are many things I like about this game. Just because I don't recommend it doesn't mean you wouldn't like it.

"Not really what I'd consider a "Tycoon" namesake, far too linear and defined. The game is very rigid and instead of allowing you to mold a channel for a specific audience, it forces you to cater to a random viewer-type each hour. About the only thing I was actually able to customize about my channel was it's name, and which of 3 avatars I wanted running it.

The economics seem well designed, and progression was available; however, after a few days I realized my MTV-Squared channel was only playing romance and anime to cater to the women and children that were tuning in. If I had been able to pick my content, and draw an ever-increasing target audience, I'd still be playing it... but the way it's delivered is far too unrealistic (afterall, how often does lifetime cater to frat boys?)

The graphics, music and character movement were all enjoyable, and I really appreciated the programmers sense of humor and references to movies."

I'd recommend this to anyone looking for a management simulation with strict defined goals... but a tycoon? Imagine Dino-Park tycoon with a pre-built park, and you need to add the dinos the current guests want to see. An actual tycoon game allows for a lot more freedom, typically does not have a hard ending in which you win or lose, and is less competitive in game (more about staying in the black, and ever-expanding your business/park/etc.)

UPDATE: I would like to point out that I didn't dislike the current game mode; quite the contrary. Multiplayer in this style game is actually a very cool concept. I'd also like to expound a bit on what I think would make this a better tycoon.

1.) Dynamic Crowds/Custom Stations Example: A player buys the game wanting to make a specific-style network (ie. TNT, Nickelodeon, Lifetime, Sci-fi Channel, Comedy Central) but is immediately burdened with the fact they cannot legally purchase several titles within that genre. The player goes to set programming, but the audience is random throughout the day (great for the initial arcade style mode, not so great for cultivating an audience in your personal sandbox.). The player starts airing only one Genre, over and over, and eventually the audience adapts (people stop tuning in when they hate the channel, and make more of an effort to find time when they love it.)

*Granted... the math behind this one may be a challenge with the current system.

2.) Custom Touches, The Little Things: Minor aesthetics go a long way, the ability to set your office decor (maybe a handful of movie posters for the wall, or genre-styled pieces for display). Anything to make the office seem more the players creation and less a defined element.

3.) Office Design/Building: While not entirely necessary to feel more like a tycoon, it would be highly liberating to be able to design your TV Station building to some degree. If a single play/endless style mode were introduced, it would be beneficial to have a smaller overall building to ease navigation and
be less overwhleming for a player to decorate.

4.) More defined identity: Add some additional content for stations that pursue a specific style. (ie. Bonuses/Perks for players who specialize in ads, directing their own films, or broadcasting pirated material.) Reward players for adhering to a specifc discipline or for balanced approaches.

More to come.

I love tycoon and simulation games, but I just can't recommend this game at this time.

The premise is good and the core design works, so there's potential in my opinion. But here are the reasons I've quit playing for now:

-Viewership seems to be genre based only (and some of it doesn't make sense). The plot and other aspects of the show mean nothing. Lovers don't like Sports movies. So if you program a sports movie for them, they won't tune in, even though it's a love story too. I haven't seen a change in Elderly viewership based on when the movie was made. The elderly don't like comedies, but they wouldn't tune in for a Laurel and Hardy movie? And what's up with guys not liking comedies?

-Multi-hour films have no effect on viewership. A huge audience of lovers will watch the first hour of a romance film, then just leave in the middle of it. A geek will sit down to watch a 3 hour Sci-Fi film after 2/3s of it is over. Good marketing should bring in viewers early. A good film should keep some viewers engaged for its duration.

-The necessity of moving to different parts of the building is annoying to me. I get that it's part of the time-management mechanism, but when I am browsing ads or movies, I'd really like to see my schedule and the viewers for upcoming time slots. As it stands, you have to memorize your day's schedule to make the trip to the other floor productive. My guy doesn't have a PDA or a printer he can use to take the schedule to the movie vault with him?

-Purchased advantages give no advantage. The developers acknolwedge this in the forums. When you buy tech from Doc Brown, the AI players get it at the exact same time. So there's no advantage to purchasing them. The developers state they may change this, but by doing so will either make the fame game extremely hard (when the AI gets tech and you don't) or easy (when you get it but the AI doesn't have the fame yet.) This leads into the next item.

-Tech jumps are far too steep. When I got the 'streaming' tech recently, my viewership jumped massively. It's such a steep change, all the ads in your queue that haven't had a block filled are removed from your list, and those that you can finish are ridiculously easy. There needs to be more upgrades to purchase that provide smaller boosts. It would add to the strategy of the game, while diminshing the tech gap issue in the previous item.

One of the tech gap issues revolves around the library of available films. If you get the tech that gives you higher rated movies and your opponent doesn't, you could really outpace them on fame. This is why when you get it, the AI gets it too. It's a dumb mechanism.

So with all the flaws, I'll be putting this game on the back shelf for a while.
