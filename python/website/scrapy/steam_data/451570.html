About This Game

Demetrios is a quirky point & click adventure game inspired by classics like Broken Sword, packed with tons of humor!

Bjorn Thonen, a slob of an antique dealer living in Paris, is robbed one night after coming home drunk. Forced to conduct his own investigation with the help of his neighbor Sandra, he ends up involved in a murky, mysterious affair. Will these unlikely heroes be able to rise up and discover ancient secrets?

The game offers situations and puzzles for beginners and experts alike, with a high degree of interactivity. Explore ridiculous item combinations, just to see what happens!

  • A long, varied, standalone adventure (8 to 12 hours of gameplay)
  • Over 15,000 lines of dialog, filled with laughs!
  • Hand drawn art in Full HD
  • High level of interactivity. Characters react to your actions!
  • Fun mini-games throughout the story
  • Search for hidden cookies on every screen for optional puzzle hints!
  • Compatible with gamepads, for relaxed, television viewing!

"There is more stupidity than hydrogen in the universe, and it has a longer shelf life." - Frank Zappa

Well... Demetrios is COWCAT games' first project and an adventure game that I'm sincerely on the fence with. There is a truckload of good and bad about it, just within the boundaries of a perfect balance. Considering it is the first ever game of the developer, I think congratulations are in order. Aside the questionable humor, game dynamic is more than decent for an adventure game.

It all begins with probably the most disgusting and pathetic excuse of a human being with an IQ level below repair: our protagonist, Bjorn Thonen. He is a genuine slob who enjoys eating food off the floor and lives by the extent of toilet humor every second of his life. He is rude, immoral and perverted. Oh, and he is an antique dealer that collects random junk hoping to sell them at excruciating rates to some tourists in Paris. There is literally nothing to make Bjorn sympathetic for us - alongside our other starring characters: the exaggerated bimbo next door neighbor Sandra and her insufferable + evil daughter Caroline. For being out loud, I think the primary negative about the game is the lack of either sympathetic or relatable characters.

One night, Bjorn hits home dead drunk and receives a strange phone call in the middle of the night, warning him about a grave danger approaching. Following the warning, he gets knocked out by an intruder in his own home and later discovers that he's been robbed. Beginning his investigation with little to nothing at hand, Bjorn will fast find himself in an adventure that one should indeed be cynical about hearing. Through a series of blatantly idiotic encounters - and a surprisingly well-written background story - we'll take on a journey only probably to define as a European version of Indiana Jones crosses over the worst American Pie movie ever.

As you can so far guess, the humor of the game is crude and downright obnoxious with sexual remarks, toilet humor and random absurdities. Thus, it misses the point of being funny quite often and ending up being simply gross. With that warning in mind, if you are to endure the humor you'll soon discover a genuinely interesting story with naively creative elements and a collection of brain scratching puzzles. The second half of the game, where Bjorn will take a journey abroad to continue his investigation was way more interesting both in dynamic and conceptual humor.

Contrary to your usual P&C adventure, we have a first person camera and a static environment that can be investigated very much like a hidden object game. Aside that, you'll gather items, use them in proper places, converse with NPCs. etc. All the expected. There is an interesting hint system in the game: you'll be collecting cookies in each scene - very similar to a hidden object game - and later eat those cookies to get a hint about what to do next. Also, you are free to discover countless obscure ways to kill Bjorn through "not so wise" story and environment choices - which will be a mandatory stress relief for you. They are mostly a dedication to Bjorn's horrendous IQ, thus his low survival rate anyhow.

A game time of 12-15 hours are to be expected which is more than decent for a game in this genre. Aside the narrative tone and insufferable characters, the game dynamic was surprisingly interesting and entertaining. I've been informed that the developer came with the idea of working on a "toilet humor filter" that will present an alternative narrative mode for the game which will be included in a patch coming this summer and will possibly make a cleaner gaming experience on the humor department. Cheers!

Don't let the play time fool you, I've finished the game in offline mode.

Demetrios is a game that looks like a bunch of fart jokes at first but actualy proves to be more than that. Sure, there's a lot of toilet humor and if you hate it, you probably won't enjoy yourself. And there is a LOT of jokes everywhere. You literaly can't go 5 minutes as Bjorn without doing something stupid, but this gives the game a certain sort of charm. I found Bjorn to be extremely charismatic and you can't help but feel bad for him when he says or does an act of utter stupidity that can even get him arrested or killed.

The gameplay itself is quite simple. You click around and try to find useful items and solve all kinds of puzzles in a wide array of enviroments. There are also mini-games, which are quite fun to play, despite being quite simple - but hey, there's a reason they call them MINI games. Other that that, you also try to find cookies, scattered all over the place. They act like clues. If you're stuck, eat a cookie and all will be better (sounds like a motto for a cookie brand... anyone knows the number of the Oreos headquarters?). Khm... back to the topic. The hints can help quite a lot, because you will get stuck. Despite the fact puzzles are usualy not that hard to solve, a few of them can prove to be a hard nut to crack. But hey, at least you can get fat for a reason.

So... what about the graphics? Well, the game looks REALLY nice. Everything is hand drawn, suits the game perfectly and I can only praise the game in this apect. It runs great too - even integrated graphic cards can handle the game without breaking a sweat... which is actualy not that big a deal... I mean... we ARE talking about a 2D point and click adventure. But hey, it's still worth a mention.

As for the sounds, there is a lot of farting. I mean, even some of the sound effects are replaced by farts. While this is humorous at first, it does get annoying after some time. The soundtrack itself is not the most memorable one I've ever heard, but it does fit the game and it's not bad at all. There are even aditional ones you can unlock in the main menu, which is nice.

Conclusion: Demetrios is a great adventure for people who like toilet humor and constant jokes. However don't expect the jokes to be sophisticated, because most of them are far from it. As for the gameplay, it is varried and the puzzles are fun to solve. Did I mention the development studio consists of a single person? The lenght is quite nice too, you'll spend around 9 hours to finish the entire game, which is not bad for a point and click adventure. Do I recommend it? Try the demo and if you find yourself laughing in the first 10 minutes, go for it.

Final score: 7.4/10

What it is
A relatively long (for the genre) 'adventure' game which describes itself as like Broken Sword, but is probably more like a cross between a hidden object game and a visual novel.
As a lover of classic point and clicks I was excited to play this, and although it wasn't terrible, it didn't live up to my expectations, mainly due to lack-lustre, one-dimensional characterisations and sub-par execution.

The Good Stuff
+ A decent length for the money
+ Art style isn't bad
+ Story is OK, if a little silly (which to be honest, I expect from this kind of game anyway)
+ Some reasonably entertaining mini games
+ Puzzles were not too difficult (perhaps a little too easy in places)
+ Quite a lot to find (including hidden cookies and a lot of different ways to die)
+ Devs seem active, responsive and helpful on the forums - always a plus.

The Not-So-Good-Stuff
- For the most part it's just static screens - you don't move your character (which reminds me more of ten-a-penny hidden object games rather than your classic point and click)
- Music gets a little grating after a while as there's not a huge amount of variety
- Am I supposed to even vaguely like any of the characters? The main protaganist is rude and stupid (not in a lovable kinda way), incredibly disrespectful to the woman he supposedly likes, and all round disgusting. The woman is pretty stereotypical (she wants to buy all the crappy looking stuff the mechant has, because gosh, she just loves shopping *that* much!) and the kid is deliberately annoying
- The humour misses the mark more than it hits it. If I tell you one of the characters is called 'Dumgivafuk' I think I've probably given you the level of wit here in a nutshell
- I realise it's not a game with a huge budget (and the price tag reflects that) but I feel like some voice acting - even if only in parts - really would have brought this game to life a little more

Conclusion
Just because your game is set in multiple countries/cities does not make it 'like Broken Sword'. It's nothing like Broken Sword. That said, it was a moderately entertaining diversion and I do appreciate the effort they've put into it. I've played far worse.
5 'mehs' and a shrug out of 10.
