About This Game

Venture into the world of The Divergent Series: Allegiant in this 10-minute immersive story that brings film characters to life in VR in an all new way. Join Tris, Four & Caleb (Shailene Woodley, Theo James, & Ansel Elgort), on a mission to prevent catastrophe and discover the true intentions of David and his Bureau of Genetic Welfare…
  • The Allegiant VR Experience was created as an experiment in VR storytelling. Interactivity is present, but limited.
  • As the first VR experience with three photo-real characters, acted and performance captured via participation from their real-life counter-parts, Lionsgate and Summit Entertainment is dedicated to creating novel virtual reality experiences featuring boundary-pushing elements, freely made available for home use.
IMPORTANT: The Allegiant VR Experience requires a high-end computer to run. A 980ti graphics card or better is strongly recommended.

For more information: http://thedivergentseries.movie/vr
See the movie!: http://tickets.thedivergentseries.movie

#Allegiant
The third installment of the blockbuster Divergent series franchise, ALLEGIANT takes Tris [Shailene Woodley] and Four [Theo James] into a new world, far more dangerous than ever before. After the earth-shattering revelations of INSURGENT, Tris must escape with Four and go beyond the wall enclosing Chicago. For the first time ever, they will leave the only city and family they have ever known in order to find a peaceful solution for their embroiled city. Once outside, old discoveries are quickly rendered meaningless with the revelation of shocking new truths. Tris and Four must quickly decide who they can trust as a ruthless battle ignites beyond the walls of Chicago which threatens all of humanity. In order to survive, Tris will be forced to make impossible choices about courage, allegiance, sacrifice and love.

Posted: 18 May
If you're a fan of the Divergent series you should probably give this a try, otherwise, just pass it by.

Pros:
  • It's free
  • If you're a fan of the series I'm sure it's really cool to be in that universe.
  • Same voice actors as the movies.
  • The shooting is fun.
  • When talking to a character, they will actually look at you.

Neutral:
  • It's a quick experience, which would make it good for demoing the Vive (assuming you don't have issues). However, it also leaves more to be desired.
  • While characters do look at you while reciting dialog, outside of that they don't interact at all. Like if you get right up in someones face they don't care at all and just continue on their scripted animations.

Cons:
  • I had lots of edge artifacts (Like when you play a game with no anti-aliasing on).
  • FPS was also an issue, any moving objects seemed to lag about every 3 seconds or so.
  • Vive controllers were assigned to the wrong hands. I checked the home screen, and I did have them in the correct hands. I also tried the game three different time. It happened every time. Also after I closed the game and launched a different game the controllers were in the correct hand.
  • Poor animation transitions. It's a bit immersion breaking when a character suddenly goes rigid, then goes into it's next animation.
  • On two different runthroughs I had black screen crashes where I had to restart my computer. No other VR experience has done this to me.

For what it is I can't harp on it too much. It's a free, quick experience to show off the Vives capabilites. Bonus if they're a fan. But I wouldn't go out of my way to recommend it.

Rig: Intel Core i7-5930K, 32GB DDR4 2800, (x2) EVGA GTX 980 SC (one dedicated to PhysX), 500GB Samsung 840 Evo SSD, Win 10.

Update: After disabling SLI and setting my secondary card to only PhysX my screen and object jitter completely went away. No black screens since the switch. Edge artifacts are still present however (no tweakable AA settings that I could found), and in-game hands are still flipped sometimes too.

Even after I now have it running properly I'm still not going to recommend it. I will admit that I'm much closer to neutral on it now. However, I'm still sticking by that unless you're a fan, it isn't a "must play" kind of experience.
