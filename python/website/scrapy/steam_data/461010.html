About This Game

Enter the world of Roll'd where rolling comes in at a new degree.


In this arcade, multiplayer-focused endless runner you will be fine as long as you remember the twist: you do not control the hero...you control the track.

Challenge yourself and your reflexes to keep the track level for as long as possible. Sounds easy? Why not give it a try to see for yourself?

Now introducing: MULTIPLAYER!

Invite some friends over for a local game or catch up for a match online...it's time to get rolling! Single player is available as well, so hit the road best your previous record and unlock all the goodies: new runners, locations, themes and difficulties.

Run through the varying settings all specifically stylized with references and inspirations ranging from the CGA era, through 8-16bit NES/SNES to the golden ages of the Amiga and more. But at the same time they are all up to date with modern lighting effects, dynamic weather and some other cool visual treats.

But be wary though, Roll'd can and will be unforgiving and reaching that extra point with each game will prove to be a demanding yet exciting task.

Features:

- A unique twist on a classic genre
- Addictive, easy to grasp, hard to master gameplay
- Highly addicitive multiplayer mode! Both Local and Online. 2-4 players.
- Leaderboards & Achievements
- 5 diverse and varied graphical themes, 4 difficulty settings each
- 25 unlockable runners
- Pleasing to the ear original soundtrack
- and a cool set of varying retro inspired post effects

Simple endless runner game which is suitable to be played in short sessions when you are bored.

You start as a pig character, running on a roller coaster terrain, which you will have to balance with the right and left keys to prevent your character from falling off to your death. With only 2 keys to control, it sounds simple but that couldn't be further from the truth. It has quite a learning curve, and in the beginning, you will die a lot of times within seconds of the game, making you want to rage quit and throw your keyboard / mouse at the wall. However, once you get the hang of it, the fun starts. You get to level up, and unlock more characters, themes / locations and difficulties (easy, normal, or hard). The variety is quite extensive, and will definitely keep you playing for some time. The background music is a mix bag of soothing music to chiptunes, depending on the location, which sometimes may be annoying.

Overall, a good game well worth the cheap admission price!

Review copy provided from developer. Note that my ability to enjoy a game is not influenced by how I recieved it. If you would like to see the TL;DR, go to the summary at the bottom of the review.

Roll'd
is a simplistic endless runner with the same sort of novelty provided from games such as Timberman.

The gameplay is, like most simplistic games, simple. You assist a variety of characters through a variety of different worlds by making their journey across different terrains horizontal (to some extent). You do this by pressing the arrow keys for keyboard and left stick for controller. Personally, I found keyboard to be the easiest way to control the game as it felt more precise. In regards to how fun this gameplay is, it's more the type of game you can jump into for 10-15 minutes to try and beat your old highscore. For a single dollar, it gives you a fair amount enjoyment.

Roll'd has decent enough graphics, and an interesting idea of switching up the art style of each area you unlock which manages to keep it mildly refreshing. I did notice that in the second area, the graphics do not seem anywhere near as crisp as they did in the other areas.

The music tends to get quite repetitive and somewhat annoying. The sounds (e.g. the annoying pig squeal played each time you die) were even more annoying and drove me to switch them off in the settings.

The only other issue I had was with the fact that the game isn't exactly fullscreen once you start playing.

"Roll'd" is a really surprisingly fun game. I bought it due to my prefererence for SAS (short attention span) games and multiple levels to be unlocked with a gradual progression. Figured if I played for an hour it would be reasonable at this remarkably low price. After three days I have most of the achievements and have passed level 40 (of 50 for which achievements are offered.)

The premise of Roll'd is that instead of the character moving, the path does. Perhaps a small change, but a very nice variation from the norm. At various points new levels and variations are unlocked, as well as new and bizarre "runners". The numerous paths may only be different in appearance, but they certainly look VERY different, and give the feel of not being at all similar in gameplay. If it's an illusion, it's a good one.

The art and music of Roll'd are really nice, and all in all this is a remarkable game for this low price of less than a dollar. If you like dexterity or running games, Roll'd is certainly one to be considered near the top of the list for players of all ages.

Thank you.

Right off the bat, I wasn't sure what you think of the music. It's nice, yet at the same time, it got annoying after a while. I think the problem was mainly that it was the same music playing over, and over, and over again. I finally ended up just turning off the music. There are multiple characters to choose from, but this is done via an unlock/leveling up system. Map selection also goes off this as well. It's really nice they offer it as a leveling perk instead of just "You beat the course, here's an unlock!" What's even better is that even if you die, you still get some levels depending on how far you got. Considering I'm terrible at this game, that was quite helpful for me.

The concept of Roll'd is pretty basic and fun. Don't roll and you win. Sounds easy, but it can be challenging, which is nice. I really enjoyed this game, even if I kept dying. One issue was that even if you were full screen, the actual game was not. It's weird because every other aspect about the game is full screen except for the actual gameplay. I suppose it's not as big of a deal, and doesn't make too big of a difference, but it'd still be nice if we're in full screen to actually have full screen gameplay.

There are actually quite a lot of high points about this game. Controller support, Steam achievements, trading cards, mlocal co-op, multiplayer, and even cross platform multiplayer. The developers really went all out on this game and made it very much Steam friendly. On top of this Roll'd is only 0.99 cents. Pretty good price for this. I took a look at the developer interaction, and from what I'm seeing, the last developer to community interaction was two weeks ago, and that was simply to step in for a forum moderation role. According to their Steam page, the last actual update was a month ago. Given how little the developer is interacting now, it worries me that they've given up on this game. If that is the case, it's a real shame, because Roll'd is very fun, and had a lot of possibilities.


Positives:

  • Character variety.
  • Unlock system. (you unlock from completing or failing a course)
  • Map variety.
  • Fun concept.
  • Controller support.
  • Steam achievements.
  • Trading cards.
  • Co-Op.
  • Multiplayer.
  • Cheap.

Negatives:

  • Even in full screen the game itself is not full screen.
  • Music gets annoying after a while.
  • No updates in over a month, and communication from the developers has ceased.
