About This Game

In Diner Mania you’ll find out what’s it like to succeed in restaurant business.
Besides pleasing customers by serving them delicious dishes you’ll have to deal with really bad bunch of gangsters. Add some really hot spices to their orders to see if they’re as tough as they look.
Earn money to repair and upgrade restaurants to face even more challenges - in this business it’s eat or be eaten!

Game features:
  • 4 different restaurants (italian, fast food, cafe, chinese),
  • More than 50 dishes to prepare,
  • More than 100 items to purchase or upgrade,
  • Mini Games before each level,
  • Almost unlimited number of levels in the game.

Omega Flowey
( 8.4 hrs on record )
Posted: 17 February
Diner Mania is what it sounds like, a combination of Diner Dash and Cake Mania. As far as content goes, Diner Mania is bare bones, offering only the most minimal of content a game of this genre can present. The storyline is fairly straightforward: A string of family friendly restaurants are driven out of business by rowdy bikers who vandalize, terrorize, and refuse to pay their bill, leaving only the owners of the Italian restaurant in charge of helping rid their town of the biker plague and put their neighbouring restaurants back in business. Despite this endearing storyline, this game itself is anything but endearing,

There's no dialogue, written, spoken or otherwise, and the character models are terrifyingly plastic with dead expressions and no personality. The only thing you get from the sparce selection of patron models are grunts and coughs of which there are only about three or four total.

The coding is shoddy as attempting to take seperate orders from a single serving station results in the primary order being dropped from the queue and the waitress taking only the second order, resulting in botched orders and ultimately waisting time and cash. The spice rack also oftentimes presents incorrectly, showing me full spices at the beginning of a new restaurant, only to go into a game with missing spices.

There is no real challenge to the game with only bikers as primary enemies. They serve only to take up space and waste food.

If you're into these click frenzy games, it certainly delivers as the game can get terrifyingly hectic and at its worst, can definitely help pass the time, but with only a few hours or gameplay, the replayability is deathly low. It's only marginally worth its normal price, but certainly won't make you regret purchasing it for a dollar.

Pros
- Fairly relaxing music

Cons
- bad coding
- bland content
- ugly character models

seph.au
( 1.3 hrs on record )
Posted: 28 June, 2015
Placed in charge of several broken down restaurants, your goal is to bring them back to life, scare away the thugs and make lots of cash. Gameplay is quite similar to the "Dash" games. Clients walk into the shop, usher them to a table and proceed to take orders. Difference is the bikers, taking up valuable table space while not paying for their meal you want to get them out of the diner asap. How you ask? By feeding those manly men chilli.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n_jOykPSzRI
Progress through each day rewards cash which can then be spent on store upgrades. Other ways to gain cash include increasing reputation enough for the townsfolk to run charity days that invest in your store and playing the mini games between service, which in-turn increase your daily profits since you have special meals to sell. Diner Mania contains 4 different stores that require upgrading, each has their own different themes, menu items and background music. Upgrades included better quality meals, drinks, deserts, tables for more customers and plants that could be placed to increase tip profits.

Surprisingly decent 2.5D artstyle, almost clay animation. No rebindable controls but the entire game is mouse driven with a clean UI. With many customers in the store I still found it easy to click on the right people and orders. One great feature is the ability to queue commands up. Able to click on customers, have the chef prepare orders and set your pathway for delivery all while the game is still processing the first move. Not sure how many moves could be prepped ahead since there is no visual indication but it worked rather well.

Not bad for the price overall, worth checking out of you enjoyed the Dash series or time management games. Casual by nature but more then enough to stay interesting and keep you on your toes. Cheers folks. :)

Posted: 27 June, 2015
This is a really chaotic diner simulator. Just like in Cook, Serve, Deliciou! you will be going on a clicking frenzy.

I think the most awesome feature this game has to offer however, is you can load up a long queue of actions for your waitress to fulfill, and she will remember each and every task perfectly making the game play so much more fun.

It definitely has simple mechanics, but it's still challenging enough, but not overly for a casual game. Given the amount of content and stuff you can buy/ unlock as well as the minigames and virtually endless levels, I think there is great replay value here, especially if you're looking through your Steam library and have no idea what you're in the mood for. This game is colourful and fun with insanely cute graphics and smooth animations. Watching all the little people eating their meals is fun in itself, although you won't find yourself idle for long! The music as well is perfect and cheerful, and all of the little sound effects are all appropriate and helpful.

Overall this is a high quality casual game which is simply a great pleasure to play. My only problems with it is sometimes clicking the little circles to make stuff is a bit tedious at times if you're trying to speed your way through, and I also think there could be more place settings on the benches to store more dishes, but overall I give this game an:
8/10
