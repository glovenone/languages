    About This Game

    As a member of the CTU – Counter Terrorism Unit, you will be under fire and under pressure as you are deployed to several dangerous locations to detain a rising criminal threat. Anyone can fire a gun, but there are only a few who can bring down a suspect with intimidating presence, and force them to lay down their weapons and surrender.



    At your disposal are a selection of weapons and tools that will aid you in fighting terrorism. These include the snake cam (used to see what dangers may face you behind a closed door), the night vision goggles, riot shield and three types of grenade. Based on your performance for each mission, players will be awarded with money which can be spent on new weapons, tools and hiring new team members.



    AI team mates can be ordered to open doors, cover locations, move to area, use snake cams and an assortment of other tasks. You can also play online with up to four friends in five player online co-op.

    Features:

    • Up to five player online co-op
    • Give commands to AI partners in single player
    • Use a selection of tools ranging from night vision goggles to snake cams
    • Use intimidation to force enemies to surrender
    • Earn money from completing missions to spend on new teammates and weapons
    • Go for stealth to increase your chances of arrest or go in and shoot everything in a rampage
    • Level Editor with Steam Workshop integration

    Full Bammsters article here: http://bammsters.thang.dk/wordpress/ctu-counter-terrorism-unit-review/

    Aside from the typical movement keys for a game of this type, the controls for the game are a bit wonky at times and don’t always make much sense. One blatant example of this that you can use the “Interact” key to pick up evidence, but when you take down an enemy that key does nothing. You have to use the spacebar to get the sub menu to come up and then click on the option to report back to your central command that they are down or have been arrested.
    Then there are times when you shoot an enemy and they fly 10ft up into the air when they die before falling back to the ground. Not game breaking, more humorous than anything else but not something one would expect out of a finished product. One particularly annoying thing was that at times the AI would appear to surrender, only to go “Nope, nevermind” pull up their weapon and start shooting even though you had them dead to rights. It was bad enough that it made you just want to shoot any enemies rather than take the chance you’d get shot and not have enough health to take another hit before completing the map.


    I was unable to test out multiplayer as I never did find anyone else playing, so unless you and a few friends are going to go in on a few copies together, expect to play this solo.


    Even with the things mentioned before however, you could play the game and potentially have fun doing it. Visually it was OK but not an awesome looking game, and it is not all that fast paced, which one shouldn’t expect from a tactical shooter anyway, but the real game-breaking thing for me was that it never seemed to save my profile. Once I quit the game and would come back to it later none of my progress was saved and I would have to start from square one all over again. None of the upgraded weapons or gear I purchased, no extra guys on my squad, nothing.


    The developers for the game are active on Steam from what I could see and are publishing regular updates, so it is possible that after more time in development and continued improvements that the game could become better and maybe at some point even worth the asking price.

    CTU tries to create a similar experience to SWAT 4. Unfortunately it fails doing so for the most part. The game is not terrible and can potentially be fun with friends, but it's not the tactical shooter I was waiting for.

    Mechanics:
    The game has a good variety of different mechanics (Snake cam, various grenade types, leaning). Unfortunately most of them are not very well implemented. This is especially true for the movement: It is "floaty" meaning that you continue moving after releasing the key (even in "walk" mode). In general the controls feels unresponsive and clunky.

    Level Design:
    The level design is actually decent. You have multiple ways to go and different angles to cover. Potentially creating interesing tactical decisions. The biggest downside is, that there are only 4 different maps for the single player (+ 4 tutorial missions). There is a map editor in the game but it's in beta and is very bare bones. It has Steam Workshop integration however and a few people created maps already.

    AI:
    The AI is in my opinion the most important part for a tactical shooter especially the one for your teammates. Unfortunately this is the weakest part of the game. You can order your squad mates to either go to a specific point or to follow you around. The first option is very finnicky if you try to make them face a specific direction. When following they sometimes get stuck (on terrain or on each other) but it works for the most part.
    It gets bad when you try to open and clear a room. Their positioning in front of a door is terrible. When you tell to "Open and Clear" they will make one step into the doorframe and engage the first enemy in sight (which is usually the one diagonally across) blocking line of sight for you. Sometimes they bug out and attempt to clear the room they were coming from (doing a 180° turn in the door frame).
    If you give the order to "Open, Flash and Clear" they open the door (while not being in cover at all) and throw a flash greande in the room (which is ridiculously underpowered btw). If they don't see an enemy right away they will wait for the flash to go off and then attempt to clear the room. If they see an enemy though they will move into the room and try to engage him getting flashed by their own grenade.
    The enemy AI is pretty bad as well and you get away with just run and gunning if you don't care for score (arresting people gives way more score than killing them).


    Conclusion
    I think the game can be reasonable fun if you play it in co-op and restrict yourself to not play optimally and instead coordinate proper door breaches and stuff. The SP experience is very lackluster, though. Definitely not the game I was hoping for.

    CTU is obviously trying to be a successor to SWAT 4 and Rainbow Six 3. Which is great, we need a modern take on both games. Unfortunatly CTU falls short like all the other games that have tried.

    First of all, CTU seems to be using the default Unity character controller. This leads to "slippery" momentum based movement that feels just plain bad.
    It ships with very few missions on top of the unskippable tutorial missions.
    Poor pathfinding caused my ai teammates to get stuck on closed doors while they tried to reach me on the other side of the building many times.
    Settings don't seem to save after closing and relaunching the game.

    It's not all bad though. CTU has a few features that would have been welcomed in other games such as Takedown Red Sabre.
    There's career progression in the form of recruiting new officers and purchasing different equipment, including a shield which was missing from SWAT 4.
    There's a map editor. So if a large enough community forms, the lack of official missions could be fixed.
    You can lean while walking, and leaning causes your character to "step out" making it really easy to check a corner.
    CTU allows you to play a "run and gun" style, or a slower "no casualties" style, and rewards you more for arrests over kills. This is nice to see in a police themed game.

    Overall, there's a lot of potential here, but it's not worth it at it's current price and still needs a lot of work and polish. I would be a lot more hesitant to give this a thumbs down if it was an early access title. Keep looking SWAT players.
