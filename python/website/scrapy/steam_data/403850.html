About This Game

Sky to Fly: Faster Than Wind is a stunningly beautiful steampunk-themed runner about saving a sky-city from cruel pirates. Play on 20+ different levels, upgrade your ship and discover the mysteries of the malicious marauders.

This game lifts the veil on the wonderful world of Sky to Fly, where everything is out of the common run. It swings you to an alternative reality in which people had to abandon the solid earth buried under debris, for the islands floating high above. In the next three months we will give you an opportunity to engage in epic sky battles on a variety of airships, and in a year you may create your own civilization and challenge other members of the world of Sky to Fly. Meanwhile, enjoy 20 different levels in the Story mode and limitless missions from friends you are going to meet during the game, upgrade your ship and discover the mysteries of the malicious marauders.

Features:
  • 3 hours in the story mode
  • 20 core missions + 9 additional quests
  • 10 types of beautiful landscapes that will surprise even the most pampered player
  • 12 charismatic characters, who get you acquainted with the new virtual world
  • 4 types of ships to fly and upgrade
  • 8 crew members to hire for the unique playing experience
  • 9 types of enemies
  • 6 boosters to pick up while flying
  • 4 types of resources to collect

Tippy tap thats the sound of an iPad screen, well what should be an iPad screen.

Sky to Fly faster than I can say the title of this game is a game that would be right at home on a mobile device (i'm pretty sure its on google play!). Though this version has increased graphical quality over its mobile counterpart. The two main controls are left and right and well thats about it what do you expect.

You're a pilot for a ship and your goal is to reach the end of the level and by golly there are things in the way! Floating islands, weird animals and the most difficult to get past enemy ships. When completing a level you get a star rating out of 3. This means that anyone who is kind of a completionist like myself will be replaying levels for that 3 star rating. Since your ships goes at about the same pace without powerups or other bonuses found on the map its a little weird having "time" as one of the ratings in the ending screen.

You can change your ships crew giving you different bonuses which gives a small amount of customisation.
If you're a mobile enthusiast, temple run addict or anywhere in between you'll probably like this game. 7/10

Sky FTW is pretty neat, steampunk world with some silly characters and for $3.99 you can't really go wrong.

Posted: 7 April
I also covered this game on YouTube: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_VvjDmdHNAA

I am not normally a fan of mobile ports to the PC but Sky to Fly really surprised me. It's kind of like a runner except it's mission based as opposed to endless and it has a story, though it's pretty threadbare and also poorly translated.

The game play is done with only two keys (with a third later on) and largely involves dodging things as you get from place to place to complete story and side missions. You collect two different forms of currency along the way, which can be used to acquire better crew members with different traits, as well as upgrade your ship. It's not complex or difficult in the slightest but it has a very relaxes, zen feel. The art style is superb and conveys the unique world of floating islands very nicely and it's accompanied with very nice, chill music. This isn't a game you play for challenge or a massive sense of accomplishment, it's just relaxing fun.

This is a game that clearly has free-to-play roots from mobile as some mechanics like timers are still in this (though made so fast, they're utterly pointless) but there are no in-game purchases whatsoever and you're given plenty of currency and can finish the entire 3-4 hour adventure in one sitting if you want. Many formerly free-to-play mobile games that port to PC either leave the microtransactions in or jack up the price to an absurd level to avoid them. Sky to Fly only costs $4 and has no microtransactions. For me, the experience was an absolute steal at that price.

If you want something challenging or "hardcore", you're not going to find that with Sky to Fly but as someone who does love hardcore games, this was a nice little diversion when I just wanted to check out and chill for a while. You can't go wrong for the price in my opinion.

I give a non-recommendation for two things, firstly, completely broken games, and secondly, games that I find mediocre and/or flawed, Sky To Fly: Faster Than Wind falls into the later. It’s a running game set in a steampunk universe, and while it’s in Early Access I feel the game has shown its original roots and developer intentions.

Review - https://youtu.be/7j0p_KE1aVU

I’ll address the elephant in the room; yes it’s a mobile port. This is shown through the use of a premium currency and though it is obtainable in game it is extremely slow. Also the premium currency ships and crew members are superior to the ‘f2p’ options in every single way. It bares its ugly teeth when you have to wait 20 MINUTES! for a ship to recover its HP after a mission, but don’t worry, with some premium currency we’ll have ‘er up and runnin’ in no time!

Usual runner stuff applies, go through a level, pick up coins, dodge terrain and enemies, and use premium currency to give yourself a boost for that level. Unlike most running games though the controls are slow and stiff, and there is no feeling of speed. Your control options are either left or right and the use of an ‘active’ ability, this is less than your usual smart phone runner. Also the sense of difficulty is non-existent due to the slow gameplay and if you run into any difficulty it’s due to the limitation of the controls and generally poorly designed enemies, but if you do struggle you can spend that good ol’ premium currency and revive yourself!

Sky To Fly: Faster Than Wind is an example of a game that is extremely flawed for the PC space. While it may boasts great graphics and story mode for a smart phone game, this is the PC and the standards are different. The laziness shown through the keeping of premium currency and all the ‘features’ it entails is just something you can’t ignore, and turns something that could be a somewhat enjoyable experience sour.

Edit: The game is now fully released and none of the issues have been addressed. The points in the review still hold true.
