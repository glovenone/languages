About This Game

Inspired by the Disney/Pixar feature film, Toy Story and the 4D Toy Story Mania attraction at Disneyland Resort and Walt Disney World Resort, Toy Story Mania! is now available for PC. In addition to galleries adapted from the attraction, the game includes new and original galleries and a series of mini games, all hosted by beloved characters from the movies. The thematically-connected levels are designed for up to four players of all ages with competitive and co-operative multiplayer options. 'Toy Story Mania!' game logo Toy Story Mania! also includes bonus 3D features, transforming the game experience with eye-popping visuals. Join all the classic Toy Story characters in Toy Story Mania! as Woody, Buzz and the gang stage their own zany, fun-filled carnival. Celebrate the joy of play with family and friends of all ages and face off in the ultimate living room showdown.

TeamTom Reviews
( 0.6 hrs on record )
Posted: 21 August, 2015
Toy Story Mania is one of the many Disney released games on Steam over the years; this game is based on the idea of the extremely popular attraction in the Disney World Theme park, Hollywood Studios, in Orlando Florida. The queue times for the ride it self are sometimes over five hours! With the physical attraction being so popular, it is hard not to imagine Disney wouldn't try and squeeze a little bit more out of it in a video game.

It is essentially a shooting gallery style game, based around the Toy Story franchise. It was actually initially released on the iOS first, before later being released on PC and then consoles. The game had it's biggest hit on the Wii since this also came packed with a peripheral device in the European markets to use to play.

The gameplay it self is very simple and family friendly, just aim with the mouse and click to fire. You can be throwing pies, darts, rings, balls and all sorts in the six different galleries. Different targets grant more points, there are also a number of different tasks to complete on each stage such as knocking over a certain amount of one target or finding a secret entrance etc. These add a little variety to the stage rather than a simple shoot and aim mechanic.

Unfortunately the graphics in Toy Story Mania really let it down. You cannot play in 1080P which nowadays is an absolute must, and there are minimal graphical options which can sometimes look like you are playing a extremely outdated game. It doesn't look like any work was done at all on the PC port of the game as it is so poor graphically.

Whilst each stage offers a spinet of a story, there is no overlaying plot, I would look at this game as a casual pick up and play sort of game. Don't expect any new story from the Toy Story gang.

All of the original voice acts play their respective roles in Toy Story Mania, some of which I believe are just ripped straight out of the films, but I believe there are some original lines to be found. This is a huge bonus when you compare to Toy Story 3 on Steam, which don't have the full cast.

Each of the gallerias are well designed and offer a lot of variety on each stage, I really liked how the whole screen can be full of targets to hit, but I still had trouble hitting them! There is so much to look at and you only have a small amount of time to do it in, you really feel a sense of urgency on each level.

Another negative point would be the game's length, I actually finished the whole game's single player mode in just over half an hour. Toy Story Mania really encourages you to replay levels to get better scores and to earn more tickets to unlock new characters and bonus stages, so if you really want to squeeze every little bit of content out, you can probably get about five hours to action here.

This game is no by no means difficult. It is clearly heavily aimed towards a younger audience, which is a shame since the majority of the Toy Story fans will have grown up with it and be in their mid twenties now. You can get to the harder stages by really aiming to get the very high scores on each stage though.

Apart from the horrible graphical options and no higher resolution support, I didn't have any other technical problems such as crashing or bugs. It's just a badly ported PC game.

To conclude, it's actually a bit of a difficult one for me - I really enjoyed playing Toy Story Mania, mainly because the ride at the theme park is my absolute favorite and I love the idea that I can boot this game up and feel like I am back at Disney World about to go onto the ride instead of waiting for five hours in the blistering sun.

People who don't have that nostalgic feeling will probably find this game dull, and boring. So I am still going to give this a thumbs up review, but with a recommendation to only pick it up when on sale for about £5 if you want to try it out. Fans of the ride will really want to jump on here and bask in nostalgia

Tom's Score Card
1) Stay away
2) Only recommended when on sale
3) Recommended
4) Highly recommended
5) This is a must play
