About This Game

Welcome to the Arena, where high-ranking warriors are transformed into spineless mush. Abandoning every ounce of common sense and any trace of doubt, you lunge onto a stage of harrowing landscapes and veiled abysses. Your new environment rejects you with lava pits and atmospheric hazards as legions of foes surround you, testing the gut reaction that brought you here in the first place. Your new mantra: Fight or be finished.

System Requirements

    3-D Hardware Accelerator with full OpenGL® support, Pentium® 233 Mhz MMX®processor with 8 MB Video Card or Pentium II 266 Mhz processor with 4 MB Video Card, or AMD® 350 Mhz K6®-2 processor with 4 MB Video Card, 64 MB RAM, a 100% Windows® XP/Vista compatible computer system (including compatible 32-bit drivers for video card, sound card and input devices), 25 MB of uncompressed hard disk space for game files (Minimum Install), plus 45 MB for the Windows swap file, 100% DirectX 3.0 or higher compatible sound card, 100% Microsoft-compatible mouse and driver, joystick (optional)

I bought this game for at least three times: one was a copy in cracked version of CD ROM format back in nearly more than 15 years ago.
2nd time: bought from a store in New Zealand with valid verification code.
3rd time: bought on STEAM, ironically the verification code is invalid.

game experience: this game recalls my time and sweat in childhood when my mates and me spent countless hours practicing on T2 and at that time, CPL league re-defined the gaming industry in early shape of so called' cyber-athelete'. Makavelli, Fatal1ty, Blue, the names were heroes and iconic which starts the frenzy trend of cyber gaming circel.

Q3A was the only game that re-shaped the FPS world, entered new era of mass media and audience even watching on satelite TV for example: NOW. That was the earliest version of live streaming, however the nowadays live streaming only begins its birth 3 years go by Twitch.

Q3A also was hardware killer with the awareness aroused by gamers for OpenGL powered 3DFX, and no ticket was given for Nvidia.More rams , more powerful display card drives the hardware industry. Interesting scene.

Back to the game, it still has its unique gameplay experience with ruthless NPCs in Nightmore mode and the duels, FFA, deathmatches still co-composed the epic world of best FPS game ever.

Oh man. I remember when my uncle and his friend would bring their monster PCs over to the house when I was 10 years old and played the crap out of this game, they had lan parties all the time. When they went to take a break I asked to play.

My mind was blown. This game was amazing to play and enjoy.

3 frags left.
This is one of those games where investing a lot of time into it and mastering it has a lot to show. I remember when playing it I would get totally destroyed, after a while of getting dominated I'd see patterns, hops, and start seeing why I was getting decimated.

Once I would adopt new strategies and work around my weaknesses I would go from getting demolished to absolutely destroying other players. You'd have people going from killing you with rail and LG, to them running away when you pick up an 8-ball or Rail.

Not a lot of people play this nowadays but It is THE staple arena shooter. This is a game I'll probably play and enjoy until the end of time.
