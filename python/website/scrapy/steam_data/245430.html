Begin your adventure in Crusaders of the Dark Savant. Import your characters from Bane of the Cosmic Forge or create a new party capable of surviving the rigors of a strange alien world.

Both friends and foes you'll discover as you search for the forgotton secret of a once madman - the secret of the very universe itself. And you won't be alone in your search. Others as enterprising as you have also entered this dimension. Only your actions - both past and present - will determine just how well you fare. From the depths of a dungeon to the blue and limitless skies, you'll see it all - you'll hear it all - in this latest Wizardry adventure.

Never
Never has a game been as true to authentic fantasy role playing. Never has a game had four different beginnings. Never has a game been so vast.

Sometimes
Sometimes you won't find everything as you left it; you're not alone in this world. Sometimes the people you meet will like you... if you've been nice to their friends.

Forever
Forever true to the most acclaimed fantasy role-playing simulation ever, the sequel to Bane of the Cosmic Forge. Forever beautiful graphics and sound - indoors and out. Forever in the tradition of... Wizardry

I played the game years ago, but never completed it. Bought it again so I could finally finish the game. A thoroughly enjoyable, but very difficult, crpg. The amount of race/class combinations and abilities is astounding. The puzzles are almost impossible to figure out without the hint book. Luckily, you get the hint book for free in the program's file folder. Expect to reload saves often.

I imported a party from Wizardry 6. One downside is that it took a very long time before I found weapons and armor better than what I had imported with my party from the previous game. So a lot of the early treasure seems a bit worthless.

It takes a long time to finish this game. Especially if you don't download one of the many save game file editors that are available. Expect to spend years trying to beat the game if you don't use the hint book. Some of the mazes are extremely complicated.

You certainly get a sense of accomplishment when you finally do complete the game.

Recommended if you like Dungeons and Dragons, but want a few more options when leveling your character. If you liked "Eye of the Beholder" or Wizardry 6, you'll probably like this as well. Just keep in mind that the puzzles and mazes are significantly more complicated than "Eye of the Beholder." Certainly beatable with the use of the hint book.

Oh wow, a blast from the past that requires some love.

20 years ago, I thought I was good at RPGs. Then I played Crusaders of the Dark Savant and realized I was good at following the bread crumbs that easy to moderate RPGs lay out for us.

This game was incredibly difficult in the era when there was no gamefaqs.com to download walkthroughs on. When FAQs became a common theme, I re-installed and found it was still pretty tough. That being said, it has a unique world setting, a greatly diverse character class and race structure, and an extensive story-line that is unique and engaging.

Wizardry is not a world for the feint of heart, but for those who embrace a challenge, love RPGs and have an affection for Old School gaming, Crusaders of the Dark Savant is a challenge you must embrace. A stellar game that received far too little fanfare in it's day and is deserving of re-incarnation.

Alright Wizardry 7. I hope you have played Wizardry 6 before thinking playing this Game. ;-)
This may have the better graphics and the sound is pretty amazing here, but rust me, play Wizardry 6 before or don't play this series at all.

You ask why?
Well it's pretty simple while Wizardry 6 is hard as hell, I feel it's still waaaaay easier than this one. Additionally if you finish Wizardry 6 you can import your Party winto W7 and continue in this adventure.
Generally the Wizardry Series tend to be much harder than other dungeon crawler games, even the older ones, so if you're new to the genre, I really suggest you try Lands of Lore 1 and then Eye of the Beholder first.

Alright, let's talk about W7.
The game is highly complex with a ton of spells, most of them useful in at least some scenarios. There are plenty of riddles in this game, a lot of them also requires to carefully read and note basically everything. A lot of information may just appear dead, but at some point, you need it.
The battles are sometimes tough and sometimes really easy. You can adjust the difficulty in some kind, that if the game gets too difficult you can change the classes of your party, which makes a lot of encounters trivial. But it may require some grinding at some point, altough I think you can make it through without senseless grinding. There are plenty of random encounters anyway.
The much harder thing in this game are the riddles and "quests". Did I mention that you don't have a quest book? Well you don't have one. The good thing is: You have a minimap. Well kind of. You have to raise the card drawing skill first and even then, it's not a full minimap.
The riddles and dungeon mechanics are so hard that I think if you play this game the first time without consultating a walkthrough you probably end up with 750 - 1000h of gameplay.
The game content is about 3 times as big as in Wizardry 6 and the map is huge. Additionally there are plenty of dungeons and they have some of the most annoying mechanics I've ever seen, which requires some map drawings even if you have the minimap.

I really suggest this game to anyone who likes dungeon crawling games and is looking for a real challenge. This is as hard as it can get.
