About This Game

Make the play with your favorite Major League Baseball team and win the World Series! R.B.I. 16 features enhanced lighting and graphics, revamped defensive AI, an improved batting system, and the ability to sim through games in Season mode. Feel the action with new fielding moves like dives, wall catches, pump-fakes and more! Take your skills to the next level and compete online.

* Play Season, Postseason, Exhibition and Multiplayer modes
* Modify your lineup with complete MLB rosters or play classic R.B.I. Baseball 16-player rosters
* Downloadable roster updates throughout the 2016 season
* Track your season stats by team, player and league leaders across multiple seasons
* 30 Authentic MLB ballparks feature enhanced lighting, field and graphics
* Adjustable difficulty settings

This game keeps it simple and doesn't waste your time. When a batter strikes out, it gets right to the next one without making you watch him walk up to the plate. When a play is finished, there are no slow motion replays or commentary to slow down the action. You can wrap up a nine-inning game in no time and move right on to the next one, which makes playing an entire season seem like an actual possibility.

It sticks to the basics. There's a roaring crowd, cheerful organ music, and umpires to tell you who's safe and who's out. Despite its simplicity, it also features all the real-life teams, players, and stadiums, keeping you updated with all the latest roster updates.

The controls are nice and simple. They remind me of the old days of playing on the SNES as a kid. The pitching and batting are two-dimensional, so the ball never goes above or below the strike zone. The options let you manually control the fielders or give you AI assistance. You can also specify your favorite team, so you can spend less time in the menus and more time playing ball.

It's a simple baseball game the way it should be. Just like Mom used to make (if your mom used to make video games).

OK, so this was more of a bunch of how-to tips instead of a review... As for getting the game, I'd stick with MLB The Show if you have a Playstation. I love the RBI Baseball games on Steam and 16 is no exception. It has working multiplayer which is rare in the baseball genre on the PC. It's best to have friends on your list to play the game against. Finding random players is rare because the playerbase is so small.

Aside from that, there have been overall improvements to the game. Graphics look much better than in 15 and the pitching is fixed. Batting system has been improved. New fielding moves (automatic). The AI has been revamped and has downloadable roster updates as the season progresses.

This game is arcadey, fast paced and simplistic. Don't let the word simplistic fool you though, it's still a lot of fun.

My only gripes are the Max FPS not having a disable option (as it is now you can only choose 30fps or 60fps) and when enabling v-sync the game suffers from a bit of studder\lag. We need an option to disable Max FPS. Also, V-Sync seems like it needs more work and be polished up some.

If you've played a previous RBI baseball game, this is more of the same

Arcade controls, overall lacks depth, no career mode (single season + playoffs only)

Pitching is shallow: you can throw a fastball or a slowball, and you can control if the ball goes inside or outside the plate. No pitcher specific pitches, so no high heat from your closer followed by a nasty slider.

The presentation is, again, shallow. Very little to no commentary, the splash screen between innings is pretty bad. It's somewhere they could have easily put some work into, but didn't.

Team management is absent: no trades, no AAA promotions, no FA.

It is a real MLB game though: stadiums, uniforms (including throw-backs), and players are all here.

Overall very simple to control, you can pick it up and play it easily.

Has both online and local multi-player.

At this price, and considering it is the only MLB licensed game on PC atm, it's worth playing if you need to scratch that baseball itch.

Wish there was a neutral vote for game rating. It's not a bad game, it's just not a great game. It doesn't seem fair to hold not being a AAA title against it when it only cost $19.99.
