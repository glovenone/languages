What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Castle Story is a game in active development, meaning there are bugs and missing gameplay mechanics. Purchase Castle Story if you’re interested in seeing a game get made but only if you’re not afraid of bugs, missing features and wonky gameplay.
Castle Story is a very ambitious project created by a small, independent team and backed by a great kickstarter community! We’re constantly working on improving the game and introducing new gameplay mechanics.

We’d love to hear your opinion on our game! If you find bugs or have interesting ideas to share, please let us know on our forums at http://forums.castlestory.net”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We don't have a specific release date at this point. We'd like to continue adding content to the game and release it once we feel the project is complete.”

About This Game

An epic adventure awaits you in Castle Story, a strategy game where you command friendly creatures called Bricktrons. Have them explore, carve, dig and gather resources from vast, spectacular floating islands, reshaping the voxel-based world to your desire!

Design and build any structure you can imagine! From mighty and legendary castles to sprawling Bricktron villages. Beware, your Bricktrons will have to take arms and defend themselves against mysterious creatures, the Corruptrons. Build catapults and hurl explosive barrels at the invaders, or lure them into strategically placed traps! The physics system will also challenge the sturdiness of your architecture! A tower whose foundation is destroyed will crumble to the ground realistically.

Castle Story is also a multiplayer experience! Challenge your friends to a round of Conquest or join forces with them to repel the enemies in the Co-Op Invasion gamemode. Of course, you can also group up in sandbox and build together!

The maps in Castle Story are meticulously hand-crafted by the developers and we’re giving you access to the same tool we use! Create your own worlds with our awesome World Editor, share them with the community and try out other player-made maps!

Posted: 15 June
I have got this game for a long time. I did build a lot of castles, both in Survival/Invasion and Sandbox modes. I did not do much in the Conquest mode, even though it is fun as well. What I like and what is CS good at:

Being a civil engineer I love the construction aspect: you can really build amazing castles and see how they stand against invasion waves of enemy hordes. Or you can build a set of defensive walls and trenches with towers. You can do a combination of those. The choice is yours and you can see your strategy either fail or succeed. It is kind of like the tower defence games but in 3D with real structural physics where you have to think it trough and then build it up brick by brick. I always enjoyed constructing buildings and structures in Minecraft. And this gives you another aspect: combat level.

You also have to set up archers and knights around and organize them in the most effective manner because this game is not forgiving.

This game had some issues in past. I think it had developed well from those "dark ages" and is now very well playable giving players lots of fun, with even more to come in future.

What I dislike: Friendly fire: the archers sometimes hit their own, knights are too weak (in version 0.6x). Sometimes it has to be reloaded due to stuck or unresponsive bricktrons.

Final word: I do enjoy this game but is it good for you as well? Do you like building castles and defences? Do you like strategy thinking how to protect your area? Do you like games with combat and some basic resource management? If yes this game is for you too.

Early Access Review
Posted: 26 June
OK, so a while back I did a review on this game. I still said it was positive, but I basically said "It's buggy but it'll get better". And now I feel is the time to re-review it.

Let me start off by saying this is one of the coolest and prettiest castle builders I've seen in a long while. It has a near perfect arrangement of blocks to suit all of your construction needs, yet it simultaniously keeps you materials as basic as possible. You can build with Stone, or Wood. There are a few other materials that can be used to make useful structures, but those are the two things your castle is really gonna be made of. Yet in that simplicity, you can make very cool-looking castles with ease. And what's even cooler is that in the survival gamemodes, all of the castles you built for purpose and purpose alone still turn out looking amazing.

So let's talk about how you build castles. Basically, you have little yellow blobby units called "bricktrons", which you have to tell what to do. This can consist of mining, cutting trees, building your castle, repairing damages, etc. Getting them to do these things is very simple. Want to build something? Create a construction task, place blueprints, and assign bricktrons to the task, they do the rest. The way they go about building these things still isn't the best... but it gets the job done.

Then there's combat. So there's two survival gamemodes: Invasion, and conquest. Conquest has you go and capture crystals, and invasion has you defend against infinitely increasing waves of enemies. But both will have you be fighting 3 enemies: Little corruptrons, medium corruptrons, and big corruptrons. Not the most original lineup, but bare with me here. Anyway these 3 classes will act exactly like you expect. Little ones are fast, and big ones are slow but deadly. You have 3 ways of killing them. Warrior bricktrons with swords, Archer bricktrons, or catapults. In my opinion, the combat is probably one of the weaker points of the game, especially now that they nerfed your warriors. So the waves can get unfairly difficult, and you absolutely need a catapult. But even then, it's hard to learn how to lead your shots. It's not bad per say, but just be aware this game can get pretty brutal.

Real quick I'll talk about the visuals, they are just shy of a masterpeice! Fantastic texture work, a great lighting engine, dynamic shadows on the higher settings, and they did a great job of bumpmapping everything. Unfourtunately the frames can take a dip at times, and there's no real particle affects, so the distance can look a bit ugly.

And I'll be honest, the game still has some bugs. It still crashes from time to time (though it's not really all that bad) Bricktrons can have some quirky pathfinding at times, and they for some reason really want to place blocks they can't reach. Which really bothers me. Also, sometimes your knights will refuse to retreat when you tell them to, and just get pummeled. Which, again, can get very troublesome. I would advise saving regularly if you plan on playing survival.

But this game absolutely oozes charm and love. As you build your castle you grow attatched to both it, and your little bricktron minions. (For example there was one moment where I had gotten a builder stuck, so I killed him with my catapult. And that genuinley made me sad.) You start seeing your castle as the home of these little guys, and every once in a while, you pan your camera down, see all your bricktrons working, see the gorgeous lighting and shadows, as well as the majesty of your castle, and your immediately reminded why you play this game.

So, should you buy it? It depends. You can't speed up time (and for good reason, optimizing that would be nightmare for anyone) so you'll be waiting a while as your bricktrons slowly build up your castle. For me, this further strengthens the bond between me and what I've built, because I sit there thinking about how much time I've put into it. But some people aren't as patient and will get bored. Which I'm sure is where some of the more recent negative votes come from. (And some people are saying it's to complicated... what? This is as simple as they come... you wanna see a complex sandbox game, go look at Dwarf Fortress!) So ask yourself these questions:
1: Are you a sentimental person?
2: Are you patient?
3: Can you deal with a few rough edges?

If you answered no to any of those questions, then I'd say castle story either isn't for you, or maybe you should wait a little longer for more updates.

I'm torn on this one. The idea is neat. It is not what I was expecting- I was thinking the units were more automatic a la Dwarf Fortress and randomly built a castle based on certain constraints and orders you set. No, instead, you literally choose to place a blueprint for each and every block of the castle. This is great too, but the issues arise when fighting the rough UI for terrain management and the like. The game has its own style and charm. However, at this point it has very, very awkward controls. Once you begin to get the hang of the system, it gets better, but there is still a lot of fighting against the UI when trying to align zones or work easily by switching bricks (everything runs through a right click/drag menu that has a slowness to it.)

Also, and this isn't new for games of this nature with AI beings, you will find your Bricktrons (workers) more often than not standing around doing nothing because they can't find a path, can't find resources that are next to them, or for no apparent reason whatsoever... I just had to quit my first game because a small archer tower I was making was simply deemed impossible to build by my workers- no matter how I tried to rectify issues, they stood there doing nothing, either complaining about resoruces that were i a nearby stockpile, or unable to path to some point, even though there were stairs or other means of traversal. Not a unique problem for games of this sort, but it is in need of work as of June 2016.

Now, all that said, I think the game is headed for a successful conclusion, it simply needs more TLC from the devs, who seem to be on the ball. If I could ask for one thing though, it would be a more streamlined UI with ease of use (and speed) in its place. For instance, once in "build" mode, you could quickly choose block types via the scroll wheel. I currently give it a yes, but with the knowledge it is not as smooth an experience as other games of its sort at this point. Looking forward to future iterations.
