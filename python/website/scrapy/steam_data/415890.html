About This Game

Crystal Picnic is a lighthearted and colourful tribute to the classic era of action RPGs! Join a sarcastic gardener and a wannabe knight as they journey across the kingdom chasing after ants who stole magic crystals from the castle. Oh, and did we mention the ants have gone mad because they're EATING those crystals? Yeah, that makes things much more unpredictable! Hours of exploration, mesmerizing platform-style combat, plenty of new friends to meet and loads of wacky enemies to encounter. When you fight chubby birds and ants carrying bazookas, you know you're in for a good time!

Crystal Picnic

Crystal Picnic is a nice little RPG tribute to the classic ages of gaming. The game is fairly well-done except for some problems currently existing. I will add a TL;DR at the end.

Gameplay

Crystal Picnic is an RPG designed with colourful creatures and interesting characters. You start off as Egbert a kind-hearted and pure Duck who has been trying to join the Royal Guard for quite some time now unsuccessfully, and he is with his good friend Frogbert, a common gardener who helps his friend. You are near the castle when all of a sudden ants dig out and walk right on by with crystals from the castle, which begins the main plot of the storyline.

Control-wise the game is fairly simple, however do note that the controls are not actually introduced to you at first. This makes it so that depending on how you play (Controller/Keyboard) you will have to do a bit of a guessing game. The combat portion of the game is not like most RPG games with a turn-based mechanic, but rather has a real-time combat situation where you and your computer controlled companions will fight against whatever you come across. The combat is not bad at all, however the programming of your companions is another story. What would usually happen is that I would fight and my companion would end up running into the enemies usually getting themselves injured or killed. It became a tad bit frustrating and I would prefer it there was an option to not include them in combat (You would fight independently and if you fall you can come back as your companion to help minimize the mistakes a CPU is capable of making). The game also has a nice mini-game of whack-a-skunk and overall is a very playful and nice environment. The maps however can get a tad bit large and annoying to traverse especially with the ability I can always slip over a banana and get attacked by one group, then another, and one more just in case. Once you are able to grasp the controls nicely this becomes quite a relaxing and enjoyable experience for the more casual gamers, and the story is just a tad silly.

Graphics/Sound

For an pixelated RPG the graphics are rather impressive and overall polished for the characters and level design. Very reminiscent of the older days of gaming when it all started to develop. The music in this game is superb and I honestly loved it, it was perfect for the game and was able to make a very relaxing experience and improve the game's overall quality. The only problem is I'd like an option to disable all other sounds except the music at some points so I can just listen while playing.


Pros/Cons

Pros

+ Very polished Graphically
+ Fun style of combat
+ Good relationship between Egbert and Forgbert making an authentic feeling of friendship between the two
+ Music is beautifully relaxing
+ Not hard

Cons

- No introduction to controls
- Random combats can become annoying when you get caught in a loop of more than one battle with no time to even move
- No sort of map system or assistance with objectives
- Problem where to consume the potion/item you want you have to first use whatever item is first then you can select the item you want
- No options menu

Suggestions

These are suggestion that I feel should be added to the game to polish it and make it more enjoyable

- Quick introduction to controls at the begging or an options menu where you can see the controls
- Option to fight independently in combat so you don't have to worry about your companion
- An objective assister or local map which can help guide you

Final Thoughts

Crystal Picnic has overall done a good job expect for the problems which have already been listed. I was able to enjoy my time playing this even with the little snips that gave me a bit of rage. The music always helped me to relax into this game and I honestly love Egbert and Frogbert's relationship, truly enjoyable there. The storyline was quite silly in my opinion, but I was a duck trying for the royal guard and whose best friends was a frog. Overall yeah I'd say this game was worth my time and be for a more casual gamer who simply want's to relax and enjoy something silly (I especially love the "The Accused" achievement showing the developers have a sense of humor).

TL;DR

Although an enjoyable experience, Crystal Picnic has its fair share of issues. The most annoying being that there is no introduction to the controls. The game succeeds in its goal of feeling like a classic RPG from the simpler time of the gaming industry. The soundtracks helps to fully immerse you into the game and overall is one of the things I was able to love about this game. Crystal Picnic is definitely enjoyable for those who like the genre and are not too picky,
so I would recommend this to more casual gamers who are able to look at the work and effort seen in this title, because once you figure out the controls (whether it be controller or keyboard) it's actually a pretty fun title.

I just had two annoying bugs: the game crashed when I tried to push stones over the jump points in the cavern area. Additionally, I could not open a certain treasure box because the game froze when I tried. Perhaps the developers can work on that.

All in all, I good game with a lot of humour and adorable characters in which the rpg elements are nicely blended with jump and run.

Controls arent explained, most items arent explained, and either no or poor explaination of what you are supposed to do. Meandering, complex areas with no local map system. The view feels too close, causing it to further complicate while navigating. Pointless (and unpleasant to me) combat system with the illusion of avoiding fighting enemies, except you slip on banana peels every three seconds forcing you to fight all enemies on screen. There is no xp to and only the chance to get a couple coins rarely dropped, where the only things to buy are a couple different healing items you probably wont need anyways, so it really is pointless. I honestly cannot see why people have found this so appealing.
