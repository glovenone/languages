About This Game

ZombieRun is a retro platformer with the aim to bring sexy back to local multiplayer gaming. It's you, your friends and guns against all the living dead roaming around. If that's not enough there are also apples, carrots and superpowers in play.

Story

After a massive apocalyptic thunderstorm you find yourself in the middle of a forest graveyard with two zombies drooling over you. It seems that the thunder wasn’t the only thing that hit the world while you were out. It’s time to act fast, stay alive and get to the city for some answers. This is where it all begins it's you your fast reflexes and guns agains all the dead running around.

Features

The game features classical weapons, gang of living dead and superpowers. Yes, you have SUPERPOWERS. So get your cape on and start saving the world. ZombieRun has addictive single player mode and awesome local multiplayer games. Up to 4 players can play at the same time in either single screen co-op mode, where you have to work together as one or split screen competitive mode, where everyone's racing to the finish. And also there's the Battle mode where players can blast at each other with what they've got.

ZombieRun has controller support so dust of the old joypads and get cracking.

Game modes

So we’ve got intense solo mode where there’s only you against the world. Then we have co-op mode where up to 4 players can tackle the enemies as one team. And no we don’t leave anyone behind. Then there’s also the competitive mode that allows also up to 4 players to compete in split screen. Competitive mode is like a race to the finish. The one who’s first wins. Is it all already. No it’s not. I left my favorite to last. It’s the battle mode. This is straight up shoot everything that moves game mode. You go head to head with other players and only one can come out of the game as the winner. There are no limits in battle mode, everything is allowed, we actually encourage some elbow action when it seems that you might be losing.

This game was reviewed using a code sent by the developer.

ZombieRun is an action platformer that has it's problems.

The screenshots and information shown on the store page for this game initially had me very interested, I haven't seen many platformers take on the theme of Zombies before so was eager to play. As with all other platforming games; I plugged in my Xbox controller and sat back in my chair to play. Sadly upon loading up, I was faced with my first problem; I was unable to navigate through the main menu with the controller. While this isn't a massive issue, it is the sort of thing I have come to expect from games like this, especially when they are marked as "Full controller" support on their store page.

I navigated through all the menu options as I always do, wanting to check out the controls, this is where I found my second issue. The controller page is just horrendously messy. I didn't know what actions were referring to which buttons, it was just all over the place. This option may as well be taken out as it stands as it will be no use to anybody, other than a hindrance.

So I start the game, thinking the controls can't be that hard to figure out as I can always test buttons as soon as I am in the game. NOPE, as soon as you load into the first level, you have to effectively move out of the way of an enemy more or less straight away otherwise you die. While you may not think this is a problem, it is, especially when the controller doesn't work...sadly it is just not me who has the problem, many people online are also complaining of it too. I'm sure you know, playing a platformer game with keyboard is challenging at it's best, but I tried, sadly again the controls were even more of an issue. In order to move around you have to use the arrow keys, and you shoot with your WASD keys, who'd a thought it? This to me is completely backwards.

The actual game play is very basic too; this may as well call it self a rogue-like too, because you only have one life and if you get hit by a zombie, you go right back to the start; it's brutally hard. I managed to clear a few levels before managing the controls and completing jumping puzzles became too much of an issue.

So in conclusion, ZombieRun has major controller issues which virtually makes the game unplayable after the first few levels, and even though it's a really cheap game, you'll still feel ripped off if you pick it up and try it.
