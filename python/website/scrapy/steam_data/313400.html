About This Game


In this long awaited shooter from action masters Cross Eaglet, enemies are not just right in front of you; they're all around you! Using a unique perspective rotation mechanic, players can bring enemies and items into view, putting the odds in their favor in an otherwise impossible situation.



In Revolver 360 RE:ACTOR, you don't only have your bullets to save you. You're also armed with a powerful laser that pierces through weaker enemies, and a screen-clearing EMP. It's the exact kind of weaponry you're going to need if you hope to survive this harsh and hostile environment.

Use your weapons in concert with each other to toy with your enemies and reap higher score rewards. Killing your foes quickly is not always the best course of action.



Revolver 360 RE:ACTOR comes with a full suite of challenging mini-missions that will not only teach you advanced techniques to improve your scoring and survivability, but also challenge the most hardcore of shmup players with insane scenarios. Not quite sure how to complete a challenge? Challenge mode also has a dynamic hint system to help you learn how to properly surmount a challenge.

DAAAAAAAMN! THIS GAME IS AWESOME!
REVOLVER360 RE:ACTOR is a shoot'em up game; but it has a very interesting difference from other games in this genre: you can rotate the enviroment!
For example, you can use this ability to line up several rows of enemies and destroy them at once or to avoid screen filling attacks. This ability also made the game a lot challenging since the enemies and some goodies are placed around the enviroment. In addition to this ability, you have access to a straight laser attack which can eliminate enemies and some projectiles, and an overdrive move which removes every projectile from the screen.
There are three modes to play in this game. I recommend completing the challenges before playing through Reactor mode to learn the basics.

Positive Points:
- The enviroment rotation mechanic is very innovative and has made the game unique
- Multiple paths in stages
- Nice difficulty (The game is hard at first. But when you get used to the mechanics it becomes easier.)
- Nice visuals and soundtracks (Soundtracks are bundled with the game.)

Negative Points:
- TOO MUCH BLUE COLOUR!

Notes:
- The game can freeze on launch due to one reason: downloading scoreboards. To avoid this, check ((Offline Mode)) in game's config file which is in the game's main directory.
- I haven't finished the game, so it's early to tell the game is short or not. I'll update the review later about this.

Fast, blue, stylish and clean. Ikaruga's wilder, more futuristic cousin I think. That's a good place to start when discussing RE:ACTOR. From Cross Eaglet, this bullet hell shmup takes a simple premise and adds a unique mechanic, instead of polarity, you're dealing with rotation. The field of play can be rotated along a 360 degree axis around your ship. In effect, you're flying through a "cylinder" as opposed to a flat playfield as is often the case, with enemies swirling all around you, and the landscape forming a three dimensional tube that often branches and splits, with numerous junctions appearing that will allow you to choose your route (this offers -immense- replayability as you high score hunt or figure out new routes through various stages).

As you rotate the field of view, the bullets also rotate, so a near impossible field to navigate suddenly becomes a flat line that's easy to dodge, and all of a sudden the game forces you to think in three dimensions, with objects often coming at you from "depth" as well as from the top, sides, front and back. It would be a bit easy to lose track were it not for the utterly gorgeous aesthetic which keeps things simple and easy to keep track of. Everything is presented in a washed out, futuristic neon blue, clean lines and clear presentation keep the game running at a flawless 60fps no matter what you're doing, with everything maxed out on my rig the game looks nothing short of artistic (Oh god please let there be an occulus rift option down the line).

As is the norm for such japanese bullet hell games, the music is energetic, electronic and upbeat, it maintains a strong pace and a good sense of place, but isn't exactly going to reach the heady heights that Ikaruga managed (but then nothing is ever going to manage the soundscape of Ikaruga, let's be fair). It's good enough though, as is the sound effects, which do the job with satisfying explosions and gun noises, everything sounds correct, which is really all that needs to be said. You don't buy these games for the sound, but it's good to see that Cross Eaglet got everything lined up and the sound presentation is clearly up to spec.

What makes this game stand out is the rotational mechanic, which is nothing short of inspired, it imposes a learning curve, and with an Xbox or PS pad, you'll be wise to map it to the shoulder buttons, but after half an hour of getting used to it, you'll be swooping back and forth like a pro, and the game opens up, once you make it past the opening stage, you'll then find junctions everywhere, and you'll be able to navigate through a gorgeous cityscape.

It's hard, as any good bullet hell should be, but it's worth every second of your attention, offering visual and gameplay delights as you duck and weave your way through a constant barrage of firepower. Each boss makes full use of the rotation mechanic too, forcing you to quickly improvise and adjust to the constant incoming flak that they toss your way, and within a few goes, rotating will be second nature, you may even look at other bullet hells and feel a little... lacking... when you try to change perspective and find you can't.

At this price, it's an absolute steal as well, for what you are paying for, you're getting one of the best experiences since Ikaruga, if you're a fan of the shmup genre, this is an easy sell, if you're new to the genre, getting this and Ikaruga should be high on your priority list. Both of these games are well worth your attention.

I have an odd relationship with shmups because I'm not that great at them. I don't always manage to complete all of the highest regarded ones. The best just feel awesome to play, though.

This game has a silly name but the triggers (if on a controller) rotate the screen around the action. This lets you turn a screen full of bullets into a single line and then use a laser to blast the bullets apart for points. It sounds like a gimmick but it's a lot of fun and provides a really interesting challenge and a one of the most fun and unique scoring systems I've seen.

The rotation mechanic is also important because you auto-kill items and enemies that are in the background, which you can only see/target if you are rotated a certain way. There are sections where you have to keep your screen facing "forward" to destroy obstacles in the way, while balancing the need to rotate to survive enemy shots.

I think there may be 4 levels with bosses. I'm not sure because I have only managed to beat the stage 3 boss in the single mission mode. I'm definitely going back though, and I recommend it highly.
