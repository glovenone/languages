About This Game


The evil emperor Zaraaima has kidnapped the infant heir to the land of Dareca. King Kandar must call upon the aid of the seven gods of Dareca as he traverses the six worlds of his kingdom in a bid to save his son. He must face the emperor’s hordes as he battles through dark woods, slimy swamps, sinister mills, infernal volcanos, snowy mountains, golden cities and demonic castles. Each world is guarded by level bosses each sworn to bring about the king’s swift demise. To help him in his quest King Kandar can invoke god-given armour, wield different weapons and cast fanatical spells. Can the King save his son and his kingdom or will Dareca fall into the evil emperor’s grasp?



The original soundtrack will be available on installation. Music files can be found in the Cast of the Seven Godsends folder under "Soundtrack"

Features


  • Easy controls - only two buttons to play (shoot and jump)
  • Six very different and long worlds to conquer
  • Checkpoint save positions throughout worlds
  • Five basic weapons to master
  • Seven god-like armour sets
  • 15 different bonus items to boost your hero
  • Thirty-five magic combat spells to utilise
  • Over 50 different enemies
  • Twelve mid and end-of-level bosses
  • Four difficulty levels
  • Different endings
  • Hand-drawn pixel graphics with a 16-bit look-and-feel
  • Special art gallery with achievements to unlock
  • Easter eggs to discover.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 or higher
    • Processor: Pentium 4 1.7Ghz / Athlon XP 1700+
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Any DirectX 10 / Shader Model 3.0 or upper compatible GPU (for instance: GeForce 8500 GT, Mobility Radeon HD 5650)
    • DirectX: Version 10
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
    • Sound Card: Any DirectX9 compatible soundcard or higher

In a nutshell it's Ghosts 'n Goblins mixed with Mega Man. The price point is well chosen, so if you like 8/16 bit platform games you should really consider to give this one a try.

The game isn't easy, but it is fair and the levels aren't very innovative, but still well designed. The gameplay reminded me a lot of Ghosts 'n Goblins and I think that is intended. It's really more of a homage and not a ripoff and there are a lot of new features included to keep it interesting. First and foremost the inclusion of different armor types that grant you different abilities. While this isn't new at all, it certainly feels right when you mix it with a Ghosts 'n Goblins game.

The graphics are colourful and well made. At times they might feel a bit random, but overall they are fitting to the style of the levels. The music and sounds are good and also add to the atmosphere of the game.

I'd like to leave a few words about the developer of the game as I think it does matter in this case. There was a problem with the game on launch day and the developer put a lot of effort into fixing it and releasing a patch just a few hours after the launch on Steam which fixed the problem. Afterwards I posted about a gameplay problem that I had and again the developer looked into it right away and offered a fix within a day. That plus the price of the game really show that there are indie developers out there that love making games and care for their customers. My sincere respect for that.

If you like retro games and/or challenging games you should really give this game a try.

Yeah, this is definitely one of those games that went un-noticed until recently. It's like Ghouls 'n Ghosts, and it's absolutely amazing! The soundtrack is really good, especially in the later levels. To fully appreciate the game, play it on Normal first, then go to Hard, then if you're brave enough, Retro.

Like Ghouls 'n Ghosts, there are favorable weapons, then there are weapons you'd consider absolute trash. In the case of Cast of the Seven Godsends, the best weapon(s) have better hitboxes as well as higher damage. When I was routing a speedrun I came across this very useful information that just might help you out: No matter what weapon you have, a god power will make it infinitely better. The Mace's arc is so bad, but with the Wind Armor's help it has a nicer angle that can help defeat those shielded enemies.

All this to say, Caste of the Seven Godsend is incredibly diverse. There are so many different potential playstyles, whether you favor certain god armors over others, or certain weapons over others. It very much rewards the player can mash attack the quickest, so keep that in mind. Either way, the game's fantastic, and it's got soooo much spirit.
