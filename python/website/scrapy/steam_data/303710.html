About This Game

Cheerfully wandering across blocky terrain, the Monomino babies haven’t a care in the world. But little do they know they’re walking into peril. You are responsible to deliver them to safety.

Monomino is a playful puzzle game developed by final year students from Nanyang Polytechnic, School of Interactive & Digital Media in Singapore. Help guide the Monominos home by building paths out of variously shaped blocks, or use power-ups that gift the Monominos with special abilities. You’ll have to think quick and put your spatial awareness skills to the test in order to protect the adorable cuboid babies, as they unwittingly walk themselves into trouble.



Key Features
  • Visually bold level design
  • Choice of blocks to rotate and position
  • Character augmenting power-ups
  • Fast forward and pause levels
  • 60 Levels to complete


Developer: The developers are final year students studying at Nanyang Polytechnic, School of Interactive & Digital Media in Singapore. Monomino was the final year project, and it was just so good it couldn’t help but get it published. They have developed industry partnerships with Sony Computer Entertainment Japan Asia, and in 2012 were officially appointed the PlayStation®First Academic Partner status, the first academic partner in Asia!

Publisher: Degica works to connect the dots by joining game devs, distributors, and publishers. They are passionate about helping small businesses and closing the gap between the Eastern and the Western markets. Degica aims to bridge gaps, make connections and open doors for innovative emerging talent.

So far this is a great puzzle/indie game. It's cute and generally it's just a timefiller.

The design is fitting the style although it's a little basic. Everyone with small talents of art could pull off something like this. The babies are easy to distinguish in the levels and the overall clarity of elements and level parts are great.

The sound is somewhat mediocre. I feel that not much effort has been put into, especially since most tracks are constantly 10-seconds looped soundbits. It becomes boring after a short time and there is no mute button.

The puzzles are really balanced and gets progressively tougher. It's definitly the strongest point of the game, although for veterans I feel like it's just a tad too easy, you'll roll right through most of the levels on the first try, but for younger audiences, this is will be a challenge!

There are a few bugs though, sometimes a baby will simply fall through a block without reason, or you'll end up having a wrong count. The worst bug so far is one that is currently still in the game (which I already mentioned in forums), where when you reach LVL 60 it will automatically complete LVL 61 as well and you can't progress as clicking it won't open the level, and neither unluck LVL 62.

For the score, I have to give this game a 7/10 because I think for filling your time, it does its job and the few glitches almost never happen, and reloading a level is one click away. But, I do hope the dev(s) fix this issue and please add a mute button!

As such, this game gets a thumb up!

Do not buy this game if you are older than maybe 5. All levels but one will bore you to death with how easy they are and no real way to fast forward. In addition to that you will experience bugs, bad controls, bad game design, and the unpleasant artwork. You have been warned.

If you want a good game that plays like Monomino, play MouseCraft here on Steam or the original Lemmings which is available as a free browser game, instead.


buggy:
- With the first two solutions for level 59 I did not get 3 stars, because babies randomly fell through blocks. I had to rearrange the blocks until the babies did not glitch to death anymore
- the level list shows 100 levels, but there are actually only 60

bad controls:
- no hotkeys for the tetrominos and special abilities
- no pause build mode
- level list keeps going back to the first group of 20 levels instead of staying where you left it and moving to the next non-3-star level automatically
- fast forwarding is disabled until you have used up everything, and a bunch of levels can easily be solved with some stuff left over. And when it finally lets you fast forward, it's still so slow you can go afk while waiting, on some levels

bad game design:
The pause button brings up a huge window that blocks pretty much the whole screen, obviously to keep you from planning out the level in peace. So there is no way to plan out the levels without failing to save the babies. When they are all dead you cannot continue to plan, instead you get whacked with the pause window, again blocking everything from view. Imagine thinking through different tetromino placements while the babies fall to their deaths literally right through the area where you are planning, in a continuous stream. Talk about a downer, seeing them all die when your goal is to save them, over and over. The only thing left to do is to trap them somewhere to give you some peace and time to plan. Basically you add your own working pause button to the game ...

boredom: about 57 of the 60 levels are so easy a 3 year old would get bored, then you have maybe 2 levels that take a few tries optimize the solution. When finally you get to level 60, which is a nice challenge, the game ends directly afterwards. So basically you spend about one and a half hours wading through boredom to be rewarded with a single entertaining level.

unpleasant artwork: This is strictly personal taste, but since I was extremely bored for most of the game, the ugly office style graphics and bad music soon got on my nerves. While I was thinking of a solution for the final and only challenging level, I did not mind them so much. Turning babies into suicide demolition bombs is not to everybody's taste either.
