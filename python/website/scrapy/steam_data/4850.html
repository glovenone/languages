About This Game

To stand alone in war is extremely unpleasant. Extremely pleasant, on the other hand, is the stand-alone version Cossacks: Back to War. Containing all of the gameplay features of Cossacks: European Wars and Cossacks: The Art of War, this is a complete care package for all current and future Cossacks fans. This add-on contains 100 captivating missions taking place between the 16th and 18th centuries. In addition, two new nations, Switzerland and Hungary, are included. These countries waged war in the late Middle Ages and essentially influenced the development of European military strategies. This addition causes the second add-on of Cossacks to pass the threshold of allowed gaming fun.


Features:

  • Thrilling real-time strategy game with historical background.
  • Fascinating 3D landscapes.
  • Play takes place in Europe between the 16th and 18th centuries.
  • Stand-alone game, other Cossacks games not required to play.
  • A total of 20 different European nations to command.
  • 100 new single missions.
  • 2 new nations: Switzerland and Hungary with a total of 8 new units.
  • Spectacular real-time battles with up to 8,000 units.
  • 4 different difficulty levels.
  • 1 new camel riding unit for Algeria and Turkey: Bedouin.
  • This version with low system requirements is adapted for comfortable play on any hardware from low-end computers, laptops and netbooks, to high-end PCs (with Windows Vista/7).
  • Ad-Hoc multiplayer play (offline mode).
  • Infinite AI skirmishes on randomly generated maps.

System Requirements

    • OS: Microsoft Windows XP Home (Service Pack 3)
    • Processor: Intel Atom N270
    • Memory: 512MB RAM
    • Graphics: Intel GMA 950
    • DirectX®: DirectX9
    • Hard Drive: 550MB space free
    • Sound: 100% Direct Sound Compatible
    • Internet Connection: 56 Kbit/sec
    • Controller: "mouse" or touchpad

Though my hours are few I plan on adding many more, all I can say is this game is right up my alley and as a fan of history (specficially Protestant Prussia) I whole heartily approve of this game with some caveats:

The common cry among the negatives reveiw is that it is not optimized for modern computers and that is true, was not the case for me but a common warning nevertheless.

It is quite different from modern RTS, wheras modern RTS have since had their shift focused more on the combat and less on the economy (or reducing it to 2-3 resources) this game I find is a balance, you need a good economy to make a good military which unlike most modern RTS is set in the 17th-18th century.

Gameplay wise, while every nation has roughly identical units (to a greater or lesser degree, some nations have completely unique and distinct units) their sprites are distinct for each nation, culture and people (hell, you can set wars between Prussia and Bavaria in what was once vestige states of the "Holy" Roman Catholic Empire, 100 points to Martin Luther for smashing that to peices).

I highly recacomend this game, doubly so if you computer has Windows XP virtual drive.

Best RTS game ever. I used to play this game a lot in my youth. The best thing about it, is you have no limit on how many troops you can create! You just need to have enough resources and dwellings, as long as you have enough, you can create as big of an army as you want.

For those of you complaining about this game not working on Windows 10
Here is how to make it work (and stop crashing):

1. Go to your steam library, right click on "Cossacks: Back to War" and select "Properties"
2. In "Properties", go to the "Local Files" tab, and select "Browse Local Files"
3. Open the "bin" folder, then scroll down & find "csbtw" right click it & select "troubleshoot compatibility"
4. After a moment you should get to a screen where you can select "Try Recommended Settings"
5. Once you have selected it, you can click on "Test the Program" to do a test run of the game, then exit the game
6. Now select "Next" and then "Yes, save these settings for this program" it will then try to detect other issues
7. After this, it should say that it is fixed, click on "Close"

There you have it! The game should now run fine without crashing on you, and you can change the game speed without problems. My only issue now is getting it to display in widescreen, other than that, it runs perfectly. I found the above steps in a youtube video, and it worked. I hope this helps.

It was one of my first computer games and it's still one of my favorites!

-Amazing battles
-Many possible strategies : mortars to attack from a huge distance, cavalry to attack the rearguard or to quickly pillage mines, officers and drums to make troop formations (up to 196 soldiers with one officer)...
-Enormous battlegrounds filled with soldiers (up to 10 000 if I remember correctly)!
-A lot of available countries with different architectures and troops
-Possibility to capture citizens, artillery and buildings with only some simple soldiers (there are priests too but they can heal a bit)
-Interesting use of ressources (to be able to fire you need coal, to keep mercenaries on your side you need gold etc.) so if your coal mines get captured for example you can no longer fire anything! (of course you can buy coal on the market but the prices can vary a lot depending on your behavior)
-Pretty good soundtracks

It's often really cheap on sales so if you enjoy RTS games, grab it!
