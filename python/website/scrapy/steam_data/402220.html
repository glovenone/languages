Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Because even if the game it's already playable, and there is a lot of things to do in the game, included multiplayer fights, the game will be kept updated fixing bugs and adding new features depending on the players feedbacks.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Approximately from 6 to 12 months, but this may vary depending on the success of the game.
If it will have a lot of success, then more features will be added, so the early access will last longer.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“As mentioned in the Greenlight page of the game, new features will be added, such as new islands where to travel, other activities and characters to know, different multiplayer modes to play, and obviously the full version will have bugs fixed as well.
Once all the good ideas from the players feedbacks plus the things I have already in mind will be completed, the game will be changed to Full Version.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“It is well playable and you have several things to do. Also, it has been tested a lot to fix all the bugs I could find.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“There is no plan to change the price for the Full Version”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“In the main menu of the game there is a help page and I have left an email address where to send feedbacks for new feature requests and bugs reports as well.
This will help me to understand which will be the next feature to add and the possible bugs to fix.”

About This Game

Lift It


Lift It is a fresh and dynamic game where you have to train your character in order to gain stats.
You can do 4 types of exercises: bench press increases your strength, squat your health, deadlift your defense and treadmill your stamina.
Increasing your stats will help you to beat your enemies when travelling from the main island (Gym Island) to the other islands.
Beat the 8 gym owners in order to get money, that could be used to buy integrators or weapons.

Integrators and Weapons


With the money you earn you can buy integrators. Each type of integrator can boost a specific stat, doubling it temporarily (3 minutes per dose).
Once you have earned enough money, you can also buy some weapons from Bill, the armorer.
The weapons you can buy are: swords, armors, shoulder armors, shields and belts, each one affecting a specific stat.
These weapons are available in different quality: the cheapest is the wooden quality, then you can find iron, bronze, silver, gold and diamond quality. Higher quality weapons give more stats multiplier.

Combat


You can also practice the sword fight with the 4 trainers in the Gym Island, and get money by beating them.
With the skilled combat system, you have to guess where your opponent will hit you (up, down, left or right) and shield that side. You can also attack in the same way and your opponent needs to guess as well where you are hitting him.
Other than the Gym Island, up to now there are 2 other islands where you can travel, Labyrinth Island and Zombie Island, but more islands will be added with the updates.

Multiplayer


Fight against other players in a 1 vs 1 sword fight!
Other than skills, your stats and weapons quality can make a huge difference!

Other Activities


Lift it allows you to do some other activities in the game, such as playing the guitar with Rosario or Larry. 12 keys are assigned to different parts of the songs, so that you can play them in the order you prefer.
You can also go to the bar and burp with Benny. There are different tonalities of burp, for a total of 2 octaves, diesis included.
Last but not least, you can have fun by dancing with Sarah, with a series of 12 famous dance moves, such as the Gangnam Style, the Moonwalk and the Robot.

Music


There are more than 60 songs you can listen throughout the Gym Island. While training at the gym, rock music will help you to feel powerful and push harder with the weights. While having fun at the bar, softer music will help you to relax or dance, depending on what you decide to do.

Updates


The game is now an alpha build, for the moment, English is the only language available, but with the updates more languages will be added soon: Italian, Spanish, Portuguese and French for sure.

The updates will consist in adding new islands to discover, new multiplayer modes, and new content in the main island too, depending on the players feedbacks.

Early Access Review
Posted: 6 July
Have you ever been a gym junkie? Well I have found the game for you. It’s our second day of running 89AM Studio’s games and today’s game is Lift It. If you’re a recovering gym junkie or want to experience that life style then you may want to check this game out… That is until you wear out your up key. You see, shout out to Xavier Woods of WWE fame, this is the game that is all about the up up but not the down down. Every task you perform at the gym is a race to see how fast you can press the up arrow key. Every single bloody task.

You see you can lift it, you can bench it, hell you can even tread mill your way to glory and fame but every single one of those will be a repetitive press of the up key. The game may have been a quality game – well at least better than what it is – if there was any thought put into the game design. Why not have the tread mill be a combination of left and right arrows signifying the moving of your feet. Weight lifting could could be up and down to signify the fact that you’re raising it and lowering it, squats could be an arrow key and a down key to signifying an action of bending, etc. It wouldn’t take much thought but the monotony of the actions would be decreased and you may want to play further than ten or twenty minutes into the game.

I had to laugh when I saw that the first non-player controlled character was ‘old master.’ He looks like what I would imagine a 20-year old Asian gang member to look like. Quite funny and no disrespect at all meant at all to the gang members. The graphics appear to be straight out of the very first year of Information Technology programming class’s programming language and it plays similarly too.

Given that it’s still in early access it’s too early to earmark this one down as a dud but a lot of work will have to go into raising its level. Given that the developers have worked on and released 3 Coins at School since putting this one out into early access it is doubtful that it will receive the attention that it desperately needs.

Trolling/jokes aside: This could actually be an interesting reverse RPG title. I commend the developers for doing something different, however the execution is too basic to really be fun. Everything looks the same, not to mention like it's the PlayStation 1 days once more, there's no variety in training other than mashing the up button, the battle controls seem archaic... there's just little fun to be had in such a basic title with honest-to-goodness anti-RPG ideas that genuinely seem fresh in a world of RPG Maker titles.

The other big issue is the cost. Nearly $10 for this title is just too much. The comedic "so bad" value of the Johnny Bravo simulator is lost once you realize the island is populated by the childeren of the Sega Dreamcast's Seaman creatures, leaving you to regret such a high priced title. $4.99 US is even pushing it, but would have been a little more justifiable to the game's shortcomings...

**EDIT: At some point, the price for this game dropped from $9.99 when not on sale, to $7.99. Just pointing this out, as I have no idea when it was done. I think it's still a bit too steep (I stand by my $4.99 suggestion), but it is a little more tantalizing a price point than the earlier $9-$10 ticket for admission.**

Early Access Review
Posted: 10 November, 2015
Man, the desire to meme here is real, but I'll try my best to keep it professional:

Lift It is not worth any meaningful amount of money, however if you can acquire it through a bundle, Steam sale, a friend playing a gift prank on you, or drunk Steam shopping, you definitely should try it out. Lift It is a hellish mess of glued-together concepts, awful 3d models, and repetitive gameplay elements, but boy did I get some good laughs out of it. I'm genuinely curious if 89AM Studios is a couple of 15-year-olds making a project for a programing class or if this game is the work of some present day gamer David Lynch; either way this game is equal parts horrible and genius.

Let's just break it down shall we?

Pros:
+Described on paper, this game has about everything your modern gamer desires in one package.
+Hour(s?) of amusement .
+There's multiplayer?.

Cons:
-Graphics of a year 2000-esque PC game with random 2010-ish gloss and water effects thrown in.
-Characters models all look deformed or addicted to steroids.
-There's a couple of pre-set resolutions to chose from...all of which look awful on a 16:10 monitor.
-No option to change the controls what-so-ever.
-W&S keys move forwards and backwards, but A&D do not strafe for some reason.
-You walk slow as hell and the sprint key makes you run at a hilariously inhuman pace.
-Even with being able to run the game world is entirely too big.
-I don't think there's a text menu in the game without a spelling or grammar error.
-The core of the game is mashing the up key. That's it.
-I'm not sure it's possible to 'beat' the game without using a macro to mash the up key as fast as technically possible. I barely beat the last 'boss' with a macro set to mash the up key at .01 millisecond intervals, so be prepared to flex those finger muscles if you plan to play this legitimately.
-The game is also entirely grinding. You mash the up key to gain stats. You then mash the up some more key to beat gym leaders and get "golds". You spend the golds to buy armor and fight people. That's the whole game.
-Golds also take entirely too long to earn; in order to unlock top tier armor you would need to play this game for hundreds of hours.
-Fighting is stiff, confusing, and boring.
-I can't even begin to describe the music choices in this game. I can't walk into a gym without laughing hysterically because of how amusing hearing a 'hard rock' version of Auld Lang Syne is.
-NOBODY'S BUTTS ARE ANIMATED. WHEN YOU RUN YOUR BUTT STAYS PERFECTLY IN PLACE WHILE THE REST OF YOU MOVES.
-Dying just punts you back to the main menu; hope you saved!
-The ♥♥♥♥ing eyelashes

In summary, 10/10, get in the alpha early and start getting those GAINZ
