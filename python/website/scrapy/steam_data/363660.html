About This Game

Touch down in the tropics and transport freight between twelve exotic islands in more than a hundred exciting missions. On each of those twelve islands new missions will be waiting for you. Up and coming freight pilots such as yourself can choose from many different jobs including both lucrative and risky missions. Each island comes with its own airstrip for players to start from and land on. They also act as a hub for getting new transport jobs. Stopovers are possible at three basic airfields, where you can also park your planes in your three personal hangars and refuel them. To complete missions as good as possible you can chose from three different planes with different characteristics you can boost using upgrades for speed, sturdiness or fuel capacity. And thanks to the game's open-ended gameplay, the fun just never stops!

Over a hundred different transport missions, some of them very lucrative, but also quite risky.
Exotic setting on an archipelago with twelve islands.
Three different planes, which can be improved using upgrades for speed, sturdiness and fuel capacity
Personal hangars and three basic airfields.
Mouse and keyboard controls

This game is hardly a simulator. I can forgive a flying game for having loose flying mechanics but this one is pretty much as arcadey as they come. In first person none of the instruments work, they are all just a static image, the game does not support joystick or controller. Keyboard and mouse controls are simple enough but the stock plane handles like a stunt plane, climbs to 29,000 feet, does not exceed 210mph even in a dive. The in game menu refers to the controls as accelerate and "breaks," mind you not even brakes. There are some pretty graphics, but the runways are all short, the islands hardly habitable. I have not seen this game dip low enough in price to even be worth it on sale.

This is a fun game, but it is plagued by several bugs and is lacking in features.

What I Like
  • Easy to play - This is a good thing and a bad thing. It is a good thing because the controls are not overwhelming like DCS World and it is very forgiving if one were to make a mistake, which promotes the mellow feel to the game.
  • Fun arcade feel - This game is by no means a Flight Simulator, X-Plane, or DCS World, and it clearly plays like a casual game. I do enjoy the realistic flight simulation games, but it is nice to play a mellow flying game.
  • Relaxing atmosphere - Do you remember Microsoft Flight? If not, it was a based in Hawaii and had a relaxing atmosphere. This game has a similar feel, in that it is more tropical and worry free.
  • Walk around - You can actually get out of your plane and walk around the airfields in this game, which is a nice addition. It is not much, but it certainly alleviates the cramped feeling as if you can never leave the plane.
  • A lot of missions - The game has many missions, but they all have the same premise: go to this island, pick up a product, and deliver it to this island. This game spices it up with the ability to transport legal and illegal products; for the latter, you could risk getting caught by the police or getting cheated by the seller and not getting paid.
  • The fart sound - I seriously think that the player's character walks around naked (or maybe flies naked) because when you get in and out of the plane, there is a sound of a flight suit being zipped which sounds strangely like a fart.

What I Do Not Like
  • Easy to play - This is a good thing and a bad thing. It is a bad thing because the game feels too easy when flying. For example, you can hit the runway pretty hard and not suffer the consequences.
  • Bugs - In the version that I am reviewing this game for, which is likely 1.0, there are many bugs. The bugs are not very game breaking (or at least I have not come across any), but they are noticeable and could be annoying to some players.
  • No joystick ability - When I first bought this game, I immediately took out my flight joystick (Logitech Extreme 3D Pro), but I was severly disappointed to find that there is only keyboard and mouse controls. You cannot use game controllers or flight joysticks in this version. I hope they add it later.
  • Lack of controls Similar to the first point, this game is too easy. There are very little controls, so do not expect to have flap adjustments, air drag compensation, or anything like that. There is a landing gear button, however.

Overall, the game is certainly fun, but it has several bugs and lacks features. (No joystick support!) You can probably squeeze a good ten hours out of this game before shelving it, so it is somewhat justifiable at $20. I do admire the team's ambition in creating this game, and I wish them the best of luck in this game and their future titles. I love the first mention in the "special thanks" ;)

Rating: 6.5/10
