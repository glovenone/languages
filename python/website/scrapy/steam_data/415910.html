Gigachess v1.01 Update

Hints in Puzzle Attack mode, a quick start feature in Endless and some welcome polish advance Gigachess ahead a version! :)



GIGACHESS V1.01 CHANGELOG
  • A hint for each of the 48 Puzzle Attack stages can now be accessed after 3 defeats, projecting an overlay onto the board that shows the first move towards one possible par solution
  • 'Last Formation' button added to the Endless mode party builder that calls up the last starting lineup of hero pieces played (starting now!)
  • A screen shake added to Endless mode when lives are lost
  • A special sprite effect now plays when a hero piece is resurrected
  • Screen scrolling when selecting a game mode from the main menu is a touch smoother
  • French localization for achievements added
Later via quiet patch:
  • An undo bug that would permanently disable the ability to resurrect hero pieces fixed
  • Undo may now be used on the first turn of an Endless game
  • The undo button is now disabled until a move has been made during a turn
  • A bug where campaign tooltips (hits / deaths) for hero pieces could disappear when accessing the dropdown menu fixed
  • Tooltips added for the music + hint buttons, and difficulty (skull) indicator
  • Tooltips on the Queen-up and Trapmine HUD icons not displaying properly during Puzzle Attack and Tutorial modes fixed
In testing right now is an addition to Endless mode where a special attack gets triggered every X waves (could be 10, 15, 20 waves, still fooling around with it!). It should make Endless mode a little more exciting with some thrilling close calls :)

If you catch any bugs or weirdness in the game, be sure to report it on the community forums! Thanks for playing Gigachess <3 http://steamcommunity.com/app/415910/discussions/0/494632338494579407/

Gigachess v1.00 Update

Improvements to Puzzle Attack progression and overall game presentation step Gigachess forward a version ! :)



GIGACHESS V1.00 CHANGELOG
  • Conditions for unlocking Puzzle Attack blocks have been relaxed, now taking only 5 stage clears in the current block to give access to the next 8 stages
  • A satisfying & subtle sound effect has been added when initiating click-and-drag movement of a hero piece
  • Frame rate has been tweaked and the game now runs at a solid 60 FPS
  • Puzzle Attack Stage 13's available power-up has been changed to a Queen-up
  • A graphical bug with the 'Defeat!' shield's semi-transparency fade-in fixed
  • A layering bug with the volume slider and the language button at Tutorial mode start-up fixed
  • A bug where campaign tooltips (hits / deaths) for hero pieces weren't displaying properly during Puzzle Attack mode fixed
A couple cool features to look forward to in the next update:
1) a 'hint' added to each Puzzle Attack stage that will offer a clue to the par solution
2) a 'Last Party' button added to the Endless mode party builder screen that will instantly load up your most recently played starting line-up

About This Game


Gigachess is a puzzle board hero defense. With your custom party of Knights, Rooks and Bishops, defend against the relentless waves of enemy Pawns running a blitz for the board's bottom. The seemingly unbreakable formations and constant downward march of your foe transform chess from slow burn to tactical rush in this deeply addictive single-player take on the grandmaster of turn-based strategy.

How long can you survive the Pawn onslaught?

GIGACHESS v1.01 FEATURES

  • Endless mode with randomly-generated waves and a choice of 4 difficulty levels for a perpetual Pawn menace (now with persistent quicksaves!)
  • Puzzle Attack mode offering 40+ high-stakes, logic-testing stages to clear (now with hints!)
  • Playable Tutorials & Tooltips for a crash course in the mechanical intricacies and insight on demand
  • Strategic Power-ups that block advancing Pawns, plant explosive traps & more!
  • Competitive Leaderboards to rank your best played games against the world
  • Achievements & Trading Cards for the complete Steam experience

Chess for one has never been more compelling. Get your head around Gigachess today!

    • OS: Windows XP SP2
    • Processor: Intel Atom 1.6 GHz (or equivalent)
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • DirectX: Version 8.0
    • Storage: 75 MB available space
    • Additional Notes: 100% mouse controllable

Introduction
Gigachess was developed and published by Gigatross Games. The game itself is interesting because it takes the rules of chess and makes it into a game of it's own, which it being both challenging and fun to play.

Gameplay
This game is rather clever, with the way that it takes the game of chess and twists it in a way that makes it both fun yet challenging to play. The basics of the game are that your chess pieces can be either a Bishop, Rook or a knight. Your enemies are a number of pawns which you must take out before they reach the end of the chess board and take a life from you. That's basically the whole game.

Although it doesn't seem that much, this game only has to two different types of gameplay. This doesn't mean to say that the game is limited to it's gameplay value, that would be an understatement to say the least. These two modes that are in the game are Endless and Puzzle mode, puzzle mode creates a scenario and you have to try to take out the pawns before they get to the end. Endless mode, you are able to choose 4 pieces and try to defeat as many pawns as you can before you run out of lives.

In endless mode you can choose 4 pieces and your objective is to take out as many pawns as you can before they reach the end and take all of your lives away. The game makes you think about where you would move and how that would affect the gameplay altogether, much like a game of chess. You will be presented wave after wave with a number of pawns that you must take out in order to succeed. The pawns are laid out differently each time, usually only on the first two rows in order to try to take some of your pieces.

With endless mode, you are given the choice of four different types of difficulties depending on how advanced you are with the game, Novice, Medium, Expert and Master. Novice runs at a slow rate, you are able to take out the pawns easily and have a move or two to get your pieces set up. Medium is a bit more of a faster pace, making you have to think more and spawning the next wave more quickly. Expert seems to be the same pace as Medium, but they add a new type of pawn that flashes and jumps two spaces forward each turn, as for Master, every turn a new wave spawns. A bit of overkill on the difficulty if you ask me.

The game also includes power ups that you can use by collecting enough SP by killing the amount of pawns with the amount of pieces you have in a single turn. These power ups give you the ability to block the pawns for a few turns, makes one of your chess pieces move in the sense that it was a queen for a turn and a mine that you get to place down and blow up a pawn. So overall there is a nice variety of powerups that you can use to help you play through endless mode.

Puzzle mode is what you would expect it to be. You are given a set of pieces and have to try to take out the pawns before they get to the other side. This time you are only given a single life, so each move is crucial in order to succeed. There are around 48 puzzles for you to complete. But they only unlock 8 at a time after you have completed the previous 8 puzzles. For me this can sometimes limit the way that I play the puzzle mode, as I get discouraged by playing a single level over and over and fail too many times.

There is always a best way of being able to complete the puzzles in a certain number of moves. So you might get a puzzle and it reads par 6, meaning that this puzzle can be done in 6 moves at the best. But you can do the puzzle in more moves than that, you just won't obtain a star on that level to say that you have completed it in the shortest amount of moves.

The music to this game is really fitting to the sort of game that it is. You may find that the song gets repetitive, in which case there are 2 others that you can choose from or you can just turn it off completely if you so wish to. For me these 3 music tracks were decent enough for me to keep switching between them to keep me in the mood for this game.

The graphics of the game seem to fit the chess sort of feel to the game, being sort of older looking to the game. This makes the game look more appealing to me because of the consideration of the chess board and pieces looking less like you would expect modern ones to look like.

Pros
+ Challenging yet fun gameplay
+ 2 different modes
+ 4 different difficulties on Endless Mode
+ Nice power ups
+ Lots of puzzle levels
+ Nice music
+ Steam Achievements & Trading Cards

Cons
- Master difficulty on Endless is overkill
- Sometimes puzzle levels can be a bit of a drag

Result / Final Thoughts
Gigachess is a fun yet challenging game, it balances these two elements well for the player to enjoy the game and not too challenging either. The two game modes seem to limit the game, but really there is a lot of replay value for the game, the music and graphics are both great and really suit the game.

Rating: 7.9/10
