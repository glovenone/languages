7 July

Update (v1.0.3) - New Scene, Troll Mode

Waltz of the Wizard update 1.0.3 introduces a number of changes, including a new and hidden teleportation scene. The race is on to discover the scene — and its secrets!



Version 1.0.3 also introduces an experimental feature that allows a second player to assume control over an in-game character using a mouse and keyboard. The second player can fly through the environment, interact and shoot projectiles, and can be killed or captured by the VR player.



To the person playing in VR the second player looks like a flying skull. The feature can be used in a number of ways, enabling activities like playing hide and seek, engaging in shootouts or using it to show VR players interesting things to do. The gameplay mode can be activated by the VR player in the main menu, or alternatively activated by F1 on the keyboard without the VR player’s knowledge. This latter option is the reason for the humorous title “Troll Mode”, since it can be a very fun way to conclude a gameplay session when you’re showcasing VR to someone for the first time.

Keep in mind that Troll Mode is experimental and may cause performance issues. (The experience is automatically set to low quality when Troll Mode is enabled).

Changelog

  • New teleportation scene! The race is on to discover it.
  • Troll Mode: Press F1 to enable two player mode and assume control of the skull. Use WASD and mouselook to fly around, left/right mouse-clicks shoot projectiles & number keys 1 and 2 to taunt. NOTE: This feature is highly experimental and may cause performance issues!
  • Various bug fixes and improvements.

Update (v1.0.2)

This update brings a number of spell improvements and bugfixes.

Changelog

  • Puppeteer spell: By popular demand you can now puppeteer the sword, crossbow firing and liquid pouring with ingredient flasks!
  • Reanimator spell: Redesigned spell effect and behavior giving a more powerful magical result.
  • Bridge scene duration shortened and portal moved to a more obvious location.
  • Modified initial door scene layout and added note that gameplay videos are allowed.
  • Added a "Game paused" message to main monitor when the SteamVR menu is active.
  • Minor changes to quality setting to ensure that the monster shadow can be seen in the Hallway teleportation scene.
  • Fixed a bug that caused lanterns to respawn inside the table.
  • Fixed a bug that caused the trumpet to stick to your hand.
  • Rearranged Main Menu to prevent accidental changing of quality settings.
  • Fixed game window freezing when attempting to quit the game.
  • Various minor fixes and improvements.

About This Game

Waltz of the Wizard is a virtual reality experience that lets you feel what it’s like to have magical powers. Combine arcane ingredients into a boiling cauldron with the help of an ancient spirit trapped in a human skull. Unleash creative or destructive wizardry upon a fully interactive virtual world. Travel to new places, finding yourself in mysterious circumstances in an experience inspired by the atmosphere of films such as Fantasia and Harry Potter.

ABOUT ALDIN
Aldin has been working exclusively on virtual reality software since early 2013, specializing in applying behavioral analysis and artificial intelligence to empower experiences. We combine creative vision with cutting edge technologies to build worlds that are aware of our presence, environments that respond convincingly to movements and characters that understand our intentions. Aldin is currently working on Ghostline analytics and visualization tool for VR, used extensively in development of Waltz of the Wizard.

Aldin has published a number of consumer products for the PC and Mobile VR market (Trial of the Rift Drifter, Asunder, Twisted Realms and Waltz of the Wizard).

http://www.aldindynamics.com

Aldin’s Ghostline VR tool for local testing was used in the development Waltz of the Wizard to ensure an engaging and polished user experience. Ghostline is an easy-to-use plugin that generates interactive recordings of users within VR worlds, providing teams with the ability to visualize the user experience and gather metrics on engagement. Browsing Ghostline sessions is made easy using conventional screens, or by stepping into sessions in virtual reality to evaluate content alongside users.

http://ghostline.xyz
