About This Game

Make friends, then blow them to bits!

Trench Run is a mayhem-filled, laugh a minute casual multiplayer action game brought to you by the makers of King Arthur’s Gold and Soldat.

Blow your enemies to bits in muddy trenches, then sip wine with them at the bar in the first multiplayer shooter with no death.

Listen to a relaxing feel-good gypsy jazz soundtrack as you watch your screaming enemies ride rockets and explode crashing into walls. Howl with laughter. Repeat.

Turn into a shrub. Have a dance off. Buy a pet crocodile.

See you at the bar!

Features:
  • Unique team-based "Run" mode for 2-10 players
  • Free-for-all Skirmish mode for 1-4 players (perfect for a fun evening with friends)
  • 5 classes with special skills to choose from (Assault, Sniper, Demolitions, Commando and Medic)
  • Semi-random destructible maps
  • Down on the ground? Grab a medikit and continue fighting!
  • Play basketball and meet new friends between rounds
  • Spend coins on drinks and cigarettes from the VIP bar
  • Buy customized costumes and pet animals
  • Online and Local couch-based multiplayer available
  • Designed to be easy to pick up and play, but deep enough to play seriously

Review

This is a casual Multiplayer game, which means that you won't find a Story mode here.
There are two game modes for Local and Online: Skirmish and Run.

Skirmish:
  1. For 4 players (humans or bots)
  2. Free For All - Every man for himself!
  3. You must have 8 kills to win
  4. The winner gets coins which can be spent on the Lobby
  5. You can choose between 4 classes: Sniper, Commando, Assault and Demolitions

Run:
  1. For 10 playes (humans or bots)
  2. Two teams with 5 playes each
  3. Each team starts in one side of the map. The goal is to cross the map and reach the other side
  4. Best of 5 rounds

In both game modes you'll find:
  1. If you get shot you fall on the ground. If you manage to get a medkit (drops from the sky on a parachute) before the end of round you'll be up on foot and back on the game
  2. You start with limited ammo, but just like the medkits it also drops from the sky
  3. Each round lasts 1 minute

The online mode unfortunately is a desert place even though it has an entertaining Lobby.
You can spend here the coins you win:
  1. To buy skins
  2. To buy pets
  3. To buy toys for your pets
  4. To drink coffee, beer or wine
  5. You can ask the band to play a song
  6. You can play 2D basketball
  7. You can see the Leaderboards to find out who has the most wins, loses, who drank more and who score the most on basketball

Rating:

7/10

Pros:
  1. Funny Lobby
  2. Challenging AI. I haven't won a game against the bots yet on Skirmish mode, only on Run mode
  3. Windows, Mac and Linux version

Cons:
  1. No achievements (if it bothers you)
  2. No trading cards
  3. Empty servers

Fast, accessible and completely hilarious mayhem. You have two game modes to choose from and one of the best online multiplayer lobbies I've ever seen in my life. There is the Skirmish mode, which is a free for all single life per round game mode. First to get eight kills wins the match. Second there is the Run mode, two teams try to reach the opposite side of the map. First team to cross, or eliminate the other team, wins. Match is the best of five rounds. There are simple yet distinct classes to choose from, based on classic shooter archetypes.

And the lobby is seriously entertaining too. Hang out and chat at the barrista, play a pick up game of 2D basketball, or pick up a fancy outfit or a pet. Of course you can always spend your hard earned winnings the old fashioned way and get hammered at the bar.

The game also boasts crisp and responsive controls and totally over the top physics. Round that out with online and local multiplayer with full bot support so you never have to go without a game. Totally worth the price of entry and then some.

If you were a fan of King Arthur’s Gold then I have some delightful news to share with you – if you weren’t already aware. Transhuman Design are back with their second Steam game. This time the indie geniuses have put their mind to a multiplayer intended action game that features a sweet singleplayer mode for those who can’t get into a multiplayer game. To date Steamified have featured several multiplayer (including local multiplayer only) games that have been pretty much presumed dead on arrival for a lack of a competent singleplayer mode. I’d like those developers to cast a watchful eye over this game and study it. You can create a game that is fully intended to be played as a multiplayer game – which I believe Trench Run is – and have a successful and well implemented singleplayer mode. It may not save your game entirely but it will give those who gave you their hard earned cash the ability to play your game and not immediately utilize Steam’s refund system.

The fact of the matter is that I have yet to get into a local or online multiplayer game of Trench Run. This could have annoyed the hell out of me. I could have screamed blue murder at the developer but they implemented one hell of a singleplayer mode that makes me think that the game is a worthy purchase even if you never end up playing the fully realized multiplayer game. Yes, I’d love to get a game but I haven’t had any luck yet. That’s okay because I play on the Australian servers and usually at weird times. I’m certain that I will get into a game at some point. I can try to play on other servers but as is generally the case with region specific servers I fear that the lag would make the game unplayable for me and that I would be at such a disadvantage as to not make the game a particularly enjoyable experience. This is not on the hands of the developers but a simple reality given the quality of Australia’s Internet and the distance data must travel.

The lobby is one of the best for any online game I have ever seen. I love the fact that there is quite a bit to do in this mode while you wait for other players to come on board unless you want to get straight to the action and play the single player mode which is, I believe, the same as games with real players but where you play against bots so that you can get into games quicker. The bots aren’t just a pathetic challenge either as their AI has been programmed very well. Other players, who I hope to play with at some point, have argued that their AI is much better than a lot of the players that they’ve had actual matches with so I have to give kudos to the devs on a job well done.

The pixel graphics are fantastic but that was one of the trademarks of King Arthur’s Gold so it is a continuation of what we have come to expect from the devs. I love the choice of music as it really meshes with the gameplay that I have experienced thus far and it makes for a fantastic touch to the lobby.

Just as I was about to quit my session another player came on and we were able to have a game with bots. Thanks to ‘Iron Raccoon’. The game was fun and very similar to the normal bot games but Iron Racoon ended up winning the game and doing very well.

Unfortunately the game currently lacks achievements and trading cards so I hope that they are added in the future.
