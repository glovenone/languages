Wanda is one of those games where you aren't really sure if you're going to like it or not. Until the story unfolds and you get sucked into it. Wanda is that game. You see the robots speak to each other and you try to see what they're trying to say. The world seems pretty persistent on it's own and as a player my curiousity grew. Which very much got me sucked into this game.

The puzzles are very logical thinking. It draws a little from Portal as in trying to flip switches and portals but it's unique on it's own where it's not a direct ripoff. However, this game is a bit short I think. But honestly, disregarding the puzzles the story and atmosphere is where it's main focus is. Don't expect to enjoy a full-fledged puzzle game. That's only like a snack or morsel, compared to the story presented in this magnificent game.

Now, can't forget one thing about this game that just gave it such a big wow factor. The music. I never experienced something so phenomenal and such a surprise hit to me. The music just blended so well with the in-game cutscenes and the way the story presented itself. At the end of the day, the game was just so damned good. Atmosphere, music and story but damn, it's too short. It's like when you finish it it's like saying bye to a good friend. I think it's worth the purchase just for that.

So if you're into puzzle games or even people who enjoyed Undertale and even Earthbound where it has it's own unique world I highly recommend it. Please, if you start playing. Just be ready to embrace the FEELS. Damn.

Pros:

+Logic based puzzles that give you that "AHA!" moment
+An interesting game world packed with mystery and intrigue
+God-tier soundtrack done by David Lister

Cons:

-Short. The game was real good that I wish there were more to do.
