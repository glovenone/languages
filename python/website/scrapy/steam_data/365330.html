About This Game

Jump/Boxer is a pseudo 8-bit action platformer with fighting game elements. As a young martial artist named Dag, players will make their way through each level using parkour-like abilities to overcome the various platforming challenges as well as engaging in combat with mechanics similar to those found in fighting games.

Features:

  • An 8-bit experience with parkour and fighting game elements
  • Overcome various platforming challenges
  • Fight martial artists of various fighting styles
  • Supports Xbox 360 Controller

    • Processor: 1.6
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: 512 MB

Without a doubt. The most broken, frustrating, unsatisfying experience I have had in a LONG time.

Horribly clunky unresponsive, glitchy movement. Luck based pointless spamfest "fighting". Absolutely no story to speak of whatsoever.

All you do is go through an ambiguously stereotypically asian city filled with spikes, rolling barrels that spawn literally out of thin air, bullets that literally spawn out of thin air, and ambiguously stereotypically asian enemies. You beat them all up in exactly the same fashion, none of them act differently other than slightly different barebones animations, even the "bosses" are exactly the same as the mooks, except with more health and a unique sprite.
Once you beat the last "boss" the game cuts to a black screen with "...a good workout" or something like that, and then your booted to the title screen.

Also did I mention that there is NO way of saving progress in any way. You quit? You start over. Your PC died suddenly? You start over.

Many MANY times I have been killed because the game just did not read my inputs, or straight up did the wrong thing, resulting in my death, and sometimes the game just sends you flying upwards forever when you jump up a ledge (thankfully there is a reset checkpoint button), and the collision detection is extremely random, something that would normally kill me doesn't, and vice versa.

The last level (Level 5) is absolutely disgusting and highlights all of the game's problems I mentioned previously. As well as stupidly long rooms.

The soundtrack is decent at best. But writing this review after beating it, I don't remember any of it already.

Overall I can't recommend this to anyone wanting to actually have fun. AVOID.

From start to finish this was a entertaining game through and through. There are 5 levels and each one requires some pretty good platforming skills to get through them. The fighting in this game isn't really anything to praise. It has its punches and high and low kicks with blocking which the AI can be tricky to get past. You could try to be technical to beat enemies, but there really isn't anything stopping you from trying your luck with button mashing the attack button.

There aren't that many differences in the common enemy, but the bosses in each stage has some unique looks to them. While the fighting isn't really anything special, the real reason to play this game is for the platforming. The platforming parts require good precision with your button presses and good reaction times. Its got a good difficulty set so you you'll get kick out of this game. The game might be short, but for a buck(79 cents for me on sale) you get a great challenge that gives you an old school like feel.
