About This Game

Gaming experiment in genre of music runner - not a game, aesthetic concept.


Put the pedal to the metal or your girl will die!


Her heart works with help of the car engine and it’s you who decide how much time she has.
And it depends on you how many neon landscapes she will see - the road to the sky has no end in the last trip.

The endless world in Retrowawe setting where you'll burn tyres of the car to the strongest tracks of Retowawe/Synthwawe composers.
Neon landscapes with mountain chains and beaches will be your companions in the fated race.


If the car engine stops working, she will die and her blood will be on your hands!


Experience the atmosphere of the world inspired by 80s with their neon lights, VHS tapes and synth pop. Buckle up!
See you on the fluorescent road in your last ride towards the sunset!

PS: Try to hold out for 8 minutes...

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windiws XP
    • Processor: i3
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: GTX560
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: It is likelihood that the game could be launched on Spectrum ZX processor.
    Recommended:
    • OS: Win 10
    • Processor: i5
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: GTX560
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 1 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Wear sunglasses, a jacket bomber and preferably a toothpick for full immersion.

The developer goes out of their way to explain that it's "not a game, but an aesthetic concept". Ok, I get that. To start with I could not work out how to move the car at enough speed to last more than 10 seconds but once I realised I needed to rebind the Boost key from SHIFT to another key I found this game entertaining.

The car handling is not smooth as it likes to fling itself about a bit but once I got the hang of using the booster to keep it under control I found myself enjoying its unique handling experience.

The track varies a little as the game goes on, with the occasional car trying to smack into you head on, a helicopter shooting at you and road blocks.

The music was enjoyable and kept the pace flowing.

Like some other reviews I did find after about 30min I needed a rest. Well actually I lost it on a sharp incline bend and decided to stop.

This is a game I think I will play every so often to relax.

If its on sale at a good price then I would recommend you try it.

Welp, another useless thing sitting in my steam library gathering pixel dust.


Storyline: Your girl gets injured and you link her heartbeat (I assume), to your car and drive far away from the people who hurt her. You must keep a constant speed that isn't too fast and also not to slow because she will die.

[g]Gameplay:[/b] So, for the first couple of minutes it's pretty and feels decent, despite rather sluggish and delayed for a driving game. I can't help but link this to a poorly made time wasting browser game for the lack of depth.

Graphics: Really nicely rendered retro feel. Environments are pretty, but get repeated too much to continue being interesting after a short time.

Soundtrack: Outdrive seems to have a running theme of being good for a few minutes and then get boring and quite frankly irritating due to lack of depth, change and replay value. In this case, replay value is bad because it's the same track looped.

Menu: Play and exit. That's it. Basic, bland, lacking any kind of character and aids in making it feel like a time wasting web browser game.

Final Overview: Retro does NOT equal good, quit trying to ride on things that look retro and cool to suck people in and then put no effort into making the final product interesting. This has absolutely no replay ability in the slightest. I could see myself really forcing myself to make it interesting by challenging a friend and trying to beat their time, but it would still sap my drive to play it because there is nothing to it.

Good to waste a little bit of time, and then let it sit in your steam library along with the rest of relics you said you'd play some day but sit under a pile of dust. (Admit it, we all have a few in that dusty pile).

I always get sucked in. It was pretty, but alas, that's the only thing it has going for it.

I don't see why this game is getting such terrible reviews.

For the cheap price that it is listed as, I believe you get everything you could want out of it.
Sure, the gameplay is a little linear, but not to the point where your head starts to ache and you wish for anything but the eternal purgatory that is linear gameplay. The enemies get increasingly then decreasingly difficult, which is nice because in the eternal drive through the sunset- it feels good to pick up the pace and slow it down at different intervals.

The soundtrack and visuals alone are worth the money, it's gorgeous to look at and just... well, just *drive*

I don't know, I guess I'm one of those people, I go out on random drives with my SO and we just relax along the road as the sun sets, this feels like that.

Not to mention the little back story they add in the beginning, it isn't a whole lot but it nearly brought my eyes to a watering point. The driver's dedication to his girlfriend is heart warming and sad, and it sets the whole premise for your eternal drive. I love this game, and I don't care if I'll ever only play 2 hours of it or less, it's worth the small fee.
