About This Game

Fable® is a groundbreaking roleplaying-adventure game from Peter Molyneux, in which your every action determines your skills, appearance, and reputation. Create your life story from childhood to death. Grow from an inexperienced adolescent into the most powerful being in the world. Choose the path of righteousness or dedicate your life to evil. Muscles expand with each feat of strength. Force of will increases with each work of wit. Obesity follows gluttony, and skin tans with exposure to sunlight and bleaches bone-white by moonlight. Earn scars in battle and lines of experience with age. Each person you aid, each flower you crush, and each creature you slay will change this world forever. Fable: Who will you be?

Key Features:

  • Forge a hero based on your actions: Age and evolve a hero or villain through the actions you choose and the path you follow--be it for good, evil, or in-between. Ply the way of the sword, and see your muscles bulge. Weave the dark arts, and witness power crackle at your fingertips. Skulk in the shadows, and watch your skin bleach.
  • Engage in intense real-time combat: Collect battle scars as you duel with a world of cunning foes and deadly creatures. Master an array of deadly weaponry as you hone the art of blade-craft. Hunt your quarry using subterfuge and stealth. Weave death from the elements, as you harness the dark arts of the arcane.
  • Build your living legend: Through deeds and actions, build a name for yourself across the land. Recruit allies and followers. Gain glory or notoriety. Make friends and enemies. Interact with a living world of people, places, and event all reactive to you. Hero or butcher? Who will you be?
  • Explore and shape a living, evolving world: Champion or manipulate an ever-changing land with competitive and cooperative heroes, dynamic weather systems, and deformable environments. Interact with teeming cultures, creatures, and citizens from various towns and cities.
  • Hone your character with scores of unique skills and extras: Master new abilities and add possessions as you develop.
  • Never play the same game twice: Once you finish your adventure, go back and try the experience again, forging your character and thereby a new tale with unexpected twists and turns, new skills, powers, influences, allies and enemies.
  • Expanded content and tones of new choices: Discover additional regions, storylines, and side quests – as well as new optional missions. More spells to master, new armor and weapons, and even new monsters to use them on!

System Requirements

    • OS: Windows XP or later
    • Processor: 1.4 GHz equivalent or greater
    • Memory: 256MB
    • Hard Disk Space: 3GB of available hard disk space
    • Video Card: 64 MB shader-capable video card
    • Sound: Sound card and either a set of speakers or a set of headphones are required for audio

Fable: The Lost Chapters is a 2005 re-release of the 2004 killer app Fable, brought to you by Lionhead studios - a studio famous for this, having Molyneux as a creative director, and having Molyneux as a creative director who lies.

Luckily the 2005 release places it outside of recent memory, so we can ignore all promises of real-time tree growth, multiplayer and multiple guilds, and get on with the game proper.

This is not a blind review. First played this game near release, and remembered it being incredibly mediocre. How's that for a first impression? As it turns out, it was just the memory that was mediocre.

"Your health is low. Do you have any potions, or food?"
- Guildmaster

By combining all three virtues/playstyles - Strength, Skill, Will - you will end up with a character that forms many unfinished wholes into an Ultron of tolerability. Going for a specific "build" is not fun, limiting yourself is not fun, the only way to really squeeze some playability out of it is to throw it into a smoothie. Abandon all delusions of sticking to a single path, you're a spellswordbowman.

With that out of the way, the game has more to it than you'd initially expect. Will gives you a dozen tools for every situation, Strength keeps you in the action with little touches like rolling having i-frames and the guard-breaking flourishes that I swear trigger at random, and Skill supplements the other two with speed and money.

At times, especially during endgame when you've completely broken the game to become a regular munchkin, I was having legitimate fun with the gameplay alone. Something not expected from the half-remembered aeons of strafing/kiting at a distance, or continually bouncing off an enemy's impregnable defense.

But it's sloppier than a pig's feeding trough.

Movement isn't remotely 1-1, there's no combos beyond mash attack, no way to play gracefully or precise at all. Enemy attacks either telegraph from a mile away, have just enough startup to seemingly knock you out of wailing on them, or in some amazing circumstances are completely messy distance attacks with no way to predict them at all, just react.

Accept that you will be spamming your go-to preferred glorified macro lategame and you'll be happier.

"Try to get your combat multiplier even higher."
- Guildmaster

What's there is there, but Fable has largely sold itself on character morphing.

Which is okay. A bit ridiculous, you'll end up twice the size of the average character and shrivelled to a prune while the tykes at the local school still take their first lessons. There's not nearly as much character customization as you'd think - maybe 25 outfits, 10 haircuts and a handful of supplementary aspects, you'll rest easy in the knowledge that your super special Hero is going to turn out about the same as everyone else's.

The only real morphing here is of your expectations, and with no way to roll back level upgrades you're probably going to end up with a stature you're unhappy with regardless.

It's fundementally a silly game, which is fine except that means that any attempt to appear serious falls flat on its face. All the lore ends in abrupt handwaves with no payoff (Did you know that Jack of Blades is a primordial horror-demon, despite just being a smug trickster ingame you trip over and OHKO?), all the character art angles more towards the era's World of Warcraft domineered sensibilities instead of more traditional aesthetics - fine if you like that - and all the music is consistently great there's no dissing these tunes.

"There's a Quest Card waiting for you at the Guildhall."
- Guildmaster

Ultimately what Fable is, is a surprisingly mechanically fun but otherwise shallow and cliched RPG. Much like Molyneux's hopes and dreams, it aimed for the moon and landed amongst the dirt.

A final sidenote, it'll run on just about anything but you're going to have to do some moderate legwork. Deleting codecs, adjusting administrator privileges, the like.
