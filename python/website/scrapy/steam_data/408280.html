About This Game



Message Quest is a tiny stained-glass point-n-click adventure about laziness and her herald.
This is a fairy tale in the best tradition of works such as “The Hobbit” or “The Chronicles of Narnia”, inspired by the art of Studio Ghibli.

This is the story of a lazy herald called Feste. You may know heralds, the fellows who appear at the beginning of each journey, looking for the hero in an attempt to deliver that “call to adventure” thing, right before the interesting stuff starts to happen. That’s exactly the job our Feste must do. Find a hero, give him or her a call to adventure, and be done with it. No exploits, no saving the world. But all he wants to do is sleep and eat candy. You must become his conscience and lead him through the struggles and strange encounters of the Interworld Road.

A cute fantastic journey for one evening - the game lasts about an hour and a half, so you’ll get the full story in one session.

Features


  • Unusual stained glass graphics that make everything even more fairy taleish.
  • Charming and unpredictable characters who will tell you their life stories.
  • Puzzles with traditional mechanics allow you to fully enjoy the story without losing game immersion.
  • Funny boss fights in true JRPG style with zany attacks and bonuses.
  • A new dialogue system that allows you to control both characters in a conversation.
  • Jokes and easter eggs that will surprise you when you least expect it.
  • A magical soundtrack written especially for Message Quest that will immerse you in the bizarre fantasy universe of Interworld Road.

I recommend this only for the nice art style and the touching characters. And some humor. Further than that there's not much in it. Suitable as a game for your kids, but as I am no kid, I looked at it with adult eyes.

The story can be described as touching, but misses depth. Depth is emulated by having strange languages and mentioning a world and lore, but never do you get to know more about them. (The game hints at a sequel and the Devs too, so maybe there's more to come.) The story also starts off by telling you that you need to save the world, but in the end you are left with the feeling you didn't. You only got to know your character a bit better and save two families.

The gameplay is said to be point and click, and that's meant as literally as you can imagine. You point and click at dialogues and you point and click at some objects to make the story go on. There are a few easy puzzles to keep you entertained, but they feel childish. I think there were two 'fights' which were fun, but quite easy. Also, every step you take, you are taken by the hand. This is often done with some humor, so it feels intended and not too bad. But again, no challenges.

Furthermore, it's short, I completed it in just under two hours and I did not rush it. Overall, I just expected a bit more, from the whole game and from every element. It catches your attention and then doesn't really do very much with it. Can't help feeling a bit underwhelmed.

Pros
+ art style looks great
+ touching characters
+ cozy atmosphere
+ humor
+ relaxing gameplay
+ fun for kids
+ sequel?

Cons
- world misses depth
- plot misses depth
- minimal gameplay
- step by step instructions
- feels childish at times

Bought during sale at €1,79.

Wow. Quality artwork. Great story. It's short but what a journey.

This is a point-n-click style game. A few puzzles, not very many and not very difficult. And a few "battles" as well. Awesome graphics. There are voices but it is a nonesense language just for background effect, but it does portray the emotional state of the characters well. (Reminds me of an exercise in drama school where we had to do a scene and the only words we could say were "Mary had a little lamb". IT's amazing how much you can portray by voice inflection and stuff like that)

You help the main character become a reluctant hero. He just wants to sleep instead of doing his job. You have to literally drag him with the mouse to the edge of the screen in order for him to move on. He complains all the way through the game. He has to deliver a message scroll to a hero in order to save the land from impending destruction. Along the way he meets other characters that become involved in the story. I won't spoil anything for you, but there is a neat thing they do in the game after you reach the "end" of the story they do a flashback and show what happened earlier but from another character's point of view. It gives a different twist to the story. Very nice storytelling.

One of the main puzzles is to create a path across a chasm by choosing small pieces that will fit together. Then there are the battles. You have to battle by choosing from a few options, like damage opponent or heal or defend, but they are not written that way. They have phrases that are specific to the characters. There are some battles where you have to do more strategy that just attack and defend. There's also an option to increase attack or increase health. So spend some time alternating between those and healing and then once your attack is high enough you can go for the kill. Though you don't actually "kill" anyone. It's more like you defeat them, they give in to your demand.

There's lots of humor in this story. Sometimes it even breaks the fourth wall and talks to you, the player. I love that kind of stuff. Oh, and the game is kid-friendly. I dont' think there's anything that will offend anyone. It's really a game for all ages. It's not neccesarily aimed at kids but it's not aimed at adults only.

Graphics are cute, charming, kind of like stained glass or woodcuts or mosaics artwork.

Total playtime: about an hour or so. There are a few hidden things if you want all the achievements, so you might have to replay some sections.

tl;dr version:

great story
great art
point-n-click gameplay
some puzzles (easy to medium)
voices but not in a real language (like the schoolteacher in the old Charlie Brown and Snoopy movies)
1 hour playtime
