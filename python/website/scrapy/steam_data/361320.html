About This Game


For Windows XP, Vista and 7 (This game will not run on Windows 8)

Now includes The Management Expansion Pack!
Additional Casinos, cities, attractions, employees and much more.



Classic strategy game from 2003. Run your own casino in the 1970s. It's classy. It's glitzy. It's all yours. Take a gamble. Start small with a slot machine or two. Employ some staff and open the doors. Pretty soon people will be talking about your place from coast to coast.

Visitor profiles vary from wealthy businessmen, professional gamblers to trailer trash and holiday flutterers. All have tastes to suit their budget and style. Teenagers seek noisy arcade machines, and high rollers want big stakes and big returns. They all have one thing in common: cash to spend.



As you grow to own hotels and casinos you'll want to set the amount of floor space given to rooms, bar and restaurant areas, roulette, card tables, slot machines, entertainment lounges, and to also set the level of payouts, profits and cream offs on the tables and machines. You will be able to see, in high detail, which of your outfits are popular. The graphical scale allows you to track the ebb and flow of the city at a zoomed out perspective, but then to zoom inside your buildings to see the gamblers playing Black Jack at the tables.

You can actively encourage the growth of the city by your actions. This is a tourist town; it has a multitude of businesses that very visibly attract customers if popular; the airport and roads become busy. The streets get choked. The tills ring more frequently. And, provided you're taking care of business, the profits will roll in.

Key Features

  • Casino, Inc. features three fictitious cities; Little Hope Springs, Fortune Falls and Chancersville
  • There are over 120 different characters in the game
  • There are over 50 attractions you can buy for your Casinos
  • Build 1 to 5 star hotel accommodation
  • Set up Limos and Shuttle Bus services
  • Damage your rivals by starting fights, spiking drinks and shooting people!!
  • Making money has never been so much fun!

Now also includes The Management expansion pack.
Additional Casinos, cities, attractions, employees and much more.

For such a dated title this is actually really good. Hothouse was well known for making very complex games, and this is no exception. It may look simple on the surface, watching over your one starting casino, but then you realize that there is an entire living city running in the background - and every person in it is being driven by their own motivations. Advertising, transportation, and expansion all come into play here. It starts off simple & easy but get more difficult and complex as time goes on - which is precisely the winning formula for a good strategy/simulation game.

Most of all, however, is that the developers have been positive, friendly and wonderfully supportive of this project ever since it arrived here on Steam. They wasted no time fixing the few remaining bugs and then, for no reason other than to be awesome, they threw in the expansion pack at no extra charge. And mind you, Hothouse isn't even around anymore. Roger is basically the antithesis of J.C.Smith over at "Repopulation."

So if you enjoy a complex strategy game and don't mind dated graphics to get it, you really can't beat this game - especially at it's price.

Your bouncers will take card cheats to the basement and beat them to death. That's all you need to know.

Not helpful? Okay, fine. I played this game a lot back when it came out in 2003. Yes, it's basically a casino management sim. The beauty of it though is that the game never takes itself very seriously. This is the casino game with blackjack and hookers. I'm not even kidding, you can hire 'escorts' for those casino guests that are looking for a little love. You spend most of your time dealing with sabotage from competing casinos in the form of cheats, troublemakers and hitmen. You get your guards to eject them or better yet, get your bouncers to work them over. Best part is, you can get your own cheats, troublemakers and hitmen and cause chaos at the other guys' casinos. Spiking drinks at the bar and watching people throw up and pick fights is always funny.

The downside? It won't run on Windows 8. Maybe there's a way around this. Also, the game looks bad. It didn't look so hot back in 2003 and it has not aged well no matter what resolution you set it to. You do at least get a choice of most modern resolutions. But the cost of the game is less than your average trip to Starbucks and it is a ton of fun if you don't mind a dated looking management sim.

Upgrading my review from a sideways thumb to an up thumb because reasons.

**Update** [They just added a bit of extra content free of charge! hurray! Also, when I first posted this, I didn't realize the game is actually pretty old and runs well considering its age. Well done!]

I just lost a couple of hours playing this game without realizing it. It's a good time waster, though I feel like a $2.50 price tag would easily get a lot more sales.

Pros:

After you muddle through the awkward sort of tutorial and get the hang of things, it's actually really fun!

Your bouncers actually do beat people to death.

I liked competing against other casinos.

Worth the $5 if they would clean up the bugs and add more features.

Cons:

A little pricey for what it delivers.

Slow and clunky feeling while learning. Feels frustrating at first.

Buggy when trying to get the bouncers/guards to take action.

Employee unhappiness is a little overtuned. Their happiness decays a bit too quickly for my tastes and doesn't seem to make any sense. They'll be making twice what the minimum for their position is and still ready to quit.

Changing screen resolution doesn't do anything, so everything looks huge and it gets frustrating because I'm constantly looking for better zoom out capability than what is provided.
