About This Game

CONTRAST is a puzzle/platform game where you can move between a fantastic 3D world and a mysterious shadowy universe in 2D in the blink of an eye. Delve into a dreamlike and surreal 1920s world, inspired by the performance art world of vaudeville and film noir, and cradled by a smooth and sultry jazz ambiance.

In this universe, where the boundaries between showmanship, magic, intrigue and deception are blurred, you play Dawn, the imaginary friend of a young girl named Didi. You have the power to slip from the 3D dreamscape into the parallel 2D shadow world bound to it. Cast light on a shadowy story by manipulating light sources in the 3D world to distort, enlarge or stretch the 2D shadow world. Manipulating and shifting between light and shadow will be the key elements that you will need to use to solve our world of shadow-based puzzles and help progress Didi's story!

Key Features
  • Shift between the physical 3D world and the 2D shadow world, at any lit wall surface
  • Create unique shadow landscapes by manipulating light sources and real world objects, and use this ability to solve mind-bending puzzles
  • Experience a new story and world, exploring adult themes through a childâs eyes
  • Immerse yourself in a lush vaudevillian world of cabaret singers, circus performers, and magic

Contrast is a very short game with flaws but it has a charming personality with some great high notes.

Pros:
+1920's noir aesthetic is really well done.
+Many clever puzzles, shifting between the 3D world and a world of shadows.
+Collectibles are not obnoxious.
+Not terribly hard to 100%.
+When the story is flowing well, it's pretty entertaining.
+Fantastic soundtrack.
+Mostly solid voice acting.

Cons:
-Only 2-3 hours of game play to see everything.
-Some puzzle elements are pretty obtuse. Lots of "huh, I didn't know that was a mechanic" moments when solving puzzles.
-Motivation for main character is pretty slim.
-Sometimes the transitions between shadow realm and real world aren't clean, leads you to die or repeat sections often.
-The story can hop around or drag, then it ends suddenly.
-NPC girl you spend most of your time with is frustrating and pushy to no end. Her whiney screeching of non-sequitur commands was a grind.

All in all I feel that this was a game that was aiming much higher than where it landed, but it didn't end up in a bad place generally speaking. There are plenty of fun bits alongside the annoying. Worth a pickup on sale, not so much at full price.

There is a lot to like about this game. It has an interesting story, and the 'noir' atmospheric setting works well with the game and it's main mechanic - being able to enter and move using shadows and light.

The puzzles are fun and strike a good balance of difficulty, while the collectibles / achievements are not too obnoxious to find and add a little replay-ability - I was able to find most collectibles during my first play through without hunting around too much.

The story is also interesting and keeps everything moving along nicely.

However, there are a couple of criticisms. Firstly, what some might see as a fault is the short length of the game. It only takes around 3 hours to fully complete (including achievements) although some might play faster than others.

Also, although I didn't experience any glitches, the puzzles where you have to carry objects while sometimes interesting do slow the pace of the game down a bit.

My biggest criticism is the abrupt ending. Without spoiling it, I will say that the game ends quite suddenly and while I knew the end was coming I was expecting there to be some explanation for how / why events were playing out.

Overall I would recommend this game, despite the criticisms it is fun, but I would only really buy this game during a sale as I don't think it is worth the price without any discounts.

A short (~4 hours, with all collectibles) romp through 1920's vaudeville culture that tells the story of a broken family trying to pick up the pieces of shattered dreams, and a little girl trying to make the best of everything, in spite of it. The game is short, to the point, and entertaining, though slippery and squirrelly controls at some parts give a bit of frustration to some of the more precise platforming moments. Puzzles are mostly simple and serve just to break up the platofrming flow, which is nothing super timed or intense. There is no combat in the game whatsoever, despite what the trailers obtusely hint at. It is pure platforming. The game is very linear, with side treks purely for collecting objects that lend a bit of backstory to the events that occur in the game.

Everything in the game comes together to emphasize the environmental aspects, pushing the circus and performance aspects with heavy jazz and lounge singer themes, and it all comes together wonderfully. Some story bits at the end seem to have been left intentionally vague and up to interpretation, and it causes a bit of heas scratching as to how things "actually work", but it's nothing that detracts from the actual experience.

An easy recommendation for almost anyone.
