About This Game

TowerFall Ascension is the definitive version of the hit archery combat game. Inspired by classics from the golden age of couch multiplayer, it's a 4-player local party game centering around hilarious, intense versus matches. The core mechanics are simple and accessible, but hard to master and combat is fierce. Loot treasure chests for game-changing power-ups, master the art of catching arrows out of the air, or descend on your foes and stomp them into submission. TowerFall is best played competitively with friends, cross-legged on the floor within punching distance of each other.

New in Ascension is the 1- or 2-player co-op Quest mode. Players work together, fighting off a variety of monsters and enemy archers across the land of TowerFall. It's a new adventure to explore when you don't have more friends handy to square off against.

New features in TowerFall Ascension include:
  • Brand new 1- or 2-player co-op Quest mode
  • 50 additional Versus arenas, for a total of 120 unique maps
  • More game-changing power-ups, such as Drill Arrows that burrow through walls
  • 4 new unlockable, playable archers, for a total of 8
  • A huge list of variants to customize your matches - a total of 75 ways to mix things up
  • Plenty of hidden secrets and surprises to discover with your friends

Product received for free
I recieved this product for free from a Steam support person to troubleshoot some issues I was having with the Steam controller on other games. Man am I glad they gave it to me. I never would've gotten it otherwise, but honestly I would even consider recommending this game at full price. It doesn't look like it's remotely worth it's price at first look, or even necessarily at the first bit of playing. But the boss design is AMAZING, the music is tight, and in general it's a real fun game. The hardest levels/bosses on the hardest modes are extremely challenging, but well designed. It sits on the cusp of what feels like artificial difficulty at times (as if certain attacks/events cannot be avoided), but then with practice you learn that even the hardest attacks or enemies are avoidable with enough skill. The difficulty curve is also well designed in my opinion.

Funnily enough, I got almost nothing out of the versus multiplayer mode. It's fine, I guess. Very bland if you ask me, although if you're looking for something more fair and less chaotic it's good. I think most groups of friends are looking more for smash bros and would have more fun playing that.

But the co-op and single player modes can't be beat. 10/10 coop, 10/10 single player, 8/10 trials mode, and 7/10 versus mode from me. I have 100%'d the game and put about 100 hours in doing so.

MattMakesGames has always made crazy platformer games with high amount of death, which is NOT caused by:
- Falling down, because like the old platformer games, falling down a hole means you pop up upstairs instead.
- Falling on enemies. While colliding with the enemy causes you to die, booping on enemies (even players in a versus match) has been a method proven by magical plumbers everywhere as the best method of elimination.
- Running out of arrows. Have I told you this game is all about arrows? Shooting arrows, picking up stray arrows from walls, dodging AND grabbing arrows out of the air (the mystical tower archery training paid off). You will always find amusement in arrows flying everywhere and people trying to aim at little pixelated sprites with only 8 possible directions and a brilliant projectile physics...until you get shot in the face, of course.

However, glorious, hilarious deaths will certainly be caused by:
- DIfferent arrows that comes in form of a powerup from a loot chest. Be it explosions, bounces of walls, floor hazard generation, there's arrows to suit every kind of your archery needs.
- Flavourful enemies, some only spawn in certain levels. Be it the nearly-harmless slime, pestering ghosts, and dark hooded figures with pointy tools that can deflect your shots, it's better to end them before they end you.
- Getting shot in the face by a wily foe on versus mode
- Getting shot in the back by a sneaky foe on versus mode
- Getting shot in the back by an ally on co-op mode
- Shooting an arrow up, missing that â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ bird and watch as the arrow plunge down right to your own character
- Lava. I mean, if Matt Thorson made a platformer game without floor hazards, people would flip.
- Getting shot in the face by your own bouncy arrow.
- Getting shot in the face by someone else's bouncy arrow.
- Trying to play single-string guitar with your bow...with Cursed Bow mod turned on.
- Getting divebombed from above (read: booped), Super Mario style.
- Failing dodge.
- Succeeding dodge and still get shot.
- Succeeding dodge, stole their bouncy arrow, tries to shoot them and ends up bouncing it at a wall and back to one's face.
- And many more.

It has huge replayability value when you play at couch coop with your friends. Done co-op on normal mode? Go hardcore. Done versus on normal mode? Turn on random mods and have a good laugh (or ruin your friendship). I wish they would expand on the story by adding narration and further the coop campaign, but I think that's what the Dark World expansion is for (I haven't checked). I have played coop with my brother and it has been such a blast that I wish the players can immerse further by adding background stories or certain triggers.

I highly recommend this for:
- Fans of MattMakesGames' old platformer games like Runman, Jumper and Untitled Story (that one with the birdthing).
- Those who are looking for a great couch coop game, playable sober or drunk.
- Fans of mechanically-challenging games. The platformer, jumping of walls, all the while trying to aim an arrow will be a great experience for you.
- Fans of great pixel arts and detailed, fleshed out game assets.
