About This Game

Super Hexagon is a minimal action game by Terry Cavanagh, with music by Chipzel.

Important facts about Hexagons:

  • Hexagons are first order permutohedrons: The vertices of a hexagon can be formed by permuting the coordinates of the vector (1, 2, 3).
  • The north pole of the planet Saturn has a hexagonal storm cloud pattern with 8,600 mile long sides, larger than the diameter of Earth.
  • Though hexagon may appear to be a difficult word to rhyme, there are actually dozens of words that rhyme with it. For example: Autobahn, decagon, decathlon, electron, Kyrgzstan, Lebanon, leprechaun and marathon.

If you don't have a mechanical keyboard, don't even think about it, your input precision will never be able to keep up with the game.

And, if you're not interested in games that actively hate the player, might also want to reconsider. This game could be a case study in terrible user experience. 180 degree screen rotations simultaneous with the time frame that you have to determine what you have to do, combined with situations that can only be successfully navigated with foresight or luck, and excessively difficult patterns shown up so rarely that you will almost automatically fail to them every time you encounter them do to a lack of touch time to learn from mistakes.

Once you get to the higher levels, you'll feel less and less like you're doing anything wrong, because it's actively designed to obfuscate the information you'd use as feedback to do better.

It's a niche kind of game, so you may still like it, but absolutely don't recommend.
