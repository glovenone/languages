30 June

Work Goes On

There was a delay in the development of a new version - well, gamedev is a hobby for all of us and we can not devote all our time to it.

However now the work continues. Next version that will most likely be released in July will contain a couple of new side quests, new landing party weapons, new humanity upgrades (e.g. bonuses to a sight and target range of a landing party), gui and control improvements.

Most of corrections and new features are inspired by you - and you help us greatly by providing feedback and your ideas.




Though sometimes a bug can also help. Remember that green acid weapon that a mutant in the Heritage quest was using? Accidentally due to a bug it was received by a landing party, and it turned out to be so cool that we decided to introduce it in the game.



We are always open for a new ideas, so feel free to open a discussion if you have any, together we can make the game better!

10 May

Update 0.5.2

Version 0.5.2 has been released. This update contains bug fixes and balance improvements based on Steam players reports. Thanks for those who posted bug descriptions and suggestions!

What's new:

  • Increased chance of Earth-like planet generation, should increase chances of establishing a colony
  • Reduced geodata research points, should not be that easy to achieve Tech Singularity achievement now
  • Acid rains will no longer spawn instantly. Instead they will spawn on a small area and increase in size each turn. So landing party will never be suddenly trapped in the middle of deadly rain that came out of nowhere.
  • Added 'skip turn' button to the planetary mission gui.
  • Added hotkeys for inventory and map
  • Landing party weapons are no longer visible in inventory and should be selected only on landing party page. Fixed problem with weapon list being empty.
  • Fixed bug with last line of dialogs and trade lists not showing.
  • Fixed possible crash after escaping star system in FTL quest.
  • Fixed scientists being duplicated in Quarantine quest.
  • Multiple fixes of typos and minor problems.

About This Game

Player takes control over a spaceship that was built with the cooperation all human nations.
His task is to explore space, establish relationships with alien races and bring back home any valuable objects.
Anything, from artifacts of forgotten civilizations to new technologies, from unusual lifeforms to secret alien cookie recipes
can help Earth overcome its political and energy crises. The human governments will monitor your efforts:
they wonât let you waste their time and use the precious spaceship inefficiently. And who knows what dangers
can be found in outer space, dangers that can be a threat not only to the lone vessel, but to the entire civilization...

1. Adventures and combat in space, on planets and inside spaceships.
2. Procedurally generated universe: star systems, planets, even animals and plants.
3. Many different alien races. Some may become your allies while others will definitely become your enemies.
4. Global storyline and large variety of quests.
5. Communicate with your crew members and officers, discover their story and personality
6. Research and development system: discover new technologies and upgrade your ship
7. Global strategy elements - help humanity establish first colony and outposts outside Solar system and choose
how they should progress.

Posted: 8 June
Product received for free
Portable Description
AuroraRL is a rather rough, yet very compelling space adventure. Tasked to explore space, establish alien relationships and discover the whereabouts of a missing craft, a considerable amount of time will be required to see everything this game has to offer.

Gameplay
Galaxy Exploration
Ordered to locate the disconnected Aurora 1, the Aurora 2 is deployed, taking heed that a successful journey through the Star Systems require resources, relationships, and information. A large galaxy map outfitted with a coordinate system contains many Star Systems, creating the world in which the Aurora 2 will navigate and explore.

Upon completing the extremely lacking âtutorialâ, intel lead the crew of the Aurora 2 to the Kliskâs home world. As a race known for working as space merchants, with a very strict code of honor, the first step towards finding the missing craft is achieved with an exchange of favors. A quick escort quest rewards locational information of the major racesâ home worlds. With no restriction on exploration, the player is then free to lead the Aurora 2.

Star System Exploration
When traversing the galaxy map, Star Systems provide locations of major interest. Inside are fully instanced, âgalaxy mapsâ of a smaller scale. Star Systems are comprised of: gas giants, planets, moons, ships, etc. Each object of interest may be scanned or interacted with. Ships (if friendly) can sell goods or information. Planets, moons and other non-gas giant objects can be scouted using a Landing Party.

Scouting & Landing Party
If a planetary object allows for landing, a quick scan will allow the player to choose the location of landing. A small map will aid in determining whether landing is beneficial by marking resources (needed to repair the ship, etc.), life (for research purposes) or key points of interest. Upon landing, a terrain will be generated and the Landing Party will begin their exploration.

The Landing Party is comprised of variable crew members (Scientists, Engineers, and Military Personnel). Their weapons and inventory may be personalized before deployment. Unless the planet is oxygenated, a decaying oxygen meter will dictate how much oxygen is remaining before a return to the spacecraft is necessary for replenishment. The Landing Party is controlled as a singular unit. A numerical health indicator represents the life of a crew member. Once that number fully depletes, a crew member will die and the number will reset until all crew members are dead.

Each planetary object will have a different terrain/biome. Some will be inhabitable, eliminating the need to utilize an oxygen reserve. Others will have environmental hazards or creatures that will attack upon sight. A flat, rectangular map is provided to mark the relative locations of interest, including the shuttle. Although flat, the boundaries of the map do overlap to create a continuous seam. Regarding planetary exploration, a few minor grievances experienced are listed below.

Planetary Exploration Grievances:
  • Portions of the terrain are un-crossable, yet are not distinguished in any way from places that can be crossed
  • Meteor showers create an un-skippable animation that occurs every turn
  • No motivation to promote physical exploration of the planet, as opposed to re-positioning the Landing Party

Battle System
If a hostile creature/ship is discovered, it can be defeated utilizing the crewsâ/shipâs weapons. The battle system is a hybrid between real-time and turn based systems. Upon encounter, pressing the âFireâ button will project a grid of the applicable range. If in range, enemy targets may be individually attacked. Running away (by physically moving the Landing Party/ship) may result in some free hits from the enemy.

Game Saves
If the ship happens to be destroyed, the game will end. To alleviate this burden, AuroraRL maintains an autosave feature, along with 4 dedicated save slots that can be used at any time. If a save is utilized during a dialogue option, the re-load will start the dialogue from the beginning.

Maintenance & Research
The ship hull starts with 10 pips of health by default. If damaged by an enemy ship, repair is performed by assigning Engineers to repair the hull, along with utilizing resources (bought or discovered from planetary landings). Research, which rewards the player with new weapons, maps, etc. are progressed by assigning Scientists to a particular task. There are only a finite number of Scientists/Engineers, and if deployed on a task, they may not be used in a Landing Party.

Presentation
Graphics
The graphics are somewhat lacking in regards to the planetary terrain and outer space. The screenshots in the Store description will help provide some insight. During important discussions/plot-related events, or dialogue, higher quality retro style images will be used. Although the graphics leave a lot to desire, it is a far cry from being bad, just sufficient for immersion and plot progression.

HUD
The HUD needs to be reworked to improve the quality of gameplay. During space exploration, it is a bit tedious to continually hunt down the information needed from a quest log, or to assign crew members to a task. During planetary exploration, the HUD will sometimes block the view of a creature in line of sight, creating an opportunity for ranged damage if the player continues moving the party. An auto-hide feature would remedy this particular problem.

Every screen in AuroraRL, with the exception of the space background, is presented through a window (similar to a computer screen window). It would be more immersive if the dialogue and menus would be more incorporated into the background/game. Also, not allowing âEscâ to exit a window also adds tedium to the gameplay.

Conclusion
Early Access
For those that are not aware of the Early Access concept, it is perfectly acceptable for bugs to be present (only minor ones observed) and game mechanics to be missing. AuroraRL is still relatively new, and a developer update was released last month.

Verdict
With everything taken into consideration, AuroraRL is definitely recommended to those that are fans of space adventures. Although there are minor bugs and a lacking UI, the compelling storyline and exploration easily makes up for it.

I think the following 3 points describe what to expect from the game pretty well.

1. The game is not even remotely rogue-like, it even has an auto-save function plus you can save/load at any time. Also there is not much replayability value.

2. It features a lot of story telling with some exploration, it plays more like a gamebook. So expect simple graphics and A LOT of text, especially tons of background infos and secondary character dialogs which don't change much in the end.

3. The game is playable although unfinished and hence rather short. I'm now 10hours into it and I think I've completed the main story and nearly all side quests. (The remaining one(s) require a lot of search which gets difficult towards the end of the game because of performance issues, a bug I guess.)

All in all I think it's worth the 5 Euros and although it not even comes close (yet) to the size and depth of Space Rangers 2, it feels a bit like that, minus the annoying realtime ground combat.
