About This Game

Chickens. For centuries we have oppressed them, grilling, roasting, stewing, and stuffing them in buns. We should have known the time would come for us to pay. And it did.

Intergalactic chickens invaded Earth, seeking revenge for the oppression of their earthly brethren. They came out of nowhere, and everywhere. It was only you who managed to repel the invasion, and saved humanity from an eternity of servitude.

Then, things got serious. The intergalactic chickens assaulted the solar system and tried make sure that every planet was inhabited exclusively by chickens. Yet again, it was you who took the world's fate in your ketchup-stained hands and saved the day.

But it isn't over yet. Once more, it's time to don your non-stick egg-repulsing helmet and fight for our ancestral right to omelettes. Only you can make a difference. Will you succeed? Will the future of chicken burgers be secure? Or will you end up as part of the menu in a galactic chicken restaurant?

Posted: 23 February
By far the hardest of the Chicken Invaders trilogy on Steam (CI3,4,5). As someone who gets frustrated easily, i find this title a little beyond acceptable in the sadism stakes. I'll explain why.
In most of the traditional top-down or bullet-hell shooters i've played there is a pattern to foes, there are gaps between projectiles, there are tactics to be learned, there is timing to be practiced. Chicken Invaders 3 keeps up with these traditions for the most part, but is ruined by a handful of levels which throw these features out of the window. You can be having the best run of your life, having practiced and learned the movements of foes and general projectile release ideas and filled in the rest with your own reactions and nous. Then you reach a level called "Chicken Square Dance" or "Chicken Geometry" which are completely random and invade all possible safe-spaces without any measurable consistancies or rotas. It's not projectiles that get in the way, it's the foes themselves you'll get wiped-out by.
It's REALLY annoying.

The end-zone bosses are more imaginative than in CI5, but the keyboard repetition sensor is not as good as in either of the two sequels so you can't fire as quickly in this game, also the weapons overheat a lot more easily.

Do i recommend it? Hmmmm. It's not as good as CI4; that game is this series' peak, but i do think CI3 is better than CI5 (which i gave a thumbs-down to). Yes, i would recommend it but only as an introduction to the series or for those who like their shooters to be bordering on unfair. But seriously, just get CI4 if you only want to buy one of these titles.
