4 July

Update 1.25

Hello!

Finally I caught up with my regular job and found some time to work on Spell Fighter. What's new:

- permanent item-hand snapping - you can now just single-click the GRIP button, which snaps an object to your hand, until you clik GRIP button again. Just holding the GRIP button works as before.

- grabbing stuff from the ground - your real world floor level is now always a few inches below the VR ground. You are a little shorter in game, but this way big Vive controllers don't block your hands from grabbing things from the ground.

- turning with the right trackpad, when in teleportation mode, is now possible only after clicking and holding it for 2 seconds. This should prevent accidental pressing.

- teleportation system - you now point on the ground with an "arc", instead of directly. You still need to wait for your avatar to get to the spot you pointed.

- fixed a bug where you couldn't shoot the second bullet from a pistol with your other hand.

Cheers!

20 April

New teleporting system - ideas?

I read in most reviews that all you want is simple teleporting system for movement. Point on the ground and you are instantly there. This would break the game. You would be able to just ignore all enemies and teleport away to the end of every level in 5 seconds with zero difficulty.

The current solution is to make the teleport target slowly move towards the pointed spot on the ground, but this idea seems to be universally disliked.

My other ideas:

1. Teleporting cooldown - you must wait 3 seconds after teleporting (bad idea I think, it would be annoying)

2. You can't teleport at all if there are enemies nearby (15 meters away, let's say - but that would block you totally in later sections, since there are enemies everywhere)

So far, even though it's disliked, the slow moving capsule idea at least works. I can make it pretty (the capsule could be an animated human avatar), but I'm out of other ideas. Ok... so any other ideas anyone?

I'm open to suggestions. Please leave a comment if you have ideas for this :)

About This Game

REQUIRES HTC VIVE.

A FPP fantasy VR demo for movement controllers (currently only Vive), in which you can sword fight skeletons and cast spells by really speaking to the microphone (optional - read manual on Steam to activate).

You can also shoot medieval guns, bows, throw stuff at your enemies, summon and commad your wooden golem minions. It’s core is a small sandbox, where you can train your skills, but it also has a quest to complete!


You can rush through the game's quest in about 25 minutes, normal paced playthrough can take up to an hour.

System Requirements

    Minimum:

    • Processor: i5-4690 3.5GHz

Why is this game free?!

This is the best game Ive played on the vive yet. Its a game! Not an "experience" (hey, nothing wrong with those, they are lovely). Not a short arcade game. It claims its a demo. And in some sense it is, it feels a bit like an amazingly functional and wellput together early access game. So much so it feels like a game to me, and not a demo.

I play a lot of the skyrim kind, and this is pretty much the core of "the game Ive wanted to play every since I first played Ultima Underworld". The sword play is masterful, spells and guns are fun and pretty nituitive, animations are excellent, game play is fair, stuff is telegraphed nicely, the instructions are all clearly laid out nicely in books ingame, etc etc etc. I climbed to the top of a tall tower, ran off it and lived!

Whats not so good? The controls are hard. Like its hard to throw a spear, without worrying you are going to put the vive controller into the ceiling/wall. Have a big play space. Its kind of annoying you have to hold to grip button to grip stuff, toggle would be way nicer. I find it a bit confusing trying to wear all the weapons (maybe Im just unco). The movement is via trackpad, and it is something that takes some... really concious thought that you are in a game moving your avatar via a trackpad, else it would be/is nausea inducing.

Seriously give this a go. Its the best game on the vive!

This game (well..demo) is freaking amazing. For those of you who played Vanishing Realms and thought how cool that game would be with "real" graphics instead of being so cartoony , you're going to want to try this one out. What a feeling of immersion to be inside this world. The spell fighting itself is quite clunky and needs to be streamlined though. Spell casting is much too slow and clunky. You have to trace a shape with your hand to cast a spell, but are limited by the slow speed of the points you are connecting animating. It just needs to be streamlined is all, just let me trace the spell sign as fast as I want and adjust the game around that. After all I can fire a gun, shoot an arrow or swing a sword as fast as I want, why can't I cast a spell that way? Anyhow I mainly say this as suggestion and not negative because this is a demo and I'm sure the dev is experimenting and seeing what works. Weapons on the other hand, are just right. The first time you realize you can radially deviate your hand back and grab a spear so you can heft it over your shoulder is a joy.

Locomotion is a mixed bag. Teleporting is ok, it's slow for what I assume to be a limiter on it being overpowered. It's short range and it's not clear where you can and can't teleport to. You also get stuck in spots where Teleporting doesn't work, such as on one of the bridges made out of planks. You can also move with the touch sensors similar to using a game pad, but get ready for some major motion sickness. The touch sensor locomotion is far superior to the Teleporting, and I'd like to think I will eventually develop VR legs as they say. Dev note: for some reason rotate right only works sporadically, but rotate left works perfectly.

The quest is pretty cool, especially for a free demo. Dev note: Thanks for scaring me with the spiders, you owe me a new set of underwear.

I seriously think this dev needs to go on kickstarter and get a ton of money and make this the next Skyrim. If Vanishing Realms can sell their game for $20 and make money with their cartoon graphics and simple game play, how the heck can this dude not do much better? For the record vanishing realms was an awesome game, simply amazing, but Spell fighter VR has the potential to be better. Dev note: I'm first in line to hand you my money for a finished product.
