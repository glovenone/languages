About This Game

Purchase Shiftlings and get access to the Shiftlings Screen Saver! Enjoy our tenacious technicians bouncing around your screen all day long!

The galaxy's biggest no-brainer!

Control two adorably dim-witted alien space janitors on their quirky quest to repair the galaxy!

Strategic Single Player & Co-op Missions:

Play on your own, challenge a friend locally or across the galaxy (online).

50 Dynamic Interplanetary Puzzles:

5 themed planets with 10 levels each which increases in difficulty as players advance.

Cosmic Challenges of Size & Space:

Shift weight between the custodian cosmonauts quickly to avoid traps.

High Re-playability:

Blast back to the beginning to discover new hidden collectibles to unlock more secrets to the universe.

Oh wow.. been so long since I played a Sierra game. I did all the quest games.

A beautiful game with it's gameplay mechanics built on a fart joke. Things get pretty low brow throughout the game.

You play as two tube connected aliens (or one in co-op) who have to navigate though levels to solve puzzles. One is large and can push object as well as pull the small one behind him. The other is small and can fit in small spaces and bounce off the top of the big guy. The two aliens can shift thier sizes so the big one is small and the small one is big which you need to do alot in order to get both alien through most of the game.

It can be managed mostly as a single player game but probably would be alot easier as a co-op experience. The one bonus level I played seemed so hard that it probably demands to be done in co-op.

I've been able to play the game just fine on an underspeced PC as I am playing on a 64 bit dual core processor and in Cinema 4K. There is still the dreaded possiblity that the later levels will be unplayable due to slow processor. At least I had the chance to play the demo to see how good or bad it would be.

Also recommended that you play with a gamepad as it's a more natural fit for it.

Shiftlings is a very fun platformer/puzzle game filled with great challenges. You control two funny alien engineers who are attached together by a hose and one is fat and one is tiny. By pressing a button you will transfer the air to the other Alien and he will become inflated. Using that mechanic, you will progress through multiple levels trying to get to the exit after activating some device that turns on the exit's teleporter.

This is very funny! When you switch size it makes a fart noise and there's a show host that comments your good and bad decisions. The color pallette is vivid and the graphics are great.

The Controls are very easy to get and the more you progress, new mechanics appear that will challenge your brain. There are also leaderboards for each levels to add a little bit of competition with the other players.

The game can be played solo, local Co-op and online co-op. Couch co-op is very fun and will make you laugh a great deal while online co-op can be a bit frustrating if you team up with a random troll that always switch size just to giggle at the fart noise.

I recommend this game to everyone who loves co-op games, puzzles and quirky humor.

Posted: 24 June, 2015
This game is adorable, and is providing some surprisingly difficult timing, progression, and reflex puzzles.

Its a relatively simple concept; needing to control 2 inept alien maintenance workers, permanently connected by a tether hose to each other, with a forced size shifting, One must always be fwoomped up large with a weak jump ability, and the other small, more agile, and able to bounce off the head of it's puffed up partner. Switches move platforms and doors, blocks can be shoved around by the larger alien, instant-death traps are littered all over the 50+ levels and the level designs make excellent use of providing limitations for movement with a reasonable progression of difficulty as you learn new tricks to get around areas.

There's an interesting little meta story that goes along with your progression, allowing for an announcer to give commentary as you play through the levels... and there are times that gets a bit irritating, particularly if you're struggling through a difficult section where there'll be a quick quip every time you make a mistake and die, but overall it's pleasant enough.

As a solo-plyer game, you get to alternate control of each alien, or you can control both simultaneously, both following the same directions and jumping at the same time, and though that makes the timing tricky for some puzzles, its very workable. There is also the option to play co-op, and have each player control a single alien, which makes some things faster, but also more confusing.

Limited replayability for time-attacks or item collection (3 semi-hidden collections per level that you can go back at any time and try to grab if you miss them on the first run-through, They are all findable, but some will require some brain-work to snag), but it's a fairly reasonable length game if you're only planning on a single run-through.
