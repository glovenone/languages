About This Game

A polished top down action shooter set in a digital like world with upgradable mechs.

Vitris-47 has been the central stage of many conflicts over the ages. The Idalin empire rules over the planet with an ironhand and war is always present. On the other side, there´s the resistance, trying to retake the planet to its former glory and you are part of it.

Driving your mech around the gorgeous digital cityscapes of the planet, the time has come for a new power to arise and put an end to this war.

Gare or glare? Jokes aside, the grahics are a wonderful example of more is less aethestic. Beautifully rendered 2D wire-frame models, almost x-ray like in their luminosity. Maybe a little too bright and opaque enough to get stuck behind from time-to-time.

The simple gameplay is enjoyable, though with controller support lacking you may want to use Controller Companion (look it up here on Steam) or similar to make the game controller friendly...if the devs don't eventually get around to that themselves. The ability to customise your Mech from the start was a wonderful change. Gimme a slow firing, can take anything you can throw at it and will throw twice as much back at you Mech any day.

Down side? Game length and price. I was fortunate enough to score a copy in an IndieGala bundle so the price was a fraction of the listed price. For the length - 12 missions - 3-ish hours of gameplay (if you complete each mission in full before going on to the next and allowing for the odd case of 'OGM I'm surrounded...OMG what the I'm dead' moments) I'm not sure the full price ($9.99 I think) is worth it. Maybe with more levels it would certainly make the $/hr value better.

On special - go for it. Otherwise - have a think first.

A fun little diversion with a lot of excellent ideas that are used sparingly. With just 12 missions, it didn't take long to complete everything, including the optional bonuses.

It's very easy, but it also features some great pathfinding, partner and enemy AI for such a small project.

The low-key piano music is a bit odd at first, but helps create the slow pace that the game moves at. Despite being slow, it can also be frantic in a couple of the later levels, with Mission 8 being a particular stand-out for 'traditional' top-down arena shooting craziness.

Could've done with controller support and quite a lot more content. Maybe an 'endless' arcade mode, or something. But what IS here is pretty good, all in all.

Have you ever dreamed to control a mech?

Do you dare to dream further? Do you dare to dream about controlling a Sapphire mech?

If the answer is yes , sorry bruh all I can offer is a fun little twin stick shooter type of game with upgradeable mechs.

You can upgrade 3 parts of your mech - legs , body and guns. These parts have 3 categories , fast moving/shooting but lightly armored/low damage , medium , and slow moving/shooting but heavy armored /damege (which means you can basically turn your mech into a tank if you choose all heavy parts). Each type of legs gives you a special perk - the typical two legs give you a barrier that you can activate , sort of like a shield but it stays in place instead of moving with your mech exploding when its duration is up , the rat-track legs give you bullet time (which is nice sometimes as rat-tracks are hellaslow and bullets tend to be hellafast) not sure about the arachno legs as I never saw the need to use them or the medium body/weapon for that matter.

The game is a little on the short side , with only 12 missions , replay value is almost non existent , as the collectables are not that hard to find. That being said , I consider the asking price of 8 € too high (I got it as a gift) for a game that was once free to play browser game , as far as I know.
