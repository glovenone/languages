Want to participate in our Paper's Cut Edition closed beta?

Hi folks!

we have only a couple days until Paper's Cut Edition goes public! So after several successful tests with some fans and friends during the past weekend, we decided it's a good time to let you try it out! we invite you all to take part in the Flat Kingdom Paper's Cut Edition closed beta stage. It will last only a few days, and your feedback about it will be really valuable for us!



Will explain you how to access the update:

The most important point we are testing with this closed beta stage is that the old saves don't get lost/overwritten with the new 1.0.3 update, because Fat Panda found the way to keep using them after the update and continue playing your savegame without the need to start all over!

So to test this is working well without taking any risk, first thing I recommend you is to backup your savegames, they are located under: C:/users/youruser/AppData/LocalLow/Fat Panda.

Once you have a copy of your saves in a safe place, go to your Steam Library, select Flat Kingdom and go to Properties in the contextual menu of the game, then go to the Betas tab and finally activate it using the password "PapersCut2016".

That's all, at this point the game should be updating to 1.0.3 and you should be able to continue playing from your latest savepoint (if you've already beaten the tutorial in previous versions), or start over from the very beginning of the game and enjoy all the new cutscenes and tutorial we've added.

Please contact us at support@gamesstarter.com or comment here if you have any issue so we can address it as soon as possible (in that case please specify your OS, gamepad/keyboard model, etc).

This update is really important to us, as we want to address some key issues with the game (you can find the full list below). We hope you like it, and if you find we did a good job, please consider reviewing the game on Steam. That will help us to be more relevant among the myriad of platformers that are on Steam, causing other players to find Flat Kingdom, especially during the upcoming weeks that's the Steam Summer Sale.

Paper's Cut Edition / V1.0.3 CHANGELOG:

### Added
  • New and improved animations for expressions and special moves of Flat (i.e. stomp).
  • Flat is now more agile, specially when jumping and gliding (faster stomping/super stomping, he can jump even when he’s trapped in a corner, Glide doesn't get interrupted when Flat collides with a wall).
  • Added full localization to Russian and German.
  • New intro-tutorial sequences explaining in more detail about Flat past and each character's role.
  • Improved combat tutorial, more progressive and clear.
  • New and improved FX sounds (run, steps, mischievous plant, switching shapes, gameover, steps).
  • Added Logitech F310 gamepad support.
  • We added signals indicating deathly pits.
  • The game displays the number of collected coins on the upper right corner of the screen when Flat is close to a merchant.
  • Added gamepad and keyboard button/key remapping option.
  • We added a combat guide in the UI that often reminds you the relationships between the shapes and which one beats the other (this hint can be disabled at your choice).
  • Now you can check in the world map how many scrolls are left in each world, this will be a great help for those of you trying to get to 100%!

### Changed
  • New character design for Flat, adding brand new switching shape effects.
  • Camera follows Flat at the same speed when running.
  • Selecting the Continue option from the main menu now spawns you on the last checkpoint you activated (within a level) instead of the world map.
  • Some enemies designs have been improved: Roundowls, stonesnails and grumpeels.

### Fixed
  • Fixed small bugs in bosses: Quetzal, Fenrir, Kraken.

About This Game

A STORY ABOUT DIMENSIONS, SHAPES AND JEWELS


According to the Flat Kingdom’s legends, the world was once filled with malice and chaos because the 3D realm dominated all. But one day, a wise sorcerer created six Magical Jewels that kept the effects of the third dimension at bay, granting peace and prosperity to the beautiful 2D world.

However, one day an evil masked thief named Hex stole one of those Jewels and kidnapped the princess. This disrupted the balance and made the chaos return.

The king of Flat Kingdom knew there was only one being able to save their land: Flat, the little shapeshifting hero! With his unique abilities, he’s not only able to change his shape, but is also the only one in the kingdom capable of withstanding the 3D chaos.

Now the time has come. Flat must use his incredible powers to save the world and restore the Flat Kingdom to its former glory. Will he be able to stop Hex before it’s too late?

GAMEPLAY


Flat Kingdom is filled with fun platforming action, puzzles and some Metroidvania elements. It also features a unique combat system! Instead of directly attacking your foes, you’ll have to touch them while transformed in the correct shape to beat them! Kinda like a rock-paper-scissor game.

Every shape in Flat Kingdom beats the opposite, so sharp triangles can defeat the soft circles, heavy squares beat the fragile triangles and squishy circles can triumph over the slow squares. Both enemies and puzzles can be beaten using the correct shape and strategy, so you'll have to observe them and come up with the best solution.

You can also learn new abilities along the way that will help you solve even more puzzles, beat enemies faster and reach new areas that were inaccessible to you before!

FEATURES


  • Jump into a beautiful papercraft world, filled with details and references to classic games.
  • Test your might with epic and challenging battles against giant bosses.
  • Uncover all of the game’s secrets! There are side quests, upgrades and all kinds of surprises.
  • Learn all about the kingdom’s lore and history by finding hidden scrolls.
  • Music by legendary composer Manami Matsumae! (Mega Man, Shovel Knight, Mighty No. 9.

This of course was until a thief called Hex decided he wanted to kidnap the Princess and while he was at it he also stole one of the magic jewels. The land began to transform back to 3D, bringing back chaos with it. It is up to you as the hero flat to save the Princess and the Kingdom!

Flat is the only one capable of surviving encounters with 3D objects. Flat has the special ability of being able to transform into various shapes. He can be circle, triangle or square. Flat Kingdom is a 2d platformer/puzzler. It’s an amazing looking game and very well made. The game starts off very interestingly with a pretty nice story going on. Basically the world has these 6 magical jewels which have sealed the evil that is “3D” away and now a thief is trying to steal these magical jewels and in the process he also kidnaps the princess. The world is becoming 3D from the usual 2D and the animals and even the trees/plants are freaking out and going berserk. Our hero Flat must go and stop the thief.

Graphics:

The game features gorgeous looking 2d graphics sort of like Paper Mario. The art style is very unique and pleasing to the eyes. The enemies look very well made and the scenery is awesome as well. There aren’t many options in the graphics menu other than the ratio adjustment (for me only 16:9 and 16:10 were available) and full screen on/off. The game ran at a smooth 60FPS for me.

Sound:

The game has very good soundtrack. It really suits the theme and visuals of the game. The soundtrack has that ‘charm’ of a beautiful game. I really like the sound bits as well. No complaints there in the music/sound department.

Gameplay:

The game features our hero who can turn into different shapes. All the shapes give him different abilities. There’s square shape which makes him heavy and he can push objects, break wooden platforms and kill certain enemies. The circle one allows him to double jump and kill certain types of enemies. The triangle is his last shape which allows him to run fast, pop objects with the pointy triangle end and kill certain types of enemies.

All enemies have a weakness to a shape and you must figure that out either via trial and error or by making educated guesses e.g. the pointy/beaked enemies will get killed by the heavy square shape. The platforming also requires you to change in to the correct shapes to get past obstacles. There are underwater parts of level too which can be accessed via the square shape as the heavy shape sinks and you can use that with the circle shape to navigate the waters.

The controls are not the best part of the game if you’re playing with a keyboard. The keys are cannot be custom mapped and in my opinion the default mappings are not so good, so a controller is highly recommended. Even the game developer recommends using a controller as you start the game this is the first thing you’ll see.

You can collect coins throughout the world and buy extra hearts with them which will help you. The game is not very difficult, the boss battles can be interesting and fun. The checkpoints are generous. The levels are long and plenty. There are hidden sections with coins, scrolls etc. and plenty of achievements, so the game’s replay value is also pretty good. A short video of the gameplay can be found below:

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=691283257

Frustrations/annoying things: Please note I’ll be adding this section to my reviews regularly. This can make people aware of the flaws of the game as I don’t usually review the games I don’t like. Most of the games that I review also come with some flaws so I’ll be using a separate section to address these flaws/frustrations/annoying things.

This game really frustrated me with the controls as I much prefer to use keyboard. I had to use a controller to play this game as the keys cannot be remapped and the default mappings are horrible in my opinion. Other than that I found no frustrating part in the game.

Graphics: 9/10
Sound/Music: 8/10
Gameplay: 8/10
Overall: 8/10

Conclusion:

I highly recommend this game due to its cute charm and awesome art style. The story is pretty good and the idea of different shapes having different abilities is quite good and unique. The soundtrack is also pretty nice. The game is cheap and will give you quite a few hours of fun. The children will also love this game and if you’re a parent looking for a nice cute game for their kids, this game fits the bill pretty good.

A lovely platformer 2D platformer at danger of turning into a 3D platformer without your help! Quite amusing idea for a story! I prefer 2D platformers over 3D ones where I get confused easily, so I was ready to take control of Flat, the cute ball of a protagonist of the game and go on an adventure!

The game presents itself as a light hearted platformer with a simple story, simple mechanics and offers a good amount of replayability. I really enjoyed the laid back nature of this game, the overall difficulty was on the lower side, so that made it a nice and relaxing experience.

The signature mechanic of the game is shapeshifting between a circle, triangle and a square. Each of these forms get additional abilities to use to overcome any obstacles. This makes for some very interesting problem solving and unique methods for defeating enemies. If you need one reason to play this game, then this is definitely it, I really enjoyed the platforming combined with trying to figure out how to approach and defeat any opposition!

The story and art style looks more appealing to the younger audiences, but not necessarily limited to that. The typical "Oh no some evil person does something that will destroy our world" story is simple enough, but as you play through the game you'll reveal extra story bits as you discover secrets. Backtracking as you get new abilities in addition to the standard ones adds that wonderful buzzing replay value :D Gotta catch em all coins!

So far there were only a few issues I had with the game that I hope they will be addressed in the future.
* First, there were some spots where the jumping was too pixel perfect. It's possible to jump up those high platforms, sure, but requires way too much time.
* Second issue was the controls. I've already messaged regarding the PS3 controller support but even the default button mapping seems a little off. I would definitely like to see an option to remap the controls.
* Third point for critique is for a few sections where you (are very likely) expected to speed through with the Triangle. I would love to do that, but the character moves so much forwards that I have almost no time to react and need to memorize.
* And finally, some of the checkpoints are pretty far distanced. It adds a little to the difficulty, which I don't mind actually, but there were some stages with segments that slow down the pace, so going back took a long time.

Overall, I would recommend this game. If you like Kirby games, then this is surely gonna interest you. At least give the demo a try and see how you like the whole concept of shapeshifting. Shapeshifting, platforming, backtracking sounds interesting? Go for it! A pick up and play game to relax and chuckle at the cuteness? Go for it!

Genre: 2D Platformer
Developer: Fat Panda Games
Publisher: Games Starter
Copy supplied by PCGN

Flat Kingdom is a 2D platformer developed by Fat Panda Games and published by Games Starter. It has been a while since I last played a platformer: when I was very little they were the primary genre that I played, especially the movie-licensed Disney games.

STORY
The Flat Kingdom is a beautiful and peaceful 2D land, but now it’s threatened by an evil thief and becoming 3D! The only who can stop the chaos is Flat, a little hero able to change into a circle, triangle or square.
The story is very fun, and it gets more complex the more you play it. But by the time you reach the ending, it is very clear that this was supposed to be a family friendly game, and I did enjoy following our hero on his journey through the Flat Kingdom. The characters that you interact with have some really short but funny lines, and they sure cheered me up.

GRAPHICS
I fell in love with the art style! While it certainly isn't mind-blowing, seeing a 2D world made entirely by circles, squares and triangles transforming into a 3D one is a marvel to watch. The animations and character design are very cute, and the 51 enemies and bosses that you fight are all varied.
Each part of the kingdom feels different from the other, and each level has been design very well: you rarely get lost, and there are always hidden secrets that expand the lore.

GAMEPLAY
I played the game with a controller since the keyboard controls didn't feel very intuitive.
You have at your disposal four buttons: one to jump, one to turn into a circle, one to turn into a square, and one to turn into a triangle. As a circle, you can double jump. As a square, you move slowly but can move heavy objects around. As a triangle, you move fast. The further you go into the game, the more abilities you unlock to beat different puzzles.
I found the combat to be very original: since everything in the Flat Kingdom has a shape, you have to change shape in order to kill enemies. Square beats triangle, triangle beats circle, circle beats square. All you have to do is just to change shape and walk into the enemy.
The bosses that you encounter are very fun to beat, and a couple of them were starting to get very frustrating. Nevertheless, it is very fun to play and the controls are very smooth, making me fall in love for platformers once again.

CHALLENGE
The game might be family-friendly, but it sure isn't easy. All of the puzzles revolve around finding an object or pushing buttons, and they are never too hard. The real challenge is in the combat: when you are fighting enemies of multiple shapes, it isn't always easy to remember which shape to use. The boss fights are also very challenging, but after understanding the weak spot of an enemy, you can easily exploit your strategy.

SOUND
The soundtrack is very cheerful and it will definitely appeal to younger players. The sound effects are pretty standard, and the voice acting is fine.

REPLAYABILITY
In each level there are hidden scrolls to find, and multiple coins to collect. There is also a secret ending unlocked at 100% completion, and 48 Steam achievements to unlock.

Overall, Flat Kingdom is a very good platformer with effective gameplay, fun story, charming art style and great challenge. A must buy for fans of the genre.

What is Flat Kingdom?
Well, it's a 2D Platformer and that's a given, but it's a quirky 2D Platformer with heart!

The story is pretty standard; the princess has been kidnapped, you need to save her and stop the villain from aquiring the 6 jewels, but the story isn't why you should play the game!

The world not only looks stunning, but it feels pretty special to play. I had a few complaints with the way the game played and the occasional odd design choise, but Fat Panda Games' latest update fixed almost every issue I had.

The music goes perfectly with this well crafted world and was actually one of the things I was looking forward to. As much as I was excited to find out where I'd be going next, I was also equally as excited to hear the next theme, since the soundtrack is pretty great!

The levels are fun, the bosses are clever, the final world is phenominal! I didn't know what I would be getting into, but I can say that I immensely enjoyed my time with this game, and I'm definitely up for revisiting it in the future!

I initially gave it a 7/10. But the amount they improved easily earned them an extra point.
This game deserves an 8/10.

Thank you, Fat Panda Games! I look forward to the next one! :)
