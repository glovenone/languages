About This Game

Galcon 2 is a demolition derby in space! Send swarms of ships from planet to planet to conquer the galaxy in this fast paced multiplayer strategy game! Galcon 2 includes:

  • Clans battling for galactic supremacy
  • Tournament to prove your amazing skills
  • Betting to take Galcoins from the weak
  • Multiple ship shapes like triangle and trapezoid
  • Slick planets made of lava and ice

What a fun game. In Galcon 2, players try to conquer all planets on a map by colonizing planets. Planets under your control will produce ships, which can then be sent to nearby planets to spread your territory. Planets of different sizes will produce ships at different rates, but may cost more or less depending on their production times. So larger planets will produce ships faster, but will be harder to reach and harder to acquire. Smaller planets will be more accessible, but will produce ships slower. The goal of Galcon 2 is to find the right balance and utilize strategy to conquer as many planets as possible.

What I enjoy about Galcon 2 is how simple it is to play. There are no complicated colonization protocols. Any planet may be colonized so long as it contains fewer ships in storage than the amount of ships attacking it. That's it.

However, there is an interesting level of strategy to Galcon 2. First, only gray neutral planets will display colonization cost, ie. a neutral planet might cost 25 ships, making your decision easy in whether to attack it. However, planets colonized by other players will *not* show colonization costs, so pay close attention to ship traffic locations from enemy player activity to guess where their weak spots are.

Second, diplomacy often comes into play in large multiplayer matches. Games will begin as mad rushes to colonize planets and begin producing ships. Then, as the number of neutral planets dwindles, conflicts begin to arise. Players must juggle whether or not they can take over their neighbor's territory, and if doing so will make them appear vulnerable to other players. There is a certain strategy in trying to incite conflicts while biding time to make a proper attack.

Third, though Galcon 2 is typically a free-for-all game (designated team matches exist, but are not as common), teamwork finds itself arriving in the most incredible of situations. In a three-player skirmish, when one player assumes large control of the map, the two other weaker players may realize silently they must work together temporarily to stop the growing threat. Galcon 2 is full of spontaneous blasts of teamwork, but also backstabbing and surprise attacks.

Galcon 2 is one of the most accessible and friendly real-time strategy games on the market today. I myself am hardly a strategy player, preferring simple and obvious games, yet I have so much fun with Galcon 2. Now that it's free there is no excuse not to give it a try!

TL;DR Galcon 2 is a high quality, fast paced, indie RTS and it's totally free. No matter if you're looking for something to play for a couple minutes to pass the time, or you want an RTS that you can really dig into and master Galcon 2 is for you.

Actual review: Alright, I may as well throw a review out there for Galcon 2. Long story short I've been playing different versions of Galcon since mid 2008. I've always loved real-time strategy games, and Galcon (being the very core of what makes them great) hooked me instantly. Galcon takes the idea of an RTS and condenses it into it's purist form. No spending the first 5-10 minutes building up your base, scouting the enemy, making sure you build the right units to counter your opponent. You have planets that generate ships, those ships can be sent to attack other planets to generate more ships or left to defend your planets. Now add 8 or so other people and you get an action packed fast paced arcade style RTS.

The game is super easy to learn, there's a tutorial, single-player bots with a wide range of difficulty levels, and even a co-op mode to play with your friends against bots. Once you've got the hang of it, multiplayer is a whole new world. Galcon 2 is the very definition of easy to learn, hard to master.

To top it all off, this version is totally free. The only micro-transactions are cosmetic, running a tournament, or running your own server which you really only need if you want to run mods (did I mention the game is modable?). You don't need to pay a penny to get the full experience, plus you can earn the in-game currency over time (but I'm not going to dive into that system here).

The community around this game is also dedicated and nice, there's always someone willing to help teach you if you'd like to learn more, just visit the forums and ask.

Anyway, I'm absolutely biased, like I said I've been playing versions of this game for 5 or 6 years now. I also currently help moderate the forums, and have admined past versions of the game. But I've only stuck around this long because I really do love the game and it's community.

If you've ever got any questions about the game, want help getting better, or just a friendly face to play some rounds with, feel free to message me (Chi-Ro) on the website and I'll be happy to help!
