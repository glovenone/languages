About This Game

Turn a scripted steel-cage wrestling match into a real fight in this 250,000-word interactive novel!

You've always dreamed of becoming pro wrestling's biggest star...but a wrestler's world is fraught with hardship and betrayal, in and out of the ring. Become a powerhouse, a technician, a high-flier, or focus on your promo skills. There's more than one road to success.

But none of those roads will be easy. This is a world where your biggest fans are your harshest critics; where the front office is more dangerous than the squared circle; where friends can become enemies with a single heel turn; where, sometimes, the only way to win is to lose, spectacularly.

This is professional wrestling. And you're about to change it, forever.

Slammed! is an epic interactive professional-wrestling novel by Paolo Chikiamco, where your choices control the story. The game is entirely text-based--without graphics or sound effects--and fueled by the vast, unstoppable power of your imagination.

There's never been a professional wrestling game like this, a game where the outcome of your final match, your choice of opponent, and your relationships affect the ending. When's the last time you played a pro-wrestling RPG with a "kayfabe" stat?--or where your trash-talking "promo" ability is as important as your core strength and wrestling technique?

  • Enjoy a 250,000-word personal tale of friendship, competition, and revenge.
  • Develop not only your physical abilities, but a favored wrestling style.
  • Become a heroic face, or a villainous heel--or even turn heel.
  • Turn a scripted match into a real fight--and vice versa.
  • Decide when to keep kayfabe, and when to break it.
  • Play as male or female, gay or straight.

I played Slammed on mobile, loved it so much I bought it again on a device that I can actually read reliably, and then quickly took to a review to extoll its virtues.

Slammed has your character rise from an no-name indie star to the top match at the biggest PPV at the biggest professional promotion. The story is written with heart and charm and genuine love of wrestling. With a brilliant little cheat on the part of the author (Matches are sometimes real and sometimes scripted), you have a game where everything can be on the line in a fight, and in another situation you might meet the aging veteran who never had the push he deserved or the tacky, unprofessional wrestler who takes issue with the booking. This could have ended up being a disastrous attempt to merge both the magic of kayfabe and the grit behind the curtain, but the writer pulls it off wonderfully.

Even better, there aren't just several endings, there are at least three different whole final scenarios depending on your choices, a few secret endings here and there, multiple romances to pursue, there is some replayability here.

I only have a couple of complaints about the game and they both stem from it being a standard Choice of Games game. First, there are no save slots. Pure auto-save. There are good branching scenarios throughout the whole game so replaying is not a pain, but several ending variants are determined entirely by your actions in the last three or so chapters. There are not many dead ends but you can lose the big matches and have to replay the whole story for a less unsatisfying ending. (Fairly quick if you have read it all before, thankfully.)

Second, Choice of Games shoehorns in their pet device where you can be any sex and pursue any sex, but these games are not Mass Effect in which you can lard up the game with a dozen cheesy romances of every orientation. The result is characters get gender bent and FEEL gender bent if you pick an unnatural (respective to the writing) orientation. Slammed does pull this off better than other Choice of Games games. The womens league wrestlers all get their own descriptions so even the ones that perform the same role as they would for a male player character still feel like their own characters and there is a really cute story path that is appropriately open only to female characters. But there's one specific love interest in Slammed who is either a charming feminine woman or a kinda weird feminine man. It's a small complaint overall though.

Just the concept of kayfabe as a stat and a mechanic is amazing, and it works great here. The writing is solid all around, and it's worth noting that it attempts to be accessible to non-wresting aficionados too by providing little explanations of wrestling lingo at times, though it's probably not quite enough for the entirely uninitiated. Keep your search engine of choice at the ready if that is the case for you, and you'll be a-okay.

It branches plenty, about as much as you'd expect from a Choice of game. If I was left wanting more, it is more a testament to the quality of the game (and my fascination with the sport, to be perfectly fair) than a judgement on how much content there is.

Very much recommended; it's the wrestling adventure most savvy fans dream of, and unique in what it does to the genre. I wish "traditional" wrestling games were more like this.

I bought Slammed! when it originally released as one of my first COGs, and have played it many times since then, so I’m happy to see it finally get its day in the spotlight here on steam, as it is one of the best titles they have published to date.

I know a lot of you probably aren’t wrasslin’ fans, and you don’t have to be. If you enjoy interactive fiction, you’ll still find a great story with interesting characters and a TON of character choice and different routes for replayability.

For those of you that are or have been fans, however, you have the added bonus of enjoying loads of in-jokes and references that will make you smile – maybe even a few laugh out loud moments too – and have fun matching the game’s characters to the people they are actually based on. It’s always nice when a writer has such a grasp on and love for the material he is writing as the game’s quality can only be strengthened by it. Not only is it a great read, but it is a fictionalized walk through memory lane. (It is kind of sad to play as a female wrestler, though, as the fictional divas division is certainly better handled than the real one :p )

Tldr; Slammed is a lengthy CYOA of rising to fame in the GWA. There are many ways to make it, and more than a few ways to fail; do your skills have what it takes to make you the next HBK or Stone Cold?
