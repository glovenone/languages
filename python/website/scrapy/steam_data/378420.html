About This Game

Jurassic Island: The Dinosaur Zoo

Come and build your own dinosaur world with this unique Jurassic Island Zoo game! Operate a successful dinosaur park by attracting many visitors. Join the community and build your own creation!

It takes only 2 minutes to build a Zoo: http://youtu.be/NKEDTb0mOV8

Create your own unique tour with different stations so visitors can observe the dinosaurs. The more likes you collect the more funds you accumulate (a heads up: locating the dinosaurs drinking and feeding bowls close to the stations is key). With your extra earned cash, expand the park with further services and amenities for visitors, such as gift shops, hotels, restaurants, while you are also in charge of securing food and water supplies, electricity and a necessary security system. A volcano nearby and unpredictable weather are additional challenges to manage your park successfully.

And donât forget: dinosaurs can escape and chase visitors! So make sure to build safe heavens and provide swift assistance and evacuation for the visitors to safely operate your park.

Of course the happiness and well-being of the dinosaurs are of equal importance. Build them comfortable, clean enclosures with many plants, rocks, and lots of food and drinking spots. No visitor wants to see a sick dinosaur! Did a Dino escape? No worries, you will have a variety of tools at your disposal to catch them and to restore the operation of your park.

Early Access Review
Posted: 14 November, 2015
The artistic style of this game reminds me of the Dinosaur Digs expansion for Zoo Tycoon 1. A simple, pleasing 2d display and lovely lush foliage.
In it's current form, I cannot recommend this game to people not willing to navigate clunky controls and the occasional bug or glitch, but I do see a lot of potential in this game if it can sort out the weird method of bulding fences and paths, and have a decent tutorial. I have simply had to restart several times, trying out things at random to figure out the basics of gameplay, and I still find myself going broke pretty quickly.
That being said, I find the aethetics of the game charming and would like to see it developed further, and do not regret the small amount of money spent on it. I hope to see a fully-fledged version of the game someday with decent controls and maybe more dinosaur species, as well as additional building options.
On balance, I can't recommend this title as of yet, however I will be more than willing to change my tune as the game improves.

Early Access Review
Posted: 16 June, 2015
Jurassic Island: A Dinosaur Zoo is currently in the Steam Early Access program and it's a tycoon style sim game where you must build and maintain your very own dinosaur theme park (Jurassic World anyone?). It currently has an IndieGoGo campaign going, which has nine days left. Doesn't seem like it's gonna get there.

https://youtu.be/RhIfLolVQUk

First thing I want to point out is that this is another mobile port of a game to hit the Steam Early Access landscape. Not that this is a bad thing, but Jurassic Island does exist on mobile platforms for 99c, which when compared to the $5.99 price tag on Steam can be a bit hard to justify the price increase. Also consider that I haven't played the mobile version, so where a game like Minimon is trying to separate itself from its mobile counterpart, I'm not quite sure what the differences, if any, are.

It also seems the developer has made other games using this format of top town sim with a pair of games, The Terminal and The Terminal 2. So in essence this may just be a reskinned version of those games. Currently 'The Terminal 2' sits at a paltry 24% positivity rating on Steam and was released August of 2014 and to not much surprise Jurassic Island seems to be heading in the same direction with a 33%.

During my first look I went searching for a tutorial to get an idea of what we were dealing with and hidden in the settings menu I found "Game Tutorial" which promptly took me to a 2 minute video on YouTube. This isn't an effective way to learn how to play a game. So this didn't start off as a positive experience.

My overall experience can be boiled down into one word, broken. With no tutorial everything is a confusing mess. I was unsure of what to do or how to do it. I gave up on the first look when I hit a point and the game asked me to build an "Egg Mine" and it just wouldn't let me place it, anywhere. I tried to build some roads and the pathing system is impossible to direct because it was made for a touch screen, the same goes for the electric fences to keep your dinosaurs confined. Did I say dinosaurs with an 'S' as in plural, I meant dinosaur. I was only able to hatch one, because I couldn't figure out how to hatch another one, or how to get more eggs.

Overall, Jurassic Island: A Dinosaur Zoo is a broken port of a mobile game, if it worked there may be something here. That's not the case and it seems its bound for extinction(rimshot).
