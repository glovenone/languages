2 July

Testing Branch Updated - 1.3.1a1


Hey everyone! The testing branch has just been updated to Version 1.3.1a1. Some notable additions include:

  • Difficulty selection moved under Start Game instead of in the Options menu
  • The ball now pauses for a split second when powerups are hit, in an effort to ease up some of the visual noise
  • Night mode improvements for background paintings
  • Lowered difficulty of Corestar Easy by removing corner gray blocks
  • Removed requirement for Controller to be attached to make the Input Type label change in Options menu
  • Fixed a bug where having Checkerbox selected would load Mirrobox levels, and vice versa
What we particularly need feedback on is the Ball Pause. Please let us know how this feels, whether you love it or hate it, if maybe the pause should be even longer (right now the ball will start moving as soon as all powerup effects are over) etc. There will be one or two other additions for cleaning up visual noise by the full 1.3.1 release.

Thanks and have fun!

9 May

Version 1.3 Released - Bring Your Quarters [Weeklong Sale - 25% Off!]



Prepare, one and all, for Arcade Mode! In this brand new game mode, you'll have 3 lives to get through 20 rounds, collecting gems as you go. If you run out of lives, you can spend 25 gems to restock. However, you're competing for glory on the Steam Leaderboards, so losing that many gems may also mean losing first place! There are separate leaderboards for Relaxing, Easy, Medium, and Hard difficulties, and each difficulty also restocks a different amount of lives for 25 gems (Relaxing & Easy = 3 lives, Medium = 2 lives, Hard = 1 life). Also there is a Steam Achievement for beating Arcade Mode without losing a life. Have fun with that one.

We didn't have time to implement Local Leaderboards yet, but you can expect them at some point within the next couple of minor updates (1.3.1 or 1.3.2).

There are also some other new features, like the Gem Paddle, which expands every time you collect a gem and shrinks the rest of the time; and the Gem Shield, which makes gems bounce against the wall instead of flying through. Also, you asked for Premium Boutique unlocks and you got them. Levels can now be filled with beautiful, Impressionistic background art, which can be unlocked in the Boutique for 250 gems apiece! There are 9 overall, which means you'll need 2250 gems to get every single one (we know this won't be a problem for some of you Gem Hunt connoisseurs). If you're running the game on a lower-end machine, you can reduce the size of backgrounds or turn them off entirely to improve performance.

For anyone playing on an Xbox One Controller, Dualshock 4, or other equally advanced controller on Windows 10, there is now a setting to modify how the game reads trigger states, which will prevent the endless paddle spinning bug. Since the game does not auto-detect your controller (we could but we'd be updating the game until the end of time by detecting new controller names) head into the gameplay options and set the input type to "Advanced Controller." If you don't have a controller plugged in, you don't need to do anything here!

We are also happy to annoucne the release of the Mondrian Shareware Demo! For anyone who has not yet picked up the game, this demo will give you a sense of what the game is all about. It's completely free, though content is greatly limited from the full version. The demo is available here on Steam, as well as on IndieGameStand, itch.io, and we'll be uploading it to other sites as well. We encourage you to share this demo with your friends, family, and even strangers however you'd like.

There's plenty more to check out in this update, so read through the patch list and then jump in and enjoy our favorite build of Mondrian - Abstraction in Beauty to date!

Features:

  • Backgrounds! Each of our Mondrian-inspired images is available as an in-game background for the Plastic pack, as well as some brand new ones. Each background also has variants to match emulators & color schemes. Backgrounds are available at the premium price of 250 gems apiece
  • Arcade Mode! In this mode, you have 3 lives to clear 20 levels. If you clear all 20 levels or lose all 3 lives, your score is recorded on the Steam Leaderboard corresponding to your currently selected difficulty!
  • Gem Paddle! This paddle shrinks while you're not collecting gems, but grows when you do! Unlock it for 48 gems
  • Gem Shield! This powerup makes gems bounce off the wall for a short time, instead of flying through it. Unlock it for 42 gems
  • Background Quality Option: Useful for the new backgrounds as well as ones in Benefactor packs, this option allows you to set backgrounds to 100%, 75%, 50%, or 25% resolution. Lower-rez backgrounds can improve performance on lower-end systems
  • Background Toggle Option: Don't want the backgrounds on at all? No problem! You can toggle backgrounds off entirely, giving the game its classic, minimalist feel throughout. This is also a great option for improving performance as it clears the background from RAM entirely
  • Added some more tooltips to the Gem Chest screen
  • Added Controller Type input setup under Gameplay Options. Switching between Normal + Advanced will allow you to set the game to read specific functionality in regards to a controller's triggers. If you do not have a controller attached, this button will be set to a "Don't Worry About This" mode. This is a more in-depth attempt to solve the "Constantly Moving Left" issue with more advanced controllers like the Xbox One controller, which have to do with Z-Axis values as opposed to Z-Rotation values. Advanced Controller mode will read the both the current Z-Axis and Z-Rotation Value, while Normal Controller mode will only read the current Z-Axis Value. If your paddle is spinning uncontrollably, try switching to the Advanced mode.

About This Game

Mondrian - Abstraction in Beauty is a full-circle block-breaking game about video game art history. Inspired by the works of the De Stijl masters’ limitations on form and color, Mondrian - Abstraction in Beauty takes an abstract look at the history of video game art, through unlockable features like paddles, balls, powerups, and screen effects. The game is dynamically generated, with millions of level combinations and endless replayability.

Features:

  • Break out of the boring, vertical-only block breaking experience with 360 degree freedom of motion.
  • Over 48,000,000 possible combinations in level generation
  • Collect gems to unlock goodies like paddles, balls, powerups, wall shapes, visual styles and more.
  • Level Packs and Museum Galleries based on other games for an in-depth look at video game art history.
  • Play the game your way with adjustable gameplay options, level packs, and screen effects.
  • Inspirational quotes by Piet Mondrian

Soundtrack

Mondrian - Abstraction in Beauty: Original Game Soundtrack, composed by Davidjohn Blodgett and Hannah Hoyt, features nearly an hour of acid jazz, hip hop, trip hop, ambient, electronic, and classical music. Remastered from in-game tracks and exported as 320kbps, 44.1khz MP3’s, this stunning collection of music is bound to keep you immersed in Mondrian’s geometric world at work, at home, at a party, or on the go.

Recommend! ːthumbsupː
If you are a fan of breakout games you should give this a go.

Mondrian (in my opinion) adds a unique spin and challenges to the genre. There are 3 different game modes (Endless, Gem Hunt, Arcade) to switch things up, as well as 4 difficulty settings, and 3 speeds.

And the options don't end there. Instead of just swatting the ball around and picking up buffs/debuffs as they drop into a vertical path you spin in a circle and try to collect 'gems' as they break free from the shapes you destroy. But don't forget to keep an eye on your ball or it might be game over.

So many choices.
The gems are used to buy different things to reduce or add to the challenge and many visual styles to keep your retinas interested.
  • There are 9 wall shapes/frames and 6 different rotations to pick up. Some of the frames rotate and drop multiple walls instead of the typical fixed bottom so it can become quite a bit more challenging than the standard breakout game.

  • 3 paddles, 2 ball types and 4 powerups. If you think it as simple as letting the ball bounce around inside the frame, the Breakerball, while it will plow through the whole board, will implode if your paddle doesn't make contact after its destroyed 34* amount of bricks/shapes.
    *Check the developer comment below for more detail.

  • 12 different brick/shape patterns to be loaded at random with 11 color choices for those shapes. And then 9 artistic backgrounds.
And in the end, don't worry if you are bad. Every 5 minutes you can click on the Facebook & Twitter button on the main screen to get 50 free gems. So even the worst player can eventually get some additional goodies.


Developer props
The developer has also been very responsive, active and listens to the feedback from the players. After an update that resulted in broken achievements he worked at pushing several hot fixes trying to get it working correctly as quick as possible.
