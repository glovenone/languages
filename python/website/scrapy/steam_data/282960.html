Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to take this opportunity to grow a community of players while developing the game in a sustainable manner. Critique,criticism and community are vital to our development process and improving your play experience. Early Access allows us this opportunity.

Atajrubah is still in development and as such there are missing features, content and plenty of bugs. These issues will be addressed with subsequent,frequent updates and guidance from player feedback. We want to hear from you !”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We are planning to exit early access after a 12 - 18 month period.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version of Atajrubah is planned to be a cross-platform multiplayer survival and exploration experience featuring :

- Balanced Melee and Projectile Combat mechanics.

- Player Mounts

- A fully fleshed out Story with Quests and Missions for players to embark on alone or with friends.

- NPC characters that inhabit the world of Atajrubah.

- Server Persistent Player-built structures.

- Player Customization.

- Player-Player , Player-NPC Trading.

-Resource collection through:
  • Farming.
  • Hunting.
  • Skinning.
  • Fishing

Cross Platform availability for:
  • Windows.
  • Mac.
  • Linux.

- Oculus Rift compatability.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“At present Atajrubah is still very rough, we have a core framework of mechanics in place. As such the game is in Alpha, it is functional but not feature complete.

- Survival Mechanics
Manage your Hunger,Thirst and Health by collecting Dates and Water to survive.

- Base Ghul AI Implementation.
Ghuls roam the world of Atajrubah, you may choose to engage them in combat or steer clear.

- Drag-Drop Inventory Management system with Equipable clothing and Armour.

- Base Melee Combat Mechanics.

- Base Crafting System. The list of recipes is limited at present but will grow.

- Base Day/Night Implementation.

- Base Player Persistence Implementation. Your location,stats and inventory are saved periodically and populated upon reload. In case of death the player's inventory is populated with a default load-out.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, the price will differ but will remain within reason.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We plan on engaging with the community through the Steam Forums and our own. Providing updated builds every 2-4 weeks depending on progress. Players will be able to report bugs, vote on feature prioritization and make feature proposals that may ultimately end up in the game. We also plan on providing experimental builds of the game with untested features.”

About This Game

Atajrubah is an Arabian inspired Survival game.The title is an Arabic word that means (an) 'Experience'. The game is set in a 1930's to 1940's - esque era and is strongly influenced by Arabian Folklore and Architecture. The world is open for the player to explore either on their own or with their friends. The game focuses on exploration, player-world and player-player interactions.

Early Access Review
Posted: 24 December, 2014
Atajrubah has a lot of promise but a ways yet to go.

The visuals are rough but still quite pleasant to look at. There's a bit of distortion around the edges of the models (notably trees) and the characters move in awkward ways at times.

The inventory and crafting menus are...primitive and not terribly helpful. There's an unexplained "crotch" slot that I'm wierdly curious to see what eventually goes there. I'm guessing it's a belt but stranger things have happened in games.

There are no crafting recipes as of yet and while the basic process of playing the game is relatively familiar to people who've played survival games, there doesn't seem to be a whole lot to actually do yet. I do like survival games and the Arabian theme is new in terms of the survival genre.

The game does snarl the video drivers and crash rather regularly on my machine. This is not a point in its favor but I'll withold outright hate because I don't know if this is a problem exclusive to my rig or not.

Atajrubah could be a really solid addition to the survival genre if it follows through on full release with the promise it's showing in the open beta and fixes some of the more glaring issues.

A couple of things that *must* be fixed before launch:

- Day/night cycles are somewhat erratic. Sometimes at night it's like someone just turned out the lights and there's no indicated way to make fire or light of any kind. I did respawn with a torch a couple of times but there was no indication of how to light it so I just stumbled around in the dark. The shadows also move incredibly fast as the sun moves across the sky, faster than your character can actually run. This isn't major but it is definitely disorienting and visually odd.

- The game does not pause when you open the menu. This is a pretty serious problem for obvious reasons.

- Items do not stack and that's a pretty crippling flaw when one arrow takes up one inventory slot. I'm willing to bet this will be fixed later on but for now it's not a pleasant bug.

- The crashing does not add to the experience.
