About This Game

Ocean City Racing is an open world indie driving game powered by Unreal Engine 3. OCR features the biggest, most detailed and alive open world city , advanced driving physics and highest quality visuals ever seen in a low price indie driving game.

Featuring a free roam , time-trial and a race mode on a massive environment , Ocean City Racing offers hours of entertainment for only $2.99.

KEY FEATURES
• Unreal Engine 3: Powered by the industry leading game engine Unreal Engine 3 , Ocean City Racing offers a massive open world city with a traffic and a pedestrian system , a day & night cycle, dynamic, real-time lighting and shadows, also different weather conditions such as rain,fog and sun.

• Advanced Driving Physics: Ocean City features the most advanced driving physics ever seen in an indie game.

• Open World City and Environment: Apart from the city , Ocean City Racing also features an island which is connected to the city with a bridge and a highway , a tunnel that goes under the Ocean City mountains through another town and an subway system under the city which provides a huge area for the players to discover.

• Free Roam Mode: In the ‘Free Roam’ mode players can cruise around the city either with the vehicles that are scattered around the map , or they can explore the environment by exiting the car with a first person view. Free Roam mode has a lot of secrets to find. You can find and drive a hidden kart and even drive a quadbike and cruise around the city.

• Race Mode: Become the best driver in Ocean City and satisfy your need for speed by completing this mode. Players will proceed by completing events in the first place and unlock new vehicles as they progress.

• Time Trial Mode: In the time trial mode players have the ability to choose one of the many tracks available and try to achieve the fastest time by going through the checkpoint flares and reach the car event location.

• Vehicles: Features over 20 highly detailed vehicles to drive. Ranging from sports cars , muscle cars , super cars , off road vehicles. Even a kart and a quad bike can be driven.

• Progression: Ocean City Racing allows players to progress through time trials and circuit races and allow them to unlock new cars and events on the way.

• Soundtrack: Features songs of Christopher Amott from his 'Impulses' and 'Follow Your Heart' albums , as well as other instrumental and orchestral songs that fit the atmosphere of the game.

It's like whoever modeled this game thought "we don't need pedestrians that resemble humans, but we have to make sure they're wearing meme t-shirts. That is the most important thing." Either that, or everyone was too focusing on delivering the ultimate racing experience. I'm guessing it was the latter.

....
....Alright I've had enough fun.

Truth is, this isn't a good racing game. It's definitely incomplete, should probably spend several months in the oven, and level 3 needs far more than just its graphics tightened up. I had fun with it though, more fun than I've had with numerous polished videogames. I'm a big fan of open-world driving games like Burnout Paradise, Test Drive Unlimited, and the Crash Time games. I'm actually worried that eventually the developers of this game will fix it. It'll be complete, well-designed, and yet, something will be lost. I like making all the weird discoveries, such as finding the burger king ads, pedestrians more disturbing and with creepier animations than the last, and new ways to flip my car. Eventually all that might get taken away, and I'll be sad.

Not a good game, but it's fun, and that's all that matters?

Ocean City Racing is an indie racing game, made in the UT3 engine.

The game feels and plays like an old arcade race game made by SEGA. Whenever you crash into other cars or buildings you car stays intact. You could destroy things like small fences but whenever you ride into a pedestrian your car just stops. I know this is no carmageddon but perhaps there should not have been npc's in a game like this. The cars also have some unnatural physics. It would have been nice if you could play the game with a controller but mine did not seem to work.

There are 3 game modes: Time-Trial, Race Mode and Free Roam. Obviously, Time-Trial is to beat your own times, Race-Mode is just racing opponents and Free-roam is driving around an 'open world' city.

The graphics are not that special these days but they are not that bad. The city looks OK but I doubt anyone plays this game for sight-seeing.
Although not my taste, the music is pretty good however, totally not suited for a race game, in my opinion.

Without any achievements, online leaderboards or online/offline multiplayer, the game will most likely bore you after max 1 hour. Even though the game is cheap, I still can't recommend this one.

Oh boy. I just keep finding ways to get impressed. At a retail price of $3, Ocean City Racing (well, OCEAN CITY RACING, but I think you'll forgive me if drop the caps) is a pretty cheap buy, but as a lover of racing games I'll give almost anything a try. It doesn't always go well, like Redneck Racing or Motorama, and Ocean City Racing is stacking up to be another mistake languishing in my library.

The game already fails on a technical level with a barebones options menu, offering an extremely limited resolution choice—not even offering my native resolution of 1600x900, the third most common resolution by Steam's hardware survey—no graphics options, no volume sliders, no rebindable controls...it's just a mess. And the game runs terribly and looks worse, with a massively overdone motion blur to boot that makes it more or less impossible to see what you're doing. Of course, as mentioned, there is no way to turn this off. Additionally, all the button prompts given are for keyboard controls, so anyone who wants to play with a controller will find themselves randomly pressing buttons to try to figure things out if they didn't memorize the controls from the options menu.

The car handling is utter garbage. You have very little control and will find yourself bumping off walls left and right. Conveniently there's a reset car button that doesn't seem to have much penalty, so I think this was expected. The handbrake button has been replaced by a spin out button, leading to further resets. The boost fails to give any real feeling of speed, but nonetheless makes the car all the more likely to go out of control in corners, adding yet more usefulness to the reset button unless you save it for straights. And of course there's the random physics bugs where a tap against a wall can send you flying into the air. And despite all this bumping around and lost time, the computers have it even worse; I watched them crash on their own all over the place. They don't know how to play this game any better than I do! I had an atrocious time but still somehow came in second in my first race.

So, after the disaster of a race, I decided I would try free roam mode. You walk around in a first-person camera and can't interact with anything but the cars. You drive around a little more freely which leads to a little bit less bumping around, but the driving still feels terrible, the game still looks like someone dumped Vaseline on it, you still randomly get launched into the air, and there doesn't seem to really be any point to doing any of this.

It was at this point I realized I had about all I could take of this game and decided to try collecting my thoughts together into words. That really is all there is to it: nothing in this game inspires me to play it, and on both technical and design fronts it actively discourages me from playing. There was not one moment when playing this game that I thought to myself "I had fun," and for that I cannot recommend this game for any reason.
