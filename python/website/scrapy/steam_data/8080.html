About This Game

Kane and Lynch: Dead Men is the new action shooter franchise by Io-Interactive, creators of the multi-million selling Hitman series. Kane & Lynch: Dead Men follows the violent and chaotic journey of two men - a flawed mercenary and a medicated psychopath - and their brutal attitude towards right and wrong. This volatile partnership combined with innovative technologies allow for an unparalleled gaming experience.
Kane & Lynch: Dead Men immerses players in an emotionally intense crime drama with unflinching action. Io-Interactive's proprietary technology delivers fluid high definition graphics and lighting; highly destructible environments; massive crowd AI; single, co-op, and squad-based combat; a cinematic interface; and non-stop dialog between Kane and Lynch. The title also features innovative two-player co-op and groundbreaking online game play.
  • Tense and unpredictable experience - This is the violent and chaotic journey of two men: a flawed mercenary and a medicated psychopath. Each hates the other but must work together to save themselves and possibly the ones they love. Play Kane in single-player mode and either character in two-player co-op mode.
  • Violence erupts in meticulously created 'safe' worlds - Realistic and responsive environments and crowds react when events get of out control. Crowded nightclubs and calm bank offices become scenes of sudden terror. Manipulate the environments and crowds strategically to complete objectives.
  • Wide variety of intense 3rd person game play - Rappel from buildings; fire on the run or from moving vehicles; launch surprise attacks, lay down cover fire, and shoot blind from around corners; and hurl smoke, tear, and incendiary grenades.
  • Fast and intuitive combat - Lead a crew of criminal mercenaries to increase your chance of surviving and reaching your goals. You can also fight alone as your crew AI will automatically draw fire, throw grenades, lay down cover, and engage in close combat with their own signature moves.
  • Two-player co-operative game play - Team up and play the full story with a friend as either Kane or Lynch. On the big jobs, split the command of your crew. At any point in the game you can choose to play the next, or previous, levels in co-op.
  • Innovative multi-player levels based on the game's themes for up to eight players.

This is such an underrated game.

The characters are what really sold me on this game. Unlike other series in which you play as "bad guys" who ultimately tend to do the right thing, Kane and Lynch are 100% scumbags. Kane is a self-serving, traitorous sociopath and Lynch is just completely mental. It's a real treat to see how they interact with each other and how their personalities affect the storyline.

The combat is good, not great. The cover system is a little wonky and the AI is outright terrible at times, but I was always having enough fun that these issues didn't bother me much. Shooting feels off at first, but as you progress and get a feel for the weapons, it becomes a total joy. You really get the feel of desperation that would accompany a heist gone wrong leading into a frantic shootout with the cops (like I would know). Kudos to whoever did the sound for the weapons, I haven't heard beefier sounding assault rifles in any other game!

The graphics are pretty mediocre, and look like what you'd expect from a non triple-A title in 2007. That being said, the levels you play in are more than interesting enough to make up for it. You'll go from banks and upscale office buildings to the streets and rooftops of Tokyo to Havana in the middle of a political revolution. One of my favorite set pieces was a gunfight in a busy nightclub, which seemed to be lifted straight out of the movie "Collateral."

Overall, this is a solid game with a few flaws that stand out, but definitely won't ruin the experience of playing as such interesting characters in such interesting situations. And for only $6.99 (down from $60 when it came out) you can't go wrong giving it a try!

The Game:

Kane & Lynch: Dead Men is a dark and gritty drama featuring two men, one is a flawed mercenary and the other a medicated psychopath. Forced together on a violent and chaotic path of redemption and revenge, no action is considered too harsh as this volatile partnership is pushed beyond reason in this brutally realistic, character-driven third person action shooter.

The Bad:

- Accuracy is very bad
- Voice Acting will sometimes overlap and/or glitch out
- A.I. partners feel awkward
- The Ending left something to be desired for

The Good:

- Fun commentary from Kane & Lynch
- Lots of exciting levels
- Feels healthy when compared to other games made in this generation
- Story is well executed

The Review:

Kane & Lynch easily earns its mature rating, with ferocious, bloody attacks and an excessive use of swear words. Essentially a third-person shooter with some simple squad commands, it is an uninspired, frustrating action experience.

For example, to jump into cover, you simply run up to an obstacle. Sounds easy enough, except you have to run into a wall or obstacle a couple times before you "click" into cover.

Targeting is just as irritating. Gunshots seem a hair off almost every time. Computer-controlled players prefer to play hide-and-seek rather than to engage in heated battles. Kane & Lynch showed promise with a strong story, but after a few levels it turns up dead on arrival. Overall, however, the game is an enjoyable piece of software that will keep you entertained in its sub-par length game of approx. 5 hours.

This game is an odd one. Every single criticism that you've heard about this game just might be valid, but that still doesn't hinder my recommendation of this game.

Yes, the recoil on the guns is absurd. This isn't a problem, because bullets are much more damaging when they actually hit what you want them to.

Yes, the graphics are dated, but it's early 360/PS3 graphics, so that makes more sense than it would otherwise.

Yes, the characters are all but unlikable. However, this seems to be intentional, as IO interactive stated in an interview before release that you shouldn't be trying to do so. Think about movies like The Departed. There's nobody to root for, not even the titular protagonists. Fortunately, the further you progress, the more that Kane and Lynch ♥♥♥♥ up. The characters, not the game.

Yes, the story is confusing, but it's only confusing if you don't bother to put the puzzle pieces together and try to figure out the facts from what little information. It's like the story in Dark Souls in that way. The only difference is that this is fresh territory that nobody has bothered to theorycraft yet, so go nuts blazing a trail that nobody's blazed before.

Yes, I had some problems with Games for Windows Live, but i was able to install the program for it and log in with my Microsoft Account that i use to login to Windows 10. You even get achievements for Xbox for playing with it on. It's really not much of a hassle when playing this game with it.
