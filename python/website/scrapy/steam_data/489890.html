About This Game

Puzzles At Mystery Manor is a jigsaw puzzle game, in which your task is to unravel the puzzles in the haunted manor of Jack Jasper, a madman, genius and murderer, who killed 40 people and captured their tortured souls in 40 paintings and hid them throughout the house.

In this exhilarating combination of terror and jigsaw puzzles, you travel throughout the manor helping solve puzzles related to the grisly murders he committed. Each broken picture unveils where the lunatic laid each of his victims to rest... Can you make it through the manor and survive to tell the tale?

Posted: 25 June
I picked this up during the latest Steam sale. 40 jigsaw puzzles for $1.01 seemed like a pretty good deal and it was. It took me a little over four hours to get through the game and it was a nice casual game. There isn't a lot of substance to it. You are essentially on rails, going from picture frame to picture frame where you put jigsaw puzzles together. While each puzzle is different, each one features a skull in some prominent fashion.

After you finish each puzzle you are told how long it took you and how many moves you took. It's very straightforward and if you like jigsaw puzzles, this is a really nice game. I should warn you that the story is non-existant. When you start you are given a letter by the last person who entered (and died) in the house but that's it. When you beat the game, you just get a "the end" screen. Nothing more, nothing less. At the same time, besides the fourty jigsaw puzzles, you fill find various books that you can click on, each one containing a story or poem by Edgar Allen Poe in its entirity. These are cute, but more than likely you've read or even own a lot of these already and there is no real point to reading them in game. If you do, that would definitely increase the length of time you spend with the game, but like most people, I'd rather read a book outside of a video game.

All in all, I DEFENITELY got my money's worth out of this. In a perfect world, I'd have liked a story based ending but other than that I wanted something a bit soothing and calming in the form of jigsaw puzzles and that's exactly what the game gave me. I realize it sounds a bit weird to say that a game based on a house where a serial killer murdered a bunch of people, but it oddly is. For a dollar, this is well worth picking up if you're a fan of jigsaw puzzle or the type of games you find over at Big Fish Games.
