About This Game

Matris is an easy to play but challenging logic puzzle game for everyone - casual and hardcore gamers.
Relax in Puzzle Mode or fight for the High Score in Combo Mode and Time Attack.
Play hundreds of levels or create your own thanks to Level Editor.

Two main Game Modes are Puzzle Mode and Combo Mode.



  • Arrange puzzle pieces on the board in such a way as to cover as much of the board as possible.
  • If in trouble use Lifelines for help - Reshuffle the whole pieces queue, Choose the current piece, Skip current piece, Undo last move.
  • No time limits. Relax.



  • Place the pieces on the board so they create a chain - Combo.
  • The longer the Combo the bigger the Score and Multiplier for that combo.
  • Align the pieces in lines of for extra points.
  • Choose from 20 different Bonuses. Beware that not all bonuses are good so choose wisely.



Additional Game Modes are Time Attack and Free Play.

  • In Time attack rules are similar to Combo Mode but there is a Time Limit, no Bonuses and never ending blocks on the board.
  • Free Play is exactly like Combo Mode but there is no Score Goal so you can play as long as there are blocks on the board.

Play Time Attack and Free Play modes for the highest Leaderboards score.



Matris includes 20 stock levels which can be played in any Game Mode.
It also includes Level Editor so you can play Custom Levels or create your own.



Choose from 96 different Themes and 10 different Color Palettes or create your own Custom Color Palettes.



Full support for mouse, keyboard or gamepad controls.



  • Four game modes - Puzzle Mode, Combo Mode, Time Attack and Free Play
  • 20 stock levels
  • Level Editor and custom levels
  • 25 Achievements
  • 96 themes to choose. Thousands of theme combinations
  • 10 color schemes
  • 2 user customizable color schemes
  • Fully customizable mouse, keyboard and gamepad controls

I'll be honest: My interest in this game is based purely on the soundtrack by HOME. I also happen to love the occasional puzzler. Long story short? The most talented of artists don't always sign off their music to quality products

Matris' gameplay could be best described as... the painters version of Tetris? Although I'd more say it was the Duplo or Mega Bloks of Tetris games, but even with that comparison, I think in some ways that's giving this game more credit than it deserves, as it would justify why it was made. Matris lacks that certain raison d'être, both in design and its gameplay. You kind of just stick puzzle pieces into place, and that's really it. None of the other game modes take it in an interesting direction either, as you lack that reason to care about your placement like in real Tetris when you fear your blocks touching the top of the screen. You have all day, really, and you could think out your moves precisely, and that's just not exciting.

Classic puzzle games like Tetris or Columns are about a constant assault on the player. How will you deal with what the game throws your way next? Matris (like a mattress, as in, the game will put you to sleep because it's so boring) isn't interested in asking you that question. I don't even think it's even that interested in itself with its colorless, grey and plain design. Yes I know I can customize it myself, but it says a lot when not even the designers thought fit to make the presentation eye catching for people that you know, might REFUND the game, cause you could get everything you need within five minutes.

The soundtrack by HOME, while great, just doesn't fit. The gameplay is too slow. Even in time attack - where you're given a way too generous (I'd actually call it laborious) 10 minute limit - never do I ever feel compelled to race the clock. It'd be like if Jeopardy had the Duke Nukem theme playing throughout the entirety of the show. Does it make Jeopardy more exciting? No. It'd still be the same old sassy molassy Trebek askin' questions. HOME's music deserves a better home in a moderately faster paced game.

The game reeks of mobile phone game design, where if you give the game your undivided attention, you get nothing in return, because it's just not that taxing to play. It needed something to drive the gameplay, but maybe like myself, the devs were too excited about licensed music and not the actual content of the game.

The Pro's
+Great soundtrack regardless of the game

+Good customization

The Con's
-Boring. Flat out boring

-Dull color scheme you have to fix yourself

-HOME's soundtrack goes to waste

I do highly recommend checking out the Columns clone, Anode. It will definitely scratch your itch if you're looking for a puzzle game. It has the tight gameplay and the killer soundtrack to back it.

And just when I thought that there ain't no puzzle game/arcade game that could teach the old dog some new tricks...

Matris is a dynamic puzzle game that bears resemblance to Lumines and Tetris. The player task is to fill the map/matrix with tretromino-like blocks, provided by the AI. What sounds easy to learn, seems difficult to master - and that learning curve is the main motor for neverending fun of Matris. Lots of different modifications and built-in level editor asures the player that no matter how much he'd play, there's still an awful lot to discover.

Definitely recomended for all Lumines and Tetris enthusiasts, but also everybody that likes to challenge themselves!
