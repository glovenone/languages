About This Game

Chaos has erupted throughout the galaxy. As leader of an elite squad of Republic Commandos, your mission is to infiltrate, dominate, and ultimately, annihilate the enemy. Your squad will follow your orders and your lead, working together as a team - instinctively, intelligently, instantly. You are their leader. They are your weapon.
  • Innovative Squad Control System - With intuitive and smart squad commands, the simple touch of one button easily controls your squad to perform complex commands and strategic maneuvers.
  • Multiple Gaming Mode - Choose the single-player option and command a squad of four that you can dispatch at will. Or, choose the multiplayer option and play with up to sixteen players online in different multi-player modes.
  • Prelude to Episode III - Encounter new vehicles, locations and enemies from the upcoming film.

System Requirements

    • OS: Windows 2000, XP or Vista
    • Processor: Pentium III or Athlon 1.0 GHz or faster CPU
    • Memory: 256MB RAM
    • Graphics: 64MB 3D Graphics card with Vertex Shader and Pixel Shader (VS/PS) capability
    • DirectX®: 9.0b
    • Hard Drive: 2.0GB
    • Sound: 100% Directx 9.0c

Star Wars Republic Commando is such a unique and addicting game that I find it may even rival the Battlefront series.
Basically you are the squad leader of a 4 person squad.
Delta 40
Delta 62
Delta 07
And you, Delta 38. Together, you form the Delta Squad.

You are clone troopers that have been engineered to be the best of the best. You are the future. You are the commandos.
You must strategically command your squad in order to beat back the droid army on various missions across various planets. Something i'd like to mention is that the droids are very tough in this game, while in the movie they seemed like bumbling pieces of metal that could easily be taken down. Initially I started the game on normal but had to change it to easy because it was too hard. I may re-play the game on medium though. But even on easy, the separatists are a force to be reckoned with and the enemies are remarkably tough. This makes for thrilling gameplay as all the responsibility falls on your shoulders as the squad leader, and you must make hard decisions. In the end, only 2 things matter. Win the battle, and get your squad out alive. But later in the game, you must choose which is more important....

Each squad member has a unique personality, and as the game progresses you feel very close to them.

The members are:
Scorch, (62)
Fixer, (40)
Sev, (07)
And you, 38. Or, Boss.

You are alone, cut off, and all you have is your squad. You do whatever it takes to save them.

I'll never get tired of hearing:
Engage snipe maneuver, Delta.
Rearrange some architecture, commando.
I want some explosives here, Delta!
Stop limping around and get some bacta, soldier.
Engage anti armor position, delta!

Something i'd like to mention is that your character's full number is actually 1138. 38 is just for short. I think that's cool because THX-1138 was a screenplay George Lucas wrote in college. So that's a neat little Easter egg there.
And also, at the end of the game you can hear the x wing pilots from a New Hope saying things they said during the Death Star run. So that's another cool little Easter egg.

I also like how you can attack your squad, and they fight back! I tried killing my squad mates one time, and found myself fighting against them. I actually felt really bad. XD

This game is spectacular, extremely fun, unique, and a must-buy.
It was so much fun for me because I am a HUGE fan of the Clone Troopers. I just think they're really cool, and being able to not only be one, but be a commander throughout this game, was an incredible experience.
I would definitely, whole heartily recommend this game. The only thing I'm disappointed about is that there isn't more. I thought there'd be several more planets and missions leading up to Order 66. Oh well, maybe there will be a sequel in the future called Imperial Commando. (Just please don't have EA make it)

I give this game a 10/10.
The game is extremely fun with a great story, good voice actors, great controls, hard bosses that require strategy to take down, good weapons, great graphics, and a great atmosphere.

Would play again.

I liked this game, and keep wondering why there isn't a sequel out given *spoilers* *spoilers* *more spoilers*. I'm hardly a fan of the Star Wars prequels, but the characters and plot are interesting and solid. I especially love the first half of chapter 2, with its "haunted house" feel. I can even get over the fact that Scorch is voiced by Raphael Sbarge (that guy who will forever be Carth Onasi to me and who only has one acting style), given that all the squad's personalities are both clearly defined and show development across the plot-arc. For instance, I love how Fixer is incredibly straitlaced but sometimes the facade cracks, just a little.

I also don't play a whole lot of shooters, but I really enjoyed the feeling of being in charge of a squad that reacts to me without a whole lot of complicated keyboard commands to memorize. In fact, there's only one button to direct every terrain-contextualized action, and that's okay with me! A little odd that the boys will get infinite ammo of whatever type they're using while they're in a terrain position, but hey, Scorch lobbing unlimited grenades is also okay with me. The graphics are also fine for how old it is, and I like the little details like the visor windshield wiper for rain/blood/oil/scratches(!?). It was a little frustrating at times when running low on ammo or health and with no way to get more before having to survive difficult waves of enemies, but that's almost certainly because I'm not very good at shooters. :P

Overall, I really got the feeling that I was part of a team while playing this game, and it was really awesome.
