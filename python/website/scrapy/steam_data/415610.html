About This Game


Almost 17 years on, we bring you back the true Russian video game industry classics, remastered for modern platforms.

The first part of this classical game series will be released on PC on February, 5th . The game saves the original graphics and voicing but the game code has been completely rewritten for Unity engine and it has widescreen support now. Any animation issues and old bugs have been fixed. Steam achievements and trading cards are also available. So from now on you can play in Red Comrades Save the Galaxy using any modern PC working on one of modern operating systems!

About the game:

Russia is in a midst of Civil War. Both the front line and Ural River splits the village Backwoods in two. One part of the village is somehow controlled by the brave Red Army division headed by Chapaev. Remnants of the cowardly Whites are still comfortably stationed on the other side.
Vasily Ivanovich Chapaev and his fellow aide Petka wake up to find themselves in a bad hangover and in a worse mood. They realize that someone has stolen the Red banner from the HQ. Our heroes are totally depressed with such a misfortune, just like every decent Red army fighters should be.
This unbearable offense leaves them no choice but to sneak into the enemy territory and to retrieve the precious banner.
Although it's not the end of the story yet! Somewhere on the moon, legions of alien invaders prepare to conquer Earth. But could such a little thing really give a scare to the Red Army heroes?

I didn't know the existence of this gem. I'll be honest; I kinda found it because of the last Steam summer sale. I was browsing for cheap point-&-click games, since I'm saving money for a new desktop, and current using my old laptop from the year 2k04'ish.
So, after I've read the description of the game, and check the minimum system requirements, I knew I had to have it on my steam library.

...and what can I say about this Red Comrades Save the Galaxy: Reloaded?

It's a fun, irreverent point-&-click game full of crude humor, corny jokes, cheesy situations, sexual innuendos, and alot of Democratic socialist Vs Capitalist Vs Imperialist type of jokes mixed with some 80’s/90’s Eastern and Western pop culture references, and a large number of Rabinovich, Vasily Ivanovich type of characters. Since it’s a classic point-&-click game, the gameplay is “traditional” simple, you need to get some item to make some character satisfied, then that character will give you another item for you to combine with other item, that will make you able to get another item for other character, and so on. The story is equal simple, two stooges soldiers of the red guard found out their Bolshevik banner was stolen, and they need to find it before Lenin found out. On the way to find where the hell is the red banner, they got a huge help from their beloved neighbour Anka, a blonde comrade which is also a red army spy, working in Imperialist territory, in the process our two heroes found out their major problem isn’t the missing banner, but a grand alien invasion, and a conspiracy which may jeopordize the october revolution in the first place.

I recommend this game, if you like the Point-&-click genre, and want to play something different with some similarities with the game Leisure suit Larry. The animations are quite good, same for the environment, there’s a good “hint” system, alot of easter eggs, the music is simple and corny but quite enjoyable while you’re playing, and having the original russian audio is quite good.

The few cons I found, (ok, I never played the game outside the Steam), but I think the game needed more Steam achivements, there’s some situations in it crying out loud for an achievement, the other problem is some of the animations are quite long, specially in the Alien spaceship, where there’s a part with an escalator and we need to see the character climbing the slow escalator every frikkin’ time we want to go to the control panel, which could had a "Ground Control to Major Tom" achivement.

The other problem is how can I explain the game to my communist friends? Everytime I talk to them about the game they ask “why should I play it?” and my explanation quite doesn’t make any sense and that make me smile because Red Comrades: Save the Galaxy is a good old non-sensless point-&-click game.

and I definitely recommend it.

Yes, gameplay-wise, it is a classical point-and-click adventure game with a lot of good and difficult puzzles (and with excellent Russian voiceovers). But in regards to the story, it is like an epitome of platitude! There are stupid jokes, there are dirty jokes, and there is nudity and nakedness and then even more nudity. Summary:
Fun point & click game with some flaws, but totally worth the money. Can be completed in 7-8 hours.


Recommended for:
Players who can handle crude humor, some pixel nudity and for lovers of the point & click genre.

The good:
+ Interesting characters, world and storyline
+ Well animated
+ Voice acting is good (in Russian, adding to the atmosphere)
+ Good port to Unity with plenty of achievements and trade cards
+ Alt+tab works fine, and so does the Steam overlay (even in your native resolution, which is rare for old games!)


The bad:
- Too many random pauses between voice lines
- Inconsistent art at some points (some characters look like they were drawn in MS Paint, for example the woman at the train station)
- English translation is good, but has some errors in it (been typed as "ben", shall typed as "shell, etc)
- The hints are sometimes completely useless and confusing
- Game file compression: 400 MB download, but takes 3 GB of disk space, which is A LOT for a game from 1998
- No music in a lot of interior maps
- Some of the walking animations are long, tedious and unskippable.
- There's only autosave. While this works fine, you're at constant risk to ruin your progress if you click "new game" in the main menu by accident. This is a poor design decision.
- No actual tutorial telling you that you can quick travel using the map, explains interaction, ...


Gameplay: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tXof68TtA6Y (slightly cut, no voice comments)

Basic Information
Title: Red Comrades Save the Galaxy: Reloaded
Genre: Adventure
Developer: Buka Development
Publisher: Buka Entertainment
Release Date: 1 Nov, 1998

Overview
This is a very nicely done point & click game, the graphics are really fitting for the game play. There are so many puzzles and things to explore in this game that you won't really get bored of it. The game itself is very good, although there are some things that aren't explained at all that makes this game fall a little but none the less it's a very good game,

Game Play
This game is basically a point and click puzzle game with some very funny humour implemented into it as well. From what I have played so far it seems to be really fun, the puzzles are very nicely created. If you ever get stuck there is a nice help system that points you in the right direction so that you don't get stuck and so that you can progress with the storyline. This really helps you as some point & click games are way too hard with the puzzles that they have created, but this has very simple and easy puzzles to solve. There is a lot of things to look at and find in this game so exploring everything right from the start is very essential to this game. The humour that is used is very funny at times, trying to chat up other girls, and using a substitute as a scarecrow really got me in stitches. It doesn't over do the humour in a sense that it gets boring, it does it just enough to keep you laughing and smiling when a joke does happen. There are a few problems I have with this game though, and that is the controls for the game. There is absolutely no indication of what controls do what, for example I didn't realise that I had the suitcase with all my stuff in until about a hour and half into the game. You really need to add either a controls section in the settings menu, or add it at the start of the game. This also caused the problem as Vasily Ivanovich is sometimes in the way of the arrow so that you can exit out of the area. Such as the bridge that have the guards, if you didn't know that you can move Vasily Ivanovich then you might have some trouble finding the arrow as it's right under where he is standing. Another thing that I wasn't aware of while playing the game until later on was the fast travel system. If you have been to a area then you can go onto the map and click on that area to fast travel there. This would've been really helpful if I would've known that earlier.
Score (Out Of 10): 7 - nice game play, and well balanced puzzles. Need to add some way of telling the played what controls do what so that they can understand how to play more efficently.

Graphics/art style
The art style of this game is really nicely animated. It seems that there was a lot of time and dedication when it came to drawing out the backgrounds and the characters animations. I really don't really have anything to complain about this as it is just so well animated and drawn out. Although some of the art style seems to be a little blurry and don't fit in with the rest of them, but I guess that's just to help you with what you have to click on to explore and stuff.
Score (Out Of 10): 8 - Very nice drawn graphics, and animated characters. Some of the art style seems to be a bit blurry and doesn't really fit in with the rest.

Music & Sound
The music is very fitting with the atmosphere as you walk around the areas, helping people out and solving puzzles. I would've liked to have some music in the background of the houses, as the music only comes on when your exploring the main area. The sounds are exceptionally good, I really liked all of the sounds that where played when certain things happened to characters.
Score (Out Of 10): 9 - Really nice music and sounds, would've liked some music inside the houses.

Conclusion
This is defiantly a gem point & click game. It has some really nice puzzles that aren't too difficult to figure out, although I would've liked a small tutorial for what all the buttons are as I only figured out all the buttons after a hour and a half. The music and sounds are exceptionally good, and the art style is just superb. Along with the animations of the characters, I am really surprised of how well this game is for a drawn game. Would defiantly recommend this game to anyone.

Red Comrades Save the Galaxy: Reloaded is the remastered version of Red Comrades Save the Galaxy which was released in 1998. It's a point'n'click game with the main story happening in Russia during a Civil War, where our characters go in a recovery mission for the lost flag, and they end up saving the world, at least for the moment. The main protagonist are Vasily Ivanovich Chapaev(in the subtitle Chapaev won't be mentioned) and Pete(like this his name is translated...I think in the old game he was called Petka) and for a short period even Anka.

The graphics are the original ones. The soundtrack is fitting with the environment. The only downside(if you can count it a downside) is the lack of the English voice over,English being present only as a subtitle. For the controls we have the clasic one and a new, simplified casual mode. There is an inventory system which is accesed by right click in casual mode or by selecting it from the menu in the clasic mode. Also there is a hint system which can help when you are stuck, sometimes the hints be vague and that can prologue the time required to figure what you need to do next. There are a lot of jokes and references in the game.

The game is divided in three sequences. Each one happening in another zone. First sequence is in the Red Base, second one in the White Base, where around half of the sequence Anka is the protagonist(as a spy with the name Anna), and the third one in the alien base on the moon.

If you are a big fan of achievements/trading cards no worry. Red Comrades Save the Galaxy: Reloaded have them both.

Note - This game was reviewed using a key provided by the publisher.
