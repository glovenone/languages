What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Early Access will allow me to receive much needed player feedback to improve the game balance and have players involved in the process of helping me develop the game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“I'm going to release the game before the end of 2016.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The most important feature currently missing is the quests and storyline. Also, I'm going to add the multiplayer mode to the game.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The game is now very stable and highly playable, but there are some balance problems.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price at launch will be higher than it is now.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I'm hoping that this system will encourage consistent feedback from players as it doesn't require ever leaving the game and provides me with as much information as possible.”

Early Access Review
Posted: 1 May
Event Horizon is I would say a cross between Star Control and Space invaders/asteroid that I used to play when I was a kid, ts actually quite addictive!

You start off with a main vessel and 3 other ships, a scout ship, a 'thingy' (i have no idea what it was) and a cruiser type ship. Each have their own weapons systems and shield capabilities.

A quick menu jump to the star map and you get a feel for how big this game will be, there are tons and tons of stars to visit. The only problem is that every star you visit involves a fight. In the 40 odd minutes I played I had about 6 battles. These were with ships of a similar class to mine but some had upgrades. Travelling to new star systems and then again within that system to planets and stations is done by clicking the star/station/planet you want to visit.

In most systems there are at least a couple of planets but at this time the exploration of planets is not included so despite there being a menu option to explore them... you cant. TRUST ME I HAVE TRIED!

Battles take the form of asteroid style gameplay. Fire your weapon at the enemy and try to hit them to cause them damage. Most evenly matched ships took about 6 or so hits to kill, you can then collect parts and fuel and credits.
Overall I like the idea of the game, it will become repetitive and probably a little boring but ship upgrades and new systems to explore may keep you wanting more. For me its a good one to play whilst the wife watches crap on TV!

Pro's:
  • Runs well on a laptop!
  • Game has a tutorial (takes about 4 seconds to complete)
  • Huge skill/upgrade tree

    Cons
  • Repetitive gameplay
  • Non existent story
  • Battles quickly become hard

Very addictive and complex game.

I will go straight to the facts;):

Pros:

+ Space rpg.
+ Lots of ships.
+ Weapons, upgrades, modules, which can be purchase, looted and found on the ships and then unlocked and installed in our destroyers.
+ Random loot of varying quality.
+ Fractions with their own technologies and ships.
+ Each ship requires a different approach to combating it.
+ Shops where you can buy fractions of points, ships, modules, fuel, etc. (Items is renewed in time).
+ Difficult and you need good brains in gear to extend the ship / fleet that met the challenges.
+ Planetary exploration in search of resources, modules and foremost schemes of new ships.
+ Very addictive.
+ To walk far enough and get the first (any) flagship is the need to play hundreds of hours.
+ There is a lot of builds and strategies in network.
+ Very light and compatible with android save, can run on a low spec PCs.
+ I have already mentioned is difficult? Game is very â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥â¥ difficult, but it is an advantage.Even a small change in one module in a ship can change the course of battle.
+ 6 hangars ships. Like the modules in the ship, there can be almost freely lay ships and choose the fleet which we will fight in the next battles. 6 hangars allows you to store 6 sets of different fleets.
+ Digits and "%". Here, everything is based on digits - armour, attack, speed, times of renewal energy and weapons, energy, funds, "luck", levels, it all depends on a large number of numerical values.
+ You can create unique builds ships. Even properly "modded" small size craft is able to overcome the flagship ;).
+ Satisfaction when, after long and heavy fighting finally destroy the enemy's capital and get access to the hostile workshop, where you can forge the enemy ships and new modules to our ships.
+ Millions of stars (literally), and even more planets to explore ...
+ Full of randomness on almost everything (loot, maps, quality of items, the whole universe, etc.).
+ AFK grinding with "carriers".
+ Passives tree.
+ Points faction which unlocks alien technologies, and later conquer their capitals to create their "products".
+ Continuously developed and seasonal events :)
+ Gauntlets - grind / leveling :)
+ Super stable, 300h and there is no any crashes :)

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A couple cons (unfortunately):

- Graphic does not knock.
- No mouse targeting, which means that in the early stages of the game, it is difficult to hit a fast ship. In addition, the high value of the rotate speed of the ship can be too much "turn" and does not hit the enemy, and return to a good position may cost us time or/and defeat. Automatic targeting modules will "repair" this disadvantage.
- Addictive and it takes a lot of time :D.
- No automatic fighting, so we lose a lot of time on the easy/normal fights.
- There is no way to create your own ships.
- Can`t rotate ships in hangars and modules in ships (!), so we lose some freedom to deploy a fleet of ships and improving - the same applies to modules.
- If someone does not like to grind, but such is the nature of the game ;)
- There is no storyline and quests (yet) :(
- A couple of skills / passives in the skill tree
- No galactic trading. Of corse, we can buy and sell products, but the prices are fixed.
- Difficult planet exploration, small drone versus the dozens of horny, well armed drones.

I don`t remember more pros/cons ;).

I recommend you buy it, or play the demo (look at FB ;) ). You can create and lvl up a fleet in demo, and later, when developer probably will add some storyline and quests, we already will have created fleet (if, of course, saves will be still compatible with the final version of the game) - for now saves from the demo is compatible with the version of Steam.

If You like rpgs, space, grind, loot, strategies, military, skills, lasers, torpedoes, black holes, drones, thinking, exploration etc.. I recommend this game, because it is addictive like a black hole :).
