About This Game

In his latest plot to defeat Sonic and rule the world, Dr. Eggman has harnessed the power of menacing creatures known as the Deadly Six. However, when the Deadly Six rise up against their new master, Sonic must unite with his arch nemesis Eggman and explore the mystical Lost Hex in order to take them on head-to-head. Use Sonic’s amazing new moves & incredible Colour Powers to speed across a variety of unique terrains, racing inside, outside & upside down in every level.

NEW MOVES


Sonic moves like never before. Vault over obstacles, free run up & along walls & sky dive between islands at lightning speed. Plus the Spin Dash is back!

COLOUR POWERS


New & improved Colour Powers. Sonic can now fly through the air, tear up levels & explode into enemies. He’s more powerful than ever!

NEW WORLD


Speed through a variety of exhilarating levels. Explore massive mind-bending courses, vast underground tunnels & colossal structures in the sky as you run inside, outside & upside down.

BOSS BATTLES


Save the world one fight at a time. Take on each of the devious Deadly Six in dynamic boss battles which test all of Sonic’s moves & power-ups.

NiGHTMARE ZONE DLC Included


Includes NiGHTMARE Zone DLC free - featuring monsters inspired by the hit game 'NiGHTS into Dreams'

Do you like to actively complain as you play a game? Sonic Lost World's got you. It has controls as frustrating as its level design, its music as underwhelming as its boss fights, and best of all, this game (unlike most GOOD Sonic games) isn't over in an hour or two. Lost World is a Lost Cause.

In the famous words of Benjamin Frankin, "Those who sacrifice Sonic's Speed for Super Mario Galaxy deserve neither and lose both." Sonic's revised control scheme, which consists of a run (read: NOT 'boost', but run) button, two jump buttons that serve different uses, and a new parkour system that will get you killed almost as much as the pitfall-ridden hellhole that is most of the game's stages, isn't all too great. The only thing keeping me playing through the adventure is Mike Pollock's Eggman, the promise that the game will end at some point, and the fact that I paid for it.

I wanted to like this game for what it was trying to do. I can forgive a bad Sonic game, but a Sonic game this clearly isn't. The music sucks outside of like, two levels, and everything else sucks aside from like, two levels. The graphics are nice, but for some reason all the enemy designs are direct copies of enemies from better Sonic games. I want to say it was an artistic decision, but deep down I know this is the case because they just recycled assets from Sonic Generations.

Speaking of Sonic Generations, that game is legit. Play that instead. There's a modding community there, so enjoy some glorious new mod levels. This one was for charity, I hope my sacrifice was not in vain.

TL:DR - Only recommend on sale, with caution.

Got this game from the Humble Bundle of Sonic's 25th just to play this game, and I was a little disappointed. It's decent, but has a lot of issues. Here's what I mean.

The Good
+The items. It was nice to see Sonic's items come back, such as the shields, invincibility, etc. Though you have to do missions or randomly get them from the world map, They're a nice addition, and being able to hold up to 5 to use at any time can be useful.
+Super Sonic. Whereas he was a bit disappointing in recent games, unlocking him in this game feels pretty well-earned. Plus as long as you have 50+ rings, you can switch out any time! it's a nice addition.
+The music. It roughly holds up to the status quo of recent Sonic games. It's not too bad.

The Good but Bad/Can be Better
~Sonic's moves. He got a lot better at parkouring, and he's got a lot of nice moves, like running on walls, wall jumping both horizontally and vertically, the works. While they're cool, they could've been implemented better/more consistent. Sometimes he'll run on the wall just fine, others he won't.
~The controls in general. They're a little weird to get used to, such as having to constantly hold RT to run else I'll be slow as molasses. It's not like having a Boost meter where you're somewhat fast without it. Some things aren't even explained, such as being able to spindash for a very long time. You may die a fair amount, but later in the game you get extra lives handed to you like candy, so it's tolerable.
~The levels. I like most of the zones and some of the level themes! The first level led me to believe that you'd be able to complete levels in multiple different ways with each level since the first one had branching paths. That held true for a select few levels. The others felt rather linear. Not bad, but mislead me personally. Also, a lot of people compare this to Mario Galaxy, and for the most part, it's more inspired by.
~The wisps. I liked seeing them again, and there's a lot of them! Some old, some new. However, a lot of them are underutilized and/or annoying to use. Aiming the rocket in 3D can be harder than needbe, and while the bomb is cool, I feel like it's not useful aside from clearing the few missions it's used for. Speaking of...

The Minorly Bad
=I dealt with a few glitches, but none of them are too game-breaking, such as death on an open ceiling.

The Bad
-Mission mode. It's so horrible and tedious, and most of my playtime was just plowing through them. It's not like most games where you have progressive milestones and can just continue working consistently. "Collect 100/250/500/1000/whatever (up to 4500) rings" has to be randomly picked from Omochao, and you can only get up to 3 missions at a time. Some of them are really tedious and don't need to be, and some are flatout near impossible! (Wall jump 20 times in a row, how many places can you do that? Oh wait you can slide down a little to get more movement). So if anyone wants to get achievements, good luck. Also, you don't unlock anything from doing them all.
-Final boss. Feels like a watered down version from Colors on the Wii. Not a satisfying ending.
-Price. Definitely not worth $30. I got mine from $10 thanks to Humble Bundle, and I don't recommend paying more than that.

Overall, it's not too bad to kill some time, but it does get frustrating and has assets that aren't utilized to their fullest potential.

Just played about an hour of this game. Got it with a HumbleBundle and I gotta say it's really a throwback to Sonic 2. I know people like Pros and Cons lists, so here it goes:

Pros:
-Nice graphics.
-Great cutscenes
-Enemy detection and combos work great
-Plenty of variety
-Worlds are only a few levels, but make up for that with plenty of worlds (wouldn't it be biomes since this is all on the same planet? Idk.)
-Just the right amount of frustrating
-Voice acting is great
-Soundtrack is good, but could be better. (this is a pro, because the soundtrack doesn't blow. It IS good. I just feel like it could be a bit better)

Cons:
-Everything feels kinda slow. There are parts where the speed does pick up (like how you want a sonic game to be) but the majority of it you just feel like Sonic is holding back. This is a major flaw in all the 3D Sonic games. Not just this one.
-Color powerups are finicky. They sometimes last for 10 seconds, they sometimes last for 1 second. It's a total crapshoot.
-Powerups are underwhelming. Example: the speed power up (red shoe) has a nice sountrack, but you hardly notice a difference in speed.
-Sometimes Sonic just doesn't respond to moves. I've noticed this is a problem more when you're doing a wallrun than anywhere else.

That's really all I can think of. This game is definitly worth getting on sale, but I wouldn't pay the full price. The few things that I think are wrong with it really hinder the overall flow of the game, but it's not like it kills it by any means. It's still great to play, and if you were a fan of Sonic 1 & 2, then you'll enjoy this. If I could recommend a change for this game, I would recommend focusing on Sonic's speed. Increase that and you'll have a much better gaming experience. Maybe there's a mod for that...

Recommended.
7/10.

So back when this game was initially released on the Wii U I didn't pick it up because of all of the negative reviews I heard. However I recently picked up the game via Humble Bundle. Sonic Lost World is a very mixed experience, but I think the Pros out weigh the Cons. First, while I didn't like the concept of the spherical level design at first (I would've preferred a level design mentality closer to Generations or Colors) I found it to be mostly fine. The cylindrical/spherical design rarely gave me any issues. The graphics also look very nice, on par with if not better than Sonic Generations. I did have some some slow down issues at first, but bumping the resolution down from 1080p to 720p fixed that and the game almost always ran at 60fps (the water levels gave my computer a bit of a hard time and the gameplay slowed down a lot, but not to an unplayable point). The story also, while not the best the series has to offer, at least allows for some interesting interactions between Sonic, Tails, and Eggman. I have to say though, my biggest issue with this game has to be controller support. While the game does have it, it even says that it is better experienced with a controller before you even make it to the main menu, the support isn't the best. I use a normal first party Xbox 360 USB controller to play games on my PC and sometimes upon launching the game my controller would just not even work with this game. Restarting Steam or my computer normally fixed it, but it can be a pain in the butt when you want to just play a game and have to jump through a few hoops to get it to work. However, I think the fun gameplay of this game outweighs the negatives. I don't think it is the best game in the series, but if you are dying for some more Sonic like I was then this game is worth it!
