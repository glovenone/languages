New: Multis!

There's a new creation type: Multi! Please find it in the Holder category.

A Multi allows one person, or several people joining together, to move things on a given setup. This can be anything you can think of: tabletop or dice games, playing cards, an array of creatures flying above a nest, or any use you imagine. You can click on an item to change its state or trigger its behavior, and drag an item to move it within the Multi. Static compositions with no movable parts can also be created.

About This Game

Welcome to Manyland, an open universe we invent and live together! In an infinite world of abundance, we create new things by drawing them, build new places, hang out and chat, collect, make music, party, script objects, go on adventures... and have fun together in ways none of us can predict.

"WONDERFUL ... so fun to explore, build, interact, and create ART. Such a variety of things in this world to do" -Joshua C.

"Exploring the world is a blast" -Massively

"The most exciting game I've played so far mainly because of its freedom to shape your world." -Cosmin

"i love this game!!" -Carie Corman

Draw, build, make music, party, go swim, script interacting objects and adventures, do sports, come up with puzzles, play, explore... Manyland is an open universe with no predefined story, theme or goal... it becomes what we will make of it!

Every pixel you see in Manyland is created by all of us together as community. We're two indie developers and this is our labor of love. Our top priority always is all of your happiness! We'll refuse to go compromised "pay to win" routes... let us show the world we can have a universe that's both sustainable, and makes all of us proud! Over 100 million blocks have been placed by all of us already, but we're waiting for one more person: you. If there's anything, please email us at we@manyland.com (a note on service availability)

A press kit is available.

If you want to try this game out WITHOUT paying a cent for it, just use the web version! The link is manyland.com (I usually play the web version, but if you love this game, feel free to help support it on steam!)

This is simply put; the BEST game I have ever played.

Manyland is an innovative game which allows players to create their own blocks. There are a variety of blocks you can create, and the only limit to what you can make is your imagination!

Here are some examples of types there are:

Body - Bodies can be used to create your skin. With custom animations, I'm able to be anything I want to be. You can make a skin as a baseball player, a girl with a dress, or even a dinosaur!

Dynamics - Dynamics are used to animate blocks frame-by-frame. You can use this to make cool animated objects - and much more! If you took the time, you could create a puppy to follow you around!

Interactings - I use this type the most. With interactings, you can create your own games with special commands! If you want a player to get wood for cutting down a tree, you can do it! If you want to make an XP system to keep track of a player's level, you can do it! Interactings make many things possible.

Solids & Backs - Do you just want to make a simple house, with windows? Just take another player's blocks, or create your own! You can custom-design wood for the walls, and create a log-cabin like style for your house's backgrounds! Add chairs, flowers, and windows to spice up your design.

***
Since all the blocks are drawn pixel-by-pixel, you don't have to worry about pre-sets. Everything in this game is hand drawn!

Phillip is a very active and supportive developer which will frequently update and will help assist with most of your troubles. There are many friendly people in the community, and you can make a ton of friends.

- Savant.

Manyland was created as an online sandbox where people could design solid blocks and place them into the world to create buildings and then whole worlds. Time has evolved the game to include many different kinds of blocks, ones which move, or change, ones which interact with Manyland and ones which interact with the wider internet. I have never played a game where the developpers were so keen to listen to the userbase, interact with them in game, and learn from them to improve the user experience.

Manyland

Manyland may look a bit simple and even childish at first, but due to it's complexity it ends being a hard game to explain.
Imagine Terraria in a bigger scale, merged with social games like Habbo or Second Life, and a full engine at our disposal to create almost anything. Interested? I was too, and thankfully the game ended up being better than I expected.

You start off in one of the welcome areas, surrounded by random people without any clue of what to do next. But just like the games I mentioned, there are hundreds of rooms for us to choose. In Manyland they are all divided in categories, such as Art, Cinema, Chat, Adventure, Games, Quest, Music, Party, Horror, Casino, and the list goes on and on.

There is unlimited content thanks to the possibility of creating our own items and character models, and then using them to build something entirely new. Maybe a costume party, a giant pool with robots, minigames, a cinema that actually plays videos from YouTube. There is literally no limit but our imagination.
We can attach items to our body, such as weapons, furniture, vehicles or even wings. And we can swap our character model anytime we want, either by creating one by ourselves or using one that has been already created by another player. There are specific rooms for these items, and luckily we can use a stash/inventory on a side of the screen to save everything that we like and then use it in our room.

We can build a house from scratch, using different types of floor pieces, stairs, ladders, walls, blocks, doors, anything that we can imagine. The game benefits from the online community and we can find people 24/7.

Being able of creating pretty much anything we want it's one of the highlights of the game, and it's something that I haven't seen in a long time. It can be quite hard to understand it at first, but there are plenty of tutorials, both outside and inside the game.

Playing Manyland with a group of friends it's a really fun and addictive experience, and if you're looking for a game that let's you build mini games, items and entire rooms from scratch, this is a must buy. The community it's always helping to improve it, and the devs are very active.

You can try the free version of the game here: Manyland Web

Early Access Review
Posted: 18 November, 2015
I've had the privelege to watch Manyland grow and change over the course of almost 2 years now and I have to say it is an enigma and a joy. Everyone's experience with Manyland is guaranteed to be different - it is so open ended that nearly anything is possible.

There really is no aim to the game unless you give it one. Whether you want to create a messy workshop where other players have to match parts to broken robots, a sunny beach full of partying celebrities and seashell diving, lock your friends in a haunted mansion, an art museum, a cottage in the woods, a space walk, a funhouse... the tools are at your disposal, all you need is imagination. If you'd rather just explore other people's work, that's fine too!

This game is best played with friends, as the adult player base is a little shy - we mostly hide. If you're looking to make friends be sure to come out to Manyday on the 19th of every month.

Despite having only 2 devs and no official moderators, the game is surprisingly well self-moderated and you have to go looking to find anything terribly inappropriate.

If you are looking for great places to explore - try manyunity.com, a user-made website (Azuros) cataloguing popular areas.
