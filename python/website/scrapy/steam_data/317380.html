About This Game

Choose your side and lead your soldiers, tanks and aircraft to victory.

The people of Folvos are about to lose their freedom. Their tiny planet is home an extraordinary new raw material, but they themselves don’t even know about it. The great Vasgalan Empire discovered it and is staking their claim. Clouds of war are shaping over the endless Folvos desert. You cannot wait any longer. Choose your side and take command! Lead your soldiers, tanks and aircraft to victory through Folvos varying landscapes across dozens of battles.

  • Enjoy highly developed turn-based system with many tactical options.
  • Accessible for beginners, but deep enough for seasoned veterans.
  • Play two campaigns for different sides and a tutorial.
  • Win 20 unique scenarios.
  • Command 20 different types of military unit.

This is a good modern/futuristic turn based strategy game, in the likes of Panzer General, that went completely under the radar.

War on Folvos is set in a modern/future setting, the game has a decent storyline about a nation that gets invaded and 'rises up' against the invaders. War on Folvos offers players the chance to play as either side, the invaders, or the rebels. Each side has an interesting mix of units - some based off of real-world vehicles.

The combat mechancis are good, with units having a set number of 'action points' per turn that allows them to maneuver or shoot - or both. Additionally, there is a logistical layer that limits fuel and ammunition per unit. Both sides have repair/resupply vehicles that keep units supplied and repaired.

Overall fun game. Good price. Pick it up.

Cliff Notes, Worth it but the start was weird.

This game was released in 2006 so the graphics are to be expected of an indie game from that time that is to say decent to good and fitting for the story but not going to win any awards. The combat as far as I could play was fairly simple. However it did seem that combat would become far more involved with higher tier units and larger total fighting forces.

Difficulty, most of the difficulty for me came from the interface itself. In order to select or target a unit you have to click the actual unit model rather than the hex they are on. This is an important distinction. I also found difficulty in locating my units on each new level. Neither of these have put me off the game however.

Plot, weird. Okay now to the actual review. I walk in thinking I'll be helping the Folvos army, then load up the tutorial and plot twist. Okay helping Vasgalan empire now. Read decsription another plot twist helping Folvos again. So if you like plots that aren't mandatory and will make you very confused pick this up.

There used to be a game destroying bug of doom however it appears to have been patched as part of the steam integration. Well done Lonely Troops. So even if the game may never get multiplayer it appears that at the very least bugs making it unplayable are going to be fixed.
