TLDR; While this game is undeniably old, thus the dated graphics, but I still think it's a pretty cool game to dive in.

Note: This review was done during my experience with the early game life within Lucent Heart, and is subjected to change in the near future when I reaches the later game contents.

I've played quite a bit back when the game was handled by a different company. I took a long hiatus from the game then and now I'm onto a fresh start all over again from level 1. It's been quite a while since I last played this, so I've forgotten lots of basic knowledge of the game, therefore I'm no different than any other regular newbies.

When you first login into the world, you'll be welcomed by the rather dated graphics. Since this game was released more than 5+ years ago, this is quite understandable. The day and night cycles are implimented and some monsters were supposedly appear during a certain time (which is a pretty neat feature imo).

The community so far as I've experienced was very friendly and welcoming. Although there were very much afkers hanging around in town, when you asked for help, a lot of people were eager to offer their knowledge of the game which put a smile on my face.

I enjoy playing solo most of the time in MMOs, while sometimes I do find myself participating a few dungeon runs here and there, if the game is possible to tackle alone, I much prefer it that way. That's being said, this game is certainly solo-friendly in the early game, and is very encouraging towards team work later on in game.

This game follows the same MMO formula as the others, the questing system is quite generic with the "go here, kill that, come back and return the quest to this npc" type and would probably be tiresome as this goes on, but since I'm a casual player, this doesn't concern me that much. Especially understanding that most MMOs were designed to be played that way anyways, and this game certainly tries its best to redeem itself with some fun events with mini DDR-like game (It was called Dance Masters, I think. Correct me if I'm wrong.) and possibly upcoming trivia-type event for players to get involved in.

So, overall (as of now) I'm voting "yes" for recommending this game :)

Lucent Heart, what can I say.

First off, I'm one of the dedicated players who've been playing this for years and I am happy to see it on Steam. However, it didn't went on here without its struggles. Yes, it doesn't have the best graphics, best storyline or the best anything, but this is pretty old. And if you're like me, chances are, you love this game as much as the other player that you invested over thousands of hours into it. Which I did. I would say Lucent Heart is one of those games you played in your childhood and you simply fell in love with it, like Zelda or FF7, Lucent Heart gives off the same vibe and that's one of the charms of this game.

Lucent Heart is a classic anime MMORPG developed by a Taiwanese company called 'Playcoo' and it is staggeringly popular in Japan and other Asian countries, while it is not as popular in the West, it is alive and well through these years thanks to a hardcore fanbase and Subagames who've been running it. Lucent Heart not only offers such a unique storyline and visual design but is bundled with features. Unfortunately, not all of them are out yet because Lucent Heart is currently version 10.0, but regardless, it still have plenty to offer for the time being.

Features


Lover System
Many people have found their true love through this game, though i like to think it's not a dating game, the theme of this game suggest so. (Note: This game is not dedicated to romance stuff.) But people have met and even marry in RL. Good for them, but what about you, the reader? Well if you are interested, Lucent Heart have a system called Soulmate where you can sign up with Cupid NPC and find your, well, soulmate. And if you feel she/he's the right one for you, then you can marry in-game. And yes, you can marry same-sex user, hurray for equality! (In later version, you may able to create and raise a child regardless of your gender.)

GvG/PvP
I'm sure you know what these stand for. You can't have MMORPG without a little rivalry. And boy oh boy, you better be careful playing in these matches, it's pretty brutal. In GvG, guilds are challenged randomly by opponent guild according to the system and the Guilds' strength. And you play to win by most points from killing enemies or destroying their flags. Depending how the battle plays out, GvG has 3 rounds and occurs on Friday, Saturday and Sunday. Very exciting. However, if you're in a mood for PvP, there is a channel 'Underworld - PvP' where you can freely kill players outside of the towns. Scary.

Housing
While I wouldn't say it's the best feature, you are given an house at lv25 and able to decorate your own place. And if you're brave enough, you can invade people's homes in acquire special items and points, as well as rating their home decoration.

Transportation
Transports is a must because Lucent Heart is big, pretty big. If you don't like walking all the way to your destination then you can always hop onto the Airship, there are five airships available to different stops and require no fees. Pretty sweet. But if you prefer riding mounts, keep on reading.

Mounts and Pets
Lucent got unicorns, carriage, lions, motorbikes, rhinos and even robots for mounts. It's quite a spectacle in the town, you'd be surprise how well they fit in. You also get support from your pets, or um.. *cough* slave http://puu.sh/n6H8U/f588594fe3.jpg http://puu.sh/n6HfN/208223361d.jpg Cute, aren't they? Ehehe..hehe Of course, this game also offers "pets" handsome guys.

Character customisation
From the start, you may think the character design choices are quite limited, which I agree. However, if you are patient and dive into the game and press B (which opens Item Mall.) You will find quite a lot of clothes, accessories and adjustments to design yourself. Personally, I love the butler set, fits me quite well I would say. But if you descend from the fires of hell, you can buy some flamboyant clothes or if you're a princess from uh- Wateverland, it got plenty of dresses to choose from. Honestly, this game have a few fashion diva and they take it to the max.

Classes
Naturally, you start out as a newbie who have yet chosen their class. Classes is consist of 4 classes, 8 advanced jobs, and have three transformations. You start out with a choice of two, Warrior or Mage. Then once you've done your part, the next transformation of class where you given an choice of two more classes and you keep on building up. Depending on the line of class (Check here for a map: http://puu.sh/n6Ig8/c782a0bdaa.png) There's also another class called Phantom Warlock, which is a shapeshifter class. Which I don't know much about but hey, it's pretty cool.


Gameplay
You can play Solo, by any means, but it is hard. Lucent Heart is hard. After all, the game is intended for grinding-lovers audience which they spend most of the time in. Strangely enough, it is addictive even if you're playing alone. Lucent Heart is a lot more fun when playing with your friends. You can party up to 6 members and there are plenty of deadly bosses out there to take on. The gameplay isn't exactly amazing but rather nostalgic, in terms of old gaming. Even if you're not in old games, do give this a try. It is a fantastic game, and I enjoy playing it a lot.


Overall, the game is not without its flaws but nevertheless, amazing game and I'm happy to give it a long review. Annnnnd if you made it the end, pat yourself on the back for being a reasonable gamer and join me, to critically acclaimed game in its own era, the adventure of Lucent Heart.

Note: I may update and correct mistakes on this review.
