About This Game

Congratulations on your new Little Inferno Entertainment Fireplace! Throw your toys into your fire, and play with them as they burn. Stay warm in there. It's getting cold outside!
Burn flaming logs, screaming robots, credit cards, batteries, exploding fish, unstable nuclear devices, and tiny galaxies. An adventure that takes place almost entirely in front of a fireplace - about looking up up up out of the chimney, and the cold world just on the other side of the wall.

Key Features:

  • Nominated for three awards at the Independent Games Festival including the Seumas McNally grand prize, Technical Excellence Award, and Nuovo Award, with Honorable Mentions in Design and Audio.
  • From the creators of World of Goo and Henry Hatsworth in the Puzzling Adventure
  • 100% indie - made by 3 guys, no office, no publishers, no funding
  • IAP Free - no spam, upselling, ads, of any sort. Just the game.
  • Zero Waste - short, polished, perfeccct as possible

There's a lot more to Little Inferno than meets the eye, but let's start with the basics. Do you like to burn things? If, like me, your answer is YES!, then the Little Inferno Entertainment Fireplace is for you! Additionally, if the idea of squashing and/or burning spiders appeals, then this product is also for you. If neither of these hold long term appeal, never fear - you can collect coins and purchase products (to burn and begin the process again). Everyone enjoys purchasing digital junk! Not you? Well, what about progressing through catalgoues and collecting GOLD STARS? And COMBOS. What about combos? Everyone likes combos!

It's this commentary that makes Little Inferno great - it has something to say about the state of games, about itself, about you. Yes, it is a ridiculous game, but you'll get more than you bargained for from it - or at least you should.

So, how does Little Inferno actually play?

Well, it's best described in three easy steps:
Step 1: Burn items.
Step 2: Collect coins.
Step 3: Purchase items.
Rinse and repeat.

But I'm not a pyromaniac. What's the point?

The point is to burn your way through catalogues of items to unlock new catalogues of items to burn. If you're a completionist, you can get gold stars on all of your catalogues. If this (understandably) isn't enough, there's combos - these are likely what you'll spend most of your play time on. These are Little Inferno's puzzle aspect. From the title of the combo, you have to work out which items to burn together to achieve the combo. This gets a bit trickier towards the end when you have multiple catalogues of items to choose from. That's about it for Little Inferno's gameplay. As for the /point/, well, you'll have to find that out for yourself.

But, as much as there is more to this game...

10/10 - would spend a day burning fake items in a fake fireplace again
