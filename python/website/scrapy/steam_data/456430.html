About This Game

Chtistmas Adventure: Candy Storm – is a funny Christmas Hidden Object game with puzzles and mini-games of varying difficulty.

Lisa’s grandfather, Santa Claus, has asked for her help delivering Christmas presents to a remote town. Trying to shorten her way, she drives through an old forest when suddenly her car turns into candies!! Moreover, everything around is scattered with candies and covered with other sweets! Help Lisa investigate what’s happened and help fix her car so she can make it to town in time to save Christmas!

In this game you expect more than 2 hours of Christmas gameplay.

I purchased this game because it was inexpensive and because it had a Christmas theme. While some of the objects were holiday-related, there wasn't really a holidy feel to this game at all.

It's a very short game at about 2 hours at most to complete. There are only 4 locations. Each one is fairly loaded with puzzles though. Some of the logic on where to put what items you find seemed off at times.

The story seemed very arbitrary, help a few women do some random stuff in exchange for a magical cure to fix your car.

There is either no music, or a single track from the Nutcracker on loop (that I can recall hearing). I don't remember much in audio in general.

I found a single typo. Once asked to find a "dool" when it meant "doll."

It wasn't always easy to discern what all the objects were in each puzzle, but as a test, I started selecting items from the screen that somehow stood out from the base image and was able to find most of them without even reading the item list. So it's pretty obvious what the target items are. When you revisit a hidden object location, it's extra easy to find objects, because they are all new objects that weren't there the last time.

The hint tips are useful and there wasn't any limit on using them. However, it didn't always direct me to move to a different location when needed. If it didn't point to anything, I knew to visit the other three areas.

It's okay for $1.39 (the sale price I paid). I finished it. There are better games of this genre out there though.

The winter holiday season can be one of the most joyous times of the year for many, and it goes without saying that Christmas is a big part of that joy. If you celebrate Christmas or are just familiar with the usual festivities then you’ll understand this game right away. It has all the things the holiday has; snow, candy, gifts, cooking and baking, helping out others and of course adorable little old ladies. This game is as if Kris Kringle himself sat down and decided to develop a causal game. If you’re a scrooge then bah humbug to you, try Christmas Adventure: Candy Storm anyway!

The story is very simple, you’re dashing through the snow in your red pickup truck loaded with presents when all of a sudden you’re hit with a magical wave of energy. Your car has been turned into candy and you’ve landed in what seems to be a festive holiday land! My first instinct was to eat my new diabetes-mobile but your character seeks help in restoring it to its metal form. It seems these three nice old ladies that live in a gingerbread house made a mistake with their spell and now need your help to reverse it, while they also need help with other mundane tasks. At one point I was getting suspicious that they actually stopped me on purpose to have someone help do things they couldn’t do since they’re old. However, they are very nice and they help you get back on the road again. The game’s store front page says you’re playing as Santa’s Granddaughter, Lisa, but this is never mentioned in-game, which was a missed opportunity on the part of the developers.

You use your mouse to play the game, clicking on things and people to interact with them. You also collect useful items that will need to progress through certain obstacles along the way. The main aspect of the game are hidden item scenes. You’re given a list of items to find and a chaotic scene of Christmas fun to sift through, in the end finding the one actual item that you needed. There are also puzzles as well but there are not many and most are rather easy. You will mainly be helping the old ladies out and doing quite a few hidden-item scenes in this game which I really liked.

The graphics are straight out of a Christmas card. Everything Christmasy can be found here, trees and candy canes, angels and bells, so it’s over-the-top festive. The game is very beautiful and full of color, the visuals put an instant smile on my face when I opened up the program. There are beautiful holiday lights, garland, twinkling stars and so much more everywhere. To go with the theme, the music is also holiday inspired some tunes even being old classics you may recognize. There is no voice acting at all, however.

I played this game in April but it felt like mid-December the whole time. This is a bare bones hidden object game, the plot is simple with no twists or turns and the gameplay is traditional. The game is also a bit short, I completed it within three hours. Also, I noticed a few spelling and grammatical errors in the text. However, the game is also only two dollars and has trading cards on Steam so it’s a good value for what you’re given. Christmas Adventure: Candy Storm is a fun treat any time of the year and I fully recommend it.
