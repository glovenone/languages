About This Game

Unleash a fury of powerful weapons as you blast your way through armies of Alien forces hell-bent on taking you down! Battle through 14 intense levels and prepare to face a barrage of beastly-sized bosses that will test your every skill. Each of your 10 weapons comes with two firing modes and you’ll need all of them! Battle hardened Alien forces will attack you from every direction using various flanking and attack moves. Built with powerful up-to-date graphics technology, awesome physics, destructible environments… experience a truly intense, oldschool styled shooter game!

Key Features


  • Wide variety of Alien enemy forces including Infantry, Warriors, Flying Drones, Spiders
  • Huge boss fights – Over 21 enemy types including 8 beastlysized bosses to challenge and destroy through 14 Levels
  • Strong thru-the-gun experience with 10 powerful weapons each with two firing modes
  • Skill shots, head shots and shooting accuracy heavily rewarded
  • Player-triggered destruction of environments and cover helps to advance through levels
  • Built with powerful up-to-date graphics technology, awesome physics, destructible environments

Before Alien Rage I'd played both Sniper Ghost Warrior games from CI Games and to be honest I wasn't impressed. Thus, I was a bit sceptical since gameplay videos / trailers for this game were just as promising as they were for their previous products. How wrong I was!

The Story

This is about an ongoing battle between humans and an alien species called Vorus. Remember the Unobtainium's significance in Avatar? The equivalent mineral in this game is called Promethium and it is suffice to say that this is a highly efficient source of energy so both races have all the reason to fight for it. As the intro tells us it is the Vorus that started it all and our protagonist, Jack, is the lonely hero who's dropped at a crucial mining facility (where it all started) to destroy it since it has long been taken over by the uglies. During his mission Jack is assisted by 2 people on the radio - this chit-chat often involves jokes that make the game more fun but since the campaign is not long enough this particular part you will not miss / remember.

The Gameplay

You shoot first and ask questions later! You reload sometimes but only sometimes as beside the human weapons your assortment also consists of alien arms - with these you can just shoot but it's worth taking a break from time to time as some overheat pretty quickly. You can take 2 weapons with you and you have a 3rd one - your good old pistol - whose ammunition never depletes. No grenades, no mines, nothing - just 3 weapons to wreak havoc! It should also be noted that every weapon has a secondary fire mode - you have to be careful as all use the same ammunition. It should be noted that in the heat of battle using this mode can save your life by turning the tables.

The Atmosphere

This is a fast-paced, action-heavy FPS game that takes us back to the old days. The game also looks very nice - it is a bit heavy on the blue colour (also it has a bit too much post-processing effects for my taste) but some of the locales look very promising. We go through dozens of corridors and hangars but then at one point we visit the docking bay. Lots of stuff is going on around us - ship arrivals and departures and sometimes the action is so fast and it requires you to pay attention not allowing you to appreciate your surroundings. Then Jack embarks a train and uses its mounted minigun to shoot up the place. The he visits a mothership to end the Vorus presence once and for all.

The Difficulty

This game is challenging! Very challenging! As an FPS "veteran" I started it on "hard" difficulty (even the easiest is called "challenging") and I was in for a world of hurt. I restarted several areas numerous times and at one point I had to lower the difficulty level on the go to be able to rid myself of one of the 9 bosses. Naturally, the bosses are tough and in several cases you have to figure out how to beat them (at other times running and gunning perfectly works). Still, while it is difficult the game appreciates the way you kill enemies - all explosive, melee and double kills are recognised (the voice-over shouts are akin to the ones we heard in 1998 during an exciting Unreal Tournament match) and result in scores. These, in time, unlock some perks you can apply to Jack on the go (you cannot activate more than 3 at one time) that slightly help you in the battle.

The Recap

Similarly to Hard Rest or Shadow Warrior this game puts emphasis on the action and the action only. However, unlike the aforementioned this game will be TOUGH if you give it a chance. Be prepared otherwise you'll be disappointed! Too bad Alien Rage gamers are not active enough to join me for a multiplayer session - I have to take out my disappointment. again, on Jack's favourite Mech bosses, then!

Well, I’m pretty sure that I got this one out of a Humble Bundle deal; something that I am counting myself as thankful for. Alien Rage – Unlimited is an action, FPS from CI Games. Let’s get rocking.

The Good

First on the list is the graphics. Alien Rage offers some pretty nice graphics even on lower settings. I was pleasantly surprised by this (especially considering that I’d never heard of or seen anything about this game up until I installed and played it).

Secondly, the weapon designs are cool. Every weapon I picked up felt, sounded, and looked different. You want automatics? You got it. You want explosives? You got it. Practically every weapon you can find in Alien Rage would be right at home in Quake or Unreal Tournament.

Thirdly, the game itself plays very similar to games like Doom, Quake, and Unreal Tournament. I loved those games back when I was starting to play computer games and that added bit of nostalgia helped me enjoy this game a little more.

The Bad

Games that are hard because they genuinely test your skill are games that I thoroughly enjoy. Games that are hard because your character is overly squishy are games that I do not enjoy. Combine that with recklessly aggressive AI and your bound to have a bad time sooner or later. This was perhaps my biggest gripe about Alien Rage.

The Meh

Something felt inherently off about the dialogue, but I could never quite put my finger on it. Sometimes it felt genuine, other times it felt like they were trying to hard, and still others it felt like a joke was trying to be made, but it fell far short. Whatever it was that was wrong, it rubbed me the wrong way in the most subtle way possible.

You can “jump” in the game, but it is easily the most pathetic jump I have ever seen in all of my days. You might come six inches off the ground at most. Honestly, if this is what you choose to do with your jump feature, I’d just leave it out of the game.

Verdict: Nay

On the whole, Alien Rage feels like a FPS bullet hell and becomes less fun the longer you play. If you can get it for dirt cheap then maybe, just maybe, it would be worth it.

Developed and published by CIGames, the same company behind Sniper: Ghost Warrior and Lords of the Fallen, comes Alien Rage - Unlimited; a sci-fi FPS title that's packed with non-stop action. Originally released in 2013 for PC, Xbox 360, and Playstation 3, the game has only seen a minor following to date, and it has received mixed reviews that weigh mostly on the negative side.

Let's just go ahead and rip the band-aid off; Alien Rage is a generic shooter when it comes down to the plot line and characters. It only took me about six hours to finish the game, and I couldn't even tell you what their names are, except for the guy you play as... his name is Jack. However, bland story and people aside, Alien Rage is actually an excellent, solid shooter when it comes down to it. Not to mention, it's one of the few sci-fi shooters that is currently available on Steam that doesn't involve some kind of stealth mechanic.

The basics of the plot are as such: Earth colonized on some asteroid to mine an energy substance known as Promethium. An alien species known as the Vorus got word of this, joined the Earth team on the asteroid, and helped mine the energy source. The Vorus realized that they could have the Promethium all to themselves, and turned on the humans, wiping out the miners completely. Jack is sent in to wipe out the scum, and there are a lot of them to stick bullets in to. While you're there, you gather audio logs to fill in the blanks of the story that the very brief and minor cutscenes fail to explain.

Be forewarned, this game is not for those who are not good at shooters or FPS in general; the difficulty, even on the lowest setting, can be maddening at times. There is no sticky cover system like in Gears of War, it's up to you to find something sturdy to hide behind while bullets and plasma bolts are whizzing by your head. At times, it also seems like no matter where you stand, or what you do, the enemies will be hitting you anyway. It's also incredibly easy to be overrun by Vorus, and it's best to maintain your distance at all times.

Speaking of foes, there are enough enemy types that will keep you satisfied kill after kill in Alien Rage. Everything from your standard rifle-wielding hooded Vorus, grunts that will turn invisible and bum rush you, mini-gun powerhouses, to plasma blasting elites. Each nasty alien is a force to be reckoned with, and in large groups they are nearly unstoppable. Luckily, you can use the aid of explosive barrels and secondary weapon fire to make your pest control service go as smooth as possible. There are also 9 bosses to be dealt with across the 14 levels that you will play through, and while they all have a weakness to exploit, they are immensely powerful and your death will come swiftly many times over while facing them.

There are a total of 10 weapons, from a standard assault rifle, shotgun, pistol, rocket launcher, sniper rifle, to alien weaponry such as the plasma gun. Each one has its own unique secondary firing mechanic, most of which result in a large grenade catapulting from your weapon. Using the secondary fire, you can do some sure damage to the highly destructible, and impressive, environment. While many of the areas look similar, it's important to remember that you're in a giant mining facility, although you do venture out onto the asteroid a few times. The whole game has a very cold, surgical, clean feel to it; despite the strewn about blood and guts of the former Earth miners.

For a title released 3 years ago, Alien Rage surprisingly enough looks as if it was released this year. This is of course due to the fact that the game was made in Unreal Engine 3, and it does well in showing off some highly impressive graphical and mechanical technology. The controls feel sturdy, your character feels beefy, and the optimization is near-flawless. I did experience some framerate issues while transitioning between some sections, and when there were large explosions; however, the results were a brief dip into the mid-40's, and then it was back up to 60 FPS in no time. I also wanted to add in here that the soundtrack for this game is filled with some great beats that are sure to keep your blood pumping the entire time.

Well, there you have it. For a generic shooter, as many people have laid claim to in the review sections of many sites and forums, Alien Rage does have its perks. It's a solid game, with great mechanics and gameplay, and it's surprisingly fun despite it's obvious downfalls. It's really a shame that we're lacking so heavily in action based sci-fi FPS titles, and if more games such as this one came along with a better story and more developed characters, it would be a welcome change of pace from the tried-and-true military shooters that have flooded the market. If six hours of gameplay is enough for you to sink $21.99 CAD over, then go for it. Otherwise, hold out for a sale and definitely pick this one up in the $5-10 price range. Don't miss out on this one if you're craving a great action packed, sci-fi shooter.

Rating: 3.5/5.0 - Average, can take it or leave it.
Belphegore's Hell House Reviews (Group)

Rage against the alien machine

Alien Rage: Unlimited from CI games, the development house responsible for the Sniper: Ghost Warrior series, is a game few would have played which explains the reason it turned up in one of the bundle deals recently. Alien Rage's chance of success, while probably never really high to begin with was only further diminished after professional critics tore the game to shreds - which is a terrible shame because underneath all the Halo/Gears of War (insert any other shooter featuring marines in space) similarities is a terribly old-school on-the-rails corridor shooter that should be appreciated more by gamers who grew up playing the original DOOM and Wolfenstein back in the early nineties.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=301430217
Set upon a massive asteroid that has become home to both humans and an alien species who are seemingly able to co-exist jointly mining the rock for the highly efficient energy source known as Prometheum until this shaky alliance is turned on its head when all the human miners are systematically eradicated by the alien hoard. With all operations further suspended your task is to get into the mining facility and destroy it using what ever force necessary.

The game will have you battling through 14 intense (and I say intense) levels because Alien Rage was designed to be difficult. With difficulty settings starting at challenging some might find it a tad too difficult during the initial levels and I have to agree with some of the more professional reviewers that the game does suffer from some severe difficulty spikes that I could well see a person leaving the game due to frustration. Things do bed down a bit once you start unlocking perks which can then be applied to your character such as 50% less damage from projectiles (which I found incredibly handy because at the start of the game any alien shooting projectile damage towards my character would instantly result in his death). The game does use a checkpoint system to save the game and thankfully they are not used sparingly so as difficult as the game can get sometimes the player should be able to make steady progress. Alien Rage features a leaderboard and uses score modifiers so the more headshots and destructible environmental damage you cause the higher your score.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=288804235
Alien Rage was developed using the trusty Unreal Engine 3 and it shows in some of the games more unique underground mining settings. While plenty of reviewers were quick to point out that the player runs through an endless labyrinthine of generic levels, I have to disagree. Yes while some level design is often repeated throughout the 14 scenarios presented I found some of the levels particularly striking as I stood still for a few seconds just to take in the sights and sounds. Alien Rage can really be quite the looker for the most part and if you don't believe me just run on over and take a squizz at the screenshots I have posted for the game.

If I do have some slight gripes it's that of all the weapons presented I found only a handful of them were really effective which forced me to discard experimenting with various weapons early on in game because most of what is needed to see yourself safely execute this mission is presented from the get-go. The games boss battles are also a source of frustration. They are long and tedious fights of attrition and often you will find you have run out of ammo which results in you having to fall back on your weaker arsenol such as your pistol - which while still effective - only end up dragging the boss fights on longer than need be because of the smaller damage they do. Most bosses go through two or more phases during a fight which is is where the frustrating aspect rears its ugly head because should you die during the bosses second or third phase you are doomed to repeat everything from the beginning. One particular boss fight near the end of the game really had me on the verge of a rage-fit but thankfully a step away from the game for a while was all that was needed.

If you are not prepared to pay the full price of $19.99 ( I got the game in a bundle deal for less that $4) then I can heartily recommend you pick it up during a Steam sale.

A fun but flawed first person shooter. AI has shown the capability to flank however the appearance of rushing the player is apparent. Aliens do have the ability to melee as well as stasis the player so I won't hold AI rushing against the game. Aliens can and do flank though they are not the brightest bulbs and will get themselves killed rather easily when isolated. Boss fights are numerous but straight forward not showing much imagination.

The game has three difficulty modes each increasing in challenge. I started with the middle difficulty but initially my life lasted as long as a Gastrotrich, so normal it was as my mood was for limiting restarts as much as possible given the game's propensity to kill you.

Story elements in the form of recordings are spread around the levels but I only found one third of the content. Whether I missed their obvious placement by chance or because they were hidden is unknown though the story can be ignored from the small portion I heard. Bonus mini pyramids are also strewn about, three per level but again I never completed a set. The pyramids add to your score as Alien Rage awards points during game-play and includes leader-boards as well.

Secondary ammo is also strewn about. They look like suitcases so they aren't hard to miss although initially I wasn't sure what they were which brings us to the weapons. An important part of any shooter. The weapons are imaginative. You get to carry only two plus a revolver. There is a high reliance on secondary fire in order to implement much needed crowd control. Primary ammo is picked up through gun pick ups as a dropped gun retains it's ammo or by walking over the same gun as you possess. Weapons in the game consist of both alien and human. Human weapons consist of differing semi automatic rifles, a sniper rifle, mentioned revolver, shotgun and rocket launcher. Sounds and reads standard however each weapon has an important secondary fire mode as mentioned.

The rocket launcher, for example, when equipped will affect your sprinting ability and to use or un-equip the launcher the full animation must be run through making the weapon a bit strategic and difficult to use, a welcome feature considering the weapon's ability to quickly change the battlefield. Which reminds me of enemy hit-boxes which I found to be well implemented. I can remember shooting a rocket through enemy legs and cursing myself for lack of careful aiming.

The alien arsenal consists of energy weapons and is somewhat more limited. No rocket launcher for example but the weapons are fun to use. The alien rifle for instance has no reloads, however the weapon will overheat, again ensuring the player makes strategic use of the weapon. The alien shotgun is very cool and very powerful, the cool anti gravity secondary fire helps immensely. To summarize the S in FPS, the shooting is exceptional, it's explosive and fun.

Some reviewers have panned the levels as being too generic. So what. The game looks and sounds great. You feel Alien Rage, your feel of the game, both visual and auditory is significant. Many of the levels have nice set pieces and it's great to see a publisher like City Interactive enable their development team to build a game with strong immersive environmental elements. Great explosions not only permeate the combat but play a central role. Graphically the game is exceptional though performance will vary but not enough to detract from the game.

Lastly I will address the utter disaster that is the Alien Rage checkpoint system. Wait, it is not a system. Systems by definition imply some sort of systematic routine. No, the check-pointing, no, make that the checkpoint restart logic in Alien Rage is not just buggy. I've come to the conclusion that it is unfinished. There is no other reason for checkpoint restarts missing assets, many of which will prevent you from continuing the game. Explosives for blowing apart levels to continue, needed secondary ammunition for combat , platforms not reset so you're left looking like a shopper waiting for a mall elevator.

I've found that after each death, instead of using the checkpoint continue, I had to exit to the main menu and continue from there. It is beyond my wildest imagination how the developers obviously knowing that there checkpoint restart system was broken could not of just rewired their checkpoint code to use the continuation logic from the main menu. From the continue on the main menu the game loaded correctly every time. As the game loads very fast, circumventing the intended method for checkpoint restarts is not a significant issue.

If you enjoy a good FPS then Alien Rage presents itself as a very challenging fun FPS that will satisfy most any FPS urge.

i want to start by saying that the overall negative reviews of this game have been heavily unfair and undeserved - this is a thoroughly decent fps title that promises nothing more than good shooting fun, and on that front it delivers.

this quick review is based on about 1 hr of gameplay, but i feel that i have seen enough to give a proper evaluation. the game is thin on story or character development, and is more of a throwback to the old days of shooters, when u needed very little reason to shoot other than the fact that aliens r on the receiving end of it. the graphics is not groundbreaking but certainly decent, as the unreal engine does a good job with a colourful design (more so than most unreal engine games). the sounds is decent too, but the highlight for me is the gameplay, which is fast and despite the flaws, extremely entertaining and more than worth the excursion.

speaking of flaws, it should be noted that there r occasional moments which detract from the otherwise excellent action - the AI is not the best in the world, with enemies sometimes seemingly frozen for moments, and much of the difficulty if delivered through otherwise very tough and accurate enemies. on one occasion, i found limbs of enemies blown up floating in the air. however, such instances r few and far in between, and dont distract from the otherwise excellent fps action. you don't need a top-tier machine to run the game at its finest (i ran it on a fairly modest laptop config).

for any fps fan, this game is very easy for me to recommend, specially when on a sale (i bought it at 80% off at gmg), and looking at the level list i have ahead of me, the campaign does seem to be of a decent length (at least by current fps campaign standards). my major gripe is with multiplayer tho, the mode seems more than serviceable, but a lack of gamers online here is of concern, altho to b honest, i would blame the negative reviews this game has received so far on this. and its a shame, for the fps action is solid, the guns come it both the human and alien variety and r fun to play around with. the highlight for me so far was one moment when i tore a minigun off the rails, and went on a rampage, and while doing so, the protagonist starting roaring........brilliant touch.
