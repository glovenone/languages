Pretty good. More hectic than I'd like. You have to clear the board quickly because you will get attacked every so often. I'm used to taking my time with puzzle games and I find it hard to think fast in this one. But there are spells that help and armor you can buy to boost your defense. You can heal at an inn, though they are few and far between. There are one-time use only spells and also ones that you can learn in your spell book and keep permanently. You can only have 10 active spells or items (one-time use scrolls) at a time, so choose wisely.

I have to say, though, that the map is epic sized. Looks like an RPG or historical war game. The graphics are nice for a puzzle game. There is a little character that moves around on the map, but you don't see any enemies, it just goes to the puzzle screen. And no story scenes or anything like that. But there's enough to just spice up the usual freecell game and make it a bit more special.

The levels start out really short and easy but they do get harder. Also you only have one free cell in the beginning. You gain more by collecting random cards after winning battles or buying them from stores. Once you have a full deck of all suits it lets you get another free cell, then you start again collecting for your next one. Later levels have more than one set of card suits, like there might be on heart, one spade, but two diamonds and two clubs. If you hover your mouse over a town it tells you the difficulty you are facing so you can decide where you want to go and work your way up to the difficult ones. If you get into trouble you can back out, go heal, and come back to try again. I haven't figured out if the levels are random or pre-arranged. If they are random, then some of the difficulty is due to luck of the draw.

If you like free cell, you will probably like this game. It will give you a reason to play because you want to conquer the world!

It doesn't have gamepad support natively, but the mouse-heavy controls map nicely to the Steam Controller, if you've got one of those.

I'm going to recommend this game with reservations.

Overall the presentation is good and if you like freecell then it is worth buying. The variation in the number of suits and stacks, as well as being able to develop more free slots throughout the game, alters the difficulty and keeps things interesting.
I'm not sure that the rpg elements, overlaid on it, work all that well. It is an interesting idea but I think it needs to be refined. Freecell is a game that rewards taking time to think through numerous moves in advance. The game, by having attacks upon your health while playing, which essentially functions as a fancy timer, forces you to play quickly and make dumb mistakes as well as constantly interrupts your concentration. The early boards are so simple that this isn't a problem, but as the game progresses you will feel more harried. It becomes more irritating than enjoyable. At one point, I found myself having to backtrack to capital cities to rest and restore my health and mana after one or two boards. This was more a chore than anything else. Thankfully this was ultimately alleviated by being able to open up another free slot.

Still, if you like playing freecell, this is a variation that I would try, especially when it's on sale.
