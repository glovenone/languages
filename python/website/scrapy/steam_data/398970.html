What is Fasaria World Online?

About the Game:


Fasaria World Online is a strategy, building RPG style mmo that can be played through any web browser even on your tablet or phone. Posted: 14 October, 2015
A few things I want to make clear first. First, this isn't a MMO. According to their own player count ingame, there has been a max of 15 unique players online today, of which the highest concurrent number I've seen in the 3 hours I've been playing is 3 with me included. Infact, in the top 50 players list, only 20 of them are above level 1. That's saying something when it took 3 hours for me to get to level 7 while idling at least 1 hour away. It's not a Massive Multiplayer Online game, but just a regular Multiplayer Online game. Second thing I want to say is that this is very similar to mobile games like Clash of Clans. If you've enjoyed that, you'll more than likely enjoy this game.

Now, onto my review. This is a full-on browser game. When you install the game on Steam and launch it, a launcher comes up with options to run their chess game, or Fasaria World Online. Clicking on FWO launches Internet Explorer and the website itself.

I have a quick list of grievances with this game that I first want to air out.

When you first play the game, you start off with 10K money, food, and iron. These are the resources in this game that you must acquire. The only problem with this system is that gathering resources is way too simple. In the three hours I've been playing, I've probably gained over 15M of each resource and used/sold most of them. First off, you can hire workers to generate a steady source of income every tick/month/15 minutes but that isn't needed because you make a lot more money by grinding on mobs. Like a million in less than a minute.

Second, the engineering lab. If you're trying to understand why a health pack requires compressors, generators, and micro parts, well you're not alone there. Everything in the engineering lab requires those three sub resources for some strange reason. Be it the health pack, energy pack, strength upgrade, defense upgrade, health upgrade, or even energy upgrade, all of them require differing amounts of generators, compressors, and micro parts to my utter bewilderment.

The third grievance of mine is with the Assembly Factory. In the AF, you create factories to produce generators, micro parts, and compressors. Sound good? Well, not exactly. Even if you have two of each factories, you're only allowed to collect from one factory each tick. It's as if you paid a restaurant for three meals but they only allowed you to pick one meal every fifteen minute. Utterly frustrating and it feels as if the game is trying to hard to be a grind fest.

Now a few good things that I do like about this game.

First, the combat is very simple to pick up on. In fact, this reminds me of Adventure Quest, except without all the animation. Is it the best combat out there? Of course not, but it's damn well good enough considering you can also play it on mobile browsers according to the devs.

Second, I have to say that the community(although the playerbase is quite lacking) is very nice and helpful. I'd had more than a dozen questions about this game and the veteran players patiently answered all of them.

Last, it has a very high potential to be addicting. This is one of those games where you check back every 15 minutes to upgrade your base, add more troops to your army and create new factories in the Assembly Factory while collecting all your idle resources. The fact that you can log into your steam account directly on your phone's browser also adds to the fact that you can play it anywhere and at anytime.

Ultimately, I'm giving this game a thumbs up. A lot of the problems I think this game has is more or less a balancing issue with resources. Otherwise, it's a pretty fun game though lacking in the graphics department.

KEEP IN MIND: This is a browser based, strategy, building mmo. I believe the description of the game is given in the video and text. If anyone is confused about what type of game this is, I advise that you read a little bit about it before getting the game.

I've been playing this game since the legacy server when Fasaria World Online was under another title. I was a little upset when they decided to clear the database and start the game all over but now that I'm back on, I see why it was done. This version also has a lot of updates that the old version did not have that I've taken notice and like, but enough about that. I'll talk about the game in it's current state.

Fasaria World Online has a lot a features to it so I'll try to break down the basic ones but keep in mind that I won't scratch the surface of what this game has to offer as there are so many things to do that you will learn over time. This game was in development for over 2 years before they got it on steam so I've seen a lot of updates till now.

JRPG turn based fighting - You get to select a class/race that will determine the type of character you have in the game. The turn based rpg fighting portion of the game helps you level up and earn resources but the major aspect of the game will be how you build your colony, your armies and defend your investments. There is one major world and 3 moons to explore. As you level up, each new area opens up with new weapons/armor to purchase and new mobs to fight. You can also craft items and build factories to gain more parts needed for character upgrades. Very detailed system.

Building your colony - Your colony can be built over time by purchasing buildings with the resources you gain. As your colony grows, the more resources you gain with every tick (15 minutes). There is also research and construction you can do that will open up new features to the game.

Building Your Army - This is where the game shines and requires strategy and wit. You can unlock multiple troops overtime and build 3 armies. You can send your troops out to attack other players earning pvp points. As you earn pvp points your pvp medal increases and bonuses stats are added to your troops. You can make your troops a lot more powerful this way. You can only attack players around your same score level or higher so you will never be attacked by a higher level player just looking to decimate the noobs.

Clan System - I had a clan on the legacy server but haven't tested it on this one yet but it looks to have the same features. You can have up to 15 players in your clan, you can give each player ranks where they receive a badge of a sort, clan chat, email and forum etc. The best feature is the collective clan experience all members earn for their clan that ranks them against other clans. Also their is a clan achievement that once the clan level reaches, can earn crystonian crystals for all clan members just for being a member. You can use crystonion crystals to purchase stuff in the VIP Shop, no real cash needed.

Score System - The score system seems to have been updated. It seems to include all activities in the game. I noticed my score go up after performing multiple tasks. Your score updates every tick. I am assuming this as I could be wrong.

Achievements - This is an awesome addition to the game. As you earn achievements, you earn shards each day that you can craft into crystals. These crystals can be used to purchase anything in the VIP shop without having to use cash. It takes a little patience but on the legacy server I was cashing in on enough crystals every other day to purchase mega packages worth $40.00 in the shop. Like I said, no real cash is ever needed.

The VIP Shop - Speaking of the VIP Shop, I think it should remove the option to spend real money. There is no need to as you can earn more crystals, I mean a LOT more crystals just by playing the game. This is my only dislike but it's just an opinion as some players seemed to have purchased crystals so far so it serves it's purpose for some I guess.

Politics, Country and Maps - There are too many things to discuss about that so I'll just not for now. You'll learn about all of that as you play.

Chat system and community - The chat system is great and shows your user progress and even the weapons/armor you have equipped which keeps things fresh. There are a few issues with the censorship but nothing major.

Over all - Like I said, I played this game before and I mean A LOT before it reached steam. There were some good players on the old server that I hope they come back because players are what makes this game shine and without a community well, this is just "Civilizations" solo mode. Clan wars and such are what makes a game like this awesome so I do hope it finally builds a strong community.
