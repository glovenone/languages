27 April

Tales of Symphonia Patch Notes - April 27th, 2016

Hello Tales of Symphonia players! A new update is live featuring a handful of fixes. See below for the full list. And thank you again for your support of Tales of Symphonia!

Cheers,
-Bandai Namco Community Team

  • Remote Island Ranch did not allow players using Genis to successfully activate the sorcerers ring to change directions during the cart puzzle.
  • Fixed crash at Welgaia when receiving the Chipped Dagger.
  • Fixed game becoming unresponsive if game pad was unplugged during movement.
  • Audio will now save based on the last save slot loaded.
  • Sound Test should now be present on the main menu at all times if the last game a player loaded was NG+
  • Blurriness double checked to ensure there were no left over instances of FXAA. Post texture shaders were updated from linear, back to point.
  • Updated credits.
  • Various localization and corrupt text fixes throughout the game.

About This Game

THE EPIC BATTLE FOR SURVIVAL

In a dying world, legend has it that a Chosen One will one day rise from amongst the people and the land will be reborn. The line between good and evil blurs in this epic adventure where the fate of two interlocked worlds hangs in the balance.

AN EPIC ADVENTURE – Over 80 hours of gameplay in this epic, emotionally charged storyline.

REAL-TIME 3D BATTLE SYSTEM – Experience the fierce, action-packed battle system. Combine hundreds of special attacks and magic spells.

A CLASSIC ART STYLE LIVES ON – Become absorbed in endearing cel-shaded characters designed by renowned artist Kosuke Fujishima

FIRST TIME ON STEAM – Based on the PlayStation®3 port of the original GameCube game, this RPG classic is finally available for PC.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows Vista 32/64
    • Processor: Intel Core 2 Duo 2.4Ghz / AMD Athlon 64 X2 5200+, 2.6GHz
    • Memory: 1 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GeForce 8800GT / ATI Radeon HD 4830
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 7 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Gamepad support
    Recommended:
    • OS: Windows 7 (x64) / Windows 8 (x64)
    • Processor: Intel Core i3-530, 2.93 GHz / AMD Phenom II X4 940, 3.0GHz
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GeForce GTX 560 Ti / AMD Radeon HD 7850
    • Storage: 7 GB available space
    • Additional Notes: Gamepad support

One of my favorite games as a kid on gamecube, Story is awesome. Lots of plot twists and character depth and personality for a game that comes off kinda PG-rated. Still remember being awestruck when you find out who Lloyd's real father is, as well as when you find out about how both worlds affect each other. I remember the combat being a lot harder than what my current experience is, but then again, now I'm using blocking and dodging and not just jamming on the attack buttons. The combat is pretty straight forward if you've played any amount of JRPGS. Battles happen in real-time and are not turn based but the battle pauses if you bring up the in-battle item menu or if you're targeting a different enemy - which I like. Also, each weapon actually has its own unique aesthetic when you're in-battle. There are these fire/ice blades you get for Lloyd somewhere near end game and they look badass.


Look at it this way: It released on Gamecube, then they ported it to PS3, and then they ported it to PC.

Obviously this game is ♥♥♥♥ing great.

I'm writing this review for the main purpose of confirming that Co-op play is indeed possible. LOCAL Co-op. I have not tried with more than two people, and I have not tried with anything other than two controllers (no keyboard/mouse/controller combinations). I had trouble even finding confirmation of that, so I wanted to post this for anybody else who is looking for an answer on this. It's there. =)

My first Tales game was Tale of Vesperia, and although the systems and graphics have matured, Symphonia fits nicely into what I expect from a Tale JRPG. Combat is fun and real-time. Story thus far is pretty typical. Save the world, form a party, etc. Graphics do look a bit dated, but I think overhaul they hold up quite nicely.

I am happy that this support Co-op play as this has made the game more fun for me since I get to play with my GF. I recommend it from what I have played thus far.

You were my favorite game. The story gripping and cunning, yet idealistic and melodic. Gone are the days when I was able to sit down on a couch with a gamecube controller in hand. It's about the game, nothing more, nothing less. My final point on the review is this: Play this game, as what it used to be, a phenominal soul-changing game. Not for it's lack of FPS or DLC or whatever. Just enjoy the game.

Once upon a time when the Gamecube came out I was on the youngish side. I was looking for a game to play and I was recommended by the store clerk to try Tales of Symphonia because he knew I liked rpgs.
Right from the beginning I was drawn into the world and the storyline. These characters became a better part of my gaming childhood I suppose. I put so many hours into it, worked every quest to completion, every side boss and storyline. I burned through two copies of the game.
And then time wore on and I packed my Gamecube away for other consoles, but Tales of Symphonia always had a special place in my heart as my favourite game.
It was released on Playstation 3: not my console of choice but I was still grabby hands at the collectors edition and sat down once more to play through the game. It still hadn't lost it's magic and again I played the game until I lost my charger for my controllers and I couldn't find a replacement so I put it away.
And then lo and behold it was finally released for pc, the port was awful at first so I held off on buying it, but I was watching, waiting until it was patched and soon as I was able I picked it up and I've just started replaying it.

Tales of Symphonia for me is timeless, it's one of those games I can pick up and play over and over and over and it never loses it's magic. This game was a big part of my life as a youngling and seeing it here and being able to play it on my pc makes me really happy. It should've been ported a long time ago, better too because it's a game that anyone can love. You find yourself drawn into the struggles and stories of the characters and the overarching plotline. The graphics might be old, but the music and gameplay carry it well, even years later.

I've always enjoyed the combat system of the Tales series and Symphonia is no exception, though I do confess I needed to rebind a few keys it wasn't the most friendly of set ups but once you get the hang of it it's pretty easy to waltz through the battles or make it a challenge for yourself.

Either way, for someone who has played this game for hours upon hours, who bawled her eyes out when the opening music started playing, this is a game that will worm it's way into your heart with a fondness that you'll carry with you for years to come.
