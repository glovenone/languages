About This Game

SEEP Universe allows the exploration of four bizzarre planets, inspired by retro games.
Run, Jump and battle enemies through more than 20 stages for a 100% retro feeling. This is the game you have always dreamed of from the 16 bit era!
The battles against the bosses change the rules of the game. For example, in the first world you'll need to run away with a SEEP car from a huge boss.
This makes the game experience varied and intriguing!
You can also collect the SEEP Coins to unlock many secrets in the SEEPedia, a huge gallery where you can discover many secrets.

Various gameplay: platform, action, puzzle, racing and much more!
Free DLCs.

SEEP Shooter: excite two players co-op mode!
XInput and Direct Input controller support.



The Story:

A strange frequency from the videogame world has taken control of the universe. Four planets have been threatened by five obscure videogame bosses.
Each planet has its own army but this has been broken and now all the negative effects are woven together to create chaos.
SEEP, an indie developer team, has intercepted the code in the timeline that is causing the disaster. The problem seems to be the corruption of the source code of some old arcade systems, these can also teleport people from one place to another where the code is corrupted.
So the brothers Sergio and Enrico, members of SEEP, decide to go into action. Only they can save the universe by crossing through various arcade systems that have been hacked by a mysterious AI.

This game is really impressive - if it's 1985. But it's 2016. This is a poorly implemented, clunky 2D retro super mario ripoff, without the same level of endearing gameplay.

The Engrish is strong: "This game support the xinput controller" is the first thing the developers smack you in the face with. Telling you that it's not really aimed at the English market, and it's not aimed at the PC gaming market. Who's it for then? Confused Italians, is my best guess.

Graphics are terrible (it's been a long time since the NES came out - 30 years, in fact). So when your highest technological achievement is something that was surpassed 30 years ago, some introspection is badly needed.

Gameplay is weak - the only positive is the addition of co-op gaming on a single system (because apparently it's 1985 and networking protocols haven't been invented yet). It's a super mario ripoff with a push mechanic instead of a jump on head mechanic.

No reason to buy this. Better examples of this dead genre are out there, if you're that way inclined.

The heart is there, but this feels like more like something kids would code during their summer vacation than anything else. The keyboard controls can't be rebound, so if you don't like the way the coders laid it out (ASZX trying to emulate the layout of a controller), then you're screwed. Using A to run, S to jump, and X to attack just feels wrong.

Your attack extends about ten pixels, the "jumping" feels off (hard to properly explain, but easy to understand once you experience it), there's plenty of "walking dead" situations (for instance, if you run out of special attacks when you're faced with a puzzle that requires special attacks, your only option is to kill yourself and restart), at least one stolen sound effect (hi, Megaman), every time you start the game up you have to sit through a bunch of "credits" screens, every time you load the main menu you have to press F4 for keyboard - the list of little flaws goes on and on.

And, finally, $12 for this game is far, far too high a price point.

Just because you use pixel art and make references to video games doesn't mean you can counter all criticism with "oh, it's retro, you just don't get it". Sure, it's retro - but the retro games it brings to mind are Color Dreams and Sachen multi-cart games. It's not quite Action 52 bad, but it's far from Megaman and Mario - or even VICE: Project Doom or River City Ransom. And it's not really "Bad Dudes" so-cheesy-it's-good.

There's enough heart and enough well-intentioned nostalgia here that I feel like a bit of a heel for voting this down. I love the Super Mario jaggy lines at the finish line. I am not immune to the appeal of the old school. But there are no other negative reviews. And there's a lot wrong here.

The tutorial helps, but the controls are still counterintuitive. It's still ultra-awkward pressing one button to run (while moving, and provided you have enough space) and then pressing another button to jump, in order to clear obstacles. And there's no impact in collisions with enemy characters. I punch once as I'm apparently passing into an opponent, and then punch a second time while we are submerged in one another, and it seems to do the trick, but I'm left unsure if I've sustained damage. I then have to keep my eye on the top-left corner in the next fight to try to figure out if I have, and discover I haven't. This is the first time in my history playing games that I am let down that I haven't sustained damage. It just FEELS wrong.

The sound design is underdeveloped, too. When I throw a punch, I need a sound that helps me believe there's been an impact. And the controls are just slow enough to respond that the whole thing feels floaty and dreamlike. That's not a recipe for immersion for me, or for fun. Especially not when the P/R sets me up for a responsive, intuitive, satisfying old school experience.

At 2.99, and to support a couple of guys trying to get started, I can see it, but 11.99 is hard to justify. I can't in good conscience recommend this one.
