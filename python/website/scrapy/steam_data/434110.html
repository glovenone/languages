7 July

Premium Pool Tournament Update



Tournament mode
Tournament mode is now released. Tournament can be played up to 10 wins or until three losses. After each match, the reward will increase and when you reach the end of the tournament you will get all the coins you have earned so far. The maximum reward in the tournament is 10,000 coins after 10 wins.
After 10 wins or 3 losses the tournament will end, but re-entering is possible at any time. The tournament does not require to play all matches in one session. Instead the matches can be played one at a time and the tournament can be continued in the next session.

Other updates
There are also a selection of new frames available in the game store, like for example steampunk and cherry blossom frames. Finally, a set of new achievements that can be unlocked has been added.

Bug fixes in this update
- Overlapping yellow and red bars
- Double popups
- Stability and connection issues

Premium Pool Update

Notes for 6/17/2016

- Option to link mobile devices and mobile Premium Pool accounts to your Steam
- Improved aiming
- Bug fixes in UI and multiplayer

Instructions to link devices
Linking devices means that you can play the game on Steam, but also on your Android and iOS mobile devices and get all the experience and coins to your one unified account.

You can link your devices in the game settings by pressing the green button "Link devices". First create a code on one device and then input the code in the other, after a few seconds and then you can play using the same account anywhere!

Download Premium Pool for your Android and iOS devices from Google Play or from iTunes for free and play the game anytime and anywhere.

About This Game

Premium Pool is a smooth and enjoyable way to play billiards on your computer. Practice in single player against AI and then put your skills into use in a tournament or in a wide range of different custom locations online. You can also challenge your friends locally or online. In Premium Pool everything can be customized. Gather credits from your wins and change the cue, table, cloth and balls to your liking from a vast collection of beautifully designed equipment. Who wouldn’t want to play with a royally designed table on the beach? Or use one of the uniquely designed cues in a casino? Finally personalize your character by selecting one of the awesome theme characters in the game.

KEY FEATURES
  • Online multiplayer
  • Local multiplayer
  • Single player
  • Challenge your friends
  • 8 Ball
  • Tournament mode
  • Super smooth controls
  • Specially themed game rooms
  • Customize every equipment
  • Awesome set of avatars
  • Chat with your competitor
  • Option to fast forward; no need to wait all balls to settle down to continue playing
  • Achievements and collectible cards

Future updates

Regular content updates will be released for the game. In addition to new equipment and game locations there will be tournament mode, daily challenges and player ranking system. Also new game modes like 9 ball and snooker are in development.

Posted: 16 March
I got this FREE game the day it came out, as i'm a big fan of pool games. Alas, it leaves much to be desired, based on the version up to and including the patch on 16th March. See below:

PROS:

- It's fun enough to play while watching the telly, for a time, and if you've nothing else to do.
- It's free.

CONS:

- Extremely derivative: It's a pool game clone, and a poor one at that. It is outdone, not only by the majority of browser-based pool games, but also by the likes of Arcade Pool for the Amiga, which is now over 20 years old.

- Putting spin on the ball has barely any effect whasoever. This needs to be ramped up significantly. I enjoy playing pool, though i consider myself an amateur, and even i can put triple the amount of spin on the cue ball as this game allows. As such, many shots which would be easy enough in real life (and most other pool games) are impossible here.

- Only one mode of play: 8-Ball

- Poor matchmaking, which can mean waiting forever for a game, even when there was an odd number of people in the sparsely-populated rooms. I've left it running for about 8 minutes without getting a game. However, exiting out of the room and rejoining seemingly got me a game much quicker than just waiting in the queue, indicating a glitch.

- The "hit boxes" for the balls are clearly some way off. It is possible to hit an opponents ball when, very clearly, you should have hit your own ball first.

- Although the latest patch states that you should "win" the game if your opponent disconnects, this isn't the case. I've lost my stake through no fault of my own because i've had to leave a game that my opponent disconnected from.

- Sometimes, you can not pot any balls, and miraculously, it is your go again. Obviously, this is particularly annoying when it happens for your opponent.

- The physics still need some work, even after 2 or 3 hotfixes.

- The actual proper rules of 8-ball pool are not taken into consideration, e.g. at break-off, snookering behind a played balll without hitting a cushion/rail first.

- Syncing issues which can cause you to lose time through no fault of your own.

- Graphical glitching at the menu and room screens.


I don't like writing reviews like this, but the fact of the matter is that this game is outdone by just about every other pool game available, and is at best a bare-bones pre-alpha. It could end up working, but at the minute, it isn't worth recommending, even for nothing. There are plenty of other, much more worthy, free, online multiplayer pool games out there that you could be playing.
