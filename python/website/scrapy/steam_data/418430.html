About This Game

Story


Long ago the Scientist of Miolhria worked on a cure for the multiple illnesses of their time. One of these "Cures" had horrible side effects turning man into Z's. This cause Havoc and Chaos all over Miolhria.

Miolhriankind's last hope was left with these same scientist who continued to experiment with the Virus which changed so many of the Miolhrians into these Z's.

(As you can tell not all of the People of Miolhria were Genuses..)

After years of experimentation and luck a second cure was found but didn't work as they expected.
Side effect from the second "Cure" allowed some people of Miolhr to Control Elemental Skills.
These People were called Users. Elemental type(s) are Fire, Water, Poison and Ice.

(User are people with the ability to control Element Powers.)

After years of training, the Top Users created DoJo's to train
and perfected their talents.

Years later Z's stated to build immunities and split off into 3 forms.
Poison. Blazing. Earth!

This also allowed them to use these Skills.

At the same time User trained developed 2 New Skills. Physical
and Tool.
With these 6 Skills the World of Miolhria was balanced
for a short time.

DoJo leaders stared to develop Dual User abilities.
This was mostly through their training.
This gave them the ability to control 2 Elements at once.
For years DoJo leaders ruled the lands of Miolhria...

..until now...

Posted: 23 March
A 90's game in 2016? It's more likely than you think!

"Survive Me Miolhr" is an RPG based game with a zombie theme. When I first played the game, it reminded me a lot of the old 90's Pokémon. Not only does the gameplay remind me of Pokémon, but the main character looks similar to Ash. At first, I had a great nostalgic feeling. However, I was very disappointed when I encountered one of the many bugs in this game. Despite the crashes and bugs, the similarities to Pokémon kept me motivated. I continued to play, and I recommend it even with those errors.


Pros:
Any non-ancient PC can handle the game. If you want to check if you can handle the game, go to "canyourunit.com".
Good choice of sound, it is a little repetitive but it does fit the game very well.
Very nice graphics, I love the nostalgic feeling this game gives you.
I appreciate the storyline in this game. The first few hours are a bit confusing, but after this short struggle, it makes sense and is a positive addition.
Motivating achievements, very easy to unlock.
Training mode which gives you a taste of the game and teaches you how to play it.
Awesome developer, who takes suggestions into account.
This game is great for experienced players. It starts out tough, which is nice for a good challenge.
Cons:
The keyboard controls could use some fine-tuning. It would be more user-friendly to have one row of controls, instead of what it is right now.
While I like the sounds overall, there is a barking noise that is irritating. I would prefer if it was removed.
There are several bugs and crashes due to the game still being in Early Access. They are being looked at by the developer and should be fixed by the time it is fully released, so I can forgive this.
The tutorial could provide some more information on certain items, including details about keys.
There are typos in the game and on the store page. A proofreader would be helpful.
The character controls a bit stiffly, and is slower than needed. I would like the movements to be smoother.

Overall experience:

The concept of "Survive Me Miolhr" is good. Its unique flare and compelling story makes it worth playing. It has a lot to improve on, but as an Early Access game, this is to be expected. I recommend giving it a try, either in Early Access or when it is fully developed. Certainly Pokémon lovers would find something in common with it, but it has other appealing elements too.


Rating:

I rate this game 6/10 for its Early Access score. When it has been fixed up, I will bump that number up accordingly!"

Early Access Review
Posted: 17 December, 2015
NOTE: I was supplied with this key by the developer, this however did not affect the review.

Intro

Survive Me Miolhr is a RPG based on a zombie theme. It has graphics reminiscent of Nintendo DS Pokemon games, a slightly confusing sound, and gameplay that is just too confusing to make sense of.

Graphics

The graphics for this game are fine and I actually like them. However the graphics are hindered significantly by a cluttered HUD (Heads-up Display) and side menus that just take up the whole screen. It’s to the point where the user cannot even see the graphics, let alone enjoy them.

Sound

The sound is decent, it’s nothing special. The only issue I really had with the sound is that there was this an annoying barking noise that seemed to be part of the soundtrack and it only served to irritate me to the highest degree.

Gameplay

The gameplay is the weakest part of this game. It’s absolutely horrendous and atrocious to put it mildly. Basically, the game throws a storyline and gameplay information at you that is so hard to make sense of. It tries to stuff two hours of plot and gameplay tutorials into two minutes and it just does not work.

Pros & Cons

Pros:

• The graphics are really cool and brought back nostalgia of Pokemon HeartGold and SoulSilver on the Nintendo DS.
• The soundtrack isn’t too bad apart from the barking noise I described earlier.
• The story has a lot of potential.

Cons:

• The screen is too cluttered by text and menus.
• The barking noise is extremely annoying.
• The gameplay is near non-existent because it’s so hard to understand.

Overall Thoughts

This game is a disaster, just don’t even mess with it right now. However, in fairness, it is in Early Access and is self-admitting when it comes to the fact that it is far from a complete product. It has a ton of potential, it’s just up to the Developers on whether they want to make a proper foundation for that potential to flourish and blossom. As of right now, that potential is not being utilized properly.

A 90's game in 2016? It's more likely than you think!

"Survive Me Miolhr" is an RPG based game with a zombie theme. When I first played the game, it reminded me a lot of the old 90's Pokémon. Not only does the gameplay remind me of Pokémon, but the main character looks similar to Ash. At first, I had a great nostalgic feeling. However, I was very disappointed when I encountered one of the many bugs in this game. Despite the crashes and bugs, the similarities to Pokémon kept me motivated. I continued to play, and I recommend it even with those errors.


Pros:
Any non-ancient PC can handle the game. If you want to check if you can handle the game, go to "canyourunit.com".
Good choice of sound, it is a little repetitive but it does fit the game very well.
Very nice graphics, I love the nostalgic feeling this game gives you.
I appreciate the storyline in this game. The first few hours are a bit confusing, but after this short struggle, it makes sense and is a positive addition.
Motivating achievements, very easy to unlock.
Training mode which gives you a taste of the game and teaches you how to play it.
Awesome developer, who takes suggestions into account.
This game is great for experienced players. It starts out tough, which is nice for a good challenge.
Cons:
