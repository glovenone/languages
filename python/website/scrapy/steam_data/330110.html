About This Game

Wild Frontera is a top-down action shooter set in a western-fantasy world

You are a lonesome gunslinger, that will have to get rid of hordes of enemies and defeat spectacular bosses in order to escape to the frontier.

Armed with a rusty six-shooter that you can swap very quickly for new weapons, for a fistfull of dollars looted while playing the game.

Ultra fast single-action revolvers.
Long-range rifles with impressive damage.
Or, if subtlety is not your forte, devastating shotguns : deadly in close quarter combat but leaving you with a very small margin of error.

Fun, nervous and difficult, Wild Frontera offers non stop action, worthy of the best western movies, where every detail is a nod to the spaghetti genre

You will encounter stereotypical villains, outlaws, wild bloodthirsty animals and more...

You'll travel across grasslands, the darkest forests, fields of bones, deserts, a gold mine and towns in order to escape this crazy country and discover why you are now the most wanted bandito in the wild west.

Features


  • Play solo or online co-op up to 4 players
  • 8 stages with 17 boss fights
  • Mouse / keyboard and gamepad twin-stick support
  • Procedurally generated weapons
  • Online leaderboards for single player runs
  • Challenging gameplay with fast respawn checkpoint system

Set loose in the wild west as a gunslinger out for revenge, traveling towards the safety of the frontier while saving damsels in distress, looting gold and killing as many bad guys as possible. How cliche.. but FUN!
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GaC531nSO3w
Equipped with a basic pistol you slowly work through the levels collecting loot for purchasing and upgrading of weapons along with boosting base attribute points. Weapons included a range of pistols, rifles and shotguns that all had procedurally generated stats. Levels are generally segmented into arenas, clearing each area reopened invisible barriers allowing further progression until eventually facing mini-boss fights. Each boss used alternate weapons, attack patterns and insults.

Comedy wise it's quite good, bit of a shame there was no voice-work. With a decent little story it really would have added to the overall immersion but the soundtrack and artstyle are very spaghetti western and do a wonderful job on their own. Control wise both kb+mouse and gamepad was suitable and responsive, while all the keyboard controls were able to be remapped

With co-op support, decent price point and tight twin-stick western shooter action, can easily give Wild Frontera the big thumbs up. Did not have a chance to try co-op but I'm sure that would only be more enjoyable. Cheers folks. :)

My First Play on Launch Day Video + First Impressions Review:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gHkOtaQG5tM

This game offers an eye-pleasing game experience and it shows that the developers took a lot of time on the attention to detail with the levels and art-style of this game.

However, while the art style is well done, the action is reletentless and never slows down, I wish the developers had spent a little more time paying attention to the actual gameplay itself. The fluidity and responsiveness of the game seems to be lacking that of the top-notch twin stick shooter games, and it gives the game an overall little bit of sluggish feel that brings down my over-all first impressions score from what I feel it "could" have been with just a little more work.

I like that up to 4 players can play online co-op, but I think i was the first one to buy and play this game so that aspect wasn't able to be tested at the time of this early review.

The game is challenging early on, and a lot of fun, and the controls aren't horrible by any stretch, they are pretty good, but certainly not nearly as crisp and responsive as I like to see twin stick shooters like this.

With the developers stating about a 2.5 hour expected play through, and then having to wait for additional content and game modes or playing the game again at an 'extreme" difficulty level, it's hard for me to see a ton of value in this game even at the low initial price point of $9.99 USD.

Still, I believe the game is pretty fun, with pretty good (just not great) controls, and it does offer an eye-catching western beauty style that I enjoy myself. It it certainly good enough to make me wish it was just a little bit better.

My overall first impressions review based on Stage 1 play solo play... 72% (out of 100)
