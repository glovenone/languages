A fun little side-scrolling shooter with sufficient mechanics and its own personality. You use WASD to move, mouse to aim, space to jump. You know the drill.

The goods:
  • The mechanics feel pretty good for the most part.
  • Lots of ways to customize your character & play style through gear acquisition.
  • The graphics are sufficient for the game's price point and age.
  • The music and sound define the game's attitude very well and contribute to overall immersion.

The bads:
  • The enemy variety is a tad lacking and becomes a bit repetitious.
  • The game's formula doesn't really change up to much. It's either walk to the right and shoot everything with one of your two guns or jump around this little area and shoot everything with one of your two guns.
  • There's no longer a player base. This may not be an issue if you already have a group of RL friends who'll be accompanying you.
  • The humor/character/personality of this game is a bit sophomoric. It feels like it's targeted at pre-teen children. "Look, I'm drinking beer to regain health! How edgy and/or hilarious!"
  • The game is a grind. You have to defeat stages to acquire nuts to spend on gear. You have to gain levels to unlock the ability to buy the gear. Additionally, you will be replaying stages multiple times that are likely to grow stale by the end of the first time you play through them.

Overall, I do like the concept of this game and its core gameplay. However, the overall level design and variations on gameplay are really nowhere to be found, and it severely punishes the game's longevity. I think playing with friends would help in that regard, but is there any game that doesn't hold true for? I give it a 65/100. I'm really close to recommending this game; I don't think it'd take much more content to make it loads of fun given it has such a strong gameplay foundation. But as is, I don't think I'd play it again outside of the <6 hours I already spent on it.

The PC version has sharper graphics than the Xbox360 version, but they removed both local co-op play, and all gamepad support. Had I realized these two features were missing, I definitely would not have bought this game. But it didn't occur to me to actually research whether they were removing features for the PC version... I learned a lesson there.

From the devs on their forums: "Regarding gamepads - the game plays waaaay better with mouse/keyboard so we elected to not support gamepads."

The lack of local co-op means you can only play multiplayer online, despite the original game being designed around having up to four players sitting around a TV at once. That feature is probably gone due to the PC version's controls. They added a keyboard+mouse control scheme, but completely removed any option to even use a gamepad. The keyboard controls aren't especially bad or really hard to use, but for an arcade-style shooter/platformer, gamepad is what I'm always going to prefer.
