About This Game

Play as two special operatives and fight your way through a mad scientist's secret asteroid base whilst defending yourself against his army of evil mutants. The amazing brawler Cye is trained in melee combat and uses powerful gadgets of mass destruction; or out-smart the enemy with the master tactician Chrome, an agent of ranged warfare and master of AI turret defence systems.

Develop the skills of your Agent, increase their power and overcome 24 uniquely challenging levels which get harder as you progress. Will you use a “punch now, ask questions later” approach? Or are you a tactical genius that tricks the enemy into bottle necks, melting them down with a devastating turret barrage?

Play solo, or draft in a loyal friend for epic Co-op action. Use everything at your disposal in order to eliminate the mutant army and put a stop to their evil before it spreads across the universe.

Final Game Features

24 Scientific Labs to Dominate and 3 Environments to Conquer – Hack the power cells in 24 action packed levels and take over the mad scientist’s asteroid base.

14 Powers and Turrets – Combining your strategic mind with a Brawling skill set you will have to use everything at your disposal to defeat the waves of mutants.

Over 200 upgrades for each character – Increase your range, power up your damage, or upgrade your AoE radius to better dispose of the vile mutants.

17 Unique Enemies – Defend against mutant Wasps, Scorpions with blasters, exploding Ticks and the titanic Crusher Bugs.

3 Challenging Bosses – Face off against the most destructive scientific experiments created by the mad scientist

Replayability – Unlock achievements and star related challenges through every level, adding extensive replayability and bragging rights!

Early Access Review
Posted: 8 June, 2014
ZAMB! Biomutant Extermination is an interesting concept of tower defence and isometric brawler or shooter mechanics thrown into one, with a whacky and vibrant comical theme to it. The game is currently said to contain around a third of all content that will be available on the full release according to the developer, but it is highly playable and feels fairly complete outside of the lack of levels.

However, it has some serious tightening up to do in terms of the mechanics, because while it presents a fantastic idea, it simply becomes a chore to play due to the poor combat and frustrating AI. I certainly recommend ZAMB! Biomutant Extermination as something you keep your eye on, because what is there is fairly promising, but from what I’ve seen, it’s nothing more than promise due to its fundamental technical issues. If they were improved, I would have a much easier time recommending it, but as it stands, I’d say hold off until significant improvements are made.

You can play it in alone or with a friend online (I couln’t wait for matchmaking so I played solo). In solo mode gameplay is shared with an AI. The AI intelligence is basic but fortunately won’t always just run to it’s death.

Graphics and Sound nothing spectacular but not bad

You take control of one or alternate between two characters. One character is a melee caster. While the other character has a ranged attack and places turrets. You unlock and upgrade powers and turrets as you go.

Surprisingly I found that from a relatively early stage the gameplay can be a challenging. You need to simultaneously defend multiple targets in different locations from waves of enemies. Which requires strategy. Combination of turret types and placement matters.

It’s fun. You can probably get a substantial amount stimulating game time out it. I’ll be optimistic and say it’ probably enough to make it worth the price.
