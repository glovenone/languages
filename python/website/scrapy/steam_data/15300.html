About This Game

Eastern Europe, 2008. War has broken out on the borders of Russia and the fate of the world hangs in the balance. That's when the call goes out for the Ghostsan elite handful of specially trained Green Berets, armed with the latest technology and trained to use the deadliest weapons. Their mission: Spearhead the way for a NATO peacekeeping force, and keep the lid on the conflict before it mushrooms... literally.
  • All the realism, sweat, and fear from the Game of The Year: Award-winning tension and deadly realism on tomorrow's battlefields.
  • Multiplayer: Online or LAN multiplayer with up to 36 players.
  • Weapons: Standard and special-issue weapons like the M16A2, the M4 carbine, and the fearsome and deadly OICW.
  • Gameplay: The total combat experience that teaches a deadly, realistic lesson in how to fight on tomorrow's battlefields.
  • Includes: 23 single-player missions, 11 multiplayer maps, and six multiplayer modes.

System Requirements

    • OS: Windows® 2000/XP (only)
    • Processor: 450 MHz Pentium® 2 or equivalent
    • Memory: 128 MB
    • Graphics: 16 MB DirectX®-compliant 3D card (see supported list*)
    • DirectX Version: DirectX 8.0 or higher (included)
    • Sound: DirectX 8-compliant sound card
    • Hard Drive: 1 GB
    • *Supported Video Cards at Time of Release: ATI® Rage 128 and RADEON® chips, Intel® 82810 or higher, Matrox Parhelia™, NVIDIA® TNT2 and GeForce™ chips, S3 Pro Savage and UniChrome
    • Laptop versions of these chipsets may work but are not supported. These chipsets are currently the only ones that will run this game.

Ghost Recon still holds up to this day with its non-cumbersome, accessible gameplay and immaculate interface; yet uncompromising in its tactical essence. The attention to detail is present in the story, environments, animations, and artificial intelligence. Movement and weapon handling feel tactile and make sense. The strategic aspect of this game works well, allowing you to control up to 3 teams in real time. You are able to navigate your forces throughout each mission environment and adjust their rules of engagement. Enemy A.I. is generally quite smart and is fully capable of eliminating the player and their soldiers. They will flank when you least expect it, toss grenades at you, take cover, suppress you, assume better positions, etc.

Most missions in the 15 part sequence of events - based around Russian ultranationalism - truly feel like Special Forces military operations that would occur in reality. Most important is that you feel involved, and that these missions you are carrying out are only a small contribution to a much larger war effort. Ghost Recon allows you to choose 6 or less Green Berets and/or specialists of various backgrounds (military and ethnic) per mission, either rifleman, support, demolition or sniper. These soldiers can be upgraded upon finishing a mission in the fields of team leadership, weapon control, stealth, and physical endurance. Your A.I. companions generally adapt to situations accordingly, meaning that they will take cover and return fire when necessary, or take a better position. So you don't have to worry about Bravo team on the opposite side of the map.

Now, after a long 10+ years of not playing co-op, I am happy to report that emulating LAN with Hamachi works perfectly and is very, very easy to do. Co-op is great fun (obviously,) and if you cannot handle the wrath of the big boys in Eastern Europe with a buddy, this game allows 7 A.I. soldiers to aid you in accomplishing your objectives. The two expansion packs both contain an 8 mission campaign, set in their respective locations. These are well worth 5 bucks each and I recommend those as well. Owning the expansions opens up the door to an incredible modding scene, which requires a whole new topic of discussion.

In the end, Ghost Recon hasn't aged a day and represents the meridian of a low complexity/ high depth game design mentality. The game feels and runs great on modern machines. GR strikes the perfect balance of military simulator and first-person shooter; stripping away the unwieldy mechanics of games such as Operation Flashpoint: Cold War Crisis and ARMA, thus presenting a more polished military tactical shooter (that means fun.) This Tom Clancy series debut remains as intense and immersive as it was when I played it over a decade ago. Ghost Recon is authentic, memorable and brilliant. The pinnacle of squad-based battlefield combat. For anyone seeking the real deal, look no further.

Gameplay
-No regenerating health.
-One shot, one kill difficulty. Occasionally you may get wounded.
-Frag grenades have a good 20 foot kill zone.
-Rocket launchers have realistic back blast that can kill/wound you and your squad.
-Rockets/demo charges required to destroy armor. (Not bullets/grenades)
-Objectives that require specific equipment to complete.
-Missions areas are very large. Learn by trial and error.
-All maps can be played in adversarial or coop modes.
Graphics
-Supports 1080p resolution in game. (Game menu does not)
-Game says it caps out at 32fps but actually runs at 60fps.
-Game is from 2001 so don't expect crazy good textures.

Recommendation?
If you buy this game I recommend Desert Siege and Island Thunder expansion packs since they are also on steam.
Also do yourself a favor and download the Heroes Unleashed mod from here.
(http://www.moddb.com/mods/heroes-unleashed)
When you're all said and done the game has about 175 coop missions to play. I have no idea how many weapons.
If you want to use a controller I recommend Anti-micro. (https://github.com/Ryochan7/antimicro)

Highly recommend the game for any fan of the tactical FPS.
