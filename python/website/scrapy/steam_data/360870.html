About This Game

Ultimate Tic-Tac-Toe takes the classic game of Tic-Tac-Toe and adds a strategic layer to it.

Description


  • Each square of the 3x3 game board contains another, smaller, 3x3 game board.
  • Where you make your move in a square of any small board, you send the opponent in the respective square of the big board.
  • 3 in a row in a small board wins the small board and the big square.
  • 3 small boards in a row wins the game.
  • Strategize your play, plan your next move, let the opponent win some small boards, while you win the game!
  • On the Forget game subtype, each 4th move of the same player in a board will erase his oldest move.

Features

  • Classic and ultimate tic-tac-toe games.
  • Each of the classic and ultimate types can also be played in Forget mode.
  • Single-player.
  • Hot-seat, a multiplayer mode from the same PC.
  • Multiplayer, through LAN or Steam.
  • 3 levels of difficulty.
  • Rankings: play with users closer to your skill level.
  • Leaderboards.
  • Achievements.

Gameplay

  • When the game starts, the player with the X piece will move first, and can place his piece wherever he wants. To let the opponent (AI or friend) place the first piece, you can choose to play with O. Placing the first piece will not give the player such a big advantage as in the classic tic-tac-toe game, he still needs to create a strategy in order to defeat you.
  • Next, your opponent will have to play the board respective to the square that you've just filled.
  • Your next move will be in a board that your opponent sends you to, given by the square that he just made his move on.
  • What happens if the opponent sends you to an already won or draw board? Then you can go wherever you like, but be sure not to send your opponent to a closed board, because he would have the same advantage.
  • After playing a little, you will see that, even though you could win a small board easily, the fact that if the move you make would put your opponent at an advantage, you could chose to not win that board at the time, or perhaps forfeit it, if it will give you an advantage to complete other 3 boards in a row and win the match.
  • A draw board will not count for either X, nor O.
  • You can also play on the Forget mode, which erases your 4th oldest game piece, thus having another strategic element which you must focus on, and there won't be any boards that end-up in ties.
  • When playing against the AI, you have 3 choices of difficulty:
    • Easy: The AI tries to send you to boards where it's most difficult for you to make a move, even though he could win others.
    • Medium: The AI will always try to win small boards.
    • Hard: The AI will win small boards, if it doesn't give you an advantage on the next move; it tries to create a balance between winning and not giving you the upper hand. It also has a random element to it, so it provides more diversified plays overtime.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7
    • Processor: 800 MHz
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: 32 MB
    • DirectX: Version 11
    • Storage: 4 MB available space
    • Sound Card: Audio output capability

Bullet points
  • A legitimately-strategic version of Tic-Tac-Toe
  • Contains traditional Tic-Tac-Toe
  • Local multiplayer (hot seat)
Full review

Well I'll be; somebody actually made a version of Tic-Tac-Toe that's legitimately strategic and challenging to play against a skilled opponent. And the graphics are nice, too; I mean, it's not Unreal Engine 4 but for what the game is, it's great.

First off, to address the obvious question, yes the game contains traditional Tic-Tac-Toe if you want a little nostalgia; the game refers to it as "Classic" and it's just as boring as it ever was, although this time you get realistic chalkboard sounds to yawn along with. The fun starts when you try "Classic Forget" mode, though. Now you've got the same rules, but you only get to keep three moves at a time. That's right, starting on move #4 your oldest move gets removed from the board. I'd read the description but I kinda forgot about it as I twiddled with Classic for a bit, then played it, did three moves, whatever, then did the fourth one and saw my first move disappear and proceeded to spend the next 30 seconds staring at the screen, planning my strategy, at which point I stopped and smiled because I realized I'd just spent the last 30 seconds thinking hard about Tic-Tac-Toe strategy.

The titular "Ultimate" mode is where it's at, though. While the instructions on how to play left a little to be desired, the actual gameplay is easy to grasp once you realize what's happening. I'm not going to recreate the store page here, but in a nutshell the game takes the same basic ruleset of Tic-Tac-Toe and adds a meta layer on top of it, and you really will have to think to win.

There's also "Ultimate Forget" with the same difference as the classic modes (starting on the fourth move, every new move you make simultaneously erases your oldest existing move), but honestly that just seems like too much to keep track of; if you're looking for the Dark Souls of Tic-Tac-Toe, though, there you go.

So, 0.5 hours might not sound like a lot of play time to be making a review, but in this case it's justified. The gameplay can be complex (especially if you're playing "Ultimate Forget" mode), but understanding the rules doesn't take that long.

At the end of the day, if you've got a warm fuzzy spot for Tic-Tac-Toe and you're intrigued by the idea of a grown-up version, you should definitely check this out. It's got online and local (hot seat) multiplayer, and I'm thinking you could probably get a little mileage out of it as a party game with a bunch of people playing as a team against the computer...it's deceptive because you look at it and it looks like Tic-Tac-Toe and you're like whatever, this is easy, and then the AI beats you down.

If you like this game, check out...
  • Egyptian Senet
  • Simply Chess
  • Tabletop Simulator
