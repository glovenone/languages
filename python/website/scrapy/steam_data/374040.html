10 things Portal Knights does better than other vox based games:

1. The game has LAN: why is this great? Because it means the game will never go microtransactions pay to play like Creativerse (and now many other early access games similiar to this). LAN is a big feature and they would have to remove it entirely to go that route. A very good safety insurance.

2. Better graphics: as you will see mentioned A LOT, the graphics and art in this are by far the greatest I have seen in any of these new vox games

3. Complete trees: about time someone added complete trees/bushes so that you don't end up with floating trees that don't have any more trunks because you removed the trunk for wood. The only problem is the lack of any LARGE trees.

4. Lock on combat with ranged weapons and classes: this is by far the greatest combat system in any vox game around. However, I have not noticed a "sprint" option with stamina, this needs to happen...

5. Unique character creation: really tired of playing as some lame kid or whatever, this is also the greatest character creation I have seen in any vox game. However, it does/is slightly "childish" and could use a tad more "grown up" spin/option.

6. Bosses: most vox games don't even have a single boss. Terraria should be what we strive for. Portal Knights has a great start.

7. Unique monsters: this one actually suprised me, some of the enemies moves and such I was very pleased, could get better but even in current state it crushes games like Creativerse.

8. The UI is large!: seriously, thank you! Playing on my 52 inch TV and some games the UI and words are unreadable! The UI is great in this game, albeit very lacking (even more so than games like Creativerse) in content- ie. very few things to build.

9. RPG elements: a reason to kill things and get better! Awesome! However I haven't played long and have already leveled up to 5-10 ish out of I think 30. Currently in place but once again, seem to be designed for a child? Wish that it would "evolve" into something a tad bit more serious, or at least "middle of the road".

10. Kids friendly: now this is my #10 for pro as well as con, take it or leave it the game currently is a LOT easier than other vox based games and seems to be designed in a way that an eight year old could play...

10 things Portal Knight fails at vs other vox based games:

1. Terrible item management: inventories are brutally small with the first chest you can make needing cooper to make which isn't even found on your starting island. Once you build some chests you find that item stacking is ridiculously small (in games like this items need to stack to 250/500/1000, not 20) and there is not "auto sort" or "linked storage". These are all huge fails.

2. Small Islands: you can't dig, you will fall and die. You can't explore, you will fall off the edge and die. Worlds are the smallest I have seen in any vox game, and with only island exploration (a huge part of vox based games) you are punished for exploring. In a game that should foster exploration and immagination this is a huge fail.

3. Building is difficult: seriously, it is way too difficult to place blocks! I don't think I have played ANY vox based game where it has been this difficult to build! Hard to build, easy to fall off ledges, small inventories and stacks, the list goes on. The building in this game really takes a back stage in this game.

4. Worlds are dead: no water, no rain, no weather, creatures seems to have "spawn points" where they spawn in the same spots everynight/day and just "run around in circles", skies are empty with no huge purple moon at night or a blazing sun during the day. The world (islands) seem very much dead.

5. Terrible loot/repair monster: with Zelda and Terraria being two of the games they wanted this to be like, the loot/gear is terrible. Pick Terraria, Minecraft OR Zelda, the loor and gear set up is better. Personally I would rather have a Dungeon Defender (the first) gear set up. They opted to go with the "durability monster" in this game and let me tell you, it is a monster! You will shoot off a few arrows and RED ITEM DURABILITY! Zelda/Terraria didn't have this feature, it is frankly a stupid feature and should just "go away".

6. No tower defense or threats: Terraria/Minecraft etc, when you start you feel a real panic, a sense of "I have to build some shelter!" because things were coming after you. Now some games like Terraria MASTER this in having waves of enemies hit you, having zombies all of the sudden be able to open doors, ect, while other like Minecraft at least get your feet wet before you basically just ignore it once you get something going- Portal Knights has nothing. There is no reason to build other than "you feel like it". Now this is fine and some people will defend this but that doesn't make it "great" either, in fact it does the opposite.

7. No real reason to progress: I have no idea why I am here or why I need to go from island to island. There are no enemies coming after me, why bother? There is no storyline to bring me there, so why bother? If the claim is "meh, just do what you want to!" well then the question back should be "well then why would I do it in Portal Knights when ____ does it better and/or has more?". In open world games like Skyrim if you "wander around" you still feel rewarded for doing so, with quests, loot, exploration and awe while here you feel punished for exploring as the game funnels you and pushes you to go from island to island to island so fast that you basically just ignore 85% of a VERY small island.

8. Lacking features: no PVP, no controller support, no key remapping, etc. There seems to be a lot of little features that seem to come "standard" in other games that are just not here.

9. Toxic community: seriously, this is an issue in 100% of the sandbox/vox based communities- it might be because most of the community is/are 12 year olds who shouldn't even be allowed to comment in the forums but it is always there. This is a huge problem for any early access, because if people don't feel like they can go into the forums and give their advice without being trolled/attacked/flamed/harassed then what is the point in taking part in the early access experience? Dev's need to start clamping down on explosive fan boi's, but they don't, if you are unlucky you'll get dev's coming in and patting the crazies on the back! Currently haven't seen that in PK, but I also have seen a lot of people attacked for suggesting ANYTHING and silence from the dev's. Dev's need to be a LOT more active in their forums for an early access title!

10. Too kid friendly: now this is my #10 for pro as well as con, take it or leave it the game currently is a LOT easier than other vox based games and seems to be designed in a way that an eight year old could play...


BOTTOM LINE: It is still in early access so many of the con's can be changed and impoved on so I give it a rcommend; if the game was not in early access it would be a different story.
