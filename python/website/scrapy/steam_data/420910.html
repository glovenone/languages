Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“I am just one guy. Sure I have plenty of real world experience in the cellular sales industry to make this as accurate as possible. But, I want to build this game along side the community, getting input from the users on what they want to see and what they think will make the game a better playing experience. Because ultimately, its the player who should shape the game into what it becomes.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“I will hope to be out of early access by the summer of next year.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Planned features not currently in the Early Access build:

Store Upgrades
New Locations
Multi-Store Management

Obviously there is going to be more than what is listed, but those are the heavy-hitters. These are the main parts that still need to be added before I will deem this game completed. Things are subject to change as the community is going to play a big role on what they want to see.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current state of Early Access is managing all the aspects of one store. You can hire 4 employees, manage their stress levels, breaks and happiness. You maintain the inventory levels, ordering new devices each day (or however often you please) and set their prices. Marketing the store has 6 different options you can do on a daily basis. There is also a slew of different reports ready including daily and lifetime stats of your store as a whole, each individual employee and each individual device. There are also reports on a Marketing Score that your store is given as well as individual stress reports for each of your employees.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game will be priced differently once out of Early Access. It will go up in price, final amount is not known as of yet.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“I plan on heavily involving the community. I am going to frequent the Steam forums for the game, I have already set up separate forums on the game's official site, as well as a subreddit. I plan on going to the community for bug reports as well as feature requests for future builds.”

About This Game



Store Manager Cellular Edition will be the most comprehensive and accurate retail store simulator ever created. As an ex- store, sales, and district manager in the cellular world myself, as well as being best friends with a current store manager, I feel I am right at home when making this game.



This game incorporates all the actions a store manager has to do. Find employees by searching through resumes. Each resume is randomly generated with names, old jobs, education and more. Each one of these individually affects that employee's "ability skill". That skill is much needed in many situations. If a customer comes in knowing what phone they want and you have it in stock, a salesman with a higher ability will be able to sell them on the accessories they need, driving up the profits on that sale. If you don't have a phone in inventory, a higher ability is going to be needed to persuade a customer into a phone you do have in stock, and ensure they are going to keep it without returning it later.



You have full control over the inventory! Every day you must order new phones to keep your inventory fresh for the customers! A new phone comes out that's a dud? Well you better discount it to get it off the shelves! As it stands right now there are 16 different phones for customers to choose, all from 8 different manufacturers divided up into three different OSs. There are accessories made for each phone and the player can decide how hard they would like their game to be. The player can choose to control all accessories down to which ones product by product, or if they don't like that amount of micromanagement they can choose to instead just buy a bulk of accessories that work for every phone.



The customers come into the store, most with an idea of the type of phone they want. Customers can have their mind changed on what to purchase, but if they don't like it they will come back and return their purchase. Each customer has their own unique name and data to keep track of. Keep those customers happy and they will tell their friends about your location. Other customer traffic is based on advertising campaigns that you as manager must select. Peak customer hours change frequently so you better be on top of the schedule.



In the beginning you are running a small shop that can hold four employees. Two of them can be working at a time. There is one opening shift and one closing shift. Don't neglect your workers by not scheduling them, and on the contrary don't over work them. Doing these things might get them fed up just enough to quit! You must schedule your employees correctly from the time they come in, to the time they break, to the time they leave. But be careful! Schedule breaks during peak customer times and the employee might not get to break that day, resulting in a fine for you!



As you gain profits it is up to you to upgrade all the displays and looks around the store to drive up customer ratings, giving more free word of mouth advertising which drives up revenue! You can upgrade individual displays as well as things like the desks up front your employees stand behind, the flooring and more! This isn't all for aesthetics. Customers like clean and modern stores! The better it looks, the more money they might be willing to spend! Gain enough money and maybe you can even move into that new huge retail space they just built across town! Room for even more employees and customers!



This game will be available for the PC, Mac OSX to start with a Linux build coming shortly. The Linux build is completed, I just don't have the proper tools to test it thoroughly. It will be happening soon!

Early Access Review
Posted: 20 June
tl;dr : I'd recommend this title after it releases and receives more content and polish. For now, you would probably be dissapointed with paying $7 for this game.

----
As the owner and manager of a small cellphone retail store, you manage your stock and employees through a crude UI until you are making more money than you are losing. Every day you select what forms of advertising you wish to pursue for that day and who you will employ. Then every evening you select how much inventory to add on the following morning.

I skipped the tutorial and quickly skipped all the brief-explanations this game offered me before allowing me to start my first day. I know I missed some details, but I felt happier about my success within the first week without having read any of the tutorial. To me, this game responds intuitively and that is good game design (although I can't tell if it was accidental or intentional still). I had quickly spent all my intial income making sure I carried as much variety in stock as I could and then decided to take out a $10k loan to further improve my stock. Availability is key to customer satisfaction - you can't sell what you don't have to sell. Plus it made customer calls easy to respond to when they asked for particular products (especially since, again, I rushed through everything and barely studied all the different product names and honestly didn't know if I actually had the product or not on the first customer call). By mixing around with newspaper and radio and TV ads and hiring employees by word-of-mouth, I was able to payback the bank loan and start expanding my store within the first week... which is where the Early Access aspect comes in. It seems you can't actually upgrade your store yet as all the buttons say "coming soon."

I suspect the difficulty ramps up as you continue to play and expand. I would start paying more attention to the employees and products as the business started to grow at a rate where I really needed to maximize the profit margins with the details, however I think the intuitive and easy gameplay I witnissed during the first week is an excellent way to ease the player into the game mechanics without spoiling them with a tutorial. I could, however, see where some explanations during progression would be good for players unfamiliar with retail management. Considering it is Early-Access with active development and some promising progress, I'd recommend this title after it releases and receives more content and polish. For now, you would probably be dissapointed with paying $7 for this game.

P.S. - More dynamic and interesting than AdVenture Capitalist, though not at all the same genre. Adventure Capitalist you can't ever fail at (it's like the complete opposite of Tetris) while this one you can either profit or sink.

Early Access Review
Posted: 26 May
At this point, I think that the game is rather dull and uninspiring in its current state.

You know when you have this great idea you love, but people end up hating it? The ingame UI dressed in a cell phone is just that. Tedious, clumsy, unresponsive, no feedback, inaccurate, annoying eventually. Everything is mouse driven, not even F1, F2, F3, F4 to set the game speed, which also resets every time your phone rings. More hotkeys for keyboard driven control would be nice.

That's all you do during the day, customers most commonly call to ask if something is in stock, or to complain; to which you either aplogise or give them free stuff. All in-game related data is displayed on virtual sheets of paper, that you can't drag around, to have the more important ones side by side.

"[...] the most comprehensive and accurate retail store simulator ever created [...]" has shallow mechanics, a small static table of items to stock up on and sell, with no real market research at hand, and full of fantasy quantities of money / prices.

Employee salary seems to range from $400 to $700 daily, that's $12,000 to $21,000 a month, or $144,000 to $252,000 annually. Yet, your employees are so unhappy, they quit every couple of days. Meeting with your employee to ask how they feel, does cost you $500... wait, what for? A little stress relief for $500?

And I can't get over the hiring process, especially when you find people through word of mouth and like none of them; the game repeatedly reminds you with an extra message box, that you'll lose the roster and the money you spend on job ads. Sigh.

There's fantasy prices for marketing, too - $100 cold calling, $250 newspaper ad (one expensive newspaper), $500 for some sign flipper, $1000 for a radio ad, $1500 for a networking seminar (don't as me how that's advertisement), $2000 for a TV ad.

But of course you're not allowed to spend ANY money on marketing unless you end up with $2,000 or more at the end of the day, because you're "too close to the red". How does that make sense? Who owns this business, the developer, or the player? Who is he to tell you how and when to spend your money?

You can have at most 2 employees working in the store, although you can hire up to 4. Using the 2 spare ones gives your 2 main ones a stress relief, so they're less likely to quit their $144,000+ anual salary job. Break room upgrades are not yet implemented. You will not be able to serve customers yourself when things get busy. You'll sit at your desk all day long, answering your annyoing cell phone.

I can't see how this game would live up to its promise of being the most comprehensive and accurate any time soon. I've played Tycoon games 20+ years old with deeper mechanics.

- More control over stock levels/inventory position
- Increased analytics around the "market"
- Weekly cycles/opening times

If we start to see some of these things or other things that increase the depth and challenge to the game, this review will easily become a positive one. I'll return to cover it again if a significant update is made.

Early Access Review
Posted: 9 December, 2015
Not bad, there's just not enough here in its infancy (v0.0.1) to recommend.

You start off with $10,000 and and empty store. You have to staff the store and order inventory to sell. Since there's a lot of phones and you don't have much money to start with, most of your customers will leave empty handed because you don't have their ideal phone or their 2nd choice. The game provides statistics for you to see the breakdown of all the models and their demand, allowing you to manage your stock with your limited funds more effectively. Once revenue is in the green things are easy. You'll have money for experienced staff to handle customers faster, marketing campaigns to get more in the door, and enough stock to satisfy those crazy days where every seems to want one particular phone.

And... that's about it for the moment. I got to the point where with good employees and top-tier marketing I was selling about 40 phones per day, netting around 15-20 grand in profit daily. The only thing you can do with money besides putting it into inventory, marketing, and staff are upgrades. At the moment you can put demo models on the tables or set up displays to advertise particular brands. My store already had a high satisfaction rating and setting up demo models didn't seem to get more customers in my store. I set up four Kiwi yPhone displays and sales were roughly the same; Some days I sold mostly "Robot" phones, on others I mostly yPhones.

I had a look at the developer's site and there's some pretty neat ideas planned for the future like the possibility of thefts, but for now there's less to it than a typical lemonade stand game.
