About This Game

Mars, 2044. The UN establishes MARSCORP (Martian Security and Economics Committee) under the leadership of Samuel Longwood. In the years that follow, the UN gradually loses control of MARSCORP, mainly due to the non-transparent licence policy of its director. Longwood uses his autonomous status to create vassal relations between the corporations and MARSCORP. The unshakable power position of MARSCORP is supported by the companies and thus they acquire additional rights and licenses. Longwood and his consortium become extremely wealthy from this arrangement. However, dissenting voices in MARSCORP begin to challenge Samuel Longwood's authoritarian policy. They demand his dismissal as director and a complete redistribution of the mining rights.

But Samuel Longwood keeps an ace up his sleeve for occasions like thisâ¦

Chaser awakens on board the spaceship H.M.S. Majestic. He doesn't know who he is, what he's doing here, why he is being sought by men in black uniforms and what they will do to him when they find himâ¦

The player's goal is to find out who Chaser really is and why he is being hunted. The story leads the player from the H.M.S. Majestic to Montack City on Earth, then on to Siberia and finally to Mars, where the trail ends and hopefully the truth can be found.


Key features:

  • Based on Cauldron's cloakNT engine. Boasts state of the art graphical features and is perfectly suited for rendering large environments.
  • Authentic weapons: Nearly all the weapons in the game are modelled on real-life equivalents. The developers placed a great deal of emphasis on building detailed replicas.
  • Engaging storyline: In the search for his identity, Chaser becomes involved in a plot full of twists and turns.
  • Highly developed A.I.
  • Multiplayer support via GameSpy as well as dedicated servers.
  • Numerous multiplayer modes including teamplay modes in the style of Counter-Strike and Team Fortress as well as deathmatch and capture the flag.

I played through the entire game.
Thought it would be a nice retro-thingy 'cause I grew up with these kinda games.
Glad I missed out on this one...oops .. there is my opinion in a nutshell.

Gameplay :
--
Movement :
Responsive and great for what it wants to be.
The jump is a bit crap, but there is crouch-jumping to get you over most obstacles.
Found it a bit to easy to get stuck on level geometry, especially with slanted/pointy bits.
--
Combat :
Nice veriaty of guns. There is a gun design for each environment.
Kept me wondering what the next new gun would look like.
Some of them felt very weak compaired to whenever an AI was using one.
Hitdetection is amazingly accurate. You can shoot through missing pixels if your good enuff.
--
Puzzles :
Well, MAZE..... thats kind of it.
Ther were a few that needed a bit of memory, but mostly just maze.
Objectives could sometimes have been better worded though.
"find the missing part to fix the truck" ... that is great, but what part am I looking for?
--
Difficutly:
I played it on normal.
'cause that is the standard the game is made with.
The AI can be really stupid, or wait to set on a pre-determent path.
I know its from 2003, but Kingpin from 1999 had better AI then this.

Also the further you get the more OP the weapons get in AI hands.
Surviving 5 grenades to the face while I cant take a small splashdamage then on 100% health. F-off.
Take an entire clip of OP gun-vaporize-everything, but I cant survive a single shotgun bullet?
Maybe because my character is'nt wearing a helmet... I dunno.
It's probably balanced with the aiming skill of the AI.
That sometimes cant seem hit a freak'n planet, even it there on that same planet.
--
Levels:
--
Graphics :
Its a game from 2003 so keep that in mind.
Some textures just dont age well.
Even if you set it all your setting to max, some bits will just look like crap.
--
Level design :
I'm a level designer myself so I did see where the designers of this game where going with it.
With the limited stuff they got, they did allot. Lots of veriety, combined with changing gameplay.
I was generaly impressed....untill the later levels.

When the levels where starting to get bigger, and more confusing, getting lost is way to easy.
Repeating textures dont work in your favour either when trying to find where to go next.
There is a logic to making levels like: a prison, railsystem, or an underwater level.
I found myself inside one of the bigger levels just getting bored stupid.
I know the scale of the world is a big thing of this game, but damn.

Consistancy with level boundaries would have been nice aswell.
Invisible wall here, but a solid walk into death there?
And in the next level that does'nt kill me? *facepalm*

Also the hitboxes of some environmental object can really mess up your day.
--
Atmosphere :
Nicely done.
Every location felt the way it was suppost to be.
From isolation in a space station to freezing your butt off.
Event the main menu's music has nice little touches that made me intregued.
--
Story :
--
Plot :
Interesting.
Your imagination can go buckwild with the little scraps of story it throws at you every now an then.
Lots of potential
To bad it does'nt deliver. I could not be more disapointed when the credits started to roll.
Everything is resolved with a bit dialog dump in the end. +endings without closure are crap.
--
Acting :
The acting in the cutscenes was for a 2003 game very nice.
And then my character started to talk.
Like the voice-actor was in a differend studio, another timezone, not located in this universe.
I kinda expecting my character to somewhere go : "Yes my fellow hU-mAns. You want to go skateboards?".
The rest of the cast was fine. They sounded inversted in what was going on.
I liked that. Made me feel invested in wat was going on.
--
Overall :
--
The game sets out to be something, and 80% of the time it get that thing right.
Even though I might be disappointed with it, I cannot say its a bad game.
Every game has high and low points, and we mostly remember the low points.
Like getting lost for 20 min, or waiting for a train your riding to finaly get wherever.
Looking back I had fun with this game, but lacked a few vital points that could have made it great.
I just had a way to many : OMG why?!!?! - moments in this game.
Lot of potential wasted, in my opinion.
