Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“To obtain game-play feedback in order to improve the game (e.g. balancing). Multiplayer is implemented but lacking parts (e.g. avatars, messaging). Ditto for manual, tutorial and modding instructions (severely lacking).”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“About 4-12 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Please be warned that documentation is lacking. In general, here's the plan:
  • Implementation of feature requests by players (i.e. you guys!).
  • Game-Play: balancing, adding/removing features.
  • Improvement of the AI (currently weak).
  • Maps: additional maps such as Punic Wars I-III, Random Map generator etc.
  • Multiplayer: additional features such as custom avatars, messaging, player profiles and clans.
  • Improve the tutorial.
  • Update for graphics & sound FX.
  • Finish the manual.
  • Improve the modding Wiki.
  • Complete all translations.
  • Adding Trading Cards.
  • Additional Achievements.
  • Update server infrastructure (e.g. for multiplayer) based on needs.
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“It's fully playable including multiplayer games.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“All in-game purchases are up to -80% OFF during Early Access and will be at full price after release.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Please post suggestions, problems and bug reports here in Steam or in the official forums.”

About This Game

Demise of Nations is a 4X turn-based grand strategy wargame during the rise and fall of the Roman Empire. Command your armies in one of the many ancient countries including the Roman Empire, the Greeks, Carthage, Sparta or the Gaul. From Rome to Celtic nations, you create your own warring experience. Wage colossal wars solo, against the AI, or take on your gaming friends in cross-platform multiplayer games. Form alliances and fight co-op style with the AI and other players for ultimate victory.

Range of functionality is comprised of trading, diplomacy, expansion, conducting research, exploration, managing resources, building, food distribution, governing and city management. Units at your disposal are Scouts, Swordsmen, Spearmen, Archers, War Elephants, Galleys and Longboats amongst others. Demise of Nations incorporates changing weather patterns such as shifting climates and storms. Rain during summer providing much needed humidity for your crops, while snowy winters will proof if you are ready for the challenge. Assemble your armies and take on the world and achieve greatness in this epic historical strategy game.

  • Turn-Based Grand Strategy during the Rise & Fall of Rome.
  • 4X Strategy: eXplore, eXpand, eXploit, and eXterminate.
  • Challenging AI based on Genetic Algorithm.
  • Weather and Seasons including Snow, Rain and Storms.
  • Diplomacy, Research, Trade and City-Building.
  • Cross-Platform Multiplayer/Hotseat-Play including co-op team games.
  • Random Map Generator.
  • Nations: Roman Empire, Carthage, Greeks, Sparta, Persia, Gaul, Celts, Germania amongst others.
  • Troops: Swordsmen, Archers, Horsemen, Scouts, Barbarians, Mercenaries amongst others.
  • Ships: Transport Ships, Galleys and Longboats.
  • Played as WE-GO Turn-Based Game allowing for an Unlimited Number of Players.
  • High Score and Playing Statistics.
  • Engaging Music and Sound Effects.

The game includes one map for FREE. Other maps are available for download and cost $3.99+ for single maps or $19.99 for all maps. Multiplayer games are generally free to join. Hosting multiplayer games costs 5 cents/game. There is no subscriptions. This game is NOT pay-to-win.

If you like this game, please also try our other strategy games. You should be able to find the games in the same marketplace!

Thanks & Enjoy the Game!

Early Access Review
Posted: 5 May, 2015
This is a pretty shaky thumbs up from me. Demise of Nations: Rome is a turn based strategy game set during the roman times. You control several units, and have five resources that you have to manage. As the game progresses, you research things like unit upgrades, buildings, and terrain upgrades. The basic scenario starts out with Rome at war with a neighbor, and all other contries are neutral.

This game needs improvement. During your turn, it gives no indication of which units need to move. When you click on a space with an enemy unit as an action, it moves your unit there and only attacks if the enemy unit is still there. There is no option for resource icons. The game takes forever to run it's course, and the aforementioned flaws make winning annoying. The tutorial, though improved since release, leaves much to be desired. Capture the Flag mode is thouroughly broken.

The multiplayer isn't much better. It takes coins to create a game, which take forever to regenerate. Half of the time the game isn't created, and a fourth of the time the created game is unjoinable. Server times are dismal.

The game has some pay content, mostly just extra maps and a generator. I haven't seen the need to spend money on this, but the generator has promise.

But still, this game has potential. It's still in early access, and Noble Master has done a good job of responding to bugs and likewise problems. It's a fairly easy 100%, although the dev has said he might add more acheivements. Plus, besides the extra maps, it's free.

With some improvement, this can be a great game. I give it 6/10, so long as the Dev keeps it up.

EDIT -- 6/10/15

After about a month of development since I posted this review, this game has truly improved.

The tutorial is much better. The We Go system is much better, and many of the single player flaws I mentioned are fixed. Resource Icons are there, which makes it a lot easier to manage your land. The new acheivements have been added, and some of them are quite difficult.

There have been some other changes too, but I found that playing this time arround was much easier than before.

New Rating: 8/10

Early Access Review
Posted: 23 April, 2015
This is written after completing an easly access build of the game on single player.

The game has potential, and for an early build it's not bad. However...

The "we-go" system does not really work for me. This is especially true when trying to pin down the last unit of an otherwise defeated enemy. It sucks to have to plan out several turns worth of movement to trap that last guy in a position you can attack. True turn based might fit this game better.

The tech tree is a bit shallow. However once it gets fleshed out it and stream lined, I can live with it. What's really missing are specialized soldiers/buildings for each nation.

City development is also pretty annoying at times. Some buildings only serve as stepping stones to the ones you want. They have no other function than to increase the cost and time to build he one you really want. This is sort of poor design.

There is a stat for population happiness, but happiness improves over time... Seems like a wasted feature. My people were always either happy, or a few turns away from it. With some adjustment this could add another layer of strategy to conquest. As it is now, it's just too easy to keep people happy.

I'm not much for multi-player, and don't find the value in it. A game like this might be do better with some older cheaper multi player tech. Game servers cost money, but play by email doesn't. For me it's not a selling point, so what do I know.

It's by no means a bad game, just not fully fleshed out. With a little more work, I think this game would be a pretty fun diversion. It reminds me a lot of shareware games from the 1990's, and I think a price point like those games would work far better than charging for dlc, or a sub for multi-player.

I know this is a long list of the problems, but its really not bad. People are a little unfair in their judgements of video games. This one works, and I think that alone is worth 5/10. It's still being worked on, so it could get better over time.

If you like these kinds of games, it's worth a try, you might find something cool.
