About This Game

In an open sand box world you choose your allies, your enemies, what provinces to conquer, what castles to siege and what quests to embark on. With Fire & Sword builds and expands upon the highly regarded combat system from Mount & Blade: Warband. Firearms have been introduced to the battlefield. Additionally, muskets and pistols can now be used as sidearms in hand-to-hand combat to quickly drop your foes. In addition to the enhanced singleplayer mode, With Fire & Sword also provides a host of original multiplayer content including the new game mode: Captain.

Key Features:

  • A storyline based on the cult novel, "With Fire and Sword", from Nobel-prize winning author Henry Sienkiewicz.
  • A new century of warfare that brings pistols, muskets, grenades, and other advanced weaponry to the battlefield.
  • Fully customizable armies; players can outfit their soldiers with each piece of weaponry & armor.
  • Additional quest types. More political intrigue with colorful factions. More adventure in a new land.
  • Multiple endings to increase re-playability.
  • Deeply researched historical component encompassing every aspect of the game's assets.
  • Ferocious new multiplayer action, bringing players the thrill of pitting old technology against the looming threat of firearms.
  • 3 Main Story-driven quest-lines for the player to delve into once they've become known in a particular faction.
  • Additional quest types.
  • Implementation of firearms, adds a whole new layer of strategic depth to the franchise's proven combat model.
  • New Siege options to choose before besieging a town or castle.

This Mount & Blade was poorly done. It just doesn't compare to its predecessors.

To start off, I'd like to say that Fire & Sword has an incredible problem with experience points and leveling, and on top of that, if you want to level up, you've got to kill countless people with firearms- It doesn't matter what armor you wear, damage taken can be as low as 16 points in heavy armor to a full 60 with none. You get shot in this game, your fun is over. The big problem there is that firearms are actually quite common to everyone except you... Want a good one? Be prepared to spend $2000 or more. In addition to that, when you're actually trying to "aim", be prepared for ridiculous misses, while the enemy seems to have no trouble hitting you from halfway across the map.

Aside from the experience points problem and the stacked cards against you in the gun-crazed Eastern European atmosphere, the balance for the game feels a bit off, and nothing is as "pretty" here as Warband. The map is overall darker, the bandits seem less regional as they are in Warband, and the game feels less alive than Warband. My biggest problem with it? It just isn't worth it when an infuriating mistake occurs. Ride by a reloading musketman, put the reticle over him, miss about 2 paces from him, and get shot in the back as you're riding away... When you finally *DO* kill him, you get 30 experience points. Yay you. 23 more to go before you'll level up!

Mistakes like these show how little thought and reception was given to community concerns. It's a dumbed down model, and I've been pushed away by the ridiculous level-up problems.

In addition to these slated issues, another problem is in regard to armor. Armor is completely unbalanced, and much of the decent equipment is ridiculously overpriced. There seems to have been no thought put into balancing the economy in that regard, which makes it nigh-impossible to outfit yourself to survive a few bullets. So how do you deal with these gun crazy fools when your armor- and that which you manage to buy- barely protects you? Spend about 20 minutes circling 13 Brigands until they run out of ammo, and you'll see my point.

This M&B is a definite Thumbs Down. It just wasn't well-thought-out, and I don't think development was very receptive to the community on its issues, and there are many.
