About This Game

The player is Geralt of Rivia, a professional monster slayer, a witcher. Entangled in the political turmoil that engulfed Temeria, Geralt helped quell the rebellion of the Order of the Flaming Rose. Soon after, he saved King Foltest’s life when the monarch was attacked by a witcher-like assassin. He continues to protect the king, serving as his bodyguard as Foltest strives to bring peace to his kingdom. The Order’s last bastions have yielded to the royal army, yet one more task remains - the Baroness La Valette announced her secession from the realm, and her fortress must be taken. A month after the attempted assassination, Foltest’s armies stand at the gates of La Valette Castle, preparing for a final assault. Still at Foltest’s side, Geralt is among them, unable to begin his personal quest to discover the mysterious assassin’s origin and identity…
Thanks to the new, ultra-modern REDengine, The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings features both beautiful graphics and sophisticated in-game mechanics, drawing players into the most lively and believable world ever created in a video game.
Defining new standards for realistic, non-linear game narration, The Witcher 2 spins a mature, thought-provoking tale to produce one of the most complex and unique RPGs ever released on PC. In addition to its epic story, the game features a complex combat system that uniquely combines dynamic action and tactical depth.

Enhanced Edition features:

Compared to the initial award winning PC release, the Enhanced Edition features lots of new and exciting content:
  • Additional hours of gameplay: New major adventures set in previously unseen locations, expanding the story and introducing new characters, mysteries and monsters.
  • New Game Introduction and cinematics: All new animations and cut scenes, including a new, three and a half minute pre-rendered cinematic depicting the assassination of King Demavend of Aedirn.
  • BAFTA Award winner and Academy Award nominee Tomasz Baginski brings this key historical event to life, setting the stage for the story told in The Witcher 2.
  • All of the up-to-date DLC and improvements introduced in the 2.0 version of the PC game, including:
    • Arena Mode – an arcade mode that allows players to fight against endless waves of enemies and test their combat skills.
      Players can share their results with their friends by uploading high scores to the forums or to Facebook .
    • A new, extensive tutorial system - allows gamers to be gradually and smoothly immersed in the game world and Geralt’s
      adventures.
    • Dark Mode – a difficulty level designed for hardcore players, with unique dark-themed items. At this difficulty level, even
      greater emphasis is placed on battle preparation, defensive maneuvers and opportunistic attacking.
  • Official soundtrack in MP3 format.
  • World Map - A map of the game's world.
  • Quest Handbook - The Witcher 2 quest handbook for both novice and experienced role-playing fans.
  • Game Manual - A rulebook and interface explanation to help you get started.
  • Digital Comic Book - Reasons of state comic book

I only discovered The Witcher due to the extensive media buzz aroudn the third. Not wanting to leave empty handed during the Summer sales I decided to see what the buzz was and picked up the second for £2.

First impressions are good, the game looks like it has a massive world and story to discover with graphics that suit the scope it aims for. It's nice to feel early on that I'm not missing out by not playing the first game, picking up the basics as I go, learning a bit about the main character Geralt as I play.

For an older game the graphics are brilliant, showing Skyrim up for what period pieces should look like when money is spent wisely. The game runs at high settings on my machine, the ultra for the first time seem to run awfully, though I'm hesitant to just blame the game rather my PC showing it's limits finally. The landscapes are fantastic, big broad views and draw distance keep the world wide open. Character models look great though the voice acting sometimes drag it all down into parody territory.

However the controls, combat and UI are just short of disgusting. The layout of the buttons is annoying on keyboard, using Alt to aim while trying to cycle to 'R' or 'E' to parry and press the mouse button to attack, he's not the quickest at reacting to the key presses so it becomes tiresome fast. Switch to a controller and discover the preset key bindings are barely configured, these can't be changed in game so you have to exit if you want to correct them. The fighting itself is slow and calculating, opting for a more realistic approch with two main options to fight, strong attack or light attack. Then there's powers to be used, traps/ranged attacks and an option to attack after a parry. It feels like everyone had an idea on what the Witcher fighting should be like and no ones ideas were shot down.

Walking feels cumbersome and janky, it works but there's something odd about it. Perhaps it's the camera that doesn't really attempt to follow the character in any decent fashion, often gifting you the view of the back of a bush while you struggle to fit a small army. There's no jump feature either so the world has a very linear feel, restricting you to a set level.

The UI is horrible at best, an entire screen filled to the brim with all kinds of options, inventorys and menus. There's no realy layout scheme that makes any sense, compared to Skyrim or other RPG's that utilise all of it's features in a nice easy to view way. Crafting, levelling up, sub levelling up and this weird power menu and another power wheel where you can take a nap or quick view your weapons all feel like over kill, you don't need to add that many features. I honestly can't be bothered to learn it all, I want to immerse myself in the world not get bogged down trying to earn my degree in Witcher menus.

Overall, this is much more beautiful than Skyrim ever was and the story is engaging. However the actual gameplay drops this title into Two Worlds territory for me. Absolute ambivelance I'm happy to part with £2 for.
