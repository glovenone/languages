About This Game

Solitaire Christmas. Match 2 Cards is a holiday card game filled with the joyful spirit of the holidays. The anticipation of Christmas miracles and 120 unique levels form a winning combination for fans of classic solitaire!

This game is for people who enjoy Spider Solitaire, Pyramid, Klondike, Free Cell, Crescent, Golf, Addiction, Three Peaks, Yukon, Forty Thieves, Scorpion, Gaps, Canfield, Crazy Quilt, Algerian, Montana, Eight Off, Calculation, and Clock Solitaire.

Solitaire Christmas. Match 2 Cards is a fun new twist on traditional solitaire for players who love to try new types of deals! Find and match any 2 cards quickly to increase your winnings! Buy bonuses using the following coins: shuffle, undo last move, and joker. Choose your difficulty level, and maybe even go for Professional, but be warned: this is only for true lovers of solitaire! Let the relaxing music and premium graphics take you away into the wonderful atmosphere of the holidays.

Key Feauters

  • Card solitaire game for new players and experts alike
  • Unique game mechanics – choosing pairs of cards
  • Use different bonuses - mulligan, shuffle, joker
  • Collect gold cards to complete the level and unlock next location
  • Collect cards fast - do combos and get good rewards
  • 12 locations, 120 levels and several difficulty modes
  • For daring players, we offer special tasks and more than 20 colorful trophies
  • Colorful high-quality graphics put you right in a winter wonderland!
  • Original themed decks and 9 card backs
  • Beautiful music to help you relax and concentrate on the game

    • Processor: Intel Atom N455 (1660 MHz)
    • Memory: 1024 MB RAM
    • Graphics: Intel GMA 3150
    • DirectX: Version 9.0c
    • Storage: 56 MB available space

This solitaire game has a very low completion rate without using any cheat mechanisms (which you will have to buy for ingame currency per each use).

Despite having a timed mechanism (match faster to grow your combo multiplier), the game has a very annoying 1 second delay after every match during which you can't click any other cards so if you're too fast and clicked the card right after the match and didn't notice that it didn't register (the selection highlight is barely noticeable), you'lll be very confused.

To progress in the game after the first set of 10 levels, you have to collect 50/58 'special cards' during these levels. That's a really high threshold to reach for a ''newbie" difficulty. It pretty much means that you'll have to replay the same levels over and over again to either farm for gold or get lucky. I mean, is having a 75% clearance rate not enough for letting a casual player progress through your game? What is even the point of gatekeeping levels on a "newbie" difficulty? Just let me have fun, damnit!

I went into this game expecting easy and quick fun but all I got was frustration. There are loads of other solitaire games on Steam that do it better.

Posted: 25 February
A nice casual Christmas-themed solitaire game. Though the gameplay is different than Faerie Solitaire and games like that. In this one you click on match pairs of the same number. Your goal is to get all the gold special cards. There are 3 bonus abilities you can use: Shuffle, Undo, and Wild Card. You can buy them in the in-game store with the coins you earn by playing, or occasionally you get a free one by matching a card with an asterisk (*) on it.

There are 2 difficulty levels, and 12 groupings of 10 levels each. In the easy difficulty you can replay any level you want any time. In the hard difficulty you have to replay from the first level in each group. Each group has a different background (though some are just a mirror image or a smaller portion of a previous larger one).

You can change the image on the card decks any time, even mid-game. This is especially helpful on levels with a dark background or light background where you want a contrasting color to see the cards easier.

Note to achievement hunters: If you want to get all the achievements then I recommend you NOT to purchase any bonuses in the store, because one of the achievements is to earn one million coins. And if you keep buying stuff then it will take longer to get than achievement. It took me about 30 hours to get all the achievements.

The music is very nice and pleasant. It changes and shifts so that it doesn't get boring.

Minor bugs: One level has a black background; whatever the image is supposed to be, it does not load correctly. And occasionally a bug will happen when matching one of the * cards where it magically collects all the gold cards even though they are still buried. But that's a bug in my favor so I'm not complaining!

That being said, it's a nice casual game that's not too expensive.
