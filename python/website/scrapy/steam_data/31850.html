About This Game

Nancy Drew®: Ghost Dogs of Moon Lake is a first-person perspective, point-and-click adventure game. The player is Nancy Drew and has to solve a mystery. Explore rich environments for clues, interrogate suspects, and solve puzzles and mini-games.

A friend’s frantic note sends you, as Nancy Drew, on the trail of a mysterious pack of dogs whose glowing eyes and mournful howls threaten to chase you away forever. Local residents say that the dogs have come back from the grave to protect the secrets of their master. Are the ghostly legends true, or is there a flesh-and-blood explanation for the haunting howls? You must solve the mystery before the hounds catch your scent! Dare to Play™!

  • Explore a cemetery and a speakeasy
  • Take photos of birds and collect bugs
  • Find a way through a forest maze
  • Interrogate intriguing suspects
  • Choose from Junior or Senior Detective difficulty levels

Love love love. Ive played this many times over when I had the original CD and on steam. This is a classic point and click detective game. Just from the beginning you are sucked in, wanting to know more about those mysterious "ghost dogs." The game does have a bit of a spooky vibe to it in the beginning (at least when I was a kid) but not nearly as much as some other Nancy Drew games.

Story: Nancy's friend calls asking her to come to this old cabin she bought. Upon your arrival Nancy's friend has left because she was too scared (what a puss). After her friend explains over the phone what is going on and no way for Nancy to leave she is left to her first spooky experience during the night at Moon Lake. You also learn about the history of the cabin which was owned by Mickey Malone, a notorious gangster who happen to own a few dogs.

Gameplay: Has some good exploration including a few activities that have you run around in the woods looking for things such as bugs and taking pictures of birds. The puzzels are fun and not too difficult other than one that requires you understand roman numerals (ugh). The characters are interesting and you get to befriend an old grumpy man who birdwatches. You also get to see some cute dogs which is always a plus.

All in all its a fantastic point and click game and id probably rate it a 9/10. Although its great for anyone to play I would really recommend for older kids because I had a blast playing these games with my mother when I was younger!

Hounds of Baskerville, Speakeasys and Prohibition

This is exactly what this game is having as contents, exactly not the Baskerville creature but the "ghost" - dogs of a former Gangster. You have to uncover a lot of clues, which at last lead to the final solution of the mysterie behind the secret of Moon Lake.

Nancy Drew Adventures are quite often disregarded as childish, and especially girlie adventures - I beg to differ. They are worthwhile and even for adults they offer a lot of relaxing if not outright fun of the suspense story kind. Forget those blasted Visual Novels, most of them aren't worth the black dirt from under your fingernails. But digging up real mysteries and trying to make sense out of leads, or fall for red herrings is indeed a lot of fun.

Sure, the graphics are already dated. But the story that is told inside this game is still very vibrant and good. Nancy Drew is sometimes compared as a Mary Sue character, but if you delve deeper into this mater you will take note that she isn't she can have accidents, which sometimes draw her off a case, or even... well kill her... and you'll come to love the stories that are told in the adventures. Why, you ask? Because they remind you of the stories you have read as a kid, or you have seen series with the same suspense stories.

I recomend this game to anyone who loves Adventure, Riddle and Suspense games, with real culprits to catch and ghosts that only exist because somebody is still telling their stories.

Ah the classics, you don't get much better than that. The whole concept of this mystery game was brilliant. You get to stay on a lake, in a house that belonged to a gangster, and get to explore a speakeasy, it's awesome! Every time I play this game I'm always thrilled and deeply amused by it. Everything you do in this mystery challenges your brain and you get to do cool things like getting thrown into a shack and you have to escape before you burn up in flames. The thing I like the most in this game is the detail of the house with countless references to Mickey Malone's dogs. You really get the vibe from this game just how much one person can truly value their pets -- even if they are the key to finding secret loot. For this being one of the very small handful of first made Nancy Drew games, this is a pretty good game and it challenges your sleuthing skills. It's also a really good game to go back to for a refresher if you haven't played a mystery game in a while.
