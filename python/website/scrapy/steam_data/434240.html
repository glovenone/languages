26 June

Alien Blitz 1.4.4

Update 1.4.4 is now live on Steam !
What's new ?

  • [new] Save slot selection, when starting the game choose from 3 save slot selection to play.
  • [new] Added an option to confine the mouse to the game (activated by default), it means the mouse can not exit the game window when playing, it is released when opening a menu.
  • [update] COMPET-N challenge/achievement now needs to be done under 2 hours instead of 1 hour and 30 minutes
  • [update] In game buttons are now disabled by default.
  • [update] Resolution and fullscreen changes now have a confirmation box with a delay (15 seconds) to confirm changes. This should solve some issues with resolution change
  • [update] Weapon can now be switched mid-reload in hard and more difficulty (you have to wait half reload time before being able to switch, it was full reload time before, it's still immediate in easy/normal)
  • [bug] UAC sprite for challenge was invisible


More information here : http://www.microbasic.net/2016/06/alien-blitz-steam-update-1-4-4/

If you notice any bug please post them on this forum

About This Game

Your island has been invaded by aliens during the night, take your shotgun, and kick them out!

You play a soldier resting on his native island, but it is quickly overrun by monsters from outer space and no outside help is available. You put on your mecha armor, take your shotgun and go to find out what happened at the peril of your life.

Alien Blitz is a 3d action-shooter with lots of bullets, missiles, weapons, maps and monsters.

  • Action-shooter, 3D top-down view
  • Lots of bullets, shooting and monsters, "Bullet hell"
  • 3 difficulty settings + 2 new game + modes
  • 37 maps + 4 secret maps + Steam Workshop maps
  • 14 upgradeable weapons (improve accuracy, damage, reload time and bullet speed)
  • Experience and leveling
  • Level editor + Steam Workshop

At first, it didn't seem that good but the more I played, the more it felt reminiscent to doom. Both in gameplay and it captured the addictiveness and wonder of doom that I remember having playing when I was a kid.
Thev voxel graphics are quite nice, at first they felt a little amatuerish and low budget but grew on me.

I think the worst thing about the game is the sound. The effects are low quality and the music is royalty free, stock music which I don't think matches very well with the game at all. In fact the endless loops of it puts me off to the point of wanting to stop playing.

I think the developer was probably working on it by himself or with a few people, without much budget, hence the library stock music. I'd suggest getting a composer/sound engineer to redo the sound and music to create a better atmosphere.

However, minus the sound, this is a super addictive little game and for OCD people like myself, there are plenty of collectibles, secrets and stuff to unlock (similar system to doom again).

4/5 I've had the Indie Game Stand (DRM-free + Android APK file) version of Alien Blitz (Snorms) for over a year already. So my Steam stats don't paint the whole picture here.

Alien Blitz/Snorms plays like Doom with an overhead view and weapon upgrades.

PROS:
+ Arcade style action.
+ RPG-like upgrading system.
+ Level layouts encourage exploration.
+ Cleared maps can be replayed with new weapons and upgrades in towe.
+ Level editor will probably result in newer and cooler maps hitting the web.
+ Controls via m/k or controller (both Direct input and Xinput).
+ Windows/Mac/Linux versions all included.
+ Modest hardware requirements.

CONS:
- Shadow system takes some getting used to.
- No multiplayer...yet.

*Indie Game Stand version is the better deal. It is DRM-free and includes the Android APK file for Android devices, and the Desura and (now) Steam keys.
