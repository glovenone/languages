“At the current time the development of the base Strayed game is planned to run enough time to add a lot of new content, making the game as complete as possible following its context. (Every update will be previously warned).”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Strayed will continue to grow and evolve throughout development, features will be refined and adjusted based on Community feedback. Players can look forward to exciting updates, we will always warn previously for the everyone's consent.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We plan to do not raise the price, new content and features are included in the game without more costs.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

About This Game

Early Development Note:

Strayed is currently a work in progess, most content presented is not a final product, changes will happen.

Joseph Toddy. Ex-marine, a wise man. Now retired, fishing was one of his greatest passions and he also used it as an extra income. And so followed by some years of his life, as usual, he woke up early, gave his wife a kiss and headed towards another day of fishing, but his partner, who was always with him, unfortunately could not accompany him this time. Even knowing the risks of sailing on the sea alone and its tricks as well, he decided to proceed. All quiet on the high seas, the day was productive, certainly he would make good money, until he noticed a small flaw in the engine of his boat. Joseph was startled, but he did not care so much. A fatal error. After a few hours, when the day began to fall and the fisherman was about to go home, he realized that the engine of his boat could not work more. He had experience with the sea, but he wasn't a good mechanic. The man found himself adrift, surrounded by the sea water, when suddenly spotted a desert island, he knew it wasn't a place inhabited, but that could be his unique chance to survive.

You are stranded with your vessel, explore islands with your improvised raft to find required parts to fix it and return home.

Early Access Review
Posted: 17 June
Honestly this isn't even a game yet.

The whole game feels like it just been pieced together from various parts of models and code created by others in the unity community and nothing has been adjusted. Really it looks like a school project.

About the "game" itself. You're told the controls rightaway and what you need to do. You then proceed to find an axe.
The axe is easy to find as there is a glowing orb on it and the orb won't disappear after picking up the axe.
You can't swing the axe around so you'll feel like you're not pressing the right button for it. You'll crouch while trying every button and you'll see that the axe gets squished. That's when you'll realise how Pre-Pre-Alpha this is.

Cutting down trees is simple. You just walk at them and there's a cutting animation. Or you can stand near them and then the axe works. I prefer walking at the tree. Oh, and turn of the sound because it'll make your ears bleed.

Then you'll see a pretty neat animation of the tree falling and BAM the tree turn into two logs that sound like plastic chopsticks while rolling on the ground.
You then take both logs and carry them to your ship to build a stairway into the ship. It takes two logs to build one step(what the hell man?).

All is fine, you think, you'll just keep doing this to see the end. But then you'll see nothing as there's a night-day cycle and you don't have anykind of lightsource. And cutting trees down means losing your chopsticks as you can't find them in the dark. You keep going on for a while and then quit and write a review.

I'll check around once in a while to see if there's any updates and maybe I'll even update my review into something more positive.

Oh yeah, I forgot to add that this definitely isn't a game worth 15€.
