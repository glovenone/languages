About This Game

Laura doesn’t suffer from nightmares anymore. But, despite the fact that it has been 2 years since she came out of the coma, she is haunted by the promise of Sandman to take revenge. Laura and Tim go to ski resort hoping to distract Laura from heavy thoughts.

There, on the edge of the world, misfortune befalls her. Tim goes out of the house in a fit of lunacy and falls off the cliff. He did not die, but is in a coma. It is not hard to guess that the fall off the cliff and coma are caused by intrigues of the Sandman.

It would seem what might be easier for Laura, having the absolute Power, to awaken her husband? However, being received the Power, Laura has made a commitment not to use the Power for her own benefit. This means that she can’t help Tim to wake up. And she has 2 choices: watch how her beloved slowly dies in the hospital, or have recourse to the help of an old friend again who once helped her when she was in a coma.

When Laura met an old friend (the player), she stops the time to pass by the doctors unchecked and infiltrate in Tim’s ward, and then she immerses the player into the subconscious of Tim, whose body is almost seized by the Sandman.

The Sandman operates under the old scheme: by sending nightmares, he suppresses the will of the victim to resist.
Will the player have time to wake up Tim before the Sandman turns him into his heir?

  • Continuation of game series;
  • Original game world;
  • More than 40 gaming locations;
  • The world changes during walkthrough;
  • 12 mini-games;
  • More than 30 3D videos and cut-scenes;
  • Unique meta-game on search of beholders (variation of Hidden Object’s mechanics);
  • Meta-game on search of diary’s pages with prehistory of game events
  • 2 game modes (normal and advanced);
  • Achievements system

After saving Laura from her dreams and from the Sandman, she has now the Power. However, she can't use it for herself and the Sandman used that knowledge to attack her dear friend and now husband Tim. You're the only one she can count on, so, you're going to save Tim from his nightmares and put an end to the Sandman's revenge.

Dreamscapes: Nightmare's Heir is the sequel of Dreamscapes: The Sandman. While it's not an obligation to play the first game, I suggest you to strongly do it, as you will have a better understand of what's happening. The story is still nicely written and if you played the first game, you knew that there was going to be a sequel at one point. Here it is, with the Sandman ready to take over Tim and taunting you about failing this time (though he was sure of that in the first game too). As usual, a cute creature is helping you, though it's not Benny anymore but another one.

There was a huge change in the gameplay: now, you have hidden objects scenes included, where the first game was just a point-&-click with puzzles. It doesn't change anything in the end, as it's still pleasant to play puzzles (they're easy) and to clear the scenes, with a well-balances difficulty. The beholders are still there, however, where in the first game, it was easier to find them, here, they're kinda very small apart for some.

As you're now in Tim's nightmare, you have different fears to face than in Laura's story. They're less into "exotic" locations: everything is way more darker than for Laura - if I think about the depths of the sea and the swamp - else, Tim was twice in a house, in an apartment and on a road. I don't mind a change of scenery but I wish it was more inviting to a journey. However, I found the apartment's dream very interesting as everything is in black and white, like in an old polar story and items themselves in your inventory were in black and white. But the graphics are still good, though I found Laura's hands a little too big in the cutscenes.

The soundtrack is also fitting to the ambiance. And the voice acting is good too, mainly our adorable creature's sounds.

The big relief is that Dreamscapes: Nightmare's Heir doesn't suffer of crashes, like for The Sandman where you had to set the CPU affinity to 0 or 1. It means mainly that you can play the game without having to worry about any workaround or crash or corrupt files. I don't know what really changed for that though.

Dreamscapes: Nightmare's Heir is the sequel you were waiting for, without crashes, with new worlds to visit, though now, it's Tim who is affected, and with hidden object scenes that are breaking the monotony of using items and going back and forth between locations.

Short version: 82%
Dreamscapes 2 tries to venture into the hidden object game territory, but at its core it is still a casual point ‘n’ click. The story is a direct sequel, but with shorter dreams of somewhat wavering quality.

Long version:
Shaman Games likes to create games that look and feel like hidden object games, but are actually lightweight point ‘n’ clicks. Dreamscapes 2: Nightmare’s Heir is a rare exception: it retains most of the developer’s usual clever puzzle design, but this time there is a larger focus on item gathering, and there are even a few traditional hidden objects scenes.

The story brings us back to Laura (the damsel in distress of the first game), who, despite her new-found superpowers, is only present as a supporting character; it is up to the new protagonist to save her husband. His dreams are probably more mundane than Laura’s were, but there are many of them, so even the duller ones quickly give way to the better segments.
Still, it becomes obvious fairly early that despite their number, the new dreams don’t last long. This naturally results in a very short game (about half of an afternoon long), even by casual game standards.

DaveLessnau
( 3.8 hrs on record )
Posted: 12 October, 2015
"Dreamscapes: Nightmare's Heir - Premium Edition" is yet another barely recommended game. Most of the game is of acceptable quality for a casual adventure Hidden Object Game (HOG). The voice acting, graphics, game play, and story are about par for the genre (in the main game, anyway). Nothing really stands out either way. The bonus chapter, though, is of far less quality than the main game (it's only about a half an hour of play, so it's not a big thing). There, the story really turns dumb and the game play becomes trivial. Also, the developers seem to have neglected their English translations more. There's a lot of "something must be applied" flying around there. Also, at one point, a character lies on the floor with an implication of being dead. The text reads something about being deafened. Yet, the character is revived by something trivial (with an odd name). In the main game, the only real translation difficulties I noticed was the use of the term "balloon" for tank (twice, I believe). My only real complaint with the whole game is the bugs ("Beholders") that we're supposed to find. If you finish taking everything out of an object without first getting that bug, the object will close and you're SOL: can't re-open the container, no saves, no checkpoint. Ditto if you finish a dreamscape area.

I got a total of 3.8 hours of acceptable play out of the game. By my dollar an hour metric, at it's current list price of about $7, it's a bit overpriced. Look for it on sale for less than $4 and that would be better.
