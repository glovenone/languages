About This Game

ALL-STARS DUNGEONS AND DIAMONDS (ASDAD for short) is a competitive Arcade-style game where two players face each other in a diamond-collecting race. The player with the largest number of gems at the end of the round wins. Things quickly become more complicated, though, as collecting diamonds will reveal items buried underground that will grant players the ability to attack the opponent or obtain temporary boosts that will help them win the race.

  • Intense 2 Player (local multiplayer) Competitive Arcade action!
  • 7 Different Characters! (4 Original Characters + 3 Guests from other indie titles!*)
  • 10 different items and 4 different worlds!
  • Customizable match settings (for normal mode) allow you to change the rules and pace of the game!
  • Extra game mode "Race for the Idol" where the first player to reach the sacred gold statue at the end of the maze wins!

* Guest Characters are featured in ASDAD with the proper permission from their owners.

Posted: 4 October, 2015
This is a tentative recommendation. Whether or not this is worth buying completely depends on whether you have someone entertaining to play with, which is true of a lot of local co-op games, but even more so with this one, because mechanically it's too simple and repetitive to enjoy playing just for the sake of the gameplay. The real pleasure comes from the trashtalk as powerups are deployed - think Mario Kart, but instead of racing gokarts you're collecting jewels in a maze using Pac-Man's controls. There is a single-player option, even though it's not listed under the game's features, but it's an exercise in frustration as the AI controlled character is able to operate flawlessly, and doesn't get thrown off by things like the control-altering confusion effect in the way a human player would. For practicing purposes, you're better off playing a co-op game solo, as it's easier to get a handle on the controls and learn what all the powerups do without the stress of the computer using powerups against you.
There are some games meant for co-op that can still be fun solo, like Nidhogg, but this is not one of those games. For a lot of people, this will be a turn off, especially since it's local co-op only, but it's also a lovingly crafted game with cute visuals, nice music, and responsive controls. I think it's exactly the game the developer wanted to make, and for that I have to give it the thumbs up.

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 4 October, 2015
ASDAD: All-Stars Dungeons and Diamonds is an interesting local multiplayer game but it is not going to be for everyone.

In ASDAD you play as one of two explorers who are trying to collect as many diamonds as possible. You traverse a maze collecting diamonds as well as items that either give you an advantage or hurt your opponent.

The game has two main modes. In one mode players compete to collect the most diamonds. In the other mode the players race to collect an idol which is located at the end of the maze. I personally preferred the diamond collecting mode since the idol mode relies too heavily on the use of the items.

While the game does have a single player mode, it serves mostly as a training exercise for the multiplayer. If you don't have another person to play the game with locally, you probably aren't going to get a lot out of the game.

I had fun with ASDAD but it is not the type of game that I would play for hours at a time. It is the type of game that I would play for maybe a half hour and then come back to at a different time. While you can do quite a bit of customizing in each level, I kind of wish the game had a couple more levels.

If you have another person to play the local multiplayer with and like the concept of traversing a maze collecting items, I think you can have some fun with ASDAD: All-Stars Dungeons and Diamonds.

Note: I received a free review copy from the developer.

Posted: 1 October, 2015
Firstly let me say that this game epitomises Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde, in that it has the capacity to both appease and alienate players.

Modes:
Race for the Idol: You and another player must circumvent an arena whilst avoiding various obstacles along the way. The first person to reach the end wins.
Player versus Player: Self explanatory
Practice: Self explanatory

Positives:
- The art work, sound effects and overall presentation are not only excellent, but they wouldn't feel out of place in any Treasure (of Gunstar Heroes, Radiant Silvergun or Ikaruga fame), Capcom or 2D Konami offering. They're really quite awesome, and really add to the quality of the overall package.
- Excellent local co-op mode. The competitive multiplayer aspect not only includes the player either having to try and collect more coins than their rival, but also having to race to the end.
- Fun and varied character roster.

Negatives:
- The multiplayer maps are limited in that each half of the map is symmetrical. The only variation within the multiplayer gameplay modes is the positioning of the chests (which are not necessarily the same for both players).
- The player in second place has very little chance of regaining the advantage due to the dominating player dictating the pace throughout the match due to power-up advantages.
- By what I've seen, the game is only local co-op as opposed to online co-op, which severely limits its potential.
- Lack of any meaningful single player campaign.

Conclusion:
To conclude then, this game would really benefit from a dedicated single-player campaign in that it seems to be a lot more focused on the competitive multiplayer aspect, but with that being said it's a highly polished and fun game to play overall, especially with your friends.
