About This Game

Red Risk
Volunteers fight in a bloody contest for freedom and money.
You'll play as one of three volunteers armed with their favorite weapons.
Survive waves of enemies with a large quantity of rounds, enemies and bosses.

You will need to eat, drink and fill your ammo in order to survive.

Elite monsters will cast an unique set of skills to hunt you down.

This game requires a lot of strategy and rational thinking.

Key Features
  • 3 Players Co-op
  • Original Soundtrack
  • Deadly traps
  • Skilled Enemies
  • Blood, gore and dancers!
  • Level Up System
  • Steam Achievements/Trading Cards

I'll start saying that the price is fine considering the general offer. It has achievements and trading cards wich is a very good thing.
The game is an arena shooter and your goal is to survive all 10 levels of each match, there will be regular enemies and some bosses that can be pretty difficult to take down. After completing a level you gain a point that can be spent on ammo, water, food, or health whose levels must be checked in order to keep your fighter alive. There are three characters available: a guy armed with a sniper rifle, a girl armed with two pistols and a close-combat guy armed with a mace.
It can be played in single or in multiplayer with other two people. The latter option is recommended even if there aren't many people to play it online, it's better to have a friend to play with.

The graphic is not too bad considering the price.
The soundtrack features a death metal theme that fits perfectly with the game's atmosphere but gets repetitive being the only song you can listen to.

Sadly I have to make and important complaint: the movement system doesn't allow you to use WASD, but forces you to move the character by pressing the right click button of the mouse and moving it in the direction you want to go. This should absolutely be fixed. The mouse should be used only to aim and shoot at enemies.

There are way better games available but I'll recommend it for the trading cards and the achievements.

(tl;dr):

7/10 - It's a fun game, both on singleplayer and on co-op modes, but has a few problems, like kite controls (move and shoot with your mouse, R-click moves, L-click shoots) and no key rebinding.

More detailed review

In Red Risk you participate in tournaments where you must survive several waves of “infected”, in 10 action-packed rounds, all with a nice soundtrack. You can choose between 3 characters with different skills and gameplay styles, 3 difficulty modes and 7 arenas that will be unlocked once you complete a few tournaments (you win “rank points” at the end of each tournament, which will be used to unlock new arenas).

Pros:
- Fast paced action
- Nice soundtrack
- Different characters and a reasonable amount of maps
- Can be challenging enough with the appropriate difficulty mode

Cons:
- Uses only kite controls (you both move and shoot with your mouse - R-click moves, L-click shoots)
- You can't move and shoot at the same time
- No WASD movement option (players have asked for it with no reply yet)
- No key rebinds

Conclusion: 7/10 - Can be pretty fun, but if you can't stand the kite controls, it's probably not for you.

Red Risk is a top-down action game made by Microblast Games (Dead Bits, Overcast, Particula). When I think of hell then flames, go-go dancers, and demonic beasts come up to my mind; and Red Risk fulfilled my image of hell.

You play as one of the three characters, each with specific weapon (axe, pistol or sniper rifle) and unique skill. Red Risk tournament starts immediately as you are thrown into the arena - you have to survive through rounds, while taking care of your health, ammo, starve and thirst (stats are regained at a specific place in the arena for credits you get for surviving).

The developers chosen pretty uncomfortable controls, as you have to use one mouse button to move, and the second button to attack. Keyboard is just used for your special ability and to check your stats.

Even though the game is obviously low-budget and the graphics style is not terrible as you could expect, the animations make it look pretty bad in action. Your melee character will swing his axe like he would be on drugs, enemy characters usually attack just by making weird moves next to you. Those moves might seem funny, but they're actually taking your health down, of course.

Ranged characters in the game play pretty bad, as they share the same controls with the melee character, except they have to reload their weapons. It seems that they are made with thought for the co-op multiplayer, as they are pretty weak on their own, especially the sniper.

As I've already mentioned, the graphic style is not that bad until something moves, and there's also indifferent heavy metal soundtrack, that even for a metalhead might feel cheesy, but it does its job. There's several arenas where you can get access after you level up (by killing enemies in unlocked arenas), so you can expect getting few hours of playtime from this, if you'll be having fun. Also, there are three difficulties that indicate the health and attack of your enemies.

Red Risk, as it is, isn't worth recommending. It's a slighty below mediocre top-down action game with bad animation and uncomfortable controls. Also, another important thing to mention is the fact that the developers are known for permanently lowering price of their games shortly after release, so if you are interested, you still might want to wait. (Source: Price history on SteamPrices; Selling thousands of keys for giveaways/random key deals)


Disclaimer: My copy of Red Risk was obtained for free for reviewing purposes. This fact hasn't changed my opinion about the game (and the review) in any way.
