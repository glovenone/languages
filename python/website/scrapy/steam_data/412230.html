About This Game

InfiniTrap is a fast paced puzzle game for hardcore players. The game contains 40 levels and is available on Windows, OSX and Linux.

Every level starts with the player character standing on the start tile. The start tile is perfectly safe-- No, trust me. It's safe. Once you leave the start tile, you have a limited time to reach the treasure before the level crushes you. To complicate things, each level will include a number of obstacles to waste your time and possibly kill you. You can make your own levels!

FEATURES


  • 40 challenging levels.
  • Blood and gore can be disabled.
  • Full gamepad support.
  • A full-featured level editor.
  • Custom levels support.
  • Supported languages: English, French, German, Spanish, Russian, Japanese, Portuguese

I have never really played a game like this before. It is quite difficult, and on one of the levels I played through and died about 30 times in a row before I figured out what I was doing wrong. Since I have young game players in the house, and also because I prefer not to watch bloody and gory animations, I truly appreciate that the developer included a 'no gore' option to make the game family friendly. It's always a positive when gamers are given a choice, and it shows that the developer is willing to put work into the game to make it accessible to everyone.

Since I grew up in Ohio, I'll admit that I'm a bit biased toward liking the title. Control works excellent with my Xbox 360 controller. Difficulty level is high, and I suppose that could be frustrating for some, but I felt that it was worth the money.

This game is both infinitely frustrating, yet fun in a challenging way. While it is not necessarily a kid friendly game as the dev suggests, I think little tykes may like some levels. I got frustrated at quite a few levels and almost stopped playing, but I decided to trudge on with it. I'm glad I did because this is a wonderful little indie game with a supportive and caring dev. Please don't make me eat those words though! I'll get around to finishing it once I clear out some other games on my list. Definitely will be on my need to finish list.

Pro's:

  • Challenging game. while it does frustrate me quite a bit, I enjoy sitting bit with my controller.

  • Heartfelt story in the beginning about the Dev and his son.

  • Game Adjusts well to both keyboard and gamepad- I use a Logitech F310, and have no problems.

  • Kid friendly options but I would NOT recommend this for kids. More in my cons section on this.

  • Seemingly generic story at first, but throws a little twist that made me have a chuckle.

    Con's:

  • In the beginning the dev recommends options to turn off for kids. I would NOT recommend this for kids- I got frustrated quite a bit. This doesn't make it a bad games by any means, I quite enjoyed it still.

  • As many other's have stated, this is over priced. I would put this at $2.50, and drop it to a $1-1.50 during steam sales. Sure it has 30 odd levels, but some of them got old pretty quickly, or repeptitive.

  • The zombies and traps are quite frustrating- I feel like I should be able to be able to run away from the zombies quicker rather than stand there like a derp. I also wish I could just over some of the traps such as the mini flamethrowers.
