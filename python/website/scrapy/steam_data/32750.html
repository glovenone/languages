About This Game

The best-selling Comanche series returns with this fast-paced action game that places you at the controls of a Comanche RAH-66 helicopter. In 6 single player campaigns and multiplayer, the game combines serious firepower with effortless controls and stunning, interactive terrain.

Features

  • NON-STOP ASSAULT — Blast away at tanks, jets, aircraft carriers, helicopters, missile-launching troops and more.
  • UNLEASH THE POWER — Shoot Hellfire and Stinger missiles, rockets and a 20mm cannon.
  • AIRBORNE IN MINUTES — Quickly master flying and fighting using the keyboard & mouse, or for the sim fan, joystick, throttle & pedal controls.
  • STUNNING INTERACTIVE TERRAIN — Immerse yourself in detailed environments with rotor wash, shadows, terrain scarring and spectacular explosions.
  • 6 EXTREME CAMPAIGNS — Fly across urban, forest, arctic, desert and sea environments while you protect diplomats, support Delta Force, subdue renegade forces, attack warships and more.

System Requirements

    • OS: Windows 2000, XP & Vista
    • Processor: Pentium II 450MHz or better
    • Memory: 128MB minimum
    • Graphics: Direct3D w/ 16MB or better
    • DirectX®: DirectX version 8.0 or higher (included)
    • Hard Drive: 250MB Free
    • Sound: DirectX-compliant

Posted: 27 December, 2015
I first played a demo of this game which came with a GeForce 4 graphics card. I thought it was fun back then in 2002 and I kept playing it occasionally even in 2008. Now, 13 years later, I remembered this thing existed and found full version of Comanche 4 on Steam.

Pros:
1. Militaristic feel to the game. Still somewhat immersive.
2. Explosions looking good.
3. Distant explosion sound travels across levels, that means you don't hear explosions instantly when they happen a mile away.
4. Elevation toggle control and stuff, pretty solid controls.
5. Diverse missions.

Cons:
1. Only few 4:3 resolution presets. No custom resolutions support, not even using .ini tweaking. This is fatal. I can stand dated graphics, but playing 4:3 on a relatively small 16:9 screen never feels good. I could use scaling though, but I doubt I can accept stretched visuals nowadays.
2. You can't see much beneath you and can't shot what you don't see (unless you've locked on a target, but you need to see it in order to lock on). This is not how helicopter cannons work, m8. They should point atleast 45 down, but in this game it's limited by tilt and vertical FOV. This forces you to fly on low altitudes which is not what I'd expect from a game like this.
3. All intense fighting equals "rapidly clicking right mouse button to lock on targets and shooting down the targets with the corresponding weapon".

Will I recommend this game? If you are OK with playing on 4:3 resolutions and not afraid of ♥♥♥♥ ripping hardcore missions, this might be waht you seek. Otherwise, it's a thumb down.

Comanche 4 is quite OLD and it shows. It really does. But for a 2001 game, it is to be expected! Still, I had fun playing this helicopter simulation. The game is quite challenging as if you get destroyed, you have to restart the whole mission. Thankfully (or not), the missions aren't very long and you can beat the game under 4 hours, sadly.

PROS:
+Interesting missions.
+Tight controls (plays very well with Keyboard and Mouse).
+Gameplay is great!
+Authentic and very accessible (easy learning curve).
+Game works without a hitch on modern systems.

CONS:
-Graphics are indeed dated for modern gaming but I'd guess it wasn't that great looking back in 2001. Also, knowing Novalogic, that never were their strong suite anyway.
-Might not appeal to the hard-core sim fans.
-Very short campaign mode (can be beaten under 4 hours).

RECOMMENDATION:
To any and all fan of military helicopter simulation, I'd say give this game a whirl!
