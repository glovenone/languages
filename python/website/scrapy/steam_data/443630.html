About This Game

Chicku is a nail bitingly hard platformer in which the player has to escape the Barrington farm. You’ll be forced to dodge saws, spikes and many other deadly traps in order to save your brethren and rise against the evil Dr. Barrington. Play alone or with friends in this epic adventure and slaughter save millions of chickens along the way.

When you’re done saving your species, challenge your friends’ flying skills or their ability to handle a weapon in the bloodthirsty cockfight arena.
Also, why not wear a fancy hat while you do it?

Playing with a controller/gamepad is highly recommended

Features:

  • Explore six major environments with well over 200 maps.
  • Local multiplayer, play together with friends. Just as easy as picking up your controller and pushing a button.
  • Master the maps to gather coins to unlock cosmetics.
  • Learn to fly as a chicken! A unique way of controlling the character which is easy to learn, but hard to master!
  • Replay your favorite environments in our new brawl mode with crazy modifiers to alter the gameplay.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7/8/10
    • Processor: Quad-core Intel or AMD, 2.0 GHz or faster
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Radeon HD 5750 or higher

Ever wonder where your chicken nuggets come from? This platformer is Super Meat Boy slid his double quarter pounder into Flappy Birds and gave it his secret sauce. That is Chicku. BTW, do yourself a favor and play this game with a controller.
WARNING: The game is graphic in that is has copious amounts of cartoon blood. If you are with PETA, move along, this is not the game you were looking for.

ON TO THE REVIEW!
Sound: 7/10
Graphics: 8/10
Gameplay: 10/10
Replayability: 7/10

This platformer does have integrated multiplayer. Co-Op and Deathmatch. Nothing like playing a suicide bomber chicken to take out your friends.

SOUND:
Basic sounds and music that doesn't annoy you after 4 straight hours of gameplay.

GRAPHICS:
Cute cartoon mayhem within this slaughterhouse of horrors. Every death allows for another layer of blood to wash over the background with each subsequent death (as long as you remain on that level obviously).

GAMEPLAY:
I feel this game is well worth the $10 it is going for, for its coming out of Early Access sale price. Even for the regular price of $12.99, I would still pay that much for this game. (***Note, publisher provided a game key for review purposes, but in no way has influenced my personal or professional feelings on this game***). If you mess up slightly and barely nick a hazard, it is not always game over! (YAY!) That is because it is possible to loose your wings, legs, and even head, and still be able to complete the level. I really really enjoyed this little perk of game play, so you can literally run around in game as a chicken with it's head cut off.
All in all, there are 3.5 world types with over 120 levels to beat. After certain levels, you get to unlock perks to add to your multiplayer death match, like Zero Gravity. The levels occasionally will have multiple paths to take to beat them, depending upon your play style.
Co-Op consists of local players dropping into game by pressing 'A' on their controller and helping you to clear the level.

REPLAY:
This game has decent replay as you go back through and attempt to beat your previous level clear times. There are a total of 3 coins per level to be obtained, which can then be used to purchase custom hats and headgear for your chicken. There is nothing like running around blowing friends up while your chicken wears a bloody Jason Hockey Mask!

Overall score: 8/10
Now go cause some mayhem!

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 April
Chicku's influences are clear: Flappy Birds, Angry Birds, Super Meat Boy... even the film Chicken Run. The result is a simple, amusing game that's charming enough to keep you playing for a good session or two.

The selling point of the game is the simple but annoying control system, based on rotating your chicken around 360 degrees, and tapping a button to flap in that direction. There's also a button that freezes you for a second, then fires you in the chosen direction, taking you safety past obstacles such as blowy pipes.

These controls result in genuinely funny (and gory) moments of chaos, and while you do get better with practice, the fact your chicken is so prone to gravity means you're never far from death.

Gameplay highlights video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9J16IWK3Z9w

Chicku's campaign mode consists of LOADS of short, spiky levels. Almost too many - the game spreads itself quite thin and you might be waiting quite a while before something genuinely new turns up. But the levels are so short (some can be completed in a couple of seconds) that it's got a great sense of pace, urging you onwards.

Most levels are just "get to the end", but occasionally you'll need to collect stuff over the course of as many lives as you need. For completists, each level has a one, two or three coin award depending on your best speed. The coins can currently be spent on randomly allocated hats.

There are more unlockables (that seem to come after a certain number of levels or coins, I wasn't entirely sure) that can be used to modify the game with crazy disco balls and the like.

The multiplayer versus mode, which I haven't tried yet, puts you in a choice of arenas of similar size to the campaign levels. A variety of weapons and tools spawn for you to collect and use to kill the other player. Time limits can be changed, silly modifiers (once unlocked) can mix things up.

So it's one of those games you jump in, get confused by for a few minutes, get into the spirit of it and blitz through half of it very quickly. Beyond that, it'll be worth a few more goes, plus waiting to see what is added over the course of early access. If you can sort out local multiplayer, the battle mode does look fun, although these days it's up against a LOT of arena games that offer more complexity.

* Version reviewed: 1.01 (Early Access)
