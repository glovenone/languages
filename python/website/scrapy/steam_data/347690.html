29 June

Keyboard mapping available in BETA

Hi,

due to popular demand I added a (rudimentary) keyboard mapping to the current BETA version.

It allows you to specify which keys you want to use when playing with a keyboard instead of a game controller.

Side note: Natural Soccer plays way better with an (analogue) controller, so do yourself a favor and use a game controller instead of the keyboard controls. If you happen to have problems using your controller, please post to the forums what the problem is and what controller you use.

But now on with the keyboard mapping:

For now there is no user interface to change the keyboard keys. Instead you create a text file containing the mappings you want to use.

To create a custom mapping, please create a file called "keyboard.txt" in the data folder of Natural Soccer in your user profile.

The full path to that file would be:

%USERPROFILE%\.NaturalSoccer\keyboard.txt


A popular request is to switch the D and S keys. To do so please copy and paste the following to the newly created "keyboard.txt" file:

UP=Up
DOWN=Down
LEFT=Left
RIGHT=Right

PASS=S
SHOOT=D
CHIP=A
SWITCH=Q
SPRINT=L-Shift


I hope the file itself is self-explanatory. Please note that everything in there is case sensitive!

About This Game

Natural Soccer is a highly playable arcade soccer game with fast-paced action, accurate physics and intuitive controls.


If you are a fan of classics like Kick Off, Sensible Soccer, Mircroprose Soccer, Goal! or Tekhan World Cup, Natural Soccer is for you!

Key Features

  • Fast paced action
  • Accurate and fun ball physics
  • Full and direct control of the ball, including "Aftertouch"
  • Smooth graphics (60fps) with our proven and loved top-down view
  • Simple and intuitive controls - easy to learn, hard to master
  • Up to 4 players against each other or local-coop
  • Very fine-tuned gameplay for maximum playability
  • Over 200 teams with more than 2.200 individual, carefully crafted players (due to licensing rights, no real names, sorry!)
  • Choose from different formations and set your tactics' balance to create your very own style of play
  • Strong but never unfair AI for both computer opponents and goalies
  • 4 different pitches to play on - each with unique characteristics
  • Play vertically or horizontally, alter camera height and tilt
  • Action Replays
  • Nice sound effects for a great stadium atmosphere
  • Pure fun gameplay that just feels natural

'Natural Soccer' tends to reminisce in the nostalgic 80's era of sports games, literally the classics such as 'Sensible Soccer' or 'Kick Off'.

I had quite a lot of fun playing this game (even though I'm not a die-hard soccer fan) and it's clear to see that the developer put quite an effort into this game!.
It may look a simple game from up top but beneath the exterior there's much, such as, a bunch of countries to choose from with team's from their respective division's, tweaking of pitch/gameplay conditions, incorporation of difficulty setting's for the goalkeeper's, live replay's, e.t.c.

*Play using a gamepad as it's the only way to truly experience the game and player movement, using the keyboard is a chore.

Pro's:-
Supports gamepad's.
Lot's of customization setting's for gameplay (replay's, goalkeeper settings,tactic's).
Simple but yet intresting graphics choice (looks like the classic's).
Bunch of countries to choose from, with team's from their division's.
Top-down camera doesn't move sluggish nor too fast (as in some other games).
Polished game with no bugs encountered.

Con's:-
Could add a bit more variation's to the crowd sounds (not that it's bad, just repititive after a while)
Non-existant tutorial and no keyboard button mapping option. (keyboard doesn't seem to have got much optimization)
Could use a few changes to the score and name display at the bottom. (bit hard to see sometimes)
No player/club customization nor stats or info about them.


The pricing for the game seem's just a tad bit high at $15, would have been great at about $10-$13.
Overall I'd recommend this game since it provides a polished experience with pretty deep gameplay, staying true to the formula which made 'Sensible Soccer' and 'Kick Off' great.

Please note: I received a free key for the explicit purpose of reviewing this game. No one associated with this game has compelled me to leave positive feedback. Observations and comments, whether positive or negative, are entirely my own. If you would like me to review a game, check out: fiverr dot com slash ThatAwesomeTerr.

Before buying this game, make sure you own a controller. Gameplay can be frustrating without one. I wasn't able to find a keyboard layout, or any instructions on how to use the keyboard whatsoever. As best I could tell, these are the keyboard keys:

- ESC Pauses the game/brings up the menu except when you're in Replay mode; pressing ESC again will not unpause.
- Arrow Keys Control player direction.
- A Slidekick when you don't have the ball; kick or pass when you do have the ball(not sure which).
- S Kick or pass the ball (Not sure which); starting/goalie kick; unpause/exit menu
- D Enter replay mode when paused; nothing when unpaused?

Like I said, the keyboard controls are extremely unclear. In addition, if you absolutely insist on playing with a keyboard, know that you won't have anywhere near the same level of dexterity as with a controller. That being said, playing with a controller improved my gaming experience by leaps and bounds. It was only with a controller that the game actually became a lot of fun to play.

For a game that feels very casual, I was surprised by the amount of in-game options there were. They go beyond just setting the basic difficulty. There are separate settings for goalie difficulty, how dribbling is handled, camera angles, and more.

Game setup goes even further. While there are only a handful of countries represented, there are dozens of teams to choose from spread across different divisions. The developer has even wisely incorporated a "Tactics" mechanic that allow you to determine how your team is positioned in the field.

Graphics are simple, but colourful. The ball can sometime be a bit difficult to see, but usually you can tell who has it by what direction computer-controlled players are moving. There's nothing really more to say on that.

A lot of attention to detail went into crafting the options for gameplay, which is why I'm so perplexed by the control scheme. It seems like a huge oversight. The developer should really consider adding some kind of keyboard layout or key mapper. If they really want to try and give mouse-and-keyboard players a similar level of dexterity, they might even try using the mouse to control direction and run speed.

At the end of the day, I can only recommend this game if you have a controller, though. It solved all of the gameplay and interface problems. As for pricing, I find it just a bit steep at CA$17 (USD$15). But, if you're someone who does enjoys soccer and wants a game with a great balance of depth and simplicity, it's worth the price.

But like I said-- use a controller.

With the newest update, 1.4.0, i finally feel comfortable giving this game a solid positive review. Before the update, i was not on the fence as to weather it was a good game or not. It still is and was a good game, with amazing support from the developer, who actually gives a damn about his product and the people who purchase it.

However, the game felt a little lacking. I felt less control than i would have liked. I dont mind hard games and challenges, but i also dont want to feel cheated by the limitations of the simplicity of the game.

That is where the beauty is in this one. You can tweak the settings to suit your gameplay style. The developer gives you the option to make this game your own. With each update, he doesnt remove options for the player, he just adds more and more.

speaking of this most recent update, that is what got me to write the review. I saw the update note and thought it was going to be the much anticipated player editor. While that will be an amazing addition when it eventually comes, this update was a welcome surprise for me. Adding the sprint option to speed up gameplay and really let me use the open pitch and give me the ability to get around defenders was wonderful. Then also adding the "easy" dribbling control to go along with it, so that it was really my doing when i lost the ball to the defense by being sloppy with turbo or passing was the icing on the cake. I feel like i lose or win now based on my actions on the pitch. The first touch is noticeable, i dont feel as though the defense is stripping me of the ball nearly as often as before, because i can turn and run.

Notes:

-This is a hard game, easy to pick up and play (especially with friends) but difficult to master
-This game almost requires a controller to play, but the controls are tight and responsive
-There are still some improvements to be made, but the arrow is always pointing up as this is clearly a passion project that deserves the support of anyone who is interested.

If you are a fan of the classic soccer/futbol games of years past, this is a title you must pick up. With a developer that gives great support and is always updating and improving the game based on suggestions from players like you and I, This game feels fresh and like its improving on a successful formula. I can now proclaim it is a great game.
