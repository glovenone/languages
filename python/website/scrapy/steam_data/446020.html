Jalopy Performance Patch

Hello everyone,

Today weâre releasing a new update for Jalopy which introduces a few changes to performance. As you know, optimisation is something that we are planning to work on throughout development and it will take some time.

ChangeLog:
  • Flickering lights option in the option menu now works
  • Work done towards framerate drop at night
  • Trunks should no longer duplicate items when placing 1x2x1 items in roof rack
  • Placing engine components into trunk should no longer make your car freak out

Adjusting graphic settings:
Weâve noticed that V sync can cause the game to suffer from a severe FPS loss. If you are experiencing any FPS drops at night, we recommend you turning off Vsync and SSAO. Running the game on low to medium settings during this can make the game run on our machines at around 90FPS in towns during night, where on high they could drop to about 20FPS.

Do adjust your graphical options, the low poly allows for a lot more leeway on these settings and know that optimisation will continue to be an ongoing process during development.

About This Game

***JALOPY IS AN EARLY ACCESS TITLE. THE GAME IS IN A CONSTANT STATE OF IMPROVEMENT AND IS NOT FINISHED. PURCHASING THE GAME NOW WILL HELP SUPPORT THE GAME AND WILL ALLOW US TO CONTINUE WORKING ON JALOPY TO REFINE, ADD MORE CONTENT, IMPROVE QUALITY AND IMPLEMENT REQUESTS FROM THE COMMUNITY***


Navigate miles and miles of tyre changing, fuel burning, carburettor busting, mud clattering terrain, through night and day, rain and shine. Adapt to whatever the procedurally generated world of Jalopy can throw at you.

Upgrade, maintain and care for your Laika 601 Deluxe motor vehicle. Keep close attention to everything from the state of your tyres, the condition of your engine and even the space in your trunk. Repair each aspect of your scrappy little car and install unique upgrades to deal with the changing world. Everything from cargo weight to the condition of your carburettor will determine how your car performs on the open road.

The rise of capitalism brings an economical conundrum. Scavenge for scraps to make a small return of investment, or become a baron of the open road and smuggle contraband under the eyes of border patrols to make a sizable profit.

Developer Greg Pryjmachuk worked on the Formula 1 franchise from 2009 through to 2014. In late 2014, Greg began work on this new driving simulation featuring the fictional Laika 601 Deluxe car; reminiscent of the East German âTrabbieâ, it will need much love and care to keep it going on this memorable road trip!

This is a single player game, it always has been, and it will continue to be one. The developer has confirmed on numerous occassions that there will be no multiplayer, and it's for the simple fact that there is no point in having a person have the ability to sit in the passenger seat and do nothing. So, if your looking for a game to play with your friends, go somewhere else. With that cleared up, this is a great single player game based around a trip to hungary with your annoying uncle who can't keep his storys to himself, the routes are partically randomized when you begin your trip, and along the way you will find lots of different loot to collect and use to make serious bank. The downside to the way he sets up the trips is that the game can feel really repeative, because there is a sense of random courses, but they do all look very similar. Currently you can't make your way all the way to Hungary, which limits the amount of gameplay before you get bored of driving the same routes over and over again, with the only thing changing being the loot along the way. For the price of the game I do recommend it, because it is a really nice indie game that you can get quite a good amount of game time out of.

This game is early access, so it does have it bugs. As it states, it is in Pre-Alpha.

Pro's:
- Unique gameplay
- Nice artstyle
- High replayability (contraband/loot, spawns random along your trip). Keep your eyes open while you drive!
- You can pimp your Lada (Laika)! (technical aspects mostly thus far).

Cons(for now):
- The game has a few bugs in its current state, but once aware, you can work around them mostly.
- Play value, in time, in it's current state, ranges from 2 to 10 hours (depending on how much you wanna pimp your ride and how many bugs your run into).

Note that when you run into a bug that prevents you to continue, you can always restart from the starting point of your trip, but keep everything you have (contraband & tools in the trunck and carupgrades).

This is a unique game, if it really interests you and you can handle the cons pointed out above, I urge you
to support the developer.

Jalopy is an AMAZING game, and I'm quite surprised it isn't extremely popular.

The game is set in Germany in the early 90's, where your 'uncle' shows you how to repair a car, leading you to drive on a trip through the east. Throughout the game, there will be issues with your car, such as the engine needing to be fixed, and the need for gasoline. I won't say any more though, as this game is worth the play.

Yeah, it is in Early Access, a very low point of development (pre-alpha to be exact), but the game is still amazing to play for what it is right now. I do have to say though, the game abruptly stops when you get to the bridge to go to Hungary. Even then, it's still worth buying.

Now, my ONLY complaints would be that this game constantly crashes.
