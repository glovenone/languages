Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Alongside our pre-designed levels, we also want to facilitate the creative minds of our players. Using Unreal Engine 4 and Steam Workshop integration, we are currently working on a Level Editor for players to create their own unique puzzles.
We have already started development on the Level Editor and plan on fully releasing the game once this is complete.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Less than 6 months.
Our programming team is fairly small and developing an end user Level Editor is new territory, but having already started development on the Level Editor they are confident that it will take less than 6 months.
The team at Itsfine Games are still studying, so we ask for your patience as we continue to develop our skills to grow Split further.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We intend to release the full game with a fully functioning Level Editor and with a total of 20 levels.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“For our Early Access release, we have 16 unique, fully-functioning levels for you to enjoy, each employing a combination of our various mechanics.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Price will not change when we leave early access”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We are hoping to get feedback from the community regarding any issues they have with the game and testing the Level Editor. Ultimately, we want players to be able to contribute their own creativity through new puzzles and levels to the Steam Workshop once the Level Editor is complete.”

Patch Notes 19 April 2016

Thanks everyone for waiting so patiently for us to get a break in our studies to patch Split. We have collected all of your feedback and now that we have had a chance we have a fairly big update. Level Fixes

  • Made the bridge in Jungle Crisis faster
  • Linked the third bridge in Cauldron
  • Changed the material for falling cubes in Stairy Chess.

  • Game now automatically ends when there are no cubes left.

  • Pressing escape twice no longer makes the game quit out.

About This Game

Get to the door
Try not to lose yourself



Split is a beautifully minimalist puzzle game where you divide yourself to explore, solve and create.

In Split, you take control of a cube, which has the ability to break up and split into smaller cubes. Using whatever cubes you have at your disposal, you must try to solve puzzles to reach the door at the end of the level, bringing as much of yourself with you as possible.


  • Beautifully crafted 3D environments
  • Challenging spatial puzzles
  • 4 Unique puzzle mechanics
  • 16 Hand-crafted levels
  • An elegant original soundtrack





Split is based off of a 1 week prototype originally designed for a university paper, which we have had the opportunity to develop over a few months

For our Early Access release, we have 16 unique, fully-functioning levels for you to enjoy, each employing a combination of existing and new mechanics.




Want to see your cubes do things never before thought possible? We are currently working on making these little cubes do some amazing things, alongside our pre-designed levels, we also want to facilitate the creative minds of our players.

Using Unreal Engine 4 and Steam Workshop integration, we are currently working on a Level Editor for players to create their own unique puzzles and then, share them with the community.


Made as part of MDS Studios

Music designed by Michael Borcard

Early Access Review
Posted: 3 April
Overall I had fun with Split. The mechanic of splitting the cube in order to solve the puzzles is an interesting idea and the game does a good job creating puzzles that use the idea well. The puzzles are clever but are a little easy. The artwork is great. People who like these type of puzzle games should enjoy Split.

While I think Split is a good game, it has a couple negatives at this point.

  • The camera could use some work since in some of the puzzles it becomes pretty hard to position the camera so you can see what you want to see.
  • I don't really like the timed switches/buttons. On some of the puzzles the switches don't give you enough time which means you need very good/near perfect timing in order to finish the puzzles even if you know what to do.
  • The game has some small glitches here and there which includes the game not always recognizing when you want to move one of the cubes and sometimes your cubes get destroyed for unexplained reasons.
  • At this stage the game is pretty short since it doesn't have a lot of puzzles. It takes only around three hours or so to beat all of the puzzles in the game. I hope the final version of the game adds more puzzles.

At this point I think Split is a good puzzle game that can be a great puzzle game if some of these issues are addressed.

If the idea behind the game interests you and you like puzzle games, I think you will enjoy your time with Split.

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 March
This is a unique multi-tasking puzzle game with a lovely art style. You split your cube into up to 8 separate bits and activate switches, move bridges and other things to reach the goal. The puzzles are decently forgiving in that you can basically "skip" a level as long as 1/8th of your cube makes it to the exit, but you have to find a way to get 100% of them to perfect a level, and if you skip too many levels without perfects you can't advance.

Despite the game being in Early Access it seems to be mostly done except the level editor, which sounds fun of course, but most of the single player is there and it doesn't really "feel" early access. Puzzle difficulty ramps up fairly quickly leaving little wasted time.

The one major downside I've noticed so far is performance is pretty rough. I have a 6950 (old but should be enough for this) and an i7 4770k and I have to turn almost all the settings down to their lowest to get it running at 60 FPS, it's a little under 30 at all max. Like I said it's early access so that might improve, and it doesn't get too much in the way of things. Just note you'll probably have to play with options if your hardware isn't top of the line.

Early Access Review
Posted: 7 March
Public Review on behalf of the Opium Pulses Steam group

Disclaimer: The copy of this game was provided by the developer for review purposes

Few words about the game
Colorful, beautifully minimalistic game with plenty of different levels, where each of them requires players to strain their brains. In Split, you're in control of cube with ability to split into smaller ones. Your goal is to reach doors using this power to figure out how each level works and how to pass them perfectly. And you will, don't worry, difficulty is not that hard, yet sometimes it will take you a while to solve some of them. Try not to loose parts of you on the way!

Advantages
  • Level design is good and ingenious, each level is really different, what makes this game hard to get bored of
  • Game graphics are minimalistic, nonetheless are pretty and very nice to look at
  • Variety of soundtrack in each level makes it even better to play through
  • Level editor is in progress, can't wait to see what community can create from it

Disadvantages
  • Game is a bit glitchy (graphic options reset after rebooting game or sometimes small cubes throw other ones into the air when they touch each others)
  • Camera spins only around center of levels and in some of them, for example Stormberg or Wild Rooftoops it's hard to place it comfortably (possibility of flying camera around would be much better)

Summary
Game is as pleasing to the eyes and ears as it's entertaining in every single level, 100% worth getting for puzzle lovers. It might feel a little expensive, but still it's challenging enough to have ton of fun playing it and trying to get all achievements (achievement hunters - embrace, it won't be easy!). It also have a potential of a tablet game, I hope we will get an opportunity to try it out there

Personal score
8.5 / 10
