About This Game

Ladra is a stealth action adventure that tests your abilities as the ultimate thief.

Estella lost her family at an early age due to war and disease. She escapes her foster parents only to stumble upon an old man that shelters her. He passes on his skills to her which she now uses to sneak around Brumine. Through the capital's dark streets she makes her way past guards stealing what she can grasp. After finding out the King's intention to start a war she decides to put her expertise to a better use.

Estella doesn't kill or incapacitates her enemies. Instead she uses the shadows, her speed and intelligence to always be one step ahead. With a keen eye she uses secret passages and tunnels to stay out of sight. With help from her friend Tonan, they'll do everything they can to put an end to the King's plan.

Key Features
  • Conceal yourself: Hide in places to avoid being seen.
  • Remain undetected: Use the shadows to keep out of sight.
  • Loot: Explore the levels to find all the hidden treasures.
  • Steal: Steal from guards to get important items and precious loot.
  • Secret Passages: Search for secret pathways in order to find treasure and new routes.
  • Pure stealth: Be a ghost. Get in, get out. Undetected.

Posted: 20 December, 2015
Ever play a game in the "Thief" series? This is what you get when someone decides to make a Thief game with RPG Maker and it's a fun experience.

No combat, just sneaking through shadows, crouching behind counters and fences, hiding in crates and closets while observing the guards patrol patterns and waiting for the right opening to make your move. Get spotted even once and you have to restart from the last checkpoint but since every room you enter is a checkpoint this is not an issue and the game maintains a quick pace.

Graphically, other than the main character's sprite, the game seems to use default RPG maker assets. If you ever played an RPG Maker game you know how this looks and controls. Nonetheless visual design is clean. Everything is laid out well, for the most part it's clear where you can hide and important details like treasures to pick up or entrances to secret passages are clearly visible. There is also a minimal but decent story about the character trying to sabotage war preparations, it does the job providing some context on the missions and the character's motivations.

On the negative side there are odd spelling errors here and there. Controls are solid but they have some quirks that could have been ironed out (my main gripe is how you have to be completely still to crouch or get up from the crouching position) and early on it requires some trial and error to figure out how the enemies field of vision works. Also keep in mind the game is extremely linear, each room/screen has one solution/route you have to figure out so it's best to get into it knowing each mission is a series of connected stealth/timing puzzles rather than expecting an "open" stealth game.

In conclusion this is a neat little indie title well worht it's full (low) prize. If you're a fan of stealth games and don't specifically dislike RPG Maker games this should earn a cozy spot in your wishlist and hopefully your library. Heartily recommended.

Yes another RPG Maker game...

Here is my quick look video:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AieoBV7On-4

But this one is pretty well done and has the unique aspect of trying to use stealth and shadows to sneak through halls and rooms unseen. It's actually quite fun and makes it feel and play a little different then all of the other RPG Maker games I've played.

There are a few mis-spelled words now and then, but it doesn't detract much from the game. My biggest gripe in the time I've played the game so far is that the escape key doesn't work at all, and I was forced to CTRL-ALT-DEL to manually abort the game. The game saves automatically, but still the ESC key should work as a method of escaping the game.

These are pretty minor gripes, and I think people who enjoy these classic RPG style games will be very happy with this entry on Steam, especially at it's low price point of $3.99 USD. Catch this game on sale, and it's even a better value.

If you are the type that doesn't usually enjoy these kind of RPG Maker games, then the unique steath aspect of the game will not likely make it fun for you, so I'd caution against buying it just for that aspect alone.

Grading this game on what I've played of the game.. I'd give it a 7 out of a possible 10. Recommended for people who enjoy these classic RPG sort of games, and not recommended for people who enjoy fancier/pretty RPGs or more action oriented games.
