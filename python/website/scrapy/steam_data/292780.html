About This Game

Join Pajama Sam, the world's youngest superhero!

...as he journeys to World Wide Weather, the storm-stirring factory in the sky! Sam's spent on to many evenings cowering under the couch, so he sets out to conquest his fear of thunderstorms. Just as our little guy begins to learn that storms aren't as bad as they seem, Sam accidentally send the weather system into a frenzy. Now, there is snow in Saigon, and sunshine in Seattle! He'll need your child's help to explore this wonderful world filled with challenging puzzles, fun games, and fantastic new friends as they help Thunder and Lightning fix the broken machines and return the world's weather to normal before Mother Nature finds out!

  • Play again and again, New puzzles, new friends and new challenges await each time you play!
  • Click points reveal hundreds and hundreds of hidden surprises!
  • Special games and activities encourage creativity, help teach spatial-relations and sharpen problem-solving skills.
  • Out of time? Save the game and finish it later.

Posted: 5 January
As I've looked through a lot of the Humongous library on Steam, I've also seen a lot of fans who grew up with the games (including myself) giving high reviews to this game. Like 10/10's and what not. To be frank, I like these games as well. But are they absolute perfect? Hell, no. Not by my standards at least. The best Humongous game was probably Pajama Sam 1, and I'd probably give that a 7/10 or 8/10. But what about Pajama Sam 2? Ah yes, dear reader. What ABOUT Pajama Sam 2?

The game does have a lot of color and charm. It looks nice , and there are plenty of nice jokes only adults would get, as usual. However, the biggest dip in the game would be the story. In the original Pajama Sam, the story had just enough control in the player's hands to make the story build to an ultimate climax no matter how they got their objects. In Pajama Sam 2, the same thing is present, but out of nowhere, after you put in the third missing piece, a new story element pops out of nowhere in the appearance of a new character that is there purely for the reason of building tension. What was interesting about the tension in Pajama Sam 1, is it was shown through the visuals and the build-up both inside and outside of the story. Everything was connected, but detached enough for you to have your own adventure.

And I think the biggest reason that the story just isn't that good is because of the set-up. Pajama Sam 1 had the point of the story be that you don't need to be afraid of the dark. Simple but still good. And the story built up to that overall point being the main message. In Pajama Sam 2, Sam instantly finds out, "Hey! My fears are just controlled by a couple ladies in a sky-factory! There's nothing to be afraid of!", leaving nothing to be learned but just having the player wait until the ending finally arrives.

Pajama Sam 2 is, by no means, a bad game. If you liked the charm of the last game, it's still here. At least that hasn't been killed off.... right Atari? But Pajama Sam 1 was so good, and I think Humongous tried to bring back the magic by doing a lot of the same things from the first game all over again. Sam overcoming his fears, the room with four questions, the lost items scattered across the world. It's all the same. Hell, Pajama Sam 2 loves to reference the first game a lot as well. You run into the carrot, some of the portraits have characters from Pajama Sam 1 in them for no reason, and there are binoculars for you to go look at the Land of Darkness. Pajama Sam 2 is basically Humongous saying, "It worked in the first game. Let's just do it again!" And I don't like that.
