About This Game

YDKJ: Movies is considered the most challenging game in the series – are you up for the challenge? Test your movie knowledge from back when movies were movies, and movie candy didn't have high fructose corn syrup!

If you've always wanted to see your name in lights, albeit lights from your computer monitor, then YOU DON’T KNOW JACK Movies is the trivia game for you! YDKJ Movies covers everything from Fred Astaire to Freddy Krueger, and all the Freds in between. With all the irreverence, humor and style of the original award-winning YOU DON’T KNOW JACK, YDKJ Movies will have moviegoers rolling in the sticky aisles.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, Vista, 7, 8
    • Processor: 500 MHz processor or faster
    • Memory: 128 MB RAM
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 675 MB available space
    • Sound Card: 16-bit sound card
    • Additional Notes: The game runs at 640x480 max resolution. Manually adjust your Desktop resolution if you want the game to fill the screen.

The game is fun, and definitely follows the YDKJ pattern, but the movie trivia does become only more dated and obscure with time, as even what were newer movies when the game was made are now decades old. Even if you're really into movies, you will still be lucky to get more than a handful of correct answers in a full game, despite the impression caused by the screenshots. This is especially true if you don't watch and rewatch classic films and the popular movies from the 70s, 80s, and 90s on a regular basis. It's still very good, and I definitely look forward to playing the multiplayer and hopefully letting others narrow down the answers a bit for me sometimes, but it seems fair to warn everyone that this is not the type of trivia game where one can reasonably expect to be really "good" at it. If you cannot have fun while being utterly clueless and guessing the wrong answers to silly questions, you will despise the game very quickly.

This game is really for hardcore movie buffs. I've watched probably close to 3000 movies in 3 decades but this particular YDKJ game is still quite hard. I wouldn't recommend it to a casual player, more for the super hardcore quiz / movie buffs.

On a note to the game itself, I'm pretty disappointed. There is no option to play full screen, no choice of screen resolution or similar. Looks crappy, it's a old game and to be expected. The commentator is funny for the most part but sometimes I'd rather just play than to listen to him.

Hate the fact that you always have to watch the intro which takes like a minute until you can actually play. There should be an option to skip the intro.

Verdict: Good value game for hardcore quiz (movie) buffs. Terrible integration on steam as in no achievements, no nothing, no option for screen resolution etc. The "Millionaire" games may be better suited for the casual player.
