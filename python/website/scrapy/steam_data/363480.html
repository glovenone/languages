2 March

The Next Generation of Grave Prosperity

It may be a bit sloppy to post this here, but I know many people out there are curious about what's been happening with that "patch gone rogue" which is now called "City of Amber."
It's still too early to make its Greenlight page, but I would like to post the completed opening FMV here so people can see where it's all going.

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z_iyLVH1Ab0

Grave Prosperity was never presented with a story. It was meant to slowly unravel as the game progressed through its episodic play. Unfortunately that meant that nothing was known about the Protagonist either. It made it difficult to really care about Amber and what she was doing. With City of Amber I decided to make it in the exact opposite way.

This time you will know your character's past, her feelings on that past, and where she plans to take her future- all right out of the gate. Even her body is a representation of her history and character. From the battle scars that cover her back, to the large wings tattooed under them which represent her desire for freedom from her current life. Everything tells a story in this game. And the rather thickly laid on theme in the intro will have heavy influence over the creatures and horrors Lilly will encounter in Prosperity. There aren't just unexplainable nastys crawling around in the dark anymore. Now many of the monsters (and sometimes locations) will be a product of Lilly's own personal demons and faltering sanity.

There is a lot going into this on all fronts, and I am very excited about its completion!

25 January

An Interesting Realization Has Dawned On Me!

I just had an interesting interaction with an individual on the home website of the Construct 2 engine that I am using to create GP: Redux. I feel that it was definitely a revealing and necessary one, so much that I would like to share it here. The guy's name is Taistelusopuli, and this was our conversation based around the combat demo video I just put out:

====================================================
**Taistelusopuli**
"That was a nice fight It seems though that evading Malik's swings is more about luck than timing..? The space is so small. I might be wrong though"
====================================================

This was very interesting to me, and it caused me to explain and really break down what is going on inside the project.

====================================================
**LDC Studios**
"Haha yes, it definitely takes some practice to get used to his attacks. But by the time you get to him you'll have had so much practice from fighting other smaller creatures that any flinch from an enemy will have your muscles trained to either block or dodge immediately. That is the main draw I want to come from this game. Players developing skill in themselves from experience while fighting throughout the story. I don't want grinding for experience points and increasing stats to be the safety net that carries them to victory. Answering this will actually give me the opportunity to really explain why/how I've done things the way I have!

Mechanically, the game is rather shallow in the sense that everything takes place on the same two dimensional plane, so I have to add depth by making enemies that are unpredictable and always unique. They are tough, but fair. As quick as the boss Malik may be (or any other enemy you may encounter), Amber is just as quick. And the quicker the player becomes, the quicker Amber becomes, giving you the edge. Also, having the enemy and the protagonist on the same plane means that no one is handicapped by things like a poor sense of depth perception. That means that the enemy is always within reach, and so are you. This levels the playing-field to shear skill and timing.

I also want the players to have to learn how each encounter works, and realize that no two creatures will ever approach you the same way. They're being created as organic individuals that act in their own unique way. So some may dodge attacks, some may block, and some may even try to counter. With these possibilities always being present, that means that the player's play-style will have to constantly adapt to the adapting threats. The story itself is not based in realism, but I want the combat to feel real in the respect that you are a stranger to this place and you will never walk into a situation fully knowing or understanding your opposition. In the game, as in real life, stats fall second to skill, meaning all you can truly rely on in this hostile environment is knowing yourself and use what skills you have/develop to survive.

I'm hoping that these things will keep the game fresh and consistently challenging. I don't want players to have just another mindless hack n slash on their hands. You could say that I want Grave Prosperity to be the "Bullet Hell of Hack N Slash," which demands extreme focus and accuracy at all times a threat is present."
====================================================

Sometimes I don't even understand the motivation of the things I create, but having to think on and answer to a comment like his is very revealing even to myself. I never full realize the logic behind what I'm doing until long after I've started! Haha!

About This Game

Grave Prosperity: Redux follows Amber Ridge as she finds herself broken down on a mountain road. Without any reception and no signs of passers-by, she is forced to walk to the nearest town- Prosperity. A place that knows no God and exists only to make you suffer. Utilize a deadly arsenal of agility, blade-work and gun-play to fend off the darkness that stalks your every movement. Collect EXP through solving puzzles and slaying grotesque creatures in order to upgrade your stats to better suit you survival needs.

** This game is no longer under development, and no longer for distribution by its creator. The patch that was being worked on for this title has been made into another game entirely called "City of Amber." Progress on its development can be found here: https://www.facebook.com/City-of-Amber-108073936245507/ **

+ Good animations despite low budget
+ Good voice acting for the protagonist

- Extremely low quality backgrounds
- No key remapping?
- Think i noticed a Husk model from Mass Effect......ehm, copyright???

REVIEW:
Clearly rendered in DAZ 3D, this challenging platformer action-horror game takes place in a Silent Hill like town called Prosperity, and just like the protagonist says "ironyyyyy". Upon exploring a hotel in the town, the world turns into a hellish version where monsters pop out. The starting seconds are very stylish. First i expected it to be a quirky slick Japanese game with bad quality for the sake of humour, wich i would definately have supported.......if it was the intention. The protagonist is very well voice casted, wich just like the soundtrack, wich sounds like Diego Stocco's score for Chernobyl Diaries, came surprising as the visual quality was low.

Unfortunately the game had so many flaws wich prevented me from living into the game and fully enjoy it. First of all,
our heroine can only be seen from two angles even when you can move upward and downwards, this is seen as sloppy work and it looks weird when she is sidestepping in and out of the image. The backgrounds for this game is the biggest flaw i think. The backgrounds are clearly 3D at core, but they have been inserted as 2D image files and given a corny unclear look. This collides with the clear and solid look of the 3D models. The proportions of the background objects such as doors also collides, with some doors for instance being three times bigger than the models. Who lived in this town, descendants of Goliath? This is absolutely not needed, the game would have worked fine with 3D backgrounds!
I also happened to become disoriented because of this. However the inventory display is stylish and well composed.

When entering combat, things helped ease up the pain a bit. It was fast, furious (still platformer though) and challening. It also grants you Experience Points you'll going to use to upgrade your character. And yes you will be able to sprint later in the game. I also noticed something absolutely amazing imo, wich completely came surprising since i was so disappointed with the overall. Several areas had its own death scene! These cutscenes have not very good, but solid cinematography, who the hell are these guys?!
It was sad to see this cute woman with glasses die though :<
However, i also often experienced enemies not following me over to the next screen
(image changes when reaching end of one platform), wich was......weird. Oh well, good for my health i guess!

This would have been a beautiful game if the backgrounds and controls were much more better and comfortable and
if the combat design would have been a little bit more stabile. Many of the pros i have given does not really affect the gameplay and the gameplay is what separates a game from a movie.
It's extremely hard for me to not recommend this. I wouldnt feel comfy with recommending it, but it has a
solid potential. This game, deserves a remake or a fully 3D remake with over-the-shoulder camera.
I would love a open-world horror game with the Dark Souls difficulty!

Grave Prosperity and I have an... interesting history, to say the least. Back at the last site I worked for (VGU.TV), I was given the opportunity to review the original form of this game given my history with streaming horror games, and presumably because I was the new guy and no one else wanted it. So I accepted, and... let's just say it wasn't great. Clumsy combat, awkward controls, unforgiving enemies, brutal bosses, and basically anything else you could think of to infuriate a player was present.

But this is the "Redux" up here on Steam, so let's not dwell too much on the past. I was genuinely surprised a new version had emerged at all, so I decided to give it a shot out of morbid curiosity. And, in fairness, things improved... slightly. I'm sorry I'm using so many ellipses in this. Anyway, a lot of fat had been trimmed from the game's initial version. Shorter intro, with some of the more ridiculous bits (like suriviving a sledgehammer to the head) cut out. Things felt a bit more navigable, and finding your way around wasn't quite as much a chore. At the same time, combat felt even more frustrating and impossible, and those ridiculous death animations still felt excessive and repetitive, especially given how often the game likes to kill you off.

I've never been a fan of the visuals, either; the enemies, whatever they are, mostly feel like generic "mutants" or "zombies" with no real appeal, and our heroine looks like a commission job from a Poser or Blender fetish artist on DeviantArt. Not feeling it. Despite all this, I made it to the end of the Redux version, which stops a great deal sooner than the first iteration. I'm guessing things are going to be more episodic this time around, between that and the "Part 1" designation. At least it didn't end on an impossible boss battle this time.

Despite my multiple fronts of frustration with Grave Prosperity, I give the developer some credit for stubbornness in sticking to its baby. Clearly, they believe in this property, and want to find a way to make it work. Whether it can, I can't say, but hopefully they at least take some lessons away from this and apply this to a different game or find a new format to tell the tale they want to.

As the game stands right now, I can't recommend it. I'm curious what the "major update" in the game's description will bring, so I may give it one more chance once that's done and released. If so, I'll update and amend this review accordingly. But in its current state? Nope.
