Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We've been working on the game for around a year making sure that the core of the game is rock solid! All gameplay mechanics are in place, combat is fully featured, the game is playable from go!

Now we want all of you wonderful and beautiful people to help us build the game up to be even more insane and fun to play - more crazy weapons, enemies, traps, tile sets! We want your feedback on the features we have and the new features you want!”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We expect to be in Early Access for around 6 months, though this is subject to change of course!”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We'd like to significantly expand the content of the game. Currently we have a basic set of weapons, enemies, traps, and a dungeon tileset to generate levels from - by the time we ship the full version, we'd like to have hugely expanded on all these areas, add in steam features such as stats & achievements, and add any new gameplay features requested by the community”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Fully playable! Combat is fully featured, keyboard/mouse controls as well as gamepad support implemented, and the initial set of assets (enemies, weapons, tiles, traps etc) have all been tested and rebalanced with our suite of gameplay mechanics, the procedural level generator refined to accomodate it all and all the new assets to come.

There is no final level so the dungeons will continue to spawn indefinitely as the player progresses. There are minor bugs with some sound effects, and some improvements to be made to the GUI, but our public beta & playtesting sessions have not revealed any game breaking bugs, though the possibility that they exist is always present”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes! We plan to raise the price of the game as it develops during early access. The intial price is low as we feel it is a fair price for what you are getting at the time of purchase, we do not believe that you should pay now for what you might get at the end, but should pay for what your getting in the game at the time you pay for it.
With the exception of Sale events, the price will not go lower than when you see it!”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We want to be talking to you fine, fine folks constantly about where we're at with the game, what you like / dislike, what you think would make it 5794% more awesome, and share with you our upcoming feature / content plans so you can feedback to us where you think we're going right or wrong”

About This Game



You play Barbara-ian, a mythical badass with perma-rage & a penchant for smashing. Featuring super fast paced physics based mayhem, one hit is all that stands between you and a quick restart

Barbara-ian is a randomly generated dungeon crawler focused on pure action! Gameplay consists of marauding through dungeons smashing everything in your path - doors, tombs, gelatinous cubes etc - using physics-driven combat ensuring nothing stands in your way. Smash through chests & loot the weapons inside to progress deeper into dungeonland!

Unlike other dungeon crawler heroes, Barbara-ian has no inventory, no skill system, no health bars. She doesn't want to duel with her enemies, doesn't want to carry a bag full of callipers OR tongs, and she doesn't need a spreadsheet to tell her how to kick â¥â¥â¥. One hit is all it takes to kill anything, even her!


In terms of progress, the game is feature complete and very playable but we want to add content content content and we need your feedback in order to do this. Early adopters can expect to be part of a rapid and responsive development process, as zany and oddball weapons fill Barbara-ian's arsenal, a multitude of labyrinthine dungeons are unearthed and enemies spring from the pages of our classic D&D Monster Manual!

We hope you'll join us and Barbara-ian for the ride!

QUICKRATING

GAMEPLAY 3/5
Nothing ground breaking, but a solid formula well executed. Seldom there are minor issues with the AI (doesn't attack), but it's totally acceptable for an early access game. I would love to see the main char use some sort of dash to make more use of your surroundings and kick/punch enemies in the traps instead of relying on the AI to trigger them by accident.

CONTROLS 5/5
Plays fluently, the button layout makes sense to me and is rebindable.

MUSIC & SOUND 4/5
Fitting the scenario and thrilling. Not a fan of the menu music tho, as the joke wears off quick.

VISUALS 4/5
Clean and distinct. Fixed lightning here and there wouldn't harm, if the hammer of the gods goes all over the place again (no pun intended), destroying light sources.

ADDITIONAL INFO

The music is GOLD and the controls are fluid, which is a must for this type of game - and the devs pulled them off real nice. Playing it with mouse/kb or gamepad feels both equal, but i prefer the gamepad to have all functions within the reach of my thumb.

Basically you clear a level and descent deeper into the dungeon. You can find different weapons that really make a difference to your gameplay. Combine that with Barbara-Ians slick moves, only ONE hitpoint and some mean traps and you are up for a good amount of fun.

I bought it in early access despite making some terrible experiences with that business model in the past, but Barbara-Ian was 100% worth it. I recommend it to anyone, who likes Gauntlet/Crimsonland/Hotline Miami 1 and brawlers in general.

Unfortunately the trailer gives a wrong impression of the fluid gameplay and camera control. It might be rendered in 30 FPS +/-, so keep in mind, that it doesn't resemble the actual quality of these aspects ingame.

The Gameplay

Barbara-ian is a top-down run and gun action game with a female protagonist. This should already make you want to play the game. If you need further convincing, read on.

With only one hit point standing between you and certain death (and the loss of your beloved Tome of Laser Vision), Barbara-ian is the true definition of exciting. One of my favourite parts about this game is how it forces you to diversify your strategies and step out of your comfort zone. You donât get a choice about which weapons you unlock, so learning how to use them efficiently and without blowing yourself up is a must, and youâll need to learn quickly. From OwlBear (Developers behind Icebreaker and Env) brings you Barbara-ian! the oddly charming dungeon crawler with a paper mache esque style and a woman whom closely resembles Arnold Schwarzenegger as the protagonist. Each level begins when Barbara-ian is dropped into a dungeon where she must face ghouls,goblins galore while also trying to collect weapons within chests that are dotted around the map.

Pros:
1.The Action is very consistent with a variety of wepaons you're never bored of throwing hammers or casting spells.
2.The movement of the character is very fluid with a choice of either running or rolling closely resembling hotline Miami.
3. The graphics of the game is quite inviting with a style that matches other games such as Gang Beasts or Teraway.
4. The developers personality shines through to the main product with their singing in the intro to their obvious willing to create a fun,friendly game that anyone could pick up.

Cons:
1. Their is an obvious lack of depth anywhere from the non-existent story to the lack of variety in the map
2. Their was virtually nothing stopping me from continuesly rolling on the ground without hitting a single enemy ever.
3. Their was barely any variety in the enemies with only 3 i could find. i stopped playing at 4 chests so their may be more that i simply haven't seen yet.
4. their were a few times that i glitched through the map and fell into the abyss but luckily their was a restart button.
5. Their wasn't enough options in the main menu.

Tags: Newstalgia, Action, Dungeon, Woman protagonist, Charming.

Ending Notes: Overall i would say that the pros outway the cons and that this game shows extreme potential in the developers and i hope to see Barbara-ian again in a sequel to come (Perhaps Barbara-ian in Space!). Also i played this game for 1 hour not 5 minutes, steam bugged out.
