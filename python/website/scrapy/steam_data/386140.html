Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“After The End: The Harvest is a game which is still in Alpha and ongoing development. This means that new features, graphics and content will continue being added as time goes on.

In terms of how the game is being developed, features and content could be added endlessly. Alpha, and Beta are primarily used to distinguish that the game is still in on-going active development and subject to changes.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“There is still so much more that I want to do with the game before taking it out of Early Access.
I want to ensure that each planet and the space overworld which exists within the game is completely populated with caves, villages, cities, quest-lines and dungeons. Planets currently have some of these things already, but if you play the game you will see that there is still much more that can/will be done.

Graphics and audio are still subject to change.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version is planned to have trading cards, game-pad support, achievements and more.

The long term goal is to add even more planets and areas within space with their own inter-woven storylines, cultures, quests and more. As this is an open-world game, a lot is also being done to ensure as little bugs as possible.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“After The End is currently a full game in the sense that it can be played from start to finish.
You can go on quests, explore one of the galaxies, fight with Titans and reach an ending.
Some areas are more populated then others, but overall there is things to find, see and do in each location.

The complete game will include multiple galaxies and even more content then whats currently included.

Current build is Alpha V0.09 and may still contain some bugs.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The game originally started as Free To Play on various websites up until V0.05.
As more content is being added the price will gradually increase.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The community is really important to me. They are the reason that the game has gotten this far. I have been listening closely to their complaints, suggestions and requests. I hope that people continue giving their requests for things they'd like to see in future updates.

There is so much possible with After The End: The Harvest! I want the universe to feel as full and alive as our own. The community is very important in helping me decide what to work on next.”

Steam Summer Sale + New Info

Announcement time!

RELEASE DATE-
After The End: The Harvest the full game will be releasing in Q4 of this year!
I have decided that at this stage of development, it is better to spend the time finishing the game, rather then releasing smaller updates which then need monitoring and then possible bug fixes and subsequent patches. This means that there will more then likely be no more updates on Early Access until the game is completed.
It has been a little while this time since the last content update, and I apologise too everyone who was/is looking forward to playing more of the game early, but, fortunately, the goal line is not far away, and some of the things in store for the future of game will hopefully make you all very excited.

Tomorrow is the beginning of the Steam Summer Sale and ATE:TH will be receiving a significant discount for the duration of the whole sale. This will more then likely be the last sale that ATE:TH will be apart of until the release of the completed game in Q4. Once the game is released and leaves Early Access, the price will be increasing.


E-BOOK-
Now let's get into some announcement specifics-
I'm pleased to announce the title of the free companion E-Book which will be releasing with the game! The E-Book will be titled-
"After The End: Orkess 'O' Orc and The Holy Army"
This story will connect directly to the game, and follow the story of Orkess.
Orkess, a former friend of Onuel's and fellow Titan Hunter, barely survived the devastation of The Harvest. After a few unfortunate events, Orkess forms an army and attempts to hunt down those who had wronged him, and, ultimately, attempts to establish a new identity for himself within the destroyed universe.

Orkess can be found on the current Early Access build as an optional follower who can help you fight, but in the current development build, and in the final completed version, Orkess will play a much more important and vital role.


STORY-
Speaking of story...
The next version/completed game will be expanding heavily!
I don't want to spoil anything, but I think a lot of you will really enjoy it.
The open-world gameplay you have all come to know and love, plus loads more cutscenes, gripping multiple endings, loads of dialogue choices/branches and so much more.
The current playable Steam build is quite light in a main story, but I think everyone will be quite pleasantly surprised with the direction that the game is heading in.


SCREENSHOTS- (HUD SWITCHED OFF FOR SCREENSHOTS)
This brings me to the picture which was posted on yesterday's announcement. Yes there is a Planet Earth, yes the game has undergone a bit of a graphical upgrade and change. Rather then explain, here are some screenshots taken in-game!










WORLD MAPS-
World maps are back and they are bigger then ever!
Planets now consist of different regions or "Countries" which are home to many different villages, dungeons and more.
When wandering around the various planets, the name of the country will fade in and out on the side of the screen. This will also extend to Space, where travelling between Deep Space and other Galaxies will trigger text which informs the player of their location.

PLANETS-
I'm happy to confirm that the full version of After The End: The Harvest will include over 15 explorable planets. Most planets will also have seperate explorable moons, with their own locations as well. (Similar to the current playable Steam build). In terms of the games size alone, the current playable build includes roughly 300 maps. Once again, I dont want to spoil much, but this number in development is now well and truely in the thousands. The game has grown in size, performance and polish tremendously since the latest public update. The game has never looked, or run this good and I sincerely cannot wait for you all to experience it.

VISUAL ARMOR-
If you look closely in the screenshots above, you can see different styles of armor and clothing options which will be included in the full game. Clothing will be paramount when exploring certain kinds of areas, and occasions.


HUD UI-
A fuel gauge will be added when flying any of your Spaceships.
In the current Steam build, you can track your fuel consumption by equipping the fuel item to your hotbar during flights. In the coming version, you will be able to find a gauge which automatically appears when taking off and tracks your usage.
Very helpful when planning your trips through space!
Another useful feature coming soon is the oxygen indicator, whenever you enter an area which has no oxygen, a notification will pop up to inform you.


SPACESUIT UPGRADE-
The Spacesuit will in the future, by default, include a Grapplinghook.

NPC'S-
All NPC facesets will be matching. Most NPC's will also have multiple pieces of dialog when spoken to repeatedly. Some NPC's can also be questioned through dialogue branches. When attacking a friendly NPC or village guard in the current Steam version, only Guards would react. In the coming version, guards will still attack, but NPC's will now also run away from the player and begin screaming untill the disturbance is over.

SETTLEMENTS-
Settlements will work in conjuction with the Earth's Assembly Point which was referenced in prior announcements.
Around the universe you will encounter many different humans and aliens with different proffesions. Persuading some to join you back on the Assembly Point will prove invaluable when trying to figure out the right combinations of settlement survival.

Do you send two medics and one soldier when building a settlement on a planet full of monsters? Or two soldiers and one medic on a plot of land being held by helpless natives. Depending on your choices and combinations, your settlers will either survive or perish. Scouting a location first can give you tips on the type of hazards which are needed to be overcome for succesful survival. Succesful settlements attract rare merchants and provide gold at a regular interval.

More information coming soon!

About This Game

Humanity has all but disappeared from its original planet and home, with only its remnants remaining scattered.
Those who were not worthy, did not graduate the cycle.
By chance encounter with an intelligent being, you begin your journey to unravel the great mystery of the universe itself.

There is no one way to reach the finish. Players are almost immediately left to explore at their own leisure.

-Huge Open World Set In Space
-Action RPG Combat
-Lootable Enviroments Including Planets, Moons, Derelict Spacestations, Monster Infested Asteroids & More
-Control, Fight & Navigate With Your Very Own Spaceship
-Constant Danger: Enemies Get Stronger The More You Play
-Crafting Hunting & Scavenging Mechanics

and much more!

Dont be put off by the introduction. This game is huge and does take a little bit of time getting used to the style.
I was expecting that I could immediately find a ship and fly into space. This wasnt the case.
You can rush through the beginning portions and get the ship in about 5-10 minutes.

This game is definantely an open world sandbox but also at its core has some nice RPG style progression which helps
structure the overall arching story. You start off as your players daughter learning the controls around a peaceful little town. After a few events which slightly change based on what you did in the tutorial. The town becomes anything but peaceful. The introduction does a decent job at setting up the various tones which exsist through the different explorable games worlds.
After this introduction and tutorial you are finally let loose on the huge explorable worlds that make up the game.
This is where the game truely begins to shine.

At first I had spent my time trying to avoid the main quest line which involved tracking down and defeating giant monsters
called Titans and instead just visiting the different planets and moons.
During my travels I experienced many different things. I got robbed by raiders in the middle of space, went to jail for killing villagers, slaughtered countless villagers , explored space, defeated giant space worms who also drop a fairly strong summon btw. crafted support turrets and even bought a house.

Controls are a bit different then your usual action game but I think thats because it isn't really an action game but
more a hybrid between action and traditional rpg.
There is a review that mentioned Fallout and I guess this game does feel like a 2d version of that.
For the price, I dont know what more you could want.

Some issues that I did experience is that there was a couple of typos and a few things that are walkable that I dont think are meant to be. As well as a couple of quests that I just could not figure out mainly the one involving the reptilian captial. All in all they were small issues and didn't really impact the game at all but I hope they get fixed.

Early Access Review
Posted: 22 July, 2015
At the moment, (version 0.07) this is a game that shows a lot of promise, but definitely is covered in "construction" signs.

You are The Wanderer, which seems an odd title, as you clearly weren't a wanderer before the start of the game, unless you just wandered around town. Maybe all the other people in town are wanderers too? Anyway, you're a character sprite you choose from a list of character sprites with no backstory because the plot's not really written yet. A Beautiful Woman chased by The Bad Guys crashes near your house, so you naturally do the heroic thing, and steal her ship for yourself so you can go joyriding and see the universe, meet strange and fascinating new people, then murder them and loot their corpses for their stuff! Ostensibly, you are doing this to find The Beautiful Woman's soul because she's apparently now your Love. (So what if you're a girl, you apparently swing that way!) You have to rescue her soul because The Bad Guys... umm... think it would taste great with jam? Or maybe just put it in a lava lamp or something? Anyway, you have to go to The Centre to find it and beat the game. This amounts to fighting several giant Bad Guys, and one slightly bigger giant Bad Guy who shows he means business because he's a different color than the others and deals marginally more damage.

Along the way, you'll meet several alien races, most of which are hostile because The Harvest swept up all the good races, and took them to a Higher Plane of Existence. Basically, it's the Rapture, but with dinosaurs and little gray men instead of the Antichrist.

The new version brought with it a "sci fi" pack of portraits, so the little gray men no longer use standard RPGMaker elf portraits, and now means the weapon dealers have a face that looks like a discarded cicada shell, and the catgirls look like goth girls with witch hats. The dev assures us that none of the art is final, but it's somewhat amusing in its current state, anyway.

The game really only has about 3 required missions, and the rest are basically you just visiting random places, being asked to pick a side in some battle between two opposing races, and murdering whichever side strikes your fancy or didn't give a bid. In some senses, it's a bit like Skyrim in that regard, although also like Skyrim, it tends to wind up with me walking into some alien base, killing everything, then accidentally hitting a self-destruct button, and then being told "Congratulations, I just completed a quest I didn't actually accept!" Gee, I hope I didn't miss any important lead-up to that. I wonder who I just killed?

Enemies range from wolves and lizardpeople and skeletons you kill by the dozen with a couple strong attacks to giants that take shooting for a full minute while dodging their fireballs and chugging potions like crazy while trying not to think about whether the junk made by those red imp guys are really without any side effects when you take 40 times the reccomended dosage. The fireballs are slow enough to make them easy to dodge if you stand further than 4 tiles away, but many weapon attacks tend to force you to stay still for a second or so, and so it's best to dodge, then shoot. A lack of ability to aim your character without stepping a tile towards your enemy is also an annoyance, as there's a rather small finite number of tiles on the screen to start with, and the player character is such a cool customer, (s)he never bothers to go at more than a walking pace. So what if you're being followed by a flaming boulder rolled by a living volcano, you didn't pick the character who wears leather pants and shades at night to break your cool!

Weapons range from projectiles with various blast patterns to melee weapons you sell to buy more ammo for the projectile weapons. The low number of frames of animation mean an enemy is already hitting you by the time the axe appears, so melee combat is essentially impossible to dodge and comes down to spamming the attack button to kill it before it kills you. Save your potions, abuse your ranged weapons.

Currently, a few imbalanced weapons make the game fairly easy, as stun weapons can paralyze even a titan that by all rights should squish you like a bug. Magic and projectiles in general need reworking, as they tend to be either easily evaded or, in the case of some spells, are impossible to evade and hit 20 times in a row with enough damage to kill you if you aren't spamming the potion button like crazy.

It takes some clear references from Secret of Mana and Legend of Zelda. Some of the monsters are clearly in the art style of Secret of Mana. You have a "grappleshot" to cling to posts, bombs to open caves blocked by rocks, etc. You can even find outright expies where "Dink" is asked to fight "Cannondork" to save "Princess Xena"...

While it undoubtably has helped speed development, as there are a lot of scripts available for RPG Maker "off the shelf" at this point, the biggest limitation of this game seems to be its engine. It would play better without the RPG Maker engine, whether it's because the RPG Maker engine is so badly optimized that even these graphics lag the game, or because it forces the game to stick to a grid-based system, when free-form movement would better suit the game.

Early access games should generally be rated on how they are, rather than what you hope they might someday be. What it IS is still clearly under development at a pre-alpha level. (There are some towns that just don't have doors because the shop interiors haven't been made yet...) That said, for the dirt-cheap price they're asking for it at this early state, it's more than enough entertainment to justify itself. I give this game 8 stunlocked final bosses I kill with a weapon I got in the first village out of 10.
