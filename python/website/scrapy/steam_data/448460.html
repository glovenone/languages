Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We want to make sure the game is stable and fun. We had a good time playtesting this game and we are happy that you can now join us. Early Access has been created so YOU can influence the development process. Help us to make this game a KILLER”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We plan to stay in Early Access for 3 months to polish the game and balance it.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Currently the game is in Beta. During Early Access we plan to focus on improving the core gameplay and the balance. We also plan to add graphical improvements. But this is only what WE plan - your input could be pivotal!”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Fully operating multiplayer - have fun! (Ok, we THINK it is stable! You might prove us wrong)
- Three main roles - Civilian, Sheriff and a Killer (It should be fun to play any of these!)
- Many additional roles
- A large amount of items which affect gameplay, improving the player experience
- Voice chat
- You gain random items on level-ups and can unlock new ones.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We intend to keep the game free, allowing players to only pay for items they really want.
The first month of Early Access will be completely free. (You cannot pay even if you want to!)”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“Well, the more players we have - the more feedback we can get so the better the improvements to the game.
Our team intend to communicate with players directly on the forums and give answers and solutions as quickly as possible. You may find us playing the game with you - and can have a direct conversation over voice chat as well.”

About This Game

Every round the Player is allocated one of three roles: Killer, Sheriff or Civilian and should behave accordingly.
As a Murderer:
  • Will you hunt Civilians one by one in a dark corner?
  • Or will you take out your blade and run through the middle of a crowd?
  • They will all scatter in FEAR!
As a Sheriff:
  • You are the last hope of the civilians and the first target of the maniac.
  • Will you suspect anyone? Will you choose to save lives?
  • Will you reveal your identity early?
As a Civilian who will you become?
  • A fearless journalist, who takes photos of the bloody drama in front of him, risking his own life?
  • A Medic, desperate to help the victims of violence?
  • A Zombie – back from dead to hunt your murderer (and others)?
  • A Scientist, a Psychic, a Ghost, a fireman… Anyone else?
  • A Survivor?

In any case – LOADS OF FUN guaranteed!
And you WILL come back for more.

Early Access Review
Posted: 28 April
Crazy Killer is still relatively based on the pretty famous party game by Dmitry Davidoff in 1986, or rather commonly known as the Mafia; Werewolf; Town of Salem. However this game is much more simpler compare to the original gameplay. Crazy Killer consists of the Killer, Sheriff, and the Civilian. The maximum players in a round is 14 players, 2 Killers, 2 Sheriffs, and 10 Civilians. This game doesn't have a day/night phases. Players will be given a non-weapon period from the start, where you can explore and hide your positions or collect stars. Stars will later be converted to silver coins; in-game currency. There's also gold coins to be earned to buy special items from the Shop. At a start everyone will be logged into the Lobby area, where you can purchase weapons, skins, tutorials, and banking. There will be a round countdown timer before it starts, or when there's an ample amount of players that are ready for the next round.

Basically the Killer will have to kill all the Civilians and Sheriffs in the round, in order to win. While the Sheriffs will have to kill the Killer. Civilians will basically be staying alive throughout the round, without being killed. All players have a chance to point to the public which Killer is, while the Killer also have a chance to divert the attention of his role. A fancy box will also be spawned at random areas, should the Killer be not found after Sheriffs have been killed. However the weapon will only last for that particular round only, for one usage. The items go by the per usage round, so there's no items that are permanent usage, probably they might implement them as a cash item. However the weapons shouldn't have an issue, since all weapons does a relative same amount. The guns will have an set amount of ammo though, so it's best to think whom to shoot for the Sheriffs.

The game is build based on the Unreal Engine 4, so you can expect pretty smooth gameplay. However though this is still in the Early Alpha stages of this game, there's no other features, like servers or graphical enhancements, inventory or cash payment system. For networking wise, I'm getting around 300ms ping that's connecting from Singapore. The framerate will be capped at 80 fps, on high settings. There will be times when you leveled up, the leveled up items doesn't show up once you completed the round. You might have to exit to the menu and enter the lobby again. There might some desyncing issues when you try to pinpoint the players whom are the killers, because of the camera view and the cursor movements. In overall this game is worth to be downloaded if you like funny TPS games.

Improvements can be made for example indicating whom is the in-round names matching to whom is the Steam name. Everyone will appear as in-round randomly generated names, but once after the round ends the overview shows everyone's Steam name and their roles. Which I think it might make a confusion to players, as to did Killer killed his mate, or even Sheriffs killing their own mates?
