What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We have been working on the game for more than two years and now want to invite the steam community to join the development process to help us create as fun an experience as possible.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“The full release date will ultimately depend on player feedback and interest. Ideally we would aim for a late 2015 release.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“We currently have a solid platform to expand from. We have a lot of fun ideas but we’re also reluctant to make promises we may not be able to keep. That being said, we can guarantee the following:

More polished gameplay
More game modes
More levels
More weapons
Getting rid of what bugs might show up.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We’ve been working on finishing a game that isn’t riddled with too many bugs, and we feel can work as a platform to properly showcase the game and its potential.

We currently offer deathmatch and speed-run game modes.
Players can choose between 4 levels and 7 different weapons for combat.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“Yes, we believe the people participating in early access are doing us a service, and the early access will therefore be slightly cheaper than the finished game.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We plan to be active on the dedicated steam forum and hope you’ll join us, regardless of your opinion.”

About This Game

Gunjitsu

Gunjitsu is a fast-paced 2.5D Multiplayer Platform Shooter, where players fight out hectic gunfights in fully destructible levels. Apart from a range of crazy weapons the game features a unique grappling hook mechanic and different styles of jumps that allows players to navigate the levels, dodge bullets and make insane headfirst assaults.

Gunjitsu is a game of skill and reflex, and all about keeping your cool. Learn to use the hook to assault and evade, learning to curve rockets, make precise shots, to cover your opponents in hails of well aimed bullets, or to make devastating fly-by attacks. Be the fastest on the trigger, the most acrobatic with the hook. Learn to aim and time your shots like a pro, or outmanoeuvre your opponents with lightning fast hit-and-run attacks. No matter how you choose to fight, these are all parts of mastering the art of Gunjitsu.

The game is heavily inspired by old platform shooters such as Soldat and Liero, and the feel and pace of these games. We wanted Gunjitsu to be all about fun, adrenaline and skill, and as such the focus is on creating an experience that rewards skill in a setting that is both hectic and nerve-racking. Gunjitsu is bringing a gun (or rocket launcher!) to a knife fight.

Challenge your friends or test your skills against the world.

Key Features

  • An awesome grappling hook - fly about the level like a true ninja
  • 2 game modes: deathmatch and speed-run (solo and multiplayer)
  • Custom game modes (eg. sniper only, rockets+guns, no-grenades)
  • Fights against bots, or add them to your multiplayer games (both online and offline)
  • 4 different and fully destructible levels
  • 7 different weapons for combat to suit your fighting style
  • Leaderboards

We have dedicated servers in the following regions: EU, US, JP, AU, ASIA

It's always nice to play a game that you thought was going to be complete crap and end up enjoying yourself. There isn't a lot to Gunjitsu, it's just a really solid sidescrolling multiplayer shooter. But it's the few extra little things sprinkled on top that build on the simple core gameplay to make it really enjoyable. Destroyable pixellated enviornments are a splendid touch that sometimes adds an unexpected dimension of fun or strategy to the match. The vertical arena layout makes kills extra satisfying, making blood and corpses tumble downward in copious amounts. While I hesitate to recommend a multiplayer game with a non-existent online playerbase, I found the bots to be surprisingly worthy foes. As amusing as Gunjitsu is, it can only benefit from additional development, so I do hope this one doesn't get abandoned.
