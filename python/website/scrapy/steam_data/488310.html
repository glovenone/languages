Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“This game is in an early stage and has room to grow, we plan yet to add more features and be open to community feedback for improvements to the game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“We don't have an estimate for the date of the full release, the quicker the development of the remaining features goes the sooner the full release.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The largest and most popular feature request we get is for online multiplayer and this is one of our main focuses before taking the game out of early access.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“Two complete portions (Single Player 1 vs. 1, and Waves) are available and are ready to be played.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We plan to gradually raise the price as we ship new content and features.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“By listening to feedback and continuing to deliver improvements through updates.”

About This Game

Single player virtual reality ping pong! Play Single Player for a 1 vs. 1 regulation match or take the Waves challenge and face serves of all difficulty to rack up points for accurate returns.

Simulation Details
The details of regulation ping pong were closely adhered to and the game accurately models the masses and restitution coefficients of the ball, paddle, and table as well as the magnus force and air resistance which give the players the ability to place spins of the ball that cause arced trajectories.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 10
    • Processor: Intel i5-4590 or AMD FX 8350 equivalent or greater
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: Nvidia GeForce GTX970, or AMD Radeon R9 290 equivalent or greater
    • Storage: 500 MB available space

This game doesn't actually play anything like ping pong.

It is like playing with a bare wooden paddle. There is no grip, no spin, no controllability, no nothing. I kept trying to return the ball with hard top spin and had about a 20% chance of even hitting the ball as my paddle just clipped through due to the speed of the slice. I was only able to return the ball about 10% of the time as any spin I put on it was ignored and the ball flailed off in a random direction. I couldn't even get off one succesful serve. I am not a professional, but I play alot, and this isn't anywhere close to being an accurate simulation of ping pong.

Also, they put the stool with balls immediately to the left, preventing me from even playing that side of the table.

While I appreciate the developers intention to make a ping pong game, they clearly have never played the game in any serious manner.

I do not think this game is quite ready. The physics do not feel like ping pong and the spin mechanics are very basic. It is very hard to judge how hard to hit the ball. Hit it normally and the ball will go almost nowhere. Hit it harder and it flies away. Without proper spin mechanics you cannot do a top spin shot so any power goes to waste.

I also find that the ball appears to change speeds as it bounces. It may just be an optical illusion due to the lenses but it is a bit disorienting.

I believe there is potential here but the developer should be focusing purely on the physics right now. That is what will make the game. Rockstar Table Tennis was great because it was accurate, visceral, and consistent.
