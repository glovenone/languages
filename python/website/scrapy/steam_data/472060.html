About This Game

AN EARLDOM AWAITS YOUR HAND.

A truly medieval card game, that is free to download in the modern world!

Ortus Regni is a new and novel card game inspired by the late Anglo-Saxon period of English history. The Late Anglo-Saxon era in English history is a time of warring Earls. Minor kings all, they claim whatever lands they can. They establish fiefs, cultivate powerful vassals, and fight and engage in endless political struggles. All the while, the Vikings roam not just the sea, but the land.

It is truly a Dark Age. Or is it?

This is Ortus Regni, “the birth of the kingdom.”

The ancient world is dead. The world is reborn. Anyone can become a king through politics and diplomacy – or by the blade. And it is always, in the end, by the blade. Will it be you?

Ortus Regni is a unique closed-deck, deck-design strategy card game. Players take the role of great Earls vying to control what will become England. Ortus Regni was inspired not only by Anglo-Saxon history, but also by the art of illuminated medieval manuscripts.

QUICK AND EASY DECK BUILDING: Or let the app build you one, and the save the decks you like.

TUTORIAL SYSTEM: Journey through the basics of the game.

COMPLETE CARD SET INCLUDED: no in game “currency” or card harvesting.

NOVEL STRATEGIC CARD GAME INTERFACE: Pictorial cards and a replicated tabletop experience.

Ortus Regni is based off of the premium tabletop card game of the same name. To learn more visit: http://ortusregni.com

I have read in the reviews and on the forums that some dont get the game or that they think it is too hard.

It isnt hard at all if you have ever played other card games like magic.

It is simple yet deep enough at the same time. The atmosphere, the look of the cards and the sounds feel very medieval. Very.Medieval. You couldnt ask for more.

Also the game is free not free with payment option but free. Yay.

You dont unlock cards you have them all from the beggining.

The tutorial is nice enough. It gives you a quick overview over the game mechanics. But you have to play some matches for yourself after to really understand. Though the story it told was nice enough.

You can plot against other players with your cards, bribe their armies, control the church, call out tournaments and go to war. You can even conquer their lands kill their lords and much more.

You can delay the vikings...make them come faster... try to bribe them or prepare to defend.

Your lords can be taken prisoner in battle under some circumstances and you can ransom them.

Also you need to make sure that your line doesnt die without an heir. Or else the game is lost. I wont explain how you do it here i just want to give you a quick overview.

If you build a balanced deck and play your cards right then there is a counter for everything.

Battles have a random factor but not in a annoying way.

I understood the game in less than 1 hour... now i have not mastered it but i dont even need to read what the cards do anymore.

Its sad that on one hand people demand for more complex and thought out games and on the other hand they turn arround and say a game sucks when its just what they demand in the firstplace.

No offense if this game is hard to you you have the attention span of a goldfish...

downvote me all you want but if you cant even take the time to read and understand simple card explanations then you should go back to.... DUN DUN DUNNNNN cod.

100% FREE CARD GAME (no currency at all).

NOT a Collectionable Card Game, you have all the cards immediatly.

TRULY ORIGINAL RULES explained in a really nice way on this page of their own : http://www.ortusregni.com/learn/ as well as on a in-game tutorial I didn't try.

2 to 4 players !

GREAT THEME with Faithful game interactions !

LIMITED randomness : There's always a way to influence the random aspects (even if you can never reach the 100% chance to win a random pull). Since there's not much different cards AND deck size isn't high, you quickly have an idea of your opponent's decks, possibly their draw probability but yours for sure !

The only downsides are :
- Not a beautiful art on cards (still fits really well with the theme).
- Requires patience to integrate the rules (no problem if you have patience or someone to explain :p).
- Few cards (helps to read the game).

SideNotes :
Those who, like I do, enjoy challenging their brain, particularily when it is to beat other players, will enjoy the refreshing ideas this game brings on the table!
I really enjoy the amount of possibilities despite quite a low amount of different cards, the way the vikings interacts with the players, the speed in which you can make a deck between games (I don't know if they added it but I read that in the phyiscal card game you can completely change your deck between games in Bo3 or Bo5 and that the winner may even have his deck locked so that the looser can adapt)!
This game also implies "mind games" and I love it.

My congratulations to the game Author, Jon Sudbury, and to the entire team !

I have not yet played multiplayer so as of now thats all I see that need some attention.

Give the game a try, it's free for crying out loud. Nothing to lose but your time and or friendships. And heck if you like it go and support the team by buying the actual physical copy.
