Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“We need Your feedback to improve the game. There are still features that we need to finish before the game release such as guilds. We want to grow our user base steadily so that we can adapt to their needs.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“As long as we have polished the game enough and finished all the main features that we would like to see in the game, hopefully with many additions from Steam Community suggestions.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“Full version will have less bugs and be better in every way. There will be guilds, Steam Trading Cards, more animations(we are hoping to find a talented graphic artist to join our team) and many more features.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“We have been working on the game for three years so far. It has quite a lot of content already - 25 unique hand crafted maps, over 1450 items, 15 skills and over 200 different enemies to fight with. Basically we have enough content to keep even the most hardcore gamers entertained for months.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We are not planning to change pricing, game is free to play and will stay that way.

It is possible to buy premium currency that can be used in the game but that is not required and the game has been designed so that nobody will have to miss out on anything if they are unable to buy premium currency. In fact, we will give it away to our most loyal players for just playing the game.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We have a suggestions forum where players can post their ideas. We are hoping to see some great ideas that we can use in the making of the game.”

About This Game

RPG MO is an online multiplayer role-playing game that gives players the opportunity to explore many paths. Players can build up combat and magic skills and acquire spells, weapons and armor to become formidable fighters, and they also may build up crafting skills and create items for themselves or for sale to others.

The game is an open-world sandbox experience where players may choose their own road, and a key component of gameplay is a vibrant player market where any object may be sold to other players. It is a true free-to-play game; even the most desirable items can be acquired from other players with in-game currency that is built up through gameplay.

RPG MO is created with adult gamers in mind, and progress into higher levels will not come quickly. Younger players also may enjoy gameplay, particularly if they are willing to be patient and develop skills.

RPG MO Includes:
  • Explore 25 hand crafted beautiful pixeled large worlds
  • Explore randomly generated dungeons with up to a 4 player party
  • Fight monsters
  • Fight other players in many different modes - guild wars, RSC-wilderness like map, last man standing arena
  • Level up 15 different skills with no level caps
  • Build your own house and invite your friends over for a party
  • Choose or breed yourself a pet from our 160+ pets
  • Become a collector and try to get all our 1450+ items

Follow us on Twitter https://twitter.com/RPGMO

Early Access Review
Posted: 10 June
This game can be fun, it can even be great if you meet the right people, and play the right way. Unfortunately, the converse is very VERY true. (no, this isn't a review solely about moderators. Keep reading.)

When I first quit
I left this game probably about eight months ago, mostly out of boredom for the lategame being slow and largely just lacking content. It seems like it wants to be something along the lines of Runescape, but it just can't manage because instead of content in the late game, you just get filler.

Example: Smithing. You get to the point where you can make steel, and then you make steel for the rest of eternity, due to materials being overly difficult to obtain, or steel being the better option when you do the cost-benefit analysis.

Current state
Eight or so months have passed now, and I'm back. I honestly can't see many changes that have taken place over that timeframe (Sure, there's archery, but that's about it). RPG-MO has been (and probably will be) plagued by the same issues it's had from day one.

  • There's little in the way of mid to late-game content. I understand MMOs are grindy. I've played more than my fair share of them. There's just a difference between fun grinding and endless mind destroying grinding. This game needs something at middle-upper levels that gets people excited, something unique to look forward to and work towards.

  • There's issues with chat mods being jumpy. To their credit, they put up with a lot of crap in a day. (and before anyone speculates, I have never even recieved so much as a warning or removal of a message) Changed this for two reasons. They've been -trying- to improve lately. There's some days that they get flak over doing things right, and other days where they certainly DESERVE flak for being too jumpy, and sometimes even breaking their own rules directly after enforcing them! For the sake of not getting this review removed, I won't post examples, but I'm more than happy, willing, and able to share screenshots with people who contact me.

  • The documentation on the game is poor/remains to be poor. If all the info were centered in one wiki instead of spread out across three places (the forums, the wikia, and the in-game wiki), it would be much MUCH easier

  • The UI design is lacking and in some places, downright incompetent (namely the bank and the market). One example of this is when you're using the market to trade with a specific person -- why offer a dropdown where the name is if you know it won't even fit on the screen? Instead, make it pop up a list of people you've recently whispered/who've talked in chat/who are on your friends list.

  • The game relies on mods (which come included) to fix gaping issues with some of the systems. I understand the developers would like to give people options to fix or leave something alone, but this should be given in the form of an options menu (which is considered as base-game) instead of a mods menu. This gets back to the whole UI issue above.

  • Some controls are downright unlisted. Nowhere that I saw mentioned you had to ctrl+left click an item in your bank to favorite it. Might have missed it, but it's not even there.

  • The world map is highly outdated. If you open a local map, it's fine, but unless you're experienced and know all of the locations, you're going to be asking questions.

  • Combat tends to be fairly bland, particularly with the complete lack of animations. It's difficult to find someone to animate everything, but it's something that desperately needs to be done for the game's future playability.

I've tried to love this game, I really have. Despite this, my review may even come across as harsh, or even toxic. I'll probably end up playing it more to see how it turns out, but in its current state, I simply cannot recommend it because there are many issues that have been left alone for so long.

Posted: 19 June
It says 0.2 hours on record but that is not true as I have played it through the desktop client at MO.EE for over the past 2 years.

Most may know me in game as Sanitarium and as long as I've played the game i figured it's time I gave an honest review on it.
It has a nostalgic feeling to it from when Runescape was first released which is what brought me to it.

Massive variety of things to do in it, many skills, an island system where you can build your own farm, house, graveyard, boss trophy collection area, raise and breed your own pets to sell to other players. pretty much anything you want there.

End game content caps at 120 but there is no level cap, there is a wiki for anything you need to know about and it continuously gets updated with new content added.

The community is helpful and friendly as well as you can multi-boss with friends, go into Player Quest Dungeons with them, skill together and eventually I believe an in game guild system may be introduced.

The staff is helpful and listens to their players. I've seen many reviews complain about staff members saying they abuse their powers but that is not the case considering they could not take responsibility for breaking the rules and would rather blame staff for their own mistakes.
Many reviews I've seen that are negative on here about the staff are people who I watched break the rules or were people who were botters that were banned.

The rules are very simple, be respectful, don't cheat, and if you're going to talk about something that is considered an adult topic then go to the 18 chat. That includes about other video games, as silly of a rule as some people think, this is actually a rule on quite a lot of MMORPGs to not talk about "competition"

The combat system is simple to understand, magic adds to your next melee hit. The UI has been frequently updated over the past several months on things making the game more and more enjoyable.

The only thing I have a problem with on here is that I believe the tutorial system needs to be updated to include directions on how to use the wiki as I find myself answering the same questions daily to new players as well as the tutorial system should be updated to include the current version of the game.

All in all I give it a 9.5/10.

No game is perfect, it still has minor flaws here and there but as someone who has watched this game progress the past 2 years I can proudly say I thoroughly love this game and hope to see it become even better than it already is.

I would recommend it to anyone who loves RPGs and doesn't think graphics are everything that makes a great game.

Early Access Review
Posted: 30 June
Having spent over 500 hours total at least on this game (I moved over to Steam not too long ago hence the low hour count on it as I played on the main website), I would count myself as experienced enough to write my opinion on this game.

The community is extremely helpful and any questions anybody may have will most certainly always be answered. Furthermore, there is a moderator or a staff member at most times of the day for any further enquiries. After many years the game is still being played by around 300 or so unique users a day so it is far from dead and is being updated monthly with big patches coming out every few months. The latest big patch was the archery and fletching addition and there was a smaller patch around a week or so ago. There are many things being currently worked on and upcoming additions into the game as well as the anniversary coming up on the 21st or so of July marking the 4th year of RPG MO if i am not mistaken. But anyways, the game is very active and not about to die as long as people keep playing which many will.

The game is NOT pay to win however, you can drop a little bit of cash in order to save the coin costs on certain items like the firelord set, orbs of luck and such. I definitely would recommend this game to anybody who loves spending hours on grinding in a social, friendly environment. I will not lie, it is very similar to RuneScape in many ways but has its own charm and is somewhat different from it. Those who love a grind fest will adore this game. I personally love grinding and spend a lot of time doing so. This game is definitely not for everybody but you can give it a try and may stay for the community if not for the grind itself. The game is not pay to win but pay to profit. You can become rich but you still need to grind to gain levels and use various equipment which has level requirements. You can be as rich as you want but you wont be able to use 99% of items.

Mechanics

The combat system itself is RNG with the factors of Strength, Accuracy and Defence being the main factors of the damage dealt, received and % chance of hitting. There are other mechanics such as Archery and Magic. Magic add magic damage to your next hit but costs money as you need to use spell scrolls to obtain spell charges in your magic pouch while archery will use arrows which you put into your quiver. The combat system itself is easy to understand and you gain XP according to the damage you deal to monsters which is where the grindfest comes in.

There is a "skilling and harvesting" part of the game which is my favourite mechanic in the game. You can mine, cut wood, farm and fish. These, for now, are the main harvesting skills. (Mining, Woodcutting, Farming and Fishing)

There are also "skilling" or "crafting" skills in RPG MO which are: Forging, Jewelry, Fletching, Alchemy, Breeding, Carpentry and cooking.

Overall

As I have said before, this game is not for everyone, it has its good qualities which is subjective but for me, the grind, community and chat are what keep me attached to the game.

However, the moderation or work that staff do in chat can be questionable and many quit because of it. If you can withstand the attitude of some moderators and contain yourself or use a different place to chat with your friends (AKA steam, skype, discord etc...), you will be fine.

Other than that, the game is updated often enough to have new content. It takes a long time to reach "end-game" and there is a LOT to discover along the way.

Most of the downsides are explained by Deets so I will not go there.

This is now the main MMO that I play. The grinding aspects have enticed me into it and the friends I have made on there keep me playing. It is special in its own way and I will hopefully be able to see it grow as well as see a growing community. I would rate it a 8/10 and definitely recommend you to try it and at least see if you like it. You can also play it on mobile or tablet in your spare time using the apps provided by the game.
