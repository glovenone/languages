About This Game

The creators of the beloved “Toy Story” films re-open the toy box and bring movie goers back to the delightful world of our favorite gang of toy characters in “Toy Story 3”. As Andy prepares to depart for college, Buzz, Woody and the rest of his loyal toys are troubled about their uncertain future. Directed by Lee Unkrich (co-director of “Toy Story 2” and “Finding Nemo”), “Toy Story 3” is a comical new adventure in Disney Digital 3D that lands the toys in a room full of untamed tots who can’t wait to get their sticky little fingers on these “new” toys. It’s pandemonium as they try to stay together, ensuring “no toy gets left behind.” Pixar veteran Darla K. Anderson ("Cars" and "Monsters, Inc.") produces, while Michael Arndt, Academy Award-winning screenwriter of "Little Miss Sunshine," brings his unique talents and comedic sensibilities. In the Toy Story 3: The Video Game help Buzz, Woody and the rest of the Toys ensure no toy gets left behind. Dive into all new heroic adventures in Story Mode or let your imagination run wild in the exiting new open world of Toy Box Mode! Come and play to infinity…and beyond!

READ THIS REVIEW BEFORE PURCHASING, PLEASE

I have purchased this game for the Nintendo Wii, the Xbox 360, and now the PC via Steam, and I feel confident in saying that the Xbox 360 version is hands-down the best version of the game, and that the version sold on Steam is hands-down the worst version for a number of reasons, which I will now give to you:

First of all, right after download, the game WON'T WORK FOR YOU. I cannot stress this emphatically enough: IT WILL NOT WORK. The game requires "Games For Windows Marketplace," a completely unrelated and obscure Microsoft download (not normally included in Windows) to be running, and then if the stars align the file MIGHT start up (after starting three times in a row for me, the fourth time it decided that it was done). This is pretty frustrating for what is essentially a kids' game.

IF (and that's a pretty big "if") you get the game running, the second major issue is controller support. The Steam description touts "Full controller support," but this is not entirely accurate or true. I am using a wireless Xbox 360 controller with a USB dongle, which works flawlessly on all of the dozens of games in my library which were released after 2008 -- but not this one. The game has trouble recognizing that a controller is present, and seems to forget that one is connected quite frequently. Again, when the stars align and you do have the game running and the controller working, it does finally begin to resemble the Xbox 360 game in terms of playability, even if the game is not coded to display the Xbox 360 buttons, instead opting to show generic controller buttons (for example, the yellow "Y" 360 button is mapped to a gray "1"). This in itself is not that big of a deal, but it isn't good for young children using the Microsoft product.

I honestly wish I could tell you more about the gameplay, but the chances are that you'll never see it which is a shame (although a little "floaty," the game is worth it for the "Woody's Round-up" segment alone, which rewards exploration and quests with a lot of genuinely fun unlockable content). This version is a complete waste of money and time. If you already own an Xbox 360 and are looking for something to play with a child, or just want a good, solid light-hearted game, buy the Xbox 360 version, which has great visuals and two-player splitscreen co-op in a world filled with quests, upgrades and exploration. If you have no friends and hate yourself, check out the Wii version, which omits 2-player and replaces good old-fashioned button presses with wildly unpredictable waggling motion controls. As for the Steam version...

This Steam version is only for three types of people: 1.) Software developers who enjoy figuring out why a program doesn't work and Rube-Goldberging a solution 2.) Conscientious software pirates who want to own a legitimate copy of the game they torrented before the government finds them 3.) Richard Pryor in the movie "Brewster's Millions" (I feel this last one is self-explanatory, but in case you haven't seen it, he has to spend one million dollars in a very limited time on in order to win a larger inheritance, the rub being at the ethat nd of the time alotted he has to have nothing to show for it, which descrbes this game perfectly).

Everyone else should just avoid it at all costs.

Toy Story 3: The Video Game is a platform game loosely based on the film Toy Story 3. Almost the entire Toy Story case from the films return in voice acting, apart from the two key characters, Buzz and Woody, for me, it is an absolute shame that they didn't get Tom Hanks and Tim Allen in...

Game play wise, Toy Story 3 is a platformer, where players control either Woody, Buzz or Jessie. Each of the characters have their own special move. There are two main modes in the game, Story and Toybox. The story mode has nine levels, this follows the events in the movie, as well as the opening sequence from Toy Story 2. Toybox mode, you can create and customize levels, fill it with inhabitants and missions. Completing various objectives and missions in this mode, you will earn money, which in turn can be used to purchase new objects, toys and to expand your city. Some of the characters are also from other Disney and Pixar franchises!

It almost looks as if you are watching the movie with the graphics on show here; it's a shame I couldn't play it in full 1080p, the resolution option just wasn't available. Also I was disappointed to see that it is capped at 30 FPS, so it would look even better if you could play it on a proper resolution/frame rate...

All of the levels are well presented, there are a copious amount of jumping puzzles, and BOOOOOOOOOOOY do I love jumping puzzles.....You will be playing in well known locations such as Andy's bedroom, Sunnyside Daycare and Junkyards. All of these look amazing and filled with lots of little nuts and crannies to explorer. My favorite level is the one based on the Buzz Lightyear Video game (the one Rex plays), it just resonates nostalgia. Everything is there from the laser eye, to the wall of spikes, to the secret passage on the left.

I have to admit, I did struggle at times with a number of the jumping puzzles, but then again, I struggle with them on any game, other than that, there aren't any real hard points, that should be expected from a game mainly based at the younger audience though.

Sadly the main issue I have with Toy Story 3 The Video game, is the length. I finished the story, all of the side missions and most of the collectibles in just under two hours. To me that is unacceptable for a game of this stature, I would of been satisfied if it was maybe five hours, but two hours is just not on. There is so much more which could of been included into the story mode to flesh it out.

With that being said, if you grew up with Toy Story, you will have so many memories and nostalgia towards the whole gang, and you will more than likely get a lot of enjoyment with this game if only for those reasons only. I would certainly not recommend anyone pick this up at full price. Sadly since it is a Disney title, it probably won't go on sale very often, so keep your eyes out.
