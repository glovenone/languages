About This Game

"Get Over here" puts you in the shoes of a Reality Show contestant!
This Reality Show is a bloody competition where you gotta pull your enemies using your hook Gun and finish them with your special move. If you're skillful enough to survive the tournament, beat all your enemies and dodge all the traps, you will be crowned the winner!

AIM, PULL and FINISH THEM!

Features:
Up to 4 players battle, Local and Online!
12 over the top, stylized characters!
7 different studios filled with dangerous Traps.
Spice things up with SPECIAL ITEMS that may make or break your strategy!
Stunning finishing moves!

Sheep!
( 2.4 hrs on record )
Posted: 16 April
Get Over Here is long on art assets but short on design. It looks good and sounds good, briefly, but it won't hold your attention long at all.

The first thing you'll probably notice is that the menu is poorly designed. It accepts mouse-clicks as input, but doesn't acknowledge the pointer, so you'll likely confuse yourself greatly until you realize that the menu must be navigated with keyboard commands. Don't take your hand off the mouse, though, because you'll need it in play. And try not to lose track of where the pointer is, which will be tricky, because the pointer is rather small and bland against a very vibrant and cartoony playfield.

You will have a choice from a rogues' gallery of a dozen goofy characters. It doesn't matter which one you pick, for gameplay purposes -- they perform identically. Assuming that you actually play more than once, you might want to find one whose attack phrase you find the least annoying.

Gameplay is very simple, and more strategic than it appears at first glance. Four characters with identical abilities run around in an arena full of hazards. You want to kill each other in a manner consistent with the match goal (most kills, highest kill streak, whatever) and have only one attack with which to do it. Your attack is a slow-firing "special attack" that kills everyone in a small radius around you, but is interruptable. You also have a hook-shot weapon, a la Scorpion from Mortal Kombat which targets with the mouse (that's why you're trying not to lose the pointer) and can be used to pull an enemy to you, or to pull yourself to an enemy. Either way, the hookshot stuns enemies, giving you an opportunity to get your attack off. The hook can also pull your enemies into hazards. You also have a dash move that's good for avoiding hooks -- CPU-controlled characters don't use it much.

Because your attack is so slow, strategic play tends to involve prowling around looking for opportunities. Running up to an enemy and attacking will probably result in a stunning hook to your face, breaking your attack and making you vulnerable to a counter-attack. Hooking an enemy while he's in the process of attacking someone else, however, will give you easy prey.

When you die, and you will die often, since this is a one-hit kill system, you respawn somewhere on the playfield. Find yourself quickly, because spawn protection is fleeting, and it's possible for someone to have killed you again before you even see where you are.

Powerups appear on the screen from time to time. Some change the game briefly but significantly, like the item that puts a temporary moratorium on hooks. A sword that fills your screen with text is a trap-item. Other powerups have an imperceptible effect. I collect them anyway, because grabbing a powerup is a long-conditioned reflex, but I still don't know what half of them do.

The simplicity of the gameplay suggests that Get Over Here is trying to be like Divekick, a game that is deliberately simple and short on tactical options, to invite the player to really master range and timing. Unfortunately, I can't see much reason to do so. NPC opponents are pushovers, and on the rare occasions I can manage to schedule friends to multiplay something, I'd prefer to spend the time something with more depth.

This is one of those no purpose/play only with friends kind of game where you have a set up to only play it for a while and not waste much of a time here. Although the game can last longer than you wish.

I explain. The game has a system where you'll play these top-down 2d "fighting" game where a single attack will automatically kill the enemy. It is like one of those minigames from the M***o Party series, where you need to achieve the most amount of points, or kill your enemies the most, until the time ends or the adversary lives end. But, sometimes, you'll play the same stage for a full 5 minutes, which is a lot of time and it shouldn't be like this. It can become tedious after a while.

Well, the game is actually funny in some aspects, the characters are pretty good and memorable, and each of them is pretty unique in its attack and presentation (like their lines and movement). But, still, It feels like it is missing something, it is not really that fun to play.

This one is pretty trick, as if I should recommend it or not. I will say yes, but only if you take it for a pretty cheap price. And here I'm hoping the developers will actually improve the game over time. If this game maintains as it is, as of this date, I can't say it will be really successful. But that is something that my review is not responsible for.

spoderman
( 6.0 hrs on record )
Posted: 2 December, 2015
NOTE: The online multiplayer in this game is 100% broken, and the online portion is the reason I intended to play this game. The lag I experienced was so bad that you can't even move around without a huge delay. There is a warning when you access the online mode telling you that it's experimental, but you'll only realize this after playing the game. They don't mention it on the store page or anywhere else as far as I know.

AVOID the game at all costs if online MP is all you're looking for. Now, onto reviewing the other parts of the game.

Pros:

- Cool original character designs, nice animations and sprites. Good overall graphics.
- Some of the levels were more challenging than I was expecting and work well with the scorpion "get over here" move.
- The AI for the bots is pretty good if you plan on playing the game alone, and in certain game modes provide a good challenge.

Cons:

- Scorpion gimmick gets old fast, providing maybe an hour or so of gameplay before the fun runs out.
- Bots sometimes don't attack you after they've stunned you, which makes overcoming them a little too easy occasionally.
- No real incentive to keep you playing, there's no campaign or rewards for winning. Would be nice if they added unlockable characters, costumes, or literally any kind of reward for progressing. It doesn't even have achievements.
- As stated before, the online multiplayer is completely broken. I'm willing to try almost any game, no matter how bad it is in online MP so the lack of it is very disappointing.

For a local multiplayer game, it's probably not too bad but there are much better multiplayer arena games you can play and the scorpion gimmick isn't really adding anything you can't live without. Not worth the $9.99. If you're intent on buying it, only get this if you can get it for $0.50 or in a bundle.
