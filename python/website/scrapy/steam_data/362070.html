About This Game

Metal Reaper Online is based on gun shooting battle's MMORPG.


With post apocalyptic background, merging military shooting contents and MMORPG to give various contents and better accessibility.
Game material and control are very similar with FPS, and serve comfortable contents.

[ Features ]

    • 4 different playable military branches

      - Former RPG's job. Each branch has it's own unique skill and weapon to use

    • Use gun to hunt down various type of enemies

      - 8 kind of weapon types to defeat enemies!

    • Character appearance will changes belongs to your character equipment

      - Equip 120+ kind of armor to upgrade yourself

    • Lots of map and quests

      - 85+ maps to explore and 1300+ quests to do

    • PvP and PvE Contents

      - Currently 3 kind of PvP mode is available
      - 20+ Missions(former dungeon) and Zombie Annihilation contents

    • Immersive in-game storyline

Metal Reaper Online is a MMORPG that aims at being simple and easy to play. It's not the first game in its genre. Its kind of gameplay is somewhat really close to an old ARPG (from 2005) named "Restricted Area", but with features that come closer to Diablo 2 when it comes to skills and inventory management.

The graphics and sounds are simple, so the game can easily play on a low-end rig, but it's also relatively good looking even in its simplicity.

The controls is a merge of mouse's point&click with the ability to move (a lot more precisely) with WASD. There's quite a couple of keys you might never uses unless you want its perks like the CTRL key to crouch which raise you accuracy but make you stay put... while the game doesn't really put any kind of challenge that would requires you to actually use it. (Only one class actually benefit from this action through its passive skills.)

The quests are quite generic : Kill X enemies, find X items from enemies, Search something at a specific point on map, kill everything in the area, etc. There's not much of a puzzle or a "harder" challenge. Some special event does happen, but they are short and somewhat lacking.

The Cash shop is quite straight forward and 90% of the content can be unlocked with a relatively high amount of in-game money. For example, the inventory locked rows can be unlocked with cash-shop currency or with an amount of in-game money highly raise at every row bought with such money. (1-3 rows are free. Row 4 cost 10K, Row 5 cost 100K, etc.)

If I had to put the worse part of the game, that would be its english that happens to contains a bunch of typos mistakes as the game is originally from Korea. I stopped counting them when I reached 30 errors such as typos. (Even in the character's screen!) The game clearly wasn't double-checked when it comes to its english text. I wouldn't doupt that the devs company hired an external company to translate the text, the typed the english text in and never double-checked that everything was alright... or even worse... the external translating company can't type anything without making a mistake at every 30 words.

Otherwise, the game is enjoyable, while neither ground breaking nor perfect. It's a good entertainment.

A fun, but slightly glitchy/laggy shoot&loot. Fair, easy to get into, not badly pay to win, and easy to play with friends. Progression is gradual and never leaves you starved for things to do, and the class system is fun and easy to grasp. Imagine Runescape with guns. The translations are pretty bad, but the game is legible and the quests are self-explainatory enough that it doesn't take away from anything. Lots of variety and weapons, fun customization options. The quests do start to feel a lot of the same after a while, but the gameplay is fluid enough that it'll keep your attention longer than a lot of other free to plays.

Pros
+Easy to understand, easy to grasp, easy to follow
+Dungeons, missions and lengthy story
+Fun to explore, maps are varied
+Not pay to win
+Small download, easy to run on most computers
+Looting is fun and rewarding, you never feel like you can't get your hands on something you want
+Good customization and weapons list.

Cons
-Bad translation, feels like the dev threw it in google translate and called it done.
-Quests can get repititive.
-Sometimes glitchy, many enemies on screen at once generates copious lag in some places.

What makes the game kinda hard/boring/ maybe bad?

YET ANOTHER DECENT GAME GETTING BAD REVIEWS BY PEOPLE WHO DO NOT PUT ENOUGH TIME INTO SAID GAME TO WRITE A FACTUAL REVIEW. As short as this game currently is, at least a few hours would show some credibility.

DO NOT LET MY STEAM TIME FOOL YOU. Steam will never reflect my true time in this game, I would much rather continue using the standalone client.

Review Update 10-17-15
As my opinions of the game itself have not changed, I do have some gripes with the current DLC. In short, I do not consider cash shop content to be DLC, & I find it silly to be sold as DLC.

I have been playing this game long before they decided to put it on steam. When the starting point was inside of a tent rather than a medium action heli strike. And currently I hold 3 characters 2 which have already reached current endgame status. <Correction it has been a while since I have played the game and did not realize they raised the cap again. So I have 2 characters sitting at 50. Current end game status is now at 60.

As many have said this game follows in the veins of Diablo (if Diablo had guns). But also I would like to include Shadowgrounds, Alien Breed, Space Siege, Torchlight...I think you are getting the idea...

One of, in the small handful of, true MMOARPG games out. If you have played Dekaron, Dragon's Nest or Defiance, then you are already familiar with how a true MMOARPG should feel. Fast fluid action & reaction, giving you the feeling you are almost chaining skills & attacks. Much like a platformer fighting game it makes you feel you should be watching for a combo meter when attacking.

Some misconceptions already out there.

1. Cash shop items. Only thing that may be desirable...costumes or a vehicle. Honestly neither are desirable. It's a gun game...you will get shot riding on your vehicle anyway as it is not fast enough to outrun bullets and you cannot shoot from your vehicle. Other vehicles are already available in game through a vendor & better PERMANENT costumes can be unlocked with "miles" just for playing the game. Even exp bonus/premium status is abolutely unnecessary even for those of us who work that cannot play everyday.

2. No actual gear can be bought in the cash shop. No crafting boost items can be found in the shop.

4. If you run out of ammo...then you really did not look at the game. Or you are a horrible shot. Every character can hold 3 (+1 Single use/mission specific) weapon slots max from the very moment it was created, even in the lacking intro tutorial you have this ability. Each weapon type has it's own ammo pool. Shotguns only use shotgun ammo, rifles only use rifle ammo, etc...etc...starting pools are: Single Use Weapon 192 Max/ Pistols 772/ Rifles 970/ Heavy Machine Gun 100/ Shotgun 20/ Sniper 10/ Flamethrower Fuel 100/ Rocket Launcher 8. This is before bonuses on equipment and class specialization.

3. Rocket launchers are not supposed to be accurate like guns, they are splash damage weapons.

4. Again best things in life, or in this case, game...are free. And that is how they have built this game. The best weapons, armor, & recipes only drop. You will not find any of these in the cash shop. And this is a loot friendly game. Enemies are walking piÃ±atas and drop loot like candy.


Now for some of the good:

Compared to many other MMO's the interface is ridiculously simple.

Quests will carry you all the way out to current endgame status.

Loot, loot, loot...

All gear uses an RNG method for assigning bonuses to the gear when dropped, much like Borderlands & Defiance. So it gives you true tailorization of your character to fit your playing style.

If you want to play with a controller, well the option is not there unless you have programming software...but if you do you can play this game like a twin stick shooter.

Very laid back, sit in your chair and enjoy killing stuff type of game.

Lower tier equipment can be just as good and in some cases better than top tier equipment. This way you do not have to spend a good chunk of your life grinding for top tier items just to keep up with other players.

Account wide storage. There are 2 storage types, personal & account. Both storage types can be decently upgraded with game earned currency. Much like any other F2P game that uses storage to increase it massively will require real cash but, with the RNG bonus stat system and drop rates I doubt you will be storing much. I feel the account wide storage would see more use than personal. But that is just a personal opinion. More so, it's something you would have to see and experience yourself.

True character buidling, each class specialization can be built in different ways to fit how you play vs the old "one build rules all" most games with classes and character building accidentally fall into.

Now for some of the bad:

This is definitely not a "dedicate some time to" type of game. The grinding in quests can get repetitive. It's more heavily gun based than balanced between guns & skills. So if you are good with games of this type you can easily solo areas that were meant to be partied in. This is more of "have fun demolishing enemies with firepower when you want to with friends" type of game.

Yes, control can feel slightly sluiggish on any PC.

The game, though playable, has not even reached a 1.0 status yet, so the graphics engine could use some major tweaking. In short, I have seen full 3D games with much more immersive graphics run much faster than this more simpled 2.5d title, which is pretty sad.

There is my personal concern that, one particular class build is semi-godly overpowered to the point that it makes the strongest enemies look like paper, regardless of weapon level. Fortunately as long as I have been playing this game I have not seen anyone use this build so that information I will keep that to myself(No, I have no affiliation with this game, it was just the first character I built that I unwittingly found this unbalance on). The game is easy enough for anyone to play with just guns alone, so do not ask about it. I will put you on permanent ignore.

Currently, very short game for an MMO, I think I reached endgame on 2 characters in a week. But that week was also spent dedicated to this game. And it's why I do not suggest dedicating daily time to it. It is too quick to reach the current endgame status. But please bear in mind this game has not been out very long, even prior to it's steam release.

Classes vs Backgrounds - At the beginning you will see each character background has a bonus & once you reach the level to choose a specialization, it will not matter. If you are good at these types of games all the classes will feel the same unfortunately. Since I had mentioned earlier about being more gun based than skill + gun balanced, you will most likely forget you have skills and just gun everything down.

Overall as short as this game is, I wholeheartedly recommend it as a time filler. But I cannot yet recommend it as a must try.


Forgot to add, if you guys like this game there is another game like this that did not have a grand/well known release, not on steam yet either, called Bounty Hounds Online hosted by Subagames. It's another fairly new game. But has the tiny population problem this one also suffered from before steam. Because of not enough advertisement. Similiar to this, but with more of a classic MMORPG feel & full 3D camera. I'm hoping that game will also make it to steam.
