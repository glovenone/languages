About This Game

Key Features
  • Gamindustri in 1080! Experience all the humor, turn-based battle, and crazy HDD transformations of Re;Birth2 with the sheen of 1080p excellence!
  • Four or More for War! Have up to 4 characters on the battlefield, and 4 more on the sidelines, ready to jump in and give their support!
  • Stella goes Rogue! Send Stella out to brave Gamindustri’s dungeons in this realtime mini-game and she’ll collect all kinds of treasure for you – you just sit back and eat pudding! Be careful though, because if she dies in the dungeon, she’ll lose all her special gear!
  • The World’s your Oyster! Customize the Gamindustri experience with Plans, which allow you to do things like increase/decrease enemy difficulty, change the items you’ll receive from a dungeon, and more!
  • Build-a-Sword! Use Idea Chips and Blank Discs to create custom equipment that can open up new worlds of skills for you!

My continued love of all things Nep continues, as I have completed Re;Birth2, having recieved the normal ending.

There were a couple of strange technical issues. Screen tearing was pretty awful, and nothing I did could fix it. My old system, (GTX 650ti, AMD FX 6300) crashed on the very first battle, but since my upgrade earlier this year (AMD R9-390, i5 6600k) I experienced no such crashes. In the end I simply learned to live with the screen tearing. Oh yeah, and every time I would start up the game, the controller wouldn't work until I went into the menu and switched the control scheme over to mouse for some reason. More of an annoyance than a game breaker though.

Re:Birth 2 was a lot like Re;Birth 1. Most assets are recycled, but I expected this, so I was okay with it. This time around the game focused on the CPU candidates; Nepgear, Uni, Ram, and Rom. The writing was still top notch, but I think this was a weaker game having earlier completed Re;Birth1. It was a bit shorter too, and I got to the end after about 20ish hours.

Stella's Dungeon was an unexpected and neat feature. Basically you send Stella and her cat off into dungeons after picking her equipment, and she does so in real time. You could be in the middle of a scene or fight, and you'll get a notification that Stella's exploration was successful, or if she met her untimely demise. Success means earning unique items and plans.

Overall, I would recommend this game, especially for fans of the series, but I won't fault you if you opt to skip it. Personally, I'll be moving straight away to Re;Birth3, which I've heard is the best of the three, so I'm pretty excited about it. Still, I enjoyed my time with Re;Birth 2, and I'm glad I played it.

I don't know if this qualifies as a review, but I played my fair share of Nep and I feel like telling you what I loved and hated about RE;Birth2. I'll be comparing it to the first one, for the most part.

Loved:

- You have 4 characters instead of 3, and that's great.
- There's NepNep.
- Voice actors were the same.
- The game looks way more forgiving regarding when you can talk to someone for plan unlocking. Most of the time, you've got the whole chapter available to collect stuff.
- Harvest Shift is useful.
- Stella's Dungeon is frustrating and a time waster, but it's there and it's pretty nice. I mean, why not.
- NepNep
- You don't have to waste 4 months of your life raising lily ranks: they go up quite nicely.

Disliked:

- Nepgear theme during EXE Drives doesn't start at the same time as RE;Birth1. Way less exciting.
- DLCs break the game. Yes you can always NOT use those items you paid for, but it's still annoying.
- The plot.


There will be spoilers.
There will be spoilers.
There will be spoilers.

I know they were going for a more serious and well written plot, and that's good since in the first game you had a quite lazy amnesia plot. But in RE;Birth2 the general setting is way darker, and I just didn't like it very much.
It gets better when you get Neptune back, unless you're on the Conquest Ending.
Or the Slaughter Bloodfest Ending.

That was INFURIATING. Basically you ♥♥♥♥IN MURDER your best friend, one CPU in front of her little sisters, then you swiftly proceed to murder them as well while they're in tears and sobbing, and then you murder your own sister. You know, just for good measure. And then... AND THEN! You can visit the oracles after the deed, with no purpose other than watch them hate you and desperately cry.
Does this sound like a Neptune game to you? Because it doesn't to me.
And I was not infuriated because it was intense. Yes, it was intense, and there was a reason behind all of that. I was infuriated because I had to sit through that and I really didn't want to.
I bought the Neptune games because they're supposed to be funny, lighthearted and grindy. And this was very, very disappointing. I appreciate the fact that they tried to make their plot more interesting, but then again, I'm not interested at all in that kind of Shakesperian tragedy.

Even so, it is a game I would recommend. Just pretend that ending is not there.

TL;DR: The game is worth buying but tends to freeze after 4+ hours. If you don't mind go for it.

I like this game.
I enjoy the turn-based combat, I enjoy the dialogues (not as much as rebirth1 but eh), and there isn't really any grinding needed (by the time I'm satisfied I've cleared the dungeons, I tend to be overlevelled for the story bosses).
An additional party member gives a lot of flexibility.
The Lily rank requirements based on one character for the endings are kind of annoying but they fit into the story so whatever.

However I can't recommend this game unless you tend to have high patience for bugs and can remember to save at every single opportunity.

-Noticeable delays every time you break guard on an enemy (Not such a big deal, and you can repack rebirth1's assets into this game's assets to fix it)
-Crashes when loading into dungeon saves (Fixed by running in winXP compatability mode)
-Rarely there are missing textures on models and the dungeons (Aside from looking really spooky, Falcom didn't seem affected by the black abyss on her face)
-Chance to eternally hang when loading assets. This is the big one. The game has the chance to freeze whenever: You enter a dungeon, you exit a dungeon, you use an EX mode attack, you use HDD mode, you enter a event scene, you exit an event scene, you load different models while changing equipment, etc. Granted this tends to only happen after multiple hours (4+) of gameplay so you can progress just fine as long as you save after every major action. Perhaps it's because I only have 6GB ram, I don't know.

I wish I could choose to give a neutral recommendation instead of a yes or no.

Just like the game preceding it Hyperdimension Neptunia Re;Birth 2 is a silly JRPG that really doesn't take itself, or the Game Industry, very seriously. Expect 4th Wall Breaks and LOTS of references to popular Anime, Video Games and Popular Culture.

The main character in this game is different from the first however, this time around we have Neptune's little sister Nepgear as our Main Playable Character. Luckily our dear Neptune does reappear about half way through the game so don't worry.

As for playtime I beat this one in 24 hours...not all at once mind you but still...it's a lot shorter than Re;Birth1 but I feel that's because there's less grinding in this game.

As usual the DLC add Over-Powered equipment and characters, they're not required but they do make the game significantly easier, allowing you to deal disgusting amounts of damage to your enemies if your into that sorta thing.

The only thing I would warn you about is that this game tends to Crash often so do a lot, and I mean a lot, of SAVING!
