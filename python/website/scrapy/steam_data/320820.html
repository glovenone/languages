About This Game



Experience the mysterious, timeless world of Toren, the first adventure game from Brazilian indie developer Swordtales. You are Moonchild, destined to climb the tower (known as Toren) on a hauntingly solitary journey to find your purpose. You must solve puzzles and face monsters as you struggle to climb to the top of this beautiful, yet treacherous environment, driven by the will to find your freedom.

A Journey of Learning & Fulfilment - As the story progresses your character will age from a mere infant to grown woman creating new mechanics and challenges for each learning experience and phase of her life. In addition, the Tree of Life within the tower grows as she does, providing access to new areas to explore and new items to find.

Poetry brought to Life â Toren features dark fantasy storytelling in a universe filled with symbolism. The mysterious, fantastical atmosphere is conveyed through an award-winning art design and original score. As your journey unfolds you will discover scrolls of poetry, which reveal the story and true origins behind Toren and aids the Moonchild in discovering her purpose.

Rich Puzzle Solving - Every stage of the game features new and progressively more deadly challenges for the player: solving environmental puzzles, how avoid and survive attacks from dangerous creatures, and eventually defeating the end boss.

Let me be blunt; Toren is worth playing, but only for a certain type of gamer.

If you dislike short games, you'd best keep walking. Toren will take you around two hours to complete, perhaps a little more if you really like to soak up the scenery.

Lofty expectations

Toren, a game from Brazilian indie outfit Swordtales, is at its heart a coming of age tale. Much like ICO and Brothers: A Tale of Two Sons before it, you take on the role of a young girl simply known as the moonchild whose fate it appears is to ascend a daunting tower and slay a menacing dragon and in the process learn that the ways of the world are often cruel and spiteful as you depart childhood for a grey and morally ambiguous adult world. You initially start the game as a young toddler who has to learn to crawl before she can learn to walk and over the course of the time it takes to clear the game you will get to watch her grow into a young woman. Much like the beloved ICO before it you will need to guide your young protagonist around a large and looming tower littered with an assortment of puzzles. Having said that, that is where all similarities with ICO should end. While ICO was an utter joy to play that left deep and lasting impressions, Toren is muddle of ideas (some great, some not) that seem to have been sloppily sewn together.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=533023553
The biggest problem with Toren is that in the short time it takes to clear the game (2-3 hrs depending on your playstyle) the central mystery around why this girl finds herself born to such a fate is never really clearly explained. Pulling in mysticism and philosophy from various religions and told through simple yet cryptic poetry, Toren ends up being its own Tower of Babel leaving audiences more confused then when they started. These pieces of prose are often so cryptic and vague they end up not really telling you much at all which leaves Toren in a bit of an unfortunate predicament. There is very little to piece together and what pieces there are don't really inform the player much about this mysterious world. If you are the kind of gamer that likes their stories to be direct, clear and precise and not beat about the bush then approach Toren with caution because it could end up frustrating you no end. While I don't particularly mind the fashion the story is presented in, and while I don't necessarily need everything spelt out for me, I would have liked a bit more to cash in on because it would have only increased my vested interest rather than simply going through the motions to reach an end.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=533220404
One can't fault Swordtales when it comes to artistic merit. Toren can sometimes be downright beautiful to look at even when character and environment models appear blocky and washed out. Toren in many ways resembles a painting that appears to have taken on a life of its own and climbing the tower can be a visual delight as you watch your young charge grow from a baby into a fine young woman all the while navigating some incredibly creative scenery. During your ascend to the top of the tower seasons will pass in and out which manages to frame the game beautifully giving it the sense of passing time. However, as creative as the visuals can get sometimes, the games puzzle elements and controls leave a lot to be desired. It almost feels like they were an afterthought where at some point the developers seem to have realized that this fairy tale needed something to make it represent a game so they ran roughshod through the genre tropes that barely function and only just. Nothing in Toren will tax your brain or have you scratching your head in perplextion as you try to figure out what to do next. The game mechanics seem to come off even worse with jumping and climbing being an incredibly clunky and downright shoddy affair. Combat, yes there is a wee bit to be found - remember you are here to slay a dragon - shows no improvement either. While they function just don't come into this world expecting precision and smoothness.

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=528536577
My biggest gripe however with the game comes from the games camera that will insist on always rotating to get the most cinematic view. While it certainly does help get some glorious and grand screenshots it actually ends up handicapping the player more than anything else. It's a static camera so you have no control over how it is going to present the next frame and it often leaves the player at a slight disadvantage because of what they can no longer see in front of them.

At the end of the day I left Toren's world feeling ambivalent. While I certainly didn't hate my short time spent playing the game, neither did it delight and charm me in the ways ICO and Brother: A Tale of Two Sons did. While I certainly don't not recommend you dive in and give it a try - because as they say one man's trash is another mans gold - Toren took key concepts that were unique and creative and that were the reason my interest was initially piqued and then spun them off into the deep end of the abyss of mediocre. One for a sale most definitely!

Toren is an adventure game where an interesting story and concept are sadly marred by the actual gameplay.

PROS:

-interesting,albeit cryptic,story

-very nice art style

-great soundtrack

CONS:

-menus are clunky,no quick "back" button and it's somewhat difficult to understand what's highlighted

-clunky controls where turning, jumping and attacking often feel awkward and imprecise

-dream sequences remember what you've done game-wise but have no mid-checkpoints so if you fall you have to start from the beginning

-some annoying platforming sections, made worse by the aforementioned controls

-weak graphics overall, while the levels themselves are decent the char models and some effects are less impressive

-some noticeable screen tearing even with vsync on

-bland puzzles

-underwhelming and repetitive last boss fight

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=453205768

It's with a sad heart that I give Toren a thumbs down.
The story was interesting and the game concept is indeed nice, but I often felt that the actual gameplay was not only not adding anything to the game value, it was actually detracting from it and getting in the way of my fun...bland puzzles, slippery platforms paired with awkward jumping, boring combat all take their toll over the (short) game length.
Now,the game itself isn't a disaster or plain horrible and the dev team did manage to get some things right so I hope they can try their luck again with an higher budget and perhaps more time to polish their game.
I got it for 6$ on release day,for that price and despite my negative review I do not feel like I've completely wasted my money but I'd advise those wanting to experience the game story to wait for a sale.
