About This Game

Test Drive Unlimited 2 transforms the driving genre, adding the persistency, progression and customization of the latest multiplayer games to the auto racing experience.
Test Drive Unlimited 2 blends single player and multiplayer experiences seamlessly, creating a dynamic world of evolving content and challenges. Players define their online persona through unparalleled customization of their avatars and vehicles, and the acquisition of property, clothing and other accoutrements of a luxurious international lifestyle. All-new race types, vehicle classes, environmental challenges, locations and much more push this epic franchise to new levels of performance.
Chase glory from behind the wheel of the most exclusive performance cars and take the race online to challenge rivals around the world. Customize your life as you tear through the streets and kick up dust off - road on the sun - kissed islands of Ibiza and Hawaii.

Lets jump straight into the pros and cons.

Pros:

1: Extensive and engaging storyline.
2: Very good soical racer in its time.
3: Lots of things to do after you have completed the storyline, with friends or without friends.
4: An insane amount of cars.
5: An installable "Unoffical Patch" that grants you even more cars.
6: MASSIVE open world all there for the taking
7: In its time it had a great community.

Cons:

1: Impossible for new players, as registering an account is very difficult, and if you are on windows 10, theres a 50/50 chance you will or will not be able to play.
2: Atari and Eden games were dead many years ago, so dont expect any new updates or customer support.
3: No email verification, so if you forget your password, your save data and your entire account are done for.
4: Some glitches and bugs that can be frustrating, but do not happen very often.
5: Pretty hefty price tag for a game that is this old and may or may not work.
6: Graphics are very outdated, but that is to be expected from a early 2011 game.
7: Servers that are practically dead, in my past few days of playing i have not found one online player, which is very frustrating in my opinion.

Sugguestions to beat the cons:

1: DO NOT BUY IF YOU ARE ON WINDOWS 10, and research on a way to make an account, im sure there is a way.
2: Install the unoffical patch, as this grants many new cars to play around with.
3: Write your password down.
4: If these glitches do happen, for example hitting a stop sign in some areas will flip your car, do not get frustrated! You can always dodge or overcome these glitches.
5: Buy this game when it is on sale. It goes down to around 4.99 USD occaisonally.
6: Not really a fix for this one, but maybe there is some kind of mod out there that can give you a better graphics experience.
7: Find some friends that are playing! Post in the community discussions and look for people that are already playing the game.

Overall, this is my favourite game ever made. If one day i become a billionare, i am going to buy out atari and eden games and make a tdu3. Thanks for reading and i hope this helped you make your decision on whether to buy the game or not.

TLDR; Multiplayer DOES still function (for me anyway), Meh car handling, decent variety of cars, nice visuals, awful story & voice acting and 2 very large maps (takes about an hour to drive around them). 7 out of 10,
ONLY BUY WHEN ON SALE FOR 75% OFF

Test Drive Unlimited 2 is an open world driving game with a heavy emphasis on the "super car" lifestyle; collecting cars, houses and clothes and various other material possesions. There is a story but its so badly written and voice acted, you wont miss anything interesting if you choose to skip the cutscenes, it's just a weak excuse to have races across Ibiza and Hawaii. The actual racing itself is fairly arcadey, cars drift easily and generally don't have much feeling of weight but some simulation-ness is still present, of you mash the accelerator while drifting and you have the driving aids turned off you'll just spin out. The AI is fairly dumb in the early game, but it can get vaugely difficult when you're getting into the mid to late game. I can't speak about the multiplayer gameplay, but let it be known that IT DOES STILL WORK, just not that well and the multiplayer is on a ticking time bomb of whenever Atari decides to shut down support.

Overall this game is worth a try if it's on sale and you like cars and you if can bully some friends to get it as well, you can get a fair bit of play time out of it.

The rumors of TDU2's demise are greatly exaggerated.

I played this game extensively on Xbox 360 and migrated over to PC when my old 360 called it quits. I was wary about buying TDU2 because of all the reviews stating that the servers are down, that you can't log into the game, et cetera. Those are all false. I've been playing this online with real people for several days now. The servers aren't dead and they're not ghost towns, there are many people roaming Ibiza and Hawaii and most are willing to race or exchange messages. The game does require you to create an account in order to play online, but that is easily accomplished by visiting the TDU2 website and signing up there. Installation went well, and the initial start up and every start up since has not posed any problems for me on Windows 8.1.

TDU2 is an older racing game, but is still a lot of fun, especially if you enjoy collecting cars and free roaming two huge islands. I would happily pay $19.99 for this game right now on PC, and I definitely recommend it at the sale price of $4.99.
