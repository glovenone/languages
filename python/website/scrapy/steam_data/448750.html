About This Game

Infinity Saga's story revolves around Tetra, a teenage boy enrolled in college as an art major, who becomes smitten by teenage girl named Autumn through a social networking site. One day Tetra gets invited to Autumn's home for movie night, as well as to meet her cats. After running out of snacks, and unable to find Autumn's favorite chips, things go from bad to worse when the electricity goes out, and leaves Tetra and Autumn vulnerable to the evil lurking outside their door.

Zombies!
Yes Zombies, and a host of other horrors await our heroes in the game Infinity Saga. (As well as new friends!)

Will our heroes be able to survive the onslaught of level grinding forced upon them? (Will you?)
Will our heroes be able to formulate strategies and plans to dispatch hard to take down foes? (Not on your first go)
Will Tetra get lucky and get a smooch? (Maybe more?)
Will Autumn be able to save her favorite chips from the clutches of evil? (They are pretty damn tasty!)

Only you can decide! But beware what lurks around the corner and save often.


Game Features:
ORIGINAL ART!
-Pixelated Cat Petting!
-Casual Play (Save anywhere and continue where you left off!)
-Side Quests
-Arcane Magic System
-Skill System

Posted: 12 April
I've not beaten the game entirely, but from what I've done so far, I'm somewhat to a half point/near the end I think. Anyways, so far I've enjoyed the game very much. I love the humor it has and the artwork for the characters is awesome, as well as the backstory to the game. As for the fighting style of gameplay, it's your pretty standard classic rpg style and it's pretty relaxing aside from running into monsters very frequently, but it's relatively easy to just escape a fight and keep on exploring. Very rarely have I been ambushed or couldn't escape a battle with too serious of injuries.

Grinding in the game so far is pretty easy, but the boss fights can get rather challenging. If anything, I would've liked to have the enemies have health bars so I could see whether or not they're close to death and so I could better determine which enemies to strike at first, but that's just a minor suggestion.

The sound effects can get pretty loud if you're a head/earphone user like I am. There were a few times I did have to remove my earphones due to it being too loud, but you can easily tune the volume up or down with your own computer's volume if you must. I would suggest a volume control system in the game, but it's really not necessary as there isn't really anything too important that you have to really listen in to unless you're the type that wants to mute the music and listen to the sound effects, or vice versa. There is both, really cool sound effects and awesome choices of music though.

Overall, I am enjoying the game and I'd definitely recommend it to others to try out at the very least. My only advice would be to pick your fights carefully and never be afraid to use the "Run-away" option so you could keep on exploring and survive.

So for starter’s I have been playing RPG game since I was little, I’m not little no more but I still play them just as much if not more so.

Game process:

I was one of the game tester's and I found no major issues in the game, it “never crashed on me not even once” nor to anyone else that was testing (I know someone that was having a hard time with it on his computer back before it ran smooth and yet still it had never crashed on him). For other’s it had only slowed down on them.

There have been quite a few changes since the testing and all are good, with that being said this was all done back in testing phase so nothing was perfect but still there was no major issues.
So I as a tester had no problems with any of it.

Sure I found some glitches when I went through it but that’s what testing is all about. And “all” games are going to have some glitches; “Bethesda” need I say more and they don’t fix them…

Even now I’m going through the game again and testing things out; while talking with the creator about them and fixing them.

And if any issues do arise they are getting fixed very quickly from the creator.

Gameplay:

As other’s have already mentioned, this is your “Classical RPG”.

It can be a little hardcore at times (ever heard of Ghosts 'n Goblins); but that depends on how much a veteran you are to RPG games. I as a tester had no problems with any of its gameplay.

The art is very well done it’s all original from the creator. I have watched him personally myself creating the art.

The story line is good; for some it may not be great but it’s not bad in any shape or form.

There is plenty of humor in it for kicks and giggles (which most should get).

It’s not a long game but it’s also not short, so it should appeal to everyone.

This game was made by RPG Maker which most should already know of and personally I love RPG Maker it really brings back the childhood in all that we loved in RPG games…..

Sure you got repetitive, monster’s showing up all the time, hardcore gameplay, and not knowing what to do next………This is nothing new to the old world of “Classical RPG”.

All in all this game is well worth $2.99.
