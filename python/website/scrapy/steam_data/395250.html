About This Game

To the Fat Mobile, let's go !!
You are the crime fighter for the city of Shadowlawn. Just when you're complaining about how dull things have been, a supervillain named Toxicman is created from a botched act of corporate sabotage. It is up to you, then, to tear yourself away from food long enough to save the city from certain destruction...

There is fun, laughs and even some heroic moments to be had in The Adventures of Fatman: Toxic Revenge! Now available at budget price for Steam collectors, the first commercial AGS game returns to your screens. This special edition includes extra easter eggs & an improved ending. Additional special features, trading cards & soundtrack are also included!

Yesterday's technology, today !!
+ Comedy adventure game suitable for all (some mild comic violence)
+ Parody point & click with a retro kick
+ Full English voice acting
+ Interesting locations to visit & fun characters to meet
+ Hand-drawn art & animations, original soundtrack
+ Designer commentary in-game
+ Easy mode (normal, hints & retries) or hard mode (no saves, no hints + sudden death)

A story about a fat dude that tries to be Batman (and somewhat succeeds).

There are tons of tropes in this game, both well and terribly done, but overall I liked it. The villian, the sidekick, the SCUMM inventory system, the art direction, classic Sierra death scenes, some easter eggs, etc. The voice overs are good, but they suffer from poor production values - tons of ambient noise + distorted audio that could be prevented with a simple EQ pass and compression.

Most of the game and its puzzles are well thought, except for some contraventions here and there, which I don't know if were made to mock the player or it's just bad design - i.e. when you need to change suits, it gets annoying super super fast. Showing only 4 items in the inventory system is too constraining, should have been expanded to at least 6.
Uses a old version of the AGS engine that isn't really working for 2016, Fullscreen doesn't work in Windows 10, and has no filters. Regardless, the game is playable. Also, MIDI music.

But if you want to enjoy a light hearted story (and a 'classic' AGS-made game), give it a try, it's cool.

Posted: 2 October, 2015
Disclaimer: This review was written using a press key supplied for free by the developer.

Fatman is, unsurprisingly, a fat, lazy and dense slob who decided to dress up and become a superhero to fight crime: when a mutated toxic zombie tries to infect the whole city of Shadowlawn he'll be the only hero trying to stop it before it's too late, since Superman seems otherwise preoccupied.
The game originally came out in 2003 as an independent project and is now out on Steam with voiceovers, some easter egg and a slightly changed ending: while suffering from some awkward and difficult puzzles The Adventures of Fatman manages to stay fun due to its story and humour.

PROS:

-the retro soundtrack is truly great

-fully voice acted, said voice acting is generally decent

-Fatman is a pretty silly character and he really works as a parody

-the Fatcomputer is also pretty funny and its voice kinda sounds like Stephen Hawking, it made me chuckle several times

-nice amount of humour can be found in dialogues, item descriptions and even whenever you try to interact with objects you're not supposed to

-while simple the story is enjoyable due to the succession of funny, and at times surreal, scenes you're presented with

NEUTRAL:

-the game age and low budget do show in its graphics, gameplay (difficult puzzles, pixel hunting) and an action menu (walk to, look at, talk to etc...) that anyone familiar with adventure games from the early 90s will remember right away: this will be a nice trip down memory lane for some but could turn off gamers who only played newer entries in the genre

CONS:

-certain screens look a bit too bare and lack details, some objects are also used several times in different scenes (e.g. trash, red shoes, bottles) thus leading to some repetition

-some dialogues are of lower quality due to a less effective voice actor or a somewhat noisy recording

-some puzzles can go from hard to truly frustrating, sometimes there's a few too many logical leaps involved in what you're supposed to do, you'll face some pixel hunts to get all the needed items and there'll be times where you'll be have to interact several times with the same object or use a command that isn't all that reasonable

-pretty short at 2,5/3 hours

http://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=528195124

I had fun with The Adventures of Fatman despite its limitations, it really served as a trip down memory lane back when adventure games were a more popular genre: I found its puzzle design to be its weakest point as I had to use a walkthrough at times to find out what I missed in one of my pixel hunts or how to interact with an item to get the desired result, but the omnipresent humour, ridiculous protagonist and decent, silly story made me want to continue and see what'd happen.

The Adventures of Fatman might not be a game for everyone due to its age and challenging gameplay, but gamers from the target audience ( veterans, newcomers wanting to try an old-style adventure or people appreciating the parody elements) will find it a worthwhile purchase as long as they can look past its intricate puzzles or have the patience to face them head on.

I enjoyed this comedic romp. The voice-acting was pretty good and resembles many old CD point & click adventure games from the early 1990s. The visuals also fall in line with games from that era and the gameplay is simple and functional. As with all Point & Click adventure games, The Adventures of Fatman holds little to no replay value. You can beat the game in a little less than 5 hours, so if you do that math a game at this price point is roughly a dollar an hour to play. It should be noted that the game does also feature another full-game in the special features section as well as some extra developmental content.

I got this game for under $4.00, and it kept me entertained for a Saturday night; if you look at those economics, it was cheaper than going to movie. Buy this game for that purpose, a cheap way to play a night away (don't expect it to replace your favourite mmofpsrpg...etc.).
