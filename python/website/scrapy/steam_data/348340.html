About This Game

Long time ago, when mages ruled the land, common people raised the rebellion against their magic, arrogance and tyranny. They used catapults, ballistas and bombs in the battle against magic castles and powerful spells. Now their destiny is in your hands

Epic catapult-shooter arcade game. Crush your enemies and their castles before their magic crash you! Unlike most modern catapult games, enemies in Siege Wars can act - periodically cast harmful spells upon your weapons!

Features

Features:
  • medieval siege weapons - catapults, ballistas, trebuchets and others
  • 90 unique levels in 5 scenarios
  • 10 enemy types including bosses!
  • 32 weapon upgrades
  • 7 construction materials with different properties
  • various magic shields, healing spells and much more
  • epic music and sound effects

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP
    • Processor: Pentium III, 1500Mhz
    • Memory: 512 MB RAM
    • Graphics: OpenGL 1.1 capable video

    • DirectX: Version 9.0c

Better than Angry Birds!

Does Angry Birds have siege engines that you can upgrade? No. Does Angry Birds have mages that cast spells to destroy one or more of your siege engines? No.

Yes this game has the same basic ideas as Angry Birds: launch a projectile to hit all the enemies in a set number of turns. But Angry Birds was not the first game to do that, so why do people disregard games like this just because they are similar? Who cares which was first, all that matters is if a game is good or not. Sure there are lots of crappy games that are made to try to get easy money, but dont' assume that this is one of them.

For one thing, this game has no animals. No birds, no pigs. The player has siege engines such as catapults and arrow launchers. The enemies are mages of different types. If you don't kill them in a turn or two the mages cast a spell and destroy one or more of your siege engines. And the mages don't laugh and mock you for losing like the pigs in Angry Birds.

There are 4 different types of siege engines you get throughout the game. You can't choose which to use, they are set in each level. But you can choose which ones you want to upgrade. You get upgrade points (I can't remember what they are called in the game) from time to time (I think for beating a level fast or maybe not losing a siege engine or something) and there are occasional orbs in the level that you can get, though they are usually in hard to reach places.

There are also spells you can use. I've gotten one at the beginning of each world. Once you use it, it's gone. But it does help when you're in a tough spot.

Graphics are nice, cartoony, cute.

Sound is ok.

The price is great. Pretty cheap for the amount of content you get.

Plus there are Achievements and Trading Cards!

This is a mixed bag, but if you get it for a low price, there is fun to be had for a few hours.
It does some things better than Angry Birds, and some things worse. It has some obvious, simple-to-fix design issues that make it seem like an unpolished cash-grab.

Edit: if you want to get 100% achievements in this game, it is not enjoyable, it is a tedious chore and it looks like the developers never tried to do that while testing, because the design flaws fly right into your face then. On Steam you cannot buy spells (which would make things very easy) like in the mobile versions through microtransactions, but the game was built around that feature it seems.
If you are not a completionist but want to have some hours of simple fun, the cautious recommendation still stands.

Good:
+accessible gameplay, if you don't have Angry Birds, this game fills that hole very well
+your weapons are upgradeable
+varied level design. Some are contraption mechanisms that I found quite funny (you hit a ball that rolls down a slope to topple a stone and so on and so on). Most of the levels are not like that ofcourse, but still are varied.

Bad:
-the trajectory of the previous throw is not shown (as in AngryBirds), and the parameters of your previous throw are discarded. Aiming is made more difficult for no reason.
-depending on the weapon, you can not adjust all parameters (the power of the shot is fixed for some weapons, for no reason, very annoying)
-the enemies attack your weapons randomly afaik. This makes a huge difference and brings randomness and alot of restarting the level.
-your highscore for a level is not shown anywhere. You only see if you have beaten it or not, but not how high it was/is.
-upgrading your weapons can have a negative side, because for example the range of the standard catapult can not be adjusted when using it (only the angle). If you extend its range, it is extended and you are not able to reach some close enemies anymore.
-it doesn't show you in the list of levels in which levels you still have to collect something. You have to play the level to look for collectible spheres.
-it doesn't show you what those spells do, and if you activate some of them at the wrong time, they have no effect and you just loose them. They should be greyed out during those times.
