This is rated down a lot, so I'll be a contrary voice.

Yeah, it's somewhat unpolished. It also seems fairly basic. OK, I can't argue with that.

But, on the other hand, it's pretty inexpensive (especially on sale) and makes a good game. I come back to play it when I have other, more polished but vaguely similar games in my library. Why is this one special? It works for what it is. Some others fail by trying too hard (like the Montezuma and Heroes of Helias games), with balance or just strange bug problems (like the Might & Magic Match 3 game, which I wanted to love) or just weird blandness issues (Puzzle Kingdoms or Tidalis). It even beats some puzzle games I really, really want to like because they're so artistic (the Kami paper color change game, for one) but just can't. In comparison to all of those, I sometimes semi-regularly just want to play this game...

...and that means thumbs up from me. Your mileage may vary, but, since I disagree with the average rating, I had to weigh in.

The Steam Store's description of this game is short on information, so I'll try to add a bit more to the discussion. Cobi Treasure Deluxe is certainly functional as a Match 3 puzzler, but its core mechanic, manuevering the blocks, just feels unwieldy. If the blocks are along either side of the screen, it becomes impossible to rotate them, forcing the player to move one space left or right, rotate, then move back. This has caused me to make multiple mistakes. There are additional items that can break up the monotony--check the video to see some in action--and this is a good addition, causing the player to rethink his or her strategy. The game seems to be envisioned as a low-budget time-killer, and I will agree that it's good for that much. But with better games out there in this genre, why not kill your time with those instead? I quit after an hour in.
