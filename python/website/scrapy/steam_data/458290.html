NOTICE: Made For VR

Space Bit Attack was made with VR in mind and we strongly recommend you buy this game only if you have or plan to get a VR HMD. However the SBA is playable without a VR HMD, but will be lacking some HUD UI and the full immersive experience we designed it for.

Thanks for your interest in our game and we hope you enjoy Space Bit Attack.

About This Game

The galaxy needs you! Transport yourself into a retro-inspired, VR space-fight adventure! Space Bit Attack lets you be the hero as a gunner pilot saving the galaxy from an evil alien invasion. Duck, dive, and dodge your way through level after level, blasting holes in the enemy defenses as you go.

Space Bit Attack is an entirely new and captivating take on a retro arcade classic that gives you the space pilot experience you’ve always wanted from the cabinet games of yesteryear. Each level of this virtual world features a swarm of aliens poised to attack. Navigate through the galaxy in a fighter ship fully equipped with the best laser guns available and destroy their forces one enemy at a time. As you progress, you’ll unlock features and abilities that give you the power to battle more challenging opponents. Those aliens won’t know what hit them!

Features:
  • Endless survival gameplay with infinite waves of enemies to destroy
  • A challenging VR space battle, viewed from a unique perspective
  • Unlockable abilities and features that help you boldly forge ahead
  • An amazing battle atmosphere, complete with epic sound effects
  • Easy to learn and a blast to play

Disclosure: I got a copy of this to review.

One of the interesting things about VR is how taking an age-old concept and combining it with presence can make something so engaging. This is a modern take on Space Invaders, except in 3D with VR controls.

The VR controls feel good, and there are two options, one where you move with your look and aim with Vive controllers, and one where you aim with your look and move with a regular controller. By using the Vive controllers you can split your firing directions, but I had the habit of looking at an enemy's incoming fire which moved me into it, so I think the regular controller is the best. The visuals are enjoyable, you can hardly go wrong with space in VR. I had no problems with anything using a Vive to play the game.

When they say the game is made for VR, they mean it. It can technically be played without, but I had some issues where I was unable to hit the enemies because I couldn't find a way to shoot any way other than straight ahead when trying to play with mouse and keyboard on a monitor.

If you're looking for something simple and fun, pick it up. I found that it's a great way to wind down when you still want to play in VR after tiring yourself out with some intensive roomscale games.
