About This Game

Summary

Blasted Fortress is a physics-based castle assault game with voodoo magic and resource management. When you're not playing, seasons change, and you may come back to be greeted by disasters that happened while you were away or gifts to bolster your resources. It's a blend of physics decimation, hoarding resources, and RPG-style enhancements.

If you're more creatively inclined and want to add more content to the game, you can even create your own buildings to siege using the built-in Building Editor.

Throughout the course of the game you'll unlock new ammo types that can each fill a niche in your strategy; from brutish explosive cannonballs that stick to blocks and blow up violently, to cannonballs gushing acid that impart a vicious debuff.

We're also dedicated to providing 100% free updates, so join up with us and discuss your desires as we roll out new content.

Game Features

  • Crush physics-enabled castles with the aid of a powerful cannon that can fire an assortment of strange ammunition.
  • Over 70 random buildings will stand in your way during a siege, and more can be added to the random rotation by saving custom levels in the Building Editor.
  • Choose a difficulty level to increase or decrease the time limit, and select from a Small, Medium, or Large building to really tune in on the difficulty you desire.
  • Purchase one-shot voodoo spells or gain powerful passive abilities by completing sieges and leveling up.
  • Fill your Vault with stolen gold and watch it rain!
  • One minigame that allows you to earn quick gold, with two more on the way.
  • Any and all future updates, even ones that include content, are 100% free.

New Steam Features

  • Blasted Fortress first came out in 2013, but many changes and additions were made new and exclusive to Steam owners!
  • New maps have been built-in, bringing the total amount to over 100.
  • Balance and playability have been tweaked in dozens of areas very carefully to make for faster, more rewarding gameplay.
  • Brand new Plunger and Detonation Blocks have been added; several old maps and most of the new maps make use of these awesome additions.
  • 20 Steam achievements add even more replay value.
  • Global statistics in-game for many things, such as total blocks broken by the community, gold vaulted by the community, etc.
  • Bigger plans ahead!

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP or newer
    • Processor: Intel Core 2 Duo E6750 or equivalent
    • Memory: 4 GB RAM
    • Graphics: NVIDIA Geforce 8600 or equivalent
    • DirectX: Version 9.0
    • Storage: 100 MB available space

Review of Blasted Fortress - 9th of January 2015 (version 1.15)

I would in its current state, at this price point, not recommend this game. There is more content coming supposedly, so I will update my review once that happens and I get a chance to try the updated version.

The Good (Positive)
  • Level Editor is always a plus.
  • Simple fun for a casual game, took me about 1 hour 15-20 min to finish all content, probably able to do it in 1 or less. (Note: This does not include achievements, just unlocking all things in-game etc.)

The Bad (Negative)
  • Gets very repetitive, very fast.
  • The other ammunition feels weak. I ended up only using the first 2 types (normal cannonball and 3-shot). Leveling up the passive perks didn't feel to make any considerable change.
  • Feels like there is still a lot of unused potential, fixable in content patches. F.ex would be nice to upgrade the Farm to get more money per meat, etc.
  • There are three spells in the game, but I think I used them 5 times and that was that. Feels like a waste buying the one-time use spells.

The Meh (Neutral/mixed)
  • The vault/hoard where you can choose to save money is very nice, and seeing the money spray all over once you start filling it up is nice. However, gaining gold goes very slow, and you use it to purchase all your ammunition.
  • With the slow gold rate I ended up making a new level in the editor that was filled with the resource boxes. This gave me some decent income, but at the same time it had the possibility to crash the game cause of just too much happening.
  • I had a few cases where there game turned extremely sluggish and was chugging along at 3-5 FPS. From what I noticed this happens when a block gets semi-stuck at the start of a level, which seems to affect the physics engine.
  • The "boss" fights are kinda meh, as they are all more or less exactly the same. Another thing that could be resolved with a patch.

Overall, not the worst game in history, but very repetitive and the lack of content (unlock everything in 1 hourish) makes this a not recommended purchase as of now, in my opinion..

In a video I watched on YouTube that was posted in October last year, the developer mentions that Blasted Fortress 2 is underway. This should also make you really consider if you should get this game or not.
Hopefully they do not keep back features etc, and put those in BF2, but we will have to wait and see.
