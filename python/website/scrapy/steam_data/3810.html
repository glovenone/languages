About This Game

In the years between the World Wars, Agent BloodRayne works as a killing machine for The Brimstone Society--a top secret fraternity that hunts down and destroys supernatural threats. Two missions, five years apart, turn out to be connected by one man. For years, this man has been searching the world for powerful occult relics to bring Germany into a new age of domination. Rayne must face his elite Nazi army and prevent them from releasing horrific creatures in their quest for the artifacts. Starring the super-sexy, super-lethal, supernatural heroine BloodRayne, this original action horror game unleashed the red headed dhampir on the world.

Be prepared to spend about an hour trying to get it to work.

When I was able to play it, however, I was really surprised at just how good it looks. Comparable to modern-day MMO graphics, BloodRayne is a brutal hack and slasher that is surprisingly more like a dungeon crawler than you would expect. The gameplay is very repetative, but that doesnt stop it being fun, and I was very impressed with the real-time removal of limbs. The writing and voice acting so far is supurb, really nailing the genre without cheesing it up, and I'm eager to play more.

This game is difficult, however, so you'll need to take certain situations very seriously, namely water. That's right, the only major source of my death so far has been environmental hazards - there are point where you're forced to make your way through flooded areas, which steadily drains your health, and if you're indoors then jumping doesn't help much. This game also features old-school style saving - no quicksaves, only saving at area transitions, which can be quite a chunk of gameplay, especially if you die after a boss. Enemies are generally inconsiquential, unless you're forced to fight an extended number than you can't drain life from, or you encounter a bug that makes them invunerable.

All in all, this is a surprisingly good game. Don't let its age or "sexiness" fool you, Rayne is a take-control badass that I'd like to see more of in the games industry.

FOR WINDOWS 7/8 TIPS ON HOW TO RUN THE GAME SMOOTHLY AND WITHOUT ISSUES, SEE BELOW!

BloodRayne is truely a nostalgic classic for me and I have to say, I still enjoy this title even after 12 years of my initial first playthrough. It has many flaws, but above all these, it has a swift and amusing gameplay, great heroine constantly throwing out black humor quotes and the "cutting system" (you can literally cut any imaginable little piece of body, like a quarter of face or piece of fingers. Or you can slice and dice the bad guys like a bread into a thousand of segments - what a highly sophisticated technology for such an old game! :-D ) of enemies can be really strangely satisfying.

Sadly, as this is an old game, it has issues when you try to run it on newer versions of Windows, such as low resolution, crashes and also sound clipping during cutscenes. I did my research and manage to find an ultimate solution for every problem:

To solve crashes, go to your Steam directory Steam/steamapps/common/BloodRayne and in the folder, right click on rayne.exe and set it's compatibility mode to Windows 98/ME. Also, be sure to check you have disabled the DPI scaling (it is in the same tab as the compatibility setting). This solved all the crashes for me and I haven't got a single crash in 4 hour playthrough.

For low resolution solution, launch the Bloodrayne first and after entering the menu, starting a new game, go through the first cutscenes and then quit the game. After that, go to your Steam directory Steam/steamapps/common/BloodRayne/system and in the folder, open rayne.ini - In the .ini file, search for these two gamePIXY= gamePIXX= and after the "=", type your desired resolution (for example gamePIXY=1440 gamePIXX=2560). I run the game in 2560x1440 without any problem.

And finally, to solve the sound clipping issue, install MSI Afterburner. The clipping is caused by the fact that the game is designed to run on 30 FPS max and newer computers run it on higher FPS - that is causing the problem. So you have to lock/cap your FPS to 30. To do that, install MSI Afterburner and load it. Wait until a RivaTuner icon shows in your system tray and after that, right click on it, select Show and set your FPS limit in the interface to 30 FPS. Remember to always turn Afterburner on if you want to play BloodRayne.

Hope this will help to enjoy this great classic! Cheers!

Bloodrayne is a third-person, action, hack and slash game that originally came out on October 15, 2002. It's been 11 years since i last played it on the original XBOX. Exploring the levels and meeting all the antagonist and monsters again is really nostalgic. Rayne is the main protagonist in the game. She is a dhampir who is in search of her father; Kaegan. She is later recruited into the Brimstone Society and that is where the game begins.

Story:
The story in Bloodrayne is not complex at all. You and your mentor Mynce from the Brimstone Society are sent to Mortton, Lousiana to investigate a disease that is turning humans into mutates. The unique enemy in this act are called Maraisreqs. Not much happens in this act apart from your first boss fight the Queen of the Underworld and your first meet with Jurgen Wulf (The main antagonist) who is the founder of the G.G.G and is trying to become immortal by acquiring powerful relics scattered across the world. The next act takes place 5 years later in Argentina, Rayne is assigned to eliminate several high ranking G.G.G.(Gegengeist Gruppe) officers before they can get ahold of one of the powerful relics. These officers serve as the mini-boss and bosses of the game. Midway through the act a new enemy type is introduced called Daemites. Act III takes place in Germany. Rayne is assinged a new list of G.G.G targets to eliminate. This act also introduces another enemy type called Feral Vampires. I can't put the rest of this act in words without spoiling major parts of the story.

Gameplay:
The combat system is plain and repetitive but enjoyable; Rayne's primary weapons are her blades which can do up to a 5 meele combo as long as she makes contact with an enemy. Her secondary weapons are guns. There are about 33 different weapons including Pistols, Rifles, Shotguns, SMGs, Assault Rifles, Dynamite and Grenade Launchers) in the game. She has 8 slots for weapons, 3 for light weapons, 3 for heavy weapons, 1 for grenades and 1 for special weapons. Rayne has 3 unique abilities that she can use, one from the start of the game and two you unlock in Argentina. Aura Sense: Targets and destinations will shine bright blue, Dilated Perception: Slow motion, and Extruded View: Allows you to zoom. The game will take you anywhere from 6-8 hours to complete.

Pros:
+ Basic but enjoyable combat.
+ Decent amount of variety.
+ Great level progression.
+ Immersing experience.
+ Rayne's dialogue.

Cons:
- Checkpoint system can be an annoyance at times.
- Graphics are outdated.
- The last few words of all dialogue in cutscenes cuts out. (Fixed by turning V-Sync ON = 30 FPS)

Notes:

Posted: 29 November, 2015
The action adventure market is crowded with titles these days, with developers and publishers trying to create the next Mrs. Croft, but as many publishers are finding out, making a decent action adventure game is no small task. Majesco was obviously feeling confident when they enlisted Terminal Reality to create BloodRayne, though. The folks at Terminal Reality are no strangers to the genre, with one pretty damn scary action adventure called Nocturne under their belts.Their project known as BloodRayne is a fast-paced action oriented blood-fest. The game is brimming with violence and animated boobs - yes, animated bouncing boobs. It's got plenty of eye-candy and some really good action. So is it good enough to warrant your attention?

As the half-vampire BloodRayne, you have sworn your life to protect humanity from the supernatural. (Bad monsters, as opposed to good monsters like you) Your latest assignment: Seek and destroy the nefarious vampire Jurgen Wolf before he can use powerful occult relics to unleash untold horror upon the world. Jurgen is a Nazi of course, and our heroine BloodRayne is employed by the secretive anti-Nazi society (Brimstone) to thwart his evil plans.

BloodRayne is a product of her human mother's ♥♥♥♥ by her vampire father. Born with powers of a vampire without all the weaknesses, and just like Blade, BloodRayne is on the edge and very hard to contain. She puts the hurt on Nazis like you wouldn't believe - chopping off limbs, snapping spines and sucking their blood to feed. Her only weakness is water. BloodRayne doesn't like water - in fact, if she stays in it for too long, she dies. By making her vulnerable to water, the designers have in fact found a clever way to limit BloodRayne's movement, and make the game appear less linear than it really is.

But even with this clever trick the designers couldn't really disguise the game's obvious linearity. Gameplay wise this is not a downside as BloodRayne doesn't really try to be anything but a straight-up linear action game. There is a lot of fighting, shooting and gallons of blood being splattered across the walls. Luckily, the action is as smooth and interesting as in any action adventure out there. In fact, it is even comparable to the feel of Max Payne. There are just so many things that Terminal Reality's new Infernal Engine can do.

Visually, it does a wonderful job of creating different types of environments. Scary settings, outdoor areas, cool looking Victorian style houses - there are just loads of tricks that this piece of code can pull off. The bump mapping effects on the models look awesome, the camera work is fluid and it doesn't take away from the action. The models look detailed and very well animated, and the real-time shadow effects are eye-catching but not too intrusive. In a word, the engine boasts some very solid visuals for an action adventure game. The water surfaces look beautiful and the reflection of the moon in the water adds a special flair to the outdoor areas. Because of the excellent animation, bump-mapping and the engine's proprietary bullet-time effect the action looks smooth and very appealing.

As far as the controls are concerned it all works very fluently and intuitively. BloodRayne can use both of her hands to fight. You'll be controlling the left hand with one mouse button and the other one with your right mouse button. What this does is give more freedom during fights to choose between melee type of attacks, and ranged weaponry, which includes all manners of fire arms like bazookas and chainguns. I should also mention that the physics engine works very well and that almost every object in the game is destructible.

The levels themselves are decently designed. As I said, the game is linear but the designers have provided a nice enough combination of indoor and outdoor environments and some very interesting boss creatures to keep the player interested at all times. The level design and puzzles are not nearly as versatile as in say, Indiana Jones and the Emperor's Tomb, but they're tough enough to provide a decent challenge for the player. The same goes for the AI routines. Nazis are fairly smart and I couldn't notice any serious flaws in their behavior while playing the game. The AI code never took my attention away from the action, which is good enough for me.

The only thing that this game truly lacks is a half-decent story and professional voice acting. Action adventure games are as much about the story and character development, as they are about cool action and dazzling 3D effects. Unfortunately, BloodRayne is completely lacking in this department. The story is mundane, uninspiring, and above all, is not conveyed well. It never really grabs your attention as the game never bothers to truly explain any of the character's motives, much less explaining the story's chapter jumps.

The plot has done virtually nothing to further my interest in this game. Tolkien once said that people are usually attracted to the history behind a story, and the realization that there is a huge wealth of information and proceedings that led up to the events in it. BloodRayne has virtually none of this. You don't feel a part of the game world, and you don't know why you are there. The history behind it all isn't properly explained and the narrators have failed to create a living and breathing fantasy world.

In summary, great action, smooth controls and a very good 3D engine can only do so much for a game. Ultimately, you have to have a decent story in there and some good voice acting to make the entire thing believable and immersing. Without these crucial components an action adventure game can never truly become great. BloodRayne is a decent action-adventure that should provide players with some fun moments, albeit without any background or meaning to it. I had fun with it, but not as much fun as I could've had if the story and voice acting were even half-decent.
