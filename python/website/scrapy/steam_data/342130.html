Hashtag Dungeon
Hashtag Dungeon is a dungeon crawler with roguelike elements that uses Twitter as its method of dungeon generation! Create dungeons yourself using our built-in dungeon editor and tweet your levels to the world, or venture through levels created by other players.

6 June

Hashtag Dungeon Update! (Version 1.1)

Hey everyone!

Based on some feedback (and a couple of post-launch bugs we've found...) we've added the following fixes to the game which should be coming out tonight:

  • The Plague Lord will no longer gravitate to one side of the screen for no apparent reason.
  • The Plague Lord will no longer spawn an impossible number of enemies at any one time.
  • Old pre-launch title screen music no longer plays when you try making a new dungeon.
  • The Mana Lords rock attack has been edited to increase it's optimisation. It should no longer lag out low spec PC hardware.
  • The Earth Mage rock attack has been edited to increase it's optimisation. It should no longer lag out low spec PC hardware.
  • Rock attacks no longer all play a sound on creation to improve performance on low spec PC hardware. Plus it sounds less annoying too!

We will be doing some more fixes in the near future. Currently we are looking at adding in-game volume controls and a search feature for the Player Dungeons room.

Best wishes,
Sean

Hashtag Dungeon Released!

So we've finally arrived at the launch of Hashtag Dungeon on Steam! It's great to finally have the game out in the open for everyone to play. Thanks to everyone who has stuck with us over the alpha and beta and is still playing the game today.



We'll have a few weeks of downtime (minus a few bug fixes and tweaks here and there) and then get to work on the first major update for the game. We're working on a bunch of new traps, enemies and nasty bosses for you to go up against. Far more difficult than the ones currently in the game.

The currently build has been tested pretty thoroughly, but there might be the odd bug here and there - you can contact us via Twitter or on the Steam forums about any bugs you find. We will try to sort them as soon as we can.

Thanks guys, your support has meant the world to us.

About This Game

Something evil is stirring in the depths of the world. The great sigils that once protected this land are failing, and the legendary Hashtag Dungeons are reopening. Will you be brave enough to enter the dungeons and face the horrors that lie within? Or will you join the dark forces yourself and become an expert Dungeon Master?

Features

  • Challenging gameplay reminiscent of the classic Legend of Zelda and The Binding of Isaac. Will you be able to overcome the devilish traps, monsters and challenges that await?
  • Creative collaboration is key: every dungeon in the game is made by players and published to Twitter. Everyone can add rooms, enemies, traps and treasures to dungeons with the help of our in-game âDungeon Masterâ editor.
  • Six characters to unlock and play as, each with their own unique gameplay and tactics: The Magician, The Paladin, The Blood Mage, The Ninja, The Huntress and The Robot!

Acknowledgements


Hashtag Dungeon is an experimental game designed to assess how a game might use Twitter to store levels. It was originally developed as part of a third year dissertation at The University of Lincoln. We would like to thank Dr Patrick Dickinson, Dr Ben Kirman, Dr Kathrin Gerling, Dr John Shearer and Dr Oliver Szymanezyk for their support during the lengthy development process. We would also like to thank Robin French for her work on the Steam trading cards, Barney Oram for his sound design, Alex Saye for his work on several enemies, and Liz Rainsberry for her musical talents.

It is not often that I return to a review to change my opinion on a game. Hashtag Dungeon has done that for me though, my original review contained some complaints about issues I had encountered that caused my first hour and a half of gameplay to be less than pleasing. Interestingly the developers fixed not only those, but a few minor complaints I had as well. And they did it in a couple of days.

Taking cues from the Binding of Isaac, Hashtag Dungeon gives you a series of randomish dungeons and abilities that let each play through have a different feel. It also provides you with a system for slowly increasing the power of your avatar as you play through each session. Then there is the core draw of the game, the player made maps. The biggest problem with that is the use of social media.

I understand the intent of the gimmick, I just refuse to use Redigg, Twitbook, Facespace, or Myterdit... Or whatever else the world comes up with to try and track and shape my behaviour.

So, without a Twitter account you cannot make the maps, unforunate. But you can still access others' without any trouble. You can also rate them positively or negatively so future players will know which to avoid and which are popular.

Overall the graphics are not as vividly distinct as the Binding of Isaac, using a pseudo-retro look instead of the drawn feel of the Binding of Isaac. This will occasionally leave you unable to see money when it drops into shadows or onto the yellow floor of a Hive room. Lighting is somewhat more round than what one would expect from a pixelated game, but it never becomes a problem. The look is relatively consistent in its presentation and never feels like something does not really belong.

A good change of pace from the somewhat septic feel of the Binding of Isaac that fans of the game will no doubt want to pick up.
