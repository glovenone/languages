Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

Note: This Early Access game is not complete and may or may not change further. If you are not excited to play this game in its current state, then you should wait to see if the game progresses further in development. Learn more

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Face It is all about how the gameplay makes you feel. We want to hear from the players themselves how THEY feel while playing so we can improve the game. That said, playtesting is extremely important for the balancing, adding new challenges and feedback. It is an introspective game shaped by how the players react to the game.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“From 6 months to a year. The time necessary to design all new features and levels and playtest them with the community.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The full version will feature improved 3D assets, 9 game worlds, narration, new platforms, new enemies, new mechanics, new magic sigils and new music from several artists.”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current version has a game world with 10 levels, 2 types of enemies and 8 types of interactive platforms.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“We understand that an Early Access title, being incomplete, has to be priced somewhat lower than the full product. The sooner you buy the game, the less you pay. We plan to gradually raise the price as we ship new content and features.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“We really need the community to help us balance the levels. We have to create a good difficulty curve to make playing Face It a good experience and we need to find out what kind of mechanics trigger specific feelings. We are counting with the feedback from the community to help us make this game as good as it can be.”

About This Game

FACE IT is a game about facing the most challenging feelings of the human experience. It uses a dark atmosphere combining classic platform mechanics with survival dynamics to bring the feeling of wandering in the deepest and darkest places of the mind and soul.

Defeat Ghosts with the power of your inner light.
Face dangerous traps in a challenging platform experience.
Find your way through mazes and puzzles.

Through game design techniques, the levels are made to invite you to make a magical and metaphorical trip to your subconscious thoughts and deal with your inner issues.
The levels represents dark aspects of human consciousness, like Fear or Attachment. By beating a level, you shall find brighter states of mind, like Love or Truth.




As you might pass through the levels, you may unlock the magical sigils and symbols that represent those new brighter states of mind and unlock the keys for the levels from the Darkest World.

The sigils are symbolic representations of the magician's desired outcome. They are symbols created to cut through the walls of the conscious thought and affect directly the unconscious mind. It's at some extent, similar to subliminal messages.

One of the techniques we use to create sigils is a Sigilization method created by Austin Osman Spare, who was a magician, as well as a painter and graphical artist. The technique consists in writing spells with your intents, and agglutinate the letters to reduce the written statements in abstract symbols. The conscious mind may not remember the meaning of the symbols, but the subconscious keeps the memories about the symbol being made. This way, the symbols and their intents charge each other, exponentially increasing their influence on the mind.

2) Collect all letters to unlock a bonus level. Sounds like a good relaxation after your work? Nopes. Those bonus levels are exceptionally tough. When a game's tough above a threshold it doesn't feel fun anymore. It becomes a chore instead.

UPDATE: Last update added a new way to control camera. Now the gameplay feels much more intense (especially when ghosts are around) and camera doesn't jerk that much. Overall experience improved a lot. Controls still feel unresponsive and not tight enough (not sure why). When I'm jumping through saws, for example, it feels much more dependent on luck, than on skill, and it's hard to understand where I'm going to land. I understand that this is a subjective matter, but I wish jumps in this game felt more "precision based".

Controls are clunky and physics doesn't feel good, but that could be fixed - it's an early access game after all. What's hard to be fixed is core gameplay. That, and the main idea.
Mechanics are simple, approachable and clear, that's a plus. But I simply find them boring, with lack of depth and no way to evolve in something more intresting through polishing or new content.
This game tries hard at being meaningful. Sadly, it doesn't even try to create metaphors for player to make sense of. It just keeps shouting you in the face messages it's trying to deliver and repeats same enigmatic pretencious phrases over and over.

If you're looking for a simple, dark and pretencious puzzle - maybe you'll like it. Other than that - wouldn't recommend.
