About This Game

Description



Black Home is one of Warfare Studios' most refined games, featuring an enthralling dark tale and a creepy, well detailed atmosphere. It's also one of their most popular games along with Vagrant Hearts, Ashes of Immortality and Valiant.
Note: to toggle between screen modes, press ALT + ENTER.

Storyline



The apparition of your loved one stands before you. The rain comes down so hard it stings, and the lightning flashes to illuminate the darkened windows of the decrepit monastary you stand before.

Inside this maze of ruins, is your loved one, lost, trapped, and alone. No one will come to your aid. No one will offer you any support, or weapons, or tools, save those you must find yourself.

Can you solve the mystery of the Monastery? Can you uncover the secrets contained within? Brave the unknown terrors inside, find hidden rooms and secret chambers, and unlock the mysterious past before the sun comes up, and you may get out alive!

non.horation
( 16.5 hrs on record )
Posted: 11 September, 2015
Black Home is a turn-based RPG about a woman who teams up with a group of travellers to find her missing husband. The characters are pretty interesting and have unique dialogues, but the story is pretty generic (blah blah evil cult etc). It was an interesting idea to have the entire game set in one building and its grounds.

The game isn't bad really but it could have used a bit more polish. There are a few typos in the dialogue. The battle system is functional but it was never really clear to me whether various elemental attacks actually had different effects or not so I always ended up just spamming the highest level skill I had rather than using them strategically. Additionally, it was kind of annoying that there was no way to see how much health an enemy has left, so it was hard to know whether to use a skill that used up a lot of SP or not, and you didn't know how far through a boss battle you were. Music was okay but the looping clips were very short so if you were really exploring an area it could get annoying pretty quickly.

Many items that you can interact with are indicated by a little star, but some are not, so sometimes progression would halt for a bit because I didn't have an object that I didn't know I could pick up. Also in some areas it's hard to tell if the edge of the room is a transition to another area or just a wall.

Enemies are visible on the map so they're avoidable if you don't want to fight them at that moment (though it's a little weird that all enemies use the same 3 or 4 sprites) and enemies don't respawn when they're killed, which makes exploration a little more bearable. You will be backtracking over areas as you acquire equipment that makes new areas accessible so this is definitely a good thing.

Posted: 27 August, 2015
My full review can be found here: http://www.the-backlog.net/black-home-review/

Disclaimer: I received a copy from the publisher for review.

Lecia Coy is trying to find her husband who is missing in an abandoned monastery. Unfortunately, this monastery is home to a number of secrets. She'll have to team up with several other people who have come here for different reasons to try and find her husband.

Black Home is an around 6-7 hour RPG that also features multiple endings. I'm not quite sure how you trigger the different endings though as I only got one. The entire game takes place within the monastery though the areas look different enough that it doesn't start to feel too dull to always be in the same place. There's only one set of collectibles to find, chess pieces, and you'll have to do a lot of digging and searching to try and find them all.

While Black Home might not be something to write home about in the end, it is an interesting game. Most of the game is fairly straightforward - I only got lost once while trying to find what I needed to progress. Some parts may make you think a bit, and as long as you don't mind that the AI of enemies trying to chase you can be a tad shaky at times, then check out Black Home.

Posted: 13 August, 2015
A game with a bit of difference, but still of the 16-bit style, Black Home is an absorbing and exciting tale that takes place in an ominous old and supposedly long deserted monastery that was previously being explored by the main protagonist's (Lecia) husband who is now missing/lost within the walls of the menacing old building.
So she bravely goes in alone, and finds herself in a huge, maze-like, and (not entirely) deserted assortment of rooms, corridors, hallways, secret passageways, etc. looking for her missing loved one. And, of course, she will find strange companions (a priest, a raider, she herself is an alchemist), mysterious items, apparitions, hidden places, and spooky monsters of all sorts as she searches.
It appears that the game takes place entirely within and on the grounds of the monastery which adds to the story by confining the action to the goal of discovering all of the secrets the place has to reveal.

Artwork and graphics are very well done, especially the monsters to fight. And the monsters are visible, and sometimes appear with eerie sound effects.
And the sound effects throughout genuinely add to the creepiness of the game.

The story's pacing is very well handled as just enough information, weapons, and supplies are made available as you go ---- but you must look everywhere very carefully to discover all the secrets.
Although it is fairly linear in the overall layout, you must still stay alert so as to not bypass or overlook items or places that are important.

It's a little different, but no less fun or exciting and still keeps the 16-bit rpg flavor that I enjoy so much. Story is exceptional, and I would certainly recommend adding this one to any library. Great fun here.
