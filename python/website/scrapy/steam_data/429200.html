About This Game

A simple construction worker with a simple objectiveâ¦ save the world.
Sounds quite simple, huh?
This might be a little difficult to achieve though.

Super Helmets on Fire DX is a crazy side scrolling action platformer game, with lots of nods to old school games, mixed with hardcore runner gameplay and a pinch of RPG lite elements.

You take control of The Worker, a simple guy that works at a building site in a bustling megalopolis. But one day, while mining some rocks, he discovered a secret that would have better been hidden forever!
The Ancient Evil God, a huge sphinx with the power to destroy the world, wakes up from his eternal slumber and sends our hero into an adventure through time and space, where he will try to save all his companion workers and the world!

Key features:

  • Classic side scrolling running gameplay.
  • High difficulty using fast reflex based gameplay. You will need to conquer the jump and crouch buttons if you want to survive.
  • Story mode with escalating difficulty and bosses.
  • 5 worlds to visit, including The City, Egypt, The Future...
  • Procedurally generated levels that will learn from your gameplay behaviour.
  • RPG-lite stat based challenge mode where you can modify your favourite character.
  • Various skills that will help you during runs to get better records.
  • More than 10 characters to unlock and play with, parodying popular characters!
  • Endless running Classic mode, with no character abilities. Only your skills and proficiency will help you in this mode.
  • Awesome chiptune soundtrack created by Carlos Viola.
  • STEAM online leaderboards and achievements. Show your friends who is the best!
  • Trading cards and badges.
  • For Windows, Mac and Linux.
  • Supports controller and keyboard. Although we recommend using a controller.

This is a very well-polished and interesting game. Easily worth the price, and then some. Want to see the gameplay in action? Check out my video here: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=p3MlnGhO3wI

On the surface level, it's a basic free-runner style game. You control a construction worker, jumping and ducking your way through the stages while rescuing your friends. Each level consists of two stages: a normal running stage and a boss battle.

The art direction really shines in this game. The pixel style brings about nostalgia while not feeling too overdone. The colors are bright and vibrant. The soundtrack is plenty catchy too, utilizing NES-era sounds to go with the graphics.

Not to mention, this game isn't a walk in the park. It's simple enough to learn, but you'll have a tough time mastering it. I'm looking forward to playing more with the new patch that was just released.

All in all, a fun game worth playing. Cheers!

UPDATE2 - 2/16/2016: Ive changed my review to a Thumbs-Up. However, you should read the below review. As things are changed via updates I will eliminate the gripes that are no longer applicable. Its getting better and I think the dev(s) will continue to improve the title. At this point, I think if youre a retro game fan, then it is worth buying. But be aware that there are still needed improvements

I will address the "Cons" first but I caution the reader that a gripe over a specific feature is not a reason to dismiss or not buy a game. My car has some stupid features. But I still bought it because its fast, gets chickenheads, and has impeccable styling.


1) Without the ability to have some horizontal movement or speed control (aside from a dashing slide that gets you to the center of the screen) whats the point of bringing in multiplayer? The two of you are going to be on the same place on tha map making this even more confusing

2) The platforms you run along (at least at first) are the yellow and black construction diagonals. But the ones you cant run or land on are also the same color and just slightly darker. Now, I normally wouldnt have a problem with that but its so taxing on the eyes that it causes my eyes to scream. On top of it, My mind doesnt register where I can and cant land all the time. Rule #1 in platformers, your platforms need to be different from the background. They have ignored this rule and seem to want to subconciously torture you.

3) Weapons against bosses are single-use. For a game this difficult I think each hammer you pick up should translate into 2 throws. Right now, you grab a hammer, toss it at the boss, and have to wait to pick up another. Or better yet, keep the hammer single use, and put in a weaker weapon that, when picked up, has more than one shot/use

A few things the game does well

1) Sounds. Spot on for a retro theme game

2) A level that changes/procedural generation on each spawn. Cool feature that makes a game more challenging

3) Challenge mode allows for using rescued workers as currency to unlock upgrades. I beleive story mode does this too but I cant confirm that because the color scheme irritates my eyes. I cant play it for more than a few minutes.

4) Standard double-jump with collected energy drinks giving you 3, 4 multi-jumps. Only reason why this is a "good" thing in this game is because the yellow and black construction stripes tend to really strain the eyes. And being able to stay in the air longer and higher give your eyes a moment to relax

Conclusion: Is it worth $1.60? I think so. Two days ago I said "No". But the devs respond to feedback and make changes. Im not great at too many games. But the games I am good at are the retro and retro-inspired games. They are my forte. I was in 4th grade when NES came out. So my 8 and 16 bit history is long and detailed; my skills on the platforms are top-notch. I have a NEO-GEO Pocket Color in my desk drawer in front of me. I try not to be overly critical of retro-inspired games. But they have to at least play correctly. This didnt at first. Everything is just a little off on this game. Ive played almost as many bad games as TAVGN. So I know how to quickly ID a bad 8bit or 16bit game. I want to like this and if the changes keep coming then its going to be easy to wipe the gripes right off of this review. With some fine-tuning I think this could be great. For example, first boss, have the hammers get two uses. This gives you that little bit of room to navigate the level. It also lets you discrimante between which hammer you are going to try and pick up. But if I have to tell them that then I should just make the game. The story is dumb enough to be awesome and the artwork is great. The sounds? Couldnt be a better fit for the style. I feel like its 1989.
