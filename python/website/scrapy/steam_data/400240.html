About This Game

"The Deer" is an educational inter­ac­tive expe­ri­ence for children.
You'll be an ancient Deer during the Pleistocene (Ice Age) epoch and you'll discover everything about this marvelous era.

- Did you know that during the Pleistocene there were no dinosaurs? They had become extinct at the end of the Cretaceous Period, more than 60 million years before the Pleistocene Epoch began.

- Did you know that many animals, specifically, mammals were much larger in body form than their modern relatives?

- Did you know that this age witnessed the final extinction of the giant shark Megalodon, that had been the top predator of the oceans for millions of years?

Here are some of the interesting facts that you'll find out playing "The Deer"!
Unfortunately there were no T-Rexes during this age since they were already extinct millions of years before.


All artwork is by illustrator Giulia Airoldi.

It's an interactive picture book for small children. Something where you take your three-year-old onto your lap, and go, "Oh, look, a deer!" and let the kid pick out more things and naming them, as well as you reading the text to them.

Think the short books with thick cardboard pages you may have at home or find in the doctor's waiting room. As that, it does the job nicely.

Unlike with a cardboard book, here your child can move the deer back and forth, and play a few simple minigames; memory, a type of jigsaw, and pick the right objects from an image. (If you set a key for vertical movement, you can even make the deer vanish.)

And that's it. I can imagine plenty of entertainment value for a small child (I met a few in my life), at the age where they like to go through always the same picture book, and get a giggle out of (wrongly) suggesting to feed the deer chips and chocolate.

For what it is, I recommend it. Others just need to consider whether they'd pick up a cardboard book of the same price for a child at the right age.

OVERVIEW
The Deer is an educational indie experience developed by Fabio Ferrara and Giulia Airoldi and published by Chubby Pixel. You are a deer that is exploring the area around him to learn about the Pleistocene (Ice Age) epoch and interesting facts about mammals. It has a few minigames and a dozen or more facts.

PROS
+ Cheap (can find free code giveaways anywhere)
+ Controller Support
+ Steam Trading Cards
+ Educational

CONS
- Short (est. <20 mins)
- Made for children yet uses very large words (Pleistocene, Megalodon)
- Minimal mini games (2 both take a few minutes)
- Facts aren't read to the player

OVERVIEW
I went into this game knowing it was an educational experience made for kids, and it still disappointed me. The facts were good, no denying that, but the actual experience was not immersive enough to hold a childs attention. The graphics were cute and so was the music, but nothing exceptional. If there were more mini games this experience would go over better. With such limited "gameplay" I still don't feel like it matched what other games can do.
2.5/10
