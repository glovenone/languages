About This Game

Airline Tycoon Deluxe is a funny economy simulation proceeding in realtime and assigning you the complete responsibility for an airline.
However, donât be fooled by the funny comic style or the included jokes: A variety of tasks are requested and your competitors never miss a chance to set a trap for you in order to put a spoke in your wheel.

Your abilities are put to the test continuously playing Airline Tycoon Deluxe: Will you be able to use your aeroplanes to capacity and land some profitable charter flights? Can you establish routes for lucrative scheduled flights? Aeroplanes cost millions; will you be capable of increasing the share price and obtaining enough funds by the issuing of securities?

Can you stand up for the construction of new aeroplanes in your role as an engineer? Are you able to avoid your competitorsâ deceitful attacks? Accept this unusual challenge and win virtual laurels with Airline Tycoon. The deluxe version contains 20 new airports as well as new opportunities. It is as interesting for professional tycoons as for beginners.

  • Funny economy simulation in comic style
  • Complete freedom of action and freedom to move within the airport facilities and more than 30 places to be visited
  • Control of all parts of management: purchase of aeroplanes and their fittings, financing as well as stock market listing, planning of routes and filling order books, personnel, marketing and maintenance as well as many other details
  • Easily operated construction of own aeroplanes and the opportunity to exchange these on the Internet
  • Use additional security precautions against competitorsâ attacks or sabotage competitors yourself
  • Compatible for local networks of up to 4 players

When I was younger I played the hell out of this game on my old Dell Latidude with windows XP, the game ran smoothly with no problems, it was very forgiving on outdated systems back then

First impressions:

You will no doubt be charmed by the groovy soundtrack this game has to offer, I like each and every song and I can appreciate a good soundtrack to play to, the ambience in the airport is also so immersive, hearing the flight announcements, chattering and bustling of people going on holiday and returning from holiday, and the game is rather easy to start playing thanks to the advisor that acts as a tutorial whenever you do something new for the first time

Gameplay:
It's simple, you control your airline CEO (I like Phoenix/Falcon Lines because of where their offices are placed) - and you go your day to day business of taking flights and ordering your planes to carry out orders, you need to make sure your security is in check (security office is on the top floor on the airport, look for a window that looks out of place). You need to buy a filofax and a cellphone so you dont have to keep going back to your office, once you unlock your laptop you can carry out orders from anywhere and make some changes to your planes. You can even hire a saboteur to make life hard for your opponents, from placing heavily salted food on board, to giving them a computer virus, or even planting a bomb in their office or causing a plane crash! Of course the airport manager will eventually find out it's you and a hefty fine will come your way.
Like any good CEO, you need to keep the cash coming in and the expenses low, for this you have to hire and fire staff, your friendly personnel team will help you out with this, and you can hire advisors to give you a little edge over the competition. You can buy and sell shares of the other 3 airlines, as well as buy and sell planes. You have so much to work with and at first it can be a struggle to juggle it all, but as I said, your lovely advisor will chip in whenever you feel clueless, she is a button click away.

Sound:
As said above, the soundtrack is very charming and upbeat, definitely a good choice to have this kind of music. The ambience is top notch and makes you feel like you are in the airport. Unfortunately you can't seem to change the music but why would you want to, and the music settings are a little hard to balance because some sound effects are rather loud to begin with, so spend some time balancing ambience, music and sound effects

Visuals:
It's a 2003 game so don't expect anything fancy, but the cartoon style graphics and smooth frame rate make this game very very enjoyable to play, the game will only lag if you use the cheat to bring a massive crowd to the airport (i think this is just cosmetic).

Multiplayer:

Unfortunately I never managed to play this game on multiplayer so I can't comment on this, but I'm sure your imagination will tell you it's rather fun, the AI in the game is so predictable that I can remember exactly who drops first, second and third to leave me as the sole CEO, because of what they do (issuing more shares, overzealous sabotage leading to excessive fines from the airport manager

Overall:
I give this game a 9, it's hard to flaw given that it was a very very good game at the time, I seem to remember that in the early 2000s to 2005 there was an influx of rather good tycoon and simulation games, for example Monopoly Tycoon, Mall Tycoon, Rollercoaster Tycoon (who can forget that), Hotel Giant and The Sims, I'll have no problems in admitting Airline Tycoon should also be on that list.

Get this game, it's Â£7 and you'll have lots of fun with it, just make sure to get your security up to scratch first!

The first few hours are a game of short-term memory. You go to the travel agency, and it lists six or so trips you can assign to your planes. Now you have to remember which plane is going to be in which city at which time - if you pick up a ticket that you can't fly, you pay a fairly-hefty penalty. If you don't pick up enough tickets, you won't be able to beat your competitors.

Now you've picked up your tickets, it's time to assign them to your planes! The new short-term memory game is to remember which airports have which abbreviations in their schedules (London is LHR, for instance) - the airline-schedule side of the screen uses the airport-abbreviation codes, and the ticket side of the screen uses the city name. Then assign the tickets to the planes in the most efficient way so you aren't flying the planes empty too often - using a bit of trial an error, since you haven't memorized the airport abbreviations yet.

And the short-term memory game is made harder because your guy has to actually WALK between the travel agencies and your office. Repeatedly. Picking up no more than six tickets at a time. (And really, no more than three tickets, because the profitability of about half of the tickets is abysmal.) While you try to remember which plane has a capacity of 180 passengers, and which plane has a capacity of 90 passengers, and which city it's in so it can get to the ticket's starting city in time, and when it's available, and dangit, this much memorization just isn't fun. I found myself wondering whether the game should do this scheduling automatically, only to realize that this short-term memory thing is supposed to *be* the game.

Also, don't work for Honey Airlines. Their office is the one furthest away from the travel agencies, so your guy spends even longer walking.

A) The game Airline tycoon
This game was released during 1998,deluxe[2003] during the time there was thirst for tycoon games so this made out pretty good , Breaking down everything complex . This is a tycoon game where you are one of the owners of a airline company , you get to choose one among 4 companies and do missions where the airport manager sets a specific task as the mission goal.
Unlike most tycoon games that tend to get serious with max info and mechanics AT1 takes a simple humorous approach.
- Initial missions are setup so that you can learn the various aspects of the game
- There is more than one way to win a mission, if you want you can take down your competitors and become sole survivor.
- From reading newspaper in the airport to sabotage it has everything you can imagine.
- Sweet music
Overall its a fun tycoon game that can satisfy anyone playing it there is also the freeform mode where you can play without any goals, multiplayer if you can get it work. The graphics presentation hides the complexity of the game so its easier for new players to play the game
B) Airline Tycoon Deluxe VS Airline Tycoon 2 Gold
Now on the steam store you will find there are two game with the name airline tycoon , The second is the sequel to the first game released by kalypso. It makes me extremely sad to talk about this sequel because it is a terrible experience, Instead of Improving the first game this sequel got everything wrong and made it worst and terrible. Needless to say airline tycoon deluxe is the final iteration of the game vs airline tycoon 2 gold which is the final version of the sequel.
I have ripped upon the second game more than once but will post why the first game is better
- AT 1 has a better presentation and ui compared to the jumbled basic terrible stuff AT2 has.
- The planes used in AT1 have real names aka Boeing , airbus etc so you know what is good and what is bad. AT2 features computer generated random names 486 dx 2 is a plane name////
- AT 1 Despite being released in 1998 looks nice and plays better (if compatibility is set properly) AT2 runs like garbage on any system and looks terrible.
- AT deluxe has a lot of features content wise , AT 2 gold just added the features in AT 1 as dlc.
So yeah Airline Tycoon 2 was a disaster of a sequel none of us AT 1 fans wanted to see but at least we have the first game back.

C. Game compatibility with modern systems

-Unfortunately No doubt that Airline Tycoon deluxe is a awesome game but , The game is over 10 years old so no doubt there will be issues with it running on modern systems and newer os.
Sadly The game engine itself has a certain limits of the time as in it runs in around 20-30fps an can slowdown when there is too much on screen. Aka a lot people in the airport etc.
