About This Game

The world's first and longest running Persistent MMORTS is finally on Steam! SAGA has been running since 2008, when it revolutionized the genre. We call SAGA an MMORTS because you build cities, command troops, and manage resources like in a traditional RTS, but also go on quests, chat with your friends, join guilds, and trade like an MMO. SAGA is an RTS with battling similar to the Total War series, mixed with city building from Stronghold. Build your army by collecting cards found in booster packs, or trade in-game resources for them in the auction house.

Persistent World

  • Design, build and expand your own kingdom.
  • Manage peasants as they collect resources to help you build and maintain your fortifications even when you're not online!.
  • Research and discover new technologies in your quest for total dominance.

Customizable Units

  • Your units level up and gain experience points that persist between battles.
  • Collect over 100 different kinds of troops and over 50 spells.
  • Collect loot from fallen enemies to customize and improve your units.

Solo and Co-op Questing

  • Join with friends in Cooperative Mode or battle solo on over a thousand quests!
  • Create and join guilds which regularly give resource benefits.
  • Trade resources and troops with other players directly, using the Auction House, and with trade routes.
  • PvP is completely optional, but participate in PvP for unique rewards including booster packs, troops, and items!

FAQ

I heard this game is pay to win. Is that true?
With the latest 1.50 update, we are focusing on bringing many more cards to the game's marketplace for in-game currency, making the full experience available for free. So pay to win is now a thing of the past!

Will my current SAGA account work on the Steam version?
Yes, since the Steam version uses the same servers, your account will still work. You can also still use the standalone client if you prefer.

Does SAGA have subscription fees?
No. SAGA has absolutely no subscription fees. Instead, players are encouraged--but not required--to buy their troops in booster packs. Purchased troops remain a player's property, and can be traded with other players for different troops. Troops can also be obtained from the market with in-game gold.

Troops become more powerful as you use them, gaining experience, new weapons and armor over time. Players can customize their armies to their nation type, drawing from over 150 different troop and spell types to find the combination that works best for them.

Where can i find more information about the game?
You can find loads more information about the game on our official site.
We also have an official gameplay and support focused FAQ.

ITS BAD.

SAGA was originally let loose on the world back in 2007 and while its visuals were decent for the time and the music was good the game eventually died in 2011.

But the cynicism crept in slowly and thickly the more I played.
The game crashes, a lot! You won't recieve any error message and if you are above and beyond the minimum specs as you no doubt was even when the game came out originially then its even more boggling how unoptimized it is that even a high end system can chugg along trying to run it.
Feel free to turn all the graphical effects off though because they firstly look bad and secondly make the game harder because the foilage that the enviroment adds can cover up enemy units!

The game is not pay2win, no, we need a special tag for this game. Pay to survive! Enemy difficulty leaps up! after the tutorial prepare for a blast to the past as you spam chat with "LFP" which wouldn't even be the worst offense if it wasn't for the fact; why spend money on the game to buy stronger units at all when the difference in power is not noticable?!

Speaking of not noticable, the game asks you to pick a starting faction. Pick any. Seriously, they all start out exactly the same, with human units, so why even have a choice? And its one of those games, you know the kind, 'Pick a starting race, you will be stuck with it and you cannot change your choice before you even know the pros and cons of your choice'
You picked undead? were expecting an undead army? Wrong son you get humans! Picked Orcs? So on and so forth.

Now these are all sprinkles on a turd. Most players can overlook these niggles and enjoy the game. What will be the breaking point however is army building. To get cards you need to enter tournament play...And win. You get nothing for taking part so winning is all that matters. Pay2win? With the steam release, I thought it would be fun to venture back into SAGA. I played this game a ton after it came out a long time ago. I've hundreds of hours of play logged back then, but it has been many years since my last login. But since it's on steam...

Anyhow, I digress...

What is SAGA. It's a mmorts, though you can play it mostly single player, or coop, or PVP, or do tournaments or whatever. You basically build up a city, build an army, train your troops, get epic loot, trade for new troops, join guilds, etc. All the standard MMO stuff, but with your "character" being a city and armies. Each player has their own plot of land on some nebulous world (you can't see who is adjacent to you or anything) and you can expand your land over time. The game is mostly about building up your own little empire, but with a heavy focus on your creating and outfitting the perfect army. There are 6 nations to choose from, and each is very unique in how it functions.

GRAPHICS

The game is old. It shows. The graphics aren't super great, but they weren't amazing when it released back in 2008 or so either. They are adequate. Not much can be said. An overhaul of the graphics would make a huge difference, but I'm not sure if that's something that could be done with a game this old.

SOUND

Both good and bad here. The music is amazing. When I started up and was greeted with the old tunes it was like meeting an old friend. I LOVE the music in SAGA. The composer was fantastic, but I haven't followed him since the old days. His name is Panu Aaltio, I think. So the music is fantastic, but the sound effects are kind of not great. It's not that they are super bad, but the mixing and when they happen isn't great. I wish they could remix how they are being done. Some are really great, but others are just kind of glaring in your ears. If you hit charge like 10 times fast you'll see what I mean.

UI

The UI is serviceable, much like the graphics. There's actually quite a few buttons, but they aren't super invasive. There are little tricks and hidden things too, like lots of hotkeys and toggles for various bits and ends.

GAMEPLAY

Ok, this game is more comlex than it seems. At first it seems pretty simple, but by the time you figure in all the items, abilities, formations, and other little bits, there's a ton of number crunching and theorycrafting to be had. You can see that the game has some roots in collectible card games, so the standard realities of that sort of game apply. You can theory craft for days and still not come up with all the best builds. Back in the day there were things that seemed over powered, but there was almost always a counter. Be aware, though, that you're going to probably be a bit confused when you start playing. Thankfully there is a help chat and usually somewhere there to help you out when you're confused.

So basically, there's city building, battling (RTS style - sort of like the total war games), questing, coop questing, loot gathering, tournaments, PVP, and a few other things.

CONTENT

There are a megaton of quests in the game as well. Some are really good, and others can fill like filler at times. There is also PVP (which can be brutal if you go up against someone who knows the game really well, but there is a ton of gameplay and fun to be had in the PVP). There is also just the city building aspect, which is kind of like sim city super-lite. Building takes time, so you can tinker a little each day, do a few quests to gather resources, and then just put SAGA down until the next day.

My favorite part about the content is playing COOP. Almost any game I play these days needs to have COOP (except RPGS) and SAGA allows it for all the quests, which is cool.

There is also some advanced stuff like raiding of other players outskirt territories and stuff, though I know there were some changes with espionage and that sort of thing and I haven't played for a long time so I'm not sure how that is functioning currently. There were some safeguards to prevent players from ransacking your territories to death, but again, you'd have to check on the forums or ask around for more updated info.

STORY

There is quite a bit of story and history hidden away in SAGA, but it's definitely not the strong point You aren't going to see an amazing plotline unfold with great characters and whatnot.

OTHER THOUGHTS

Will you like it? That depends. There is a lot to try out and enjoy in SAGA, but it's old, and parts of it feel really old. As I mentioned before, I'd LOVE to see an overhaul to the engine that focused on graphics, smoothness of animations (I didn't mention those...they aren't great), and an overhaul to how sounds work. But even with how old it is, there's a lot to enjoy. You can spend countless hours developing your empire, questing with your friends, and just having a lot of fun figuring out new strategies and running PVP or tournaments. If you don't want to play with other people, just turn off chat and treat it like a single player game. There's not a great story, so don't be expecting that. If you like card games, RTS, total war-styled battles, simple city building, trading, theory crafting, and things like that, then you may like SAGA. I guess it's free now, and it looks like a legit F2P game, so there's not much risk.

I like it. I'm not sure if I'll get into it like I did almost a decade ago, but I'm enjoying tinkering and reminiscing. Grab a friend. Laugh at how clunky some of the games of the past were, and give it a go. I'd recommend at least an hour or two before bailing on it, but there's a billion great games out there. To each his or her own, right?

7.5/10 in it's current old state. =)

Gotta wait a looooooooooong time. You need more to make a squad? I hope you enjoyed your nintieth birthday.

I remember playing SAGA almost a decade ago and it being such a demanding game that my HP desktop would slow to a crawl and overheat. Even today the game contains some highly unique and somewhat interesting features but due to the lack of funding and resources the game has stagnated for a good number of years now and is quite unstable at times, both server and client crashes aren't uncommon. The single developer has began work to port the game over to a modern engine which should improve things massively but will take some time.

The core gameplay is good, the meshing of the three genres (RTS, CCG, nation building) and other ideas are great but the implementation is fairly poor. I do believe it certainly is worth an hour of anyone's time but it sadly won't appeal to everyone for perfectly understandable reasons.
