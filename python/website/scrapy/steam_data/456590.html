13 June

Vive Camera Pass-Through aka Beer Mode

We are excited to bring you our brand new camera pass-through mode which lets you see your hands, keyboard, phone, beer, etc without having to take off the HMD (Vive only). It is available for both Swift and Swift Desktop.

The update features:

  • Vive camera pass-through. (Blended Reality)
  • Detailed floors which react to light cast by the screen realistically.
  • Improvements to Swift Desktop Beta for screen readability and UI options for better customization of the screen.
  • Many games (including OpenGL ones) can now be played directly from Swift Desktop. Press CTRL+SHIFT+SPACE to toggle first person mode while the game is running. (might require setting the game's video mode to fullscreen windowed from the game's menu).

Some minor issues to keep in mind:
  • The Vive camera needs to be enabled in SteamVR. See our Swift Vive guide.
  • The Vive camera sometimes hangs. In this case, please restart the HMD by right-clicking on the green Vive icon in SteamVR.
  • SteamVR sometimes conflicts with Swift and games crash on startup. If this happens, restart SteamVR or try running without SteamVR enabled temporarily.
  • Vive flicker: in a few games that heavily stress the GPU, the Vive starts to flicker with black frames. Tweaking the VSYNC game settings seems to fix this. (Oculus is not affected)
  • Oculus users: we recommend keeping SteamVR turned off while you use Swift to avoid conflicts and free up CPU cycles for your other tasks.

About This Software

Swift converts existing non-VR games into immersive VR experience, and also has a desktop viewer for browsing the web, watching videos, or just using your computer in VR.

Swift provides:
  • 360 view in game based on head tracking (ideal for playing games in the first person perspective).
  • Cinema mode for playing casual games on the big screen or watching movies (including stereo 3D, encoded in side-by-side format).
  • Desktop viewer for using your Windows apps or browsing the web (Windows 8, 8.1 and 10 only).
  • Blended reality / Vive camera pass-through aka beer mode so you can see your keyboard, popcorn, beer (Vive only).
  • GUI to configure Swift's functionality. Enable and setup Swift once, then start your apps the regular way.

Swift comes with 2 components:
  • MyDream Swift: DX9 + DX11 games VR injector for playing your games in 360 view on your VR display system.
  • Swift Desktop: for use in games not working with the MyDream Swift injector and to display your favorite Windows apps in VR. Swift Desktop can also switch to first person mode (where your head orientation maps to the corresponding look direction in game) and supports many DX9-12 and OpenGL (eg DOOM) games. Note that game performance might be marginally lower than the injector and you might need to choose windowed fullscreen mode in the game's options.

With our image sharpening algorithms and pass-through camera support (Vive only), you don't need to take the VR HMD off to see your keyboard or read small text on the screen.

Imagine playing your favorite FPS and vehicle piloting games with the freedom to look and aim instantly, or getting extra awareness in your favorite RTS or MOBA on a massive virtual cinema screen, or even blocking out the sights and sounds around you to chill and relax to a good show. Download Swift now and turn that imagination into a reality!

Why wait for VR content tomorrow when you can have it today?

Notes:
  • Set the game's FOV to 100-120 if the game supports it. Then adjust Swift's screen size (CTRL+SHIFT+[+/-]) until turning feels natural.
  • Swift supports DK2 and CV1 and requires Oculus Home: https://www.oculus.com/en-us/setup/. It does not work with the legacy Oculus runtime 0.8 or earlier.
  • Swift supports the HTC Vive. SteamVR must be running when Swift is enabled and Steam Desktop Game Theater must be disabled.

For more detailed instructions on how to set Swift up, refer to the Swift guide. Few games require extra steps to setup, which is described in the FAQ.

    • Storage: 20 MB available space

I use this tool with OSVR HDK 1.4 Headset. It works very good with all sorts of games, for example Skyrim and ArmA3. For some games, who natively support VR it is not required, for example DCS or Warthunder. But if I want to play other games I always use the Swift. I am really happy with it and wanted to recommend this to all VR goggles owners, especially OSVR.

In general, when playing games in VR you will find the user interface overlays not fit into the FOV in your VR view. This is not a prob with Swift. For some games there are good solutions, e.g. Skyrim.


LIST OF GAMES I PLAYED WITH SWIFT:
(List is not complete)

All Source Engine based games like Portal, Contagion etc
Skyrim Elder Scrolls (very good in combination with Flawless Widescreen)
Dark Messiah (excellent)
Lichdom (good, can't see the overlays though, but experience is great)
SpinTires (great, but overlay menues are hard to read)
Assetto Corsa (great, you need FreePie and FreetrackNoIR for this as well for head tracking)
Savage Lands (good, but UI overlays don't fit in FOV)
Verminetide Warhammer

How it works:

1. start your OSVR Server in Direct Mode
2. Start SteamVR /make sure this is all green)
3. Run the MyDream Swift and select "Insert Swift" then "Run"

Hint: For Skyrim select the TESV.exe from the Skyrim folder

LIST I COULD NOT MAKE IT WORK WITH:
Warhammer 40k Space Marine (Don't know why it is not working)

LIST of Games I am testing still:

Wolfenstein New Order
Doom (the new one)
