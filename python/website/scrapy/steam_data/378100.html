About This Game


You are stranded in desolate Lakeview Woods, plunged into darkness. As you move across unfamiliar terrain your flashlight battery drains with each passing second. Along the way you collect battery packs to keep your light burning bright so you don’t get devoured by the night.

But even greater danger lurks. “The Keeper” senses your presence in his woods. He watches and whispers secrets. You must unravel his puzzles to overcome your fear of the dark and bring back the light.

Fight the fright. Find the light.

FEATURES:

▪ Vast, immersive map filled with secrets and surprises.
▪ Randomly spawned battery packs for a new experience every time.
▪ Spooky 3d environment with vivid graphics.
▪ Interactive storyline with cut scenes and journal feature.
▪ Challenging Steam Achievements.

Product received for free
Steam has dropped it's quality control over the past months and again this game is a clear proof of that. Nyctophobia offers little to nothing to my personal interest while poorly taking elements from other similar games in the same genre. The game uses Unity, so graphics-wise you can't expect anything very pretty. Length wise it lasts about 30 minutes, which has an annoying perma-death throughout most of the game. Does it make it challenging? Yes. Is it entertaining? No.

The only difficulty in this game is literally finding batteries which last about 30 seconds. The mechanics are complete nonsense. Honestly I can't even call this a horror game. The only horror element is darkness, therefore you have a flashlight. There's no jumpscares, there's barely any creatures at all to scare you. All you get is a shadow thing that doesn't appear until the end section who calls himself the Keeper, you get a weird zombie sitting on a table who gets an epileptic attack when you enter said house, also he doesn't attack you. Oh and who could forget the skeleton on the end of the boat section that is downright laughable. I'm usually very sensible to horror and find myself getting scared with the most little things even in the worst games, but this one had absolutely nothing, honestly it doesn't even try to scare you.

I feel like this game is also missing any sense of proportion at all. Sometimes things like ladders are just too big, it feels like the game character is a midget sometimes. As I've stated, the main game last 30 minutes at most, the story makes no sense but since it's a horror game that's totally okay. The sound effects and soundtrack are so out of place, sometimes there's a music building up to... nothing.

There's also another game mode that you get after beating the main one, it's called Own the Night. It adds about more 10 minutes of gameplay where you turn on some lights and kill zombies with the lights. The zombies (which are just a person fully painted in black with a worker jacket) sounds make the worst zombie sound I've ever heard in a game, ever. They literally sound like ghosts effects from a children's movie or something.

The negatives obviously beat the positives in this game, if you get Nyctophobia for free, try it out and get some laughs of it, but don't think about spending money on this game. 2/10.

This ultra low budget horror adventure game starts out with you having crashed your truck in the forest, upon where you immediately find a note (personally addressed to you!) from some dark forest lord named The Keeper, telling you to leave or else. However, he's kind enough to let you know in precise detail that the generator beyond the river is where he harnesses his power, but you'll never stop him anyway because you don't have the gas can, key and cable to get the generator running.

No kidding, that's what he says in his note.

I spent about 30 minutes ambling around a hideously ugly forest that mostly consists of dirt and a sparse few PS1-era trees, picking up giant battery packages to keep my flashlight running and avoiding land mines seemingly drawn in MS Paint before I decided I had enough of this game. If you tried Nyctophobia for free you'd probably be angry at the time wasted in downloading and playing it, never mind spending a fiver for this dubious privilege.

It should also be noted that this evil spirit of the forest and darkness wrote his sinister warning message on spiral bound notebook paper. Perhaps "The Keeper" is short for "The Trapper Keeper."
