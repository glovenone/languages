About This Game

Planet Alcatraz is a 3D role playing game with isometric projection. The player travels through the game world and fights against enemies in the real-time mode. In a general sense Planet Alcatraz can be portrayed as a non-linear adventure with lots of shooting. Several genres are mixed in this game but despite that fact it isn't stuck between them and appears to be a classical RPG that is based on a specially developed system.


The action takes place on prison planet Alcatraz. Its inhabitants are inveterate criminals that were banished there for the life term for their terrible crimes. The main objective of the game is to control a sabotage group that consists of the only one person in the beginning and enlarges through the game. The player has to use all sorts of resources and means to accomplish the mission.

Key features:

  • Absolutely unique universe
  • Creation of the main character allows changing basic characteristics and appearance including facial animation based on LifeMode technology
  • Tense atmosphere determined by deep immersion to planet's criminal world
  • Comprehensive dialogue system allowing the player to choose the way of behaving and communication on his own
  • More than 100 kinds of cold steel and firearms
  • More than 200 characters involved in dialogues
  • More than 80 original monsters, including flying and poisonous
  • Fights and adventures with different behavioral and battle options
  • Original sounds and music

In general:
This game has nice ideas and nice death moments, which can easily triggered by chosing wrong dialogue option.
Some are hilarious, some are simply annoying.

Fighting:
The guards will sometimes attack you even if you've put away your weapon (just happened to me, and I noticed it when I had already saved my game...) and they're so tough you probably won't beat them.
Well, when it comes to fighting the whole fighting system is a huge mess it feels so clunky it ruined the gaming experience for me.
It feels like you want to hit a fruit fly, you'll hit with a chance of say...10% maybe. And there aren't enough interesting drops (see items) to motivate you, and no motivating quests...
It often is the basic thing : Kill x monsters and return, bring me this and that...

Items:
There are lots of clothes to find but they don't have any interesting stats only amour and an attitude rating which I've never experienced influencing anything, they just look different.
And there isn't much of an interesting weapon choice, neither.
I found ranged weapons to be even more useless than melee weapons at least most of them.

Characters:
They all seem very dump, dialogues are a bit funny and often pointless. You'll sometimes have different group members, but they have nearly no personality and they react not too intelligent.
There are a lot of unimportant nameless people running around always doing the same thing (dancing, cleaning)

Graphics:
Is o.k. but won't keep you exploring, small square-like maps which are all very similar to each other.

---> I wouldn't recommend the game, but for that low price, or better, on sale, you can try it, there are some funny sequences in it, but the gameplay is very annoying ( I often found me rather forcing me to play it any further than actually wanting it)

It's the 26th century. You're an imperial soldier sent as an undercover convict to a penal colony on Planet Alzatraz with a mission to sabotage a spacecraft the convicts are building to escape the planet. The landing pod crashes, you are separated from your teammates, get captured and thrown in a labor camp. Because of your excellent build and opportunistic tendencies, you quickly rise through the ranks and become the go-to person for many Planet Alcatraz inhabitants, who have 25 hours' worth of quests for you.

The RPG-style 3rd person game lets you build your skills, as well as those of the members from your old squad which you pick up along the way. Overall, the game offers 2 major mission campaigns with dozens of places to investigate and clear out, and a final push through 3 extremely difficult areas to reach a shuttle and escape Planet Alcatraz. At one point, you're required to find item F for person E who has unfinished business with person D who first wants person C dead, but they turn out to be dead, and the trail is found with person B last seen at location A but it turns out he's actually held captive at location Z... this is just an example.

Pros - Immersive storyline and missions which get you really enthused to level up in the right skills. Fair hit system, smooth gameplay (although the animations are quite wooden), generous XP and money rewards for quests.

Cons - The storyline is pretty linear. It gives you the illusion that you can play both sides, but in the end you're left with no choice with regards to your loyalty. The developers could have spent more time developing alternative endings. Graphics aren't the best since the game came out in 2009. There isn't really a big variety of weapons, most are absolutely worthless and there's only 2-3 ones worthwhile keeping. Not many places to go for XP. Enemies can be permanently elimiated, so once you clear out an area it stays clear after re-loading the area.

Planet Alcatraz is a decent RPG with a Fallout vibe. The combat system is more in line with the Baldurs Gate and Icewind Dale games i.e. Combat is in real time but you can pause the action with the space bar to issue commands.

As other reviewers have stated the English translation and voice acting are terrible but can often be (unintentionally) amusing. However bad the translation is it does not prevent you from following the plot or understanding what to do next.

The minimum spec graphics card for this game is nVIDIA GeForce 6600/ATI Radeon X800, 128 MB. I have an nVIDIA 8800 GT 512MB and it was working overtime running this game with the default settings. I recommend changing all graphic settings to Low. I honestly couldn't see much difference after I did.

Don't go the desktop when the game is running. When you try to go back to the game it often slows to a crawl and you have to shutdown the game through task manager.

The game is pretty unforgiving early on so I can offer the following tips to prevent you having to restart like I did.
* Put all your skill points into melee combat so you can actually hit the enemy
* Wait until you have earned two perk points then select the Botanist perk (Botanist gives you extra skill points when you level up).
* Don't complete side quests until you have recruited all of your comrades (the last one being Demon).

I've played through the entire game and didn't encounter any major bugs.

Hope this helps.
