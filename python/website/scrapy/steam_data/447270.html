19 May

Update: Adding some minor things

Hi!

We have made some minor updates to the IKEA VR kitchen; now that you have meat and veggie balls, you'll probably try to cook them in the frying pan. So we thought that turning the hood on was a good idea. Now, maybe you can find out if your meat and veggie balls are actually tasty. ;=) Another feature of this new update is that you can now open both ovens.

And if you would like to add even more reality to your VR, you can put a real chair on the floor, with a fixed position to your calibrated physical floor space. (The IKEA chair IDOLF gives you the most accurate experience but any chair will work).

Instruction to do the real/virtual chair test:
If you press “5” on the numeric keyboard the virtual chair appears. Use the hand controllers placed on the seat of the real chair to find the right position for the real chair to correspond to the virtual. If you want to redo it fast then set some easily removable markers on the floor. If you press “5” again you go back to the normal mode. Remember to move the chair out of the way afterwards ;-)


For best experience read below:
The virtual floor should match your physical floor. If it doesn't, the room will feel slightly too small or too big. Calibration instructions are included below. Also, aspire to set the IPD (distance between the center of your eyes in millimeters) on the headset to your personal values.

Fine tuning floor Height Calibration:
Put one of the hand controls or HMD on the floor.
Use PageUp/PageDown to move the floor up or down 1 centimeter so the HMD/controls rest on the floor.
Press "S" on keyboard to save to the next session.

We would love feedback on your experience, especially on how well the physical and virtual sizes correspond. To be continued!

About This Game

Put on your VR headset and prepare to explore our kitchen in full 3D.
Enjoy walking around and interacting with some parts of it just like you were there.
Instantly change the look and feel of the kitchen by selecting a different material finish.
Explore the kitchen from a child’s point of view or as a tall person.
Find the drawers that can be opened. Pick up a frying pan and place it on the stove.
Recycle the vegetable skins in the waste sorting station.
Try our “teleport” function.

This is an experience where we expect you to spend about 5 minutes.
It is a pilot/test that we will support until august 2016.

This kitchen is very similar to a kitchen in the 2016 IKEA Catalogue, can you find it?

Find press release here:
http://www.ikea.com/us/en/about_ikea/newsitem/040516_Virtual-Reality

Feedback:
We would love to get your input on what you would like us to change/add/create in this kitchen or in our next VR experience.
Send feedback to vrdevelopment@ikea.com

For best experience read below
For best experience the virtual floor have to match your physical floor. If not, the room will feel slightly to small or to big. Calibration instructions below. You also should set the IPD (distance between the centre of your eyes in millimeters) on the headset to your personal values.

Fine calibrate floor Height Calibration:
1. Put one of the hand controllers on the floor
2. Use PageUp/PageDown to move floor up or down 1 centimeter
3. Press "S" on keyboard to save to next session

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows 7 or higher
    • Processor: Intel(R) Core(TM) i7 -4790K CPU @ 4.00GHz
    • Memory: 16 GB RAM
    • Graphics: GeForce GTX 980 or equal
    • Storage: 2 GB available space
    • Sound Card: Yes
    • Additional Notes: HTC Vive VR headset with two working handcontrollers

Have you ever been lost in an IKEA store, unable to find the exit route? Now you can replicate that experience at home.

You are trapped in an IKEA kitchen, unable to escape. Your goal is to survive. A small amount of rations are provided for you. If you're not vegetarian, your rations are doubled.

Outside, the wall is cut away, revealing the infinity of the Construct. There is no escape. Soon, you will run out of food, and you will starve to death.

In all seriousness, an interesting quick tech demo. I'd be interested to see this built out more, with construction options. I suspect this will be most useful with an AR setup, where it can place virtual IKEA furniture in your play space, so you can see how it would work in your house.

Modular furniture (like IVAR) would work well in here, because you can experiment with different layouts in order to design your ideal solution.

It feels like a demo that IKEA have hacked together in a couple of weeks, and I'd be interested to see where the future of furniture shopping goes.
