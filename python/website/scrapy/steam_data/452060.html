Early Access Game

Get instant access and start playing; get involved with this game as it develops.

What the developers have to say:

Why Early Access?

“Development of Caveblazers has been a long process, but the game is now in a stable and enjoyable state. We want the community to be as involved in possible with the development process, and we will be prioritizing highly-requested features. You can voice your opinions on the Caveblazers subreddit (http://www.reddit.com/r/caveblazers), on Twitter (https://twitter.com/RupeckGames), or here on Steam.”

Approximately how long will this game be in Early Access?

“Caveblazers will be developed continuously, and will leave Early Access when it has enough content to justify a full release. Our current estimate is 4-6 months.”

How is the full version planned to differ from the Early Access version?

“The features in the full release will largely depend on community feedback. However, our initial thoughts about future features are as follows:
  • Loads more items, weapons, equipment, etc.
  • New melee and ranged weapon types which can be selected in the starting area.
  • Character customization.
  • Improvements to AI characters, making them smarter and more varied.
  • Additional areas.
  • More enemies, specific to each area.
  • An ending.
”

What is the current state of the Early Access version?

“The current version is an Alpha version, most of the core functionality is in the game, and a lot of content is yet to be added.

This version includes the first 4 areas, a substantial amount of items, procedural level generation. All 4 of the current areas are unfinished, and there is no end to the game yet.”

Will the game be priced differently during and after Early Access?

“The price may increase for the game's full release.”

How are you planning on involving the Community in your development process?

“The community is an important part of the development process. We will be focusing on community requested features first, and blog posts will be added about each major update.

You can voice your opinions on the Caveblazers subreddit (http://www.reddit.com/r/caveblazers), on Twitter (https://twitter.com/RupeckGames), or here on Steam.

We are very much looking forward to seeing your feedback throughout the Early Access phase”

"Boss Update" v0.2.0 Available now!



Hello! This is the first major update for Caveblazers. It has been worked on for about 1 month (since I wasn't able to work on the game between April & June).

From now until September I will be working on the game full-time, so expect large updates between now & then.

Change Log:

Features:
  • 4 Bosses
  • Boss music track with individual intros for each boss
  • Poison, fire & bleeding is not affected by 'damage resist'
  • Bows which fire multiple projectiles have their damage spread over the number of projectiles
  • New Temple generation technique (WIP)
  • New enemy appearing in the Forsaken Jungle & Fungal Grotto
  • New enemy appearing in the Fungal Grotto.
  • Temporary ending after the Fungal Grotto.
  • Since the game is no longer infinite, impossible scores have been removed from the leaderboards
  • Tripled the default size of the arrow hitbox
  • Burst knockback when acquiring a blessing
  • Boxes, pots, chests, etc can no longer bleed or be poisoned
  • Poison now only reduces hp to 1
  • Bazwood Bow changed from 'slow' to 'very slow'
  • Bats now have a 'dash' ability instead of damage on contact
  • Slimes now only cause damage while jumping
  • Default 'drop item' button changed to X on controllers
  • 'B' button on controllers can now be used to close menus & inventory
  • Increased sword hitboxes

Bug Fixes:
  • Explosions create light correctly
  • Improved performance for projectiles, blood & particles
  • Fixed freezing from Ankh resurrect effects on low-end PCs
  • General improved performance on low-end PCs
  • Potential fix for "Out of memory" and "Fatal memory error" crashes
  • Disactivated menu buttons can no longer be pressed using controllers

A few additional notes...

Thank you all for your boss & enemy suggestions! I didn't quite get around to adding any of them, but the next 2 bosses that I will be working on will be based on your suggestions.

I expect the bosses will require some tweaking over the next few days. Since we don't have a team of testers at our disposal, it's difficult to balance game's the difficulty, so be sure to give your thoughts about what you like/dislike on the forums!

Thanks!

About This Game

Caveblazers is an action focused platformer roguelike set in a fantasy world. Each game is unique with procedurally generated levels to explore, and a massive amount of items, weapons and equipment.
The game takes place in a recently discovered cave, said to hold "unimaginable power". You play as an adventurer setting out to explore the cave and find out what riches it holds, however you are not the only one.

As you delve deeper in to the cave, you'll discover new areas to explore, new weapons and new monsters to try them out on.

Key Features

  • AI Characters - Throughout your journey in Caveblazers you will encounter other adventurers. These adventurers are AI controlled characters which have randomized behavioral characteristics, determining how they interact with other adventurers. Some will follow and help you, some will run from you, and some will be aggressive towards you. The advanced pathfinding system means that these adventurers will most likely be able to track you down wherever you hide. There is no escape. Be afraid.
  • Platformer Roguelike - Caveblazers is a modern platformer, but also incorporates some Roguelike and RPG elements such as permanent death, randomized potion identities, RPG-style inventory and a massive amount of items, weapons, equipment and upgrades.
  • Procedural Level Generation - Each level is procedurally generated, destructible, and full of dangerous monsters, items and things for the player to interact with.
  • Epic Boss Fights - Between each area you'll encounter a boss. There are currently 4 bosses in the game, each very unique and requires a different strategy to defeat. More bosses will be added throughout the Early Access period.
  • Melee and Ranged Combat - This is a feature which is mostly overlooked in other modern roguelikes. Caveblazers attempts to move away from the simplistic combat systems we've seen in other games in the genre, and incorporates fun, fluid and fast-paced combat which is accentuated by the game's brutal and persistent blood effects.
  • Modern/Retro - Caveblazers features retro pixel art combined with modern technologies and sound effects, resulting in an interesting aesthetic.

Planned Features

The features in the full release will largely depend on community feedback. However, our initial thoughts about future features are as follows:
  • Loads more items, weapons, equipment, etc.
  • New melee and ranged weapon types which can be selected in the starting area.
  • Character customization.
  • Improvements to the AI characters, making them smarter and more varied.
  • Additional areas.
  • More enemies, specific to each area.
  • An ending.

System Requirements

    Minimum:
    • OS: Windows XP, Vista, 7, 8 or 10
    • Processor: Dual Core
    • Memory: 2 GB RAM
    • Graphics: 512 MB

Blaze It

Full disclosure: review copy provided by developer/publisher!

I have a question for you, reader: do you enjoy Spelunky, the roguelite darling that captured the hearts and minds of, well, a lot of people? Then have I got good news for you! Claveblazers IS Spelunky... which is pretty much damning Caveblazers with praise. Yes, it's mechanically sound. Yes, it has some interesting little deviations from the Spelunky formula. Yes, there are a lot of interesting additions planned for the full launch.

It is, however, unabashedly Spelunky Lite in nearly every meaningful way. There's imitation, and then there's Caveblazers.

You play as a tiny man/kid/dude armed with sword and bow, who's heard tell of a legend: find this cave, blaze within, profit. An ornery old man warns you against this, forgetting he's an NPC in a videogame and CAN'T TELL ME WHAT TO DO. He is disregarded, and your adventure begins in earnest.

For Spelunky people this is all familiar territory: you enter a randomly generated vertical dungeon and descend, smashing crates and chests for loot, items, mysterious potions, and treasure, while avoiding traps and killing cave denizens. Variety at the moment is wanting... there's only one trap I've encountered so far (standard spike trap), and you'll slay a lot of the same bats, slimes, and little dudes over and over again.

Treasure is used as a currency for various shrines that can give you all manner of boosts, and there are little blessings sprinkled randomly throughout each dungeon that can provide the same for free.

However, this is a PSEUDO-MUTIPLAYER title, meaning little dudes that look identical to you will spawn on occasion during your descent. I'm... not entirely sure if they're always hostile or not (apparently their behavior is randomized â friendly, enemy, fearful). First playthrough I'm pretty certain the little pseudo-player fella that spawned for me was friendly until I peppered him with too much arrow fire. But after that every single one that spawned hated my guts â fortunately they hate the standard enemies with nearly the same enthusiasm, and they can all kill each other well enough.

These pseudo-players can grab power-ups, items, treasure and weapons just like you so it's critical you stay ahead of them. They also employ a more human-like strategy to ruining (or assisting!) your day, so best kill them or get out of the level as quickly as possible.


It's a cute little game, and I enjoy it. The combat feels good â enemies move fast, and each fatal blow feels impactful. The sounds (music, combat, ambiance) are excellent, the dungeon layouts are interesting, the RNG-y bits are RNG-y... it's just a bit more hollow, and a bit less charming than Spelunky. Now it's Early Access which means they intend to add new weapons and enemies, bosses, a proper endgame, customization â all that good stuff.

I recommend Caveblazers in that it is a fun game that feels complete. Even if they don't finish every planned feature, it's an excellent little game that stands on its own currently. If you have any doubts or wish to see any feature properly implemented and not just hypothetical in hypothetical land, I recommend holding off and seeing how Caveblazers develops.

So after playing this at my friend's house for a few hours, I was gifted a copy - the idea is wonderful. The game itself controls pretty well, with some glaring issues(for me) - and unfortunately, I read in a few forums that the developer ditched progress on this game, so it's not likely to get fixed. Here are my issues:

~ It feels like the challenge in this game doesn't come from skill or learning how to get out of situations, it comes down to making do with the things you are denied, as a player - which smells like lazy game design, to me. It's the old Resident Evil trick of limiting bullets to make a game "scary" - but Caveblazers is not at all atmospheric, and the denial of abilities is cumbersome.

~ The bow only fires at a single pixel, and the hit box isn't expanded - you should be able to fire in analog directions, not just the cardinal 8. It SEVERELY limits the bow's usefulness, especially with the damn bats, who take multiple hits to kill and come in at an angle your bow can't quite hit because of a cliff or obstruction.

~ Your melee weapon does not hit its' own hit box - it swings a box ahead. That makes breaking pots and chests and stuff that are on the edges of platforms very tricky, if you only have a single square to swing - you literally have to carefully scoot to the edge or hop down and back up the wall to smack it, and then the treasure falls down somewhere you have to retrieve. This really breaks the flow of the game, for me.
\ related: I have not seen any melee weapons that swing past the arc of the regular sword, yet, which is disheartening because it's got such limited range.

~ No customization, all your weapons and bows and their swings/shots look the same in the animation. You look like a white guy in a blue shirt. The fake AI players are white guys in a blue shirt. It wouldn't take much to color a weapon strike, or draw a few extra pixels to differentiate them visually - especially in a minimalist game like this. It would be a weekend job, tops.

~ The fake AI players serve no real purpose other than to annoy the hell out of you and steal your stuff or hunt you down - I would be fine with this if they offered some kind of reward for killing them, maybe dropping some of the loot they acquired, but they simply serve to block progress.

~ The monsters, also, are just barriers to progress - they don't drop loot or items, and makes killing and getting past them really boring, really fast.

This game is great, in theory - it just needs polishing. But as for right now, I wouldn't recommend it.

Early Access Review
Posted: 1 June
It's hard to keep my attention nowadays. I picked up Caveblazers on a whim because I'm one of the few who enjoy, not only early access titles, but bargain kicks by indie developers. With only a couple hours in here, let's recap what I've found:

1) Evolving Early Access title with low risk (+)
2) Bargain at <$5 (+)
3) Replayability that keeps me coming back (+)
4) Unique concept with crazy NPC AI (+)
5) Platformer with controller support (+)
6) Cute retro graphics that even my laptop can crank (+)
7) Lots of unique loot (+)
8) Procedural generation keeps it fresh (+)
9) Satisfying, even in quick doses (+)
10) Just good, solid fun (+)

That's a lot of pluses with a minimal investment. If you're looking for a fresh take on a platfrom roguelike, and you're into having a good time...pick this up!
