About This Game

Welcome, bold adventurer! Card Hunter is an online collectible card game which blends together card play, deck building, tactical combat and fantasy role-playing in a way you've never seen before. Grab your cards, miniatures, dice and snacks, then journey to a new world of adventure on the tabletop of your Game Master, Gary.

Build your party and set forth on a grand adventure in the massive single-player campaign. Each adventure will challenge your deck-building skills and tactical prowess as you battle horrifying Black Oozes, savage War Monkeys and more.

Amass a collection of fantastical swords, shields and other adventuring gear. In Card Hunter's unique deck building system, equipping your party with these items builds your deck for you.

Enter the fray on a grid based battlefield. Prepare for turn-based strategy where you must play your cards right to defeat your foes and be rewarded with experience and loot.

When you’re ready, challenge other players in casual games, ranked matches or regularly scheduled leagues. Can you rise to the top of this month’s season? Can you and your undead minions triumph in the Graveyard Gambol league? Multiplayer Card Hunter is a never-ending challenge.

Don't want to face the monsters alone? Adventure co-operatively with one or two friends and share the dangers of Cardhuntria as well as the rewards.

When you're done with the huge core campaign, try out the adventures of Omin, Binwin and Jim from Acquisitions Incorporated with an exclusive story written by Penny Arcade's Jerry Holkins. Then get ready for when robots and insane computers invade in the all new Expedition to the Sky Citadel set. Or, if you prefer your monsters from the murky mists of time, brave the challenge of prehistoric monsters and intelligent weapons in the fiendish Attack of the Artifacts.

A turn based strategic card game, with some RPG elements. This game is not for everyone, people with a bit of patience and love for strategy games will enjoy this.

The concept of the game is simple, you have three races to choose from, with each race having it's advantages and disadvantages. Then you have three types of heroes for each race. Mage, Warrior and Healer. You get a set of cards for each of them and as you progress you get better cards. At the start of the round you are given random cards from you deck, that you can play out on the board.

The campaign keeps you entertained, you can complete the story, but you can't complete the game 100 percent unless you are willing to spend money. Online multiplayer is pretty smooth and keeps you entertained with competitive matchmaking and rare item drops.

Give this game a try once.

It's basically a f2p mobile game but with a presentation and UI built for PC. There's a sort of mobile game stamina system in that you can only do each campaign once a day and progression is totally dependent on equipment drops. Your characters levels only impact unlocking equipment slots, the actual cards (ie the actions you have access to) are tied to the equipment. The game pretends it's not too bad initially with scripted enhanced drops and free premium currency, but it quickly becomes clear that the common equipment is filled with too many weak actions and you need the good stuff fight higher level enemies. Good equipment requires buying epic loot chests, buying premium account for an additional drop every dungeon, or buying gold to buy rares that show up in the shops. You can also buy access to 'premium adventures' that give a guarenteed good drop.

As for multiplayer, co-op mode is broken if you only have one friend as it doesn't let either person take two characters and difficulty is based around having three players. It doesn't reduce the difficulty with only two players, so you quickly end up having to abandon co-op to solo grind. PvP is straight up p2w. RNG aside, you have better equipment you're probably going to win. Strategy can't overcome someone doing 4 times more damage because they have stronger attack cards.

The core gameplay is simple but interesting and fun (at first), so it's kind of sad this wasn't just made a p2p game with occasional DLC add-on campaigns. This kind of business model is just something I can't support on PC. I'd rather pay $10-20 for this up front and not have to deal with the good stuff being locked behind a paywall.
