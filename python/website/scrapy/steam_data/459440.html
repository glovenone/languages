Tower 3D
Tower!3D is the successor to the best-selling Tower! 2011 airport tower simulator. Using an intuitive command interface, users of all levels can enjoy the Tower!3D experience. Tower!3D brings the whole environment to life in full 3D with realistic foliage, real time weather changes and volumetric lighting.

About This Game

Your assignment is to guide aircraft of various sizes and capabilities to and from the active runway for landing and takeoff. To help you to assure the safe movement of the airplanes on the ground and in the air Tower!3D provides flight strips, ground and air radar screens and full 3D views of the airport.

  • 3 photorealistic airports
  • Advanced AI pilots to follow your commands
  • Stunning 3D rendering of the airports
  • Runway assignments for arriving and departing aircraft may be changed
  • Runway intersection takeoffs are simulated
  • Advanced routing to and from the runways and terminals can be assigned
  • Dynamic lighting and shadows, day-night cycles provide a stunning visual experience
  • Live airport with service cars for airplanes and vehicle traffic around the airports

System Requirements

    • Processor: 1.2 GHz Processor

    • Graphics: 256 MB

The mechanics of this simulator is pretty spot on, it's more realistic and much less "gamey" than others I have used. As others have stated the graphics are pretty much Google Earth pics with a simulator played atop it, not really a negative in my book short of the pictures of planes on the runway that aren't really there, just part of the Google picture capture.

That said, there are a few bugs that make playing Frustrating as these bugs are mainly with the scoring. Each successful action nets you only 10 points but every negative action costs you 500. I'll have the occassional plane that will never leave the terminal and every new plane that enters or exits a nearby terminal to that stuck plane costs my 500 points due to hazard warnings. LAX is riddled with departure and arrival scoring bugs and they get a bit quirky because they're hit and miss when you get the hazard hit. If I've got a plane on 7L lined up and waiting with a plane landing on 7R I get hit with a penalty. If I've got a plane taking off on 6R and one landing on 7R I get hit with a hazard penalty.

If you completely ignore the scoring (which is the biggest negative to this sim) it is fun to play, especially managing the ground traffic. I've never been in the positive numbers due to these scoring bugs, which is BIG negative experience to this sim.

Overall, I'm giving it a thumbs up simply because I do enjoy playing this app and hope the developers give the scoring another pass and fix these bugs.

- Graphics issues (landing lighs are spheres of illumination the radius of a 737 cockpit).
- UI issues (limited zoom, fixed camera location, no way to change the size of text on the strip window).
- Planes readback is not in common avionic parlance (250 is read "two fifty" instead of "two five zero").
- Limited setup options (can't turn off bloom, turn on AA, etc).
- No manual or charts.

Feels like a beta more than a finished sim. Hope they will fix the issues.

What is it?

Tower 3D is an Air Traffic Controller game aimed towards the casual end of the 'simulator' genre of games. It's no ATC2 or Global ATC (and doesn't pretend to be), but it's not a lightweight time management game like Airport Madness either. Tower 3D is aiming for a very neglected middle ground for ATC sims, so I had huge hopes for this one!

However, given that Tower 3D is really the only middle ground title that's both globally available and current, I'm disappointed at the missed potential here. It didn't need to do anything different (all the basics for an excellent casual game are right there) but it should have done them all so much better! Anyway, here's a list of the good and bad stuff so you can decide for yourself:


What's to like?
  • It's a fun presentation of ATC (ie more than maps and a radar screen) for people new to the genre
  • A basic tutorial mode is provided
  • Aircraft strips have plenty of details about speed, direction, attitude and altitude
  • There is flexibility to type specific commands as well as click pre-defined instructions
  • The game offers 3D views of planes taking off and landing
  • It's an ATC game that doesn't require an Einstein-esque level of knowledge in Geometery and Physics!


What's not to like?
  • As many others have said, the graphics are pretty bad - definitely NOT 'stunning' as described on the store page. I'm playing the game on "Fantastic" setting with an nvidia Titan Black 6Gb and 1920x1080 screen: two example screenshots - view full-size for best accuracy. A 'fantastic' setting should ideally not comprise jagged lines and muddied textures.
  • There are no free-camera options whatsoever - you can only zoom in slightly and have no control over the direction of view, and the camera frequently gets stuck pointing at nothing until the next plane arrives which can take a while
  • It's missing some basics such as 'holding short' of the runway, expediting aircraft, accounting for wind factor and such
  • Planes inexplicably stop and wait - and wait more - before carrying out a take-off command
  • Only the ground controller role is simulated (there's no tower, approach or departure gameplay)
  • All commands are read by the same female voice - there are no 'radio' voice recordings and no variety
  • The GUI leaves a lot to be desired with windows that are difficult to resize or reposition for a better fit, and various buttons in dire need of basic tooltip descriptions
  • No manual to explain more detailed options
  • It feels very unfinished - a sentiment echoed in nearly all reviews so far. Maybe it was released to an unforgiveably tight deadline, in which case, hopefully the developers will be able to relax a little and start the polishing this game needs.


Should you buy it?

Hard to say. If the developers voice intentions to improve it, then there IS a good game lying beneath all the faults. Right now though, and without that commitment, £22 (current price) is just too high for the quality offered. It's not an Early-Access title so (unless FeelThere declare otherwise) there has to be the assumption that no further improvements will happen. As a result, I find it hard to recommend - yet wish I could because this exact game is needed badly on PC. Hopefully, it will be improved and polished, and if that happens, I will adjust this review accordingly.


Some alternatives to look for:

Casual
Airport Madness! (stupidly fun, casual time-management game)

Intermediate
ATC3* (pitched at the same level as Tower 3D)

Serious
ATC2 (vintage, but still current and the most serious ATC to date)
Global ATC ('new kid on the block' but has a free playable demo you can try)

______________

* ATC3: I Am An Air Traffic Controller (its full title) can only be bought as a full product from Technobrain (in Japanese only). For international gamers, there are fan-made English versions of ATC3 for download from the web. Note that ATC3 is in no way similar to the much more serious title ATC2.

Please read this all text for an understanding of why I recommended this game despite its current deficiencies. This is based on a very short stint (the tutorial, plus a few minutes outside of the tutorial). So with that qualification:

While the premise is promising and there is definitely potential, the game has a very unfinished feel to it. The very first tutorial aircraft I encountered pushed back from the the St. Thomas terminal like a proper airliner... wow, neat.... except for the fact that it was a Cessna. OK then. Then on the GA ramp that typically would have hosted the Cessna, a Dash 8 type turboprop taxiied right though the farm of its fellow turboprops to get to the taxiway. The game took a slight hit to suspension of disbelief there. And the Cessna thing seemed pretty darned obvious to me.

Rushed. But could be fixed.

Pros: Promising mechanics, aircraft behave more or less as they should when commanded.
Cons: Rudimentary graphics and textures. Aircraft movement is not terribly smooth. View control is poor; zoom functionality is not terribly effective.

This has the potential to be a really good and engaging sim, but it's not there yet.

My advice - buy it to support the developer and encourage them to keep working on it. Try it out. You'll probably be disappointed. But come back in 6 months and I expect (or hope) that it will be signficantly improved from its launch state.
