Gurun has moved

3 June

Automatic run command, item recall tweaks, disbanded guild options.

- Added an automatic run command "rr". Automatic run stays in effect until "rr" is said again, or the player changes zones.

- Changed automatic fight command to "ff" from "aa".

- Players can now easily stop in a diagonal orientation. Previously, it was quite difficult, and players would mostly end up facing directly north/south or east/west, even if they were moving diagonally before stopping.

- Players who founded guilds that are now disbanded may restart them for 20000 gold by saying 'guild' to Tavelor. Founders of disbanded guilds may also now unengrave items engraved to their old disbanded guild at Tavelor's. Guilds with no one left in them are now immediately disbanded.

- Item recall now works in vaults, but there is a warning before using the scroll in your own vault. The warning is to prevent potential chaos from an accidental click. In all vaults, all items engraved to the user are recalled. For personal vaults, guild items are only recalled if the player is in their own vault, or the vault of someone who is not in their guild. In guild vaults, only the guild leader may recall guild engraved items. Guildmate's items are never recalled in any vault. These special rules only apply to items on the ground of vaults (items carried by other players work as if you were not in a vault. Clear as mud, but hopefully it will seem that the recall scroll "does the right thing".

- Players may now recall specific engraved items by saying the name of the item first, then using a scroll of item recall. For example, if you say 'dagger' and then use the scroll, then only your daggers will be recalled to you.

- Reflection is now displayed as a resistance. The percentage displayed is the chance to reflect.

- Fixed a problem for some Steam players who had trouble customizing their character using customization credits.

- Increased the delay between satyr butts by about 30%. DPB remains unchanged.

About This Game

Aberoth is a fantasy massively multiplayer online role playing game with charming retro graphics and tons of freedom.

You start your adventure in an orcish prison with no possessions or skills. Once you escape, your journey of exploration begins. To survive, you must acquire items, learn skills, and join forces with other players to combat the hordes of enemies who inhabit the world.

What makes Aberoth unique?
  • No dungeon instancing.
  • Enemies pick up and use equipment, potions, scrolls, and magical items they find.
  • Dropped items never disappear.
  • You can build permanent structures with items.
  • Unrestricted trading. There are no soulbound or no-drop items.
  • Full PvP. Player killing is allowed almost everywhere (but the game contains many anti-griefing mechanisms).
  • Tame monsters and make them your pets.
  • Polymorph yourself and play as a monster.
  • Over 190 unique items to discover. Item graphics are never reused for different items.
  • Over 30 different monsters to defeat.
  • Over 60 quests to complete.
  • 16 large areas to explore.
  • 9 Non player characters to interact with.
  • 8 unique skills to master.
  • Free to play with no registration required.
  • Works well on low end systems.
  • New content almost every week.

I will continue adding to the lists below if and when I think of more things to add...

PROS:
  • Simple controls and gameplay, easy to pick up and play, no tutorial really needed.
  • Up-to-date and useful Wiki. Most of what the game doesn't tell you explicitly you'll be able to research here.
  • Big nostalgia feels when playing Aberoth, especially if you've ever played games like King's Quest or others in your younger years.
  • More difficult than it appears at first glance. There's also a lot of "hidden difficulty" that you'll only truly discover through your own in-game disasters, or through the word-of-mouth of veterans who have experienced those things before.
  • Once you get a big enough vault, and get a hang of how item placement behaves, it's pretty fun to organize your loot and build things inside your vault.
  • Combat style specializations, each with their own benefits and drawbacks.
  • Very affordable member subscription option compared to other MMOs (less than 10% of the cost of typical MMO subscriptions). Also, I've gotten the feeling that the dev wishes to keep all member benefits strictly in the non-pay-to-win category. So the member benefits are pay-for-convenience at most, and I doubt we'll ever see anything close to pay-to-win in the list of member benefits.
  • Gold, loot, bunnies, powerful weapons, magic, skills, boss fights, pvp, guilds, trading, quests, and more!

CONS:
  • Low player base.
  • Random server lag happens. Rarely it's even to the point of being extreme (lag and server kick killed me and several others once, some loots were lost).
  • Very grindy, but this might not be a con for some people.
  • Mob density in the various zones leaves a lot to be desired. Personally, I'd love a big increase in mob density. Since the game is so grindy (I don't mind this), you are easily able to clear out an entire zone of the mobs it has available, meaning you either have to change zones and farm somewhere else, or go afk for a while until mobs respawn. This problem only gets more severe if there are more players than just you running a zone during the time you're there.
  • Difficulty scaling between zones could use some tweaking. Perhaps adding intermediate zones in between the existing ones would resolve my complaints here. When you start the game, you'll find yourself farming the ♥♥♥♥ out of mobs in the rogue den before you're strong enough to move onto farming mobs in the orc cave. Rogues will become meatsims long before you feel comfortable moving on to the orc caves. I've had a similar experience between orc caves and satyr forest so far, so I wouldn't be surprised if this continues as I progress further.
  • The gold auto-pickup is nice in theory, but I don't think it's fast enough. The current speed of gold pickup makes it more of a nuisance than a benefit, as I'm usually always able to manually pick up dropped gold faster than the auto-pickup does for me, even when 9 or 10 pieces drop at once.
  • If you start your character on one of the more pvp-friendly realms (you won't know this going in), you probably won't have as fun of a time as I've had. Obviously this depends based on if you enjoy pvp or not. Personally, I didn't want pvp in my early game, so I'm glad I lucked out by choosing Cyan, which has a pretty friendly community. Only times I've had to really worry about pvp on Cyan is when the realm is lit, and players from other realms pour over to get at the fresh pvp meat while it's hot. As a noob, you should start engraving gear as soon as possible, but since gear and gold are some of the very grindy elements to this game, losing something via PVP (even with the 90% safety via engraving) hurts morale a lot. I don't recommend trying pvp until late game, as higher-level players will surely identify you as a KOS target.
  • I wish mob health bars didn't cease to appear once your character's health reaches a certain value above their's. I'd personally love to be able to see current mob health regardless of difficulty level.
  • Inventory display is fine as-is, but I wish there was a better utility for your currently equipped gear. Sure, text commands exist for each gear slot, but they aren't obvious unless you visit the Wiki. When trying to right-click drop an equipped glove, for example, you'll usually, if not always, drop your equipped weapon instead. Same goes for trying to drop waist slot items, you'll drop your amulet. Furthermore on this point, it would be great to have confirmation prompts for dropping gear or using higher-level consumes. My mouse has sensitive buttons, and I've consumed some nice potions a few times that I would have rather saved in my vault for later.
  • After killing rogues for a while, you'll make the same mistake I did, and wonder into the orc field, because exploring is fun. Then you'll get absolutely annihilated by the first orc you meet, and will from then on realize that there's no way to gauge zone or mob difficulty unless you let them hit you for free at least once.

It may seem like I have a lot more negatives to say about Aberoth than positives, but I still give this a positive review. Realize, though, that I deem these more of constructive criticisms rather than set-in-stone negatives towards the game, as I know it's being actively developed with regular updates. I wouldn't have put this many hours into the game so far without really enjoying it. I'm just the type of person who finds it much easier to point out and describe flaws in anything, rather than explain and praise its good qualities. I also think negative aspects are more important to identify, as it gives the most worthwhile information for improving the game. People will discover their own positives, I find, and this will occur only after they've tried it for themselves.
