About This Game



On its 5th anniversary, Ninja Senki leaps from the shadows onto Steam to become Ninja Senki DX! When Princess Kinuhime is slain by a demon, Hayate becomes obsessed with revenge! Casting aside the art of invisibility, the blue ninja unleashes the power of shurikenjutsu upon mythological creatures, demons and other adversaries in his action-filled quest for vengeance!




  • Fight your way through 16 deadly scenes!
  • Discover multiple endings!
  • Remixed soundtrack by Patrice Bourgeault
  • Test your speedrun and superplayer abilities with a brand-new Challenge mode!
  • Unlock 2 secret modes!
  • Supports achievements
  • Supports Steam Leaderboards

Pre-Release Review
Posted: 23 February
Ninja Senki DX is essentially a remaster of the original Ninja Senki freeware game by Tribute Games, now on Steam, PS4, and PS Vita. I've included my full thoughts about the game in video format here:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q6ttH2kswAY

Taking the role of the ninja Hayate, you must traverse 16 stages to avenge the death of Princess Kinuhime!

Ninja Senki DX is a tough-as-nails action-platformer with a focus on perfection in execution and memorization. While it's possible to sight-read levels to get through them, collecting all of the coins, killing all of the enemies, or completing a level within the 'speedrun' time limit definitely requires practice.

There's a lot of intentionally-NES-style design here. Enemy placements are such that they'll catch you offguard the first time almost every time. Jumps are tight, leaps of faith are not always marked. I would call this a negative for most games, but Ninja Senki has been built with this style in mind. Instead of being frustrated by unfair design, I found myself wanting to take another shot at it and perfect my technique.

The DX version includes a completely remastered soundtrack that is *exceptional*, along with the charming 8-bit style graphics, and tight controls, Ninja Senki DX ends up being a pretty satisfying platformer in the $5 range.
