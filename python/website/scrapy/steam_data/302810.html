Product received for free
Divinia Chronicles: Relics of Gan-Ti is an ARPG where you're going to play as a guy from a village in a middle of the forest that in the begining he doesn't know anything about his fate but his during the adventure will discover it.

At the first sight, the game has remembered me the Wii version of Legend of Zelda

The game is in early acces now, but you can play five chapters and try some of the features and contents.

In the first chapter, the game introduces you the story and characters.

In the second one, you are going to learn how to fight, and solve some puzzles.

In the third one, you are going to awake a fairy that will teach you how to use magic powers and you will fight to your first boss.

These three chapters are a sort of tutorial, the adventure really begins in the fourth one.

The game unblocks chapters when you arrive to some checkpoint and you can not save during them.

The character is easy to control using keyboard and mouse. WASD to move and mouse to fight using one button for hit and one for block.

The graphics are nice anime style. There are full of colours and animations are normal for a game of these characteristics. The scenarios are quite good (the game uses source engine), water effects are good and the enviroment changes a little bit during night and day. Maybe developers must improve the models of the characters, they are a litlle bit simply, specially the enemies (there are three types: squirrels, frogs and plants). They must be better, because all of them are low detailed and have the same look and textures.

The map is a little bit complex. I miss a little map of the area in the corner of the screen and some sort of quick travel because I spent a half of time walking around between the main sites of the map.

The game is very linear and there's no side-quests, but it's well written and its story seems long enough (I have only play the first three chapters) to give you some hours of entertainment.

You improve your skills fighting, each enemy gives you points of expirience that raise your level but I haven't been able to find new equipment (you change the sword between chapter two and three) so it neither seems there is inventory management or traits/abilities/skill point. Maybe in the next chapters this point changes,I hope so.

Music and sound are quite good, there are some qualilty tunes and sfx.

The game has no achievements neither trading cards, I encourage to developers to add them in next releases

The game needs some work but developers are working hard on it so I think it is a good choice to play, if you like Zelda style role games.

Divinia Chronicles: Relics of Gan-Ti is an early access Action RPG with a bit of puzzle and substantial amount of story. Above is a short video of my first half hour of game play. Below are my initial thoughts on the game and purchasing recommendation.

TheHiddenLevels.com was provided a preview copy from Team Monkey and for that we thank them.

This game has potential and the opportunity to be relatively good if the final product is slightly more polished. The initial mechanics are a bit rough in many areas, control, combat, and overall flow of game play. If the developers were not actively addressing these concerns openly I might be inclined not to suggest this game. The developers have been very active in updating the news for what they are improving, and most of my concerns are on their list. The one issue I did have is that between zones or chapters the loading screens seem to remove you from the experience a bit.

That being said, the game is a cute ARPG with similarities to The Legend of Zelda (Ocarina of Time or Majora's mask) in terms of it's feel. There are puzzles and combat, a seemingly large map, and multiple chapters of story which seem deep enough. Be prepared for the atypical JRPG dialogue, it makes more sense than some, but is clearly a translation that doesn't always match up. The story initially is intriguing and mysterious.

The combat is lacking, but again development is planning to improve this aspect. Currently it's sort of a repetitive experience with some variation in attacks at a higher level. The AI isn't extremely skilled and fights feel more like a task than a combat engagement. The combat controls and movement controls have some to do with this problem, and with some refinement they could be solid for the games purpose.

I'll say if you are willing to watch the game develop and help attempt to shape it's future a bit then it's worth picking up. The potential to be a fun Zelda-esque game is there, but as their early access description says, this is a small passionate development team. This is one you should pick up if you want to see this type of Action RPG developed further and refined into a finished product.

Thanks for watching and reading. Check out www.thehiddenlevels.com for news and reviews, and please follow us on twitch, twitter, facebook, and Steam!

Product received for free
Divinia Chronicles: Relics of Gan-Ti is an ARPG where you're going to play as a guy from a village in a middle of the forest that in the begining he doesn't know anything about his fate but his during the adventure will discover it.

At the first sight, the game has remembered me the Wii version of Legend of Zelda

The game is in early acces now, but you can play five chapters and try some of the features and contents.

In the first chapter, the game introduces you the story and characters.

In the second one, you are going to learn how to fight, and solve some puzzles.

In the third one, you are going to awake a fairy that will teach you how to use magic powers and you will fight to your first boss.

These three chapters are a sort of tutorial, the adventure really begins in the fourth one.

The game unblocks chapters when you arrive to some checkpoint and you can not save during them.

The character is easy to control using keyboard and mouse. WASD to move and mouse to fight using one button for hit and one for block.

The graphics are nice anime style. There are full of colours and animations are normal for a game of these characteristics. The scenarios are quite good (the game uses source engine), water effects are good and the enviroment changes a little bit during night and day. Maybe developers must improve the models of the characters, they are a litlle bit simply, specially the enemies (there are three types: squirrels, frogs and plants). They must be better, because all of them are low detailed and have the same look and textures.

The map is a little bit complex. I miss a little map of the area in the corner of the screen and some sort of quick travel because I spent a half of time walking around between the main sites of the map.

The game is very linear and there's no side-quests, but it's well written and its story seems long enough (I have only play the first three chapters) to give you some hours of entertainment.

You improve your skills fighting, each enemy gives you points of expirience that raise your level but I haven't been able to find new equipment (you change the sword between chapter two and three) so it neither seems there is inventory management or traits/abilities/skill point. Maybe in the next chapters this point changes,I hope so.

Music and sound are quite good, there are some qualilty tunes and sfx.

The game has no achievements neither trading cards, I encourage to developers to add them in next releases

The game needs some work but developers are working hard on it so I think it is a good choice to play, if you like Zelda style role games.

Divinia Chronicles: Relics of Gan-Ti is an early access Action RPG with a bit of puzzle and substantial amount of story. Above is a short video of my first half hour of game play. Below are my initial thoughts on the game and purchasing recommendation.

TheHiddenLevels.com was provided a preview copy from Team Monkey and for that we thank them.

This game has potential and the opportunity to be relatively good if the final product is slightly more polished. The initial mechanics are a bit rough in many areas, control, combat, and overall flow of game play. If the developers were not actively addressing these concerns openly I might be inclined not to suggest this game. The developers have been very active in updating the news for what they are improving, and most of my concerns are on their list. The one issue I did have is that between zones or chapters the loading screens seem to remove you from the experience a bit.

That being said, the game is a cute ARPG with similarities to The Legend of Zelda (Ocarina of Time or Majora's mask) in terms of it's feel. There are puzzles and combat, a seemingly large map, and multiple chapters of story which seem deep enough. Be prepared for the atypical JRPG dialogue, it makes more sense than some, but is clearly a translation that doesn't always match up. The story initially is intriguing and mysterious.

The combat is lacking, but again development is planning to improve this aspect. Currently it's sort of a repetitive experience with some variation in attacks at a higher level. The AI isn't extremely skilled and fights feel more like a task than a combat engagement. The combat controls and movement controls have some to do with this problem, and with some refinement they could be solid for the games purpose.

I'll say if you are willing to watch the game develop and help attempt to shape it's future a bit then it's worth picking up. The potential to be a fun Zelda-esque game is there, but as their early access description says, this is a small passionate development team. This is one you should pick up if you want to see this type of Action RPG developed further and refined into a finished product.
