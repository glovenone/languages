About This Game

The only videogame that exclusively features the MX1 and MX2 championships, comprehending all the teams and all the world-wide-known bikers and the amazing 2011 Monster Energy FIM Motocross of Nations.
Pure and freestyle Motocross you have never seen before!


A high value license: 3 official competitions (MX1, MX2 and MxoN championships); 12 locations and all the sponsors of the 2011 championship; 32 official teams between MX1 and MX2; 84 real riders.


Monster Energy FIM Motocross of Nations: 16 national teams, 48 riders and bikes (with all the suits and liveries of the different national teams) including worldwide champions of the United States.


Personal competition finishes when the round begins!
MUD World Tour: live the whole MUD experience through the life of special characters: the HEROES! Face a quantity of different levels and various types of events and demonstrate how keen you are on rising up the Motocross world!


Monster Energy Trick Battle Mode: 30 different tricks in 6 spectacular arenas, since “Motocross” also means Freestyle!

Key Features

  • Incredible artistic style, studied and realized directly from Milestone’s graphic department
  • Advanced motorbike physics and animation
  • Real-time terrain Deformation
  • Photo Mode
  • Online ranking system
  • Real motorbikes sounds

This is a fun game when you play it for what it is. I really enjoyed the time I put into it. The game plays much different than any of the current Motocross racing games and it shouldn't be compared to them because of this reason. You can't play this game expecting it to play like the MX vs ATV series or MXSIMULATOR, this is a racing game on dirt bikes, not a simulator.

The single stick driver controls feel dated when comparing it to MvA Reflex, but the controls work for this game. The scrubing feature feels great, even though the animation does look a little wonky when it comes back. But the way it plays is nice in the game and you'll be spotting your landing on the way back not staring at the animation.

I never got a chance to play online against other players, that would have been very interesting. The AI gives you a good run from time to time, but nothing like another human player would.

If you're looking for a fun, good looking dirt bike racing game I would suggest this game. The Tour mode is like a career mode where you level your rider up, get new gear, better teams, etc... I really enjoyed playing this mode. My only complaint is that you're not able to put your name and rider number on your rider, these are assigned for you.

Overall I would give this game a 7\10 and I suggest that any fan of dirt bike videos games give it a chance.
